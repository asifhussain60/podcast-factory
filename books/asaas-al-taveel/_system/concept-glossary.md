# Concept Glossary — Asaas al-Taʾwīl

## Cosmology and the prophetic cycle

- **nāṭiq** (ناطق): a "speaker prophet" who inaugurates a cosmological cycle. There are seven in al-Nuʿmān's scheme — Adam, Nūḥ, Ibrāhīm, Mūsā, ʿĪsā, Muḥammad, and the awaited Qāʾim.
- **asās** (أساس): the "foundation" — the figure who carries the inner meaning of the nāṭiq's law. ʿAlī is the asās of Muḥammad's cycle.
- **ḥujja** (حجة): "proof" — the senior teacher under the imām; the bridge between the imām and the believer.
- **dawr** (دور): "cycle" — one complete nāṭiq-to-nāṭiq turn of cosmic history.
- **qāʾim** (قائم): "the standing one" — the awaited final figure who closes the seventh cycle and discloses what was hidden.
- **ghayba** (غيبة): "concealment" — the state of the awaited one before his appearance.
- **intiẓār** (انتظار): "waiting" — the believer's posture during the ghayba.

## Hermeneutics (the method)

- **taʾwīl** (تأويل): allegorical interpretation that returns a verse to its first principle. The title of the book.
- **tanzīl** (تنزيل): the literal sending-down of revelation; the surface text. Pair with taʾwīl.
- **ẓāhir** (ظاهر): the outer / manifest meaning of scripture.
- **bāṭin** (باطن): the inner / hidden meaning — what taʾwīl discloses.
- **rumūz** (رموز): "symbols" — the encoded references in scripture that taʾwīl decodes.
- **abjad** (أبجد): the numeric value of Arabic letters used to decode symbolic numbers in scripture (see `content/_shared/arabic/06-abjad-numerals.md`).

## Neoplatonic emanation (al-Nuʿmān's metaphysics)

- **mubdaʿ** (مبدع): "the originated" — the first emanation from God; identified with the First Intellect.
- **ʿaql awwal** (عقل أول): "First Intellect" — the first cosmic principle; corresponds to the nāṭiq in the cosmic order.
- **nafs kulliyya** (نفس كلية): "Universal Soul" — the second cosmic principle; corresponds to the asās.
- **fayḍ** (فيض): "emanation" — the outpouring through which existence descends from God.

## Doctrinal terms (Fatimid Ismaili vocabulary)

- **daʿwa** (دعوة): "the call" — the Fatimid missionary enterprise; the structured invitation to esoteric knowledge.
- **dāʿī** (داعي): "the caller" — the Fatimid missionary, the carrier of the daʿwa.
- **walāya** (ولاية): "guardianship" / "spiritual authority" — the unique allegiance owed to the imām, distinct from prophethood.
- **ʿiṣma** (عصمة): "infallibility" / "protection from error" — attributed to the prophets and to the imāms.
- **takhalluq** (تخلق): "becoming charactered" — the believer's interior conformation to the imām's qualities.

## Honorific and titular conventions

- **PBUH** (sallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam): said after Muḥammad's name (rendered once per chapter per R-HONORIFIC-ONCE).
- **AS** (ʿalayhi al-salām): said after a prior prophet's name (rendered once per chapter).
- **amīr al-muʾminīn** (أمير المؤمنين): "Commander of the Believers" — title given to ʿAlī.

## Editorial / textual terms

- **muqaddima** (مقدمة): the editor's or author's introduction.
- **fihris** (فهرس): "index" — the table of contents at the back of the book.

## Closure terms (introduced in EP06)

- **al-sābiʿ** (السابع): "the seventh" — the awaited Qāʾim, the unwritten chapter.
- **sukūt al-muʾallif** (سكوت المؤلف): "the author's silence" — the deliberate omission of the seventh chapter as itself an interpretive gesture.
