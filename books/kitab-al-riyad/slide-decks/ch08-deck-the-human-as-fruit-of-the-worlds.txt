# The Human as Fruit of the Worlds

*The visual companion to Chapter Eight. The audio chapter is the prose; this file is its argument made structural. Same concepts, same depth, restructured for slide-deck rendering. Covers all seven sub-chapters of Chapter Five of the source book, the author's adjudication of the dispute over what the human being is the fruit of.*

## Where this chapter picks up

A handoff chain. *Chapter Four* settled the rank of the speaking soul. *Chapter Five* turns from the *what* to the *whom*.

| Chapter | Question settled | Carried into Chapter Five |
|---|---|---|
| Chapter Four | What rank do the speaking souls hold | They are the second emanation, jewels by their own essence, continuous with the chain by the running of the originating power |
| Chapter Five | Who is the full creature in whom soul and body meet | The human being, the rank at which the chain arrives |

The seven sub-chapters work one question. Three voices set the stage.

| Voice | Position | Stake |
|---|---|---|
| Author of *The Correction* | The human being is the fruit of *this* world | The natural chain of cause-and-effect culminates in the human form |
| Author of *The Defense* | The human being is the fruit of the *higher* world | The *Ma'ad* (the return) is real; the human being moves to another world |
| The author al-Kirmani | The human being is the fruit of *all* the worlds | Each is half-right, half-wrong; the human stands where two overflows meet |

The political stake sits one layer below the metaphysics.

| If only fruit of this world | If only fruit of that world | The author's settlement |
|---|---|---|
| The *Ma'ad* is a story for the consolation of mortals | The bodily life is incidental to the believer's vocation | The body is the field in which the human is harvested for the higher world |
| The materialist conclusion | The *sacred law* and the *limits* lose their hold | The higher world is the rank in which the first perfection is consummated |

## Sub-chapter one, the fruit of which world

A three-position contrast pair plus the author's third word.

Column A: the author of *The Correction*

- Claim: man is the fruit of this whole world
- Stages of the natural ascent: principles of the individuals, then individuals, then compounds, then the natural form prepared to receive the three souls
- The three souls: vegetative (growth and nourishment), sensitive (perception), speaking (rational, the soul peculiar to the human)
- The picture: the natural world is the long preparation; the human form is what the preparation produces

Column B: the author of *The Defense*

- Claim: that is the saying of the materialists, the *dahriyya*
- Structural argument: a fruit belongs to its tree; if the human were the fruit of this world, this world would *hold* him
- Counter-position: the human is the fruit of the *higher* world, the honorable and illuminating world
- Evidence: the prophets were sent to warn people to provide for the *Ma'ad*; the human *moves* to another world

Shared row: both agree the human being is a *fruit* of *some* world; both agree the question is which.

The author's third word:

> Each is truthful in what he said on one side, and each is untruthful on another. The side on which they are both truthful is that each of them has said of human beings what belongs to him. The side on which they are both untruthful is in denying the other its share.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter one.

The doctrine the author lands on, named in compact form:

> For man rightly bears the title of fruit of all the worlds, because human beings issue from between the two worlds. From this world he receives his first perfection; from that world he receives his second.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter one.

## The two perfections, named and assigned

An annotated structure. The human being is the single creature in whom two harvests arrive. Each perfection is named, sourced, and given its content.

| Perfection | Source channel | Content | What it produces |
|---|---|---|---|
| First | The natural world, the channel of bodies | The issuance of the bodily form by way of the circular bodies, the luminous planets, the proximate causes | The form prepared to receive the three souls |
| Second | The world of the Intellect, the channel of form | The consummation of the spiritual image: the knowledge of the Oneness of Allah Almighty by means of the *limits*, and obedience to them in knowledge and deed | The harvest of the higher world, the precursor to the natural thing, intrinsic to it, and which endures |

The second perfection's doctrinal weight, isolated:

- It is *the precursor to the natural thing*
- It is *intrinsic to it*
- And it is *enduring*

What this means in plain terms: the bodily form passes. The work the soul does in and through the bodily form does not. That work is what the higher world's harvest is.

The highest authority for the doctrine is named:

> This corrects what we have said in that statement of Mawlana Amir al-Mu'minin the Fatimid caliph Maad ibn Ismail, may Allah's prayers be upon him, in *The Interpretation of sacred law*, where he says that man is the purpose of the second world and the end of the action of nature.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter one.

The Imam is named three ways in the citation:

| Mode of naming | Form used |
|---|---|
| Given name | Maad ibn Ismail |
| Regnal name | the Fatimid caliph, the Strengthener of God's Religion |
| Office | Amir al-Mu'minin, Commander of the Faithful |

## Quranic and prophetic anchors for the two perfections

Three scriptural anchors hold the architecture in place.

> Surely We created man of the best stature. Then We reduced him to the lowest of the low, except those who believe and do righteous deeds, for them is a reward unfailing.
> Quran 95:4-6, *Surat at-Tin*, in the rendering after Pickthall.

How the verse maps to the architecture:

| Phrase | What it names |
|---|---|
| Of the best stature | The first perfection, the bodily form prepared for the three souls |
| Reduced to the lowest of the low | What becomes of the form left to itself, with no work done from the side of the higher world |
| Those who believe and do righteous deeds | The second perfection, the consummation of the spiritual image through obedience and worship |
| A reward unfailing | The precursor to the natural thing, intrinsic to it, and which endures |

> Verily We have honoured the children of Adam. We carry them on the land and the sea, and have made provision of good things for them, and have preferred them above many of those whom We created with a marked preferment.
> Quran 17:70, *Surat al-Isra*, the chapter on the night journey, in the rendering after Pickthall.

The verse names the dignity of the *first* perfection: the bodily creature is, in its first perfection, already the bearer of a *karama* given before any work the soul has yet done. The form prepared is not a neutral substrate; it is the dignified recipient of what the higher world will pour into it.

The Commander of the Faithful's opening sermon condenses the two-perfection architecture into one paragraph.

> Then Allah, glorified is He, collected from the hard, the soft, the sweet, and the saline earth, clay which He dripped in water till it got pure, and kneaded it with moisture till it became gluey. From it He carved a form with curves, joints, limbs, and segments; He solidified it till it dried for a fixed time and a known duration. Then He blew into it of His spirit, whereupon it took the pattern of a human being, with a mind that governs him, with intelligence which he makes use of, with limbs that serve him, with organs that change his positions, with sagacity that differentiates between truth and untruth, and with the tastes and smells, the colours and forms.
> *Nahj al-Balagha*, Sermon 1, attributed to Imam Ali, Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him).

The sermon's structure maps cleanly onto the two perfections:

| Phase in the sermon | Perfection it names |
|---|---|
| Collected from the hard, the soft, the sweet, the saline earth | First perfection, the natural ascent |
| Kneaded, dried, carved with curves, joints, limbs, segments | First perfection, the form prepared |
| Then He blew into it of His spirit | Second perfection, the gift from the higher world |
| Mind, intelligence, limbs, organs, sagacity, tastes, smells, colours, forms | The harvest in the rank where the two meet |

The closing seal, traditionally attributed:

> Whoever knows himself knows his Lord.
> Hadith attributed to the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him).

To know oneself is to know what one *is*, the rank one occupies, the harvest of which one is the fruit. To know the *full* rank, fruit of all the worlds, body from below and spirit from above, is to know the One from whom both overflows run.

## Sub-chapter two, *The Defense*'s argument turned back

A reversal structure. *The Defense*'s own rule is applied to *The Defense*'s own claim and breaks it.

| Step | Move |
|---|---|
| 1 | *The Defense* says: if man were the fruit of this world, this world would hold him |
| 2 | *The Defense* concludes: therefore man is the fruit of the higher world |
| 3 | The author applies the same rule: if man were the fruit of the higher world, that world would hold him |
| 4 | But the human *descends*, takes up bodily life |
| 5 | Therefore by *The Defense*'s own rule, man is not the fruit of the higher world either |

The verdict, in the author's words:

> He has handed his opponent the reversal of the matter against him.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter two.

The prophets' mission illustrates the two-sided architecture, not the one-sided claim:

| What *The Defense* used as evidence | The same evidence turned back |
|---|---|
| The prophets were sent to warn people for the *Ma'ad* | The prophets were sent *to this world*, in bodies, into bodies |
| Proof that man is the fruit of the higher world | Equally proof that man is the fruit of this world |
| One-sided reading | Both-sided reading: the mission has both feet planted in both worlds |

The chapter's structural formula, stated in the author's own voice:

> Human beings are in fact human beings precisely because they are formed by the meeting of the overflow of the worlds: from the natural world, what flows in the channel of the bodies; and from the world of the Intellect, what flows in the channel of the form. And this is in the middle.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter two.

The two overflows, named and traced:

| Overflow | Direction | Channel | What it conveys to the human |
|---|---|---|---|
| Natural | Downward | The channel of bodies, the chain of motion and rest, elemental compound and natural form | The bodily composite, the form prepared |
| Higher | Downward | The channel of form, the chain of emanation: Intellect-which-is-the-Word, Soul, speaking souls | The gift conveyed through the *limits* into the prepared form |

The verses traditionally attributed to the Commander of the Faithful name the same architecture:

> Do you suppose yourself to be a small body? Within you is folded the greater world. You are the manifest Book by whose letters the hidden becomes manifest.
> Verses traditionally attributed to Imam Ali, Commander of the Faithful, gathered in the *Diwan*.

The mapping:

| Image in the verse | Architecture in the chapter |
|---|---|
| A small body | The human bodily form, the harvest of the natural world |
| The greater world folded within | The higher world overflowing into the form |
| The manifest Book | The rank at which the hidden becomes manifest, the meeting of the two overflows |

## Sub-chapter three, emanation is not marriage

A contrast pair. The two reformers each err in their own way on emanation. The author corrects both.

Column A: the author of *The Correction* on emanation

- Claim: the Soul emanates from the First Intellect in perfection; nature emanates from the Soul in perfection; the image emanates from the *Hayula* (prime matter) in perfection
- Endorsement: the Soul-from-Intellect link is correct
- Error: the *Hayula* does not emanate *from* the Soul; *Chapter Three* of this book showed that the chain works differently at the *prime matter* link

Column B: the author of *The Defense* on emanation

- Claim: production requires the pairing of one rich-and-perfect with another deficient-and-in-need
- The analogy: the heavens are like the male (perfect, self-sufficient); the earth is like the female (deficient, in need)
- Error: he refuted himself by his own admission that the products were in *both* in potency
- Polemical accusation: charged *The Correction* with contradiction; that charge is itself the contradiction

The heaven-and-earth marriage analogy, refuted by *The Defense*'s own admission:

| What *The Defense* claimed | What *The Defense* admitted | What follows |
|---|---|---|
| The earth is deficient relative to the heavens | The metals, forms, plants, animals were in potency in *both* the heavens and the earth | The heavens and earth are *equal* in respect of their potency for the products |
| Production works by perfect-male joining deficient-female | None of the products appears visibly in the heavens or the earth or the mothers | The production is not by deficiency-marriage but by *action* of the heavens upon the earth |

The doctrine of perfection that the author is protecting:

> We have shown that deficiency is found only in things, not in their selves, but in their ranks when they are added to one another, and in their actions when they do not exist except in something other than that which is done with their existence. In itself each is perfect; for if their selves were not perfect in the existence that befits them, nothing could be done by them, neither by themselves nor by another.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter three.

The architecture stated as a rule:

- Each rank, in itself, is perfect, perfect in the existence that befits *it*
- Deficiency is found *only* in proportions when ranks are added to one another
- The earth is not deficient in itself; only when added to the heavens
- The heavens are not absolutely perfect; their perfection lives in a rank, in a proportion
- The chain runs by emanation, by the running of power, by proportion, not by marriage of substances

The Quranic anchor for the heaven-and-earth relation, which refuses the marriage picture:

> Then He turned to the heaven when it was smoke, and said unto it and unto the earth: Come both of you, willingly or loth. They said: We come, obedient.
> Quran 41:11, *Surat Fussilat*, in the rendering after Pickthall.

The verse's picture, contrasted with *The Defense*'s analogy:

| *The Defense*'s analogy | The Quranic picture |
|---|---|
| Heavens as perfect male overflowing onto deficient female earth | Both heaven and earth respond *together* to the One who calls |
| Production by marriage of substance to substance | Production by common obedience to a single originating call |
| Hybrid third produced from splicing perfect with deficient | Each rank, in itself, perfect; chain holds by proportion |

## Sub-chapter four, the name *the doctrine of perfection*

A narrow controversy, a decisive reply. *The Defense* accused *The Correction* of failing to answer a question that *The Correction* never raised.

| Stage | Position |
|---|---|
| *The Defense*'s complaint | *The Correction* did not answer where the name *the doctrine of perfection* falls, whether on the Creator or on complete creativity |
| The author's reply | *The Correction* mentioned nothing of the sort |
| What *The Correction* actually quoted | The saying of the sages: the perfect Creative is complete creativity, and what is perfect was only perfect |
| The author's verdict on the saying | A saying than which none is more correct |
| What the saying obliges by its own logic | The Perfect and the perfected is the Creator Almighty; the name *the doctrine of perfection* is attributed to the Creator |
| Verdict on *The Defense*'s accusation | The author of *The Defense*'s claim here is prejudice against the author of *The Correction* |

The doctrine that lands:

- The word *the doctrine of perfection* belongs to the Creator
- The Creator is the Perfect and the perfected
- The careful reading does not require the convoluted route *The Defense* charges *The Correction* with not having taken
- The doctrine arrives by the saying itself

## Sub-chapter five, the names that are synonyms by addition

A hierarchy. The divine names work by the same architecture as the Intellect-and-Word identity that *Chapter Four* settled.

*The Defense*'s position, stated compactly:

- Creativity is the effect of the Creator
- On the side of the Creator, he and his action are one thing, namely perfection
- The Creative with complete creativity is only perfect
- We do not need to say additionally that the Creator is perfect, or that he is the cause, or that he and the Creative are one thing

The author's reply, stated compactly:

- We do not say the Creator is *complete creativity*, because creativity is itself perfection
- The Creative is the perfection, only perfect
- The Creator is the creativity
- He is the cause, the effect, the perfect, and the perfected

The doctrinal payload:

> These names are synonymous according to the additions, because there is a multiplicity of meanings upon which these names fall, yet there is only one thing.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter five.

The architecture of *synonyms by addition*, made explicit:

| Names that fall | Aspect under which they fall | Side of the named |
|---|---|---|
| The cause | The Creator considered as origin | One thing |
| The effect (al-the effect) | The Creator considered as what proceeds | One thing |
| The perfect | The Creator considered as complete | One thing |
| The perfected | The Creator considered as the consummated | One thing |

The rule: multiplicity is in the *meanings* upon which the names fall. The One upon whom the names fall is *one*. The Creator is *not* a thing with attributes; the Creator is the One upon whom the names fall according to the additions. This is the same architecture *Chapter Four* settled at the close, that the Intellect is the Word, not two things in relation but one thing called by two names according to the aspect.

The author closes with his signature phrase:

> Enough of the length.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter five.

The careful reader knows where to go for the full treatment, *the author's other treatise*, the *well-known book*.

## Sub-chapter six, the universal Soul did not seek

A process refusal. *The Defense* offers a picture of the universal Soul that the author refuses at the root.

*The Defense*'s picture, traced as a process flow:

```
This world is the act of the Soul
   ↓ this world is highly ordered and arranged
The Soul's action is therefore directed
   ↓ direction implies seeking
The Soul sought to reach a noble rank she had not yet attained
   ↓ the seeking culminates in
The human being, the fruit of the Soul's attainment of her end
```

The author's refusal:

> The universal soul, in what is indicated of her, did not seek in her action to reach a rank she had not attained. The universal soul, which is the second, overflows upon this world not to be drawn to it for what is not hers. God enriched her when he originated her, with no need to seek what she was not given.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter six.

The corrected picture of the universal Soul:

| What *The Defense* held | What the author insists |
|---|---|
| The Soul sought a rank she had not attained | The Soul did not seek; God enriched her at origination |
| The Soul's action is the instrument of her self-completion | The Soul's action is the work of giving, not the work of getting |
| The overflow is a search for what she lacks | The overflow is the surplus of her enrichment |
| The human being is the fruit of her seeking | The human being is the rank at which her surplus arrives |

What the refusal protects:

- The chain runs *down*, not up
- The higher does not depend on the lower
- No rank above is *completed by* what occurs below
- *Chapter Three* and *Chapter Four*'s categorical refusal of higher-needs-lower stands

## The human's two sides, the chapter's widest formula

The chapter's central named-axis 2x2. The human being is the rank at which two distinct overflows meet, and the two sides must be held together.

Axes:

- Horizontal: Channel of conveyance, body-channel left vs form-channel right
- Vertical: Source world, natural world below vs higher world above

Quadrants:

| | Body-channel (left) | Form-channel (right) |
|---|---|---|
| **Higher world** (above) | Not the human being's mode; the higher world does not work the body-channel | The second perfection: the consummation of the spiritual image, the knowledge of the Oneness of Allah by means of the *limits*, the precursor to the natural thing, intrinsic to it, enduring |
| **Natural world** (below) | The first perfection: the bodily form prepared, the harvest of the natural ascent, the four-element earth carved into curves, joints, limbs | Not the natural world's mode; the natural world does not convey form-from-above |

The diagonal that the human being occupies: bottom-left and top-right together. The first perfection from below by the body-channel; the second perfection from above by the form-channel. Two sides, one creature.

The author's settled formula:

> Human beings have two sides: one part natural, one part divine. He is not the fruit of this world alone, nor the fruit of that world alone, but the fruit of all the worlds. He is the last in the universes, united in eternity with what has been acquired by time. Therefore it has been said that what came first of them was the last of them.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter six.

Unpacking the deepest line:

| Phrase | What it names |
|---|---|
| The last in the universes | The human being is at the *end* of the chain, beneath which there is no further rank |
| United in eternity | The higher world, the rank that does not pass |
| With what has been acquired by time | The work the soul does in the body in time |
| What came first was the last of them | The first (the Intellect-which-is-the-Word, the originating rank) is the last (the rank at which the harvest arrives); the chain is folded |

The Quranic verse that names the question:

> Did you then think that We had created you in vain, and that you would not be returned to Us?
> Quran 23:115, *Surat al-Mu'minun*, in the rendering after Yusuf Ali and Sahih International.

The verse refuses two pictures at once:

| Picture refused | Whose reading it would underwrite if taken alone |
|---|---|
| Created in vain | *The Correction* taken alone, the human as fruit of this world only |
| Not be returned to Us | The materialist conclusion *The Defense* charged *The Correction* with |

The verse's answer: the human being was *not* created in vain; the human being *will* be returned. The returning is *what* the human being is *for*.

The chapter's closing prayer:

> Whoever knew the houses of the Prophets, the guardians, the imams, and the righteous, may God's prayers be upon them, from that, then he is ready for salvation. May God gather us with our pure loyalists in the Abode of Holiness, Jerusalem.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter six.

The prayer's architecture, made explicit:

| Phrase | What it names |
|---|---|
| Knew the houses of the Prophets, the guardians, the imams, the righteous | The work of the believer in time, to know the *limits* |
| The *limits* | The *speaker-prophet*, the *guardians* (the *awsiya*), the imams, the righteous |
| Ready for salvation | The reception is the readiness; the readiness is the second perfection's harvest |
| *the Abode of Holiness, Jerusalem* | The House of the Holy, the abode at the brink of the upper world |

## Sub-chapter seven, order, system, and the First's metaphorical action

An annotated structure. The First Creator's action is dissected and shown to be unlike the action of any *doer* in the lower ranks.

*The Defense*'s general principle, and its limits:

| Premise of *The Defense* | The author's distinction |
|---|---|
| Every doer does what he does in pursuit of what he wishes to achieve | True of doers in motion and order |
| Especially when the action is from the doer in movement, in order, and in affirmation | True of doers in motion and order |
| Therefore the universal Soul must be pursuing something | False; the universal Soul did not seek (sub-chapter six) |
| And by extension, this rule applies to the First Creator | False; the First's action is metaphorical, not in motion nor in order |

The First Creator's action, annotated:

| Aspect | What it is |
|---|---|
| Mode | Metaphorical, not in motion nor in order |
| Internal structure | The first is the last; the beginning of it is the middle, and the middle of it is the end |
| Order in the First | None |
| System in the First | None |
| Relation to the order below | The systems and order in what comes after *benefit from* the lights of what is not in order or system, that is the First Intellect |

The author's exact statement:

> This is not necessary for the First Creator and Originator, because the action there is metaphorical, not in motion nor in order. Rather, the first is the last; the beginning of it is the middle, and the middle of it is the end. There is no order in it, no system; rather, the systems and order in what comes after benefit from the lights of what is not in order or system, that is the First Intellect.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter seven.

The Soul's relation to the Intellect, traced:

```
Soul united with the Intellect
   ↓ sees as the Intellect sees
Perceives the forces of things as a whole, without divisions of order and system
   ↓ requires pushing back
Deformed religious things of dark nature (corrupted doctrines mistaking the lower for the higher)
   ↓ if pressed back, the union holds
Soul retains the Intellect's character
```

The polemical pressure turned back on *The Defense*:

| The author's questions to *The Defense* | What the questions expose |
|---|---|
| How can order and arrangement exist only from the Intellect, if the Soul is unaffected by the Intellect | *The Defense*'s framing makes the Soul into something less than what she is |
| How can general things be preferred only by the Intellect, if the Soul stands off from the Intellect's effects | The framing puts the speaking Soul on the footing of the animals (which have only the sensitive soul) |
| Why has God lifted knowledge from the Soul until the Intellect's effects bear upon her | The framing effectively lifts knowledge from the Soul; that *The Defense* cannot have meant |

The closing doctrinal turn:

> We have already said that the issue does not lie with the actors: not every actor seeks by his action to attain a goal or purpose, which one might appreciate by saying that the First Intellect itself is not a vehicle, so it is orderly and arranged. But as for perception, the intellect perceives only the truth.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Five, sub-chapter seven.

The architecture's final formula:

| What the chain does NOT run by | What the chain runs by |
|---|---|
| Seeking | Perception |
| Acquisition | Overflow |
| Marriage of perfect with deficient | Emanation |

The Intellect is not a *vehicle*, not an instrument *in order to* something. The Intellect *is* what it is, perfectly, in its place. From it the order and arrangement of the lower ranks unfold. The Intellect *perceives the truth*; the Soul in union with the Intellect *sees* what the Intellect sees; the human being, fruit of all the worlds, *receives* what the Soul has perceived.

## The chapter's settled formula

A two-vantage summary. The seven sub-chapters land on one doctrine.

| What "fruit of this world" denotes | What "fruit of that world" denotes |
|---|---|
| Half-truth; the bodily form is the harvest of the natural ascent | Half-truth; the spiritual image is the harvest of the higher overflow |
| Taken alone, the materialist conclusion | Taken alone, the body becomes incidental |
| First perfection: the form prepared to receive the three souls | Second perfection: the consummation of the spiritual image, the precursor that endures |
| Channel of the bodies | Channel of the form |

Neither half taken alone gives the human being. The settled formula, in compact form:

```
Human being = fruit of all the worlds
   = last in the universes
   = united in eternity with what has been acquired by time
   = one part natural, one part divine
```

The doctrinal load the chapter has been protecting, restated as architectural rules:

- The chain runs by emanation, not by marriage of perfect-and-deficient
- Each rank, in itself, is perfect; deficiency is in proportion when ranks are added
- The divine names are synonyms by addition: multiplicity of meanings, one named
- The universal Soul did not seek; God enriched her at origination
- The First Creator's action is metaphorical, not in motion nor in order
- The believer's work is to know the houses of the *limits*, ready for salvation

## The Sufi convergence, the same architecture in compressed form

A visual metaphor. Five centuries and a continent apart, the Shadhili tradition and the Fatimid *the call* converge on the same picture of the *fruit*.

> Bury your existence in the earth of obscurity, for whatever sprouts forth, without first being buried, flowers imperfectly.
> a later teacher, *Hikam*, Aphorism 11.

The mapping:

| Image in the aphorism | Architecture in the chapter |
|---|---|
| The earth of obscurity | The first perfection, the bodily form, the natural composite, the field |
| Burial of existence in the earth | The bodily life as the field in which the soul does its work |
| What sprouts forth | The second perfection, the consummation of the spiritual image |
| Flowers imperfectly without first being buried | The work that is acquired by time and united in eternity requires the bodily field |

The two perfections held in one image:

```
Higher world (eternity)
   ↑ what endures, the harvest
Spiritual image, consummated by work in the body
   ↑ requires
The bodily field, the earth of obscurity
   ↑ given by
Natural world (time)
```

The Shadhili master and the Fatimid teacher converge: the burial in the bodily field is what makes the harvest of the higher world possible. The two perfections are one architecture, in which the lower is the *condition* of the higher's harvest.

## What comes next: motion, stillness, and the substrate

A handoff hierarchy. *Chapter Five* having settled the *whom*, *Chapter Six* takes up the *how* of the form.

| Chapter | Settled | What is carried forward |
|---|---|---|
| Chapter Five | The human being is the fruit of all the worlds, the rank at which two overflows meet | The architecture of *sacred law*, of *walaya*, of the *Ma'ad*, rests on this rank |
| Chapter Six | Motion, stillness, and image; the substrate of *prime matter* and the form impressed upon it | How the natural composite that the chain reaches comes to be the natural composite at all |

The reader carries forward three settled claims:

- The human being is the rank at which the chain comes to its harvest
- The architecture of *sacred law* and the *Ma'ad* rests on this rank
- The work the soul does in the body in time is the precursor to the natural thing, intrinsic to it, and which endures

The closing image the chapter leaves with the reader: the two reformers arguing across decades, one with his *fruit of this world*, the other with his *fruit of the higher world*. The author, generations later, arriving at the third word: *fruit of all the worlds*. The believer who knows the houses, ready for salvation, gathered in *the Abode of Holiness, Jerusalem*. The architecture holds. The doctrine is settled. The human being stands, fruit of all the worlds, last in the universes, united in eternity with what has been acquired by time.
