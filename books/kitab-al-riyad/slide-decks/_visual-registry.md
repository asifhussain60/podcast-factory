# Visual Registry — Kitab al-Riyad

Per-book visual conventions for the 15-chapter Kitab al-Riyad slide-deck cohort. Established 2026-05-23 from the Slide Deck Challenger's cross-cohort review of EP01–EP15. The Challenger's Architectural Pass 4 (Cross-Episode Consistency) reads this file. Any deck that breaks a convention here must update the registry to record the deliberate change, or be flagged inconsistent.

The conventions are organized into three sections: **Entities** (named individuals and positions), **Pairings** (recurring two-term relations), and **Motifs** (recurring visual structures like the impossible quadrant). 

---

## Entities

### Jonathan, author of *The Correction* (al-Islah)
- **Visual convention**: left column in every contrast pair; left node in every chain showing the dispute. Color cue (when NotebookLM allows): cool blue.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §5 (Jonathan-vs-Samuel central dispute).
- **Reason**: Jonathan is the position the author al-Kirmani defends and refines. The author reads from his side; placing him left mirrors the author's editorial orientation.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP02, EP03, EP04, EP05, EP06, EP07, EP08, EP09, EP10 (background), EP14 (silence as critique).

### Samuel, author of *The Defense* (al-Nusra)
- **Visual convention**: right column in every contrast pair; right node in every chain showing the dispute. Color cue: warm red.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §5.
- **Reason**: Samuel is the position the author refutes. Right-side placement throughout the cohort lets the listener pattern-match Samuel = the contested view.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP02, EP03, EP04, EP05, EP06, EP07, EP08, EP09.

### The author, al-Kirmani (Hujjat al-Iraqayn)
- **Visual convention**: centered or below the disputants when shown adjudicating; positioned as a third row in three-position tables (Position C). When the author speaks in his own voice (EP14, EP10), no positional contrast is needed.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §2 (three-position stage of the book).
- **Reason**: Adjudicator role. Centered placement signals "verdict between the two."
- **Episodes referencing**: all 15.

### First Intellect
- **Visual convention**: left in every pair (higher rank). The "cause" side of every cause-effect diagram. Top of every hierarchy when paired with Soul.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §6 (identity-vs-rank 2x2; First Intellect anchors the top-left quadrant of perfection-in-itself).
- **Reason**: Higher rank is left throughout the cohort (established EP01).
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP02, EP03, EP05, EP06, EP07, EP08, EP09, EP11, EP14.

### Soul (Universal Soul, the Second Emanation)
- **Visual convention**: right of First Intellect in every pair (lower rank, same kind). Bottom of the cause-effect chain when paired.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §6 + §11 (sun-and-moon principle).
- **Reason**: Same-kind-lower-rank convention from EP01.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP02, EP03, EP04, EP05, EP06, EP07, EP08, EP09.

### Speaker (*natiq*)
- **Visual convention**: left in any Speaker-Foundation pair (higher rank).
- **First defined in**: EP01 §11 (sun-and-moon principle, alongside other higher/lower pairs); fully developed EP12, EP13.
- **Reason**: Speaker is the prophetic bearer of revelation; positioned higher than the Foundation who guards it.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP12, EP13, EP14 (background).

### Foundation (*asas*)
- **Visual convention**: right of Speaker (lower rank, same kind).
- **First defined in**: EP01 §11; developed EP12, EP13.
- **Reason**: Same-kind-lower-rank convention.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP12, EP13.

### Imam, Imam al-Hakim, Imam Ali, Imam Hasan (the lineage)
- **Visual convention**: when shown as a genealogy chain, top-down or left-right with explicit arrows; the ruling Imam of the author's time at the chain's end (bottom or right). Honorific titles (peace and blessings upon him / them) applied once per figure per deck (R-HONORIFIC-ONCE).
- **First defined in**: EP01 §1 (the author's invocation chain).
- **Reason**: Lineage is directional. The chain runs from source to the present.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP12, EP13.

### The Qa'im
- **Visual convention**: end-of-cycle node in any cycle diagram; the figure that completes the prophetic cycle rather than abrogating it.
- **First defined in**: EP13 §6 (Qa'im raising the laws as completion not abrogation).
- **Reason**: EP13 explicitly settles the role; the convention is "Qa'im completes, not abrogates" rendered as the chain's culminating node.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP13.

### Pen (*qalam*) / Tablet (*lawh*) / Writer (*katib*)
- **Visual convention**: three-tier vertical architecture. Pen at top (the active originating cause), Tablet middle (the receiving substrate), Writer below or to the side (the agent who relates the two). When rendered as a process flow, Pen → Tablet → Writer reads top-to-bottom.
- **First defined in**: EP11 §5 (Pen-Tablet-Writer architecture from Quran 54:49).
- **Reason**: The audio source establishes the three-tier metaphysical architecture; the registry locks vertical positioning.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP11.

## Pairings

### Sun / Moon
- **Visual convention**: Sun left (higher rank), Moon right (lower rank). Both within one orbit (*falak*) when rendered as concentric or shared-space layout.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §11 (sun-and-moon principle).
- **Reason**: Same kind, differing rank — the master pair of the cohort.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP02 (sub-ch 11), EP03.

### Day / Night
- **Visual convention**: Day left (higher rank), Night right.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §11.
- **Reason**: Same convention as Sun/Moon.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01.

### Body / Sperm
- **Visual convention**: Body higher (left or top), Sperm lower (right or bottom).
- **First defined in**: EP01 §11; explicit in EP03 sub-ch 24.
- **Reason**: The body is the formed, the sperm is the unformed-but-bearing-form. Higher form = left.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP03.

### Male / Female
- **Visual convention**: Male left, Female right in any pair-by-rank diagram. (This is rank-positional, not normative.)
- **First defined in**: EP01 §11; developed EP03 sub-ch 27.
- **Reason**: The audio source uses the pair to carry rank-without-dispossession; left = higher rank convention.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP03.

### *Qada* (estimation) / *Qadar* (detail)
- **Visual convention**: *Qada* left (the original estimation, higher in the order-of-decree), *Qadar* right (the detail, the elaboration). Or top-down for sequential rendering: *Qada* above, *Qadar* below.
- **First defined in**: EP11 §3-4 (central qada/qadar contrast).
- **Reason**: The chapter establishes qada as the original measure and qadar as its temporal elaboration. Left/top = upstream.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP11.

### Intellect / Time
- **Visual convention**: Intellect left/above (upstream), Time right/below (downstream). The author corrects Jonathan's claim that "Intellect and time are one" — the registry reflects the correction: they are NOT on the same rank.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §9 (Intellect-and-time dependency order).
- **Reason**: Audio source's explicit doctrinal correction.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP05, EP09.

### Cause / Effect (general)
- **Visual convention**: Cause left, Effect right; cause-ending-in-effect arrows point left-to-right; effect-ending-in-cause arrows point right-to-left.
- **First defined in**: EP05 (intellect-encompasses / soul-moves contrast); EP09 (cause-effect proportions).
- **Reason**: Standard directional convention; locked here for consistency across the cohort.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP05, EP09.

### Adam / Noah (Shariah parallel)
- **Visual convention**: Adam top or left (first Speaker), Noah bottom or right (second Speaker). When shown as a Shariah-establishment contrast pair, Adam = Column A, Noah = Column B.
- **First defined in**: EP12 §17 (Noah's establishment of Shariah parallel for Adam).
- **Reason**: Sequential first-Speaker / next-Speaker rendering.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP12, EP13.

### *The Correction* (al-Islah, Jonathan's book) / *The Defense* (al-Nusra, Samuel's book) / *The Harvest* (al-Mahsul, the target)
- **Visual convention**: three-book triangle when shown together. *The Correction* left, *The Defense* right, *The Harvest* center or below (the target both responded to). When rendered as a hierarchy: *The Harvest* at top (the contested original), *The Correction* and *The Defense* below as opposed responses.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §2 (situation that prompted the book).
- **Reason**: The whole-book frame requires this three-book schema.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP14.

## Motifs

### The impossible quadrant
- **Visual convention**: in any named-axis 2x2 where one quadrant is logically impossible per the audio chapter's argument, label that quadrant explicitly as IMPOSSIBLE with a one-line reason. Never leave the quadrant blank or merely shaded out. The label is the structural commitment.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §6 (the Identity + Deficient quadrant of the central 2x2: impossible because nothing exists if deficient in itself).
- **Reason**: The cohort uses the impossible quadrant as a memorability hook; the label converts a 4-quadrant grid into an argument made spatial.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP05 (overflow 2x2; withholding-at-limits labeled impossible), EP07 (capacity 2x2 with impossible top-left), EP08 (two-sides 2x2 with off-diagonal explicitly marked "not the human's mode"), EP11 ("flee from qada to qadar" 2x2).

### The cascade dismantling
- **Visual convention**: when an opponent's argument has multiple chained moves (Move 1 → Move 2 → Move 3 → Move 4), each dependent on the previous, render as a directed vertical chain with the author's counter to each move in a parallel right column. Breaking Move 1 cascades; the right column shows the cascade.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §7 (*Samuel's* attack architecture across the first ten sub-chapters).
- **Reason**: Captures dependency, not just enumeration. The author's strategy is to break the foundation move and let the rest fall.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP01, EP02, EP14 (with each of the 16 refutations as cascade nodes).

### The concentric layers (apophatic register)
- **Visual convention**: when the audio source describes nested degrees of meaning, render as concentric circles or concentric layers. The innermost is the closest to silence / *tawhid*; outer rings carry derivative meanings.
- **First defined in**: EP15 §7 (closing apophatic concentric layers).
- **Reason**: Audio source's own metaphor (the *Imam's* dark-path / deep-ocean / secret). Concentric layout makes the apophatic structure spatial.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP11 (Imam's dark-path/deep-ocean/secret concentric); EP15 (closing).

### The Refutation Grid (polemic-source pattern)
- **Visual convention**: when the chapter consists of N refutations of N opposing propositions, render as a single N-row by 4-column matrix: Proposition (the opposed claim) | Why it fails | What it implies | Author's counter. The matrix becomes the chapter's spine.
- **First defined in**: EP14 (16-row Refutation Grid covering sub-chapters 1–16).
- **Reason**: When the source IS a refutation chapter, the matrix is the most compact rendering. Avoids 16 separate H2 blocks that would dilute.
- **Episodes referencing**: EP14.

### Higher rank = left convention (universal)
- **Visual convention**: in every pair (Sun/Moon, Speaker/Foundation, Intellect/Soul, Day/Night, etc.), the higher-rank entity occupies the LEFT or TOP position. Lower-rank entity occupies RIGHT or BOTTOM. This is invariant across the cohort.
- **First defined in**: EP01 §11 (sun-and-moon principle establishes the universal directional rule).
- **Reason**: Without a fixed convention, listeners cannot pattern-match across decks. The convention is structural, not normative.
- **Episodes referencing**: all 15.

### Honorifics applied once per figure (R-HONORIFIC-ONCE)
- **Visual convention**: each named figure receives their full honorific (peace and blessings be upon him / peace be upon him / may God have mercy on him) ONCE per deck at first mention. Subsequent references use the bare name.
- **First defined in**: EP01 (Prophet's first mention with full salutation; subsequent references bare).
- **Reason**: Visual + auditory hygiene — repeated honorifics fatigue the slide and the listener.
- **Episodes referencing**: all 15.

### Arabic transliterations italicized, never phoneticized inline (R-PHONETICS-OUT)
- **Visual convention**: Arabic terms in italics inside the deck source (e.g., *taswiya*, *qada*, *kamal*). Phonetic guides live ONLY in the framing's pronunciation block (if present), NEVER in the deck source.
- **First defined in**: EP01 throughout.
- **Reason**: Slide rendering cleanliness. Phonetic parens in slides look like data.
- **Episodes referencing**: all 15.

### Verbatim Quranic verses preserved in blockquote with attribution
- **Visual convention**: every Quranic verse (and named hadith / Imam saying) appears as a blockquote with translation and surah:verse attribution on the next line. Format: `> *transliteration*` / `> *translation*` / `> Quran X:Y` (or `> *Source*, Saying/Sermon N`).
- **First defined in**: EP01 throughout.
- **Reason**: NotebookLM renders blockquoted citations as slide-quotable assets; attribution lines anchor the citation.
- **Episodes referencing**: all 15.

## How the registry updates

When a new chapter introduces a recurring entity, pairing, or motif that earns 2+ episode appearances, the Worker proposes a registry entry. The Challenger's Architectural Pass 4 reads this file; inconsistencies between a new deck and the registry are flagged.

If an episode deliberately breaks a convention here (e.g., placing Samuel left instead of right because the audio source argues a specific point), the deck must include a brief note explaining the deviation, AND the registry should be updated to record the exception or the new convention.

---

## What is NOT in the registry

- Single-occurrence entities (mentioned in only one chapter) — not yet stable enough to lock a convention.
- Style preferences not derived from the audio sources (e.g., specific colors, typefaces) — NotebookLM handles those.
- Conventions for diagrams that DON'T recur (one-off process flows specific to a single chapter) — not registry-worthy.

The registry is the cross-cohort consistency layer. Per-chapter visual choices not relevant to other chapters stay in their own deck.
