# Slide Deck Framing, EP08: The Human as Fruit of the Worlds

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio episode. They have not read *The Correction* or *The Defense*.

## Core Principle

Each slide must add visual information the audio cannot carry. Slides exist to make the two reformers' positions simultaneous, to render the human being's two-sides as one spatial 2x2, and to show how the same rule holds across every sub-chapter.

## Visual Priorities

- The three-position contest: *The Correction* (fruit of this world) versus *The Defense* (fruit of the higher world) versus the author (fruit of all the worlds).
- The human being's two-sides as a named-axis 2x2: source-world vs channel of conveyance.
- The divine names as a synonyms-by-addition hierarchy.
- The emanation-versus-marriage contrast pair on production.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a chapter or sub-chapter title.
- No literal illustrations: no fruit, no trees, no calligraphy, no mosques, no scholar portraits.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams. Every diagram has axes, nodes, or rows.
- No slides that restate one sentence the audio already speaks.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide must add visual information not present in the audio.
- For the human's two-sides, use a 2x2 with both axes named.
- Place *The Correction* to the left and *The Defense* to the right across every contrast pair.
- When the chain is shown, arrows run downward by emanation, never sideways by marriage.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
