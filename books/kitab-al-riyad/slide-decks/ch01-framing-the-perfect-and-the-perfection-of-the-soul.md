# Slide Deck Framing, EP01: The Perfect and the Perfection of the Soul

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio episode. They have not read *The Correction*, *The Defense*, or *The Harvest*. The deck must do what the audio cannot.

## Core Principle

Each slide must add visual information the audio cannot carry. Slides exist to make Jonathan-versus-Samuel simultaneous, to render identity-versus-rank as one spatial 2x2, and to show the book's ten-chapter architecture in one glance.

## Visual Priorities

- The Jonathan-versus-Samuel dispute as a side-by-side contrast pair, attribute by attribute.
- The identity-versus-rank distinction as a named-axis 2x2, with the impossible quadrant explicitly labeled.
- The book's ten-chapter architecture as a hierarchy table, referee mode versus author's own voice.
- The sun-and-moon principle as concentric pairs sharing one orbit: same kind, differing rank.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a chapter or section title.
- No literal illustrations: no calligraphy stock images, no mosques, no scholar portraits.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams. Every diagram has axes, nodes, or rows.
- No slides that restate one sentence the audio already speaks.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide must add visual information not present in the audio.
- Show the relationships between entities, not a list of them.
- For identity-versus-rank, use a 2x2 with both axes named and the impossible quadrant labeled.
- Place the higher-rank entity to the left in every pair; maintain across the deck.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
