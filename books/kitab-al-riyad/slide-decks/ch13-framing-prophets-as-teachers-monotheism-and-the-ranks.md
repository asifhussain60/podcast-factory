# Slide Deck Framing, EP13: Prophets as Teachers, Monotheism, and the Ranks

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio. They have not read *The Correction* or *The Defense*.

## Core Principle

Each slide adds visual information the audio cannot carry. Slides render the cycles of knowledge and ignorance as one arc, the seven lines and six aradas as one architecture, and the three readings of that architecture side by side.

## Visual Priorities

- The seven-lines-and-six-aradas architecture as an annotated structure: seven lines, six transverse aradas, the seventh with no arada after it.
- The three readings (the book *The Correction*, the book *The Defense*, the author) as a comparison matrix.
- The cycles of knowledge and ignorance as a process flow looping from pre-Adam through six speakers to the Qa'im and back.
- The two routes to monotheism, innovation versus acquisition, as a contrast pair.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a sub-chapter title or verse reference.
- No literal illustrations: no calligraphy, no arks, no mountains.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams.
- No slides that restate one sentence the audio speaks.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide adds visual information not present in the audio.
- Draw the seven-lines-and-six-aradas geometry explicitly; the seventh line has no arada after it.
- For the three readings, use a comparison matrix with rows for *lines*, *aradas*, and *verdict on Adam's sacred law*.
- Show relationships between entities, not a list of them.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
