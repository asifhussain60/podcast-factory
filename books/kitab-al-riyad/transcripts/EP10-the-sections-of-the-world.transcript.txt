Amit al-Din Ahmad steps directly into a severe textual dispute between 2 earlier philosophical works, The Correction and The Defense, to settle the map of the entire cosmos. The central problem he tackles is determining the exact sections of the natural world and where the human being sits within that continuous chain of existence. We are examining how prime matter and form constitute the physical body and how humanity's specific place among the generated beings dictates how the entire chain of receivership functions. Well, the core debate starts with a major claim from the correction. That text argued for a very clean four-part world. Four distinct sections. Yes. And in that system, humankind stands entirely separate from the rest of creation. It claims humanity has absolutely no peer. It treats the human as a completely different type of substance, severing our connection to the physical environment. It isolates us from the soil and the animals. Yes. It caters to a human desire to view ourselves as an isolated genus. But Ahmad rejects that isolation. He delivers the verdict that the world is not simply 4 sections. It is a vast spectrum. Running from the inert mineral core. Running from the inert mineral core all the way out to the encompassing ocean. Within the spectrum, the human is not an isolated genus at all. The human is a distinct rank residing squarely within the animal genus. But defined specifically by the possession of the speaking soul. Yes. Okay, so mapping our exact location in this cosmic map is essential. It helps us understand the profound theological and moral stakes of where humanity actually belongs. He details the doctrine of pairing by occasion. The human is the plastic part of the world. Plastic meaning adaptable. Yes. When a person stands near a perfume seller, they take on the pleasant scent regardless of whether they purchase anything.
