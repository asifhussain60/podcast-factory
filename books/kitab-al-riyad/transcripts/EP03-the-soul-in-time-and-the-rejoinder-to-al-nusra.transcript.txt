Samuel wrote his book, The Defense, with a single unyielding target in his sights. He aimed to prove the soul is inherently deficient. His entire logic rests on one observation regarding the cosmic hierarchy. The soul sits below the intellect. Yes, because the soul occupies a lower rank, he argues, it must be flawed in its very essence. He assumes that a secondary position demands A defective identity. Hamid al-Din Ahmad sets out to dismantle that exact assumption. He writes to defend an earlier thinker, Jonathan, who authored the correction. And the final movement of Hamid al-Din Ahmad's argument establishes A vital philosophical distinction. He proves the soul is perfect in itself. It is only deficient in rank. The position it holds does not corrupt the reality of what it is. That is correct. Your mission here listening to this discussion is to trace how the author takes apart Samuel's arguments piece by piece. We are going to explore how mistaking a lower rank for a flawed identity fundamentally distorts the understanding of existence. Samuel tries to force the soul's supposed deficiency into natural and religious hierarchies. The author refuses that mapping at every turn. He exposes the logical fractures in Samuel's thinking. We see those fractures immediately when Samuel attempts a biological analogy. Samuel argues the relationship between the soul and the intellect is parallel to the relationship between a sperm and a man. Right. He claims the sperm is a man in potency and truth. and it eventually becomes a man in actuality. Following that logic, he says the soul is merely the intellect in potency. He views it as waiting in a deficient state to become perfect by receiving benefits from the intellect. That feels intuitive if we only look at the physical world. We see things grow and develop. But the author delivers a sharp rebuttal by questioning the mechanics of that biology. A sperm is not a miniature man. No, it is not. It does not contain a tiny unformed human waiting to expand. A sperm is raw matter prepared to receive form. It is vacant of forms. Yes, and it only acquires them in stages through a physical process. The author clarifies that the natural soul operates similarly in the physical realm. The natural soul is a preparation. So it is empty of forms and acquires knowledge to become self-fulfilled. Exactly. Yes, that is the mechanism. Samuel confuses an image with a vessel. But the problem goes far deeper than biology. The author points out a severe logical impossibility in Samuel's premise regarding the universal realm. Let us trace the logic there. If the soul were truly just an image of the intellect, the intellect would have to possess an image of itself for the soul to reflect. If the intellect possesses an image of itself, the intellect must split its own essence. It would have the part that is the core essence and the part that is the image. Which splits the indivisible. The intellect's essence is 1. Its existence is purely creative and otherness has no place in it. An image of an image regress cannot even begin in that realm. Samuel's biological analogy collapses when applied to the universal intellect because the universal intellect does not have parts. It does not have an image to cast down into a waiting vessel. Samuel realizes the biological argument fails, so he pivots to religious hierarchy. He attempts to map his theory onto the speaker prophet and the foundation who stands in his place. Samuel argues that because the foundation's rank is below the speaker prophet, the foundation representing the soul in this specific framework must be deficient. The author counters this by looking at the specific actions prescribed in religious doctrine. He anchors his rebuttal in the prophet. its sacrifice of camels. We need to examine the physical act of this slaughter. It consists of delivering iron directly to the heart. The purpose is to ensure no corrupted blood remains in the animal. Yes. How does a physical sacrifice map onto the intellect and the soul? The action seems distant from the cosmic hierarchy Samuel is trying to define. Well, the author explains the mechanism behind the ritual. Blood represents doubt. Iron represents the covenant. I see. Delivering iron to the heart to remove corrupted blood represents the dispelling of doubts and the taking of the covenant. The prophet performed this specific action to indicate something profound about the foundation in the world of intellect. The foundation attained perfection first. Taking the covenant first and then informing the followers of the degree of perfection implies a sequence. It establishes who is 1st and who is second. It does not imply A deficiency in the foundation's ultimate state. That is the core of it. Sequence does not equal deficiency. The author uses another religious structure to solidify this. He looks at the dawn prayer. The dawn prayer consists of two rakahs, 2 units of prayer. In the first rakah, reciting the praise is obligatory. In the second rakah, reciting al-Qanut in place of the praise is obligatory. Both the first and the second rakahs are perfect as units of prayer. They are entirely complete in themselves. The first rakah is not more perfect than the second, simply because it happens earlier. But the only distinction between them is their sequence and existence. One is the beginning, the other is the second. They stand in a rank relation. They do not stand in a perfection relation. Samuel continuously confuses rank with essence. We see Samuel make this exact error when he tries one more structural analogy. He looks at pairs in the natural world and claims that in any given pair, one must be deficient. He claims the male is complete and the female is deficient. I want to look closely at how the author masterfully reframes this. He does not just dismiss the claim. He redefines the mechanism of the relationship. The author states the female is complete in herself. The male is complete in himself. Their inequality is solely a matter of rank when they are joined together. It is a relational sequence. Samuel leaps from observing a lower rank to declaring something deficient in self. He observes the female is in the lower rank when joined with the male and immediately assigns A flawed essence to her core identity. He does this with the foundation. He does this with the soul. It is a relentless misreading of how relationships function. Having exhausted these analogies, Samuel shifts his attack entirely. He moves to an argument grounded in raw human experience. He points out that speaking souls succumb to physical desires. Yes, they are led by nature. They incline to pleasures. They forget their own high origins, their own light and their own splendor. He contrasts this with the intellect, which does not incline to nature or desire it for its own sake. This is a highly persuasive argument on a surface level. We see human frailty every single day. We see individuals enslaved to natural appetites and destructive impulses. When Samuel points to this frailty the soul is flawed, it resonates with lived experience. It feels true. But the author exposes this as a massive category error. Mixing 2 distinct existents into one concept is the entire engine of Samuel's attack here. Samuel deliberately confuses the sensual soul with the successor soul. The sensual soul is the one that struggles in the natural world. It is the entity led by desires, persuaded by the onset of ugliness and vulnerable devices. But Jonathan, the thinker the author is defending, was writing about the successor soul. The successor soul is the universal soul. It is the second intellect. It is perfect and complete from the very first instant of its existence. It confers perfection on the lesser souls that descend through nature. The author maps this vital distinction onto the Quran's description of the soul in its various stages. The text does not treat the soul as a monolith. It outlines 3 distinct conditions. There is the soul inclined to evil, there is the self-reproaching soul, and there is the soul at peace. Samuel take the flaws and frailties of the very first stage, the soul inclined to evil, and forces them onto the universal reality of the third stage. We can look at the specific Quranic evidence. the author relies on to refuse this conflation. In the chapter of the Heights, the text speaks of the descendants of Adam testifying of themselves. This describes the soul in a state of accounting and struggle. But in the chapter of the Dawn, the text addresses a completely different state. It says, soul at peace, return to your Lord, well pleased and pleasing. The grammar of the text itself refuses to blend these distinct states into a single, universally flawed entity. This separation of states is present across different centuries and different idioms. Jalal al-Din Rumi opens his Mathnawi with the famous image of the reed cut from the reed bed. The reed is complaining of separation. It laments its distance from the source. That reed is the natural soul. It is lamenting its distance from the higher world. And its path home requires rigorous knowledge and discipline. That reed is not the universal soul. No. The universal soul does not lament a lost perfection because its perfection was complete from the first instant. It was never cut away into a state of deficiency. This distinction leads directly into the mechanics of forgetting. Samuel insists the soul is deficient because it forgets its world and does not remember it. The author asks a fundamental question regarding the nature of existence. How can the universal soul forget when forgetting is an accident? We need to define what an accident means in this philosophical context. It is not a mistake or a crash. An accident is a condition or an event that happens to a substance. It is a change in state. The author argues accidents only happen to things hovering between potentiality and actuality. That hovering state, the process of becoming, of changing, of gaining and losing, belongs exclusively to the bodily realm. It belongs to the natural world. The universal soul belongs to the creative world. It is fully actualized from its beginning. Because it is eternal and fully actualized, it cannot accept accidents. It has no potential state to fall back into. Therefore, it cannot forget. Forgetting requires a timeline. It presupposes a time of knowing and a subsequent time of not knowing. The eternal admits no such alternation. If we are talking about natural human souls forgetting, Samuel's claim holds up. Natural souls forget. But that has zero bearing on the universal soul. The author actually springs a gentle trap for Samuel regarding this exact point in Samuel's own writing. Yes, in Samuel's later book, Amikalid, specifically in Proposition 44, Samuel proves natural souls cannot come from the world of intellect. That directly contradicts his entire argument in the defense. He spends the defense arguing the universal soul is deficient because human souls forget. Then, in his later book, he proves those human souls did not come from that high intellectual realm in the 1st place. He severed his own connection. The author notes this severe contradiction. He points out Samuel's later book was composed after the defense. Rather than gloating over the logical victory or mocking the inconsistency, the author simply prays for Samuel. Samuel was his former teacher. The author asks God to seat him with the pure loyalists. He lets the contradiction stand as proof and responds with grace. Samuel tries another angle using the philosopher's fourfold division. He notes all things exist in four senses, the object, the idea, the word, or the writing. Samuel claims the intellect encompasses objects themselves, while the soul only grasps the ideas presented to it. He uses this division to prove the soul is fundamentally incomplete compared to the intellect, acting merely as a receiver of secondary forms. The author rebuts this by explaining the true purpose of that schema. The fourfold division describes how things exist for natural souls. Objects exist outside the soul in the physical world. Ideas are forms in the mind. Words represent those ideas in speech. Writing solutes as a deputy for words. This is a description of acquisition in the natural world. It maps how a natural mind learns. Using that schema to prove the universal soul's inferiority is misreading the philosopher and misapplying the tool. Samuel takes a framework designed for natural souls acquiring knowledge and forces it onto the successor soul, which is already complete. The universal soul does not need to move from object to idea to word to writing. It does not acquire. The final major dispute between them rests on the nature of time itself. Samuel claims the intellect and time were created together at once. He believes if the soul's actions happen in time, that duration proves its deficiency. He thinks being subject to time means being subject to wear, delay, and sequence. The author argues it is impossible for anything to be co-prisoned with the first intellect. We have to define time to understand why. Time is a measure of motion. Motion belongs to the natural world. It belongs to the physical heavens and the spheres. We turn to the quote from Ali, the commander of the faithful. from the opening sermon of The Path of Eloquence to grasp this. He states, his being preceded times. We must be careful with the word preceded here. Preceding time does not mean existing earlier on a standard timeline. Eternity is not simply a longer stretch of time stretching backward forever. Eternity is a rank without earlier or later. The first intellect does not lie alongside time as one thing alongside another. To ask whether the intellect was with time or before time or after time is to force the category of natural duration into a realm where it simply does not exist. I want to push on the concept of the first intellect acting alone. The first intellect is independent in action. There is nothing within it other than itself. It does not require something existing alongside it to act upon. That independence is crucial. The first intellect exists in a self-completed motion. It does not learn commands in a sequence. Because it acts without needing time, It brings things in the world below from potency into actuality. Only the complete can confer completion. The first intellect brings out the existent by potentiality through its overflow. The universal soul, the second intellect, is perfect in itself and acts as the conduit for this overflow into the natural world. It operates above the constraints of temporal delay. This brings us to the resolution of the author's philosophical defense. He notes philosophers in their own times often erred because they relied solely on their own intellects. They pursue these massive questions of origination, hierarchy, and time without the balance of revelation. They reached divergent conclusions based solely on what their individual frameworks indicated. The author contrasts this reliance on unguided intellect with the clarity that comes from the House of Revelation. The balance of religion corrects the errors of those who try to map the universal realm using only the crude tools of the natural world. When Samuel used biology or physical hierarchy or human frailty to judge the universal soul, he failed because his tools were too small for the subject. The chapter does not end with a triumphant argument slam. It ends with a prayer. The author asks God to enlighten intellects, to seal affairs with happiness, and to grant success in good words and good deeds. That prayer reveals the ultimate purpose of this entire theological debate. The doctrine is preparation for lived action. Establishing the soul is perfect in itself and only deficient in rank is not just a theoretical victory. It is the clarity that allows individuals to move through the natural world. It provides the framework for souls to attain their second perfection through time and acquisition. When you understand the successor soul is perfect, you understand your own natural soul's struggles are temporary conditions of the natural realm. They are not a reflection of a permanently flawed higher reality. How often do you conflate your current struggles or your rank in a given hierarchy with your ultimate unchanging self? Consider what changes when you view your own forgetting not as a permanent deficiency of your core identity, but as a temporary condition of the natural world.
