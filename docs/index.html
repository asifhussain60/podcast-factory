The Problem

These books deserve an audience. Getting there is hard.

Classical Islamic scholarship spans hundreds of texts written in dense, technical Arabic across a millennium. Most have never been translated. Fewer still have been made accessible in any modern format.

"A 12th-century treatise on the soul, written in Arabic, locked inside a scan of a photocopy of a manuscript — the podcast factory turns that into a conversation two people can listen to on a walk."

The traditional path from raw Arabic manuscript to something a modern listener can engage with takes years of specialist translation, editorial work, and academic publishing. Even then, the result is a book — still inaccessible to anyone who doesn't sit down and read it.

Podcast Factory eliminates that bottleneck. It uses Azure cloud services for OCR and translation, and Claude AI for deep scholarly interpretation, to move from PDF to publishable audio script in a matter of hours — without a human translator in the loop for routine episodes.

The result is a podcast episode that reads like a conversation between two knowledgeable hosts who have actually studied the text: they quote it, they explain it in plain English, they surface the ideas that matter, and they cite every reference they make.

How It Works

Eight steps. One PDF in, one podcast series out.

Each step is automated, audited, and reversible. Human review gates sit at the points where editorial judgment matters.
Step 1
📄

Scan & Read

Azure Document Intelligence reads the PDF, even from low-quality manuscript scans.

Step 2
🔤

Translate & Refine

Azure Translator produces a raw English draft. Claude then refines it for scholarly accuracy and fluency.

Step 3
🗣️

Pronounce

Every Arabic proper name is given a phonetic pronunciation so NotebookLM speaks it correctly.

Step 4
📋

Design Episodes

Claude studies the whole book and designs an episode structure — which chapters to combine, what each episode's thesis is.

Step 5
📚

Enrich

Quranic verses and hadith citations in the text are identified, verified, and tagged so they can be cited accurately in the episode.

Step 6
✍️

Author

Claude writes the episode framing — the contextual guide that shapes how NotebookLM presents the material.

Step 7
🔍

Challenge

A separate AI reviewer checks the episode against 15 scholarly standards. It rewrites anything that fails until all checks pass.

Step 8
🎙️

Publish

The finished episode bundle passes 12 publish gates, then uploads to NotebookLM, which generates the final audio conversation.

The Intelligence Layer

Citations that are verified, not invented.

One of the hardest problems in AI-assisted scholarship is hallucinated references. Podcast Factory solves this with a dedicated knowledge extraction and verification system.

Every book that passes through the pipeline feeds a growing knowledge base — a structured library of Quranic verses, authenticated hadith, and doctrinal concepts, each linked to the chapter and page where it was found. When an episode script is written, the augmenter pulls verified citations from this library and injects them directly. If a citation cannot be verified, it is not used.

📖

Quranic Verse Extraction

Every Quran reference in the source text is identified by surah and verse number. The canonical Arabic is confirmed before any English rendering appears in the episode.

🕌

Hadith Recognition

Hadith citations are cross-referenced against canonical collections and given their chain of transmission. Episodes cite the collector, not just the content.

🗂️

External Reference Works

Major Islamic reference anthologies — such as the Kashkole, a vast historical collection — can be independently processed and fed into the knowledge base as a source of enrichment.

Nothing ships without passing every gate.

Every episode goes through a convergence loop — a challenger reviews it, a fixer corrects what fails, and the loop repeats until all checks pass or the episode is escalated for human review. Only episodes with a SHIP-READY or SHIP-WITH-CAUTION verdict move forward. After that, twelve publish gates run before the episode is copied to the public catalog.

How It Was Built

Six waves of engineering. One coherent system.

The pipeline was rebuilt from scratch across six focused waves of work, all completed May 2026. Each wave added a capability that the next wave depended on.
A

Foundation

Built the shared infrastructure every other part of the system depends on: a single way to find files, a structured database for knowledge storage, and an archetype registry that tells the pipeline what kind of book it's working with.

B

Intelligence

Added the citation pipeline: extract Quranic and hadith references from every chapter, store them in the knowledge base, deduplicate across books, and automatically inject verified citations into episode scripts.

C

Format Expansion

Extended the system beyond the original scholarly deep-dive format to support three new content types: dramatic dialogues (Play-Novel), recorded lectures (Lecture-Series), and vast encyclopedic works with dozens of epistles (Encyclopedic-Epistolary — specifically for the Rasāʾil).

D

Planning & Dashboard

Built a live planning surface showing the full roadmap, current execution state, and per-book progress — so the entire pipeline is visible at a glance without reading log files.

E

Retroactive & Extended Gates

Added enrichment for already-published books without re-running them through the pipeline. Extended the publish gate from 7 to 12 checks, including UI resilience tests that ensure the planning dashboard doesn't regress.

F

Archetype Completion

Completed the three newest content-type specs — each with a full anti-patterns catalog (what not to do) and an exemplar episode (the gold standard to aim for). The pipeline can now recognize and correctly handle any of seven distinct types of Islamic scholarly text.

What Runs It

Cloud services doing heavy lifting. AI doing judgment.

☁️

Azure Document Intelligence

Scans PDFs of manuscript-quality Arabic text and produces structured, searchable content. Handles multi-column layouts, marginal notes, and low-resolution scans.

🌐

Azure Translator

Produces the first English draft from Arabic. The pipeline then hands this to Claude for scholarly refinement — Azure handles the volume, Claude handles the nuance.

🤖

Claude (Anthropic)

Responsible for all the judgment calls: episode structure, framing authorship, citation verification, quality review, and fixer rewrites. The intellectual labor of the pipeline.

🎙️

Google NotebookLM

Receives the finished episode bundle — source chapters plus framing — and generates the actual audio conversation. The AI hosts are powered by NotebookLM's Audio Overview feature.
