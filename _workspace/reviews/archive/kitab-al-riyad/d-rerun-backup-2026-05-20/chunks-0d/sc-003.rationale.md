`ch05-soul-spirit-and-the-first.txt` covers Bāb 3 (On the judgment among the disputants concerning what is spoken of in regard to soul and nature: whether they are like the First or not) in its entirety — all six fusūl as a single coherent episode. The source slice is the slimmest of any bāb in the treatise (2,762 source words), but the argument is unified around a single delicate question: what does the predicate "born from" actually claim, and where in the originative chain does it properly apply? Fasl 1 opens with al-Islah's two-sealed-worlds picture (light above, dark below, no link) and al-Nusra's continuous-gradient corrective (the soul has two extremes, nature has two ends); al-Kirmani's adjudication grants what each was reaching for (al-Islah's instinct that al-Hayuli does not resemble the Second; al-Nusra's instinct that the chain must be continuous) while refusing both pictures and introducing the corrective language of *emanation at the lower proportion*. Fasl 2 corrects al-Nusra on al-Islah's own terms — the Second has two aspects, neither like the First, but the continuity is by chain-of-strata not by half-light-half-dark hybridity. Fasl 3 demolishes the "two sides" geometry of the soul (what has sides has spatial extension; the non-corporeal Second has no spatial extension) and reframes it as two *proportions* of the same essence (cause to what is below, effect to what is above). Fasl 4 makes the proportion-vs-distance move: al-Hayuli is unlike the Second not by distance but by which proportion of the Second produces it. Fasl 5 corrects al-Nusra's "decrease" predicate: perfection at the second rank is differently perfect, not less perfect. Fasl 6 is the longest and most lyrical of the six — al-Nusra's image of nature as the soul's incomplete telling of the splendor seen above — and al-Kirmani's reply is correspondingly gentle, letting the lyrical core stand while restricting its metaphysical force. There is no thematic seam inside the six fusūl that would justify a split; the entire bāb is the single discrimination of the predicate of resemblance, applied stratum by stratum. The episode is enriched (per Phase 0e) with four Qur'anic anchors (24:35 the Light Verse, 42:11 the unlikeness verse, 38:72 the breathing-in of the spirit, 35:15 the asymmetry of need), three passages from Imam Ali's *Nahj al-Balagha* Sermon 1 and an attributes-paraphrase, and a closing paraphrase in the manner of *al-Sahifa al-Sajjadiyya*. Landed at 7,349 words — mid-band for the extended tier, consistent with the four sibling episodes from Bāb 1 and Bāb 2 (6,595 / 6,695 / 6,113 / 6,825).
