**Chapter Six of the Book of al-Riyad**

In it he speaks of motion, stillness, and form.

**Chapter One of Section Six**

The author of *al-Islah* said:

As for the discussion of bodies and form, they are with respect to motion and stillness like body for them. The first *hayula* (prime matter) is illusory motion in the vehicles, and the *hayulanis* (matter-like things) in a single statement of three. What has been said of that in this book contains much confusion. I say, in brief, that motion and stillness are to *hayula* and form as form and *hayula* are to body — for motion and stillness are gentler than body, and body is denser than soul; likewise motion and stillness are gentler than *hayula* and form, and *hayula* and form are denser than motion and stillness. To represent the gentler with the soul and the denser with the body is appropriate, but to represent the denser with the body and the gentler with the soul is too early. This is clear, and the proof goes with it.

**Chapter Two of Section Six**

Then he required himself to say that the soul is gentler than the body — because the soul is a body, and that is impossible: the soul is not embodied to have density or gentleness, for density and gentleness are specific to(1) that which is a body, just as air, which is a body, is specifically gentle, and earth, which is a body, is specifically dense. The soul is the soul, and the evidence is established that it is not embodied. Once the soul is not embodied, then to say of it that it is gentler or more dense, or to make a likeness on that basis, is impossible.

**Chapter Four of Section Six**

Then he made motion divisible into three parts resembling the body, on the grounds that the body is divided into length, width, and depth. This is impossible. The three sections into which the body is allegedly divided — namely the three dimensions — each one of these three dimensions is not embodied, and is not said to be a body. The motion of each of its sections is itself motion, and is said to be motion. So how is it likened to the body, which is divisible into nine sections, as we have shown in our well-known book *(Milestones of Religion)*, by a motion that is divided into three sections, or six sections? That is far off. And the discussion in this chapter on the part of the author of the harvest, the author of *al-Islah*, and the author of *al-Nusrah* is irregular disorder.

**Chapter Five of Section Six**

The author of *al-Islah* said:

The First Cause, which is creativity, is the word of the Almighty and Exalted. Its form is *kun* (Be), which is two voweled letters (kaf and nun), and the unvoweled letter — the kaf and the nun — of the word stand in the position of body; motion and stillness stand as the soul. This is the example of motion and stillness with the word. The first cause, which is the word, will be the whole world, for example. It must therefore be said that motion and stillness are in all the essences of *al-hayula*, and form is below it in all the worlds, and that the whole of the discussion concerns the three worlds. The first beings are illusory: they are motion and stillness; they are *al-hayula*. Their form is the second *hayula*, which are individuals and the begotten of the first *hayula*. The third *hayulas* are the four composite mothers from the second *hayulas*.

Then we say:

The Houthi and its form are the act of the command, which is creativity, as we have said. *Hayula* is illusory, and so creativity is imaginary; it is shown only in the first creative.

The First [Effect] is imaginal, and He made it motion and stillness; then He made it motion, stillness, and hayula. The image is not these hayulas, but the motions and the stillnesses are the supports, and the image is an essence emanating from the First upon what has been discussed above — including the lower world. Section Seven of Chapter Six.

Then he said:

When he made the First Hayula correspond to the matter that is ibda, the First Hayula is imaginal just as ibda is imaginal, and it shows itself only in the First Creator. He said that this must hold in the presence of the Almighty, because of His pride in that: since ibda had not yet appeared, He became Creative through ibda. This is a grave error, because the doctrine and belief in monotheism is that what is other than Allah Almighty — and He is the Creative One and the Originator — had no existence at all; and even if it had, ibda is nothing other than the Creator Himself. We have built upon this the demonstration sufficient to our argument.
