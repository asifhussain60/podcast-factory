`ch04-first-intellect-as-originator.txt` covers Bāb 2 (On the First Intellect, the First Originator) in its entirety — all nine fusūl as a single coherent episode. The source slice is short (3,730 source words; the smallest of any bāb in the treatise), but the argument is unified: it is one extended exploration of whether motion (haraka) and rest (sukun) apply to the First Intellect, used as the occasion for al-Kirmani's most positive statement of what the First Intellect actually is — the very act of origination, behind which there is only al-Bari. There is no thematic seam inside the nine fusūl that would justify a split; fasl 1 establishes the foundational claim (the First Intellect is not a body, so motion does not apply), fasls 2–5 work out the implications across al-Islah's symmetry, the honor problem, the matter-and-form formula, and al-Nusra's alternative; fasls 6–7 address motion of perfection (heavens, prophet) and the structure of overflow through the hudud; fasls 8–9 close with the ocean image and the disambiguation of "soul moving in a movement of subsisting" between natural-soul and Soul-as-Successor readings. The episode is enriched (per Phase 0e) with four Qur'anic anchors (36:82, 21:33, 17:31, 4:6), one hadith (the Pen), two paraphrased passages from Imam Ali's *Nahj al-Balagha* Sermon 1, and a closing paraphrase from *al-Sahifa al-Sajjadiyya*. Landed at 6,825 words — mid-band for the extended tier, consistent with the three sibling episodes from Bāb 1 (6,595 / 6,695 / 6,113).
