## The Fourth Chapter of the Book of *al-Riyad*

On the judgment between (("the hunters")) and the discussion concerning the soul — whether it has parts and effects.

## Chapter One of Part Four

The author of *al-Islah* said:

According to what the author of *al-Nusra* has established: as for the parts that are in us, they are traces of the First Essence, not parts of it. Our union [with it] is by way of the effects of the First Essence, not by its parts; and the union of the First is not by its parts, because the First is united by the very same Word. The parts that are in us, even though they are not parts of [the First Essence], nor united with it [by way of parts], are nevertheless united with it by its traces — as we have shown of the union of its handiworks with our traces in it. And the union of the First Essence is likewise by the Word, because the First Essence is united by the very Word — until [the First] and the Word became one thing. This is what he said.

The author of *al-Nusra* said:

But the statement (3) of the author of *al-Islah*, in what he said and in his refusing to give the souls the name "part," is along this line: he saw that what are integral are bodies that are inseparable from the nine accidents and are aware of the five senses, whereas the soul is not a body, nor is it overflowing with object [in the manner of bodies]. So he did not see fit to give it the name "part," lest it be run in the course of bodies — so that nothing should match what we have said of it. When he did not assent to that, he said "traces," and named [the souls] by the fact that they are an essence and remain. What is meant by this is that the essence and survival of these souls is by way of the effects of those paradoxical essences which reach them from the side of the *hudud* (limits), who are the mediators — peace be upon them. ## Chapter Two of Part Four

The author of *al-Nusra* said:

That the souls are parts of the universal soul rests on no proof. If [the universal] is the totality of those parts, then the whole is divided into two parts — [and] all these parts [together] — he has been obliged [to say] that the souls are parts of the universal soul. The souls in the abode of this world are sustained by the evidence that has been given before and after. The Second is what is named "soul," whose existence is not in the house of the body — which is the house of the world. And if there is no [universal] in this world to be the Second, [containing] both classes of these souls, and if it is not possible for there to be a whole of these souls, then these are not parts of it.

## Chapter Four of Part Four

After [the discussion of] the parts, [the author of *al-Islah*] gives what he gives to them all together. These souls were parts of the Second — and we found them neither knowing nor wise; not all of them are parts of this category, and a belief like that, to that extent, is great unbelief — we seek refuge in God. For if it is known that the substances of the Messengers (God's prayers be upon them) [come] from it, which is the closest cause of the overflow upon the world below, the souls also (1), if they are not parts of [it], then since these souls are not parts, we say: the souls are of the nature of those paradoxical things whose existence is creative, [whose] appearance and existence are only among the natural and phenomenal worlds. It is a natural force that acquires what is gained from the *hudud* (limits) through obedience and worship (2). So it emits from itself an abstract subsistent image that is like the abstract things in the house of the Hereafter on the one hand, and the opposite of it on the other.

Chapter Five of Section IV

The author of al-Nusrah said:

Likewise, the effects bound to us through our crafting do not accept further effects from us once we have finished our work; nor do they accept the products that are ready to be received from us, except by introducing corruption into them and changing them from their states, (1) because they are not parts of us, nor are they subsisting substances; (2) they are transient accidents. This is what he said.

We say:

What is meant by this is that bodies are limited in what they can accept. They reach a point where they accept nothing further alongside it — like iron heated in fire: once it is fully red, that is its limit; it does not grow hotter. Likewise, a black body does not grow blacker; its blackness may reach a point beyond which it will not be increased. This is part of [the meaning of what he claims], and he is the first to accept what his own statement requires: the effects are not what bring forth, but the receiver — that is, the body subject to the succession of accidents and effects.

We say:

The truth is that the soul is infinite in what it can accept of what he described, and this is one of the proofs that the soul is not embodied. The matter regarding the soul being parts of the Second [Emanation] is not as he held it to be in what he said, claimed, and embraced. The best that can be granted to what he said is this: for those who wish to call them parts, these souls are effects insofar as they are rational. As such, the [universal] Soul and what she has produced are one thing — she is what is hers, distinguished from her offspring only by rank. So it is like the part of a thing in which the whole already exists; in all of them, their nature is in themselves one and the same from this aspect.

Chapter Seven of Section IV

The author of al-Nusrah said:

Also: to say that the rational souls existing in this world are not parts of the universal Soul nullifies prophethood, imamate, guardianship, revelation, and the laws. For it is not permissible for the soul to perceive its influencer, encompass him, or benefit from him; and yet the prophets (peace be upon them) have claimed that they saw the noble upper world, stood upon the spiritual ranks, and benefited from what was sent down upon them in light and manifestation from those above them — the Origin and the three branches — and that they saw the angels. If their pure souls were merely effects of the universal Soul, they would not have been able to grasp what was inspired in them, nor stand upon the spiritual entities that are parts of the universal Soul.

We say:

It has already been said that the souls are not parts or effects, but a second emanation. What is meant by those who say they are effects is that the actions performed through them

The prophets (peace be upon them) were able to perceive these things absent to them from the upper world after they had reached the level of perfection: they came to stand at the limit at its end, and when they returned to themselves they realized that the transcendent selves are one thing in respect of wholeness, with no difference between them except in rank. Chapter Eight of Section IV

The author of al-Nusrah said:

Rather, it is necessary to say that the Intellect is creative through the Word, and the Soul is the image of the Intellect; Nature is born of the Soul, and the rational souls are parts of the universal Soul, so that by this order tawhid, reward and punishment, prophethood, imamate, shari'a, and revelation are upheld.

We say:

The validity of tawhid, reward and punishment, prophethood and imamate, the messengership and the laws drawn from revelation, is not proved from the standpoint that the Intellect is creative through the Word, or that the Soul is the image of the Intellect. It is proved through another aspect of the Intellect that we have explained in our letters and treatises about them. As for the Intellect, it is not permissible to say that it is creative through the Word; the Word must be presented to the Intellect because of the imbalance in its modes (1) [regarding]
