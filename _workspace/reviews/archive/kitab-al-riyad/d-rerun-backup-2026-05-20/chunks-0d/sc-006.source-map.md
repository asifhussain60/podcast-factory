| 6 | Bāb 6 — On Motion, Stillness, and Form (9 fusūl) | ch08-motion-stillness-form-and-image.txt | 2,340w is the second-slimmest bāb in the treatise, below the extended tier band but within reach via enrichment; the nine fusūl form one continuous dialectical movement — fusūl 1–4 dismantle al-Islah's graded-ladder analogy (motion-stillness gentler than hayula-form, hayula-form gentler than body) and al-Nusra's dimensional-resemblance analogy (motion divides in three as body divides in length-width-depth) on the shared metaphysical ground that the soul is not embodied (density and gentleness do not apply to it) and that motion and stillness are *contraries that cannot coexist* while hayula and form are *correlatives that cannot exist apart* (the dyads belong to different logical genera); fusūl 5–7 take up al-Islah's reading of the First Cause as the word *kun* (moving *kaf* and stationary *nun* standing in for body, motion-and-stillness for soul), accept the reading only at the level of *analogical understanding* (the two states point by inference to two honorable relations of the originative apex — its being a cause and its being effected), affirm the apophatic correction (*above the First Effect there is no kaf, no nun, no motion, and no stillness — pure ibda and complete causation*), and correct al-Islah's grave error (*Allah became Creative through ibda*) by the structural teaching that *ibda is nothing other than the Creator Himself*; fusūl 8–9 close the bāb on al-Nusra's general approval-with-apophatic-correction and the compressed teaching that *contradictions occur in bodies, not in spirits — bodies are bodies because they are objects; difference and contrast lie in the images by which what animates them is animated*.
