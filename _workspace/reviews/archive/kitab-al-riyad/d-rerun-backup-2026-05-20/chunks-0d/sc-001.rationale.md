`ch01a-perfection-versus-the-perfect.txt` covers Bāb 1, §1 through §13 — the opening third of the largest *bāb* in the book. The seam between §13 and §14 is clean: §1–§13 establish the architecture (kamāl/tamm distinction, domain-discrimination between origination and the natural world, the principle that essence-completeness and ranked-deficiency are both true and do not contradict each other), while §14 opens with al-Islah's distilled formula ("what is incomplete is the action of the soul, not the soul itself") which al-Nusra then attacks with his three-horned fork. Treating §1–§13 as one episode keeps the architecture-establishing work together as a continuous walk-through of the dispute, with al-Kirmani's first major adjudication on the kamāl/tamm question (§1), his unwinding of the cause/effect inversion in the natural-world ladder of powers (§3), and his decisive argument that the First Intellect cannot be an intermediary (§9) all reaching their proper rhetorical landing. Landed at 6,595 words, mid-band for the extended tier.

`ch02b-where-the-disputants-erred.txt` covers §14 through §26 — the middle third. The seam at §27 is also clean: §27 opens al-Kirmani's affirmative account of the Speaker-Foundation relation with the famous moon-at-the-fourteenth-night image, which feels like a different mode of writing from the prosecutorial work of §14–§26. The middle third holds together as a sustained dialectical critique: al-Kirmani picks apart al-Nusra's three-way fork (§14), catches him out on the al-Razi smear (§14), forces him to admit the world is perfect by citing his own *al-Ikhtisar* against his *al-Nusra* (§18), demolishes the sperm-and-man analogy (§24), and consolidates the Speaker-Foundation parallel through the bodily liturgy of the two rak'ahs of dawn prayer (§25). The §16 placement of the Hadith al-Thaqalayn — which al-Kirmani ties to the joint emanation of Soul and matter from the First Intellect's two proportions — sits at the center of this middle third as the scriptural anchor of the whole metaphysical scheme. Landed at 6,695 words.

`ch03c-the-soul-as-second-emanation.txt` covers §27 through §38 — the closing third. This is the section where the dialectical work gives way to affirmative doctrine: the Foundation's reception of the Speaker's light (§27), the precise distinction between the sensual soul and the Soul-as-Successor (§28), the proof from the First Intellect's self-sufficiency that the Second must exist as the channel of overflow (§29), the three-fold demonstration that forgetting cannot mark the Soul-as-Successor (§30–§32), and the closing assessment of the philosophers' partial reach (§38) with its prayer for illumination. The §29 argument about the First Intellect's relation to potentiality-and-act is the most rigorous single demonstration in the *bāb* and needed to be carried whole within a single episode. The bāb ends in §38 with the prayer "We ask God to illuminate our intellects" — a natural close that the episode honors as its destination. Landed at 6,113 words, the shortest of the three but still mid-band for the extended tier and tonally appropriate to a closing-third movement that turns toward prayer.
