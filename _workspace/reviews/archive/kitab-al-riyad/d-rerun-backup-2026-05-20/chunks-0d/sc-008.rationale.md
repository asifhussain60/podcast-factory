`ch10-qadr-and-the-shape-of-destiny.txt` covers Bāb 8 (On the Adjudication Between the Two Disputants in Their Discourse on Decree and Destiny) in its entirety — all twenty-four fusūl as a single coherent episode that closes the doctrinal arc the prior bābs have built on the originative-emanative chain and opens the historical-architectural register the closing two bābs (Bāb 9 on the law of Adam and the trusteeship of Noah; Bāb 10 on the critique of al-Mahsul on tawhid) will continue. The source slice is 8,376 words — squarely in the extended-tier band (5,500–9,500), and ideally suited to single-episode treatment with proportional Phase 0e enrichment. The bāb carries the doctrinally densest material in the treatise after Bāb 1: the threefold-by-threefold replacement of the disputants' meanings (*qadā* names the natiq, the actualization of revelation, the necessary unfolding of events; *qadar* names the promised Qā'im, the Sharia and parables, the world together with its parts); the correction of the *aql-hadith* (the intellect that holds the worlds is *not* the First Intellect but the rational soul perfected through the two acts of worship); the reading of Quran 54:49 under the Pen-Tablet-Writer imagery (the *al-Qalam wa al-Lawh* — *al-qa-lam wa al-lawh* — architecture central to the Ismaili Da'wa); the rereading of the Prophet's hadith *Flee from God's decree to His destiny* as *a saying about how the Prophet ﷺ received and opened revelation*; the political-historical rereading of Moses on the mountain (Quran 7:143) as a narrative on the architecture of *political timing* under *qadar* — Moses asked the *author of the Command* (*who is Shu'ayb*, his master and guide) for a commission *before his time had come*, and the mountain is *this man who has worldly power, equipment, and capacity*, a contemporary political figure who *could have stood* but did not; and the decisive refutation of antinomianism at Fasl 23 — al-Nusra had read Quran 5:93 as permission for *the prophets to abandon the use of the laws*, and al-Kirmani refuses absolutely, reading the verse as *a license under three conditions* to *study the sciences of other peoples* as a weapon for establishing the truth (*the license is to consider, not to abandon*). The bāb's compressed closing principle — *every interpretation that leads to declaring permissible what Allah has forbidden, or forbidding what He has permitted, or altering the ranks of the limits of Allah Almighty, is a corrupt interpretation* — is the bāb's most direct teaching on the Da'wa's discipline against antinomian readings. The episode is enriched (per Phase 0e) with four Qur'anic anchors at the head (54:49 *bi-qadar*; 36:82 *kun fa-yakun*; 7:143 Moses on the mountain; 5:93 the conditional license) plus three more invoked by al-Kirmani himself inside the bāb (41:53 *We will show them Our signs in the horizons*; 70:6–7 *They see it as far off, but We see it as near*; 18:77 the wall about to collapse), a paraphrase in the manner of Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib's sayings on the secret of *qadāʾ and qadar* (*two of Allah's secrets; do not unfold them, lest the unfolding undo you*), one Prophetic hadith from Bukhari/Muslim (the Witr supplication: *protect me from the evil of what You have decreed — for indeed You decree, and none decrees over You*), one paraphrase in the manner of *Ta'wīl al-Sharī'a* of Imam al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah on the Pen-and-Tablet as two names of the originative dyad, a closing paraphrase in the manner of *al-Sahifa al-Sajjadiyya* on knowledge-and-deed (*let what I know carry me to what I do*), a second closing supplicatory paraphrase on the discipline of *listening* at the foot of the table rather than *disputing about the order of the letters*, and al-Kirmani's own corpus pointer to *Rahat al-'Aql* preserved from his Fasl 9 reference. The bāb's seven thematic movements were grouped into six chapter sections to keep the episode in one continuous arc: Fasl 1–2 (the tailor's garment and the metaphysical mismatch); Fasl 3–8 (the three meanings of *qadā* and the three meanings of *qadar*, with the charitable synthesis after Fasl 8); Fasl 9 (the *aql-hadith* and the intellect that rises from nature); Fasl 10–15 (the Pen, the Tablet, the Writer under Quran 54:49); Fasl 16–19 (the Prophet's hadith *Flee from God's decree to His destiny* and the three meanings al-Nusra adduces); Fasl 20–22 (Moses, Shu'ayb, and the mountain); Fasl 23 (the verse is a license to study, not a license to abandon); and Fasl 24 (beginning, middle, end, and the closing in the register of *listening*). No internal seam justifies fragmentation — the bāb's argumentative arc carries from the diagnosis at Fasl 1–2 through the architectural replacement at Fasl 3–8 to the four extended passages and the antinomian refutation as a single discipline of *returning the disputants' partial readings to the Da'wa's threefold meanings*. The chapter landed at 9,430 words — upper-band for the extended tier, consistent with the rising-arc pattern of the prior bābs (6,595 / 6,695 / 6,113 / 6,825 / 7,349 / 8,476 / 8,932 / 9,486 / 9,496 → 9,430), reflecting the bāb's doctrinal density and the load-bearing weight of its closing teaching on the architecture of decree-and-destiny under the discipline of the Sharia.
