`ch06-souls-as-parts-of-the-real.txt` covers Bāb 4 (On the judgment among the disputants concerning the soul, whether it has parts and effects) in its entirety — all eight fusūl as a single coherent episode. The source slice is the second-slimmest of any bāb in the treatise (2,671 source words), but the argument is unified around a single discrimination: which predicates can carry the metaphysical weight of describing the relation between the partial rational souls in this world and the universal Soul above them. Fasl 1 lays out al-Islah's attempt to hold *parts*, *traces*, and *essences in themselves* together, al-Nusra's sharp diagnosis that the predicates pull against each other, and al-Kirmani's adjudication that grants both critics what they were right about while replacing the vocabulary: the souls are *jewels existing by themselves after acquisition* that *gain their survival* through the effects passed down from the *hudud* — the named teaching ranks of the Da'wa. Fasl 2 runs the *reductio* on al-Nusra's part-arithmetic: if the universal Soul were the sum of the partial souls, the whole would have to inhabit the same lower rank where the parts are, which it doesn't; the partition-language fails for lack of an inhabitable whole. Fasl 3 presses the trilemma: if the partial souls are parts of the Second, either all of them constitute the Second in this world (fails because the partial souls are in potency, the Second in actuality), or some are in this world and some above (fails because some of those above would inherit the Second's actuality while remaining at the rank of potency, confusing the two ranks), or *parts* means something other than its strict sense (which is the door Bāb 4 will walk through). Fasl 4 delivers the positive proposal — the predicate that does the work neither *part* nor *effect* can do: the souls are *a second emanation*, neither parts of the universal Soul nor mere effects, but kin to the prophetic substances in metaphysical fabric, situated as *natural forces that acquire what is gained from the hudud through obedience and worship* and *emit* in turn an abstract subsistent image that becomes the soul's presence in the Hereafter. This is *the second origin*, recursive with the first origin at the top of the chain. Fasl 5 turns to bodies: bodies are *limited in what they can accept* — the iron reaches a maximum red, the black body a maximum blackness; past the ceiling, change-of-state occurs. The body is the *receiver* whose finitude determines what it can hold. Fasl 6 names the contrast: the *soul* is infinite in what it can accept of the light from above, and this infinity is itself proof that the soul is not embodied. And Fasl 6 gives the part-language its restricted right reading — *for those who wish to call them parts*, the partial souls are *like the part of a thing in which the whole already exists*, the whole present at every rank, differently expressed by rank. Fasl 7 takes up al-Nusra's most charged objection — that rejecting the part-language nullifies prophethood, imamate, revelation, the laws — and answers with the bāb's climactic teaching: the prophets, at the limit of their purification, *see the architecture itself*, perceiving that the transcendent selves are one thing in respect of wholeness with no difference between them except in rank. Fasl 8 closes by detaching the theological claims of the Da'wa (tawhid, prophethood, imamate, shari'a, revelation) from al-Nusra's metaphysical system entirely — they are proved through *another aspect of the Intellect* — and by correcting the Intellect-and-Word relation: the Intellect *is* the Word, one thing under two names, per Maulana Imam al-Mu'izz li Din Allah (peace be upon him) in *Ta'wil al-Shari'a*. The chain is named in full: First Intellect → (Soul + Nature, the First Emanation, with Soul nobler) → rational souls (the Second Emanation), with the closing architectural axiom *what occurred in the first of them is recapitulated in the last of them*. There is no thematic seam inside the eight fusūl that would justify a split; the entire bāb is one continuous discrimination of the predicates *part* and *effect*, leading to the naming of *second emanation*. The episode is enriched (per Phase 0e) with three Qur'anic anchors (91:7–10 on the soul as proportioned-and-inspired, 17:85 on the soul as *of* the affair of the Lord, 89:27–28 on the reassured soul's return), two paraphrases in the manner of *Nahj al-Balagha* (the believer's soul as receiver, the human as small world), and a closing prayer-figure in the manner of *al-Sahifa al-Sajjadiyya* that names the bāb's whole architecture as devotion: *not a part of You, nor a mere effect of what You have made*. Landed at 8,476 words — upper-mid-band for the extended tier, consistent with the five sibling episodes from Bāb 1, Bāb 2, and Bāb 3 (6,595 / 6,695 / 6,113 / 6,825 / 7,349).
