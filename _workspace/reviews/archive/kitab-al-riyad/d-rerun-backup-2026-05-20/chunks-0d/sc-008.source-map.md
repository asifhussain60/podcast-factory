| 8 | Bāb 8 — On Qadr and Destiny (24 fusūl) | ch10-qadr-and-the-shape-of-destiny.txt | 8,376w sits squarely in the extended tier band (5,500–9,500) and supports a single coherent episode that closes the doctrinal arc on the originative-emanative chain and opens the historical-architectural register the closing bābs continue. The twenty-four fusūl form one continuous movement on three load-bearing teachings: the threefold-by-threefold replacement of the disputants' meanings (*qadā* names the natiq, the actualization of revelation in the soul, the necessary unfolding of events; *qadar* names the promised Qā'im, the Sharia and parables brought down, the world together with its parts — with each disputant's reading holding *charitably* on one of the three pairings and missing the others); the correction of the *aql-hadith* at Fasl 9 (*the intellect that holds the images of the worlds is not the first creative intellect, but the intellect that rises out of the world of nature by acquiring works and knowledge* — Bāb 4's architecture read from the side of the result); and the bāb's compressed antinomian refutation at Fasl 23 (*the verse contains no mention of permission for the prophets to abandon the use of the laws* — the license is *to consider* the sciences of other peoples, not *to abandon* the Sharia). The bāb walks through four extended passages — Quran 54:49 (*all things We created with predestination*) under the Pen-Tablet-Writer imagery; the Prophet's hadith *Flee from God's decree to His destiny*; Moses on the mountain (Quran 7:143) rereadable as a political-historical narrative on *political timing under qadar*; and Quran 5:93 (the conditional license) — each read by the disputants in ways al-Kirmani must correct. No internal seam justifies fragmentation: Fasl 1–2 diagnoses the disputants' shared mistake (both have built on meanings *belonging to the world of the body, not the world of creation*); Fasl 3–8 gives the threefold-by-threefold replacement and the charitable synthesis; Fasl 9 corrects the aql-hadith; Fasl 10–15 walks through the Pen-and-Tablet reading of Quran 54:49; Fasl 16–19 reads the Prophet's hadith as *a saying about how the Prophet ﷺ received and opened revelation*; Fasl 20–22 rereads Moses and Shu'ayb politically; Fasl 23 refutes antinomianism decisively; Fasl 24 closes in the register of *listening at the foot of the table rather than disputing about the order of the letters*. Single episode enriched per Phase 0e with four Qur'anic anchors at the head (54:49 *bi-qadar*; 36:82 *kun fa-yakun*; 7:143 Moses on the mountain; 5:93 the conditional license) plus three inside (41:53 *We will show them Our signs in the horizons*, quoted by al-Kirmani at Fasl 18; 70:6–7 *They see it as far off, but We see it as near*, cited at Fasl 8; 18:77 *the wall about to collapse*, invoked by al-Islah at Fasl 16); an Imam Ali paraphrase (*decree and destiny are two of Allah's secrets; do not unfold them, lest the unfolding undo you*); the Witr supplication hadith from Bukhari 1425 / Muslim 770 (*protect me from the evil of what You have decreed — for indeed You decree, and none decrees over You*); a paraphrase in the manner of *Ta'wīl al-Sharī'a* of Imam al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah on the Pen-and-Tablet as two names of the originative dyad; one closing supplicatory paraphrase in the manner of *al-Sahifa al-Sajjadiyya* on the unity of knowledge and deed; a second closing supplicatory paraphrase on the discipline of *listening*; and al-Kirmani's own corpus pointer to *Rahat al-'Aql* preserved from his Fasl 9 reference.
