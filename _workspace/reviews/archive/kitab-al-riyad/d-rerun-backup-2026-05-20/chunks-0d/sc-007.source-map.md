| 7 | Bāb 7 — On the Divisions of the World (7 fusūl) | ch09-divisions-of-the-world.txt | 3,823w sits below the extended tier band but is well-suited to a single episode that closes the anthropological arc Bāb 5 opened — the seven fusūl form one continuous movement on three load-bearing teachings: the threefold-not-fourfold correction of the world's actual genera (mineral, plant, animal — with the human *within* the animal genus, distinguished by *a kind of conscious awareness*); the plural-pairing anthropology where the human, being a species within the animal genus, has *many pairs in many respects* — paired by *what he is satisfied with* with angels, lions, wolves, foxes, crows, hawks, tigers, or monkeys (the principle that *alliance constitutes belonging*, with the universal-quantifier reading of Quran 5:51 — *whoever takes them as allies, he is one of them* — applied positively at Fasl 3 to the prophetic-and-Imamic line as the human's *true pair*); and the closing image at Fasl 7 of *the child in his coming-to-be between his parents* — *this world* giving the matter, *that world* giving the form, *the midwife* carrying the matter — which articulates the matter-form distinction Bāb 6 secured as the anthropological architecture of the human at the seam. No internal seam justifies fragmentation.
