# Biography of the Prophet (peace be upon him)

## The Conversion of Ṭufayl ibn ʿĀmir

The disbelievers and hypocrites of Mecca had circulated a grave accusation against the Messenger of Allāh ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫: that he was a sorcerer of extraordinary power, whose speech enslaved all who heard it. When ⟪ar:طفیل بن عامر طوسی⟫ — a man of considerable standing and influence among his own people — arrived in Mecca, he encountered the same warning. The Meccans took deliberate precautions, stuffing their ears with cotton so that even by accident they might not hear the voice of the Messenger of Allāh. [^cite-1]

Yet as Ṭufayl performed the ritual circumambulation of the Kaʿba, he observed a man of striking beauty standing in prayer, reciting aloud with reverence. Prompted by curiosity, Ṭufayl inquired of those nearby: "Who is this man?" They answered at once: "This is the sorcerer of whom we warned you." But Ṭufayl, reflecting upon the dignified bearing and noble countenance before him, thought to himself: "Surely this cannot be the face of a magician." [^cite-2]

A second consideration then arose in his mind — one born of his own station and self-regard. *I am a leader among my people*, he reasoned, *and no fool to be swayed by mere eloquence. I shall listen to what he has to say with discernment. If his words contain truth, I shall accept them; if falsehood, I shall reject them.* With this resolve, Ṭufayl removed the cotton from his ears and seated himself near the Messenger of Allāh.

When the prayer concluded, the Prophet ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ rose, and Ṭufayl approached him with a direct question:

⟪ar-quote:یا محمد! ما عندک؟⟫

"What have you brought, O Muḥammad?"

The Messenger ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ replied with words of profound luminosity:

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّ الحَمدَ لِی اللہَ أحمَدَہُ وَ أستَعِینَہُ وَ أومِنُ بِہِ وَ أتَوَکَّلُ عَلَیہِ ۔ مَن یَّھدِہِ اللہُ فَلاَ مُضِلَّ لَہ وَ مَن یَّضِلَّ لَہُ فَلاَ ھَادِیَ لَہ⟫

"Verily, all praise belongs to Allāh — Him do I praise, from Him do I seek aid, in Him do I believe, and upon Him do I rely. Whomsoever Allāh guides, none can lead astray; and whomsoever He leads astray, none can guide."

The effect upon Ṭufayl was immediate and transformative. His entire being trembled at these words, and from the depths of his heart there came a cry of wonder:

⟪ar-quote:أعد أعد! فقد بلغنا قاموس البحر⟫

*Repeat! Repeat!* he exclaimed in rapture. *With this single utterance, you have touched the depths of the boundless ocean!* [^cite-3]

The Prophet ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ spoke the words again, each syllable carrying the same luminous truth. This time, as Ṭufayl listened with his whole being open to reception, his eyes filled with tears. In that moment of profound recognition, he said:

"O Muḥammad, teach me the *kalimah* (the testimony of faith). I believe in you and in your Lord."

Thus did Ṭufayl ibn ʿĀmir, once a leader bound by the prejudices of his people, become a believer — his conversion a testament to the transformative power of divine speech when the heart is prepared to receive it.
