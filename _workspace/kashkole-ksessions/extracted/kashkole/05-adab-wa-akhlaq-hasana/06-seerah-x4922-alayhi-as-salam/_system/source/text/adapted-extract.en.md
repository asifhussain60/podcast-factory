# Biography of the Prophet (peace be upon him)

## The Conversion of Ṭufayl ibn ʿĀmir

The disbelievers and hypocrites of Mecca had circulated a grave accusation against the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him): that he was a master of sorcery whose words enslaved all who heard them. Ṭufayl ibn ʿĀmir al-Dausī (ṭufayl ibn ʿāmir), a man of considerable standing among his own people, arrived in Mecca to encounter this same warning from its inhabitants. So fearful were they of the Prophet's eloquence that they took extraordinary precautions—stuffing their ears with cotton so that even a chance word from him might not penetrate their hearing.

Yet as Ṭufayl performed the ritual circumambulation of the Ka'ba, he observed a striking figure: a man of noble bearing, standing in prayer, his voice raised in recitation. Seized with curiosity, Ṭufayl inquired of those nearby, "Who is this?" They replied without hesitation: "This is the sorcerer of whom we warned you." But reflection gave Ṭufayl pause. The face before him—serene, luminous in devotion—bore no mark of enchantment or deception. His own station as a leader of his tribe emboldened him to trust his judgment. "I am no fool," he resolved, "to be swept away by mere words. Rather, I shall listen with discernment. What is true, I shall embrace; what is false, I shall reject." With this resolution, he removed the cotton from his ears and took his place near the Messenger of Allah.

When the Prophet (peace be upon him) concluded his prayer, Ṭufayl approached and asked:

⟪ar-quote:یا محمد! ما عندک؟⟫

"What do you possess, O Muhammad?"

The Messenger responded:

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّ الحَمدَ لِی اللہَ أحمَدَہُ وَ أستَعِینَہُ وَ أومِنُ بِہِ وَ أتَوَکَّلُ عَلَیہِ ۔ مَن یَّھدِہِ اللہُ فَلاَ مُضِلَّ لَہ وَ مَن یَّضِلَّ لَہُ فَلاَ ھَادِیَ لَہ⟫

"Verily, all praise belongs to Allah. I praise Him and seek His aid; in Him I believe and upon Him I place my trust. Whomsoever Allah guides, none shall lead astray; and whomsoever He leads astray, none shall guide."[^cite-1]

These words struck Ṭufayl with the force of revelation. He trembled, and from his lips came words unbidden:

⟪ar-quote:أعد أعد! فقد بلغنا قاموس البحر⟫

"Repeat! Repeat! A single utterance from you has comprehended the very ocean itself!"

The Prophet (peace be upon him) spoke the invocation again:

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّ الحَمدَ لِی اللہَ أحمَدَہُ وَ أستَعِینَہُ وَ أومِنُ بِہِ وَ أتَوَکَّلُ عَلَیہِ ۔ مَن یَّھدِہِ اللہُ فَلاَ مُضِلَّ لَہ وَ مَن یَّضِلَّ لَہُ فَلاَ ھَادِیَ لَہ⟫

Tears now flowed from Ṭufayl's eyes as conviction crystallized within his heart. "O Muhammad," he declared, his voice trembling with emotion, "teach me the Kalimah (the Islamic declaration of faith)! I bear witness to you and to your Lord."[^cite-2] In that moment, the leader of the Dausī tribe—whom the Meccans had sought to shield from truth through cotton and calumny—entered the fold of Islam, his submission as complete as it was sudden.
