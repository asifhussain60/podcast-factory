## The Prophet's Counsels to ʿAlī (Waṣāyā)

O ʿAlī: There are six noble qualities, and when they are gathered in six persons, they become most excellent indeed. Justice is a noble trait, and when found in a ruler, it becomes most excellent. A ruler lacking justice is like a cloud bearing no rain; yet a just ruler shall receive a reward like that of the Prophet Ishmael. Generosity is a noble trait, and when found in the wealthy, it becomes most excellent. The rich who lack generosity are like a fruitless tree; yet a generous rich person shall receive a reward like that of the Prophet Abraham. Piety is a noble trait, and when found in the learned, it becomes most excellent. A scholar without piety is like a candle that consumes itself while illuminating others; yet a pious scholar shall receive a reward like that of the Prophet Jacob. Patience is a noble quality, and when found in the poor, it becomes most excellent. The poor who lack patience are like barren earth from which nothing grows; yet a patient poor person shall receive a reward like that of the Prophet Ayyūb (upon him be peace). Repentance is a noble trait, and when found in the young, it becomes most excellent. The youth who lacks repentance is like a well without water. Modesty is a noble trait, and when found in a woman, it becomes most excellent. A woman without modesty is like unsalted bread; yet a modest woman shall receive a reward like that of the lady Maryam.

O ʿAlī: Do not befriend the drunkard, for he is cursed; nor the seller of wine, for he too stands accursed. They are called enemies of God in the heavens. So too is the usurer, an enemy of God and one who wages war against Him. Allah the Almighty has said to them, "You are enemies of Allah and prepare to fight Allah!"[^cite-1]

O ʿAlī: When you pray, extend your hands in supplication, yet raise not your voice in prayer, for it disturbs the prayers of others.

O ʿAlī: Whoever hires a worker and compels him to labor, then withholds his full wages, Allah shall render his deeds void, and I shall be his adversary on the Day of Resurrection![^cite-2]

O ʿAlī: Consume olive oil and anoint yourself with it. When illness befalls you, recite this invocation:

⟪ar:سُبْحَانَکَ لاَ اِلَہَ اِلاَّ اَنْتَ عَلَیْکَ تَوَکَّلْتُ وَاَنْتَ رَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِیْم⟫

Glory be to Thee; there is no god but Thee. Upon Thee do I place my trust, for Thou art the Lord of the Mighty Throne.

And repeat this invocation, which Gabriel (upon him be peace) has taught me:

⟪ar:اَللّٰھُمَّ اِنِّی اَسْئلُکَ الْعَفْوَ وَالْعَافِیَۃَ وَالْمُوَافَاۃَ الدَّائِمَہْ فِی الدِّیْنِ وَالدُّنْیَا وَالآخِرَۃ اِنَّکَ عَلَی کُلِّ شَئیٍ قَدِیْرٌ⟫

O Allah, I ask of Thee forgiveness, well-being, and eternal accord in religion, this world, and the Hereafter. Verily, Thou art Able to do all things.

O ʿAlī: When sorrow and distress encompass you and you seek succor, raise your hands to Allah and say:

⟪ar:یَا حَیُّ یَا قَیُّومُ لاَ اِلَہَ اِلاَّ اَنْتَ بِرَحْمَتِکَ اَسْتَغِیْثُ وَ بِعَفْوِکَ اِرْحَمنِیْ وَاَصْلِح لِیٰ شَانِی کُلَّہُ وَ فَرِّجْ ھَمِّیْ وَغَمِّیْ بِرَحْمَتِکَ یَا اَرْحَمَ الرَّاحِمِیْنَ⟫

O Living One, O Self-Subsisting One! There is no god but Thee. By Thy mercy do I seek aid. Through Thy pardon have mercy upon me, and set right all my affairs, and lift from me all grief and sorrow, through Thy mercy, O Most Merciful of all the merciful.[^cite-3]

O ʿAlī: Two prostrations performed at night surpass a thousand performed during the day. The countenance of one who rises in the night for prayer shall be more luminous than that of one who prays only by day.

O ʿAlī: When Allah created Paradise, He asked it, "For whom have I created you?" Paradise replied, "For every generous soul and one who fears You." Paradise said, "Master, I am well pleased with that." Thus whoever opposes his base desires—Paradise becomes his dwelling; yet whoever becomes enslaved to desire—Hell becomes his abode.[^cite-4]

O ʿAlī: When Allah created Hell, He asked it, "My Lord, why have You created me?" Allah said, "I have created you for every stingy person and every arrogant soul." And Hell said, "Master, I am well pleased with that."

O ʿAlī: When you give alms, give from what is good to you. If you give but a single date-palm, obtained through lawful means, living by that restraint, it is better than a thousand dinars given in charity from abundance after you have already acquired much.[^cite-5]

O ʿAlī: Perform your ritual ablution (wuḍū) with utmost care before retiring to rest, for it is a part of faith. When you perform ablution, do not waste the water, and recite Sūrah *Al-Qadr* (The Night of Power). Allah shall reward such ablution as though you had worshipped for fifty years.

O ʿAlī: Whoever washes his feet and blesses me ten times after completing ablution—Allah shall remove his grief and accept his prayers.

O ʿAlī: When you have completed your ablution, take water and wipe it upon your throat, and say:

Glory be to Thee, O Allah, and praise be to Thee. Thou hast no partner. Forgive me and accept my repentance.

Then look toward the earth and say:

I bear witness that Muḥammad (may Allah's peace be upon him) is the servant and messenger of Allah; may Allah's peace be upon him.

Whoever does this—Allah shall forgive him all his sins, great and small.[^cite-6]

O ʿAlī: Beware of sitting with the spiritually dead—those who remember God not. I inquired, "O Messenger of Allah, who are these dead?" He replied, "Wealthy people who regard their accumulated wealth as a mother guards her child." Such people suffer immense loss.

O ʿAlī: If the scholar is neither pious nor repentant, his sermons and exhortations shall fall from people's hearts as eggs fall from a bird's nest.

O ʿAlī: Give alms and charity for the deceased, for Allah and His angels present these charities to those who have passed on, saying, "O Allah, forgive him who illuminated our graves and grant him the glad tidings of Paradise as Thou hast granted us." But those who give no alms for their dead—the deceased shall lament, saying, "Woe upon those whom we have left behind!"

O ʿAlī: The religion of one who consumes what is lawful (ḥalāl) becomes pure; his heart grows tender; his eyes flow with the fear of God; and the veil is lifted from his prayer—meaning his prayer is answered.[^cite-7]

O ʿAlī: Confide your secrets neither to men nor to women, for women do not keep secrets.

O ʿAlī: Eat sparingly so that you may rest peacefully at night. Excessive eating hardens the heart.

O ʿAlī: If you take no loan, you shall live in freedom and security. If you shun backbiting (ghība), you shall become true friends. If you restrain your anger, Allah shall fill your hearts with light and your breast with knowledge.

O ʿAlī: If you have relations with your wife on Thursday or Friday, and a boy is born, he shall be righteous, pious, and God-fearing.

O ʿAlī: If you sit with the poor, you shall attain a high station with Allah.

O ʿAlī: Whoever honors the wealthy while humiliating the poor shall be called an enemy of Allah in the heavens and on earth.

O ʿAlī: Always speak the truth; let no falsehood ever leave your mouth. And betray not, ever. Fear God as though you see Him. Expend your life and wealth for your religion. Cultivate noble character and shun base conduct.[^cite-8]

O ʿAlī: Allah loves three things:

Whoever fulfills the duties ordained by Allah the Almighty becomes a great worshipper. Whoever refrains from what Allah has forbidden becomes deeply pious. And whoever is content with what the Lord has given him becomes abundantly wealthy.

O ʿAlī: The reward of three deeds is found both in this world and in the Hereafter:

The pilgrimage that removes poverty; charity that dispels evils; and kindness to kinship that prolongs life.

O ʿAlī: On the Day of Resurrection, all eyes shall weep save three:

The eye that has kept vigil in the worship of Allah the Almighty; the eye that has refrained from gazing upon the forbidden; and the eye that flows with the fear of God.

O ʿAlī: When you look into the mirror, say:

Allah is Most Great, Allah is Most Great, Allah is Most Great, Allah is Most Great. Just as Allah has made my outward form beautiful, so too has He made my character beautiful.

O ʿAlī: When difficulty befalls you, say:

May Allah ease my hardship through Muḥammad and the family of Muḥammad.

O ʿAlī: When you begin eating, first taste salt; when you finish, taste salt again. It cures seventy diseases, among them leprosy and skin affliction.

O ʿAlī: Do not have relations with your wife on the first, middle, and last nights of the lunar month (the thirteenth, fourteenth, and fifteenth). Have you not observed how the afflicted cry out abundantly in these nights?

O ʿAlī: Do not wear a ring upon your middle finger or index finger. In these fingers the people of the Prophet Lūṭ (upon him be peace) wore rings—upon them descended the wrath of God.

O ʿAlī: Speak no lies, for a lie blackens the face, and the liar is written down as false in God's sight. Through truthfulness a man's face becomes radiant and he is written righteous in God's sight. Remember: truth is blessed, and falsehood is calamity.[^cite-9]

O ʿAlī: Remember this: A wicked yet generous person is better in Allah's sight than a stingy worshipper.

O ʿAlī: Respect your neighbor, even if he be a disbeliever. Love him and show him compassion. The wealth and life of one who envies his neighbor shall be ruined.

O ʿAlī: Recite Sūrah *Yāsīn*. There are ten blessings in it:

If he hungers, he shall find food. If he thirsts, he shall be satisfied. If he recites it without clothing, Allah shall provide him garments. If he is unmarried, he shall find a spouse. If he is afraid, he shall find peace. If he is sick, he shall be cured. If he is imprisoned, he shall be freed. If he is a traveler, it shall aid his journey. If it is recited for the deceased, the severity of death shall be lessened and punishment reduced. If the misguided recite it, they shall be guided.

The Holy Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: "On the Day of Resurrection, a creature named Harīsh, descended from a scorpion, shall emerge from Hell. Its length shall span from heaven to earth, and its width from east to west. Gabriel shall ask, 'Where are you heading?' It shall reply, 'To the field of judgment.' Gabriel shall ask, 'Whom do you seek?' And it shall say it seeks five kinds of people:

Those who do not pray; those who do not pay the obligatory alms (zakāt); those who disobey their parents; drunkards; and those who speak of worldly matters in the mosque."

*Compiled by: Late al-Ḥāj Mullā Aḥmad Khurshīd Ḥasan (gifts he used to send to the believers on the festival of ʿĪd)*

## Aqwāl Zarrīn — Golden Sayings

When charity leaves the hand of its giver, it speaks five sentences: "I was mortal wealth—you saved me. I was your enemy—now you have made me your friend. Before today you guarded me—now I shall guard you. I was despicable—you made me great. First I was in your hands—now I am in God's hands."

When you take someone as a friend, make a cemetery in your heart where you may bury their faults and secrets.

A true friend, no matter how many times he stumbles, should be celebrated; for just as scattered beads of the rosary are gathered and chosen, so too the friend of tried virtue shall ever be honored.

When the eyes begin to behold the desires of the soul, the heart becomes blind forever after.[^cite-10]

People are not transformed by attaining power, wealth, and dominion—rather, they are revealed thereby.

Seek your sustenance in the light of day, yet in the darkness of night seek that provision which shall truly sustain you.

If someone you desire rejects you, it is his misfortune. If no one desires you and you force yourself upon them, it is the shame of your soul.

Humans do not suffer from affliction; rather, they suffer from the hopes they place in others.

If you wish to belong to someone, belong with complete truth; otherwise, cultivate within yourself such a reality that someone shall desire you in truth.

It is Allah's blessing upon you that people's needs are bound to you.

When you continue receiving blessings from the Almighty despite your sins, know that your reckoning draws near and shall be severe.

I prefer sitting in the mosque more than in Paradise, for my soul shall find joy in Paradise, yet my Lord shall be pleased in the mosque.

A believer is not one whose public gathering is pure; rather, a believer is one whose solitude is pure.

The raindrop falls into the mouth of both serpent and oyster. The oyster transforms it into a pearl; the serpent turns it into venom. The capacity lies within the recipient.

One truth that ought never be uttered is self-praise.

Strive to be in the world, not of the world. The boat floats well so long as it remains in the water; but when water enters the boat, it sinks.[^cite-11]

Do not test the quickness of your tongue against one who has taught you to speak.

My God, it suffices me that I am Thy servant. And it suffices my heart that Thou art my Lord. Be for me as Thou wilt; only make me as Thou dost will.

Guard five things in five places: the tongue in gatherings, the eye in the marketplace, the heart in prayer, the stomach at the table, and modesty in one's gaze.

Three things keep a person distant from God: estimating highly one's own deeds, forgetting one's sins, and considering oneself the best.[^cite-12]

Six things ruin spiritual progress: finding fault in others, hardness of heart, love of the world, lack of modesty, distant hopes, and oppression of people.

A true friend is recognized in three circumstances: in times of need, behind your back, and after your death.

If you wish to test someone's character, give him more respect! If he possesses nobility, he shall give you more respect in return; if he lacks it, he shall deem himself superior.

Allah the Almighty bestows five things along with five others: with gratitude, abundance of wealth; with prayer, answered supplication; with forgiveness, pardon; with charity, acceptance; and with mercy, compassion.[^cite-13]

A perfect believer is one whose morals are good.

The people inquired whether a wealthy woman whose husband forbade her to perform the pilgrimage could go. The answer was that it is not permissible for her to go without her husband's permission.

Whoever humbles himself before the wealthy out of respect for their wealth, even if they be not tyrants, shall lose a part of his faith.

Nothing in Allah's sight is as detestable as divorce.

Whatever you bring from the market for children, give first to the daughter, then to the son.[^cite-14]

If a person buys cloth for ten dirhams and one dirham's price is unlawful (ḥarām), Allah shall not accept his prayer so long as that cloth remains upon his body.

When a person commits sin, Allah bestows four favors upon him: He does not cut off his sustenance; He does not hinder his well-being; He does not reveal his faults; and He does not punish him immediately.

Four deeds rank among the most difficult: forgiving sins when angry; showing generosity in poverty; maintaining solitude in spiritual recollection; and speaking truth in times of fear or desire.[^cite-15]

Good deeds grant two comforts: one when the deed is performed, and the second when the reward is received.

Haste is the Sunnah in six things, and haste in others is of Satan: in feeding the guest, in burying the dead, in marrying a girl, in paying a debt, in repenting from sin, and in responding to the call to prayer and going to the mosque.

Do not belittle four things: debt, illness, enmity, and fire.

Eight signs reveal a rebellious heart: committing sin in hope of repentance; acquiring knowledge without practicing it; acting without sincerity; consuming sustenance without gratitude; witnessing death without learning; and lacking in three things is better than possessing them: eating excessively, dreaming idly, and speaking thoughtlessly.

There are three levels of error: unintentional mistake, deliberate transgression, and willful wrong.

There are three levels of certainty: knowledge of certainty (ʿilm al-yaqīn), the certainty of observation (ʿayn al-yaqīn), and the certainty of truth (ḥaqq al-yaqīn).

Three addictions are extremely swift—the first two degenerate, the last progressive: the addiction to wealth, the addiction to beauty, and the addiction to knowledge.[^cite-16]

Ten things devour ten things: good devours evil; knowledge devours arrogance; repentance devours sin; lawful sustenance devours unlawful; justice devours oppression; charity devours heedlessness; the sorrow of remembrance devours anger; and regret devours past generosity; backbiting devours good deeds.

Five things form the foundation of corruption: harm, harsh speech, coercion, usurping the earth, and transgression.

A man's happiness rests upon five things: a virtuous wife, righteous children, a god-fearing friend, a good neighbor, and sustenance in one's own city.

Eight things do not come to an end: the eye's sight through sleep, the earth from rain, woman from man, the world from knowledge, questioning from the seeker, wealth from the greedy, the river from water, fire from wood.[^cite-17]

Three things fill a person's dreams with unease: fear of serpents, sight of milk, and fear of rats.

There is no greater worship in Allah's sight than to gladden the heart of a Muslim brother.[^cite-18]

The worst sin after associating partners with Allah is to harm God's creation.

Honor the elders, and Allah shall cause the young to honor you in your old age.

Whoever does not respect the elders and show compassion to the young shall not be of my community.

The deed most worthy of forgiveness is openness of heart and gentle speech.

Fear the prayer of the oppressed, for there is no veil between him and God.

Do not curse your lives, your children, your servants, or your wealth, lest it coincide with an hour of divine response and your curse be answered.

Love of the world is the root of all sin; love of something blinds and deafens.[^cite-19]

Cursed is he who places his trust in a creature like himself.

There is no greater gift a father can give his son than fine education and moral training.

I fear for my community hypocrites and those with long tongues more than any other evil.

A generous sinner is better in Allah's sight than a stingy worshipper.[^cite-20]

Shall I not inform you of the treasure that is best? Listen: she is a virtuous woman.

Whoever abandons quarreling, even when he is in the right, I shall guarantee him a house in Paradise.

I guarantee a house in the midst of Paradise for one who abandons lying, even in jest.

I guarantee a house in the highest ranks of Paradise for one of excellent character.

In the latter days, you shall follow the Jews and Christians step by step, following them so closely that if they enter a hole in the mountain, you shall enter it too.

When the Holy Prophet returned from war, he said, "We are coming from the lesser struggle (al-jihād al-asghar) to the greater struggle (al-jihād al-akbar)." The people asked, "O Messenger of Allah, what is this greater struggle?" He replied, "The struggle against your soul (nafs), for your greatest enemy is your self dwelling between your two sides."[^cite-21]

He who fulfills the needs of a brother is as if he has served Allah his whole life.

Help your brother whether he be oppressed or oppressor. The help of the oppressed is to free him from the oppressor; the help of the oppressor is to prevent him from oppressing.[^cite-22]

There is no greater worship in Allah's sight than to gladden the heart of a Muslim brother. He who fears Allah takes no retribution. The worst sin after polytheism is to harm people, and the best good after faith is to comfort God's creatures.

On the Day of Judgment, the poor neighbor shall intercede for the rich neighbor.

The disbelieving neighbor has a share of rights; the Muslim neighbor has double rights; and the relative neighbor has triple rights.

He who persecutes his neighbor shall be in Hell, even if he worships all night and fasts all day. By Allah in Whose hand is the soul of Muḥammad! The right of the neighbor is fulfilled by one upon whom Allah has mercy. The deed most worthy of forgiveness is openness and gentleness.

Those who rejoiced that people stood up for them should consider their dwelling in the Fire.

The Holy Prophet was asked who is the best woman, and he replied: "She whose husband sees her and is pleased, who obeys his command, and does not oppose what he dislikes concerning herself, his property, and his life."[^cite-23]

People described a woman of extreme piety, great devotion, constant fasting, and much remembrance of God. Yet her neighbors were harassed by her sharp tongue. The Messenger of Allah said, "She is an inhabitant of Hell."

## Brotherhood (Ukhuwwa) and Backbiting (Ghība)

In Sūrah al-Ḥujurāt, Allah teaches the believers how to practice brotherhood and cultivate it:

⟪quran 49:10⟫

The believers are brothers to one another. Therefore establish peace among your brothers, and fear Allah, that you may be shown mercy.

The bond of kinship through a common father is one that is generally recognized and understood by all people. Yet there exists a second relationship, which Allah and His Messenger have ordained—the bond of faith. Allah addresses the believers, saying, "The believers are brothers to one another." By this declaration, this bond of faith becomes even more elevated than the bond of kinship, for it issues from Allah and His Messenger. After establishing this fraternal bond, it becomes incumbent upon the believers to seek reconciliation with one another and to work for each other's betterment. Finally, He commanded them: "Fear Allah, that you may be shown mercy." The nature of the brotherhood that Allah and His Messenger have described reveals how a believer must conduct himself toward his fellow believers. The subsequent verses explain what should not be said and done while practicing brotherhood and reconciliation:

⟪quran 49:11⟫

O you who believe! Let not men mock at other men, perchance they may be better than them. Neither let women mock at other women, perchance they may be better than them. And do not taunt one another, nor call one another by hateful names. Evil is a bad name after faith. And whoever does not repent—those are the wrongdoers.

In this verse, self-praise and the belittlement of others are forbidden. The believers are warned that if you mock others, presuming yourselves superior, those whom you ridicule may well be better in Allah's sight. The same applies to women. It is evil to call believers by offensive names; those who do so are wrongdoers. And Allah says to such transgressors:

⟪quran 49:12a⟫

A painful punishment awaits the wrongdoers.

The next verse addresses the things to be avoided and their grave consequences:

⟪quran 49:12⟫

O you who believe! Avoid much suspicion, for some suspicion is a sin. And spy not on one another, nor backbite one another. Would any of you like to eat the flesh of his dead brother? You would abhor it. So fear Allah. Verily, Allah is the Great Acceptor of Repentance, the Merciful.

What a penetrating and clear observation of human nature! How often we entertain suspicions about ourselves and others; how much effort we expend to learn the affairs of our neighbors and acquaintances; and how prevalent is backbiting! Where even four people, be they men or women, gather together, it is common for them to seek out each other's faults, to engage in backbiting, and to pry into each other's private affairs. Every believer must reflect carefully upon these matters and grasp how grievous suspicion, spying, and backbiting truly are, and must strive earnestly to avoid them.

We are deeply concerned when the meat we purchase from the market or consume in an establishment may be unlawful or impure. Yet when we sit together, we eagerly consume meat which the Qur'ān describes as the flesh of our dead brother. The Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said, "The good deeds of the one who backbites are taken from his record of deeds and entered into the record of the one about whom he backbites."[^cite-24]

The renowned ascetic Ibrāhīm ibn Adham, who had abandoned his kingdom to become a dervish, once accepted an invitation to a feast. Upon arrival, he found the guests sitting and backbiting their relatives and acquaintances. He arose and departed, saying, "I did not know you preferred to eat the flesh of your dead brothers before partaking of the feast."

We are deeply distressed that we concern ourselves so much with the lawfulness and unlawfulness of meat from the market or restaurant—with external purity and impurity—yet we pay little heed to true spiritual impurities such as suspicion, contravention of religious precepts, oppression, and dishonesty, which defile the soul, heart, and mind alike. We are meticulous about the meat of the market and restaurant, yet we consume the flesh of our dead brother with great passion and pleasure.

Remember: in the verse cited above, it is said, "O you who believe!"—addressing the believers—that while teaching the lesson of brotherhood and commanding the avoidance of evil, He said, "Fear Allah." The peculiarity of this age is that neither God nor the thought of death is remembered while one commits wrong! Allah's address to the believers indicates that only true believers are those who purify themselves from these evils; and those who indulge in them are outside the pale of faith. May Allah guide us and all the believers.[^cite-25]

### Finding Your Own Faults

The Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said:

I am occupied with the faults of my own soul and am far from finding faults in the souls of others. Whoever is occupied with knowing the faults of his own soul becomes distracted from finding faults in the souls of others.[^cite-26]

When the preposition *fī* (in) is used in conjunction with the Arabic root *shaghal* (to occupy), it means to be engaged in something. When the preposition *ʿan* (from) accompanies it, it signifies non-engagement. This is the subtlety of the Arabic tongue.

*(Lessons of the Night of the Hijra, 500. Mullā Ghulām Ḥusayn ibn Ḥaḍrat Nusrallāh Ṣāḥib)*

## Divine Commandments (Aḥkām Khudāwandī)

Do not take Allah's commandments as a jest. Remember the favors Allah has bestowed upon you. Do not forget His grace—that He has sent down to you the Book, the Word of Wisdom, and the Scripture, so that He may instruct you through these ordinances. And beware of disobedience to Allah, for Allah has knowledge of all things.[^cite-27]

It may be that you dislike a thing which is good for you, and that you love a thing which is bad for you. Allah knows, and you know not. Do you think that you were created without purpose, and that you shall not be returned to Him?[^cite-28]

Seek solace through patience and prayer to endure calamity. The Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) says to the people, "My Lord increases provision for whom He wills and restricts it for whom He wills." Yet people are unaware of what the distribution of sustenance truly means.

O believers! Spend from what We have given you, before death comes to you, and then you say, "Would that my Lord had granted me respite for a little while, that I might have given alms and been among the righteous servants."[^cite-29]

Whoever intercedes in a good matter shall have a share in it, and whoever intercedes in an evil matter shall bear a share of its burden. Allah has power over all things. Whatever misfortune befalls you is by reason of what your hands have done, and Allah pardons much of your wrongdoing.[^cite-30]

*Compiled by: Late al-Ḥāj Mullā Aḥmad Khurshīd Ḥasan (gifts he used to send to the believers on the festival of ʿĪd)*

## The Marks of a Good Person

Thus has the Noble One—the Beloved of the Holy Prophet—described the good person. Allah the Exalted has said:

⟪quran 49:13⟫

The noblest of you in the sight of Allah is the most pious.

And the Beloved of Allah has declared:

The righteous person who benefits mankind is the best of people.[^cite-31]

Align your life with the standards set by Allah and His Beloved. Both your religion and your worldly affairs shall flourish.

Allah has explained what must be given and spent in order to benefit people:

⟪quran 3:92⟫

You will not attain righteousness (al-birr) unless you give of that which you love.

The word "righteousness" refers to *al-abrār*, the righteous—a station of the Prophets. The good deeds through which the Prophets (upon them be peace) attain the rank of *al-abrār* are attained through spending from things most precious to them. Ordinarily, people donate things of little use or value. Such giving is not *nikah* (recognition, appreciation).[^cite-32]

*Compiled by: Late al-Ḥāj Mullā Aḥmad Khurshīd Ḥasan (gifts he used to send to the believers on the festival of ʿĪd)*

## The Rights of Parents and the Rights of Servants of God (Ḥuqūq al-ʿIbād)

The Lord of the Universe has said in His Holy Word: "Be dutiful and kind to your parents."[^cite-33] Speak to them with gentleness and courtesy. Refrain from rebuking them, and say not even "uff" (a word of slight disdain) to them. The dignity owed to our parents is evidenced by the fact that in every prayer, the *tashahhud* (the testimony of faith) includes a supplication for their forgiveness and mercy in gratitude for their nurture during our childhood.

It is narrated that the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) once came to deliver a sermon. When he ascended the pulpit, he said "Amīn." When he took two steps higher, he said "Amīn" again; and when he reached the top of the pulpit, he said "Amīn" a third time. After delivering his sermon, he descended. The people asked, "O Messenger of Allah, why did you say 'Amīn' three times?" He replied:

The first time, Gabriel came to me and said, "May the nose of one be rubbed in dust—that one before whom you were mentioned and he did not send blessings upon you." So I said "Amīn."

Then, as I ascended, Gabriel said again, "May the nose of one be rubbed in dust—that one who found the month of Ramaḍān and did not obtain forgiveness," meaning one who did not fulfill the fasts, the prayers, and the ordinances of the faith.

Then Mr. Gabriel came a third time and asked him to reject the deeds of the one who met his parents or either of them but did not treat them well. So I said "Amīn."[^cite-34]

The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: "May Allah disgrace one who admonishes others to do good deeds and does not himself act upon them." Allah has rebuked such people in the Qur'ān:

⟪quran 2:44⟫

Do you command others to do good while you forget yourselves?

The famous companion of the Holy Prophet, Abū Dharr al-Ghiffārī, once stood near the door of the Ka'ba and addressed the people, saying, "O people, hear my counsel! Whoever journeys prepares provisions according to his journey. So too must you prepare for your journey to the Day of Judgment. A man asked, "O Abū Dharr, what is that provision?" He replied: "Perform the pilgrimage for the forgiveness of your grave sins, fast to ward off the severity of the Day of Judgment, and to protect yourself from the terror of the grave, rise in the darkness of the night and pray two units of prayer."[^cite-35]

### Counsel:

Surely, Allah is the Pardoning, the Merciful; yet He is also the Just and the Mighty, Swift in reckoning. Forgiveness of sins is His characteristic, but oppression, abuse, and cruelty toward people; unlawful transactions; and the loss and non-payment of rights are crimes that Allah does not forgive. Only those who have wronged others can grant them forgiveness. If Allah were to forgive the wrongs done to His servants without their consent, it would contradict His justice. Do not forget that the justice of the Master is powerful and stern, and none can escape it—neither small nor great. Keep careful account of your transactions. If there is deficiency or surplus, make it good. Give people their rights, for on the Day of Judgment you shall have to render them anyway, and you shall be punished for the wrong. Allah says, "The calamity that befalls you is because of what your hands have wrought."[^cite-36] Sickness, trouble, difficulty, and hardship are the consequences of one's own deeds. How good it would be if matters of debt and transactions were completed and cleared in one's lifetime; otherwise, without their settlement and clearance, there is no redemption and no salvation, and the punishment is terrible and severe. Wisdom lies in freeing oneself from such burdens as soon as possible and hastening, for the respite is short. The Lord of the Universe has said:

O builders of lofty palaces, cease! Verily, you are dwellers in the grave.[^cite-37]

## Istighfār — Seeking Forgiveness and Its Method

The Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: "Even if a person commits sins as numerous as the stars in the sky, as many as the sands upon the earth, and as many as the birds in the air—if he repents sincerely, Allah shall forgive him all his sins."[^cite-38] He said: "In order to seek the forgiveness (istighfār) of Allah, pursue seven things:

Say "Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm" (*In the Name of Allah, the Compassionate, the Merciful*) before all you do.

Praise be to Allah—say *al-Ḥamdu li-llāh* (*All praise belongs to Allah*) after everything.

When something good comes to you, attribute it to Allah. Say: "Good comes from your Lord, and evil comes from my soul." Say *Subḥān Allāh* (*Glory be to Allah*) upon all praise.

If you make a mistake, immediately ask Allah's forgiveness and seek His pardon.

If something abhorrent occurs, say, "There is no power and no strength except in Allah, the Most High, the Mighty" (*Lā ḥawla wa-lā quwwata illā bi-llāh al-ʿalī al-ʿaẓīm*).

If calamity befalls you, say, "Verily we belong to Allah, and verily to Him shall we return" (*Innā li-llāh wa-innā ilayhi rājiʿūn*).

Recite the testimony *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* (*There is no god but Allah*) at all times, morning and evening.[^cite-39]

It is narrated from the Imām Ḥusayn (upon him be peace) that he observed a man glorifying Allah and seeking forgiveness. He recited the following verse:

⟪quran 40:55⟫

So glorify the praises of your Lord and ask His forgiveness. Verily, He is the One Who accepts repentance.

Then he said, "O seeker of forgiveness, what are you doing? Do you not know what forgiveness truly is? Forgiveness is the station of repentance, and repentance is the station of certainty that encompasses six meanings. If you can do those six things, then you may ask for forgiveness; otherwise, do not ask!"[^cite-40]

He said: "First, regret what has passed and be assured that it shall be cleansed—as a pilgrim becomes pure of his sins upon returning from the Field of ʿArafāt.

Second, resolve that you shall not return to the sin—as milk shall never return after it leaves the cow's udder.

Third, pay back the rights of all people whom you have wronged, so that when you return to Allah, no burden weighs upon you.

Fourth, do not neglect a single duty; perform all that Allah has ordained upon you.

Fifth, consume with the fire of your heart and regret the flesh that has come upon you through the unlawful, until your skin meets your bone.

Sixth, grow new flesh from your good deeds and new actions, making it taste the agony of sorrow, just as you made your former flesh taste the sweetness of sin and transgression.

After you have done these six things, then if you wish, you may ask Allah's forgiveness—but not before!"[^cite-41]

*Majlis of Ḥaḍrat Mullā ʿAbdullāh Ṣāḥib*

## Two Things… (a maxim)

There are two things that are valued only by those who have lost them: youth and well-being.

There are two things whose value only the deprived understand: power and wealth.

There are two things whose presence is unwelcome: sickness and an impoverished relative.

There are two things that bring no good outcome: oppression and evil.

There are two things that are difficult to attain to their fullest extent: knowledge and intellect.

The reward of two things cannot be weighed: forgiveness and justice.

Two things form the foundation of religion: truth and faith.

There are two things that cannot be equaled: righteous piety and true faith.

The people of our community are like oranges—their fragrance is delicate and pure, and their outward appearance is most fair.[^cite-42]

## Questions to the Prophet and His Answers

A man came to the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) and asked him questions concerning worldly and spiritual matters.

O Messenger of Allah, I desire to become the greatest of men!

The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) replied: "Fear Allah, and you shall become the greatest of men." Through fear of God and following the path He has shown, the doors of knowledge and wisdom open to mankind.[^cite-43]

O Messenger of Allah, I desire to be esteemed among men!

The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: "The most esteemed man is the one who benefits people." Be of benefit to all.

O Messenger of Allah, I wish to become learned and a judge!

The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: "Do unto others as you would have them do unto you."[^cite-44]

O Messenger of Allah, I wish to be nearest to Allah!

He said: "Remember Allah abundantly, and you shall be nearest to Him."

O Messenger of Allah, I wish to become virtuous and good!

He said: "Pray as if you see Allah; and if that is not possible, then pray as if Allah sees you."[^cite-45]

O Messenger of Allah, I wish my faith to be complete!

O Messenger of Allah, I also wish to be obedient to Allah!

O Messenger of Allah, I desire to meet Allah free from all sin!

O Messenger of Allah, it is my wish to be raised with light on the Day of Resurrection!

O Messenger of Allah, I wish that Allah have mercy upon me!

O Messenger of Allah, I wish to be the greatest of all people!

## The Mothers of Sins — Pride (Kibr), Envy (Ḥasad), and Greed (Ḥirṣ)

Morality is one of four things that remain in the body even after the soul has departed:

The morality and character that have become habitual; the sciences he acquired; the beliefs he established; and the deeds he accomplished through his own effort and intention.

In respect to satanic morality, the first is like the pride (al-takabbur) of Iblīs; the second is like the greed (al-ḥirṣ) of Adam; and the third is like the envy (al-ḥasad) of Cain. These three traits are the root of all sins and evils.[^cite-52]

### Signs of Arrogance

Liking one's own opinion; abandoning the acceptance of truth and refusing to acknowledge it; disobeying the commands of those whose obedience is obligatory; stubbornness and transgression of proper bounds; oppression and coercion in one's dealings; unfairness in matters and taking lightly what is obligatory; abusing people and addressing them with rudeness; being harsh, arrogant, deceitful, and cunning in transactions; displaying greatness through pardon; denying the virtue of blessings.

Whoever believes in arrogance (al-takabbur) is an enemy of obedience, and obedience is the supreme name of Allah through which the heavens and the earth were established. The truth is obedience to God and accepting it. It has been said: "Whoever humbles himself before Allah, Allah shall make him great; and whoever is arrogant, Allah shall humble him."[^cite-53] Allah (Exalted is He) says:

⟪ar:الكبرياء ردائي والعظمۃ إزاري ، فمن نازعني واحداُ منھما عذبتہ⟫

Majesty is My cloak and greatness is My garment. Whosoever contends with Me in either of these, I shall punish him.[^cite-54]

Allah says in the Qur'ān: "Is not Hell the abode of the arrogant?"[^cite-55]

### Signs of Greed

False covetousness of things; running about to gather and accumulate provisions; hoarding excessively, stinginess, and inferiority born of fear of dependence; failing to benefit from what one possesses and not allowing others to benefit; rigidity in transactions, miscalculation, mistrust of the truthful, blame of the good; betrayal of trust, earning the unlawful and committing evil deeds; lying to hide the truth; making excuses in transactions; overstepping the bounds of antagonism; false oaths.[^cite-56]

### Signs of Envy

Nurturing malice and deceit and venting them upon finding opportunity; anger, ruthlessness, harshness, cursing, and talking nonsense; creating causes of conflict, whether openly or in secret; backbiting through lies and slander.[^cite-57]

Greed leads to deprivation. The envious person is an enemy to Allah's blessings. The one who envies gains nothing but envy itself. These traits and deeds should be shunned, for they are the morals of the devils. Those who bear hatred for one another are the army of Iblīs. Allah has said in the Qur'ān that when one group enters Hell, it shall curse the other.[^cite-58]

From this, it becomes clear that arrogance, envy, and greed are the root of all evils, leading to all mischiefs and transgressions. They create division among people; bonds of enmity form in hearts; and relationships are severed. Distance arises between brothers; love is extinguished; homes are destroyed; sorrow, grief, and bitterness arise in hearts; the soul is tormented; life becomes forbidden; and both this world and the Hereafter are ruined. May Allah protect all of you from these evils.[^cite-59]

## Ṣadaqa — Charity

All that we separate from ourselves with good intention and that which we extract from a sincere heart constitutes charity (ṣadaqa). If we purge ourselves of our sins, that too is our charity. Charity is practiced in three ways:

The highest charity is obligatory prayer performed in the presence of Allah.

Praying for others is also charity. While obligatory duties are reserved for Allah alone, supererogatory prayers may be performed on behalf of His servants as well as for Allah.

Everything that becomes known is charity. Maulānā ʿAlī was asked, "What is the known?" He replied: "Everything that a believer can convey is known. That is, every good deed of a believer is charity."[^cite-60]

### Recognition of Charity

Imām Jaʿfar (upon him be peace) says that the known can only be fulfilled through three qualities: *tasghīr*, *tassīr*, and *tajīl*.

*Tasghīr* means to diminish one's good deeds so that one may remain humble before Allah—that is, to offer one's prayer to Allah with utmost humility.

*Tassīr* means to perform it easily and conveniently. That is, after performing the deed, one should not boast of it or speak of it. When we offer our prayer to Allah, we should do so in such a manner that we recognize it as His right and acknowledge our neediness. Whether He accepts our prayers or not, we must pray. Whether the Imām accepts our form or not, we must cultivate proper form.

*Tajīl* means to hasten in performing this good deed.[^cite-61]

### A Statement

When an elder gives something to a younger person, it is called a gift. When a younger person gives something to an elder, it is called a reward, for thereby he connects himself to that higher one through his gift.[^cite-62]

## The Marks of a Person of Intellect (ʿAql)

Maulānā ʿAlī says that a person's intellect is recognized through three things: the gift, the messenger, and the book.[^cite-63]

*Al-Hadiyyah* (The Gift): A person's gift shows his intellect in determining whether the thing being given is useful to the recipient and will benefit him.

*Al-Rasūl* (The Messenger): The person through whom one sends a message demonstrates one's intellect in selecting someone capable of representing the sender's objectives.

*Al-Kitāb* (The Book): A person's writing and manner of speech reveal his intellect.[^cite-64]

The Messenger of Allah (may peace and blessings be upon him) said: "I have given my Community a gift—it is prayer; I have sent a Messenger who is ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib; and I have given a Book—it is the Qur'ān al-Majīd (the Glorious Qur'ān)."[^cite-65]

## Q&A with Ḥaḍrat Isḥāq Ṣāḥib

*In the Name of Allah, the Compassionate, the Merciful*

Ḥaḍrat Mullā Isḥāq Ṣāḥib, a distinguished scholar and guide, was asked by Jinnat Ḥāshim Yar Jung (who served as a judge in the High Court during the Nizam's reign) to answer questions for a guest book of his choosing. Below are these questions and answers:

What is your favorite quality?

Resurrecting the dead of ignorance.

What is your favorite trait in a man?

Worship of Allah, knowledge, and righteousness.

What is your favorite trait in a woman?

Chastity, devotion to her husband, and grace.

What is your favorite pursuit?

Anything in this world by which Allah and His Messenger are pleased.

What is your distinctive personal trait?

Self-knowledge.

What is the highest point of happiness for you?

To be called righteous among God's creatures.

What grieves you?

Goodness in creation coupled with evil in the sight of the Creator.

Your favorite color and flower?

White, pearls, and emeralds.

If you were not yourself, whom would you most wish to be?

My grandfather, Ḥaḍrat Mullā Ẓiyāʾ ʿAlī Ṣāḥib, son of al-Masʿūd, student of Shaykh Ibrāhīm Ṣāḥib.

Where do you most like to dwell?

In the vicinity of my Lord.

Your favorite prose author?

Ḥaḍrat Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib.

Your favorite poet?

Ḥaḍrat Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib.

Your favorite sculptor?

The Artist of Nature—the Creator.

Your favorite singer?

The melodies of David, upon him be peace.

Your favorite man?

Ḥaḍrat ʿAlī and Imām al-Ḥusayn, upon them be peace and blessings.

Your favorite woman?

Our Lady Khadījah and our Lady al-Malika ʿAwrā, daughter of Aḥmad.

Your favorite tale?

The story of Joseph and Zulaykha.

Your favorite food and drink?

Cheese and buttermilk, and whatever Allah provides and grants to drink.

Your favorite names?

Muḥammad and ʿAlī, Yaʿqūb, Ṭāhirah, Amīr.

What do you despise?

Regarding oneself as greater than one's station warrants.

Which persons in history do you most dislike?

Ibn Muljam, Yazīd the accursed, and all tyrants of religion.[^cite-66]

What is the state of your heart at present?

Trembling with the fear of Allah.

Which faults do you most readily forgive?

Every fault that I have the authority to forgive.

What is your favorite saying?

"And whoever places his trust in Allah—He is sufficient for him."[^cite-67] Whoever trusts in Allah, Allah is sufficient for him.
