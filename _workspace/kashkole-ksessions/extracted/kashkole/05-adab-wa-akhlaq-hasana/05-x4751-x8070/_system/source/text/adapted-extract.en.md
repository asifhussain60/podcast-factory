## Further Sayings of Wisdom

⟪ar:ان الحکیم قال⟫

| {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_open.gif}} Al-Bahour Arba'a! The world is the sea of sins, the sea of desires, the sea of death, and the grave is the sea of IMG_URL regret. /images/quote_closed.gif}} |
| --- |
| There are four types of oceans. The world is an ocean of sins, and the soul is a sea of lusts, and death is an ocean of deeds, and the grave is an ocean of regret.

The sea, in this teaching, signifies abundance and all-encompassing fullness, a particular attribute of existence itself. The world is a habitation from which no soul can escape the commission of sin—or rather, the world, ⟪ar-quote:دار السقط اللہ⟫ the house of God's wrath, presents countless opportunities and occasions for transgression. Similarly, the self generates desires in waves without number; should a person fail to exercise discernment through intellect, the nafs (self) shall lead him toward ruin and destruction. After death, nothing remains of a person save the record of deeds, good and evil; thus should one strive ceaselessly toward virtue. When a person is interred in the grave and experiences its chastisement, he regrets his misdeeds with profound intensity—wishing he had abstained from such acts in the earthly realm, or had instead increased in righteous works.[^cite-1]

---

| {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_open.gif}} Al-Masjid Parts and Zikr Allah Parts and Qur'an Parts {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_closed.gif}} |
| --- |
| There are three castles! A mosque is a fortress, the remembrance of Allah is a fortress and the recitation of the Qur'an is a fortress.

A fortress is a place of refuge where one fortifies oneself against the adversary. The speaker expounds that if a person desires to shield himself from Satan, three pathways are opened before him. The first is engagement in acts of worship and devotion. The second is constant remembrance of Allah—*dhikr* (remembrance)—for remembrance of the Divine elevates the powers of the human soul and diminishes the dominion of Satan. The third is recitation of the Qur'an coupled with reflection, as ⟪ar:مولانا علی زین العابدین علیہ السلام⟫ the Master has said: "When you recite verses of mercy, your heart yearns for the bounties of Allah; when you recite verses of punishment, your body trembles with fear of Him."[^cite-2] Thus ought a person to occupy himself continually with these three disciplines: either in prayer and worship, or in remembrance of Allah, or in recitation and meditation upon His ordinances and precepts.

---

| {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_open.gif}} Arba'a Tammaha Barba'a. All the prayers in prostration, and the fasting of the righteous in the form of al-Fitr, and the pilgrimage of the fidyyah, and the faith of jihad {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_closed.gif}} |
| --- |
| There are four things that accomplish four things. The completion of prayer is through prostration, the completion of fasting is through the observance of the fitrah, the completion of Hajj is from the animal of sacrifice, and the completion of faith is through jihad.

This maxim demonstrates that until the final deed is completed, the rewards of the preceding deed remain suspended, not yet crystallized. Just as one who labors through all the stations of prayer yet omits the prostration (*sujud*) has effectively not prayed at all—so too does the person who observes fasting but neglects the obligatory *fitrah* (alms-giving at the end of Ramadan) find his thirty days of toil rendered void. Should a pilgrim undergo the trials of the *Hajj* but fail to offer the sacrificial animal, his pilgrimage stands invalidated. In like manner, the perfection of faith is realized only through constant struggle (*jihād*). Herein lies the critical importance of the terminus: if a person exerts himself throughout his lifetime yet his life does not culminate in the *wilāya* (guardianship) of ʿAlī and his descendants, then all his striving is for naught.[^cite-3]

## Arbaʿa Jawāhir fī Jism Banī Ādam — The Four Jewels in the Body of the Children of Ādam

| {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_open.gif}} Arba'a Jawahir fi Body's Bani Adam Yazilah Barba'a Things. Ima al-Jawahir al-Aql al-Aql wa al-Hayya wa al-Amal al-Salih.  					Al-Ghazrab Yazil al-'Aql, al-Envy Yazil al-Din, Wa'l-Ta'a'a Yazil al-Hayya, and al-Ghaybah Yazil al-Salih {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_closed.gif}} |
| --- |
| There are four essences (real forces) in the human body which are destroyed by four things. The Four Jewels

There exist within the human body four jewels (*jawāhir*), four essential forces: intellect, religion, shame, and righteous deeds. Yet each of these treasures is imperiled by a corresponding corruption—anger destroys the intellect; envy destroys the religion; greed destroys shame; backbiting destroys the righteous deed.

### Anger

The first jewel is intellect (*al-ʿaql*), which conferred upon humanity the station to be called ⟪ar:اشرف المخلوقات⟫ the noblest of all created things. Allah, the Creator of all existence, has endowed His creation with many perfections, yet among all creatures, humankind alone has been invested with the title ⟪ar:'اشرف المخلوقات'⟫ of supreme nobility. This preeminence is granted because intellect enables a person to deliberate, to choose between good and evil, to accept righteousness or embrace transgression. Humankind is the sole creature for whom Paradise and Hell have been ordained, for we alone possess the capacity to recognize and pursue the good, or to reject it willfully. Even among all human beings, true nobility belongs to those gifted with the treasure of sound intellect—those whose righteous deeds evolve not as isolated acts but as a progressive transformation into nobler forms. Yet intellect itself is not nobility; its exercise is the true mark of honor. Similarly, to accept the pledge of allegiance (*bayʿa*) is not itself nobility, but rather to actualize that pledge in one's beliefs, intentions, pursuit of knowledge, and conduct—this is genuine nobility.[^cite-4]

The second jewel is religion (*dīn*), which grants a person the faculty to distinguish truth from falsehood. Religion is the sphere within which intellect operates and exerts itself. The third jewel is shame and modesty (*ḥayāʾ*), and the fourth is righteous deed (*ʿamal sāliḥ*).

Anger and wrath (*al-ghaḍab*) destroy the intellect. Only Allah possesses the prerogative to be angered at His servants for their transgressions. Anger corrupts memory and discernment. The Commander of the Faithful has said in one place: ⟪ar-quote:الغضب، أولہ الجنون و آخرہ الندم۔ ولم یندم فھو مجنون⟫ "Anger: its beginning is madness, its end is regret. And he who does not regret has remained mad." The Prophet (peace and blessings upon him) taught: "The best among you is he who becomes angry yet speedily returns to contentment; the worst is he who grows wrathful and tarries long in displeasure." When Moses grew wrathful and struck a man, killing him, he at once recognized his error and said: "This is from the work of Satan, who is an evident enemy."

> ⟪quran 28:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَدَخَلَ ٱلْمَدِينَةَ عَلَىٰ حِينِ غَفْلَةٍۢ مِّنْ أَهْلِهَا فَوَجَدَ فِيهَا رَجُلَيْنِ يَقْتَتِلَانِ هَذَا مِن شِيعَتِهِۦ وَهَذَا مِنْ عَدُوِّهِۦ ۖ فَٱسْتَغَثَهُ ٱلَّذِى مِن شِيعَتِهِۦ عَلَى ٱلَّذِى مِنْ عَدُوِّهِۦ فَوَكَزَهُۥ مُوسَىٰ فَقَضَىٰ عَلَيْهِ ۖ قَالَ هَذَا مِنْ عَمَلِ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ عَدُوٌّۭ مُّضِلٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And he entered the city at a time of unawareness of its people, and he found there two men fighting, - one of his party (his religion - from the Children of Israel), and the other of his foes. The man of his (own) party asked him for help against his foe, so Musa (Moses) struck him with his fist and killed him. He said: "This is of Shaitan's (Satan) doing, verily, he is a plain misleading enemy." *

In another utterance, the Commander of the Faithful declared: ⟪ar-quote:العفو عند الغضب⟫ "The noblest act is to show clemency whilst one is angry." And in yet another place: ⟪ar-quote:العفو عند المقدرۃ⟫ "To forgive even when you possess the power of vengeance." He also said: ⟪ar-quote:لکل شئی زکواۃ۔ العفو زکواۃ الظفر⟫ "Everything has its *zakāt* (purifying alms); forgiveness is the *zakāt* of victory."

### Envy

Envy (*al-hasad*) corrupts and destroys a person's religion. The purpose of religion is self-perfection and elevation. It is an instrument by which one shapes both this world and the world to come. Yet when jealousy takes hold, the envious person abandons the pursuit of his own good and becomes consumed with the destruction of others, caring nothing for his own loss. Thus did Qābīl become envious of Hābīl and slew him; yet the murder profited him naught save a grave transgression and the ruin of his eternal recompense.[^cite-5]

### Greed

Greed and avarice (*al-ṭamaʿ*) destroy a person's sense of shame and modesty, rendering him shameless. It is a mark of honor and dignity that one should extend his hand only before Allah. Yet when greed seizes the heart, shame dissolves: the person stretches forth his hand before others and cares not that his dignity crumbles among the people. His sole concern is the satisfaction of his appetite. This is a most calamitous condition.

### Backbiting

Backbiting (*al-ghibya*) consumes and destroys a person's righteous deeds, devouring his virtue as if it were devoured by flame. Backbiting always requires two parties—the speaker requires a listening ear. Thus is the listener an equal partner in the evil of the backbiter, becoming an accomplice in the waste and nullification of his own good works. 

Once, when the Prophet (peace and blessings upon him) was in the chamber of ʿĀʾisha, a woman arrived from a distant place seeking counsel on matters of knowledge and jurisprudence. After her departure, ʿĀʾisha remarked: "O Messenger of Allah, how dark and fat was this woman, how coarse her voice, how strange her gait!" The Messenger of Allah said: "ʿĀʾisha, you have engaged in backbiting!" ʿĀʾisha replied: "O Messenger of Allah, you know that I speak the truth; all that I have said is accurate." He responded: "Herein lies the very nature of backbiting—it is not falsehood. Backbiting is always true! But your truthfulness is this: if that woman without her veil had heard what you said, she would have been wounded and pained. That is the reality—backbiting is a truth that ought never be uttered."[^cite-6]

Moreover, Allah has likened backbiting to consuming the flesh of one's dead brother. The meat of the deceased is already forbidden and fills one with revulsion; yet Allah has specifically termed backbiting the flesh of a dead *brother*—which is rendered incomparably more abhorrent and vile.

## Al-Qalb Thalātha — The Heart Is of Three Kinds

⟪ar:و قال علی علیہ السلام:⟫

| {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_open.gif}} Al-Qalb Triple! The heart is busy with the world, the heart is busy with the eagle and the heart is busy with the master. The heart is busy in the world. Wama al-Qalb is busy with the Aqaba, Filah al-Jannah. Wama al-qalb al-mula engaged Fula al-Din {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_closed.gif}} |
| --- |
| There are three types of hearts. One heart is engaged in the world, the other is engaged in the Hereafter and the third heart is engaged in its Lord. For the heart that engages in the world, there is misery, torment and curse. Paradise is for the heart that is engaged in the Hereafter. And the heart that is preoccupied with its Lord attains religion.

The ⟪ar:'قلب'⟫⟪ar:رأئس البدن⟫ heart is the sovereign of the body; every action of a human being flows from the will and state of the heart. In his teaching, the Commander of the Faithful expounds that humankind strives for three things: the world, the Hereafter, and the Divine. The person who pursues this world and makes it his ultimate aim shall inherit three calamities: his heart shall become wrathful and turbulent; he shall be humiliated among people; and he shall be overtaken by misfortunes. These are the characteristic attributes that befall those absorbed in worldly pursuit. Yet the heart that continually contemplates the Hereafter and prepares its deeds for eternal recompense shall inherit Paradise—the reward of the righteous.[^cite-7]

This saying intimates that if Paradise is the recompense of those who contemplate the Hereafter, then surely Hell awaits those who chase after the world. The heart engaged in knowledge of its Lord—that is, contemplation of the Divine attributes (*al-ḥudūd al-ʿāliya*, the highest limits), supplication, and devotion to the great Imāms—attains *dīn* (religion), which is the positive transformation and perfection of one's state in this world. Thus, if pursuit of the world brings misery, degradation, and calamity, then surely attachment to the Divine brings mercy, honor, and blessing.

The second consideration of profound importance: engagement with the world and the Hereafter remain matters of individual benefit—the world-seeker profits himself; the seeker of Paradise likewise pursues his own salvation. Yet the person who strives in knowledge of his Lord directs his effort toward the sake of his Master Most High. For this reason, such a person's station increases greatly in the sight of Allah.

<!-- section 4 (id=1447, raw_sort=4): ʿĀrif Billāh Labīb — The God-Conscious Sage of Discernment -->

It has been transmitted that:

| {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_open.gif}} Arif Billah Labib wal Qalb Basir wal Amal Li Allah Kathir wa Arif Billah Wafi wa Qalb Zaki wa Amal Safi {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_closed.gif}} |
| --- |
| He who has the knowledge of Allah is wise. And his heart is all-seeing, and he is much more proactive in the cause of Allah. And Arif is loyal to Allah, his heart is pure and his deeds are pure.

The one who possesses *maʿrifat Allāh* (gnosis or intimate knowledge of Allah) is *labīb* (wise, discerning). His heart is *basīr* (perceptive, all-seeing)—able to apprehend both the apparent and to penetrate to inner realities. His distinguishing characteristic is that he is abundantly *akthar ʿamal li-Allāh* (active and prolific in deeds for the sake of Allah). He does not confine himself to the bare minimum of obligation; rather, he pursues all forms of worship, glorification (*tasbīḥ*), and praise (*taʿẓīm*). He endeavors ceaselessly to please his Lord and to gain His favor. Allah declares in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 8:74⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَهَاجَرُوا۟ وَجَهَدُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَٱلَّذِينَ آوَوا۟ وَّنَصَرُوٓا۟ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ حَقًّۭا ۚ لَّهُم مَّغْفِرَةٌۭ وَرِزْقٌۭ كَرِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And those who believed, and emigrated and strove hard in the Cause of Allah (Al-Jihad), as well as those who gave (them) asylum and aid; - these are the believers in truth, for them is forgiveness and Rizqun Karîm (a generous provision i.e. Paradise).*

And in another place:

> ⟪quran 22:78⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَجَهِدُوا۟ فِى ٱللَّهِ حَقَّ جِهَادِهِۦ ۚ هُوَ ٱجْتَبَىٰكُمْ وَمَا جَعَلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلدِّينِ مِنْ حَرَجٍۢ ۚ مِّلَّةَ أَبِيكُمْ إِبْرَهِيمَ ۚ هُوَ سَمَّىٰكُمُ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ مِن قَبْلُ وَفِى هَذَا لِيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ شَهِيدًا عَلَيْكُمْ وَتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَىٰ ٱلنَّاسِ ۚ فَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَٱعْتَصِمُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ هُوَ مَوْلَىٰكُمْ ۖ فَنِعْمَ ٱلْمَوْلَىٰ وَنِعْمَ ٱلنَّصِيرُ ‎⟫
> *And strive hard in Allah's Cause as you ought to strive (with sincerity and with all your efforts that His Name should be superior). He has chosen you (to convey His Message of Islamic Monotheism to mankind by inviting them to His religion, Islam), and has not laid upon you in religion any hardship, it is the religion of your father Ibrahim (Abraham) (Islamic Monotheism). It is He (Allah) Who has named you Muslims both before and in this (the Quran), that the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) may be a witness over you and you be witnesses over mankind! So perform As­Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), give Zakat and hold fast to Allah [i.e. have confidence in Allah, and depend upon Him in all your affairs] He is your Maula (Patron, Lord, etc.), what an Excellent Maula (Patron, Lord, etc.) and what an Excellent Helper!*

Two interpretations (*taʾwīl*, esoteric exegesis) emerge in these verses. In the first verse, the command is to wage *jihād* (struggle and striving) in the way of Allah—encompassing jihād of the self, jihād of wealth, and all forms of spiritual exertion. In the second verse, Allah states: ⟪ar:'جھد'⟫ "Strive in the way of Allah," which signifies striving to attain the knowledge of His Oneness (*tawḥīd*) and intimate knowledge of Him, and to comprehend one's own position and station, and whose dominion is the heavens and whose is the earth. To know these realities (*al-ḥudūd*, the limits) and to actualize their rights is what is truly meant by ⟪ar:'جاہدو فی اللہ'⟫ "striving in Allah."[^cite-8]

The person who exerts himself to attain knowledge of his Lord is wise (*labīb*), and his heart is visionary (*basīr*)—capable of perceiving both the outward form and penetrating to inward reality. The mark of such a person is that he labors abundantly for the sake of Allah. He does not limit himself to scant obligations; rather, he follows all acts of worship, invocation, and glorification. He strives earnestly to please his Lord (may He be exalted) and to earn His pleasure. And he who possesses such intimate knowledge of Allah (*ʿārif bi-Allāh*) remains loyal and true to the Imām and his system of *daʿwa* (the summons, the invitation to the faith). He fulfills both the external and inward duties entrusted to him, as is said:

> ⟪quran 18:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَتَحْسَبُهُمْ أَيْقَاظًۭا وَهُمْ رُقُودٌۭ ۚ وَنُقَلِّبُهُمْ ذَاتَ ٱلْيَمِينِ وَذَاتَ ٱلشِّمَالِ ۖ وَكَلْبُهُم بَسِطٌۭ ذِرَاعَيْهِ بِٱلْوَصِيدِ ۚ لَوِ ٱطَّلَعْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ لَوَلَّيْتَ مِنْهُمْ فِرَارًۭا وَلَمُلِئْتَ مِنْهُمْ رُعْبًۭا ‎⟫
> *And you would have thought them awake, while they were asleep. And We turned them on their right and on their left sides, and their dog stretching forth his two forelegs at the entrance [of the Cave or in the space near to the entrance of the Cave (as a guard at the gate)]. Had you looked at them, you would certainly have turned back from them in flight, and would certainly have been filled with awe of them.*

In this verse, reference is made to the dog as an exemplar of extreme loyalty to his master (the Malik, the owner). The ⟪ar:'داعی'⟫ *dāʿī* (the summoner, the one who invites to the path) follows every command of his Imām without hesitation, undeterred by any difficulty. His heart is purified of all impurities of doubt, polytheism, and disbelief; his deeds are pure and unmixed, undertaken solely for Allah's sake. Therein he acts not from his own desire, nor does he seek praise from people or derive any worldly benefit from his actions.

## ʿAlī's Verses at the Burial of Mawlātnā Fāṭima

Mawlānā ʿAlī placed Mawlānā Fāṭima into the grave and covered it with soil. As the people stood near, he cried out in grief: ⟪ar:فاطمۃ! فاطمۃ!⟫ "O Fāṭima! O Fāṭima! Who will answer?" Then he intoned:

⟪ar-quote:مَا لِی وَقَفتُ عَلیَ القُبُورِ مُسَلِّمَۃ         قبرُ الحبیبِ فلا یَرُدُّ جوابِ⟫

*Why do I stand over graves, yet the grave of the beloved returns no greeting?*

This was she who rose at the slightest signal from me. Today my voice echoes, yet there comes no response.

⟪ar-quote:أحبیبُ مَا لَکَ لاَ تَرُدُّ جوابَنا                   أنَسیتَ بَعدِ خُلَّتِ الأحبابِ⟫

*O beloved, why do you not answer? Have you forgotten the friendship of companions?*

Do the dead who enter the grave forget? Yes, I believe they do forget. All bonds of fellowship dissolve in that darkness.

⟪ar-quote:لکلِّ اجتماع من خَلِیلَینِ فُرقۃٌ           وکُلَّ الذَّی قَبلَ المماتِ قَلیلُ⟫

*Every gathering of friends must part; all that precedes death is fleeting.*

Of course—every companionship dissolves, every connection breaks, every bond shatters like stone. Death arrives and separates. The people sleep in their graves; none sleeps beside them. Today Fāṭima has been parted from me.[^cite-9]

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّ افتِقَادِی فاطمۃَ بعد أحمدا           دلیلٌ علی ان لَّا یَدُومُ خلیلُ⟫

*That I have lost Fāṭima after Aḥmad is proof that no companion endures.*

With these very hands I once lowered Muḥammad (peace and blessings upon him) into the grave. Today with these same hands I bid farewell to Fāṭima. And thus it has become manifest to me: no one can sustain another in this realm. However long a companion walks with us, that companionship must break.
