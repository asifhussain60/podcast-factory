## Ali and False Allegations Concerning the Qur'an

There exists among certain Shi'a communities a group that holds a grave misconception: that the Qur'an was revealed to ʿAli ibn Abi Talib rather than to Muhammad ibn ʿAbd Allah (peace be upon him), and that in the original text, the names of ʿAli and his progeny appeared explicitly and clearly, only to be subsequently excised. According to this erroneous belief, the Qur'an has undergone alteration in the manner that Jews and Christians once altered the Torah and Gospel. This notion stands in direct contradiction to revealed truth, for Allah has issued an unambiguous covenant of protection over the Qur'an itself:

⟪quran 15:9⟫

> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّا نَحْنُ نَزَّلْنَا ٱلذِّكْرَ وَإِنَّا لَهُۥ لَحَفِظُونَ ‎⟫

Before we proceed to refute this false doctrine, it is appropriate to clarify the hierarchical distinction between Muhammad and ʿAli—that is, between the ranks of Prophet and successor. By explicating this difference both through revealed law and through rational demonstration, the orthodox belief of the community of truth shall emerge luminous and unambiguous.

### The Prophet Muhammad as Adam

According to both revealed law and the heavenly scriptures, Adam is the progenitor of all mankind, and Eve was created from among his ribs. Allah declares:

⟪quran 4:1⟫

> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱتَّقُوا۟ رَبَّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍۢ وَحِدَةٍۢ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًۭا كَثِيرًۭا وَنِسَآءًۭ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى تَسَآءَلُونَ بِهِۦ وَٱلْأَرْحَامَ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ عَلَيْكُمْ رَقِيبًۭا ‎⟫

The lexicographers of the exoteric interpretation (tafsīr) confirm that "nafs wāḥida" (the single soul) refers to Adam, who was fashioned from clay, while the creation of his mate denotes Eve, and the proliferation of males and females speaks to the multitude of their descendants.[^cite-1]

Yet the people of esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl) teach that each prophet constitutes the renewal of his age, for he is the founder and originator of his time, to which the entire epoch is ascribed. Just as Adam was the first human being to manifest the form of humanity, and the first to be granted knowledge of all names and earthly things, so likewise in the final age, the Prophet Muḥammad—whom the Qur'an names Seal of the Messengers (Khātam al-Nabiyyīn) and Master of the Sent Ones (Sayyid al-Mursalīn)—emerged as the first to embody the form of ḥanīfiyya (pure monotheism) in his time. He stands foremost not in body alone, but in his luminous inner reality, and first in the acquisition of the sciences of the hereafter.[^cite-2]

### ʿAli ibn Abi Talib as Eve

As Allah created Adam—whom He designated "the single soul"—from dust, and from him fashioned his wife Eve, so too did He create from the Prophet (peace be upon him) his spiritual and initiatic successor, ʿAli ibn Abi Talib, in a manner both mystical and esoteric. The Commander of the Faithful (Amīr al-Muʾminīn) bears the burden of the Prophet's trust and stands as custodian of mysteries, revelation, and the sciences of the Qur'an. This is precisely as a woman safeguards the seed of her male and gives form to the formless within her womb.[^cite-3] By reason of this sublime correspondence, the Prophet declared, "O ʿAli, you and I are the parents of all believers"—a saying that would be absurd and false if taken in the literal, corporeal sense, for manifestly the two of you cannot be the physical progenitors of mankind. Rather, the Prophet's declaration of fatherhood is established by his own words: he is father to the believers by spiritual and legal necessity, not by carnal generation. When the Prophet's wives are designated mothers of the believers, the Prophet himself must be their father—a station established religiously, not physically.

From Adam and Eve were born multitudes of men and women; in like manner, from Muhammad and ʿAli there flowered spiritually and psychologically the line of Imams (silsilat al-Aʾimma), illuminating all generations. The difference between Muhammad and ʿAli, then, mirrors the difference between heaven and earth, between male and female, between Adam and Eve. How, then, could the Qur'an have been revealed to one who came into being through the agency of the Prophet?

### The Nature of Revelation

Let us now turn to consider what revelation truly is and what is signified by revelation granted to the prophets. The fundamental distinction between ourselves and the prophets lies in this: we are servants enslaved to nature, our souls bound by lust and enslaved to the pursuit of bodily pleasure, concerned solely with mending the affairs of this world. The prophets, by contrast, stand in the opposite condition. Nature serves them; they have abandoned lust and corporeal desire, dedicating themselves to the perfection of the hereafter.

For this reason, we remain dependent upon the five corporeal senses for the whole of our knowledge and perception. The prophets, by virtue of their acuity of intellect (ʿaql) and luminosity of soul, acquire and comprehend the sciences of religion and the hereafter without recourse to the outward senses. They then clothe these sciences in meaningful words fashioned from letters, conveying them to us through speech, which our ears receive and from which we derive benefit. The prophets stand so near to the angels by reason of their asceticism, worship, and purity of soul that they readily acquire these sciences from them, clothing them thereafter in words composed of alphabetic letters, thereby transmitting them to our faculty of hearing. This is why Allah declares:

⟪quran 26:193⟫
⟪quran 26:194⟫
⟪quran 26:195⟫

> ⟪ar:‏نَزَلَ بِهِ ٱلرُّوحُ ٱلْأَمِينُ ‎⟫
>
> ⟪ar:‏عَلَىٰ قَلْبِكَ لِتَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنذِرِينَ ‎⟫
>
> ⟪ar:‏بِلِسَانٍ عَرَبِىٍّۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫

The Trustworthy Spirit (al-Rūḥ al-Amīn)—the Angel Gabriel—descended with the Qur'an upon the heart of the Prophet, not upon his ears. What descends upon the heart requires no words; it is transmitted as pure knowledge. Then comes the command: that he clarify this Qur'an in lucid Arabic speech and warn the people. In other words, the Qur'anic content revealed to his heart as unveiling—without words, without intermediary—is then conveyed and explained to the people of this world in articulate form.

When revelation descended upon the Prophet, those present witnessed upon him a profound burden and heaviness. He would lose consciousness; sweat would drench his blessed body; and there descended upon him such a state of abstraction that even if a piece of flesh were cut from his form, he would perceive nothing. It was this condition that caused the disbelievers and adversaries to call him mad—an accusation Allah Himself repudiated. Yet this state itself testifies to the majesty of revelation and the Prophet's elevation above ordinary human condition.

### The Rational Proof of the Prophet's Eminence

Let us now examine this matter through reason. When we survey all genera, we discover in each an exemplar that encompasses the nobility of the whole species. Consider a stone: among all stones exists one variety called the red ruby. Though it too is stone, certain qualities elevate it above all others. Allah distinguished it, granting it special color and luminosity by the influence of the sun. The Prophets stand in exactly such relation to mankind. Though they are human beings as we are, they resemble rubies among stones—incomparable in beauty, form, and priceless worth. Allah granted them the power of prophethood and enabled them to bear the burden of His trust. Similarly, among plants there exists a nobility of grain; among flowers, the date palm; and among animals, humanity—superior to all by reason of intellect, reflection, and reason. The Prophets represent the fullest flowering of humanity through purity of soul and nobility of character. Those who follow them, the Imams (Aʾimma), constitute the next rank in this sacred hierarchy.

### On the Alteration of the Qur'an's Meaning

Secondly, those who spread falsehood and corruption claim that the present Qur'an is deficient, that its wording has been altered, that it once contained the names of ʿAli and the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt—peace be upon them—only for these to be removed. They say the Qur'an has been distorted as the Jews and Christians distorted their heavenly books. This belief is baseless and false. The words of the Qur'an cannot be altered. The Qur'an present today is the very same that the Messenger of Allah praised and preserved.

What has indeed occurred, however, is this: the disbelievers in the guardianship (wilāya) of ʿAli and the enemies of the Ahl al-Bayt—peace be upon them—have arbitrarily reinterpreted (taʾwīl) the meanings and significations of its verses. They have twisted the sense to suit their own desires. The verses that honor the infallible Imams (al-aʾimma al-maʿṣūmūn) remain present in the text, yet their true meaning has been obscured.

Consider, for example, Allah's words:

⟪quran 2:143⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونُ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫

In this verse, the "middle nation" (umma wasṭ) refers to the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt, who serve as witnesses to all peoples. Each Imam is a witness to the people of his time, and the Prophet is witness over all of them. Yet the enemies of the Imamate and guardianship have reinterpreted this to mean that every Muslim is the middle of the nation and that all Muslims are mutual witnesses. Such an interpretation is patently absurd and void of sense. If every Muslim is a witness, then who is the one being testified to (al-mashhūd)? If one claims it is the Jews, Christians, and disbelievers, then the interpretation becomes even more confused and incoherent—for the rulings of Islam are incumbent only upon Muslims, not upon non-believers. The function of witnesses is to testify whether the followers of the Messenger have adhered to his way and heeded his commands. What bearing does this have on Jews, Christians, and polytheists? What meaning can there be in testifying against them?

In this manner, the exegetes of the common people have altered the meaning precisely as Allah describes:

⟪quran 5:13⟫

> ⟪ar:‏فَبِمَا نَقْضِهِم مِّيثَقَهُمْ لَعَنَّهُمْ وَجَعَلْنَا قُلُوبَهُمْ قَسِيَةًۭ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ عَن مَّوَاضِعِهِۦ ۙ وَنَسُوا۟ حَظًّۭا مِّمَّا ذُكِّرُوا۟ بِهِۦ ۚ وَلَا تَزَالُ تَطَّلِعُ عَلَىٰ خَآئِنَةٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا مِّنْهُمْ ۖ فَٱعْفُ عَنْهُمْ وَٱصْفَحْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُحْسِنِينَ ‎⟫

Words are displaced from their rightful places, substituted with distorted meanings. Yet these are the words of Allah and the Imams—peace be upon them.

In similar fashion, the verse of mutual supplication (Mubaḥalā), revealed solely in honor of the Panjtan al-Āhir (the Five Pure Ones) and their sacred station, has been seized by these exegetes and expanded to include all people. The verse spoke only of Muḥammad, ʿAli, Fāṭima, Ḥasan, and Ḥusayn, yet it has been appropriated to encompass the community at large.

The letters and lines of the Qur'an, it is true, have not been physically altered. Yet profound changes have been wrought in their meanings. The Qur'an itself attests to such distortion when it recounts Satan's boast:

⟪quran 4:119⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَلَأُضِلَّنَّهُمْ وَلَأُمَنِّيَنَّهُمْ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُبَتِّكُنَّ آذَانَ ٱلْأَنْعَامِ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُغَيِّرُنَّ خَلْقَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَمَن يَتَّخِذِ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ وَلِيًّۭا مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ فَقَدْ خَسِرَ خُسْرَانًۭا مُّبِينًۭا ‎⟫

"I shall lead them astray; I shall fill them with vain desires. I shall command them to mutilate the ears of cattle, and command them to alter the creation of Allah."

It is not outwardly apparent that Satan and his company have changed the physical forms of people, nor are they capable of doing so. Had such alteration been possible, there would be a manifest difference between the forms of the disbelievers and the believers—the disbelievers would bear deformed shapes distinct from the human form created by Allah. Yet this has not come to pass. What Satan has distorted, rather, are the spiritual and psychological features of his followers. He has corrupted their beliefs, destroyed their inner form, and twisted their understanding. In this way, the apparent words of the Qur'an remain preserved and inviolate, while the meanings have been perverted and obscured.

*(Kitāb al-Majālis al-Muʾayyada, Sayyidunā al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)*

## The Disputation of al-Maʾmun al-Rashid with Forty Scholars

This is an account of a celebrated debate that took place between al-Maʾmun al-Rashid and Qāḍī Isḥāq, together with forty other prominent Sunni jurists. The exchange is preserved in the classical work *al-ʿAqd al-Farīd*. Al-Maʾmun al-Rashid asserted that Imam ʿAli ibn Abi Talib—peace be upon him—was the finest of all Companions and the most worthy of the caliphate. Qāḍī Isḥāq and the forty legal authorities opposed this claim, yet al-Maʾmun stood alone against them all. During this gathering, the veil of dominion and suppression was lifted; all participants enjoyed complete liberty of expression. From dawn until near midday, the two sides debated vigorously. In the end, justice was manifest: the field of argument remained always in al-Maʾmun's hands.

---

**Al-Maʾmun:**

My religion teaches me that after the Holy Prophet—peace be upon him—ʿAli ibn Abi Talib—peace be upon him—is the best of all caliphs and the most deserving of the caliphate.

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

I perceive no rational foundation for what you claim regarding ʿAli. Since you have accepted this debate, I request that you furnish proof of your assertion.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Qāḍī Isḥāq, permit me first to ask you: by what means do men increase in nobility relative to one another?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

By virtuous deeds.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Precisely. Now tell me: if a person held the highest station during the lifetime of the Prophet, yet after the Prophet's death, others performed deeds of virtue surpassing his own, would those later individuals become more noble than he who was supreme in the Prophet's time?

Upon hearing this question, Qāḍī Isḥāq fell into perplexity.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

You cannot answer "yes," for I would then ask: have not many others had opportunity for virtuous deeds—jihād, pilgrimage, fasting, prayer, and charity—exceeding those who came before? By your logic, all of them would exceed the earlier ones in nobility!

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Indeed, no one could ever equal one who held supremacy in the Prophet's own time.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Consider now: how many prophetic reports (aḥādīth) and traditions concerning the virtues of ʿAli ibn Abi Talib have been transmitted by your own Companions, Followers, traditionists (muḥaddithūn), and those scholars whom we all regard as guides and leaders of our faith? Compare these with the aḥādīth you have received regarding the virtues of Abū Bakr. If they equal those of ʿAli, you may claim Abū Bakr's superiority to ʿAli. No, by Allah! Combine the virtues of Abū Bakr and ʿUmar together, then compare that combination with the virtues of ʿAli alone. Even if the combined virtues of the two were merely equal to ʿAli's, you would have grounds to claim them his superiors. No, by Allah! Rather, gather the virtues of Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, and ʿUthmān—all three—and place them alongside the virtues of ʿAli alone. Even should they prove equal, you may declare the three superior. No, by Allah! Nay, assemble the virtues of the Ten Whom the Prophet Testified Would Enter Paradise (al-ʿAshara al-Mubashshara), whose excellence the Prophet himself witnessed, and aggregate all their virtues together, comparing them to those of ʿAli ibn Abi Talib. Should they prove greater, then you have your case. Otherwise, abandon this false notion.

Now, setting all of this aside: Qāḍī Isḥāq, tell me—what constituted the noblest deed at the time when the Prophet was sent forth?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

The utterance of the testimony of faith with sincere conviction.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

But was it not nobler still to be foremost in accepting Islam?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Indeed, it was!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Allah declares in the Holy Qur'an—those foremost are only those who were first to embrace Islam. In this verse, the "foremost" are those who took the lead in converting to Islam. Tell me: do you know of anyone who became Muslim before ʿAli?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Indeed, ʿAli embraced Islam at a tender age, at which time the Prophet could not have troubled him. Abū Bakr reached the age of discretion (kamāl awwal, primary perfection) before becoming Muslim, when he was in distress.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

First, tell me who was the very first person to embrace Islam. Then I shall argue with you regarding maturity and distress.

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Without doubt, ʿAli embraced Islam before Abū Bakr, though he was but a child!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

It is true that ʿAli embraced Islam at a young age. Now tell me: did ʿAli accept Islam by the Prophet's invitation, or was he divinely inspired?

Qāḍī Isḥāq grew troubled at this question and fell silent in thought.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

You cannot say ʿAli was divinely inspired, for then you would rank ʿAli above the Prophet himself—since the Messenger of Allah had not yet received inspiration at that time. Rather, it was only with Gabriel's arrival that the Prophet came to know Islam.

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Assuredly, ʿAli was not divinely inspired. Rather, it was the Prophet who invited him to Islam.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Then this cannot be so without one of two conditions: either the Prophet invited ʿAli to Islam by Allah's command, or by his own whim and preference?

Qāḍī Isḥāq was bewildered.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

You cannot accuse the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—of speaking from personal desire, for Allah Himself testifies of the Prophet that he does not speak from personal inclination, but only speaks what is revelation. And this is precisely what He says.

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Of course not. Rather, by Allah's command, the Prophet invited ʿAli.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Is it permissible for Allah to command His Messengers to invite a person who is not yet capable of bearing any hardship?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Allah forbid! Not at all.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Qāḍī Isḥāq, you said that ʿAli embraced Islam while young, at an age when it was impermissible for him to endure hardship. Does this mean the Prophet caused harm by inviting his child to Islam in a matter beyond the child's capacity? Furthermore, if such a child were to apostatize after accepting the Prophet's invitation and becoming Muslim, would there be no punishment for that apostasy? Was it permissible for the Messenger of Allah to harm a child in this way? Can you say such a thing of the Messenger of Allah?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Allah forbid! Not at all.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Therefore, it is established that you believe the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—invited ʿAli to Islam and bestowed upon him supremacy over all creatures. By reason of this excellence, the Prophet distinguished ʿAli from all others, so that people might understand his virtues. Had Allah commanded the Messenger to invite absolute children indiscriminately, the Messenger would have invited other children of his family and kin as he did ʿAli!

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Of course!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Do you know whether the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—invited any other child from his family or relatives to Islam? I ask this so you do not think that merely because ʿAli was his cousin, the Messenger invited him, as though ʿAli possessed no intrinsic excellences.

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

I do not know, and therefore cannot say whether the Messenger invited another child or not.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Qāḍī Isḥāq, do you suppose you will be questioned on the Day of Resurrection regarding matters you did not know and did not understand?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

No.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Then why do you speak and argue about matters Allah has removed from your knowledge and from ours, without cause? Very well, tell me: what has been the noblest deed since the beginning of revelation?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Jihād in the path of Allah!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Can you find among the Companions of the Prophet any whose services in jihād match those of ʿAli?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

In which battle?

**Al-Maʾmun:**

In any battle whatsoever!

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Well, let us take the Battle of Badr.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

The Battle of Badr! Are not the exploits of others far inferior to those of ʿAli in that engagement? Tell me: how many of the polytheists were slain?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Approximately sixty.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

And how many fell by ʿAli's hand alone?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

I do not know.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Know, then, that ʿAli slew twenty-three of them, while the remainder of the Muslim army together slew only forty. Now you may comprehend the measure of his superiority.

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

However, the Holy Prophet—peace be upon him—also saw that Abū Bakr was present in this battle, standing with the Messenger of Allah in the command pavilion.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Yes. But what were they doing there?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

They were planning strategy!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Woe unto you! Did the Messenger of Allah formulate a separate strategy? Or was he one with the Messenger? If the Messenger of Allah possessed a strategy, did the Prophet take them as his partners, or were they dependent upon his judgment? Which of these three positions do you maintain?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

The Messenger did not conceive a strategy apart from himself, nor did they become partners with the Prophet, nor was the Messenger of Allah in need of their counsel.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Brother, what then is the virtue of sitting in the command pavilion? Is not one who fights with the sword, protecting the Messenger of Allah, surely nobler than one who sits in comfort?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Yet jihād was being waged by the entire army. What, then, was ʿAli's distinguishing characteristic?

**Al-Maʾmun:**

True, all were fighting. But he who fights with the sword, defending the Messenger of Allah and those with him, is certainly superior to those who sit comfortably. Have you not read in the Holy Qur'an that those who remain at home and those who strive with their lives and property are not equal? Allah has given superiority to those who strive with their lives and wealth over those who sit at home. Though Allah has promised good to all believers, He has granted the strivers a great reward above those at rest.[^cite-4]

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

But Abū Bakr and ʿUmar too were mujāhidūn!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Did Abū Bakr and ʿUmar possess superiority over those who did not participate in that battle?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Of course!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

In the same manner, is not he who sacrifices his life and fights superior to Abū Bakr and ʿUmar, who sit comfortably in the command pavilion?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Yes, that is also true.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Qāḍī Isḥāq, you recite the Qur'an. Tell me: what is Sūrat al-Insān?

Qāḍī Isḥāq began to recite the verse. When he reached the verse concerning those who feed the poor, the orphans, and the captives for the love of Allah, saying they feed them only for Allah's countenance, desiring no reward and no thanks, al-Maʾmun interrupted:

**Al-Maʾmun:**

In whose honor was this verse revealed?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

In the honor of ʿAli ibn Abi Talib.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Do you know that when ʿAli fed the poor, the orphan, and the captive, he did not say, "We feed you seeking only Allah's countenance"? Have you heard that Allah has praised ʿAli as He has praised no one else?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

ʿAli did not utter those words, nor has such praise been revealed in the Qur'an for anyone!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Precisely! And the reason is that Allah Himself was fully aware of ʿAli's noble character. Now tell me: do you testify that the Ten Are the Inhabitants of Paradise—those to whom the Prophet gave glad tidings of Paradise?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

I do testify!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

If a person says, "I do not know whether this ḥadīth is authentic, or whether the Messenger of Allah truly spoke it," does that make him a disbeliever (kāfir)?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Allah forbid, not at all!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

But if that same person says, "I do not know whether Sūrat al-Ḥajj is the word of Allah or not," does he become a disbeliever?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Yes, he becomes one!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Explain to me the difference between these two statements! And tell me, Qāḍī Isḥāq: do you also narrate ḥadīths?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Yes.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

If you know a ḥadīth, relate it to me!

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

I narrate the ḥadīth of the roasted bird (ṭayr): A roasted bird was presented to the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him. At that moment, he prayed, "O Allah, send to me the one whom You love most, so that he may partake of this meal with me!" At that very moment, ʿAli came, and the two of them together ate the bird.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Qāḍī Isḥāq, until now I was debating with you believing you not an enemy of truth. But now your enmity toward truth has become evident! Are you certain this ḥadīth is authentic (ṣaḥīḥ)?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Yes, this ḥadīth is sound, for it is narrated by those traditionists whose reports cannot be rejected.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Do you comprehend that if a person believes this ḥadīth authentic and yet still maintains that someone in the Ummah was superior to Imam ʿAli—peace be upon him—such a person must necessarily hold one of three positions. Either: Allah did not answer the prayer of the Messenger of Allah (meaning, Allah did not send him whom He loved most). Or: though that person was the best among people, Allah nonetheless loved another most. Or: Allah did not distinguish between the best and the superior, and therefore sent ʿAli in response to the Prophet's prayer for another reason. Which of these three positions do you affirm?

Qāḍī Isḥāq became greatly distressed upon hearing this and could not speak.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Qāḍī Isḥāq, you certainly cannot be satisfied with any of these three positions! Otherwise, I shall extract from you a confession of your error. If a fourth interpretation exists, please state it!

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

I am humbled in my response, but Abū Bakr also possesses certain virtues.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Indeed, if there were no virtue in him, it would be meaningless to say ʿAli is superior to Abū Bakr. For the superior is one who possesses more virtue than the other. But what virtue did you recall at that moment?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

The verse of the cave, in which Allah says:

⟪quran 9:40⟫

...that Abū Bakr was the Companion of the Messenger of Allah!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Surely, Allah called Abū Bakr the Companion of the Prophet—peace be upon him. Yet Allah also called a disbeliever the "companion" of one whom Allah was pleased with and who was pleased with Allah. Allah declares:

⟪quran 18:37⟫

His companion said to him while conversing with him, "Do you disbelieve in your Lord Who created you from dust..." 

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

But that companion was a disbeliever, whereas Abū Bakr was a believer!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

That is precisely the point! If Allah designates a disbeliever as the "companion" of one whom Allah is pleased with, then it is certainly possible for Him to designate a believer as companion to His Prophet. Yet that designation alone does not make such a person the best believer!

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Indeed, this verse is of great significance for Abū Bakr, for Allah says: "The second of the two" (thānī ithnain). At that time, Abū Bakr and the Messenger of Allah were in the cave, and the Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—was saying to his companions, "Do not grieve! Indeed, Allah is with us."

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Qāḍī Isḥāq, tell me: what was the cause of Abū Bakr's grief? Was it joy or sorrow?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

It was on account of the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him. Abū Bakr feared that some calamity might befall the Prophet.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

That does not answer my question! What was the cause of his grief? Was it from joy or from sorrow (gham)?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

It was from the pleasure (raḍā) of Allah!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Then it is established that Allah sent to us a Messenger who forbade people from the pleasure (raḍā) of Allah!

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Allah forbid! This is impossible!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Did you not just say that it was from the pleasure of Allah that Abū Bakr grieved?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Of course, I said so.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Yet do you not see in the Qur'an that the Messenger of Allah forbade them from grief, saying, "Do not grieve"? In this way, did the Messenger forbid the pleasure of Allah or not?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

What an error I have made!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Now tell me what Allah Almighty said:

⟪quran 9:40⟫

"Allah comforted them." Does "them" refer to the Messenger of Allah or to Abū Bakr?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

No, it refers to the Messenger of Allah.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

And in this verse:

⟪quran 9:25⟫

"Allah sent down His Sakīna (tranquility) upon His Messenger and upon the believers." Who do you mean by "the believers" in this verse?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

I do not know.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

In the Battle of Hunayn, all the Muslims fled, and none remained with the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—except seven men from Banū Hāshim. Among them, ʿAli was fighting with his sword to protect the Prophet. ʿAbbās was holding the reins of the Prophet's mule, while five other men surrounded the Prophet so that no harm from the polytheists would reach him. This continued until Allah granted victory to His Messenger—peace and blessings be upon him. Thus, "the believers" in this verse refers specifically to ʿAli and those from Banū Hāshim who remained with the Prophet in that perilous hour. Now tell me: were those who were with the Messenger of Allah in that difficult time better, or those who fled with their lives—including Abū Bakr and ʿUmar—whom Allah did not see fit to comfort?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

No, the best are those upon whom Allah sent down His tranquility.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Tell me: is the person who was in the cave with the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—better, or he who slept upon the bed of the Messenger of Allah, saved the Prophet's life by placing himself in mortal danger, and enabled the Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—to accomplish his migration? When the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—commanded ʿAli to sleep upon his bed, he said to him, and ʿAli wept. The Prophet asked, "O ʿAli, do you weep from fear of death?" ʿAli replied, "By Allah, I do not weep from fear of death, but because I am thinking of you—lest some calamity befall you. If I sleep in your bed, will it save your life?" The Messenger of Allah said, "Yes, it will!" Then ʿAli's grief turned to joy, and he said, "O Messenger of Allah, what have you to worry about? I shall sleep with great satisfaction and shall gladly give my life to save yours!" That night, he wrapped the Prophet's cloak about himself and lay down peacefully upon his bed. Shortly thereafter, the polytheists of Quraysh came and surrounded him. Thinking he was the Messenger of Allah, they agreed that one man from each Quraysh tribe would strike a single blow with the sword, so that no single tribe could be held responsible for his blood and the Banū Hāshim would find no single line to pursue for vengeance. Yet ʿAli listened to all their counsel and watched them ready themselves to take his life, and he did not fear. He lay peacefully, prepared to die. In contrast, Abū Bakr was frightened in the cave and wept.[^cite-5]

When ʿAli woke in the morning, the polytheists of Quraysh looked at him and asked, "Where is Muḥammad?" ʿAli replied, "What do I know?" Then they said, "O ʿAli! It seems you have been the enemy of your own soul since last night!" Thus, throughout the life of the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—ʿAli was proven superior to people in every deed and circumstance, never inferior to anyone in any virtue. 

Now, Qāḍī Isḥāq, relate to me the ḥadīth of Ghadir!

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Isḥāq narrated the ḥadīth of Ghadir Khumm...

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Qāḍī Isḥāq, you see that this ḥadīth imposed upon Abū Bakr and ʿUmar an obligation that it did not impose upon ʿAli—namely, that ʿAli was bound to obey both of them, but they were not bound to obey ʿAli.

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

People say the reason for this ḥadīth was Zayd ibn al-Ḥāritha, because there were disputes between him and ʿAli. He denied the guardianship (wilāya) of ʿAli. The Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—said [this ḥadīth to affirm ʿAli's position]...

**Al-Maʾmun:**

At what point did he pronounce this ḥadīth? Did he not speak it at Ghadir Khumm on his return from the Farewell Pilgrimage (Ḥajjat al-Widāʿ)?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Yes, he spoke it on that occasion.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

When Zayd ibn al-Ḥāritha was martyred before the Battle of Ghadir, how can you attribute the ḥadīth of Ghadir to him? Well, tell me: if one of your sons, who is but five years old, says, "Whoever is my master is also the master of my son," would you not find this strange and improper—that your son would tell people such an absurd thing that people themselves already know and do not deny?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

It would indeed seem bad.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Do you then prefer for the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—what you would not prefer for your own son? Woe unto you! Have you not heard the ḥadīth of the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him: "O ʿAli, you are to me as Aaron was to Moses"?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Yes, I have heard this ḥadīth.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Is it possible that the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—spoke this ḥadīth in jest?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Allah forbid! Not at all!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Then either he spoke what makes no sense, or what no one can understand?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Allah forbid! Neither is possible!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Do you not know that Aaron was the true brother of Moses?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Yes, I know.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

And that Aaron was a prophet, while ʿAli was not a prophet?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Yes, that is correct.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

So ʿAli was neither the true brother nor the prophet of the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—whereas Aaron possessed both these qualities. What, then, was the meaning of the Prophet's—peace and blessings be upon him—statement?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

The Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—wanted to please ʿAli because the hypocrites had said that the Prophet left ʿAli in Medina during his journey to the Battle of Tabūk out of some resentment in his heart.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

So you are saying that the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—wished to please ʿAli through falsehood?

Qāḍī Isḥāq grew exceedingly distressed at this question and could not respond.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Isḥāq, the meaning of this analogy is made clear in the Holy Qur'an itself.

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Master, what is that meaning?

**Al-Maʾmun:**

In the words of Prophet Moses, Allah declared:

⟪quran 20:29⟫

"And Moses said to his brother Aaron, 'Be my minister among my people, reform them, and do not follow the way of the corrupting people.'"

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

Indeed, Moses appointed Aaron as the caliph over his people while he was alive and went to Mount Ṭūr by the command of Allah. Thus, the Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—also fought the Battle of Tabūk during his lifetime.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

Tell me: when Moses appointed Aaron as caliph, did any of his companions or any of the Israelites accompany him?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

No, no one went with him. He ascended the mountain alone.

**Al-Maʾmun:**

And did Aaron become the caliph over a mere handful, or over the entire Ummah?

**Qāḍī Isḥāq:**

He became caliph over the whole Ummah!

**Al-Maʾmun:**

But when the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—departed for the Battle of Tabūk, he did not leave in Medina except the elderly, the sick, women, and children. He took all the able-bodied men with him. How, then, is this similar to Moses?

The truth is that Allah said to Moses:

⟪quran 20:29⟫

"O my Lord, expand my breast, ease my task, and remove the impediment from my speech, that they may understand my word. And grant me from Thy mercy, for Thou art the Most Merciful."

No, rather:

⟪quran 20:29⟫

"And give to me a coadjutor from my family—Aaron, my brother. Increase my strength by him, and associate him with me in my task."

Similarly, the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—said, "O ʿAli, you are to me as Aaron was to Moses." You are my minister and my brother, through whom Allah has strengthened my back, and whom He has made my partner in my work, so that we may both glorify Him abundantly. Now, is it possible to give any meaning to this ḥadīth other than these meanings that would not either render the Prophet's statement false, absurd, or devoid of sense?

---

The author of *al-ʿAqd al-Farīd* records that the debate continued in this manner, growing ever more lengthy, until the hour approached midday. At that point, Qāḍī Isḥāq declared: "By Allah, the Prophet has made the truth manifest to all present and has proven his claim with arguments that no one can refute!" 

Hearing this, al-Maʾmun prayed: "O Lord, I have counseled them to goodness and fulfilled my duty in commanding toward the right. O Lord, I seek to draw nearer to You through love and the guardianship (wilāya) of ʿAli, and I am a follower of this religion."

*(Kitāb al-ʿAqd al-Farīd, ash-Shahābuddīn Aḥmad)*

## The Debate between Maulana Muhammad al-Baqir and al-Hururi

This celebrated disputation dates to the epoch of Maulana Imam Muhammad al-Baqir (peace be upon him). A renowned interlocutor named al-Hururi (الحروری) approached the Imam with a specific intention: to dispute with him concerning the rightful claim of Hazrat Abu Bakr to the office of Caliph. The Imam responded with characteristic openness: "Open your heart and argue, I am ready to answer your every question." Al-Hururi proceeded to enumerate four exceptional qualities allegedly bestowed upon Abu Bakr alone—virtues, he contended, that rendered him uniquely qualified for the caliphate.

| Merit | Claim |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | He believed first |
| 2 | He was the Messenger's companion in the cave |
| 3 | He led the prayer during the Prophet's lifetime |
| 4 | He is buried alongside the Messenger of Allah |

Upon hearing this catalogue, the Imam posed a discerning question: "O Hururi! Are you listing their virtues or their defects?" Al-Hururi was taken aback. "O Imam, do you claim that all four constitute faults?" The Imam affirmed: "Indeed! I shall demonstrate that these are in fact the gravest deficiencies you have enumerated."[^cite-6]

### Abu Bakr's Precedence in Faith

The Imam began his refutation by addressing the claim of precedence. "Did not Amir al-Muʾminīn (أمير المؤمنين, the Commander of the Faithful) embrace Islam before Abu Bakr—indeed, before Umar as well?" Al-Hururi conceded the point: "True, O Imam, yet ʿAlī was merely a youth, a child at that time." The Imam's response was immediate and devastating: "Yet was not the Messenger of Allah also not a child? If the Prophet's childhood acceptance of the faith of ʿAlī was valid, how can you dismiss it on grounds of age alone?" Al-Hururi fell silent.[^cite-7]

### The Companionship in the Cave

The Imam then addressed the second claim with exacting precision. "You assert that Abu Bakr accompanied the Messenger of Allah into the cave at the Hijra (الهجرة, the Migration). Do you not know that when the Messenger of Allah was commanded to depart, Abu Bakr sold him a fleet camel at ten times its ordinary price—a transaction born not of devotion but of commercial necessity? He accompanied the Prophet not by invitation but by compulsion, forced to flee alongside him into concealment."

The Imam then invoked sacred testimony. When Abu Bakr wept in the cave from fear of the idolaters, Allah revealed a verse—one that illuminates the very hierarchy of witness and protection:

### The Leadership of Prayer

Al-Hururi raised a third point: on one occasion when the Messenger fell ill, Aʿisha requested her father to lead the Maghrib prayer. Yet scarcely had Abu Bakr commenced the prayer when the Prophet, with great effort, rose and removed Abu Bakr from his place, completing the prayer himself.

The Imam's response was acute. "Tell me, O Hururi—when the Prophet removed Abu Bakr from the position of imam (إمام, leader), did this removal invalidate the prayer?" Al-Hururi replied that it did not; rather, Abu Bakr had been leading on behalf of the Prophet, and the Prophet's intervention confirmed the validity of the rite. The Imam then posed a question that silenced the assembly: "Have you ever witnessed a prayer in which there existed two imams simultaneously? Your precedent presents an absurdity—a leader who is neither truly an imam nor truly a follower!"[^cite-10]
