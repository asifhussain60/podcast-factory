## The Three Degrees of Certainty

Certainty (*yaqīn*, تحقق) unfolds across three distinct levels, each representing a deepening apprehension of divine truth. The first, knowledge of certainty (*ʿilm al-yaqīn*), is mere intellectual assent—the acceptance of a proposition as true by reason or testimony alone. The second, the eye of certainty (*ʿayn al-yaqīn*), supervenes when one observes the reality directly with one's own faculties and thereby knows it through immediate experience. The third and highest, the truth of certainty (*ḥaqq al-yaqīn*), is attained only when the believer becomes wholly absorbed in faith, united with all its levels through sincerity, integrity, and unwavering perseverance.

The Qur'ān itself illuminates these degrees through the story of Ibrāhīm (Abraham), peace be upon him. In the chapter of the Cow (*Sūrat al-Baqara*), the Prophet poses a question to his Lord:

> ⟪quran 2:260⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ إِبْرَهِیمُ رَبِّ أَرِنِى كَيْفَ تُحْىِ ٱلْمَوْتَىٰ ۖ قَالَ أَوَلَمْ تُؤْمِن ۖ قَالَ بَلَىٰ وَلَكِن لِّيَطْمَئِنَّ قَلْبِى ۖ قَالَ فَخُذْ أَرْبَعَةًۭ مِّنَ ٱلطَّيْرِ فَصُرْهُنَّ إِلَيْكَ ثُمَّ ٱجْعَلْ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ جَبَلٍۢ مِّنْهُنَّ جُزْءًۭا ثُمَّ ٱدْعُهُنَّ يَأْتِينَكَ سَعْيًۭا ۚ وَٱعْلَمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ عَزِيزٌ حَكِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when Ibrahim (Abraham) said, "My Lord! Show me how You give life to the dead." He (Allah) said: "Do you not believe?" He [Ibrahim (Abraham)] said: "Yes (I believe), but to be stronger in Faith." He said: "Take four birds, then cause them to incline towards you (then slaughter them, cut them into pieces), and then put a portion of them on every hill, and call them, they will come to you in haste. And know that Allah is All-Mighty, All-Wise."*

Ibrāhīm asked his Lord: "Show me how You give life to the dead." The Lord replied: "Do you not believe?" Thus posed, Ibrāhīm said: "Yes, my Lord, I believe; yet I desire the tranquility of my heart." The Lord then instructed him: "Take four birds, slaughter them, mingle their parts together so that no trace of each bird remains distinct, scatter the pieces upon different mountains, then call upon them—and they shall come to you in haste." Ibrāhīm obeyed the divine command, and when he called, the birds reassembled and returned alive. Through this trial, he traversed the first two degrees: he possessed *ʿilm al-yaqīn*, knowledge of certainty through revelation, and then attained *ʿayn al-yaqīn*, the eye of certainty through witnessing the miracle with his own eyes.[^cite-1] Thereafter, having passed through these two stations, he ascended to *ḥaqq al-yaqīn*, the truth of certainty, and his rank rose above that of many prophets because of this supreme degree.

The principle is evident: the higher one ascends in the degrees of certainty, the nearer one draws to divine knowledge itself. The Gospel revealed to ʿĪsā (Jesus), peace be upon him, recounts that he walked upon water. The Messenger of Allāh, may Allāh's blessings and peace be upon him, said: "Had Jesus possessed greater perfection in his certainty, he would have walked upon the air itself."[^cite-2] Consider, then, the rank of the Master of the Universe, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him. Human intellect and understanding lack the capacity to encompass his teachings within their thoughts.

If one were to ask ʿAlī, "Master, how far does your certainty extend?" he would reply: "I have advanced in the knowledge of God to such a degree that ⟪ar-quote:لَوکُشِفَ الغِطاَءُ ماَ اَزدَتُّ یَقِیناَ⟫—even if all the veils that conceal the universe were torn away from before me, my certainty would not increase by even the smallest measure."[^cite-3]

## The Funeral Prayer for One Still Living

Upon hearing this statement of ʿAlī regarding certainty, certain adversaries conspired against him. They placed a living man in the mosque's courtyard, concealing his life beneath a shroud, seeking to embarrass ʿAlī and demonstrate the falsity of his claim. They then summoned him to lead the funeral prayer. ʿAlī came forth and performed the funeral rites over the living man.

When the Master of the Universe had finished the salutation that concludes the prayer, they approached him and said: "O ʿAlī! You claim that even if all veils were lifted, your certainty would not increase. Yet this man is alive, not dead! How, then, did you lead his funeral prayer? How could your certainty not perceive with your own eyes that he yet lives?"

ʿAlī replied: "If this person is alive, then show me." When they removed the shroud, behold—the man was dead.

—*As recorded by Mullā Anṣār Aḥmad ibn Sayyid Amīr al-Dīn*

## The Swiftness of ʿAlī's Judgment

On one occasion, the Commander of the Faithful resolved a profoundly intricate matter with such grace and discernment that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb, despite his own acumen, found himself unable to untangle it. Witnessing this knowledge and power, ʿUmar grew troubled and said to ʿAlī: "O Abū al-Ḥasan! Do not be so hasty in your judgments! You may yet commit an error."

The Commander of the Faithful asked him: "O ʿUmar! How many fingers do you have upon one hand?" ʿUmar replied without hesitation: "Five."

"How did you answer so swiftly?" asked ʿAlī.

ʿUmar said: "Is there need for deliberation on such a matter? These five fingers are always before my eyes; why should I delay in my response?"

ʿAlī then replied: "So it is with me, O ʿUmar. The Qur'ān, its rulings, and all the knowledge of the outward and inward workings of this universe stand perpetually before my eyes. How, then, should I tarry in my judgment? It is for this reason that I say: even if the veil were lifted, my certainty would not increase."[^cite-4]

ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb himself bore witness to this truth. He declared that Allāh has divided all the knowledge of the universe into six portions. Of these six, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib alone has received one complete portion in which he has no partner. The remaining fifth portion is shared among all the people of the world—and ʿAlī himself participates in this fifth share as well. Yet within that fifth portion, there dwell more scholars than all the people of the world combined. Exalted is Allāh! Thus does the testimony stand.

—*From the assembly (*majlis*) of Ḥaḍrat al-Manṣūr ʿAbdullāh ibn Hibatullāh*

## The Pigeons of the Mosque Dome

One day, ʿAmmar Yāsir sat in the mosque of Kūfa reading, while the Master of the Universe stood nearby. ʿAlī gazed upward at the dome and said three times: "Ṣādiqīya! Ṣādiqīya!"—"O truthful one, true to your covenant!" ʿAmmar, perplexed, looked about but saw no one to whom the Master of the Universe might be speaking. He returned to his reading. When ʿAlī repeated the invocation a third time, ʿAmmar finally inquired: "O Master, to whom are you speaking?"

The Master of the Universe replied: "A male pigeon and his mate are upon the dome. The male has lost his way homeward, and the hour grows late. His mate accuses him of infidelity, saying he has taken another beloved. The male protests that he has merely lost the path and delayed his return. The female refuses to believe. In her distress, she swore by the Dome—that is, by me. Therefore, I called to him thrice; the first two times, he comprehended not my voice, but at the third call, he understood and believed, and they flew away together."

ʿAmmar then asked: "O Master, do you understand the speech of birds?"

ʿAlī replied: "The Prophet Sulaymān, peace be upon him, was my disciple, and I taught him all the languages—those of jinn, beasts, birds, and dragons. If a student possesses such power that he can comprehend and speak all these tongues, how difficult a thing would it be for his teacher?"[^cite-5]

## Gabriel, the Student

One day, Ḥaḍrat Jibrīl (Gabriel) came to the Prophet, and ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib was present in the assembly. When Gabriel perceived ʿAlī, he smiled in greeting and said: "Peace be upon you." The Messenger of Allāh asked: "O Gabriel, do you know this one?"

Gabriel replied: "He is my teacher."

The Prophet, astonished, asked for explanation. Gabriel said: "O Messenger of Allāh! When Allāh created me, He addressed me thus: 'Who are you, and what is your name? Who am I, and what is My name?' I comprehended not the answer to this question at that moment. Then this righteous one—who had already been created in the world of light before me—came to me in that realm and said: 'Answer the Lord as follows: You are the Lord, Mighty and Glorious; Your name is Mighty. I am Your servant, despised and lowly; my name is Gabriel.' It is thus a common principle that a name is always bestowed by another; it was ʿAlī who named me."[^cite-6]

## Where Is Gabriel Now?

When the Muslims entrusted the caliphate to ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, he ascended the pulpit in Kūfa and proclaimed: "Praise be to Allāh! I am grateful that truth has come to its rightful place. Ask me whatsoever you wish before you lose me! Inquire of me concerning the heavens, for I know the pathways of heaven better than I know the earth!"

At this, a man stood and asked the Master of the Universe: "O Commander of the Faithful, where is Gabriel now?"

ʿAlī said: "Wait—let me perceive." He looked upward to the heavens, and his gaze ascended through the veils until it reached the Throne of the Most High. He then gazed downward to the earth, and his sight penetrated through all the layers of the ground. Next, he turned his vision toward the east, and then toward the west, searching through all corners of creation. When he could discern Gabriel nowhere, he understood. He addressed the questioner and said: "O Gabriel! You are Gabriel!" At his command, Gabriel rose from among the people, broke through the roof of the mosque with his wings, and ascended into the sky.

The people began to cry out in takbīr. They then asked the Master of the Universe: "O Lord, how did you perceive that this was Gabriel?"

He answered: "When I looked upward at the heavens, my gaze pierced through the veils and reached the Upper Throne. When I gazed upon the earth, my sight traversed through all its layers. When I turned toward the east and the west and could not find Gabriel anywhere in creation, I understood that it was he—for Gabriel is not confined to any place."[^cite-7]

## The Wall of Gold

One day, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib came to the House of Revelation and asked his wife, Fāṭima al-Zahrāʾ: "O daughter of the Prophet, is there anything to eat in this house?"

She replied: "O Abū al-Ḥasan, what could I bring you? The condition of our household is such that I have lulled Ḥasan and Ḥusayn to sleep these two nights past on empty bellies."

ʿAlī asked to borrow her patched cloak. Taking it, he went to a Jewish merchant and said: "O Jew, pledge this garment and give me something for it."

When ʿAlī returned with the provisions, the Jew called out to him: "O Abū al-Ḥasan, wait a moment! I have a question for you. Your brother Muḥammad claims to be the Messenger of Allāh and leader of all the prophets. Yet why does he not pray to Allāh to deliver you from this straits and enrich you?"

Hearing this, ʿAlī bowed his head and was silent for a moment. Then, with one blessed finger, he struck the earthen wall beside him and said:

> O Jew! The servant of Allāh says: If I swear by Allāh that this wall shall become gold, then it shall become gold!

At that instant, the wall transformed into gleaming gold. Then ʿAlī said: "Return to earth!" and the gold wall reverted to clay.

The wonder of ʿAlī lies in this: it was neither prayer nor command, but merely a gesture by which the wall changed its very nature. Yet because of this virtue and these exalted powers and perfections that he possesses, ʿAlī declares: "Even if the veil were lifted, my certainty would not increase."[^cite-8]

## Muʿāwiya in the Court of ʿAlī

Sayyid Ḥātim has recorded this incident in his work. On one occasion, ʿUzbak was present in the court of the Master of the Universe when two men approached in dispute. ʿAlī issued his judgment, pronouncing one man in the right and the other in the wrong. The man against whom the verdict was rendered said: "O ʿAlī, you have wronged me!"

ʿAlī replied: "I have judged according to the commandments of Allāh and the Qur'ān."

The man insisted: "No, ʿAlī, you have wronged me!" ʿAlī grew angry and warned: "If you say once more that I have wronged you, Allāh shall transform you into a dog."

The man repeated his accusation. Before his words had finished, he was transformed into a black dog. ʿUzbak trembled at the sight. The man's companion, in humility, beseeched the Commander of the Faithful: "O Lord! Restore this man to his former state, for surely You are the Most Merciful."

When ʿAlī saw his sincere supplication, he said: "Very well, I shall restore him." But first, let him understand that he must abide by my judgment and speak no words against me. Then ʿAlī prayed: "O Allāh, I beseech You by the truth of Muḥammad, Your Messenger, by the truth of Mūsā and ʿĪsā and the Spirit of Allāh, and by the truth of all the prophets—restore this man to his original state." ʿUzbak relates that as soon as the prayer ended, the man returned to human form. The man declared: "I believe in Allāh, and I acknowledge you as the true guardian (*walī*) of Allāh! I offer my repentance to you!" They departed in peace.

ʿUzbak then said to ʿAlī: "O Lord, your miracle is great, yet I am astonished at your silence concerning Muʿāwiya."

ʿAlī replied: "O ʿUzbak! That which has been decreed in the past shall be fulfilled, no matter how much the Truth dislikes it. But do you wish to witness a still greater miracle?" He said: "Look at what Allāh does!" He raised his hands, and his lips began to move. Then he said: "O Muʿāwiya, come before me!" ʿUzbak recounts: "In that instant, I witnessed Muʿāwiya seized by a wind from Damascus and brought before ʿAlī in Kūfa. Muʿāwiya stood with bent knees, downcast eyes, and trembling tongue."

The Commander of the Faithful said: "O rebellious one! You dare show boldness against Allāh and His saints! Are you not fearful of the Divine? Yes, Allāh executes His command! Mark this!" He warned him sternly and then said: "Return to your place. There is yet an appointed term for you to fulfill." ʿUzbak says that Muʿāwiya vanished immediately.

When Muʿāwiya was seized by the wind, he had been upon his throne in Damascus, plotting an assault upon ʿAlī. All those in his court were bewildered—where had Muʿāwiya gone? When he returned, they asked: "O Muʿāwiya, where had you gone?" He said: "I reached Kūfa before ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib," and he recounted the entire incident. They counseled him: "We must conceal this matter and reveal it to no one, lest we suffer great humiliation."

The Commander of the Faithful then addressed ʿUzbak and said: "O ʿUzbak! Have you read the story of Sulaymān in the Qur'ān? The Prophet asked his court: 'Who among you shall bring to me the throne of Bilqīs before you come to me?' Then Āsaf, the one possessing knowledge of the Book (*al-ʿilm min al-kitāb*), said: 'I shall bring it to you before your gaze can return to you'—and he brought the throne in the twinkling of an eye. O ʿUzbak! Āsaf received but a small portion of the knowledge of the Book, and it enabled him to accomplish this deed. But by Allāh, I have been given the entirety of the knowledge of the Book! I am the command of Allāh (*amr Allāh*), the word of Allāh (*kalām Allāh*), the cause of Allāh (*sabab Allāh*), the light of Allāh (*nūr Allāh*)! What difficulty is there for me in such a thing?"

Then ʿUzbak fell in prostration and declared: "I believe in you, O guardian of Allāh, and you are able to do all things. You are the most truthful."

The remembrance of the Prophet makes the atrocities fade.
The skies are illuminated with the light of takbīr.
Yet when rotation holds sway over human beings,
Taking ʿAlī's name alone takes away the calls!

## The Testimony of the Dead

In the book *Muʿjizāt al-Kullīya*, Maytham al-Sammār recounts: We were sitting with the Commander of the Faithful in the mosque of Kūfa when a tall man approached and asked: "Who among you is the just judge? Who is the arbiter? Who is ʿAlī?"

ʿAlī raised his head and said: "Yes, I am the one who possesses the knowledge of prophethood (*ʿilm al-nubuwwa*). I am ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib! What do you wish to say?"

The man replied: "I have come on behalf of seven thousand people. These people are perplexed concerning a victim—they do not know who has slain him. You are the proof (*ḥujja*) of Allāh. Tell us the truth of this matter."

ʿAlī said to Maytham: "Go forth and proclaim that the brother of Muḥammad, upon whom be peace, has been given complete knowledge. Let whoever wishes to witness it come to Najaf." In a short time, a multitude had gathered.

The body was brought forth, and ʿAlī approached the victim. He instructed Maytham to uncover the face from the shroud. It was a young man named Madrik ibn Anzal ibn Salama, dead these forty days past. Fifty people had been arrested on suspicion of his murder. ʿAlī declared: "His killer is his own uncle, who is also his father-in-law. The young man married without his consent, and the uncle slew him in anger."

Then ʿAlī said: "I shall not rest content with my testimony alone. Let the victim himself come alive and testify!" At that moment, the Master of the Universe smiled. He praised Allāh and said: "O people! The prophets of Israel were not of greater station than my brother. It was Jesus who raised the dead to life in the course of seven days. Allāh has blessed me with all of these powers. I am the brother of the Messenger!"

Approaching the corpse, ʿAlī said: "Rise up, by the leave of Allāh, O Madrik ibn Anzal ibn Salama!" The young man rose and sat up, crying out: "Here am I! Here am I, O proof of Allāh upon the earth!"[^cite-9]

The people asked him: "Who killed you?"

He replied: "My uncle killed me."

When the Commander of the Faithful had finished, the young man clung to his feet, saying: "Do not hand me over to them, for they will slay me again!" ʿAlī said: "May Allāh curse whoever has placed enmity between you and you!" He took the young man with him, and he remained until he was martyred in the Battle of Ṣiffīn.

## The Return of the Sun

When the Commander of the Faithful rebuked the first Caliph, saying: "You have assumed a position for which you lack the capacity! How will you bear this burden on the Day of Judgment?"—Abu Bakr said: "The Prophet did not appoint you as successor."

ʿAlī said: "If the Prophet were to come again and declare before you that he has appointed me as successor and Caliph, would you then surrender the caliphate to me?"

Abu Bakr said: "How is it possible that the Messenger of Allāh would come again?"

ʿAlī then cleared all people from the mosque, permitting only certain ones to remain, and closed the doors. He entered the courtyard and prayed: "O my Lord! Have mercy upon me. Send to me my brother Muḥammad in the cluster of angels, for You have power over all things."

When the prayer of the Commander of the Faithful was complete, the Holy Prophet descended in a cluster of angels. The sounds of takbīr echoed throughout. ʿAlī warned the people not to advance. He and the Prophet held conversation and discourse with one another.

Then the Prophet said to Abu Bakr: "O cursed one! Woe unto you! Where have you taken your seat! Behold, this is ʿAlī's place, the place most exalted!" The Prophet then ascended again into that cluster of angels, and ʿAlī stood in tears, saying these words: "How I long that I could journey with you to that high destination!"[^cite-10]

## The Battle of the Covenant

An elderly companion named Zamāri participated alongside the Prophet and ʿAlī in the Battle of the Covenant. During the fierce fighting, he witnessed the Prophet and ʿAlī engaged in combat. Yet when he withdrew to the tent, he found the Prophet and ʿAlī sitting in peaceful conversation with each other. His expectations were confounded.

When he returned to the battlefield, once again he saw both of them fighting. This mystery revealed to him that they possessed the capacity to be in multiple states simultaneously—a sign of their supernatural knowledge and power.

## The Drilling of Noah's Ark

There was a time when a man with one arm severed came into the presence of the Holy Prophet, peace be upon him, and spoke with him. In the midst of their discourse, the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, entered the gathering. As soon as the man saw ʿAlī, he transformed into a bird and flew to a nearby branch.

The Messenger of Allāh asked him: "What happened that you fled and went there?"

He replied: "O Messenger of Allāh! I am seized with great fear of this man."

The Prophet asked the reason. The man said: "O Messenger of Allāh! I was one of the many jinn in the time of Nūḥ. When the Prophet Nūḥ was boarding the Ark with his companions, I began to drill a hole in it to sink the vessel. Then this man came upon me and struck my arm with his sword such force that my limb was severed from my body. Since that day, I have lived in great terror of him."

At this, the Prophet introduced the jinn to ʿAlī, and the creature returned to human form and remained in the gathering. This incident reveals that there was no epoch in this world when the Essence of the Universe was not present, and that he aided all the prophets in their tribulations.[^cite-11]

## The Well of Knowledge

Once, the Master of the Universe was returning from the Battle of Banī Muṣṭalaq in the company of the Holy Prophet, peace be upon him, along with three other companions. The Prophet sent them to fill the water-skins (*mushkīz*) at a well. The people of the village told them: "There is a well inhabited by jinn who greatly disturb us."

The Prophet then asked: "Who among you will go and fight against them? Whoever fights them, I guarantee him paradise." Two Muslims stepped forward. The Messenger said to them: "You shall not turn back from the passion with which you depart." They replied: "O Messenger of Allāh! It suffices us that you guarantee us paradise!" Both entered the well and were martyred.

Then the three companions and forty others came to the well, and they beheld the trees around it engulfed in terrible flames that seemed to speak to the heavens. Countless heads appeared upon each tree, emitting horrifying sounds that caused all the people to flee in terror and return to the Messenger of Allāh.

The Messenger then sent the Master of the Universe. He proceeded to the well with the companions. Upon arriving, he saw the same terrifying scene. Though he encouraged the companions to advance with him, they turned back in fear. The Master of the Universe drew Dhū al-Fiqār, his sword, and leaped into the well.

The people heard endless screams and cries. When ʿAlī emerged, his garments were stained with blood, and his sword dripped crimson. The Messenger asked: "O ʿAlī, how did you combat them?"

He replied: "I fought them in three ways:

| 1 | I destroyed them by burning (*tafjīr*). |
| 2 | I recited verses of the Holy Qur'ān and destroyed them. |
| 3 | I clothed them in human form and slew them with this sword. |

This was accomplished through divine designation. No other Imām save him could have performed this deed. ʿAlī engaged in four great battles as a constituent: this was one of them. When asked whether ʿAlī had slain all the jinn, he replied: "No. Those who remain shall be reckoned with by the sword of al-Qāʾim. Yet there is no other Imām between ʿAlī and al-Qāʾim who possesses the power to slay these jinn. These creatures are in reality a group of malevolent and corrupt jinn."[^cite-12]

## The Horseman of the Hijaz

One day, the Commander of the Faithful was eating dates upon the roof of his house. He was then twenty-seven years of age. Salmān al-Fārisī sat in the courtyard sewing his garment. ʿAlī tossed a bundle of dates toward Salmān, who said: "O ʿAlī! I am old and you are young; why do you jest with an old man?"

ʿAlī replied: "O Salmān! You speak as though you are elder to me and I but a child. Have you forgotten the story of ʿArzān in the desert, when a man delivered you from the lion?"

Salmān was astonished and said: "O ʿAlī! I have never told this story to any soul until now. How have you come to know of it?"

ʿAlī asked: "Tell me the story first."

Salmān said: "When I embraced Islām, my father became my enemy and sought my life. One day, I fled my home and came to a land called ʿArzān. There I rested for a time. Upon awakening, I went to a nearby spring to bathe and entered the water. Suddenly, a tiger emerged from nowhere, approached me, and stood upon my garments. When I saw the beast, I cried out in fear: 'O Fāris al-Ḥijāz, come to my aid!' (*Yā Fāris al-Ḥijāz Adrikanī!*)

After a moment, I beheld a mounted warrior who, with a single stroke of his sword, clove the tiger in two. I emerged from the water and threw myself upon the horseman's stirrup, kissing it. Since spring was upon the land and flowers bloomed in the desert, I plucked a rare bloom and gave it as a gift to the young rider. Then he departed, and I never saw him again."

Then Salmān asked ʿAlī: "O ʿAlī! Now tell me—how did you come to know of this?"

ʿAlī drew forth a flower from his pocket and gave it to Salmān. When Salmān saw it, he recognized the very rare bloom he had given to the horseman that day!

This incident reveals the following truth: Salmān harbored a subtle pride in his age, thinking to himself, "Even if ʿAlī is superior to me in rank, am I not at least his elder, and thus superior to him in this inferior station?" ʿAlī read Salmān's heart and cast the dates at him, thereby unveiling the secret of his breast. Then, by producing the flower, he demonstrated that they transcend the limitations of age and time, and that they are exalted above him in every degree of rank and excellence. Thus did ʿAlī remove the slight arrogance that had arisen in Salmān's heart and purify him completely.[^cite-13]

## Imām al-Mustaʿlī and the Signs of Imāmate

Nizār, the brother of Maulānā al-Mustaʿlī, son of Maulānā al-Mustanṣir Billāh (whose claim led to the schism of the Nizārī community), falsely laid claim to the Imāmate. Imām al-Mustaʿlī warned Nizār and his brothers that there are certain limits, proofs, and signs by which the Imām of the time may be known.

He placed a sword before them and said: "Behold! This is the sword of our ancestor, the Commander of the Faithful, Dhū al-Fiqār, which only the true Imām of the time can lift." They all attempted to raise it, yet none could move it. Then Imām al-Mustaʿlī stepped forward and drew the sword with ease.

At this, Nizār said: "You have demonstrated physical strength! I believe instead in spiritual power. Do you see that tiger in the palace? Summon it and command it to attack me. If you are a true Imām, it shall obey you!"

The tiger was brought forth and released. The Imām said: "Beware! Remember that it shall be your turn when you obey my command!" Nizār ordered: "O lion, come forth and tear Imām al-Mustaʿlī to pieces!" The tiger advanced toward the Imām but began to rub its head at his feet in submission. At that moment, the Imām said: "O Nizār! Now I give the command: seize him!" The tiger pounced upon Nizār, but he hid behind a door, and the guards controlled the beast.[^cite-14]

## Imām ʿĀmir Billāh and the Tiger of the Carpet

In the time of the ʿAbbāsid Caliph, a magician came seeking to humiliate Imām Maulānā ʿĀmir Billāh. He asked the Caliph to arrange a banquet and invite the Imām to attend. The Imām accepted the Caliph's invitation.

When all were seated and eating, the Imām reached for bread. The magician, through sorcery, caused the bread to move away. The Imām attempted to take another loaf, but again the magician deflected it. The people laughed at the Imām's plight.

The Imām then said to the Caliph: "O Commander of the Faithful! Do you wish to witness something yet more wondrous than this?" There were carpets hanging in the hall, one of which bore the image of a lion. The Imām addressed it, saying: "O lion, rise! And consume this sorcerer!"

Suddenly, the lion began to roar from the carpet and then emerged from it alive, swallowing the magician whole, and then returned to become a picture upon the carpet once more.

At this, the Caliph pleaded: "Surely you are the Imām, and you have wrought a great miracle! Now, I beseech you, have mercy and restore the magician from the belly of the lion!"

The Imām replied: "If the staff of Mūsā, after swallowing the serpents of the sorcerers, had then regurgitated them, then surely this lion would restore the magician! But such is not the nature of truth when it encounters falsehood."[^cite-15]

## Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq and the Scratching of the Back

A man came into the presence of Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, peace be upon him. Upon seeing him, the man thought: "He is but a human being like ourselves. I have heard that the Imām is of the highest rank, yet he appears as an ordinary man."

Every thought within him was revealed to the Imām. The Imām called the man to him and said: "I have an itch upon my back; scratch it for me."

When the man removed the Imām's garment to scratch his back, he beheld that the entire back was covered with boils and abscesses, with pus flowing forth. The man became even more anxious in his heart. He thought: "So he is not even a normal human being—there is something far stranger here!" He sat and scratched the Imām's back.

After some time, the Imām ordered him again: "Scratch my back once more."

Now, when the man removed the garment, he found the Imām's back perfectly clean and whole. Upon seeing this, he became even more troubled, his certainty wavering.

Then, for a third time, the Imām commanded: "Scratch my back again."

This time, when the man removed the garment, there was no back visible at all—his hands moved through empty air. It is through such a miracle that the Imām made manifest his absolute dominion (*wilāya*) over his own being and matter itself. Through this display, he revealed his authority (*sulṭān*) over body and form.[^cite-16]

## Disrespect Toward Imām al-Ḥākim

In the time of Imām al-Ḥākim, peace be upon him, a man entered a mosque. There he found three people seated and insulting the Imām and backbiting against him. The man sat and listened, his blood boiling with anger, yet because of his poverty, he could do nothing. He prayed to the Imām, saying: "O Imām of the Age, you are the Almighty! Help me and lighten my grief!"

His prayer had not yet ended when the three men were seized by a supernatural effect. Each one, when looking at the others, beheld the form of Imām al-Ḥākim. Because of the hatred and enmity in their hearts toward the Imām, each one attacked the other, believing he saw the Imām, and they killed one another.

After this incident, the poor man saw the Imām standing before him. The Imām said: "You remembered me with sincerity, so I came to you!"[^cite-17]
