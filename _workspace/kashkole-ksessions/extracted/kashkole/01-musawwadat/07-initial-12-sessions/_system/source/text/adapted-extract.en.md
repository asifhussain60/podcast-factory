## God's Questions to Humankind

In the Qur'an, the Almighty poses fundamental questions to human consciousness. Did you come into existence by chance, without a Creator? Does not such a premise contradict all observable reality?

There exists an ancient philosophical theory—now long discredited—that all phenomena occur mechanically and without design. Did the sun shine forth automatically? Did the moon cool itself spontaneously? Did the winds grow gentle through mere happenstance? Did the earth spread itself, mountains establish themselves, oceans and rivers commence their flow, flowers bloom, and plants grow all of their own accord? Such mechanistic determinism collapses under scrutiny.[^cite-1]

| Hatch Things Don't Happen Automatically | Hatch Iron Daggers Don't Shoot |
| --- | --- |
| Hatch is not solved, teacher | Don't let the slave get away with it.
| Maulvi never shuddered | Taghulam Shams Tabrize Na Shad |

There lived in medieval Anatolia a great scholar, philosopher, and logician of singular brilliance: ⟪ar:جلال‌الدین محمد بلخى⟫ Rumi, who died in 1273. His most famous composition—a mystical poem bearing the title "When I Die"—will be explored in our subsequent session. An inscription adorns his grave, testifying to the depth of his spiritual vision: "When I die, do not seek my tomb in the earth; seek it instead in the hearts of those who loved me."

His seminary was situated near a pool, and upon a wooden table lay his entire library—volumes meticulously arranged in orderly rows. When the wandering Sufi ⟪ar:شمس تبریز⟫ Shams Tabriz passed through, he observed this display and inquired with a piercing directness: "What is this?" To which Rumi responded with arrogance: "This is what you cannot comprehend." Shams descended to the pool and drew forth every volume, laying them to dry upon the ground. When Rumi cried out in anguish—"What have you done?"—Shams replied with equal simplicity: "This is what you did not understand."

### The First Question: Spontaneous Existence

The first query posed to human reason: Are you born of mere chance, without a Creator? This supposition has long been abandoned by rational minds. Yet the text continues to challenge those who deny intentional design.

### The Second Question: Self-Creation

⟪ar:أَم ھُمُ الخالقون⟫—Are you the fashioner of yourselves? Did you paint your own eyes? Did you determine your own facial features, your stature, your complexion? Did you choose your own nation, family, region, and country? If such were true, you would indeed possess complete liberty. Yet introspection reveals the falsity of such claims.[^cite-2]

### The Third Question: The Creation of Heavens and Earth

⟪ar:أَم خُلِق السموات والارض⟫—"Did you create the heavens and the earth?" Should you prove yourself the architect of creation, then no demand would be made upon you. Build temples or mosques as you please; fashion wine or idols according to your will. Yet this claim dissolves under the weight of evidence.

## The Purpose of Human Intellect

If these three propositions cannot be sustained—if you are neither self-existent, self-created, nor the creator of the cosmos—then before pursuing worldly knowledge, one must first ask: Who is my Creator? What is His nature? Why did He create the heavens and the earth? And most crucially: Why did He create us, and what does He require of us?

Are we fashioned merely to eat and drink, to accumulate wealth, to build dwellings, to marry, to bear children, and then to perish? If this alone were our purpose, animals accomplish the same. The animal sleeps in the forest; we sleep in dwellings. It consumes raw food; we cook and prepare ours. Both seek entertainment; ours merely takes different forms. Both nurture their young as we do ours. Yet animals perform these acts instinctively, without deliberation.

God has bestowed upon humankind a faculty that He has withheld from the animals: intellect. *Why*, then, would He grant us this supreme gift if we employ it merely to replicate animal existence? What meaning inheres in intellect if it serves only to make more comfortable what animals accomplish through instinct alone?

> ⟪quran 3:190⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ فِى خَلْقِ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَٱخْتِلَفِ ٱلَّيْلِ وَٱلنَّهَارِ لَآيَتٍۢ لِّأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *Verily! In the creation of the heavens and the earth, and in the alternation of night and day, there are indeed signs for men of understanding.*
> — **Quran 3:190**

The Almighty concludes this verse by addressing ⟪ar:'لأولی الألباب'⟫ those possessed of intellect and understanding. God declares: "I have granted you wisdom so that you may reflect upon the creation of the heavens and the earth, and upon the succession of day and night." Did all of this unfold automatically? If such regularity occurs spontaneously, why does the night not precede the day? Why does water not flow upward? Why does the earth's velocity remain constant? A mere automobile cannot run without a driver; how then could this boundless universe operate without an intelligent will to sustain it?

The Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt have alluded to these mysteries. ⟪ar:سیدنا جعفر بن سیدنا سلیمان⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Sadiq is reported to have indicated that the perfection of faith (*kamāl al-īmān*) resides in understanding the stations of men: whence they came, why they were sent, and whither they shall return. Glory be to God, who has distilled the objectives of all creation into a mere four verses!

## Why Did God Create Us? What Does He Require of Us?

A profound question haunts the human heart: Why are the desires of humanity never wholly satisfied? People spend their days immersed in digital distraction—Facebook, mobile devices, email, labor, entertainment—yet amid this constant stimulation, loneliness persists. An inexplicable anxiety dwells within the chest. If wealth were the source of contentment, why do the prosperous take their own lives? Why do multitudes flee into intoxication and narcotic oblivion? The poor chase after riches, which we might understand; but why does the wealthy pursue ever greater wealth?[^cite-3]

### The Parable of the Two Dogs

An urban dog once wandered from the city into the forest, where he encountered a wild dog. The city dog was robust and well-fed; the wild dog's ribs protruded visibly from hunger. The forest dweller asked: "What sustains you so well?" The city dog replied: "I eat meat and eggs, drink milk. Come with me to the city; I shall feed you, and you too shall grow fat and strong." The wild dog, tempted, followed. Yet as they emerged from the forest into the light, the city dog's neck bore a leash—a mark of captivity. Startled, the wild dog demanded: "What is this?" The city dog answered: "This is the strap of servitude, the sign of slavery." The wild dog, observing the bonds, refused: "Keep your meat and milk! I cannot accept such constraint. The freedom of the mountains and wilderness is worth more than your abundance."

So too has humanity mistaken the trappings of comfort for liberty. We have exalted the pleasures of the flesh—fine foods and intoxicants—as though they were freedom. We have celebrated luxury automobiles, prestigious titles, wealth, honor, fame, and applause as marks of independence. Yet invisible shackles encircle our necks.

The musician Junaid Jamshed, at the apex of his career, gave testimony to this truth. He possessed all that a young Pakistani might aspire to: acclaim, wealth, sensory gratification both lawful and unlawful. Yet he confessed: "My heart remains empty. I am like a ship adrift without harbor." Behind the façade of success lay only disillusionment—betrayal in each friendship, pleasure that lasted mere moments before the hunger for "something more" resurged. He had squandered his youth in pursuit of hollow satisfactions.

### The Heart's True Longing

The reason becomes clear when we understand the nature of the human heart. All worldly comforts address the body; the heart, being spiritual (*al-rūḥāniyyāt*, the spiritual dimension), finds no peace in material things. The heart seeks perpetually for "something more" because it cannot find what it truly desires. A lost phone creates an ache that no other possession can soothe; we search relentlessly until it is recovered. The search ceases only when the object is found. What, then, does the human heart perpetually seek? For whose discovery would it find rest?

The answer is this: the heart is not of flesh and blood. It is spiritual in nature. It searches for the Divine, for ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ God, and it shall know no peace until it encounters Him. As a fish cannot survive without water, so too the hearts that have not found God languish in spiritual drought. ⟪ar:خواجہ غلام فرید الدین⟫ Khwaja Ghulam Farid al-Din expressed this yearning in verse:

| The dervish who thirsts for union | Finds no contentment in other things |
| --- | --- |
| The heart seeks the Unseen | Not the fleeting comforts of the world |

Where can one parched with thirst find solace in wine or syrup? Thirst is quenched only by water.

### The Testament of al-Ghazālī

⟪ar:علامہ غزالی⟫ Al-Ghazālī, the celebrated logician and philosopher, rose to the highest station in his society—Chief Justice, honored and revered. Yet turbulence seized his inner being. External sciences, he discovered, have no access to the heart. After four years in this exalted position, ⟪ar:غزالی⟫ al-Ghazālī renounced his rank and wandered for a decade as an ascetic in the desert. When questioned about the fruit of his abandonment, he replied with a verse:

| I departed from Layla and Saada | In search of my true Beloved. |
| As I walked, when courage failed me, | When my feet were bloodied by tears, |
| And I was about to fall, | I beheld the signs of my Beloved, |
| The signs of my destination. | My heart whispered: "A little further, beloved!" |

Thus did al-Ghazālī abandon the vice-chancellory and discover God in exchange.

## The Essential Questions of Existence

So God poses to us the cardinal question: Why have you come into this world? Where are you going? Who sent you?

**What is knowledge?** To possess true knowledge of a person is to comprehend their station and reality. Knowledge manifests in two modes: *external* and *inner*. Outward knowledge recognizes one's mother by her form and features—the knowledge that acknowledges "she is my mother." This external knowledge is mere recognition, the foundation of confession (*iqrār*). Yet inner knowledge comprehends her sacrifice during pregnancy and birth, her vigilant nights, her tender concern—and from this inner knowing, love is born. Love grows as knowledge deepens; fear and reverence follow.

Yet God is so transcendent and exalted that neither external nor inner knowledge—in the manner we know created beings—is possible for human minds. ⟪ar:'اللہ اکبر'⟫ God is beyond all measure. The most we can affirm is that He *is*; we cannot comprehend His being. How then shall we undertake the journey toward understanding the Divine? There is but one path: through contemplation of His creation. Only through the created universe can an argument be mounted regarding the Creator. We examine the order of the microcosm—this world and its systems—and through it come to know the macrocosm, the divine order of all existence.

## Who Is God?

### The Four Oceans of Human Existence

⟪ar:ان الحکیم قال⟫ The wisdom-bearers have taught:

| There are four oceans: the world is an ocean of sins; the soul is an ocean of lusts; death is an ocean of deeds; and the grave is an ocean of regret. |
| --- |

By "ocean" is meant abundance and profusion, and the particular character intrinsic to each domain. The world, as ⟪ar:دار السقط اللہ⟫ the house of God's wrath, abounds in opportunities for transgression. The self generates desires in endless waves; without the governance of intellect, the soul leads toward destruction. After death, only one's deeds remain of the person; thus, one should strive ceaselessly for righteous action. When the person is enshrouded in the grave and subjected to its questioning, regret surges immeasurably—a wish that evil deeds had been foregone, and that good deeds had multiplied.

### Knowledge of God: Confession and Love

It is impossible to know God in the manner we know creatures. **What is knowledge?** Knowledge is the acquisition of complete understanding of a person's station and reality. Two modes of knowledge exist: outward and inner.

To recognize one's mother through her form and features—to know "she is my mother"—is external knowledge. Such knowledge prevents us from calling another woman "mother." Yet this is merely confession, bare acknowledgment.

Inner knowledge, by contrast, comprehends what she endured in bearing and delivering us, her sleepless nights of care, her maternal rights upon us. When inner knowledge flourishes, love is born. As knowledge increases, so increase love and reverence.

Yet God is so exalted and immense that neither external nor inner knowledge is attainable. Imam Jaʿfar al-Sadiq declared: The existence of God can only be acknowledged—that He *is*. It cannot be *understood*. So how does one begin the journey toward understanding God? There is only one way: through His creation. Only through creation can argument be raised regarding the Creator. For this reason, we first contemplate the system of creation in this microcosm (this world), and through it come to understand the system of creation manifest in the macrocosm (the greater cosmos).

## The Divine Pattern: Creation, Sign, Manual, Representative, and Gnosis

When a company manufactures a product, its first consideration is: What market need does it serve? Who shall use it? The purpose of any object is its use; a product with no buyers is a failed venture.

When Apple designed the iPhone, its goal was to simplify communication for humankind. All commerce, directly or indirectly, centers upon human welfare. Humans have always been the axis of creation and enterprise; no business has been established for the convenience of chairs or tables. Automobiles were designed for human benefit; car accessories exist to facilitate human comfort.

Thus we observe: *the purpose of every created thing in this world is the benefit of humankind*.

When Apple produces its device, it affixes its logo—its seal—so that observers recognize Apple as its creator. The seal or emblem proves the creator's handiwork.

A user manual accompanies each product. Why? So that even the less educated can benefit from it properly. Apple's iPhone manual instructs: how to make calls, how to send messages. It also warns: do not drop from heights; do not wash in machines. The manual's purpose is to ensure the thing's survival and correct use through negation and affirmation, so humans benefit from it.

Yet a manual alone is insufficient. Reading words conveys nothing unless one understands the language and style. Therefore, Apple appoints representatives—*mumaththilūn*—who demonstrate all the phone's functions before the public. They enact what the manual prescribes so buyers need not struggle in confusion. These representatives show: how to adjust settings at home, in the office, while traveling, while sleeping and wishing not to be disturbed. Thus humans learn through lived example.

Then, those who have mastered outward use and wish to understand *how* Apple engineers created this marvel—to access its inner capabilities and perhaps design accessories like headphones, Bluetooth speakers—must consult the *technical manual*. This requires knowledge of motherboards, circuits, wiring, microphone and speaker construction. But such a manual is not for the ordinary person—the doctor, professor, or carpenter. They cannot comprehend it, and attempting to follow it would destroy the device and bring loss upon themselves.

In essence, a creator provides: (1) the benefit of the user; (2) a seal identifying the creator; (3) a user manual; (4) representatives embodying the manual's teachings; and (5) a technical manual for those whose minds have matured in understanding.

## God's Creation: The Universe as Divine Manifestation

Now we return to the cosmic order. Allah the Almighty created this vast universe and within it, a world. For whom was it made?

Three categories of created being dwell upon earth: minerals, plants, and animals—and humanity. Minerals lie inert, contributing nothing of themselves. Plants grow, feeding animals; otherwise they rot and perish. Was creation made for animals? Our reason tells us that animals lack intellect. They cannot organize themselves or defend against predators. A thousand sheep cannot designate one as shepherd to guide them to pasture or shield them from wolves. If the universe existed for beasts, the strong would devour the weak until only carnivores remained. It is unthinkable that the Almighty would set the entire machinery of existence in motion for irrational creatures.[^cite-4]

Yet God has endowed *humankind* with intellect—an essence capable of distinguishing good from evil, of exercising dominion over all creatures. Through intellect, humans enslaved minerals and plants, extracting from them profit: jewelry, weapons, textiles, shelter, tools. Humans rule over animals—some for sustenance, some for riding, some for labor in the fields. Humans alone among creation possess the faculty of speech and language; all others communicate only through inarticulate sounds.

Therefore, reason concludes: *God created this world for humankind*, since only they can fully benefit from its gifts.

If the Almighty created the world for humans, then there must be a seal upon it—an emblem proving God's handiwork. That seal is the testimony ⟪ar:'لا الہ الا اللہ'⟫ "*There is no god but God*." Scholarly books demonstrate how this confession is imprinted upon all creation.

When God created the world, He must have issued a user manual containing what benefits humankind and what harms—affirmations and negations. This is the Holy Qur'ān.

Yet the Qur'ān is mute; it does not speak of itself. Thus there was need for a representative who could not only recite its commands but embody them in practice—demonstrating worship, virtue, patience, sincerity, courage, and how to act in poverty and in affluence. This is the Prophet Muhammad, upon whom be peace.

Beyond the manual and its representative, the Almighty provided a *technical manual*: the Imāms descended from Amīr al-Mu'minīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, whose line shall continue until the Day of Resurrection. These Imāms expound the inner secrets of the Qur'ān's outward commands. They reveal: whence came humanity, why they were sent, what transpires after death, where the soul journeys. They disclose the connections between the Hereafter and this world, the realities underlying prayer, fasting, pilgrimage, alms-giving. They answer: Why are the Maghrib prayers three *rakʿas* (cycles) and ʿIshāʾ four? If time constraints determined this, why are Fajr prayers but two *rakʿas*, when daylight is abundant? Why do some prayers contain one prostration and others two? Such mysteries are unveiled only to those whose intellects have matured and who have entered the era of the Imām of the Age (*Imām al-Zamān*).

These sciences are not granted to all. To teach them to the unprepared brings only confusion and harm. When a person cannot grasp something, he distorts it into a form he can understand—often corrupting it entirely. The scriptures of every prophet have been altered by those who removed what displeased them and inserted what pleased them. Yet the Qur'ān, after 1,400 years, remains preserved. The reason: Muslims have not understood the Qur'ān's true realities—whom it addresses and whom it concerns. Had they understood, they would have altered it to suit their preferences.

## God's Declaration of Purpose

So God created the universe; its seal is ⟪ar:'لا الہ الا اللہ'⟫ *There is no god but God*. Its manual is the Qur'ān. Its representative is the Prophet, peace be upon him. Its technical manual is ʿAlī and the Imāms. Thus we come to know God's existence through His manual, His representative, and His technical teachings. Thereafter, reason itself attempts to verify whether God is truly God.

For whom, then, did God create all of this?

The Almighty proclaims:

⟪ar:هُوَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ لَكُم مَّا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ جَمِيعًۭا⟫

"It is He who created for you all that is upon the earth."

⟪ar:ثُمَّ ٱسْتَوَىٰٓ إِلَى ٱلسَّمَآءِ فَسَوَّىٰهُنَّ سَبْعَ سَمَوَتٍۢ ۚ وَهُوَ بِكُلِّ شَىْءٍ عَلِيمٌۭ⟫

"Then He turned to the heavens and perfected them as seven heavens, and He has knowledge of all things."

Then, introducing Himself, He asks: "Do you know who I am?"

⟪ar:إِنَّنِىٓ أَنَا ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّآ أَنَا۠ فَٱعْبُدْنِى وَأَقِمِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ لِذِكْرِىٓ⟫

"Verily, I am God—there is no god but I. So serve Me, and establish prayer in My remembrance."

What is His nature?

⟪ar:ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْحَىُّ ٱلْقَيُّومُ⟫

"God—there is no god but He—the Ever-Living, the Self-Sustaining."

⟪ar:لَا تَأْخُذُهُۥ سِنَةٌۭ وَلَا نَوْمٌۭ⟫

"Slumber does not overtake Him, nor sleep."

What belongs to Him?

⟪ar:لَّهُۥ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۚ مَن ذَا ٱلَّذِى يَشْفَعُ عِندَهُۥٓ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ⟫

"His is all that dwells in the heavens and upon the earth. Who shall intercede with Him save by His leave?"

What are the bounds of His knowledge?

⟪ar:يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَىْءٍۢ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِۦٓ إِلَّا بِمَا شَآءَ⟫

"He knows what lies before them and what lies after; and they encompass not anything of His knowledge save what He wills."

What are the bounds of His dominion?

⟪ar:وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَـُٔودُهُۥ حِفْظُهُمَا وَهُوَ ٱلْعَلِىُّ ٱلْعَظِيمُ⟫

"His Throne encompasses the heavens and the earth; bearing them tires Him not. And He is the Most High, the Immensely Great."

What is His will concerning you?

⟪ar:هُوَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن تُرَابٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن نُّطْفَةٍۢ ثُمَّ مِنْ عَلَقَةٍۢ⟫

"It is He who created you from dust, then from a sperm-drop, then from a clot of blood."

⟪ar:ثُمَّ يُخْرِجُكُمْ طِفْلًۭا⟫

"Then He brings you forth as a child."

⟪ar:ثُمَّ لِتَبْلُغُوٓا۟ أَشُدَّكُمْ⟫

"That you might attain your full strength."

⟪ar:ثُمَّ لِتَكُونُوا۟ شُيُوخًۭاۚ⟫

"Then you become aged."

⟪ar:وَمِنكُم مَّن يُتَوَفَّىٰ مِن قَبْلُ ⟫

"And among you are those who are called to death before."

⟪ar:هُوَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن طِينٍۢ ثُمَّ قَضَىٰٓ أَجَلًۭا⟫

"It is He who created you from clay, then ordained a term for you."

God proclaims: Strike as many blows as you wish; contrive as much as you can; flatter the people of the world; lie and deceive; strive as much as your hearts desire! Honor shall come from Me; humiliation shall come from Me.

## Death (*al-Mawt*)

In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate.

Death is not flight or escape. Whether you confront it or flee from it, it shall overtake you:

⟪ar:الموت لیس منہ الفوت۔ ان أَقَمتُم أَخَذَکُم، وَ اِن فَرَرتُم مِنہُ اَدرَکَکُم⟫

And as the wise have said:

⟪ar:ھَیھاتَ أَن یَفُوتَ المَوتَ مَن طَلَبَ، أَو یَنجُو مِنہُ مَن ھَرَبَ⟫

It is impossible for one who seeks death to escape it, and impossible for one who flees to evade it.

Our affirmation "We belong to God" (*innā li-Allāh*) is our confession that we acknowledge God's dominion over us. Our utterance "And to Him we shall return" is our acknowledgment of our inevitable dissolution:

⟪ar:أِنَّا قَولَناَ أِنَّا للہ أِقرارٌ علی أَنفُسِناَ بِالمِلْک، وقولنا أنا الیہ راجعون، أِقرارٌ علی أنفُسِنا بِالھُلْک⟫

The wise have taught that death is an unwelcome guest, a murderer from whom there is no recompense, a rival whom none can vanquish:

⟪ar:أن الموت لزائزٌ غیر محبوب، وواترٌ غیر مطلوب، وقِرنٌ غیر مغلوب⟫

Yet there is a remedy: to kindle within ourselves longing for the blessings of the Garden, that we may come to love death and abhor this transient life:

⟪ar:شَوِّقُوا أَنفُسِکُم الی نعیم الجَنَّۃ، تُحِبُّوا الموتَ وَتَمقُتُو الحیاۃَ⟫

The appointed hour comes inevitably, and all that approaches draws near:

⟪ar:کُلَّ مُتَوَقِّعٌ آت۔ وَکُلَّ آتٍ قَرِیب۔ وَکُلَّ قَریبٌ دان۔ کُلَّ أَمرءِ لاقَ حِمَامُہُ۔⟫

How can one who yearns for death find safety? And how can one forget death when all its signs perpetually remind?

⟪ar:کیف یَسلمُ من الموت طالِبُہُ؟ کیف تَنسیٰ الموت و آثارُہُ تُذَکِّرُکَ⟫

Every rising star must descend; every soul encounters its appointed hour; every living creature dies:

⟪ar:لِکُل نَاجَمَ أَفُولٌ وَ لِکُلِّ نَفسٍ حِمامٌ، وَلکل حَیٍّ مَوتٌ⟫

Neither the abundance of wealth saves the rich from death, nor the scarcity of means preserves the poor. Had death been purchasable, the wealthy would have bought immortality:

⟪ar:لَن یَنجُو مِن الموتِ غَنِیٌّ لِکثرۃِ مالِہ، ولَن یَسلَمَ مِن الموت فَقیرٌ لأِقلالِہ۔ لَو أَنَّ الموت یُشتریٰ ، لأشتَراَہُ الأغنِیَاءُ⟫

Do you not observe how the rotation of night and day wears away all that is new, brings near all that is distant, and fulfills all that is promised?

⟪ar:أَفلاَ تَرَونَ اِلی اللیل و النھار، یَبلِیاَنِ کُلَّ جَدید وَ یَقِرَّبانِ کل بعید وَ یَأتِیاَنِ بِکُلِّ مَوعُود⟫

## Creation Ordained Through Love

Do you wish your spouse to cherish you? Your neighbors to hold you dear? Understand then that the foundation of both the cosmos and divine religion rests upon love (*muḥabbat*), not upon enmity and anger. The world is sustained by love; when love departs, so too does the world.

The Prophet's Companion Nusayrallah is reported to have taught: "Know that on the Day of Resurrection, each person will regard only himself. The father will not inquire after his son; the mother will not ask for her daughter; the brother will concern himself nothing with his brother." This dissolution of familial bonds reveals a truth: love will not persist in that realm. The day love ceases is the day the doomsday arrives. Thus the end of love is the end of creation itself.

### The Parable of Pharaoh's Clown

In Pharaoh's court there stood a jester who mocked Moses, caricaturing his movements and speech for the amusement of the tyrant. When the moment came that Pharaoh was drowned in the sea, this clown stood upon the shore, witnessing the spectacle. Upon being questioned by Moses, who reported the matter to God, the reply came: "O Moses, you comprehend not the reality of love! I did not drown the clown because he was, at that moment, your image and likeness. To drown the image of the beloved contradicts the principle of love itself."

### Nourishment as Acceptance

Nourishment operates upon the principle of acceptance. Observe: minerals do not become part of a plant unless the plant acknowledges them, draws them in, and integrates them into its own substance. The plant does not consume minerals; rather, the minerals acknowledge the plant's reality and yield themselves. When the goat accepts itself—surrenders itself—it becomes food for the human. The human eats it and makes it part of his body. Similarly, unless a human acknowledges the Divine nature of ⟪ar:'أنا'⟫ the self—the ego—that person cannot become nourishment for the angelic realm. And this acknowledgment is possible only through love, never through lust or desire alone.

### God's Love

The scholar al-Zamakhsharī writes in his *Hayāt al-Hayawān* that Hazrat ʿĪsā (Jesus, upon whom be peace) once passed by a mountain and beheld a worshipper in solitude, engaged in prayer. Years of devotion had bent his back; his body had become dry and withered from ascetic practice. Jesus inquired: "O righteous one, for how long have you been devoted to worship?" He replied: "O Prophet of God, for seventy years—yet not a single petition has the Almighty granted me." The elder then beseeched the blessed Prophet: "I am His special servant; please intercede with Him on my behalf." Jesus asked: "What do you desire?" The worshipper said: "I beseech the Almighty to grant me a single particle of His pure love."

Hazrat ʿĪsā raised his hands in prayer, and the supplication was answered. Days later, when ʿĪsā passed by that place again, he found the shrine demolished, the ground split asunder, and the worshipper within, mouth agape, eyes closed, gazing upward toward the heavens.

> *The eyes of those who are veiled have found You in distance vast;*
> *But the heart, in its closeness, sees You ever near.*

When ʿĪsā greeted the elder, there came no response. Astonished, he turned to God in prayer: "What is the state of this elder?" The answer came: "O Prophet! This worshipper entreated us for a particle of Our pure love. Knowing he could not bear it, We granted him but one part of seventy thousand portions of Our love. Look upon his condition now! Tell Me, what would become of him had We granted him any greater measure?"

### True Love Is Reciprocal

The mark of authentic love is that it flows both directions—not one-sided affection, but mutual regard. As God declares:

⟪ar:يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مَن يَرْتَدَّ مِنكُمْ عَن دِينِهِۦ فَسَوْفَ يَأْتِى ٱللَّهُ بِقَوْمٍۢ يُحِبُّهُمْ وَيُحِبُّونَهُ⟫

"O you who have faith! If any of you renounce your faith, know that in place of them, God shall bring a people whom He loves and who love Him."

God desires them for His friends, and they, in turn, love God. You become His property, and He becomes your possession. The sweetness of love exists only when passion burns on both sides. The love of God belongs solely to those who follow His Messenger in truth and sincerity. The Almighty proclaims:

> ⟪quran 3:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِن كُنتُمْ تُحِبُّونَ ٱللَّهَ فَٱتَّبِعُونِى يُحِبِّبْكُمُ ٱللَّهُ وَيَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ ذُنُوبَكُمْ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH to mankind): "If you (really) love Allah then follow me (i.e. accept Islamic Monotheism, follow the Quran and the Sunnah), Allah will love you and forgive you of your sins. And Allah is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful." *
> — **Quran 3:31**

Thus the path to God's love lies in steadfast devotion to His Messenger and his teachings. Through such devotion, the human heart finds its rest, and love—both given and received—becomes the animating force of existence itself.
