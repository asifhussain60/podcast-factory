A deriviative of this word is MAWLA, the one who is "in-charge"; the one who is "in-control".
And this is why Wilayah is related to the Shahadah, which begins with لا الہ الا اللہ..... There is no ILAH except Allah. Shahdah begins with negation. Salaah
Once this direction has been set; once the beloved has been identified as the QIBLA of the heart, the next thing is to now "connect" with the beloved. That is Salaat. The word SALAAH comes from SILA, which literally means to connect with; to join with. It also means to "burn up". Because when you're connected to Allah, you burn up in his love! Another reason is because sins burn away in his presence. So now that we have fallen in love with him, the next natural step, is to constantly connect with. That's أِقَامِ الصَّلاۃ..... Establish prayer, or establish the connection. Because this connection is what nourishes love. It helps the love grow. The more you connect with the beloved the more you the intensity of love increases. In old times, this connection was done through letters; then it became emails, now its whatsapp. But these are all forms of connection with the beloved. And this is why SALAAH is such a beautiful gift for the true lovers. This is why they cannot wait until the next prayer time. And once they're connected with Allah, they prolong it as much as they can.


Zakaat
The next thing is spending in the pleasure of the beloved. When you love somone, and you're constantly connected with that person, the next natural consequence is to love the ones that the beloved loves. So for example if I love you, and I'm constantly connected with you, I will obviously develop an affection for the people you love; and the people you care for. So if I find out that your son is visiting my hometown, I'll make sure he's properly taken care of; to the best of my abilities. Why? Because he is important to you! So I'll naturally make him important to me. That's the concept of Zakaat. And if you study the Quran, you'll always find these two things mentioned together
وَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ
Establish prayer and give Zakaat..... Why? Because if you love me, then you will take care of the ones I love. And so even in the hadees, immediately after the mention of SALAAH, we get the command to give zakaat.....
وَأِقَامِ الصَّلاۃ، وَ أِیتَاءِ الزَّکاۃ

SAUM
Until this point scholars say, the believer is at the stage of حُب. The believer is in the state of *love* with his beloved. But with this constant reinforcement of SALAAH and ZAKAAT, the believer now gets to the stage of passionate love, عشق. This is now the level of love that is approaching insanity. Where the lover is so obsessed with the beloved, that he is no longer interested in food and drink. And this is the stage of SAUM. Where the lover now begins physically sacrificing his own desires for the desires of the beloved. One of the meanings of the word ISHQ is "to search", "to strive for", "to seek". Another meaning for the word ISHQ, is a fool. Because  AASHIQ appear to be foolish people. Because a AASHIQ is a person who has given up his intellect, for his heart. ISHQ is also used for a fatal disease. It is related to a vine that strangles the life out of the plant that it gets hold of. Because the plant gives away all of its nourishment to the vine. It withers and die attempting to feed the vine.
And this is SAUM, fasting in the passionate love of Allah(Azza Wa Jal). Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) said in a hadees
قال اللہ تعالی، کُلَّ عَمَلَ ابْنَ آدَمَ لَہُ اِلَّا الصِّیَام..... فَأِنَّہُ لی وَ أنَا أُجزِیَ بِہِ
All of the actions of the children of Adam are for them except fasting. Fasting is solely for me and I will reward him for it

Now all acts of worship are for Allah, then why did He say that fasting is for me and I will reward him for it? Isn't Allah the one who will reward for all the other acts of worship as well? Then why did he single this one out? Scholars say its because of its honoured status before Him. And also because it is a demonstration of sincerity towards Him. Fasting is a secret act between a believer and his master, which no one can see except Allaah. And because it is much more difficult form of worship, because the believer has to give up his desire for food for the love of Allah.
Another reason is that every other act is mentioned by a number, because the reward of a good deed is 10 fold, but by Allah saying "Fasting is solely for me and I will reward him for it", he's saying that I will reward him according to my discretion. I will multiply its reward as many times as I want. Because fasting is an act of patience (صَبَر), and he says in the quran
إِنَّمَا يُوَفَّى ٱلصَّبِرُونَ أَجْرَهُم بِغَيْرِ حِسَابٍۢ
 Indeed, the patient will be given their reward without account
So SAUM is a state of abandonment. Linguistically, the word SAUM means "to abandon", "to sacrifice for the beloved". It elevates love (حُب) to passionate love (عشق). And you will always find an AASHIQ in a state of destitution. Why? Because he is so engrossed in the love of his beloved, that he loses interest in every other desire of the world. He abandons everything else. The world can simply no longer hold his interest. That's the state of ISHQ.

Junaid Baghdadi was performing tawaaf around the Kaaba when he saw this young girl doing tawaaf as well, and reciting these verses

إِذَا اشْتَدَّ شَوقِى هَامَ قَلبِى بِذِكرِهِ ..... فَإِنْ رُمتُ قُرباً مِن حَبِيبِى تَقَرَّبَا
يَبدُو فَأَفنٰى ثُمَّ أَحْيٰى لَهُ بِهِ ..... وَيُسْعِدُنِى حَتّٰى ألَذَّ وأطْرَبَا

I tried so hard to hide my love, but I failed; and now my love has come out in broad daylight

Junaid Baghdadi hears this girl reciting these verses and he goes into a rage, and starts yelling at this girl. What are you saying? How dare you say such things at this place? Don't you know where you're standing? And she says, Don't misunderstand the meanings of my verses. My verses are for the same one you are doing tawaaf for

If I did not love Allah, you would find me sleeping in the comfort of my room
It is the love of Allah that has dragged me here before him, and estranged me from my country

Then she asked Junaid, what are you doing tawaaf around? And he said, I'm doing tawaaf of the Kaaba. When she heard this, she started laughing and she said

يَطُوفُونَ بالأحجار يَبغُونَ قُربَةُ..... إِلَيكَ وَهمٌ أَقسٰى قُلُوباً مِنَ الصَّخَرِ
فَلَو أخْلَصُوا فِى الوُدَّ غَابَت صِفَاتِهِم..... وَقَامَتُ صِفَاتُ الوَدَّ لِلحَقِّ بِالذِّكَرِ

What crazy people are these who walk around these stones searching for you. Indeed their hearts are harder than stones
Were they sincere in their love for you, they would forget their own self; and would only remember the attribute of your love

When Junaid baghdadi heard these verses he passed out. When he gained his consciousness that girl had left.

So there is this progression of love that takes place in these obligatory forms of worship. Shahadah is the introduction. It is identifying the beloved by negating everyone else, and it is understanding the final product of that love which is Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam). Shahadah is تَعَرُّف; and then SALAAH and ZAKAAH are تَعَلُّق. They connect the lover with the beloved, which inculcates love. And then there is SAUM, which then ignites the fires of passion, elevating love to the level of ISHQ. In this state the beloved willingly abandons everything else for the beloved. The beauties and pleasures of this world no longer hold the interest of the lover. And then this ISHQ forces the lover to want to meet the beloved, which takes him to the door of the beloved, and that's when the beloved invites the lover for a meeting, which is HAJJ. Hajj is وِصَال، لِقَاءُ المَحبُوب.

تیرے کوچے اس بہانے مجھے دن سے رات کرنا
کبھی اِس سے بات کرنا، کبھی اُس سے بات کرنا

The essence and the spirit of Hajj, is to set out in the search of Allah. It is the final HIJRAH. The word Hijra means to move from one location to another permanently, leaving behind everything. There is a ZAAHIRI HIJRA that Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) did, which was his moving from Mecca to Medina. And that physical HIJRA is a sign, and a reminder for every believer, that they too need to make a spiritual HIJRA; which is moving from sins, from transgressions, from disobedience to submission; from evil to good; from spiritual tribulations to peace; to سلامۃ; to اسلام. Someone asked the prophet:
یا رسول اللہ! من الاسلام؟
Ya Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam), what is Islam? He replied
أَن تُسَلَّمَ قَلبَکَ لِی اللہ
Submitting your heart to Allah!

So this is the essence of Hajj. A determination to change the heart before returning back home. It is that shift in mentality, that until now I've lived life according to my will and desires; but moving forward, I will live my life according to Allah's will and comamnds, which is the SEERAH of Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam). To do what he asks us to do, and refrain from what he forbids us to do. That is the essence of لب بیک اللھم لبیک.....

Hajj is the greatest and most complete form of worship, because it includes all the other forms of worship. In Hajj, you are moving towards the QIBLA, which is the essence of Shahadah; you are praying; you are spending money; you are fasting; and each and every action you do requires exertion, which is JEHAD. JEHAD comes from the word ja-ha-da, which means "to struggle or to strive". And every action of worship includes effort and struggle in it.
