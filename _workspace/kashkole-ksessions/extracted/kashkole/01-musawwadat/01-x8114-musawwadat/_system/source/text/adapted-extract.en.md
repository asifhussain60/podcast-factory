When a deceased guide (shaikh) departs this world, he does not carry away all the forms he once embodied. Rather, the elder transmits the forms of the three orders to the succeeding elder, that they may continue training those under their charge. For this reason, it is forbidden to perform supererogatory prayer in the graveyard between the tombs. Prayer, after all, signifies invitation; the invitation of the grave's occupant flows perpetually at that site. To establish one's own call there would create interference. Hence it is disallowed.

## Paradise and Its Gate

Every house requires a main door as the sole passage by which inhabitants and guests may enter. So too with Paradise: entry is granted only through its appointed gates. Paradise is none other than the Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal), and its gates are embodied in the Messengers ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و سلم⟫ in their respective epochs, and in their successors thereafter, who hold this station in their own times. The key to this celestial gate is the Word—the testimony of faith: ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ محمد الرسول اللہ⟫. Only those who pronounce this confession with sincere hearts shall receive the key and cross the threshold into Paradise.[^cite-5]

That the intellect constitutes tomorrow's paradise becomes evident when we observe that all human comfort, ease, and tranquility flow from intellectual understanding. Conversely, those bereft of intellect—who possess no capacity for wisdom—suffer grievous torment: they inflict upon themselves sorrows, hardships, and terrors born of ignorance. The wise person remains unharmed by worldly afflictions, whereas the ignorant writhes beneath sorrow, transgression, and base desire. Thus, when we recognize that the partial intellect possessed by humanity—through which this world itself becomes paradise and every comfort becomes attainable—we must conclude that true paradise is the Intellect, for it encompasses all perfections. And since the one who stands nearest to this supreme Intellect in the world becomes the gate of paradise (as the gate stands adjacent to the house), it has been demonstrated that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫the Messenger was nearest of all creatures to the Intellect, and therefore the Gate of Paradise.[^cite-6]

Thus, whoever obeys the Messenger of Allah, accepts his ordinances, and grasps the inner truth of the Prophet's words and deeds shall enter Paradise. Allah Most High declares:

> ⟪quran 4:80⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَّن يُطِعِ ٱلرَّسُولَ فَقَدْ أَطَاعَ ٱللَّهَ ۖ وَمَن تَوَلَّىٰ فَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ حَفِيظًۭا ‎⟫
> *He who obeys the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), has indeed obeyed Allah, but he who turns away, then we have not sent you (O Muhammad PBUH) as a watcher over them.*

Every Messenger in his time has been the gate of Paradise. Through adherence to his law—undertaken with knowledge of its inner meaning—a believer attains Paradise. Yet whoever accepts the law without taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis) finds the gate barred. Allah Most High says:

> ⟪quran 39:71⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَسِيقَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ جَهَنَّمَ زُمَرًا ۖ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءُوهَا فُتِحَتْ أَبْوَبُهَا وَقَالَ لَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَآ أَلَمْ يَأْتِكُمْ رُسُلٌۭ مِّنكُمْ يَتْلُونَ عَلَيْكُمْ آيَتِ رَبِّكُمْ وَيُنذِرُونَكُمْ لِقَآءَ يَوْمِكُمْ هَذَا ۚ قَالُوا۟ بَلَىٰ وَلَكِنْ حَقَّتْ كَلِمَةُ ٱلْعَذَابِ عَلَى ٱلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *And those who disbelieved will be driven to Hell in groups, till, when they reach it, the gates thereof will be opened (suddenly like a prison at the arrival of the prisoners). And its keepers will say, "Did not the Messengers come to you from yourselves, reciting to you the Verses of your Lord, and warning you of the Meeting of this Day of yours?" They will say: "Yes, but the Word of torment has been justified against the disbelievers!"*

That Allah states here that the gates of Paradise were opened reveals they had previously remained shut. This signifies that the laws transmitted by the Prophets ⟪ar:انبیاء علیہم السلام⟫ are enfolded in signs and parables, and that the deliverance of humanity depends upon their disclosure. Just as a closed door denies rest, so when opened it grants sanctuary: those charged with interpreting these mysteries—the Prophets ⟪ar:انبیا⟫ and their appointed successors, the Inheritors ⟪ar:اوصیاء علیہم السلام⟫—unlock paradise through taʾwīl. When they illuminate the inner reality of these allusions, the gate swings open and the seeker finds peace. In every age, the gates of paradise shall be unlocked for all the faithful through the Imam of the Age ⟪ar:امام الزمان⟫.[^cite-7]

Every gate requires a key, and none approach it without one. The key to paradise's gate is the Kalima-e-Tayyaba—the Pure Word. Therefore, none have come to the Messenger of Allah save through acceptance of this testimony of witness. The Messenger promised Paradise to those who pronounce the word with sincerity of heart:

> *Whoever recites the word of sincerity with a pure heart has entered Paradise.*

Thus it stands proven: the testimony is the key, and whosoever obtains it and reaches the gate shall enter in.

## The Establishment of Astrology

Hazrat Idris (a.s.) was the first to uncover the science of the stars and devoted thirty years of his life to perfecting it. The creation of this astrology was his miracle, which he imparted to his contemporaries. He flourished in the generation seventy (reckoned between Adam and Noah). The Imam of his time, likewise named Idris, appointed him his successor before departing this life; yet the people rejected him as Imam.

He posed a question to those summoned by the daʿwa (the divine invitation): "Is it conceivable that Allah's earth remains bereft of His proof before His eyes?" All answered that it was impossible. He then commanded them: "Search out who is the proof of Allah upon this earth!" They asked, "How shall we discern him?" He replied, "I have created this astrology and taught it to you for this very purpose."

Once they had mastered this knowledge, he instructed them to divide the earth into four quarters and observe in which quarters the proof of Allah dwelt. They complied and perceived that in all four quarters, the proof of Allah existed. He then bade them subdivide the quarter wherein they stood, seeking the proof within those four parts. Again they found him there. He continued this division through successive iterations until at last he said, "Behold, we have discovered for you the proof of Allah whom Allah has made a witness against us."

(From *Lessons of Anwar al-Tayfa*, attributed to Muhammad ibn Tahir)

## The Reality of Knowledge

Knowledge is a point from which ignorance has strayed. There exist two pathways of knowing: one proceeds from unity into multiplicity—from source to emanation—which is the way of revelation and manifestation. The second ascends from multiplicity back to unity, from emanation to source—the method of inner progression. The first path is most majestic; through it we grasp what has been unveiled concerning soul and body, matter and form, the heavens and elements, and creation itself. The second is subtle and inward, wherein we strive to perceive what lies beneath the point of existence and dwell within mysteries and inner realities. Thus we come to know the secrets contained in the heavenly scriptures, or grasp the mysteries of our Prophet ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫ which were manifested during the Night of Ascension (Laylat al-Miʿrāj).

I have been given knowledge of the First and the Last. Allah Almighty has unveiled to me the inner truth of all things, for I am the lowest point of existence! Ask me of all things beneath the Throne, for I am that point which the people know as the Point of Non-existence—the point which precedes nothing. It is the point toward which the central point of being orients itself, toward which the lines of its circle converge and curve. Each circle possesses two corresponding points: one marking the beginning, the other the end; thus the circle completes its form through roundness. Therefore Allah says: "As you began, so shall you return." The Divine Names manifest these two dimensions—the First and the Last, the Manifest and the Hidden ⟪ar:ظاہر · باطن · ابتدا · انتہا⟫—and eternities both future and past refer to these twin points. The arc, too, follows this same trajectory, for it signifies the discontinuity within the realm of becoming, creating distance between the constrained and the Absolute, between the possible and the necessary. This letter marks the station of divine proximity. Therefore creation and return, descent and ascent, capacity and perfection are unified in truth despite their apparent duality. No station exceeds this except ⟪ar:' أو ادنی'⟫—the station of unicity (Ahadiyyat), where intellect is annihilated and observances dissolve. From this point proceeds the point of prophethood and illumination. Both these stations pertain exclusively to Muhammad and ʿAlī (a.s.), for prophethood and the Guardianship of Succession belong to them alone.[^cite-8]

Knowledge manifests in three stages. He who reaches the first stage becomes proud and sits in judgment. He who attains the second becomes obedient and considers himself humbled. He who arrives at the third embraces poverty and perishes, knowing that he knows nothing. Sharing knowledge with Allah is ignorance before Him.

## Illusion versus Delusion

*(no content in source)*

**Illusions**

An illusion is fundamentally a deceptive perception. Illusions manifest through sight (most commonly), hearing, taste, touch, or smell—perceiving something that exists but interpreting it wrongly. They operate upon stimuli genuinely present yet rendered difficult or misinterpreted. Illusions entertain us at magic performances and provide the stock-in-trade of street-corner charlatans. A practical example: a spare uniform hung over a doorway to air is glimpsed in darkness; the watcher, heart racing, believes an intruder stands before him—only to find himself facing a hanger draped in clothing. Another: hearing one's name called whilst the radio plays. Illusions occur universally and signal no mental disorder unless persistent and disruptive to daily life.

**Delusions**

Delusions are fixed beliefs—false or fanciful—held despite contradictory evidence, their holder unable to distinguish fact from fantasy. They divide into bizarre and non-bizarre forms. A bizarre delusion claims the implausible: aliens have removed and replaced one's organs. A non-bizarre delusion holds mistaken but possible content: one lives under constant police surveillance. Such fixed beliefs, however impossible, resist refutation in the mind that holds them.

## Genuine and Counterfeit Wealth

Brothers and Sisters of the Jamāʿah—the Lord of all worlds has bestowed upon us countless blessings. Some reside within ourselves; others surround us in such abundance that enumeration proves impossible. Gratitude to Allah thus becomes imperative at every moment, in every circumstance. Gratitude preserves blessings and multiplies them—as the Quran declares: "If you are grateful, I shall increase you." Money stands among His most precious gifts. It travels and enables travel. Locked within pockets or vaults, wealth loses its virtue; confined to a house, neither the person nor his understanding flourishes nor experience grows.

When wealth circulates—deployed in productive enterprise—it multiplies in value and quantity. Now that you have journeyed for the *Urs Sharif*, surely your pockets contain the currency of your expenses. I shall not presume to examine them, yet this much is certain: each carries some money. This external wealth serves our worldly affairs; similarly, inner knowledge (*Batini ʿUlūm*) proves critical for our journey beyond death. Whether worldly riches or spiritual treasure, money provides passage; it ensures an easy traverse, elevating both rank and station.

Consider currency itself: it is paper, small or large. Its value depends not upon weight or quantity but upon the imprint upon it. Not every paper constitutes currency; it requires the Governor's signature and the government's watermark. Genuine currency holds value everywhere. Remember this, Brothers and Sisters: every human in earthly form resembles a banknote. One's worth lies in one's own hands to increase. By striving, one may become a note of thousand rupees, ten thousand, even a lakh. Hazrat ʿAlī has said: "A human shall attain the rank for which he prepares himself; strive therefore for higher stations." Worth derives not only from character, morals, and conduct, but from bearing the seal of Sulaimān and the watermark of their love and devotion. Just as counterfeit notes are rejected and sent to the rubbish heap, so too our deeds must not be cast aside for falseness. Genuine currency circulates and continues its journey; counterfeits reach a false destination.

Alhamdulillah, all assembled here—young and old alike—for the *Urs Sharif* bear the Sulaimānī insignia imprinted upon your hearts. In the presence of the Guardian (Humāmī) and before Allah, your worth and value shall, God willing, increase continually, your wealth shall flourish, your circumstances shall improve. Your journey and that of your wealth shall end in success and triumph, becoming a shield against Hellfire and its flames—Amīn.
