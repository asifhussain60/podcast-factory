The deceased elder does not carry with him all the forms he possessed. Rather, the Elder entrusts to his successor the forms of the three Orders for their training. Likewise, certain configurations are deposited upon the tombs of the elders in cemeteries, where the elder instructs them. Every person in the grave maintains his gaze upon his own grave—that is, upon the assembly of people gathered there. For this reason, it is forbidden to establish prayer in the graveyard between the graves, for prayer signifies invitation (daʿwa), and the invitation of the grave's occupant is ongoing at that site, making it impermissible to institute one's own invitation there.

Following this is the form of the brain, which corresponds to the bodily frame. Allāh says in the Holy Qurʾān: "The poor, the orphans, and the prisoners of war."

When five people—ʿAlī, Fātimah, al-Hasan, al-Husayn, and their offspring—fasted, the poor, the orphans, and prisoners came to their door, and all five gave them their bread. The teachings of the Imām (upon whom be peace) spread throughout the cosmos. Here "the poor" signifies a preacher who was present at the feast of the Five Pure Ones; they were called poor because they distributed peace to people. These preachers were enslaved by attachments yet were not commanded to be useful to others. The five liberated them from lust and enabled them to grant benefits. The virtue of distributing bread among the five is that it was their collective feast, wherein all five freed themselves from the solemn prayers of their individual stations. An orphan is one who has no equal—that is, a person of exalted rank—or one whose benefactor has passed away. Those at this feast who, though of elevated rank, were prevented from benefiting others for a time, were also liberated by the five. A prisoner is one held in a place where the scholars govern—the opposite of imprisonment where one can neither benefit nor be benefited. The five also released such persons from that bondage.

## Basmalah (In the Name of God)

*Sacred fact:*

Most prayers are made through the Name of Allāh. For example: "Allāh is the Creator of all things." In this invocation, the conscience of Allāh—or, put differently, in the Basmalah—is advancing toward ʿAyn al-ʿAẓīmah. Their names and their hearts are the means through which we question. Just as we are known by our names, those who can make us know the Divine are names or their veils—Muḥammad the Arab—upon whom be peace and blessings.

## The Death of the Speakers Through the Veils of the Executors

*Sacred fact:*

Sayyidunā Ismāʿīl ibn Hībah said in al-Kawākib al-Durriyyah: "On this night, the veil of the Prophets dies, and the Speakers (al-Nuṭaqāʾ) die through the veil of the Prophets." The transitions of the Prophets occur first and after the Prophet. Just as ʿAlī died before the Prophet—though ʿAlī lived thirty years after the Prophet's death, in reality the Prophet's veil was functioning throughout that entire period. For this reason the Prophet said to ʿAlī: "I grant you the right to divorce ʿĀʾishah after my death."

*Received from the exoteric teaching:*

When the Messenger of Allāh (upon whom be peace and blessings) was bewitched, Gabriel (upon whom be peace) said to him: "You are one of the Protected." The recitation of Sūrat al-Falaq eliminated the effect of this magic.

## The Sacred Qurʾān

**1. The Basmallah in the Qurʾān**

The Basmallah—"In the Name of Allāh, the Compassionate, the Merciful"—is repeated fourteen times in the Qurʾān for many sacred reasons.

Everything derives from the Basmallah and the Compassion and Mercy of Allāh.

---

**2. The Furqān as Light**

For the sake of the Qurʾān, the Master of the Worlds declared: "This is the Distinction (al-Furqān) which cannot be separated from Proof (Burhān). This is the light that the Emanators cannot comprehend. This is a river whose depth cannot be measured. This is a fountain that shall never run dry." [^cite-4]

---

**3. Four Layers of Qurʾānic Knowledge**

Imām al-Hasan (upon whom be peace) said: "Within this Qurʾān are four types of knowledge: (1) *ʿIbārah* (exoteric meaning for the common people); (2) *Ishārah* (allusion for the elite); (3) *Latāʾif* (subtle intimations for the saints of Allāh); (4) *Ḥaqāʾiq* (profound truths for the Prophets)." [^cite-5]

---

**4. The Qurʾān's Supernal Origin**

All other scriptures and books were revealed from the lower realms of the spiritual world. The Qurʾān, however, was revealed from the highest realm—namely, from the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal).

---

**5. The Qurʾān's Sufficiency**

If the people of the Seventies were so elevated as to understand, only Sūrat al-Fātiḥah would have been revealed, for it contains the essence of the entire Qurʾān. In the Period of Disclosure, more than this shall be revealed.

---

**6. The Fifty Imāms in Sūrat al-Mursalāt**

At one time, most verses—fifty in all—were revealed in Sūrat al-Mursalāt, indicating that there would be fifty Imāms from Imām al-Hasan (upon whom be peace) onward, after which the Day of Judgment would arrive. In this sūrah the same word is repeated ten times: "Ten shall be brought down upon the Rock."

---

**7. Seventy Interpretations of Each Verse**

Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (upon whom be peace) said: "I can expound every word of the Qurʾān in seven ways." Then he said: "Nay, I can describe it in seventy ways!" [^cite-6]

## Ḥadīth and Qurʾān

The Qurʾān and the Ḥadīth bear the same meaning. Both emanate from the same Light. That which the Messenger of Allāh designated as a verse became the Qurʾān; that which he termed "my saying" became a Ḥadīth. Both proceed from the same source. Yet when we read the Qurʾān, we never question whether it be a Ḥadīth; nor, when we read a Ḥadīth, do we doubt its authenticity against the Qurʾān. Your wonder—and rightly so—is that they have preserved such different accents for both!

## The Transformation of Veils

When the Prophet declared, "Whoever I am master of, ʿAlī is his master," the veils of both were transformed: ʿAlī took the Prophet's veil, and the Prophet took ʿAlī's veil. The Speakers and Executors die through their veils—that is, ʿAlī died during the Prophet's lifetime. The veil of the Prophet's death is truly the veil of ʿAyn (the Source). That is, ʿAyn died before Mīm.

All the Prophets die in the lifetime of the Prophet, for the Prophet is the Master of the Speakers (Sayyid al-Nuṭaqāʾ); hence their affairs are the same.

## The Circle of Covenant

Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (upon whom be peace) says that believers are speakers; if someone departs from this circle, Nature brings him back to it; if someone wishes to enter from without, Nature prevents him. The Qurʾān describes this same matter thus: "Within it is mercy; without it is punishment."

ʿAlī said in the Four Hundred Precepts: "If a person falls asleep after reciting *Qul Hu Allāh*, seventy thousand angels protect him until he awakens." Reciting the Qurʾān three times is equivalent to performing the full Qurʾān.

The Messenger of Allāh (upon whom be peace and blessings) made Madīnah his base. Even after the conquest of Mecca, he did not make Mecca his stronghold. "There is no migration after the conquest"—that is, after the conquest, the blessings of the migration end.

## Knowledge and Travel

Why did the Messenger of Allāh (upon whom be peace) say, "Seek knowledge even if you must travel to China"? Because China was an island in those days. The point was: even if you must leave your kinfolk and journey to distant islands, you should seek knowledge.

Ubayy ibn Kaʿb attained the heart of Imām al-Hasan's temple.

If there were no Prophets, we would not have been born. They are the origin of our existence. "Thus are the heavens and the earth created."

## The Two Mahdīs

Maulana ʿAlī says:

"I have no wish for confinement. One shall depart from the other. The Unique One, the Mahdī, and the chosen, the satisfied one.

Two of us shall emerge. The first shall descend from the second generation. The first shall be named Mahdī; the second, Executor."

The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said: "I have received four things from the Throne of the Mahdī, which I alone have received: (1) Sūrat al-Fātiḥah; (2) the Throne Verse (Āyat al-Kursī); (3) the final verses of Sūrat al-Baqarah, which we recite and lay the deceased in the grave; (4) Sūrat al-Kawthar." [^cite-7]

In doubt, recite the Throne Verse (Āyat al-Kursī).

If you wish to sharpen your memory, recite Sūrat al-Muzzammil. This shall so sharpen your memory that people will call you a sorcerer.

Recite Sūrat al-Wāqiʿah: Never be in want, and never stretch forth your hands.

Recite Sūrat Yā Sīn: Every word shall give you glad tidings of Paradise.

Recite *Istighfār Allāh* (seeking forgiveness) seven times after each obligatory prayer. Two things of peace have come from Allāh: one has departed and one remains in us. What has departed is the Essence of the Messenger of Allāh; what remains is the word of seeking forgiveness, which is recited at the conclusion of the Throne Verse. These forgivenesses are in truth the virtues of the Mahdī (upon whom be peace).

## ʿAlī's Conquest of Yemen

When Maulana ʿAlī went to Yemen on the orders of the Holy Prophet, he conquered all of Yemen through a sermon, and the whole of Yemen entered Islām.

When the Messenger of Allāh appointed Maulana ʿAlī as judge, you were afraid, asking: "How shall I discharge this responsibility?"

The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) gave ʿAlī his saliva. Then he said to him: "Close your mouth, and let not a single word emerge from it until you have performed two rak'ahs of prayer. In the first rak'ah, recite the Throne Verse; in the second, recite Sūrat al-Aʿrāf." [^cite-8]

Maulana ʿAlī did so, and thereafter became the most learned person in the Ummah and the finest judge. Such is the miracle of the Prophet's saliva—what then of his other gifts!

## Truth and Patience

In Sūrat al-ʿAṣr, truth is linked with patience—that is, patience in the difficulties that arise in recognizing truth. The destination of truth is singular, but there are different stages along the way. Truth refers to the essence of the Imām (upon whom be peace). It is necessary to have patience in comprehending them.

Drinking water signifies the knowledge of esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl); food signifies the knowledge of profound truths (ḥaqāʾiq).

Hearing (Samāʿ) is an instrument given for the perception of what has passed, what has vanished, and what has died—that is, for learning about the past.

A thinker is an instrument through which knowledge of the future is obtained. They impress upon their souls the form of what is about to transpire and establish it in their own being.

Allāh Almighty presents such things to His servants in dreams. He shows them things of the past to inform them of the future, giving them good tidings or warning them of evil.

## Will and Inheritance

The meaning of a will is obedience to Allāh and His Messenger, and obedience to the one to whom command has been entrusted. The rest are matters of inheritance, which are apportioned according to Allāh's command. Whether a will is made or not, the inheritance is divided into six portions—the *sattah sahām*: oneself, father, mother, son, daughter, wife.

The meaning of the will is to say "There is no god but Allāh; Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allāh," to obey the Prophet, and then to perform religious duties. A will is made to the soul about to depart. It is an optional act. Those left behind have no will, for after death a person has no authority over inheritance matters.

The name of supplication to Allāh is "will."

## Marriage and Divorce

Marriage and divorce are truly matters of form. Maulana ʿAlī says: "I would take up the sword, but I would not divorce him." The exclusion of form once it has entered the temple is called divorce.

Each Imām has a particular portfolio upon which he works. Imām al-Hasan married fifty-three times and divorced them—they were circumstances where his responsibility was to exclude form. Imām ʿAlī Zayn al-ʿĀbidīn brought a portfolio of prayers. The Messenger of Allāh brought the affairs of the Commander of the Faithful (ʿAlī).

The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said: "Whoever neglects the Witr prayer or delays the Fajr prayer is not of us and not of our followers. Whoever commits adultery with a married woman, uses intoxicants, is not of us. Whoever does not know the truth of me and my Ahl al-Bayt is not of me."

## The Prophet's Instructions and ʿAlī's

The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said: "Haste is an error, and patience is among the attributes of Might and Majesty." Yet there is a command to hasten in three things:

1. The guardianship of orphans

3. The funeral of the deceased

1. The miswāk (tooth-cleaning stick)
2. The use of perfume (ʿaṭṭār)
3. Love for women"

## The Miswāk

Performing the miswāk as the Prophet did was to gain the benefit of one's limits (ḥudūd). Teeth are an example of limits. He would dispel doubts about his limits through his teachings. He taught all limits together, not separately; thus the miswāk is applied in breadth, not in length.

## The Use of Perfume

Perfume immediately becomes the nourishment of the soul. Passion for worship arises from perfume, and passion itself arises.

## Love for Women

The Messenger of Allāh greatly loved his wives. He had twelve wives, nine at a time. Though he was occupied with the affairs of the feast, none of his wives complained that he neglected them. The Prophet said: "Whoever does not marry is not of my Ummah." Whoever commits fornication has committed it; he does not marry a fornicatress, but he does marry one.

## The Shrine of Gardens

1. Your soul belongs to Allāh and is about to depart. It is your possession, yet it belongs to Allāh.

2. One that is yours and shall remain yours: your action. The soul takes form as a result of action.

3. One that is yours and one that is Allāh's: your supplication has been asked, and Allāh has answered it—this refers to the supplication we make at the end of ablution.

Saturn (Zuḥal) and Mars (Bahram) are the names of two of the seven stars.

The first hour of Sunday is the Sun. The second hour is Venus.

The first hour of Monday is the Moon. The first hour of the week is Saturn.

Saturn is *Salman* in Persian. Upon Jupiter is Jaʿfar; then Mars; then ʿAmmār; then the Sun is Abī ibn Kaʿb. That is, in the first hour of the week, the lights of Saturn from every circle shall be established upon this world. Maulana Salman al-Fārsī has been entrusted with responsibility for the first hour. The speakers are inherent in their place, and their wise ones work with them.

Kevan's (Jupiter's) function is to increase enmity when establishing influence—the antithetical summons—so that deeds may be completed and the believers purified. Kevan and Mars are good in themselves, but their effects are bad in outward appearance. The example is like a father's scolding: when he scolds, we do not say ill of him. His scolding, though painful, is good. On Saturdays, people should hunt and slaughter. The parable of hunting is taking the time of the noble (the sacrificial offering) on the day of Ghadīr Khumm. The Imām hunts our souls and casts the snare of truth so that our souls become ensnared. Al-Qāʾim—ʿAlī, the Remembrance of Islām—shall be the greatest hunter. The speakers in the *daʿwa* are those familiar with it—from whom the people of the *daʿwa* gain knowledge—and they are the Imām (upon whom be peace). Al-Qāʾim (upon whom be peace) shall migrate to Mārib and invite the whole world to Islām.

The marriage ceremony is performed at the time of Saturn's influence.

## Paradise and Its Gate

That the intellect constitutes the paradise of eternity is demonstrated through the following consideration: all human comfort, ease, and tranquility flow from the intellect. Therefore, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫the Messenger of Allāh stood nearest among all creation to the intellect. Thus it is proven: the Messenger of Allāh is the gate of Paradise.[^cite-2]

> *He who obeys the Messenger (Muhammad peace be upon him) has indeed obeyed Allāh; but he who turns away—We have not sent you as a guardian over them.*

In the same manner, every messenger has been the gate of Paradise in his time. By following his laws with understanding (taʾwīl—esoteric exegesis), a person attains Paradise. Yet he who accepts the law of the Messenger without knowledge of interpretation finds the gate of Paradise closed. Allāh declares:

> ⟪quran 39:71⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَسِيقَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ جَهَنَّمَ زُمَرًا ۖ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءُوهَا فُتِحَتْ أَبْوَبُهَا وَقَالَ لَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَآ أَلَمْ يَأْتِكُمْ رُسُلٌۭ مِّنكُمْ يَتْلُونَ عَلَيْكُمْ آيَتِ رَبِّكُمْ وَيُنذِرُونَكُمْ لِقَآءَ يَوْمِكُمْ هَذَا ۚ قَالُوا۟ بَلَىٰ وَلَكِنْ حَقَّتْ كَلِمَةُ ٱلْعَذَابِ عَلَى ٱلْكَافِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *And those who disbelieved will be driven to Hell in multitudes, until, when they arrive at it, its gates shall be opened. And its keepers will say to them: "Did there not come to you messengers from among yourselves, reciting to you the verses of your Lord and warning you of the meeting of this Day of yours?" They will say: "Yes indeed; but the word of punishment has been justified against the disbelievers."*

That Allāh states the gates of Paradise have been opened demonstrates that these gates were previously sealed. This signifies that the laws revealed through the Prophets (ʿalayhim al-salām) are bound by signs and parables, and the salvation of mankind depends upon their unveiling. It resembles a closed door that affords rest only when opened. The interpreters of these allusions have always been the Successors (al-awṣiyāʾ ʿalayhim al-salām).[^cite-3] When they illuminate the reality of these hints through taʾwīl, the door opens and the seeker finds tranquility. In the same way, the gates of Paradise shall be opened for all believers in each age through the Imam of the Time (Imām al-Zamān).

Every door requires a key, and none approaches the gate without it. The key to the gate of Paradise is the Testimony of Purity (Kalimah al-Ṭayyibah). For this reason, none has come to the Messenger of Allāh save by accepting the word of witness. The Messenger of Allāh (peace and blessings upon him) promised Paradise to those who accepted the Word with sincerity.

⟪ar-quote:مَنْ قاَلَ لاَ اِلہَ اِلاَّ اَللّٰہ خَالِصاً مُخْلِصاً دَخَلَ الْجَنَّۃَ⟫ (*Whoever recites the word of sincerity with a pure heart enters Paradise.*)

Thus it stands proven: the word of witness is the key to the gate of Paradise, and whoever obtains this key and reaches the door shall enter Paradise.

## The Formation of Astronomical Knowledge

Hazrat Idrīs (upon him be peace) was the first to discover the science of astrology (ʿilm al-nujūm), spending thirty years of his life perfecting it. The creation of this science constituted his miracle, which he imparted to his contemporaries. He lived in the time of seventy generations between Adam and Noah. Before his death, the Imam of his era—himself named Idrīs—appointed a successor to the Imamate; yet the people rejected him as Imām.

Idrīs posed a question to those summoned to him: "Is it possible for the land of Allāh to be deprived of His argument in the sight of Allāh?" All answered that it was not possible. He then said, "Discover who is the proof of Allāh upon this earth!" When they asked how they might do so, he replied: "I have created astronomy and taught it to you."[^cite-4]

Once they had acquired this knowledge, he commanded them to divide the earth into four regions and observe in which parts the proof of Allāh dwelt. They complied and found that in all four divisions, the proof of Allāh was present. He then instructed them: "Now divide the region in which you yourselves are located, and where the proof of Allāh dwells, into four further parts, and determine in which of these four the proof of Allāh resides." Again they did so, and again they found him in the fourth quarter. He continued this process of subdivision repeatedly, until at last he declared: "We have revealed to you the proof of Allāh, whom Allāh has appointed as a witness against us."

*(Lessons from Anwar al-Ṭāʾifa, attributed to Muḥammad ibn Ṭāhir al-Sayyid)*

## The Reality of Knowledge

Knowledge is a point from which ignorance (jahl) emanates in excess. There are two types of knowledge: first, that which proceeds from unity toward multiplicity—that is, from the source to the emanation (al-manbaʿ), the method of revelation and manifestation. The second moves from multiplicity toward unity, or from emanation to source, which is the method of inner progression (sulūk al-bāṭin). The first method is exalted and sublime; it concerns understanding what has been revealed regarding the soul and body, the physical realm, the heavens and elements, and generation. The second method is subtle and inward: we endeavor to know what lies beneath the point of existence and what dwells within the realm of mysteries and hidden truths. For example: to apprehend the mysteries of the Heavenly Books, or to grasp the secrets of our Prophet (peace and blessings upon him), which were disclosed on the Night of the Ascent (Laylat al-Miʿrāj).[^cite-5]

*I have been granted knowledge of the first and the last.* Allāh the Exalted has made me aware of the reality of all things, for I am the ultimate point of the boundary-post! Ask me concerning all that dwells beneath the Throne, for this is the point known to people as *Ibādān*—the point which has no place before it. It is the point toward which the center-point of existence is oriented, and toward which the lines of its circle converge and encircle.

Every circle possesses two corresponding points: one is the beginning (al-ibtidāʾ) and the other the end (al-intihāʾ); thus the circle achieves its circular form. Hence Allāh says: "As you began, so shall you return." The Names of Allāh subsist in these two senses—the First and the Last, the Manifest (ẓāhir) and the Hidden (bāṭin)—and eternity and perpetuity refer to these two points. The arc likewise moves in this direction, for it signifies the discontinuity in the realm of existence, creating distance between the contingent (al-mumkin) and the necessary (al-wājib), between the bounded and the absolute. This letter indicates the station of proximity to the Divine Names. For this reason, creation and return, descent and ascension, power and capacity are unified with reality despite their apparent duality. There exists no place more elevated than this save the station of Unicity (Aḥadiyyah—⟪ar:'أو ادنی'⟫). This is the place of the annihilation of intellect and the effacement of forms. From this point emanates the point of prophecy and luminosity. Both of these stations belong exclusively to Muḥammad and ʿAlī (upon them be peace), for prophethood and the Wilāyah of the All-Compelling belong to them alone.[^cite-6]

And he who arrives at the third level embraces poverty and perishes, knowing that he knows nothing. The sharing of knowledge with Allāh constitutes ignorance before Him.

## Illusion versus Delusion

**Illusions**

An illusion, in its simplest definition, is a misleading perception. Illusions typically involve sight (most commonly), hearing, taste, touch, or smell—perceiving or misinterpreting stimuli that are genuinely present. Unlike hallucinations, illusions deal with actual sensory input that is misread or poorly interpreted. They are the stock-in-trade of magic performances and confidence schemes alike. A practical example: you hang your spare uniform over the doorframe to air; returning home late, you glimpse a shadow in the corridor and perceive an intruder, your heart racing—only to discover the "intruder" is your hanging garment. Another instance occurs when one hears one's name called while the radio plays. Illusions can affect anyone; they do not necessarily indicate mental illness unless they become persistent and disruptive to daily functioning.

**Delusions**

Delusions are fixed, deeply held beliefs—whether false or fanciful—maintained despite contradictory evidence or information. Delusional individuals cannot reliably distinguish reality from fiction.

Delusions are classified as either bizarre or non-bizarre. A bizarre delusion is implausible and highly unusual—for instance, that extraterrestrials have removed all one's organs and replaced them with another's. A non-bizarre delusion contains false content yet remains theoretically possible, such as constant police surveillance.

## Genuine and Counterfeit Currency

Brothers and Sisters of the Jamāʿah—the Lord of the Worlds has bestowed upon us countless blessings. Some are inherent within ourselves; others surround us in abundance beyond enumeration. Therefore, gratitude toward Allāh is imperative at every moment and in every circumstance. Gratitude not only preserves blessings but increases them—as the Qur'ān states: *"If you are grateful, I will surely increase you."* Money is among His most precious gifts, for it not only travels but enables the traveler. When confined to pockets or vaults, wealth loses its utility; similarly, a person confined to his home neither increases his wealth nor his intellectual capacity nor his experience of the world.

When money and wealth circulate—that is, when employed productively in commerce—their value and quantity multiply. Having journeyed for the Urs Sharīf, each of you surely carries something for expenses; I do not presume to examine your purses, yet it is certain that every pocket contains currency. Undoubtedly, this external wealth serves our worldly affairs; similarly, the inner knowledge (ʿilm bāṭinī) is essential and vital for our journey beyond death. Whether worldly wealth or spiritual wealth in the inner sciences, money provides sustenance for our voyage and ensures safe passage, elevating both our worldly and spiritual stations.

The point I wish to convey concerns the currency we carry during our journey—mostly paper notes or travelers' cheques. What is currency? It is paper—some pieces small, some large. Its value rests not upon the weight or quantity of paper but upon the imprint. Not every paper constitutes currency; it must bear the governor's signature and the watermark approved by the government. Genuine currency circulates everywhere with full recognition. Brothers and Sisters of the Jamāʿah, observe and remember this lesson: every human in earthly form is like a paper note. Increasing one's value lies within each person's hands; with effort, one may become a thousand-rupee note, a ten-thousand-rupee note, or even a one-lakh-rupee note. Hazrat ʿAlī (may Allāh honor him) declared: *"A human attains the rank which he prepares for himself; therefore strive for yourselves toward higher stations."* Our worth is determined not only by character, morals, and conduct, but also by bearing the Sulaymānī certification—the watermark of his love and devotion. Just as counterfeit currency is rejected and consigned to destruction, so too our deeds should not be rejected as spurious. Genuine currency continues its circulation and completes its journey; counterfeit notes head toward a counterfeit destination.

Praise be to Allāh, all our brothers and sisters gathered here for the Urs Sharīf—young and old alike—bear the Sulaymānī insignia imprinted upon their hearts. In the presence of Humāmī and before Allāh, their worth and value shall, God willing, be such that their wealth increases continually and their circumstances improve. Their journey and their wealth shall conclude in success and triumph, becoming a shield against Hell and the scorching fire—Amīn.
