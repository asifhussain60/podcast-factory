## Quranic Miracles

Is the Qur'an a word, or is it a scripture? Is it spoken text, or written text? When we speak of words, we recognize three essential constituents: the speaker, the speech itself, and the manner of its articulation. Each deserves scrutiny, for their interdependence shapes the whole.

### The Speaker

Consider a financial advisor. If an eight-year-old child offers you investment counsel, you will dismiss it. Yet the same advice, delivered by an established manager, commands your attention. Why? Because the speaker's authority—his credentials, his proven competence—lends weight to his words. A weak speaker renders even sound doctrine weightless. [^cite-1]

### The Speech

Imagine attending a sermon delivered by a stranger. You stand outside the auditorium, unable to enter, yet his words carry through. You have not seen the speaker. Perhaps he is a seventeen-year-old in casual dress, an upside-down cap upon his head. Yet his sermon moves you profoundly. You infer, from his eloquence alone, that he must be a seasoned scholar of many years. Your judgment rests entirely upon *the word itself*—its force, its clarity, its depth. Thus does speech itself become a measure of authority.

### The Manner of Expression

Three elements remain: *who speaks, what they say, and how they say it.* Style carries its own power. Suppose two teachers urge you toward prayer. One speaks harshly: "What shamelessness! Are you Muslim or infidel? How dare you neglect the prayer!" The other, with warmth: "Dear friend, the prayer time has come. I know you are occupied, so I merely remind you." Both speak truth. Both are learned. Yet their manner of expression determines whether the heart opens or closes. A sermon rich in content yet delivered in monotone will exhaust an audience; the fault lies not in what is said, but in how it is voiced.

---

### The Miracle of the Qur'an: A Threefold Radiance

Why does the Qur'an constitute a miracle? For three reasons: the Speaker, the Speech, and the Style.

The Speaker is Allah—All-Mighty. The Speech is the Qur'an itself, perfect in every letter, immovable, exact. And the Manner of Expression is incomparable in its beauty and force.

Yet here lies a crucial distinction. Believers recognize the Speaker and affirm the Speech; disbelievers do not. Therefore, for the believer, both the Speaker and the Speech manifest miraculous power. For the disbeliever, no miracle resides in these, for he grants them no credence. But what even the disbeliever cannot deny is the *beauty and charm of the Qur'an's diction itself*—its luminous style, its inimitable eloquence. Throughout history, enemies of Islam heard these verses and, unable to dismiss them as mere poetry or sorcery, attributed them instead to the influence of a powerful man or a skilled magician. Yet never—*never*—has any critic, however hostile, called the Qur'an uninspired or plain. Its style alone compels attention. [^cite-2]

Herein lies a paradox: when we translate the Qur'an into another tongue, we preserve something of its meaning, its purpose, its sense. But what we lose entirely—one hundred percent—is its *style*. For the style dwells in Arabic itself, in the particular genius of that language. Even an Arab speaker, hearing another Arab recite these verses, perceives that no human tongue could have woven them thus. The true splendor of Allah's Word—its majesty, its inimitable grace—cannot cross the threshold of translation. Some meaning may be carried over; the living resonance, the transformative power of the original utterance, cannot. 

Consider the distance between ourselves and Allah: it is infinite. So, too, is the distance between our translations and His Word. Translation is the word of His creation; the Qur'an is the Word of the Creator. Content bleeds away in translation; the whole luminous style evaporates. [^cite-3]

### The Question Posed

How do modern voices present the Qur'an's miracle? They speak of scientific principles embedded in its verses. They catalog future predictions. They invoke numerological theories. Yet we must ask: Do we believe *every* verse of the Qur'an is a miracle, or only *some*? Do we hold that its miraculous character was evident from the moment of revelation, or did it crystallize only as centuries passed?

Consider the facts. How many verses contain predictions? A handful. How many expound scientific principles? Few indeed. How many such matters were known to the Arabs of Muhammad's time? Almost none. Yet what overwhelmed them when the Messenger opened his mouth? What left them speechless, stammering, unable to utter a reply? It was not scientific foreknowledge. It was something else entirely: the *beauty of the utterance itself*.

Two historical accounts illuminate this truth.

### The Story of Tufayl ibn 'Umar al-Dawsī

The disbelievers of Mecca spread a rumor: the Messenger of Allah possessed a sorcerer's tongue. Whoever heard him would fall under his spell. They feared especially that Tufayl, a great leader of his tribe, might be ensared. When he arrived in Mecca, they warned him urgently: "Guard yourself! Do not listen to him, or his words will bewitch you." They took precautions, stuffing cotton into their ears lest the Messenger's voice reach them even by accident.

Yet while circumambulating the Ka'ba, Tufayl glimpsed a handsome man at prayer, reciting aloud. "Who is this?" he asked.

"The sorcerer we warned you of," came the reply.

But Tufayl thought to himself: I am a leader of my people, not a fool easily swayed. I will listen to his words with discernment. If they contain truth, I shall accept them; if falsehood, I shall reject them. Thus emboldened, he removed the cotton from his ears and sat near the Messenger of Allah. [^cite-4]

When the prayer ended, Tufayl approached and asked: ⟪ar-quote:یا محمد! ما عندک؟⟫ (O Muhammad, what do you offer?)

The Messenger replied with words that, even to those unfamiliar with Arabic, convey an incandescent beauty:

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّ الحَمدَ لِی اللہَ أحمَدَہُ وَ أستَعِینَہُ وَ أُومِنُ بِہِ وَ أتَوَکَّلُ عَلَیہِ ۔ مَن یَّھدِہِ اللہُ فَلاَ مُضِلَّ لَہ وَ مَن یَّضِلَّ لَہُ فَلاَ ھَادِیَ لَہ⟫

(Truly, praise belongs to Allah. I praise Him, seek His aid, believe in Him, and place my trust in Him. Whomsoever Allah guides cannot be led astray; whomsoever He leaves astray shall find no guide.)

The words struck Tufayl like lightning. He trembled and cried out spontaneously:

⟪ar-quote:أُعِد أُعِد! فقد بلغنا قاموس البحر⟫

(Repeat! Repeat! You have touched the deepest fathom of the ocean with a single phrase!)

When the Messenger spoke these words again, tears streamed down Tufayl's face. "O Muhammad," he declared, "teach me the *Kalimah* (the testimony of faith). I believe in you and in your God."

What had moved him? Not a scientific marvel. Not a prediction of distant events. It was the *style*—the overwhelming, unmistakable beauty of the utterance. [^cite-5]

### The Story of 'Utbi ibn Rabī'a

'Utbi ibn Rabī'a stood among the greatest orators of Arabia. His eloquence was legendary; he could dismantle an opponent's position with words alone. The Quraysh, desperate to counter the Messenger's growing influence, came to 'Utbi: "Go to Muhammad. Match him in speech. Show the people that his words are mere rhetoric, no different from any skilled speaker's."

Why did they choose 'Utbi? Because they perceived that the Messenger's power lay in *speech and style*. To counter him, they sent not a warrior but a master of language.

'Utbi approached the Messenger and assailed him: "O Muhammad! What is the end of 'Abd Allah? Silence! Are you better than 'Abd al-Muttalib? Silence! Are you superior to your father? Your father? Your grandfather?" (His rhetoric was calculated not to elicit response but to provoke and confuse.)

Then 'Utbi leveled graver accusations: ⟪ar-quote:فرقت دیننا، و شطنتا آلھتنا، و عبت دیننا، و فرقت بیننا⟫ (You have torn asunder our ancestral faith, mocked our gods, disparaged our way, and divided brother from brother.)

He then offered inducements, attempting to reduce the Messenger's mission to the pursuit of worldly gain:

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّما أِن کُنتَ تُرِیدُ بِہِ مُلکاً، مَلَّکناَ عَلی رُؤُسِنَا⟫ (If you seek kingship, we shall crown you.)

⟪ar-quote:أِن کُنتَ تُرِیدُ بِہِ الشَّرَف، لا نَقطَعُ أَمراً دُونَک⟫ (If you seek honor, we shall grant it without measure.)

⟪ar-quote:أِن کُنتَ تُرِیدُ بِہِ البآءَ، جَمَعنَا لَکَ نِساَءَ قُرَیشً فَخطَ أَیُّ نِساَءَ قُرَیشً فَنُزَوِّجْکَ عَشَرا⟫ (If you seek beauty, we shall gather for you the most beautiful women of Quraysh—choose any ten, and we shall wed them to you.)

⟪ar-quote:أِن کُنتَ تُرِیدُ بِہِ المال، جمعنا لک أَموَالَناَ حَتّٰی تَکُونُ أَغنٰی قُرِیشً مالا⟫ (If you seek wealth, we shall amass for you riches until you become the richest of Quraysh.)

'Utbi's strategy was calculated: discredit the Messenger's character, render him contemptible, and his words lose their force. A fallen speaker speaks to empty air.

When 'Utbi finished, the Messenger asked: ⟪ar-quote:أَفَرغْتَا ابا الولید؟⟫ (O Abu al-Walid, have you finished?) 'Utbi nodded. The Messenger replied: "Now listen," and he began to recite the Qur'an:

⟪ar-quote:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم

حمٓ۔ تَنزِيلٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلرَّحْمَنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ۔⟫

(In the name of Allah, the Merciful, the Compassionate. Hā, Mīm. This is a revelation from the All-Merciful, the All-Compassionate.)

With the very opening, the Qur'an addresses 'Utbi's implicit claim: *These are my words, and I speak them to gain your favor.* No. The Qur'an declares: *This is a revelation from Allah. You are not negotiating with the Messenger; you face Allah Himself.* [^cite-6]

The Messenger continued:

⟪ar-quote:‏كِتَبٌۭ فُصِّلَتْ آيَتُهُۥ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّۭا لِّقَوْمٍۢ يَعْلَمُونَ۔⟫

(A book whose verses are expounded, a Qur'an in Arabic for a people of understanding.)

⟪ar-quote:‏بَشِيرًۭا وَنَذِيرًۭا فَأَعْرَضَ أَكْثَرُهُمْ فَهُمْ لَا يَسْمَعُونَ۔⟫

(A herald of good tidings and a warner—yet most turn away and do not listen.)

⟪ar-quote:وَقَالُوا۟ قُلُوبُنَا فِىٓ أَكِنَّةٍۢ مِّمَّا تَدْعُونَآ إِلَيْهِ وَفِىٓ آذَانِنَا وَقْرٌۭ وَمِنۢ بَيْنِنَا وَبَيْنِكَ حِجَابٌۭ فَٱعْمَلْ إِنَّنَا عَمِلُونَ۔ ‏

قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أَنَا۠ بَشَرٌۭ مِّثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ أَنَّمَآ إِلَهُكُمْ إِلَهٌۭ وَحِدٌۭ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُشْرِكِينَ۔

‏ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَفِرُونَ۔ ‏إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ لَهُمْ أَجْرٌ غَيْرُ مَمْنُونٍۢ۔

قُلْ أَئِنَّكُمْ لَتَكْفُرُونَ بِٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ فِى يَوْمَيْنِ وَتَجْعَلُونَ لَهُۥٓ أَندَادًۭا ۚ ذَلِكَ رَبُّ ٱلْعَلَمِينَ۔

‏وَجَعَلَ فِيهَا رَوَسِىَ مِن فَوْقِهَا وَبَرَكَ فِيهَا وَقَدَّرَ فِيهَآ أَقْوَتَهَا فِىٓ أَرْبَعَةِ أَيَّامٍۢ سَوَآءًۭ لِّلسَّآئِلِينَ۔

ثُمَّ ٱسْتَوَىٰٓ إِلَى ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَهِىَ دُخَانٌۭ فَقَالَ لَهَا وَلِلْأَرْضِ ٱئْتِيَا طَوْعًا أَوْ كَرْهًۭا قَالَتَآ أَتَيْنَا طَآئِعِينَ۔

‏فَقَضَىٰهُنَّ سَبْعَ سَمَوَاتٍۢ فِى يَوْمَيْنِ وَأَوْحَىٰ فِى كُلِّ سَمَآءٍ أَمْرَهَا ۚ وَزَيَّنَّا ٱلسَّمَآءَ ٱلدُّنْيَا بِمَصَبِيحَ وَحِفْظًۭا ۚ ذَلِكَ تَقْدِيرُ ٱلْعَزِيزِ ٱلْعَلِيمِ۔

أَعْرَضُوا۟ فَقُلْ أَنذَرْتُكُمْ صَعِقَةًۭ مِّثْلَ صَعِقَةِ عَادٍۢ وَثَمُودَ⟫

As the verses unfolded, the color drained from 'Utbi's face. His expression shifted. Tears began to fall. Here stood the very enemy of Islam, the man most hostile to the Messenger's mission—and he wept. As the recitation grew more intense, reaching its climax, the Messenger spoke the verse:

⟪ar-quote:أَعْرَضُوا۟ فَقُلْ أَنذَرْتُكُمْ صَعِقَةًۭ مِّثْلَ صَعِقَةِ عَادٍۢ وَثَمُودَ⟫

(They turn away. Say: I warn you of a thunderbolt like the one that struck 'Ād and Thamūd.)

At this moment, 'Utbi leaped up in sudden terror. He placed both his hands over the Messenger's mouth and cried: "By your father! By your mother! I beg you, speak no more. Do not utter another word!"

He rose, trembling, and departed the gathering. When he returned to the assembly of Quraysh, they saw at once that he was transformed. "This is not the 'Utbi who left us," they murmured.

He addressed them: "O assembly of Quraysh! I am your leader, not a madman. I have deliberated, and I tell you now: accept my counsel, at least this once. If you will not heed me for the rest of your lives, hear me now: *say nothing more to this man. He has uttered words that have overturned the very foundations of my being.* It was neither poetry nor magic nor mere eloquence. He warned us of the thunderbolts that destroyed 'Ād and Thamūd. You know that Muhammad has never lied to you. And I fear—I fear deeply—that you too shall be consumed by divine fire!" [^cite-7]

## The Way of Life in Islam

Is life merely a transaction—earn, accumulate, build, adorn oneself, then wrap the body in a shroud and vanish into the earth, all memory erased? Was this infinite universe, radiant with the brightness of sun and moon, adorned with stars and galaxies, blessed with changing seasons and infinite hues, blessed with waves and cloud-drifts and cascading waters and verdant valleys—was it all created for the sake of wealth-gathering, deception, betrayal, and dishonor?

What, then, is the aim of merely earning, earning, earning?

There was a person consumed by this hunger. "It is mine," he insisted. "It is mine." He would not release it. And then his eyes closed. Everything vanished. [^cite-8]

Set a purpose for your life. Every traveler chooses a destination before embarking. If you wish to go to Lahore, you select the appropriate flight; you do not board any aircraft at random. Life itself is a journey, and the most critical element of any journey is the *destination*. Where do you wish to arrive? Along what path? Toward what end?

Every game has a terminus that defines it. What is the terminus of this game called life? Is it merely to earn and expire?

Ask the One who created you: Why did you create me? Yet most wander in bewilderment, ignorant of their Maker, ignorant of the correct path, knowing neither where they journey nor what awaits them beyond death.

These are the great questions. Why does the phantom of wealth haunt us the moment we wake? Life passes. Hair whitens. Age arrives. Yet the same relentless melody plays on: *accumulate, accumulate, accumulate.* There is only one refrain: *ascend higher*.

Look at the ruins of their mansions! Behold their desolate graves! Witness the extinction of entire generations! Each scorched stone, each empty tomb cries out a truth: *This world is not a destination; it is a way-station.* Wealth, houses, honor—these are not the destination but the path. The destination is *other*.

Who is that Other? Is it Allah (God)?

What is He like? If there were one like Him, I would tell you. But there is none like Him. None is worthy of worship save He alone. [^cite-9]

### The Divine Call

⟪ar-quote:وأذا سألت فسأل اللہ⟫ When you ask, ask of Allah.

⟪ar-quote:وأذا استعنت فستعن باللہ⟫ When you seek aid, seek it from Allah.

Do not invoke the creatures of Allah. Do not call upon the servants of God. Call upon Him who created all, who depends on none, to whom all depend. You must go to listen to Him; He need not come to listen to you. You must speak; His word requires no utterance. You need not journey to find Him; He is nearer than your jugular vein, yet free from all spatial constraint. [^cite-10]

⟪ar-quote:لا یحویہ مکان، ولا یشتمل علیہ الزمان⟫ No place contains Him; no moment encompasses Him.

He is beyond the bounds of east and west, north and south. He is free from time itself.

He is free from all need.

Where is He? ⟪ar-quote:فَأَيْنَمَا تُوَلُّوا۟ فَثَمَّ وَجْهُ ٱللَّهِ⟫ (Wherever you turn, there is the face of Allah.)

He is nearer than nearness itself. The eye cannot perceive Him; imagination cannot encompass Him. Yet He is closer to you than your own lifeblood. [^cite-11]

No one is His father; no one is His son. He is Al-Wahid (the Unique One), As-Samad (He upon whom all depend and who depends upon none). The treasuries of the heavens and the earth are His. All power resides in His grasp. On the Day of Reckoning, the heavens and earth shall be rolled up in His right hand as easily as a scroll. All dominion is His.

He is free from hunger; He nourishes all. Free from sleep; He sustains all creation. Free from weariness; yet He brings comfort to all. Free from need; yet He gives to all. Free from form; yet He bestows form upon all. Free from color; yet He gives color to all. Free from deficiency; yet He fulfills all needs. Free from partnership; all beings are equal before Him. [^cite-12]

⟪ar-quote:سَوَآءٌۭ مِّنكُم مَّنْ أَسَرَّ ٱلْقَوْلَ وَمَن جَهَرَ بِهِۦ وَمَنْ هُوَ مُسْتَخْفٍۭ بِٱلَّيْلِ وَسَارِبٌۢ بِٱلنَّهَارِ⟫

(Alike unto Him are those who conceal their words and those who speak openly, and those who hide in the night and those who walk openly in the day.)

He knows what you whisper in secret and what you proclaim aloud. [^cite-13]

⟪ar-quote:يَعْلَمُ سِرَّكُمْ وَجَهْرَكُمْ⟫ He knows your secrets and your declarations.

### The Reckoning

Allah will give death to this universe. The heavens shall perish. The stars shall fall. The mountains shall crumble. The animals, the beasts, the seas—all shall pass away. The sky shall split asunder. The stars shall scatter. The seas shall surge with flame.

Then comes the Resurrection. All souls shall rise from their graves. On that Day, each person shall know what he has done. Every living being shall taste death. Everything that lacks life shall perish. All shall come to an end.

But Allah is free from death. Allah is free from all defect. Make Allah your destination. When you bow before Allah, recognize Him as your Lord.

Praise be to Allah, Lord of all the worlds. Exalted is my Lord, the Most Great. Exalted is my Lord, the Most High. To You alone belongs all praise, my Lord. I seek refuge in the Lord of the daybreak. I seek refuge in the Lord of humankind.

Your Lord is Allah, who created the heavens and the earth in six days, then established Himself upon the Throne. He draws the night over the day, and the day over the night. The sun, the moon, the stars—all are made subservient to His command. Creation and command belong to Him alone. Blessed be Allah, Lord of all the worlds. [^cite-14]

God has placed no restriction upon you. The only demand is that you *find* God. Seek Allah. Make Allah your destination.

### The Covenant of Servanthood

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم، خلقتک لعبادتی فلا تَلعَب⟫ O son of Adam! I have created you for My worship. Do not fritter yourself away.

Do not squander yourself in wine, in gambling, in lies, in oppression, in adultery. Be My servant. Therefore, whether you are in America or Mecca, if you remain My rebel, even Mecca becomes the fire of hellfire. If you remain My obedient servant, even America becomes Mecca itself.

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم، خلقتک لعبادتی فلا تَلعَب⟫ O son of Adam! I have created you for Myself. Be Mine. What have you done? Still, be Mine.

⟪ar-quote:وتکفلت برزقک فلا تطعب⟫ I have taken upon Myself to sustain you. Do not grow weary.

Cast out from your heart the thought: "If I do not lie, where shall sustenance come? If I do not cheat, how shall I prosper? If I do not sell forbidden things, how shall profit reach me?" This is a lie.

⟪ar-quote:من ارسَلَ الیک الغذا وَ انت جَنِینٌ فی البَطنِ الاُمِّ⟫ Who provided for you when you were a lifeless clot in your mother's womb?

When you emerged and lay upon your mother's breast, who provided the milk? [^cite-15]

⟪ar-quote:نُّسْقِيكُم مِّمَّا فِى بُطُونِهِۦ مِنۢ بَيْنِ فَرْثٍۢ وَدَمٍۢ لَّبَنًا خَالِصًۭا سَآئِغًۭا لِّلشَّرِبِينَ⟫

(We give you to drink from what is in their bellies, between ordure and blood: pure milk, palatable to the drinker.)

Who formed your eyes? Who created your tongue? Who shaped your two lips? Who gave you form and color and image while you lay unaware in your mother's womb?

The One who did not forget you in the womb, who did not forget you upon your mother's lap—why would He forget you in America, in London, in Lahore, in Karachi? The Lord of Pakistan is the Lord of India. The Lord of all lands is One. Why, then, do you say: "If I do not lie, how shall it work? If I do not sell forbidden things, how shall business prosper?" No!

⟪ar-quote:وتکفلت برزقک فلا تطعب⟫ I have taken your sustenance upon Myself. Do not grow weary!

To grow weary means to resort to unlawful means—lies, theft, gambling, forbidden trade. This is weariness of spirit. But I am watching you. The fish in the ocean's depths—I sustain them. The insects in the earth's darkness—I sustain them. Those who disbelieve in Me, who deny Me, who associate partners with Me—I still sustain them. I still adorn their tables. Shall I forget *you*?

I feed the poisonous snake. I feed the scorpion. I feed the filthy swine. Shall I forget you, My servant? [^cite-16]

O son of Adam! O My servant! Where do you run? Come to Me! Become Mine. I promise you: if you find Me, you find all things. If you lose Me, you lose all things.

Finding Allah is the destination of life. This is the journey's end. Everything else is the road. The destination is Allah. How can you erase Allah's existence from your life?

A small motor-car runs only if it is driven. A motorcycle moves only if it is ridden. This immense universe—the rising waves, the falling rain, the flashing lightning, the stars—all tell us: *There is Allah.* [^cite-17]

The sun shines with brilliance. This moon is the subject of your Allah; this sun is the subject of your Allah; these rains and stars are the subjects of your Allah. Darkness and light are slaves of your Lord. Human and jinn are slaves of your Lord. Gabriel and Michael are slaves of your Allah. Become Allah's. This is the declaration of *Lā ilāha illā Allah* (There is no god but Allah). This is the covenant of *Lā ilāha illā Allah*: "O Allah, may You be pleased with all that occurs." Then whatever comes to pass, if it is Your will, you accept it. If it is not Your will, you refuse it. If you must sacrifice your life, you do so freely. If you must leave your home, you do so gladly. If you must sacrifice your children, you do so willingly. [^cite-18]

My brothers! Decide today: Do you want Allah, or do you want this four-day spring that fades? Do you want Allah, or do you want the world—no matter how much you gather it, how much you adorn it, you cannot take it with you? Do you know anyone who has died? Have you seen whether his baggage accompanied him into the grave?

Everything astounds the caravan when the load is gone.

### What Is Islam?

What is Islam? *Providing food and gentle speech.* 

What is faith? *Patience and forbearance.*

Who is the best Muslim? *One who harms no one with hand or tongue.*

You cannot abandon prayer.

When two people meet, it is charity for one to greet the other with a smile. If two Muslims quarrel, their rewards are suspended until they reconcile.

Be a blessing to your family. Be a comfort to your household. In the marketplace, speak truth. Do not cheat.

When truth is spoken, the reward knows no bounds.

On the twelfth of Rabi al-Awwal, at 4:20 a.m. on Monday, April 22, 572 C.E., he came into the world. Allah says:

⟪ar-quote:الحمد لله الذی خلق السماوات والأرض وجعل الظلمات والنور⟫

Praise be to Allah, who created the heavens and the earth and made the darknesses and the light.[^cite-33]

Al-Azlumāt (darknesses) is plural; al-Anwār (lights) is singular. Oppression and darkness are innumerable; light is one. There is no chemistry of darkness, only the chemistry of light. There is no physics of darkness—physics pertains to light alone. When light is absent, that absence is called darkness. Darkness itself possesses no substance; it is the privation of light. All knowledge relates to light, nothing to darkness.

When darkness covers the eyes, we cannot see the way of this world. When it covers the hearts, we cannot see the path of the Hereafter. We perceive the light of worldly gain, so we plan for it, arrange for it. Yet we do not arrange any light for the heart's darkness, and thus man strays from the paths. Allah says:

⟪ar-quote:أم ترى إن اتخذوا من دون الله آلهة أنهم يضرونهم ولا ينفعونهم⟫

"Will you tell me about those who have taken gods besides Allah? Surely they profit them not and harm them not."

Their hearts have corroded from sin; because of this corrosion, the reality is no longer visible. Allah does not blind the sight, yet He blinds the hearts within the breasts. Their eyes are not blind; their hearts are blind. They have hearts yet are bereft of light.

Allah has given an example of those who go astray:

⟪ar-quote:أَوْ كَظُلُمَتٍۢ فِى بَحْرٍۢ لُّجِّىٍّۢ يَغْشَىٰهُ مَوْجٌۭ مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ مَوْجٌۭ مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ سَحَابٌۭ ۚ ظُلُمَتٌۢ بَعْضُهَا فَوْقَ بَعْضٍ إِذَآ أَخْرَجَ يَدَهُۥ لَمْ يَكَدْ يَرَىٰهَا ۗ وَمَن لَّمْ يَجْعَلِ ٱللَّهُ لَهُۥ نُورًۭا فَمَا لَهُۥ مِن نُّورٍ⟫

Or [the state of a disbeliever] is like the darknesses within an unfathomable sea covered by waves upon waves, covered by clouds—darknesses piled upon darknesses. When one puts forth his hand, he can scarcely see it. And to whomsoever Allah does not give light, no light has he.[^cite-34]

A beautiful example from Allah the Mighty. When all light is extinguished and the sun has set and darkness prevails everywhere, how shall one find the way? Allah has not illustrated the darkness of the heart with the gloom of a room, nor with the darkness of a street—for in both, light filters in or refracts. Allah illustrates the darkness of the heart with the darkness of the sea.

Imagine your boat in the deep sea, *fī baḥr al-lujjī* (in the fathomless deep). The water, too, has turned black with darkness. A wave rises and increases the darkness further. The night falls black. Yet the moon and stars still cast some light. Allah says: the moon and stars are hidden in the clouds. A boat lost in the sea—wave upon wave compounding the darkness—dark, black clouds obscuring moon and stars. How shall the boats find their way in such darkness? If you drive a highway without a single signboard, no exit sign, your GPS inoperative, how shall you find the way? If you cannot find the way, how shall you reach your destination? Similarly, when darkness overwhelms the hearts, how shall one find the path to Paradise?

⟪ar-quote:وَأَنَّ هَذَا صِرَطِى مُسْتَقِيمًۭا فَٱتَّبِعُوهُ ۖ وَلَا تَتَّبِعُوا۟ ٱلسُّبُلَ فَتَفَرَّقَ بِكُمْ عَن سَبِيلِهِۦ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ وَصَّىٰكُم بِهِۦ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ⟫

And verily this is My path, the Straight Path. Follow it, then, and follow not other ways, lest they scatter you from His way. Thus He hath charged you, that haply ye may be godfearing.[^cite-35]

But since the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) is a universal prophet sent to all humanity:

A Book have We sent down to thee to bring mankind out of the darknesses into the light.[^cite-53]

Your task is to bring all humanity out of darkness.[^cite-54]

Unless this light of the heart is kindled, man cannot bow before Allah or His Messenger. Therefore, it is incumbent upon us to strive to attain this light, one by one. Parents themselves must first receive light from these two sources, then prepare their children to receive it. The world can establish laws, rules, and regulations; it can craft policies. My family could compel me to prayer by force, yet they could not compel me to ablution. Islam will not come through coercion. On the contrary, force drives the human being away from religion. And if someone attempts to prevent you from entering, will you stop?

Thus this light is granted by Allah through personal effort. Just as Allah provides sustenance—He is the Lord—yet He gives according to labour, so nothing is achieved without effort. Even when the infant cries hard, the mother nurses. When this light has descended into the heart, Allah has presented a most beautiful verse:

## The Difference between *Khalīl* and *Ḥabīb*

There is a *Khalīl*; there is a *Ḥabīb*. You sow, the crop grows. You harvest, and the next crop emerges. There is a limit to friendship, an end to it; but there is no limit to love, no boundary. The *Ḥabīb*'s first station is *Khalīl*. The Throne trembled, the Chair bowed, Gabriel and Michael and Isrāfīl knelt. From the smallest creature to the lion, from the drop to the sea, from the pebble to the mountain—all creation was subjugated and His rule established over all.

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّ هَذَا ٱلْقُرْآنَ يَهْدِى لِلَّتِى هِىَ أَقْوَمُ⟫ — wherever you direct your gaze, there is Allah. Wherever you look, Allah is present.

Yet we have forgotten God. He called to us; we did not answer. He explained His signs; we closed our ears. He has set forth innumerable parables; we have drawn veils over our eyes. Consider the Moti Mahal—the Pearl Palace—that Noor Jahān built in her time. This was no mere Sheesh Mahal (Hall of Mirrors), but a structure adorned with pearls. When the Sikhs conquered it, they extracted the pearls. The British replaced them with glass. Mughāl emperors had wrought the roof in pearl, erected pillars of gold. Yet centuries later, a poet beheld those ruins and wept:

*There stands a broken house at the edge of the narrator's tale, where day and night merge in tourism's veil. They say this resting place belonged to Noor Jahān.*

### God's Absolute Transcendence and Unity

Our gravest transgression is ignorance of God. Allah is the One free from decay and deterioration. He is the God who neither slumbers nor sleeps, who has taken neither wife nor child.

⟪ar-quote:لم یتَّخِذ صاحبا ولا ولدَا⟫

His power, dominion, and property never diminish. He has no beginning and no end.

⟪ar-quote:ھو الاول و الاخر و الظاھر و الباطن⟫

*He is the First and the Last, the Manifest and the Hidden.*

Allah is the Eternal; He transcends both space and time.

⟪ar-quote:لا یحوِیہِ مکانٌ ولا یَشتَمِلُوہُ علیہ زمانٌ⟫

*No place encompasses Him, no time contains Him.*

My God says: "Call upon Me, invoke none but Me." Has He ever said He grew weary? Did He ever claim to slumber? Has Allah ever complained that the work has grown too great, that this vast universe exceeds His care, and therefore we should seek another's aid? Has He ever said His treasures run low, that we should seek assistance elsewhere?[^cite-27]

⟪ar-quote:کفا باللہ رقیبا؛ کفا باللہ حفیظا؛ کفا باللہ شھیدا؛ کفا باللہ ولیا؛ کفا باللہ علیما؛ کفا بربِکَ ھادیاً و نصیرا؛ الیس اللہ بکافٍ عبدا؟ فسیکفیکھُمُ اللہ و ھو السمیع العلیم⟫

*Sufficient is Allah as guardian; sufficient is Allah as protector; sufficient is Allah as witness; sufficient is Allah as friend; sufficient is Allah as knower. Sufficient is your Lord as guide and helper. Is not Allah sufficient for His servant?*

The entire universe has been reduced to insignificance. All things have been nullified.

### The First Crime: Forgetting God

The first crime is not knowing God, not truly knowing Him. For this ignorance, Allah's anger is kindled against grave transgressions. Consider the vast systems of usury that have been erected—declarations of war against God Himself.

⟪ar-quote:فازنو بحرب من اللہ و رسولہ⟫

*O you who believe, either abandon usury, or be warned of war against Allah and His Messenger.*

When you have declared war upon Allah and His Messenger, where shall refuge be found? Where shall you seek shelter? There exists one place alone of safety and security. Yet we have waged war precisely against that haven.

Know God. Befriend Him. Become His intimate. *Tavalīnī, tawallak!* (*Take me as your friend, entrust yourself to me!*) *Asafīnī, asāfik!* (*Confide in me, and I shall grant you your desire!*) Make Him your friend once. He will bestow upon you the right of true friendship. Deal with Him justly, and He shall render to you your due.

The Throne itself trembles at the cry of the oppressed. The angels scream in protest. It was for the oppressed that God said: "I am the companion of the oppressed until they obtain justice." To oppress one's parents is to declare war upon Allah. To know God is to befriend Him.

### The Second Crime: Abandoning the Prophet

The second crime is our failure to recognize Muḥammad Muṣṭafā (upon whom be peace and blessings). Can a nation whose leader is Muḥammad (upon whom be peace) follow leaders more blind than themselves? Our guide is Muḥammad. He alone is the beloved of the Creator. The Maker loves what He has created; He swore by this:

"O Abū Sufyān! I have brought you the miracles of both this world and the next (*Ja'atkum bi-karāmat al-dunyā wa-l-ākhira*)! Yet we have abandoned the feet of the Messenger of Allah and run after the blind. We have made Martin Luther King, Mother Teresa, and Shahrukh Khān our guides, dressing according to their prescribed manner, following their words.

He abandoned His Messenger.

Look at His companion—the one whom Allah Himself summoned to His Throne: *Subḥāna alladhī asrā bi-ʿabdihi* (*Glory be to Him who carried His servant by night*). Behold his journey! He mounted, and it became as nothing. See his destination: he stood before ʿĪsā (peace be upon him), messenger of Allah.

Witness his Prophethood: *Tabāraka alladhī nazzala al-furqān ʿalā ʿabdihi, liyakūn li-l-ʿālamīn nadhīrā* (*Blessed is He who sent down the Qur'ān to His servant that he might be a warner to the worlds*).

Contemplate the piercing vision that beheld the night of the Ascension (*al-Isrāʾ wa-l-Miʿrāj*)—yet we deny it. Those privileged to speak with Allah before His Throne proclaim: "Falsehood is ruin; truth is salvation." Yet we say: "If we do not lie, how shall we profit? If we do not cheat, how shall we grow rich?" Whom have we denied? Against whom have we rebelled? Who came to deliver us from slavery?

For whom was this entire universe created? *Lammā khalaqt al-aflāk* (*Had you not come, I would not have created the spheres*). We have abandoned that Prophet for whom the cosmos itself was fashioned.

He said: "Adorn my religion with modesty and generosity." These two adornments—*al-ḥayāʾ wa-l-jūd*—comprise the very essence of his faith. Where is modesty today? Where is generosity? Where is honour? These lights in which we walk have become instruments of indecency made fashionable. The clothing of girls grows ever tighter.

The wealthy, once bearers of generosity, have become tyrants. Is this truly the illumination by which we should proceed? We need not such lights; we must kindle others. Do we desire a culture in which the humiliation of parents is deemed "cool"? We yearn for a life of honour and service to those who bore us. We must learn. We must labour.

Where have the stone-pelleted peoples gone (*al-ḥijāra*—those destroyed by stones)? Where are the builders of ancient civilizations? Whither went Thamūd and Pharaoh? We do not desire the customary life of nations; we desire the life of Muḥammad and ʿAlī.

If you are loyal to Muḥammad, then we are yours.

## The Grievous Loss (*Al-Niqṣān al-ʿAẓīm*)

⟪ar-quote:قُلْ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ قَدْ جَآءَكُمُ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَنِ ٱهْتَدَىٰ فَإِنَّمَا يَهْتَدِى لِنَفْسِهِۦ ۖ وَمَن ضَلَّ فَإِنَّمَا يَضِلُّ عَلَيْهَا ۖ وَمَآ أَنَا۠ عَلَيْكُم بِوَكِيلٍۢ⟫

*Say: O mankind, the truth has come to you from your Lord. Whosoever is guided is guided only for his own soul, and whosoever goes astray, it is to his own detriment, and I am not a guardian over you.*

### The Creator and the Created

All humans throughout the world—Allah is the Creator of each, male and female alike. Allah is the Giver to all of them, and Allah shall make them stand before Him in account. Every word we possess is borrowed from our Creator; we cannot praise Him through His creation. It is impossible to do so. When I say "eternal," the term itself refers to a prolonged duration, yet Allah is the Creator of time itself. He is free from beginning and end, transcendent above limitation.

⟪ar-quote:قدیم بلا ابتدا دائمٌ بلا انتھا⟫

*Ancient without beginning, Eternal without end.*

Our words cannot adequately describe the eternity and perpetuity of this God. Allah is Creator of time, so time cannot contain Him. If Allah built the house, He cannot be confined within it. The creature and the Creator are utterly distinct. Allah alone creates all things—peoples and high and low, north and south, east and west, throne and chair. He Himself transcends containment within them.

The only words Allah gave us to recognize the Creator are these: *Anā* (*I am*). *Allāh* (*God*). For we required explanation, and had Allah brought forth a word we did not understand, how would we have known Him? That is why Allah revealed His word to us with clarity. He clothed His essence in our language and sent forth His message. Then He introduced Himself: *Anā Allāh* (*I am God*).

Who is God? *Lā ilāha illā Huwa* (*There is no god but He*). If there is none like Him, how shall you know who He is? The parable makes it comprehensible.

Therefore you must believe in the unseen—we believe in God without seeing Him. We cannot fully comprehend this universe. The universe remains distant; if we cannot even understand our own existence, how could we grasp the infinite? If we do not understand ourselves, how shall we know the Eternal? Thus, place trust in the words of the Prophet when he declares what Allah is: *Lā ilāha illā-Llāh* (*There is no god but Allah*).

How is He? *Al-Ḥayy* (*The Living*)—He is alive and requires neither bread nor water. *Al-Qayyūm* (*The Self-Sustaining*)—so self-subsistent that He needs not the earth on which to stand, requires no chair on which to sit, needs no house for dwelling. How extraordinary! What is the nature of the Eternal? It has neither beginning nor end. Pure from first to last, it is both placeless and timeless. He says: "I require no place because I am placeless." What is a place? It possesses six directions—north, south, east, west, above, below. That which contains these six qualities is a place. God says: "I am neither east nor west, neither north nor south, neither in heaven nor on earth."

*Aynamā talū fa-thumma wajh Allāh* (*Whichever way you turn, there is the face of Allah*).

I am *Al-Qayyūm* (*the Self-Sustaining*).

You tire of ruling a small kingdom, of office work and household affairs, of sleeping and waking. I am the One who neither tires nor sleeps, who slumbers not:

He did not say *Lan nām* (*We shall not slumber*); He said *Lā talḥaquh* (*it does not touch Him*). He cannot even be approached by such conditions.

### The Absolute Oneness of God

What is it? There exists no peer in heaven and earth. Allah alone is King. Is there anyone in conflict with Him? Allah asks: "If there were, show me!" God did not father a son, nor does any god stand beside Him. If it were otherwise, each god would have taken what he created, and some would have risen above others.

⟪ar-quote:لم یتَّخِذ صاحبا ولا ولدَا⟫ *He has neither taken a companion nor begotten a son.*

Do you not see? Whenever two powers arise, they clash. If there existed another like Him, would you not know that one occupies three heavens while the other holds four? One drew forth the sun while the other sank it? One increased the moon while another diminished it? One commanded the stars while another arrested them? What then would occur? *Alā fasadā* (*Indeed the order would have been corrupted*)—the entire cosmos, the heavens, and the universe would collapse. Not even a *dirham* would remain secure.

*Lā yadrikuhā al-layl wa-l-nahār* (*Neither the sun should perceive the moon nor should the night precede the day, and each swims in its orbit*).

Can the power of the moon collide with the power of the sun? *Wa-l-shams tajrī li-mustaqarr lahā* (*And the sun runs unto a set for it*). This is the appreciation of the Mighty, the All-Knowing.

Consider the sun: merely two points appear to left and right. Yet it exceeds the earth in size 1.2 million times. It travels at 600,000 kilometres per hour. Its temperature reaches 15 million degrees Celsius. Yet it proceeds through the void with absolute precision—not even a centimetre's deviation! Why? *Thalik taqdir al-ʿazīz al-ʿalīm* (*That is the measuring of the All-Mighty, the All-Knowing*). It has been ordained by the mighty Creator.

*Lā ilāha illā Huwa* (*There is no god but He*).

### The Disease of False Devotion

Yet you have fashioned your idols. Some have adorned the idol of employment, others the idol of beauty, still others the idol of wealth or fame. Some have made trade their idol. You appear to worship me alone, yet in truth you worship these. Your heart bows to them; your forehead bows to me. I gaze upon your heart, and when you prostrate, you do not even remember me. What can I say to such servants?[^cite-28]

This is the condition of worshippers: the head is in prostration, yet the heart is in the marketplace, at work, with food and clothing. Yet Allah does not see merely what the eye shows; the heart sees what dwells within the heart. This is the most terrifying verse of the Qur'ān:

I will not merely observe your prayer and fasting. I shall see what has been accumulated within your breast. Whom did you love? Whom did you circumambulate? I shall perceive that.

*Wa al-qulūb tumawwahal* (*O the secrets held within the breasts!*).

The heart circumambulates things—circling gold, silver, wealth, and fame, while the forehead rests upon God. When I bowed my head in prayer, I heard a voice rising from the ground:

*Your heart is preoccupied; what shall you gain from prayer?*

Allah meets only those whose hearts prostrate alongside their foreheads.

### The Knowledge of God

Who is God? This was the first knowledge we needed to acquire. We did not know Him. Schools have become commercial enterprises. On the very first day, teachers taught a pomegranate, a goat. They taught *Alif Allāh, Bā Bismillāh* (*A is for Allah, B is for In the Name of Allah*). But they never conveyed the greatness of Allah to parents or pupils.

Satisfying this God is the primary purpose of our life. If I do not please the Emperor, I am worthless. Everything I possess is squandered; I have failed entirely. Should I find Allah, I obtain everything. Should I not find Allah, I obtain nothing. If my God is pleased, even if the entire world were destroyed, I would succeed. If Allah is displeased, even if the whole world prostrated before me, even if I accumulated every wealth, honour, fame, and distinction—I swear by Allah, I have failed utterly.

*Allāh al-ʿAẓīm* (*Allah the Mighty*).

There is no god but Allah! Who is He? *Huwa al-Qayyūm* (*The Self-Sustaining*). How does He sustain? *Lā talḥaquhu shuʿūr* (*Slumber touches Him not, nor sleep*). How is His dominion? *Lahu mulk al-samāwāt wa-l-arḍ* (*His is the kingdom of the heavens and the earth*). What of His glory and majesty? *Man dhā alladhī yashfaʿ ʿindahu illā bi-idhnihi* (*Who can intercede with Him except by His permission?*). Can anyone speak before Him without His leave? No one shall utter a word except by His permission.

⟪ar-quote:وَخَشَعَتِ ٱلْأَصْوَاتُ لِلرَّحْمَنِ فَلَا تَسْمَعُ إِلَّا هَمْسًۭا⟫

*All voices shall be silent before the Merciful, and you shall hear naught but whisper.*

On that day, voices will be hushed, tongues locked. No sound shall be heard except footsteps. The faces of all creation shall be bowed before this *Al-Qayyūm*. All eyes shall be lowered. No one shall dare lift their gaze.

What kind of knowledge does He possess? *Yaʿlam mā bayna aydīhim wa-mā khallfahum* (*He knows what is before them and what will come after*). He has forgotten nothing of what lies behind Him. 

⟪ar-quote:یعلم عدَدِ القَطرِ الامطار⟫

*He knows the number of raindrops.*

Those who spend their days praising their deals, their business, their clothing—they continue praising their children. Tell them to sit and praise Allah and His Messenger, and they cannot speak for five minutes. The tongue becomes locked. There is not ten minutes' worth of words about my God, my greatest benefactor, my greatest friend, my Master, my Protector, my Witness, my Guardian.

Who praises His beautiful names and attributes in these exquisite words? Yet I cannot offer even a minute's praise to Him. What then is this *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* (*There is no god but Allah*)?

### The Heart's Witness to Oneness

Whether you are Arab or non-Arab, you have no god but Allah.

Yet the dictionary grows thin without your heart's testimony. Unless the melody of *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* echoes upon the strings of my heart, what benefit shall it bring me on the Day of Resurrection merely to speak it with my tongue?

This *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* has not freed me from usury, nor from falsehood, nor from deceit, nor from shameless conduct, nor from wickedness and immorality, nor from absurd songs, nor from slander. How then shall this *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* save me from the fire of Gehenna?

I kiss and say: I am a Muslim; there is no god but Allah.

Yet the belief must burn like Khalīl's fire—the intoxication of divine intimacy itself.

### The Crisis of Insincerity

Hear the cry of our age—uncertainty is worse than slavery. There is no disease worse than doubt. I pronounce *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* and lie upon my tongue. I declare *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* and harbour hatred for Muslims in my heart. I recite *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* while disobeying my parents. I proclaim *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* while slandering another. I assert *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* while conducting business through usury.

If my heart does not tremble at the call to prayer (*al-adhān*), if it does not incline toward worship, then what is my *Lā ilāha illā Allāh*?

This is not the *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* that my Prophet called the key to Paradise's locks. It is not the *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* that opens the gates of Paradise for me to walk straight through. It demands complete faith, whole-hearted sincerity, and arduous labour. For this, the heart's *Qiblah* (direction of prayer) must become Allah, just as the body's *Qiblah* is the House of Allah.

If you turn away from the House of Allah, there is no prayer. Likewise, if the heart's *Qiblah* is not Allah, then deeds bear no fruit.

*Aqbil al-laṭīf wa-l-kathīf bi-l-kathīf* (*Draw near, subtle One, with subtlety and with substance*).

What then happens within the breasts? Yes, yes! "I shall not see your worship, I shall not see your fasting. I shall see first what dwells within your hearts!"

### The Supreme Goal: Divine Pleasure

My brothers and sisters, make the greatest matter of your lives the pleasure of Allah. Let the first concern of your life, the middle concern, the last concern, and the greatest concern be this: *May my Allah be pleased with me.* I wish to satisfy His beloved—Muḥammad first and foremost. The middle shall be Muḥammad, the last shall be Muḥammad, and we ourselves are all Muḥammad!

When death arrives, let Allah, His Prophet, the Prophet's successor, and the Imāms meet me smiling. Let this verse be recited over me:

⟪ar-quote:یا ایتھا النفس المطمئنۃ، ارجعی الی ربِّکِ راضیۃً مرضیۃ⟫

*O tranquil soul, return to your Lord, pleased and pleasing!*

Enter the Paradise of your Lord. Join His righteous servants. This is the greatest and most serious matter of my existence.

All other concerns pale like a child's tears over a broken toy—as though no greater problem could ever exist. Yet the elder laughs, seeing how the child weeps over such trivial things.

So it is with us before God. He observes our crying, our weeping, the way we fret over worldly matters *as if* this were life's greatest problem. No! No! The supreme concern is to please Allah. Sacrifice whatever must be sacrificed for it. The transaction is with God! The bargain is with God.

How long will anyone persist in this world's luxuries? How long shall you rest in your home? How long will you sleep upon soft, delicate beds? How long will you walk upon velvet carpets? Funerals depart from grand mansions and funerals depart from humble huts alike. Whether a four-channel bungalow or a fakir's dwelling, the funeral procession is the same—the white shroud identical, the weeping of mourners the same, the dark grave alike, the final reckoning one.

*There stands a broken house at the edge of the narrator's tale.*

Noor Jahān was queen of the entire subcontinent. After such lengthy time, a poet beheld her tomb and wept. What does today's woman possess?

⟪ar-quote:وَكَمْ أَهْلَكْنَا قَبْلَهُم مِّن قَرْنٍ هَلْ تُحِسُّ مِنْهُم مِّنْ أَحَدٍ أَوْ تَسْمَعُ لَهُمْ رِكْزًۢا⟫

*How many generations have We destroyed before them? Do you sense from them anyone, or hear from them a sound?*

Is there a footstep's sound from any of them? Is there a name for any of them? Where are those great kings who ruled, who made millions boast, who bent the necks of peoples before them? Where are they?

The entire universe bows before Him; let me bow as well. You too must bow. Do not take this life for granted. Do not take these breaths for granted.

Money is kept safe in banks lest it be stolen. Jewels are locked in safes lest they be taken. But consider your breath! This single breath I have drawn—now all the mountains of wealth in the universe, all gold and silver combined, cannot restore that one breath of mine. Yet how much time do we spend watching television, viewing dramas, on our laptops, our phones? Can these hours ever return?

It is not forbidden—certainly watch! But attend to what is essential first. When a child returns from school, parents say: "Son, complete your homework, then sit and watch television." Why? So that homework is not neglected in favour of television. For homework is what matters. If a drama is missed, no harm follows; but if homework is neglected, loss ensues. So too with prayer and worship—our spiritual homework. Complete it on time, then do other things as well.

How much time is consumed in gossip upon the phone? How much is spent in complaint? How long does a drama occupy us? God is watching. He says: I am not sleeping; I observe all things.

⟪ar-quote:أَن تَحْسَبُوا۟ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَافِلٌۭ عَمَّا يَعْمَلُ ٱلظَّالِمُونَ ۚ إِنَّمَا يُؤَخِّرُهُمْ لِيَوْمٍ تَشْخَصُ فِيهِ ٱلْأَبْصَٰرُ⟫

*Do not suppose that Allah is heedless of what the wrongdoers do. He delays them only until a day on which eyes shall stare.*

I am not heedless! I am not inattentive! I note everything. A single fish? On that day when eyes shall open wide!

*Yaẓ al-āṯāma min wa-l-qulūb* (*Hearts and minds shall turn away with fear*).

Fear the day when your heart shall flee, your eyes shall turn away in dread, your heart shall leap to your throat. You shall cry: "Hide me somewhere!"

⟪ar-quote:لا تخفی مِنکم خافیۃ⟫

*Nothing of you is hidden.*

Allah shall say:

⟪ar-quote:این المفر؟ این المفر؟⟫

*Where is the flight? Where is the escape?*

"Where will you run today? If you can flee, then flee!"

### The Day of Reckoning

This universe is God's slave. All His creation is enslaved to Allah. Whenever He wills, He can burn it, swallow it. But this world is not a place of punishment; it is a testing ground to see who pleases Him and who pleases his own soul.

If the Great Blast comes, there will be a catastrophe. If the Scream is raised, there will be a sound that deafens the ears.

*Al-qāri'ah, mā al-qāri'ah?* (*The Striking Hour—what is the Striking Hour?*)

I cannot imagine how terrible that sound must be.

*Muḥawwal 'alā al-muḥawwal!* (*Attachment upon attachment!*)

What is the perception of attachment? A great truth—which you denied in the world! How do you now know what that reality is? Today, when the veil has been lifted from your eyes, wax removed from your ears, negligence stripped from your hearts—today you shall see this reality.

*Wa-jāʾa rabbuka wa-l-malak ṣaffan ṣaffan* (*And Your Lord comes and the angels rank upon rank*).

When Allah comes, His angels shall come, their ranks arrayed.

A call shall be proclaimed: "Bring so-and-so!"

⟪ar-quote:یُجاءُ ابن آدم یوم القیامۃ فَیُوقَف بین یدی اللہ تعالی کأنہ بزج⟫

*The son of Adam shall be brought forth on the Day of Resurrection and stood before Allah, Most High, like a small young goat.*

The mightiest warriors, the greatest wrestlers, shall tremble before Allah like a newborn goat trembling—attempting to rise, then standing with trembling legs. Allah the Almighty says they shall tremble. The voice of Allah shall come from above:

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم! اعطیتک، و خولتک، و انعمت علیک ماذا صنعت فیھا؟⟫

*O son of Adam! I gave you provision, I invested you with authority, I bestowed blessings upon you—what have you wrought with them?*

Man and woman alike—what have you brought forth this day? Show me what you have accumulated for me in return for what I gave you! This shall be the moment when all tremble, terrified as to what shall befall them. Terror will seize the onlookers; how much more the one held accountable by Allah? God shall say:

⟪ar-quote:ارنی ماذا قدمت! ارنی ماذا قدمتی⟫

*Show me what you have sent forward! Show me what you have sent forward!*

⟪ar-quote:یا رب جَمَعتُہ و ثمَّرتُہُ و ترکتُہُ اکثر ماکان⟫

*My Lord, I accumulated it, I multiplied it, I left it greater than it was.*

Allah will respond:

⟪ar-quote:ارنی ماذا قدمت!⟫

*Show me what you have sent forward!*

I am not asking what you left behind; I am asking what you accumulated for today! Bring it forth! Today your heaven and hell shall be determined. Your success and failure shall be judged upon this—not upon what preceded, not upon what came before.

*Yawma yajʿal al-walad al-shaybah* (*The day when the newborn shall grow old*).

This is the time when a child shall become aged through fear, his hair turning white.

I did not prevent him from coming to my house, my office, my business. But do we see the Prophet's way reflected in our marriages? They marry according to the Sunnah (*Al-Sunnah*—the tradition of the Prophet); they perform the rites according to Hindu ways.

*Ṣalātā, ṣalātā! Al-Dīn, al-Dīn!* (*Prayer, prayer! Religion, religion!*) The essence of my twenty-three years of endeavour is prayer. O my Community, do not abandon your prayers. Then he said: *"Allāh, rāfiq al-aʿlā"* (*Allah, the Highest Companion*). O Beloved, call me now to yourself; my eyes grow heavy.

If you adopt the life of the Messenger of Allah, you shall reach Allah. Otherwise, you will wander lost along the way. There will be neither destination nor path. The goal is not becoming a house-owner, acquiring cars, rising to the rank of CEO. My aim is to please Allah. My ruin is to incur His anger.

My whole life passed in the evening, and then I realized something was amiss. Now it is the evening of life. Who will salvage it now?

⟪ar-quote:قِيلَ ٱرْجِعُوا۟ وَرَآءَكُمْ فَٱلْتَمِسُوا۟ نُورًۭا فَضُرِبَ بَيْنَهُم بِسُورٍۢ لَّهُۥ بَابٌۢ بَاطِنُهُۥ فِيهِ ٱلرَّحْمَةُ وَظَهَرُهُۥ مِن قِبَلِهِ ٱلْعَذَابُ⟫

*It shall be said: Turn back and seek light. But a wall shall be raised between them, within which shall be mercy, and outside of which shall be torment.*

The doors close. It is time, it is time, *it is now*. We might yet turn back. We might yet alter our course, change our trajectory. But do not let the appointed time expire, the doors shut fast, and return become impossible even if we wish it.

### Repentance and the Life of Discipleship

It is the grace of Allah that He forgives the sins of a lifetime upon a single sincere repentance. If repentance is genuine, no sin exists that Allah cannot pardon. Islam is the discipline of learning the pure life of the Beloved of Allah. When the hour comes to depart, we shall meet Allah in joy, and He shall be pleased to receive us. This is the matter of learning life itself.

### The Elimination of Slander

Backbite no one. What is backbiting? It is speaking of someone—man or woman—in a way that pains them to hear. If you sever the words of others from your tongue, life becomes Paradise. Allah (May He be exalted) says:

*O you who have believed, avoid much assumption. Indeed, some assumption is sin. Do not spy upon one another, do not backbite one another. Would any of you like to eat the flesh of your dead brother, which you hate? And fear Allah. Indeed, Allah is the Acceptor of Repentance, the Merciful.*

The fire burning in the heart is not extinguished by cool air from the air-conditioner in your house. It merely dries the sweat upon your body. If the fire of the heart is to be quenched, come into the servitude of the Messenger of Allah and cultivate the character of the Prophet. This is a blessed life for which one must learn and labour hard.

## The Standard of Greatness

Allah has exalted His Prophets to the highest station, to the greatest rank. Yet our measure of human dignity remains inverted. We diminish persons not by examining their character, but by comparing their possessions. We call wealthy, the man of considerable fortune a great man—regardless of whether his soul is base and corrupt. The poor man, however virtuous his conduct, we label small. Our criterion is impoverished indeed.

God, the Creator, knows intimately what He has created. When we ourselves devise something, no matter how defective, we impose upon it some definition of worth. Yet the Creator Himself speaks of His creation with perfect clarity:

> And the life of the world is nothing but the pleasures of vanity.

All that appears before your eyes deceives. He elaborates with the image of thirst:

> Like a mirage in the desert, the thirsty man counts it as water.

Consider the traveller in arid lands. When sunlight strikes the sand, it shimmereth like a lake, a river, a sea. The thirsty man cries out: "Water! Water!" The wise man replies: "No—it is a mirage." Yet the desperate runner presses forward, growing only more parched as the illusion recedes. The evening of life approaches. When at last he reaches that place, ⟪ar-quote:حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءَهُۥ لَمْ يَجِدْهُ شَيْ⟫[^cite-29]—he discovers nothing. His companions spoke truly. He has exhausted himself in pursuit of emptiness, and now lacks even the strength to return.

When such seekers return, do not condemn them. Watch them with care.

The Creator compares the refuge of those who reject Him to the house of a spider—⟪ar-quote:بَيْتُ ٱلْعَنكَبُوتِ⟫—constructed of the finest silk, yet so fragile that a breath disperses it. The Prophet Muhammad (upon him be peace and blessings) himself declared: "The houses of this world are like the wings of a mosquito in the sight of God."[^cite-30] Why, then, should we expend our lives pursuing status, rank, and the false admiration of others, when God Himself proclaims:

> And the life of the world is nothing but the pleasures of vanity?

If we examine the history of the Prophets, we find that Allah Almighty enriched only one or two among them. Nearly all who came to this earth lived in simplicity and want. Moses possessed no bed—only a mat upon which he slept. In the morning, he placed fodder for his goats upon it; in the evening, he swept it and lay down again. Yet Moses—he whose name appears one hundred and thirty-six times throughout the Qur'an, mentioned in all but two surahs—is so revered by the Creator that God Himself addresses him directly.

Consider the magnitude: Here stands a Prophet so great that his name saturates the Scripture, yet he owns nothing that would grant him status in our eyes. We, by contrast, possess beds in our homes—we, who are but servants to cooks and servants. By our false reckoning, Moses would rank beneath the household help. And yet, God regards him with such intimacy that the Lord Himself converses with him:

⟪ar-quote:إِنِّىٓ أَنَا۠ رَبُّكَ فَٱخْلَعْ نَعْلَيْكَ ۖ إِنَّكَ بِٱلْوَادِ ٱلْمُقَدَّسِ طُوًۭى۔ وَأَنَا ٱخْتَرْتُكَ فَٱسْتَمِعْ لِمَا يُوحَىٰٓ۔ إِنَّنِىٓ أَنَا ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّآ أَنَا۠ فَٱعْبُدْنِى وَأَقِمِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ لِذِكْرِىٓ۔ إِنَّ ٱلسَّاعَةَ آتِيَةٌ أَكَادُ أُخْفِيهَا لِتُجْزَىٰ كُلُّ نَفْسٍۭ بِمَا تَسْعَىٰ۔ فَلَا يَصُدَّنَّكَ عَنْهَا مَن لَّا يُؤْمِنُ بِهَا وَٱتَّبَعَ هَوَىٰهُ فَتَرْدَىٰ⟫[^cite-31]

Allah converses at length with His servant—a man so destitute that he lacks a bed for sleep. What eloquence can capture the grandeur of one to whom the Emperor of the universe speaks directly?

## The Prophet's Petition

When Moses approached Pharaoh, he waited three days before gaining an audience. Three days elapsed before one whom Allah Himself had appointed as His Messenger could stand before the king. The poor man came seeking permission to speak. Our own sovereigns grant audiences far more freely to any petitioner who enters their courts.

Witness how Allah repeatedly recounts the story of Moses in the Qur'an:

> Did you receive the account of Moses? He called upon the Lord of the Worlds. Pharaoh said, "Do you dare to call me lord? I will imprison you." Moses replied, "Even so, I shall guide you to your Lord." Then was revealed the greatest verse. But he lied and turned away in arrogance. Then the Lord seized him. ⟪quran S:79 A:17-25⟫

Consider what Moses then requested of his Lord:

> My Lord, expand my breast. Make easy the task for me, and loosen the knot upon my tongue that they may understand my speech. Grant me a helper from my family—Aaron is my brother. Strengthen me through him, that I may glorify You greatly and remember You always. Indeed, You have ever been watching over us.

What an extended conversation Moses held with the Lord of Creation! Such detailed discourse, such intimate petition. Go to the court of an ordinary monarch, and you will scarce speak two sentences before he dismisses you. Approach a company executive, and he will grant you but two minutes: "Make it quick." When a king or CEO speaks at length with someone, what does it signify? That person possesses dignity in their eyes. That person is esteemed.

Yet Allah converses with Moses—a man with nothing to eat, nothing to drink, no bed to sleep in, dwelling in a hut, his livelihood the herding of goats—at such length and with such care.

We possess grand houses. Our bedrooms are separate from our dining halls; our dining halls distinct from our sitting rooms; each room serves its appointed function. And yet God does not speak to us. Do we not exceed Moses in wealth and station? Then why does the Lord not address us with such frankness? Why does He not grant us such intimate discourse?

This reveals the bankruptcy of our standards. The metric by which we measure honor is fundamentally false, fundamentally base. The criterion we have adopted for being impressed is too narrow, too corrupt. We have not truly seen the human being. We have looked only at his possessions, his garments, the dimensions of his dwelling. We have not examined his morality, his character. A person possessed of immense wealth and property may be utterly debased—cruel, oppressive, miserly, shameless. Yet because of his riches, by our corrupted standard, he ranks as great.

God has never greeted any earthly king with the salutation He bestows upon His Prophets. There are twelve forms of greeting, and the most noble is the peace (salaam) that Allah extends to His chosen servants. Reflect upon this: Does a subject approach a king's court and address the monarch himself, or does the king speak first? The order is inverted in the divine realm. Allah, the King of the Universe, Himself greets His Messengers:

> Peace be upon Noah among the worlds.
> Peace be upon Abraham.
> Peace be upon Moses and Aaron.
> Peace be upon all the Messengers.

Yet when Allah bestowed greeting upon the Beloved Muhammad (upon him be peace and blessings), He set a singular distinction. He did not call him by name. He named not "Muhammad" nor "Ahmad," but rather:

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ وَمَلَٓئِكَتَهُۥ يُصَلُّونَ عَلَى ٱلنَّبِىِّۚ⟫[^cite-32]

Verily, Allah and His angels invoke blessings upon the Prophet. The verb "salloo"—they invoke blessings—carries the weight of continuous action. They do not bless once, but perpetually. Allah and the angels shall continue to invoke blessings upon this noble Prophet until the Day of Judgment. And Allah commanded the believers:

⟪ar-quote:يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ صَلُّوا۟ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا۟ تَسْلِيمًا⟫[^cite-33]

O you who believe, invoke blessings upon him and greet him with the utmost respect.

This command was given to no other Prophet. To Noah, Abraham, and Moses—and to Jesus and Elijah—Allah said, "Peace be upon them," but He did not command the believers to invoke blessings upon them. Yet here, Allah sent down His own blessings and those of the highest angels together, and then turned to His servants: "Why do you not follow? Why are you silent?

⟪ar-quote:صَلُّوا۟ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا۟ تَسْلِيمًا⟫

Invoke blessings upon him with complete submission and respect."

## The Work of the Prophets

The Prophets were appointed to a sacred duty—to convey the message of Allah. We, by contrast, busy ourselves from dawn until dusk chasing position and protocol. We enslave ourselves to status. What is the task of the Prophets? To rise in the morning and bear witness to God's truth. How strenuously did Noah labor? The Lord relates:

> I prayed to my Lord: O my Lord, I have called my people day and night.

Night and day, Noah invoked his Lord on behalf of his people. And what was their response?

> But their aversion only increased.

The more Noah called them, the further they fled. Yet why did he call them? For his own glory? For renown? No:

> And whenever I called them to forgive them, you placed your fingers in your ears and covered yourselves, and you persisted in your arrogance.

⟪ar-quote:جَعَلُوٓا۟ أَصْبِعَهُمْ فِىٓ آذَانِهِمْ وَٱسْتَغْشَوْا۟ ثِيَابَهُمْ وَأَصَرُّوا۟ وَٱسْتَكْبَرُوا۟ ٱسْتِكْبَارًۭا⟫[^cite-34]

They placed their fingers in their ears, drew cloth across their faces, persisted in rejection, and grew ever more arrogant. Had I spoken to such a people, I would have said: "Enough. If you will not listen, then begone. I have offered you my time, my explanation, and you respond with disdain. This lesson is finished. The next session is cancelled." But Noah did not abandon them:

> Then I announced to them openly, and I confided to them secretly.

I proclaimed to them loudly. I spoke in hushed tones to individuals, pleading, beseeching, hoping that if not today, then tomorrow they might accept guidance. If not this generation, then perhaps the next would come seeking forgiveness.

Such is the work of the Prophets. Such is the labor that earned them such esteem in the sight of Allah. The Lord called to His Prophet Muhammad:

> O wrapped one, arise and warn.
> Warn the people and glorify your Lord.
> Proclaim the greatness of God before them.

Tell them: This world is not all. The Hereafter approaches. You shall stand before your Lord and give account of every deed, every word, every dirham. Warn them to perform righteous works so that they may receive from Allah an immense reward.

The manner in which the Lord addressed His Prophet reveals divine affection. He did not call him "Muhammad" or "Ahmad," but rather:

> O wrapped one (al-Mudaththir).
> O concealed one (al-Muzzammil).

He called upon his Prophets by name—O Noah, O Abraham, O Moses, O Jesus, O David. Yet to His Beloved, He spoke in titles of tender address. To call someone by name in Arabia was customary, but to honor them, the people would employ titles: Abu al-Hasan, Abu Dujana, Abu Dharr al-Ghaffari. The bedouins would approach the Prophet saying, "O Muhammad." But Allah rebuked them:

> Do not address the Messenger as you address one another.

Beware! Let no one call my Beloved by his bare name. Say instead, "O Messenger of Allah." And the Lord Himself refrained from calling him by name. Instead:

> O wrapped one, arise.
> O concealed one, arise.

How can we fathom the depths of Allah's love in such words? We do not even comprehend the Arabic. When a mother's love overflows, does she call her child by name? No. She says, "My soul, the light of my eyes, my beloved, my sweetheart." Why? Because a single name cannot contain such love. Behind every tender epithet lies a flood of affection, and no name alone can encompass it. Thus does the Almighty address His Prophet with titles of love:

> O wrapped one, arise and warn.
> Glorify your Lord and have patience.

Allah gave His Messenger a duty. By means of him, He gave his Community the same duty. What are the requirements of this work? To warn. To proclaim the greatness of Allah. To tell the people: Your Lord is Allah, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate. Your Creator fashioned the heavens and the earth.

> Your Lord is Allah, who created the heavens and the earth in six days, then established Himself upon the throne. He created you from clay.

Tell them their Lord raised the heavens:

> Allah, who created seven heavens and raised their canopy and made them equal.

Tell them Allah governs day and night. Tell them He spread out the earth. Tell them He brought forth from it water and pasture:

⟪ar-quote:أَخْرَجَ مِنْهَا مَآءَهَا وَمَرْعَىٰهَا۔فَأَنۢبَتْنَا فِيهَا حَبًّۭا وَعِنَبًۭا وَقَضْبًۭا وَزَيْتُونًۭا وَنَخْلًۭا وَحَدَآئِقَ غُلْبًۭا وَفَكِهَةًۭ وَأَبًّۭا⟫[^cite-35]

From the earth He brought forth water and pasture. From it We produced grain and grapes and herbage and olives and date-palms and gardens dense with trees and fruits and herbage. Your Lord created all of this, and for what purpose? For your benefit and the benefit of your herds, that you and your animals might flourish.

Stand and warn. Keep your outer garment clean; purify your outer form. Purify your inner self. Distance yourself from filth. Let no stain soil your clothing. Let no lie, no deceit, no arrogance, no envy, no avarice, no immorality, no baseness, no cowardice defile your inner being. Let your outer raiment be pure, and let your inner garment be pure as well.

Do not perform good deeds seeking the praise of men. Work for the sake of Allah alone. Possess patience for the sake of Allah.

A Companion of the Messenger of Allah received guests. The night was cold, and a fire burned in the hearth. After the meal, he washed the hands of his guests and offered a towel. As each guest dried his hands, the towel grew damp. When all had finished, the Companion cast the towel into the fire. The people cried out in alarm: "What are you doing?" With tongs, he withdrew the towel from the flames. Not a single thread was burnt. When the people marveled, he explained: "Once the Messenger of Allah came to my house. After eating, I presented him with this very towel to dry his hands. From that day, when this towel was touched by the hands of the Holy Prophet, fire has had no power over it."

If the fire of this world cannot touch that which the Messenger touched, how can the fire of the Hereafter touch one whose existence becomes like that of the Messenger?

Seek to earn your livelihood, but do so lawfully. Gabriel has revealed to my heart:

⟪ar-quote:بلا وَ أنَّ جِبرَئِیلَ نَفَسَ فی رُّوحیِ اَنَّ النَّفسَ لَم تموتَ حتّٰی تَستَک مِنَ الرِّزقَھا۔⟫

No soul departs this world until it has received its allotted sustenance. Therefore:

⟪ar-quote:الا فأَجمِلُ فی الطَّلَبِ وَ التَّقُو اللہ⟫

Seek sustenance with excellence, and fear Allah.

The Prophet has said: "My Community may commit any sin, but they cannot lie and deceive." Yet today, deception and Islam have become indistinguishable. Lies and Muslims have become one and the same. What we do to others, our Community does likewise. Do not blame the bandits who waylay the caravan; blame instead the guides who led them astray.

## The Living Qur'an

The character of Islam is not lengthy discourse. That is the way of strangers. The Final Sermon of the Messenger of Allah, delivered at Ghadir Khumm before seventy thousand people, lasted but ten or fifteen minutes. No long oration was needed—for the Prophet was a living Qur'an. His character, his morals, his words, his very silence embodied the Qur'an entire.

Once a disbeliever in Madinah asked a Companion: "Show me the Qur'an of your religion." As Mawla ʿAlī (the Master Ali) passed by, the Companion pointed and said: "There is the Qur'an—the Qur'an in practical form, walking before you." Such was the character of those of that time. Where is the Qur'an today? It remains. The Qur'an still exists in the form of our Imam, our guide—yet it is hidden from our sight. The Prophets who interpret the Qur'an stand before us, but we have no time to study the Qur'an ourselves. How then shall we learn?

Allah commanded His Prophet:

⟪ar-quote:قُلْ هَذِهِۦ سَبِيلِىٓ أَدْعُوٓا۟ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ ۚ عَلَىٰ بَصِيرَةٍ أَنَا۠ وَمَنِ ٱتَّبَعَنِى ۖ وَسُبْحَنَ ٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أَنَا۠ مِنَ ٱلْمُشْرِكِينَ⟫[^cite-36]

Say: This is my path. I call to Allah upon clear vision—I and those who follow me. Glory be to Allah, and I am not among the polytheists.

This is my path. Place your finger upon it. Look. This is my way. We sit together, and I point and say, "This is my way"—yet none knows whom I indicate. The demonstrative word points to something specific. Allah says: This is the path of Islam for me and for those who obey me, based upon clear understanding (baṣīra, clear vision). Here He distinguished Himself and His followers from all others. Me and those who follow me—you give us our way. All the Muslims gathered. Yet He set us apart.

What is that way? The invitation to Allah, with clear vision—the daʿwa (the summons, the invitation to God)—given to those who obey the Messenger of Allah.

## Allah is the Lord

The first revelation of Allah in the Qur'an announces His Name: "I am God." The divine Essence becomes known through His attributes. When we recite al-Ḥamdu lillāh (All praise belongs to Allah), we acknowledge that the divine nature itself is established through attribution. The Names al-Raḥmān (the Merciful) and al-Raḥīm (the Compassionate) complete the definition of the divine personality.

When we invoke Allah alone, all ninety-nine attributes are comprehended within that single Name. "Allah" is not an abbreviation but the complete designation of the One who depends upon nothing for His subsistence. The Self reveals itself through its attributes; therefore, when Allah declares, "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds" (Rabb al-ʿālamīn), He describes Himself as the Sustainer and Nurturer. The Arabic word ʿālam (world) in its plural form ʿālamīn encompasses not merely the visible cosmos but all domains of existence. Allah announces: "I am not merely the sustainer of your small terrestrial sphere; I am the Guardian of all the worlds, all the spheres of knowledge and being."[^cite-37]

Our understanding begins with contemplation of the single world we inhabit—the Akbar (the Greater World). How does Allah provision it? We observe that He is not only the Almighty but also the intimate Protector of human existence.

### The Lord of the Great World

The Earth rotates at approximately one thousand kilometers per hour, a speed that produces the rhythm upon which all life depends: a roughly twelve-hour day and twelve-hour night, with minor seasonal variation. Labor accomplishes its purposes in daylight; rest restores the body in darkness. Consider what would occur if Allah reduced this velocity to five hundred kilometers per hour: the day would extend to twenty-four uninterrupted hours, the night likewise. The sun's fire would ignite all matter; the nocturnal cold would freeze it. Neither advanced nations nor their technological mastery could arrest such a catastrophe. Yet Allah sustains this precise velocity. Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.[^cite-38]

Conversely, if the velocity doubled to two thousand kilometers per hour, we would possess six hours of daylight and six hours of darkness. Work would remain incomplete; rest would prove insufficient. Our existence itself would terminate. Allah upholds the exact measure. Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

The divine has placed gravity within the Earth. The planet's radius measures approximately twenty-four thousand kilometers. Were this distance doubled, all terrestrial inhabitants would walk as one moves through saturated soil or wet cement—each step labored, feet adhering to the ground. Had the measure tripled, the strong might with effort lift their feet; the weak would remain immobilized. Were gravity multiplied by five, its attractive force would compress the human form to diminutive proportions: a tall youth would shrink to the size of a small creature, the gravitational pressure preventing normal growth. Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

Were Allah to remove gravity altogether, the Earth would not remain suspended in the void. The Sun, a body of immense magnitude situated ninety-three million miles distant, projects flames extending three hundred thousand kilometers outward. Each second, thermonuclear fusion releases energy equivalent to five hundred million atomic bomb explosions, converting hydrogen into helium. Without gravitational restraint, the Earth would accelerate toward the Sun at five thousand kilometers per hour. Within months, our planet would fall into the solar envelope, where temperatures reach twenty-five million degrees Celsius—a heat at which iron becomes liquid. Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

The atmosphere contains precisely calibrated proportions: twenty-one percent oxygen, seventy-eight percent nitrogen, less than one percent carbon dioxide, and trace gases. No gauge constrains these measures in human hands. Were Allah to increase oxygen by merely one percent—from twenty-one to twenty-two percent—every dwelling would ignite; the flame of a simple stove would consume entire households. At twenty-five percent, forests would spontaneously combust. Yet one element remains fixed at twenty-one, another at seventy-eight. Whose hand sustains this balance? Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

We possess no dominion over the clouds. Should Allah withhold rain, all life ceases. The Qur'an testifies:

> Have you not observed that Allah forms clouds, then arranges them, then makes them into layers, and you see the rain emerging from their midst?

The divine speech also declares:

⟪ar-quote:أَنَّا صَبَبْنَا ٱلْمَآءَ صَبًّۭا۔ ثُمَّ شَقَقْنَا ٱلْأَرْضَ شَقًّۭا۔ فَأَنۢبَتْنَا فِيهَا حَبًّۭا۔ وَعِنَبًۭا وَقَضْبًۭا۔ وَزَيْتُونًۭا وَنَخْلًۭا۔ وَحَدَآئِقَ غُلْبًۭا۔ وَفَكِهَةًۭا وَأَبًّۭا۔ مَّتَعًۭا لَّكُمْ وَلِأَنْعَمِكُمْ⟫[^cite-39]

The descent of water follows a singular design. Rain typically forms at altitudes of twelve hundred meters. An object falling from such a height, under gravitational acceleration alone, would strike the ground at five hundred fifty-eight kilometers per hour—a velocity matching artillery fire. Were rain to descend at this speed, raindrops would pierce all structures; wooden dwellings would splinter within thirty minutes of precipitation. Yet Allah has arranged countless resistances throughout the atmosphere. Air friction, wind patterns, and the structure of clouds themselves retard the descent. Water reaches the ground at eight kilometers per hour—a gentle fall that nourishes rather than destroys. Allah dissipates five hundred fifty kilometers of destructive velocity through the air itself. Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

God is the Lord! The One who sustains and manages this small terrestrial world also sustains all universes and galaxies. He is the Lord of the Worlds—Lord, God!

Having expounded these marvels, I now return to the central matter: how Allah constitutes our Lord.

The believers most deceived are those who acknowledge Allah as God but deny Him as Lord. A Muslim does not deny that God alone merits worship; when he prostrates, he declares, "I bow before Allah." Ask an ordinary Muslim, one who does not pray, to prostrate before a stone, and he will refuse. Though his own observance may be negligible, he testifies: "Stone is not a god; my God is Allah alone."

Yet even the lax Muslim recognizes the Creator. The profound deception touches the Rabb (the Lord). A believer who performs five daily prayers and stands in night vigil may genuinely acknowledge Allah as God, yet live as though other lords govern his existence. Allah is not merely the deity to be worshipped; He is the Sustainer, the Provider, the One who alone deserves obedience in all affairs.[^cite-40]

This is the most subtle and difficult aspect of Islamic belief. The greatest deception enters the human heart regarding lordship. One pursues sustenance with anxious striving. One obsesses over worldly necessities as though survival depended upon one's own exertion. The Qur'an begins by establishing this truth: "Do not grieve. Do not fear. The provision of all creatures rests with Me. Yes, I shall examine you: I shall place before you the lawful and the forbidden, the true and the false, honesty and deception, justice and oppression. I command you to choose truth, to abandon falsehood; to choose lawfulness, to shun what is forbidden; to choose justice, to reject oppression."

Satan intercedes and whispers: "Where does your sustenance lie? In wealth. Where lies your dignity? In fame and reputation. Where lies your security? In the abundance of this world." At such moments, recall the divine words: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." The Lord is God. He alone provides.

Consider the test of daily life. A peaceful sleep overwhelms you. The crucial hour of business approaches. A moment of pleasure arrives. The office summons you to an urgent meeting. The muezzin calls: ⟪ar-quote:حی علی الصلاۃ!⟫ (Come to prayer!) Satan objects: "Why would you abandon such sweet repose? Your clients await. Your meeting begins. Your examination looms. Your entire enterprise will crumble." At this juncture, remember: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." The Lord is God. He will sustain me.

After a year, when wealth accumulates, Allah commands: "Now expend from it in My Name. Extract 2.5 percent for My impoverished servants." For one of modest means, this proves burdensome. For one possessing ten million rupees, the obligation becomes two hundred fifty thousand rupees—a substantial sum. Satan urges: "Invest this amount elsewhere. Generate profit from it. Establish another enterprise." Allah instructs: "Do not forget: 'Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds!' Do not forget that I granted you this wealth. I ask only 2.5 percent of what I have given—the rightful share of your poor brother."

Just as you honor the mosque as a sacred space, honor the poor as a sanctuary for your charity and zakāt (obligatory alms). Do not mock them; should you humiliate the recipient, I shall nullify your charity and alms upon My face.

The more we internalize this verse, the more peace and ease pervade our existence.

God is the Lord! Not merely your Lord, but Lord of the Worlds—the Lord of all domains and beings. When Allah created the souls, He posed the primordial question:

⟪ar-quote:وَإِذْ أَخَذَ رَبُّكَ مِنۢ بَنِىٓ آدَمَ مِن ظُهُورِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَأَشْهَدَهُمْ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ أَلَسْتُ بِرَبِّكُم⟫[^cite-41]

He did not ask, "Am I not your God?" Rather: ⟪ar-quote:أَلَسْتُ بِرَبِّكُمْ⟫ "Am I not your Lord?"

We responded: ⟪ar-quote:قَالُوا۟ بَلَىٰ⟫ "Yes, You are our Lord."

Then Allah declared: "Proceed into the world. I shall observe whether you recognize Me as Lord or whether you assign lordship to another."

When the Qur'an was revealed, it began with the Lord: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." It concludes with the Lord: "Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of Mankind." From beginning to end, lordship. Then, upon death, one proceeds to the grave, where the first question is posed: "Who is your Lord?" The dead are asked not "Whom did you worship?" but "Who is your Lord?"—the question returns eternally to lordship.

Consider the prayer structure itself. In each rak'ah, the worshipper recites: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." Rising from genuflection, one declares: "Our Lord, praise belongs to You." Entering prostration: "Glory be to my Lord, the Most High." Lord! Lord! Lord! Why is this so? Because Allah knew that when His servant stands in prayer, the heart may focus. But when that same servant sits in the shop, the mind thinks: "Where shall I obtain bread?" At work, the thought arises: "The company sustains me." At that moment, lordship is forgotten. 

The divine reminders address this forgetfulness directly:

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم خَلَقْتُ السمٰوات و الارضِ ولَم أَعیَکَ بِخَلقھم⟫[^cite-42]

O son of Adam! I created the seven heavens and seven earths, and all between them, yet creating them has not fatigued Me. How then should providing for your small existence weary Me?

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم لا تُکاَلِمْکَ بِعَمَلِ غَد⟫

O My servants! I do not burden you with actions not yet performed. Tomorrow remains unknown—you cannot predict fortune or calamity.

⟪ar-quote:فلا تَسأَلنِی عَن رِزْقِ غَد⟫

Therefore, do not petition Me for tomorrow's sustenance. Do not fret over the future. I have taken it upon Myself.

⟪ar-quote:فأِنِّی لَم أَنسأ مَن عَصاَنِی فَکَیفَ بِمَن أطاعنی⟫

O My servants! I do not forget those who disobey Me day and night—how then would I neglect those who obey?

⟪ar-quote:أَذکُرُکَ وَلا تَنسَانِی⟫

You forget Me; I do not forget you. I remember each and every servant.

⟪ar-quote:استترک فلا تغشانی⟫

I conceal your sins, yet you flee from Me in shame. Hide yourself from people if you must, but where will you hide from Me?

⟪ar-quote:خیری الیک نازل و شَرُّکَ عَلَیکَ سَاعد⟫[^cite-43]

My mercy descends upon you continuously. Your misdeeds ascend toward Me continuously. Your eyes see rightly. Your ears hear rightly. Your tongue speaks rightly. Your hands move rightly; your feet move rightly. Your intellect functions rightly. Your heart beats rightly. Blood flows rightly through your veins. From the crown of your head to the tips of your toenails, each cell functions in perfect coordination. Who sustains all this? Your Lord cares for you every day. And every day, your transgressions, your heedlessness, your disobediences, your lies, your deceits, your oppressions ascend to Me. Yet when night falls,

⟪ar-quote:أ تجعلُك النَّومِ وَكأَنَّكَ لَم یعصُولي⟫

I grant you sleep as though you had not disobeyed Me all that day.

God is the Lord! God is the Lord! This is the most difficult article of faith. This is the hardest test of belief. Blessed is the one who fulfills it.

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوا۟ رَبُّنَا ٱللَّهُ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَقَمُوا۟⟫[^cite-44]

Indeed, those who say "Our Lord is Allah" and then remain steadfast—

⟪ar-quote:تَتَنَزَّلُ عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ⟫

Angels descend upon them.

What is their reward for believing Allah to be Lord?

⟪ar-quote:أَلَّا تَخَافُوا۟ وَلَا تَحْزَنُوا۟ وَأَبْشِرُوا۟ بِٱلْجَنَّةِ ٱلَّتِى كُنتُمْ تُوعَدُونَ⟫

Do not fear, and do not grieve. Rejoice in the Paradise that was promised to you.

The Lord is the One whose treasures overflow perpetually, the One free from slumber and fatigue, the One who does not hesitate to give lest His stores diminish. The Lord illuminates with equal generosity in darkness and in light. What Lord is blind? What Lord is deaf? What Lord sleeps upon His own? What Lord is dependent upon another? What Lord is sick? What Lord dies? What Lord trembles in fear? What Lord is subject to disease, old age, death?

A true Sustainer is He who is flawless, eternal, incomparable, without beginning or end. Glory be to Allah from the beginning! Glory to Allah! If you possess the treasures of the world, you are Allah. If a throne occupies space, it is Allah's throne! If you rule day and night, you are Allah. If you are sovereign over east and west, you are Allah. If you govern north and south, you are Allah.

Allah is the One who sees through my eyes. Allah is the One who speaks through my tongue. Who brought forth the sun in daylight for me? Who revealed the moon and stars in the night? Who alternates day into night and night into day? Allah causes grain to grow from the earth. If fruits possess color and sweetness, Allah fills the fruits with these qualities. Allah fills clouds with water. Allah brings it down, drop by drop. Who places milk in the udders of animals? Allah. Who places the scent of musk in the navel of the deer? Allah. Who deposits pearls around the serpent? Allah. Who draws silk from the silkworm's mouth? Allah. Who is sovereign over past and present and future? Allah. Allah created the Throne, the Chair, the Tablet. Gabriel, Michael, Azrael, and Israfil derive sustenance from Allah. The Prophets and Imams before whom we bow are sustained by Allah! Paradise is Allah's creation. Hell is Allah's creation. The One who possesses dominion over life and death, who creates, sustains, terminates, and resurrects—

Only He deserves to be called Lord! And He is Allah alone!

God posed the question: ⟪ar-quote:أَلَسْتُ بِرَبِّكُمْ⟫ "Am I not your Lord?" We answered: ⟪ar-quote:بلی⟫ "Yes, You are our Lord." Then He said: "Proceed now into the marketplace! Go to the shop! Attend school and university! My servant, speak truth in the office, in the shop, in the market. Do not lie!"

The employer says: "Lie, or lose your position." We lied. But was Allah our Lord?

The merchant declares: "Lie, or your profit diminishes." We lied. But was Allah our Lord?

The school announces: "Lie, or your grades suffer." We lied. But was Allah our Lord?

"My servants, sell not intoxicants. I am your Lord!" If we abstain, how will profit accumulate? How will the business flourish?

"My servants, consume not usury. I am your Lord!" We built our enterprises on interest. If we reject interest, where does increase come from?

"My servants, preserve modesty. I am your Lord!" We embraced shamelessness. Garments tightened toward exposure. The headscarf was discarded—how else would fashion thrive? What would people say?

Does not Allah declare that He is the Sustainer? You were born utterly helpless. You could not move. You could not speak. Your hands were useless; your feet were useless. Who drew milk from your mother's breast when you were so dependent?

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم! مَن أرسَلَ أِلیک الغِضَابُ أَنتَ الَجِنینٌ فِی بَطنِ أُمِّک⟫

O My servants, when you were embryos in your mother's womb, who sustained you? When you lay upon your mother's breast, who provided milk? Who stirred the hearts of your parents at the sound of your cry? If a child weeps, a headache pierces the parent. Who created this bond? Who placed this love in the parents' hearts to nurture you? O My servants! Did I forget you in your mother's womb? Will I forget you in America? Will I forget you in England, in India, in Pakistan, in Dubai? I provide sustenance even to the filthiest creatures—pigs and swine. Will I forget you? I nourish the serpent, the most poisonous creature. Will I forget you?

God is the Lord! This is a severe test that Allah has placed upon us. Do not break your covenant. You vowed, you confessed to Me that I am your Lord. Do not renege upon this promise.

⟪ar-quote:أولکم، آخرکم، انسکم، جنکم ، حیکم، میتکم، رتبکم، یعبثکم، صغیرکم، کبیرکم، ذکرکم و انثاکم⟫[^cite-45]

Allah has explained in twelve manifestations: your first and your last, your human and your jinn, your living and your dead, your old and your new, your small and your great, your male and your female.

⟪ar-quote:قامو فی سعدی واحد⟫

Stand together in a single matter!

⟪ar-quote:فسألونی⟫

Now supplicate to Allah together. Do not stand in sequence! Do not ask for one petition, then another. If I am addressing you and you listen, and another begins speaking while I continue, you will not understand what either of us says. If background noise alone fills the air, speech becomes inaudible. You need only do one thing: mute your microphones. Yet My Lord says: "Petition Me together." Six thousand languages are spoken in the world today. Ask in your own tongues. There is no restriction to Arabic. Imagine the cacophony if all petitioned simultaneously. Are you unable to speak? Petition with your heart. Do not even let your tongue produce sound. Beseech Me through signs! My Lord declares: "When you supplicate together in your languages and through your signs, from the depths of your hearts,"

⟪ar-quote:یَسمَعُ سَکیجَھُم بأختلافِ الُّغاتھم فالأحجاتھم⟫

I shall hear and comprehend the plea of each. I shall not confuse one petition with another's. I shall not mistake whose request is whose.

⟪ar-quote:فأعطیت کلَّ انسانٍ مسألک⟫

Then I shall grant to each person his supplication. And what result follows?

⟪ar-quote:مَا نَقَصَ ذالک مِن ما عِندِی الَّا کما ینقص المَخیَۃُ الی أُدخِلَ البحر⟫

My treasury is diminished no more than a needle diminishes the ocean when dipped within it.

Why do we resort to falsehood? "Without it, the transaction cannot be completed. Without it, the work cannot proceed." But Allah is the Lord! Allah is the Lord!

## The Covenant Witnessed

There is a teaching through the example of ʿAbdullāh ibn Jarīr's resolve. He placed his hand in the hand of the Prophet and covenanted: "As long as I live, I shall not deceive anyone. I shall desire the best for all." 

Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds. The Lord is God.

Allah created this world as a testing-ground. He placed truth and falsehood within it, the permissible and the forbidden, marriage and adultery, commerce and usury, the lawful and the unlawful, honesty and betrayal, fidelity and fraud. Then He placed allure in the forbidden; He removed allure from the lawful. Then He declared:

⟪ar-quote:قُل لَّا يَسْتَوِى ٱلْخَبِيثُ وَٱلطَّيِّبُ وَلَوْ أَعْجَبَكَ كَثْرَةُ ٱلْخَبِيثِ⟫[^cite-46]

Say: The corrupt and the pure are never equal, though the abundance of corruption captivates you.

Then He asked us: ⟪ar-quote:أَلَسْتُ بِرَبِّكُمْ⟫ "Am I not your Lord?" We answered: ⟪ar-quote:بلی⟫ "Yes." Then He commanded: "Now go forth! Avoid deception. Avoid lies. Avoid intoxicants. Avoid adultery. Avoid usury. Avoid the unlawful. Avoid betrayal. Avoid theft. Avoid shamelessness. Avoid indecency. Avoid slander."

What did we accomplish? What did we achieve in this examination-hall? What was our performance in that testing arena?

Is the examination contained only in the mosque? Is it merely in ritual observance? No. The test is in the marketplace! In shops, in enterprises, in schools, in universities, in offices. Allah wishes to discern: Do you believe Me to be your Lord, or do you believe the customer to be your lord? Do you believe Me to be your lord, or do you believe the shopkeeper to be your lord? Do you believe Me to be your lord, or do you believe the teacher, the principal to be your lord? Do you believe Me to be your lord, or do you believe the company, the boss to be your lord?

The Lord is God! God is the Lord!

We questioned: "O Allah, if we obey these commands, where shall sustenance come from?" Allah replied:

⟪ar-quote:لا تَخشیٰ مِنَ الفَقر ماذا ما خَزَائِن مَلئَا⟫

O My servants, do not fear poverty while My treasures overflow. Learn to petition Me! Speak with Me!

⟪ar-quote:توالینی و اوالیک، تصافینی و اصافیک⟫

Take Me as your ally, and I shall take you as My allies. Establish friendship with Me, and I shall establish friendship with you.

You have spent your life cultivating friendships with people. No one has remained loyal. You have deceived everyone. O My servants, befriend Me once! I shall honor the compact of friendship. Love Me once! I shall repay the right of your affection.

Such is the Lord! What deception have we surrendered ourselves to? "If I do not lie, I shall lose employment. If I do not deceive, I shall gain no profit." But Allah has freed us from the trial of godhood. There exist worlds that have fallen before stones, crumbling before animals, prostrating before humans like themselves. Allah has liberated us from this trial. By Allah's grace, we have been spared. Our test is the next declaration: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." Lord of the Worlds. God is the Lord! The Lord is God.

What then must be done? Speak truth! What must be done? Deceive no one. What must be done? Do not engage in unlawful commerce. What must be done? Accept no usury! If you possess a day like this, you have recognized Me as Lord! Then you have truly believed in Me as Lord. Then you have fulfilled the right of the Lord of the Worlds.

Mosques are not the sole source of Paradise. The marketplace is also a source of Paradise. The mosque is not the only path to Paradise; schools, universities, offices, shops—all are pathways to Paradise through righteous earning. The examination of the mosque is straightforward; the examination of the bazaar is profoundly difficult. One perspires in cold; one withstands countless trials. Prayer occupies two or three hours of labor. The integrity of the marketplace operates twenty-four hours daily.

When the Messenger of Allah passed through the market, he observed a heap of wheat in one location. He thrust his hand within and withdrew grain—it was wet. He inquired: "Why is this wet wheat concealed beneath the dry?" The merchant replied: "Rain fell and dampened it. I placed dry wheat above it to hide the defect." The Messenger said: "Remove it so that buyers may see the damage and know what they purchase." Then he declared:

⟪ar-quote:من غَشَّ لیس مِنّٰی⟫

Whoever deceives does not belong to my community.

When he spoke of abandoning prayer, he said: ⟪ar-quote:من ترک الصلوۃ قد کفر⟫ "Whoever neglects prayer has disbelieved." And of the deceiver, he said: "He is not of my people."

You are the servant of Allah! With this belief, the condition of the marketplace-goer transforms. With this conviction, the office-worker, the school-student, the university-student, the shopkeeper is transfigured. But whoever regards gold, silver, dollars, pounds as lord will live in an entirely different state.

Thus, first, our deeds became tainted with unlawfulness. But from the remainder of our affairs issued lies, deceptions, thefts, slander, and anger.

## The Trial of Righteous Conduct

There was a cruel ruler, al-Ḥajjāj ibn Yūsuf, who executed approximately one hundred twenty-five thousand people before him. In his time, a rebellion arose. Two sons of Ibn al-Khārash participated in the revolt. This man was renowned for never uttering falsehood. When the rebel faction was defeated, the two sons fled and concealed themselves. After some time, they returned home in secret. Spies informed al-Ḥajjāj that Ibn al-Khārash's sons had come home. Al-Ḥajjāj resolved: "Today I shall test Ibn al-Khārash. Let me see how truthful he truly is."

He summoned him and said: "Brother Ibn al-Khārash, your two sons participated in war against me, then fled somewhere. Do you know where they are?" Ibn al-Khārash smiled and replied: "O al-Ḥajjāj! Life and death rest in Allah's hands. They have returned home. If you wish to apprehend them, do so." Al-Ḥajjāj said: "Ibn al-Khārash! I grant your two sons freedom for this truth! Had you lied, I would have severed their necks and yours."[^cite-47]

The Prophet was asked: "O Messenger of Allah, can a Muslim be cowardly?" He replied: "Yes, perhaps." He was asked: "Can a Muslim be miserly?" He replied: "Yes, perhaps." He was asked: "Can a Muslim lie?" He did not say "No." If one has a true nature, one cannot do two things simultaneously: one cannot always tell truth, and one cannot deceive another. Today we accomplish nothing without lies and deceptions. May Allah first grant us—and grant all of you—the strength to reform.

God is the Lord! The Lord is God. This world is small; the human being inhabiting it is small; the stomach is small; life is brief; necessities are few. Yet He said: "No! Go forth! Earn! Because this is an examination-hall. When you return to Allah, present the result. And what compensation awaits that result? Life free from death; youth free from old age; health free from disease; happiness free from sorrow; ascendance free from decline." If comfort were free from sorrow, how mighty would such a soul become?

Since you have been sent to the world, hold both truths in mind: this is an examination-hall, and within it reside the lawful and the forbidden. Now only the lawful remains lawful; not a trace of the forbidden remains.

Three deeds that contain oppression, lies, and deceit—humiliation is inscribed in their destiny. Even if one performs voluntary prayers throughout the night, performs Hajj and ʿUmrah yearly, such practices cannot rescue him from the punishment of Hell. And three deeds that contain no oppression, no falsehood, no deception—honor is inscribed in their destiny. The truthful will not lie. The just will not oppress. The honest will not deceive. Allah shall honor them.

There exists one divine law for people in this world, and another in the Hereafter. Therefore, be truthful. My Prophet declared: ⟪ar-quote:الصدق ینجی و الکذب یھلک⟫ "Truth delivers; lies destroy." ⟪ar-quote:لعنت اللہ علی الظالمین⟫ "Allah's curse upon the oppressors." ⟪ar-quote:إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلْخَآئِنِينَ۔ وَأَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى كَيْدَ ٱلْخَآئِنِينَ⟫ "Allah does not love the deceivers, and Allah does not guide the stratagem of the deceitful."

Consider Allah to be your Lord! Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds! Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds. Reflect! Examine your life and observe where you have turned to falsehood, where you have resorted to fraud.

My Prophet declared: "Do not enter the mosque after consuming raw onions, for there emanates from your mouth an unpleasant odor." If one lies, does the mouth stink in a physical sense? Yet my Prophet said: "When a person tells a lie, his mouth produces such an odor that angels flee from him for miles. The angels depart! Angels are our hope. They are our defense. When Satan assails us, angels form our first line of protection. If they withdraw, who can shield us from the devil's assault?

Islam is a struggle to achieve this conviction. It is necessary to transform this belief-system from the roots of the heart: that the Savior is Allah, that the Sustainer is Allah. Therefore, believe! Believe in Allah as Lord! Guard Allah's treasures. The Lord is God! Do not lie to preserve trivial wealth. Do not lie for meager gain. Do not gaze upon the harm of this temporary existence; look toward the benefit of the life to come. Is there not a life ahead? Is there not a confrontation with Allah and His Messenger?

Iḥsān Dānish is a writer from Pakistan. He has composed a book titled *The Wisdom of Life*. He recounts that during British rule in Delhi, a pickpocket operated in the markets. He maintained many apprentices who stole purses and brought their earnings each evening. One day, a disciple brought only two rupees—meager earnings. He said: "Teacher, today I encountered a very wealthy Englishman. I cut his purse; it contained considerable wealth. Yet after four steps, I thought: On the Day of Judgment, if Jesus complains to my Prophet that his community member stole from his people, how my Prophet will be shamed because of me! So I returned his money and lifted only two rupees from another's purse." The teacher was delighted and awarded the disciple ten rupees. "You have conceived a noble thought."

This disciple possessed awareness of the Prophet's dignity. We are believers; yet we show no concern for the Prophet's humiliation. We also dwell among the British, among the peoples of Moses and Jesus. Let us deal with them honestly; let us not deceive them, lest harm befall our Prophet on the Day of Resurrection. The Messenger of Allah himself declared in his final sermon: ⟪ar-quote:لا تسود وجھی یوم القیامۃ⟫ "O my community, do not shame me before Allah on the Day of Resurrection. Do not humiliate me. If you cannot honor your Prophet, at least spare him from the taunts of shame. You can preserve him from humiliation."

⟪ar:### اللہ رب العالمین⟫

Thus does life emerge and then reduce to nothing. Release greed; release desire.

We dwelt in Kuwait; in one hour, life transformed. Years of accumulated earnings froze in a single hour. Then one had to begin anew from zero. What benefit is such wealth?

Certainly, this truth is confirmed:

There was a scholar among the Israelites who preached with great fervor: "Whoever recites 'Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm' with faith, even if he walks upon water, shall cross it." A villager came the next day and said: "Reverend Scholar, you have eased my life greatly." The scholar asked: "How so?" He replied: "According to your teaching, I recited 'Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm' and walked upon the river, crossing it without drowning. Before, I suffered greatly; I had to pay the ferryman when I had no money. Your teaching has made it simple." The scholar, who preached this sermon, replied in amazement: "Is this true? Do you speak honestly?" He said: "Yes, absolutely." The scholar replied: "No, you are lying. If you are truthful, show me." The two approached the river. The villager proclaimed aloud: "Bismillāh," placed his foot upon the water, and walked across. Then he said to the scholar: "Now you come," and the scholar said: "Tie me with a rope first, then I shall place my foot upon the water." As you observe, such faith cannot function in a heart where doubt resides.

Allah is our Savior. All things rest in His control: ⟪ar-quote:قُلِ ٱللَّهُمَّ مَلِكَ ٱلْمُلْكِ تُؤْتِى ٱلْمُلْكَ مَن تَشَآءُ وَتَنزِعُ ٱلْمُلْكَ مِمَّن تَشَآءُ وَتُعِزُّ مَن تَشَآءُ وَتُذِلُّ مَن تَشَآءُ ۖ بِيَدِكَ ٱلْخَيْرُ ۖ إِنَّكَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌۭ⟫[^cite-48]

⟪ar-quote:تُولِجُ ٱلَّيْلَ فِى ٱلنَّهَارِ وَتُولِجُ ٱلنَّهَارَ فِى ٱلَّيْلِ ۖ وَتُخْرِجُ ٱلْحَىَّ مِنَ ٱلْمَيِّتِ وَتُخْرِجُ ٱلْمَيِّتَ مِنَ ٱلْحَىِّ ۖ وَتَرْزُقُ مَن تَشَآءُ بِغَيْرِ حِسَابٍۢ⟫[^cite-49]

You grant kingdom to whomever You will, and You withdraw kingdom from whomever You will. You exalt whomever You will, and You humble whomever You will. All goodness rests in Your hand. You have power over all things. You merge night into day and day into night. You bring forth the living from the dead and the dead from the living. You provide sustenance to whomever You will without measure.

No one can lengthen the night; no one can shorten the day. This conviction arises from belief in "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." The Lord is God.

Education is the responsibility of parents. Schools cannot instill true training. Train through love, not through force. If a child receives religious training at home, place him in any school—success follows. Teach a child to swim, and whether you cast him into the river or into the sea, he shall swim. If he has not learned to swim, he will drown in the river. Parents say: "Grant him a holiday. Let the teacher train him." But where can a teacher train a child lacking parental formation?

Islam is a quality of life, not a mere title.

## Identity

When a person attempts to solve the world's problems relying solely upon his intellect and his own effort, confusion multiplies rather than diminishes. Should he chart his own course, he becomes a wanderer upon unseen paths. And if he establishes his own priorities, he finds nothing but desires and failures. Allah has made him weak; He has granted him only a little knowledge. Yet the truth—the real and enduring truth—is His alone.

⟪ar-quote:وَٱللَّهُ أَخْرَجَكُم مِّنۢ بُطُونِ أُمَّهَتِكُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ شَيْـًۭٔا⟫

God brought you forth from your mothers' wombs knowing nothing.[^cite-50] This is our fundamental condition: human origin lies in ignorance, an ignorance without limit. Knowledge, by contrast, comprises but a few words. Though we may read books and accumulate learning, Allah Himself declares:

⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ أُوتِيتُم مِّنَ ٱلْعِلْمِ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا⟫

What you have been given of knowledge is very little.[^cite-51] Observe the precision of the divine word: *autītum*—"you have been given"—indicates that this knowledge comes as a gift. You have not seized it for yourselves. God is the Giver. He does not say you have *striven*, *learned*, *studied*, or *memorized*. The Almighty could have employed any of these terms:

⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ أستمتعتم؛ ومآ تعلمتم؛ وما أخذتم؛ ومآ درستم ؛ وما حفظتم⟫

Yet He chose instead to emphasize the act of divine bestowal: ⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ أُوتِيتُم⟫—*I have given it to you*. And how much has He given? ⟪ar-quote:إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا⟫—*only very little*.

With such meager knowledge in our possession, how can we presume to move forward relying upon it alone? Certainty of error awaits us. Should we attempt to govern a nation, administer institutions, manage households, or direct our own selves by our intellect alone, darkness—only darkness—lies ahead. We become as one lost in a dense forest, finding no exit from its depths.[^cite-52]

Yet there is more. Allah sets forth still graver conditions, further accusations concerning human nature itself:

⟪ar-quote:وکان الانسان عجولا⟫

Man is profoundly hasty. Armed with scanty knowledge and driven by haste, ⟪ar-quote:وکان الانسان اکثر شئی جَدَلا⟫—humans are argumentative, contentious over trifles. Lesser knowledge coupled with greater haste and anger leads to:

⟪ar-quote:وکان الانسان کفورا⟫

Man is deeply ungrateful. And compounding all these defects:

⟪ar-quote:وکان الانسان انہ کان ظلوماً جَھُولا⟫

Man is not merely a petty tyrant but a great one—ignorant and oppressive. So the list compounds: scanty knowledge, excessive haste, anger, ungratefulness, and now cruelty. Observe what happens when hardship touches him:

⟪ar-quote:وَإِن مَّسَّتْهُ ٱلشَّرُّ فَيَـُٔوسٌۭ قَنُوطٌۭ⟫

When affliction befalls him, he falls into despair and despondency, complaining to Allah and losing all hope.[^cite-53] And when ease comes:

⟪ar-quote:وَلَىِٕنْ أَذَقْنَهُ نَعْمَآءَ بَعْدَ ضَرَّآءَ مَسَّتْهُ لَيَقُولَنَّ ذَهَبَ ٱلسَّيِّـَٔاتُ عَنِّىٓ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ لَفَرِحٌۭ فَخُورٌ⟫

When We grant him blessing after hardship has touched him, he declares his misfortunes have departed, and he becomes boastful and arrogant. He strides upon the earth as though about to tear it asunder, his neck raised as though to surpass the mountains themselves—though:

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا⟫

You shall never break through the earth, nor shall your stature ever match the mountains. He swiftly forgets his station. When Allah grants position, wealth, beauty, or renown, man immediately grows proud and arrogant, oblivious to his own insignificance.

The Qur'an abounds with such indictments. Many other verses speak to human flaws, weakness, and ignorance—evils embedded within each of us, in women and men alike. With so little knowledge, so limited intellect, and so many defects attached to our nature, how can such a being govern a nation, administer a city, manage a household, direct a school or university by his intellect alone? There awaits him no destination save wandering, no end but being lost, no outcome but failure. Man simply does not possess the capacity to lay the foundations of life upon intellect and experience alone.[^cite-54]

The Prophets come into this world precisely to deliver us from this defect. For the knowledge of the Prophets is not acquired through reading or study. The knowledge of the Prophets comes to them directly from Allah Himself:

⟪ar-quote:وَمَا عَلَّمْنَهُ ٱلشِّعْرَ وَمَا يَنۢبَغِى لَهُۥٓ ۚ إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا ذِكْرٌۭ وَقُرْآنٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ⟫

I did not teach you poetry, nor is it befitting for you. This is naught but a Remembrance and a clear Qur'an.[^cite-55] In the Arabia of those times, poetry was the highest art. When a son was born, people did not say, "A boy has come to your house"; they said, "A poet has come." They did not ask the child's name; they asked, "What is the poet called?" Yet the Qur'an insists that the Prophet's knowledge comes not from human learning, not from the accumulated wisdom of the Arabs, not from literary craft. And when the people said:

⟪ar-quote:إِنْ هَذَآ إِلَّآ أَسَطِيرُ ٱلْأَوَّلِينَ⟫

This is nothing but tales of the ancients—Allah replied:

⟪ar-quote:وَأَنزَلَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْحِكْمَةَ وَعَلَّمَكَ مَا لَمْ تَكُن تَعْلَمُ ۚ وَكَانَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكَ عَظِيمًۭا⟫

I have sent down to you the Book and Wisdom, and I have taught you that which you knew not. Great is the favor of Allah upon you.[^cite-56] The Almighty Himself became his teacher:

⟪ar-quote:الم نشرح لک صدرک⟫

Have We not opened your breast?

⟪ar-quote:ووضعنا عنک وزرک⟫

And removed from you your burden?

⟪ar-quote:الذی انقد ظھرک⟫

Which had weighed upon your back?

⟪ar-quote:ورفعنا لک ذکرک⟫

And raised high your remembrance?

Allah, the Most High, grants His Prophets knowledge directly through Revelation (waḥy). Wahb ibn Munabbih declared: "What Allah gave to 125,000 Prophets is like a grain of sand; what He gave to His Beloved is like an ocean." The knowledge of all other prophets stands to the knowledge of Muhammad as a single drop to the vast sea. God taught him directly.[^cite-57]

⟪ar-quote:وَكَذَلِكَ أَوْحَيْنَآ إِلَيْكَ رُوحًۭا مِّنْ أَمْرِنَا ۚ مَا كُنتَ تَدْرِى مَا ٱلْكِتَبُ وَلَا ٱلْإِيمَنُ وَلَكِن جَعَلْنَهُ نُورًۭا نَّهْدِى بِهِ⟫

Thus We have revealed to you a Spirit from Our command. You knew not what the Book was, nor what faith; but We have made it a light by which We guide whom We will.[^cite-58]

Ignorant Muslims argue that the Prophet could neither read nor write, adducing the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah as proof. But consider: is it conceivable that the Teacher of all creation should be unlettered? They cite how Hazrat ʿAlī wrote at the Prophet's behest: *Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm* (In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate). When Suhail ibn ʿUmar objected—"We do not know the Most Merciful; write instead 'In Your name, O God'"—the Prophet ordered ʿAlī to alter it. ʿAlī was reluctant, saying, "O Messenger of Allah! How can I erase these letters with my own hand, having attested to you as the Messenger?" Yet the Prophet directed him: "Show me where you wrote it." When ʿAlī pointed to the words, the Prophet placed his thumb upon them and erased them, saying, "Write: Muhammad, the Messenger of Allah."

Such critics reason: If the Prophet could write, why ask to see what was written? Yet consider: when a chief executive signs a document, does he not sometimes ask his manager where to affix his signature? Does this ignorance of his reading and writing? The authority resides in the one who directs; the instrument is merely the hand that executes.[^cite-59]

> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلنَّجْمِ إِذَا هَوَىٰ ‎ ‏مَا ضَلَّ صَاحِبُكُمْ وَمَا غَوَىٰ ‎ ‏وَمَا يَنطِقُ عَنِ ٱلْهَوَىٰٓ ‎ ‏إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا وَحْىٌۭ يُوحَىٰ ‎ ‏عَلَّمَهُۥ شَدِيدُ ٱلْقُوَىٰ ‎ ‏ذُو مِرَّةٍۢ فَٱسْتَوَىٰ ‎ ‏وَهُوَ بِٱلْأُفُقِ ٱلْأَعْلَىٰ ‎ ‏ثُمَّ دَنَا فَتَدَلَّىٰ ‎ ‏فَكَانَ قَابَ قَوْسَيْنِ أَوْ أَدْنَىٰ ‎ ‏فَأَوْحَىٰٓ إِلَىٰ عَبْدِهِۦ مَآ أَوْحَىٰ ‎ ‏مَا كَذَبَ ٱلْفُؤَادُ مَا رَأَىٰٓ ‎ ‏أَفَتُمَارُونَهُۥ عَلَىٰ مَا يَرَىٰ ‎ ‏وَلَقَدْ رَآهُ نَزْلَةً أُخْرَىٰ ‎ ‏عِندَ سِدْرَةِ ٱلْمُنتَهَىٰ ‎ ‏عِندَهَا جَنَّةُ ٱلْمَأْوَىٰٓ ‎ ‏إِذْ يَغْشَى ٱلسِّدْرَةَ مَا يَغْشَىٰ ‎ ‏مَا زَاغَ ٱلْبَصَرُ وَمَا طَغَىٰ ‎ ‏لَقَدْ رَأَىٰ مِنْ آيَتِ رَبِّهِ ٱلْكُبْرَىٰٓ ‎⟫
> *By the star when it goes down, (or vanishes). Your companion (Muhammad PBUH) has neither gone astray nor has erred. Nor does he speak of (his own) desire. It is only an Inspiration that is inspired. He has been taught (this Quran) by one mighty in power [Jibrael (Gabriel)]. Dhu Mirrah (free from any defect in body and mind), Fastawa [then he (Jibrael &shy; Gabriel) rose and became stable]. [Tafsir At-Tabarî]. While he [Jibrael (Gabriel)] was in the highest part of the horizon, Then he [Jibrael (Gabriel)] approached and came closer, And was at a distance of two bows' length or (even) nearer, So did (Allah) convey the Inspiration to His slave [Muhammad PBUH through Jibrael (Gabriel) &gt;&gt;]. The (Prophet's) heart lied not (in seeing) what he (Muhammad PBUH) saw. Will you then dispute with him (Muhammad PBUH) about what he saw [during the Mi'raj: (Ascent of the Prophet PBUH over the seven heavens)] . And indeed he (Muhammad PBUH) saw him [Jibrael (Gabriel)] at a second descent (i.e. another time). Near Sidrat-ul-Muntaha [lote-tree of the utmost boundary (beyond which none can pass)], Near it is the Paradise of Abode. When that covered the lote-tree which did cover it! The sight (of Prophet Muhammad PBUH) turned not aside (right or left), nor it transgressed beyond (the) limit (ordained for it). Indeed he (Muhammad PBUH) did see, of the Greatest Signs, of his Lord (Allah).*
> By the star, when it sets, your companion does not go astray, nor does he say anything of his desire. This is but a revelation that comes to him. The mighty Gabriel has taught him, the one of great strength, so he stood still, and he was on the highest horizon, then he drew near, then he drew near, then the distance was two arcs. It was equal to or less, then He instilled in the heart of the servant of Allah, and the heart did not deny what it saw, but you disputed about what it saw, and He saw it another time at the Lotus of Lotus, with which is the Garden of Al-Maawiya, while it was overshadowing the lote-tree that overwhelmed it. He did not stray his gaze, nor did he transgress. Indeed, he saw the greatest signs of his Lord.

⟪ar-quote:سُبْحَنَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِۦ لَيْلًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْحَرَامِ إِلَى ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْأَقْصَا⟫

Exalted be He who carried His servant by night from the Sacred Mosque to the Farthest Mosque.[^cite-60] The Night Journey was appointed for the darkness of night, a sign itself: should you wish to draw near to Allah, to meet your Lord face to face, you must consecrate the night hours to Him. You must rise from your bed, abandon your devices, and grant some portion of the darkness to your God. For communion with the Divine takes place in the depths of night.

Gabriel came. The Prophet was transported to Jerusalem, and from thence to the heavens themselves. Yet observe the language the Qur'an employs. One might expect it to read: "Exalted be He who night-journeyed with His servant," or "who secretly journeyed with His servant." Instead, the Divine Word brings the letter alif with the word *asrā* (he caused to journey), and the letter bāʾ with *ʿabdihi* (His servant): ⟪ar-quote:سُبْحَنَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِۦ⟫. The bāʾ is grammatically unnecessary. Yet Allah brought both alif and bāʾ deliberately, signaling to us: When a personage of great rank approaches, do not remain seated in your chamber. Rise and go forth to greet him, stand at the threshold to welcome him. For when one you hold dear—your teacher, your beloved—arrives, you do not wait passively; you hasten to receive them. Thus the Almighty has woven both letters into the verse to teach us: "O My servants! Follow the example of your Prophet. When I summoned him to heaven, I Myself came to receive him. I did not merely send Gabriel; I did not call from afar. I came Myself to escort him." Hence: ⟪ar-quote:سُبْحَنَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِۦ⟫—Exalted be He, the One who made His servant walk through the darkness of the night. A Prophet whose honor is such that the Creator Himself came to welcome him, whose dignity is such that Allah Himself showed respect, whose station is such that the Almighty grants him miracles—you too must understand and honor him.[^cite-61]

The Prophet ascended through the heavens, stage by stage, until he reached a place of unbounded intimacy:

⟪ar-quote:‏ثُمَّ دَنَا فَتَدَلَّىٰ فَكَانَ قَابَ قَوْسَيْنِ أَوْ أَدْنَىٰ⟫

Then he drew near and descended, until he was two bow-lengths away, or even closer. Language fails. Interpretation cannot adequately convey what the heart knows. How can twenty-eight letters encompass the greatness of one whom Allah has made His Beloved, His Chosen? How can words describe the station of the one to whom the Almighty gave such honor?

These are the beings whom Allah nourished in vessels of gold and silver, who dwelled in houses of marble, who wore garments of finest silk, who reclined upon soft cushions, who rode steeds like Burāq, and for whom food descended from heaven morning and evening. Yet my Prophet's household—the home of Muhammad—lacked even fire in the hearth for two consecutive months. His most beloved daughter, Fatimah, whom he called "a part of my liver," lived in poverty alongside her husband ʿAlī and her children al-Hasan and al-Ḥusayn, destined to be the leaders of the youth of Paradise.

When the Prophet learned that Fatimah lay ill, he visited her, accompanied by the companion ʿImrān ibn al-Ḥusayn. At the door, he asked, "May I enter, O daughter?" She replied, "O Messenger of Allah, I have not even a single sheet to cover myself. How can I receive you?" The Prophet removed his own outer garment and said, "Cover yourself with this, my daughter." Then he and ʿImrān entered. When he saw her condition and learned that for three days no food had entered her house, that hunger was coupled now with sickness and no medicine available, the Prophet drew her close. He placed his chin upon her head. And tears—tears that had not come to his eyes even when stones were cast at him in the streets of Ṭāʾif, tears that had not fallen when he fasted and ate dust in the covenant of Badr—began to flow. "Daughter," he said, "be patient. For three days your father has eaten no bread, yet you find us complaining ungratefully after our five daily meals. Today is the third day I have fasted. My Lord said to me: 'O My Beloved! Shall I transform all the mountains of Arabia into gold and silver for you?' I replied: 'No, my Lord! I choose the prophecy of poverty over the prophecy of kingdoms.' Therefore know: it is sufficient for you that you are the leader of the women of Paradise."[^cite-62]

These leaders of the believers—these chiefs of the Ummah—were not found adorned with jewels, nor clothed in branded garments, nor riding in fine carriages, nor dwelling in grand mansions. Rather, they were found with stones tied to their bellies to still the pangs of hunger. They were clothed in patched robes. Yet when little Hasan and little Husayn cried from hunger, the Prophet held them to him, placed his own tongue in their mouths, and they sucked and fell asleep, finding comfort in their father's sacrifice. Through such trials came their elevation to leadership.

The Almighty said: "I have gazed upon all creation, and from it I selected the Arabs. From the Arabs, I chose the Mudrites. From the Mudrites, I singled out the Quraysh. From the Quraysh, I chose Banū Hāshim. And from Banū Hāshim, I chose myself. I am the noblest of all creation—in lineage and in character alike."[^cite-63]

I would sacrifice myself for the love of this Prophet, for the tenderness he showed you, saying: "When you supplicate Allah, ask not for hunger; ask instead for wellbeing. Say: 'O Allah, grant us good in this world and good in the Hereafter.' Yet when he raised his hands for his own children, he prayed: 'O Allah, You are the Provider of Muhammad's family. O Allah, diminish our sustenance.' Not from pride, but so that none might say on the Day of Judgment that his children lived in ease while ours starved. First his children would hunger. First the daughters of the Prophet would have their veils removed. Only then would the turn come to his Ummah. If sorrow befalls you, remember his sorrow, and your own will fade.[^cite-64]

This is Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (may God's peace be upon him), whom his Creator Himself honored! We have expelled him from our homes, from our offices, from our schools and universities, from our commerce and our politics, from every sphere—even from our hearts. How then shall our problems be solved?

No Prophet reached the station that was granted to my Messenger. His feet touched the Throne where no feet had walked before, where none shall walk again until the Day of Judgment. Infinite veils hung before the Throne of Allah. The Almighty parted them all and said: "O My Beloved, come near to Me face to face." And the greeting came forth: "Peace be upon you, O Prophet! Peace be upon you, O My Beloved!" The very Lord of all beings offered His salutation.[^cite-65]

Perfect knowledge—true and complete knowledge—is the knowledge of our Prophet. We must draw light from his wellspring. Only then shall we solve the problems of this world and the next. Otherwise, darkness will deepen, spreading like a tide, and we shall sink ever deeper into the mire. Seek the light from him who was himself the light, who came as the light itself.

⟪ar-quote:يَٓأَهْلَ ٱلْكِتَبِ قَدْ جَآءَكُمْ رَسُولُنَا يُبَيِّنُ لَكُمْ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا كُنتُمْ تُخْفُونَ مِنَ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَيَعْفُوا۟ عَن كَثِيرٍۢ ۚ قَدْ جَآءَكُم مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ نُورٌۭ وَكِتَبٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ⟫

O People of the Book! There has come to you Our Messenger, clarifying much of what you concealed from the Scripture, and forgiving much. There has come to you from Allah a Light and a clear Book.[^cite-66]

⟪ar-quote:یا أبا سفیان! جئتکم بکرامت الدنیا و الآخرۃ⟫

O Abū Sufyān! I have brought you not merely the honors of this world, but the honors of the world to come.[^cite-67]

⟪ar-quote:آنا سَیَّدُ ولد أبا آدمِ یومَ القیامۃِ ولا فخرا⟫

I am the master of all the children of Adam on the Day of Resurrection—and I say this without pride. I shall hold the banner of Allah in my hand. The keys to Paradise shall rest in my grasp. Paradise remains forbidden to all the Prophets until I enter it. Paradise is closed to all peoples until my Ummah has passed through.[^cite-68]

My beloved ones! My Prophet has brought a *way of life*—not merely a religion of five prayers daily, of fasting for one month, of paying alms once yearly, of making pilgrimage once in a lifetime. These are but minor elements. What of the remaining hours of the day? After fasting ends, what then? After alms are paid, what then? After the pilgrimage is completed, what remains? We dedicate perhaps two hours to formal worship in a span of sixteen waking hours. What of the remaining fourteen?

In the full span of twenty-four hours, my Prophet declared:

⟪ar-quote:ترکتکم علی مَحَجَّۃٍ بَیضا، لَیلُھَا کَنَھَارُھَا، لا یَزِیغُ عَنھَا الا ھالک⟫

I leave you upon a radiant path—a path upon which night and day are as one, a path from which only the destroyed shall stray.[^cite-69] Thus our Prophet has given us an entire *way* (dīn), a comprehensive system called Islam. There is but one path to success, and that path is Islam.

⟪ar-quote:وَمَن يَبْتَغِ غَيْرَ ٱلْإِسْلَمِ دِينًۭا فَلَن يُقْبَلَ مِنْهُ وَهُوَ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ مِنَ ٱلْخَسِرِينَ⟫

Whoever seeks a religion other than Islam, it shall not be accepted of him, and in the Hereafter he shall be among the losers.[^cite-70]

The first pillar of Islam is to embed in your heart the testimony: *La ilāha illā-Allāh*—There is no god but Allah. Only One God governs this universe; all matters rest in His hands alone.

⟪ar-quote:لِلَّهِ ٱلْأَمْرُ مِن قَبْلُ وَمِنۢ بَعْدُ⟫

To Allah belongs the command before and the command after.[^cite-71] Allah alone rules first; Allah alone rules last. What transpires will be as Allah wills, not as we desire. His sovereignty extends over all lands, over all heavens, over all the angels, over clouds and rain and earth itself. When the fruits ripen and the vines bear their grapes, all is under the command of my Lord. The sun and moon and stars belong to Allah—all creation obeys His rule.

Look anywhere: from east to west, from north to south—Allah reigns. Gaze upward: Allah. Gaze downward: Allah. Look before you: Allah. Look behind: Allah. Turn right: Allah. Turn left: Allah.[^cite-72]

This body of mine is fashioned from clay; yet something within me—this spirit—cries out that there exists the Divine. The birds in flight bear witness: there is God! The pearl in the shell carries the message: there is God! The fragrant musk from the navel of the deer announces: there is God! A single drop of water, entering the belly of a small fly, transforms into honey and declares: "Allah exists." That same drop, entering the mouth of a serpent, becomes poison—and testifies: "Allah exists." The turning of the seasons, the stories of empires rising and falling—all proclaim that God is. How many powerful kings have been cast upon their faces, their names and emblems erased, while Allah declared: "I alone am the Ruler." How many weak and seemingly worthless saints have been elevated to thrones of sovereignty, that all might know: "I am the One who reigns."[^cite-73]

My Lord said: "I am known through the shattering of great plans."[^cite-74] Wherever you look, there is Allah—but only if your eye is purified, cleansed of lust and covetousness. If your eye sees the forbidden, a film of sin descends upon it, like a cataract. Only through the surgeon's blade can it be healed. Pour the shame of my Prophet into your eyes as a healing balm; then truly, by Allah, you will see His glory written upon all things. You will perceive His power in every corner. The entire universe rests in His hands.

Bow before Allah! There exists no one beside Him who can create, no one who gives or withholds, no one who elevates or abases, who kills or gives life. There is no creator, no sustainer, no being upon the throne save Him alone. He brings forth the day and withdraws the night; He brightens the stars and warms the sun; He cools the moon. He alone honors and humiliates.

⟪ar-quote:قُلِ ٱللَّهُمَّ مَلِكَ ٱلْمُلْكِ تُؤْتِى ٱلْمُلْكَ مَن تَشَآءُ وَتَنزِعُ ٱلْمُلْكَ مِمَّن تَشَآءُ وَتُعِزُّ مَن تَشَآءُ وَتُذِلُّ مَن تَشَآءُ ۖ بِيَدِكَ ٱلْخَيْرُ ۖ إِنَّكَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌۭ⟫

Say: "O Allah, Master of all sovereignty! You grant sovereignty to whomsoever You will, and withdraw it from whomsoever You will. You honor whomsoever You will and abase whomsoever You will. In Your hand is all good; verily, You have power over all things."[^cite-75]

Allah pervades all; Allah alone is God. Whatever He uttered, it was, and it shall be. All that was yesterday, all that is today—Allah. This is the first proclamation of my Prophet: seek to please Allah. Attend not to gain or loss, to benefit or harm. Ask only: Is Allah pleased? Do not regard wealth or poverty, renown or obscurity. Let Allah's pleasure be your desire. Then change your values entirely. Do not love wealth; if wealth comes, it comes. But if it departs, do not let it grieve you. For Allah is not like a child who can be abandoned. Should Allah withdraw His hand from you, you lose everything. Our very first step has gone awry. A nation whose deeds become corrupt can yet be reformed. But a nation whose very foundation becomes corrupt—that is nearly impossible to repair.[^cite-76]

Today, our fundamental understanding has eroded. We have ceased to honor Allah with due reverence. We have abandoned the Prophet of Allah. We no longer give rightful place to Mawlānā ʿAlī (the Commander of the Faithful) and the Imāms. Instead, we follow our own blind guides. We heed the philosophies of Martin Luther King and Mahatma Gandhi.

⟪ar-quote:لا تستطیہ بنار المشرکین⟫

Do not take the idolaters as your advisors.[^cite-77] They will cast you into the pit. When our leader is Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā, why do we run after others? Why do those who stand before the example of the Ahl al-Bayt (the Prophet's Household), bearing the message of Karbalāʾ, wander in darkness? Why are we blind to the light before us? Why do we not extinguish the lamps that have obliterated the distinction of religion? Why do we not quench the "light" that has made us forget the worth of parents, that has stripped girls of their shame and modesty, that has them dancing before every lustful eye at our weddings? We do not need such a "civilization." If this is the light of modern civilization, then blessed be the darkness of our religion! In that darkness dwells the honor of parents; in that darkness, daughters wear the veil. In that darkness, the wealthy show generosity and the poor retain their dignity.[^cite-78]

This, then, is the foundation: O Allah, my purpose is to please You. Whatever You have granted me, I shall thank You. Whatever is taken from me, I shall bear with patience. Your pleasure is the aim of my life. This too is the message of Karbalāʾ: that one may endure hunger and thirst, may see one's children slain, may be pierced by a lance, yet never anger one's God. Yazīd made a poor bargain and has been cursed from that hour until now. But al-Ḥusayn (may Allah's peace be upon him) made a noble bargain, sacrificing himself and his family for Allah's pleasure, and mercy has rained upon him ever since.[^cite-79]

Is it easy to watch your children die before your eyes? Is it simple to see them slaughtered? Yet the children of my Prophet—already destined as leaders of Paradise—were slain. And he who could have saved everything with a single word, who could have preserved his entire household, chose instead to offer them all. For what need had they of Paradise, who had been promised it since childhood? Why then was one writhing in poison and another's head raised upon a spear?—that the message might reach us. For without this, the message would have remained incomplete. They came to teach us that everything—*everything*—can be sacrificed for Allah's pleasure. If you lose Allah, you have lost all.[^cite-80]

Do not sell your faith for the passions of power, wealth, or fleeting love. Let Allah's pleasure be your goal. Consider: if Allah is pleased with you, all is well in your life. If He is not pleased, all your happiness turns to dust. Read your history. Study the biography of your Prophet. Who succeeded? Who found God? Who failed? Who lost God? Mere tears shed on the days of Rabīʿ al-Awwal, the month of the Prophet's birth, mean nothing if your life does not change, your character does not transform. Neither processions nor public mourning on the Day of ʿĀshūrāʾ will suffice unless every moment becomes a remembrance of the Prophet, unless the message of Karbalāʾ penetrates the depths of your being.

These messages were sent so that none would barter away faith for bread, so that none would trade faith for love or fame or reputation. Know this: if my Allah does not sleep, then rest assured—you shall never find happiness in unlawfully earned wealth. And Allah does not sleep.[^cite-81]

He neither slumbers nor sleeps. There is no heaven and no earth that are not within His keeping. The one who earns lawfully shall never see autumn in his provisions, while he who earns through the forbidden shall never see spring. This is the message of my Prophet: seek God, seek God! Learn to live for Him. Learn to die for Him. Then Allah shall be yours. When Allah is angry, when His Messenger is angry, to whom shall we turn for refuge? We ourselves strike our boat with the arrows of our sins, piercing holes in our own vessel. How then can we escape drowning in the waves?[^cite-82]

The problems that plague our lives do not arise because the government is corrupt, the situation dire, or money scarce. The root cause is that Allah is displeased with us. Therefore, my Prophet has given us a message: Keep Allah before you always. What is the path to Him? Embrace the way of my Beloved, and you shall reach me. This path has two dimensions: the rights of Allah (ḥuqūq al-Allāh) and the rights of people (ḥuqūq al-ʿIbād). The rights of Allah constitute but ten percent; the rights of people comprise ninety percent.[^cite-83]

Thus has Allah set forth His truth in the Qur'an: your God has decreed that you shall not worship any save Him. One sentence—complete. Yet concerning the rights of parents, Allah revealed at length:

⟪ar-quote:وَبِٱلْوَلِدَيْنِ إِحْسَنًا ۚ إِمَّا يَبْلُغَنَّ عِندَكَ ٱلْكِبَرَ أَحَدُهُمَآ أَوْ كِلَاهُمَا فَلَا تَقُل لَّهُمَآ أُفٍّۢ وَلَا تَنْهَرْهُمَا وَقُل لَّهُمَا قَوْلًۭا كَرِيمًۭا۔ وَٱخْفِضْ لَهُمَا جَنَاحَ ٱلذُّلِّ مِنَ ٱلرَّحْمَةِ وَقُل رَّبِّ ٱرْحَمْهُمَا كَمَا رَبَّيَانِى صَغِيرًۭا⟫

And [Allah decrees] goodness toward parents. Should they attain old age with you—one or both—do not say to them a word of disrespect, nor repel them. Rather, speak to them with kind words. Lower to them the wing of humility out of mercy, and say: "My Lord, have mercy upon them as they reared me in childhood."[^cite-84]

⟪ar-quote:رَّبُّكُمْ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا فِى نُفُوسِكُمْ ۚ إِن تَكُونُوا۟ صَلِحِينَ فَإِنَّهُۥ كَانَ لِلْأَوَّبِينَ غَفُورًۭا⟫

Your Lord knows best what is in your souls. If you are righteous, He is forgiving to those who turn back to Him.[^cite-85]

In eleven detailed verses, Allah has expounded the rights of parents; in one brief verse, He has stated His own right. A long scroll unfolds describing the duties toward those we love. Our Prophets have brought solutions to every problem, both of this world and the next.[^cite-86]

The way to connect with Allah is through worship. Yet recall:

⟪ar-quote:إِن يَنصُرْكُمُ ٱللَّهُ فَلَا غَالِبَ لَكُمْ ۖ⟫

If Allah grants you victory, none can overcome you.

⟪ar-quote:وَإِن يَخْذُلْكُمْ⟫

And if He abandons you,

⟪ar-quote:فَمَن ذَا ٱلَّذِى يَنصُرُكُم مِّنۢ بَعْدِهِۦ⟫

who can help you after Him?[^cite-87]

Fallen nations always blame others for their ruin. "It is America's conspiracy; it is England's plot; it is the fault of those scholars and teachers; it is the parents' doing." But tell me: when mothers are disrespected within our own communities, is this a foreign conspiracy? When immodesty and shamelessness flourish among us, is this an English plot? When teachers exploit their students, when the powerful oppress the weak, when loans are obtained by lies within the community itself, when investments are made through fraud—whose conspiracy is this? We are responsible. We have destroyed ourselves.[^cite-88] The sailors do not drown because of the waves; they drown when they take an axe to their own vessel's hull. The ship sinks when those aboard fire arrows at their own sails.

So do not speak of it; rather, ask yourself: *Why was the caravan looted?* I do not blame the robbers—I blame the caravan's guide. Our moral decay has destroyed us, not America. We abandoned the principles of Islam ourselves; therefore we have fallen. We expelled the Holy Prophet, Mawlānā ʿAlī, and the Imāms from our lives—not the Jews or Christians, but we ourselves. We have become slaves to our egos. None forgive one another; all are enslaved to their desires, unwilling to sacrifice the pleasures of this world.[^cite-89]

This is the utterance of my Prophet: Three things, should they spread within a nation, will cause its self-destruction. There is no need for foreign conspiracy or military strikes.

First: **Tyranny** (ẓulm). We are all oppressors. Even to ourselves—we commit injustice against our own souls. We know we are sinning, that we walk toward the Fire, yet we persist.

Second: **Falsehood** (kadhib). Out of a hundred people, nearly all lie. None can truthfully claim, "I never lie."

Third: **Betrayal** (khiyāna). We betray our religion. The Messenger of Allah left us two precious things: the Qur'ān and his exemplary way (Sunnah). Yet we have abandoned both. How many of us read the Qur'ān? We master the world's most difficult languages yet cannot be bothered to learn the language of our Prophet, nor do we wish to.[^cite-90]

Why would you need another to drown you? You are drowning yourself.

Repent from oppression. Repent from lies. Repent from betrayal and deceit. Protect yourself from your own hands before you fear the hands of others. Do not wrong yourself; this earthen body cannot withstand the heat of hellfire. Do not commit deeds that will consign you to the Fire.

⟪ar-quote:الا لعنت اللہ علی الظالمین⟫

Cursed are the wrongdoers.[^cite-91]

Guard yourself from falsehood; do not lie to yourself. Do not rationalize open disobedience to Allah. When you gaze upon the forbidden, you know what you do. When your eyes fall upon a stranger with lustful intent, you know the nature of your act. When you give a pittance to the poor—enough for a single meager meal—do not deceive yourself that you are being generous. When your body stands in prayer while your mind wanders the marketplace, do not pretend that you are praying. My Prophet declared:

⟪ar-quote:الصدق ینجی و الکذب یھلک⟫

Truth saves; falsehood destroys.

⟪ar-quote:الا لعنت اللہ علی الکاذبین⟫

Cursed are the liars.[^cite-92]

Do not betray the trust of your Prophet's religion. When the Messenger of Allah was asked, "Can a Muslim be a coward?" he replied, "Yes, but he will not lie; he will not deceive." Consider the example of Jarīr ibn ʿAbdullāh. He instructed his servant to purchase a horse from the market. The servant returned with an Arabian steed purchased for three hundred dirhams. When Jarīr examined it, he saw it was extraordinary—the owner himself had been ignorant of its true value. The transaction was entirely lawful; one may sell for ten or for one hundred. Yet Jarīr, a chief and a connoisseur of horses, seeing that the horse had been acquired cheaply, approached the owner: "If I give you four hundred, would you object?" "Not at all," replied the man. "What of five hundred?" "No." "Six hundred?" "No." "Seven hundred?" "No." "Eight hundred?" At last the owner asked, "What profit do you seek by raising the price?" Jarīr answered, "Brother, this horse is worth eight hundred dirhams, not three hundred. I have given my hand to the Messenger of Allah and sworn: 'As long as I live, I will not defraud anyone.'" He then instructed his servant, "Give him eight hundred and bring the horse."[^cite-93]

When our brother falls into hardship, we exploit his desperation. We bargain ruthlessly for something worth ten thousand, offering five hundred. Instead, if we see a needy person, we should ask: "Can I help? Can I lighten your burden?" Yet we ask: "Is the rooster defective? Is the goat lame?"—seeking faults to drive down the price further.

What have we done with the covenant we made, placing our hand in the hand of the Prophet on the Day of Covenant? The Ummah that was taught generosity, compassion, and sacrifice has become a band of robbers. Teachers become thieves, merchants become thieves, lawyers and doctors all become robbers.[^cite-94]

The Prophet once said: "When mothers lose their honor and dignity, the Hour will draw near." He did not link the Hour to disbelief or shirk (idolatry), not to adultery or intoxication—but to the humiliation of mothers. This is how fundamental filial respect is to the faith.[^cite-95]

Abandon negative thinking; eschew resentment and bitterness. Embrace positive thought instead. Life is short—why harbor enmity, why wage battles? If coal endures heat and pressure, it transforms into diamond. So too shall you. If we learn to bear hardship patiently, to endure for Allah's pleasure, then Allah will transform us into gems and grant us a place among those closest to Him.

Let me give you a summary of the entire religion:

**Control your tongue.**

Master your speech. Every defect will be remedied if you do. It is easy to stop a speeding truck; the tongue is far harder to brake. It is simple to calm storms; controlling speech is arduous. The whole of religion hinges upon your words. A kind word is sweeter than all the honey of creation. A harsh word is more bitter than all the poison of the universe. A gentle word heals great wounds; bitter speech inflicts wounds that death itself cannot soothe. Learn to rule your language. Answer unkindness with silence; meet abuse with prayer; respond to backbiting with praise. Then, by Allah, Allah's shade will be upon you.[^cite-96]

Remove from your tongue all abuse, all backbiting, all falsehood, gossip, complaint, and accusation. The Prophet of Allah said: "Those who quarrel are as filthy in Allah's sight as excrement." Sweeten your words. Luqmān the Wise said to his son: "My child, you have never regretted your silence, but you have regretted your words."[^cite-97]

Abū Dajānah was asked, "What is the greatest act of worship in Allah's sight?" He replied:

⟪ar-quote:ما أٓذیتُ أحداً بی لِسَانِی! و لا أجِدُ فی قلبی غلاًّ لی احد⟫

I have never harmed anyone with this tongue of mine, nor do I harbor any grudge against any soul.[^cite-98]

Do not burden yourself with excessive prayers if you neglect your duties. But rid yourself of lies, oppression, betrayal, and bitter speech. Even a few prostrations will suffice if your heart is sincere. In the religion of my Prophet, forgiveness and kind words and comforting a grieving heart carry rewards that cannot be matched by nights of worship. Anger is the key to every evil; gentleness opens the door to every good.[^cite-99]

Islam must work for itself; the mill must grind by its own motion.[^cite-100]

The purpose of knowledge has always been to awaken consciousness. Knowledge is not meant for earning bread or a living; it is meant to open the windows of the mind, to unravel the mysteries of creation. Yet we have turned knowledge into a commodity, acquiring it merely for degrees and certificates. Create a connection with knowledge itself. Learn for the sake of learning.

We carve our own milestones; we are not shaped by time. Bring your learning to such a point that people come to you seeking wisdom. It was knowledge that elevated Ādam before the angels and caused the angels to bow down to him.[^cite-101] Ādam attained his nobility and honor through knowledge alone. Be devoted to learning; respect those from whom you learn, for disrespect closes the gates of knowledge. Mawlānā ʿAlī declared: "Whoever teaches me a single letter has made me his slave—even if he sells me in the marketplace."[^cite-102] Thus, the most learned of all, second only to the Prophet, taught us:

**Respect those you learn from.**

There is a city of darkness, a king, a guide. In that city dwell the ignorant and the wise. Find the guide and follow him; avoid the darkness of ignorance.

Pray for your parents; do not merely pray *to* them, but pray *for* them. Honor your spouse; love her. You have brought another's daughter into your home. Do not greet those who brought her to you with ingratitude. Train your children—not with blows, not with mockery, not with humiliation. That will shatter their spirit. My Prophet said:

⟪ar-quote:اکرمو اولادکُم⟫

Honor your children.[^cite-103] Admonishment is necessary, but it is not humiliation. Parents must counsel with compassion; teachers must nurture with mercy. Pupils should sit at the feet of their teachers with reverence.[^cite-104]

Treat your siblings with affection. Let the verses of the Qur'ān echo in your homes—not music and songs. Ensure none in your household remains ignorant of prayer. Bring the life of the Prophet and his Household into your homes: a life of love, morality, forgiveness. If you honor your wife, your children will honor their mother. But if you dishonor your spouse, how will your children learn to respect theirs?[^cite-105]

The Prophet declared: "The best among Muslims are those who are best to their wives." Yet he also told his wives: "Were it not forbidden, I would have commanded you to prostrate before your husbands." Hold the balance between these two principles.

The way to heal society is not to fix society first—it is to heal yourself. When Asif Hussain becomes whole, all shall improve around him. When you change, the society will change. When you become righteous, righteousness spreads.[^cite-106]

## The End of Those Who Abandon Prayer

At present, all the people of the world—whether Muslim or non-Muslim—are absorbed in the pursuit of their desires. Whatever the heart craves, they follow without restraint. The constitution of our lives has become the fruit of three centuries of labor devoted to vanity. The fear of Allah, His Messenger, and the Hereafter has either vanished from our hearts or grown so dim as to be imperceptible.

⟪ar-quote:يَعْلَمُونَ ظَهِرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَهُمْ عَنِ ٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ غَفِلُونَ⟫

They know only the outward life of this world and remain heedless of the Hereafter. These few days of earthly life are deemed good enough; what comes after death is forgotten entirely. Yet this universe belongs to Allah. The earth and the heavens belong to Allah, and the Throne belongs to Him alone. What Allah wills shall come to pass—not what the kings of the world desire.

⟪ar-quote:وَلِلَّهِ غَيْبُ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَإِلَيْهِ يُرْجَعُ ٱلْأَمْرُ كُلُّهُۥ فَٱعْبُدْهُ وَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَيْهِ ۚ وَمَا رَبُّكَ بِغَافِلٍ عَمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ⟫

His dominion knows no decline. ⟪ar-quote:كُلَّ يَوْمٍ هُوَ فِى شَأْنٍۢ⟫ He was before all things and remains after all things. ⟪ar-quote:من قبل⟫ He was, and ⟪ar-quote:وہ من بعد⟫ He will be. He has no partner and no equal:

⟪ar-quote:مَا ٱتَّخَذَ ٱللَّهُ مِن وَلَدٍۢ وَمَا كَانَ مَعَهُۥ مِنْ إِلَهٍ ۚ إِذًۭا لَّذَهَبَ كُلُّ إِلَهٍۭ بِمَا خَلَقَ وَلَعَلَا بَعْضُهُمْ عَلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ ۚ سُبْحَنَ ٱللَّهِ عَمَّا يَصِفُونَ⟫

He has no partner in dominion, no children, and no protector from humiliation:

⟪ar-quote:وَقُلِ ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ ٱلَّذِى لَمْ يَتَّخِذْ وَلَدًۭا وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ شَرِيكٌۭ فِى ٱلْمُلْكِ وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ وَلِىٌّۭ مِّنَ ٱلذُّلِّ ۖ وَكَبِّرْهُ تَكْبِيرًۢا⟫

He was not born of any, nor did He beget any. ⟪ar-quote:لم یلد ولم یولد⟫

He created the whole universe, free from self-origination. He sustains all things yet is free from death. Perfect in His will, He does as He wills: ⟪ar-quote:ما شاء اللہ کان⟫ What He wills comes to be. ⟪ar-quote:وما لم یشاء لا یکن⟫ What He wills not shall not be. ⟪ar-quote:وَمَا تَشَآءُونَ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ رَبُّ ٱلْعَلَمِينَ⟫ You desire nothing except by the will of Allah, Lord of all worlds. You are entirely dependent upon Allah, and your desires are fulfilled only when Allah decrees.

Consider Pharaoh, whose strength was such that he determined to slay Moses. Yet it was Allah's will that Moses should live. Pharaoh slaughtered so many children that his court grew anxious, saying, "If you kill all the children, who will serve your kingdom?" Thus he alternated: one year he spared the children; the next year he slaughtered them. Aaron was born in the year of mercy; Moses was born in the year of the sword. Had Allah willed, He could have created Moses in the year of mercy, but God chose to demonstrate: *This plan is Mine. I am the Lord. I am the King. Sovereignty belongs to Me alone.*[^cite-74]

Then Allah cast Moses into the waters and brought him to Pharaoh's very court. Moses' mother grieved, fearing he would drown or perish. But Allah said to her: ⟪ar-quote:فَإِذَا خِفْتِ عَلَيْهِ فَأَلْقِيهِ فِى ٱلْيَمِّ⟫ "When you fear for him, cast him into the river." Where would the river carry him? ⟪ar-quote:فَلْيُلْقِهِ ٱلْيَمُّ بِٱلسَّاحِلِ⟫ "The river shall cast him upon the shore." What then? ⟪ar-quote:يَأْخُذْهُ عَدُوٌّۭ لِّى⟫ "An enemy of Mine shall take him"—⟪ar-quote:وَعَدُوٌّۭ لَّهُۥ⟫ his enemy shall take him. Moses' mother's heart trembled: "O Allah! Whoever I try to protect him from will seize him—how can he be saved? He will kill my son the moment he sees him." But Allah said: ⟪ar-quote:وَلَا تَخَافِى وَلَا تَحْزَنِىٓ⟫ "Grieve not, nor fear his death or separation." ⟪ar-quote:إِنَّا رَآدُّوهُ إِلَيْكِ⟫ "We shall return him to your arms." ⟪ar-quote:وَجَاعِلُوهُ مِنَ ٱلْمُرْسَلِينَ⟫ "And We shall make him among the Messengers." She could not die until she witnessed these two promises—that her son returned to her embrace and became a Prophet of Allah.[^cite-75]

Whether he fell into Pharaoh's lap, descended into flames, or was engulfed by tempestuous waves, every force in the universe served his protection when it was Allah's will. When Allah intends to kill, those very instruments of protection become instruments of death. When He wills honor, the causes of humiliation are turned to honor. When He decrees humiliation, even causes of honor bring shame. When He creates love, enemies become devoted. When He creates enmity, closest friends become adversaries. The sovereignty of this universe belongs to Allah alone. Whatever Allah wills shall come to pass.

Consider Nimrod, whose might seemed sufficient to cast Abraham into the fire. Wood was gathered, flames were ignited—a fire so intense that birds flying overhead were reduced to ash before reaching the ground. When the moment came to throw Abraham into the flames, how could one approach? An ingenious device was fashioned—a catapult mechanism. Ropes bound Abraham, and he was hurled skyward. As Abraham flew through the air, Gabriel came to his right and the Angel of Water to his left, both accompanying him. Abraham remained silent. They repeated: *Hasbi Allah wa Na'am al-Wakil; Hasbi Allah wa Na'am al-Wakil*—"Allah suffices me, and He is the Best of Trustees." The Angel of Water awaited Abraham's command to extinguish the fire. Gabriel awaited his instruction. Yet Abraham spoke no word. Seeing his silence, the angels grew anxious, fearing they would perish in the flames—for they too know fire consumes. They cried: "O Abraham! What do you require?" He answered: ⟪ar-quote:أمَّا الَیک فَلا⟫ "I have no need of you." ⟪ar-quote:أما الی اللہ فنعم!⟫ "But yes, I am in need of Allah!"[^cite-76]

Abraham descended toward the inferno, neither turning to Gabriel nor to the Angel of Water, his gaze fixed upon his Lord alone. Then Allah commanded directly, not through the angels: ⟪ar-quote:يَنَارُ كُونِى بَرْدًۭا وَسَلَمًا عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ⟫ "O fire, become cool and safe upon Abraham!" The fire, obedient to her Master, rushed forward and embraced Abraham as a mother embraces her child, laying him gently upon the ground as a mother lays her infant upon a bed. When Abraham's father Azar—who had been his mortal enemy and sought his death—witnessed this, his mouth involuntarily uttered: ⟪ar-quote:نعمَ الرَّبُّ رَبُّک یا اِبرٰھیم!⟫ "Blessed is your Lord, O Abraham!"

My children, know this: all that exists in this universe belongs to Allah. It is wrought of His creation. Command belongs to Allah alone. What He says to the earth, the earth does. What He says to water, it obeys. What He says to fire, it complies. What He says to the heavens, they answer. The entire cosmos belongs to Allah, and the judgment of the Hereafter belongs to Him alone. Not even the combined powers of the world can alter Allah's decree.

When people's deeds become corrupt, Allah corrupts their circumstances as well—destroying both this world and the world to come. Wealth and riches have honored no one in Allah's sight before, nor shall they today, nor ever shall they in the future. The power of the world has made no one successful before Allah, nor can it today, nor will it ever. Herein lies today's grave deception: we pursue our desires heedlessly, ignorant of Allah's law and what pleases or angers Him, of whom He forgives and whom He punishes. This is why humanity stands in extreme peril.

All the deeds that incur Allah's wrath are happening within our homes today, yet we sleep so soundly that we do not know that what we do displeases Allah. The whole world today challenges Allah with its great sins, filling the earth with transgression.

⟪ar-quote:ظَهَرَ ٱلْفَسَادُ فِى ٱلْبَرِّ وَٱلْبَحْرِ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ أَيْدِى ٱلنَّاسِ⟫

Allah says through the Qur'an's eternal law: "Hear what I did to peoples like you who committed deeds like yours! Heed the people of Noah who came before. They filled the earth with disbelief. Yet when My Prophet warned them, they mocked: ⟪ar-quote:فاَءتِنَا بِمَا تَعِدُنَآ إِن كُنتَ مِنَ الصادقین⟫ 'Bring upon us the punishment you threaten, if you speak the truth.'" Then came the Day: ⟪ar-quote:وَفَجَّرْنَا ٱلْأَرْضَ عُيُونًۭا فَٱلْتَقَى ٱلْمَآءُ عَلَىٰٓ أَمْرٍۢ قَدْ قُدِرَ⟫ "We burst forth the earth's springs, and the waters met by Our decree." Notice—He did not say He drew water from the earth, but rather ⟪ar-quote:وَفَجَّرْنَا ٱلْأَرْضَ عُيُونًۭا⟫ "We made the whole earth pour forth water." From every corner of earth, water surged, and from the sky water poured down. The deluge spread across creation. The entire nation of Noah was destroyed, including his own son, before their very eyes:

⟪ar-quote:قَالَ سَـَٔاوِىٓ إِلَىٰ جَبَلٍۢ يَعْصِمُنِى مِنَ ٱلْمَآءِ ۚ قَالَ لَا عَاصِمَ ٱلْيَوْمَ مِنْ أَمْرِ ٱللَّهِ إِلَّا مَن رَّحِمَ ۚ وَحَالَ بَيْنَهُمَا ٱلْمَوْجُ فَكَانَ مِنَ ٱلْمُغْرَقِينَ⟫

His son cried, "I will flee to a mountain for safety!" Noah replied, "No refuge exists today from Allah's command except for those upon whom He shows mercy." A wave came between them, and the boy was drowned before Noah's eyes.

All the deeds that Noah's people performed are being done today—among Muslims, in our communities, throughout the world.

Then came 'Ād, the people of Hud, who were mighty and arrogant. They cried out in defiance:

⟪ar-quote:وَقَالُوا۟ مَنْ أَشَدُّ مِنَّا قُوَّةً⟫

"Who is mightier than we? Produce him before us! Who threatens us? Is anyone stronger?" They said to Hud:

⟪ar-quote:إِن نَّقُولُ إِلَّا ٱعْتَرَىٰكَ بَعْضُ آلِهَتِنَا بِسُوٓء⟫

"Our gods have afflicted your mind." But Allah responded:

⟪ar-quote:أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا۟ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَهُمْ هُوَ أَشَدُّ مِنْهُمْ قُوَّةًۭ⟫

"Do they not see that the God who created them is mightier than they?" When the proof from Allah was complete, they grew ever more arrogant, persisting in rebellion. Allah sent upon them first a famine—hunger and terrible thirst. They pleaded with Hud, "Ask your Lord to send us water." Yet some refused obedience still. Then Allah sent a black cloud:

⟪ar-quote:فَلَمَّا رَأَوْهُ عَارِضًۭا مُّسْتَقْبِلَ أَوْدِيَتِهِمْ قَالُوا۟ هَذَا عَارِضٌۭ مُّمْطِرُنَا ۚ⟫

"When they saw it approaching their valleys, they said, 'This cloud brings rain!'" But Allah said:

⟪ar-quote:بَلْ هُوَ مَا ٱسْتَعْجَلْتُم بِهِۦ ۖ⟫

"No! This is the torment you demanded of My Prophet Hud, claiming, 'Who is mightier than we to harm us?'" Now prepare yourselves:

⟪ar-quote:رِيحٌۭ فِيهَا عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۭ⟫ ⟪ar-quote:تُدَمِّرُ كُلَّ شَىْءٍۭ بِأَمْرِ رَبِّهَا⟫

"A wind carrying grievous punishment, destroying all things by their Lord's command!" The wind swept them away; their homes flew like straw through the air. Their heads collided violently. Skulls burst, brains melted. Then Allah lifted them and hurled them to the ground. Heads were severed from bodies, limbs scattered. Then Allah cried out:

⟪ar-quote:فَهَلْ تَرَىٰ لَهُم مِّنۢ بَاقِيَةٍۢ⟫

"Do you see any of them remaining?"

⟪ar-quote:كَذَلِكَ نَجْزِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلْمُجْرِمِينَ۔⟫

"Thus do We punish the criminals." Today we commit the same deeds 'Ād committed, repeating them daily.

### Prayer's Protection

Then came the people of Thamud. Having heard that a wind destroyed 'Ād, they carved dwellings into mountains, fashioning caves where they settled, believing the wind could not reach them in such depths. They plotted against Allah:

⟪ar-quote:وَمَكَرُوا۟ مَكْرًۭا وَمَكَرْنَا مَكْرًۭا۔ فَٱنظُرْ كَيْفَ كَانَ عَقِبَةُ مَكْرِهِمْ⟫

"They plotted, and We plotted. Behold the outcome of their cunning!"

⟪ar-quote:فَتِلْكَ بُيُوتُهُمْ خَاوِيَةًۢ بِمَا ظَلَمُوٓا۟ ۗ إِنَّ فِى ذَلِكَ لَآيَةًۭ لِّقَوْمٍۢ يَعْلَمُونَ۔ وَأَنجَيْنَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَكَانُوا۟ يَتَّقُونَ⟫

"Their dwellings stand empty because of their wrong. In that is surely a sign for a people who know. And We saved those who believed and feared."  An angel came and uttered a cry. That single scream burst their livers, melted their brains, blackened their faces. The entire people perished from one cry.

### Deception in Trade

Then came the people of Shu'ayb (peace be upon him), in an age when commerce flourished and merchants ruled. They engaged in bribery, usury, and deception. Shu'ayb implored them:

⟪ar-quote:وَأَوْفُوا۟ ٱلْكَيْلَ إِذَا كِلْتُمْ وَزِنُوا۟ بِٱلْقِسْطَاسِ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمِ⟫

"Give full measure when you weigh, and weigh with an honest scale." But they replied:

⟪ar-quote:قَالُوا۟ يَشُعَيْبُ أَصَلَوٰتُكَ تَأْمُرُكَ أَن نَّتْرُكَ مَا يَعْبُدُ آبَآؤُنَآ أَوْ أَن نَّفْعَلَ فِىٓ أَمْوَلِنَا مَا نَشَٓؤُا۟⟫

"Shu'ayb, does your prayer command you to abandon what our fathers worshipped, or to conduct our wealth as we please?" They said, "Sit quietly in your mosque. Pray in silence. Do not interfere with our business. Does prayer forbid the ways of our ancestors? If we conduct commerce your way, we shall starve!" Sit in your mosque and do not advise us.

Today, if one asks a merchant to trade honestly, he responds: "Then I cannot pay the electric bill. If I do not reduce the measure, how will business survive? Without deception and fraud, how do I conduct trade? Without manipulation and bribery, how do I profit? Without usury, how do I run my business?"—all the objections made to Shu'ayb (peace be upon him), merchants still make.

Yet we have not come to run businesses in this world. We have come to please Allah. If trade halts through obedience to Allah, glory be to Allah! When business prospers, praise be to Allah! We do not believe we exist merely to fill our bellies and feed our children. We believe we must earn according to Allah's command and spend accordingly. Whatever remains, we consume and feed our families. If nothing remains, we fast, and our children fast. We cannot anger Allah for our children's sake. We cannot bear the fire of Hell for their sake.

Umar ibn 'Abd al-'Aziz ruled with justice, yet lived in poverty. When death approached, he gathered his children and said: "My children, two paths lay before me. The first: to amass wealth for you, whether lawful or unlawful, knowing I would enter Hell for it. The second: to teach you piety, to trust in Allah, to please Him. My children, I, your father, cannot endure Hell's fire. I have not fed you unlawful sustenance, nor have I gathered forbidden wealth for you. I have shown you the path of piety. When you need anything, call upon my Lord. It is His promise: ⟪ar-quote:وَهُوَ يَتَوَلَّى ٱلصَّلِحِينَ⟫ 'He befriends the righteous.' If you follow the path of piety, Allah will grant you success. If you abandon it, I will not grieve for your deaths."[^cite-77]

And so it came to pass—governors of that era who had gathered haram (forbidden) wealth, who ate from golden vessels and clothed their children in finery, fell to sitting upon mosque steps begging for alms. Yet the children of Umar ibn 'Abd al-'Aziz became so prosperous that they distributed one hundred horses in charity in a single gathering.

We must understand: we are Muslims first—merchants, officers, students, and parents only thereafter. A wife's husband is secondary; a child's father is secondary. We are commanded to sacrifice all for Allah's pleasure. We are not commanded to sacrifice Allah's command for our desires. We are commanded to sacrifice our desires for Allah's command.

The people of Shu'ayb mocked: "How will trade proceed? Where will bread come from?" They were silent and would not heed. Allah punished them with three afflictions:

⟪ar-quote:فَأَخَذَتْهُمُ ٱلرَّجْفَةُ فَأَصْبَحُوا۟ فِى دَارِهِمْ جَثِمِينَ⟫

An earthquake struck them.

⟪ar-quote:وَأَخَذَ ٱلَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا۟ ٱلصَّيْحَةُ فَأَصْبَحُوا۟ فِى دِيَرِهِمْ جَثِمِينَ⟫

A scream seized them.

⟪ar-quote:أَخَذَهُمْ عَذَابُ يَوْمِ ٱلظُّلَّةِ⟫

The rain of embers consumed them. Allah burned the entire people of Shu'ayb and the markets of Midian.

⟪ar-quote:ولا یَبِیثَنَّ أُناسٌ مِن أٌمَّتِی عَلی أَشَرٍ و بَتَرٍ و لحوٍ و لَعبٍ فَیُسبِحُو قَد مُسِقُو قِرَدَۃً و خَنازِیر بِسْتِحلالِھِمُ المَحاَرِم وَ أَکلِھِمُ الرِّباَء وَ لُبسِھِمُ الحَرِیرْ و شُربِھِمُ الخمُور  وَ تِّخٰذِھِمُ الخَیناَت وَ قَطِیعاَتِھِمُ الرَّحَم⟫

"There shall come a time when certain people of my Ummah sink into extravagance, arrogance, amusement, and frivolity—singing and dancing, listening to music—until one night they sleep, and when they wake in the morning, they will be transformed into apes and swine. This occurs because they permit what is forbidden: ⟪ar-quote:بِسْتِحلالِھِمُ المَحاَرِم⟫ ⟪ar-quote:وَ أَکلِھِمُ الرِّباَء⟫ They consume usury. ⟪ar-quote:وَ لُبسِھِمُ الحَرِیرْ⟫ Men wear silk garments, gold rings, and gold chains. ⟪ar-quote:و شُربِھِمُ الخمُور⟫ They drink wine. ⟪ar-quote:وَ تِّخٰذِھِمُ الخَیناَت⟫ They sing and dance. ⟪ar-quote:وَ قَطِیعاَتِھِمُ الرَّحَم⟫ They sever ties of kinship. When all this transpires, Allah will surely transform them into apes and swine.

This is the saying of the Prophet—he who wept thousands of times in grief for his Ummah, who sobbed so intensely that Allah Himself consoled him, asking why he grieved so:

⟪ar-quote:لَعَلَّكَ بَخِعٌۭ نَّفْسَكَ أَلَّا يَكُونُوا۟ مُؤْمِنِينَ⟫

⟪ar-quote:فَلَعَلَّكَ بَخِعٌۭ نَّفْسَكَ عَلَىٰٓ آثَرِهِمْ إِن لَّمْ يُؤْمِنُوا۟ بِهَذَا ٱلْحَدِيثِ أَسَفًا⟫

⟪ar-quote:فَلَا تَذْهَبْ نَفْسُكَ عَلَيْهِمْ حَسَرَتٍۚ⟫

⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ أَنتَ عَلَيْهِم بِوَكِيلٍۢ⟫

⟪ar-quote:لَّسْتَ عَلَيْهِم بِمُصَيْطِر⟫

⟪ar-quote:نَّحْنُ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ ۖ وَمَآ أَنتَ عَلَيْهِم بِجَبَّارٍۢ⟫

⟪ar-quote:فَلَا يَحْزُنكَ كُفْرُهُ⟫

⟪ar-quote:وَلَقَدْ نَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ يَضِيقُ صَدْرُكَ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ⟫

Yet hear from the Qur'an the fate of these disobedient ones. Those who died committing grave sins—those who drank wine and abandoned prayer—the Messenger of Allah shall point to them on the Day of Resurrection and say:

⟪ar-quote:یا ربِّ أِنَّ قَومِ التَّخذُو ھٰذا القُرآنَ مَحجُورَا⟫

"My Lord, my people took this Qur'an as something to ignore."

Tell me—those who pray only on Fridays, one day in eight, and count themselves Muslims: do they dwell on Allah's earth only one day in eight? Do they consume Allah's sustenance only one day in eight? Do they sleep peacefully under Allah's protection only one day in eight? Do they drink the water Allah gives only one day in eight? Do they breathe the air Allah provides only one day in eight? Do they see Allah's light only one day in eight? Do they hear with ears Allah fashioned only one day in eight? Do they benefit from their bodies' faculties only one day in eight? Do they enjoy the sun's warmth only one day in eight? Do they feel the moon's glow only one day in eight? Do they sit with wives and children only one day in eight?

What has become of their hearts, turned to stone? Why does Allah's message not reach them? Why are they so desolate? Even stones are not so hard. 'Attā'llāh Shāh Bukhārī once said, "O people of India, I have recited so much Qur'an to you that had I recited it to a mountain, it would have become water! Had I recited it to stones, they would have wept! Had I recited it to rivers, they would have stopped their flow! Had I recited it to the seas, they would have become sweet! I do not know what our hearts are made of, what substance composes them. Our breasts do not contain hearts—they contain stones, yet harder than stones:

⟪ar-quote:ثُمَّ قَسَتْ قُلُوبُكُم مِّنۢ بَعْدِ ذَلِكَ فَهِىَ كَٱلْحِجَارَةِ أَوْ أَشَدُّ قَسْوَةًۭ ۚ وَإِنَّ مِنَ ٱلْحِجَارَةِ لَمَا يَتَفَجَّرُ مِنْهُ ٱلْأَنْهَرُ⟫

Yet what stone are our hearts fashioned from, that the Great King calls us five times: *"Hayya 'ala al-salāh, hayya 'ala al-salāh"*—"Come to prayer, come to prayer"—and we do not even glance backward? We ignore such a summons? Call a petty police officer; summon a court judge; call a school principal to his office; summon a government official to his chamber—and all work ceases. We rush to answer. Yet let the King of heaven and earth call us five times daily, and His voice does not reach our ears. We come to the mosque once a week, one day in eight! Have you eaten on that one day alone? Do you drink water only then?

We show such infidelity—to ourselves, to the Devil, to our shops, to our business—and yet claim fidelity to Allah? Is there a greater crime than to inhabit a land, to prostrate upon it, yet refuse to prostrate before the One it worships? Adultery is sin; drinking is sin; but abandoning prayer is a greater sin than adultery! Abandoning prayer is a greater sin than intoxication. Murder is grave; abandoning prayer is graver still.

What sin did Satan commit? What wine did he drink? Whom did he murder? Consider what he actually did: he refused to prostrate—merely to prostrate, not a thousand times, just one act of refusal! For that single refusal to bow, he was cast out until the Day of Judgment and cursed forever. Yet we refuse five prostrations daily, perhaps more, and then eat comfortably, sleep soundly on soft beds, sit contentedly with friends, laugh and converse freely.

Has Allah punished Satan for this crime alone and forgiven us? Will He punish him for non-prostration and release us unpunished?

Why do we not fear? "I cannot wake so early!" "It is too hot!" "It is too cold!" "It is too dark!" Do you not remember the resurrection from the grave? Do you not remember the grave's heat? Do you not remember the grave's darkness? Has Hell's fire been extinguished? Have Paradise's blessings vanished? We have forgotten all! We have forgotten Paradise's delight, forgotten Hell's torment, forgotten Allah's sight, forgotten the Prophet's vision, forgotten 'Alī and the Imāms.

This treachery toward Allah is such that the sea cries out: "O Allah, permit me to drown them all!" The earth says, "O Allah, permit me to swallow them all!" The angels say, "O Allah, permit us to strike them down!" And the one whose prostration is denied, whose Prophet's law is mocked, toward whom Allah's own jealousy burns—these are made of My hands, created from clay, fashioned from filthy water and impure seed, alive by My sustenance, alive by My breath, alive by the system I have given, yet they deny Me.

Who created such grand existence! He illumined their eyes, fashioned their ears with delicate instruments, strengthened their brains, empowered their hands, set their feet in motion, gave their hearts rhythm, placed speech upon their tongues, circulated blood throughout their bodies, installed various systems within them, hardened their bones, softened their muscles, granted them strength to move their jaws and grind their teeth. He placed two ducts about the throat, separating breath from food, creating a protective veil. When food enters the mouth, the airway closes. Even when wine is drunk, Allah does not alter this system—it is a mercy. He could, in a single instant, guide wine into the breathing passage; one cough and death would follow. But Allah protects us by His mercy. What a system He has created!

Each day, dead cells shed as nails. If we cut a nail slightly too short, we spend the whole day sighing in discomfort—"Ah, ah!" We cannot even thank Allah for a fingernail. What gratitude could we offer if He withdrew the joints that bend and straighten? We cannot thank even a joint!

How dare we rebel against the One who has done all this for us! Does any logic support such rebellion? If a servant earning only 1,500 rupees fails to bring us tea on time, we curse his mother and sister, evict him from the house—yet we rebel against the Master who has given us so much?

⟪ar-quote:وَٱلْأَرْضَ وَضَعَهَا لِلْأَنَامِ۔ فِيهَا فَكِهَةٌۭ وَٱلنَّخْلُ ذَاتُ ٱلْأَكْمَامِ ۔ وَٱلْحَبُّ ذُو ٱلْعَصْفِ وَٱلرَّيْحَانُ ۔ فَبِأَىِّ آلَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ⟫

"I have laid out the earth for you, and in it I have placed fruits and palms with sheaths, grain with husk and fragrance, that you may eat, drink, and rejoice. Then I brought forth flowers and their perfume for your pleasure. Then why do you deny My blessings?" Do you not see?

⟪ar-quote:رَبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقَيْنِ وَرَبُّ ٱلْمَغْرِبَيْنِ⟫

"I am the Lord of the two easts and the two wests"—the Lord of your every sunrise and sunset.

⟪ar-quote:فَبِأَىِّ آلَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ⟫

"Then why do you deny My blessings?"

⟪ar-quote:خَلَقَ ٱلْإِنسَنَ مِن صَلْصَلٍۢ كَٱلْفَخَّارِ۔ وَخَلَقَ ٱلْجَآنَّ مِن مَّارِجٍۢ مِّن نَّارٍۢ۔ فَبِأَىِّ آلَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ⟫

"I created humanity from clay like pottery, and jinn from flames of fire. Then why do you deny My blessings?"

Do you not see that every day your Lord is engaged in a new matter?

⟪ar-quote:كُلَّ يَوْمٍ هُوَ فِى شَأْنٍۢ⟫

⟪ar-quote:فَبِأَىِّ آلَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ⟫

"Then why do you deny My blessings?" If you do not hear these words of Mine, if you do not believe them, then know:

⟪ar-quote:سَنَفْرُغُ لَكُمْ أَيُّهَ ٱلثَّقَلَانِ⟫

"We shall give you Our full attention, O both creations!" Have you not been told of that Day?

⟪ar-quote:فَإِذَا ٱنشَقَّتِ ٱلسَّمَآءُ فَكَانَتْ وَرْدَةًۭ كَٱلدِّهَانِ⟫

"When the heaven splits asunder and becomes crimson like red oil."

⟪ar-quote:يُرْسَلُ عَلَيْكُمَا شُوَاظٌۭ مِّن نَّارٍۢ وَنُحَاسٌۭ فَلَا تَنتَصِرَانِ⟫

"Upon you shall be hurled flames of fire and molten brass; you shall not be helped."

⟪ar-quote:يُعْرَفُ ٱلْمُجْرِمُونَ بِسِيمَهُمْ فَيُؤْخَذُ بِٱلنَّوَصِى وَٱلْأَقْدَامِ⟫

"The criminals shall be known by their marks; they shall be seized by the forelocks and the feet," and it shall be said:

⟪ar-quote:هَذِهِۦ جَهَنَّمُ ٱلَّتِى يُكَذِّبُ بِهَا ٱلْمُجْرِمُونَ⟫

"This is the Hell whose reality you denied!"

## Did This Life Begin By Itself?

Did this life begin through its own strength? Did we come into the world by our own will? Since we did not come of our own accord, we must understand why we came. And we must understand why the One who sent us did so.

Do we die through our own happiness? Do we choose death? Do we die by our own will? If not, we must understand: the One who causes death—why does He do so?

It is a tremendous tragedy that ninety-nine percent of humanity possess no means to live, while the one percent who possess means have no time to enjoy them. Eventually, both die. Who retrieves them? What system is this? An elderly father sits as his child descends to the grave. An innocent newborn is born as the mother dies and is buried. The child does not know who gave him birth.

As a child, a melody plays in the mind—a tune we must dance to. Then youth arrives, and we begin to take life seriously: I must earn a degree. Once the degree is obtained, the goal shifts: I must secure a good, well-paying job. We begin to run. Some succeed in creating something; others fail. By the time wealth is acquired, we have become elderly, perhaps fifty or sixty years of age. It must be evening already. The sun approaches the horizon.

Who operates this entire system of existence? Life and death are within it. Morning and evening are within it. Spring and autumn are within it. Youth and old age are within it. Lament and celebration are within it. Faith and disbelief are within it. Monotheism and polytheism are within it. Dancing at night and prayer at night are within it. Attending parties at night and imploring Allah at night are within it. Honest scales and oppression are within it. Weeping before Allah at night and drinking wine all night to deceive oneself are within it. Will it continue thus?

Greek philosophers believed this cycle continues eternally, unending. We do not hold such a belief. Yet our lives proceed as if we do. Do what you wish, you will see. Act as you please. If we must submit even a small piece of paper for examination, we prepare from morning to evening. How much preparation we undertake for a small exam! Yet I have raised the greatest question: Why have I come into this world? Who sent me? Why must I die? I do not wish to. Who compels my death?

Desires press upon the head while death is rising. Needs are upon the head while death strikes. Intoxication with power is upon us while death twists our necks and drags us beneath the ground. The intoxication of kingship is upon us while insects make our bones their dwelling. Why does one who lived like a king, clothed in silk, descend into the grave wrapped in a simple shroud?

Death follows you. Death follows me. One day we shall be seized. Hazrat 'Alī (peace be upon him) said:

⟪ar-quote:الموت ! لیسَ مِنہُ الفَوت۔⟫

"Death! There is no escape from it."

⟪ar-quote:أِن أقمتُم لَہُ أخَذَکُم !  أِن فَرَرتُ مِنھُم أَدرَکَکُم⟫

"If you stand against it, it overcomes you. If you flee from it, it catches you." What can you do when you cannot fight it, cannot flee from it, cannot hide? Then:

⟪ar-quote:الوحا الوحا⟫

"Hurry! Hurry!"

What is the hurry? 

⟪ar-quote:النجاہ النجاہ۔⟫

"Salvation! Salvation!" Gather the means of success before the One who is about to depart from this world.

Today we live as if there is no God at all. At night, there are spectacles as if Allah sleeps and no one observes.
