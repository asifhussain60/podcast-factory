## The Quranic Miracles

Is the Qur'an a word or a scripture? Is it spoken text, or is it written text?

When there is a word, three elements constitute every utterance: (1) the speaker, (2) the word being spoken, and (3) the manner of speech. These form the complete act of communication.

### Speaker, Speech, Style

What renders these three elements significant? Are all three necessary? Are some more vital than others? Let us examine them individually.

**The Speaker**

Suppose an eight-year-old child offered you sound financial or investment advice. Would you accept it? Of course not—not because the advice itself is flawed, but because the source lacks authority. Yet if an experienced investment manager offered the same counsel, you would readily embrace it. Why? Because the speaker's credibility matters profoundly. The first prerequisite for heeding speech is assurance of the speaker's capability and standing. When the speaker's credentials are weak, his words carry no weight.

**The Speech**

Consider attending a sermon in an unfamiliar mosque. You have never met the preacher and know nothing of him. Finding no seat inside, you stand outside the auditorium and listen from the doorway. You cannot see the speaker. Inside, a seventeen-year-old boy stands at the pulpit—dressed in jeans and a t-shirt, wearing his cap backwards. Yet he delivers an extraordinary sermon. You hear only his words, never glimpsing his face. What impression forms in your mind? You imagine him to be an elderly scholar, learned and experienced through decades of study. On what basis do you construct this judgment? Solely upon the quality of his speech. Thus the speech itself carries weight independent of the speaker.

**The Style**

The third element—how the word is expressed—proves equally consequential.

Who speaks? What do they say? How do they say it?

Consider this: the hour of prayer arrives, and you remain absorbed in your work, indifferent to the time. A *Maulana Sahib* approaches and speaks sternly: "What shameful behavior! Are you a Muslim or an infidel? You claim faith yet neglect prayer at its appointed hour. Do you not fear the Fire?"

Later, another *Maulana Sahib* comes and addresses you with tenderness: "My son, the time for worship has arrived. I see you occupied with your work and perhaps unaware of the hour. I thought it best to remind you gently."

Both speak to the same purpose. Yet to whom do you respond, and whom do you reject? Why does the difference matter? It is the style—the manner of expression. Both may be righteous men; both may be learned scholars. Yet the first one's harsh tone obliterates the force of his message, while the second's affectionate manner carries it home. Thus style is not merely ornamental; it determines whether speech moves the heart.

Similarly, a preacher delivering vital truths in a monotone voice may so bore his audience that they drift into slumber, though his content be sound. The problem lies not in what he says but in how he says it.

---

Why, then, is the Qur'an a miracle? For three reasons: the speaker, the speech, and the manner of expression.

**The Speaker is Extraordinary.** Allah—the Almighty, whose authority is beyond question. Every educated and uneducated Muslim acknowledges this; the disbeliever does not.

**The Speech is Extraordinary.** The Qur'an itself—perfect in every way. Not a single letter can be displaced without loss. Muslims accept this truth; the disbelievers reject it. Thus believers find miracle in both speaker and speech, while disbelievers find none, for they deny both Allah and His word.

**Yet what transcends this division—what moves both believer and disbeliever alike—is the miraculous beauty of its style, the stunning manner in which Allah has expressed His truth.**

Throughout history, every person who heard the Qur'an—believer and disbeliever alike—was moved by its eloquence. Disbelievers called it the word of kings, of priests, of sorcerers, but never—never—did anyone say it was uninspiring, ungraceful, or unimpressive. No historical record exists of anyone dismissing the Qur'an as dull or poorly expressed. Why? Because of its style, its incomparable manner of speech.[^cite-1]

When one translates the verses of the Qur'an, we retain the speaker: Allah remains the speaker. We may preserve something of the meaning and purpose of the words in translation. Yet what is lost utterly, completely, one hundred percent? The style. Why? Because style resides in language—in Arabic. Even an Arab narrator, using his own tongue, could not replicate the manner in which Allah has expressed these truths.

The true beauty and majesty of Allah's words can never be fully translated.[^cite-2] Something of the meaning may cross into another tongue, but the actual force—the true impact that the original possesses—no translation in any language can capture. A translation is the words of the translator. The Qur'an is the Word of Allah. If we wish to grasp the distance between them, we need only remember the distance between ourselves and Allah. So vast is that gulf that it measures the difference between human speech and the Speech of God. A translation can never substitute for the original text.

Content fades in translation; much is lost. The style—its beauty, its power—vanishes entirely.

---

How is the miracle of the Qur'an typically presented? A search reveals: scientific principles discussed in the Qur'an, prophecies of future events, numerological patterns. But we must ask: Do we truly believe every verse of the Qur'an is a miracle? Or only some verses? When was the Qur'an a miracle—from the moment of revelation, or only after time had passed?

Of the Qur'anic verses, how many contain predictions? Very few. How many contain scientific principles? Very few. How many of these matters were known to the Arabs of that era? Scarcely anything.

Then what was it that mesmerized those people? What caused them, each time the Messenger opened his mouth, to fall into stunned silence, unable to speak?[^cite-3]

### Tufail ibn 'Amir al-Dawsi

Two accounts from history illustrate that the Qur'an's perfection resides in the beauty of its utterance.

The disbelievers and hypocrites of Mecca had spread the word about the Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫: "He is a great sorcerer. Whoever hears his word becomes his follower." ⟪ar:طفیل بن عامر طوسی⟫ was a renowned leader among his people. When he arrived in Mecca, the people warned him of this very danger. Why? If the leader of a nation accepted Islam, the nation would follow. The *mushrikeen* had taken precautions; they stuffed cotton in their ears so that the Messenger's voice would not penetrate their hearing, even by accident.

While circumambulating the Ka'bah, Tufail saw a handsome man standing and praying, reciting aloud. He asked those around him, "Who is this?" They replied, "This is the sorcerer of whom we warned you." Tufail thought to himself: "When I beheld his face, I could not believe it the face of a sorcerer." He reasoned further: "I am the leader of my people—no fool who falls under anyone's spell. I shall listen with discernment. If what I hear is true, I will accept it; if false, I will reject it." With this resolve, he removed the cotton from his ears and sat near the Messenger of Allah.

When the Messenger ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ finished his prayer, Tufail asked him:

⟪ar-quote:یا محمد! ما عندک؟⟫

"What do you have, O Muhammad?"

If you do not know Arabic, the meaning alone may escape you. Yet listen closely: can you perceive the beauty of the expression itself? Can you feel the elegance of the style?

The Messenger ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ replied:

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّ الحَمدَ لِی اللہَ أحمَدَہُ وَ أستَعِینَہُ وَ أُومِنُ بِہِ وَ أتَوَکَّلُ عَلَیہِ ۔ مَن یَّھدِہِ اللہُ فَلاَ مُضِلَّ لَہ وَ مَن یَّضِلَّ لَہُ فَلاَ ھَادِیَ لَہ⟫

"Truly, all praise belongs to Allah alone. I praise Him, I seek His aid, in Him I believe, and upon Him I place my trust. Whomsoever Allah guides, none can lead astray, and whomsoever He causes to stray, none can guide aright."

The moment Tufail heard these words, he trembled and cried out spontaneously:

⟪ar-quote:أُعِد أُعِد! فقد بلغنا قاموس البحر⟫

"Speak again! Speak again! A single utterance from you has exhausted the very depths of the sea!"

The Messenger repeated:

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّ الحَمدَ لِی اللہَ أحمَدَہُ وَ أستَعِینَہُ وَ أومِنُ بِہِ وَ أتَوَکَّلُ عَلَیہِ ۔ مَن یَّھدِہِ اللہُ فَلاَ مُضِلَّ لَہ وَ مَن یَّضِلَّ لَہُ فَلاَ ھَادِیَ لَہ⟫

With tears streaming down his face, Tufail said: "O Muhammad, teach me the testimony of faith (*Kalimah*)! I believe in you and in your God."

### 'Utbi ibn Rabi'ah

'Utbi ibn Rabi'ah (ʿUtbi ibn Rabi'ah) was among the most formidable orators of his age, a debater without peer in all Arabia. He could reduce any opponent to shame. When the disbelievers came to him in desperation, they said: "O 'Utbi! Muhammad has brought ruin upon us. He has caused us great trouble. Go to him and argue with him. Use your eloquence to silence him." Why did they choose 'Utbi? Because they were deeply concerned about the Messenger's words and his manner of speech. They selected someone of equal—or superior—eloquence. Their strategy: match words with words, style with style.

'Utbi came to the Messenger and said: "O Muhammad! What is it you claim about Abdullah ibn 'Abd al-Muttalib? What of your grandfather? What of your father? Are you superior to them?" He then launched into his objection:

⟪ar-quote:فرقت دیننا، و شطنتا آلھتنا، و عبت دیننا، و فرقت بیننا⟫

"You have destroyed the religion of our forefathers. You have made their gods contemptible. You have rent asunder what binds us together. You have divided brother from brother."

Then he continued his assault with offers:

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّما أِن کُنتَ تُرِیدُ بِہِ مُلکاً، مَلَّکناَ عَلی رُؤُسِنَا⟫

"If you desire kingship, we shall make you king over us."

⟪ar-quote:أِن کُنتَ تُرِیدُ بِہِ الشَّرَف، لا نَقطَعُ أَمراً دُونَک⟫

"If it is honor you seek, we shall not undertake any matter without your consent."

⟪ar-quote:أِن کُنتَ تُرِیدُ بِہِ البآءَ، جَمَعنَا لَکَ نِساَءَ قُرَیشً فَخطَ أَیُّ نِساَءَ قُرَیشً فَنُزَوِّجْکَ عَشَرا⟫

"If you desire beauty and companionship, we shall gather for you the most beautiful women of Quraysh. Tell us which you prefer, and we shall give you ten."

⟪ar-quote:أِن کُنتَ تُرِیدُ بِہِ المال، جمعنا لک أَموَالَناَ حَتّٰی تَکُونُ أَغنٰی قُرِیشً مالا⟫

"If it is wealth you seek, we shall gather our treasures until you become the richest among Quraysh."

Notice the structure of his argument: 'Utbi attempts to strip away the spiritual force of the Message by reducing it to human motives—kingship, honor, women, wealth. He does not attack the words directly; he attacks the man. He does not challenge the truth; he challenges the Messenger's integrity. This is the strategem of those who cannot meet the Qur'an on its own ground: make the speaker appear unworthy, and his words lose their power.

The Messenger ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ waited for him to finish, then asked: "Are you done, Abu al-Walid?" 'Utbi said, "Yes." The Messenger replied, "Then listen."

And he began to recite the Qur'an:

⟪ar-quote:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم

حمٓ۔ تَنزِيلٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلرَّحْمَنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ۔⟫

Immediately, the Qur'an establishes what 'Utbi had tried to obscure: this is not the word of Muhammad. This is a revelation from Allah—the All-Merciful, the Ever-Compassionate. You are not negotiating with a man; you are confronting the speech of God.

⟪ar-quote:‏كِتَبٌۭ فُصِّلَتْ آيَتُهُۥ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّۭا لِّقَوْمٍۢ يَعْلَمُونَ۔⟫

"A Book whose verses are clearly distinguished, a Qur'an in Arabic for a people who understand."

'Utbi had asked for specific worldly gains. Allah responds with something far grander: the Book is distinguished in its clarity. Its terms are precise, its argument unambiguous. It is set before you in the tongue you know.

⟪ar-quote:‏بَشِيرًۭا وَنَذِيرًۭا فَأَعْرَضَ أَكْثَرُهُمْ فَهُمْ لَا يَسْمَعُونَ۔⟫

"A bearer of good tidings and a warner. Yet most turn away and do not listen."

⟪ar-quote:وَقَالُوا۟ قُلُوبُنَا فِىٓ أَكِنَّةٍۢ مِّمَّا تَدْعُونَآ إِلَيْهِ وَفِىٓ آذَانِنَا وَقْرٌۭ وَمِنۢ بَيْنِنَا وَبَيْنِكَ حِجَابٌۭ فَٱعْمَلْ إِنَّنَا عَمِلُونَ۔⟫

"Our hearts are wrapped in coverings against what you call us to. Our ears are sealed. A barrier stands between us and you. Act as you will; we too shall act."

⟪ar-quote:قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أَنَا۠ بَشَرٌۭ مِّثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ أَنَّمَآ إِلَهُكُمْ إِلَهٌۭ وَحِدٌۭ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُشْرِكِينَ۔⟫

"Say: I am but a mortal like you. I am inspired with the message that your God is one God. Therefore direct your hearts to Him and seek His forgiveness. Woe to those who associate partners with Allah."

⟪ar-quote:ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَفِرُونَ۔⟫

"Those who do not pay the *zakat* and deny the Hereafter—"

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ لَهُمْ أَجْرٌ غَيْرُ مَمْنُونٍۢ۔⟫

"—while those who believe and do righteous deeds shall have a reward without end."

⟪ar-quote:قُلْ أَئِنَّكُمْ لَتَكْفُرُونَ بِٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ فِى يَوْمَيْنِ وَتَجْعَلُونَ لَهُۥٓ أَندَادًۭا ۚ ذَلِكَ رَبُّ ٱلْعَلَمِينَ۔⟫

"Say: Do you deny Him who created the earth in two days? Do you set up equals with Him? That is the Lord of all worlds."

⟪ar-quote:‏وَجَعَلَ فِيهَا رَوَسِىَ مِن فَوْقِهَا وَبَرَكَ فِيهَا وَقَدَّرَ فِيهَآ أَقْوَتَهَا فِىٓ أَرْبَعَةِ أَيَّامٍۢ سَوَآءًۭ لِّلسَّآئِلِينَ۔⟫

"He placed firm mountains upon it, and blessed its sustenance, and appointed its nourishment in four days—equal for all who seek."

⟪ar-quote:ثُمَّ ٱسْتَوَىٰٓ إِلَى ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَهِىَ دُخَانٌۭ فَقَالَ لَهَا وَلِلْأَرْضِ ٱئْتِيَا طَوْعًا أَوْ كَرْهًۭا قَالَتَآ أَتَيْنَا طَآئِعِينَ۔⟫

"Then He turned to the heavens while they were smoke, and said to them and to the earth: 'Come willingly or unwillingly.' They said: 'We come obedient.'"

⟪ar-quote:‏فَقَضَىٰهُنَّ سَبْعَ سَمَوَاتٍۢ فِى يَوْمَيْنِ وَأَوْحَىٰ فِى كُلِّ سَمَآءٍ أَمْرَهَا ۚ وَزَيَّنَّا ٱلسَّمَآءَ ٱلدُّنْيَا بِمَصَبِيحَ وَحِفْظًۭا ۚ ذَلِكَ تَقْدِيرُ ٱلْعَزِيزِ ٱلْعَلِيمِ۔⟫

"So He perfected them as seven heavens in two days, and inspired in each heaven its function. And We adorned the lower heaven with lamps and protection. That is the ordaining of the Mighty, the All-Knowing."

⟪ar-quote:أَعْرَضُوا۟ فَقُلْ أَنذَرْتُكُمْ صَعِقَةًۭ مِّثْلَ صَعِقَةِ عَادٍۢ وَثَمُودَ⟫

"If they turn away, say: 'I have warned you of a thunderbolt like that which struck 'Ad and Thamud.'"

As each verse descended, 'Utbi's countenance changed. His very visage shifted. The harshness of the words intensified with each recitation. When that final verse—the warning of the thunderbolt—fell from the Messenger's lips, 'Utbi could bear no more. He sprang up suddenly, thrust both hands over the Messenger's mouth, and cried: "O Muhammad! By your lineage, I beg you—utter not another word!"

He rose, trembling violently, and departed. When he returned to the gathering of Quraysh, they saw him and said: "The 'Utbi who left us is not the one returning. This is a different man."

They asked what he had brought back. He replied: "O assembly of Quraysh! I am your leader. I am not mad. Take my counsel today, if only this once. Never again will you hear my voice on this matter. Do not speak to this man. He has brought a word that has turned my whole being upside down. It is not poetry. It is not sorcery. It is not fiction. He warned you of the thunderbolt that struck 'Ad and Thamud. You know that Muhammad has never lied to this day! I fear the very lightning that destroyed them shall strike you!"[^cite-4]

## The True Way of Life in Islam

Is life merely the act of earning, accumulating, and then departing? Build something, acquire possessions, adorn yourself, consume—and then wrap yourself in that same sheet of burial cloth and vanish, forgotten forever. Is this the purpose for which the cosmos was fashioned?

Consider the brilliance of the sun, the beauty of the moon and stars, the journeys of galaxies, the turning of seasons, the radiance of dawn, the waves of seas, the clouds, the raindrops, the valleys fair and green, the rivers flowing. Such a vast and magnificent universe has Allah created. Was it merely so that humans might hoard wealth, deceive one another, betray trusts, and dishonor themselves?

What then is life's true aim? Is it only to earn and to accumulate?

I once heard of a man who journeyed through life claiming everything as his own: "This is mine, that is mine. I shall not share it." Yet when his eyes closed at last, all was gone. Everything abandoned.

Each traveler must choose a destination before boarding the plane. If you wish to reach Lahore, you select a flight bound for Lahore—not just any aircraft. Life too is a journey, and the journey itself is paramount. It is the most valuable of journeys. If it is short, then the path is traversed quickly, the destination reached. But you must know: where are you headed? What is your destination? What is your direction? What are you striving toward?

Every game has a purpose that defines its end. What is the purpose of this game of life? Is it merely to earn and die?

Ask the Creator why He created. Everyone becomes entangled in deception. Few know who the Creator is. Few understand which path in life is righteous. Most do not know where they are going, nor what the end shall be, nor what transpires after death.

These are the profound questions of existence. Yet the moment we awaken, the specter of wealth looms over our heads. Life passes. Hair turns white. Old age arrives. Yet the same refrain continues—the endless pursuit of money.

There is but one melody that plays: acquire more and rise higher.

Look upon their ruined mansions! Behold their desolate graves! Witness the extinction of their generations! Each silently proclaims: this world is not a destination—it is a waystation. Wealth is not the goal; houses, honors, possessions are the path. The destination lies elsewhere. But where? Is it Allah?

What is this destination like? If there is one like it, hear this: there is none like unto Him. None is worthy of worship save Him alone.

⟪ar-quote:وأذا سألت فسأل اللہ⟫ "When you ask, ask of Allah."

⟪ar-quote:وأذا استعنت فستعن باللہ⟫ "When you seek aid, seek it from Allah."

Do not invoke Allah's creation. Do not call upon His servants. Call upon Him whom none has created, who needs no summons to hear—He is ever-listening. You must come to speak; He has no need to come to listen. You have no choice but to speak; He has no compulsion to attend. You cannot reach Him anywhere, for He is beyond place and time.

⟪ar-quote:لا یحویہ مکان، ولا یشتمل علیہ الزمان⟫ "No location contains Him, no period encompasses Him."

He transcends the boundaries of east, west, north, and south. He is free from the constraints of time.

He is free from need. Where then is He? 

⟪ar-quote:فَأَيْنَمَا تُوَلُّوا۟ فَثَمَّ وَجْهُ ٱللَّهِ⟫ "Whichever way you turn, there is the face of Allah."

Yet He is nearer to you than your jugular vein.

"The eyes perceive Him not, nor can imagination grasp Him"—yet despite such distance, His nearness is absolute.[^cite-5]

No one is His father; no one is His son. He begets not, nor is He begotten. ⟪ar-quote:لم یلد و لم یولد⟫

He alone is the Mighty, the Self-Sufficient (*al-ʿAzīz*, *as-Samad*). All treasures of the heavens and earth belong to Him: ⟪ar-quote:للہ خزائن السموات والارض⟫

Everything is within His dominion. On the Day of Judgment, He will fold the heavens and the earth in His grip. He holds the reins of the heavens and earth alike. All weather obeys Him. All creatures are His servants.

He is free from hunger; He is the Creator of all things. He is free from fatigue; He sustains everyone. He is free from sleep; He is the Comfort of all creation. He is free from need; He gives to all. He is free from form and color; He is the One who grants form and color to all. He is without need; yet He fulfills the needs of all. He is without a partner; everyone else has a partner.

⟪ar-quote:سَوَآءٌۭ مِّنكُم مَّنْ أَسَرَّ ٱلْقَوْلَ وَمَن جَهَرَ بِهِۦ وَمَنْ هُوَ مُسْتَخْفٍۭ بِٱلَّيْلِ وَسَارِبٌۢ بِٱلنَّهَارِ⟫

"Alike among you is he who conceals his speech and he who proclaims it, he who hides in the night and he who walks openly in the day."

Whether you whisper words or speak them aloud, whether you hide yourself in darkness or move about in daylight—

⟪ar-quote:يَعْلَمُ سِرَّكُمْ وَجَهْرَكُمْ⟫

"He knows your secrets and your declarations."

He knows the hidden and the manifest.

---

Allah will bring death to this entire universe. 

The heavens shall perish. The stars shall fall. The mountains shall crumble. The animals shall die. The beasts shall perish. The seas shall dry. The sky shall burst asunder. The stars shall scatter. The sea shall ignite with fire. Then comes the Resurrection—mankind raised from the graves.

On that day, each soul shall know what it has wrought. Every living thing must taste death. Everything that exists must perish.

Yet Allah alone is free from death! Allah is unblemished! Allah is free from all defect!

Make Allah your destination. When you bow before Allah, accept Him as your Lord. 

Praise be to Allah, Lord of all worlds. Glory to my Lord, the Mighty. Glory to my Lord, the Most High. To You, my Lord, belongs all praise. Say: "I seek refuge in the Lord of the Daybreak." Say: "I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind."

Your Lord is Allah, who created the heavens and the earth in six days, then established Himself upon the Throne. He covers the night with the day, which follows it swiftly. And the sun, the moon, and the stars—all are made subservient to His command. His is creation, and His is command. Blessed is Allah, Lord of all the worlds.[^cite-6]

You need place no restrictions upon yourself. Allah has imposed but one demand: seek Him. Seek Allah. Make Allah your destination.

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم، خلقتک لعبادتی فلا تَلعَب⟫

"O son of Adam! I have created you for My worship. Do not squander yourself!"

Wherever you are, be Mine. Do not waste yourself in wine, in gambling, in lies, in oppression, in adultery. Be a benefit to my creation. Thus whether you dwell in America or in Mecca, if you remain Mine, America becomes paradise. But if you rebel against Me, Mecca becomes the fire of Hell.

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم، خلقتک لعبادتی فلا تَلعَب⟫

"O son of Adam! I have made you for Myself. So be Mine."

⟪ar-quote:وتکفلت برزقک فلا تطعب⟫

"I have undertaken to provide your sustenance. Do not grow weary!"

Remove from your heart the false belief that if you do not lie, you cannot earn a living. If you do not cheat, how will you survive? If you do not sell alcohol, whence comes your profit?

⟪ar-quote:من ارسَلَ الیک الغذا وَ انت جَنِینٌ فی البَطنِ الاُمِّ⟫

"Tell me—who provided you nourishment when you were a helpless, motionless clot within your mother's womb?"

Corruption appeared upon land and sea through the deeds of human hands, so that people might taste the consequence of their actions and thus return to the right path. ## Light and Darkness in the Heart

Allah says (in the interpretation of the meaning):

Praise be to Allah, who created the heavens and the earth and made the *azlumāt* (darknesses) and the *anwār* (light).

*Al-Azlumāt* is plural; *Anwār* is singular. Oppression and darkness are countless, yet light and illumination are one and the same. There exists a chemistry of light, but no chemistry of darkness. There is a physics of light, but no physics of darkness. When light does not exist, we call that absence "darkness." Darkness itself is not a thing; it is the absence of light. All knowledge relates to light; nothing pertains to darkness.

When darkness covers the eyes, we do not see the paths of this world. When it covers the hearts, we do not see the paths of the Hereafter. We see the light of the worldly paths and so we plan for them. We manage the outward illumination. But we do not see the darkness of the hearts, nor do we arrange any light to dispel it. This causes one to stray from the paths. Allah says (in the interpretation of the meaning):

Their hearts have rusted because of their sins, and because of this the real Truth is no longer visible to them.[^cite-15]

It does not blind the sight, but it blinds the hearts within the breasts. Their eyes are not blind; their hearts are blind. They do not see the way to the Hereafter, for they possess hearts devoid of light.

Allah has given an example of those who go astray:

⟪ar-quote:أَوْ كَظُلُمَتٍۢ فِى بَحْرٍۢ لُّجِّىٍّۢ يَغْشَىٰهُ مَوْجٌۭ مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ مَوْجٌۭ مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ سَحَابٌۭ ۚ ظُلُمَتٌۢ بَعْضُهَا فَوْقَ بَعْضٍ إِذَآ أَخْرَجَ يَدَهُۥ لَمْ يَكَدْ يَرَىٰهَا ۗ وَمَن لَّمْ يَجْعَلِ ٱللَّهُ لَهُۥ نُورًۭا فَمَا لَهُۥ مِن نُّورٍ⟫[^cite-16]

This is a beautiful parable given by Allah. If all light is extinguished, if the sun sets and darkness covers everything, how will you find your way? Allah did not give the example of hearts' darkness from the darkness of a room or a street, where some light might enter or refract. ## The Way of Life

When a nation falls, it collapses from within. We have no taste for knowledge; we have a taste for money. If we look at history, we will find that the problems of perished nations were never that they lacked money or resources. When the attributes fade, when lies spread in a nation, when deception spreads, when oppression spreads—the right to honor is taken from them. No one has brought us to this point. We have reached it ourselves. The passengers of the boat dig holes in the boat with hammers and drills. Why do those with arrows in hand pierce the sails themselves, then blame the waves of the sea? This is not a conspiracy of waves. This is the work of our hands. Do not speak here and there—tell me why the caravan was looted.

I am not a herd of monks.

Today children humiliate their parents—is this America's conspiracy? Alcohol is consumed in congregations—is this the elders' conspiracy? ### Difference Between *Khalīl* and *Habīb*

There is *Khalīl*; there is *Habīb*. *Khalīl* is one whose love is in the heart; *Habīb* is one whose love has no bounds. *Khalāla* (friendship) wants something of love; *Habba* (love) wants only to see the beloved. *Khalīl* derives from *khalal*; *Habīb* derives from *habba*—a grain that has no limits. You keep planting; the crop grows. Harvest, and the next crop comes. There is a limit, an end to friendship; there is no end, no limit, no boundary to love. *Habīb*'s first rank is *Khalīl*. First one becomes a friend, then a lover.

Allah made the Messenger of Allah the prophet, the *Khalīl*, the *Habīb*, and declared: "O you who believe, until you bring me forward, do not believe. Say to them, 'I shall not accept your faith until you place me between you and them.'"[^cite-49] If they follow you, they are successful; if they do not, they fail.

There is a difference between love and love. Love is divided—from father, mother, brother, sister, friend. But when this love grows and becomes indivisible, belonging to no one else, it is called *ḥubba* (love).

Look for the character of your prophet in every area of your life. Take the form of Muḥammad! Kill his copy. You will pass in the Hereafter.

The magician became Muslim. Moses said, "These are my enemies: how did they believe?" He replied, "They came in your form. I liked it and put faith in their hearts."[^cite-50]

Everything has a form in which it appears beautiful. Muḥammad (peace be upon him) is the most beautiful and exalted form created by Allah!

What will happen to the one whom Allah has singled out for His love?

We have made religion a tradition. When *Rabīʿ al-Awwal* (the month of the Prophet's birth) comes, there are rallies and processions, yet no one is ready to change their life. When *Muḥarram* (the month of ʿAshūrāʾ) comes, we must cry, yet no one is ready to change their life. The message is not for twelve days! The message is not for ten days. We trade our lives, we trade our children, just so we can cry for ten or twelve days, and then the same mornings, the same evenings. The same cruelty, the same deception, the same lies, the same false joy, the same ecstasy. Is this why the message came? Is it easy to see your children being slaughtered?

When the severed head came before Ibn Ziyād, he said, "I have never seen such a beautiful face." After death, blood flows, the face withers. He said, "I have never seen such a beautiful face." The wretch had a stick in his hand. He struck your face, struck your lips. Then he struck a second blow. Zayd ibn Arqam stood up and said, "O unfortunate one, remove this stick from your lips. I have seen the lips of the Prophet of Allah upon these lips. Is it easy?"[^cite-51]

One word could have saved his own life and the lives of his children. If he had accepted allegiance, he would have saved himself and the children too. And did Ḥusayn do all this for Paradise? "I am spending here so that Allah may be pleased with me. Perhaps Allah will be pleased with me by bearing the name of His beloved, forgive me, and grant me Paradise. You are listening; you take out your time, sit and listen, so that Allah may be pleased and grant you Paradise." But Ḥusayn's Paradise was decided at the age of his nursing.[^cite-52]

*Al-Ḥasan wa al-Ḥusayn Sayyidā al-Shabāb Ahl al-Jannāh*—Hassan and Ḥusayn are the masters of the youth of Paradise!

So why did he suffer poison? And why was his head mounted upon a spear? This is so that we can be successful. So that Islam should remain and reach us—you and I. So that by doing this, we can enter Paradise. Ḥusayn made this sacrifice for me! Ḥusayn made this sacrifice for you. Who did so much for us, and what are we doing with them? Karbalāʾ is a message: whatever you must do to please Allah, you must cross whatever limits. Climb the spear. Drink poison. Cut off the children.

We cannot simply stop the music.

Ignorance also made it a habit. This is a life-changing message for Allah's pleasure. Allah Almighty created the highest family, the *Ahl al-Bayt* (People of the House). If an item costs ten rupees, you see it at a glance. If it costs one hundred thousand, you look at it differently. These were the *Ahl al-Bayt*, the greatest family. Every drop of their blood was worth more than every ounce of wealth in the world. What blood was shed! These were the *Ahl al-Bayt*, the highest noble household, the greatest family. The Hereafter is a matter of the latter. Let me tell you the story of the world.

A widow went to the governor of a place in the Samarkand region. "I am from the *Ahl al-Bayt*. I am poor; I need a place for myself and my daughters. I am a stranger!" The Muslim governor asked, "Do you have any proof of being from the *Ahl al-Bayt*?" "I have no document," the woman said. "Everyone here says he is a *Sayyid*," the governor replied. "Is there anyone else here?"

Someone said there was a Parsi (Zoroastrian) here who is very generous. She went to him and said, "I am from the family of the Prophet of the Muslims. I am in need and looking for a place for myself and my daughters." As soon as he heard this, he sent his wife to receive them. Everyone was fed, kept comfortably, given blankets—for Samarkand is a cool region.

That night the governor dreamed. The Prophet of Allah was in Paradise, and he too was standing there. There was a magnificent palace in Paradise at the door of which the Prophet of Allah stood. He asked, "O Messenger of Allah, whose palace is this?" The Prophet replied, "This is the palace of a believer." He said, "I am also a believer." The Prophet said, "Give some proof of your faith; bring witnesses." He trembled.

Then the Prophet said angrily, "My daughter came to you, and you began asking her for proofs of being from the *Ahl al-Bayt*! Get out of my sight!"[^cite-53]

When he awoke, he let out a loud scream and ran barefoot. Someone asked, "Is the woman staying in the Parsi's house?" He knocked on the door and said, "The people you have are my guests; return them to me." The Parsi said, "I do not wish to return them." The governor said, "Take whatever you want from me." Then the Parsi began to weep. "I apologize of course. Although I made a deal without seeing it, now that I have seen it, the deal cannot be canceled. The dream in which you were being rejected—I also saw it. The Messenger of Allah said to you, 'Get away from my sight.' Then he addressed me and said, 'You gave my daughter a place. God has ordained Paradise for you. You and your family shall enter this palace. Blessings have been written for all of you.'" The Parsi said, "I have already read the *Kalimah*. I have become Muslim. I will not go back now!"[^cite-54]

This was five centuries ago. Here, the grandson's son is being slaughtered directly! Maulānā Umm Salamah saw the Messenger of Allah in a dream. His complexion was yellow, his body covered in mud, his beard filled with dirt. She asked in alarm, "O Messenger of Allah, what happened?" He replied, "I was just coming from the place where my Ḥusayn was killed. I saw his children being murdered at *Maqtal al-Ḥusayn*."[^cite-55]

It is not an event; it is a message. So that we understand what a noble message this is—that we hold it to our chests, live by it, and pass it to future generations. If this reward had been a world of punishment, it would never have happened that Ḥusayn and his children were martyred while Yazīd sat upon the throne and drank wine. But Allah Almighty has given the message: this is a world of afflictions. All kinds of trials will descend here. Do what Allah commands. Do what the Beloved of Allah tells you to do.

When he went to the house, the Messenger of Allah would say to Fāṭimah, "Ayn al-qaṭʿah, ayn al-qaṭʿah?" (Where are my naughty ones?). Ḥaḍrat Fāṭimah would say, "O Messenger of Allah, they are with me." If you had prepared them quickly, they would have become like camels in front of him. He would place both knees on the ground, place both hands on the ground, and they would say, "Come, come on my back!" Once ʿUmar saw that Allah's beloved was walking around the room with the two children sitting on his back. It escaped ʿUmar's tongue: "*Naʿm al-muṭīʿah*" (What a mighty rider). ⟪ar:و نعم الراکبان!⟫ (And what excellent riders!)

The coolness of my eyes is beauty and beauty. Hassan resembled the Prophet from head to waist. Ḥusayn resembled the Prophet from the waist to the feet. Ḥusayn resembled ʿAlī from head to waist, from waist to toe resembling the Prophet. He used to say, "Hassan is my son, Ḥusayn is ʿAlī's son."

"Do not raise your hands for them, but raise your hands for us when Malik Alamut came to take you. He said, 'Come and ask me what will I do with my Ummah, and I will tell you whether I want to go or not.'"

You will find out about humanity from the Qur'ān and *Ḥadīth*. You will not find it in school.

One of the companions was Abū Dujānah. He was with the Prophet from the Battle of Badr until the final Hajj. Regarding the Battle of Badr, the Prophet said, "I have forgiven the people of Badr whatever they wish." They are the companions of Ḥudaybiyyah. Allah said to them:

*Raḍī Allāhu ʿan alladhīna bāyaʿūka taḥta al-shajrah*—Allah was pleased with those who pledged allegiance to you beneath the tree.[^cite-56]

These are the companions of the Messenger of Allah for whom Allah said, "Tomorrow is the promise of Allah." I promise good fortune to all your companions.

This Abū Dujānah is saying, "I have only one act of forgiveness on the Day of Resurrection." What is it? Prayer? Fasting? Jihad? Pilgrimage? Badr? Any conquest? The conquest of Mecca? What then is that deed? "I have never hurt anyone with my tongue till this day, and I do not hold any grudge against any Muslim in my heart."[^cite-57]

He said to Muʿādh ibn Jabal, "Shall I tell you the summary of the entire religion?" He asked, "O Messenger of Allah?" The Prophet stuck out his tongue and held it between two fingers of his hand and said, "*Kafāhu hādhā*" (This is enough).[^cite-58]

Control it, for the most-pushing thing to Hell is the tongue.

## 26 July 2014 New Discourse

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّهُۥ لَقَوْلٌۭ فَصْلٌۭ وَمَا هُوَ بِٱلْهَزْلِ⟫ Verily the Qur'an is the definitive word, and it is not a matter of jest. ⟪ar-quote:إِنَّ هَذَا ٱلْقُرْآنَ يَهْدِى لِلَّتِى هِىَ أَقْوَمُ وَيُبَشِّرُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَعْمَلُونَ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أَنَّ لَهُمْ أَجْرًۭا كَبِيرًۭا⟫ Indeed, this Qur'an guides to that which is most upright and gives good tidings to the believers who perform righteous deeds that they shall have a great reward. ⟪ar-quote:وَتَمَّتْ كَلِمَتُ رَبِّكَ صِدْقًۭا وَعَدْلًۭا ۚ لَّا مُبَدِّلَ لِكَلِمَتِهِۦ⟫ And the word of your Lord has been fulfilled in truth and justice; there is none who can change His words.

The speaker begins with a profound acknowledgment of human limitation. I am a human being not free from sin, he confesses, recognizing that my tongue may err—whether through forgetfulness, carelessness, or deliberate fault.[^cite-14] Interpretation itself may diverge from intent; what I have conceived in mind may emerge differently upon my lips. Such failings are inseparable from the human condition. Only the prophets speak with the authority of revelation (waḥy), their words preserved from error by divine protection. No ordinary person can claim immunity from mistake. In our present age, which has grown poisoned by suspicion and haste, we rush to accusation and slander before comprehending one another's intent.

I am but an ordinary Muslim among you, full of shortcomings and flaws—the ideal belongs to the Prophets and the Imams alone. Consider the postman: you do not scrutinize his personal conduct before accepting a letter correctly addressed to you. Should you later discover him to be of questionable character, you do not return all previous letters and parcels to him, demanding that he retrieve them from your hands. You keep what has been delivered; the message remains valid regardless of the messenger's failings.[^cite-15] Similarly, if you receive something of benefit from my words today and accept it, that benefit remains yours even should you later find fault in my character.

⟪ar-quote:لا تنظر من قال، انظر ما قال۔ و لا تنظر من نجح، أنظر کیف نجح⟫ Do not regard who has spoken; regard what has been spoken. Do not regard who has succeeded; regard how he has succeeded. This is the principle taught by Maulana ʿAlī. I am condemned for sinning and rejoice in goodness; I possess the capacity to repent of transgressions and to celebrate righteousness. This is the station of the ordinary believer—we are all of one rank, one class of faith. What grace has befallen me through the blessing of my elders is simply that I have been able to convey something to you. Yet this brings no claim that my actions are faultless.[^cite-16] Rather, the Beloved of my God speaks thus: ⟪ar-quote:وَمَا يَنطِقُ عَنِ ٱلْهَوَىٰٓ۔ إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا وَحْىٌۭ يُوحَىٰ۔ عَلَّمَهُۥ شَدِيدُ ٱلْقُوَىٰ۔ ذُو مِرَّةٍۢ فَٱسْتَوَىٰ⟫ He does not speak out of desire; it is naught but revelation revealed, taught him by One mighty in power, of strength most firm and true. The rest of us remain ordinary human beings—creatures of limitation and fallibility.

Allah has blessed us with the blessing of the Qur'an. The Holy Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) was honored with his prophetic station; his successor, Maulana ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, was blessed with the grace of his succession. The Imams who followed were blessed with the blessings of their imamate. We have been given the confession of monotheism (tawḥīd), the word of truth, the religion of truth. There is no limit to God's bounties; yet His greatest grace is that He has granted us faith through these luminous beings and the boundaries they have established for us.

Today, as Muslims, we find ourselves in deep trouble. Many of us doubt: Does God truly exist? The tongue speaks of faith, yet the heart bears no witness. Is religion true? If so, why do such calamities befall us? If God loves us, why does He allow such suffering?

Allah governs this universe by an unshakeable law. The creation moves in perfect accord with divine ordinance—there is no deficiency, no flaw. When a human being breaks the law of Allah, the system turns against him. Allah has explained His law clearly: ⟪ar-quote:وَٱلسَّمَآءَ رَفَعَهَا و الارض بعد ذالک ضحاحی⟫ He raised the heavens and spread out the earth. ⟪ar-quote:والشمس و القمر دائبین⟫ The sun and moon orbit continuously. Night is drawn over day and day over night. The sun, the moon, and the stars—all obey their appointed courses. The heavens and earth are held in place by divine command, so that nothing can shift from its station.

⟪ar-quote:لَهُۥ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَا وَمَا تَحْتَ ٱلثَّرَىٰ ۔ ‏وَلِلَّهِ ٱلْمَشْرِقُ وَٱلْمَغْرِبُ ۚ فَأَيْنَمَا تُوَلُّوا۟ فَثَمَّ وَجْهُ ٱللَّهِ⟫ To Him belongs all that is in the heavens and all that is on the earth, and all that is between them, and all that is beneath the soil. To Allah belong the East and the West; wherever you turn, there is the face of God. Whether you journey to the farthest reaches of the East, the extreme edges of the West, the North or the South—whether you gaze upon the seas or explore the depths, whether you ascend to the mountains or look upon the seven heavens adorned with stars—Allah is the sovereign Ruler over all. He raised the heavens as smoke and said to them and to the earth, "Come willingly or unwillingly," and they said, "We come in obedience." He created the seven heavens in two days and revealed His command in every heaven. He fashioned the angels and subdued them before Him. The angels possess diverse ranks: some bear two wings, others three, others four, others many. All were made subject to His will. The Throne trembled, Gabriel bowed, Michael bowed, Isrāfīl bowed. From the smallest creature to the mightiest, from the droplet to the sea, from the pebble to the mountain, from sand to desert, from east to west, from north to south—all were made to bow before Him, and His dominion was established over them.

Yet we have forgotten God. He called out to us; we did not heed. He showed us signs; we closed our ears. He displayed similitudes; we drew veils over our eyes. Where is the lesson in a hundred examples? Consider the Pearl Palace of Nūr Jahān, wife of Jahāngīr—not a house of mirrors, but a palace adorned with pearls, its roof of pearl, its pillars of gold. The Sikhs destroyed it and extracted the pearls; the British replaced them with glass. Many years later, a poet beheld that ruin and wept:

*There stands a broken house at the edge of the city,*  
*Where day and night blend in the cycle of time.*  
*It is said this was the rest of Nūr Jahān.*

Our greatest transgression is that we do not know God. He is the God free from decay, the God free from sleep, free from slumber, free from wives and children. ⟪ar-quote:لم یتَّخِذ صاحبا ولا ولدَا⟫ He has taken neither companion nor child. His power, His dominion, His empire, His sovereignty do not diminish. He is the God without beginning and without end. ⟪ar-quote:ھو الاول و الاخر و الظاھر و الباطن⟫ He is the First and the Last, the Manifest and the Hidden. Allah is the Eternal One, free from all constraint. ⟪ar-quote:لا یحوِیہِ مکانٌ ولا یَشتَمِلُوہُ علیہ زمانٌ⟫ No place contains Him, no time encompasses Him.

My God says, "Call upon Me alone; call upon none but Me." Has God ever said He is weary? Has He ever said He slept? Has He ever said to us, "The work has grown too great; bring someone else to help"? Has He ever said, "My treasures run low; seek another's aid"? Our function is to rely upon Him alone, not to make another our intercessor while He remains present. ⟪ar-quote:کفا باللہ رقیبا؛ کفا باللہ حفیظا؛ کفا باللہ شھیدا؛ کفا باللہ ولیا؛ کفا باللہ علیما؛ کفا بربِکَ ھادیاً و نصیرا؛ الیس اللہ بکافٍ عبدا؟ فسیکفیکھُمُ اللہ و ھو السمیع العلیم⟫ Sufficient is Allah as guardian; sufficient is Allah as protector; sufficient is Allah as witness; sufficient is Allah as ally; sufficient is Allah as knower. Sufficient is your Lord as guide and helper. Is not Allah sufficient for His servant? Allah will suffice them, for He is the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing.[^cite-17]

The entire universe has been rendered insignificant; all creation has been reduced to zero before Him. Our first and greatest crime is not to know God—to remain ignorant of His reality. Allah becomes wrathful at the commission of grave sins: systems of usury so immense they constitute a declaration of war against God. ⟪ar-quote:فازنو بحرب من اللہ و رسولہ⟫ "O you who believe, either renounce usury, or take notice of war from Allah and His Messenger."[^cite-18] When one has declared war against Allah and His Messenger, where shall one find refuge? We have made war in the very place we sought for peace. If we rebel against Him, where will we find tranquility now? There was only ever one refuge and one sanctuary.

Allah's jealousy (ghayra) awakens at grave transgressions. The Throne shakes at the cry of the oppressed. The angels shriek. God created the angels and asked them, "O angels, whose companion are you?" They replied, "We are the companions of the oppressed until they receive justice." The oppression of parents is a declaration of war against Allah.

Know your God. Befriend Him. Make friendship with Him: *Tawallanī, Tawallakum* (Turn towards Me, and I shall turn towards you). *Asaffinī, Asaffik* (Seek My aid, and I shall grant you aid). Make friends with Him once, and I shall grant you the right of true friendship. Deal justly with Me, and I shall give you your due.

The second crime is that we did not recognize Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (upon whom be peace). How can a nation whose leader is Muḥammad follow those more blind than themselves? Our guide is Muḥammad—the only being whom the Creator loves. The Maker loves what He has created. He swears by this truth.

⟪ar-quote:یا ابا صفیان جأتکم بکرامت الدنیا و الآخرۃ⟫ "O Abū Sufyān, I have come to you with the honors of this world and the next."[^cite-19] Abū Sufyān, follow me! I bring not only the miracles of the Hereafter but the honors of this world as well. Yet today we have abandoned the feet of the Messenger of Allah. We run after the blind—we have appointed Martin Luther King, Mother Teresa, and Bollywood celebrities as our guides, dressing according to their prescribed manner, following their words.

He has abandoned his Messenger.

Behold his Companion, whom Allah Himself called to His Throne: *Subḥān al-Ladhī Asrā* (Glory be to Him who took His servant on a night journey from al-Masjid al-Ḥarām to al-Masjid al-Aqṣā al-Ladhī Bāraknā). Look at his flight! And in that night, he ascended near.

Observe his rank: the Messenger of Allah stood before ʿIsā (upon whom be peace).

Behold his prophethood: the Messenger of Allah said, "Blessed is He who sent down the Criterion to His servant, that it might be a warning to all creatures."

Consider the power of his vision: the eye that beheld the Night of the Ascent—we deny it. Those who were granted the honor of standing before Allah proclaim: "Falsehood is destruction; truth is salvation." Yet we say, "If you do not lie, how shall you succeed? If you do not cheat, how shall you prosper?" Whom have we denied? Against whom do we rebel? Who came to save us? Who came to free us from slavery?

Whom have we abandoned? For the sake of whom the entire universe was created! *Lamā khalaqt al-aflāk* (Had you not come into being, I would not have created the heavens)—if you were not to come, O Beloved, I would not have created this universe. He alone brought us the good of this world and the Hereafter: *Jaʾtukum Bi-Kharāti al-Dunyā wa-al-Ākhira* (I have come to you with the honors of both worlds). The Prophet of all humans, of all beasts, of all living things and the dead, of the first and the last, the master of all prophets.

We were given prophets, guides, leaders, and messengers who showed us the destination and taught us the path to reach it. We have abandoned this Prophet.

He said, "Beautify my religion with modesty and generosity." These two qualities form the adornment of his faith: modesty and munificence. Where is modesty today? Where is generosity? Where is honor? What are these lights by which we walk, where indecency has become fashionable? The clothing of girls grows tighter and more revealing with each passing day. The wealthy and the powerful, who should be generous, have become tyrants instead. Are these the lights we wish to follow? We have no need of such lights. Extinguish them.

Do we desire a culture in which the disrespect of parents is considered sophisticated? We desire a life of honor, of service to our parents, of modesty and dignity. These virtues must be learned. We desire the life of our Prophet—a life of modesty, courage, honor, and dutiful service to parents; a life of truthfulness and trustworthiness, of love and noble character. These must be learned and cultivated through effort.

Where have those who built civilizations with stone gone? Where are Thamūd and Pharaoh? We do not desire a life of mere custom and habit. We desire the life of Muḥammad and ʿAlī.

If you are loyal to Muḥammad, we are yours.

## A Great Diminishment

⟪ar-quote:قُلْ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ قَدْ جَآءَكُمُ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَنِ ٱهْتَدَىٰ فَإِنَّمَا يَهْتَدِى لِنَفْسِهِۦ ۖ وَمَن ضَلَّ فَإِنَّمَا يَضِلُّ عَلَيْهَا ۖ وَمَآ أَنَا۠ عَلَيْكُم بِوَكِيلٍۢ⟫ Say: "O people, the truth has come to you from your Lord. Whosoever is guided is guided only to his own benefit; whosoever goes astray does so to his own loss. I am not your guardian."

All humans across the world—Allah is their Creator, male and female alike. Allah is their Provider. Allah shall cause them to stand before Him for judgment. All the words we possess are creations of our own making, and we cannot praise our Creator by means of our own creations. This is impossible.

When I say that God is eternal (*qadīm*), the word "eternal" does not capture Allah's reality, for "eternal" denotes a prolonged span of time, and Allah is the Creator of time itself—He is neither bound by its beginning nor by its end. He transcends both the first moment and the final moment. ⟪ar-quote:قدیم بلا ابتدا دائمٌ بلا انتھا⟫ Ancient without beginning, Eternal without end. Our words cannot adequately describe the eternity and perpetuity of this God. If Allah is the Creator of time, He cannot be contained within it. If Allah is the Builder of the house, He cannot be confined within it. Creator and creation are not of the same order. Allah is the One who brought all things into being: the people, the high and the low, the north and the south, the west and the east, the throne and the footstool—yet He Himself does not inhabit them.

The only words God has given us to recognize the Creator are those of His own revelation: *Man* (Who). *I am God.* Since we required explanation, had Allah brought forth a word we could not comprehend, how would we have known Him? This is why Allah has revealed His Word to us in the clearest manner, clothed His meanings in our language, and sent it unto us. He then introduced Himself to us, saying: *Anā Allāh* (I am God). *Iyyāya Allāh* (I am He, God). Who is God? *Lā ilāha* (There is no god). If there is nothing like unto Him, how shall you know who I am? Only through similitude can meaning be conveyed.

So you are commanded: "Believe in the unseen!" We are to believe in God without seeing Him. We cannot comprehend this vast universe. If we cannot even understand our own existence, how can we understand God? If we do not understand ourselves, how can we understand this Mighty One? Therefore, believe only in the words of the Prophet: *Lā ilāha illā Allāh*—there is no god but Allah. This is the instrument of faith.

What then is His nature? *Al-Ḥayy* (the Living): He lives and requires neither bread nor water. *Al-Qayyūm* (the Self-Subsisting): He is so established that He requires not the earth upon which to stand, not a chair on which to sit, not a house in which to dwell. How wondrous this is! He has neither beginning nor end, pure from both the first and the last. He is both homeless and timeless. He says: I need no place because I am homeless. What is a home? It is that which has six directions—north, south, east, west, up, and down. God says, "I am neither in the East nor in the West, neither in the North nor in the South, neither in heaven nor on earth." How then? *Faynamā Tawallū Faththamma Wajh Allāh* (Wherever you turn, there is the face of God). So in whatever direction you look, in whatever direction I turn, I am there.

I am *al-Qayyūm*—the Self-Subsisting One.

You grow weary ruling a small kingdom, managing your affairs, conducting your business, sleeping and waking. But I am the One who does not grow weary, who does not slumber, who does not sleep: He does not sleep, nor does slumber touch Him. He did not say sleep touches Him not; He said He cannot even be touched by such things.

And what is His dominion? There is no heaven competing with another heaven. The earth is the sole kingdom of the heavens. Is there one who rivals Him? Allah says, "If there were any, show Me!" God did not beget a son, nor is there with Him any god, for then every god would have taken away what he created, and some would have risen above others. Praise be to Allah above what they describe. Allah has not begotten a son, nor is there any god with Him. Had there been, each god would have seized what he created and tried to overcome the others—the universe would have descended into chaos.

Do you not see that whenever two powers arise in conflict, they collide? If there were another god like Me, would you not know it? Today one would occupy three heavens, another four; one would have drawn out the sun while another sank it; one would have increased the moon while another diminished it; one would have arrested the stars while another released them. What would have been the result? *Alā Fasāda* (Corruption would have resulted). The earth, the sky, and the entire universe would have been destroyed. Not even a *dirham* would have remained intact.

The night does not overtake the day, nor does the day overtake the night. Each swims in its appointed orbit. Neither the power of the moon nor the power of the sun can collide with the other. *Wa-al-Shams Tajrī li-Mustaqarrin Laha* (The sun runs to its appointed time). *Dhālika Taqdīr al-ʿAzīz al-ʿAlīm* (That is the decree of the Mighty, the All-Knowing).[^cite-20] The sun extends a thousand times larger than the earth on either side. Its velocity is six hundred thousand kilometers per hour. Its temperature reaches fifteen million degrees centigrade. Yet it moves along its path with such precision—not even a centimeter's deviation is permitted. Why? *Dhālika Taqdīr al-ʿAzīz al-ʿAlīm* (That is the order of the Almighty, the All-Knowing). It has been ordained by the Mighty Creator.

*Lā ilāha illā Hu* (There is no god but Him).

Yet you have fashioned idols for yourselves. Some have adorned the idol of employment, others the idol of beauty, others the idol of wealth, others the idol of fame, still others the idol of commerce. In appearance, you worship Me alone, but in reality, you worship them. Your heart bows to them; your forehead bows to Me. But I look at the heart. You do not even remember Me in your prostration—so what am I to say to you now? This is the condition of our worship: the head is in prostration, but the heart is in the marketplace, at work, thinking of food and clothing. This is the state of the worshippers.

Allah does not look at your worship and fasting—He looks at what has settled in your heart. This is the most terrifying verse of the Qur'ān. I will not merely see your prayer and fasting! I will look into your heart and see what dwells there: whom have you loved? Whom have you circumambulated? What has been nurtured in your breast?[^cite-21]

The heart is engaged in the circumambulation of material things, circling around gold and silver, wealth and fame. Yet the forehead rests upon God.

When I lowered my head, I heard a voice arising from the ground:

*Your heart is absent; what will you gain from this prayer?*

Allah only meets those whose hearts bow down in prostration along with their foreheads.[^cite-22]

Who is God? This is the first knowledge we must acquire. Yet we did not learn it. Schools have become commercial enterprises. On the very first day, children are taught trivial things—a pomegranate, a goat. They recite, "*Alif* is Allah, *bā* is Bismillāh" (though *bā* could just as well signify something else). Neither parents nor teachers conveyed the magnificence of Allah. Winning the pleasure of this God is the ultimate purpose of our life. If I do not satisfy the Almighty, I am worthless. Everything I possess is wasted; I am a failure. If you find Allah, you have found everything. If you do not find Allah, you have found nothing. If my God is pleased, then even if the entire world is destroyed, I am successful. If Allah is displeased, then what use are all the wealth, honor, fame, and greatness in the world? Let the entire world bow in prostration before me—I swear by Allah, I have failed utterly. The Mighty Emperor is displeased.

*Lā ilāha illā Allāh*—There is no god but Allah. *Man* (Who)? Where is the inspiration (*waḥy*)? *Al-Qayyūm*—The Self-Subsisting. *Kayf* (How)? There is no sleep and no slumber. *Kayfā* (How is His dominion)? There is no heaven and no earth. What is the glory and majesty (*ʿizzā wa-ʿaẓamā*)? *Man Dhū al-Qawwah Yashfaʿu ʿinda Ilāhi Bī-ʾidhnihi* (Who is he that can intercede with Him except by His permission?). Can someone intercede? If He permits, then the right of intercession is granted. No one can speak a word without His permission. ⟪ar-quote:وَخَشَعَتِ ٱلْأَصْوَاتُ لِلرَّحْمَنِ فَلَا تَسْمَعُ إِلَّا هَمْسًۭا⟫ Voices will be humbled before the Merciful, and you will hear naught but whispers.[^cite-23]

On that Day, voices will fall silent and tongues will be bound. No one will hear anything save the sound of footsteps. All faces will bow before this *al-Qayyūm*, and every eye will be cast down. No one will dare to raise their gaze.

What of His knowledge (*ʿilm*)? *Yaʿlamu mā bayni adyāhim wa-mā khalfuhum* (He knows what is before them and what is after them). He knows what will come to pass before it comes. He has forgotten nothing of what has passed. ⟪ar-quote:یعلم عدَدِ القَطرِ الامطار⟫ He knows the number of raindrops.[^cite-24]

Those who spend all their days praising their business deals, lauding their enterprises, glorifying their clothes, and celebrating their children—when asked to sit and praise Allah and His Messenger, they cannot speak for five minutes. Their tongues become locked. They have not ten minutes' worth of words to say about their God, their greatest Benefactor, their greatest Friend, their Master, their Protector, their Witness. But they have entire volumes praising the attributes of His names—yet they do not have a single minute of speech to praise Him. What then is the meaning of *Lā ilāha illā Allāh*?

Whether you are an Arab or a non-Arab, you have no god but Allah. Yet the dictionary of the tongue remains impoverished unless your heart bears witness. Unless the song of *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* reverberates through the strings of your heart, what good is it on the Day of Resurrection to recite *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* with your tongue alone?

This *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* has not freed me from usury; it has not freed me from lies; it has not freed me from deceit; it has not freed me from immodesty and corruption; it has not freed me from obscene songs; it has not freed me from abuse. How then can this *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* save me from the Fire of Hell?

I merely kissed and said, "I am a Muslim who has no god but Allah."

Belief like that of Khalīl (Abraham) is true belief; the belief of Allah is the intoxication of the soul itself.

Listen to the cry of this civilization—uncertainty is worse than slavery itself. There is no disease worse than doubt. I say *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* while lies rest upon my tongue. I say *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* while hatred of my Muslim brothers dwells in my heart. I say *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* while I disobey my parents. I say *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* while I abuse someone. I say *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* while conducting usurious business. And if my heart does not tremble at the call to prayer and does not move toward worship, then what is my *Lā ilāha illā Allāh*?

This is not the *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* that my Prophet proclaimed as the key to the locks of Paradise. This is not the *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* that opens the gates of Paradise so that I walk straight within. It requires complete faith, perfect sincerity, and rigorous striving. For this to be realized, the *qiblā* of the heart must become Allah, just as the *qiblā* of the face is the House of Allah. If one turns away from the House of Allah, there will be no prayer. Similarly, the *qiblā* of the heart is Allah; if the heart does not turn toward Allah, then deeds cannot profit. *Aqbal al-Laṭīf wa-al-Kathīf bi-al-Kathīf* (Turn the subtle and the coarse toward the subtle)—face toward Allah with both the delicate and the substantial dimensions of your being.

What then happens in the breasts? Yes! I will not look at your worship; I will not examine your fasting. I will look first at what dwells in your heart.

My brothers and sisters, let the greatest concern of your life be this: that my Allah be pleased with me. The first concern of my life, the middle concern of my life, the last concern of my life, and the greatest concern of my life must be to please Allah. I wish to be the beloved of His Beloved—our first is Muḥammad, the middle is Muḥammad, the last is Muḥammad, and we are all Muḥammad.

When the time of my death arrives, let Allah, His Prophet, the Prophet's successor, and the Imams meet me with a smile. Then will be recited to me: *Yā Ayyuhā al-Nafs al-Muṭmaʾinnā* (O soul at rest). *Irjaʿī ilā Rabbik Rāḍiyyah Marriyyah* (Return to your Lord, pleased and pleasing)—Come, good soul, come to your Lord; enter the Paradise of your Lord; join His righteous servants. This is the greatest and most serious concern of my life. All other concerns are like a child crying because his toy has broken, thinking there is no greater problem in the world, while an elder laughs and wonders why he grieves so over such a trifling matter.

This is the state of my God: He observes us, He washes our weeping, He causes us to weep. We worry about worldly affairs as if they are the greatest problems in life. No! No! The greatest problem is to please God. Sacrifice whatever must be sacrificed for it. Your transaction is with God! Your transaction is with God. How long will anyone enjoy the luxuries of this world? How long will you live in your house? How long will you sleep upon your soft and delicate bed? How long will you walk upon velvet carpets?

Funerals depart from great mansions, and funerals depart from humble huts alike. Whether a bungalow of four kanals or the dwelling of a fakir, the funeral ascends at last. The same white shroud, the same weeping of those gathered, the same dark and narrow grave, the same loneliness of the departed.

*There stands a broken house at the edge of the city.*

Nūr Jahān was queen of the entire subcontinent. After such a long time had passed, a poet beheld her grave and wept. What has become of this great woman today?

⟪ar-quote:وَكَمْ أَهْلَكْنَا قَبْلَهُم مِّن قَرْنٍ هَلْ تُحِسُّ مِنْهُم مِّنْ أَحَدٍ أَوْ تَسْمَعُ لَهُمْ رِكْزًۢا⟫ How many a generation have We destroyed before them! Do you perceive from any of them a single soul, or hear from them even a whisper?

Where are those mighty kings who ruled, who made countless claims and caused peoples' necks to bow before them? Where are they now? The entire universe bows before Allah, so let me too bow down. You also bow down. Do not take this life for granted. Do not squander these breaths. Money is safeguarded in banks so it will not be stolen; jewels are locked in vaults so they will not be lost. But consider that breath—this single breath I have just drawn: all the mountains of wealth in the universe, gold and silver together, cannot purchase back this one breath of mine once it has passed. How much time do we spend watching television, watching dramas, on our laptops, on our phones? Can these breaths return to us in time? Such activities are not forbidden—watch by all means. But first do what is essential.

When a child comes home from school, parents say, "Son, do your homework first, then watch television." Why? So that the homework is not neglected in favor of the drama. Because the essential thing is the homework. If the television show is missed, there is no harm; but if homework is neglected, there is loss. Prayer is our homework; worship is our homework. Do it in its proper time, then do other things as well.

How much time is spent gossiping on the telephone? How much time is wasted in complaint? How long does one sit watching dramas? Allah is watching us. He says: I am not sleeping; I am watching everything. ⟪ar-quote:وَلَا تَحْسَبَنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَافِلًۭا عَمَّا يَعْمَلُ ٱلظَّالِمُونَ ۚ إِنَّمَا يُؤَخِّرُهُمْ لِيَوْمٍ تَشْخَصُ فِيهِ ٱلْأَبْصَارُ⟫ Do not think that Allah is heedless of what the wrongdoers do; He only delays them until a day when eyes will be wide open.[^cite-25]

I am not heedless; I am not inattentive! I have taken everything into account. Not a single fish in the depths escapes My knowledge. On the day when eyes shall open!

*Taḥdā Yawm Taqallabu Fīhi Qulūb wa-Abṣār* (A day when hearts and eyes will turn in fear)—fear the day when your heart will be turned away, your eyes will turn in terror, your heart will leap to your mouth. You will say, "Hide me somewhere!" ⟪ar-quote:لا تخفی مِنکم خافیۃ⟫ Not a secret of yours will remain hidden. Allah will say: ⟪ar-quote:این المفر؟ این المفر؟⟫ "Where is the flight? Where is the escape?"

This universe is the slave of God. All His creation is slave to Allah. Whenever He wishes, He can burn it or swallow it. But this world is not a place of punishment; it is a testing ground. We will see who pleases Him and who pleases only his own desires.

If the great calamity comes, there will be a major accident. If the shriek comes, there will be a sound that deafens the ears. *Al-Qāʾrah, mā al-Qāʾrah!* And who has made you comprehend what is the Deafening Blast?[^cite-26] A horrible sound! You cannot imagine how terrible that sound must be.

Attachment to it is attachment. What is the perception of attachment? A great truth (which you used to deny in the world). How would you understand what that reality is! Now, when the veil has been lifted from your eyes, the plugs removed from your ears, the blindfold of heedlessness taken from your heart, now you will behold that reality.

*Wa-Jāʾa Rabbuka wa-al-Malak Ṣaffan Ṣaffan* (Your Lord will come, and the angels rank upon rank).[^cite-27] When Allah comes—or rather, when His presence becomes manifest—the angels will come in their ranks.

A call will be given, and so-and-so will be summoned.

⟪ar-quote:یُجاءُ ابن آدم یوم القیامۃ فَیُوقَف بین یدی اللہ تعالی کأنہ بزج⟫ The son of Adam will be brought on the Day of Resurrection and made to stand before Allah, the Exalted, like a goat brought to slaughter.[^cite-28]

The mighty warriors and the great champions will tremble before Allah like a newborn kid trying to stand, its legs shaking beneath it. Allah Almighty says they will tremble. And the voice of Allah will come from on high:

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم! اعطیتک، و خولتک، و انعمت علیک ماذا صنعت فیھا؟⟫ "O son of Adam! I gave you sustenance; I granted you dominion; I bestowed on you My blessings. What have you brought for today?"[^cite-29]

O My servant, I provided for you, I granted you wealth, I gave you life and honor. Have you brought something for this day? Show me what you have brought for Me in return for what I granted you! What a state will overcome both man and woman when, terrified and in dread, they wonder what will now befall them. Terror will overwhelm the witnesses, so imagine the condition of the one standing in judgment before Allah. Allah will say:

⟪ar-quote:ارنی ماذا قدمت! ارنی ماذا قدمتی⟫ "Show Me what you have sent forward; show Me what you have sent forward."[^cite-30]

⟪ar-quote:یا رب جَمَعتُہ و ثمَّرتُہُ و ترکتُہُ اکثر ماکان⟫ "My Lord, I gathered much; I earned much; I accumulated much; I left it behind in plenty."[^cite-31]

Shall I bring it? Allah will say: ⟪ar-quote:ارنی ماذا قدمت!⟫ "Show Me what you have sent forward!"

I am not asking what you have left behind; I am asking about what you have accumulated for today. Show it to Me! On that day, your Heaven and your Hell will be decided. Your success and your failure will be determined by it—not by what came before, not by what lies ahead.

*Wa-Yukhāfu al-Mawqif Yaʿīd Walidā al-Shaybū Kathīran Minhum* (And he whom We grant long life, We cause to decline in constitution)—the time will come when even a newborn will grow old from fear and his hair will turn white.

"I did not allow him into my house, nor into his office, nor into his business. Do we see the Sunnah (way) of the Prophet in our marriages? They get married according to the Sunnah of the Prophet, yet they perform the ceremonies according to the ways of the Hindus.

*Alā Tatrūk al-Ṣalāt, Alā Tatrūk al-Ṣalāh* (Do not abandon prayer, do not leave prayer). The essence of my twenty-three years of striving is prayer. O my Community, do not abandon your prayers. Then he said, *Allāhu Rāfiq al-Aʿlā*—O Beloved, call me to You now, and my eyes are closing.

If you adopt the life of the Messenger of Allah, you will reach Allah; otherwise, you will become lost somewhere along the way. There will be no destination and no path. It is not a destination where you acquire a house and cars and become a CEO of a company. My goal is to be pleasing to Allah. And my ruin is to be displeasing to Allah.

My entire life passed in the evening, and then I realized that something was amiss. Now it is the evening of life. Who will plunder it now?

⟪ar-quote:قِيلَ ٱرْجِعُوا۟ وَرَآءَكُمْ فَٱلْتَمِسُوا۟ نُورًۭا فَضُرِبَ بَيْنَهُم بِسُورٍۢ لَّهُۥ بَابٌۢ بَاطِنُهُۥ فِيهِ ٱلرَّحْمَةُ وَظَهِرُهُۥ مِن قِبَلِهِ ٱلْعَذَابُ⟫ It will be said: "Go back and seek light." But a wall will be erected between them, whereon is a gate, the inner side whereof containeth mercy, while the outer side thereof is toward the doom.[^cite-32]

The doors close. It is time—it is time, it is now. We can still turn back. You can still change your course. You can still transform yourself. But beware lest the deadline passes and the doors are sealed, and you cannot return even if you wish.

It is the grace of Allah that He forgives the sins of a lifetime upon a single sincere repentance. If repentance is genuine, there is no sin that Allah will not forgive. Islam is the labor of learning to live the pure life of the Beloved of Allah. When the time comes to depart, we are at peace, and Allah is pleased to meet us. This is what it means to learn how to live.

Do not backbite anyone. What is backbiting (*ghībah*)? Speaking of a man or woman in a way that would hurt him were he to hear it. If you sever idle gossip from your tongue, life becomes paradise. Allah (may He be exalted) says: ⟪ar-quote:يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ ٱجْتَنِبُوا۟ كَثِيرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلظَّنِّ إِنَّ بَعْضَ ٱلظَّنِّ إِثْمٌۭ وَلَا تَجَسَّسُوا۟ وَلَا يَغْتَب بَّعْضُكُم بَعْضًۭا ۚ أَيُحِبُّ أَحَدُكُمْ أَن يَأْكُلَ لَحْمَ أَخِيهِ مَيْتًۭا فَكَرِهْتُمُوهُ ۖ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابٌ رَّحِيمٌۭ⟫ O you who have believed, avoid much of suspicion; indeed, some suspicion is sin. And do not spy, nor backbite one another. Would any of you like to eat the flesh of his brother while dead? You would hate it. And be wary of Allah. Indeed, Allah is the Ever-Returning, the Merciful.[^cite-33]

The fire kindled within the heart cannot be extinguished by the cool air of air-conditioning in the house; it merely dries the sweat upon the body. If the fire of the heart is to be extinguished, then enter into the servitude of the Messenger of Allah and cultivate the noble character of the Prophet. This is a blessed life, and it must be learned and practiced with diligence.

## On the True Measure of Human Worth

⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ has exalted His Prophets to the highest stations. Yet our measure of human worth remains inverted and contemptible. We assess greatness not by virtue or knowledge, but by the accumulation of wealth and status. A man of prosperous business and vast fortune we call "great," regardless of the poverty of his character. The destitute we pronounce "small," even should his moral stature eclipse the heavens. Our thinking, in this regard, is most deficient.

The Creator Himself—who fashioned all things—understood the insignificance of worldly gain. He has disclosed this truth in His revelation: ⟪ar-quote:وَمَا ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَآ إِلَّا لَهْوٌ وَلَعِبٌ⟫[^cite-34] "The life of this world is nothing but amusement and play." All that we perceive in it is mere illusion, a deception wrought upon the eye. He then clarified this metaphor with a similitude: ⟪ar-quote:كَمَثَلِ غَيْثٍ أَعْجَبَ ٱلْكُفَّارَ نَبَاتُهُ⟫—like a mirage wherein the thirsty traveller beholds what appears to be water. When the desert sun beats upon the sand, what seems a flowing river or tranquil pool manifests before the weary eye. The parched wanderer cries out in hope: "Water! Drink!" Yet the man of wisdom knows better. He calls out: "No, no—it is merely a mirage!" Yet the thirsty one, blinded by his need, runs toward this phantom. The further he advances, the farther it recedes. It is the very image of life's end. At last, when he reaches the place, ⟪ar-quote:حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءَهُۥ لَمْ يَجِدْ شَيْئًا⟫[^cite-35]—he discovers nothing whatsoever. His companions had spoken truth; yet he has arrived exhausted and broken. What then remains for him to witness? Only emptiness. So drained is he now that he lacks even the strength to turn homeward.

When such souls return to their stations, observe them in silence and without judgment.

Those who understood the truth of the destination perceived another way. The Most High speaks of the spider's house: its web is but the thinnest shelter. What is its worth? Breathe upon it and it dissolves. His beloved Messenger—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace—said: "The house of the spider is the weakest of all houses."[^cite-36] Where, then, lies the wisdom in conferring status upon a person in this ephemeral world—in calling one man "great" and another "small," this one "VIP" and that one "VVIP"? Where is the discernment in striving after such empty designations or deriving satisfaction from them, when ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ Himself has declared: "The life of this world is nothing but amusement and play"?

Consider the record of history. The Most High did not grant wealth or kingship to His Prophets, save for one or two among them. All who came to this world lived in simplicity and want. Moses possessed not even a bed upon which to rest. A single sheet served him: in the morning he spread fodder upon it for his goats; in the evening he swept it clean and used it as his bedding. Yet he is the Prophet whom ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ chose to receive the Book. His name is mentioned one hundred thirty-six times throughout the Qur'an; only in Suras 5 and 14 is he not named, yet even in those chapters his essence permeates the text. Such a Prophet as this—and he had no bed! By our reckoning, he would be deemed far inferior to the lowliest servant in our households. Yet in the sight of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫, his dignity was such that the Creator Himself addressed him directly and spoke with him at length.

⟪ar-quote:إِنِّىٓ أَنَا۠ رَبُّكَ فَٱخْلَعْ نَعْلَيْكَ ۖ إِنَّكَ بِٱلْوَادِ ٱلْمُقَدَّسِ طُوًۭى۔ وَأَنَا ٱخْتَرْتُكَ فَٱسْتَمِعْ لِمَا يُوحَىٰٓ۔ إِنَّنِىٓ أَنَا ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّآ أَنَا۠ فَٱعْبُدْنِى وَأَقِمِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ لِذِكْرِىٓ۔ إِنَّ ٱلسَّاعَةَ آتِيَةٌ أَكَادُ أُخْفِيهَا لِتُجْزَىٰ كُلُّ نَفْسٍۭ بِمَا تَسْعَىٰ۔ فَلَا يَصُدَّنَّكَ عَنْهَا مَن لَّا يُؤْمِنُ بِهَا وَٱتَّبَعَ هَوَىٰهُ فَتَرْدَىٰ⟫[^cite-37]

Behold: ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ held this prolonged conversation with a servant so impoverished that he owned no bed. How great, then, must be the one to whom the Sovereign of the universe addressed Himself?

He journeyed to the court of Pharaoh, yet for three days no gate opened to him. Three days elapsed before the Messenger of the Most High could gain audience with an earthly king. We have sovereigns who sit idle, granting audience to any stranger who approaches. But this Moses—of whom ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ speaks repeatedly, recounting his story with reverence—came as a poor man seeking the king's ear.

## On the Prophet's Conduct and the Measure of Service

The Most High recounts the dialogue between His servant and the Creator. From the depths of humility, Moses called upon his Lord: "O Lord, expand my breast. Make smooth my tongue that they may understand my speech. Appoint for me a helper from my family—Aaron, my brother. Strengthen me through him and involve him in my task. We shall glorify Thee abundantly and remember Thee continually, for Thou hast ever been watching over us."[^cite-38]

What a profound conversation unfolds between the Prophet and the Lord of the Worlds—spoken in such meticulous detail! When you enter the hall of an ordinary king, you dare not speak more than two sentences, nor does he address you beyond a few words. Present yourself before the chief executive of a corporation and he may say: "I have two minutes—make them count." When a king or executive speaks to someone at such length and with such candor, it signifies that the person holds weight in his estimation. It means he is esteemed, even magnificent.

Yet here stands ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫, conversing with Moses for so long—a man who possessed nothing to eat, nothing to drink, no bed whereon to sleep, dwelling in a humble hut and tending goats for his livelihood. We, by contrast, dwell in spacious mansions with separate chambers for sleeping, dining, leisure, and recreation. ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ does not speak to us. Are we not superior to Moses in wealth and rank? Why then does He not address us with such openness? Why does He not show us such intimacy?

This reveals the corruption of our measure. The standard by which we assess worth is grievously flawed. We have fashioned an inferior and false criterion for esteem. We appraise not the man, but his possessions. We observe his garments and the size of his dwelling. We fail to perceive his morals or character. A person may possess immense wealth yet be base in nature—cruel, oppressive, miserly, obscene. Yet because riches surround him, we deem him magnificent by our distorted standard.

⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ has never greeted an earthly king and said "Salaam." Yet from the King of the Universe Himself comes the greeting to His Prophets. There are twelve varieties of greeting; the noblest among them is the salaam (peace) that ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bestows upon His chosen servants. Reflect upon this: when you approach a royal court, do *you* greet the king, or does the king greet *you*? Surely it is the latter—for he is sovereign. Yet He, the King of the Universe, greets His Messengers:

"Peace be upon Noah in the worlds."[^cite-39]
"Peace be upon Abraham."[^cite-40]
"Peace be upon Moses and Aaron."[^cite-41]
"Peace be upon all the Messengers."[^cite-42]

But when ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ Almighty sent salutations upon His beloved Muhammad Mustafa—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace—He established a singular eminence. He did not merely greet him as He had greeted the others. Rather, He declared:

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ وَمَلَٓئِكَتَهُۥ يُصَلُّونَ عَلَى ٱلنَّبِىِّۚ⟫[^cite-43]

"Verily, ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet." The word *yusallin* (send blessings) is drawn from the trilateral root whose cognates bear the meaning of repetition and continuity. Thus the passage signifies that ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ and all the angels of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫—not only once, but continuously, perpetually, until the Day of Judgment—shall send blessings upon this great Messenger. Then ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ turned to the believers and commanded:

⟪ar-quote:يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ صَلُّوا۟ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا۟ تَسْلِيمًا⟫[^cite-44]

"O you who believe! Send blessings upon him and greet him with a complete greeting."

⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ has never commanded the believers to do the like for any other Prophet. To Noah, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus He sent greetings, yet He commanded the people at no point: "Send greetings upon them." Here alone, in the case of His Prophet—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace—did He send blessings together with the angels of the seven heavens, and then turn to His servants saying: ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ says to you: ⟪ar-quote:يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟⟫—"O you who believe! Will you remain silent? ⟪ar-quote:صَلُّوا۟ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا۟ تَسْلِيمًا⟫—Send blessings upon him and greet him wholly."

## On the Work of the Prophets

The duty of the Prophets centers upon the declaration of His glory. We fritter away our days pursuing commerce from dawn to dusk, enslaved to status and protocol. But what was the work of the Prophets? They rose each morning to convey the message of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫. Consider Noah's labor: "The Lord said: I called to my people day and night."[^cite-45] He cried out: "O Lord, I have summoned my people to Thee ceaselessly!" And what was their response? ⟪ar-quote:فَلَمْ يَزِدْهُمْ دُعَآؤُنَآ إِلَّا فِرَارًا⟫[^cite-46]—"Each time I called them to come forward, they fled all the more." Yet Noah did not desist in anger, saying to ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫: "I have summoned them that they might turn unto Thee, that Thou mightest forgive them. I did not call them for fame or reputation, but that they might approach Thee and receive Thy forgiveness and Thy pardon." And how did they respond to his call? ⟪ar-quote:جَعَلُوٓا۟ أَصَبِعَهُمْ فِىٓ آذَانِهِمْ وَٱسْتَغْشَوْا۟ ثِيَابَهُمْ وَأَصَرُّوا۟ وَٱسْتَكْبَرُوا۟ ٱسْتِكْبَارًۭا⟫[^cite-47]

They thrust their fingers into their ears, drew their garments over their heads, and grew arrogant in their denial. Yet Noah—when his call fell upon deaf ears—did not abandon his task. He spoke ever louder. He proclaimed his message openly and conveyed it in secret to individuals, one by one, pleading with each: perhaps today they would not heed, yet perhaps tomorrow. Perhaps they would not seek forgiveness now, yet perhaps they would come seeking it on another day. Bring forth a speaker who might address such a throng without uttering abuse, without casting aside all guidance! Yet the Prophet Noah—peace be upon him—continued his summons.

This is the work of the Prophets! This is the task that earned them such exalted standing in the sight of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫. This is the deed that has granted them their magnificence before the throne of the Most High.

⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ commanded His Prophet—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace:

"O thou wrapped in garment! Arise and warn!"[^cite-48]

"O My beloved, arise! Go forth to thy task. And what is thy task? Warn the people. Proclaim to them the imminence of the Hour. Help them to understand the majesty of their Lord. Make clear to them that this world is not all; the Hereafter approaches, and before ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ we shall stand in reckoning. Each soul must account for its deeds, its wealth, its words. Warn them to perform righteous acts, that they might receive from ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ a mighty reward."

This summons—"O thou wrapped in garment!"—is itself a term of affection. Behind it lie profound meanings, seven distinct appellations of tenderness. But we shall limit ourselves to these essentials for now. ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ addressed His Prophet—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace—with reverence and tenderness. Yet note: He called upon all the other Prophets by their names—"O Noah, O Abraham, O Moses, O Jesus, O David." To call someone by name does not diminish their honor. Yet He never addressed His beloved Muhammad by his given name, nor did He permit others to do so. Rather, He commanded: "Do not call the Messenger among you as you call one another."[^cite-49] He rebuked those who said "O Muhammad!" Instead, He taught us to say: "O Messenger of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫." In the same manner, ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ Himself refrained from using the Prophet's name and addressed him instead through titles of affection: "O thou wrapped in garment! O thou enfolded!" 

How else might we perceive the tenderness of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫'s love? Perhaps our knowledge of Arabic is limited. Yet when a mother's love becomes excessive, she does not call her child by his name. She says: "My soul, the light of my eye, my darling, my sweet one." Why? Because the name itself cannot encompass her love. Within her breast there flows a flood of tenderness, and one name alone is insufficient to express it. Thus she summons him through names of love and endearment.

So ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ speaks to His Messenger: "O thou wrapped in garment! O thou enfolded! Arise, My beloved. Warn them. Glorify thy Lord. Be patient."

⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ charged His Messenger—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace—with a task, and through him, He entrusted that same task to his community. What are the obligations? To warn. To proclaim the greatness of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ to the people. Tell them: your ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ is the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate. Inform them: your Creator is ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫, Who created the heavens and the earth. Say unto them:

"Your Lord is ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫, the Creator of the heavens and the earth, Who fashioned them for your sustenance. He created mankind from clay."[^cite-50]

Tell them that their Lord is He Who raised the heavens high: ⟪ar-quote:اللہ الذی خلق سبع سماوات وَرَفَعَ سَمْكَهَا فَسَوَّىٰهَا⟫[^cite-51] Tell them that ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ is He Who governs the cycle of day and night. Tell them that ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ spread forth the earth: ⟪ar-quote:وَٱلْأَرْضَ بَعْدَ ذَٰلِكَ دَحَىٰهَا⟫ Tell them that from the earth ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ has brought forth:

⟪ar-quote:أَخْرَجَ مِنْهَا مَآءَهَا وَمَرْعَىٰهَا۔فَأَنۢبَتْنَا فِيهَا حَبًّۭا وَعِنَبًۭا وَقَضْبًۭا وَزَيْتُونًۭا وَنَخْلًۭا وَحَدَآئِقَ غُلْبًۭا وَفَكِهَةًۭ وَأَبًّۭا⟫[^cite-52]

Water and pasture, grain and grapes, verdure and olive, date-palms and gardens of dense foliage, fruit and herbage. Your Lord created all these bounties, and why? That you and your beasts might benefit. Thus arose the imperative: "Arise and warn! Keep your garments clean. Purify thy outer form and thy inner heart. Remain distant from filth—let no stain of deception, no mark of arrogance, no blemish of avarice, no taint of lust, no shade of cowardice, no trace of cruelty besmirch thy being. Let thy outer raiment be pure and thy inner garment likewise."

Do not perform good deeds in hope of reward from the people. Rather, be steadfast for the sake of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ alone.

A guest came to the home of one of the Companions of the Messenger of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace. The weather was cold and a fire burned in the hearth. After the meal had been taken, the Companion washed the hands of his guest and offered him a towel. As each guest dried his hands, the towel became saturated. The Companion then cast the towel into the fire. The people cried out in astonishment: "What do you do?" He withdrew the towel from the flames with tongs, and not a single thread had been burned. When the people marveled, he explained: "The Messenger of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ once came to my home, and after eating, I presented this very towel to dry his hands. From that day when this towel touched the blessed hands of the Messenger, fire has had no power over it."[^cite-53]

If fire cannot touch that which the Messenger of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ has touched, what of the fire of the Hereafter? If one's being is made to resemble that of the Messenger, will the fire of Perdition ever consume him?

Earn thy livelihood, but do so through lawful means. The Archangel Gabriel—may peace be upon him—has instilled this truth within my breast: ⟪ar-quote:بلا وَ أنَّ جِبرَئِیلَ نَفَسَ فی رُّوحیِ اَنَّ النَّفسَ لَم تموتَ حتّٰی تَستَک مِنَ الرِّزقَھا⟫[^cite-54] "No soul shall depart this world until it has received its full sustenance." Therefore, let thy seeking be noble and let thy piety remain steadfast.

The Messenger of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace—declared: "My community may commit any transgression, yet they cannot lie and deceive."[^cite-55] Yet in our time, Islam and deception have become indistinguishable. To lie and to be Muslim have become one. What we do unto others, our community does unto itself.

Therefore, do not speak carelessly and then ask me: "Why was the caravan plundered? Why were the merchants robbed?" I do not fault the bandits; rather, I ask: where is thy guidance? Where is thy character?

*(A digression: The Incident of the Monk)*

The nature of Islam is not lengthy discourse. That manner of extended speech belongs to those estranged from the faith. The farewell sermon of the Prophet—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace—which he delivered at Ghadir Khumm before a multitude of seventy thousand souls, lasted but ten to fifteen minutes.[^cite-56] Why require more? For he himself was the Qur'an walking upon the earth. His character, his morals, his words, and his silences were the embodiment of the Qur'an itself.

Once a disbeliever in Medina said to a Companion: "Show me your Qur'an, your book." At that very moment, the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī, passed before him. The Companion gestured and said: "This is our Qur'an—the Qur'an in human form. If you wish to see the Qur'an made manifest, behold him as he walks before you."[^cite-57] Such was the character of those people in those times. Where is the Qur'an today? It remains—still present in the form of our Imam, our guide, yet concealed from our sight. Before us walk the Messengers who interpret this Qur'an for us, yet we have not the time to understand even the Qur'an itself. How then shall we learn?

Thus ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ commanded His Prophet—may ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ bless him and grant him peace:

⟪ar-quote:قُلْ هَذِهِۦ سَبِيلِىٓ أَدْعُوٓا۟ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ ۚ عَلَىٰ بَصِيرَةٍ أَنَا۠ وَمَنِ ٱتَّبَعَنِى ۖ وَسُبْحَنَ ٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أَنَا۠ مِنَ ٱلْمُشْرِكِينَ⟫[^cite-58]

"Say: 'This is my path; I call unto ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ with clear vision—I and whosoever follows me. Glorified be ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫, and I am not of the idolaters.'"

This is my path! Place thy finger upon it and declare: "Look! This is my path!" When we all sit together and I point and say "This is my way," no one understands whom I am indicating. The demonstrative *hādhā* specifies a particular path. ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫ says: "This is the way of Islam, for me and for those who obey me, with clear discernment and insight." Observe that He set Himself apart from all others: "I and those who follow me." All the Muslims had gathered, yet He distinguished Himself and His followers from the rest.

What is this path? The summons unto ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫, with insight. This is the way of me and those who obey the Messenger of ⟪ar:'اللہ'⟫.

## Allah Is the Lord

The first revelation Allah granted to humanity was His name. "I am God"—this utterance declares the Divine reality. Yet a name without attributes remains incomplete; caste is known by its qualities. When we recite *Alhamdulillah* (all praise is due to Allah), the name itself has emerged from the invocation. Then comes the definition through attributes: *al-Rahman al-Rahim* (the Compassionate, the Merciful). When an adjective adorns the Divine name, the personality becomes fully disclosed.

When we pronounce "Allah" or invoke "Allah" alone, all ninety-nine other attributes are inherent within that utterance. The name Allah is an abbreviation for the Divine itself—He who depends upon no one for His existence or sustenance. The self is identified through its attributes. Thereafter Allah declared: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." *Rabb al-ʿālamin*—the Sustainer, the Nurturer of all existence. The designation does not confine itself to this small terrestrial sphere. In Arabic, one cosmos is *ʿālam*; many worlds are *ʿālamīn*. Thus Allah has praised Himself as Lord of the Worlds—not merely keeper of your solitary realm, but Guardian of all sciences, all universes, all realms beyond our comprehension.

When we first contemplate our own world—this sphere called *al-ʿālam al-akbar*, the Greater World—we perceive how Allah provides it sustenance. We observe how the Almighty, beyond all measure, is both its Protector and its Nurturer, and how He preserves the life of the human dwelling within it.[^cite-59]

---

### The Lord of the Greater World

The Earth rotates at a velocity of approximately one thousand kilometers per hour. By this precise rotation, day and night are measured—roughly twelve hours of daylight, twelve hours of darkness, with only minor fluctuation. Labor is accomplished in daylight; rest is completed in the night.

Suppose Allah were to reduce that speed from one thousand to five hundred kilometers per hour. Could we, as a species, intervene? Could even the most developed nations—America, England—arrest such a decline? What would occur at five hundred kilometers per hour? The result would be twenty-four consecutive hours of daylight, followed by twenty-four consecutive hours of darkness. The sun's fire would scorch and incinerate; the night's cold would freeze and kill. Who maintains this velocity at its present measure? Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

If, conversely, Allah were to accelerate the rotation to two thousand kilometers per hour, could we apply brakes to the celestial sphere? Then six hours would pass as day, and six hours as night. Work would remain incomplete; rest would never arrive. We ourselves would perish. Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

Allah has positioned gravity within the earth—the force of attraction binding all things. Had gravity doubled, so that earth's diameter became not twenty-four thousand but forty-eight thousand kilometers, we would walk upon the ground as one walks in soft mud or wet cement. The gravitational pull would grip us so tightly that our feet would remain stuck.[^cite-60]

Had the earth tripled in mass, perhaps the strong man might extract his feet, yet the weak would remain helpless, unable even to lift them.

Had Allah magnified gravity fivefold, the attraction would have become so powerful that the youth of today—standing six feet tall—would be compressed to the size of a mouse. Gravity would pull so fiercely that growth itself would cease. Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

Now consider: what if Allah were to remove gravity entirely? The Sun stands ninety-three million miles distant, yet its flames extend three hundred thousand kilometers into space. Every second an explosion transpires there, converting helium into hydrogen, releasing energy equivalent to five hundred million atomic bomb detonations. Were gravity absent, the Earth would begin its descent toward the Sun at a velocity of five thousand kilometers per hour. The Sun's pull would increase that speed. Within mere months, the Earth would fall into the Sun's embrace, where the temperature reaches twenty-five million degrees Celsius. Iron becomes liquid at fifteen hundred degrees. Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

The atmosphere contains precise proportions: oxygen, twenty-one percent; nitrogen, seventy-eight percent; carbon dioxide, less than one percent; the remainder, trace gases. We possess no gauge, no instrument to maintain oxygen at exactly twenty-one percent. Is there any device preventing its increase? Yet if Allah were merely to increase it by one percent—from twenty-one to twenty-two—every dwelling would ignite. The flame from the kitchen stove would engulf the entire house's atmosphere. Were oxygen to reach twenty-five percent, forests would spontaneously combust. One element is set at twenty-one; another at seventy-eight. Whose hand controls this gauge? Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.

Do we possess dominion over the clouds? Should Allah withhold the rain, all life ceases. The Lord has said:

> Have you not observed how Allah drives the clouds forward, then heaps them together, then piles them into layers, and thereafter you see the rain emerging from within them? ⟪quran 24:43⟫

⟪ar-quote:أَنَّا صَبَبْنَا ٱلْمَآءَ صَبًّۭا۔ ثُمَّ شَقَقْنَا ٱلْأَرْضَ شَقًّۭا۔ فَأَنۢبَتْنَا فِيهَا حَبًّۭا۔ وَعِنَبًۭا وَقَضْبًۭا۔ وَزَيْتُونًۭا وَنَخْلًۭا۔ وَحَدَآئِقَ غُلْبًۭا۔ وَفَكِهَةًۭا وَأَبًّۭا۔ مَّتَعًۭا لَّكُمْ وَلِأَنْعَمِكُمْ⟫[^cite-61]

We have sent down water in abundance. Then We split the earth in fissures. Then We caused grain to grow in it, and grapes, and herbage, and olives, and palms, and gardens dense with growth, and fruits, and pasture—provision for you and your livestock.

Consider the mechanism of rain itself: ordinarily, clouds form at altitudes of twelve hundred meters. Were an object cast from such a height, gravity would accelerate it to a velocity of five hundred fifty-eight kilometers per hour. A bullet travels at seventeen hundred kilometers per hour—only three times faster. Should Allah release rain from such heights without the exercise of His power, drops would fall at five hundred fifty-eight kilometers per hour. Two drops would suffice to strike a person dead. Our dwellings, fashioned of wood, would be annihilated within half an hour of rain. What force could withstand such destruction?

Yet Allah has placed innumerable resistances within the air—mechanisms of friction that reduce the speed of falling rain. By the time drops reach the ground, their velocity is merely eight kilometers per hour. Allah Almighty dissipates five hundred fifty kilometers per hour of destructive force in the air itself. Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.[^cite-62]

God is the Lord! The One who nurtures and governs this small world also sustains all universes and galaxies. He is truly the Lord of the Worlds.

### On the Meaning of Lordship

Having set forth these proofs, I return to my central theme: what it means that Allah is our Lord.

The most deceptive temptation that befalls believers is this: to deny Allah as Lord even while acknowledging Him as God. A believer will never commit shirk (association) by prostrating to stone or accepting another as God. Ask an ordinary Muslim—one who does not even pray—to bow before a stone. He will refuse. Yet he will say, "Stone is not divine; my God is only Allah." Even a weary, spiritually exhausted Muslim knows instinctively that the object of prostration is Allah.

But here lies the tragedy: even a worshipper who prays five times daily, who rises for *tahajjud* (the pre-dawn vigil), often fails to recognize Allah as Lord. To acknowledge Allah as God is simple; to accept Him as Lord is the most difficult dimension of faith. The most deceptive trial that overtakes a person concerns precisely this—the Lordship of Allah. He runs after bread in the present world. He chases the necessities of life. Yet Allah introduced Himself first through this attribute, saying: "Be free from anxiety! Do not grieve! It is My function to sustain you. Yes, I shall test you to distinguish the lawful from the forbidden. I command you to pursue the path of legality and forsake what is illicit. On one side stands truth; on the other, falsehood. I say: advance toward truth; abandon lies. I am the Giver of all sustenance. Everything rests in My hands!"[^cite-63]

On one side rests honesty; on the other, deception. "I command: do not deceive; be honest." On one side stands justice; on the other, oppression. "Therefore: do not act unjustly; practice justice."

Then Satan approaches and whispers: "Money is everywhere—so where does it lead? Fame is abundant—where is its benefit? I am your enemy and shall raise you against His teaching. The truth? The only reality is money! The only substance is reputation. The only truth is worldly status. The only reality is the world itself." At such a moment, remember these words: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." The Lord is God. God is the Provider. You must renounce what you have been commanded to renounce, however beneficial it may appear. You must perform what you have been commanded to perform, however costly it may seem.

A sweet, restful night's sleep approaches. The peak of your business venture draws near. The height of enjoyment beckons. The office meeting is about to commence. The *muezzin* calls out: ⟪ar-quote:حی علی الصلاۃ!⟫ "Come to prayer!" Satan whispers: "Where are you going? You are abandoning such sweet repose. Your clients are waiting. Why would you leave? You have a meeting. Where are you going? Your examination looms. All your work will be ruined."

At that moment, remember: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." The Lord is God. He will provide for me.

Then, after a year of accumulated wealth, Allah commands: "Now expend from it in My name. Take out two and a half percent for My poor servants." It is not a trivial sum. For one possessing ten million rupees, it amounts to two hundred fifty thousand rupees. Satan whispers: "Invest this elsewhere. Initiate another transaction." But Allah says: "Do not forget: 'Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.' Do not forget that it is I who granted you these millions. I ask only two and a half percent in return—what I have already given—which is your poor brother's right."

Just as you honor the mosque, so too honor the poor. The poor are the location of your *zakāt* (obligatory alms). Do not humiliate them; should you do so, I will nullify your charity and your *zakāt* before My face.

The more we believe in this verse, the more peaceful and abundant our lives become.

God is the Lord! Not merely your Lord, but Lord of the Worlds. Lord of all that exists. When Allah created human souls, He posed this question:

⟪ar-quote:وَإِذْ أَخَذَ رَبُّكَ مِنۢ بَنِىٓ آدَمَ مِن ظُهُورِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَأَشْهَدَهُمْ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ أَلَسْتُ بِرَبِّكُم⟫[^cite-64]

He did not ask, "Am I not your God?" Instead, He asked: ⟪ar-quote:أَلَسْتُ بِرَبِّكُمْ⟫ "Am I not your Lord?"

And we answered: ⟪ar-quote:بَلَىٰ⟫ "Yes, You are."

Then Allah said: "Very well. Now go into the world. I shall observe whether you recognize Me as Lord or whether you submit to another's lordship."

When He later revealed the Qur'an, He commenced it with the concept of Lordship: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." When He completed the Revelation, He concluded likewise: *Qul awʿūdhu bi-rabbi al-nās*—"Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of Mankind." Beginning and ending rest upon Lordship. Then comes the grave after death. The first interrogation there is: "What is the meaning of Lord? Who is your Lord? To whom did you bow?"

Then consider prayer. In every *rakʿah* (unit of prayer), we recite: "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." We rise from bowing, saying: *Rabbanā laka al-hamd*—"Our Lord, to You belongs all praise." We proceed to prostration: *Subhāna rabbī al-ʿalā*—"Glory be to my Lord, the Most High." Lord, Lord, Lord, Lord! The word recurs constantly.

Why is it inscribed thus? Because Allah knew: "If My servant prostrates, he will indeed perform the act. But when he sits in the marketplace, he will say, 'Where shall I obtain bread?' When he goes to work, he will say, 'The company sustains me.' At that moment, he will forget that the Lord is Allah."

Therefore, Allah reminds us:

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم خَلَقْتُ السمٰوات و الارضِ ولَم أَعیَکَ بِخَلقھم⟫

O son of Adam! I have created the seven heavens and seven earths—all that exists between them—and I have not grown weary in creating them.

⟪ar-quote:أفیوعنی یغیث اغیثہ اسوخہ الیک⟫ Will your Lord then grow weary of sustaining you alone?

⟪ar-quote:یا ابن آدم لا تُکاَلِمْکَ بِعَمَلِ غَد⟫

O My servants! I did not ask you to labor yesterday. I shall not examine you on tomorrow's deeds. I do not know whether fortune awaits you tomorrow.

⟪ar-quote:فلا تَسأَلنِی عَن رِزْقِ غَد⟫

Therefore, do not ask Me today for tomorrow's sustenance. Do not torment yourself with anxiety for the future. I have made it incumbent upon Myself.

Indeed, those who said, "Our Lord is Allah," and thereafter remained steadfast.

The angels will descend upon them. And what is the reward for those who believe in Allah as Lord?

The angels will say to them: "Do not fear, nor grieve. Rejoice in the Paradise which you were promised."

The Lord is One whose treasures overflow without diminishment. God is He who requires neither sleep nor fatigue. The Lord is One who gives without reluctance, unburdened by the fear of depletion. The Paradise that has been created—Allah created it. The Hell that has been formed—Allah fashioned it. He who controls life and death, creates and kills, resurrects and regenerates!

The One who possesses all these attributes alone deserves to be called Lord! And He is Allah alone![^cite-67]

### The Examination of the World

God created this world and made it a testing ground. He placed within it truth and falsehood. Honey as well as poison. Marriage as well as adultery. Commerce as well as usury. Lawful sustenance and forbidden. Honesty and betrayal. Trust and fraud. Then He made the forbidden attractive and stripped attraction from the lawful. And then He said:

⟪ar-quote:قُل لَّا يَسْتَوِى ٱلْخَبِيثُ وَٱلطَّيِّبُ وَلَوْ أَعْجَبَكَ كَثْرَةُ ٱلْخَبِيثِ⟫[^cite-68]

Say: The impure and the pure are not equal, even though the quantity of impure things may astonish you.

Then He said to us: "Am I not your Lord?" ⟪ar-quote:أَلَسْتُ بِرَبِّكُمْ⟫ We responded: "You are our Lord." ⟪ar-quote:بلی⟫ Then He commanded: "Go forth! Avoid deception. Avoid lies. Avoid alcohol. Avoid adultery. Avoid usury. Avoid the unlawful. Avoid betrayal. Avoid theft. Avoid indecency. Avoid obscenity. Avoid backbiting."

What did we do? What did we accomplish in this examination hall? What did we achieve in that test?

Is it merely a test to sit in a mosque and recite? Is it merely a trial to sit at prayer? No—this is a test of the marketplace! It is a test in shops, in businesses, in schools, in colleges, in universities, in office examinations. God wishes to observe: does he believe Me as Lord, or does he believe in God? Does he consider Me as Lord, or does he consider the customer his lord? Does he believe I am Lord, or does he consider the shop his lord? Does he recognize Me as Lord, or does he accept the teacher or principal as lord? Does he acknowledge Me as Lord, or does he bow to the company and the boss?

The Lord is God! God is the Lord!

We said: "O Allah, if we do this, where shall sustenance come from?" God replied:

⟪ar-quote:لا تَخشیٰ مِنَ الفَقر ماذا ما خَزَائِن مَلئَا⟫

Do not fear poverty so long as My treasures are filled. Only learn to ask Me. Speak to Me.

⟪ar-quote:توالینی و اوالیک، تصافینی و اصافیک⟫

If you befriend Me, I will befriend you. If you are sincere with Me, I will be sincere with you.

You have spent your life attempting friendship with people. Yet none has remained loyal to you. You have deceived everyone. O My servants, befriend Me once! I will grant you the right of friendship. Love Me once. I will bestow upon you the right of your love.

Such is the Lord! Into what deception have we fallen? "If I do not lie, I will lose my job. If I do not cheat, I will not profit."

God has freed us from the trial of accepting another as god—a trial into which much of creation has fallen, reducing humanity to the status of animals. God has liberated us. Allah has extracted us from that trial. Praise be to Allah! Our trial is the subsequent matter: the Lord of the Worlds! Lord of the Worlds. God is the Lord! The Lord is God. So what must we do? Speak truth! What must we do? Do not deceive anyone. What must we do? Do not engage in unlawful transactions. What must we do? Do not consume interest. If you accomplish even one such day, you have considered Me your Lord. Then you have believed in Me as Lord. Then you have fulfilled the right of the Lord of the Worlds.

The mosque is not the only path to Paradise. The marketplace too is a path to Paradise. The mosque is not the sole source of Paradise. Schools, colleges, offices, shops—all these, through righteous labor, lead to Paradise. Yet the test of the mosque is straightforward, whereas the test of the marketplace is profoundly difficult. Toil in cold; labor in heat. Prayer demands two or three hours of effort. The integrity of the marketplace demands twenty-four hours of constancy.

When the Messenger of Allah passed through the market, he observed a heap of wheat in one place. He inserted his hand and withdrew a grain. It was wet. "Why is this wet grain hidden beneath the dry?" he asked. The merchant replied: "It rained and became wet. I placed dried wheat upon it to conceal it." The Prophet commanded: "Remove it so that the buyer knows it is wet and perceives the defect." Then he declared:

⟪ar-quote:من غَشَّ لیس مِنّٰی⟫

Whoever deceives does not belong to my community.[^cite-69]

You are the Lord's servant! With this belief, the marketplace-goer will be transformed. With this conviction, the office-worker, the student, the shop-owner will be different. But whoever considers gold, silver, dollar, or pound as his lord will exist in a different state.

Thus, first and foremost, our deeds emerge tainted with unlawfulness. Yet beyond this, deceit, theft, backbiting, anger arise from the remainder.

### The Test of Truth

*Hajjāj ibn Yūsuf* was a ruler of terrible cruelty. He executed approximately one hundred twenty-five thousand people before his time ended. In his era, a revolt arose. Two sons of Ibn al-Khārash participated in the rebellion. This family was known for never uttering falsehoods. When the rebellion was suppressed, the two sons fled and concealed themselves. After time passed, they returned home in secret. Spies informed *Hajjāj ibn Yūsuf* that Ibn al-Khārash's two sons had returned home. Al-*Hajjāj* decided: "I shall test Ibn al-Khārash today. I shall observe how much he truly speaks truth."

He summoned Ibn al-Khārash and said: "Brother Ibn al-Khārash, your two sons participated in war against me and then fled. Do you know where they are?" Ibn al-Khārash smiled and replied: "O *Hajjāj*! Life and death are in God's hands. They have returned home. If you wish to seize them, do so." *Hajjāj* said: "Ibn al-Khārash! I grant you your two sons alive in exchange for your truth-telling! Had you lied, I would have severed your necks and theirs."

The Prophet was once asked: "O Messenger of Allah, can a Muslim be a coward?" He replied: "Yes, perhaps." Then: "Can a Muslim be stingy?" "Yes, perhaps." Then: "Can a Muslim lie?" He did not say "No." For if one takes a mate, two things are impossible: one can never lie; one cannot deceive another. Yet today we do nothing without lies and deception.

May Allah grant me and grant you all the strength to recognize this truth.

### The Lordship of Divine Providence

God is the Lord! The Lord is God. This is a small world; humanity is diminished within it; the stomach is limited; the lifespan is brief; the necessities are finite. Yet He said: "No! Go forth! Labor! For this is an examination hall. When you return to Allah, then present the results."

What is the outcome of this result? Life is freed from death. Youth is freed from old age. Health is freed from disease. Joy is freed from sorrow. Elevation is freed from decline. If comfort were freed from sorrow, how magnificent would such a soul be? If you are sent into the world, hold both truths in mind: this is a place of examination. The lawful and the forbidden exist here. Now only the lawful is lawful; no trace of the forbidden remains.

Three types of deeds will never succeed. Within them exists oppression, within them exists lies, within them exists deception—humiliation is written in the destiny of such a one. Even if he spends entire nights in *tahajjud*, performs *hajj* and *ʿumrah* every year, he cannot be saved from Hell. Conversely, three types of deeds will never fail. Honor is written in their destiny. He who speaks truth will never lie. He who practices justice will never oppress. He who acts with honesty will never deceive. God will honor him.

There exists one law of Allah for people in this world and a different law in the Hereafter. Let us be honest. The Prophet declared:

⟪ar-quote:الصدق ینجی و الکذب یھلک⟫

Truth delivers; lies destroy.[^cite-70]

⟪ar-quote:لعنت اللہ علی الظالمین⟫ May Allah's curse be upon the wrongdoers.

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلْخَآئِنِينَ۔ وَأَنَّ ٱللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِى كَيْدَ ٱلْخَآئِنِينَ⟫[^cite-71]

Allah does not love the deceivers. Allah does not guide the stratagems of the deceivers.

Consider Allah as your Lord! Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds! Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds. Think upon this! Then examine your life: where have we resorted to lies? Where have we turned to fraud?

The Prophet said: "Do not come to the mosque after eating raw onions, for there is a foul odor from your mouth." If I lie, will my mouth stink? Yet the Prophet declared: "When a person tells a lie, his mouth emits such a stench that the angels flee from him for miles." The angels depart! The angels are our hope. They are our guardian protectors. When Satan assails us, our first line of defense is the angels. If they flee, who can protect us from Satan's attack?

Islam is the struggle to attain this belief. It is essential to transform this belief system from the very root of the heart—that the Savior is Allah. God is the Provider. Therefore, I believe in God! I believe in God as my Lord! I keep watch over God's treasures. The Lord is God! Do not lie for a pittance of money. Do not lie for a small profit. Do not regard the harm of this world; look to the benefit of the life to come. Is there not a life ahead? Is there not a meeting with Allah and His Messenger?

There is a narrative from *Ehsan Danish*, a Pakistani writer. He has authored a book entitled *Life of Wisdom*. In it, he relates: During British rule in Delhi, there lived a pickpocket with many disciples. Each evening they would bring him their stolen earnings. One day, a disciple arrived with two rupees—merely two rupees! The teacher asked: "Today, did you not find a wealthy target?" The disciple replied: "Teacher, today I entrapped a very wealthy European. An Englishman's pocket held considerable wealth. I removed four steps forward, but then I reflected: on the Day of Judgment, if Jesus complained to my Prophet that his companion had stolen from his people, how ashamed would my Prophet be because of me. So I returned his money and instead took two rupees from someone else's pocket." The teacher was overjoyed and rewarded the disciple with ten rupees, saying: "You have thought of something most elevated."

This disciple was conscious of the embarrassment of the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him). We are believers, yet we do not care about the embarrassment of the Messenger of Allah. We too live here among people of various faiths. Let us deal with them honestly and not deceive them, so that no harm comes to our Prophet on the Day of Resurrection. The Messenger of Allah himself said in his final sermon:

⟪ar-quote:لا تسود وجھی یوم القیامۃ⟫

O my community! Do not shame me before Allah on the Day of Resurrection. Do not humiliate me. If we cannot honor our Prophet, let us at least spare him the humiliation of reproach. We can preserve him from that disgrace.

In this way, life comes forth, and everything else becomes nothing. Release greed and lust.

We were in Kuwait; in one hour, life transformed. Years of earnings froze in that hour. Then we had to begin again from zero. What use is such wealth?

### Faith and Conviction

There is a narrative from among the Children of Israel. A cleric would preach with great conviction: "Whoever recites *Bismillāh al-raḥmān al-raḥīm* (In the Name of Allah, the Compassionate, the Merciful) with faith, even if he walks upon water, will cross without drowning."

The next day, a villager approached and said: "Maulvi sahib, you have made my life so easy!"

"How so?" asked the cleric.

"According to your words, I recited *Bismillāh al-raḥmān al-raḥīm* and walked upon the river and crossed without drowning. I always faced trouble before and had to pay the boatman when I had no money. Now it has become simple for me."

The cleric, who had preached this sermon, exclaimed in astonishment: "Really? Are you telling the truth?"

"Yes, absolutely," replied the villager.

The cleric said: "No, you are lying. If you are truthful, show me." They both approached the river. The villager loudly proclaimed *"Bismillāh"* and stepped upon the water, crossing it. Then he said to the cleric: "Now come." The cleric replied: "Tie me with a rope first, then I will step upon the water."

Thus you see: it cannot work that way.

Allah is our Savior. Everything rests in His control:

⟪ar-quote:قُلِ ٱللَّهُمَّ مَلِكَ ٱلْمُلْكِ تُؤْتِى ٱلْمُلْكَ مَن تَشَآءُ وَتَنزِعُ ٱلْمُلْكَ مِمَّن تَشَآءُ وَتُعِزُّ مَن تَشَآءُ وَتُذِلُّ مَن تَشَآءُ ۖ بِيَدِكَ ٱلْخَيْرُ ۖ إِنَّكَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌۭ⟫[^cite-72]

Say: "O Allah, Master of Dominion! You grant dominion to whom You will, and You remove dominion from whom You will. You honor whom You will, and You humiliate whom You will. In Your hand is all good. Truly, You have power over all things."

⟪ar-quote:تُولِجُ ٱلَّيْلَ فِى ٱلنَّهَارِ وَتُولِجُ ٱلنَّهَارَ فِى ٱلَّيْلِ ۖ وَتُخْرِجُ ٱلْحَىَّ مِنَ ٱلْمَيِّتِ وَتُخْرِجُ ٱلْمَيِّتَ مِنَ ٱلْحَىِّ ۖ وَتَرْزُقُ مَن تَشَآءُ بِغَيْرِ حِسَابٍۢ⟫[^cite-73]

He bestows dominion upon whom He wills; He withdraws it from whom He wills. He elevates whom He wills; He humbles whom He wills. He may increase the sustenance of whom He wills or diminish it. No one can lengthen the night. No one can shorten the day. This belief springs from the recognition that "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." The Lord is God.

### Training and the Heart

Training is the responsibility of parents. Schools cannot educate the heart. Train with love, not with force. If you provide religious training at home, then place your child in any school you wish—success will follow. Teach a child how to swim; then cast him into the river or the sea—he will swim. Should he never learn to swim, he will drown even in a stream. Parents say: "Give my child a holiday. Let him go to the teacher." Yet where can the teacher train the child's character?

Islam is not a title; it is a quality of life.[^cite-74]

## Identity

When a person attempts to solve the world's problems through his own intellect and effort alone, confusion multiplies rather than diminishes. Should he chart his own path, he becomes a wanderer upon unseen roads. Should he establish his own priorities, he inherits only desires and failures. Allah has made mankind weak; He has granted only meager knowledge. The Real belongs to Him alone.

God brought you forth from your mothers' wombs knowing nothing:

⟪ar-quote:وَٱللَّهُ أَخْرَجَكُم مِّنۢ بُطُونِ أُمَّهَتِكُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ شَيْـًۭٔا⟫

In truth, then, the origin of human existence is ignorance—an ignorance boundless and without measure. Knowledge, by contrast, comprises only a handful of words. We read a few books, yet Allah Himself declares:

⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ أُوتِيتُم مِّنَ ٱلْعِلْمِ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا⟫

The knowledge you have been given is exceedingly little. Observe that the Qur'an employs the word *autitam*—"given to you"—indicating that knowledge is bestowed rather than seized. God is the Giver; He did not say "what you have earned," or "what you have learned," or "what you have taken," or "what you have studied," or "what you have memorized." All these alternatives might have appeared here. Instead, God declares: *"I have given it."* How much has been given? *"Except a little."* Very little indeed.[^cite-75]

If we advance relying upon so meager a knowledge, we shall surely stumble into error. Should we attempt to govern nations, manage institutions, direct households, or guide ourselves by intellect alone, darkness alone awaits us. We become as one lost in a dense forest, finding no passage to freedom.

Yet this is not all. Allah presents even graver accusations against the human state. Man is exceedingly hasty (*ʿajūl*). With scant knowledge and hasty temperament, error compounds. Man quarrels over trifles (*jadal*). Less knowledge, more haste, more anger. Man is ungrateful (*kafūr*). Less knowledge, more haste, more anger, more ingratitude. Man is a great tyrant to himself and others (*ẓalūm jahūl*). With diminished knowledge comes hastiness, anger, ingratitude, and cruelty multiplied. When hardship touches him, he despairs and loses hope (*yuʾs qunut*). Thus the human condition gathers: meager knowledge, excessive haste, anger, ingratitude, cruelty, and despair.[^cite-76]

⟪ar-quote:وَإِن مَّسَّهُ ٱلشَّرُّ فَيَـُٔوسٌۭ قَنُوطٌۭ⟫

Yet when We grant him ease after trial, he becomes arrogant and proud, imagining his misfortunes have departed and boasting of his merit:

⟪ar-quote:وَلَىِٕنْ أَذَقْنَهُ نَعْمَآءَ بَعْدَ ضَرَّآءَ مَسَّتْهُ لَيَقُولَنَّ ذَهَبَ ٱلسَّيِّـَٔاتُ عَنِّىٓ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ لَفَرِحٌۭ فَخُورٌ⟫

He walks upon the earth with heels striking as though to tear it asunder, his neck raised as if to rival the mountains, heedless that:

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا⟫

What hole can you pierce in My earth? By what height can your neck rival the mountains? Swift is his forgetting of his station. When Allah grants position, wealth, beauty, or fame, pride and arrogance flood the heart at once.[^cite-77]

These verses from the Qur'an, remembered here, represent but a fraction of the divine testimony to human frailty, weakness, and ignorance. Many other reproaches fill the Qur'anic text, all declaring that mankind bears profound defects within—in men and women alike. With such limitations, with intellect and knowledge so diminished, how can anyone presume to govern a nation, administer a city, manage a household, or oversee school, college, or university through reason alone? There is no destination but wandering. There is no end but being lost. There is no result but failure. Mankind does not possess the capacity to establish the foundations of life upon intellect and experience alone.

The Prophets descend into this world to redeem humanity from this defect. For the knowledge of the Prophets does not arise from reading or learning. The knowledge of the Prophets comes directly from Allah.[^cite-78]

⟪ar-quote:وَمَا عَلَّمْنَهُ ٱلشِّعْرَ وَمَا يَنۢبَغِى لَهُۥٓ ۚ إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا ذِكْرٌۭ وَقُرْآنٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ⟫

O My beloved! I did not teach you poetry, nor was poetry fitting for you. Tomorrow none should say that he authored the Qur'an himself. The children of Arabia were poets. When a boy was born into a house, they did not say, "Congratulations, a son has come!" Rather they asked, "What is the poet's name?" Such was their veneration of the poetic craft.

The people whispered that these words came from times past:

⟪ar-quote:إِنْ هَذَآ إِلَّآ أَسَطِيرُ ٱلْأَوَّلِينَ⟫

But Allah said to His Beloved: "I Myself shall teach you directly!" and He revealed:

⟪ar-quote:وَأَنزَلَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْحِكْمَةَ وَعَلَّمَكَ مَا لَمْ تَكُن تَعْلَمُ ۚ وَكَانَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكَ عَظِيمًۭا⟫

It is I Who gave it to you! I expanded your breast:

⟪ar-quote:الم نشرح لک صدرک؟⟫

I removed from you your burden:

⟪ar-quote:ووضعنا عنک وزرک⟫

That which broke your back:

⟪ar-quote:الذی انقد ظھرک⟫

And I raised your remembrance:

⟪ar-quote:ورفعنا لک ذکرک⟫

Allah Almighty bestows upon His Prophets directly through revelation (waḥy). It is narrated that Wahb ibn Munabbih said: "What Allah granted to one hundred twenty-five thousand Prophets is like a single atom compared to what He granted His Beloved—like a drop beside an ocean." The knowledge of one hundred twenty-four thousand Prophets stands to the knowledge of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) as a single grain to an entire desert. God taught him directly:

⟪ar-quote:وَكَذَلِكَ أَوْحَيْنَآ إِلَيْكَ رُوحًۭا مِّنْ أَمْرِنَا ۚ مَا كُنتَ تَدْرِى مَا ٱلْكِتَبُ وَلَا ٱلْإِيمَنُ وَلَكِن جَعَلْنَهُ نُورًۭا نَّهْدِى بِهِ⟫

Thus did We reveal unto you, O My Beloved, a spirit from Our command. You knew not the Scripture nor the faith; yet We made it a light by which We guide whom We will.

Ignorant Muslims claim that the Prophet (peace be upon him) could neither read nor write. God forbid! Is it conceivable that the teacher of all mankind lacked these skills? This objection arises from the event at Ḥudaybiyyah (⟪ar:سلیہ ہدیبیہ⟫). It happened thus: Hazrat ʿAlī, upon the Prophet's command, was inscribing the peace treaty. He wrote: "In the name of Allah, the Most Compassionate, the Most Merciful." Suhayl ibn ʿUmar objected: "Cut out 'the Most Merciful'—we do not know who that is. Write it as 'In Your name, O Allah.'"[^cite-79] The Prophet instructed ʿAlī: "Erase it and write as he requests." Then ʿAlī wrote: "This is the covenant Muhammad, the Messenger of Allah, has made with the Quraysh." Again Suhayl protested: "If we accepted you as Messenger, we would not have fought you. Write: 'Muhammad ibn ʿAbd Allah.'" The Prophet told ʿAlī: "Erase the title." Yet ʿAlī replied: "O Messenger of Allah! I cannot erase what my hand has written after accepting you as Messenger of Allah in my heart." The Prophet said: "Show me where it is written." When ʿAlī pointed, the Prophet placed his finger upon those letters and erased them himself, saying: "I am Muhammad, the Messenger of Allah, and Muhammad ibn ʿAbd Allah."

Yet some argue: If the Messenger knew how to write, why did he ask ʿAlī to show him where the words appeared? Our scholars respond: When a chief executive must sign a document, he may ask his secretary where to sign. Does this indicate he cannot read or write? The analogy is clear. The Prophet's question revealed not ignorance but his deliberate act of having ʿAlī himself perform the erasure—a gesture of respect and a test of loyalty.

When mankind attempts to advance through intellect alone, ignoring divine guidance, he finds only darkness. The Prophets came to rescue him from this predicament. Their knowledge does not come from study or books, but directly from Allah. This is the foundation upon which human salvation rests.

## The Night Journey and the Meeting with the Divine

⟪ar-quote:سُبْحَنَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِۦ لَيْلًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْحَرَامِ إِلَىٰ ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْأَقْصَا⟫

Glory be to Him who brought His servant by night from the Sacred Mosque to the Farthest Mosque. The journey appointed was in darkness—the night was fixed for this ascent. There lies in this verse a profound indication: he who desires to encounter Allah, to meet with Him, must surrender the night hours to his Lord. One must rise from the laptop and the computer, from bed and worldly comfort, to devote the darkness of night to prayer and remembrance. Intimacy with the Divine flowers in darkness.

Gabriel came. The Prophet was conveyed to Jerusalem. From there unfolded the ascent through the heavens. The Qur'anic phrasing itself bears witness. The verse should have said only "Subḥān al-lladhi asrā biʿabdihi"—"Glory be to Him who brought His servant"—yet it also includes "bīʿabdihi"—"with His servant." In Arabic grammar, either the alif or the bāʾ suffices; both are not necessary. Yet Allah brought forth both letters, signaling both the departure and the intimate companionship. When a great personage arrives, one does not remain seated indoors waiting. One stands outside to receive him. When your teacher, your beloved approaches, you do not tarry but hasten to greet him. Thus Allah inscribed both letters in this verse, teaching His servants: "Emulate your Prophet's honor! When I Myself came down to receive him, neither did I summon him nor did I dispatch Gabriel to call him. I came Myself to receive My Beloved. Therefore understand his standing." And thus it is said: *"Glory be to Him who caused His servant to walk in the darkness of the night."*

The ascent continued from heaven to heaven until the Prophet reached a place where:

⟪ar-quote:ثُمَّ دَنَا فَتَدَلَّىٰ فَكَانَ قَابَ قَوْسَيْنِ أَوْ أَدْنَىٰ⟫

Then he drew near and descended, and was at a distance of two bows' lengths or nearer still. The veil between creature and Creator grows thin. Yet how can human language, confined to twenty-eight letters, encompass the immensity of a Prophet whom Allah has made His Beloved, His chosen one? The interpretations remain too small; his station stands too high. How can words in any tongue adequately convey what eyes have witnessed and hearts have known?

These are the great beings whom Allah sustained in vessels of gold and silver, dwelling in houses of marble, wearing garments of silk, resting upon the softest of cushions, riding upon creatures like Burāq itself—yet the Prophet's own household lacked even fire for two months to warm their dwelling. His most beloved daughter, Fāṭimah—a piece of his heart—he said: "Whoever hurts her hurts me; whoever gladdens her gladdons me." She is the leader of the women of Paradise. His grandsons, al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn, are the leaders of the youth of Paradise. ʿAlī is his guardian, the one who settles his debts.

When the Prophet learned that Fāṭimah had fallen ill, he set out to visit her, accompanied by his companion ʿImrān ibn Ḥuṣayn. He knocked at her door and asked: "May I enter, O daughter?" Fāṭimah replied: "O Messenger of Allah! I have not even a single sheet to cover myself." The Prophet removed the cloth from his own shoulder and said: "Wrap yourself in this." Thus covered, she received her father and his companion. When he inquired about her state, she wept, saying: "Father! For three days, there has been nothing to eat in this house. First came hunger without food; now comes sickness without medicine." The Messenger of Allah stepped forward, held her to his breast, placed his chin upon her head, and tears flowed from his eyes. How many nights had he spent in the deserts of Ṭāʾif eating stones, pursued and wounded, his eyes remaining dry? Yet now, seeing his daughter's suffering, tears fell like rain upon her face.

She is the finest daughter in all creation; he is the finest father in all creation. Together they wept. And then he said: "O daughter, be patient! For three days now, your father has not eaten a morsel of bread. You grieve, yet even now, unburdened as I should be, you bear hunger. My Lord said to me: 'O My Beloved, shall I make for you all the mountains of Arabia into gold and silver?' And I replied: 'No, my Lord! I choose the poverty of prophecy over the luxury of a kingdom.' Then He said: 'It suffices you, O Fāṭimah, that you are the leader of the women of Paradise.'"

These chieftains of the Prophet's house wore no jeweled ornaments, no designer garments, no luxurious automobiles, no grand mansions. These leaders of the youth of Paradise were found with stones bound to their stomachs to still their hunger. They were found in patched garments, worn and torn.

A man can bear his own hunger, but the hunger of his children—this is unbearable. When al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn began to cry from hunger, the Prophet said: "Bring al-Ḥasan to me." He took the child in his arms, placed his own tongue in the child's mouth, and the boy sucked and fell asleep, soothed. Then he said: "Bring al-Ḥusayn," and repeated the same tender mercy. Thus were the leaders of Paradise nurtured—in hunger, in patience, in the presence of their grandfather's boundless love.

These are the Ahl al-Bayt (the People of the Household)! These are the Apostles! They are the finest family in all creation. Allah said: "I looked upon the entire universe and chose the Arabs; among the Arabs I chose the Quraysh; among the Quraysh I chose the Banū Hāshim; and from the Banū Hāshim, I chose myself." The Prophet is the finest man in creation, both in lineage and in character.

I sacrifice myself for love of this Prophet, for his love toward you. He taught us: "When you supplicate Allah, do not ask for hunger; ask for redemption. Say: 'O Allah, grant us good in this world and good in the Hereafter.'" Yet when he raised his hands for his children, he prayed: "O Allah, You are the Sustainer of the family of Muhammad. O Allah, reduce our provision in this life." He did not wish that on the Day of Judgment anyone should say to him: "Your children enjoyed abundance while our children suffered hunger." Rather, let his children taste poverty first, let them wear patched clothes first, let them drink poison first, let them be slaughtered first—only then would it be his people's turn. If sorrow weighs upon you, remember his sorrows, and your own grief shall seem light.

This is Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (peace be upon him)—he whose Creator is his own appreciator, He who created the creation and valued the creature! We have expelled him from our homes, from our offices, from our schools and universities, from commerce and politics, from every sphere—even from our hearts. How then shall our problems find solution?

No prophet has attained the station your Prophet has attained. He set foot upon a threshold where no creature's feet had ever trod before, and none shall tread there again until the Day of Judgment. Infinite veils hung before the Throne of Allah; He withdrew them all and said: "O My Beloved, come face to face." The Lord Himself offered greeting: *"Peace be upon you, O Prophet!"* Thus does the Creator honor the creature.

Perfect knowledge is the knowledge of your Prophet (peace be upon him). We must draw our light from that source. Only then shall we solve the problems of this world and the next. Otherwise, this darkness shall increase and compound; we shall sink ever deeper into the mire. Take the light from that source—from him who is himself a light, who came as light itself.[^cite-80]

⟪ar-quote:يَٓأَهْلَ ٱلْكِتَبِ قَدْ جَآءَكُمْ رَسُولُنَا يُبَيِّنُ لَكُمْ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا كُنتُمْ تُخْفُونَ مِنَ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَيَعْفُوا۟ عَن كَثِيرٍۢ ۚ قَدْ جَآءَكُم مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ نُورٌۭ وَكِتَبٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ⟫

O People of the Scripture! Our Messenger has come to you, clarifying much of what you concealed from the Scripture and overlooking much. There has come to you from Allah a Light and a clear Book.

To Abū Sufyān he declared:

⟪ar-quote:یا أبا سفیان! جئتکم بکرامت الدنیا و الآخرۃ⟫

O Abū Sufyān! I have brought you not only the honors of this world, but the honors of the Hereafter as well. Follow me!

And the Prophet proclaimed:

⟪ar-quote:آنا سَیَّدُ ولد أبا آدمِ یومَ القیامۃِ ولا فخرا⟫

I am the leader of the children of Adam on the Day of Resurrection—not in boasting, but in truth. In my hand shall be Allah's banner. In my hand shall be the keys to Paradise. Paradise itself is forbidden to all the Prophets until I enter it first. Paradise is forbidden to all nations until my *Ummah* has passed through.

## The Comprehensive Path

O beloved ones! The Prophet has brought not merely ritual but a life—a complete way of being. This is not confined to the mosque, nor to five daily prayers, nor to fasting, *Ḥajj*, or *Zakāt*. These are the outward forms, the visible pillars. Yet what comes after? Having fasted for a month, what then? Having paid the *Zakāt* once yearly, what then? Having performed *Ḥajj* once in a lifetime, what then? Two hours of worship distributed across sixteen waking hours leaves fourteen hours unaccounted for.

In a life of twenty-four hours, the Prophet (peace be upon him) declared:

⟪ar-quote:ترکتکم علی مَحَجَّۃٍ بَیضا، لَیلُھَا کَنَھَارُھَا، لا یَزِیغُ عَنھَا الا ھالک⟫

I leave you upon a radiant path—a *Sharīʿah* (the way) so luminous that its night is like its day. None shall deviate from it except the ruined. Should you follow, you shall reach your destination. Should you turn aside, you shall perish. The Prophet's bequest is a path called Islam (islām)—a state of surrender and peace. There is but one way to success, and that way is Islam.

⟪ar-quote:وَمَن يَبْتَغِ غَيْرَ ٱلْإِسْلَمِ دِينًۭا فَلَن يُقْبَلَ مِنْهُ وَهُوَ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ مِنَ ٱلْخَسِرِينَ⟫

Whoever seeks a religion other than Islam, it shall not be accepted from him, and in the Hereafter he shall be among the losers.

One foundation of Islam is paramount: inscribe upon your heart *Lā ilāha illā Allāh*—there is no god but Allah. All dominion rests in His hands alone:

⟪ar-quote:لِلَّهِ ٱلْأَمْرُ مِن قَبْلُ وَمِنۢ بَعْدُ⟫

To Allah belongs all command, before and after. His is the rule first; His is the rule last. What occurs here shall be as He wills, not as we will. He commands the lands; they obey. He commands the heavens; they obey. He commands the angels; they obey. He commands the clouds; they obey. He commands the rains; they obey. He commands the earth; it obeys. He causes fruits and seeds to grow upon it. Over all this extends the command of your Lord:

> Over the date palms and the grapes. Over all fruits and vegetation. The sun, the moon, and the stars—all are in Allah's dominion. *Is not He who created the creation the one who judges?* Allah is the Lord of the worlds.

The Creator is Allah. The Provider is Allah. The Ruler is Allah. From east to west, from north to south, Allah governs everywhere. Wherever the eye turns, Allah reigns. *"Allah is the Lord of the East and the West."* Look upward—Allah. Look downward—Allah. Look forward—Allah. Look backward—Allah. Look to the right—Allah. Look to the left—Allah.

My body is fashioned from clay. The Lord of wondrous attributes! This soul within me proclaims: *There is Allah!* The birds in flight cry out: *There is Allah!* The pearl within the shell whispers: *There is Allah!* The musk in the deer's navel spreads the message: *There is Allah!* A drop of water enters the belly of a small fly and transforms into honey, announcing: *There is Allah!* That same drop, passing into a serpent's mouth, becomes poison and declares: *There is Allah!* The turning of the seasons testifies; the rise and fall of kingdoms bear witness. Mighty kings have been cast upon their faces, their names and symbols erased, while Allah proclaims: *I am the Ruler!* And the weakest have been raised to thrones, that all might know: *I am the Sovereign!*

My Lord says:

⟪ar-quote:عرفت ربی بفسق العظائم⟫

I have known Allah through the shattering of my great plans.

Look everywhere and you shall see Allah—but bring eyes that do not gaze upon the forbidden. Respect no one if that respect veils the divine. If a sister cannot rise in shame before someone's daughter, then the glory of Allah fills every atom. When the eye beholds the unlawful, a film of sin clouds the lens. When cataracts form, surgery is required to restore sight. Pour into your eyes the shame of my Prophet—that medicinal shame—and then behold! By Allah, you shall see divine glory in the unseen world. You shall perceive divine power everywhere. The entire universe rests in Allah's hands.

Bow your head before Allah! There is none beside Him who creates, none who gives, none who withholds, none who raises, none who brings low, none who gives death, none who grants life, none who sits upon the Throne, none who descends from it. There is none who brings forth the day, none who brings forth the night. There is none who lights the stars, none who heats the sun, none who cools the moon. There is none who grants honor or inflicts humiliation.

⟪ar-quote:قُلِ ٱللَّهُمَّ مَلِكَ ٱلْمُلْكِ تُؤْتِى ٱلْمُلْكَ مَن تَشَآءُ وَتَنزِعُ ٱلْمُلْكَ مِمَّن تَشَآءُ وَتُعِزُّ مَن تَشَآءُ وَتُذِلُّ مَن تَشَآءُ ۖ بِيَدِكَ ٱلْخَيْرُ ۖ إِنَّكَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌۭ⟫

Say: O Allah, Master of all sovereignty! You grant kingship to whom You will and strip it from whom You will. You exalt whom You will and abase whom You will. In Your hand is all good. You are capable of all things.

Allah is everywhere! Allah is God! Whatever He willed, He brought into being. Whatever occurred yesterday is as He decreed. All that comes to pass—Allah! This is the first proclamation of the Prophet: *Seek only Allah's pleasure!* Do not look to profit or loss. Do not regard money or poverty, wealth or renown. Seek only the pleasure of Allah. When you have secured His contentment, you have secured all. Change your standards! Realign your preferences! Return the wealth. If wealth comes, return it. If poverty comes, *Mā shāʾ Allāh*—such is Allah's will. If you become poor, do not despair. If you become rich, do not become arrogant. But Allah is not a child that He may be deceived! He is not unaware! If Allah abandons you, you are lost. Your very first foundation has cracked. A nation whose deeds are corrupted may yet be reformed. But a nation whose foundations are corrupted—reform becomes exceedingly difficult.

Today our fundamental understanding has deteriorated. We no longer accord any honor to Allah. We do not honor the Prophet of Allah. We do not honor Mawlānā ʿAlī or the Imāms. We follow our own blind guides. We adopt the philosophies of Martin Luther King, of Gandhi. Yet Allah commanded:

⟪ar-quote:لا تستطیہ بنار المشرکین⟫

Do not take the polytheists as your advisors.

They shall cast you into the pit. When our leader is Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (peace be upon him), why do we turn to these others? Why do those who possess the example of Banū Hāshim, the message of Karbalāʾ, wander in darkness? Why are we blind? Why do we not avail ourselves of these lights? We extinguish the lamps of our religion. We erase the light that honored our parents. We adopt fashions that strip away the modesty of our daughters, making them shameless, dancing before every lustful eye. We do not need such a "civilization." If this is the light of modern culture, we choose the darkness of our religion! In that darkness, parents are honored and daughters wear the veil. The wealthy show generosity; the poor maintain self-respect.

## The Foundation: Allah's Pleasure Alone

This is the first principle, O Allah: *I must seek Your pleasure.* You have given me something; I shall give thanks. Something departs from me; I shall be patient. Your contentment is the purpose of my life. This too is the message of Karbalāʾ. Imām al-Ḥusayn endured hunger and thirst; his children were slaughtered before him; he was cut and mounted upon a spear—yet he would not anger his Lord. Yazīd made a wretched bargain, securing his throne while earning eternal curse. Imām al-Ḥusayn made a sublime bargain, sacrificing himself and his family for Allah's pleasure; mercy has poured down upon him from that day until now.

How difficult it is to witness children dying before one's eyes! How unbearable to see them slaughtered! Yet the Prophet's grandsons were being slaughtered, and he who suffered this calamity possessed the power to save all—his household, his children—with a single word. Yet he refrained. Al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn are the leaders of the youth of Paradise (*Sayyidā shabāb ahl al-Jannah*). Paradise was already decreed for them from their childhood. Why then does one writhe in poison and the other's head rest upon a spear?

So that the message might reach us. The message would have been incomplete without this sacrifice. It was conveyed that everything can be surrendered before Allah. Lose Allah, and you lose all. They proclaimed that faith must not be sold for the passions of government, for the lust of wealth, for false petty loves. To please Allah—this alone is the goal.

*If Allah is pleased with me, then everything in my life is well. If He is displeased, then all my happiness is dust.*

Read your history. Read the biography of your Prophet. Who won? Who found Allah? Who lost? Who lost Allah? It is not mere tears that wash away sorrow. Neither life changes nor character transforms through weeping alone. The twelve days of Rabīʿ al-Awwal are not for processions only; every moment must be a remembrance of the Prophet. The message of Karbalāʾ is to remember sacrifice in every moment.

These messages were given so that no one should sell his faith for bread, for love, for fame, for honor. If my Allah is not asleep, then know with certainty: *You shall never see blessing in unlawful earnings.* And Allah does not sleep.

*There is no slumber nor sleep for Him. The heavens and the earth stand by His command.* He who earns lawfully shall never see autumn (deprivation), and he who earns unlawfully shall never see spring (relief). This is the message of my Prophet! *Find Allah! Learn to live for Allah! Learn to die for Allah!* Then Allah shall be yours.

When Allah is angry, where shall we seek refuge? When our own sins have pierced holes in our boat with our own hands, how can we escape drowning in the sea?

The problems in our lives do not arise because the government is corrupt, because conditions are difficult, because money is scarce. The problems arise because *Allah is angry with us.* The Prophet has given the message: *Keep Allah before you!*

What is the path to Allah? *Follow Muḥammad (peace be upon him). Embrace the life of my Beloved, and you shall reach me.* This path has two divisions: *the rights of Allah* (Ḥuqūq Allāh) and *the rights of the people* (Ḥuqūq al-ʿIbād). The rights of Allah comprise but ten percent; *ninety percent are the rights of the people.* This is how Allah has established the balance in the Qur'ān.

*Your Lord has decreed that you shall not worship save Him.* One sentence! Complete! Now Allah revealed the rights of parents:

⟪ar-quote:وَبِٱلْوَلِدَيْنِ إِحْسَنًا ۚ إِمَّا يَبْلُغَنَّ عِندَكَ ٱلْكِبَرَ أَحَدُهُمَآ أَوْ كِلَاهُمَا فَلَا تَقُل لَّهُمَآ أُفٍّۢ وَلَا تَنْهَرْهُمَا وَقُل لَّهُمَا قَوْلًۭا كَرِيمًۭا۔ وَٱخْفِضْ لَهُمَا جَنَاحَ ٱلذُّلِّ مِنَ ٱلرَّحْمَةِ وَقُل رَّبِّ ٱرْحَمْهُمَا كَمَا رَبَّيَانِى صَغِيرًۭا⟫

And your Lord has decreed that you worship none but Him, and that you be kind to parents. If one or both of them attain old age in your life, say not to them "uff" (a word of disrespect) nor repel them, but address them in terms of honor. And lower unto them the wing of submission and humility through mercy, and say: "My Lord! Bestow on them Your Mercy as they did nurture me when I was small."

⟪ar-quote:رَّبُّكُمْ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا فِى نُفُوسِكُمْ ۚ إِن تَكُونُوا۟ صَلِحِينَ فَإِنَّهُۥ كَانَ لِلْأَوَّبِينَ غَفُورًۭا⟫

Your Lord knows best what is in your hearts. If you are righteous, then for those who turn to Him in repentance, He is Most Forgiving.

In eleven verses Allah has established the rights of parents, while in a single verse He has established His own right. A long catalog of duties follows. Our Prophets have brought solutions to the problems of both this world and the Hereafter.

The path to connecting with Allah is through worship and obedience:

⟪ar-quote:إِن يَنصُرْكُمُ ٱللَّهُ فَلَا غَالِبَ لَكُمْ ۖ⟫

If Allah supports you, none can overcome you.

⟪ar-quote:وَإِن يَخْذُلْكُمْ⟫

And if He abandons you,

⟪ar-quote:فَمَن ذَا ٱلَّذِى يَنصُرُكُم مِّنۢ بَعْدِهِۦ⟫

who can help you after Him?

## The Corruption Within

Nations in decline always blame others for their failings. They cry: *This is America's conspiracy! This is England's plot! This is the fault of Hazrat and the authorities! This is the fault of our parents, our teachers.* Yet how many in our congregations are drinking? How much adultery occurs? Are these English conspiracies? How much indecency and shamelessness flourish in our communities? Are these the machinations of the West? Do teachers conspire when they seduce their students? Do foreigners conspire when the powerful oppress the weak, when those in authority rob the needy?

We must look inward. *We are the problem. We bear responsibility.* We are destroying our own community. It is not the waves that sink ships; it is the sailors who, taking an axe in hand, begin to pierce the hull. When the riders have arrows and are shooting holes in their own sails, the ship sinks.

*So do not ask me why the caravan was looted. I do not blame the robbers—I blame your guidance.* Our moral decay has destroyed us. Not America. We ourselves abandoned the principles of Islam. We cast out the Holy Prophet (peace be upon him), Mawlānā ʿAlī, and the Imāms from our lives. This was not done by Jews and Christians—*we did it to ourselves.* Today we have become slaves to our ego. No one is ready to forgive anyone. All are enslaved to desire, unwilling to sacrifice the pleasures of the world.

This is the word of the Prophet: *Three things bring the ruin of a nation upon themselves. When these spread, there is no need for external conspiracy, no need for bombing from air or ground.*

First: *Injustice* (ẓulm). We are all tyrants today. Never mind others—we are the greatest tyrants to ourselves. We commit sins, we know we are heading toward the Fire of Hell, yet we persist in wickedness.

Second: *Lies.* One hundred out of one hundred people lie. Not a single person among us can say, "I do not lie."

Third: *Betrayal.* We betray our religion. The Messenger of Allah gave us the sacred trust of Islam, faith, and the Qur'ān. Yet we have abandoned both the Qur'ān and the Prophet's example. How many of us read the Qur'ān? We learn the most difficult languages in the world, yet we do not learn the language of our Prophet, nor do we try, nor do we wish to.

*Why should anyone drown me when I drown myself?*

Repent from injustice! Repent from falsehood! Repent from deception and betrayal! *Protect yourself from oppression before you condemn others.* Do not oppress your own self. This body of clay cannot withstand the heat of the Fire:

⟪ar-quote:الا لعنت اللہ علی الظالمین⟫

The curse of Allah is upon the wrongdoers.

Protect yourself from lies. Do not lie to yourself. Do not rationalize flagrant disobedience to Allah. When you look at forbidden things, *you know what you are doing.* When you gaze upon a stranger, a strange woman or man, *you know the nature of your act.* When you give a poor person only enough for a single meal and call it charity, *do not deceive yourself that you are generous.* When your body prays while your mind shops in the market, *do not lie to yourself that you are worshipping.*

The Prophet declared:

⟪ar-quote:الصدق ینجی و الکذب یھلک⟫

Truth saves; lies destroy.

⟪ar-quote:الا لعنت اللہ علی الکاذبین⟫

The curse of Allah falls upon liars.

Do not betray the religion of your Prophet. Do not violate his trust.

When asked, "O Messenger of Allah, can a Muslim be a coward?" the Prophet replied that a Muslim could be many things, but never a liar, never a deceiver. Jarīr ibn ʿAbd Allāh sent his servant to purchase a horse in the market. The servant returned with an Arabian horse sold for three hundred dirhams. When Jarīr examined it, he realized it was of far greater value than the seller knew. The transaction was entirely lawful—one man's ignorance is another man's fortune. Yet Jarīr was a chief, a man of knowledge and discernment. He said to the horse's owner: "If I gave you four hundred, would you object?" "Not at all," the man replied. "What of five hundred?" "No." "Six hundred? Seven hundred? Eight hundred?" Each time the owner agreed. At last Jarīr asked: "Brother, what joy would you gain if I paid you what this horse is truly worth?" The man said: "What do you mean?" Jarīr replied: "This horse is worth eight hundred dirhams, not three hundred. It would have been fraud for me to keep it at that price." He instructed his servant: "Give him eight hundred and bring the horse." When the servant later asked Jarīr why he had paid double, he said: "When I came to the Messenger of Allah, I placed my hand in his hand and made a covenant: 'O Messenger of Allah, as long as I live, I will never deceive anyone.'"

If a brother falls into hardship and we see an opportunity to exploit it—buying something worth ten thousand for five hundred—we should not seize the advantage. Rather, if we can, we should sell it for fifteen to help the distressed. Do we ask, "Has a rooster become unlawful? Has a goat become forbidden?" *What covenant did we make on the Day of Covenant (Yawm al-Mīthāq), placing our hands in the Prophet's hand?* The *Ummah* that was taught generosity, compassion, and sacrifice for others has become a band of thieves. Teachers cheat; merchants cheat; lawyers cheat; doctors cheat.

## The Stories of Sacrifice and the Values of Karbalāʾ

When asked, "O Messenger of Allah, when will the Hour come?" the Prophet replied: "Allah knows best." When pressed for a sign, he said: "When mothers are dishonored by their children, the Hour approaches." He did not link it to disbelief or polytheism (*shirk*), not to adultery or alcohol—*but to the humiliation of mothers.*

Replace negative thinking with positive thought. There is little life left—why harbor enmity? Why wage battles? Endure! Be patient! If coal endures, it becomes a diamond. So too are we—clay—and if we learn patience, if we endure for Allah's pleasure, He shall refine us into jewels and place us among the nearest to Him.

Shall I summarize the entire religion for you? *Guard your tongue!* Guard your tongue. All defects will vanish. A runaway horse is easy to stop; a truck without brakes is manageable—but the tongue! It is harder to brake than a speeding train. The whole of religion lies in the tongue. A sweet word is sweeter than all the honey of creation. A bitter word is more bitter than all the poison in existence. A kind word heals great wounds. A harsh word inflicts wounds that even death cannot ease. *Learn to control your speech.* Be kindness in the face of cruelty. Become prayer where there is abuse. Offer praise where there is backbiting. Thus, by Allah, you shall dwell in Allah's shadow.

Remove from your tongue: abuse, backbiting, lies, gossip, complaints, accusations. Strip away all these poisons. The Prophet said: "Those who cause discord between two people are as filthy in Allah's sight as excrement." *Sweeten your words.* Luqmān said to his son: "My son, you shall never regret your silence, but you shall often regret your words."

Abū Dajānah said to the Prophet: "The greatest act of devotion in my sight is this:

⟪ar-quote:ما أٓذیتُ أحداً بی لِسَانِی! و لا أجِدُ فی قلبی غلاًّ لی احد⟫

I have never wounded anyone with my tongue, and I harbor no grudge against anyone in my heart."

Do not multiply prayers while neglecting duty. Rather, abandon lies, tyranny, betrayal, harsh words. A few sincere prostrations shall suffice. In the religion of the Prophet, forgiveness, kind speech, and comforting a sorrowing heart merit a reward not attainable through nights of worship. And what comes through anger is ruin.

*Islam must work for itself. This mill must grind itself.*

## The Purpose of Knowledge

The purpose of knowledge is always to awaken consciousness. Knowledge is not meant to earn a livelihood or gain status. Knowledge is meant to open the windows of the mind, to unravel the knots of the universe. Yet we have turned knowledge into a commodity, acquiring it for degrees and certificates. *Create a living relationship with knowledge. Read for the sake of knowledge itself.*

*We carve our own milestones. We are not shaped by time.*

Elevate your knowledge to a point where people come to learn from you. Knowledge exalted even Adam above the angels—the angels bowed before man because of his knowledge. *Do not demean yourself. Be a lover of knowledge!* Those from whom you learn, respect them! Disrespect closes the doors of knowledge. Mawlānā ʿAlī said: "Whoever teaches me a letter, I am his slave—sell me in the market if you wish." Even the most learned one after the Prophet himself said: *Respect your teacher.*

*Respect those from whom you receive knowledge.*

There is a city of darkness; there is a king; there is a baker's bread, a cook's food. Pray for your parents! Do not curse them. Honor your wife! Love her! You brought someone's daughter to your home—do not greet those who disrespect her. Train your children—not with love alone, not with beatings, not with criticism, not with humiliation. Such things wound. The Prophet said:

⟪ar-quote:اکرمو اولادکُم⟫

Honor your children.

Warning is necessary; humiliation is catastrophe. Parents must warn, not insult. Insult damages self-respect. Teachers must nurture children with compassion. Students must sit at the feet of those whose words they recite.

Treat your brothers and sisters with love. Let the melodies of the Qur'ān echo in your homes, not music and frivolity. Let there be no one without prayer. Bring the prophetic society into your households—loving, moral, forgiving. You must honor your wife so that your children will honor your mother. If you dishonor your wife, your children shall dishonor you. The Prophet said: "The best Muslim is the one who treats his wife well." And he said to his wives: "If I were to command anyone to prostrate before another, I would command wives to prostrate before their husbands." Yet *balance the two.*

The path to healing society is not to heal society in the abstract. Rather, heal yourself first—*Asif Hussain*—and all shall be well. Society shall improve on its own.

## The Consequence of Abandoning Prayer

At present, the peoples of the world—Muslim and non-Muslim alike—pursue their desired courses of life according to the whims of the heart, doing or refraining from what inclination dictates. The constitution of one's own life has become the fruit of vanity's arduous labor over the past three centuries. The influence of Allah, His Messenger, and the life to come has either faded from our hearts or become so obscured as to be nearly imperceptible.

⟪ar-quote:يَعْلَمُونَ ظَهِرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَهُمْ عَنِ ٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ غَفِلُونَ⟫

They know only the outward aspect of worldly life, yet remain heedless of the Hereafter. These four days of earthly existence alone seem good to us; we are oblivious to the life that awaits after death. Yet this universe, the earth and the heavens, the Throne itself—all belong to Allah. What He wills shall come to pass, not what the kingdoms of this world desire.

⟪ar-quote:وَلِلَّهِ غَيْبُ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَإِلَيْهِ يُرْجَعُ ٱلْأَمْرُ كُلُّهُۥ فَٱعْبُدْهُ وَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَيْهِ ۚ وَمَا رَبُّكَ بِغَافِلٍ عَمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ⟫

His sovereignty knows no decline:

⟪ar-quote:كُلَّ يَوْمٍ هُوَ فِى شَأْنٍۢ⟫

He was before all things, and shall be after all things. He has no partner, no equal:

⟪ar-quote:مَا ٱتَّخَذَ ٱللَّهُ مِن وَلَدٍۢ وَمَا كَانَ مَعَهُۥ مِنْ إِلَهٍ ۚ إِذًۭا لَّذَهَبَ كُلُّ إِلَهٍۭ بِمَا خَلَقَ وَلَعَلَا بَعْضُهُمْ عَلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ ۚ سُبْحَنَ ٱللَّهِ عَمَّا يَصِفُونَ⟫

He has no partner in dominion, no children, no helper to shield Him from abasement:

⟪ar-quote:وَقُلِ ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ ٱلَّذِى لَمْ يَتَّخِذْ وَلَدًۭا وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ شَرِيكٌۭ فِى ٱلْمُلْكِ وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ وَلِىٌّۭ مِّنَ ٱلذُّلِّ ۖ وَكَبِّرْهُ تَكْبِيرًۢا⟫

He was not born of anyone, nor did He beget:

⟪ar-quote:لم یلد ولم یولد⟫

He created all that exists, pure from the need of creation itself. He causes all things to perish, yet remains beyond death. His intentions are perfect. What He wills comes to be:

⟪ar-quote:ما شاء اللہ کان⟫

And what He wills not shall not be:

⟪ar-quote:وما لم یشاء لا یکن⟫

If He desires not, none can accomplish it:

⟪ar-quote:وَمَا تَشَآءُونَ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ رَبُّ ٱلْعَلَمِينَ⟫

You too are in need of Allah, and your desires are fulfilled only when Allah wills. [^cite-81]

Consider Pharaoh's might: he resolved to slaughter Moses. Yet it was Allah's will that Moses should live. So many children perished in his nation that the courtiers feared the kingdom's governance. Thus he altered his decree—killing in one year, sparing in another. Aaron was born in the year of sparing; Moses in the year of slaying. Had Allah willed, He could have created Moses in the year of clemency. But He desired to manifest His sovereignty: *I am the Master. I am the King. Dominion is Mine alone.* [^cite-82]

Then He cast Moses into the water, bringing him to Pharaoh's very court. Moses' mother, overwhelmed with sorrow, thought that if she surrendered him to the river, he would either drown or perish. But Allah commanded:

⟪ar-quote:فَإِذَا خِفْتِ عَلَيْهِ فَأَلْقِيهِ فِى ٱلْيَمِّ⟫

"Cast him into the river." Whither would the river bear him?

⟪ar-quote:فَلْيُلْقِهِ ٱلْيَمُّ بِٱلسَّاحِلِ⟫

"The river shall cast him upon the shore." What then?

⟪ar-quote:يَأْخُذْهُ عَدُوٌّۭ لِّى⟫

"My enemy, Pharaoh, shall take him."

⟪ar-quote:وَعَدُوٌّۭ لَّهُۥ ۚ⟫

"And he shall be his enemy as well." In her anguish, Moses' mother thought: if all I wish to preserve him ends in his capture, how can he be saved? He will slay him the moment he sees him. But Allah spoke:

⟪ar-quote:وَلَا تَخَافِى وَلَا تَحْزَنِىٓ⟫

"Grieve not for him, nor fear his death."

⟪ar-quote:إِنَّا رَآدُّوهُ إِلَيْكِ⟫

"I shall return him to your arms."

⟪ar-quote:وَجَاعِلُوهُ مِنَ ٱلْمُرْسَلِينَ⟫

"And I shall make him a messenger in your lifetime." She would not depart this world until she beheld these two things fulfilled. Whether he fall into Pharaoh's lap or into raging fire or be surrounded by tempestuous waves—when your Lord wills protection, every cause in the universe is marshaled to his salvation, and none can be turned to his harm. When He wills death, the very means of safety become instruments of destruction. When He desires honor, the causes of shame bring glory. When He wills abasement, even honors are turned to humiliation. When He would kindle love, enemies become devoted. When He would sow hatred, the closest friends become foes. The sovereignty of this universe belongs to Allah alone—and thus it shall be. [^cite-83]

Consider too Nimrod's strength: he resolved to cast Abraham into fire. Wood was gathered, flames kindled—a conflagration so fierce that birds falling from the sky turned to ash. When the hour came to hurl Abraham into that inferno, what means remained? The Devil fashioned a contraption like a catapult; binding him with cords, they cast him skyward. As Abraham flew through the air, Gabriel approached from the right and the angel of water from the left—both bearing him aloft. Abraham stood silent. Both his companions cried: *Hasbi Allah wa na'am al-wakil—Allah suffices me, and He is the best guardian.* Yet Abraham did not petition them. The angel of water awaited his command to extinguish the flames; Gabriel waited for his word. When they saw he spoke not, they grew anxious: if they fell into that fire, it would consume them. Even Gabriel knows fire's nature. They implored: "O Abraham, what do you need?" He answered:

⟪ar-quote:أمَّا الَیک فَلا⟫

"As for you—no need."

⟪ar-quote:أما الی اللہ فنعم!⟫

"But for Allah—I have need." Abraham flew toward the fire, neither glancing at Gabriel nor at the angel of water, his gaze fixed alone upon the divine decree. Allah commanded neither angel directly, but rather:

⟪ar-quote:يَنَارُ كُونِى بَرْدًۭا وَسَلَمًا عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ⟫

"O fire, be cool and a means of peace upon Abraham!" The fire leapt forward and gathered him as a mother gathers her child into her arms, laying him gently upon the earth as a mother places her infant upon the bed. Witnessing this, Abraham's father Azar—erstwhile mortal enemy, would-be murderer—spontaneously cried out:

⟪ar-quote:نعمَ الرَّبُّ رَبُّک یا اِبرٰھیم!⟫

"What a blessed Lord is your Lord, O Abraham!"

My children, all that exists in this universe is Allah's, fashioned from His substance. His command alone is sovereign. He who speaks to the earth—it obeys. He who speaks to water—it obeys. He who speaks to fire—it obeys. He who speaks to the heavens—they obey. The entire universe belongs to Allah, and the affair of the Hereafter belongs to Allah alone. The combined might of all the world cannot alter even one of Allah's decrees.

When people's deeds grow corrupt, Allah corrupts their condition as well—destroying both world and afterlife. Wealth has never bestowed honor before Allah, does not now, nor ever shall. The power of this world has never brought success before His throne, does not now, nor ever shall. Herein lies our present deception: we pursue our desires heedless of what pleases Allah and what angers Him, toward whom His mercy extends and whose transgression He seals. Thus all humanity stands in gravest danger.

All those deeds that kindle Allah's wrath now occur in our households, yet we slumber so deeply that we remain ignorant—unaware that our very actions provoke His displeasure. Today the whole world challenges Allah with its grave transgressions, filling the earth with sin:

⟪ar-quote:ظَهَرَ ٱلْفَسَادُ فِى ٱلْبَرِّ وَٱلْبَحْرِ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ أَيْدِى ٱلنَّاسِ⟫

Through His Sunnah in the Qur'an, Allah speaks to us: "Hear what I did to those who came before you when they acted as you act now. The people of Noah filled the earth with disbelief, yet they challenged my Prophet, saying:

⟪ar-quote:فاَءتِنَا بِمَا تَعِدُنَآ إِن كُنتَ مِنَ الصادقین⟫

'Bring upon us the punishment you have promised, if you speak truly.' Then came that day:

⟪ar-quote:وَفَجَّرْنَا ٱلْأَرْضَ عُيُونًۭا فَٱلْتَقَى ٱلْمَآءُ عَلَىٰٓ أَمْرٍۢ قَدْ قُدِرَ⟫

We burst forth the earth as fountains, and the waters met by divine decree." He did not merely draw water from beneath the earth; rather:

⟪ar-quote:وَفَجَّرْنَا ٱلْأَرْضَ عُيُونًۭا⟫

We transformed the earth itself into water. Springs gushed from every corner; torrents fell from the sky. The waters covered all creation. The entire nation of Noah perished—even his own son, before his very eyes:

⟪ar-quote:قَالَ سَـَٔاوِىٓ إِلَىٰ جَبَلٍۢ يَعْصِمُنِى مِنَ ٱلْمَآءِ ۚ قَالَ لَا عَاصِمَ ٱلْيَوْمَ مِنْ أَمْرِ ٱللَّهِ إِلَّا مَن رَّحِمَ ۚ وَحَالَ بَيْنَهُمَا ٱلْمَوْجُ فَكَانَ مِنَ ٱلْمُغْرَقِينَ⟫

"I shall flee to a mountain that will shelter me from the waters!" Noah replied: "Nothing shields from Allah's decree today save those upon whom He bestows mercy." A wave rose between them, and he was among the drowned.

All the deeds the people of Noah once committed are being repeated today—among Muslims, within our own communities.

Then came ʿAd, the people of Hud (the Summoner), who possessed great strength. Yet they boasted:

⟪ar-quote:وَقَالُوا۟ مَنْ أَشَدُّ مِنَّا قُوَّةً⟫

"Who is more powerful than we? Show us one!" 

⟪ar-quote:إِن نَّقُولُ إِلَّا ٱعْتَرَىٰكَ بَعْضُ آلِهَتِنَا بِسُوٓء⟫

"One of our gods has corrupted your mind." Is there anyone more mighty than we? Allah answered:

⟪ar-quote:أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا۟ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَهُمْ هُوَ أَشَدُّ مِنْهُمْ قُوَّةًۭ⟫

"Do they not see that He who created them surpasses them in might?" When the proof from Allah was complete, they grew ever more arrogant, persisting in rebellion. Allah sent famine upon them first—hunger and thirst consumed them. Some implored Hud: "Ask your Lord to send us rain." Yet others would not cease their defiance. Then Allah sent a black cloud:

⟪ar-quote:فَلَمَّا رَأَوْهُ عَارِضًۭا مُّسْتَقْبِلَ أَوْدِيَتِهِمْ قَالُوا۟ هَذَا عَارِضٌۭ مُّمْطِرُنَا ۚ⟫

"Behold the rain-bearing cloud!" But Allah declared:

⟪ar-quote:بَلْ هُوَ مَا ٱسْتَعْجَلْتُم بِهِۦ ۖ⟫

"Nay, it is the very torment you demanded!" The punishment about which you taunted my Prophet Hud—claiming none could harm you—now comes. Prepare yourselves:

⟪ar-quote:رِيحٌۭ فِيهَا عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۭ⟫

"A wind bearing terrible torment!"

⟪ar-quote:تُدَمِّرُ كُلَّ شَىْءٍۭ بِأَمْرِ رَبِّهَا⟫

"It destroys all things by command of its Lord." The wind tore their dwellings asunder; they were swept aloft like chaff, their heads striking one another. Their skulls shattered, their brains spilled forth. Allah lifted them up and dashed them to the ground again—severing head from body, body from limb. Then He cried out:

⟪ar-quote:فَهَلْ تَرَىٰ لَهُم مِّنۢ بَاقِيَةٍۢ⟫

"Do you see any remnant of them?"

⟪ar-quote:كَذَلِكَ نَجْزِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلْمُجْرِمِينَ۔⟫

"Thus We recompense the criminal people." We commit these same deeds that the people of ʿAd perpetrated—every day, repeatedly.

### An Example of Prayer [^cite-84]

Then came the people of Thamud. They had heard that wind destroyed ʿAd. So they carved dwellings into the mountains, hollowing them into caves, believing the air could not penetrate so far beneath stone. They plotted against Allah:

⟪ar-quote:وَمَكَرُوا۟ مَكْرًۭا وَمَكَرْنَا مَكْرًۭا۔ فَٱنظُرْ كَيْفَ كَانَ عَقِبَةُ مَكْرِهِمْ⟫

"They schemed a scheme, and We schemed a scheme. Behold the outcome of their scheming!"

⟪ar-quote:فَتِلْكَ بُيُوتُهُمْ خَاوِيَةًۢ بِمَا ظَلَمُوٓا۟ ۗ إِنَّ فِى ذَلِكَ لَآيَةًۭ لِّقَوْمٍۢ يَعْلَمُونَ۔ وَأَنجَيْنَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَكَانُوا۟ يَتَّقُونَ⟫

"See their dwellings, emptied by their transgression. Therein is a sign for those who understand. And We delivered those who believed and were mindful." An angel came and uttered a single cry. His voice caused their livers to burst, their brains to dissolve, their faces to blacken. The entire nation was annihilated by one scream.

### Deception in Commerce [^cite-85]

Then came the people of Shuʿayb (the Summoner). This was an age when commerce flourished, and merchants seized complete dominion over trade. They began extracting bribes, charging usury, deceiving the people. Shuʿayb implored: "O my people, depart from this!"

⟪ar-quote:وَأَوْفُوا۟ ٱلْكَيْلَ إِذَا كِلْتُمْ وَزِنُوا۟ بِٱلْقِسْطَاسِ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمِ⟫

"Give full measure when you measure, and weigh with the correct scale." Yet they answered:

⟪ar-quote:قَالُوا۟ يَشُعَيْبُ أَصَلَوٰتُكَ تَأْمُرُكَ أَن نَّتْرُكَ مَا يَعْبُدُ آبَآؤُنَآ أَوْ أَن نَّفْعَلَ فِىٓ أَمْوَلِنَا مَا نَشَٓؤُا۟⟫

"O Shuʿayb, does your prayer command us to abandon what our fathers worshipped, or to cease doing as we please with our wealth? Why not pray silently in your mosque without troubling us? Do you imagine us so foolish as to follow your counsel? If we reduced our measures, how would business survive? Without deception, without fraud, how could we conduct trade? Without striking our scales, without accepting bribes, without charging interest—how could business endure?" These objections, which merchants and shopkeepers raise today, were the very objections raised to Shuʿayb (peace upon him).

Yet understand this: we are not here to conduct business in this world. We have come to please Allah. If trade must cease for Allah's pleasure, then glory be to Allah! When business flourishes, praise be to Allah! We do not consent to the belief that we must feed our bellies and clothe our children through forbidden means. We hold that earnings must accord with Allah's command, and spending likewise. If something remains, we eat and feed our families. If nothing remains, we ourselves go hungry, and so do our children. I say to them: "O children, I cannot anger Allah for your sake. I cannot endure the fire of Hell for your sake." [^cite-86]

ʿUmar ibn ʿAbd al-ʿAziz ruled as caliph yet spent his entire life in poverty. When death approached, he summoned his children and said: "O my children, two paths lay before me. One was to gather wealth for you—halal or haram alike—but accept for myself the fire of Hell. The second was to teach you piety, to instill in you trust in Allah, to please Allah. My children, I, your father, cannot endure Hell's torment. Therefore I have not fed you forbidden sustenance, nor gathered unlawful wealth for you. I have shown you the path of piety. When you need anything, beseech my Lord."

⟪ar-quote:وَهُوَ يَتَوَلَّى ٱلصَّلِحِينَ⟫

"He is the Guardian of the righteous." He continued: "If you follow the path of piety, Allah will grant you success. And if you transgress, I shall not grieve for your death."

Then earth and heaven witnessed a day when the governors of that era, who had amassed haram wealth, eaten from vessels of gold and silver, and filled their children's coffers with forbidden gain, came to sit upon the mosque steps and beg. Yet Allah granted such bounty to the children of ʿUmar ibn ʿAbd al-ʿAziz that in each gathering they distributed a hundred horses as charity in Allah's name.

Know this: we are Muslims first, traders second. We are Muslims first, officers second. We are Muslims first, scholars second. A child's parents are secondary; Islam is primary. A wife's husband is secondary; Islam is primary. We are commanded to sacrifice all for Allah's pleasure—not to sacrifice Allah's command upon the altar of our desires. We are commanded to relinquish our whims at the behest of Allah.

The people of Shuʿayb began to mock: "Where then is your business? How shall we eat bread? Be silent; do not meddle in our affairs." Allah punished them with three torments:

⟪ar-quote:فَأَخَذَتْهُمُ ٱلرَّجْفَةُ فَأَصْبَحُوا۟ فِى دَارِهِمْ جَثِمِينَ⟫

An earthquake;

⟪ar-quote:وَأَخَذَ ٱلَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا۟ ٱلصَّيْحَةُ فَأَصْبَحُوا۟ فِى دِيَرِهِمْ جَاثِمِينَ⟫

A cry that seized the evildoers;

⟪ar-quote:أَخَذَهُمْ عَذَابُ يَوْمِ ٱلظُّلَّةِ⟫

The torment of the Overshadowing Day—a rain of burning embers that consumed all the people of Shuʿayb and the markets of Midian.

The Messenger (upon him be the blessing and peace of Allah) said:

⟪ar-quote:ولا یَبِیثَنَّ أُناسٌ مِن أٌمَّتِی عَلی أَشَرٍ و بَتَرٍ و لحوٍ و لَعبٍ فَیُسبِحُو قَد مُسِقُو قِرَدَۃً و خَنازِیر بِسْتِحلالِھِمُ المَحاَرِم وَ أَکلِھِمُ الرِّباَء وَ لُبسِھِمُ الحَرِیرْ و شُربِھِمُ الخمُور  وَ تِّخٰذِھِمُ الخَیناَت وَ قَطِیعاَتِھِمُ الرَّحَم⟫

"There shall come a time when some of my community will abandon themselves to luxury, wantonness, music, and play, and in that hour, when they sleep at night, they shall wake as apes and swine."

This transformation shall occur ⟪ar-quote:بِسْتِحلالِھِمُ المَحاَرِم⟫ through their permitting the forbidden, ⟪ar-quote:وَ أَکلِھِمُ الرِّباَء⟫ through their consumption of usury, ⟪ar-quote:وَ لُبسِھِمُ الحَرِیرْ⟫ through men wearing silk garments and adorning themselves with gold rings and chains, ⟪ar-quote:و شُربِھِمُ الخمُور⟫ through their drinking of wine, ⟪ar-quote:وَ تِّخٰذِھِمُ الخَیناَت⟫ through their music and entertainment, ⟪ar-quote:وَ قَطِیعاَتِھِمُ الرَّحَم⟫ through their severing of kinship bonds. When all this occurs, it shall surely come to pass that Allah transforms them into apes and swine. [^cite-87]

This is the cry of the Prophet—he who has wept a thousand times for his Ummah (community). So many times did he grieve that Allah Himself had to console His beloved Prophet:

⟪ar-quote:لَعَلَّكَ بَخِعٌۭ نَّفْسَكَ أَلَّا يَكُونُوا۟ مُؤْمِنِينَ⟫

⟪ar-quote:فَلَعَلَّكَ بَخِعٌۭ نَّفْسَكَ عَلَىٰٓ آثَرِهِمْ إِن لَّمْ يُؤْمِنُوا۟ بِهَذَا ٱلْحَدِيثِ أَسَفًا⟫

⟪ar-quote:فَلَا تَذْهَبْ نَفْسُكَ عَلَيْهِمْ حَسَرَتٍۚ⟫

⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ أَنتَ عَلَيْهِم بِوَكِيلٍۢ⟫

⟪ar-quote:لَّسْتَ عَلَيْهِم بِمُصَيْطِر⟫

⟪ar-quote:نَّحْنُ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ ۖ وَمَآ أَنتَ عَلَيْهِم بِجَبَّارٍۢ⟫

⟪ar-quote:فَلَا يَحْزُنكَ كُفْرُهُ⟫

⟪ar-quote:وَلَقَدْ نَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ يَضِيقُ صَدْرُكَ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ⟫

How many verses of the Qur'an contain Allah's consolation to His Prophet: "O My beloved, do not grieve! O My Messenger, do not sorrow!" Yet listen to the fate of the disobedient from that same Qur'an—those who died in grave sin. Those who abandoned prayer and drank wine: when the Messenger of Allah points to them on the Day of Rising and says:

⟪ar-quote:یا ربِّ أِنَّ قَومِ التَّخذُو ھٰذا القُرآنَ مَحجُورَا⟫

"My Lord, my people have rejected this Qur'an as mere custom."

Tell me: those who pray only on Friday, appearing once every eight days, claiming to be Muslims—do they remain upon Allah's earth for eight days only? Do they consume Allah's sustenance merely once in eight days? Do they sleep in peace only once in eight days? Do they drink water given by Allah only once in eight days? Do they breathe the air of Allah only once in eight days? Do they witness the light of Allah only once in eight days? Do they hear with ears fashioned by Allah only once in eight days? Do they benefit from the blessings of their bodies only once in eight days? Do they enjoy the warmth of the sun only once in eight days? Do they experience the gentle moonlight only once in eight days? Do they sit with wives and children only once in eight days?

What has become of their hearts of stone? Why does the message of Allah not penetrate them? Why have they become desolate? Even stones are not so hard. The great saint Attaullah Shah Bukhari once declared: "O people of India, I have recited the Qur'an to you so much that had I recited it to the mountains, they would have melted! Had I spoken it to the stones, they would have become soft! Had I proclaimed it to the rivers, the currents would have ceased! Had I announced it to the seas, their waters would have turned sweet!" Yet our hearts—what are they made of? What is their substance? Our breasts do not contain hearts; they contain stones—but stones far harder than any stone:

⟪ar-quote:ثُمَّ قَسَتْ قُلُوبُكُم مِّنۢ بَعْدِ ذَلِكَ فَهِىَ كَٱلْحِجَارَةِ أَوْ أَشَدُّ قَسْوَةًۭ ۚ وَإِنَّ مِنَ ٱلْحِجَارَةِ لَمَا يَتَفَجَّرُ مِنْهُ ٱلْأَنْهَرُ⟫

Yet the stones themselves—from some of them issue forth rivers. What stone are our hearts fashioned from, that such a great King calls to us five times daily: *Hayya ʿala al-salawa* (Come to prayer), *Hayya ʿala al-falah* (Come to success)—and we do not even turn to look? Call a minor police officer, call a court judge, summon the school principal to your room or the office head to his chamber—all work ceases and everyone hastens to answer. Yet let the King of Heaven and Earth call upon us five times each day, and His summons does not reach our ears. We come to the mosque once in eight days! Do you eat only once in eight days? Did you drink water only today?

This is the treachery we commit—treachery to ourselves, to the Devil, to our shops, to our businesses. Why this disloyalty to Allah? Is there a greater transgression than to stand upon the earth itself without prostrating before so mighty a King? Adultery is a sin; wine-drinking is a sin; but abandoning prayer is a crime graver than adultery! Abandoning prayer is a crime graver than intoxication. Murder is a grave transgression, yet abandoning prayer is graver still.

What transgression did the Devil commit? What wine did he drink? Whom did he slay? Think carefully: what exactly was his crime? He refused to prostrate. A single prostration—he refused it! Not thousands, but one refusal to bow brought upon him rejection until the Day of Judgment and a curse eternal. Why do we not comprehend that we refuse five, six, or more prostrations each day, yet we eat in comfort? We sleep peacefully upon soft beds? We sit contentedly with friends, laughing and conversing with them? [^cite-88]

Has Allah seized Satan alone for this transgression and released us? Will He punish him for refusing one prostration and overlook us for refusing many?

Why do we not tremble in fear? "I cannot rise at dawn! It is too hot! It is too cold! It is too dark." Do you not remember the resurrection from the grave? Do you not recall the heat of the grave? Do you not remember the darkness of the grave? Has the fire of Hell been extinguished? Have the gardens of Paradise been destroyed? Today we have forgotten all of it! We have forgotten the blessings of Paradise, the torments of Hell, the vision of Allah, the sight of the Prophet and the Imams. [^cite-89]

And this disloyalty to Allah is such that the sea itself cries out: "O Allah, permit me to drown them all!" The earth says: "O Allah, permit me to swallow them!" The angels say: "O Allah, allow us to strike their necks!" Against Him whose prostration is denied, whose Prophet's law is mocked, against whom Allah's own jealousy rages—these are created of My hands, fashioned from water and defiled seed, kept alive by My sustenance, breathing by the winds I send, living by the water I provide, sustained by the system I have ordained—yet they deny Me!

Who created such magnificent existence? He lit the lamps of the eyes, placed instruments in the ears, strengthened the brain, gave power to the hands, moved the feet, set the heart to beating, granted speech to the tongue, coursed the rivers of blood through the entire body, constructed various factories within the frame, bound the bones, softened the muscles, granted strength to move the tongue, gave the power to cut with the teeth. He fashioned two passages around the throat, separated breathing from swallowing, veiled one from the other. When food enters the mouth, the airway closes. Even should one drink wine, Allah—by His mercy—does not alter His system. One second of His will, and the wine would enter the breathing tube; we would choke there and die. But in His mercy, He protects us. What a system it is!

Every day, dead cells depart in the form of nails. Cut a nail too short, and we spend the entire day moaning in discomfort. We cannot even thank Allah for a nail's sake. How shall we thank Him for joints and ligaments? How shall we render gratitude for limbs?

And yet we rebel against Allah, who has done so much! Is there any sense in this? If a servant earning merely fifteen hundred rupees fails to receive his tea on time, we drive him from the house with curses upon his mother and sister. Yet we rebel against the Master who has given us so much:

⟪ar-quote:وَٱلْأَرْضَ وَضَعَهَا لِلْأَنَامِ۔ فِيهَا فَكِهَةٌۭ وَٱلنَّخْلُ ذَاتُ ٱلْأَكْمَامِ ۔ وَٱلْحَبُّ ذُو ٱلْعَصْفِ وَٱلرَّيْحَانُ ۔ فَبِأَىِّ آلَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ⟫

"We have spread forth the earth for you, and brought forth for you therein fruits and palm-trees bearing clusters, grain and fragrant plants. What blessing of your Lord do you deny?"

He brought forth sustenance that you might eat and drink and find pleasure. He produced flowers and their fragrances, that you might delight. Why then do you deny His blessings? Do you not perceive that:

⟪ar-quote:رَبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقَيْنِ وَرَبُّ ٱلْمَغْرِبَيْنِ⟫

"I am Lord of the two Easts and Lord of the two Wests."

⟪ar-quote:فَبِأَىِّ آلَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ⟫

"What blessing of your Lord do you deny?"

And yet you repudiate His blessings:

⟪ar-quote:خَلَقَ ٱلْإِنسَنَ مِن صَلْصَلٍۢ كَٱلْفَخَّارِ۔ وَخَلَقَ ٱلْجَآنَّ مِن مَّارِجٍۢ مِّن نَّارٍۢ۔ فَبِأَىِّ آلَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ⟫

"He created man from sounding clay like pottery, and created the jinn from a smokeless flame. What blessing of your Lord do you deny?"

Why do you deny His blessings? Do you not see that each day your Lord is engaged in different affairs:

⟪ar-quote:كُلَّ يَوْمٍ هُوَ فِى شَأْنٍۢ⟫

⟪ar-quote:فَبِأَىِّ آلَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ⟫

"What blessing of your Lord do you deny?" And if you heed not these words, believe them not, then know this:

⟪ar-quote:سَنَفْرُغُ لَكُمْ أَيُّهَ ٱلثَّقَلَانِ⟫

"Soon shall We attend to your reckoning, O you two races!" Have you been told of that Day?

⟪ar-quote:فَإِذَا ٱنشَقَّتِ ٱلسَّمَآءُ فَكَانَتْ وَرْدَةًۭ كَٱلدِّهَانِ⟫

"When the heaven is split asunder and becomes red like molten oil,"

⟪ar-quote:يُرْسَلُ عَلَيْكُمَا شُوَاظٌۭ مِّن نَّارٍۢ وَنُحَاسٌۭ فَلَا تَنتَصِرَانِ⟫

"There shall be sent upon you a flame of fire and a smoke; you shall not be able to defend yourselves."

On that day you shall breathe in the smoke of torment. Who shall save you then?

⟪ar-quote:يُعْرَفُ ٱلْمُجْرِمُونَ بِسِيمَهُمْ فَيُؤْخَذُ بِٱلنَّوَصِى وَٱلْأَقْدَامِ⟫

"The criminals shall be known by their marks, and shall be seized by the forelocks and the feet."

On that Day, the evildoers shall be recognized by their countenances, seized by the hair of their foreheads, and told:

⟪ar-quote:هَذِهِۦ جَهَنَّمُ ٱلَّتِى يُكَذِّبُ بِهَا ٱلْمُجْرِمُونَ⟫

"This is Hell, which you used to deny!"

O you who abandoned prayer! O you who rebelled against Me! Come now—this is the Hell the Prophet foretold you, yet you laughed at it. Now:

⟪ar-quote:يَطُوفُونَ بَيْنَهَا وَبَيْنَ حَمِيمٍ آنٍۢ⟫

"They shall be sent round between Hell and boiling water." The wrongdoers shall roil in Hell and in scalding waters, writhing in agony and parched with thirst. But listen also to this:

⟪ar-quote:وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِۦ جَنَّتَانِ⟫

"But for him who feared standing before his Lord are two Gardens."

⟪ar-quote:ذَوَاتَآ أَفْنَانٍۢ⟫

"With branches fair and spreading;"

⟪ar-quote:فِيهِمَا عَيْنَانِ تَجْرِيَانِ⟫

"In both flow two springs."

⟪ar-quote:فِيهِمَا مِن كُلِّ فَكِهَةٍۢ زَوْجَانِ⟫

"In both are fruits of every kind in pairs."

⟪ar-quote:مُتَّكِـِٔينَ عَلَىٰ فُرُشٍۭ بَطَآئِنُهَا مِنْ إِسْتَبْرَقٍۢ ۚ وَجَنَى ٱلْجَنَّتَيْنِ دَانٍۢ⟫

"Reclining upon couches lined with silk; and the fruits of both gardens hanging low within reach."

⟪ar-quote:فِيهِنَّ قَصِرَتُ ٱلطَّرْفِ لَمْ يَطْمِثْهُنَّ إِنسٌۭ قَبْلَهُمْ وَلَا جَآنٌّۭ۔ كَأَنَّهُنَّ ٱلْيَاقُوتُ وَٱلْمَرْجَانُ۔ وَمِن دُونِهِمَا جَنَّتَانِ۔ مُدْهَآمَّتَانِ۔ فِيهِمَا عَيْنَانِ نَضَّاخَتَانِ۔ فِيهِمَا فَكِهَةٌۭ وَنَخْلٌۭ وَرُمَّانٌۭ۔ فِيهِنَّ خَيْرَتٌ حِسَانٌۭ۔ حُورٌۭ مَّقْصُورَتٌۭ فِى ٱلْخِيَامِ۔ لَمْ يَطْمِثْهُنَّ إِنسٌۭ قَبْلَهُمْ وَلَا جَآنٌّۭ۔ مُتَّكِـِٔينَ عَلَىٰ رَفْرَفٍ خُضْرٍۢ وَعَبْقَرِىٍّ حِسَانٍۢ⟫

"In them shall be maidens with downward glances, whom neither man nor jinn hath touched. As if they were rubies and coral. Beyond these two are two other gardens—dark in their verdure, with springs gushing forth. In them shall be fruits and dates and pomegranates. In them shall be the choicest companions, fair ones confined to the pavilions. Whom neither man nor jinn hath touched. Reclining upon green carpets and beautiful pillows."

You are so eager to visit the vacation destinations of this worldly life—those dusty, desolate, rocky mountains! You count your money and gather your wealth, thinking that with such riches you might travel to these places. Yet let me show you the vacation grounds of my Lord!

You are so absorbed in seeking petty worldly pleasures, desolate places in this temporary life. Have you thought of my vacation? If I do so much for you, then tell me: is not the recompense for kindness itself a kindness?

⟪ar-quote:هَلْ جَزَآءُ ٱلْإِحْسَنِ إِلَّا ٱلْإِحْسَنُ⟫

"O My servants, is not the reciprocal of goodness itself goodness?" Is this the recompense I offer for My favor to you? I bestow upon you thousands of blessings each day, and you cannot offer Me five prostrations in return?

What blessing of Mine will you deny? Surely there is some shame, some honor remaining. Surely I deserve some respect.

This negligence of prayer is a disaster most grave. How can I adequately express it? An angel shall make the proclamation:

⟪ar-quote:سَیَعلَمُونَ الیَومْ مَن أصحاَبَ الکِراَم!⟫

"Today they shall know who are the honored ones!"

⟪ar-quote:أینَ الَّذِی تَتَجاَفُ جُنُوبَھُم عَنِ المُضاَجِعِہْ⟫

"Where are those whose sides departed from their beds at night?" Today the prayer-keepers shall be honored. And those who spend in Allah's name shall be honored as well.

If Allah grants you life, you have not the power to fulfill His due. If Allah has given you sustenance, it is not solely yours to claim. Prayer is the debt of the soul, and charity is the debt of wealth. Give to your poor relatives.

## Reflection on Life's Beginning and End

Did this life commence through your own strength? Did we arrive in this world by our own volition? Since we came not of our own will, we must know why we have come into this world. And He who sent us—why did He send us?

Do we depart from life through our own happiness? Do we choose death? Do we die by our own intent? If not, then we must understand why He who gives death grants it.

It is a tragedy upon all humanity that ninety-nine percent possess not the means to fulfill this purpose. And if one percent possess the means, they have not the time to benefit from them. [^cite-90]

In the end, both perish. Who then collects the harvest? What system is this? The aged father sits while the child descends to the grave. An innocent babe is born, yet the mother departs, leaving her newborn orphaned, knowing not who brought her into the world.

In childhood, a melody plays in the mind—a melody one must pursue. Youth arrives, and with it comes gravity: now one must obtain a degree. The degree achieved, the goal shifts: a good position with ample wealth. Money becomes the pursuit. They gather what they can, what they cannot. By the time wealth accumulates, fifty or sixty years have passed—nearly the evening of life itself. The sun draws near to setting.

Who directs this entire system of existence? Within it lie life and death, morning and evening, spring and autumn, youth and age, lamentation and song, faith and disbelief, monotheism and idolatry. Within it are nights of dance and nights of worship, attendance at revelry and prostration before Allah, weighing justly and oppressing others, weeping before Allah in joy and drinking wine through the night in deception. Will this cycle continue endlessly?

The ancient Greek philosophers believed it would—that such has been the eternal pattern and such it shall forever remain. We do not hold such belief, yet this is how we live. We do as we please, confident we shall be seen—confident in doing as we will. If we must complete the smallest piece of paper, we prepare from dawn to dusk. How much preparation for a mere examination! And yet I have raised a great question: why have I come into this world? Who sent me? Why must I die—I who do not wish to? Who is it that compels my death?

Desires press upon us, and funeral rites multiply. Needs burden us, and death strikes. Youth's intoxication clouds our vision, yet death wrings the neck and drags us beneath the earth. Power's narcosis blinds us, yet worms feast upon bone and sinew. Why should a man who lived as a king, arrayed in silk, descend to his grave wrapped in but the simplest shroud?

Death pursues you. Death pursues me. One day we shall both be seized. Hazrat Ali (upon him be peace) declared:

⟪ar-quote:الموت ! لیسَ مِنہُ الفَوت۔⟫

"Death! From it there is no escape."

⟪ar-quote:أِن أقمتُم لَہُ أخَذَکُم !  أِن فَرَرتُ مِنھُم أَدرَکَکُم⟫

"If you stand against it, it seizes you. If you flee from it, it overtakes you."

What shall you do when you cannot fight it, cannot flee from it, cannot hide? He cried out:

⟪ar-quote:الوحا الوحا⟫

"Make haste! Make haste!"

⟪ar-quote:النجاہ النجاہ۔⟫

"Seek salvation! Seek salvation! Gather the means to succeed before the One from whom you cannot escape!"

Today we live as though no God exists. At night, we conduct ourselves as if Allah sleeps and no one watches.
