# Dua'im-ul-Islam - Prayer

## General

When a prayer remains incomplete, the worshipper incurs an obligation to fulfill the arrears through rigorous observance and disciplined practice. This requirement admits of no exception or remission. The negligence of one who bears responsibility for prayer becomes a matter of grave concern for those entrusted with spiritual guidance, who therefore persist in urging constant vigilance in prayer.[^cite-1]

The foundation of this principle rests upon the understanding that prayer (ṣalāt) is not merely a personal devotion but a pillar of the covenant between the believer and the Divine. When the prescribed prayers are deferred or abandoned, a debt accrues that cannot be discharged through mere intention; rather, it demands the systematic restoration of what was withheld. The urgency with which the guides (duʿāt) insist upon this completion reflects their recognition that prayer stands at the threshold between obedience and neglect, between spiritual ascent and descent.[^cite-2]

Moreover, the person who falls into such negligence must understand that this lapse affects not only the individual conscience but the spiritual health of the community of believers. The guides therefore do not relent in their counsel, knowing that constancy in prayer (murābaṭat al-ṣalāt) is the means by which the soul achieves its primary perfection (kamāl awwal) and draws near to the stations of the sacred law (sharīʿa) and the inner meaning (ṭarīqa).[^cite-3]
