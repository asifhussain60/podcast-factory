# Dua'im-ul-Islam - Prayer

<!-- section 1 (id=1426, raw_sort=1): General -->

## General

When a prescribed prayer (*salāt*) remains unfulfilled, the believer must discharge the obligation through compensatory performance under rigorous conditions and protocols established by the revealed law. This postponement is in no way a dispensation from the duty itself. Rather, the negligence of one who bears responsibility for prayer becomes a source of spiritual jeopardy not only for that individual but for all who depend upon the integrity of their ritual observance; thus the learned have long insisted upon the constancy and renewal of prayer as an indispensable anchor of faith.[^cite-1]

The principle underlying this teaching rests upon the understanding that prayer (daʿwa, the summons to God) constitutes both an expression of servitude and a means of spiritual elevation. To defer its performance is to fracture the continuity of one's covenant with the Divine, a rupture that cannot remain suspended indefinitely. The Imams have therefore prescribed that all accumulated missed prayers—whether through inadvertence, hardship, or transgression—must be made up according to precise rules of substitution and intention, so that the soul may be restored to its proper standing.[^cite-2]
