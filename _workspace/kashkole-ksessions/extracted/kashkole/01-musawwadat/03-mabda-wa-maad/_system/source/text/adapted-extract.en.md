## The Distinction Between Islam and Faith

The word "Islam" derives from ⟪ar:سَلَمَ⟫ (to obey), signifying submission of the head to the Prophet's utterance and compliance with his commands. The word "faith" (*īmān*) derives from ⟪ar:أمن⟫ (to attain peace), denoting the peace that accrues from genuine belief. Faith cannot be attained without Islam, just as tranquility cannot exist without acceptance. Faith represents the post-Islamic station, arising from intimate knowledge of the inner (bāṭin) limits of the outward (ẓāhir) words of witness and the fulfillment of their spiritual rights. Islam constitutes the outer paradise; faith, the inner paradise.[^cite-1]

The understanding of inner meaning from outward statement may be illustrated as follows. When the Prophet (ṣallā-Allāh ʿalayhi wa-ālihi wa-sallam) recited the outward words—⟪ar-quote:اِنَّ الشَّمسَ تَطَّلِع مِن مَغرَبِھَا عَلیَ رَأسِ الثَّلاَثۃ الف سنۃ⟫, "The sun will surely rise from the west after three thousand years"—he referred esoterically to ⟪ar:امام مہدی علیہ السلام⟫ the Imam al-Mahdī, who appeared from the West. The Prophet thereby prepared his community to progress inwardly toward the esoteric word with sincerity. After fulfilling the letter of the law, sincerity becomes obligatory.[^cite-2] Through the outward word, one enters the sphere of Islam and the sword is withheld; yet one does not enter the Garden. That is, there occurs no union with the celestial boundaries.

### The Sphere of Divine Unity

The scope (*dāʾira*) of divine unity encompasses ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* (the First Intellect), which has given no companion, and thus is not accompanied by an assembly of intellects. At His command arose ⟪ar:منبعث اول⟫ *al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal* (the First Emanator), and with him came the gathering of all intellects—a day in the month of the divine calendar. ⟪ar:منبعث اول⟫ *Al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal* is what is termed ⟪ar:جنت الماوا⟫ *Jannat al-Māwā* (the Garden of Abode), and *al-ʿAql al-Awwal*, being at the boundary (*Sidrat al-Muntahā*), is that transcendent station which is the Divine Essence (*al-Dhāt*).[^cite-4]

> ⟪quran 53:14-15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏عِندَ سِدْرَةِ ٱلْمُنتَهَىٰ ‎ ‏عِندَهَا جَنَّةُ ٱلْمَأْوَىٰٓ ‎⟫
> *Near Sidrat-ul-Muntaha [lote-tree of the utmost boundary], Near it is the Paradise of Abode.*
> — **Quran 53:14-15**

Although countless souls inhabit each rational realm, the chief of every intellect progresses with his proper realm. When the second intellect (ʿaql thānī) merges into its own sphere, only two circles remain: that of ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* and that of ⟪ar:منبعث اول⟫ *al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal*.

Likewise, when we depart from this world, our soul does not reach the Imam (who dwells in the palace of *al-ʿAql al-Awwal*), but rather reaches ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ *Bāb al-Abwāb* (the Gate of Gates, who stands in opposition to the First Emanator). ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ *Bāb al-Abwāb* bears the form of a gathering, not the form of a sanctuary. When the Imam recites the Qur'an to his successor, the successor receives it from the congregation gathered at ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ *Bāb al-Abwāb*, and the Imam transmits the entire congregation to his successor—he who was hitherto the *ḥujja* (proof). ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ *Bāb al-Abwāb* assumes the form of the heart in this gathering. The Imam, who until now has been merely a practitioner (*nāṣwī*, one who follows the law), now receives the Divinity (*Lāhūt*), which is the Imamate itself. When the Imamate descends upon him, he becomes the Imam *bi-al-fiʿl* (in actuality), from his former state as Imam *bi-al-quwwa* (in potentiality), and the current Imam (the father) dies.

⟪ar:لاَ حَولَ وَلاَ قُوَّتَ اِلاَّ بِاللہ⟫ *Lā ḥawla wa-lā quwwata illā bi-llāh*—there exists no power or strength save through Allāh—signifies that ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* alone possesses all power. All strength emanates from Him and returns unto Him. This elevates our wonder: how magnificent must be the ⟪ar:مبدِع تعالی⟫ *Mubdiʿ Taʿālā* (the Originator, Most High) who has fashioned such an incomparable ⟪ar:مبدَع⟫ *Mubdaʿ* (creation)!

## The Imamic Structure: Human Nature and Divinity

All major loci (*maqāmāt*) reach one hundred (*kamāl awwal*, primary perfection). When the count reaches one hundred, it is time for their ⟪ar:أرتفاع الدرجۃ⟫ *irtifāʿ al-darajah* (elevation of rank). There exist one hundred and ten instances signifying that one has transcended the ten degrees of ⟪ar:حجیت⟫ *ḥujjiyyat* (the rank of proof) and entered the next rank of ⟪ar:حجابیت⟫ *ḥijābiyyat* (the rank of veil).

*(Al-Kawākib al-Durriyyah; transmitted by Sayyidunā Ismāʿīl ibn Hibatullāh)*

### The Cause of Organic Defect

⟪ar:سیدنا قاضی نعمان⟫ Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān, in his work ⟪ar:دعائم الاسلام⟫ *Daʿāʾim al-Islām*, states: "Allāh has apportioned faith among all the organs of the human being such that the faith of each organ is distinct from that of another."[^cite-5] This stands in contrast to ⟪ar:ھیکل امامی⟫ *al-haykal al-imāmī* (the Imamic Structure), which is fashioned upon human form; therein the divine boundaries (*ḥudūd*) occupy the stations of the various organs. The faith of every member of the human body is perfected when that member obeys and honors the limit (*ḥadd*) stationed before it. For example, the faith of the eyes is fulfilled through obedience to the ⟪ar:ھیکل امامی⟫ limits that stand opposite the eyes.

⟪ar:مولانا حسین بن جدین اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ Mawlānā al-Ḥusayn ibn Jadīn (may Allāh elevate his secret) teaches: "I have elevated you through the senses." Corresponding to the senses of this Imam are the learned scholars, who are the limits of the Imam. Should a person deny a limit, or harbor doubt concerning its station, or treat it lightly, his refusal (*nafs ḥassiyya*, the sensate soul) will diminish his vital powers; and the organ situated within that limit will experience deterioration. All organs are servants of the soul and profit from it. When decline befalls the self, loss inevitably follows in the organ.[^cite-6] Only those born with defective limbs are they who, in prior spheres, denied the ⟪ar:ھیکل امامی⟫ limits that were appointed to the stations of their presently defective organs.

In the ⟪ar:شریعت نبویۃ⟫ *Sharīʿat al-Nabawiyyah* (the Prophetic Law), through ⟪ar:سباقیت و تخلط⟫ *sabāqiyyat wa-takhallūṭ* (precedence and mixture) in ⟪ar:أوامر و نواحی⟫ *awāmir wa-nāḥī* (commandments and prohibitions), the effect of hastening and delaying becomes established in the organs of the human body. When an invitation is presented to the realm of intentions, should a person deny a limit within that invitation, then upon his birth into this world, the effect of his denial will manifest as an organic defect. Should he enter this world and thereafter confess that limit and offer repentance (*tawba*), then in the next sphere the defect will disappear; otherwise, the consequence of one sphere continues to affect the next, and that the next sphere in turn.

*(Al-Kawākib al-Durriyyah; transmitted by Sayyidunā Ismāʿīl ibn Hibatullāh)*

## Masters of the Realms, Spheres, and Forms

> ⟪quran 71:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱللَّهُ أَنۢبَتَكُم مِّنَ ٱلْأَرْضِ نَبَاتًۭا ‎⟫
> *And Allah has brought you forth from the (dust of) earth.*
> — **Quran 71:17**

This verse alludes to divine nativity (*wilāda*), and in context refers to the emergence of the human race. Similarly:

> ⟪quran 16:78⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱللَّهُ أَخْرَجَكُم مِّنۢ بُطُونِ أُمَّهَتِكُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ شَيْـًۭٔا وَجَعَلَ لَكُمُ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْأَبْصَرَ وَٱلْأَفْـِٔدَةَ ۙ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And Allah has brought you out from the wombs of your mothers while you know nothing. And He gave you hearing, sight, and hearts that you might give thanks (to Allah).*
> — **Quran 16:78**

The learned master prepares his son from his progeny and establishes a succession for him. Twenty-seven persons accompany him, and through his son the Imamate is secured.

---

Between the first and the last stand twenty-eight great personalities (*ʿuẓamāʾ*) through whom spirituality attains completion. At the conclusion of one cycle, the heavens and earth are folded; at the commencement of the next cycle, a surge of divine realities (*āliha*) erupts. The four-hundredth cycle originates from the Ka'ba. Likewise, the form (*jism*) of the four-hundredth Qa'im (*al-Qāʾim*, the Riser) is also born from the Ka'ba.[^cite-7]

Revelation commenced with the eternity (*azaliyyat*) of the Qa'im. His true son and successor shall be the next emergent Qa'im. Yet he established his son as Imam and appointed him caliph (*khalīfa*) over the eighth sphere, so that the intellect of the eighth was elevated to the rank of the ninth, and thus even the third intellect and its sphere were merged into *Haḍrat al-Quds* (the Sanctified Presence). Now the governance of the physical realm rests in the hands of the eternal form that appeared at the beginning of the cycle; when he became the eighth, and later died, he reached the station of his father (the ninth), where he establishes a seat (*kursī*). The Imam's manifestation over seven thousand years constitutes his emergence. He occupies the seats of all the seven-thousand-year Imams from the station of the ninth. This is a single seat. In this manner, there are six seats of the Qa'im, and beneath each seat lie the seats of seven thousand years of Imams. When the seventh Qa'im arrives, he gathers his seven-thousand-year Imams and then the prior six seats, and becomes a greater seat—the seat of the Qa'im period of *Kashf* (unveiling). Thereafter, seven thousand years pass. Their Qa'im gathers these seven-thousand-year Imams and likewise collects the seat of the Master of *Kashf*, and this is termed a *Nufkha* (trumpet call)—comprising the fifty-seven-thousand-year seat of the Imams.

In this manner, there are seven *Kashf*s and seventy in one era (*dawr*). When they are established, they gather all the seats of four hundred thousand years and become the established era. Should there be four hundred thousand such years repeated ten times, a form (*ṣūra*) is established. Then, when ten such forms are established, a grand congregation (*jama'a*) is formed, containing the seats of four hundred thousand periods. This is the *Khalīfat al-Khalīfah* of the former eternal cycle (*azaliyyat al-Jathā*), who receives partial caliphate (*khalīfat al-juzʾ*) from the eighth sphere. Only the Master of the Qa'im receives complete caliphate.

The first is the Caliph of the Qa'im; the second is the Caliph of the Qa'im. Yet the reality is that the true Caliph of the Master of the Qa'im is the Master of the Later Qa'im (*Ṣāḥib al-Qāʾim al-Akhīr*), who shall appear at the close of the tenth covering. The founder of the last cycle is the successor of the master of the first cycle, who shall ascend with the entire congregation; thereafter, materiality shall cease, and only the spiritual realm shall remain.

This is the tenth form of the Qa'im that shall appear at the beginning of the tenth covering. When this reaches its conclusion (the form of the Tawra), it possesses a distinct process, differing from the closure of the second covering.

*(Al-Kawākib al-Durriyyah; transmitted by Ḥaḍrat ʿAbdullāh Ṣāḥib)*

## The Lote-Tree of the Uttermost Boundary and the Garden of Abode

In Arabic, ⟪ar:سدرۃ⟫ *al-Sidra* is called the "jujube tree." This tree possesses such singular glory that it always grows in solitude, with no other tree neighboring it. ⟪ar:جنت الماوی⟫ *Jannat al-Māwā* (the Garden of Abode) denotes a garden wherein people may enter and find refuge.[^cite-8]

⟪ar:سدرۃ المنتہی⟫ *Al-Sidrat al-Muntahā* (the Lote-Tree of the Uttermost Boundary) is called the First Intellect because it stands alone in its realm—a realm of pure unity wherein naught exists within its sphere save it alone, with no other beside it or in relation to its sphere. It was the sole agent of creation. ⟪ar:جنت الماوی⟫ *Jannat al-Māwā* refers to the one who advanced after beholding the First Intellect, yet he possessed a circle (*dāʾira*) of twenty-seven who came under his protection. All the intellects shall merge into the realm of the First Source (*al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal*) as the day of the divine month progresses. For this reason, its circle is called ⟪ar:حضیرۃ القدس⟫ *al-Ḥaḍīrat al-Quds* (the Sanctuary of Holiness). This same structure was thereafter established in all worlds and realms.[^cite-9]

In the physical world, the eternal form (*al-jism al-azalī*) dwells in the palace of the First Intellect, serving as the sole master of its entire cycle; its successor resides in the palace of the First Intellect, destined to oversee the entire cycle. In the celestial realm, the sky stands in the palace of the First Intellect—for it contains neither stars nor planets; all stars are in the constellation, which itself is in the palace of the First Emanator. In the seventy periods, the *Ayn al-Akhira* (the Last Essence) dwells in the palace of the First Intellect, while the *Ayn al-Awwala* (the First Essence) are their successors, standing opposite the First Source. All the Imams shall be gathered into the first place. Similarly, in every era, the Imam dwells in the palace of the First Intellect, and his successor (*bāb*) dwells in the station of the First Source. The gathering of the forms of all believers occurs at *Bāb al-Bāb* (the Gate of Gates). Likewise, in the realm of matrimony, the husband dwells in the palace of the intellect and serves as the sole custodian of the household within his dwelling, while his wife dwells in the palace of the soul, in whose womb children are nurtured.

## The Disobedient Company

The eighth sphere (*Āsher*) came to recognize that the spirits under his dominion had grown so oppressed that their density could not be rectified by keeping them in the spiritual world. He resolved to create a physical world into which this disobedient company (*qāmat al-fityya*) could be brought—at human cost—and invited to respond. Those accepting the invitation would be guided toward subtlety; those refusing obedience would remain in the world of materiality.[^cite-10] He conceived this because the Creator had formed the soul in the spiritual world in the most perfect form.

When the disobedient company fell from the spiritual world, they praised Āsher for his descent, as the Qur'ān attests:

> ⟪ar:بِسْمِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلرَّحْمَنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
> دَعْوَهُمْ فِيهَا سُبْحَنَكَ ٱللَّهُمَّ وَتَحِيَّتُهُمْ فِيهَا سَلَمٌ وَءَاخِرُ دَعْوَهُمْ أَنِ ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَلَمِينَ⟫
> *Their prayer therein will be, "Glory be to You, O Allah," and there will be their mutual greeting of peace, and their prayer will end therein: all praise be to Allah, the Lord of all the worlds.*
> — **Quran 10:10**

Thus this company did not reproach Āsher, saying, "We were corrupted because of you; had you not corrupted us, we would not have departed hence"; rather, they praised him. On account of this praise, Āsher took upon himself the burden of delivering them from that realm first. For this reason, this prayer is recited at the conclusion of every gathering, as it was chanted when Āsher departed the assembly.

The wise ones teach that when the disobedient company transgressed against Āsher and subsequently confessed their error, seeking his forgiveness, both statements commenced with the *Kalima* (the Word). In disobedience, they declared, "We do not hear nor obey you." In acknowledgment, they proclaimed, "There is no god but Āsher."

Just as the invitation (*daʿwa*, the summons) of the seven intellects was established in the spiritual world by command of the First Intellect, so too did Āsher intend to establish his invitation in the physical world. The distinction lay in this: the invitation of the First Intellect transcended time and space, whereas Āsher's invitation was confined within temporal and spatial bounds. Just as the subtle candle cannot be removed without its dense tablet, so too was it necessary to transmute light into physicality in order to rescue these beings from the spiritual realm.[^cite-11]
