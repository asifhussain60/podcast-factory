## The Distinction Between Islam and Faith

The word "Islam" derives from ⟪ar:سَلَمَ⟫ (to obey), signifying submission to the words of ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ and obedience to his commandments. The word "faith" derives from ⟪ar:أمن⟫ (to attain peace), denoting the attainment of tranquility. Yet faith cannot be achieved without Islam, just as peace proves impossible without acceptance. Faith constitutes the station that follows Islam and emerges through knowledge of the inner limits concealed within the outer utterance of testimony, and the fulfillment of their respective rights. Islam yields a paradise of the outward, and faith yields a paradise of the inward. An illustration of understanding the esoteric meaning from an exoteric utterance follows: The Messenger of God declared, "Verily the sun shall rise from the west after three thousand years,"—a statement referring to ⟪ar:امام مہدی علیہ السلام⟫ (the Mahdi, peace be upon him), who emerged from the western regions.[^cite-1] Between the sun (Shams) and the moon (Qamar) stand multiple parallelisms:

### The Sphere of Unity

⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ The First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal) exists within ⟪ar:دائرہ وحدت⟫ the Circle of Unity. He has not emitted any peer, and thus he stands unaccompanied by any assembly of intellects. He issued the call to ⟪ar:منبعث اول⟫ the First Emanator (al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal), and by his command the assembly of all intellects comes to be with him on the Day of Resurrection. ⟪ar:منبعث اول⟫ The First Emanator is that of which it is said, ⟪ar:جنت الماوا⟫ "the Garden of Abode" (Jannat al-Māwā), and ⟪ar:سدرۃ المنتہا عقل اول⟫ the Lote-tree of the Limit is the First Intellect in its ⟪ar:ذات⟫ essence (dhāt).

> ⟪quran 53:14-15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏عِندَ سِدْرَةِ ٱلْمُنتَهَىٰ ‎ ‏عِندَهَا جَنَّةُ ٱلْمَأْوَىٰٓ ‎⟫
> *Near Sidrat-ul-Muntaha [lote-tree of the utmost boundary (beyond which none can pass)], Near it is the Paradise of Abode.*
> — **Quran 53:14-15**

Although countless souls dwell in each rational realm, the noble of every intellect progresses within his own sphere. The second intellect ⟪ar:عقل ثانی⟫ (al-ʿAql al-Thānī) merges into its own sphere. Two circles alone remain: that of ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ the First Intellect and that of ⟪ar:منبعث اول⟫ the First Emanator.

In the same manner, when a soul departs this life, it does not reach the ⟪ar:صورت⟫ form (sūrat) of the Imam—who dwells in the palace of ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ the First Intellect—but rather reaches ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ the Gate of Gates (Bāb al-Abwāb), who corresponds to ⟪ar:منبعث اول⟫ the First Emanator and stands opposite to him. The Gate of Gates possesses the form of a gathering, not the form of a sanctuary. When the Imam recites the Qur'an to his son, he receives it from the gathering of the Gate of Gates and bestows the entire gathering upon his son (who until now was the Proof). It takes the form of the locus of the heart within this gathering. The Imam, who until now practiced only the esoteric dimension (Nāsūt), now receives the divine reality (Lāhūt), which is Imamate. When Imamate meets the Imam, he becomes the Imam ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ in actuality (bi-l-fiʿl) from ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ potentiality (bi-l-qūwwa), and the former Imam (the father) departs.

⟪ar:لاَ حَولَ وَلاَ قُوَّتَ اِلاَّ بِاللہ⟫ "There is no power and no strength save in Allah"—this proclaims that ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ the First Intellect alone possesses power. All potency emanates from Him and returns to Him. This compels us to marvel at how sublime must be the ⟪ar:مبدِع تعالی⟫ Originator, Most High (Mubdiʿ Taʿālī), who has fashioned such a magnificent ⟪ar:مبدَع⟫ emanation (mubdaʿ)![^cite-3]

### The Cause of Organ Defect

⟪ar:سیدنا قاضی نعمان⟫ Sayyidunā al-Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān states in his work ⟪ar:دعائم الاسلام⟫ *Daʿāʾim al-Islām* (The Foundations of Islam): "Allah has apportioned faith among all the organs of the human body such that the faith of each organ is distinct from that of another."[^cite-5] This stands in contrast to the ⟪ar:ھیکل امامی⟫ Imamate Body (Haykal al-Imāmī)—which is patterned upon the human form, within which the limits of religion occupy the stations of the various organs. The faith of every member of a human being is consummated by his repenting and obeying the limit stationed before that organ. For instance, the faith of the eyes is fulfilled through obedience to the limits stationed before the eyes.

⟪ar:مولانا حسین بن جدین اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ Mawlānā Ḥusayn ibn Jādīn (may Allah sanctify his secret) states: "I have elevated you through ⟪ar:مدبر عالم⟫ the governance of the world (mudabbir al-ʿālam), which is ⟪ar:انسان کلیۃ (امام)⟫ the universal human being—the Imam (al-insān al-kulliyya), by means of ⟪ar:قامت بشریۃ کلیۃ⟫ the universal human constitution (qāmat al-bashariyya al-kulliyya). The faculties of this Imam stand in contrast to the bodily senses; the religious scholars are those senses, and they are the limits of the Imam. When a person ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ denies a limit of the Imam (upon whom be peace), or doubts his station, or treats him lightly, then his sin diminishes ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ the sensate soul (al-nafs al-ḥissiyya) and his limb that stands within that limit. All organs serve the soul and are beneficial to it. When the soul diminishes, the organ suffers loss correspondingly. Those alone who are born with defective limbs are they who in previous cycles denied the limits that were ⟪ar:ھیکل امامی⟫ stationed in place of their defective limbs.

In ⟪ar:شریعت نبویۃ⟫ the Prophetic Law (al-Sharīʿa al-Nabawiyya), there are ⟪ar:سباقیت و تخلط⟫ precedence and delay (al-sabāqiyya wa-l-takhallut) and ⟪ar:أوامر و نواحی⟫ commands and prohibitions (al-awāmir wa-l-nawāḥī), and the effect of haste and delay is established in the organs of the human body. When an invitation is presented in the world of intentions, if a person denies a limit in that invitation, then upon birth into this world he shall witness the effect of his denial in the form of a defect in his soul. Should he enter this world and confess that limit, offering his repentance, then in the next sphere the defect of his organ shall vanish; otherwise, the effect of one sphere continues to impress upon the next, and the next upon the third.[^cite-6]

Between the first and the last, there are twenty-eight great personalities through whom spiritual perfection is completed. At the conclusion of one cycle, heaven and earth are folded, and at the beginning of the next cycle, a surge of divine souls emerges. The four-hundredth cycle originates from the Kaaba; similarly, the body of the four-hundredth Imam is born from the Kaaba as well.

Revelation began with the unfolding of the first cycle. His true son and successor shall be the next unfolding cycle. Yet in part, he established his son as Imam and made him the regent of the Eighth Intellect, elevating the Eighth Intellect's rank. The Ninth then merged into the Eighth, and thus the Third Intellect and its sphere merged into the Holy Presence (Ḥaḍrat al-Quds).[^cite-7]

The stewardship of the physical world now rests in the hands of the eternal body that emerged at the beginning of the cycle; this being now became the regent (al-Qāʾim). Upon his death, he reaches the station of his father (the Eighth Intellect), where he establishes a throne. The Imam's manifestation spans seven thousand years as the appearance of the Imam. He occupies the thrones of all the Imams across those seven thousand years, viewing them from the station of the Eighth Intellect. It becomes his singular throne. Thus there are six thrones of the Qāʾim (the Establisher), and beneath each throne lie the thrones of the Imams of seven thousand years. When the seventh Qāʾim arrives, he gathers all his Imams of seven thousand years and collects the previous six thrones, merging them into a single great throne—that of the Qāʾim period of *kashf* (unveiling). Thereafter, seven thousand years elapse. Their Qāʾim gathers these seven thousand Imams and also collects the throne of the Master of *kashf*, and this is called a "trumpet" (ṣūr), comprising the gathering of fifty-seven thousand years of Imams.

In this manner, there are seven unveilings (*kashf*) and seventy in a single epoch. When they are established, they gather together all the thrones of four hundred thousand years and become the established epoch. Should there be four hundred thousand such years repeated ten times, then a pattern is formed. When ten such patterns are established, a quorum-form is created, containing the four hundred thousand epochs. This is the Caliph al-Kall of the earlier eternal gathering, who receives the partial caliphate of the Eighth Intellect. Only the Master of *Mūr* obtains complete caliphate. The first is the Caliph of *Mūr*; the second is the Caliph of *Mūr*.

Yet the truth is this: the true Caliph of the Master of *Mūr* shall be the Master of the Final *Mūr*, who will appear at the end of the tenth *Mūr*. The founder of the final cycle is the successor of the master of the first cycle, and he will ascend with the entire assembly, after which physicality ceases and only the spiritual realm remains. This is the tenth body of the cycle that shall appear at the beginning of the tenth unveiling. When this constitutes the conclusion of the tenth form (the configuration of the *Tawrāt*), it possesses a special process distinct from the conclusion of the second unveiling.[^cite-8]

## The Lote-tree of the Limit and the Garden of Abode

In Arabic, ⟪ar:سدرۃ⟫ the Lote-tree (al-Sidra) is called "the tree of solitude," for it possesses such singular glory that it always grows in isolation and no other tree stands beside it. ⟪ar:جنت الماوی⟫ Jannat al-Māwī (the Garden of Abode) denotes a garden into which souls may enter and find refuge. ⟪ar:سدرۃ المنتہی⟫ The Lote-tree of the Limit is the First Intellect itself, because it is unique in its world and stands alone in the realm of unity—that which exists within its sphere possesses no peer and none stands alongside it or in relation to it. It is the sole agent of creation. ⟪ar:جنت الماوی⟫ The Garden of Abode refers to that which moved forward after witnessing the First Intellect, yet it possessed a circle of twenty-seven souls who came under its protection. All the intellects merge into the sphere of the Garden of Abode as the cycle progresses. For this reason, its circle is called ⟪ar:حضیرۃ القدس⟫ *Ḥaḍrat al-Quds* (the Holy Presence). This same configuration was then mirrored in all worlds and realms.

In the physical world, the eternal body dwells in the palace of the First Intellect—the sole sovereign of its entire cycle—and its heir dwells in the palace of the Garden of Abode, who shall inherit the entire cycle. In the celestial (*barza*) world, heaven stands in the palace of the First Intellect, for neither stars nor planets exist there; all stars abide in the constellation, which dwells in the palace of the First Intellect. In the seventy epochs, *ʿAyn al-Ākhira* (the Final Eye) dwells in the palace of the First Intellect, while *ʿAyn al-Ūlā* (the First Eye) stands as its heir, positioned opposite to the Garden of Abode. All the Imams culminate in the palace of the First Intellect. Likewise, in each epoch, the Imam dwells in the palace of the First Intellect and his heir dwells in the station of the Garden of Abode. The gathering of the forms of all believers reaches *Bāb al-Abwāb* (the Gate of Gates). Similarly, in the realm of matrimony, the husband dwells in the palace of the intellect and remains the sole custodian of the household within his dwelling, while his wife dwells in the palace of the soul, in whose womb children are nurtured.

## The Disobedient Company

The Eighth Intellect became convinced that the souls under his charge had become so oppressed and their density so entrenched that it could not be lifted by keeping them in the spiritual world. He resolved to create a world in which this ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫ disobedient company (qāmat al-fiyya) could be brought down—albeit through the human condition—and invited anew. Those who would accept the invitation should be led toward subtlety, while those who would not obey should be allowed to remain within this world of density. He pondered this because the Creator had fashioned the soul in the spiritual world in the most excellent form.[^cite-10]

When the disobedient company fell from the spiritual world, they did not rebuke the descending Eighth Intellect, as the Qur'an attests:

> ⟪ar:بِسْمِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلرَّحْمَنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
دَعْوَهُمْ فِيهَا سُبْحَنَكَ ٱللَّهُمَّ وَتَحِيَّتُهُمْ فِيهَا سَلَمٌ وَءَاخِرُ دَعْوَهُمْ أَنِ ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَلَمِينَ⟫
> *Therein will be their cry: Glory be to You, O Allah! And their greeting therein will be: Peace! And the last of their cry will be: All the Praises and thanks be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.*
> — **Quran 10:10**

Instead, this company praised him. They did not protest: "We were corrupted because of you; had you not corrupted us, we would not have fallen hence." Rather, they gave praise. It was precisely because of this praise that the Eighth Intellect accepted the responsibility to deliver them from this state. For this reason, that prayer is recited at the close of every gathering, just as it was recited when the Eighth Intellect departed the assembly.

The wise say that when the disobedient company transgressed against the Eighth Intellect and thereafter acknowledged their error and sought his forgiveness, both utterances opened with *al-kalima* (the Word). When in disobedience, they said: "We do not hear you, nor do we obey you." And when they made acknowledgment, they said: "There is no god but the Eighth Intellect."

Just as the invitation of the seven intellects was established in the spiritual world by the command of the First Intellect, the Eighth Intellect likewise resolved to establish his invitation in the physical world. The difference lay in this: the invitation of the First Intellect transcends time and space, whereas the invitation of the Eighth Intellect operates within the confines of temporal succession. Just as a candle (which is subtle) cannot be separated from its candlestick (which is dense), so too, in order to remove these beings from the spiritual world, it was necessary to transmute light into physicality.[^cite-11]
