## The Virtue of Surah Al-Fatiha

⟪ar:سورۃ الفاتحۃ⟫ possesses innumerable blessings. It has been said that the glory of this Surah is so profound that one need not be surprised if it were recited over the deceased, for through its blessings the departed soul might rise. This great Surah has been designated ⟪ar:'أم الکتاب'⟫ (the mother of the Book), inasmuch as the light of the entire Qur'an is derived from this single Surah. All other surahs serve as extended exegesis of this one great composition, which stands as their source and fountainhead.

The Qur'an has also been compared to the Gospel by way of analogy. The Gospel, it is said, represents an interpretation of the four words that Christ preached to his four chosen disciples. These four disciples collected and transmitted His words, gathering them into a single book which they named ⟪ar:'انجیل'⟫, a term derived from ⟪ar:'نجل'⟫, meaning "offspring." Thus the origin—the *um al-Kitab*—of the Gospel consists of those four utterances. Similarly, the source of the Qur'an is Surah al-Hamd (the Chapter of Praise), which itself is called the *um al-Kitab*.[^cite-1]

The virtue of this Surah lies in its indispensability to prayer (*ṣalāh*, the obligatory devotional act). One may recite only this Surah alone in prayer without reciting another, and the prayer shall be fulfilled. Yet should one recite another Surah but omit this one, the prayer will not be accepted—it will be as though lost entirely without ⟪ar:خیر العمل⟫ this Surah.

Explaining the rank of this Surah, the Almighty says to His Messenger (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him):

> ⟪quran 15:87⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَكَ سَبْعًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْمَثَانِى وَٱلْقُرْآنَ ٱلْعَظِيمَ ‎⟫
> *And indeed, We have bestowed upon you seven of Al-Mathani (the seven repeatedly recited Verses), (i.e. Surat Al-Fatiha) and the Grand Quran.*
> — **Quran 15:87**

It is thus a collection of seven verses that are recited repeatedly in every prayer.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) declared: "The banner that shall be given to me on the Day of Resurrection, by which I shall be distinguished from all other prophets, is called *Liwa al-Hamd* (the Banner of Praise). It shall bear seven rings, each extending in length from east to west."[^cite-2]

The Prophet (peace be upon him) also said: "Were I to wish, I could write commentaries on Surah Al-Hamd so voluminous that fifty camels would be needed to carry them."

### Other Narratives Concerning Surah Al-Fatiha

Imam al-Ḥusayn (upon him be peace) appointed a teacher to instruct his son in Surah al-Fatiha. When the son had learned it and recited it before the Imam, he was filled with such joy that he adorned the boy's mouth with jewels.

During the Battle of Siffīn, a man's hand was shattered. He came to Mawlānā ʿAlī (upon him be peace) saying, "Master, my hand pains me greatly, and I cannot lift a sword because of it." Mawlānā ʿAlī placed a cloth upon the hand, breathed upon it, and when the cloth was removed, the hand was wholly restored. The man asked, "O Master, what words did you recite and breathe upon my hand?" He replied, "I recited Surah Al-Fatiha." The man made light of the matter and asked, "Did healing come merely from reciting Surah Al-Fatiha?" It is said that thereupon his hand broke again and returned to its former state.[^cite-3]

When the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) would sneeze, he would recite Surah Al-Fatiha in its entirety, blow upon both palms, and wipe his face. Adam (upon him be peace) was the first to sneeze; immediately upon doing so, he said, "All praise belongs to Allah." The Almighty was so pleased with this utterance that He elevated his station. For this reason, we continue this practice of Adam's in response to sneezing.

Noah (upon him be peace) was called *ʿAbd al-Shakūr* (the Grateful Servant) because whenever he ate something, he would first say "Bismillah-ar-Raḥmān-ar-Raḥīm" (In the Name of Allah, the Merciful, the Compassionate), and at the end would say "Al-Ḥamdulillāh" (Praise be to Allah). The sustenance of the spiritual realm was granted in the form of the Qur'an, whose very first words became "Al-Ḥamdulillāh."

The majesty of Surah Al-Fatiha is such that were the people of the Seventy (the seventy nations) to comprehend it fully, there would be no need for the revelation of the rest of the Qur'an—for the entire essence of the Qur'an dwells within this Surah. Thus none of our prayers are accepted without it. In any prayer wherein there is no bowing (rukūʿ), Surah Al-Fatiha is not recited; therefore, it is not recited in the funeral prayer, which has its own particular principles.

All other surahs were revealed in fragments, yet Surah Al-Fatiha was revealed in perfect, complete form.

Amīr al-Muʾminīn declared: "Were I to wish, I could explain the inner meaning (*taʾwīl*, esoteric exegesis) of Surah Al-Fatiha at such length that seventy camels would be needed to bear it."

## The Jewels of Surah Al-Fatiha

The outward verses and words of Surah Al-Fatiha are countable, yet the luminous gems (*jawāhir nūrāniyyah*) emanating from it are beyond enumeration, and their virtues cannot be fully reckoned. The outward essence consists of twenty-one letters. Of these twenty-one, fourteen are called *Jawāhir Nūrāniyyah* (Luminous Jewels). These fourteen subdivide into two categories: seven are *Nūrāniyyah Khafīfah* (Light Subtle Jewels) and seven are *Jawāhir Nūrāniyyah Thaqīlah* (Heavy Luminous Jewels). Beyond these there are seven *Jawāhir al-Ẓulm* (Jewels of Oppression or Darkness).

The third category—the jewels of darkness—must be understood in a subtle sense: although they are dark by nature, they resemble a dark house within which a lamp burns, illuminating the entire space. The light is divine, while the house of darkness is their corporeal form, which is mortal and transient. In addition to these three categories, there are seven further letters of *al-Muʿjam* (the spelled-out letters), which are oppressive and weighty.

| *Al-Nūrāniyyah Thaqīlah* (Light Heavy) | I | P | S | K | M | N | — |
| *Al-Ẓulmaniyyah Khafīfah* (Darkness Subtle) | B | T | D | Y | Z | G | — |
| *Al-Ẓulmaniyyah Thaqīlah* (Darkness Heavy) | W | C | — | — | — | — | — |

#### The Light Subtle and Light Heavy Letters

The seven letters of the Luminous Subtle refer to the seven Primordial Intellects (*al-ʿUqūl al-Qadīmah*) of the spiritual world, which are free from all deviation. The seven of the Luminous Heavy refer to those intellects of the spiritual world that emerged after the eternal intellects and became their successors (*khalīfāt*).

In another sense, these seven letters likewise signify the seven intellects of the cosmos. The word *thaqīlah* (heavy) refers to weightiness; the light of light itself becomes the cause of weight—hence the dots appearing within certain of their letters.

#### Darkness (*Ẓulm*)

Surah Al-Fatiha comprises twenty-one letters among which the dark-heavy letters are not enumerated. Darkness refers to *al-Naqqa*, which, though dark by virtue of its purity, stands adjacent to the light. Though these possess evil and oppression, the divine light shines within this darkness, rendering them luminous. The analogy is a lamp within a dark house: by lighting the lamp, the darkness becomes light. Similarly, these possess *Lāhūtī* (divine) light, and therefore they are themselves luminous beings.

Their subtle forms are attached to the beams of support in such a way that if a blue mirror is placed before the sun, it will appear that the mirror itself is the sun.

#### Declaration

Throughout the entire Qur'an, Surah Al-Fatiha alone contains no letter *fā'* (ف) of calamity. In all other surahs, this letter appears. Among the oppressive letters, the highest is *bā'* (ب), and the lowest syllable is *fā'* (ف).

#### Non-Recurring and Recurring

The seven intellects of the spiritual world are non-recurring (*al-ʿUqūl al-Qadīmah*), while discursive reason (*al-ʿAql al-Nāṭiq*) is recurring in comparison to them. Points recur seven after seven, and shall manifest in seventy in each period. Similarly, the time of prayer surpasses prayer itself. We cannot pray without waiting for its appointed time; waiting itself precedes and supersedes the waiting. For this reason, the *Ḥujjat* (the proofs of the Imam) are superior to the established Imams, for all the Imams await them, and even the *Qā'im* (the Final Imam) awaits. The Imams are non-recurring, and the *Ḥujjat* are recurring in contrast to them.

We recite Surah Al-Fatiha (which is non-recurring) in each *rakʿah* (unit of prayer) and change the subsequent Surah (which is recurring). Surah Al-Fātiḥah signifies the inner *daʿwa* (the summons/invitation), while the other verses signify the outer *daʿwa*. This means that the inner invitation has remained constant and shall remain so from the bearer of the spirit to the established Imams and the *Qā'im*. There is variation in the outward invitation—the styles shift according to times, temperaments, and intellects—yet the origin remains eternally the same.

## The Esoteric Interpretation (*Taʾwīl*) of Surah Al-Fatiha

### *Bismillāh-ar-Raḥmān-ar-Raḥīm*

This verse alludes to the world of innovation (*al-ibdāʿ*), wherein the Creator brought forth all creation from the very beginning of the universe.

### *Al-Ḥamdulillāh Rabb al-ʿĀlamīn*

⟪ar:'الحمد'⟫ Praise belongs to Him—the First Intellect (*al-ʿAql al-Awwal*, the First Intellect)—by whose praise all the worlds (the Kingdom, the Soul, the Intellect, the Physical realm) have been established. Their Lord is He by whom all these worlds stand and to whom they remain bound. Through Him they perpetuate themselves, and all strive toward the perfection of their authentication and return to their ⟪ar:مَبدَع⟫ Originator.

### *Al-Raḥmān Al-Raḥīm*

Although these are two words, their substance and origin are one and the same. Both derive from ⟪ar:'رحم'⟫. Yet ⟪ar:'رحمن'⟫ is reserved exclusively for Allah, while ⟪ar:'رحیم'⟫ shares in creation.[^cite-4]

### *Mālik Yawm ad-Dīn*

He is the Lord of the Day of Recompense (⟪ar:یوم الجزا⟫), wherein the final correlation reveals the first. The cause begins as the Absolute Cause, and the final state shows itself as the Absolute—a perfect return to origin.

When the discourse reached this point, all creators and creatures confessed with a single voice:

### *Iyyāk Naʿbud wa Iyyāk Nastaʿīn*

This is because the authentication and relationship of all beings rest with the First Existence, and from this primordial existence flows the life of all.

### *Ihdnā as-Sirāt al-Mustaqīm*

That is, every lower limit prays to be elevated beyond itself, for through this elevation lies its life, survival, and sustenance.

### *Sirāt al-Ladhīn Anʿamta ʿAlayhim Ghir al-Mughdūb ʿAlayhim wa al-Dāllīn*

⟪ar:انعمت علیھم⟫ This refers to the blessing bestowed by Allah upon His special servants—the First *Masīḥ* (Messiah) and the Second *Masīḥ*—as He says:

> ⟪quran 43:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا عَبْدٌ أَنْعَمْنَا عَلَيْهِ وَجَعَلْنَهُ مَثَلًۭا لِّبَنِىٓ إِسْرَٓءِيلَ ‎⟫
> *He was no more than a slave upon whom We bestowed favor, and We made him an example to the Children of Israel.*
> — **Quran 43:59**

⟪ar:غیر المغضوب علیھم⟫ refers to the Jews in general and specifically to the Jews (*Banū Isrāʾīl*) within the Muslim community. *Wa-lā al-Dāllīn* (and not the misguided) refers to Christians in general and specifically to the Christians among those who profess Islam.[^cite-5]

## The Seven Opposites of the Accents (*Alḥān*) in Surah Al-Fatiha

The oppressive syllables refer to the letter-forms (*rasm*) of the alphabet, which are the pronunciations of the lower veil (*al-ḥijāb al-dānī*). The souls of these people are oppressed, and their spirits are sinners devoid of the light of divine support (*anwār al-wilāyah*). Their example is a dark house with no lamp kindled within it. These are the people from whom the light of support has been withdrawn, left to wander in darkness. The Qur'an addresses them:

> — *Image text (from image 003; citation unverified)*
> (image preserved at ../images/003.png)

That is, until now these people dwelt among the boundaries of the *daʿwa* (the summons), yet through their misdeeds, Allah removed the light of support from them. For example, Dāwūd ibn Qutb stood within the limits of Sayyidnā Dāwūd ibn al-Ajab Shāh, but through his transgression, the light of support was withdrawn from him, and he entered the ranks of the dark-heavy letters.

These seven dark-heavy letters have not been able to enter Surah Al-Fatiha proper (the inner invitation), yet they have entered the accents—the tones of this great Surah (the acoustic qualities of the inner invitation).

#### The Seven Dimensions of the Accents (*Alḥān*)

Before reciting the Qur'ān (the Word of Allah), Allah says:

> — *Image text (from image 004; citation unverified)*
> (image preserved at ../images/004.png)

These seven dimensions enter through the accents and divert people from the inner to the outer, preventing them from reaching the inward truth, which is the station of the Muslims. We journey from the outward to the inward.

An analogy: if an elder gives a person a banana and that person locks it in a safe without eating it, we would call him foolish. The elder gave the banana so he would peel it and eat the fruit within. The Holy Prophet gave the Shariah (*divine law*) to the people so they would strip away its outer shell and grasp the inner secrets behind it. Yet these Muslims became trapped within the shells. The purpose was to travel from the outward Qur'an (Muḥammad) to the inner Qur'an (ʿAlī).

These additions entered the tones of the inner invitation. Therefore, the *duʿāt* (those who summon others to the faith) must speak with great care, lest their manner of speech mislead the one before them or cause distortion in their condition. Let them not speak what involves their own selfish desires. In other words, let the devil not enter the accents. For this reason, it is said that on the Day of Resurrection, Allah will say to the *Muftīn* (those teachers who embellish their teaching with personal desire): "Now break your carved idols!"—meaning: you have shattered the idols you placed in the minds of your students through your personal desires, though your deeds appeared good. Yet your error brought retribution upon them and upon their followers. Such is the danger with Surah Al-Fatiha; the inclusion of desire in its recitation is like the inclusion of desire in the tone of the inner invitation. For this reason it was said that many prayers destroyed their people, because there were no true explanations in the words. This is why we always supplicate Allah to grant us the gift of speech!

In the phrase "Lā ilāha illā-Allāh" (There is no god but Allah), there is first negation and then affirmation. We cannot move toward the Name of Allah until all other deities are negated through "Lā ilāha illā-Allāh." Similarly, on the Day of Ghadīr, when the Messenger of Allah declared: "Man Mullā, fahza ʿAlī Mullā" (Of whomever I am the Lord, ʿAlī is the Lord), all other limits were suspended. This utterance denies all other limits and affirms only ʿAlī's authority. The Prophet declared: "There is no *ḥadd* (boundary/limit) in the realm of the *daʿwa* capable of attaining true glory except for the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib."

#### Obstinacy Always Arises from the *Daʿwa*

When a person falls ill—say, from fever—it first affects the face. Similarly, when disorder arises in the *daʿwa*, it manifests in the limits of the Supreme Command, which are the face of the invitation. The lower limits do not cause such errors.

Abū Bakr and ʿUmar entered the presence of the Holy Prophet through their previous services, yet later they fell away. The same shall occur with the *Qā'im*: there will be a disturbance in their boundaries. These additions are, in truth, the additions of the lower veil (*al-ḥijāb al-dānī*)—not the true self—of those who obstruct. The *ḥijāb* (veils) are those who rise above the limits. For example, Firat ibn Aḥnāf became the *ḥijāb* of Mawlānā Ismāʿīl ibn Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq.[^cite-6]

#### Statement

It is narrated that Dāwūd ibn Qutb became corrupted when he witnessed the amount of *zakāt* (alms). Sayyidnā Dāwūd ibn al-Ajab Shāh had fixed his sustenance at five rupees—at a time when a goat cost one rupee. Yet the amount of *zakāt* totaled eighty thousand rupees. This disparity led to his spiritual collapse.

The stubbornness of the *daʿwa* in its self and form (an evil conception) is cruel—such stubbornness rises against the invitation of the saints of Allah and becomes obstinate, spoiling the invitation.

#### Facts

Obstinacy is always the *ḥijāb*, not the obstinacy itself. All prophets and imams have been killed, yet there shall be no resistance to the *Qā'im*. This is because the *Qā'im* ʿAlī stands in the position of *Dhikr al-Salām* (the Remembrance of the One Who grants Peace)—Allah Himself—and it is impossible to oppose Allah. Satan does not oppose Allah nor stands against Him (for he cannot), but rather against His servants, the *ḥijāb* Imams.

(From the *Lessons of Tuḥfat al-Ṣāʾifī*, on the letters of *al-Muʿjam al-Nawādir*; attributed to Sayyidunā Ḥasan Khān ibn ʿAbd al-Malik)

## Surah Al-Fatiha as the Cause of Healing

Surah Al-Fatiha became a cure for all spiritual ailments because the oppressive letters—which are the negative attributes—have been excluded from it. Since we have wearied of multiplication, this Surah Al-Fatiha shall now carry us from this lower world to the realm of the High (*ʿAlawi*). Any gathering involving multiplication shall never be elevated.

Therefore, if the believer's death is rooted in the concept of abandonment and detachment from all attachments, this pure inner invitation shall convey the form of that believer to the Imam's sanctuary, and then on the Day of Judgment, all these forms shall enter the spiritual realm through the *Sulṭān* (divine authority).

The perfection of the self is in the noble *Daʿwa*! The invitation becomes the cause of the development of a person's soul precisely because it contains no additions. The Qur'ān says:

> — *Image text (from image 010; citation unverified)*

## The Seat of the *Daʿwa* in Surah Al-Fatiha

Within Surah Al-Fatiha has been placed the entire seat of the *Daʿwa*—from the intellect of the spiritual world down to the intellect of the physical world. The spiritual movements of all these ranks are present in this Surah. For this reason, this Surah was made obligatory in prayer. The *daʿwa* cannot be established without Surah Al-Fatiha.

We are confined within a circle of seven days of the week and cannot escape any one of them. The day we exit this circle is the day of our death. Similarly, we are locked within the circles of these seven intellects (*al-ʿuqūl al-sabʿah*). Should we escape them, we shall experience spiritual death.

*Bismillāh-ar-Raḥmān-ar-Raḥīm* is sometimes included in the surahs and sometimes excluded. For instance, Surah Al-Fatiha includes *Bismillāh-ar-Raḥmān-ar-Raḥīm* as its first verse, while in all other surahs it does not become the first verse. Similarly, sometimes we include the Imam in the enumeration of the *ḥudūd* (limits/ranks), and sometimes we exclude him. When we say there are ten degrees from *Mustajīb* to the Imam, we include the Imam. When we say the Imam is supreme and the rest are subordinate, we exclude him. The same applies to the First Intellect (*al-ʿAql al-Awwal*).

## The Distinction Between Creator (*Khāliq*) and Lord (*Rabb*)

When Allah Almighty began His Word, He began with "Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds." Some might suppose it more fitting to say: "Praise be to Allah, the Creator of the Worlds." Yet Allah praised Himself through His Lordship, not through His creation. The rank of lordship—*rabb* (training and sustenance)—is higher than creation, just as secondary perfection (*kamāl thānī*, secondary perfection) is greater than primary perfection (*kamāl awwal*, primary perfection). Similarly, it is nobler for parents to raise a child than merely to beget one. This is why the mother holds great virtue—because she nurtures and trains.

| Creator (*Khāliq*) | Lord (*Rabb*) |
| --- | --- |
| Primary Perfection | Secondary Perfection |
| Begetting | Training/Sustenance |

In this verse, praise is given precedence—Allah did not say "Praise be to Allah," but rather "All praise belongs to Allah" (*Al-Ḥamdulillāh*). The Imam is the Proof of Allah (*Ḥujjat Allah*), and praise is due to Him whose son is the Imam. The true meaning of an Imam can only be followed if we believe in his successor. The Messenger of Allah can only be followed if his successor ʿAlī is followed.[^cite-7]

| *Al-Ḥamd* (Praise) | *Allah* (God) |
| --- | --- |
| The Proof/Son of the Imam | The Imam |

## The Point of *Bā* and *Nūn* in Surah Al-Fatiha

The Surah begins with the point of *Bā* and ends with the point of *Nūn*. The point of *Bā* is the Commander of the Faithful (*Amīr al-Muʾminīn*), and the letter *Bā* itself is the Lord of the Universe. (*Bā* is distinct, and its point is different.) That is, if the *wilāyah* of the Commander of the Faithful is removed, then the religion of the Prophet shall have no meaning whatsoever—it becomes obsolete.

The *Nūn* of Surah Al-Fatiha (the *Nūn* of the misguided) refers to the Jews of the Ummah of Muḥammad, just as the Christians of the Ummah of Muḥammad. So the last person who shall be gathered in his feast is the spiritual devil, and that is the point of *Nūn*. That is, the point of the devil shall come within the scope of the day of judgment.

This symbol is placed within the *Nūn* of Surah Al-Fatiha. The point within *Nūn* is the spiritual *Iblīs*, that is, the spiritual devil shall also come to your feast.
