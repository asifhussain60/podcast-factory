## The Holy Quran

The word "Qur'an" (Arabic: ⟪ar:'قرآن'⟫) derives from the verbal root meaning "to read aloud" or "to recite." Since its revelation, the Qur'an has been recited continuously; its recitation echoes throughout the world at every moment of the day and will continue until the Day of Judgment. The Qur'an is that book which was revealed by divine word—brought down in Arabic by Gabriel Amin to the blessed heart of the Prophet Muhammad Mustafa (peace and blessings of God be upon him). God Himself testifies to this:

> ⟪quran 26:193-195⟫
> ⟪ar:‏نَزَلَ بِهِ ٱلرُّوحُ ٱلْأَمِينُ ‎ ‏عَلَىٰ قَلْبِكَ لِتَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنذِرِينَ ‎ ‏بِلِسَانٍ عَرَبِىٍّۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *Which the trustworthy Ruh [Jibrael (Gabriel)] has brought down; Upon your heart (O Muhammad PBUH) that you may be (one) of the warners, In the plain Arabic language.*

The descent of the revelation is thus explicitly tied to the Prophet's heart, the organ through which divine knowledge enters the human vessel. When we examine the Qur'an—the three-lettered root—we perceive another layer of meaning:

| **S** | **T** | **A** |
|-------|-------|-------|
| **Heart** | **Messenger** | **God** |

The Qur'an, understood in its deepest sense, refers to the heart of God's Messenger, for it was upon that heart that the divine verses descended. The letter Nun (ن) of the Qur'an carries an additional significance; God Himself invokes it in Sūrat al-Nūn, saying:

> ⟪quran 68:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏نٓ ۚ وَٱلْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Nun. [These letters (Nun, etc.) are one of the miracles of the Quran, and none but Allah (Alone) knows their meanings]. By the pen and what the (angels) write (in the Records of men).*

The Qur'an is precisely the unity of its words and meanings; it is inseparable from the Arabic in which it was revealed. To translate it into any other tongue is to render it something other than the Qur'an. This principle is neither arbitrary nor merely custodial. Rather, it reflects the sacred bond between divine utterance and human reception—hence the obligation to recite the Arabic verses themselves in prayer; any rendering in another language cannot fulfill this requirement.[^cite-1]

The Qur'an is also called al-Furqān (the Criterion, that which distinguishes right from wrong). God says:

> ⟪quran 25:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏تَبَارَكَ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ ٱلْفُرْقَانَ عَلَىٰ عَبْدِهِۦ لِيَكُونَ لِلْعَلَمِينَ نَذِيرًا ‎⟫
> *Blessed be He Who sent down the criterion (of right and wrong, i.e. this Quran) to His slave (Muhammad PBUH) that he may be a warner to the 'Alamîn (mankind and jinns).*

## The Descent of the Qur'an

Revelation (nuzūl) signifies the unveiling and descent of divine truth. Each verse was revealed in response to specific circumstances and for particular purposes. History records that when the Prophet (peace and blessings of God be upon him) was unable to pray the Isha or night vigil prayers for one or two nights due to some difficulty, a Jewish woman taunted him, saying, "It appears your Lord has abandoned you." In that moment, the verses of Sūrat al-Ḍuhā were revealed to console and strengthen his heart:

> ⟪quran 93:1-3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلضُّحَىٰ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا سَجَىٰ ‎ ‏مَا وَدَّعَكَ رَبُّكَ وَمَا قَلَىٰ ‎⟫
> *By the forenoon (after sun-rise); And by the night when it is still (or darkens); Your Lord (O Muhammad (Peace be upon him)) has neither forsaken you nor hated you.*

These verses embody a glorious wisdom: they were revealed to address a specific need, demonstrating the responsive and living nature of revelation. Over the course of the Qur'an's descent, the verses acquired diverse designations according to the circumstances, places, and times of their revelation.[^cite-2] The revelation began in the Cave of Hira in Mecca and concluded on the plain of Arafat (Jabal Rahmah). The verses were categorized thus:

| **Category** | **Description** |
|---|---|
| **Traveling verses** | Those revealed while the Prophet was in motion |
| **Standing verses** | Those revealed while standing |
| **Daytime verses** | Revealed in daylight hours |
| **Nighttime verses** | Revealed during the night |
| **Summer verses** | Revealed in intense heat |
| **Winter verses** | Revealed in cold |
| **Reclining verses** | Revealed while resting on a bed |
| **Sleeping verses** | Revealed during sleep (as in Sūrat al-Kawthar) |
| **Celestial verses** | Revealed during the Night Journey (Isrā') |
| **Meccan verses** | Revealed in Mecca |
| **Medinan verses** | Revealed in Medina |

### The Duration of Qur'anic Revelation

God declares that the Qur'an was sent down in the month of Ramadan:

> ⟪quran 2:185⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهْرُ رَمَضَانَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أُنزِلَ فِيهِ ٱلْقُرْآنُ هُدًۭى لِّلنَّاسِ وَبَيِّنَتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْهُدَىٰ وَٱلْفُرْقَانِ ۚ فَمَن شَهِدَ مِنكُمُ ٱلشَّهْرَ فَلْيَصُمْهُ ۖ وَمَن كَانَ مَرِيضًا أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍۢ فَعِدَّةٌۭ مِّنْ أَيَّامٍ أُخَرَ ۗ يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ بِكُمُ ٱلْيُسْرَ وَلَا يُرِيدُ بِكُمُ ٱلْعُسْرَ وَلِتُكْمِلُوا۟ ٱلْعِدَّةَ وَلِتُكَبِّرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ عَلَىٰ مَا هَدَىٰكُمْ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *The month of Ramadan in which was revealed the Quran, a guidance for mankind and clear proofs for the guidance and the criterion (between right and wrong). So whoever of you sights (the crescent on the first night of) the month (of Ramadan i.e. is present at his home), he must observe Saum (fasts) that month, and whoever is ill or on a journey, the same number [of days which one did not observe Saum (fasts) must be made up] from other days. Allah intends for you ease, and He does not want to make things difficult for you. (He wants that you) must complete the same number (of days), and that you must magnify Allah [i.e. to say Takbîr (Allahu-Akbar; Allah is the Most Great) on seeing the crescent of the months of Ramadan and Shawwal] for having guided you so that you may be grateful to Him.*

He further says:

> ⟪quran 97:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَهُ فِى لَيْلَةِ ٱلْقَدْرِ ‎⟫
> *Verily! We have sent it (this Quran) down in the night of Al-Qadr (Decree)*

Yet the Qur'an was not revealed in its entirety within thirty days. Rather, it descended gradually upon the Prophet's pure heart over twenty-three years. The deeper truth is this: the Pen (al-Qalam), understood as the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal), inscribed the entire meaning of the Qur'an upon the Preserved Tablet (al-Lawḥ al-Maḥfūẓ) during the Night of Decree (Laylat al-Qadr) in the month of Ramadan—that is, on the twenty-third night of the month.[^cite-3] Then God revealed these meanings gradually to the pure heart of His Messenger (peace and blessings of God be upon him), and he translated them continuously into articulate speech throughout those twenty-three years.

The primary concern of the Qur'an is humanity itself. All its teachings aim toward the welfare of the human being—in this world and in the hereafter.
