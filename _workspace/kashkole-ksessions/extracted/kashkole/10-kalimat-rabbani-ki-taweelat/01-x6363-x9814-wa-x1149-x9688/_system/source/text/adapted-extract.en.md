# The Holy Quran and Al-Furqan Al-Hamid

## The Noble Qur'an

The term "Qur'an" ⟪ar:'قرآن'⟫ derives from a verbal root denoting the act of recitation—literally, to read aloud. From the moment of its revelation until the present day, it has been recited continuously; its recitation echoes across the world without cessation and shall endure until the Day of Judgment. The Qur'an refers to a book conveyed through divine revelation, revealed in Arabic by Gabriel the Trustworthy (Jibrāʾīl al-Amīn) upon the blessed heart of the Prophet Muhammad Mustafa—may peace and blessings be upon him. The Almighty testifies to this in these words:

> ⟪quran 26:193-195⟫
> ⟪ar:‏نَزَلَ بِهِ ٱلرُّوحُ ٱلْأَمِينُ ‎ ‏عَلَىٰ قَلْبِكَ لِتَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنذِرِينَ ‎ ‏بِلِسَانٍ عَرَبِىٍّۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *Which the trustworthy Ruh [Jibrael (Gabriel)] has brought down; Upon your heart (O Muhammad PBUH) that you may be (one) of the warners, In the plain Arabic language.*

The term "Qur'an" admits of another interpretation, one grounded in the letters themselves. When the three essential letters—Q, R, and N—are taken in succession, they correspond to the heart (qalb), the Messenger (rasūl), and Allah (the Almighty). In this reading, the Qur'an truly designates the heart of the Messenger of Allah, for it was upon his heart that the Qur'anic verses descended.[^cite-1] The letter Nūn itself carries particular significance; Allah swears an oath by it in Surah al-Qalam, declaring:

> ⟪quran 68:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏نٓ ۚ وَٱلْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Nun. [These letters (Nun, etc.) are one of the miracles of the Quran, and none but Allah (Alone) knows their meanings]. By the pen and what the (angels) write (in the Records of men).*

The Qur'an, understood as the unified whole of words and meanings, is constituted by revelation in Arabic alone. Its essence cannot be transferred into another tongue; translation renders it no longer the Qur'an in the technical and juridical sense. This is why the recitation of the Qur'an's Arabic verses has been made obligatory in the ritual prayer (ṣalāt)—should one substitute a rendering in any other language, the prayer itself becomes invalid.[^cite-2] The Qur'an is also called al-Furqān (the Criterion) ⟪ar:'فرقان'⟫, a name denoting that which distinguishes right from wrong. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 25:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏تَبَارَكَ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ ٱلْفُرْقَانَ عَلَىٰ عَبْدِهِۦ لِيَكُونَ لِلْعَلَمِينَ نَذِيرًا ‎⟫
> *Blessed be He Who sent down the criterion (of right and wrong, i.e. this Quran) to His slave (Muhammad PBUH) that he may be a warner to the 'Alamîn (mankind and jinns).*

## The Descent of the Qur'an

Revelation (tanzīl) signifies the unveiling and descent of the divine word. Each verse carries within it an occasion of revelation—the particular circumstance that occasioned its descent. The Prophet—may peace and blessings be upon him—once found himself unable to perform the night prayers (ʿIshāʾ and Tahajjud) for one or two nights owing to some difficulty. A Jewish woman seized upon this moment to taunt him, saying, "It appears your Lord has abandoned you." In response, Allah revealed the verses of Surah al-Duhā:

> ⟪quran 93:1-3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلضُّحَىٰ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا سَجَىٰ ‎ ‏مَا وَدَّعَكَ رَبُّكَ وَمَا قَلَىٰ ‎⟫
> *By the forenoon (after sun-rise); And by the night when it is still (or darkens); Your Lord (O Muhammad (Peace be upon him)) has neither forsaken you nor hated you.*

These verses possess a profound significance: they were revealed to address a specific need, to provide solace and reassurance in the face of doubt. Indeed, the Qur'anic verses have been assigned various designations according to their place, circumstances, and time of revelation. The initial stage of the Qur'an's descent occurred in the Cave of Hirā' in Mecca; its final destination was Mount Arafat (Jabal Raḥma), the plain of standing on the Day of Assembly.[^cite-3]

Verses have been further classified according to their conditions of revelation:

| Classification | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Traveling | Revealed while the Prophet was in a state of travel |
| Standing | Revealed while the Prophet stood upright |
| Daytime | Revealed during the day |
| Nocturnal | Revealed during the night |
| Summer | Revealed in intense heat |
| Winter | Revealed during the cold season |
| Waking | Revealed while the Prophet lay awake upon his bed |
| Sleeping | Revealed during sleep (as in Surah al-Kawthar) |
| Celestial | Revealed during the Night Journey (al-Isrāʾ) to the heavens |
| Meccan | Revealed in Mecca |
| Medinan | Revealed in Medina |

### Duration of the Qur'an's Revelation

The Almighty states:

> ⟪quran 2:185⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهْرُ رَمَضَانَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أُنزِلَ فِيهِ ٱلْقُرْآنُ هُدًۭى لِّلنَّاسِ وَبَيِّنَتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْهُدَىٰ وَٱلْفُرْقَانِ ۚ فَمَن شَهِدَ مِنكُمُ ٱلشَّهْرَ فَلْيَصُمْهُ ۖ وَمَن كَانَ مَرِيضًا أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍۢ فَعِدَّةٌۭ مِّنْ أَيَّامٍ أُخَرَ ۗ يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ بِكُمُ ٱلْيُسْرَ وَلَا يُرِيدُ بِكُمُ ٱلْعُسْرَ وَلِتُكْمِلُوا۟ ٱلْعِدَّةَ وَلِتُكَبِّرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ عَلَىٰ مَا هَدَىٰكُمْ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *The month of Ramadan in which was revealed the Quran, a guidance for mankind and clear proofs for the guidance and the criterion (between right and wrong). So whoever of you sights (the crescent on the first night of) the month (of Ramadan i.e. is present at his home), he must observe Saum (fasts) that month, and whoever is ill or on a journey, the same number [of days which one did not observe Saum (fasts) must be made up] from other days. Allah intends for you ease, and He does not want to make things difficult for you. (He wants that you) must complete the same number (of days), and that you must magnify Allah [i.e. to say Takbîr (Allahu-Akbar; Allah is the Most Great) on seeing the crescent of the months of Ramadan and Shawwal] for having guided you so that you may be grateful to Him.*

Allah further clarifies that the Qur'an was sent down during Laylat al-Qadr ⟪ar:'لیلۃ القدر'⟫ (the Night of Decree):

> ⟪quran 97:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَهُ فِى لَيْلَةِ ٱلْقَدْرِ ‎⟫
> *Verily! We have sent it (this Quran) down in the night of Al-Qadr (Decree)*

History testifies, however, that the entire Qur'an was not revealed within the thirty days of Ramadan alone. Rather, it descended gradually and incrementally upon the pure heart of the Prophet over a period of twenty-three years. The reality is this: the Pen (al-Qalam)—which in our esoteric understanding corresponds to the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal)—inscribed upon the Preserved Tablet (al-Lawḥ al-Maḥfūẓ) the totality of the Qur'an's meaning on that blessed Night of Decree in Ramadan, which is the twenty-third night of that sacred month.[^cite-4] Thereafter, Allah revealed these meanings gradually to the sanctified heart of the Messenger of Allah—may peace and blessings be upon him—throughout those twenty-three years, and the Prophet, in turn, continued to render them into articulate speech and Arabic expression.

The cardinal theme of the Qur'an is humanity itself. Every statement within it—whether prescriptive, narrative, or exhortative—is oriented toward the welfare and dignity of the human being in both this world and the world to come.
