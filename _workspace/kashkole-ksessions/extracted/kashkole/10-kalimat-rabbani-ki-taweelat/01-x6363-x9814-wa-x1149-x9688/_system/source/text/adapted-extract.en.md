# The Holy Qurʾān and al-Furqān al-Ḥamīd

## The Noble Qurʾān

The word ⟪ar:’قرآن‘⟫ is derived, by the morphological pattern (wazn) of its triliteral root q-r-ʾ, from a verbal stem whose actions and attributes all converge upon the single sense of *reciting aloud*. From the moment of its first descent, its recitation has continued without interruption; somewhere upon the earth it is being recited at every hour of the day and night, and so it shall remain until the Day of Judgement.

The term "Qurʾān" denotes a book composed by divine revelation, brought down in Arabic by Jibrīl al-Amīn (Gabriel the Trustworthy) to the blessed heart of the Prophet Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (peace and blessings be upon him), as God Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 26:193-195⟫
> ⟪ar:‏نَزَلَ بِهِ ٱلرُّوحُ ٱلْأَمِينُ ‎ ‏عَلَىٰ قَلْبِكَ لِتَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنذِرِينَ ‎ ‏بِلِسَانٍ عَرَبِىٍّۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *The Trustworthy Spirit (al-Rūḥ al-Amīn) has brought it down upon thy heart, that thou mayest be among the warners, in a clear Arabic tongue.*
> — **Qurʾān 26:193–195 (inline widget)**

A further sense may be drawn from the three letters of the root q-r-ʾ themselves:

|  |
|  |

| S | T | A |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Heart | Rasoul | Allah |

That is to say: in its inner truth, the Qurʾān designates the very heart of the Messenger of God, for it was upon his heart that its verses descended. The *nūn* of "Qurʾān" is a morphological addition; God Himself swears by this letter at the opening of Sūrat al-Qalam:

> ⟪quran 68:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏نٓ ۚ وَٱلْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Nūn. By the Pen, and by that which they inscribe.*
> — **Qurʾān 68:1 (inline widget)**

The Qurʾān is the name proper to the union of its words and their meanings. Whatever has been sent down in Arabic, in that Arabic alone may bear the name Qurʾān; no translation into another tongue may rightly carry the title. It is for this reason that the recitation of the Qurʾān's Arabic verses has been made obligatory in prayer, and the prayer of one who substitutes for them a rendering in any other language is not valid.

The same Book is also called al-Furqān ⟪ar:’فرقان‘⟫ — that which separates and discriminates between truth and falsehood — as He says:

> ⟪quran 25:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏تَبَارَكَ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ ٱلْفُرْقَانَ عَلَىٰ عَبْدِهِۦ لِيَكُونَ لِلْعَلَمِينَ نَذِيرًا ‎⟫
> *Blessed is He who sent down the Criterion (al-Furqān) upon His servant, that he might be a warner unto the worlds.*
> — **Qurʾān 25:1 (inline widget)**

In the taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis) of the duʿāt (the Summoners), this doubling of names is no mere ornament: the Qurʾān is the unifying speech of God in which inner and outer meaning cohere, while al-Furqān is that same speech as it cuts cleanly between guidance and error, the believer and the dissenter, the apparent and the hidden.[^cite-1]

## The Descent of the Qurʾān

The word *nuzūl* signifies the act of being sent down; correspondingly, the *nuzūl* of any given verse refers to the immediate occasion that prompted its revelation. It is recounted, for instance, that the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) was unable, owing to some constraint, to perform the ʿishāʾ and tahajjud prayers for one or two nights, and that a Jewish woman taunted him with the words, "It seems your Lord has forsaken you." In response, the opening verses of Sūrat al-Ḍuḥā were sent down to him:

> ⟪quran 93:1-3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلضُّحَىٰ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا سَجَىٰ ‎ ‏مَا وَدَّعَكَ رَبُّكَ وَمَا قَلَىٰ ‎⟫
> *By the morning brightness, and by the night when it grows still: thy Lord has not forsaken thee, neither does He hate thee.*
> — **Qurʾān 93:1–3 (inline widget)**

Each such revelation came forth to elucidate a particular purpose. Beyond this, the verses themselves have been classified by where, when, and under what circumstances they descended. The first stage of the Qurʾān's descent was the cave of Ḥirāʾ at Mecca; its final station was Jabal al-Raḥma, upon the plain of ʿArafāt.

### The Duration of the Qurʾān's Descent

God Most High declares that the Qurʾān was sent down in the month of Ramaḍān:

> ⟪quran 2:185⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهْرُ رَمَضَانَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أُنزِلَ فِيهِ ٱلْقُرْآنُ هُدًۭى لِّلنَّاسِ وَبَيِّنَتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْهُدَىٰ وَٱلْفُرْقَانِ ۚ فَمَن شَهِدَ مِنكُمُ ٱلشَّهْرَ فَلْيَصُمْهُ ۖ وَمَن كَانَ مَرِيضًا أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍۢ فَعِدَّةٌۭ مِّنْ أَيَّامٍ أُخَرَ ۗ يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ بِكُمُ ٱلْيُسْرَ وَلَا يُرِيدُ بِكُمُ ٱلْعُسْرَ وَلِتُكْمِلُوا۟ ٱلْعِدَّةَ وَلِتُكَبِّرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ عَلَىٰ مَا هَدَىٰكُمْ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *The month of Ramaḍān, in which the Qurʾān was sent down — a guidance for mankind, and clear proofs of guidance, and the Criterion. Let him among you who is present in the month fast it; and whoever is sick or upon a journey, then a number of other days. God desires ease for you and does not desire hardship, that you may complete the count and magnify God for that He has guided you, and that you may be grateful.*
> — **Qurʾān 2:185 (inline widget)**

He further specifies that it was sent down during ⟪ar:’لیلۃ القدر‘⟫, the Night of Power, which falls within the month of Ramaḍān:

> ⟪quran 97:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَهُ فِى لَيْلَةِ ٱلْقَدْرِ ‎⟫
> *Verily, We sent it down on the Night of Power.*
> — **Qurʾān 97:1 (inline widget)**

History, however, records that the Qurʾān was not delivered within the thirty days of any single Ramaḍān; it descended gradually upon the heart of the Prophet over the span of twenty-three years. The reconciliation lies in the doctrine that, on the Night of Power — the twenty-third night of that month — the Pen (al-Qalam), which is al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect), inscribed the entire meaning of the Qurʾān upon the Preserved Tablet (al-Lawḥ al-Maḥfūẓ).[^cite-2] Thereafter, those same meanings were unfolded by God, little by little, upon the purified heart of His Messenger across the twenty-three years of his prophetic mission, and he in turn translated them into the words of clear Arabic speech.[^cite-3]

The central subject of the Qurʾān, throughout its descent and unfolding, is the human being. Every one of its statements is directed toward the welfare of humankind, in this world and in the world to come.

[^cite-1]: See Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān, *Asās al-Taʾwīl*, where the Qurʾān–Furqān pairing is treated as a single revelation bearing two functions — the gathering speech (qurʾān) and the discriminating speech (furqān) — corresponding to the unifying and distinguishing dimensions of prophetic teaching.

[^cite-2]: Compare al-Kirmānī, *Rāḥat al-ʿAql*, where the Pen of the Qurʾānic oath is identified with al-ʿAql al-Awwal, the first emanation through which all subsequent realities are inscribed upon the cosmic order.

[^cite-3]: See Nāṣir-i Khusraw, *Jāmiʿ al-Ḥikmatayn*, on the relation between the Pen and the Preserved Tablet within the hierarchy of emanation, by which the meanings of revelation pass from the First Intellect into the receptive soul before issuing in prophetic speech.
