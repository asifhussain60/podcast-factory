# Interpretation of Quranic Verses

## The Book Without Doubt

Allah Most High declares:

> ⟪quran 2:1-2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏الٓمٓ ‎ ‏ذَلِكَ ٱلْكِتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًۭى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *Alif-Lam-Mîm. [These letters are one of the miracles of the Quran and none but Allah (Alone) knows their meanings]. That book, whereof there is no doubt, a guidance to those who are Al-Muttaqun [the pious and righteous persons who fear Allah much (abstain from all kinds of sins and evil deeds which He has forbidden) and love Allah much (perform all kinds of good deeds which He has ordained)].*

The opening letters ⟪ar:'الٓمٓ'⟫ constitute far more than ordinary orthographic markers; they represent a profound category of divine speech. ⟪ar:'الف'⟫ stands for Allah Himself, the One and Only. ⟪ar:'لام'⟫ designates ⟪ar:جبرئیل امین⟫ (Gabriel the Trustworthy, *jibrāʾīl al-amīn*), the bearer of revelation. ⟪ar:'میم'⟫ signifies ⟪ar:محمد علیہ السلام⟫ (Muhammad, peace be upon him, *muḥammad ʿalayhi al-salām*), the seal of the prophets. By swearing through these three exalted beings, the Almighty declares: ⟪ar:ذلک الکتاب لا ریب فیہ⟫ "There is no doubt whatsoever concerning the truth and majesty of this Qur'an, and it is a guidance for the God-fearing."

The choice of the demonstrative ⟪ar:'ذلک'⟫ (that, referring to the distant) rather than ⟪ar:'ھذہ'⟫ (this, referring to the near) invites careful reflection. If the Qur'an were directly at hand before the reader, the proximal form ⟪ar:'ھذا'⟫ would be more natural. Yet Allah employs ⟪ar:ذلک⟫—the distal pronoun—suggesting that the passage refers not to the outward codex alone, but to another book entirely, one of transcendent significance.[^cite-1]

This raises a vital question: to whom does Allah address this solemn oath? Should the disbelievers and polytheists hear it, they remain unmoved, neither bowing before its majesty nor embracing faith. Their hearing of such an oath, sworn by the Messenger and Gabriel in whom they do not believe, yields no spiritual fruit. And if the verse addresses the faithful, who have already confirmed the Qur'an's truth, what purpose does such assurance serve?

The Ahl al-Bayt (the People of the Household of the Prophet, *ahl al-bayt*) interpret this passage with profound insight: the "book" encompasses all letters, words, and meanings present in the heart of the author as intelligible content. The written Book expresses these meanings through words, while the Book of Allah comprises the totality of divine utterances describing God's purposes—hidden within creation and the ordering of worship. It declares the rewards prepared for the obedient and the punishment ordained for the transgressor, recounting events of the prophetic epochs and foretelling what shall occur until the Day of Judgment.

This Book manifests in two forms. One is ⟪ar:'صامت'⟫ (the mute, *ṣāmit*), comprising non-vocal letters inscribed upon paper. The other is ⟪ar:'ناطق'⟫ (the speaking, *nāṭiq*)—the very essence of the Messenger of Allah, who is the truthful and transparent interpreter of the Qur'an's meanings, resolving its ambiguous expressions. From this distinction arise the two categories that Allah reserves to Himself and to ⟪ar:'راسخون فی العلم'⟫ (those deeply rooted in knowledge, *al-rāskhūn fī al-ʿilm*):

> ⟪quran 3:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
>
> *It is He Who has sent down to you (Muhammad PBUH) the Book (this Quran). In it are Verses that are entirely clear, they are the foundations of the Book; and others not entirely clear. So as for those in whose hearts there is a deviation (from the truth) they follow that which is not entirely clear thereof, seeking Al-Fitnah (polytheism and trials, etc.), and seeking for its hidden meanings, but none knows its hidden meanings save Allah. And those who are firmly grounded in knowledge say: "We believe in it; the whole of it (clear and unclear Verses) are from our Lord." And none receive admonition except men of understanding.*

The mute Book encompasses the conditions of all three temporal dimensions—past, present, and future—addressing every issue that shall unfold until the Day of Judgment. So too does the Qur'an embrace all these realities. ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی علیہ السلام⟫ (the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī, *amīr al-muʾminīn ʿalī ʿalayhi al-salām*), in his inaugural sermon upon assuming the caliphate, proclaimed from the pulpit with utmost clarity ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫: "Ask me before you lose me! Question me concerning all that has passed and all that shall transpire until the Day of Judgment."[^cite-2] The Qur'an similarly declares:

> ⟪quran 6:38⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا مِن دَآبَّةٍۢ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَا طَٓئِرٍۢ يَطِيرُ بِجَنَاحَيْهِ إِلَّآ أُمَمٌ أَمْثَالُكُم ۚ مَّا فَرَّطْنَا فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ مِن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ ثُمَّ إِلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ يُحْشَرُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *There is not a moving (living) creature on earth, nor a bird that flies with its two wings, but are communities like you. We have neglected nothing in the Book, then unto their Lord they (all) shall be gathered.*

The knowledge revealed to Adam and through which the prophets were sent to all creation flows through ⟪ar:رسول اللہ خاتم النبین⟫ (the Messenger of Allah, Seal of the Prophets, *rasūl allāh khātim al-nabiyyīn*) and his pure descendants. Yet why does the verse employ ⟪ar:'ذالک'⟫—the distant form—if not to reference something beyond the apparent text? The Book of which there is no doubt in its greatness is the very essence of the Prophet himself. This declaration aims to establish the authority and guardianship of the Guardian (⟪ar:وصی رسول⟫, *waṣī rasūl*, the Successor of the Messenger) and to purify the hearts of the faithful from hesitation concerning ⟪ar:'امیر المومین علی ابن ابی طالب'⟫ (the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, *amīr al-muʾminīn ʿalī ibn abī ṭālib*).[^cite-3]

⟪ar:ھدی للمتقین⟫ The Qur'an—this synthesis of the mute Book and the speaking Book—becomes a guidance for ⟪ar:'متقین'⟫ (the God-fearing, *al-muttaqūn*), those who harbor no doubt concerning the guardianship of ʿAlī and permit no skepticism to arise. This Book is not a guidance for the doubters and hypocrites, whose inner corruption precludes its benefit.

## Those Who Believe in the Unseen and Establish Prayer

Allah proclaims in Sūrat al-Baqarah:

> ⟪quran 2:3-5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *Who believe in the Ghaib and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and spend out of what we have provided for them. And who believe in (the Quran and the Sunnah) which has been sent down (revealed) to you (Muhammad Peace be upon him) and in [the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel), etc.] which were sent down before you and they believe with certainty in the Hereafter. They are on (true) guidance from their Lord, and they are the successful.*

### Believers in the Unseen

The apparent meaning encompasses faith in realities hidden from sensory perception: ⟪ar:اللہ تعالی وحدہ لا شریک لہ⟫ (Allah Most High, alone without partner, *allāh taʿālā waḥdahu lā sharīk lahu*), His angels, Paradise and Hell, the Sirat (the bridge over perdition), ⟪ar:بعث من فی القبور⟫ (the resurrection of those in the graves, *baʿth man fī al-qubūr*), the reckoning, and all other realities which the Messenger disclosed. Though hidden from external sense, these truths are not absent from intellect, consciousness, and spiritual insight—they are present and substantiated. Should the Messenger have called to matters imperceptible to both sensation and rational mind, his invitation would lack validity. For Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 43:86⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا يَمْلِكُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ مِن دُونِهِ ٱلشَّفَعَةَ إِلَّا مَن شَهِدَ بِٱلْحَقِّ وَهُمْ يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *And those whom they invoke instead of Him have no power of intercession; except those who bear witness to the truth (i.e. believed in the Oneness of Allah, and obeyed His Orders), and they know (the facts about the Oneness of Allah).*

A statement may be true, yet if the witness is ignorant or unaware, testifying only by rote, his testimony is inadmissible. Similarly, belief in the unseen divorced from rational discernment becomes false, and the testimony of such ignorance carries no weight.

Consider the analogy of the self (nafs, *nnafs*) dwelling within the body, or meaning hidden within words. Every rational being apprehends that the body exists for the sake of the self, yet the self remains invisible; without the self, the body does not endure. Words likewise are fashioned to convey meaning; utterances devoid of inner significance are void. In this verse, the unseen refers to the taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis, *taʾwīl*) of the Qur'an—its concealed and implicit dimensions disclosed through its manifest words. These meanings pertain to intellect rather than sensation. For those intellectually blind, the Qur'an declares:

> ⟪quran 30:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَعْلَمُونَ ظَهِرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَهُمْ عَنِ ٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ غَفِلُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *They know only the outside appearance of the life of the world (i.e. the matters of their livelihood, like irrigating or sowing or reaping, etc.), and they are heedless of the Hereafter.*

They grasp only the external dimension of worldly existence and remain heedless of the Hereafter.

The knowledge of the Qur'an's interpretation encompasses diverse categories: the Hereafter and resurrection, the angelic realms, the hierarchy of spiritual and corporeal limits, the Divine Throne and its footstool, Paradise and Hell, the raising of the dead from their tombs. All these constitute the unseen through authentic monotheism (tawḥīd, *al-tawḥīd*)—a doctrine free from both anthropomorphism and negation of divine attributes. Hidden from sensation, they dwell within intellect; the human mind can apprehend them, and the testimony of one who affirms them holds authority.

Knowledge itself unfolds in layers, progressively unveiling what initially appeared concealed. Each higher degree of knowledge renders what lay below it obscure by comparison. Since knowledge is boundless, the unseen likewise has no limit. In this sense, the Messenger called the Qur'an's unseen dimensions to light; the Prophet is a *sirāj munīr* (illuminating lamp, *sirāj munīr*), revealing sight just as outward illumination aids the eye. As the blind cannot benefit from a lamp, so the spiritually sightless cannot profit from prophecy and its guidance. The eye and the lamp together yield complete vision; likewise, the intellect and prophethood unite to unveil the truths of the Hereafter—apart from this convergence, understanding cannot arise.

### Establishment of Prayer

Outwardly, *iqāmat al-salāt* (establishment of prayer, *iqāmat al-salāt*) denotes the performance of ritual worship. The external prayer functions like the walls encircling a city, within which every form of charity and blessing circulates to benefit its inhabitants. The Prophet therefore called it "the sum of good deeds," commencing with the *takbīrat al-iḥrām* (the opening declaration of sanctity, *takbīrat al-iḥrām*) and concluding with acceptance, encompassing bowing, prostration, recitation, tashahhud, and standing—all forming one unified action. Against this outer prayer stands an inner prayer: the *daʿwa* (summons/invitation, *al-daʿwa*) of the people of truth. In this interpretation, the literal and figurative meanings of prayer become established simultaneously; the invitation to truth itself stands firm. This spiritual summons also forms a rampart, and entering it reveals the true fruits and objectives of outward prayer—realities imperceptible to those perceiving only externals. Through entry into this call to truth, one perceives the true meaning and purpose of almsgiving, fasting, pilgrimage, striving, and all divine commandments. This culminates in authentic *tawḥīd* (monotheism, *al-tawḥīd*), free from the defects of both likening God to creation and denying His attributes, transcending all spiritual and corporeal limitation.

Parallel to the *takbīrat al-iḥrām* stands the covenant and bond binding every invitee; one accepts the guardianship of the Imām al-Zamān (the Imam of the Age, *al-imām al-zamān*), for whoever is bound to this call to truth participates in the congregation of those who establish prayer and reaps the benefits of the outer prayer in the Hereafter.

### Spending from Divine Provision

Sustenance manifests in two forms: material and physical provision, and spiritual and intellectual nourishment. Those who feed the hungry and needy deserve commendation; yet those who impart spiritual sustenance rank far higher. This sustenance reaches those holding fast to the cord of the guardianship of ʿAlī and entering his summons through covenant and obligation. Every person receives physical provision suited to their constitution; the outwardly inclined are taught the Book according to their temperament and comprehension, while the inwardly disposed receive wisdom. Thus the apostles provide both services, dispensing to all from Allah's bounties. So too do those who follow your ordinances.

### Faith in the Messengers and Certainty of the Hereafter

In this verse, Allah unites three temporal epochs—past, present, and future—teaching that faith is perfected through affirmation of previous prophets, obedience to the present Messenger, and certainty in the coming Resurrection. Whoever renounces any of these conditions stands excluded from the circle of the faithful. Allah declares: "Whosoever fulfills all conditions in this verse—believing in the Qur'an's interpretation, establishing the call to truth, affirming all prophets and the forthcoming Resurrection—such are guided by their Lord and shall prosper."

## Those Who Disbelieve

Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 2:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ سَوَآءٌ عَلَيْهِمْ ءَأَنذَرْتَهُمْ أَمْ لَمْ تُنذِرْهُمْ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *Verily, those who disbelieve, it is the same to them whether you (O Muhammad Peace be upon him) warn them or do not warn them, they will not believe.*

The disbelievers mentioned here do not refer merely to the Arab polytheists who venerated idols without reflection, following ancestral custom in ignorance. Rather, ⟪ar:'کفر'⟫ (disbelief, *kufr*) in its lexical sense signifies concealment; the farmer (*kāfir*, ⟪ar:'کافر'⟫) conceals the seed beneath soil. In this light, a disbeliever is one who deliberately denies truth despite full awareness, feigning ignorance through mystification—"I do not know"—when indeed knowledge is present. Such a person remains impossible to convince through demonstration.

Among the disbelievers are those who denied the Messenger: Jews and Christians whose scriptures contained clear prophecies and tidings of the Prophet of the End-Times, with explicit commendation and his name written therein. The location, symbols, and circumstances of his appearance were recorded in the Torah and Gospel. Yet through enmity they altered these texts, and thus they became disbelievers despite their possession of truth.[^cite-4]

In the Messenger's time, disbelievers also meant those Muslims who deliberately rejected him despite knowledge. They refused obedience though aware that ⟪ar:سباقت فی الاسلام⟫ (being foremost in Islam, *sabāqat fī al-islām*) brought ʿAlī distinctive virtues, and that ⟪ar:من کنت مولاہ فھذا علی مولاہ⟫ (whoever I am master of, ʿAlī is his master, *man kuntu mawlāhu fa-hādhā ʿalī mawlāhu*) was proclaimed before thousands at Ghadir Khumm.[^cite-5] What greater disbelief exists than this?

The people of the Qur'an's external meaning understand disbelievers as those outside Islam—idolaters and adherents of other faiths. Yet according to the people of *taʾwīl* (esoteric exegesis, *al-taʾwīl*), all Islamic sects who rejected the Prophet's successor are included in this verse, mirroring the rejection of the Jews and Christians. They possessed full knowledge; the Qur'an itself taught them that among all previous prophets, none established succession (waṣiya, *al-waṣiya*) without first clarifying that prophethood remains incomplete without guardianship.

⟪ar:'أنذر'⟫ (to warn, *undhir*) means to alert before calamity arrives, as in the proverb: "He who warns is excused." Thus Allah described the patriarch as a "warner" in Sūrat al-Fāṭir:

> ⟪quran 35:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَهُمْ يَصْطَرِخُونَ فِيهَا رَبَّنَآ أَخْرِجْنَا نَعْمَلْ صَلِحًا غَيْرَ ٱلَّذِى كُنَّا نَعْمَلُۚ أَوَلَمْ نُعَمِّرْكُم مَّا يَتَذَكَّرُ فِيهِ مَن تَذَكَّرَ وَجَآءَكُمُ ٱلنَّذِيرُۖ فَذُوقُوا۟ فَمَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ مِن نَّصِيرٍ ‎⟫
>
> *Therein they will cry: "Our Lord! Bring us out, we shall do righteous good deeds, not (the evil deeds) that we used to do." (Allah will reply): "Did We not give you lives long enough, so that whosoever would receive admonition, - could receive it? And the warner came to you. So taste you (the evil of your deeds). For the Zalimun (polytheists and wrongdoers, etc.) there is no helper."*

The warner is he who alerts to death's imminence and to the imminent loss of worldly blessings. For those intoxicated by luxury and forgetful of the Hereafter, no warning avails; the sleeper may be awakened, but one who feigns sleep cannot be roused.

## The Seal upon Their Hearts and Hearing

Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 2:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏خَتَمَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِهِمْ وَعَلَىٰ سَمْعِهِمْ ۖ وَعَلَىٰٓ أَبْصَرِهِمْ غِشَوَةٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
>
> *Allah has set a seal on their hearts and on their hearings, (i.e. they are closed from accepting Allah's Guidance), and on their eyes there is a covering. Theirs will be a great torment.*

At face value, if Allah has sealed the hearts of disbelievers, barring them from His knowledge, and deprived them of benefit through veiling their ears and eyes, denying them hearing and sight, then they appear helpless, worthy of no reproach.

### The Seal upon Hearts Interpreted

The people of the *daʿwa* (summons, *al-daʿwa*) teach that here "heart," "ear," and "eye" do not denote physical organs but rather those persons and entities who have become the heart, ear, and eye of their communities. In the human body, the heart commands all organs; at its direction they act, and from it life radiates. The human being constitutes a ⟪ar:'عالم صغیر'⟫ (microcosm, *ʿālam ṣaghīr*), the heart's station corresponding to ⟪ar:عالم کبیر⟫ (the macrocosm, *al-ʿālam al-kabīr*)—that of the sun. Similarly, among the people of religion stands a single chief: the heart and soul of the faith. This was the Prophet in his age, and after him, his successor and the *Imāms* ⟪ar:علیھم السلام⟫ (upon them be peace, *ʿalayhi al-salām*), upon whom the law rests. These are the centers of life and religion, from which all the confines of the summons and the believers draw eternal life, through which the system of divine law is sustained.[^cite-6]

Conversely, those who call toward misguidance and usurp the rights of the Imams, seizing their stations, become the hearts of their followers:

> ⟪quran 28:41⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَجَعَلْنَهُمْ أَئِمَّةًۭ يَدْعُونَ إِلَىٰ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ وَيَوْمَ ٱلْقِيَمَةِ لَا يُنصَرُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *And We made them leaders inviting to the Fire, and on the Day of Resurrection, they will not be helped.*

Their people regard them as leaders, obeying them as bodily organs obey the heart. To say ⟪ar:ختم اللہ علی قلبھم⟫ ("Allah has sealed their hearts") means these false imams are themselves misled and mislead their followers. No ⟪ar:'تائید یزدانی'⟫ (divine assistance, *taʾyīd-i yazdānī*) or ⟪ar:'انوار ربانی'⟫ (divine lights, *anwār rabānī*) illuminates their breasts. Deprived of revelation, they grasp neither truth nor guidance. Since their hearts are darkened, their thoughts densify; they comprehend nothing save the fabrication of illusion.

The heart's function is contemplation and reflection. Thought that unveils monotheistic knowledge and clarifies the stations of the Messenger and Imams constitutes the heart's true work. As Allah says: "Those who contemplate the creation of the heavens and the earth."[^cite-7] The Messenger taught that one moment's reflection surpasses a year of worship. A heart that fails to ponder these realities degrades to bestiality rather than humanity.

### The Veiling of Hearing and Sight

When the heart's reality clarifies, so too do its organs—those who follow an ignorant or false imam, hearkening to his summons, become his ears and eyes. The ear and eye are receptive organs; these people accept their false imam's claims and adapt their understanding to his. Obedient to their corrupted hearts, they hear no word of truth nor perceive it. For them awaits severe punishment in the Hereafter.

In the same chapter, Allah speaks similarly:

> ⟪quran 2:10⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فِى قُلُوبِهِم مَّرَضٌۭ فَزَادَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ مَرَضًۭا ۖ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۢ بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْذِبُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *In their hearts is a disease (of doubt and hypocrisy) and Allah has increased their disease. A painful torment is theirs because they used to tell lies.*

The distinction between this verse and the preceding lies in focus: the previous addresses the sealing of hearts, while this turns to the disease of hearts among disbelievers. Ibrāhīm (peace be upon him) speaks of his Lord's attributes:

> ⟪quran 26:78-80⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَنِى فَهُوَ يَهْدِينِ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِى هُوَ يُطْعِمُنِى وَيَسْقِينِ ‎ ‏وَإِذَا مَرِضْتُ فَهُوَ يَشْفِينِ ‎⟫
>
> *"Who has created me, and it is He Who guides me; "And it is He Who feeds me and gives me to drink. " And when I am ill, it is He who cures me;*

Note that Ibrāhīm attributed his creation, guidance, and sustenance to Allah alone, yet concerning illness he said not "When He makes me ill, He heals me" but rather "When I become ill, He cures me." This assignment of bodily disease to the self demonstrates that both physical and cardiac ailments arise from human agency.

Cardiac disease refers here to doubts and skepticism that sicken the soul as physical illness weakens the body. Doubt deprives the self of knowledge; the doubter cannot benefit from the Messenger's wisdom or commands and rejects them—as a sick person gains no benefit from even the finest nourishment. The one consumed by doubt cannot spiritually attain right understanding; religious sciences, like sustenance, cannot be digested by a diseased heart, and if presented as the most refined knowledge, it is rejected.

Since doubt originates in the heart itself, not externally imposed, Allah states: ⟪ar-quote:فی قلوبھم مرض⟫ "Disease dwells in their hearts." Regarding physical ailments, *Amīr al-Muʾminīn* (the Commander of the Faithful, *amīr al-muʾminīn*) taught that they do not come from without but are born of man's negligence, observing: "Your illnesses are from you, yet you know it not; your cure lies within you, yet you perceive it not."

Allah attributes the seal to Himself because it is the consequence of their evil deeds and punishment for their actions. The disbelievers, by refusing the Guardian and persisting in denial, have earned inability to comprehend any good concerning him. Thus it is written:

## The Physical and Spiritual Patient and His Cure

Allah proclaims in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 41:53⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَنُرِيهِمْ آيَتِنَا فِى ٱلأٓفَاقِ وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ يَتَبَيَّنَ لَهُمْ أَنَّهُ ٱلْحَقُّ ۗ أَوَلَمْ يَكْفِ بِرَبِّكَ أَنَّهُۥ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ شَهِيدٌ ‎⟫
>
> *We will show them Our Signs in the universe, and in their ownselves, until it becomes manifest to them that this (the Quran) is the truth. Is it not sufficient in regard to your Lord that He is a Witness over all things?*

This verse illuminates how Allah has made His signs manifest in both the outer realm and the inner self—the apparent and the concealed. He has grounded His religion upon principles observable in creation. As a patient recovers through consultation with a skilled physician, so too has Allah appointed a healer for spiritual disease, one who mends the soul.

The physical patient must seek a specialist physician, disclosing his ailment without concealment and requesting treatment. When prescribed medicine, he must take it at proper times in correct quantities. Similarly, one afflicted spiritually should turn to the scholars and elders who benefit from the *Ahl al-Bayt* (the People of the Household, *ahl al-bayt*), those possessed of the power to dispel various doubts—whether concerning guardianship, divine law, or faith in the unseen. As the physician discerns the patient's pulse and administers cure, the religious scholar identifies the spiritual patient's malady through his discourse and comportment, which mirror the Qur'an, then prescribes remedy.

The physician must possess both expertise and integrity: he must never mislead the patient for worldly gain and must refrain from deepening the ailment through false counsel. Likewise, the religious scholar must not increase the spiritual patient's doubts for the sake of fleeting renown or praise. Every scholar must be mature, powerful, and expert in knowledge, yet also compassionate, gentle, and loving toward the patient.

The physician himself must be well. He cannot suffer an affliction that blinds him to the patient's pulse or prevents proper examination. Similarly, the religious scholar's beliefs must be sound, his concepts correct, and his thoughts healthy. He must not fall prey to confusing doubts but possess clarity and acuity sufficient to recognize, upon hearing the patient's words, which vein of faith is defective and through what corruption it arose. The truly learned physician possesses a sound pulse; the genuine scholar is a realist.

The patient must faithfully take prescribed medicine at appointed times in proper doses, avoid what the physician forbids, and exercise patience until recovery. Likewise, the spiritually afflicted must practice the actions the religious scholar recommends—whether pertaining to divine law or wisdom—abandon the evils he counsels against, and persevere until disease vanishes and health is restored.

When patient and physician both possess these qualities, cure proves effective. Both the transmitted sciences and the esoteric interpretation shall yield their fruits.

### The Killing of a Human

The foremost of these three is the murderous taking of an innocent life. Against this external killing stands an interior murder—far more grievous than outward homicide. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 5:32⟫

Superficially, no single human life possesses such universal consequence as to affect all humanity. Yet when interpreted through the true self—⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ not referring to an ordinary person but to ⟪ar:'نفس عالَم'⟫ (the "self" of a world, *nafs ʿālam*), a prophet, messenger, or imam—the statement proves absolutely true. The souls of the entire world are bound to theirs. Hence it is said: "The death of a scholar is the death of an entire world."[^cite-8]

Whoever slays the Prophet, messenger, or *Imām* from his station, whether outwardly or inwardly, has in truth murdered all of creation. The *Imām* is the guarantor of the eternal life and salvation of all beings; his guardianship is the very soul of divine law. Remove it, and the law becomes a corpse lying lifeless. Conversely, the verse continues: whoever revives one soul has revived all mankind—meaning, whoever restores the Prophet, messenger, or *Imām* to his rightful station confers benefit upon all humanity, for his restoration ensures the salvation of every soul.

Note the careful distinction: at the beginning, the verse states ⟪ar:من قتل ⟪ar:'نفسا'⟫⟫ (the killing of "a self," using the indefinite form), not ⟪ar:من قتل ⟪ar:'انسانا'⟫⟫ (the killing of "a human being"). Conversely, those who stand against usurpers and restore to the Guardian his rights deserve reward as if they had saved the lives of all creation.[^cite-9] ⟪ar:'نفس رسول'⟫ ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب'⟫ What wickedness have not the hypocrites inflicted upon the land through inwardly slaying these noble souls?

## When It Is Said to Them: Believe as the People Have Believed

In this verse, Allāh describes the hypocrites whose outward profession of faith masks an inner rejection of truth. The very word "hypocrisy" derives from *Nafiq al-Irbū*—the burrow of a rat or fox—which possesses not one exit but many; close one and the creature escapes through another. So too with the hypocrites: like creatures that share the natures of birds and beasts alike, they navigate between worlds, their gaze fixed upon worldly advantage rather than spiritual reality. Their disloyalty to faith has rendered them dangerous in every age; even in the Prophet's own time, no group inflicted greater harm upon the community than these dissemblers.

True faith (īmān), however, means *taṣdīq*—affirmation grounded in knowledge. A person cannot genuinely confirm what remains unknown to them; mere assumption, however charitable toward the speaker, confers no validity upon such affirmation. Allāh Himself does not accept the testimony of a witness save with knowledge.[^cite-11] For this reason, the claim that Allāh requires belief in the unseen (*al-ghayb*) while depriving us of the means to know it is both false and absurd. Rather, Allāh has ordained two avenues of knowledge: the senses, which perceive the manifest and tangible world; and human reason—also called spiritual insight—which grasps what lies beyond sensory reach.

### The Tangible and the Rational

Through the senses, Allāh has endowed us with eyes, nose, tongue, and ears to perceive colour, smell, taste, and sound, and to feel through touch the subtle textures and dense substances distributed across the hand and throughout the body. Yet many things transcend perception: they are accessible to reason alone. This second category encompasses all the ordinances and doctrines of religion. The Prophet—peace and blessings upon him—summoned people to faith in matters the Scripture and Law disclose (the Throne and the Footstool, Paradise and Hell) which belong entirely to the realm of the unseen. They cannot be felt by the senses, yet reason can grasp them—*provided* one has someone to convey them and possesses the will to understand.

⟪quran 2:8⟫
⟪ar:‏وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَقُولُ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَبِٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ وَمَا هُم بِمُؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫

The hypocrites who abandoned the master of *ta'wīl* (esoteric exegesis)—and would not even attempt to understand the interpretation that clarifies the reality of the unseen—have transformed their affirmation into mockery. All matters pertaining to the Sharī'a and the Book elude sensory perception; all are rational truths. Yet these hypocrites have declared enmity toward those possessed of intellect. Thus their faith—their bare assent without comprehension—is hollow, an affirmation that sickens every thinking person and invokes Allāh's displeasure.[^cite-12] 

⟪quran 2:13⟫
⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ آمِنُوا۟ كَمَآ آمَنَ ٱلنَّاسُ قَالُوٓا۟ أَنُؤْمِنُ كَمَآ آمَنَ ٱلسُّفَهَآءُ ۗ أَلَآ إِنَّهُمْ هُمُ ٱلسُّفَهَآءُ وَلَكِن لَّا يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫

Among such hypocrites are those swayed by conjecture and mere opinion. They do not penetrate the belief that Allāh will raise the dead from their graves upon the Day of Resurrection; rather, they accept it formally because the Messenger of Allāh has mentioned it in the Book. In the same manner they accept all doctrines concerning the Tablet and the Pen, the Throne and the Footstool, the Balance and the Bridge, Paradise and Hell and the Hereafter—accepting them solely because the Messenger conveyed them, and since the Messenger does not deceive, all must be true. Yet they refuse to acknowledge that, though these matters belong to the unseen, they are rational and comprehensible to human intellect.[^cite-13] Therefore Allāh will not accept such faith under any circumstance; it produces neither purification of the soul nor luminosity of heart. Such faith is rejected in Allāh's sight because it is stained with *ẓulm* (wrongdoing, spiritual darkness). Allāh praises the true believers:

⟪quran 6:82⟫
⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَلَمْ يَلْبِسُوٓا۟ إِيمَنَهُم بِظُلْمٍ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ لَهُمُ ٱلْأَمْنُ وَهُم مُّهْتَدُونَ ‎⟫

The common understanding takes *ẓulm* here to mean *shirk* (association of partners with Allāh)—an interpretation entirely sound and beyond dispute. Allāh says in His Book:

⟪quran 31:13⟫
⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ لُقْمَنُ لِٱبْنِهِۦ وَهُوَ يَعِظُهُۥ يَبُنَىَّ لَا تُشْرِكْ بِٱللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّ ٱلشِّرْكَ لَظُلْمٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫

Yet shirk (association) takes two forms: *shirk jāli* and *shirk khafī*.

### The Manifest and the Hidden Forms of Association

*Shirk Jāli* (Manifest Association) is the form in which the Arabs and idolaters of the Age of Ignorance were ensnared—the worship of stone idols, the trinitarian belief of Christians, the dualism of Zoroastrians.

*Shirk Khafī* (Hidden Association) is that which entangles the community of the Messenger of Allāh—peace and blessings upon him. The Prophet declared: "Shirk in my community is more concealed than the creeping of a black ant upon a black stone in the darkness of night."[^cite-14] This form of association escapes the comprehension of the average believer. The poor soul recites the *Kalimah al-Tayyibah* and supposes himself monotheistic, unaware that true monotheism requires recognizing the stations (*ḥudūd*, sing. *ḥadd*) of Allāh—both the spiritual and the manifest—and never associating others in their ranks. Whoever ascribes to another the rank (*ḥadd*) that belongs to the Imāms has contaminated his faith with association, and has incurred grave wrong and sin. As a result, he is deprived of peace and true guidance.

⟪quran 6:82⟫
⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَلَمْ يَلْبِسُوٓا۟ إِيمَنَهُم بِظُلْمٍ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ لَهُمُ ٱلْأَمْنُ وَهُم مُّهْتَدُونَ ‎⟫

That is, those hypocrites whose faith is tainted with association were told: "Believe with sincere intention and true understanding, as people—*the people*—believe with pure hearts and affirmation rooted in knowledge." There exist two species of humankind. One is the party of *Hizb al-Raḥmān* (the Party of the Merciful), whose members are bound to the assembly of Allāh; the other is *Hizb al-Shaytān* (the Party of Satan). In this verse, *al-nās* ("the people") denotes those who are the servants of the Merciful and bound to Allāh's community. Those addressed in the verse—these hypocrites—are not, in Allāh's sight, truly "people" at all. Though they possess human form and physiology, there is no correspondence between the two groups in spiritual and psychological reality. Therefore this second type was commanded to believe and affirm like the first type—the true humans—so that spiritual kinship might be established between them, as there already exists resemblance in physical form and bodily organs.[^cite-15]

It is fitting to draw analogy with the Messenger and the Prophet, for they are parents of the community in their respective eras. Likewise, after the Prophet, the Imām and his *ḥujaj* (the proofs) are masters of guidance and right conduct. The "people" to whom this verse alludes—*Ibād al-Raḥmān*—are the Imāms who succeed the Prophet—peace and blessings upon him. The Qur'ān itself attests this claim:

⟪quran 4:54⟫
⟪ar:‏أَمْ يَحْسُدُونَ ٱلنَّاسَ عَلَىٰ مَآ آتَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ مِن فَضْلِهِۦ ۖ فَقَدْ آتَيْنَآ آلَإِبْرَهِيمَ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْحِكْمَةَ وَآتَيْنَهُم مُّلْكًا عَظِيمًۭا ‎⟫

Here *faḍl* (grace, favour) refers to the khilāfah and the Imāmah. Imām Ja'far al-Ṣādiq—upon him be peace—has said in his exegesis of this verse: "We are the people whom people envy because of what Allāh has granted us through His grace—namely, the Khilāfah and the Imāmah! Why did He not bestow this grace upon all?" In this verse, "the great kingdom" signifies: We have made them Imāms whose authority extends over human hearts; their obedience and disobedience are conjoined with our obedience and disobedience. The question thus arises: why do people acknowledge this great kingdom in the family of Ibrāhīm but not in the family of Muḥammad—peace be upon him?

### Who Is the Perfect Man?

The absolute and perfect human being (*al-insān al-kāmil*) is one in every age and era. The Prophet in his time, the *Wasi* (legatee) in his time, and the Imām in his time—each is the perfect human; whoever draws nearer to this absolute human in religious knowledge and resembles him more closely in learning and belief deserves the title "humanity" and stands distant from the beasts.[^cite-16] Yet in response to Allāh's call, these hypocrites said:

⟪quran 2:13⟫
⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ آمِنُوا۟ كَمَآ آمَنَ ٱلنَّاسُ قَالُوٓا۟ أَنُؤْمِنُ كَمَآ آمَنَ ٱلسُّفَهَآءُ ۗ أَلَآ إِنَّهُمْ هُمُ ٱلسُّفَهَآءُ وَلَكِن لَّا يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫

## Their Likeness: Like One Who Kindled Fire

### The Nature of Fire

Fire is the noblest of the four elements. Unlike water and earth, it possesses no solid existence in the ordinary sense. Allāh created it to consume and transform all things; its virtue lies in Allāh's subjugating it to human use—a privilege withheld from all animals. Only man possesses the wisdom to kindle fire from wood, stone, and iron, and to preserve it in receptacles: cotton, papyrus, and the like. Sulfur exemplifies this property: it harbors fire's hidden essence and readily accepts the spark struck from flint and steel, storing the flame within itself. Without such ready fuel, the conflagration of great logs from the forest would be impossible; their density and dampness render them slow to ignite, yet once kindled, they burn long and grow ever hotter.

Thus fire first ignites objects whose substance readily yields its heat, then progressively consumes denser materials with increasing vigor. At times it grows so fierce it becomes a wildfire, impossible to control. In the parable of humanity, *insāniyyah* (humanness) is concealed as fire lies hidden within flint. Of all creatures, only humankind possesses the capacity to strike this spark; no beast can do so. The person who illuminates this hidden humanity is the perfect human—the guide of his age, the prophet or imām.[^cite-17] Just as flint's spark is useless until it reaches a hand capable of kindling true fire, so every human intellect remains unenlightened until it attaches itself to the Messenger and surrenders to him. The Messenger rubs it against the commandments of the Sharī'ah and renders it bright and resplendent. He alone guides to salvation; whoever the Messenger rejects is himself rejected and harmed.

### The Spiritual Fire

Moreover, the fire that dwells hidden in stone and plant can be kindled only by man—never by beasts. Likewise there exists a spiritual and luminous fire that Mūsā beheld upon the mountain; this is called the *Rūḥ al-Quds* (the Holy Spirit). This spiritual fire is, in truth, the fire of *isnad* (support), of prophethood, of the will, and of the Imāmah. It is also called the *Sulṭān* (authority, dominion) which Allāh bestows upon His chosen servants, through which they are exalted and become His elect, His Messengers. This dominion extends over both body and soul of humankind, and through its power the Messenger of Allāh prepares his people for the Hereafter. This spiritual fire draws near to all as Allāh's blessing, yet only those with capacity and ability can benefit from it—and these are solely the *awliyā'* (saints) of Allāh. Not everyone possesses the aptitude to profit from it.

⟪quran 2:257⟫
⟪ar:‏ٱللَّهُ وَلِىُّ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ يُخْرِجُهُم مِّنَ ٱلظُّلُمَتِ إِلَى ٱلنُّورِ ۖ وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ أَوْلِيَآؤُهُمُ ٱلطَّغُوتُ يُخْرِجُونَهُم مِّنَ ٱلنُّورِ إِلَى ٱلظُّلُمَتِ ۗ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫

This verse alludes to hypocrites who were bound to the Messenger of Allāh—peace and blessings upon him—the *Sulṭān* (the possessor of the Fire of Support), and who clung to the firm rope of Allāh. Yet:

⟪quran 2:17⟫
⟪ar:‏مَثَلُهُمْ كَمَثَلِ ٱلَّذِى ٱسْتَوْقَدَ نَارًۭا فَلَمَّآ أَضَآءَتْ مَا حَوْلَهُۥ ذَهَبَ ٱللَّهُ بِنُورِهِمْ وَتَرَكَهُمْ فِى ظُلُمَتٍۢ لَّا يُبْصِرُونَ ‎⟫

When the lights of prophethood began to spread in every direction, and prophethood flourished as a branch in the bequest of the Prophet, in his *wasi* (legatee), and in the Imāmah of the Imāms:

⟪quran 2:17⟫
⟪ar:‏مَثَلُهُمْ كَمَثَلِ ٱلَّذِى ٱسْتَوْقَدَ نَارًۭا فَلَمَّآ أَضَآءَتْ مَا حَوْلَهُۥ ذَهَبَ ٱللَّهُ بِنُورِهِمْ وَتَرَكَهُمْ فِى ظُلُمَتٍۢ لَّا يُبْصِرُونَ ‎⟫

Allāh withdrew from them their share of the light because of their envy, hatred, and arrogance, and they were plunged into darkness once more.[^cite-18] The hypocrites despised becoming branches of the Prophet's genealogy and rejected the flourishing of his line; they attempted to cast shadows upon the light. Though the light itself could not be extinguished, the light they had received was taken from them. Allāh deprived them of the illumination of the bequest and the Imāmah, and they reverted to the darkness of ignorance.

### Can "Light" Mean the Qur'ān?

In other verses, the exegetes of the common people interpret *nūr* (light) as the Qur'ān; yet they do not comprehend the meaning of "light" in this verse. Since the Qur'ān rests in everyone's hands, what sense does the "snatching away" of light convey? The written Qur'ān remains in their outward possession; their eyesight is sound; the outward light of sun and moon shines bright.

The scholars say that *nūr* here denotes not the outward Qur'ān inscribed upon paper, but rather *ṣāḥib al-Qur'ān* (the master, the embodiment of the Qur'ān)—the inner, spiritual Qur'ān, which is called the *Wasi* or the Imām of his time. By rejecting him, people forfeit the *nūr* (luminous knowledge) of his meaning:

⟪quran 2:257⟫
⟪ar:‏ٱللَّهُ وَلِىُّ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ يُخْرِجُهُم مِّنَ ٱلظُّلُمَتِ إِلَى ٱلنُّورِ ۖ وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ أَوْلِيَآؤُهُمُ ٱلطَّغُوتُ يُخْرِجُونَهُم مِّنَ ٱلنُّورِ إِلَى ٱلظُّلُمَتِ ۗ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫

### What Is Meant by Darkness?

Similarly, in their denial of the *ṣāḥib al-Qur'ān* (the embodiment of Qur'ānic truth), their vision and heart have grown blind. The sciences of the spirit, the truths of the Qur'ān, and sacred knowledge—all light—have been denied them. They stumble at every step, unable to answer the simplest academic question, trapped in a labyrinth of meaningless words. They worship, yet are ignorant of the true purpose of worship; thus they are ensnared in an eternal, perpetual darkness of the soul. The outward light and darkness shall one day vanish with this world, yet the darkness of their souls shall remain forever. Referring to this inner light and darkness, Allāh says in the *Āyah al-Kursī*:

⟪quran 2:257⟫
⟪ar:‏ٱللَّهُ وَلِىُّ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ يُخْرِجُهُم مِّنَ ٱلظُّلُمَتِ إِلَى ٱلنُّورِ ۖ وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ أَوْلِيَآؤُهُمُ ٱلطَّغُوتُ يُخْرِجُونَهُم مِّنَ ٱلنُّورِ إِلَى ٱلظُّلُمَتِ ۗ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫

That is, Allāh—through the Imām of the time—draws the believers out of the darkness of mere words and the obscurity of figurative speech into eternal, abiding light.[^cite-19]

Those who disbelieve in the *ḥudūd* (stations, limits) of Allāh and associate others with Him have the *taghūt* (false lords, tyrants) as their guardians. The *taghūt* is anyone who opposes the Prophet, the *wasi*, or the Imām, or whosoever ascends the pulpit in the place of the Messenger of Allāh—peace and blessings upon him—without warrant. Their function is to lead people from the light (inner meaning) into darkness (outward literalism). Since they comprehend not the meaning of the Qur'ān, they rest content with its apparent, literal sense and mislead all into the darkness of mere words. In truth, darkness and obscurity have overwhelmed their souls because they turned away from those who would guide them to the realm of the soul and the light.

## He Who Made the Earth a Bed and the Heaven a Canopy

Allāh says—interpretation of the meaning:

⟪quran 2:22⟫
⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى جَعَلَ لَكُمُ ٱلْأَرْضَ فِرَشًۭا وَٱلسَّمَآءَ بِنَآءًۭ وَأَنزَلَ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ فَأَخْرَجَ بِهِۦ مِنَ ٱلثَّمَرَتِ رِزْقًۭا لَّكُمْ ۖ فَلَا تَجْعَلُوا۟ لِلَّهِ أَندَادًۭا وَأَنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫

"He Who made the earth a bed for you, and the sky a vault."

Allāh's word reaches every human being—the learned and the ignorant, the elite and the masses—alike; each understands it according to his own knowledge and capacity. Common Muslims understand by "heaven and earth" the visible heavens and earth of this physical world, wherein both animals and humans dwell and fulfill their bodily needs.

Yet the mystics of truth say: since the human being is a mixture of two principles—the soul and the body—there must necessarily exist, in parallel to the physical heaven and earth, a spiritual heaven and earth belonging to the realm of the soul and religion. The visible, tangible earth to which our body is attributed is indeed born of this material earth; it makes the earth its floor, its cradle, its soil; and after death, it is buried within it and dissolved into dust. But our soul is not of the genus of this material earth; it has no kinship with the physical realm.

The Prophet—peace and blessings upon him—said: "The earth is your mother, and she treats you with goodness."[^cite-20] If we interpret this of the material earth alone, then neither is she our mother nor can she exercise will or treat anyone kindly. To show goodness requires volition and independence; yet the earth is compelled, possessing no will of its own. True, it provides the medium through which plants grow from which we derive our sustenance. Yet in light of this prophetic tradition, we must acknowledge another earth—a spiritual mother—for such language cannot apply to inert matter. This spiritual earth possesses will and power; from it every believer derives and develops all the necessities of spiritual life, and after death returns to it and becomes one with it. This is the esoteric earth of the Prophet or the Imām—upon him be peace—from which the believer's soul receives its spiritual nourishment, is born into divine sciences, and after death returns and merges with them. Thus this spiritual "earth" (the *Wasi* or Imām) is more deserving of the title *umm al-birrāh* (the mother of goodness).

⟪quran 2:22⟫
⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى جَعَلَ لَكُمُ ٱلْأَرْضَ فِرَشًۭا وَٱلسَّمَآءَ بِنَآءًۭ وَأَنزَلَ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ فَأَخْرَجَ بِهِۦ مِنَ ٱلثَّمَرَتِ رِزْقًۭا لَّكُمْ ۖ فَلَا تَجْعَلُوا۟ لِلَّهِ أَندَادًۭا وَأَنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫

Similarly, in this verse the heaven refers to a hidden, inner heaven—the heaven of religious scholarship, which is spiritual and concealed. Heaven and earth bear the same relationship to each other as man to woman. The heaven bestows water upon the earth; the earth receives this gift, absorbs it into itself, and gives bodily form flourishment. Yet this outward heaven, despite its elevation and grandeur, is mute, deaf, and blind, for it encompasses only the dense human body. It has no dominion over souls; yet the human soul, despite its bodily confinement, encompasses this vast world and possesses knowledge of all it contains.

Thus the soul—or the heaven of religion—belongs to him who is the master of the Sharī'ah: the Messenger of Allāh or the Imām of his time. He elevates the soul to its highest station and bestows upon the spiritual earth (*Wasi* or *ḥujjah* Imām) his blessings; the earth thereupon develops the form of the soul and renders it worthy of the Hereafter and the world of eternity. This psychical heaven and all the religious sciences pertaining to it are eternal and perpetual, while the outer heaven and its entire system are destined for destruction and decay.

⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى جَعَلَ لَكُمُ ٱلْأَرْضَ فِرَشًۭا وَٱلسَّمَآءَ بِنَآءًۭ وَأَنزَلَ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ فَأَخْرَجَ بِهِۦ مِنَ ٱلثَّمَرَتِ رِزْقًۭا لَّكُمْ ۖ فَلَا تَجْعَلُوا۟ لِلَّهِ أَندَادًۭا وَأَنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫

From the progression of the verse it is evident that, like heaven and earth, water and fruits too possess esoteric meaning. For common people, water denotes the outward water with which they quench thirst, and fruit signifies the outward fruits and vegetables that provide pleasure and nourishment. The wise say: "The water that descends from the sky refers to *waḥy* (revelation), which Allāh conveyed to the Messenger of Allāh—peace and blessings upon him—who clothed it in the words of the Qur'ān and brought it down to the earth of the guardian, that is, who transmitted this Qur'ānic knowledge to his successor (ʿAlī)."[^cite-21] Similarly, water denotes the knowledge obtained from the Imām—upon him be peace—in every era through his *ḥujjah* (proof).

### The Outward and Inner Waters

Allāh says—interpretation of the meaning:

⟪quran 21:30⟫
⟪ar:‏أَوَلَمْ يَرَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ أَنَّ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ كَانَتَا رَتْقًۭا فَفَتَقْنَهُمَا ۖ وَجَعَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلْمَآءِ كُلَّ شَىْءٍ حَىٍّ ۖ أَفَلَا يُؤْمِنُونَ ‎⟫

"Have not those who disbelieve seen that the heavens and the earth were joined together as one, then We parted them, and made of water every living thing?"

If this refers to the physical heavens and earth, the question arises: do angels exist as created beings or not? Assuredly they do, for if they did not exist, they would be nothingness; yet existing things are those which have reality. Every Muslim believes that angels exist and are creatures of Allāh just as we are. Do they then possess life from the same water as ours? Yet Allāh says—interpretation of the meaning:

⟪quran 8:11⟫
⟪ar:‏إِذْ يُغَشِّيكُمُ ٱلنُّعَاسَ أَمَنَةًۭ مِّنْهُ وَيُنَزِّلُ عَلَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ لِّيُطَهِّرَكُم بِهِۦ وَيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمْ رِجْزَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ وَلِيَرْبِطَ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِكُمْ وَيُثَبِّتَ بِهِ ٱلْأَقْدَامَ ‎⟫

Certainly, it is intelligible to send down physical water from the sky for our bodily cleansing. But how can this water remove the filth of Satan? Had outward water possessed such power, there would exist no disbeliever or polytheist in the world today; denial of Allāh and His Messenger would cease. Similarly, revelation of the physical waters does not increase love in hearts, nor transform enemies into friends, nor strengthen wavering steps, nor guide the misguided.[^cite-22]

It becomes clear from these verses that there is but one water corresponding to the outward water in the spiritual domain: the knowledge of the Imām—upon him be peace. This knowledge vivifies the inner life of the angels and all souls; this knowledge removes the filth of Satan (doubt, disbelief, association); this knowledge unites scattered hearts and steadies stumbling feet.

⟪quran 8:11⟫
⟪ar:‏إِذْ يُغَشِّيكُمُ ٱلنُّعَاسَ أَمَنَةًۭ مِّنْهُ وَيُنَزِّلُ عَلَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ لِّيُطَهِّرَكُم بِهِۦ وَيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمْ رِجْزَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ وَلِيَرْبِطَ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِكُمْ وَيُثَبِّتَ بِهِ ٱلْأَقْدَامَ ‎⟫

The water that descends from the sky divides into two kinds: that which is directly used, such as quenching thirst; and that which seeps into the earth and produces fruits and vegetables—the food of human and beast. In both modes, water is a source of life. Likewise, revelation sent down from the heaven of the Prophets divides into two: the outward ordinances one is commanded to obey, which instill in the soul virtues of purity and piety and likeness to the angels—such as *salāt* (prayer), which guards against obscenity and wrongdoing, and *sawm* (fasting), which teaches abstinence from food and drink as the angels practice, and through which all moral transgressions are warded off. Wealth is purified through *zakāt* (alms-giving), and all sins are forgiven through *ḥajj* (pilgrimage). The second kind is the knowledge of the revelation, hidden within the earth in the form of the Qur'ān's words—called *ta'wīl* (exegesis) and *al-ḥaqāʾiq* (realities)—concerning which Allāh says:

⟪quran 3:7⟫
⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫

Just as the human being—the essence of the world and its intellect—brings forth fruits and vegetables from the earth through his labours, so the scholar of *ta'wīl* (the *wasi*) draws out the truths and meanings hidden in the words of the Qur'ān, and informs the believers of true monotheism and the knowledge of the Qur'ān's origin, bestowing upon them the eternal fruits of wisdom.[^cite-23]

One of the greatest virtues of the Qur'ānic fruits is that they never diminish through use, unlike worldly fruits that are consumed and gone. The more one contemplates them, the more fresh and profound the meaning becomes; the more one ponders a verse, the more it yields new significance and flavour each time. For this reason, Imām Ja'far al-Ṣādiq—upon him be peace—has said: there is no limit to the meanings of the Qur'ānic verses. They are like the Sea of Wonders (*Baḥr al-ʿAjāʾib*), in which every diver emerges bearing treasure. The knowledge of the Qur'ān resembles the fruits of Paradise, which increase rather than diminish through consumption. When someone asked the *Amīr al-Mu'minīn* (ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib), "What are the fruits of Paradise in this world?" he replied: "The Qur'ān!" Likewise, these Qur'ānic sciences are like a lamp that kindles thousands of other lamps without its light growing dim.

⟪quran 3:7⟫
⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫

That is: do not take any other than your Lord as creator and provider. The outward sustenance is obtained through the earth; the inner sustenance through the Prophets and their legatees. So associate no one with Him in worship.

## If You Are in Doubt Concerning What We Have Revealed to Our Servant, Produce a Surah Like It

Allah addresses humanity with a challenge of unparalleled force:

> ⟪ar:‏فَإِن لَّمْ تَفْعَلُوا۟ وَلَن تَفْعَلُوا۟ فَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱلنَّارَ ٱلَّتِى وَقُودُهَا ٱلنَّاسُ وَٱلْحِجَارَةُ ۖ أُعِدَّتْ لِلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫
> — *But if you do it not, and you can never do it, then fear the Fire (Hell) whose fuel is men and stones, prepared for the disbelievers.*

This Quranic challenge operates simultaneously on two levels: the outward (*ẓāhir*) and the inward (*bāṭin*). Externally, the disbelievers and polytheists doubted that the Qur'an was the word of Allah. Supported by certain among the People of the Book, they fabricated false claims against the Messenger ⟪quran 2:24⟫. Allah answered their doubt by challenging them: if you possess ability in eloquence and command of language, produce a single surah comparable to it. The whole of the Qur'an lies beyond their reach.

Internally, however, the hypocrites harbored a deeper doubt. They denied that the Prophet, in designating his successor, had acted by divine command. They insisted instead that he had chosen on the basis of kinship alone—affection for his cousin and son-in-law born of worldly attachment and personal preference. To these doubters, Allah directed the challenge in a spiritual register: if you deny that this Messenger stands as Allah's Caliph upon the earth, then produce a scholar equal to him—one upon whom the Messenger of Allah opened a thousand chapters of knowledge, and from each chapter a thousand more were unlocked.[^cite-24] Failing that, bring forth at least one who approaches the lower stations of the Successor's station of *daʿwa* (summons) and prove that he possesses the capability and wisdom befitting the Guardian of the Messenger.

The position of the Messenger of Allah in revelation and law mirrors that of a farmer extracting sustenance from the earth through labor and skill. Similarly, the Guardian draws upon the fertile ground of the Qur'an through inferential reasoning (*istikhrāj*), generating meanings both timeless and incomparable—meanings that illuminate the human soul eternally, shield it from ruin, and secure for it perpetual survival and spiritual life.

### The Surah: Elevation and Encompassing

The term *Surah* (surah) derives from two sources. First, it carries the sense of eminence and exaltation. When a Surah was revealed to the Prophet, it elevated his rank and station. Second, a Surah encompasses, as a city wall encompasses all dwellings and inhabitants within it. The Qur'an itself, in this sense, encompasses true *tawḥīd* (monotheism); it distinguishes Allah from all attributes that would liken Him to the physical and material. It knows the origins of time itself—all limits and boundaries within which the Shariah of the Prophet operates.

Each station of the *daʿwa* (the Summoners' invitation) functions as a wall of the Gate of the City of Knowledge—a boundary within which a complete spiritual city resides. When Allah addresses those who deny the Gate of the City of Knowledge, He says in effect: If you doubt that it was established by divine command, that it is not the Messenger and Word of Allah, then produce other comprehensive attributes of equal merit, demonstrate knowledge of the universe equal to that of his supplications. Yet you will not dare to produce even one example from those who spoke before.[^cite-25] Rather, show us one of his companions—such as Salman al-Farsi, Miqdad ibn al-Aswad, Abu Dharr al-Ghaffari, ʿAmmar Yasir, or Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr—and prove his capability.

This address of the Almighty extends to all factions and parties of the deniers: those who reject the outward Qur'an as the Word of Allah, and those who deny its inward significance. The Prophet was sent as both bearer of glad tidings and as warner to all humanity. Allah declares: ⟪quran 34:28⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَكَ إِلَّا كَآفَّةًۭ لِّلنَّاسِ بَشِيرًۭا وَنَذِيرًۭا وَلَكِنَّ أَكْثَرَ ٱلنَّاسِ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫

And the Messenger of Allah himself testified: "I have been sent to all mankind, whether Arab or non-Arab, white or red." Therefore, this divine challenge encompasses both the outward deniers and the inward hypocrites.

### Allah's Challenge to All Creation

Allah then declares with even greater magnitude: ⟪quran 17:88⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قُل لَّىِٕنِ ٱجْتَمَعَتِ ٱلْإِنسُ وَٱلْجِنُّ عَلَىٰٓ أَن يَأْتُوا۟ بِمِثْلِ هَذَا ٱلْقُرْآنِ لَا يَأْتُونَ بِمِثْلِهِۦ وَلَوْ كَانَ بَعْضُهُمْ لِبَعْضٍۢ ظَهِيرًۭا ‎⟫

Even if all humanity and all jinn were to unite in purpose, they could not produce the like of this Qur'an, though each supported the other.

Note that the verse addresses neither angels nor demons. Humanity and jinn alone are mentioned—for these two species disobey Allah's commands. Angels, by contrast, are bound in obedience; they perform what they are commanded and execute what they are ordained to do.[^cite-26]

When Allah renders the entire creation incapable of producing an outward equivalent to the Qur'an with such emphatic language, then necessarily they prove even more incapable regarding the Qur'an's inner dimension. The outward is mere form; the inward is the soul animating that form. A sculptor may craft the most beautiful human figure, yet cannot breathe life into it—that inner reality lies beyond his reach. Therefore, if humans cannot produce a book to match the outward Qur'an, they are infinitely more incapable of producing the esoteric Qur'an or the Book of the Speaker (*Natiq*).[^cite-27] This is why Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq declared: "Whoever possesses an outward miracle possesses an inner miracle of surpassing greatness."

### The Call to Witnesses

Allah emphasizes their complete inadequacy by adding: ⟪quran 2:24⟫

> — *Image text (from image 035; citation unverified)*

Bring your witnesses—not Mine—and judge between us. See whose speech excels in Arabic eloquence and clarity.

#### The Significance of Testimony

Testimony (*shahāda*) exists to establish truth and falsehood when two parties dispute. If the witness is just and unflinching under interrogation, his testimony is accepted and right is restored to its rightful possessor. In this verse, Allah intensifies the challenge: when you present your word against a Surah of the Qur'an, bring witnesses of your own choosing—not from us—and let them judge. Will your speech surpass the Word of Allah in eloquence? Surely your own witnesses will confess that Allah's Word stands exalted above all.

Allah repeatedly invokes martyrdom to authenticate His message. He addresses those who deny these testimonies with force: We shall give you so many proofs within your own souls and throughout your universe that you shall be compelled to confess the truth. He then declares Himself a witness: ⟪quran 13:43⟫

> Say, "Allah suffices as a witness between me and you, and whoever possesses knowledge of the Book."

He made the angels witnesses. And He made the Messenger of Allah and the *Umma* witnesses:

⟪quran 2:143⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونُ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهُ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫

Thus have We made you a middle *umma* (nation), so that you may be witnesses over mankind, and the Messenger may be a witness over you. Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq explained: "We are the middle nation and witnesses to the deeds of all peoples, and the Messenger of Allah witnesses over us as to whether we have conveyed Allah's commands to His servants or not."

Allah emphasizes witness and testimony to establish the truth of all matters, great and small. Addressing those who deny the Prophet's eloquence and wisdom, He says: Bring a word like it and bring as witnesses those who can testify that your words surpass the Qur'an—or the Surah brought by the Messenger of Allah, each word of which testifies that it originates from Allah.

Yet in truth, the testimony of the Qur'an is the Qur'an itself. Just as a farmer extracts grain from the earth and humanity derives food and livelihood from every fruit and animal, so too the Qur'an's hidden meanings are extracted from its literal words. The Qur'an rests in the hands of all, yet they understand it not. This virtue is reserved solely for the Prophet and his Ahl al-Bayt (People of the Household)—they alone can infer the meanings and unveil the true purpose and reality. They alone are the true witnesses of the Qur'an. In other words, the outward book stands as the miracle of the Messenger, while its inner interpretation (*taʾwīl*) constitutes the miracle of his true descendants. No one, however they might strive—a thousand nations together—can usurp this station.

At verse's end, Allah declares: ⟪quran 2:24⟫

> — *Image text (from image 043; citation unverified)*

Here appears the word *sādiqūn* (those who speak truth), typically understood as truthfulness, contrasted with *kādhib* (falsehood). The question arises: who speaks truth, and who lies?

We say the Hereafter is truth, for truth is that which endures in all circumstances without decay. Falsehood perpetually changes and ultimately vanishes. Consider the world: it sustains no one in stable condition but brings rise and fall. Spring becomes autumn; summer turns to winter. The body's three stages—childhood, youth, age—flow one into another and culminate in death. Wealth transforms into poverty; health becomes disease; life yields to death; honor surrenders to humiliation. Nothing persists; no state remains unchanged. By contrast, the abode of the Hereafter is the abode of peace and tranquility, pure and untouched by change. There is health without sickness, life without death, honor without humiliation. Every state endures eternally.

**Note**: Allah has placed proof of the spiritual world's reality in the human form—in the heart. Whatever dwells in a person's heart and soul is true, unmarked by falsehood. Yet when that same truth is clothed in words and given bodily form through letters and sound, falsehood emerges.

Those who speak of the abode of truth are themselves the truthful: the Prophets, the Messengers, the righteous, the Imams and their successors. These are the *sādiqūn* whom Allah intends in this verse. Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq declared: "We are the righteous, and this verse was revealed for us." The world of subtlety and immateriality is the Hereafter—free from all change and transformation—while the physical realm teems with falsehood. Therefore, "be with the truthful" means: join those who teach you of the Hereafter and the supernal world, who guide you beyond fear and material acquisition toward nearness to Allah. Do not cleave to those trapped in the realm of falsehood alone—mere materialism, mistaken for totality.

### The Fire: Inward and Outward

After challenging all disbelievers and polytheists, Allah announces the result: ⟪quran 2:24⟫

> — *Image text (from image 045; citation unverified)*

If you cannot produce a Surah like the Qur'an, aided by your false witnesses, then fear the Fire prepared for the disbelievers. Allah has set an impossible condition intentionally, thereby erasing all possibility of their success. He has fortified His Word: No matter how eloquent human speech becomes, it cannot compare to the words of Ahsan al-Khaliqeen (the Best of Creators). Similarly, Allah alone knows which choice is right in selecting the Prophet's Successor—his *khalīfa* and guardian. If misguided servants, their intellects clouded by Satan, were to choose, they would select one like themselves: neither chaste nor learned. Yet the Qur'an dominates and rules over all humanity as humanity rules over the animal realm. This Book's soul is the soul of the human world and the true *qibla* (direction) of every human spirit. It is the Refuge of the Creator, the Self-Sufficient, upon whom all existence depends.

This is why Ali cried from the pulpit: "Ask me before you lose me." No flaw has been found in Ali's eloquence—the Speech of the Commander (*Kalam al-Amīr*)—nor has the whole world, for all its advance in jurisprudence, discovered fault in his judgments. The Book of the Universe was open to him; nothing was hidden. He would say: "I have reached such certainty that if all veils were lifted from my sight, my faith would not increase by a hair." By the knowledge-power transmitted from the Messenger, I am aware of every particle of creation and can discern the truth and origin of all things. Who can stand against one of such knowledge-rank? You dwell at a height no one has scaled or can scale—yet it remains impossible to compare you with your companions and those who drank from your light.

### The Inner Fire: Light and Heat

Material fire possesses two qualities: heat and light. Their union constitutes fire. The inner fire—the revelation of prophethood—likewise has two attributes. For believers, it is light, guiding them toward the illuminated realm and delivering them from darkness. For disbelievers, hypocrites, and those estranged, it is heat—the intensity of the Messenger's prophethood burns them, conducting them toward chastisement and eternal torment. Prophethood remains one reality, yet for the believer it manifests as light, for the denier as consuming heat.

### The Fuel of the Fire: People and Stones

Allah addresses both disbelievers and hypocrites with warning of this prophethood's burning intensity:

> — *Image text (from image 046; citation unverified)*

Allah's statement regarding the fuel of this fire is indeed strange and troubling: "The fuel is humanity and stones." Yet then He says, "Prepared for the disbelievers." This raises puzzlement. Stone is inanimate, flammable through mineral sulfur and the like—this is comprehensible. But who are these "people" (*al-nās*) who fuel hellfire? And if disbelievers are mentioned separately, are they not also from *al-nās*? What, then, is the relationship Allah establishes through the conjunction *wa* (and) between stone and people?

**Note**: The conjunction *wa al-ʿaṭf* (the connecting *wa*) joins only things alike and corresponding—such as righteousness and piety, strength and courage, love and affection, Muhammad and Ali.

Stone is inanimate matter—matter that cannot melt, cannot be reformed into other mineral objects, cannot become productive. Moreover, pairs in Arabic are always of complementary or identical status. If stones mean hard-hearted humans, then who is distinct from them? And if both terms denote the same—hard-heartedness—why mention them twice?

The People of Truth (*Ahl al-Haqq*), in interpreting this verse, say: those who reject the beneficial influence of the Messenger's teachings are themselves stones. To them Allah refers: ⟪quran 2:74⟫

> — *Image text (from image 047; citation unverified)*

**The Stones (*al-Hijāra*)**: These are those who recited the Messenger's words but rejected the guidance of the Imams and guides who came after him. They followed the outer scripture but abandoned the wisdom revealed alongside it. Though human in appearance, they are harder than stone through stubborn resistance, their hearts unmoved even as they witnessed the martyrdom of the Prophet's household—as with Muʿāwiya and Yazid—yet their hearts did not break.

**The People (*al-Nās*)**: Part of the aforementioned assembly, they are those who accepted the Imams after the Messenger and disavowed their enemies. ### Good Tidings and the Bearer of Glad News

In Arabic lexicography, a *bashīr* is one who journeys far ahead of his people through forests, seeking water. Upon finding it, he stands in place, waves his sword or cloth, and announces the glad news of water discovered. The Messenger of Allah too is a *bashīr*—he journeyed far ahead of his people and all humanity, acquiring knowledge of the Mother of the Book, salvation, and the eternal life of the human soul. Through divine aid and revelation, upon achieving his goal, he called all humanity to this fountain of life from that exalted station. With gestures and signs, as a *bashīr* waves sword or cloth, the Messenger employed both sternness and gentleness, threats of hellfire and promises of paradise, intimidation and understanding—drawing people toward *lā ilāha illā-llāh* (there is no god but Allah), the source of eternal life and true *tawḥīd* (monotheism). For those who heed his call, the Messenger is guarantor of both paradise and gardens. Since he employed both methods, he is titled both *bashīr* (bringer of good news) and *nadhīr* (warner): ⟪quran 25:56⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَكَ إِلَّا مُبَشِّرًۭا وَنَذِيرًۭا ‎⟫

### The Believers

Faith (*īmān*) literally means affirmation or assent; in another sense, it means granting security. Faith is a transitive action requiring not merely an agent but also a participle—the object upon which faith acts. Two elements constitute faith: the agent (the believer) and the object (that in which one believes). If faith means affirmation, one must ask: what is affirmed? The affirmed entity is the *Musaddiq*, the object. If faith means granting security, there must similarly be an object: to whom or what security is given.

The Prophet combined both meanings in a hadith: "A believer is one who grants his neighbor security from his harm." Because of these dual meanings, a person is termed *muʾmin* (believer). Among Allah's most beautiful names is al-Muʾmin (The Grantor of Security). A person becomes a believer by affirming Allah's promises; Allah is a believer in that He has protected creation from His deadly punishment.

| Logical | Believer | Verification | Allah, Messenger, Imam |
| Protective | Believer | Security | Believers, Muslims |
| Divine | Allah | Security | Punishment |

In this verse, believers receive glad tidings of paradise—those who affirm the Messenger of Allah and all his commands, and who grant other believers and Muslims security from harm and suffering.

We interpret otherwise: righteous deeds do not merely comprise the conventional virtues listed above; rather, their essence lies in following the righteous believers—the Imams of all times and epochs, those powerful in Qur'anic commands and knowing its true meanings. **Principle**: Ali possesses the same relation to the Imams as an Imam does to believers. Just as an Imam is *Ṣāliḥ al-Muʾminīn* for believers, Ali ibn Abi Talib is *Ṣāliḥ al-Muʾminīn* for the Imamic succession.

Allah grants glad tidings of paradise to those who follow the righteous believers and spend their lives in the circle of their allegiance.

## None Know Its Interpretation Save Allah and Those Firmly Grounded in Knowledge

When believers are questioned—"Do you claim that the Qur'an possesses meanings beyond the apparent words, meanings rooted in Arabic lexicography yet unknown to the common people, comprehensible only to yourselves?"—the proper response is this: it is not the Ismaili community alone that affirms taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis) and the existence of inner meanings transcending literal translation. The rationalist jurists (Ahl al-Raʾy) and the traditionalist scholars (Ahl al-Ḥadīth) are themselves compelled by rational necessity to accept interpretation in certain verses, for the apparent meaning proves untenable. They abandon literal reading and resort to taʾwīl despite their denial of the method—forced by reason itself. Consider three verses whose surface meaning contradicts the doctrine of divine unity (Tawḥīd):

⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫

"Allah bears witness that none has the right to be worshipped but He, and the angels, and those endowed with knowledge…" To take these literally—that God "witnesses," that angels and the learned "stand"—would enfold the divine in corporeal attributes. Sight, standing, and motion are properties of bodies; the Most Exalted transcends all embodiment. When conventional scholars could offer no resolution, they too took refuge in interpretation. Why then do they deny taʾwīl when the Qur'an itself declares: [^cite-30]

⟪quran 3:7⟫  
⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫

"It is He Who has sent down to you the Book; therein are verses clear and definite—these are the foundation of the Book—and others allegorical. As for those in whose hearts is deviation, they follow the allegorical part, seeking discord and seeking to interpret it. Yet none knows its interpretation save Allah and those firmly grounded in knowledge, who say: 'We believe in it; all is from our Lord.' And none receives admonition save those of understanding."

Their position hinges upon where one places the pause (waqf). They declare: "None knows the interpretation except Allah"—and they stop there, treating the second clause—"and those firmly grounded in knowledge say we believe in it"—as a separate statement. This reading suggests that the *rasikhūn fī al-ʿilm* (the firmly grounded in knowledge) do not themselves possess interpretive knowledge; they merely affirm Allah's knowledge without comprehending it. Yet this interpretation collapses under scrutiny.

To believe means to affirm, and affirmation requires understanding. A person cannot truthfully confirm what he does not comprehend; such assertion becomes mere empty assent. If the firmly grounded in knowledge profess belief in taʾwīl without understanding it, they do not truly affirm—they merely say "yes" to a "yes." But the Qur'an itself declares: [^cite-31]

⟪quran 3:18⟫  
⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫

"Allah bears witness that none has the right to be worshipped but He, and the angels, and those endowed with knowledge bear witness, standing by justice…" Those possessed of knowledge stand with the balance of justice—they cannot stand without understanding.

### The Prophet's Knowledge of the Qur'an

Consider the fundamental question: Did the Messenger of Allah know the Qur'an or not? If he did not—if he could only transmit words without comprehension—would that not betray profound ignorance in the very guide whom Allah appointed? Yet if the Messenger (upon him be blessings and peace) possessed knowledge of the Book's interpretation, then the daʿwa (the invitation to the faith) itself was entrusted to one who understood its depths. When a believer asked the Prophet, "O Messenger of Allah, what is the meaning of such-and-such verse? What is the purpose of this command? What is the wisdom of that prohibition?"—would it not be contemptible for him to reply, "I do not know"? Ignorance is no virtue; it is a flaw. Yet our opponents grant the Prophet was indeed learned in the Qur'an. Then they commit a grave inconsistency: they forbid others to do what the Prophet himself did.

In truth, the Messenger (upon him be peace) stands supreme in knowledge, a leader among the learned. The Qur'an places him in ranks beside the angels themselves: [^cite-32]

⟪quran 41:53⟫  
⟪ar:‏سَنُرِيهِمْ آيَتِنَا فِى ٱلأٓفَاقِ وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ يَتَبَيَّنَ لَهُمْ أَنَّهُ ٱلْحَقُّ ۗ أَوَلَمْ يَكْفِ بِرَبِّكَ أَنَّهُۥ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ شَهِيدٌ ‎⟫

"We shall show them Our signs in the horizons and in themselves until it becomes clear to them that it is the Truth. Is it not sufficient that your Lord is a witness over all things?" Only those possessed of knowledge are elevated to nobility and excellence. Not every scholar is among the perfectly wise, but every perfecter of wisdom is indeed a scholar. The Prophet and his legatee, the Commander of the Faithful ʿAlī, and the Imams who inherit his knowledge are the *rasikhūn fī al-ʿilm*—those in whom knowledge is firmly rooted.

### Rational and Qur'anic Evidence

Through reason and the principle of wisdom, our claim proves true. The Almighty declares:

⟪quran 43:86⟫  
⟪ar:‏وَلَا يَمْلِكُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ مِن دُونِهِ ٱلشَّفَعَةَ إِلَّا مَن شَهِدَ بِٱلْحَقِّ وَهُمْ يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫

"And those whom they invoke besides Him have no power of intercession, except for those who bear witness to the truth and they know it." A saying attributed to the Prophet states: "Allah has founded His religion upon the pattern of His creation, that His creation might be judged by His religion, and His religion by His creation." The Qur'an presents two aspects of the divine sign: the cosmos (ʿāfāq) and the soul (anfus). Similarly, hadith presents creation and religion as paired principles. Both Scripture and Tradition teach that the ordinances of the faith—the path to felicity—mirror the ordinances of creation. The world moves through causation: matter and energy remain inert until an inner stimulus (spirit) moves them. So too do human beings, animals, and plants arise and move through an indwelling principle. Hidden within creation are subtle meanings invisible to the eye yet intelligible to those who perceive. Thus do the heavens, earth, and the establishment of religion stand equal in rank.

### The Four Elements and Dual Benefits

Examine humanity's connection to the earth in two modes: it sustains us as ground beneath our feet, and it produces vegetation that nourishes our bodies. The wind sustains us twofold: we breathe its essence and it affects our sustenance. Fire benefits us doubly: it provides light and heat, and it prepares grain for consumption. Water likewise: in one form we drink it and sustain bodily life; in another, it soaks into earth and produces vegetation—the substance of our livelihood. Remove water, and both human and beast perish.

Contemplate creation in this light, then return to religion—which, according to the Prophet, mirrors creation's pattern. There we find symmetry:

| **The Cosmos** | **Religion** |
|---|---|
| **Action/Effect** | **Deed/Practice** |
| **Body** | **Spirit** |

Action in the temporal world resembles the outer commandment: obedience to explicit law and the performance of worship, as rain descends to refresh the body. Knowledge represents inner water, from which the soul derives intrinsic benefit and receives eternal life. After such investigation, who but the stubborn would deny interpretation and its science?

## The First House Established for Mankind

Allah declares: [^cite-33]

⟪quran 3:96⟫

"Verily, the first House appointed for mankind was that at Bakkah, full of blessing and a guidance for all beings."

The Qur'an is fundamentally a book of explicit verses (muhkamāt). Many verses are clarified, many remain opaque. Verses beginning with *alif-lām* are definite texts in which no ambiguity or disorder enters. These are what Allah has termed "the foundation of the Book." In this verse, the Most High proclaims: "Blessed is this House for the worlds and a guide to them." The plurality of worlds indicates not one realm but many; hence the Qur'an opens with "Praise be to Allah, Lord of the worlds," and the Prophet was sent as "a mercy to the worlds." The House of Allah is the Kaʿba in Mecca, yet this verse specifies it stands at Bakka. The distinction between Mecca and Bakka is profound. The Commander of the Faithful (may Allah be pleased with him) declared: "I am the point beneath the letter *bāʾ*." Whoever recognizes this point knows its reality; whoever grasps the point shifts his gaze from Mecca to Bakka. Mecca signifies the city; Bakka signifies its heart. In an esoteric sense, *Mecca* corresponds to Muḥammad (the outward), *Bakka* to ʿAlī (the inward)—for upon ʿAlī the Kaʿba was spiritually constructed. [^cite-34]

### The Proof of Precedence

For any claim to deserve belief, one must first establish its legitimacy. When a person makes an assertion, he must provide substantiation; without it, his claim becomes groundless. If Allah is asked, "Why do You call Yourself Creator?" He replies, "I have created all the worlds; thus I am the Creator." Similarly, Allah sent 124,000 prophets to this world, and all visited this first House. Had the Almighty established His sanctuaries in different places at different epochs, some prophets would have journeyed to those places while others proceeded to the Kaʿba. Yet history witnesses that all prophets converged upon a single Kaʿba, proving this is the first and preeminent House of Allah.

### The Nobility of Position

At this spot, the reward of a single rakʿa (unit of prayer) is reckoned as 100,000 rakʿāt performed elsewhere. Without facing this House, prayer cannot be accepted. Seventy thousand angels descend daily to this place at a fixed hour, never to ascend again. From the beginning of time, Allah decreed that the Kaʿba shall not move; the entire world shall gravitate toward it. Though ages pass and earth becomes water, water becomes earth, the Kaʿba remains fixed. So too ʿAlī's station: he shall not deviate; all believers must turn toward him and labor in contemplation of his gnosis.

### The Builder of Sanctuaries

All mosques issued from this House; the Kaʿba became the focal point of every sacred place. It is recounted that in Noah's flood, this House of Allah ascended to the fourth heaven. Later, Abraham rebuilt it at God's command. For the remaining mortar after its construction, Allah instructed Abraham through Gabriel to gather it and carry it to heaven, then scatter it across the earth. The day of gathering fell upon Friday; the day of scattering upon Saturday. Where these particles fell, sacred enclosures were erected—mosques of assembly, of stone and lime. [^cite-35]

The *anwār* (inner lights) gather on Fridays and radiate on Saturdays. The Prophet gathered all the lights in ʿAlī; ʿAlī transmitted them to the Imams.

When the culmination of sainthood had been reached, the moment arrived to declare this House the birthplace of the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib. Whoever emerges from this place shall be named ʿAlī; none save ʿAlī shall be born therein. ## Creation of the Heavens and Earth, Darkness and Light

⟪quran 6:1⟫  
⟪ar:‏ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ وَجَعَلَ ٱلظُّلُمَتِ وَٱلنُّورَ ۖ ثُمَّ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ بِرَبِّهِمْ يَعْدِلُونَ ‎⟫

Why does the verse employ the plural for heavens and darkness, yet the singular for light? The apparent interpreters raise an objection: the verse commands the creation of darkness, yet reason teaches that darkness possesses no independent reality—it is merely the absence of light. Through abandonment of the Qur'an's depths, even apparent knowledge has been lost to the apparent worshippers; they flounder in the darkness of ignorance, unable to fathom these matters.

This verse alludes to the outward invitation (daʿwa) and the inward summons—the invitation to the settled and the invitation to the established. The heavens represent the feast of the established; the earth, the feast of believers. Darkness is the outward invitation; light, the inward. The epistemic state of this invitation mirrors light, while outward confusions are described as darkness. The Prophet's outward commandments belong to Allah's Law, and they are many (purity, prayer, fasting, etc.), yet their inner meaning is singular. Therefore, light appears singular, darkness plural. The apparent bestowal in the creation of darkness may now be justified: the outward commands originate from Allah. Thus each servant of the invitation is called the Lord, yet by "Lord" we mean those summoned to the inner world—the Imams, sent by Him. [^cite-37]

The Almighty bestowed upon humanity the blessing of intellect (ʿaql), rendering mankind worthy to be Allah's vicegerent upon earth. Little gratitude is shown for this supreme favor. Despite this blessing, should one neglect the soul's refinement, this very gift becomes a curse. If one fails to cultivate moral virtues, one ruins one's existence. A person's outward morals may appear noble, yet if he is not trained according to the inner invitation, he remains inferior. Just as the prayer of a worshipper lacking the *wilāya* of the Prophet and the love of ʿAlī in his heart is rejected.

## Those Who Deny Our Signs and Are Arrogant Against Them

Allah declares in Sūrat al-Aʿrāf: [^cite-38]

⟪quran 7:40⟫  
⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ كَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَتِنَا وَٱسْتَكْبَرُوا۟ عَنْهَا لَا تُفَتَّحُ لَهُمْ أَبْوَبُ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَلَا يَدْخُلُونَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ حَتَّىٰ يَلِجَ ٱلْجَمَلُ فِى سَمِّ ٱلْخِيَاطِ ۚ وَكَذَلِكَ نَجْزِى ٱلْمُجْرِمِينَ ‎⟫

### The Verses and Their Inner Reality

The verses here refer outwardly to the Qur'anic verses, which polytheists reject; inwardly, they denote the infallible Imams, who elucidate the outer Qur'anic verses and radiate the light of guidance upon earth, as fire and illumination emanate from a luminous stone. These Imams of the Qur'an stand in the rank of soul to the dead body. When they are removed from their station, the path of guidance is severed.

In lexicon, "verse" (*āya*) signifies knowledge—a flag or sign. The infallible Imams are the symbols of guidance and light, directing to eternal survival and immortality. Referring to this inner verse, Allah declares: [^cite-39]

⟪quran 43:86⟫  
⟪ar:‏وَلَا يَمْلِكُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ مِن دُونِهِ ٱلشَّفَعَةَ إِلَّا مَن شَهِدَ بِٱلْحَقِّ وَهُمْ يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫

"Those endowed with knowledge bear witness, standing by justice." The infallible Imams, Allah's signs, are the pillars of His religion. None departs this mortal realm until he establishes an Imam like himself, one of kindred character.

In another place, regarding ʿĪsā and his mother, Allah declares: [^cite-40]

⟪quran 23:50⟫  
⟪ar:‏وَجَعَلْنَا ٱبْنَ مَرْيَمَ وَأُمَّهُۥٓ آيَةًۭ وَآوَيْنَهُمَآ إِلَىٰ رَبْوَةٍۢ ذَاتِ قَرَارٍۢ وَمَعِينٍۢ ‎⟫

"We made the son of Mary and his mother a sign, and gave them refuge on a mound with a resting place and flowing water."

The Commander of the Faithful, proclaiming his excellence, states: "I am a clear sign!" He employs the plural because all Imams who are signs descend from him. You are their synthesis and their foremost.

### Arrogance Against the Verses

The argument proving that the verses denote the Imams rather than the Qur'anic text is this: Allah declares that people were arrogant *against* these verses. The apparent verses of the Qur'an may be denied or refuted, but words composed of letters possess no consciousness; arrogance toward lifeless phrases is nonsensical. Arrogance is directed toward persons of greatness and power. What meaning has arrogance against what is inanimate and self-unaware?

### The Gates of Heaven

Then, setting forth the penalty, Allah says: "The gates of heaven shall not be opened to them." The sky is the realm of bright stars; from it descends the sustenance of servants as rain and water. Allah elevated the heavens above earthly bodies, casting shadow upon them. Bodies cannot reach to touch those realms. Yet humans, being subtle and spiritual, readily ascend to those heavens and gain knowledge thereof, completely mastering them. The outer heavens dominate human bodies; the human soul dominates the outer heavens. But Allah created contrasting heavens that dominate the human soul as the outer heavens dominate the body. These are the saints of heaven, Allah's holy ones—majestic, noble, miraculous. Their states affect the soul for good or ill. Their gates are opened to believers; veils are lifted. Inner lights are kindled; spiritual rains descend. Thus this warning against those denying Allah is inward, not outward. [^cite-41]

### Entry into Paradise

Paradise (*janna*) derives from *janana*, *jinān*, *ajtanan*, *jinn*—all bearing hidden meanings because none are visible to the naked eye. A fetus (*janīn*) is a child concealed in the mother's womb. *Ajtanan* means sheltering behind a shield. A *jinn* is a nation invisible to sight. Paradise likewise denotes a garden whose dense, fruited shrubs block the sun's heat.

There are two paradises: potential and actual. Sperm placed in the mother's womb becomes human; when it grows there under the womb's beneficial effects, protected from harm, and is born by Allah's command, it becomes a human being. Similarly, the invitation of the Family of Muḥammad is a potential paradise leading to the actual. Allah says of Paradise: "Its width equals the width of the heavens and earth combined."

### The Camel Passing Through the Needle's Point

The verse concludes: "Until the camel passes through the needle's eye." The camel is praised in some scriptural passages and condemned in others. Though praiseworthy in many respects, the Prophet declared: "One should not pray when a camel stands before him, for every camel harbors a devil within." Apparently this disparages the camel; yet examined by the standard of wisdom, the hadith's truth emerges.

The camel is a heavy-bodied, burden-bearing creature. Metaphorically, it denotes a person appearing lofty through outward observance of law, display of piety, and hypocrisy, who walks with a stiffened neck. The marks of prostration on his forehead deceive people into thinking he bears the Shariah's weight. He possesses prominence among the masses through his height. Yet truly, he rivals and opposes the Prophet. People mistake his hypocrisy for his caliphate. Here the arrogant is likened to the camel. Allah says: "He shall not enter Paradise until the camel has passed through the needle's eye."

## Did Not the Disbelievers See That the Heavens and Earth Were Joined?

Allah declares in Sūrat al-Anbiyāʾ: [^cite-46]

⟪quran 21:30⟫  
⟪ar:‏أَوَلَمْ يَرَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ أَنَّ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ كَانَتَا رَتْقًۭا فَفَتَقْنَهُمَا ۖ وَجَعَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلْمَآءِ كُلَّ شَىْءٍ حَىٍّ ۖ أَفَلَا يُؤْمِنُونَ ‎⟫

"Have not the disbelievers seen that the heavens and earth were one mass, which We then tore asunder? And We made from water every living thing. Will they not believe?"

A question arises: when the disbelievers did not exist at the time the heavens and earth were created, how could they witness that they were once joined and then separated? When mankind had not yet been brought forth, the category of believer and disbeliever had no meaning.

When the scholars of the apparent could offer no fitting interpretation, they declared: "These are Allah's mysteries, and we need not apply our intellect to them. We must accept without reflection. It suffices us to say: the Messenger of Allah came with a truth and never lied. If he said it, it is true." Yet the root of all these defects lies here: after the Prophet, the guardians of the Shariah were ignorant ones. They first declared human reason and religion to be adversaries, forbade the study of rational sciences and the understanding of rationalities, and issued verdicts of disbelief against those who questioned the inner nature and character of religion. Had the people instead turned to the heirs of the Prophet's knowledge—those who uphold the Book and the Balance of Justice—all their beliefs would have been clarified, all knots untied, and the most difficult questions answered with ease.

### The Meaning of the Verse

The verse signifies that both heavens and earth were hidden and implicit in the Word of Allah. Then We manifested them in deed, as Allah declares: [^cite-47]

⟪quran 16:40⟫  
⟪ar:‏إِنَّمَا قَوْلُنَا لِشَىْءٍ إِذَآ أَرَدْنَهُ أَن نَّقُولَ لَهُۥ كُن فَيَكُونُ ‎⟫

"When We will a thing to be, We say to it: 'Be,' and it is."

When we desire to bring something into existence or manifestation, it comes to be at our command. It emerges from non-being into actuality, from potentiality to function. While in potential state, it is *ratq* (undifferentiated, formless); when manifested, it is *fataqa* (distinct, shaped). This differentiation—this bringing-forth of form and manifestation—is implicit in the command. What is the reality by which non-existence becomes existence? Whose command transforms one state into another?

There exists also a spiritual heaven and earth contrasting with the physical. The inner heaven is the reality of the Prophet (upon him be peace); the inner earth is his guardian, his legatee. The outer heaven sends water; the outer earth absorbs it and produces vegetation. Similarly, the Prophet imparts knowledge to his legatee, who preserves it in his heart and through this knowledge nourishes believers' souls. As the outer heavens and earth produce only flesh, blood, and bone—all destined to perish—so the inner heavens and earth grant eternal life and survival to human souls. Their stations and ranks exceed the physical heavens and earth immeasurably. [^cite-48]

The disbelievers and polytheists, and then the hypocrites, witnessed with their own eyes that the Prophet remained silent, uttering nothing, for he was devoid of divine support. He had not yet preached—he was in a state of silence, of potential. But when We bestowed upon him Our revelation and aid, he began calling to Allah and passed into actuality. Thence came forth the successor and the Imams, who are as the earth, and thus the heavens and earth were separated.

What is the level of divine revelation by which the Prophet became a prophet and the inner heaven became real? Similarly, his legatee too was in a state of potential, which the Prophet brought to actuality through his knowledge, establishing him as legatee. The Prophet declared: "I and ʿAlī are from a single light." From this hadith we know that ʿAlī's light was hidden within Muḥammad's light, and heaven and earth were joined. When the Prophet said to ʿAlī at Ghadīr: "Whoever takes me as his guardian takes ʿAlī as his guardian," appointing him as his successor, ʿAlī's light became manifest, and heaven and earth were separated!

Then the heavens rained upon them spiritual and intellectual grace from Allah's command, making the earth verdant, becoming the source of life for the human soul. The Prophet and the Imams were the first to call to Allah—to pass from silence and potential to action and actuality—and began their affairs. Abū Lahab, Abū Jahl, Abū Sufyān, Muʿāwiya, Ibn Khattāb, and Abū Bakr witnessed all these events with their eyes. Allah says: "When We appointed ʿAlī as successor and separated the guardian's status (the earth) from the guardian's station (the heavens), did not the disbelievers see?"

## The Eternal and the Perishable

Everything whose existence is eternal and firmly established is identical with God alone. That which is subject to corruption and dissolution cannot be Allah. The Almighty declares:

⟪quran 28:88⟫
⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَدْعُ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ إِلَهًا آخَرَ ۘ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ كُلُّ شَىْءٍ هَالِكٌ إِلَّا وَجْهَهُۥ ۚ لَهُ ٱلْحُكْمُ وَإِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ ‎⟫

The phrase "His Face" (wajhahu) requires careful exposition. The face of any object is that aspect through which the object becomes known and recognizable—that dimension from which its true reality is most readily apprehended. In this created universe, that which endures imperishably from eternity to eternity is religion itself, specifically the religion of resilience and constancy (al-dīn al-Matīn) through which humanity attains tawhīd (the recognition of God's absolute unity).[^cite-49] This religion has persisted across all epochs and has been brought by every prophet in succession, until the Seal of the Messengers conveyed it in its perfected form as Islam.

Because this religion achieves consummate perfection, it is incorruptible and shall remain eternally. The prophets—from Adam to the end of the age—have invited humanity toward this single, unchanging reality. The mystery of Islam is concealed within the seven letters of *lā ilāha illā 'llāh* (there is no god save God), each letter corresponding to a Rational Principle (ʿaql):

| L | Ā | I | L | Ā | H | L | L | Ā | H |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| *Adam* | *Noah* | *Abraham* | *Moses* | *Jesus* | *Muhammad* | | | | |

Islam constitutes the means (*sabab*) through which we come to know Allah. Since we have received and understood Islam and faith from the Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace and blessings), the Messenger himself becomes the intermediary through whom God's knowledge reaches us.[^cite-50] When the community accepts the Messenger as the conduit of divine guidance, it logically follows that the Messenger's successor and the rightful Caliph—standing firm with divine knowledge and summoning humanity toward truth and understanding—likewise deserves to be recognized as a means (*sabab*) of divine guidance.

Just as the face of an object reveals the true reality of that thing, so too do these saints of Allah (awliyāʾ Allāh) impart to us the authentic knowledge of God's absolute unity. In this capacity, they merit the designation of divine causality (*sabab Allāh*).

There exists no conceivable pathway to comprehend the true nature of the Creator, for such knowledge is impossible: the Creator is Necessary Being (wājib al-wujūd), while man is contingent existence (mumkin al-wujūd). Between the two lies no connection by nature. Yet man, by virtue of being the noblest of creatures, was deemed worthy of a singular grace: God caused him to know His Face—that is, the perfect religion and knowledge of the Blessed One—through which he was vouchsafed communion with saints, prophets, angels, the Throne, the Footstool, Paradise, and all the spiritual realms and mysteries of divinity.[^cite-51]

It is through these veilings and divine mysteries that certain divine attributes become knowable. This is why the Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace and blessings) declared: "Whoso knows his own soul knows his Lord." Since the soul itself is a spiritual entity, one who has not gained knowledge of the creature can never attain knowledge of the Creator. The creature stands closer to human perception, and hence is more readily understood.

It has been established that religion and its teachings are indeed the eternal cause (sabab) through which God's unity is apprehended—imperishable, incorruptible, yielding no corruption or ruin. Only the person who clings to this permanent remainder will be absorbed into it and endure eternally. Alluding to this eternity and permanence, Allah declares:

⟪quran 4:69⟫
⟪ar:‏وَمَن يُطِعِ ٱللَّهَ وَٱلرَّسُولَ فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ مَعَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنْعَمَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيْهِم مِّنَ ٱلنَّبِيِّۦنَ وَٱلصِّدِّيقِينَ وَٱلشُّهَدَآءِ وَٱلصَّلِحِينَ ۚ وَحَسُنَ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ رَفِيقًۭا ‎⟫

The righteous person (*al-sālih*) is one in whom no corruption or defilement finds lodging. In the exegesis of this verse, the Prophet, the truthful one (al-siddīq), and the martyr (*al-shahīd*) are mentioned as interpretations and manifestations of the truly righteous (*al-sālihīn*).

### Scholarly and Practical Worship

The religion of resilience comprises two modes of worship. The first is scholarly worship (*ʿibādat ʿilmiyya*), and the second is practical worship, fulfilled through the observance of the Sharīʿah's prescriptions. Similarly, the human being is composed of two elements: soul and body. From the philosophical and mystical perspective, the soul represents the aggregation of the body's noble faculties—abstract and immaterial in nature. The faculties of reasoning, comprehension, and knowledge are functions of the soul, not the body. For this reason, the knowledge that comes from recognizing divine unity belongs to the soul and not to the flesh. This constitutes scholarly worship, which pertains to the human soul and merits cultivation because of the soul's nobility, virtue, and dignity.

The body, by contrast, is in itself inert and lacks the faculty or capacity to achieve understanding or attain knowledge. Therefore, it has been made incumbent upon the body to observe practical worship, which disciplines corporeal existence, so that through the perfection of bodily obedience, piety, and ritual observance, the body becomes worthy of participating in the worship of knowledge.

*Drawn from al-Majālis al-Muʾayyadiyya (The Sessions of al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)*

## The Heart of the Qurʾān and the Cosmic Orbits

Sūrah Yāsīn is the heart of the Qur'ān, and the heart of Sūrah Yāsīn is precisely this verse—though not positioned at the geometrical center, but rather two verses removed from the midpoint, just as the human heart resides not in the anatomical center of the body but slightly toward the left.

The remarkable property of this verse—called "Kullun fī falakin yasbaḥūn" (all swim in their orbits)—is that whether recited in its correct sequence or read in reverse, it maintains its meaning and form:

⟪quran 36:40⟫
⟪ar:‏لَا ٱلشَّمْسُ يَنۢبَغِى لَهَآ أَن تُدْرِكَ ٱلْقَمَرَ وَلَا ٱلَّيْلُ سَابِقُ ٱلنَّهَارِ ۚ وَكُلٌّۭ فِى فَلَكٍۢ يَسْبَحُونَ ‎⟫

The term "orbit" (*falak*) denotes a circular path. No celestial body can transgress the boundaries of its prescribed sphere. In the same manner, every rank within the summons (*daʿwa*) to faith—whether the Makāsir (the highest rank), the Mazun (the deputy), the dāʿī (the Summoner), or others—operates within a defined sphere of authority.[^cite-52] Each must remain faithfully within the bounds of his station. Should anyone attempt to exceed his prescribed limit, the order of the summons becomes disrupted, precisely as if the planets were to abandon their orbits, the cosmos would convulse and its equilibrium would be shattered.

*(Instruction from Mullā Saʿdullah ibn Hazrat Nasrullah Sahib)*

## The Reward of Prophethood and Love of the Kin

Once the people of Medina approached the Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) bearing vessels filled with precious gems. They offered substantial portions of their wealth to the Lord of all creation, saying: "You have labored greatly on our behalf; you have delivered us from perdition and guided us toward Paradise. You have transformed us from enemies into brothers. We implore you: accept this tribute as recompense for your prophetic mission." The Prophet remained silent. Then Allah revealed this verse:

⟪quran 42:23⟫
⟪ar:‏ذَلِكَ ٱلَّذِى يُبَشِّرُ ٱللَّهُ عِبَادَهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ ۗ قُل لَّآ أَسْـَٔلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلَّا ٱلْمَوَدَّةَ فِى ٱلْقُرْبَىٰ ۗ وَمَن يَقْتَرِفْ حَسَنَةًۭ نَّزِدْ لَهُۥ فِيهَا حُسْنًا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ شَكُورٌ ‎⟫

Herein Allah addresses the Prophet, saying: "Tell the people: I ask of you no recompense for my prophethood save only love for my kindred." Where there dwells love, obedience follows; where obedience flourishes, earnest devotion takes root. This verse recalls that love which the Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) sealed at Ghadir Khumm when he proclaimed the mastership of ʿAlī.

### The Gravity of This Request

There was a time when the disbelievers of Mecca brought before the Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) the gold of Mount Abū Qubais, offering him wealth and dominion on condition that he abandon his prophetic claim. He cast aside all such things, declaring: "Though you place the sun in one palm and the moon in the other, I shall not forsake the message of Allah." If one who received such abundance without requesting it spurned it utterly, then when that same person makes a request, surely it must be of supreme consequence. The Prophet asked only for love of the Ahl al-Bayt (the Household of the Prophet). This reveals that the love of the Prophet's family surpasses all the treasures, splendor, and dominion of the world.[^cite-53]

### The Universe Created from Love of the Panjtan Pak

The cause and purpose of this entire universe is nothing but love for the Five Pure Ones (Panjtan Pak). Every being fashions and acquires what it loves according to its nature. Allah has brought forth this vast cosmos out of love for these five sanctified souls. He declared: "I have not raised the vault of heaven, nor spread the foundations of earth, nor kindled the luminaries of sun and moon, nor set in motion the turning spheres, nor created the surging seas and sailing ships—except out of love for these five pure beings robed in the Cloak of Prophecy and adorned with absolute purity."

This resplendent creation came into being solely through love of the Ahl al-Bayt, as testified by Allah's words: "Had it not been for you, O Prophet, I would not have created the heavens." The Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace and blessings) proclaimed: "Allah first created my light; then He created the light of all the worlds. Then He said: 'I and ʿAlī are from a single light.' From our light came forth the earth and heavens; from our light descended the light of beauty and splendor, from which the sun and moon were formed." At that moment, the light of Mawlānā Fātimah al-Zahrāʾ (upon her be peace) came into being—hence she is called *Mashiyyat Allāh* (the Will of Allah), the expression of God's desire and purpose.[^cite-54]

By divine grace, all things have been created for these five holy ones. Yet how presumptuous would it be for us earthbound servants to fashion anything out of love for the Panjtan Pak? We ourselves are creatures; what power have we? Man is created weak in essence. Our only recourse is to grasp the Strong Rope of Allah (*al-ʿurwa al-wuthqā*) and to love the Ahl al-Bayt of the Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) with undivided hearts. The Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) declared: "Adorn your gatherings with remembrance of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, for remembrance of ʿAlī is my remembrance, and my remembrance is the remembrance of Allah, and remembrance of Allah is worship itself." Thus has the mention of ʿAlī become a form of devotion. He declared elsewhere:

> O ʿAlī! Allah has granted the Prophets and Messengers superiority over the angels, and He has granted me superiority over all the Prophets and Messengers. After me, O ʿAlī, this excellence belongs to you and to the Imāms who shall come after you. The angels who bear the Throne of God and the angels who glorify God around the Throne beseech forgiveness for those who recognize our authority (wilāya).

### The Weight of the Request

This is no trivial petition. Rather, it is a request that carries absolute consequence: whoever does not render this due reward to the Prophet's mission has in effect insulted his prophetic office and shall surely be called to account upon the Day of Resurrection. It is significant that a request is made only when none gives willingly without being asked. This indicates that when the Messenger of Allah completed his proclamation, he awaited the people's voluntary recompense; but seeing that none came forward, the Messenger was commanded by Allah to request the reward of his prophethood.[^cite-55]

A second reason for this request lay in the Prophet's knowledge of the future through divine insight: he foresaw ʿAlī's martyrdom, Fātimah's tribulation, Hasan's poisoning, and Husayn's beheading. He thus addressed the people, saying: "If you would make any return for my prophetic mission, then show mercy to my kinfolk and cause them no harm." This command descended from Allah so that the persecution of the Ahl al-Bayt might be stayed.

### Compensation as the Basis of Permissibility

Nothing is lawful without proper compensation and recompense. Should a person perform the prayer while clothed in stolen garments or unwashed raiment, can that prayer be valid? Consider then: if a prayer is void without correct attire, what becomes of the prayer when one refuses to render the due compensation to the one who brings the message itself? Every ordinance of the Sharīʿah constitutes part of the Prophet's proclamation. If that proclamation receives no recompense, then no legal act proceeds from it can be accepted.[^cite-56]

### The Discourse of Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq on Unlawful Gain

Once Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (upon him be peace) was traveling through the market when he witnessed a man steal two pomegranates from one shop, then two loaves of bread from another, before proceeding to a corner and distributing these four items to a poor and destitute person. The Imām questioned him regarding this deed, and the man replied: "O son of the Messenger of Allah, you know that in God's law, the reward for one good deed is tenfold, while the punishment for one sin is but one. I have stolen four items—four sins are recorded in my ledger. I then gave these four items to the poor—forty good deeds are written. Thus from forty good deeds, four sins are subtracted, leaving me with thirty-six good deeds to my account."

The Imām responded: "May your mother be bereft of you! You are heedless of God's Book and His command. Your reckoning is false! The correct calculation is this: you stole four items, multiplying your sins fourfold. You did not render to the owner his rightful due, thereby misappropriating his property. Thereafter you gave what was never yours to give to one to whom you had no right to bestow it. Thus four additional sins are inscribed upon you for failure to render the due, and you have gained no good whatsoever—rather, your transgression is eight-fold!"

Consider the implication: if those who benefit from the Messenger's proclamation do not render him his due reward, what becomes of all the observances of the Sharīʿah they have received from him? Allah has commanded the Muslims to perform the prayer, give the poor-tax (zakāt), fast, undertake pilgrimage, and wage struggle—each of these possesses independent efficacy. Should one pray but not fast, the prayer yields its reward even though one is questioned about the fast. But when it comes to love of the Ahl al-Bayt—the very compensation for prophethood itself—if the Messenger's reward is withheld, then none of the legal prescriptions derived from the Messenger will bear fruit. Therefore, it is absolutely unlawful to dispose of another's property without rendering due compensation.

### The Light Refers to the Five Pure Ones

Allah declares:

⟪quran 64:8⟫
⟪ar:‏فَـَٔامِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلنُّورِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلْنَا ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫

Some commentators claim that "light" (*nūr*) refers to the Qur'ān. Yet consider: if light means the Qur'ān, why does Allah mention the Book separately in the subsequent verse?

⟪quran 5:15⟫
⟪ar:‏يَٓأَهْلَ ٱلْكِتَبِ قَدْ جَآءَكُمْ رَسُولُنَا يُبَيِّنُ لَكُمْ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا كُنتُمْ تُخْفُونَ مِنَ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَيَعْفُوا۟ عَن كَثِيرٍۢ ۚ قَدْ جَآءَكُم مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ نُورٌۭ وَكِتَبٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫

This verse makes clear that light and the Book are distinct entities! In the first verse, the Messenger and the Light are mentioned; in the second, the Light and the Book appear—demonstrating that light is a third thing, distinct from both the Messenger and the Scripture, being neither messenger nor book.

The Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace and blessings) proclaimed: "I and ʿAlī are of a single light." This establishes that ʿAlī (upon him be peace) is a light. He declared: "Fātimah is from me"—hence if the Messenger is the light, then Fātimah too is light. To Hasan and Husayn he said: "Their flesh is my flesh, and their blood is my blood"—thus Hasan and Husayn share in the light of the Prophet. The light encompasses these Five Pure Ones.

### The Meaning of Kinship

Kinship is of two kinds: temporary and eternal. Temporary kinship—such as the bond between husband and wife—subsists only so long as both remain united; divorce severs all connection. Similarly, the relationship between a house and its occupant is transient, as are all friendships, which may be sundered tomorrow. Eternal kinship, by contrast, embraces the relationships of mother, father, sibling, brother, sister, aunt, uncle—bonds of blood immutable and perpetual. In the Prophet's request for recompense, it is essential that we understand his true kindred: those who are the People of Purity, united to him by eternal bonds of relationship.

Shortly after this verse's revelation, someone asked the Messenger: "O Messenger of Allah! Whom did you bring to the Mubāhalah (the Curse Invocation), and for whom was this verse revealed?" He answered: "Myself, ʿAlī, Fātimah, Hasan, and Husayn." Thus he established Fātimah al-Zahrāʾ as the axis around which all kinship revolves—at once daughter, wife, and mother, embodying every form of relationship.

### When Is Recompense Demanded?

Religion has forever remained Islam in the sight of Allah. The Prophet brought no new faith; rather, he conveyed the same religion that began with Adam. Though ordinances transformed and laws were refined across ages, the very name "Islam" has endured unchanging from its inception until now and shall so remain. This name has been linked to prophets in succession: at times to Adam, then to Noah, then to Abraham the Friend of Allah, then to Moses the Interlocutor of Allah, then to Jesus the Spirit of Allah. The final union of this name was with Muḥammad the Messenger of Allah—yet it could not be perfected without ʿAlī, the Authority of Allah. This is the very ʿAlī who struck his chest and declared: "Within me reside the scholars," and who proclaimed from the pulpit: "Ask of me before you lose me."

Before the Messenger of Allah, one hundred twenty-four thousand prophets came forth; yet none demanded recompense for their prophethood or messengership. Only the Messenger of Allah requested that his people render him the reward of his prophetic mission. This merits deep reflection. A laborer requests his wages only upon completing his work; when work remains unfinished, no compensation is sought. Throughout the ages preceding Islam, the religion continuously progressed toward perfection—hence no earlier prophet demanded recompense. It was during the time of the Messenger of Allah that Islam achieved its consummate perfection, its ordinances were fully codified, and when he proclaimed at Ghadir Khumm, "Whomsoever I am the authority over, ʿAlī too is his authority," Allah revealed this verse, which shall shine eternally: "This day have I perfected your religion for you, and chosen Islam as the best way for you." At that moment the work of his prophethood was complete, and he requested his reward—a recompense not for himself alone but for all the prophets who had labored to bring Islam to its final form.[^cite-57]

### The Distinction Between the Community and the Kinfolk

The Commander and the one who receives the command are distinct. Allah is He who commands the Messenger, and the Messenger is he who commands the people. Allah cannot be a Messenger, nor can a Messenger be Allah. Similarly, the community (*ummah*) renders the recompense to the kindred (*qurbā*), while the kindred receive it—they are not interchangeable. The community differs from the kinfolk. The community cannot become the kindred, nor the kindred become prophets. Here the community is instructed to love the Prophet's kinfolk as compensation, because the message of prophethood was preached to the community, not to the kinfolk. Had the prophetic mission been entrusted to the kinfolk, they too would have fallen under this command and would have been asked for recompense.

### The People's Understanding of the Verse

Wages are demanded from those who have received service. The Messenger's mission was not entrusted to him by the community; rather, it came from Allah, who sent him as a messenger. Therefore, only Allah can render recompense to the Messenger of Allah. By what right does the community owe the Messenger anything? The community stands in need of the Messenger's intercession—what recompense can it offer? Thus it becomes evident that the community cannot possibly render due recompense to the Messenger. The Qur'ān itself declares:

⟪quran 34:47⟫
⟪ar:‏قُلْ مَا سَأَلْتُكُم مِّنْ أَجْرٍۢ فَهُوَ لَكُمْۖ إِنْ أَجْرِىَ إِلَّا عَلَى ٱللَّهِۖ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ شَهِيدٌۭ ‎⟫

A group from the Literalists (Ahl al-Ẓāhir) claimed that this verse abrogates the former, arguing: "In this verse, Allah declares that the Messenger's reward comes from Allah alone." Therefore, Muslims need only treat their relatives and kinfolk with kindness—no more is required. This cannot be correct under any circumstance. Countless people in the community have kinfolk who are unbelievers. How could it be that Allah will reward the Messenger for the community's love of disbelievers?

They have led themselves and others astray. This second verse does not abrogate the first; rather, it supports and affirms it. The true meaning is this: the community will alone benefit from the reward which the Messenger (upon him be peace and blessings) has asked of them in the form of love for his Ahl al-Bayt. Neither the Prophet nor the Ahl al-Bayt will benefit from such love—for the Prophet and his House are rich in spiritual attainment, while the community is poor. Without their guidance, paradise remains unattainable; whoever turns from the Ahl al-Bayt finds paradise distant and hell near. Allah alone renders recompense to the Messenger and the Ahl al-Bayt.

### Conclusion

Just as every sūrah of the Qur'ān is blessed and reliable—whether one as grand as Sūrat al-Baqarah or as brief as Sūrat al-Kawthar—so too among the Ahl al-Bayt (upon them be peace), all are worthy of respect and love, whether they be Muslim ibn ʿAqīl, Hānī, Qāsim, and ʿAbbās (resembling the shorter chapters), or Imām Hasan and Imām Husayn (resembling the longer ones). The love and authority (*wilāya*) of all has been enjoined upon us by the Messenger of Allah.

### The True Fruit of the Reward for Prophethood

The recompense for prophethood is love for the Imāms of divine authority—the descendants of the Commander of the Faithful—without which no spiritual beauty bears fruit. The Qur'ān has reached all Muslims, yet faith has not entered their hearts. It is only those who render this recompense of prophethood and practice the authority (*wilāya*) of the Commander of the Faithful and the Ahl al-Bayt (upon them be peace) who receive faith into their hearts. Those who neglect this authority cannot achieve true belief.

*From the assembly of Hazrat Ẓiyāʾ ʿAlī, may Allah sanctify his spirit*

## Forgiveness of Prior and Subsequent Lapses

When Sūrat al-Fatḥ was revealed, the Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) experienced great joy, for within it lie many hidden excellences. The sūrah itself possesses great merit when recited each dawn. It contains mention of the Clear Conquest (Fatḥ al-Mubīn)—the victory manifest in the external sense through which Allah granted him triumph. Yet beyond all else, this verse points to a profound truth: that Allah forgave the Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace and blessings) for his prior and subsequent transgressions.

⟪quran 48:2⟫
⟪ar:‏لِّيَغْفِرَ لَكَ ٱللَّهُ مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِن ذَنۢبِكَ وَمَا تَأَخَّرَ وَيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكَ وَيَهْدِيَكَ صِرَطًۭا مُّسْتَقِيمًۭا ‎⟫

In elucidating the profound meaning of this verse, the Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) himself declared: "Muḥammad is exalted and elevated, immaculate and purified (free from all error and transgression)." The "Muḥammad" mentioned in this verse is one of the veils of the prophetic summons (*daʿwa*), appearing as the covering of Muḥammad (upon him be peace and blessings). Such veils—when ascending toward perfection—are not protected from error; they may stumble in understanding.

### Types of Veils

The veils of the Imāms, which are formed as boundaries while advancing toward the divine station, do not fall within the sphere of protection from error. They are liable to mistake. Those veils, however, which are fashioned of the substance of nostalgia and longing—they dwell within the realm of perfect preservation and stand free from all transgression.

### The Prior Transgression

His prior transgression consisted in his inclination toward Abū Bakr—that he should be given a place in the inner summons so that both the external and inward aspects of the summons would be safeguarded. Yet it was the decree of Allah's nature that Abū Bakr should not be granted even a place in the inner summons. Therefore, Gabriel came to the Prophet bearing a grave warning from Allah, saying: "Should you associate anyone with ʿAlī in the authority of the inner summons, Allah will render all your deeds void." At that, the Messenger of Allah accepted the divine will, yet his inclination toward Abū Bakr had already been established in his heart. Though this inclination did not spring from disobedience to Allah—indeed, it arose from the Messenger's desire to preserve the summons—it nevertheless became in Allah's sight a transgression.[^cite-58]

### A Lesson for the Believers

Here lies a profound teaching for the faithful: the Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace and blessings), who proclaimed the divine message for twenty-three years and endured every hardship, beloved of Allah as none other, was addressed thus: "Should you grant to anyone the station of ʿAlī, We shall nullify all your works!" The believer contemplating this must tremble to consider: if the community were to mingle love for the enemies of ʿAlī with love for ʿAlī himself, what would Allah do to their deeds? (May Allah's grace be upon us!)

### The Subsequent Transgression

The latter transgression of the Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) consisted in delaying the proclamation of ʿAlī's authority. When he returned from ʿArafāt after performing the Ḥajj, revelations descended upon him with such force that his camel repeatedly knelt beneath the burden of the divine word. That revelation concerned the proclamation of ʿAlī's authority. The command "Deliver what has been revealed to you from your Lord" had already begun to press upon him, yet he sought the opportune moment to make the proclamation. Because of this delay, the moment passed, and the caravans departed from the valley of ʿArafāt. When the Messenger reached Ghadir Khumm, Allah addressed him with terrible severity:

⟪quran 5:67 (paraphrase)⟫

*O Messenger! Convey what has been revealed to you from your Lord. And if you do not, you have not conveyed His message.*

The very revelation that rang out was: "Convey what has been revealed to you concerning ʿAlī from your Lord"—though the hypocrites afterward erased ʿAlī's name from the verse.

### A Harsh Word from Allah to His Beloved

The Messenger, upon whom Allah bestowed throughout his life the titles of honor—Muzammil, Muddaththir, Ṭāhā, Yāsīn—is here addressed as "O Messenger!" (*yā ayyuhā al-rasūl*). This constituted a most stern rebuke from Allah to His own Messenger.[^cite-59]

The moment this verse descended and the Prophet received the glad tidings of Allah's protection for his religion, he caused the people to cease their journey. He recalled those who had gone ahead and waited for the stragglers, gathering all together. The meaning of this gathering, as the Summoners explain it, is profound: at that place, all the prophets of former ages and the spiritual templates of their nations assembled; and all the forms that would emerge after him until the Day of Judgment awaited this gathering.

When the appointed hour came, he proclaimed ʿAlī's authority, declaring: "Whomsoever I am the authority over, ʿAlī too is his authority." Thus he took from all those present—from past and future generations alike—the oath of allegiance to ʿAlī's divine authority. Yet the delay in so doing was a sin in Allah's sight. In this verse, Allah forgives the Muḥammad of the veil (the Messenger in his prophetic office) for these two transgressions.[^cite-60]

*From the lessons of al-Rawḍah al-Jannāt (The Garden of Paradise), as related by Syedna Jaʿfar, transmitted through Mullā Ghulām Ḥusayn Sahib*

## Divine Proximity and the Soul's Three Stations

⟪quran 50:16⟫
⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا ٱلْإِنسَنَ وَنَعْلَمُ مَا تُوَسْوِسُ بِهِۦ نَفْسُهُۥ ۖ وَنَحْنُ أَقْرَبُ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ حَبْلِ ٱلْوَرِيدِ ‎⟫

We have created man, and We know all that his soul whispers to him. And We are nearer to him than his jugular vein.

A profound question arises: how can a Being who transcends the limits of time and space, who stands infinitely above the categories of near and far, be "near to the jugular vein" of the human neck? If this refers to the essence of Allah, would not the singular form "I am" (*ana*) be more fitting than the plural "We"? What purpose is served by the use of the plural pronoun?

Some commentators dismiss this as mere eloquence, claiming nothing is amiss. They offer an analogy: a solitary king, when addressing his subjects, refers to himself in the plural, saying "We command!" But what a blemish upon true monotheism is this explanation!

Syedna Ghulām Ḥusayn Sahib would transmit that the Sultan of the Knowers of Allah (*Sulṭān al-ʿĀrifīn*) has declared: "Whosoever among you understands his own soul more intimately understands his Lord more profoundly."

We imagine the soul to be singular, yet upon reflection we discover three dimensions within it. The lowest degree of the soul manifests as vegetation—the power of growth. Beyond this, the miracle of life is expressed through sensation—the animal soul perceive the world through the senses. But transcending both these is the rational soul, the human faculty—and it is this third aspect that constitutes the true manifestation of humanity. Nay, it is humanity itself—the self that attains knowledge of the Creator and penetrates the secrets of the universe; thus is man the noblest of creatures.[^cite-61]

Addressing this human being, the Almighty asks: "Where do you wander in search of truth when truth dwells within you?" You yourself are a repository of mysteries. The Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) declared in a sacred ḥadīth: "*al-Insān sirī wa-ana sirru-hu*"—Man is My secret, and I am his secret. The Prophet himself conveyed: "Understand the allusion within this verse!"

The three souls correspond to three principles: *Nūn* (*n*) signifies *Namiyyah* (growth); *Hāʾ* (*h*) signifies *Ḥassāsiyyah* (sensation); and *Nūn* (*n*) signifies *Nāṭiqah* (rationality).

| N | H | N |
|---|---|---|
| *Namiyyah* (Growth) | *Ḥassāsiyyah* (Sensation) | *Nāṭiqah* (Rationality) |

Ponder these three souls! The plural "We" (*naḥn*) encompasses these three dimensions. Understand the powers of the soul, grasp their proximity, and then the soul will perceive the soul of the cosmos—will understand that the soul stands nearer to the human being than the divine essence itself! After having arrived at this knowledge, he will declare with truth: "I have not rendered the due right of knowing You," and he will understand aright that the inability to perceive constitutes true knowledge.*

*(From Nakhbat al-Kalām [The Selection of Discourse], as transmitted by Syedna Ghulām Ḥusayn Sahib)*

## None Shall Touch It Save the Purified

Allah says in the Holy Qur'an:

⟪ar:‏لَّا يَمَسُّهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱلْمُطَهَّرُونَ ‎⟫
⟪quran 56:79⟫

In these verses, the Almighty declares that none may touch the Qur'an except those who are purified. At first blush, this statement appears problematic: for unbelievers also handle the Qur'an, yet remain unpurified. This apparent contradiction dissolves once we recognize that the touching (*yamass*) mentioned here does not denote outward, physical contact, but rather the inward comprehension of meaning.[^cite-62] The verse addresses not the contingencies of letter and page, but the penetration into the Qur'an's inner reality—a faculty reserved to those whom God has cleansed of heedlessness.

### The Body of the Qur'an: Words and Form

The topics of the Qur'an rest upon a twofold division: the external and the internal; the manifest and the hidden. Just as creation itself divides into this world and the Hereafter, and the human being into body and soul, so too does the Qur'an manifest this sacred duality. The outer dimension remains in constant flux and transformation; the inner dimension abides in an unchanging state. The changes that befall the body—the passage from youth to strength to weakness, from health to illness—mirror the temporal flux that seasons undergo: winter yields to heat; spring turns to autumn. Similarly, in the Qur'an, which is embodied in letters and words, profound differences abound. Although it is a book of guidance, its outward forms occasion far more perplexity than certainty. Allah declares at the Qur'an's opening:

⟪ar:‏إِنَّهُۥ لَقُرْآنٌۭ كَرِيمٌۭ ۝ فِى كِتَبٍۢ مَّكْنُونٍۢ ۝ لَّا يَمَسُّهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱلْمُطَهَّرُونَ ‎⟫
⟪quran 56:77⟫⟪quran 56:78⟫⟪quran 56:79⟫

In Arabic, *dhālik* ("that") denotes something distant and absent; *hādhā* ("this") applies to what is near and present. The question then presents itself: when a reader holds the Qur'an in hand, is it not before them? Why, then, does the verse employ the distant demonstrative? The text should read, "This is the Book wherein is no doubt." Yet by using the distant sign in the very first verse, Allah stirs the reader's wonder and invites contemplation of a meaning that transcends the surface.

### Disagreement Over Divine Manifestation

As the reader progresses through the Qur'an, conflicting images form and reform. One verse establishes a notion; soon after, another verse contradicts it. For example, one encounters:

⟪ar:‏وُجُوهٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ نَّاضِرَةٌ ۝ إِلَىٰ رَبِّهَا نَاظِرَةٌ ‎⟫

From this, the reader concludes that God may be seen. Yet immediately the verse follows:

⟪ar:‏لَا تُدْرِكُهُ ٱلْأَبْصَرُ ۖ وَهُوَ يُدْرِكُ ٱلْأَبْصَرَ ‎⟫

Now what is one to understand? In one place, Allah declares that certain faces shall behold Him; in another, that sight cannot encompass Him. Which of these two is true?

### Disagreement Over Human Will

The same perplexity attends the question of free will. When the reader encounters:

⟪ar:‏فَمَن شَآءَ آمَنَ وَمَن شَآءَ كَفَرَ ‎⟫

One concludes that belief and disbelief rest upon human volition and lie within human control. Yet the very next verse declares:

⟪ar:‏وَمَا تَشَآءُونَ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ‎⟫

And still more explicitly:

⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُضِلُّ مَن يَشَآءُ وَيَهْدِي مَن يَشَآءُ ‎⟫

Thus the reader is confounded: does human will possess any reality whatsoever, or is all reduced to the divine will alone? What, then, is meant by human agency?

### Dispute Over the Qur'an as the Word of Allah

Similarly, the question arises whether the Qur'an is the word of Allah. While reciting, the reader encounters:

⟪ar:‏وَإِنَّهُۥ لَتَنزِيلُ رَبِّ ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ ‎⟫

From this, the reader establishes that the Qur'an is undoubtedly the word of Allah, revealed to the heart of the Messenger. Yet upon turning further, one reads:

⟪ar:‏وَإِنَّهُۥ لَقَوْلُ رَسُولٍ كَرِيمٍۢ ‎⟫

Now confusion arises: is this the Qur'an or the utterance of the Messenger?

### Disagreement Over the Creation of the Heavens and Earth

Those who ponder the creation of the heavens and earth encounter yet another apparent contradiction. One verse states:

⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِي خَلَقَ لَكُم مَّا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ جَمِيعًۭا ثُمَّ ٱسْتَوَىٰ إِلَى ٱلسَّمَآءِ فَسَوَّىٰهُنَّ سَبْعَ سَمَوَتٍ ‎⟫

This declares that the heavens were created first, then the earth spread forth—a matter seemingly clear and unambiguous from the surface. Yet in another place, Allah declares:

⟪ar:‏قُل أَئِنَّكُمْ لَتَكْفُرُونَ بِٱلَّذِي خَلَقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ فِي يَوْمَيْنِ وَتَجْعَلُونَ لَهُۥ أَندَادًۭا ذَٰلِكَ رَبُّ ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ ۝ وَجَعَلَ فِيهَا رَوَاسِيَ مِن فَوْقِهَا وَبَارَكَ فِيهَا وَقَدَّرَ فِيهَآ أَقْوَاتَهَا فِي أَرْبَعَةِ أَيَّامٍ ۚ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَوَىٰ إِلَى ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَهِيَ دُخَانٌۭ فَقَالَ لَهَا وَلِلْأَرْضِ ٱئْتِيَا طَوْعًۭا أَوْ كَرْهًۭا قَالَتَآ أَتَيْنَا طَآئِعِينَ ۝ فَقَضَىٰهُنَّ سَبْعَ سَمَوَتٍۢ فِي يَوْمَيْنِ ‎⟫

Here it is declared that the earth was created first, then the heavens. Which of the two accounts is correct?

Beyond these instances, the Qur'an contains many verses wherein apparent contradiction and conflict abound. Such perplexity inevitably leads to divergence in understanding and causes the believer to waver in faith. It is recorded that one of the preachers raised the Qur'an above his head and exclaimed, "By Allah! This Book has led astray the nations of the nations!"

### The Spirit of the Qur'an: Meaning and Soul

Yet unlike the Qur'an in its external form—the letters and words—there exists a soul (*rūḥ*), a spiritual meaning that does not undergo the vicissitudes and changes that afflict the body, but remains steadfast and immutable.[^cite-63] All agree that the soul constitutes the true reality within the human being, and therein lies his virtue. A scholar or physician, however esteemed in this world, possesses neither learning nor medical skill once the soul departs the body. A living human being possesses great worth; a corpse is utterly worthless. Thus the true value resides not in the body but in the soul.

So it is with the Qur'an: its words form the palace of the body, for it is indeed a book of written words. Yet the Ahl al-Bayt (the People of the House, *a.s.*) are as the soul—they reveal the hidden meanings (*maʿānī*) concealed within these words. Should the soul be separated from the Qur'an, it becomes lifeless and devoid of meaning. For this reason, the Commander of the Faithful (*Amīr al-Muʾminīn*), ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, called the Qur'an "the silent speaker" and "the speaking silence": the Qur'an is word; the Ahl al-Bayt is meaning. Allah designated the Qur'an as *dhikr* (the Remembrance) and the Ahl al-Bayt as *Ahl al-Dhikr* (the People of the Remembrance), and commanded:

⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
⟪quran 21:7⟫

If you merely read the Qur'an, you become Muslim; if you understand it, you become a believer. The Messenger of Allah, upon whom be peace and blessings, declared: "I am leaving two precious things (*thaqalayn*) among you—the Book of Allah and my Progeny (Ahl al-Bayt). So long as you hold fast to both, you shall never go astray. There shall be no separation between them until they return to me at the Pool of Kawthar." The Prophet further proclaimed: "There shall be no severance between the Qur'an and the Ahl al-Bayt, nor between the Book and its People, nor between the Remembrance and the People of Remembrance."[^cite-64] If the Qur'an exists in every age, then the Ahl al-Bayt must also exist in every age. If the Qur'an is guidance, then the Ahl al-Bayt embodies guidance itself. Were one alone sufficient, there would be no need to bind it to the other. Allah Himself explicates this conjunction in His Clear Book (*Kitāb Mubīn*). On the one hand, Allah declares of the Book:

⟪ar:‏وَعِندَهُۥ مَفَاتِحُ ٱلْغَيْبِ لَا يَعْلَمُهَآ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِى ٱلْبَرِّ وَٱلْبَحْرِ ۚ وَمَا تَسْقُطُ مِن وَرَقَةٍ إِلَّا يَعْلَمُهَا وَلَا حَبَّةٍۢ فِى ظُلُمَتِ ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَا رَطْبٍۢ وَلَا يَابِسٍ إِلَّا فِى كِتَبٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
⟪quran 6:59⟫

And on the other, Allah declares concerning the Imam:

⟪ar:‏إِنَّا نَحْنُ نُحْىِ ٱلْمَوْتَىٰ وَنَكْتُبُ مَا قَدَّمُوا۟ وَآثَرَهُمْۚ وَكُلَّ شَىْءٍ أَحْصَيْنَهُ فِىٓ إِمَامٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
⟪quran 36:12⟫

Thus, touching (*yamass*) in the verse signifies understanding the meaning of the Qur'an. It is preferable that one approach it with outward purity (*ṭahāra*, ritual ablution), yet this is not obligatory. In reality, Allah declares that none may comprehend the true meaning of the Qur'an except the pure and sanctified servants—that is, none may attain the hidden meanings behind the Book's words save the Imāms of the Ahl al-Bayt. These pure servants are those to whom Allah sent down the verse of purification (*āyat al-taṭhīr*), which designates the Ahl al-Bayt:

⟪ar:‏وَقَرْنَ فِى بُيُوتِكُنَّ وَلَا تَبَرَّجْنَ تَبَرُّجَ ٱلْجَهِلِيَّةِ ٱلْأُولَىٰۖ وَأَقِمْنَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتِينَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَأَطِعْنَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۚۥٓ إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ ٱلرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ ٱلْبَيْتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمْ تَطْهِيرًۭا ‎⟫
⟪quran 33:33⟫

For them the Qur'an declares: "Allah desires only to remove from you all uncleanliness, O People of the House, and to purify you with a thorough purification." Therefore, if one wishes to know the hidden and manifest dimensions of this Book, one must ask the Clear Imam (*al-Imam al-Mubīn*). The foremost of these Ahl al-Bayt is the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, who proclaimed from the pulpit: "Ask before you lose me!" The person attached solely to the outward Qur'an shall perpetually stumble and never attain true guidance.

—*From the Majālis al-Muʾayyad (Sessions of al-Muʾayyad), as related by Sayyidnā al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī*

## By the Dawn, by the Ten Nights, by the Even and the Odd, by the Night When It Departs

Allah swears in Sūrat al-Fajr:

⟪ar:‏وَٱلْفَجْرِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلَيَالٍ عَشْرٍۢ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلشَّفْعِ وَٱلْوَتْرِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ ‎⟫
⟪quran 89:1⟫ ⟪quran 89:2⟫ ⟪quran 89:3⟫ ⟪quran 89:4⟫

### The Dawn (*al-Fajr*)

The beginning marked by *al-Fajr* (the dawn) signifies the conjunction of forms—those manifestations of the past that have progressed through the ranks and are now destined to appear in the Perfect Station (*Qamat al-Kāmil*).

### The Ten Nights (*Laylāl ʿAshar*)

These ten nights represent the ten stages through which forms progress and attain human perfection (*kamāl insānī*):[^cite-65]

| Level | Stage | Level | Stage |
|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Mineral Strength | 6 | Power of Honor |
| 2 | Organic Power | 7 | Power of Dominion |
| 3 | Animal Passion and Anger | 8 | First Sanctity (*Qudsiyya Ūlā*) |
| 4 | Human Power (comprising Reason and Speech) | 9 | Power of the Hereafter |
| 5 | Power of Wisdom | 10 | Power of Humanity |

### The Even and the Odd (*al-Shafʿ wa-l-Watr*)

*Al-Shafʿ*—the soul that is the origin of the universe—represents the interconnection of form and substance; form depends upon matter, and matter depends upon form for its existence.

*Al-Watr*—the singular unity—signifies the separation of matter from the senses and marks the degree of sanctity (*ḥadd al-quds*). At that station, all duality dissolves.

### The Night When It Departs (*al-Layl Idhā Yasrī*)

This denotes the unfoldment of form toward the attainment of perfection (*kamāl awwal*)—the primary perfection. The forms have reached this first station and must now ascend to secondary perfection (*kamāl thānī*) through their own exaltation.

## By the Sun and Its Brightness, by the Moon When It Follows

Allah declares in Sūrat al-Shams:

⟪ar:‏وَٱلشَّمْسِ وَضُحَىٰهَا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلْقَمَرِ إِذَا تَلَىٰهَا ‎⟫

In this verse, *al-Shams* (the Sun) refers to the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, and its brightness (*ḍuḥā*) to the light of his call and proclamation. By swearing thus, Allah invokes the Messenger of Allah, then says, "By the moon when it follows the sun." The moon (*al-Qamar*) signifies the Master (*Maulānā*) ʿAlī, who follows the Prophet in perfect obedience and submission. Thus in the second verse, Allah swears by the Messenger of Allah and declares the obligation to obey the Messenger through obedience to ʿAlī. The oath is sworn by the greatest of things.

## Your Lord Will Give You, and You Shall Be Well-Pleased

Allah speaks of the days to come for the Messenger of Allah, upon whom be peace and blessings:

⟪ar:‏وَلَسَوْفَ يُعْطِيكَ رَبُّكَ فَتَرْضَىٰٓ ‎⟫
⟪quran 93:5⟫

"Verily, your Lord will give you, and you shall be well-pleased."

We ordinarily concern ourselves with how we may please our Lord; yet for the Messenger of Allah, Allah Himself concerns Himself with the Prophet's satisfaction and contentment.[^cite-66]

There is a subtle point illustrated in this verse. Suppose we engage a laborer and agree upon a wage of two hundred rupees. When he completes the work, we pay him. Should he regard us with a look of dissatisfaction, desiring more, we present him additional money as a gift, hoping to bring him contentment. Once we perceive his satisfaction, we cease to give—his pleasure and satisfaction withhold our gift.

So too does this verse convey a nuance: so long as the Messenger of Allah is pleased, the bestowal of Allah's blessings will continue without cessation. In other words, since the Messenger shall never cease desiring, the process of divine giving shall never end. For this reason, Allah declares in the Qur'an:

⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ وَمَلَٓئِكَتَهُۥ يُصَلُّونَ عَلَى ٱلنَّبِىِّۚ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ صَلُّوا۟ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا۟ تَسْلِيمًا ‎⟫
⟪quran 33:56⟫

"Allah and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet. O you who believe, send blessings and peace upon him." This verse establishes an eternal chain: the blessings never cease upon him. Allah declares, "We have not ceased to send blessings, nor shall the angels cease, nor should you cease"—an unbroken succession of divine mercy.

—*From the Lessons of Tuḥfat al-Sayrfī (meaning the letters of al-Muʿjam al-Nawādir), as transmitted by Sayyidnā Ḥasan Khān ibn ʿAbd al-Malik*

## There Is No Blame Upon the Believers and the Righteous for What They Consumed

Allah declares in the Holy Qur'an:

> There is no sin on those who believe and do righteous deeds for what they eat and drink, so long as they fear Allah and believe and do righteous deeds, then fear Allah and believe, then fear Allah and do good.

It is narrated from the Commander of the Faithful, may Allah be pleased with him, that when Allah forbade the consumption of intoxicants, the Messenger of Allah pronounced a detailed condemnation, cursing not only the drinker but also the seller, the buyer, the one who carries it, the one who directs others to the tavern, and the one who profits from its sale. At that time, many persons had become Muslims, yet they still drank wine and did not consider it forbidden. They departed this life before the prohibition was revealed. When the prohibition of intoxicants came down, the Companions of the Prophet approached him and said: "O Messenger of Allah, you have spoken of the impurity of wine as you have. Yet tell us—what of our brothers who drank wine abundantly and died in that state, before the Lord forbade it to us?"[^cite-67]

This verse was revealed in response to that very question posed by the Companions. Thus Allah the Almighty forgave those who drank the forbidden wine yet remained believers and doers of good. They committed no mischief or corruption in religion; they were true lovers of the Prophet, and their sole reason for drinking was that it pleased their palate and was their favorite beverage. Moreover, it had not yet been formally forbidden. Allah accepted their faith, their piety, and their righteousness, and gave them the glad tidings that Allah loves those who do good.

In conclusion, we pray that Allah the Almighty may preserve us from error and transgression, and grant us and all the believers good success in word and deed through the Prophet Muḥammad, upon whom be peace.

Praise be to Allah, who has opened the paths of guidance for us through the Imāms and has saved us by their grace from wandering and misguidance. May Allah the Almighty send blessings and prayers upon Muḥammad, peace be upon him, who wears the finest raiment and cloak, who was blessed with revelation and supported by the angels; and upon his successor, who rested upon the Prophet's bed on the night of migration, preserved the Prophet's life, and sacrificed his own life—ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him, the foremost in obedience to his Lord, than whom none surpassed. And peace upon the pure Imāms whose lovers have prospered in religion, in this world and the Hereafter, whose enemies have become miserable; and peace upon our Master Imām al-Ṭayyib Abū l-Qāsim, the Commander of the Believers, who stands at the threshold of the third cycle of Imāms. May Allah the Almighty send blessings and peace upon all of them until the Day of Judgment. Allah is sufficient for us—what an excellent Protector, an excellent Master, an excellent Helper.

—*From Risālat 20, al-Qawal wa-l-Jawāb bi-l-Ṭawīl, as transmitted by Sayyidnā ʿAlī ibn Sayyidnā Sulaymān, may Allah be pleased with him*

## And the Hereafter Is Better for You Than This Life

Allah declares:

> That which is to come is better than that which has passed.

In one sense, the Messenger of Allah, upon whom be peace and blessings, was reassured by this verse: those spouses who came to you before—Aʿīsha and Ḥafṣa—do not sorrow over them, for those who shall come to you—Umm Salamah—are superior to them and shall serve you abundantly, becoming a solace to your heart.

In a higher sense, these two verses allude to a transformation of the Master of the Universe's virtues across two ages, whereupon you shall emerge as the *Qāʾim* (the Establisher). Furthermore, they intimate that the Āʾimma of the latter age shall surpass in rank the Āʾimma of the former. Or, stated otherwise: the *Mīm* of the Hereafter, which shall manifest before the Mahdi's rise, shall be exalted above the *Mīm* of the First Age, by which the seven Pens (*al-Aqlām al-Sabʿa*) shall come under his dominion.

The succession of Imāms preceding the Prophet was called the Ābbā (the fathers), and the succession of Imāms following him until the Day of Judgment was called the Abnāʾ (the sons). Therefore, the meaning of "And the Hereafter is better for you than the First" is that the Imāms of the Qāʾim's period surpass the Imāms of the Ibrāhīmī period.[^cite-68]

In yet another sense: the final seventy of this cycle exceeds all the seventy-year periods preceding it. This is because the glory of the *Qāʾim* who emerges at the end of this seventy shall comprehend and integrate all the forms of unveiling (*Kashf*) and the seventy periods to which the Qur'an refers as the *Mīqāt*:

> *Nothing is hidden from Him whether on earth or in the heavens, and to Him all matters are returned.*

And He is called *Malik Yawm al-Dīn* (Master of the Day of Judgment) in Sūrat al-Fātiḥa. If there is dominion in the world, it shall be the dominion of the Final Religion. Thus, after this sixth seventy, the age of revelation that commences shall be possessed by the *Qāʾim* through the Remembrance of Peace. Your dominion shall continue through your son.

—*From the Lessons of the Prophet, as transmitted by Sayyidnā Ḥusayn ibn Jadīn*

—*From the Lessons in the City of Allah, as transmitted by Mullā Ghulām Ḥusayn*

## Piety Is Not That You Enter Houses From Their Backs

Allah declares:

⟪quran 2:189⟫
⟪ar:‏ يَسْـَٔلُونَكَ عَنِ ٱلْأَهِلَّةِ ۖ قُلْ هِىَ مَوَقِيتُ لِلنَّاسِ وَٱلْحَجِّ ۗ وَلَيْسَ ٱلْبِرُّ بِأَن تَأْتُوا۟ ٱلْبُيُوتَ مِن ظُهُورِهَا وَلَكِنَّ ٱلْبِرَّ مَنِ ٱتَّقَىٰ ۗ وَأْتُوا۟ ٱلْبُيُوتَ مِنْ أَبْوَبِهَا ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫

"They ask you, O Muhammad, concerning the crescent moons. Say: They are signs marking fixed periods of time for the people and the pilgrimage. Piety is not that you enter houses from their backs, but true piety belongs to those who fear Allah. Enter houses through their proper doors, and fear Allah that you may prosper."

Every scholar and every common person enters their home through the door. It seems strange that the Qur'an—the Book of Guidance—exhorts such an obvious and commonplace matter; it appears useless and superfluous. Yet this oddity itself signals that "house" and "door" here do not denote structures of brick and lime, but rather point to spiritual realities—certain sanctified individuals who are the living houses and gates of Allah. The Prophet, upon whom be peace and blessings, declared: 

⟪ar-quote:أنا مدینۃ العلم و علی بابھا۔ فمن اراد العلم فلیات الباب⟫ 

"I am the City of Knowledge, and ʿAlī is its gate. Whosoever desires knowledge must enter through the gate."

Thus it became evident that there was but one gate—⟪ar:'باب علی'⟫ (the Gate of ʿAlī)—through which one might enter the city of knowledge. This gate stood in opposition to that of Moses, the ⟪ar:'باب حطۃ'⟫ (the Gate of Repentance), as Allah declared:

⟪quran 2:58⟫
⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قُلْنَا ٱدْخُلُوا۟ هَذِهِ ٱلْقَرْيَةَ فَكُلُوا۟ مِنْهَا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمْ رَغَدًۭا وَٱدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْبَابَ سُجَّدًۭا وَقُولُوا۟ حِطَّةٌۭ نَّغْفِرْ لَكُمْ خَطَيَكُمْ ۚ وَسَنَزِيدُ ٱلْمُحْسِنِينَ ‎⟫

"And when We said: Enter this town and eat bountifully therein with pleasure and delight wherever you wish, and enter the gate in prostration, saying 'Forgive,' and We shall forgive you your sins and shall increase for those who do good."

As the people of Moses refused to enter the gate—that is, they denied their successor Aaron—and they corrupted the word ⟪ar:حنطۃ⟫ into ⟪ar:'حطۃ'⟫, so were they punished.[^cite-69]

Allah states in this verse that the righteous and the pious are those who enter the house through the gate—not by tearing down the wall. That is, whoever acknowledges ⟪ar:'علی ولی اللہ'⟫ (ʿAlī as the Walī—the Guardian and Master) speaks the truth. The ⟪ar:'باب مدینہ العلم'⟫ (the Gate of the City of Knowledge) is acknowledged! If this were not the meaning of this verse, what excellence or piety would arise merely from entering a house through its physical door?[^cite-70]

Furthermore, the word ⟪ar:'بر'⟫ (*birr*, righteousness) at the beginning of this verse and the word ⟪ar:'تقوی'⟫ (*taqwā*, piety) in the middle have no connection whatsoever to one who enters an ordinary brick-and-lime dwelling. The supreme virtue ⟪ar:'بر الوالدین'⟫ (kindness to parents) denotes the duty toward one's parents; yet the true spiritual parents are Muḥammad and ʿAlī. The Prophet declared: 

⟪ar-quote:أنا و أنت یا علی أبوین المومنین⟫ 

"You and I, O ʿAlī, are the parents of the believers." The Imām, peace be upon him, has said: "Piety is my religion and that of my father." Whoever is not pious is ungodly!

From the interpretation of this verse, it becomes clear that true righteousness and piety consist in entering the house of the Sharīʿa of Muḥammad by joining in the *Wilāya* (spiritual guardianship) of ʿAlī. One is not righteous merely by obeying the rulings of the Sharīʿa while rejecting ʿAlī or following some other caliph. The example of such a person is that of one who breaches the wall.

## The Six Days of Creation and Allah's Sitting Upon the Throne

Allah declares in His Clear Book: "He created the universe in six days and then sat upon His Throne." The "day" we reckon is the Earth's complete revolution around the Sun. Yet a question arises: when Allah was creating the universe, neither the Sun nor the Earth yet existed. What, then, is meant by Allah's Day (*Yawm*)? And why did He rest upon the Throne on the seventh day, when He Himself declares:

⟪ar:‏وَعِندَهُۥ مَفَاتِحُ ٱلْغَيْبِ لَا يَعْلَمُهَآ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِى ٱلْبَرِّ وَٱلْبَحْرِ ۚ وَمَا تَسْقُطُ مِن وَرَقَةٍ إِلَّا يَعْلَمُهَا وَلَا حَبَّةٍۢ فِى ظُلُمَتِ ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَا رَطْبٍۢ وَلَا يَابِسٍ إِلَّا فِى كِتَبٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
⟪quran 6:59⟫

And in the *Āyat al-Kursī*, He declares:

⟪ar:‏ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْحَىُّ ٱلْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُۥ سِنَةٌۭ وَلَا نَوْمٌۭ ۚ لَّهُۥ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ مَن ذَا ٱلَّذِى يَشْفَعُ عِندَهُۥٓ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِۦ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَىْءٍۢ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِۦٓ إِلَّا بِمَا شَآءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَـُٔودُهُۥ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ ٱلْعَلِىُّ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ‎⟫
⟪quran 2:255⟫

The Wise One teaches that the six days of Allah are the six Words (*al-Kalimat*) He has sent forth, and the six dimensions, for man is encircled by six dimensions, with the seventh dimension being man himself—the quintessence of all, for without him none of these dimensions would have meaning.

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Above | Below | Right | Left | Forward | Behind | Human |

Because man possesses these six sides to his being, Allah the Almighty sent six Messengers (*Rusul*) to humanity. Thus, in the exemplary order of spiritual practice, the Messenger Adam came from the upper dimension of humanity; Noah came from the left; Abraham from behind; Moses from below (confronting Adam); Jesus from the right (confronting Noah); and Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā came from the front (opposite to Abraham).[^cite-71]

These six Messengers, emanating from the six sides of mankind, proclaimed deeds and promised reward for those deeds. When six Messengers came from the six directions of the world and proclaimed their message, this proves that no other Prophet shall come after the Messenger of Allah. Now, whoever comes is He who will grant them their reward and recompense.

### The Inward Significance of the Six

Man is encircled by six dimensions, and he himself constitutes the seventh. The six dimensions signify the existence of man and acknowledge that he exists—which is why we too exist. In the same manner, Allah has sent six Messengers and appointed guardians (*wilāya*) over them to guide the people. These six Messengers indicate the existence and manifestation of the *Qāʾim* (the Establisher). They acknowledge that were the *Qāʾim's* manifestation not the ultimate purpose, these six would not have appeared in this world.

When these Words of Allah came, they proclaimed to the people different matters. The inward meanings of their proclamations made each an individual expression of the Sharīʿa, just as a walnut differs in form from an apple, yet both are fruits. The walnut possesses meanings not found in the apple. When Allah sent a Messenger to the people, He sent with each an interpreter (*Muʿallim*) to explain the meanings of his outward actions, lest it be said: "We have not been instructed."

These Messengers or Natiq (speakers) explained the outward actions, while those who explained their meanings were the Asās (foundations) or Waṣī (successors).[^cite-72] Thus Muʿallim Sheesh succeeded Ādam; Muʿallim Sām succeeded Nūḥ; Muʿallim Hārūn was the successor of Mūsā; Muʿallim Shamʿūn was the successor of ʿĪsā; and Muʿallim ʿAlī al-Murtaḍā was the successor of Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā, peace be upon them all.

### The Asās and the Waṣī

During the lifetime of the *Nāṭiq* (the Speaker-Prophet), his successor is called *Asās* (the Foundation). After the *Nāṭiq's* death, he is designated *Waṣī* (the Executor-Successor). Muʿallim ʿAlī was the *Asās* during the lifetime of the Messenger of Allah, and after the Prophet's death, he became the *Waṣī*.[^cite-73]

### The Seven Days of the Week

It is narrated that the Messenger of Allah began the creation of the universe on Sunday, completed it on Friday, and rested on Saturday. Moreover, Allah the Almighty declares in the Holy Qur'an that the word *Yawm* in Arabic denotes both day and night as one complete cycle. Only the daylight portion is called *Nahār* (day), and only the night is called *Layl* (night). When Allah uses the term *Yawm*, it encompasses the full twenty-four-hour day-and-night cycle.

Just as there are seven days in a week with six days intervening between each two weeks, so these six Messengers came in the midst of two periods, like the six days themselves.

| Saturday | Sunday | Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday | Saturday |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| *Qāʾim Fāṭir* | Ādam | Nūḥ | Ibrāhīm | Mūsā | ʿĪsā | Muḥammad | *Qāʾim Sātur* |
| — | Sheesh | Sām | Ismāʿīl | Hārūn | Shamʿūn | ʿAlī | Mahdī |

Likewise, within each cycle, periods intervene between the two Messengers to explain the inner meanings of interpretations and outward actions:

| Saturday | Sunday | Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday | Saturday | Sunday |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Speaker | Imāma | Khalīfa | Martyrdom | Succession | Concealment | Establishment | Occultation | Speaker |

This is because the Law (*Sharīʿa*) of Ādam was advanced by Mūsā; the Law of Nūḥ was advanced by ʿĪsā; and the Law of Ibrāhīm was advanced by Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā. For this reason, in the *Durūd* (the formula of blessings), peace is sent upon Muḥammad and upon Ibrāhīm and his family. By mentioning both names in the blessing, the believer prays: "O Allah, join the community (*ʿItrat*) of Ibrāhīm and the family of Ibrāhīm just as You have joined the community of Muḥammad and the family of Muḥammad."

—*From al-Mubtadāʾ wa-l-Intihāʾ (The Beginning and the End), as transmitted by Ḥaḍrat Ẓiyāʾ ʿAlī Ẓiyāʾī*
