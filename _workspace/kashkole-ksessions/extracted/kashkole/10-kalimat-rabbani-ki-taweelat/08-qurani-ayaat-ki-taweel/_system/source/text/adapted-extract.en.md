## That Book, Wherein Is No Doubt

God says (interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 2:1-2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏الٓمٓ ‎ ‏ذَلِكَ ٱلْكِتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًۭى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ ‎⟫
> *Alif-Lam-Mîm. [These letters are one of the miracles of the Quran and none but Allah (Alone) knows their meanings]. That book, whereof there is no doubt, a guidance to those who are Al-Muttaqun [the pious and righteous persons who fear Allah much (abstain from all kinds of sins and evil deeds which He has forbidden) and love Allah much (perform all kinds of good deeds which He has ordained)].*
> This is the Book, in which there is no doubt, a guidance for the God-fearing.
> — **Quran 2:1-2**

The luminous letters ⟪ar:'الٓمٓ'⟫—Alif, Lam, Meem—are not arbitrary signs, as the unlearned suppose, but constitute a sublime category of divine knowledge. Each letter signifies a great reality: ⟪ar:'الف'⟫ (Alif) represents God Himself, the Unique and Incomparable; ⟪ar:'لام'⟫ (Lam) signifies the Archangel Gabriel, the trusted messenger ⟪ar:جبرئیل امین⟫; and ⟪ar:'میم'⟫ (Meem) denotes the Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him ⟪ar:محمد علیہ السلام⟫.[^cite-1] By swearing through these three supreme realities, God declares with absolute authority: there exists no doubt whatsoever concerning the truth and majesty of the Qur'an, and it stands as a guidance for those who guard themselves against evil.

Yet the phrase itself—⟪ar-quote:ذلک الکتاب لا ریب فیہ⟫ (that book of which there is no doubt)—conceals a profound meaning that escapes the literalist's grasp. If the Qur'an were indeed the immediate subject of these verses, the proximal demonstrative ⟪ar:'ھذہ'⟫ (*this*) would have been far more appropriate than the distal ⟪ar:ذلک⟫ (*that*). The Arabic tongue reserves *dhalik* for distant objects and *hadha* for the near; since the Qur'an lies in the reader's very hands, why would God employ the language of distance? The answer becomes evident: God alludes not to the written codex alone, but to *another book*—the Book of Divine Realities that transcends the merely textual.

This raises a critical question concerning divine wisdom itself. To whom does God address this mighty oath? If the disbelievers and polytheists are His audience, the oath proves powerless: they hear these verses unmoved, neither bowing before the Qur'an's grandeur nor embracing faith. They deny the Messenger and refuse to acknowledge Gabriel—what force, then, does an oath by these very beings exert upon them? Conversely, if the believers are addressed, they have already surrendered to the Qur'an's truth; why would God need to convince the convinced? Under such a literalist reading, these verses become hollow utterances, devoid of purpose.

The People of the Household (Ahl al-Bayt), peace be upon them, offer the true interpretation. They teach that the *book* (kitāb) signifies the totality of meanings—all letters, words, and content—that dwell within the heart of the speaker as intention and purpose. The written book expresses divine meaning through mortal language; thus, God's Book comprises the comprehensive disclosure of all divine purposes hidden within creation and the human vocation to worship. It reveals the reward God has ordained for the righteous and the punishment prepared for the wicked. It recounts the trials that befell the Prophets in ages past and warns of all that shall come to pass until the Final Day.[^cite-2]

This divine Book exists in two manifest forms. First, the *silent book* (al-kitāb al-sāmit), composed of non-vocal, mute letters inscribed upon parchment. Second, the *speaking book* (al-kitāb al-nāṭiq)—the very essence of the Messenger of God, peace and blessings upon him—who embodies the correct and luminous interpretation of the Qur'an's meanings, the elucidation of its obscurities and ambiguities. These Imams stand forth from that station of *revelation* (tanzīl), which remains distinct from *esoteric exegesis* (taʾwīl)—that hidden knowledge which God has reserved for Himself and for those firmly grounded in understanding:

> ⟪quran 3:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *It is He Who has sent down to you (Muhammad PBUH) the Book (this Quran). In it are Verses that are entirely clear, they are the foundations of the Book [and those are the Verses of Al-Ahkam (commandments, etc.), Al-Fara'id (obligatory duties) and Al-Hudud (legal laws for the punishment of thieves, adulterers, etc.)]; and others not entirely clear. So as for those in whose hearts there is a deviation (from the truth) they follow that which is not entirely clear thereof, seeking Al-Fitnah (polytheism and trials, etc.), and seeking for its hidden meanings, but none knows its hidden meanings save Allah. And those who are firmly grounded in knowledge say: "We believe in it; the whole of it (clear and unclear Verses) are from our Lord." And none receive admonition except men of understanding.*
> It is He who has sent down to you the Book, in which some of its verses are definitive, they are the origin of the Book, and others are allegorical. I don't know. They say, We believe in these things; they are only from our Lord. Only those who possess understanding believe the admonition.
> — **Quran 3:7**

The silent book encompasses the conditions of all three temporal dimensions—past, present, and future—and encompasses every matter that shall occur until the Day of Standing. So too does the Qur'an. The Commander of the Faithful, ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی علیہ السلام⟫ Ali ibn Abi Talib, peace be upon him, proclaimed from the pulpit in his first sermon upon assuming the caliphate, declaring openly ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫: ⟪ar-quote:سلونی قبل ان تفقدونی! سلونی عما کان وعما یکون الی یوم القیامۃ⟫ "Ask of me before you lose me! Ask me concerning all that has passed away and all that shall come until the Day of Judgment."[^cite-3] The Qur'an confirms this comprehensive knowledge:

> ⟪quran 6:38⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا مِن دَآبَّةٍۢ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَا طَٓئِرٍۢ يَطِيرُ بِجَنَاحَيْهِ إِلَّآ أُمَمٌ أَمْثَالُكُم ۚ مَّا فَرَّطْنَا فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ مِن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ ثُمَّ إِلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ يُحْشَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *There is not a moving (living) creature on earth, nor a bird that flies with its two wings, but are communities like you. We have neglected nothing in the Book, then unto their Lord they (all) shall be gathered.*
> There is not a moving creature on the earth, nor a bird that flies with its two wings, but they are communities like yourselves. We have left no lag in their destiny. Then to their Lord they are gathered.
> — **Quran 6:38**

And the Book of Revelation proclaims: ⟪ar-quote:أنا بکل شئی علیم⟫ "Verily, I am the Knower of all things."

The Righteous Imams, peace upon them, have transmitted that the knowledge revealed to Adam, peace be upon him—through which the Prophets came to all creation—and the knowledge of the Messenger of God, Seal of the Prophets, and his pure descendants constitute a singular, flowing stream.[^cite-4] Yet why does the verse employ ⟪ar:'ذالک'⟫, the language of distance? The answer clarifies itself: this book is no mere artifact but a reference to the Book of Reality. That book, which admits no doubt as to its grandeur, is the very essence of the Prophet; its purpose is to underscore the station of the *Guardian* (waṣī), the ⟪ar:وصی رسول⟫ *Commander of the Faithful*, ⟪ar:امیر المومین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ Ali ibn Abi Talib, and to purge the hearts of the wavering of all uncertainty.

Thus ⟪ar:ھدی للمتقین⟫ the Qur'an—a synthesis of the silent and speaking books—serves as a guide to those who possess *true godwariness* (taqwā). The truly pious ⟪ar:'متقین'⟫ are those whose hearts harbor no doubt concerning the Guardianship of Ali, peace be upon him, and whose conviction remains unwavering. This book is not a guide for those who doubt and harbor hypocrisy in their breasts.

## Those Who Believe in the Unseen and Establish Prayer

God addresses us in Surah al-Baqara:

> ⟪quran 2:3-5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *Who believe in the Ghaib and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and spend out of what we have provided for them [i.e. give Zakat, spend on themselves, their parents, their children, their wives, etc., and also give charity to the poor and also in Allah's Cause - Jihad, etc.]. And who believe in (the Quran and the Sunnah) which has been sent down (revealed) to you (Muhammad Peace be upon him ) and in [the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel), etc.] which were sent down before you and they believe with certainty in the Hereafter. (Resurrection, recompense of their good and bad deeds, Paradise and Hell, etc.). They are on (true) guidance from their Lord, and they are the successful.*
> Those who believe in the unseen, and perform the prayer, and spend (benevolently) out of what We have provided for them, and those who believe in what has been revealed to you, and what was revealed before you, and who are certain of the Hereafter; it is they who are on the path of their Lord, and it is they who are the felicitous.
> — **Quran 2:3-5**

### Believers in the Unseen

The surface reading suggests that faith in the unseen (al-ghayb) means affirming things imperceptible to the senses—the Creator Most High, free from all association, His angels, Paradise and Hell, the Bridge over perdition, the Resurrection of those dwelling in the graves, the reckoning of the Hour. These realities, though hidden from sensory perception, do not elude reason, consciousness, and spiritual insight; they are present to the intellect and the heart. Had the Messenger of God, peace and blessings upon him, called people toward things invisible alike to sense and to intellect, his summons would have been void of all warrant. For God declares:

> ⟪quran 43:86⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا يَمْلِكُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ مِن دُونِهِ ٱلشَّفَعَةَ إِلَّا مَن شَهِدَ بِٱلْحَقِّ وَهُمْ يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those whom they invoke instead of Him have no power of intercession; except those who bear witness to the truth (i.e. believed in the Oneness of Allah, and obeyed His Orders), and they know (the facts about the Oneness of Allah) .*
> And those whom they invoke besides Him have no power of intercession, except those who testify to the truth and they are confirmed.
> — **Quran 43:86**

A testimony rings hollow when the witness speaks in ignorance or without certainty; to bear witness blindly, without knowledge of its truth, renders the testimony void. Similarly, faith in the unseen and the hidden, divorced from rational reflection, constitutes falsehood, and such testimony as the ignorant give carries no weight.

Consider the example of the soul dwelling within the body, or meaning hidden within words. Every rational soul knows that the body exists for the sake of the self, yet that self remains invisible. Strip away the soul, and the body becomes a lifeless husk. Words themselves are fashioned to convey a meaning; to utter words devoid of hidden significance is absurdity. In like manner, the unseen spoken of in this verse refers to the esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl) of the Qur'an—that interior knowledge concealed within its outward words and accessible to reason rather than sensation. For those whose intellect lies in darkness, the Qur'an declares:

> ⟪quran 30:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَعْلَمُونَ ظَهِرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَهُمْ عَنِ ٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ غَفِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *They know only the outside appearance of the life of the world (i.e. the matters of their livelihood, like irrigating or sowing or reaping, etc.), and they are heedless of the Hereafter.*
> They know the outward appearance of the life of the world, and of the Hereafter they are heedless.
> — **Quran 30:7**

They apprehend only the manifest, sensory realm and remain oblivious to the realities of the world to come.

The knowledge comprised within the unseen takes many forms: knowledge of the Hereafter, of the Angels, of spiritual and corporeal limits, of the Throne and the Footstool, of the Gardens and the Fire, of the Resurrection and the Path of the Grave, of true monotheism—a belief in the Creator purged alike of anthropomorphism and negation of His attributes. All these mysteries lie concealed from the senses, yet they dwell within the grasp of intellect; the human mind may comprehend them, and he who affirms them speaks with a testimony that carries weight.

Yet knowledge itself admits of degrees and stations; the veil that obscures lower knowledge withdraws gradually as one ascends to higher knowledge. Thus God declares:

> ⟪quran 2:286⟫—"and above all knowledge He is the Most High."

At each level, that which seemed unknowable to the lower station becomes apparent to the higher; since knowledge itself possesses no ceiling, the unseen too remains infinite. In other words, the Messenger of God invited mankind to the esoteric exegesis of the Qur'an through this verse. The Prophet stands as a "Luminous Lamp," guiding the blind to sight as an outward light enables the eye to perceive. A blind person cannot benefit from a lamp's radiance; similarly, one blind in spiritual insight cannot profit from Prophethood and prophetic guidance. Only when lamp and eye unite does vision flourish. In the same way, understanding the mysteries of the Hereafter arises from the union of spiritual insight and prophethood; without both, such understanding remains impossible.

### And Establish Prayer

On the surface, this command means to perform the ritual prayer (al-salāh). The outward prayer functions as a wall surrounding a city, within which all manner of charity and blessing are gathered for its inhabitants. Therefore, the Prophet, peace and blessings upon him, termed it "a good deed" (hasana)—prayer comprises the sum of all righteousness. It commences with the *magnification of holiness* (takbīrat al-iḥrām) and concludes with acceptance before the Almighty. Within this span unfold the bowing, the prostration, the recitation, the testimony of faith, the standing, and the sitting. The aggregate of these movements constitutes prayer.

Yet prayer has an inner dimension that mirrors and transcends the outer form: it is the *summons to truth* (daʿwa), the invitation extended by the people of reality to their followers. In this sense, both the literal utterances and the inner meanings of prayer stand established through the daʿwa itself. This summons to truth likewise forms a fortress; those who enter it come to know the true benefits and aims of the outward prayer, which remain hidden from those who perceive only its external face. So too do the meanings of almsgiving, fasting, pilgrimage, and struggle in God's way become illuminated to those who enter into the covenant of the daʿwa. This path leads to true monotheism (tawḥīd)—a belief in God free from the defects both of likening Him to creation and of denying His attributes, transcending all spiritual and corporeal limits.

Just as the *magnification of holiness* initiates the outward prayer, so too does a sacred covenant initiate the daʿwa—a solemn bond that binds every seeker to obedience to the Imam of the Age, peace be upon him. The first obligation upon the *Summoner* (dāʿī) is acceptance of the Imam's command and the dedication of one's entire self in return. Whoever remains connected to this summons to truth participates in the assembly of those who establish prayer and reaps the spiritual fruits of the outward prayer in the life to come.

### And from What We Have Provided for Them, They Spend

Sustenance takes two forms: the material and the spiritual. Though those who feed the hungry and the needy merit praise, those who impart spiritual provision—who teach wisdom and esoteric knowledge—rank far higher. This sustenance flows to those who hold fast to the Cord of the Guardianship of Ali, peace be upon him, and enter the summons by covenant and pledge. Each soul receives from God sustenance suited to its constitution and capacity; the outward-minded are taught the Book according to their comprehension, while those of inward orientation receive also wisdom. Thus the Messengers and their successors provide both services to humanity, sustaining them from God's bounty. So too do those who follow your commandments impart this twofold blessing.

### And Those Who Believe in What Has Been Revealed to You

In this verse, God unites the three temporal epochs—past, present, and future—declaring that perfection is achieved through fulfillment of faith, acknowledgment of all the Prophets before, obedience to the present Prophet, and certitude in the Resurrection yet to come. Whoever rejects any of these dimensions stands excluded from the circle of faith. As God declares: ⟪ar-quote:اولائک ھدی من ربھم و اولائک ھم المفلحون⟫ "They are upon guidance from their Lord, and they are the felicitous." Whoever realizes all the conditions of this verse—who grasps the esoteric exegesis of the Qur'an, establishes the summons to truth, believes in all the Prophets, and affirms the coming Resurrection—truly attains success.

## Those Who Disbelieve: To Them It Is All the Same

God says (interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 2:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ سَوَآءٌ عَلَيْهِمْ ءَأَنذَرْتَهُمْ أَمْ لَمْ تُنذِرْهُمْ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily, those who disbelieve, it is the same to them whether you (O Muhammad Peace be upon him ) warn them or do not warn them, they will not believe.*
> As for those who disbelieve, it is all the same whether you warn them or do not warn them, they will not believe.
> — **Quran 2:6**

By disbelievers (al-kāfirūn), the verse does not refer solely to the Arab polytheists who, in ignorance of reflection, worshipped stones inherited from their forefathers. In the Arabic lexicon, ⟪ar:'کفر'⟫ *kufr* literally signifies concealment and hiding; thus the farmer (al-kāfir) conceals the seed beneath the soil. In its essential sense, a disbeliever is one who deliberately denies what he knows, who obscures truth while feigning ignorance, crying "I know not!" even as his heart harbors certain knowledge. To convince such a soul is not difficult—it is impossible.

There exists a category of disbelievers who reject the Messenger of God: the Children of Israel and the Christians. Their own scriptures contained prophecies of the Final Prophet, couched in unmistakable language, replete with praise of his virtue. His name itself was inscribed in their texts; the nobility of his family, the signs of his emergence, and the circumstances of his age were all mentioned in the Torah and Gospel. Yet they altered everything through enmity toward the Prophet and his line.

In the time of the Messenger himself, disbelief took another form: Muslims who knowingly and deliberately rejected the Messenger's successor. They refused obedience despite their knowledge that ⟪ar:سباقت فی الاسلام⟫ Ali had raced to embrace Islam, that he possessed unique excellences and virtues unmatched by any other companion, and that the Messenger himself, before a multitude of thousands, proclaimed at Ghadir Khumm: ⟪ar:من کنت مولاہ فھذا علی مولاہ⟫ "Of whomsoever I am the Master, Ali too is the Master."[^cite-5] What greater disbelief than this?

What the Jews perpetrated against the Prophet, his successors suffered in equal measure. For those who attend to the apparent meanings of the Qur'an, the disbelievers are those outside the pale of Islam—idolaters and followers of the Book alike. Yet according to the people of esoteric exegesis (ahl al-taʾwīl), all the sects of Islam stand included in this verse—those who disbelieved and rejected the Prophet's successor, just as the Jews and Christians rejected the Messengers. They did so despite knowing, and the Qur'an itself having taught them, that among the Prophets before Muhammad, not one lacked a successor, and that Prophethood reaches its completion only through a Testament.

The word ⟪ar:'أنذر'⟫ *andhara* means to warn of an approaching calamity, to alert and awaken. Hence the proverb states: ⟪ar-quote:فقد أعذر من أنذر⟫ "He who warns has absolved himself of blame." God has described the righteous aged man as a Warner (al-nadhīr) in Surah al-Fatir:

> ⟪quran 35:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَهُمْ يَصْطَرِخُونَ فِيهَا رَبَّنَآ أَخْرِجْنَا نَعْمَلُ صَلِحًا غَيْرَ ٱلَّذِى كُنَّا نَعْمَلُۚ أَوَلَمْ نُعَمِّرْكُم مَّا يَتَذَكَّرُ فِيهِ مَن تَذَكَّرَ وَجَآءَكُمُ ٱلنَّذِيرُۖ فَذُوقُوا۟ فَمَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ مِن نَّصِيرٍ ‎⟫
> *Therein they will cry: "Our Lord! Bring us out, we shall do righteous good deeds, not (the evil deeds) that we used to do." (Allah will reply): "Did We not give you lives long enough, so that whosoever would receive admonition, - could receive it? And the warner came to you. So taste you (the evil of your deeds). For the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong­doers, etc.) there is no helper." *
> And they will cry out therein, Our Lord! Bring us out, and we will do righteousness, the opposite of what we used to do. Did We not give you a life long enough to understand? And there came to you a warner, so taste it, for the wrongdoers will have no helper.
> — **Quran 35:37**

The Warner alerts humanity to the approach of death, to the imminent loss of the world's fleeting pleasures. Yet those intoxicated by worldly ease, those who have forgotten the Hereafter, cannot benefit from the Messenger's warnings. As one may awaken a sleeper, so too may one guide the seeker; but he who feigns slumber in order to resist the truth—upon such a one, all warnings fall uselessly.

## God Has Sealed Their Hearts and Upon Their Hearing

God says (interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 2:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏خَتَمَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِهِمْ وَعَلَىٰ سَمْعِهِمْ ۖ وَعَلَىٰٓ أَبْصَرِهِمْ غِشَوَةٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Allah has set a seal on their hearts and on their hearings, (i.e. they are closed from accepting Allah's Guidance), and on their eyes there is a covering. Theirs will be a great torment.*
> Allah has set a seal on their hearts and upon their hearing, and on their eyes is a covering, and there is a great punishment for them.
> — **Quran 2:7**

On the surface, this verse presents a troubling image: if God Himself has sealed the hearts of the disbelievers, withholding them from His knowledge, and deprived their ears and eyes of benefit by casting a veil upon them, then they stand helpless and impoverished, undeserving of reproach.

### The Sealing of Their Hearts

The learned of the daʿwa teach that by "heart," "eye," and "ear," the verse does not denote mere physical organs, but rather those persons and beings who have become the heart, eye, and ear—the guides and leaders—for their communities. In the human frame, the heart rules all the other organs; by its command all perform their function, and from it flows the circulation of life. Since the human being is a microcosm (ʿālam ṣaghīr), the station of the heart within him mirrors the position of the sun in the macrocosm (ʿālam kabīr). So too within the realm of religion stands one supreme authority—the heart and soul of the faith itself. This was the Prophet in his age, and after him his successors and the Imams, peace upon them, upon whom the Shariah depends. These are the centers of spiritual life and the wellspring of religion; from them flow all the bounds and ordinances of the daʿwa, and from them believers receive eternal life. When they are severed from their stations, the system of Shariah itself loses its vital force.

Likewise, those who preach misguidance against the rightful summons, those who usurp the rights of the Imams and seize their positions, become the false hearts of their followers:

> ⟪quran 28:41⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَجَعَلْنَهُمْ أَئِمَّةًۭ يَدْعُونَ إِلَى ٱلنَّارِ ۖ وَيَوْمَ ٱلْقِيَمَةِ لَا يُنصَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We made them leaders inviting to the Fire, and on the Day of Resurrection, they will not be helped.*
> And We made them leaders inviting to the Fire, and on the Day of Resurrection they will not be helped.
> — **Quran 28:41**

Their followers regard them as leaders, obey them as the body's organs obey the heart, and execute their commands. When it is said that ⟪ar:ختم اللہ علی قلبھم⟫ God has sealed their hearts, it means that these misguided Imams and their followers are deprived of the radiant lights of divine inspiration and the luminous rays of revelation. No longer do the lights of divine succor illuminate their hearts; bereft of revelation, they cannot grasp the truth and reality. Since their hearts have darkened, their thoughts too have become dense and murky; they comprehend nothing save the false world of their own making.

The heart's function, as the organ of discernment, is to contemplate and reflect. Such reflection as would unveil the knowledge of true monotheism, as would clarify the stations of the Messenger and the Imams—this is the reflection God praises when He says: ⟪ar-quote:ویتفکرون فی خلق السماوات والارض⟫ "Those who contemplate the creation of the heavens and the earth." The Prophet, peace and blessings upon him, declared that one moment of such reflection surpasses a whole year of worship.[^cite-6] A heart that refrains from such contemplation is not the heart of a human being; it is the heart of a beast.

### And Upon Their Hearing, and Upon Their Eyes Is a Covering

When the reality of the heart—in this sense, the true guide—becomes clear, it becomes equally plain that those same people who remain ignorant or who follow a false imam become the ears and eyes of that deluded heart. Eye and ear are organs of reception; they accept external impressions. These persons accept only the false claims of their misguided leaders, molding their understanding to conform with those leaders' ideas. They heed the words of their false hearts and hear not the voice of truth; they see not the light of guidance. For them awaits in the Hereafter a punishment severe and enduring.

In another verse of the same surah, God speaks of a similar condition:

> ⟪quran 2:10⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فِى قُلُوبِهِم مَّرَضٌۭ فَزَادَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ مَرَضًۭا ۖ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۢ بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْذِبُونَ ‎⟫
> *In their hearts is a disease (of doubt and hypocrisy) and Allah has increased their disease. A painful torment is theirs because they used to tell lies.*
> There is a sickness in their hearts, so Allah has increased their sickness, and there awaits them a painful punishment, for they used to lie.
> — **Quran 2:10**

The distinction between the two verses lies in this: in the first, God speaks of sealing as a divine act of closure in response to deliberate rejection; in the second, He attributes the disease to those who harbor it. Abraham, the Friend of God, described the attributes of his Lord:

> ⟪quran 26:78-80⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَنِى فَهُوَ يَهْدِينِ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِى هُوَ يُطْعِمُنِى وَيَسْقِينِ ‎ ‏وَإِذَا مَرِضْتُ فَهُوَ يَشْفِينِ ‎⟫
> *"Who has created me, and it is He Who guides me; "And it is He Who feeds me and gives me to drink. " And when I am ill, it is He who cures me;*
> Who created me, He guides me, and He who feedeth me and gives me to drink; and when I am ill, it is He who cureth me.
> — **Quran 26:78-80**

Abraham attributed to God his creation, guidance, and sustenance; yet when he spoke of illness, he said not ⟪ar-quote:وأذا امرضنی فھو یشفین⟫ "When He makes me ill, He heals me." Rather, he ascribed illness to himself, attributing healing to God alone. This teaches a subtle truth: both physical and cardiac diseases originate within the human being, arising from human negligence and discord. The disease of the heart spoken of here consists in the doubts and suspicions that sicken the soul, weakening it as bodily illness weakens the body. When doubt takes root, the self becomes emptied of knowledge. The soul no longer grasps the wisdom of what the Messenger proclaims or commands; instead of obedience, it falls into rejection, much as a sick body cannot benefit from the finest nourishment. Offer the gravely ill person exquisite foods, and they become poison to him. Similarly, those whose hearts harbor doubt concerning the words of the Messenger of God cannot profit from the Qur'an or his traditions. The doubtful soul cannot attain the correct understanding of spiritual realities; the sciences of religion, which are like sustenance to the spirit, cannot be absorbed by such a soul. Should one present to it the truths of the faith—the most refined of nourishment—it rejects them outright.

Since doubt is born within the heart and does not enter from without, God declares: ⟪ar-quote:فی قلوبھم مرض⟫ "There is a disease in their hearts." But as Abraham taught us, physical diseases too arise from within—from human negligence. The Commander of the Faithful expressed this truth when he said:

> Your illnesses stem from within you, yet you perceive it not; your cure dwells within you, yet you are unaware.

In contrast to this, God attributes the sealing to Himself because it constitutes the recompense for their evil deeds, the punishment for their willful rejection. The disbelievers punished themselves through their denial of the Guardian and their obstinate refusal; now, stripped of all capacity to comprehend any good concerning him, they stand as described:

> ⟪quran 61:5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ مُوسَىٰ لِقَوْمِهِۦ يَقَوْمِ لِمَ تُؤْذُونَنِى وَقَد تَّعْلَمُونَ أَنِّى رَسُولُ ٱللَّهِ إِلَيْكُمْ ۖ فَلَمَّا زَاغُوٓا۟ أَزَاغَ ٱللَّهُ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلْفَسِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when Musa (Moses) said to his people: "O my people! Why do you hurt me while you know certainly that I am the Messenger of Allah to you? So when they turned away (from the Path of Allah), Allah turned their hearts away (from the Right Path). And Allah guides not the people who are Fasiqun (rebellious, disobedient to Allah).*
> And when Moses said to his people, 'O my people, why do you trouble me, when you know that I am God's Messenger to you?' So when they turned away, God turned away their hearts; and God guides not the people who are defiantly disobedient.
> — **Quran 61:5**

On the basis of this principle of divine recompense, the Imams, peace upon them, taught: "Do not cast the words of wisdom before the ignorant, lest you wrong the wisdom and deprive the righteous of their right." Rather, it is through the descriptive knowledge and esoteric exegesis of the Messenger and Prophet that the delusions of the confused soul can be dispelled and doubts removed.[^cite-7]

## Corporal and Spiritual Illness and Its Remedy

God declares in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 41:53⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَنُرِيهِمْ آيَتِنَا فِى ٱلأٓفَاقِ وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ يَتَبَيَّنَ لَهُمْ أَنَّهُ ٱلْحَقُّ ۗ أَوَلَمْ يَكْفِ بِرَبِّكَ أَنَّهُۥ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ شَهِيدٌ ‎⟫
> *We will show them Our Signs in the universe, and in their ownselves, until it becomes manifest to them that this (the Quran) is the truth. Is it not sufficient in regard to your Lord that He is a Witness over all things?*
> We will show them Our signs in the world and in their own souls until it becomes clear to them that it is the truth. Is it not sufficient that their Lord is a witness over all things?
> — **Quran 41:53**

This verse makes manifest that God has inscribed His signs throughout the horizons and within the self—in both the outer and the inner realms. He has grounded His religion upon principles accessible to both. Just as a person stricken with bodily illness seeks cure through a physician and returns to health, so has God appointed a physician for spiritual sickness—one who heals the wounded soul. The parallels between these two forms of healing illuminate one another, and understanding them together leads to wisdom.

The corporal patient must approach a skilled physician and lay bare his ailment without concealment, requesting treatment. When the physician prescribes medicine to remedy his condition, the patient must take it in the proper measure and at the proper time. Likewise, the person sick in soul must turn to the learned and the wise among those who have drawn from the fountain of the Ahl al-Bayt, peace upon them—those possessed of power to dispel doubts through manifold sciences. To such scholars, the patient must confess all manner and type of doubt: whether it concerns the Guardianship, the rulings of the Shariah, or faith in the unseen. Just as a physician reads the patient's pulse and prescribes accordingly, the religious scholar must identify the spiritual malady through the patient's words—those utterances that pulse from the heart as if from the wrist—and through the manner and tone of speech, which mirror the Qur'an itself. Only then may the physician of the soul prescribe the remedy.

The corporal physician must unite two virtues: expertise in his art and scrupulous integrity. He must not offer false counsel for worldly gain, nor worsen the patient's condition through ignorance or negligence, selling false hopes. Similarly, the physician of the soul must refrain from advancing statements that heighten his psychiatric patient's suspicions, whether from love of praise or worldly renown. Every true scholar must be mature, powerful, and expert in knowledge; yet he must also be loving, compassionate, and gentle toward his patient.

The physician himself must enjoy good health—not suffering from an ailment that blinds him to the patient's pulse, not blind to the examination of the body's signs. Just so must the scholar's faith be firm and sound, his understanding correct, his thoughts clear and vigorous. He must not himself be caught in doubts that cloud and confuse his mind; rather, his intellect must remain luminous and keen, able to discern from a patient's first words where the defect of faith lies and who has planted it. A healthy physician possesses a keen sense of the pulse; similarly, a true scholar is a realist, grounded in vision.

The corporal patient must take the medicine prescribed at the right time and in the right amount, persevering in its use; he must avoid what the physician forbids and endure with patience until the illness passes and he is made whole. So too must the spiritual patient practice the actions that the scholar prescribes—whether these be Shariah ordinances or words of wisdom—and forsake the evils he is commanded to abandon. He must persist in steadfastness until the disease departs and he attains wellness.

When patient and physician alike possess these qualities, recovery becomes assured. Both the apparent treatment and the esoteric exegesis will prove efficacious.

## And When It Is Said to Them: Do Not Cause Corruption in the Land

God commands us to refrain from mischief:

> ⟪quran 2:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ لَا تُفْسِدُوا۟ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ قَالُوٓا۟ إِنَّمَا نَحْنُ مُصْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when it is said to them: "Make not mischief on the earth," they say: "We are only peacemakers." *
> And when it is said to them, Do not cause turmoil in the land, they say, We are only peacemakers.
> — **Quran 2:11**

The scholars of the daʿwa teach that ⟪ar:'فساد فی الارض'⟫ corruption in the land encompasses three categories of grave transgression—sins momentous in both their outer and inner dimensions:

| 1 | The unjust slaying of a human being |
| --- |
| 2 | Cutting the way (highway robbery and banditry) |
| 3 | Consuming the wealth of orphans |

### The Slaying of the Human Being

The most grievous of these three is the unlawful killing of an innocent person. Yet against this outward murder stands an inner murder, surpassing even the outer in its gravity. God declares:

> ⟪quran 5:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مِنْ أَجْلِ ذَلِكَ كَتَبْنَا عَلَىٰ بَنِىٓ إِسْرَٓءِيلَ أَنَّهُۥ مَن قَتَلَ نَفْسًۢا بِغَيْرِ نَفْسٍ أَوْ فَسَادٍۢ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ فَكَأَنَّمَا قَتَلَ ٱلنَّاسَ جَمِيعًۭا وَمَنْ أَحْيَاهَا فَكَأَنَّمَآ أَحْيَا ٱلنَّاسَ جَمِيعًۭا ۚ وَلَقَدْ جَآءَتْهُمْ رُسُلُنَا بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ ثُمَّ إِنَّ كَثِيرًۭا مِّنْهُم بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ لَمُسْرِفُونَ ‎⟫
> *Because of that We ordained for the Children of Israel that if anyone killed a person not in retaliation of murder, or (and) to spread mischief in the land - it would be as if he killed all mankind, and if anyone saved a life, it would be as if he saved the life of all mankind. And indeed, there came to them Our Messengers with clear proofs, evidences, and signs, even then after that many of them continued to exceed the limits (e.g. by doing oppression unjustly and exceeding beyond the limits set by Allah by committing the major sins) in the land!. *
> That is why We decreed for the Children of Israel: Whoever kills a human being, except for murder or for spreading corruption in the land, it is as if he had killed all mankind, and whoever gives life to another has saved the life of all mankind. Our Messengers have come to them with clear commands, yet many of them transgress in the land.
> — **Quran 5:32**

At first glance, this assertion seems extravagant: the soul of any individual appears too insignificant for the death of one person to reverberate through all mankind. Yet when this verse is returned to its true signification, clarity emerges. By "soul" (al-nafs), the verse refers not to every mortal, but to a transcendent soul—a Prophet, a Prophet's successor, or an Imam—upon whose soul the souls of all humanity depend. Therefore it is said: ⟪ar-quote:موت العالِم موت العالَم⟫ "The death of a scholar is the death of the world," signifying that to slay the Prophet, his successor, or the Imam, to strip him from his station or to kill him in spirit, is in truth to kill all the people of the world. For the Imam stands as the guarantor of eternal life and salvation for all humanity; his Guardianship is the soul of the Shariah, and strip away that soul, and the law lies as a corpse, lifeless and without function. In the second part of the verse, God declares that whoever revives one soul—whoever restores the Prophet, his successor, or the Imam to their rightful station—has revived all mankind. Through this restoration, salvation flows to every soul.

Observe that at the verse's beginning God uses the word "soul" (nafs), yet does not say ⟪ar:من قتل ⟪ar:'انسانا'⟫⟫ "killed a human being." At the end, He uses "souls" (al-nufūs), yet does not say ⟪ar:کانما قتل⟫ "as if he killed human beings" (al-nās). This apparent incongruence—far from the harmony and proportion one would expect—hints that the Almighty's intention transcends the literal surface. The truth is this: whoever removes the soul of the world—the Guardian (al-wāsī), from his station of Guardianship, or the Imam from the throne of Imamate, or the Summoner from his office of summoning, usurping their authority or killing them in body or in spirit—has played with the lives of countless millions and deserves a punishment commensurate with the murder of all humanity. Conversely, those who struggle against the usurpers and restore to the Guardian his rightful place merit a reward as great as saving all the world's people. ⟪ar:'نفس رسول'⟫ What evils have not the hypocrites spread in the earth through the inner slaying of ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ Ali ibn Abi Talib, the Commander of the Faithful?

### The Cutting of the Way

Brigands waylay the caravan upon the road; those who defend their goods are slain, and terror is sown among travelers, deterring them from that path. In the spiritual realm, the hypocrites followed this same method. After the Prophet's departure, they inflicted atrocities upon those walking the path of righteousness, barring them from the way of guidance. They slew—in spirit if not in body—those who had sworn their allegiance to the Guardianship of Ali and severed these from the path of truth. In the end, they brought martyrdom even upon the guides and leaders themselves. These hypocrites, who parade as reformers, are in reality the greatest corruptors.

### The Consumption of Orphans' Wealth

The hypocrites perpetrated this crime as well. Outwardly, they seized from Fāṭimah al-Batūl, daughter of the Prophet, her rightful inheritance. The orphan stands defenseless—thus the Messenger of God said to Fāṭimah, peace upon her: ⟪ar-quote:واللہ انتم المستضعفون من بعدی⟫ "By God, you shall be made weak after me." In the spiritual dimension, there are orphans—the pure Imams in their respective ages—unparalleled, each one a unique and precious pearl (durr yatīm) whose value surpasses measure. Every Imam stands as a perfected human being, a personification of completeness. God addressed the Messenger in this sense:

> ⟪quran 93:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَلَمْ يَجِدْكَ يَتِيمًۭا فَـَٔاوَىٰ ‎⟫
> *Did He not find you (O Muhammad (Peace be upon him)) an orphan and gave you a refuge?*
> Did He not find you an orphan then give you shelter?
> — **Quran 93:6**

To usurp the wealth and rights of these spiritual orphans—to seize the office of Imamate and Caliphate—is a grave transgression. It was never lawful for the usurpers to sit in judgment as bearers of the sciences of revelation and esoteric exegesis, as repositories of the Shariah and truth, to ascend the Prophet's pulpit and deliver sermons in his name. To commit such acts is to consume the wealth of orphans. The Qur'an threatens this crime with severe punishment.

God declares in Surah al-Hijr:

> ⟪quran 15:87⟫

> — **Quran 15:87**

With God's grace, there are seven sūrahs of the Qur'an—the first being Surah al-Baqarah and the last Surah al-Anfāl—or in another interpretation, the seven verses of Surah al-Fātiḥah. Yet in truth, the *Seven Repetitions* (al-Saba' al-Muthānī) refers to the Imams of the age of Muhammad. These Imams are organized into seven cycles (adwār), and within each cycle stand seven Imams, making a total of forty-nine Imams across the epochs.

The gathering of all these (forty-nine) Imam-forms will be assembled in his luminous presence. In other words, the mention of the Mighty Qur'an that follows the seven repetitions indicates that there shall appear seven cycles of Imams during the age of Muhammad, comprising in total forty-nine Imams. Just as the Qur'an brings together all verses into a unified whole, so too shall all these Imam-images be gathered together in him.[^cite-9]

## Those Whose Evil Deeds God Transforms into Good Deeds

God declares in Surah al-Furqān:

> ⟪quran 25:70⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِلَّا مَن تَابَ وَآمَنَ وَعَمِلَ عَمَلًۭا صَلِحًۭا فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ يُبَدِّلُ ٱللَّهُ سَيِّـَٔاتِهِمْ حَسَنَتٍۢ ۗ وَكَانَ ٱللَّهُ غَفُورًۭا رَّحِيمًۭا ‎⟫

> Save him who repents and believes, and does righteous deeds, for whom Allah will change the evil deeds into good deeds; and Allah is all-forgiving, all-merciful.
> — **Quran 25:70**

This verse seems to negate the assertion found elsewhere in the Qur'an that "Whoever does an atom's weight of good shall see it, and whoever does an atom's weight of evil shall see it." God declares in another place that He will render one's deeds present and manifest before him. Taken at face value, a profound contradiction seems to emerge between the apparent verses of the Qur'an. What resolves this apparent discord?

Conversely, "evil deeds" in this sense signifies the false Imams of error, who mislead and lead astray, summoning their adherents toward deeds that end in Hell.

| Good Deeds | Evil Deeds |
| --- |
| Imams of Truth | Imams of Misguidance |

When a person who in former times obeyed the false Imams, disobeying and showing enmity toward the true Imams, turns by God's grace toward the Straight Path and accepts the covenant of the Imam of Truth, pledging his obedience, and acquires understanding of all the Prophets and Imams and comes to love the Imam and his followers, then such a person exemplifies one whose evil deeds God has transformed into good. God removes him from the center of disobedience and inclines him toward the Source of Beauty—the Imam of Truth—diverting him from the path to Hell toward the path to Paradise. His evil thoughts are transmuted into virtuous thoughts, his evil end into a good one. Here lies the correct resolution that dissolves the seeming contradiction!

## "When It Is Said to Them: Believe as the People Have Believed"

In this verse, Allāh describes the hypocrites (al-munāfiqūn). The term "hypocrisy" itself derives etymologically from *nifāq al-yarbūʿ*—the burrow of a rat or jackal—which possesses multiple exits. Close one passage, and the creature escapes through another. The hypocrites resemble such creatures: outwardly they share characteristics with both the believers and the unbelievers, yet their hearts remain fixed upon worldly gain alone. They harbor no genuine concern for religion or faith. In every age, this group proves perilously destructive; even in the time of the Prophet, no faction inflicted greater harm upon the Muslim community than the hypocrites themselves.

True faith (*īmān*) signifies confirmation grounded in knowledge. One cannot authentically affirm what one does not comprehend; mere assumption that a speaker does not lie confers no validity upon such affirmation. Allāh does not accept the testimony of a witness devoid of knowledge.[^cite-1] For this reason, the belief that Allāh has obligated us to affirm the unseen while simultaneously depriving us of the means to know it contradicts both reason and divine justice. Allāh has ordained two paths of knowledge: sensation, through which we perceive tangible phenomena; and intellect (*ʿaql*, also termed *baṣīra*—direct insight), through which we apprehend intelligible truths.

**Sensible Things:**

Allāh has endowed us with eyes, nose, tongue, and ears to perceive color, smell, taste, and sound, and with bodily sensation to detect texture and density throughout our frames.

**Intelligible Things:**

Matters beyond sensory reach—understood through reason alone, not felt through sensation—comprise all religious rulings and doctrinal beliefs. The Prophet brought through the Qur'ān and Sharīʿa (referring to the Throne and Footstool, Paradise and Hell) all belong to this second category of unseen realities. Though imperceptible to the senses, reason can comprehend them—*provided one possesses a teacher who explains them and the will to understand*.[^cite-2]

These hypocrites, having abandoned the author of *taʾwīl* (esoteric exegesis) and refusing even to seek understanding of the inner meanings that illuminate the unseen, render their "faith" a mockery rather than authentic belief. All matters of Sharīʿa and Scripture elude sensory perception; they are entirely rational in nature. Yet the hypocrites have taken enmity toward those possessed of intellect. How, then, can they understand? Their affirmation becomes ignorant assent to the Messenger without comprehension—a confession that repels every thinking person and incurs Allāh's displeasure. Rejecting such baseless faith, Allāh declares:

> ⟪quran 2:13⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ آمِنُوا۟ كَمَآ آمَنَ ٱلنَّاسُ قَالُوٓا۟ أَنُؤْمِنُ كَمَآ آمَنَ ٱلسُّفَهَآءُ ۗ أَلَآ إِنَّهُمْ هُمُ ٱلسُّفَهَآءُ وَلَكِن لَّا يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when it is said to them, "Believe as the people have believed," they say, "Shall we believe as the fools have believed?" Unquestionably, it is they who are the fools, but they do not know.*

Among them are those who harbor mere opinions and conjectures. They do not grasp the truth that Allāh will raise the dead from their graves on the Day of Resurrection, yet they profess it formally only because the Messenger has mentioned it in the Qur'ān. Similarly, they accept all doctrines concerning the Tablet and the Pen, the Throne and the Footstool, the Balance and the Path, Paradise and Hell, and the Hereafter solely because the Messenger has commanded such belief—and since the Messenger does not lie, all must be true. Yet they fail to recognize that these realities, though unseen, are intelligible to reason, and that the human intellect can comprehend them. Allāh utterly rejects such faith; it yields neither purification of self, sincerity of heart, nor nobility of soul. It is faith mixed with *ẓulm* (oppression; literal: darkness), and therefore stands rejected in Allāh's sight. In contrast, Allāh praises the true believers:

> ⟪quran 6:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَلَمْ يَلْبِسُوٓا۟ إِيمَنَهُم بِظُلْمٍ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ لَهُمُ ٱلْأَمْنُ وَهُم مُّهْتَدُونَ ‎⟫
> *Those who believe and do not confuse their faith with idolatry, theirs is security, and they are on the right path.*

Ordinary Muslims interpret *ẓulm* here as *shirk* (polytheism), which is entirely correct and beyond doubt. Allāh states in His Book:

> ⟪quran 31:13⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ لُقْمَنُ لِٱبْنِهِۦ وَهُوَ يَعِظُهُۥ يَبُنَىَّ لَا تُشْرِكْ بِٱللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّ ٱلشِّرْكَ لَظُلْمٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And when Luqman said to his son, while he was admonishing him, "O my son, do not associate anything with Allah; indeed associating others with Allah is a tremendous injustice."*

Two forms of *shirk* exist: *shirk jālī* (manifest polytheism) and *shirk khafī* (hidden polytheism).

**Manifest Polytheism (*Shirk Jālī*):**

This is the polytheism in which the pre-Islamic Arabs and idolaters were entangled—the worship of stone idols, the trinitarian belief of Christians, and the dualism of the Zoroastrians.

**Hidden Polytheism (*Shirk Khafī*):**

This polytheism afflicts the Umma of the Messenger of Allāh. He said: "Shirk in my community is more concealed than the crawling of a black ant upon a black stone in the darkness of night."[^cite-3] This shirk escapes the comprehension of the average believer. The simple person imagines that upon reciting the *Kalima Ṭayyiba* (the declaration of faith), he has become a monotheist. Yet he does not understand that shirk consists in failing to recognize Allāh's stations—both spiritual and temporal—and in attributing to others what belongs to Allāh alone. Whosoever ascribes the attributes of prayer (*duʿā*) to another has contaminated his faith with shirk, committing thereby a grave injustice and enormous sin, and has thereby been deprived of peace and guidance.

> ⟪quran 2:8⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَقُولُ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَبِٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ وَمَا هُم بِمُؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫
> *And of mankind, there are some (hypocrites) who say: "We believe in Allah and the Last Day" while in fact they believe not.*

The hypocrites, whose faith was thus tainted with polytheism, were commanded: "Believe with sincerity and truthfulness, even as the people believe with sincere intention and affirm with understanding." Two categories of humanity exist: one—the party of the Merciful (*Ḥizb al-Raḥmān*)—consists of the servants of Allāh (*ʿIbād al-Raḥmān*) aligned with Allāh's congregation; the other forms the party of Satan. In this verse, "the people" refers to those servants of the Merciful associated with Allāh's assembly. Those addressed to believe are *not* "the people" in Allāh's reckoning. Though human in outward form and appearance, the two groups possess no correspondence in spiritual and psychological reality.[^cite-4] Therefore, the hypocrites were commanded to believe and affirm like the first group—the true people—so that spiritual compatibility might be established between them, as physical resemblance already exists through shared human organs and appearance.

It is fitting to draw an analogy with the Messenger and the Prophet, for both are parents of their respective communities. Similarly, after the Prophet, the Imam and his *ḥudūd* (the upper and lower limits—his designated representatives) are the masters of guidance and rectitude. Those "people" to whom this verse refers are the Imāms descended from the Holy Prophet. The Qur'ān itself furnishes proof:

> ⟪quran 4:54⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ يَحْسُدُونَ ٱلنَّاسَ عَلَىٰ مَآ آتَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ مِن فَضْلِهِۦ ۖ فَقَدْ آتَيْنَآ آلَإِبْرَهِيمَ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْحِكْمَةَ وَآتَيْنَهُم مُّلْكًا عَظِيمًۭا ‎⟫
> *Or are they jealous of the people for what God has given them of His bounty? We have given the family of Abraham the Book and wisdom, and We have given them a great kingdom.*

Here, *faḍl* (bounty) refers to the Khilāfa and the Imāma. Maulānā Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) expounds this verse: "We are the people of whom Allāh has said they have been wronged—for why did Allāh bless us with His grace, namely the Caliphate and the Imāmate? Why did He not bestow this grace upon all? In this verse, the 'great kingdom' means that We have appointed them Imāms whose sovereignty extends over the hearts of the people, and their obedience and disobedience are conjoined with ours. The question arises: Why do the people acknowledge this great kingdom in the family of Ibrāhīm but deny it in the family of Muḥammad?"

**Who Is the Perfect Man?**

The absolute man, the perfect human being (*al-insān al-kāmil*), remains one in every age and epoch. In his time, the Prophet embodies this perfection; in his time, the *Wasī* (the Executor of the Prophet's Will) embodies it; in his time, the Imām embodies it. Whoever draws nearer to this Absolute Man in religion, whoever resembles him more closely in knowledge and belief, deserves more truly the title of humanity and stands farther removed from the animal state. In response to Allāh's call, the hypocrites said:

> ⟪quran 2:13⟫ (repeated)
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ آمِنُوا۟ كَمَآ آمَنَ ٱلنَّاسُ قَالُوٓا۟ أَنُؤْمِنُ كَمَآ آمَنَ ٱلسُّفَهَآءُ ۗ أَلَآ إِنَّهُمْ هُمُ ٱلسُّفَهَآءُ وَلَكِن لَّا يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when it is said to them, "Believe as the people have believed," they say, "Shall we believe as the fools have believed?" Unquestionably, it is they who are the fools, but they do not know.*

**Who Is Called a Fool?**

Foolishness and stupidity manifest in three domains:

| In Speech | In Opinions and Counsel | In the Expenditure of Wealth |
|---|---|---|
| *(The foolish speak carelessly)* | *(The foolish offer poor counsel)* | *(The foolish squander their means)* |

Allāh states:

> ⟪quran 4:5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تُؤْتُوا۟ ٱلسُّفَهَآءَ أَمْوَلَكُمُ ٱلَّتِى جَعَلَ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ قِيَمًۭا ‎⟫
> *And give not unto the foolish your property which Allah has made a means of support for you.*

Plainly, the Prophet and his followers suffered from none of these three defects. The Holy Prophet was known from childhood by the title *al-Amīn* (the Trustworthy), a title his own people bestowed upon him on account of his integrity, intellect, noble character, and virtues. Even after his commission as Prophet, when they refused to accept him, Allāh said:

> ⟪quran 6:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَدْ نَعْلَمُ إِنَّهُۥ لَيَحْزُنُكَ ٱلَّذِى يَقُولُونَ ۖ فَإِنَّهُمْ لَا يُكَذِّبُونَكَ وَلَكِنَّ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ بِـَٔايَتِ ٱللَّهِ يَجْحَدُونَ ‎⟫
> *We know that what they say grieves you, wherefore they do not deny thee, but it is the revelations of Allah that the wrongdoers reject.*

The exegetes have rendered this as: they do not deny your trustworthiness and veracity, for they well know you to be trustworthy and never false. Their denial concerns only Allāh's signs, not your character. Two words in this verse merit attention. The first clause employs denial (*taksīb*), while the second employs rejection (*jḥād—deliberate aversion or defiance). Stylistically, one might expect *yakzibūn* (they lie) in the second clause as well. Yet *jḥād* signifies deliberate turning away or denial born of willfulness, as Allāh states:

> ⟪quran 27:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَجَحَدُوا۟ بِهَا وَٱسْتَيْقَنَتْهَآ أَنفُسُهُمْ ظُلْمًۭا وَعُلُوًّۭا ۚ فَٱنظُرْ كَيْفَ كَانَ عَقِبَةُ ٱلْمُفْسِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *And they belied them out of cruelty and arrogance, while their hearts were sure of them. So see what was the end of the mischief-makers.*

This proves their refusal stemmed solely from injustice and arrogance. Moreover, Allāh terms the disbelievers *ẓālimīn* (the unjust), not *kāfirīn* (the unbelievers). Had they denied the outward Qur'ān and its verses, they would more aptly deserve the name "unbelievers." All this testifies that through *taʾwīl*, these people are those who have corrupted and defiled their faith with oppression. The greatest form of oppression is polytheism. Therefore, these outward believers and inward hypocrites did not oppose the outward commandments of the Messenger; rather, they opposed his successor and refused to recognize the status of the Imāms who will descend from his progeny until the Day of Judgment—the true signs of Allāh. Thus they are oppressors; and since outward Islam persists in them, such disbelievers and polytheists are not those with whom the Muslim is obliged to sever relations. Finally, Allāh declares:

> ⟪quran 2:256⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَآ إِكْرَاهَ فِى ٱلدِّينِ ۖ قَد تَّبَيَّنَ ٱلرُّشْدُ مِنَ ٱلْغَىِّ ۚ فَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱلطَّغُوتِ وَيُؤْمِنۢ بِٱللَّهِ فَقَدِ ٱسْتَمْسَكَ بِٱلْعُرْوَةِ ٱلْوُثْقَىٰ لَا ٱنفِصَامَ لَهَا ۗ وَٱللَّهُ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ ‎⟫
> *There is no compulsion in religion. Verily, the Right Path has become distinct from the wrong path. Whoever disbelieves in Taghut and believes in Allah, then he has grasped the most trustworthy handhold that will never break. And Allah is All-Hearer, All-Knower.*

The stupidity of these hypocrites lay in their failure to comprehend that *wilāyat* (spiritual guardianship) of the *Wasī* is as obligatory as obedience to the Messenger of Allāh.[^cite-5] Just as the *Kalima Ṭayyiba* (the declaration "there is no god but Allāh") first negates and then affirms, as Allāh says:

> ⟪quran 2:256⟫ (repeated)
> ⟪ar:‏فَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱلطَّغُوتِ وَيُؤْمِنۢ بِٱللَّهِ ‎⟫
> *Whoever disbelieves in Taghut and believes in Allah*

Here negation (*kufr bi-l-Ṭāghūt*) comes first, affirmation (*īmān bi-llāh*) second. And similarly:

> ⟪quran 2:256⟫ (repeated)
> ⟪ar:‏فَقَدِ ٱسْتَمْسَكَ بِٱلْعُرْوَةِ ٱلْوُثْقَىٰ ‎⟫
> *(He has grasped) the most trustworthy handhold*

Here too, affirmation of Allāh's unity precedes the repudiation of false lords.

Those who suppose others to be fools are themselves foolish in the extreme—they have squandered and ruined a great portion of their religion through stupidity and ignorance. Deprived of knowledge of Allāh, an invaluable treasure for soul and intellect, they have failed to appreciate the eternal and imperishable blessings Allāh bestows. They do not themselves know what they have lost or how profoundly they have suffered:

> ⟪quran 2:121⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آتَيْنَهُمُ ٱلْكِتَبَ يَتْلُونَهُۥ حَقَّ تِلَاوَتِهِۦ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِهِۦ ۗ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِهِۦ فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْخَسِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Those to whom We gave the Book recite it as it ought to be recited; they believe in it. Those who disbelieve in it, theirs are the losers.*

(From *Majālis al-Muʾayyadiyya*, Sayyidnā al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)

## "Their Likeness Is as the Likeness of One Who Kindled a Fire"

**The Meaning of Fire:**

Fire ranks among the noblest of the four elements. Unlike water and earth, it possesses no solid substance. Allāh created it to refine all things; its virtue lies in Allāh's subjugation of it to all human beings—indeed, to no other creature. Only humanity has been granted the wisdom to strike fire from wood, stone, and iron, and to preserve it in receptacles that will accept it, such as cotton or paper. Sulfur exemplifies such materials—its constituent elements harbor fire in hidden form, and it readily catches the flame from iron and stone, preserving it within itself. Without this property, it would be impossible to kindle great timbers from a spark struck in the forest, for their density and frequent dampness resist ignition. 

Fire, therefore, first ignites and illuminates those substances whose composition rapidly yields to combustion, and then gradually consumes harder materials, burning with ever-increasing vigor. In extreme cases, it becomes a conflagration beyond human control.

Within human nature, "humanity" (*insāniyya*) lies concealed as fire lies hidden within flint. Of all creatures, only the human strikes the spark—the fire—from this outward stone. Similarly, the man who kindles humanity is the perfect man, the guide of his age, the Prophet or the Imām. Just as a spark proves useless until it reaches the hand of one who can draw forth light, so too the intellect of every human remains powerless until he benefits from the Messenger and surrenders himself to him. The Messenger rubs the spark upon the stone of Sharīʿa and renders it luminous and bright. He alone is saved; whoever the Messenger rejects stands rejected and harmed.

**Spiritual Fire and Sensible Fire:**

Second, the fire latent within stones and vegetation can be struck only by humanity—never by beasts. Similarly, there exists a spiritual fire that Moses beheld upon the mountain—what is termed the *Rūḥ al-Quds* (the Holy Spirit). This spiritual fire is none other than the fire of support (*ʿiṣma*), prophethood (*nubuwwa*), the testament (*waṣiyya*), and the Imāmate (*imāma*). It is also called the Sovereignty (*sulṭān*) granted by Allāh to His chosen servants, through which they become exalted and appointed as Allāh's elite. This dominion extends over both the body and soul of humanity; through its power, the Messenger of Allāh prepares people for the Hereafter. This spiritual fire approaches all as a divine blessing, yet only those possessing the capacity and qualification can benefit from it—and these are solely the saints of Allāh. Not all possess the ability to draw advantage from it.

> ⟪quran 2:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَثَلُهُمْ كَمَثَلِ ٱلَّذِى ٱسْتَوْقَدَ نَارًۭا فَلَمَّآ أَضَآءَتْ مَا حَوْلَهُۥ ذَهَبَ ٱللَّهُ بِنُورِهِمْ وَتَرَكَهُمْ فِى ظُلُمَتٍۢ لَّا يُبْصِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Their likeness is as the likeness of a man who kindled a fire, and when it lighted all around him, Allah took away their light and left them in darkness where they could not see.*

This verse depicts the hypocrites who profess attachment to the Messenger of Allāh—he who is the Sovereign of the spiritual fire and cleaves to the strong rope of Allāh. Yet when the lights of prophethood began to radiate outward and prophethood flourished as a branch in the testament of the Prophet, in the will of the Prophet, and in the Imāmate of the Imāms, Allāh stripped them of their portion of light due to envy, enmity, arrogance, and pride, and consigned them again to darkness. The hypocrites could not bear that a branch should spring from the Prophet's lineage and flourish; they sought to cast dust upon the light. The light itself could not be extinguished, but Allāh withdrew from them the light they had received. He deprived them of the illumination of the testament and the Imāmate, and they reverted to the darkness of ignorance.

**Can "Light" Mean the Outward Qur'ān?**

In other verses, the exegetes of the people interpret "light" as the *Qur'ān*, yet they do not grasp its meaning in this verse. If light signified the outward Qur'ān, which rests in every hand, what purpose would Allāh's withdrawal of it serve? The Qur'ān remains in their outward possession, their sight intact, and the physical light of sun and moon shines clearly upon them.

The scholars therefore declare that "light" does not refer to the outward Qur'ān inscribed upon paper, but rather to the *ʿAlim* (the Knower) of the Qur'ān, or to the spiritual and inner Qur'ān, which is termed the *Wasī* or the Imām of his time. Through their denial of him, people are deprived of luminous knowledge (*nūrāniyya*).

> ⟪quran 2:257⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱللَّهُ وَلِىُّ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ يُخْرِجُهُم مِّنَ ٱلظُّلُمَتِ إِلَى ٱلنُّورِ ۖ وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ أَوْلِيَآؤُهُمُ ٱلطَّغُوتُ يُخْرِجُونَهُم مِّنَ ٱلنُّورِ إِلَى ٱلظُّلُمَتِ ۗ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *Allah is the Protector of those who believe; He brings them out of darkness into the light. As for those who disbelieve, their patrons are the devils; they bring them out of light into darkness; they are the inhabitants of the Fire, and they will abide therein forever.*

**What Is Meant by "Oppression"?**

By denying the author of the Qur'ān, their vision and heart grow blind. They are stripped of spiritual sciences, Qur'ānic truths, and radiant knowledge. They stumble at every step, unable to answer even the simplest question of learning. Trapped in a labyrinth of meaningless words, they worship yet remain ignorant of worship's true purpose. This eternal and everlasting darkness envelops them. Though outward light and darkness shall vanish with this world, the darkness of their souls endures forever. Alluding to this light and darkness, Allāh says in *Āyat al-Kursī* (the Verse of the Throne):

> ⟪quran 2:257⟫ (repeated)
> ⟪ar:‏ٱللَّهُ وَلِىُّ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ يُخْرِجُهُم مِّنَ ٱلظُّلُمَتِ إِلَى ٱلنُّورِ ‎⟫
> *Allah is the Protector of those who believe; He brings them out of darkness into the light.*

That is, Allāh—through the Imām of the age—draws the believers forth from the darkness of superficial words and figurative speech into eternal and enduring light.

As for those who deny the stations of Allāh and ascribe to others what belongs to Him alone, their guardians are the *Ṭāghūt* (false lords and tyrants). A person who opposes the Prophet, the Prophet's heir, or the Imām—or one who ascends the pulpit in the Messenger's place without authorization—is a Ṭāghūt. Their function is to draw people from light (inner meaning) into darkness (outward words alone). Since they comprehend not the Qur'ān's meaning, they content themselves with literal translation and lead all astray in a labyrinth of words. Darkness and blindness have truly overtaken their souls, for they turned away from those who would have guided them to the realm of the soul and illumination.

(From *Majālis al-Muʾayyadiyya*, Sayyidnā al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)

## "He Who Made the Earth a Bed for You, and the Heaven a Canopy"

Allāh states:

> ⟪quran 2:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى جَعَلَ لَكُمُ ٱلْأَرْضَ فِرَشًۭا وَٱلسَّمَآءَ بِنَآءًۭا وَأَنزَلَ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭا فَأَخْرَجَ بِهِۦ مِنَ ٱلثَّمَرَتِ رِزْقًۭا لَّكُمْ ۖ فَلَا تَجْعَلُوا۟ لِلَّهِ أَندَادًۭا وَأَنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Who made the earth a bed for you, and the sky a canopy, and sent down water from the sky, and brought forth therewith fruits as sustenance for you. So do not set up rivals to Allah while you know.*

Allāh's word addresses every human being—the ignorant and the learned, the elect and the common people alike—according to their understanding and knowledge. To ordinary Muslims, "heaven and earth" signify the heavens and earth of this physical world, wherein both animals and humans dwell and meet all their material needs.

Yet the mystical sages of truth perceive differently. Since the human being comprises two elements—the soul and the body—there must necessarily exist, by analogy to the heavens and earth of the material world, a heaven and earth belonging to the realm of the soul (religion). This visible, tangible earth, which our body inhabits, has unquestionably been made our floor, our bed, our soil; we are born from it, and after death we are buried within it, returning to dust. Our soul, however, is not of the substance of this physical earth—it has nothing to do with material earth at all.

The Prophet declared: "The earth is your mother, and she treats you well." If we interpret this as referring to the material earth alone, the statement becomes nonsensical—the physical earth is neither our mother, nor can it treat us well, for benevolence requires will and independent agency, while earth is compelled and acts without volition. Yet the earth does sustain our bodies: it produces plants by which we nourish ourselves.

In light of this hadith, we must acknowledge another earth—one endowed with will, knowledge, and power. From this spiritual earth, every believer obtains and cultivates all the necessities of spiritual life. After death, he returns to it and merges with it. This spiritual and inner earth is the Prophet or the Imām (peace be upon him), from whose spiritual being the believer's soul draws its nourishment. From this earth the believer springs forth, born into divine sciences; into this earth he returns, merging with its truth. Thus this earth—the *Wasī* or the Imām—deserves the epithet *umm al-Barr* (the good mother).

Similarly, the heaven in this verse denotes an unfamiliar heaven—the heaven of religious knowledge, which is inward and spiritual. Heaven and earth maintain the same relationship as man and woman. Heaven bestows upon earth its waters; earth receives this bounty, absorbs it, and gives bodily form flourishing. Yet this apparent heaven, despite its elevation and eminence, remains dumb, deaf, and blind, encompassing only the dense human body and having no dominion over the soul. The human soul, however, though confined within the body's density, encompasses this vast world and possesses all knowledge concerning it.

Thus the soul, or the religious heaven, is the one who possesses the Sharīʿa—the Messenger of Allāh or the Imām of his time—who elevates the soul to its highest rank and bestows blessings upon the religious earth (*Wasī* or the Proof of the Imām). The earth develops the soul's form and renders it worthy of the Hereafter and perpetual life. This psychic heaven and all related religious sciences are eternal and everlasting, while the outer heaven and its entire system shall perish and pass away.

| Heaven | Earth |
|---|---|
| Speaker | Guardian |
| Imām | Proof of the Imām |

It is evident from the chain of verses that water and fruits, like heaven and earth, possess hidden meanings in this passage. To ordinary people, water means the outward liquid by which they quench thirst, and fruit means the outward produce from which they derive pleasure and fill their bellies.

The wise declare: "The water that descends from heaven signifies the revelation (*waḥy*) that Allāh sent to the Messenger of Allāh, which the Messenger clothed in the words of the Qur'ān and conveyed to the earth of the Guardian—that is, he transmitted this Qur'ānic knowledge to his successor (ʿAlī)." Similarly, water refers to knowledge obtained from the Imām in every era through his proof.

**Outer Water and Inner Water:**

Allāh states:

> ⟪quran 21:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَوَلَمْ يَرَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ أَنَّ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ كَانَتَا رَتْقًۭا فَفَتَقْنَهُمَا ۖ وَجَعَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلْمَآءِ كُلَّ شَىْءٍ حَىٍّ ۖ أَفَلَا يُؤْمِنُونَ ‎⟫
> *Do not the unbelievers see that the heavens and the earth were one mass, then We tore them asunder, and We created every living thing of water? Will they not believe?*

One might ask: Are angels not Allāh's creation? Certainly they are; they exist as created beings. If they did not exist, they would be non-beings, yet objects are things that possess existence. Every Muslim acknowledges that angels exist and are created by Allāh as are we. Do they not require the water of life? Allāh then states:

> ⟪quran 8:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِذْ يُغَشِّيكُمُ ٱلنُّعَاسَ أَمَنَةًۭ مِّنْهُ وَيُنَزِّلُ عَلَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭا لِّيُطَهِّرَكُم بِهِۦ وَيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمْ رِجْزَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ وَلِيَرْبِطَ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِكُمْ وَيُثَبِّتَ بِهِ ٱلْأَقْدَامَ ‎⟫
> *When He cast slumber upon you as a repose of Himself, and sent down water from the sky upon you, to cleanse you with it, and to remove from you the uncleanness of Satan, and to strengthen your hearts, and to steady your feet with it.*

It is intelligible that outward water descends from the sky for our physical cleansing. But how can outward water remove Satan's filth? Had outward water possessed such efficacy, there would exist no disbeliever or polytheist in the world today, nor any denial of Allāh and His Messenger. Likewise, physical water cannot increase the love of hearts, reconcile enemies, steady wavering steps, or guide the astray to firmness. It is manifest from these verses that the water appropriate to religious knowledge is nothing other than the knowledge of the Imām. This knowledge is what enables the life of angels and all souls; through it alone can the filth of Satan—doubt, disbelief, polytheism—be cleansed away; and through it scattered hearts are united and faltering feet are steadied.

> ⟪quran 3:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *It is He who has sent down to you the Book, in which some of its verses are definitive, they are the origin of the Book, and others are allegorical. As for those in whose hearts is deviation, they follow that which is allegorical thereof, seeking dissension and seeking its interpretation; none knows its interpretation except God and those firmly grounded in knowledge. They say, We believe in it; it is all from our Lord. And none are reminded except those who possess understanding.*

The water descending from heaven is twofold: first, water that brings direct benefit, such as quenching thirst; second, water that seeps into the earth, producing fruits and vegetables that become sustenance for humans and animals. Water, thus, is a source of life in both modes.

Similarly, revelation sent from heaven is twofold. First, the outward commandments to be obeyed, which instill in the soul virtues of purity, godliness, and conformity to the angels—as in prayer, which removes indecency and wrongdoing, and fasting, which teaches abstinence from eating and drinking as the angels do, thereby guarding oneself from all moral transgression. Wealth is purified through alms, and sins are forgiven through pilgrimage. Second, the knowledge of revelation hidden within the earth in the form of Qur'ānic words—called *taʾwīl* (esoteric interpretation) and *ḥaqīqa* (truth)—of which Allāh says:

> ⟪quran 3:7⟫ (repeated)
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ‎⟫
> *(None knows its interpretation except God and those firmly grounded in knowledge.)*

Just as the human being—the essence of the world and the intellect—produces the fruits and vegetables of the earth through his labor, so the scholar of *taʾwīl* draws out the realities and meanings from the Qur'ānic words (the *Wasī*) and conveys to believers the truth of monotheism and knowledge of the Qur'ān's origin, bestowing upon them the delight of eternal and imperishable fruits.

One of the Qur'ān's greatest virtues is that, however much its fruits are consumed, they do not diminish or decrease—unlike worldly fruits, which are exhausted by eating. The more one reflects upon it, the more new and profound becomes one's understanding; each further meditation upon a verse yields a fresh meaning (pleasure and flavor) every time. For this reason, Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) taught that the meanings of Qur'ānic verses have no limit.[^cite-6] This is the boundless sea into which every seeker must dive and emerge with something precious. The Qur'ānic knowledge resembles the fruits of Paradise, which increase rather than diminish through consumption. Someone asked *Amīr al-Muʾminīn* (the Commander of the Faithful), "What fruit of Paradise exists in this world?" He replied, "The Qur'ān!" Moreover, these Qur'ānic sciences resemble a lamp that kindles thousands of lamps without its own light diminishing.

> ⟪quran 2:22⟫ (repeated)
> ⟪ar:‏فَلَا تَجْعَلُوا۟ لِلَّهِ أَندَادًۭا وَأَنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *(Do not set up) rivals to Allah while you know.*

That is, do not take anyone besides your Lord as creator and sustainer. Outward sustenance is obtained through the earth; inward sustenance through the Prophets and their heirs. Therefore, ascribe no partnership to Him, but worship Him alone.

## And if you are in doubt concerning that which We have revealed unto Our servant, then produce a chapter like it

Allah Most High declares:

⟪quran 2:24⟫
⟪ar:فَإِن كُنتُمْ فِي رَيْبٍۢ مِّمَّا نَزَّلْنَا عَلَىٰ عَبْدِنَا فَأْتُوا۟ بِسُورَةٍۢ مِّن مِّثْلِهِ ۖ وَادْعُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَكُم مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫

This divine challenge addresses two distinct categories of adversaries: the manifest deniers of the Qur'an's authenticity, and those who concealed their rejection beneath an outward profession of belief. The external meaning is sufficiently clear—if those who doubt that the Qur'an proceeds from God possess any capacity to match its eloquence, let them produce a single chapter of comparable merit. Yet the Qur'an directs its challenge at multiple strata of opposition simultaneously.

### The Apparent Challenge and the Hidden Doubt

At the outward level, the disbelievers and polytheists—supported by elements among the Jews and Christians who fabricated lies against the Prophet—questioned whether the Qur'an truly emanated from the Divine. Allah thus challenged them: if you possess genuine ability, bring forth a book comparable to this Qur'an. The impossibility of such production stands as testimony to its inimitable origin.

Yet beneath this surface challenge lies a more profound dimension. The hypocrites (*munāfiqūn*) harbored a concealed doubt: that the Messenger of Allah—may God's blessings and peace be upon him—had not established his successor by divine command alone, but rather through kinship ties and personal attachment. They alleged he had made his bequest solely through family relationship, not through God's ordination.

To answer this inner objection, Allah addresses those who deny the divine appointment of the Guardian (*walī*) with a summons just as formidable: "If you doubt that this Messenger of Allah stands as God's Caliph upon God's earth, then produce a scholar comparable to him—one to whom the Messenger opened a thousand chapters of knowledge, and from each of these thousand chapters opened yet a thousand more."[^cite-7] Yet this standard remains beyond your reach. Rather, produce even one scholar from among your own ranks who approaches the ḥadd dānī (the lower limit) of the Imams' daʿwa (the summons of faith), and demonstrate that he possesses the knowledge and spiritual capacity befitting the successor of God's Messenger.

The Messenger's position in the sharīʿa and in religion resembles that of a farmer engaged with the land itself. Through toil and discernment, the farmer extracts the means of sustenance from the earth's depths. Similarly, the Imām (*the Imam*) draws forth from the soil of the Qur'an, through the power of taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis), meanings that are timeless and incomparable—meanings that illuminate the human soul eternally and preserve it from ruin, granting it both deathless survival and everlasting life.

### The Etymology and Meanings of Sūra

The term sūra (chapter) derives from the notion of elevation and exaltation. When a sūra was revealed to the Prophet—may God's blessings and peace be upon him—it elevated his rank and station. Each revealed chapter raised him higher in nobility and spiritual authority.

In another sense, sūra signifies a comprehensive enclosure, like the city wall that encompasses all within it. The Quranic sūra enfolds the remembrance of the Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—and contains matters of past and future, embracing all as the wall embraces the city and its inhabitants. In this way, the Qur'an encompasses true tawḥīd (monotheism), distinguishing Allah from all attributes that would liken Him to physical beings. It encompasses knowledge of time's origins—all the boundaries and limits that the sharīʿa of the Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—dominates. Every limit of the daʿwa (the invitation to faith) forms a wall of the Bāb Madīnat al-ʿIlm (the Gate of the City of Knowledge), within which resides a living city of wisdom.

Thus Allah addresses those who deny the Gate of the City of Knowledge, saying: "If you doubt that it has not been established by Allah's command, that it is not the Messenger of Allah and the Word of Allah, then produce other comprehensive attributes of equal measure. Demonstrate knowledge of the universe as vast as His. Yet you dare not do this."[^cite-8] Instead, show us one of his companions—such as Salmān al-Fārisī, Miqdād ibn Aswad, Abū Dharr al-Ghiffārī, ʿAmmār Yāsir, or Muḥammad ibn Abī Bakr—who possesses even a fraction of his knowledge and spiritual standing.

This address encompasses both the sects and parties among the disbelievers: those who deny the outward Qur'an as God's Word, and those who deny the inner meaning of revelation. The Messenger of Allah—may God's blessings and peace be upon him—was sent as mubashshir (a bearer of glad tidings) and nadhīr (a warner) to all the worlds, just as Allah declares:

⟪quran 34:28⟫
⟪ar:وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَكَ إِلَّا كَآفَّةًۭ لِّلنَّاسِ بَشِيرًۭا وَنَذِيرًۭا ‎⟫

The Prophet himself stated: "I have been sent to all mankind, to the fair-skinned and the dark-skinned alike."[^cite-9] Therefore, Allah's challenge addresses both the outward disbelievers and the inward hypocrites without distinction.

### The Challenge Extended to Jinn and Mankind

Allah then proclaims:

⟪quran 17:88⟫
⟪ar:قُل لَّىِٕنِ ٱجْتَمَعَتِ ٱلْإِنسُ وَٱلْجِنُّ عَلَىٰٓ أَن يَأْتُوا۟ بِمِثْلِ هَذَا ٱلْقُرْآنِ لَا يَأْتُونَ بِمِثْلِهِۦ وَلَوْ كَانَ بَعْضُهُمْ لِبَعْضٍۢ ظَهِيرًۭا ‎⟫

Should all jinn and mankind combine their efforts to produce a Qur'an like unto this, they shall not succeed—even were they to aid one another.

Whereas the initial verse addressed the disbelievers alone, this verse extends the challenge to all human scholars and jinn combined. Why does the Qur'an not mention the angels? Because both jinn and mankind possess the capacity for disobedience against Allah's command, whereas the angels are wholly devoted to obedience—they perform whatever they are commanded without hesitation.

When Allah renders the entire world incapable of producing an example to match the outward Qur'an with such overwhelming force, they become yet more helpless before the inner Qur'an. For the outward form is but the vessel; the inner dimension is its very soul. A sculptor may fashion the most exquisite human form, yet he cannot breathe life into it—that animation belongs to an inner reality beyond his reach. If humanity cannot produce a text to rival the outer Qur'an, how vastly more impossible is it to rival the inner Qur'an, the book of the Nāṭiq (the Speaker—the Prophet), whose esoteric dimensions are fathomless? This is why Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq said: "Whoever possesses an outward miracle possesses an inner miracle of immeasurable greatness."[^cite-10]

### The Invocation of Witnesses

Allah further emphasizes the magnitude of this challenge by invoking testimony:

⟪quran 2:24⟫
⟪ar:وَادْعُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَكُم مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ ‎⟫

"Call forth your witnesses other than Allah."

**On the Significance of Testimony**

Testimony in all affairs serves to determine truth from falsehood between disputing parties. If the witness proves just and steadfast under scrutiny, his testimony is accepted and the right is rendered to its rightful claimant. Here, Allah amplifies the challenge: "When you produce a word that matches one of Our chapters, bring also your own witnesses—not Ours—and let them judge. Let them determine whether your speech surpasses the Qur'an in Arabic eloquence. Surely your own witnesses will confess that Allah's Word stands exalted above all else."

The Word of Allah repeatedly invokes testimony to establish its truthfulness. It addresses those who deny such testimonies, declaring:

⟪quran 41:53⟫
⟪ar:سَنُرِيهِمْ آيَتِنَا فِي الْآفَاقِ وَفِي أَنفُسِهِمْ ‎⟫

"We shall show them Our signs in the horizons and within their own souls until it becomes manifest to them that this is the truth."

At other junctures, Allah calls Himself as witness:

⟪quran 13:43⟫
⟪ar:قُلْ كَفَىٰ بِٱللَّهِ شَهِيدًۢا بَيْنِى وَبَيْنَكُمْ وَمَن عِندَهُۥ عِلْمٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْكِتَٰبِ ‎⟫

"Say: Allah suffices as witness between me and you, and he upon whom rests knowledge of the Book."

He has established the angels as witnesses, and He has made the Messenger and the community of believers as witnesses:

⟪quran 2:143⟫
⟪ar:وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ‎⟫

"Thus We have made you a middle nation, that you might bear witness over mankind, and that the Messenger might bear witness over you."

Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq expounded this verse, saying: "We are the middle nation, and we bear witness to the deeds of all peoples. The Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—bears witness to us, attesting whether we have faithfully conveyed God's commands to His servants."

Allah thus places supreme emphasis upon witness and testimony to establish the truth of all matters, great and small. Therefore, addressing those who deny the Qur'an's eloquence, He declares: "Produce a word like it, and bring witnesses testifying that your words are superior and more eloquent than the Qur'an—than every single chapter the Messenger of Allah brought forth, each word of which bears witness to its divine origin."

Yet indeed, the true witness of the Qur'an is the Qur'an itself. It extracts meaning from the silent letters of this book as a farmer extracts grain from the earth, as humanity derives sustenance and nourishment from the fruits of trees and the flesh of animals. The Qur'an rests in the hands of all people, yet they remain unable to comprehend it. This supreme virtue belongs exclusively to the Holy Prophet—may Allah's blessings and peace be upon him—and his Ahl al-Bayt (his Household). They alone possess the capacity to extract meaning from words and to elucidate its true purpose and inner reality. They are the true witnesses of the Qur'an. The outward book constitutes the miracle (*muʿjiza*) of the Messenger of Allah, while its esoteric interpretation is the miracle of his true descendants—a miracle no thousand aspirants could usurp or rival.

At the conclusion of the verse, Allah says:

⟪quran 2:24⟫
⟪ar:إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫

"If you are truthful."

The word *sādiqīn* (truthful ones) is typically understood to denote those who speak truth, while *kādhib* denotes those who lie. The question naturally arises: who speaks truth, and who utters falsehood?

We respond thus: the Hereafter is the truth, and this temporal world is falsehood—for truth is that which endures eternally, never decaying, while falsehood ceaselessly changes and ultimately perishes. By this principle, observe the state of the world: nothing in it sustains itself in any fixed condition. Everything rises and falls. Spring gives way to autumn; summer yields to winter. Human life itself transforms through childhood, youth, maturity, and old age, finally ending. Wealth shifts to poverty; health deteriorates to sickness; life becomes death; honor descends to shame. No state, no condition remains permanent. By contrast, the abode of the Hereafter is the Abode of Peace, utterly pure and free from all transformation. There is health without disease, eternal life without death, honor without humiliation. In every circumstance and state, it endures immutable in its perfection.

**A Subtle Point**

Allah has placed the proof of the spiritual world's truth within the human form itself—within the heart. Whatever dwells in a person's heart and innermost being is truth, bearing no mark of falsehood. Yet when that same inner reality emerges upon the tongue—when it dons the garment of words and takes shape through letters in the body of language—falsehood may appear.

After this prelude, we declare: those who testify concerning the Abode of Truth are themselves the truthful—the Prophets, the Messengers, the saints, the Imāms and their descendants. These are the *sādiqūn* (*the truthful ones*) whom Allah intends in His verse:

⟪quran 9:119⟫
⟪ar:يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَكُونُوا مَعَ الصَّادِقِينَ ‎⟫

"O you who believe! Fear Allah and be with the truthful."

Mawlānā Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq proclaimed: "We are the righteous, and this verse was revealed for us." In other words, the realm of subtlety and transcendence is the Hereafter—free from all change and mutation—while the physical world overflows with complete falsehood. Therefore, "Be with the truthful" means: join those who teach you of the Hereafter and its world of spirit, who guide you toward Allah's nearness and away from fear and material acquisition. Do not join those ensnared solely in the world of falsehood—materialism—believing it to be all that exists.

After challenging every disbeliever and polytheist, Allah announces the consequence:

⟪quran 2:24⟫
⟪ar:فَإِن لَّمْ تَفْعَلُوا۟ وَلَن تَفْعَلُوا۟ فَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱلنَّارَ ٱلَّتِى وَقُودُهَا ٱلنَّاسُ وَٱلْحِجَارَةُ ۖ أُعِدَّتْ لِلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫

"If you cannot do it—and you shall never be able to do it—then fear the Fire whose fuel is mankind and stones, prepared for the disbelievers."

Since Allah has deliberately established an impossible condition in the preceding verse, rendering manifest the utter weakness of the challengers and closing all avenues of future attempt, He now strengthens His Word, declaring: "Know well that no eloquence or eloquence, however refined, attains to the Word of Ahsan al-Khāliqīn (the Best of Creators). Thus Allah alone knows which choice can stand against the Lord of the Worlds in the selection of the Prophet's Caliph and successor. Servants upon whose intellect Satan has fixed his saddle, when they choose, select someone like themselves—neither chaste nor knowledgeable. This is because the Qur'an dominates and prevails over all humanity as humanity dominates the entire animal kingdom. This book's soul is the soul of the human world; it is the true Qibla of all human souls. It is the return (*marja*) to the Creator, the Self-Sufficient (*al-Samad*), upon whom all existence depends.

Such is why Ali—peace be upon him—cried out in the pulpit: "Ask me before you lose me!" No flaw has been discovered in Ali's eloquence (known as Kalām al-Amīr, the Speech of the Commander), nor has all the world, despite its advanced knowledge of judgment and adjudication, uncovered any deficiency in his rulings. The Book of the Universe lay open before him, with nothing hidden from his sight. Thus he declared: "I have attained the degree of certainty such that were all veils removed from my face, my faith would not increase by even the slightest degree. I possess awareness of every particle of the universe through the power and might of knowledge I received from the Messenger of Allah—may God's blessings and peace be upon him—and I can discern the truth and origin of all things."[^cite-11] Who, then, can stand against one possessed of such knowledge and elevated station? You occupy a height that none have scaled and none can scale—yet it is impossible to compare you to your companions and those who have learned from you and been illumined by your light.

### The Inner Fire

Apparent fire possesses two qualities: heat and light. Their combination constitutes fire itself. Similarly, the inner fire—the revelation of prophethood—manifests two attributes. For the believer, it is light that guides toward the luminous world and delivers from darkness. For the disbeliever, the hypocrite, and the denier, it is heat that the Prophet's prophethood carries toward Hellfire, inflicting eternal torment. Prophethood remains one reality, yet for the believer it becomes light, and for the disbeliever, heat.

| Prophethood's Fire |  |
| --- | --- |
| Light (Believers) | Heat (Disbelievers, Hypocrites) |

### The People, the Stones, and the Disbelievers

Allah thus addressed both disbelievers and hypocrites with warning of prophethood's heat:

⟪quran 2:24⟫
⟪ar:فَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱلنَّارَ ٱلَّتِى وَقُودُهَا ٱلنَّاسُ وَٱلْحِجَارَةُ ‎⟫

"Guard yourselves against the Fire whose fuel is mankind and stones."

The Almighty's discourse concerning the fuel of this fire is most extraordinary and unsettling. He declares: "The fuel of this fire shall be human beings and stones"—and then adds, "prepared for the disbelievers." A puzzlement arises: stones burn readily enough, for their combustible mineral components—sulfur and similar substances—kindle swiftly and accelerate the blaze. Yet who are these *al-nās* (the people) constituting the fire's fuel? The disbelievers have already been mentioned separately. Are they not themselves people (*al-nās*)? What then is the relationship between stones and people that Allah has joined them with the conjunction *wa* (and)?

**A Linguistic Observation**

The conjunction *wa* (and) serves to connect two entities only when they share similarity and correspondence—as in goodness and piety, strength and courage, affection and love, Muḥammad and ʿAlī. Yet stone is inanimate matter, and moreover, matter without use—for it does not melt so as to be recast into other mineral forms and render itself productive. Moreover, a pair must be reciprocal or equivalent. If stones denote hard-hearted human beings, then what becomes of the *al-nās* also mentioned? Are they not equally hard-hearted, and thus equally human? If both refer to the same hard-hearted humanity, why mention them twice? Once would suffice.

**The Inner Interpretation**

The People of the Truth (*Ahl al-Ḥaqq*), in their explanation of this verse's esoteric meaning, declare: those who reject the beneficial influence of the Messenger's teachings are themselves stones. These are the people concerning whom Allah says:

⟪quran 2:74⟫
⟪ar:ثُمَّ قَسَتْ قُلُوبُكُم مِّن بَعْدِ ذَلِكَ فَهِىَ كَٱلْحِجَارَةِ أَوْ أَشَدُّ قَسْوَةً ‎⟫

"Then your hearts hardened after that, becoming like stones or harder."

These are people who heard the Messenger's words yet denied the guidance of the Imāms and guides of the community after him. They followed the outward scripture but abandoned the wisdom revealed alongside it. Though human in appearance, they are worse than stone through their stubbornness and hardness of heart. They witnessed the martyrdom of the Prophet's household—as when Muʿāwiya and Yazīd committed their atrocities—yet their hearts did not break or yield.

**The People**

*Al-nās*—the people forming part of the aforesaid group—are those who accepted the Imāms after the Messenger yet exceeded the bounds of love for the Ahl al-Bayt (the Prophet's Household). They exaggerated their exaltation, elevating them above the Messenger himself, calling them "Lord of the Worlds" and "Lord of the Realized Ones," and made this their creed. Though they began with the guidance of proper affection, they deviated from the teachings of the infallible Imāms and abandoned religion as an arrow flees the bowstring.

| Category | Characteristic | Error |
| --- | --- | --- |
| *Al-Ḥijāra* (Stones) | Deny the Imāms' guidance | Muqassir (Diminisher) |
| *Al-Nās* (The People) | Exaggerate the Imāms' status | Ghālī (Exaggerator) |

For these two groups, the Messenger of Allah declared: "O ʿAlī! On account of your knowledge, two parties have perished. One deprives you of your rank; the other elevates you beyond it."[^cite-12]

The first group obeyed only the outward ordinances of the sharīʿa and abandoned the Prophet's ʿItrat (his Household). The second plunged into *wilāya* (guardianship) yet abandoned the Book and its commandments. Allah likened the first hard-hearted group to stones; the second He named *al-nās* (the people).

Then Allah declares:

⟪quran 2:24⟫
⟪ar:أُعِدَّتْ لِلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫

"Prepared for the disbelievers."

**On *Kufr* (Disbelief)**

*Kufr* literally means to cover and conceal. In Arabic, the farmer is called a *kāfir* because he presses the seed beneath the soil. The river too is called *kāfir* because everything within its depths is hidden. Islam thus names those who veil the truth as disbelievers. Two types of disbelievers exist:

The first are those who, driven by lust for dominion, worldly glory, and enmity toward truth, deliberately veiled it. They denied the incident at Ghādīr Khumm. Even after acknowledging the matter and professing belief, they turned away in ignorance—like ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb—becoming apostates. The second type are those who followed their masters, impressed by appearance and deceived by rhetoric. They did not intend opposition to truth; rather, they merely deviated by following their elders without reflection. The first type represents the most severe disbelief—those who resisted the guidance of their age and usurped the rights of the rightful. This verse addresses this first category.

Thus the verse's meaning becomes clear: the fire of prophethood and its heat awaits the disbelievers, the *muqassirūn* (diminishers), and the *ghullāt* (exaggerators). All three groups shall become victims of the Holy Prophet's wrath and displeasure. The first comprises figures like Muʿāwiya, Yazīd, Pharaoh, and Nimrod, whom the Almighty explicitly called disbelievers. The second consists of those who embrace apparent truth, reciting the Qur'an with correct intonation yet oblivious to its meaning and reality. The third comprises those who claim to be Shīʿa of ʿAlī yet believe as the Nāṣibīs (the opponents of ʿAlī) believe. All shall taste the heat of prophethood's fire and merit Allah's punishment.

## And give glad tidings to those who believe and do righteous deeds

Allah says:

⟪quran 2:25⟫
⟪ar:وَبَشِّرِ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أَنَّ لَهُمْ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَارُ ‎⟫

### The Meaning of *Bashīr* (Bearer of Glad Tidings)

In Arabic lexicography, a *bashīr* is one who journeys far ahead of his people through forests and wilderness seeking water. When he discovers a spring, he stands in place and signals with his sword or cloth, proclaiming the good news of water's discovery.

Thus the Messenger of Allah—may God's blessings and peace be upon him—stands as *bashīr*, having journeyed far ahead of his people and all mankind in quest of knowledge of the Mother of the Book (*Umm al-Kitāb*), salvation, and the eternal life of the human soul. When, through Allah's aid and revelation, he achieved this goal, he summoned all humanity to this fountain of eternal life from his elevated station. He called with words and gestures, now with gentleness, now with sternness; sometimes threatening Hellfire, sometimes promising Paradise; at times intimidating, at others encouraging—drawing the people toward *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* (There is no god but Allah), the very source of eternal life and true monotheism (*tawḥīd*). For whoever accepts this summons, the Messenger of Allah becomes the guarantor of both Paradise and salvation.

Because the Messenger employed both methods of summons, he was designated not only as *bashīr* but equally as *nadhīr* (warner):

⟪quran 25:56⟫
⟪ar:وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَكَ إِلَّا مُبَشِّرًۭا وَنَذِيرًۭا ‎⟫

"We have sent you only as a bearer of glad tidings and a warner."

### The Believers

In its literal sense, belief (*īmān*) signifies affirmation and confirmation. In another sense, it means to grant security and assurance. Belief is a transitive action that does not conclude with the verb alone but requires an object. Two elements constitute belief: an agent and a recipient. If belief means affirmation, one must ask: what is being affirmed? The one affirmed is the *muṣaddiq* (the one being confirmed), and this constitutes the object. If belief signifies granting security, there must be an object—security granted to whom or to what? The Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—unified these two meanings in a single ḥadīth, declaring: "A believer is one who grants his neighbor security from his wrongdoing."

Because of these dual meanings, a person is called *muʾmin* (believer). Among Allah's Most Beautiful Names is *al-Muʾmin* (the Grantor of Security). A person becomes a believer by affirming Allah's promises; Allah is *al-Muʾmin* by granting creatures protection from His deadly punishment.

| Role | Action | Object |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Believer | Affirmation | Allah, the Messenger, the Imam |
| Believer | Granting Security | The Created |
| Allah | Granting Security | The Torment |

In this verse, the believers are given glad tidings of Paradise—those who affirm the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—and all his commandments, and who grant other believers and Muslims security from harm befalling them.

### Performing Righteous Deeds

In common parlance, righteous deeds (*al-ʿamal al-sālih*) refer to charity, kindness, honoring one's parents, benefiting people, and obedience to the sharīʿa's commandments. These actions are well-known; everyone understands them readily, and even the simplest intellect recognizes their virtue. Yet such deeds are not so profound and magnificent that Allah need explain them to His servants through revelation.

Here we deviate from the customary interpretation: the purpose of "righteous deeds" is not limited to the good works mentioned above. Rather, the primary meaning is to follow the righteous believers—the Imāms of all times and eras. Those who possess comprehensive knowledge of the Qur'an's commandments and comprehend its true meaning. Allah declares concerning their greatness:

⟪quran 3:18⟫
⟪ar:شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٰٓئِكَةُ وَأُولُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ‎⟫

"Allah witnesses that there is no god but He, and the angels and those possessed of knowledge stand by justice."

Here, *Ṣāliḥ al-Muʾminīn* (the Righteous of the Believers) refers to ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib—peace be upon him—father and source of all the Imāms. His station in relation to the believers mirrors the Imāms' station in relation to believers.

**A Subtle Point**

ʿAlī—peace be upon him—bears the same relationship to the Imāms as the Imāms bear to the believers. Just as an Imām is *Ṣāliḥ al-Muʾminīn* for believers, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib is *Ṣāliḥ al-Muʾminīn* for the community of Imāms.

The deeds of all believers depend upon you. You are the locus of goodness and the fountain of beauty, and all manner of righteous deed connects to you. These include justice, benevolence, trust, honesty, rendering the orphan and kinfolk their due. It is solely through you that we attain knowledge of Allah's true *tawḥīd* and the reality of obedience. All the moral virtues for which the Messenger of Allah—may God's blessings and peace be upon him—was sent to perfect are those blessed ones concerning whom the Messenger said: "Al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn are from me, and I am from them."[^cite-13] And honoring their mother, he declared: "Fāṭima is one of the hour *īs* of Paradise." And praising their grandfather, he said: "O ʿAlī, your flesh is my flesh and your blood is my blood. Your station with me is as the soul's station in the body."[^cite-14]

These pure beings, standing so near the Prophet, are precisely those who embody the Qur'an's moral teachings and who teach them. In short: those who follow the righteous are the ones who perform righteous deeds. All their actions are righteous, and whoever abandons *Ṣāliḥ al-Muʾminīn*—called also *Ḥabal Allāh al-Mateen* (the Strong Rope of Allah)—and becomes his enemy, shall find every outward good deed nullified. His striving becomes sin; his Islam reverts to primal ignorance (jāhiliyyah), to idolatry (*shirk*), and his soul grows dark.

**The Ablution and Entry into the Mosque**

Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib—peace be upon him—proclaimed: "Whoever enters the mosque after performing complete ritual ablution (*wuḍūʾ*) is considered to remain in prayer until he performs a *ḥadath* (an act that nullifies ritual purity). His ablution remains unbroken."

In the Qur'an's esoteric interpretation (*taʾwīl*), this means: whoever takes the oath of allegiance to the Imām of the Age—peace be upon him—and purifies himself from the impurities of doubt and idolatry, and participates in the Imām's *daʿwa* (the invitation to faith)—which is the mosque or House of Allah—shall, so long as he remains in that *daʿwa* and performs no act that severs his oath of allegiance, find all his deeds acceptable to Allah, whether worldly or religious. Yet should he commit a *ḥadath*—should he mock the Imām and his ordinances, backbite against them, or openly oppose them—he withdraws from the *daʿwa* and severs his oath. Thereafter, none of his actions shall find acceptance with Allah.

Allah grants glad tidings of Paradise to those who follow the righteous believers and spend their lives within the circle of their allegiance.

## Do not say of those slain in Allah's path: They are dead. Rather, they are alive

Allah declares:

⟪quran 2:154⟫
⟪ar:وَلَا تَقُولُوا۟ لِمَن يُقْتَلُ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ أَمْوَتٌۢ ۚ بَلْ أَحْيَآءٌۭ وَلَكِن لَّا تَشْعُرُونَ ‎⟫

In his work *Rawdat al-Mustafīd*, Sayyidunā al-Ḥusayn ibn Jidʿān clarifies that being slain in Allah's path (*sabīl Allāh*) signifies the usurpation of the ranks of Allah's saints and the denial of their spiritual stations. Yet these acts of deprivation do not sever the saints from their inner service; rather, their ministrations continue inwardly, in secret. Thus Allah says: "Do not imagine them dead, for they go about their tasks quietly in their stations, though you perceive it not."

In another verse, Allah affirms that the martyrs remain alive and receive sustenance from Him. This means that although the oppressors outwardly seized the ranks and stations of the Imāms and Allah's saints, all of them continued receiving knowledge and support from Amīr al-Muʾminīn (the Commander of the Faithful). Allah's pronouncement that they fear not nor grieve means these saints experience no dread of privation nor sorrow at material loss. Even should the outer *daʿwa* (the invitation) be halted, the inner *daʿwa* persists.

## O you who believe! Do not render vain your charity by reminders and injury

Allah declares in Sūrat al-Baqara:

⟪quran 2:264⟫
⟪ar:يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ لَا تُبْطِلُوا۟ صَدَقَتِكُم بِٱلْمَنِّ وَٱلْأَذَىٰ ‎⟫

Every sect and group has interpreted this verse according to its own understanding. We affirm that Allah has placed a weighty matter before us here, and in truth addresses the inner charity through discourse upon outer charity and giving.

### The Two Forms of Charity

On the surface, the charity a person dispenses from his wealth—a small or large portion given by the rich to the poor—represents material almsgiving. There exist two types of forgiveness and giving: the first is material and corporeal, benefiting the body and protecting one from worldly hardship. The second is intellectual and spiritual, profiting the soul, making it conscious of the Hereafter, and furnishing it with sustenance against all calamity in that eternal realm.

The true possessor of this second wealth, and those who can dispense and forgive it, is the Messenger of Allah—peace and blessings be upon him—who established the sharīʿa and revealed the path of salvation. The Imāms descended from his household after him are the possessors of this wealth and worthy of dispensing it. Those rebuked, warned, and threatened in this verse are those who became Caliph of the Prophet without any divine sanction. The Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—did not appoint them; rather, they forcibly usurped the rights of the rightful and ascended the Prophet's pulpit. Subsequently, they attacked other lands in Islam's name without legitimate cause and achieved grand conquests, becoming celebrated as *Fātiḥ-i Aʿẓam* (the Greatest Conqueror, referring to ʿUmar). To sustain their image, they discussed prayer and fasting—small portions of the sharīʿa, just as alms constitute a small portion of wealth. Therefore, Allah forbade such people from inviting others to Muḥammad's sharīʿa, for on one hand they harm the Prophet's children, usurp their rights, and even martyr them, while simultaneously presenting themselves as servants of Islam. These are hypocrites (*munāfiqūn*) displaying ostentation. They seek only fame and renown, disobeying Allah while believing in their obedience. This charity finds no acceptance with Allah. Because they perform all acts without Allah's permission and command, all their endeavors shall be reckoned as nullified and wicked; moreover, they shall be punished.

### The Parable of the Stone and Dust

⟪quran 2:264⟫
⟪ar:كَٱلَّذِى يُنفِقُ مَالَهُۥ رِئَآءَ ٱلنَّاسِ وَلَا يُؤْمِنُ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۖ فَمَثَلُهُۥ كَمَثَلِ صَفْوَانٍ عَلَيْهِ تُرَابٌۭ فَأَصَابَهُۥ وَابِلٌۭ فَتَرَكَهُۥ صَلْدًۭا ‎⟫

Their parable resembles a rock with soil upon it. Due to the hardness of heart and obduracy, Allah has likened their hearts to stones. In such a heart, the word of wisdom casts not its light, from which the cultivation of knowledge might grow and flourish. As thousands of pails of water or prolonged rain cannot cause vegetation to grow upon a bare stone, so knowledge cannot take root within such hearts.

Then Allah adds: "Some dust lies upon this stone." When soil rests upon the heart's surface, and the water of knowledge flows upon it, soil becomes a medium for producing vegetation alongside water. Yet when soil lies upon stone, water washes away the soil, leaving no cultivation or production. Dust signifies faith in the inner dimension, which shapes the cultivation of the Hereafter, just as dust externally manifests the forms of this world. Thus dust upon stone denotes faith founded upon deceit and pretense—professing with the tongue "Āmannā" (we believe) while harboring disbelief in the heart. Inevitably, the knowledge of the Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—flowing upon such a person becomes wasted. The dust is swept away, and the stone beneath is exposed; the pretense of faith also falls away, revealing the open opposition and enmity concealed within.

⟪quran 2:264⟫
⟪ar:لَّا يَقْدِرُونَ عَلَىٰ شَىْءٍۢ مِّمَّا كَسَبُوا۟ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫

"They shall not be able to achieve anything from their earnings; and Allah does not guide the disbelieving people."

When rain falls upon the stone, it leaves it in hardness—these teachings do not affect them. Their works avail them nothing in the Hereafter. Since they reject the knowledge of the Prophet and the Imāms—the very source of guidance—they shall forever remain astray.

### The Parable of the Garden

In the following verse, Allah declares:

⟪quran 2:265⟫
⟪ar:وَمَثَلُ ٱلَّذِينَ يُنفِقُونَ أَمْوَلَهُمُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ مَرْضَاتِ ٱللَّهِ وَتَثْبِيتًۭا مِّنْ أَنفُسِهِمْ كَمَثَلِ جَنَّةٍۭ بِرَبْوَةٍ أَصَابَهَا وَابِلٌۭ فَـَٔاتَتْ أُكُلَهَا ضِعْفَيْنِ ‎⟫

The example of those who expend their wealth seeking Allah's pleasure and firmness of soul—their parable resembles a garden upon an elevated hill. When interpreted esoterically, the relationship between the two becomes luminous: pure *tawḥīd* (monotheism) is neither established nor proven except through the station of *wilāya* (guardianship) and *imāma* (imamate)—which constitute the essence of religion itself. They explain Allah's words, revealing the boundaries between the spiritual and physical realms. They alone provide the means by which Allah is purified and distinguished from His attributes. Without *wilāya* and *imāma*, such *tawḥīd*—free from comparison and negation—cannot be attained.

⟪quran 2:265⟫
⟪ar:كَمَثَلِ جَنَّةٍۭ بِرَبْوَةٍ ‎⟫

"Like a garden upon a height..."

Every scholar and learned one of religion constitutes a paradise containing such fruits and produce of knowledge that believing souls derive comfort and pleasure, and minds find peace and tranquility. Just as the human body finds ease and relaxation in outward gardens, the religious scholar who holds this station stands in *Rabwa*—a lofty, elevated position.

**A Subtle Point**

*Rabwa* signifies that the religious scholar stands closer to obtaining material and support from those above him. By occupying this elevated station, he disperses the blessings he receives upon his subordinates.

⟪quran 2:265⟫
⟪ar:أَصَابَهَا وَابِلٌۭ فَـَٔاتَتْ أُكُلَهَا ضِعْفَيْنِ ‎⟫

"When heavy rain fell upon it, it produced twice its yield."

When this scholar receives support from the exalted scholars above him—that is, when heavy rain falls upon this garden—he yields doubly. Through these endorsements, he unites the scholars through both the text and the esoteric interpretation (*taʾwīl*), establishing consensus between the intelligible and the intelligible realms, rendering both this world and the Hereafter subject to wisdom.

⟪quran 2:265⟫
⟪ar:وَإِن لَّمْ يُصِبْهَا وَابِلٌۭ فَطَلٌّۭ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ بَصِيرٌ ‎⟫

"If it receives no heavy rain, even light rain suffices. Allah sees all that you do."

If this scholar's station does not reach such heights as to receive the revelation analogous to heavy rain, he yet obtains at least sufficient substance from his own power of reflection and imagination to cultivate something for the Hereafter's harvest, providing eternal and infinite sustenance to his subordinates. Allah is witness to all your works.

## None Know Its Interpretation Save Allah and the Firmly Grounded in Knowledge

When believers are questioned—"Do you claim that the Qur'an possesses meanings beyond its apparent words, meanings rooted in Arabic lexicon yet concealed from the common people, known only to yourselves?"—the response is decisive: it is not the Ismailis alone who affirm that the Qur'an contains taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis); rather, even the Ahl al-Raʾy (the Rationalists) and the Ahl al-Hadith (the Traditionalists) are compelled by rational necessity to acknowledge it in certain verses, for the literal sense renders them incoherent.[^cite-15] Where the apparent meaning contradicts reason, both schools abandon literalism and resort to interpretive analysis—they interpret despite their stated denials of interpretation. Consider three verses whose surface meaning threatens to undermine Tawḥīd (divine unity):

| *Literal Translation* | *Qur'anic Expression* |
| --- | --- |
| The Coming of the Lord | ⟪ar:مِجِيء رَبِّك⟫ |
| Looking to the Lord | ⟪ar:نَاظِرَة إِلَى رَبِّهَا⟫ |
| Going to the Lord | ⟪ar:ذَهَب إِلَى رَبِّه⟫ |

These three, taken at face value, embody and localize Allah—attributes proper to bodies alone, from which the Most Exalted is absolutely transcendent. Coming, seeing, and going are marks of corporeality; yet Allah, in His essence, is beyond all spatial movement and limitation. When even the literalists found themselves unable to answer this difficulty, they too sought refuge in interpretation. Why then do they deny taʾwīl when the Qur'an itself speaks of it?

The verse states: ⟪quran 3:7⟫ and preserves the word *taʾwīl* explicitly. Yet they now dispute the placement of the waqf (pause). They insist: "None knows the interpretation save Allah"—and halt there, placing the pause after *illa-Allāh*. They then read the remainder, "and those firmly grounded in knowledge say: We believe in it," as a separate utterance—as though the *ar-Rāsikhūn fī al-ʿilm* (the firmly grounded in knowledge) themselves do not comprehend interpretation, but merely profess belief in Allah's knowledge of it. Their reasoning: if these grounded scholars possessed knowledge of interpretation, they would share in Allah's knowledge, which they equate with polytheism.[^cite-16]

We reply: Faith (īmān) means *confirming*, and confirmation requires the truthfulness of what is affirmed. One cannot truly affirm what one does not understand; such affirmation is merely empty assent. If the firmly grounded in knowledge were to believe in Allah's interpretation without themselves understanding it, they would be saying "yes to yes," not confirming truth. But Allah Himself declares: ⟪quran 3:18⟫—"Allah bears witness that there is no god save He, and the angels, and those possessed of knowledge bear witness"—establishing thus that the knowledgeable are themselves witnesses to truth.[^cite-17]

### The Messenger and the Knowledge of Interpretation

A second argument: Did the Messenger of Allah (may Allah bless him and grant him peace) possess knowledge of the Qur'an or not? If they say no, they attribute the gravest deficiency to the Prophet—he preached what he himself could not grasp. Imagine a questioner asking, "O Messenger of Allah, what is the meaning of this verse? What is the wisdom in this ordinance? What is the purpose of this prohibition?" and the Messenger replying, "I know not." Would this manifest virtue or ignorance? Surely ignorance cannot be praiseworthy. But if they concede that the Messenger possessed knowledge of the Qur'an's interpretation, then it is invalid and contradictory to deny that the firmly grounded in knowledge likewise possess it—for the Messenger shared in Allah's knowledge, and by his very nature as the Prophet, he stands highest among those possessed of understanding.

Indeed, the Messenger is supreme and superior in knowledge; he is their leader, elevated above all scholars. Allah places them in the rank of angels: ⟪quran 3:18⟫—and thus mentions them in the same breath as the celestial beings. Only those who possess true knowledge surpass those whose knowledge is merely conventional; not every scholar is a fully grounded knower, yet every Messenger is the most grounded of all.

### Rational and Scriptural Proof

By reason and by the principle of wisdom, our position stands vindicated. Allah says: ⟪quran 41:53⟫—"We shall show them Our signs in the horizons and in their own souls until it becomes manifest to them that it is the truth."[^cite-18] The Messenger taught: "Allah has fashioned His religion after the pattern of His creation, that through creation you may judge His religion, and His religion is one."[^cite-19] 

The Qur'an addresses two realities: the cosmos and the soul. The hadith likewise mentions creation and religion. Both scripture and tradition teach that the principles of religion—the path to Paradise—derive from the principles of cosmic creation. All worldly things are either organic or inorganic; nothing moves unless an inner stimulus (spirit, *rūḥ*) imparts motion through its effect. Similarly, human beings, animals, and plants are established and sustained by an intrinsic vital force. Within them lie meanings not apparent to the senses—yet grasped by those who perceive. Thus, the creation of the heavens and earth and the establishment of religion are equivalent.

| *Aspect* | *Cosmos* | *Religion* |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Manifest | Motion (object) | Practice (action) |
| Hidden | Stimulus (spirit) | Knowledge (inner meaning) |

### The Fourfold Argument

Consider how humans are bound to the earth in two ways: it sustains our weight, and from its depths it brings forth vegetation that nourishes us. So too does wind benefit us doubly: we live by breathing it, and it affects the ripening of our food. Fire serves us twofold: it provides light and heat, and we use it to cook grain, making it edible. Water also grants a dual gift: that which falls from above we drink to sustain life, and that which seeps into the earth produces vegetation—the very substance of our sustenance. Without this water, both human and animal perish.

Reflecting thus upon the created world, we turn to religion, which—the Messenger teaches—mirrors creation. Here we observe a parallel:

| *World* | *Religion* |
| --- | --- |
| Outward action | Obedience, worship |
| Inward sustenance | Knowledge, gnosis |

The outward commandments correspond to water falling from heaven, which we drink and by which our bodies are nourished. Inner knowledge is like the water absorbed into earth, from which the soul draws eternal life and sees reality. After grasping these arguments, who but the obstinate can deny interpretation and its science?

*(From the Majālis al-Muʾayyada of Sayyidnā al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)*

## The First House: The House Appointed for Mankind

Allah says: ⟪quran 3:96⟫—"Verily, the first house appointed for mankind is that at Bakkah, full of blessing and a guidance for the worlds."

The Qur'an is fundamentally a text containing countless verses; some are *muḥkamāt* (decisive, unambiguous), and others are *mutashābihāt* (allegorical, requiring interpretation). The verses beginning with the particle *anna* are clear utterances—admitting no obscurity or evasion. These Allah has established firmly. Yet when He says in this verse: "Blessed is this house for all the worlds, a guide unto them," He indicates not a single *ʿālam* (world) but many worlds—as He declares in Sūrat al-Fātiḥa: "Praise be to Allah, Lord of all worlds," and speaks to the Messenger: "You are a mercy to the worlds."[^cite-20] Thus the Messenger became mercy to all the worlds of which Allah is Lord, and this House is equally blessed for all these worlds, overflowing with guidance.

The House of Allah is the Kaʿba in Makka. Yet this verse specifies "Bakkah," not Makka. There is a profound distinction. Amīr al-Muʾminīn (may Allah be pleased with him) declared: "I am the point beneath the letter *Bāʾ*."[^cite-21] Whoever recognizes this point recognizes its reality; whoever recognizes the point sees beyond Makka and turns his gaze toward Bakkah. Makka is the city's name; Bakkah is the name of its heart. In numerological terms: *Makka* (Muhammad) is the letter *Bāʾ*, and *Bakkah* (ʿAlī) is the point—the dot below that letter. The Kaʿba was built upon this point.

### The Argument from the Succession of Prophets

For any claim to be believed and confirmed, one must first demonstrate the condition of *wilāya* (guardianship). When someone makes an assertion, he must present evidence; without it, the claim is groundless. If Allah were asked, "Why do You call Yourself the Creator?" He would reply, "Because I have brought all these worlds into being." Similarly, Allah sent one hundred thousand prophets to this world, and every one visited this first house. Had Allah established His houses at different times and places, some prophets would have gone here, others there. Yet history testifies that all prophets journeyed to the same Kaʿba—proof that this alone is Allah's first house.

### The Nobility of Position

At this place, the reward for a single *rakʿa* (unit of prayer) equals one hundred thousand rakʿas elsewhere. Prayers not oriented toward this direction are rejected. Seventy thousand angels descend at this site each day at the same hour—and they do not return.

From the primordial decree, Allah established that the Kaʿba shall never depart from its place; rather, all worlds move toward it. Ages pass, earth becomes water and water becomes earth, yet the Kaʿba remains immobile. So too is ʿAlī's station: he does not deviate, and all believers must move toward him, striving in the mystical understanding of his essence.

### The Primal Scattering of the Mosque

From this house all mosques emerged; the Kaʿba became the focal point for them all. It is recounted that during Noah's Flood, Allah's House was raised to the fourth heaven. Later, Ibrāhīm (peace be upon him) rebuilt it at Allah's command. For the lime remaining after its construction, Allah commanded Ibrāhīm through Gabriel to gather every particle and carry it to heaven, then scatter it across the earth. The day of gathering was Friday; the day of scattering, Saturday. Where these particles fell—on soil, stone, or lime—congregational mosques (*Jāmiʿ*) were established.

*Inner Meaning:* The Lights (Anwār) gather on Friday and radiate on Saturday. The Prophet gathered all the Anwār in ʿAlī, and ʿAlī broadcast them to the Imāms.

### Interpretation in the Style of the *Dāʿwa*

The Kaʿba became the direction of prayer for Adam (peace be upon him), and after him for all one hundred twenty-four thousand prophets. When the *qibla* of all the saints of this world reached its zenith, the time came to declare this House the birthplace of Amīr al-Muʾminīn, the Lion of God, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib. From this place none shall be born except he who is named ʿAlī—no other. Thus Allah says: in Arabic, *dawāda* means to place a thing in its rightful station justly; to place it wrongly is *ẓulm* (oppression).

### The House of Rest

The word *Nās* (people) here does not mean the generality of humankind, but rather those familiar with the *daʿwa* (the divine summons), who hold fast to the mantle of Mawlānā ʿAlī, who rightly acknowledge his guardianship and grasp his meaning. Thus the verse means: ʿAlī was made a dwelling-place for the people of true knowledge. A home is where one dwells and finds repose. In the Qur'an, *Bayt* appears twenty-eight times. *Bayt* signifies house, and in esoteric sense, it is the very essence of the Imām, wherein the souls of all believers find eternal rest.

*Numerological Note:* The numerical value of *Bakkah* is twenty-seven; he who possesses it becomes the twenty-eighth.

*Blessed Continuity:* The word *blessing* refers to the unbroken succession of Imāms descending from ʿAlī and Fāṭima until the Day of Judgment.

### The Kaʿba and ʿAlī

*Exegetical Principle:* Every utterance from the Prophet's tongue not part of the Qur'an is a *ḥadīth* (tradition). Hadith is of two kinds: one that pleases people alone is called *Hadīth Sharīf*; the second, pleasing both to people and to Allah, is the *Hadīth Qudsī* (sacred tradition). In the latter category is the *Ḥadīth Qudsī*: "The example of ʿAlī is the example of the Kaʿba."[^cite-22] Whatever is understood of the Kaʿba should be understood of ʿAlī, and vice versa.

If the Kaʿba is inscribed in the Book, so too is ʿAlī. If the Kaʿba was designed for the common folk, ʿAlī was designed for the sincere believers. If the Kaʿba is blessed and a source of guidance, ʿAlī too is blessed and a fountain of gnosis. In the Kaʿba we find *Mustajar* and *Multazim*; in ʿAlī we find outer and inner chambers. As the *Mīzāb al-Raḥma* (gutter of mercy) crowns the Kaʿba's height, so Mawlānā Fāṭima stands as his noble proof. As the Black Stone in the north receives the pledge of all believers, so the line of Imāms carries the pledge sufficient for us. As the Kaʿba has four corners—the Syrian, Iraqi, Yemenite, and Shāmī—so ʿAlī possesses four sanctuaries. Whoever prays facing the Kaʿba, his outward prayer is accepted; and he whose heart is illumined with ʿAlī's love finds his inner prayer accepted.

| *Outer Form* | *Inner Meaning* |
| --- | --- |
| The Key Opens, Lock Turns, Bounty Flows | Heaven's Echo Upon the Earth |
| The Prophet's Cups | All Prophets Pray, Uttering Blessings |
| Knowledge's Mill Grinds the Universe | The Soul's Cry Calls Out to ʿAlī |

### The Kaʿba and Muhammad (the Prophet)

*Numerical Correspondence:* The word *Kaʿba* derives from *Kaʿb* (height, eminence), referring to the person of Muhammad. By numerical reckoning, *Muḥammad* and *Kaʿb* both equal ninety-two:

| Value | 20 | 70 | 2 | | **92** | Kaʿb |

Upon his birth, ʿAlī first opened his eyes and gazed upon the Kaʿba. Within the Kaʿba—that House of Honor—he received his training. By the Prophet's command, he rested upon the Kaʿba's bed. When the Mubahalā (ordeal by invocation) came, he followed the Kaʿba. He prayed facing the Kaʿba; all believers stand behind ʿAlī, and ʿAlī stands behind the Prophet. In the Mosque of the Two Qiblas, when the Prophet turned his face from Jerusalem to the Kaʿba, ʿAlī turned toward the Kaʿba in accordance with the Prophet's motion. From the Prophet, ʿAlī learned prayer; at the Prophet's command, he paid the *zakāt* (alms tax); by the Lord of the Kaʿba's decree, he wed the Prophet's daughter.

In truth, the Kaʿba is the very essence of the Prophet, and the Black Stone is the essence of ʿAlī. Thus he said, "I am the point of the worlds"—meaning, "However elevated I am in this corporeal realm, I remain ever beneath the Messenger of Allah in rank."[^cite-23]

## Creation of the Heavens and Earth, Darkness and Light

⟪quran 6:1⟫ reads: "All praise and thanks be to Allah, Who created the heavens and the earth and made the darkness and the light."

A question naturally arises: Why does the verse use the plural "heavens" and "darkness," yet say "light" in the singular? And why speak of "creating" darkness, which is not a substance but merely the absence of light?

When rational scholars turned to these difficulties, some simply declared them beyond human understanding—matters of divine mystery not subject to reason. Yet this abandonment of inquiry leaves minds adrift in ignorance, unable to grasp such verses. However, the inner teachings reveal that these heavens, earth, darkness, and light refer not to physical phenomena but to realities of the inner world—to that gnosis (*maʿrifa*) which the people of inner knowledge possess.

These spiritual heavens and earth existed before the Prophet's advent and shall endure until the Day of Judgment. The apparent objection—that reason forbids creation of darkness, which is mere privation—dissolves when we turn from the surface to the essence. Those who cling to literalism have lost even the knowledge of the apparent, wandering in ignorance unable to comprehend.

### The Dual Invitation

This verse alludes to the outward *daʿwa* (the summons) and the inward *daʿwa*—that is, the call to those established in the *sharīʿa* (the revealed law) and the call to the *mustafʿ* (the initiated into inner wisdom). The heavens are the realm of those established in the law; the earth, the realm of the believers. Darkness signifies the outer call; light, the inner call. The epistemic state of this call is illumination—light—while the confusion and divergence in outward matters manifest as darkness. The outer commands of the prophets spring from God's law and are many (purity, prayer, fasting, etc.); yet the inner meaning within them is singular. Thus light appears singular, yet its manifestations are plural.

Why, then, does Allah "create" darkness? Now the answer is plain: these outer commandments originate from Allah, and thus each servant of the summons is called *Rabb* (Lord)—though by Rabb we mean those who teach the people of inward knowledge, namely, the Imāms sent forth by Him.

| *Reality* | *Heavens* | *Earth* | *Light* | *Darkness* |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| *Daʿwa* | Established in Law | Believers | Inner Call | Outer Call |

### The Gift of Intellect and Its Neglect

The Almighty has blessed humanity with intellect such that we are worthy to become Allah's vicegerents upon earth. Little is our gratitude for so great a blessing. Yet if one neglects the training of the soul despite possessing intellect, that very intellect becomes a curse. To refine oneself in the disciplines of the *daʿwa* is essential; without it, even outwardly virtuous deeds are hollow. A person may appear morally upright, but if untrained in the *daʿwa*, he is base indeed—just as the prayer of a worshipper bereft of the wilāya (guardianship) of the Prophet and the love of ʿAlī in his heart is utterly sterile.

*(From Kitāb al-Nakhbat al-Kalām of Sayyidnā Ghulām Ḥusayn Sāhib)*

## Those Who Deny the Signs: The Sealed Gates

Allah declares in Sūrat al-Aʿrāf: ⟪quran 7:40⟫—"Verily, those who deny Our signs and treat them with arrogance, the gates of heaven shall not be opened for them, nor shall they enter Paradise until the camel passes through the eye of the needle."

### The Meaning of "Our Signs"

The *āyāt* (signs) here refer outwardly to the verses of the Qur'an, which the polytheists deny. Inwardly, they are the infallible Imāms, who illuminate the meaning of the Qur'an's outward signs and from them emit the light of guidance—as fire and light emanate from a luminous stone. These Imāms of the Qur'an stand in relation to the spiritually dead as the soul to the body. When they are removed from their stations, the path of guidance crumbles.

Etymologically, *āya* (sign) means knowledge or a marker; thus the infallible Imāms are the symbols of guidance and illumination, directing toward eternal life and immortality. Referring to this inner meaning, Allah says:[^cite-24] *In other words, these Imāms—Allah's signs and proofs, His religion's upholders—will not die from this mortal dwelling until each establishes an Imam like himself, or one who mirrors his character.*

In another place, speaking of ʿĪsā and his mother: ⟪quran 23:50⟫—"We made the son of Mary and his mother a sign."[^cite-25] Thus the Imāms are signs manifest in the world.

### Arrogance Against the Verses

The strongest evidence that the verse refers to the Imāms, not the apparent Qur'anic verses, is this: Allah says those "treated them with arrogance." One may deny or refute words and letters—but to be arrogant *toward* them? Arrogance is directed at persons of greatness and power. Words and phrases have no independent existence; they cannot merit arrogance. What harm can arrogance do to that which lacks consciousness?

### The Gates of Heaven

Then comes the punishment: the gates of heaven will not be opened to them. The heavens are the realm of bright stars, from which sustenance descends to creatures in the form of rain and water. Allah has elevated the heavens above earthly bodies, casting their shadow over them; physical bodies cannot reach or touch them. Yet the human soul, being subtle, easily ascends to these heavens, gains knowledge of them, and masters them completely. Thus the outer heavens govern the bodies of humans, while the human soul dominates these outer heavens.

But Allah has created inner heavens in correspondence to the outer—heavens that govern the human soul as the outer heavens govern the body. These are the saints of the heavens, the saints of Allah (*awliyāʾ Allāh*)—majestic, noble, and miraculous beings. Their states and attributes have profound effects upon the soul. Their gates have been opened to believers, their veils lifted; for them, the Lights (Anwār) are kindled and spiritual rains shower down. Thus this warning to those who deny Allah is inward, not merely outward.

### Entry into Paradise

Then Allah says:[^cite-26] *Those arrogant ones will not enter Paradise until the camel passes through the eye of the needle.*

Consider three terms here: *Paradise*, *camel*, and *needle*.

*Jannat* (paradise) derives from *janin* (fetus—hidden in the womb), *ajtanan* (seeking shelter behind a shield), and *jinn* (the unseen). All carry hidden meanings—things not visible to the naked eye. A fetus is a child concealed within the mother's womb. *Ajtanan* means hiding behind protection. The *jinn* are an invisible people. Similarly, paradise is a garden whose tall, dense, fruit-bearing trees block the sun's heat.

Paradise exists in two modes: potential (*bāl*) and actual (*fʿil*). Consider an example: sperm placed in the mother's womb represents a human being in potentiality. As this sperm develops, protected within the womb from harm and nurtured by its goodness, it is born as a human in actuality. Likewise, the *daʿwa* of the Family of Muhammad (peace be upon them) is a paradise in potentiality, leading to the paradise of actuality. Allah defines Paradise: "Its breadth equals the breadth of the heavens and earth."[^cite-27]

One who has not been established in the *daʿwa* of the Imāms will have no existence in Allah's reward-house. Just as one cannot exist in this world without passing from the father's seed into the mother's womb and developing there, so too one cannot be reborn in the hereafter without entering the inner *daʿwa* of ʿAlī and the successors, without becoming attached through *wilāya* to ʿAlī and his descendants. Without this, one shall not enter Paradise.

### The Camel Passing Through the Needle's Eye

At the verse's end: *Until the camel passes through the eye of the needle.*

The camel is an animal praised in one place in the law and condemned in another. Though it has many virtues, the Prophet said:[^cite-28] "One should not pray when a camel is before you, for in every camel dwells a devil."

Outwardly this seems to disparage the camel; yet examined through the lens of wisdom, its truth emerges clearly. The camel is a heavy-bodied animal bearing immense loads. In this sense, it represents a person who appears towering above others through his strict observance of the *sharīʿa*, his display of piety, and his hypocrisy—walking with stiff neck, bearing upon his brow the marks of countless prostrations. The people deem him to bear the burden of the law heavily. In other words, just as the camel's height distinguishes it among animals, so this person stands prominent among common folk. Yet in reality, he has become the rival and opposite of the Prophet. Through hypocrisy, people mistake him for the Prophet's successor—*khalīfa*. Thus the arrogant are likened to the camel: Allah says to such a one, "He shall not enter Paradise until he has passed the camel through the needle."

### The Tailor and the Needle

Outwardly, a tailor tears cloth, cuts it, and shapes it into a garment that adorns and fits the wearer's body. Inwardly, he is the one who fashions the garment of piety—the true monotheism (*tawḥīd*) that becomes the raiment of the soul. Then he dons this garment of monotheism upon the believer, enabling him to enter Paradise clothed in it. Allah says: ⟪quran 7:27⟫—"O children of Adam, do not let Satan deceive you as he drove your parents from Paradise, stripping them of their raiment."[^cite-29] Thus the *Walī-Allāh* (guardian of Allah) fashions the garment of piety, and in wearing it, the believer becomes a miracle before Allah. This is why Allah says: *The garment of piety is best.*[^cite-30]

A tailor's needle is the delicate instrument through which he stitches. Its point is finer still—so fine that only a single thread may pass through. Similarly, the subtle doctrines of the *Walī-Allāh*'s monotheism can enter the mind only of one who, in humility, has become "thin as a needle," as ʿAlī taught. The arrogant, possessing gross minds, cannot fathom these sciences. They must sit in utter humility before the *Walī-Allāh*—no matter how despicable he appears to them—if ever they hope to understand. Only then can they don the garment of piety, the raiment of Paradise, and enter in.

### The King and the Preacher: A Parable

It is recounted that a mighty eastern king once asked a preacher to expound this verse. The preacher replied: "I shall teach you its meaning, but on one condition: descend from your throne and seat of kingship. Sit in my place, and allow me to sit in yours. Then sit before me as a slave listens to a master."

The king accepted. He descended from the throne and sat humbly before the preacher, who said: "Behold! You are the camel because of your power and the height of your dominion. I am the tailor, and this covenant between us is the needle's eye through which you have now passed by accepting your humiliation. That, in essence, is the meaning of this verse."[^cite-31]

*(From the Majālis al-Muʾayyada, Twenty-second Session, of Sayyidnā al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)*

## Do the Disbelievers Not See? The Separation of the Heavens and Earth

Allah says in Sūrat al-Anbiyāʾ: ⟪quran 21:30⟫—"Do not the disbelievers see that the heavens and the earth were one mass (*ratq*), and We tore them asunder (*fataqqnāhumā*), and We created every living thing from water? Will they not then believe?"

A profound question at once presents itself: When did the disbelievers exist at the moment of creation of the heavens and earth, that they might "see" the heavens and earth as a single mass before being separated? Humans had not yet been brought forth; how could believers and disbelievers exist?

When the scholars of the exoteric schools found themselves unable to furnish a sound interpretation, they resorted to doctrinal closure: "These are matters of Allah's will, beyond the scope of human reason. Accept them without inquiry. The Messenger of Allah came with truth and never erred; therefore, if he said it, it must be true."

Yet this very refusal to investigate is the root of all deficiency. After the Prophet's passing, those ignorant of the inner sciences became rulers of the *sharīʿa*. They declared human reason and revelation to be enemies; they forbade the study of rational sciences and the investigation of *dīn* (religion), and issued *fatwā* of disbelief against those who questioned the true nature of faith. Had the people instead gone to the heirs of the Prophet's knowledge—to those upholding the Book and the Balance of Justice—all beliefs would have been illuminated, all knots untied, and even the most difficult questions answered with ease.

### The Esoteric Interpretation

The true meaning is this: both the heavens and the earth were hidden and implicit in Allah's Word. Then We revealed them into actuality, as Allah says: ⟪quran 16:40⟫—"When We intend a thing, We say to it: 'Be,' and it is."[^cite-33] When we desire to bring something from non-existence into manifestation, it emerges by Our word. As long as a thing exists in *potentiality* (*quwwa*), it is in a state of *ratq* (undivided, formless, abstract). Once it becomes *actuality* (*fʿil*, the state is one of *fataqqun* (separation, form, distinctness). This separation—this appearance of shape and differentiation—is implicit in the divine utterance. The word *ratq* signifies the undifferentiated state; *fataqqun* signifies the emergence of form and individuation. What is the substance that transforms non-existence into existence? What command changes one state into another? The answer: the creative word.

### The Religious Heaven and Earth

There exists also a religious heaven and earth, invisible and hidden, in correspondence to the physical heavens and earth. The inner heaven is the essence of the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him); the inner earth is his *walī* (guardian, successor). The outer heavens send down water; the outer earth absorbs it and brings forth vegetation. Similarly, the Prophet imparts knowledge to his guardian, who preserves it in his heart and thereafter nourishes the souls of believers through this knowledge. Just as the outer heavens and earth produce only flesh, blood, and bone—all destined to perish—so the inner heavens and earth grant eternal life and immortality to the soul. Their ranks and stations are therefore infinitely more exalted than the material heavens and earth.

### Interpretation in Terms of the *Daʿwa*

In other words, the disbelievers and polytheists, and then the hypocrites, witnessed with their own eyes that the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) remained silent, without speech, until he was established in the rank of prophethood—for he had not yet received divine aid. He had not yet called to Allah; he was in a state of latency (*ratq*). But when We bestowed upon him Our revelation and support, he began to call to Allah and entered the station of actuality (*fataqqun*). From this emergence came the manifestation of his successor and the Imāms, who are as earth in relation to his heaven—and this is the separation of heaven and earth.

At what level does divine revelation transform the Prophet into a prophet, rendering the inner heaven real? In the same manner, the testator (*waṣī*) was also in a state of potential power until the Prophet, through his knowledge, raised him to the station of the testator actualized. The Prophet said:[^cite-34] "I and ʿAlī are from the same light." From this we know that ʿAlī's light was concealed within Muhammad's light; the heavens and earth were unified, *ratq*. When the Prophet proclaimed on the day of Ghadīr: *"Whomsoever I am master of, ʿAlī is master of,"*[^cite-35] and appointed ʿAlī as his successor, ʿAlī's light was manifested, and the earth separated from the heavens.

Then the heavens rained upon them the spiritual and intellectual rains of Allah's word, making the earth verdant and becoming a source of life for the human soul. In other words, the Messenger and the Prophets were the first to call unto Allah—to remain in concealment and power. When revelation descended, they became active and began their appointed works. Abu Lahab, Abu Jahl, Abu Sufyān, Muʿāwiya, Ibn Khattāb, and Abū Bakr witnessed all these events with their own eyes. Allah asks them: *"Did not the disbelievers perceive when We established ʿAlī as successor and separated the station of the guardian (earth) from the station of the guardian (heavens)?"*[^cite-36]

*(From the Majālis al-Muʾayyada of Sayyidnā al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)*


<!-- section 22 (id=833, raw_sort=22): لا الہ الا ھو کل شئی ھالک الا وجھہ -->

## ⟪ar:لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ كُلُّ شَىْءٍ هَالِكٌ إِلَّا وَجْهَهُۥ⟫ (There is no deity save Him; all things perish except His Face)

Everything whose existence is eternal and immutable is identical with God Himself. That which may be subject to decay and destruction cannot be Allah. The Qur'an attests to this principle when it declares: ⟪quran 28:88⟫, ⟪ar:وَلَا تَدْعُ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ إِلَهًا آخَرَ ۘ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ كُلُّ شَىْءٍ هَالِكٌ إِلَّا وَجْهَهُۥ ۚ لَهُ ٱلْحُكْمُ وَإِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ ‎⟫

### The Meaning of "Face"

The "face" (*wajh*) of any thing denotes that aspect by which the thing is known and recognized, that dimension through which its true reality is most readily perceived. What remains imperishable in this cosmos—what has subsisted from eternity to eternity—is the religion of Mateen (the firm faith), through which humanity has attained the knowledge of divine unity (*tawḥīd*). This religion, preserved and transmitted across all epochs, has been brought by every prophet in succession until the Seal of the Messengers conveyed it in the form of Islam.[^cite-37]

Because this religion embodies perfection, it is not subject to corruption; it shall endure from everlasting to everlasting. From Adam to the end of the world, the prophets have extended the summons (*daʿwa*) to this faith. This mystery lies concealed within the seven letters of "La ilaha illallah," each letter corresponding to a rational principle:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A | L | A | Q | L | A | M |
| Adam | Noah | Abraham | Moses | Jesus | Muhammad | Permanent Being |

Islam is the cause through which we have come to know Allah. Since we have received Islam and the faith from the Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace), the Messenger himself became for us the manifestation of Allah's guidance. When we accept the Messenger as the cause of Allah's knowledge, then by the same principle, the Messenger's successor—the true Caliph—must also be called the cause of Allah (*sabab Allāh*), for he stands to guide humanity and invite them to truth and the knowledge of the Real.[^cite-38]

Just as the face of something grants us knowledge of its true reality, so too do these saints of Allah (*awliyāʾ Allāh*) impart to us the true knowledge of divine unity. In this capacity, they deserve to be called the cause of Allah's knowledge among humankind.

### Knowledge of the Creator

There is no path to knowledge of the Creator's essence, and such knowledge is indeed impossible. The Creator is necessary in existence (*wājib al-wujūd*), whereas man is contingent (*mumkin*). Between the two can be no direct correspondence. Yet man, being the noblest of creatures, has been granted the blessing that God made him know His face—that is, the perfect religion and knowledge of the Blessed One. Through this knowledge he has been blessed with the companionship of saints, prophets, angels, the Throne, the Chair, Paradise, and all the spiritual realms and divine mysteries.[^cite-39]

It is through this occultation (*ʿibāra*) and divine secrets that certain attributes of Allah become knowable. Thus the Messenger of Allah (upon him be the finest prayers and peace) declared:

> "He who has attained knowledge of his own soul (*nafs*) has attained knowledge of his Lord."

Since the soul itself is a spiritual being, one who does not understand the creature can never understand the Creator. The creature lies near to human sensation, making it readily comprehensible.

### The Religion as the Imperishable Remainder

It has been established that religion and its teachings are in truth the cause of Allah—that which is imperishable, unchanging, which brings neither corruption nor destruction. Only the person who cleaves to this "remainder" (*bāqī*) shall merge into it and endure forever. Referring to this eternity and permanence, Allah declares: ⟪quran 28:88⟫.

A righteous person is one in whom no corruption or vice resides. In the exegetical traditions (*taʾwīl*), this verse is interpreted with regard to the Prophet, the truthful one (*al-Siddīq*), and the martyr—all embodiments of righteousness (*al-ṣālihīn*).

### Scholarly and Practical Worship

The religion of Mateen comprises two dimensions of worship. One is scholarly worship (*ʿibāda ʿilmiyya*), and the other is practical worship (*ʿibāda ʿamaliyya*), which is fulfilled through the observances of the sacred law (*sharīʿa*). Human beings likewise are composed of two elements: soul and body. From the philosophical and mystical perspective, the soul is understood as the sum of the noble capacities of the body—the abstract and incorporeal powers. Thinking, understanding, and knowing are operations of the soul, not of the body. Therefore, the work of knowledge (*maʿrifa*) and the worship attending upon it belong to the soul alone, and not to the body. This worship is gnosis (*maʿrifa*), which pertains to the human soul, which merits such knowledge by virtue of its nobility, virtue, and dignity.

The body, by contrast, is itself inert and possesses neither the capacity nor the aptitude for comprehension and the acquisition of knowledge. Therefore, Allah has made it obligatory upon man to perform practical worship, which governs the body, so that through the attainment of perfection in bodily discipline, piety, and bodily worship, he becomes worthy of the worship of knowledge.

*(From the Assemblies of al-Muʾayyad, attributed to Sayyidnā al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)*

## ⟪ar:وَكُلٌّۭ فِى فَلَكٍۢ يَسْبَحُونَ⟫ (Each floats in an orbit)

The heart of the Qur'an is, indeed, the heart of Sūrat Yāsīn; for this verse occupies the central position within that chapter. It is not precisely at the mathematical center, but two verses removed from it—just as in the human body the heart lies not at the exact center, but slightly to the left.

The particular virtue of this verse is that whether it is recited in the forward direction or reversed, it remains perfectly preserved:

⟪quran 36:40⟫: ⟪ar:‏لَا ٱلشَّمْسُ يَنۢبَغِى لَهَآ أَن تُدْرِكَ ٱلْقَمَرَ وَلَا ٱلَّيْلُ سَابِقُ ٱلنَّهَارِ ۚ وَكُلٌّۭ فِى فَلَكٍۢ يَسْبَحُونَ ‎⟫

### The Circle and the Cosmic Order

The term "orbit" (*falak*) refers to the circular path. No planet can escape its celestial sphere. Similarly, each rank in the summons (*daʿwa*)—whether the foundation (*asās*), the silent one (*sāmit*), the summoner (*dāʿī*), and others—possesses a circle (*dāʾira*) within which he executes his duties. It is impermissible for a person to overstep the authority (*wilāya*) of his station. Should he do so, the system of the invitation will suffer disruption, just as if the planets were to abandon their orbits, the world would fall into chaos and its equilibrium would be disturbed.[^cite-40]

*(Transmitted from Mullā Saʿd Allāh ibn Hazrat Nasrallāh, may God preserve his secret)*

<!-- section 24 (id=482, raw_sort=24): قل لا أسئلکم علیہ أجرا الا المودۃ فی القربی -->

## ⟪ar:قُل لَّآ أَسْـَٔلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلَّا ٱلْمَوَدَّةَ فِى ٱلْقُرْبَىٰ⟫ (Say: I ask of you no reward except love of kinship)

### The Historical Occasion

Once the people of Medina came before the Prophet (upon him be peace) bearing bags laden with precious jewels. Many of them offered substantial portions of their wealth, saying: "You have delivered us from great tribulation; you have drawn us forth from the Fire and shown us the path to Paradise. We were enemies unto one another; you have made us brethren. Accept what we offer as recompense for your service." In that moment, the Prophet remained silent. Then this verse descended from Allah: "O Prophet, ask of them as a reward that they love My family (*ahl bayti*)." ⟪quran 42:23⟫: ⟪ar:‏ذَلِكَ ٱلَّذِى يُبَشِّرُ ٱللَّهُ عِبَادَهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ ۗ قُل لَّآ أَسْـَٔلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلَّا ٱلْمَوَدَّةَ فِى ٱلْقُرْبَىٰ ۗ وَمَن يَقْتَرِفْ حَسَنَةًۭ نَّزِدْ لَهُۥ فِيهَا حُسْنًا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ شَكُورٌ ‎⟫

In this verse, Allah addresses the Prophet: "Announce to the people, 'I do not ask of you any recompense for my prophethood except that you love my family (*ahl al-bayt*).'" Where love exists, obedience follows; where obedience dwells, observance flourishes. This verse alludes to the very love that the Prophet proclaimed at Ghadīr Khumm concerning ʿAlī.[^cite-41]

### The Weightiness of the Request

There was a time when the unbelievers of Mecca brought before the Prophet the gold of Mount Abū Qubays, offering him beauty and power on the condition that he would renounce his prophethood. He rejected all these things, declaring: "Even were you to place the sun in one hand and the moon in the other, I would not abandon the message of Allah." If a person has been offered such abundance without asking and has spurned it, then when that person makes a request, it must surely be a matter of profound importance—a request of supreme significance. And what did he ask for? Only the love of the Ahl al-Bayt. This demonstrates that love for the family of the Prophet transcends all the wealth, beauty, and dominion of the world.

### The Reason for the Creation of the Universe: Love of the Panj-Ākh-i Pāk (Five Pure Ones)

The reason for the creation of this entire cosmos is none other than love for the Five Pure Ones. Every human being acquires and fashions things according to his nature for the sake of his beloved. Allah created this vast universe out of love for these five, and He declared: "I have not raised the vault of the heavens, nor laid the floor of the earth, nor kindled the lamps of the sun and moon, nor set the spheres in rotation, nor created the seas that flow and the ships that sail therein—except in love for the five pure ones clothed in the mantle (*kisāʾ*) and purified from all defilement."[^cite-42]

This beautiful cosmos has been created solely for the love of the Ahl al-Bayt, as testified by Allah's words: "Were it not for you, O Prophet, I would not have created the heavens." The Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace and blessings) said: "Allah created my light first, then created the light of all the worlds." He further declared: "ʿAlī and I are from a single light (*nūr*)." He explained: "From our light came the earth and the heavens; then from our light came the light of beauty and grace, from which were fashioned the sun and moon." At that moment, the light of Mawlānā Fāṭima al-Zahrāʾ (upon her be peace) was born—and thus she is called Mashiyyat Allāh (the will of Allah), through which the love and intention of Allah the Most High was expressed.

It is by the grace of Allah that He has created all things for these five holy ones. How dare we, servants of clay, create anything in love of the Panj-Ākh? We ourselves are creatures; what then have we to offer? Man is created weak in his very essence. We can express our love only by holding fast to the mighty rope of Allah and loving the Ahl al-Bayt of the Prophet with wholehearted devotion. The Messenger of Allah declared: "Adorn your gatherings with the remembrance of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, for the remembrance of ʿAlī is my remembrance, and my remembrance is the remembrance of Allah, and the remembrance of Allah is worship." So the mention of ʿAlī has become a form of worship itself.[^cite-43]

In another place he proclaimed:

> O ʿAlī! Allah has given His Prophets and Messengers superiority over His angels, and He has given me superiority over all the Prophets and Messengers. After me, O ʿAlī, this virtue shall be yours, and for the Imams who come after you. Indeed, the angels are servants of us and of those who love us. O ʿAlī! The angels who bear the Throne of God and those who glorify God around the Throne seek forgiveness for those who believe in our guardianship (*wilāya*).

### The Significance of the Request

This is no trivial request, but a matter of fundamental recompense (*ujra*). Whoever does not render this recompense to the Prophet's prophethood has, in effect, slighted his prophethood and shall surely be called to account for it on the Day of Resurrection. It is significant that a request is made only when no one gives willingly without being asked. This shows that when the Messenger of Allah completed the proclamation of his message, he awaited the people's voluntary recompense. But when the Messenger of Allah saw that they would not give of their own accord, he was commanded by Allah to request the recompense for his prophethood.

A second reason for this request lay in the Prophet's prescient knowledge (*ʿilm al-ghayb*) through divine support. With foresight, he beheld ʿAlī's martyrdom, the tragedy that would befall Fāṭima, Ḥasan drinking poison, and Ḥusayn beheaded. He thus appealed to the people: "If you would offer me any recompense for my prophethood, then have mercy upon my family and do not cause them suffering." This command came from Allah so that the persecution of the Ahl al-Bayt might cease.

### Recompense as the Boundary Between the Lawful and the Unlawful

Nothing is permitted without recompense and compensation. If a person prays in stolen garments or in clothes not properly sewn, can his prayer be valid and acceptable? Consider then what befalls the prayer of one who does not render compensation to him who delivers the message of that prayer. Every commandment of the sacred law is a part of the proclamation of the Messenger of Allah. If recompense is not rendered for it, no deed of the sacred law will be acceptable.

### The Debate of Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq on Truth

One day Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (upon him be peace) proceeded through the market. He observed a man stealing two pomegranates from one shop, then two loaves of bread from another. The man then proceeded to a street corner and distributed these four items to a poor and destitute person.

The Imām questioned him about this deed. The man replied: "O son of the Messenger of Allah, you know that in Allah's law, the reward for one good deed is ten good deeds, while there is one sin for one sin. I stole these four things—thus four sins were recorded in my book of deeds. Then I gave these four things to a poor man—thus forty good deeds were recorded. From the forty good deeds, four sins were deducted, and thirty-six good deeds remain to my account."

The Imām said: "O man, may your mother bereave you! You are heedless of Allah's Book and its commandments. Your reckoning is false! The correct reckoning is this: You stole four things, increasing your sins fourfold. You did not render compensation to the owners of the property—indeed, you appropriated it wrongfully. Then you gave it to a poor person to whom you had no right to give it. Thus four additional sins were written in your account, and you have gained no good deed whatsoever—rather, you have accumulated eightfold in your transgression."

There is a matter of deep concern here: If the Messenger of Allah performs prayer and other observances of the sacred law without receiving recompense for his prophethood, will he be rewarded or will he incur sin?

In His Clear Book, Allah commanded the Muslims to pray, to give the alms-tax (*zakāt*), to fast, to perform the pilgrimage, and to struggle in His cause. Each of these acts is distinct in its nature; if a person prays but does not perform pilgrimage, he receives the reward of prayer, even though he will be questioned about the pilgrimage. But when it comes to the matter of love for the Ahl al-Bayt, it is bound up with recompense. If the Messenger does not receive the recompense for his prophethood, he will not be rewarded for any of the acts of the sacred law transmitted from the Messenger. Therefore, it is never permissible to dispose of property without rendering its price under any circumstance.

### The Light (*Nūr*) Refers to the Five Pure Ones

Allah declares: ⟪quran 5:15⟫: ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَهْلَ ٱلْكِتَبِ قَدْ جَآءَكُمْ رَسُولُنَا يُبَيِّنُ لَكُمْ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا كُنتُمْ تُخْفُونَ مِنَ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَيَعْفُوا۟ عَن كَثِيرٍۢ ۚ قَدْ جَآءَكُم مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ نُورٌۭ وَكِتَبٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫

If we understand the word "light" (*nūr*) in this verse to refer to the Qur'ān, as many commentators have stated, then why did Allah mention the Book separately after referring to it already in this verse? The verse speaks of the Messenger and the Light, then speaks of the Light and the Book separately. This indicates that the light is something distinct, the Book something else—a third reality besides the light, the messenger, and the Book, which is neither messenger nor book.

The Messenger of Allah declared: "ʿAlī and I are of one light." This indicates that ʿAlī is a light. The Messenger proclaimed: "Fāṭima is of me." Thus, if the Messenger is light, then Fāṭima too is light. He said of al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn: "Their flesh is my flesh, and their blood is my blood." It becomes evident that al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn too share in the Prophet's light.

### Kinship (*Qurba*)

There are two forms of kinship: the permanent and the temporary. Temporary kinship is the relation between husband and wife; so long as both remain together, the rights of kinship endure. But if divorce occurs, all kinship is severed. Likewise, the relationship between a house and its occupant is temporary, and all friendships too are temporary and may be broken tomorrow.

Permanent kinship is the bond of mother, father, sibling, brother, sister, aunt, and uncle. In the Prophet's request, we must seek out his permanent relatives and love those who are the people of purity and who are related to him through descent.

Shortly after the revelation of the verse, someone asked the Messenger of Allah: "O Messenger of Allah! Who did you take with you to the *mubāhala* (mutual imprecation), and who are those to whom this verse was revealed?" He replied: "We are Fāṭima, and with us are Fāṭima—meaning her father, her husband, and their two sons." Thus he made Fāṭima al-Zahrāʾ the pivot around which all relations revolve. She is at once daughter, wife, and mother.

### When Is Recompense Demanded?

Religion has always been Islam in Allah's sight. The Prophet did not bring a new religion; rather, it is the same religion that began with Adam. With the passage of time, laws changed and ordinances were codified. But the name Islam has remained constant since its inception and shall remain so until its end. With this word, the name of Adam was associated with the Messenger of Allah; sometimes Nūḥ the Prophet of Allah, sometimes Ibrāhīm the Friend of Allah, sometimes Mūsā the Intimate of Allah, sometimes ʿĪsā the Spirit of Allah. The final addition to this word was Muḥammad the Messenger of Allah, but it could not be completed without ʿAlī the Guardian of Allah. This is the same ʿAlī who patted his breast and declared: "Here are the scholars (*ʿulamāʾ*)," and who said from the pulpit: "Ask me before you lose me."

Before the Messenger of Allah, 124,000 prophets came, yet none requested recompense for their prophethood and prophecy. It was only the Messenger of Allah who asked the people for recompense for his prophethood. This invites reflection and demands attention. The worker requests his wages only when he completes his work. When the work remains incomplete, recompense is not sought. Throughout the ages past, the religion of Islam progressed gradually; therefore, no other prophet requested recompense for his prophethood. In the time of the Messenger of Allah, the religion of Islam was perfected, its ordinances were codified, and when he declared at Ghadīr Khumm: "Whomever I am the master of, ʿAlī too is the master of"—at that moment Allah revealed this verse, which shall shine forth until the end of the world: "Today We have perfected your religion for you and completed Our favor upon you, and have chosen Islam as your way." At that moment, the work of his prophethood was complete, and he requested his recompense. All the prophets labored in bringing Islam to its final form and endured trials and tribulations. Therefore, the Prophet's request for recompense for his prophethood was, in truth, a request for recompense on behalf of himself and for the tireless efforts of all the prophets.

### The Distinction Between Relatives and the *Umma*

The one who commands is distinct from the one to whom the command is directed. Allah is the one who commands the Messenger, and the Messenger is the one who commands the people. Allah cannot be a messenger, and a messenger cannot be Allah. In the same way, the *umma* (the community) is the one who renders recompense to the relatives, and the relatives are the recipients of the recompense. The *umma* is one entity, the relatives another. The *umma* cannot become relatives, and the relatives cannot be the *umma*. Here, the *umma* is commanded to love the relatives as a recompense, because the message of prophethood has been preached to the *umma* and not to the relatives. Had prophethood been preached to the relatives, they too would fall under this command and recompense would be demanded from them.

### The People's Opinion on This Verse

Wages are demanded from those who have performed labor. The Messenger's mission was not given to him by the *umma*; it was given to him by Allah and he was sent as a messenger by Allah. Therefore, only Allah can render recompense to the Messenger of Allah. What right does the *umma* possess, and how can it compensate the Messenger of Allah? The *umma* is in need of the Messenger's intercession; what recompense then can it render him? It became evident that it was impossible for the *umma* to compensate the Messenger of Allah. The Qur'ān itself declares that the Messenger's recompense rests only with Allah: ⟪quran 34:47⟫: ⟪ar:‏قُلْ مَا سَأَلْتُكُم مِّنْ أَجْرٍۢ فَهُوَ لَكُمْۖ إِنْ أَجْرِىَ إِلَّا عَلَى ٱللَّهِۖ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ شَهِيدٌۭ ‎⟫

A sect among the *ahl al-zāhir* (the literalists) claimed this verse abrogates the previous one. They asserted: "In this verse, Allah Most High declares that Allah alone will reward the Messenger for his prophethood." Therefore, the Muslims need only treat their relatives kindly. This cannot possibly be correct, for many among the *umma* have relatives and kinfolk who are unbelievers. How then could Allah reward the Messenger of Allah for love of unbelievers?

These people have led themselves astray and others as well. This second verse does not abrogate the first; rather, it supports and confirms it. The true meaning of this verse is that the *umma* alone will benefit from the recompense that the Messenger of Allah has requested from the *umma* in the form of love for his Ahl al-Bayt. Neither the Prophet nor the Ahl al-Bayt will derive benefit from it. This is because the Prophet and his family are rich in knowledge and blessing, while the *umma* is poor and in need. Paradise cannot be attained without their guidance. Whoever abandons the family of the Ahl al-Bayt has Paradise far from him and Hell near him. Only Allah rewards the Messenger of Allah and the Ahl al-Bayt.

### Statement

Just as every *sūra* (chapter) of the Qur'ān is blessed and trustworthy—whether it be a long *sūra* like *Sūrat al-Baqara* or a short one like *Sūrat al-Kawthar*—in the same way, among the Ahl al-Bayt, each is honored and beloved, whether it be Muslim ibn ʿAqīl, Hānī, Qāsim, and ʿAbbās, like the shorter *sūras*, or Imām al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn, like the longer ones. The love and guardianship of all of them has been enjoined upon us by the Messenger of Allah.

### What Is the True Benefit of the Recompense for Prophethood?

The recompense for prophethood is the love of the Imāms of the guardianship (*wilāya*) of Amīr al-Muʾminīn and his descendants, without which the beauties cannot bestow any benefit. The Qur'ān has reached all Muslims; so why has faith not entered their hearts? It is known that only those who render this recompense for prophethood and perform the guardianship of Amīr al-Muʾminīn and the Ahl al-Bayt enter into faith. Those who neglect the guardianship cannot enter fully into belief.

*(From the assemblies of Hazrat Ẓiyā ʿAlī, may God sanctify his secret)*

## ⟪ar:لِّيَغْفِرَ لَكَ ٱللَّهُ مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِن ذَنۢبِكَ وَمَا تَأَخَّرَ⟫ (That Allah may forgive you your sins, past and future)

When Sūrat al-Fatḥ was revealed, the Prophet (upon him be peace) rejoiced greatly, for this chapter contains many concealed virtues. There is great merit in reciting this *sūra* each morning. Within it lies mention of the clear victory (*fatḥ al-mubīn*), which refers to prophethood's outward manifestation—the clear triumph Allah bestowed upon him. Yet more exalted still: this verse indicates that Allah forgave the Messenger of Allah his sins, both those that preceded and those that followed.

⟪quran 48:2⟫: ⟪ar:‏لِّيَغْفِرَ لَكَ ٱللَّهُ مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِن ذَنۢبِكَ وَمَا تَأَخَّرَ وَيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكَ وَيَهْدِيكَ صِرَطًۭا مُّسْتَقِيمًۭا ‎⟫

In expounding the realities of this verse, the Prophet declares: "Muḥammad is exalted, superior, and sinless—free from all transgressions and sins." The 'Muḥammad' mentioned in this verse is one of the limits (*ḥudūd*) of the invitation (*daʿwa*), appearing as a veil (*ḥijāb*) to Muḥammad (upon him be peace). Such veils are susceptible to error.

### Concerning the Limits (*Ḥudūd*) of the Invitation

The veils of the Imāms, which are constituted as limits progressing through the ranks, do not fall within the realm of infallibility (*ʿisma*). They are liable to mistake. But those veils constituted from the perspective of divine love (*maḥabbat al-ilāhiyya*) fall within the realm of infallibility and are free from all error.

### What Preceded: The Earlier Sin

His earlier sin was his inclination toward Abū Bakr—that he be granted a place in the invitation's inward dimension (*bāṭin*), to preserve the invitation both in its external and internal forms. Yet it was the decree of nature that Abū Bakr should not be granted even a position in the inner invitation. Therefore, Gabriel came with a warning from Allah to the Prophet:

*If you associate anyone with ʿAlī in the inward invitation, Allah will render all your deeds null.* [^cite-44]

At that moment, the Messenger of Allah was satisfied, yet his inclination toward Abū Bakr had been established. Though your inclination was not rooted in disobedience to Allah—indeed, you desired only to preserve your call—it became, in Allah's sight, a transgression.

### A Lesson for the Believers

Here lies a profound lesson for the believers: The Messenger of Allah, who called upon Allah's command for twenty-three years and endured all manner of hardship, and who is the beloved of Allah, is told: "If you associate anyone with the station of ʿAlī, We shall render all your deeds void!" The matter of concern for the believers is this: if they mix love of the enemies of ʿAlī with the love of ʿAlī, what will Allah do to their deeds? (May God grant us protection!)

### What Followed: The Later Sin

The final sin of the Prophet was his delay in proclaiming ʿAlī's bequest (*waṣiyya*). When he returned from ʿArafāt after performing the pilgrimage, revelation from Allah began descending upon him, so heavy was its burden that his camel bent and knelt repeatedly. That revelation was, in truth, the revelation of ʿAlī's bequest. The command "Convey what has been revealed to you from your Lord" had already begun descending, yet the Prophet sought an opportune moment to make the proclamation.

Because of this delay, the opportunity was lost as he left the valley of ʿArafāt. When he reached the station of Ghadīr Khumm, Allah commanded him with great severity:

⟪quran 5:67⟫: *O Messenger! Convey what has been revealed to you from your Lord. If you do not, you have not conveyed His message. And Allah will protect you from the people.*[^cite-45]

If you have not conveyed it, then you have not conveyed the message.

The verse that truly stands out is this:

*Convey what has been revealed to you concerning ʿAlī from your Lord.*

But the hypocrites later expunged ʿAlī's name from this verse.

### A Profound Statement

The Messenger to whom Allah Most High has granted titles of affection throughout his life—at times "Muzzammil" (the enfolded one), at times "Muddaththir" (the covered one), at times "Ṭāhā," at times "Yāsīn"—is here addressed: "O Messenger!" This was a most severe address from Allah to His Messenger!

As soon as this verse was revealed and the glad tidings of Allah's protection of your faith were received, you gathered the people, called back those who had gone ahead, and waited for those who lagged behind, so that all might assemble. In describing the significance of this gathering, the Summoners declare: "At that moment, all the prophets of ages past and the templates of all former nations were assembled at this place, and all the forms that would appear after him were present at this gathering until the Day of Judgment."

When the seventh hour came, he proclaimed ʿAlī's bequest and declared: "Whomever I am the master (*mawlā*) of, ʿAlī too is the master." That is, he took from all those present—past and future generations—the oath of ʿAlī's guardianship.

Yet this delay in doing so was a sin in Allah's sight. In this verse, Allah forgave the veil of Muḥammad (upon him be peace) for these two sins!

*(From the lessons of *Risālat al-Rawḍat al-Jināna*, transmitted by Syednā Jaʿfar, related by Mullā Ghulām Ḥusayn Ṣāḥib)*

## ⟪ar:وَنَحْنُ أَقْرَبُ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ حَبْلِ ٱلْوَرِيدِ⟫ (We are nearer to him than his jugular vein)

⟪quran 50:16⟫: ⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا ٱلْإِنسَنَ وَنَعْلَمُ مَا تُوَسْوِسُ بِهِۦ نَفْسُهُۥ ۖ وَنَحْنُ أَقْرَبُ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ حَبْلِ ٱلْوَرِيدِ ‎⟫

*We have created man, and We know the whisperings of his soul. We are nearer to him than his jugular vein.*

How can a Being who transcends the bounds of time and space, who is beyond the near and the far, draw near to the jugular vein of the neck? If this means the divine essence, would not the singular pronoun (*anā*) have been more fitting than the plural? What is the virtue in using the first-person plural?

Some say this is excellent speech and there is nothing amiss. The king, though alone, may address the people saying "We command!" Yet what a perversion of divine unity is this!

Syednā Ghulām Ḥusayn Ṣāḥib used to say that the Sultan of the Gnostics (*Sulṭān al-ʿĀrifīn*) has proclaimed: "Whoever knows himself knows his Lord." One supposes the self (*nafs*) to be singular, yet upon reflection, we discover three within it.[^cite-46]

### The Three Dimensions of the Soul

The lowest degree of the soul is manifested in plant life (*al-nafs al-nāmiya*). The next manifestation of the soul's vital forces appears as sensation (*al-nafs al-ḥāssiya*), evident in the animal. Above these both stands the rational soul (*al-nafs al-nāṭiqa*), whose manifestation is the human being. Rather, he is the manifestation of these three souls. This human being is the one who attains knowledge of the Creator and understands the secrets of the cosmos—and thus is he the noblest of creatures. It is to this man that the Almighty says: "Where do you go seeking truth when you are full of mysteries? *Al-Insān sirr walaʾinī sirruhu*—Man is My secret, and I am his secret." The Prophet declared in a sacred hadith (*ḥadīth qudsī*): "Grasp the allusion of this verse!"

The three souls may be understood as:

| N | H | N |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Nāmiya (the vegetative soul) | Ḥāssiya (the sensitive soul) | Nāṭiqa (the rational soul) |

Comprehend these three! The *nūn* (plural "we") stands nearer to the jugular vein of this man. Understand the capacities of the soul, perceive its proximity, and then the soul of the world will become apparent to you, and the concept of nearness will be grasped. You will then discover that the soul is nearer to the human being than the divine essence! Having known this, one will say and truly believe: "I have not fulfilled the right of Your knowledge," and will rightly understand that the inability to perceive is itself a form of knowledge.

## None Shall Touch It Save the Purified

Allah states in the Holy Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 56:77⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّهُۥ لَقُرْآنٌۭ كَرِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *That (this) is indeed an honourable recital (the Noble Quran).*

> ⟪quran 56:78⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فِى كِتَبٍۢ مَّكْنُونٍۢ ‎⟫
> *In a Book well-guarded (with Allah in the heaven i.e. Al-Lauh Al-Mahfuz).*

> ⟪quran 56:79⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَّا يَمَسُّهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱلْمُطَهَّرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Which (that Book with Allah) none can touch but the purified (i.e. the angels).*

In these verses, Allah declares that none may touch the Qur'an—that preserved Scripture—save those who are purified. To the superficial reader, this pronouncement appears contradictory to observable fact: do not the disbelievers themselves handle and recite the Qur'an with their hands? This apparent contradiction dissolves when one recognizes that "touching" (*al-mass*) here refers not to outward, physical contact but to inward comprehension and penetration of meaning. The verse speaks to a deeper reality.

### The Body and Spirit of the Qur'an

The themes of the Qur'an rest upon two dimensions: the exoteric (the manifest, *al-ẓāhir*) and the esoteric (the hidden, *al-bāṭin*), just as the created world itself divides into the visible realm and the Hereafter, or as the human being comprises body and soul. One dimension is external, subject to constant change and transformation; the other is inward, remaining stable within its own nature. The transformations that affect the body—as time itself imposes different effects in different seasons—mirror the cosmic pattern: winter gives way to heat, spring turns to autumn. Similarly, the human frame moves from youth through strength to weakness, from health to illness.

The Qur'an, embodied as it is in letters and words, exhibits profound variations.[^cite-47] Although it is the Book of Guidance, its outward appearances are fraught with apparent contradictions and ambiguities. Allah declares at the opening of the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 2:1⟫
> *Alif-Lam-Mim. That is the Book, wherein is no doubt, a guidance to the God-fearing.*

In Arabic, the demonstrative *dhālika* (that, those) refers to matters absent or distant, whereas *hādhā* (this, it) designates things near and present. A question therefore arises: when the reader holds the Book in hand and reads it before him, why does Allah employ the distant demonstrative? Would it not be more natural to say, "This is the Book of which there is no doubt"? Yet in the very first verse, Allah affirms the Book's clarity and its guidance for humanity—and precisely this use of the distant form leaves the attentive reader perplexed, wondering what the Qur'an intends to convey through such linguistic subtlety.

### Apparent Contradictions in Divine Attributes

As the reader advances through the Qur'an, one verse establishes a conception in the mind, yet shortly thereafter another verse compels imagination toward an opposite understanding.[^cite-48] Consider the believer who reads:

> ⟪quran 75:22-23⟫
> *Faces on that Day radiant, looking upon their Lord.*

From this, the reader conceives that God may be beheld with sight. But immediately upon reciting:

> ⟪quran 6:103⟫
> *Vision seizes Him not, yet He seizes all vision; He is the Subtle, the All-Aware.*

The mind is thrown into confusion: which understanding is correct? In one place, Allah declares that certain faces shall see Him; in another, He states that eyes cannot perceive Him. How is the believer to reconcile these?

### The Question of Human Will

Likewise, there arises the matter of human will. When one reads:

> ⟪quran 76:29⟫
> *Whosoever willeth may believe, and whosoever willeth may disbelieve.*

One establishes the belief that acceptance of faith and disbelief rest upon human choice, within human control. Yet soon thereafter comes:

> ⟪quran 81:29⟫
> *Yet you shall not will it, unless God wills.*

And even more unmistakably:

> ⟪quran 76:30⟫
> *For Allah wills whatsoever He wills.*

One is left to wonder: does human will possess any reality at all, or is everything merely the will of Allah? The concept becomes clouded.

### The Qur'an as the Word of God

Similarly, the Qur'an is the Word of Allah. As one recites, one encounters:

> ⟪quran 26:192-194⟫
> *This is the revelation of the Lord of the Worlds, brought down by the Trustworthy Spirit upon thy heart, that thou mayest be one of the Warners.*

From this, the belief becomes firm that the Qur'an is undoubtedly Allah's Word, revealed to the heart of the Messenger of Allah. Yet when one continues to read:

> ⟪quran 69:40-41⟫
> *This is not the word of a poet; little do you believe. Nor is it the word of a soothsayer; little do you remember.*

Confusion arises: Is it the Qur'an or the utterance of the Messenger? The reader is perplexed.

### The Creation of the Heavens and the Earth

In the same manner, those who believe in the heavens and the earth wonder whether one was created before the other, for two seemingly contradictory verses exist on this subject. In one, Allah states:

> ⟪quran 2:29⟫
> *It is He Who created for you all that is on earth, then turned to the heaven and formed it as seven heavens.*

This is clear and unambiguous—the earth was created first, then the heavens. Yet elsewhere:

> ⟪quran 79:27-28⟫
> *Are you the harder to create, or the heaven He built? He raised its roof and fashioned it, then darkened its night and brought forth its dayspring.*

Here it is stated that the heavens were created first, then the earth. Which of these two is correct?

Beyond these examples lie countless other verses wherein apparent contradiction and conflict abound. This inevitably leads to differences in understanding and plants doubt in the believer's heart regarding his faith. For this reason, one of our preachers lifted the Qur'an above his head and exclaimed, "By Allah! This Book has led astray generation after generation!"[^cite-49]

### The Spirit of the Qur'an

Yet unlike the body of the Qur'an—its words—there exists a spirit, a meaning (*rūḥ*, *maʿnā*) that does not admit the fluctuations and influences that affect the human body, but remains constant in its essence. All agree that the soul is the true reality in the human being and the source of virtue. A scholar or physician of great renown in the world possesses neither learning nor medical knowledge once the soul departs from the body. A living person has value; the dead are worthless. Thus the real worth resides not in the body but in the soul.

The same principle applies to the Qur'an. Its words dwell in the mansion of the body, for it is a written book of words. Yet the Ahl al-Bayt (a.s.)—the Family of the Prophet—are the soul of the Ahl al-Bayt (a.s.) who unveil the hidden meaning beneath the words. Should their soul be separated from the Qur'an, it becomes lifeless and dead.[^cite-50] For this reason, the Commander of the Faithful (*Amīr al-Muʾminīn*), ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (a.s.), called the Qur'an a mute speaker and a speaking mute. The Qur'an is the utterance; the Ahl al-Bayt are its meaning. Allah called the Qur'an the *dhikr* (remembrance) and the Ahl al-Bayt the *Ahl al-Dhikr* (People of the Remembrance), and said:

> ⟪quran 21:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *If you do not know, ask the People of the Remembrance.*

When you read the Qur'an with the lips, you become a Muslim; when you understand its meaning, you become a believer. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) declared, "I leave among you two precious things: the Book of Allah and my Itrah (my Family, the Ahl al-Bayt). As long as you hold fast to both of them, you shall not go astray. There shall never be separation between them until they return to me at the Pool of Kausar."

The Prophet further said, "There shall never be separation between the Qur'an and the Ahl al-Bayt, between the Book and the People of the Book, between the Remembrance and the People of the Remembrance." If the Qur'an exists in every age, then the Ahl al-Bayt must also be present in every age. If the Qur'an is guidance, then the Ahl al-Bayt too embody guidance. Were one sufficient by itself, there would be no need to link it with the other. Allah Himself explains this conjunction in His Clear Book. On the one hand, He says of the Book:

> ⟪quran 6:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ وَعِندَهُۥ مَفَاتِحُ ٱلْغَيْبِ لَا يَعْلَمُهَآ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِى ٱلْبَرِّ وَٱلْبَحْرِ ۚ وَمَا تَسْقُطُ مِن وَرَقَةٍ إِلَّا يَعْلَمُهَا وَلَا حَبَّةٍۢ فِى ظُلُمَتِ ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَا رَطْبٍۢ وَلَا يَابِسٍ إِلَّا فِى كِتَبٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *There is not a grain in the darkness of the earth, nor anything fresh or dry, but is written in a Clear Record.*

On the other hand, He says to the Imam:

> ⟪quran 36:12⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّا نَحْنُ نُحْىِ ٱلْمَوْتَىٰ وَنَكْتُبُ مَا قَدَّمُوا۟ وَآثَرَهُمْۚ وَكُلَّ شَىْءٍ أَحْصَيْنَهُ فِىٓ إِمَامٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *We have recorded all things in a clear Imam.*

Thus, "touching" in the verse means understanding the meaning of the Qur'an. It is indeed better if one approaches the apparent text with formal purification (ritual ablution), yet this is not strictly obligatory. In this verse, Allah declares that none can touch the true meaning of the Qur'an—its inner reality—except the pure and sanctified servants, that is, none can attain the hidden meaning behind the words of the Book except the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt. These pure servants of Allah are those upon whom Allah has sent down the verse of purification:

> ⟪quran 33:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ ٱلرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ ٱلْبَيْتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمْ تَطْهِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *Allah wishes only to remove uncleanness from you, O People of the House, and to purify you with a thorough purification.*

Concerning these, the Qur'an declares, "Allah intends to purify the Ahl al-Bayt from all impurity and to cleanse them as they deserve." Therefore, if you wish to comprehend the manifest and hidden things recorded in this Book, ask the Imam al-Mubeen (the Manifest Imam). The foremost of these Ahl al-Bayt is the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (a.s.), who said from the pulpit, "Ask me before you lose me!" Whosoever clings only to the outward Qur'an shall forever stumble and shall never be guided aright.

*(From the Majālis al-Muʾayyadiyya of al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)*

## By the Dawn and the Ten Nights

Allah swears:

> ⟪quran 89:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلْفَجْرِ ‎⟫
> *By the dawn;*

> ⟪quran 89:2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَيَالٍ عَشْرٍۢ ‎⟫
> *By the ten nights;*

> ⟪quran 89:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلشَّفْعِ وَٱلْوَتْرِ ‎⟫
> *And by the even and the odd;*

> ⟪quran 89:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ ‎⟫
> *And by the night when it departs.*

The *Fajr* (dawn) marks the conjunction and opening of transformation. The *Fajr* refers to those forms of the past that have progressed through the ranks and are now to appear in the *Qayyam al-Qiyāma*—the resurrection of standing.

The *Waliyal ʿAshar* (the Ten Nights) represents the ten degrees through which the forms advance and achieve human station. They are:

| 1 | Mineral Power | 6 | The Power of Honor |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2 | Organic Power | 7 | The Power of Ownership |
| 3 | The Power of Animal Anger | 8 | Quddsī Power (the Holy Power) |
| 4 | The Power of Humanity (in two aspects: lower and rational) | 9 | The Power of the Hereafter |
| 5 | The Power of Wisdom | 10 | The Power of Perfected Humanity |

As for the *Shafʿ* (the Even) and the *Witr* (the Odd):

The *Shafʿ*—the soul that is the origin of the universe—connects form and substance to one another. Form depends upon matter; matter in turn depends upon form for its existence.

The *Witr*—the separation of matter from sense-perception—represents the degree of sanctity (*qudsiyya*). At this station, the particular form reaches its consummation.

And the *Lail idha yasri* (the Night When It Departs):

This denotes the transition of the form toward development, such that the form attains the limit of primary perfection (*kamāl awwal*). Here they have achieved the state of first completion and must now attain secondary perfection (*kamāl thānī*) through their own exaltation.

## Your Lord Will Give to You, and You Will Be Well-Pleased

Allah says concerning the days after the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings be upon him):

> ⟪quran 93:5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَسَوْفَ يُعْطِيكَ رَبُّكَ فَتَرْضَىٰٓ ‎⟫
> *Your Lord will give you, and you will be well-pleased.*

While we ordinarily concern ourselves with how to gain the pleasure of our Lord, for the Messenger of Allah, it is Allah Himself who is concerned with the satisfaction of the Messenger.

There lies a subtle point in this verse, illustrated thus: when we hire a laborer and agree upon a wage of two hundred rupees, and he completes the work, we give him his payment. Yet if he looks upon us with discontent, suggesting his dissatisfaction with the sum and his desire for more, we bestow additional money from our own abundance as a gift, so that he becomes content. Once we see his satisfaction, we cease to call upon him and give no more. That is to say, his contentment and satisfaction arrest the flow of our giving.

Similarly, a nuance of this verse is that where the Messenger of Allah is satisfied, the bestowing of Allah's blessing ceases. In other words, the Messenger shall never be satisfied, and therefore the process of Allah's giving shall never cease.[^cite-52] This is why Allah says in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 33:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ وَمَلَٓئِكَتَهُۥ يُصَلُّونَ عَلَى ٱلنَّبِىِّۚ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ صَلُّوا۟ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا۟ تَسْلِيمًا ‎⟫
> *Allah and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet. O you who believe, send blessings and peace upon him.*

This is the nexus in this chain: the blessing upon him shall never cease. Allah declares, "We have not ceased to bestow blessing upon him, nor shall the angels cease to send blessing upon him, nor should you cease to send blessing upon him."

*(From the Tuhfat al-Ṣāʾifī—Letters of al-Muʿjam al-Nawādir of Sayyidunā Ḥasan Khān ibn ʿAbd al-Malik)*

## No Blame Upon the Believers Who Do Righteous Deeds

Allah states in the Holy Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 5:93⟫
> *There is no blame upon those who believe and do righteous deeds for what they have eaten, provided they fear Allah and believe and do righteous deeds, then fear Allah and believe, then fear Allah and do good.*

It is narrated from the Commander of the Faithful (may Allah be pleased with him) that when Allah forbade the drinking of wine, the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings be upon him) condemned the drinker in explicit detail—together with the seller, the buyer, the bearer, the one who guides to the tavern, and the drinker himself. At that time, there were many who had become Muslim yet still drank wine and did not consider it unlawful. They passed away before the prohibition was made explicit. When wine became forbidden, certain Companions of the Prophet came to him and asked, "O Messenger of Allah, we have heard what you said regarding the impurity of wine. But tell us: what will become of our brothers who drank wine and drank much, and they died in that state, before um al-Khabīth was made forbidden?"

This verse was revealed in answer to the Companions' question. Thus did Allah Most High forgive those who drank um al-Khabīth (the vile drink) but were believers and righteous-doers: they did not commit mischief or corruption in religion; they were true lovers of the Prophet, and their drinking stemmed only from the fact that it was their cherished drink before its prohibition was made clear. Allah accepted their faith, their piety, and their righteousness, and He gave them the good news that "Allah loves those who do good."

In conclusion, we pray that Allah Almighty may save us from error and slip, and grant us and all believers success in word and deed in following the Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him).

Praise be to Allah, who has opened for us the paths of guidance through following the Imams and has protected us by their blessing from wandering and going astray. May Allah Most High send blessings and prayers upon Muhammad (peace be upon him), adorned in the finest raiment and mantle, blessed with revelation and supported by the angels, and upon his successor, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (a.s.), who lay upon the bed of the Prophet on the night of the Hijra, thereby saving the Prophet's life and sacrificing his own person. He was the first in obedience to his Lord, and none could match his devotion. And may peace be upon the pure Imams whose lovers have become righteous in this world and the Hereafter, whilst their enemies have become wretched. And may peace be upon our Master, the Imam al-Ṭayyib Abū al-Qāsim, the Commander of the Believers, who brings to completion the third cycle of the Imams. May Allah Most High send blessings and peace upon all of them until the Day of Judgment. Allah suffices us as the Best Protector, the Best Master, and the Best Helper.

## The Hereafter Is Better for You Than the First Life

Allah says (in the meaning of interpretation):

> ⟪quran 93:4⟫
> *And the Last is better for you than the First.*

That which is to come exceeds that which has come. In one sense, the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings be upon him) is reassured by this verse: those who have come to you in the past—ʿĀʾisha and Ḥafṣa—do not trouble yourself concerning them, for those who will come to you—Umm Salamah and others—are better than they and shall serve you with greater devotion and bring you peace of heart.

In a higher sense, these lines indicate a transformation in the Master of the Universe. After two cycles, you shall emerge as the Qāʾim. Further, it is indicated that the later Aʿyān (essential realities) shall surpass in rank the earlier Aʿyān, or that the Mīm of the Hereafter—which shall manifest before the establishment of al-Mahdī—shall exceed the Mīm of the First Life, whereby the seven Aqlām (Pens) shall come under the domain of His obedience.

The series of Imams who came before the Prophet (s) was called the Fathers (al-Ābāʾ), and the series of Imams after him until the Day of Judgment was called the Sons (al-Abnāʾ). Therefore, the meaning of "the Last is better for you than the First" is that the Imams of the Qāʾim's period surpass the Imams of the prophetic period.

In yet another sense, the final seventy (years) of this age surpasses the six seventy-year periods that preceded it. This is because the glory of the Qāʾim, who emerges at the end of this seventh cycle, shall integrate all the forms of unveiling and the cycles that came before, which the Qur'an designates as the *Mīqāt* (the appointed time).[^cite-53]

And who is called *Malik Yawm al-Dīn* (Master of the Day of Judgment) in Sūrat al-Fātiḥa. If there be sovereignty in the world, it is a distant religion. That is, after this seventh cycle, the age of revelation that shall commence will be owned by the Qāʾim, the Mention Among the Peacemakers! Your dominion shall continue through your offspring.

*(From the Lessons of the Prophet—Sayyidnā Ḥusayn ibn Jadīn)*

*(From the Lessons in the City of Allah—Mullā Ghulām Ḥusayn)*

## Piety Is Not That You Enter Houses from the Rear

Allah says (the interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 2:189⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَسْـَٔلُونَكَ عَنِ ٱلْأَهِلَّةِ ۖ قُلْ هِىَ مَوَقِيتُ لِلنَّاسِ وَٱلْحَجِّ ۗ وَلَيْسَ ٱلْبِرُّ بِأَن تَأْتُوا۟ ٱلْبُيُوتَ مِن ظُهُورِهَا وَلَكِنَّ ٱلْبِرَّ مَنِ ٱتَّقَىٰ ۗ وَأْتُوا۟ ٱلْبُيُوتَ مِنْ أَبْوَبِهَا ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *They ask you about the crescent moons. Say, "These are signs of time for mankind and for the Hajj. And piety is not that you enter houses from behind them, but piety is he who fears Allah, and that you enter houses by their doors, and fear Allah, so that you may prosper."*

It is common knowledge that both the learned and the ignorant enter their homes through their doors. It seems strange, therefore, that the Qur'an—the Book of Guidance—should exhort such a commonplace matter, which appears not only evident but necessary. This apparent superficiality indicates that "house" and "door" here do not refer to structures of brick and lime, but rather speak, in a spiritual sense, to certain exalted individuals—those who are the living houses and gates of Allah. The Prophet declared:

> ⟪ar-quote:أنا مدينة العلم وعلي بابها. فمن أراد العلم فليأتِ الباب⟫
> *"I am the City of Knowledge, and ʿAlī is its Gate. Whosoever seeks knowledge, let him come to the Gate."*

The student must approach through the door. It became plain that there existed but one gate—the Gate of ʿAlī—through which one might enter the City of Knowledge, corresponding to that mentioned by Moses:

> ⟪quran 2:58⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قُلْنَا ٱدْخُلُوا۟ هَذِهِ ٱلْقَرْيَةَ فَكُلُوا۟ مِنْهَا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمْ رَغَدًۭا وَٱدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْبَابَ سُجَّدًۭا وَقُولُوا۟ حِطَّةٌۭ نَّغْفِرْ لَكُمْ خَطَيَكُمْ ۚ وَسَنَزِيدُ ٱلْمُحْسِنِينَ ‎⟫
> *And when We said, "Enter this city and eat freely therein wherever you will, and enter the gate in prostration, saying 'Forgive us,' and We shall forgive you your sins and shall increase the reward of those who do good."*

Just as the people of Moses refused to enter the gate—they rejected his successor Aaron—and changed the word ⟪ar:حطة⟫ ('forgiveness') to ⟪ar:حنطة⟫ (grain), so were they treated with humiliation.

Allah says in this verse that the righteous and the God-fearing are those who enter the house through the door, not by tearing through the wall.[^cite-54] That is, the one who acknowledges the words ⟪ar:علي ولي الله⟫ (*ʿAlī is the Wilāya of Allah*) speaks truth! The gate of the City of Knowledge is ⟪ar:علي⟫ (*ʿAlī*). If this be not the meaning of the verse, then what virtue or piety could arise from the mere act of entering a house through a door?

Furthermore, the word ⟪ar:بر⟫ (*birr*, righteousness) at the beginning of this verse and the word ⟪ar:تقوى⟫ (*taqwā*, piety) in the middle are terms that have nothing to do with a person entering an outward house of brick and mortar. The greatest righteousness, ⟪ar:بر الوالدين⟫ (*birr al-wālidayn*, kindness to parents), refers to showing kindness to one's parents—and the parents in the faith are Muḥammad and ʿAlī, as the Prophet declared:

> ⟪ar-quote:أنا وأنت يا علي أبوا المؤمنين⟫
> *"You and I, O ʿAlī, are the fathers of the believers."*

The Imam said, "Piety (*taqwā*) is my religion and the religion of my father." He who lacks this piety is ungodly! From this interpretation of the verse, it is clear that true righteousness and piety lie in entering the house of the Sharīʿa of Muḥammad by embracing the *wilāya* (guardianship) of ʿAlī. It is not the righteousness of one who obeys the rulings of the Sharīʿa while rejecting ʿAlī or recognizing the fourth Caliph. The example of such a person is that of one who breaks through a wall instead of using the door.

## The Creation in Six Days and Allah's Sitting Upon the Throne

Allah declares in His Clear Book: "He created the universe in six days, then sat upon His Throne."[^cite-55] What we ordinarily reckon as a "day" is the Earth's complete orbit around the Sun. Yet the question arises: when Allah was creating the universe, neither the sun nor the earth existed. What, then, is meant by Allah's "Day"? And why should He rest upon the Throne on the seventh day, when He Himself states in His Book:

> ⟪quran 50:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَفَعَيِينَا بِٱلْخَلْقِ ٱلْأَوَّلِ ۚ بَلْ هُمْ فِى لَبْسٍۢ مِّنْ خَلْقٍۢ جَدِيدٍۢ ‎⟫
> *Were We wearied with the first creation? Nay, but they are in doubt about the recreation.*

And in the *Āyat al-Kursī* (the Throne Verse), He says:

> ⟪quran 2:255⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْحَىُّ ٱلْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُۥ سِنَةٌۭ وَلَا نَوْمٌۭ ۚ لَّهُۥ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ مَن ذَا ٱلَّذِى يَشْفَعُ عِندَهُۥٓ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِۦ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَىْءٍۢ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِۦٓ إِلَّا بِمَا شَآءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَـُٔودُهُۥ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ ٱلْعَلِىُّ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah: there is no god but He, the Ever-Living, the Self-Subsisting. Neither slumber seizes Him nor sleep; to Him belongs all that is in the heavens and all that is on the earth. Who is there that can intercede with Him save by His permission? He knows what lies before them and what shall come after them, and they comprehend not anything of His knowledge save such as He wills. His Throne encompasses the heavens and the earth, and the maintaining of them burdens Him not. He is the All-High, the All-Great.*

### The Six Words and the Seven Dimensions

The Ḥakīm (the Wise One) explains: "The six days of Allah are the six words (divine utterances) He sent forth. And they are the six dimensions, for man is surrounded by six dimensions, and the seventh dimension is man himself, who is the quintessence of all—for were he not there, then whose dimensions would exist?"

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Above | Below | Right | Left | Forward | Backward | Man |

Therefore, because of the six directions surrounding the human body, Allah sent forth six messengers to mankind. Thus, in the pattern of practical spirituality (*ʿilm al-rūḥāniyyāt al-ʿamalī*): Adam came from above the human dimension; Noah from the left; Abraham from behind; Moses from below (opposing Adam); Jesus from the right (opposing Noah); and Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā from the front (opposing Abraham).[^cite-56]

All these prophets—these six messengers—came from the six aspects of humanity and conveyed to them deeds and promised them the reward of those deeds. When six prophets came from six quarters of the world and proclaimed their message, it demonstrated that no other prophet shall come after the Messenger of Allah. Should anyone arrive hereafter, it would be He who recompenses them and bestows reward.

### The Relationship of the Six to the Seventh

Man is likewise surrounded by six dimensions, and he himself is the seventh among them. The six dimensions signify man's existence and acknowledge that humanity exists—which is why we, too, exist. In the same manner, Allah sent forth six Speakers (al-Nāṭiqīn) and their guardians to guide the people, and these six point to the existence and manifestation of al-Qāʾim. They testify that were the appearance of al-Qāʾim not the supreme purpose, they would not have appeared in this world.

When these words of Allah (Mighty and Glorious) came, they conveyed different teachings, the inward sense of which made each the individual embodiment of the Sharīʿa, just as a walnut differs in form from an apple. For walnuts possess meanings not found in apples, yet both are fruits. When Allah sends forth a messenger to the people, He sends with each an interpreter (*muʾayyad*) who explains the meaning of his outward actions, so that it cannot be said, "We were not made aware of the meaning."

### The Nāṭiq, the Asās, and the Wasī

These were the Speakers (al-Nāṭiqīn) or Prophets—those who brought the outward law—and their interpreters (*muʾayyads*), who unveiled the meanings. Thus: Hazrat Adam (a.s.) was succeeded by Mawlānā Sheesh; Hazrat Noah by Mawlānā Sām; Mawlānā Hārūn was the successor of Hazrat Mūsā; Mawlānā Shimʿūn was the successor of Hazrat ʿIsā; and Mawlānā ʿAlī al-Murtaḍā was the successor of Hazrat Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (peace be upon them all).

**A Note on Nomenclature**: During the lifetime of the Nāṭiq (the Speaker), his successor is called the Asās (the Foundation). After the Nāṭiq's death, he is called the Wasī (the Successor). Mawlānā ʿAlī served as the Foundation during the lifetime of the Messenger of Allah; after the Prophet's death, he became known as the Successor.

### The Seven Days of the Week

It is narrated that the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings be upon him) began the creation of the universe on Sunday, completed it on Friday, and rested on Saturday. Additionally, Allah Most High declares in the Holy Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 3:184⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِن كَذَّبُوكَ فَقَدْ كُذِّبَ رُسُلٌۭ مِّن قَبْلِكَ جَآءُو بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ وَٱلزُّبُرِ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلْمُنِيرِ ‎⟫
> *If they deny you, so were messengers before you denied, who brought clear proofs and scriptures and the enlightening book.*

In Arabic, "day" (*yawm*) denotes both day and night. Only the daytime itself is called *nahār*, and only the night is called *layl*. When Allah uses the word "day" in this verse, He means the full cycle of day and night.

Just as there are seven days in a week, with six days between each pair of weeks, so too these six Speakers came within the interval of two cycles of Qa'im—each set of six days between two weeks:

| Saturday | Sunday | Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday | Saturday |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Qāʾim al-Fātar | Adam | Noah | Ibrāhīm | Mūsā | ʿIsā | Muḥammad | Qāʾim al-Sāṭir |
| | Sheesh | Sām | Ishmāʿīl | Hārūn | Shimʿūn | ʿAlī | al-Mahdī |

### The Succession Within Each Cycle

Likewise, in every age, there are stages between two prophets wherein those who interpret the esoteric meaning explain the inner significance and outward actions:

| Saturday | Sunday | Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday | Saturday | Sunday |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Speaker | Completion | Successor | Martyrdom | Exchange | Guide | Paradise | Ascetic | Speaker |

This is because the Law of Adam was advanced by the Law of Moses; the Law of Noah by the Law of Jesus; and the Law of Abraham by the Law of Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā. For this reason, peace is sent in the prayer upon Muḥammad (peace be upon him) and upon the family of Muḥammad in the same manner that it is sent upon Ibrāhīm and the family of Ibrāhīm.

By mentioning the names of both in the salutation (*al-Durūd*), the believer supplicates: "O Allah, join the shrine of Ibrāhīm and the family of Ibrāhīm in the same way that You have joined the shrine of Muḥammad and the family of Muḥammad."

*(The Race is a Start and a Cycle—Ḥazrat Ẓīʾ ʿAlī Ẓīʾī)*
