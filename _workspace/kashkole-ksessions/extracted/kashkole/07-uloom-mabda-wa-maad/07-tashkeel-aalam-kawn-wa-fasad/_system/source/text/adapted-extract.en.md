## The Three Intentions of al-ʿĀshir

In shaping the physical world, al-ʿĀshir al-Mudabbir al-Ḥakīm held three principal intentions:

| 1 | Creating Man in a Thousand Ways |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | To imprison the disobedient faction within the constraints of length, width, and depth |
| 3 | To establish the physical universe through cosmic motion |

Although the creation of man constituted the supreme purpose and first thought of al-ʿĀshir, in the order of execution he brought man into being last. This accords with the principle: ⟪ar-quote:اَوَّلُ الفِکَر وَ آخِرُ العَمَل⟫ (the first in thought, the last in action).[^cite-1] When a person conceives of achieving a goal, he first arranges and prepares all prerequisites that must precede the goal's fulfillment, and only then performs the final act that brings it to completion. Thus, although man's creation was al-ʿĀshir's supreme aim, he first brought the world of physicality and corporeality into being so that time and space might be arranged for humanity. Thereafter he established ⟪ar:موالید ثلاثۃ⟫ (the three kingdoms of birth) so that man's sustenance might be secured. Only when all necessities for man's existence were fulfilled did he bring man forth.

Similarly, when an imām or dāʿī (Summoner) assumes his station, his first concern is to prepare his successor for the caliphate; yet in practice, he accomplishes this final task at the end.

## The Creation of the Universe in Six Motions

Al-ʿĀshir executed a total of six motions (ḥarakat) in the formation of the physical world, composed of six ⟪ar:مُحَرِّک⟫ (movers). The Qurʾān testifies to this:

> ⟪quran 50:38⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَا فِى سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍۢ وَمَا مَسَّنَا مِن لُّغُوبٍۢ ‎⟫
> *And indeed We created the heavens and the earth and all between them in six Days, and nothing of fatigue touched Us.*
> — **Qurʾān 50:38**

The six motions may be enumerated as follows:

| First Motion | Removal of luminosity from the disobedient faction and creation within them of the three dimensions—length, width, and depth—of illusion |
| --- | --- |
| Second Motion | Tajādhub al-Qutbayn (Attraction of the Two Poles): stabilization through four celestial forces |
| Third Motion | The eruption of the cosmic vessel; transformation of spirituality into physicality |
| Fourth Motion | Generation of the celestial spheres (kawākib) and the terrestrial realm (saqrāʾ) |
| Fifth Motion | Formation of the celestial spheres through emanating rays; establishment of the four lower elemental kingdoms |
| Sixth Motion | Initiation of astronomical cycles beginning from Saturn |

Following these six motions, the physical world acquired the capacity to sustain itself perpetually. Al-ʿĀshir did not execute a seventh motion; rather, after completing the six, he established himself upon his ⟪ar:عرش⟫ (throne) as the ⟪ar:مُدبر⟫ (Divine Administrator), and the appointed celestial beings beneath him set the machinery of creation in motion.[^cite-2] This is why Allāh says:

> ⟪quran 25:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَا فِى سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍۢ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَوَىٰ عَلَى ٱلْعَرْشِ ۚ ٱلرَّحْمَنُ فَسْـَٔلْ بِهِۦ خَبِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *He who created the heavens and the earth and all that is between them in six Days, then established Himself upon the Throne. The Most Merciful—ask Him! He is the All-Knowing.*
> — **Qurʾān 25:59**

The duration of each cosmic "day" (yawm) is not to be measured by terrestrial reckoning. Allāh has declared:

> ⟪quran 22:47⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَيَسْتَعْجِلُونَكَ بِٱلْعَذَابِ وَلَن يُخْلِفَ ٱللَّهُ وَعْدَهُۥ ۚ وَإِنَّ يَوْمًا عِندَ رَبِّكَ كَأَلْفِ سَنَةٍۢ مِّمَّا تَعُدُّونَ ‎⟫
> *And a Day with your Lord is as a thousand years of what you reckon.*
> — **Qurʾān 22:47**

Thus, across the span of six thousand terrestrial years, the planets—from Saturn to Mercury—completed the entire configuration of the universe.

### The Distinction Between Command and Creation

The Creator pronounced it (⟪ar:أمر⟫) and al-ʿĀshir brought it into being (⟪ar:خلق⟫). The Divine Command operates in an instant—⟪ar:کن فیکون⟫ (Be! and it is)—whereas the Creator of the physical universe labored across ⟪ar:فی ستۃ ایام⟫ (six days), demonstrating the immense creative effort required to establish material existence.[^cite-3]

---

**Reflection:** It is worthy of profound contemplation that al-ʿĀshir, who brought this vast universe into being, was once a human being like ourselves. This fact illuminates what extraordinary capacities we have lost through our forgetfulness.

## The First Motion: Length, Width, and Depth

Al-ʿĀshir resolved to fashion the physical world from the disobedient faction and to confine them within it. Yet in their spiritual state, their imprisonment was impossible; he was therefore obliged to transmute them into corporeality first. The analogy is of a candle burning upon a tablet: one cannot remove the candle without its tablet.[^cite-4] In executing this transmutation, his first intention was to generate within these beings the three dimensions of illusion—length, width, and depth—which are the hallmarks of physicality. The Qurʾān illuminates this decree:

> ⟪quran 77:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱنطَلِقُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ ظِلٍّۢ ذِى ثَلْثِ شُعَبٍۢ ‎⟫
> *Depart you to a shadow of three columns.*
> — **Qurʾān 77:30**

In implementing al-ʿĀshir's command (⟪ar:اھبطو⟫, "descend"), he first began to diminish the luminosity of the disobedient faction, whereupon they grew dense and terror-stricken.

### First Movement: The Generation of Length

As the brightness of these beings faded, panic seized them and they began to affect one another—colliding and rubbing together as clouds chafe against one another. To escape this state of dread and confusion, each being moved away from itself toward the self of another, and this movement created length. This length was not yet physical, but rather existed in the "wild" or potential state (⟪ar:طول بالقوۃ⟫) from which physical length would eventually be born. This constituted the first motion. From this longitudinal motion, heat was generated, and two celestial constellations—Aries and the second Aries—were formed. As heat declined toward its contrary extreme, coldness emerged, and two further constellations—Pisces and Libra—came into being with this coldness. All these creations unfolded in the potential realm:

| Heat (ḥarāra) | Cold (burūda) |
| --- | --- |
| Pregnancy and gestation | Fixity and solidity |

### Second Movement: The Generation of Width

After the first motion, terror intensified among them. To escape this condition, they executed a second motion in which they fled from one another, generating a whirling motion. Alongside this, moisture (ruṭūba) and dryness (yubūsa) were produced. Two celestial authorities—Capricorn and Aquarius—were formed with the moisture, while with dryness came Sagittarius and Cancer.

| Moisture (Ruṭūba) | Dryness (Yubūsa) |
| --- | --- |
| Capricorn and Aquarius | Sagittarius and Cancer |

### Third Movement: The Generation of Depth

When length and width had been created, the beings perceived that they had grown increasingly imprisoned within matter. To escape the horror of these two dimensions, they enacted a third motion, generating the third dimension—depth (ʿumq). This motion produced no new constellation or temperament (mizāj).

Before the advent of length, width, and depth, all beings existed as distinct individuals, each maintaining its own reality. Yet when overwhelmed by the ⟪ar:ثلاثہ ابعاد⟫ (three dimensions), they lost their individuality entirely. The analogy is instructive: if three distinct colors are placed separately, each remains clearly visible; but when mixed and blended, their individual natures dissolve and they fuse into a single different hue. So too did these beings, upon being seized by length, width, and depth, commingle with one another until their individuality vanished and they coalesced into a unified condition—the ⟪ar:ھیولی⟫ (hylē, primordial matter) in its most potential state.

After these three motions, the beings did not execute a fourth; this was precisely what they desired. They lament that had they possessed patience and refrained from motion, they would not have descended into the physical world. Their impatience precipitated their division into hylē and form (ṣūra).

## Primordial Matter and Secondary Matter: Hylē I and Hylē II

When the three motions were completed, all beings took on the nature of ⟪ar:ھیولی⟫ (hylē, primordial matter) and ⟪ar:صورت⟫ (ṣūra, form), and owing to the ⟪ar:ثقل⟫ (weight) of the hylē they began to densify. This is why all things in the present age are encompassed within these three dimensions.

Within this collective mass, those individuals who had responded receptively to al-ʿĀshir's daʿwa (the summons / the invitation) rose to the higher ranks and became ⟪ar:صورت⟫ (form)—the actuating principle. Those less receptive became the ⟪ar:ھیولی⟫ (hylē)—the receptive principle. Form is always more potent and luminous and more active; hylē is more transparent and passive. Through Divine solicitude (⟪ar:عنایت الٰہیہ⟫), al-ʿĀshir determined to fashion objects from hylē and form. Those superior in capacity and endowed with the status of masculine (the influencers) were made into the celestial spheres (⟪ar:افلاک⟫, aflāk); those of feminine status (the effect-receivers) were made into the elemental kingdoms (⟪ar:امہات⟫, ummahāt).

| Masculine (Influencers) | Feminine (Effect-Receivers) |
| --- | --- |
| Seven Planets and the Zodiacal Sphere | The Four Elemental Kingdoms |

The term ⟪ar:ھیولی⟫ (hylē) is composed of two words: ⟪ar:ھیاوا + اولی⟫ (hiyāwāʾ + ūlā), meaning "the first preparation" or "primal receptivity." Preparation denotes capacity and readiness to receive influence. Hylē, then, is a substance capable of accepting impressions and effects. In the condition of Hylē, all beings—whether masculine or feminine—possessed the capacity to receive influence and thereby came to consciousness of causality, so that they might be extracted from the spiritual world.

### Hylē I (Primordial Matter)

When light was stripped from these beings, they fell into panic and enacted three motions that generated the three delusory dimensions of length, width, and depth. All beings commingled with one another, their individuality vanishing; they coalesced into one undifferentiated mass. This condition is termed ⟪ar:ھیولی أولی⟫ (Hylē I).

### Hylē II (Secondary Matter)

Even in the state of Hylē I, these beings received invitations—both toward mercy and toward rebellion. Both invitations were being transmitted; all beings were hearing them and responding to their effects. Because of their responsiveness, they became divided into different factions according to their stance toward al-ʿĀshir. Some remained adamant in disobedience and arrogant; others repented of their error and stood ready to obey al-ʿĀshir; yet others wavered in doubt, uncertain whether to repent or persist in defiance.[^cite-5] Thus they became mentally divided into three sects.

The greater the density of intention crystallizing within them, the more profoundly length, width, and depth ⟪ar:تجسم⟫ (took material form) upon them, leading to the emergence of another form of ⟪ar:ھیولی⟫ and ⟪ar:صورت⟫. In the terminology of the daʿwa, the hylē is called ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ (the receptive principle, lit. "the naming"), and the form is called ⟪ar:حسیۃ⟫ (the sensible principle). The hylē that then ⟪ar:صورت⟫ assumed dominion was designated ⟪ar:ھیولی ثانی⟫ (Hylē II), and it received the command ⟪ar:اھبطو⟫ (descend).

By this point three sects had been established in their intentions: the repentant (⟪ar:نادمین⟫), the vacillating (⟪ar:شاکین⟫), and the obdurate (⟪ar:مصرین⟫). Some individuals were shifting between sects by accepting the influence of both the merciful call (⟪ar:رحمانی دعوت⟫) and the call of rebellion. Some who initially insisted on disobedience came to accept the merciful invitation and joined the ⟪ar:نادمین⟫ (repentant). Others who first showed signs of repentance succumbed to the call of rebellion (⟪ar:ابلیسی دعوت⟫) and became ⟪ar:مصرین⟫ (obdurate). This fluidity was occurring in the spiritual realm. Yet as soon as the command ⟪ar:اھبطو⟫ (descend) was executed, al-ʿĀshir effected a freezing of all intentions. Never again could any being shift from one sect to another. This phenomenon is called ⟪ar:انجماد مآء⟫ (the freezing of waters). When the command of descent was issued, beings fell downward from the subtle realm due to their density, for every dense thing falls downward by the law of gravity, while every subtle thing rises upward by its own nature.[^cite-6]

| Hylē I | The state of length, width, and depth in which all beings became confused and lost their individuality |
| --- | --- |
| Hylē II | The post-descent state in which all intentions were frozen, in which the repentant, the vacillating, and the obdurate were separated from one another according to their respective intentions |

> — *Correlative observations*

The first faction, realizing their error, repented and sought forgiveness, acknowledging the preeminence of the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal). Through this repentance, they became distinguished from the other two sects. Though they could not be prevented from descending, they were halted at the level corresponding to their repentance. Yet because this repentance occurred after the freezing had taken effect, they were obliged to join the physical world. This sect is known as ⟪ar:نادمین ومستغفرین⟫ (the Repentant and the Seeking Forgiveness).

The second faction, remaining in ⟪ar:شاک و متحیر⟫ (vacillation and bewilderment), could not determine whether to repent or persist in error. They are called ⟪ar:شاک و متحیر⟫ (the Vacillating and Bewildered).

The third faction, insisting on denial and rebellion against the Creator, are termed ⟪ar:مصرین و مستکبرین⟫ (the Obdurate and the Arrogant).

| Repentant and Seeking Forgiveness | Belief in the Creator; acknowledgment of the Precedence of the First Intellect |
| --- | --- |
| Vacillating and Bewildered | Belief in the Creator but considering all beings equal in primacy |
| Obdurate and Arrogant | Deniers of the Creator and opponents of all revealed truth |

When the ⟪ar:ثلاثہ ابعاد(طول، عرض، عمق)⟫ (three dimensions: length, width, depth) converged and ⟪ar:حیت ہبوت⟫ (the descent took effect), the physical world was formed according to the intentions and natures of each faction and their hylē. The purest portion—drawn from the first sect—was exalted into the heavens, a luminous realm whose radiance reflects the beauty and righteousness of these beings. The celestial sphere possesses minimal density relative to lower realms and was therefore stationed at the apex. ⟪ar:بروج⟫ The zodiacal sphere, being denser, occupies a lower position; below it ⟪ar:فلک زحل، فلک مشتری،⟫ the seven heavens descended in descending order of subtlety. The degrees of density in these celestial spheres mirror the varying density-grades of bodily organs, each organ having been formed according to the intention (and density-level) of those beings from which it arose. ⟪ar:شاک و متحر⟫ From the second sect—the Vacillating—the four elemental kingdoms (fire, air, water, earth) were fashioned, whose essential nature is restlessness and movement. The instability of the elements springs from the uncertainty of those who comprise them. ⟪ar:مصر و مستکبرین⟫ Due to the rigidity and obstinacy of the third sect, they were constituted as the frozen crust (saqrāʾ) ⟪ar:(crust)⟫ and the terrestrial realm.

### Establishment of Intentions in the Subtle World (ʿĀlam al-Niyyāt)

Because all these beings—possessing different intentions and natures—were intermixed, they began to develop relationships and intentions with one another, and through these interactions they created circumstances. All relationships, friendships, enmities, bonds, forms, garments, and neighborhoods were established in this realm through three mechanisms:

| Istiḥsān | Regarding an evil thing as good |
| --- | --- |
| Milān | Inclining toward and assisting in evil |
| Qiṣāṣ | Enacting the evil deed oneself |

Beyond merely creating relationships and enmities, the intentions themselves gave rise to our outward forms, stature, complexion, and all other characteristics within ⟪ar:عالم نیات⟫ (the World of Intentions). Whatever one desires in the eternal realm, one receives in this world. To offer clarification by example: those whom we regarded in eternity with love alone became our brothers or sisters in this world. One we observed with mingled love and hatred became our spouse. Those seen through eyes of plague became our parents. Thus some faces inspire love in our hearts, and others hatred, even though we have never met them—their appearance resonates with intentions formed long before.

## The Prayer of the Descending Repentant

When ⟪ar:أنجماد مآء⟫ the freezing of waters occurred and Hylē was commanded to descend, al-ʿĀshir and ⟪ar:اہل کلمم⟫ those of his word (the celestial ministers) gazed upon the repentant faction with a look of longing and mercy, consoling them. The Qurʾān preserves the divine pledge:

> ⟪quran 2:38⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ مِنْهَا جَمِيعًۭا ۖ فَإِمَّا يَأْتِيَنَّكُم مِّنِّى هُدًۭى فَمَن تَبِعَ هُدَاىَ فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎⟫
> *We said: "Descend, all of you from here. Yet if there comes to you guidance from Me, those who follow My guidance shall have no fear, nor shall they grieve."*
> — **Qurʾān 2:38**

> ⟪quran 19:72⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ نُنَجِّى ٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّقَوا۟ وَّنَذَرُ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ فِيهَا جِثِيًّۭا ‎⟫
> *Then We shall deliver those who feared, leaving the evildoers therein on their knees.*
> — **Qurʾān 19:72**

Al-ʿĀshir assured them: "Though you must descend into this physicality as consequence of your error, take solace! Since you have held fast to us, we shall come into this world as Muḥammad and ʿAlī and shall guide you from this physical world toward peace and salvation." As they descended, the repentant faction offered praise to Allāh, which the Qurʾān has preserved in immortal words:

> ⟪quran 10:10⟫
> ⟪ar:‏دَعْوَىٰهُمْ فِيهَا سُبْحَنَكَ ٱللَّهُمَّ وَتَحِيَّتُهُمْ فِيهَا سَلَمٌۭ ۚ وَآخِرُ دَعْوَىٰهُمْ أَنِ ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَلَمِينَ ‎⟫
> *Their invocation therein is "Glory be to You, O Allāh!" and their greeting therein is "Peace!" and the close of their invocation is "All praise belongs to Allāh, Lord of the Worlds."*
> — **Qurʾān 10:10**

In their descent, the repentant sect praised al-ʿĀshir, offering no complaint that he had corrupted them or that without his action they would not have fallen. Instead they praised him—and it was precisely because of this praise that al-ʿĀshir undertook to be the first to draw them back from the physical world. For this reason this prayer is recited at the conclusion of every gathering, just as it was recited when al-ʿĀshir departed the assembly.

The tradition records that when the disobedient faction transgressed against al-ʿĀshir and subsequently confessed their error and sought his forgiveness, both moments were marked by the letter *lā* (negation). When they disobeyed, they declared: ⟪ar-quote:لاَ نُسمِعُ وَلاَ نُطِیعُ⟫ ("We do not listen to you, nor do we obey you"). When they acknowledged their error, they proclaimed: ⟪ar-quote:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ ("There is no god save al-ʿĀshir"). Thus the entire spiritual map has been established upon the foundation of the Word of Sincerity (Kalimah).

## The Second Motion: The Attraction of the Two Poles (Tajādhub al-Qutbayn)

The terminology of celestial mechanics requires clarification:

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Flak (Sphere) | Orbit |
| Kokab (Star) | Planet |
| Stars | Fixed stars |
| Rotation | Spinning in place |
| Durān | Orbiting |

The zodiacal sphere revolves (orbits) only; the seven planets both rotate and revolve; the fixed stars only rotate in place.

| The Zodiacal Sphere | The Seven Planets | The Fixed Stars |
| --- | --- |
| Orbit only | Rotate + Orbit | Rotate only |

In the second motion, the most repentant essence rose upward—this is called ⟪ar:اول النوادم⟫ (the First of the Repentant). This occurred because subtle substance always ascends. Al-ʿĀshir then set this leaven—the First of the Repentant—into circulation, and through this action four celestial powers came into being:

| Tenth (ʿāshir) | West (ghārib) | Fourth (rābiʿ) | East (ṭāliʿ) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| North | West | South | East |

These are like four spiritual anchors that held the entire cosmic mass in place and prevented further descent. It is due to these four forces that the whole world remains fixed in its position. All four dwell within the ⟪ar:فلک محیط⟫ (the Encompassing Sphere) and upon the border of the zodiac. The first, the ⟪ar:طالع⟫ (Ascendant), stands above, at the eastern horizon of the zodiac. The second, the ⟪ar:رابع⟫ (Fourth), lies below the earth—which is why the earth only rotates in place and cannot orbit. Since the entire universe is held stationary by these four forces, its center—the earth—remains motionless. All other planets ⟪ar:(سیارات السابتۃ)⟫ except earth orbit their spheres. The third force, the ⟪ar:عاشر⟫ (Tenth), stands in the east. The fourth, the ⟪ar:غارب⟫ (West), stands in the west. Two further dimensions complete the scheme: the ⟪ar:قطب شمالی⟫ (North Pole) and ⟪ar:قطب جنوبی⟫ (South Pole), representing the vertical axis of up and down. Thus these four forces complete the six dimensional axes: up, down, right, left, forward, back.[^cite-7] For this reason, the entire physical world and all within it is encompassed in six dimensions.

## The Governors of the Spheres and the Zodiac

These four forces became magnets of subtlety, drawing subtle essence upward from the hylē. Through their attraction, the most refined beings—the ⟪ar:نادمین و مستغفرین⟫ (Repentant and Seeking Forgiveness)—gathered at the periphery; the ⟪ar:شاک و متحیر⟫ (Vacillating and Bewildered) assembled at the middle; and the ⟪ar:مصر و مستکبرین⟫ (Obdurate and Arrogant) crystallized at the interior. The highest among the Repentant gathered at the circumference of the zodiacal sphere. These twelve beings are termed the Governors of the Zodiac (⟪ar:مدبری البروج⟫). They are also called ⟪ar:عقول عالم طبعیت⟫ (the Intellects of the Physical World).

---

**Learned Observation:** The celestial sphere of environment is the most subtle of all heavens and contains no visible stars or planets. The celestial constellations and zodiacal signs are in truth the governors of the heavens, constantly working to extract subtle essences and raise densities. Among the wise ones whom al-ʿĀshir commissioned as counselors, two bear special names: one is called ⟪ar:عین⟫ (ʿayn, the Supreme), and his deputy is called ⟪ar:میم⟫ (mīm, the Completing).

Through these four forces, the entire universe was drawn into the shape of an egg. ## The Generation of Time and Space

With the eruption of the cosmic vessel, physicality came into being, and with physicality came time and space. The ⟪ar:کواکب سابتۃ⟫ (fixed stars) and ⟪ar:کواکب سیارۃ⟫ (wandering planets) undergo no corruption or alteration; neither do they diminish nor increase in magnitude. Their motion is purely circular, a form that signifies the perfection of celestial movement. The circle is the most perfect form in geometry!

---

**Learned Observation:** The author of the *Epistles of the Brethren of Purity* (⟪ar:صاحب رسائل اخوان الصفا⟫) explains that time is intrinsically connected to space, and the two are mutually dependent. The existence of one is impossible without the other.

---

These seven heavens exert their influence upon the four elemental kingdoms through ⟪ar:موالید ثلاثۃ⟫ (the Three Kingdoms of Coming-to-be), combining them with the ⟪ar:نفوس (حسیۃ، نامیۃ)⟫ (souls—both sensible and receptive) for the purpose of their manifestation, that they might be born into this world. The elemental kingdoms cannot accomplish this independently; they depend upon the heavens, just as a woman depends upon a man to conceive a child.

### Time (al-Zamān)

The author of the *Epistles* teaches that time is a pure and formal power whose effects manifest in the soul through the unceasing passage of day and night. An analogy illuminates this: imagine numbering that commences with one and progresses through continuous addition: one plus one yields two; then one plus two yields three; and so forth. Time's origin is bound to this physical world—as soon as the world was brought forth and physicality descended upon it, time commenced from that very moment. Thus time is not the designation of a fixed span, but the mark of the termination of time itself (⟪ar:یوم القیامۃ⟫, the Day of Resurrection).

### Space (al-Makān)

The author of the *Epistles* further teaches that space does not exist outside this world; rather, it is a condition intrinsic to physicality. Where physical bodies exist, space exists. Thus to debate whether a vacuum or a plenum exists beyond the limits of the universe is an idle inquiry. For there is no space outside this world, and where space is absent, neither void nor plenum can obtain. The boundary of any physical object is its space. The Brotherhood of Purity denies the existence of vacuum beyond this world. Empty space is a space containing nothing and left void. Space is an attribute of physical things—it can be encountered only where physical objects exist. Therefore, where physical objects do not exist, space itself cannot exist; and where space does not exist, neither emptiness nor fullness can be found. To the contrary, the Brotherhood affirms that though space may appear empty, it is never truly devoid. Within this world there is no place wherein spirits do not dwell. Elucidating this truth, the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allāh be upon him) declared: ⟪ar-quote:لاَ خَلاَ وَلاَ مَلاَ فِی الھَوَا⟫ ("The air is neither empty nor full").

### The Transition from Spirituality to Physicality

Spirituality is subtle; physicality is dense. All subtle things ascend toward spirituality, and all dense things descend toward physicality—for all things return to their origin. Since all beings of Hylē II belonged to the disobedient faction, their intention was coarse and unrefined. This density was mitigated in the Repentant faction by their remorse. The Vacillating faction was denser than the Repentant ⟪ar:شاک و متحیرین⟫, for their intention was more cumbersome. The Obdurate ⟪ar:مصر و مستکبرین⟫ possessed the greatest density. The density of these three factions was transformed into physicality by the weight of their conditions. Thus in this present physical world, the heavens are the subtlest of all realms, the elemental kingdoms occupy a middle density, and the terrestrial sphere (saqrāʾ) is the most dense.

An instructive example is water vapor, which is subtle; yet when cooling generates weight within it, the vapor becomes liquid water—a denser form. When this water is cooled further, density increases until it freezes into ice, the densest form of the same substance. Coldness, being contrary to oppression, inclines toward tranquility; the more coarse the intention, the more sluggish the descent (⟪ar:ھبوط⟫) and the more scarce good deeds become, and consequently the greater the density. Heat, by contrast, dwells in the realm of subtle intention and inclines toward motion, generating movement toward righteous deeds and creating the subtlety that is the cause of every ascent!

## The Fourth Motion: The Generation of the Stars and the Terrestrial Sphere

Al-ʿĀshir circulated the hylē a fourth time through the sphere of the heavens, an action that caused the heavy elements within the heavens to gather and crystallize.

### The Fixed Stars (Kawākib Thābita) and the Wandering Planets (Kawākib Sāyyāra)

Like the human form, the heavens were gradually differentiated and arranged. From this hylē the seven stars of the seven heavens and the stars of the eighth constellation came forth, and they began to revolve in their respective spheres. The portion of hylē possessing the lowest density (greatest subtlety) formed the ⟪ar:فلک محیط⟫ (Encompassing Sphere)—the most subtle of all heavens.

From the outer surface of the universe to the center of the earth lies the circumference sphere in which the Encompassing Sphere operates. The zodiacal constellation extends from the lower boundary of the circumference to the earth's center. Similarly, the sphere of each heaven extends from its outermost limit to the earth's center. For this reason, on the Day of Judgment, when the heavens are rolled up, they shall descend upon the earth—not ascend. Though the paths and expanse of these heavens are immeasurably vast, they float in the upper regions.

In a single day (twenty-four hours), the Encompassing Sphere completes a full revolution of the entire world through ⟪ar:حرکۃ کلیۃ الہیۃ⟫ (the All-Encompassing Divine Motion) of the wise one. Since the earth is the center of this motion, it completes one rotation in twenty-four hours—for the faster the periphery rotates, the faster its center must turn.

## The Malefic Stars: Saturn and Mars

Saturn and Mars, though composed of the elements that constitute the Repentant, are designated ⟪ar:نحسین⟫ (the Two Malefic Stars). To construct any edifice, one must first dismantle the old structure and level the ground. Since Saturn's assignment is to break down and freeze, it was placed foremost in the celestial order. ## The Fifth Movement: Creation of the Asdāf and the Mothers

When the Kawākib (celestial bodies) and Saqqara were brought into readiness, a subtle smoke, ⟪ar:دُخَّانِ لَطِیف⟫, which had belonged to the faction of Nadmin (the rebellious ones), remained about Saqqara in accordance with the Divine Will. Since these elements were part of Nadmin, they could neither unite with Saqqara nor join the Umma (the cosmic principles). These were the essences which, though they had turned in remorse, were compelled to descend to the earthly realm because of Divine retribution.

Asher (the Originator) then set the heavens in motion again—a fifth movement—which caused all celestial spheres and the planetary bodies, ⟪ar:کواکب سیارۃ⟫, to move in concert. For the first time, all the stars cast their rays toward Saqqara by the force of their movements. Yet Saqqara, which was composed of bone-like substance, ⟪ar:خود مصرین⟫, could not receive the rays of any constellation, for those rays were components of Nadmin. When these rays struck Saqqara and rebounded to their source, they united once more with the rays of their own celestial sphere—their constituent parts. The subtle smoke, ⟪ar:دُخَّانِ لَطِیف⟫, from the solar and lunar portions was absorbed into the lunar rays, and similarly from all the spheres: the rays gathered their constituent elements from the sides of Saqqara, returned to their source, and from this smoke there became formed the asdāf (celestial bodies' dense shells), which are also termed ⟪ar:أجرام الکواکب⟫.[^cite-8]

## Creation of the Four Mothers (*Ummahat*)

Countless millions of years elapsed as these heavens and principles formed themselves. The various acts set in motion by Asher occurred at intervals stretching across different epochs. Some of these intervals were brief; others immeasurably long. After the Originator granted the heavens a total of six movements, the earth acquired the capacity to move of its own accord.

### The Lithosphere

When all the planets were set in motion by Asher's fifth movement, the atmosphere beneath the sky became exceedingly hot and arid owing to the movement of the moon—which bears the nature of fire. At that moment, extreme dryness united with intense heat, ⟪ar:کرۃ النار⟫⟪ar:کرۃ أسیر⟫, and thus the first of the four Mothers was produced: the sphere of fire at the cosmos's center. This is fire of such a nature that the eye cannot perceive it. The scholars teach that had the Originator granted light to this heat, we should never have been able to look upon the heavens—thus revealing one of the Divine wisdoms.

### The Atmosphere

When the effect of the moon's motion reached downward and its rays diffused below, the temperature began to diminish. As heat decreased, condensation ceased, and moisture appeared in its place. When temperature and humidity reached equilibrium, ⟪ar:کرۃ الهوا⟫ (the Sphere of Air) was produced.[^cite-10]

> Statement:
> 
> The decreasing motion of this heat resembles a person floating in water. The water closest to the swimmer moves with far greater agitation than water at a distance; the water far removed experiences no movement at all.

### The Hydrosphere

When the effect of the moon's motion withdrew entirely, the components relaxed and the heat dispersed. There was formed, by the union of cold with moisture, ⟪ar:کرۃ المآء⟫⟪ar:کرۃ زمحریر⟫ (the Sphere of Water), which enveloped the entire earth. All this occurred in essence—that is, the water was not yet manifest in sensible form, but its essential nature had come into being.

At the end of each ⟪ar:دور، کور، مور⟫ (cycle, core, mauress) there appears precisely what appeared at the beginning. Their appearances are in the form of the noon-point. ⟪ar:صاحب مور⟫ In truth, he is the Sultan.

## The Conditions of the Cycles of Unveiling and Concealment

Just as an ordinary day comprises twenty-four hours and is divided into three periods—morning, afternoon, and night—so too does the Divine Day possess a religious duration of fifty-seven thousand years, ⟪ar:دور کشف، فترت اور ستر⟫. The daytime extends for fifty thousand years, with the first portion being forty-seven thousand years, which is ⟪ar:دور کشف⟫ (the Cycle of Unveiling—the morning), and the final portion being three thousand years, which is ⟪ar:دور فترت⟫ (the Cycle of Interruption—the afternoon). The night lasts seven thousand years, termed ⟪ar:دور ستر⟫ (the Cycle of Concealment):

| Morning | Afternoon | Night |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Unveiling Cycle | Interruption Cycle | Concealment Cycle |
| 47,000 years | 3,000 years | 7,000 years |

### Conditions of the Unveiling Cycle

During ⟪ar:دور کشف⟫ (the Unveiling), the inner truths and realities, ⟪ar:علوم باطن حقیقیۃ⟫, shall be revealed. Although the ordinances of the revealed Law shall not be binding in the period of unveiling, the people of that era shall attain such spiritual heights that their obligatory practices shall exceed the practices of the concealment-era believers. Just so: though fasting is not binding upon them, they shall fast more ardently than those ⟪ar:اہل دور ستر⟫ of the concealment cycle, and though zakāt (the obligatory alms) is not binding, their voluntary charity shall surpass sevenfold that of the concealment-era faithful. ⟪ar:دور کشف⟫ The practice of circumcision shall continue, but in ⟪ar:ہَشْفَۃ⟫ some individuals (the circumcised) it shall not become apparent.

The bodies of the unveiling cycle shall resemble those of the concealment cycle, yet with this difference: they shall be exceedingly delicate and pure. During this period there shall be no disease, for there exist no germs therein. Germs are in fact manifestations of our own transgressions and iniquities, which arise together with corporeality. In the era of unveiling, only halāl (permissible) crops and halāl animals shall exist. All the malevolence of harmful creatures—mosquitoes, fleas, and the like—shall be prevented from the people of unveiling.

In the unveiling cycle, believers shall alone exist, yet alongside them shall also be brought forth the pure Shiʿa of ʿAlī; all of them, however, shall be subordinate to the believers and shall serve as their servants. Very lofty realities and exalted worlds, ⟪ar:عولم حقائق عالیۃ⟫, shall be revealed upon the pulpit. In this era, there shall be no enmity, and thus no hostility anywhere. Should it occur anywhere, it shall be exceedingly slight, remaining only at the level of the heart and never expressed outwardly; and all this shall also occur at the end of the world, in the ⟪ar:(دور فترت)⟫ (Interruption Cycle).

Beyond this, all the burdensome ordinances of the revealed Law shall be lifted. Only ⟪ar:شریعت عقلیۃ⟫ (the Intellectual Law)—matters of marriage, slavery, purity, inheritance, and the like—shall remain. ⟪ar:حسیۃ⟫ The sensory experiences shall diminish, thereby removing anger, hardness of heart, physical exhaustion, and lust. People shall be inclined toward piety and reverence to the utmost degree, and the conjugal bond between husband and wife shall occur but once yearly, solely to fulfil the Sunna (exemplary practice). When he enters ⟪ar:برج حمل⟫ (the House of the Ram), ⟪ar:بیت الشرف⟫ only then shall the husband cohabit with his wife so that she might conceive and fulfill the Divine command. Beyond this, there shall be no marriage. Death too shall come without sickness. People shall intend their own death and impose it upon themselves.

### Conditions of the Concealment Cycle

There are two types of summons (daʿwa, the invitation) in the concealment cycle: the inward summons and the outward summons. The inward summons comprises the inward invitation of the Divine Stations (the Imāms), ⟪ar:مقامات الٰہیۃ (ائمہ)⟫, their ⟪ar:ابواب⟫ (gates), ⟪ar:حجتوں⟫ (proofs), the ⟪ar:دعات⟫ (summoners), and the ⟪ar:ماذونین⟫ (authorized ones)—all dwelling in ⟪ar:ھیاکل نورانیۃ⟫ (luminous temples). When the cycle began with Adam (peace be upon him), these people concealed themselves for fear of their enemies. The outward summons comprises the supplications of the Imāms. These supplications arise from the outward summons and receive their command from the Imām hidden behind the veil, summoning people to him. Those who respond to the outward summons are drawn from it and guided toward the inward summons of their ⟪ar:امام⟫ (Imām), their ⟪ar:دعوت باطنۃ⟫ (inward invitation). For this reason, our Master ʿAlī al-Jamālī (the Graceful) said: ⟪ar:سیدنا علی الجمالی⟫ "I am ⟪ar:امام⟫ like a pen in the hand of a scribe—the ⟪ar:امام⟫ Imām."
