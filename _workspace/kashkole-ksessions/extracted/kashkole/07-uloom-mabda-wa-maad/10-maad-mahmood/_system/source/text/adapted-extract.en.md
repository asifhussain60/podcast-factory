## The Resurrection (maʿād) of the Sincere Believer

The sincere believer is composed of three parts, each corresponding inversely to the three jewels of the testimony of sincerity (kalimat al-ikhlāṣ). After death these three parts pass through three distinct phases: each is purified in its own station, and each proceeds to its own destination. Throughout his life the believer is at work cultivating the ⟪ar:خمیرۃ الأبدائیۃ⟫ (the leaven of primordial origination) within himself; even his preparations for dying are arrangements so that, once severed from the body, each of his parts may reach the place fitted to cleanse and perfect it. Through these labours he transmutes his image, transforming what belongs to the ⟪ar:ناسوت⟫ (the realm of created humanity) into what belongs to the ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ (the realm of divine reality), while the ⟪ar:فضلات⟫ (corporeal residues) and ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (volatile remnants) are discharged below. By this discipline the higher portion of his ⟪ar:(نفس)⟫ ascends from physicality into spirituality.[^cite-1]

## Mubashshir and Bashīr: The Noble Form (ṣūrat sharīfa)

It is well established from the hadith of ⟪ar:آنحضرت صلعم⟫ that at the moment of death every soul is met — by ⟪ar:’مبشروبشیر‘⟫ if it is virtuous, by ⟪ar:’منکر و نکیر‘⟫ if it is wicked. The inner reality (ḥaqīqa) of this teaching is that the Speaker (nāṭiq) and his Legatee (waṣī), in every age, are themselves the ⟪ar:مبشر و بشیر⟫ for those who love them, and the ⟪ar:منکر و نکیر⟫ for those who reject them. The Imam of the age likewise: he is ⟪ar:مبشر و بشیر⟫ to the friends of his time and ⟪ar:منکر و نکیر⟫ to its enemies. It is for this reason that ⟪ar:مولانا امیر المومنین⟫ declared:

⟪ar-quote:یاَ حاَرِ ہَمداَن مَن یَّمُت یَرِنِی مِن مُومِنٍ اَو مُناَفِقٍ اَجَلا، یَعرِفُنیِ اِعتَرِفِہِ وَ عاَرِفُہُ بِأِسمِہٖ وَالْکُناَ وَماَ فَعَلا⟫

"O ⟪ar:حار ھمدان⟫, whoever dies — believer or hypocrite — shall see me at the appointed term: he shall know me, and I shall know him, by his name, by his agnomen, and by what he has wrought."[^cite-2]

### Mubashshir and Bashīr for the Friend (walī): The Noble Form

Humankind lies scattered throughout the cosmos. Within each person is a ⟪ar:’نفس نامیہ‘⟫ (vegetative soul) whose calling is growth, and whose foundation is set in the ⟪ar:قمر⟫ (the lunar sphere). It is acquired through the ⟪ar:سلالہ⟫ (seminal extract) and the ⟪ar:نطفہ⟫ (drop of semen) as the ⟪ar:نامیہ⟫ unfolds within the womb. With birth into the world, the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ (sensitive soul) is met under the sun; with adulthood and the taking of the covenant of allegiance, the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ (rational soul) is received from the vertical light (al-nūr al-qāʾim). The more a person performs ⟪ar:اعمال صالحہ⟫ (righteous works) and acquires ⟪ar:علم حقیقی⟫ (true knowledge), the more is the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ prepared and joined with the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫, until together they constitute his ⟪ar:’صورت نورانی‘⟫. At the hour of death, in measure as the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫, the ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫, and the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ have been brought into obedience, the ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ is taken up into the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ of the Imam; what remains is consigned to the grave.[^cite-3]

Every ⟪ar:مقام (ناطق، وصی، امام)⟫ moves the vertical light. That light, issuing from the realm of holiness, descends upon the believer by joining itself to the form of each saintly friend; every good is bound to it. When the believer's death draws near, the ⟪ar:’شخص نورانی‘⟫ — fashioned from his ⟪ar:علم الہی⟫ and his ⟪ar:عمل صالح⟫ — appears to him, and the believer rejoices at its sight. He knows then that his end shall be a goodly one. This luminous person is the very form he has produced through walāya (allegiance) to the people of truth, and from his own ⟪ar:علوم الہیہ⟫ and ⟪ar:اعمال صالحہ⟫.

The ⟪ar:انسان فاضل (حدود کرام، دعات)⟫ — the perfected human, comprising the noble limits (ḥudūd kirām) and the duʿāt (the Summoners) — fashions his likeness without ceasing, through ⟪ar:اخلاق حسنہ⟫ (noble character) and ⟪ar:اعمال جمیلہ⟫ (beautiful deeds). In this earthly state he is ⟪ar:ملک بالقوۃ⟫ (an angel in potency). When he dies and quits the body, he becomes ⟪ar:ملک بالفعل⟫ (angel in actuality), enters the order of the angels, and reaches the ⟪ar:مجمع عالیہ الشریفہ (ھیکل شریف)⟫. For this reason ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ said: "When God raises a believer from the grave, there comes forth with him a beautiful form." This form is a ⟪ar:’ملک‘⟫ of light who watches over every member of his body throughout his life — diffused through all his limbs, tending to the perfection of each. When the believer is afflicted with fear in the grave, this ⟪ar:ملک (صورت)⟫ says to him: "Do not fear; no harm shall reach you." The believer looks upon her and asks, "Who are you, whose mercy is upon me — you who have given me the promise of peace and set my dread at rest?" She answers: "I am a creature of God, whom He fashioned from the substance you earned by your good acts toward the believers; today I have come to be your joy."

The Messenger of God said to ⟪ar:قیس ابن آسم⟫: "O Qays, you have a companion who shall be buried with you — living while you are dead. If you are noble he shall honour you; if you are vile he shall consign you to his keeping. Your resurrection shall be with him, and concerning him you shall be questioned. Make him righteous: if good, you shall delight in him; if evil, you shall recoil from him. He is your ⟪ar:’عمل ‘⟫ — your companion."

## The Return of the Spiritual Form

When a believer departs in ⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫ (a goodly end), the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫ acts as ⟪ar:سیقلدار⟫ (a polisher) upon the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ and the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫, drawing them into its own likeness. These three together become a single being — the ⟪ar:’صورت نورانی‘⟫, which is the ⟪ar:’صورت شریف‘⟫. During the believer's lifetime they were *mujāwir* (neighbours dwelling side by side); after death they become *mujāwiz* (conjoined as in marriage), one substance, one essence. Once these have departed, what is left behind is consigned to the grave as residue.

The noble form drinks in the vertical light and joins itself to the form of its upper limit (ḥadd ʿālī). The form of the believer and the form of his upper limit become one soul, and remain subservient to the soul of that upper limit — so that, even if the soul of the higher limit should later be impaired, no harm reaches the form joined to it. Death itself occurs through the motion of the beneficent light, and it comes because the form of the believer's limit has reached its completion. She hears with his ear, sees with his eye, and answers with his tongue. As this great one ascends through ever higher degrees, the conditions of his bearing alter accordingly, until at length even he passes away.[^cite-4]

**Points to note:**

- This is the manner of all the ⟪ar:جزائر⟫. The ⟪ar:حضرات⟫ who have undergone the proper preparation can no longer be wronged in their ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ and ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫. When such a believer dies, his form is merged into the form and soul of his upper limit.

### Whence Came the Form of the Upper Limit?

If the noble form is ready at the time of death, what then of the form of the upper limit — that which came forward to receive it and to constitute its boundary? The truth is that the Divine Essence is fashioned within its own lifetime. Acts performed in the affairs of the sharīʿa leave their imprint upon the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ and the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫; knowledge and inner action leave their imprint upon the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫. The form of those who stand at high station is not only made ready in their lifetime but proceeds outward and reaches the gates (abwāb) of the Imam; in its place there remains the impress (athar) of that form, by which they continue throughout their days in exceeding piety. For this reason ⟪ar:سیدنا غلام حسین صاحب اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ pointed out that **"the womb is gone, only the trace remains"** — meaning, in the manner of ⟪ar:(سیدنا علی بن محسن شبام)⟫, that his form has ascended to his upper limit, while its effect alone remains here below.

---

Upon the passing of the upper limit, the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ is drawn up and added to its form (which has already absorbed the form of its own limit); thus the two ascend, returning from limit to limit until they reach the gates of the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ of the Imam. This ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ then prepares the issue of the ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ for the Imam. This very fact is declared in the Qurʾān:

> ⟪quran 89:27-30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيَّتُهَا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ‎ ‏ٱرْجِعِىٓ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةًۭ مَّرْضِيَّةًۭ ‎ ‏فَٱدْخُلِى فِى عِبَدِى ‎ ‏وَٱدْخُلِى جَنَّتِى ‎⟫
> *(It will be said to the pious): "O (you) the one in (complete) rest and satisfaction! " Come back to your Lord, Well-pleased (yourself) and well-pleasing unto Him! "Enter you, then, among My honoured slaves, "And enter you My Paradise!" *
> O soul of peace, return to your Lord, well-pleasing to Him, well-pleasing to You, so be one of My servants and enter My Paradise.
> — **Quran 89:27-30 (inline widget)**

⟪ar-quote:يَٓأَيَّتُھا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّۃ⟫ *(O soul at peace)*

Here the speaker is the ⟪ar:امام الزمان⟫, not the ⟪ar:’عقل‘⟫; the one addressed is the ⟪ar:’نفس‘⟫. It is the rational soul — the ⟪ar:نفس⟫ in its aspect as ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ — that, at the hour of death, gathers in the ⟪ar:نفوس حسیۃ و نامیۃ⟫ and gives them refuge; by it both these souls are tranquillised and brought to contentment.

⟪ar-quote:ارجعی الی ربک راضیۃ مرضیۃ⟫ *(Return to your Lord, well-pleased and well-pleasing.)*

By this is meant the return of the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫, together with the ⟪ar:نفوس حسیۃ و نامیۃ⟫ now united in the ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫, to its ⟪ar:’رب‘⟫ — both having returned with full consent and satisfaction.

⟪ar-quote:فادخلی فی عبادی⟫ *(Enter among My servants)*

⟪ar:عِباَدِی⟫ refers here to the ⟪ar:’حد عالی ‘⟫: the noble form is commanded to merge with this upper limit.

⟪ar-quote:وَٱدْخُلِی جَنَّتِی⟫ *(And enter My Paradise)*

The form of the upper limit is the first paradise (al-janna al-ūlā), with which our form is brought into reconciliation. The second meaning of ⟪ar:جَنَّتِی⟫ is the ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫. Note that ⟪ar:عِباَدِی⟫ is reached *through* (⟪ar:’فی‘⟫) something, whereas ⟪ar:جَنَّتِی⟫ carries no ⟪ar:’فی‘⟫: this indicates that, although the believer's form will continue its ascent toward the Day of Reckoning at the ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫, it is merged with the Most High instantly upon death.

### The Servant Saving His Master

If a believer's soul is corrupted, he is left lying in the corporeal body. When the form of his upper limit arrives, it rescues the conception (taṣawwur) of the limit and bears it up to the further limits, even as the self perishes. His reward is that he served his master and preserved his master's image. Once his good imagination has departed, no power remains to preserve this self, and that self is corrupted — as befell ⟪ar:ابو بکر⟫ in the case of ⟪ar:داؤد بن قطب⟫. Thereafter a form arises from his evil deeds, leaving its impress upon the ⟪ar:حسیۃ⟫ and the ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫: this is the ⟪ar:صورت ظلمانیۃ⟫.

## The Outer Gate (bāb ẓāhir) and the Inner Gate (bāb bāṭin)

An Imam appoints two gates: the ⟪ar:’باب ظاہر‘⟫ for the islanders (jazāʾir), and the ⟪ar:’باب باطن‘⟫ for the lofty presences (ḥaḍarāt). The Outer Gate becomes the heart of the Inner Gate by gathering up all the forms of the islands. At the command of the Inner Gate, all the forms of the Imam are assembled into the ⟪ar:اُم الذکر⟫, and become the ⟪ar:’قلب‘⟫ — the issue of the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫. This same principle operates within every sphere: Imams become the gates of higher Imams, and so on up the hierarchy. This is the reference of the verse of ⟪ar:سورۃ الرحمن⟫:

> ⟪quran 55:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِۦ جَنَّتَانِ ‎⟫
> *But for him who [the true believer of Islamic Monotheism who performs all the duties ordained by Allah and His Messenger Muhammad PBUH , and keeps away (abstain) from all kinds of sin and evil deeds prohibited in Islam and] fears the standing before his Lord, there will be two Gardens (i.e. in Paradise).*
> And for him who fears to stand before his Lord are two gardens.
> — **Quran 55:46 (inline widget)**

In this verse the ⟪ar:رَبِّہِ⟫ refers to ⟪ar:علی علیہ السلام⟫; the ⟪ar:’مَقَامَ رَبِّہ‘⟫ refers to the Imams descended from the line of ʿAlī; and the ⟪ar:’جَنَّتاَنِ‘⟫ refers to the two gates — the ⟪ar:باب باطن⟫ and the ⟪ar:باب ظاہر⟫. He who stands in awe of the spiritual powers of the Imam is therefore merged with both the outer and the inner gate.

**Points to note:**

- ⟪ar:سیدنا سلیمان صاحب أعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ holds that there are gates established at the level of manifestation as well.

The phrase ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ means "the gate of gates." There are twelve gates by which one may reach the Imam, the last of which is the ⟪ar:’باب الابواب‘⟫. The Imam of the time takes the assembled forms up from the Bāb al-Abwāb, ranges them in order, and sets them upon the human composite according to the station of each. None there is wronged, and none favoured: each receives his recompense in measure of his deeds. All these forms come to resemble intellect (ʿaql) within the Bāb al-Abwāb, for intellect is the essence (jawhar) of man. That is what is called the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ — and the ⟪ar:’امامت‘⟫. When this ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫, or this ⟪ar:امامت⟫, is given over to the Imam, he is called the ⟪ar:’امام‘⟫; before that, he stands in the rank of ⟪ar:حجت عظمی⟫.

**Points to note:**

- Where there has been deviation through sin, the Bāb al-Abwāb leaves these cases for the Imams to come — even though the deceased had died a believer in ⟪ar:خاتمہ بالخیر⟫. The later Imams then undertake the cleansing.

The light of these forms is so intense that no human eye can endure it. ⟪ar:سیدنا مؤید اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ remarks: "If the eyes of man cannot bear the light of the sun, whose light is yet corporeal, how then should they look upon this spiritual light?"[^cite-5]

### The Presence of Khaḍir (peace be upon him)

In the lexicon of the daʿwa (the summons), the ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ is referred to by the name of ⟪ar:خذر علیہ السلام⟫, because the Prophet Khaḍir is the one who drank of the water of life and shall therefore live until the Day of Judgement. In the same way, the Bāb al-Abwāb has absorbed all the luminous forms, and these can never be undone — their life has been made everlasting. In another sense, the ⟪ar:’آب حیات‘⟫ refers to universal knowledge (al-ʿilm al-kullī), after the attainment of which the soul can never be corrupted: death — in the sense of distortion — can never fall upon it.

### Types of the People of Truth (ahl al-ḥaqq)

The people of truth are of two kinds. The first are those who possess true knowledge — the ⟪ar:علوم الہیۃ⟫ — who perform righteous deeds, and who bear allegiance to all the Imams, the Speakers, and their hierarchies. These limits, on reaching the Imam, become the ⟪ar:اعضاء رئیسۃ⟫ (the principal organs) of his ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانیہ⟫, in virtue of their walāya, knowledge, righteous deeds, and gnosis. Within this ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانیہ⟫ the elevation and luminosity of each is proportioned to his walāya, his real knowledge, and his works.

After them, in the ⟪ar:نورانی ھیکل⟫, come those who perform righteous deeds and bear allegiance but lack true knowledge. These pure-hearted believers occupy a lower station in the form of the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ than the first class.

**Points to note:**

- Our parents are, in the most literal sense, our ⟪ar:صورات شریفہ⟫ of the last two generations: the younger image of ourselves, born into the lower sphere, is our mother; and the image that stands behind us is our father.

## The Return of the Rīḥiyyāt: The Remainder of the Vegetative Soul (Baqiyyat al-Nāmiya)

During earthly life, the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ (nafs nāmiya, the vegetative soul) and the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ (nafs ḥissiya, the sensitive soul) are conjoined within the believer. At death they part company. The upper portion — the form belonging to the ⟪ar:(نفس حسیۃ) صورت⟫ — departs together with its bearer; the lower portion, the ⟪ar:(نفس نامیۃ)⟫, remains within the body. After the funerary washing, this residue begins to emerge as a subtle vapour, the ⟪ar:(ریحیات)⟫ (rīḥiyyāt, the airy souls). Once the corpse has been laid to rest, the whole of the ⟪ar:بقایہ شریفہ⟫ (baqiyya sharīfa, the noble remainder) takes three days to depart.[^cite-6]

Among the ⟪ar:مؤمنین مستقیم السیر⟫ (muʾminīn mustaqīm al-sayr, the believers of upright course) of earlier ages, the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ would only emerge in the last part of those three days. By the glorious effect of ⟪ar:سیدنا سلیمان صاحب⟫, however, the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of the ⟪ar:مؤمنین مستقیم السیر⟫ of the present age begin to issue forth at the very moment of death, and depart within the first part of that period. The reason is plain: their remnants are now cleaner than in any prior age, since they have been subject to regular examination.

These ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ — being the remainder of the ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ — rise from the grave and ascend upon the rays of the moon, which is the centre of the ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫. It is for this reason that the moon swells with light on the fourteenth. During the ⟪ar:ایام بِیض⟫ (ayyām bīḍ, the bright nights: the thirteenth, fourteenth, and fifteenth), the ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ gathers within the moon. Whatever the moon collects, it hands over to the sun by degrees, until the whole of the ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ has been delivered. From the first to the twelfth of the month are days of ascent; on the thirteenth, fourteenth, and fifteenth, the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ — that is, the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ — assemble in the moon; from the sixteenth to the end of the lunar month, the ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ is disclosed to the world.

When the pure conception of prayer is elevated, the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ and the ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ rise still further with their bearer. Some attain such a height that their ⟪ar:قُوَّۃِ اَدمِغَۃ⟫ (quwwat-i admigha, the cerebral faculties) and certain parts of the heart also develop.

What emerges from the body as the ⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ (nafs rīḥiyya, the airy soul) is bitter. Though soul, it remains corporeal; for this reason it lingers in the body at the moment of death and only later leaves the grave to ascend to the moon for cleansing. The moon then hands these ⟪ar:نفوس ریحیات⟫ on, by way of Mercury and Venus, to the ⟪ar:شمس⟫ for further purification. The ⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ endures the heat of the sun and is subjected to a torment that is the atonement for its sins. Where hell would have been, it is given the heat of the sun — a heat far gentler than the fire of hell. These souls remain in the sun until the faces of their opponents reach Bāb al-Abwāb. Upon arrival, the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ are pressed and chastised; they are turned into sweat and made to suffer. The sun melts them, and ⟪ar:تخمیر⟫ (takhmīr, the leavening) is performed upon them so that they may be made fit to join the nāsūt of the coming Imam. All this is undertaken so that the ⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ of the believer may attain that nearness to the divine Imam — a proximity to the lāhūt that the unblessed cannot share.[^cite-7]

In the human body, the first thing that arises is ⟪ar:حرارت غریزی⟫ (ḥarārat gharīzī, instinctive heat), formed in the sphere of the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫. ⟪ar:دم قرمیزی⟫ (dam qirmizī, crimson blood) and the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ are then developed by feeding within the mother's womb in the next sphere.

These ⟪ar:نفوس ریحیات⟫ fall within the circle of the sun, which is the very substance of all heat and the centre of all ⟪ar:اجسام غریزیۃ⟫ (instinctive bodies) proper to the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫. Every body that has descended from the heavens is gathered together in the sun, where it forms its ⟪ar:حرارت غریزی⟫ and its ⟪ar:یبوست⟫ (yubūsat, dryness). By the same token, these bodies have been deposited in the moon, which makes them ⟪ar:رطوبت و برودت⟫ (ruṭūbat wa-burūdat, moist and cool).

| Sun | Moon |
| --- | --- |
| Instinctive heat + dryness | Moisture + coolness |

After this purification the sun delivers the soul to Jupiter, which is ⟪ar:'سعد اکبر'⟫ (saʿd akbar, the Greater Benefic). Mars lies above the sun, yet these souls do not reach that star, for it is a star of ill omen. The sun refers them instead directly to Jupiter. In Jupiter the soul becomes utterly transparent and clear. After this cleansing, the purified and lucent soul returns to the sun and is there fixed.

When the Imam wills that his successor — the son who will later become the Imam — should be born, the ⟪ar:مدبر عالم طبعیۃ⟫ (mudabbir-i ʿālam-i ṭabīʿiyya, the governor of the natural world, that is, the intellect of the universe) moves his tongue and hands this purified ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ over to ⟪ar:مشتری (سعد اکبر)⟫ and, by way of the sun's rays, to ⟪ar:زہرہ (سعد اصغر)⟫ (Venus, the Lesser Benefic) and the moon. The moon then deposits this lucid substance in the form of a fruit — one untouched by fire — or as dewdrops upon pure water. The ⟪ar:عصیر⟫ (ʿaṣīr, the juice) of such a fruit is, in truth, ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫.

*Note:* ⟪ar:بی بی فاطمۃ⟫ and Mawlānā ʿAlī were born of an apple, while the Prophet (ṣ) was born of pure water.

This substance — whether fruit or water — is guarded by angels until it reaches the place of its miracle: the Imam and his ⟪ar:زوج (اُم الذکر)⟫ (zawj, umm al-dhikr, the wife who is the Mother of Remembrance). The Imam and his consort are the consecrated vessels prepared for the coming Imam. When the two of them partake of it, the ⟪ar:مادۂ تولیدی (سلالہ)⟫ (māda-yi tawlīdī / sulāla, the generative substance) within each is brought to readiness. The Imam receives it through the nose, his wife through the mouth; their shared eating of this fruit is itself their act of marriage. Through that act, the Imam's wife then receives the seed from the Imam, and the Imam's son is conceived. In this manner, a subtle portion of the ⟪ar:نفوس نامیۃ⟫ of the believers — purified by the ⟪ar:تخمیر شمس⟫ (takhmīr-i shams, the leavening of the sun) and the motion of the celestial spheres — joins the seed of the Imam's son by ⟪ar:عنایت الہیہ⟫ (ʿināyat ilāhiyya, divine providence) and becomes the ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ of the Imam's son.

During pregnancy, the mother of the Imam's son receives all the food that reaches his body; it gathers near her navel, and the Imam's son draws nourishment from it, as is the case with any other infant. Since the Imam and the ⟪ar:بی بی فاطمۃ⟫ themselves are personages of supreme rank, their affairs — the act of marriage, the period of gestation, the moment of birth — function as their veil and their exemplification.

The Imam's wife becomes the holy vessel for the Imam's son. When she gathers the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ from his seed, the Imam's son is generated. During childbirth, she receives every ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ that comes; the higher ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ come to the Imam, the lower ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ to his wife. From these foods are produced the extracts that nourish the seed. The Imam's mother is utterly pure, free of every defilement that pertains to ordinary women; for this reason they are called ⟪ar:'مطہرات '⟫ (muṭahharāt, the purified ones). It is to this that God refers when He says:

Our own body is composed of fire, air, water, and earth; the Imam's body, by contrast, is composed of the subtlest portion of the ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ of the believers, and his divinity (lāhūt) is composed of the forms of those believers. Until the nāsūt is prepared, the lāhūt cannot be received.[^cite-8]

### Growth Speed

When the soul of a pious believer dies, his Imam — the master of the whole universe — comes to him in the form of ⟪ar:'خیال'⟫ (khayāl, the inner image). The thought of the Imam draws the form of this believer toward the spirit of paradise, and the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ rise from the grave, traverse the ⟪ar:امہات⟫ (ummahāt, the elemental mothers), pass through the heavens, and reach the moon. There the soul meets the good spirits who have passed before him. The whole of this journey is accomplished as swiftly as a ⟪ar:نجم ساقب⟫ (najm thāqib, a piercing star) and a ⟪ar:برق الاَّمع⟫ (barq al-lāmiʿ, gleaming lightning); nothing stands in the soul's way, for the believer to whom this journey belongs has joined himself to the Imam of Truth. The bearer of ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of a lower rank reaches the heavens at the speed of the wind; one of a still lower rank moves with the pace of a swift Arabian horse; one lower still moves with the gait of someone striving toward a destination; and the bearers of the lowest ⟪ar:ریحایات⟫ proceed as one weak and burdened.

| Higher ranks → Lower ranks |
| --- |
| Lightning · Wind · Arabian horse · The striver |

Recitations of the Fātiḥa and acts of charity are offered for the deceased because such offerings benefit his ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ greatly. These ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ must become part of the Imam's body, and the Imam will never take to himself a defiled ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫. The sooner they are cleansed, the sooner the believer's seat is established with the Imam.

When the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of the believers reach the heaven of the moon, they are saved from the heat of the ⟪ar:کرہ اسیر⟫ (kura-yi athīr, the sphere of the ether). Sentinels — the ⟪ar:ملائکۃ شر(خبیث ارواح)⟫ (the angels of evil, the wicked spirits) — stand upon the road, barring the evil notions of wicked persons from reaching the moon. He who has passed through these two perils — ⟪ar:(کرۃ الأسیر اور عقدتین)⟫ (the sphere of the ether and the two nodes) — and arrived at the heaven of the moon has truly been saved. Once there, he beholds the power of the ⟪ar:رحمن⟫ (al-Raḥmān, the All-Merciful) within those gardens; the moon hands him to Mercury, Mercury to Venus, Venus to the sun. In the sun he meets his faithful brethren and there rejoices.

All these gentlemen of the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ press the visitor with questions concerning the believers who concern them. If he tells them that the believer in question is still alive, they receive the news as glad tidings, and wish that those still in the world might come to join them. If, however, he says that the believer has passed but has not arrived, they conclude that he has been lost to the Darakāt because of his evil deeds.

### Why the Imam Falls under the Compass of Qiṣāṣ

The Imam's body — his nāsūt — is the ⟪ar:'مؤثِر '⟫ which receives effect; his soul and his form are the ⟪ar:'مؤثَر'⟫ which exert it. As soon as the seed of a believer is settled, it begins to receive the influence of the ⟪ar:افلاکیات⟫ (aflākiyyāt, the celestial bodies), and it continues to receive that influence for the whole of his life. The same principle applies to the Imam: his nāsūt — composed of the gathered ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of the believers — receives the effect of the spheres. For this reason the Imam falls sick, grieves, is wounded, sheds tears, and at the appointed hour is buried. But his soul, magnificent as it is, exerts effect upon and rules over the heavens. There is therefore an Imam who is ⟪ar:'مدبِر'⟫ (the governor) and an Imam who is ⟪ar:'مدبَر'⟫ (the governed).

The descendants of the Imam are composed of the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of the believers, and so the Imam falls under the compass of qiṣāṣ (just retribution). The ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ of the Imam is so powerful that he is able to act from the heavens down to ⟪ar:تحت الثرا⟫ (taḥt al-tharā, beneath the soil). Yet because his body is composed of the parts of the believers, and is bound by the law of retribution, his body accepts the malign influences of the ill-omened planets. Thus he is afflicted with sickness, and even — as the idiom has it — eats spears. Though the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ are brought to the Imam in a state of high purity, there remains a slight reckoning. The body of the Imam is therefore that which receives effect, while the soul of the Imam is the master of the firmament.

## The Convergence of Lāhūt and Nāsūt

The Imam possesses no bodily limbs in the ordinary sense; his very corporeality is constituted from the believers themselves. His ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ — the subtle vapours (rīḥiyāt, the ethereal exhalations of the purified) of the faithful — together with their ⟪ar:نفوس ریحیہ⟫ (nufūs rīḥiyya, the vaporous souls) form what is named his honour, his frame, and his estate. To grasp this teaching one must first distinguish the human constitution from the Imam's. The ordinary believer is a composite of three elements:

| Waste | Rīḥiyāt (Baqiyyat al-Nāmiya) | Honourable Face |
| --- | --- | --- |

The Imam, however, has only two:

| Nāsūt | Lāhūt |
| --- | --- |

The physical aspect of the Imam, ⟪ar:ناسوت طبعی⟫ — the natural Nāsūt (the human, terrestrial nature) — is itself fashioned out of the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of the believers of the islands. These rise from the grave as a fine vapour and ascend to the sphere of the moon; from the moon they pass through Mercury, Venus, and the Sun, before arriving at Jupiter, where they are cleansed. Once purified they return to the Sun, and there the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ wait until the Imam — through his ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫, the Bāb al-Abwāb (the Gate of Gates) — has prepared the ranks of these believers and the ḥudūd (limits) who watch over them.

> **Facts:**
> So long as it abides in the precinct of the Bāb al-Abwāb, it is called Ṣūrat. When it is completed and transferred from the Bāb to the Imam, it is called ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانیۃ و قدسانیۃ⟫ — the luminous and sanctified haykal (corporeal frame). At that point the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ is named Lāhūt (the divine nature) or Imāmat.

When the children of the Imam reach the age of training — four years old — at the behest of the father-Imam and his ⟪ar:اُم الذکر⟫ (Umm al-Dhikr, the Mother of Remembrance), the Bāb al-Abwāb passes away of his own accord and attains the place ⟪ar:قلب ھیکل⟫ — the heart of the haykal — within the precinct of the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫. Three durations are described for this passage of consecration:

| Four Days | 40 Days | Four years |
| --- | --- | --- |

> **Facts:**
> ⟪ar:مولانا عبد المطلب⟫ acquired ⟪ar:لاہوت شریف⟫ — the noble Lāhūt — already during the pregnancy of ⟪ar:والدۃ سلمیٰ⟫: a most unusual occurrence. For this reason ⟪ar:مولانا عبد المطلب⟫ was born already an Imam. A sign of this was that his hair was white at birth, prompting his mother to exclaim, ⟪ar-quote:ولدت شیبۃ⟫ — "I have given birth to an old man." This pointed to the exalted status of ⟪ar:مولانا عبد المطلب⟫.

Each Imam has two doors: ⟪ar:باب ظاہر اور باب باطن⟫ — an outer door and an inner. The community of the islands gathers around the outer and is then drawn into the inner. The Bāb al-Bāṭin (which is precisely the Bāb al-Abwāb) passes away, and the assembly that had collected around the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ becomes its ⟪ar:’قلب‘⟫ — its heart.

When the Imam's sons are ready — that is, when they are prepared within the door of the Imam, that ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ which is the true counterpart of their primordial ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ — then ⟪ar:مدبر عالم طبعیۃ⟫, the Governor of the Natural World, instructs the Imam to designate his son before the people of the present age. The son at that moment is four days, forty days, or four years old. The Imam transmits this designation through the voice of ⟪ar:باب الابواب (باب باطن)⟫, and through ⟪ar:اُم الذکر⟫. The Bāb al-Abwāb dies at that very instant and takes the place of the heart in proximity to ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫, arriving at the Imam together with the whole gathering of the verses. The Imam confers the Ṣuwarāt (now sometimes rendered "forms") upon his son, joining him with the ⟪ar:ناطقۃ اور حسیۃ⟫ — the rational and the sensible — and with ⟪ar:نفس⟫ (nafs, soul). ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ thus passes from ⟪ar:’لاہوت ‘⟫ to ⟪ar:’امامت ‘⟫. By this connection the Imam's ⟪ar:’نفس‘⟫ becomes for him a ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫, and this in turn imprints itself upon the Imam's soul through its manifestation in ⟪ar:عالم حیات⟫, the World of Life. The ⟪ar:نفس امام⟫, the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫, and the ⟪ar:ناسوت⟫ are thereby raised together, and of the three this last is the noblest.

As soon as the son is connected with the ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ of his father, he himself becomes a ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانی⟫, lending nobility to that veil — the body of the Imam. At that instant the son becomes the ⟪ar:’حجاب‘⟫ and the ⟪ar:’باب‘⟫ of his father, the Imam. In this manner the son of the Imam comes to possess a true convergence of Nāsūt and Lāhūt, drawn together out of the ⟪ar:صورات⟫ of the believers and their limits — out of the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫. The ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ and ⟪ar:صورات⟫ that were severed from one another at the believers' deaths are restored together at the Imam, reunited in him.[^cite-9]

> **Facts:**
> ⟪ar:ناسوت امام ’ لا الہ‘⟫ stands in contrast to ⟪ar:لاہوت امام⟫, which corresponds to ⟪ar:’الا اللہ‘⟫. When the Imam is fully established and the Nāsūt has attained to the Lāhūt, one pronounces ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫: every power belonging to the Nāsūt derives from his divinity.

So long as he was kept in the precinct of the Bāb al-Abwāb, the status of the ⟪ar:ھیکل شریف⟫ was high; once he reaches the door of the son of the Imam, however, the status of the ⟪ar:ھیکل شریف⟫ rises far higher still. Now that the whole brotherhood has gathered into a single assembly and become one ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانی⟫, the vitality, power, and knowledge of all are magnified together — more noble, more exalted, more illumined than before. For this whole assembly to arrive at this ⟪ar:رتبہ عالیہ⟫, this lofty rank, is indeed a paradise in their world. And in this way, with ⟪ar:نور الٰہی⟫ shining through, the connection of the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ with that which was emanated from ⟪ar:عالم قدس⟫, the World of Holiness, is brought to completion.

> **Facts:**
> The matter that constitutes the father of the Imam descends from the First Originated. The matter constituting the son of the Imam descends from al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect). The remaining Intellects look toward the ten ḥudūd below the Imam, and the Tenth has its gaze fixed upon the believers.[^cite-10]

The Imam is the ⟪ar:’فتح ‘⟫ — the Opening — for his son; and as soon as the son's stewardship begins, the father becomes for him the ⟪ar:’جد‘⟫, the Forefather. One of the highest ḥudūd of the Imam is called ⟪ar:’خیال‘⟫, the Imagination, and this rank is always attached to the son of the Imam.

Since two sovereigns cannot reign at once, the father dies almost immediately after the decree — within a minute or a minute and a half — and becomes ⟪ar:مقیم الامام⟫, the Sustainer of the Imam, ascending with his congregation (the assembly of trumpets received from his own father) to grow into the ⟪ar:برزق عاشر⟫, the Tenth Isthmus. Thereafter the new Imam gradually takes upon himself the burden of the Imamate. The ⟪ar:جد، فتح اور خیال⟫ — Forefather, Opening, and Imagination — are themselves trained and refined. The new Imam's stewardship begins, and his chief purpose is to draw forth from his own loins a son and a successor, just as his father drew him forth from the daʿwa (the summons). This order is perpetual.

An Imam ⟪ar:صاحب لاہوت⟫ — a Possessor of Lāhūt — manifests with a gathering of one hundred thousand Ṣuwarāt within his divinity. This is why one who prays in the House of Allah obtains the recompense of a hundred thousand rakʿats for a single rakʿa: opposite the outer House of Allah stands the inner House, which contains the gathering of a thousand trumpets, and their total illumination falls upon this mosque. From these one hundred thousand forms the Imam may manifest any form he wishes. He could appear from a thousand veils, yet he chooses not to.

With the attainment of Lāhūt, sovereignty is established from the ⟪ar:فلک محیط⟫, the All-Encompassing Sphere, down to ⟪ar:تحت الثرا⟫, what lies beneath the soil. Every miracle is possible by God's leave; yet unless the light of the divine has shone forth upon the horizon, no miracle can be displayed. With the text of the Imamate the Imam is bound by ⟪ar:مادۃ (روحانیات)⟫ — matter of a spiritual order — to the Tenth, which itself was issued from al-ʿAql al-Awwal. In truth there are thousands upon thousands of connections, indeed millions, between the Tenth and the Imam.

> **Facts:**
> When ⟪ar:امام حسن و حسین ۸⟫ were seven years old, the Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ said: ⟪ar-quote:اَلحَسَنُ وَالحُسَینُ اِماَمَ حَقًّ قاَمَ أَو قَعَدَ⟫ — "Both Ḥasan and Ḥusayn are Imams of right, whether they stand or sit." By this saying the Prophet ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ effected upon them a ⟪ar:نص خفی⟫, a hidden designation, drawing certain special verses near the Prophet's door and joining them to the two Imams.

The form of the Lāhūt rests upon a human form in which all the organs are arranged. The ḥudūd occupy the same positions within the Lāhūt that they occupy within the Nāsūt of the believers: the one who holds the place of the head in the Lāhūt holds the place of the head in the Nāsūt as well. Since the Bāb al-Abwāb occupies the heart's place in the divine realm, another stands in the place of the heart within the constitution of the Imam's son until that one's own death. As soon as the Bāb al-Abwāb dies, his ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ likewise attain to the place of the heart in his constitution.

## The Return of the Effluents

⟪ar:خمیرۃ ابدائیۃ⟫ (khamīra ibdāʿiyya, the primordial leaven). A particular fluid surrounds the heart, called ⟪ar:'دم قرمیزی'⟫ (dam qirmizī, crimson blood), and a set of breaths attends it, called ⟪ar:'حرارت غریزی'⟫ (ḥarāra gharīziyya, innate heat). In the same way, within the ⟪ar:جسمانی کعبۃ اﷲ⟫ (jismānī Kaʿba Allāh, the corporeal Kaʿba of God) there is an inner water called ⟪ar:'مآء ساعد'⟫ (māʾ sāʿid, ascending water), encircled by a particular breath. As there is one ⟪ar:'رقامۃ الحمرۃ '⟫ (raqāmat al-ḥumra, the crimson tracing) within the ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫ — the highest place in the Kaʿba, the very spot where Mawlānā ʿAlī was born — so too within our own hearts there is a single ⟪ar:رقامۃ الحمرۃ⟫ upon which the ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ (nāṭiqa, the articulating soul) exerts its influence, drawing out the highest constituents of the heart, rousing them, and transmuting them into spiritual substance.

## The Return of the Hard Bones of the Believers

As for the ⟪ar:صاحبان معرفت⟫ (ṣāḥibān-i maʿrifa, the people of gnosis) and the ⟪ar:صاحبان اہل ظاہر⟫ (the people of the exoteric) — the virtuous and the active among them — their flesh, skin, and soft bones all ascend through the heavens to the zodiac. The hard bones, by contrast, linger long in the grave. Then, by gradual stages, they issue forth as vapour and, through ⟪ar:مزاج ممتزج⟫ (mizāj mumtazij, the mingled temperament), descend as rain upon the world, falling into the seas and caves, where they are formed into rubies and pearls.

A note on terms: ⟪ar:'مزاج'⟫ (mizāj) is the name given to the emergence of the four elements — fire, air, water, and earth — while ⟪ar:'ممتزج'⟫ (mumtazij) is the name given to rain and showers. The agent responsible is the ⟪ar:عمود نور⟫ (ʿamūd al-nūr, the pillar of light): the perpendicular light that acts as a divine magnet, uniting what has been dispersed and dispersing what has been united, and restoring all constituents to their proper places — ⟪ar:مزاج و ممتزج⟫.

These jewels adorn the crowns of kings and fill their treasuries; they are worn upon the hands of men and the necks of women, whether scholars or the ignorant, friends or deniers. The ⟪ar:فضلات شریفہ⟫ (faḍlāt sharīfa, noble effluents) tarry with the ignorant and the deniers only on account of the latter's worldly fortune. This persists until the time of their return arrives. When it does, they pass once more through ⟪ar:تخمیر⟫ (takhmīr, leavening) in the heavens and, together with the other organs of the human body that have descended from the zodiac, return to the earth and grow gradually until they appear as a human being at his ⟪ar:قامت الفیہ⟫ (qāmat alfiyya, millennial stature). That is, the constituents which were formerly hard bones are reintegrated once more into the human organs.

All this unfolds so that some portion of the effluent may develop sufficiently to be wrought, by ⟪ar:عنایت الہیہ⟫ (ʿināya ilāhiyya, divine providence), into a ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ (nafs, soul) by the agency of the planet's eye. ## The Categories of the Imam's Descendant-Body (Nawāsiyya)

That is to say, ⟪ar:مولانا علی⟫ conducted his work in Mecca, Medina, and Kūfa from behind the ⟪ar:حجاب حجاب تناسلی⟫.

## On the Rank of the Believer in the Coming Cycles

A soul descends from the zodiac and enters the world after a thousand years. The ⟪ar:عمود نور⟫ (the column of light) begins to draw the human being toward the position from which he can take up the ⟪ar:عہد شریف⟫ (the noble covenant). When he reaches puberty, he is invited to the truth; he accepts the daʿwa (the summons), the covenant is taken from him, and he advances by gradual stages in the religious ranks and in true knowledge (ʿilm-i ḥaqīqī). With the inception of the noble covenant, his upright posture is conjoined to the bond of light, and this bond will remain a shadow attendant upon him for the rest of his life — even in his ⟪ar:حسیہ⟫ (sensitive faculty) and ⟪ar:نامیہ⟫ (vegetative faculty). He no longer admits doubt; he abides in ⟪ar:'یقین'⟫ (certainty) forever, because his first cycle ended in certitude. Once a ⟪ar:ناطقہ⟫ (rational soul) is born to truth, it can never cease.[^cite-15]

> **Origin & Duration**
>
> What remains with us, by way of the ⟪ar:خمیرۃ الأبدائیۃ⟫ (the primordial creative leaven) of the ⟪ar:عالم نیات⟫ (the world of intentions), is the ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ (primary perfection): knowledge, power, and life. The name of the connection that the ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ (rational soul) maintains with that leaven is itself ⟪ar:خمیرۃ الأبدائیۃ⟫.

The believer keeps climbing the ladder of progress until he attains, in the new cycle, the station he had reached in the previous one. A soul who had attained the station of ⟪ar:'داعی'⟫ (Summoner) in the prior cycle is by nature transported into a second-cycle position of ⟪ar:داعی⟫. The one who had stood at ⟪ar:'باب'⟫ (the gate) likewise enters the next cycle already at ⟪ar:باب⟫. From there he resumes his striving and ascends to the next degree.

A believer cannot pass beyond his station until he has completed its ten degrees. Every station contains ten, and on appearing in the next cycle the believer crosses only one degree. If he stood at the fifth degree of faith, he begins the next cycle at the sixth — and however much knowledge he acquires within a given cycle, he is reborn in the next as a believer still, until all ten degrees of belief are exhausted. Only then does he enter the first rank of the ⟪ar:مکاسرت⟫ (the breakers of veils). For martyrs, two degrees suffice. A portion of the ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ (soul) takes shape at the close of each cycle: for those who walk uprightly, or who attain martyrdom, both heart and liver become soul. But this is reserved for those whom God describes in these terms:

> ⟪quran 23:2-6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ فِى صَلَاتِهِمْ خَشِعُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ ٱللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَوٰةِ فَعِلُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِفُرُوجِهِمْ حَفِظُونَ ‎ ‏إِلَّا عَلَىٰٓ أَزْوَجِهِمْ أَوْ مَا مَلَكَتْ أَيْمَنُهُمْ فَإِنَّهُمْ غَيْرُ مَلُومِينَ ‎⟫
> *Those who offer their Salat (prayers) with all solemnity and full submissiveness. And those who turn away from Al-Laghw (dirty, false, evil vain talk, falsehood, and all that Allah has forbidden). And those who pay the Zakat . And those who guard their chastity (i.e. private parts, from illegal sexual acts) Except from their wives or (the captives and slaves) that their right hands possess, for then, they are free from blame;*
> Those who are humble in their prayers, and those who turn away from what is indecency, and those who pay the poor-rate, and those who guard their private parts, except their wives or those whom their right hands possess, that there is no blame on them.

It is said of the one martyred in jihād that he enters Paradise without reckoning: his form reaches the ⟪ar:ھیکل امامی⟫ (the Imamic temple) directly, unobstructed, and he is not made to traverse every one of the ⟪ar:حدود عالیہ⟫ (the upper limits) in turn. His body, likewise, has no need to climb through the spheres but is joined at once to the ⟪ar:ناسوت امام⟫ (the corporeal humanity of the Imam). His very garment becomes part of his ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (subtle spiritual substances). For this reason it is enjoined that a martyr be buried in the clothes he wore.[^cite-16]

> **Facts**
>
> A cycle (kawr) endures a thousand years. If a person becomes ⟪ar-quote:مستقیم السَّیر⟫ *(a walker on the straight path)*, his whole body is purified across 360 degrees — that is, the body comes to occupy the station of the ⟪ar:'قلب'⟫ (heart, the stable centre), and as a soul he comes to occupy the station of the ⟪ar:'ناطق'⟫ (the rational, articulate one).

Every thousand years the believer returns. After his death his form is joined to the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ (temple, bodily form) of his Imam. In this way, over the length of an age, his several parts are gathered with successive Imams in the modes of ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ (divinity) and ⟪ar:ناسوت⟫ (humanity), in subtle form and in dense form. When the Qāʾim appears and assembles all the Imams, every constituent of that person is gathered into a single human shape; at that moment his first, slender form becomes the head of all the rest of his constituents.

Every core has its origin in the ⟪ar:جثہ ابدائیہ⟫ (the primordial body), and thereafter its progeny advance by means of marriage. The Resurrection of the Corps is called ⟪ar:'قیامت کلیۃ'⟫ (the universal resurrection). In the span of one core the cords of gravity loosen and the world perishes. In that interval all the ⟪ar:امہات⟫ (the elemental mothers) and all the ⟪ar:سقرا⟫ (the lower realms) vanish, while many of the ⟪ar:قامت الفیہ⟫ (the millennial statures) come forth and mature. Then the ⟪ar:امہات⟫ are made ready again for the next cycle. What a human being has built up departs with him as ⟪ar:صاحب کور⟫ (lord of the cycle). What remains comes to the next cycle for further development — and this disposition of affairs is the very order of regret in the spiritual world.

### Ten Levels of Faith

Faith is the name of degrees, stations, and classes. Islam alone is the station of ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫. But the moment one enters faith, ranks come into play, and these belong to ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ (secondary perfection). Each station of faith contains ten degrees. The world of ⟪ar:لِم⟫ (the "why" — the realm of intelligible causes) is the world in which the count ⟪ar:'۱۰'⟫ first found its proper place in the spiritual realm. Because the intellects are ten, the degrees here are ten. So the Qurʾān testifies: ⟪ar-quote:تِلْكَ عَشَرَةٌۭ كَامِلَ⟫ — "That is ten complete."[^cite-19]

In addition there is the prayer ⟪ar:بین المبدع و المبدع کلمۃ⟫: that is, between the ⟪ar:مبدِع⟫ (the Originator) and the ⟪ar:مبدَع⟫ (that which is originated) there stands ⟪ar:'نور کلمہ'⟫ (the light of the Word). When the First Intellect attained to the unity of the Originator and reached the ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫, he was overwhelmed by ten veils of joy in which he erred. He remains occupied still with lifting those very veils. This is the meaning of the Prophet's saying — peace and blessings be upon him: ⟪ar-quote:مَن عَرَفَ نَفسُہُ فَقَد عَرَفَ رَبُّہُ⟫ — "Whoever knows his own soul has thereby known his Lord." One station is given as a ⟪ar:تشبیح⟫ (likeness) for another. Since no one fully knows his own self, no one can fully know his Lord.

### Key Summary

A small portion of one's ⟪ar:روحانی شبیح⟫ (spiritual likeness) appears in a cycle. Even a part of that small portion appears as a body; everything else is scattered across the cycle in the form of food, garments, and other goods that a person collects in the course of his life. When he has assembled every part allotted to his cycle, he dies, and his original substance — ⟪ar:صورت + نفس حسیہ⟫ (form together with the sensitive soul) — is separated from him forever. The rest then passes upward into the next cycle, where it continues its development.

The form thus separated ascends to the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫, and then through the smaller and the larger ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ until it reaches the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ of the Qāʾim, where it takes on the figure of a spiritual being. In this way its parts gather, by degrees, alongside different lists in different cycles. The ⟪ar:ھیاکل⟫ assemble in the distant core; the ⟪ar:ھیاکل⟫ of that core form, in turn, the peacock ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ of the cycle. When the ⟪ar:صاحب مور (سلطان)⟫ is constituted, all of these parts come together to form a complete ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫. The ⟪ar:شبیح⟫ that had been created in perfect state in the spiritual world: this same ⟪ar:شبیح⟫ is now able to ascend once more into the spiritual realm — to become ⟪ar:صاحب مور⟫ — and so to attain secondary perfection.

## The Division of the Imam's Nāsūt

When an Imam and his successor are interred, no decay or odour issues from their bodies, and they remain in the earth no more than three days. Their physical frames ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ are composed of spiritual exhalations (riḥiyyāt), each abstract emanation being itself a ⟪ar:ریحیہ⟫ (rīḥiyya). Since their ⟪ar:ناسوت⟫ (nāsūt — the corporeal realm) is itself rooted in ⟪ar:'ریحیات'⟫, the body is detained beneath the soil for only three days. How could the body of a believer — whose ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ depart at death in their original purity — linger longer than three days within the corpse? By the same scale, the body of a dāʿī (Summoner) remains in the grave for thirty years, and the body of a muʾmin (believer) for three hundred.[^cite-20]

| Rank | Duration in the grave |
| --- | --- |
| Imam | 3 days |
| Bāb (gates) | 21 years |
| Dāʿī | 30 years |
| Muʾmin | 300 years |

**Notes:**

- It is for this reason that ⟪ar:امام اسماعیل بن جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ was seen in the marketplace of Baṣra three days after his passing. The Imam cannot remain in the grave longer than the time required by his ⟪ar:ریحیات ۳⟫: his ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ depart from the grave and reassemble in a fresh manifestation. The believer's grave is the very place where he is buried, but the qāʾim's grave is this world itself; hence when he manifests, he does not remain in this world more than three days. He passes into the veil that conceals the realm of purity, and from that moment his radiance rises immeasurably — on the morrow he is the very veil of the Imam.

### Allāhumma:

The Imam is a composite of five elements. The highest of these is the ⟪ar:'صورت شریف'⟫ (ṣūrat sharīfa — the noble form), which he fashioned out of his yearning in the spiritual realm. When the Imam departs this world, his form draws to itself his ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ (nafs — soul) and his ⟪ar:'ذات'⟫ (dhāt — essence). With its power his ⟪ar:'لاہوت'⟫ (lāhūt — the divine plane) also departs, and with lāhūt his ⟪ar:'ناسوت خاص'⟫ (nāsūt khāṣṣ — particular corporeality) ascends further still, together with his ⟪ar:'ناسوت ظلی'⟫ (nāsūt ẓillī — shadowy corporeality). What remains is what belongs to the islanders' ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫, and that alone is buried. After his death the Imam carries everything with him except the ⟪ar:ناسوت طبعی⟫ (nāsūt ṭabīʿī — natural corporeality), composed of the islanders' ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫, which alone remains because the transactions of those islanders are still pending and must be accounted for by the chastisement of the grave. It is the ⟪ar:ناسوت طبعی⟫ that lies beneath the earth.

The word ⟪ar:اللّٰھُمَّ⟫ (Allāhumma) refers to the Imam himself. It contains five letters that point to the Imam's five constituents. We cannot interpret ⟪ar:اللّٰھُمَّ⟫ as the ⟪ar:'عین عظیمہ'⟫ (al-ʿayn al-ʿaẓīma — the supreme essence), for we have the Imams before us, and these Imams are themselves the ⟪ar:عین عظیمہ⟫. In ⟪ar:اللّٰھُمَّ⟫ the ⟪ar:الف⟫ (alif) ascends, taking with it both the ⟪ar:لام⟫ (lām) and the ⟪ar:ھا⟫ (hā). The hā has two eyes, pointing to the particular and the shadowy nāsūt. Only the ⟪ar:میم⟫ (mīm) remains — the letter that falls to the ground in script, and that is buried in the grave.

| Letter | Constituent | Significance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Alif | Noble Form (ṣūrat sharīfa) | The Imam's form, the spiritual or secondary perfection (kamāl thānī), which he fashioned and through which he received the light by his own intention in the spiritual world. |
| Lām | Self (nafs) | The Imam's faculty of origination. |
| Lām | Lāhūt | The aggregate of all forms. |

### The Form of the Imam as the Original:

Although the Imam's ⟪ar:ناسوت⟫ contains its own assembly, his ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ likewise lacks the progression that is demanded of the believers. Lāhūt is the original in relation to nāsūt. Yet lāhūt itself is no longer the ultimate original; rather, the ⟪ar:'نفس امام'⟫ (nafs al-imām — the Imam's soul) is the original over which the ⟪ar:'صورت '⟫ exercises its act. Thus the Imam's ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ becomes the original. It is the law of God, Most High, that no ⟪ar:رئیس⟫ (raʾīs — head) shall progress without his ⟪ar:مرؤسین⟫ (marʾūsīn — those under his charge). It is by virtue of this divine intention towards the ⟪ar:اماموں⟫ that progression is effected through the ⟪ar:مامومین⟫ (the followers). Therefore only the Imam's ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ remains — possessed of such power that it merges into itself all the assemblies of the believers, lāhūt together with nāsūt, and progresses to the lists and thence to the forms. The ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ and the ⟪ar:فضلات⟫ (faḍalāt — residues) are left behind, and the days of the month are exhausted within the circles of reckoning.

## The Mergers of the Imams

When an Imam unites with his ⟪ar:عالی علیہ⟫ (ʿālī ʿalayhi — the one above him), the two become as a single candle. Even if many candles are joined, they constitute one candle and shine with one light. Thus, when an Imam reaches his appointed limit, the two become ⟪ar:'نورٌ واحدۃ'⟫ (nūr wāḥida — one light) after the merger. It is for this reason that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ said, ⟪ar-quote:اَناَ وَ عَلِی مِن نُور وَاحِد⟫ — "ʿAlī and I are of one light." And again: ⟪ar-quote:اَوَّلُناَ مُحَمَّد وَ اَوسَطُناَ مُحَمَّد وَ آخِرُناَ مُحَمَّد وَ کُلُّناَ مُحَمَّد وَ کُلُّناَ عَلِیٍّ⟫ — "Our first is Muḥammad, our middle is Muḥammad, our last is Muḥammad; all of us are Muḥammad, and all of us are ʿAlī." After such mergers no component may be detached from another. Only ⟪ar:'حدت'⟫ (ḥidda — acuity) remains: the more numerous the integrations, the greater the power of the Imam.[^cite-21]

None could rival the ⟪ar:حدت نظر⟫ (ḥiddat al-naẓar — sharpness of insight) of al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect). In the same manner, this sharpness of insight performs the work of the elder, of whom ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ said, ⟪ar-quote:اِتَّقُو مِن فِراَسَتِ المُؤمِن ھُوَ یَنظُر بِنُور اللہ⟫ — "Beware the discernment of the believer, for he sees by the light of God." The wisdom of the believer is his ⟪ar:'حدت نظر'⟫, and the light of God is the ⟪ar:'تائیدات'⟫ (taʾyīdāt — divine supports) of his ⟪ar:عالی علیہ⟫.

The Imam departs this world and arrives with his ⟪ar:برزق عاشر⟫ (barzaq ʿāshir — the tenth barzaq), which is the abode of every ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ (haykal — form) and of all the ⟪ar:ھیاکل⟫ (hayākil — forms). There they have their seat. The ⟪ar:برازق عاشریہ⟫ (barāziq ʿāshariyya — the tenth barzaqs) lie beneath the surface of life — the ⟪ar:فلک محیط⟫ (falak muḥīṭ — the encompassing sphere). At this station all the ⟪ar:ھیاکل⟫ come to a halt, until the seventh draws the previous six into himself and merges them within his own being; then he passes away and joins the ⟪ar:برزق عاشریہ⟫. All ⟪ar:ھیاکل⟫ abide within the ⟪ar:برزق عاشریہ⟫ in the ⟪ar:'سلطان'⟫ (sulṭān — sovereign), until the one who unites them all and develops within him brings them into the ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ (ʿālam rūḥānī — the spiritual world). The Imam does not progress through himself alone, but together with the ⟪ar:صورت⟫ (ṣūra — form) and the ⟪ar:ذات⟫ (dhāt — essence) of others. Since we have laid hold of him, he will not abandon us. God says:

> ⟪quran 35:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ أَنتُمُ ٱلْفُقَرَآءُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِۖ وَٱللَّهُ هُوَ ٱلْغَنِىُّ ٱلْحَمِيدُ ‎⟫
> *O mankind! it is you who stand in need of Allah, but Allah is Rich (Free of all wants and needs), Worthy of all praise.*
> O men, it is you who stand in need of God; and God is the All-sufficient, the All-laudable.

That is, we stand in need of the Imam, while the Imam stands in no need of us, for he is self-sufficient. He is under no obligation to take us up. The ⟪ar:محتجب⟫ (muḥtajab — those who are veiled) flourish behind the ⟪ar:حجاب⟫ (ḥijāb — veil).

## The Assembly Around the Believer

Every humble believer is also, in his own way, a master. For this reason it has been said that when a believer prays alone, two rows of angels pray behind him. This is so because he has an assembly about him, though he himself does not perceive it. Even the supplicants do not feel their own congregation. In truth, the assembly that gathers about the believer is what is called ⟪ar:'حسنات'⟫ (ḥasanāt — virtues, good deeds). The believer is superior to this assembly, and that is why the assembly has come to him. When we pray outwardly, "O God, increase our good deeds," what we are in fact praying for is the increase of our community.

### The Believer's Questions:

The assembly is bound by particular conditions. It is said that sometimes a man poses a question that is not truly his own; rather, the people in his assembly ask their questions through him, and he remains a mere medium. After death the process of living ends, but the acquisition of knowledge does not. Sometimes seekers of such knowledge come to a man who has no inclination for it, and they complain about him to their Lord. Then these forms drag the man to an elder and put forth their questions through his lips. The answers do not benefit the man himself, for he has no interest in such questions. It has been said that there are those who study the realities for years and yet, when asked even the most elementary questions, cannot answer — for they had been acquiring all their knowledge on behalf of their audience.

## The Grave and the Relationship of Its Occupant

Although the form (ṣūra) departs the body at death, its attention remains forever fixed upon that body. This is the chief purpose of pilgrimage (ziyāra). Believers go to the graves of the saints, offer prayers, and present their needs there, knowing that the grave holds the ⟪ar:ھیکل امامی⟫ (haykal imāmī — the imāmic frame), and that the form's attention rests upon what remains of its body. And since the powers of the ⟪ar:لاہوت امام⟫ (lāhūt al-imām — the divine plane of the Imam) increase greatly when they reach the grave, when believers come there and pray, their prayers are heard and granted. If the occupant of the grave is higher in rank than the pilgrim, the pilgrim benefits; if the pilgrim is the higher, then it is the occupant who benefits.[^cite-22]

The form's attention is fixed upon its own body because that body prayed and fasted at the soul's bidding, and bore the hardships of the Sharīʿa, by which its virtues became exceedingly pure. This attention of the soul remains upon its body until it is reunited with that body on the day of resurrection.

---

As the moment of his death drew near, tears welled in the eyes of ⟪ar:سیدنا مؤید أعلی اﷲ قدسہ⟫. He was asked, "My lord, do you not desire death?" He replied, "Indeed, I yearn for my own death. But I weep at leaving the house — that is, the body — in which I came to know God. Now that I depart this house, I shall meet him again only after a season. So I weep in grief over this loss."

---

The human form has been fashioned in the most excellent shape — the form of the intellectuals. Though man dwells in physical confinement, his form is filled with light, and the eyes of flesh cannot perceive it. This light is visible only to those beings who have become ⟪ar:عقول صافیۃ⟫ (ʿuqūl ṣāfiya — pure intellects) through their own clarity and purity, such as the Imams and the loftiest of supplicants. Just as the ⟪ar:قائمی صورت⟫ (qāʾimī ṣūra — the qāʾim's form) is the partial in relation to the believer's form — that is, the believer's form is a part — and the ⟪ar:قائمی ھیکل⟫ (qāʾimī haykal — the qāʾim's frame) stands over against it as the whole, so likewise the body of man is a part and his ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ (صورت)⟫ (nafs nāṭiqa — the rational soul, the form) is the whole.
