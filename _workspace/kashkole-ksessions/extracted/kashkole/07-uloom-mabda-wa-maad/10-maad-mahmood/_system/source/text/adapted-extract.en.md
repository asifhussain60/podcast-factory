## The Resurrection of the True Believer

A true believer possesses three distinct components, which stand in contrast to the three jewels of the utterance of sincerity. These three parts traverse three separate phases after death, each undergoing purification in different places and arriving at different destinations. Throughout his life, the believer cultivates the *khimīrah al-ibdāʾiyyah* (the primordial substance), and even beyond death he arranges for the believer's parts to reach the appropriate stations of cleansing and development. He transmutes the images of *lahūt* (the divine realm) into *I*, and into the *I* of *nāsūt* (the realm of humanity), developing the higher aspect from physicality into spirituality through the *nafs* (the soul).

|-----------|-----|---------------------|-------|
| The Believer's Parts | Face | Growth-Remnants | Refuse |
| Nature | Subtle | Intermediate | Gross |
| Purification Locus | Divine Realm | Celestial Spheres | Terrestrial |
| Destination | Lahūt | Nasūt | Earth |

## Mubāshir and Bashīr: The Honored Form

According to the ḥadīth of the Prophet (may God's blessings be upon him), every soul is attended at the moment of death by *al-mubāshir wa-l-bashīr* (the Bringer of Good Tidings and the Bringer of Glad Tidings) for the righteous, and by *munkar wa-nakīr* (the Denier and the Rejecter) for the wicked.[^cite-1] The reality of this divine arrangement is that the Speaker—the Guardian of all ages—manifests as *al-mubāshir wa-l-bashīr* to those who love him, while presenting as *munkar wa-nakīr* to those who harbor hatred. In the same manner, the Imam of the Age embodies these roles for his contemporaries and those beyond his time. This is why the Commander of the Faithful declared:

> ⟪ar-quote:يَا حَار هَمْدَان مَن يَّمُت يَرِنِي مِن مُومِنٍ اَو مُناَفِقٍ اَجَلا، يَعرِفُنِي اِعتَرِفِهِ وَ عاَرِفُهُ بِأِسْمِهِ وَالْكِناَ وَماَ فَعَلا⟫
> "O Hāmidān, whoever dies shall soon see me, whether he be a believer or a hypocrite; and he shall know me by his eyes, and I shall know him by his names and titles and by his deeds."

### Mubāshir and Bashīr in the Wali: The Honored Form

Human beings are scattered across the world. The *nafs al-nāmiyyah* (the growing soul), which must evolve at its core around the *qamar* (moon), develops through stages. When the *sulālah* (the seed), the *nutfah* (the sperm drop), and the *nāmiyyah* are united, perfection is achieved. At birth, the human encounters the *nafs al-ḥassiyyah* (the sensory soul) and meets the sun. In adulthood, upon taking the covenant, he receives the *nafs al-nāṭiqah* (the rational soul) from the vertical light. When a man acquires *aʿmāl ṣāliḥah* (righteous deeds) and *ʿilm ḥaqīqī* (true knowledge), he refines the *nafs al-nāṭiqah* and *nafs al-ḥassiyyah* together, preparing the *ṣūrat nūrāniyyah* (the luminous form). At the hour of death, the more he obeyed the *nafs al-ḥassiyyah*, the *nafs al-nāmiyyah*, and the *nafs al-nāṭiqah*, the more he was elevated toward the Imam's *haykal* (the temple); the remainder was placed in his grave.[^cite-2]

Every station—whether *nāṭiq* (Speaker), *wasī* (Executor), or *imām* (Imam)—transmits the vertical light emanating from the Holy World, which reaches the believer through connection with the luminous form of every saint. All goodness is bound to this vertical light. When a believer approaches death, the *shakhs nūrānī* (luminous figure) he has become through *ʿilm al-ilāhī* (divine knowledge) and *ʿamal ṣāliḥ* (righteous action) gladdens him with the knowledge of his future reward. This thought-form originates from what he has created through the *wilāyah* (spiritual guardianship) of the People of Truth and through the *ʿulūm al-ilāhiyyah* (divine sciences) and *aʿmāl ṣāliḥah* that he has wrought.

The scholar and the practitioner—the *insān fāḍil* (the perfect human), whether among the *ḥudūd kirām* (the noble limits) or the *duʿāt* (the Summoners)—continuously cultivate within themselves *akhlāq ḥasanah* (noble character) and *aʿmāl jamīlah* (beautiful deeds), existing in a state of *mulk bi-l-quwwah* (potential kingship). When death comes and the body is abandoned, this potential becomes *mulk bi-l-fiʿl* (actual kingship), and the believer enters the category of angels and reaches the *majmaʿ ʿāliyyah al-sharīfah* (the lofty noble assembly)—the honored *haykal*. Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq said, "When God raises a believer from the grave, a righteous form shall accompany that believer."[^cite-3] This form is a *malak* (an angel)—a light that perpetually watches over every part of his being, radiating throughout the entire form and ensuring that all parts develop and reach their proper station.

When a believer trembles in fear in the grave, the *malak* (the form) says to him: "Fear not! No harm shall come to you!" At that moment the believer looks upon this face and asks, "Who are you? God's mercy be upon you! You have just promised me peace, and you have fulfilled it; you have made me truthful and eased my fears." The form replies, "I am one of God's creations, made from the substance you earned through your righteous deeds toward the believers. I am here only to bring you joy today!"

The Messenger of God—may peace be upon him—said to Qays ibn Āsim: "O Qays, you have a companion with whom you shall be buried, one who shall be alive while you are dead. If you are gracious to him, he shall have mercy upon you; if you curse him, he shall deliver you unto torment, and your resurrection shall be with him. You shall be questioned about this companion. Make him virtuous! If he is good, you shall take pleasure in him; if he is evil, you shall be horrified by him. He is your *ʿamal* (deed)."

## The Return of the Spiritual Form

When a believer concludes his life with *khātimah bi-l-khayr* (a good ending), his *nafs al-nāṭiqah* (rational soul) does something more: it causes the *nafs al-nāmiyyah* (growing soul) to resemble itself, and the *nafs al-ḥassiyyah* (sensory soul) to conform to it as well. These three souls together become one entity called the *ṣūrat nūrāniyyah* (luminous form) or *ṣūrat sharīf* (the honored form). During the believer's lifetime, both were *majāwar* (neighbors), but after death they become *majāwazah* (merged) and unite into one caste, one essence, one thing. After these two are elevated, the dead body is left behind in the form of *faḍlāt* (refuse), which is buried in the grave.[^cite-4]

The honored form absorbs the light of the vertical light and connects it to the form of its exaltation. The believer's form and the form of his exalted self become one essence, remaining in service to this supreme soul. This arrangement ensures that should the supreme soul become corrupted, the form shall not be damaged. Death occurs through the movement of the beneficial agent, who causes death because the boundary form has reached completion. He hears through the believer's ear, sees through his eyes, and speaks through his tongue—answering the questions posed. As the soul advances to the highest degree, so too do all the conditions of its context progress. Even this great being eventually passes away.

**Principles**

This pattern applies to all the *jazāʾir* (islands—the individual believers). Those who have been *rāffiyah* (elevated) become incapable of committing further transgression. When a believer dies, his form merges completely with his exalted form and self.

### Whence comes the form of the Noble One?

If the honored form is ready at the moment of death, then what of the exalted form that has brought the boundary form into being? The truth is that the Divine Essence takes shape during the believer's own lifetime. The work performed in matters of the *Sharīʿah* (the sacred law) has an effect upon others through the *nafs al-ḥassiyyah* and *nafs al-nāmiyyah*, and the effect of knowledge and action influences the *nafs al-nāṭiqah*. The form of noble prayers becomes ready not only during his life but also extends outward and reaches the chapters of the Imam itself, and in its place the effect of this form is established. Those who receive this influence use it for the remainder of their lives and become extremely pious. For this reason, our master Ghulām Ḥusayn (may God sanctify his secret) pointed out that **"the womb has departed; only the trace remains."** That is, he indicated that my form has gone to my exalted limit—named 'Alī ibn Muḥsin of Shabām—and only its effect persists.

Upon the death of the Supreme Limit, the *nafs al-ḥassiyyah* is drawn up and added to its form (merging with the boundary form), and in the same manner both proceed and continue ascending through the higher limits until they reach the chapter of the Imam's *haykal*. The Imam prepares this *haykal* for his son, the Imam. This fact is stated in the Qur'ān:

> ⟪quran 89:27-30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيَّتُهَا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّة ‎ ‏ٱرْجِعِىٓ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةًۭ مَّرْضِيَّةًۭ ‎ ‏فَٱدْخُلِى فِى عِبَادِى ‎ ‏وَٱدْخُلِى جَنَّتِى ‎⟫
> *(O soul at peace! Return to your Lord, well-pleased and pleasing! Enter among My servants and enter My Paradise.)*

The speaker in this verse is the *Imām al-Zamān* (the Imam of the Time), not merely the *nafs* (soul) or the *ʿaql* (intellect) that is being addressed. The *nafs al-nāṭiqah* (rational soul) at the moment of death returns and takes refuge in him—that being from whom both the *nufūs al-ḥassiyyah wa-l-nāmiyyah* (sensory and growing souls) are satisfied and pleased.

⟪ar-quote:ارجعی الی ربک راضیۃ مرضیۃ⟫ *(Return to your Lord, well-pleased.)*

To the *rabb* (Lord) to whom both the *nufūs al-ḥassiyyah wa-l-nāmiyyah*, the *ṣūrat sharīf*, and the *nafs al-nāṭiqah* return with full consent and satisfaction.

⟪ar-quote:فادخلی فی عبادی⟫ *(Enter among My servants.)*

The term *ʿibādī* (My servants) refers to the *ḥadd ʿālī* (upper limit), this honored form. In other words, in this moment the Sharif is being commanded to merge with his Majesty.

⟪ar-quote:وَٱدْخُلِی جَنَّتِى⟫ *(And enter My Paradise.)*

The form of the Upper Limit is our first paradise, the source of our reconciliation. The second meaning is this: He came with the preposition *fī* (in) to *ʿibādī*, but did not come with *fī* to *jannātī*. This distinction indicates that although the believer's form will progress toward the *Bāb al-Abwāb* (the Gate of Gates), it merges with the Almighty immediately after death.

### The Servant serving his Master

If the believer's soul becomes corrupted, he is placed within the body. When the form of his limitation reaches him, it preserves the concept of the limit and carries it to the limits of the limit, and the self becomes corrupted. It is his reward that he served his master and preserved his imagination. Since his good imagination has departed, no force works to preserve this self, and that self becomes corrupted—as happened to Abū Bakr Ya Dāʾud ibn Qutb. Then a form emerges from his evil actions, possessing an effect through the *ḥassiyyah* and *nāmiyyah* wings: the *ṣūrat ẓalmāniyyah* (the dark form).

## The Outer Gate and the Inner Gate

An Imam prescribes two gates. The *bāb ẓāhir* (outer gate) is for the islanders, and the *bāb bātin* (inner gate) is for the gentlemen. The Outer Gate becomes the heart of the Inner Gate by gathering all the forms of the islands. At the behest of the inner gate, by means of *umm al-dhikr* (the Mother of Remembrance), all the forms of the Imam are gathered together and become the heart-son of the Imam's *haykal*. The same principle operates in every sphere: Imams become the gates of higher Imams, and the Lists above them likewise. This is referenced in *Sūrah al-Raḥmān*:

> ⟪quran 55:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهۦ جَنَّتَان ‎⟫
> *(For those who fear to stand before their Lord, there shall be two gardens.)*

This verse's *rabbih* (his Lord) refers to *ʿAlī* (peace be upon him), and the *maqāh al-rabbih* (the standing before the Lord) refers to the Imams descended from the progeny of 'Alī. The *Jannatān* (two gardens) are the *bāb bātin* (inner gate) and the *bāb ẓāhir* (outer gate). That is, he who reverences the spiritual powers of the Imam merges with both the outer and inner gates.

| Lord | Location of the Lord | Jannatān |
|------|----------------------|----------|
| ʿAlī | The Imams | Gate of Revelation + Inner Gate |

**Principles**

Gates established at the status of manifestation are presided over by our master Sulaymān (may God sanctify his secret).

The *Bāb al-Abwāb* (Gate of Gates) means the door of doors. There are twelve gates through which one reaches the Imam, the last and supreme of which is the Gate of Gates. The Imam of the Time gathers this assembly from the Bāb al-Abwāb, arranges all the forms within it, and places them upon the human form according to their rank. There no one is wronged, no one is favored, but each receives his reward according to his deeds. All these forms become like intellect in the Bāb al-Abwāb, because intellect is the essence of humanity—this is what is called *Haykal* and *Imāmah* (Imamate). When this *Haykal* or *Imāmah* son is given to the Imam, he is called the Imam. Before that, he holds the position of *Ḥujjah al-ʿUẓmā* (the Greatest Proof).

**Principles**

In cases where deviation occurs (due to sins), the Bāb al-Abwāb leaves them for future Imams, even though *khātimah bi-l-khayr* (a good ending) was the believer's right. Later Imams then cleanse them.

The light of these forms is so magnificent that the human eye cannot bear its radiance. Our master al-Muʾayyad (may God sanctify his secret) says: "If human eyes cannot endure the light of the sun, whose light is physical, how could they possibly perceive this spiritual light?"

### Ḥaḍrat Khiḍr (peace be upon him)

The term *Bāb al-Abwāb* is mentioned in connection with *Khiḍr* (peace be upon him) because the Prophet Khiḍr drank the water of life and shall therefore live until the Day of Judgment. In the same way, the Bāb al-Abwāb has absorbed all the forms of the *Nīrah* (the divine sustenance), which can never be reversed—that is, their life has become eternal. In another sense, it refers to the knowledge of the *Whole*, after which the soul can never become corrupted; that is, death (distortion) cannot befall it.

### Types of People of Truth

There are two categories of People of Truth. The first are those who know the truth through *ʿulūm al-ilāhiyyah* (divine sciences), who perform righteous deeds, and who love all the Imams, the *Nuqqabāʾ* (the Notaries), and their followers. These are the limits who, upon reaching the Imam, become the *aʿḍāʾ al-rāʾisah* (the principal organs) of the Imam's *Haykal al-Nūrāniyyah* (Luminous Temple) by virtue of their guardianship, knowledge, righteous deeds, and understanding. Within this *Haykal al-Nūrāniyyah*, the elevation and luminosity of each is proportionate to their *wilāyah*, true knowledge, and actions.

Then behind them are those who perform righteous deeds and guardianship yet do not know the truth. These pure believers occupy a lower station within the Imam's *Haykal* than the first category.

**Principles**

Our parents are literally our *ṣūrāt sharīfah* (honored forms) of the last two generations. When we were born into the last sphere and when we passed away after living our lives, the younger version of us is our mother, and the young girl in the back row is our father.

## The Return of the Subtle Essences: The Remnants of Growth

After birth in the world, the *nafs al-nāmiyyah* (growing soul) and the *nafs al-ḥassiyyah* (sensory soul) become united. The upper aspect of this compound—the *ṣūrah* (form) of the *nafs al-ḥassiyyah*—departs upward, while the lower aspect—the *nafs al-nāmiyyah*—remains within the believer's body. After the believer's death and ritual ablution, the subtle essences called the *rīḥiyyāt* (the vaporous essences) begin to emerge. After burial, they leave the body over three days, during which the entire *baqāyah sharīfah* (honored remnant) is withdrawn.

**Principles**

In the time of previous supplicants, the *rīḥiyyāt* used to emerge in the final part of the three days among the *muʾminīn mustaqīm al-sayri* (the believers of upright conduct). Our master Sulaymān (may God sanctify his secret) has said: By virtue of your sublime status, the *rīḥiyyāt* of present-day *muʾminīn mustaqīm al-sayri* immediately begin to emerge after death and depart in the first part of the three days. This is because the refuse is now cleaner than ever before, undergoing constant examination.

These *rīḥiyyāt*—the vaporous essences of the *nafs al-nāmiyyah* (growing soul)—leave the grave and travel toward the moon through moonbeams, which is the center of the *nāmiyyah*. For this reason, on the fourteenth day the moon is filled with light. During the *ayyām al-bīḍ* (the white nights—the 13th, 14th, and 15th), the *nāmiyyah* gathers in the moon. Whatever *nāmiyyah* he continues to collect with *Qamar* (the Moon), he keeps handing over to *Shams* (the Sun) little by little until he delivers it in its entirety.[^cite-5] The ascending days extend from the 1st to the 12th. The *rīḥiyyāt* are assembled in the moon on the 13th, 14th, and 15th, and then are revealed to the world from the 16th onward.

| Period | The Movements of Nufūs al-Nāmiyyah in Qamar |
|--------|----------------------------------------------|
| 1–12 | The Days of the Ascension of Nufūs al-Nāmiyyah |
| 13–15 | The Gathering of Nufūs al-Nāmiyyah in the Moon |
| 16 onward | The Day of the Manifestation of the Soul in the World |

**Principles**

When the pure concept of supplication is elevated, the *nafs al-ḥassiyyah* and *nafs al-nāmiyyah* ascend even further. Some rise so high that their *quwwat al-admighah* (cerebral powers) and the parts of the heart also develop.

What emerges from the body as the *nafs al-rīḥiyyah* (the vaporous soul) is subtle, though it remains physical in nature. Therefore, at the moment of the believer's death, it remains within the body and then leaves the grave, traveling to the moon for cleansing. The moon hands them over, through Mercury and Venus, to *Shams* (the Sun) for further purification. The *nafs al-rīḥiyyah* endures the heat of the sun and suffers torment that expiates his sins. Instead of the heat of hell, they are given the heat of the sun, which is far less severe. They remain in the sun until the faces of their adversaries reach the Bāb al-Abwāb. When they reach the sun, the *rīḥiyyāt* are compressed and punished. The sun melts and *takhammara* (ferments) them so that they are able to join the *naswah* (the sperm-essence) of the coming Imam. All this is done so that the *nafs al-rīḥiyyah* (vaporous soul) may become one with those who have reached Divine Proximity through the Imam's *Lahūt*.

**Principles**

In the human body, the first *ḥarārah ghārīziyyah* (innate heat) is developed, which forms in the *nafs al-ḥassiyyah* in the next sphere. The *dam al-qirmīzī* (red blood) is developed by its nourishment in the mother's womb in the next sphere.

These *nufūs al-rīḥiyyāt* fall within the circle of the sun, which is the essence of all heat and the center of all *asjām ghārīziyyah* (innate bodies). All the bodies that have descended from heaven have been gathered in the sun, which supplies their heat and light. In the same manner, these bodies were deposited in the moon, which provided them coolness and soothing.

| Sun | Moon |
|-----|------|
| Innate Heat + Dryness | Humidity + Coolness |

After this purification, the sun hands over the essence to Jupiter, which is called *al-saʿd al-akbar* (the Greater Fortune). Although Mars stands above the sun, these essences do not reach this star, for it is a malevolent star; the sun refers them directly to Jupiter. In Jupiter, the essence becomes extremely transparent and clear. After this purification, the pure and transparent essence returns to the sun and cools.

When the Imam intends that his *khalīfah* (caliph)—the son who shall later become the Imam—be born, then *al-mudabbir ʿālam al-ṭabīʿiyyah* (the Administrator of the Natural World, the Universal Intellect) moves his tongue and hands over this pure essence from *Mushtarī* (Jupiter, the *Al-Saʿd al-Akbar*) to the sun, and from the sun to the moon through solar rays. The moon drops this transparent substance in the form of fruit (which fire has not touched) or as dew drops upon pure water. The juices of this *ʿaṣīr* (essence) are in fact the *rīḥiyyāt*.

**Principles**

Maulana 'Alī was born from an apple, and the Prophet (may God bless him) was born from pure water, as was the Lady Fāṭimah.

This substance—whether fruit or water—is guarded by angels until it reaches the place of miracle, that is, the Imam and his *Zūj* (*Umm al-Dhikr*, the Mother of Remembrance). The Imam and his wife are holy vessels prepared for the coming Imam. When both consume it and receive their portion from it, both become *māddah al-talwīdiyyah* (the procreative substance, the *sulālah*). They receive the *rīḥiyyāt* through the nose, and his wife through the mouth; their joint consumption of this fruit constitutes their act of marriage. Then, through the marital act, the Imam's wife receives sperm from the Imam, which produces the Imam's son. In this way, a subtle part of the essence—refined by the sun's *takhammara* (fermentation) and the movement of the celestial bodies—joins the sperm of the Imam's son through *ʿināyah al-ilāhiyyah* (divine care) and becomes the *nafs al-nāmiyyah* of the Imam's son.

During pregnancy, the mother of the Imam's son receives all the nourishment—the *rīḥiyyāt*—that reaches his body and accumulates near the navel. The Imam's son receives his sustenance from this source, just as ordinary human beings do. Since the Imam and the *Bātūlah* (the Pure One) themselves are exalted personalities, their affairs—the act of marriage, the period of pregnancy, the birth of the child—are their *ḥijāb* (veils) and exemplary demonstrations.

The Imam's wife becomes a holy vessel for the Imam's son. When the wife collects the *rīḥiyyāt* from the sperm, she creates the Imam's son. During childbirth, the Imam's wife receives everything—the *rīḥiyyāt*—she obtains. Those that reach the higher Imam come to his wife; those that reach the lower Imam come to the wife of the lower Imam. Extracts are produced from these nourishments that sustain the sperm. The Imam's mother is supremely pure and free from all the impurities that ordinary women possess. They are called *muṭahhirāt* (the Purified Ones). This is why God says (in the interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 33:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَرْنَ فِى بُيُوتِكُنَّ وَلَا تَبَرَّجْنَ تَبَرُّجَ ٱلْجَهِلِيَّة ٱلْأُولَىٰۖ وَأَقِمْنَ ٱلصَّلَوٰة وَآتِينَ ٱلزَّكَوٰة وَأَطِعْنَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهۚۥٓ إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ ٱلرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ ٱلْبَيْتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمْ تَطْهِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *(And stay in your houses, and do not display yourselves as in the days of ignorance. Establish prayer and pay the poor-rate, and obey God and His Messenger. God only desires to remove uncleanness from you, O people of the house, and to purify you with a thorough purification.)*

**Principles**

Our body is composed of fire, air, water, and earth. The Imam's body is composed of the most subtle part of the believers' *nafs al-nāmiyyah*, and his divinity (*lāhūt*) is composed of the forms (*ṣūwar*) of these believers. Until the *nasūt* is perfected, the *lāhūt* is not complete.

### The Speed of Growth

When the soul of a pious believer dies, it comes to him in the form of his Imam—*al-Imām al-Ḥaqq*, the owner of the entire universe, whose *khiyāl* (imagination/thought) leads the believer's form to the spirit of Paradise. The *rīḥiyyāt* emerge from the grave, cross the sky, and reach the moon. ### Why Does the Imam Come Under the Scope of Qiṣāṣ (Retribution)?

The Imam's innocence—that which *muʾathir* (influences)—accepts the influence; his soul and form are *muʾaththar* (influenced). For this reason, the Imam becomes sick, grieves, suffers wounds, sheds tears, and must be buried. Yet his soul, which is so great, influences and rules over the heavens. Therefore, there is an Imam who is *mudabbir* (the Administrator) and an Imam who is *mudabbar* (the Administered).

Because the Imam's grandchildren are composed of the *rīḥiyyāt* of believers, the Imam comes under the scope of *Qiṣāṣ* (retribution). Although the *rīḥiyyāt* are brought to the Imam in a very clean and pure state, there is still a slight reckoning. Therefore, the Imam's body is *muʾaththir* (influenced), but his soul is master of the celestial spheres.

## Reversal of the Hard Bones of the Summoners

After ⟪ar:مومن مستقیم السیر⟫ a straight-walking believer is interred, it takes three hundred years for his excrement to emerge from the grave. By contrast, the ⟪ar:نواسیت الدعات⟫ cosmic embodiments of the Summoners (*duʿāt*) require but thirty years for their excrement to be perfected. The duration for the higher ranks—the prayers and arguments of the lofty—is even briefer. ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ The Gate of Gates (the Imam) dissolves his physical form in twenty-one years; the body of the Imam proper requires only three days. ⟪ar:پیغمبر (ﷺ)⟫ The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) required but a moment for his excrement to be clarified. As soon as he was buried, all his waste ⟪ar:ریحیۃ⟫ was instantly dispersed. He had attained such purity and sanctity that no trace of the grave's influence remained upon his blessed form. In his place was left a ⟪ar:عبد اللہ ابن رواحا⟫ veiling shroud bearing the torment of the grave.[^cite-15]

**Primary Facts:**

The higher Summoners (preachers and lofty prayers) also possess excellences, but their excellences are inferior to those of the Imam.

The bodies of the Summoners are returning to the bodies of the Imams and their organs are being reformed—a matter of supreme nobility. That is, the waste of these Summoners becomes so refined that they ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ are incorporated into the palace of the Imam. The Imam's form is constituted of these purified ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ essences.

⟪ar:حدود علیہ السلام⟫ The *ḥudūd* (divine limits) are servants of the manifest and the hidden worlds. At the time of death, their celestial subtleties ascend to the spiritual realm, and what little ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ generative soul remains in the body emerges from the grave for ⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ three days as a fragrant essence and ascends to the sun. From the body depart the tail, flesh, muscles, veins, and all other parts except the hard bones, hair, and nails, which then ascend to the zodiacal mansions and assume the stewardship of one of the constellations. The residues of that constellation descend to earth and dissolve into dust. Then, through ⟪ar:مزاج ممتزج⟫ cosmic fermentation, they transform into sustenance and drink that is untouched by fire. From there, ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫ they are resurrected as *nutfah* (the sperm-drop); when invited by the Summoners, they accept and enter the ranks of the saints. They progress and advance within the summons (*daʿwa*, the invitation to faith) until they attain the station of a *ḥadd* (divine limit) serving within the daʿwa itself. The rank they possessed in the former sphere is preserved, yet the acceleration of their progress elevates them to the next degree.[^cite-16]

**Primary Facts:**

Yet we are so burdened with sin that the Imam must select the lowest ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ essences from among us and assume that form when meeting us. ## What Station Shall the Believer Hold in the Next Cosmic Cycle?

A man descends from the zodiacal spheres and enters the world anew after one thousand years. ⟪ar:عمود نور⟫ The Pillar of Light initiates his cosmic reformation so that he ⟪ar:عہد شریف⟫ may attain a station worthy of his noble covenant. When he reaches maturity, he is summoned to the truth. He accepts this invitation, his covenant is taken, and he advances gradually through the ranks of religion and in true knowledge. With the epoch of the Noble One, his vertical axis merges with the connection of Light. This bond shall remain a shadow of him—⟪ar:حسیہ⟫⟪ar:نامیہ⟫ his sensory and generative faculties—for the remainder of his life. He shall have no doubt and shall ⟪ar:'یقین'⟫ possess unshakeable certainty forever, for his first sphere concluded in certainty. Once a cosmic cycle ⟪ar:ناطقہ⟫ endowed with the speaking principle is born, it can never cease.[^cite-21]

**Origin and Duration:**

⟪ar:خمیرۃ الأبدائیۃ⟫ We are left with the primordial leaven, by which ⟪ar:عالم نیات⟫ we possess knowledge of the world of souls, together with ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ primary perfection—namely knowledge, power, and life. The name of the connection to this ⟪ar:خمیرۃ الأبدائیۃ⟫ primordial leaven is ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ the speaking principle.

He continues ascending the ladder of advancement until he attains the primary station he possessed in the former sphere. For instance, a person who achieved the rank of ⟪ar:'داعی'⟫ Summoner in the previous sphere shall be transported by his nature to the second ⟪ar:داعی⟫ degree of Summoner in the next sphere. One who held the station of ⟪ar:'باب'⟫ Gate shall likewise become this *daffāh* (the afforder) ⟪ar:باب⟫ Gate in the new cycle. Then, through struggle and effort, he progresses to the next rank.

A believer cannot transcend his station until he has perfected the ten degrees within it. There are ten levels in each grade. When the believer appears in the next sphere, he crosses one level. For example, if a believer occupied the fifth degree of faith in this sphere, upon manifestation in the next, he shall occupy the sixth degree of faith. Even if that believer acquires extensive knowledge in this sphere, he shall still be born in the next sphere as a believer. When he has completed the ten degrees of the believer grade, ⟪ar:مکاسرت⟫ he shall enter the first rank above. There are only two degrees for martyrs. A portion ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ of the heart forms in the back of a sphere. Those who are lofty or who attain martyrdom—both their heart and liver become soul. But this applies only to those of whom God says (interpretation of the meaning):

### The Ten Degrees of Faith

Faith is the designation of degrees, stations, and categories. Islam is the sole grade that ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ possesses primary perfection. But the moment one enters faith, ranks are established, which is the state of ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ secondary perfection. There are ten degrees within each faith-stage. The world of ⟪ar:لِم⟫ his ⟪ar:'۱۰'⟫ numbers existed in the spiritual realm. Because there are ten intellects, there are ten degrees here. This is what the Quran expresses: ⟪ar-quote:تِلْكَ عَشَرَةٌۭ كَامِلَ⟫ "That number of ten is perfect."

In addition, he prays: ⟪ar:بین المبدع و المبدع کلمۃ⟫ There is a word between ⟪ar:مبدِع⟫ the Originator and ⟪ar:'نور کلمہ'⟫ the light of utterance, and ⟪ar:مبدَع⟫ the originated. When the First Intellect attained the monotheism of the Creator and achieved *kamāl thānī* (secondary perfection), he was overwhelmed by ten veils of bliss in which he erred. He is yet engaged in lifting these very sheaths. This is why the Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said: ⟪ar-quote:مَن عَرَفَ نَفسُہُ فَقَد عَرَفَ رَبُّہُ⟫ "He who knows his own soul has known his Lord." ### Key Summary

When he has collected all the parts of his sphere, he dies and his original entity (⟪ar:صورت + نفس حسیہ⟫ form and sensory soul) is separated from him eternally. ## The Apportionment of the Imam's Corporeal Manifestation

When the Imam is entombed, the Imam's body undergoes no corruption and emits no odour; when interred, it remains in the earth no longer than three days. His body is ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ constituted of refined essences, and the essence itself is ⟪ar:ریحیہ⟫ fragrant. Since the ⟪ar:ناسوت⟫ embodiment is primordial ⟪ar:'ریحیات'⟫ in its essence, they remain imprisoned for three days. ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ How can the body of a believer remain interred for more than three days when it departs the body in its original state? The body of a Summoner remains in the grave for thirty years, while the body of a believer remains for three hundred years.

## The Mergers of the Imams

When an Imam merges with ⟪ar:عالی علیہ⟫ the Sublime One, the two become like a candle; even if many candles are united, they form one candle with but a single light. For this reason ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the Messenger of God (upon whom be peace and blessings) said: ⟪ar-quote:اَناَ وَ عَلِی مِن نُور وَاحِد⟫ "ʿAlī and I are of one light." Or he declared: ⟪ar-quote:اَوَّلُناَ مُحَمَّد وَ اَوسَطُناَ مُحَمَّد وَ آخِرُناَ مُحَمَّد وَ کُلُّناَ مُحَمَّد وَ کُلُّناَ عَلِیٍّ⟫ "Our first is Muḥammad, our middle is Muḥammad, our last is Muḥammad; all of us are Muḥammad, and all of us are ʿAlī."
