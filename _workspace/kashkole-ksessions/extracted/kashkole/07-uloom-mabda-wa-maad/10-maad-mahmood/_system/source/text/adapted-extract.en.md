## The Return of the True Believer

A true believer possesses three distinct parts, which correspond inversely to the three jewels of the shahāda (declaration of faith). These three parts traverse three separate phases after death. Each undergoes purification at different cosmic stations, with distinct destinations. Throughout his earthly life, the believer develops what is called the ⟪ar:خمیرۃ الأبدائیۃ⟫ (the primordial ferment, or original spiritual substance). Even after death, the Divine arrangements ensure that each part of the believer reaches its proper station for cleansing and development. The believer transforms the images of ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ (the divine realm) into the ⟪ar:ناسوت⟫ (the physical realm), and develops ⟪ar:فضلات⟫ (excesses or vaporous essences) and ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (subtle spiritual emanations), elevating the higher aspect of ⟪ar:(نفس)⟫ (the soul) from physicality to spirituality.

## Mubashir and Bashir: The Noble Form

According to hadith transmitted from ⟪ar:آنحضرت صلعم⟫ (the Prophet, peace be upon him), every soul receives at the moment of death the presence of ⟪ar:'مبشروبشیر'⟫ and ⟪ar:'منکر و نکیر'⟫ (the Bringers of Good Tidings and those who Question, and the Deniers and those who Reject)—bearers of gladness for the righteous, interrogators of the wicked. The reality of this doctrine teaches that the Speaker (al-Nātiq, the Prophet) and the Guardian of all ages serves as ⟪ar:مبشر و بشیر⟫ (mubashir and bashīr, the bringer of good news and the announcer of joy) to those who love them, while functioning as ⟪ar:منکر و نکیر⟫ (munkar and nakīr, the questioner and the denier) to those who reject them. Similarly, the Imam of the Age stands as both for his contemporaries and all others.[^cite-1] This is why ⟪ar:مولانا امیر المومنین⟫ (Mawlānā ʿAmīr al-Muʾminīn, the Commander of the Faithful) declared to one who would transmit the knowledge:

> ⟪ar-quote:یاَ حاَرِ ہَمداَن مَن یَّمُت یَرِنِی مِن مُومِنٍ اَو مُناَفِقٍ اَجَلا، یَعرِفُنیِ اِعتَرِفِہِ وَ عاَرِفُہُ بِأِسمِہٖ وَالْکُناَ وَماَ فَعَلا⟫
>
> "O ⟪ar:حار ھمدان⟫ Hār of Hamdān! Whoever dies shall soon behold me, be he believer or hypocrite. He knows me by his recognition and I know him by his names, his epithets, and his deeds."

### The Mubashir and Bashir in the Case of the Wali

Human beings are scattered throughout creation. Each possesses a ⟪ar:'نفس نامیہ'⟫ (nāfs nāmiya, a growing or vital soul) that must evolve. At its core dwells the ⟪ar:قمر⟫ (the Moon), the celestial correspondent of this faculty. When the ⟪ar:سلالہ⟫ (essence or genetic material) and ⟪ar:نطفہ⟫ (the sperm-drop) undergo development through ⟪ar:نامیہ⟫ (vital growth), the infant is born into the world. At that moment, he encounters the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ (nafs ḥassāsa, the sensory soul). When maturity arrives and the covenant is taken, he receives illumination from the vertical light of truth. As the believer acquires ⟪ar:اعمال صالحہ⟫ (righteous deeds) and ⟪ar:علم حقیقی⟫ (true knowledge), the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ (nafs nāṭiqa, the rational soul) becomes refined. Through this refinement, a ⟪ar:'صورت نورانی'⟫ (a luminous form) is prepared—a gathering that combines the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫, ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫, and ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ into a unified ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ (noble form). At the hour of death, as the degree of obedience increases, so too does the elevation of this noble form to the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ (hikal, the luminous temple-form), while the remainder is left behind in the grave.[^cite-2]

Each ⟪ar:مقام (ناطق، وصی، امام)⟫ (station—whether Speaker, Legatee, or Imam) generates a vertical light from the Sacred World that reaches the believer through union with the form of every saint. All good things are linked to this vertical radiance. When the moment of a believer's death arrives, a ⟪ar:'شخص نورانی'⟫ (luminous person) manifests—one made of ⟪ar:علم الہی⟫ (divine knowledge) and ⟪ar:عمل صالح⟫ (righteous action). The believer rejoices upon seeing this form and knows with certainty that good awaits him. This luminous figure is none other than the form he has cultivated through the wilāya (guardianship) of the People of Truth and through ⟪ar:علوم الہیہ⟫ (divine sciences) and ⟪ar:اعمال صالحہ⟫ (righteous deeds).

The ⟪ar:انسان فاضل (حدود کرام، دعات)⟫ (the virtuous human—among whom are the Noble Limits and the Summoners) who embodies ⟪ar:اخلاق حسنہ⟫ (virtuous character) and ⟪ar:اعمال جمیلہ⟫ (beautiful deeds) perpetually fashions himself in his own image. He exists in a state of ⟪ar:ملک بالقوۃ⟫ (kingship in potentiality). Yet when death comes and he departs his body, that ⟪ar:ملک بالقوۃ⟫ becomes ⟪ar:ملک بالفعل⟫ (kingship in actuality), and he enters the rank of the angels and reaches the ⟪ar:مجمع عالیہ الشریفہ (ھیکل شریف)⟫ (the exalted noble assembly, or noble luminous temple). For this reason, ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ (Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, peace be upon him) declared: "When Allāh raises a believer from the grave, a beauteous form shall emerge with that believer."[^cite-3] This form is a luminous ⟪ar:'ملک'⟫ (angelic being) that perpetually gazes upon every part of the believer's body, radiating throughout and ensuring that all parts develop and reach their appointed station. When a believer becomes fearful in the grave, this ⟪ar:ملک (صورت)⟫ (angelic form) says to him: "Fear not! No harm shall come to you!" The believer gazes upon this countenance and asks: "Who are you? May God's mercy be upon you! You promised me peace, and you have fulfilled that promise. You have made me truthful and eased my fears." The form replies: "I am one of God's creations, fashioned by you from the deeds of goodness you performed toward the believers. Today I bring you joy!"

The Messenger of Allāh (peace be upon him) said to ⟪ar:قیس ابن آسم⟫ (Qays ibn ʾĀsim): "O Qays! You possess a companion with whom you shall be buried—while you shall be dead, he shall be alive. If you are virtuous, he shall show you mercy; if you are cursed, he shall deliver you unto torment. Your resurrection shall be with him, and you shall be questioned about him. Make this companion virtuous! If he is good, you shall delight in him; if he is evil, you shall recoil from him in horror. He is your ⟪ar:'عمل '⟫ (work, your deeds)."

## The Return of the Spiritual Form

When ⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫ (the believer ends in goodness) and the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫ (rational soul) departs, it directs the ⟪ar:سیقلدار⟫ (perhaps a subtle essence or coordinator) such that the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ (vital soul) and ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ (sensory soul) are fashioned in its likeness. These three beings become unified into what is called the ⟪ar:'صورت نورانی'⟫ or the ⟪ar:'صورت شریف'⟫ (luminous or noble form). In the believer's earthly life, these two were neighbors (mujāwar), but after death they become spouses (mujāwaz) and unite into a single caste, substance, and essence. When the two ascend, the physical corpse—the ⟪ar:فضلات⟫ (waste or gross matter)—remains behind to be interred in the grave.

This believer's form absorbs the light of the vertical radiance and becomes connected to the form of the Highest Limit. The believer's form and the form of his exalted self merge into a single soul and remain subservient to the soul of the Highest Limit, such that should that loftiest soul become corrupted, the form remains unharmed. Death occurs through the departure of the vital principle, which causes the death of the form once the boundary-form has reached its completion. Yet even this noble being hears through his ear, sees through his eye, and speaks with his tongue in response to the questioning that comes.[^cite-4]

As the soul progresses through the highest degrees, so too do the conditions of its context transform. Yet even these exalted beings must face death.

For all ⟪ar:جزائر⟫ (the islands or inhabitants of the soul), there exists a principle: the ⟪ar:حضرات⟫ (the Presences or Dignities) who have undergone such elevation become incapable of committing further transgression. When the ⟪ar:حضرات⟫ believer dies, his form merges with his exalted form and soul. At that moment, the union of ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ and ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ in their refined state becomes complete.

### The Origin of the Noble Form

If the noble form is prepared at the moment of death, what then is the exalted form from which the boundary-form arises? The truth is that the Divine Essence takes shape within the believer's lifetime. The work accomplished in the affairs of the Sharīʿa produces an effect upon the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ and the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫, while knowledge and action exercise an effect upon the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫. The form of great prayer becomes prepared not only during life but also radiates outward and reaches the chapters (divisions) of the Imam's being. In place of this form, its influence is established. The people use this influence for the remainder of their lives and become exceedingly pious. For this reason, ⟪ar:سیدنا غلام حسین صاحب اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ (our Master Ghulām Ḥusayn, may God elevate his station) indicated: "The womb is gone; only its margin remains!" That is, the form has ascended to my exalted limit, leaving only its effect behind. By this saying, he alluded to how ⟪ar:(سیدنا علی بن محسن شبام)⟫ (our Master ʿAlī ibn Muḥsin of Shabām) understood that the subtle essence persists even after the vessel departs.

Upon the death of the Supreme Limit, the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ is drawn upward and united with its form (which merges the boundary-form), and in this manner both ascend continually, returning to higher limits until they reach the chapter of the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ (luminous form). This ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ prepares the son of the Imam for his station in ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ (the divine realm). This truth is expressed in the Qur'ān:

> ⟪quran 89:27-30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيَّتُهَا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ‎ ‏ٱرْجِعِىٓ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةًۭ مَّرْضِيَّةًۭ ‎ ‏فَٱدْخُلِى فِى عِبَدِى ‎ ‏وَٱدْخُلِى جَنَّتِى ‎⟫
>
> *(To the pious soul it will be said): "O soul at peace! Return to your Lord, well-pleased and well-pleasing to Him! Enter among My honoured servants, and enter My Paradise!"*

The address ⟪ar-quote:يَٓأَيَّتُھا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّۃ⟫ refers to the soul at perfect peace. Here the Speaker is the Imam of the Age ⟪ar:امام الزمان⟫, not merely the ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ (soul) nor the ⟪ar:'عقل'⟫ (intellect) that is being addressed. The ⟪ar:نفس⟫ (nafs, the soul) at the time of death is the ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ (rational soul) that draws refuge, together with ⟪ar:نفوس حسیۃ و نامیۃ⟫ (the sensory and vital souls), both of which become satisfied and pleased.

⟪ar-quote:ارجعی الی ربک راضیۃ مرضیۃ⟫ (Return to your Lord, well-pleased):
The ⟪ar:نفوس حسیۃ و نامیۃ⟫ along with the ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ and the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫ return to the ⟪ar:'رب'⟫ (Lord—understood here as the Imam) with full consent and satisfaction.

⟪ar-quote:فادخلی فی عبادی⟫ (Enter among My servants):
The ⟪ar:عِباَدِی⟫ (My servants) refers to this noble form. In other words, the Sharīf is commanded to merge with the Majesty!

⟪ar-quote:وَٱدْخُلِی جَنَّتِی⟫ (And enter My Paradise):
The form of the Upper Limit constitutes our first paradise, from which our form gains peace. The ⟪ar:جَنَّتِی⟫ second meaning is the ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ (the Gate of Gates). He came with ⟪ar:'فی'⟫ (with, in) the Garden, but he did not come with ⟪ar:'فی'⟫ the Gate of Gates. This indicates that although the form of a believer progresses toward the Day of the Month and the ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫, it merges with the Almighty immediately after death.

### The Servant Serving His Master

If the believer's soul is corrupted, he is placed in the body as a prisoner. When the form of his boundary-limit reaches him, it saves the essence of the limit, takes it to the limits beyond, and the self becomes corrupted. His reward is that he served his master and preserved his imagination. Since his righteous imagination departs, no power works to save this soul; therefore it becomes corrupted, as happened to ⟪ar:ابو بکر⟫ Yā (referring to some individual) and ⟪ar:داؤد بن قطب⟫ (Dāwūd ibn Quṭb). Then there forms from his evil deeds a ⟪ar:صورت ظلمانیۃ⟫ (dark form) whose effects are manifest as ⟪ar:حسیۃ⟫ (sensory) and ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ (vital) emanations.

## The Outer Gate and the Inner Gate

Every Imam prescribes two chapters: to the outer islanders goes the ⟪ar:'باب ظاہر'⟫ (the Outer Gate), and to the people of subtlety goes the ⟪ar:'باب باطن'⟫ (the Inner Gate). The Outer Gate becomes the heart of the Inner Gate by gathering all the forms of the islands. At the behest of the inner chapter, all the ⟪ar:اُم الذکر⟫ (the Mother of the Remembrance or feminine principle of Truth) forms of the Imam unite, becoming the ⟪ar:'قلب'⟫ (heart) of the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ (luminous temple). The same principle operates throughout every sphere: the Imams become the gates of greater Imams, and the Lists stand above them. This truth is referred to in the Qur'ān:

> ⟪quran 55:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِۦ جَنَّتَانِ ‎⟫
>
> *And for him who fears to stand before his Lord, there shall be two gardens.*

This verse refers to ⟪ar:علی علیہ السلام⟫ (ʿAlī, peace be upon him), and ⟪ar:'مَقَامَ رَبِّہ'⟫ (the standing before his Lord) signifies the Imams descended from the progeny of ʿAlī. ⟪ar:'جَنَّتاَنِ'⟫ (The two gardens) have other meanings: that is, the one who fears the spiritual powers of the Imam is merged with both the ⟪ar:باب باطن⟫ (inner gate) and the ⟪ar:باب ظاہر⟫ (outer gate).[^cite-5]

| **Lord** | **Location of Rabbah** | **Jannatan** |

| **ʿAlī** | **Imams descended from him** | **Outer Gate + Inner Gate** |

Chapters are established on the station of manifestation. As ⟪ar:سیدنا سلیمان صاحب أعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ (our Master Sulaymān, may God elevate his station) has indicated, the ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ (Gate of Gates) means the 'door of doors.' Twelve gates lead to the Imam, the last of which is the ⟪ar:'باب الابواب'⟫ itself. The Imam of the Age draws this gathering from the Bāb al-Abwāb and arranges all the forms within it, placing them upon the human body according to their rank. No one is wronged, no one is favored; everyone receives his reward according to his deeds. All these forms become like intellect within the Bāb al-Abwāb, for intellect is the essence of humanity. This is what is called the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ and the ⟪ar:'امامت'⟫ (imamate). When this ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ or ⟪ar:امامت⟫ is given to the son, he is called the ⟪ar:'امام'⟫ (Imam). Before that, he holds the station of ⟪ar:حجت عظمی⟫ (the Greatest Proof).

In cases where deviation occurs due to sins, the Bāb al-Abwāb defers them for future Imams, even though they were the ⟪ar:خاتمہ بالخیر⟫ (endings in goodness) of the believer. Later Imams then purify them.

The light of these forms is so immense that the human eye cannot bear its brilliance. ⟪ar:سیدنا مؤید اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ (our Master al-Muʾayyad, may God elevate his station) said: "If human eyes cannot bear the light of the physical sun, how then could they behold this spiritual light?"

### Hazrat Khiḍr (peace be upon him)

It is said that in the term of invitation, the ⟪ar:خذر علیہ السلام⟫ (Prophet Khiḍr, peace be upon him) is mentioned because the Prophet Khiḍr drank from the water of life and shall therefore remain until the Day of Judgment. Similarly, the Bāb al-Abwāb has absorbed all the forms of Nīra (perhaps meaning the subtle essences of the believers), which can never be reversed—that is, their life has become eternal. In another sense, it refers to the knowledge of the whole after which the soul can never become corrupt; that is, death (distortion) cannot befall it.[^cite-6]

### Types of the People of Truth

Two categories of the People of Truth exist. The first are those who possess ⟪ar:علوم الہیۃ⟫ (divine sciences), true knowledge, righteous deeds, and love for all the Imams, the Nāṭiqs (Speakers), and their followers. These noble ones, when they reach the Imam, become ⟪ar:اعضاء رئیسۃ⟫ (vital organs) of the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانیہ⟫ (luminous temple) through their wilāya (guardianship), knowledge, righteous action, and understanding. The elevation and luminosity of each corresponds to their wilāya, true knowledge, and deeds.

Behind them are those who perform righteous works and practice wilāya but lack true knowledge. These pure believers occupy a lower ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ station within the Imam's ⟪ar:نورانی ھیکل⟫ (luminous form) than the first category.

Our parents are literally our ⟪ar:صورات شریفہ⟫ (noble forms) from the last two generations. When we were born in the final sphere and passed away after living our span, the younger aspect of us becomes our mother, and the aspect in the rear position becomes our father.

## The Return of the Subtle Essences: The Remainder of the Vital Soul

After birth into the world, the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ (vital soul) and ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ (sensory soul) become unified. The upper aspect of this union departs together with the ⟪ar:(نفس حسیۃ) صورت⟫ (form of the sensory soul), while the lower aspect—the ⟪ar:(نفس نامیۃ)⟫ (vital soul)—remains within the believer's body. After death and ritual washing, subtle ⟪ar:(ریحیات)⟫ (essences or ethereal emanations) begin to emerge, and following burial, the body remains for three days. During this period, the entire ⟪ar:بقایہ شریفہ⟫ (noble remainder) is extracted.[^cite-7]

In the time of previous Imams, the ⟪ar:مؤمنین مستقیم السیر⟫ (believers proceeding straight) would have their ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ emerge in the final portion of those three days. Through the grace of ⟪ar:سیدنا سلیمان صاحب⟫ (our Master Sulaymān), the effect of his splendor is such that in the present age, the ⟪ar:مؤمنین مستقیم السیر⟫ and their ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ begin to emerge immediately after death and depart in the first portion of the three days. This is because the waste is now purer than ever before, undergoing constant refinement.

These ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ and ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ (the subtle emanations and the vital soul of the believers) emerge from the grave and ascend to the Moon ⟪ar:قمر⟫ through lunar rays, which is the celestial house of the ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ (the vital principle). For this reason, on the 14th, the moon becomes fully illumined. During the ⟪ar:ایام بِیض⟫ (the white days—the 13th, 14th, and 15th), the ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ gathers in the moon. Whatever the ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ collects with the Moon, it gradually hands over to the Sun, until the entire transfer is accomplished. The ascending days run from the 1st to the 12th. The ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ gather in the moon on the 13th, 14th, and 15th, and then are revealed to the world from the 16th to the month's end.

| **Phase** | **The Movements of the Vital Soul in the Moon** |

When the refined concept of prayer is elevated, the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ and ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ ascend even further with it. Some are so elevated that their ⟪ar:قُوَّۃِ اَدمِغَۃ⟫ (the power of their brains and subtle faculties) and parts of the heart also develop.

What emerges from the body is ⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ (the ethereal soul or subtle essence), bitter yet still a soul, though of a physical nature. Therefore, at the moment of the believer's death, it remains in the body, then emerges from the grave and ascends to the moon for purification. The moon hands this essence over through Mercury and Venus to ⟪ar:شمس⟫ (the Sun) for further cleansing. The ⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ endures the heat of the sun and suffers a torment that serves as expiation for sins. Instead of the heat of hellfire, they are given the heat of the sun—far less severe than infernal flames. They remain in the sun until the faces of their opponents reach the Bāb al-Abāb. When they arrive at the sun, the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ are squeezed and refined. These subtle essences are transformed, melted through ⟪ar:تخمیر⟫ (fermentation or purification by heat), so that they are able to join the naswat (physical composition) of the coming Imam. All this occurs so that the ⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ becomes equal to one who has reached the Divine Imam—a proximity to Lahūt that is most fortunate indeed.

In the human body, the first ⟪ar:حرارت غریزی⟫ (latent or innate heat) develops, formed in the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ sphere. The ⟪ar:دم قرمیزی⟫ (crimson blood) and ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ are developed by feeding the fetal essence in the mother's womb.

The ⟪ar:نفوس ریحیات⟫ fall within the circle of the sun, which is the essence of all heat and the center of all ⟪ar:اجسام غریزیۃ⟫ (innate bodies). All bodies descended from heaven have been gathered in the sun, concentrated in its heat and light. In like manner, these bodies were deposited in the moon, which imparted coolness and soothing moisture.

After this purification, the Sun hands the essence over to Jupiter, which bears the name ⟪ar:'سعد اکبر'⟫ (the Greater Fortune). Though Mars stands above the Sun, these essences do not reach that star, for it is a malefic planet. The Sun transfers directly to Jupiter, where the essence becomes very transparent and refined. After this cleansing, the transparent and pure essence returns to the sun and there is set in waiting.

When the Imam intends that his successor (the son who will later become Imam) be born, the ⟪ar:مدبر عالم طبعیۃ⟫ (the Regulator of the Natural World, the Intellect of Nature) moves his tongue and causes this purified and excellent essence from ⟪ar:مشتری (سعد اکبر)⟫ (Jupiter, the Greater Fortune) to be handed to ⟪ar:زہرہ (سعد اصغر)⟫ (Venus, the Lesser Fortune) through the rays of the sun and moon. The moon drops this transparent substance in the form of a fruit (untouched by fire) or as dew drops upon pure water. The ⟪ar:عصیر⟫ (juice or essence) of this fruit is in fact ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (subtle essences).

The tradition teaches that ⟪ar:بی بی فاطمۃ⟫ (Lady Fāṭima, peace be upon her) and Mawlānā ʿAlī were born from an apple, while the Prophet (peace be upon him) was born from pure water.

This substance—whether fruit or water—is guarded by the angels until it reaches the place of miracle, which is the Imam and his ⟪ar:زوج (اُم الذکر)⟫ (spouse, the Mother of the Remembrance, the feminine principle within the Imam). The Imam and his wife are holy vessels prepared for the coming Imam. When both consume the fruit and receive their portion, from which both are made ready, they obtain the substance: the Imam through the nose and his wife through the mouth. Their eating of this fruit together constitutes their marriage. Through the act of marriage, the Imam's wife receives sperm from the Imam, which generates the Imam's son. In this manner, a subtle part of the essence of ⟪ar:نفوس نامیۃ⟫ (the vital souls) purified from the sun and the movement of the planets joins ⟪ar:عنایت الہیہ⟫ (divine solicitude) with the sperm of the Imam's son and becomes the ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ of the son.

During pregnancy, the mother of the Imam's son receives all the nourishment—the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (subtle essences)—that reaches her body and accumulates near the navel, from which the Imam's son draws sustenance as do ordinary human beings. Since the Imam and the Mother of Truth themselves are of exalted rank, their affairs—marriage, pregnancy, childbirth—are their veils and symbols.

The Imam's wife becomes a holy vessel for the son. When she collects the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ from the sperm, the son is created. During childbirth, she receives all the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ that reach her body. Some ascend to the higher Imam, others descend to the wife. Extracts from these essences nourish the sperm. The mother of the Imam is supremely pure, free from all impurities that ordinary women experience. They are called ⟪ar:'مطہرات '⟫ (the purified ones). This is why Allāh says, in words of interpretation:

> ⟪quran 33:33⟫

Our bodies are composed of fire, air, water, and earth. The Imam's body is composed of the most subtle aspect of the ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ (vital souls) of the believers, and their divinity is composed of the images of these believers. Until the nasūt is prepared, the divine realm is not accepted.

### The Speed of Ascent

When the soul of a pious believer dies, it appears to him in ⟪ar:'خیال'⟫ (the form of imagination or in the image) of the Imam—the One who owns the entire universe. The thought of the Imam leads the form of this believer to the spirit of Paradise. The ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ emerge from the grave and cross the heavens, reaching the moon, where they meet the righteous spirits who departed before them. This entire journey is accomplished with the speed of ⟪ar:نجم ساقب⟫ (the piercing star) or ⟪ar:برق الاَّمع⟫ (the flashing lightning), and nothing obstructs those who are connected with the Imam of Truth. The ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of higher rank ascend at the speed of the wind, those of a lower rank at the gait of a swift Arabian horse, and those of an even lower rank move like one striving to reach his destination. Apart from them, those in the lowest ranks of ⟪ar:ریحایات⟫ move like one who is weak and burden-heavy.

The Fātiḥa and charity are given to the deceased because such offerings benefit the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of the deceased greatly. They must become part of the Imam's body, and the Imam will never adopt the wicked ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫. Therefore, the sooner purification is achieved, the sooner the seat is established with the Imam.

When the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of believers reach the heavens of the moon, they are saved from the heat of the moon's sphere. There are sentries on the paths who prevent the evil imaginations of the wicked from reaching the moon. All the dignified ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ question the visitor about the believers from whom he has come. If he says the believer is still alive, they receive good tidings and wish that those remaining in the world would also come to them. But if he says the believer has passed away, they are sorrowful and convinced that he perished due to his evil deeds.

### Why Does the Imam Fall Under the Law of Retaliation?

The Imam's innocence is the one ⟪ar:'مؤثِر '⟫ (the agent, the one who influences), while his soul and form are ⟪ar:'مؤثَر'⟫ (the ones influenced, the affected). As soon as the sperm of the believer takes root, he begins to receive influence and continues to accept effect for all his life. The same principle applies to the Imam: his nassūt (physical body, composed of our ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ collection) accepts the effect. For this reason, the Imam becomes sick, sorrowful, wounded, sheds tears, and must be buried. Yet his soul, so exalted, influences and governs the heavens. Thus there is an Imam who is ⟪ar:'مدبِر'⟫ (the regulator, the one who governs) and an Imam who is ⟪ar:'مدبَر'⟫ (the regulated, the one governed).

The grandchildren of the Imam are composed of ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (subtle essences) of the believers, so the Imam falls under the law of retaliation (qiṣāṣ). The ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ (sensory soul) of the Imam is so powerful that it can act from ⟪ar:تحت الثرا⟫ (beneath the earth) to the heavens. Yet since his body is fashioned from the parts of the believers and is subject to the law of retaliation, it accepts the evil of these malefic planets. Therefore they fall sick and endure suffering. Although the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ are brought to the Imam in a very clean and pure state, there remains a slight measure of reckoning. Therefore, the body of the Imam influences, but the soul of the Imam is master of the celestial spheres.

## The Union of Lahūt and Nasūt

The Imam is not constituted of defects; rather, his body is made of the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (subtle essences) of the believers. The Imam's reputation is formed by the believers and the noble ⟪ar:نفوس ریحیہ⟫ (ethereal souls). Humans possess three components:

From there, passing through Mercury, Venus, and the Sun, they are refined in Jupiter. From Jupiter, the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ return cleanly to the sun and remain there until the Imam has ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ (the Gate of Gates) prepared the boundaries of these believers and their noble limits.

For this reason, he was born as an Imam. As a sign of his great station, when born, his hair was white. His mother exclaimed: ⟪ar-quote:ولدت شیبۃ⟫ "I have borne an elder!" Each Imam has two gates: the ⟪ar:باب ظاہر اور باب باطن⟫ (outer and inner gates). The chapter of the visible universe gathers and merges with the chapter of the inner. When these sons are ready for the Imam—that is, when they are perfected in the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫, which stands opposite to their innocence of the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫—the Imam then commands his son to make the invitation public. The Imam bestows the ⟪ar:ناطقۃ اور حسیۃ⟫ (the rational and sensory souls) and the ⟪ar:نفس⟫ on his son, joining them with him through the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫, the ⟪ar:'لاہوت '⟫ (divine realm), and the ⟪ar:'امامت '⟫ (imamate). Through this union, the Imam's soul becomes for the ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ of his son a ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ and influences the coming Imam's soul through his ⟪ar:عالم حیات⟫ (life realm or world of becoming). The ⟪ar:نفس امام⟫ (soul of the Imam), the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫, and the ⟪ar:ناسوت⟫ (physicality) raise and perfect the three.

As soon as this son becomes connected with the Imam through the ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ (divine realm), he becomes himself a ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانی⟫ by lending nobility to this ⟪ar:'حجاب'⟫ (veil or body). At that point, he becomes his father's ⟪ar:'باب'⟫ (gate). In this manner, the son of the Imam contains a gathering of both nasūt and lahūt: that of the believers and the noble limits whose ⟪ar:صورات⟫ (forms) and ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (subtle essences) were separated at the hour of their death and now are reunited in the Imam.

The nasūt of the Imam is compared to "lā ilāha" (there is no god) and the ⟪ar:لاہوت امام⟫ (lahūt of the Imam) to ⟪ar:'الا اللہ'⟫ ("except Allāh"). When the Imam is established and the nasūt attains to the lahūt, it is said ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ (there is no god save Allāh)—meaning that all power of the physical realm derives from his divinity.

As long as he was in the ⟪ar:ھیکل شریف⟫ (noble form) of the Bāb al-Abāb, his station was exalted. Yet when he reaches the position of the son of the Imam, his station becomes vastly more elevated. Now that all the brotherhood has gathered in this gathering and become one ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانی⟫ (luminous form), the vitality, power, and knowledge of all become far more noble, exalted, and resplendent. For this whole multitude to arrive at such ⟪ar:رتبہ عالیہ⟫ (an exalted rank) is truly a paradise in their world. And in this manner, the connection of the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ with ⟪ar:نور الٰہی⟫ (the divine light) that pervades the ⟪ar:عالم قدس⟫ (the realm of holiness) is made complete.

The substance of the Imam's father comes from the First Source, while the substance of the Imam's son comes from the First Intellect. The remaining intellects regard the ten ranks below the Imam. The tenth intellect fixes its gaze upon the believers.

The Imam becomes for his son ⟪ar:'فتح '⟫ (the opening or the one who opens), and as soon as the son's dispensation begins, he becomes ⟪ar:'جد'⟫ (the grandfather or the ancient one). One of the Imam's loftiest stations, called the one always attached to the Imam's son, is the ⟪ar:'خیال'⟫ (the imagination or the subtle presence).

Since no two sovereigns can rule at one time, the father dies immediately after the decree (within one to one and a half minutes) and becomes a ⟪ar:مقیم الامام⟫ (one who establishes or strengthens the Imam) and grows with the congregation—the gathering of subtle essences received from his father. Following this transition, the new Imam gradually assumes the responsibility of the imamate. He is trained and instructed through ⟪ar:برزق عاشر⟫ (the tenth sustenance) and ⟪ar:جد، فتح اور خیال⟫ (grandfather, opening, and imagination). Now the plan of this new Imam begins, his main purpose being to remove his son and successor from his invitation, just as his father removed him from his. This system continues eternally.

An Imam who appears as ⟪ar:صاحب لاہوت⟫ (the possessor of the divine realm) possesses a gathering of one hundred thousand subtle forms within his divinity. When a person performs a prayer-cycle in the House of Allāh, he receives the reward of one hundred thousand prayer-cycles of a single cycle, for in the inner House (which is the Imam's reality), there gathers a thousand subtle forms, and their collective illumination falls upon this mosque. From among these hundred thousand forms, the Imam can identify and discern any form he wishes. An Imam may manifest through a thousand veils, yet he does not exhaust them.

Through the attainment of Lahūt, authority extends from the ⟪ar:فلک محیط⟫ (the encompassing sphere) to ⟪ar:تحت الثرا⟫ (beneath the earth). All miracles become possible through Allāh. Unless the light of divinity shines upon the horizon, no miracle can be displayed. Along with the clear text of the imamate, the Imam is connected by a principle emanating from the First Intellect, which was issued forth. In reality, there exist countless and infinite connections between this principle and the Imam.

The form of Lahūt is upon a human frame in which all the organs are placed. The saints occupy in the Divine Revelation the same stations they occupy in the nasūt of the believers. That is, he who occupies the station of the head in Lahūt will also occupy the position of the head in the believers' nasūt. ## The Transmutation of Excrement

Just as a believer attains the form of divine perfection through the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (spiritual essences), so too the supreme purpose of virtue is to extract and elevate the highest elements of the heart from the grave and transmute them spiritually. Around the heart there exists a special aqueous substance called ⟪ar:'دم قرمیزی'⟫ (crimson blood), and certain subtle winds known as ⟪ar:'حرارت غریزی'⟫ (instinctual heat). The physical body itself forms a ⟪ar:جسمانی کعبۃ اﷲ⟫ (corporeal sanctuary of God), containing within it an inner water called ⟪ar:'مآء ساعد'⟫ (vascular fluid) surrounded by its own distinctive winds. Just as the Kaaba possesses one ⟪ar:'رقامۃ الحمرۃ '⟫ (sacred crimson mark)—the highest point within that ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫ (House of God) where Maulana Ali was born—so too within our hearts exists a corresponding ⟪ar:رقامۃ الحمرۃ⟫ where the ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ (speaking faculty) exerts its influence, drawing forth the heart's highest qualities, awakening them, and transforming them into spirituality.[^cite-1]

Prayer divides the human body into six constituent parts. Upon the death of a believer, the physical remains are termed "excrement" (⟪ar:فضلات⟫), for the reality—the soul—has departed, leaving only what is superfluous behind.

The excrement of a believer emerges from the grave and the four elements—fire, air, water, and earth—separate and ascend toward the Kaaba through twenty-eight celestial stations. ### The Ten Degrees of Faith:

Faith is the designation of degrees, destinations, and stations. Islam exists at a single level alone. But as soon as one enters the realm of faith, the ranks proliferate—this is the state of ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ (secondary perfection). There are ten levels within each stage of faith. The spiritual world was constituted with his ⟪ar:لِم⟫ (why)—his ⟪ar:'۱۰'⟫ (ten) numbers. Because there are ten intellects, there are ten degrees here. Thus does the Qur'an declare: *⟪ar-quote:تِلْكَ عَشَرَةٌۭ كَامِلَ⟫ "That is a perfect ten."*[^cite-7]

In addition, he prays. Between the ⟪ar:بین المبدع و المبدع کلمۃ⟫ (Originator and Originated, there is the Word) stands ⟪ar:مبدِع⟫ (the Originator) beyond ⟪ar:'نور کلمہ'⟫ (the Light of the Word), and then ⟪ar:مبدَع⟫ (the Originated). When the First Intellect attained the tawhīd (oneness) of the Creator and reached secondary perfection, it was overwhelmed with ten veils of bliss in which it became lost. It remains occupied with lifting those same sheaths. Therefore did the Prophet ﷺ declare: *⟪ar-quote:مَن عَرَفَ نَفسُہُ فَقَد عَرَفَ رَبُّہُ⟫ "He who knows his own soul has thereby known his Lord."*[^cite-8] You have given one circumstance to ⟪ar:تشبیح⟫ (praise and glorification) another. Since no one can fully comprehend one's own soul, one cannot comprehend one's Lord.

### The Imam's Composition:

The Imam constitutes an assembly of five dimensions. His supreme element is that which he has fashioned from his remorse in the spiritual world—⟪ar:'صورت شریف'⟫ (the noble form).
