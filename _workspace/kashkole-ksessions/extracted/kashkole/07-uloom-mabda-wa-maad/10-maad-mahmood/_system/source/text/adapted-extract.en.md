## The Resurrection of the True Believer

A true believer comprises three constituent parts, which stand as the counterparts to the three jewels of the declaration of faith. These three parts undergo three distinct transformations after death, each undergoing purification in different realms and arriving ultimately at separate stations. Throughout his life, a believer tends to ⟪ar:خمیرۃ الأبدائیۃ⟫ (the primordial leaven), nurturing its development; even after death, the Divine ordering ensures that each part of the believer reaches its proper place for cleansing and refinement. The believer transforms the images of ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ (the Divine Realm) into himself and himself into ⟪ar:ناسوت⟫ (the human realm), while ⟪ar:فضلات⟫ (waste) and ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ (the ethereal forms) develop the higher aspects from physicality toward spirituality through the ⟪ar:(نفس)⟫ (the soul).

## Mubashir and Bashir (The Noble Form)

⟪ar:آنحضرت صلعم⟫ According to the hadith recorded from the Prophet (may peace be upon him), at the moment of death two figures appear to every soul—one for the righteous and one for the wicked. The reality of this teaching is that the Speaker (the ناطق, *Natiq*) and the Guardian of every age function as ⟪ar:'مبشروبشیر'⟫ (the bearer of glad tidings and the bringer of good news) for those who revere them, while serving as ⟪ar:'منکر و نکیر'⟫ (the inquisitors) for those who deny them. In the same manner, the Imam of the Age operates as ⟪ar:مبشر و بشیر⟫ for his contemporaries and others, fulfilling also the role of ⟪ar:منکر و نکیر⟫ according to their spiritual condition.[^cite-1] For this reason, ⟪ar:مولانا امیر المومنین⟫ (Mawlana the Commander of the Believers, ʿAlī) declared: ⟪ar-quote:یاَ حاَرِ ہَمداَن مَن یَّمُت یَرِنِی مِن مُومِنٍ اَو مُناَفِقٍ اَجَلا، یَعرِفُنیِ اِعتَرِفِہِ وَ عاَرِفُہُ بِأِسمِہٖ وَالْکُناَ وَماَ فَعَلا⟫ "O ⟪ar:حار ھمدان⟫ Harith of Hamadan! Whosoever dies shall see me presently, whether believer or hypocrite—he knows me by his recognition, and I know him by his names and epithets and what he has done."

### Mubashir and Bashir Manifest through the Guardian's Noble Form

Human souls are scattered throughout creation. Yet there is one ⟪ar:'نفس نامیہ'⟫ (the growing soul) which must evolve from its origin ⟪ar:قمر⟫ (the moon). When it develops through ⟪ar:سلالہ⟫ (the germ-plasma) and ⟪ar:نطفہ⟫ (the sperm-drop), it becomes ⟪ar:نامیہ⟫ (the nourishing principle). Upon birth into the physical world, the individual encounters the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ (the sensing soul). When he reaches maturity and takes the oath of allegiance, he receives the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ (the rational soul) from the vertical light descending from above. As a human acquires more ⟪ar:اعمال صالحہ⟫ (righteous deeds) and ⟪ar:علم حقیقی⟫ (true knowledge), the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ is prepared, and both it and the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ together fashion the ⟪ar:'صورت نورانی'⟫ (the luminous form). At the hour of death, all three—⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫, ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫, and ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫—ascend according to their degree of obedience; the more perfected among them rises toward the Imam, whereupon a ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ (noble form) becomes manifest, while the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ (the frame) and remainder are left behind in the grave.[^cite-2]

Every ⟪ar:مقام (ناطق، وصی، امام)⟫ (station—whether of Speaker, Executor of the Will, or Imam) is moved by the vertical light. This vertical light, emanating from the Holy World and reaching the believer through connection with the form of every saint, conveys all goodness. When the moment of a believer's death arrives, a ⟪ar:'شخص نورانی'⟫ (luminous person) appears bearing ⟪ar:علم الہی⟫ (divine knowledge) and ⟪ar:عمل صالح⟫ (righteous action); the believer rejoices upon seeing this form, knowing that his end shall be good. This form is fashioned from what the believer has created through the walayah (guardianship) of the people of truth and from ⟪ar:علوم الہیہ⟫ (divine sciences) and ⟪ar:اعمال صالحہ⟫ that he has wrought.

The ⟪ar:انسان فاضل (حدود کرام، دعات)⟫ (virtuous human—the noble limits, the summoners) who is scholar and practitioner continuously portrays himself through ⟪ar:اخلاق حسنہ⟫ (excellent character) and ⟪ar:اعمال جمیلہ⟫ (beautiful deeds). While in his earthly life, he exists in a state of ⟪ar:ملک بالقوۃ⟫ (potential kingship); upon death and departure from the body, his state becomes ⟪ar:ملک بالفعل⟫ (actual kingship), and he enters among the angels and reaches the ⟪ar:مجمع عالیہ الشریفہ (ھیکل شریف)⟫ (the elevated noble assembly, the sacred frame).[^cite-3] For this reason, ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ (Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq) said: "When Allah raises a believer from the grave, a righteous form emerges with him." This form is a ⟪ar:'ملک'⟫ light that perpetually gazes upon every part of his being, radiating throughout his entire person and ensuring that all parts develop and attain their station. When a believer feels fear in the grave, this ⟪ar:ملک (صورت)⟫ (angelic form) says to him, "Do not fear; no harm shall touch you!" The believer then looks upon this countenance and asks, "Who are you?" He replies with wonder: "You have given me tidings of peace and fulfilled that promise! You have vindicated my hopes and eased my distress. Who art thou?" The form answers, "I am one of Allah's creations. Allah created me from the deeds of righteousness that you performed toward the believers. I bring you gladness today!"

The Messenger of Allah (may peace and blessings be upon him) said to ⟪ar:قیس ابن آسم⟫ (Qays ibn Āsim): "O Qays, you have a companion in the grave with whom you shall be buried. While you lie dead, he shall remain alive. If you be virtuous, he shall show you mercy; if you be vile, he shall deliver you unto torment. Your resurrection shall be with him, and you shall be questioned concerning this companion. Make him righteous! If he is good, you shall take pleasure in him; if he is wicked, you shall recoil from him in horror. He is your ⟪ar:'عمل '⟫ (deed)."

## The Return of the Spiritual Form

⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫ (the good ending) When a believer's ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫ (rational soul) departs, something else is done for him through ⟪ar:سیقلدار⟫ (the keeper)—the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ (nourishing soul) and ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ (sensing soul) are made like unto itself. These three together become what is called a ⟪ar:'صورت نورانی'⟫ or ⟪ar:'صورت شریف'⟫ (a luminous or noble form). During the believer's life, the two were 'neighbors' (majawar), but after death, they become 'joined' (majawaz), merging into a single essence and rank. After both are elevated, his physical corpse is left behind as ⟪ar:فضلات⟫ (waste), which is interred in the grave.

The believer's form absorbs the light of the vertical light and connects it to its own exalted form. The form of the believer and the form of his exalted self become one soul, remaining subject to the soul of the highest limit; should this highest soul be corrupted, the form shall not be affected. Death occurs through the movement of the beneficial; the Fashioner brings death because the form of the boundary has reached completion. Through the departed soul's ear the questions are heard; through its eyes, responses are perceived; through its tongue, answers are spoken. As the soul ascends to the highest degree, so too do the conditions of its context transform. Even this great soul eventually passes beyond.

**Facts** · For all ⟪ar:جزائر⟫ (the islands), those ⟪ar:حضرات⟫ (noble ones) who have been purified find it impossible to commit further transgression. When ⟪ar:حضرات⟫ a believer dies, his form mingles with his exalted form and soul.

### Whence Comes the Form of the Boundary?

If the noble form is ready at the moment of death, what is the exalted form that comes forth and effects the form of the boundary? The truth is that the Divine Essence takes shape in the believer's own lifetime. The works done in accordance with the Law have an effect upon ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ and ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ (the sensing and nourishing souls), and the effect of knowledge and action extends to the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ (the rational soul). The form of great prayers becomes ready not only during his life but goes forth and reaches the levels of the Imam! In that place, this form's influence becomes established. Believers use this influence for the remainder of their lives, becoming extremely pious. For this very reason, ⟪ar:سیدنا غلام حسین صاحب اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ (our Master Ghulam Husayn, may Allah elevate his station) indicated: **"The form has departed to my exalted limit; only its effect remains!"** He thus pointed out that the form itself has ascended to his higher station, while ⟪ar:(سیدنا علی بن محسن شبام)⟫ (Sayyidina ʿAlī ibn Muhsin of Shabwah) preserves its lingering influence.

---

Upon the death of the Supreme Limit, the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ is drawn upward and adjoined to its form (merging with the form of its boundary), and in like manner the two proceed, ascending until they reach the level of the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ (frame). This ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ prepares the son of the Imam in ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ (the Divine Realm). The Qur'an expresses this reality thus:

> ⟪quran 89:27-30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيَّتُهَا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ‎ ‏ٱرْجِعِىٓ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةًۭ مَّرْضِيَّةًۭ ‎ ‏فَٱدْخُلِى فِى عِبَدِى ‎ ‏وَٱدْخُلِى جَنَّتِى ‎⟫
>
> *(O you soul in complete rest and satisfaction! Return to your Lord, well-pleased and well-pleasing unto Him. Enter you, then, among My honored servants, and enter My Paradise!)*

⟪ar-quote:يَٓأَيَّتُھا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّۃ⟫ *(O soul at peace)*

Here the speaker is the ⟪ar:امام الزمان⟫ (the Imam of the Age), not the ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ (the soul) alone, nor the ⟪ar:'عقل'⟫ (the intellect). ⟪ar:نفس⟫ At the hour of death, the ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ (rational soul) is drawn upward and finds refuge in Him, while both the ⟪ar:نفوس حسیۃ و نامیۃ⟫ (sensing and nourishing souls) are satisfied and content.

⟪ar-quote:ارجعی الی ربک راضیۃ مرضیۃ⟫ *(Return to your Lord, well-pleased.)*

⟪ar:نفوس حسیۃ و نامیۃ⟫ and ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ with the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫ to ⟪ar:'رب'⟫ (the Lord)—they return with full consent and satisfaction.

⟪ar-quote:فادخلی فی عبادی⟫ *(Then enter among My servants.)*

⟪ar:عِباَدِی⟫ (My servants) refers to the ⟪ar:'حد عالی '⟫ (the upper limit) form. In other words, the noble form is being commanded to merge with the Most High's presence!

⟪ar-quote:وَٱدْخُلِی جَنَّتِی⟫ *(And enter My Paradise.)*

The form of the Upper Limit constitutes our first paradise, from which our form receives reconciliation. The ⟪ar:جَنَّتِی⟫ second paradise is the ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ (the Gate of Gates). Yet notice: ⟪ar:عِباَدِی⟫ comes with ⟪ar:'فی'⟫ (*in*), but ⟪ar:جَنَّتِی⟫ does not. This indicates that although the believer's form shall progress toward ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ (the Gate of Gates) as toward Paradise, it merges with the Almighty immediately upon death.

### The Slave Serving His Master

If the believer's soul becomes corrupted, he is placed within the body. When the form of his limitation reaches him, it preserves the boundary's conception and elevates it to the limits of the boundary; should the self become corrupted, it is his reward that he served his master and preserved his imagination. Since his righteous imagination has departed, no force labors to preserve this self, and it decays as befell ⟪ar:ابو بکر⟫ and ⟪ar:داؤد بن قطب⟫ (Abū Bakr and Dāwūd ibn Qutb). From his wicked deeds there then comes a ⟪ar:صورت ظلمانیۃ⟫ (a dark form) that bears the effect of ⟪ar:حسیۃ⟫ and ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ wings.

## The Outer Gate and the Inner Gate

An Imam prescribes two gates: one ⟪ar:'باب ظاہر'⟫ (the Outer Gate) for the common believers, and one ⟪ar:'باب باطن'⟫ (the Inner Gate) for the elect. The Outer Gate becomes the heart of the Inner Gate by gathering together all the forms of the islands. At the behest of the inner gate, all the forms of the Imam are assembled together and become the ⟪ar:'قلب'⟫ (the heart) of the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ (frame). This same principle operates in every sphere: lower Imams become the gates of the Imams above them, and the Lists rank above them. This is indicated in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 55:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِۦ جَنَّتَانِ ‎⟫
>
> *(And for him who fears to stand before his Lord, there are two gardens.)*

This ⟪ar:رَبِّہِ⟫ verse refers to ⟪ar:علی علیہ السلام⟫ (ʿAlī, peace be upon him), and ⟪ar:'مَقَامَ رَبِّہ'⟫ (the standing before one's Lord) pertains to the Imams descended from the line of ʿAlī. ⟪ar:'جَنَّتاَنِ'⟫ (Two gardens) designates the ⟪ar:باب باطن⟫ and ⟪ar:باب ظاہر⟫ (Inner and Outer Gates). That is, whoever stands in awe before the spiritual powers of the Imam merges with both the outer door and the inner door.

| Lord | Station of the Lord | Two Gardens |
|---|---|---|
| ʿAlī | The Imams | Outer Gate + Inner Gate |

**Facts** · ⟪ar:سیدنا سلیمان صاحب أعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ (Our Master Sulaymān, may Allah elevate his station) established gates according to the station of manifestation.

---

⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ signifies the 'Gate of Gates.' Twelve gates lead to the Imam, the final and supreme of which is the ⟪ar:'باب الابواب'⟫ (Gate of Gates). The Imam of the Time receives all forms from the Bāb al-Abwāb and arranges them all, placing each upon the human frame according to its rank. There none is wronged, none is shown favor; all receive their reward according to their deeds. All these forms become like intellect within the Bāb al-Abwāb, for intellect is the essence of humanity. This is what is called the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ and ⟪ar:'امامت'⟫ (the frame and Imamate). When this ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ or ⟪ar:امامت⟫ is conferred upon the Imam, he is called the ⟪ar:'امام'⟫ (Imam); before that, he holds the station of ⟪ar:حجت عظمی⟫ (the Great Proof).[^cite-4]

**Facts** · In cases where there is deviation due to sin, the Bāb al-Abwāb leaves such persons to future Imams for their ⟪ar:خاتمہ بالخیر⟫ (good ending), even though they were once the believer's. Later Imams then cleanse them.

The radiance of these forms is so sublime that the human eye cannot bear its brightness. ⟪ar:سیدنا مؤید اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ (Our Master al-Muʾayyad, may Allah elevate his station) observed: "When human eyes cannot endure the light of the sun—a physical light—how can they possibly perceive this spiritual luminescence?"

### Hazrat Khizar (upon him be peace)

The term is mentioned in the station of the ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ because Prophet ⟪ar:خذر علیہ السلام⟫ (Khiḍr, peace be upon him) drank from the ⟪ar:'آب حیات'⟫ (water of life) and shall therefore live until the Day of Judgment. Similarly, the Bāb al-Abwāb has absorbed all the forms of ethereal essences, which can never be reversed—their life has become eternal. In another sense, it refers to the all-encompassing knowledge, after which the soul can never become corrupted; that is, distortion and death cannot touch it!

### The Two Types of the People of Truth

There are two ranks among the people of truth. The first consists of those who know the truth, possess ⟪ar:علوم الہیۃ⟫ (divine sciences), perform righteous deeds, and love all the Imams, the Proofs, and their followers. These are the limits (ḥudūd) who, upon reaching the Imam, become guardians ⟪ar:اعضاء رئیسۃ⟫ (principal members) of the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانیہ⟫ (luminous frame) because of their guardianship, knowledge, righteous deeds, and wisdom. The elevation and luminosity of each of them within the ⟪ar:ھیکل نورانیہ⟫ accords with their guardianship, real knowledge, and actions.

Behind them stand those who perform righteous deeds and practice guardianship, yet do not know the truth. These pure believers occupy a lower rank in the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ (frame) of the Imam than the first rank.

**Facts** · Our parents are literally our ⟪ar:صورات شریفہ⟫ (noble forms) from the last two generations. When we were born in the last sphere and passed away after living our lives, the younger version of us becomes our mother, and the elder becomes our father in the form of the Imam.

## The Return of the Ethereal Forms: The Remnant of the Nourishing Soul

After birth into the world, the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ (nourishing soul) and ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ (sensing soul) become unified. The upper aspect of this conjugation—the ⟪ar:(نفس حسیۃ) صورت⟫ (form of the sensing soul)—departs together, while the lower aspect—the ⟪ar:(نفس نامیۃ)⟫ (nourishing soul)—remains in the believer's body. After the believer's death and the ritual bath, a fever begins to emerge in the form of a subtle ⟪ar:(ریحیات)⟫ (ethereal essence); after burial, it leaves the body over three days. During this three-day period, the entire ⟪ar:بقایہ شریفہ⟫ (noble remnant) is withdrawn.[^cite-5]

**Facts** · In the time of earlier supplicants, the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ used to emerge in the final part of the three days. Through the grace of ⟪ar:سیدنا سلیمان صاحب⟫ (our Master Sulaymān), the effect of his majesty is such that immediately upon the death of present ⟪ar:مؤمنین مستقیم السیر⟫ (the steadfast believers), the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ begins to emerge and departs in the first part of the three days, because the waste is now purer than ever, undergoing constant scrutiny.

These ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ and ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ (the ethereal essences and nourishing soul) ascend from the grave and reach the moon through the moon's rays, which are the moon's ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ center. For this reason, the moon appears filled with light on the fourteenth. On the ⟪ar:ایام بِیض⟫ (the white days—the thirteenth, fourteenth, and fifteenth), ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ gathers in the moon. Whatever ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ he continues to collect with the moon (qamar), he gradually hands over to the sun (shams) until he delivers it in its entirety. ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ ascend from the first to the twelfth. On the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ thirteenth, fourteenth, and fifteenth they are assembled in the moon, and then from the sixteenth to the end they are unveiled to the world.

| Timeline | Movement of the Nourishing Soul in the Moon |
|---|---|
| Days 1–12 | Ascension of the Nourishing Soul |
| Days 13, 14, 15 | Gathering in the Moon |
| Days 16–30 | Unveiling to the World |

**Facts** · When the pure conception of prayer is elevated, the ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ and ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ ascend even further with it. Some are so elevated that their ⟪ar:قُوَّۃِ اَدمِغَۃ⟫ (the brain powers) and the seats of the heart also develop!

⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ (the ethereal soul) that emerges from the body is subtle. Although it is a soul, it carries a physical character. Therefore, at the believer's death, it remains in the body and then exits the grave, ascending to the moon for purification. The moon hands it over through Mercury and Venus to ⟪ar:شمس⟫ (the Sun) for cleansing. The ⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ endures the sun's heat and suffers the torment which atones for sins. Rather than the fire of Gehenna, they are given the sun's heat, which is far less severe. They remain in the sun until the faces of their opponents reach the Bāb al-Abwāb. When they reach the sun, the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ are compressed and tormented; it is turned to sweat and suffers. The sun melts it and ⟪ar:تخمیر⟫ (ferments) it, so that they become able to join the nasut (physical form) of the coming Imam—all done so the ⟪ar:نفس ریحیۃ⟫ of the believer may become equal to one who has reached the Divine Imam, drawing near to ⟪ar:لاہوت⟫ (the Divine Realm).

**Facts** · In the human body, ⟪ar:حرارت غریزی⟫ (instinctual heat) is first developed, formed in the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ of the next sphere. ⟪ar:دم قرمیزی⟫ (crimson blood) of the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ is developed by its nourishment within the mother's womb in the next sphere.

The ⟪ar:نفوس ریحیات⟫ (ethereal souls) fall within the circle of the sun, which is the essence of all heat and the center of all ⟪ar:اجسام غریزیۃ⟫ (instinctual bodies). All bodies descended from heaven gather in the sun, which contains their heat and light. In the same manner, these bodies were deposited in the moon, which endows them with coolness and soothing moisture.

| The Sun | The Moon |
|---|---|
| Instinctual Heat + Constipation | Humidity + Coolness |

After this purification, the sun hands over the soul to Jupiter, which is the ⟪ar:'سعد اکبر'⟫ (the Greater Fortune). Although Mars stands above the sun, these souls do not reach that star, for it is a malefic planet. The sun refers them directly to Jupiter, where they become exceedingly transparent and pure. After this cleansing, they return to the sun and there are frozen.

## The Return of Believers' Hard Bones

⟪ar:صاحبان معرفت⟫ Those who possess gnosis and ⟪ar:صاحبان اہل ظاہر⟫ those learned in the exoteric sciences—their flesh, skin, and soft bones all ascend through the heavens toward the constellations. All of this unfolds so that at least some portion of this waste may develop sufficiently to be fashioned by ⟪ar:عنایت الہیہ⟫ divine solicitude into ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ the soul, becoming a component ⟪ar:(حرارت غریزی) نفس حسیہ⟫ of the heart throughout this thousand-year arc of becoming.

### The Ten Degrees of Faith:

Faith is the very name of ranks, stations, and degrees. Islam alone is the sole rank that ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ exists without internal stratification. Yet the moment one enters faith, the ranks establish themselves—and this marks the state of ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ secondary perfection. Within each degree of faith lie ten tiers. The ⟪ar:لِم⟫ reason his ⟪ar:'۱۰'⟫ number exists in the spiritual realm is that there are ten intellects; hence ten degrees here mirror them. The Qur'an testifies: ⟪ar-quote:تِلْكَ عَشَرَةٌۭ كَامِلَ⟫ "Verily the number ten is complete." *

Moreover, the Summoner conducts what lies ⟪ar:بین المبدع و المبدع کلمۃ⟫ between the Originator and the originated—that is, between ⟪ar:مبدِع⟫ the Creator and ⟪ar:'نور کلمہ'⟫ the light of the word (al-Kalimatullāh, الكلمة) and ⟪ar:مبدَع⟫ the created. When the first intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal) attained monotheistic knowledge of the Creator and achieved secondary perfection, he was overwhelmed by ten veils of joy within which he errs. He remains eternally engaged in removing these same sheaths. Thus the Messenger of God declared: ⟪ar-quote:مَن عَرَفَ نَفسُہُ فَقَد عَرَفَ رَبُّہُ⟫ "Whosoever knows his own soul has thereby known his Lord." You have attributed one state to another. Since no one can fully comprehend one's own essence, neither can one fully comprehend one's Lord—this reciprocal mystery being the nature of ⟪ar:تشبیح⟫ the resemblance.[^cite-11]

### Essential Summary:

A minute portion of one ⟪ar:روحانی شبیح⟫ spiritual similitude manifests in a sphere. Even a fraction of this fragment appears as a body. All else scatters as food, clothing, and sustenance—things a person continuously receives throughout life. When he has gathered all the components of his sphere, he dies and his original ⟪ar:صورت + نفس حسیہ⟫ form and sensate soul are separated from him forever. Everything else then ascends for development in the next sphere.

The form that departs ascends to the Imam's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ temple, then ascends through smaller and larger ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ temples until it reaches the Sustainer's ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ temple, where it takes the shape of spiritual being. In this way, its parts gradually aggregate near different ledgers across various spheres. These ⟪ar:ھیاکل⟫ temples gather in the distant cycle, and the peacock ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ of temples of the cycle. When this is fashioned, all these parts unite to form a complete ⟪ar:شبیح⟫ similitude—one ⟪ar:شبیح⟫ created in perfection within the spiritual world. Thus she ⟪ar:شبیح⟫ is able to ascend to the spiritual realm anew, ⟪ar:صاحب مور⟫ and then attain secondary perfection.

### The Composition of the Imam:

The Imam comprises a unity of five elements. His highest aspect is that ⟪ar:'صورت شریف'⟫ noble form which he has fashioned from his regret in the spiritual world. With his power, their ⟪ar:'لاہوت'⟫ divine dimension also departs, and with the divine dimension even more ⟪ar:'ناسوت خاص'⟫ the special corporeal form, and further still ⟪ar:'ناسوت ظلی'⟫ the shadowy corporeal form. What remains are the essences ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ of the islanders that are buried in the grave. That is, at death the Imam takes all with him except ⟪ar:ناسوت طبعی⟫ the natural corporeal form, which is woven from the island peoples' ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ essences—and this remains because their deeds remain active and they bear the grave's retribution. ⟪ar:ناسوت طبعی⟫ This natural form is what is buried.

## The Confluences of the Imams

When an Imam merges with his ⟪ar:عالی علیہ⟫ predecessor, the two become as candles joined. Just as multiple candles combined yield but one flame, so when the Imam reaches his limit, the two become one ⟪ar:'نورٌ واحدۃ'⟫ single light after merger. Thus ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the Messenger of God declared: ⟪ar-quote:اَناَ وَ عَلِی مِن نُور وَاحِد⟫ "ʿAlī and I are of one light," or again: ⟪ar-quote:اَوَّلُناَ مُحَمَّد وَ اَوسَطُناَ مُحَمَّد وَ آخِرُناَ مُحَمَّد وَ کُلُّناَ مُحَمَّد وَ کُلُّناَ عَلِیٍّ⟫ "Our first is Muhammad, our middle is Muhammad, our last is Muhammad; we are all Muhammad; we are all ʿAlī." That is, after these confluences, the components cannot be separated. There remains only ⟪ar:'حدت'⟫ oneness—the greater the confluences, the mightier becomes the Imam's singular power.

None could rival the ⟪ar:حدت نظر⟫ first intellect's unified vision. Likewise does the senior Imam perform the work of ⟪ar:حدت نظر⟫ unified vision—for ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the Messenger declared: ⟪ar-quote:اِتَّقُو مِن فِراَسَتِ المُؤمِن ھُوَ یَنظُر بِنُور اللہ⟫ "Beware the discernment of the believer, for he sees by the light of Allah!"[^cite-12] The discernment (al-firāsah) of the believer is his ⟪ar:'حدت نظر'⟫ unified vision, and the light of Allah belongs to ⟪ar:'تائیدات'⟫ the succoring aids given by ⟪ar:عالی علیہ⟫ the Imam.

When the Imam passes away, he departs bearing with him ⟪ar:برزق عاشر⟫ the tenth sustenance—the ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ temple that houses all ⟪ar:ھیاکل⟫ temples. They dwell therein. ⟪ar:برازق عاشریہ⟫ The world rests beneath the veil of life. ⟪ar:فلک محیط⟫ At this juncture, all temples halt until the seventh Imam draws the previous six into himself, merges with them, and dies—then ⟪ar:برزق عاشریہ⟫ joins the tenth sustenance.
