## The Condemned Souls: The Blaming Self and the Self Inciting to Evil

Since fallen beings are divided into categories according to their own remorse, their doubt and bewilderment, and their obstinacy and arrogance, these three natures manifest themselves in every species and gender. Prayer that is pleasing to Allah is good; prayer that clings to the face of the worshipper and brings death is evil. In the same manner, there exist three types of human beings classified according to the sensible soul (nafs ḥassiyya):

### The Self Inciting to Evil

Allah, Most High, declares:

> ⟪quran 12:53⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ وَمَآ أُبَرِّئُ نَفْسِىٓ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلنَّفْسَ لَأَمَّارَةٌۢ بِٱلسُّوٓءِ إِلَّا مَا رَحِمَ رَبِّىٓ ۚ إِنَّ رَبِّى غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *"And I free not myself (from the blame). Verily, the (human) self is inclined to evil, except when my Lord bestows His Mercy (upon whom He wills). Verily, my Lord is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful."*

The nafs ʿāmira bil-suʾ (the self inciting to evil) draws the soul toward transgression.[^cite-1] This is the self that has spread its wings in rebellion, refusing obedience and fleeing from the divine summons. Just as Allah, through His Messenger, sends forth souls inclined toward goodness, so too does He permit the emergence of corrupted souls. Allah, the Exalted, declares further:

> ⟪quran 91:12⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِذِ ٱنۢبَعَثَ أَشْقَىٰهَا ‎⟫
> *When the most wicked man among them went forth (to kill the she-camel).*

The one whose soul is utterly wretched stands in the gravest peril. Allah shall punish such a soul by casting it into the lowest depths of Hell, for it has arrived through sheer stubbornness and denial of the divine way.

### The Blaming Self

Another category of self exists—the nafs lawwāma (the blaming self)—which perpetually dwells in a state of doubt and is troubled by suspicion. This self permits neither side to reach a final determination, leaving the soul suspended between contention and hesitation. The Qur'ān bears witness:

> ⟪quran 75:2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَآ أُقْسِمُ بِٱلنَّفْسِ ٱللَّوَّامَةِ ‎⟫
> *And I swear by the self-reproaching person (a believer).*

## The Slaughter of the Self and Its Hamstringing

Allah, the Most High, declares concerning the people of Thamūd:

> ⟪quran 91:10-14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ خَابَ مَن دَسَّىٰهَا ‎ ‏كَذَّبَتْ ثَمُودُ بِطَغْوَىٰهَآ ‎ ‏إِذِ ٱنۢبَعَثَ أَشْقَىٰهَا ‎ ‏فَقَالَ لَهُمْ رَسُولُ ٱللَّهِ نَاقَةَ ٱللَّهِ وَسُقْيَهَا ‎ ‏فَكَذَّبُوهُ فَعَقَرُوهَا فَدَمْدَمَ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّهُم بِذَنۢبِهِمْ فَسَوَّىٰهَا ‎⟫
> *And indeed he fails who corrupts his own self. Thamud denied through their transgression. When the most wicked among them went forth, the Messenger of Allah said to them: "Beware of the she-camel of Allah and her drink!" But they denied him and hamstrung her. So their Lord destroyed them for their sin and made them equal in destruction.*

The term ʿaqr (hamstringing) literally signifies killing; it is the name given to lawful death—the dhbīḥa (ritual slaughter). Yet in this verse, while the outward reference appears to concern the she-camel, the inner meaning (taʾwīl) turns upon the souls of those who perpetrated such evil, who hamstrung their own souls and delivered them to an unlawful death through obstinate denial.[^cite-2]

## The Death and Resurrection of the Believer Who Falls Short

The one who commits grave transgression is called the muʾmin muqāṣir (the believer who falls short). There exists a distinction between the death of a true believer and that of such a person. Although every human being is culpable in some measure, the muqāṣir is specifically one who has perpetrated grave sins (kabāʾir). These grave transgressions include: associating partners with Allah, lying, adultery, consuming wine, and murder.

The first punishment befalling such a person differs from that of a true believer: the torment of the grave. The sensible soul (nafs ḥassiyya) does not depart completely; according to the magnitude of the transgression, this sensation reverberates through the bodily form. The soul is buried in the grave in a semi-animate state, enduring the severe torment of the grave for three days.[^cite-3] Thereafter, the body is subjected to punishment commensurate with human stature and to the anguish of wrench and monkey-form. Yet divine mercy ensures that such a soul does not don seventy garments of each type nor endure seventy links within each. Rather, the cosmic order punishes according to justice, then restores the soul to human dignity.

## How Does Punishment for Forbidden Deeds Come to Pass?

When a person commits what is forbidden or neglects what has been commanded, the very essence of the self (jauhar al-nafs) swiftly becomes darkened. Evil deeds obstruct the path of guidance, rendering the soul unclean and leading it toward error, entrapping it in sin's snare. Such deeds then incite enmity toward the divine limits and ultimately cast the soul into perdition.

**It is established principle**: Allah forgives sins committed against Himself, yet He does not forgive transgressions against His servants, except through the means (fasāḥ) by which He brings the wrongdoer and the wronged before mutual reckoning, fulfilling retaliation justly.[^cite-4]

When obscene acts or their like occur, retaliation becomes obligatory, for such deeds blacken the soul's essence with extreme swiftness, causing it to fall. First, the soul is clothed in a shirt of filth (rijs), the punishment of human stature in which it completes its retribution. It is then clothed in a shirt of rejection (raqṣ), through which it fulfills its retaliation by the bond of marriage. It is said of them that their faces are in their breasts. Then it is clothed in a shirt of reversal (ʿaks), wherein retribution is fulfilled through the act of marriage. In every such instance, the soul acts upon its condition, and all these matters depend upon the abundance or scarcity of sin in the world.

It sometimes occurs that many commit adultery with a single woman. Her retaliation shall be fulfilled such that she is clothed in different shirts, and in each shirt she is married differently. It has been observed that the male pig incites his female to go forth and mate with other animals; and sometimes in such cases, the union between two beasts occurs with their pleasure and will—for the soul acts by its own free choice and desire.

## The Death and Resurrection of the Righteous

Those who take not the covenant yet outwardly follow Islamic ordinances, who oppress no one, who remain chaste and pious and beneficent, who lie not nor betray, who do not follow the walāya (guardianship) of the Prophet and his successor, and who recognize their descendants without bearing grudge—these are called the ṣāliḥūn (the righteous). When such people die, the spirituality of the moon and Venus stirs within them; their soul diffuses throughout their bodies. Their bodily heat diminishes and their blood congeals. The imaginal form they have created through their outward deeds departs from their bodies.

**It is established**: Allah wastes not the good of anyone. ⟪ar-quote:اَلشَّرُّ لاَ اَصلُ لَہُ فِی اِلبدَاء⟫ *(Evil has no origin in creation)*. If an unbeliever performs a good deed, he shall be rewarded for it. Allah rewards the good deeds of such righteous ones by exalting their deeds, even though they have not received a good ending (khātima bil-khair).[^cite-5]

> ⟪quran 6:160⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَن جَآءَ بِٱلْحَسَنَةِ فَلَهُۥ عَشْرُ أَمْثَالِهَا ۖ وَمَن جَآءَ بِٱلسَّيِّئَةِ فَلَا يُجْزَىٰٓ إِلَّا مِثْلَهَا وَهُمْ لَا يُظْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Whosoever brings a good deed shall have ten times the like thereof, and whosoever brings evil shall be recompensed with the like thereof, and they shall not be wronged.*

This good conception of theirs reaches first the moon (Qamar); the moon then transmits the conception to the sun (Shams). The imagination ascends to the solar realm, where it ferments and undergoes purification. This conception remains in the sun until the appointed time for its return draws near. The body slowly dissolves and rises through evaporation, ascending to the moon and thence to composition. From there it descends through rain and becomes absorbed into pure and sweet sustenance. This food is consumed by its parents, from which sperm is produced, and thus it manifests anew in the world. When it appears in the world, it receives the vegetative soul (nafs nāmiya) from the moon; and when it emerges from the mother's womb, conception comes from the sun through Venus and the moon, connecting with body and spiritual soul (nafs ḥassiya). That which was diffused into the body after death—this very conception—now gathers together, and these three (body, self, and conception) unite and become mingled (mumāzaj) with the body. The state of this conception is better and more noble than before, greater, more grand, and more honorable. This is the reward of his good deeds, bestowed upon him by Allah.

The example is that of a traveler who leaves his place of residence and his family, living in poverty like one who journeys abroad, until he acquires wealth and returns to his home in a condition superior to before. By contrast, there is the traveler who departs his family and lives in poverty as a wayfarer, yet never returns to his kin—meaning their imagination, self, and body all perish, constituting a form of punishment.

## The Death and Resurrection of the Non-Believer

Save for the muqāṣirūn (those who fall short) and the ṣāliḥūn (the righteous), all people are deserving of punishment, and their death agonies are proportionate to their deeds. After death, they must endure the most severe torments. There is a slight difference between the death of a Muslim and the death of an unbeliever, provided the Muslim harbors no grudge against ʿAlī and the children of ʿAlī.

**As one has stated**: ⟪ar:حضرت ضیا علی صاحب⟫ "If a ladder is erected and people of every sect climb it, the one who falls from the top shall be hurt more grievously than one who falls from the bottom. Since the Bohorās have fallen closest to us, they shall suffer most intensely after death."

When the time of death arrives for non-believers and those who are followers of enemies of the Imams and saints, a dark essence (jauhar ẓulmānī—the dual evil principles, ʿuqdatayn) is stirred within them, for they serve as magnets for every wicked person. This dark essence pulls from them the sinister form of their stubbornness. Since their lord possesses no narrator-form that can attract their senses, their soul becomes diffused (shāʾiʿ) within their body as though a thermometer breaks and mercury spreads throughout it. The self transforms into the body, and both become a single thing. From both, the evil imagination (taṣawwur khabīth) and the whimsical self (nafs hawāʾiyya)—the part of his body left within—are separated; and his body is left with his soul, which is termed in the invitation (duʿā) as the grave-dwellers.

**Origin and Duration**: The self of the body is progress; the diffusion of the self into the body is degradation. Even if the body is burned, its parts remain, for what Allah has created cannot be destroyed. Only its form may change; existence cannot become non-existent.[^cite-6]

### The Origin of Evil Imagination

His evil imagination is in fact his beliefs and wicked deeds, which he has forged through obstinate defiance of the Imams and saints or through separation from them. This conception is termed the dark, Satanic, devilish self (nafs ẓulmāniyya iblīsiyya shayṭāniyya). It takes the form of evil's burning. Sometimes it assumes the appearance of fire—that which burns those whose things are consumed; and that fire is evil. At times this conception remains in the body and does not depart, and the body is buried with it. This evil form (ṣūrat shayṭāniyya) burns the body for a time and inflicts punishment. It has been observed that at night the grave of such a person trembles with wrath, and sometimes the grave appears ablaze—yet when approached, no fire is seen. The man of the grave, whom his imagination burns and torments, becomes ever more wicked in his deeds and more intense in his enmity.

### The Punishment of the Grave

The non-believer is buried in his grave with the sensible soul (ḥassiya), and his condition is like that of one buried alive. When he perceives his wicked form, he is terrified and prays that there had been great distance between himself and this evil shape. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 3:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَوْمَ تَجِدُ كُلُّ نَفْسٍۢ مَّا عَمِلَتْ مِنْ خَيْرٍۢ مُّحْضَرًۭا وَمَا عَمِلَتْ مِن سُوٓءٍۢ تَوَدُّ لَوْ أَنَّ بَيْنَهَا وَبَيْنَهُۥٓ أَمَدًۢا بَعِيدًۭا ۗ وَيُحَذِّرُكُمُ ٱللَّهُ نَفْسَهۥ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ رَءُوفٌۢ بِٱلْعِبَادِ ‎⟫
> *On the Day when every soul will see before it all the good it has done and all the evil it has done, it will wish that there were a great distance between it and its evil. And Allah warns you against Himself, and Allah is Kind to His bondsmen.*

Then he perceives the Divine Universe in the form of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (upon whom be peace), which grants him the taste of Hell.

For forty days after burial, this form circumambulates the sides of the grave. It strives to exit this physical realm and reach the world of light, but celestial rays draw it back to the earthly sphere. It then attempts to reunite with its body, yet cannot. It merely circles the body in a state of frustrated longing, its condition most painful. As the grave torment intensifies, the soul grows mad with anguish and begins to shriek and scream. For this reason, sounds of lamentation can be heard at night in cemeteries; sometimes their words are intelligible (ʿunayim). In this attempt to re-enter the body, the form strikes it violently, causing intense pain to the body, which contains the full capacity for sensation.

## The Wandering of Evil Forms

When this evil form is separated from its body after forty days, it shapes itself as it desires and acts as it wishes. This is because, having departed the body's confinement at death, it has reached the full extent of its wickedness. While joined to the body, its powers were constrained and it was a devil in potentiality (shayṭān bil-quwwa); but now, separated, no restraint binds it, and it becomes a devil in actuality (shayṭān bil-fiʿl).[^cite-7] The body had been a prison for this wicked form, from which it is now liberated. It is the justice of Allah that He does not prevent the wicked from fulfilling their retribution. Punishment itself becomes manifest through the wicked person's own free will and unwilling acts. When it emerges from the grave, it moves between heaven and earth. This is why the Messenger of Allah declared: ⟪ar-quote:لا ملا ولا خلا فی الھوا⟫ *"The air is neither filled nor empty."*

This evil form, having left its body, works against the righteous, yet only against those with whom reckoning remains due. It cannot harm everyone. Some of these wicked forms occasionally speak truth: for when they inhabited flesh, though wicked, they yet spoke truly. Alongside evil dwelt truth within them. For example, Nostradamus's prophecies held some accuracy because the signs (jinn) that spoke to him told him truths. This flows from Allah's justice, for Allah does not obliterate the evil and good in anyone.

**According to the Messenger of Allah**, these evil forms (ṣūrāt khabītha) affect only ignorant men, ignorant women, and children. All actions spring from fear of their evil forms. Even in sleep, those who pray for safety pray for protection from their influence, for their measures are released at night. These evil spirits sometimes come in dreams to the sleeper and report things, some true and some false. If any of these come to pass, people suppose them truthful, which destroys them and their followers and increases their hatred. Whoever works against the Imam becomes a condemned jinn (jinn manzūm), continuing to increase his hatred and enmity. For this reason, when we pass through unclean places such as desolate plains, we pray for safety, for these are the abodes of these evils.

As long as the wise man does not will it, the evil form remains in its state. It takes refuge only in desolate, dark houses; in foul-smelling places; in deserts and wastelands; on pathways and crossroads; in terrible places; and in slaughterhouses and latrines. Sometimes, in the sleep of wicked people like itself, it manifests imaginally and speaks to them according to its evil opinions and beliefs, further corrupting their convictions and intensifying their enmity toward the righteous. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 6:112⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Thus have We appointed for every prophet an enemy—devils among men and jinn—who inspire one another with deceitful speech as delusion. Yet had your Lord willed, they would not have done it; so leave them and what they fabricate.*

These people, to whom this form speaks of evil, are ignorant men or ignorant women or children. This evil state encounters them, and the common people call this evil state a jinn (jinn). The form then speaks in the tongue of that man or woman, and in conversation tells of coming events. Yet even herein it sometimes tells truth and sometimes falsehood. It works sorcery with this ignorant person and commands him to sin. Sometimes it departs from him and goes to another body; sometimes it clings to his body and does not leave it until death. These are what are called condemned jinns (jinnāt manzūm) and devils (shayāṭīn), the wretched ones (afarīṭ). When people recite the Qur'ān, they hear it in amazement and flee.

Apart from this, sometimes this form is used to describe the evils of the wicked enemies of truth, which increases their enmity and inclines their hearts toward his words. They affirm and obey this form and do what it commands. All this occurs only because there are similarities between these people and the wicked. In the same way, it casts whispers into the hearts of the truthful, misleads them, brings them near to Satanism (ablīsiyya), and plunges them into sin.

When this person dies, they abandon it and are absorbed into the dual principles (ʿuqdatayn), which is their Barzakh, and join the congregation of the former wicked forms. In this state, they are devils in potentiality (shayāṭīn bil-quwwa). Then they fall to the ground and through the will (qāmat al-fīhi) they reach devils in actuality (shayāṭīn bil-fiʿl). In the first state they wrought evil through others; now they are able to commit evil themselves. When the Hidden Imam (ḥijāb imām) appears—with whom retributions are to be fulfilled—they also appear in the world at that time so they may fulfill their revenge. Those of them whose evil forms become Muslims become praiseworthy jinns (jinn maḥmūd), while those who persist in disobedience remain condemned jinns (jinn manzūm).[^cite-8] Many deny the existence of jinn due to lack of knowledge.

Below the moon are two stars in dragon form. These two spheres do not fall within the circumferential circles—that is, they are frozen in place and possess no celestial spheres. Rāsh and Zanab are composed of people who continue to sin even in their state and remain steadfast in stubbornness. In Saqāra, which lies within the earth, the bodies of the multitude are punished, and the evil forms (ṣūrāt khabītha) are tormented. These two stars have become magnets for evil images. From there these wicked things cast their evil effects upon this world, causing all calamities and sinful acts. Their tactics run counter to the Imam's (upon whom be peace) tactics. These wicked forms shall be clothed before being cast into Saqāra. At the times of solar and lunar eclipses, the maneuvers of these three dual principles (ʿuqdatayn) begin. These evil conceptions remain in this world as long as the wise man wills, continuing to influence those like them and their likes.

**It is established**: In Rāsh are the enemies of the prophets (Naqaʾ); in Zanab are the enemies of the successors (Awṣiyāʾ) and the Imams. Therefore Zanab is more evil than Rāsh. Rāsh is the principal devil and Zanab is his minister, or Rāsh is the devil and Zanab the devil-deputy. On the Day of ʿAshūrā, these wicked ones clung to Shimr Dhi-l-Jawshan and made him commit the evil of the martyrdom of Imam al-Ḥusayn (upon whom be peace). In like manner, this wicked form struck the blow that killed the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (upon whom be peace), through Ibn Muljam the accursed.

When they pass through the seven punishments and reach the final stage, the angels lead them to severe torment. The waves of Hell lift them to the heat of the sky (asr—the sphere of fire) and then to the depths of the sky (zamharrīr—the cold sphere). The angels strike them with shooting stars (shahāb sāqiṭ)—that is, with falling meteors (rajm). They remain in this state as long as earth and sky rotate. When the advent of the Qāʾim (upon whom be peace) draws near, these evil souls will appear in the world. If they are held to account, their trumpets will fall upon their filthy bodies in the form of fire and burn them to ash. Then the ash shall enter through the rays of Saturn and Mars into the midst of Saqāra, where they shall continue to suffer for a very long time.

## Evil Forms Become the Opposites of the Sacred Stations

There is a gathering of evil conceptions (ʿuqdatayn). They are granted respite so that the person with whom they are to be taken may appear in this world. When both are present and the time for retribution arrives, the evil forms complete their reckoning. By the will of the wise man, these dual principles descend from the Aqṭāʿīn to the earth and are absorbed into food and drink. Those foods enter the sustenance of the deserving people, whether among the saints or the elders. This is why the children of the Imams are born and the children multiply; yet these wicked ingredients have no effect upon those who consume them; rather, they come to light through them. Similarly, the idols and false gods are made of these same wicked ingredients.[^cite-9]

When the dead are buried, their bodies rot and decompose in the grave, and their components scatter into the four elements. Vapors of filth ascend as fire, breath as air, phlegm as water, and bile dissolves into earth. These four vapors are then gathered from the universe; they become clouds and rain upon the earth. They enter water, soil, and humans to retrieve their residual parts from them. They are then formed into the bodies of wicked plants and animals, which are consumed by evil people like themselves. That is, these forbidden people gather their likes in order to take them to the other side or to give birth to their likes. They remain with them until the wise man wills their reappearance.

When the time comes for his appearance, he appears as a child through his parents, as a devil in potentiality (shayṭān bil-quwwa), and is trained until he becomes a devil in actuality (shayṭān bil-fiʿl). He then claims the status of truth and conceals its station. Sometimes he becomes so obstinate that he intends to kill the Imam in the same way that he hid his own nature—a spiritual murder—and kills the rightful heir in order to usurp his position.

Thus there was one who was the opposite of the Prophet Noah (upon whom be peace); one who was the opposite of Shem and killed him; one who was the opposite of the Prophet Abraham—such as Nimrod; one who was the opposite of the Prophet Moses; one who was the opposite of the Prophet Jesus. They set themselves against the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him and his family)—Abū Lahab and Abū Jahl and Walīd ibn Mas'ūd. For this reason, the Messenger of Allah declared: ⟪ar-quote:لکل نبی شیاطن ولی شیطانان⟫ *"Every prophet has a devil, but I have two."* And so with the Commander of the Faithful ʿAlī (upon whom be peace) came such stubbornness, and Ibn Muljam the accursed, who killed him in the end. The stubbornness of Muʿāwiya poisoned and killed Imam al-Ḥasan (upon whom be peace) through his wife Jaʿda bint al-Ash'ath. The stubbornness of Yazīd caused the martyrdom of Imam al-Ḥusayn (upon whom be peace) through the accursed Shimr. Likewise, the poison killed the Mustanṣir (upon whom be peace), and the poison of al-Khaybariyya killed the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him and his family). Two people were present at the meal with the Messenger of Allah. When one of them died, the Prophet prayed for the other, which saved him from the poison's effects. Yet that poison remained with the Prophet until it led to his death, coming from the Aqṭāʿīn.

**It is established**: The Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) mixed poison with the meat and herbs. As soon as she placed the herb in her mouth, she cried out, "O Messenger of Allah, poison has been mixed with me!" The Messenger immediately spat out the herb. In truth, it was composed of herbal and poisonous ingredients. Since the herb outranked the poison in station, it revealed its true condition to the Messenger of Allah.[^cite-10]

Sometimes these evil forms dwelling in the Aqṭāʿīn are revealed in a plant, and that vegetation becomes poisonous, causing the death of an Imam or a Nāṭiq (speaker). For example, the poison reached the Prophet (peace be upon him) or Imam al-Ḥasan. These plants, composed of evil-form components, arrive at the places where murder is to occur. When these wicked forms approach minerals, they take the form of knife and sword, which are used for attack and retaliation. Arrows also form from minerals, which strike and kill with the bow. The swords of Shimr and Ibn Muljam (may Allah's curse be upon them) were a gathering of such wicked forms.

All of this is enacted under the will of the wise man and according to Allah's balance of justice. The guardians of these evil forms are the three dual principles (ʿuqdatayn), who protect them until the one with whom they have reckoning appears. When there are Imams of divine power (ṣāḥib sayf), they establish boundaries for sinners and scourge or kill them. When such people die, their evil form differs from the dual principles, and then they appear in the form of poison and sword and complete the reckoning with the Imam. This is Allah's mighty justice.

The person who delivers this poison to the noble station is also a composite of these accursed forms. First, there are evil conceptions combined into three beliefs. Apart from these, the curses inflicted upon them are distinct, remaining in the beliefs. When the vilest person of the age appears, all these curses descend from the Aqṭāʿīn and accumulate in that person. When such a person commits atrocities, he commits injustice to the entire era—everyone is enveloped in his tyranny. The Imam cursed the people of Kūfa and Iraq because they abandoned him at his hour of need and oppressed him. He cursed them: "A man shall come to you who will afflict you most severely." These curses gathered in the Aqṭāʿīn, and then al-Ḥajjāj ibn Yūsuf al-Thaqafī, a most cruel person, came forth from the Aqṭāʿīn, inflicting terrible atrocities upon the people of Iraq and Kūfa.

## Munkar and Nakīr: The Evil Form

It is widely known from the traditions of the Prophet that at the moment of death, there come forth bringers of good tidings and those who deny. The reality is that the Speaker (the Imam) and Guardian of every age is a bringer of good tidings (mubashṣir) for those who love him, yet both become those who deny and challenge (munkir wa-nakīr) for those who hate him. In the same way, the Imam of the age is a bringer of good tidings for his contemporaries and those who acknowledge him, yet he becomes a denier for those who reject him. This is why the Commander of the Faithful ʿAlī (upon whom be peace) declared: ⟪ar-quote:یاَ حاَرِ ہَمداَن مَن یَّمُت یَرِنِی مِن مُومِنٍ اَو مُناَفِقٍ اَجَلا، یَعرِفُنیِ اِعتَرِفِہِ وَ عاَرِفُہُ بِأِسمِہٖ وَالْکُناَ وَماَ فَعَلا⟫ *"O Hāshim Darmān! Whoever dies shall soon see me—whether believer or hypocrite—and shall know me by his recognition or non-recognition, and I shall know him by his name, surname, and deeds."*[^cite-11]

### Denial and the Multiplication of Evil Form

On the contrary, one who is obstinate becomes, through ignorance, an enemy to his Lord and claims for himself what he does not merit. He becomes the leader of misguidance and the captain of error. When his evil imagination leaves his body, he becomes a devil, and his soul does not depart; it falls to the lowest depths (asfal al-sāfilīn). Then he is revealed to those like himself, misleading them, frightening them in sleep and waking, moving between earth and sky, making desolate places his dwelling. Allah says: "We have made them leaders calling to the Fire, and they shall not be helped on the Day of Resurrection."

When he dies, he acts by Allah's plan (a.s.), a plan different from the Satanic learning and wicked deeds he had prepared through stubbornness and enmity toward the people of truth. The form he created in his lifetime becomes at his death a dark person most terrifying. He abhors seeing it and is gripped with fear. That evil form frightens him, harms him, becomes terrible to him. Then he knows he is from the people of evil.

The Messenger of Allah said to Qays ibn Āsim: "O Qays, you have a companion with whom you shall be buried, alive while you are dead. If you are gracious, he shall have mercy upon you; if you are wicked, he shall deliver you unto evil, and your resurrection shall be with him, and you shall be questioned about this companion. Just make this companion virtuous! If he is good, you shall delight in him; if he is bad, you shall be horrified by him. He is your deed."

⟪ar:سیدنا حمید الدین⟫ Pointing to this very matter, he said to Azdā: "His evil deeds become beasts that frighten him and hurt him."[^cite-12]

## The Lower Torments

After death, the believer's body becomes self, which is his development; the non-believer's soul diffuses into the body so that when torment comes upon his body, he feels it. Soul and body unite and merge with soil. Then they rise like vapor and fall as rain. This rain produces lightning and thunder that are deadly, and floods that drown nations and destroy fields, producing bitter cold. The effect of these evil forms, when they cause trouble in this world, is also reckoned in this account. They are then ushered into the courtyard and led to the Bridges of Punishment, which has in each category seventy garments and in each garment seventy links. The person passes through all these garments while in torment and testifies that he entered this realm because of his enmity toward truth. At each station, he is told of what crime he is being punished. Were he not made aware, there would be no punishment; yet when suffering, he is often not conscious of his transgression.

### Abomination

When their rain reaches the ground, plants grow forth from it, eaten by animals. The beast then enters the food of the person on whom this wicked form must appear in the form of sperm—for this is the first garment, the beast-form (bashārī). These are nations with whom it is impossible to speak to the people of truth. It passes through seventy species of this shape, and seventy links in each species.

### The Transition

When they leave the first door, they enter the second, which is humanoid, with monkeys and wrenches. They are then clothed in this form with seventy garments and seventy links in each. This is monkey-form and wrench-form. Those who commit adultery and drink wine are brought to this category. It is in the nature of the monkey to commit adultery, and in the character of the wrench to eat and drink intoxicants and wine. The actions they happily performed in the world, nature now makes them do as punishment. They commit adultery repeatedly, and this act becomes like an itch—no matter how much they fulfill it, their desire is not satisfied.

**It is established**: If believers commit similar sins, they too are brought to this punishment and tormented. Yet they need not don seventy garments and seventy links in each. Nature punishes them according to justice, then returns them to human stature.[^cite-13]

### Wild Beasts

The third category is that of wild land and sea animals. There are lions, leopards, foxes, and creatures with claws and fangs, and they feel these torments. They tear and devour one another, and their accounts are settled. After donning the seventy garments, they proceed to the next door.

### The Mute

This is the station of silence. Until now the animals could make sounds; in this process, they become mute. These are creatures that move by crawling against the ground—deer, snakes, rats, scorpions, and the like.

### The Filthy

This is the station of birds. There are predatory birds such as eagles, crows, and hawks.

### The Defective

This stage concludes the realm, and it is the station of vegetation. This is why Allah says: "You shall be returned to ignorance." It is a state of ignorance after knowledge in which only growth (namū) remains. These are forbidden plants that slay the living.

### The Hardened

The final strain is of wicked minerals—hard and unclean stones. They construct wretched things.

## The Employed Forms

Their bodies suffer punishment, and their evil form is inflicted upon that body. Upon completing the punishment, they are invited to the Imam (upon whom be peace). If he refuses, he remains as a condemned jinn (jinn manzūm) and proceeds to the next punishment. If he repents and becomes Muslim, he becomes part of the praiseworthy group (jinn maḥmūd). He then ascends to the sun through the moon, and services are performed for him through which he remains. They are also called the employed forms (ṣūrāt mustakhdama). They number 140,000 and are of three types:

They are entrusted with various services in this world. They assist the believers and defend them if one of their former companions attempts to harm the Imam or the Prophets. When a person who is an employed form (ṣūrat mustakhdama) fulfills his obligatory services, he comes into the world through good food and drink, in cool and soothing air, and in the form of fragrance which his parents consume, from which sperm is produced, and it manifests in him. When he reaches maturity, he is invited to accept it and enters the realm of truth, rising swiftly in religious ranks and attaining very high stations (bāb al-abwāb). This is because in his state of evil, he repented and entered the realm of truth, fulfilling all his services. This creates in him tremendous acceptance, which shall make him traverse the religious stations most swiftly.

### The Incident of Ḥām ibn Ḥayy

One day, the Holy Prophet (peace be upon him) was sitting in his mosque surrounded by Muslims when suddenly a voice was heard, yet no one was seen. The Prophet asked, "Who are you?" The voice replied: "I am Ḥām ibn Ḥayy ibn Lāqis ibn Iblīs. I have believed in Allah, and now I bear witness that there is no god but Allah and that you are His Prophet. When Ḥām believed, he ascended to the sun through the moon until the Prophet Abraham appeared. Then he descended, believed in Abraham, and returned to the sun. He did likewise in the time of each Prophet. When the Messenger of Allah appeared, he descended from the sun to believe in him. He subsequently passed away and appeared (qāmat al-fīhi) for me. Whoever believes in a prophet in a previous age must believe in him even in his physical state. He was only given physical condition and rose to the level of a religious hierarchy (bāb), which is the limit of righteousness.

## The Middle Torments

These are the four corners of the earth where the climate is not moderate, and there are extreme temperatures and great and terrible calamities. Only those who have already undergone the first punishment arrive here, and these are the stations of the prophets and the Imams.

### The Northern Part: The Station of the Imams

The north is dark, painful, and terrifying, wherein the multitude of Imams is kept. There is no sunrise for six months, causing the bitter effect of the cold sphere (kurat al-zamharrīr) to be fully felt. Animals are kept there in beastly form, unable even to move from their place due to bitter cold, as though turned to stone. They endure the most severe and grievous punishment from the cold and the frigid zone. This is what Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (upon whom be peace) refers to when he speaks of ⟪ar-quote:برد الحدید⟫ *(the cold of iron)*.[^cite-14]

### The Southern Part: The Station of the Prophets

The southern part belongs to the heat zone, where the sphere of fire (kurat al-asir) shows its full effect. There is scorching heat and toxic winds. Here the multitude of prophets and messengers are punished. They are gathered in the form of wicked beasts of ugly creation, each as tall as a mountain. They do not possess external senses, yet they feel the torment completely.

### The Eastern Part: The Station of the Helpers of the Prophets

The east is where the sun rises, and there those who help the prophets are punished—those who opposed the prophets and messengers (aḍdād al-anbiyāʾ wa-l-mursalīn). There are towering, terrible mountains very close together because of their height. Here they are placed as if a noose has been drawn around their necks, bound in chains and chains, suffering greatly.

### The Western Part: The Station of the Helpers of the Imams

The west is dark and wavy, called the sea of jinn (baḥr al-jinn). Its waves are like mountains. This is the punishment for the helpers of the Imams. This part is inferior to the northern section of torment. In this sea, the helpers are cast into shapeless beasts where they devour and attack one another.

## The Return from the Extremal Torments

After enduring the punishment of the quarters, they are led to the inhabited quarter (rubʿ al-maskūn)—the fourth part of the earth where human beings dwell—where they appear in the Garden of Paradise (ʿAsīs) and are presented before the Qāʾim, then led to the Heaven of the Living.

Among those who appear are those who have turned away from torment, and some who have completed the suffering of the quarters. When they turn, they ascend the climbing staircase. First they take form in minerals beneficial to humans. From there they proceed to lawful plants, then to lawful animals. This form is eaten by the walī (guardian) or the stubborn one who becomes the conduit for its appearance. If a person's punishments have been fulfilled, he goes to the guardian; if remnants remain, he goes to the stubborn one. All this transpires under the will of Allah the Almighty. Then he appears (qāmat al-fīhi) and is presented with the guardianship (wilāya) of the one whom he had previously denied. If he accepts, he is saved from punishment and joins the people of truth; if he denies and is arrogant, he falls back to punishment. In this way the cycle of punishment and reward continues.[^cite-16]

When the advent of the Qāʾim of the Age (qāʾim al-qiyāma) approaches, all the conceptions of the six periods from the time of Adam until his own time appear. The condition of these multitudes is the same as it was at their death (denial and arrogance). Likewise, all prophets, imams, and guardians appear in person. All shall be stationed in the fields of the Day of Resurrection, and Allah shall judge between them in truth and shall not wrong anyone. When the Qāʾim appears, these multitudes, also referred to as Gog and Magog (Yaʾjūj wa-Maʾjūj), shall be drawn forth from the islands and appear before the Qāʾim. Their eyes shall be fixed upon the Qāʾim in amazement. ʿYāʾjūj (Gog) refers to the multiplication of places, while Maʾjūj (Magog) refers to the helpers of that multiplication.

## The Great Punishment

All the Ḥudūd (the cosmic limits), Prophets, and Imāms will gather in the presence of the Qāʾim, who is mentioned as ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫. They shall inform all creation of the deeds wrought by their multiplication. Thereafter, the Qāʾim will slay them all. A fire descends from them—⟪ar:عقدتین⟫—which consumes their defiled and lifeless forms; these ascend as vapor into ⟪ar:عقدتین⟫ and are absorbed within it. When the Aqtain reaches the lowest depths of the earth in its rotation, Saqqara becomes inflamed ⟪ar:عقدتین⟫ by the heat, for it is suited to receive it.[^cite-17]

Saqqara lies at the earth's nadir. The fever penetrates the interior of the sacred realm through its pores. Once within, the vapors evaporate and revert to their original state as they were in time. An example illuminates this: a brass vessel with a rigid mouth contains a jafdar (pit-shaped depression) in its center, filled with ⟪ar:کبریتی⟫ (sulphorous) stones. Saqqara is fashioned as an inverted earthen pot. Its upper and lower surfaces are sealed and rigid; ⟪ar:لاوا⟫ fills the middle section—which is Hell itself. Perforations in the lower part permit vapor to pass through, exit, and ascend. It resembles ⟪ar:بیت الخلا⟫—an exceedingly heated, wholly enclosed chamber. This is what the Qur'ān refers to as ⟪ar:سجین⟫.[^cite-18] The scholars have formulated this understanding based upon Saqqara's nature. The scorching fuel beneath Saqqara possesses a heat equivalent to that of the sun itself. As fire heats a pot from below, so too does the sun heat Suqra from beneath, causing the material of Saqqara—already intensely hot—to ignite.[^cite-19]

When these vapors become tormented, those who preceded them observe them and inform them ⟪ar:سقرا⟫ of why they were cast therein. Saqqara bears another designation: ⟪ar:بوطا⟫. Some shall cry to others, "Woe unto us! We were heedless, and we were among the wrongdoers." They shall be told, "Those whom you worshipped besides Allāh are also in Hell, serving as its fuel." Their assistants shall say to their Imāms, "Had you been true Imāms, we would not have been consigned to this saqqarah." All these people shall remain in this place eternally.

## The Description of Saqqara

Our earth is ⟪ar:بیضوی⟫ (oval) as well as ⟪ar:مسطح⟫ (flat-surfaced) and ⟪ar:مستوی⟫ (even); on its other side lies ⟪ar:سقرا⟫, which is ⟪ar:سجین⟫ (the record of the wicked) and ⟪ar:جہنم کبریٰ⟫ (Hell the Greater). The center of the earth's surface is the center of ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫ (the House of God). On the earth's opposite side is Saqqara, fashioned like a penetration whose lowest part is the abode of ⟪ar:اہل طاغوت⟫ (the people of tyranny). Four corners of the earth—called the sides of the earth—are where the punishment of the sides, of opponents and oppressors, is meted out. These sides adjoin Saqqara. So frigid are they that passage is impossible. In addition to the aforementioned openings (munāfiz) surrounding the terrestrial realm, many more exist in Rūbā Muskūn (the inhabited region); the greatest ⟪ar:دُردُور⟫ lies in the river between China and India, toward which all rivers flow. It is said that manifold sounds emanate from this opening. ⟪ar:نعوذ باللہ منھا⟫ (We seek refuge in God from it.)

When the sun revolves before the earth, it is day for those upon it and night for ⟪ar:اہل سقرا⟫ (the people of Saqqara). When ⟪ar:اہل سقرا⟫ the sun rises, there is day for them and night for the earth's inhabitants. In this single day and night, the Day of Judgment passes. In the days of Saqqara's people, extreme heat by day and bitter cold by night—beyond reckoning—assail them. When both affect them in succession, punishment increases a hundredfold.[^cite-20]

It is crucial to remember that the Almighty created Saqqara before the earth. In the Word of Allāh, He has granted Saqqara a distinct identity; earth, Saqqara, and sky are mentioned separately in a single verse. Nine planets and seven constellations orbit this earth and Saqqara. The heavens, being superficial, possess planar and ⟪ar:کواکب سیارۃ⟫ (wandering stars) rotation; their motion is both axial and rotational.

One significant utterance attributed to ⟪ar:سیدنا مالک⟫ (our master Mālik) at this juncture is that the earth is spread like a pot beneath the earth's surface. But where ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫ (the House of God), ⟪ar:مقام ابراھیم⟫ (the Station of Abraham), and ⟪ar:روضہ اقدس⟫ (the sacred garden) exist—such as ⟪ar:غدیر خم⟫ (Ghadīr Khum)—there is no Saqqara, but rather a state of space wherein dwell all ⟪ar:ارواح نورانیہ⟫ (luminous spirits) and all ⟪ar:ارواح حسنۃ⟫ (virtuous spirits). Conversely, in places such as ⟪ar:امیال⟫, ⟪ar:وادی شیطان⟫ (the Valley of Satan), and the ⟪ar:جمرات ثلاثہ⟫ (three stone pillars), there ⟪ar:ارواح خبیثہ⟫ (malevolent spirits) hold greater sway. And in the upper part of these holy places, opposite to the heavens, ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫ resides in the heavens themselves. There are pores in these places, yet these pores must not be understood as located in the heavens; rather, they are free from spatial limitation, wholly spiritual and transcendent.

The center of the entire universe is the Sun, which orbits the Earth. Only human fermentation can manifest upon Earth. The earth derives its essence from the sun. All celestial bodies orient themselves toward the earth so that humanity—⟪ar:استخراج انسانیت⟫ (the extraction of humanity)—may emerge here. Thus it has been said that the development of humankind shall occur from the earth, and humanity has not been created elsewhere.[^cite-21]

The true land (the land of the feast) possesses only ⟪ar:سقرا⟫, ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫, and ⟪ar:مقدس مقامات⟫ (sacred stations) upon its platform. The land scattered about the remainder is not feast-land. The authentic earth is centered upon ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫. It is surrounded by dust—a sphere of clay—and Saqqara lies beneath the earth. Saqqara was created before the earth; these ⟪ar:مصر ومستکبرین⟫ (arrogant and haughty ones) constitute its substance. ## Do the People of Punishment Know They Once Possessed Human Stature?

They say indeed they know! When they exist in the presence of animals and change their forms, they are made cognizant of the sin for which they are punished. They merely war with one another and slay each other, but as soon as one dies, he returns to life and fighting resumes. This punishment endures for a lengthy ⟪ar:کور اعظم⟫ (age of darkness). Regret avails naught at this juncture. When their sentence concludes, they are resurrected in ⟪ar:انسانی قامت⟫ (human stature), and the punishment they have endured is forgotten.
