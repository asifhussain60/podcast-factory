# First Mind

<!-- section 1 (id=1075, raw_sort=1): عقل اول کی توحید تجرید تنزیہ -->

## Monotheism, Abstraction, and Transcendence of the First Intellect

One of the innumerable luminous essences (⟪ar:شبیح⟫, ⟪ar:اشباح نورانی⟫) who had been created together with all the others—the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal)—in a state of primary perfection (kamāl awwal, ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫)—began to contemplate himself and his own genus without external inspiration (mulḥim, ⟪ar:مُلحِم⟫). For this mystical state, the Commander of the Faithful, al-Muʾayyad bi-Allāh (⟪ar:سیدنا مؤید اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫), declared: ⟪ar-quote:نَظَرَ وَ فَکَرَ وَ حَجَمَ وَ عَلِمَ⟫ "He saw, he thought, he withdrew, and he knew."[^cite-1]

He turned his gaze inward—**observation** (naẓar, ⟪ar:نظر⟫)—and began to contemplate the jewel of selfhood within him. Through **reflection** (fikr, ⟪ar:فکر⟫), he pondered: Am I the source of my own existence? In this rumination, he addressed the relationship to himself (⟪ar-quote:نسبت الی ذاتہ⟫). It then became clear to him: I lack the power and capacity to create myself. He examined all beings of his own rank and considered whether they had fashioned him. He realized this was impossible, for all were equal to him in station. If he could not originate himself, neither could they originate him—neither individually nor collectively could such beings as himself generate his existence.

### Monotheism

When he grasped that he was neither self-originated nor created by his peers, a profound **recoil** (ḥujūm, ⟪ar:حجوم⟫) seized his intellection. In that moment he **knew** (ʿalima, ⟪ar:عَلِمَ⟫) that there must exist another—utterly unlike him and all created beings—one who is absolutely singular (waḥīd, ⟪ar:أحد⟫) and unique. For this realization, he turned his attention to the relationship to his Originator (⟪ar-quote:نسبت الی من أوجدہ⟫).[^cite-2]

### Abstraction

In this process, he first performed **negation** (nifāʾ, ⟪ar:نفی⟫) of divinity (ilāhīya, ⟪ar:الہیت⟫) from himself and all other beings, confessing that none of them possess divinity—that is, none possesses the power to draw existence from absolute nothingness. He then performed **affirmation** (ithbāt, ⟪ar:اثبات⟫) of divinity for the Originator, Most High (Mubdiʿ Taʿālā, ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫), bearing witness to His oneness (waḥdānīya, ⟪ar:وحدانیت⟫) and engaging in glorification and sanctification (tasbīḥ wa-taqaddus, ⟪ar:تسبیح وتقدیس⟫).[^cite-3]

### Transcendence

He confessed his own inferiority by humbling himself before God, bearing witness to divinity by adopting the attributes of greatness, majesty, and sanctity. He established servitude (ʿubūdīya, ⟪ar:عبودیت⟫) to the Originator, Most High, cleansing himself of all attributes belonging to creatures, and confessing that the Creator transcends every attribute of His creation. Through this very humility—this profound monotheism (tawḥīd, ⟪ar:توحید⟫)—he attained perfection and succeeded in his sacred endeavor.

Because of this monotheism of the First Intellect, all subsequent beings' confession was established between negation and affirmation, and they can never escape this bond. Their confession remains hidden within the spiritual world (ʿālam rūḥānī, ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫), journeying from negation to affirmation—a religious journey between "no god" (lā ilāh, ⟪ar:لا⟫) and "except Allah" (illā-Allāh, ⟪ar:اللہ⟫).

| **Negation** | **Religious Journey** | **Affirmation** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ⟪ar:الہ⟫ | Tawḥīd | Allah |

When the First Intellect confessed before the Originator, Most High, he articulated what the Qur'ān confirms: ⟪ar-quote:شَھِدَ اللہُ اَنَّہُ لاَ اِلٰہ اِلاَّ ھُو⟫ "Allah bears witness that there is none worthy of worship save Him."[^cite-4] The three letters of "Allah"—**knowledge** (ʿilm, ⟪ar:علم⟫), **power** (qudra, ⟪ar:قدرت⟫), and **life** (ḥayāt, ⟪ar:حیات⟫)—signify three essences. Through these three essences, the First Intellect acknowledged: although these jewels are supremely noble, they cannot serve as a path to the Originator, Most High. He acknowledged that we reach only to the negation of divinity from ourselves (lā ilāh, ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫) but cannot attain the affirmation of His essence (illā Huwa, ⟪ar:ھویت (الا ھو)⟫).

### Why Was It Called "Intellect"?

In Arabic, the word ʿaqala (⟪ar:عَقَلَ⟫)—to bind, to secure—derives from the practice of tethering a camel's feet. Without such restraint, the animal cannot be protected. Thus intellect is the name for confining oneself within the commandments and boundaries (awāmir wa-nawāḥī, ⟪ar:أوامر و نواحی⟫). The Creator brought forth all souls in holiness. Yet the First Intellect bound itself within the precinct of the Word of Divine Unity (kalima at-tawḥīd) through monotheism of the Creator and fettered itself to the limits of that Word, understanding that knowledge of the Creator through the Word was impossible to transcend. Thus the term ⟪ar:عقل⟫ (intellect) came to be applied to it.[^cite-5]

---

### Equality Between the First Intellect and the Respondent in Monotheism

In his work *Treasures of Wisdom Hoarded for the Masters of the Divine Names* (Khunūz al-Ḥikmatayn al-Madhkūra li-Arbāb al-Asmāʾ), al-Sayyid ʿAlī ibn Hibat Allāh (⟪ar:سیدنا علی بن ھبۃ اللہ⟫) states that in the knowledge of God, the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal) and the Respondent (al-Mustajīb, ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫) stand equal and parallel, for both are unable to fully comprehend Him.[^cite-6] The luminous principle proclaimed: ⟪ar-quote:بَینَ المُبدِعِ وَالمُبدَعِ کَلِمَۃ⟫ *"Between the Originator and the emanated is the Word."* The First Intellect could not fully fathom the Word. The ten veils of bliss that envelope the Originator astonish and perplex the First Intellect within itself. This is why the Prophet (peace be upon him) taught: ⟪ar-quote:منَ عَرَفَ نَفسُہُ فَقَد عَرَفَ رَبُّہُ⟫ "Whoever knows his own soul knows his Lord." If you have erred in knowledge of your own soul and cannot grasp even it, how can you claim to know your Lord, the Originator Most High?

## Quantity and Quiddity of the First Intellect

I establish two perspectives on the monotheism of the Originator, Most High, regarding the First Intellect:

### Quantity

**Quantity** (kumīya, ⟪ar:کمیت⟫) refers to the appearance, covering, or manifest aspect of an object. When the First Intellect first began to contemplate himself, he gazed upon himself and his genus, pondering: "Have I created all these beings?" and then: "Have they created me?"

### Quiddity

**Quiddity** (lumīya, ⟪ar:لمیت⟫) refers to origin, interior, or essential reality. When the First Intellect came to know that neither he nor his kind possessed divinity (ilāhīya)—the power of origination—he realized that there must exist One entirely unlike us: the Creator of myself and all of us, utterly untouchable and exalted beyond all our attributes.[^cite-7] Since his gaze was fixed upon that Other, the Originator Most High, which was genuinely divine, his gaze became exalted.

The First Intellect's initial relation was to his own essence (ذات, dhāt), to his own nature (⟪ar:نسبت الی ذاتہ⟫). This relation was inferior because it concerned the created (makhluq, ⟪ar:مخلوق⟫). Through this, he confessed his humility and weakness, negating divinity from himself. His secondary relation was to the Creator's attribute—the Originator, Most High—upon which he fixed the power of divinity and acknowledged His superiority and greatness, testifying to His divinity: ⟪ar-quote:شھد اللہ انہ لا الہ الا ھو⟫ "Allah bears witness that there is no god but He." This relation was superior because it was directed toward the Creator (khāliq, ⟪ar:خالق⟫).

The First Intellect perceived and received first **primary perfection** (kamāl awwal) and then secondary perfection (kamāl thānī, ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫) through monotheism of the Originator, Most High. Although he attained secondary perfection through the exalted gaze (naẓar ashruf, ⟪ar:نظر اشرف⟫), he achieved it through vision of the higher kind. From these two gazes emerged the principle: the outward must first be understood before the inner can be grasped. For this reason, the external laws of the Sharīʿa are comprehended first, and only then are their inner meanings and realities unveiled.

| **Quantity** | **Quiddity** |
| --- | --- |
| *Lā ilāh* | *Illā Hu* |
| Relation to the Self (Created) | Relation to the Creator of Existence |
| Outer Covering | Esoteric Principle |
| Sharīʿa (Law) | Meaning (Taʾwīl) |

## Secondary Perfection and Knowledge of What Has Been and What Will Be

When the First Intellect completed the acts of monotheism (tawḥīd), abstraction (tajrīd), and transcendence (tanzīh), he drank from the wellspring of the Creator's greatness and majesty, becoming devoted to Him through these means. As reward for this deed, the Originator, Most High, bestowed upon him sacred matter—the luminous substance of the Word "There is no god save Allah" (nūr kalima lā ilāha illā-Allāh, ⟪ar:مواد قدسی (نور کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ)⟫)—which was called **flowing knowledge** (ʿilm jārī, ⟪ar:علم جاری⟫) and **encompassing light** (nūr sārī, ⟪ar:نور ساری⟫). This is also called **the Command** (al-amr, ⟪ar:الأمر⟫)—the intention of the Originator, Most High. In the radiance of this luminous Word, the First Intellect acquired knowledge of all past and future things (mā kān wa-sayakūn, ⟪ar:ما کان⟫⟪ar:سیکون⟫).

### The Past

Al-Sayyid ʿAlī ibn Sulaymān (⟪ar:سیدنا علی بن سلیمان⟫) states that knowledge of the past (mā kān, ⟪ar:ما کان⟫) was that the Originator, Most High, brought existence into being **not from anything** (⟪ar-quote:لا من شئی⟫), **not within anything** (⟪ar-quote:ولا فی شئی⟫), **not by means of anything** (⟪ar-quote:ولا بشئی⟫), and **not alongside anything** (⟪ar-quote:ولا مع شئی⟫).

### The Future

In knowledge of the future (sayakūn, ⟪ar:سیکون⟫), he came to know how the summons (daʿwa, ⟪ar:دعوة⟫) would be established thereafter, how many intellects would embrace monotheism, how many souls would exist, how many would fall into the physical world through disobedience, how many would be freed from it, how many Imāms there would be in each cycle, how many believers each Imām would gather, how the entire cosmos would be constituted, how it would develop, and when the final resurrection (qāmat al-sāʿa, ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫) would occur. In essence, the entire map of future affairs was established within his intellection.

| **The Past** | **The Future** |
| --- | --- |
| Establishment of Both Gazes (Exalted and Lower) | Secondary Perfection Achieved Through These Two Gazes |

The First Intellect attained all knowledge of what has been and what will be in an instant, precisely as the Qur'ān declares:

> ⟪quran 54:50⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَمْرُنَآ إِلَّا وَحِدَةٌۭ كَلَمْحٍۭ بِٱلْبَصَرِ ‎⟫
> *And Our Commandment is but one, as the twinkling of an eye.*
> — **Quran 54:50**

The First Intellect received all knowledge of what has been and will be—encompassing the spiritual, subtle, and corporeal worlds—**in potentiality** (bi-al-quwwa, ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫). All that remained was their **actualization** (bi-al-fiʿl, ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫). This material was bound to the sacred essence, and through it secondary perfection was attained. Because he was the first to accomplish monotheism, he was called the First (al-Awwal), the Pen (al-Qalam), and the Precedent (al-Sabīq).

## Essence and Form of the First Intellect

The Originator, Most High, created the **essence** (dhāt, ⟪ar:ذات⟫) of the First Intellect in a state of primary perfection. His **form** (sūra, ⟪ar:صورت⟫) was brought into being by the union of the acts of monotheism and the sacred matter. In the state of primary perfection, he existed as essence alone; yet in the state of secondary perfection, his essence emerged in luminous form comprising knowledge, power, and life (⟪ar:حیات⟫, ⟪ar:قدرت⟫, ⟪ar:علم⟫). Form surpasses bare essence; it is superior to the way essence existed in primary perfection. The sacred matter—the luminous Word released by the Originator, Most High—was even higher than essence itself. For without that light, the First Intellect would possess no luminous form. Therefore it is said:

*Between the Originator and the Emanated (the First Intellect) is the Word.*

The Word surpasses both intellect and Creator in certain respects: it is higher than intellect yet below the Word-giver. The First Intellect has been described as the **Holy Spirit** (rūḥ al-Quds, ⟪ar:روح القدس⟫). The word "spirit" (rūḥ, ⟪ar:روح⟫) refers to the luminous Word—"There is no god but Allah"—and "holy" (al-quds, ⟪ar:القدس⟫) refers to the sacred essence of the First Intellect.[^cite-8]

| **Spirit** | **Holy** |
| --- | --- |
| Luminous Word: *Lā ilāha illā-Allāh* | The First Intellect |

## The Veils of Joy of the First Intellect

When the First Intellect encountered the luminous Word and attained his station of unity (aḥdīya, ⟪ar:أحدیت⟫), his reverence, humility, and awe intensified with the recognition of the Creator's greatness. Knowledge begets fear; humility compounds with understanding. Upon receiving this knowledge, he achieved perfect intellection and attained yet more of eternality (azalīya, ⟪ar:ازلیت⟫) and perpetuality (abadīya, ⟪ar:ابدیت⟫).

Upon receiving this reward from the Originator of the Originator, the First Intellect trembled with joy. As Ḥaḍrat Ẓiyāʾ ʿAlī would explain, consider a student who excels beyond all classmates: such a student's joy is immense even if he has surpassed only a handful of peers. How, then, can one measure the joy of the First Intellect, who surpassed the infinite multitude of beings in the spiritual realm? His bliss transcends the imagination of thought, the pen of the writer, and the conception of reason.[^cite-9]

The reward that the First Intellect received for performing the act of monotheism manifested as ten veils of happiness enfolding him. Though the Creator had endowed all beings with light and holiness from their inception, now through the support of the Originator, that light began to grow and radiate (shaʿshāʿān, ⟪ar:شعشعانی⟫), becoming distinct from all others.

## The Two Joys of the First Intellect

The joys of the First Intellect manifested in two modes:

### The Exalted Joy

His first joy arose from the true monotheism of the Originator Almighty—the very purpose for which all beings were created. He negated divinity from himself, himself, and all creatures through the act of monotheism, abstraction, and transcendence (fiʿl tawḥīd, tajrīd, tanzīh, ⟪ar:فعلِ توحید، تنزیہ، تجرید⟫), and he singularized the Creator's attribute of divinity. His elation sprang from having accomplished the primordial deed for which the Almighty had created him. In this rejoicing, his gaze was fixed upon his Creator.

### The Lower Joy

His second joy was rooted in his being with all other essences. It was based on his pleasure and precedence—the thought that he had gone ahead of all other essences in performing monotheism of the Originator, Most High, and accomplished it first (sābiq, ⟪ar:سابق⟫). This was the lower joy, for in it his gaze was directed toward beings like himself.

| **Lower Joy** | **Exalted Gaze** |
| --- | --- |
| *Lā ilāh* | *Illā Hu* |
| Looking at Creation | Looking at the Creator |
| The Joy of Success in Monotheism | The Joy of Recognition in Monotheism |
| Abundance of Gender | Oneness of the Originator |

Al-Sayyid Ḥamīd al-Dīn al-Kirmānī (⟪ar:سیدنا حمید الدین کرمانی اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫) offers this example: A person enters a mosque intending to pray. His primary purpose is to worship Allah Almighty. The joy he derives from this true intention is the exalted gaze. Upon entering, he perceives that he has arrived first and surpassed all other worshippers in precedence. This perception of being foremost gives him the joy of seeing those who follow. The joy derived from this secondary perception is the lower gaze.[^cite-10]

These two gazes function in every sphere. When we eat, we regard its primary purpose—to sustain bodily powers so we may persist in righteous deeds and various acts of worship. This is the noble gaze. The secondary gaze concerns the taste and pleasure of food, which is the lower gaze.

## The Two Forms of Worship of the First Intellect

Allah says in the Qur'ān: "I have not created jinn and mankind except to worship Me."

> ⟪quran 51:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا خَلَقْتُ ٱلْجِنَّ وَٱلْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ ‎⟫
> *And I (Allah) created not the jinns and humans except they should worship Me (Alone).*
> — **Quran 51:56**

Worship in its truest essence is monotheism of the Originator, Most High. The First Intellect dwelt in the spiritual realm through two modes of worship.

**Scientific Worship** (ʿibāda ʿilmīya, ⟪ar:عبادت علمی⟫) was his contemplation of the Originator, Most High—his negation of divinity from himself and affirmation of His essence (nifāʾ al-ilāhīya wa-ithbāt al-huwīya, ⟪ar:نفی الٰہیت⟫ and ⟪ar:اثبات ھویت⟫). **Practical Worship** (ʿibāda ʿamalīya, ⟪ar:عبادت عملی⟫) was his vocalization of the Word—"There is no god but Allah." These two modes existed in the First Intellect: one was his intention (nīya), the other his action (ʿamal). His intention ranked higher. This is why the Prophet (peace be upon him) taught: ⟪ar-quote:اِنَّماَ اَلاَعماَلُ بِالنِّیاَّت⟫ "Deeds are valued by their intentions." And again: ⟪ar-quote:نِیَت اَلمُومِن اَبلَغُ مِن عَمَلِہِ⟫ "The intention of a believer speaks louder than his action."[^cite-11]

His intention arose from the scientific form (sūra ʿilmīya, ⟪ar:علمی صورت⟫) of his thinking. When he performed monotheism and expressed his intention in words, he acquired the practical form (sūra ʿamalīya, ⟪ar:عملی صورت⟫). The practical form eclipsed the scientific form. Although he possessed knowledge of true monotheism within himself, perfection was not attained until he gave it utterance. Through this vocalization, he actualized secondary perfection.[^cite-12] The Qur'ān has merged the words of the First Intellect into its illuminating pages:

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah bears witness that La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), and the angels, and those having knowledge (also give this witness); (He is always) maintaining His creation in Justice. La ilah illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.*
> — **Quran 3:18**

| **Intention** | **Covenant** |
| --- | --- |
| Scientific Form | Practical Form |

In the same manner, all believers initially exist in primary perfection, but upon taking the covenant (bayʿa), their status ascends to secondary perfection. Yet the covenant alone does not perfect one's form, though intention be sincere. One must maintain that covenant throughout life to nurture the practical form. This is why the era of the Imām of the Age is so vital: the covenant taken in mere minutes through hand-clasp with a righteous guide encompasses all the remaining years of one's spiritual life.

## The Divinity and Chieftaincy of the First Intellect

In Arabic, to make a noun definite, one adds the definite article (ḥarf al-maʿrifa (al), ⟪ar:حروف معرفۃ (ال)⟫). For example, "a book" (kitāb) may refer to any book, but "the book" (al-kitāb) indicates a particular one. The three letters of "the divine" (al-ilāh, ⟪ar:الہ⟫)—alif, lām, and hāʾ—signify three jewels: knowledge, power, and life. In potentiality, all intellects stand in the rank of the divine. But when one is attached to the definite article and becomes "Allah" (⟪ar:(ال+الہ)⟫ ⟪ar:اللہ⟫), he attains the rank of chieftaincy through secondary perfection. The First Intellect, in primary perfection, exists as "the divine"; through secondary perfection, he becomes "Allah."[^cite-13]

| **Device** | **Allah** |
| --- | --- |
| Wisdom of Eternity | First Mind |

| ***Lā ilāh*** | ***Illā Huwa*** |
| --- | --- |
| The Created | The Essence |

Because of monotheism, the First Intellect became singular and unique, without peer in his circle—as all other intellect-realms were subsequently formed. This is called the circle of unity (dāʾira waḥda, ⟪ar:دائرہ وحدت⟫). He possessed an eternity (azalīya) that was complete and perpetual. Through attainment of the sacred matter, he attained elevation, greatness, majesty, blessedness, and immaculacy—all created beings and creation came under his knowledge, and he surpassed them all.[^cite-14]

### The Divine Names

The First Intellect became the counter (ʿādd) and was not reckoned among the counted (muʿaddad). He is the beginning of enumeration of the first emanator's emanation (al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal, ⟪ar:منبعث اول⟫) in primordial uniqueness (aḥad, ⟪ar:أحد⟫). However, he merited the greatest names (asmāʾ aʿẓam), the supreme names (asmāʾ al-aʿlā), and the divine names (asmāʾ al-ilāhīya) for two reasons:

| **The First Reason** | He was bewildered and astonished by the realization of his Creator's identity, humbling himself in the knowledge of the Creator. |
| --- | --- |
| **The Second Reason** | The entire spiritual world stood astonished and amazed at the deed of monotheism (which made him singular) and the greatness and majesty he would derive. They marveled that the First Intellect performed monotheism from his own essence when in primary perfection all were equal to him. |

It was due to the desperate effort he expended to realize the Creator's divinity. The more he wished to advance toward it, the more his servitude would restrain and turn him back. Since this state applied to none but the First Intellect, it became his alone and became reserved for him as "the divine." All other beings learned servitude by witnessing the First Intellect. He became the primordially final—that is, none of the infinite beings will ever surpass him. Thus he became the First and the Last, and none can ever remove him from his station.[^cite-15]

The Originator, Glorified, made the First Intellect His veil and gate, establishing the principle that all other intellects shall not attain perfection until they acknowledge his primacy and greatness, and their light shall not touch the Word "There is no god but Allah." All intellects are dependent upon the substance which the First Intellect receives from the Originator, Most High. They yearn for knowledge, mercy, blessings, and grace from him. Just as the other heavens are heated and set in motion by the movements of the celestial sphere, and spiritual blessings are established in the lower heavens of the religious boundaries through this circulation, so too do the other intellects and worlds remain alive through the warmth of the First Intellect's knowledge and his bestowals. The First Intellect is the possessor of movement and blessing (ṣāḥib al-ḥaraka wa-l-baraka). In other words, all worlds depend upon his salvation, and all seek his mercy, while the First Intellect depends upon none. This is the meaning of the verse:

> ⟪quran 28:88⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَدْعُ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ إِلَهًا آخَرَ ۘ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ كُلُّ شَىْءٍ هَالِكٌ إِلَّا وَجْهَهُۥ ۚ لَهُ ٱلْحُكْمُ وَإِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ ‎⟫
> *And invoke not any other ilah (god) along with Allah, La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He). Everything will perish save His Face. His is the Decision, and to Him you (all) shall be returned.*
> — **Quran 28:88**

The Face of Allah that shall never perish is the First Intellect! He was the first to glorify the Originator, Most High, and to follow in His footsteps, preserving all beings. The Originator, Most High, made his survival the survival of all. All intellects turn toward him in relation. He is the finest of all essences and the most perfect of all elements. All turn to him, and from him flows all completeness and perfection. With him all began, and in him all shall end.

## The Beautiful Names of the First Intellect

The glory of the First Intellect differs from all others. He journeyed through the spiritual realm and the station of unity without external guidance, becoming a plateau of knowledge. He began wisdom by contemplating not only monotheism, abstraction, and transcendence of the Originator, Most High, but by performing these acts on behalf of all other beings—through his essence, for his essence, and from his essence. Through this deed of monotheism, he became a predecessor whom none could equal, and thereby he became worthy of the noble names that no other being would ever merit. The ninety-nine attributes of Allah manifest in their entirety within the First Intellect.[^cite-16]

### The First Creator and the First Created

He is the Creator and the first to be created. The word "created" encompasses all those brought into being, yet only the First Intellect is called "the First Creator," because he was the first to perform the deed of monotheism that none else performed, and he was the first to attain secondary perfection.

### The First Existent

He is existence itself, and he is the First Existent. The term "existent" applies to all who come forth from nothingness into being, yet only the First Intellect is called "the First Existent," for his essence entered into being and attained secondary perfection first.

### The Truth and the First Truth

He is the truth and the first truth. He was called truth because he was the first to know the reality of all things in the light of knowledge of what has been and what will be. He is the First Truth because he was the first to stand firm upon the truth in the spiritual realm.

### Unity, the Unique, and the First Unique

He is unity and the first unique. Unity is because through monotheism he reached a station none else has reached and none ever shall, becoming "the one." The First Unique is because none was with him in precedence. Upon this, the number is founded, and from it enumeration flows.[^cite-17]

### The Perfect and the First Perfect

He is perfection and the first perfect. Perfection bears the number seven; because of this, all others were perfected, and the seven intellects and their circles came into being. He is the first because he was the first to complete his deed of monotheism.

### Completeness and the First Complete

He is completeness and the first complete. Completeness bears the number ten. It is called completeness because the perfection of others derives from his knowledge—he was the first to receive it in secondary perfection—and through the acquisition of the sacred matter from him, ten intellects were compiled.[^cite-18]

### Eternity and the First Eternal

He is the eternal and the first to be eternal. He was called eternal because through his knowledge and obedience, others were made eternal, their eternity becoming everlasting. He is the First Eternal because he gained eternality from the knowledge of the luminous Word and was the first to do so. Now all others shall gain knowledge through him.

### The Intellect and the First Intellect, the Wise and the First Wise

*Intellect literally means to bind, to take into restraint.*

He is the intellect and the first of all. He was called intellect because he became the intellect of his scholars through his deeds and the knowledge he received. He is the First Wise because he first restrained himself from what was not his—that is, he denied the attribute of divinity from his own essence.[^cite-19]

### Knowledge and the First Knower

He is the knower and the first scholar. He was called knower because the flowing and encompassing knowledge reached him and through him became the center of knowledge for all others. He is the First Scholar because the flowing and encompassing knowledge was first joined unto him by the Originator, Most High.

### Power and the First Powerful

He is power and the first powerful. He was called powerful because from him flowed rulership, obedience, and glorification and sanctification that came after him. He is the First Powerful because he was the first to be able to accomplish his deed of monotheism.

### Life and the First Living

He is life and the first living. He was called life because he became true life unto those who came after him, and bestowed eternal life upon all others. If not for him, all other beings would not have advanced toward eternal life. He is the First Living because he was the first to come into contact with the spirit of true life from the Originator, Most High. The power of life which the Creator gave to his soul made him living, eternal, and everlasting.

### Precedence and the First Precedent

He is precedence and the first precedent. The word "precedence" is used because his station is an extreme that no other swift runner could reach. He is the First Precedent because he first gained precedence over his own kind.[^cite-20]

### Infallibility and the First Invulnerable

He is infallibility and the first invulnerable. For whosoever speaks unto him is cleansed from sin and error. And since he was the first protected from all fault and misguidance by the light of the Word "There is no god but Allah," he is also called the First Invulnerable.

### The Creator of the Ages and the First Age

He is the creator of the ages and himself the first age. He is the one who performs the action through negation and affirmation, the one who causes monotheism to happen, and the one who teaches eternal life. Because of him and by his example, all others proceeded to the action of beings and became eternal. That is why he is called the Creator of the Ages, for the ages are in reality the upper limits, and the First Intellect is the one who supports them through divine sciences. He is the first age because he was the first to make himself the upper limit and bestow upon himself eternality, establishing within himself true existence and immortality, supporting it through his actions.

## Why Was the First Intellect Called the Word?

The true intellect is the essence of the First Intellect because he was the first to utter the Word. He testified to his Creator, as the Qur'ān affirms:

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah bears witness that La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), and the angels, and those having knowledge (also give this witness); (He is always) maintaining His creation in Justice. La ilah illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.*
> — **Quran 3:18**

He is called the Word because he was the first to articulate this utterance in the spiritual realm. The scientific and practical qualities of this Word were born in him and came into being through him. All other intellects and realms refer to him, for they are brought into secondary perfection because of the First Intellect, while the First Intellect refers to none. The seven intellects have been named words (kalimāt) because of their relation to the First Intellect. Those intellects were meaningless letters until the First Intellect organized them into meaningful words.[^cite-21]

Just as the deficient refers to the perfect and the weak to the powerful, so all intellects, though they have attained the heights of grace, nobility, blessing, and perfection as shown by the First Intellect, remain surpassed by his precedence. The more all intellects progress through monotheism and dual forms of worship—scientific and practical—the more they will become superior to themselves, yet still surpassed by him. The treasures of divine blessing cannot be diminished. Although with time the joys and blessings of all intellects will increase, none shall ever equal the happiness and blessings of the First Intellect.

## Movement and Stillness

The Originator, Most High, brought forth His command through the word "Be" (kun), which comprises two letters. The letter Kāf (ك) signifies movement, and the letter Nūn (ن) signifies stillness.

| **Kāf** | **Nūn** |
| --- | --- |
| Movement | Stillness |

In the second verse of Sūrat al-Fātiḥa—⟪ar-quote:الحمد للہ رب العالمین⟫ "Praise be to Allah, Lord of the Worlds"—the Lord is the essence of the First Intellect, while Allah is the Originator, Most High. The essence of the First Intellect is worthy of praise because the Creator brought him forth in a state of complete and perfect primacy. It is intellectually necessary that he who receives existence directly from the Perfect must himself be perfect in every way; otherwise, doubt would fall upon the Creator's perfection. Since the First Intellect must be perfect, he must also be characterized by stillness and not movement.[^cite-22]

The general principle is that movement belongs to that which lacks something—it moves to remedy this deficiency or satisfy its need. Since the First Intellect, having attained secondary perfection, is perfect in every way and lacks nothing, he has no need to move. This First Creator is called the Pen (al-Qalam) in the language of the Sharīʿa. Regarding the pen, the Prophet (peace be upon him) declared: "Whatever shall come into existence until the Day of Judgment has been written by the pen upon the preserved tablet." After this, there is no need for movement. After the Pen come things that have appeared in various forms—whether subtle or corporeal—and because these means and resources are themselves deficient, they are compelled to move toward perfection so that they may become complete and free from defect. Where perfection is attained, movement ceases to be need-driven; instead, there emerges a subtle movement born of gratitude alone, not of seeking. His actions are acknowledgments of the benefaction and mercy of the Bestower. For this reason, there is also a delicate motion in the realm of monotheism of the First Intellect—a gesture of gratitude toward the Creator. Were this not so, the stillness of the First Intellect would merge with the stillness of the Almighty Himself.

In this physical world, the Messenger of Allāh (peace be upon him) stands in the rank of the First Intellect, his former and latter sins forgiven by Allāh, as He has given glad tidings:

> ⟪quran 48:2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لِّيَغْفِرَ لَكَ ٱللَّهُ مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِن ذَنۢبِكَ وَمَا تَأَخَّرَ وَيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكَ وَيَهْدِيكَ صِرَطًۭا مُّسْتَقِيمًۭا ‎⟫
> *That Allah may forgive you your sins of the past and the future, and complete His Favour on you, and guide you on the Straight Path.*
> — **Quran 48:2**

Yet despite these glad tidings, he labored so intensely in worship that his feet became swollen and his joints ached from constant bowing and prostration. Witnessing his condition, a companion asked, "O Messenger of Allāh! When Allāh has forgiven all your sins, why do you exert yourself so in worship?" He replied: ⟪ar-quote:افلا احب ان اکون عبداً شکوراً ؟⟫ "Should I not be a grateful servant to my Beloved?"[^cite-23] This tradition makes clear that after attaining perfection, movement is not driven by necessity but by gratitude alone.

## The Word of the First Intellect: Remembrances of Divine Light

Wherever there is emanation (inbiʿāth, ⟪ar:انبعاث⟫), there are correspondingly more forms. The same applies to remembrances (anwār dhikriyya, ⟪ar:أنوار ذکریہ⟫). The word Qur'ān derives from the root meaning "connection," signifying that the Qur'ān is bound to the People of the House (Ahl al-Bayt, ⟪ar:آل⟫). Whatever the People of the House perform becomes the command of the Qur'ān, and whatever the Qur'ān utters becomes the deed of the People of the House.

Allāh the Almighty said in His Book:

> ⟪quran 15:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّا نَحْنُ نَزَّلْنَا ٱلذِّكْرَ وَإِنَّا لَهُۥ لَحَفِظُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily We: It is We Who have sent down the Dhikr (i.e. the Quran) and surely, We will guard it (from corruption).*
> — **Quran 15:9**

And He said to the People of Remembrance—those in the station of divine essence—whom He inspired, His Messengers:

> ⟪quran 16:43⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن قَبْلِكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۚ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent not (as Our Messengers) before you (O Muhammad PBUH) any but men, whom We inspired, (to preach and invite mankind to believe in the Oneness of Allah). So ask of those who know the Scripture [learned men of the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel)], if you know not.*
> — **Quran 16:43**

| **The People of Remembrance** | **The Remembrance** |
| --- | --- |
| Messenger of Allāh (peace be upon him) | Qur'ān |
| Divine Essence | Human Form |

All that is spoken in love with the Prophet (peace be upon him) and the People of the House is never lost. In the same way, through recitation of the words of the Qur'ān, luminous emanations (anwār) pour forth, and the form of these lights is constituted as the established community (qāmat al-fiʾa, ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫).

### The Alms of the First Intellect

All scriptures, books, and scriptures were revealed from the exterior (ẓāhir). Yet those revealed from the Qur'ān emerged from the First Intellect. Through the monotheism of the Originator, Most High, he extracted sacred knowledge from the sacred matter and delivered 2.5 percent of that substance to the first emanator (al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal, ⟪ar:منبعث اول⟫). Subsequently, from this matter—from which he had received it—the first emanator extracted 2.5 percent and conveyed it to the third intellect and its scope. In this manner, each intellect absorbed this substance and reduced its intensity toward the next level, until it reached the remembrances of divine light. By the time it arrived at the heart of the Messenger, the intensity of the words of the First Intellect had diminished sufficiently that they could clothe themselves in speech. At every prior level, the luminosities were so intense that it was impossible to garb them in words.[^cite-24]

### The Remembrances and the Heart of the Messenger (peace be upon him)

The physical world could only bear the burden of these luminosities through the grace of the Messenger of Allāh. At that time, there was no other who could withstand the intensity of these manifestations and clothe them in words. Allāh Himself makes this clear in the Clear Book:

> ⟪quran 59:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَوْ أَنزَلْنَا هَذَا ٱلْقُرْآنَ عَلَىٰ جَبَلٍۢ لَّرَأَيْتَهُۥ خَشِعًۭا مُّتَصَدِّعًۭا مِّنْ خَشْيَةِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَتِلْكَ ٱلْأَمْثَلُ نَضْرِبُهَا لِلنَّاسِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَتَفَكَّرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Had We sent down this Quran on a mountain, you would surely have seen it humbling itself and rending asunder by the fear of Allah. Such are the parables which We put forward to mankind that they may reflect.*
> — **Quran 59:21**

In this verse, the limits (ḥudūd) of the mountain are the limits of Mawlānā Abū Ṭālib (⟪ar:مولانا ابو طالب علیہ السلام⟫). He possessed a mountain of knowledge, yet none in him had the strength to bear the heat of these waves and transform them into speech. Only the heart of the Messenger possessed the capacity for this sacred task!

When the Messenger of Allāh (peace be upon him) departed the valley of ʿArafāt following the Farewell Pilgrimage, he was receiving abundant revelation and inspiration. The weight of these luminosities was so great that the camel upon which he rode sat down repeatedly under the burden of these anwār. Among all beasts, the camel is the strongest bearer of loads. When that mighty creature could not endure the weight of these luminosities, it demonstrates the extraordinary fortitude of the heart of the Prophet that carried the burden of these celestial lights![^cite-25]
