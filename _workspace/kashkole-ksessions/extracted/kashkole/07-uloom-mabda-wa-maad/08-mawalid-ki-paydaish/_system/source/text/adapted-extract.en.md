## The Creations of the Heavens

The principal function of all the heavens was the creation of minerals, plants, animals, and human seed (silāla, سلالۃ). Each sphere exercised its influence over the terrestrial realm according to its nature, extracting diverse substances and bringing them forth upon the earth. The four kingdoms of being are constituted from the refuse of the three celestial orders, and the heavens draw this refuse from the Ummah (the Primordial Matter, الأم) and manifest it upon earth. When these creatures perish, their bodies dissolve into the four elements, each returning to its source. Thus in the celestial kingdom, minerals, plants, and animals were born in their fullness—yet the human seed alone remained incomplete; their essences were gathered upon the earth in potential form.[^cite-1]

The division of labor among the spheres was precisely ordained:

| Sphere | Function |
| --- | --- |
| Saturn | commanded to produce minerals alone |
| Jupiter | commanded to produce minerals and vegetation |
| Mars and subsequent planets | commanded to produce minerals, vegetation, and animals |
| All heavens collectively | commanded to prepare the human seed |

All creatures born of these heavens emerged from the celestial treasuries, and within each category of creation, the three conditions arose. Consider minerals: the excellent minerals—which form the substance of the repentant (al-nādimīn, نادمین)—appear alongside intermediate minerals that compose the wavering and perplexed (al-shākk wa-al-mutaḥayyirīn, شاک و متحیر), and base minerals that constitute the deniers and arrogant (al-miṣrr wa-al-mustakbir, مصر و مستکبر).[^cite-2]

**Fact:** Just as the ultimate purpose of all heavenly births, of the Ummah and the Saqqāra (the Sphere of Spheres, السقراء), was the emergence of humanity, so too does the appearance of all the Imāms (al-Imāmāt, الإمامات) manifest the true purpose of those who stand opposite them—the Complete Human (al-insān al-kull, انسان کل).[^cite-3]

---

### Government of Saturn

Before the human form could be brought forth, it was necessary to prepare its sustenance: minerals, plants, and animals. To this end, the Wise Disposer (al-mudabbir al-ḥakīm, المدبر الحکیم) entrusted Saturn with the first government, and it began its solitary reign. Its method combined the four elemental qualities, producing a vapor of distinct form—as when water poured upon fire yields steam, a substance wholly different from its parents. These vapors accumulated into clouds, bringing torrential rains upon the earth; these rains, however, brought harm rather than benefit (ghayru al-naf', غیر النفع). The entire world became ocean. Frozen snow covered the lands in bitter cold. During this reign, mountains and forests were born. The sun's rays were obscured by heavy clouds, and darkness enveloped the atmosphere. Since Saturn possesses an evil (naḥs, نحس) nature—its function being destruction and solidification—during this first thousand years all harsh and vile substances emerged: iron, copper, lime, stone. Filthy and saline waters inundated the land, creating conditions of desolation.

| Category | Manifestation |
| --- | --- |
| Minerals | All rocky minerals and base metals such as iron formed during this reign; their mines were established. |
| Human seeds | In this time emerged the seeds of deniers, troublemakers, hard-hearted ones (such as Yazīd and Ibn Muljam), and likewise the seeds of tillers, enslaved peoples, and the peoples of Sudan. |

### Jupiter's Government

After Saturn's solitary thousand-year reign, Jupiter came to aid it. Good and evil now mixed together, and the two ruled in concert for a thousand years. Jupiter's heat caused all fluids to harden and take semi-solid form. In this period, great pillars were erected and mountains were shaped. The atmosphere grew warm and the ground leveled. Twelve islands were established. Jupiter began to produce beneficial things, sorting and separating good substances from the admixed evil. It was commanded to produce both minerals and vegetation.

| Category | Manifestation |
| --- | --- |
| Minerals | All excellent minerals—usable by men and women—were formed, such as silver. Though all were fine, they were of middling quality. |
| Plants | Vegetables and lawful herbs grew. Moreover, ants were generated and began to move, and small harmless insects were born, as well as caterpillars. |
| Human seeds | The seeds of the devout, whatever their faith; the righteous, the believers in God, the modest, the God-fearing (the chaste), and prophetic seeds of all such people. |

---

### Government of Mars

When Mars came to meditation upon the heavens, it joined with Saturn—the two evil (naḥsayn, نحسین) planets—and their misfortune intensified. Mars, by its extreme heat and dryness, completely solidified what Jupiter had rendered semi-solid, then began to break it apart. It crushed the mountains, for its nature is destruction. From this arose sand and dust. All evil things produced in their reign came from the filth of the earth; whatever evil emerges from the mixture and admixture (al-mizāj wa-al-mumtazij, المزاج وممتزج) of these two, they become magnets for it. For this reason, iron and brass are not worn in prayer, as they were born in the ages of Saturn and Mars.

| Category | Manifestation |
| --- | --- |
| Minerals | All light metals—coal, copper, brass—were made under his rule. Diamond mines were also born in this period. Though diamonds appear exceedingly beautiful, they are in truth vile and poisonous, created from the bones of those who, though arrogant and deniers, performed good deeds—thus a bitter reward. |
| Plants | Among vegetation, he produced the cactus—the worst of all plants. All poisonous shrubs grew in his kingdom: rivers (of poison), hyacinths, and the like. |
| Animals | All crawling insects, those which burrow in soil, snakes, scorpions, mice, lizards, spiders, ants, locusts—all were born in his time. Likewise, all savage beasts—lions, leopards, and others—emerged during his reign. |
| Human seeds | The seeds of executioners, swordsmen, sadists, and the hard-hearted were gathered in this time. |

### Government of the Sun

During Saturn and Mars's dominion, the entire cosmos lay shrouded in fog. When the Sun (al-shams, الشمس) began to support the kingdom, it dispelled Saturn's coldness, warming the atmosphere and dissolving all fog and mist. Under the Sun's tenure, the atmosphere became completely clear. This moderated the rainfall, which had fallen continuously for three thousand years. After Mars, Saturn gained all the excellent ministers—namely, the Sun, Venus, Mercury, and the Moon (al-shams, al-zuhrah, al-ʿutārid, al-qamar; الشمس، زہرہ، عطارد، قمر).

| Category | Manifestation |
| --- | --- |
| Minerals | During this reign, gold, rubies, emeralds, pearls, coral, and other precious minerals were produced—the minerals of kings. The pillars and arguments of our prayers are built from jewels. |
| Plants | All fruits and nuts were prepared. |
| Animals | All noble animals were born: the lion among beasts, the bee among insects, the date palm among plants, and the ruby among minerals. All things that are sovereign in their kind. |
| Human seeds | It overflowed with the seeds of kings, nobles, and rulers. |

**Fact:** All kings who came into this world were those who had performed certain acts in the spiritual realm, creating within them a state of mastery; owing to this accumulation of spiritual wealth, they were born into this world as sovereigns.

### Reign of Venus

This star is renowned for its extreme delicacy and surpassing beauty. During its reign, many springs gushed forth and the weather became exceedingly fair. The air grew pure, and cool, nourishing breezes blew. Trees flourished with vegetation; buds began to open; delicious fruits and birds were born.

| Category | Manifestation |
| --- | --- |
| Minerals | All lustrous minerals—mercury, platinum, and others—were produced in this time. |
| Plants | All fruit-bearing trees were born: apples, mangoes, and others. Fragrant flowers and precious woods were generated. All beautiful flowers emerged during this reign. |
| Animals | All lawful animals beneficial to man—horses, camels, and others—were born in this time. All birds were created and took to flight. |
| Human seeds | The seeds of the Arabs and of women were gathered here. The seeds of all musicians, artists, and craftspeople were generated. |

### Mercury's Reign

Mercury (al-ʿutārid, عطارد) created both excellent and base things; in another sense, it produced middling things—neither very good nor very bad. This further moderated the rains. During this period, stronger winds blew, causing cross-pollination among plants. By the end of this era, all minerals, plants, animals, and their varieties had been produced in full. Only humanity remained—which came forth during the Moon's dominion.

| Category | Manifestation |
| --- | --- |
| Minerals | All intermediate mineral substances were produced during this reign. |
| Plants | All middling plants—neem, bitter gourd, and others that are functional—arose in this time. |
| Animals | All intermediate animals—donkeys, mules, and creatures useful to humans—were born. Animals fit to be food for man, such as goats and cattle, were created. |
| Human seeds | The seeds of all civilized peoples came together. The seeds of all scribes, ministers, accountants, and officials were prepared in this government. |

### Moon's Government

Before creating humanity, God prepared its complete sustenance in the world. When the births of all three kingdoms (al-mawālid al-thālātha, موالید ثلاثۃ) were completed, the Wise Disposer began to plan for the birth of humanity's nobility and excellence—and this was the First Thought and the Last Deed (awwal al-fikr wa-ākhir al-ʿamal, أول الفکر و آخر العمل).[^cite-4]

## Human Birth from the Pits

When the Moon came to aid Saturn, the birth of humanity commenced. This is because the moon is the seventh heaven, holding a unique status in human creation (khilq ākhir, 'خلق آخر'). At the beginning of each cosmic cycle, these same pits are born from the primordial body (al-jaththa al-ibdāʾiyya, جثہ ابدائیۃ).

Over six thousand years, all lawful minerals, plants, and animals that had been born underwent their natural deaths. From the essences of various fragrances and all other subtle things, and from the finest, most moderate, and purest part of river waters, vapor rose up, and preparations began for the first manifestation of the highest members of the category of the repentant. Clouds formed from these vapors, which themselves rained upon the very pure earth without external force. These rains created depressions in the ground—pits not very deep in form.

**Fact:** These pits are said to have dimensions of six yards. For this reason, the command regarding graves should not exceed six yards.

In the second six-thousand-year cycle, mountains, hills, rains, and the subtle waters of former rains accumulated in sacred pits (beneath the earth) that resided in a woman's water-palace. The delicate water that fell as cloud and descended as rain dwelt in the man's palace; these two waters were brought together in complete admixture (al-imtizāj al-kullī, امتزاج کلی) and became semen (al-nutfa, نطفہ). At that moment, all the heavens stood in their noblest dwellings (bayt al-sharaf, 'بیت الشرف'). The Sun was in its house of honor—Aquarius (bayt al-sharaf dalu, بیت الشرف دَلو).

This water was heated by the earth's warmth, causing it to vaporize. As it rose toward the pit's surface, winds clothed the water on both sides, creating a chill that caused it to fall back down. This process continued, with the water rising and falling until it began to thicken, taking on the form of male semen and becoming oily. When the Sun reached Gemini (burj al-jawzāʾ, برج جوزا), summer winds began to blow. As the upper earth grew warm, the oily water dried and froze; forms began to take shape, and organs were formed through the alternation of heat and cold. Each constellation labored in the birth of these bodies' organs.

It was during the earliest period of the Moon's government that the human form (al-sūrat al-insāniyya, صورت انسانیہ) was first born. The body began to flatten slightly in its place until it sat upon its muscles in such a posture that the chin rested upon both knees and both hands wrapped around the chest—the fetal position. This is what the Qur'ān declares: "And God has brought you forth from the earth as vegetation."

> ⟪quran 71:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱللَّهُ أَنۢبَتَكُم مِّنَ ٱلْأَرْضِ نَبَاتًۭا ‎⟫
> *And Allah has brought you forth from the (dust of) earth. [Tafsir At-Tabarî, Vol.29, Page 97].*
> And Allah has produced you from the earth in a manner of growth.
> — **Quran 71:17**

Then the rays of the sun—the sensate spirit (al-rūḥ al-ḥassiyya, روح (حسیۃ))—were blown into it. When this united with the sensate soul (al-nafs al-ḥassiyya, نفس حسیۃ), the body's inner organs began to function. It felt the moderate breeze and began to draw its share of oxygen through breathing via both nostrils. Its movements increased. Both feet began to absorb water from the pit through the pores of the soles, as a magnet (al-ḥajar al-muqnāṭīs, حجر مقناطیس) draws iron. Though one is stone and the other iron, this process occurs through their partnership and compatibility. Just as a physical child receives nourishment from its mother through the navel, so does this heavenly child receive nourishment through its feet. Through the minute pores of both feet, the pit's material climbed into the body and reached throughout.

**Note:** The foot is related to the brain. This is why pressing the soles induces sleep through calm.

The child began to move and turn within its place. The pit's water continued to soak the body as massage soaks a child's body in the world. Through its pores, the body absorbed this oily water and drew sustenance from it. This continued until nine months (270 days) were completed. Then the child opened its mouth and began to receive food through the mouth, which had been prepared for it. It began to suck from the thumb, where nature had released materials and secretions for it. This continued until the child grew strong enough to rise up and walk within a year.

Though born a child, this heavenly child's physical state was that of a four-year-old—a consequence of its parents' strength. Since it was the child of the heavens (father) and the earth (mother), its powers were immense. Among nature's provisions, it first turned to figs and grapes, as these are easily consumed and produce no waste. Then, as it grew, it began to consume other foods (grains, birds, cattle) that had been created as a test. It commanded all of them by virtue of its rank and the perfection of its majesty. Here was the First Thought, and this was the Last Deed.

**Fact:** The body of the first human was born at the wings of the Kaaba's Red Station (al-maqām al-ḥumra, مقام الحمرۃ). This person was the Great Alchemist (al-kīmiyāʾ al-aʿẓam, کیمروت اعظم), and there was no deity besides him.

Countless humans were born throughout the world before the first form. These human persons (al-ashkhās al-bashariyya, اشخاص بشریۃ) appeared across the twelve islands (al-qāʾmat al-fīʾa, قامت الفیۃ). The lands in these regions have been named: the Arabs, Iran, Rome, India, Sindh, Abyssinia, Berber, Khazaria, Turk, and Zanzibar (al-ʿarab, al-ʿayrān, al-rūm, al-hind, al-sind, al-ḥabsha, al-barbar, al-khazar, al-turk, al-zanj; عرب، عیران، روم، ہند، سند، ہبشہ، بربر، خزر، ترک، زنج). Since this period marks the beginning of Unveiling (kashf, الكشف), all those in the repentant category (al-nādimīn, نادمین) were born in this era.

All the first humans born from the pits were male. When they emerged, rain fell again upon the water remaining in the pits until all women were born from them. Women are therefore inferior to men, as they were created from the water left after men. Since each pit resided in the mother's palace, the man and woman born from the same pit became brother and sister. Later, men from one pit married women from another, and thus the human race progressed.

**Fact:** The first to descend (into a state of falling, ḥālat al-inḥidār, حالت انحدار) was the Primordial Adam (ādam al-ibdāʾiyya, آدم ابدائیۃ), who was stationed at the highest limit of the world of life (ʿālam al-ḥayāt, عالم حیات) and was marked (muḥaddad, 'محدد'). There was no shadow upon him, and he achieved perfection. Yet what was corrupted by witnessing him appeared as the leader of this primordial body—a veil.

The Honored Fath Allah (Ḥaḍrat Fath Allāh Ṣāḥib Muqaddas, حضرت فتح اللہ صاحب مقدس) often recited these verses:

*They came here as a seed of deeds from Eternity, or remained here as if a tree in the valley of the Garden.*

*Flowers must go elsewhere; still much fruit remains in the orchard.*

## Two Kinds of Birth

God has created two types of creation in this world. One is like vegetation emerging from the ground, which occurs at the beginning of each cosmic cycle; the other is through birth from the womb (raḥm al-mādir, 'رحم مادر').

When these shadowy forms (al-ashbāḥ al-sūriyya, اشباح صوریۃ) appeared in dense state in the three gates, the Divine Disposition (al-taddbīr al-ilāhī, تدبیر الٰہی) required that they be scattered and spread throughout the heavens, the Ummah, and the Saqqāra, so that they might be purified through trials and tests—just as gold is purified by burning in fire, and pollution is removed by placing it in denseness. After scattering, the Wise Disposer created humanity by combining these components, employing two methods to bring forth this order (al-qāʾmat al-fīʾa, قامت الفیۃ).[^cite-5]

### Birth from Pits

The first birth-method is from the pit at the beginning of each great cycle (kūr al-aʿẓam, کور اعظم), which is the noblest birth. God says (in interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 71:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱللَّهُ أَنۢبَتَكُم مِّنَ ٱلْأَرْضِ نَبَاتًۭا ‎⟫
> *And Allah has brought you forth from the (dust of) earth.*
> And Allah has produced you from the earth in a manner of growth.
> — **Quran 71:17**

It has been stated in the sacred teachings that God created them in the form of luminous shadows (al-ashbāḥ al-nūrāniyya, اشباح نورانیۃ)—in human form (al-qāʾmat al-fīʾa, (قامت الفیۃ)). Beings were born first, and humans later. The purpose of this description is to convey that all the multitude who would gather until the end would evolve into human form. And the ordering of this human frame (al-haykal, ھیکل) will correspond to the order of their appearances in this world. Those who ascend last will occupy the place of the foot. God Almighty created humanity from a state of being; now humanity must return to the ultimate state of the self. Only then will it be possible to be freed from it.

### Birth from the Womb

When the first creation was completed, He then planned a second birth, similar to the first creation. The substances that were to be revealed to the world were scattered into minerals, plants, and animals and made into food for the primordial body (al-jaththa al-ibdāʾiyya, جثہ ابدائیۃ). This is why God says (in interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 23:12⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا ٱلْإِنسَنَ مِن سُلَلَةٍۢ مِّن طِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *And indeed We created man (Adam) out of an extract of clay (water and earth).*
> And indeed We created man from an extract of clay.
> — **Quran 23:12**

He gathered all the subtle nutrients that had been divided into flesh, blood, and the four natures (purity, melancholy, phlegm, and blood) according to all their kinds. These foods were the purest and noblest of the primordial body. They took the subtle from them and removed the impure. This continued until the time came for the male and female bodies to be reunited. At that moment, the subtlest part of both ascended from their bodies and climbed toward the brain, so that the brain might sort and arrange them. The brain converted it into thick white blood drawn from all the body's organs (small and large, high and low). This white blood flowed down to its source—the uterus in woman and the backbone in man. Through marriage, the two were united by white blood, combining the four natures. Within all these natures dwelt a hidden monstrous life, secretly known as the vegetative soul (al-nafs al-nāmiyya, نفس نامیۃ)—called the form-making (al-sūrat al-nāmiyya, صورت نامیۃ). It was concealed in these four natures as fire is hidden in lightning-struck wood. That is, in the eternal world, the essence of the creature—called articulate (nāṭiqa), sensate (ḥassiyya), and vegetative (nāmiyya) (ناطقۃ، حسیۃ، نامیۃ)—is known in this world by the primordial leaven (al-khimīra al-ibdāʾiyya, خمیرۃ ابدائیہ) and is settled by the semen (al-nutfa, نطفہ).

When this essence had become temperate and was permitted to appear, it went to the clear, pure, and moderate place of the mother's womb. Four forces—the Ascendant, Descendant, Midheaven, and Nadir (al-ṭāliʿ, al-ghārib, al-ʿāshir, al-rābiʿ; طالع، غارب، عاشر، رابع)—worked to stabilize it. It arrived in such a state that it was receptive, passive, and capable of accepting the influences about to befall it in the womb. It came in the form of a substrate (al-hayūlā, ھیولی) so that it might accept the influence of the Creator's form (al-sūrat, صورت). It proceeded through the ranks of creation as yogurt makes yogurt, and yogurt makes cheese.

*What is life? The appearance of elements in order.*

*What is death? The disturbance of these elements.*

At this point, all the planets began to shine and exert their influences, making it subtle until it became like the essence of the radiance of the planets—like the essence of milk. It became absorbed in all four temperaments, and several essences began to form within it. The first of this sperm—the heart ('al-qalb', 'قلب')—was passive, just as it was the first to be active in the heavenly world (al-shams, الشمس). It accorded with the nature of the heart, which is heat and moisture. The heart began to absorb this essence and became the center for the whole body. Then, one by one, all remaining organs began to become passive.[^cite-6]

The planets in the paternal palace began to influence the body from the heavens, while the elements in the maternal palace began to influence the mother's womb. Each organ had one overseer (al-mutawallī, مُتَوَلِّی) from among the planets from whom it received influence. Whatever the mother ate during pregnancy, she distributed the food to her baby's organs. Each part of the child joined with its corresponding maternal organ and began to receive its share—the child's hands receiving from the mother's hands, the child's heart from the mother's heart, and so on. The heart aided the three organs above it and the three organs below, just as the Sun aids the three superior heavens (Saturn, Jupiter, Mars) and the three inferior heavens (Venus, Mercury, Moon) (al-shams, which helps al-thālātha al-akbar: al-zuhrah, al-ʿutārid, al-qamar; الشمس، زہرہ، عطارد، قمر).

**Fact:** In the same way, the articulate scholar of religion (al-nāṭiq ʿalayhi al-salām, ناطق علیہ السلام)—like the Sun (al-shams, الشمس)—derives the three upper limits (grandfather, victor, imagination: al-jad, al-fath, al-khayāl; جد، فتح، خیال) through divine endorsements and supports the three lower limits (trustee, Imam, Summoner: al-waṣī, al-imām, al-dāʿī; وصی، امام، داعی) by means of divine knowledge (al-ʿulūm al-ilāhiyya, علوم الٰہیۃ).

Each of the seven planets acts upon the sperm for one month until seven months are completed and the seven organs of the human body are perfected, or the seven states are fulfilled. In the seventh month, the baby is fully prepared by the Moon's strategy. Therefore, if a child is born in the seventh month, it lives.

## The Heavens' Arrangements in the Birth of the Fetus

### Saturn: First Month

In the first month, the human sperm is conceived by Saturn, which holds the first place in the heavens. Saturn's nature is to coagulate. In the births from pits, Saturn solidified the pit's boiling water and froze it so that water might boil and emerge from the pit. In the same way, in the womb, it acts upon the sperm to coagulate it.

### Jupiter: Second Month

In the second month, Jupiter's strategy begins. Being a fortunate planet, it has created growth and development of this frozen blood through its heat and moisture (al-quwwat al-numu, قوت نُمُو). All parts of the sperm come together in the form of a blood clot. The power of growth arises at the time of Jupiter.

### Mars: Third Month

In the third month, Mars's maneuver begins—an evil planet. Through its warmth, it converts the sperm into a clot (al-ʿalaqa, علقۃ) and a small lump of flesh (al-muḍgha, مضغۃ). The heat of this sphere increases the power of growth and opens pathways in the form of pores for nourishment. In that month, fleshy parts begin to appear.

### The Sun: Fourth Month

In the fourth month, the Sun's strategy begins. Being the king of the heavens, its attention is especially focused on the heart—the king of the human body. In its measure, all the vital organs, especially the heart and brain that were present in the design of the lump of flesh (al-muḍgha, مضغۃ), emerge, and the sensate soul (al-nafs al-ḥassiyya, نفس حسیۃ) emerges.

### Venus: Fifth Month

In the fifth month, Venus's plot begins—a very delicate star. This planet distinguishes between male and female in the embryo. It reveals the eye and nose, and the head protrudes between the two ears. In this month, all the fetus's bones are prepared.

### Mercury: Sixth Month

In the sixth month, Mercury begins its maneuver. Flesh climbs upon the bones, and the hands and feet become quite prominent and separated. The fetus begins to realize its surroundings. It is able to open its mouth and move its lips and begins to feel with its tongue. In this month, a sense of sleep and wakefulness arises.

### The Moon: Seventh Month

In the seventh month, the Moon's strategy begins, granting the power of perception. In this month, it attains the human form (al-qāʾmat al-fīʾa, قامت الفی۲ۃ) and its vital organs (al-aʿḍāʾ al-raʾīsa, اعضاء رئیسۃ) grow stronger. It begins to move constantly, feeling the womb's tightness and desiring to exit.

### Saturn: Eighth Month

If the time for emergence does not come, the sperm remains in the mother's womb, and Saturn's plan is reversed upon it—it becomes an evil planetary influence (naḥs al-falakī, نحس الفلکی). Its nature is coldness and dryness, which are the natures of death. In this month, the effect of gravitational pull (al-kashsh al-thiqal, کشش سقل) on the embryo becomes very strong. If the embryo is born in the eighth month, Saturn will freeze it due to its misfortune, and the child will die.

### Jupiter: Ninth Month

If the child remains in its mother's womb until the ninth month, then Jupiter's plan begins—the greatest fortune (saʿd al-akbar, سعد اکبر). Its function is heat and softness, which are the natures of life. In this month, the fetus's movements greatly increase. These movements are voluntary, triggering contractions. In the ninth month, Jupiter enters the constellation of the Fishes, ensuring the embryo's birth.

---

The existence of these voluntary movements of the soul depends on the surrounding conditions. If the things around the self are corrupted, that self separates. When the newborn emerges from its mother's narrow womb and becomes a complete person (shakhṣ kāmil, شخص کامل) in the vastness of the earth, the sensate power (al-quwwat al-ḥassiyya, قوت حسیۃ) becomes even more awakened. The heart of the heavens—the Sun—is the center of the other sensate souls (al-nufūs al-ḥassiyya, نفوس حسیہ), just as the Moon is the center of the minister vegetative souls of the heavens (al-nufūs al-nāmiyya, نفوس نامیۃ). It spreads the sensate power throughout the newborn's body and creates movement. Then the sensate power (al-quwwat al-ḥassiyya, قوت حسیۃ) and the vegetative power (al-quwwat al-nāmiyya, قوت نامیۃ) combine to form a new color. From this life, the newborn begins to breathe the sphere of the breeze (al-kura al-nasīm, کرہ نسیم), which becomes food for its sensate power and its vegetative power's joints.

The vegetative soul (al-nafs al-nāmiyya, نفس نامیۃ) is divided into four elements (fire, air, water, earth) placed under the Moon's sky. In the fourth month, the heart moves—that is, three chambers form in the heart and it begins to beat. When the eighth month begins, Saturn has an effect again, making the child lifeless, so the child is born dead in the eighth month. But the ninth month belongs to Jupiter, so the baby is born healthy. Here only the soul's status remains, and therefore its virtue is greater.

In this way, the seven planets and their wills come one after another, and the Mahdī (the guided one), the successor of the Qāʾim, stands opposite the soul. Though the baby is fully prepared in seven months, it still takes another two months to be born. These two months indicate the equivalent of the heavenly world and the constellation, or the universe of limits.

### Regarding This Embryo, the Master Mu'ayyad Says:

If only you knew why you were drawn out from the powers of the heavens and bodies, why you were made to exist in a narrow and confined space, and then why you were brought out of the world's narrowness![^cite-7]

This points to the sperm adjacent to the vegetative soul and pulled from the three births (minerals, plants, animals: al-mawālid al-thālātha, موالید ثلاثۃ). Then He created within you an inner meaning, whose signs are hidden in the heavens and the earth. This refers to your sensate power (al-quwwat al-ḥassiyya, قوت حسیۃ) that connects to the newborn at birth. When a child emerges from its mother's womb, its life and death, joy and sorrow, wealth and poverty, fame and obscurity, all fall under the influence of the stars and the heavens. Yet the command of the Creator—who is the Creator and Ruler of all heavens and stars in His majesty and glory—stands above all heavenly influences! All these heavens, stars, and planets are subject to His command and obey His every decree.

## The Senses and the Powers of the Brain

Those parts of the body that are firm and solid are called organs (al-aʿḍāʾ, 'اعضا'), and those that arise from the four qualities in the form of vapors—very subtle, pure, and transparent in essence—are called temperaments (al-mizājāt, 'مزاجات'). Spreading throughout the body is the animal soul (al-rūḥ al-ḥayawānī, 'روح حیوانی').

| Solid | Intermediate | Subtle |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Organs | Temperaments | Animal Soul |

This soul begins at the heart's animal locus and spreads through the pathways of the mind. It passes through the eye-holes, ear-canals, tongue-sockets, and extends to all the upper and lower (al-raʾīs wa-al-asfal, رئیس و اسفل) organs, finally reaching the toes. This soul clothes the whole body as hands clothe a glove.

There are five external senses in the human body: touch, taste, smell, hearing, and sight (al-lams, al-dhāʾiqa, al-bū, al-samāʿ, al-baṣar; لمس، ذائقۃ، بو، سماعت وبصر). Of these, three senses—touch, taste, smell—develop in the womb, while the remaining two—hearing and sight—are created when the child emerges. Corresponding to these five external senses are five internal senses: common sense, imaginative power, thinking power, estimative power, and memory (al-ḥawāss al-mushtaraka, al-muṣawwira, al-mufikira, al-wahmiyya, al-ḥāfiẓa; حواس مشترکۃ، مصورۃ، مفکرۃ، وہمیۃ وحافظۃ). All these senses are fulfilled in the fetus in potential (bi-al-quwwa, بالقوۃ) before birth and begin to manifest in actuality (bi-al-fiʿl, بالفعل) at their appointed times after birth. Ultimately, this is the articulate soul (al-nafs al-nāṭiqa al-mijāzī, نفس ناطقۃ مجازی).

Common sense resides in the first part of the brain. It is called "common" because it has access to every sensible power, and every force resembles this intellect in some manner. For example, everything the eye can see, the ear can hear, the hand can feel, the tongue can taste, the nose can smell—all these sensations originate in the mind. This is why this part of the brain is called the "repository of sensible forms" (makhzan al-sūr al-muḥassasāt, 'مخزن صور محسوسات').

The imaginative power (al-quwwat al-takhyīl, قوت تخیل) resides in another part of the brain. When this power serves and aids the intellect, it is called the thinking power (al-quwwat al-mufikira, قوت مفکرۃ). When its attention is directed toward bodily desires and gives rise to conjecture and imagination, it is called the imaginative power (al-quwwat al-mutakhayyila, قوت متخیلۃ). When the imaginative power is highlighted, the intellect stores this thought in the third part of the brain, called the memory (al-quwwat al-ḥāfiẓa, قوت حافظۃ). The estimative power (al-quwwat al-takhminiyya, قوت تخمینۃ) is present in the fourth part of the brain, which is in the middle of the head. Memory is placed in the back of the brain, storing every form that the imaginative power sends to it. The recollective power (al-quwwat al-mutadhakkira, قوت متذکرۃ) retrieves it—the form that the imaginative power sends—whenever it wishes. The memory retains all bodily impressions. This power exists in humans and animals and continues to work as long as the four temperaments maintain their balance in the body. Yet because of the eternal nature of the self, a part of the imaginative power is always intact and never entirely ceases.

The intellect (al-ʿaql, 'عقل') is like a postmaster to whom messengers bring news or letters about various events, which he then arranges. The imaginative power (al-mutakhayyila, 'متخیلۃ') is like his secretary, to whom he gives letters to place in his bag. The recollective power (al-mutadhakkira, 'متذکرۃ') is like a king to whom the imaginative power delivers all letters so that he may read them and derive good or bad news from them. The king hands these letters (ideas) to his treasurer so that he may safely store them in the treasury of memory. The thinking power (al-mufikira, المفکرۃ) is the king's official who can re-deliver those letters (memories) from the treasury to the king whenever he desires, upon his orders.

## Humanity as the Essence of the Universe

The author of the *Epistles of the Sincere Brethren* (al-ʿAlīm al-Muʿallim, صاحب رسائل اخوان الصفا) explains that the world ('ālam, 'عالم') refers to the seven heavens, the earth, and all that is between them. They also call it the "Greater Human" (al-insān al-kabīr, 'انسان الکبیر'). This is because, like a human being, the world itself is like a body with different organs. This world also possesses a soul whose powers flow through its organs. A person's soul has an effect upon their body. The manifestations of this one effect appear in different forms: the tongue speaks, the eyes see, the ears hear, steps walk, and so on.

From the circumference of the heavens to the center of the Saqqāra, this world is like a body, or a human being, called the "Greater Universe" (al-ʿālam al-kabīr, 'عالم الکبیر'). And the Creator, for the Greater Universe, exerts His influence upon this monstrous body. Different parts of the Greater Universe accept this influence in terms of their receptivity and capacity, and on account of this, their different qualities emerge. For example, the soul's effect is manifested as motion in the heavens, but as rest in the earth. It creates fusion in the Ummah and stagnation in minerals. It manifests as growth in plants and sensation in fauna. In humans, it manifests as logic and discernment. For this reason, the Creator is called the Mover of the Heavens, the Rester of the Earth, the Solidifier of Minerals, the Grower of Plants, and the Giver of Life to Animals.

The heavens above the moon and the heavens themselves are perfect in their essence and goodness and are given by them glorification. In contrast, there are variations in the lower part below the celestial moon, where good and evil mix, as illustrated by the particular human. Man is created between the two—the Universal Human and the Particular Human—and therefore receives influence from both. It is created in the same manner that the lower parts below the moon are created from the heavens. These parts are always subject to the heavens and receive influences from them.

It is said by the author of the Epistles that the Ummah (primordial matter) appeared after the heavens. Minerals came after the Ummah, plants came after minerals, animals came after plants, and humans came after animals. This series is not limited to any one time; these manifestations will continue to occur in the same order. Minerals will continue to become plants, plants will continue to become animals, and animals will continue to appear in human form. But after becoming human, there will be no further progress into any other form. The reason is that humanity is the essence and the summary of these three (minerals, plants, animals). After returning to the original state, there is no longer a need to change into any other form!

A perfect human being who has attained the original purpose of birth has completed their development. This is why God did not create any other creature after humanity, for humanity is the perfect form of the flesh, the plant, and the animal—the true and ultimate purpose of their birth. The main purpose of minerals, plants, and animals is to develop and emerge in human form. After becoming human, there is no need to develop into any other form. That is why there is a link between humanity and the three births (al-mawālid al-thālātha, موالید ثلاثۃ). These three depend upon humanity's existence, and humanity uses all three for its life.
