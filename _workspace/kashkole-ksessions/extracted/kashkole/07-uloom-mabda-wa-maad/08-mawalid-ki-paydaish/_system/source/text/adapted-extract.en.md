## The Heavens' Creations

The principal purpose governing all the celestial spheres was to generate minerals, plants, animals, and human seed ⟪ar:(سلالۃ)⟫. In their appointed succession, each sphere exerted its influence upon this terrestrial realm according to its nature, extracting and constituting diverse elements, and bringing them forth upon the earth. These four categories of being are constituted from the residue of the three principles, and the heavens continually draw this residue from the elemental realm—called the Ummahat, or Mothers—and generate it upon the earth. After their natural dissolution, their bodily forms decompose into the four elements, each returning to its proper center. Within the dominion of these heavens, minerals, plants, and animals were produced in their complete forms; yet the seed of humanity alone remained suspended, its essences gathered in the earthly realm.

### Government of Saturn:

Before the generation of humanity, it was necessary first to create their sustenance: minerals, plants, and animals. For this ⟪ar:مدبر حکیم⟫ wise governor, Saturn was given the first dominion, initiating its solitary rule. Its method combined four phenomena that produced vapor—a form distinct from those four elements. As when water poured upon fire generates steam, a substance differing from both fire and water, so too did vapors accumulate into clouds, bringing forth torrential rains upon the earth. These rains, ⟪ar:غیر النفع⟫ harmful in their excess, inundated the entire terrestrial sphere. Everywhere lay frozen snow, and bitter cold prevailed. During Saturn's reign, mountains and trees were born. The sun's rays withdrew behind dark clouds, and the atmosphere grew murky. Since Saturn possesses a malevolent nature ⟪ar:نحس⟫—its function to bring ruin and to freeze—during this first millennium all things harsh and base were produced: iron, copper, lime, and stone. Filthy and saline waters covered the land, causing all manner of corruption.

### Jupiter's Government:

After Saturn's solitary reign of one thousand years, Jupiter came to its aid, bringing a mixture of good and evil. The two governed together for a thousand years. Through Jupiter's heat, all fluids began to harden and achieve semi-solid form. In this era, many peaks rose up and mountains were established. The atmosphere grew temperate, the ground flattened. Twelve islands were established during his dominion. Jupiter began to manifest goodness, sifting and separating the good from the evil with which it had mingled.

| Minerals | In his reign all noble minerals—those serviceable to men and women—were fashioned, such as silver. Though excellent, they remained of middling quality.
| --- | --- |
| Plants | Vegetables and permissible herbs flourished. Furthermore, in his dominion ants arose and began their procession, and small insects were born that cause no harm. Caterpillars too were brought forth.
| Human seed | The seed of the pious, regardless of faith, was gathered here: the righteous, the believers in God, the modest ones, those who fear the Divine (the chaste), and prophets of such virtue. [^cite-2]

⟪ar:ألف ثالث⟫When meditation turned to Mars, the meeting of these two ⟪ar:نحسین افلاک⟫ malevolent spheres intensified misfortune. From this arose sand. All base things in their dominion arose from the earth's filth. ⟪ar:مزاج وممتزج⟫ Whatever evil arose, these two temperaments became magnets for it. ### The Government of the Sun:

During Saturn and Mars's dominion, the entire cosmos was shrouded in fog. When the Sun ⟪ar:شمس⟫ began its governance, it dispelled Saturn's coldness, warming the atmosphere and clearing away mist and haze. Under the Sun's tenure, the sky became entirely lucid. This moderated the rainfall that had continued without respite for the previous three thousand years. After Mars, the Sun gained all excellent ministers: ⟪ar:شمس، زہرہ، عطارد، قمر⟫ namely Venus, Mercury, and the Moon.

This star is portrayed as supremely delicate and resplendent in beauty. In its dominion, many springs gushed forth, and the weather became surpassingly fair. The air grew pure, and cool, nourishing breezes blew across the lands. Flowering trees began to bud, and delicious fruits and birds emerged.

## The Birth of Humanity from the Pits

When the Moon came to aid Saturn, the process of human generation commenced. This is because the Moon is the seventh heaven, holding a particular station in the ⟪ar:'خلق آخر'⟫ ultimate creation of humanity. At the beginning of each age, the same pits are born from ⟪ar:جثہ ابدائیۃ⟫ primordial bodies.

Over six thousand years, all lawful minerals, plants, and animals that had been generated died their lawful deaths. Besides these, from the essences of diverse fragrances and all other subtle things—and from the most rarefied, moderate, and pure fraction of fresh river water—water rose as vapor, and preparations commenced for the first manifestation of the highest elements of the deniers' category. From these vapors, clouds formed which, of their own accord, rained upon the exceedingly pure earth without external compulsion. The force of these rains carved potholes into the ground—craters neither very deep nor shallow.

## Two Types of Creation

The Divine has created two types of creation in this world. One emerges as vegetation from the earth, occurring at the beginning of each age; the other occurs through birth ⟪ar:'رحم مادر '⟫ from the maternal womb.

⟪ar:اشباح صوریۃ⟫ When these luminous forms appeared in dense state within the three gates, ⟪ar:تدبیر الٰہی⟫ divine governance required them to be scattered and dispersed throughout the heavens, the Ummahat, and the Saqqara, that they might be purified through examinations and trials—as gold is purified by fire, base matter removed through ordeal. After ⟪ar:مدبر حکیم⟫ this wise governor dispersed them, He created humanity by ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫ combining these scattered components. He employed two methods to bring them forth ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫ in their form.

### Birth from Pits:

The first method of birth is from the pit at the beginning of each age—the most excellent birth. The Divine says (interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 71:17⟫

> *And Allah has brought you forth from the (dust of) earth. [Tafsir At-Tabarî, Vol.29, Page 97].*

It is stated in the facts that the Divine created ⟪ar:اشباح نورانی⟫ radiant forms in the ⟪ar:(قامت الفیۃ)⟫ form of humanity. Beings were born first, humanity afterward. The purpose of its description is that all the multitude gathered until the end shall evolve into human form. The rank of this ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ human vessel shall accord with the order of appearances in this world. Those who climb last shall occupy the place of the sole of the foot. The Almighty created humanity from a state of being; now humanity must return to the ultimate state of selfhood. And it is then that liberation becomes possible.

## The Heavens' Governance in the Formation of the Foetus

### Saturn: (First Month)

In the first month, the human sperm receives the influence of Saturn, for it is first among the heavens. The nature of this sphere is to coagulate. In the generation from pits, Saturn solidified the pit's boiling water and froze it, allowing the water to cease its boiling and emerge from the pit. Similarly, in the womb, it acts upon the sperm to coagulate it.

### Jupiter: (Second Month)

Then in the second month, Jupiter's governance commences. Since it is a beneficent sphere, it creates growth and increase of this ⟪ar:قوت نُمُو⟫ power of growth through its heat and moisture. All parts of the sperm gather together in the form of a blood clot. The power of growth awakened during Jupiter's month.

### Mars: (Third Month)

In the third month, Mars's governance begins—⟪ar:نحس⟫ a malevolent sphere. Through its warmth, it transforms it ⟪ar:علقۃ⟫ into clot-form and then into ⟪ar:مضغۃ⟫ lump-form. The heat of this sphere increases the power of growth and opens pathways in the form of pores for nutrition. In this month, the essential vital parts begin to appear. [^cite-7]

### The Sun: (Fourth Month)

In the ⟪ar:شمس⟫ fourth month, the Sun's governance begins. Since it is the king of the heavens, its attention focuses especially on the heart, the king of the human body. Under its influence, all ⟪ar:اعضاء رئیسۃ⟫ vital organs—especially the heart and brain that lay present in the lump-form ⟪ar:مضغۃ⟫—emerge and become active. The ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ sensate soul emerges.

### Venus: (Fifth Month)

In the fifth month, Venus's governance commences—⟪ar:شمس⟫ a supremely delicate sphere. This sphere distinguishes male from female within the embryo. It reveals the features of eye and nose; the head protrudes between the two ears. In this month, all the fetus's bones become prepared.

### Mercury: (Sixth Month)

In the sixth month, Mercury's governance begins. In this month, flesh climbs upon the bones, and the hands and feet become quite prominent and fully separated. The fetus begins to perceive its location. It is able to open its mouth and move its lips, commencing to feel them with its tongue. In this month, the sense of sleep and wakefulness arises.

### The Moon: (Seventh Month)

In the seventh month, the Moon's governance begins, imparting the power of perception. In this month it ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫ achieves complete human form, and its ⟪ar:اعضاء رئیسۃ⟫ vital joints grow ever stronger. It begins to move constantly, sensing the narrowness of the womb and the desire to emerge from it.

### Saturn: (Eighth Month)

If there is no time for its emergence, the sperm remains in the mother's womb, and Saturn's governance reverses its effect upon it, which is ⟪ar:نحس الفلکی⟫ celestial malevolence. Its nature is coldness and dryness, the natures of death. In this month, the effect of gravity ⟪ar:کشش سقل⟫ upon the embryo becomes very strong. If the embryo is born in this month, Saturn freezes it through its misfortune, and the child dies.

### Jupiter: (Ninth Month)

If the child remains in the mother's womb until the ninth month, then Jupiter's governance begins—⟪ar:سعد اکبر⟫ the greater beneficence. Its function is heat and softness, the natures of life. In this month, the fetus's movement increases greatly. This movement becomes voluntary, triggering contractions in the womb. In the ninth month, Jupiter enters the Pisces constellation, which assures the embryo's birth.

---

The existence of these voluntary movements of the soul depends upon the conditions surrounding it. When conditions become corrupted, that soul separates. When the newborn emerges from its mother's narrow womb and becomes one ⟪ar:شخص کامل⟫ complete human being in the vastness of the earth, the ⟪ar:شعاء الشمس⟫ rays of the Sun become even more ⟪ar:قوت حسیۃ⟫ awakened as sensate power, emerging ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ in actuality. ⟪ar:شمس⟫ The Sun, heart of the heavens, is the center of the other ⟪ar:نفوس حسیہ⟫ sensate souls, just as the Moon is the center of the ⟪ar:نفوس نامیۃ⟫ vegetative souls of the heavens. Its ⟪ar:قوت حسیۃ⟫ sensate power spreads through the newborn's body and creates movement within it. Then ⟪ar:قوت حسیۃ⟫ sensate power and ⟪ar:قوت نامیۃ⟫ vegetative power combine to form a new integrated power. From this union, the newborn begins to breathe ⟪ar:کرہ نسیم⟫ the sphere of air-breath, which becomes sustenance for his ⟪ar:قوت حسیۃ⟫ sensate power and his ⟪ar:قوت نامیۃ⟫ vegetative power.

## The Stages of Foetal Formation

The Qur'an describes the seven stages of foetal birth:

> ⟪quran 23:12-14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا ٱلْإِنسَنَ مِن سُلَلَةٍۢ مِّن طِينٍۢ ‎ ‏ثُمَّ جَعَلْنَهُ نُطْفَةًۭ فِى قَرَارٍۢ مَّكِينٍۢ ‎ ‏ثُمَّ خَلَقْنَا ٱلنُّطْفَةَ عَلَقَةًۭ فَخَلَقْنَا ٱلْعَلَقَةَ مُضْغَةًۭ فَخَلَقْنَا ٱلْمُضْغَةَ عِظَمًۭا فَكَسَوْنَا ٱلْعِظَمَ لَحْمًۭا ثُمَّ أَنشَأْنَهُ خَلْقًا آخَرَ ۚ فَتَبَارَكَ ٱللَّهُ أَحْسَنُ ٱلْخَلِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *And indeed We created man (Adam) out of an extract of clay (water and earth). Thereafter We made him (the offspring of Adam) as a Nutfah (mixed drops of the male and female sexual discharge) (and lodged it) in a safe lodging (womb of the woman). Then We made the Nutfah into a clot (a piece of thick coagulated blood), then We made the clot into a little lump of flesh, then We made out of that little lump of flesh bones, then We clothed the bones with flesh, and then We brought it forth as another creation. So blessed be Allah, the Best of creators.*

| Salalah (سلالہ) | The substance that dwells in the father's loins and the mother's womb before fertilization takes place. The components of manifestation are scattered throughout the world; the subtle essence is fixed upon the father's backbone and in the mother's womb, which is called Salalah.
| --- | --- |
| Sperm-drop (نطفہ) | When these two substances unite and the two fluids mingle in the mother's womb, fertilization occurs and the sperm-drop is formed.
| Lump (مضغۃ) | This sperm-drop receives nourishment and takes the form of a lump, called a mudghah.
| Clot (علقۃ) | The structure of this lump before the bones are formed is called the clot. ⟪ar:'علاَّقۃ '⟫ In Arabic, it is named 'adhering one' or 'hanging one.'
| Bones (عظام) | Within this structure lies a shell of bones, called bones.
| Flesh (لحم) | The flesh that climbs upon these bones is called meat.
| Another Creation (خلق آخر) | When the child emerges from the mother's womb, the soul is breathed into it. A six-month-old fetus can be born because the soul is breathed at the end of the sixth month—at the beginning of the seventh—when it is able to receive sensation. The remaining two months are devoted to the infant's growth. If it even slightly sneezes at the time of birth, it receives sensation; if it does not sneeze, it receives none, and the child is born lifeless.

⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ The vegetative soul is divided into four elements (fire, air, water, earth) which are placed under the sphere of the Moon. In the fourth month, the heart moves—that is, three chambers form in the heart and it commences its beating. When the eighth month begins, Saturn exerts its effect again; Saturn renders the child dead, so the child born in the eighth month is born dead. But the ninth month is Jupiter's, and the infant is born healthy. Here only the soul's status remains, and therefore its virtue is greater. [^cite-8]

In this way, the seven Spheres and their influences come one after another, and the Mahdi, the successor of the Qaim, stands opposite the soul. Though the infant is ready in seven months, it yet takes another two months to be born. These two months represent the equivalent of the heavenly world and the constellation sphere—the universe of the boundaries and limits.

### Regarding This Embryo, Sayyidina al-Muʾayyad Says:

If only you knew why you were drawn forth from the powers of the heavens and bodies, and why you were placed in a narrow and confined space, and then brought out of the narrowness of the world.

This points to the sperm-drop that adjoins it—the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ vegetative soul—which has been extracted from ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ موالید ثلاثۃ (معدن، نبات، حیوان)⟫ the three births: minerals, plants, and animals. These ⟪ar:موالید ثلاثۃ⟫ three births were drawn from the four Ummahat—fire, air, water, and earth—which are the forces of the heavens. Then He created within you an inner reality, whose signs are hidden in the heavens and the earth. This refers to your ⟪ar:قوت حسیۃ⟫ sensate power, which connects to the newborn at the moment of birth. When an infant emerges from its mother's womb, its life and death, its joy and sorrow, its wealth and poverty, its fame and ignominy, all lie beneath the influence of the stars and the heavens. Yet the command of the Creator—the Originator and Master of all the heavens and stars in His majesty and glory—transcends all celestial influences! All these heavens, stars, and planets are subject to His command and obey His every decree.

## The Senses and the Powers of the Brain

Those parts of the body that are firm and solid are called ⟪ar:'اعضا '⟫ organs; those that are in the form of vaporous essences—very subtle, pure, and transparent—arising from these four qualities and pervading the body are ⟪ar:'مزاجات '⟫ temperaments; and the subtle substance itself is ⟪ar:'روح حیوانی '⟫ the animal soul.

| Solid | Intermediate | Subtle |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Organs | Temperaments | Animal Soul |

This soul originates at the animal place—the heart—and spreads through the passages of the mind. Then it passes through the eye-sockets, ear-canals, tongue-cavities, and extends to all ⟪ar:رئیس و اسفل⟫ superior and inferior organs, finally reaching the toes. This soul invests the entire body as hands invest themselves in gloves.

The human body possesses five external powers: ⟪ar:لمس، ذائقۃ، بو، سماعت اور بصر⟫ touch, taste, smell, hearing, and sight. Of these, ⟪ar:لمس، ذائقۃ، بو⟫ the three—touch, taste, and smell—develop within the womb. The remaining two ⟪ar:سماعت وبصر⟫ hearing and sight—are created when the infant emerges from the womb. Corresponding to these five external senses are ⟪ar:حواس مشترکۃ، مصورۃ، مفکرۃ، وہمیۃ اور حافظۃ⟫ five internal senses: the common sense, the imaginative power, the thinking power, the estimative power, and the faculty of retention. All these senses exist in the fetus ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ in potentiality before its birth and begin to manifest ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ in actuality at their appointed times after birth, culminating in ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ مجازی⟫ the metaphorical rational soul.

| Order | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | Sense |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| External | Touch | Smell | Taste | Hearing | Sight | Power of Sensation |
| Internal | Common | Imaginative | Reflective | Estimative | Retentive | Power of Intellection |

The common sense dwells in the foremost part of the brain. It is called 'common' because every corporeal power flows through it, and every faculty resembles this intellect in some way. All that the eye sees, the ear hears, the hand touches, the tongue tastes, the nose smells—all these sensations originate in the mind. This part of the brain is therefore called ⟪ar:'مخزن صور محسوسات '⟫ the repository of sensible forms.

⟪ar:قوت تخیل⟫ The imaginative power dwells in another part of the brain. When this power serves and aids the intellect, it is called ⟪ar:قوت مفکرۃ⟫ the reflective power. When its attention turns toward bodily desires and gives rise to supposition and conjecture, it is called ⟪ar:قوت متخیلۃ⟫ the imaginative power. When ⟪ar:متخیلۃ⟫ the imaginative power is activated, the intellect stores this thought in the third part of the brain, which is ⟪ar:قوت حافظۃ⟫ called the retentive power. ⟪ar:تخمینۃ⟫ The estimative power resides in the fourth part of the brain, in the middle of the head. ⟪ar:قوت حافظۃ⟫ Retention is positioned in the back of the brain, storing every ⟪ar:شبیح⟫ form-impression that ⟪ar:متخیلۃ⟫ the imagination transmits to it. ⟪ar:قوت متذکرۃ⟫ The recollective power retrieves ⟪ar:شبیح⟫ such impressions when required. ⟪ar:حافظۃ⟫ Retention preserves all bodily impressions and sensations. This power exists in humans and animals and continues to function as long as the four temperaments maintain equilibrium in the body. Yet by reason of the eternal nature of the soul, ⟪ar:متخیلۃ⟫ a portion of the imagination always remains intact and never perishes.

⟪ar:'عقل '⟫ The intellect is like a postmaster to whom messengers bring news and letters concerning various events, which he then arranges and organizes. ⟪ar:'متخیلۃ '⟫ The imagination is like his secretary, to whom he gives letters to place in the bag. ⟪ar:'متذکرۃ '⟫ Recollection is like the king, to whom ⟪ar:متخیلۃ⟫ imagination delivers all letters so that he might read them and derive benefit or harm from them. The king then hands these letters—these thoughts—to his treasurer, ⟪ar:حافظۃ⟫ retention, to be safely stored in the treasury. ⟪ar:مفکرۃ⟫ Reflection is the king's official, who can retrieve those letters—those memories—from the treasury whenever he wishes, at the king's command.

## Humanity as the Quintessence of the Universe

⟪ar:صاحب رسائل اخوان الصفا⟫ The author of the Epistles of the Brethren of Purity explains that ⟪ar:'عالم'⟫ the world refers to the seven heavens, the earth, and all that lies between them. They also call it ⟪ar:'انسان الکبیر '⟫ the Macrocosm or Great Human. This is because, like humanity, the world itself possesses a body with distinct organs. This world also possesses a soul whose powers flow through its organs. A human soul produces effects upon its body; the manifestations of this single effect appear in diverse forms. The tongue speaks, the eyes see, the ears hear, the feet walk—each organ displays its particular function.

From the circumference of the heavens to the center of the Saqqara, this world is itself a body—or a human being—which is called ⟪ar:'عالم الکبیر'⟫ the Universal World or Macrocosm. There dwells within it a ⟪ar:نفس کل⟫ Universal Soul which exerts its influence upon this vast body. Different parts of the ⟪ar:عالم الکبیر⟫ Universal World receive this ⟪ar:نفس کل⟫ influence in accordance with their receptivity and capacity, and from this their various natures emerge. For example, the soul's effect is manifested as motion in the heavens, but as rest in the earth. This ⟪ar:نفس کل⟫ Universal Soul creates mingling in the Ummahat (the elemental mothers) and stasis in minerals. It manifests as growth in plants and sensation in animals. In humanity, it manifests as reason and discernment. For this reason, the Almighty is described as "Creator of the Heavens, Dweller upon the Earth, Stillness of Minerals, Growth of Plants, and Life-Giver to Animals." [^cite-9]

The hierarchy illustrates the order of creation descending from the Mover (muḥarrik), through the concentric heavens, down through the elemental mothers and the four categories of being—mineral, plant, animal, and human—to the Universal Body.

The heavens and the moon-sphere above it are perfect in their essence and goodness; their perfection flows forth as ⟪ar:تشبیح⟫ glorification. ⟪ar:'انسان کلی'⟫ The Universal or Perfect Human represents this celestial perfection. In contrast, in the lower realm beneath the moon's sphere, where good and evil mingle, there appears ⟪ar:'انسان جزوی'⟫ the Particular or Imperfect Human. Humanity is created between these two and thus receives influence from both. ⟪ar:انسان کلی وانسان جزوی⟫ The Particular Human is created from the lower celestial realm, ⟪ar:انسان کلی، انسان جزوی⟫ just as those lower realms are created from the heavens. These lower domains remain perpetually subject to the heavens and receive their influences continuously.

⟪ar:صاحب رسائل⟫ It is said by the authors of the Epistles that the Ummahat (elemental mothers) appeared after the heavens; minerals came after the Ummahat; plants came after minerals; animals came after plants; and humanity came after animals. This series is not confined to any single epoch; rather, these emanations continue to occur in the same order perpetually. Minerals continue to become plants; plants continue to become animals; and animals continue to manifest in human form. Yet after becoming human, there is no further progress into any other form. The reason is that humanity is the essence and quintessence of these three—minerals, plants, and animals. Once returned to the original state of perfection, there is no further necessity to transform into any other form.

A perfect human being who has attained the original purpose of his birth and whose development is complete—this is the reason the Divine did not create any creature after humanity, for humanity is the perfect embodiment of the mineral, plant, and animal realms, and the true and ultimate purpose of their generation. The principal purpose of minerals, plants, and animals is to evolve and manifest in human form. Once humanity is achieved, there is no need to advance into any other form. This is why humanity is the link and the bond. ⟪ar:موالید ثلاثۃ⟫ These three births—minerals, plants, and animals—depend upon humanity's existence, and humanity makes use of all three for its sustenance and continued life.
