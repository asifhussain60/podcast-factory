## The Distinction Between Divinity and Creation

There are two fundamental modes of bringing into being: divinity (al-ilāhiyya) and creation. The first consists in bringing forth from absolute non-existence; the second involves transforming the ordering of existing substances—as when wood is fashioned into a chair. The Creator, praised be He (Subḥāna), stands apart from all that is created; He alone possesses the attribute of absolute origination.

### The Proof of Divinity

The proof of the Creator's divinity is established through the Qur'anic testimony:

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah bears witness that there is no deity except Him, and [so do] the angels and those of knowledge—[that He is] maintaining [creation] in justice. There is no deity except Him, the Exalted in Might, the Wise.*

In this verse, the Originator Himself—praised be He (Mubdiʿ Taʿālā)—bears witness to His divinity through His First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal), who ⟪ar:'ھو'⟫ testifies to the divinity ⟪ar:'الہیت'⟫ of the Creator of all existence. Yet this raises a subtle point: testimony is ordinarily given by one being concerning another, not by the Creator regarding His own essence. The resolution lies in understanding that ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Creator ⟪ar:الٰہیت⟫ manifests His divinity through the witness-bearing of His noblest creation.[^cite-1]

Our Master, the Illustrious Ḥamīd al-Dīn al-Kirmānī—may God sanctify his secret—clarified this doctrine in his luminous work ⟪ar:راحت العقل⟫ (*The Repose of the Intellect*). He systematically negates all conventional causes for creation and establishes instead a truth of surpassing subtlety: the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā brought forth the entirety of existence as pure grace and mercy, seeking neither cause nor compensation. For it lies in the very nature of the Generous (al-Karīm) and the Powerful (al-Qādir) ⟪ar:کریم و قادر⟫ to bestow bounty without reason or prerequisite; and it is the attribute of the Mighty and the Sublime (al-ʿAzīz wa-al-ʿAẓīm) ⟪ar:عزیز و عظیم⟫ to grant gifts in the absence of need or obligation.[^cite-2]

### The Purpose of Creation

The Tenth Imam, the Master ⟪ar:عاشر علیہ السلام⟫, revealed the purpose behind creation in the sacred Qur'an: the jinn and humankind were brought into being for a singular reason—that they might worship Him.

> ⟪quran 51:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا خَلَقْتُ ٱلْجِنَّ وَٱلْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ ‎⟫
> *And I have not created the jinn and mankind except that they might worship Me.*

This worship constitutes the very essence of the Tawhīd of the Lord of all Worlds (Tawḥīd Rabb al-ʿĀlamīn) ⟪ar:' توحید رب العلمین '⟫. For the worshipper (ʿabd) ⟪ar:'عبد'⟫ must know his own nature and recognise his Lord, the Worshipped One (al-maʿbūd) ⟪ar:'معبود'⟫. Without such recognition, the very purpose of creation would be rendered void. How could creation attain its appointed end if those fashioned for worship remained ignorant of their Creator?

## The Creation of the Spiritual World

The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā brought forth the spiritual world (al-ʿālam al-rūḥānī) ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ in an instantaneous emanation, from absolute non-existence in such manner that no prior substrate was prepared ⟪ar:'عدم غیر موجود'⟫. He did not create it upon hylē primordial (al-hayūlā al-ūlā) ⟪ar:ھیولیٰ اولیٰ⟫, nor did He establish it in the first place or location (al-makān al-awwal) ⟪ar:مکان اول⟫ as a precedent for subsequent creations. Rather, in one singular act, in one eternal moment (dafʿatan wāḥida) ⟪ar:دفعتاً واحدۃ⟫, the Originator—Most High—brought forth the spiritual world from pure non-being (ʿadam) ⟪ar:عدم⟫ into absolute existence (wujūd) ⟪ar:وجود⟫. It consisted of luminous forms, essences, and emanations (nūrānī ashbāḥ / dhawāt / mubdaʿāt) ⟪ar:نورانی اشباح / ذوات / مُبدَعاَت⟫.

All these beings were brought into existence in states of joy, pleasure, and spiritual delight. Their source, their wellspring, and the fountain of their birth lay in beatitude alone—never in sorrow or anger. The Qur'an witnesses to their inconceivable multitude:

> ⟪quran 74:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا جَعَلْنَآ أَصْحَبَ ٱلنَّارِ إِلَّا مَلَٓئِكَةًۭ ۙ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا عِدَّتَهُمْ إِلَّا فِتْنَةًۭ لِّلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لِيَسْتَيْقِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَيَزْدَادَ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِيمَنًۭا ۙ وَلَا يَرْتَابُ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۙ وَلِيَقُولَ ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِم مَّرَضٌۭ وَٱلْكَفِرُونَ مَاذَآ أَرَادَ ٱللَّهُ بِهَذَا مَثَلًۭا ۚ كَذَلِكَ يُضِلُّ ٱللَّهُ مَن يَشَآءُ وَيَهْدِى مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ جُنُودَ رَبِّكَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ وَمَا هِىَ إِلَّا ذِكْرَىٰ لِلْبَشَرِ ‎⟫
> *And We have made the guardians of the Fire only angels. And We have not made their number except as a trial for those who disbelieve—that those who were given the Scripture will be certain and those who have believed will increase in faith, and those who were given the Scripture and the believers will not doubt. And so that those in whose hearts is disease and the disbelievers will say, "What does Allah intend by this as an example?" Thus does Allah leave astray whom He wills and guides whom He wills. And none knows the hosts of your Lord except Him. And this is not but a reminder to mankind.*

The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā endowed these emanations (al-mubdaʿāt) ⟪ar:مُبدَعات⟫ with such overwhelming multitude because there exists no limit to His grace and His bestowal of gifts.[^cite-3]

### Simultaneous Creation

These existences were not brought into being sequentially, marked by before and after; rather, they sprang forth in a single instant (dafʿatan wāḥida) ⟪ar:دفعتاً واحدۃ⟫. Had the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā caused one group to come into being and then a second, injustice would have followed. The former (al-sābiq) ⟪ar:سابق⟫ would inevitably possess superiority (al-sabāqiyya) ⟪ar:سباقیت⟫ over the latter, and hierarchy would be established—a violation of perfect justice. But the Creator, in His absolute fairness, brought all beings into existence in a single state and at one degree, such that no difference, however minute, separated them. To this truth the Qur'an testifies:

> ⟪quran 67:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ سَبْعَ سَمَوَتٍۢ طِبَاقًۭا ۖ مَّا تَرَىٰ فِى خَلْقِ ٱلرَّحْمَنِ مِن تَفَوُتٍۢ ۖ فَٱرْجِعِ ٱلْبَصَرَ هَلْ تَرَىٰ مِن فُطُورٍۢ ‎⟫
> *It is He who created seven heavens in layers. You do not see in the creation of the Most Merciful any inconsistency. So return [your] vision; do you see any breaks?*

Our master Ḥasan Khān ibn ʿAbd al-Malik—may God exalt his sanctuary—⟪ar:سیدنا حسن خان بن عبد الملک أعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ explained this truth with luminous precision: "It is as though the rays of the sun illumine the entire world instantaneously, or as fire kindles every corner of a dark house in a single moment." There was no temporal succession (qabla wa-baʿda) ⟪ar:قبل⟫ and ⟪ar:بعد⟫ in the spiritual world—only the simultaneity of creation's outpouring.[^cite-4]

### The Form of Alif

The Creator fashioned all these beings in the form of Alif (Qamat al-Aliffiya) ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫, for the letter Alif stands as the supreme form among all letters of the alphabet. When afterward the Originator brought humankind into existence in the physical realm, He created human form also in accordance with this eternal archetype of Alif.

*—as transmitted by Muḥammad Isḥāq al-Diyāʾī ibn al-Maʾmūn al-Munsūb Muḥammad Ibrāhīm al-Diyāʾī* ⟪ar:محمد اسحاق ضیائی بن حضرت المنصوب محمد ابراھیم ضیائی⟫

### The Spiritual World Is Not a Place

The term "spiritual world" (al-ʿālam al-rūḥānī) ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ may occasion the misunderstanding that "world" (ʿālam) ⟪ar:'عالم'⟫ denotes a house or location. This is erroneous. The spiritual world is not a dwelling into which intellects were first created and then transferred. To suppose this would mean that the Originator gave the intellect (ʿaql) precedence before establishing the world—an inversion of reality.[^cite-5] Rather, the spiritual world came into being through the very emanation of the intellects themselves; it is neither a container nor a space. The intellects (ʿuqūl) ⟪ar:عقول⟫ did not come into a pre-existing realm; rather, from the emanation of the intellects the spiritual world arose. 

Consider a helpful analogy: the gathering (Dāwat al-Sharīf) is not the name of a chamber where the elderly sit to feast. Rather, it is the name of a living system to which all are organically bound. The spiritual world similarly possesses attributes of essential reality (ṣifāt ʿayniyya) ⟪ar:صفات عینیت⟫ and divinity (al-ilāhiyya) ⟪ar:الہیت⟫; it is neither above nor below us, but rather encompasses us, and we exist within its very substance.

### The Final Creation

The Creator expressed Himself through His first creation, the divinity (al-ilāhiyya) of the emanations. No second act of creation shall follow, for should it occur, those created earlier would possess precedence over those created later—an injustice incompatible with the nature of the Divine.

### True Paradise

Paradise is not singular but manifold. Each exalted rank (janna) ⟪ar:'جنت'⟫ serves as a paradise for those below it, and each lower station constitutes a world (dunyā) ⟪ar:'دنیا'⟫ for those above. Concerning true Paradise, the Prophet—peace and blessings upon him—declared it to be a place "which no eye has beheld, no ear has heard, and no heart has ever imagined." The true spiritual world (al-ʿālam al-rūḥānī) ⟪ar:'عالم روحانی'⟫ is indeed that paradisiacal realm wherein joy and beatitude reign eternal, unmarred by the faintest trace of sorrow.

## Primary Perfection (al-Kamāl al-Awwal)

The Creator brought into being all essences (dhawāt) ⟪ar:'ذوات'⟫—from non-being (hast) ⟪ar:ہست⟫ to existence (nīst) ⟪ar:نیست⟫—in a single instant (dafʿatan wāḥida) ⟪ar:دفعتا واحدۃ⟫, elevated at once to the state of primary perfection (kamāl awwal) ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ in the form of Alif (Qamat al-Aliffiya) ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫. This bestowal of perfection (kamāl) ⟪ar:'کمال'⟫ flowed from the grace and mercy of the Creator; indeed, it is the very nature of the Generous that He show mercy.[^cite-6] All these emanations were created by the Almighty in a single manner, from one perspective (ḥayithiyya) ⟪ar:حیثیت⟫, at one level of whatness (lamiyya) ⟪ar:لمیت⟫, from one vantage of quantity (kamiyya) ⟪ar:کمیت⟫, and with one essence (māhiyya) ⟪ar:ماہیت⟫—such that not the slightest variation existed among them. This primary perfection (kamāl awwal) ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ constituted the first material substance (mawād awwal) ⟪ar:مواد أول⟫ that the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā bestowed upon them. All perfection (kamāl) ⟪ar:' کمال '⟫ in the emanations (mubdaʿāt) ⟪ar:مبدعات⟫ rested upon three essential jewels:

| **The Three Jewels of Primary Perfection** | | |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Essence** | **Manifestation** | **Exemplar** |
| Self-Existent, Primordial | Additional Attribute | |
| Life (Ḥayāt) | The Eternal Essence | Perpetual vitality that admits of no corruption or annihilation |
| Dominion (Quwwa) | Absolute Power | All capacities and faculties, with no impediment to their exercise; full liberty and authority to act or refrain |
| Knowledge (ʿIlm) | Universal Knowing | Complete knowledge of all powers, abilities, and faculties resident in life itself |

**A Statement of the Master al-Mansūb Diyāʾ ʿAlī** ⟪ar:حضرت المنصوب ضیاء علی صاحب⟫:

He declared, "The Creator fashioned us free and self-subsisting, perfected with full knowledge, power, and life." We ourselves forged slavery, dependence, and servitude. Today we rely upon parents for birth, upon teachers for learning, upon employment for livelihood—all because we have veiled ourselves with ignorance and false attribution.

### The Three Letters and the Word of Tawhīd

These three jewels are themselves the opposite faces of the Divine attestation—the letters Alif, Lām, and Hāʾ (al-Alif, al-Lām wa-al-Hāʾ) ⟪ar:الف، لام اور ھا⟫. For the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā Himself is "Allah" (al-Ilāh) ⟪ar:الہ⟫—the Divine Reality—and He endowed all beings with three essences corresponding to the letters of His greatest name: Life, Dominion, and Knowledge as the letters Alif, Lām, and Hāʾ (al-Ilāh: al-Alif, al-Lām, wa-al-Hāʾ) ⟪ar:الہ (الف + لام + ھا)⟫. The three letters ⟪ar:الف، لام، ھا⟫ possess the quality of letters in their potentiality (bi-al-quwwa) ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ and exist as mute letters (ḥurūf muʿjam) ⟪ar:حروف معجم⟫ in their original state. When these three letters are arranged in their proper sequence and each is placed in its true position, they produce the sublime word of negation and affirmation: "There is no god but Allah" (Lā ilāha illā al-Lāh) ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ ⟫, which constitutes their actualized state (bi-al-fiʿl) ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫. 

Thus, though the word "No god but" (Lā ilāha) ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫ originates from these three letters (Alif, Lām, Hāʾ: ا ل ہ) ⟪ar:ا ل ہ⟫, the realization of the full attestation "No god but Allah" (Lā ilāha illā al-Lāh) ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ depends upon their being arranged and actualized in their proper configuration. The negation "Lā ilāha" (No god) ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫ itself cannot fulfill the purpose of these letters unless they enter into the fullness of articulation. Similarly, though the jewels of these beings were created in a potential state (bi-al-quwwa) ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫, their supreme purpose lay in their actualization (bi-al-fiʿl) ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ through the active exercise of their own faculties.

| Life (Ḥayāt) | Dominion (Quwwa) | Knowledge (ʿIlm) |
| Vegetative Soul (Nafs al-Nāmiyya) | Sensible Soul (Nafs al-Ḥissiyya) | Rational Soul (Nafs al-Nāṭiqa) |

**On Origin and Duration:**

We too received this first material substance ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫, yet we could not properly comprehend it. That is, from the jewels within us (the letters: ا ل ہ) ⟪ar:ا ل ہ⟫ we failed to compose the word that was our essential purpose. Because of this error, we were relegated to lower ranks. Then the Originator created us anew in this physical world, granting us again the three essences in the form of the rational soul (nafs nāṭiqa) ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫, the sensible soul (nafs ḥissiyya) ⟪ar:حسیۃ⟫, and the vegetative soul (nafs nāmiyya) ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫. Now our task is to gain knowledge of Him ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫ through learning and action in this world, ascending through the ranks of perfection until we attain again to the first perfection. When we reach that station once more, we shall commence our journey toward secondary perfection ⟪ar:الا اللہ⟫.

### The Pre-eminence of Life

These three jewels encompass all praiseworthy qualities (khiṣāl maḥmūda) ⟪ar:خصال محمودہ⟫. Any excellence, virtue, happiness, or felicity may be traced back to them. The foremost of these three is life itself (al-ḥayāt) ⟪ar:حیات⟫, for it is the origin and the wellspring from which every action flows. Without life (al-ḥayy) ⟪ar:حی⟫, none could be intelligent (ʿāqil) ⟪ar:عاقل⟫, enduring (bāqī) ⟪ar:باقی⟫, knowing (ʿālim) ⟪ar:عالم⟫, perfect (kāmil) ⟪ar:کامل⟫, or powerful (qādir) ⟪ar:قادر⟫; without life, nothing could be complete (tāmm) ⟪ar:تام⟫, singular (wāḥid) ⟪ar:واحد⟫, or existent (mawjūd) ⟪ar:موجود⟫. Even if all other attributes were stripped away, if life alone remained, all the remaining attributes would persist through it, for all others are bound to it eternally (abadan) ⟪ar:أِبدا⟫.

This demonstrates that in the essence of life (al-ḥayāt), within primary perfection (kamāl awwal) ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫, lies the real jewel—the core substance (al-ilāh) ⟪ar:الہ⟫ of the attestation "No god but Allah" (Lā ilāha) ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫. By removing it, every other attribute becomes invalidated. Just as the letter Alif (al-Alif) ⟪ar:' الف'⟫ remains the original letter of the alphabet, and all other letters depend upon it for their existence and articulation—the very shape of Alif gives rise to all other letters through subtle modifications of its form—so too does life stand as the primordial essence from which all other perfections derive.

### The Qualities of Primary Perfection

All beings in primary perfection possessed two singular characteristics:

| **1. Perfect Equality** | In the first state of perfection, all beings stood in absolute equality. No entity was superior or inferior to another. The perfection bestowed upon the First Intellect was identical to that granted to all the rest, reflecting the Almighty's perfect justice. In this state, even the first intellect and those who would later fall into error remained equal in spiritual rank, with no hierarchy or gradation. |
| --- | --- |
| **2. Potential Innocence** | This perfection was bestowed in a potential state, not an actualized one. Though the three jewels existed within all beings, they possessed only the potentiality (bi-al-quwwa) ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ to exercise them; the actualization (bi-al-fiʿl) ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ remained before them. In this state, all beings were innocent (maʿṣūm) in their actions, yet capable of error. |

An analogy illuminates this state: the Almighty created all children with full potential, yet the power to develop those talents belongs to them alone. They may become physicians or engineers through diligent cultivation, or remain ordinary farmers by negligence. Similarly, every seed contains within itself the potentiality to become a tree, yet this potential cannot be actualized (bi-al-fiʿl) ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ until it is sown in soil and nurtured by rain.

| **The Positive and Negative Poles** | | |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Letter** | **Positive Form** (Ummah al-Barr) | **Negative Form** (Ummah al-Maʿāṣī) |
| Alif (Life) | Life-Giving | Arrogance |
| Lām (Dominion) | Authority and Justice | Envy and Oppression |
| Hāʾ (Knowledge) | Wisdom and Guidance | Heedlessness and Error |

The fact that all these beings were created simultaneously in the same state attests to Divine justice (al-ʿilm fī al-ghayb al-ghuyūb) ⟪ar:غیب الغیوب⟫. In this manner, every entity (dhāt) ⟪ar:ذات⟫ received an equal opportunity in the race toward perfection. In the state of primary perfection, no essence surpassed another. The Qur'an proclaims this truth:

> ⟪quran 4:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَفَلَا يَتَدَبَّرُونَ ٱلْقُرْآنَ ۚ وَلَوْ كَانَ مِنْ عِندِ غَيْرِ ٱللَّهِ لَوَجَدُوا۟ فِيهِ ٱخْتِلَفًۭا كَثِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *Do they not reflect upon the Qur'an? Had it been from [any] other than Allah, they would have found within it much contradiction.*

**What is meant here:** The Qur'an has gathered all its chapters (sūras) in perfect harmony, just as the spiritual world gathered all its inhabitants in perfect equity.

### The Powers of Primary Perfection

Even in this state of potentiality, the capacities of these beings were magnificent beyond measure. The Summoner speaks of their powers in these terms: "He was the entirety of sight perceiving the whole, the entirety of hearing encompassing the whole—the totality of sensation manifest as One." Between them and the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā stood only the Tawhīd (absolute oneness in faith). Each being possessed the potential to progress indefinitely. All that remained was for them to exercise their authority and fulfill the work for which they were created. The very actualization of their jewels testified that they must perform an action (fiʿl) ⟪ar:'فعل'⟫ that would transform those jewels from potentiality into actuality. Had this not been the case, the Creator would have brought them into existence already in an actualized state.

The proof of the mighty powers resident in these beings lies in our own selves—a despicable fragment of this material world. Our souls are imprisoned within our bodies, yet our thoughts transcend all constraint of time and space. In an instant they traverse from present to past, from future to now; in the blink of an eye they soar beyond this world to the heavens and stars, embracing in their scope what distances immeasurable cannot contain. If we—confined to physical density and separated by the veil of matter—possess even this fraction of such swiftness of thought, what then must be the powers of those beings in the spiritual world (al-ʿālam al-rūḥānī), who are fashioned entirely of light (kull) ⟪ar:کُل⟫, untrammeled by time or space, unconstrained by any corporeal limit? We cannot even begin to conceive the capacities of that world of luminous subtlety while we dwell in this realm of gross materiality!

## Secondary Perfection (al-Kamāl al-Thānī)

### The Imperative of Actualization

It was necessary for these beings to exercise the jewels of their life, power, and knowledge in action, thereby transforming what existed in potentiality (bi-al-quwwa) ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ into actuality (bi-al-fiʿl) ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫, and advancing from the state of primary perfection (kamāl awwal) ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ into secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫. This transformation was indeed the very purpose for which the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā brought them into being, as the Qur'an declares:

> ⟪quran 51:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا خَلَقْتُ ٱلْجِنَّ وَٱلْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ ‎⟫
> *And I have not created the jinn and mankind except that they might worship Me.*

The real purpose of creation is devotion and worship (ʿubūdiyya / ʿibāda) ⟪ar:عبودیت⟫ and ⟪ar:عبادت⟫. Every form of worship possesses as its soul the Tawhīd of the Lord (Tawḥīd Rabb) ⟪ar:'توحید رب'⟫. The worshipper (ʿabd) ⟪ar:'عبد'⟫ must know himself and recognize his Lord, the Worshipped One (al-maʿbūd) ⟪ar:'معبود'⟫. The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā was in no need whatsoever of these creatures. Rather, it became incumbent upon those brought into existence to contemplate themselves and wonder: why do these three jewels exist within me, and from whence do they come? Upon gaining knowledge of these blessings, they must recognize their Benefactor.

It was necessary for these beings to awaken the potentialities that had been placed within them in potential form, so that the true purpose of these jewels might be fulfilled. Had the Creator willed, He would have created these jewels already in actualized form. But then how could precedence be distinguished? How could the difference between the former and the latter be established? The true purpose of primary perfection was that the servant (ʿabd) ⟪ar:'عبد'⟫ should recognize his Lord, the Worshipped One (al-maʿbūd) ⟪ar:معبود⟫, and through Tawhīd advance toward secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫, thereby receiving reward from his Lord. Failure to attain it or delay in its acquisition was their own responsibility. Neither in the Eternal Abode (Dār al-Abidāʾ) ⟪ar:دار الأبدا⟫ nor in the House of Emanation (Dār al-Inbʿāth) ⟪ar:دار الأنبعاث⟫ was there any substance or barrier that would prevent them from devotion to Tawhīd. Therefore, there existed no obstacle or impediment to the attainment of these blessings through action (fiʿl) ⟪ar:' فعل'⟫.

### The Acquisition of Secondary Material

When these beings employed their faculties in the practice of Tawhīd (devotional oneness) ⟪ar:'توحید'⟫, they received from the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā what is known as secondary material (mawād thānī) ⟪ar:مواد ثانی⟫ or sacred material (mawād qudsī: streaming knowledge and flowing light) ⟪ar:مواد قدسی (علم جاری و نور ساری)⟫. From this sacred substance, their jewels sprang forth, transforming from potentiality (bi-al-quwwa) ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ to actuality (bi-al-fiʿl) ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫, and they entered into secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫. The first material (mawād awwal) ⟪ar:مواد اول⟫ was a grant of grace. When these beings made proper use of it, they received their true reward: the secondary material (mawād thānī / mawād qudsī) ⟪ar:مواد ثانی (مواد قدسی)⟫. In truth, secondary perfection is the actualization of the form.

### The Character of Secondary Perfection

Secondary perfection manifested in two distinct ways:

| **1. Establishment of Hierarchy** | In secondary perfection, a hierarchy of superiority came into being among all beings, and the order of the upper limit and lower limit was established. No two beings remained equal to one another in the state of secondary perfection. |
| --- | --- |
| **2. Perfect Immunity from Error** | In secondary perfection, all beings achieved perfect innocence (ʿiṣma) and became incapable of error. This occurred because they became annexed to the sacred material (mawād qudsī). |

We note that all intellects who have attained perfection are constantly increasing in their luminosity and their knowledge, power, and life (ʿilm qudrat wa-ḥayāt) ⟪ar:(علم قدرت اور حیات)⟫ are ceaselessly growing. Now their task is to continue advancing and perpetually increasing, for the bounties of the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā know no limit.[^cite-7]

## Is the Innocence of Secondary Perfection Compulsory?

Their innocence is not forced or coercive; rather, it is voluntary, for the Almighty does not oppress. Consider: we possess the capacity to consume carrion if we choose. Yet our nature recoils from it utterly, for we recognize such an act as abominable, and our souls are too noble to introduce such filth into our bodies. Similarly, these intellects have achieved such lofty and magnificent perfection that they are disinclined—not compelled—to make any error or mistake.

Innocence (ʿiṣma) is not subject to compulsion, but rather to voluntary realization. That is, they are not forced to abstain from error; rather, their knowledge, life, and the essence of their power have reached such elevation that they can never incline toward falsehood. The sacred material (mawād qudsī) ⟪ar:مواد قدسی⟫ has so perfected them that error becomes as repugnant to them as filth is repugnant to human nature.

An illuminating example: Certain sectarian disputants once challenged the believers, saying: "You claim that Allah is omnipotent, yet you deny He can commit injustice. Do you thereby diminish His power? If Allah can do all things, surely He can also perform injustice, for He is Almighty (ʿAlī Kull Shayʾin Qadīr) ⟪ar:علی کل شئی قدیر⟫!" The believers replied with an analogy: "Tell us—do you have the power to eat and drink whatsoever you will?" The disputants answered: "Indeed!" The believers then challenged: "Then eat this filth lying before you on the road!" In this way they demonstrated that the impossibility of doing something vile is not a limitation of power, but rather a reflection of perfect nature and wisdom.

The state of secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ in these beings is such that despite possessing all powers, they cannot be inclined toward error. Before their attachment to the sacred material (mawād qudsī), these entities (beings in primary perfection, kamāl awwal) ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ were indeed liable to mistake. But with the attainment of sacred material, their very souls (nufūs) ⟪ar:نفوس⟫—their knowledge, dominion, and life—have become so radiant and purified that they can never incline toward error (filth), just as no human being can ever incline toward consuming filth.

## The Distinction Between Primary and Secondary Perfection

The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā brought all beings into existence from nothingness in a single instant (dafʿatan wāḥida) ⟪ar:دفعتا واحدۃ⟫, elevating them to primary perfection (kamāl awwal) ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫. This perfection (kamāl) ⟪ar:'کمال'⟫ consisted of the three jewels: knowledge, dominion, and life. When the intellects performed the Tawhīd (negation and disassociation) ⟪ar:تنزیہ⟫ and the Tawhīd of separation (Tawḥīd Tajrīd) ⟪ar:توحید ⟪ar:تجرید⟫ ⟫, they received from the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā the Light of the Word "No god but" (Nūr Kalimah Lā ilāha) ⟪ar:نور کلمہ لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ ⟫—or what is otherwise called streaming knowledge and flowing light (ʿIlm Jārī wa-Nūr Sārī) ⟪ar:علم جاری⟫ and ⟪ar:نور ساری⟫ from the Almighty (Allāh) ⟪ar:اللہ⟫. The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā ⟪ar:مبدِع تعالی⟫ grants to them from this sacred material. Through this knowledge, these intellects transformed from potentiality (bi-al-quwwa) to actuality (bi-al-fiʿl), and this state is called secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫. In this condition, they attained perfect innocence, such that error became impossible.

After attaining perfection, the name "Allah" was applied to them on account of their nobility and majesty. All human intellects are bewildered regarding even their lowest attributes and cannot fully comprehend them, so it remains impossible even to conjecture their highest nature! When the emanations (mubdaʿāt) in their primary perfection (kamāl awwal) ⟪ar:مُبدَعات⟫ are incapable of comprehending our lowest attributes, how is it conceivable that they could comprehend the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā, the Creator of all? How is knowledge of the Creator possible? For our understanding's sake, we may say: the difference between primary perfection (kamāl awwal) and secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ is as great as the difference between a seed and a mature tree, or between a drop of sperm and a fully developed young man. 

What was created in the state of primary perfection is the essence (dhāt) ⟪ar:' ذات'⟫; what was acquired through secondary perfection is the form (ṣūrat) ⟪ar:**' صورت'**⟫.

**A Statement of the Master al-Mansūb ʿAbdullāh** ⟪ar:حضرت المنصوب عبد اللہ صاحب⟫:

Describing this state with subtle precision, he declared: "The form (ṣūrat) ⟪ar:صورت⟫ created by the creature through the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā is greater than the essence (dhāt) created directly by the Originator. For the essence is created from non-being, while the form is fashioned from the sacred material itself, which flows eternally from above (min fawq) ⟪ar:من فوق⟫."

### Table of Distinction

| **Aspect** | **Primary Perfection (Kamāl Awwal)** | **Secondary Perfection (Kamāl Thānī)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Nature of Being** | Essence (Dhāt) | Form (Ṣūrat) |
| **State of Existence** | Standing Firm | Already Existing and Manifest |
| **Material Substance** | First Material (Knowledge, Life, Power) | Sacred Material (Streaming Knowledge and Flowing Light) |
| **Ranks** | Uniform in rank, without hierarchy | Single-Ranking and Ordered in hierarchy |
| **Innocence** | Potential innocence only | Perfect and absolute innocence |
| **Attestation** | Lā ilāha (No god but) [Negation] | Illā Allāh (but Allah alone) [Affirmation] |

### Alif and Circle

The form of Alif is straight; the form of a circle is curved. Alif is the noblest letter of the alphabet, and this is why the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā brought all beings into existence in the form of Alif (Qamat al-Aliffiya) ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫. Alif possesses two extremities, and neither end connects to itself; the line remains open. This signifies that in primary perfection (kamāl awwal) these beings had no spiritual connection to each other. Yet there is no break in the form of a circle; every point connects to every other point. This means that in secondary perfection, each level becomes joined to the one above it. The letter Alif (as essence, dhāt) signifies what is individual and singular, without connection; the circle (as form, ṣūrat) represents what is mutually reinforcing and eternally unified. For this reason, the form of the lower boundary merges with the form of the upper boundary, and the two become one indivisible whole.

## The Jewels of the Physical World

When we journeyed from the spiritual world into the physical realm and took on human form, the Almighty granted us the same three jewels of knowledge, power, and life that had been bestowed upon all beings in the spiritual world. The Qur'an attests to this truth:

> ⟪quran 51:20-21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَفِى ٱلْأَرْضِ آيَتٌۭ لِّلْمُوقِنِينَ ‎ ‏وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ ۚ أَفَلَا تُبْصِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And on the earth are signs for those of certain faith, and within yourselves. Will you not then perceive?*

Now we must take utmost care not to repeat the error we committed in the spiritual world. We must make proper use of our knowledge, dominion, and life to create a noble form—the form of secondary perfection—thereby rectifying what was left incomplete and ascending to that station of supreme felicity from which we once fell. In this physical world, we have been granted a second opportunity: to know our Creator through His signs manifest in the earth and in ourselves, and through this knowledge to transform our potential perfection into actual virtue, that we might stand among those who have truly understood the Divine reality and attained to the essence of worship.
