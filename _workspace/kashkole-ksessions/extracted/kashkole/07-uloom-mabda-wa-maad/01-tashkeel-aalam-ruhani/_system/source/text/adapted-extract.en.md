## Divinity and Creation: Two Distinct Orders

There exist two fundamental categories of origination, each operating according to its own principle. The first concerns divinity itself (al-ilāhiyya, the Godhood or the divine nature), which entails the bringing forth of being from absolute non-existence without intermediary. The second pertains to creation proper, wherein existing substances undergo transformation—as when wood is fashioned into a chair—thereby altering the arrangement of what already subsists. The first is attributed to the Originator, Most High (Mubdiʿ Taʿālā); the second, to the wise disposer of affairs.[^cite-1]

### The Testimony to Divinity

The proof of the Creator's divinity is established in the Qur'anic verse:

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫

This verse presents a profound mystery. That ⟪ar:'ھو'⟫ God Himself testifies to His divinity appears initially paradoxical, since testimony is ordinarily borne by one concerning another. Yet here, the meaning deepens when understood through the lens of esoteric interpretation (taʾwīl): it is the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal)—the highest of all created beings—who, in witnessing the transcendent nature of ⟪ar:'الہیت'⟫ divinity, attests to it on behalf of creation. This intellect perceives ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Originator, Most High, and declares His absolute uniqueness.

⟪ar:الٰہیت⟫ In his treatise *Raḥat al-ʿAql* (The Repose of the Intellect), ⟪ar:سیدنا حمید الدین کرمانی اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ Sayyidnā Ḥamīd al-Dīn al-Kirmānī, may God elevate his station, carefully negates all rational arguments that might be proposed as causes or purposes for creation. He demonstrates that the Originator, Most High, brought the cosmos into being purely as an act of grace (karāma) and mercy, not from necessity or want.[^cite-2] For it is the very nature of ⟪ar:کریم و قادر⟫ the Creator—the Generous and the Powerful—to bestow bounty without reason; and it is the very nature of ⟪ar:عزیز و عظیم⟫ the Creator—the Mighty and the Magnificent—to grant all good without expectation of recompense.

### The Stated Purpose of Creation

⟪ar:عاشر علیہ السلام⟫ Yet the Creator has revealed the purpose for which He brought creatures into being. The Holy Qur'an declares:

> ⟪quran 51:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا خَلَقْتُ ٱلْجِنَّ وَٱلْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ ‎⟫

He created the jinn and humankind only that they might worship Him. This worship encompasses the profound meaning of bearing witness to the unity of the Lord of all beings (⟪ar:' توحید رب العلمین '⟫ *tawhīd Rabb al-ʿālamīn*). For true worship requires that the servant (⟪ar:'عبد'⟫ *ʿabd*) come to know himself, and through that self-knowledge, come to recognize his Lord (⟪ar:'معبود'⟫ *maʿbūd*).[^cite-3] Had humanity remained ignorant of its Creator, the very purpose of its existence would have been rendered void.

## The Creation of the Spiritual World

### The Origination from Non-Existence

The Originator, Most High, brought forth ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ the spiritual world (al-ʿālam al-rūḥānī) in a single, instantaneous act. He created it from ⟪ar:'عدم غیر موجود'⟫ non-existence (ʿadam ghayr mawjūd)—that is, from absolute nothingness—without recourse to a primordial matter (⟪ar:ھیولیٰ اولیٰ⟫ *hylē awwalā*), without reliance upon a preexisting substrate, and without placing it upon any initial location or ground (⟪ar:مکان اول⟫ *makān awwal*).

In a single, simultaneous instant (⟪ar:دفعتاً واحدۃ⟫ *dafʿatan wāḥida*), He brought forth from ⟪ar:عدم⟫ non-being (ʿadam) the realm of ⟪ar:وجود⟫ existence (wujūd), populating it with luminous beings (⟪ar:نورانی اشباح / ذوات / مُبدَعاَت⟫ *nūrānī ashbāḥ / dhawāt / mubdaʿāt*). All these beings were brought into existence from the source of divine clemency and grace—not from the divine justice that brings forth rectitude, nor from wrathful retribution, but from the pure spring of mercy and delight.[^cite-4] Their number transcended all reckoning; indeed, they were so multitudinous that enumeration itself could not contain them, nor could any part of their vast host be numbered. The Qur'an itself bears witness to this infinite abundance:

> ⟪quran 74:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا جَعَلْنَآ أَصْحَبَ ٱلنَّارِ إِلَّا مَلَٓئِكَةًۭ ۙ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا عِدَّتَهُمْ إِلَّا فِتْنَةًۭ لِّلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لِيَسْتَيْقِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَيَزْدَادَ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِيمَنًۭا ۙ وَلَا يَرْتَابَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۙ وَلِيَقُولَ ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِم مَّرَضٌۭ وَٱلْكَفِرُونَ مَاذَآ أَرَادَ ٱللَّهُ بِهَذَا مَثَلًۭا ۚ كَذَلِكَ يُضِلُّ ٱللَّهُ مَن يَشَآءُ وَيَهْدِى مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ جُنُودَ رَبِّكَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ وَمَا هِىَ إِلَّا ذِكْرَىٰ لِلْبَشَرِ ‎⟫

The immensity of ⟪ar:مُبدَعات⟫ these creations reflects the boundlessness of the Creator's grace and liberality.

### The Simultaneous Generation

These spiritual beings were not brought into existence according to the measure of temporal succession. Rather, ⟪ar:دفعتاً واحدۃ⟫ all were created in one instantaneous act, simultaneously and uniformly. Had the Originator, Most High, created one company and then later another, injustice would inevitably have arisen: the first would have possessed priority (⟪ar:سابق⟫ *sābiq*), and through this ⟪ar:سباقیت⟫ priority (*sabāqiyya*), would have attained superiority. Those created later would necessarily have stood in a subordinate position, their very existence marked by deficiency in rank relative to the first. But divine justice dictated otherwise. The Originator, Most High, established all these beings in absolute equality, in a single state and identical degree, so that not the slightest distinction arose between them. The Qur'an itself proclaims this harmony:

> ⟪quran 67:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ سَبْعَ سَمَوَتٍۢ طِبَاقًۭا ۖ مَّا تَرَىٰ فِى خَلْقِ ٱلرَّحْمَنِ مِن تَفَوُتٍۢ ۖ فَٱرْجِعِ ٱلْبَصَرَ هَلْ تَرَىٰ مِن فُطُورٍۢ ‎⟫

This verse illuminates a profound truth: there is no discordance, no failure, no break in God's creation. ⟪ar:سیدنا حسن خان بن عبد الملک أعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ Sayyidnā Ḥasan Khān, may God elevate his station, explains through apt analogy that creation in ⟪ar:دفعتاً واحدۃ⟫ one simultaneous instant resembles the rays of the sun illuminating the entire cosmos at once, or as fire that in an instant fills every corner of a darkened chamber with light. In the spiritual world there is no distinction of ⟪ar:قبل⟫ before and ⟪ar:بعد⟫ after; there is only the eternal present, in which all these beings arise together.

### The Form of Alif

The Creator fashioned all these beings in the form of Alif (⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫ *qāmat al-alfiyya*), because the letter Alif is the most perfect and primary of all letters in the alphabet. ⟪ar:محمد اسحاق ضیائی بن حضرت المنصوب محمد ابراھیم ضیائی⟫ Later, when the Originator brought humanity into being in the physical realm, He preserved this same primordial form.[^cite-5]

### The Spiritual World Is Not a Place

The term ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ "spiritual world" (al-ʿālam al-rūḥānī) must not be misconstrued as denoting a physical location or dwelling. The word ⟪ar:'عالم'⟫ "world" does not here signify a house, a realm, or a space that was created first and then inhabited by beings subsequently fashioned. Such an interpretation would suggest that the Originator, Most High, had given priority to the intellect (⟪ar:عقول⟫ *ʿuqūl*) itself over its very nature. In truth, the spiritual world came into being through and with the emanation of the intellects themselves. The Originator did not create a world as a vessel into which He then poured its inhabitants; rather, He created it through the actualization of the essences of the intellects (⟪ar:صفات عینیت⟫ *ṣifāt ʿayniyya*). The spiritual world is constituted by the ⟪ar:الہیت⟫ divine nature of these beings themselves.

A simple analogy may clarify: when we speak of *dawat-e-sharīf* (a noble gathering or banquet), we do not mean merely a room in which people sit to feast. Rather, ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ we mean the entire system of courtesy, fellowship, and shared purpose that binds those assembled. The spiritual world is similarly not a locale; it is a unified order to which all its beings are intrinsically connected. It is not above or below, not external or circumscribed; rather, it is all-encompassing and integral. We exist within it even as we participate in its structure.

### The Final and Irrevocable Creation

The Originator, Most High, will not create again after this first creation. To do so would introduce temporal succession and thereby injustice: those brought into being in an earlier act would claim superiority over those created subsequently, and this would constitute an affront to divine justice. God is far removed from such conduct. The spiritual world stands as the ultimate and eternal emanation.

### The True Paradise

Paradise is not singular but manifold. Each elevated degree constitutes a paradise for those below it; each lower rank constitutes a worldly state (⟪ar:'دنیا'⟫ *dunyā*) for those above it. Regarding the true and ultimate paradise, the Prophet—peace and blessings upon him—declared: "Paradise is that which no eye has beheld, no ear has heard, and upon which no human heart has ever pondered." This description points beyond all sensory experience and rational conception.

The true paradise (⟪ar:'عالم روحانی'⟫ *al-ʿālam al-rūḥānī*) is a realm in which there exists only unalloyed joy and felicity, untouched by even a whisper of sorrow.

## The Seven Names of the Spiritual World

⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ The Originator, Most High, brought the spiritual world into existence ⟪ar:دفعتاً واحدۃ (⟫ in a single, instantaneous act. ⟪ar:)⟫ This realm is known by seven names:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| World of Existence | World of Command | World of Knowledge | The Spiritual World | World of Origination | World of Subtlety | World of Purity |

Each name illuminates a different facet of this singular reality:

**World of Existence** — The Originator, Most High, brought all beings forth from non-existence into the state of existence and primary perfection (kamāl awwal). All these beings constituted the first degree of actualization. Yet through their bearing witness to the unity of the Lord of all beings (tawhīd Rabb al-ʿālamīn), they ascended to secondary perfection (kamāl thānī), thereby attaining a true and enduring mode of existence.

**World of Command** — This realm is called by this name because within it, the divine command manifested itself through the word "Be!" (kun). The emanation unfolded through divine decree.

**World of Knowledge** — The first intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal), through the light of the divine word, received perfect knowledge from the Creator. All subsequent beings were illuminated through this primordial knowing.

**The Spiritual World** — All beings who achieved primary perfection in the Creator's emanation came to be called "souls" (nufūs). This term reflects the fact that they possess eternal life through their bearing witness to unity (tawhīd). For this reason, the entire realm is denominated the spiritual world.

**World of Origination** — In reference to the first intellect as the primary emanator (al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal), this realm is called the world of origination, for all beings stem from that primordial source.

**World of Subtlety** — It is called by this name because there is no density or corporeality of any kind within it; all forms are luminous and refined.

**World of Purity** — It is likewise known as the world of purity because it is purified of all contamination, all impurity, and all blemish.[^cite-6]

## Primary Perfection

### The State of Primary Perfection

The Creator brought forth all ⟪ar:'ذوات'⟫ essences from ⟪ar:ہست⟫ being itself, transmuting them out of ⟪ar:نیست⟫ nothingness, ⟪ar:دفعتا واحدۃ⟫ in a single, simultaneous act, into the state of ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ primary perfection, and according to the form of Alif (⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫). This ⟪ar:'کمال'⟫ perfection was itself the manifestation of the Creator's grace and mercy. For it is inscribed in His very nature that ⟪ar-quote:لا یکون من الکریم الا الکرم⟫ from the Generous One nothing but generosity can proceed. All these creations were brought into being by the Almighty in a single modality (⟪ar:حیثیت⟫ *ḥayṯiyya*), according to a single quiddity (⟪ar:لمیت⟫ *lammiyya*), with a single quantity (⟪ar:کمیت⟫ *kammiyya*), and according to a single essence (⟪ar:ماہیت⟫ *māhiyya*). There was not the slightest difference among them.

This ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ primary perfection constituted the first provision (⟪ar:مواد أول⟫ *mawād awwal*) that all ⟪ar:مبدعات⟫ creations received. ⟪ar:' کمال '⟫ This perfection consisted of three jewels (*jawāhir*):

| **Life** | **Power** | **Knowledge** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| *Ḥayāt* (eternal existence) | *Qudra* (absolute capacity) | *ʿIlm* (comprehensive knowing) |

**Life (ḥayāt)** — The first essence was eternal life, an enduring existence that knows no termination. No matter how much these beings might will their own destruction, they cannot extinguish the very principle of life that constitutes their being. All their capacities and potentialities were rooted in this eternal vitality.

**Power (qudra)** — The second essence was absolute and unlimited power. These beings were endowed with every capability, and there was no impediment to their execution. They were granted complete authority to act or refrain from acting as they chose. They possessed absolute freedom, and there was no coercion upon them whatsoever.

**Knowledge (ʿilm)** — The third essence was comprehensive knowledge of the whole. These beings received full knowledge of all the powers, capacities, and abilities that resided within their eternal life. Had they been denied knowledge of these very capacities, how could they have exercised them?[^cite-7]

### The Teaching of a Delegate

⟪ar:حضرت المنصوب ضیاء علی صاحب⟫ It was taught that the Creator brought all beings into existence ⟪ar:'⟫ free ⟪ar:'⟫ from constraint and ⟪ar:'⟫ independent ⟪ar:'⟫ of all external determination, endowing them with every perfection through the bestowal of knowledge, power, and life. We ourselves, through our own failings and deficiencies, have forged the chains of servitude and dependence. Today we rely upon our parents for birth, upon teachers for learning, and upon employment for sustenance—all consequences of our own impoverishment of spirit and denial of our original station.

### The Three Gems as Opposites to Life, Power, and Knowledge

These three jewels stand as the supreme goods that encompass all ⟪ar:خصال محمودہ⟫ praiseworthy qualities. Every virtue, every excellence, every good and refinement can be traced to these three. The first and foundational essence is ⟪ar:حیات⟫ life itself, which is both the origin and the source from which all action proceeds. If these beings were not ⟪ar:حی⟫ alive, none of the other attributes could subsist: they would not be ⟪ar:عاقل⟫ intelligent, they would not ⟪ar:باقی⟫ endure, they would not be ⟪ar:باقی⟫ permanent, they would not be ⟪ar:عالم⟫ knowing, they would not be ⟪ar:عاقل⟫ rational, they would not be ⟪ar:عالم⟫ aware, they would not be ⟪ar:کامل⟫ perfect, they would not be ⟪ar:قادر⟫ powerful. If they were not ⟪ar:تام⟫ complete, if they were not ⟪ar:کامل⟫ perfected, if they were not ⟪ar:واحد⟫ unified, they would not be ⟪ar:تام⟫ whole. If they did not ⟪ar:موجود⟫ exist, none of these attributes could manifest. And if they did not exist ⟪ar:أِبدا⟫ eternally, their existence would be nullified.

*The Hierarchy of Divine Attributes in Primary Perfection*

⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ This demonstrates that within the essence of ⟪ar:حیات⟫ life resides the true and indispensable gem. The word ⟪ar:الہ⟫ "God" itself is the negation of whatever contradicts this life. By its removal, every attribute becomes void. Even if all other attributes were withdrawn while life alone remained, those other attributes would persist through their connection to life. Similarly, ⟪ar:حروف معجم⟫ among the letters of the alphabet, the letter ⟪ar:' الف'⟫ Alif stands as the original and foundational. The survival and subsistence of all other letters depend upon it. By altering the stroke and curvature of Alif, all the other letters of the alphabet arise.

### The Qualities of Primary Perfection

Two distinctive qualities characterized all beings in this state:

**First Quality: Absolute Equality** — In the state of primary perfection, all beings stood in perfect equality. No being was elevated above another; no being occupied a lower station. Each possessed the same measure of perfection as every other. This equality flowed from divine justice. In this state, there was no hierarchy, no ordering of ranks, no gradation whatsoever. The first intellect and all other spirits inhabited the same degree of spiritual station.

**Second Quality: Potential, Not Actualized** — This perfection was bestowed upon all beings in a potential state (*bi-al-quwwa*), not in an actualized state (*bi-al-fiʿl*). That is, the three jewels—life, power, and knowledge—were present in each being as capacities, but the actualization of these capacities was entrusted to the beings themselves. In this state of perfection, all beings were innocent of wrongdoing, yet they remained capable of error. They had not yet been sealed by divine preservation from deviation.

Consider the analogy: The Originator, Most High, brings every child into the world endowed with full potential (⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ *bi-al-quwwa*). But the development of that potential has been placed in the hands of the child itself. A child may cultivate its talents and become a physician or engineer; or it may neglect them and remain an ordinary tiller of the soil. Similarly, the seed contains within itself the capacity to become a mighty tree. Yet this capacity remains dormant (⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫) until the seed is sown in fertile earth and nurtured with water and sunlight. Only then does it actualize into ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ *al-fiʿl* (the actual state).

### The Significance of Simultaneous Creation

The fact that all beings were created together in the identical state testifies to ⟪ar:غیب الغیوب⟫ the hidden mystery of mysteries—divine justice. In this way, every being received an equal opportunity to strive in the race toward greater perfection. In the state of primary perfection, no single ⟪ar:ذات⟫ essence was superior to another. The Qur'an itself echoes this truth:

> ⟪quran 4:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَفَلَا يَتَدَبَّرُونَ ٱلْقُرْآنَ ۚ وَلَوْ كَانَ مِنْ عِندِ غَيْرِ ٱللَّهِ لَوَجَدُوا۟ فِيهِ ٱخْتِلَفًۭا كَثِيرًۭا ‎⟫

Had the Qur'an—and by extension, creation itself—proceeded from any source other than God, inconsistency and contradiction would abound. But because it flows from divine wisdom, perfect harmony and unity pervade it. Here ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ the spiritual world, like ⟪ar:قرآن⟫ the Qur'an, manifests the unified order of divine justice: ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ all beings are gathered together in perfect consonance, just as the Qur'an gathers all chapters into seamless coherence.

### The Manifestation of Powers

Even in their potential state, the powers of these beings were magnificent. The Summoners (duʿāt) have spoken of their capacities in these terms: ⟪ar-quote:نَظَرً کُلُّ بَصَرً کُلُّ سَمعً کُلُّ لَمسً کُلُّ⟫ "He was the entire eye that beholds all, the entire ear that hears all, the entire sense of touch that perceives all." There stood nothing between these beings and ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Originator, Most High, save for the veil of ⟪ar:توحید⟫ bearing witness to unity. All possessed the potential to advance and ascend. What was required of them was only the exercise of their authority to perform the work for which they were created. The very presence of their jewels in potential form was itself a sign that they must do something (⟪ar:'فعل'⟫ *fiʿl*) that would transform these potential gems into actualized realities. Had the Creator intended otherwise, He would have created all beings in an already-actualized state.

The evidence of these beings' powers lies in our own selves—that despicable fragment of the spiritual world. Our souls are imprisoned within our bodies, yet our thoughts transcend all bonds of time and space. In an instant, they traverse from the present moment into the distant past and far future. In the blink of an eye, they shed the constraints of this dense world and journey through the heavens and the starry spheres. Despite the vast expanse they traverse, our thoughts encompass all in their sweep. If we, who are but the most insignificant particle of ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ the spiritual world, possess such swiftness of intellect and such freedom from material constraint, what then must be the powers of those beings in the spiritual realm—beings who neither know the confinement of time and space, nor labor under any physical constraint whatsoever? We cannot even fathom the true forces of that realm of subtlety when we ourselves are bound within this realm of density.

## Secondary Perfection

### The Imperative to Actualize the Potential

It was necessary that these beings exercise the jewels they possessed—their eternal life, their absolute power, and their comprehensive knowledge—in order to perform deeds that would transform these potential capacities (⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ *bi-al-quwwa*) into actualized realities (⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ *bi-al-fiʿl*), thereby ascending from the state of primary perfection (⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ *kamāl awwal*) into that of secondary perfection (⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ *kamāl thānī*). This is the purpose for which the Originator, Most High, created them, as the Qur'an describes:

> ⟪quran 51:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا خَلَقْتُ ٱلْجِنَّ وَٱلْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ ‎⟫

The true purpose of creation is ⟪ar:عبودیت⟫ servitude—the conscious acknowledgment of dependence upon the divine—and ⟪ar:عبادت⟫ worship, the outward expression of that inner recognition. Every genuine act of worship possesses a soul, and that soul is ⟪ar:'توحید رب'⟫ bearing witness to the unity of the Lord. It is incumbent upon every ⟪ar:'عبد'⟫ servant to come to know himself and thereby recognize his ⟪ar:'معبود'⟫ Lord and Master. ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ The Originator, Most High, was in no wise in need of these creatures or their worship. Rather, it was necessary for those brought into being to reflect upon themselves and wonder: Why do these three jewels exist within me? Whence did they come? And having recognized these blessings, to acknowledge their Benefactor.

It was necessary for these beings to awaken the powers that lay dormant within them (⟪ar:کمال اول⟫), slumbering in potential form, so that the true purpose of these jewels could be fulfilled. Had the Creator willed, He would have created these jewels already actualized from the beginning. But then how could there be precedence—the ascending of one being above another? How could any distinction be established between former and latter? The real purpose of primary perfection was for each ⟪ar:'عبد'⟫ servant to come to know his ⟪ar:معبود⟫ Lord and, through bearing witness to unity (⟪ar:توحید⟫), to advance toward secondary perfection (⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫), thereby receiving a reward from his Creator. The fault lay not with the Creator if they did not receive this perfection, or if they delayed in its acquisition. Neither substance nor impediment stood in the way of their achieving unity. Thus there was no obstacle or barrier to the attainment of ⟪ar:'فعل'⟫ action.

### The Acquisition of Holy Material

When these beings employed themselves in bearing witness to divine unity (⟪ar:کمال اول⟫), they received from the Originator, Most High, a secondary provision (⟪ar:مواد ثانی⟫)—also called the Holy Material (⟪ar:مواد قدسی (علم جاری و نور ساری)⟫ *mawād qudsiyya*, the flowing knowledge and flowing light). Through this holy substance, their jewels—which had been present as capacities—became actualized. The potential (⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫) became the actual (⟪ar:بالفعل⟫), and they emerged from the state of primary perfection into ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ secondary perfection. The first provision was their initial grace. When these beings properly utilized it, they received what was their true reward: the second provision (⟪ar:مواد ثانی (مواد قدسی)⟫). This state of ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ actualization—wherein the form becomes real—is the designation of ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ secondary perfection.

### Two Characteristics of Secondary Perfection

There emerged two distinctive marks of secondary perfection (⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫):

**First Characteristic: Hierarchy and Order** — In the state of secondary perfection, hierarchy became established among all beings, and the rank-ordering of the highest and lowest limits came into being. No two beings now remained equal. Each had ascended according to the measure of their striving and the quality of their response to divine grace.

**Second Characteristic: Divine Preservation and Infallibility** — In this state of perfection, all beings became innocent of error, and the capacity to transgress was forever sealed from them. This occurred because they became united with the Holy Material (⟪ar:مواد قدسی⟫).

The intellects that have achieved this secondary perfection are in continuous increase. Their light perpetually grows; their effusion and abundance never cease. ⟪ar:(علم قدرت اور حیات)⟫ The knowledge and power, the life-force itself, flows ever stronger within them. All that remains for them is to continue their onward journey, to grow eternally, for there is no limit to the blessings and the bounty of ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Originator, Most High.

## Is the Preservation in Secondary Perfection Compelled?

The innocence and infallibility that characterizes secondary perfection is not compulsory in nature; rather, it is a matter of choice and natural inclination. The Originator, Most High, does not compel any being toward error or away from it. Consider the analogy: A human being possesses the capacity to consume filth and refuse. Should a person choose to do so, nothing prevents them from the act itself. Yet our nature—our intellect, our sense of dignity—recoils in disgust and abhors such degradation. We recognize the disorder and corruption that such an act would entail, and our souls, by their very nobility, refuse to permit such defilement to enter our bodies.

Similarly, the intellects (⟪ar:عقول⟫) that have attained secondary perfection have grown so great, so elevated, so refined in their state of perfection, that they find it inconceivable to commit any error or transgression. They possess not the slightest inclination toward what is unseemly. Their knowledge, their power, and the very essence of their nature—these have reached such perfection that they can never be drawn toward error. ⟪ar:عصمت کلی⟫ Perfect preservation is not a shackle imposed from without; it is the spontaneous outflowing of their perfected nature.

Perfection is not subject to compulsion, but ⟪ar:عصمت⟫ preservation is optional—that is, it flows not from external constraint, but from the intrinsic state they have attained. Their knowledge, their life, and the essence of their power have ripened to such a degree that they can never lean toward wrongdoing.

An anecdote illustrates this truth. A group of theologians once posed a question to a gathering of believers: "You say that God is all-powerful, yet you claim He cannot commit injustice. Are you not thereby limiting His power? If God can do everything, then surely, if He wished, He could also commit an injustice—for He is ⟪ar:علی کل شئی قدیر⟫ omnipotent over all things. Why do you diminish His might by denying Him this?" The believers responded: "God has given you the capacity to eat and drink. Can you not choose to consume any food?" The theologians replied: "Of course we can!" The believers then challenged them: "Then eat the filth and refuse that lies upon the road before you!" The theologians recoiled in horror, unable to do so—not because of any external restraint, but because their very nature forbade it.

So too, beings in ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ secondary perfection, despite possessing all powers and all capacities, cannot be inclined toward any transgression. Their state of perfection through ⟪ar:مواد قدسی⟫ the holy material has transformed their very being. Their ⟪ar:نفوس⟫ souls—their knowledge, their power, their life-essence—have become so luminous that they can never be drawn toward anything unseemly, just as no human soul, however debased, can ever bring itself to consume filth willingly.

## The Distinction Between Primary and Secondary Perfection

### The Two States

⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ The Originator, Most High, brought all beings into existence ⟪ar:دفعتا واحدۃ⟫ in a single, simultaneous instant, creating them from non-existence into existence and establishing them ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ in the state of primary perfection. This ⟪ar:'کمال'⟫ perfection consisted of the three jewels: knowledge, power, and eternal life. When the intellects ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ performed both ⟪ar:تنزیہ⟫ the transcendence of the divine from all limitation and ⟪ar:توحید ⟪ar:تجرید⟫ the stripping away of all attributes save the divine unity, they were granted by ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Originator, Most High, the ⟪ar:نور کلمہ لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ light of the testimony "there is no god but God"—what is otherwise called ⟪ar:علم جاری⟫ flowing knowledge and ⟪ar:نور ساری⟫ flowing light from the holy material (⟪ar:مواد قدسی⟫). Through this knowledge, the intellects transformed what was potential (⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫) into what was actual (⟪ar:بالفعل⟫), and this state of realization is called ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ secondary perfection. In this station, they attained to such infallibility that error and transgression became impossible for them.

### The Transformation of Being

After achieving this secondary perfection, the divine word ⟪ar:اللہ⟫ was applied to them because of the majesty and nobility of these beings. All human intellects remain bewildered concerning the lowest and least of the attributes of these exalted beings; they cannot comprehend them fully, and it is therefore impossible even to conjecture regarding their higher attributes. When ⟪ar:مُبدَعات⟫ creatures are so incapable of fathoming even the lowest qualities of these ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ exalted spirits, how is it conceivable that they could come to know ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ the very ⟪ar:مبدِع تعالی⟫ Creator of these beings? To speak of the Creator in such terms is impossible.

To convey the disparity through humble analogy: the difference between the state of primary perfection and that of secondary perfection is as vast as the difference between a seed and a full-grown tree, or between a drop of sperm and a fully developed human being. ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ What the Creator brings into being in the state of primary perfection is the ⟪ar:' ذات'⟫ essence itself. ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ What these beings themselves actualize through striving is the ⟪ar:'صورت'⟫ form.

### The Teaching of a Delegate

⟪ar:حضرت المنصوب عبد اللہ صاحب⟫ Describing this transformation with profound subtlety, it is taught that the form which flows from the actualization of ⟪ar:صورت⟫ the creature's own endeavor is greater than the essence which ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Creator brings into being, and which ⟪ar:نفوس⟫ the soul receives directly.

### Comparative Table

| **Primary Perfection** | **Secondary Perfection** |
| --- | --- |
| **Essence** (*dhāt*) | **Form** (*sūrat*) |
| Standing firm (from creation) | Already realized (through actualization) |
| Knowledge, Life, Power (in potential) | Flowing Knowledge and Flowing Light (in actuality) |
| All beings equal in rank | Hierarchies established; degrees differentiated |
| Innocent yet capable of error | Innocent and incapable of error |
| Bearing witness: "There is no god" (*lā ilāh*) | Bearing witness: "There is no god but He" (*lā ilāh illā hu*) |

### The Symbolism of Alif and Circle

The shape of the letter Alif (⟪ar:کمال اول⟫) stands as the emblem of primary perfection, while the circle (⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫) represents secondary perfection. Alif, being the most perfect of all letters, was why ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Originator, Most High, gave birth to all beings ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫ in the form of Alif. Yet Alif possesses two extremities; neither endpoint connects to anything beyond itself. That is, ⟪ar:کمال اول⟫ these beings in primary perfection had no direct spiritual connection ⟪ar:من فوق⟫ with that which stood above them.

But the circle has no discontinuity; every point connects seamlessly to the next. Thus, in secondary perfection, each degree of being becomes united with the degree above it. The letter Alif, being distinct and individual, denotes the ⟪ar:ذات⟫ essence—a singular and separate entity. The circle, having no isolated points, signifies the ⟪ar:صورت⟫ form—that which achieves unity not through isolation, but through mutual interconnection and reinforcement. For this reason, the form of the lower degree merges with the form of the upper limit, and the two become one.

## The Jewels of the Physical World

### The Bestowal of the Same Three Essences

When the intellects descended from the spiritual world into the physical realm and took on human form, the Originator, Most High, bestowed upon them the same three jewels that they possessed in the spiritual world: knowledge, power, and eternal life. The Qur'an testifies to this descent and this bestowal:

> ⟪quran 51:20-21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَفِى ٱلْأَرْضِ آيَتٌۭ لِّلْمُوقِنِينَ ‎ ‏وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ ۚ أَفَلَا تُبْصِرُونَ ‎⟫

Signs (⟪ar:آيات⟫) of divine truth exist within the earth for those of certain faith; and signs exist within your own souls. Will you not see?

The physical world presents us with the same opportunity that was afforded to the denizens of the spiritual realm. Now, in our human existence, in this dense and corporeal form, we must not repeat the errors of our primordial state. We must make proper use of our knowledge, power, and life in order to fashion a noble form—the form of secondary perfection—that we may return to our station and rise even higher.
