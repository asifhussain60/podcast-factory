# Formation of the Spiritual World

## Divinity and Creation Distinguished

There exist two fundamentally distinct modes of origination: the divine and the creaturely. The table below sets forth their essential difference:

| Divinity | Creation |
| --- | --- |
| Bringing forth from non-being into being without precedent | Transformation of existing substance into new form—as wood becomes a chair |
| The Originator, Most High (Mubdiʿ Taʿālā) | The wise arranger of secondary causes |

### The Proof of Divine Origination

The proof of the Creator's divinity is established in the Qur'anic witness:

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah bears witness that La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), and the angels, and those having knowledge (also give this witness); (He is always) maintaining His creation in Justice. La ilah illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.*

This verse presents a profound paradox: Allah testifies to His own divinity. Yet testimony ordinarily concerns what another attests about someone else. The resolution lies in understanding that the verse speaks not of Allah's self-testimony—for He transcends such need—but of the testimony borne by the highest created order, the al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect), which witnesses to the Creator's unity and stands as the supreme emanation of divine grace.[^cite-1] This First Intellect, being the noblest of all created beings, speaks on behalf of all creation and of itself the truth concerning the Originator, Most High (Mubdiʿ Taʿālā).

The Imam Ḥamīd al-Dīn al-Kirmānī, may Allah sanctify his secret, addresses this matter with consummate clarity in his work *Raḥat al-ʿAql* (The Repose of the Intellect). He marshals all manner of rational objection to creation and demonstrates systematically that none of them can withstand scrutiny, until at last he establishes a truth as radiant as the sun: the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High) created the entirety of existence solely as an overflowing of grace, forgiveness, and reward.[^cite-2] For it is the nature of the Creator—the Generous and the All-Powerful (al-Karīm wa-l-Qādir)—to bestow bounty without reason or purpose, and the nature of the Creator—the Mighty and the Great (al-ʿAzīz wa-l-ʿAẓīm)—to give in absolute freedom from need or return.

### The Rationale for Creation

The reason the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High) brought all existence into being is disclosed in the Holy Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 51:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا خَلَقْتُ ٱلْجِنَّ وَٱلْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ ‎⟫
> *And I (Allah) created not the jinns and humans except they should worship Me (Alone).*

The essence of all worship is the tawhīd Rabb al-ʿālamīn (the affirmation of the Lord of all worlds). For the servant (ʿabd) must first know himself, and then know the Worshipped One (al-maʿbūd). Had the servant remained ignorant of his Lord, the very purpose of his creation would be rendered void. Thus the knowledge of Allah emerges as the indispensable foundation upon which all devotion rests.[^cite-3]

## The Creation of the Spiritual World

### The Emanation from Non-Existence

The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High) brought the ʿālam al-rūḥānī (the spiritual world) into existence in a single, timeless instant. He created it in such manner that no prior material substance (hylē ūlā) underlay its coming-to-be, nor did He fashion it from any preexisting thing. The spiritual world was not created upon any foundation, nor was it set in any place. Rather, from absolute non-existence (ʿadam), the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā emanated it all at once (dafʿatan wāḥida) into being (wujūd).[^cite-4]

This spiritual world consisted of luminous forms and spiritual essences (al-ashbāḥ al-nūrāniyya / al-dhawāt / al-mubdaʿāt)—all brought forth in a state of joy and delight, untouched by sorrow or wrath. The very source of their emanation was the wellspring of divine pleasure and generosity. Their number exceeded all counting: so vast that enumeration itself could encompass none of their multitude, nor could a single portion be reckoned.

The Qur'an attests to the immensity of these spiritual hosts:

> ⟪quran 74:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا جَعَلْنَآ أَصْحَبَ ٱلنَّارِ إِلَّا مَلَٓئِكَةًۭ ۙ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا عِدَّتَهُمْ إِلَّا فِتْنَةًۭ لِّلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لِيَسْتَيْقِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَيَزْدَادَ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِيمَنًۭا ۙ وَلَا يَرْتَابَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۙ وَلِيَقُولَ ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِم مَّرَضٌۭ وَٱلْكَفِرُونَ مَاذَآ أَرَادَ ٱللَّهُ بِهَذَا مَثَلًۭا ۚ كَذَلِكَ يُضِلُّ ٱللَّهُ مَن يَشَآءُ وَيَهْدِى مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ جُنُودَ رَبِّكَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ وَمَا هِىَ إِلَّا ذِكْرَىٰ لِلْبَشَرِ ‎⟫
> *And We have set none but angels as guardians of the Fire, and We have fixed their number (19) only as a trial for the disbelievers, in order that the people of the Scripture (Jews and Christians) may arrive at a certainty [that this Quran is the truth as it agrees with their Books i.e. their number (19) is written in the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel)] and the believers may increase in Faith ( as this Quran is the truth) and that no doubts may be left for the people of the Scripture and the believers, and that those in whose hearts is a disease (of hypocrisy) and the disbelievers may say: "What Allah intends by this (curious) example ?" Thus Allah leads astray whom He wills and guides whom He wills. And none can know the hosts of your Lord but He. And this (Hell) is nothing else than a (warning) reminder to mankind.*

The number of these mubdaʿāt (emanated essences) was immeasurable precisely because there is no bound to the grace and gifts of the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High).

### The Unity of Simultaneous Creation

These spiritual beings were not created in sequence across time, but rather all at once (dafʿatan wāḥida). Had the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā created one group first and another group thereafter, injustice would have resulted. The first group would possess precedence (sābiq) over the second, and their superiority would be established. Those created later would necessarily be inferior to those brought forth earlier. Yet the perfect justice of Allah demanded that all beings be created in a single state, at one and the same degree, such that not the slightest difference distinguished one from another. The Qur'an bears witness:

> ⟪quran 67:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ سَبْعَ سَمَوَتٍۢ طِبَاقًۭا ۖ مَّا تَرَىٰ فِى خَلْقِ ٱلرَّحْمَنِ مِن تَفَوُتٍۢ ۖ فَٱرْجِعِ ٱلْبَصَرَ هَلْ تَرَىٰ مِن فُطُورٍۢ ‎⟫
> *Who has created the seven heavens one above another, you can see no fault in the creations of the Most Beneficent. Then look again: "Can you see any rifts?"*

The noble Sayyidunā al-Ḥasan Khān ibn ʿAbd al-Malik, may Allah exalt his station, offers an illuminating analogy: "The emanation of all these beings in a single instant (dafʿatan wāḥida) resembles the rays of the sun flooding the entire world at once, or fire illuminating every corner of a dark house in an instant." In the spiritual world there was no temporal succession—no before (qabla) and after (baʿda)—but only the affairs of mutual interdependence and unity.

### The Form of Alif

The Creator fashioned all these beings in the form of Alif (the letter alif), for Alif is the noblest form of the alphabet. Later, when the Creator brought humanity into the physical world, He likewise formed humanity upon the form of Qamat al-Alif (the standing posture of the alif)—that eternal prototype echoing the original creation.

### The Spiritual World Is Not a Place

The term ʿālam al-rūḥānī (the spiritual world) creates a misleading impression, as though it designated a house, dwelling, or location into which creatures were placed after their creation. Were this so, it would imply that the Creator gave precedence to the intellect, establishing it prior to the spiritual realm itself. In truth, the spiritual world came into existence through the very emanation of the intellect. The Creator did not establish a separate domain and then fill it; rather, the spiritual world is the totality constituted by the emergence of the ʿuqūl (intellects). A helpful analogy: the daʿwa al-sharīf (the noble summons) is not a room where the learned sit and feast, but rather a living system to which we are continually bound. It is neither above nor below us, but rather present on all sides—we exist in its very midst, enfolded within its embrace.[^cite-5]

### No Further Emanation

The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā has expressed Himself through this first creation, His self-disclosure realized in the spiritual world's emanation. He will not create anew, for were He to do so, those emanated first would possess precedence over those emanated later, and this would constitute an injustice toward the latter. Allah is far removed from any such injustice.

### Paradise: The True Spiritual Realm

Paradise is not a singular place but rather a multiplicity of stations. Every elevated rank serves as paradise for those below it, and every lower station serves as the physical world for those above. Regarding Paradise, the Prophet—may Allah's peace and blessings be upon him—said: "Paradise is that which no eye has beheld, no ear has heard, and no human heart has conceived." The true paradise (al-jinnat al-ḥaqīqa) is the spiritual world, wherein dwells only joy and gladness, untouched by grief or suffering.

## The Seven Names of the Spiritual World

The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā created the spiritual world in a single timeless instant (dafʿatan wāḥida). This realm bears seven designations, each illuminating a different facet of its reality:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| World of Existence | World of Command | World of Knowledge | The Spiritual World | World of Emanation | World of Subtlety | World of Purity |

#### Exposition of the Seven Names

| **World of Existence** | The Creator brought all beings from non-existence into a state of existence and perfection. These beings were the first to attain actuality. Through the tawhīd (affirmation of unity) of the Creator of all worlds, they acquired the secondary perfection, which constituted their true and permanent existence. |
| --- | --- |
| **World of Command** | This realm is so called because the creative command of the Creator appeared herein through the divine utterance "Kun" (Be). |
| **World of Knowledge** | It bears this name because the knowledge flowing from the Creator—transmitted through the radiance of the divine Word—was first bestowed upon the al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect). |
| **The Spiritual World** | All beings who attained perfection through the Creator's emanation are called "souls" (nufūs). Because they possess eternal life through tawhīd (affirmation of unity), this realm is designated the spiritual world. |
| **World of Emanation** | Referring to the al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect), it is called the world of emanation (ʿālam al-ibdāʿ), for therein the first creative act transpired. |
| **World of Subtlety** | It is named the world of subtlety (ʿālam al-ṭifāf) because there exists within it no density of any kind—no material weight, no physical constraint. |
| **World of Purity** | It is also known as the world of purity (ʿālam al-ṣafāʾ) because it is utterly cleansed from all defilement and corruption. |

## Primary Perfection (Kamāl Awwal)

### The Emanation in Primary Perfection

The Creator brought into being all existences (al-dhawāt) from non-being into existence, all at once (dafʿatan wāḥida), in the state of kamāl awwal (primary perfection), in the form of Qamat al-Alif. This bestowal of primary perfection was an expression of the grace and mercy of the Creator, for it is in the very nature of the Generous One (al-Karīm) to show mercy alone. All these emanated essences (al-mubdaʿāt) were created in a single condition, on one plane (ḥaythiyyat), with a single character (lamiyyat), a single quantity (kammiyyat), and a single essence (māhiyyat)—not the slightest difference separated them. This kamāl awwal was the first material (al-mawād al-ūlā) they received.

These emanated beings consisted of three essential jewels:

| **Essential Gem** | **Nature** |
| --- | --- |
| **Life** (Eternal Duration) | The first essence was eternal life—an everlasting vitality that no being, however they might wish, could ever destroy or diminish. Within this life lay hidden all the capacities and potentialities of these beings. |
| **Power** (Absolute Capability) | The second essence was absolute power and all-encompassing might. They were endowed with every capability, and no impediment stood in the way of their exercise. They possessed complete authority to act or refrain from acting, granted perfect freedom with no coercion whatsoever. |
| **Knowledge** (Universal Knowing) | The third essence was knowledge of the whole—a comprehensive knowing of all the powers, abilities, and capacities inherent in their life. Without this knowledge of what life contained, how could these beings employ their gifts? |

#### The Three Jewels Illustrated

Sayyidunā al-Mansūb, may Allah elevate his station, declared: "The Creator fashioned us free and autonomous, endowed with all perfections through the bestowal of knowledge, power, and life. We ourselves created bondage, dependence, and servitude. Today we depend upon parents for our birth, upon teachers for our learning, upon employment for our sustenance—all because of the limitations and defects we have imposed upon ourselves."[^cite-6]

#### The Letters and the Essence

These three jewels are the opposites of life, power, and knowledge, expressed in the sacred letters: alif (ا), lām (ل), and hāʾ (ه). Since the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā is Himself "Allah" (al-Ilāh), He endowed all these beings with three essences corresponding to these three letters:

| **Alif (ا)** | **Lām (ل)** | **Hāʾ (ه)** | **Emanated Perfection** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Life | Power | Knowledge | Primary Perfection |

The letters alif, lām, and hāʾ, in their latent state (bi-l-quwwa), possess the quality of being letters of the alphabet. Originally they exist as mute, formless elements. Yet when these three letters are arranged in proper sequence, each positioned in its rightful place, they produce the four meaningful words composing the declaration *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* (There is no god save Allah), which represents their actualized state (bi-l-fiʿl).[^cite-7] That is, the word *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* is the origination of these three letters—yet until they escape their latent condition (bi-l-quwwa) and achieve actuality (bi-l-fiʿl), expressing themselves in meaningful utterance, their true purpose remains unfulfilled.

Similarly, although these beings were created in possession of the jewels of life, power, and knowledge in a latent state (bi-l-quwwa), their original purpose was to actualize these jewels (bi-l-fiʿl) through their own exercise and endeavor.

#### The Threefold Manifestation

| **Life** | **Power** | **Knowledge** | **Original Title** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Generative Soul (al-Nāmiyya) | Sentient Soul (al-Ḥassāsa) | Rational Soul (al-Nāṭiqa) | The Spoken Affirmation |

The sacred declaration *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* represents what we received in primary perfection (kamāl awwal), yet we failed to comprehend it—that is, we could not actualize these jewels into the living utterance of *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* that was their appointed purpose. Because of this failure to realize our jewels, we were cast down in rank. Then the Creator brought us forth into this physical world and granted us these three essences anew, in the form of the vegetative soul (al-nufus al-nāmiyya), the animal soul (al-nufus al-ḥassāsa), and the rational soul (al-nufus al-nāṭiqa). Now our endeavor in this world is to acquire knowledge of the Lord through understanding and righteous action, to advance through the ranks, and to attain again the state of primary perfection (kamāl awwal). When we achieve this station again, we shall begin our ascent toward secondary perfection (kamāl thānī)—the state of *Illā Allāh* (save Allah alone).

### Life: The Primary Jewel

These three jewels encompass all praiseworthy qualities (al-khiṣāl al-maḥmūda). Every excellence, happiness, virtue, or nobility—when considered closely—resolves into one of these three essences. Yet the first and foremost of these three is the essence of life (al-ḥayāt), for it is the origin from which all action springs. If these beings were not alive (ḥayy), they would not be rational (ʿāqil); if they were not rational, they would not be knowing (ʿālim); if they were not knowing, they would not be perfect (kāmil); if they were not perfect, they would not be powerful (qādir); if they were not powerful, they would not endure (bāqī); if they were not enduring, they would not exist (mawjūd); if they were not existent, they would not be (abadan).

This demonstrates that within the essence of life (al-ḥayāt) in primary perfection, there resides the true fundamental essence—the very opposite of the letter alif that stands at its origin. Should life be removed, every other attribute becomes void. Even if all other attributes were stripped away and life alone remained, those attributes would yet persist through their connection to it. Similarly, the letter alif (ا) remains the original, and the survival and existence of all other letters depends upon it. By altering the form of the line composing alif, all the remaining letters of the alphabet come into being.

### The Two Qualities of Primary Perfection

The beings in kamāl awwal (primary perfection) possessed two remarkable qualities:

| **Quality 1: Absolute Equality** | In their state of primary perfection, all beings were entirely equal. No being stood higher or lower than another. Each received the same measure of perfection as every other—a distribution rooted in the justice of the Almighty. At this stage, there existed neither hierarchy nor order, neither gradation nor rank. In this condition, both the al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect) and even Iblīs (the Devil) possessed the same spiritual station. |
| --- | --- |
| **Quality 2: Potential, Not Actualized** | This perfection was bestowed upon all beings in a latent, potential state (bi-l-quwwa), not in an actualized state (bi-l-fiʿl). That is, the three jewels of life, power, and knowledge existed within them, yet they possessed the power to bring these gifts to life. In this state, all beings were innocent of wrongdoing and incapable of error—yet this innocence was conditional, not absolute, for they possessed the capacity to stumble.[^cite-8] |

An analogy clarifies this condition: Allah the Almighty created all children in this world endowed with full potential. Yet the power to develop these talents rests with the children themselves. They may employ their gifts to become physicians or engineers, or they may remain ordinary farmers through neglecting them. Similarly, the seed: nature has placed within every seed the potential (bi-l-quwwa) to become a mighty tree. Yet this cannot become actuality (bi-l-fiʿl) until the seed is sown in soil and nurtured with water.

#### The Positive and Negative Forms

| **Aspect** | **Alif (ا)** | **Lām (ل)** | **Hāʾ (ه)** | **Community** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Positive Form** | Life | Knowledge | Power | Those Who Obey (Amat al-Barr) |
| **Negative Form** | Arrogance | Envy | Jealousy | Those Who Transgress (Amat al-Masī) |

The very fact that all these beings were created together in the same state bears witness to divine justice (ʿadl). In this way, each received an equal opportunity in the spiritual race. In primary perfection, no being surpassed another. The Qur'an affirms this truth:

> ⟪quran 4:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَفَلَا يَتَدَبَّرُونَ ٱلْقُرْآنَ ۚ وَلَوْ كَانَ مِنْ عِندِ غَيْرِ ٱللَّهِ لَوَجَدُوا۟ فِيهِ ٱخْتِلَفًۭا كَثِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *Do they not then consider the Quran carefully? Had it been from other than Allah, they would surely have found therein much contradictions.*

That is, had the spiritual world (ʿālam al-rūḥānī) been created by a being other than Allah, or had the Qur'ān emanated from a source other than the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā, one would discover vast inconsistencies within it. Yet the perfect unity and coherence testify to its divine origin.

### The Capacities Within

Even in their state of primary perfection, these beings possessed magnificent powers. The Summoners (al-duʿāt) speak of their capacities thus: "He was the whole vision of the whole, the whole hearing of the whole—the whole of sensation in its entirety."[^cite-9] Nothing stood between them and the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā save the tawhīd (affirmation of divine unity). All possessed the potential to advance. What remained was for them to exercise the authority granted to them and to perform the action for which they were created. The very bestowal of these jewels in a latent state was itself an indication that they must perform a deed (fiʿl) that would actualize these gems. Had this not been so, the Creator would have emanated all beings in an already-actualized state.

The evidence of these beings' immense capacity is apparent even in ourselves—a despicable fragment of the spiritual realm. Though our souls are imprisoned within our bodies, our thoughts transcend the constraints of time and space. In a single instant, they journey from the present to the past and future; in the twinkling of an eye, they abandon this world and travel to the heavens and constellations. Despite the burden of flesh, our thoughts encompass them. If we, possessing only this meager portion of the spiritual world, enjoy such rapidity of thought, what then must be the powers of those luminous beings who were formed whole and unencumbered, bearing neither the confinement of time and space nor any physical bondage whatsoever? We cannot even imagine the forces of this subtle realm while dwelling in this realm of density!

## Secondary Perfection (Kamāl Thānī)

### The Imperative of Actualization

It was necessary for these beings to employ the jewels of life, power, and knowledge in deed, thereby transforming what existed in latent form (bi-l-quwwa) into actuality (bi-l-fiʿl), and to ascend from the state of primary perfection (kamāl awwal) into secondary perfection (kamāl thānī). This alone was the purpose for which the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā created them. The Qur'an describes this purpose thus:

> ⟪quran 51:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا خَلَقْتُ ٱلْجِنَّ وَٱلْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ ‎⟫
> *And I (Allah) created not the jinns and humans except they should worship Me (Alone).*

The true purpose of creation is ʿubūdiyyat (servanthood) or ʿibādat (worship), and the very soul of worship is the tawhīd Rabb (affirmation of the Lord's unity). Every servant (ʿabd) must come to know himself, and then recognize the Worshipped One (al-maʿbūd). The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā was in no need whatsoever of these creatures' servitude. Rather, it was incumbent upon those brought into existence to examine themselves and inquire: Why do these three jewels dwell within us, and whence did they come? Having recognized these blessings, they were obligated to acknowledge their Benefactor and honor His unity.

It was necessary for these beings to awaken the powers that lay dormant in them (bi-l-quwwa), so that the true purpose of these jewels might be realized. Had the Creator willed, He could have emanated these jewels in an already-actualized state (bi-l-fiʿl). Yet then how could precedence be possible? How could the difference between the first and the subsequent be established? The true purpose of kamāl awwal (primary perfection) was that the servant (ʿabd) recognize his Lord (maʿbūd) and move toward tawhīd (affirmation of unity), so that he might receive a reward from his Lord. The attainment of kamāl thānī (secondary perfection) was not a matter of compulsion but of striving. Nor was there any impediment—neither substance nor accident—that could hinder them from achieving tawhīd. Therefore, the acquisition of kamāl thānī depended entirely upon their own action.

### The Reception of Secondary Material

When these beings employed themselves in tawhīd (affirmation of divine unity), they received from the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā the mawād thānī (secondary material)—also called mawād qudsī (the holy material): ʿilm jārin wa-nūr sārī (flowing knowledge and pervasive light). From this secondary material, their jewels sprang forth and were transformed from latent potential (bi-l-quwwa) into actualized reality (bi-l-fiʿl), emerging into the state of kamāl thānī (secondary perfection). The mawād awwal (primary material) had been a grace freely given. When these beings made proper use of it, they received what constituted their true reward: the mawād thānī. In truth, kamāl thānī is the name of the form (al-ṣūra) that results from their acquisition.

### The Two Characteristics of Secondary Perfection

There were two defining characteristics of these beings in kamāl thānī (secondary perfection):

| **Characteristic 1: The Hierarchy of Excellence** | In the state of secondary perfection, a hierarchy of superiority was established among all beings, and an order of upper and lower limits (ḥadd ʿālī / ḥadd dānī) came into being. No two beings remained equal to one another. Each occupied a unique station corresponding to the measure of their achievement and the degree of their tawhīd. |
| --- | --- |
| **Characteristic 2: Infallibility** | In the state of secondary perfection, all beings became innocent (ʿāṣim) and could no longer make mistakes. This transformation occurred through their union with the mawād qudsī (the holy material). |

He, the All-Knowing, testifies that all these intellects who have attained secondary perfection continue to increase in their radiance and flow (ʿilm, qudrat, wa-ḥayāt), and their perfection grows ever greater. Now all that remains is for them to move forward perpetually and continue to expand, for there is no limit to the blessings and grace of the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā.

## Is the Infallibility of Secondary Perfection Compulsory?

Their infallibility is not compulsory but rather voluntary, for Allah does not oppress them. Consider an analogy: we are free to eat refuse if we wish, yet our nature will not tolerate it, because we recognize such disorder and our souls are too noble to place such filth within ourselves. Similarly, these intellects have attained such exalted and lofty stations in their perfection that they are incapable of committing any error or transgression.

Infallibility (ʿiṣma) in the state of secondary perfection is not subject to compulsion (jabar) but rather optional (ikhtiyār). That is, they are not forced to refrain from error; rather, their knowledge, life, and the essence of their power have achieved such perfection through the mawād qudsī (the holy material) that they can never be inclined toward mistake.

#### An Illuminating Dispute

An example of this principle is found in a dispute that arose among some of the obstinate misbelievers (al-ʿilḥiyya) and a group of believers. The misbelievers asked: "You say that Allah can do all things, yet you deny that He can commit injustice. Why do you limit the power of Allah in this way? If Allah can do everything, then surely, if He wished to do wrong, He would be able to commit injustice—for is He not *ʿalā kulli shayʾin qadīr* (capable of all things)?"

The believers answered with a practical demonstration: "Do you have the power to eat and drink whatever you wish?"

They said: "Yes!"

The believers said: "Then eat the filth lying on the road before you!"

The offended parties fell silent, unable to respond. For although they possessed the power to consume refuse, their nature and reason revolted against it. The very perfection of their faculties precluded such an act, not through external compulsion but through the excellence of their own constitution.[^cite-10]

#### The State of Secondary Perfection

The state of kamāl thānī (secondary perfection) is precisely analogous. Despite possessing all powers, these intellects are incapable of being inclined toward error. Since the mubdaʿāt (emanated beings) had not yet been annexed to the mawād qudsī (the holy material) in their state of kamāl awwal (primary perfection), they remained susceptible to mistake. Yet with the attainment of the mawād qudsī, their nufūs (souls)—the essences of their knowledge, power, and life—have become so radiant that they can never be drawn toward any error, just as no sane human being can ever be drawn to consume filth.

## The Distinction Between Primary and Secondary Perfection

### The Transformation Through Unity

The Mubdiʿ Taʿālā created all beings from non-existence into existence, all at once (dafʿatan wāḥida), in the state of kamāl awwal (primary perfection). His creation consisted of the three jewels of knowledge, power, and life. When the intellects engaged in tanzīh (divine transcendence) and tawhīd (affirmation of unity), practicing tajrīd (mystical abstraction and stripping away), they received from the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā the nūr kalima lā ilāha illā Allāh (the light of the utterance "There is no god but Allah") through the mawād qudsī (the holy material)—what is also called ʿilm jārin wa-nūr sārī (flowing knowledge and pervasive light).

Through this knowledge, these intellects transformed from being potentially intellectual (bi-l-quwwa) to being actualized intellect (bi-l-fiʿl), and this state is named kamāl thānī (secondary perfection). At this station, they became entirely innocent, for they could no longer commit error.

### The Divine Title Applied

After attaining secondary perfection, the word "Allah" (al-Ilāh) came to be applied to them because of the nobility and majesty of these personalities. Yet all human intellects remain bewildered before even the lowest of their attributes and cannot comprehend them fully; therefore, how could it be possible even to hazard a conjecture about their higher stations? And when the mubdaʿāt (emanated beings) are so incapable of comprehending even our lowest attributes, how is it possible for us to comprehend them, and how can we possibly come to know the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā, their Creator?

For the sake of our understanding, we can say: there is as much difference between the states of kamāl awwal (primary perfection) and kamāl thānī (secondary perfection) as exists between a seed and a tree, or between sperm and a living young person. What was created in the state of primary perfection is the **dhāt** (the essential substance), while what was acquired in secondary perfection is the **ṣūra** (the form).

#### A Subtle Testimony

Sayyidunā al-Mansūb ʿAbd Allāh, may Allah sanctify his secret, describes this state of affairs with profound subtlety, saying: "The thing created by the Creator (al-ṣūra) is greater than the thing created by the creature (al-nufūs)."[^cite-11] That is, the secondary perfection acquired through tawhīd surpasses even the primary perfection originally emanated—for what is gained through effort and knowledge exceeds what is passively received.

### The Comparison of States

| **Aspect** | **Primary Perfection** | **Secondary Perfection** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Essential Nature** | Dhāt (essential substance) | Ṣūra (form) |
| **Mode of Being** | Standing firm in its own right | Already realized and existing |
| **Content** | Knowledge, Life, Power (latent) | Flowing knowledge and pervasive light |
| **Rank** | Equal in all beings | Hierarchy established |
| **Innocence** | Conditioned and potential | Absolute and infallible |
| **Utterance** | *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* (the negation) | *Illā Allāh* (save Allah alone—the affirmation) |

### Alif and Circle: The Geometric Correspondence

The form of alif (ا) is the form of primary perfection; the form of the circle (◯) is the form of secondary perfection. Alif is the noblest letter of the alphabet, which is why the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā emanated all beings in Qamat al-Alif (the standing form of alif). Yet alif has two extremities, and both its endpoints are isolated—unconnected to anything else. That is, these beings in kamāl awwal (primary perfection) possessed no spiritual connection with what stood above them (min fawq).

The circle, by contrast, has no break or discontinuity—every point is connected to every other point. That is, in kamāl thānī (secondary perfection), the connection of each rank with the rank above it is established. The letter alif refers to the **dhāt** (the individual essence), which is singular and self-standing, while the circle refers to the **ṣūra** (the form), which has no individuality of its own but is constituted through mutual reinforcement and connection. For this reason, the form of the lower limit merges seamlessly with the form of the upper limit, and the two become one—a seamless unity where the boundary becomes indistinguishable from what lies beyond.

## The Jewels of the Physical World

### The Descent Into Flesh

When we departed from the spiritual world and descended into the physical world, taking on the form of human beings, Allah granted us the same three jewels—knowledge, power, and life—as He testifies in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 51:20-21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَفِى ٱلْأَرْضِ آيَتٌۭ لِّلْمُوقِنِينَ ‎ ‏وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ ۚ أَفَلَا تُبْصِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And on the earth are signs for those who have Faith with certainty, And also in your ownselves. Will you not then see?*

There are signs of the divine in the earth for the believer, and within your own souls—will you not perceive them?

### The Imperative of Rectification

Now, in this physical world, we must not repeat the error we committed in the spiritual realm. We must make right use of our knowledge, power, and life—the same three jewels granted to us—to fashion a noble form that reflects the secondary perfection that is our true destiny. Through proper conduct in the world of flesh, through knowledge and righteous action, we may yet ascend toward the state from which we fell, and perhaps advance further still toward stations yet unknown to us in this earthly exile.
