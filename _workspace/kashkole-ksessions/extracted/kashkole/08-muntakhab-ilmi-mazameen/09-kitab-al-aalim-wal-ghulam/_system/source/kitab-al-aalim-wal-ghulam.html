اللہ تعالی قرآن کریم میں ارشاد فرماتا ہے:

سورۃ فصلت   { 41:53 }
‏سَنُرِيهِمْ آيَتِنَا فِى ٱلأٓفَاقِ وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ يَتَبَيَّنَ لَهُمْ أَنَّهُ ٱلْحَقُّ ۗ أَوَلَمْ يَكْفِ بِرَبِّكَ أَنَّهُۥ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ شَهِيدٌ ‎

53 عنقریب ہم اپنی نشانیاں انہیں دنیا میں دکھائیں گے اور خود ان کے نفس میں یہاں تک کہ ان پر واضح ہو جائے گا کہ وہی حق ہے کیا ان کے رب کی یہ بات کافی نہیں کہ وہ ہر چیز کو دیکھ رہا ہے 

اس آیت سے معلوم ہوتا ہے کہ اللہ تعالی نے اپنی نشانیوں کو آفاق اور انفس، یعنی ظاہر (آفاق)  و باطن (انفس) سے واضح کیا ہے۔ اس نے اپنے دین کا پایہ دنیا کے اصولیات پر رکھا ہے۔ جس طرح ایک مریض، طبیب کے ذریعہ سے اپنے مرض کو دور کرتا ہے اور صحت یاب ہوتا ہے، اسی طرح اللہ ایک نفسانی بیمار کے لئے اللہ تعالی نے ایک نفسانی طبیب قائم کیا ہے جو اس کے نفوس کو صحت عطا کرتا ہے۔ 

جسمانی مریض کے لئے لازم ہے کہ وہ اپنے علاج کے لئے کسی ماہر طبیب کی طرف رجوع کرے۔ اس سے اپنے مرض کا کلی طور پر، بغیر کوئی بات چھپائے ذکر کرے اور اس سے علاج کی درخواست کرے۔ جب طبیب اس کے مرض کو دور کرنے کے لئے اسے دوائی بتائے تو اس پر لازم ہے کہ وقت پر اس دوا کو صحیح مقدار میں لیتا رہے۔ اسی طرح نفس کے بیمار کو چاہئے کہ وہ اہل بیت سے استفادہ کرنے والے علماء اور بزرگان دین  کا دامن تھامے جو اس کے شکوک کو مختلف علوم سے دور کرنے کی طاقت رکھتے ہیں۔ ان سے اپنے تمام شکوک کی ہر نوعیت اور قسم کو واضح کر دے کہ آیا وہ ولایت میں شک کرتا ہے یا احکام شریعت میں، یا غیب پر ایمان لانے میں اس کو شک ہے۔  اور جس طرح حکیم مریض کی نبض کو سمجھ کر علاج کرتا ہے، عالم دین کو چاہئے کہ مریض کے کلام سے، جو نبض کے محل میں ہے، اور اس کی طرز ادا اور بیان سے جو قارورۃ کا ممثول ہے، مریض کے مرض کی شناخت کرے اور پھر اس کا علاج کرے۔

طبیب کے لئے یہ ضروری ہے کہ وہ اپنے فن میں ماہر بھی ہو اور مامون (confident) بھی۔ یعنی مریض کو خواہ مخواہ چند پیسوں اور معاشی فائدہ کے لئے غلط مشورہ نہ دے اور غلط علاج  نہ کرے، اور اگر کوئی بات سمجھ میں نہ آتی ہو تو اناپ شناپ بک کر مریض کے مرض میں اضافہ نہ کرے۔ اسی طرح عالم دین پر لازم ہے کہ تھوڑی سی دنیاوی تعریف، یا شہرت کے لئے اپنے نفسانی مریض کے شکوک کو بڑھانے والی باتیں نہ کرے۔ ہر عالم دین کو اپنے علم میں پختہ، مضبوط اور ماہر ہونے کے ساتھ مریض کے ساتھ محبت، شفقت اور نرمی کرنا بھی آنا چاہئے۔

طبیب کا خود صحت مند ہونا لازم ہے۔ وہ خود ایسے مرض میں مبتلا نہ ہو جو مریض کی نبض دیکھنے میں حائل ہو، اور وہ اندھا نہ ہو کہ مریض کے قارورۃ کا معائنہ نہ کر سکے۔ اسی طرح عالم دین کے عقائد پختہ اور صحیح ہونا چاہئے۔ اس کا تصور صحیح اور خیالات صحت مند ہوں۔ خود ایسے شکوک میں مبتلا نہ ہو جو اس کو متحیر اور سرگرداں کر دے بلکہ اس کا دماغ صاف اور اس قدر تیز ہونا چاہئے کہ اپنے مریض کا کلام سنتے ہی سمجھ لے کہ کون سی رگ دکھتی ہے۔ عقیدہ کی خرابی کہاں ہے اور کس کی پیدا کی ہوئی ہے۔ صحت مند طبیب صحیح نباض ہوتا ہے۔ اسی طرح صحیح عالم، حقیقیت شناس ہوتا ہے۔

مریض کو چاہئے کہ طبیب جو دوائی تجویز کرے، اسے صحیح وقت پر اور صحیح مقدار میں استمرار کے ساتھ لے، اور جن چیزوں سے پرہیز بتائے ان سے پرہیز کرے، اور بیماری کے دور ہونے تک صابر رہے۔ اسی طرح نفسانی مریض کو چاہئے کہ عالم دین اس کو جن اعمال کی نصیحیت کرے چاہے وہ احکام شریعت سے ہوں یا کلام حکمت سے، ان پر بغیر چون و چرا باقاعدگی کے ساتھ عمل کرے اور جن برائیوں کو ترک کرنے کے لئے کہے ان کی چھوڑ دے اور اس راہ میں صبر سے کام لے، یہاں تک کہ مرض دور ہو جائے اور وہ صحیت یاب ہو جائے۔

اگر مریض اور طبیب میں یہ تمام صفات موجود ہونگے تو علاج اچھا ہوگا۔ تنزیلی اور تاویلی دونوں علاج اثرانداز ہونگے اور  کارگر ثابت ہونگے۔
 
[1] Now it has been narrated to us that a number of the truly faithful (مؤمنین) and a group among those who call people to true religion (دعوت) once said to one of their scholars: ‘You have liberated us by helping us learn about an affair (الامر)’ of great importance, for which we feel obligated to show our gratitude for three reasons:
 
  1. Our gratitude for you having called us to the true religion (دین)
  2. Our gratitude for the knowledge (علم) that you directed us to
  3. Our gratitude for the religious practices (عمل) you ordered us to perform.

So inform us how we should express our gratitude for these three reasons. Then inform us about:
 
  • the rights (حقوق) and duties that are obligatory on us as directed by the ordinances of religion (حدود دین);
  • and the obligations of a seeker in his mode of questioning,
  • and the obligations on the responses of the scholar being sought.

And inform us, to the best of your capabilities about the ways (مذاہب) of the righteous (صالحین) and the proper behavior (آداب) of the seekers.’  

 
[2] The scholar replied: ‘Now the affair to which I invite you all, is the religion that Allah has bestowed as an honor on His servants; which He has perfected for them and through which He honors whoever responds to Him. So for each beginning (أول), He has placed in it an end (آخر), and for each end, a goal (نہایۃ); and each goal has a limit (غایۃ) whose full extent cannot be attained. These are the way-stations (منازل) of the people of true understanding. Their key is constantly remembering Allah (ذکر اللہ), their initial levels are trials (اولھا بلوی); the middle ones are of right guidance (اوسطھا ھدی) and the final ones are piety (آخرھا تقوی). So whoever is seeking to learn the basics of Allah's affairs, and then discovers the ranks of the divinely determined (حدود الدین المقدور), he has sought guidance from the right sources and has set upon it in the best possible manner.
  • As for showing gratitude to the scholar (who guided you), that is through his obedience;
  • and the gratitude for the knowledge (he gave you), is turning it into action and calling others to it;
  • and the gratitude for the right practice (he taught you), is striving patiently (صبر) in it and in calling others to it.

[3] As for teaching you the ways of the righteous (مذاہب الصالحین) and the proper behavior (آداب الطالبین) of the seekers, the following story has been narrated to us about a man from Persia (فارس) who was among those subject to the trial of spiritual ignorance. Although he had a rich heart and a brilliant intellect, and had acquired an agreeable culture and education, nonetheless his ignorance outweighed his knowledge, because of his earlier experiences and the environment in which he grew up. So he was restless in the burning heat of his thirst, assuming the glimmering of mirages to be the reflection of water, until he reached it, and found it to be nothing. However, there he found Allah, and paid Him his account in full! Then Allah honored his abode and removed the veil in front of his intellect. He found him straying, so He guided him; He found him in need, so He satisfied him with knowledge. Thus by His graces he eventually became one of the scholars of Sinai (الطور) and the angelic dwellers in the well-populated temple (بیت المعمور)
 
[4] Finally when his guidance had been completed and he reached the goals of his aspiration, he was duty-bound to show gratitude to his Master and to exert himself for his Lord. He used to hear his spiritual father repeat a proverb, which held the rank of soul to his body: ‘The most excellent of deeds is giving life to the dead (ان افضل الحسنات أحیا الأموات)’   So he said to himself: ‘I used to be dead, and he gave me life; I was ignorant, and he gave me knowledge. I am not the first person to ever be ignorant, such that I attained knowledge before everyone else; nor am I the last one to be ignorant, such that the process of spiritual teaching has come to an end on me. Therefore out of gratitude for this blessing, it is only appropriate for me to pass on this divine trust to those who come after me; just as those who preceded me have handed it down to me. For the origin of this affair is from Allah, and it only reached me through its many intermediaries (اسباب), the first passing it on to the second, the second passing it on to the third, until it descended from the heavenly host (الملا الأعلی) to the creatures of this lower world. After that, did it tear asunder the veil (خرق الحجاب) between Allah and His creations, and did the ‘gateways’ (ابواب) pass it onward until it reached me, only so that I could be its final goal and ultimate end? Not at all! For those who have transmitted it and handed on this trust before me are more deserving of precedence and spiritual gains! What they have earned does not belong to me, so that I could rely on it. No! I (myself) am part of what they have earned through their acts (of teaching me). Nor does their precedence relieve me from having to act! So now I need to strive to spread knowledge, just as I needed to strive to seek knowledge in the beginning.’ He realized that he is now obligated to pass on his spiritual understanding to others, just as his master was obligated to teach him, and that his final duty was similar to his initial duty (وأن الآخر أمرہ فی الحکم مثل الاول).

[5] So he travelled towards his Lord leaving his people and possessions behind, calling people to the good (خیر داعیاً), in order to deserve a gratitude, similar to what was incumbent upon him for his master. He started traveling through countries, passing among the non-Arab (عجم) and the Arab tribes, scrutinizing their faces for signs of spiritual aptitude and posing religious questions. But he did not get any response, nor did he meet anyone seeking knowledge, until he ended up in the furthest part of the jazira. There he entered a city where nobody knew him; and while he was recalling Allah’s blessings and searching various gatherings, he noticed a group of people from the town who were discussing religion without any guidance, and recklessly following the inclinations of their passions. He sat nearby, out of sight, listening carefully to their discussion and analyzing them carefully to determine which of them was closer to the right path.  
 
[6] Then he said to himself: ‘These people are more deserving of life (spiritual knowledge) for three reasons:
  1. First, because the true divine argument (حجۃ) has reached them;
  2. Second, because they belong to the people of my community (الملۃ);’
  3. and third, because they are closer to the right path shown by their keen interest in religion and the fact that they are inquiring about it and discussing it —for the person who is already searching for something is much closer to finding it.’ 
 
[7] [The narrator] So when those people completed their discussion, they turned to the scholar and asked: "Who are you, and where are you from, O youth (یا فتی)?’ 
 
[8] ‘I am Abdullah and I am among the residents of His sanctuary,’ he replied.
 
[9] What is your business here and what is your work?’ they asked.
 
[10] ‘My business has completed,’ he replied, ‘and as for work, I am looking for it.’

[24] ‘All right,’ he replied. He stood up, facing the attentive group and began...  

[25] ‘Verily He created with His power, then arranged everything perfectly! Who determined a path through His generosity, then guided! It is He who brought forth paths of the pastureland (شوارع المرعی), then made its outer form a dried-up brush (ظاہرہ غثا) and its inner aspect broad in scope and content (وباطنہ أحوی)! whose ways are not obscure (لا ملتبس السبل) and whose traces will never be obliterated (ولا مندرس الآثار)—for if it were so, then intellects would fail to seek it, and would despair reaching it. His knowledge does not exist outwardly, so that the unworthy could not possess and alter it. Instead it is benevolent and well-protected (کریم مغمود)! Because of its excellence, it is well concealed, and its secrets are well-guarded for those worthy of them! Its dignity (شرف) is the splendid robe of guidance, and its perfection is the garment of piety (لباس التقوی)! It is marked out by the light of lights (نور الانوار), entrusted safely in the hearts of the best and chosen ones - the truly pious, guardians, protectors, generous and recorders! This knowledge is a trust which they safely pass on, and do not transgress the divine ordinances. It is the law of the Supreme Judge containing all the benefits of the All-Merciful! He honored it by making it inward (باطن), and He protected it by keeping it secret. He veiled it with His obligations, and crowned it with His covenants. For it cannot be reached by the aspirations of those who rely on the strength of their intellects (فلا تنالہ ھمم المتفکرین), nor can it be attained by those who are scheming for it (ولا تبلغہ فکر المحتالین). Its outer aspect is a reminder and a sign for those who are rightly guided, while its inner aspect is a sweet drink gushing forth.
 
His lofty heavens come down close, and his earth is arranged for those who inquire about Him and His guiding stars are arranged for those who seek Him. He has watering-places for those who move towards Him, and signs (آیات), whose wonders follow each other in succession, pointing to Him through their symbols. His accustomed way is preferment (سیرتہ تفضیل), and He is approached through distinguished ranks (ومنھاجہ تفصیل). ‘His duties are obligatory (أحکامہ فرائض) and His accustomed ways are paths (to be walked on) (وسنتہ شرائع). Sincerity ennobles his obligations, and justice purifies the efforts made for him. For He has made his ways (Shariah) a guide and a path leading to good, with its outer aspect a protective shell, and a sign pointing toward its inner dimensions. Intellects are forced by their neediness and ignorance to seek knowledge and open abundance. So blessed be the One Who placed the night and the day in succession, for those who reflect and those who wish to be grateful! May Allah’s blessings be on the one who He has specially chosen from His servants, and made a warner (نذیر) for all the worlds!’
 

بےشک اللہ نے اپنی قدرت سے (اس علم کو) پیدا کیا، اور اسے بہترین ترتیب عطا کی! اس نے اپنی سخاوت سے راستہ کا تعین کیا اور اس کی طرف ہدایت دی! وہی تھا جس نے چارہ گاہوں (شریعت) کو پیدا کیا، پھر اس کے ظاہر (عمل) کو خشک اور کھردرا کر دیا اور اس کے باطن (علم) کو کشادہ اور مواد میں وسیع کیا! جس کے طریقے مبہم نہیں ہیں اور جس کے آثار کبھی برباد نہیں ہونگے! کیونکہ اگر ایسا ہوتا تو عقلیں اسے حاصل کرنے سے ناکام، اور اسے پانے سے عاجز ہو جاتیں۔ اس نے اپنے علم کو ظاہر میں نہیں رکھا کہ نا اہل لوگ اس کے مالک بن بیٹھیں اور اسے تبدیل کر دیں؛ بلکہ اس کا علم کریم اور (ظاہر کے خول میں) محفوظ ہے۔ اپنے کمال کی وجہ سے وہ انتہائی پوشیدہ ہے اور اس کے اسرار استحقاق رکھنے والوں کے لئے محفوظ ہیں۔ اس (علم) کا شرف، ہدایت کا عظیم الشان لباس ہے، اور اس کا کمال، اس کے تقوی کا لباس ہے۔ وہ نور الانوار سے نمایاں ہے جو بہترین اور منتخب لوگوں کے دلوں میں محفوظ ہے۔ ایسے لوگ، جو متقی، سر پرست، محافظ اور حساب رکھنے والے ہیں۔ یہ علوم ایک امانت ہے جسے وہ حفاظت سے آگے منتقل کرتے رہتے ہیں اور الہی حدود کی خلاف ورزی نہیں کرتے۔ یہ اعلی ترین حاکم کا قانون ہے جس میں رحمٰن کی تمام نعمتیں موجود ہیں۔ اللہ نے ان علوم کو باطن میں چھپا کر انہیں کرامت دی، اور انہیں راز بنا کر ان کی حفاظت فرمائی۔ اس نے ان علوم پر اپنے فرائض کے پردے چڑھائے اور انہیں اپنے عہد کا تاج پہنایا۔ وہ لوگ جو اپنی عقل پر اعتماد کرتے ہیں ہرگز ان علوم تک نہیں پہنچ سکتے، اور نہ ہی وہ لوگ انہیں پا سکتے ہیں جو ان کے حصول کے لئے تدبیر کر رہے ہیں۔  اس کا ظاہر، ہدایت پانے والوں کے لئے ایک یاد دہانی اور نشان ہیں؛ اور اس کا باطن موجیں مارتا ہوا میٹھا شربت ہے!

اس کے بلند آسمان تشویش کرنے والوں کے لئے جھکائے گئے ہیں، اور اس کی زمین ان کے لئے سجائی گئی ہے؛ اور اس کی ہدایت کے ستارے اس کو تلاش کرنے والوں کے لئے روشن کئے گئے ہیں۔ اس کی طرف بڑھنے والوں کے لئے اللہ نے پانی کے گھاٹ (ائمہ) تیار کئے ہیں۔ یہ ایسی نشانیاں ہیں جو ایک کے بعد ایک مسلسل ظاہر ہوتی رہتی ہیں، اور اپنی علامتوں کے ذریعے اس کی طرف اشارہ کرتی ہیں۔ درجات میں ترقی اس کا معروف طریقہ ہے جس پر اہل مراتب کے ذریعے چلا جاتا ہے۔ اس کے احکامات فرض ہیں اور اس کی شریعت سنت ہے۔ ان احکامات کی ادائگی میں اخلاص انہیں شرف عطا کرتا ہے، اور ان کو پورا کرنے میں انصاف برتنا، ان کے لئے کی جانے والی کوششوں کو پاک کرتا ہے۔ اس نے اپنی شریعت کو خیر کا رہنما اور راستہ بنایا ہے۔ پھر اس کے ظاہر کو ایسا محفوظ خول بنایا، جو اس کے باطنی طول و عرض کی طرف اشارہ کرتا ہے۔ عقلیں اپنی حاجت اور جہالت کی تنگی کی وجہ سے اس علم کی وسعت کو تلاش کرنے پر مجبور ہیں۔ بس مبارک ہے وہ اللہ جس نے غور و فکر اور شکرگزار بننے والوں کے لئے رات اور دن کو ایک دوسرے کے پیچھے ترتیب دی۔ اور اللہ کی رحمت ہو اس ذات مبارک پر جسے اللہ نے تمام عالمین کو خبردار کرنے کے لئے اپنے بندوں میں سے منتخب کیا!

[27] Then his eyes overflowed with tears and he stopped talking and bid the group farewell. Still weeping, he got up and walked toward his travel baggage. All in the group were overcome with emotions by the intensity of his speech and the effectiveness of his sermon. They wanted to accompany him, but he prevented them from doing so. Since they didn’t know what else to do, they all returned to their homes. But a young man from the group, who was the youngest of them, yet had the best character, accompanied him. He had completed the education of his intellect, and reflection had awakened his heart. His thoughts coincided with, and were reinforced by what he heard in the scholar’s speech - both in what he had presented and what he had alluded to. This young man's father was a great Sheikh among the most distinguished people of Arab by lineage and was one of the most devoted supportees of the people of his religion. With the scholar's permission, the young man accompanied him until they reached his (the scholar's) inn. The scholar entered and invited the young man in. He then ordered food, and when they finished eating and were comfortable enough to talk, 
 
[28] The young man said: ‘O wise man (حکیم)! You spoke and we heard! You preached with eloquence and adorned religion with the most beautiful of ornaments, and motivated us with the most beautiful of motivations and the noblest of preparations. You called us through the most eminent of responsibilities, enticing our minds to enquire from you about all that has struck our hearts as a result of your speech. Such excellence could only manifest through one whose knowledge has perfected internally and whose character has completely developed externally, making him a source of wisdom! You are like a tree... its roots firm, its splendor towering over the worlds, its fruits hanging low from its branches for those who seek them. So I ask you to be one of those blessed branches for me, from what I can see of your distinguished judgment, your native aptitude and your perfect intellect. You mentioned that our minds are forced by their ignorance to seek knowledge and abundance. Now my mind is the most needy and indignent of all, so is there any way to life for me?’
 
[29] The scholar replied: Surely the One Who created by His power, did not create without purpose, nor did He create them entirely knowledgeable. He did not leave them wandering in ignorance without end, nor did He want them to adopt foolishness as their behavior, or wear the garments of ignorance. So He established a way out for them by means of an "instrument" (intellect) around which He shaped their constitution (الف بھا ترکیب) and by which He perfected their creation (وأکمل بھا خلقھم). He made their hearing, eyes and hearts, an openings to accept the light of His knowledge as an act of generosity toward them, and by it completed His argument for them. He then placed the treasures of His wisdom and the specific characteristics of His wishes that pleased Him (مذاہب) in the pure, chosen, elite of His creation. He gave them the responsibility of worshipping Him according to those ways, and calling others to those ways, while imposing on people the duty of responding to their call by using their instrument (intellect), so that He may distinguish the wicked from the good. Hence the scholar will reveal himself plainly through his seeking of that knowledge, while the ignorant will be distinguished by his falling behind. Therefore there is no argument against Allah after His messengers, nor is there any excuse for straying after the right path has been clearly pointed out.’    
 
[30] The young man replied: My lack of excuse has frightened me, and the existence of signs pointing to the right path makes me desire setting out on that search; so I ask you to be a guide, and I'll be your humble servant’ 
 
[31] The scholar answered: ‘Surely Allah did not create His creatures as fully grown scholars. He created them as infants, small and ignorant, not knowing anything, so that they grow big after having been small, and come to know after having been ignorant. Thus their being small is a precondition for their becoming big, and their ignorance is a sign pointing toward knowledge. The same is true of spiritual knowledge. One can only attain its essence on the basis of preceding, lower forms of knowledge, which hearts can quickly assimilate and intellect can easily grasp. And so that the first stage of spiritual growth is established in their minds, and they are humbled enough to carry out the required duties. This opens up their hearts to the essence of that knowledge and enable their intellects to bear its goal. That is Allah's accustomed way regarding His servants! So we neither need to depart from the opinions of religious communities, nor do we need to follow anything other than the accustomed way to be guided.’ 
 
[32] ‘But I am an infant in relation to what I wish to achieve on this path,’ the young man said, ‘so raise and educate me! I am ignorant with regard to what I have been seeking, so teach me and help me learn. I am forced to rely on your gracious favor, so have mercy on me! Surely you were once in this state, and Allah brought His bounties to you through the hands of the one who became so important for you, and to whom you owe such gratitude! So you already understand the state of someone like myself’, caught in the vice of ignorance and so needy, that he will desperately resort to any way out.’   
 
[33] The scholar said: ‘I myself was once in the state you’ve described, and I surely wish to spare you the desperation. But my compassion towards you will not bring you closer to what you hope for without your "working" for it! For just as every word has a proper employment (تصریف) that can only be accepted if it is sincere and true, similary knowledge is of no benefit until it has been put into action; and action is of no benefit until it is accepted by Allah. Now the true and sincere form of speech is that, which is just. And the signs pointing to knowledge are the revealed paths of righteous acts, and faithfully conforming to the right models throughout all that is encompassed by the furthermost regions and the variation of day and night. So do not seek knowledge impatiently, and do not dismiss it out of negligence! Nor should you accept it out of blind imitation, without any convincing argument — for in such a case it would slip out of your heart at the first onset of doubt. But do not deny the argument once it has been firmly established, or you will become the pleader for all that is false. And do not stop inquiring about that knowledge and reflecting upon it, or your judgment will become sterile. You must trust me without prejudice, and make every effort to understand without confusion, seeking the truth and asking Allah’s help for it — for Allah does not neglect a recompense, nor does He forbid reward if He sees you seeking for the good!’
 
[34] ‘Surely you have spoken most justly, accomplishing great good, and you have pointed the way to the truth with the most beautiful of words,’ said the young man. ‘But now I am seeking Allah’s help, and I am asking you about that affair to which you are calling and specifically referring to. What is it? Who does it come from? and to Who does it lead?’
 
[35] ‘That is Allah’s religion which He has approved!’ replied the scholar. ‘It is from Him, so He will accept none other! Specifically, it leads to the pure elite among His messengers, and generally to all of His creation. So the person who has been singled out for His favor should be grateful for it, and put it into practice, and call others to it. But the person who has been excluded (from that knowledge) should keep seeking it until he comes to know of it, and should willingly obey whoever is guiding him.’ 
 
[36] ‘Do you think,’ asked the young man, ‘that this favor by which Allah has singled out His friends is at the same level, such that they are all equal in its excellence? Or do some of them have a rank of excellence (فضل) over others?’   
 
[37] ‘Haven’t I already mentioned,’ said the scholar, ‘that every knowledge has a preceding stage of knowledge below it, and that for every favor there are “bearers” (حملۃ)? So they have ranks of honor with their Lord; each entrusted with what he has earned through his knowledge. Thus He gives of His bounty (فضل) to every merituous person, and out of His justice (عدل) towards His creatures, and His loving mercy (رحمۃ) towards His servants!’ 
 
[38] ‘Then what you are calling to,’ asked the young man, ‘is it this infinitely divine favor towards all creatures, or is it something else?’ 
 
[39] The scholare responded: ‘It is not exactly the same as that favor, nor is it something other than that. Rather, what I'm calling you to, is related to this favor, as a handful of water is related to the vast extent of the Euphrates! I am calling you to the religion which Allah approved for His servants and which He ennobled on the day He made it complete:

This day, I have perfected your religion for you, completed My Favour upon you, and have chosen for you Islam as your religion

It is sublime, protected, noble, hidden. He has ennobled it with His rewards in honor of those who uphold it, and rebuked those with painful punishments who neglect it, making them a sign of dishonor. Its outward dimension is formal prescriptions (ظاہرہ رسوم), and its inner aspect is the various levels of knowledge (وباطنہ علوم). Its authority is illuminating (حکمُہُ نیر) and its justice most clear (وعدلہ بین). This is what I am calling you to!’  
 
[40] The young man asked: But what singled you out for this affair instead of others, and made you more deserving of it than others, since you just claimed that it comes from Allah to all creation in general? Indeed everyone lays claim to it (for himself or his own group), as opposed to others. So what has made you all more deserving of the name “the truth” than those others, when this affair only came to you in the same way it came to them?’ 
 
[41] The scholar replied: ‘There is not a single person who speaks a word of truth, that has not been taken from the same sources from which we have taken it. So they are in agreement with us about that word of truth, since we both affirm it. But then they separated from us by abandoning the sources of that very truth, while we persevered in our allegiance to them by obeying them. Through our preserverance we held on to it, while they let go of it and abandoned it. Hence they agreed with us in their affirmation, but differed from us in their actions. Thus, what they affirm bears witness against them, but bears witness in our favor, and their abandoment caused their need for us and the need for what we possess. They are like the brothers who inherited enormous wealth from their father and divided it equally among themselves. Then some of them lost their wealth and fell into poverty, while the others economized and traded with it, and multiplied their wealth many times over. The impoverished brothers were then forced to come to the rich ones and serve them and ask for their help. Likewise those who have lost the path of their messengers, have been forced by their ignorance to seek knowledge from those of noble sources. So the sign that indicates that we are more deserving of the truth than them, is their need for us and our self-sufficiency without them. That is how our scholar has come to invite the ignorant among them; and how our clothed ones have come to cover up the naked; and how our rich have come to feed the hungry. For the true right belongs to the one who has precedence and true merit (وجب الحق الفاضل), while precedence and true merit belongs to the one who acts kindly (والفضل لکل ذی نعمۃ); and gratitude is due to the one who favors (والشکر لکل ذی احسان)’
 
[42] ‘Their greater merit and precedence over others has become clear,’ said the young man. ‘And as for your argument for the truth, that has been verified against the common people who have neglected the truth. For I am affirming what you have mentioned, upholding what you have described, denying what you have denied, acknowledging the truth and its precedence, and loathing falsehood and its people!’ 
 
[43] ‘My argument against you,’ replied the scholar, ‘is similar to my argument against them, since even though you disagreed with them about the description of the truth, yet you actually agreed with them about its inner meaning.’ 
 
[44] ‘But how can that be?’ asked the young man, ‘since I am affirming that you hold the truth while others are denying it!’
 
[45] ‘Even if you disagreed with them by your affirmation of the truth, the scholar continued, 'still you were in agreement with them in preventing access to it, when they prohibited it. For merely verbal affirmation is of no benefit without any repentance and reverting back to the truth; and denial does no harm to the person being denied when he has clear evidence.’

For the latest book is more deserving of being followed than the one's that came before it— even though they all are from Allah — because the latest one is more recent in time and clearer in its way of proceeding, since it has replaced what was before it, and nothing has come after it to replace it.’

  4. Don't ask me about anything until I bring it up with you; and
  5. Don’t speak about me to your father.’

Example of how one should approach the truth


[326] ‘Abu Malik exclaimed, 'Indeed, I am deeply troubled by your words. For we have always known that Al Bakhtari and his son are among people of sound intelligence (اھل الحلم)’ and scrupulous piety (والورع). Therefore it is not possible for us to judge them and convict them of being at fault without first listening to them, since we would be doing them an injustice. Nor can we assume that they are following true guidance, since in that case we would be doing our souls an injustice by blindly imitating them (تقلید). Therefore we cannot say that they have been deceived, since we would be condemning them without any real knowledge about them—and we know that they are certainly more noble than that!

Instead, we need to look more closely at this news, examining it with an eye of impartiality, in the light of justice, and with the firmness of our intellects. Now it is said that the authority of the one who stays firmly grounded in his affairs, will continue to be protected; but whoever is hasty in his affair might slip up. (من تثبت فی الأمور لم یزل سلطانہ محروزاً، ومن عجل فی أمرہ لم یأمن زلۃ قدمہ)”. Therefore in most matters a foolish error is something that can be excused and whose damage can be readily compensated, such as an error with food. Its harm is limited and its remedy is easy. On the other hand, a foolish error regarding religion entails great damage and enormous loss, since such a fault can’t be excused, and it can’t be easily exchanged. It is like a foolish error made in prescribing a medicine: the doctor cannot undo its effects, nor can he exonerate himself!
 
‘It has been said that the most excellent of treasures are the treasures of enlightened hearts and the acquisition of proper modes of behavior (ان افضل الذخائرکنور الالباب و اکتساب الاداب).” For surely the fruits they provide are praiseworthy! Among their fruits is the pursuit of sound wisdom (حلم). Indeed, sound wisdom is the crown of the learned (ان الحلم التاج العلما), and all their noblest aspirations ascend to the heights of its crowning glories, and their preminence manifests every where they go, through what they have attained of those noble treasures, and by the familiarity of the subject they are seeking. We too have gathered such treasures of sound intelligence and proper behavior over a long period of time despite our trying circumstances. We have been persevering in that because of its excellence, despite the vicissitudes (a change of circumstances or fortune, typically one that is unwelcome or unpleasant) of fate and the changing nature of our fortunes.’ Thus we cannot throw away all that acquired wisdom . Now we truly need those treasures in this auspicious moment and use it to deal with this affair that has overcome our brothers! 

‘So there is no sense in abandoning them, and what they are following, without first interrogating and debating with them about it, relying on the sound intelligence of those who are self-controlled, and the insight of those who are seeking! We cannot fall in doubt over our beliefs, nor assume they have deserted the truth heedlessly, because we know that they are wiser than that. I don't see how they could have chosen to abandon the truth (حق) for error (ضلال), when error is far smaller than truth. Neither is falsehood so eminent that they would abandon the plain truth to follow it!   ‘Indeed, if you carefully think over this, their choice of this new belief can only be for three reasons:

(1) Either these people are still following our religion, and this new change is only an additional element drawn out from the traditions of proper behavior, stories, or poetry - because there is an abundance of such sayings (from various people).
(2) Or, the stranger has brought to them something else in addition to their religion which they found very pleasing, and in that case “knowledge is the lost camel of the person of true faith (العلم ضالۃ کل مؤمن),” and an increase in knowledge is certainly better than a lack of it!
(3) Or perhaps their guidance stands completely apart from what they knew before, and it comes from a true source of prophethood, in which case they may have followed him with clear guidance. For indeed no one can be denied of Allah’s command, and the creatures will never attain the understanding of Allah’s wise judgments and His will regarding them, no matter how hard they strive. For surely Allah is involved daily in work"

Because the earth is never without a just witness from Allah for a single blink of the eye, either publicly and openly, or in fear and concealment.’ (فلم تخل الارض عن عدل اللہ طرفۃ عین اما ظاھرا موجودا و اما خائفا مغمودا)
 
[530] ‘Your words cannot be refuted,’ declared Abu Malik, ‘and a firm saying that can’t be doubted! And as for the continuity of Allah’s argument through His just witness and through following the messengers, that has been firmly established and clearly shown. So now indicate to me the real meaning of their absence in our time. What is the reason behind that?’
 
[531] ‘What was the reason for Moses’ being hidden,’ Salih asked, ‘when he went out of the city of Firaun and crossed from it, in fear and clandestinely, to the land of Midian and the prophet Shoayb?’ 
 
[532] ‘He fled from the people of Pharaoh,’ said Abu Malik, ‘hid from them because he feared for his life, since they had agreed to kill him!’  
 
[533] Salih said to him: ‘Then have you heard in this revealed path of any one of the just witnesses, the witnesses of Allah and the descendants of His prophets, who were murdered while persevering in their role as the intermediaries of religion? 
 
[534] ‘Yes,’ said Abu Malik, ‘a great many people among them have been murdered’ . 
 
[535] ‘Then, for which one of Allah’s just witnesses would you want to reveal himself publicly to you and to the people of your time?’ Salih asked him 'Is it a martyr that Allah should resurrect for you after his death? Or one fleeing from you, while you seek his return to the death that he is fleeing from? Therefore His just witness has followed Allah's accustomed way regarding His prophets and His messengers in persevering patiently under persecution, and in remaining hidden until Allah judges between him and us—for He is the best of those who judge wisely.’ 
 
[536] ‘There is no fault in whoever has followed the accustomed ways of the righteous ones in fearing for himself and fleeing from his people,’ Abu Malik responded. ‘No indeed, the fault surely lies with the communities of evil and the helpers of injustice in their killing Allah’s prophets and His just witnesses, and in their all agreeing together about that.’  
 
[537] ‘Then what do you say,’ Salih asked him, ‘about those who murder Allah’s just witnesses and His guide; what is their ultimate fate or that of those who help them?’ 
 
[538] ‘In the Fire,’ responded Abu Malik, ‘along with whoever has blackened a flag or filled an inkwell for them!’ 
 
[539] ‘But what do all your legal scholars (فقھا) say about that?’ Salih asked him. 
 
[540] ‘They all pray for Allah’s mercy for the murdered one,’ said Abu Malik, ‘but they don’t see the sin of the murderer!’
 
[541] ‘Now, as for the person who willingly obeys the murderer,’ Salih asked him, ‘isn’t he his accomplice in the blood of the murdered victim?’
 
[542] ‘By Allah, of course he is!’ exclaimed Abu Malik. ‘For he is the murderer’s associate, rather even worse than him.’ 
 
[543] ‘His association with the murderer is obvious, because of his helping him. But how does he become worse than him?’ Salih asked him. 
 
[544] ‘Were it not for the opinions passed down and circulated by the legal scholars, Abu Malik responded, ‘the oppressors could not have maintained their domination over the foolish ones.’ 
 
[545] ‘You’ve spoken truly, O Abu Malik,’ Salih said to him. ‘Indeed would you yourself be satisfied to have their kingdom as your home, their land as your sanctuary, their armies as your neighbors, and their guardians as your best friends? Yet you are a witness for them, judging in their favor with your “knowledge” and affirming that they fulfill your needs. For you have prayed for them in asking them for money, and you have thanked them for their help with your needs. And you have dressed their wrongdoing with the crown of your ‘justice,” so they filled with pride. And you adorned their proud boasting with the outer garments of your “revealed path,” so they went to even greater extremes. You have illuminated their falsehood with indifference to your truth. And the despised ones were deceived by your actions and those who resemble you among the so-called “scholars.” For they said: “We have a model to imitate in them, since they are the legal scholars of the community.” So they have gone astray by all you scholars, just as you all went astray due to the oppressors. Thus you imagined that  the person responsible for this attrocity was someone other than yourselves, since you forgot Allah’s saying—may He be praised:

“And do not be dependent upon those who do injustice, lest the fire touch you [and you have no protecting friends but Allah] !“ (‘1:1 1 ).

So you all have cut off what Allah ordered to be connected, while you were all looking on! And you have proclaimed the outer appearances of “justice” while you strayed far away from its path. You turned away from his saying:

“Obey Allah, and obey the Messenger and the possessors of authority among you!’ (4:59),

You claimed that these wrong doers were the “possessors of authority over you,” even though they openly rejected Allah. In doing so you forgot Allah’s saying—may He be exalted:

“Fight those who reject (Allah) who are close to you” (9:1 23)

Thus you all willfully disobeyed the person you had been commanded to obey, and you willingly obeyed those whom you had been ordered to struggle against. And all the while, you assumed that you were those who are performing good deeds! ‘So the self-delusion of error carried you all so far in the end, and your own opinions overpowered you to such an extent that you all said that Allah will never send a messenger or a warner in your time! Is it that as a result of this imagined “goodness” of your actions, you felt you had no need for the friends of Allah to carry out His argument? Or was it the ugliness and evil of your actions, such that you gave up all hope for forgiveness from Allah through repentance? Or is it part of your “justice” that you should pass judgment against Allah, and that you should be satisfied and pleased with the sort of systematic persecution and rejection of your Messenger that you would never be satisfied with for yourselves? For would any one of you be satisfied to see his own child killed or expelled from their home and possessions without any right?’ 
 
[546] ‘None of us would be satisfied with that!’ Abu Malik exclaimed. ‘If any one of us had his child killed or was expelled from his property, he would become outraged, and his people would be outraged for him, so they would not be satisfied until he was satisfied.’
 
[547] ‘Surely Allah and His Messenger are more deserving that you all should be outraged and be angry for him,’ Salih answered him, ‘when His viceroy (khalifa) was killed and His friend (wali) was removed from the earth?’
 
[548] ‘Indeed, it is hard for us that they should kill Allah’s friend among us or remove him from his earth,’ said Abu Malik. ‘But (His friends) have decided to hide themselves from us, while the oppressors are unjustly dominating us.’ 
 
[549] ‘If you were all to help the friends of Allah, then they would win out,’ Salih answered him. ‘And if you were to abandon His enemies, then they would not be victorious. But (in reality), as far as your relation to the friends of Allah, you can all be judged to belong to one of these three levels. There is the relentless murderer, who never tires of hunting them down and whose blood thirst is not quenched even when he wins out. And there is their  inveterate enemy, who bites his nails in rage, who will go to any length to incite enmity against them, and who never forgets his hatred of them, even if they overlook (him). Finally, there are those who do dislike those enemies’ actions, who are sympathetic to the friends of Allah but are really abandoning them, who have no spiritual insight in regard to faith, and who cannot be relied upon to protect the secret (کتمان) of the imams. So it is because of that that the friends of Allah have concealed themselves from you, out of precaution against you and this immense number of people who have taken their passions as their lord, who fight like dogs over the stuff of this lower world, and who conspire together about killing the prophets. Since such people are unaware of the wrongfulness of their actions, they do not trouble their souls with the wrongfulness of their evil-doing and they do not humble themselves in repentance for what they have done. Indeed they go so far as to attribute the wrongfulness of their actions to Allah and to ascribe them to Him. So they say that Allah will never send a prophet after their prophet, nor any warner. Thus they sought to legitimize the slaughtering of the friends of Allah without any right. And in so doing they did wrong with their actions, they turned away from (the truth) in their speech, and they transgressed by calling (them) liars.’ 
 
[550] ‘Yes,’ said Abu Malik. That is the description of this community of ours, and that is what they say. So how can we be saved from that, and where is the refuge from Allah’s punishment?’ 
 
[551] ‘With Allah!’ Salih answered him. ‘Seek protection with Him and He will give you refuge. Turn to Him in repentance and He will accept that repentance from you. And ask Him for the path of His friends and He will rescue you through your own right action from the deeds of those who do injustice. The same is true for all these other people: if they were to turn to Allah, He would accept their repentance. And Allah would not punish them, for Allah does not do a dust-speck’s weight of wrong, even if they themselves have done wrong and denied Allah’s friends. For He does not hasten to punish them, even when they have hastened to disobey (Him).’ 
 
[552] ‘Then I am seeking protection from Allah’s punishment,’ Abu Malik replied, ‘through repentance and turning to Him. And I am searching for the connection to what pleases Him. So be generous to the one who comes to you seeking (help), and help to guide rightly the one who is seeking your guidance—may Allah reward you for that!’   
 
[553] ‘If you do that,’ Salih answered him, ‘you will find that Allah, in His forgiveness, is closer to you than your repentance and returning to Him, for surely He loves those who turn to Him in repentance. And you will find me full of concern for you in regard to guiding you.’ 
 
[554] [The narrator continued:] Then Salih's eyes filled with tears at the mention of repentance, so he drew his speaking to an end. Since he was in his own rooms, he asked them to go away to his father’s house. Then he went in to see his (spiritual) father, the scholar who had called him, and he consulted with him about the matter of Abu Malik and his companions. 
 
[555] Then the scholar said: ‘You know your companions better (than I do), so if you recognize some good in them, then guide them rightly as you were guided. But if you are apprehensive about them, then don’t let your desire for them lead you to approach them while putting yourself at risk. And test them by ignoring them, but not harshly. Order your father to treat them kindly and to be respectful toward them for a while. For the person who is truly seeking the good will not remain hidden. Surely Allah will not leave you behind and He will open up for you, from the light of His providential arrangement of things, that through which He manages the affair of His creatures. And He will open up for you from the gateways of right guidance what will show you the (appropriate) actions of those who are rightly guided.’ 
 
[556] [The narrator] continued: So Abu Malik and his companions continued to go through their different levels of testing, until their affair was complete and they recognized their right guidance. And it was Allah’s friend among them who took care of their guidance, and they thanked Allah for that. Then they returned to their people warning them, so that through them Allah guided a great many of His servants to His religion.
