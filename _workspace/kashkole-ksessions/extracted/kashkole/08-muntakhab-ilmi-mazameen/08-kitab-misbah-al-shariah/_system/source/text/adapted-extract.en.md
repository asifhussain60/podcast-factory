## Servitude

All matters rest upon four aspects, each of which is grounded in seven principles. This formulation accords with the twenty-eight limits inherent in the twenty-eight principles of the testimony "There is no god but Allah" (*lā ilāha illā Allāh*).

**Seven Principles of Dealing with Allah:**

| 1 | Fulfilling all His rights |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Adhering to His prescribed limits |
| 3 | Rendering gratitude for His blessings |
| 4 | Accepting His judgment and decree |
| 5 | Bearing patiently His trials |
| 6 | Exalting His sanctity and majesty |
| 7 | Seeking earnestly His pleasure |

**Seven Principles of Dealing with Oneself:**

| 1 | Fear of Allah (*taqwā*) |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Striving in effort and perseverance |
| 3 | Enduring hardship in the path of truth |
| 4 | Establishing spiritual and moral discipline |
| 5 | Searching sincerely for truth |
| 6 | Restraining the self from base inclinations |
| 7 | Binding the self to poverty and humility |

**Seven Principles of Dealing with Creatures:**

| 1 | Forbearance |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Pardoning the transgressions of others |
| 3 | Adopting humility |
| 4 | Manifesting generosity |
| 5 | Acting with compassion |
| 6 | Offering counsel freely |
| 7 | Dealing with justice |

**Seven Principles of Dealing with the World:**

| 1 | Contentment with what Allah has bestowed |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Valuing the present above the distant |
| 3 | Abandoning striving after the unattainable |
| 4 | Despising an excess of worldly things |
| 5 | Practicing piety and God-consciousness |
| 6 | Recognizing the world's corruption and rejecting its allurements |
| 7 | Negating dominion by the world over the heart |

When all these qualities unite in a person, he becomes among the nobles, the worshippers, and the saints of Allah.

Worship is an essence whose inward reality is lordship. Without the "Lord," the "servant" (*ʿabd*) cannot exist. Every attribute from which obedience is absent belongs to lordship; every attribute hidden in lordship is manifest in servitude. All the essences of the servant are present in the Lord, yet no essence of the Lord dwells in the servant.[^cite-1]

Allah says—and His word is the measure of truth:

> ⟪quran 35:28⟫

This signifies that regardless of the servant's state—whether in existence or non-existence—the Lord always remains. The meaning of obedience is liberation of the self from all desires and enslaving passions. The pathway to this liberation requires forbidding the self every acquisition it desires, and accustoming it to bear all that it abhors. The essence of this practice is that one abandon all rest, choose solitude, and pursue the knowledge of Allah.[^cite-2]

The Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ said: "Worship Allah as though you see Him. If you cannot attain this vision, know that He sees you."

The word "servant" (*ʿabd*) contains three letters, each bearing profound significance:

| The first letter signifies knowledge (*ʿilm*) of the knowledge of Allah |
| --- |
| The second letter denotes distance (*buʿd*), meaning severance from all creatures |
| The third letter indicates proximity (*dhukkān*), meaning nearness without the appearance of the false self |

## Lowering the Gaze

Nothing benefits a person more than lowering his gaze from what is forbidden. Yet the gaze cannot be lowered from Allah's prohibitions until His glory and majesty have been inscribed upon the heart.

⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ was asked: "What enables one to lower the gaze?" He replied: "Submission to the power and dominion of the One who knows all your secrets—the eye of man, the spy of his heart, and the messenger of his intellect. Lower your gaze from all that violates your faith, from all that your heart dislikes and your reason rejects."

⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ declared: "If you lower your gaze, you will witness wonders."[^cite-3]

⟪ar:عیسی علیہ السلام⟫ said to his disciples: "I counsel you against gazing upon forbidden things, for they sow the seeds of desire in the heart and lead the soul toward corruption."

⟪ar:یحیا علیہ السلام⟫ spoke thus: "I prefer death to an unseemly glance!"

⟪ar:حضرت عبد اﷲ ابن مسعود⟫ said to a man returning from visiting a woman: "Would that you had been blind rather than have witnessed what you have seen!"

When the gaze is directed toward the forbidden, a knot of desire is tied in the heart. This knot can only be undone in two ways:

| 1 | The person sheds tears of remorse and sorrow, repenting sincerely to Allah |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | The person obtains the object of his forbidden gaze |

Whoever obtains through cruelty and arrogance the forbidden object of his gaze shall be led by it toward the fire of Hell. Whoever instead offers sincere repentance to Allah with tears of sorrow and regret shall find his abode in Paradise, where he shall receive countless blessings from Allah's bounty.

## Walking with Intention

If you possess understanding, undertake your journey with firm resolve and pure intention, for the soul by nature inclines toward transgression and is drawn to the forbidden. As you walk, observe and reflect upon the wonders of Allah's creation surrounding you. Do not walk with arrogance or haughtiness, for Allah says—and His word is truth:

> ⟪quran 31:18⟫

Turn your eyes away from all bodily pleasures and forbidden things as you walk. It is related in the hadith that every place in which Allah is mentioned or praised shall testify on behalf of its visitor on the Day of Resurrection, interceding for him and beseeching Allah's forgiveness and his admission to Paradise.

Engage in unnecessary conversation with none upon the road, for this is among the marks of ill-breeding. Know that many pathways are the snares and markets of Satan; never feel secure from his deceptions. Proceed upon your journey in obedience to Allah and with the intention of seeking His pleasure, for all your movements are recorded in your book. Allah says—and He speaks truth:

> ⟪quran 99:7-8⟫

## Knowledge

Knowledge is the beginning of every noble state and the culmination of every lofty achievement. For this reason, the Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ said: "Acquiring knowledge is incumbent upon every man and woman." He further declared: "Whoever knows his own self knows his Lord."[^cite-4] And he said: "You must acquire knowledge to establish sincerity in action; without sincerity, every deed is worthless." We seek refuge in Allah from knowledge that benefits the soul not at all and does not transform into righteous action.

Know that little knowledge demands much action. It is necessary that a person act throughout his life upon what he acquired in a single hour. ⟪ar:عیسی علیہ السلام⟫ said: "I saw a stone inscribed: 'Turn me.' When I turned it, I read on its reverse: 'Whoever does not act upon his knowledge shall be punished by eternal seeking of knowledge to dispel doubts, and his very learning shall become a burden upon him.'"

Allah said to Dawud (upon him be peace): "The lowest punishment I inflict upon one who does not act according to My knowledge shall be worse than seventy inward punishments resulting from the loss of the sweetness of My remembrance." There is no path to Allah save through knowledge. Knowledge is man's adornment in this world, his guide to the Hereafter, and the means of attaining Allah's pleasure.[^cite-5]

A true scholar is one whose actions are steady and whose prayers are sincere. His witness is not speech, debate, rhetoric, analogy, or claims, but rather his every deed testifies to truth and piety. In former times, those who sought knowledge were the wise, the pious, the discerning, and the cautious. Yet today we see that those acquiring knowledge lack all these qualities.

**The Scholar Should Possess These Eight Qualities:**

| Intellect |
| --- |
| Kindness |
| Compassion |
| Good counsel |
| Forbearance |
| Patience |
| Contentment |
| Generosity |

**The Student Should Possess These Seven Qualities:**

| Longing desire |
| --- |
| Will and resolve |
| Devotion |
| Piety and God-consciousness |
| Caution and watchfulness |
| Memory and retention |
| Steadfastness of purpose |

## Making Judgment

It is lawful only for one endowed with these qualities to render judgment:

| Inner purity and cleanliness of heart |
| --- |
| Sincerity in all outward and inward actions |
| Clear proof from the Lord |

For judgment is a decree, and a decree proceeds only by Allah's permission and through His proof. Whoever judges without thorough investigation of circumstances, or through negligence, is ignorant and shall be seized by his ignorance and burned by his judgment. The knowledge of right judgment is a light that Allah illuminates only in the hearts of His chosen servants.

The Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ said: "Among you, he who judges against someone with the least carefulness is most despicable to Allah."[^cite-6] Does not the judge consider that he stands as a barrier between Allah and His servant, suspended between Paradise and Hell?

Sufyan ibn ʿAynah said: "How can my knowledge benefit others when I have forbidden it to myself?" No one among all creation has the right to decide between the lawful and unlawful except one who guides the people of his time, his village, and his city toward truth through the Prophet's teaching, and who understands where his judgment applies. The Prophet ⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ said: "Judgment is a grave matter wherein there is no room for 'perhaps,' 'maybe,' or 'possibly.'"

⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنین علی علیہ السلام⟫ said to a judge: "Do you know which verses of Allah are abrogating and which are abrogated?" The judge replied: "No." He asked: "Do you possess complete mastery of Allah's will hidden in the inner meanings and interpretations of Qur'anic verses?" The judge answered: "No." He said: "Then you have destroyed yourself and have destroyed others."

A judge must know these things:

| 1 | Knowledge of all Qur'anic verses |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Knowledge of the reality of the Prophets' affairs and their inner signs, ethics, and commandments |
| 3 | Knowledge of all principles on which scholars agree or disagree |

And these four qualities must be evident in the one who judges:

| Strength of reasoning | Stability of judgment | Wisdom | Piety |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Commanding Good and Forbidding Evil

Only he who has abandoned all worry, purified himself of his own evils and desires, defeated Satan, and entered under Allah's protection has the right to command good and forbid evil. If someone undertakes this without acquiring these qualities, his own past action shall testify against him, and people shall not benefit from his counsel. Allah says in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 61:2-3⟫

Such a person is told: "O deceiver! Do you demand of My creatures that which you have rejected for yourself, loosening the reins of your own soul?"

It is narrated that Talha al-Asadi asked the Prophet ⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ about this verse. He replied: "Command people to what is right and forbid them from what is wrong, and bear patiently whatever befalls you, and be steadfast with them. But when you see people pursuing evil, selfish desires, and arrogance, then turn away from them and concern yourself with your own affairs."[^cite-7]

The one who commands good and forbids evil must first know the lawful from the unlawful, and must harbor no personal inclination in his counsel. He should be a sincere advocate for the people, gentle and merciful. He should understand the diverse temperaments of men and invite them to truth with clear, gentle arguments so all may benefit. He should be aware of the soul's desires and Satan's snares. He should bear adversity with patience, not seek compensation for his advice, nor complain of their response. He should not be harsh or vindictive. His anger should be for Allah's sake alone, and he should seek His aid.

If people are harsh with him or oppose him, let him be patient. If people accept his counsel, let him be grateful to Allah and entrust all affairs to Him, always watching his own faults so as not to incline toward self-praise.

## The Ruin of Scholars

Piety and fear are the heritage of knowledge and its measure. Knowledge is a ray of the light of gnosis and the heart of faith. A scholar who abandons piety cannot be a scholar, even if he could unravel the most difficult of sciences.

Scholars are ruined by eight things:

| 1 | Lust and stinginess |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Pretense and prejudice |
| 3 | Rejoicing in self-praise |
| 4 | Seeking truth about matters beyond their capacity |
| 5 | Excessive effort to render speech effective |
| 6 | Shamelessness before Allah |
| 7 | Arrogance and haughtiness |
| 8 | Failure to act upon their knowledge |

⟪ar:عیسی علیہ السلام⟫ said: "Most despicable of all creatures is one known for knowledge alone but not for deeds!"[^cite-8]

⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ said: "Do not sit with the heedless, for they will invite you from certainty to doubt, from sincerity to pretense, from humility to pride, from good counsel to enmity, from piety to base desire. Cleave instead to one close to Allah, for he will guide you from pride to humility, from pretense to sincerity, from doubt to faith, and will shield you from base desire and turn enmity into good counsel. None among all creation has the right to teach knowledge save one who has transcended all evils through his righteousness—one aware of Scripture's errors and the distinction between truth and falsehood, the dangers of conjecture and the allure of passion."

⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنینِ علی علیہ السلام⟫ said: "Be like a merciful physician who places the remedy where it benefits most."

⟪ar:عیسیٰ علیہ السلام⟫: When asked "Whose company should we keep?" he replied: "That of one whose existence reminds you of Allah, whose speech increases your knowledge, and whose deeds make you yearn for the Hereafter."

## Protection of the Soul

He who guards his heart against the carelessness of heedlessness, against the ignorance of intellect, and against the pleasures of passion has joined the cautious. Then whoever protects his faith from innovation that his heart may be guarded, his knowledge from conjecture and sophistry that his intellect may be safeguarded, and his wealth from the unlawful that his soul may be protected from base pleasure—such a one is among the righteous.

The Prophet ⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ said: "Acquiring knowledge is incumbent upon every man and woman." By this, he meant knowledge of one's own soul. Therefore it is incumbent upon a person to exist either in a state of constant gratitude or in readiness to make excuse for his ingratitude. Should Allah accept his gratitude, it is a great kindness from Allah; should He not accept it, he shall be held answerable for his ingratitude and lack of excuse.

It is incumbent upon every soul to strive earnestly in obedience to Allah and to protect itself from that which harms it. This striving rests upon three things:

| 1 | Complete reliance upon Allah and entire dependence upon Him |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Piety and God-consciousness |
| 3 | Obedience to His commandments |

The key to this success is that one:

| 1 | Entrust all affairs to Allah |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Break hope through remembrance of death |
| 3 | Remain ever mindful that before a tyrant one is helpless |

Through these practices come liberation from captivity, protection from the enemy (Satan), and peace of soul. Pure obedience with perfect harmony arises only when one establishes the notion that life endures but for one day. The Prophet ⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ said: "The span of this world is but one hour; spend it in obedience to Allah."

The manner of living is that one distance himself from the world through constant contemplation. Yet distance from the world comes only through contentment and disdain for trivial matters. Contemplation arises from emptiness; emptiness from piety; the fulfillment of piety from fear; and fear is the gateway to piety.

Know that all things have their root in "fear," and the proof of this fear is evident in magnifying Allah's greatness, in pure obedience to Him, in caution and protection from the unlawful. And the leader of all these is "knowledge." Allah says—and His word is true:

> ⟪quran 35:28⟫

## Gratitude

It is incumbent upon a person to give thanks to Allah with every breath. Indeed! A thousand thanks, and more. The lowest degree of gratitude is that one understand every blessing to come from Allah alone, regardless of the means, and without attachment of heart to those means, be grateful to Allah alone, and be satisfied with what Allah has bestowed. Do not bless the unbelievers by opposing His commandments.

Be grateful servants of Allah in every way, and you shall witness Allah as the Most Generous of all things. Had there been any worship greater than gratitude, Allah would have mentioned it before all others. Since there is no worship exceeding gratitude, He elevated the grateful in this verse, saying:

> ⟪quran 27:15⟫

True gratitude is expressed when a person sincerely seeks Allah's forgiveness for his incapacity to be grateful for even one of Allah's blessings. Therefore it becomes incumbent upon him to recite Allah's glorification (*tasbīḥ*), praise (*taḥmīd*), and thanksgiving (*tahmīd*).[^cite-9]

The very faculty of gratitude itself is a supreme bounty that Allah bestows upon His blessed servants, making gratitude for this grace obligatory! And the level of gratitude for this grace exceeds gratitude for the initial blessing. Thus for every gratitude there cycles an eternal gratitude. How can a weak and helpless creature render true gratitude for Allah's blessings? How can the weight of one's gratitude equal the weight of Allah's bounty?

Allah has no need of His servants' obedience and gratitude, for if He wills, He possesses the power to increase His blessings forever. Therefore be a grateful servant of Allah and witness His wonders.

## Departing from Home

Step from your house as one who knows he shall never return to it. Go forth only for obedience to Allah and fulfillment of faith, proceeding toward your destination in tranquility and dignity, remembering Allah in secret and aloud as you walk.

⟪ar:حضرت ابوذر غفاری⟫: One of his companions came to his dwelling and asked his family where Abū Dharr had gone. They replied: "He has gone out."[^cite-10]

Wherever you journey, learn from Allah's creation, whether the righteous or the straying. Beseech Allah to make you among His sincere servants and to number you among the righteous who have passed before you. Praise Him for it and be grateful to Him for sparing you the desires of the wrongdoers and the wickedness from which He has saved you. As you walk, lower your gaze from all bodily pleasures, forbidden and suspicious things, and fix your purpose. Always beware of Satan. Fear Allah at every step as if crossing a narrow bridge. Greet Allah's creatures along the way; if they greet you, return their greeting. Give aid to those who ask you for help in righteous deeds; guide those who have gone astray; overlook the ignorant.

When you return home having accomplished your purpose, enter as a corpse enters the grave—seeking only Allah's mercy and forgiveness.

## Sleep

Seek sleep not as the heedless do, but as the conscious do. The most discerning of the wise are those who sleep not from deliberate idleness and sloth, but to gain rest. ⟪ar:رسول اکرم (ﷺ)⟫ said: "My eyes sleep, but my heart remains ever wakeful."

Sleep with the intention that you lighten your burden upon the angels and sever yourself from base desires. Draw a lesson from your sleep for your life. Reflect upon your weakness and helplessness in sleep. Consider that while sleeping you have no mastery over your movements, and you are wholly dependent upon Allah's justice and will. Know that sleep is the sister of death, and use it as a guide to death, for there is no awakening after death to return to righteous action. He who sleeps while obligatory or Sunnah prayer is due errs greatly; his sleep is the sleep of the heedless, and he walks the path of the losers. Yet he whose sleep comes after rising to fulfill his duties and the worship of the Sunnah—his sleep is praiseworthy.

I know of nothing safer in these times for those who have acquired such qualities than sleep, for people have abandoned guarding their faith and neglected their moral discipline. ⟪ar:اصحاب یسار⟫ have taken their path. A person who strives not to speak when compelled to speak lest he defend himself—should sleep not be a shield for such a one? Allah says—and He speaks truth:

> ⟪quran 25:47⟫

Yet there are many evils hidden within excessive sleep, even when practiced as we have described. Excessive sleep comes from excessive drinking, and excessive thirst from eating to fullness. These two burden the soul, which obstructs obedience to Allah, hardens the heart, and distances the servant from Allah's remembrance and humility.

Make sleep the last of your worldly concerns, and remember Allah in your heart and upon your tongue. Let obedience to Allah overcome your sins, and seek His aid while you sleep. Guard yourself while sleeping, for when your eyes open in the middle of the night, Satan tells you: "Sleep still, much night remains!" He seeks to waste your time before Allah and your contemplation. When you wake, do not neglect to ask Allah's forgiveness, for at the time of dawn (*fajr*) there is great purity in worship and seeking forgiveness.

## Remembrance of Allah

Only those who obey His commands remember Allah; whoever forgets Him is disobedient. The sign of guidance is obedience; the sign of error is disobedience. The root of both is "remembrance of Allah." Make your heart the seat of your tongue's discourse. Do not let your tongue move with certainty until the heart assents and intellect agrees. Indeed, Allah knows what you reveal and what you conceal.

Be as one who has removed his soul from his body, or as one who stands alone in the throng of the Day of Judgment. Such a person is not heedless of Allah's obligations, commandments, and promises. Wash your heart with the waters of remorse and fear.

Make remembrance of Allah a wondrous part of your thoughts, even as Allah remembers you at all times. Allah remembers you though He has no need of you. Allah's remembrance of you is far more glorious, far more exalted, far more praiseworthy, far more complete, and far more ancient than your remembrance of Him.

The knowledge gained from remembering Allah and His remembrance of you will instill humility, modesty, and remorse within you, through which you shall witness the nobility of Allah and His infinite blessings. Beholding these great blessings, your worship, however vast, shall seem despicable in your own eyes, and you shall be sincerely submissive.

But if you are conscious of your remembrance and the abundance of your worship, this awareness shall lead you toward pretense, arrogance, ignorance, and depravity of character. Such consciousness proves that you regard your worship and obedience as great, forgetting Allah's countless blessings and generosity in comparison. This thought shall drive you from Allah with each passing day and increase your alienation from Him.

There are two types of remembrance (*dhikr*, the remembrance of Allah): the first is that in which a person remembers Allah with sincerity and harmony of heart. The second is that in which he forgets the remembrance of all save Allah.

⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ said: "O Allah, I cannot render You the praise You deserve, for You praise Yourself more perfectly than any praise I can give."[^cite-11] For this reason ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ placed no limit upon praising Allah, knowing that the more a person remembers and praises Allah, it remains less than Allah's remembrance of His servants. Therefore it is more fitting for those after the Messenger that they place no limits upon remembrance of Allah and His praise. Yet let the servant always remember that he cannot remember Allah until Allah Himself remembers him first and grants him the capacity to remember.

## Satanic Whispering

Satan gains dominion over Allah's servants only when they have abandoned His remembrance, grown disdainful when faced with His prohibition, felt complacent in their disobedience, and forgotten that Allah witnesses their secret acts. The whisper (*waswāsa*) is that which enters the heart from without, by the tacit permission of reason, and is sustained by man's own nature. Once established in the heart, it leads to error, misguidance, and disbelief. Allah called upon His servants with His subtle summons and informed them of Iblis's enmity:

> ⟪quran 36:60⟫

Stand against Satan as one standing near a shepherd's hound would appeal to the hound's master to keep it away. Similarly, when Satan comes to whisper and lead you from truth and cause you to forget Allah, seek refuge with your Lord and his Lord. He shall defend the truth against falsehood and aid the wronged, as He says:

> ⟪quran 22:38⟫[^cite-12]

A person will only accomplish this if he knows how Satan approaches and can recognize his methods of whispering through constant vigilance, sincerity in service, awe of the All-Knowing, and increased remembrance of Him.

Yet whoever neglects to spend his time in watchfulness shall surely be Satan's prey. He should learn the lesson from what Satan does with such a soul: he leads it to misguidance, deception, and haughtiness by deceiving the person into admiring his own actions, his worship, and his discernment.

Satan's insolent treatment of him brings a curse upon his knowledge, his gnosis, and his faculty of reason for all eternity; yet he has no power over those who are not heedless. Therefore, hold fast to the firmest rope of Allah, which is to seek refuge with Allah and maintain a sincere need for Him with every breath. Do not be deceived when Satan beautifies your acts of obedience before your eyes. Should he open ninety-nine doors of good for you, it is only that he might prevail over you through the hundredth. So meet him with opposition, block his way, and reject his allure.

## Certainty of Faith

Faith conducts a person to every noble station and glorious place. The Prophet of Allah ⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ explained the magnitude of faith, saying that ⟪ar:عیسی علیہ السلام⟫ walked upon water because of his faith. He further declared: "Had their faith been stronger, they would have walked upon the wind." By this, he indicated that all the prophets, despite their greatness, possess different degrees of faith in Allah Almighty.

The glory of faith is that it grows and continues growing eternally. Believers differ in strength or weakness according to their faith. The sign of strong faith is that a person finds no power within himself except what Allah has granted him. He obeys Allah's commandments and adheres to His outward and inward worship. Whether Allah brings him near or holds him distant, increases or decreases his provision, praises or insults him, renders him strong or weak—all remain equal in his eyes, for he sees all equally. Conversely, the weak in faith attach themselves to external affairs and leave their souls unbridled. They accept people's customs and sayings without verification, accumulate worldly wealth, regard it, and affirm it with tongue and heart.

There is none to withhold or grant except Allah, and none can obtain save what has been destined for him. Man's striving cannot increase his sustenance, yet he denies this with his tongue and heart. Allah says—and His word is truth:

> They say with their mouths what is not in their hearts.

Allah has shown kindness to His servants by permitting them to earn, provided they transgress not His limits, do not abandon their duties to Him, do not deviate from the Prophet's way, do not breach their trust in Him, and do not fall into greed's trap. Yet when they forget this and indulge in the forbidden, they shall be among those destroyed on the Day of Resurrection save for a false boast.

Not every earner is trustworthy, for he may obtain unlawful or doubtful things. Such a person is known by the effects of his earnings: his greed for wealth never diminishes, and he expends much for the sake of this world.

He who is permitted to earn, though his body labors, keeps his heart in trust toward Allah. Should he accumulate much wealth, he is as a trustee who knows that having or lacking wealth are equal. If he withholds, he withholds for Allah's sake; if he spends, he spends according to Allah's command. Both his actions are for Allah's sake.[^cite-13]

## Contentment

Contentment is a state wherein a person is satisfied with all that he loves and accepts all that he hates. Contentment is a ray of the light of knowledge. The contented one is he who has extinguished all his desires, and such a person is truly pleased with Allah.

Contentment is a name ⟪ar:أسم⟫ for worship ⟪ar:عبدیت⟫, and it is the joy of the heart's ⟪ar:صفت⟫ attribute.

I have heard from my father ⟪ar:محمد باقر علیہ السلام⟫ that attachment to something present is a kind of association of partners with Allah (*shirk*), while attachment to something absent is disbelief (*kufr*). These are two forms of heedlessness. I marvel at the one who claims to be Allah's servant ⟪ar:عبد⟫ and then contends against His decree. The contented among the wise are far from such contention.

## Love of Allah

In general, man's perception of Allah is that He is the Mighty and the Overpowering, the Punisher of disbelievers, polytheists, and the disobedient. For this reason, most people worship Allah out of fear and dread, obeying Him from fear that if they do not, He will seize them with punishment. Although it is true that Allah possesses the authority of the Day of Judgment and punishment, Allah has identified Himself not as the Mighty and the Overpowering but as the Most Merciful. For this reason, He begins His great Book with the verse: ⟪ar-quote:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫ "In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Compassionate."

Had He wished, He could have said: ⟪ar:بسم اللہ الجبار القہار⟫ "In the name of Allah, the Mighty, the Overpowering." But He chose to identify Himself by the attributes of mercy and compassion, for He had determined that in all His dealings with creation, He would act with mercy. Therefore let us obey Him and worship Him out of love rather than fear.

If Allah desired oppression and wrath, no creature in the universe could prevent it. His servants constantly disobey Him, and this should provoke the anger of all creatures. Allah (may He be glorified) says—and His word is absolute truth:

**⟪ar:مَا مِن یَوم اِلاَّ والبَحرُ  یَستَئذِنُ رَبَّہُ فی اَن یورِقُ ابن آدم۔  والارضُ یَستَئذِنُ فی أَن تَبتَلِعَہُ۔ والملائکۃ یَستَئذِنُ فی ان تُعاَجِلَہُ وَ طُولِکَہ⟫**

Not a day passes but the sea asks permission of its Lord to drown the children of Adam. The earth asks permission to swallow them. The angels ask permission to hasten their punishment and prolong their torment.[^cite-14]

Water, earth, and angels are creatures like ourselves, yet they burn with desire to punish our disobedience. But He who is disobeyed responds to them:

⟪ar:تَعُو عَبدِی فأنا أَعلَمُ بِعَبدِی مِنکُم ۔ ان کان عبدَکم فَشَأنَکم بِہِ۔ وأن کان عبدی فَمِنِّی وأِلیَّ عبدی⟫

"Leave My servant alone, for I know My servant better than you. If he is your servant, then do with him as you wish. But if he is My servant, then hand him over to Me, and let him be My concern."

**⟪ar:یسأل الملائکۃ، فلماذا عقدُہُ؟ یستجیب اللہ، إنني أتطَّلِع إلی التوبتَہُ⟫**

The angels ask: "Why do You withhold from him?" Allah replies: "I am awaiting his repentance."[^cite-15]

**⟪ar:أِن اتانی نھاراً قبلتہ أِن اتانی لیلا قبلتہ⟫**

Whether he comes to Me repenting by day or by night, I shall accept his repentance. I shall not hear from him any word of complaint or reproach.[^cite-16]

**⟪ar:مَن تَقَرَّبَ أِلَیَّ تَلَقَّیتُہُ مِن بَعِید وَ مَن أَعرَضَ عَنِّی ناَدَیتُہُ عَن قَرِیب⟫**

Whoever draws near to Me, I advance to meet him from afar; whoever turns away from Me, I call out to him from near.[^cite-17]

**⟪ar:أَوَلَکَ رَبٌّ غَیرِی؟ الحَسَنَۃُ عِندِی بِعَشرِأَمثَالِھَا وَ أَزِید۔ وَوَصَّیَّتُ عِندِی بِمِثلِھَا وأَعفُو و أغْفَر⟫**

Have you found any lord but Me? With Me, a good deed is recorded as ten of its like, and I increase beyond this. A sin is recorded as one of its like, and I forgive whenever I will.[^cite-18]
