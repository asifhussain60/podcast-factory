## Servitude

All matters rest upon four fundamental aspects, each of which itself depends upon seven principles. This formulation accords with the twenty-eight limits contained within the twenty-eight principles that derive from the declaration "La ilaha illa Allah" (There is no god save Allah).

**Seven Principles of Relationship with Allah:**

| 1 | Fulfilling all His rights upon the servant |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Adhering steadfastly to His prescribed limits |
| 3 | Rendering gratitude for His blessings |
| 4 | Accepting His judgment and decree with submission |
| 5 | Demonstrating patience in the face of His tests |
| 6 | Magnifying His sanctity and glory |
| 7 | Yearning earnestly for His pleasure |

**Seven Principles of Relationship with Oneself:**

| 1 | Fear of Allah (*taqwa*, reverence and God-consciousness) |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Striving with resolve (*ijtihād*, rigorous spiritual exertion) |
| 3 | Enduring suffering upon the path of truth |
| 4 | Establishing spiritual and moral discipline |
| 5 | Seeking truth and sincerity with unwavering commitment |
| 6 | Restraining the self from attachment to worldly desires |
| 7 | Binding the self to poverty and detachment from material wealth |

**Seven Principles of Relationship with Others:**

| 1 | Exercising patience in dealings |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Forgiving the transgressions of others |
| 3 | Cultivating humility in one's station |
| 4 | Practicing generosity toward others |
| 5 | Treating all with compassion and mercy |
| 6 | Offering counsel that is sincere and true |
| 7 | Conducting oneself with justice and fairness |

**Seven Principles of Relationship with the World:**

| 1 | Contentment with what Allah has bestowed |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Preferring the immediate duty to distant concerns |
| 3 | Renouncing struggle for unattainable goods |
| 4 | Cultivating dislike for excessive worldly attachments |
| 5 | Embodying piety (*wara'*, scrupulousness and spiritual caution) in all dealings |
| 6 | Recognizing the corruption of worldly desires and abandoning them |
| 7 | Negating the dominion that the world holds over the soul |

When all these qualities converge within a person, he becomes numbered among the noble ones, the sincere worshippers, and the saints (*awliyā'*) of Allah.[^cite-1]

Servitude is an essence whose inner reality is lordship; without "Lord" there can be no "Servant" (*ʿAbd*). Every attribute from which obedience is absent finds its counterpart in lordship, and every attribute concealed within lordship reveals itself in servitude. Thus all the essences of the Servant are encompassed within the Lord, yet not a single attribute of the Lord inheres in the Servant.

Allah declares (in the meaning of revelation):

>   (image preserved at .. /images/002.png)

This means that regardless of the Servant's state—whether he exists or passes away—the Lord remains eternally. The essence of obedience (*ṭāʿa*) is the liberation of the self from all desires and base inclinations. The path to this liberation consists in forbidding the self to acquire whatever it craves, while persuading it to endure whatever it abhors. The very substance of this practice is that one should abandon comfort and ease, seek solitude for contemplation, and dedicate oneself to the knowledge of Allah.[^cite-2]

The Messenger of Allah (upon whom be peace and blessings) instructed: "Worship Allah as though you perceive Him; and if you cannot attain such perception, then worship Him as though He perceives you."

The word ʿAbd (Servant) contains three letters, each bearing profound significance:

| Letter | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| ʿAyn | Signifies "ʿilm" (knowledge of Allah) |
| Bāʾ | Denotes "baʿīd" (distance—separation from all creatures) |
| Dāl | Represents "danī" (proximity—closeness to Allah without self-manifestation) |

## Lowering the Gaze

Nothing benefits a person more profoundly than lowering his gaze from that which is forbidden. Yet the eyes cannot be turned away from transgression until the glory and majesty of Allah become deeply embedded within the heart.

⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ When someone asked him, "What enables me to lower my gaze?" the Commander of the Faithful replied: "Surrender yourself entirely to the power and might of the One who knows all your secrets—He who observes your every glance, who is the spy upon your heart, and who conveys all of this to your intellect. Therefore, turn your eyes away from whatever your faith forbids and from whatever your heart despises and your intellect rejects."

⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ The Prophet declared, "If you lower your gaze, you shall witness wonders."

⟪ar:عیسی علیہ السلام⟫ Jesus, upon whom be peace, said to his disciples: "I counsel you not to gaze upon the forbidden, for such gazes sow the seeds of desire within the heart and lead a person toward waywardness."

⟪ar:یحیا علیہ السلام⟫ John the Baptist said, "I prefer death to a gaze that serves no purpose!"

⟪ar:حضرت عبد اﷲ ابن مسعود⟫ To a man returning from visiting a woman who had fallen ill, ʿAbdullah ibn Masʿūd remarked, "It would have been better for you had you become blind than to have witnessed what you have witnessed."

When the gaze alights upon the forbidden, a knot of desire binds itself within the heart. This knot can be loosened only through one of two means:

| 1 | Shedding tears of sincere repentance while lamenting one's transgression |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Acquiring possession of the object or desire toward which the gaze was directed |

Whoever seizes the forbidden desire upon which his eye had fixed through cruelty and arrogance will find that object becomes the instrument of his torment in Hellfire. Conversely, whoever offers sincere repentance to Allah, accompanied by tears of sorrow and remorse, shall dwell in Paradise and receive from Allah an abundance of blessings beyond measure.[^cite-3]

## Walking the Path

If you possess intelligence, you should undertake your journey with firm resolve and pure intention. This is essential, for it is the nature of the human soul to transgress and to be drawn toward the forbidden. As you walk, observe and reflect upon the wonders of Allah's creation that surround you on every side. Yet walk not with arrogance or haughtiness, for Allah has declared (in the meaning of revelation):

## Knowledge

Knowledge is the beginning of every glorious state and the culmination of every highest perfection. For this reason did the Messenger of Allah (upon whom be peace and blessings) declare, "It is incumbent upon every man and woman to acquire knowledge." He further said: ⟪ar-quote:مَن عَرَفَ نَفسُہُ فَقَد عَرَفَ رَبُّہُ⟫ "Whosoever knows his own self knows his Lord." He commanded also: "You must acquire knowledge in order to perfect sincerity in your actions; for without such sincerity, every action becomes corrupt and without benefit." We seek refuge in Allah from knowledge that does not sanctify the soul and does not translate into righteous action.

Know well that the knowledge one acquires in but an hour demands that one practice it throughout the entirety of one's remaining life. Jesus, upon whom be peace, said: "I once saw a stone upon which was inscribed, 'Turn me over!' When I turned it, I found written upon its reverse: 'Whosoever does not act upon his knowledge shall be punished by perpetual seeking after knowledge to dispel his doubts, and his very learning shall turn against him.'"[^cite-4]

Allah said to David, upon whom be peace: "The least punishment I shall inflict upon one who does not act according to My knowledge shall be worse than seventy inward anguishes that derive from the sweetness of My remembrance." There is no path to Allah save through knowledge. Knowledge is man's adornment in this world, his guide toward the Hereafter, and the means by which he attains the pleasure of Allah.

A true scholar is one whose actions accord with his learning and whose prayers spring from sincerity. Not his eloquence, his argumentation, his speech, his analogical reasoning, or his claims—but rather his every deed bears witness to truth and piety. In former ages, those possessed of wisdom, piety, and cautiousness strove earnestly for knowledge. Yet in our time we see that those who pursue knowledge are often devoid of these very qualities.

**Eight Qualities That Should Reside in the Scholar:**

| Intellect (*ʿaql*) | Sound reasoning and discernment |
| --- | --- |
| Kindness (*riqqat qalb*) | Tenderness of heart and compassion |
| Sympathy (*hamm*) | Deep concern for others |
| Good Counsel (*nasīḥa*) | Sincere guidance and advice |
| Forbearance (*hilm*) | Patience and clemency in adversity |
| Clemency (*hilm*) | Restraint from hasty judgment |
| Contentment (*qanaʿa*) | Satisfaction with what suffices |
| Generosity (*jūd*) | Open-handedness and magnanimity |

**Seven Qualities That Should Reside in the Student:**

| Longing (*raghba*) | Earnest desire for knowledge |
| --- | --- |
| Determination (*ʿazm*) | Strength of will and resolve |
| Devotion (*diyān*) | Sincerity in pursuit of learning |
| Piety (*taqwa*) | Reverence and spiritual caution |
| Carefulness (*ḥadhr*) | Vigilance against error and delusion |
| Memory (*dhikr*) | Retention and recollection of teachings |
| Resolve (*ʿazīma*) | Fortitude in overcoming obstacles |

## Making Judgment

It is permissible for one to render judgment only if he possesses these requisite qualities:

| Inner Purity and Cleanliness of Soul |
| --- |
| Sincerity in both Outward and Inward Actions |
| Clear Proof from His Lord (*Bayyina min Rabbih*) |

This is because whoever renders judgment issues a decree, and a decree may be issued only with the permission of Allah and His evidence. Whoever decides without thorough examination of the circumstances, or through heedlessness and negligence, is ignorant, and shall be seized by his ignorance and burned by his judgment. The knowledge of rendering judgment aright is a light that Allah kindles only within the hearts of His chosen servants.[^cite-5]

The Messenger of Allah (upon whom be peace and blessings) declared, "Among you, the one most audacious in rendering judgment against another is the most loathsome to Allah." Does not the judge consider that he stands as a barrier between Allah and His servant, suspended between Paradise and Hell?

Sufyan ibn ʿAynah remarked, "How can another benefit from my knowledge when I have forbidden it to myself?" (meaning he has failed to benefit from it himself). None among Allah's creatures has the right to judge between lawful and unlawful except he who guides the people of his time, his locality, or his city toward truth through the teachings of the Messenger of Allah, and who understands the scope of his judgment's application. The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said, "Judgment is a grave matter in which there is no room for words such as 'perhaps,' 'maybe,' or 'possibly.'"

Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī (upon whom be peace) said to a judge: "Do you know the distinction between verses of the Qur'an that are abrogated and those that abrogate?" He replied, "No." He asked further: "Do you possess complete mastery of the will of Allah concealed within the inner meanings and interpretations of the Qur'anic narratives?" He answered, "No." Then ʿAlī declared: "You have destroyed yourself and led others to destruction."

It is reported that Imam al-Bāqir (upon whom be peace) said: "The person who renders judgment or establishes a ruling must know all of these matters:

| 1 | All the verses of the Qur'an |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | The reality of the Prophets' circumstances and their inner marks, courtesies, and ordinances |
| 3 | The principles upon which the learned agree and those upon which they differ |

And these four qualities must be manifest in the person who renders judgment:

| Strength of Proof | Firmness of Process | Wisdom | Piety |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Commanding the Good and Forbidding Evil

He alone has the right to command what is good and forbid what is evil—he who has abandoned all his preoccupations, purified himself of his own vices and base desires, vanquished Satan, and entered into the protection and guardianship of Allah. Should someone attempt to command good or forbid evil without first acquiring these qualities, his own past actions will become an argument against him, and people will be unable to benefit from his counsel. Allah declares in the Qur'an:

The Prophet answered: "Command people to righteousness and forbid them from wrongdoing, and bear with patience whatever calamity befalls you in the course of this duty. Be steadfast with them. But so long as you perceive that people are pursuing evil, enslaved to selfish desire, and drowning in arrogance, withdraw from them and attend to your own work."

He who commands what is good and forbids what is evil must first discern what is lawful and what is forbidden. His counsel must not be colored by personal inclination. He should be a sincere advocate for his people, kind and tenderhearted. He must understand the varying temperaments of those he addresses and invite them to truth with gentle and lucid arguments, so that all may benefit from his counsel. He must be vigilant against the desires of the self and the stratagems of Satan. Let him be patient in the face of harm; let him not demand recompense from those to whom he offers advice, nor complain to them. Let him not be harsh or prone to anger. His indignation should arise for Allah's sake alone, and he should seek Allah's aid.

If people respond to him with harshness or opposition, he should meet it with patience. And if they accept his counsel, let him give thanks to Allah, entrust all his affairs to Him, and keep ever watchful of his own faults, lest he fall prey to self-admiration.[^cite-6]

## The Ruin of Scholars

Piety and fear of Allah are the inheritance of knowledge and its true measure. Knowledge is a ray emanating from the light of divine wisdom and the essence of faith. A scholar who renounces piety (*taqwa*) cannot be called a scholar, even though he may possess mastery of the most difficult sciences.

Scholars are ruined through eight things:

| 1 | Lust and avarice |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Pretense and prejudgment |
| 3 | Rejoicing in self-praise |
| 4 | Investigating matters beyond their capacity to understand |
| 5 | Exerting excessive effort to make their speech persuasive |
| 6 | Shamelessness before Allah |
| 7 | Arrogance and haughtiness |
| 8 | Failure to act upon their knowledge |

Jesus, upon whom be peace, declared, "The most despised of all creatures is one whose deeds do not accord with his knowledge—one who possesses learning yet does not practice it."

⟪ar:رسول اﷲ (ﷺ)⟫ The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said, "Do not sit in the company of those who lack vigilance. Such persons will invite you away from certainty to doubt, from sincerity to ostentation, from humility to pride, from sincere counsel to enmity, and from piety to base desire. Yet draw near to the world—nay, draw near to one who has transcended all worldly things—and he will guide you from pride to humility, from ostentation to sincerity, from doubt to faith, away from base desire, and from enmity to sincere counsel." No one among all creatures has the right to teach knowledge save one who has transcended all evils through his righteousness, who is aware of the errors concealed in Scripture, and who understands the difference between truth and delusion, between the disadvantages of conjecture and the seduction of carnal desire.

⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنین علی علیہ السلام⟫ ʿAlī, upon whom be peace, said: "Be like the merciful physician who places the remedy where the ailment resides and where it will bring greatest benefit."

⟪ar:عیسیٰ علیہ السلام⟫ When someone asked Jesus, upon whom be peace, "O Spirit of Allah, with whom should we keep company?" He replied, "With he whose very existence reminds you of Allah, whose words increase your knowledge, and whose deeds make you yearn for the Hereafter."[^cite-7]

## Guarding the Self

He who guards his heart from the heedlessness that clouds it, his intellect from the ignorance that obscures it, and his soul from the pleasures that corrupt it—he has joined the ranks of the cautious and the watchful. Whoever protects his faith from innovations so that his heart remains sound, his knowledge from conjecture and sophistry so that his intellect remains clear, and his wealth from unlawful things so that his soul remains free from base desires—he is among the righteous.

The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said, "It is incumbent upon every man and woman to acquire knowledge"—meaning the knowledge of one's own self. Therefore it is obligatory for each person to exist in one of two states: either in a state of constant gratitude, or prepared always with an excuse for his lack of gratitude. Should Allah accept his gratitude, it is a tremendous favor from Him; should He not accept it, the person bears responsibility for his ingratitude and absence of excuse.

It is incumbent upon every soul to strive in obedience to Allah and to protect itself from acts that corrupt it. This striving is rooted in three foundations:

| 1 | Complete reliance upon Allah and absolute dependence on Him |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Piety (*taqwa*) and reverence |
| 3 | Obedience (*ṭāʿa*) to His commands |

The key to success in this endeavor lies in these practices:

| 1 | Entrusting all one's affairs entirely to Allah |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Breaking one's hopes through remembrance of death |
| 3 | Constantly recognizing one's helplessness in the face of the Powerful One |

Through such discipline, one attains liberation from bondage, protection from the enemy (Satan), and peace for the soul. Pure obedience coupled with perfect sincerity arises only when one establishes the understanding that life endures but for a single day. The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said, "The span of this world is but one hour; spend it in obedience to Allah."

The method of detachment from the world lies in constant contemplation. And there can be no distance from worldly attachment except through contentment and disdain for transient matters. Contemplation springs from emptiness of heart; emptiness arises from piety; the perfection of piety is reverence; and reverence is the gateway to piety.

Know that the root of all virtue is fear of Allah, and the evidence of this fear appears in acts of magnifying Allah, in pure obedience, in caution and protection from the unlawful. And the leader and master of all these is knowledge itself. Allah declares (in the meaning of revelation):

## Gratitude

It is obligatory for a person to give thanks to Allah with every breath—a thousand times, and more besides. The lowest level of gratitude is that one should recognize that every blessing comes from Allah alone, regardless of the means through which it arrives; without attaching one's heart to that means, one should render gratitude to Allah alone and be content with what He has bestowed. Do not curse the blessing by acting in opposition to His commands.

Be grateful servants of Allah in all ways, and you shall witness that Allah is the most munificent of all things. Had there been any path to worship other than gratitude, Allah would have established it before all other acts of devotion. Since there is no worship superior to gratitude, He has exalted the grateful servants in this verse:

> — *Image text (from image 011; citation unverified)*
>   (image preserved at .. /images/011.png)
>   *Note:* AUTONOMOUS_STUB — placeholder sidecar. The chapter is sealed with needs_human_review=true. Human reviewer to revisit and replace with a real classification.

True gratitude is expressed when a person sincerely asks Allah's forgiveness for his inability to render adequate thanks for any single one of His blessings. Therefore it becomes obligatory to recite Allah's magnification, praise, and glorification.[^cite-8]

The very capacity for gratitude is itself a tremendous blessing that Allah bestows upon His chosen servants, for which they must in turn be grateful! And the degree of gratitude owed for this grace exceeds the gratitude owed for the initial blessing. Thus for every act of gratitude arises an eternal cycle of further gratitude. How can a weak and helpless mortal render true thanks for Allah's blessings? How can the weight of one's gratitude ever equal the weight of Allah's mercy?

Allah does not stand in need of the obedience and gratitude of His servants, for if He willed, He could increase His blessings eternally. So be a grateful servant of Allah, and you shall witness His wonders manifest before you.

## Departing from Home

Leave your house as one who knows he shall never return to it again. Depart only for the sake of obedience to Allah and the fulfillment of your faith, proceeding toward your destination with peace and dignity, remembering Allah both secretly and aloud as you walk.

⟪ar:حضرت ابوذر غفاری⟫ When one of his companions came to the house of Abū Dharr al-Ghifārī seeking him, his family replied, "He has gone out."

Wherever you journey, learn from Allah's creation—whether they are the righteous or those who have strayed. Pray that Allah make you among His sincere servants and join you with the righteous who preceded you. Offer praise for this and gratitude to Him for the desires of the wrongdoers and the wickedness from which He has protected you. As you walk, lower your gaze from all carnal pleasures, forbidden things, and temptations, maintaining focus on your journey's purpose. Ever beware of Satan! Fear Allah at every step as though you were crossing a perilous bridge.

Be among the first to greet Allah's creatures whom you encounter; if they greet you, return the greeting. Give to those who seek your aid in righteous deeds; guide those who have gone astray; and bear with patience the ignorant.

When you return home after accomplishing your purpose, enter it as a corpse enters a grave—with the sole intention of seeking Allah's mercy and forgiveness.[^cite-9]

Take your sleep not as the heedless do, but as one who is conscious and watchful. The wisest among the conscious are those who sleep not from intentional idleness and sloth, but to obtain needful rest. ⟪ar:رسول اکرم (ﷺ)⟫ The Noble Messenger declared, "My eyes sleep, but my heart remains ever vigilant."

Sleep with the intention that you are easing the burden upon the angels and severing your self from its desires. Draw a lesson for your life from sleep. Reflect upon your weakness and helplessness during slumber. Consider that as you sleep, you exercise no control over your movements; you are entirely dependent upon the justice and will of Allah. Know that sleep is the sister of death, and let it be your guide toward death—for there is no returning from death to righteous action. A person who sleeps at the time of obligatory or recommended prayer errs grievously; his sleep is the sleep of the heedless, and he walks upon the path of the losers. But he who sleeps after completing his duties and the practices of the Sunna—his sleep is praiseworthy.

I know of nothing more secure for one who has cultivated these qualities than sleep in this age. For people have abandoned the safeguarding of their faith and have ceased to tend their morals. ⟪ar:اصحاب یسار⟫ They have taken the path of those who reject. A person who exerts himself not to speak when compelled to speak—should he not defend himself by remaining silent? Sleep serves such a person as a shield. Allah declares (in the meaning of revelation):

Yet many evils lurk within excessive sleep, even when attained in the manner we have described. Excessive sleep results from excessive drinking; excessive thirst results from fullness of stomach. These two burdens weigh upon the soul, preventing the servant from obeying Allah, hardening the heart, and distancing the servant from the remembrance of Allah and from humility before Him.

Make sleep the final matter of your worldly affairs, and remember Allah with your heart and with your tongue. Let obedience to Allah predominate over your sins, and seek His help even as you sleep. Guard against sleep, because when your eyes open during the night, Satan whispers to you, "There is still much of the night remaining!"—wanting to waste your time in heedlessness and rob you of contemplation. When you awaken, hasten to ask Allah's forgiveness, for the time of the dawn prayer (*Fajr*) is rich with sincere worship and the seeking of pardon.[^cite-10]

## Remembrance of Allah

Only those who obey His commands remember Allah truly; whoever forgets Him is disobedient. The mark of guidance is obedience, while the mark of error is disobedience. The origin of both is remembrance of Allah (*dhikr Allāh*). Make your heart the center of your remembrance. Let not your tongue move with certainty until the heart points the way and the intellect gives consent. Indeed, Allah knows what you reveal and what you conceal.

Be as one who has extracted his soul from his body, or as one standing alone in the multitude of the Day of Judgment. Such a person is never heedless of Allah's obligations, His commands, and His promises. Wash your heart with the waters of remorse and fear.

Make the remembrance of Allah a wondrous part of your thoughts, just as Allah remembers you at all times. Allah remembers you, though He has no need of you; and thus His remembrance of you is far more glorious, far more exalted, far more praiseworthy, far more complete, and far more ancient than your remembrance of Him.

The knowledge gained through remembrance of Allah and His remembrance of you shall instill within you humility, modesty, and remorse. Through these you shall witness the nobility of Allah and His infinite blessings. When you behold these great mercies, your own worship, however grand, shall appear despicable in your eyes, and you shall offer yourself in sincere submission to Him.

But if you become conscious of your remembrance and the abundance of your acts of worship, this awareness shall lead you toward ostentation, arrogance, ignorance, and depravity of character. Such thinking proves that you have granted importance to your own devotion and obedience, forgetting the countless blessings and generosity of Allah in comparison. This thought shall drive you daily further from Allah and increase your separation from Him.

There are two types of remembrance (*dhikr*): the first is that in which a person remembers Allah with sincerity and consonance of heart. The second is that in which one forgets the remembrance of all save Allah.

⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ As the Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said, "O Allah, I cannot render Thee the praise that is Thy due; Thou alone art Thy perfect praise." This is why ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the Messenger set no limit upon the praise of Allah, for he knew that the more one remembers Allah and is remembered by Him, it remains less than the remembrance Allah bestows upon His servants. Therefore, it is fitting for those who come after the Messenger that they too set no limit upon the remembrance of Allah. Yet the servant must always bear in mind that he cannot remember Allah until Allah Himself remembers him first and grants him the ability to do so.[^cite-11]

## Certainty of Faith

Faith leads a person toward every exalted station and glorious rank. The Prophet of Allah (upon whom be peace and blessings) ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی علیہ السلام⟫ explained the greatness of faith, saying: ⟪ar:عیسی علیہ السلام⟫ "Through his faith, Jesus walked upon water." And he continued, "Had their faith been stronger still, they would have walked upon the wind." Through this statement he indicated that all the prophets, despite their excellence, possess differing degrees of faith in Allah Almighty.

The glory of faith is that it grows perpetually, ever increasing without cease. The strength or weakness of believers derives from the measure of their faith. The mark of one whose faith is strong is that he finds no power within himself save that which Allah has granted him. He obeys the commands of Allah and fulfills His obligations both outwardly and inwardly. Whether He draws him near or removes him far, whether He increases or decreases for him, whether He praises him or disparages him, whether He strengthens or weakens him—all are equal in his sight, for he sees all these things on one level. On the other hand, one whose faith is weak surrenders himself to external circumstances and leaves his soul unbridled in them. He accepts the customs and sayings of people without scrutiny, accumulates the wealth of the world, fixates upon it, and acknowledges it with his tongue while his heart confirms it.

There is none who withholds or grants except Allah, and a person receives only what has been destined for him. A man's effort cannot increase his sustenance. Yet he denies this with his tongue and his heart. Allah declares (in the meaning of revelation):

They say with their mouths what is not in their hearts, and Allah knows best what they conceal.

Allah has been gracious to His servants by permitting them to earn and acquire wealth, provided they do not transgress the limits set by Allah, do not abandon their duties to Him, do not deviate from the Sunna of the Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings), do not break their trust in Allah, and do not fall into the snare of greed. But when they forget these conditions and indulge in what was forbidden them, they shall be among the destroyed on the Day of Resurrection, bearing only a false claim.

Not every earner is trustworthy! For he may obtain unlawful or doubtful things in his earnings. Such a person is known by the effects of his acquiring: his greed for wealth never diminishes, and he expends lavishly for the sake of this world.

He to whom earning is permitted—though his body labors hard, his heart trusts in Allah. If he accumulates great wealth, he acts as a trustee who knows that possessing or lacking wealth are equal. If he withholds, he withholds for Allah's sake; if he spends, he spends as Allah has commanded. Both his deeds are directed toward Allah alone.[^cite-13]

## Contentment

Contentment is the state in which a person finds satisfaction in all that he loves and acceptance of all that he abhors. Contentment is a ray of the light of knowledge. A contented person is one who has destroyed all his desires; such a person alone is truly pleased with Allah.

Contentment is a name ⟪ar:أسم⟫ for a station of worship ⟪ar:عبدیت⟫, and the delight of one's ⟪ar:صفت⟫ heart.

I have heard from my father ⟪ar:محمد باقر علیہ السلام⟫ that to be attached to what is present is idolatry (*shirk*, association of partners with Allah), and to be attached to what is absent is disbelief (*kufr*). These are two forms of heedlessness. I marvel at one who claims to be Allah's servant ⟪ar:عبد⟫ and then contends against His divine decree. Contented mystics are far removed from such behavior.[^cite-14]

## Love for the Sake of Allah

Man's common perception of Allah is that He is stern and unyielding, punishing the disbelievers, the polytheists, and the disobedient. For this reason, most people worship Allah out of fear and dread. They obey Him for fear that if they do not, He will seize them with His punishment. Though it is true that Allah is the Master of the Day of Judgment and of punishment, Allah has identified Himself as the Most Merciful rather than the Mighty. For this reason He begins His Noble Book with this verse: ⟪ar-quote:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫ "In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Abundantly Merciful."

Had He willed, He could have said: ⟪ar:بسم اللہ الجبار القہار⟫ "In the name of Allah, the Overpowering, the Subduing." Yet He chose the attributes of Mercy and Abundant Mercy as His identifier. This is because He decreed that in all His dealings with His creatures, He would act with mercy. Therefore it is more fitting to obey Allah and worship Him out of love, not on the basis of fear alone.

Had Allah willed to oppress and wreak vengeance, none in all the universe could prevent Him. His servants perpetually disobey Him; witnessing this makes His creatures furious and enraged. Yet Allah (exalted be He) declares (in the meaning of revelation):

**⟪ar:مَا مِن یَوم اِلاَّ والبَحرُ  یَستَئذِنُ رَبَّہُ فی اَن یورِقُ ابن آدم۔  والارضُ یَستَئذِنُ فی أَن تَبتَلِعَہُ۔ والملائکۃ یَستَئذِنُ فی ان تُعاَجِلَہُ وَ طُولِکَہ⟫**

*Not a day passes but the sea seeks permission from its Lord to drown the son of Adam. The earth asks permission to swallow him. The angels petition to accelerate his death.*

Water, earth, and angels are creatures like ourselves, yet they grow indignant at our disobedience. But the One whom we have disobeyed answers them:

⟪ar:تَعُو عَبدِی فأنا أَعلَمُ بِعَبدِی مِنکُم ۔ ان کان عبدَکم فَشَأنَکم بِہِ۔ وأن کان عبدی فَمِنِّی وأِلیَّ عبدی⟫

"Leave My servant; I know him better than you. If he is your servant, then deal with him as you wish. But if he is My servant, then leave him to Me and depart."

**⟪ar:یسأل الملائکۃ، فلماذا عقدُہُ؟ یستجیب اللہ، إنني أتطَّلِع إلی التوبتَہُ⟫**

*The angels ask, "Why do You not seize him?" Allah replies, "I am watching and waiting for his repentance."*

**⟪ar:أِن اتانی نھاراً قبلتہ أِن اتانی لیلا قبلتہ⟫**

*If he comes to Me by day repenting, I will accept his repentance. If he comes to Me by night repenting, I will accept it. I will not hear a single word of complaint or resentment from you.*

**⟪ar:مَن تَقَرَّبَ أِلَیَّ تَلَقَّیتُہُ مِن بَعِید وَ مَن أَعرَضَ عَنِّی ناَدَیتُہُ عَن قَرِیب⟫**

*Whoever draws near to Me, I go forth to meet him from afar; and whoever turns away from Me, I myself go to him and call him to return.*

**⟪ar:أَوَلَکَ رَبٌّ غَیرِی؟ الحَسَنَۃُ عِندِی بِعَشرِأَمثَالِھَا وَ أَزِید۔ وَوَصَّیَّتُ عِندِی بِمِثلِھَا وأَعفُو و أغْفَر⟫**

*Have you found any Lord other than I? The good deed in My sight is rewarded tenfold and more. One sin I record as one sin, yet I forgive and show mercy as I will.*[^cite-15]
