# Mafatih al-Hikmah
*Source: KASHKOLE, Binder 23 (Selected Scientific Subjects), Chapter 148. 3 topics. Language: ur.*<!-- section 1 (id=4629, raw_sort=1): سرکس کے جانور -->Look at the circus, how many bloodthirsty beasts and savages are made to perform different stunts with them. The works of man are taught to the animal. The circus seller generates thousands of rupees through these spectacles. In this spectacle of blood and lust, Maulana Muqaddas gave many lessons. He said:

The circus is the holy person of our Prophet, Muhammad Mustafa (peace be upon him). How many arrogant, rebellious and arrogant human beings did this. I trained them and got my work out of them. He offered a lesson to others. But the rebelliousness, disobedience, and suppressed lust to be great in his nature did not go away. On the other hand, the Prophet's eyes were closed, and the reality of these beastly barbarians came to light. He hurt someone, injured someone, killed someone and created chaos and anger in the entire arrangement. Just as lions and leopards, when freed, kill the children of the healer, or his caretakers, and forget all the favors that were done to teach and train them. It is true that no matter how much they treat barbarians and beasts, they cannot become human beings.

Whose nature is that of a wolf will show brutality. Even if he pretends to say something against his nature while in the company of man, it is temporary. The same was the case with the Umayyads. After the conquest of Mecca, Abu Sufyan embraced Islam, but his descendants were born beasts like Mu'awiya and Yazid.

In the training of prophets, saints and other guides, if a savage and beastly attribute (such as a lion or leopard) comes and seduces the hearts of human beings with human actions, then he does not become a human being. Nor can it be included in the category of human beings. Yes! The outward benefit that reaches the owner and the money earned through them are rewarded in such a way that even the oppressor and the rebellious become famous, and those who praise him remain until the Day of Resurrection. There are a large number of his followers and admirers in every era.<!-- section 2 (id=4630, raw_sort=2): وحشی جانوروں کی لڑائی -->Where the fight between chickens and quails takes place, it is called 'pali'. The spectacle of fighting wild animals was called 'Saath Mari'. In Baroda, such spectacles were shown in the princely states.  A huge field was surrounded by walls. Spectators sat upstairs. Pavilions were built for kings, nobles and special guests. Elephants were fought with elephants in the field. In addition, there would be competitions between elephants and bulls, and bulls and bulls.

All these fighters would have been left free. The provocateurs would provoke them and induce them to fight, and suppressed opposition or rivalry would erupt. They attacked each other. Mara Marie, Bloody Blood. All the courage was exerted to prevail over each other, but where the king of the time ordered that all this beating should be stopped! Then every man returned quietly to his dwelling-place.

The wise man has given some freedom to different countries. When the time comes, we start fighting over the slightest thing. And when the reckoning of the retributions and transactions is over, there is no reconciliation, and then they go about their own pursuits. The whole world is amazed as to where the matter became and how it has now become distorted.<!-- section 3 (id=4631, raw_sort=3): طوطے کا تماشا -->In Baroda, various items were produced for the interest of the guests at weddings. The parrot groomers here were famous, and often the Brotherhood invited them to their ceremonies. A parrot spectacle was also shown at a brother's wedding. The parrots delighted the audience by displaying various wonders, and everyone was amused. After the spectacle was over, Maulana gave an insightful and instructive speech. The following is a summary of what I heard from your interpreter:

When a parrot imitates a man's work, such as picking pearls, testing fake money, drawing water from a well, recognizing card cards, exercising, dropping a gun, playing acrobatics, etc., groups of humans gather to watch these movements. Our Messenger (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) has taught us the deeds of the angels. One ⟪ar:’خیر العمل‘⟫ of them is prayer. His actions are the actions of the angels, such as standing, bowing, prostrating, and standing. If we carry out these acts, the angels will surely gather to watch this spectacle. It has been said that when the worshipper prays, he is surrounded by angels.

See, the time for the spectacle is fixed. If the parrot didn't do it at that time, then who would appreciate it? In the same way, if the prayer is offered at the right time, and if it is offered in the prescribed manner, it will be accepted, otherwise it will be hit on the face. The parrot does not realize that the actions and words it is saying have some meaning. He only knows how to copy. But humans love parrots and birds that speak and talk like them, because they cheer them up by speaking the human language, such as mina and cockatoos.  In the same way, when those who are adorned with outward knowledge, who are in the atmosphere of monotheism and knowledge, give speeches and say something about the truth, then the people of truth, who are real people, are happy that they have to say something about the truth. ⟪ar:الحمد للہ⟫Learn to speak some dialects. Hazrat Qibla used to give the example of the apparent clerics like parrots and cockatoos in the same way.
