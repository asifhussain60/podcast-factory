# Risalat-e-Hikmat al-Maut

## The Wisdom of Death in Human Existence

God has wisely connected the soul with the body and made intellect supreme over both. The intellect extends kindness to the self so that it may transform itself back to its original spiritual state and return to the world of the spirit. In the same way, the soul is kind to the body in order to transform it from ⟪ar:'جسمانیت'⟫ (corporeality) into ⟪ar:'نفسانیت'⟫ (spirituality). This represents the profound wisdom underlying death.

All animals abhor death and love life. Most scholars and wise men acknowledge that death is real and that God has concealed a wisdom within it—yet they cannot discern what that wisdom truly is. They cite the Quranic verse as evidence without fully grasping its meaning:

> ⟪quran 67:2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ ٱلْمَوْتَ وَٱلْحَيَوٰةَ لِيَبْلُوَكُمْ أَيُّكُمْ أَحْسَنُ عَمَلًۭا ۚ وَهُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْغَفُورُ ‎⟫
> *Who has created death and life, that He may test you which of you is best in deed. And He is the All-Mighty, the Oft-Forgiving.*

Although these scholars recite and affirm this verse, they nonetheless hate death and love life. Only when stripped of worldly luxuries or incapable of receiving the world's pleasures do they begin to prefer death.

When a wise person contemplates the structure of the human body, he observes that every organ is arranged in the most purposeful manner for its function.[^cite-1] Different bones are connected through joints in a specific configuration, with limbs mounted upon them. Upon closer inspection, he notes that God has placed more joints along the sides of the body and fewer in the middle. Within these intervals lie ⟪ar:'حس'⟫ (sensation) and ⟪ar:'شعور'⟫ (consciousness)—soft, delicate tissues that enable movement. Had these muscles been kept rigid, the limbs could never have moved so freely at their joints. Furthermore, God has woven an intricate network of ⟪ar:عروق⟫ (vessels) throughout the body, beginning from within the liver and extending to the heart through embedded channels. These vessels transport blood from all parts of the body to the heart. Similarly, ⟪ar:عروق دموی⟫ (arterial vessels) carry blood from the heart to all the organs. Clear segments are arranged in layers throughout the body to distribute benefits and remove harm.

This system is made equal for all humans—men and women alike—yet God created a special distinction between the two. In the man, He created the ⟪ar:'نطفہ'⟫ (sperm), and in the woman, the ⟪ar:'رحم'⟫ (womb). The sperm emerges from the man and reaches the woman's womb, where it is nurtured and developed. That sperm eventually grows ⟪ar:رحم مادر⟫ (in the maternal womb) into a child and is born into the world. The child then passes through the stages of childhood, youth, and old age. When he reaches old age, he feels the changes within his body; his powers begin to fade, and death strikes him. The inanimate body becomes altered, producing foul air and odor. It is then placed in the earth, where the body dissolves into its four elements.[^cite-2]

⟪ar:عاقل الَّبیب⟫ (The wise and intelligent one) observes that during the child's development, he encounters three trials in ⟪ar:رحم⟫ the womb:

| Darkness | Darkness of the belly | Darkness of the womb |
| --- | --- | --- |

Only by passing through these three hardships can the sperm be born into the world. The child has dwelt in this cramped space, where he was treated with kindness and comfort so that his creation would be perfected and his form would reach completion. The baby remains ⟪ar:'منقلب'⟫ (turning, sometimes head-up, sometimes head-down) in the womb until full maturity. Then he breaks through all barriers that prevented his departure. The blood and secretions flow—retained by divine wisdom so the fetus could exit the womb with ease—and the child meets his mother with great urgency and effort. All these matters astonish the intellect of scholars.

After passing through all these stages, an embryo moves from the womb to the world and begins to receive the winds of this world, the rays ⟪ar:انوار شمس⟫ (of the sun), the pleasures of luxury, and the blessings of creation. When the wise person reflects upon all this, he understands: if God brought this child out of such a narrow place after fulfilling his physical development, would He not bring him out of the hardships of this world once his soul had reached its perfection? Just as no obstacle can prevent his appearance in the world after his completion in the womb, nothing will prevent his transition to the higher world upon the fulfillment of his soul—not even his body. Thus death becomes wisdom: God delivers the human soul through death from this narrow, dark, and impoverished place (the world and the body).

Understand therefore that the condition of the soul is the same as that of the fetus in the womb: ⟪ar:'جسم'⟫ (the body) is ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ (the soul) like the womb, and the fetus like the soul in the body. The condition of the human soul after death is like that of a child at birth, for the death of the body in this world is, in fact, the birth of the soul in the Hereafter. Similarly, the birth of a fetus in the world is nothing but its death in the womb. What we call death in this world is the birth of our soul in the Hereafter—which is why the Messenger of God said: ⟪ar-quote:الدنیا رحم الآخرۃ⟫ "This world is a womb for the Hereafter."[^cite-3] Only through the death of the body in this world can the soul be born in the next. There are but two paths for the soul to safely leave the world:

## The Purpose of Death

Everything has a beginning—called ⟪ar:'زندگی'⟫ (life)—and an end, called ⟪ar:'موت'⟫ (death). Therefore, man's beginning is his life, and his end is his death. Life begins when a person becomes sperm and settles in the womb. In the world of the womb, he dies when he leaves it after passing through the stages of creation. This departure is called birth, marking the beginning of his ⟪ar:عالم طبیعیۃ⟫ (natural world) life. When he has completed this life and leaves the body, the end of this natural existence is called death. Just as a child attains life by departing from the womb's death, so too does the human soul separate from the body and attain the life of the Hereafter. Death—the separation of the soul from the body—is the final threshold of the physical world for him, for he enters the Hereafter only after reaching this point and severing himself from the body.[^cite-4]

Though the child completes its form and organs in the womb, it derives no benefit from this completion until it emerges from the womb. Similarly, the soul that acquires perfections and virtues while united with the body does not fully benefit from them until it leaves the body.

As the mother's womb is small, narrow, and dark compared to the physical world, so too is the natural world small, insignificant, and dense compared to the Hereafter. Just as the mother's womb is necessary for the child until he has completed the stages of creation within it, so is the natural world necessary for the soul until it has attained perfections and virtues.

As the child must leave the womb at the completion of its creation or at the end of his appointed time, so too must the human soul leave the body and physical world after attaining perfection and virtue in the natural world. Death is the cause of eternal life, and worldly life is the cause of real death. Until man is born into this world, he cannot enter the Hereafter. Man's physical life causes his physical death, and his physical death causes his spiritual life in the Hereafter—eternal and everlasting. Thus there is great wisdom in both ⟪ar:'⟫⟪ar:'⟫ (states).

After the embryo is perfected in the womb, it gains nothing by remaining there until it appears in the world through birth. Similarly, when the self attains its perfection by connecting ⟪ar:'صورت'⟫ (form) with the body, and its virtues are complete, it derives no benefit from remaining in the body until it leaves it. When that form departs the body, it gains benefit from it in the Hereafter. For this reason, death is wisdom: without it, there is no eternal survival. It is also necessary for the soul to leave the body because the body is dense and the soul is subtle. Since the Hereafter is subtle, only the subtle—which is the soul—can enter it. The body, being dense, cannot enter.

## The Nature of Life

What is meant by ⟪ar:'مَا ھِیَ'⟫ (the nature or essence) of a thing? Every entity is identified within three circles:

| Genus | Species | Individual |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Animal | Believer | Asif Hussain |

A species exists within the realm of genus, and an individual within the realm of species. There are two types of life and death: physical and spiritual.

| Physical Life | Physical Death |
| --- | --- |
| Spiritual Life | Spiritual Death |

### Physical Life

Physical life is truly the self's use of its own body. When the soul ceases to use the body, it is called physical death. Likewise, consciousness is merely the self's use of bodily senses. Sleep is simply the name given to the self's temporary relinquishment of sensory use.

### Spiritual Life

In contrast to physical life, the soul is an essence that exists by virtue of its own nature and does not depend on anything else for its existence, unlike the soul's dependence on the body for physical life. The soul is ⟪ar:'حی'⟫ (alive) in its essence ⟪ar:'بالفعل'⟫ (in actuality) as ⟪ar:علامۃ بالقوۃ⟫ (a knower with latent powers and capacities). It manifests its actions through the body, its forms, and its essence. For example, when the soul desires worship, it moves the body to complete the parts of prayer. The body can do nothing unless the soul ⟪ar:'محرک'⟫ (moves it). The body's life consists in proximity to the soul and closeness to it—that the body remain always near its self. Just as air is dark in its essence yet illuminated by the proximity of the light of sun, moon, and stars, so too is the body animated by the presence of the soul.

### Spiritual Death

The forgetting of one's self constitutes the death of the self. In this world, such death occurs through indulgence in unnecessary things. The death of the body is not necessary for the death of the soul. Drowning in ⟪ar:ھاویۃ⟫ (the abyss), indulging in bodily pleasures, pursuing bodily desires, and becoming heedless of one's own essence—coupled with ignorance of ⟪ar:علم ماکان سیکون⟫ (knowledge of what was and what will be) and lack of ⟪ar:قدرت تامۃ⟫ (perfect power) and ⟪ar:حیات ابدی سرمدی⟫ (eternal and everlasting life)—leads to the death of the soul. Such people recognize only the lowest, most degraded, fragmented physical life. For them, God says (in the meaning of His word):

> ⟪quran 3:185⟫
> ⟪ar:‏كُلُّ نَفْسٍۢ ذَآئِقَةُ ٱلْمَوْتِ ۗ وَإِنَّمَا تُوَفَّوْنَ أُجُورَكُمْ يَوْمَ ٱلْقِيَمَةِ ۖ فَمَن زُحْزِحَ عَنِ ٱلنَّارِ وَأُدْخِلَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ فَقَدْ فَازَ ۗ وَمَا ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَآ إِلَّا مَتَعُ ٱلْغُرُورِ ‎⟫
> *Every soul shall taste of death; and you will be paid in full your reward on the Day of Resurrection. Whoever is kept away from the Fire and admitted to Paradise has indeed succeeded. The life of this world is nothing but the enjoyment of delusion.*

Then He says:

> ⟪quran 29:64⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا هَذِهِ ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَآ إِلَّا لَهْوٌۭ وَلَعِبٌۭ ۚ وَإِنَّ ٱلدَّارَ ٱلْآخِرَةَ لَهِىَ ٱلْحَيَوَانُ ۚ لَوْ كَانُوا۟ يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *This life of the world is only amusement and play. Verily, the home of the Hereafter, that is the true life, if they but knew.*

The life ⟪ar-quote:لھو⟫ (game) of this world is ⟪ar-quote:لَعَب⟫ (sport and jest). This world is a place of heedlessness because of its outward beauty and the rivalry of wealth and children. People wish to abide here forever, as God says (in the meaning):

> ⟪quran 30:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَعْلَمُونَ ظَهِرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَهُمْ عَنِ ٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ غَافِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *They know only the outward appearance of the life of this world, and of the Hereafter they are heedless.*

God further says (in the meaning):

> ⟪quran 4:77⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى ٱلَّذِينَ قِيلَ لَهُمْ كُفُّوٓا۟ أَيْدِيَكُمْ وَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ فَلَمَّا كُتِبَ عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلْقِتَالُ إِذَا فَرِيقٌۭ مِّنْهُمْ يَخْشَوْنَ ٱلنَّاسَ كَخَشْيَةِ ٱللَّهِ أَوْ أَشَدَّ خَشْيَةًۭ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ رَبَّنَا لِمَ كَتَبْتَ عَلَيْنَا ٱلْقِتَالَ لَوْلَآ أَخَّرْتَنَآ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ قَرِيبٍۢ ۗ قُلْ مَتَعُ ٱلدُّنْيَا قَلِيلٌۭ وَٱلْآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌۭ لِّمَنِ ٱتَّقَىٰ وَلَا تُظْلَمُونَ فَتِيلًا ‎⟫
> *Hast thou not regarded those to whom it was said, "Restrain your hands, and establish the prayer, and pay the alms"? Then when fighting was prescribed for them, behold, a party of them feared people as they feared God, or with even greater fear, and said, "Our Lord, why hast Thou prescribed fighting for us? Why hast Thou not deferred us to a near term?" Say: "The enjoyment of this world is little, and the Hereafter is better for him who fears God. You shall not be wronged even by the width of a thread."*

Many other verses likewise condemn the world and those who love it. The life of the Hereafter is truly the keeping alive of the soul in the Hereafter. The soul is eternal life.

### Physical Death

The body is dead in its very essence—it is that which cannot move without the soul and, because of its material nature, it changes without it. It becomes foul-smelling and hideous, finally dispersing into its components and returning to its origin, as God says (in the meaning):

> ⟪quran 20:55⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ مِنْهَا خَلَقْنَكُمْ وَفِيهَا نُعِيدُكُمْ وَمِنْهَا نُخْرِجُكُمْ تَارَةً أُخْرَىٰ ‎⟫
> *From the earth We created you, and into it We will return you, and from it We will bring you out once again.*

The term "death" refers to the soul, not to the body, for the body is already dead. Death derives from *wafā*, meaning the fulfillment of a promise. When the soul has progressed and fulfilled the promise it made to its Imam and has reached its standard, it becomes obligatory for it to die so that it may receive the boundless reward for its labors. This is why death is wisdom and mercy from God Almighty.

## The Part and the Whole

Our soul is one ⟪ar:'جزو'⟫ (part) in comparison to the spiritual world; our body is one part in comparison to the physical world. The soul world stands ⟪ar:' کل'⟫ (as the whole) before the self, and the physical world stands as the whole before the body. Each part always maintains a relationship and connection with its whole.

The connection ⟪ar:علم الریاضیۃ⟫ (of arithmetic) of one self with another is the perfection of mathematics—just as a number connects to another number. By adding two numbers, a new number is created; by multiplying them, a second number arises; by dividing them, a third emerges, and so on. The manner and way in which two numbers relate is based on their operations.

In the same way, the same effects are established in the manner and way that one self relates to another. When two souls meet—the ⟪ar:اعلی نفس جزویۃ⟫ (higher partial soul) and the ⟪ar:ادنی نفس جزویۃ⟫ (lower partial soul)—the higher influences and draws forth the wisdom and virtues ⟪ar:بالفعل⟫ (in actuality) that were hidden ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ (in potentiality) within the lower. The purpose of the higher self is to perfect the lower ⟪ar:ھیولی جزویۃ⟫ (partial matter), so that the partial self attains its perfection and is transformed into ⟪ar:'صورت'⟫ (form). This completion is called the perfection ⟪ar:ھیولی⟫ (of matter into) ⟪ar:صورت⟫ (form).[^cite-5]

A husband develops physical union with his wife and brings forth a child from it. The child possessed the power potentially within both, but when the two were combined in a certain way, the child was able to manifest at the boundary. In the same way, the lower takes on the image of its whole. The superior then trains his lower part—now teaching him governance, prudence, noble character, correct belief, pure deeds, and true knowledge—and endeavors to make him like himself. This is expressed in the language of wisdom: ⟪ar-quote:اِنَّھَا تُشَبِّہُ بِالالہ⟫ *"The lower becomes like the one above it!"*

When the human soul reaches its goal and completes the manifestation of its virtues, the body becomes useless to it. That is, when the partial self attains perfection, it no longer needs the body. At this point, it leaves the body and returns to a higher state where it is then nurtured and developed to ever-higher levels—a development not possible while in the body. The virtues and capacities that the soul created through dwelling in the body are, upon leaving the body, nurtured and cultivated at progressively higher standards. As God says (in the meaning):

> ⟪quran 56:61⟫
> ⟪ar:‏عَلَىٰٓ أَن نُّبَدِّلَ أَمْثَلَكُمْ وَنُنشِئَكُمْ فِى مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *That We may transfigure you and create you in forms that you know not.*

Then God created ⟪ar:نشأۃ الآخرۃ⟫ (the creation of the Hereafter) for it—that is, He prepared for his soul another dwelling superior and more exalted than his body. While he was attached to the body, he was only ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ (soul); now that he has reached his limit, he becomes ⟪ar:'صورت'⟫ (form) and develops as a form.[^cite-6]

Just as a child cannot even imagine the vastness of the world, the illumination of light, or the morning by dwelling in the narrowness, darkness, and constraint of the womb, similarly the soul cannot imagine the Hereafter while in the body. The relationship between the duration of the womb and the known, and the unknown between birth, is the same as between life and death. Until the soul dies and is born into the next world, it cannot understand the greatness of that place or perceive its realities—just as a fetus cannot feel or comprehend the pleasures of the world while in the womb.

For this reason, the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) said: ⟪ar-quote:الناسُ نیامٌ فأذا مات اِنصَبَتُ⟫ *"People are asleep, and when they die, they awake."*[^cite-7] Their slumber is their heedlessness, even if it persists after death—as in the example of the disbelievers. When the intoxication of death overwhelms them and reality appears, their soul departs their body. At that moment, the soul perceives the truths that once called it to heaven during its life. As God says (in the meaning):

> ⟪quran 50:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَّقَدْ كُنتَ فِى غَفْلَةٍۢ مِّنْ هَذَا فَكَشَفْنَا عَنكَ غِطَآءَكَ فَبَصَرُكَ ٱلْيَوْمَ حَدِيدٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Indeed, thou wast heedless of this; now We have removed thy veil from thee, and thy sight this day is sharp.*

This is why God commands the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him): "Worship your Lord until certainty comes to you." And indeed, every human being shall taste death:

> ⟪quran 15:99⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱعْبُدْ رَبَّكَ حَتَّىٰ يَأْتِيَك ٱلْيَقِينُ ‎⟫
> *And worship thy Lord until certainty comes to thee.*

As long as the child grows in the mother's womb, the mother values ⟪ar:مشیمۃ⟫ (the placenta) highly, knowing it is through the placenta that her child is nourished. But when the child emerges from her womb, she detests ⟪ar:مشیمۃ⟫ (the placenta) and refuses even to touch it. The same is true of the body of the soul. As long as the soul was growing within it, this body had great dignity and value for the soul. But after leaving it, the body holds no value. For this reason, the body is buried immediately.

Death is wise, for without death the soul cannot return to ⟪ar:رحمن و رحیم⟫ (the Merciful and Compassionate One), and it cannot return to the blessings God has promised it:

> ⟪quran 89:27-30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيَّتُهَا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ‎ ‏ٱرْجِعِىٓ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةًۭ مَّرْضِيَّةًۭ ‎ ‏فَٱدْخُلِى فِى عِبَادِى ‎ ‏وَٱدْخُلِى جَنَّتِى ‎⟫
> *O thou soul at peace! Return unto thy Lord, well-pleased and pleasing unto Him. Enter thou among My servants, and enter thou My Paradise.*

That is, the true return to God is such that the soul is willing to leave the body and the body is willing to leave it. This shows that death is the name of wisdom and the best of God's forgiveness and reward to His servants, for it ⟪ar:حیات یزدانی و دائمی⟫ (is the cause of divine and eternal life).

**Statement:**

Either the self evolves into a form, or it becomes degraded into a body by descent. Death is the cause of the destruction of the body so that the soul may come to life. The self is made from the most noble parts of the body.

## The Tale of the Blind Man and the Disabled Man

There were two people living in a city. One was blind, the other disabled in both legs. The two became companions and came to a garden, desiring to enter it. When the garden's owner saw them, compassion stirred within him, and he said: "If We grant you shelter in this garden and provide you with all the fruit you need, will you dwell herein with good conduct and not spread corruption? Will you not spoil the other fruits?"

They answered: "How could we spoil your garden and its fruits? Our condition is evident—one of us is blind, the other is crippled. How could we reach the bushes and damage them?" The owner of the garden then said: "Very well, enter the garden!" He appointed a dwelling place for them and ordered the gardener to care for them and provide them with fruit according to their needs.

Having given this command, the owner departed. The two lived in the garden, and the gardener cared for them, providing fruit as needed, so the garden flourished. One day, the disabled man said to his blind companion: "O wretched one! You have feet—you walk. There are all manner of fruits on the garden's trees that the gardener does not give us. Why not devise some stratagem to obtain them?"

The blind man replied: "You have kindled a desire in me, but you can see and I cannot. Tell me—what shall we do to get these fruits?" Together they devised a scheme. The blind man said: "Listen to me: you shall carry me upon your shoulders and bring me into the garden. Wherever good fruit is found, I will direct you, and you shall bear me there. Wherever my hand can reach, I will pluck the fruit. I will feed you, and I will eat myself. And wherever fruit hangs on the high branches, I will strike them down with your stick, and we shall gather them afterward. But all this can happen only in the gardener's absence."

The blind man said: "You have given me good counsel. Tomorrow we shall attempt it."

The next day, when the gardener closed the garden's door and departed, the disabled man climbed upon the blind man's shoulders. Together they spoiled the fruits in the garden, then returned to their place and slept. When the gardener returned, the garden's condition was not hidden from him. The fruits reserved for the finest specimens were missing. Seeing this, the gardener came to the owner and asked whether anyone had entered the garden in his absence.

"We know nothing of it," they replied. The blind man said, "I cannot see," and the crippled man said, "I was sleeping." The gardener believed them. The next day, when the gardener went to work again, the two rose and spoiled more fruits than before. When the gardener returned and saw the garden's condition, he feared what the owner would say, thinking the owner might believe he either sold the fruits or failed to protect the garden. He began to think how to discover who was causing this mischief.

The next day, the gardener told them he was departing, but he hid behind the wall. When the gardener departed, they went to the orchard and began plucking and destroying the fruits. The gardener observed their deeds, and once they had finished and returned to their place, he came to them and said: "O wretches! Is this the reward for the owner's kindness to you? Is this how you would destroy his garden?"

Frightened by the gardener's words, the two fell silent. The gardener continued: "I have seen with my own eyes what you have done." When they heard that the gardener had witnessed everything, they confessed and said: "Do not tell the owner of the garden. We repent and shall never do such a thing again."

The gardener accepted their repentance and began to admonish them: "Look, if you need fruits, come to me. In this way, neither the garden nor the owner will suffer harm, and you will not incur guilt before the owner." Both of them confessed when they heard the gardener's words. Yet when the gardener left them and returned to his work, the two resumed their thievery—even more vigorously than before. When the gardener returned and saw all this, he again admonished them and made them fear God, but they did not obey the gardener and continued their deeds.

One day, when the owner of the garden came, the gardener had to inform him of the garden's condition and the two men's conduct. The owner said: "I feared that this crippled man would climb upon the blind man's back and together they would create mischief in the garden." The gardener replied: "They did all this despite my advice and warnings."

The owner of the garden said: "They deserve punishment," and ordered that they first be beaten severely and then expelled from the garden. After expulsion, they wandered in the forests, pursued by beasts, until they perished from hunger and thirst.

---

**The Interpretation of the Tale:**

In this story, the self is called disabled or crippled because it does nothing by itself—it acts only through the body. The body is called blind because it walks at the self's command without thinking. The garden represents the world, and the fruits represent the pleasures and desires of the world. The garden's owner is God Almighty. The gardener is the intellect of man—that faculty which tells him what is good and what is bad, enjoins good and forbids evil. The intellect commands the self to attain the world's pleasures according to its needs, but the self becomes enslaved to lust, disobeys the intellect, and uses the body as a means to accomplish all that is harmful to it. By refusing to obey the intellect, a person ruins both his religion and his world, falling into error and misguidance, until the angels of God's wrath seize him, and he comes to regret his evil deeds. All this becomes known when the time comes for his soul to depart, and this is the outcome God has announced:

> ⟪quran 22:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَعْبُدُ ٱللَّهَ عَلَىٰ حَرْفٍۢ ۖ فَإِنْ أَصَابَهُۥ خَيْرٌ ٱطْمَأَنَّ بِهِۦ ۖ وَإِنْ أَصَابَتْهُ فِتْنَةٌ ٱنقَلَبَ عَلَىٰ وَجْهِهِۦ خَسِرَ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَٱلْآخِرَةَ ۚ ذَلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْخُسْرَانُ ٱلْمُبِينُ ‎⟫
> *And among mankind is he who worships God on the edge; if good befalls him, he is content with it, but if a trial befalls him, he turns back upon his face. He loses this world and the Hereafter. That is the manifest loss.*

By contrast, there are the righteous—those saved by piety, godliness, and righteous deeds—to whom God has decreed:

> ⟪quran 39:61⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَيُنَجِّى ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّقَوْا۟ بِمَفَازَتِهِمْ لَا يَمَسُّهُمُ ٱلسُّوٓءُ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎⟫
> *And God will deliver those who fear Him to their salvation. Evil shall not touch them, nor shall they grieve.*

This anecdote illuminates the mentality of the common person and its fate. Those who obey the self's whispers and follow its desires are portrayed in various ways throughout the Holy Quran. To the wrongdoers and worldly people, it is said:

> ⟪quran 2:86⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱشْتَرَوُا۟ ٱلْحَيَوٰةَ ٱلدُّنْيَا بِٱلْآخِرَةِ ۖ فَلَا يُخَفَّفُ عَنْهُمُ ٱلْعَذَابُ وَلَا هُمْ يُنصَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *These are they who have bought the life of this world at the price of the Hereafter. Their torment shall not be lightened, nor shall they be helped.*

And regarding those immersed in the world and its comforts, He says:

> ⟪quran 3:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏زُيِّنَ لِلنَّاسِ حُبُّ ٱلشَّهَوَتِ مِنَ ٱلنِّسَآءِ وَٱلْبَنِينَ وَٱلْقَنطِيرِ ٱلْمُقَنطَرَةِ مِنَ ٱلذَّهَبِ وَٱلْفِضَّةِ وَٱلْخَيْلِ ٱلْمُسَوَّمَةِ وَٱلْأَنْعَامِ وَٱلْحَرْثِ ۗ ذَلِكَ مَتَعُ ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا ۖ وَٱللَّهُ عِندَهُۥ حُسْنُ ٱلْمَـَٔابِ ‎⟫
> *Beautified for mankind is the love of desires—of women, children, heaped-up heaps of gold and silver, horses, cattle, and tilth. That is the enjoyment of the life of this world, yet with God is the best return.*

Concerning ordinary life, He says:

> ⟪quran 6:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَآ إِلَّا لَعِبٌۭ وَلَهْوٌۭ ۖ وَلَلدَّارُ ٱلْآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌۭ لِّلَّذِينَ يَتَّقُونَ ۗ أَفَلَا تَعْقِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *The life of this world is nothing but a sport and amusement, while the home of the Hereafter is better for those who fear God. Will you not then understand?*

For those gone astray, He says:

> ⟪quran 13:34⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَّهُمْ عَذَابٌۭ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا ۖ وَلَعَذَابُ ٱلْآخِرَةِ أَشَقُّ ۖ وَمَا لَهُم مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ مِن وَاقٍۢ ‎⟫
> *For them is torment in the life of this world, and the torment of the Hereafter is more severe. They have no protector against God.*

By contrast, good news has been given to the righteous, and it has been said:

> ⟪quran 10:64⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَهُمُ ٱلْبُشْرَىٰ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَفِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ ۚ لَا تَبْدِيلَ لِكَلِمَتِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ ذَلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ‎⟫
> *For them is good tidings in the life of this world and in the Hereafter. There is no change in the Words of God. That is the great success.*

There is good news for God's servants and the believers:

> ⟪quran 40:51⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّا لَنَنصُرُ رُسُلَنَا وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَيَوْمَ يَقُومُ ٱلْأَشْهَدُ ‎⟫
> *We will assuredly aid Our Messengers and those who believe, in the life of this world and on the Day when the witnesses shall stand forth.*

Had the life of this world been the true purpose, it would have been especially for God's beloved Prophet. Yet God did not grant it to him, saying:

> ⟪quran 39:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّكَ مَيِّتٌۭ وَإِنَّهُم مَّيِّتُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily, thou shalt die, and they too shall die.*

The world and the life of the world are both beneficial and harmful: beneficial to him who obeys his intellect and performs righteous deeds, to whom the gates of mercy are opened. ⟪ar:جنت الماویٰ⟫ (His abode becomes the gardens of refuge)—a place of stage after stage for the development of his soul. This is where the angels welcome the visitor and say:

> ⟪quran 39:73⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَسِيقَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّقَوْا۟ رَبَّهُمْ إِلَى ٱلْجَنَّةِ زُمَرًا ۖ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءُوهَا وَفُتِحَتْ أَبْوَبُهَا وَقَالَ لَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَا سَلَمٌ عَلَيْكُمْ طِبْتُمْ فَٱدْخُلُوهَا خَالِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *And those who feared their Lord shall be led to Paradise in multitudes. When they arrive at its gates, opened shall they be, and its keepers shall say to them: "Peace be upon you! You have done well. Enter, and abide therein forever."*

By contrast, he who rejects the intellect's counsel, follows the self's desires, deceives himself, begins evil deeds, regards religion as light and worthless, turns away from it, disobeys God's commands, and treats people unjustly—usurping their rights and believing lies—ruins his own end. Death comes to him as punishment, causing his soul to suffer terrible torments. Though warned and advised to avoid this fate, he does not heed and does not listen to the sound of exhortations.

Death is the moment when the deceased sees his fate—both good and bad. He who has done good fears not death but is ready for it. For him, death is a source of joy, for it brings an end to his labors and troubled life; his soul is freed, and he enters the Hereafter, receiving rewards and advancement. Conversely, he who has done evil through obedience to the self is always afraid of death. He knows his wicked deeds and the terrible punishment awaiting him, and therefore dreads death greatly. He wishes to avoid it at all costs. When death finally comes to him and his terrible end begins to appear, he cries out, begging for respite: "O Lord, if You would grant me a little respite, I might atone for some of my sins, perform righteous deeds, seek revenge, fulfill the rights of the people, ask their forgiveness for the wrongs I have done, and repent for the sins I have committed."

But now the time is spent; the door of repentance is shut. ⟪ar:احکم الحاکمین⟫ (The decree of the Most Just of judges) has been issued, and in its fulfillment there will be not one hour less or more. The tongue departs the present with the saying:

*Look at me as a beholder.*

*Listen to me, for this is a wise admonition.*

God says in Surah al-Munāfiqūn:

> ⟪quran 63:10-11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنفِقُوا۟ مِن مَّا رَزَقْنَكُم مِّن قَبْلِ أَن يَأْتِىَ أَحَدَكُمُ ٱلْمَوْتُ فَيَقُولَ رَبِّ لَوْلَآ أَخَّرْتَنِىٓ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ قَرِيبٍۢ فَأَصَّدَّقَ وَأَكُن مِّنَ ٱلصَّالِحِينَ ‎ ‏وَلَن يُؤَخِّرَ ٱللَّهُ نَفْسًا إِذَا جَآءَ أَجَلُهَا ۚ وَٱللَّهُ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And spend from what We have provided you before death comes to one of you, and he says, "My Lord, why did You not defer me for a little while, so that I might give alms and be among the righteous?" And God will not defer a soul when its time has come. And God is Aware of what you do.*

**In this verse, "spending" one's provision means translating knowledge into action.**

In the physical world, life and death are truly for the soul. The body is the soul's servant and instrument. Therefore, ⟪ar:مولائے کائنات امیر المؤمنین حضرت علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ (the Master of Creation, the Commander of the Faithful, the honored 'Alī ibn Abī Ṭālib), in explaining the reality of the human being, said:

| **You are a human by virtue of your soul, not your body** |
| --- |
| Be yourself and perfect its virtues. For you are fashioned from the soul, not from the body. |

And since ⟪ar:'ابدیت'⟫ (eternal life) is attained by the soul, he has explained its beginning and end in one of his sermons thus:

⟪ar-quote:اِنَّمَا خُلِقتُم لِلاَبَدِ * وَلٰکِن مِنَ دَّارِ اِلَی دَّارِ تُنقَلُونَ * مِنَ الاَصلاَبِ اِلَی الاَرحَامِ * وَ مِنَ الاَرحَامِ اِلَی الدُّنیَا * وَمِنَ الدُّنیَا اِلَی البَرزَخِ * وَ مِنَ البَرزَخِ اِلَی الجَنَّۃَ وَالنَّارِ⟫

*You were created for eternity, yet you travel from house to house: from the loins to the wombs, from the wombs to the world, from the world to the Barzakh, and from the Barzakh to Paradise or Hell.*[^cite-8]

## The Progress of the Soul

O brother! Children possess souls that are ⟪ar:'عاقل بالقوۃ'⟫ (potentially rational). That is, although the child possesses intellect, its powers have not yet been actualized. ⟪ar:عقلا بالغین⟫ (There are adult human minds) and ⟪ar:'علامۃ بالقوۃ'⟫ (potential wise ones). ⟪ar:'عاقل بالفعل'⟫ (There are actually rational people,) ⟪ar:علما⟫ (who are knowers.) ⟪ar:فلسَفیۃٌ بالقوۃ⟫ (There are potentially philosophical souls.) ⟪ar:علامۃ بالفعل⟫ (There are actually wise ones.) ⟪ar:حکماء⟫ (There are the truly wise.) And ⟪ar:ملائکہ بالقوۃ⟫ (there are potentially angelic souls.) ⟪ar:فلسَفیۃٌ بالفعل⟫ (When actually philosophical) they become angels. And because of death, it is wisdom and mercy from God.

| Children | Adult Minds | Wise Ones | Actually Wise | Angels |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

**Statement:**

⟪ar:حکیم⟫ *A wise person* (*ḥakīm*) is one who practices philosophy, while ⟪ar:فلسفی⟫ *a philosopher* (*falsafī*) is one whose philosophy remains merely theoretical and who does not practice his knowledge.

Know that when minerals develop, they enter the bodies of plants. In the same way, when plant bodies develop, they enter animal bodies—and the noblest of the animals is ⟪ar:'انسان'⟫ (humankind). The plant's form is inverted—its head is in the ground and its feet are above. Yet by virtue of its nobility, it receives ⟪ar:نفس حیوانیہ⟫ (the animal soul), which causes the plant to transcend its form. The animal's posture is crooked, with head and legs on the same level. Yet the human soul receives it and, by virtue of its nobility and elevation, redeems it from its prior form.

The human form stands ⟪ar:'قامت الفیہ'⟫ (upright and erect)—placed between Heaven and Hell, with hellish things beneath its feet and heavenly things above its head. It is progressing toward ⟪ar:'صورت ملائکۃ '⟫ (angelic form). That is, whoever perfects the soul and preserves it shall have, for ⟪ar:'ملک'⟫ (that soul), angels that either lift it to heaven or cast it down to hell. As God says (in the meaning):

> ⟪quran 95:4-6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا ٱلْإِنسَنَ فِىٓ أَحْسَنِ تَقْوِيمٍۢ ‎ ‏ثُمَّ رَدَدْنَهُ أَسْفَلَ سَفِلِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَّا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ فَلَهُمْ أَجْرٌ غَيْرُ مَمْنُونٍۢ ‎⟫
> *We created man in the finest form, then reduced him to the lowest of the low, save those who believed and did righteous deeds—for them is a reward without end.*

Death is what grants this ⟪ar:' اَجرٌ غَیرُ مَمنُون '⟫ (reward without end) mentioned in this verse. After death, the soul receives its reward in the Hereafter, which cannot be measured or limited. Consider all these things, for you are in a most perilous place. If you do not progress to heaven, you shall surely descend to hell. If you ⟪ar:اعمال صالحۃ⟫ (perform righteous deeds) with ⟪ar:اخلاق جمیلۃ⟫ (beautiful character) and ⟪ar:علوم حقیقۃ⟫ (true knowledge), I wish you to be saved from the fire of hell—which is the world itself—and that you pass through the heavens to reach Paradise, a place of eternity, joy, bliss, and felicity. I pray that you join the ⟪ar:انبیا⟫ (prophets), ⟪ar:صدیقین⟫ (the truthful), ⟪ar:شہداء⟫ (the martyrs), and ⟪ar:صالحین⟫ (the righteous). There you will find good companions and the finest of friends for others—and this is a bounty from God that comes through death.

## The Governance of the Soul

To ⟪ar:'سیاست '⟫ (*siyāsa*, governance or administration) means to plan and manage something. The soul ⟪ar:'مدبر'⟫ (*mudbir*, the manager) is what performs ⟪ar:'تدبیر'⟫ (*tadbīr*, management) of the body in order to unleash its inner potential. The soul convinces the body to act so that it might benefit itself. Similarly, every superior soul administers the lower soul and convinces it to perform progressive actions.

When the lower self is satisfied with the higher self's governance, it grants the superior soul a place in its house and permits it to care for its family and servants—even though they are its own family and servants. Yet it trusts the higher self and grants it the right to rule over them, knowing and understanding that this is for the good of itself, its family, and its servants. In the same way, the body trusts the soul and permits it to exercise command over its various organs and make them perform actions that benefit everyone.

When the soul accepts the governance and tactics of its superior and teacher, and at its behest adopts the finest just character toward family, household, and servants, it becomes possible for it to leave the household and govern its tribe. And when it succeeds in properly governing the tribe, it becomes obligatory for it to govern the entire city. And when it has properly governed all the people of the city, it becomes obligatory for it to perform ⟪ar:'ناموس الہی'⟫ (*nāmūs-i ilāhī*, divine governance) and show others the right path. And it is possible for the one who has performed proper ⟪ar:ناموس الہیۃ⟫ (divine governance) to ascend and progress in the heavenly realm, where he receives the reward and recompense for all the deeds he performed while in the world.

Know this, O brother! Death is wisdom for this very reason. If trouble befalls you, seize upon ⟪ar:ناموس الہی⟫ (divine governance) and its family and guardians, so that through their intercession you may be saved from Hell and ascend with them to the kingdom of the heavens, entering Paradise through God's mercy, grace, and forgiveness.

## The Union of Body and Soul

The Prophet (may the peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: "The grave is either one of the gardens of Paradise or one of the pits of fire." For the soul separated from the body at death, the grave becomes a paradise. Yet if the soul remains bound within the body, then that body becomes ⟪ar:حفر⟫ a pit for it. The grave of the soul is its body itself. This is why ⟪ar:سیدنا جعفر بن سیدنا سلیمان⟫ declared: "I am in the grave," and ⟪ar:سیدنا سلیمان⟫ likewise said, "I am in the grave," for all the knowledge of ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the Messenger of Allah—upon him be peace and blessings—is buried and concealed within ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ Ali, the son of Abū Ṭālib.[^cite-9]

The self acquires knowledge, wisdom, and virtue while dwelling within the body. Yet the body remains a foreign realm, a place for ⟪ar:'رب'⟫ ⟪ar:'الہ'⟫ the lord and master of the soul; for the body renders all knowledge merely of the flesh, and we obey each command as slaves obey ⟪ar:الہ⟫ a deity. When the soul begins to walk the straight path, it receives discipline from the higher self, which then becomes the dwelling place for ⟪ar:'الہ'⟫ the divine summons within it. The body, that "straight path extended between this world and the Hereafter," becomes the soul's passage. In other words, the body is a road upon which the self journeys. If the soul conducts itself rightly through the body, it shall prosper; otherwise, it shall lose its way. And when at last the soul has crossed this bodily path and is delivered from the calamities that attend it, that soul becomes free to pursue the paths of the next world.

It is a profound humiliation for the soul to become imprisoned and enslaved within its own body, made ⟪ar:' کنیز '⟫ a bondmaid to bodily pleasures. The body has been called a slave-woman because it is the wellspring of every filth, impurity, waste, urine, excrement, saliva, blood, and pus. All these ⟪ar:'جسم'⟫ corruptions arise from and grow within its defects. The body's first state is that of sperm—the ⟪ar:قوۃ الرجالیت⟫ masculine force—subtle and delicate at the outset, yet it culminates in a stinking corpse. Between these two states, the soul dwells its entire life.

The soul ceaselessly purifies the waste of the body through ablution, bathing, and fragrance, striving to restore health to this afflicted form. It covers the body's ⟪ar:عریانیت⟫ nakedness with garments; protects it from cold, heat, hunger, thirst, shock, and countless other calamities.[^cite-10] All filth and corruption in the world issues from the body alone! Were there no physicality, there would be no defilement. Yet when the soul ceases to seek knowledge, worship, and preparation for the Hereafter—when it surrenders instead to bodily pleasures such as fine raiment, rich food, drink, houses, lands, women, and mounts—it becomes like one who worships and bows before an idol night and day. Of such a soul, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 25:43⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَرَءَيْتَ مَنِ ٱتَّخَذَ إِلَهَهُۥ هَوَىٰهُ أَفَأَنتَ تَكُونُ عَلَيْهِ وَكِيلًا ‎⟫
>
> *Have you seen him who has taken as his god his own desire? Will you then be a guardian over him?*

> ⟪quran 45:23⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَفَرَءَيْتَ مَنِ ٱتَّخَذَ إِلَهَهُۥ هَوَىٰهُ وَأَضَلَّهُ ٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ عِلْمٍۢ وَخَتَمَ عَلَىٰ سَمْعِهِۦ وَقَلْبِهِۦ وَجَعَلَ عَلَىٰ بَصَرِهِۦ غِشَوَةًۭ فَمَن يَهْدِيهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أَفَلَا تَذَكَّرُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *Have you seen him who has taken his desire as his god, whom Allah has led astray with knowledge, sealing his hearing and his heart, and placing a veil over his sight? Who will guide him after Allah? Will you not then take heed?*

## The Body as Infidel and Jew

Our body is a disbeliever in relation to the soul! It is called kafir (infidel) because, just as the kaafir cannot recognize Allah, so too does the body fail to acknowledge the greatness and virtue of the soul. Like a disbeliever, the body knows not who provides for it or who nurtures it. The body is ignorant, obstinate, and hasty—it does not contemplate future matters but rushes headlong to gratify present desires. Though the body appears outwardly to be a friend of the soul, inwardly it is hostile and antagonistic to it.[^cite-11]

The body is also ⟪ar:'ابلیس'⟫ called Iblis (Satan) because it exists in a state of ⟪ar:وسواس⟫ whispering and doubt, never resting in any stable condition. It forever invites discord and enmity. The body is a corpse that the soul carries upon its shoulders like a pallbearer. Though the soul grows weary under this burden, it finds no relief until the body is finally buried in the earth.

Likewise, the body is a shadow standing between us and the sun—it has no true reality. The darkness of ⟪ar:اخلاط اربع (سود، سفرا، بلغم، دم)⟫ the four humours—black bile, yellow bile, phlegm, and blood—obscures the light of the intellect from our view. The body, though it appears to produce effects, is ignorant of their true nature. For instance, it satisfies hunger by eating, yet cannot distinguish between the lawful and the unlawful.

The ⟪ar:نفس جزویہ⟫ particular self is exceedingly noble and elevated in comparison to the body, yet it suffers greatly, trapped as it is in this material realm. ⟪ar:ھیولی⟫ It has been placed within the body as prime matter, to endure the tumults of the physical world. It continually feels imprisoned, even when surrounded by pleasures.

Consider the likeness of a wise and learned man who has fallen in love with a beautiful woman—yet she is a harlot, ignorant, poor, and immoral. He must now live his life bound to her. This woman constantly demands fine food, luxurious garments, jewels, a house adorned with needless ornament, and endless desires. The poor sage, driven by devotion and the intensity of his ardour, strives to fulfill all her wishes, hoping somehow to make her sensible and virtuous. Only in this hope does he endure her shame. Were it not for love, when would he have fled? His entire focus becomes the reformation of this woman, until he forgets to cultivate himself and neglects his own virtues. In his effort to please his wife, he forgets the city from whence he came; he forgets his kinsmen and those who raised him; he forgets the blessing in which he was nurtured. Despite his wisdom and learning, he becomes like a disciple of Satan, overcome by the woman's pleasures. Of such a state, Allah says concerning Shaitan:

> ⟪ar:‏يَبَنِىٓ آدَمَ لَا يَفْتِنَنَّكُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ كَمَآ أَخْرَجَ أَبَوَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱلْجَنَّةِ يَنزِعُ عَنْهُمَا لِبَاسَهُمَا لِيُرِيَهُمَا سَوْءَتِهِمَآ ۗ إِنَّهُۥ يَرَىٰكُمْ هُوَ وَقَبِيلُهُۥ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَا تَرَوْنَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّا جَعَلْنَا ٱلشَّيَطِينَ أَوْلِيَآءَ لِلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *O Children of Adam! Let not Satan deceive you, as he got your parents out of Paradise, stripping them of their garments to show them their nakedness. He and his soldiers see you from where you cannot see them. We have appointed the devils as friends for those who do not believe.*

**The Body as Iblis, Who Expelled Adam and Eve from Paradise**

Know that the power of the soul is not of this world, but of heaven! Its origin lies in the spiritual realm where it must labour. That soul is alive in itself and has no need of food, raiment, or such things—only the body, its consort, requires these things to sustain itself. The body is therefore enslaved to these necessities. Everything a person needs to live in this world springs from the body's needs. Every such desire is the cry of a body hastening toward decay and dissolution. Meanwhile, the soul exerts itself to perfect the body and draw profit from it.

The body is never steadfast in any state. So long as the soul remains bound to this body, it endures sorrow and distress. The body suffers, and the soul suffers with it—for the body is the cause of all its anguish. The soul bears this only that it may develop the body. In this condition, the soul is in travail, tormented, made dependent upon the world, imprisoned until its Lord calls it forth. And the Lord will not call until the body has been perfected. So long as the self is joined to the body, it cannot find peace. Just as a wise man cannot find tranquility unless he divorces his beautiful but harlot wife—until then, he will not be satisfied, though she lavish upon him affection and love. Therefore, death is the name of wisdom, blessing, and mercy for virtuous souls. Those souls who surrender their bodily affliction find in death a blessing![^cite-12]

## The Reality and Essence of Soul and Body

One truth that a person often forgets is his own ⟪ar:' اصلیت و حقیقت '⟫ essence and reality—and whether this be called error, negligence, or folly, it causes him incalculable loss. He ought to consider what he is, what he is fashioned from, what is his foundation and true nature. Understanding this requires no great difficulty, only reflection. Body and soul together constitute a human being, wherein the soul is everything and the body is nothing. The body is the house; the soul is the master of the house. The body is the slave; the soul is the master. The body is the mount; the soul is the rider. If one thinks upon it: what is a soul without a body? A mere heap of dust—worse than dust itself. A heap of dirt remains inert where it lies; a body without a soul cannot remain still. They rush to bury it, for if delayed, it decays and releases foul odours.

And what becomes of the body even when united with the soul? It must be cleansed perpetually—bathed, washed, kept clean. It is full of filth. Food enters it and emerges corrupted and foul-smelling. Fluid seeps from every part—sweat in one place, refuse in another. Pure air enters the lungs only to be poisoned within. Many appear fair and beautiful from a distance, yet when approached and spoken to, the stench of their breath becomes apparent. Where is fragrance to be found? Perfumes must be applied to mask and conceal the body's odour. The foolish man labours to groom it—how he strives and labours to beautify it! Yet what does it finally become? A heap of discarded refuse. Meanwhile, the soul that owns this body forgets to groom itself.

Is there not a need to reform the self? Does it not require healing? Is it not sick? It is sick indeed—this is why it has entered this world. What are all the religions but remedies? The ordinances of religion are all prescriptions for curing the diseases of the soul. This soul bears diseases and wounds.[^cite-13] The true cure must be directed toward the self; even if the body is wholly neglected, no harm results. At this juncture, the path of the people of wisdom diverges from the path of the people of the world. Those who employ reason and wisdom to groom their soul and make it introspective become indifferent to the body. Yet they tend to the body for the soul's sake and betterment.

But the body—is it a useless thing? No! Despite its defects, if used rightly, it is a noble instrument indeed. Through communion with the soul and by following wisdom, it attains great heights. A good human being is one whose soul and body are both refined; a wicked human being is the opposite. The difference is this: the pious person purifies both soul and body; together with the soul, the body too attains heights. These purified soul and body together lead to good deeds, to obedience to religion and divine law, to fulfilling Allah's commands, to generosity and justice. Many souls have graced this world and remain alive even after centuries have passed—their virtues and excellences keep them living still. Nushirwan, the just king, is remembered to this day for his justice, though many ages have passed since his death. Hatim Tai remains alive through his generosity. Some have achieved immortality through noble deeds, others through knowledge and wisdom. What manner of beings are these? They came as embodiments of virtue and departed, yet they lit the lamp of guidance for all humanity. Such pious ones also made their bodies pure and fragrant through their excellence. Therefore, if the body is to be groomed and made fragrant, it must be done in the proper way and with right understanding.

There is no limit to goodness and virtue. Not every person possesses resources vast enough to perform great deeds and grand acts of charity. Can a person of limited means not be virtuous? Every person can be virtuous if he acts for good according to his ability and to the extent of his means. For a believer, even to command the right and forbid the wrong—⟪ar-quote:امر بالمعروف والنہی عن المنکر⟫—is a great virtue, even if one merely speaks the good without yet practising it fully. The observance of religion and divine law, of the lawful and the unlawful, of good manners and right conduct—these all lie within the reach of every person. One need not possess great wealth and abundant resources to be virtuous. However, if someone possesses wealth but does not spend it wisely, instead locking it away in safes and banks, neglecting to serve people despite his resources and capacity, such a person is truly unfortunate—living a purposeless life through his own negligence. What memory shall such a person leave behind? Moreover, they often wrong people, usurp their rights, commit injustices; naturally their names shall be mentioned with curses. Therefore, if life has been granted, one must not forget life's purpose. What wisdom lies in devoting every moment to the transient world while forgetting the good of the eternal Hereafter, thereby defiling both body and soul? How fleeting and unreliable is the life of this world—does this require explanation? And if one does not heed reason even after knowing all this, who can counsel one who strikes himself with an axe upon his own hands and feet? Who would sympathize with such a soul?

Life is brief, and it demands that we think deeply about life's purpose and then strive to achieve it. The saying of the beloved Prophet should always be kept close to the heart: ⟪ar-quote:خَیرَ النَّاسَ مَن یَنفَعُ النَّاسَ⟫ "The best of people are those who benefit others." Make this blessed decree your life's goal. Come—do whatever you can, however small, to benefit someone. The respite is very short! Let not life pass away without you having done something of worth. May Allah grant all people understanding of life's true purpose and enable them to fulfil it, so that when our soul departs the physical world and enters the Hereafter, it shall be successful and adorned. And may we be among those whom the angels welcome, saying:
