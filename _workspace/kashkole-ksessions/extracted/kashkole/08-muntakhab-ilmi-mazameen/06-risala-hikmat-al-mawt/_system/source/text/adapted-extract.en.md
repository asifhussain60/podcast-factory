## The Wisdom of Death in Human Existence

God has wisely conjoined the soul with the body and established the intellect (al-ʿAql) as its supreme faculty. The intellect acts benevolently toward the self, enabling it to transform back to its primordial state and return to the spiritual realm. Similarly, the soul is kind to the body so that it may transmute itself from its material aspect (jismanīyat) into its essential, psychic nature (nafsānīyat). This transformation constitutes the supreme wisdom of death.[^cite-1]

All animals abhor death and cherish life. Most scholars and sages affirm that death is real and that God has concealed a profound wisdom within it—yet they comprehend not what that wisdom is. They cite the Quranic verse without understanding its depths:

> ⟪quran 67:2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ ٱلْمَوْتَ وَٱلْحَيَوٰةَ لِيَبْلُوَكُمْ أَيُّكُمْ أَحْسَنُ عَمَلًۭا ۚ وَهُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْغَفُورُ ‎⟫
> *Who has created death and life, that He may test you which of you is best in deed. And He is the All-Mighty, the Oft-Forgiving.*

Yet even as they recite these words and affirm them, they still despise death and love life. They prefer death only when worldly luxuries have been stripped from them, or when they can no longer partake of life's pleasures.

When the wise man contemplates the structure of the human form, he perceives that each organ has been laid out with perfect purposefulness. The bones connect through joints with exquisite precision; the limbs articulate upon them. Observing closely, he discerns that God has placed more joints at the body's sides and fewer between them. Within these intervals lie fine, soft substances—the muscles—upon which sensation and consciousness depend, and through which a person can move his limbs with ease.[^cite-2] Had these muscles not been kept supple, the joints could never have granted such fluidity of motion. The wise man then beholds a web of veins woven throughout the body, beginning in the liver's depths and coursing through the heart after embedding themselves in flesh. These vessels carry blood from all parts of the body to the heart. Similarly, a network of arteries distributes blood from the heart to all the organs. God has created clear segments in the body, layered upon one another, to deliver benefit and remove harm.

Furthermore, God has made this system equal for all human beings, male and female alike, with one special distinction: He created the sperm in the man and the womb in the woman. The sperm departs the man and reaches the woman's womb, where it is nurtured and perfected. Eventually, that sperm develops into a child and is born into the world. It then passes through the stages of childhood, youth, and old age. When the child reaches advanced years, he feels transformation within his body and witnesses his powers beginning to ebb away. Death strikes him. The inanimate body then undergoes alteration; it begins to produce air and odor. At last, it is immersed in soil where the body dissolves into the four elements.

In the maturity of the child, he begins to wonder at the child's creation in the womb and perceives that the fetus must pass through three afflictions: darkness within darkness—the darkened belly, the darkened womb. Only after traversing these three trials can the sperm be born into this world. He reflects on how the child lived in that confined space, treated with tenderness and comforts so that his creation would be fulfilled and his form would reach perfection. The baby remains in rotation within the womb—sometimes head upward, sometimes downward—until completion. Then it breaks through all barriers that prevented its departure. Blood and secretions flow forth, retained with wisdom so that the fetus might emerge easily. Then it meets its mother with great struggle and intensity. All these things astonish the intellect of scholars.[^cite-3]

After passing through these stages, the embryo transitions from the womb to the world and begins to receive the winds of existence, the rays of creation, the light of the sun, and the blessings of the world. Having considered all these things, the wise man understands: if God brought this child forth from such narrow confinement after his physical development, why would He not bring him from the hardships of this world once his soul has matured? Just as no restraint can prevent his emergence into the world after his completion in the womb, nothing will hinder his ascension to the higher realm at the fulfillment of his soul—not even the body! This is the wisdom of death: through it, God delivers the human soul from this narrow, dark, and impoverished place.

In this way, perceive that the state of the soul bears the same relation to the body that a fetus bears to the womb. The body is like the womb; the soul is like the fetus. The condition of the human soul after death is the same as that of a child at birth, for the death of the body in this world is in truth the birth of the soul in the Hereafter. Conversely, the birth of a fetus in this world is but its death in the womb. What we call death in this world is the birth of our soul in the Hereafter—this is why the Messenger of God declared: ⟪ar-quote:الدنیا رحم الآخرۃ⟫ "This world is like a mother's womb for the Hereafter." Only through the death of the body in this world can the soul be born in the next. There are two paths by which the soul may emerge from the world in safety:

## The Purpose of Death

All things have a beginning, which is called life, and an end, which is called death. Therefore, man's beginning is his life, and his end is his death. Life commences when a person becomes a sperm and settles in the womb. In the womb-world, he dies when he emerges from it, having passed through the stages of creation. This is called birth—and here his physical life (ʿālam al-ṭabīʿiyya) begins. When he completes this natural life and departs the body, the end of this physical existence is called death. Just as a child attains life through death in the womb, in the same manner the soul of man separates from the body and attains the life of the Hereafter. Death—the separation of soul from body—is the final frontier of the physical world for him; he enters the Hereafter only after crossing this threshold. Though the child completes his form and organs in the womb, he derives no benefit from this completion until he emerges. Similarly, the soul that acquires perfections and virtues while dwelling in the body obtains no true benefit from them until it departs the body.

Just as the mother's womb is a cramped, narrow, dark world compared to the physical realm, so too is the physical world a small, insignificant, dense place compared to the world of the Hereafter. Just as the womb is necessary for the child until he completes the stages of creation within it, so the natural world has served the soul until it attains perfections and virtues.

As the child must leave the womb upon completing his development or upon the end of his time there, so too must the human soul leave the body and the physical world after attaining perfections and virtues in the natural realm. Death is the cause of eternal life; worldly life is the cause of true death. Until man is born into this world, he cannot enter the Hereafter. Man's physical life is the cause of his physical death; his physical death causes his spiritual life in the Hereafter, which is eternal and everlasting. Thus great wisdom resides in both.

After the embryo is completed in the womb, it gains nothing by remaining there until it appears in the world through birth. Similarly, when the self attains its perfection by bonding with the body, and its virtues are complete, it derives no benefit from remaining in the body until it departs. When that form leaves the body, it then benefits from it in the Hereafter. For this reason death is wisdom: without it there is no eternal survival. It is necessary for the soul to leave the body because the body is dense and the soul is subtle. Since the Hereafter is subtle, only the subtle—which is the soul—can enter it; the body cannot, for it is dense.

## The Nature of Life

What is the meaning of the word "nature" (māhīya)? All things are identified within three circles: genus, species, and person. Within the genus resides the species; within the species resides the person. There are two types of life and death: physical and spiritual.

| Physical Life | Soul Death |

### Physical Life

Physical life is in truth the soul's use of its own body. When the soul ceases to employ its body, this is called physical death. Consciousness is nothing but the self employing the bodily senses. Sleep is merely the name for the temporary suspension of sense-use by the self.

### Spiritual Life

In contrast to physical life, the soul is an essence that exists from its own nature and is dependent on no other for existence—as the soul does depend on the body for physical life. The soul is alive (ḥayy) in its essence; it is a knower (ʿālim) by its nature and wise about its powers and faculties. It manifests its actions in the body, its forms, its soul and its configurations. For example, when the soul desires to worship, it causes the body to complete the parts of prayer. The body cannot do anything unless the soul exists as its mover. The life of the body consists in proximity to the soul and nearness to it—that is, the body should always remain close to its self. Just as air is dark in its essence, it is illuminated by the proximity of the sun's light, the moon's light, and the stars' radiance.

### Spiritual Death

Ignorance of one's self is the death of one's self. In this world, self-inflicted death occurs through indulgence in unnecessary things. The death of the body is not necessary for the soul's death. To immerse oneself in the body's desires, to revel in bodily pleasures, to pursue bodily lusts, and to remain heedless of one's own essence leads to the death of the soul. Such people recognize only the lowest, most degraded, fragmented physical life. Of them God says:

> ⟪quran 3:185⟫
> ⟪ar:‏كُلُّ نَفْسٍۢ ذَآئِقَةُ ٱلْمَوْتِ ۗ وَإِنَّمَا تُوَفَّوْنَ أُجُورَكُمْ يَوْمَ ٱلْقِيَمَةِ ۖ فَمَن زُحْزِحَ عَنِ ٱلنَّارِ وَأُدْخِلَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ فَقَدْ فَازَ ۗ وَمَا ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَآ إِلَّا مَتَعُ ٱلْغُرُورِ ‎⟫
> *Everyone shall taste death. And only on the Day of Resurrection shall you be paid your wages in full. And whoever is removed away from the Fire and admitted to Paradise, he indeed is successful. The life of this world is only the enjoyment of deception.*

And further:

> ⟪quran 29:64⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا هَذِهِ ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَآ إِلَّا لَهْوٌۭ وَلَعِبٌۭ ۚ وَإِنَّ ٱلدَّارَ ٱلْآخِرَةَ لَهِىَ ٱلْحَيَوَانُ ۚ لَوْ كَانُوا۟ يَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And this life of the world is only amusement and play! Verily, the home of the Hereafter, that is the life indeed, if they but knew.*

The life of the world is game (lahw) and jest (laʿab). This world is a place of pride on account of outward beauty, of competition in wealth and children. People wish to abide here forever, as God says:

> ⟪quran 30:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَعْلَمُونَ ظَهِرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَهُمْ عَنِ ٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ غَفِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *They know only the outside appearance of the life of the world, and they are heedless of the Hereafter.*

God also says:

> ⟪quran 4:77⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى ٱلَّذِينَ قِيلَ لَهُمْ كُفُّوٓا۟ أَيْدِيَكُمْ وَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ فَلَمَّا كُتِبَ عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلْقِتَالُ إِذَا فَرِيقٌۭ مِّنْهُمْ يَخْشَوْنَ ٱلنَّاسَ كَخَشْيَةِ ٱللَّهِ أَوْ أَشَدَّ خَشْيَةًۭ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ رَبَّنَا لِمَ كَتَبْتَ عَلَيْنَا ٱلْقِتَالَ لَوْلَآ أَخَّرْتَنَآ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ قَرِيبٍۢ ۗ قُلْ مَتَعُ ٱلدُّنْيَا قَلِيلٌۭ وَٱلْآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌۭ لِّمَنِ ٱتَّقَىٰ وَلَا تُظْلَمُونَ فَتِيلًا ‎⟫
> *Have you not seen those who were told to hold back their hands and perform prayer and give alms, yet when fighting was ordained for them, behold, a section of them fear men as they fear God or even more. They say: "Our Lord! Why have you ordained for us fighting? Would that you had granted us respite for a short period?" Say: "Short is the enjoyment of this world. The Hereafter is far better for him who fears God, and you shall not be dealt with unjustly even equal to the Fatila."*

Many other verses likewise condemn the world and those who love it. The life of the Hereafter is in truth the name for keeping the soul alive there. The soul is eternal life itself.

### Physical Death

The body is dead in its original essence—it cannot move without the soul and cannot sustain itself by its own nature. Without the soul, it changes, becomes foul and ugly, and finally disperses into its components, returning to its origin, as God says:

> ⟪quran 20:55⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ مِنْهَا خَلَقْنَكُمْ وَفِيهَا نُعِيدُكُمْ وَمِنْهَا نُخْرِجُكُمْ تَارَةً أُخْرَىٰ ‎⟫
> *Thereof We created you, and into it We shall return you, and from it We shall bring you out once again.*

The word "death" (al-mawt) refers to the soul, not to the body, for the body is already dead. Death derives from the word wafāʾ, meaning the fulfillment of a promise. When the soul has progressed and fulfilled the covenant it made with its Imam and reached its appointed measure, it becomes obligatory for it to die so that it may receive the boundless reward for its labor. This is why death is wisdom and mercy from God Almighty!

## The Part and the Whole

Our soul is one in relation to the spiritual world, and our body is one part (juzʾ) in relation to the physical world. The soul-world stands as the whole (kull) before the self; the physical world stands as the whole before the body. Each part always maintains a relationship and connection with its whole.

The connection of one soul with another soul is the completion of arithmetic (ʿilm al-riyāḍa), just as a number connects with another number. By adding two numbers, a new number is created; by multiplying them, a second emerges; by dividing them, a third arises, and so on. The manner in which two numbers relate depends on their operations. Similarly, the same effects are established in the manner and way one soul relates to another soul. When two souls meet—the higher partial soul and the lower partial soul—the higher influences and creates the wisdom and virtues hidden in potential within the lower. The purpose of the higher self is to fulfill the lower self, so that the partial soul can attain its perfection and transform into form (ṣūra).[^cite-4] This transformation is called perfection (kamāl).

The husband develops physical contact with his wife and draws the child from it. The child, who possessed the power in potential, manifests in actuality when the two unite in a certain manner. Similarly, the lower takes on the form of its whole. The higher trains the lower, teaching him governance, prudence, virtues, correct beliefs, pure deeds, and true knowledge, attempting to make him like himself. This is expressed in the language of wisdom: ⟪ar-quote:اِنَّھَا تُشَبِّہُ بِالالہ⟫ *"The one below became like the one above him!"*

When the human soul reaches its goal and fully expresses its virtues, the body becomes useless. When the partial self attains perfection, it no longer requires the body. At this point it leaves the body and returns to a higher state, where it is then nurtured and developed to ever higher levels—a development impossible while bound to the body. The virtues and faculties the soul created by dwelling in the body are now, upon departure, nurtured and cultivated at progressively higher standards. As God says:

> ⟪quran 56:61⟫
> ⟪ar:‏عَلَىٰٓ أَن نُّبَدِّلَ أَمْثَلَكُمْ وَنُنشِئَكُمْ فِى مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *To transfigure you and create you in forms that you know not.*

Then God created for him the next creation (nushʾat al-ākhira), preparing for his soul another dwelling superior to his body. While attached to the body, he was merely a soul (nafs); now that he has reached his limit, he has become a form (ṣūra) and develops as such.

Just as a child confined in the narrowness, darkness, and suffocation of the womb cannot even imagine the vastness of the world, the illumination of light, or the dawn, similarly the soul cannot imagine the world of the Hereafter while bound to the body. The relationship between the womb's duration and the known and unknown between birth is the same as between life and death. Until the soul dies and is born in the next world, it cannot understand the greatness of that place or perceive the things there.[^cite-5] Just as a fetus cannot feel or perceive the pleasures of the world while in the womb. This is why the Prophet declared: ⟪ar-quote:الناسُ نیامٌ فأذا مات اِنصَبَتُ⟫ *"People are asleep; when they die, they awake."* Their sleep is their negligence, and even after death, as with the disbelievers, the intoxication of death may overwhelm them with reality. Then their soul leaves their body. At that moment, the soul beholds the realities that called it to heaven in life. As God says:

> ⟪quran 50:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَّقَدْ كُنتَ فِى غَفْلَةٍۢ مِّنَ هَذَا فَكَشَفْنَا عَنكَ غِطَآءَكَ فَبَصَرُكَ ٱلْيَوْمَ حَدِيدٌۭ ‎⟫
> *"Indeed you were heedless of this, now We have removed your covering, and sharp is your sight this Day!"*

This is why God commands the Prophet: *"Worship your Lord until certainty comes to you."* And in another place it is said that every human being shall taste death:

> ⟪quran 15:99⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱعْبُدْ رَبَّكَ حَتَّىٰ يَأْتِيَكَ ٱلْيَقِينُ ‎⟫
> *And worship your Lord until there comes unto you the certainty (i.e. death).*

As long as the child grows in the mother's womb, the mother values him greatly, knowing that it is through him her child develops. But when the child emerges from her womb, she despises it and will not even touch it. So too is it with the body in which the soul grows: while the soul was developing in it, this body held great dignity and value. But after the soul leaves it, the body has no value for it. For this reason the body is buried at once. Death is wise, for without it the soul cannot return to its Lord, and it cannot attain the blessings God has promised it.

> ⟪quran 89:27-30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيَّتُهَا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ‎ ‏ٱرْجِعِىٓ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةًۭ مَّرْضِيَّةًۭ ‎ ‏فَٱدْخُلِى فِى عِبَدِى ‎ ‏وَٱدْخُلِى جَنَّتِى ‎⟫
> *"O you soul in complete rest and satisfaction! Come back to your Lord, well-pleased and well-pleasing unto Him! Enter among My honoured slaves, and enter My Paradise!"*

That is, true return to God occurs when the soul is pleased to leave the body and the body is also willing to release it. This shows that death is the name for wisdom and is the noblest of God's forgiveness and rewards to His servants, for it is the cause of eternal, perpetual life (al-ḥayāt al-yezdānīya wa-l-dāʾima).

*The self will either evolve into a form or descend into a body. Death is the cause of the body's destruction so that the soul may come to life. The self is made from the finest parts of the body.*

## The Tale of the Blind Man and the Lame Man

There were two men dwelling in a city. One was blind; the other was crippled in both legs. They became companions and came to a garden, wishing to enter it. When the garden's owner saw them, he felt compassion and said to them: "If I shelter you in this garden and provide you with fruit according to your needs, will you conduct yourselves well and not spread mischief or spoil the other fruits?" They answered, "How could we spoil your garden and fruits? Our condition is plain: one of us is blind, the other crippled. How could we reach the trees and damage them?" The garden's owner then said, "Very well, enter." He appointed a dwelling place for them and ordered the gardener to care for them and provide fruit according to their needs.

After giving these orders, the owner departed. The two dwelt in the garden while the gardener cared for them, continually providing fruit as needed, and the garden flourished. One day the crippled man said to his blind companion, "O wretched one! You have feet; you walk. There are all manner of fruits upon the trees that the gardener does not give us. Devise some trick to obtain them." The blind man replied, "You have kindled desire in me, yet you can see while I cannot. Tell me what we should do." They began to conspire together. The blind man said to him: "I have thought of a plan. You shall carry me on your shoulders and take me through the garden. Wherever fine fruit is found, I shall direct you. You shall bear me there, and I shall pluck the fruit with my hand. Where the fruit hangs from high branches, I shall strike it down with a stick, and we shall gather it afterward. But all of this must happen while the gardener is away." The blind man said, "You have devised a good scheme. Tomorrow we shall execute it."

The next day, when the gardener closed the gate and left, the cripple mounted the blind man's shoulders. Together they spoiled the fruits in the garden, then returned to their place and slept. When the gardener returned, the garden's condition was not hidden from him. The reserved fruits were missing. Coming to them, he asked whether anyone had entered in his absence. "We know nothing of it," they replied. The blind man said, "I cannot see," and the cripple said, "I was sleeping." The gardener believed them. The next day he departed again, and the two rose and spoiled more fruit than before. Upon his return and seeing the garden's state, the gardener feared what the owner would say—that either the gardener sold the fruit or failed to protect the garden. He began to think how he might discover who was causing the trouble.

The following day, the gardener told them he was leaving, but in fact hid behind a wall. When the gardener's absence was confirmed, they went to the orchard and began plucking and destroying fruit. The gardener watched their deeds; when they had finished and returned to their place, he came to them and said, "O you wretched ones! Is this the reward for the owner's kindness? Thus do you ruin his garden?" The two were frightened at his words and fell silent. The gardener said, "I have seen with my own eyes what you have done." Upon hearing that the gardener had witnessed everything, they confessed and said, "Do not tell the owner of the garden. We repent and shall not do such things in future." The gardener obeyed them and began to admonish: "Look, if you need fruit, ask me. In this way neither the garden nor its owner will be harmed, and you shall not be guilty before the owner. Both confessed when they heard the gardener's words. When he left and returned to his work, the two resumed their acts, indeed with greater mischief than before. When the gardener returned and saw all this, he counseled them again and reminded them of God, but they did not obey and continued as before.

One day the owner of the garden arrived. The gardener was obliged to inform him of the garden's condition and the two men's deeds. The owner said, "I feared that this cripple would climb upon the blind man's back and together create riot in the garden." The gardener replied that they had done all this despite his counsel and refusal. The owner declared, "They deserve punishment," and ordered that they be first beaten thoroughly, then expelled from the garden, so that they would wander the forests, pursued by beasts, until they perished of hunger and thirst. And this was done.

In this story, the self is called crippled or lame because it accomplishes nothing by itself but does all through the body. The body is called blind because it walks at the self's command without reasoning. The garden is likened to the world; the fruits to the world's pleasures and desires. The owner of the garden is God Almighty. The intellect of man is what tells him what is good and bad, commanding the right and forbidding wrong. The intellect commands the self to attain the world's pleasures according to its needs, but the self becomes enslaved to lust, disobeys the intellect, and uses the body as a means to commit all things harmful to itself.[^cite-6] In this way, by refusing to obey the intellect, a person corrupts both his religion and his world, falling into error and misguidance until the angels of God's wrath seize him. Then he regrets his evil deeds. All of this becomes known when the time comes for his soul to depart, and this is the outcome God has announced:

> ⟪quran 22:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَعْبُدُ ٱللَّهَ عَلَىٰ حَرْفٍۢ ۖ فَإِنْ أَصَابَهُۥ خَيْرٌ ٱطْمَأَنَّ بِهِۦ ۖ وَإِنْ أَصَابَتْهُ فِتْنَةٌ ٱنقَلَبَ عَلَىٰ وَجْهِهِۦ خَسِرَ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَٱلْآخِرَةَ ۚ ذَلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْخُسْرَانُ ٱلْمُبِينُ ‎⟫
> *And among mankind is he who worships God as it were upon the very edge; if good befalls him, he is content therewith; but if a trial befalls him, he turns back on his face. He loses both this world and the Hereafter. That is the evident loss.*

In contrast, there are the righteous, saved by piety and good deeds, for whom God has decreed:

> ⟪quran 39:61⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَيُنَجِّى ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّقَوْا۟ بِمَفَازَتِهِمْ لَا يَمَسُّهُمُ ٱلسُّوٓءُ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎⟫
> *And Allah will deliver those who fear Him to their places of success. Evil shall not touch them, nor shall they grieve.*

This anecdote illuminates the mentality of the common person and its fate. Those who obey the self's word and follow its desires are described in various ways in the Holy Quran. To the wrongdoers and lovers of this world it is said:

> ⟪quran 2:86⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱشْتَرَوُا۟ ٱلْحَيَوٰةَ ٱلدُّنْيَا بِٱلْآخِرَةِ ۖ فَلَا يُخَفَّفُ عَنْهُمُ ٱلْعَذَابُ وَلَا هُمْ يُنصَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Those are they who have bought the life of this world at the price of the Hereafter. Their torment shall not be lightened nor shall they be helped.*

For those entranced by the world and its comforts, He says:

> ⟪quran 3:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏زُيِّنَ لِلنَّاسِ حُبُّ ٱلشَّهَوَتِ مِنَ ٱلنِّسَآءِ وَٱلْبَنِينَ وَٱلْقَنطِيرِ ٱلْمُقَنطَرَةِ مِنَ ٱلذَّهَبِ وَٱلْفِضَّةِ وَٱلْخَيْلِ ٱلْمُسَوَّمَةِ وَٱلْأَنْعَمِ وَٱلْحَرْثِ ۗ ذَلِكَ مَتَعُ ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا ۖ وَٱللَّهُ عِندَهُۥ حُسْنُ ٱلْمَـَٔابِ ‎⟫
> *Beautified for men is the love of things they covet: women, children, much of gold and silver, branded beautiful horses, cattle and well-tilled land. This is the pleasure of the present world's life; but Allah has the excellent return with Him.*

For ordinary life, He says:

> ⟪quran 6:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَآ إِلَّا لَعِبٌۭ وَلَهْوٌۭ ۖ وَلَلدَّارُ ٱلْآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌۭ لِّلَّذِينَ يَتَّقُونَ ۗ أَفَلَا تَعْقِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And the life of this world is nothing but play and amusement. But far better is the house in the Hereafter for those who fear Allah. Will you not then understand?*

For those gone astray, He says:

> ⟪quran 13:34⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَّهُمْ عَذَابٌۭ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا ۖ وَلَعَذَابُ ٱلْآخِرَةِ أَشَقُّ ۖ وَمَا لَهُم مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ مِن وَاقٍۢ ‎⟫
> *For them is a torment in the life of this world, and surely harder is the torment of the Hereafter. And they have no protector against Allah.*

In contrast, good news is given to the righteous and to them it is said:

> ⟪quran 10:64⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَهُمُ ٱلْبُشْرَىٰ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَفِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ ۚ لَا تَبْدِيلَ لِكَلِمَتِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ ذَلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ‎⟫
> *For them are glad tidings in the life of the present world and in the Hereafter. No change can there be in the Words of Allah. This is indeed the supreme success.*

There is good news for the servants of Allah and the believers:

> ⟪quran 40:51⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّا لَنَنصُرُ رُسُلَنَا وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَيَوْمَ يَقُومُ ٱلْأَشْهَدُ ‎⟫
> *Verily, We will indeed make victorious Our Messengers and those who believe in this world's life and on the Day when the witnesses will stand forth.*

If the life of this world had been the true purpose, it would have been granted to God's beloved Prophet. But God did not reserve it for His beloved Prophet and said to him:

> ⟪quran 39:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّكَ مَيِّتٌۭ وَإِنَّهُم مَّيِّتُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily, you will die and verily, they too will die.*

The world and life in the world are beneficial to those who obey their intellect and perform righteous deeds—the gates of mercy open to them. Their abode is one stage succeeding another for the soul's development. This is the place where the angels welcome the arrival and say:

> ⟪quran 39:73⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَسِيقَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّقَوْا۟ رَبَّهُمْ إِلَى ٱلْجَنَّةَ زُمَرًا ۖ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءُوهَا وَفُتِحَتْ أَبْوَبُهَا وَقَالَ لَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَا سَلَمٌ عَلَيْكُمْ طِبْتُمْ فَٱدْخُلُوهَا خَلِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *And those who kept their duty to their Lord will be led to Paradise in groups, till when they reach it and its gates will be opened, and its keepers will say: Peace be upon you! You have done well, so enter here to abide therein.*

But the one who rejects the intellect's counsel and follows the soul, deceiving himself and committing evil deeds—he who deems religion and the righteous path light and worthless, turning from them, disobeying God's commands, and treating people unjustly with oppression and mistreatment in his dealings, not fulfilling their rights but usurping them—such a man has corrupted his own end. Death comes to him as punishment, causing his soul to suffer painful torments. He was warned of this fate and counseled to avoid it, yet he paid no attention and heeded not the sound of these exhortations. Death is the moment when the dying perceive their fate, both good and ill. He who has done good fears not death but is prepared for it. For him, death is a source of joy, for it ends his labor and troubled existence; his soul is freed and he enters the Hereafter, receiving rewards and elevation. In contrast, the person who has committed evil by obeying the self is always afraid of death. He knows his misdeeds and the terrible punishment awaiting him after death, and thus dreads death. He desires to avoid it at all costs. When death finally comes and his terrible end begins to appear, he cries out. He begs, "If my Lord would grant me respite, I might make amends for some of my sins, do some good deeds, take some vengeance, pay the rights of people, seek their forgiveness for wrongs I have done, and repent for my sins." But now the time is finished, the door of repentance has closed, and the decree has been issued. In its execution, not one hour less or more will pass. The tongue departs from the present, saying:

*Look at me as a beholder.* *Listen to me, which is wise admonition.*

God says in Surah al-Munāfiqūn:

> ⟪quran 63:10-11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنفِقُوا۟ مِن مَّا رَزَقْنَكُم مِّن قَبْلِ أَن يَأْتِىَ أَحَدَكُمُ ٱلْمَوْتُ فَيَقُولَ رَبِّ لَوْلَآ أَخَّرْتَنِىٓ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ قَرِيبٍۢ فَأَصَّدَّقَ وَأَكُن مِّنَ ٱلصَّلِحِينَ ‎ ‏وَلَن يُؤَخِّرَ ٱللَّهُ نَفْسًا إِذَا جَآءَ أَجَلُهَا ۚ وَٱللَّهُ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And spend of that with which We have provided you, before death comes to one of you and he says: "My Lord! If only You would give me respite for a little while, then I should give Sadaqah and be among the righteous." And Allah grants respite to none when his appointed time comes. And Allah is All-Aware of what you do.*

*In this verse, spending one's sustenance means converting knowledge into action.*

In the physical world, life and death are in truth concerns of the soul. The body is subservient to the soul and serves as its instrument. Therefore, while explaining the reality of man, the Master of Creation, the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, said: ⟪ar:مولائے کائنات امیر المؤمنین حضرت علی ابن ابی طالب⟫

| **Ajhud ʿalā al-nafs wa-stakmal faḍāʾilhā** | **You are a human being in the soul, not in the body** |
| --- | --- |
| Perfect yourself and complete your virtues. For you are made of the soul and not of the body. |

Since the soul has attained eternity (abadīya), he has explained its beginning and end in one of his sermons as follows:

⟪ar-quote:اِنَّمَا خَلَقتُم لِلاَبَدِ * وَلٰکِن مِنَ دَّارِ اِلَی دَّارِ تُنقَلُونَ * مِنَ الاَصلاَبِ اِلَی الاَرحَامِ * وَ مِنَ الاَرحَامِ اِلَی الدُّنیَا * وَمِنَ الدُّنیَا اِلَی البَرزَخِ * وَ مِنَ البَرزَخِ اِلَی الجَنَّۃَ وَالنَّارِ⟫

*You were created for eternity, but you are transferred from house to house: from the loins to the wombs, from the wombs to the world, from the world to the Barzakh, and from the Barzakh to Paradise or Hell.*

## The Soul's Progression

Brother, know this! Children have souls that are intellects in potential (ʿāqil bi-al-quwwa). Although the child possesses intellect, its powers have not yet been actualized. There are people who are intellects in actuality (ʿāqil bi-al-fiʿl), and knowers in potential (ʿālim bi-al-quwwa). There are others who are knowers in actuality (ʿālim bi-al-fiʿl) and philosophers in potential (faylasūf bi-al-quwwa). And there are those who are philosophers in actuality (faylasūf bi-al-fiʿl) and angels in potential (malāʾika bi-al-quwwa). When their souls leave their bodies, they become angels. And because of death, it is wisdom and mercy from God.

| Children | Adult Intellects | Philosophers | Wise Men |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Potential Intellects | Actual Intellects | Actual Philosophers | Potential Angels |

*The "wise one" (al-ḥakīm) is he who practices his wisdom, while the "philosopher" (al-faylasūf) does not practice his knowledge and philosophy.*

Know that when minerals develop, they grow in the bodies of plants. When plants develop, they enter the bodies of animals—and humanity is the noblest of animals. The plant's form is inverted, with its head in the ground and feet above. Yet even as the origin points downward, it obtains nobility through the animal soul (nafs al-ḥayawānīya). In other words, the animal soul brings death to this plant and causes it to develop. The animal's form is oriented so that head and legs are level. Nevertheless, the human soul receives it, by virtue of its nobility and elevation, redeeming it from its original form.

The human form, then, stands like an upright position or vertical line. It is positioned between Heaven and Hell. Things of hell are beneath his feet and things of heaven above his head. The form of the angels (ṣūrat al-malāʾika) liberates man from his standing form. That is, whoever attains his soul and saves it shall have that soul become angelic (malik). The angels either ascend to heaven or descend to hell. As God says:

> ⟪quran 95:4-6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا ٱلْإِنسَنَ فِىٓ أَحْسَنِ تَقْوِيمٍۢ ‎ ‏ثُمَّ رَدَدْنَهُ أَسْفَلَ سَفِلِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَّا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ فَلَهُمْ أَجْرٌ غَيْرُ مَمْنُونٍۢ ‎⟫
> *Verily, We created man of the best stature, then We reduced him to the lowest of the low, save those who believe and do righteous deeds—for them shall be a reward without end.*

Death is the "reward without end" (ajr ghayru mamūn) that God mentions in this verse. After death, he shall have reward in the Hereafter that can never be exhausted. Consider all these matters, for you stand in a very dangerous place. If you do not progress toward heaven, you shall surely descend to hell. I urge you to hasten in righteous deeds (aʿmāl ṣāliḥa), noble character (akhlāq jamīla), and true knowledge (ʿulūm ḥaqīqa), so that you may be delivered from the fire of hell, which is the world itself. I hope you shall pass through the heavens and reach Paradise, a place of eternity, joy, delight, and bliss. I pray that you join me there in the company of the Prophets (anbiyāʾ), the Truthful Ones (ṣiddīqīn), the Martyrs (shuhadāʾ), and the Righteous (ṣāliḥīn). There you will find good friends and the finest of companions for one another—and this is a bounty from God that comes through death.

## The Governance of the Soul

To govern (siyāsa) means to make a plan concerning something. The self is what it does as a manager (mudbir) in conducting affairs (tadbīr) on the body in order to release its inner potential. The self convinces the body to act so that it may benefit itself. In the same way, every superior soul conspires with the lower soul and persuades it to perform progressive actions.

When the lower self is satisfied, it allows the superior self to dwell in its house and to manage its family and servants—though they are its own family and servants. Yet it trusts in the higher self and grants it the right to rule over them, for it knows and understands that this is for the good of itself, its family, and its servants. Similarly, the body trusts the soul and permits it to exercise command over its various organs, directing them to act in ways that improve all.

When the soul embraces the governance and strategy of its superior and teacher, and at his behest adopts the finest justice in character with his family, household, and servants, it then becomes possible for him to leave the house and govern his tribe. When he successfully conducts the affairs of his tribe properly, it becomes obligatory for him to govern all the people of the city. And when he has properly administered the people of the city, it becomes incumbent upon him to practice divine governance (nāmūs al-ilāhī) and show others the right way.[^cite-7] It is possible for the one who has executed divine governance correctly to ascend and progress in the heavenly realm! There he receives the reward and elevation of all the deeds he performed while in the world.

Know well, brother, that this is why death is wisdom! If tribulation befalls you or if you find divine law (nāmūs al-ilāhī) and divine governance (nāmūs al-ilāhīya) difficult, then seize his family and his guardian so that you may be delivered from Hell through their intercession and ascend with them to the kingdom of the heavens, entering Paradise by God's mercy, grace, and forgiveness.

## The Union of Body and Soul

The Banu Karim (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) stated: ⟪ar-quote:إن القبر روضۃ من ریاض الجنۃ أو حفرۃ من حفر النیران⟫ "The grave is either one of the gardens of Paradise or one of the pits of the Fire." For the soul that has been separated from the body at death, the grave becomes a paradise. Conversely, if the soul remains imprisoned within the body, then that body becomes for it a pit of torment. The grave of the soul is indeed the body itself. This is why Sayyidna Jaʿfar ibn Sayyidna Sulayman declares: "I am in the grave, and Sayyidna Sulayman is in the grave, because all the knowledge of the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.s.) is buried in Ali."[^cite-8]

The self acquires knowledge, wisdom, and virtues while residing within the body. Yet for the self, the body constitutes a separate realm—a place where the self makes all its commands known to the flesh, even as one obeys every utterance of the divine. When the soul begins to walk upon the straight path, it receives the discipline of the higher self, which becomes the dwelling-place for the divine light. The body has been fashioned as the straight path (ṣirāṭ al-mumaddūd bayn al-dunyā wa-l-ākhirah) between this world and the Hereafter. In other words, the body is the way upon which the self travels. Should one perform righteous deeds through the body, one shall flourish; otherwise, one shall go astray. And when the soul has crossed this bodily path and freed itself from the calamities that accompany it, that soul becomes capable of treading the paths that follow.

It is a lamentable state when the soul becomes a prisoner in its own body and becomes enslaved to it (kanz). The soul becomes a servant to the pleasures of the body. The body has been likened to a maidservant because it is the source of every impurity: filth, decay, urine, phlegm, saliva, blood, pus, and foul-smelling perspiration. All these defects arise from and accumulate within the body (jasm). The first state of the body is sperm, which contains the force of manhood (quwwat al-rujūliyyah); its initial condition is base, and its final state is a putrefying corpse. Between these two extremes one lives one's entire life.

The soul is ceaselessly purifying the waste products of this body through ritual ablution, bathing, and perfuming, striving always to restore health to this ailing vessel. It covers its nakedness (ʿarīyyaniyt) with garments; it shields it from cold, heat, hunger, thirst, and countless other calamities. All the filth and impurity that exists in the world springs from the body! Were physicality not to exist, there would be no corruption. When a soul ceases to acquire knowledge, to worship, to contemplate its covenant, and to prepare for the Hereafter—when instead it indulges in bodily pleasures such as fine clothing, food, drink, elaborate dwellings, land, women, and riding—it becomes like one who worships an idol day and night. Of such persons Allah says (interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 25:43⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَرَءَيْتَ مَنِ ٱتَّخَذَ إِلَهَهُۥ هَوَىٰهُ أَفَأَنتَ تَكُونُ عَلَيْهِ وَكِيلًا ‎⟫
> *Have you (O Muhammad PBUH) seen him who has taken as his ilah (god) his own desire? Would you then be a Wakîl (a disposer of his affairs or a watcher) over him?*

> ⟪quran 45:23⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَفَرَءَيْتَ مَنِ ٱتَّخَذَ إِلَهَهُۥ هَوَىٰهُ وَأَضَلَّهُ ٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ عِلْمٍۢ وَخَتَمَ عَلَىٰ سَمْعِهِۦ وَقَلْبِهِۦ وَجَعَلَ عَلَىٰ بَصَرِهِۦ غِشَوَةًۭ فَمَن يَهْدِيهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أَفَلَا تَذَكَّرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Have you seen him who takes his own lust (vain desires) as his ilah (god), and Allah knowing (him as such), left him astray, and sealed his hearing and his heart, and put a cover on his sight. Who then will guide him after Allah? Will you not then remember?*

## The Body as Disbeliever and Adversary

Our body is a disbeliever (kāfir) in relation to the soul. It is called disbelieving because, just as the disbeliever fails to recognize Allah, so the body fails to recognize the greatness and virtues of the soul. Like a disbeliever, the body does not know who provides for it or who nurtures it. The body is ignorant, stubborn, and hasty, dwelling not upon future concerns but rushing to satisfy every immediate craving. Though the body appears outwardly as a friend to the soul, inwardly it is hostile and treacherous.

The body is also called Iblis (the Devil) because it exists in a state of whispered doubt (waswās) and never rests in a single state; it perpetually invites enmity. The body is a corpse that the soul carries upon its shoulders, yet the soul does not grow weary under this burden—only when it finally buries the body in the earth does it find relief.[^cite-9]

In the same manner, the body is a shadow interposed between us and the sun, possessing no true reality. The darkness of the four humours (the four temperaments: akhṭāt arbaʿah—black bile, yellow bile, phlegm, and blood) prevents us from perceiving the light of the intellect. Though the body appears to produce effects, it remains ignorant of its own actions. It satisfies hunger by eating, yet it cannot distinguish between the lawful (ḥalāl) and the forbidden (ḥarām).

In comparison with the body, the individual soul (nafs juzʾiyyah) stands exceedingly high and noble, yet exists in great distress, trapped as it is in this world. It has been placed within the body (which is as unformed matter, hayūlā) to endure the tumults that arise. The soul is forever conscious of imprisonment, even when surrounded by abundance and pleasure.

Consider the example of a wise and virtuous man who has become enamoured of a beautiful but depraved woman—ignorant, impoverished, and immoral. He must now spend his life with her. She constantly demands of the sage fine foods, luxurious garments, jewels, ornately decorated houses filled with unnecessary things, and endless desires. The poor sage strives to fulfill all these desires because of the depth of his affection and love for this woman, hoping somehow to render her wise and sensible. It is solely in this hope that he endures all her depravities. Were it not for this, long ago would he have fled? His focus remains forever upon attempting to reform his wife, until he neglects his own concerns and abandons the cultivation of his own virtues. In his desire to please her, he forgets the city from which he came, his relatives and kinfolk who raised him, and the blessing within which he was nurtured. Though he himself is wise and learned, he becomes like a follower of Satan, overwhelmed by his love and her pleasures. Allah speaks of such a Satan:

> ⟪quran 7:27⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَبَنِىٓ آدَمَ لَا يَفْتِنَنَّكُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ كَمَآ أَخْرَجَ أَبَوَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱلْجَنَّةِ يَنزِعُ عَنْهُمَا لِبَاسَهُمَا لِيُرِيَهُمَا سَوْءَتِهِمَآ ۗ إِنَّهُۥ يَرَىٰكُمْ هُوَ وَقَبِيلُهُۥ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَا تَرَوْنَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّا جَعَلْنَا ٱلشَّيَطِينَ أَوْلِيَآءَ لِلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ ‎⟫
> *O Children of Adam! Let not Shaitan (Satan) deceive you, as he got your parents [Adam and Hawwa (Eve)] out of Paradise, stripping them of their raiments, to show them their private parts. Verily, he and Qabîluhu (his soldiers from the jinns or his tribe) see you from where you cannot see them. Verily, We made the Shayatin (devils) Auliya' (protectors and helpers) for those who believe not.*

**The Body is Like Iblis, Who Cast Adam and Eve from Paradise**

Know that the power of the soul belongs not to this world, but to the heavens. Its origin lies in the spiritual realm where it must labor. That soul is alive in itself and requires neither food nor clothing—unlike the body, which has dire need of these things. The body is therefore enslaved to such necessities for its own preservation. Everything a person needs to live in this world arises from the body's demand. Every such desire is the cry of a body approaching decay and dissolution. The soul strives its utmost to elevate the body to its own state and to draw benefit from it.

The body is never constant in any state. As long as the soul remains joined to the body for its appointed time, the soul will be reproached with sorrow and distress. The body suffers, and the soul suffers with it—suffering caused by the body itself. The soul endures all this solely so that it might refine the body. In this condition the soul is distressed, afflicted, tormented, and rendered dependent upon the world, where it remains imprisoned until its Lord summons it. Nor will the Lord call the soul forth until it has perfected the body. As long as the self remains bound to the body, it cannot find rest. Just as a wise and learned man cannot find peace whilst married to a beautiful but dissolute wife, so the soul finds no tranquility. It will not be satisfied with her love and affection until it parts from her. Therefore, death is for righteous souls the name of wisdom, blessing, and mercy. Those souls that shed the burden and weariness of the body—at that moment death becomes a blessing for them.

## The True Nature of Soul and Body

One thing that people commonly overlook is their own fundamental reality and essence (aṣliyyah wa-ḥaqīqah). Whether from mistake, negligence, error, or foolishness, this forgetfulness brings them great loss and ruin. One ought to consider what one is, of what one is composed, what one's foundation and true reality are. It is not difficult to comprehend, if one would but think and understand. Body and soul together constitute a human being, wherein the soul is everything and the body is nothing. The body is the house and the soul is the master of the house; the body is the servant and the soul is the master; the body is ridden and the soul is the rider. If one considers it carefully: what is a soul without a body? It is far less than a mere heap of dirt. A heap of dirt can lie inert; place it aside and it remains where it is. But a body without a soul cannot lie still. People rush to take it and bury it, for if there is delay, there is fear of putrefaction and foul odour.

And what becomes of the body even when it remains with the spirit? It requires constant cleansing. It must be bathed, washed, kept pure. It is full of impurities. Food and everything that passes through it emerges as foul and malodorous matter. Fluid seeps from every part of the body—here as perspiration, there as other secretions. The pure air breathed into it becomes poisoned as it is exhaled. Many persons appear beautiful and fair from a distance, yet when one draws near and they speak, the stench of their breath becomes apparent. Is there fragrance emanating from anywhere? Perfumes must be applied to mask and conceal the body's stink. The ignorant man busies himself with grooming it. How much he labours and what care he takes! Yet what does it ultimately become? He acquires cast-off wares for himself. But the soul who truly owns this body—how does he forget to groom himself?

Is there no need for the reform of one's own self? Does it not require healing and medicine? Is it not diseased? Indeed it is sick, and this is precisely why it has entered this world.[^cite-10] What are these religions but remedies and prescriptions for the cure of the soul's maladies? There exist diseases and wounds within this self. The true treatment must be for this self alone; even were the body entirely neglected, there would be no harm. At this point the paths of the religious and the worldly diverge. Those who are concerned with using wisdom and understanding to perfect their soul and render it introspective become heedless toward the body. Yet they do so for the benefit and elevation of the soul.

But is the body a useless thing? No! Despite its defects, when used rightly, it becomes a great instrument. Through communion with the soul and obedience to wisdom, it attains sublime heights. A good human being is one of noble soul and refined body; a wicked human being lacks both. The distinction lies herein: the pious person purifies both soul and body, rendering both pure and radiant. Along with the soul, the body too attains heights. These souls and bodies together incline toward righteousness, toward obedience to the divine law, toward the fulfilment of Allah's commands, toward benevolence, generosity, and justice. Throughout history there have been such people who remain alive even centuries after their death—their virtues and excellences keep them living in memory. Nushirwān was a just king and is remembered to this day for his justice and fairness, although many ages have passed since his death. Similarly, Ḥātim al-Tāʾī lives on in memory for his generosity. Some have become immortal through their noble deeds, others through their knowledge and wisdom. What can we say of these extraordinary souls? They came as embodiments of virtue and departed, leaving behind a lamp of guidance for the peoples of the world. Such righteous persons also purify and perfume their bodies through their excellences and virtues. Therefore, if the body is to be perfected and rendered fragrant, it must be understood and used in the proper way.

There are no fixed limits to virtue and benevolence. Not every person possesses resources so vast that they can perform great deeds of charity and philanthropy. Can a person of limited means not be virtuous? Every person can be virtuous if they perform good according to their ability and within their means. For a believer, even to command what is right and forbid what is wrong (amr bi-l-maʿrūf wa-l-nahī ʿan al-munkar) is a great virtue, even as they themselves practice that good. The observance of the religious law and its prohibitions, the distinction between the lawful and forbidden, good character and conduct—all these every person can accomplish.[^cite-11] There is no need to possess a vast hoard of wealth and abundance of resources to be virtuous. Yet if someone is rich and does not spend properly but locks it away in safes and banks—if they do not serve the people despite having resources and capacity—such people are most unfortunate, living a purposeless life through missed opportunity. What will such people be remembered for? Besides this, they also wrong people, usurp their rights, and commit injustices; thus their names will be spoken only with curses.

Therefore, having found life, one must not forget the purpose of life. Where is the wisdom in devoting every moment to worldly ends and forgetting the good works and virtue of the never-ending life of the Hereafter—and thereby defiling both body and soul? How brief and unreliable is the life of this world; does this need demonstration? If someone heeds not the call of reason even after knowing all this, who can help one who strikes themselves with an axe upon their own hands and feet? And who would show sympathy for such a person?

Life is fleeting, and it is necessary to think and understand what the purpose of life is, and then strive to achieve that purpose. The saying of the Beloved Prophet should always be remembered: ⟪ar-quote:خَیرَ النَّاسَ مَن یَنفَعُ النَّاسَ⟫ "The best of people is one who benefits the people."[^cite-12] Make this blessed decree the goal of your life. Come, benefit someone as best you can. The respite is very brief! Do not let life end without having done anything. May Allah enable everyone to understand the true purpose of life and to fulfil it, so that when our soul departs the physical world and enters the Hereafter, it shall be successful and adorned. May we be among those whom the angels welcome, saying:
