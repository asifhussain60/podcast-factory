Mother of the Book:

It is narrated that one day, the Messenger of Allah (Sallallahu alaihi wa sallam) finished the Zuhr prayer and addressed the people and said, "Which of you was reciting um al-Kitab (Surah Al-Fatiha) behind me in this prayer?" A man said, "O Messenger of Allah, I was reciting it." The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) said, "Whoever of you recites um al-Kitab behind me, it is as if he wants to lead the prayer."

Apparently, um al-Kitab is Ali Murtada (a.s.). The Imams are called the Book in the language of interpretation, and in terms of spiritual reproduction, Isas is the mother of the Imams, i.e., um al-Kitab (a.s.) and Natiq (a.s.) is their father.

Interpretation of placing the hand on the chest:

When the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) stood up for prayer, he used to place his hands on his chest because the image of the chest is the Wasi (peace be upon him). The Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.a.) had collected his limits (the likeness of his hands) with his successor. When he entrusted the command of his invitation to his successor, he left his hands in prayer as well.
