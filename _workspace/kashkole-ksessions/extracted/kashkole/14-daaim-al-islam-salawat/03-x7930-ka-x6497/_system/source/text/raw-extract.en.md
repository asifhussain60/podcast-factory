For this reason, etiquette has been prescribed for both. One should not talk idle in the mosque just as one should not sit in the toilet for too long. We come to the mosque to make up our spiritual needs, and we also enter the toilet to make up our physical needs. For this reason, the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.a.) addressed Ali and said, "Whoever does not take care of the twelve etiquettes of the toilet, he has neither known you nor me!" He did not say this for prayers and fasting, but for the etiquette of the toilet.

There is a lot of wisdom hidden in the etiquette of the toilet and its prayers. A person should not worship until the filth comes out of his stomach. The Messenger of Allah (swt) said to Ali, "O Ali, I advise you that your death should come when your stomach is empty." The purpose of all the speeches of the Prophet (s) to Ali is to convey the message to the lowest believer by addressing the highest believer. Therefore, a believer should not enter the mosque of Allah or meet death (Allah) until his stomach is empty. They did not stand up for the worship of Allah until they had completely emptied their stomachs.

Apparently, it is commanded that when a believer comes out of the toilet, he should wash his hands because he has touched Sibeline. In the inner world, it means that the believer should purify himself from the knowledge of non-Da'wah (which is in the palace of impurity) before entering into Da'wah Sharif.

Principle and Dates

In a sense, the mosque refers to the Imam (a.s.) because he has a gathering of the Imam, just as the worshippers gather in a mosque.

These sciences are impure because they were written on the orders of the souls, not through endorsement. In the same way, the believer takes their wise words from his elders and extracts them in his own form.
