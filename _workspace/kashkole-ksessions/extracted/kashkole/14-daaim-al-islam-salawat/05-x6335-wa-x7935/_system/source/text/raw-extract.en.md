# Adhan and Iqamat
*Source: KASHKOLE, Binder 26 (Dua'im al-Islam: Prayer), Chapter 97. 4 topics. Language: ur.*<!-- section 1 (id=1288, raw_sort=1): اذآن -->The muezzin is the preacher and the muezzin is the call to prayer while standing at the place of prayer and the call to prayer is an invitation to the knowledge of the preacher. And the meaning of saying the adhan out loud is that it broadcasts the call of the preacher, the narrator or the Imam in every particular and public place. The place where the call to prayer is an example of the Prophet of the distant time and the fact that the minarets of the mosque to give the call to prayer is an example of the fact that the Prophet (peace be upon him) is the highest and noblest of the physical limits. When the muezzin gives the azaan, all the people should repeat the words of the azaan. The Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) said, "If anyone hears the call to prayer in the mosque and then leaves the mosque, he is a hypocrite!" (Whose tongue says something else and the heart says something else).

In one sense, the call to prayer is an invitation to the Islamic tradition and in another sense it is an acknowledgement that we are bound by a chain that begins with Ali and ends with Ali. The call to prayer contains 22 words that indicate the knowledge of the 22 words of the Qur'an (from Ali ibn Abi Talib to Imam Ali al-Tayyib).

The total number of words of the call to prayer is ten which indicate the ten limits of religion, of which five are spiritual limits (intellect, soul, grandfather, victory, thought) and five physical limits (Imam, Bab, Hujjat, Da'i, Ma'dun). And the repetition of these two times indicates that there is a combination between the two, the intellect and the self, and the rational and the foundation. A reading of these sentences one after another indicates that the rank of each upper one is similar to that of the lower one and that the rank of each upper one is superior to that of the lower one.

Allahu Akbar Allahu Akbar

These four Allahu Akbar are based on the four limits of religion (the first intellect, the second intellect, the rational and the basic) and indicate the elevation of their ranks.

Ashhad in La ilaha illa Allah (twice)

This indicates that the status of the first intellect is unique and that it is the true Lord of the spiritual and physical worlds, and that all limits are perplexed and perplexed in its knowledge. The first word is for the spiritual world and the second is for the physical world.

Ashhad in Muhammad al-Rasool Allah (twice)

This is an indication of the high status of the Prophet (peace be upon him) and that his association with the first intellect was such that no one had ever had it before. That is, he became so close to the first intellect that there was no connection between the first intellect and the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him), and he received material directly from the first intellect, and he became the servant and messenger of the first intellect.

The first martyrdom is for Muhammad Mustafa (Sallallahu alaihi wa sallam) and the second is for Maulana Muhammad bin Isma'il (a.s.).

Ashhad in Maulana Ali Waliullah (twice)

The first testimony is for Hazrat Ali ibn Abi Talib and the second is for Qaim-ul-Qiyamah. In this testimony, we refer to Maulana Ali as 'Maula' and in the previous testimony, we do not call Muhammad (PBUH) 'Maula'. It is in acknowledgment and knowledge that on the day of Ghadir, when the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.a.) said, "Man Mullah, Fahza Ali Mullah, then the Mawlaiyat was transferred from him to Ali!"

Hayya 'ala al-salat (twice)

This is for the believers and adults who adhere to the limits of the Qur'an and those who argue the truths of the religion. These people have good news because they are the boundaries of the companions of Yemen.

Hayya 'ala al-falah (twice)

Turning their faces to the left is good news for those who have just entered the era of Imam al-Zaman and to exhort them to try and join the companions of the Yemenis.

Hayya Ali Khair-ul-Amal (twice)

This refers to the Imam's service to the supplication of the Prophet (peace be upon him) and the acknowledgment that he is the one who frees the drowned souls in the sea of chaos, which is the best deed.

Muhammad and Ali Khair al-Bashar wa 'Atrathama Khair al-Attar (twice)

It is an indication that the carpet and the carpet should be denied. It is also pointed out that Muhammad ibn 'Abd Allah and 'Ali ibn Abi Talib and their descendants are better than all the Imams of previous times. This word is called two duffahs in the adhan. The second section indicates that the Imams of the Mahdi, Qayyim, and their successors will be superior to the Imams of the Muhammadan era. In a higher sense, it also refers to a time when the hijabs of Muhammad and Ali were equated for a brief period in rank in the Triple Crown.

Allahu Akbar Allahu Akbar

Saying two times indicates that the status of the first intellect is the highest among all spiritual and physical limits.

La ilaha illa Allah (twice)

It is pointed out that the position of the first intellect is that of 'unity' and 'realm of unity' in comparison to all intellects. No other intellect has the ability to reach the status of oneness. All intellects are opposite to 'Allah', but none of them have the ability to become 'Allah' (the first intellect)

This indicates that the da'i stayed with both the outward and the inward invitations, because he was obliged to stay on both of them.

When the call to prayer is given, the revelations are sent down from the world of life. The isharaq of the adhaan is weakened by the isharaq of iqaama. The Ishraaqat of the call to prayer is called the Islamic form, but with the iqama is the form of faith. The call to prayer is the propagation of prophethood and prophethood, while the iqamah is the cause of the establishment of a qa'im and the place of infallibility.

It is a command to give the call to prayer with a good tone because it brings together the sounds that used to be apparent in this world and now they gather for prayer in a spiritual capacity as well. On the other hand, it is said to be fast, and without tone, because the forms that are illuminated by it are the concepts of the best and the people of the inner. They are not interested in the outward voice, they only come for prayer at the invitation of the muezzin.

In the Iqama, when the muezzin says Hayya 'ala al-salawa, it is the illumination of the Islamic form, and that is why he says "La haul wa wa quwat illa bi al-Allaah al-'ala al-'azeem." In the Iqama, when the muezzin says "Hayya 'ala al-falah", then there is the Ishraq of the Faithful Forms, which is the best Ishraq and that is why it is called Mashallah.

The call to prayer is to take Tawassul from the Imam (a.s.) and the Iqamah is to take Tawassul from his deity.
