# Qualities of Faith

## Sincerity

⟪ar:صاحب رسائل⟫ The author of the *Epistles* observes that sincerity constitutes one of the cardinal conditions of faith and one of the defining marks of the believer. ⟪ar:' اخلاص'⟫ **Sincerity (ikhlāṣ) pertains fundamentally to the quality of action.** When a believer performs a deed solely for the sake of Allah's pleasure, with no expectation of worldly reward or divine recompense in return, that action is deemed sincere. Sincerity, in essence, means acting without regard to outcome or temporal gain. The term ikhlāṣ derives from *khālis*, meaning "that which is undiluted" or "purified"—every action executed with transparent intention, wholly free from the admixture of personal or material interest, exemplifies true sincerity.[^cite-1]

### Sincerity of Parents

A luminous example of sincerity appears in the mother who nurses her child yet seeks no recompense from that child for her milk. Her actions flow purely from love, unmarred by expectation. Parents, likewise, who nurture their children well perform an act of profound sincerity, for they understand that Allah has entrusted this sacred duty to their nature—a responsibility they fulfill in all circumstances without awaiting compensation from their young. The term "parents" (⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ) و علی⟫ *al-wālidayn*) encompasses both father and mother. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ) و علی⟫ the Commander of the Faithful, ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, never sought recompense from the Community (*Umma*) for their service, nor did they demand compensation from Allah, nor did they ever lodge complaint. They regarded their service as duty itself and discharged it with consummate sincerity. All the divinely protected Imāms (*ḥudūd*) served their Lords with this same purity of intention.[^cite-2] ⟪ar:حضرت سلمان فارسی⟫ Salmān al-Fārsī, the honored Persian companion, would sweep the house of ⟪ar:مولاتنا فاطمۃ الزھرا⟫ our mistress Fāṭima al-Zahrāʾ with his own cloak.

A servant who serves from fear, coercion, or desire for ransom is called ⟪ar:عبد السوء⟫ a wicked servant. Similarly, whosoever performs duties for Allah from longing for Paradise or dread of Hell—Allah will not accept such action. For such a person possesses neither sincerity in prayer nor integrity in deed. Thus the Master of Creation, ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ the Commander of the Faithful, declares in his supplications: ⟪ar-quote:اِلٰہِی مَا عَبَدتُّکَ شَوقاً بِجَنَّتِکَ وَلاَ خَوفً مِن نَّارِکَ بَل وَجَدتُّکَ مُستَحَقًّ لِلعِباَدَۃ فَعَبَدتُّک⟫ "O Allah, I worship Thee not from passion for Thy Paradise nor from fear of Thy Fire; rather, I have found Thee worthy of worship, and so I worship Thee for that alone."[^cite-3]

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*

## How Sincerity is Generated

Sincerity cannot manifest in any action or prayer unless the believer surrenders his ⟪ar:حول⟫ *ḥawl* (stratagem) and his ⟪ar:قوت⟫ *quwwa* (strength). **⟪ar:حول⟫ *Ḥawl* denotes cunning and artifice—the capacity of the psyche—while ⟪ar:قوت⟫ *quwwa* means the force and vigor of the body.** When one encounters difficulty or seeks to accomplish something, there exist but two paths: either to achieve one's aim through stratagem and trickery, or through bodily force and compulsion. Sincerity emerges when the believer becomes firmly convinced that he possesses no power over any thing. He cannot dispel his suffering through cunning, deception, or psychological maneuvering; nor can he gain benefit through physical might alone. He comes to believe that ⟪ar:حول⟫ all stratagems and ⟪ar:قوت⟫ all strength belong to Allah alone, and he declares with complete sincerity: ⟪ar-quote:لاَ حَولَ وَلاَ قُوَّتَ اِلاَّ بِا ﷲِ العَلِیِّ العَظِیم⟫ "There is no stratagem and no strength save in Allah, the Most High, the Mighty."[^cite-4] Neither in the soul ⟪ar:حول⟫ nor in the body ⟪ar:قوت⟫ does anything reside; all proceeds according to Allah's will. Only then, when one prays or acts, does sincerity dwell within the heart.

| **Pertaining to** | **The Psyche** |
|---|---|
| **Nature** | Cunning and Stratagem |
| **Corresponding to** | ⟪ar:حول⟫ *Ḥawl* |

| **Pertaining to** | **The Body** |
|---|---|
| **Nature** | Strength and Force |
| **Corresponding to** | ⟪ar:قوت⟫ *Quwwa* |

Consider those who board a ship upon the sea. As they embark, they supplicate to Allah for safety and security, yet their prayer lacks sincerity. Though they petition Allah and profess reliance on Him, their trust remains compounded: they lean upon the vessel's soundness, upon prediction of the weather, upon the sailor's skill, and upon the captain ⟪ar:رَبَّان⟫ *rabbān*. This admixed confidence brings their hearts comfort and satisfaction. But when the vessel reaches the deep waters and waves rise, growing louder and more violent until the boat rocks terribly, they witness the vessel and the captain ⟪ar:رَبَّان⟫ upon whose prowess they relied now seized by terror and dread, their faces marked plainly with signs of despair. At that hour, they cry out to Allah as they did at embarkation—yet now their prayer is ⟪ar:مُخلِصِینَ لَہُ الدِّینَ⟫ sincere, for now they know with certainty that none can save them save Allah alone ⟪ar:حول و قوت⟫. Before, their prayer had relied ⟪ar:رَبَّان⟫ upon the captain's might and ⟪ar:حول و قوت⟫ stratagem; now, after encountering the tempest, their prayer rests solely upon Allah—a prayer of pure sincerity, unstained by trust in any other.

Similarly does the sincere person among those learned in the sciences of causality trust in Allah despite their knowledge, and pray to Him. Though possessed of full understanding of impending evil and knowledge of all means to overcome it, yet they place their trust in Allah. They believe that only Allah can avert this evil if He wills, and that they can accomplish nothing by knowledge alone unless Allah decrees it. Thus they hold firm to the conviction that ⟪ar:لاَ حَولَ وَلاَ قُوَّتَ اِلاَّ بِا ﷲِ العَلِیِّ العَظِیم⟫.[^cite-5]

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*

### Distinction Between the Wise and the Knower

⟪ar:عاقل⟫ *ʿĀqil* (the wise person) is one who employs intellect to solve a problem and arrives at a solution, yet does not turn toward Allah. ⟪ar:عارف⟫ *ʿĀrif* (the knower, or gnostic)—whose station stands higher—is one who, even upon discovering the solution, turns toward Allah and remains sincere to Him. 

The distinction clarifies through example. Imagine two learned persons aboard a vessel—one wise, the other a knower—both versed in the science of navigation. When a storm strikes the sea, ⟪ar:عاقل⟫ the wise person employs astrology to determine whether the boat will sink or survive, who will perish and who be saved. ⟪ar:عارف⟫ Yet even after the knower has ascertained everything through that knowledge, he trusts in Allah and believes that what Allah wills shall come to pass. In other words, ⟪ar:عاقل⟫ the wise person trusts in science, while ⟪ar:عارف⟫ the knower trusts in the Creator. Because of this sincerity, the supplication of the wise is not accepted, but the prayer of the knower is ever accepted.

Both the wise and the knower engage in causality. The wise person first devises a plan relying upon ⟪ar:حول و قوت⟫ stratagem and force; when that fails, only then does he turn his trust to Allah. ⟪ar:عارف⟫ The knower, conversely, first places his trust in Allah and surrenders his ⟪ar:حول و قوت⟫ stratagem and force to Allah, then begins his work seeking Allah's aid. For this reason, the wise person panics in tribulation, while the knower remains ever calm. The knower's support reveals itself in the wise person's distress.[^cite-6]

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*

## Piety and Trust in Allah

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq declared: "Faith is a blessing of such magnitude that were one to comprehend its worth, one would never begrudge the disbeliever his wealth or ease." Thus did the Commander of the Faithful observe: ⟪ar-quote:رَضِینَا قِسمَتُ الجَبَّارِ فِینَا لَنَا عِلمٌ وَلِی اَعداَءِ ماَلُ⟫ "We are content with Allah's decree over us—to us He has given knowledge, to our enemies wealth."[^cite-7] Those who trust in Allah shall enjoy a tranquil life in this world and joy in the Hereafter. Trust in Allah (*tawakkul*) is a haven for the estranged, a companion in solitude, light in darkness, strength for the weak, and medicine for the afflicted. There lived before you those who trusted Allah, whom men slew and burned, whose bodies were severed and boiled alive—yet they demanded no retribution, but relied solely upon the reward from their Lord. Emulate the ranks of such people; endure the calamities of your time, that you too may attain the highest degrees.

No one can taste the sweetness of faith unless he has grown indifferent to all that this world offers of pleasure. So long as worldly desire dominates the heart, the sweetness of faith remains forbidden to him. Whoever becomes pious toward this world—Allah plants wisdom in his heart and makes it flow from his tongue, revealing to him the evils of the world such that he turns away from all its allurements and returns to the gardens of Allah in peace.[^cite-8]

## Patience

Allah declares in the Qur'ān regarding patience:

> ⟪quran 11:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِلَّا ٱلَّذِينَ صَبَرُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ لَهُم مَّغْفِرَةٌۭ وَأَجْرٌۭ كَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Except those who show patience and do righteous good deeds, those: theirs will be forgiveness and a great reward.*

> ⟪quran 13:24⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَلَمٌ عَلَيْكُم بِمَا صَبَرْتُمْ ۚ فَنِعْمَ عُقْبَى ٱلدَّارِ ‎⟫
> *"Peace be upon you for that you persevered in patience! How excellent is the final home!"*

> ⟪quran 23:111⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنِّى جَزَيْتُهُمُ ٱلْيَوْمَ بِمَا صَبَرُوٓا۟ أَنَّهُمْ هُمُ ٱلْفَآئِزُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily! I have rewarded them this Day for their patience—they are the triumphant.*

> ⟪quran 3:200⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱصْبِرُوا۟ وَصَابِرُوا۟ وَرَابِطُوا۟ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Endure and be more patient, and guard your territory, and fear Allah, that you may prosper.*

⟪ar:صاحب رسائل⟫ The author of the *Epistles* teaches: "Patience constitutes one of the conditions of faith, and one of the marks of the believer—indeed, it is ⟪ar:راس الأیمان⟫ the very head of faith." Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 16:127⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱصْبِرْ وَمَا صَبْرُكَ إِلَّا بِٱللَّهِ ۚ وَلَا تَحْزَنْ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا تَكُ فِى ضَيْقٍۢ مِّمَّا يَمْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And endure patiently—thy patience is only from Allah. Grieve not over them, and be not distressed by their scheming.*

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*

### Adversity and Patience

Patience is the virtue of steadfastness in grave hardship, without agitation, for the sake of noble ends. It denotes a state in which the soul maintains composure and equanimity. Whatever brings suffering to the human being is termed affliction (*muṣība*), and it is incumbent to bear every affliction with patience. The authorities distinguish two forms of trial. The first is that which results from one's own misdeeds: the gambler becomes impoverished, the drunkard loses health, the adulterer and miscreant becomes destitute. This kind of suffering befalls all humanity. The second is that wherein one is tested and one's spiritual ranks are elevated—a calamity reserved for the special servants of Allah.[^cite-9]

Patience unveils the hidden light and purity within a person; panic and anxiety, by contrast, expose the darkness concealed within. Many claim patience, yet only the sincere believer persists steadfastly upon this path. At tribulation's onset, the difference between the patient and the anxious becomes manifest. The patient one grows humble at the trial's beginning and implores forgiveness for his sins. The troubled one complains and grows angry; when relief does not come swiftly, he tires and turns toward resignation. Every patience begins bitter, yet its end is exceedingly sweet. For those first troubled, then forced to endure, Allah says:

> ⟪quran 2:155⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَنَبْلُوَنَّكُم بِشَىْءٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْخَوْفِ وَٱلْجُوعِ وَنَقْصٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْأَمْوَلِ وَٱلْأَنفُسِ وَٱلثَّمَرَتِ ۗ وَبَشِّرِ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *And We shall surely test you with something of fear and hunger, loss of wealth and lives and fruits—but give glad tidings to the patient.*

But for the truly patient, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 8:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ وَلَا تَنَزَعُوا۟ فَتَفْشَلُوا۟ وَتَذْهَبَ رِيحُكُمْ ۖ وَٱصْبِرُوٓا۟ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ مَعَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *Obey Allah and His Messenger, and dispute not lest you lose heart and your strength depart. Be patient—surely Allah is with the patient.*[^cite-10]

*(Kitāb al-Miṣbāḥ al-Sharīʿa) Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq*

### When is Patience Incumbent?

Patience for the common person commences when one attempts to escape a trial and finds escape impossible. Were a man thirsty and water present, and he chose not to drink, he would not be deemed patient—for he possesses the means to remedy his difficulty. But when no water is obtainable and escape is impossible, then the stations of patience truly begin. Thus did Imām al-Ḥusayn and his companions in Karbalāʾ endure when they were denied water. The scholars have identified two modes of patience:

|---|
| **Patience in the absence of what one desires** |
| **Patience in the presence of what one abhors** |

### The Patient Ones and Their Ranks

Allah fashioned mankind ⟪ar:اشرف المخلوقات⟫ as the most noble of His creations, granting him dominion over the universe through intellect. Yet he could not master the smallest part of his body—a lump of flesh called the heart! Rather, it mastered him, and throughout his life he remained enslaved to the needless desires and commands of that heart. Those who conquer their hearts, who slay their base passions and desires for Allah's sake, accomplish truly the most difficult and arduous of tasks. There exist three types of patience and love of Allah: the first two are metaphorical, while the third is real.

#### Type One: Deficient Patience

These are those who believe in Allah, yet when affliction strikes them, they cry out in complaint—screaming and wailing without bearing their suffering with fortitude. This is a flawed form of patience and love.

#### Type Two: Lower Patience

These are those who acquiesce to Allah's will and endure their calamity in silence, without complaint. This is the lowest degree of love and patience.

⟪ar:ذوالنون مصری⟫ Dhū al-Nūn al-Miṣrī once passed through the marketplace of Kūfa and encountered a man—blind in both eyes, paralyzed in both hands and feet. Yet this man called upon his Lord with profound sincerity and devotion, saying: "O my Lord! How can I give thanks in a manner worthy of Thee? O my God! Thy grace encompasses all Thy creatures equally. What can be said of Thy grace? Thou hast bestowed it upon the ungrateful as Thou dost upon the grateful!" Dhū al-Nūn trembled upon hearing this supplication. He approached, greeted the man, sat beside him, and asked: "O brother, I must inquire of thee: tell me, for what blessing dost thou give thanks to thy Lord despite thy affliction and misery?" The elder replied: "Glory be to Allah! How dare I fail to be grateful for His blessings? His blessings are an unceasing rain falling constantly upon me. See how I am—I cannot see, I cannot move—yet despite this, He sustains me continually. He has placed me in a state wherein His will and contentment reign. O questioner! Is this not worthy of His gratitude?" Dhū al-Nūn was profoundly moved and said: "O elder, is there any need I might fulfill for thee?" The man replied: "Yes, a need has lately arisen. I have a son who provides my sustenance, but three days ago he disappeared. Can you help me find him?" ⟪ar:ذوالنون⟫ They rose and set out in search. What they discovered was that the son had been torn apart by wild beasts. They returned to the man, pondering how to convey this tragedy. Dhū al-Nūn spoke: "O elder, dost thou recall the story of Prophet Ayyūb?" The man replied: "What of Ḥaḍrat Ayyūb? He was the most patient and grateful servant of Allah!" Dhū al-Nūn then said: "O righteous man, may Allah grant thee patience in thy son's grief. He has passed away, torn by wild animals." The man raised his sightless eyes to heaven and declared: ⟪ar:اَلحَمدُ ﷲِ الَّذِی لَم یَجعَل فِی قَلبِی حَسرَۃً مِّنَ الدُّنیَا⟫ "Praise be to Allah, who has not planted in my heart any longing for this world." As he spoke, a sigh issued from his depths, and at that moment he fell to earth and departed. ⟪ar:اِناَّ لِلّٰہِ وَ اِناَّ اِلَیہِ راَجِعُون⟫ "To Allah we belong, and to Him we shall return."

The scholars observe that despite this pious and grateful elder's extraordinary fortitude and patience, even this patience is ordinary and inferior to patience of the highest order.[^cite-11]

#### Type Three: Sublime Patience

The third type comprises those who find joy in the hardships presented in the path of Allah. Their longing is such that their hearts yearn perpetually for increased suffering. This is the highest and purest form of love and patience—yet it is reserved for Muḥammad and the family of Muḥammad alone. History testifies that as affliction multiplied upon these people, the radiance of their faces grew ever brighter.

⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ Consider the life of Ibrāhīm the Friend of Allah: he did not hesitate to offer his beloved son ⟪ar:اسماعیل⟫ in sacrifice to Allah—and not merely upon the basis of a dream, but upon divine command. Here the blind elder could not bear the news of his son's death; ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم⟫ yet Ibrāhīm consented to slaughter his own son with his own hand. And behold the patience of his noble son ⟪ar:اسماعیل علیہ السلام⟫ Ismāʿīl, who said to his father: "Do what thou hast been commanded! ⟪ar:انشأ اللہ⟫ God willing, thou shalt find me among the patient!"

⟪ar:منجلیخ⟫ When ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ Ibrāhīm was cast into the fire, ⟪ar:جبرئیل علیہ السلام⟫ Jibrīl approached him saying: "O Ibrāhīm, pray to Allah to deliver thee from this trial!" He replied: "What need have I to pray? If this is the will of my Lord, I shall accept it gladly and be patient in His pleasure!"

The deepest source of their patience is this: Allah has granted them power to remove their difficulties by a mere command of their tongues whenever they wish. Yet these souls, possessing such immense power and authority, sacrifice their own relief for Allah's pleasure and endure the severest trials with joy. To fulfill the vision of his father, one offers his neck for sacrifice; joyfully, they offer themselves and their families and children as martyrs in Karbalāʾ's field.[^cite-12]

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*

*(Muḥarram) Ḥaḍrat Nasrullāh ibn Hibatullāh*

### The Reason for Suffering Upon the Prophets

How strange it appears that the one whom Allah draws near to Himself, to whom He entrusts the weighty responsibilities of Prophethood, should be placed in trial and affliction! Does it befit the Beloved to bring distress upon His beloved? Yet in the Divine Wisdom, this principle was established for Allah's chosen ones: the condition of love is suffering. The nearer one draws to Allah, the severer one's trial; the farther removed from Him, the lighter the examination. And all know that pure essence does not shine until placed upon the anvil; stone does not reveal its beauty until hewn; the finer the grinding, the brighter the glow; gold is known only after passing through the crucible's fire. Nature has established progress as contingent upon trial and then elevation.

| **Who has tasted the fruits of unwavering loyalty?** | **Who has obtained the crown?** |
|---|---|
| **Who has drunk the cup without blood?** | **Who bears the standard of victory?** |
| **Who possesses the wages of sleepless devotion?** | **Who has earned knowledge through patient labor?** |
| **Those whose ranks are highest** | **They first endured trial!** |

*(Muḥarram) Ḥaḍrat Nasrullāh ibn Hibatullāh*

### The Nature of Affliction

Every calamity that befalls the believer aims solely at purification and the elevation of his ranks, whereas misfortune visited upon the disbeliever constitutes punishment for his evil deeds. The Prophet ﷺ pointed to this distinction, saying: ⟪ar-quote:اَلمُؤمِنُ مُمتَحِن وَ الکاَفِرُ مُبتَلا⟫ "The believer is tested; the disbeliever is punished."[^cite-13]

**Statement:** When ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ the Risen One upon whom be remembrance and peace shall appear, they will subject the believers to trial of extraordinary severity—it is reported that they will draw them through examination as the last drop is wrung from a cloth. The purpose of these trials shall be to elevate their spiritual degrees.

### Pain Upon the Soul

The author of the *Magazine* speaks thus concerning the animal soul (⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ *al-nafs al-ḥassāsa*, the sensing soul): Allah has made the soul more vulnerable to pain than any other faculty, so that it may be guarded from the corruption and ruin of the body. Pain is continually inflicted upon the soul to preserve bodily existence. For example, when one places the hand in fire, the soul suffers—but this suffering serves the body's preservation, for the body might otherwise burn and perish. Since the soul senses fire's pain, it ever protects the body from burning. Were the soul not to feel pain, it would not guard the body's welfare. Since the soul cannot endure in this world without the body, Allah's wisdom ordains that the soul feel pain so that the body may be protected.[^cite-14]

### The Concept of Trust in Patience

Some believers imagine that Allah wrongs them by inflicting calamity. Others suppose that Allah is unaware of their affliction. Still others believe Allah knows yet do not think He cares for them, or that they matter little to Him. Some imagine Allah to be harsh-hearted. All such notions squander the reward of patience.

The Prophets and righteous believers endured hardships, yet their patience stemmed purely from love of Allah. They believed that Allah's purposes manifested in their afflictions and their Community's trials, and that these tribulations served their betterment—though this truth often remained hidden from the wise. They confessed that their Allah alone is the Living, the Self-Subsisting, the Omnipotent, the All-Wise. They believed that Allah has governed this world and all within it with supreme wisdom, and that every command from Allah—whether easy or arduous—contains hidden wisdom and benefit known only to Allah.

### Benefits of Patience

The supreme benefit of patience is that it bestows freedom. It removes the yoke of desires from one's neck and creates in him wealth and independence of spirit. The impatient soul remains bound in chains of desire, though he hears not their clanking. Only those who love freedom can attain true patience.[^cite-15]

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*

## Trust in Allah

Human existence oscillates between two states: one of intensity and strain, the other of ease and rest. A person shall be either anxious or calm, joyful or sorrowful, healthy or infirm, prosperous or impoverished.

### The Believer's Trust

The believer is one who does not turn away from obedience to Allah in either circumstance. When happy, healthy, and successful, he places his trust in Allah and ascribes all blessings to Him, seeking His aid. He attributes all ⟪ar:حول و قوت⟫ stratagem and strength to Allah. Thus did ⟪ar:نبی سلیمان علیہ السلام⟫ Prophet Sulaymān, when granted the kingdom and taught the languages of all beasts and birds, declare:

> ⟪quran 27:40⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱلَّذِى عِندَهُۥ عِلْمٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنَا۠ آتِيكَ بِهِۦ قَبْلَ أَن يَرْتَدَّ إِلَيْكَ طَرْفُكَ ۚ فَلَمَّا رَآهُ مُسْتَقِرًّا عِندَهُۥ قَالَ هَذَا مِن فَضْلِ رَبِّى لِيَبْلُوَنِىٓ ءَأَشْكُرُ أَمْ أَكْفُرُ ۖ وَمَن شَكَرَ فَإِنَّمَا يَشْكُرُ لِنَفْسِهِۦ ۖ وَمَن كَفَرَ فَإِنَّ رَبِّى غَنِىٌّۭ كَرِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *One with whom was knowledge of the Scripture said: "I will bring it to thee within the twinkling of an eye!" And when he saw it placed before him, he said: "This is of the Grace of my Lord, to test me whether I am grateful or ungrateful! Whosoever is grateful, his gratitude is for his own self; and whosoever is ungrateful—truly my Lord is Rich, Bountiful."*[^cite-16]

When the believer encounters distress, he seeks Allah's forgiveness, bears with patience, and believes that his affliction stems from his own sins, for his Allah alone is All-Good. Not only does he endure this hardship; he gives thanks to his Lord in the midst of it, knowing that through these trials his sins are forgiven. He hopes for his Lord's mercy and accepts His commands with patience and gratitude, seeking pardon for his transgressions.

> ⟪quran 2:156⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ إِذَآ أَصَبَتْهُم مُّصِيبَةٌۭ قَالُوٓا۟ إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّآ إِلَيْهِ رَجِعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Those who, when afflicted with calamity, say: "Truly! To Allah we belong and truly, to Him we shall return."*[^cite-17]

Some of Allah's special servants became Prophets not merely by enduring such hardships but by praising and thanking their Lord for such a state.

### The Trust of the Disbeliever

By contrast, the disbeliever ascribes every good condition to himself alone, saying: "Whatever blessing I have received, I received by virtue of my own ability, effort, knowledge, and cunning." He refers all of Allah's grace to the means themselves and relies upon those means. Through such reasoning, he turns away from his Lord. Thus Qārūn declared: "I have obtained all this treasure (whose keys alone required seventy camels to bear) through my knowledge alone." When calamity befalls the disbeliever, he grows disgusted with Allah's mercy, blames Allah for his misfortune, and says: "These afflictions have come upon me from Allah."[^cite-18]

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*

## If Patience Were a Man, He Would Be a Righteous Man

The Prophet ﷺ said: ⟪ar-quote:اِذاَ کاَنَ الصَّبَرُ رَجُلً لِکاَنَ رَجُلً صاَلِحاً⟫ "Were patience created as a human being, he would be created as a righteous man." ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ In elucidating this noble hadith, Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq explains: "Patience ⟪ar:امام مستودع⟫ is an example of the Imām as Trustee (*mustaudiʿ*), and ⟪ar:رجل صالحا⟫ the righteous man exemplifies the Imām as Established (*mustaqarr*). That is, when patience develops and takes form, it becomes ⟪ar:امام مستودع⟫ the Imām as Trustee and ⟪ar:امام مستقر⟫ is manifested as the Imām Established. ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ) و علی⟫ In the Prophet's ﷺ time, they were the congregation, and ⟪ar:مولانا سلمان فارسی⟫ our master Salmān al-Fārsī was he to whom the congregation of all ⟪ar:صابرین⟫ the patient ones of former times had been gathered. Likewise, ⟪ar:امام حسن علیہ السلام، مستودع⟫ Imām al-Ḥasan as Trustee, there are those who progressed ⟪ar:مستقر⟫ and joined the chain. That is, whoever is to become ⟪ar:مستودع⟫ the Trustee shall become ⟪ar:مستقر⟫ the Established, as did ⟪ar:امام حسن علیہ السلام⟫ Imām al-Ḥasan. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq states: "Thou shalt advance ⟪ar:امام حسن علیہ السلام⟫ to the sphere of Imām al-Ḥasan and obtain the supreme station." That is, in ⟪ar:صبر (سلمان فارسی)⟫ the patience of Salmān al-Fārsī and ⟪ar:رجل صالح (امام حسن علیہ السلام)⟫ the righteousness of Imām al-Ḥasan, it shall be made and expressed.[^cite-19]

| **Patience** | **Righteous Man** |
|---|---|
| **Imām as Trustee** | **Imām Established** |
| **Salmān al-Fārsī** | **Imām al-Ḥasan** |

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*

## Asceticism and Renunciation

Piety (*zuhd*) is a quality wherein the believer renounces what is forbidden as well as what is lawful—all for the sake of Allah's pleasure and approval. Even in lawful things, the ascetic chooses the most humble. The believer does not shrink from Allah's blessings; rather, he partakes of them only according to his need. Mawlānā ʿAlī described the ascetic thus: one who, if a worldly blessing comes to him, finds no joy; if it departs from him, feels no sorrow. To live in the world while renouncing its allures—this is the nature of true asceticism.

Once Mawlānā ʿAlī was offered a sweet delicacy of ice and fruit. He tasted it with but two fingers and set it aside. When questioned, "Mawlānā! This falooda is made from lawful milk—why do you not eat?" he replied: "I am devoted to asceticism. I fear lest my soul become accustomed to the pleasure of it."

Asceticism in this world and inclination toward the Hereafter constitute one of the marks of faith. **Human nature is such that one resists abandoning present benefit for a distant promise (the Hereafter). Yet when one becomes convinced that the profit of the distant reward surpasses present gain immeasurably, then one abandons the present for the sake of that distant debt and embraces asceticism.** When the souls of the ascetics part from their bodies, they shall possess all the pleasures for which they had renounced the world. At that hour, the Hereafter for which they strived shall be their world. The distant shall become immediate, and the debt shall become cash! Thus did Allah say to the Prophet ﷺ:

> ⟪quran 93:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَلْآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْأُولَىٰ ‎⟫
> *And indeed the Hereafter is better for thee than the present.*[^cite-20]

Each thing is bound to its end, and what comes at the end surpasses what comes first. Effort is bound to success; childhood to youth; the world to the Hereafter. The purpose of childhood is to attain adulthood; the purpose of effort is to reach success. A childhood well-spent yields a virtuous youth; a well-lived life yields a dignified old age. Each final state depends upon the state preceding it. Similarly are the world and Hereafter intertwined—the Hereafter being far superior to the world. Thus ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the Messenger of Allah ﷺ declared: ⟪ar-quote:مَن زُہُدَ فِی الدُّنِیاَ اِھاَنَۃ عَلَیہِ الدُّنِیَا⟫ "Whosoever becomes ascetic toward the world—the world shall be humiliated for him."[^cite-21]

Scholars, the learned, and the Prophets abandoned the world's immediate desires to acquire the desires of the Hereafter, which brought them far greater pleasure. They cultivated longing for the Hereafter and for things difficult and delayed in coming. Yet they did so only when the reality of the Hereafter became clear to them. While dwelling in this world, they began to perceive the blessings of the Hereafter with ⟪ar:عیون القلب⟫ the eyes of the heart and ⟪ar:نور العقل⟫ the light of intellect, just as a person perceives the things of this world through ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫ the five senses. For these great souls, this world became the Hereafter because they knew that the Hereafter's destination is reached by passing through this world. Thus they lived in this world as though dwelling in the Hereafter.

The path to true knowledge of the Hereafter is attained when the believer contemplates the affairs of this world through the lens of utility, for this world has been fashioned after the pattern of the Hereafter. In other words, the world serves as exemplar and the Hereafter as the reality. When one observes the world's affairs and relates them to the Hereafter's, one becomes convinced of the Hereafter's blessings. ⟪ar:اہل زہد⟫ The people of asceticism follow all the ordinances of the Sharīʿa without requesting any reward, and they alone attain the Hereafter. Those who seek recompense for their righteous deeds for their own pleasure and benefit, disregarding the Hereafter, belong to the people of heedlessness.[^cite-22]

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*

## Purification of the Soul and Knowledge

Allah has bestowed upon mankind two categories of blessings. The first comprises ⟪ar:خارج الجسد⟫ what lies external to the body—possession of wife, children, and the world's wealth. The second ⟪ar:داخل الجسد⟫ dwells within the body and is of two kinds: one pertains to the body itself, the other to the soul. The blessing within the body includes good health and what flows from it—a comely face, well-formed body, strength, vigor, and the like. The blessing residing in the soul also has two aspects. The first is ⟪ar:حُسنِ خُلق⟫ good character, and the second is ⟪ar:زکاوت النفس⟫ purification of the soul. ⟪ar:زکاوت النفس⟫ This refers to the capacity to embrace what is good and the capacity to renounce what is evil. All Allah's blessings are rooted in ⟪ar:زکاوت النفس⟫ the purification of the soul—the foundation upon which all other blessings rest.

⟪ar:زکاوۃ النفس⟫ Purification of the soul is a blessing useful in acquiring ⟪ar:معارف⟫ the knowledge (*maʿārif*)—that which confers genuine understanding. There exist four categories of knowledge:

| **Type** | **Description** |
|---|---|
| **Type 1** | Those given faith but not given knowledge (*maʿārif*) |
| **Type 2** | Those given apparent knowledge but not given faith |
| **Type 3** | Those given neither knowledge nor faith |
| **Type 4** | Those given both knowledge and faith |

Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 30:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ وَٱلْإِيمَنَ لَقَدْ لَبِثْتُمْ فِى كِتَبِ ٱللَّهِ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْبَعْثِ ۖ فَهَذَا يَوْمُ ٱلْبَعْثِ وَلَكِنَّكُمْ كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those who have been given knowledge and faith will say: "Verily! You have stayed according to the Decree of Allah, until the Day of Resurrection, so this is the Day of Resurrection—but you knew not."*[^cite-23]

*Baʿth* (awakening) means to be roused and made aware. That is, on the Day of Resurrection you shall be awakened through knowledge and faith. In this verse, Allah has identified ⟪ar:معارف⟫ those who possess the highest station among people. The noblest knowledge is that of the Resurrection and the rising—the knowledge which the learned and the faithful have acquired.[^cite-24]

### Those Who Believe But Do Not Know

These are people who believe in the Books of the Prophets and their contents, yet do not comprehend their details. When told of the Prophet's origin and mission, of the angels and their stations, of the Resurrection and the rising, of the Reckoning and the Balance, of the Bridge of the Straight Path, and of the rewards of the righteous and punishments of the wicked, they believe and affirm all. Yet they do not acquire knowledge of these matters; they do not contemplate or discuss them, for their souls lack fervor. They ask not "How? What? Why? Where?"—for they lack the quest to discover truth.[^cite-25]

### Those Given Knowledge, Not Faith

These are people who have received a share of worldly knowledge but have not been granted faith. These examine the apparent books and discourse upon their details, incorporating what is written into their understanding. They master various branches of worldly knowledge—medicine, engineering, mathematics, geometry, logistics, astronomy, physics, chemistry, and others. They become so enamored with these sciences that they become them entirely, forgetting what is inscribed in the Qur'ān and Ḥadīth. They abandon the Prophets ⟪ar:تنزیلات⟫ and their revelations, nor do they explore the mysteries of the Sharīʿa's teachings. The result is that the hidden dimensions conceal from them the truths of the Prophets' knowledge. They become blind to the knowledge of causality in the visible world, begin to doubt religion's verities and knowledge of the unseen, and grow perplexed about the science of esoteric meaning (*taʾwīl*). For such people Allah says:

> ⟪quran 40:83⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَلَمَّا جَآءَتْهُمْ رُسُلُهُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ فَرِحُوا۟ بِمَا عِندَهُم مِّنَ ٱلْعِلْمِ وَحَاقَ بِهِم مَّا كَانُوا۟ بِهِۦ يَسْتَهْزِءُونَ ‎⟫
> *Then when their Messengers came to them with clear proofs, they were glad with what knowledge (of worldly things) they had, and that (punishment) which they used to mock surrounded them.*[^cite-26]

Thus the purpose of worldly knowledge becomes evident.

### Those Having Neither Knowledge Nor Faith

These are people who have been given neither knowledge nor faith. They spend their entire lives in pursuit of desire alone, surrounded by the world's blessings and the sweetness of pleasure. They have abandoned the assemblies of virtue and learning, deprived of the Sharīʿa's ordinances and all religious matters, whose chief aim is the soul's liberation and the attainment of the Hereafter. Allah has made the world a mere passage for them. Allah has forsaken them; they accumulate the world's wealth until it consumes them, obtaining the world as a wild beast obtains food. Hell has been prepared for them.

### Those Given Both Knowledge and Faith

These are the ⟪ar:صاحبان معرفت⟫ ones possessing both knowledge and faith—the chosen servants of Allah. These are those who fully benefit from the blessings Allah has given and continue to ascend to the highest stations of faith in their pursuit of knowledge. They shine forth as the moon amidst the prophets and messengers.[^cite-27]

*(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imām Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh*
