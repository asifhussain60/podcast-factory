## Sincerity

⟪ar:صاحب رسائل⟫He said, "One of the conditions of faith is a condition, and one of the characteristics of a believer is a trait ⟪ar:' اخلاص'⟫." Sincerity (ikhlāṣ, lit. "purification" or "freedom from admixture") stands as a foundational quality intimately related to righteous action. When a believer performs a deed solely for the sake of Allah's pleasure, without expectation of reward or compensation in return, that action is termed sincere. The word ikhlāṣ derives from *khalis*, meaning "uncontaminated" or "without adulteration." Thus sincerity denotes action undertaken without regard to worldly gain or personal benefit—the performance of duty for its own sake, as an offering to the Divine.[^cite-1]

### Sincerity of Parents

A luminous example of sincerity appears in the mother who nurses her child yet seeks no recompense from that child for her milk. Her actions flow from complete sincerity. Similarly, parents who raise their children well act with sincerity, recognizing that this duty has been entrusted to them by Allah through their nature (fitra). They expect no compensation from their children, understanding their responsibility as a sacred trust. The term "feast" (diyāfah) refers to father and mother together. ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ) و علی⟫The Prophet Muhammad and ʿAlī never demanded reward from the Community for their service, nor did they ever lodge complaint with Allah for the burdens they bore. They regarded their service as duty itself and discharged it with perfect sincerity. All the limits (ḥudūd) have served their masters with this same sincerity. ⟪ar:حضرت سلمان فارسی⟫Salman al-Fārisī would sweep the house of ⟪ar:مولاتنا فاطمۃ الزھرا⟫our master Fāṭima al-Zahrāʾ with his own garment.

---

A servant who serves his master from fear or greed—anticipating ransom or favor—is called a bad servant (ʿabd al-sūʾ). Likewise, whoever performs religious duties for the sake of Allah yet harbors desire for Paradise or fear of Hell has not achieved sincerity; such a person's prayer and deed lack true purification. The Master of the Universe, ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫the Commander of the Faithful ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, expresses this truth in his supplication: ⟪ar-quote:اِلٰہِی مَا عَبَدتُّکَ شَوقاً بِجَنَّتِکَ وَلاَ خَوفً مِن نَّارِکَ بَل وَجَدتُّکَ مُستَحَقًّ لِلعِباَدَۃ فَعَبَدتُّک⟫ "O Allah, I worship You not in passion for Paradise nor in fear of Hell, but I have found You worthy of worship and worship You for that reason alone."[^cite-2]

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)

## How Sincerity Comes to Exist

Sincerity cannot emerge in any action or prayer unless the believer first relinquishes his ⟪ar:حول⟫ *ḥawl* (cunning, artifice) and his ⟪ar:قوت⟫ *quwwa* (bodily strength). The *ḥawl* denotes the trickery and stratagem belonging to the soul's domain; *quwwa* signifies the physical power that the body possesses. When a person faces trouble or seeks to accomplish something, two paths lie before him: he may reach his goal either through cunning or through force. Sincerity arises when the believer establishes firm conviction that he possesses no power (ḥawl) or strength (quwwa) over anything whatsoever. He cannot dispel suffering through tricks and deceit, nor can he obtain good through bodily force alone. He believes that all ⟪ar:حول⟫ *ḥawl* ⟪ar:قوت⟫ and *quwwa* belong to Allah alone, and he pronounces with complete sincerity: ⟪ar-quote:لاَ حَولَ وَلاَ قُوَّتَ اِلاَّ بِا ﷲِ العَلِیِّ العَظِیم⟫"There is neither ⟪ar:حول⟫ cunning ⟪ar:قوت⟫ nor strength save in Allah the Most High, the Magnificent."[^cite-3] Whatever occurs does so according to the will of the Almighty. Only then, when he prays or acts, does sincerity manifest within him.

An instructive example clarifies this distinction. Consider travelers aboard a ship crossing the sea. Upon boarding, they pray to Allah for safety, yet their prayer lacks sincerity. Although they invoke Allah's protection at the journey's beginning, their trust in Allah mingles with reliance upon the ship's soundness, weather forecasts, and the skill of the ⟪ar:رَبَّان⟫ *rabbān* (captain). This compound trust calms their hearts. But when the vessel reaches deep waters and waves rise with increasing force, the ship begins to rock violently. At that moment, they perceive that the ship and the captain—those powers upon which they had leaned—also tremble in fear and anxiety, their faces bearing marks of dread. Then they cry to Allah for safety as before. Yet now their prayer differs fundamentally: ⟪ar:مُخلِصِینَ لَہُ الدِّینَ⟫ *mukhliṣīn lahu al-dīn* (making the faith purely His), for they are now convinced that none but Allah can deliver them. Their former prayer, while boarding, relied upon the ⟪ar:قوت⟫ captain's ⟪ar:حول⟫ *ḥawl* and *quwwa*. But after the tempest seized them, their prayer became reliant upon Allah ⟪ar:حول⟫ alone—utterly devoid of ⟪ar:قوت⟫ trust in any other, containing complete sincerity unmixed with reliance on created beings.

Similarly, the sincere person among those learned in astrology places his trust in Allah despite his knowledge and prays to Him. Though he possesses full understanding of coming misfortunes and knows all remedies to overcome them, he places his trust in Allah. He believes that only Allah can remove this evil if He wills, and that knowledge avails him nothing without Allah's will. In other words, he holds firmly that ⟪ar-quote:لاَ حَولَ وَلاَ قُوَّتَ اِلاَّ بِا ﷲِ العَلِیِّ العَظِیم⟫.

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)

### Difference Between the Wise and the Knower

⟪ar:عاقل⟫The *ʿāqil* (wise person) employs his intellect to find solutions to problems and discovers remedies, but does not turn to Allah. ⟪ar:عارف⟫The *ʿārif* (gnostic or knower), whose station transcends that of the wise, turns to Allah and remains sincere to Him even while possessing knowledge of the solution.

Consider two astrologers aboard a ship—one wise, one a gnostic. When a tempest strikes, the ⟪ar:عاقل⟫ wise person consults his astronomical learning, determining whether the vessel will sink, who shall perish and who survive. ⟪ar:عارف⟫Yet the gnostic, despite possessing equal knowledge from his astrology, places his trust in Allah and believes that whatever transpires shall be according to Allah's will. Put plainly: ⟪ar:عاقل⟫the wise person trusts in astrology as his ⟪ar:عارف⟫guide, while the knower trusts in the Creator. Because of this sincerity, the wise person's prayer finds no acceptance, whereas the prayer of the gnostic is always answered.

Both possess knowledge (ʿilm), yet they differ in reliance (tawakkul). The wise person first devises his plan through reliance on himself ⟪ar:حول و قوت⟫—his cunning and strength—and only when his efforts fail does he place trust in Allah. ⟪ar:عارف⟫The gnostic, by contrast, first places trust in Allah and surrenders his ⟪ar:حول و قوت⟫ cunning and strength to Allah, then begins his enterprise seeking Allah's aid. Therefore, the wise person trembles in difficulty, while the gnostic never panics. The gnostic's support and strength are ever manifest.

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)

## Piety and God-Consciousness

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (a.s.) has said: "Faith is such a magnificent blessing that if a person truly comprehended its worth, he would never envy the wealth of disbelievers or their comfortable earthly lives." For this reason, the Commander of the Faithful declared: ⟪ar-quote:رَضِینَا قِسمَتُ الجَبَّارِ فِینَا لَنَا عِلمٌ وَلِی اَعداَءِ ماَلُ⟫"We are content with the decree of the Mighty One concerning us: He has given us knowledge, and to our enemies, wealth."[^cite-4] Those who place their trust in Allah (*tawakkul*, reliance upon the Divine) shall find comfort in this world and joy in the Hereafter. Trust in Allah becomes a harbor for every exile, friendship for every loneliness, light for every darkness, strength for every weakness, and cure for every illness. 

History bears witness to people before us who trusted in Allah—they were slain, burned, their bodies dismembered and boiled alive—yet they sought no vengeance. Instead, they relied wholly upon the reward from their Allah. Pray for the ranks of such martyrs and endure the calamities of your own time, that you too may attain those lofty stations.

A person cannot taste the sweetness of faith unless he becomes indifferent to all that might bring him pleasure in this world. As long as worldly desires dominate the soul, the sweetness of faith remains forbidden to him. Yet whoever cultivates piety (taqwā) in this life shall find Allah placing wisdom in his heart and making it known through His eloquent speech. Allah shall reveal to such a person the corruptions hidden in worldly desire, turning his soul away from all temporal attachments and drawing him toward the Gardens of Allah in peace.

### Adversity and Patience

Patience (ṣabr) is the name for steadfastness during severe trials, conducted without panic and undertaken for the sake of righteous outcomes. It stands opposed to haste and distress. Any trial causing a person suffering is termed a calamity (muṣība), and it is necessary to bear each trial with patience. Scholars have identified two categories of affliction. The first arises as consequence of a person's own misdeeds: the gambler falls into poverty, the drunkard loses health, the adulterer and transgressor becomes destitute. This first form of suffering is common to all. The second category consists of trials wherein a person is tested and his spiritual ranks elevated. This second form pertains to the special servants of Allah.

(Muharram) Hazrat Nasrullah Sahib

Patience illuminates the hidden light and purity within a person, while panic and anxiety expose the darkness concealed within him. All claim patience, yet only the pure believer walks this path steadfastly. At the onset of any trial, the difference between the patient and the anxious becomes plain. The patient becomes humble at trouble's beginning, asking forgiveness for his sins. The anxious person complains and grows angry; when relief does not come, he grows weary and turns toward despair. The beginning of every patience is bitter, yet its end proves sweet. Allah says:

> ⟪quran 2:155⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَنَبْلُوَنَّكُم بِشَىْءٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْخَوْفِ وَٱلْجُوعِ وَنَقْصٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْأَمْوَلِ وَٱلْأَنفُسِ وَٱلثَّمَرَتِ ۗ وَبَشِّرِ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *And certainly, We shall test you with something of fear, hunger, loss of wealth, lives and fruits, but give glad tidings to As-Sabirin (the patient ones, etc.).*
> — **Qur'ān 2:155**

And for those truly patient, Allah proclaims:

> ⟪quran 8:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ وَلَا تَنَزَعُوا۟ فَتَفْشَلُوا۟ وَتَذْهَبَ رِيحُكُمْ ۖ وَٱصْبِرُوٓا۟ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ مَعَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *And obey Allah and His Messenger, and do not dispute (with one another) lest you lose courage and your strength depart, and be patient. Surely, Allah is with those who are As-Sabirin (the patient ones, etc.).*
> — **Qur'ān 8:46**[^cite-6]

(Kitab al-Masabih fi Ithbat al-Imama) Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (a.s.)

### When is Patience Obligatory?

The patience of an ordinary person begins when he endeavors to escape a trial yet finds escape impossible. If, for example, a thirsty person refrains from drinking water though water is available, he is not reckoned patient, for he possesses the means to remedy his suffering. But when no water exists and none can be obtained, then the stages of patience commence—as Imam al-Ḥusayn (a.s.) and his companions endured at Karbala when water eluded them. Scholars have identified two modes of patience:

| Restraint from what is coveted | Endurance of what is abhorred |

### The Ṣābirūn and Their Grades

Allah created humankind ⟪ar:اشرف المخلوقات⟫as the most noble of all creatures, and through intellect mankind has mastered the universe entire. Yet he could not subdue the smallest region of his body—a lump of flesh called the heart! Instead, it brought him under its dominion, enslaving him throughout his life to its needless desires and commands. Therefore, those who seize control of their hearts and slay their pleasures and lusts for Allah's sake undertake the most arduous and difficult of all tasks. There exist three grades of patience and love for Allah. The first two are metaphorical love; the third alone constitutes true love.[^cite-7]

**The First Grade (Deficient Patience):**

The first comprises those who believe in Allah, yet when affliction strikes, they open complaints and raise cries, unable to bear the weight of suffering. This represents a flawed form of patience and love.

**The Second Grade (Lower Patience):**

The second consists of those who accept Allah's will and endure calamity in silence without complaint. This is the lowest form of genuine patience and love.

⟪ar:ذوالنون مصری⟫Dhū'l-Nūn al-Miṣrī once passed through the marketplace of Kufa. There he encountered a man blind in both eyes and paralyzed in both hands and feet. Yet this man called upon his Lord with great sincerity, saying: "O my Lord! What thanksgiving befits Your majesty? O my God! Your bounty encompasses all Your creatures—what can speech say of Your grace? You bestow it upon the ungrateful as upon the thankful!" When Dhū'l-Nūn heard this supplication, he trembled. He approached, greeted the man, sat beside him, and said: "O brother, I wish to ask you a question. Tell me: for what blessing do you give thanks despite your misery?" The elder replied: "Glory be to Allah! How could I refrain from gratitude? His blessings rain upon me without ceasing. See how I am—neither seeing nor moving—yet He sustains me constantly! And He has placed me in a state wherein His will and pleasure dwell. O questioner, is this unworthy of thanksgiving?" Dhū'l-Nūn was deeply moved. He said: "O elder, do you have a need I might fulfill?" The man answered: "Yes, I have a recent need: I have a son who provides for me, but he has been missing three days. Can you find him?" ⟪ar:ذوالنون⟫Dhū'l-Nūn rose and searched for the boy. He discovered the son had been torn apart by wild beasts. Returning to the man, Dhū'l-Nūn considered how to break this tragic news. He then addressed the elder: "O old man, do you remember the story of Prophet Ayyūb (Job)?" The elder said: "What of Ayyūb? He was the most patient and grateful servant of Allah!" Dhū'l-Nūn then said: "O righteous one, may Allah grant you patience in your son's grief. He has passed away, torn by wild animals." The blind man raised his sightless eyes to heaven and said ⟪ar:اَلحَمدُ ﷲِ الَّذِی لَم یَجعَل فِی قَلبِی حَسرَۃً مِّنَ الدُّنیَا⟫: "Praise be to Allah, who has not planted worldly regret in my heart." Upon these words, a sigh escaped his breast, and he fell to the ground and died. ⟪ar:اِناَّ لِلّٰ�ہ وَ اِناَّ اِلَیہِ راَجِعُون⟫[^cite-8]

Scholars observe that despite this righteous elder's great strength of endurance and gratitude, even his patience remains ordinary and inferior in station.

**The Third Grade (Exalted Patience):**

The third grade comprises those who rejoice in the hardships presented along the path of Allah. Their yearning intensifies to such a degree that their hearts perpetually desire an increase in suffering. This represents the highest and purest form of love and patience—a station reserved uniquely for Muḥammad and the Family of Muḥammad. History testifies that the greater the tide of affliction upon these people, the brighter their countenances became.

⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫Consider Prophet Abraham: his life shows that he did not waver in offering to sacrifice his beloved son ⟪ar:اسماعیل⟫for Allah's sake—and this not merely upon a dream, but upon a command! Where the blind elder could not bear news of his son's death, here ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم⟫Abraham consented to slaughter his own son with his own hands. And observe the remarkable patience of his noble son ⟪ar:اسماعیل علیہ السلام⟫Ishmael, who said to his father: "Do as you have been commanded! ⟪ar:انشأ اللہ⟫God willing, you shall find me among the patient!"

⟪ar:منجلیخ⟫When ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫Abraham was cast into the fire, ⟪ar:جبرئیل علیہ السلام⟫the angel Gabriel came and said: "O Abraham, pray to Allah to deliver you from this trial!" He replied: "What need have I to pray to him? No! If this is my Lord's will, I accept it and shall be patient with His pleasure!"

The root of their patience lies in this: Allah has granted them power to remove their difficulties by His command through their very words whenever they wish. Yet these chosen ones, possessing such immense power, sacrifice their own ease for Allah's pleasure and bear the severest adversity with joy. To fulfill his father's vision, Ishmael offers his neck for sacrifice; and in the plains of Karbala, the Master offers himself and his family, his children for the Divine path.

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)

(Muharram) Hazrat Nasrullah ibn Hibatullah Sahib

### The Cause of Trial Upon the Prophets

How paradoxical it seems: the one whom Allah draws near to Him, entrusting him with the weighty responsibilities of Prophethood, is cast into trial and suffering. Does it befit a beloved to place his beloved in distress? Yet Divine Wisdom has established for those specially favored by Allah this principle: the condition of love is suffering. The closer one draws to Allah, the severer the trial; the farther from Him, the lighter the test. All understand that essence does not refine until it encounters the fire, stone does not attain beauty until carved, and the finer the crushing, the brighter the gleam. Gold reveals itself only upon the touchstone, and nature has ordained that advancement is witnessed only through trial, and elevation follows examination.

| Who has drunk the cup of sacrifice? Who has worn the crown of victory? |
| Who possesses wealth earned by sleeplessness? Who has gained knowledge through arduous toil? |

### The Purpose of Suffering

Every affliction that befalls a believer is directed purely toward his purification and elevation of rank. Conversely, misfortune striking a disbeliever constitutes punishment for his evil deeds. The Prophet (peace be upon him) alluded to this distinction: ⟪ar-quote:اَلمُؤمِنُ مُمتَحِن وَ الکاَفِرُ مُبتَلا⟫"The believer is tested; the disbeliever is punished."[^cite-9]

**Statement on the Qāʾim's Trials:**

When ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫the Qāʾim (the Risen One) appears, he shall test his appointed limits most severely, drawing them through trials as a cloth is wrung to release its final drop of water. Yet the purpose of these ordeals shall be to elevate their spiritual stations.

### Pain and the Self

The author of the periodical discusses the animal (sensate) soul (nafs ḥassiyya): Allah has made the soul more sensitive to pain than any other faculty, that it might guard against corruption of the body. Pain is inflicted upon the soul for the body's preservation. When one places a hand in fire, the soul suffers; but this pain serves the body, for the body would burn and decay were pain not felt. Since the soul is aware of fire's pain, it perpetually protects the body from burning. Had the soul felt no pain, it would not shield the body. Since the soul cannot survive in this world without the body, Allah's wisdom ordains that the soul feel pain—so that the body may be preserved.

**Interpretation:**

The boundary (ḥadd) exists in relation to its body, as the soul exists in relation to its limit.

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)

### The Concept of Trust During Patience

Some believers harbor the misconception that Allah wrongs them by inflicting affliction. Others suppose Allah is unaware of their calamity. Still others believe Allah knows of their trial but does not care, or that they do not matter in His sight. Some imagine Allah to be hard-hearted. Such misconceptions squander the reward of patience.

Prophets and righteous believers indeed endure hardship, yet their patience flows purely from devotion to Allah. They recognize that various divine purposes lie concealed within calamities, that these afflictions serve their betterment and that of their Community—though often this wisdom remains hidden from ordinary understanding. They confess that their Allah is the One, the Living, the All-Powerful, and the Wise. They believe that Allah rules this world and all it contains with perfect wisdom. And there is no decree of Allah in this world—neither easy nor burdensome—that does not conceal some divine wisdom and benefit, known only to Allah.

### Benefits of Patience

The greatest benefit of patience is that it brings freedom. It removes the veil of desires from a person's neck and creates within him detachment and indifference to all but Allah. The impatient person is bound in chains of desire, though he hears not the sound of his fetters. But patience can be attained only by those who truly love freedom.

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)

## Trust in Allah

The human being exists perpetually in two states: one of intensity and one of rest. A person is either anxious or tranquil, sorrowful or joyful, sick or healthy, impoverished or wealthy.

### The Believer's Trust

The true believer does not turn from obedience to Allah in either circumstance. When joyful, healthy, and successful, he places his trust in Allah and returns all his blessings to Allah, seeking His aid. He attributes all to ⟪ar:حول و قوت⟫*ḥawl* and *quwwa* (all cunning and strength) to Allah alone. Thus ⟪ar:نبی سلیمان علیہ السلام⟫Prophet Solomon, when granted dominion and taught the language of beasts and birds, declared: "This is a favor from my Lord, that He may test me whether I am grateful or ungrateful."

> ⟪quran 27:40⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱلَّذِى عِندَهُۥ عِلْمٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنَا۠ آتِيكَ بِهِۦ قَبْلَ أَن يَرْتَدَّ إِلَيْكَ طَرْفُكَ ۚ فَلَمَّا رَآهُ مُسْتَقِرًّا عِندَهُۥ قَالَ هَذَا مِن فَضْلِ رَبِّى لِيَبْلُوَنِىٓ ءَأَشْكُرُ أَمْ أَكْفُرُ ۖ وَمَن شَكَرَ فَإِنَّمَا يَشْكُرُ لِنَفْسِهِۦ ۖ وَمَن كَفَرَ فَإِنَّ رَبِّى غَنِىٌّۭ كَرِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *One with whom was knowledge of the Scripture said: "I will bring it to you within the twinkling of an eye!" then when [Sulaiman (Solomon)] saw it placed before him, he said: "This is by the Grace of my Lord to test me whether I am grateful or ungrateful! And whoever is grateful, truly, his gratitude is for (the good of) his ownself, and whoever is ungrateful, (he is ungrateful only for the loss of his ownself). Certainly! My Lord is Rich (Free of all wants), Bountiful."*
> — **Qur'ān 27:40**[^cite-10]

When a believer faces affliction, he pleads earnestly for Allah's mercy and bears hardship with patience. He believes that whatever difficulties he encounters stem from his own misdeeds, knowing that his Allah alone is just. He not only endures this suffering but also offers thanksgiving to his Lord, recognizing that through these trials his sins are being expiated. He harbors hope for mercy and accepts his Lord's commands with gratitude and patience, seeking forgiveness for his transgressions.

> ⟪quran 2:156⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ إِذَآ أَصَبَتْهُم مُّصِيبَةٌۭ قَالُوٓا۟ إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّآ إِلَيْهِ رَجِعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Who, when afflicted with calamity, say: "Truly! To Allah we belong and truly, to Him we shall return."*
> — **Qur'ān 2:156**

Some of Allah's specially chosen servants became prophets because they not merely endured such hardships but praised and thanked their Lord for every condition.

### The Trust of the Disbeliever

The *kāfir* (disbeliever), by contrast, attributes every blessing to his own self, declaring: "Whatever I have received, I have received through my own ability, effort, knowledge, and cunning." He returns all of Allah's blessings to material causes and relies upon them. For this reason, he turns away from his Lord. So Qārūn boasted: "I have obtained this vast treasure (its keys requiring seventy camels to bear) through my knowledge alone." When a disbeliever is struck by affliction, he becomes disgusted with Allah's mercy and blames Allah for his misfortune, saying: "The calamities that have befallen me come from Allah."

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)

## If Patience Were a Man

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: ⟪ar-quote:اِذاَ کاَنَ الصَّبَرُ رَجُلً لِکاَنَ رَجُلً صاَلِحاً⟫"If patience were created as a human being, he would be made into a righteous man."[^cite-11] ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫Imam Ja'far al-Ṣādiq (a.s.), explaining the inner meaning of this ḥadīth, states: "Patience ⟪ar:امام مستودع⟫constitutes an *imām mustawdaʿ* (hidden Imam) and ⟪ar:رجل صالحا⟫a righteous man ⟪ar:امام مستقر⟫signifies an *imām mustaqarr* (established Imam)." That is, when ⟪ar:امام مستودع⟫the hidden Imam is developed and manifested, he ⟪ar:امام مستقر⟫becomes established. In the time of ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ) و علی⟫the Prophet Muḥammad and ʿAlī, they constituted the congregation ⟪ar:مولانا سلمان فارسی⟫of the Persians, to whom the ⟪ar:صابرین⟫assembly of all the patient ones from preceding ages had gathered. Similarly, ⟪ar:امام حسن علیہ السلام، مستودع⟫Imam al-Ḥasan (a.s.) served as the hidden Imam, and ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫Imam Ja'far al-Ṣādiq says: "You shall ⟪ar:امام حسن علیہ السلام⟫advance through ⟪ar:مولانا سلمان فارسی⟫Salman al-Fārisī to the next sphere and receive the Imamate." That is, ⟪ar:صبر (سلمان فارسی)⟫ what was hidden ⟪ar:رجل صالح (امام حسن علیہ السلام)⟫shall be made manifest.

| Patience | Man of Righteousness |
| --- | --- |
| Hidden Imam | Established Imam |
| Salman al-Fārisī | Imam al-Ḥasan (a.s.) |

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)

## Asceticism and Abstinence

Piety (zuhd) denotes an attribute wherein a believer relinquishes both the forbidden and the lawful for the sake of Allah's pleasure and reverence. He discovers the highest halāl (permitted) things through a profound renunciation of the mundane. The believer does not flee from the world's blessings; rather, he receives all of Allah's bounties only according to his genuine need. Maulana ʿAlī (a.s.) has said: the *zāhid* (ascetic) is one who neither rejoices when worldly blessing comes to him nor grieves when it departs. Living in the world while renouncing worldly attachment—this is true asceticism.[^cite-12]

Once Maulana ʿAlī was offered a frozen dessert (fālūdha). He tasted it with merely two fingers and set it aside. When questioned—"O Maulana, this fālūdha is made from halāl milk; why do you not eat it?"—he replied: "I devote myself to asceticism! I fear lest my soul grow accustomed to the pleasure of this fālūdha."

Asceticism regarding this world and inclination toward the Hereafter constitute characteristics of faith. Human nature is formed such that a person is reluctant to forgo present gain for a distant promise (the Hereafter). Yet when a person becomes convinced that the profit of the distant period far exceeds present benefit, he abandons the present for the sake of the deferred gain and embraces asceticism. When the souls of ascetics depart their bodies, all the pleasures for which they renounced the world throughout their lives shall be theirs. At that moment, the Hereafter for which they strove becomes their world. The debt becomes currency! This is why Allah said to the Prophet:

> ⟪quran 93:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَلْآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْأُولَىٰ ‎⟫
> *And indeed the Hereafter is better for you than the present (life of this world).*
> — **Qur'ān 93:4**

Each state is connected to its completion; what comes later proves superior to what preceded it. Effort links to success, childhood to adulthood, the world to the Hereafter. Childhood's purpose is to achieve adulthood; effort's purpose is to achieve success. A good childhood yields good youth, and a good life yields a dignified old age. Each later state depends upon the one preceding it. Similarly, the world and Hereafter are intertwined—the Hereafter being far superior to the world. Hence ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah said: ⟪ar-quote:مَن زُہُدَ فِی الدُّنِیاَ اِھاَنَۃ عَلَیہِ الدُّنِیَا⟫"Whoever turns away from the world in asceticism—the world holds him in contempt."[^cite-13]

Scholars, wise ones, and prophets abandoned the world's immediate pleasures to acquire the desires of the Hereafter—blessings meant for their own joy. They cultivated desire for the Hereafter and yearning for things difficult and delayed. They did so only after the reality of the Hereafter became luminous to them. While dwelling in this world, they began perceiving the blessings of the Hereafter with ⟪ar:عیون القلب⟫the eyes of the heart and ⟪ar:نور العقل⟫the light of intellect, just as an ordinary person perceives this world's affairs through his ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫five senses. For these great souls, this world transformed into the Hereafter itself, for they understood that the destination of the Hereafter is attained by traversing this world. Thus they lived in this world as though they were living in the Hereafter.

The path to true knowledge of the Hereafter unfolds when the believer begins contemplating the affairs of this world through the lens of their usefulness and connection to the eternal. For this world has been fashioned as a mirror of the Hereafter; the world is the image, the Hereafter the original. When one perceives the affairs of this world and connects them with the affairs of the Hereafter, conviction in the blessings of the eternal realm grows. ⟪ar:اہل زہد⟫The people of asceticism follow all the commandments of the Sharīʿa without seeking reward and are those who shall inherit the Hereafter. Those who perform their deeds seeking recompense for their pleasure and benefit, neglecting the Hereafter, belong to the people of appetite.

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)

## The Purification of the Self and Knowledge

Allah has bestowed upon humankind two categories of blessing. One is ⟪ar:خارج الجسد⟫external to the body: possession of spouse, children, and the wealth of the world. The second type of blessing ⟪ar:داخل الجسد⟫lies within the body and is of two kinds—one residing in the body proper, the other in the soul. The blessing within the body encompasses sound health and all that flows from it: beauty of face, perfect form, strength, and agility. The blessing within the soul likewise divides into two. The first is ⟪ar:حُسنِ خُلق⟫*ḥusn al-khuluq* (excellence of moral character), and the second is ⟪ar:زکاوت النفس⟫*zakāt al-nafs* (the purification of the soul). The latter refers to ⟪ar:زکاوت النفس⟫the capacity to receive something good within one's self and the capacity to reject evil. The root of all ⟪ar:زکاوت النفس⟫blessed blessings of Allah is this foundation, upon which all other blessings rest.[^cite-14]

*(no content in source)*

⟪ar:زکاوۃ النفس⟫The purification of the soul is a blessing useful in obtaining ⟪ar:معارف⟫*maʿārif* (gnosis or spiritual knowledge)—that knowledge which bestows true understanding. Four categories of knowledge exist:

| Knowledge |
| --- |
| 1. Those given faith but not the knowledge of gnosis |
| 2. Those given apparent knowledge but not faith |
| 3. Those given neither knowledge nor faith |
| 4. Those given both knowledge and faith |

Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 30:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ وَٱلْإِيمَنَ لَقَدْ لَبِثْتُمْ فِى كِتَبِ ٱللَّهِ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْبَعْثِ ۖ فَهَذَا يَوْمُ ٱلْبَعْثِ وَلَكِنَّكُمْ كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those who have been bestowed with knowledge and faith will say: "Indeed you have stayed according to the Decree of Allah, until the Day of Resurrection, so this is the Day of Resurrection, but you knew not."*
> — **Qur'ān 30:56**[^cite-15]

*Baʿth* (awakening or resurrection) refers to being awakened. That is, you shall be awakened on the Day of Resurrection through knowledge and faith. In this verse, Allah identifies ⟪ar:معارف⟫those possessing gnosis as the highest of people. The noblest knowledge is to acquire understanding of Resurrection and Awakening, which the scholars and believers are attaining.

### Those Who Believe But Lack Knowledge

These are people who believe in the Books of the Prophets and what they contain, yet do not penetrate their details. When informed about the origin and duration of prophethood, the angels and their stations, Resurrection and Awakening, Reckoning and the Weighing of deeds, the Bridge, rewards and punishments, the blessings of Paradise and torments of Hell, they believe and affirm all this. Yet they do not acquire *maʿrifa* (gnosis), nor do they contemplate these matters deeply, for their souls lack spiritual warmth. They do not ask "How?" "What?" "Why?" or "Where?"—having no yearning to uncover the hidden realities.

### Those Who Possess Knowledge Without Faith

These are people granted a portion of worldly knowledge but not faith. They peruse apparent books and discuss their details, incorporating what these texts contain into their understanding. They dominate various worldly sciences: medicine, engineering, mathematics, geometry, astronomy, physics, chemistry. They become so attached to these sciences that they become identified with them, forgetting the Qur'ān and Ḥadīth. They leave the ⟪ar:تنزیلات⟫revelations of the prophets behind and do not contemplate the mysteries of Sharīʿa matters. Consequently, nature conceals from them the realities of the prophetic tradition. They become blind to the cause-and-effect of their worldly knowledge and begin to doubt the facts of religion and the knowledge of the unseen, becoming bewildered about the knowledge of their meanings. For them, Allah says:

> ⟪quran 40:83⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَلَمَّا جَآءَتْهُمْ رُسُلُهُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ فَرِحُوا۟ بِمَا عِندَهُم مِّنَ ٱلْعِلْمِ وَحَاقَ بِهِم مَّا كَانُوا۟ بِهِۦ يَسْتَهْزِءُونَ ‎⟫
> *Then when their Messengers came to them with clear proofs, they were glad (and proud) with that which they had of the knowledge (of worldly things): And that at which they used to mock, surrounded them (i.e. the punishment).*
> — **Qur'ān 40:83**

The purpose of worldly knowledge is here exposed.

### Those Who Possess Neither Knowledge Nor Faith

These are people who have been given neither knowledge nor faith. They spend their entire lives pursuing their desires, surrounded by the world's blessings and the sweetness of their pleasures. They have abandoned the assembly of the virtuous and learned ones. They are deprived of the Sharīʿa's commandments and all religious knowledge, which has as its fundamental purpose the liberation and elevation of the soul toward the Hereafter. Allah points to them and says: "We have made the world a mere passage for them." Allah has forsaken them. They accumulate worldly wealth until wealth becomes their master. And they obtain the world just as a wild animal obtains its food. Hell has been prepared for them.

### Those Given Both Knowledge and Faith

These are the ⟪ar:صاحبان معرفت⟫*ṣāḥibān maʿrifa* (the people of gnosis)—those possessing both knowledge and faith—and they are the chosen servants of Allah. These people fully utilize the blessings Allah has granted and continue rising toward the highest stations of faith through the pursuit of gnosis. These people appear as the daylight among the night of the prophets and messengers.

(Rasa'il Ahmadiyyah) Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbd Allah (a.s.)
