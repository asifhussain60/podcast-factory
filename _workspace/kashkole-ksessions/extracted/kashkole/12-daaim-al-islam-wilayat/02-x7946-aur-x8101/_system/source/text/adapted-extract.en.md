# Islam and Faith

## What is Faith?

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (the Sixth Imam) stated: "Believing in Allah is the best of all deeds, and therefore its reward is greatest." He defined faith as the testimony embodied in the formula: ⟪ar-quote:أشھد ان لا الہ الا اللہ وحدہ لا شریک لہ و أشھد ان محمد عبدہ و رسولہ⟫ — "I testify that there is no god but Allah, alone without partner, and I testify that Muḥammad is His servant and messenger." Beyond this verbal affirmation, faith encompasses the conviction that Paradise is true, that Hell is true, and that resurrection from the grave and the Day of Judgment are realities. It requires ⟪ar:انبیا و مرسلین⟫ confirming the Prophets and Messengers, ⟪ar:ائمہ طاہرین علیہم السلام⟫ affirming the Pure Imams (upon whom be peace), and ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ obeying the Imam. Faith demands obedience to the commands that Allah has enjoined upon His servants and abstention from all that He has forbidden.[^cite-1]

The Imam teaches that faith comprises three essential dimensions:

| **Confirmation of the Heart** | **Expression of the Tongue** | **Action of the Limbs** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Heartfelt conviction | Words spoken aloud | Bodily deeds |

When asked whether affirmation of the heart and expression of the tongue constitute an act, he replied: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان عمَلُ کلُّہ⟫ "Faith is an act in its entirety." Speech itself is an action performed by the faculty of language. Belief and the acquisition of knowledge are actions of the heart; the engagement of all bodily faculties in obedience to Allah and His Messenger constitutes the action of the body. **Thus faith is nothing less than the totality of acts** — a unified expression of conviction, utterance, and deed.

| **Function of the Heart** | **Function of the Tongue** | **Function of the Body** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The practice of belief and knowledge | The practice of testimony | Actions through all organs |

Allah, Most High, declares:

> ⟪quran 2:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَالِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and do righteous deeds—those are the inhabitants of Paradise; they shall dwell therein forever.*

Wherever the Qurʾān mentions faith, it conjoins it with righteous action, establishing their inseparable bond. Although faith pertains to the heart and action to the organs and faculties, their integration defines the believer. ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ The Commander of the Faithful expresses this in ⟪ar:نہج البلاغہ⟫ the *Nahj al-Balaghah*: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان اعتقاد بالقلب و عمل بالارکان و اقرار بالسان⟫ "Faith is conviction of the heart, practice through the limbs, and confession by the tongue."[^cite-2] Faith originates within the heart and culminates in righteous deeds, which necessarily include confession of the tongue. The light of faith rises from within and shines outward, whereas Islam (submission) emerges from the tongue as an external declaration. Islam addresses the outward observance of the Law (*Sharīʿah*), while faith penetrates to its inner meaning (*bāṭin*).

### The Semantic Roots of Faith

The word faith (īmān) literally signifies confirmation or attestation, and carries the sense of granting security. As a transitive verb, it requires both action and a recipient of that action. When we speak of confirmation, we must ask: who or what is being confirmed? The one who confirms becomes the *muṣaddiq* (affirmer), and that which is confirmed becomes the object. If faith means to grant security or protection, then equally a recipient of that protection must exist. The Prophet Muḥammad (upon whom be peace) unified both meanings in a single ḥadīth: ⟪ar-quote:ھِینَ اَلمُؤمِن مَن آمَنَ جَورِہِ بِوَایقَۃ⟫ "A believer is one who safeguards his neighbour from his wrongdoing."[^cite-3] Thus a person is called ⟪ar:اَلمُؤمِنُ⟫ a believer (*al-muʾmin*) — one of the ⟪ar:اسمآء الحسنی⟫ Divine Names — both because he affirms Allah's ⟪ar:وَعَدُ وَعِیدْ⟫ promise and threat, and because Allah Himself grants him security from His punishing wrath.

| **Active Agent** | **Verb of Action** | **Effect** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The believer | Confirmation (*taṣdīq*) | Of Allah's word |
| The believer | Security (*amān*) | Granted to him by creatures |
| Allah | Protection (*amān*) | From His punishment |

## Faith of the Heart, Tongue, and Body

The Imam teaches: "Allah has made faith incumbent upon every part of the human being and has apportioned it among all the organs." Not a single one of the twelve principal organs of the human body remains exempt from the obligation to embody faith in its own manner. Each organ possesses its own specific faith (*īmān khāṣṣ*), distinct from that of all others. Allah has distributed faith among the following organs:

| **Organ** | Heart | Tongue | Ear | Eye | Hand | Monday | Face | Farj |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Count** | 1 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 1 | 1 |

### Faith of the Heart

The heart, from which understanding and insight flow, is the sovereign of the entire body. No organ may act without its permission and command. Allah has ordained for the heart these forms of faith: confession, knowledge, belief, acknowledgment, and acceptance. The believer must confess inwardly that Allah is One, that none deserves worship save He alone. ⟪ar-quote:وحدہ لا شریک لہ⟫ He is singular and self-subsisting, possessing neither wife nor offspring. ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ Muḥammad, the Chosen One, and all the Prophets are the servants of Allah and His true messengers. The heart must then acknowledge all that has been revealed by Allah—the Book and the Prophet—and must acquire the knowledge of the limits (*ḥudūd*), which strengthens faith itself. This is the practical and cognitive faith that Allah has decreed for the heart. The Qurʾān attests:

> ⟪quran 16:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَن كَفَرَ بِٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ إِيمَنِهِۦٓ إِلَّا مَنْ أُكْرِهَ وَقَلْبُهُۥ مُطْمَىِٕنٌّۢ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ وَلَكِن مَّن شَرَحَ بِٱلْكُفْرِ صَدْرًۭا فَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Whosoever disbelieves in Allah after having believed—save he who is compelled whilst his heart remains at peace in faith—but whosoever opens his breast to disbelief shall have wrath from Allah, and theirs shall be a great torment.*

> ⟪quran 2:284⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لِّلَّهِ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَإِن تُبْدُوا۟ مَا فِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ أَوْ تُخْفُوهُ يُحَاسِبْكُم بِهِ ٱللَّهُ ۖ فَيَغْفِرُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَن يَشَآءُ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌ ‎⟫
> *To Allah belongs all that is in the heavens and all that is in the earth. Whether you disclose what is in your hearts or conceal it, Allah shall call you to account for it. Then He forgives whom He wills and punishes whom He wills. Allah is over all things powerful.*

> ⟪quran 5:41⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ لَا يَحْزُنكَ ٱلَّذِينَ يُسَرِعُونَ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ آمَنَّا بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَلَمْ تُؤْمِن قُلُوبُهُمْ ۛ وَمِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَادُوا۟ ۛ سَمَّعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ سَمَّعُونَ لِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ لَمْ يَأْتُوكَ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ مَوَاضِعِهِۦ ۖ يَقُولُونَ إِنْ أُوتِيتُمْ هَذَا فَخُذُوهُ وَإِن لَّمْ تُؤْتَوْهُ فَٱحْذَرُوا۟ ۚ وَمَن يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ فِتْنَتَهُۥ فَلَن تَمْلِكَ لَهُۥ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ شَيْـًٔا ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَمْ يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ أَن يُطَهِّرَ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا خِزْىٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O Messenger, let not those who rush into disbelief grieve thee—those who say with their mouths "We believe" whilst their hearts believe not; and those among the Jews who hearken unto falsehood, hearkening to other folk who came not unto thee, who distort the words from their places, saying "If you are given this then take it, but if you are not given this then beware." Whomsoever Allah wills to lead into trial, thou hast no power for him against Allah. Those are they whose hearts Allah willed not to purify. For them is disgrace in this world, and in the Hereafter a great torment.*

> ⟪quran 13:28⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَتَطْمَىِٕنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ تَطْمَىِٕنُّ ٱلْقُلُوبُ ‎⟫
> *Those who believe and whose hearts find rest in the remembrance of Allah—verily in the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest.*

### Faith of the Tongue

The faith ordained for the tongue is the faith of words and utterance—the articulation in speech of what the heart has affirmed and believed. As Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 3:84⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْنَا وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَإِسْمَعِيلَ وَإِسْحَقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَٱلْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَآ أُوتِىَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَٱلنَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُۥ مُسْلِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say: We believe in Allah and in that which has been revealed unto us, and that which was revealed unto Abraham, Ishmael, Isaac, Jacob and the Tribes, and that which was given unto Moses and Jesus, and that which the Prophets were given from their Lord. We make no distinction between any of them, and unto Him we have surrendered.*

> ⟪quran 33:70⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَقُولُوا۟ قَوْلًۭا سَدِيدًۭا ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! Fear Allah and speak words that are true.*

> ⟪quran 18:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيُؤْمِن وَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيَكْفُرْ ۚ إِنَّآ أَعْتَدْنَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ نَارًا أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ سُرَادِقُهَا ۚ وَإِن يَسْتَغِيثُوا۟ يُغَاثُوا۟ بِمَآءٍۢ كَٱلْمُهْلِ يَشْوِى ٱلْوُجُوهَ ۚ بِئْسَ ٱلشَّرَابُ وَسَآءَتْ مُرْتَفَقًا ‎⟫
> *And say: The truth is from your Lord; so let him who will believe, and let him who will disbelieve. Verily We have prepared for the wrongdoers a Fire, whose walls will encompass them. If they cry for relief they shall be relieved with water like molten brass that scaleth the faces. Evil the drink and evil the resting place!*

### Faith of the Ears

The ears bear the duty to hear Allah's command and attend to it with full concentration, and to abstain from listening to that which is forbidden or displeasing to Allah. He says:

> ⟪quran 4:140⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنْ إِذَا سَمِعْتُمْ آيَتِ ٱللَّهِ يُكْفَرُ بِهَا وَيُسْتَهْزَأُ بِهَا فَلَا تَقْعُدُوا۟ مَعَهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّكُمْ إِذًۭا مِّثْلُهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ جَامِعُ ٱلْمُنَفِقِينَ وَٱلْكَفِرِينَ فِى جَهَنَّمَ جَمِيعًا ‎⟫
> *Already has He sent down unto you in the Book that when ye hear the signs of Allah being denied and ridiculed, sit not with them until they engage in some other discourse. Verily in that case ye would be like them. Allah will gather the hypocrites and the disbelievers all together in Hell.*

> ⟪quran 6:68⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخُوضُونَ فِىٓ آيَتِنَا فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦ ۚ وَإِمَّا يُنسِيَنَّكَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَلَا تَقْعُدْ بَعْدَ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And when thou seest those who engage in a false discourse concerning Our signs, turn away from them until they engage in some other discourse. Should Satan cause thee to forget, then sit not after recollection with the wrongdoing folk.*

> ⟪quran 39:18⟫
> ⟪ar:فَبَشِّرْ عِبَادِ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَمِعُونَ ٱلْقَوْلَ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ أَحْسَنَهُۥٓ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمْ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *Bear glad tidings to My servants—those who listen to the word and follow the best of it. Those are they whom Allah has guided, and those are they possessed of understanding.*

> ⟪quran 23:1-4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَدْ أَفْلَحَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ فِى صَلَاتِهِمْ خَشِعُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ ٱللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَوٰةِ فَعِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *Successful indeed are the believers—those who are humble in their prayers, those who turn away from vain discourse, and those who pay the alms.*

### Faith of the Eyes

Allah has ordained that the eyes refrain from gazing upon that which is forbidden, keeping the gaze lowered from all things that Allah has proscribed. This constitutes the faith of the eyes, as He declares:

> ⟪quran 24:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Tell the believing men to lower their gaze and guard their modesty. That is purer for them. Verily Allah is aware of what they do.*

### The Faith of the Chastity-Zones

The verse means that a believer must not gaze upon the private parts of his brother, and must safeguard his own modesty so that none may look upon him. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq clarifies that, whilst most verses concerning the protection of modesty address the prevention of adultery, this particular verse concerning ⟪ar:غَضُّ بَصَر⟫ the lowering of the gaze refers to not looking upon the private parts themselves. Allah speaks of the duties of heart, tongue, eye and ear in a single verse:

> ⟪quran 17:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْبَصَرَ وَٱلْفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَانَ عَنْهُ مَسْـُٔولًۭا ‎⟫
> *And pursue not that of which thou hast no knowledge. Verily the hearing and the sight and the heart—each of these shall be questioned.*

> ⟪quran 41:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَن يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَآ أَبْصَرُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَكِن ظَنَنتُمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And ye did not think to hide from your hearing, your sight and your skins bearing witness against you, but ye supposed that Allah knew not much of what ye do.*

### Faith of the Hands

Allah has ordained that both hands be employed only in lawful pursuits: in charity, kindness, maintaining kinship, striving in Allah's path, and ritual ablution, as He declares:

> ⟪quran 5:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ فَٱغْسِلُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْمَرَافِقِ وَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِرُئُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُمْ جُنُبًۭا فَٱطَّهَّرُوا۟ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَآءَ أَحَدٌۭ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَمَسْتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا۟ مَآءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا۟ صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا فَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُم مِّنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُم مِّنْ حَرَجٍۢ وَلَكِن يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! When ye rise up for prayer, wash your faces and your hands up to the elbows, rub your heads and your feet up to the ankles. And if ye are in a state of greater impurity, purify yourselves. But if ye are ill or on a journey, or one of you cometh from the closet, or ye have touched women, and ye find not water, then go to clean earth and rub your faces and your hands with it. Allah would not place a burden upon you; but He would purify you and would perfect His grace upon you, that ye may give thanks.*

> ⟪quran 8:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ زَحْفًۭا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلْأَدْبَارَ ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! When ye meet those who disbelieve in the advance, turn not your backs to them.*

> ⟪quran 47:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ فَضَرْبَ ٱلرِّقَابِ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَثْخَنتُمُوهُمْ فَشُدُّوا۟ ٱلْوَثَاقَ فَإِمَّا مَنًّۢا بَعْدُ وَإِمَّا فِدَآءً حَتَّىٰ تَضَعَ ٱلْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا ۚ ذَلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَآءُ ٱللَّهُ لَٱنتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ وَلَكِن لِّيَبْلُوَا۟ بَعْضَكُم بِبَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱلَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ فَلَن يُضِلَّ أَعْمَلَهُمْ ‎⟫
> *So when ye meet those who disbelieve, smite the necks until, when ye have subdued them, bind them fast. Then afterwards either grace or ransom till the war lay down its burden. That is the ordinance. And if Allah willed He could have punished them Himself, but He ordaineth it thus that He may try some of you by means of others. And those who are slain in the way of Allah, He rendereth not their actions vain.*

In this verse, Allah makes incumbent upon the hands the duty to strike at the necks upon the battlefield; for striking is an action of the hands, and thus constitutes a dimension of faith.

### Faith of the Feet

Allah has ordained that the feet walk in the paths of obedience, never carrying the believer toward wrongdoing and disobedience. Rather, the feet must bear him toward Allah's command and Allah's pleasure. He declares:

> ⟪quran 17:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَمْشِ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا ‎⟫
> *Walk not upon the earth exultantly. Verily thou wilt neither rend the earth nor reach the mountains in height.*

> ⟪quran 31:19⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱقْصِدْ فِى مَشْيِكَ وَٱغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلْأَصْوَتِ لَصَوْتُ ٱلْحَمِيرِ ‎⟫
> *And be modest in thy gait and lower thy voice. Verily the harshest of voices is the voice of the ass.*

> ⟪quran 62:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا نُودِىَ لِلصَّلَوٰةِ مِن يَوْمِ ٱلْجُمُعَةِ فَٱسْعَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! When the call is heard for the prayer of the day of congregation, haste unto remembrance of Allah and leave your trading. That is better for you if ye did but know.*

> ⟪quran 22:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ لْيَقْضُوا۟ تَفَثَهُمْ وَلْيُوفُوا۟ نُذُورَهُمْ وَلْيَطَّوَّفُوا۟ بِٱلْبَيْتِ ٱلْعَتِيقِ ‎⟫
> *Then let them complete their ritual obligations, pay their vows, and circumambulate the Ancient House.*

Allah attests in the following verse that on the Day of Resurrection, the hands and feet shall testify by speech against themselves and their masters, bearing witness to what they have wrought:

> ⟪quran 36:65⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ نَخْتِمُ عَلَىٰٓ أَفْوَهِهِمْ وَتُكَلِّمُنَآ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَتَشْهَدُ أَرْجُلُهُم بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
> *This Day We set a seal upon their mouths. Yet their hands speak unto Us, and their feet bear witness as to what they used to earn.*

### Faith of the Face

Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 22:77⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱرْكَعُوا۟ وَٱسْجُدُوا۟ وَٱعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ وَٱفْعَلُوا۟ ٱلْخَيْرَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! Bow down and prostrate yourselves and worship your Lord, and do good, that ye may prosper.*

In another verse He says:

> ⟪quran 72:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنَّ ٱلْمَسَجِدَ لِلَّهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا۟ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ أَحَدًۭا ‎⟫
> *And verily the places of worship belong to Allah. So invoke not anyone along with Allah.*

Thus Allah has made it incumbent upon the face to be purified and to perform prayer, and describes prayer itself as faith in His Book. When Allah commanded His Messenger during the prayer to turn from ⟪ar:بیت المقدس⟫ the House of the Sacred (Jerusalem) toward ⟪ar:بیت الحرام⟫ the Sacred House (Mecca), the people asked, "O Messenger of Allah, what of our previous prayers?" Allah answered:

> ⟪quran 2:143⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Thus have We appointed you a middle nation, that ye may be witnesses unto mankind, and that the Messenger may be a witness unto you. And We appointed the qibla which thou wast wont to face only that We might know him who followeth the Messenger from him who turneth on his heels. In truth it was a hard test save for those whom Allah guided. But it was not that Allah should make your faith of no effect. Lo! Allah is Full of Pity, Merciful toward mankind.*

**In this verse, Allah describes prayer itself as faith.** One who departs from this world with all his limbs protected from sin, each organ having fulfilled its faith-obligation with excellence, shall receive complete faith from Allah and shall enter Paradise. Conversely, whoever has betrayed Allah in the slightest or transgressed His commands through these organs shall meet Allah in a state of defective faith. Whoever has donned the covenant with Allah and the Imam of the Age, yet neither acts upon nor acquires knowledge, cannot be called a believer. Mere verbal affirmation without corresponding action grants him no benefit and confers no standing in Allah's sight or in the Imam's esteem; rather, such a person stands outside the circle of the Imam of the Age.[^cite-4]

## Three Types of Faith

⟪ar:قرآن حکیم⟫ According to the Wise Qurʾān, there exist three categories of faith:

| **1** | **Pure Faith** |
| --- | --- |
| **2** | **Deficient Faith** |
| **3** | **Faith mixed with Polytheism** |

### Pure Faith

Those possessed of pure faith (*īmān kāmil*) are those whom Allah Himself commends and to whom He announces glad tidings of Paradise. Such persons manifest the two pillars of faith: verification (*taṣdīq*) and security (*amān*). They hold the rank of those who, though their bodies dwell in this world ⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫ the Abode of Falsehood, their subtle souls (*nufūs laṭīfah*) ⟪ar:دار الصدق و صفا⟫ are stationed in the Abode of Truth and Purity. Outwardly present in this world, they inwardly fix their gaze upon the Hereafter, contrary to ordinary believers who fix their gaze upon this world whilst dwelling in it.

Security (*amān*) comes upon them in such wise that when they reach ⟪ar:نفوس لطیفۃ⟫ Paradise ⟪ar:دار السلام⟫ the Abode of Peace, they shall be secure from every calamity that befalls the body after death. For them, death becomes a sweet perfume of deliverance. Through death they are completely liberated from ⟪ar:دار کدورت⟫ the Abode of Turbidity and shall endure forever in felicity. For this reason the Prophet (upon whom be peace) said, "Death is a fragrant messenger of glad tidings to the believer,"[^cite-5] and ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, gesturing to his throat, declared: "When the soul of my believer arrives here, he rejoices!"

⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی علیہ السلام⟫ The Commander of the Faithful and ⟪ar:ائمہ طاھرین⟫ the Pure Imams teach that ⟪ar:ولی أمر⟫ the Imam and Guardian of the Age, being the ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ successor of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, to whom the address ⟪ar:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ "O ye who believe!" is directed in the Qurʾān, commands the obedience of all believers.

The Prophet (upon whom be peace) declared: "Those whom Allah addressed in the Qurʾān with ⟪ar-quote:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ are the leaders (*qāʾidūn*) and commanders; and this leadership and command shall remain with the descendants of ʿAlī as long as believers remain who are addressed [by these words]." Since this address encompasses both ⟪ar:مُتَقَدِّمِینَ⟫ the ancients and ⟪ar:مُتَأَخِّرِینَ⟫ the moderns—all believers throughout time—this religious leadership and command shall endure ⟪ar:اِلیٰ یَومِ الدِّینَ⟫ unto the Day of Judgment. ⟪ar:محمد و علی(ﷺ)⟫ Muḥammad and ʿAlī are the witnesses and guides of all believers. Whoever disobeys the Imams is disobedient to Allah and His Messenger and merits the title of ⟪ar:خارجی⟫ one who goes out (separates himself); when such a person dies, he dies in a state of ignorance. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger and those in authority among you. And if ye dispute concerning any matter, refer it unto Allah and His Messenger if ye are believers in Allah and the Last Day. That is better and more seemly in the end.*

### Deficient Faith

The second category comprises those of imperfect or deficient faith (*īmān nāqiṣ*). These are newly ⟪ar:دعوت ایمانیۃ⟫ entered into the Summons of Faith and are merely ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ responsive to it—the neophytes, those ignorant of religious sciences and matters. Their example is that of a newborn child who, though called human by virtue of possessing all the physical faculties in their completeness, nonetheless requires constant supervision and protection at every moment and step, for he lacks the power and ability to use these faculties. He cannot accomplish anything by himself. Similarly, ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ those in the early stages of the Summons, though they bear the name *believer*, remain dependent upon the guidance and training of their spiritual elders at every turn. Allah Himself elucidates this defective grade of faith in this verse:

> ⟪quran 4:136⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ آمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ مِن قَبْلُ ۚ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَلًۢا بَعِيدًا ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! Believe in Allah and His Messenger and the Book which He hath revealed unto His Messenger, and the Scripture which He revealed aforetime. Whoso disbelieveth in Allah and His angels and His scriptures and His messengers and the Last Day, he verily hath wandered far astray.*

In this verse, Allah addresses those whom He has already called *believers*: "O ye who believe, believe!" If these persons already possessed ⟪ar:کامل الایمان⟫ perfect faith, such a command would be superfluous. Yet Allah first testifies to their believing by saying "O believers," then enjoins them to "believe." The paradox resolves thus: those addressed are believers at a lower rank. Allah is exhorting them, "Perfect your faith! You have entered the circle of faith after a period of neglect; you have attained religious birth. Now continue advancing in religious knowledge and ascend to the rank of ⟪ar:' آمَنُو'⟫ perfection. Remain in the company of the righteous—your spiritual parents—and benefit from their spiritual training until you attain their degree of perfection, deserving to dwell in ⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫ the Abode of Falsehood no longer but in ⟪ar:دار الصدق و امان⟫ the Abode of Truth and Security." Just as young children accept the discipline and instruction of their parents and teachers, ascending to their station, so too does Allah teach:

> ⟪quran 52:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and whose issue follow them in the faith—We cause their issue to join them (in the Garden), We deprive them of nought of their deeds. Each soul is a pledge for that which it hath earned.*

Here "issue" refers necessarily to spiritual and causal descendants, not to lineage by blood. The believers at a lower rank—the ⟪ar:مستجیبین⟫ responsive ones—followed the Imams of the higher rank in the Summons (*daʿwah*), and thus at last We made them equal to their spiritual elders, elevating them above them.[^cite-6]

### Faith Mixed with Polytheism

This is a faith that has absorbed polytheism and shares nothing of true faith in Allah and His Messenger. True Islamic faith demands that we accept the commands delivered by the Messenger of Allah through Allah's revelation without any alteration, ⟪ar:مِنُّ و عَن⟫ in their pristine state, without hesitation or double-mindedness; for such obedience purifies our souls and secures our salvation.

Allah commands us: ⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ آتَیكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوہُ وَمَا نَھٰكُمْ عَنْہُ فَانتَھوا۟⟫ "Take what the Messenger gives you, and refrain from what he forbids you."[^cite-7] Do not interpose your opinion in the slightest upon the commands and obedience of the Messenger. The perfect believer is one who stands in direct relation to the Prophet (upon whom be peace) in all matters of worship, the lawful and unlawful, and all religious affairs—one who permits no intrusion of personal opinion, conjecture, or independent reasoning. Conversely, one who mingles his desires with the Messenger's commands and employs opinion, speculation, and individual reasoning (*ijtihād*) to satisfy his inclinations is not a believer but an innovator (*muḥdith*). Because he has associated his judgment with Allah's, his faith becomes tainted with polytheism (*shirk*), leading him toward error instead of guidance, toward ruin instead of salvation. No good deed—however many prayers he performs or fasts he observes—shall benefit such a person.[^cite-8]

The revelation sent to the Messenger resembles water falling from the sky. Water cleanses and purifies the body, clothing, and all things so long as it remains undefiled. Once impurity overcomes it, that water becomes useless to the Muslim, even though it retains the name "water." The defect renders it unfit for drinking and unusable for purification. Similarly, when the desires of the self mingle with the rulings of revelation, pure religion ceases to exist and transforms into innovation (*bidʿah*). The Prophet (upon whom be peace) said, "Every innovation is misguidance, no matter how fair it may appear."[^cite-9] Even after innovation takes hold, every pillar of Islam may seemingly remain in place—the opponents also pray, as do the Imams—yet the difference between the two is manifest. The prayers of the opponents will be cast upon their faces on the Day of Judgment because they have mingled the bitterness of polytheism and the corruption of innovation into their faith. In condemnation of such people, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 12:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا يُؤْمِنُ أَكْثَرُهُم بِٱللَّهِ إِلَّا وَهُم مُّشْرِكُونَ ‎⟫
> *And most of them believe not in Allah except that they attribute partners unto Him.*

#### On the Alms of the Polytheists

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq reports that his father, ⟪ar:مولانا محمد الباقر علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Muḥammad al-Bāqir, once asked ⟪ar:حضرت جابر بن عبد اللہ الانصاری رضی اللہ عنہ⟫ the Companion Jābir ibn ʿAbd Allāh al-Anṣārī: "O Jābir, has Allah made the alms-tax (*zakāh*) incumbent upon the polytheists?" ⟪ar:حضرت جابر⟫ Jābir replied, "No, Allah has made alms-tax incumbent only upon the Muslims." The Imam then said, "Have you not heard the word of Allah:

> ⟪quran 41:6-7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أَنَا۠ بَشَرٌۭ مِّثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ أَنَّمَآ إِلَهُكُمْ إِلَهٌۭ وَحِدٌۭ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُشْرِكِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَافِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say: I am only a mortal like you. It is revealed unto me that your God is One God. So take the straight path unto Him and seek forgiveness of Him. And woe unto those who ascribe partners [unto Him]—those who give not the alms-tax and who are disbelievers in the Hereafter.*

⟪ar:حضرت جابر⟫ Jābir said: "When I heard this verse from the blessed lips of ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ the Imam al-Bāqir, it was as though I had never read this verse before, though it is in Allah's Book!" ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ Imam al-Ṣādiq then explained: "This verse was revealed concerning those who have entered into the *wilāyah* (sacred guardianship) but withheld their alms-tax from one who was established as their leader." ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ It is reported that ⟪ar:مولانا علی علیہ السلام⟫ the Commander of the Faithful declared: "This verse shows that Allah has called those Muslims who do not pay alms-tax as polytheists!"[^cite-10]

*(Majālis al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)*

*(Rasāʾil Aḥmadiyyah, Imām Aḥmad ibn ʿAbdullāh)*

## Three Grades of Believers

⟪ar:مؤمنین⟫ Believers are divided into three ranks:

| **First Grade** | Those who oppress themselves—that is, who acquire knowledge but do not put it into practice. |
| --- | --- |
| **Second Grade** | Those who evaluate themselves—who balance both religion and worldly affairs, keeping both in view. |
| **Third Grade** | Those who give away in charity for Allah's sake—those who orient themselves wholly toward the Hereafter. |

## Eight Classes of Faith

Just as Paradise possesses eight gates, faith itself possesses eight grades:

| **1** | Kindness to parents—this is the highest grade of faith. |
| --- | --- |
| **2** | Beneficence toward relatives. |
| **3** | Kindness toward orphans. |
| **4** | Kindness toward the poor. |
| **5** | Favor toward the neighbor who is also a kinsman and a believer. |
| **6** | Beneficence toward a believing neighbor unrelated by blood. |
| **7** | Kindness toward a neighbor with whom one shares neither kinship nor religion. |
| **8** | Kindness toward travelers and slaves. |

## Qualities of a Believer

A believer attains not to perfection of intellect until he develops within himself these ten qualities:

| **1** | He takes only good from the people of this world. |
| --- | --- |
| **2** | He harbors no fear of evil from them. |
| **3** | He deems his own good deeds few in comparison to the good deeds of others. |
| **4** | He views his own evil as abundant, but the evil of others as scanty. |
| **5** | He never tires of inquiring after people's needs. |
| **6** | He does not weary of acquiring knowledge; he pursues it throughout his life. |
| **7** | Patience is more precious to him than glory or honor. |
| **8** | Poverty is more esteemed by him than wealth. As the Prophet (upon whom be peace) declared: "My poverty is my pride." |
| **9** | A little food suffices him. |
| **10** | When he encounters a righteous person, he considers that person more virtuous and pious than himself. |

## Precedence and Degrees in Faith

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ The Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) teaches that if faith were uniform—without deficiency or excess—then no believer would surpass another in virtue, and all would share equally in the rewards bestowed for faith. Yet such equality does not obtain. Believers enter Paradise by virtue of perfect faith, and it is their ⟪ar:راجح⟫greater faith that grants them superiority in God's sight over others. Those whose faith bears ⟪ar:مقصرین⟫deficiency shall enter the Fire.[^cite-3]

Regarding the degrees of faith, the Imam explains: "God has appointed for the believers a competition in faith, just as horses race upon an appointed day." Each believer who was first to embrace the faith received a rank according to the precedence of acceptance. Neither does the believer who lags in faith merit preference over the earlier believer, nor is virtue accorded equally to all the virtuous. This precedence—this privilege of the first believers in the *Umma*—grants them superiority over those who believed later. ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ Thus the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him), was foremost among all the Companions in faith, and thereby held superiority over all other believers.

Had the first believers possessed no advantage over those who came after, the last believer in this *Umma* would stand on equal footing with the earliest. Yet if precedence were not made a criterion of virtue, many of the latter generation—⟪ar:ارباب فضل و کمال و زھد وتقوی⟫possessed of excellence, perfection, asceticism, and piety—would outrank the former. Many among the latter are far more assiduous in practice than their predecessors: they pray extensively, fast diligently, perform pilgrimage repeatedly, and engage abundantly in jihād and charity in God's path. Were precedence and priority among believers not the foundation of nobility and virtue, the latter would indeed surpass the former through the excess of their deeds. Yet God does not desire that one deferred in faith be placed in the rank of the first, nor that one whom He has advanced in faith's status be relegated. God has explained this precedent in these verses:

> ⟪quran 57:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَابِقُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ مَغْفِرَةٍۢ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَجَنَّةٍ عَرْضُهَا كَعَرْضِ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ أُعِدَّتْ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ ۚ ذَلِكَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ ذُو ٱلْفَضْلِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ ‎⟫
> *Race one with another in hastening towards Forgiveness from your Lord (Allah), and towards Paradise, the width whereof is as the width of heaven and earth, prepared for those who believe in Allah and His Messengers. That is the Grace of Allah which He bestows on whom He pleases. And Allah is the Owner of Great Bounty.*

> ⟪quran 56:10-11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلسَّبِقُونَ ٱلسَّبِقُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلْمُقَرَّبُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those foremost [(in Islamic Faith of Monotheism and in performing righteous deeds) in the life of this world on the very first call for to embrace Islam,] will be foremost (in Paradise). These will be those nearest to Allah.*

> ⟪quran 9:100⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلسَّبِقُونَ ٱلْأَوَّلُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ وَٱلْأَنصَارِ وَٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّبَعُوهُم بِإِحْسَنٍۢ رَّضِىَ ٱللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا۟ عَنْهُ وَأَعَدَّ لَهُمْ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى تَحْتَهَا ٱلْأَنْهَرُ خَلِدِينَ فِيهَآ أَبَدًۭا ۚ ذَلِكَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ‎⟫
> *And the first to embrace Islam of the Muhajirun (those who migrated from Makkah to Al-Madinah) and the Ansar (the citizens of Al-Madinah who helped and gave aid to the Muhajirun) and also those who followed them exactly (in Faith). Allah is well-pleased with them as they are well-pleased with Him. He has prepared for them Gardens under which rivers flow (Paradise), to dwell therein forever. That is the supreme success.*

> *(And there is also a share in this booty) for the poor emigrants, who were expelled from their homes and their property, seeking Bounties from Allah and to please Him. And helping Allah (i.e. helping His religion) and His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH). Such are indeed the truthful (to what they say);- And those who, before them, had homes (in Al-Madinah) and had adopted the Faith, love those who emigrate to them, and have no jealousy in their breasts for that which they have been given (from the booty of Banî An-Nadîr), and give them (emigrants) preference over themselves, even though they were in need of that. In this verse, God distinguishes ⟪ar:محاجرین و انصار و تابعین⟫the Emigrants, the Helpers, and the Followers through a graduated hierarchy. The ⟪ar:محاجرین اولین⟫first Emigrants are mentioned first in precedence. Then comes the ⟪ar:انصار⟫second rank—the Helpers—and then the ⟪ar:تابعین مخلصین⟫third rank of sincere Followers. This verse also presents the ⟪ar:متقدمین اہل ایمان⟫earlier people of faith seeking forgiveness, thereby illustrating the grace granted to the ancients. God thereafter mentions the virtues and excellence of His saints in the following verses:

If Allah had willed, succeeding generations would not have fought against each other, after clear Verses of Allah had come to them, but they differed - some of them believed and others disbelieved. If Allah had willed, they would not have fought against one another, but Allah does what He likes.*

And whosoever disbelieves in the Oneness of Allah and in all the other Articles of Faith [i.e. His (Allah's), Angels, His Holy Books, His Messengers, the Day of Resurrection and Al-Qadar (Divine Preordainments)], then fruitless is his work, and in the Hereafter he will be among the losers.*

The phrase ⟪ar:کفر بالایمان⟫to reject faith means to fail in following God's commandments, or to act without confirmation of the heart, or to affirm and act yet refrain from verbal profession. None of his works shall benefit him. God declares:

## Outward and Inward Faith

The reward God has promised the faithful in the Hereafter is Paradise. Reward is deferred to the Hereafter because faith is a trait that ⟪ar:خیراتِ بشریات⟫encompasses ⟪ar:فضائل الملائکۃ⟫excellence akin to the virtues of the angels. Since one cannot ascend to angelic station whilst dwelling in this world, the reward is preserved for the Hereafter and Paradise is pledged. Similarly, disbelief is a trait encompassing ⟪ar:شَرُّ البشریات⟫the worst of human corruption, and for this the Fire has been appointed as ⟪ar:وعید⟫threat. Faith comprises two branches: outward faith and inward faith.[^cite-4]

**Terminology:**
- ⟪ar:وعدہ⟫ *Waʿd* (promise): reward for righteous deeds, as in the promise of Paradise.
- ⟪ar:وعید⟫ *Waʿīd* (threat): punishment for transgression, as in the threat of Hell.

### Outward Faith

Outward faith (*islām*) refers to the verbal profession of five essentials:

| **First** | **Affirmation of Allah (God)**: The acknowledgment that there exists one Creator of the cosmos, unique, eternal, all-powerful, all-wise, and Creator of all creation. He is the Master of all creatures; no partner shares in His dominion. |
|---|---|
| **Second** | **Confession of the Angels**: These are God's noblest creations, appointed to serve and worship Him. God has made them guardians of His creation and assigned them stations of great responsibility to execute His will. These angels never disobey Allah; they perform precisely what they are commanded. |
| **Third** | **Acknowledgment of the Prophets and Messengers**: God has chosen from among His servants a select group whom He has elevated as Prophets and Messengers on earth. He has appointed angels as mediaries between Himself and these chosen servants, conveying to them His message. This communion of elect servants receives glad tidings and divine directives from the angels and transmits them to humankind. |
| **Fourth** | **Acknowledgment of the Revealed Books**: All messages that the Prophets received through revelation from the angels were presented by them to their contemporaries in their own language and idiom. Thus, one must profess belief in the **Books** revealed to all the Prophets. Though the message remains constant, vocabulary and style have adapted to the times. |
| **Fifth** | **Belief in the Resurrection**: The acknowledgment that the Day of Resurrection approaches, bringing creation anew and transforming all circumstances. On that day, all creatures shall be raised, judged according to their deeds: the righteous rewarded and the wicked punished. |

The believers are those who affirm these five matters completely. God describes them in His Book:

> ⟪quran 2:285⟫
> ⟪ar:‏آمَنَ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ ‎⟫
> *The Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) believes in what has been sent down to him from his Lord, and (so do) the believers. Each one believes in Allah, His Angels, His Books, and His Messengers. They say, "We make no distinction between one another of His Messengers" - and they say, "We hear, and we obey. (We seek) Your Forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the return (of all)."*

When a believer confesses these five realities aloud, he distinguishes himself from all Jews, Christians, and Zoroastrians. In each such confession lies an implicit denial of all other gods; by this utterance, he binds himself to prayer, fasting, pilgrimage, almsgiving, and jihād.

### Inward Faith

Inward faith (*īmān*) is the confirmation of these same five doctrines in the depths of one's heart. With such inner verification, the inward dimension of faith is established. The deeper the confirmation, the loftier the stations of inner faith—it is these believers whom God has praised in His Book as ⟪ar:یاَ اَیُّھاَ اَلَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫"O you who have faith," and to them He has promised Paradise.[^cite-5]

The paradise belonging to outward faith is distinct from that of inward faith. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) alluded to this distinction when he said: "Whoever pronounces ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫'There is no god but Allah' shall enter Paradise." Then he added: "He who pronounces ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫'There is no god but Allah' with sincerity of heart shall enter Paradise." The first group speaks the formula only with the tongue and enters the paradise of **⟪ar:اسلام⟫** submission (outward). The second group professes with both tongue and sincere heart-affirmation and enters the paradise of **⟪ar:ایمان⟫** faith (inner).

| Verbal Profession | Verbal Profession + Heartfelt Affirmation |
|---|---|
| Outer Paradise (Islam) | Inner Paradise (Faith) |

Achieving this inner faith requires a fourfold discipline:

| **Tafakkur** (Reflection) | Deep meditation and contemplation that exhausts every angle of investigation; proving to oneself the truth of what is being considered. |
|---|---|
| **Iʿtibār** (Consideration) | Comparison of one reality with another, drawing distinctions and discerning relationships between them. |
| **Qiyām bi-l-Sharāʾiṭ** (Fulfillment of Conditions) | The observance of all precepts that God has enjoined regarding this knowledge. |
| **Wājib Ḥaqquh** (Discharging Its Due) | Complete execution of all obligations that God has made binding regarding this knowledge. |

Entry into the paradise of faith is no light undertaking. God Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 3:142⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ جَهَدُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *Do you think that you will enter Paradise before Allah tests those of you who fought (in His Cause) and (also) tests those who are As-Sabirin (the patient ones, etc.)?*

## The Precedence of Faith over Knowledge

The elevation of ranks is bound to knowledge and faith, not to mere action. The degrees of knowledge differ, as do the degrees of faith. To clarify this distinction, God declares:

> ⟪quran 58:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجْلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you are told to make room in the assemblies, (spread out and) make room. Allah will give you (ample) room (from His Mercy). And when you are told to rise up [for prayers, Jihad (holy fighting in Allah's Cause), or for any other good deed], rise up. Allah will exalt in degree those of you who believe, and those who have been granted knowledge. And Allah is Well-Acquainted with what you do.*

In this verse, God affirms that He raises the ranks of those who believe and those who possess knowledge. Yet the believers are mentioned before the scholars, suggesting that the elevation of the faithful precedes that of the knowledgeable. Faith holds priority over knowledge, for knowledge is the means by which faith increases—and a thing can increase only when it already exists. In other words, faith comes first in the order of things. Even in the genesis of the world's religions, faith was created before knowledge. When the Prophet of Islam (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) first called people to the faith, he said: "Say ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫'There is no god but Allah'—and you shall prosper." He commanded confession first, which is the foundational requirement of faith. He did not demand prior knowledge of its meanings; he asked only for profession and belief. Once the people professed, the stages of deepened verification began, in which the meanings of ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫the creed became luminous and the people attained knowledge of it.[^cite-6]

**Principle:**
It is upon confession of monotheism that the spiritual realm, the First Intellect, and the knowledge of all that was and shall be are revealed to the soul. For this reason, faith takes precedence over knowledge.

God also establishes a hierarchy among the learned, declaring that over every knower stands One who knows more. The word ⟪ar:' فوق'⟫"above" in this context signifies the elevation and differentiation of ranks:

> ⟪quran 12:76⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَبَدَأَ بِأَوْعِيَتِهِمْ قَبْلَ وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَخْرَجَهَا مِن وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ۚ كَذَلِكَ كِدْنَا لِيُوسُفَ ۖ مَا كَانَ لِيَأْخُذَ أَخَاهُ فِى دِينِ ٱلْمَلِكِ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ نَرْفَعُ دَرَجَتٍۢ مَّن نَّشَآءُ ۗ وَفَوْقَ كُلِّ ذِى عِلْمٍ عَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *So he [Yusuf (Joseph)] began (the search) in their bags before the bag of his brother. Then he brought it out of his brother's bag. Thus did We plan for Yusuf (Joseph). He could not take his brother by the law of the king (as a slave), except that Allah willed it. (So Allah made the brothers to bind themselves with their way of "punishment, i.e. enslaving of a thief.") We raise to degrees whom We please, but over all those endowed with knowledge is the All-Knowing (Allah).*

### Knowledge and Faith

Knowledge is the form that the soul creates when it apprehends a reality. When the soul then confesses this apprehended form with the tongue, affirms it with the heart, and embeds it within itself through bodily action, that form is called faith. Verification is truthfulness, and truthfulness requires investigation. For instance, all of us as Muslims acknowledge the existence of Paradise, Hell, and the Bridge of Sirāt; we affirm and verify their reality. Yet since these cannot be perceived through the ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫five senses, verification demands both intellectual inquiry and rigorous research to establish their existence and reality.

| **Knowledge** | **Faith** |
|---|---|
| **Form** | **Confirmation** |
| **Perception** | **Affirmation of the Heart** |
| **Awareness** | **Verbal Acknowledgment** |
| | **Action through the Limbs** |

**Principle:**
In the soul of the scholar, the form impressed by knowledge is called ⟪ar:علم⟫knowledge, while the profession, affirmation, and practice of that knowledge is called ⟪ar:ایمان⟫faith. The essential reality is faith; knowledge serves as the means to increase that faith.

### Verification and Inquiry

Knowledge possesses a dual nature. The first is ⟪ar:علم ظاہر⟫outward knowledge, which precedes faith. It is the state of acquiring mere awareness of something through information about its reality—for example, knowing that Paradise and Hell exist. The second is ⟪ar:علم معرفت⟫cognate knowledge, which follows faith. Here, the truth is first acknowledged verbally and then affirmed with certainty. To deepen this truth requires detailed inquiry into its reality, which generates knowledge and heightens verification through research. This is the post-faith condition. The more one's knowledge of these realities grows through inquiry, the more one's affirmation deepens, and the more perfect one's faith becomes. Consider the questions: What is Paradise? What is Hell? What is the Bridge? Where do they exist? How vast are they? Who shall enter them? How long shall they dwell there? As knowledge of these particulars increases, affirmation increases correspondingly, strengthening both the knowledge itself and ⟪ar:علمی صورت⟫the intellectual form and ⟪ar:ایمان⟫faith together.[^cite-7]

| **Outward Knowledge** | **Hidden Knowledge** |
|---|---|
| **Awareness** | **Inquiry & Verification** |
| **Incomplete Faith** | **Perfect Faith** |

God has made faith—not knowledge—the criterion of success. He says:

> ⟪quran 2:2-5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ذَلِكَ ٱلْكِتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًۭى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *That book, whereof there is no doubt, a guidance to those who are Al-Muttaqun [the pious and righteous persons who fear Allah much (abstain from all kinds of sins and evil deeds which He has forbidden) and love Allah much (perform all kinds of good deeds which He has ordained)]. Who believe in the Ghaib and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and spend out of what we have provided for them [i.e. give Zakat, spend on themselves, their parents, their children, their wives, etc., and also give charity to the poor and also in Allah's Cause - Jihad, etc.]. And who believe in (the Quran and the Sunnah) which has been sent down (revealed) to you (Muhammad Peace be upon him ) and in [the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel), etc.] which were sent down before you and they believe with certainty in the Hereafter. (Resurrection, recompense of their good and bad deeds, Paradise and Hell, etc.). They are on (true) guidance from their Lord, and they are the successful.*

Here God says ⟪ar:'یؤمنون بالغیب'⟫believers in the Unseen, not ⟪ar:'یعلمون بالغیب'⟫knowers of the Unseen. That is, those who believed in all these realities attained success. Faith is always achieved through the Unseen, through knowledge of the Unseen. Faith cannot occur without affirmation, nor can affirmation occur without epistemology. In essence, the degrees of faith are built upon the rungs of knowledge.

## The Distinction Between Islam and Faith

To clarify the difference between Islam (*islām*, submission) and faith (*īmān*, belief), God declares:

> ⟪quran 49:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ قَالَتِ ٱلْأَعْرَابُ آمَنَّا ۖ قُل لَّمْ تُؤْمِنُوا۟ وَلَكِن قُولُوٓا۟ أَسْلَمْنَا وَلَمَّا يَدْخُلِ ٱلْإِيمَنُ فِى قُلُوبِكُمْ ۖ وَإِن تُطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ لَا يَلِتْكُم مِّنْ أَعْمَلِكُمْ شَيْـًٔا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهُ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌ ‎⟫
> *The bedouins say: "We believe." Say: "You believe not but you only say, 'We have surrendered (in Islam),' for Faith has not yet entered your hearts. But if you obey Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), He will not decrease anything in reward for your deeds. Verily, Allah is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful."*

> ⟪quran 49:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَمُنُّونَ عَلَيْكَ أَنْ أَسْلَمُوا۟ ۖ قُل لَّا تَمُنُّوا۟ عَلَىَّ إِسْلَمَكُم ۖ بَلِ ٱللَّهُ يَمُنُّ عَلَيْكُمْ أَن هَدَىٰكُمْ لِلْإِيمَنِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *They regard as favour upon you (O Muhammad PBUH) that they have embraced Islam. Say: "Count not your Islam as a favour upon me. Nay, but Allah has conferred a favour upon you, that He has guided you to the Faith, if you indeed are true.*

> ⟪quran 51:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَمَا وَجَدْنَا فِيهَا غَيْرَ بَيْتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *But We found not there any household of the Muslims except one [i.e. Lout (Lot) and his two daughters].*

These verses demonstrate that Islam and faith are distinct realities. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫The Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq teaches that faith indeed lies within the domain of Islam, but Islam does not encompass the domain of faith. Islam concerns the outward utterance and deeds; faith concerns the inner reality—the sincerity of the heart. When once asked to explain the true meaning of Islam and faith, ⟪ar:صادق آل محمد علیہ السلام⟫the Truthful One of the Family of Muhammad replied: "Faith pertains to the heart, while Islam pertains to the observance of the laws of marriage and inheritance."[^cite-8] Whoever enters the circle of Islam finds his bloodshed forbidden, his life protected, and his wealth and property secured.

The form may be illustrated thus: it is possible for a Muslim not to be a believer, but every believer is necessarily a Muslim first. Mawlānā ʿAlī observed that Islam is mere verbal profession, while faith requires knowledge coupled with profession of the heart. Thus, when God grants a servant knowledge of Himself, His Messenger, and His Imām, and that servant confesses with the tongue, he becomes a believer. The granting of knowledge is itself a complete proof from God and a manifestation of His grace and favor. The servant's verbal confession is itself a favor from God, bestowed upon whomsoever He wills. In other words, knowledge is the supreme favor God deposits in the heart of His servant.

Confession is an act originating in the heart—the knowledge that God has created in the heart takes form in verbal utterance.

Thus no argument can stand against one whom God has not made knowledgeable. Such a person is bound to act within the bounds of his knowledge and to refrain from what he does not know; he must bring such matters to those who do know, for God says:

> ⟪quran 21:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent not before you (O Muhammad PBUH) but men to whom We inspired, so ask the people of the Reminder [Scriptures - the Taurat (Torah), the Injeel (Gospel)] if you do not know.*

## The Minimal Conditions of Faith, Shirk, and Misguidance

When ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him), was asked, "O Commander of the Faithful! What is the lowest condition that makes one a believer, and what is the lowest condition that makes one a polytheist?"—he responded with profound clarity.

### The Lowest Condition of Faith

The Commander of the Faithful stated: "The lowest condition that makes one a believer is this: when God grants a servant the knowledge of Himself, that servant acknowledges God in obedience. When God grants him knowledge of His Messenger, he acknowledges them in obedience. When God grants him knowledge of the Proof of God on earth and His witness over His creation—the Imām—he believes in the Imāmate and acknowledges it in obedience. Such a person is a believer. If he knows of nothing else, he should obey when commanded and refrain when forbidden."

### The Lowest Condition of Polytheism

He then said: "The lowest condition that makes one a polytheist is when one treats that which God has forbidden as though it were commanded by God. Such a one imagines that God has enjoined the worship of something other than Himself. For the worship of any other than God constitutes polytheism. Therefore, such a person is reckoned a polytheist."

### The Lowest Condition of Misguidance

Finally, he said: "The lowest condition that leads one astray through its practice is when one seeks neither the Proof of God on earth nor knowledge of God's witness over creation, and follows not the Imām. One deprived of knowledge of the Imām (peace be upon him) is certainly astray."

## The True Purpose of the Islamic Invitation

The Prophet's *daʿwa* (summons/invitation) to outward Islam possessed an inner purpose: the invitation to the inward reality of faith. Yet because the inward is reached only through the outward, the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) first invited people to Islam, saying: "Pronounce ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫'There is no god but Allah'—and you shall prosper." Then, among those whom maturity of faith had ripened and whose hearts had been prepared for the *daʿwa*, he proclaimed at ⟪ar:یوم غدیر⟫Ghadīr, the Day of the Pool: "Whoever's master I am, ʿAlī is his master." This ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫utterance represents the inner *daʿwa*—the essence of faith.[^cite-9] Thus ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫"There is no god but Allah" is the word of the outer *daʿwa*, the summons to submission, while faith is the veil beneath which the inner word dwells concealed. This is indicated when God says in ⟪ar:سورۃ مزمل⟫the Sūrat al-Muzzammil (Chapter 73):

> ⟪quran 73:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(He Alone is) the Lord of the east and the west, La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He). So take Him Alone as Wakîl (Disposer of your affairs).*

The numerical value of the phrase "there is no god but He" (⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illa Huwa") totals 110, which equals the numerical value of ⟪ar:علی⟫ʿAlī. This verse thus alludes esoterically to the inner invitation: take ⟪ar:علی⟫ʿAlī as your guardian and protector.

The Messenger of Allah declared: "As long as the people of my *Umma* continue to pronounce the word ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫and fulfill all the obligations and duties of the Faith, the wrath of God will be kept far from them." Otherwise, these words shall be hurled back upon their faces, and they will be told: "You are liars! You never truly verified this word." Indeed, the fulfillment of the religious obligations is the journey from the outer ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫(the negation of all gods but One) to the inner ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫(the affirmation of God alone).

## The Paradise of Islam and the Paradise of Faith

When the ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫Originator, Most High, first spoke the word of monotheism through the ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫First Intellect, He did not say merely ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫"there is no god"—rather, He proclaimed ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫"there is no god but He":

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah bears witness that La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), and the angels, and those having knowledge (also give this witness); (He is always) maintaining His creation in Justice. La ilah illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.*

The word through which the ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫First Intellect itself was emanated and through which its excellence and elevation were accomplished is precisely this word: ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫"There is no god but He."[^cite-10] Similarly, believers cannot be prepared and perfected except through the *wilāya* (guardianship) of ⟪ar:علی⟫ʿAlī and the progeny of ⟪ar:علی⟫ʿAlī, and through belief in the inner summons. By the proclamation ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫"There is no god but Allah" of the Messenger of God, Muslims have entered ⟪ar:اسلام⟫the Paradise of Islam. The sword of the sword-bearer (the Law) was withheld from them; yet there is no guarantee of their perseverance in this creed. For they lack that ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫speaking witness—the ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫noble form—through whom constancy is achieved. It is attained only when the meaning of the inner word is established within the outer word—that is, when ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫deeds sanctioned by the Law flow forth through the ⟪ar:ولایت امیر المومنین⟫guardianship of the Commander of the Faithful, through covenant and solemnity, giving ⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫a blessed and enduring conclusion.

| **Outer Summons** | **Inner Summons** |
|---|---|
| *La ilaha illallah* | *La ilaha illa Huwa* |
| **Islam** | **Faith** |
| **Primary Perfection** | **Secondary Perfection** |

There is but one level in Islam: no Muslim stands above any other Muslim, for the Prophet declared, "All Muslims are brethren and sisters." Thus the Messenger of God established all believers on an equal footing—this is the mark of the outward. Yet within the inner dimension dwells a power that not only reinforces external adherence but elevates some believers above others. In the circle of faith exist hierarchies and degrees; not all believers are peers. Thus, while Islam knows no superiority of one over another, faith establishes a ladder upon which some believers ascend higher than others, drawing ever nearer to God in knowledge, verification, and spiritual proximity.

## The Emanation of Islam and Faith

The doctrine of emanation (al-munbaʿithāt, the Emanators) establishes a fundamental distinction between the cosmic principles underlying Islamic practice and faith itself. Those whose station constituted the highest veil—whose name was derived from ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫—brought forth ⟪ar:اسلام⟫ (Islam, the submission to Divine will).[^cite-6] ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ ⟪ar:عین⟫ (the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him, the very essence), whose hijāb (veil) occupied an inferior rank, gave birth to faith (īmān).

The numerical correspondence that emerges from this cosmic architecture proves instructive. When one adds the letters ⟪ar:میم⟫ and ⟪ar:کاف⟫ to the name ⟪ar:محمدؐ مصطفیٰ⟫ (Muḥammad, the Chosen One), the sum yields a value equal to the numerical value of ⟪ar:اسلام⟫; likewise, when these letters are added to the name ⟪ar:علی⟫ (ʿAlī), the sum corresponds precisely to the numerical value of faith. This correspondence does not arise by accident but reflects the cosmic order through which the ⟪ar:منبعثات⟫ (the Emanators) structure both the external Islamic framework and the inner spiritual reality.[^cite-7]

The Prophet ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وآلہ وسلم⟫ himself declared this distinction with clarity: "I have established ⟪ar:اسلامی ھیکل⟫ (the Islamic structure)," while ⟪ar:أمیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ proclaimed, "I have established ⟪ar:ایمانی ھیکل⟫ (the structure of faith)."[^cite-8] Islam constitutes the outer observance and the visible bonds of the community; faith represents the inner illumination and the esoteric knowledge (taʾwīl, esoteric exegesis) through which the believer draws near to the Divine. Each finds its perfection in the guidance of the Imam, who unites within his person both the legislative authority and the spiritual authority that sustains all creation.
