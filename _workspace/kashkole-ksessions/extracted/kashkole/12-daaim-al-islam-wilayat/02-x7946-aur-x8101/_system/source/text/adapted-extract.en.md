## What is Faith?

The Commander of the Faithful reports that Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) said: "Believing in Allah is the best of all deeds, and therefore its reward is greatest." He defined faith as the testimony that "there is no god but Allah, alone without partner, and Muhammad is His servant and messenger." Faith encompasses acknowledgment of Heaven as truth, Hell as truth, and the resurrection from the grave and the coming of the Day of Resurrection as reality. It includes confirming the prophets and messengers (anbiyāʾ wa-mursalīn), affirming the pure Imams (al-aʾimmat al-ṭāhirīn), obeying their commands, following the ordinances that Allah has enjoined upon His servants, and withdrawing from what He has forbidden. Obedience to the Imam and acceptance of his words constitute faith itself.[^cite-1]

Faith, according to this teaching, comprises three dimensions working in concert:

| Affirmation of the Heart | Expression through Speech | Action through the Body |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Heartfelt conviction | Words from the tongue | Bodily performance |

When asked whether affirming belief in the heart and expressing it with the tongue constitutes an *act*, the Imam replied: "Faith is an act in its entirety (al-īmān ʿamal kulluh)." Speech itself is an action—the tongue's action. Belief and the acquisition of knowledge constitute the heart's action. Acting with all bodily organs according to God's command and the Messenger's teaching constitutes the body's action. Thus, **faith is nothing other than the sum of these acts**.[^cite-2]

| The Heart's Function | The Tongue's Function | The Body's Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Practice of belief and knowledge | Practice of utterance | Actions of all limbs |

Allah Most High declares in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 2:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and do righteous good deeds, they are dwellers of Paradise, they will dwell therein forever.*

Wherever Allah mentions faith in the Qur'an, He couples it with righteous deeds. These two matters are indissolubly linked. Although faith and righteous action are conventionally distinguished—faith relating to the heart and action relating to the limbs—their combination alone makes a person a believer.[^cite-3]

The Commander of the Faithful (Amīr al-Muʾminīn) teaches, in the *Nahj al-Balāgha*, that "faith is conviction of the heart, practice of these convictions with the body, and affirmation of these convictions with the tongue." Faith originates in the heart and terminates in righteous deeds, which include confession through speech. It rises from the heart and descends through the tongue, the limbs, and the bodily members. Islam, by contrast, emerges from the utterance of the word of testimony (kalimat lā ilāh illā ʾAllāh) and reaches the limbs and members. The light of faith emanates from within and shines from without; Islam, by contrast, designates that which is outward. Islam pertains to the external prescriptions of the Sharīʿa, while faith relates to the inner meaning—the *taʾwīl* (esoteric exegesis)—of that Sharīʿa.

### The Linguistic and Theological Dimensions of Faith

The root meaning of faith (īmān) is to confirm or affirm, and in another sense, to grant security. Faith is a transitive verb, requiring both a subject and an object: one who affirms (*muṣaddiq*) and that which is affirmed. Even when faith means "to grant security," an object is necessary. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) united both meanings in a single tradition: "The believer is he who grants his neighbor security from his harm." It is owing to these two senses that one is called a believer (muʾmin). The term derives as well from Allah's name al-Muʾmin (the Grantor of Security): a person is called a believer because of his affirmation of Allah's promise and threat (waʿd wa-waʿīd), and Allah has granted him security from His fatal punishment.[^cite-4]

| Agent | Verbal Action | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Believer | Affirmation | of Allah and His Messenger |
| Believer | Granting Security | to creatures |
| Allah | Granting Security | from doom |

## Faith of the Heart, Tongue, and Body

The Imam said: "Allah has made faith obligatory and binding upon every organ of the human being (ibn Ādam) and has apportioned it among them. Not a single one among the twelve organs of the human being is relieved of the obligation to practice faith; the faith incumbent upon each organ differs from that incumbent upon others." Allah has distributed faith among the following organs of the human body:

| Heart | Tongue | Two Ears | Two Eyes | Two Hands | Two Feet | Face | Private Parts |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 1 | 1 |

### Faith of the Heart

The heart, from which a person derives understanding and discernment, is the sovereign of his entire being. No organ may take in or expel anything from the body without its opinion and command. The faith that Allah has enjoined upon the heart comprises confession, knowledge, belief, acceptance, and acknowledgment. The believer must confess in his heart that Allah is One, that there is no god but Him. He is alone and unique (waḥdahu lā sharīk lahu), without consort or offspring. Muhammad, the Chosen One (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him), and the Imams are the servants of Allah and His true messengers. After this confession, the heart must acknowledge all that Allah has sent down—the Book and the Prophet. Following this acknowledgment, the heart must acquire the knowledge of the limits (ʿilm al-ḥudūd), which strengthens faith. Thus, this is the practical and scientific faith that Allah has enjoined upon the heart. The following verses indicate this teaching:

> ⟪quran 16:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَن كَفَرَ بِٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ إِيمَنِهِۦٓ إِلَّا مَنْ أُكْرِهَ وَقَلْبُهُۥ مُطْمَىِٕنٌّۢ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ وَلَكِن مَّن شَرَحَ بِٱلْكُفْرِ صَدْرًۭا فَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Whoever disbelieved in Allah after his belief, except him who is forced thereto and whose heart is at rest with Faith but such as open their breasts to disbelief, on them is wrath from Allah, and theirs will be a great torment.*

> ⟪quran 2:284⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لِّلَّهِ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَإِن تُبْدُوا۟ مَا فِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ أَوْ تُخْفُوهُ يُحَاسِبْكُم بِهِ ٱللَّهُ ۖ فَيَغْفِرُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَن يَشَآءُ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌ ‎⟫
> *To Allah belongs all that is in the heavens and all that is on the earth, and whether you disclose what is in your own selves or conceal it, Allah will call you to account for it. Then He forgives whom He wills and punishes whom He wills. And Allah is Able to do all things.*

> ⟪quran 5:41⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ لَا يَحْزُنكَ ٱلَّذِينَ يُسَرِعُونَ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ آمَنَّا بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَلَمْ تُؤْمِن قُلُوبُهُمْ ۛ وَمِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَادُوا۟ ۛ سَمَّعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ سَمَّعُونَ لِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ لَمْ يَأْتُوكَ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ مَوَاضِعِهِۦ ۖ يَقُولُونَ إِنْ أُوتِيتُمْ هَذَا فَخُذُوهُ وَإِن لَّمْ تُؤْتَوْهُ فَٱحْذَرُوا۟ ۚ وَمَن يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ فِتْنَتَهُۥ فَلَن تَمْلِكَ لَهُۥ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ شَيْـًٔا ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَمْ يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ أَن يُطَهِّرَ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا خِزْىٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O Messenger (Muhammad PBUH)! Let not those who hurry to fall into disbelief grieve you, of such who say: "We believe" with their mouths but their hearts have no faith.*

> ⟪quran 13:28⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَتَطْمَىِٕنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ تَطْمَىِٕنُّ ٱلْقُلُوبُ ‎⟫
> *Those who believe (in the Oneness of Allah - Islamic Monotheism), and whose hearts find rest in the remembrance of Allah, Verily, in the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest.*

### Faith of the Tongue

The faith that Allah has enjoined upon the tongue concerns the utterance of words and meanings—that is, expressing in speech the confession and belief that the heart has embraced. As Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 3:84⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْنَا وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَإِسْمَعِيلَ وَإِسْحَقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَٱلْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَآ أُوتِىَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَٱلنَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُۥ مُسْلِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH): "We believe in Allah and in what has been sent down to us, and what was sent down to Ibrahim (Abraham), Isma'il (Ishmael), Ishaque (Isaac), Ya'qub (Jacob) and Al-Asbat [the twelve sons of Ya'qub (Jacob)] and what was given to Musa (Moses), 'Iesa (Jesus) and the Prophets from their Lord. We make no distinction between one another among them and to Him (Allah) we have submitted (in Islam)." *

> ⟪quran 33:70⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَقُولُوا۟ قَوْلًۭا سَدِيدًۭا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Keep your duty to Allah and fear Him, and speak (always) the truth.*

> ⟪quran 18:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيُؤْمِن وَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيَكْفُرْ ۚ إِنَّآ أَعْتَدْنَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ نَارًا أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ سُرَادِقُهَا ۚ وَإِن يَسْتَغِيثُوا۟ يُغَاثُوا۟ بِمَآءٍۢ كَٱلْمُهْلِ يَشْوِى ٱلْوُجُوهَ ۚ بِئْسَ ٱلشَّرَابُ وَسَآءَتْ مُرْتَفَقًا ‎⟫
> *And say: "The truth is from your Lord." Then whosoever wills, let him believe, and whosoever wills, let him disbelieve.*

### Faith of the Two Ears

The ears bear the responsibility of hearkening to God's command and attending to it with full presence. One must not listen to that which is forbidden or which Allah dislikes. Regarding this, He declares:

> ⟪quran 4:140⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنْ إِذَا سَمِعْتُمْ آيَتِ ٱللَّهِ يُكْفَرُ بِهَا وَيُسْتَهْزَأُ بِهَا فَلَا تَقْعُدُوا۟ مَعَهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّكُمْ إِذًۭا مِّثْلُهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهُ جَامِعُ ٱلْمُنَفِقِينَ وَٱلْكَفِرِينَ فِى جَهَنَّمَ جَمِيعًا ‎⟫
> *And it has already been revealed to you in the Book (this Quran) that when you hear the Verses of Allah being denied and mocked at, then sit not with them, until they engage in a talk other than that.*

> ⟪quran 6:68⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخُوضُونَ فِىٓ آيَتِنَا فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦ ۚ وَإِمَّا يُنسِيَنَّكَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَلَا تَقْعُدْ بَعْدَ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And when you (Muhammad PBUH) see those who engage in a false conversation about Our Verses (of the Quran) by mocking at them, stay away from them till they turn to another topic.*

> ⟪quran 39:18⟫
> ⟪ar:فَبَشِّرْ عِبَادِ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَمِعُونَ ٱلْقَوْلَ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ أَحْسَنَهُۥٓ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمْ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *Those who listen to the Word [good advice La ilaha ill-Allah (none has the right to be worshipped but Allah) and Islamic Monotheism, etc.] and follow the best thereof (i.e. worship Allah Alone, repent to Him and avoid Taghut, etc.) those are (the ones) whom Allah has guided.*

> ⟪quran 23:1-4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَدْ أَفْلَحَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ فِى صَلَاتِهِمْ خَشِعُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ ٱللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَوٰةِ فَعِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *Successful indeed are the believers. Those who offer their Salat (prayers) with all solemnity and full submissiveness. And those who turn away from Al-Laghw (dirty, false, evil vain talk, falsehood, and all that Allah has forbidden). And those who pay the Zakat.*

### Faith of the Two Eyes

Allah has enjoined upon the eyes the duty to refrain from gazing upon that which is forbidden, and to lower one's gaze from things that Allah has interdicted. This restraint enters into faith and constitutes the work of the eyes, as explained in this verse:

> ⟪quran 24:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهُ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Tell the believing men to lower their gaze (from looking at forbidden things), and protect their private parts (from illegal sexual acts, etc.). That is purer for them. Verily, Allah is All-Aware of what they do.*

The meaning of this verse is that the believing man must not gaze upon the private parts of another and must guard his own private parts from the sight of others. Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq taught that all the verses of the Holy Qur'an concerning the protection of private parts aim at guarding them from fornication. Yet the aforementioned verse, concerning the lowering of the gaze (ghaḍḍ al-baṣar), signifies that one must not look upon one's own private parts. Allah joins the duties of the heart, tongue, ear, and eye in a single ordinance:

> ⟪quran 17:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْبَصَرَ وَٱلْفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَانَ عَنْهُ مَسْـُٔولًۭا ‎⟫
> *And follow not (O man i.e., say not, or do not or witness not, etc.) that of which you have no knowledge (e.g. one's saying: "I have seen," while in fact he has not seen, or "I have heard," while he has not heard). Verily! The hearing, and the sight, and the heart, of each of those you will be questioned (by Allah).*

> ⟪quran 41:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَن يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَآ أَبْصَرُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَكِن ظَنَنتُمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And you have not been hiding against yourselves, lest your ears, and your eyes, and your skins testify against you, but you thought that Allah knew not much of what you were doing.*

### Faith of the Two Hands

Allah has enjoined upon the two hands that they be employed only in what is permitted, dedicating them instead to charity, kindness, kinship, striving in God's way (jihād), and ritual ablution, as He declares in this verse:

> ⟪quran 5:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ فَٱغْسِلُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْمَرَافِقِ وَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِرُئُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُمْ جُنُبًۭا فَٱطَّهَّرُوا۟ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَآءَ أَحَدٌۭ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَمَسْتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا۟ مَآءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا۟ صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا فَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُم مِّنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُم مِّنْ حَرَجٍۢ وَلَكِن يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you intend to offer As-Salat (the prayer), wash your faces and your hands (forearms) up to the elbows, rub (by passing wet hands over) your heads, and your feet up to ankles. If you are in a state of Janaba (i.e. had a sexual discharge), purify yourself (bathe your whole body).*

> ⟪quran 8:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ زَحْفًۭا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلْأَدْبَارَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you meet those who disbelieve, in a battle-field, never turn your backs to them.*

> ⟪quran 47:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ فَضَرْبَ ٱلرِّقَابِ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَثْخَنتُمُوهُمْ فَشُدُّوا۟ ٱلْوَثَاقَ فَإِمَّا مَنًّۢا بَعْدُ وَإِمَّا فِدَآءً حَتَّىٰ تَضَعَ ٱلْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا ۚ ذَلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَآءُ ٱللَّهُ لَٱنتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ وَلَكِن لِّيَبْلُوَا۟ بَعْضَكُم بِبَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱلَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ فَلَن يُضِلَّ أَعْمَلَهُمْ ‎⟫
> *So, when you meet (in fight Jihad in Allah's Cause), those who disbelieve smite at their necks till when you have killed and wounded many of them, then bind a bond firmly (on them, i.e. take them as captives).*

Striking at the neck on the battlefield is therefore a duty Allah has made obligatory, for it is the work of both hands and constitutes a dimension of faith.

### Faith of the Two Feet

Allah has made it incumbent upon both feet to walk in the path of obedience. One must not walk these feet toward evil deeds that constitute disobedience to Allah; rather, one must walk them toward God's command and pleasure. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 17:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَمْشِ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا ‎⟫
> *And walk not on the earth with conceit and arrogance. Verily, you can neither rend nor penetrate the earth, nor can you attain a stature like the mountains in height.*

> ⟪quran 31:19⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱقْصِدْ فِى مَشْيِكَ وَٱغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلْأَصْوَتِ لَصَوْتُ ٱلْحَمِيرِ ‎⟫
> *"And be moderate (or show no insolence) in your walking, and lower your voice. Verily, the harshest of all voices is the voice (braying) of the ass."*

> ⟪quran 62:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا نُودِىَ لِلصَّلَوٰةِ مِن يَوْمِ ٱلْجُمُعَةِ فَٱسْعَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe (Muslims)! When the call is proclaimed for the Salat (prayer) on the day of Friday (Jumu'ah prayer), come to the remembrance of Allah [Jumu'ah religious talk (Khutbah) and Salat (prayer)] and leave off business (and every other thing), that is better for you if you did but know!*

> ⟪quran 22:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ لْيَقْضُوا۟ تَفَثَهُمْ وَلْيُوفُوا۟ نُذُورَهُمْ وَلْيَطَّوَّفُوا۟ بِٱلْبَيْتِ ٱلْعَتِيقِ ‎⟫
> *Then let them complete the prescribed duties (Manasik of Hajj) for them, and perform their vows, and circumambulate the Ancient House (the Ka'bah at Makkah).*

On the Day of Resurrection, Allah will cause the hands and feet to bear witness against themselves and their possessor, as He declares:

> ⟪quran 36:65⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ نَخْتِمُ عَلَىٰٓ أَفْوَهِهِمْ وَتُكَلِّمُنَآ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَتَشْهَدُ أَرْجُلُهُم بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
> *This Day, We shall seal up their mouths, and their hands will speak to Us, and their legs will bear witness to what they used to earn.*

### Faith of the Face

Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 22:77⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱرْكَعُوا۟ وَٱسْجُدُوا۟ وَٱعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ وَٱفْعَلُوا۟ ٱلْخَيْرَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Bow down, and prostrate yourselves, and worship your Lord and do good that you may be successful.*

And in another verse:

> ⟪quran 72:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنَّ ٱلْمَسَجِدَ لِلَّهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا۟ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ أَحَدًۭا ‎⟫
> *And the mosques are for Allah (Alone), so invoke not anyone along with Allah.*

Allah has thus ordained the purification of the limbs and the performance of prayer. In the Qur'an, He describes prayer itself as faith. When the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) received the command to change the direction of prayer from Jerusalem (Bayt al-Maqdis) to Mecca (Bayt al-Ḥarām), the people asked: "O Messenger of Allah, what of our prayers that we have already offered?" The Most High answered:

> ⟪quran 2:143⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهُ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Thus We have made you [true Muslims - real believers of Islamic Monotheism, true followers of Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Sunnah (legal ways)], a Wasat (just) (and the best) nation, that you be witnesses over mankind and the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) be a witness over you. And We made the Qiblah (prayer direction towards Jerusalem) which you used to face, only to test those who followed the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from those who would turn on their heels (i.e. disobey the Messenger). Indeed it was great (heavy) except for those whom Allah guided. And Allah would never make your faith (prayers) to be lost (i.e. your prayers offered towards Jerusalem).*

**In this verse, Allah designates prayer itself as faith.**[^cite-5]

The believer who departs from this world with his limbs guarded from sin and each organ having performed its duty with excellence will receive perfect faith from Allah and will enter Paradise. Conversely, whoever betrays Allah in the slightest degree or transgresses His commands through his organs will come before Allah in a state of deficient faith. Whoever takes upon himself the covenant of Allah and the Imam of the Time yet fails to act or acquire knowledge will not be called a believer. Such mere verbal affirmation of faith will avail him nothing; he will have no standing in the sight of Allah and the Imam. Rather, such a person will be reckoned outside the circle of the Imam of the Time.

## Three Types of Faith and Believers

The Noble Qur'an speaks of three categories of faith:

| Pure and Perfect Faith | Deficient Faith | Faith Tainted by Polytheism |
| --- | --- | --- |
| *Īmān kāmil aḥsan* | *Īmān nāqiṣ* | *Īmān mushōbah bi-l-shirk* |

### Pure and Perfect Faith

Those possessed of pure faith are they whom Allah Himself commends and grants glad tidings of Paradise. These are the people who realize the two pillars of faith—verification (taṣdīq) and secure trust (amān). They possess the station of the righteous: although their bodies inhabit this world of falsehood and illusion, their souls remain in the realm of truth and purity. Though ostensibly present in the material world, they keep their gaze upon the Hereafter; ordinary Muslims, by contrast, keep their concern fixed upon the Hereafter while inhabiting the present world.

Amān comes upon them in the sense that when they arrive at Paradise—the House of Peace—they are freed from all the afflictions that befall bodies after death. For them, death becomes a bearer of glad tidings and tranquility, for through death they are wholly liberated from the abode of turbidity and darkness and saved forever. For this reason, the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: "Death is good news for the believer" (al-mawt rīḥānat al-muʾmin). Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, pointing to his throat, said: "When the soul of my believer reaches this point, it rejoices!"[^cite-6]

The Commander of the Faithful teaches that obedience to the pure Imams and the Guardians of the Order (walī al-amr) is not a matter of constraint; rather, the heart is drawn to it naturally. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) declared: "Those to whom the address in the Qur'an is directed—'O you who believe'—are the leaders and princes; all others who bear this address belong to the descendants of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib. This leadership and guardianship shall remain in the hands of ʿAlī's descendants as long as those addressed by this title endure." Since the address "O you who believe" encompasses all believers, past and future, and since the Qur'an's authority extends continuously, this religious guardianship (wilāya) will persist over all believers until the Day of Judgment.[^cite-7] Whoever disobeys the Imams disobeys Allah and His Messenger and is reckoned among those who rebel against God's command. When such a person dies, he dies in a state of ignorance (jāhilīya). Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority. (And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day. That is better and more suitable for final determination.*

### Deficient Faith

The second type of affirmation and belief in Allah and His Messenger is that of incomplete or deficient faith. These are those newly entered into the summons of faith (daʿwat al-īmānīya), who are counted among the respondents (mustajībūn) but remain ignorant of religious knowledge. They resemble a newborn child who, though possessing all the physical organs of humanity, cannot act independently without continuous supervision and guidance. Lacking power and capacity, the child is helpless. Similarly, the respondent in the early stages of the summons is called a believer but remains dependent upon the spiritual training and mentorship of his elder at each step. Allah describes this defective state of faith in the verse:

> ⟪quran 4:136⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ آمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ مِن قَقْبُ ۚ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَلًۢا بَعِيدًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Believe in Allah, and His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and the Book (the Quran) which He has sent down to His Messenger, and the Scripture which He sent down to those before (him), and whosoever disbelieves in Allah, His Angels, His Books, His Messengers, and the Last Day, then indeed he has strayed far away.*

Here Allah addresses: "O you who believe, believe in Allah and His Messenger!" If those to whom this address is directed were already perfect believers, then to command them further to believe would be redundant. Yet Allah testifies to their belief by saying "O believers," then adds the command "Believe." This apparent contradiction resolves when we understand that the believers addressed are of a lower station whose faith remains incomplete. They are being told: "Deepen your faith. You have entered the circle of belief and received religious birth; now progress further in religious knowledge and ascend to perfect faith. Remain in the company of the righteous—your spiritual parents—and benefit from their guidance, so that you may reach the station of perfection and dwell in the abode of truth and security." Just as children accept the training and education of their parents and teachers to attain their station of maturity. Allah illustrates this principle:

> ⟪quran 52:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and whose offspring follow them in Faith, to them shall We join their offspring, and We shall not decrease the reward of their deeds in anything. Every person is a pledge for that which he has earned.*

Here "offspring" (dhurrīya) means spiritual or causal descendants, not biological lineage. That is, believers of lower rank who followed the summons of those of higher rank are thus joined with their spiritual elders and elevated to their station.[^cite-8]

### Faith Tainted by Polytheism

This is faith that has been corrupted by the admixture of associating partners with Allah. It bears no true relationship to genuine faith in Allah and His Messenger. True Islamic faith requires accepting the commands that the Messenger of Allah conveyed through divine revelation without alteration, in their original state, without hesitation or equivocation—for they constitute the purification of our souls and the salvation of our spirits.

Allah commands us: "Take what the Messenger gives you, and refrain from what he forbids you." One must not inject one's own opinion into the commands and obedience of the Messenger in even the slightest degree. The person who directly adheres to the Prophet's guidance in worship, in permissible and prohibited matters, and in all religious affairs, without admitting personal opinion or conjecture, is a perfect believer. But whoever blends his desires with the Messenger's commands and employs opinion, speculation, and independent reasoning (ijtihād) to satisfy his appetites is no believer but an innovator. Having associated his opinion with Allah's command, his faith becomes tainted with polytheism. This taint leads him to error instead of guidance and to ruin instead of salvation. His good deeds, no matter how numerous his prayers or extensive his fasting, will bring him no benefit.[^cite-9]

Consider the revelation sent down to the Messenger as water descending from the sky. Water remains drinkable and purifying only so long as it remains uncontaminated. Once impurity overwhelms it, that water is useless to a Muslim, despite still being called water. The impurity renders it unfit for drinking and for purification. Similarly, when the desires of the self are mixed with the ordinances of revelation, pure religion ceases to exist and innovation takes its place. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: "Every innovation is a deviation; no innovation, however good it may appear, will ever be straight."[^cite-10] Even after innovation is introduced, every external aspect of Islam remains in its place. For instance, both the Imams and their opponents perform prayer; yet the difference between the two is manifest. The prayers of the opponents are destroyed upon their faces because they have mixed the bitterness of associationism and the impurity of innovation into their faith. In condemnation of such people, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 12:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا يُؤْمِنُ أَكْثَرُهُم بِٱللَّهِ إِلَّا وَهُم مُّشْرِكُونَ ‎⟫
> *And most of them believe not in Allah except that they attribute partners unto Him.*

#### Zakat Among the Polytheists

It is related that Imam Muḥammad al-Bāqir (peace be upon him) once asked Jābir ibn ʿAbdullāh al-Anṣārī: "Has Allah made the alms tax (zakāt) obligatory upon the polytheists?" Jābir replied: "No. Allah has made zakāt obligatory only upon Muslims." The Imam responded: "Have you not heard the words of Allah:

> ⟪quran 41:6-7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أَنَا۠ بَشَرٌۭ مِّثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ أَنَّمَآ إِلَهُكُمْ إِلَهٌۭ وَحِدٌۭ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُشْرِكِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَفِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH): "I am only a human being like you. It is inspired in me that your Ilah (God) is One Ilah (God - Allah), therefore take Straight Path to Him (with true Faith Islamic Monotheism) and obedience to Him, and seek forgiveness of Him. And woe to Al-Mushrikun (the disbelievers in the Oneness of Allah, polytheists, idolaters, etc.). Those who give not the Zakat and they are disbelievers in the Hereafter."*

Jābir said: "When I heard these verses from the blessed tongue of the Imam, it was as if I had never read them before, though they are in the Book of Allah." The Imam continued: "This verse addresses those who have been united to the walīs through the guardianship (wilāya) and have given their alms to someone who has set himself against Allah and His Messenger." This verse thus designates even those Muslims who withhold the alms tax as polytheists![^cite-11]

## The Eight Grades of Faith

Just as Paradise possesses eight gates, faith encompasses eight grades:

| Grade | Category |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Kindness to parents—this is the highest grade of faith. |
| 2 | Doing favors to relatives. |
| 3 | Treating orphans with kindness. |
| 4 | Kindness to the poor. |
| 5 | A neighbor who is both a kinsman and a believer. |
| 6 | A neighbor of the threshold (jār al-janab)—a believing neighbor without kinship. |
| 7 | A neighbor with whom one shares neither lineage nor faith. |
| 8 | Kindness to travelers and servants. |

## Characteristics of a Believer

A believer does not achieve true faith until his reason (ʿaql) becomes perfected. Perfection of reason cannot come about except through the cultivation of ten qualities:

| Characteristic | Description |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | From the people of the world, he takes only good. |
| 2 | He harbors fear of no evil from anyone. |
| 3 | He deems even many of his own good deeds inferior to the few good deeds of others. |
| 4 | He views a little of his own evil as much, and much of others' evil as little. |
| 5 | He never tires of asking for what people need. |
| 6 | He never wearies of acquiring knowledge and continues learning throughout his life. |
| 7 | Patience with hardship far exceeds glory and honor for him. |
| 8 | Poverty is more precious to him than wealth—as the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said, "My poverty is my pride" (faqrī fakhri). |
| 9 | A little food suffices him. |
| 10 | When he encounters a righteous person, he considers that person more virtuous and devout than himself. |

## Precedence and Degrees in Faith

Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) teaches that if faith were uniform, with neither deficiency nor excess, no believer would surpass another, and all would share equally in the rewards bestowed for faith. Yet the reality is otherwise. The believers shall enter Paradise through the perfection of their faith, and it is precisely the superiority of some believers' faith over others that grants them precedence in God's sight. Those whose faith is deficient shall enter the Fire.[^cite-10]

Regarding the degrees of faith, the Imam explains: "God has made a competition among believers to lead one another in faith, just as horses race upon a given day." Thus, those who believed first received ranks according to their precedence. The believer who lags in faith receives no precedence over the earlier believer, nor does virtue grant superiority over virtue itself. This precedence constitutes a distinct virtue—the first believers of the *Umma* (community) possess superiority over those who believed later. The Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him), was the foremost of all the Companions to embrace faith, and therefore held superiority over all other believers.[^cite-11] Had the first believers possessed no advantage over the later ones, the last believer would stand equal to the first. Were precedence not established as a criterion of virtue, many latter-day believers—who excel in prayer, fasting, pilgrimage, and striving in God's path—would outrank the ancients through sheer abundance of deeds. Yet God wills not that the one placed last in the ranks of faith should be elevated to the rank of the first, nor that those He has deferred in faith should be advanced, nor that those He has advanced should be deferred.

The Qurʾān itself establishes this principle clearly:

> ⟪quran 57:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَابِقُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ مَغْفِرَةٍۢ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَجَنَّةٍ عَرْضُهَا كَعَرْضِ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ أُعِدَّتْ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ ۚ ذَلِكَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ ذُو ٱلْفَضْلِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ ‎⟫
> *Race one with another in hastening towards Forgiveness from your Lord (Allah), and towards Paradise, the width whereof is as the width of heaven and earth, prepared for those who believe in Allah and His Messengers. That is the Grace of Allah which He bestows on whom He pleases. And Allah is the Owner of Great Bounty.*

> ⟪quran 56:10-11⟫

Should a believer from among the first undermine his faith through defect or transgression, his primacy alone shall not avail him. The Almighty declares:

> ⟪quran 5:5⟫

> *Made lawful to you this day are At-Tayyibat [all kinds of Halal (lawful) foods, which Allah has made lawful (meat of slaughtered eatable animals, etc., milk products, fats, vegetables and fruits, etc.). The food (slaughtered cattle, eatable animals, etc.) of the people of the Scripture (Jews and Christians) is lawful to you and yours is lawful to them. (Lawful to you in marriage) are chaste women from the believers and chaste women from those who were given the Scripture (Jews and Christians) before your time, when you have given their due Mahr (bridal money given by the husband to his wife at the time of marriage), desiring chastity (i.e. taking them in legal wedlock) not committing illegal sexual intercourse, nor taking them as girl-friends. And whosoever disbelieves in the Oneness of Allah and in all the other Articles of Faith [i.e. His (Allah's), Angels, His Holy Books, His Messengers, the Day of Resurrection and Al-Qadar (Divine Preordainments)], then fruitless is his work, and in the Hereafter he will be among the losers.*

To reject faith (kafara bi-l-īmān) does not merely mean to refuse the divine commandments; it signifies either failure to confirm truth with the heart while professing with the tongue, or to profess and act while harboring disbelief in the heart. Faith rests upon the triple foundation of belief, word, and deed. Without all three, faith cannot exist. When belief, affirmation of the heart, verbal profession, and action unite, faith reaches perfection.[^cite-14] Conversely, he who confesses with tongue and performs outward deeds while his heart holds fast to falsehood is no believer, however earnest his labors. His works avail him nothing. God declares:

> *And We shall turn to whatever deeds they (disbelievers, polytheists, sinners, etc.) did, and We shall make such deeds as scattered floating particles of dust.*

> *Some faces, that Day, will be humiliated (in the Hell-fire, i.e. the faces of all disbelievers, Jews and Christians, etc.). Labouring (hard in the worldly life by worshipping others besides Allah), weary (in the Hereafter with humility and disgrace). They will enter in the hot blazing Fire,*

## Outward and Inner Faith

The reward that God has promised for faith in the Hereafter is Paradise. The reason why the Hereafter becomes the proper locus of reward is that faith encompasses all human excellence (al-khayrat al-bashariyyat, the goods of humanity)—virtues that elevate one toward the angelic realm. Since one cannot attain the station of the angels whilst dwelling in this world, God has reserved the reward in the world to come and promised Paradise. Likewise, disbelief is a trait that encompasses all human evil, and for this God has threatened the Fire. Faith comprises two dimensions: the outward and the inner.

Faith divides into two branches, each with distinct characteristics and consequences. The outward (*islām*) pertains to external utterance and deed; the inner (*īmān*) relates to the sincerity of the heart. The distinction between promise (*waʿd*)—the reward for righteous deeds such as Paradise—and threat (*waʿīd*)—the punishment for evil such as Hell—illuminates this separation.

### Outward Faith

Outward faith refers to the verbal confession of five essential matters:

| **1. Affirmation of God** | One affirms that a single Creator fashioned this world—One, Eternal, Mighty, and All-Wise. He alone is Master of all creation and admits of no partner in His governance. |
| --- | --- |
| **2. Acknowledgment of the Angels** | One confesses belief in the angels, the noblest of God's creatures. God has appointed them to His service and worship, made them guardians of His creation, and entrusted them with vital functions in executing His will. These angels never disobey God and always obey His commands. |
| **3. Belief in the Prophets and Messengers** | One acknowledges that God has chosen certain servants upon His earth—the Prophets and Messengers—and has appointed the angels as intermediaries between Himself and these chosen ones. The angels convey the Lord's message to these elect servants, who in turn communicate it to all creation. |
| **4. Acceptance of the Revealed Books** | One confesses the Books revealed by God to all the Prophets. While the message remains constant, its language and style have adapted to the times and understanding of each community. |
| **5. Conviction in the Day of Resurrection** | One believes that the Resurrection shall come to bring forth a new creation. All creatures shall be raised, judged according to their deeds—the righteous rewarded, the wicked punished. |

The believers are those who affirm these five principles completely, as God states:

> ⟪quran 2:285⟫
> ⟪ar:‏آمَنَ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ ‎⟫
> *The Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) believes in what has been sent down to him from his Lord, and (so do) the believers. Each one believes in Allah, His Angels, His Books, and His Messengers. They say, "We make no distinction between one another of His Messengers" - and they say, "We hear, and we obey. (We seek) Your Forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the return (of all)."*

When a believer professes these five truths aloud, he separates himself from all who follow Judaism, Christianity, and Zoroastrianism. In every profession lies an implicit denial of all beside God; through this confession, the believer binds himself to the performance of prayer, fasting, pilgrimage, almsgiving, and striving in God's path.

### Inner Faith

Inner faith consists in affirming—from the depths of one's heart—the five principles one has confessed with the tongue. This heartfelt confirmation establishes the inner dimension of the believer's faith. The greater the sincerity of this confirmation, the higher the degrees of inner faith one attains. God has praised such believers in His Book and promised them Paradise.[^cite-15]

The Paradise of outward faith and the Paradise of inner faith are distinct. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him and his family) alluded to this distinction, saying: "Whoever utters *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* has entered Paradise." Then he added: "Whoever utters *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* with sincerity has entered Paradise." The first group—those who speak the shahāda with the tongue alone—enters the Paradise of *islām* (outward submission). The second group—those who confess with tongue and heart alike, achieving sincere affirmation—enters the Paradise of *īmān* (inner faith).[^cite-16]

To attain inner faith, four conditions must be fulfilled:

| **Reflection** (*tafkīr*) | The act of thinking deeply and contemplating with utmost concentration; investigating the object of knowledge and establishing its truth within oneself. |
| --- | --- |
| **Consideration** (*iʿtibār*) | Comparing one matter to another, perceiving their differences and relationships. |
| **Fulfilling the Conditions** (*qiyām bi-sharāʾiṭihā*) | Meeting all the obligations that knowledge entails; performing what God has commanded regarding it. |
| **Fulfilling Its Due** (*wājib ḥaqquhā*) | Giving full account to the knowledge God has made obligatory. |

Entry into the Paradise of faith is not easily achieved. God Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 3:142⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ جَهَدُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *Do you think that you will enter Paradise before Allah tests those of you who fought (in His Cause) and (also) tests those who are As-Sabirin (the patient ones, etc.)?*

## Faith Takes Precedence Over Knowledge

The elevation of ranks is bound to knowledge and faith rather than action alone. The degrees of knowledge differ, as do the degrees of faith. God clarifies this distinction:

> ⟪quran 58:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجْلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you are told to make room in the assemblies, (spread out and) make room. Allah will give you (ample) room (from His Mercy). And when you are told to rise up [for prayers, Jihad (holy fighting in Allah's Cause), or for any other good deed], rise up. Allah will exalt in degree those of you who believe, and those who have been granted knowledge. And Allah is Well-Acquainted with what you do.*

This verse states that God raises the ranks of both the believers and those possessed of knowledge. Yet believers are mentioned before the scholars—a textual ordering that indicates the precedence of the believers' elevation over that of those who know. Faith holds priority over knowledge, for knowledge serves as the means by which faith increases. What cannot exist cannot increase; thus faith must precede in existence and in rank. Moreover, in the order of creation, faith was established before knowledge. When the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) called people to Islam, he commanded: "Say *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* and you shall prosper." He did not require detailed knowledge of the statement; he asked only for confession and belief in it. Only when people had made this confession did the stages of deeper understanding commence, wherein knowledge of *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* became manifest, and people were brought to recognize its truth.[^cite-17]

The spiritual realm itself provides a reality principle: the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal, the principal emanation of intellection from the Originator, Most High) received knowledge of all that was and shall be only after the act of confession to monotheism was granted to it. Thus faith precedes knowledge in ontological order and in hierarchical rank.

The Qurʾān further elucidates the hierarchy among the learned:

> ⟪quran 12:76⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَبَدَأَ بِأَوْعِيَتِهِمْ قَبْلَ وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَخْرَجَهَا مِن وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ۚ كَذَلِكَ كِدْنَا لِيُوسُفَ ۖ مَا كَانَ لِيَأْخُذَ أَخَاهُ فِى دِينِ ٱلْمَلِكِ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ نَرْفَعُ دَرَجَتٍۢ مَّن نَّشَآءُ ۗ وَفَوْقَ كُلِّ ذِى عِلْمٍ عَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *So he [Yusuf (Joseph)] began (the search) in their bags before the bag of his brother. Then he brought it out of his brother's bag. Thus did We plan for Yusuf (Joseph). He could not take his brother by the law of the king (as a slave), except that Allah willed it. (So Allah made the brothers to bind themselves with their way of "punishment, i.e. enslaving of a thief.") We raise to degrees whom We please, but over all those endowed with knowledge is the All-Knowing (Allah).*

### Knowledge and Faith

Knowledge is the form or concept that the soul receives through cognition of reality. When the soul professes this concept with the tongue, affirms it with the heart, and embeds it within itself through bodily action, that concept becomes what is termed faith. Truth-verification (taṣdīq) requires investigation and cannot occur without rational inquiry. All of us, as believers, know of Paradise, Hell, and the Bridge of Sīrāṭ; we confirm their existence and believe in them. Yet since these are not perceived by the five senses (al-ḥawāss al-khamsah), we require verification through investigation to establish their reality.

The relationship between knowledge and faith may be represented thus:

| **Knowledge** | **Faith** |
| --- | --- |
| Perception | Heartfelt confirmation |
| Awareness | Verbal acknowledgment |
| Conception | Physical embodiment |

In the scholar's soul, the form of knowledge is termed *ʿilm* (knowledge); the profession, confirmation, and enactment of that knowledge is called *īmān* (faith). Faith itself is the reality; knowledge is the means by which faith grows and deepens.

### Verification and Research

Knowledge possesses two conditions. The first—that which precedes faith—is called manifest knowledge (ʿilm ẓāhir). It consists of acquiring awareness through information about something or a reality; for instance, the knowledge that Paradise and Hell exist. The second condition—that which follows upon faith—involves first affirming with the tongue that Paradise and Hell are true, then confirming this truth. To achieve fuller understanding, one must gather detailed information and conduct research. This deeper knowledge, achieved through investigation and understanding, is called knowledge of recognition (ʿilm al-maʿrifah).

The more knowledge one gains through recognition, the more one's confirmation increases, and the more perfect one's faith becomes. Consider the questions: What are Paradise, Hell, and the Bridge of Sīrāṭ? Where are they situated? How long shall they endure? Who shall enter them and for how long? As knowledge of these matters deepens, so too does confirmation of them, strengthening both the intellectual form and the faith itself.

The Prophet and the Qurʾān emphasize faith—not knowledge alone—as the criterion of success. God declares:

> ⟪quran 2:2-5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ذَلِكَ ٱلْكِتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًۭى لِّلْمَتَّقِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *That book, whereof there is no doubt, a guidance to those who are Al-Muttaqun [the pious and righteous persons who fear Allah much (abstain from all kinds of sins and evil deeds which He has forbidden) and love Allah much (perform all kinds of good deeds which He has ordained)]. Who believe in the Ghaib and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and spend out of what we have provided for them [i.e. give Zakat, spend on themselves, their parents, their children, their wives, etc., and also give charity to the poor and also in Allah's Cause - Jihad, etc.]. And who believe in (the Quran and the Sunnah) which has been sent down (revealed) to you (Muhammad Peace be upon him ) and in [the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel), etc.] which were sent down before you and they believe with certainty in the Hereafter. (Resurrection, recompense of their good and bad deeds, Paradise and Hell, etc.). They are on (true) guidance from their Lord, and they are the successful.*

Observe that God says "those who believe in the unseen" (*yū'minūn bi-l-ghayb*), not "those who know the unseen." Belief brings success through the unseen and through knowledge of the unseen. Faith cannot exist without confirmation, and confirmation cannot be achieved without epistemology. In other words, the degrees of faith are erected upon the ladder-rungs of knowledge.[^cite-18]

## The Distinction Between Islam and Faith

God clarifies the difference between *islām* (submission) and *īmān* (faith) in the following terms:

> ⟪quran 49:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ قَالَتِ ٱلْأَعْرَابُ آمَنَّا ۖ قُل لَّمْ تُؤْمِنُوا۟ وَلَكِن قُولُوٓا۟ أَسْلَمْنَا وَلَمَّا يَدْخُلِ ٱلْإِيمَنُ فِى قُلُوبِكُمْ ۖ وَإِن تُطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ لَا يَلِتْكُم مِّنْ أَعْمَلِكُمْ شَيْـًٔا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهُ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌ ‎⟫
> *The bedouins say: "We believe." Say: "You believe not but you only say, 'We have surrendered (in Islam),' for Faith has not yet entered your hearts. But if you obey Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), He will not decrease anything in reward for your deeds. Verily, Allah is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful."*

> ⟪quran 49:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَمُنُّونَ عَلَيْكَ أَنْ أَسْلَمُوا۟ ۖ قُل لَّا تَمُنُّوا۟ عَلَىَّ إِسْلَمَكُم ۖ بَلِ ٱللَّهُ يَمُنُّ عَلَيْكُمْ أَنْ هَدَىٰكُمْ لِلْإِيمَنِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *They regard as favour upon you (O Muhammad PBUH) that they have embraced Islam. Say: "Count not your Islam as a favour upon me. Nay, but Allah has conferred a favour upon you, that He has guided you to the Faith, if you indeed are true.*

> ⟪quran 51:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَمَا وَجَدْنَا فِيهَا غَيْرَ بَيْتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *But We found not there any household of the Muslims except one [i.e. Lout (Lot) and his two daughters].*

These verses demonstrate that Islam and faith are two distinct realities. Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq teaches that while faith necessarily exists within the domain of Islam, Islam does not necessarily embrace the domain of faith. Islam pertains to outward words and deeds; faith concerns the inner realm—the purity and sincerity of the heart.[^cite-19] When asked to explain the essence of Islam and faith, the Imām stated: "Faith resides entirely in the heart, while Islam is the maintenance of the ordinances of marriage and inheritance." Whoever enters into Islam has his blood, honor, wealth, and property made sacred and protected.

The distinction may be depicted as follows: it is possible for a Muslim not to be a believer, yet every believer is necessarily a Muslim first. The Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī (peace be upon him), explained that Islam requires only outward profession with the tongue, whereas faith demands both confession and inner knowledge of the heart. Thus, when God grants a servant the capacity to recognize Him, His Messenger, and His Imām (the divinely-appointed guide), and that servant confesses with his tongue, he becomes a believer.[^cite-20] As it is said, "To grant a person knowledge is a complete proof from God and an act of His grace and bounty." The servant's verbal profession is itself a divine favor. God bestows such grace upon whomsoever He wills—knowledge is the supreme gift that God places in the heart of His servant.

Profession springs from the heart. The knowledge that God has created in the heart takes the form of verbal utterance.

Therefore, no argument can be established against one whom God has not granted knowledge. Such a person is obligated to act according to the limits of his knowledge, refraining from actions concerning matters he does not know, and referring such matters to those who possess knowledge, for God says:

> ⟪quran 21:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent not before you (O Muhammad PBUH) but men to whom We inspired, so ask the people of the Reminder [Scriptures - the Taurat (Torah), the Injeel (Gospel)] if you do not know.*

## The Minimal Conditions of Faith, Polytheism, and Misguidance

The Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him), was asked: "O ʿAlī! What is the lowest condition that makes one a believer, and what is the lowest condition that makes one a disbeliever?"

### The Minimal Condition of Faith

ʿAlī replied: "The lowest condition that constitutes faith is that when God grants one of His servants the capacity to recognize Him, that servant should acknowledge God in obedience. Similarly, when God enables him to recognize His Messenger, he should acknowledge the Messenger in obedience. When God enables him to recognize and establish the Proof of God upon earth—His witness to creation—he should believe in the Imāmate (al-imāmah, the divinely-ordained leadership) and acknowledge it in obedience. Such a person is a believer. If he knows nothing beyond this, he must obey when commanded to act and refrain when forbidden."

### The Minimal Condition of Polytheism

"The lowest condition that makes one a polytheist," he continued, "is that one takes something God has forbidden and supposes God has commanded it. He worships something other than God, thinking he obeys God's command, yet worship of any other than God is polytheism. Such a person is truly a polytheist."

### The Minimal Condition of Misguidance

"The lowest condition that leads one astray," he concluded, "is that one neglects to seek the Proof of God upon earth—the knowledge of God's witness upon creation—and does not follow it. One who is deprived of knowledge of the Imām (al-imāmah) is surely among those misguided."

## The True Aim of the Islamic Summons

The Prophet's purpose in inviting people to outward Islam (*islām*) was ultimately to summon them to the inner self. Yet since the inner is accessible only through the outward, the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) first called people to Islam, saying: "Utter *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* and you shall prosper." In that same setting, those who had matured in the Islamic summons (al-daʿwa, the call to divine truth) and developed the spiritual aptitude for it, the Prophet went on to invite them to the word of faith—the inward summons. Thus *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* is the word of Islamic or outward calling, and faith is the inner veil of that word. The Qurʾān alludes to this inward invitation:

> ⟪quran 73:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(He Alone is) the Lord of the east and the west, La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He). So take Him Alone as Wakîl (Disposer of your affairs).*

In the phrase *Lā ilāha illā Huwa*, the numerical value of the letters totals 110, which equals the numerical value of the name ʿAlī. Thus this verse points mysteriously to the inward invitation—establishing the guardianship of the Master of the East and West.[^cite-21] 

The Messenger of God declared: "So long as the people of my Community continue to recite this word and fulfill all the obligations and duties of the faith, God's wrath shall be averted from them." Otherwise, this word shall be hurled back in their faces, and they shall be told: "You are liars! You did not establish the verification (*taṣdīq*) of this utterance." In truth, the fulfillment of the religion's obligations constitutes the journey from the outward *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* to the inner *Lā ilāha illā Huwa*.[^cite-22]

## The Islamic and Faithful Paradises

When the Originator, Most High (Mubdiʿ Taʿālā), bestowed upon the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal, the foremost emanation of intellective capacity) the declaration of divine unity, that intellect did not merely utter negation; rather, it affirmed the truth: *Lā ilāha illā Huwa* ("There is no god but He").

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah bears witness that La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), and the angels, and those having knowledge (also give this witness); (He is always) maintaining His creation in Justice. La ilah illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.*

The utterance *Lā ilāha illā Huwa* is the word through which the First Intellect attained perfection (kamāl thānī, secondary perfection)—its glory and elevation increased.[^cite-23] In the same manner, believers cannot be prepared for the state of perfection unless they embrace the guardianship (walāyah) of ʿAlī and the children of ʿAlī, and believe in the inward summons.

With the Prophet's pronouncement of *Lā ilāha illā Allāh*, Muslims entered into the Paradise of Islam (jannāt al-islām, the outward paradise of submission). The sword of Islam has been withheld from them—their lives and property are protected—yet there is no certainty that they shall endure in this utterance. For they lack the word that constitutes them, the word that gives them form. This is achieved when the meaning of *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* comes to be realized within the truth of *Lā ilāha illā Huwa*. When deeds are actualized through the law-giving word (al-nāṭiqah, the speaking one) and through the Covenant of the Commander of the Faithful (ʿAlī), the protection (ʿiṣmah, divine guidance and preservation) is attained, which is the guarantor of final beatitude.[^cite-24]

In Islam there exists but a single rank, with no Muslim superior to another—the Messenger established all Muslims as brothers and sisters on equal footing. Yet the inner life possesses a power that not only strengthens outward belief but also elevates one through hierarchies of rank. In the circle of faith, there exist degrees and levels wherein one believer cannot be equal to another.

## The Emanation of Islam and Faith

The Messenger of God, upon whom be peace and blessings, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ whose station constituted the highest veil (*hijab*) and whose name was Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā, ⟪ar:محمد مصطفیٰ⟫ was the emanation (*munbaʿith*, al-Munbaʿithūn) of Islam. ⟪ar:اسلام⟫ The Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, upon whom be peace, ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ whose station bore a secondary veil, gave forth the emanation of faith (*īmān*). ⟪ar:ایمان⟫

This relationship finds expression in the arithmetic of sacred names. Should one add the numerical values of the letters of Muḥammad's name, the sum equates to the numerical value of the word *Islam*; similarly, the numerical sum of the letters of ʿAlī's name equals the numerical value of the word *faith* (īmān).[^cite-22] Thus is revealed the correspondence between the persons of the Prophet and the Commander of the Faithful and the cosmic realities they embody.
