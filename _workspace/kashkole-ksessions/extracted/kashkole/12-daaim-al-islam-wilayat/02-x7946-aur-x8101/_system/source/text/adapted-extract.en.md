## What is Faith?

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ — Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq — declared that belief in Allah is the noblest of all deeds and that, accordingly, its reward is the greatest of all rewards. He defined faith (īmān) as the testimony: ⟪ar-quote:أشھد ان لا الہ الا اللہ وحدہ لا شریک لہ و أشھد ان محمد عبدہ و رسولہ⟫ — "I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, alone, without partner, and I bear witness that Muḥammad is His servant and His Messenger." Paradise is a reality; hellfire is a reality; the rising from the grave at the advent of the Resurrection is a reality. To affirm the ⟪ar:انبیا و مرسلین⟫ (the prophets and the apostles) and the ⟪ar:ائمہ طاہرین علیہم السلام⟫ (the pure Imams); to obey the ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ (the Imam) in all that he enjoins; to fulfil what God has commanded upon His servants and abstain from what He has forbidden; to accept whatever the Imam declares — this composite of inward acceptance and outward obedience is what constitutes faith.

In the Imam's exposition, faith is the name given to three things considered together:

| Confirmation in the Heart | Declaration on the Tongue | Action through the Limbs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Heartfelt affirmation (taṣdīq) | Verbal testimony | Bodily practice |

When asked whether inward affirmation and verbal declaration could themselves be counted as acts, he replied: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان عمَلُ کلُّہُ⟫ — "Faith is action in its entirety." Speech is itself an act performed by the tongue. In the same manner, believing and acquiring knowledge are acts of the heart, while compliance with the command of God and His Messenger through every limb is the act of the body. **Faith, then, is nothing other than the sum of these acts.**[^cite-1]

| The Heart's Work | The Tongue's Work | The Body's Work |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The practice of belief and knowledge | The practice of testimony | Actions performed by all the organs |

Allah, exalted is He, has said:

> ⟪quran 2:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe (in the Oneness of Allah swt- Islamic Monotheism) and do righteous good deeds, they are dwellers of Paradise, they will dwell therein forever.*
> And those who believe and do good deeds - they are the inhabitants of Paradise; they will abide therein forever.
> — **Quran 2:82 (inline widget)**

Wherever the Qurʾan mentions faith, it mentions righteous deeds in the same breath: the two are inseparably bound. They are commonly distinguished because faith pertains to the heart while action pertains to the limbs and organs; yet it is the conjunction of the two that constitutes a person a believer (muʾmin). ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ — Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī — said in the ⟪ar:نہج البلاغہ⟫ (Nahj al-Balāgha): ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان اعتقاد بالقلب و عمل بالارکان و اقرار بالسان⟫ — "Faith is conviction in the heart, practice through the limbs, and declaration upon the tongue." Faith begins in the heart and culminates in righteous works; confession upon the tongue is folded into those works. Faith proceeds from within and moves outward into tongue, limb, and joint, while islām (submission) begins with the ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ (the formula "there is no god but Allah") spoken on the tongue and reaches the limbs and joints from there. The light of faith rises from within and shines outward; islām is the name given to an outward light. Islām corresponds to the manifest sharīʿa (the revealed law), while faith corresponds to the inner meaning of that same sharīʿa — its taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis).[^cite-2]

### Confirmation and Security (taṣdīq wa-amān)

In its literal sense, īmān bears two meanings: to confirm or affirm, and to grant security. Faith is a transitive act that is incomplete with the verb alone; it requires its object. If faith means to affirm, then one must ask: who is being affirmed? The party affirmed will be called the ⟪ar:مُصَدِّق⟫ (muṣaddaq, the one whose word is verified), and he stands as the object of the act. And if faith carries the further sense of granting security, then again one must ask: whom is one securing, and from what?

The Prophet, peace be upon him, combined these two senses in a single ḥadīth (prophetic report): ⟪ar-quote:ھِینَ اَلمُؤمِن مَن آمَنَ جَورِہِ بِوَایقَۃ⟫ — "The believer is the one who secures his neighbour from his own harm." It is in virtue of this double sense that a person is called muʾmin; and among ⟪ar:اسمآء الحسنی⟫ (al-asmāʾ al-ḥusnā, the Most Beautiful Names of God), God Himself bears the name ⟪ar:اَلمُؤمِنُ⟫ — al-Muʾmin. A person is named muʾmin because he affirms God's ⟪ar:وَعَدُ وَعِیدْ⟫ (waʿd wa-waʿīd, His promise and His warning); and God in turn is named ⟪ar:اَلمُؤمِنُ⟫ al-Muʾmin because He secures the believer from the doom of His punishment.

| Agent | Act | Recipient |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The believer | Affirmation | Allah, His Messenger, the foremost command |
| The believer | Granting security | Created beings |
| Allah | Granting security | Damnation |

[^cite-1]: Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān, *Daʿāʾim al-Islām*, opening sections on īmān in the Book of Walāya.
[^cite-2]: Abū Yaʿqūb al-Sijistānī, *Kitāb al-Yanābīʿ*, on the distinction between islām as ẓāhir and īmān as bāṭin.

## The Faith of the Heart, the Tongue, and the Body

It is said: God has made faith obligatory upon every limb of the son of Adam and has apportioned it among them. There is, therefore, not a single member among the twelve limbs of the human person upon which the practice of faith is not incumbent — and the faith proper to each limb differs from that proper to the others. God has distributed faith among the human organs as follows:

| Limb | Heart | Tongue | Ears | Eyes | Hands | Feet | Face | Pudendum (al-faraj) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 12 | 1 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 1 | 1 |

### The Faith of the Heart

The heart, from which the human being draws understanding and discernment, is sovereign over the whole body. Without its judgment and command, no organ may admit anything into the body or expel anything from it. The faith that God has enjoined upon the heart is fivefold: confession, knowledge, conviction, acceptance, and submission. That is, the believer must confess inwardly that God is One, that there is no god but He, that He is ⟪ar-quote:وحدہ لا شریک لہ⟫ — alone and without partner, without consort, without offspring — and that ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ is His servant and His true Messenger. Thereafter the heart must acknowledge all that God has sent down through Book and Prophet alike. And after this affirmation, it is incumbent upon the heart to acquire knowledge of the ḥudūd (the cosmic and religious limits), by which faith is strengthened. This, then, is the practical and intellectual faith that God has imposed upon the heart — that recognition (maʿrifa) of God and of the rightful imam of the age precedes and grounds every outward act of the limbs.[^cite-3] The following verses bear witness:

> ⟪quran 16:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَن كَفَرَ بِٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ إِيمَنِهِۦٓ إِلَّا مَنْ أُكْرِهَ وَقَلْبُهُۥ مُطْمَىِٕنٌّۢ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ وَلَكِن مَّن شَرَحَ بِٱلْكُفْرِ صَدْرًۭا فَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Whoever disbelieves in God after having believed — save him who is compelled while his heart is at rest in faith — but whoever opens his breast to disbelief, upon them is wrath from God, and theirs is a great torment.*
> — **Quran 16:106 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 2:284⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لِّلَّهِ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَإِن تُبْدُوا۟ مَا فِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ أَوْ تُخْفُوهُ يُحَاسِبْكُم بِهِ ٱللَّهُ ۖ فَيَغْفِرُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَن يَشَآءُ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌ ‎⟫
> *To God belongs whatsoever is in the heavens and whatsoever is in the earth; whether you disclose what is in your souls or conceal it, God will bring you to account for it. He forgives whom He will and chastises whom He will, and God is powerful over every thing.*
> — **Quran 2:284 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 5:41⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ لَا يَحْزُنكَ ٱلَّذِينَ يُسَرِعُونَ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ آمَنَّا بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَلَمْ تُؤْمِن قُلُوبُهُمْ ۛ وَمِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَادُوا۟ ۛ سَمَّعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ سَمَّعُونَ لِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ لَمْ يَأْتُوكَ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ مَوَاضِعِهِۦ ۖ يَقُولُونَ إِنْ أُوتِيتُمْ هَذَا فَخُذُوهُ وَإِن لَّمْ تُؤْتَوْهُ فَٱحْذَرُوا۟ ۚ وَمَن يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ فِتْنَتَهُۥ فَلَن تَمْلِكَ لَهُۥ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ شَيْـًٔا ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَمْ يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ أَن يُطَهِّرَ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا خِزْىٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O Messenger, let not those grieve you who race toward disbelief — those who say with their mouths, "We believe," while their hearts have not believed; nor those of the Jews who eagerly listen to falsehood and listen on behalf of another folk who have not come to you, who pervert words from their settings. Whomsoever God wills to try, you have no power over for him against God. They are those whose hearts God has not willed to purify; for them in this world is disgrace, and in the Hereafter a great torment.*
> — **Quran 5:41 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 13:28⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَتَطْمَىِٕنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ تَطْمَىِٕنُّ ٱلْقُلُوبُ ‎⟫
> *Those who believe and whose hearts find rest in the remembrance of God — surely, in the remembrance of God do hearts find rest.*
> — **Quran 13:28 (inline widget)**

### The Faith of the Tongue

The faith that God has enjoined upon the tongue is the faith of utterance and articulation — namely, that the tongue should declare in words the confession and conviction that the heart has already secured. As God says:

> ⟪quran 3:84⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْنَا وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَإِسْمَعِيلَ وَإِسْحَقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَٱلْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَآ أُوتِىَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَٱلنَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُۥ مُسْلِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say: "We believe in God and in what has been sent down to us, and in what was sent down to Abraham, Ishmael, Isaac, Jacob, and the Tribes, and in what was given to Moses and Jesus and the prophets from their Lord. We make no distinction between any of them, and to Him we have submitted."*
> — **Quran 3:84 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 33:70⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَقُولُوا۟ قَوْلًۭا سَدِيدًۭا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe, fear God and speak words upright and true.*
> — **Quran 33:70 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 18:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيُؤْمِن وَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيَكْفُرْ ۚ إِنَّآ أَعْتَدْنَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ نَارًا أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ سُرَادِقُهَا ۚ وَإِن يَسْتَغِيثُوا۟ يُغَاثُوا۟ بِمَآءٍۢ كَٱلْمُهْلِ يَشْوِى ٱلْوُجُوهَ ۚ بِئْسَ ٱلشَّرَابُ وَسَآءَتْ مُرْتَفَقًا ‎⟫
> *And say: "The truth is from your Lord; so whoever wills, let him believe, and whoever wills, let him disbelieve." Indeed, We have prepared for the wrongdoers a Fire whose walls will encompass them; and if they cry out for relief, they shall be answered with water like molten brass that scalds the faces. Evil is the drink, and ill is the place of rest.*
> — **Quran 18:29 (inline widget)**

### The Faith of the Two Ears

It is the duty of the ear to attend to God's commandment and to give it full heed, and not to listen to anything forbidden or hateful to God. On this God says:

> ⟪quran 4:140⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنْ إِذَا سَمِعْتُمْ آيَتِ ٱللَّهِ يُكْفَرُ بِهَا وَيُسْتَهْزَأُ بِهَا فَلَا تَقْعُدُوا۟ مَعَهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّكُمْ إِذًۭا مِّثْلُهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ جَامِعُ ٱلْمُنَفِقِينَ وَٱلْكَفِرِينَ فِى جَهَنَّمَ جَمِيعًا ‎⟫
> *He has already sent down upon you in the Book that when you hear the signs of God being disbelieved and mocked, do not sit with them until they enter upon some other discourse; for in that case you would be like them. Truly, God will gather the hypocrites and the disbelievers together in Hell.*
> — **Quran 4:140 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 6:68⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخُوضُونَ فِىٓ آيَتِنَا فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦ ۚ وَإِمَّا يُنسِيَنَّكَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَلَا تَقْعُدْ بَعْدَ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *When you see those who plunge into idle talk about Our signs, turn away from them until they engage in another discourse. And should Satan cause you to forget, then, after the remembrance, sit not with the wrongdoing folk.*
> — **Quran 6:68 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 39:18⟫
> ⟪ar:فَبَشِّرْ عِبَادِ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَمِعُونَ ٱلْقَوْلَ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ أَحْسَنَهُۥٓ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمْ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *Give good news to My servants — those who listen to the word and follow the best of it. They are the ones whom God has guided, and they are the possessors of understanding.*
> — **Quran 39:18 (inline widget)**

The discipline of hearing is, in the Ismaili reading, the very door of the daʿwa (the summons): the seeker first hears the call, then weighs it, then chooses the better.[^cite-4]

> ⟪quran 23:1-4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَدْ أَفْلَحَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ فِى صَلَاتِهِمْ خَشِعُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ ٱللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَوٰةِ فَعِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *Truly successful are the believers — those who are humble in their prayers, who turn away from idle speech, and who are active in giving zakāt.*
> — **Quran 23:1-4 (inline widget)**

### The Faith of the Two Eyes

God has enjoined upon the eyes that they not gaze upon what is forbidden, and that one lower one's sight from what God has prohibited. This too is reckoned within faith and is the proper work of the eyes. The verse makes it plain:

> ⟪quran 24:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Tell the believers to lower their gaze and to guard their private parts. That is purer for them. Truly, God is well aware of what they do.*
> — **Quran 24:30 (inline widget)**

### The Faith of the Pudendum (al-Faraj)

The meaning of the verse is that the believing man should not look at his brother's private parts and should guard his own, lest any look upon them. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ teaches that whereas every other verse in the Holy Qurʾān concerning the safeguarding of the private parts is meant to prohibit adultery, the verse on ⟪ar:غَضُّ بَصَر⟫ implies the more specific injunction that one ought not even to expose one's own nakedness to the gaze of another. In another verse, God speaks at once of the obligations belonging to the heart, the tongue, the eye, and the ear:

> ⟪quran 17:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْبَصَرَ وَٱلْفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَانَ عَنْهُ مَسْـُٔولًۭا ‎⟫
> *And do not pursue that of which you have no knowledge. Truly, the hearing, the sight, and the heart — each of these shall be questioned.*
> — **Quran 17:36 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 41:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَن يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَآ أَبْصَرُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَكِن ظَنَنتُمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *You did not conceal yourselves lest your hearing, your sight, or your skins should testify against you; rather, you supposed that God knew not much of what you were doing.*
> — **Quran 41:22 (inline widget)**

### The Faith of the Two Hands

God has enjoined upon the two hands that they not be employed in anything forbidden, but that they be used for almsgiving, kindness, the bonds of kinship, struggle in the way of God, and the ritual ablution — as God says:

> ⟪quran 5:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ فَٱغْسِلُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْمَرَافِقِ وَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِرُئُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُمْ جُنُبًۭا فَٱطَّهَّرُوا۟ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَآءَ أَحَدٌۭ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَمَسْتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا۟ مَآءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا۟ صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا فَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُم مِّنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُم مِّنْ حَرَجٍۢ وَلَكِن يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe, when you rise to prayer, wash your faces and your hands to the elbows, and wipe your heads and your feet to the ankles. If you are in a state of major ritual impurity, then purify yourselves. If you are ill or on a journey, or one of you has come from relieving himself, or you have touched women and find no water, then perform tayammum with clean earth and wipe your faces and your hands with it. God does not desire to lay any hardship upon you, but He desires to purify you and to complete His favour upon you, that perhaps you may be grateful.*
> — **Quran 5:6 (inline widget)**

Among the Fatimid duʿāt the outward act of ablution carries an inward sense as well: the limbs are made fit for the sacred encounter by being cleansed of the residue of worldly attachment, just as the soul is made fit for receiving knowledge by being purged of false opinion.[^cite-5]

> ⟪quran 8:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ زَحْفًۭا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلْأَدْبَارَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe, when you encounter the disbelievers advancing in battle, do not turn your backs upon them.*
> — **Quran 8:15 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 47:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ فَضَرْبَ ٱلرِّقَابِ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَثْخَنتُمُوهُمْ فَشُدُّوا۟ ٱلْوَثَاقَ فَإِمَّا مَنًّۢا بَعْدُ وَإِمَّا فِدَآءً حَتَّىٰ تَضَعَ ٱلْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا ۚ ذَلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَآءُ ٱللَّهُ لَٱنتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ وَلَكِن لِّيَبْلُوَا۟ بَعْضَكُم بِبَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱلَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ فَلَن يُضِلَّ أَعْمَلَهُمْ ‎⟫
> *When you encounter the disbelievers, strike at their necks until, when you have subdued them, bind them firmly; then either grant them grace or accept ransom, until the war lays down its burdens. So it is; and had God so willed, He could Himself have taken vengeance upon them, but it is that He may try some of you by others. As for those slain in the way of God, He will not let their deeds be lost.*
> — **Quran 47:4 (inline widget)**

Thus God has made the striking of necks on the battlefield an obligation, for the striking of necks is the work of the hands, and it is reckoned as part of faith.

### The Faith of the Two Feet

God has made it obligatory upon the two feet that they walk in the path of obedience — that one not walk with them to evil deeds in which there is disobedience to God, but rather that one walk with them toward God's command and God's good pleasure. God says:

> ⟪quran 17:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَمْشِ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا ‎⟫
> *And do not walk upon the earth in arrogance. Truly, you can neither cleave the earth, nor reach the mountains in height.*
> — **Quran 17:37 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 31:19⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱقْصِدْ فِى مَشْيِكَ وَٱغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلْأَصْوَتِ لَصَوْتُ ٱلْحَمِيرِ ‎⟫
> *Be moderate in your gait and lower your voice; truly, the most hateful of voices is the voice of the ass.*
> — **Quran 31:19 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 62:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا نُودِىَ لِلصَّلَوٰةِ مِن يَوْمِ ٱلْجُمُعَةِ فَٱسْعَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe, when the call to prayer is proclaimed on the day of congregation, hasten to the remembrance of God and leave off trading. That is better for you, if you only knew.*
> — **Quran 62:9 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 22:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ لْيَقْضُوا۟ تَفَثَهُمْ وَلْيُوفُوا۟ نُذُورَهُمْ وَلْيَطَّوَّفُوا۟ بِٱلْبَيْتِ ٱلْعَتِيقِ ‎⟫
> *Then let them complete their rites of purification, fulfil their vows, and circumambulate the Ancient House.*
> — **Quran 22:29 (inline widget)**

God further makes plain that on the Day of Resurrection the hands and the feet will themselves bear witness, by word, against the limbs and against their owners alike.

> ⟪quran 36:65⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ نَخْتِمُ عَلَىٰٓ أَفْوَهِهِمْ وَتُكَلِّمُنَآ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَتَشْهَدُ أَرْجُلُهُم بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
> *On that Day We shall set a seal upon their mouths, and their hands shall speak to Us, and their feet shall bear witness to what they used to earn.*
> — **Quran 36:65 (inline widget)**

### The Faith of the Face

God says:

> ⟪quran 22:77⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱرْكَعُوا۟ وَٱسْجُدُوا۟ وَٱعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ وَٱفْعَلُوا۟ ٱلْخَيْرَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe, bow down and prostrate yourselves, and worship your Lord, and do good, that perhaps you may prosper.*
> — **Quran 22:77 (inline widget)**

And in another verse God says:

> ⟪quran 72:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنَّ ٱلْمَسَجِدَ لِلَّهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا۟ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ أَحَدًۭا ‎⟫
> *The places of prostration belong to God; so call not upon anyone alongside God.*
> — **Quran 72:18 (inline widget)**

Thus God has made it obligatory upon the limbs that they be purified and that they offer the ritual prayer, and He has called prayer itself "faith" in His Book.[^cite-6] When God commanded the Messenger of God (peace and blessings of God be upon him and his family) to turn away in the prayer from ⟪ar:بیت المقدس⟫ toward ⟪ar:بیت الحرام⟫, the people asked: "O Messenger of God, what shall become of our former prayers?"

> ⟪quran 2:143⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Thus We have made you a middle community, that you may be witnesses over mankind and that the Messenger may be a witness over you. We made the qibla which you formerly faced only that We might distinguish those who follow the Messenger from those who turn on their heels. It was indeed a heavy matter, save for those whom God has guided. God would never let your faith go to waste; truly, God is full of tenderness and mercy toward mankind.*
> — **Quran 2:143 (inline widget)**

**In this verse God has named the prayer itself "faith."**

Whoever departs this world in a state such that his limbs have been preserved from sin and every member has discharged its proper office with goodly grace, that one will receive his full measure of faith from God and will enter Paradise. As for the one who has betrayed God in the slightest degree or transgressed God's commands through these organs, he will come before God with his faith diminished. And whoever takes upon his neck the collar of the covenant of God and of the Imam of the Age, and yet thereafter neither acts nor seeks knowledge, will not be called a believer. A merely verbal faith profits him nothing, and he has no standing in the sight of God or of the Imam (peace be upon him); rather, such a person is reckoned outside the circle of the Imam of the Age.

[^cite-4]: Cf. al-Sijistani, Kitab al-Yanabiʿ, on hearing as the threshold by which the soul receives the daʿwa (the summons).
[^cite-5]: Cf. Nasir-i Khusraw, Wajh-i Din, on the taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis) of ritual ablution: the washing of the outward members corresponds to the purification of the inward faculties.

## The Three Categories of Faith and of the Faithful

The ⟪ar:قرآن حکیم⟫ (Qurʾān al-Ḥakīm, the Wise Recitation) distinguishes three kinds of faith:

| 1 | 2 | 3 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pure Faith (īmān ṣāfī) | Imperfect Faith (īmān nāqiṣ) | Faith Tainted by Polytheism (īmān yushābihu al-shirk) |

### Pure Faith

Those who possess pure faith are the very ones whom God Himself praises and to whom He gives the glad tidings of Paradise. They are the bearers of the two pillars of faith — ṣidq (truthfulness) and amān (security) — and they stand in the station of "righteousness." Though their bodies dwell in the ⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫ (dār al-kadhib, the abode of falsehood), the purity of their souls already inhabits the ⟪ar:دار الصدق و صفا⟫ (dār al-ṣidq wa-ṣafāʾ, the abode of truth and clarity). To put it another way: outwardly they appear in this world, but inwardly they live in the Hereafter while keeping the world in view — whereas ordinary Muslims live in the world while keeping the Hereafter in view.

The attribute of amān descends upon them in the sense that when their ⟪ar:نفوس لطیفۃ⟫ (nufūs laṭīfa, refined souls) arrive at the ⟪ar:دار السلام⟫ (dār al-salām, the abode of peace), they will be spared every difficulty that befalls bodies after death; for them death will be soul and sweetness. Through death they will be released completely from the ⟪ar:دار کدورت⟫ (dār al-kudūra, the abode of turbidity) and pass into eternal safety. Of this death the Prophet (peace be upon him) said, "⟪ar-quote:اَلمَوتُ رِیحاَنَۃُ المُؤمِن⟫" — "Death is the sweet basil of the believer." And ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫, pointing to his throat, said, "When the soul of my believer reaches here, he rejoices."[^cite-7] For at that moment the believer beholds ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی علیہ السلام⟫ and the ⟪ar:ائمہ طاھرین⟫ — the rightful ⟪ar:ولی أمر⟫ (walī al-amr, holder of authority) — to whom no obedience can ever be too much.

The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said that those addressed by the Qurʾānic vocative ⟪ar-quote:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ — "O you who believe" — are led and presided over by ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫, and this leadership will remain in the hands of the descendants of ʿAlī for as long as those addressed by ⟪ar:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ themselves remain. Since that title comprehends both the ⟪ar:مُتَقَدِّمِینَ⟫ (mutaqaddimīn, those who came before) and the ⟪ar:مُتَأَخِّرِینَ⟫ (mutaʾakhkhirīn, those who came after), the religious leadership it founds will endure over all the faithful ⟪ar:اِلیٰ یَومِ الدِّینَ⟫ (ilā yawm al-dīn, until the Day of Religion). To disobey the Imams of the line of ⟪ar:محمد و علی(ﷺ)⟫ is therefore to disobey God and His Messenger; such a one will be reckoned a ⟪ar:خارجی⟫ (khārijī, an outlier), and when he dies he will die the death of the Age of Ignorance. God Most High says:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority.&nbsp;(And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day.&nbsp;That is better and more suitable for final determination.&nbsp;*
>O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger and those in authority among you. And if you dispute among yourselves about anything, refer it to Allah and His Messenger, if you believe in Allah and the Last Day. That is better and better in the end.
> — **Quran 4:59 (inline widget)**

### Imperfect Faith

The second sort of affirmation of God and His Messenger is the faith of the weak. These are those who have only just entered the ⟪ar:دعوت ایمانیۃ⟫ (daʿwat īmāniyya, the summons to faith) — the ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ (mustajīb, the respondent) at the threshold — and remain as yet ignorant of religious matters. Their condition resembles that of a newborn: although by virtue of having all his bodily organs the infant is already called a human being, his lack of strength and skill in using them keeps him dependent on supervision at every step. He can do nothing on his own. So too the ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ at the early limits of the daʿwa is named a believer, yet at every step he depends on the training of his elder. The Most High has explained this defective station of faith in the verse:

> ⟪quran 4:136⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ آمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ مِن قَبْلُ ۚ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَلًۢا بَعِيدًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Believe in Allah, and His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and the Book (the Quran) which He has sent down to His Messenger, and the Scripture which He sent down to those before (him), and whosoever disbelieves in Allah, His Angels, His Books, His Messengers, and the Last Day, then indeed he has strayed far away.*
> O you who believe! Believe in Allah and His Messenger and the Book which He has sent down to His Messenger and the Book which He sent down to those before it, and whoever disbelieves in Allah and His angels and His Messengers and the Last Day, he indeed has strayed far away.
> — **Quran 4:136 (inline widget)**

The verse says, "O you who believe, believe in Allah and His Messenger." If the believers addressed by ⟪ar:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ were already ⟪ar:کامل الایمان⟫ (kāmil al-īmān, perfect in faith), then the second command ⟪ar:’ آمَنُو‘⟫ would be superfluous. On the one hand God testifies to their belief by calling them "O believers"; on the other hand He commands them to believe. The meaning is therefore plain: those of a lower rank are being told to perfect their faith. You have entered the circle of faith after the Age of Honour and have attained religious birth — now press on in the knowledge of religion until you become a perfect believer. Remain attached to the righteous, who are your religious parents; draw on their spiritual nurture; reach by their hand the station of perfection. Only then are you fit to dwell in the ⟪ar:دار الصدق و امان⟫ (dār al-ṣidq wa-amān, the abode of truth and security) rather than to remain in the ⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫. Just as small children accept the training of their parents and teachers and at last attain the rank of their elders, so the Most High declares:[^cite-8]

> ⟪quran 52:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and whose offspring follow them in Faith, to them shall We join their offspring, and We shall not decrease the reward of their deeds in anything. Every person is a pledge for that which he has earned.*
> And those who believed, and their descendants followed them in faith, We will unite with them their descendants, and We will not deprive them of aught of their deeds; every person is a pledge for his work.
> — **Quran 52:21 (inline widget)**

Here "descendants" necessarily denotes religious or causal descendants, not lineal or familial ones. The sense, then, is this: the ⟪ar:مستجیبین⟫ (mustajībīn, respondents) of the lower rank followed the ḥudūd of the daʿwa of the higher rank, and in the end We joined them to their elders and made them equal in station.

### Faith Tainted by Polytheism

The third kind of faith is mingled with shirk (polytheism) and has nothing in common with true faith in God and His Messenger. The meaning of īmān in Islam is that we accept the command which the Messenger of God conveyed to us by divine revelation, ⟪ar:مِنُّ و عَن⟫ (minnu wa-ʿan, hand to hand and face to face) — without altering it, without hesitation, without misgiving — because that is what purifies our souls and secures their salvation.

God Almighty commands: ⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ آتَیكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوہُ وَمَا نَھٰكُمْ عَنْہُ فَانتَھوا۟⟫ — "Whatever the Messenger gives you, take it; whatever he forbids you, abstain from it." That is, in the Messenger's command and in obedience to him, insert not your own opinion in even the smallest degree. A perfect believer is one who, in worship, in ḥalāl and ḥarām, and in every religious matter, stands in direct relation to the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) and inserts neither his own opinion nor his own conjecture. But the one who joins his desires to the Messenger's commandments — who deploys raʾy (opinion), ẓann (conjecture), and ijtihād to satisfy what he wishes — is no believer but an innovator. Because he has shared his opinion with God, his faith is tainted with shirk; it leads him to error in place of guidance, and to ruin in place of salvation. No prayer he prays, no fast he keeps, will profit him.[^cite-9]

The revelation given to the Messenger may be likened to the water that descends from the sky. Water is drinkable and purifying only so long as it remains uncontaminated. Once impurity overtakes it, however much it may still be called water, it is of no use to a Muslim: it cannot be drunk, nor can it purify. Just so, when the desires of the soul are mixed into the rulings of revelation, the pure religion ceases to be, and innovation takes its place. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said, "Every innovation is misguidance, however fair it may appear." After the entry of bidʿa, the outward limbs of Islam may seem to remain in place — opponents of the Imams pray, just as the followers of the Imams pray — but the difference between the two prayers is plain. The prayers of the opponents will be flung back in their faces, because they have mingled the bitterness of shirk and the impurity of innovation into their faith. Condemning such people, God Most High says:

> *And most of them believe not in Allah except that they attribute partners unto Him [i.e. they are Mushrikun -polytheists - see Verse 6: 121].*
> And most of them believe in Allah and associate others with Him.

### The Three Types of Believers

There are likewise three ranks among the ⟪ar:مؤمنین⟫ (muʾminīn, the faithful):

| 1 | Those who wrong themselves — that is, those who acquire knowledge but do not put it into practice. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Those who hold themselves in constant balance — that is, those who carry religion and the world together, keeping both in view. |
| 3 | Those who outstrip in good for their own sake — that is, those who devote themselves wholly to the Hereafter. |

## The Eight Ranks of Faith

Just as Paradise has eight gates, faith has eight ranks, each corresponding to a graded act of iḥsān (beneficence):

| 1 | Kindness to parents — the highest rank of faith. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Beneficence toward kin (dhawū al-arḥām). |
| 3 | Beneficence toward orphans. |
| 4 | Kindness to the poor and needy. |
| 5 | Kindness to a neighbour (jār) who is both related and faithful — the neighbour of the closest bond. |
| 6 | Jār al-junub: kindness to a believing neighbour who is not related. |
| 7 | Kindness to a neighbour with whom there is neither kinship nor shared religion. |
| 8 | Kindness to wayfarers and to those under one's authority. |

These eight ranks are not an arbitrary list but a graded discipline of the heart: the closer the bond, the more urgent the kindness, so that the believer learns iḥsān first in the household and only then extends it outward in widening circles.[^cite-10]

## The Qualities of the One Endowed with Faith

A believer's faith is not complete until his intellect is complete, and the perfection of intellect cannot be reached until ten qualities have been cultivated in him:

| 1 | The people of the world receive only good from him. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | None has any fear of evil from him. |
| 3 | He counts many of his own good deeds as few, and few of others' good deeds as many. |
| 4 | He counts a little of his own evil as much, and much of others' evil as little. |
| 5 | He never wearies of attending to the needs of people. |
| 6 | He never wearies of acquiring knowledge, but pursues it throughout his life. |
| 7 | Patience is dearer to him than glory and pride. |
| 8 | Faqr (poverty) is dearer to him than wealth, as the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: *"Faqrī fakhrī"* — "My poverty is my pride." |
| 9 | A little food suffices him. |
| 10 | When he meets a virtuous person, he considers that person better and more god-fearing than himself. |

These ten are not separate virtues laid side by side but a single disposition viewed from ten angles: the intellect, once it has come to rest in its Originator, can no longer entertain self-importance, and every quality on this list is a consequence of that repose.[^cite-11]

## Precedence and Degrees within Faith

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ (Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, peace be upon him) teaches that were faith a single, undifferentiated reality — admitting neither deficiency nor excess — no believer could surpass another, and all believers would share equally in the recompense allotted to faith. Each would stand at an identical station of ⟪ar:تفضیل⟫ (tafḍīl, preferment). But the reality is otherwise. Believers enter Paradise by the perfection of their faith, and only by the ⟪ar:راجح⟫ (rājiḥ, weightier or preponderant) measure of that faith do they gain superiority before God. Those ⟪ar:مقصرین⟫ (muqaṣṣirīn, the deficient ones) whose faith is flawed enter the Fire.[^cite-12]

Concerning the gradations of faith, ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ further explains: "God has appointed faith as a contest among the believers, just as horses race upon a fixed day." Each believer receives the rank that corresponds to the measure of his precedence. The latecomer is not advanced before the foremost, nor the virtuous before the more virtuous. Precedence (sabq) is itself among the merits by which the earliest of the Community surpass those who came after. It is for this reason that ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ (Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him), being the first of all the Companions to believe, attained superiority over every other believer.[^cite-13] Had the first not been preferred over those who came later, the last believer of this Community would stand on the same footing as its first. Had precedence not been the criterion of merit, many of the latter ⟪ar:ارباب فضل و کمال و زھد وتقوی⟫ (arbāb-i faḍl wa kamāl wa zuhd wa taqwā, masters of excellence, perfection, asceticism, and piety) would have overtaken the earlier ones. For among the latter there are many ⟪ar:ارباب فضل و کمال⟫ (arbāb-i faḍl wa kamāl, masters of excellence and perfection) whose practice far exceeds that of the predecessors: they pray much, fast often, perform many pilgrimages, and expend themselves in jihād and almsgiving. Were precedence not the measure of nobility, such latecomers would, by the abundance of their works, displace those who came before. But God does not permit the one whom He has placed last in faith to be raised among the first, nor the one whom He has advanced to be held back. The Qurʾān itself makes this principle explicit:

## Outward and Inner Faith

The reward Allah has promised for faith in the Hereafter is Paradise. The reason this recompense is deferred to the next life is that faith is a quality of ⟪ar:خیراتِ بشریات⟫ (khayrāt al-basharīyāt — the goods proper to humankind), embracing every fine attribute the human being is capable of, and orienting itself toward ⟪ar:فضائل الملائکۃ⟫ (faḍāʾil al-malāʾika — the virtues of the angels). Since one cannot become an angel while still bound to this world, the recompense answering to that angelic order has been held back for the Hereafter, and Paradise is its appointed return. Disbelief, by contrast, is a quality of ⟪ar:شَرُّ البشریات⟫ (sharr al-basharīyāt — the evil proper to humankind) that closes around the soul; for it the ⟪ar:وعید⟫ (waʿīd — the warning) of Hellfire has been set down.

Faith itself has two branches: outward and inner.

Two technical terms ought to be held apart at the outset:

- ⟪ar:وعدہ⟫ (waʿda — the promise) names the recompense for a good deed, as with the promise of Paradise.
- ⟪ar:وعید⟫ names the recompense for an evil deed, as with the warning of Hellfire.

### Outward Faith (al-īmān al-ẓāhir)

Outward faith is the verbal confession of five articles:

| # | Article |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | **Affirmation of Allah** — that there is one Creator of this world, One, Living, Almighty, Wise, the Maker of every creature; master of all that exists, having no partner in His plan. |
| 2 | **Confession of the angels**, who are the best of God's creations. Allah appointed them to His service and worship, made them the guardians of His world, and entrusted to them stations through which His plan is carried out. They never disobey Him; they perform only what they are commanded. |
| 3 | **Acknowledgement of the Prophets and Messengers** — that Allah has chosen from His servants upon the earth a particular group, and has set the angels as the intermediaries between Himself and that chosen group. The angels convey the message of the Lord to those special servants; the group then receives the glad tidings and the commands and conveys them in turn to all creatures. |
| 4 | **Acknowledgement of the Books** — that every message the prophets received as revelation from the angels was conveyed by them to their contemporaries in the language and idiom of those contemporaries. The message itself has remained one; only its vocabulary and style have changed with the times. |
| 5 | **Acknowledgement of the Resurrection** — that it is coming, that it will bring a new gathering and a transformation of every state. On that Day all creatures will be raised, called to account, and weighed: good deeds will be rewarded and evil deeds requited. |

The believers are those who affirm these five things completely, as Allah declares in His Book:

> ⟪quran 2:285⟫
> ⟪ar:‏آمَنَ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ ‎⟫
> *The Messenger believes in what has been sent down to him from his Lord, and so do the believers. Each one believes in Allah, His Angels, His Books, and His Messengers. They say, "We make no distinction between one another of His Messengers," and they say, "We hear, and we obey. Your forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the return."*
> — **Quran 2:285 (inline widget)**

When a believer confesses these five outward articles with his own tongue, he separates himself from Jews, Christians and Magians. In every confession there is a denial of whatever stands beside the affirmed object; and by this confession he binds himself to the duties of prayer, fasting, the pilgrimage, the alms-tax and jihād.[^cite-15]

### Inner Faith (al-īmān al-bāṭin)

Inner faith is the affirmation, from the depths of the heart, of the five articles the tongue has already confessed. By such affirmation the inner rank of the believer's faith is established. The deeper the affirmation, the higher the inner rank. It is to such believers that Allah addresses His praise — ⟪ar:یاَ اَیُّھاَ اَلَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ — in His Book, and to them He has promised Paradise.

The Paradise belonging to outward faith and the Paradise belonging to inner faith are not the same. Pointing to this distinction, the Prophet (peace be upon him) said, ⟪ar-quote:مَن قاَلَ لاَ اِلہَ اِلاَّ اَللّٰہ دَخَلَ اَلجَنَّۃ⟫ — "Whoever says ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ has entered Paradise." Then he said, ⟪ar-quote:مَن قاَلَ لاَ اِلہَ اِلاَّ اَللّٰہ مُخلِصاً دَخَلَ اَلجَنَّۃ⟫ — "Whoever says ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ sincerely has entered Paradise." The first saying concerns the one who utters ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ with the tongue only and thereby enters the Paradise of ⟪ar:اسلام⟫ — outward faith. The second concerns the one who, having confessed with the tongue, then develops sincerity — affirmation from the heart — and so enters the Paradise of ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ — inner faith.

| Confession alone | Confession with sincerity |
| --- | --- |
| Verbal acknowledgement | Verbal acknowledgement together with affirmation in the heart |

Inner faith is reached, the text tells us, through four disciplines: ⟪ar-quote:تَفکِیر وَالأعتَباَر وَ قِیاَم بِاالشَّراَءِطُھَا وَ واَجِبُ حَقُّھاَ⟫ — tafkīr, iʿtibār, qiyām bi-sharāʾiṭihā, and adāʾ wājib ḥaqqihā.

| Discipline | Sense |
| --- | --- |
| Tafkīr (reflection) | Turning the mind upon a thing with sustained thought; investigating the object and proving its truth to oneself. |
| Iʿtibār (consideration) | Setting one object alongside another to establish the difference or the relation between them. |
| Qiyām bi-sharāʾiṭihā | Fulfilling the conditions attached to this knowledge — that is, performing what Allah has commanded concerning it. |
| Adāʾ wājib ḥaqqihā | Rendering the full account that Allah has made obligatory in respect of that knowledge. |

Entering the Paradise of faith is no light undertaking. Allah Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 3:142⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ جَهَدُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *Do you think that you will enter Paradise before Allah tests those of you who strive in His cause and tests the patient?*
> — **Quran 3:142 (inline widget)**

(Rasāʾil Aḥmadiyya [Ikhwān al-Ṣafāʾ]) Imām Aḥmad b. ʿAbd Allāh (ʿalayhi al-salām)

## Faith Holds Precedence over Knowledge

The elevation of ranks is bound up with knowledge and with faith, not with action alone. The degrees of knowledge are one thing; the degrees of faith are another. Explaining this difference, Allah says:

> ⟪quran 58:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجَلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you are told to make room in the assemblies, make room; Allah will make room for you. And when you are told, "Rise up," rise up. Allah will raise in degree those of you who believe and those who have been given knowledge. And Allah is well-acquainted with what you do.*
> — **Quran 58:11 (inline widget)**

In this verse Allah says that He raises the ranks of those who believe and of those who have been given knowledge — yet the believers are named first. The order is itself instructive: it signals that the ranks of the believers are elevated before the ranks of those endowed with knowledge. Faith holds precedence over knowledge, because knowledge is the means by which faith increases, and an increase can only be predicated of something that already exists. That is, faith is prior in the very order of knowing. Even in the temporal order of this world the same priority is observed. When the Messenger of Allah summoned the people to Islam he said, ⟪ar-quote:قُولُو لاَ اِلہَ اِلاَّ اللہ تُفلِحُو⟫ — "Say ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ and you will prosper." He commanded the confession first — confession being the first condition of faith — without setting out the knowledge that stands behind the words. Only after the people had confessed did the stages of affirmation begin, in which the knowledge concerning ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ was unfolded and they were brought to know it.

Within ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ (ʿālam-i rūḥānī — the spiritual world) this same priority obtains. It was only when ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ — al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect) — confessed the oneness of ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ — Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High) — that the ⟪ar:علم ما کان سیکون⟫ (ʿilm mā kāna wa-mā sa-yakūn — the knowledge of what was and what will be) was bestowed upon it. For this reason faith is held to precede knowledge.[^cite-16]

Explaining the hierarchy of the learned, Allah further indicates that there is a knower 'above' every knower. In this verse the word ⟪ar:’ فوق‘⟫ marks the elevation and gradation of ranks:

> ⟪quran 12:76⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَبَدَأَ بِأَوْعِيَتِهِمْ قَبْلَ وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَخْرَجَهَا مِن وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ۚ كَذَلِكَ كِدْنَا لِيُوسُفَ ۖ مَا كَانَ لِيَأْخُذَ أَخَاهُ فِى دِينِ ٱلْمَلِكِ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ نَرْفَعُ دَرَجَتٍۢ مَّن نَّشَآءُ ۗ وَفَوْقَ كُلِّ ذِى عِلْمٍ عَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *So he began with their sacks before the sack of his brother; then he brought it out of his brother's sack. Thus did We plan for Yūsuf. He could not have taken his brother under the king's law unless Allah had willed it. We raise in rank whom We will, and above every possessor of knowledge there is one yet more knowing.*
> — **Quran 12:76 (inline widget)**

### Knowledge and Faith

Knowledge (ʿilm) is a conception which the soul produces in itself by coming to know some reality. When the soul then confesses this conception with the tongue, affirms it with the heart, and finally embeds it into itself by acting upon it through the organs of the body, the conception is called faith (īmān). Affirmation (taṣdīq) is a form of truthfulness, and it is not possible without inquiry. As Muslims we all know of the existence of Paradise, of Hellfire, and of the Bridge of Ṣirāṭ; we affirm them and so we believe in them. Yet since they cannot be encompassed by the ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫ (ḥawāss khamsa — the five senses), affirmation must be accompanied by inquiry into their existence and their reality.[^cite-17]

| Knowledge | Faith |
| --- | --- |
| Perception | Affirmation in the heart |
| Acceptance | Confession with the tongue, action with the limbs |
| | Continuing inquiry |

In the soul of the learned the form of the knowledge itself is called ⟪ar:علم⟫, while the confession of that knowledge, its affirmation, and the action that follows from it are together called ⟪ar:ایمان⟫. The real thing is faith; knowledge is the means by which faith is brought to increase.

(Rasāʾil Aḥmadiyya) Imām Aḥmad b. ʿAbd Allāh (ʿalayhi al-salām)

### Affirmation and Inquiry

Knowledge has a twofold character. The first is the state of ⟪ar:علم ظاہر⟫ (ʿilm ẓāhir — outward knowledge), which precedes faith. It is the awareness one acquires simply through being informed about some thing or reality — knowing, for instance, that Paradise and Hellfire exist. The second is the knowledge proper to faith: in it the truth is first acknowledged by the tongue — that Paradise and Hellfire are real — and then affirmed within. To affirm requires detailed information about the matter affirmed; affirmation is therefore what produces knowledge and what increases it through inquiry. This is ⟪ar:علم معرفت⟫ (ʿilm maʿrifa — gnostic knowledge), the conception that follows upon faith. The more knowledge enlarges through such inquiry, the more affirmation grows, and the more nearly faith approaches completeness. What are Paradise, Hellfire, the Bridge of Ṣirāṭ? Where are they? How long have they existed? Who will arrive there? How long will the soul remain in them? The more the knowledge of these questions deepens, the firmer the affirmation that answers to them; and both the ⟪ar:علمی صورت⟫ (ʿilmī ṣūra — the form of the knowledge in the soul) and the corresponding ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ are strengthened together.

| Awareness | Affirmation through inquiry |
| Imperfect faith | Perfected faith |

In other words, the degrees of faith are themselves erected upon the ladder of knowledge.

## The Difference between Islam and Faith

Explaining the distinction between Islam and faith, Allah says:

That is to say, a Muslim may not yet be a believer, but every believer is first a Muslim. Mawlānā ʿAlī says that Islam is confession of the tongue alone, whereas faith requires knowledge in the heart together with the confession. Therefore, if Allah grants a person the capacity to know Him and to know His Messenger and his Imam, and that person then confesses with his tongue, he is a believer. As the teaching makes plain, granting a servant the capacity to know is a complete proof from Allah and an expression of His grace and bounty; the servant's confession with his tongue is in turn a favour Allah bestows upon him, and He bestows such a favour upon whomever He wills. Knowledge, in other words, is the great favour that Allah deposits in the heart of the servant.

Confession is itself an act of the heart: the knowledge that Allah has set within the heart takes on, in the act of speech, the form of a verbal confession.

For this reason no argument can be established against a person whom Allah has not endowed with knowledge. He is bound to act within the limits of what he knows, not upon what he does not know; and matters of doubt are to be referred to those who hold the knowledge, as Allah commands:

## The Minimum Conditions of Faith, Polytheism, and Error

Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him) was once asked: what is the least act by which a person becomes a believer, and what is the least act by which a person becomes a disbeliever?

### The minimum condition of faith

He replied: the least by which a person becomes a believer is that, when God grants one of His servants the capacity to know Him, that servant should acknowledge Him in obedience. When God further grants him the capacity to know His Messenger, he should acknowledge the Messenger in obedience. And when God grants him the recognition of His Proof (ḥujja) upon the earth and His Witness over creation, he should affirm the imāmate of that figure and submit to it in obedience. Such a person is a believer. If he is ignorant of further matters, the rule for him is simple: when commanded, he obeys; when forbidden, he refrains.

### The minimum condition of polytheism

He then said: the least by which a person becomes a polytheist (mushrik) is that he should take what God has forbidden and persuade himself that God has commanded it — directing his worship to something other than God while imagining it to be divinely sanctioned. The worship of any other than God renders shirk binding upon the worshipper. Such a person is therefore counted among the polytheists.

### The minimum condition of error

He concluded: the least by which a person falls into error (ḍalāl) through his own act is that he should fail to seek the Proof of God upon the earth, fail to know His Witness over creation, and fail to follow him. One who is cut off from the recognition of the Imām (peace be upon him) is, without doubt, in misguidance.

This insistence — that recognition of the Imām is the foundation through which the rest of the religious life is accepted — finds its juristic crystallization in al-Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān's treatment of walāya (allegiance to the Imām) as the seventh and most encompassing of the pillars, the one through which obedience to the other six is itself accepted.[^cite-19]

## What Was the True Aim of the Islamic Daʿwa?

The Prophet's purpose in the outward invitation to Islam was, in truth, an invitation to its inward reality. Yet since the path to the inward runs only through the outward, the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him and his family) first summoned the people to Islam, saying ⟪ar-quote:قولو لا الہ الا اللہ تُفلِحُو⟫ — "Say ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ and you shall prosper." Then, in that same gathering of those who had matured in the Islamic daʿwa (the summons) and whose temperaments had been trained for that summons, on ⟪ar:یوم غدیر⟫ he turned them toward the inward call: ⟪ar-quote:مَن کُنتُ مَولاہُ فَھٰذاَ عَلِیٌّ مَولاَہُ⟫ — invoking the inner testimony ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫.

Thus ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illā Allāh is the word of the outward, Islamic summons, while the testimony of faith stands as its inner veil. The same correspondence is signaled in ⟪ar:سورۃ مزمل⟫, where God says:

> ⟪quran 73:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(He Alone is) the Lord of the east and the west, La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He). So take Him Alone as Wakîl (Disposer of your affairs) .*
> He is the Lord of the East and the West; there is no god but He, so take Him as your Guardian.
> — **Quran 73:9 (inline widget)**

In the numerology of the Arabic letters (ḥisāb al-jummal), the inner testimony ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illā huwa carries a numerical value of 110 — precisely the value of ⟪ar:علی⟫. On its inner reading the verse therefore intimates that ʿAlī is the Lord of the East and the West: take him as your Guardian. The outward call concerning God veils, and discloses to those prepared, the inward call concerning the Imām. Sijistānī, in *Kitāb al-Yanābīʿ*, draws out this same doubling of the shahāda: the outer formula conceals its inner pronoun, and to recognise the referent of *huwa* is to recognise the Imām who stands as the inward Lord of the East and the West.[^cite-20]

The Messenger of God (peace and blessings be upon him and his family) said: as long as my Umma continues to recite ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ and to discharge the duties and obligations of religion, the wrath of God will remain turned away from them. ## The Paradise of Islam and the Paradise of Faith

When the Originator, Most High (⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫, Mubdiʿ Taʿālā) brought the First Intellect (⟪ar:عقل اول⟫, al-ʿAql al-Awwal) into being, and the First Intellect uttered the divine unity, it did not pronounce ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illā Allāh but rather ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illā huwa. This is the very word borne witness to in the verse:

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah bears witness that La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), and the angels, and those having knowledge (also give this witness); (He is always) maintaining His creation in Justice. La ilah illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.*
> Allah, and the angels, and those endowed with knowledge, bear witness: there is no god but He. He is the Guardian of Justice. There is no god but He, the All-mighty, the All-wise.
> — **Quran 3:18 (inline widget)**

The word through which the First Intellect (⟪ar:عقل اول⟫) attained its secondary perfection (⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫, kamāl thānī), through which its glory and elevation were increased, is the very same ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫. Just so among the believers: they cannot be brought to full form until they enter the walāya of ʿAlī and the descendants of ʿAlī, and answer his inward summons.

By the word of the Holy Prophet (peace be upon him), the Muslims entered the Paradise of Submission (⟪ar:اسلام⟫). The sword of Islam has been withheld from them, yet there is no assurance that they will be sealed in ending with felicity (⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫) on the strength of this kalima alone. For they have not yet attained the speaking word (⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫) by which a noble form (⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫) can be constituted within them. That speaking word (⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫) is attained only when the meaning of ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illā huwa comes to life within the meaning of ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illā Allāh — that is, when deeds give birth to the inward word (⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫) through the covenant of the walāya of the Commander of the Faithful (⟪ar:ولایت امیر المومنین⟫), which alone secures ending with felicity (⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫). This is the line that runs through al-Muʾayyad's *Majālis*: the Muslim's final felicity is secured only by entering the covenant of walāya, since obedience to the outward law alone yields no guarantee of being sealed with the good ending.[^cite-22]

| لا الہ الا اللہ | لا الہ الا ھو |
| --- | --- |
| Outward summons | Inward summons |
| Primary perfection (kamāl awwal) | Secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) |

Within Islam there is only one rank, and no Muslim stands above any other Muslim. As the Messenger of God (peace be upon him) said: "All Muslims are brothers." That is, the Prophet established all Muslims as brothers upon a single plane — and this equality is the attribute of the outward. Yet within the inward there is a power that not only strengthens our outward beliefs but lifts us into ascending ranks. In the circle of faith there are degrees and gradations, and no believer stands equal in rank to another.

## The Emanation of Islam and Faith

The Messenger of God (⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫) was the highest of the veils, and from the letter ⟪ar:میم⟫ — the initial of his noble name ⟪ar:محمدؐ مصطفیٰ⟫ — Islam (⟪ar:اسلام⟫) was derived. The Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫), was the veil beneath his, and from the letter ⟪ar:عین⟫, the initial of his name, faith was brought forth. If the doubled letters within the names ⟪ar:علی⟫ and ⟪ar:محمد⟫ are reckoned into the abjad computation, the numerical sum of the name of Muḥammad equals the value of the emanators (⟪ar:منبعثات⟫, al-Munbaʿithāt — the Emanators) of Islam, and the numerical sum of the name of ʿAlī equals the value of the emanators (⟪ar:منبعثات⟫) of faith.

قُلُوبِكُمْ ۖ وَإِن تُطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ لَا يَلِتْكُم مِّنْ أَعْمَلِكُمْ شَيْـًٔا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ لَا يَحْزُنكَ ٱلَّذِينَ يُسَرِعُونَ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ آمَنَّا بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَلَمْ تُؤْمِن قُلُوبُهُمْ ۛ وَمِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَادُوا۟ ۛ سَمَّعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ سَمَّعُونَ لِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ لَمْ يَأْتُوكَ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ مَوَاضِعِهِۦ ۖ يَقُولُونَ إِنْ أُوتِيتُمْ هَذَا فَخُذُوهُ وَإِن لَّمْ تُؤْتَوْهُ فَٱحْذَرُوا۟ ۚ وَمَن يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ فِتْنَتَهُۥ فَلَن تَمْلِكَ لَهُۥ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ شَيْـًٔا ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَمْ يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ أَن يُطَهِّرَ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا خِزْىٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏آمَنَ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ جَهَدُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ لْيَقْضُوا۟ تَفَثَهُمْ وَلْيُوفُوا۟ نُذُورَهُمْ وَلْيَطَّوَّفُوا۟ بِٱلْبَيْتِ ٱلْعَتِيقِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ذَلِكَ ٱلْكِتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًۭى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏سَابِقُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ مَغْفِرَةٍۢ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَجَنَّةٍ عَرْضُهَا كَعَرْضِ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ أُعِدَّتْ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ ۚ ذَلِكَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ ذُو ٱلْفَضْلِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ فَضَرْبَ ٱلرِّقَابِ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَثْخَنتُمُوهُمْ فَشُدُّوا۟ ٱلْوَثَاقَ فَإِمَّا مَنًّۢا بَعْدُ وَإِمَّا فِدَآءً حَتَّىٰ تَضَعَ ٱلْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا ۚ ذَلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَآءُ ٱللَّهُ لَٱنتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ وَلَكِن لِّيَبْلُوَا۟ بَعْضَكُم بِبَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱلَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ فَلَن يُضِلَّ أَعْمَلَهُمْ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏فَبَدَأَ بِأَوْعِيَتِهِمْ قَبْلَ وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَخْرَجَهَا مِن وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ۚ كَذَلِكَ كِدْنَا لِيُوسُفَ ۖ مَا كَانَ لِيَأْخُذَ أَخَاهُ فِى دِينِ ٱلْمَلِكِ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ نَرْفَعُ دَرَجَتٍۢ مَّن نَّشَآءُ ۗ وَفَوْقَ كُلِّ ذِى عِلْمٍ عَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏فَمَا وَجَدْنَا فِيهَا غَيْرَ بَيْتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَدْ أَفْلَحَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ فِى صَلَاتِهِمْ خَشِعُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ ٱللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَوٰةِ فَعِلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُلْ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْنَا وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَإِسْمَعِيلَ وَإِسْحَقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَٱلْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَآ أُوتِىَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَٱلنَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُۥ مُسْلِمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أَنَا۠ بَشَرٌۭ مِّثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ أَنَّمَآ إِلَهُكُمْ إِلَهٌۭ وَحِدٌۭ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُشْرِكِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَفِرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏لِلْفُقَرَآءِ ٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُخْرِجُوا۟ مِن دِيَرِهِمْ وَأَمْوَلِهِمْ يَبْتَغُونَ فَضْلًۭا مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَرِضْوَنًۭا وَيَنصُرُونَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥٓ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلصَّدِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ تَبَوَّءُو ٱلدَّارَ وَٱلْإِيمَنَ مِن قَبْلِهِمْ يُحِبُّونَ مَنْ هَاجَرَ إِلَيْهِمْ وَلَا يَجِدُونَ فِى صُدُورِهِمْ حَاجَةًۭ مِّمَّآ أُوتُوا۟ وَيُؤْثِرُونَ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ وَلَوْ كَانَ بِهِمْ خَصَاصَةٌۭ ۚ وَمَن يُوقَ شُحَّ نَفْسِهِۦ فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏لَّا يَسْتَوِى ٱلْقَعِدُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ غَيْرُ أُو۟لِى ٱلضَّرَرِ وَٱلْمُجَهِدُونَ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ بِأَمْوَلِهِمْ وَأَنفُسِهِمْ ۚ فَضَّلَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْمُجَهِدِينَ بِأَمْوَلِهِمْ وَأَنفُسِهِمْ عَلَى ٱلْقَعِدِينَ دَرَجَةًۭ ۚ وَكُلًّۭا وَعَدَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ ۚ وَفَضَّلَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْمُجَهِدِينَ عَلَى ٱلْقَعِدِينَ أَجْرًا عَظِيمًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏لِّلَّهِ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَإِن تُبْدُوا۟ مَا فِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ أَوْ تُخْفُوهُ يُحَاسِبْكُم بِهِ ٱللَّهُ ۖ فَيَغْفِرُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَن يَشَآءُ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏مَن كَفَرَ بِٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ إِيمَنِهِۦٓ إِلَّا مَنْ أُكْرِهَ وَقَلْبُهُۥ مُطْمَىِٕنٌّۢ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ وَلَكِن مَّن شَرَحَ بِٱلْكُفْرِ صَدْرًۭا فَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏هُمْ دَرَجَتٌ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ بَصِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَأَنِ ٱسْتَغْفِرُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ ثُمَّ تُوبُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ يُمَتِّعْكُم مَّتَعًا حَسَنًا إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّۭى وَيُؤْتِ كُلَّ ذِى فَضْلٍۢ فَضْلَهُۥ ۖ وَإِن تَوَلَّوْا۟ فَإِنِّىٓ أَخَافُ عَلَيْكُمْ عَذَابَ يَوْمٍۢ كَبِيرٍ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَأَنَّ ٱلْمَسَجِدَ لِلَّهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا۟ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ أَحَدًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخُوضُونَ فِىٓ آيَتِنَا فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦ ۚ وَإِمَّا يُنسِيَنَّكَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَلَا تَقْعُدْ بَعْدَ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَرَبُّكَ أَعْلَمُ بِمَن فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَلَقَدْ فَضَّلْنَا بَعْضَ ٱلنَّبِيِّنَ عَلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ ۖ وَآتَيْنَا دَاوُۥدَ زَبُورًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقَدِمْنَآ إِلَىٰ مَا عَمِلُوا۟ مِنْ عَمَلٍۢ فَجَعَلْنَهُ هَبَآءًۭ مَّنثُورًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنْ إِذَا سَمِعْتُمْ آيَتِ ٱللَّهِ يُكْفَرُ بِهَا وَيُسْتَهْزَأُ بِهَا فَلَا تَقْعُدُوا۟ مَعَهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّكُمْ إِذًۭا مِّثْلُهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ جَامِعُ ٱلْمُنَفِقِينَ وَٱلْكَفِرِينَ فِى جَهَنَّمَ جَمِيعًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيُؤْمِن وَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيَكْفُرْ ۚ إِنَّآ أَعْتَدْنَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ نَارًا أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ سُرَادِقُهَا ۚ وَإِن يَسْتَغِيثُوا۟ يُغَاثُوا۟ بِمَآءٍۢ كَٱلْمُهْلِ يَشْوِى ٱلْوُجُوهَ ۚ بِئْسَ ٱلشَّرَابُ وَسَآءَتْ مُرْتَفَقًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْبَصَرَ وَٱلْفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَانَ عَنْهُ مَسْـُٔولًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَمْشِ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَن يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَآ أَبْصَرُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَكِن ظَنَنتُمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَا لَكُمْ أَلَّا تُنفِقُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَلِلَّهِ مِيرَثُ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ ۚ لَا يَسْتَوِى مِنكُم مَّنْ أَنفَقَ مِن قَبْلِ ٱلْفَتْحِ وَقَتَلَ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَعْظَمُ دَرَجَةًۭ مِّنَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنفَقُوا۟ مِنۢ بَعْدُ وَقَتَلُوا۟ ۚ وَكُلًّۭا وَعَدَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَا يُؤْمِنُ أَكْثَرُهُم بِٱللَّهِ إِلَّا وَهُم مُّشْرِكُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱقْصِدْ فِى مَشْيِكَ وَٱغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلْأَصْوَتِ لَصَوْتُ ٱلْحَمِيرِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلسَّبِقُونَ ٱلسَّبِقُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلْمُقَرَّبُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلسَّبِقُونَ ٱلْأَوَّلُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ وَٱلْأَنصَارِ وَٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّبَعُوهُم بِإِحْسَنٍۢ رَّضِىَ ٱللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا۟ عَنْهُ وَأَعَدَّ لَهُمْ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى تَحْتَهَا ٱلْأَنْهَرُ خَلِدِينَ فِيهَآ أَبَدًۭا ۚ ذَلِكَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وُجُوهٌۭ يَوْمَئِذٍ خَشِعَةٌ ‎ ‏عَامِلَةٌۭ نَّاصِبَةٌۭ ‎ ‏تَصْلَىٰ نَارًا حَامِيَةًۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَمُنُّونَ عَلَيْكَ أَنْ أَسْلَمُوا۟ ۖ قُل لَّا تَمُنُّوا۟ عَلَىَّ إِسْلَمَكُم ۖ بَلِ ٱللَّهُ يَمُنُّ عَلَيْكُمْ أَنْ هَدَىٰكُمْ لِلْإِيمَنِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَقُولُوا۟ قَوْلًۭا سَدِيدًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱرْكَعُوا۟ وَٱسْجُدُوا۟ وَٱعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ وَٱفْعَلُوا۟ ٱلْخَيْرَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ آمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ مِن قَبْلُ ۚ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَلًۢا بَعِيدًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ فَٱغْسِلُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْمَرَافِقِ وَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِرُئُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُمْ جُنُبًۭا فَٱطَّهَّرُوا۟ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَآءَ أَحَدٌۭ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَمَسْتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا۟ مَآءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا۟ صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا فَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُم مِّنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُم مِّنْ حَرَجٍۢ وَلَكِن يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجَلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ زَحْفًۭا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلْأَدْبَارَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا نُودِىَ لِلصَّلَوٰةِ مِن يَوْمِ ٱلْجُمُعَةِ فَٱسْعَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَتَطْمَىِٕنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ تَطْمَىِٕنُّ ٱلْقُلُوبُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَهَاجَرُوا۟ وَجَهَدُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ بِأَمْوَلِهِمْ وَأَنفُسِهِمْ أَعْظَمُ دَرَجَةً عِندَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْفَآئِزُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ أُحِلَّ لَكُمُ ٱلطَّيِّبَتُ ۖ وَطَعَامُ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ حِلٌّۭ لَّكُمْ وَطَعَامُكُمْ حِلٌّۭ لَّهُمْ ۖ وَٱلْمُحْصَنَتُ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنَتِ وَٱلْمُحْصَنَتُ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِن قَبْلِكُمْ إِذَآ آتَيْتُمُوهُنَّ أُجُورَهُنَّ مُحْصِنِينَ غَيْرَ مُسَفِحِينَ وَلَا مُتَّخِذِىٓ أَخْدَانٍۢ ۗ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ فَقَدْ حَبِطَ عَمَلُهُۥ وَهُوَ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ مِنَ ٱلْخَسِرِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ نَخْتِمُ عَلَىٰٓ أَفْوَهِهِمْ وَتُكَلِّمُنَآ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَتَشْهَدُ أَرْجُلُهُم بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
