## Special and General Prophethood

There are two types of Prophethood: one is a special message and the other is a general message.

### General Prophethood: The Inner Intellect

Prophethood in its general aspect concerns the physical and rational faculties. When Allah created the human form ⟪ar:صورۃ انسانیۃ⟫, He endowed it with all the organs necessary for its fulfillment. Through these organs, the human soul fulfills its physical needs and performs all the functions of animal life. Yet unlike the beasts, Allah created in man another faculty and essence—what is called intellect (ʿaql). It is by means of this intellect that he distinguishes between good and bad, truth and falsehood. With this intellect, he comes to understand the ⟪ar:أمرو نواھی⟫commands and prohibitions, and learns to obey his parents, teachers, and mentors. This intellect constitutes his first prophethood.[^cite-1]

### Special Prophethood: The External Guide

The Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.a.) is the true guide whom Allah has granted knowledge of the unseen and who illuminates the path to the Hereafter for the salvation of the soul. The prophets of each age, their successors, and the Imams of all times are bearers of this special mission. General prophethood (intellect) is necessary as a foundation for accepting special prophethood. A person who lacks general prophethood—the innate intellect—is incapable of receiving special prophethood, which is why the ⟪ar:شریعت محمدی علیہ السلام⟫Shariah of Muhammad does not apply to the insane or minor children.

### The Connection between First and Second Prophethood

By means of the natural and eternal intellect, man understands his worldly gains and losses and knows how to sustain his material life—he eats, drinks, satisfies his hunger, and provides himself with comforts. In such a state, he has no concern for the Hereafter, for reward or punishment. When he follows the second prophet—the spiritual guide—his afterlife becomes perfected. He comes to know his ultimate end and becomes aware of it through the promise of reward. The second prophet restrains the first prophet from evil and adorns his soul with knowledge. **As long as the first messenger is not connected to the second Prophet, he remains unobligated by the Shariah and thus is deprived of its reward.**

## Why Are Messengers Necessary?

⟪ar:حضرت آدم علیہ السلام⟫Prophethood has continued from the time of Prophet Adam onwards and will endure as long as descendants remain upon the earth. ⟪ar:رسالت اولہ (عقل)⟫Every human body exists and develops until consciousness enters through childhood; this primitive intellect imparts to the child an understanding of harm and benefit and teaches it to distinguish and differentiate.

Therefore, it is necessary for the person possessed of this intellect to exercise careful thought and imagination, to reflect deeply upon his end and the Hereafter, to remain steadfast and faithful in dignity, to conceal the secrets of the invitation (daʿwa), to seek knowledge and etiquette, to adopt ⟪ar:ورع⟫piety and ⟪ar:زہد⟫asceticism in the world, to honor his trusts, to maintain silence, and to labor with constancy. It is equally necessary for him to avoid the vile actions that contradict these virtues.

Yet the intellect alone is incapable of comprehending all these matters. It is impossible for it to grasp them through its own strength and ability. Therefore, man requires a guide and mentor—one who can tell him what pleases Allah and what provokes His anger. This 'guide' who explains these matters is the second Messenger, whom Allah has sent and chosen to perfect the fate of His servants. By virtue of his special capacities and pure thoughts, He has distinguished him from the common people, made him capable of receiving His revelation, and manifested His support for him.[^cite-2]

Man exists between two states. On one side stands the spiritual intellect, which is luminous; on the other, the lower world, which is dark and oppressive. There exists within him an evil force called the ⟪ar:قوت شھوانیۃ و غضبیۃ⟫appetitive and irascible powers. The demand of the body and physicality is such that it cannot survive without food and without the satisfaction of desires. If man becomes entirely inclined toward the ⟪ar:نفس شہوانیۃ غضبیۃ⟫appetitive and irascible soul—concerned only with eating, drinking, and indulgence, like cattle—then his self overcomes his intellect and he becomes worldly. He grows indifferent to religion and becomes engrossed in fulfilling his own desires. And the man who is ⟪ar:اشرف المخلوقات⟫the noblest of creatures, becomes worse than ⟪ar:أَشَرُّ المَخلُوقَات⟫the most ignoble of creatures, descending so far that he is wholly devoid of good human functions.

In such circumstances, Divine wisdom deemed it fitting to give the first prophet (intellect) a guide who would teach him that another realm exists beyond the physical world—one free from impurities. This guide should instruct him, cultivate his character, and impose upon him as a teacher all things necessary and requisite for the welfare and salvation of the intellect. ⟪ar:أمر ونواھی⟫He should make him aware of what is permissible and what is forbidden, bidding him to observe the command of the Shariah strictly, and ⟪ar:نواھی⟫restraining him from unlawful acts through intimidation and preventing him from committing forbidden deeds through severe punishments. Thus the end of this man of intellect shall be perfected, and he shall not ⟪ar:احسن تقویم⟫descend from the finest mold to ⟪ar:اسفل السافلین⟫the lowest of the low. There is another realm for man besides this one, concerning which the Prophet (s.a.w.a.) said:

> ⟪quran 6:48⟫

>
> *And We send not the Messengers but as givers of glad tidings and as warners. So whosoever believes and does righteous good deeds, upon such shall come no fear, nor shall they grieve.*

We do not send the apostles except to give good news and warn. Whoever believes and reforms, there shall be no fear for them, nor shall they grieve.

The messengers are the bearers of glad tidings concerning the Hereafter and warners against the punishment of Hell. Both the glad tidings and the warning relate to the reward or punishment of deeds, and both are bound to the Hereafter.

*Interpretation:* The glad tidings of the Messenger of Allah were in truth the glad tidings of ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫Ali to the believers, and his warning was to caution the disbelievers and the hypocrites against Ali—for on the Day of Resurrection, it is Ali who will distinguish between truth and falsehood.

## Why Are Successors Necessary?

When the truth and legitimacy of the first and second prophets is established, a further question arises: what is the necessity of appointing successors (awṣiyāʾ)? In this matter, certain people claim: "We have accepted the Messenger of Allah and possess Allah's commandments in the form of the Qur'an. Why then do we need a successor (waṣī)? We can believe in the commandments of the Qur'an, in Allah, and in the Last Day. There is no need for a successor for this." Allah says in this regard:

> ⟪quran 2:8⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَقُولُ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَبِٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ وَمَا هُم بِمُؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *And of mankind, there are some (hypocrites) who say: "We believe in Allah and the Last Day" while in fact they believe not.*

There are some who say, "We believe in Allah and the Last Day," but they are not believers.

Those who make such claims do not affirm either of these truths, for they imagine that knowledge of God is concentrated in human nature itself, relying upon their own intellect and understanding. Yet such claimants fail to account for a fundamental reality: even natural human processes cannot be performed by a child unless he is taught by his parents or teachers. This is evident when a human child is reared among animals in the forest. He learns to make sounds like animals, but he does not know how to speak with ⟪ar:مافی الضمیر⟫words expressive of inner meaning, as humans do. If man cannot function in such a natural matter without instruction, how can he possibly do so without a guide to Allah?

Thus it becomes clear that without the Messenger of Allah, it is impossible to know Allah. In the same manner, to understand all matters of religion, its principles, and the secrets of the Qur'an, there is need for a guide and mentor who stands in relation to the people as the Messenger does—as an intermediary between Allah and His servants. This is the successor (waṣī) of the Messenger, who after the Prophet's earthly life explains the meaning of the Messenger's utterances and interprets the inner significance of what he has conveyed. The Messenger conveys the commands: "Do such and such!" The successor explains the meaning and the secrets behind this matter of Shariah—*why* it should be done and *what* benefit will accrue from it in the Hereafter. In other words, the Messenger expounds the 'words,' while the successor expounds the 'meaning' of those words—which is always the true purpose behind the words.[^cite-3]

All the principles of faith begin with knowledge of the Oneness of Allah (tawhīd). This knowledge must be free from analogy (qiyās)—that is, divested of all attributes ascribed to the Creator and freed from the innovations of those who ascribe spirituality to Him or imagine Him composed of water and clay. The attributes of the physical person must be purified away. We must recognize that whatever arises in our imagination is not Allah, and that our most subtle thoughts, like our outward forms, are created things, brought into being as we were. The Creator, who transcends our imagination, is the creator of these spiritual and physical limitations, and they have nothing whatsoever to do with Him. This is the foundational principle of religion—by its presence religion is established and sustained, and by its decline religion is destroyed and nullified. The Messenger establishes his successor for the clarification and elucidation of all these truths.

If the Book of Allah (the Qur'an) could explain its own meaning, the word of Allah would be meaningless.

> ⟪quran 4:83⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا جَآءَهُمْ أَمْرٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْأَمْنِ أَوِ ٱلْخَوْفِ أَذَاعُوا۟ بِهِۦ ۖ وَلَوْ رَدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ وَإِلَىٰٓ أُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنْهُمْ لَعَلِمَهُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَنۢبِطُونَهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ وَلَوْلَا فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَتُهُۥ لَٱتَّبَعْتُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
>
> *When there comes to them some matter touching (public) safety or fear, they make it known (among the people), if only they had referred it to the Messenger or to those charged with authority among them, the proper investigators would have understood it from them (directly). Had it not been for the Grace and Mercy of Allah upon you, you would have followed Shaitan (Satan), save a few of you.*

And when there comes to them some news touching about security or fear, they broadcast it; and if they had relayed it to the Messenger and to those in authority among them, those among them who are investigators would have investigated it; and had it not been for the grace of Allah upon you and His mercy, you would have followed Satan, except a few.

Were ⟪ar:'حسبنا کتاب اللہ'⟫the claim of some that "the Qur'an suffices us" correct, Umar would not have depended upon Ali for every ruling and decision. ⟪ar:لو لا علی لھلک عمر⟫"Had there been no Ali, Umar would have perished." Those who usurp the positions of the Imams on the basis of outward piety and seeming righteousness resemble one who displays wealth outwardly while being poor inwardly. When he himself becomes blind, he claims to see. Such a person deceives only himself, as Allah says:

> ⟪quran 2:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يُخَدِعُونَ ٱللَّهَ وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَمَا يَخْدَعُونَ إِلَّآ أَنفُسَهُمْ وَمَا يَشْعُرُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *They (think to) deceive Allah and those who believe, while they only deceive themselves, and perceive (it) not!*

They seek to deceive Allah and those who believe, but they deceive themselves, and they do not perceive.

⟪ar:'الیوم الاخر'⟫The Day of Resurrection and the Day of Judgment ⟪ar:ذات قائم القیامۃ علی ذکرہ السلام⟫refer to the completion of the Days of Allah, of which it has been said:

> ⟪quran 45:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ يَغْفِرُوا۟ لِلَّذِينَ لَا يَرْجُونَ أَيَّامَ ٱللَّهِ لِيَجْزِىَ قَوْمًۢا بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH) to the believers to forgive those who (harm them and) hope not for the Days of Allah (i.e. His Recompense), that He may recompense people according to what they have earned (i.e. to punish these disbelievers, who harm the believers).*

Say to those who believe that forgive those who do not expect the days of Allah, so that He may recompense a people for what they used to earn.

In this verse, Allah declares that ⟪ar:'أیام اللہ'⟫He will reward people for their deeds. If the days were merely the lifeless days and nights of the year, how could they be instruments of reward or punishment? This shows that "days" (ayyām) here carries special meanings. The days refer to the Imams, and ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫the Qa'im (the Standing One) is the seal and culmination of those days. In his hands will be the first judgment (the purification of religion) and then the final reckoning. First comes a disturbance in religion, and then comes the reckoning and just recompense.

## Why Connection to the Second Prophet Is Difficult

⟪ar:رسول اول⟫The light of the intellect can benefit only one who accepts the law of ⟪ar:رسول ثانی⟫the intellect and follows its commandments. In general, the soul (nafs) resists human law because it has been accustomed to following the comfort and desires of the physical self. It recoils from ⟪ar:رسول علیہ السلام⟫things which the ⟪ar:نفس امارۃ بالسوء⟫self inciting to evil forbids and keeps away from what appears good to it.

⟪ar:رسالت ثانی⟫The example is like fire that illuminates the world and warms the atmosphere. Yet fire is first kindled in wood, and only when the 'combustible material' ignites and burns does one benefit from it. In the same way, ⟪ar:رسول علیہ السلام⟫the messengers illuminate and ignite the natural intellect through the teachings and commandments of their law. Without messengers, man cannot attain the light of guidance on his own. Thus the Ummah is one body as a whole, and the Messenger is the heart of that body. The companions are its senses and instruments. The inner knowledge of the Messenger (s.a.w.a.) is that of the ⟪ar:'روح'⟫spirit of the body, and the knowledge of the Messenger's successors comprises his limbs, muscles, and faculties that obey his commands and make the believers worthy of salvation in the Hereafter.

## Spiritual Parents and Their Obedience

Allah the Most High has fashioned the subtle essence of the human soul upon the dense essence of the body so that it may be synthesized from both ⟪ar:محسوسات⟫sensory perceptions and ⟪ar:معقولات⟫rational concepts. That is: one is the body of man, which is visible and tangible; the other is the soul of man, which is neither visible nor tangible. These two components cannot develop without the physical and spiritual parents of man.

### Physical and Spiritual Parents

Just as the birth of man's body is possible only through his parents and through bodily reproduction, so too must he require spiritual parents for his spiritual perfection. Man's physical father is the giver of bodily creation, and his mother is the receiver and nurturer thereof. Similarly, the spiritual father, like the physical father, is the giver of the soul, and the spiritual mother, like the physical mother, is the receiver and nurturer of the soul. Those in the position of spiritual father for the believer's soul are the religious fathers, who stand in the position of ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و سلم⟫the Messenger of Allah. Those in the position of spiritual mother for the believer's soul are the religious mothers, who are the successors (awṣiyāʾ) of the Messenger, embodied in ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫the Commander of the Faithful, Ali son of Abū Tālib.[^cite-4]

⟪ar:تنزیل⟫The sperm of the father is an analogy for the spiritual creation of the believer, and the sperm of ⟪ar:تاویل⟫the prophet's successor is an analogy for the mother's role in his creation. By the combination of these two spiritual seeds, a spiritual creation can be adorned for the spiritual realm, just as the combination of the two bodily seeds creates a physical being for the material world. This reality is attested to by the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.a.): "I and you, O Ali, are the father and mother of the believers." This is confirmed by Allah's words:

> ⟪quran 33:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلنَّبِىُّ أَوْلَىٰ بِٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ مِنْ أَنفُسِهِمْ ۖ وَأَزْوَجُهُۥٓ أُمَّهَتُهُمْ ۗ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَرْحَامِ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلَىٰ بِبَعْضٍۢ فِى كِتَبِ ٱللَّهِ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ إِلَّآ أَن تَفْعَلُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآئِكُم مَّعْرُوفًۭا ۚ كَانَ ذَلِكَ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ مَسْطُورًۭا ‎⟫
>
> *The Prophet is closer to the believers than their ownselves, and his wives are their (believers') mothers (as regards respect and marriage). And blood relations among each other have closer personal ties in the Decree of Allah (regarding inheritance) than (the brotherhood of) the believers and the Muhajirun (emigrants from Makkah, etc.), except that you do kindness to those brothers (when the Prophet PBUH joined them in brotherhood ties). This has been written in the (Allah's Book of Divine) Decrees (Al-Lauh Al-Mahfuz).*

The prophets have a greater right to the believers than their own selves, and their wives are as if they were their mothers, and their relatives are more related to one another in the Book of Allah than the believers and the emigrants, except that you should treat your friends kindly.

### Heaven and Earth

From the interpretation, we know that every prophet's successor is his wife (in the spiritual sense). When the Qur'an declares the successor of the Prophet to be the mother of the believers, it necessarily implies that the Prophet became the father of the believers. Thus it is said: "The earth is your mother, and she is good to you." In this hadith, the Prophet refers to the Wasi as the mother of the earth, because we now understand that he is the mother ⟪ar:اوصیاء علیہم السلام⟫of the believers. Heaven waters the earth through rain and starlight; the earth receives this water and nourishes minerals, plants, and animals. Thus the Qur'an says:

> ⟪quran 22:5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ إِن كُنتُمْ فِى رَيْبٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْبَعْثِ فَإِنَّا خَلَقْنَكُم مِّن تُرَابٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن نُّطْفَةٍۢ ثُمَّ مِنْ عَلَقَةٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن مُّضْغَةٍۢ مُّخَلَّقَةٍۢ وَغَيْرِ مُخَلَّقَةٍۢ لِّنُبَيِّنَ لَكُمْ ۚ وَنُقِرُّ فِى ٱلْأَرْحَامِ مَا نَشَآءُ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّۭى ثُمَّ نُخْرِجُكُمْ طِفْلًۭا ثُمَّ لِتَبْلُغُوٓا۟ أَشُدَّكُمْ ۖ وَمِنكُم مَّن يُتَوَفَّىٰ وَمِنكُم مَّن يُرَدُّ إِلَىٰٓ أَرْذَلِ ٱلْعُمُرِ لِكَيْلَا يَعْلَمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ عِلْمٍۢ شَيْـًۭٔا ۚ وَتَرَى ٱلْأَرْضَ هَامِدَةًۭ فَإِذَآ أَنزَلْنَا عَلَيْهَا ٱلْمَآءَ ٱهْتَزَّتْ وَرَبَتْ وَأَنۢبَتَتْ مِن كُلِّ زَوْجٍۭ بَهِيجٍۢ ‎⟫
>
> *O mankind! If you are in doubt about the Resurrection, then verily! We have created you (i.e. Adam) from dust, then from a Nutfah (mixed drops of male and female sexual discharge i.e. offspring of Adam), then from a clot (a piece of thick coagulated blood) then from a little lump of flesh, some formed and some unformed (miscarriage), that We may make (it) clear to you (i.e. to show you Our Power and Ability to do what We will). And We cause whom We will to remain in the wombs for an appointed term, then We bring you out as infants, then (give you growth) that you may reach your age of full strength. And among you there is he who dies (young), and among you there is he who is brought back to the miserable old age, so that he knows nothing after having known. And you see the earth barren, but when We send down water (rain) on it, it is stirred (to life), it swells and puts forth every lovely kind (of growth).*

O mankind! If you are in doubt about the resurrection, then We created you from dust, then from a drop of blood, then from a clot, then from a lump of flesh, a moulded figure, and without a map, that We may show unto you; and We lodge in the mercy whomsoever We will for a specified term; then We bring you forth as a child, and that you may grow up. Behold, there are some of you who die, and of you are some who are brought up to age, so that they become a state of ignorance, and you see the earth barren, and when We send down water upon it, it becomes fresh, and every kind of noble vegetation grows.

That is, ⟪ar:تنزیل⟫the sustenance and survival of the speaker (Nāṭiq) stands in the position of heaven and father, while ⟪ar:تاویل⟫the foundation (Asās) stands in the position of earth and mother. The same principle holds for rainfall. ⟪ar:تنزیل⟫Every kind of ⟪ar:تاویل⟫vegetation grows from the earth. Therefore, the Messenger (Nāṭiq) invites the entire world to outward action, while the Interpreter (Asās) reveals to all the reality and inner meaning of this outward action, through which a person is spiritually nurtured. The earth cannot produce anything until heaven sends down water upon it; heaven cannot bring forth a pair without the earth. ⟪ar:تاویل⟫That is, ⟪ar:تنزیل⟫the human soul can be nurtured through both. In another place, Allah says:

> ⟪quran 43:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۢ بِقَدَرٍۢ فَأَنشَرْنَا بِهِۦ بَلْدَةًۭ مَّيْتًۭا ۚ كَذَلِكَ تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *And Who sends down water (rain) from the sky in due measure. Then We revive a dead land therewith, and even so you will be brought forth (from the dead),*

He who sends down water from the sky in a measured manner, and We revive a dead land with it. Likewise, you will be resurrected.

### The Body and Soul of the Law

⟪ar:تنزیل⟫The body is the analogue and ⟪ar:تاویل⟫the soul is the referent. Physical death occurs when the soul separates from the body. The manifestation of the Shariah stands in the place of the body, and its esoteric meaning stands in the place of the soul. If the Shariah is practiced only in its outward aspect, it is like a corpse—for it lacks a soul. If, conversely, it is only interpreted without practice, it is like a soul without a body, which cannot accomplish anything. ⟪ar:تنزیل اور تاویل،⟫Unless these two are combined, spiritual pleasures cannot be attained, just as physical pleasures cannot be obtained without the union of soul and body.[^cite-5]

### The Necessity of the Imam

Since it has been established that there is no alternative to heaven and earth for the things born in the physical world, and that human physical birth is impossible without physical parents, it is necessary and obligatory that believers in every age have spiritual parents. People must identify their spiritual parents so that they do not become spiritual orphans. One who knows the Imam is obliged to obey him while present in his company; should he be absent, he is obliged to obey the person whom the Imam (a.s.) has appointed in his place as his representative. The Qur'an confirms this:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
>
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority. (And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day. That is better and more suitable for final determination.*

O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger and those in authority among you. And if you dispute among yourselves about anything, refer it to Allah and His Messenger, if you believe in Allah and the Last Day. That is better and better in the end.

If the Imams were necessary only for worldly affairs, then religion would be useless and vain. Allah is too exalted to render meaningless that which His beloved Prophets labored so greatly to promote—a religion that is the finest of all things. If, conversely, the Imam were necessary only for religion without regard to worldly order, then the affairs of the world would fall into chaos and disorder. It would be impossible for Allah to establish the Imam as the leader of the noble (religion) while preventing him from ordering the inferior (the world). Thus it has been demonstrated that the Imam is necessary both for religion and for the world.

## The Proof of the Imam's Existence

⟪ar:عنایت الٰہیۃ⟫Humanity alone among all animals was granted an innate intellect. When Allah bestowed this wisdom upon man, it was necessary that He send a teacher for this sacred gift and grace, so that he could nurture his natural intellect. Just as Allah gave animals a soul with which to eat and drink, and provided the elements, the stars, and the heavens to grow vegetation that animals might be nourished and their bodies constantly sustained, so too in the wisdom and justice of the Wise One, it is not fitting that He create a needy being without creating what fulfills that need.

When Allah bestows the innate intellect upon only one species of animal (mankind), it is incumbent upon the Divine Wisdom that the sustenance of this intellect—which is knowledge—be provided to only one being. Had this been outward knowledge, all people could have acquired it through effort and pursuit. Yet since this inner knowledge is given to only one being, it is necessary that this one person possess the status of a teacher of this knowledge. Allah says:

> ⟪quran 7:63⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَوَعَجِبْتُمْ أَن جَآءَكُمْ ذِكْرٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ عَلَىٰ رَجُلٍۢ مِّنكُمْ لِيُنذِرَكُمْ وَلِتَتَّقُوا۟ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تُرْحَمُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *"Do you wonder that there has come to you a Reminder from your Lord through a man from amongst you, that he may warn you, so that you may fear Allah and that you may receive (His) Mercy?" *

Do you wonder that a reminder has come to you from your Lord through a man from among yourselves, so that he may warn you, and that you may be Godwary, and that you may be praised?

In this verse, Allah employs the singular word ⟪ar:رجل⟫'man,' not the plural ⟪ar:رجال⟫'men.' This proves that the word of wisdom and exhortation has been bestowed upon only one person. He is the sole individual who is the Prophet of his time, the successor of his time, and the Imam in all times. So long as this world exists and for as long as it shall exist, mankind will never be without this one person. No human being can replace this station or claim its status.[^cite-6]

*Interpretation:* The word "men" (rijāl) also alludes to this reality. For in the station of elderhood and maturity (kuhūlīya), the ⟪ar:داعی منفرد⟫solitary Summoner (Dāʿī) possesses the capacity to create a man like himself in the matter of the summons (daʿwa) and to appoint him as his successor. This successor performs his function by establishing witnesses through the Dāʿī's authority.

An example of this reality is found in nature: if, among many cows, one became more powerful, it could not protect its fellow cows. It would be impossible for it to become a shepherd example over these cattle, protecting them from other beasts and bringing them to pasture in time and back again. This can only be done by a human being who is not like a cow but wiser than they, understanding their good and bad better than they. Similarly, though we humans possess intellect, our intellects lack the 'spiritual knowledge' that is intrinsically bestowed upon an Imam. Therefore, we are ignorant and helpless compared to the Imam, just as we are ignorant compared to humans relative to cattle. We have need of his knowledge for the perfection of our hereafter. Explaining the superiority of the Imam over mankind, Allah says:

> ⟪quran 3:110⟫
> ⟪ar:‏كُنتُمْ خَيْرَ أُمَّةٍ أُخْرِجَتْ لِلنَّاسِ تَأْمُرُونَ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَتَنْهَوْنَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ وَتُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ ۗ وَلَوْ آمَنَ أَهْلُ ٱلْكِتَبِ لَكَانَ خَيْرًۭا لَّهُم ۚ مِّنْهُمُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ وَأَكْثَرُهُمُ ٱلْفَسِقُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *You [true believers in Islamic Monotheism, and real followers of Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Sunnah (legal ways, etc.)] are the best of peoples ever raised up for mankind; you enjoin Al-Ma'ruf (i.e. Islamic Monotheism and all that Islam has ordained) and forbid Al-Munkar (polytheism, disbelief and all that Islam has forbidden), and you believe in Allah. And had the people of the Scripture (Jews and Christians) believed, it would have been better for them; among them are some who have faith, but most of them are Al-Fasiqun (disobedient to Allah - and rebellious against Allah's Command).*

You are the best of all the nations that have been sent down for mankind, enjoining what is right and forbidding what is wrong, and believing in God. Had the People of the Book believed, it would have been better for them. Some of them are believers, but most of them are transgressors.

This ⟪ar:خَیرَ اُمَّتٍ⟫verse refers to the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt (a.s.), who exhort to good and forbid evil. It is not said here ⟪ar:خَیرَ اُمَّتٍ،⟫on the basis of the Imams' outward appearance, but rather because of their unparalleled character and unmistakable knowledge. It has been made clear that every Imam surpasses, excels, and is superior to the community (Ummah) of his time. The meaning of this verse, ⟪ar:کُنتُم ،⟫though couched in the past tense, is that you have been the best nation created for your own time—not only in this age, but in every age past and future.

*Interpretation:* The word "best" (khair) always refers to elevation of rank and status. Thus the Prophet (s.a.w.a.) referred to the station of the wilayat (guardianship) of Mawlānā Ali and stated that he holds the rank of successor: "Hasan and Husayn are the Imams of truth whether standing or sitting, and their father is better than both of them."

## Who Has the Right to Choose the Imam?

The belief of certain ⟪ar:عوا الناس⟫groups is that they themselves have the right to choose the Imam. They deny that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah appointed anyone after him as his successor and caliph, one capable of performing the duties of the Imamate. Another section of the public believes that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫he indicated a person (⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr) without naming him explicitly. Their argument is that he ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫appointed ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr to lead the prayer, and since prayer is intimately connected to Zakat, it should be clear that all Muslims were to pay their Zakat to ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr after the Messenger of Allah.

⟪ar:فرقہ المرحبہ⟫The Murji'a school holds that it is incumbent upon the people to appoint a person of knowledge and virtue so that he may employ wisdom in matters of Shariah and issue rulings among the people according to the Book of Allah and the Sunnah of the Prophet. If a matter is not resolved in the Book or the Sunnah, he should exercise his own opinion. Obedience to such an Imam is obligatory as long as he himself does not disobey Allah. When he does disobey, it is not obligatory for the people to obey him, nor do they have the right to remove him from the seat of Imamate and appoint another.

⟪ar:فرقہ متعزلیہ⟫The Muʿtazila maintain that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah did not indicate any person or nominate any person for Imamate. The people have the right to appoint anyone as Imam after the Prophet. Thus the people ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫elected Abū Bakr as Imam after the Prophet's death.

⟪ar:فرقہ خوارج⟫The Khārijis say: "We have no knowledge of this matter, nor have we received any clear report as to whether the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.a.) issued a ruling regarding the position of Imamate." Yet we believe there must be an Imam to issue divine commands among God's servants. In view of this necessity, we ourselves should choose our own Imam.

---

⟪ar:سیدنا قاضی نعمان اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫The illustrious Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān, may Allah elevate his station, responds to those who say "⟪ar:رسول اللہ(ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah did not appoint anyone as Imam of his Ummah after me," declaring that such a denial cannot be accepted by us or by you under any circumstances. For one who denies the occurrence of an event has no standing as a witness to it. A valid witness must be one who confirms the event and says "I myself witnessed it." Similarly, you have denied the existence of the Imam and rejected what you dislike. But one who believes in the Imam's existence is more worthy that his testimony be accepted; it is more fitting that he serve as a witness among you. [^cite-7]

⟪ar:مقام غدیر خم⟫You have at the ⟪ar-quote:من کنت مولاہ فھذا علی مولاہ⟫gathering at Ghadīr Khum already announced: ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫"Whomsoever I am the master of, this Ali is the master of." I have named my successor, whom you too declare, and witnesses from your own people testify to, and which is also written in your books. The testimony of those who witnessed the Messenger of Allah making this announcement—those who saw and heard it—is better than the testimony of those who say "We neither saw nor heard any such announcement." If the choice of an Imam were a matter of popular election, the Messenger of Allah would have announced to the entire assembly: "Choose a suitable Imam for yourselves after me!" Instead, he ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫declared regarding Ali: ⟪ar-quote:اللھم وال من والاہ و عاد من عاداہ و نصر من نصرہ و خزل من خزلہ⟫"O Allah, befriend whosoever befriends him, be an enemy to whosoever opposes him, support whosoever supports him, and humiliate whosoever humiliates him."

The second argument: If, as you claim, it is accepted that the Imam and Caliph are chosen by the people, then such an Imam must be obedient to the people, for he attained this position and became master of it only through their choice. According to this view, the one who chooses the Imam is the true Imam, and the one chosen is merely one of his functionaries. The people would then have the right to remove him whenever they wish and elect another Imam in his stead. This cannot be true, for if it were permissible for people to choose their own imams, it would also be permissible for them to appoint a prophet themselves. Allah has made obedience to the Imams obligatory just as He has made obedience to the Prophets obligatory, and has ordained the Imams to succeed them in their communities. Prophets are God's vicegerents and rulers among the people. As for the Khārijis who claim ignorance of the Imamate: their very ignorance becomes an argument against them, for it is obligatory upon the ignorant to seek knowledge from the learned.

The third argument: Allah says in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 5:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏حُرِّمَتْ عَلَيْكُمُ ٱلْمَيْتَةُ وَٱلدَّمُ وَلَحْمُ ٱلْخِنزِيرِ وَمَآ أُهِلَّ لِغَيْرِ ٱللَّهِ بِهِۦ وَٱلْمُنْخَنِقَةُ وَٱلْمَوْقُوذَةُ وَٱلْمُتَرَدِّيَةُ وَٱلنَّطِيحَةُ وَمَآ أَكَلَ ٱلسَّبُعُ إِلَّا مَا ذَكَّيْتُمْ وَمَا ذُبِحَ عَلَى ٱلنُّصُبِ وَأَن تَسْتَقْسِمُوا۟ بِٱلْأَزْلَمِ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ فِسْقٌ ۗ ٱلْيَوْمَ يَئِسَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ مِن دِينِكُمْ فَلَا تَخْشَوْهُمْ وَٱخْشَوْنِ ۚ ٱلْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِينَكُمْ وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِى وَرَضِيتُ لَكُمُ ٱلْإِسْلَمَ دِينًۭا ۚ فَمَنِ ٱضْطُرَّ فِى مَخْمَصَةٍ غَيْرَ مُتَجَانِفٍۢ لِّإِثْمٍۢ ۙ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
>
> *Forbidden to you (for food) are: Al-Maytatah (the dead animals - cattle-beast not slaughtered), blood, the flesh of swine, and the meat of that which has been slaughtered as a sacrifice for others than Allah, or has been slaughtered for idols, etc., or on which Allah's Name has not been mentioned while slaughtering, and that which has been killed by strangling, or by a violent blow, or by a headlong fall, or by the goring of horns - and that which has been (partly) eaten by a wild animal - unless you are able to slaughter it (before its death); and that which is sacrificed (slaughtered) on An-Nusub (stone altars). (Forbidden) also is to use arrows seeking luck or decision, (all) that is Fisqun (disobedience of Allah and sin). This day, those who disbelieved have given up all hope of your religion, so fear them not, but fear Me. This day, I have perfected your religion for you, completed My Favour upon you, and have chosen for you Islam as your religion. But as for him who is forced by severe hunger, with no inclination to sin (such can eat these above-mentioned meats), then surely, Allah is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful.*

Forbidden to you is carrion, and blood, and the flesh of swine, and that which has been invoked by the name of any other than Allah, which dies by strangulation, or of injury, or of falling from a height, or by striking its horns, and that which has been torn apart by an animal, except that which you have slaughtered, and that which is slaughtered on any land, and that which is slaughtered on any land, Today the disbelievers have despaired of your religion, so do not fear them, but fear Me, this day I have perfected your religion for you, and I have completed My favour upon you, and I have chosen for you Islam as your religion. Forgiving, Merciful.

This verse was revealed when the Messenger of Allah announced the guardianship (wilāya) and Imamate on the day of Ghadīr Khumm concerning Ali. How then can people claim that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah did not explain the Imamate when the Imamate is among the most important parts of the Divine Religion? Did Allah declare, without explaining this vital position, that "I have perfected the religion for you"? Or did the people perfect the religion of Allah by electing an Imam? Did the Messenger ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ) نعوذ باللہ⟫fail to do what Allah enjoined upon him (may Allah preserve us from such a thought), and then the people explained it?

As for your claim that the Imamate was indicated to ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr, if the Imamate extended beyond the indication, why did ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr not repeat it as he himself was indicated? ⟪ar:حضرت عمر⟫When Umar and ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr ⟪ar:حضرت صہیب⟫appointed ⟪ar:حضرت عثمان⟫ʿUthmān through mutual consultation, they both contradicted the command of the Messenger of Allah—for they did not establish the successor as the Messenger had done. As Allah says:

> ⟪quran 59:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَّآ أَفَآءَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ مِنْ أَهْلِ ٱلْقُرَىٰ فَلِلَّهِ وَلِلرَّسُولِ وَلِذِى ٱلْقُرْبَىٰ وَٱلْيَتَمَىٰ وَٱلْمَسَكِينِ وَٱبْنِ ٱلسَّبِيلِ كَىْ لَا يَكُونَ دُولَةًۢ بَيْنَ ٱلْأَغْنِيَآءِ مِنكُمْ ۚ وَمَآ آتَىٰكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوهُ وَمَا نَهَىٰكُمْ عَنْهُ فَٱنتَهُوا۟ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ شَدِيدُ ٱلْعِقَابِ ‎⟫
>
> *What Allah gave as booty (Fai') to His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from the people of the townships, - it is for Allah, His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), the kindred (of Messenger Muhammad PBUH), the orphans, Al-Masakin (the poor), and the wayfarer, in order that it may not become a fortune used by the rich among you. And whatsoever the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) gives you, take it, and whatsoever he forbids you, abstain (from it), and fear Allah. Verily, Allah is Severe in punishment.*

Whatever spoils of war Allah has given His Messenger from the people of the townships, it belongs to Allah and to the Messenger and to the near of kin and the orphans and the needy and the wayfarer, so that it may not circulate among the rich among you.

As for your assertion that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah granted ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr the right to lead the prayer (as narrated by Hazrat Aisha), and that this entitlement conferred upon him the Imamate and Caliphate: if ⟪ar:حضرت عمر⟫when Umar and ⟪ar:حضرت صہیب⟫Suhaib preceded him in the prayer, ⟪ar:حضرت عثمان⟫ʿUthmān was more deserving of the Imamate and Caliphate than he was! When he was appointed ⟪ar:عمر⟫as both leader and official, then ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr himself and both ⟪ar:حضرات عمر بن العص⟫Amr ibn al-ʿĀṣ prayed behind him. And Umar ibn al-ʿĀṣ would then have prior claim to the caliphate over both of them! But the community is unanimous that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah never gave precedence to anyone over ⟪ar:حضرت علی⟫Hazrat Ali.

⟪ar:غزوۃ ذات السلال⟫In the expedition of Dhāt al-Salāsil, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:عمربن العاص⟫appointed Umar ibn al-ʿĀṣ as commander. ⟪ar:حضرت عمر⟫Umar (the second caliph) and ⟪ar:حضرت عثمان⟫ʿUthmān prayed behind him, yet neither of them prayed behind ⟪ar:حضرت علی⟫Ali. But ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ Hazrat Ali never prayed behind Abū Bakr, nor behind ⟪ar:حضرت عمر⟫Umar, nor behind ⟪ar:حضرت عثمان⟫ʿUthmān. Yet these three prayed behind ⟪ar:حضرت علی⟫Ali many times. In this too, ⟪ar:حضرت علی⟫Ali was more deserving of the Imamate and Caliphate.[^cite-8]

And when you reject the ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫testimony of the Messenger of Allah for the Commander of the Faithful, why do you accept it when it concerns ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr, ⟪ar:حضرت فاطمۃ الزھرہ سلام علیہا⟫given by Fāṭima al-Zahrāʾ and ⟪ar:حضرت عائشہ⟫Aʾisha? You know that when ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫Abū Bakr began to lead the prayer at the ⟪ar:بیت الشرف⟫House of Honor, the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.a.) came out and turned him back while he was still in prayer. The Messenger ⟪ar:ﷺ⟫himself then led the prayer.

All these arguments of the masses are weak and baseless. Can the Messenger of Allah, who did not leave even his fingernails unexplained and conveyed Allah's commands down to the minutest details, fail to explain this crucial position of Imamate and Caliphate? In that case, can his prophethood be considered complete? Can such an essential duty be left to popular election and the choice of people? The appointment of an Imam is possible only in a way that cannot be refuted by any argument—and that irrefutable way is only through explicit declaration (nass). The Messenger of Allah did exactly this, just as he ⟪ar:یوم غدیر علی ابن ابی طالب⟫appointed and named Ali as his successor after him on the day of Ghadīr. ⟪ar:محمد الباقر علیہ السلام⟫Ali then appointed ⟪ar:حسن علیہ السلام⟫Hasan, who appointed ⟪ar:حسین علیہ السلام⟫Husayn, who appointed ⟪ar:حسن⟫Hasan (al-ʿAskarī), who appointed ⟪ar:حسین⟫Husayn (al-ʿAskarī), who appointed ⟪ar:علی زین العابدین علیہ السلام⟫ʿAlī Zayn al-ʿĀbidīn, who appointed ⟪ar:جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq. This succession continues through designation until the Day of Resurrection. From ⟪ar:آدم علیہ السلام⟫Adam through ⟪ar:حضرت نوح⟫Noah and ⟪ar:ابراھیم⟫Abraham to ⟪ar:موسی⟫Moses and ⟪ar:عیسی علیھم السلام⟫Jesus, and finally to ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫Muhammad, this pattern prevailed: no prophet left the Imamate and Caliphate to the consultation of the people. As Jesus said:

> ⟪quran 61:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ عِيسَى ٱبْنُ مَرْيَمَ يَبَنِىٓ إِسْرَٓئيلَ إِنِّى رَسُولُ ٱللَّهِ إِلَيْكُم مُّصَدِّقً

## References to the Imam in Qur'anic Verses

The earth can never remain empty of the Imam's presence. When Allah sent forth the Prophets to guide mankind, He did not confine His guidance to a single generation, nor did He deprive those who came after them of such luminaries. By nature's law, no Prophet can live eternally. As ⟪ar:نبی اکرم (ﷺ)⟫ declares in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 39:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّكَ مَيِّتٌۭ وَإِنَّهُم مَّيِّتُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily, you (O Muhammad PBUH) will die and verily, they (too) will die.*

Yet Allah Almighty preserves the Shariah (religious law) itself. To safeguard His religion, He established an Imam for every age—a ⟪ar:ھادی⟫ (guide) and ⟪ar:حجۃ اللہ⟫ (proof of Allah) for humanity in that time. Allah affirms this principle, stating:

> ⟪quran 13:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَيَقُولُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَوْلَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْهِ آيَةٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّهِۦٓ ۗ إِنَّمَآ أَنتَ مُنذِرٌۭ ۖ وَلِكُلِّ قَوْمٍ هَادٍ ‎⟫
> *And the disbelievers say: "Why is not a sign sent down to him from his Lord?" You are only a warner, and to every people there is a guide.*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ interprets this verse, saying: the ⟪ar:منذر⟫ (warner) refers to ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫, while the ⟪ar:ھاد⟫ (guide) refers to the first among the ⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫, and we who are the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫ are his successors. In every era, there exists an Imam who guides the people toward the teachings of ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫. Allah revealed the Qur'an itself as the supreme guide, what is called ⟪ar:' الذکر'⟫ (the Remembrance), concerning which He declares:

> ⟪quran 43:44⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِنَّهُۥ لَذِكْرٌۭ لَّكَ وَلِقَوْمِكَ ۖ وَسَوْفَ تُسْـَٔلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And verily, this (the Quran) is indeed a Reminder for you (O Muhammad PBUH) and your people (Quraish people, or your followers), and you will be questioned (about it).*

Furthermore, Allah pledged to preserve this Remembrance until the Day of Judgment:

> ⟪quran 15:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّا نَحْنُ نَزَّلْنَا ٱلذِّكْرَ وَإِنَّا لَهُۥ لَحَفِظُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily We: It is We Who have sent down the Dhikr (i.e. the Quran) and surely, We will guard it (from corruption).*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ explains: "Allah has entrusted the preservation of His Remembrance to us, the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫. He commands all people to learn from those possessed of knowledge when they ask:

> ⟪quran 16:43⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن قَبْلِكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۚ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent not (as Our Messengers) before you (O Muhammad PBUH) any but men, whom We inspired, (to preach and invite mankind to believe in the Oneness of Allah). So ask of those who know the Scripture [learned men of the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel)], if you know not.*

Allah issued this command because none can unlock the inner meanings (taʾwīl, esoteric exegesis) of the Book save Allah Himself and those He has established in knowledge—We, the Imams, to whom Allah has granted the fullness of knowledge.[^cite-1] In this connection, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 3:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *It is He Who has sent down to you (Muhammad PBUH) the Book (this Quran). In it are Verses that are entirely clear, they are the foundations of the Book [and those are the Verses of Al-Ahkam (commandments, etc.), Al-Fara'id (obligatory duties) and Al-Hudud (legal laws for the punishment of thieves, adulterers, etc.)]; and others not entirely clear. So as for those in whose hearts there is a deviation (from the truth) they follow that which is not entirely clear thereof, seeking Al-Fitnah (polytheism and trials, etc.), and seeking for its hidden meanings, but none knows its hidden meanings save Allah. And those who are firmly grounded in knowledge say: "We believe in it; the whole of it (clear and unclear Verses) are from our Lord." And none receive admonition except men of understanding.*

The sacred duties of instruction, the answering of the seeker's questions, the clarification of the Shariah's ordinances, and the elevation of the student toward true monotheism—all these rest upon the Imam. We are ⟪ar:مُؤَیَّد مِنَ اللہ⟫ (supported by Allah), and Our innocence is absolute. The ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ declared in his final utterance: "I leave behind among you two precious legacies: the Book of Allah and my Ahl al-Bayt (the Household). These two shall never be separated from one another until they meet me at the Pool of Kausar." This precious Qur'an—the ⟪ar:قرآن صامت⟫ (silent Qur'an)—is rendered eloquent and alive by the ⟪ar:قرآن ناطق⟫ (speaking Qur'an), which is the Messenger himself and his Ahl al-Bayt. Their inseparability signifies that in every age there must exist an Imam to expound and guide.[^cite-2]

> ⟪quran 35:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ أَوْرَثْنَا ٱلْكِتَبَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱصْطَفَيْنَا مِنْ عِبَادِنَاۖ فَمِنْهُمْ ظَالِمٌۭ لِّنَفْسِهِۦ وَمِنْهُم مُّقْتَصِدٌۭ وَمِنْهُمْ سَابِقٌۢ بِٱلْخَيْرَتِ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِۚ ذَلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْفَضْلُ ٱلْكَبِيرُ ‎⟫
> *Then We gave the Book the Quran) for inheritance to such of Our slaves whom We chose (the followers of Muhammad PBUH). Then of them are some who wrong their ownselves, and of them are some who follow a middle course, and of them are some who are, by Allah's Leave, foremost in good deeds. That (inheritance of the Quran), that is indeed a great grace.*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ teaches that in this verse, those ⟪ar:سَابِقٌۢ بِٱلْخَيْرَتِ⟫ (foremost in good deeds) are the Imams of every era. Those who are ⟪ar:مُّقْتَصِدٌ⟫ (moderate) are those who recognize the rights of the Imam. And those ⟪ar:ظَالِمٌۭ لِّنَفْسِهِ⟫ (who wrong themselves) are those who harbour doubt or distance themselves from us.[^cite-3] The Prophet ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ declared: "Whoever dies without recognizing the Imam of his time dies the death of ignorance." When ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ was asked, "Who is the Imam spoken of in this verse?" he replied through the light of understanding:

> ⟪quran 17:71⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَوْمَ نَدْعُوا۟ كُلَّ أُنَاسٍۭ بِإِمَمِهِمْ ۖ فَمَنْ أُوتِىَ كِتَبَهُۥ بِيَمِينِهِۦ فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ يَقْرَءُونَ كِتَبَهُمْ وَلَا يُظْلَمُونَ فَتِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(And remember) the Day when We shall call together all human beings with their (respective) Imam [their Prophets, or their records of good and bad deeds, or their Holy Books like the Quran, the Taurat (Torah), the Injeel (Gospel), etc.]. So whosoever is given his record in his right hand, such will read their records, and they will not be dealt with unjustly in the least.*

"The Imam," he explained, "refers to him whom the people followed in this world. Thus, on the Day of Judgment, those who obeyed and followed you shall be summoned in your company, O ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫." In the same manner, he expounded all the verses referring to the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt.

Beyond these passages, the Qur'an mentions the Imams in numerous verses. From ⟪ar:سورۃ النسا⟫, for instance:

> ⟪quran 4:54⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ يَحْسُدُونَ ٱلنَّاسَ عَلَىٰ مَآ آتَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ مِن فَضْلِهِۦ ۖ فَقَدْ آتَيْنَآ آلَإِبْرَهِيمَ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْحِكْمَةَ وَآتَيْنَهُم مُّلْكًا عَظِيمًۭا ‎⟫
> *Or do they envy men (Muhammad PBUH and his followers) for what Allah has given them of His Bounty? Then We had already given the family of Ibrahim (Abraham) the Book and Al-Hikmah (As-Sunnah - Divine Inspiration to those Prophets not written in the form of a book), and conferred upon them a great kingdom.*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ interprets: "In this verse, Allah refers to us, the Imams, as ⟪ar:' الناس'⟫ (the people) whom He favours over others. He has bestowed upon us the supreme rank of Imamate, a cause of envy to many. The ⟪ar:ملک عظیم⟫ (great kingdom) mentioned here signifies that Allah sustained Prophethood and Imamate through the descendants of ⟪ar:ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫, namely ⟪ar:اسحاق و اسماعیل علیھم السلام⟫, and from their progeny raised up Imams whose obedience is obedience to Allah and whose disobedience is disobedience to Him. This is a ⟪ar:جلیل القدر⟫ (supreme excellence)—yet many deny it.[^cite-4]

When Allah bestowed the ⟪ar:ملک عظیم آل محمد علیہ السلام⟫ (the great kingdom upon the Family of Muhammad), they rejected it outright. Allah further declares:

> ⟪quran 4:58⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَأْمُرُكُمْ أَن تُؤَدُّوا۟ ٱلْأَمَنَتِ إِلَىٰٓ أَهْلِهَا وَإِذَا حَكَمْتُم بَيْنَ ٱلنَّاسِ أَن تَحْكُمُوا۟ بِٱلْعَدْلِ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ نِعِمَّا يَعِظُكُم بِهِۦٓ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ سَمِيعًۢا بَصِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *Verily! Allah commands that you should render back the trusts to those, to whom they are due; and that when you judge between men, you judge with justice. Verily, how excellent is the teaching which He (Allah) gives you! Truly, Allah is Ever All-Hearer, All-Seer.*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ teaches: "We are the Imams of the ⟪ar:اہل بیت⟫ entrusted with these sacred trusts. Each of us transmits the scrolls of wisdom, the instruments of authority, and the knowledge which Allah granted us to the Imam who succeeds him." The divine address then continues to the believers in perpetuity:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority. (And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day. That is better and more suitable for final determination.*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ declares: "We are those ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫ referred to as ⟪ar:أولو الأمر⟫ (those vested with authority), named specifically after obedience to Allah and His Messenger."

The following verse reinforces this principle:

> ⟪quran 4:83⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا جَآءَهُمْ أَمْرٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْأَمْنِ أَوِ ٱلْخَوْفِ أَذَاعُوا۟ بِهِۦ ۖ وَلَوْ رَدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ وَإِلَىٰٓ أُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنْهُمْ لَعَلِمَهُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَنۢبِطُونَهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ وَلَوْلَا فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَتُهُۥ لَٱتَّبَعْتُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *When there comes to them some matter touching (public) safety or fear, they make it known (among the people), if only they had referred it to the Messenger or to those charged with authority among them, the proper investigators would have understood it from them (directly). Had it not been for the Grace and Mercy of Allah upon you, you would have followed Shaitan (Satan), save a few of you.*

We, the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت رسول علیھم السلام⟫, alone possess the knowledge Allah has granted to extract the inner meanings of all matters. This is why obedience to the ⟪ar:اولو الامر⟫ has been made obligatory. From ⟪ar:سورۃ المآئدۃ⟫, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 5:55⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّمَا وَلِيُّكُمُ ٱللَّهُ وَرَسُولُهُۥ وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱلَّذِينَ يُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَيُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُمْ رَكِعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily, your Walî (Protector or Helper) is Allah, His Messenger, and the believers, - those who perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and give Zakat, and they bow down (submit themselves with obedience to Allah in prayer).*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ interprets: "Allah has designated us—those who establish prayer and give the purifying alms (zakat) while bowing in submission—as your protectors and guides." Allah further instructs the faithful:

> ⟪quran 9:119⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَكُونُوا۟ مَعَ ٱلصَّدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Be afraid of Allah, and be with those who are true (in words and deeds).*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ says: "We are the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt, the ⟪ar:الصدقین⟫ (the truthful ones) referred to in this verse." From ⟪ar:سورۃ التوبۃ⟫:

> ⟪quran 9:105⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱعْمَلُوا۟ فَسَيَرَى ٱللَّهُ عَمَلَكُمْ وَرَسُولُهُۥ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۖ وَسَتُرَدُّونَ إِلَىٰ عَلِمِ ٱلْغَيْبِ وَٱلشَّهَدَةِ فَيُنَبِّئُكُم بِمَا كُنتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And say (O Muhammad PBUH) "Do deeds! Allah will see your deeds, and (so will) His Messenger and the believers. And you will be brought back to the All-Knower of the unseen and the seen. Then He will inform you of what you used to do."*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ teaches: "The ⟪ar:' مومنین'⟫ (believers) mentioned here after Allah and His Messenger refer to the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫. On the Day of Resurrection, We shall witness and know the deeds of all humanity—both their hidden and manifest works—and shall inform them of all they have accomplished."

From ⟪ar:سورۃ البقرۃ⟫, Allah Almighty declares:

> ⟪quran 2:143⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Thus We have made you [true Muslims - real believers of Islamic Monotheism, true followers of Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Sunnah (legal ways)], a Wasat (just) (and the best) nation, that you be witnesses over mankind and the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) be a witness over you. And We made the Qiblah (prayer direction towards Jerusalem) which you used to face, only to test those who followed the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from those who would turn on their heels (i.e. disobey the Messenger). Indeed it was great (heavy) except for those whom Allah guided. And Allah would never make your faith (prayers) to be lost (i.e. your prayers offered towards Jerusalem). Truly, Allah is full of kindness, the Most Merciful towards mankind.*

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ explains: "We are the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt, the ⟪ar:' امت وسطہ'⟫ (moderate community), appointed as witnesses of Allah upon all creation. The Messenger of Allah is a witness unto you concerning what Allah has revealed to us. We have been appointed as witnesses over the entire Ummah. Therefore, on the Day of Resurrection, we shall vindicate the truthful and expose the false." Allah affirms this role:

> ⟪quran 29:49⟫
> ⟪ar:‏بَلْ هُوَ آيَتٌۢ بَيِّنَتٌۭ فِى صُدُورِ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ ۚ وَمَا يَجْحَدُ بِـَٔايَتِنَآ إِلَّا ٱلظَّلِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Nay, but they, the clear Ayat [i.e the description and the qualities of Prophet Muhammad PBUH written like verses in the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel)] are preserved in the breasts of those who have been given knowledge (from the people of the Scriptures). And none but the Zalimun (polytheists and wrongdoers, etc.) deny Our Ayat (proofs, evidences, verses, lessons, signs, revelations, etc.).*

## Who is Meant by the Family of Muhammad?

⟪ar:محمد و آل محمد⟫It is obligatory upon all believers to invoke blessings upon Muhammad and his Ahl al-Bayt (Family). The Family of Muhammad specifically denotes the ⟪ar:اہل بیت⟫ (the Household) of ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫, the Messenger of Allah, for to them has passed the station of the Imamate, and they constitute the true embodiment of the Prophet's teachings. When this verse was revealed to the Prophet, the people asked him: "O Messenger, what is the proper manner of invoking blessings upon you?" He instructed them in the words: "O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and the Family of Muhammad, as You sent blessings upon Abraham and the Family of Abraham. Verily, You are the Most Praised, the Most Great." In this formula, the Prophet explained with precision—just as he detailed the prescribed prayers, fasting, pilgrimage, and the purifying alms—the exact manner of this invocation.

It is recorded that when ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ was asked by a visitor, "O son of the Messenger, tell me: who are the Family of Muhammad?" ⟪ar:صادق علیہ السلام⟫ smiled and replied: "The Family of Muhammad are specifically the Ahl al-Bayt of the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him." The questioner then observed, "Yet the common people believe that all Muslims, as believers in the oneness of Allah and followers of the true Prophet, constitute the Family of Muhammad." 

The Imam's response was profound: "He has spoken both truth and falsehood. How can those who contradict the commandments of Allah and waver between extremes in their deen (religion) be counted among the Family of Muhammad when they lack the conditions of such membership? Yet in one sense he speaks truly: those believers among them who fulfill the conditions of the Qur'an do bear a kinship to the family, though not in the particular sense of the Qur'anic usage. For the Qur'an designates as the family of a people their very descendants, as it declares:

> ⟪quran 5:51⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ لَا تَتَّخِذُوا۟ ٱلْيَهُودَ وَٱلنَّصَرَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآءَ ۘ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَآءُ بَعْضٍۢ ۚ وَمَن يَتَوَلَّهُم مِّنكُمْ فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Take not the Jews and the Christians as Auliya' (friends, protectors, helpers, etc.), they are but Auliya' to one another. And if any amongst you takes them as Auliya', then surely he is one of them. Verily, Allah guides not those people who are the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers and unjust).*

And Allah has recorded the words of ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫:

> ⟪quran 14:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏رَبِّ إِنَّهُنَّ أَضْلَلْنَ كَثِيرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلنَّاسِ ۖ فَمَن تَبِعَنِى فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنِّى ۖ وَمَنْ عَصَانِى فَإِنَّكَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *"O my Lord! They have indeed led astray many among mankind. But whoso follows me, he verily is of me. And whoso disobeys me, - still You are indeed Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful.*

Impelled by reverence, the questioner then asked: "Grant me some verses from the ⟪ar:قرآن حکیم⟫ (the Wise Qur'an) as evidence, so that I may argue definitively that the Family of Muhammad—specifically the Ahl al-Bayt of the Prophet—are none but the Imams, distinct from all other Muslims." 

The Imam replied: "Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 3:33-34⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ ٱصْطَفَىٰٓ آدَمَ وَنُوحًۭا وَآلَ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَآلَ عِمْرَنَ عَلَى ٱلْعَلَمِينَ ‎ ‏ذُرِّيَّةًۢ بَعْضُهَا مِنۢ بَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ ‎⟫
> *Allah chose Adam, Nuh (Noah), the family of Ibrahim (Abraham) and the family of 'Imran above the 'Alamîn (mankind and jinns) (of their times). Offspring, one of the other, and Allah is the All-Hearer, All-Knower.*

Do you see? The offspring of every people are their direct descendants—no ambiguity there." He then cited another passage:

> ⟪quran 40:28⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ رَجُلٌۭ مُّؤْمِنٌۭ مِّنْ آلِ فِرْعَوْنَ يَكْتُمُ إِيمَنَهُۥٓ أَتَقْتُلُونَ رَجُلًا أَن يَقُولَ رَبِّىَ ٱللَّهُ وَقَدْ جَآءَكُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ مِن رَّبِّكُمْۖ وَإِن يَكُ كَذِبًۭا فَعَلَيْهِ كَذِبُهُۥۖ وَإِن يَكُ صَادِقًۭا يُصِبْكُم بَعْضُ ٱلَّذِى يَعِدُكُمْۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى مَنْ هُوَ مُسْرِفٌۭ كَذَّابٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And a believing man of Fir'aun's (Pharaoh) family, who hid his faith said: "Would you kill a man because he says: My Lord is Allah, and he has come to you with clear signs (proofs) from your Lord? And if he is a liar, upon him will be (the sin of) his lie; but if he is telling the truth, then some of that (calamity) wherewith he threatens you will befall on you." Verily, Allah guides not one who is a Musrif (a polytheist, or a murderer who shed blood without a right, or those who commit great sins, oppressor, transgressor), a liar!*

Observe closely: in this verse, Allah attributes a believing man to Pharaoh's family purely through blood kinship (nasab), despite his complete opposition to Pharaoh in matters of faith. If, then, all Muslims who believe in the Prophet and follow him were to be reckoned among the ⟪ar:آل محمد (ﷺ)⟫ solely by virtue of faith, Allah would not have attributed this believer to Pharaoh's ⟪ar:اہل بیت⟫ (household), for he stood against everything Pharaoh represented religiously. Thus does the evidence make clear: a people's family is their biological kindred.[^cite-5]

## Those Who Enter the Category of the Family of Muhammad

Yet in the sense of piety and spiritual affiliation, one who follows and obeys the ⟪ar:آل محمد (ﷺ)⟫ shall be counted among them. As ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ declared: "Whoever follows me belongs to me." The Qur'an illustrates this principle:

> ⟪quran 40:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلنَّارُ يُعْرَضُونَ عَلَيْهَا غُدُوًّۭا وَعَشِيًّۭاۖ وَيَوْمَ تَقُومُ ٱلسَّاعَةُ أَدْخِلُوٓا۟ آلَ فِرْعَوْنَ أَشَدَّ ٱلْعَذَابِ ‎⟫
> *The Fire; they are exposed to it, morning and afternoon, and on the Day when the Hour will be established (it will be said to the angels): "Cause Fir'aun's (Pharaoh) people to enter the severest torment!"*

In this verse, the ⟪ar:آل فرعون⟫ (Family of Pharaoh) encompasses not merely his blood relations but also all who followed him in obedience. They shall enter Hell because they became, through their allegiance, part of his household. When Pharaoh and his family served as their Imams, they attached themselves through love and obedience, becoming in effect his kindred.

In another place, the Qur'an states:

> ⟪quran 37:130⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَلَمٌ عَلَىٰٓ أٓلِ يَاسِينَ ‎⟫
> *Salamun (peace) be upon Ilyasîn (Elias)!*

This blessed invocation encompasses ⟪ar:یٰسٓ⟫ (Yasin, the Prophet Elias) himself and his ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫ successors. Similarly, in addressing ⟪ar:آل داؤد⟫ (the House of David):

> ⟪quran 34:13⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَعْمَلُونَ لَهُۥ مَا يَشَآءُ مِن مَّحَرِيبَ وَتَمَثِيلَ وَجِفَانٍۢ كَٱلْجَوَابِ وَقُدُورٍۢ رَّاسِيَتٍۚ ٱعْمَلُوٓا۟ آلَ دَاوُۥدَ شُكْرًۭاۚ وَقَلِيلٌۭ مِّنْ عِبَادِىَ ٱلشَّكُورُ ‎⟫
> *They worked for him what he desired, (making) high rooms, images, basins as large as reservoirs, and (cooking) cauldrons fixed (in their places). "Work you, O family of Dawud (David), with thanks!" But few of My slaves are grateful.*

And once again:

> ⟪quran 2:248⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ لَهُمْ نَبِيُّهُمْ إِنَّ آيَةَ مُلْكِهِۦٓ أَن يَأْتِيَكُمُ ٱلتَّابُوتُ فِيهِ سَكِينَةٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَبَقِيَّةٌۭ مِّمَّا تَرَكَ آلُ مُوسَىٰ وَآلُ هَرُونَ تَحْمِلُهُ ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ ۚ إِنَّ فِى ذَلِكَ لَآيَةًۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُم مُّؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫
> *And their Prophet (Samuel) said to them: Verily! The sign of His Kingdom is that there shall come to you At-Tabut (a wooden box), wherein is Sakinah (peace and reassurance) from your Lord and a remnant of that which Musa (Moses) and Harun (Aaron) left behind, carried by the angels. Verily, in this is a sign for you if you are indeed believers.*

In all these instances—⟪ar:آل فرعون، آل یس، آل داؤد، آل موسی اور آل ھرون⟫ (the families of Pharaoh, Yasin, David, Moses and Aaron)—the designation of "family" extends beyond mere ⟪ar:نسبی و صلبی⟫ (genealogical) kinship to encompass all those who adhere to them through obedience and allegiance. Therefore, it is abundantly manifest that the Ahl al-Bayt are the Imams proper. Those who establish a living bond with them and follow them in their religious obligations shall be numbered among the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫, becoming part of the ⟪ar:آل محمد(ﷺ)⟫, as the Qur'an makes abundantly clear in numerous verses.[^cite-6]

The Imam then turned to his questioner and declared with solemnity: "Believe me, no nation in all history has been more unjust and oppressive than this one! This Ummah has convinced itself that there exists no distinction between it and the ⟪ar:پیغمبر محمد رسول اللہ صلعم⟫—no superiority granted to the ⟪ar:اہل بیت کرام علیھم السلام⟫ (Ahl al-Bayt, the Noble Household). What greater oppression could there be? Such a position constitutes a grave calumny against the Lord of the Worlds and a heinous transgression. Whoever harbours such beliefs shall taste humiliation in both this world and the next, until they repent and acknowledge the virtues (faḍāʾil) and excellences that Allah has bestowed upon the ⟪ar:محمدو آل محمد⟫. 

Do not imagine that ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫ are merely the luminous stars of the Prophet's radiance, reservoirs of mercy, fountains of wisdom, and repositories of remembrance, with the angels as messengers between them and the worlds. If they truly believed and turned toward the truth, they would not be expelled from kinship with the Family of Muhammad."

## Who Are the Ummah of Muhammad?

⟪ar:امت محمدی علیہ السلام⟫The term "Ummah of Muhammad" ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیہ السلام⟫refers to those who believe in ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫and confirm the Prophethood of ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫and his Imams, following them in faith and practice. Just as believers are counted among the family of Muhammad through their obedience to the Prophet and his Ahl al-Bayt (the People of the Household), they are likewise designated as the Ummah of Muhammad. ⟪ar:امت محمدی (اہل بیت)⟫Those, however, who deny the bounty of divine truth are addressed by Allah in these terms:

> ⟪quran 4:150⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ يَكْفُرُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَيُرِيدُونَ أَن يُفَرِّقُوا۟ بَيْنَ ٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَيَقُولُونَ نُؤْمِنُ بِبَعْضٍۢ وَنَكْفُرُ بِبَعْضٍۢ وَيُرِيدُونَ أَن يَتَّخِذُوا۟ بَيْنَ ذَلِكَ سَبِيلًا ‎⟫
> *Verily, those who disbelieve in Allah and His Messengers and wish to make distinction between Allah and His Messengers (by believing in Allah and disbelieving in His Messengers) saying, "We believe in some but reject others," and wish to adopt a way in between.*

They profess belief in Allah and His Messenger, yet when instructed to acknowledge the virtue of the Imams from among the Ahl al-Bayt, they deny it entirely. 

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫It is recorded that ⟪ar:ابراھیم اور اسماعیل علیہم السلام⟫when ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم⟫Abraham completed the construction of the House of Allah, he offered this supplication:

> ⟪quran 2:127-128⟫

## The Purity and Infallibility of the Imam

Allah addresses the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt in the Qur'an with these words:

> ⟪quran 33:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَرْنَ فِى بُيُوتِكُنَّ وَلَا تَبَرَّجْنَ تَبَرُّجَ ٱلْجَهِلِيَّةِ ٱلْأُولَىٰۖ وَأَقِمْنَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتِينَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَأَطِعْنَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۚۥٓ إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ ٱلرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ ٱلْبَيْتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمْ تَطْهِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *And stay in your houses, and do not display yourselves like that of the times of ignorance, and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and give Zakat and obey Allah and His Messenger. Allah wishes only to remove Ar-Rijs (evil deeds and sins, etc.) from you, O members of the family (of the Prophet PBUH), and to purify you with a thorough purification.*

Mark the precision of Allah's words: "Allah wishes only to remove from you all uncleanness, O People of the House." He does not say that they should be cleansed of impurities—as though they were first defiled and afterward purified. Rather, Allah declares His will to keep them perpetually free from filth. To understand this distinction is vital. Should one accept the false notion—⟪ar:نعوذ باللہ⟫God forbid—that all descendants of Adam originate from an illicit union, one would be compelled to acknowledge that every prophet and messenger, including the ⟪ar:خیر المرسلین⟫best of the messengers, would be born of such transgression. ⟪ar:(نعوذ باللہ من ذالک)⟫We seek refuge in Allah from such a thought! How could Allah place upon the head of one whom He has made sovereign over all creation a blemish so grave? This is inconceivable.[^cite-10]

Contrarily, the Messenger of Allah, the ⟪ar:خیر الأنبیاء و المرسلین⟫noblest of prophets and messengers, who is ⟪ar:صادق و امین⟫truthful and trustworthy, declares: "My lineage has ever moved from ⟪ar:ارحام زکیۃ⟫pure wombs to ⟪ar:اصلاب طاہرۃ⟫pure loins, and no filth has ever touched us." For the Imamate to pass from one Imam to the next, it is an established condition that his son shall become the Imam after him. This Sunnah (Divine custom) that Allah has established admits of no alteration. Allah Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 33:62⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سُنَّةَ ٱللَّهِ فِى ٱلَّذِينَ خَلَوْا۟ مِن قَبْلُۖ وَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّةِ ٱللَّهِ تَبْدِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *That was the Way of Allah in the case of those who passed away of old, and you will not find any change in the Way of Allah.*

### The Light of the Prophet and Ali

⟪ar:حضرت جابر بن عبد اللہ الانصاری⟫The Companion Jabir ibn Abdallah al-Ansari reports that he heard the Messenger of Allah say: "Allah created me and Ali from a single light two thousand years before the creation of Adam, and this light stood before His Throne." ## All Things Have Been Preserved in a Clear Book

To illuminate the supreme station of the Imam, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 36:12⟫

If such is the state of ordinary humanity, and if we hold that the Imam transcends human nature immeasurably, what then shall we say of the grandeur of the Imam? It becomes clear from this verse that the personality of the Imam stands at an elevation far above human nature; there is no true correspondence between the two. The Imam is the lord of his age, and all things are subordinate to His authority. The Gnostics (ʿārifūn) of Allah have drawn a subtle inference from this verse: those who have advanced in spiritual understanding and joined themselves to the Imam have, in reality, become ⟪ar:لاہوت و ناست⟫the divine and human dimensions united in him—in other words, ⟪ar:شئی⟫something (objects of his care). In contrast stand all ⟪ar:لا شئی⟫other things devoid of this connection, which possess no true value whatsoever.[^cite-13]

## The Affirmation of the Imamate

**In the Name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.**

All praise belongs to Allah, who sent prophets and appointed Imams and caliphs to guide His creation. I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and that Muhammad is His servant and messenger. May Allah bless him, his family, and his companion ʿAlī, who possesses supreme knowledge in the interpretation of the Qur'an.

The Imamate constitutes the foundation of religion; all matters revolve around it. It is the source of knowledge of Allah and the Qur'an. The Imamate is affirmed through evidence and rational deduction. Those who preceded us have written extensively on the affirmation of the Imamate, citing proofs from the Qur'an and the Prophet's traditions. The necessity of the Imam is essential in natural disposition (al-jabīla, the innate human inclination), in fundamental nature (al-fiṭra, primordial human nature), in reason, in governance, in custom, and in all norms. It is obligatory in every revealed law, religion, and religious community—just as the existence of a Creator is necessary.[^cite-14]

People of different faiths have called their past Imams prophets and sages. Some misguided Muslims have applied the designation of Imam to their scholars and jurists. Whoever seeks salvation must lay aside his inclinations and desires, recognizing the deficiency in every false claimant to this esteemed station.

We shall now demonstrate the weaknesses of those who falsely claim the Imamate. Then, turning their own principles against them, we shall refute their claims and establish the Imamate of the true Imams with proof and demonstration. We shall show the necessity of obeying them, entrusting our affairs to their command, and accepting revelation, interpretation (taʾwīl, esoteric exegesis), and the confession of divine unity from them.

Allah, through His wisdom and power, created the human body with its senses and its sustenance, through which it survives. Similarly, He established principles, causes, and means for the souls, spirits, and intellectual forms by which they acquire their spiritual sustenance, nourishment, and survival.

## The Degrees of Excellence as Evidence for the Imamate

The differences in degrees of excellence (⟪ar:التفاوت التفاضل⟫) existing among all entities—comprising essences (⟪ar:أصول⟫) and their derivations (⟪ar:فروع⟫)—constitute the firmest evidence for affirming the Imamate and the Imams. Allah created entities in all genera (⟪ar:اجناس⟫) and species (⟪ar:انواع⟫), each differing from the others in degrees of excellence. For each genus and species, He established an ultimate limit (⟪ar:غایۃ⟫), a summit (⟪ar:ذرورۃ⟫), an end-point (⟪ar:نھایۃ⟫), and a virtue (⟪ar:فضیلۃ⟫) that none other in that category can attain. He made certain genera and species either necessary for human physical existence and function, or sources of nourishment, medicine, elegance, adornment, and indispensable tools for survival. Thus in all things He placed a unique advantage (⟪ar:منفعۃ⟫) not found elsewhere. He endowed in them a capacity that creation necessarily requires and cannot forgo. By creating within beings a need (hunger, thirst), He compelled them to seek and acquire these requirements, removing their deficiency.

## The Natural and Legal Testimony

All creation and its needs admonish regarding the symbolized religious reality. Such creation serves as witness for whoever seeks testimony and proof for whoever asks. It is insight for all who see and reflect. Yet it is also a proof against those who deny the symbolized religious realities of the Imams and their hierarchical ranks, their knowledge and benefit—precisely because they accept proofs of benefit from natural elements, planets, and celestial spheres. Indeed, Allah has made these elements, means, and natural causes so powerful as evidence that no one can deny them or testify against them, alter or change them; unlike what certain people have done with the religious law and the Prophet's testimony concerning the affirmation of the Imamate, its necessity, and devotion to the Imams.[^cite-16]

Furthermore, just as the testimony of the outer horizons (⟪ar:آفاق⟫) and inner selves (⟪ar:انفس⟫) is compulsory (⟪ar:جبری⟫) and not voluntary (⟪ar:اختیاری⟫), making it impossible for anyone to alter or substitute it, the Messenger likewise made testimony compulsory, without entailing any choice for witnesses to change, alter, or conceal it. The Prophet answered those who asked, "Who testifies to your prophethood?" by saying: ⟪ar:یشھد لی کل حجر و مدر⟫ [Every stone and every piece of clay testifies for me]—pointing to the testimony of the horizons and souls.

True judgment in religion and in affirming the Imamate rests upon two witnesses: one established by religious law (⟪ar:شرعی⟫) and the other compelled by nature (⟪ar:طبعی⟫). If people deny the legally established testimony, the one bound by nature—the horizons and our inner selves—reminds, enforces, and verifies the conventional witness. As Allah states: ⟪quran 41:53⟫ "We shall show them Our signs on the horizons and within themselves until it becomes manifest to them that it is the Truth."[^cite-17]

## The Categories and Their Summits Point to the Imam

We shall now mention the differences and degrees of excellence in each principle (⟪ar:اصل⟫) and natural realm (⟪ar:موالید⟫), showing that the ultimate limit and highest summit in each genus and species points to the Imam in every age and time.

The sages (⟪ar:حکما⟫) among philosophers mention the ten categories and affirm that nothing can be discussed except in reference to these. The "essence" (⟪ar:جوھر⟫) is the ultimate upon which all others rest and which excels over all others, since the remaining nine are established from it. This essence serves as proof for the permanent, intellectual, luminous, authentic substance through which all souls are illumined and upon which all nine depend—one that needs nothing created, derives benefit from nothing, yet from which everything benefits and which everything needs. That is the Imam, peace upon him. All other ranks stand below him as the nine categories stand below essence.[^cite-18]

Among the four basic constituents (⟪ar:العناثر⟫) from which the first composites (⟪ar:ترکیب⟫) were formed, two are active and two passive. Heat and cold are active; dryness and humidity are passive. The ultimate and noblest is heat, from which arise luminosity, light, motion, and elevation, while the other three are established through it. Heat has precedence in origin and existence; no foundation or natural realm would be complete without it. Likewise, the Imam illumines the soul. From Him comes intellectual light (al-nūr al-ʿaqlī), motion, and elevation. No principle or religious realm is complete without him.[^cite-19]

Among the celestial spheres, the most lofty and supreme is the outermost sphere in which they all orbit. Likewise, the Imam in His religion is the supreme religious sphere for all other spheres. Among the four elements (⟪ar:الارکان⟫), the highest and noblest is fire, the summit and peak to which no other element or nature can attain. Likewise, the Imam in his age is the highest religious pillar that illuminates souls. It was for this reason that Abraham (upon him peace) ordered the glorification of fire—not as an object of worship, but as a symbol pointing to the glorification of the imamate itself, which none must extinguish through the injustice of usurpers. The fire-worshippers fell into confusion, knowing not the symbolized reality. As Allah states: ⟪quran 9:32⟫ "They would like to extinguish Allah's light with their mouths, but Allah will not allow that [to happen] until He perfects His light, even if the unbelievers detest it."[^cite-20]

Among the many planets, there are differences and disparities in degrees of excellence. Their peak and summit in nobility is the sun, through which physical sight draws its light and through which all plants, animals, and newborns grow and achieve perfection. No plant or newborn can grow without the sun's effect. Likewise, the Imam is the sun of religion through which insight is enlightened and souls are illumined by the light of guidance and wisdom, radiating the hearts of Allah's friends.[^cite-21]

Among the twelve zodiacal signs, four are most glorious and excellent, one of which stands as the peak in nobility. Similarly, among the twelve proofs (ḥujja, the representatives), the one directly connected to the Imam (bāb al-abwāb, the Gate of Gates) is the most excellent, noblest, and most exalted of all.[^cite-22]

The fourth clime, parallel to the fourth heaven and to the sun, is the most moderate as well as the most excellent and noblest of all. Similarly, the Imam is the most excellent in the hierarchy, the most glorious of all, their peak and apex. The number four is emphasized in the four elements, seasons, humours, and directions.[^cite-23]

Among melting mineral substances, the most excellent is gold—that with which commerce is conducted, establishing the value of valuables, unaffected by fire or water, never destroyed. Likewise, the Imam is the most excellent religious and intellectual substance in his age and time. Through him the worldly and religious transactions of creation and their well-being are completed. Each melting substance has its peak in nobility. Among precious stones, the best is sapphire (⟪ar:الیاقوت⟫), and among the white, green, yellow, and red sapphires, the red ruby (⟪ar:یاقوت الاحمر⟫) is most excellent. No other jewel endures like the ruby, for it maintains its essence and is not transformed by fire or water; it always retains its purity, radiance, and gravity, obtaining traces from its source in a manner no other substance can. Neither other sapphires (⟪ar:یاقوت⟫), emeralds (⟪ar:الزمرد⟫), carnelian (⟪ar:البیجاد⟫), agate (⟪ar:العقیق⟫), diamonds (⟪ar:والألماس⟫), nor turquoise (⟪ar:والفروزج⟫) retain these qualities. Thus the ruby is the crown among jewels, just as the Imam is the peak among all creation in his excellence, unique among all ranks of the hierarchy—among the proofs (ḥujja), adjuncts (⟪ar:لواحق⟫), the summoner (dāʿī, the Summoner), the wing (⟪ar:جناح⟫), the licensee (⟪ar:ماذون⟫), and the motivator (⟪ar:مکلب⟫).[^cite-24] Therefore, no one attains the status, rank, or position of the Imam in his age or time. The seven jewels serve as proofs for the seven proclaimers (⟪ar:النطقا⟫) and the seven imams. Just as the natural symbol exists in every age and time, so too must the symbolized reality exist in every age and time, influencing the souls.

Among the seven grains providing human sustenance, the peak and summit is wheat, the most balanced in nature and nobility, helping build the body. Likewise, the Imam is the peak and summit, benefiting and nourishing souls with his knowledge, serving as the source of their spiritual livelihood and life.[^cite-25]

As for fruit-bearing trees, the peak among them is the date palm. It shares with humanity the trait of male and female gender, mating and impregnating like animals. Everything in the date palm is beneficial; nothing is wasted. Its fruit is eaten at all times, raw or cooked. To dream of plucking dates from a palm tree signifies benefiting from a certain believer—indeed, the Imam, who is the head and leader of all believers.[^cite-26]

Among medicinal plants, some have reached such refinement that they become antidotes (⟪ar:تریاقاً⟫) for those on the verge of death, curing chronic diseases while others are lesser in degree. The peak and summit among medicines points to the Imam; for his words become antidotes for the souls, a means of healing its diseases and afflictions, relief from death, illnesses, doubts, and discords. The fatal poisons and harmful effects found among medicines represent the opponents who forcefully usurp the position of the Imams; they destroy those who listen to or accompany them until the Imam reaches them with the great antidote rescuing their souls from destruction. As Allah states: ⟪quran 21:29⟫ "Certainly! You and that which you worship besides Allah are fuel for Hell! You will enter it."[^cite-27]

Among animals, the horse is the best, the eagle the best among birds, and the lion the king among beasts of prey. The disparities among these species in strength, usefulness, and capacity for training and excellence within their kind point necessarily to a peak within the human species. That summit is the Imam, peace be upon him.[^cite-28]

Certain plants are sweet, delicious, and appetizing, from which people benefit, while others are bitter, having neither benefit nor delicacy, bearing no fruit and bearing thorns that harm. The sweet, pleasant plants symbolize the Imams, symbols of their speech and knowledge that entice the soul and please the eye. The bitter, putrid, and thorny ones symbolize the leaders of the literalists (⟪ar:اہل ظاہر⟫). For this reason Allah states:

## The Necessity of the Imam in the Cycles of Prophecy

If it were permissible for creatures on earth to exist without an Imam or caliph, this should have been acceptable during the time of Adam, when only angels were present. Yet Allah stated:

> ⟪quran 2:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫

> And when your Lord said to the angels, "I am placing a vicegerent upon the earth," they said, "Will You place therein one who will spread corruption and shed blood, while we glorify You with praise and sanctify You?" He said, "I know that which you do not know."[^cite-30]

Allah clarified that the earth can never be deprived of an Imam, even if all people were impeccable and saintly, so they might understand the Imam's indispensability when most are ignorant and corrupt. Allah also made plain that if the Imam could be chosen by consultation (ikhtiyār), the angels certainly would have been given that choice—yet they were not, because they were immune from base desires and cravings for domination. Since this was not acceptable for them, it cannot be acceptable for those below them. This meaning underlies the verse regarding the rights of parents:

Allah mentions the stories of past communities and prophets so that believers recognize that the same will occur in Muhammad's cycle. The Prophet said: "My community will experience exactly what happened among the Banū Isrāʾīl," and: "You will follow the footsteps of those before you, step by step, so that if they entered the burrow of a lizard, you too would enter it."

Should this have been permissible, the angels' choice would have been more appropriate, yet they were rebuked for their objection. Allah informed them of their error and showed them the falsehood of their reasoning. The Qur'an mentions this so believers learn and do not dispute about those Allah and His Messenger chose for the Imamate. In Adam's appointment there is indication that the world can never be devoid of an Imam. All peoples accept the Imams in Adam's period but deny them in Muhammad's period—yet Allah said: "Such was Our way with the messengers whom We sent before you, and you will find no change in Our way" and "You will certainly find no change in Our way." This refers to the sunna (way) of the Imamate itself, not the sunna of ritual practices like prayer and fasting; for the Prophet changed the sunna of all previous prophets regarding worship but not regarding the Imamate.[^cite-32]

## The Imamate in the Prophetic Cycles

When Noah invited people to the ark out of compassion, knowing that those who remained behind would perish by their own choice, the ark itself was nothing but the Imam himself. Noah's invitation was to the Imam, as the Prophet said: "The likeness of my family (ahl al-bayt) among you is like Noah's ark: whoever boards it will be saved, and whoever remains behind will be drowned." Noah appointed his son Sham to the wilāya (guardianship) and Imamate by designation (naṣṣ) and appointment (tawqīf), and it continued thereafter until Abraham. Yet the opponents accept the Imams in Noah's cycle but deny them in Muhammad's family, though the sunna has not changed and people cannot do without an Imam.[^cite-33]

When Abraham appeared, he ordered the glorification of fire—a symbol, if authentic, of glorifying the light of the Imamate (nūr al-imāma). Abraham and Ishmael built the House (the Kaʿba), commanding people to glorify it—an indication that the Imamate would remain in Abraham's house and progeny, urging veneration of the Imams of his family.[^cite-34]

When Moses appeared, he ordered people to face Jerusalem, saying in essence: "Whoever among the Banū Isrāʾīl possesses the sanctified spirit is your Imam and your direction of prayer." When Jesus appeared (upon him peace), having no family or children in whom the Imamate could continue, it is reported he ordered people to face toward the east—turning toward where the light of the Imamate rises. Whoever radiates that light shall be your Imam and your direction of prayer.

When the Prophet Muhammad, the seal of the prophets (upon him and his family be peace), appeared, he ordered people to turn their faces from Jerusalem toward the Kaʿba as the direction of prayer. The Kaʿba was built by Abraham and Ishmael. This change indicated that direction had returned to Abraham's house—the family of Ishmael, meaning the Imamate had returned to the children of Ishmael, who are the children of the Prophet. He ordered people to glorify the House and perform pilgrimage to it, as no other prophet had, exhorting them regarding the supreme excellence of the Imams from Muhammad's family above all previous Imams. How iniquitous are those who accepted Imams from all prophetic periods, affirming that the world can never be deprived of an Imam and that designation (naṣṣ) determined the Imams in all previous cycles, yet now deny the Imamate of Muhammad's family and call for choice![^cite-35]

## The Virtues of the Imam and the Commander of the Age

The virtues by which Allah has preferred some people over others consist of three kinds: knowledge, struggle in Allah's cause (jihād), and piety (taqwā). As Allah states: "Allah has preferred those who strive and struggle over those who sit idle by a great reward" and "Are those who know and those who do not know equal?" and "Those given knowledge have degrees of rank" and "The most noble among you before Allah is the most pious among you."[^cite-36]

Regarding jihād, the elite and the general Muslims know that the usurpers never waged war even a single day on their own. The wars of the Commander of the Faithful ʿAlī are well-known and famous. Regarding piety, where there is no knowledge, there is no piety; piety comes through knowledge—knowing what belongs to one and what belongs to others. It is correct according to the Qur'anic verses mentioned that the Imamate belonged to the Commander of the Faithful because he had precedence in virtues: the most knowledgeable, the most pious, and the one who fought most in Allah's cause.[^cite-37]

Those rulers did not claim excellence, knowledge, or piety for themselves; rather, their companions did so for them. Abū Bakr was the first to ascend the pulpit and say: "You have appointed me, although I am not the best among you!" If he spoke truth, those claiming his excellence are liars; if he lied, a liar cannot be an Imam. The Prophet himself mentioned his rank and station that Allah had singled out for him without humiliation. He said: "I am the master of the Arabs without boasting," "I am the master of humanity without boasting," and "I am the most eloquent of the Arabs without boasting."

The Commander of the Faithful ʿAlī said: "Ask me before you lose me." He also said: "If a cushion were folded for me to sit on, I would judge the followers of the Torah by the Torah, the followers of the Bible by the Bible, and the followers of the Qur'an by the Qur'an." Such words do not constitute arrogance but rather the proper acknowledgment of one's station.[^cite-38]

When ʿUmar ascended the pulpit after Abū Bakr, he said: "The oath to Abū Bakr was a hasty decision, but Allah has averted its evil from us. Whoever repeats its like, kill him." In this, he expounded the falsehood of the literalists' claim that Abū Bakr's leadership of prayer made his Imamate obligatory or indicated his Imamate by designation.[^cite-39]

We shall now mention some merits of the lord of the age and time, the Commander of the Faithful (al-Ḥākim, the Wise One), though one such as myself can never attain the status to mention his true merits, as Allah has said: "They have not valued Allah according to His true value." Yet I shall speak according to my limited knowledge, and the Commander of the Faithful is the most worthy to accept his servant's excuse.[^cite-40]

Allah made the earth radiant with His light, as He promised when He said: "The earth shines by the light of her Lord"—the outer earth shines through the justice of the Commander of the Faithful, and the inner earth shines through knowledge and explanation.[^cite-41]

Just as in every species, genus, root, and branch there is a peak and summit in nobility and merit that nothing else can attain, so too must the Imams have a peak, summit, and ultimate degree which none has previously reached. That peak is the Commander of the Faithful ʿAlī. He is the peak of all peaks, the ultimate limit beyond which there is no other. Wisdom appeared in the rise of humanity, and their welfare became complete through humanity. The benefit of all beings returns to mankind, and it is through the Imams that the merits of human beings become complete and wisdom manifests in creation. Similarly, through the Commander of the Faithful, all affairs of the prophets, trustees, and Imams became complete, their order was perfected, and perfect wisdom became manifest in their disposition, laws, and esoteric interpretations.[^cite-42]

He is like the head and they like the body. He is like the spirit and they like the body—no body is complete without spirit. He is like the heart while all others are like the limbs. Through the fusion of spirit and life with the heart, all limbs receive life and recompense. Each hair and limb receives its share of reward through the heart. Just as it is through the Commander of the Faithful that people gain recompense and acceptance of life in the seventh stage, he awakens in everyone the desire to come to him. Through him Allah promised creatures the fulfillment of reward on the Day of Judgment, as He said: "You will be paid only that which you have earned fairly on the Day of Resurrection."[^cite-43]

Just as humankind exists as the final realm of nature in the world, having accepted all from previous realms and exceeding them in things they did not possess, and just as the Imams acquired all merits existing in humanity—being their peak, summit, and quintessence—able to accept what was beyond mankind's power, with all previous beings' benefit reaching the Imams, so too does the Commander of the Faithful gather in himself all merits of those before him, their knowledge and benefit, exceeding them many times over.[^cite-44]

Just as the Prophet's power and merit existed because he gathered the merits of all previous cycles—all their knowledge and more—the Commander of the Faithful gathers the merits of all prophets, Imams, and sages who preceded him, exceeding them in things of which they were incapable. If he had not risen to this position, there would not have been perfection of wisdom in their rise. The Commander of the Faithful is therefore the sum of everything in the worlds today—spiritual, physical, and conventional. Because of him these different worlds were created, and all are his agents and propagators until the Day of Judgment. He arose in the world for the perfection of the Creator's vicegerency among humanity.[^cite-45]

The Commander of the Faithful is the Qā'im (Resurrector) in his time and period and the Qā'im of all who preceded him, the perfector of them all. He is the sixth through whom the command of the seventh is perfected, finishing what the seventh requires—just as the Prophet established all the Qā'im would require, so the Commander of the Faithful perfects what the Prophet established and arranged.

By another calculation, the Commander of the Faithful is the ninth. The command of the seventh (al-Muʿizz) who passed away is perfected through the ninth (Al-Ḥākim), just as the perfection of the seven members, their order and function manifest through nine—that is, the four spiritual natures and the five spiritual senses. As Allah said: "Indeed, the abode of the hereafter—that is life, if only they knew."[^cite-46]

By yet another calculation, the Commander of the Faithful is the second, through whom the order at the beginning of each cycle is perfected. He is also the sixth through whose sword Allah will conquer the whole world, just as He conquered through the Prophet, the sixth from Adam.[^cite-47]

The Commander of the Faithful arose in the world perfecting the vicegerency (khilāfa, caliphate) of the Creator among His creatures, dealing with creation in all circumstances as Allah deals with His creatures regarding their constitution, sustenance, and the abundance of His mercy. Each who arose before him carried out a small portion of this; the Commander of the Faithful carried out the whole. A vicegerent carries out among those appointed to him everything done by the one who appointed him. The Prophet said: "The ruler is the shadow of Allah on the earth"—meaning the Commander of the Faithful, because the shadow imitates what casts it in all its qualities. No one deals with creatures as Allah does except the Commander of the Faithful.[^cite-48]

The philosophers have said that philosophy is seeking to resemble Allah according to human capacity—dealing with mankind as Allah deals with them, interacting according to their nature. The philosophers stole this speech from the people of truth, though none but the Commander of the Faithful has carried out its true meaning. All his treatment of humankind accords with Allah's treatment in the nature of the world, sustenance, and other matters.[^cite-49]

The compassion, mercy, bounty, and forgiveness of the Commander of the Faithful, as well as his tolerance toward people's misbehavior and their disobedience, are impossible to describe. They follow Allah's actions toward humanity, following Allah's command and adopting His manner. Surely Allah bestows blessings on people, pious or impious, believers or unbelievers. If He gave material benefit only to believers and denied it to unbelievers, people's faith would be only for worldly gain. If He denied all to believers and gave only to unbelievers, most would revert from belief to unbelief. Thus He gave to some unbelievers and denied to others, just as He gave to some believers and denied to others, giving to each at some times and denying at others.

Similarly, the Commander of the Faithful bestows worldly benefit on Muslims and unbelievers, believers and hypocrites alike, denying to them too. If he gave everything to believers, their faith would be mostly for the world; if he denied all believers always, they would despair and their good intention would weaken. Thus the Commander of the Faithful for a time bestowed blessings the likes of which none had bestowed, and at times withheld for their benefit, that they not become like those of whom Allah said: "Nay, man certainly becomes rebellious, considering himself self-sufficient."[^cite-50]

Allah gave worldly goods to the pious and impious alike, so that the deeds of the pious would not be for the sake of this world, nor restraint from immorality only for it, nor restraint from disobedience only for worldly benefit—that they not be deprived of reward in the hereafter but be exclusively for religion and Allah. Similarly, the Commander of the Faithful gives worldly benefits to the righteous and immoral alike.[^cite-51]

Allah has concealed the means to acquire worldly benefits, so that one becomes rich by one means while another through the same means has nothing; one becomes wealthy through trade while another goes bankrupt; one becomes rich through agriculture while another loses his seeds; one becomes self-sufficient through hunting while another is impoverished. All this reflects His exceeding wisdom, so He does not provide easy access to the world lest all other accesses become void and disorder pervade creation.

Similarly, the Commander of the Faithful conceals the means of attaining worldly fortune through him, giving to both the deserving and undeserving. If they knew a particular means by which all could obtain worldly goods from him, all other means would become futile, the undeserving would despair of his mercy being deprived, and there would be no merit in that.[^cite-52]

The interaction of the Commander of the Faithful regarding his proximity to people close to him and his distance from them follows the same principle. If he removed the hypocrites and brought believers close, all would become believers because of proximity, not religion. If he kept distance from all believers always, they would avoid him and anxiety would crop into their hearts, either from perceived anger or from a slip necessitating that. Thus he deals with them according to what is appropriate to prevailing times and conditions—awakening desire, alarm, removing or bringing close, granting or denying, punishing or forgiving—all following Allah's actions toward creation, so people perpetually remain in both fear and hope, neither feeling secure lest they become bold, nor despairing so as to become despondent.[^cite-53]

Those who look through the eye of reality at the actions of the Commander of the Faithful in religion and worldly matters will find that everything he does—commanding good, forbidding evil, granting and denying—parallels Allah's actions. But people are baffled because they view his actions as those of ordinary humans, comparing him with other rulers and what they have heard and understood, regarding that as opposition to what they are accustomed to. Had they viewed it in relation to Allah's actions, all would be easier. It is difficult only because, from Adam's time to now, no one has done anything like this. If anyone acted thus, it was only a small portion. Another instance is what is reported of the righteous servant (al-Khiḍr) with Moses—Moses was not patient about his actions as he saw them in relation to his own knowledge and intellect. Had he viewed them in relation to Allah's actions, it would have been contrary to what actually happened.[^cite-54]

The gifts of other monarchs are perceived as given for service, relationship, obligation, love, need, seeking benefit, or averting harm; or out of fear, expectation of fortune, fear of someone's tongue, seeking praise, desire of gratitude, as a reward to an official, or as sustenance. All is done through appraisal and assessment. But the gifts of the Commander of the Faithful transcend all this, because such gifts are not called generosity or virtue but payment of obligation or advance payment for future benefit. The gifts of Allah exceed all this, for nothing obligates Allah to give anyone, nor does He seek reward. Similarly, the Commander of the Faithful does not give for such reasons, nor demand compensation. Allah said regarding the Commander of the Faithful ʿAlī: "We do not desire reward or gratitude from them." Rather, he bestows spontaneously out of generosity, nobility, and virtue, following Allah's actions with creation.[^cite-55]

Just as Allah concealed the reasons for creating the world and explained the reasons of religion and means of attaining it, so the Commander of the Faithful concealed the means of attaining worldly fortune, giving to both deserving and undeserving, yet explained the means of attaining religion, knowledge, and wisdom, bestowing them upon those who deserved without withholding or miserliness. He bountifully bestowed favors on people, giving more than anyone could in any time or age, while withholding from the undeserving, differentiating between the people of truth (ahl al-ḥaqq) and the people of falsehood (ahl al-bāṭil), following Allah's actions and ways in governing the world and religion, building this present world and the hereafter.[^cite-56]

We ask Allah to bestow upon us gratitude for what He has blessed us with in words, deeds, and intention, and to grant us success in obedience to the lord of the age and time, peace upon him. May He provide us the means of struggling on his behalf with our hands, tongues, and hearts. May Allah open the entire world for him and through his sword purify the earth of polytheism, unbelief, and hypocrisy. This servant begs and desires the blessings of the Commander of the Faithful, peace upon him, and seeks his supreme command to examine what the servant has gathered and compiled, to grant permission for what is sound and correct, and to draw attention to mistakes, so the servant may be thankful in increasing insight and certainty, making this a means of guidance and evidence for those who seek to be guided and a proof against those who deny.[^cite-57]

Praise be to Allah, the praise of the grateful, mindful, and pious, and prayers upon His Messenger, our master Muhammad, and upon all his family.
