# Imam's Wajib and Virtues

## Special Prophethood and General Prophethood

Two forms of prophethood exist: one particular and revelatory, the other universal and intrinsic to human nature. The universal prophethood is both physical and rational. When the Almighty created the human form (⟪ar:صورۃ انسانیۃ⟫), He endowed it with all the organs necessary for its flourishing. Through these bodily instruments, the human soul fulfills its physical needs and executes the functions of animal life. Yet Allah distinguished mankind from all other creatures by instilling another power and essence—the intellect (ʿaql)—which neither beasts nor irrational beings possess. By means of this intellect, the human discerns between good and evil, truth and falsehood. Through it one comprehends commandments and prohibitions (⟪ar:أمرو نواھی⟫) and obeys the guidance of parents, teachers, and mentors. This intellect constitutes the first prophethood.

The particular prophethood belongs to the Messenger of Allah (upon whom be peace and blessings), who is the true guide vested with knowledge of the unseen by divine bestowal, guiding humanity toward salvation in the Hereafter. The prophets are the guides of their age, the successors (awṣiyāʾ) and Imams of all times. General prophethood proves indispensable for the reception of particular prophethood; one bereft of the intellect cannot accept the special revelation embodied in the Sharīʿah of Muhammad (⟪ar:شریعت محمدی علیہ السلام⟫)—which is why the insane and young children fall outside its purview.[^cite-1]

The natural and eternal intellect permits one to discern worldly gain and loss, to sustain bodily existence through food and drink, to provide oneself comfort. In such a state, one remains unmindful of the Hereafter and its rewards. When the second prophethood—the guide—takes hold, the soul's end-state improves. Through adherence to this second prophet, one becomes aware of one's ultimate outcome and the rewards and recompense awaiting. The guide forbids the first intellect from evil and adorns the soul with knowledge. Yet so long as the first messenger remains disconnected from the second, one incurs no obligation under the Sharīʿah and thus forfeits its reward.

## Why Messengers Are Necessary

Prophethood has endured since the time of Adam (upon him be peace) and shall persist eternally as long as his descendants remain. The primitive intellect (ʿaql awwalī), present in every human body from infancy as consciousness develops, bestows understanding of harm and benefit, teaching discrimination and differentiation.[^cite-2]

It is therefore incumbent upon one possessed of intellect to deliberate carefully upon one's end and the state of the Hereafter, to remain steadfast in faith and dignity, to guard the secrets of the summons (daʿwa), to seek knowledge and virtue, to embrace asceticism (zuhd) and pious abstinence (waraʿ) in the world, to honor trusts, to maintain silence, and to labor with constancy. One must likewise avoid base actions that oppose these virtues. Yet the intellect alone proves insufficient to grasp all such matters. By its own strength and capacity, it cannot encompass these truths. Thus arises the necessity for a guide—a summoner (dāʿī, pl. duʿāt)—who can reveal what pleases the Almighty and what provokes His anger.[^cite-3] This guide, whom Allah has appointed and selected to better the fate of His servants, is distinguished from ordinary people by virtue of special capacities and pure thoughts, made capable of receiving revelation, and distinguished by divine support.

Man exists between two conditions: above, the spiritual intellect shines with luminosity; below lies the corporeal realm, dark and oppressive. Within him dwells an evil force—the appetitive and irascible soul (quwwat shuhwāniyyah wa-ghadabiyyah, ⟪ar:قوت شھوانیۃ و غضبیۃ⟫)—whose demands of the body permit no survival without nourishment and the satisfaction of desires. Should one incline wholly toward this carnal self (nafs shuhwāniyyah ghadabiyyah), consuming all energy in eating, drinking, and luxury like cattle, the appetitive self overcomes the intellect. One becomes estranged from religion and absorbed in satisfying one's base inclinations, degenerating into a state worse than the brute creation. Indeed, what was intended as the noblest of creatures (⟪ar:اشرف المخلوقات⟫) becomes the basest (⟪ar:أَشَرُّ المَخلُوقَات⟫), stripped entirely of human virtue.

In such circumstances, divine wisdom deemed it fitting to grant the first intellect a guide who would testify that another realm exists—free from impurities and corruption—beside the physical world. This guide instructs and cultivates the intellect, imposing upon it, as a teacher does, all that is necessary and beneficial for its welfare and salvation. The guide clarifies what is permissible and what is forbidden (⟪ar:أمر ونواھی⟫), commanding strict observance of the Sharīʿah's ordinances and restraining from the illicit through warnings and severe penalties. Thus the end-state of one endowed with intellect is improved, ensuring he does not fall from "the finest form" (⟪ar:احسن تقویم⟫) toward "the lowest of the low" (⟪ar:اسفل السافلین⟫). Concerning this, the Prophet declared:

> ⟪quran 6:48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا نُرْسِلُ ٱلْمُرْسَلِينَ إِلَّا مُبَشِّرِينَ وَمُنذِرِينَ ۖ فَمَنْ آمَنَ وَأَصْلَحَ فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *And We send not the Messengers but as givers of glad tidings and as warners. So whosoever believes and does righteous good deeds, upon such shall come no fear, nor shall they grieve.*

The messengers are bearers of glad tidings concerning the Hereafter and warners of the punishment of Hell. Both acts—the bestowal of glad tidings and the issuance of warnings—pertain to the reward or penalty of deeds in the Hereafter.

**Esoteric Interpretation:** The glad tidings of the Messenger of Allah (upon whom be peace) were in truth the glad tidings of ʿAlī to the believers; his warning was to alert the disbelievers and the hypocrites concerning ʿAlī, for on the Day of Resurrection ʿAlī shall distinguish between truth and falsehood.

## Why Successors Are Necessary

When the truth and legitimacy of the first and second prophets stand established, a further question arises: What necessity exists for the institution of the successors (awṣiyāʾ)? Some assert: having accepted the Messenger of Allah and possessing the commandments of the Almighty in the form of the Qur'ān, why should we require a guardian? We can believe in the Qur'ānic ordinances, in Allah, and in the Last Day. A successor is superfluous. Allah, however, addresses this very delusion:

> ⟪quran 2:8⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَقُولُ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَبِٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ وَمَا هُم بِمُؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫
> 
> *And of mankind, there are some (hypocrites) who say: "We believe in Allah and the Last Day" while in fact they believe not.*

Those who make such claims rely upon their own intellect and understanding to affirm the Oneness of Allah, supposing that knowledge of the Divine is innate to human nature. Yet this oversight ignores a fundamental truth: speech itself, though a natural faculty, cannot be exercised by a human child without instruction from parents or teachers. This is evidenced when a child reared among wild beasts in a forest learns only their cries and lacks the capacity for human speech. If man cannot manage so natural a faculty without a teacher, how can he grasp the way to Allah without a guide (walī) and interpreter (ʿallām)?

It thus becomes clear: without the Messenger of Allah, knowledge of the Divine remains impossible. Similarly, to comprehend the entirety of religion—its principles and the secrets embedded in the Qur'ān—there is need for a guide and interpreter resembling the Messenger, serving as intermediary between Allah and His servants. This is the Successor (al-Waṣī), who stands as the Messenger's deputy after the latter's earthly life. He elucidates the meanings of the Messenger's utterances and interprets the truths concealed within them. The Messenger conveys the commandments: "Do such and such!" The Successor, by contrast, explains the meaning and the hidden rationale—the why and the wherefore of each ordinance, and what benefit accrues in the Hereafter from its performance. Thus the Messenger pronounces the "words" (alfāẓ), while the Successor unveils their "meanings" (maʿānī)—and meaning, always, is the true purpose of the word.

All principles of faith commence with knowledge of the Divine Oneness (tawḥīd), a knowledge purified of analogy—stripping from the Creator all attributes of created things and refuting innovations. One must recognize that the Creator is neither corporeal nor composed of water and clay; attributes of physicality pertain not to Him. Whatever imagination arises in the mind is not the Divine; our most subtle conceptions are themselves created, fashioned, and brought into being, whereas the Creator transcends our imagination entirely, the originator of all spiritual and physical boundaries, unbound by them. This is the foundation of faith, by which religion stands and is preserved, and by whose loss religion falls into ruin.

The Messenger therefore establishes his Successor to clarify and expound all these truths. Were the Book of Allah capable of self-interpretation, the word of the Messenger would be meaningless. Yet Allah ordains:

> ⟪quran 4:83⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا جَآءَهُمْ أَمْرٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْأَمْنِ أَوِ ٱلْخَوْفِ أَذَاعُوا۟ بِهِۦ ۖ وَلَوْ رَدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ وَإِلَىٰٓ أُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنْهُمْ لَعَلِمَهُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَنۢبِطُونَهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ وَلَوْلَا فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَتُهُۥ لَٱتَّبَعْتُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> 
> *When there comes to them some matter touching (public) safety or fear, they make it known (among the people), if only they had referred it to the Messenger or to those charged with authority among them, the proper investigators would have understood it from them (directly). Had it not been for the Grace and Mercy of Allah upon you, you would have followed Shaitan (Satan), save a few of you.*

Had the claim of certain companions been true—that "the Book of Allah suffices" (⟪ar:'حسبنا کتاب اللہ'⟫)—then they would not have continually sought counsel from ʿAlī in every ruling and decision. The very saying "Without ʿAlī, ʿUmar would have been ruined" (⟪ar:لو لا علی لھلک عمر⟫) attests to this dependence. Those who usurp the stations of the Imams on the basis of outward piety and apparent virtue resemble one who adorns himself outwardly while remaining inwardly poor—when blindness overtakes him, he calls himself blind. Such a person deceives only himself, as Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 2:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يُخَدِعُونَ ٱللَّهَ وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَمَا يَخْدَعُونَ إِلَّآ أَنفُسَهُمْ وَمَا يَشْعُرُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *They (think to) deceive Allah and those who believe, while they only deceive themselves, and perceive (it) not!*

The Day of Resurrection and the Day of Judgment (⟪ar:'الیوم الاخر'⟫)—referred to as the completion of the days of Allah (⟪ar:ذات قائم القیامۃ علی ذکرہ السلام⟫)—is intimated in the verse:

> ⟪quran 45:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ يَغْفِرُوا۟ لِلَّذِينَ لَا يَرْجُونَ أَيَّامَ ٱللَّهِ لِيَجْزِىَ قَوْمًۢا بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH) to the believers to forgive those who (harm them and) hope not for the Days of Allah (i.e. His Recompense), that He may recompense people according to what they have earned (i.e. to punish these disbelievers, who harm the believers).*

When Allah speaks of rewarding people for their deeds, one must ask: if "days" referred merely to the lifeless rotations of the calendar, how could they dispense reward or punishment? The term here bears a special meaning. It refers to the entire spiritual cycle and its completion; the "days of Allah" are the noble prophets (⟪ar:'انبیاء کرام'⟫), and the Qāʾim (the Riser) is the terminus of these days. In their hands lies both the reckoning and the recompense—first the upheaval in religion itself, then the ultimate accounting.

## Why Connection to the Second Messenger Becomes Difficult

The benefit of the intellect's light accrues only to those who accept the law of reason and obey its commandments. In general, the soul resists the human law because it has grown accustomed to following the comfort of base desire and to avoiding that which appears repugnant to it. The soul urges toward evil (⟪ar:نفس امارۃ بالسوء⟫); the Sharīʿah (⟪ar:رسول علیہ السلام⟫) stands opposed to it.

Consider the example of fire, which illuminates the world and warms the atmosphere. First it is kindled in wood; when the agent ignites and sustains it, one derives benefit from it. Similarly, the messengers illuminate and kindle the natural intellect through the teachings and commandments of their law. Without messengers, man cannot attain the light of guidance on his own. The Ummah is thus one body in its entirety; the Messenger is its heart. The companions represent the senses and instruments. The inner knowledge (ʿilm bāṭin) of the Messenger constitutes the vital spirit (rūḥ, ⟪ar:'روح'⟫) of the body, while the Messenger's outward knowledge manifests as limbs, muscles, and organs obedient to his command, rendering humanity worthy of salvation in the Hereafter.

## Spiritual Parents and Their Obedience

The Almighty has constituted the subtle essence of the human soul upon the dense essence of the body so that it might synthesize perceptions of the sensible (muḥassasāt, ⟪ar:محسوسات⟫) with concepts of the intellect (maʿqūlāt, ⟪ar:معقولات⟫). Man possesses a body—visible and felt—and a soul—neither visible nor felt. These two disparate realities cannot develop without both physical and spiritual parentage.

### Physical and Spiritual Parents

Just as bodily birth is possible only through parents and the union of bodies, it is equally necessary that spiritual development requires spiritual parents. The physical father bestows the gift of bodily creation; the mother receives and nurtures that gift. In the same manner, the spiritual father—like the physical father—is the giver of the soul, and the spiritual mother—like the physical mother—is the receiver and nurturer of the soul. In the position of the soul's father stands the Messenger of Allah (upon whom be peace and blessings) (⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و سلم⟫), and in the position of the soul's mother stands his Successor, the Commander of the Faithful ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫).

The outer revelation (tanzīl, ⟪ar:تنزیل⟫) serves as the example of the father's seed in the spiritual creation of the believer; the esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl, ⟪ar:تاویل⟫) serves as the example of the mother's seed. By the union of these two spiritual seeds, the believer is adorned with a form fit for the spiritual world, just as the commingling of two bodily seeds creates a form for the physical world. The Messenger of Allah confirmed this truth:

> "I and you, O ʿAlī, are the father and mother of the believers." (⟪ar-quote:اَنَا وَاَنْتَ یَا عَلِیٌّ اَبُوْ وَاُمُّ الْمُؤمِنِینَ⟫)

This doctrine is affirmed by Allah's word:

> ⟪quran 33:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلنَّبِىُّ أَوْلَىٰ بِٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ مِنْ أَنفُسِهِمْ ۖ وَأَزْوَجُهُۥٓ أُمَّهَتُهُمْ ۗ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَرْحَامِ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلَىٰ بِبَعْضٍۢ فِى كِتَبِ ٱللَّهِ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ إِلَّآ أَن تَفْعَلُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآئِكُم مَّعْرُوفًۭا ۚ كَانَ ذَلِكَ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ مَسْطُورًۭا ‎⟫
> 
> *The Prophet is closer to the believers than their ownselves, and his wives are their (believers') mothers (as regards respect and marriage). And blood relations among each other have closer personal ties in the Decree of Allah (regarding inheritance) than (the brotherhood of) the believers and the Muhajirun (emigrants from Makkah, etc.), except that you do kindness to those brothers (when the Prophet PBUH joined them in brotherhood ties). This has been written in the (Allah's Book of Divine) Decrees (Al-Lauh Al-Mahfuz).*

### Heaven and Earth

From esoteric interpretation, we know that every prophet's Successor is his wife. When the Qur'ān designates the Successor as the mother of the believers, it necessarily follows that the prophets became fathers of the believers. Thus it is said:

> "The earth is your mother, and she is merciful toward you." (⟪ar-quote:اَلاَرضُ اُمُّکُمْ وَھِیَ بِکُمْ بَرَّۃٌ⟫)

In this ḥadīth, the Prophet (upon whom be peace) refers to the Successors (⟪ar:اوصیاء علیہم السلام⟫) as the mothers of believers. The heavens water the earth through rain and celestial light; the earth receives this water and nourishes minerals, plants, and animals. Thus the Qur'ān declares:

> ⟪quran 22:5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ إِن كُنتُمْ فِى رَيْبٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْبَعْثِ فَإِنَّا خَلَقْنَكُم مِّن تُرَابٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن نُّطْفَةٍۢ ثُمَّ مِنْ عَلَقَةٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن مُّضْغَةٍۢ مُّخَلَّقَةٍۢ وَغَيْرِ مُخَلَّقَةٍۢ لِّنُبَيِّنَ لَكُمْ ۚ وَنُقِرُّ فِى ٱلْأَرْحَامِ مَا نَشَآءُ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّۭى ثُمَّ نُخْرِجُكُمْ طِفْلًۭا ثُمَّ لِتَبْلُغُوٓا۟ أَشُدَّكُمْ ۖ وَمِنكُم مَّن يُتَوَفَّىٰ وَمِنكُم مَّن يُرَدُّ إِلَىٰٓ أَرْذَلِ ٱلْعُمُرِ لِكَيْلَا يَعْلَمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ عِلْمٍۢ شَيْـًۭٔا ۚ وَتَرَى ٱلْأَرْضَ هَامِدَةًۭ فَإِذَآ أَنزَلْنَا عَلَيْهَا ٱلْمَآءَ ٱهْتَزَّتْ وَرَبَتْ وَأَنۢبَتَتْ مِن كُلِّ زَوْجٍۭ بَهِيجٍۢ ‎⟫
> 
> *O mankind! If you are in doubt about the Resurrection, then verily! We have created you (i.e. Adam) from dust, then from a Nutfah (mixed drops of male and female sexual discharge i.e. offspring of Adam), then from a clot (a piece of thick coagulated blood) then from a little lump of flesh, some formed and some unformed (miscarriage), that We may make (it) clear to you (i.e. to show you Our Power and Ability to do what We will). And We cause whom We will to remain in the wombs for an appointed term, then We bring you out as infants, then (give you growth) that you may reach your age of full strength. And among you there is he who dies (young), and among you there is he who is brought back to the miserable old age, so that he knows nothing after having known. And you see the earth barren, but when We send down water (rain) on it, it is stirred (to life), it swells and puts forth every lovely kind (of growth).*

The sustainability of the Prophet (Nāṭiq, the "Speaker" of the Sharīʿah) occupies the position of heaven and father; the Foundation (Asās, the Successor) stands in the position of earth and mother. Rainfall, in its esoteric sense, is the outer revelation (tanzīl); all manner of vegetation that sprouts forth is the interpretation (taʾwīl). Thus the summoner invites all to the outward deed, while the Successor illuminates for all the reality and interpretation through which the soul is spiritually nourished. The earth cannot create until heaven sends down water; heaven cannot bring forth a pair without the earth. That is, the human soul can be nurtured only through both revelation and its interpretation. The Almighty confirms this elsewhere:

> ⟪quran 43:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۢ بِقَدَرٍۢ فَأَنشَرْنَا بِهِۦ بَلْدَةًۭ مَّيْتًۭا ۚ كَذَلِكَ تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *And Who sends down water (rain) from the sky in due measure. Then We revive a dead land therewith, and even so you will be brought forth (from the dead),*

### The Body and Soul of the Law

The outer manifestation (tanzīl) is the body; the esoteric meaning (taʾwīl) is the soul. Physical death occurs when the soul departs the body. The manifestation of the Sharīʿah pertains to the body; interpretation pertains to the soul. If one practices the law only in its outward aspect, it becomes like a corpse—lifeless, lacking soul. If one pursues interpretation without outward practice, it becomes like a soul divorced from body, powerless to act. Unless both tanzīl and taʾwīl unite, spiritual felicity cannot be attained—just as physical pleasure cannot be realized unless soul and body remain joined.

### Obligation of the Imam

Since it has been established that there is no substitute for heaven and earth in the generation of all things in the physical realm, and that human birth is impossible without physical parents, it necessarily follows that believers in all times require spiritual parents. People must identify their spiritual parentage lest they become orphans of the spirit. One who recognizes the Imam is obliged to obey him while in his presence; if absent, one must obey whomever the Imam has appointed as his representative in that locality. The Qur'ān confirms this:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> 
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority. (And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day. That is better and more suitable for final determination.*

The Imam's necessity extends to both the worldly and the spiritual orders. Were the Imam necessary for the world alone, religion would become superfluous—and the Almighty, far too exalted, would not waste the immense effort the prophets expended to establish a faith that is the finest of all things. Were the Imam necessary only for religion without worldly governance, the order of the world would fall into disarray. Allah could not appoint the Imam as leader of what is noble (religion) while preventing him from ordering what is inferior (temporal affairs). Thus has it been proven that the Imam is necessary for both the worldly and the spiritual domains.

## The Proof of the Imam's Existence

Mankind received a faculty that no other animal obtained: the native intellect (ʿaql gharīzī). When Allah bestowed wisdom upon man, He made it incumbent to send a teacher for this sacred gift, a perfector of man's natural intellect. As Allah granted animals a soul to consume and subsist, He appointed elements, stars, and heavens to produce vegetation for their nourishment, sustaining their bodies perpetually. In the wisdom and justice of the wise, it would be improper to create a needy being without also creating one who fulfills that need.[^cite-4]

Since Allah has uniquely bestowed the native intellect upon mankind alone, it is inconceivable that the sustenance of this intellect—knowledge itself—be given to only one being, and that being the Imam. Were this knowledge merely external, all people could acquire it through diligent effort. But since this inner knowledge is bestowed upon a single individual alone, so too must the duty of teaching it belong to one person. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 7:63⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَوَعَجِبْتُمْ أَن جَآءَكُمْ ذِكْرٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ عَلَىٰ رَجُلٍۢ مِّنكُمْ لِيُنذِرَكُمْ وَلِتَتَّقُوا۟ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تُرْحَمُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *"Do you wonder that there has come to you a Reminder from your Lord through a man from amongst you, that he may warn you, so that you may fear Allah and that you may receive (His) Mercy?"*

Allah employs the singular "man" (rajul, ⟪ar:رجل⟫), not the plural "men" (rijāl, ⟪ar:رجال⟫). This establishes that the bearer of wisdom and exhortation is a single person—the sole prophet of his age, the sole Successor of his time, the sole Imam in all eras. So long as this world endures, humanity shall never lack that solitary figure. No human can supplant this station or claim its rank.

**Esoteric Interpretation:** The word "rijāl" (men) also refers to the singular Summoner (dāʿī munfarid, ⟪ar:داعی منفرد⟫), for in his station as a Caliph (wilāyah, ⟪ar:کَہُولیت⟫), he possesses the power to create a man like himself—another Summoner—in the summons, and to make him his successor. This successor, in turn, designates witnesses through the Summoner's text.

Consider this analogy: among many cattle, even the mightiest cannot protect its fellows nor stand as a shepherd. A shepherd must be human—wiser than the cattle, understanding their good and ill better than they. Similarly, though we humans possess intellect, our intellects lack the "spiritual knowledge" (ʿilm rūḥānī) intrinsic to the Imam. We are ignorant and helpless before his knowledge, as we are before all human wisdom. We desperately need his knowledge for our salvation in the Hereafter. Explaining the Imam's superiority over humanity, Allah states:

> ⟪quran 3:110⟫
> ⟪ar:‏كُنتُمْ خَيْرَ أُمَّةٍ أُخْرِجَتْ لِلنَّاسِ تَأْمُرُونَ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَتَنْهَوْنَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ وَتُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ ۗ وَلَوْ آمَنَ أَهْلُ ٱلْكِتَبِ لَكَانَ خَيْرًۭا لَّهُم ۚ مِّنْهُمُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ وَأَكْثَرُهُمُ ٱلْفَسِقُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *You [true believers in Islamic Monotheism, and real followers of Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Sunnah (legal ways, etc.)] are the best of peoples ever raised up for mankind; you enjoin Al-Ma'ruf (i.e. Islamic Monotheism and all that Islam has ordained) and forbid Al-Munkar (polytheism, disbelief and all that Islam has forbidden), and you believe in Allah. And had the people of the Scripture (Jews and Christians) believed, it would have been better for them; among them are some who have faith, but most of them are Al-Fasiqun (disobedient to Allah - and rebellious against Allah's Command).*

This verse refers to the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt (upon whom be peace), who enjoin the good and forbid evil. The designation "best nation" (khayr ummah) does not derive from their outward appearance but from their unparalleled character and unmistakable knowledge. Thus it is shown that every Imam surpasses in goodness, honor, and excellence the entire community of his time. The verse's use of the past tense—"You have been"—indicates not merely present excellence but a station that has ever pertained, stretching backward to prior ages.

**Esoteric Interpretation:** The term "best" (khayr) always denotes elevation of rank and status, just as the Prophet (upon whom be peace) referred to the station of our Mawlā ʿAlī and affirmed his rank as a Successor:

> "Both Ḥasan and Ḥusayn are the Imams of truth—whether they stand or sit—and their father is better than both of them." (⟪ar-quote:اَلحَسَنُ وَالحُسَینُ اِماَمَ حَقّ قاَمَ اَو قَعَدَ وَ أَبُوھُماَ خَیرٌ مِن ھُماَ⟫)

## Who Has the Right to Choose the Imam?

The creed held by some of the common people (ʿawāmm) asserts that people themselves possess the right to select the Imam. They deny that the Messenger of Allah (upon whom be peace and blessings) appointed anyone as his deputy and caliph to discharge the duties of the Imamate after him. Another group claims that the Messenger did indicate a person (Abū Bakr) without explicitly naming him. This indication, they allege, consisted in his commanding that individual to lead the prayers; since prayer stands closely connected to alms (zakāh), it became evident to all Muslims that they must entrust their alms-tax to this person after the Messenger's death.

The Murji'ah sect maintains that it is incumbent upon the people to appoint a learned and virtuous person—one capable of applying wisdom to religious affairs and issuing rulings according to the Book of Allah and the Sunnah of the Prophet. Regarding matters not explicitly resolved in these sources, he must exercise independent judgment. Obedience to such an Imam is obligatory so long as he himself remains obedient to Allah. When he disobeys the Almighty, his obedience no longer becomes binding, nor do people possess the authority to remove him and elect another.

The Muʿtazilah sect declares that the Messenger (upon whom be peace) neither pointed to nor nominated anyone. People, they hold, possess the right to appoint whoever they wish as Imam after the Prophet's passing. Thus did the people elect Abū Bakr as Imam following the Holy Prophet's death.

The Khawārij sect claims ignorance: "We have no knowledge nor have we received reports as to whether the Messenger of Allah issued any ruling regarding the position of the Imamate." Yet they acknowledge the necessity of an Imam to issue divine commands among Allah's servants, and therefore propose to select their own Imam.

---

**Refutation by Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān (may Allah elevate his station):**

To those who say, "The Messenger of Allah appointed no one as Imam of his community after him," Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān replies: "It is impossible for us or you to accept such an evident denial, for one who denies the existence of an event cannot serve as a witness. The witness is one who affirms the event and declares having witnessed it with his own eyes. Similarly, you have denied the existence of the Imam and rejected what you dislike. But the person who believes in the Imam's existence is more entitled to credence, and his testimony is more obligatory than yours."

He continues: "The Messenger said, 'I have named my Caliph—whom you too proclaim, and whose testimony your own sect bears, and which stands written in your books.' The testimony of those who witnessed and heard the Messenger make this proclamation (⟪ar:مقام غدیر خم⟫ at the station of Ghadīr Khumm) surpasses that of those claiming they saw and heard nothing. Thus did he announce: 'Whoever I am the protector of, ʿAlī is his protector' (⟪ar-quote:من کنت مولاہ فھذا علی مولاہ⟫). If the selection of an Imam were a matter of popular choice, would the Messenger not have proclaimed, 'Choose a fitting Imam for yourselves after me'? Instead, he asked Allah: 'O Allah, befriend whoever befriends him, and be hostile to whoever is hostile to him; aid whoever aids him, and humiliate whoever humiliates him' (⟪ar-quote:اللھم وال من والاہ و عاد من عاداہ و نصر من نصرہ و خزل من خزلہ⟫)."

A second argument: If, as you claim, the Imam and Caliph are chosen by the people, then such an Imam must obey the people—for he attained his position and lordship only through their selection. By this logic, those who choose the Imam are the Imams themselves, and he whom they choose becomes their agent. The people could then remove him whenever they wish and elect another. This is absurd. If people were permitted to choose their own imams, they could equally appoint prophets, for Allah has made obedience to the Imams obligatory as He made obedience to the prophets obligatory. The Imams succeed the prophets in their communities just as prophets are Allah's vicegerents among humankind. As for the Khawārij's claim of ignorance—their very ignorance condemns them, for the ignorant are obliged to seek truth from the learned.

A third argument rests on the Qur'ānic verse:

> ⟪quran 5:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏حُرِّمَتْ عَلَيْكُمُ ٱلْمَيْتَةُ وَٱلدَّمُ وَلَحْمُ ٱلْخِنزِيرِ وَمَآ أُهِلَّ لِغَيْرِ ٱللَّهِ بِهِۦ وَٱلْمُنْخَنِقَةُ وَٱلْمَوْقُوذَةُ وَٱلْمُتَرَدِّيَةُ وَٱلنَّطِيحَةُ وَمَآ أَكَلَ ٱلسَّبُعُ إِلَّا مَا ذَكَّيْتُمْ وَمَا ذُبِحَ عَلَى ٱلنُّصُبِ وَأَن تَسْتَقْسِمُوا۟ بِٱلْأَزْلَمِ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ فِسْقٌ ۗ ٱلْيَوْمَ يَئِسَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ مِن دِينِكُمْ فَلَا تَخْشَوْهُمْ وَٱخْشَوْنِ ۚ ٱلْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِينَكُمْ وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِى وَرَضِيتُ لَكُمُ ٱلْإِسْلَمَ دِينًۭا ۚ فَمَنِ ٱضْطُرَّ فِى مَخْمَصَةٍ غَيْرُ مُتَجَانِفٍۢ لِّإِثْمٍۢ ۙ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> 
> *Forbidden to you (for food) are: Al-Maytatah (the dead animals - cattle-beast not slaughtered), blood, the flesh of swine, and the meat of that which has been slaughtered as a sacrifice for others than Allah, or has been slaughtered for idols, etc., or on which Allah's Name has not been mentioned while slaughtering, and that which has been killed by strangling, or by a violent blow, or by a headlong fall, or by the goring of horns - and that which has been (partly) eaten by a wild animal - unless you are able to slaughter it (before its death); and that which is sacrificed (slaughtered) on An-Nusub (stone altars). (Forbidden) also is to use arrows seeking luck or decision, (all) that is Fisqun (disobedience of Allah and sin). This day, those who disbelieved have given up all hope of your religion, so fear them not, but fear Me. This day, I have perfected your religion for you, completed My Favour upon you, and have chosen for you Islam as your religion. But as for him who is forced by severe hunger, with no inclination to sin (such can eat these above-mentioned meats), then surely, Allah is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful.*

This verse was revealed when the Messenger of Allah proclaimed the guardianship (walāyah) and imamate of ʿAlī at Ghadīr Khumm. How, then, can one claim that the Messenger failed to explain the Imamate—one of the most essential pillars of the Divine Religion? Did Allah declare, "I have perfected your religion," without first establishing its cornerstone? Did the people complete Allah's religion by electing an Imam? Did they do what Allah had commanded His Messenger to do, or did they neglect his duty?

If, as some allege, the Messenger pointed to Abū Bakr for the Imamate, why did Abū Bakr not establish the sign of his Imam or Caliph, as the Messenger had done? Instead, he consulted with ʿUmar, and these two (Abū Bakr and ʿUmar) together selected ʿUthmān, deviating from the Messenger's practice. They violated the Messenger's command by neither pointing to nor establishing the Imam, thus contradicting the divine instruction:

> ⟪quran 59:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَّآ أَفَآءَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ مِنْ أَهْلِ ٱلْقُرَىٰ فَلِلَّهِ وَلِلرَّسُولِ وَلِذِى ٱلْقُرْبَىٰ وَٱلْيَتَامَىٰ وَٱلْمَسَكِينِ وَٱبْنِ ٱلسَّبِيلِ كَىْ لَا يَكُونَ دُولَةًۢ بَيْنَ ٱلْأَغْنِيَآءِ مِنكُمْ ۚ وَمَآ آتَىٰكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوهُ وَمَا نَهَىٰكُمْ عَنْهُ فَٱنتَهُوا۟ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ شَدِيدُ ٱلْعِقَابِ ‎⟫
> 
> *What Allah gave as booty (Fai') to His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from the people of the townships, - it is for Allah, His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), the kindred (of Messenger Muhammad PBUH), the orphans, Al-Masakin (the poor), and the wayfarer, in order that it may not become a fortune used by the rich among you. And whatsoever the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) gives you, take it, and whatsoever he forbids you, abstain (from it), and fear Allah. Verily, Allah is Severe in punishment.*

Regarding the claim that the Messenger granted Abū Bakr the right to lead prayer—a fact narrated by ʿĀʾishah—if this conferred the Imamate upon him, then ʿUmar, who was given precedence over him in leading prayer, would have been more deserving of the Caliphate than Abū Bakr. When ʿAmr ibn al-ʿĀṣ was appointed as governor and military commander, with both ʿUmar and his other officers praying behind him, should not ʿAmr ibn al-ʿĀṣ have superseded them in the caliphate?

All are agreed that the Messenger never granted precedence over ʿAlī (upon whom be peace) to anyone. He declared: "Neither did ʿAlī (upon whom be peace) ever pray behind Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, or ʿUthmān; rather, all three of them prayed behind him many times." By this measure, ʿAlī (upon whom be peace) was far more deserving of the Imamate and Caliphate than all three.

When you reject the testimony of the Messenger of Allah regarding ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, why do you accept the testimony of his wife ʿĀʾishah regarding her father's leadership of prayer? You know well that when Abū Bakr began to lead the prayer at the Prophet's residence (Bayt al-Sharraf), the Messenger emerged and, while still in prayer, turned him back and himself assumed the leadership. Such examples demonstrate the Prophet's clear intention.

All arguments of those who deny the explicit designation (naṣṣ) are weak and devoid of substance. Can one imagine that the Messenger, who conveyed Allah's commands even regarding smiling, failed to clarify the paramount position of the Imamate and Caliphate? Can his prophethood be deemed complete in such circumstances? Can so weighty a duty be abandoned to the whims of the common people? The designation of an Imam can only occur through an irrefutable method—and that irrefutable method is none other than explicit text (naṣṣ), just as the Messenger (upon whom be peace) designated ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib at Ghadīr Khumm, appointing him as his caliph. ʿAlī thereafter designated his successor, as did Ḥasan, then Ḥusayn, then ʿAlī Zayn al-ʿAbidīn, then Muḥammad al-Bāqir, then Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, and this chain shall endure until the Day of Resurrection.

This practice has been established since Adam (upon whom be peace) through Nūḥ, Ibrāhīm, Mūsā, and ʿĪsā (upon whom be peace) down to the Seal of the Prophets, Muḥammad (upon whom be peace and blessings). Jesus declared:

> ⟪quran 61:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ عِيسَى ٱبْنُ مَرْيَمَ يَبَنِىٓ إِسْرَٓئيلَ إِنِّى رَسُولُ ٱللَّهِ إِلَيْكُم مُّصَدِّقًۭا لِّمَا بَيْنَ يَدَىَّ مِنَ ٱلتَّوْرَىٰةِ وَمُبَشِّرًۢا بِرَسُولٍۢ يَأْتِى مِنۢ بَعْدِى ٱسْمُهُۥٓ أَحْمَدُ ۖ فَلَمَّا جَآءَهُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ قَالُوا۟ هَذَا سِحْرٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> 
> *And (remember) when 'Iesa (Jesus), son of Maryam (Mary), said: "O Children of Israel! I am the Messenger of Allah unto you confirming the Taurat [(Torah) which came] before me, and giving glad tidings of a Messenger to come after me, whose name shall be Ahmed. But when he (Ahmed i.e. Muhammad PBUH) came to them with clear proofs, they said: "This is plain magic."*

No prophet has ever entrusted the Imamate and Caliphate to popular consultation. Each prophet appointed his own successor to discharge all his duties. How then could it be conceivable that Allah's Final Messenger (the Master of all Prophets and those sent, ⟪ar:سیدالانبیا و مرسلین⟫) would abandon this paramount duty to the people, when the People of the Household (⟪ar:اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫) themselves are the custodians of knowledge and divine guidance?

## Qur'anic Verses Indicating the Imam

The earth can never be empty without the existence of the Imam. When the Most High sent the Prophets to guide mankind, He did not limit their guidance to the people of a particular age, nor did He deprive those who came after them of such direction. According to the principle ordained in creation itself, no prophet can live eternally. As the Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:نبی اکرم (ﷺ)⟫ declares:

> ⟪quran 39:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّكَ مَيِّتٌۭ وَإِنَّهُم مَّيِّتُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily, you (O Muhammad PBUH) will die and verily, they (too) will die.*

Yet the Almighty upholds the Shariah, the divine law, that these prophetic teachings have conveyed. To preserve this sacred trust, there must be a protector—the Imam of the age—a living guide and proof (*ḥujjat*) of Allah upon His creation. The Qur'an attests:

> ⟪quran 13:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَيَقُولُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَوْلَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْهِ آيَةٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّهِۦٓ ۗ إِنَّمَآ أَنتَ مُنذِرٌۭ ۖ وَلِكُلِّ قَوْمٍ هَادٍ ‎⟫
> *And the disbelievers say: "Why is not a sign sent down to him from his Lord?" You are only a warner, and to every people there is a guide.*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ explained this verse thus: the *mundhir* (warner) refers to ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ Muhammad, the Chosen One, while the *hād* (guide) denotes ⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, the Commander of the Faithful. We ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫ who are the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt (the People of the House) succeed them; in every generation there is an Imam appointed to guide the believers and shepherd them toward the teachings of the Messenger.[^cite-5] Allah sent down His Book, the Qur'ān, as guidance, concerning which He says:

> ⟪quran 43:44⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِنَّهُۥ لَذِكْرٌۭ لَّكَ وَلِقَوْمِكَ ۖ وَسَوْفَ تُسْـَٔلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And verily, this (the Quran) is indeed a Reminder for you (O Muhammad PBUH) and your people, and you will be questioned (about it).*

And thereafter He proclaimed that He would safeguard this remembrance until the Day of Judgment:

> ⟪quran 15:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّا نَحْنُ نَزَّلْنَا ٱلذِّكْرَ وَإِنَّا لَهُۥ لَحَفِظُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily We: It is We Who have sent down the Dhikr (i.e. the Quran) and surely, We will guard it (from corruption).*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ declared: "Allah has entrusted the protection of His Remembrance to us ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫, the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt, and He has commanded that seekers of knowledge turn to us by saying:

> ⟪quran 16:43⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن قَبْلِكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۚ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent not (as Our Messengers) before you (O Muhammad PBUH) any but men, whom We inspired. So ask of those who know the Scripture if you know not.*

Allah gave this command because none may unveil the inner meanings (*taʾwīl*—esoteric exegesis) of the Book save Allah and those whom He has firmly established in knowledge. We are the Imams grounded in the knowledge of Allah." This is confirmed in the verse of the firm ones:

> ⟪quran 3:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسُخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *It is He Who has sent down to you (Muhammad PBUH) the Book (this Quran). In it are Verses that are entirely clear, they are the foundations of the Book; and others not entirely clear. So as for those in whose hearts there is a deviation (from the truth) they follow that which is not entirely clear thereof, seeking Al-Fitnah (polytheism and trials, etc.), and seeking for its hidden meanings, but none knows its hidden meanings save Allah. And those who are firmly grounded in knowledge say: "We believe in it; the whole of it (clear and unclear Verses) are from our Lord." And none receive admonition except men of understanding.*

The Imam's function encompasses teaching knowledge to the ignorant, guiding the seeker, answering the questions of the people with precision, making them aware of the ordinances of the Shariah, and awakening them to the true monotheism (*tawḥīd*) of Allah. All these responsibilities belong to him. We ⟪ar:مُؤَیَّد مِنَ اللہ⟫ are fortified by Allah, and innocent of falsehood.[^cite-6]

In his final moments, the Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ declared: "I leave behind two precious things among you: the Book of Allah and the gift of my Ahl al-Bayt. These two shall never be separated from one another until they meet me at the Pool of Kawsar." The Book of Allah—the *Qur'ān al-ṣāmit* (the Silent Qur'ān)—is made living and manifest by the ⟪ar:قرآن ناطق⟫ *Qur'ān al-nāṭiq* (the Speaking Qur'ān), the Messenger of Allah himself, and after him by those who explain and expound its truths.[^cite-7] That these two shall never separate from one another signifies that in every age there must be an Imam, a living interpreter of the divine word. This is declared in the verse of inheritance:

> ⟪quran 35:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ أَوْرَثْنَا ٱلْكِتَبَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱصْطَفَيْنَا مِنْ عِبَادِنَاۖ فَمِنْهُمْ ظَالِمٌۭ لِّنَفْسِهِۦ وَمِنْهُم مُّقْتَصِدٌۭ وَمِنْهُمْ سَابِقٌۢ بِٱلْخَيْرَتِ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِۚ ذَلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْفَضْلُ ٱلْكَبِيرُ ‎⟫
> *Then We gave the Book the Quran) for inheritance to such of Our slaves whom We chose. Then of them are some who wrong their ownselves, and of them are some who follow a middle course, and of them are some who are, by Allah's Leave, foremost in good deeds. That (inheritance of the Quran), that is indeed a great grace.*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ taught that in this verse, the *sābiq bi-l-khayrat* (those who excel in good deeds) refers to the Imam of each age; the *muqtaṣid* (those who follow the middle way) denotes those who acknowledge the rights of the Imam; and the *ẓālim li-nafsih* (one who wrongs himself) indicates those who doubt the Imam and distance themselves from him. The Prophet ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ said: ⟪ar-quote:مَن ماَتَ لاَ یَعِرفُ اِماَم دَھرِہِ ھِیَا ماَتَ مَیتَۃُ جاَھِلِیَّۃ⟫ "Whoever dies without recognizing his Imam of the age, he dies the death of ignorance."[^cite-8]

When Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ was asked who is referred to as the Imam in the verse, "On the Day when We shall call every nation with their Imams," he explained:

> ⟪quran 17:71⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَوْمَ نَدْعُوا۟ كُلَّ أُنَاسٍۭ بِإِمَمِهِمْ ۖ فَمَنْ أُوتِىَ كِتَبَهُۥ بِيَمِينِهِۦ فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ يَقْرَءُونَ كِتَبَهُمْ وَلَا يُظْلَمُونَ فَتِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(And remember) the Day when We shall call together all human beings with their (respective) Imam. So whosoever is given his record in his right hand, such will read their records, and they will not be dealt with unjustly in the least.*

The Imam, he said, refers to the one whom people have followed in this world. Thus those who obeyed and followed you during your lifetime shall be called alongside you on the Day of Resurrection. In this manner he explained the verse for all the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt.

Beyond these verses, the Qur'ān makes mention of the Imams in numerous other passages. Consider, for instance, the chapter of *al-Nisāʾ*:

> ⟪quran 4:54⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ يَحْسُدُونَ ٱلنَّاسَ عَلَىٰ مَآ آتَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ مِن فَضْلِهِۦ ۖ فَقَدْ آتَيْنَآ آلَإِبْرَهِيمَ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْحِكْمَةَ وَآتَيْنَهُم مُّلْكًا عَظِيمًۭا ‎⟫
> *Or do they envy men (Muhammad PBUH and his followers) for what Allah has given them of His Bounty? Then We had already given the family of Ibrahim (Abraham) the Book and Al-Hikmah (As-Sunnah - Divine Inspiration), and conferred upon them a great kingdom.*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ stated: "In this verse, *al-nās* (the people) refers to us, the Imams ⟪ar:اہل بیت⟫ of the Ahl al-Bayt." Allah has blessed us with the exalted station of Imamate, on account of which people harbor envy toward us. The *mulk ʿaẓīm* (great dominion) means that Allah ⟪ar:ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ through Abraham sustained the prophetic office and the Imamate in his descendants ⟪ar:اسحاق و اسماعیل علیھم السلام⟫ Isaac and Ishmael, and brought forth from their line Imams of sublime station (*jallīl al-qadr*) whose obedience is obedience to Allah and whose disobedience constitutes disobedience to Allah.[^cite-9] Yet when this great dominion ⟪ar:ملک عظیم آل محمد علیہ السلام⟫ of the House of Muhammad was bestowed, they refused to acknowledge it!

The Almighty further declares:

> ⟪quran 4:58⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَأْمُرُكُمْ أَن تُؤَدُّوا۟ ٱلْأَمَنَتِ إِلَىٰٓ أَهْلِهَا وَإِذَا حَكَمْتُم بَيْنَ ٱلنَّاسِ أَن تَحْكُمُوا۟ بِٱلْعَدْلِ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ نِعِمَّا يَعِظُكُم بِهِۦٓ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ سَمِيعًۢا بَصِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *Verily! Allah commands that you should render back the trusts to those, to whom they are due; and that when you judge between men, you judge with justice. Verily, how excellent is the teaching which He (Allah) gives you! Truly, Allah is Ever All-Hearer, All-Seer.*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ explained: "We are the Imams ⟪ar:اہل بیت⟫ of the Ahl al-Bayt to whom this command is addressed. Each of us entrusts to the Imam who succeeds him the sacred books, weapons, and knowledge that have been placed in our trust." In the following verse, Allah addresses the believers for all time until the Day of Resurrection:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority. (And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day. That is better and more suitable for final determination.*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ declared: "In this verse, we ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫ are those referred to as the *ūlū l-amr* (those possessed of authority) who are mentioned after obedience to Allah and His Messenger." The Qur'ān further states:

> ⟪quran 4:83⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا جَآءَهُمْ أَمْرٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْأَمْنِ أَوِ ٱلْخَوْفِ أَذَاعُوا۟ بِهِۦ ۖ وَلَوْ رَدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ وَإِلَىٰٓ أُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنْهُمْ لَعَلِمَهُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَنۢبِطُونَهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ وَلَوْلَا فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَتُهُۥ لَٱتَّبَعْتُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *When there comes to them some matter touching (public) safety or fear, they make it known (among the people), if only they had referred it to the Messenger or to those charged with authority among them, the proper investigators would have understood it from them (directly). Had it not been for the Grace and Mercy of Allah upon you, you would have followed Shaitan (Satan), save a few of you.*

We ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت رسول علیھم السلام⟫, the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt of the Messenger, alone possess the knowledge that the Lord of the Universe has granted us to perceive the depths of all matters. This is why Allah ⟪ar:اولو الامر⟫ has made obedience to us obligatory. In the chapter of *al-Māʾidah*, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 5:55⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّمَا وَلِيُّكُمُ ٱللَّهُ وَرَسُولُهُۥ وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱلَّذِينَ يُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَيُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُمْ رَكِعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily, your Walî (Protector or Helper) is Allah, His Messenger, and the believers, - those who perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and give Zakat, and they bow down (submit themselves with obedience to Allah in prayer).*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ explained: "Allah has pointed to us—those who establish prayer and give the poor-rate while bowing." The Almighty further says:

> ⟪quran 9:119⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَكُونُوا۟ مَعَ ٱلصَّدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Be afraid of Allah, and be with those who are true (in words and deeds).*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ said: "In this verse, the *al-ṣiddīqūn* (the truthful ones) refers to us, the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt." In the chapter of *al-Tawbah*, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 9:105⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱعْمَلُوا۟ فَسَيَرَى ٱللَّهُ عَمَلَكُمْ وَرَسُولُهُۥ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۖ وَسَتُرَدُّونَ إِلَىٰ عَلِمِ ٱلْغَيْبِ وَٱلشَّهَدَةِ فَيُنَبِّئُكُم بِمَا كُنتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And say (O Muhammad PBUH) "Do deeds! Allah will see your deeds, and (so will) His Messenger and the believers. And you will be brought back to the All-Knower of the unseen and the seen. Then He will inform you of what you used to do."*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ taught: "We are the *muminūn* (believers) referred to in this verse after Allah and His Messenger." We shall witness your deeds—both the hidden and the manifest—on the Day of Resurrection, and shall inform you of all that you have done.

In the chapter of *al-Baqarah*, the Almighty declares:

> ⟪quran 2:143⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِممَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Thus We have made you [true Muslims - real believers of Islamic Monotheism, true followers of Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Sunnah (legal ways)], a Wasat (just) (and the best) nation, that you be witnesses over mankind and the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) be a witness over you. And We made the Qiblah (prayer direction towards Jerusalem) which you used to face, only to test those who followed the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from those who would turn on their heels (i.e. disobey the Messenger). Indeed it was great (heavy) except for those whom Allah guided. And Allah would never make your faith (prayers) to be lost (i.e. your prayers offered towards Jerusalem). Truly, Allah is full of kindness, the Most Merciful towards mankind.*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ explained: "We are the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt, the *ummat al-wasaṭ* (the chosen middle nation), and we are the witnesses of Allah over mankind. The Messenger of Allah is a witness over us concerning what We have revealed to him from Allah, and We have been appointed as witnesses over the entire Ummah." On the Day of Resurrection, therefore, we shall verify the truthful and refute the deceitful. Allah confirms this:

> ⟪quran 29:49⟫
> ⟪ar:‏بَلْ هُوَ آيَتٌۢ بَيِّنَتٌۭ فِى صُدُورِ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ ۚ وَمَا يَجْحَدُ بِـَٔايَتِنَآ إِلَّا ٱلظَّلِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Nay, but they, the clear Ayat [i.e the description and the qualities of Prophet Muhammad PBUH written like verses in the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel)] are preserved in the breasts of those who have been given knowledge (from the people of the Scriptures). And none but the Zalimun (polytheists and wrongdoers, etc.) deny Our Ayat (proofs, evidences, verses, lessons, signs, revelations, etc.).*

Imam al-Bāqir ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ stated: "We are the *ūlū l-ʿilm* (those possessed of knowledge) in this verse, those whose hearts have been granted knowledge; Allah confirms this by saying:

> ⟪quran 13:43⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَيَقُولُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَسْتَ مُرْسَلًۭا ۚ قُلْ كَفَىٰ بِٱللَّهِ شَهِيدًۢا بَيْنِى وَبَيْنَكُمْ وَمَنْ عِندَهُۥ عِلْمُ ٱلْكِتَبِ ‎⟫
> *And those who disbelieve, say: "You (O Muhammad PBUH) are not a Messenger." Say: "Sufficient for a witness between me and you is Allah and those too who have knowledge of the Scripture (such as 'Abdullah bin Salam and other Jews and Christians who embraced Islam)."*

After the Messenger ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ and ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ ʿAlī, the Commander of the Believers, we are the foremost Imams, and after him we signify those possessing full knowledge of the Book—we ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫ the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt. Allah further declares:

> ⟪quran 25:73-74⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ إِذَا ذُكِّرُوا۟ بِـَٔايَتِ رَبِّهِمْ لَمْ يَخِرُّوا۟ عَلَيْهَا صُمًّۭا وَعُمْيَانًۭا ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يَقُولُونَ رَبَّنَا هَبْ لَنَا مِنْ أَزْوَجِنَا وَذُرِّيَّتِنَا قُرَّةَ أَعْيُنٍۢ وَٱجْعَلْنَا لِلْمُتَّقِينَ إِمَامًا ‎⟫
> *And those who, when they are reminded of the Ayat (proofs, evidences, verses, lessons, signs, revelations, etc.) of their Lord, fall not deaf and blind thereat. And those who say: "Our Lord! Bestow on us from our wives and our offspring who will be the comfort of our eyes, and make us leaders for the Muttaqun" (pious - see V.2:2 and the footnote of V.3:164)."*

Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ said: "Allah ⟪ar:' متقین'⟫ has made us ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫ the Imams of the pious." Allah also proclaims:

> ⟪quran 15:75⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ فِى ذَلِكَ لَآيَتٍۢ لِّلْمُتَوَسِّمِينَ ‎⟫
> *Surely! In this are signs, for those who see (or understand or learn the lessons from the Signs of Allah).*

This verse refers to the *mutawassimūn* (those possessing insight)—we ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫ the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt—who perceive all things in the light of divine knowledge. Beware therefore! The Messenger of Allah once said to the Commander of the Faithful: "O ʿAlī, you and your descendants are the gatekeepers of Paradise and Hell at the *Aʿrāf* ⟪ar:أعراف⟫ (the Elevated Ground). Only those whom you know shall enter Paradise, and only those you refuse shall be denied entry." Allah confirms and verifies this:

> ⟪quran 7:44-48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَنَادَىٰٓ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ أَصْحَبَ ٱلنَّارِ أَن قَدْ وَجَدْنَا مَا وَعَدَنَا رَبُّنَا حَقًّۭا فَهَلْ وَجَدتُّم مَّا وَعَدَ رَبُّكُمْ حَقًّۭا ۖ قَالُوا۟ نَعَمْ ۚ فَأَذَّنَ مُؤَذِّنٌۢ بَيْنَهُمْ أَن لَّعْنَةُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَى ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يَصُدُّونَ عَن سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَيَبْغُونَهَا عِوَجًۭا وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ كَفِرُونَ ‎ وَبَيۡنَہُمَا حِجَابٌ۬‌ۚ وَعَلَى ٱلۡأَعۡرَافِ رِجَالٌ۬ يَعۡرِفُونَ كُلاَّۢ بِسِيمَٮٰهُمۡ‌ۚ وَنَادَوۡاْ أَصۡحَـٰبَ ٱلۡجَنَّةِ أَن سَلَـٰمٌ عَلَيۡكُمۡ‌ۚ لَمۡ يَدۡخُلُوهَا وَهُمۡ يَطۡمَعُونَ ‏ وَإِذَا صُرِفَتْ أَبْصَرُهُمْ تِلْقَآءَ أَصْحَبِ ٱلنَّارِ قَالُوا۟ رَبَّنَا لَا تَجْعَلْنَا مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎ ‏وَنَادَىٰٓ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْأَعْرَافِ رِجَالًۭا يَعْرِفُونَهُم بِسِيمَىٰهُمْ قَالُوا۟ مَآ أَغْنَىٰ عَنكُمْ جَمْعُكُمْ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَكْبِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And the dwellers of Paradise will call out to the dwellers of the Fire (saying): "We have indeed found true what our Lord had promised us; have you also found true, what your Lord promised (warnings, etc.)?" They shall say: "Yes." Then a crier will proclaim between them: "The Curse of Allah is on the Zalimun (polytheists and wrongdoers, etc.)," Those who hindered (men) from the Path of Allah, and would seek to make it crooked, and they were disbelievers in the Hereafter. And between them will be a barrier screen and on Al-A'raf (a wall with elevated places) will be men (whose good and evil deeds would be equal in scale), who would recognise all (of the Paradise and Hell people), by their marks (the dwellers of Paradise by their white faces and the dwellers of Hell by their black faces), they will call out to the dwellers of Paradise, "Salamun 'Alaikum" (peace be on you), and at that time they (men on Al-A'raf) will not yet have entered it (Paradise), but they will hope to enter (it) with certainty. And when their eyes will be turned towards the dwellers of the Fire, they will say: "Our Lord! Place us not with the people who are Zalimun (polytheists and wrongdoers)." And the men on Al-A'raf (the wall) will call unto the men whom they would recognise by their marks, saying: "Of what benefit to you were your great numbers (and hoards of wealth), and your arrogance against Faith?"*

## Who Is Meant by the Family of Muhammad?

It is obligatory to invoke blessings upon the Messenger and upon his family, for the *āl Muhammad* (Family of Muhammad) denotes the Ahl al-Bayt of the Messenger of Allah—those to whom the station of Imamate has been transferred, the true Ummah of the Prophet. The Qur'ān attests:

> ⟪quran 33:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱللَّهُ وَمَلَٓئِكَتَهُۥ يُصَلُّونَ عَلَى ٱلنَّبِىِّۚ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ صَلُّوا۟ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا۟ تَسْلِيمًا ‎⟫
> *Allah sends His Salat (Graces, Honours, Blessings, Mercy, etc.) on the Prophet (Muhammad PBUH) and also His angels too (ask Allah to bless and forgive him). O you who believe! Send your Salat on (ask Allah to bless) him (Muhammad PBUH), and (you should) greet (salute) him with the Islamic way of greeting (salutation i.e. As-Salamu 'Alaikum).*

When this verse was revealed to the Messenger ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫, the people asked him how they should invoke blessings upon him. He taught them the proper manner: ⟪ar-quote:اللّٰھم صلی علی محمد و علی آل محمد کما صلیت علی ابراھیم و علی آل ابراھیم  انک حمید مجید⟫ "O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and the family of Muhammad as You sent blessings upon Abraham and the family of Abraham; verily, You are Praiseworthy and Glorious." The Messenger explained to all people the precise manner of prayer, fasting, pilgrimage, almsgiving, and other obligatory duties because all these have been set forth in the Qur'ān in their full detail.[^cite-10]

When a questioner asked Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫, "O son of the Messenger ⟪ar:رسول(ﷺ)⟫, tell me who constitutes the family of Muhammad," the Imam replied by distinguishing the Ahl al-Bayt in their particular sense—the family of the Messenger—from broader categories of believers. The questioner observed that people believe all Muslims are the family of Muhammad. The Imam smiled and said: "He is both truthful and mistaken. How can those Muslims who profess monotheism and accept the Prophet as true, yet harbor deficiency in religion and engage in extremism, be counted as the family of Muhammad? Yet he is correct in this: those among them who are true believers do partake of the category of the family of Muhammad, not in the specific sense of kinship (*nasabī wa ṣalbī*—blood lineage), but because of their adherence to the demands of the Qur'ān."

The questioner pressed further: "Tell me, what are the conditions of the Qur'ān by which a believer becomes numbered among the family of Muhammad?" The Imam responded: "When one follows and implements the commands of the Holy Qur'ān—just as Allah has given preeminence to the family of Muhammad over all other creatures by virtue of their kinship with the Prophet—he too shall be counted among the family of Muhammad." The Qur'ān declares:

> ⟪quran 5:51⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ لَا تَتَّخِذُوا۟ ٱلْيَهُودَ وَٱلنَّصَرَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآءَ ۘ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَآءُ بَعْضٍۢ ۚ وَمَن يَتَوَلَّهُم مِّنكُمْ فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Take not the Jews and the Christians as Auliya' (friends, protectors, helpers, etc.), they are but Auliya' to one another. And if any amongst you takes them as Auliya', then surely he is one of them. Verily, Allah guides not those people who are the Zalimun (polytheists and wrongdoers and unjust).*

The Almighty also reports the words of Abraham ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫:

> ⟪quran 14:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏رَبِّ إِنَّهُنَّ أَضْلَلْنَ كَثِيرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلنَّاسِ ۖ فَمَن تَبِعَنِى فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنِّى ۖ وَمَنْ عَصَانِى فَإِنَّكَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *"O my Lord! They have indeed led astray many among mankind. But whoso follows me, he verily is of me. And whoso disobeys me, - still You are indeed Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful.*

The questioner implored: "May I sacrifice my life for you! Give me proof from the Book of Allah itself—the Noble Qur'ān—such that I may with certainty argue that the true family of Muhammad, and specifically the Ahl al-Bayt of the Prophet, are distinct from all other Muslims of the world." The Imam replied: "Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 3:33-34⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ ٱصْطَفَىٰٓ آدَمَ وَنُوحًۭا وَآلَ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَآلَ عِمْرَنَ عَلَى ٱلْعَلَمِينَ ‎ ‏ذُرِّيَّةًۢ بَعْضُهَا مِنۢ بَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ ‎⟫
> *Allah chose Adam, Nuh (Noah), the family of Ibrahim (Abraham) and the family of 'Imran above the 'Alamîn (mankind and jinns) (of their times). Offspring, one of the other, and Allah is the All-Hearer, All-Knower.*

Thus it is evident that the offspring of a people descend from its own lineage. He then cited another verse:

> ⟪quran 40:28⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ رَجُلٌۭ مُّؤْمِنٌۭ مِّنْ آلِ فِرْعَوْنَ يَكْتُمُ إِيمَنَهُۥٓ أَتَقْتُلُونَ رَجُلًا أَن يَقُولَ رَبِّىَ ٱللَّهُ وَقَدْ جَآءَكُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ مِن رَّبِّكُمْۖ وَإِن يَكُ كَذِبًۭا فَعَلَيْهِ كَذِبُهُۥۖ وَإِن يَكُ صَادِقًۭا يُصِبْكُم بَعْضُ ٱلَّذِى يَعِدُكُمْۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى مَنْ هُوَ مُسْرِفٌۭ كَذَّابٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And a believing man of Fir'aun's (Pharaoh) family, who hid his faith said: "Would you kill a man because he says: My Lord is Allah, and he has come to you with clear signs (proofs) from your Lord? And if he is a liar, upon him will be (the sin of) his lie; but if he is telling the truth, then some of that (calamity) wherewith he threatens you will befall on you." Verily, Allah guides not one who is a Musrif (a polytheist, or a murderer who shed blood without a right, or those who commit great sins, oppressor, transgressor), a liar!*

In this verse, the Almighty designates as belonging to the family of Pharaoh (*āl firʿawn*) a believing man who was actually his uncle's son—related by blood—yet stood opposed to Pharaoh in faith. Should all Muslims who believe in and follow the Prophet be counted among the family of Muhammad merely by virtue of faith and obedience, Allah would not have attributed this believer to Pharaoh's people, for his religion was contrary to theirs.[^cite-11] Thus it becomes clear from this example that a person's true family is his Ahl al-Bayt—those bound by blood kinship and spiritual inheritance.

## Who May Be Counted Among the Family of Muhammad

Whoever follows and obeys the family will be numbered among the family of Muhammad in regard to piety. As Abraham ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ said, "Whoever follows me belongs to me." The Qur'ān corroborates:

> ⟪quran 40:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلنَّارُ يُعْرَضُونَ عَلَيْهَا غُدُوًّۭا وَعَشِيًّۭاۖ وَيَوْمَ تَقُومُ ٱلسَّاعَةُ أَدْخِلُوٓا۟ آلَ فِرْعَوْنَ أَشَدَّ ٱلْعَذَابِ ‎⟫
> *The Fire; they are exposed to it, morning and afternoon, and on the Day when the Hour will be established (it will be said to the angels): "Cause Fir'aun's (Pharaoh) people to enter the severest torment!"*

Here, the family of Pharaoh comprises not merely his blood relatives, but all who followed him. Those who shall enter the Fire, save Pharaoh himself, enter because they made Pharaoh and his household their Imams and followed them out of love and devotion. Consequently, because of their obedience, they are counted among Pharaoh's family.

In another passage, the Almighty proclaims:

> ⟪quran 37:130⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَلَمٌ عَلَىٰٓ أٓلِ يَاسِينَ ‎⟫
> *Salamun (peace) be upon Ilyasîn (Elias)!*

This verse extends the designation of "family" (*āl*) to include both the Prophet ⟪ar:یٰسٓ⟫ and the Imams ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ of the Ahl al-Bayt ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫. In the same manner, Allah addresses David and his family ⟪ar:آل داؤد⟫:

> ⟪quran 34:13⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَعْمَلُونَ لَهُۥ مَا يَشَآءُ مِن مَّحَرِيبَ وَتَمَثِيلٌ وَجِفَانٍۢ كَٱلْجَوَابِ وَقُدُورٍۢ رَّاسِيَتٍۚ ٱعْمَلُوٓا۟ آلَ دَاوُۥدَ شُكْرًۭاۚ وَقَلِيلٌۭ مِّنْ عِبَادِىَ ٱلشَّكُورُ ‎⟫
> *They worked for him what he desired, (making) high rooms, images, basins as large as reservoirs, and (cooking) cauldrons fixed (in their places). "Work you, O family of Dawud (David), with thanks!" But few of My slaves are grateful.*

And elsewhere He states:

> ⟪quran 2:248⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ لَهُمْ نَبِيُّهُمْ إِنَّ آيَةَ مُلْكِهِۦٓ أَن يَأْتِيَكُمُ ٱلتَّابُوتُ فِيهِ سَكِينَةٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَبَقِيَّةٌۭ مِّمَّا تَرَكَ آلُ مُوسَىٰ وَآلُ هَرُونَ تَحْمِلُهُ ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ ۚ إِنَّ فِى ذَلِكَ لَآيَةًۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُم مُّؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫
> *And their Prophet (Samuel) said to them: Verily! The sign of His Kingdom is that there shall come to you At-Tabut (a wooden box), wherein is Sakinah (peace and reassurance) from your Lord and a remnant of that which Musa (Moses) and Harun (Aaron) left behind, carried by the angels. Verily, in this is a sign for you if you are indeed believers.*

In all these verses—those referring to the family of Pharaoh, the family of Yasin, the family of David, and the family of Moses and Aaron ⟪ar:آل فرعون، آل یس، آل داؤد، آل موسی اور آل ھرون⟫—those counted as "family" belong thereto by virtue of followership and obedience, not necessarily by blood kinship (*nisbi wa silbī*). Indeed, many in these categories bear no genealogical relation to the prophets in question. Therefore, it has become abundantly clear that the Ahl al-Bayt are the Imams ⟪ar:انبیاء اکرام علیھم السلام⟫ of the prophets of noble station. Whosoever establishes a relationship with them ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫ and follows them in matters of faith shall be numbered ⟪ar:آل محمد(ﷺ)⟫ among the family of Muhammad, as Allah has made abundantly plain in many verses of the Qur'ān.

The Imam then addressed the questioner gravely: "Believe that no Ummah in history has committed greater injustice and transgression than this one. This is because this Ummah has assumed there is no distinction between them and the Prophet Muhammad ⟪ar:پیغمبر محمد رسول اللہ صلعم⟫ and the noble Ahl al-Bayt ⟪ar:اہل بیت کرام علیھم السلام⟫—that there is no preeminence granted by Allah to the Ahl al-Bayt. What could be more unjust? Those who entertain such thoughts have attributed a grave offense to the Lord of All Beings and have committed a terrible calumny against Him." Therefore, those who harbor this belief are beset by disgrace in this world and the next, until they repent and acknowledge the virtues and excellences that Allah has bestowed upon the Ahl al-Bayt. Unless they recognize that the family ⟪ar:محمدو آل محمد⟫ are the radiant stars of prophethood, the land upon which mercy descends, the fountainhead of knowledge, the people of remembrance, and the pathways of the angels between earth and heaven—unless they acknowledge these prerogatives and turn toward the truth—they shall remain beyond the circle of the family of Muhammad and its blessings.

## Who Are the Ummah of Muhammad?

⟪ar:امت محمدی علیہ السلام⟫The term "Ummah of Muhammad" (ummah muḥammadiyyah) ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیہ السلام⟫refers to those who believe in and affirm the Prophethood of ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.s.), follow him and the Ahl al-Bayt (a.s.)—the People of His House—and obey the Imams. Such believers are counted among the Ummah of Muhammad just as they are reckoned among the family of ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫Muhammad. In other words, through their obedience to the Prophet (s) and his Ahl al-Bayt and their Imams, they are included within the category of his family and are thus called the Ummah of Muhammad. Those who deny this bounty and the truth of the Ahl al-Bayt ⟪ar:امت محمدی (اہل بیت)⟫are those of whom Allah says:

> ⟪quran 4:150⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ يَكْفُرُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَيُرِيدُونَ أَن يُفَرِّقُوا۟ بَيْنَ ٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَيَقُولُونَ نُؤْمِنُ بِبَعْضٍۢ وَنَكْفُرُ بِبَعْضٍۢ وَيُرِيدُونَ أَن يَتَّخِذُوا۟ بَيْنَ ذَلِكَ سَبِيلًا ‎⟫
> *Verily, those who disbelieve in Allah and His Messengers and wish to make distinction between Allah and His Messengers (by believing in Allah and disbelieving in His Messengers) saying, "We believe in some but reject others," and wish to adopt a way in between.*

Such persons believe in Allah and His Messenger, yet when it is said to them, "Believe in the virtue and authority of the Imams from the Ahl al-Bayt," they refuse. This constitutes a grave distinction between parts of the divine revelation—a disharmony that severs them from the unity of faith.

The Prophet Abraham (a.s.) and his son Ishmael (a.s.), when they raised the foundations of the Sacred House (al-Kaʿbah), made a supplication of profound significance. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫The Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (a.s.) transmits that Abraham said:

> ⟪quran 2:127-128⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ يَرْفَعُ إِبْرَهِيمُ ٱلْقَوَاعِدَ مِنَ ٱلْبَيْتِ وَإِسْمَعِيلُ رَبَّنَا تَقَبَّلْ مِنَّآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلسَّمِيعُ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ‎ ‏رَبَّنَا وَٱجْعَلْنَا مُسْلِمَيْنِ لَكَ وَمِن ذُرِّيَّتِنَآ أُمَّةًۭ مُّسْلِمَةًۭ لَّكَ وَأَرِنَا مَنَاسِكَنَا وَتُبْ عَلَيْنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when Ibrahim (Abraham) and (his son) Isma'il (Ishmael) were raising the foundations of the House (the Ka'bah at Makkah), (saying), "Our Lord! Accept (this service) from us. Verily! You are the All-Hearer, the All-Knower." "Our Lord! And make us submissive unto You and of our offspring a nation submissive unto You, and show us our Manasik (all the ceremonies of pilgrimage - Hajj and 'Umrah, etc.), and accept our repentance. Truly, You are the One Who accepts repentance, the Most Merciful.*

To this prayer, Abraham added another supplication of equal consequence:

> ⟪quran 14:35-37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ إِبْرَهِيمُ رَبِّ ٱجْعَلْ هَذَا ٱلْبَلَدَ آمِنًۭا وَٱجْنُبْنِى وَبَنِىَّ أَن نَّعْبُدَ ٱلْأَصْنَامَ ‎ ‏رَبِّ إِنَّهُنَّ أَضْلَلْنَ كَثِيرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلنَّاسِ ۖ فَمَن تَبِعَنِى فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنِّى ۖ وَمَنْ عَصَانِى فَإِنَّكَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎ ‏رَّبَّنَآ إِنِّىٓ أَسْكَنتُ مِن ذُرِّيَّتِى بِوَادٍ غَيْرِ ذِى زَرْعٍ عِندَ بَيْتِكَ ٱلْمُحَرَّمِ رَبَّنَا لِيُقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ فَٱجْعَلْ أَفْـِٔدَةًۭ مِّنَ ٱلنَّاسِ تَهْوِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ وَٱرْزُقْهُم مِّنَ ٱلثَّمَرَتِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when Ibrahim (Abraham) said: "O my Lord! Make this city (Makkah) one of peace and security, and keep me and my sons away from worshipping idols. "O my Lord! They have indeed led astray many among mankind. But whoso follows me, he verily is of me. And whoso disobeys me, - still You are indeed Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful. "O our Lord! I have made some of my offspring to dwell in an uncultivable valley by Your Sacred House (the Ka'bah at Makkah); in order, O our Lord, that they may perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), so fill some hearts among men with love towards them, and (O Allah) provide them with fruits so that they may give thanks.*

Allah Most High answered his prayer abundantly. ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫From the descendants of Abraham, He brought forth ⟪ar:ائمۃ المعصومین⟫a chain of the Imams of the Sinless Ones (al-aʾimmah al-maʿṣūmīn), beginning with ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.s.) and continuing through ⟪ar:مولانا علی⟫Maulana ʿAli and his descendants. These constitute ⟪ar:امت مسلمۃ⟫a nation submitted entirely to Allah. It is only the Imams ⟪ar:امت مسلمۃ⟫of the present generation—those who are ⟪ar:محمد و علی و فاطمۃ⟫Muhammad, ʿAli, and Fāṭimah and their descendants—who are truly entitled to bear the name of those whom idolatry has never touched and who have never bowed in prostration before any other than Allah. This purity attests to their immaculate character and absolute sanctity.[^cite-12]

> ⟪quran 3:104⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلْتَكُن مِّنكُمْ أُمَّةٌۭ يَدْعُونَ إِلَى ٱلْخَيْرِ وَيَأْمُرُونَ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَيَنْهَوْنَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ ۚ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *Let there arise out of you a group of people inviting to all that is good (Islam), enjoining Al-Ma'ruf (i.e. Islamic Monotheism and all that Islam orders one to do) and forbidding Al-Munkar (polytheism and disbelief and all that Islam has forbidden). And it is they who are the successful.*

How can the generality of Muslims claim to be the Ummah of Muhammad when they neither summon others to goodness, nor command what is right, nor forbid what is wrong? Only the Ahl al-Bayt possess this capacity in its fullness. But Allah goes further still:

> ⟪quran 3:110⟫

> *You [true believers in Islamic Monotheism, and real followers of Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Sunnah (legal ways, etc.)] are the best of peoples ever raised up for mankind; you enjoin Al-Ma'ruf (i.e. Islamic Monotheism and all that Islam has ordained) and forbid Al-Munkar (polytheism, disbelief and all that Islam has forbidden), and you believe in Allah. And had the people of the Scripture (Jews and Christians) believed, it would have been better for them; among them are some who have faith, but most of them are Al-Fasiqun (disobedient to Allah - and rebellious against Allah's Command).*

In declaring ⟪ar:خیر أُمت⟫"the best of all nations," Allah cannot possibly be referring to these inferior and wayward Muslims. The verse must denote the Prophets and Imams among the descendants of Abraham, from whom ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫Muhammad was sent forth. They alone are ⟪ar:رسول اللہ ، علی، فاطمۃ، حسن و حسین علیھم السلام⟫the Ummah here mentioned—the Ahl al-Bayt (a.s.) and the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.s.), from whom Allah has removed every trace of impurity. The Qur'an itself bears witness to their chastity and purity.[^cite-13] Should we marvel, then, that the Messenger of Allah is spoken of as an entire Ummah in himself, or that Maulana ʿAli is described as such, when Allah declares:

> *Verily, Ibrahim (Abraham) was an Ummah (a leader having all the good righteous qualities), or a nation, obedient to Allah, Hanifa (i.e. to worship none but Allah), and he was not one of those who were Al-Mushrikun (polytheists, idolaters, disbelievers in the Oneness of Allah, and those who joined partners with Allah).*

Thus ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫Abraham was himself an entire Ummah, a self-contained nation of righteousness, and ⟪ar:اولاد اسماعیل⟫Allah blessed him through his descendants. Prophethood and Imamate were distributed among his progeny. In the same manner, the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.s.) is a complete Ummah unto himself, and Allah has multiplied his station through his children ⟪ar:مولانا علی⟫and ⟪ar:ولاتنا فاطمۃ⟫his beloved daughter Fāṭimah and through the progeny they bore, just as Allah enlarged ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم⟫the circle of Abraham's honor ⟪ar:حضرت اسماعیل و اسحاق⟫through Ishmael and Isaac. The Imamate, proceeding ⟪ar:امام حسن و حسین⟫through al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn and their descendants, shall endure until the Day of Judgment.[^cite-14]

In another place, Allah declares:

And We made the Qiblah (prayer direction towards Jerusalem) which you used to face, only to test those who followed the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from those who would turn on their heels (i.e. disobey the Messenger). Truly, Allah is full of kindness, the Most Merciful towards mankind.*

Consider: this verse designates the Ummah as the monotheistic community of those facing the Qiblah. Yet whose testimony would be deemed unworthy in this world—perhaps in a matter as trivial as a measure of dates—how then could Allah accept their testimony over all creation on the Day of Resurrection? Here lies the reconciliation: only those whose inner substance is purified and sanctified can serve as true witnesses before the Throne of God.

## The Purity and Infallibility of the Imam

Allah addresses the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt in the Qur'an with words of singular grace:

> ⟪quran 33:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَرْنَ فِى بُيُوتِكُنَّ وَلَا تَبَرَّجْنَ تَبَرُّجَ ٱلْجَهِلِيَّةِ ٱلْأُولَىٰۖ وَأَقِمْنَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتِينَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَأَطِعْنَ ٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولَهُۚۥٓ إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ ٱلرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ ٱلْبَيْتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمْ تَطْهِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *And stay in your houses, and do not display yourselves like that of the times of ignorance, and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and give Zakat and obey Allah and His Messenger. Allah wishes only to remove Ar-Rijs (evil deeds and sins, etc.) from you, O members of the family (of the Prophet PBUH), and to purify you with a thorough purification.*

In this verse, Allah announces: "We have decreed to remove all that is abhorrent from the Ahl al-Bayt." He does not declare that they should be cleansed of impurities—a phrasing that would suggest they possessed impurity initially, which Allah then removed. Rather, the verse speaks of their essential state: impurity has been kept away from them. To suggest otherwise would be to imply that the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt (a.s.) were first ⟪ar:نعوذ باللہ⟫—God forbid—impure, and only thereafter purified by divine decree.[^cite-15]

Consider a false notion widely held, which teaches that all descendants of Adam issue from his progeny in a manner unfit to mention. According to this error, from Adam and Eve came countless children: from the first pregnancy, males alone; from the second, females only. When these siblings then married one another, the human race thus propagated. This understanding is profoundly wrong and forbidden in every Shariah, for it necessitates that brothers married sisters—a union never lawful in any divine law. To accept such a fable would mean accepting that all Prophets and Messengers, even the greatest among them, ⟪ar:خیر المرسلین⟫—the Best of Messengers—and their pure guides ⟪ar:(نعوذ باللہ من ذالک)⟫descend from such illicit unions. Is it conceivable that a being upon whose forehead Allah has placed the crown of Prophethood, whom He has made sovereign over all existence, could bear such a terrible blemish?

On the contrary, the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.s.), whom Allah calls ⟪ar:صادق و امین⟫the Truthful and the Trustworthy, testified that his descent has come to him through ⟪ar:ارحام زکیۃ⟫pure wombs and ⟪ar:اصلاب طاہرۃ⟫pure loins, and never has the soil of ignorance touched him. It is an essential condition of the Imamate that the Imam's son becomes the next Imam. This established pattern, which Allah has decreed, cannot be altered. Allah says:

> ⟪quran 33:62⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سُنَّةَ ٱللَّهِ فِى ٱلَّذِينَ خَلَوْا۟ مِن قَبْلُۖ وَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّةِ ٱللَّهِ تَبْدِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *That was the Way of Allah in the case of those who passed away of old, and you will not find any change in the Way of Allah.*

### The Light of the Prophet (s) and ʿAli (a)

There is a profound ḥadīth transmitted by ⟪ar:حضرت جابر بن عبد اللہ الانصاری⟫the Companion Jābir ibn ʿAbd Allāh al-Anṣārī in which he recounts: "I heard the Messenger of Allah say, 'Allah created me and ʿAli as a single light two thousand years before the creation of Adam, positioned before His Throne.' There we engaged in ⟪ar:تسبیح و تقدیس⟫glorification and sanctification. That is why the Prophet declared, ⟪ar-quote:أَناَ وَ عَلِی مِن نُّور وَاحِد⟫ 'ʿAli and I are of one light.' When Adam was created, we were placed within his loins. Then ⟪ar:اصلاب طاہرۃ⟫through pure loins and ⟪ar:ارحام ذکیۃ⟫pure wombs, we continually descended until we arrived at the loins of ⟪ar:صلب ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫Abraham. At no point did the impurity and corruption of the Age of Ignorance touch us, not even when we reached ⟪ar:صلب عبد المطلب علیہ السلام⟫the loins of ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib. There the light was divided into two portions: one fell to ⟪ar:مولانا عبد اللہ⟫my father ʿAbd Allāh, and the other to ⟪ar:مولانا ابو طالب علیہ السلام⟫Abū Ṭālib. Thus did ⟪ar:عبد اللہ⟫I emerge, and thus did ⟪ar:ابو طالب⟫ʿAli emerge. From me proceeded the same light, and from ʿAli along with ⟪ar:فاطمۃ⟫Fāṭimah it gathered, and from them were born ⟪ar:حسن⟫al-Ḥasan and ⟪ar:حسین⟫al-Ḥusayn. Therefore are they the light of both worlds and the light of ⟪ar:رب العالمین⟫the Lord of all existence."[^cite-16]

Allah speaks to this reality in His Book:

> ⟪quran 26:219⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَتَقَلُّبَكَ فِى ٱلسَّجِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *And your movements among those who fall prostrate (along with you to Allah in the five compulsory congregational prayers).*

The language of this verse points to a spiritual reality beyond outward motion: it evokes the Prophet's presence within every act of submission and worship performed by the faithful.

In a like manner, Allah proclaims in His Book: ⟪ar-quote:یُخْرِجُ الْحَیَّی مِنَ الْمَیِّتِ⟫"We bring forth the living from the dead." For the common believer, this signifies that Allah creates believers from among disbelievers and disbelievers from among believers. Yet this principle does not apply to the Prophets and Imams, for Allah has granted them a singular privilege: they cannot come forth from the wombs of the heedless or the unbelieving. However, they may indeed produce children who stray into unbelief, as was the case with Ham, the son of Noah (a.s.), who refused the faith of his father.[^cite-17]

## "We Have Recorded All Things in a Clear Imam"

To illuminate the supreme greatness of the Imam, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 36:12⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّا نَحْنُ نُحْىِ ٱلْمَوْتَىٰ وَنَكْتُبُ مَا قَدَّمُوا۟ وَآثَرَهُمْۚ وَكُلَّ شَىْءٍ أَحْصَيْنَاهُ فِىٓ إِمَامٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *Verily, We give life to the dead, and We record that which they send before (them), and their traces [their footsteps and walking on the earth with their legs to the mosques for the five compulsory congregational prayers, Jihad (holy fighting in Allah's Cause) and all other good and evil they did, and that which they leave behind], and all things We have recorded with numbers (as a record) in a Clear Book.*

⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫The Commander of the Faithful, ʿAli ibn Abī Ṭālib (a.s.), has expounded this verse with profound insight. He teaches that Allah has hidden within humanity the entire macrocosm of creation. The human being possesses eyes luminous as the moon and sun; within it dwell all four elements—fire, air, water, and earth. The mountains find their analogue in the skeletal heights of the body; the caves, in its hollows. Blood courses through vessels as rivers through valleys. More than this, humanity has been given dominion over all plants and beasts. ⟪ar:عالم الکبیر⟫Since this is the condition of the human form and we believe the Imam to be immeasurably superior to ordinary humanity, what then must be said of this Imam's magnificence? The entire universe and all beings within it are subject to His command.[^cite-18]

This verse makes plain that the Imam's personality stands in a wholly different and exalted order from that of ordinary humans. There exists no parallel between them and mortals. The Imam is the Lord of his Age, and all things bow to His sovereignty. The saints and masters of Allah have discerned in this verse yet another subtlety: those who have made spiritual progress and bound themselves to the Imam ⟪ar:لاہوت و ناست⟫—ascending through the divine and the human dimensions—have become in essence ⟪ar:شئی⟫*something*, possessed of true reality and value. In contrast stand all other ⟪ar:لا شئی⟫non-things, the worthless and the empty, which hold no significance in the eyes of God.[^cite-19]

## The Proof of the Imamate

In the Name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

All praise belongs to Allah, who sent prophets and appointed Imams and caliphs to guide His creation. I bear witness that there is no deity save Allah, and that Muḥammad is His servant and messenger. May Allah bless him, his family, and his companion ʿAlī, who is the most knowledgeable in the esoteric interpretation (taʾwīl) of the Qur'ān.

### The Foundation of Religion

The Imamate is the foundation of religion; all religious and worldly matters revolve around it. It is the source of knowledge of Allah's absolute oneness (tawḥīd) and of the revealed Message. The Imamate is affirmed through evidence and deduction, demonstrated in the Qur'ān and the Prophet's traditions. The necessity of having an Imam is essential in natural disposition (al-fiṭra), innate constitution, nature (al-ṭabīʿa), intellect (al-ʿaql), governance (al-siyāsa), prescription (al-sunna), and norms. It is obligatory in all revealed laws, religions, and religious communities, just as the existence of a Creator is necessary to every thinking being.[^cite-20]

People of different faiths call their past leaders "prophets" and "sages." Yet certain misguided Muslims—those who have strayed into error—call their scholars and jurists by the name Imam, wrongfully applying this elevated designation. Whoever seeks salvation must set aside his inclinations and desires, and comprehend the deficiency in every false pretender to this esteemed title. Such pretenders lack the knowledge, gnosis (maʿrifa), piety, and all other merits and conditions requisite for the Imamate.

### The Strategy of This Discourse

We shall now identify the weaknesses of those who falsely claim Imamate. We shall then turn their own principles against them, repudiating the Imamate from those unsuited for it. We shall establish the Imamate of the true Imams with proofs and demonstrations, and shall show the necessity of obeying them, entrusting our affairs to their command, and accepting the revelation, interpretation (taʾwīl), and confession of unity from them.

### The Spiritual and Physical Order

Allah, through His wisdom and power, created the human body with its senses and the sustenance through which it survives. Similarly, He made principles, causes, and means for the souls, spirits, and intellectual forms through which they acquire their spiritual sustenance, nourishment, and survival. The differences in the degrees of excellence (al-tafāwut al-tafāḍul) that exist among all entities—comprising essences (al-aṣūl) and their derivations (al-furūʿ)—are the firmest evidence for affirming the Imamate and the Imams.[^cite-21]

Allah created entities in all genera (al-ajnās) and species (al-anwāʿ), different and disparate in respect to their excellence. He established for each genus and species an upper limit (ghāya), a summit (dharwa), an end (nihāya), and a virtue (faḍīla) which none other in that category can attain. He made those genera and species either a necessity for humans—without which their physical existence and function would not be complete—or a source of nourishment, medicine, elegance, adornment, and an indispensable tool for their existence. He placed in all things a unique benefit (manfaʿa) that exists in nothing else. He endowed them with a capacity which creation would necessarily need and could not continue without. Thus, when one cannot find the remedy for that neediness in its ordinary place, one seeks it elsewhere and hastens to where it may be found.

### The Proof from the Ten Categories

The philosophers speak of the ten categories and state that nothing can be discussed except in reference to them. Among these, "essence" (al-jawhar) is the ultimate foundation upon which all others rest and which excels over all. The remaining nine are established from it. Therefore, the essence they describe is a proof for the permanent, intellectual, luminous, and authentic substance through which all souls are illumined and on which all nine depend—a substance that has no need of any created thing but rather sustains everything. That is the Imam, peace be upon him! All other ranks are below him, just as the nine categories are in respect to essence.[^cite-22]

### The Four Basic Constituents

Among the four basic constituents (al-ʿanāṣir) from which the first composites were formed, two are active and two passive. The active ones are heat and cold; the passive ones are dryness and humidity. The ultimate and noblest is heat, from which arises luminosity, light, motion, and elevation while the other three are established through it. Heat has precedence in origin and in existence, and no realm of nature would be complete without it. Likewise, the Imam illumines the soul, and from Him comes intellectual light (al-Nūr al-ʿAqlī), motion, and elevation (al-ʿAlāʾ). No principle or religious realm is complete without him.

Among the celestial spheres, the most lofty and supreme is the outermost sphere in which they all orbit. Likewise, the Imam in religion is the supreme religious sphere for all other spheres. All heavenly bodies and religious signs function under his command. If someone were to claim that this is rather a symbol for the Resurrector (al-Qāʾim), we would reply that the Imam is the Qāʾim of his time. He is the proclaimer (al-nāṭiq) in his age and assumes the position of the partial Resurrector (al-Qāʾim al-juzʾī). The Imam's position in his realm is that of the Universal Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal).

### Political Authority and Self-Governance

The philosophers divided authority (al-siyāsa) into three categories: the authority of the individual (al-khāṣṣa), the authority of the family, and the authority of the general (al-ʿāmma). Thus he punishes his soul when it errs through reproach and repentance, and rewards it when it does well by praise and happiness.

The authority of the family is managing one's household and dependents by protecting their welfare, encouraging them to acquire virtues and pleasing character, punishing those who err by reprimanding, and rewarding those who do well by praise. The authority of generality is governing cities, protecting the welfare of their livelihood, and preventing them from reprehensible morals and bad deeds. He protects all their positions, punishes those who err by reprimanding, and rewards those who do well by praising and protecting their religious and worldly affairs.

This is what they call authority; it is found neither in the world nor among those who falsely claim the Imamate, nor among the rulers and their scholars. ### The Imam in Ritual Prayer

In the ritual prayer and its arrangement, its prescribed standing and direction of the qibla (prayer direction), and in the standing of the Imam in front of all the rows, there is a powerful indication for affirming the Imamate. The Prophet ordered the Muslims to face the qibla, which is derived from *qābala*, meaning "face-to-face"; whoever faces it points his face directly to it, and that is the Imam. To be before means precedence, opposite of back and after. The word Imam derives from this meaning, and the Imam of the prayer is called an Imam because he precedes the worshippers. The indication to the qibla is an indication for the Imam, and the Prophet commanded people to follow and obey him in all forms of worship and to accept his direction.

When a man faces what is in front of him during prayers, he is facing the qibla with respect to the Imam in all his worship and obedience to him. If an individual's prayer is with his Imam—as the Prophet mentioned that congregational prayer equals twenty-seven prayers said individually—its merit is because it is with the Imam while facing him, following him, and being led by him in intention and action. That is an indication that the entire aspect of obedience is being led by the supreme Imam (al-Imām al-Aʿẓam)—guided, followed, and led by him so that obedience has excellence.[^cite-35]

The Prophet ordered them to align in rows so that there was no difference between anyone in their arising; this indicates that it is not permissible for the hearts of those who are led and guided by the Imam to be different, nor should this be the case in their actions or speech. The Prophet ordered them to follow the Imam of the prayers even if the recitation, arising, or prostration became prolonged, and not to go against him in gestures or intention. If one who is led is negligent, the Imam removes his negligence. It is evident that if obedience of the partial Imam is made obligatory and the reward is increased, then how about the obedience of the supreme Imam who is an Imam in all Sharīʿa and esoteric interpretation (taʾwīl)?

Further, if it is not permissible to oppose the Imam in any prayer action, then how can it be permissible to oppose the supreme Imam in his commands and prohibitions in the life of a believer? The believer remains forever under the patronage of the summons (al-daʿwa) in the prayer as long as he is included in the allegiance (al-wilāya) of the Imam. If he opposes him in action, intention, or decision, he will have annulled his prayers and departed from the norm of the worshipper. How about then the obedience to the supreme Imam, which Allah has made absolutely obligatory? The Prophet said: "The Imam is the one responsible [for those who follow him]"—surely not the Imam of the prayer, for he requires someone who can vouch for him. The indication is to the supreme Imam, peace be upon him, who guarantees the purification of those led by him from doubt, suspicion, confusion, forgetfulness, and guarantees their salvation as long as they are guided by him, following him.[^cite-36]

### Jihād and Legal Judgment

Jihād is one of the principles of religion and is only permissible under the banner of an Imam. It is only permissible to send out an army under a commander appointed by the Imam or by someone the Imam has appointed. The Imam is more knowledgeable of times of peace, agreement, or treaties, and concludes them. He knows whom to forgive among prisoners of war, whom to ransom, and whom to put to death. He is more knowledgeable about the ways to take the land tax (al-kharāj) and money so that it is a means of benefit and support for the Muslims.[^cite-40]

The Imam also knows the rebel. It is through the Imam that a party becomes the party of truth because it is with him. The rebel is the one who departs from obedience to the Imam. Then it is permissible for the Imam to wage war and fight, or to make peace and armistice. The fifth (al-khums) that Allah gave specifically to the Prophet's family and the Imams in place of the alms, which He forbade them, benefits are linked to the Imam.

Legal judgment between people regarding bloodshed, sexual offenses, and property is only permissible for he who knows the cases and legal opinions regarding them, who knows the legal norms, the Qur'ānic revelation and its meaning—including abrogating and abrogated verses (al-nāsikh wa al-mansūkh)—and analogical deduction (al-qiyās) according to its conditions. ### The Continuous Existence of the Imam

The world can never be devoid of an Imam at any time. All people agree and accept the Imams in the period of Adam but deny them in the period of Muḥammad, may Allah bless him. Allah, the Almighty and Glorious, explained in His words: "Such was Our way (Sunna) in the case of Our messengers whom We sent before you (Muḥammad). And you will not find in Our way any change." Allah also said: "You will certainly not find any change in Our way (Sunna)." What is meant is the Sunna of the Imamate, not the Sunna of religious practices such as prayer, fasting, and others. The Prophet indeed changed the Sunna of all prophets regarding acts of worship (al-ʿibādāt) but not the Sunna of the Imamate.[^cite-43]

When Noah invited people to the ark out of compassion, he was fully aware that those who stayed behind would perish and be drowned, by their own choice and their belief that their welfare would be in something else. Everyone who was summoned by their prophet and Imam to a matter and abstained from it and denied it were destroyed. The ark was nothing but the Imam himself, and Noah's invitation was to the Imam, just as the Prophet said: "The likeness of my family (Ahl al-Bayt) among you is like Noah's ark; whoever comes aboard will be saved, and whoever remains behind will be drowned." Noah appointed his son Sham to the legateeship and the Imamate by designation (al-naṣṣ) and appointment (al-tawqīf), and thereafter it continued one after another until the time of Ibrāhīm, peace be upon him.[^cite-44]

Then Ibrāhīm and Ismāʿīl built the House (the Ka'ba), commanding people to glorify it, an indication that the Imamate was to be in Ibrāhīm's house and progeny. He urged people to glorify the Imamate and venerate and obey the Imams of his family.

When Mūsā appeared, he ordered people to face toward Jerusalem. Since Mūsā had no children or family in whom the Imamate could continue, he pointed to Jerusalem (bayt al-muqaddas), saying: "Whoever possesses the sanctified spirit among the Banū Isrāʾīl is your Imam and your direction of prayer (al-qibla)." Then ʿĪsā appeared. He did not have a family or children, and it is reported that he ordered people to face toward the east. If this is true, his command to face toward the east meant turning towards the place where the light of the Imamate rises—whoever radiates that light shall be your Imam and your direction of prayer.

When the Prophet, the seal of the prophets, may Allah bless him and his family, appeared, he ordered people to turn their faces from Jerusalem to the Ka'ba as the direction of prayer for Muslims. The Ka'ba was built by Ibrāhīm and Ismāʿīl, peace be upon them. The Prophet ordered this as an indication that the direction had returned to the house of Ibrāhīm, which is the family of Ismāʿīl. This means that the Imamate had returned to the children of Ismāʿīl, who are the children of the Prophet Muḥammad, may Allah bless him. He ordered people to glorify the house and perform the pilgrimage to it, which no other prophet had ordered, as an exhortation about the superior excellence of the Imams from the family of Muḥammad above all other Imams in past cycles.[^cite-45]

Can there be anyone more iniquitous than this community who accepted the Imams in all prophetic periods, affirming that the world cannot be deprived of an Imam, and affirming that there was never an Imam in previous cycles by choice, but who now deny the Imamate of Muḥammad's family, reject designation (al-naṣṣ), and call for choice? Allah, the Exalted, also said: "And your Lord creates whom He wills and chooses what is good for them." If the Imamate were by choice, every people would choose whom they wanted. The elders have mentioned the discussion of designation and choice. We have stipulated that we shall mention what the elders have not mentioned in books on affirming the Imamate. ### The Virtues of the Commander of the Faithful

The virtues that Allah, the Exalted, has mentioned and by which He preferred some people over others consist of three kinds: knowledge, jihād, and piety (al-taqwā), as Allah said: "Allah has preferred those who strive and struggle over those who sit back by a great reward in stages or degrees of rank." His words also read: "Say to them, O Muḥammad, 'Are those who know and those who do not, equal?'" Similarly His words: "Those who have been given knowledge have degrees of rank," and also: "The most noble among you in the presence of Allah is the most pious among you."

As for jihād, the elite (al-khāṣṣa) as well as the Muslims in general (al-ʿāmma) know that these men did not wage war for even a single day on their own. The wars fought by the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī, are well known and famous. The only disgrace in the holy war had been their flight from the battle of Ḥunayn when ʿUmar failed to obey the order and recommendation of the Prophet to protect the mountain and incurred the blame of the Muslims, while the Prophet and ʿAlī were fighting and killing the enemies until they were routed.

As for piety: where there is no knowledge, there can be no piety; piety comes through knowledge, when one knows what belongs to him and what belongs to others. It is reported from Muḥammad ibn al-Ḥasan, Abū Ḥanīfa's companion, that some Allah-fearing man asked him to compile a book on asceticism. He therefore compiled a book on trading and when asked about it, he replied: "Whosoever does not master jurisprudence, buying and selling, how could he consume lawful things? Whosoever does not consume lawful things has no asceticism." Therefore, it is correct according to the verses of the Qur'ān that the Imamate belonged to the Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him, because he had precedence (al-sābiq) in virtues, being the most knowledgeable, the most pious, and the one who fought most in the cause of Islam.[^cite-46]

Those people who ruled did not claim any excellence, knowledge, or piety for themselves. Rather, their companions did this for them. Indeed, Abū Bakr was the first to climb the pulpit and say: "You have appointed me, although I am not the best amongst you!" If he was telling the truth, then those claiming superior excellence for him are liars; if he was lying, then a liar cannot be an Imam. If they say that he said that out of humility, then we say that there is a time and place for humility. The Prophet himself used to mention his rank and status for which Allah had singled him out and did not lower himself. That did not amount to arrogance. Thus, he said: "I am the master of the Arabs, without boasting," "I am the master of humanity without boasting," and "I am the most eloquent of the Arabs without boasting."

The Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him, said: "Ask me before you lose me." He also said: "If a cushion were to be folded for me to sit on, I would pass judgment on the followers of the Torah according to the Torah, on the followers of the Bible according to the Bible, and on the followers of the Furqān (Qur'ān) according to the Furqān." Many such examples can be recounted, but this does not constitute arrogance or negate humility. The words of Abū Bakr, "I have been put in charge of you although I am not the best amongst you," are an indication that Allah and His Messenger had neither given him preference nor indicated him as his successor in ordering him to lead the prayer or anything else. He confirmed about himself all that was to be expected of him.[^cite-47]

When ʿUmar ascended the pulpit after Abū Bakr, he said: "The oath (al-bayʿa) to Abū Bakr was a falta (hasty, unconsidered decision), but Allah has averted its evil from us. Whoever repeats the like of it, kill him." In saying this, he expounded the falsehood of what the literalists claim regarding Abū Bakr's appointment to lead the prayer—that it made his Imamate obligatory and was an indication for his Imamate. Moreover, his statement contains a refutation of what they claim in the traditions, such as the saying: "Follow the two who come after me, Abū Bakr and ʿUmar."[^cite-48]

### The Commander of the Faithful Al-Ḥākim

Now we shall mention some merits of the lord of the age and time (walī al-ʿaṣr wa al-zamān), the Commander of the Faithful (al-Ḥākim), although someone like me will never attain the status to mention his true merits, just as Allah, the Exalted, said: "They have not valued Allah according to His true value." But I shall say this according to my little knowledge and ability, and the Commander of the Faithful is most worthy to accept his servant's excuse. Just as when Allah, the Exalted, with the multitude of His bounties unto His servants, asked them to be grateful for the blessings, and they were unable to give thanks equal to the bounties, but He forgave them, being content with their saying "Alḥamdu lillāh" (praise be to Allah). This was a lesson for His creatures: whenever gratitude to a benefactor is incumbent and they are unable to express it equal to the bounty, they should do so according to their capacity and should not relinquish giving thanks entirely because of inability.

Allah, the Exalted, made the earth radiant with His light as He promised and told us in His words: "and the earth shines by the light of her Lord." That is, the earth of the outer reality shines through the justice of the Commander of the Faithful, and the earth of the inner meaning shines through knowledge and explanation.[^cite-49]

Just as in every species, genus, root, branch, and law there is a peak and summit in nobility, merit, rank, degree, and position which nothing else can attain, it is incumbent that the Imams, peace be upon them, too have a peak, summit, and utmost degree which no one else has previously attained. That peak and utmost degree is the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī. He is the peak of all peaks and the utmost limit over which there is no other limit. Since the wisdom of the world and of animals and plants appeared in the rise of human beings and their welfare became complete through humanity—and the benefit of all goes back to mankind, and through the Imams that the merits of human beings became complete and wisdom appears in creation, and the benefit of all humans goes back to the Imams—likewise, through the Commander of the Faithful, all affairs (al-umūr) of the prophets, legatees, and Imams became complete, their order (al-amr) was organized and perfect wisdom became manifest in their disposition, in their laws and their esoteric interpretations.[^cite-50]

He, peace be upon him, is like the head and they are like the body. They are the body while the Commander of the Faithful is like the spirit. Not a single body is complete without the spirit. The Commander of the Faithful is like the heart while all others who have passed away are like the limbs. It is through the fusion of spirit and life to the heart that all limbs receive life and recompense. Each nail, hair, and limb receives its share of reward by means of the heart. This is the sensual knowledge, which is the recompense of the action that occurs from the embryo in its womb during its stay in the six stages from sperm to final creation.

Likewise, it is through the Commander of the Faithful that people gain recompense and acceptance of life in the seventh stage. He, peace be upon him, is the one who awakens the desire in every one to come to him, and he is the one through whom Allah promised creatures the fulfillment of reward on His day. As Allah said of the Judgment: "And you will be paid only that which you have earned fairly on the Day of Resurrection. Whoever is removed from the Fire and allowed to enter Paradise will indeed be triumphant." Allah commands the creation to believe in Him and in His Day when He said: "They believe in Allah and the Last Day."

Just as the entirety of humankind exists because it is the final realm of nature (al-mawālīd) in the world, having accepted all that existed before in the previous realms and exceeding it by things in which other realms had no share—and just as the Imams acquired all the merits that existed in human beings, being their peak, summit, and quintessence—likewise, the Commander of the Faithful gathers in himself all the merits of those who preceded him, and all their benefit and knowledge come to him, exceeding that many times over.[^cite-51]

Just as the power and merit of the Prophet existed because he gathered in himself the merits of those in previous cycles, all their knowledge and more, similarly the Commander of the Faithful gathers in himself the merits of all prophets, Imams, and sages who preceded him in previous cycles, exceeding them in things of which they were incapable. If he did not rise in this position, there would not have been the perfection of wisdom in their rise. The Commander of the Faithful is therefore the sum of everything in the worlds today—spiritual, physical, and conventional. It is because of him that these different worlds have been created, and all of them are his agents and propagators until His Day of Judgment.[^cite-52]

Some may say that this is the description of the final Qāʾim and that the Commander of the Faithful is not the final one. We reply that the Commander of the Faithful (Al-Ḥākim), peace be upon him, is the Qāʾim in his time and period and that he is the Qāʾim of all those who preceded him, the perfector of them all. He is the sixth through whom the command of the seventh will be perfected. He will finish what the seventh requires, just as the Prophet established all that the Qāʾim would require. The Commander of the Faithful is the ninth in a different calculation. The command of the seventh (al-Muʿizz) who passed away is perfected through the ninth (Al-Ḥākim), just as the perfection of the seven members, their order, and function become manifest through the nine—that is, the four natures and five senses—and just as the perfection of the human creature is in nine months. Life in the hereafter is based on four spiritual natures and five spiritual senses, as Allah said: "Indeed, the abode of the hereafter—that is life, if only they knew."[^cite-53]

The Commander of the Faithful is the second by a different calculation. The order at the beginning of each cycle is perfected by the second. He is also the sixth through whose sword Allah will conquer the whole world, just as He conquered through the Prophet, the sixth one from Adam. The Commander of the Faithful arose in the world perfecting vicegerency (al-khilāfa) of the Creator among His creatures. Each one of those who arose before him carried out a small portion of this, but the Commander of the Faithful carried out the whole.[^cite-54]

The vicegerent is one who carries out among those over whom he is appointed everything done by the one who appointed him. Adam, like all other vicegerents, was called "vicegerent" (al-khalīfa) because he was the first of those who undertook the vicegerency. Those who came after him carried out some of the conditions of vicegerency and so were called by that name. Whoever carries out a part of a duty is called by its name. The Commander of the Faithful, however, performs the worldly and the religious vicegerency in reality.

When the Prophet said, "The ruler is the shadow of Allah on earth," he indeed meant the Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him, because the shadow of something is that which imitates what casts the shadow with all that is in it. There is no one who deals with the creatures as Allah, the Exalted, does except for the Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him. The Prophet also said: "Indeed Allah, the Exalted, establishes (yanzil) as proof through the ruler more than what He establishes through the Qur'ān," and the Commander of the Faithful is the one through whom Allah establishes what He does not establish through the Qur'ān.[^cite-55]
