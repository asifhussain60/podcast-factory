## Definition of Shi'a

⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین حضرت علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ declared: "Indeed, our Shi'a are those who believe in Allah and His Messenger." They are those who observe God's commandments, turn away from sins, and perform what He has ordained for them. Our Shi'a are those who read the heavens—observing the sun, moon, and stars to determine the times of prayer. Our Shi'a are those whose lips have grown parched and whose stomachs are drawn inward through the practice of fasting. Their faces bear the marks of asceticism. He is not among our Shi'a who usurps the rights of others, who oppresses people, or who seizes what is not lawfully his.[^cite-1]

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ addressed the people, saying: "I counsel you all to fear Allah and to obey His commands, to refrain from sins, and to fulfill faithfully what Allah has entrusted to you." I advise you to show kindness to those in your company, and I exhort you to offer your prayers in silent devotion. "When you follow the commandments of Allah which We have prescribed for you, when you forbid one another from what Allah has forbidden, when you deal with people in truth and justice, when you restore trusts equitably, when you enjoin the good and forbid the evil, then people will recognize in you the followers of such and such Imam!" May Allah have mercy upon this Imam for how excellently he has taught his companions and what noble character he has instilled in them! Then they will recognize Our grace, and then they will turn toward Our grace and perfection.[^cite-2]

Whoever neglects even the smallest duty owed to Allah shall not receive His mercy, nor shall he be entitled to the intercession of ⟪ar:مصطفی صلعم⟫ on the Day of Resurrection. Therefore, listen to us and perform whatever God has ordained for you. Do not disobey God and do not disobey us by opposing what we command you! By Allah, there is no god but Allah.

Our Shi'a are from us, and all others shall go to Hell. God is worshipped through us! Whoever disobeys us has disobeyed God, and whoever obeys us has obeyed God. Our obedience is God's command to His creation. And it is such that God does not accept the deeds of a servant except through us, and He shows mercy to no one except through us. We are the Gate of God and His proof, we are the trustees of His creation, the custodians of His mysteries, the guardians of His knowledge and His secrets. There is no share in Paradise for the one who withholds our due from his wealth.[^cite-3]

⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین حضرت علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ transmitted that the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: "Whoever Allah tests the heart of a believer for piety shall surely love us with his heart; and the person with whom Allah is angry and displeased shall surely harbor in his heart hatred and enmity toward us." Therefore, whoever loves us must love us with sincerity—like gold in which there is not the slightest trace of adulteration. And he who hates us shall be in a state of hatred. We are noble and exalted; our children are the children of prophets, and I am the leader of all the saints! I am of the army of Allah and His Messenger, while all others are of Satan's army. Whosoever doubts us, separates from us, and joins our enemies is not of us! If any of you wishes to know who loves us and who hates us, first examine his heart. If he harbors love for any of our enemies, then know that Allah and His Messenger and the angels Gabriel and Michael are his enemies, and Allah is the enemy of the disbelievers.[^cite-4]

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ narrated that the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said to some of his Shi'a: "O Shi'a, listen and understand our wills, and abide by the covenant we take from our lovers." Speak the truth and fulfill your oaths. Help one another with wealth and love one another with all your hearts. Distribute charity to the needy and hold fast to unity and consensus. Do not treat anyone with deceit, forgery, or betrayal. Do not doubt in your heart after conviction, and do not retreat cowardly after taking a step. None of you should turn his back upon his loved ones, nor should he lust after anyone else. Let not your death be for anyone but yourself, let your deeds be for none other than Allah, and let your faith and purpose be none but your Prophet.[^cite-5]

Among our Shi'a, the saints and lovers of Allah and His Messenger are those who speak the truth, and when they make a promise, they fulfill it. When they are entrusted with something, they are patient; when asked to pay their dues, they do so; when commanded, they act upon it. Our Shi'a are those from whose ears knowledge does not escape. Our Shi'a are those who do not praise those who blame us and hate us, and who do not associate with those who diminish our virtues. When our Shi'a meet a believer, they show him respect; when they meet an ignorant person, they turn away from him.

Our Shi'a are those who do not bark like dogs in the cold, nor are they greedy like crows. If our Shi'a ask for something from someone, they ask from one of their own brothers, even if they die of hunger. Our Shi'a are those who speak to us, abandoning even their friends in their love for us, and who bring the distant near to us and drive away the near.

A man asked him, "O son of the Prophet, where will you find such a Shi'a?"

He replied: "They will be found scattered across the earth. They will be very poor, yet their eyes will be bright with tranquility. If they are seen, they will not be recognized; if they disappear, they will not be sought; if they fall ill, they will not be visited; if they seek marriage, none will marry them; if they travel a road, they will be shunned. And when the ignorant speak to them with ignorance, they ⟪ar:السلام⟫ respond, 'Peace be upon you.'"

The man then asked, "O son of the Prophet, what will befall those who claim to be Shi'a with their tongues, while their hearts oppose Shi'a?"

He replied: "An era will come upon them that will destroy them and kill them through hatred and discord." Beware! By the One who helped us with His angels, God will slay them with His own hand.

A person who separates himself from the people of the world, from his parents and his children, and who loves, advises, and cooperates with his brothers for the sake of God—even if he is an Abyssinian or a Christian—when a believer is raised from his grave on the Day of Resurrection, he will be white as snow, washed in the water of Paradise. He will receive an eternal blessing and will have the company of angels, prophets, and messengers. There is no greater honor in the sight of God than a servant who has been banished for God's sake until he meets his Lord in exile. Our Shi'a are the warners of the Day of Judgment upon the earth, a lamp for those who seek it, a sign and a light, and a guide for those who obey Allah. Those who come to them find peace; those who love them are treated with pleasure and kindness. Our Shi'a are generous, forgiving, and merciful.[^cite-6]

The scholar among our Shi'a controls his tongue and is pleased with obedience to Allah and His Messenger, keeping his plans for our enemies hidden in his heart. When he goes out in the morning, he is aware of all their faults, yet he does not reveal to them what is in his heart. He beholds their wickedness with his eyes, hears their wickedness with his ears, and curses them with his tongue. Those who hate them are his friends, and those who love them are his enemies. Such a person is commanded to shade the sky, and in his worldly attribute, he finds whatever he asks of Allah.

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ narrated that the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said to his Shi'a: "You should be obliged to be ascetic and pious. Tell the truth, pay back the trusts. Remain steadfast in the path you have been shown: for when the soul of one of you reaches his throat, he rejoices. If you live, you will see that which cools your eyes; and if you die, by Allah, you will be met with the best of words. By God! You are on the religion of Allah and the religion of my fathers. By God! This means not only ⟪ar:محمدؑ (الباقر) بن علی⟫ and ⟪ar:علیؑ (زین العابدین) بن الحسین⟫, but also these noble ones ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم و اسماعیل اور اسحاق و یعقوب علیہم السلام⟫. They all share one religion. Fear God and help us with piety and devotion. By God! Prayer, fasting, Hajj, and Zakat will be accepted only from you, and God will forgive you. But remember: our Shi'a are those who follow us, not those who oppose us. When we are afraid, they are also afraid; when we live in peace, they too live in peace and tranquility. These are our Shi'a. When Iblis went among the people, they obeyed him; when he came to our Shi'a, they refused to obey him. So Iblis incited the people against the Shi'a, and that is why our Shi'a suffer at their hands."

### Shi'a Like the Sun

The Sun has proved through its millions of years of rotation that there is no deviation in its orbit. Its rising every day and its setting every evening are certain. Through its consistency and action it has demonstrated that it never breaks its promise!

The example of a true believer is also such: through his past life and steadfastness, he sets such an example that decisions can be made about him before his action unfolds—if such circumstances arise, this believing man will certainly act thus.

*(Lessons of Mulla Ghulam Hussain ibn Hazrat Nasrullah) (2005)*

## Attributes of the God-Fearing Believer

It has been narrated that ⟪ar:مولانا علی علیہ السلام⟫ and a pious and devout companion named ⟪ar:حمام ابن شریح⟫ once met. The companion said, "Tell ⟪ar:أمیر المومنین!⟫ me the characteristics of a god-fearing person in such a way that I can see them with my own eyes!" ⟪ar:أمیر المومنین⟫ replied, "O Hammam! Fear Allah and do righteous deeds, for Allah is with those who fear Him and those who do good. Hammam was not satisfied with this statement and insisted on a fuller answer. Maulana Ali was thus compelled to elaborate:

All praise is due to Allah, Creator of all creation, and peace and blessings be upon the Messenger ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ و سلم⟫ of Allah—His Mustafa, His chosen servant. Glory be to Allah, Who created all creatures in such a way that He has no need of their obedience and is protected from their sins. The sins of sinners do Him no harm, and the obedience of the obedient profits Him nothing. He has apportioned each person's sustenance and assigned to each his place in this world.

Of course, the pious and God-fearing hold a special place in His creation. Their speech is measured, their attire moderate, and their manners refined. They turn their eyes away from what Allah has forbidden and open their ears to beneficial knowledge. They live in times of hardship as they do in times of ease. Had Allah not appointed a specific span of life for them, their souls would not have lingered in their bodies even for a moment, such is their fear of Allah's reward and punishment. The grandeur of Allah is so deeply rooted in their hearts that all worldly things have become contemptible in their eyes. Therefore, Paradise to them is as though they behold it with their own eyes and taste its blessings. Hell is to them as though they see it with their own eyes and feel the agony of its punishment.[^cite-7]

Their hearts are softened, and they are protected from all evil. Their bodies are frail, their needs are few, and their souls are pure. They have earned long rest for themselves by bearing short afflictions in return. This is a beneficial exchange that Allah has made easy for them. The world sought them out, but they sought not the world. The world imprisoned them, yet they freed their souls by paying its ransom.

They spend their nights reciting the Qur'an while standing, soothing their hearts with it, and healing their spiritual ailments. When a verse comes regarding the blessings of Paradise, their souls turn toward it with great longing, as though Paradise were before them; when there is a verse about the torments of Hell, they incline their ears to it as though they hear the sounds of Hell and its anguished cries. They bow their backs in prostration, bend their jaws in submission, and place their palms, knees, and foreheads upon the ground, seeking their salvation. They spend their days in patience, knowledge, piety, and reverence. The fear of God has made their bodies thin as arrows. Should one see them, one would think them ill, though they are not. People call them mad and insane when they witness their state, for in truth, the thought of the Hereafter has consumed their reason.

They are not satisfied with their small deeds, and they despise their brilliant actions. They always blame themselves and live in fear of their deeds. When people praise them, they say, "I know myself better than others, and Allah knows me better than I know myself. O Allah! Do not hold me to account according to what others believe of me; rather, make me better than they think, and forgive me my sins—sins which only You know and they do not."

You will observe in them a humility in their faith, yet gentleness in their nature; perfection in their belief, yet eagerness for knowledge; moderation in acquiring wealth, yet fervent devotion to worship; gratitude in hunger, yet diligence in hardship; desire for lawful things, yet contentment with guidance; hatred of greed, yet constant fear lest their good deeds fall short. They are anxious to give thanks to Allah by night and to remember Him by day. They spend their nights in fear and their mornings in joy—fearing they might forget Allah's remembrance at night, rejoicing that they are recipients of His blessings and grace. **If their soul dislikes a righteous deed, they do not allow it to act as it pleases.** They do not dwell upon transient things but draw coolness of the eye from eternal deeds. **They transform their knowledge into patience, and their words into actions.**

You will see that their hopes are modest, their errors few. Their hearts are apprehensive, their souls at peace, their food simple, their religion secure, their desires dead, and their anger subdued. They expect only good and fear not evil. When they are among those who forget Allah, they are among those who remember Him. They forgive those who have wronged them and give to those who have deprived them. Those who treat them badly meet with their goodness in return.

Unseemly speech is distant from them. Their words are gentle, their vices absent, their virtues ever-present. Their good precedes them, and evil has turned away from them. **They are dignified in adversity, patient in hardship, and grateful in days of ease.** They do not transgress against those whom they dislike, nor do they commit sin for those whom they love. They accept the truth before evidence is presented against them. They do not betray their trust and do not forget what they have been given to remember. They do not revile others and do not hurt their neighbors. They take no pleasure in the suffering of others. They do not enter into error and do not stray from truth.

If they remain silent, their silence does not harm them. If they laugh, their voices are not raised. If anyone wrongs them, they endure it until Allah takes vengeance on their behalf. **Their soul suffers on their account while others find comfort from them.** **They make their souls endure hardship for the Hereafter, and they protect people from their own harm.** Their separation from people is for the sake of piety and purity; their closeness to those near them is with compassion and kindness. They do not distance themselves from people out of ostentation or arrogance, nor do they draw near to them through deceit and cunning.

⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ Upon hearing ⟪ar:حمام⟫ this sermon, he was so deeply moved that his soul departed from his body. Then ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ said, "By God, this is what I feared! This is how effective counsel strikes the intellect!"

## Attributes of the Believer

Once, when ⟪ar:امیر المومنین مولانا علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ was delivering a sermon, a man who was devout and learned in jurisprudence asked him, ⟪ar:یا امیر المومنین!⟫ to describe a believer as if he were standing before him!

Allah (the Exalted) said: "O you with understanding, a believer is a person of wisdom and discernment." His countenance is serene, his heart tender ⟪ar:حزین⟫ and sorrowful, and his breast is spacious. He abhors all transient things, remembers Allah constantly, and is patient and grateful. **He grieves over the Hereafter, and finds joy in his poverty.** His knowledge is profound, his patience vast, and his mercy abundant. He keeps himself distant from hatred and does not rush into matters. His heart is not troubled by anything, nor does anything make him despair. He does not oppress others, nor does he oppress himself. **His soul is harder than stone when facing suffering.** His striving for livelihood is sweet as honey. He is not one who becomes greedy and seizes the rights of others. He is neither harsh nor impatient, neither lost in contemplation nor preoccupied with worldly matters. When angry, he acts with justice. When asked for something, he responds with courtesy. He does not act from anger, does not oppress ⟪ar:حتک⟫ anyone, does not wrong anyone, and is a sincere lover. He is faithful to his promises and fulfills his oaths. He is the first to extend help to people. He lives his life humbly. He does not speak idle words; he is content with Allah the Almighty. He opposes his own desires and is kind to those younger than him. He does not concern himself with irrelevant matters. In relationships with friends, he does not burden them. He spends without extravagance. He deceives no one, nor does he betray anyone. He does not follow anything that reveals another's fault. He does not oppress anyone; rather, he is the guardian of people and strives on their behalf. He is the helper of the weak and the protector of the afflicted. He neither conceals nor reveals anyone's secrets. He endures much suffering but never complains with his tongue. If he sees someone's virtue, he mentions it; if he sees someone's fault, he hides it. He conceals people's defects and maintains an unseen gaze. He accepts people's excuses and pardons their mistakes. **When he learns of a good thing, he does not delay in pursuing it, nor does he fail to correct evil.** He is trustworthy and pious. His inner self is pure, and people are satisfied with him. He engages with thoughts of the Hereafter and trusts in no one but Allah. He lives in this world as a traveler passes through. **He loves solitude and longs for salvation in the Hereafter. He loves for the sake of Allah and strives for Allah's pleasure. He does not seek revenge for himself but leaves such matters to Allah.** **He does not befriend any enemy of Allah.** He seeks the company of the poor. He is the protector of people, the helper of those who speak truth, the supporter of kinship, the father of orphans, the husband to widows, and the caretaker of the afflicted. In every hardship, people look to him for assistance. He puts his hope in Allah in every difficulty. He is generous and of good cheer. He is steadfast, restrained in anger, ⟪ar:مُتَبَسِّم⟫ ⟪ar:دقیق النظر⟫ and cautious in his faith. He does not like miserliness. If people withhold his due, he is patient. He avoids evil deeds. He is rich through contentment. His modesty prevails over his desire, and his love does not allow jealousy to arise.

## What Is the Reality of Love?

Allah has created man in such a way that he loves only himself and does not love anyone other than himself. Even when he appears to love another person, it is only because that ⟪ar:'⟫ person shares a close bond with him ⟪ar:'⟫. Consider, for instance, ⟪ar:'⟫ his children, ⟪ar:'⟫ ⟪ar:'⟫ his parents, his friends, ⟪ar:'⟫ ⟪ar:'⟫ his family, and so forth. But even in love for others, the pivot of his self always remains. He is always at the center of his sphere; whoever enters that sphere, he loves them, and the closer they approach his center, the greater his love for them.[^cite-8]

For this reason, every human being is possessed of selfishness—and in this there is profound wisdom from Allah. He is not sincere with anyone but himself. Every human being is bound to the pole of his own self and is engaged in its worship. Man's soul is the center of his existence, and he continues to revolve around his temperaments and desires throughout his life. The self commands him to ⟪ar:کبر⟫ (arrogance), ⟪ar:حسد⟫ (envy), and ⟪ar:حرس⟫ (greed)—it urges him to do evil and to commit vice. Man obeys it, does all that lies in his power, and submits to its every command, falling into the trap of false desires and worthless feelings. He remains imprisoned in this bondage until death knocks upon his door. At that moment, the wages of false praise and self-glorification that he accumulated in his servitude to his own soul are of no use to him; he deserves Hell for having squandered his life.

### Love for Others

Then, when that same person falls in love with another human being, he makes his beloved the center of his existence and begins to abandon the center of his self, starting instead to revolve in the orbit of his beloved. He begins to accept his or her will in the face of every demand—great or small, legitimate or unreasonable. His sole purpose becomes to gain the agreement and acceptance of his beloved.

Yet even in this state, a person's selfishness persists, for through accepting his beloved's will, he seeks the satisfaction of his own soul. The only difference is that the center of his orbit shifts, and all his efforts now point away from his self toward his beloved's self.

In other words, Allah has endowed man with both the capacity for self-worship and the capacity for self-renunciation. Circling around your own center is selfishness; annihilating yourself in order to gain another's pleasure is love.

### Love for Allah and the Messenger (Peace Be Upon Him)

Allah says in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 51:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا خَلَقْتُ ٱلْجِنَّ وَٱلْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ ‎⟫
> *And I (Allah) created not the jinns and humans except they should worship Me (Alone).*

And I did not create the jinn and mankind except to worship Me.

In this verse, Allah Almighty employs the word ⟪ar:'عبد'⟫ (abd, slave), which denotes the state of ⟪ar:'تسلیم'⟫ (tasleem, submission). As long as this recognition is directed toward his own self or toward any other human like himself, he can never advance. This is why Allah says, "I have created the jinn and mankind for My ⟪ar:عَبدِیَت⟫ (ʿabdiyya, slavery)." That is, if a person wishes to progress beyond this mortal world, he must relinquish the slavery of his own soul and enter into the slavery of his Allah with heartfelt joy—with great joy arising from the *daʿwa* (the divine summons). He must abandon the narrow circle of his own self and become a traveler through a greater expanse. He must submit to the commands of Allah and seek His pleasure. ⟪ar:'اسلام'⟫ (islam, submission) And although this task is most difficult, it is possible to attain the highest stations. On the ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ basis of this principle of acceptance, Islam introduced the practice of showing deference and respect, in which a person removes the crown of arrogance ⟪ar:'أنا'⟫ (ana, "I") from his head and bows it at the feet of an elder.[^cite-9]

For this reason, when the Prophet made the people bear witness to his Prophethood, he first expressed himself in terms of ⟪ar:عبدیت⟫ (slavery to Allah), then in ⟪ar:'عبدیت'⟫ his testimony, and then announced his ⟪ar:'رسالت'⟫ (risala, prophethood), saying: ⟪ar:اَشھَدُ اَنَّ مُحَمَّدً عبدُہُ وَ رَسُولہُ⟫ (I bear witness that Muhammad is His slave and His messenger). Allah so loved this humility of his that when He mentioned the Prophet's Ascension, He used the word ⟪ar:رسول⟫ (rasul, messenger) rather than ⟪ar:عبد⟫ (abd, slave), because the Messenger is more honored and more trustworthy in Allah's sight than [any reference to] the ⟪ar:عبدیت⟫ (slavery) and ⟪ar:رسالت⟫ (prophethood) of others.[^cite-10]

> ⟪quran 17:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سُبْحَنَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِۦ لَيْلًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْحَرَامِ إِلَى ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْأَقْصَا ٱلَّذِى بَرَكْنَا حَوْلَهُۥ لِنُرِيَهُۥ مِنْ آيَتِنَآ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلسَّمِيعُ ٱلْبَصِيرُ ‎⟫
> *Who took His slave (Muhammad PBUH) for a journey by night from Al-Masjid-al-Haram (at Makkah) to the farthest mosque (in Jerusalem), the neighbourhood whereof We have blessed, in order that We might show him (Muhammad PBUH) of Our Ayat (proofs, evidences, lessons, signs, etc.). Verily, He is the All-Hearer, the All-Seer.*

Glory be to Him who took His servant by night from the Sacred Mosque to the Farthest Mosque, the precincts of which We have blessed, that We might show him some of Our signs. Indeed, He is the All-hearing, the All-seeing.

The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said, "Speak the truth to the people, even if they dislike it." Yet a person, owing to his selfishness, does not wish to appear bad in the eyes of others. The way of the prophets and messengers of truth has always been to proclaim truth among the people. They humbled themselves before the people in order to seek Allah's pleasure, and because of this, their station and reward are established with Allah. Allah says:

> ⟪quran 3:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِن كُنتُمْ تُحِبُّونَ ٱللَّهَ فَٱتَّبِعُونِى يُحْبِبْكُمُ ٱللَّهُ وَيَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ ذُنُوبَكُمْ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH to mankind): "If you (really) love Allah then follow me (i.e. accept Islamic Monotheism, follow the Quran and the Sunnah), Allah will love you and forgive you of your sins. And Allah is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful." *

Say, if you love God, then follow me, and God will love you and forgive you your sins. God is Forgiving and Merciful.

In this eloquent verse, Allah has established the standard of His love through His Messenger. Allah addressed the people through His Messenger and said, "Follow Me if you desire to seek the love of Allah." In this verse, Allah says: "If you obey the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) by resisting your desires and passions, Allah will love you and forgive you all your sins." The love of Allah's Messenger is such a magnet, and it carries such blessings, that Allah will forgive the sins of His servants for the sake of their love for His Messenger and their obedience to him![^cite-11]

## The Law of Attraction (Qanun al-Alf)

Allah Almighty has founded His religion upon love, which is called ⟪ar-quote:قانون الف⟫ (the Law of Attraction). Love is the act of annihilating one's own will, one's own desires, and one's own designs for the sake of another's will.

If we examine the structure of this universe, we shall see this very law operating everywhere! Unless the mineral breaks itself down, it cannot be absorbed by the plant. In order for the mineral to become vegetation, it must submit to the plant. Similarly, unless the plant submits itself to the animal and bows to it, the animal cannot consume it and form its own body from it. Unless the animal offers its neck beneath the knife of man, unprotected and unresisting, it cannot rise as ⟪ar:قامت الفی⟫ food for the slaughterer. In the same way, unless a person accepts his will, desires, actions, and inclinations at the command of Allah, Allah does not accept him and does not render him worthy of Paradise. In order to achieve progress, every living being must pass through stages of suffering and patience. Yet sincerity is a condition of love! And in order to create sincerity, this acceptance of man must be voluntary![^cite-12] For this reason, there is no force or compulsion in the religion.

This law is manifest in man's own self, and it is to this that the ⟪ar:نبی کریم (ﷺ)⟫ Prophet (upon whom be peace) referred when he said: ⟪ar-quote:حب الوطن من الأیمان⟫ ("Love of one's homeland is part of faith"). ## Love of and Friendship with God

The Qur'an presents a foundational principle concerning the relationship between the believer and the Divine. God addresses the believers with both warning and promise: ⟪quran 5:54⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مَن يَرْتَدَّ مِنكُمْ عَن دِينِهِۦ فَسَوْفَ يَأْتِى ٱللَّهُ بِقَوْمٍۢ يُحِبُّهُمْ وَيُحِبُّونَهُۥٓ أَذِلَّةٍ عَلَى ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ أَعِزَّةٍ عَلَى ٱلْكَفِرِينَ يُجَهِدُونَ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَلَا يَخَافُونَ لَوْمَةَ لَآئِمٍۢ ۚ ذَلِكَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهُ يُؤْتِيهِ مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ وَسِعٌ عَلِيمٌ ‎⟫ "O you who believe! Whosoever of you turns back from his religion, Allah will bring a people whom Allah desires and who love Him, gentle with the believers, mighty against the disbelievers, fighting in the way of Allah, and not fearing the reproach of any reproacher. That is the bounty of Allah; and Allah is all-embracing, all-knowing."[^cite-20]

This verse presents a sharp contrast: it condemns those who threaten apostasy whilst simultaneously praising a community whose defining characteristic is mutual love—they love God, and God loves them in return. During the time of the Prophet Muhammad (upon whom be peace), certain individuals attempted to coerce the Messenger by threatening to abandon Islam and join the Jews and Christians if their counsel were not heeded. To such threats God replied: your departure matters not, for We shall replace you with a people of sincere devotion. What excellence belongs to such a community, worthy of praise both by creation and Creator! The first and foremost attribute of this divine eulogy (*manqabat*) is precisely this reciprocal love: they love Allah and Allah loves them.[^cite-21]

Yet love that flows in one direction alone remains imperfect and incomplete. True love is that which travels both ways—a mutual affection wherein the beloved reciprocates the lover's devotion. Many claim to love God, but only those whose claim is proven through unwavering steadfastness shall know God's love in return. One-sided affection is flawed; perfect love is reversible love, flowing from heart to heart.

The love of God transcends ordinary human experience. It has been a source of wonder and bewilderment for the great saints and spiritual masters of every age. The human soul, in its current state, lacks the strength to bear even a fragment of divine love without being transformed entirely. Consider how worldly love, when it takes hold, dispossesses a person of reason itself—the beloved consumes all thoughts, eclipses every concern. When such is the power of earthly passion, what then of the love of the Divine Originator, Most High (*Mubdiʿ Taʿālā*)? 

### The Example of Jesus and the Elder Worshipper

The scholar al-Zubayrī relates in his *Hayat al-Haywan* an instructive account. One day Jesus (upon whom be peace) passed by a mountain and discovered a solitary place of worship where an elderly devotee sat in constant prayer. His back had grown bent from years of prostration; his body had become thin and withered. When Jesus inquired how long this worship had endured, the elder replied: "O Messenger of God, seventy years have passed, yet one need remains unfulfilled by the Almighty." He then made a request: "You are the chosen servant of God—intercede for me that Allah might grant me but a particle of His pure love." Jesus raised his hands in supplication, and God accepted his prayer.

Days later, when Jesus returned to that place, he found the sanctuary collapsed, the ground torn asunder, and the worshipper standing in a great pit, eyes closed and mouth open, gazing toward heaven. When Jesus addressed him, there came no reply. Bewildered, Jesus cried out: "O Creator! What has become of this servant of Yours?" The divine message came: "O Jesus! This worshipper asked for a particle of Our love. We granted him but one part out of seventy thousand parts of Our love—and We knew he could not bear more. Do you see the state to which he has come? Had We granted him even slightly more of Our love, you would perceive what would have befallen him!"[^cite-22]

### The Tears of Shuʿayb

In like manner, the prophet Shuʿayb (upon whom be peace), through his intense remembrance of God, lost his sight. The Almighty restored his vision. Yet he wept again, and blindness returned. A third time he wept in this way, and God spoke to him: "O Shuʿayb! If you weep for Paradise, We have made it forbidden to you. If you weep in fear of Hell, We have forbidden the Fire to you!" Shuʿayb replied: "O Lord! I weep neither for Paradise nor from fear of Hell. I weep only from love of You." God revealed: "Ask, O Prophet, what do you desire?" Shuʿayb said: "O Creator of all worlds, speak once to me and say, 'O Shuʿayb, you are my beloved!'" The divine voice came: "Yes, O Shuʿayb, you are indeed My beloved!" At these words, Shuʿayb's soul departed from the elemental realm (*Qafs*), so overwhelming was his joy.[^cite-23]

### The Three Degrees of Worship

This is why the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (upon whom be peace), proclaimed in his sermons: "O God, I do not worship You out of desire for Paradise, nor out of fear of Hell. Rather, have I found You worthy of worship, and thus I worship You." The Lord said that such worship is the worship of the free; all other worship is commerce. Those who worship for the promise of heaven engage in trade, whilst those who worship from fear of punishment act as slaves. The first group demands payment for their service; the second acts under duress, like bondsmen.

The true beloved of God is one who calls himself the friend of the Divine and whom the Divine calls friend in return. And the joy of love exists only when the fire burns on both sides! The genuine love of God belongs exclusively to those who are true followers of the Messenger (upon whom be peace). Indeed, God Himself commanded the Prophet to declare: ⟪quran 3:31⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِن كُنتُمْ تُحِبُّونَ ٱللَّهَ فَٱتَّبِعُونِى يُحْبِبْكُمُ ٱللَّهُ وَيَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ ذُنُوبَكُمْ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ "Say, if you love God, then follow me, and God will love you and forgive you your sins. God is Forgiving and Merciful."[^cite-24]

ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib alone among the companions embodied this verse in absolute obedience. Whether journeying or dwelling at home, whether in peace or in warfare, whether in health or infirmity, he never uttered a word against the Prophet's command, nor delayed in executing his will. Of his own obedience, the Commander of the Faithful said: "I have obeyed the Messenger of Allah as a young goat follows its mother."[^cite-25]

### The Three Signs of Love and Their Transcendence

The scholars teach that love possesses three external signs: the thinning of the body, the yellowing of the complexion, and the transformation of temperament—all arising from fear that the beloved might be displeased. Yet these signs appear only in those unaware of their true station with their beloved. Those who possess perfect knowledge of their condition and rank in the presence of the beloved are freed from such outward manifestations.

When someone asked the Commander of the Faithful why all other worshippers and lovers grew pale and thin whilst his face remained radiant and his body strong, he replied with profound wisdom: "These people do not know what their station is with their beloved. But since the veils have been lifted from before my eyes, I have been granted understanding that *'even if all the veils are removed before my eyes, my faith shall not increase by a hair's breadth.' When I gazed with the eyes of my heart and found my Beloved pleased with me, my fear vanished immediately. Then, when God Himself gave me glad tidings of this state, all my concerns became insignificant."* The Commander of the Faithful was always observed to be radiant and joyful in the proximity of the Divine. Yet when the moment of obedience arrived, the color of his blessed face would fade and his body would tremble with the fear of God. Once, while preparing for pilgrimage, he began to recite the sacred formula. Such trembling seized him, his face turned pale, and he fell down. When the people asked him what had happened, he said: "I was gripped by fear that if I merely voiced the formula with my lips, what would become of me in God's sight?"[^cite-26]

### The Three Types of Love

The scholars distinguish three varieties of love. The first is the love of those who cry out in complaint whenever suffering arrives from the beloved—this is a flawed and incomplete form of devotion. The second is the love wherein the lover endures pain in silence, never permitting a word of complaint to escape the tongue:

*Grief does not trouble the world,*  
*Nor does noise disturb the oyster in the river.*  
*Even in Nimrod's flames I remained silent—*  
*A believer, not one who despairs.*

The third and highest type is that in which the lover, upon encountering obstacles and difficulties in attaining the beloved's pleasure, begins to experience delight and joy in the suffering itself. The passion rises to such heights that the heart desires increase in hardship. This is the supreme destination of love, reserved only for Muhammad and the family of Muhammad (upon whom be peace and blessings). History itself bears witness: the greater the burden of suffering placed upon them, the brighter shone the radiance of their countenances. When Imam Ḥusayn faced the trials of Karbalāʾ's battlefield, his back grew only straighter.[^cite-27]

*I shall spend my life in your sorrow—*  
*What is this fleeting life of two days?*

### The Test of True Love in Warfare

War is the supreme opportunity to test the authenticity of love. In times of comfort and gathering, even enemies become friends. Yet with the prophets of all ages there gathered sincere believers who loved their Prophet and Imam with all their hearts and souls, remaining steadfast until the end. But the companions and friends of Imam Ḥusayn (upon whom be peace) presented rare and wondrous exemplars of true love and loyalty. It is better to say that seventy-two of them possessed the qualities of those "who are steadfast in their promises, truthful in their intentions, faithful in their covenant, pious and righteous, upright and loyal."[^cite-28]

Yet it is worthy of remembrance that Imam Ḥusayn did not possess such a gathering of pure believers in any prophet's time—for hypocrites and believers have always been mixed together. Even in the time of the Prophet and in the time of ʿAlī, such purity was never achieved. The hypocrites have caused immense harm to the *Umma* under the guise of friendship. But the gathering with Imam Ḥusayn was utterly purified. There appeared not a single sign of hypocrisy. All were believers! Imam Ḥusayn not only refined his own community but also refined the enemy's ranks. From among the hypocrites, he drew forth two or four believers—such as Hurr—who joined him, leaving on each side only the pure: on one side, pure believers; on the other, pure disbelievers. Truth stood arrayed against falsehood.

### Ḥabīb ibn Mazāḥir and the Perfect Love of Companionship

ʿAlī Mazāḥir was a pious believer who frequently brought his young son, Ḥabīb ibn Mazāḥir, into the presence of the Prophet (upon whom be peace). It is related that Ḥabīb harbored a profound affection for Imam Ḥusayn. Wherever the Imam went, Ḥabīb followed. One day the Prophet observed Ḥabīb following Imam Ḥusayn, gathering the dust from beneath the Imam's feet and rubbing it upon his face and eyes. The Prophet called to ʿAlī Mazāḥir: "Bring your son to me!" He seated Ḥabīb upon his lap, embraced him with love, and tears flowed from his eyes. When Mazāḥir asked the reason, the Prophet replied: "Mazāḥir, one day this child shall be sacrificed for my Ḥusayn."[^cite-29]

It is narrated that Mazāḥir once invited the Messenger of Allah to dine at his home, saying: "O Messenger of God, bring Ḥasan and Ḥusayn with you." As Ḥabīb stood watching the path for Imam Ḥusayn's approach, his foot slipped and he fell from the rooftop, dying instantly. Mazāḥir feared that if distress arose in the house at that moment, the honor of the Prophet's visit would be lost and the blessing of proximity to the Messenger forfeited. The love of the Messenger conquered the love of kinship! He concealed Ḥabīb's body within the house and strictly forbade anyone to make complaint until the Prophet and the Imams had eaten their fill.

When the Messenger arrived, Imam Ḥusayn searched for Ḥabīb and asked: "Where is my Ḥabīb?" Mazāḥir replied that Ḥabīb had stepped out briefly and would return soon. But Imam Ḥusayn said: "I shall not eat without Ḥabīb." The Imam's grief could not be contained, and the whole matter was revealed to the Prophet. Upon hearing of Ḥabīb's death, Ḥusayn wept bitterly. He entreated God: "My beloved must return to life, otherwise I shall not eat." The Holy Prophet came to Ḥabīb's body and prayed to the Lord: "Restore to life this child, who is devoted to my Ḥusayn!" By God's grace, Ḥabīb opened his eyes, wept with his head at Ḥusayn's feet, and Imam Ḥusayn embraced him.[^cite-30]

*Desires are consumed in the pleasure of a friend—*  
*Then why would not the lover wish for this?*

### The Martyrdom of Ḥabīb at Karbalāʾ

One day, while Ḥabīb ibn Mazāḥir was purchasing *khuzab* (a type of plant dye) in Kūfa, Muslim ibn ʿAwsaja passed by. Ḥabīb asked: "Brother Muslim, I see armies gathering in Kūfa and weapons being collected. Do you know the reason?" Muslim replied: "O Ḥabīb, do you not know? The people of Kūfa are preparing to kill Imam Ḥusayn!" At these words, Ḥabīb immediately cast the merchandise from his hands and declared: "By God! This beard shall be stained with the blood of my head in aid of Ḥusayn!" He at once prepared for departure to Karbalāʾ. His wife said to him: "O Ḥabīb, when you stand in the presence of the Prophet's grandson, submit your greetings. After kissing the Imam's hands and feet, say: 'O Lord, if jihād had not been forbidden to women, I myself would have come to your aid.'"[^cite-31]

Ḥabīb departed, instructing his slave: "Have the horse saddled. I shall follow you at such-and-such a place, but reveal my intention to no one!" When Ḥabīb was delayed, the slave, growing anxious, addressed the horse itself: "O horse! If my master does not come, I shall ride you myself to aid Ḥusayn!" When Ḥabīb arrived and heard these words, he wept aloud and said: "O son of the Messenger! My parents are devoted to you! What grievous times have come upon you, that even the slaves prepare to come to your aid!"[^cite-32]

On the battlefield, Ḥabīb devoted himself entirely to the Imam's service. So consumed was he with desire for martyrdom that he spent the night with arms bound, trembling with passion for the sacrifice. When Muslim ibn ʿAwsaja fell wounded and called out, "O grandson of the Messenger of God," Imam Ḥusayn reached the fallen warrior. Upon finding Muslim dead, the Imam cradled his head in his arm and said: "O Muslim, may God reward you well." At that moment, Ḥabīb said to the body of Muslim: "O Muslim! Had I known I would survive you, I would have asked you to leave me a will. But I am following you now. Yet since it is customary to ask the deceased for their bequest—if you have one, tell me." At this, Muslim's eyes opened; he pointed toward the Imam and said: "O Ḥabīb, only protect my Imam."[^cite-33] How loyal were those supporters of Ḥusayn, those heroes of Karbalāʾ! Life rested upon their lips, yet even in death, it was the Imam who occupied their minds.

When battle commenced, Ḥabīb repeatedly approached the Imam requesting permission to fight. The Imam would say: "O Ḥabīb, your broken heart is bound to the Master." With permission granted, Ḥabīb descended to the battlefield like a young lion, reciting verses of devotion before attacking the enemy ranks like a tiger among deer. Many of the oppressors fell like uprooted carrots. Then Ḥusayn ibn Amin (may he be cursed) struck Ḥabīb with a spear upon his chest. Unable to bear the blow, Ḥabīb fell from his horse and cried out: "O grandson of the Messenger of Allah!"

*All desire has departed from my heart—*  
*Now I come to you alone.*

Imam Ḥusayn came to the battlefield with ʿAbbās and ʿAlī Akbar. Seeing Ḥabīb's fall, he wept and said: "O my beloved friend, you leave us alone in the hands of our enemies!" At that moment, Ḥabīb opened his eyes and gazed upon Imam Ḥusayn. He said: "O Imam, I see your grandfather before me, giving me glad tidings: 'O Ḥabīb, come swiftly! The attendants and servants await you. Hasten, that We may give you water from the pool of Kausar!'" Saying this, Ḥabīb ibn Mazāḥir died in the lap of Imam Ḥusayn. The Imam recited the sacred words "*Inna lillāhi wa inna ilayhi rājiʿūn*" and said: "May God have mercy upon the Beloved! He used to complete the entire Qur'an in a single night!"[^cite-34]

How blessed was Ḥabīb! Not only did he possess the fortune of the Prophet's company in childhood, but in his final moments he departed from this world in the lap of the Prophet's grandson. What honor befell him—not only to be the dear friend of Imam Ḥusayn throughout his life, but also after his martyrdom to be buried at the Imam's feet, thus proving his friendship and love eternally.[^cite-35]

### The Martyrs of Karbalāʾ and the Standard of Pure Love

Ḥabīb ibn Mazāḥir and all the martyrs of Karbalāʾ set upon the battlefield the standard of pure and authentic love. Not one of them thought for a moment of preserving his life from the enemy. Each trial that came kindled a gleam in their eyes and matured their souls. The angels of heaven, witnessing their passion and the ecstasy of their martyrdom, confessed in the court of the Eternal: "O Lord, You have spoken truly: '*We know what you do not know.*' How could we have known that the descendants of Adam possessed such steel essence, that the revolutions of earth and sky together could not weaken them or turn them from their unwavering will!"[^cite-36]

## The Importance of Wilayat (Spiritual Guardianship)

The Almighty has created all creation in pairs—the visible and tangible things, as well as the intelligible and spiritual realities. As He Himself declares, pairing every outward form with its inner reality, making them companions to one another: the world and the Hereafter, the body and the soul. The advantage of this pairing is that one partner is dynamic (the soul) and the other is static (the body).

| Dynamic | Static |
| --- | --- |
| Speaker | Guardian |

God has granted humanity superiority over all creatures by virtue of the faculty of rational speech (*ʿaql*, intellect)—the ability to utter meaningful utterances. And this rational discourse comprises at minimum two elements: one dynamic (the verb) and one static (the noun). This demonstrates that God has created all existence in balance and pairing. Such correspondences appear everywhere, in all things, in every matter both religious and worldly, outward and inward, sensible and intelligible. God has said in His revelation that all things come in pairs.[^cite-37]

The Prophet (upon whom be peace) established his religion upon this same divine principle, adapting the methods of worship and guidance to this same divine pattern—the best of all patterns. When a person of reason contemplates this, he shall come to understand that Islam and the Sharīʿa of the Prophet follow the same principles as the creation of the heavens, the earth, and the entire universe. All principles have been created in pairs. After such contemplation, his faith becomes firm, and he becomes a true believer—a confirmer of the Messenger. Wherever he looks, whatever command he considers, he finds it aligned with the creation of the universe. At that moment, he becomes fully convinced of this great miracle of the Messenger—the Qur'an. The Holy Prophet himself says in his *ḥadīth*: "God has placed the foundation of His religion in accordance with His creation, so that His creation might prove His religion and demonstrate its unity and oneness with faith."[^cite-38]

### The Six Days of Creation and the Seventh Pillar

God says in His Book: "He created the heavens and the earth in six days, and on the seventh day He ascended the Throne"—meaning He established the perfect order and administration of the universe in six days so that creation would not become confused, scattered, and destroyed.

God is exalted far beyond the notion that His words are frivolous or mistaken. He is exalted beyond any supposed inability to create the universe of heavens and earth in an instant—or even in less time than an instant. He Himself declares in the Holy Qur'an that whatever He wills, He says "Be!" and it is. He can create in a moment what takes others lifetimes. Yet the fact that creation unfolded over six days demonstrates profound wisdom hidden within this divine narrative.

When God possesses the power to create at will, the question naturally arises: Why did the creation of the universe span six days? This verse surely contains inner meanings (*taʾwīl*, esoteric exegesis) of great expediency and wisdom.

### The Six Pillars of Religion and the Seventh: Wilayat

In the same manner, when we examine the foundational duties (*furūʿ al-dīn*) of Islam, we find that the Messenger of God (upon whom be peace) established his religion upon six pillars:

| Purity | Prayer | Alms-tax | Fasting | Pilgrimage | Striving |

Just as God preserved the labor of six days from wasteful dissolution by ascending the Throne on the seventh day (*isṭwā ʿalā al-ʿarsh*), so the Prophet (upon whom be peace) preserved these six pillars of his religion from dissolution through the guardianship (*wilāya*) of his successor:

| 7 |
| --- |
| Guardianship |

This guardian is he whom the Prophet made his brother on the Day of Ghadīr, whom he appointed as trustee of his treasury of knowledge and the gate of his city of wisdom. Of him the Prophet said: "I am the city of knowledge and ʿAlī is its gate."[^cite-39] This is the gate through which God has commanded us to enter the house. What would be the purpose of God's command and admonition if this were not so? For every person of ordinary sense knows that the door of a house exists precisely for the purpose of entering it. If this were not connected to wisdom and interpretation, it would be altogether meaningless to preach such elementary sense!

When the *Umma* disobeyed its Prophet and turned away from obedience to the *Walī* (the guardian), it went astray, strayed from the path, and became lost. Everyone began to follow their own opinions and interpret the Qur'an according to their own conjectures. As a result, the entire *Umma*—except those upon whom God had mercy—fell into discord, just as the Children of Israel had done. They too had been commanded to enter the gate bowing and saying with their tongues "Ḥiṭṭa" (meaning "Hearing and obedience"). But they changed what the Messenger had said and made mockery of it. Here too, the *Umma* abandoned the door of knowledge and disobeyed the Prophet.[^cite-40]

Wilayat al-Wāṣī (the guardianship of the successor) is the last and seventh duty. It is the noblest and highest in rank, and the six preceding duties depend upon its establishment and continuance for their completion.

### Wilayat as the Seventh Perfection: Argument from the Order of Creation

The wise ones and people of insight teach that humanity is the noblest of all creatures in this world and the quintessence of the powers of the entire cosmos. Though humanity appears last in the order of creation, it is comprehensive of all things, and the perfection of all things is embedded within it. Humanity holds the seventh rank in the order of existence:

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Minerals | Plants | Insects | Cattle | Beasts | Birds |

| --- |

Just as God created humanity in the seventh rank and granted it the status of excellence and perfection, so the Prophet (upon whom be peace) made the will (*waṣiyya*) the seventh obligation and gave it superiority over all six preceding duties. Among creatures below the level of humanity—minerals, plants, insects, cattle, beasts, and birds—each is counted among inferior categories and lacks the perfection of humanity. In the same way, a person who fulfills the six duties yet does not commit himself to the will and *wilāya* of ʿAlī, Commander of the Faithful, shall not attain the rank of perfection.[^cite-41]

### Wilayat Reflected in the Development of the Human Being

If we examine yet another perspective, we see that a person must pass through seven states and stages to be called truly human; otherwise, he is not. God says in His revelation that a person is created through six stages, and when he receives the seventh—the final creation (*khalq*), which is the soul—he is called human. Had he been born without this final creation, he would be called dead.[^cite-42]

| --- |

In like manner, if a person departs from this world without *wilāyat ʿAlī*, despite having performed the six duties of worship, he shall be counted as dead. He shall not be counted among the living! This bodily creation is an outward example of spiritual *wilāya* in the Hereafter.[^cite-43] This is why, as soon as the Messenger of God (upon whom be peace) completed his final obligation, God revealed: "This day have I perfected your religion for you, completed My favor upon you, and have chosen for you Islam as your religion."[^cite-44]

Just as the absence or invalidity of the seventh creation (*khalq*) renders the first six stages of human formation null and void, and the six stages of generation become useless without the creation of the rational soul, similarly, the absence of *wilāya* shall render all six preceding duties of worship null and void. They shall become useless, indeed they shall revert to the religion of ignorance (*al-jāhiliyyā*).[^cite-45]

## Knowledge of the Imam of One's Time

The Prophet (upon whom be peace) said: "Whoever dies without knowledge of the Imam (*maʿrifat al-Imām*) of his time dies the death of ignorance."[^cite-46] There exist two categories of ignorance: the first is primary ignorance, and the second is subsequent ignorance. Evidence for this distinction is found in the Holy Qur'an itself. God says in His revelation that ignorance possesses both a first and a last manifestation.[^cite-47] This is confirmed by Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (upon whom be peace), who taught that two types of ignorance exist: ignorance that is disbelief (*kufr*), and ignorance that is a kind of veiling from knowledge.

| Ignorance of Disbelief | Ignorance of Veiling |
| --- | --- |
| *Jāhiliyyat al-Kufr* (Pre-Islamic Ignorance) | *Jāhiliyyat al-Islām* (Ignorance within Islam) |

The ignorance of disbelief is what prevailed throughout the world before the Prophet's mission—the ignorance of polytheism and idolatry. The ignorance of Islam, by contrast, is that a person does not know the Imam of his time. Because if a person does not acquire knowledge of the Imam of his age, he shall inevitably remain ignorant of the sciences of his religion, shall be led astray, and shall be drowned in the tempest of innovations and misdeeds.[^cite-48]

## Sincere Worship of God

Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (upon whom be peace) said: "A person's life is a mirror of his inner self, and his inner essence is concealed within his life."[^cite-49] Some people attempt to disguise the hypocrisy of their hearts beneath a veneer of good conduct. Yet however much they strive to conceal themselves, such people quickly reveal their inner thoughts through their words or actions. A single slip of the tongue unveils the secrets of the heart, and the garment of hypocrisy is torn asunder.

By contrast, the pious and learned always endeavor to conceal their inner purity and devotion, fearing praise and renown. Yet despite such efforts, their accomplishments become known among the people, and the purity of their souls shines forth like the sun across all horizons.

Hypocrisy is the attempt to establish oneself in another's heart—whether through good deeds, appearance, dress, or any other form of outward conduct. Every such hypocrite in reality denies the Oneness of God. On the Day of Resurrection, God will say to them: "Go and seek your reward from those whom you sought to please in this world."[^cite-50]

Therefore, a true believer must perform all his deeds solely for God's sake, for God alone. He must not associate anyone else in His service or worship. The sign of one who associates partners with God in worship is that he performs good deeds and strives to display them to others, seeking their praise.

Whoever performs good deeds in secret from this world shall find that God rewards him with goodness on every day, and whoever commits sin in secret shall find that God brings upon him punishment on every day. Why, then, do those of you who commit sins do so in secret? Your very concealment of the sin is itself a powerful argument against the deed, for indeed every person bears witness against himself! The outward action of one whose inner self is pure shall always be clear and manifest, whilst the outward action of one whose inner self is impure shall always remain hidden.

Whoever sincerely performs even a small deed for God's sake, God shall reward him with much greater recompense. And whoever performs much work to impress people and exhausts his body through sleepless nights, God shall nullify all his deeds and degrade him in the sight of the One for whom he performed all his deeds.[^cite-51]

## Life Between Fear and Hope

Surely God is the possessor of all power and mercy, anger and grace. His anger accords with His mercy, and His punishments correspond to His boundless blessings. Since His mercy is beyond measure, every sinner hopes that he may be forgiven. And since He is just, every sinner fears His punishment, which he may well deserve. Therefore, a believer must always live his life suspended between fear and hope. Let him fear because he knows not for what sin he shall be held accountable, or whether his name shall be inscribed among those destined for punishment. And let him hope because his meager good deeds may be accepted, and his name may be inscribed among those upon whom God shall shower His grace. Hence the believer must always strive toward good deeds and guard himself from every evil act.[^cite-52]

Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq said: "Fear God as though you see Him. And if you cannot see Him, at least know that He sees you. If you believe that He cannot see you, then you are a disbeliever! And if you believe that He sees you, yet you still sin, then you doubt His power over you!"[^cite-53]

Indeed, a true believer knows and believes that God observes him, that He hears every good and evil word he utters. Thus does he restrain his soul from every desire and his body from every evil pleasure. Surely he who knows God fears Him, and he who fears Him turns away from this world. Only he who possesses knowledge fears God. The greater a person's knowledge increases, the more his fear increases.[^cite-54]

The believer perpetually lives in two states of fear: the first is fear concerning sins recorded in the past, since he knows not what God shall do with those sins. The second is fear for the remainder of his life, for he knows not what further sins he shall commit. The morning of such a believer dawns in a state of fear, and it is this very fear that keeps his soul upon the right path.

Whoever fears God, God shall make everything in this world fear him. And whoever does not fear God, God shall make him fear everything in this world. The Messenger of God (upon whom be peace) loved nothing more than that he should hunger and live in fear throughout this world.[^cite-55]

The believer should fear God as one about to enter a blazing fire, and should hope in God as one given glad tidings of the Gardens of Paradise. Indeed, God rewards or punishes a person according to his intention. Those suspended between fear and hope are as restless as a person whose heart is held in the grip of a bird's talons!

Surely God takes away from you His favors and blessings in order to test your patience and gratitude. At such times, hope for His mercy in a way that contains no disobedience to Him. Fear Him in a way that your fear does not destroy hope in His mercy.

A believer does not truly believe in God until he stands between hope and fear, nor can he truly stand between hope and fear unless he transforms that fear and hope into action. For a person seeks what he hopes for and flees from what he fears.[^cite-56]

My sincere counsel to you is this: place your hope in none but God, and fear nothing except your own sins. Remember God before you undertake anything! If the deed leads you to obedience to God, then perform it. If it leads you to disobedience to God, then abandon it.

A small action performed with sincerity surpasses countless insincere deeds. You were once asked: "What is the likeness of one who performs insincere deeds?" He replied: "Like one who feeds people, cares for his neighbors, serves guests, yet when the door to the forbidden is opened before him, he enters therein."[^cite-57]

## The True Nature of This World

When a person turns away from this world, all that is in it becomes worthless and contemptible to him. He neither grieves over what he does not obtain nor rejoices in what he receives. But the worldly person grieves at every loss and rupture of relationship, and rejoices at every gain. Thus does a person's happiness and sorrow become a mirror of his inner reality.[^cite-58]

The world may be compared to the salt water of the sea: the thirsty person drinks from it, and his thirst only increases until the water brings him to death. Or it is like a cocoon: the more one wraps oneself within it, the more difficult it becomes to escape, until one dies still trapped within. Or it is like a serpent: gentle to the touch, yet filled with deadly venom. I swear by God! For me, this world is like a dead carcass. When hunger compels me to take from it, I take only what necessity demands.[^cite-59]

A man came to Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq from the prison of Kūfa and asked him to pray for his release. The Imam asked him: "Tell me, how did you find the prison in Kūfa?" He replied: "May God exalt you! It is a place of severe distress and terrible conditions, and its inmates are in the worst of states." The Imam said: "You are still in such a worst prison and you seek release from it! Do you not know that this world is a prison for the believer?" Those whose hearts desire this world live in three states: constant anxiety, unfulfilled hopes, and never-ending desires.[^cite-60]

Do not think that the oppressor alone commits wrong! Every oppressor has three companions: the first is the one who commits the oppression, the second is the one who aids the oppressor, and the third is the one who witnesses the oppression and remains silent in pleasure at it. All three shall be fed together on the Day of Judgment.[^cite-61]

Two people came to Imam al-Ṣādiq with complaints of oppression against one another. Before hearing their grievances, he said: "Surely, one who succeeds in his oppression gains no good whatsoever. The oppressed, by virtue of what has been done to him, takes a loan from the oppressor—for the oppressor shall have to answer to the oppressed on the Day of Judgment. And this debt of the oppressed is far greater than whatever worldly benefit the oppressor obtains through his usurpation. The oppressor has no right to complain tomorrow when he himself is wronged, for the children of Adam reap only what they sow in the field of their lives. No one obtains sweetness from bitterness, nor bitterness from sweetness!" Hearing this, the two departed in silence.[^cite-62]

## The Counsel of Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq

When someone asked Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq for counsel, he said: "If God is the Provider, why are you anxious? Why do you become restless and hasty in seeking sustenance when it is already determined? If the reward is from God, why do you neglect your deeds? Why do you become stingy when God is the Bestower of blessings? Why do you disobey God when the Fire of Hell is true? Why do you laugh carelessly when death is certain? Why do you deceive people when it is right to appear before God? When Satan is an open enemy, why are you heedless of him? Why do you play false when it is right to walk upon the Bridge? Why are you grieved when everything has been ordained by God? When life in this world is but temporary, why do you trust in it?

You live counting your days, and death comes upon you suddenly. He who sows good reaps joy; he who sows evil reaps sorrow and regret. The sluggish do not escape their fate, nor do the hasty attain anything before its appointed time, save what God has ordained for them. Whoever receives something good, God has given it. Whoever is saved from evil, God has saved him. Whoever fears God, God shall protect him. Whoever is grateful to Him, God shall increase His blessings. Whoever lends to Him, God shall repay him fully.[^cite-63]

If this world ensnares you in its trap and its creation delights you, remember your death! Remember the solitude of your grave! Remember when your eyes shall roll upon your cheeks, when the parts of your body shall separate one from another in the grave, when the stones shall compress the flesh of your body, when your body shall decay and you shall depart from this world! All these shall drive you to action and turn your heart away from this world![^cite-64]

Surely, the one who is content is rich, and the one who always gazes upon the wealth of others dies poor. Whoever is not satisfied with God's gift doubts God's power. He who considers his own fault small always deems others' faults great. Whoever reveals another's fault, God shall reveal his fault. Whoever draws the sword of disobedience to God shall perish by it. He who digs a pit for his brother shall fall into it himself. He who has been humiliated by the ignorant, having befriended the world, has become honored. Whoever entered an evil place was blamed.[^cite-65]

Always speak the truth, even if the truth goes against you. Beware of the slanderer who sows discord in hearts. When you go in search of nobility, seek it in its proper places. Surely nobility has a place, a root in that place, and branches upon those roots. Good fruit is obtained only from a strong branch.[^cite-66]

Remember God frequently, by day and night, for God loves such servants. No good can ever be attained without obedience to God. Beware of the sins you are commanded to avoid, whether outward or inward. Do not follow your desires and conjectures, or you shall go astray. Keep performing the prayer, especially the Midday Prayer.[^cite-67]

Beware of seeking worldly greatness and glory, for glory and honor are the due of God alone. Whoever strives to be a partner in such greatness, God shall break him and humiliate him before all on the Day of Resurrection. Do not envy one another, for envy is the root of disbelief![^cite-68]

Know that Islam is the name of submission (*islām*, surrender), and submission is Islam. Thus, he who surrenders his desires and his soul to the will of God is a true Muslim. Beware! Satan deceives you and sets snares for you. He deceives you by forbidding you from doing good to your brother, and he sets snares by commanding you to sleep at the time of the Dawn Prayer.[^cite-69]

Do not be saddened by the insults of the ignorant, nor rejoice in their praise—otherwise your deeds shall make you arrogant and self-conceited. Do not ruin your property in attempting to improve others' property, for this alone shall be your capital at death. Be content with what God has given you. Be not ungrateful in times of prosperity, nor impatient in times of poverty. Familiarize yourself with the beginning and end of any matter before undertaking it. Keep silence whether you are ignorant or learned. Surely silence is a sword against the world and a shield for the ignorant.[^cite-70]

Guard your gaze, for it sows the seed of desire in hearts. Blessed is he whose vision is not in his eyes, but in his heart! Meet those who abandon you, give to those who withhold from you, show mercy to those who oppress you, act justly toward those who deal unjustly with you, and forgive those who offend you. Do you not see that the sun shines equally in every sacred place, and rain benefits both the good and the evil alike?[^cite-71]

He who slept on a full stomach whilst his neighbor slept hungry has not believed in God and the Hereafter. If anyone aids a believer in distress, God shall aid him on the Day when there is no refuge except in Him. Whoever fulfills one of his brother's needs, God shall fulfill many of his needs. Whoever clothes a believer, God shall clothe him with garments of Paradise on the Day of Resurrection. Whoever feeds a believer, God shall feed him from Paradise. And whoever gives water to a believing brother, God shall give him water from the Pool of Kausar.[^cite-72]

Whoever finds fault with his brother, God shall find fault with him on the Day of Resurrection and expose his faults to all. The lowest thing that makes a believer disbelieve is that he remembers something about his brother solely to humiliate him later. Such a person shall have no share in the Hereafter. Whoever pleases the heart of his believing brother pleases the heart of the Ahl al-Bayt, and whoever pleases the heart of the Ahl al-Bayt (upon whom be peace) pleases the Messenger of God (upon whom be peace), and whoever pleases the Messenger of God (upon whom be peace) pleases God.[^cite-73]

Beware of suspicion, for it diminishes your deeds. Avoid contention, for it disgraces you. Do not quarrel, for it shall turn you from God. Those who came before you learned silence whilst you learn speech. Blessed is he who keeps silent instead of cursing and bears every evil in the way of God. These are the true believers. By God! If any of you gave in charity gold equal to the weight of this earth, and then envied his brother, he would be burned by it on the Day of Resurrection.[^cite-74]

If you desire your brother's sincere love, do not mock him, do not argue with him, do not display your superiority to him, and do not quarrel with him. Do not tell your friend your secrets, so that tomorrow he does not become your enemy and harm you. The eloquence of the tongue consists not in sharpening the tongue and speaking abundantly, but in creating meaning and presenting proofs of God's Oneness. The man who does not guard his tongue in anger and does not treat his friend well is not of us![^cite-75]

Know that there is no piety greater than forgiving your believing brother and refraining from his backbiting. Establish love through mutual gift-giving. When someone gives you a gift, know that God has given you a gift through that person's hand, and he is worthy of praise and gratitude. Yet do not forget to thank the giver of the gift. Do not befriend sinners, lest you become a partner in their sins.[^cite-76]

If God grants you a blessing and you desire it to remain, then praise God with great praise and glorification. For God says in His Book: "If you are grateful, I shall give you much more." If you find hardship in your sustenance, then ask God's forgiveness abundantly, for God says in His Book: "Ask forgiveness from Me, and I shall forgive you."[^cite-77]

Do not eat what you dislike, for such food brings foolishness. Do not eat until you are hungry. When you eat, eat only what is lawful (*halāl*), and say "*Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm*" before eating. Remember! A person does not fill his stomach with the worst of things. But if necessity compels it, fill it with one-third food, one-third water, and one-third air.[^cite-78]

If anyone accuses you, say to him: "May God forgive me if you speak truth, and if you lie, I ask God to forgive you." Whoever threatens you with evil words, give him good counsel and care for him.[^cite-79]

Ask the scholar what you do not know, but do not ask questions to confuse or test him. Do not add your opinion to the religion, and do not make your neck a bridge for others to cross. Every perfection lies in understanding the faith and patience in adversity.[^cite-80]

A sign of patience is that you content yourself with sitting in the lowest place in gatherings, greet those you meet, do not argue about truth in any matter, and do not desire the praise of others for your piety.[^cite-81]

When your brother is absent, remember him in the best of words—words you would wish him to speak of you in your absence. If you intend a good deed, hasten to perform it. If you intend an evil deed, refrain from it.[^cite-82]

He who worships without knowledge is like one who travels in pursuit of a mirage. The faster he moves toward it, the farther he recedes from his goal.[^cite-83]

## The Primary and Secondary Causes of All Obligations

God has established two causes (*ʿill*) for all obligations, the combination of which renders an obligation truly binding. Of these two causes, one is prior and universal, while the other is subsequent and particular. That is, the first and primary cause is itself the cause of the second cause; the fulfillment of the duty becomes necessary only when the second cause comes into being.[^cite-46]

Consider the prayer of Zuhr (*salat al-zuhr*). The day itself is the first cause of this prayer, for it is not performed at night. Yet Zuhr is not obligatory at every moment of daylight; rather, it becomes obligatory only when the sun has passed its zenith. The passage of the sun is thus the second cause—particular and reserved for the performance of this specific prayer. This pattern obtains for all other obligatory prayers.[^cite-47]

Similarly, in the matter of alms-tax (*zakat*), the possession of wealth constitutes the first cause. Yet zakat does not become obligatory merely upon acquiring wealth; rather, one full lunar year must pass—the second cause—after which the obligation becomes due.[^cite-48]

So too with fasting (*sawm*). The crescent moon marking the beginning of Ramadan is the first cause. But fasting does not become obligatory at that moment alone; it becomes obligatory at dawn, which is the second cause of the obligation.

Likewise, the ability to undertake the journey is the first and most general condition for pilgrimage (*hajj*). Yet the pilgrimage does not become obligatory upon the mere acquisition of means; there exists a specific time and place—the second cause—which alone renders the obligation due.

Similarly, the cause of struggle in the path of God (*jihad*) arises from rebellion against the Imam of the time (may peace be upon him); yet jihad does not become obligatory upon rebellion alone, but only when the command of the Imam is issued.[^cite-49]

In striking contrast to all these obligations stands *wilayat* (allegiance to the Imam).[^cite-50] This obligation possesses no cause that comes into being or passes away. It is obligatory in all circumstances, from birth until death, and for this reason it does not depend upon the faculties of action but upon the heart—which every human being possesses, and from which the very possibility of human life flows forth. The heart is that faculty in which falsehood cannot take root. Even when a person stands under the sword and must speak lies to preserve his life, it is the tongue that utters the falsehood, not the heart! No one save oneself possesses dominion over the heart. This is why all actions derive their efficacy solely from *wilayat*, for *wilayat* resides in the heart, and over the heart each human being exercises complete mastery.[^cite-51]
