## Definition of the Shi'a

⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین حضرت علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ The Commander of the Faithful declared: "Indeed, our Shi'a—those who follow us—are those who believe in Allah and His Messenger, who keep God's commandments, turn away from sins, and do what We command them. Our Shi'a are those who observe the heavens, consulting the sun, moon, and stars to determine the times of prayer. Our Shi'a are those whose lips grow parched and whose bellies cave inward through fasting. Their faces bear the marks of asceticism. He is not of our Shi'a who seizes the rights of others, who oppresses people, or who wrongfully possesses what belongs to another."[^cite-1]

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Ja'far al-Ṣādiq (the Truthful), addressing the people, declared: "I counsel you all to fear Allah and obey His commands, to refrain from sins, and to render faithfully what Allah has entrusted to you. I counsel you to show kindness to those in your company, and I counsel you to embrace our silent devotions. When you follow the commandments that We have enjoined upon you, when you forbid one another from what Allah has forbidden, when you deal with people in truth and justice, when you render trusts with equity, when you enjoin good deeds and forbid evil—then people, witnessing your deeds, will say: 'These are surely the followers of such and such Imam!' May Allah have mercy upon that Imam, for how excellently he has instructed his companions and taught them noble character. Then shall they know of Our grace, and turn their hearts toward Our perfection."[^cite-2]

Whoever neglects even the slightest duty to Allah shall not receive His mercy, nor shall he be worthy of the intercession of Muḥammad ⟪ar:مصطفی صلعم⟫ (the Chosen One) on the Day of Resurrection. Therefore, listen to us and do all that God has ordained for you. Do not disobey God or us through opposition to what we say to you. By Allah, there is no god but Allah.

Our Shi'a are from us, and all others shall go to Hell. God is worshipped through us. Whoever disobeys us has disobeyed God; whoever obeys us has obeyed God. Our obedience is God's command to His creation. It is such that God accepts the deeds of a servant only through us, and He shows mercy to no one except through us. We are the Gate of God and His proof; we are the trustees of His creation, the custodians of His mysteries, the keepers of His knowledge and wisdom. There is no share in Paradise for he who does not render us our due from his wealth.

⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین حضرت علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ It was narrated that the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) said: "Whoever Allah tests by putting piety in the heart of a believer shall surely love us with sincere heart. And he with whom Allah is displeased shall surely harbor hatred and enmity toward us in his heart." Therefore, let those who love us love us with perfect sincerity—like pure gold in which there is not the slightest trace of adulteration. And he who hates us shall persist in hatred. We are noble and of noble lineage; our children are the children of the prophets, and I am the master of all the saints. I am of the army of Allah and His Messenger, and all others belong to the army of Satan. Whoever doubts us, turns away from us, and joins our enemies is not of us. If any of you wishes to know who loves us and who hates us, let him first examine his own heart. If he finds love for any of our enemies within it, then know that Allah, His Messenger, and the angels Gabriel and Michael are his enemies, and Allah is the enemy of the disbelievers.[^cite-3]

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ It is narrated that the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) said to some of his Shi'a: "O Shi'a, listen and understand our will, and abide by the covenant We take from Our lovers. Speak truth and fulfill your oaths. Help one another with wealth and love one another with all your hearts. Distribute charity to those in need, and hold fast to unity and consensus. Do not treat anyone with deceit, forgery, or betrayal. Do not doubt in your hearts after conviction, and do not retreat cowardly after taking a step. Let none of you show his back to his beloved, nor lust for another. Let your deeds be for Allah alone, and let your faith and purpose be only toward your Prophet.

Among our Shi'a are the saints and lovers of Allah and His Messenger—those who speak truth, who when they make a promise fulfill it, who when entrusted with something are patient, who when asked to pay their dues render them, and who when commanded act upon it. Our Shi'a are those from whose ears knowledge does not escape. Our Shi'a are those who do not praise those who blame us or hate us, and who do not associate with those who diminish our virtues. When our Shi'a meet a believer, they respect him; when they meet an ignorant person, they turn away from him.

Our Shi'a are those who do not bark like dogs barking in winter, nor are they greedy like crows. If our Shi'a ask for something from someone, they ask only from one of their own brothers, even if they die of hunger. Our Shi'a are those who speak to us and forsake even their friends for love of us; they bring the distant near to us and drive away the near."

A man asked him: "O son of the Prophet, where shall one find such a Shi'a?"

He replied: "We shall meet them scattered across the earth. They shall be very poor and needy, yet their eyes shall be cool with contentment. If seen, they shall not be recognized; if they disappear, they shall not be found. If they fall sick, they shall not be visited. If they seek marriage, none shall marry them. If they come to a gathering, they shall be driven away. And when the ignorant speak to them with ignorance, they ⟪ar:السلام⟫ say, 'Peace be upon you.'"

The man then asked: "O son of the Prophet, what shall become of those who profess to be Shi'a with their tongues, while their hearts oppose the Shi'a?"

He replied: "An era shall come upon them that will destroy them and kill them with hatred and dissension. Beware! By the One who aided us with His angels, God shall strike them down with His own hands.

A person who separates himself from the worldly people, from his parents and children, who loves, advises, and cooperates with his brothers for the sake of God—even if he be an Abyssinian or a Christian—such a believer shall not be raised from his grave on the Day of Resurrection except as white as snow, washed with the water of Paradise. He shall receive lasting blessings and shall have the company of angels, prophets, and messengers. There is no greater honor in the sight of God than a servant who has been exiled for the sake of God until he meets his Lord in exile. Our Shi'a are the warners of the Day of Judgment on earth, a lamp for those who seek, a sign and light, a guide for those who obey Allah. Those who come to them find peace; those who love them are treated with pleasure and kindness. Our Shi'a are generous, forgiving, and merciful."

The scholar among our Shi'a controls his tongue, is pleased with obedience to Allah and His Messenger, and hides his plans against our enemies in his heart. When he goes out in the morning, he is aware of all their faults, yet he does not reveal to them what is in his heart. He beholds their wickedness with his eyes, hears their evil with his ears, and curses them with his tongue. Those who hate them are his friends, and those who love them are his enemies. Such a person is commanded to overshadow the sky, and as for his worldly attribute, he finds whatever he asks of Allah.

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ It is narrated that the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) said to his Shi'a: "You are obliged to be ascetic and pious. Speak truth, pay trusts, and abide in the way upon which you are. For when the soul of one of you reaches his throat, he shall rejoice. If you live, you shall see that which cools your eyes. If you die, by Allah, you shall be met with the finest words. By God, you are upon the religion of Allah and the religion of my fathers—meaning not only ⟪ar:محمدؑ (الباقر) بن علی⟫ Muhammad al-Bāqir, son of ʿAlī, and ⟪ar:علیؑ (زین العابدین) بن الحسین⟫ ʿAlī Zayn al-ʿĀbidīn, son of al-Ḥusayn, but also ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم و اسماعیل اور اسحاق و یعقوب علیہم السلام⟫ the blessed Abraham, Ishmael, Isaac, and Jacob (upon them be peace). They all share the same religion. Fear God and aid us with piety and devotion. By God, prayer, fasting, pilgrimage, and alms-tax shall be accepted only from you, and God shall forgive you. But know that our Shi'a are those who follow us, not those who oppose us. When we are afraid, they too are afraid; when we live in peace, they too live in tranquility. These are our Shi'a. When Iblīs (Satan) went among other people, they obeyed him; when he came to our Shi'a, they refused to obey him. Therefore, Iblīs incited the people against our Shi'a, and that is why our Shi'a suffer affliction from them."[^cite-4]

### Shi'a Like the Sun

The sun has proven through millions of years of rotation that there is no wavering in my orbit. My rising each day and my setting each evening are certain. It has proven through its consistency and action that it never breaks its promise.

Just so does the example of a true believer manifest itself: through the consistency of his past life and deeds, a judgment is rendered about him even before his action—that if such a circumstance arise, this believing person will surely act in such a way.

## Characteristics of the God-Fearing Believer

It has been narrated that ⟪ar:مولانا علی علیہ السلام⟫ ⟪ar:حمام ابن شریح⟫ once a devout companion named Hammām ibn Sharīḥ said to ⟪ar:أمیر المومنین⟫ the Commander of the Faithful: "Describe to me the characteristics of a pious person in such a way that I might see them with my own eyes." ⟪ar:أمیر المومنین⟫ The Commander of the Faithful replied: "O Hammām, fear Allah and do righteous deeds, for ⟪ar-quote:إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ مَعَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّقَوا۟ وَّٱلَّذِينَ هُم مُّحْسِنُونَ⟫ Allah is with those who fear Him and those who do good." Hammām, unsatisfied with this response, pressed for greater detail. Mawlānā ʿAlī (the Master ʿAlī) was thus compelled to unfold a fuller description:[^cite-5]

All praise belongs to Allah, Creator of all creation. Peace and blessings be upon Muḥammad ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ و سلم⟫ (the Chosen One), the Messenger of Allah and His faithful servant. Glory be to Allah, Who created all creatures in such manner that He has no need of their obedience and is protected from their sins. The sins of sinners harm Him not, nor does the obedience of the obedient profit Him. He has apportioned the sustenance of each, and assigned to each his place in this world.

Behold, the pious and the God-fearing occupy a special place in His creation. Their discourse is measured, their garment moderate, their manner courteous. They avert their eyes from what Allah has forbidden, and incline their ears toward beneficial knowledge. They endure the days of hardship as they endure the days of ease. Had not Allah appointed them a term of life, their very souls would not remain in their bodies for even a moment—such is their fear of His reward and His punishment. The greatness of Allah is so engraved upon their hearts that all worldly things have become contemptible in their eyes. Paradise appears to them as though they gaze upon it with their own eyes and taste its blessings. Hell appears to them as though they see it with their own eyes and feel the agony of its torment.

Their hearts are softened, and they are safeguarded from every evil. Their bodies grow lean, their needs diminish, their souls grow pure. They have earned lasting rest for themselves by enduring fleeting suffering in return—a profitable bargain that Allah has made easy for them. The world pursued them, yet they did not pursue the world. The world confined them, yet they freed their souls by paying the price of ransom.

They spend their nights standing in recitation of the Qur'ān, soothing their hearts with it and healing their spiritual ailments. When a verse concerning the blessings of Paradise comes before them, their souls turn toward it with great longing, as if Paradise stood before them. When a verse concerning the torments of Hell comes before them, they incline their ears as if they hear the sounds of Hell and the painful screams of the tormented. They bend their backs in bowing and bow their foreheads in prostration, pressing their palms, knees, and feet to the ground for their salvation. They spend their days in patience, knowledge, piety, and reverence. The fear of God has made their bodies thin as an arrow. Should one see them, one might think them sick, though they are not. People call them mad and insane when they perceive their condition, for in truth, the remembrance of the Hereafter has made them as one possessed.

They are not satisfied with their own meager deeds, and their brilliant accomplishments are despised in their own eyes. They forever blame themselves and live in fear of their deeds. When people praise them, they say: "I know myself better than others, and Allah knows me better than I do. O Allah, do not hold me accountable according to what others say of me, but make me better than they imagine, and forgive me my sins which only You know and they do not."

You shall see in them humility in their religion, yet gentleness in their nature. They are perfect in faith, eager for knowledge, moderate in the acquisition of wealth, zealous in worship, grateful even in hunger, diligent even in suffering, desirous of lawful things, happy with guidance, and hating greed. They live in constant fear of their deeds. By night they are anxious to give thanks to Allah; by day they are mindful of remembrance. Their nights pass in fear, their mornings in joy—fear lest they forget the remembrance of Allah by night, joy that they are recipients of Allah's bounty and blessing. **If their soul desires a righteous deed, they do not allow it to act upon its caprice.** They do not contemplate mortal things, but derive coolness of eye from immortal actions. **They transform their knowledge into patience, and their words into deeds.**[^cite-6]

You shall see that their hopes are brief, their slips are few. Their hearts are full of awe, their souls at peace, their food simple, their religion safe, their desires dead, and their anger suppressed. Good alone is expected from them, and evil is never feared. When they are among those who forget Allah, they are among those who remember Him. They forgive those who wrong them and give to those who deprive them. Those who treat them ill, they treat with goodness.

Rude words are far from them. Their discourse is gentle, their vices absent, their virtues ever present. Good precedes them, and evil has turned away from them. **They are dignified in adversity, patient in hardship, and grateful in days of ease.** They do not transgress against those they dislike, nor do they sin for those they love. They accept truth before evidence is presented against them. They do not betray their trust, nor do they forget what they have been made to remember. They do not call others by ugly names and do not wound their neighbors. They do not delight in others' suffering. They enter not into falsehood and do not depart from truth.

When they are silent, their silence brings no harm. When they laugh, their voices are not heard. If anyone wrongs them, they forbear until Allah takes vengeance on their behalf. **Their own souls suffer on account of them, while others find comfort from them.** **They make their souls suffer for the sake of the Hereafter, and they protect people from their own harm.** Their withdrawal from people is for piety and purity, and their proximity to them is with compassion and kindness. They do not distance themselves from people out of arrogance or pride, nor do they draw near them through deceit or treachery.

⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ Upon hearing ⟪ar:حمام⟫ this sermon, Hammām was so seized by awe that his soul departed from his body. At that moment ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ the Commander of the Faithful said: "By God, this is what I feared! This is how powerful an authentic counsel affects the intellect!"

## Attributes of the Believer

Once, when ⟪ar:امیر المومنین مولانا علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ the Commander of the Faithful was delivering a sermon, a man who was devout and a jurist asked him: ⟪ar:یا امیر المومنین!⟫ "O Commander of the Faithful, describe to me a believer as if I were looking upon him!"

Allah (the Most High) said: "A believer is a wise and discerning person. His face is cheerful, his heart cordial ⟪ar:حزین⟫ yet sorrowful, and his breast is spacious. He holds every mortal thing in contempt, remembers God, and is patient and grateful. **He grieves for the Hereafter and is content in his poverty.** His knowledge is vast, his patience great, his mercy abundant. He keeps aloof from hatred and does not rush into matters. His heart is troubled by nothing, nor does he shrink from anything. He does not oppress others, nor does he oppress himself. **His soul is harder than stone when facing affliction.** His striving for livelihood is as smooth as honey. He does not become such a greedy person as to seize the rights of others. He is neither harsh nor impatient. He is neither contemplative nor preoccupied with worldly matters. When he grows angry, he acts with justice. When asked for something, he is gracious. He does not act in anger, does not oppress ⟪ar:حتک⟫ anyone, and is a true lover. He is faithful to promises and fulfills his covenants. He is first to reach out to people and help them. He lives his life in obscurity. He does not speak in vain and is pleased with Allah the Almighty. He opposes his own desires and is kind to those younger than himself. He does not occupy his mind with irrelevant things. He maintains relationships with friends without burdening them. He spends without extravagance. He does not deceive anyone nor does he betray. He does not follow anything that exposes another's fault. He does not oppress anyone and is the guardian of the people and strives for them. He is the helper of the weak and the champion of the afflicted. He neither conceals anyone's secrets nor reveals them. He faces great suffering but never complains with his tongue. When he sees someone's goodness, he mentions it; when he sees someone's evil, he hides it. He conceals people's faults and keeps an invisible watch. He accepts people's excuses and forgives their mistakes. **When he learns of something good, he does not refrain from it and does not stop correcting evil.** He is trustworthy and pious. His inner self is pure and people are satisfied with him. He engages in the remembrance of the Hereafter and trusts in none but God. He lives the life of a traveler in this world. **He loves solitude and longs for deliverance in the Hereafter. He loves for the sake of Allah and strives for the pleasure of Allah. He does not seek revenge for himself but leaves such matters to Allah.** **He does not befriend any enemy of God.** He seeks the company of the poor and destitute. He is the protector of the people, the helper of the truth-seekers, the supporter of kinfolk, the father of orphans, the husband of widows, and the guardian of the afflicted. In every calamity, people turn to him for aid. He trusts in Allah in every difficulty. He is generous and cheerful. He is steadfast, calm in anger, ⟪ar:مُتَبَسِّم⟫ smiling, ⟪ar:دقیق النظر⟫ keen in perception, and cautious in his religion. He does not love miserliness. If people are stingy in giving him his due, he is patient. He avoids bad things. He is rich because of his contentment. His modesty prevails over his desire, and his reverence does not allow the spirit of jealousy to arise.

## The Reality of Love

Allah has created man in such a way that he loves only himself and does not love any other truly. Even when he appears to love another person, it is only because that ⟪ar:'⟫ person has a close relationship with him ⟪ar:'⟫—for example ⟪ar:'⟫ his ⟪ar:'⟫ children, ⟪ar:'⟫ ⟪ar:'⟫ his parents, his friends, ⟪ar:'⟫ ⟪ar:'⟫ his family, and so forth. Yet even in love for others, his ego always remains at the center. He is forever the axis of his circle, and whoever enters his orbit is loved according to their distance from that center.

For this reason, every human being possesses self-regard (taḫallī), in which resides great wisdom from Allah. He is not sincere toward anyone but himself. Every person is bound by the pole of his own self and engaged in its service. Man's soul is the center of his existence, and he continues to revolve around his temperaments and desires throughout his life. The self commands him to ⟪ar:کبر⟫ pride, ⟪ar:حسد⟫ envy, and ⟪ar:حرس⟫ greed—to do evil and transgression. Man follows and does all that lies in his power, submitting to every command of the self, falling into the trap of false desires and worthless feelings. He remains imprisoned in this bondage throughout his life until death knocks upon his door. At that moment, the wages of false praise and self-glorification that he accumulated in the slavery of his own soul prove of no avail to him, and he deserves Hell for having ruined his life.

### Love for Others

Then, when this same person falls in love with another human being, he makes his beloved the center of his existence and begins to vacate the center of his self, to orbit instead around the axis of his beloved. He begins to accept the will of his beloved in the face of every demand, whether small or great, legitimate or unjustified. His sole purpose becomes to gain the agreement and acceptance of his lover.

Yet even in this state, a person's selfishness persists, for through the acceptance of his beloved, he seeks the satisfaction of his own soul. The only difference is that the center of his orbit has shifted, and all his efforts now flow away from his own self for the sake of his beloved's self.

In other words, Allah has endowed man with both the capacity for self-worship and the capacity for self-abnegation. To circle around one's own center is selfishness; to subvert oneself in order to gain another's pleasure is love.

### Love for Allah and the Messenger (Peace Be Upon Him)

Allah declares in the Qur'ān:

> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا خَلَقْتُ ٱلْجِنَّ وَٱلْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ ‎⟫

> And I did not create the jinn and mankind except to worship Me.

In this verse, Allah the Almighty employs the word ⟪ar:'عبد'⟫ (ʿabd, slave), which signifies man's created nature for submission (taslīm). So long as this recognition is directed toward his own self or toward any other human being like himself, he can never progress. That is why Allah says: "I have created the jinn and mankind for My ⟪ar:عَبدِیَت⟫ (slavery to Me alone)." In other words, if a person wishes to progress from this mortal world, he must abandon the slavery of his own soul and enter the slavery of his Allah—the great summons (al-daʿwa)—with heartfelt joy. Let him abandon the narrow circle of his self and become a traveler of the vast heaven. Let him submit to the commands of Allah and seek His pleasure alone. ⟪ar:'اسلام'⟫ Islam teaches this very principle. And though this task is most difficult, it is possible to attain the highest ranks. On the ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ basis of this principle of acceptance, Islam introduced the practice of kissing the foot, in which a person removes the crown of arrogance—⟪ar:'أنا'⟫ the "I"—from his head and bows it at the feet of an elder.

For this ⟪ar:عبدیَت⟫ reason, when the Prophet made people testify to his prophethood, he first expressed himself through ⟪ar:'عبدیت'⟫ the word of servanthood, and only then announced himself as ⟪ar:'رسالت'⟫ messenger, saying: ⟪ar:اَشھَدُ اَنَّ مُحَمَّدً عبدُہُ وَ رَسُولہُ⟫ "I testify that Muhammad is His servant and His messenger." Allah was so pleased with this humility that when He mentioned the Prophet's Night Journey, He employed the word ⟪ar:رسول⟫ (messenger) rather than ⟪ar:عبد⟫ (servant), for the messenger of Allah commands greater respect and trust in the sight of Allah than ⟪ar:عبدیَت⟫ servanthood alone, yet ⟪ar:رسالت⟫ messengership cannot be divorced from it.[^cite-7]

The Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) said: "Speak truth to the people, even if it displeases them." Yet a person, by reason of his selfishness, does not wish to appear ill in the eyes of others. It has ever been the practice of the prophets and messengers of truth to speak truth among the people. They humble themselves before the people in order to seek the pleasure of Allah, and for this reason their rank and reward are established with Allah. Allah declares (in the interpretation of the meaning):

In this eloquent verse, Allah has established the standard of His love through His Messenger. Allah addressed the people through His Messenger and said: "Follow me if you wish to seek the love of Allah." In this verse, Allah says: "If you obey the Messenger of Allah by transgressing your own desires and inclinations, Allah will love you and forgive you all your sins." The love of the Messenger of Allah is such a magnet, endowed with such blessings, that Allah forgives the sins of His servants for the sake of their love for His Messenger and their following of him.[^cite-8]

## The Law of Attraction (Qānūn al-Alf)

Allah the Almighty has founded His religion upon love, which ⟪ar-quote:قانون الف⟫ is called the Law of Attraction (qānūn al-alf). Love is the act of accepting another's will, desire, and intention for the sake of that other's will.[^cite-9]

If we examine the structure of this universe, we shall see this same law everywhere. The mineral must grind itself to dust before the plant can absorb it. For the mineral to become vegetation, it must submit to the plant. Similarly, the plant must surrender itself to the animal, and bow before it, so that the animal may consume it and build its own body from it. The animal, in turn, must offer its neck beneath the knife of the slaughterer, defenseless and willing, so that it may be elevated ⟪ar:قامت الفی⟫ in the service of mankind. In the same way, unless a person accepts his will, desires, actions, and inclinations at the command of Allah, Allah does not accept him and does not make him worthy of Paradise. ## Love of God and Friendship with Him

In this verse, the Creator addresses the believers with words of both warning and promise: "O you who believe, whoever of you turns away from his religion, let him turn away! God will bring in your place a people whom Allah loves, and that group loves Him. They will be humble towards the believers, mighty against the disbelievers. They will strive in the way of Allah and will not fear the reproach of any reproacher. This is the bounty of Allah, He bestows it on whom He wills, and Allah is All-Embracing, All-Knowing."[^cite-17]

The verse condemns one group whilst praising another. During the time of the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.a.w.), certain individuals threatened the Holy Prophet (PBUH), saying: "O Messenger of Allah! If you do not do this according to our advice, we will renounce Islam and join the Jews and Christians." To them Allah replies: "If you apostate, we do not care. We will bring in your place a sincere believer who loves Allah and whom Allah loves."

What can be said of the perfection of humanity and the high station of this community, which the created world should praise with such glory! The praise of people is one thing; the praise of the Creator is another entirely. This is a complete eulogy—a *manqabat* (spiritual virtue)—revealed upon the tongue of divine nature. The first attribute of this *manqabat* is that it describes those who love God and whom God loves. Many claim to love God, yet only those sincere in this claim are accounted among those whom God loves.[^cite-18] One-sided love is incomplete; perfect love is reciprocal—they love Allah and Allah loves them.

Love—the love of God! God's great love is not a trivial matter. It has been a source of wonder for the great saints and elders throughout the ages. Human power lacks the strength to bear such love. When even the slightest worldly affection gains momentum, a person loses his mental balance, his thoughts drawn away from all else save the imagination of his beloved. What then is the station of divine love, wherein the soul encounters the very presence of the Divine?

### Jesus and the Elder Worshippers

Allama al-Zubayri writes in his celebrated *Hayat al-Haywan* that Hazrat Isa (a.s.) once passed towards a mountain. There, in a place of worship, he saw a single worshipper engaged in prayer. Years of devotion had bent his back, and hardship had withered his body until it became thin as thorns. Hazrat Isa asked, "O worshipper! How long have you been engaged in worship?" He replied, "O Messenger of Allah, seventy years have passed, but there remains one need unfulfilled by Allah." The elder then made a request: "O you who are a special servant of Allah, intercede for me before Allah!" Hazrat Isa asked, "What is your need?" He answered, "I desire that Allah grant me a particle of His pure love."

Hazrat Isa raised his hands in prayer, and the prayer of the Spirit of Allah was accepted. When Jesus passed through that place again days later, he found the place of worship had collapsed, the ground beneath it torn asunder. The worshipper stood in the pit with mouth open, eyes fixed upon the sky.[^cite-19]

*The eyes of the closed ones have found you in the veil, / Away from the limits, but close to the heart.*

Jesus greeted the saint, but received no reply. Astonished, he cried out: "O Creator! What is the state of this servant of Yours?" The message came: "O Jesus! This worshipper asked for a particle of Our love. We gave him one of the seventy thousand parts of Our love, knowing he could not bear it. How are you amazed at the Prophet? Had We given him even slightly more of Our love, you would know what would have become of him."

### The Love of Hazrat Shu'aib

In like manner, Hazrat Shu'aib, who dwelt with Hazrat Musa, became so lost in the remembrance of God that his sight was taken. Allah restored to him the light of his eyes. If you weep in fear of Hell, We have forbidden the Fire to you!" Hazrat Shu'aib said: "O Lord! I weep neither for Paradise nor in fear of Hell. I weep only in love for You!"

The voice of Allah came saying: "Yes, O Shu'aib, you are indeed My beloved!" Upon this, Shu'aib was so overcome with joy that his soul departed from his body.[^cite-20]

### Types of Worship

Therefore, the Master of the Universe, Hazrat Ali ibn Abi Talib, says in his sermons: "O Allah, I do not worship You from desire for Paradise nor from fear of Hell, but I have found You worthy of worship; therefore do I worship You." The Lord declares that such worship is the worship of the free. Those who worship from passion for Paradise engage in commercial worship; those who tremble in fear of Hell are slaves in mentality. The first group desires the price of worship; the second acts in fear like servants.

It is held that the Prophet is one whom Allah loves and who is loved by Allah—one whom Allah calls His friend and who takes Allah as his friend. The joy of love exists only when there is love on both sides! True love of God belongs to those who are true followers of the Messenger. Allah Himself commands the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) to say: "Say, if you love God, then follow me, and God will love you and forgive you your sins."[^cite-21] When one becomes God's, God becomes one's own. At that time, Ali ibn Abi Talib was the only one who obeyed with absolute constancy—whether travelling or at home, in peace or in war, in health or illness—never uttering a word against the command of the Holy Prophet, nor delaying in obedience. Of his obedience, Maulana Ali himself says in his sermons: "I have obeyed the Messenger of Allah as a young camel follows its mother."

### The Three Signs of Love

Scholars state that there are three signs of love: thinness of body, pallor of complexion, and change of temperament, lest the beloved be angered. Yet these signs apply only to those ignorant of their station with their beloved. They do not apply to those fully aware of their condition and status in the presence of the beloved.

Someone once asked Amir al-Mu'minin: "O Lord, why is it that the faces of all worshippers and lovers turn pale and their bodies grow thin, yet your face is ever cheerful and your body the strongest?" How well he answered: "These people do not know what their station is! Since the veils have been removed from before my eyes—*Kashf al-Ghatama al-zaddat*—and I beheld with the eyes of my heart that my beloved is pleased with me, my fear was immediately removed. When Allah Himself gave me the glad tidings that I would succeed, my worries ceased to matter."

Moreover, Amir al-Mu'minin was always seen cheerful and at ease in closeness to the Divine. Yet when the moment of obedience came, the color of his blessed face would fade. His body trembled with the fear of God. Once, departing for Hajj with the intention to perform the pilgrimage, he began to tremble, his face grew pale, and he fell. When people asked him, he said: "I was afraid that when I uttered the *Talbiyah* (the pilgrimage invocation), God would say to me, 'What will become of you then?'"

### Three Types of Love

Scholars distinguish three types of love and affection. The first is that of those who voice complaint when they receive pain and suffering from their beloved in love. This is a flawed and incomplete form of love. The second type is love wherein the lover suffers the pain of the beloved in silence, never allowing complaint to reach the tongue. This is a kind of higher love:

*Grief is not disturbed by the world, / Noise does not disturb oysters in the river. / I am silent even in the flames of Nimrod, / I am a believer, not a spill.*

The third type is that wherein, when encountering difficulties in attaining the beloved's pleasure, the lover begins to feel pleasure and enjoyment in the difficulty itself. The passion reaches such a point that the heart desires to increase the suffering. This is the highest and most sublime destination of love. The great lovers are unable to attain this station; it is reserved only for Muhammad and the family of Muhammad. Time bears witness: the greater the burden of suffering upon them, the brighter the light of their faces. The more difficulties Imam Hussein faced on the battlefield of Karbala, the straighter his back became:

*I will spend my life in your sorrow— / What is this two-day life?*

### The Test of True Love

The best opportunity to test love is the opportunity of war. In comfortable situations and gatherings, even enemies become friends. Therefore, there has always gathered around the prophets of all ages a community of sincere people who loved their Prophet and Imam with all their hearts and souls. Yet the companions and friends of Imam Hussein were rare images of true love and strange euphemisms of loyalty. It is better to say that these were the firmly steadfast—those true in their intentions, faithful in their covenant, pious and righteous. Remarkably, Imam Husayn (a) did not have such a community of pure believers in the time of any other Prophet. Hypocrites and believers were always mixed together—even in the time of the Lord of the Universe, such a gathering did not occur, nor in the time of Hazrat Ali! That is why hypocrites have wrought great harm to the Ummah under the guise of friendship. Yet the gathering with Imam Hussein was perfected. There was no trace of a hypocrite. All were believers! What then did Imam Hussein accomplish? He not only purified his own community but also purified the ranks of the enemy. Among that group of hypocrites, there were two or three believers like Hurr, whom the Imam drew to himself. Thus remained only pure people on both sides—on one side, pure believers; on the other, pure hypocrites. Truth stood against truth; falsehood against falsehood.

### The Love of Habib ibn Mazahir

His father, Ali Mazahir, was a pious believer who often attended the Prophet (s) with his young son, Habib ibn Mazahir. It is said that Habib harbored great affection for Imam Hussein. Wherever the Imam went, Habib would follow. One day, the Prophet (s) saw Habib following Imam Hussein and picking up the dust from beneath his feet, rubbing it upon his face and eyes. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) said: "O Ali Mazahir, bring your son to me!" He seated Habib upon his arm, embraced him, and tears began to flow from his eyes. Mazahir asked: "O Messenger of Allah, what is the reason?" He said: "Mazahir, one day this child will be sacrificed for my Hussein."[^cite-22]

It is narrated that Mazahir once invited the Messenger of Allah to his house for dinner, saying: "O Messenger of Allah, bring Hasan and Husain with you." Young Habib stood at the rooftop, watching the path of Imam Hussein, when suddenly his foot slipped and he fell. He died. Mazahir was deeply troubled, fearing that if commotion arose in the house at that moment, he would be deprived of the invitation of the Prophet and lose the good fortune of his nearness. The love of the Messenger prevailed over the love of kinship! He brought Habib's body into the house and firmly insisted that no one should wail until the Messenger of Allah and the princes had eaten.

The Messenger of Allah arrived. When Imam Hussein did not find Habib, he asked: "Where is my Habib?" Mazahir said that Habib had gone on an errand and would return shortly; he urged the Imam not to grieve but to eat. Imam Hussein replied: "I will not eat without Habib." The demonstrations could not be contained. The whole matter was reported to the Prophet. Upon hearing of Habib's death, Hussein began to cry. He told the Prophet that he prayed to God that his beloved would be brought back to life, otherwise he would not eat. The Holy Prophet (s.a.w.a.) came to the body of Habib and prayed to the Lord, saying: "Bring back to life this child, who is the devotee of my Hussein." Habib came back to life by the glory of divine nature and the will of the Beloved God. He opened his eyes, started crying with his head at Hussein's feet, and Imam Hussein embraced him.

*Desires destroy your desires in the pleasure of a friend— / Then let me see why the lover does not want it.*

### Battle of Karbala

One day, Habib ibn Mazahir was buying *khuzab* (a fragrant plant) from a shop in Kufa when Muslim ibn Ausja passed by. Habib said: "Brother Muslim, I see that armies are gathering in Kufa and weapons being collected. Do you know the reason?" Muslim replied: "O Habib, do you not know? The people of Kufa are preparing to kill Imam Hussein." Upon hearing this, Habib immediately threw the *khuzab* from his hand and said: "By God! Now this beard will be stained with the blood of my head in the aid of Hussein!" At once he prepared for his departure to Karbala. His wife said: "O Habib, when you come before the son of the Prophet, submit to me. After kissing the hands and feet of the Imam, say: 'O Lord, if jihad had not been forbidden to women, I myself would have come to your aid.'"

Habib left his wife and said to his slave: "Saddle the horse. In such and such a place I will follow you. Beware, reveal my intention to no one!" It is said that Habib was delayed in reaching him. The slave, growing frightened, addressed the horse: "O horse! If your master does not come, I will ride you myself and go to the aid of Hussein." As the slave was about to do so, Habib arrived. Upon hearing his words, Habib burst into tears and said: "O son of the Messenger! My parents are devoted to you! What a terrible time has come upon you, that even the slaves are readying themselves to help you!"

On the battlefield, Habib gave his utmost for his friendship with the Imam. It is said that his devotion to the aid of Hussein was so profound that he sat with his arms bound throughout the night, burning with the passion of martyrdom. Traditions tell that when Muslim ibn Ausja fell from his horse, mortally wounded, he cried out: "O son of the Messenger of Allah!" When Imam Husayn reached the battlefield, Habib was at his side. When the Imam found Muslim dead, he placed his head upon his arm and said: "O Muslim, may Allah reward you." At that moment, Habib said to Muslim ibn Ausja: "O Muslim! Had I known I would live after you, I would have asked you for a will. But I am following you myself. Yet since it is the custom of the time to ask the deceased for his will—if you have one, tell me." Hearing this, Muslim opened his eyes, pointed toward the Imam, and said: "O Habib, take care of my Imam." How loyal were the helpers of Hussein and the brave men of Karbala! With life upon their lips, it was still Imam Hussein who occupied their hearts.

When the battle began, Habib would come to the Imam repeatedly, asking permission to go to war. The Imam would say to him: "O Habib, your broken heart is bound to Mir Daras." With permission granted, Habib came to the battlefield like a young man, recited verses of devotion, then, burning with zeal, broke upon the hypocrite army like a tiger upon a herd of deer. Many hypocrites fell like carrots beneath his sword. Husayn ibn Amin (cursed be his memory) struck him with a spear upon his chest—a blow he could not endure. He fell from his horse and cried out: "O son of the Messenger of Allah!"

*Every desire has gone away from the heart. / Come now, now you're alone.*

The Imam came to the battlefield with Hazrat Abbas and Hazrat Ali Akbar. He wept and said: "O my dear friend, you leave us alone in the clutches of our enemies." At that moment, Habib opened his eyes and looked upon Imam Hussein. He said: "O Imam, your grandfather stands before me, giving me glad tidings and saying: 'O Habib, come quickly! The *Huras* and the handmaidens of Paradise await you. Come, hasten, so that We may give you water from the waters of Kausar!'" Saying this, Habib ibn Mazahir died in the lap of Imam Hussein. The Imam recited only *Inna Lillahi wa Inna Ilaya Rajoon* (Surely we belong to Allah and to Him we return) and said: "May Allah have mercy on the Beloved!" He used to complete the entire Qur'an in a single night![^cite-23]

By Allah, how fortunate was Habib! He not only had the good fortune of the Prophet (s) in his childhood, but also in his last moments passed away upon the son of the Prophet. What an honor—to be not only the dear friend of Imam Hussein throughout his life, but after his martyrdom to be buried at the feet of Imam Hussein, proving thereby his friendship and love!

Habib ibn Mazahir and all the martyrs of Karbala set the standard of pure and true love upon the battlefield. Not for a moment did anyone think of how to save his own life from the enemies. Every calamity that came brought a gleam to their eyes and maturity to their souls. The angels of heaven, witnessing their passion and the fervor of their martyrdom, confessed before the Divine Throne: "O Lord, You have spoken the truth: 'We know what you do not know.' How did we know that the descendants of Adam possess such steel in their essence, that the rotations of earth and sky together cannot weaken them or remove them from their unwavering will!"

*Men's avoidance of life: / If there is no defeat, what else is defeat? / We are thin, we are the weavers of iron! / Humans are not mountains on the battlefield! / To those whom the Creator has given, they give wealth. / What is wealth and house in the way of God? / It is only prostration and devotion. / They do not give their heads. / Take lessons from Islam— / Learn the lesson of passing through this station. / Take the lesson of the emergence of bubbles from the river. / If you want to live, take the lesson of dying. / Ask the secret-keepers of the Qur'an! / Ask Fatima's son the lesson of death!*

And at the end of all praise, *Alhamdulillah, Rabb al-Aalamin*—All praise belongs to Allah, the Lord of all the worlds.

## The Importance of Wilayat

Allah Almighty has created all of His creation in pairs: the visible, tangible things manifest without, and the intelligible, inner realities hidden within. As He Himself says: The Lord has joined every outward manifestation with its inner reality, making them companions—such as the world and the Hereafter, body and soul. The advantage of their union lies in this: one is dynamic (the soul), the other static (the body).

| Dynamic | Static |
| --- | --- |
| Speaker | Guardian |

He gave man superiority over all creatures because of his capacity for speech and the utterance of meaningful sentences. Logic itself comprises at least two letters: one dynamic (the verb) and one static (the noun). This is proof that Allah has created all existence in pairs and pairs. That is, such pairings are found everywhere and in all things; this balance appears in every religious and worldly matter, external and internal, sensible and intelligible. Allah says:

The Prophet (peace be upon him) also founded his religion upon this same divine nature and adapted the method of worship and guidance in this divine style, which is the best style. The intelligent person who reflects will come to know that Islam and the Shariah of the Prophet rest upon the very principles that govern the creation of the heavens, earth, and all the universe—namely, that all things are created in pairs. After contemplation, his faith becomes firm, and he becomes a true believer, a confirmer of the Messenger. Wherever he looks and whatever command he considers, he finds it in accordance with the creation of the universe. At that moment, he is fully convinced of this great miracle of the Messenger—the Qur'an. The Holy Prophet (PBUH) himself says in his Hadith that Allah has founded His religion in accordance with His creation, so that creation itself becomes proof of His religion and confirmation of His Oneness and Unity with the faith.[^cite-24]

### The Six Days of Allah

Allah says in His Book: "He created the heavens and the earth in six days, and on the seventh day He ascended the Throne in order to protect and sustain the strong order of the universe. In six days He established all creation, so that creation would not be disturbed, scattered, and destroyed."

Allah is exalted and elevated above the notion that His words are vain or wrong. He is exalted and elevated above the claim that He lacks the power to create the universe of heavens and earth in an instant, or even in shorter time. He Himself says in the Holy Qur'an: "Is not He Who created the heavens and the earth able to create the like of them? Yes, indeed! And He is the All-Knowing Creator." And in another place He said: "Truly, the creation of the heavens and the earth is greater than the creation of mankind, but most of mankind do not know."

These sayings of Allah rest upon eloquent wisdom. When Allah possesses the power to create something by willing it, the question arises: why did it require six days to create the universe? This indicates that the verse contains other inner meanings—meanings which certainly hide great expediency and wisdom.

### Argument on Wilayat al-Wasi from Du'a'im al-Islam

What use are Allah's command and warning if He says: "Enter the house by the gate"? Every common person knows that the door of a house is built for this very purpose. If this has nothing to do with interpretation and wisdom, then preaching such common sense would be completely meaningless! Indeed, when the Ummah disobeyed its Prophet and turned from the obedience of the *Wali* (guardian), it went astray and wandered from the path of guidance. Everyone began to speak according to their own opinions and interpret the Qur'an according to their own conjectures. As a result, the entire Ummah—save those upon whom Allah had mercy—fell into discord like the Children of Israel. They too had been commanded to enter the gate, bowing themselves, and to speak with humility, saying "Hatta" (forgiveness); but they changed what the Messenger had said and made a mockery of it.[^cite-26]

Here too, the Ummah abandoned the gate of knowledge and disobeyed the Prophet. The *Wilayat al-Wasi* (guardianship of the successor) is the seventh duty and is the noblest, highest in rank. The six duties precede it; upon the *Wilayat* depends the establishment and survival of all six.

The proof of this lies in the statement of the wise and the people of insight: man is the noblest of all creatures in this world and the essence of the powers of the whole world. In the same way, a person who practices the six pillars but does not commit himself to the *Wasi'yah* and *Wilayat* of Amir al-Mu'minin will not attain the station of perfection.

### Argument on Wilayat from the Stages of Fetal Development

If we look at another dimension, we find that a person must pass through seven states in order to be called human; otherwise, he is not. Allah says in His interpretation of the meaning: "We created you from dust, then from a sperm-drop, then from a clot, then from a lump of flesh, formed and unformed, that We might show you Our power; and We cause to remain in the wombs what We will for an appointed term, then We bring you forth as infants..."

That is, after passing through six stages and conditions, when the soul—the final creation—is breathed into man, he is called human. Had he been brought forth without the final creation, he would be called dead.

That is why, as soon as the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) had fulfilled his final obligation, Allah said: "This day have I perfected your religion for you and completed My favor upon you, and have chosen for you Islam as your way."[^cite-27]

Just as the invalidity of the final creation renders all six stages of human development void, making all the biological births useless, so too the absence of *Wilayat* will not benefit the six pillars of practice. All of them will be useless, indeed they will revert to the religion of ignorance.[^cite-28]

## Knowledge of the Imam of One's Time

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "If a person dies ignorant of the knowledge of the Imam of his time, his death will be the death of ignorance." There are two types of ignorance: the first is *jahiliyyah* (pre-Islamic ignorance); the other is *jahl* (ignorance within Islam). Proof of this is found in the Holy Qur'an itself. Allah says: "And among His signs is the night and the day... but most of mankind know not" or, as it is interpreted, "most people are heedless."

When ignorance proved to be the first, ignorance was also the last. This is confirmed by the statement of Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (a.s.): there are two types of ignorance—*jahiliyyah* is disbelief; the other is *jahl*.

| Ignorance | The Final Ignorance |
| --- | --- |
| Jahiliyyah (Pre-Islamic Ignorance) | Jahl (Ignorance of Islam) |

*Jahiliyyah* is that ignorance which pervaded the entire world before the Prophet's mission—the ignorance of polytheism and idolatry. *Jahl* is that a person does not know the Imam of his time. This is because, if he does not acquire knowledge of his Imam of the time, he will inevitably become ignorant of the sciences of his religion, will be misguided, and will drown in the storm of innovations and misdeeds.[^cite-29]

## Sincere Worship of Allah

Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (a.s.) said: "A person's life is a mirror of his inner self, and his inner self is hidden in his life." Some people clothe hypocrisy in the garments of good manners, attempting to conceal the corruption of their hearts. Yet no matter how they strive, such people quickly reveal their inner thoughts through words and deeds. A slip of the tongue reveals the secrets of the heart; the garment of hypocrisy is torn asunder.

Conversely, the pious and learned always conceal their inner purity and devotion, fearing praise and fame. Yet despite their effort, their achievements become known among the people, and the light of their soul shines forth like the sun in all directions.

Hypocrisy (*riya'*, ostentation) is the attempt to establish a place in the human heart through good deeds, appearance, or any outward means of action. Every such hypocrite actually acknowledges more than one god. Allah will say to them on the Day of Resurrection: "Go and seek your reward from those whom you sought to please in this world."

Therefore, a true believer must perform all his deeds for the sake of Allah alone and should not associate anyone else in His service and worship. The sign of one who associates others in the worship of Allah is that he performs good deeds in secret only to show others and to praise himself.

Whoever does good deeds in secret from this world—there will not be a day on which Allah does not reward him with good. Whoever commits a sin in secret—there will not be a day on which Allah does not reward him with punishment.

Why do those among you who commit sins do so in secret? Your concealment of the sin is itself a strong argument against the act, and man is indeed a witness against himself! The outward action of one whose inner self is pure is always clear and manifest. The outward action of one whose inner self is impure is always hidden.

Whoever sincerely performs a small deed for the sake of Allah, Allah will reward him with much greater than that deed. Whoever performs much work to show people, wearing out his body through night vigil, Allah will nullify all his deeds and bring him low in the sight of Him to whom all deeds are directed.

## Life Between Fear and Hope

Surely Allah is the Owner of all power and mercy, anger and joy. His anger is in accordance with His mercy; His punishments are equal to His immense blessings. Since His mercy is boundless, every sinner hopes that he may be forgiven. Since He is mighty, every sinner fears His punishment which he may deserve. Therefore, a believer should always live his life between hope and fear—fear, for he knows not for what sin he will be seized and his name inscribed among sinners deserving punishment; hope, that his weak good deeds may be accepted and his name inscribed among those upon whom Allah will shower His blessings. Therefore, a believer should constantly strive for good deeds and protect himself from every evil act.

Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq said: "Fear Allah as if you see Him. If you cannot see Him, at least convince yourself that He is watching you. If you believe He cannot see you, you are a disbeliever! And if you believe He sees you yet you sin, you doubt His power and authority over you!"

Indeed, a true believer knows and believes that Allah sees him and hears every word—good and evil—that he utters. He restrains his soul from every desire and his body from the pleasure of every evil deed. Surely he who knows Allah fears Him, and he who fears Him turns away from this world. Only he who knows Allah fears Him. The greater a person's knowledge, the greater his fear.[^cite-30]

The believer always lives in two states of fear: the first is fear of the sin already committed and written down, for he knows not what Allah will do with that sin. The second is fear for the time remaining of his life, for he knows not what other sins he will commit in the future. Such a believer's morning is spent in fear, and it is this fear that keeps his soul upon the right path.

Whoever fears Allah, Allah makes him feared in the hearts of all things in this world. Whoever does not fear Allah, Allah makes him fear all things in this world. The Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) loved nothing more than that one should hunger and live his life in fear in this world.

The believer should fear Allah as one about to enter a blazing fire, and should hope from Allah as one given glad tidings of the Gardens of Paradise. Indeed, Allah rewards or punishes man according to his intention. Those caught between fear and hope are as restless as one whose heart is in the grasp of a bird!

Surely Allah takes away from you His favors and loans that He may test your patience and gratitude. At that time, hope for His mercy in such a way that you do not disobey Him, and fear Him in such a way that your fear does not destroy the hope of His mercy.

A believer does not truly believe in Allah until he lives between hope and fear, and he cannot move between hope and fear until he translates that fear and hope into action. For man seeks what he hopes for and flees from what he fears.

My counsel to you is this: set your hopes on none but Allah, and fear none except your sins. Remember Allah before you do anything! If it leads you to obedience to Allah, do it; if it leads you to disobedience to Allah, abandon it.

A little action done with sincerity is better than countless insincere deeds. You were asked: "What is the example of one who does dishonest deeds?" He said: "A person who feeds people, cares for his neighbors, serves his guests, but when the door of the forbidden is opened before him, he enters it."

## The Reality of This World

When a person turns away from this world, everything in it becomes useless and vile to him. Then he never regrets what he does not obtain or misses, nor is he ever happy with what he acquires. But a person attached to the world grieves at the loss of anything or the breakdown of a relationship, and rejoices at the acquisition of anything. Therefore, a person's happiness and sorrow are a mirror of his inner self.

The example of this world is like the salt water of the sea: the thirsty drinks from it, his thirst increases until the water kills him. Or it is like a silkworm: the more it wraps itself in silk, the harder it becomes for him to escape until he dies in the same predicament. Or it is like a serpent, very gentle to the touch but full of deadly venom. I swear by Allah! For me, this world is like a dead animal—when hunger compels me to eat from it, I eat a little, out of compulsion alone.

A man came to Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (a.s.) from the prison of Kufa and asked him to pray for his release. The Imam asked him: "Tell me, how did you find the prison in Kufa?" He said: "May Allah exalt you, it is distressing and terrible, and its inhabitants are in the worst condition." The Imam said: "You are still in such a worst prison and you wish to remain in it. Do you not know that this world is a prison for the believer?"[^cite-31] Those whose hearts desire this world are in three states: constant anxiety, unfulfilled hopes, never-ending desires.

Do not think that the oppressor is the only one who does wrong! Every oppressor has three companions: the first is the one who commits the injustice; the second is the one who helps the oppressor; the third is the one who rejoices at the sight of oppression. All three will be punished together.

Two people came to Imam al-Sadiq (a.s.) with complaints of oppression against each other. Before hearing their grievances, he said: "Surely, the one who succeeds in his injustice gains no good." The oppressed, because of the oppression inflicted upon him, takes a loan from the oppressor (for the oppressor will have to answer to the oppressed on the Day of Judgment). This debt of the oppressed far exceeds the worldly benefit the oppressor gained by snatching it from the oppressed. The oppressor has no right to complain when he is wronged tomorrow, for the children of Adam reap what they sow in the field of their lives. No one obtains sweetness from bitterness, nor bitterness from sweetness! Hearing this, both departed in silence![^cite-32]

## The Counsel of Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq

When someone asked Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (a.s.) for advice, he said: "If Allah is the Provider, why are you worried? Why are you anxious and hasty to obtain sustenance when it is fixed? If the reward is from Allah, why do you diminish your actions? Why are you stingy when Allah is the Giver of blessings? Why do you disobey Allah when the Fire of Hell is the truth? Why do you laugh happily when death is certain? Why do you deceive people when it is right to appear before Allah? When Satan is an open enemy, why are you heedless of him? Why do you pretend when it is right to walk the bridge? Why are you sad when everything is ordained by Allah? When life in this world is temporary, why do you trust it?

You live in the number of days, and death comes suddenly upon you. He who sows good reaps joy; he who sows evil reaps regret and sorrow. Those who are sluggish do not escape their fate, and those who are hasty attain nothing ahead of time except what Allah has ordained for them. Whoever has received something good, Allah has given it. Whoever is saved from evil, Allah has saved him. Whoever fears Allah, Allah will protect him from all calamities. Whoever is grateful to Him, Allah will increase His blessings. Whoever lends to Him, Allah will repay him in full.

If this world ensnares you in its trap and its creation delights you, remember your death! Remember the loneliness of your grave! Remember when your eyes will fall upon your cheeks, the disintegration of your body parts when they are separated from one another in the grave, the pressure that will compress your flesh, the decay of your body and your departure from this world! All these things will lead you to action and will stop you from taking joy in this world!

Surely, the one who is content is rich, and the one who always looks upon the wealth of others dies poor. Whoever is not satisfied with the gift of Allah doubts the power of Allah. He who considers his own mistake small always considers the mistake of others to be great. Whoever reveals the fault of another, Allah will reveal his fault. Whoever draws the sword of disobedience to Allah will perish by it. He who digs a pit for his brother will fall into it himself. One humiliated by the ignorant, one befriended by the world, becomes honorable. Whoever enters a bad place is blamed.

Always tell the truth, even if the truth is against you. Beware of the slanderer who sows hatred in his heart. When you go out in search of generosity, look for it in its places. Surely, there is a place of generosity, a root in that place, branches upon those branches, and good fruit is not obtained except from a strong branch.

Remember Allah frequently during the day and the night, for Allah loves such servants. Without obedience to Allah, no good can ever be attained. Beware of sins that are commanded to be avoided, whether outward or inward. Do not follow your desires and conjectures or you will go astray. Keep praying, especially the middle prayer (Asr).

Beware of attaining worldly greatness and glory, for glory is rewarded only to Allah. Whoever strives to be a partner in this greatness, Allah will break him and make him humble before everyone on the Day of Resurrection. Do not envy one another, for the root of disbelief is envy!

Know that Islam is the name of submission, and submission is Islam. So the one who submits his desires and soul to the will of Allah is a true Muslim. Beware! Satan deceives you and sets a trap for you. He deceives you so that he forbids you from doing good to your brother and sets a trap for you by commanding you to sleep at the time of the Fajr prayer.

Do not be saddened by the taunts of the ignorant, nor rejoice in their praise, lest your deeds make you arrogant and vain. Do not destroy your own property in an attempt to improve the property of others—it will be your only capital at death. Be content with what Allah has given you. Do not be ungrateful in days of prosperity, nor impatient in days of poverty. Familiarize yourself with the beginning and end of any action before undertaking it. Always keep silence whether you are ignorant or learned. Surely silence is a sword for the world and a shield for the ignorant.

Guard your gaze, for it sows the seed of desire in hearts. Successful is the one whose vision is not in his eyes but in his heart! Meet those who leave you, give to those who take from you, show mercy to those who oppress you, do justice to those who do injustice to you, and forgive those who offend you. Do you not see that the sun shines alike in every holy place, and rain benefits both the good and the evil equally?

He who spent the night with a full stomach while his neighbor slept hungry did not believe in Allah and the Hereafter. If anyone helps a believer in distress, Allah will help him on the day when there is no refuge except Him. Whoever fulfills one of his brother's needs, Allah will fulfill many of his needs. Whoever clothes a believer, Allah will clothe him with the garment of Paradise on the Day of Resurrection. Whoever feeds a believer, Allah will feed him from Paradise; whoever gives water to a believing brother, Allah will give him water from the Pool of Kausar.

Whoever finds faults in his brother, Allah will find faults in his record of deeds on the Day of Resurrection and will expose them to all. The lowest thing that makes a believer a disbeliever is that he remembers something about his brother merely to humiliate him later. Such a person will have no share in the Hereafter. Whoever pleases the heart of his believing brother pleases the heart of the Ahl al-Bayt, and whoever pleases the heart of the Ahl al-Bayt (peace be upon him) pleases the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him), and whoever pleases the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) pleases Allah.

Beware of suspicion, for it will bring down your deeds. Avoid arguments, for they will disgrace you. Do not dispute, for they will turn you away from Allah. Those who came before you learned silence while you were learning. Blessed is the one who keeps silence instead of cursing and endures every evil in the way of Allah. These are the people of the believers in reality. By Allah! If any of you gives alms equal to the weight of this earth in gold, and then envies his brother, he will be burned with it on the Day of Resurrection.

If you desire your brother's pure love, do not ridicule him, do not argue with him, do not display your greatness to him, and do not quarrel with him. Do not tell your friend your secrets, lest tomorrow he become your enemy and harm you. The eloquence of the tongue is not in sharpening the tongue and speaking much, but in creating meaning and presenting proofs of the Oneness of Allah. The man who does not guard his tongue in anger and does not treat his friend well is not of us!

Know that there is no piety greater than forgiving your believing brother and restraining yourself from his backbiting.

Establish love by giving each other gifts. When someone gives you a gift, be assured that Allah has given you a gift through that person's hand, and he deserves praise and thanksgiving. Yet do not fail to thank the giver of the gift. Do not make friends with sinners, lest they make you a partner in their sins.

If Allah bestows upon you a blessing and you desire it to last, then praise Allah with great glorification, for Allah says in His Book: "If you are grateful, I will give you much more."[^cite-33] If you find hardship in your sustenance, then ask Allah for forgiveness often, for Allah says in His Book: "Ask forgiveness from Me, and I will forgive you."

Do not eat what you dislike, for such food breeds foolishness. Do not eat until you are hungry. When you eat, eat only the lawful (*halal*) and say *Bismillah-your-Rahman ar-Rahim* before eating. Remember! A man does not fill his stomach with the worst thing! But since it is necessary, fill it with one-third food, one-third water, and one-third air.

If anyone accuses you, say to him: "May Allah forgive me if you are truthful, and I ask Allah to forgive you if you are lying." Whoever threatens you with an evil tongue, give him good counsel and take care of him.

Ask the scholar what you do not know, but do not ask questions to confuse or test him. Avoid adding your opinion to religion and do not make your neck a bridge for other people to cross. Every kind of perfection lies in understanding religion and patience in the face of adversity.

A sign of patience is this: be content to sit in the last place in a gathering, greet the person you meet, do not argue about the truth in any matter, and do not desire to be praised for your piety.

When your brother is absent, remember him in the best of words—words in which you wish him to remember you in your absence. If you intend to do a good deed, hasten to do it. If you intend to do something evil, refrain from doing it.

He who worships without knowledge is like one who goes out in pursuit of a mirage. The faster he moves towards it, the farther he gets from his destination.

## The Primary and Secondary Causes of All Obligations

God (Allāh) has established two causes for every obligatory act: a primary cause, which is general and foundational, and a secondary cause, which is particular and specific. The fulfillment of duty becomes necessary only when the secondary cause is present; the primary cause alone does not suffice to render the obligation binding.[^cite-34]

Consider the obligation of the Zuhr prayer. The primary cause is daylight itself—prayer is not performed at night. Yet daylight alone does not make the Zuhr prayer obligatory; rather, the sun must reach its zenith and begin its decline toward setting. This specific moment—the sun's decline—is the secondary cause, which renders the obligation to perform Zuhr prayer binding upon the believer. The same structure applies to all other prayers.

Similarly, in the matter of alms-tax (zakāt, the obligatory charity), the primary cause is the possession of wealth exceeding the minimum threshold (nisāb). Yet the obligation to pay zakāt does not arise at the moment wealth reaches this minimum; rather, a full lunar year must pass. The completion of this year is the secondary cause that makes zakāt incumbent. Again, the primary cause is insufficient without the secondary.[^cite-35]

The obligation to fast during Ramadan illustrates this distinction further. The primary cause is the appearance of the new moon marking the first day of Ramadan. Yet this appearance does not immediately bind the faster; only when dawn breaks does the obligation to abstain become binding. Dawn is thus the secondary cause of the fast.

For the pilgrimage to Mecca (Hajj), affordability and the physical ability to undertake the journey constitute the primary cause. Yet Hajj does not become obligatory upon the mere presence of means; rather, there are specific days and a specific sacred place designated for its performance. These particularities form the secondary cause that renders Hajj truly binding.

In the matter of struggle in the path of God (jihād), rebellion against the rightful authority represents a primary cause for defensive action. Yet jihād does not become obligatory upon rebellion alone; it becomes binding only when the Imām issues the command to engage in it.

In stark contrast to all these obligations stands *wilāyat* (spiritual guardianship, or devotion to the Imāms' authority)—an obligation that possesses no cause, whether primary or secondary. Wilāyat is binding in all circumstances, from birth until death, under every condition and in every state.[^cite-36] For this reason, it is not tied to the limbs or external faculties; rather, it resides in the heart (qalb), that inner seat of consciousness which every human being possesses and which is the very source of life. The heart admits of no falsehood; it cannot lie.

Even when a person stands under extreme duress—a sword held to the neck, life itself threatened—the tongue may utter lies to preserve the body's survival, yet the heart remains incapable of deception. No power external to the self can compel the heart to deny the truth it knows. This is why all actions derive their validity from wilāyat: because wilāyat belongs to the heart, over which every human being retains complete and inviolable sovereignty.
