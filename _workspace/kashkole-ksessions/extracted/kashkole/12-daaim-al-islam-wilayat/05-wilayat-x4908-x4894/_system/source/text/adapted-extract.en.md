## The Designation of ʿAlī by the Messenger of Allah

One day ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ stood in the mihrab of the Quba Mosque when the revelation came to him that his appointed time approached. The divine message signaled his imminent meeting with the Lord; Allah was eager to receive him. In that same period, he received the glad tidings of his conquest of ⟪ar:مکہ معظمہ⟫. When ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ informed the Community of believers of his impending death, they grew troubled. They said, "O Messenger of Allah! We fulfill all the obligations of the law when we see you and consult you directly. What will become of your Community when you depart?" He replied, "Whosoever among you obeys my command should obey the command of ʿAlī after me."[^cite-1]

After the conquest of Mecca, the Prophet broke all the idols kept in the Kaaba through ʿAlī. Having obtained the opportunity to perform the pilgrimage at last, he resolved to undertake his first and final Hajj in that year, in the tenth year of the Hijra. ⟪ar:'حجۃ الوداع'⟫ Since ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ himself was performing the pilgrimage, vast multitudes gathered that year. After completing the rites, he announced to the people, "I am departing for Medina by a different route from which I came." The people understood this as one might depart the mosque by another door than the entrance. Yet the Messenger possessed a wisdom altogether singular.

When he reached ⟪ar:'جحفا'⟫, a revelation came commanding him to proclaim the *wilāyat* (guardianship) of ʿAlī. He paused and continued. Gabriel came again with the same revelation, yet he paused once more. Only when he arrived at ⟪ar:جحفا⟫, fearing the crowd would disperse before his proclamation could take root, the revelation came a third time—this time with the most solemn rebuke from the Lord:

> ⟪quran 5:67⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ بَلِّغْ مَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِن رَّبِّكَ ۖ وَإِن لَّمْ تَفْعَلْ فَمَا بَلَّغْتَ رِسَالَتَهُۥ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ يَعْصِمُكَ مِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلْكَافِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *O Messenger (Muhammad PBUH)! Proclaim (the Message) which has been sent down to you from your Lord. And if you do not, then you have not conveyed His Message. Allah will protect you from mankind. Verily, Allah guides not the people who disbelieve.*

In this verse, Allah threatened that should the Prophet fail to proclaim what he had been commanded, all his labors in Prophethood would be rendered as naught—his entire mission undone. Yet in the same breath came the assurance: ⟪ar:وَٱللَّهُ يَعْصِمُكَ مِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ⟫ Allah would grant him ⟪ar:عصمت⟫ (protection and infallibility) against the people.

#### Did the Messenger Disobey the Command of Allah?

Some have supposed that the Prophet should have obeyed at the first revelation, and that his hesitation constituted disobedience. But consider: did not the Messenger himself teach the Community what pleases Allah and what displeases Him? Is it conceivable that he who instructed mankind in obedience would himself disobey?[^cite-2] The truth lies otherwise. The Prophet desired this stern verse to be revealed precisely so that the magnitude of ⟪ar:أمر ولایت⟫ (the matter of guardianship) concerning ʿAlī would be made manifest in its true weight. He sought the guarantee of divine protection. When the assurance came—⟪ar:وَٱللَّهُ يَعْصِمُكَ مِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ⟫—he did not hesitate an instant but immediately proclaimed the *wilāyat* of ʿAlī.

---

Upon receiving Allah's covenant of protection, ⟪ar:عصمت⟫, the Messenger halted at Ghadir Khumm. He commanded those with him: "Gather back those who have gone ahead; collect those who linger behind, until the whole multitude is assembled." He ordered all thorns and shrubs cleared from that place—a symbolic cleansing of all wickedness that would later oppose ʿAlī. It was a celebration long desired since the creation of the heavens. A pulpit was raised from camels' saddles. Ascending it, he delivered a sermon of such weight that even before any proclamation, he asked the assembled people: "Have I not brought you all the ordinances of Allah? Have I not instructed you in purification, prayer, alms, fasting, pilgrimage, and struggle in God's way? Have I concealed any command of the Lord from you? Have I not raised you from the darkness of ignorance to the light?" All answered, "Our parents are devoted to you, O Messenger of Allah! You made us human when we were as beasts."

Then he asked them thrice: "Do I not have authority over your souls more than you do over yourselves?" And thrice they replied: "Yes, O Messenger of Allah! You are greater than our own selves." Thereupon he raised Mawlānā ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib so high that the whiteness of his armpits became visible, and declared:[^cite-3]

> I bequeath to all who believe in me and confirm me the guardianship of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib. His guardianship is my guardianship; his command is my command; his covenant is my covenant. After me he shall be your guardian. For whomsoever I was master, ʿAlī is master. O Allah! Love the one who loves ʿAlī, be the enemy of the one who opposes ʿAlī, help those who help ʿAlī, humiliate those who humiliate ʿAlī, and turn the truth with ʿAlī wherever it turns.

After this proclamation, the Prophet and Mawlānā ʿAlī prayed two rakʿas together and sat in the tent. Then came ⟪ar:عمر⟫, ⟪ar:ابو بکر⟫, ⟪ar:زبیر⟫, ⟪ar:عثمان⟫, and ⟪ar:طلحا⟫, the first of them, saying: "Blessed are you, son of Abī Ṭālib! From this day you have become our master and the master of all believers!" All shook hands with the Messenger and ʿAlī and kissed their feet. The Prophet observed the condition of these hypocrites, knowing full well what they would do to ʿAlī in times to come. He then addressed the people: "Some among you harbor two hearts. I could point them out, but I prefer not to. Yet I tell you plainly: if you desire Paradise, remove bitterness toward ʿAlī from your hearts and love him. Otherwise you shall enter Hell."

After this, the Prophet fell gravely ill and wished to inscribe in writing all he had said concerning ʿAlī's leadership. He called for pen and ink, but ⟪ar:عمر⟫ objected, saying that the Messenger spoke in fever-delirium. Thus the Prophet did not write it down. ⟪ar:رحمت للعالمین⟫ passed from this world, and ʿAlī performed all the rites of his washing and burial, laying him in the sacred grave, while the rest of the Community busied themselves in choosing the next Caliph.

## The Concealment of ʿAlī's Outward Caliphate

Scarcely had ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ closed his eyes when the Muhājirūn and the Anṣār assembled at the cursed place of ⟪ar:بنو سقیفہ⟫ to choose the next Caliph. The matter became one of who held the strongest hand. ʿAmr, most powerful among them, declared, "We choose Abū Bakr as our Caliph." None dared oppose him, and thus Abū Bakr became the first. ʿUmar chose Abū Bakr on the condition that Abū Bakr would appoint ʿUmar his successor. In this way, after thirty years, ʿUmar became Caliph, and after him ʿUthmān. Throughout those three decades, the Caliphs inflicted grievous wrongs upon ʿAlī and the Ahl al-Bayt. The land of Mawlānā Fāṭima was usurped. ʿUmar struck her and broke down her door, causing the miscarriage of Muḥsin. Her house was set ablaze. A tyrant like ʿAlī was dragged through the marketplace by a rope tied to his hand. Yet ʿAlī and the companions of ʿAlī endured all these afflictions in silence, for Allah had commanded patience.

When ʿUthmān was killed and no one came forward to accept the Caliphate, the people were compelled to come to ʿAlī's door. There they found him sitting and mending his torn shoes, for which he bore the title ⟪ar:'قاصف النعل'⟫ (the one who repairs his sandal). He asked them, "What do you think is the worth of this worn shoe?" They replied, "O ʿAlī! What value has such a tattered shoe?" He said, "By Allah! In my reckoning, this torn shoe is worth far more than your Caliphate! But when the people pressed him greatly, he accepted the apparent Caliphate for the preservation of Islam. He made clear to the people: "Say that I am ⟪ar:'خلیفۃ اللہ'⟫, not that you are choosing me as Caliph!"

So estranged had the people become that when his Caliphate was announced in the mosque and he rose to address them, those around him restrained him, saying, "O Shaykh! Why do you stand? This is ⟪ar:حیدر کرار غیر فرار⟫, the mighty lion who does not flee! We have heard great tales of his deeds!" He replied, "O brothers, release me. I am ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib!" and proceeded straight to the Prophet's pulpit. There he delivered a sermon unparalleled in all of history. His opening words were:[^cite-4]

⟪ar:الحمد للہ رجعل الحق الی اھلہ! يا معاشر الناس !.. سلوني قبل أن تفقدوني ، ھذا سَفَط العلم ، ھذا لعاب رسول اللہ (ص) ، ھذا ما زقّني رسول اللہ زقّاً زقّاً ، سلوني فإنّ عندي علم الأولين والآخرين ، أما واللہ لو ثُنّيت لي وسادة فجلست عليھا ، لأفتيت أھل التوراة بتوراتھم حتی تنطق التوراة فتقول : صدق عليٌّ  ما كذب ، لقد أفتاكم بما أنزل اللہ فيّ ، وأفتيت أھل الإنجيل بإنجيلھم حتی ينطق الإنجيل فيقول : صدق عليٌّ  ما كذب ، لقد أفتاكم بما أنزل اللہ فيّ ، وأفتيت أھل القرآن بقرآنھم حتی ينطق القرآن فيقول صدق علي ّ ما كذب، لقد أفتاكم بما أنزل اللہ فيّ  ثم قال سلوني قبل أن تفقدوني ، فو الذي فلق الحبۃ وبرأ النسمۃ لو سألتموني عن أيۃ آيۃ في ليل أُنزلت أوفي نھار أُنزلت ، مكيّھا ومدنيھا ، سفريھا وحضريھا ، ناسخھا ومنسوخھا ، ومحكمھا ومتشابھا ، وتأويلھا وتنزيلھا لأخبرتكم⟫

Praise be to Allah, who has restored the truth to its rightful place! Here is the treasury of knowledge, here the very saliva of the Messenger of Allah, here what he gave me, measure upon measure. Ask me! For I possess the knowledge of the first and the last. By Allah, were I to recline upon a cushion, I would pronounce judgment for the People of the Torah by their own Torah, until the Torah itself would speak, saying, "ʿAlī spoke truth; he did not lie. He has answered you by what Allah revealed in our Torah." And were the People of the Gospel to come to me with their Gospel, I would answer them by their Gospel, until the Gospel itself would proclaim, "ʿAlī spoke truth; he did not lie. He has answered you by what Allah revealed in our Gospel." And were the People of the Qurʾān to come to me with their Qurʾān, I would answer them by their Qurʾān, until the Qurʾān itself would say, "ʿAlī spoke truth; he did not lie. He has answered you by what Allah revealed in our Qurʾān."

Ask me before you lose me. By Him who splits the seed and creates the soul, were you to ask me about any verse—whether it was sent down by night or by day, whether it was revealed in Mecca or in Medina, during travel or while settled, which verses abrogate and which are abrogated, which are manifest in meaning and which are ambiguous, what their interpretation is and what their revelation—I would tell you all!

#### Why Did ʿAlī Remain Silent for Thirty Years?

The answer lies in ʿAlī's own deliberation. Once two women came to him disputing over a child, each claiming him as hers. He commanded, "O Qambar! Take the sword and divide the child between them." Hearing this, the true mother cried out, "What do you do? Give him to the other woman, even if he is no longer mine. At least let him live!" Since Islam was that child, and its mother grieved for its safety, ʿAlī remained silent for thirty years. Had he not done so, Islam would have shattered into fragments. Otherwise, neither would Dhū al-Fiqār have dulled nor the strength of his arms, which had uprooted Khaybar, grown weak!

## The Guardianship and True Knowledge of the Five

One day al-Mufaḍḍal came into the presence of Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq. The Imām asked him: "O al-Mufaḍḍal! Do you recognize Muḥammad, ʿAlī, Fāṭima, Ḥasan, and Ḥusayn as they truly deserve to be recognized? Have you attained the truth of their knowledge?" Al-Mufaḍḍal replied, "O my master! What is their true knowledge?" The Imām said:

"O al-Mufaḍḍal! Whosoever possesses true knowledge of them dwells in the highest stations of faith."

Al-Mufaḍḍal said, "O my master! Make known to me their knowledge."

The Imām replied: "O al-Mufaḍḍal! Know that these five possess knowledge of all that Allah the Most High has created and brought into being from nothingness. They are the manifestations of Allah's power! They are the treasurers of the heavens and the earth, of mountains, deserts, and rivers. They know the number of stars and angels in the sky, the weight of the mountains, the measure of water in the rivers, the count of springs and streams. No leaf falls from a tree without their knowledge. They know every grain in the darkness of the earth. Nothing exists, dry or wet, that is not preserved in their knowledge—they know its likeness and its reality."[^cite-5]

Al-Mufaḍḍal said, "O my master! I understand, I confess, and I believe." The Imām said, "Yes, O al-Mufaḍḍal! You have spoken rightly. Blessed are you and all who hold such faith! May Allah bless you and those who believe as you do."

## The Divine Luminous Beings

Allah the Most High created His beloved Muḥammad Muṣṭafā from a light that emanated from His own majesty and grandeur. This was the very divine light that shone upon Mūsā ibn ʿImrān at Mount Sinai, a light so overwhelming that when Mūsā beheld it, he could not endure the vision. He cried out and fell senseless to the ground.

When Allah determined to create His beloved, He divided this light into two parts. From the first He created Muḥammad, and from the second ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib. No other being was created from this light save these two. Allah fashioned them both with His might and breathed into them His Spirit. He made them after His own image and made them His own. These two are Allah's witnesses over His creation, His successors among them, His eye that sees and His tongue that speaks. Allah entrusted His knowledge to them and gave them the essence of its exposition. He revealed to them His hidden secrets. One manifested the outer, the other the inner. Both sustained and supported one another, for neither could endure without the other.[^cite-6]

The outward form of both is human in appearance, yet their inward reality is bound to the Divine. Allah says:

> ⟪quran 6:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَوْ جَعَلْنَهُ مَلَكًۭا لَّجَعَلْنَهُ رَجُلًۭا وَلَلَبَسْنَا عَلَيْهِم مَّا يَلْبِسُونَ ‎⟫
> *And had We appointed him an angel, We indeed would have made him a man, and We would have certainly caused them confusion in a matter which they have already covered with confusion.*

They appeared among people in human form so that mankind could perceive them. Both are the successors of Allah and the guardians of the universe. Creation began with these two, and the cosmos and all destiny shall end with them.

Thus Mawlānā Fāṭima al-Zahrāʾ was created from the light of Muḥammad as he derived it from his own essence. And from the united light of ʿAlī and Fāṭima, Allah created the light of Ḥasan and Ḥusayn, as light is drawn from lamps. In this manner all these radiant beings were created from light transmitted from holy breast to pure womb, from one generation to the next. They are not born from impure water and defiled seed as are ordinary human beings, but from a light passing through immaculate vessels, for they are the chosen pearls.

These five have been singled out and chosen by Allah for Himself. He made them the custodians of His treasures of knowledge, His messengers to mankind. He made them His successors because He Himself cannot be seen or perceived, His condition cannot be known, His abode cannot be identified. The Qurʾān testifies:

> ⟪quran 21:23⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَا يُسْـَٔلُ عَمَّا يَفْعَلُ وَهُمْ يُسْـَٔلُونَ ‎⟫
> *He cannot be questioned as to what He does, while they will be questioned.*

For Allah cannot be questioned about His acts, yet His creatures will be questioned. Therefore He appointed them to speak for Him, to convey His message, to make clear His commands and prohibitions. Through them He revealed His power, displayed His signs and wonders. Through them He made Himself known to His servants, and through them obedience to His command was established. Had these beings not existed, there would be no knowledge of Allah and no understanding of how the Merciful One is worshipped. Allah says:

This statement establishes that there is no intercourse between Allah and creation save through these luminous intermediaries who stand as veils and channels of communion.

## The Reckoning with the Self

Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said: "Shall I tell you of the wisest and the most foolish of people?" The people replied, "Yes, O Messenger of Allah."

He said: "The wisest person is he who takes account of his own soul and labors for what comes after death. The most foolish is he who follows the desires of his soul and has vain hopes in Allah."

At that time a man asked Amīr al-Muʾminīn, "How does a person take account of his self?" He replied:

"When evening comes after the day has passed, turn your attention to yourself and address your soul saying: 'O soul! The days that have gone will not return. Allah will ask you, "What have you done in this day? Did you remember Allah and engage in His praise? Did you fulfill the rights of your believing brother? Did you ease his burden? In his absence, did you protect the honor of your family and children? Did you guard the sanctity of your heirs after your death? Did you refrain from backbiting a believer?" Remember and reckon all that you have done.

If you find that all your deeds were good, then praise Allah and glorify Him, thanking Him for enabling you to do good. If you find any fault or sin in your record, ask forgiveness from Allah and make a firm resolve never to commit such sins again. Recite blessings upon the Prophet and his family, and bear witness to the guardianship of Amīr al-Muʾminīn. Curse his enemies who usurped his rights and deprived him of his station. Purify yourself through contrition and hatred of sin."

When a believer does this, Allah says: "Because of your guardianship with My saints and your enmity toward My enemies, I shall not be harsh with you in reckoning your account on account of your sins."[^cite-7]

## The Guardianship of Zulaykhā

Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq narrates that Zulaykhā asked the king for permission to go to Yūsuf. The king said, "Are you not afraid to go to him after what you did to Joseph?" She replied, "I fear the One whom you fear!"

When Zulaykhā arrived, Yūsuf said, "What has happened to you? I see your countenance has changed." She said, "Praise be to Allah, who has made kings slaves through disobedience and slaves kings through obedience!"

Yūsuf asked, "What compelled you to do what you did to me?" She answered, "Your beauty and your excellence!"

Yūsuf said, "What would you do if you saw the Messenger whose name is Muḥammad, whom Allah will send in the last days? His beauty, his noble character, and his generosity far surpass mine!"

Zulaykhā said, "You speak the truth!" Yūsuf asked, "How do you know I speak truly?" She replied, "As soon as you mentioned his blessed name and described his qualities, love for him entered my heart."

The Lord said to Yūsuf: "What Zulaykhā has said is true! I love her for the love of My beloved that was born in her heart!" And at that moment He commanded Yūsuf to marry Zulaykhā.

## The Discourse of the Messenger and Abū Dharr

Abū Dharr al-Ghiffārī relates: One day I was sitting in the house of Umm Salamā, listening to the Messenger discourse, when ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib entered. At sight of him the Prophet's face radiated joy. He kissed ʿAlī upon his forehead and turned to me saying, "O Abū Dharr! Recognize the one who has come to us!"

Abū Dharr replied, "O Messenger of Allah! He is your brother and cousin, the husband of Fāṭima, the chief of the youths of Paradise, the father of Ḥasan and Ḥusayn."

The Messenger of Allah said: "O Abū Dharr! ʿAlī is a radiant and manifest Imam. He is the mighty banner of the Lord of the Universe and the great gate of Allah's mercy. Whosoever desires to reach Allah must enter through this gate!"[^cite-8]

"O Abū Dharr! He is the establisher of justice, the guardian of Allah's sanctity, the helper of Allah's religion, and Allah's supreme argument against His creation. Allah always maintains a proof among the nations concerning His creation because I sent a Messenger to every people.

"O Abū Dharr! Allah has appointed seventy thousand angels at each pillar of His Throne. Their occupation and praise consists in sending blessings upon ʿAlī and the Shīʿa of ʿAlī and cursing the enemies of ʿAlī.

"O Abū Dharr! Had it not been for ʿAlī, truth would not be distinguished from falsehood, the believer from the disbeliever. God would not be worshipped! For ʿAlī subdued the idolaters until they embraced Islam and worshipped Allah. Without him, there would be no concept of reward and punishment. Between Allah and creation stands no barrier save through them. They themselves are the veil. They themselves are the face of Allah."

The Messenger then explained this verse:

> ⟪quran 42:13⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ شَرَعَ لَكُم مِّنَ ٱلدِّينِ مَا وَصَّىٰ بِهِۦ نُوحًۭا وَٱلَّذِىٓ أَوْحَيْنَآ إِلَيْكَ وَمَا وَصَّيْنَا بِهِۦٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَمُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰٓ ۖ أَنْ أَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلدِّينَ وَلَا تَتَفَرَّقُوا۟ فِيهِ ۚ كَبُرَ عَلَى ٱلْمُشْرِكِينَ مَا تَدْعُوهُمْ إِلَيْهِ ۚ ٱللَّهُ يَجْتَبِىٓ إِلَيْهِ مَن يَشَآءُ وَيَهْدِىٓ إِلَيْهِ مَن يُنِيبُ ‎⟫
> *He (Allah) has ordained for you the same religion (Islam) which He ordained for Nuh (Noah), and that which We have inspired in you (O Muhammad PBUH), and that which We ordained for Ibrahîm (Abraham), Musa (Moses) and 'Iesa (Jesus) saying you should establish religion (i.e. to do what it orders you to do practically), and make no divisions in it (religion) (i.e. various sects in religion). Intolerable for the Mushrikun , is that to which you (O Muhammad PBUH) call them. Allah chooses for Himself whom He wills, and guides unto Himself who turns to Him in repentance and in obedience.*

"Whosoever wishes to be guided must become cognizant of the *wilāyat* (guardianship) of ʿAlī. Whoever Allah desires to seal off, He keeps from knowledge of ʿAlī. O Abū Dharr! ʿAlī is the standard of guidance, the proof of piety, the strong rope of Allah, the guide of My saints, and a lamp for those who obey Me. His friends are believers; his enemies are disbelievers. Whoever breaks with him leads himself astray and leads others astray. Whosoever denies his guardianship is a hypocrite!"

Abū Dharr said, "May my parents be devoted to you! Fill my heart with more knowledge!"

The Prophet replied: "On the Night of the Ascent, I was taken to heaven. When I first entered the celestial realm, an angel called the *adhān* (call to prayer) and the prayer was established. Gabriel took my hand and led me forward, saying, 'Pray with the angels! They have long desired to see you.' I prayed with seventy ranks of angels, each rank stretching from east to west. None save Allah knows their number.

"When the prayer ended, certain angels came to me, greeted me, and said, 'We have a request of you.' I thought perhaps they would ask for intercession, for Allah had granted me the Pool of Kawthar and the power of intercession. I said, 'O angels of my Lord! What is your need?' They said, 'Our need is that when you return to earth, convey our greetings to ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib and tell him, "We are eager to visit you."' I asked them, 'Do you truly know us?'

"They answered, 'O Messenger of Allah! How could we not know you when you are the first of Allah's creation? He created you from His light and appointed for you a sublime place in the Unseen so that you might glorify Him, sanctify Him, and exalt Him. Then He created the angels from various lights as He willed. We have learned all we know from you both! All goodness that descends from Allah comes first to you. And all the deeds of servants that ascend to Allah pass first through you. How then could we not know you?

"'You are the finest of Allah's creation, the custodian of His knowledge, His mighty rope, His supreme proof! You are the foundation of knowledge and wisdom. You are the gateway to all stations and ranks. You are the decisive argument against all disputation. And ʿAlī is the Dābba al-Arḍ (the one who distinguishes truth from falsehood on the Day of Judgment). You both are the Givers of Paradise and Hell. Your offspring are the Ark of salvation—whosoever boards it is saved; whosoever is left behind is drowned. You are the best of the Community and the stars of the earth.

"'You are the Tree of Prophethood, the abode of mercy, the sanctuary of prophecy, the dwelling of the angels. Gabriel descends upon you with revelation. ## The Guardianship of the Enslaved Woman

ʿAmāsh narrates: "I saw a slave girl in Medina whose face was dark and whose eyes were blind. She gave water to people saying, 'Drink in the friendship and love of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib!'

Then I saw her in Mecca, and behold, her eyes had been opened to sight. She was giving water to people saying, 'Drink in the love of him for whom Allah has given me sight!' I asked her, 'I saw you in Medina when you were blind, giving water and saying, "Drink in the friendship and love of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib." Now I see you have regained your sight. Tell me what happened!'

She replied, 'One day a man came to me and said, "O maidservant! Do you confess to the guardianship and love of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib?" I said, "Yes!" He prayed, saying, "O Allah! If what this maidservant says is true, restore her sight." By the blessing of his prayer, my eyes opened and I gained my vision.' I asked him, 'Who are you?' He answered, 'I am Khumdar, one of the Shīʿa of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib.'"[^cite-10]

## Recognizing ʿAlī in His Luminous Station

Salmān al-Fārisī and Abū Dharr asked, "O Amīr al-Muʾminīn! What is the light of your knowledge?" He replied that the Messenger of Allah said, "The knowledge of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib is a light, and the knowledge of Allah is a light. This light is the pure religion."

Amīr al-Muʾminīn said: "The worth of a person's deeds is lighter than a mustard seed beside my *wilāyat*. O Salmān! No believer's faith is complete until he recognizes me as light. When he comes to know me as light, he becomes a believer whose heart Allah has tested with faith, whose breast He has opened to Islam. Such a one becomes a true believer and observer of the religion. But he who fails to do so is one who doubts and harbors satanic suspicion.

"O Salmān and Jundub! My knowledge of Allah is Allah's knowledge, and Allah's knowledge is my knowledge. This is the pure religion. Allah says:"

> ⟪quran 98:5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أُمِرُوٓا۟ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ مُخْلِصِينَ لَهُ ٱلدِّينَ حُنَفَآءَ وَيُقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَيُؤْتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ ۚ وَذَلِكَ دِينُ ٱلْقَيِّمَةِ ‎⟫
> *And they were commanded not, but that they should worship Allah, and worship none but Him Alone (abstaining from ascribing partners to Him), and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat) and give Zakat: and that is the right religion.*

"Through my guardianship is sincerity in the worship of Allah perfected. The religion of the Hanīf is the acknowledgment of the Prophethood of Muḥammad Muṣṭafā, for his is the simplest and most direct of religions. The purpose of prayer is to establish me as guardian. Whoever takes refuge in my guardianship has indeed established prayer, for prayer is the most difficult and most arduous of duties. Giving *zakāt* (the purifying alms) means acknowledging the station of the Imāms. This is Allah's established religion! The Qurʾān bears witness that ⟪ar:'دین قائم'⟫ (the standing religion) requires tawhīd (affirmation of divine unity) with sincerity, acknowledgment of prophethood, and acceptance of guardianship. Whosoever follows this has attained the religion.

"O Salmān and Jundub! A tested believer is one who, when something befalls him by our command, Allah opens his breast to accept it without doubt. Whoever asks 'how' and 'why' has disbelieved. Obey Allah's command, for we are ⟪ar:أمر اللہ⟫ (the command of Allah). Allah has made me trustworthy over His creatures and appointed me His Caliph over His lands, cities, and servants. He has given me all that neither describers nor mystics can define. If you recognize me in this manner, then you are believers!

"O Salmān and Jundub! Allah says:"

> ⟪quran 2:45⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱسْتَعِينُوا۟ بِٱلصَّبْرِ وَٱلصَّلَوٰةِ ۚ وَإِنَّهَا لَكَبِيرَةٌ إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلْخَشِعِينَ ‎⟫

"Patience and prayer are my guardianship, which is why it says that it is a great burden. Yet then He made clear that only those of *khushūʿ* (humility before God) can bear it—those who have received insight from Our light, whom Allah has therefore excepted. All people acknowledge the prophethood of the Messenger of Allah and none dispute it. It is only my guardianship that most deny.

In another place He says:"

> ⟪quran 22:45⟫

The Amīr continued: "O Salmān and Jundub! We are the secrets of Allah that lie not hidden, the light of Allah that cannot be seen. We are His blessing for which there is no price. We grant life and death by Allah's permission. I know what you eat and what you store in your houses. I know what is in your hearts. All the Imāms among my descendants possess this knowledge, able to exercise it as they will—for we are all truly one being. Yet despite these powers, we eat and drink and walk in the markets. We are Allah's servants who do not transgress His word and obey His commands. He has made us pristine and pure. Whosoever befriends us, Allah shall not punish. Whosoever opposes us, Allah shall not show mercy.

"O Salmān and Jundub! ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ وآلہ وسلم⟫ and I were one light before those who glorified Allah glorified Him, before all creatures were created. Allah divided this light into two parts. One He commanded, 'Be Muḥammad!' and the other, 'Be ʿAlī!' For this reason the Prophet said, 'ʿAlī and I are of ⟪ar:'شئ واحد'⟫ (one being).' He also said, 'I am from ʿAlī and ʿAlī is from me. None may convey my message save I and ʿAlī.'

"In reference to this unity in the world of lights, Allah employed the verse of Mubāhala, referring to ⟪ar:'معنی واحد'⟫ (one meaning). And when Allah says, 'If the Prophet dies or is slain,' He means if the Prophet dies or the successor is slain, for we are two and ⟪ar:'نور واحد'⟫ (one light). We are united in meaning and attribute, though separate in body and name. Thus in the world of souls we are one, as the Messenger of Allah said: 'You are the spirit within my breast. In the world of bodies too, you are of me and I am of you. I am your heir and you are my heir. Your station with me is as the soul's station with the body.'

"This means send blessings upon Muḥammad and peace upon ʿAlī. Thus Allah united us in one body yet distinguished us by attributes. He said, 'Send blessings upon the Prophet and accept ʿAlī.' Your blessings upon the Prophet alone shall not benefit you unless you accept the guardianship of ʿAlī!

"When Ḥaḍrat Abū Bakr was sent to Mecca to protect himself from the polytheists, Gabriel came down and said, 'O Messenger of Allah! Your Lord commands that you yourself deliver it, or send someone from your kin.' The Messenger sent me after Abū Bakr to bring him back.[^cite-11] O Salmān and Jundub! How can one who was unqualified to convey the Messenger's message become the Imām and leader of the people?

"O Salmān and Jundub! In his time, Muḥammad was the *Nātiq* (the speaker) and I was the *Sāmit* (the silent one). In every age, the Nātiq and the Sāmit are one. Thus Muḥammad is the herald of the gathering, and I am the herald of the Resurrection. Muḥammad is the warner, I am the guide. Muḥammad is the Lord of Paradise, I am the Lord of Hell. Muḥammad is the Lord of the Pool, I am the Lord of the Liwa. Muḥammad bears the Revelation, I bear the Inspiration. ## Our Lady Fatima in the Gathering

⟪ar:سیدنا قاضی نعمان⟫ relates through his work that ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ reported from ⟪ar:شرع الأخبار⟫ the following narration: ⟪ar:رسول اﷲ صلی اﷲ علیہ و آلہ سلم⟫ declared, "On the Day of Resurrection, pulpits of light shall be established for all the prophets." Thereafter, pulpits shall be raised for all the awliyāʾ (spiritual allies), the most elevated of which shall belong to ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫. ## The Mutual Union of Muhammad and Ali

When one examines the history of Islam with care, one discerns a bond of extraordinary profundity between Muḥammad (peace be upon him) and Ali (peace be upon him). A luminous illustration of this union appears in the incident of the Hijrah. As the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings upon him) prepared to depart for Madīnah, fleeing the persecution of the disbelievers and polytheists, he entrusted Ali with a sacred task: "O Ali! Whatever deposits and trusts the disbelievers and polytheists have left with me, return them to their owners." Remarkably, the Prophet did not enumerate these trusts nor specify their nature. Yet history records not a single instance wherein someone later approached the Prophet claiming that Ali had failed to restore their possession. This singular absence of grievance testifies to the profound confidence between the Prophet and Ali, and their mutual comprehension of one another's purposes.[^cite-15]

The theological foundation of this union lies in a subtle linguistic principle: the testimony *"La ilāha illā Allah"* (there is no god save Allah) summons to the word *"Muḥammad,"* directing the believer toward the Messenger, and the word *"Muḥammad"* in turn invokes *ʿAlī Walīullāh*—Ali, the Friend of Allah. Thus, when the Almighty revealed this verse, the Prophet immediately proclaimed the merits of Ali with profound reverence, setting forth in countless ḥadīths his excellence, his virtues (manāqib), declaring that "Ali is he who pays my debts, Ali is he who distributes Paradise and Hell," and such noble utterances. Yet consider the reciprocal burden this placed upon Ali: the Prophet had, across thirty-two years of prophethood, woven thousands of traditions into the fabric of his station. Ali bore the solemn obligation to ensure that the weight of these sacred sayings never became void through any failing on his part.[^cite-16]

History itself bears witness that the Prophet never once expressed regret over these declarations, never suggested that Ali had disappointed his confidence. Across those thousands of ḥadīths, Ali ensured that not one word passed unfulfilled. This constancy testifies to a mutual trust unparalleled in human chronicle: the Prophet would never utter that which Ali could not accomplish, and Ali would sacrifice his very soul to vindicate what the Prophet had proclaimed. Such an alliance finds no parallel in all of history.

(From the *Lectures on Migration*, attributed to Mullāh Ghulām Ḥusayn ibn Ḥaẓrat Nasrullāh Ṣāḥib)

## Imam al-Baqir and the Elder

Once, Imām al-Bāqir (peace be upon him) was seated in assembly when an aged man approached, leaning upon his staff. Drawing near, the elder arose and greeted him: "Peace be upon you, O son of the Messenger of Allah, and may Allah's mercy and blessings rest upon you." He paused, awaiting response. The Imam replied in kind. The old man then turned toward the gathered assembly, offered his salutation to all present, and waited until each had answered his greeting.

Turning again to Imām al-Bāqir, the elder spoke: "O son of the Messenger of Allah! Grant me a place beside you, may Allah bless me through you! By Allah, I love you and all who love you. O Allah! This affection and friendship of mine springs not from worldly greed or desire! O Allah! I despise your enemies and declare my revulsion and hatred toward them! Yet this enmity of mine arises not from personal vendetta or malice between us, but solely from their enmity toward you. Do I have hope in you, and do you have hope in me? May Allah bless me through you!"

The Imam said: "O Shaykh! Come to my side," and made him sit beside him. Then he continued: "O Shaykh! A man once came to my father, Aʿlī ibn al-Ḥusayn, asking the very question you pose. My father replied:

*If you die upon the love of us, the family, and upon the wilāyat of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, then when your soul reaches your throat, you shall encounter us with joy and gladness. Your heart shall be tranquil, your countenance brightened. When your spirit ascends, the angels shall receive you with their arms outstretched in welcome.*"[^cite-17]

Upon hearing these words, the man wept bitterly; his breath grew shallow and labored, and he collapsed. All those assembled likewise began to weep at witnessing his state. The Imam raised him gently and wiped his tears with his blessed hands. The man seized the Imam's hand, kissed it, and pressed it against his eyes and face, then laid it upon his breast. He then rose, took his leave with blessings, and departed. As he was leaving, the Imam (peace be upon him) said to the gathering:

"Whoever among you desires to see one of the people of Paradise, let him gaze upon this Shaykh."

(From *Manāqib Ahl al-Bayt*)

## The Grandeur of the Prophet, Peace Be Upon Him

The life that the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings upon him) lived during his forty years before prophethood was of such sublime grandeur that it outweighed the lives of all one hundred twenty-four thousand prophets combined. Therefore, when he was commissioned as a messenger, Allah declared:

⟪quran 68:4⟫
⟪ar:‏وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلَىٰ خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍۢ ‎⟫

*And verily, thou art of a mighty character.*[^cite-27]

This revelation came to him at the age of forty-one, scarcely one year into his prophetic mission. Although twenty-three years of prophecy yet lay before him, he was crowned from the very outset with the pinnacle of moral excellence. This verse does not celebrate the virtues he would exhibit across those twenty-three years of preaching, but rather those forty years that preceded his prophethood—a testament to the perfection already achieved.

The advancement of the Messenger of Allah was thus extraordinarily swift, for his era was one of profound trial. His companions were subjected to merciless torment for reciting the testimony *Lā ilāha illā Allah* (there is no god save Allah), which prevented him from preaching openly in Mecca. Yet even in such a climate of oppression, he exhibited a standard of conduct and virtue so elevated that it surpassed that of all the prophets. His preaching was achieved not through words, but through the silent witness of his character. The unbelievers themselves would have embraced Islam had they truly comprehended his moral majesty.[^cite-28] This is why in the invocation *Duʿāʾ Hayāt-i Qāf* (The Supplication of Eternal Life), wherein the miracles of the prophets are enumerated, the Prophet's moral character stands foremost—a measure of its supreme excellence.

Among the conditions stipulated in the Treaty of Ḥudaybiyyah was this: "If anyone comes to us from your side, we are free to return him or keep him; but if anyone comes to us from Madīnah, you shall return him without fail." The Prophet accepted all these inequitable terms. Yet what transpired revealed divine wisdom: whenever a disbeliever came to Madīnah, he was so captivated by the Prophet's character that he embraced Islam and then returned to Mecca to spread the faith. Conversely, those sent from Madīnah were already fortified in faith. Thus his preaching was doubly effective through this singular instrument of moral excellence.

In *Sūrat al-Balad*, Allah declares:

⟪quran 90:1⟫
⟪ar:‏لَآ أُقْسِمُ بِهَذَا ٱلْبَلَدِ ‎⟫

*I swear by this city (Mecca),*

⟪quran 90:2⟫
⟪ar:‏وَأَنتَ حِلٌّۢ بِهَذَا ٱلْبَلَدِ ‎⟫

*And by thy dwelling therein.*[^cite-29]

In this sublime oath, Allah does not say "I swear by Mecca in which the House is situated," but rather emphasizes the very presence of the Prophet within it. Thus has Allah honored his life and isolated it in His oath—not the sanctuary, but the Prophet himself becomes the object of divine witness, such is the magnitude of his virtue.

**The Prophet's Silence:**

When people posed questions to the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings upon him), he never hastened to reply from his own knowledge, lest he err. Rather, he awaited divine revelation, manifesting profound silence. He himself prayed: "O Allah, make my silence a lesson, and make my quiet contemplation a marker of wisdom." His silence was so pronounced that the Bedouin visitors who came to see him believed the one claiming prophecy to be mute. In a tradition he said: "If peace and tranquility were divided into ten parts, nine would consist of silence and one of speech."[^cite-30]

**Higher Station Than the Angels:**

The Prophet (peace and blessings upon him) proclaimed: "O Ali, had it not been for us, Allah would not have created Adam, Eve, Paradise, the heavens, or the earth!"

How, then, could we not surpass the angels? We glorified and sanctified Allah before the angels, for Allah created our souls first and gave us the tongue of His monotheism and praise. Then He created the angels. When the celestial beings beheld our unparalleled light, they magnified us. We glorified the Almighty so that the angels might know we are His creation and that His glory emanates from our attributes. Witnessing our glorification, the angels themselves glorified Him through us![^cite-31]

When the angels perceived our majesty, we dissolved the oneness of Allah—that He is One and we are His servants, and none shares His worship—so that the angels recited *Lā ilāha illā Allah* (there is no god save Allah). And when they perceived the height of our station, we proclaimed *Allāhu Akbar* (Allah is Greatest), so the angels might comprehend that Allah transcends all heights inaccessible without His cause! Observing our majesty, we intoned *Lā ḥawl wa lā quwwata illā billāh al-ʿAlī al-ʿAẓīm* (There is no might nor power save in Allah, the Most High, the Most Great), teaching the angels that no strength exists without Allah's causation.

When the angels witnessed the blessings Allah bestowed upon us and the obedience made incumbent upon mankind toward us, we uttered *Al-Ḥamdu lilāh* (Praise be to Allah), so the angels might know that all praise belongs to Allah alone for His boundless grace. Thus the angels learned through us the knowledge of tawhīd (monotheism), tasbiḥ (glorification), tahlīl (the testimony of oneness), and taḥmīd (praise), becoming guided to the recognition of Allah.[^cite-32]

**Why the Angels Prostrated to Adam:**

The Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings upon him) declared: "Then Allah created Adam and placed us within his loin as a trust. He then commanded the angels to prostrate before Adam in reverence of us."

Therefore, the prostration of the angels was worship of Allah, and their bow to Adam was obedience and honor to Allah—for we were his descendants held in trust. How could we not surpass the angels when all the celestial hosts prostrated themselves before Adam on account of us?[^cite-33]
