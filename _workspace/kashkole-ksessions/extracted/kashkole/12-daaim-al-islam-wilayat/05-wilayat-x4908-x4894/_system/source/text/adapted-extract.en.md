## The Prophet's Explicit Designation of Ali

One day the Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ stood in the mihrab of the Quba Mosque when revelation came to him that the hour of his death drew near. He received tidings that he would soon meet his Lord—and that the Almighty eagerly desired that meeting. At the same time, he learned of his conquest of ⟪ar:مکہ معظمہ⟫ Mecca the Magnified. When the Prophet informed the *Umma* that his end approached, the people grew anxious. They asked, "O Messenger of Allah! We obey the Law as long as we see you and may ask you directly. What will become of your community when you depart?" He answered them with clarity: "Whoever among you follows my command should follow the command of Ali after me."

It was during the Conquest of Mecca that the Prophet—through Ali—shattered the idols enshrined in the Kaaba. Since opportunity had long been denied him, the Prophet resolved to undertake his first and final pilgrimage in the tenth year of the Hijra. Thus he proclaimed the ⟪ar:'حجۃ الوداع'⟫ Farewell Pilgrimage. That year, multitudes gathered, for the Prophet himself was performing the sacred rites. After completing the hajj, he announced to the assembled people: "I depart now for Madinah." The people supposed, as one leaves the Friday mosque by a different gate than the one entered, that the Prophet too would return by an alternate route. Yet the Messenger of Allah possessed a wisdom far more profound.[^cite-1]

When the Prophet reached the place called ⟪ar:جحفا⟫ Juhfa, a revelation descended upon him commanding that he announce the *wilayat* (spiritual guardianship) of Ali. He paused and moved onward. Gabriel descended again with the same revelation; again he paused. When at last he arrived at ⟪ar:'غدیر خم'⟫ Ghadir Khum, a final and urgent revelation came—this time bearing the severest admonition from the Lord:

> ⟪quran 5:67⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ بَلِّغْ مَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِن رَّبِّكَ ۖ وَإِن لَّمْ تَفْعَلْ فَمَا بَلَّغْتَ رِسَالَتَهۥ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ يَعْصِمُكَ مِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *O Messenger, deliver what has been sent down to you from your Lord. If you do not, then you have not fulfilled His message, and Allah will protect you from the people. Indeed, Allah does not guide the people of the disbelieving.*
> — **Quran 5:67**

In this verse, Allah made known a grave warning: should the Prophet fail to convey what he was commanded to convey, all his labors in Prophethood would be rendered void, as though he had never been sent as a messenger at all. Yet in the same breath came consolation—the assurance that Allah would shield him from the harm of men.[^cite-2]

**Annotation**

In Arabic, *ghadir* (غدير) signifies a pond or pool. The place *Khum* was known by this name.

### Did the Messenger of Allah Disobey the Lord?

Some have claimed that the Prophet should have obeyed Allah's command upon the first revelation and that his hesitation constituted disobedience to the Most High. Such a claim is answered by a simple question: Did not the Prophet, who taught the *Umma* the very meaning of obedience to Allah, understand better than all others what obeys and what disobeys the Divine Will? God forbid such a thought!

Rather, the Prophet's wisdom lay thus: he desired that this stern verse be revealed so that the supreme importance of Ali's *wilayat* would be established beyond all doubt. He sought a guarantee of protection. When the verse came with the words ⟪ar:وَٱللَّهُ يَعْصِمُكَ مِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ⟫ "Allah will protect you from the people," he hesitated not a moment longer but immediately proclaimed the guardianship of Ali.[^cite-3]

---

Having received Allah's promise of divine protection—⟪ar:عصمت⟫ *ʿisma*, the state of being preserved from error and harm—the Prophet commanded that all present halt. "Gather those who have gone ahead," he decreed. "Wait for those who lag behind. Let all the multitude assemble here." He ordered the thorny trees and shrubs removed from the place (symbolic of those who harbored enmity toward Ali). This was the gathering he had longed to witness since the creation of the heavens. A pulpit was raised from the saddles of camels, and ascending it, the Prophet delivered an address of surpassing power.

He asked the assembled people: "Have I not conveyed to you all the commands of Allah? Did I not teach you of purification, prayer, *zakat*, fasting, pilgrimage, and struggle in the way of God? Have I withheld any ordinance of the Lord from you? Have I not brought you forth from the darkness of ignorance into light?" All replied, "Indeed, O Messenger of Allah! Our parents are a ransom for you. You found us as scattered peoples and made us a community."

Then the Prophet posed a question he would ask three times: ⟪ar-quote:الست اولا بکم من انفسکم؟⟫ "Am I not more a master to you than you are to yourselves?" Three times the people answered: ⟪ar-quote:بلا یا رسول اللہ!⟫ "Yes, O Messenger of Allah, you are!"

At that moment, the Prophet raised up Maulana Ali ibn Abi Talib—⟪ar:علی⟫ Ali—and lifted him so high that the whiteness of his armpits became visible to all, and he proclaimed:

| ⟪ar-quote:من كنت مولاه فهذا علي مولاه ؛ اللهم وال من والاه و عاد من عاداه و انصر من نصره و اخذل من خذله و أدر الحق معه حيث دار⟫ *Whomever I am his master, Ali is his master. O Allah, befriend those who befriend him, oppose those who oppose him, help those who help him, humiliate those who humiliate him, and turn the truth with him wherever he turns.* |
| --- |

After this proclamation, the Prophet and Maulana Ali performed two units of prayer and sat in the tent. Then the companions came forward—the first among them being ⟪ar:ابو بکر⟫Abu Bakr, ⟪ar:عمر⟫ Umar, ⟪ar:عثمان⟫ Uthman, ⟪ar:زبیر⟫ al-Zubayr, and ⟪ar:طلحا⟫ Talha—and each said: ⟪ar-quote:بك بك يا ابن ابي طالب كنت مولانا و مولى كل المؤمنين و المؤمنات⟫ "Blessed are you, son of Abi Talib! From this day forward, you are our master and the master of all believers, men and women." All of them clasped the hands of the Prophet and Maulana Ali and kissed their feet. The Prophet, observing them with full knowledge of what the future held, watched this scene unfold. He then spoke to the assembled: "O people! Among you are hypocrites with hearts turned two ways. I could expose them if I wished, yet I forbear. But I counsel you: if you desire Paradise, purge your hearts of rancor toward Ali and love him sincerely. Otherwise, you shall dwell in the Fire."

The Prophet then fell gravely ill. He wished to commit to writing all that he had said concerning Ali's succession, and he called for pen and paper to record his will. But Umar intervened, saying the Messenger was overcome by fever and spoke in delirium. Thus, when the Prophet departed from this world to meet his Lord—⟪ar:رحمت للعالمین⟫ the Mercy Unto All the Worlds—it was Ali who personally performed the ritual washing and shrouding of his body and entrusted him to the grave. Meanwhile, the greater part of the *Umma* lay awake through the night in dispute over succession.

## Ali's Manifest Caliphate

No sooner had the Prophet closed his eyes than the Muhajir and Ansar hastened to a place called ⟪ar:بنو سقیفہ⟫ Saqifa to select the next Caliph. There, amid the clamor and jostling of factions—each pushing forward its own candidate like herders driving beasts—the strongest voice among them, a man named Amr, declared: "We choose Abu Bakr as our leader." None dared contradict him, and thus Abu Bakr became the first Caliph. Umar, in turn, pledged his allegiance on condition that Abu Bakr name him as successor, ensuring Umar's own path to authority. And after Umar, Uthman became Caliph. For thirty years, these successive rulers inflicted upon Ali and the *Ahl al-Bayt* (the Family of the Prophet) a cascade of indignities and wrongs. They usurped the lands of Maulana Fatima. Umar struck her with such force that he caused her to miscarry the son she bore. Her house was set ablaze. A rider of that time dragged Ali himself through the marketplace with a rope bound about his hand. Yet Ali and his devoted companions endured all such trials in silence, for Allah had commanded patience.[^cite-4]

When Uthman was at last killed and no one dared assume the caliphate, the people were forced to approach Ali's door as suppliants. There they found him seated, mending his torn shoe—a garment that had earned him the epithet ⟪ar:'قاصف النعل'⟫ "He Who Stitches the Shoe." Ali asked them, "In your estimation, what is the worth of this torn shoe?" They replied, "O Ali, a worn shoe has little value." He answered, "I swear by the Lord! This tattered shoe holds greater worth in my sight than your caliphate! Yet since the *Umma* has become so divided and Islam itself faces dissolution, I shall accept the apparent caliphate—but know this: you do not choose me as leader. Rather, say that I am ⟪ar:'خلیفۃ اللہ'⟫ the Caliph of Allah, and make no claim of choosing." By then, so estranged had the people become from him that when his caliphate was announced in the mosque and he rose to address them, those around him seized him and pulled him down, saying, "O Sheikh! Where do you rise? This is the station of Ali ibn Abi Talib! We have heard great tales of his deeds—⟪ar:حیدر کرار غیر فرار⟫ Haydar the Formidable, Unafraid!"

Ali said, "O brethren, release me. I am Ali ibn Abi Talib." With that, he strode directly to the Prophet's *minbar* (pulpit) and there delivered a sermon of unparalleled eloquence:

⟪ar:الحمد للہ رجعل الحق الی اھلہ! يا معاشر الناس !.. سلوني قبل أن تفقدوني ، ھذا سَفَط العلم ، ھذا لعاب رسول اللہ (ص) ، ھذا ما زقّني رسول اللہ زقّاً زقّاً ، سلوني فإنّ عندي علم الأولين والآخرين ، أما واللہ لو ثُنّيت لي وسادة فجلست عليھا ، لأفتيت أھل التوراة بتوراتھم حتی تنطق التوراة فتقول : صدق عليٌّ  ما كذب ، لقد أفتاكم بما أنزل اللہ فيّ ، وأفتيت أھل الإنجيل بإنجيلھم حتی ينطق الإنجيل فيقول : صدق عليٌّ  ما كذب ، لقد أفتاكم بما أنزل اللہ فيّ ، وأفتيت أھل القرآن بقرآنھم حتی ينطق القرآن فيقول صدق علي ّ ما كذب، لقد أفتاكم بما أنزل اللہ فيّ  ثم قال سلوني قبل أن تفقدوني ، فو الذي فلق الحبۃ وبرأ النسمۃ لو سألتموني عن أيۃ آيۃ في ليل أُنزلت أوفي نھار أُنزلت ، مكيّھا ومدنيھا ، سفريھا وحضريھا ، ناسخھا ومنسوخھا ، ومحكمھا ومتشابھا ، وتأويلھا وتنزيلھا لأخبرتكم⟫

*Praise be to Allah, who has restored the truth to its rightful place! O assembled people! Ask me before you lose me! Here is the reservoir of knowledge—here is the very essence that flowed from the Messenger of Allah. This is the knowledge he instilled in me, drop by drop. Ask me, for I hold within me the knowledge of all that came before and all that shall come after. By Allah, if you placed me upon a cushioned throne, I could answer the People of the Torah with their own Torah until it itself testified: "Ali spoke truth and did not lie—he taught them what Allah revealed concerning them." So too with the People of the Gospel: I would answer them with their Gospel until it cried out: "Ali spoke truth and did not lie—he taught them what Allah revealed concerning them." And with the People of the Qur'an, I would answer from their Qur'an until the Qur'an itself bore witness: "Ali spoke truth and did not lie—he taught them what Allah revealed concerning them."*

*Then he said: Ask me before you lose me. By He who split the seed and created the breath of life, if you were to question me about any verse—whether revealed by night or by day, in Mecca or in Madinah, during travel or at rest—which verses abrogate and which are abrogated, which are absolute in meaning and which are analogical, what is the interpretation of each and what is the literal sense—I could tell you all of it!*

### Why Did Ali Remain Silent for Thirty Years?

The answer lies in a teaching that Ali himself gave. Once, two women came before him, each claiming a child as her own. Ali commanded Qambar, "Take the sword and cleave the child in two, giving one half to each mother." At this terrible pronouncement, the true mother cried out, "No, Ali! Rather give the child to this woman, even though he is mine—at least he shall live." The child of the *Umma* was Islam itself, and like the grieving mother, Ali bore his anguish in silence lest the faith be torn asunder. For thirty years he refrained from asserting his rights, knowing that had he done so, the *Umma* would have splintered into countless fragments. Had Ali wished to claim what was rightfully his, neither would Zulfiqar (his sword) have remained sheathed nor the strength of his arm—the arm that had breached the fortress of Khaybar—have been withheld.[^cite-5]

## The Guardianship and Knowledge of the Five

One day Mufaddal came into the presence of Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (peace be upon him). The Imam asked him, "O Mufaddal, do you recognize Muhammad, Ali, Fatima, Hasan, and Husayn in the manner they deserve to be recognized? Have you grasped the truth of their knowledge (*maʿrifa*)?"

Mufaddal replied, "O my Master and my Lord, what is this true knowledge of theirs?"

The Imam said: "O Mufaddal, whoever possesses the true knowledge of these five attains the highest stations of faith."

Mufaddal asked, "Then instruct me, O Lord, concerning their knowledge."

The Imam responded: "O Mufaddal, know that Muhammad, Ali, Fatima, Hasan, and Husayn possess knowledge of all that Allah the Mighty and Majestic has created—all that exists. They are the Word of Piety and the Treasuries of the heavens and the earth. They know the measure of mountains, the weight of the seas, and the number of the stars in heaven and the angels in the kingdom. They know the weight of the mountains and the quantity of water in the rivers. Not a leaf falls from a tree but they know of it. They know every grain hidden in the darkness of the earth. There is nothing—dry or moist—that is not recorded in their book."

Mufaddal said, "O my Master, I understand this, I confess it, and I believe in it."

The Imam replied: "Yes, O Mufaddal, you are right. O blessed one! O you whom the heavens have graced! May the Almighty bless you and all who hold such faith."[^cite-6]

## The Luminous Essences of Allah

Allah the Most High created His beloved Muhammad Mustafa ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی⟫ (peace be upon him) from a Divine Light—a radiance that emanated from the grandeur and majesty of the Godhead. This very light had shone upon Musa ibn Imran ⟪ar:موسیٰ⟫ (peace be upon him) at Mount Sinai with such force that he could not bear the sight of it. When he beheld it, he cried out and fell senseless to the earth.

When Allah purposed to create His Beloved, He divided this light into two parts. From the first part He created Muhammad (peace be upon him), and from the second part He created Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him). No other being was formed from this light save these two. God fashioned them both with His mighty power and breathed into them His Spirit. He took upon Himself to perfect their forms and made them His own. These two are Allah's witnesses to His creation, His successors among the creatures, and His seeing eye and speaking tongue over all beings. Allah entrusted to them His knowledge and gave them the essence of its exposition. He revealed to them His hidden secrets. One of them declared the reality of the soul, and the other declared the reality of the self. Each was the support and sustenance of the other; neither could endure without the other.[^cite-7]

Their outward appearance is that of humanity in flesh and form, yet their inward reality is linked and united with the Divine. They appeared among mankind in human shape so that people might perceive them. Yet Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 6:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَوْ جَعَلْنَهُ مَلَكًۭا لَّجَعَلْنَهُ رَجُلًۭا وَلَلَبِسْنَا عَلَيْهِم مَّا يَلْبِسُونَ ‎⟫
> *And had We appointed him an angel, We would have sent him as a man, and We would have confused them with that which they are already confused.*
> — **Quran 6:9**

Both of them are the Successors (khalīfas) of Allah and the executors of His creative will. Creation began with these two, and with them the cosmos and destiny shall end. Thus was Maulana Fatima al-Zahra (peace be upon her) created from the light of Muhammad (peace be upon him)—she derived from his light as he derived from hers. From the light of Ali and Fatima, Allah created the light of Hasan and Husayn (peace be upon them), as one lamp is kindled from another. In this manner, all five were created from light and transmitted from one generation to another, from one father's loins to another's, from one mother's womb to another's—not from filthy water or corrupt sperm as ordinary humans are born, but from a light passed through holy succession from pure wombs to pure wombs, for they are the chosen pearls of creation.

These beings were selected and chosen by Allah for Himself. He made them the Custodians of the Treasuries of His Knowledge, the Messengers to His people, the Successors (because He Himself cannot be seen, cannot be perceived, cannot be known in His essence, cannot be reached). They are those who speak on behalf of Allah, conveying His message to creation and making clear His commands and prohibitions. Through them He displayed His power and manifested His signs and wonders. Through them He made Himself known to His servants, and through them His commandments are obeyed.

Had it not been for these noble ones, no knowledge of Allah would exist; there would be no understanding of how to worship the Most Merciful in truth. Allah says:

> ⟪quran 21:23⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَا يُسْـَٔلُ عَمَّا يَفْعَلُ وَهُمْ يُسْـَٔلُونَ ‎⟫
> *He is not questioned about what He does, but they will be questioned.*
> — **Quran 21:23**

He cannot be questioned about His acts; rather, they shall be questioned about theirs.[^cite-8]

## The Reckoning of the Self

Amir al-Mu'minin Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him) said: "Shall I tell you who are the wisest of people and who are the most foolish? The wisest are those who scrutinize their own souls and prepare for the Afterlife; the most foolish are those who follow the desires and fancies of the soul and hope in vain."

A man once asked Amir al-Mu'minin, "How does one scrutinize one's own soul?"

He replied: "When night falls and you have completed the day, sit alone with yourself and address your soul, saying: 'O soul! These days that have passed shall not return. Verily, Allah will question you: What did you do on this day? Did you remember Allah and glorify Him? Did you fulfill the rights of your believing brother? Did you ease his sorrows? In his absence, did you guard the honor of your family and children? Did you preserve the sanctity of his heirs after death? Did you refrain from backbiting a believing brother? How have you spent this day? Remember all your deeds!'

"The remembrance of the Qur'an is best, followed by praise to Allah, the Sublime, and the Compassionate, then remembrance of your sins and seeking Allah's forgiveness. Send blessings upon Muhammad and his family, declare the unity of Amir al-Mu'minin, and curse the Devil and his associates.

"If you find that all your deeds were good, then praise Allah and thank Him for enabling you to do righteousness. But if you find any error in the record of your deeds, seek forgiveness from Allah and make a vow never to commit such sins again. And accept the *wilayat* (guardianship) of Amir al-Mu'minin (peace be upon him) and curse his enemies—those who usurped his rights and deprived him of his station.

"When a believer conducts himself in this manner, Allah says: 'I shall not deal harshly with you on account of your sins because of your acceptance of My guardianship and your love for My allies.'"[^cite-9]

## Zulaikha's Acceptance of Guardianship

Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (peace be upon him) narrated: Zulaikha sought the king's permission to visit Yusuf (Joseph). The king said, "Are you not afraid to go to him after all that you did to him?"

She replied, "I do not fear that which one fears when one reveres Allah."

When Zulaikha arrived, Yusuf said, "What is wrong? I see your face has changed."

She answered, "Praise be to Allah, who has made kings slaves through disobedience and raised slaves to kingship through obedience!"

Yusuf asked, "What compelled you to do what you did to me?"

She said, "It was your beauty and your form."

Yusuf then said, "What would you do if you saw the Messenger whose name is Muhammad, who will be sent as a Messenger in the final age? His beauty, his noble character, and his generosity surpass mine immeasurably!"

Zulaikha exclaimed, "You speak the truth!"

Yusuf asked, "How do you know that I speak the truth?"

She replied, "As soon as you mentioned his blessed name and described his attributes, love for him entered my heart."

The Lord then said to Yusuf: "Zulaikha has spoken truly. I love her for the love of My Beloved that has been born in her heart." And at that moment, Allah commanded Yusuf to marry Zulaikha.[^cite-10]

## The Conversation of the Prophet and Abu Dharr

Abu Dharr al-Ghiffari (may Allah be pleased with him) narrated: One day I sat in the house of Umm Salama in the presence of the Prophet (peace be upon him), listening to his discourse, when Ali ibn Abi Talib entered. The Prophet's face radiated with joy upon seeing him. He kissed Ali upon the forehead and then turned to me, saying, "O Abu Dharr! Do you know who this person is?"

Abu Dharr replied, "O Messenger of Allah, he is your brother and your cousin, the husband of Fatima, the master of the youths of Paradise, the father of Hasan and Husayn (peace be upon them)."

The Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) said: "O Abu Dharr! Ali is a resplendent and illustrious Imam. He is the lofty standard of the Lord of the Universe and the great door to Allah's mercy. Whoever desires to reach Allah must enter through this door.

"O Abu Dharr! He is the establisher of justice, the guardian of Allah's sanctity, the helper of Allah's religion, and Allah's mighty proof against His creation. Allah perpetually establishes a proof among the nations concerning His creation, for I was sent as a Messenger to every people.

"O Abu Dharr! Allah has appointed seventy thousand angels at each pillar of His Throne. Their occupation and their praise consists in blessing Ali and the Shi'a of Ali (the devoted followers of Ali) and cursing the enemies of Ali (peace be upon him).

"O Abu Dharr! Had it not been for Ali, truth would not be distinguished from falsehood, the believer would not be separated from the disbeliever, and Allah would not be worshipped aright. For Ali subdued the polytheists until they embraced Islam and worshipped Allah. Without him, there would be neither reward nor punishment. There is no barrier between Allah and His creation save them—they themselves are the veil and the curtain."

Then the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) expounded this verse:

> ⟪quran 42:13⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ شَرَعَ لَكُم مِّنَ ٱلدِّينِ مَا وَصَّىٰ بِهِۦ نُوحًۭا وَٱلَّذِىٓ أَوْحَيْنَآ إِلَيْكَ وَمَا وَصَّيْنَا بِهِۦٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَمُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰٓ ۖ أَنْ أَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلدِّينَ وَلَا تَتَفَرَّقُوا۟ فِيهِ ۚ كَبُرَ عَلَى ٱلْمُشْرِكِينَ مَا تَدْعُوهُمْ إِلَيْهِ ۚ ٱللَّهُ يَجْتَبِىٓ إِلَيْهِ مَن يَشَآءُ وَيَهْدِىٓ إِلَيْهِ مَن يُنِيبُ ‎⟫
> *He has ordained for you the same religion which He ordained for Noah, and that which We have revealed to you, and that which We ordained for Abraham, Moses, and Jesus—saying: "Establish the religion and do not make divisions therein." That to which you call them is heavy for the idolaters. Allah draws to Himself whom He wills, and guides unto Himself whosoever turns in repentance.*
> — **Quran 42:13**

"Anyone who wishes to be guided must become familiar with the *wilayat* (guardianship) of Ali (peace be upon him). Whoever Allah wishes to seal off, He keeps away from knowledge of Ali. O Abu Dharr! Ali is the banner of guidance, the proof of piety, the strong rope of Allah, the guide of My saints, and a beacon of light for those who obey Me. His friends are believers, and his enemies are disbelievers. Whoever severs himself from him goes astray and leads others astray. Whoever denies his guardianship is a hypocrite.

"Abu Dharr, my parents are devoted to you—you have gladdened my heart! Tell me more!"

The Prophet continued: "On the night of the *Miʿraj* (Night Journey), I was taken to heaven. When I first ascended the heavens, an angel called the *adhan* (call to prayer) and the *iqama* (rising of prayer) was proclaimed. Gabriel took my hand and brought me forward, saying, 'Pray with the angels—they have been yearning to meet you for a long time.' I prayed with seventy rows of angels, each row stretching from east to west, their number known only to Allah.

"When the prayer concluded, some angels approached me, greeted me, and said, 'O Messenger of Allah, we have a need and a request.' I thought perhaps they would ask for intercession, for Allah had given me the Pool of Kausar and the power of intercession. I said, 'O angels of my Lord, what is your need?'

"They said, 'When you return to earth, convey our greetings to Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him) and tell him that we are very eager for his company.'

"I asked them, 'Do you truly know us?'

"They replied, 'O Messenger of Allah! How could we not know you when you are the first creation of Allah? He created you from His light and appointed for you a special place in the kingdom that you might glorify Him, sanctify Him, and exalt Him. Then He created us angels from various aspects as He willed. We have learned everything from both of you! Every good and blessing that descends from Allah comes to you first. ## The Slave Girl's Recognition of Guardianship

It is narrated by al-Aʿmash that he saw a slave girl in Madinah whose face was dark and who was blind, giving water to the people and saying, "Drink in the friendship and love of Ali ibn Abi Talib!"

Later, he saw her again in Mecca, and now her eyes had been restored to sight. She was again distributing water to the people, but this time saying, "Drink in her love—the one to whom Allah has restored my sight."

He said to her, "I saw you in Madinah when you were blind, and you were saying, 'Drink in the friendship and love of Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him).' Today I see you with restored sight. Tell me what happened!"

She replied, "One day a man came to me and asked, 'O slave girl, do you confess to the *wilayat* (guardianship) and love of Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him)?'

"I answered, 'Yes, I do.'

"He then prayed, saying, 'O Allah, if this slave girl speaks the truth, restore her sight.'

"By Allah, at that very moment—through the blessing of his prayer—my eyes were opened and I gained my sight."

"I asked the man, 'Who are you?'

"He replied, 'My name is Khamdar, and I am one of the Shi'a (devoted followers) of Ali ibn Abi Talib.'"[^cite-12]

Salman al-Farsi and Abu Dharr approached Amir al-Mu'minin and asked, "O Amir al-Mu'minin! What is the light of your knowledge?"

## The Mutual Covenant of Muhammad and Ali

When one examines the history of Islam with discerning eye, one discovers a profound relationship between Muhammad, upon whom be peace, and Ali, upon whom be peace. This bond is exemplified in the event of the Hijra (emigration). When the Messenger of Allah, faced with the persecution of the disbelievers and polytheists and commanded by God to depart for Medina, addressed Ali with these words: "O Ali, whatever trusts and deposits the disbelievers and polytheists have entrusted to me, you shall return them on my behalf"—yet he did not reveal to Ali the particulars of each trust. Remarkably, nowhere in the historical record do we find that anyone returned to lodge a complaint with the Prophet, saying that Ali had failed to restore his trust. This absence of complaint testifies to a profound bond of knowledge and understanding between Prophet and successor.[^cite-21]

The foundation of this correlation lies in the Divine utterance: "There is no god but Allah" (*lā ilāha illā-Llāh*) summons the consciousness to the person of Muhammad as Messenger, and the mention of Muhammad invites recognition of Ali as the *Walī-Allāh* (Guardian whom God designates). The *Walī* (guardian) of the Messenger was Maulana Abu Talib, and his *Ḥamd* (praise-worthy successor) was Maulana Ali ibn Abi Talib. Thus, when this verse was revealed to him:

> ⟪quran 93:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَمَّا بِنِعْمَةِ رَبِّكَ فَحَدِّثْ ‎⟫
> *And proclaim the grace of your Lord.*

the Prophet immediately began to speak of Ali with extraordinary eloquence, placing thousands of traditions into circulation concerning Ali's virtues, excellences, and preeminences—that Ali shall settle the Prophet's debts, that Ali shall apportion Paradise and Hell, and other such declarations. Though the Prophet spoke these words with calm deliberation, the responsibility thereby laid upon Ali was immense. Ali was obliged not to allow the weight of those traditions—narrated throughout the Prophet's thirty-two years of prophethood—to prove false. Such is true love and the deepest measure of trust! History bears witness that the Prophet never intimated, by word or sign, dissatisfaction with Ali for failing to uphold these noble sayings. That is to say, Ali never permitted any of these thousands of traditions to ring hollow. This represents an argument for mutual *tawakkul* (trust in God), for the Prophet would never have uttered what Ali could not fulfill, and Ali, in turn, was willing to sacrifice his very life to honor what the Prophet had declared. Such a covenant of souls is found nowhere else in human history.

## Imam al-Baqir and the Elder

On one occasion, Imam al-Bāqir, upon whom be peace, sat in assembly when an elderly man approached, leaning upon his staff. He came forward, stood before the Imam, and said: "Peace be upon you, O son of the Messenger of Allah, and upon you be the mercy and blessings of God." Then he fell silent, awaiting response. The Imam replied with like greeting. The old man then turned to face the gathering and greeted all assembled; having received their answers, he turned again to address the Imam directly.

He spoke thus: "O son of the Messenger of Allah, grant me a place with you, for God's blessing upon you! By Allah, I love you and I love all who love you. O Allah! This affection and love I harbor is not born of worldly greed or desire for material gain! O Allah! I despise your enemies and express my revulsion toward them! O Allah! This enmity I bear is not born of personal vendetta or private malice between us, but rather springs from their enmity toward you!"

The Imam replied with tenderness: "O Elder, draw near to me," and caused him to sit at his side. Then he said: "O Elder, a man came once to my father, Ali ibn al-Husayn, and posed a similar question. My father answered him thus:

*If Ali is the Messenger of Allah's successor, and Hasan and Husayn are Imams after him, and I myself am an Imam—then know that whoever dies in this love shall meet the Messenger of Allah, Amir al-Muʾminīn Ali, Imam Hasan, Imam Husayn, and Imam Ali ibn al-Husayn in a state where your heart shall be at peace, your soul shall be glad, your eye shall be brightened. When the soul reaches the throat [at death], the angels shall open wide their arms to welcome you.*[^cite-22]

Upon hearing these words, the man was overcome with weeping, his breath catching as tears streamed down his face; he fell to the earth. The gathering too wept at the sight of his transformation. Imam al-Bāqir rose and wiped away the man's tears with his blessed hands. The elder took the Imam's hand, kissed it, and placed it upon his eyes and countenance, then held it to his breast in reverence. Rising to depart, he took leave. As he went, the Imam spoke to those assembled:

*Whosoever among you desires to perceive the people of Paradise, let him gaze upon this Elder.*

## The Grandeur of the Prophet

The life which the Prophet, upon whom be God's peace, lived for forty years before his mission was so exalted that it outweighed the combined lives of all one hundred twenty-four thousand prophets. Thus, when he became a Messenger, the Almighty declared:

> ⟪quran 68:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلَىٰ خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍۢ ‎⟫
> *And verily, you are of an exalted character.*

This verse was revealed when he was forty-one years old. Though his prophethood had just begun with twenty-three years yet to unfold, he was crowned immediately with the mantle of moral excellence. Thus this verse does not speak of the virtues he would display during those remaining twenty-three years of prophecy, but rather celebrates the forty years that preceded his call. His progress was meteoric because his age was an epoch of unrelenting trial. His companions were subjected to severe punishment for uttering the *Kalima* "There is no god but Allah," such that he could not preach openly in Mecca. Yet even in this crucible, he presented a sublime exemplar of conduct and character, one whose weight exceeded that of all the prophets combined. He preached through silence and through deed alone, and the disbelievers would have embraced Islam had they truly beheld his moral radiance. Therefore, in the *Duʿāʾ Hayāt al-Qāf* (the Supplication of the Night Vigil), wherein the miracles of the prophets are invoked, the moral character of the Prophet is especially mentioned—a testament to the supreme excellence of his morality.[^cite-29]

One of the provisions of the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah was that if someone came to the Prophet from Mecca, he might be returned to them or retained; but if anyone came from Medina to Mecca, he must be returned without fail. The Prophet accepted even these inequitable terms. Yet what transpired was remarkable: when a disbeliever came to Medina, he was so moved by the Prophet's character that he embraced Islam, then returned to Mecca as a believer, preaching the faith to his people. Conversely, those who went from the Prophet's city already carried the spirit of Islam. Thus his call was multiplied.

God declares in *Sūrat al-Balad*:

> ⟪quran 90:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَآ أُقْسِمُ بِهَذَا ٱلْبَلَدِ ‎⟫
> *By this city,*

> ⟪quran 90:2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنتَ حِلٌّۢ بِهَذَا ٱلْبَلَدِ ‎⟫
> *and you who dwell in this city—*

In this verse, God swears by Mecca, the city wherein the Sacred House stands, God's own dwelling. Yet God did not say, "By the city in which the House dwells"; instead, God honored the Prophet's life by swearing by the city because of his presence. Such is the elevation granted to him.

This is why, when the Qur'an mentions the prophets, it mentions their failings. It said to Adam, "We do not see in them greatness." To Moses it said, "Remove your shoes, for you stand on holy ground." When Mary was about to give birth, she was commanded to depart that place, for it was a house of worship, not a house of childbirth. But when the Messenger of Allah was mentioned, he was invoked by titles of endearment—Ṭāhā, Yāsīn, al-Muzammil, al-Muddaththir—and God recited eulogies in his honor. All prophets were addressed by their names: "O Moses! O Jesus! O Abraham!" But the Prophet was never thus called; rather, it was always said, "O Muhammad!"[^cite-30]

### The Prophet's Silence

People posed many questions to the Messenger of Allah, yet he never answered save when revelation came, lest he err. He would wait in patient silence for God's word to descend. He cultivated such a deep silence that the Bedouin who came seeking him imagined the one who claimed prophethood must be mute. A hadith testifies: "If tranquility were divided into ten parts, nine would consist of silence and one of speech." This profound quietude was itself a teaching and a model of reflection.

### The Prophet's Status Above the Angels

The Prophet, upon whom be God's peace, declared: "O Ali, if it were not for us, God would not have created Adam, Eve, Paradise, the heavens, or the earth!"[^cite-31] How then could we not be superior to the angels? Yet we have glorified, magnified, and sanctified God before the angels, for God created our souls first. He endowed us with the language of God's unity and praise. Then He created the angels. When the angels beheld our incomparable light, they held us in awe. We glorify the Almighty so that the angels might know that we are His creation and that He alone is glorified above our attributes. Seeing our glorification, the angels glorified Him and magnified Him by means of our attributes.

When the angels perceived our majesty, we proclaimed, "God is One and we are His servants, and there is none to be worshipped save Him, nor any beside Him," so that the angels would know His oneness. Thus the angels recited *Lā ilāha illā-Llāh* (There is no god but Allah). When the angels witnessed the height of our station, we proclaimed *Allāhu Akbar* (God is Most Great), so that the angels might know that God is greater than any station achieved without His cause.

When the angels beheld the power bestowed upon us by God and the obedience owed to us by creation, we said *Subḥān Allāh* (Glory be to God), so that the angels might know that no power exists save through God's cause. When the angels saw the blessings conferred upon us and the obedience required of the people, we proclaimed *Al-ḥamdu li-Llāh* (All praise belongs to God), so that the angels might know that all praise is due to God alone for His blessings. Thus the angels too proclaimed *Al-ḥamdu li-Llāh*. Through us, the angels were taught *Tawḥīd* (God's unity), *Tasbīḥ* (glorification), *Tahlīl* (avowal of oneness), *Tahmīd* (praise), and were thereby guided to the knowledge of God.

### Why the Angels Prostrated to Adam

The Messenger of Allah declared: "God then created Adam and placed us in his loins as a sacred trust, then commanded the angels to prostrate before Adam out of reverence for us." The angels' prostration was an act of worship to God alone; Adam's reception of it was an act of obedience and respect to God, for we were within his being. How could we not surpass the angels when all of them prostrated before Adam?
