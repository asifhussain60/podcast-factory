# Meaning of Dua'im-ul-Islam
*Source: KASHKOLE, Binder 19 (Pillars of Islam: Wilayat), Chapter 86. 2 topics. Language: ur.*<!-- section 1 (id=1063, raw_sort=1): دعائم الاسلام کے باطنی معنی -->Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (a.s.) said: "Baniya al-Islam 'Ali Saba' (Islam is based on seven supplications)." In the world, we will see everything divided into seven, and if it is not divided into seven, we will not see it. A person has seven holes in his face, there are seven letters of Islam, seven days in a week, and so on.

In the spiritual world, the souls turned their ears to the invitation of the spiritual devil and the spiritual devil, which led to seventy (70) spiritual diseases. For the treatment of these diseases, Asher (a.s.) created seventy medicines, which he concealed in seven supplications. Each supplication of Islam is surrounded by ten (10) medicines that cure both the body and the soul so that the diseases of Iblis and Satan can be removed and the soul can return to its original state. The outward prayers of Islam refer to inner realities:

province

Facts

External wilayat refers to the love of Allah, His Messenger and obedience to His commands. In the interpretation of this obligation, the Hikmah says that no human being would have had Wilayat in his heart if he had not loved the True Guides in the world of light. Since the spiritual world developed love for these beings, it was possible to have wilayat from them in this world as well.

In the spiritual world, the name of staying with Tawheed (Tawheed) is Wilayat. The inner wilayat is to offer one's repentance to Asher.

purity

Facts

With purity comes the concept of impurity, because if there is no impurity, there is no need for purity. There are four parts to purity:

We listened to Asher's invitation in the spiritual world and listened to the words of the devil at the same time. We listened to him, not only obeyed his commands, but also invited others to him. Due to these three actions, impurity was created in this world. The esoteric meaning of purity is to distance oneself from the knowledge of Asher and away from the filth of the devil (his dirty thoughts and concepts). In the spiritual world, all our parts were complete and pure, but listening to the word of the devil, acting on it, and calling others to it, changed these parts and they appeared in this world in the form of rhetoric and other impurities. We now have to restore these components ourselves (through prayers) to their original state.

Salawah (Prayer)

Facts

In contrast to the apparent five prayers, there are five people who are the five holy ones (Muhammad, Ali, Fatima, Hasan, Husayn (a.s.). These five people have appeared in every age and in every era, and their obedience has been obligatory on the people in the ages. In the same way, in the spiritual world, the name of Iqamat-ud-Dawa (Standing by Da'wah) is Salat. There were five feasts at Ashir, and all the other points. Various commands were obligatory on those who accepted these invitations, the fulfillment of which is in fact the obligatory prayer.

Zakat

Facts

In the inner spiritual world, when forty parts of the knowledge of Ashir (a.s.) are accumulated, a part of this knowledge is called Zakat. There are different types of zakat:

Zakat:

Zakat is only obligatory on a person whose wealth has accumulated 40 parts for a year. At this time, he is commanded to give one part of Zakat for every forty.

Zakat is for Hujjat al-Zamana, who is the son of the Imam who will become the Imam tomorrow. Zakat is obligatory on 40 dinars after one year. The interpretation of this is that when the children of the Imam (a.s.) gather together in one of the positions (Mu'min, Makasir, Ma'dun, Da'i, etc.), which is the same as one year, one of them is elevated to the highest rank. For example, when there are forty people in the rank of believers, one of them is elevated to the rank of Makasar.

Facts

When the Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.s.) had forty limits, he elevated one (Maulana Salman Farsi) to his invitation and he joined them in his highest limits (Salman Muna Ahl al-Bayt).

Zakat Khums:

If the knowledge of its usefulness is obtained unexpectedly, then a fifth part of it is broadcast, which is called khums. When Sayyidina Isma'il ibn Hibatullah (may Allah be pleased with him) was unexpectedly blessed with knowledge by his Imam, he revealed the khums of these sciences in the form of his book al-Zaytunat al-Thana.

twist

Zakat is for proof, and khums is for the Imam.

Zakat al-Fitr:

These are the sciences that a Muslim can teach to any other Muslim, but it is obligatory for him to tell them. For this reason, fitrah is taken out for every adult and juvenile in the household. When we extend our hands to buy or acquire everyday things (such as food, clothes, etc.), we get what is our own with the blessing of Zakat al-Fitr.

Zakat on Agriculture

Land is an example of Dawat-e-Sharif, and cultivation is the cultivation of faith. The believers are fruits and plants; If the field receives water from the sky and is cultivated through it, then ten (10) percent of the zakat is obligatory on that cultivation. If the water for agriculture is obtained through a stream or well, then there is five (5) percent of the zakat on that cultivation.

That is, if the knowledge and wisdom of the Imam is supported by the Imam, then he is obliged to pay ten (10) percent of the knowledge of the Imam. If a preacher works hard from different books and sources and prepares gatherings with the word of wisdom and knowledge, then it becomes obligatory to pay five (5) percent of the knowledge on that knowledge.

Zakat al-Mashirah (Zakat on animals)

Forty animals are given to one animal in zakat. That is, when forty supplications are gathered at the hands of a Hajjat, he pays Zakat in the way of Allah, i.e., raises his status. Similarly, when forty arguments are collected, the Imam (a.s.) elevates one of them to the rank of the Imam (a.s.) and brings it into chapters.

Fasting (fasting)

Facts

Allaah has enjoined fasting, both outwardly and inwardly, in order to inculcate piety and piety in us. Allah, the Most Merciful and the Merciful, did not intend to keep us hungry and thirsty. In the state of fasting, we refrain from eating, drinking, and intimacy for a certain period of time, even though we can afford to eat and drink. When Ashir imparted knowledge to all his followers in the spiritual world, he made it obligatory for them not to disclose this knowledge to the undeserving (non-deserving) despite their means, and to come in a state of fasting. In the same way, we are also in a state of fasting in this seventy period, because we do not teach the non-believers the knowledge of our Da'wah. When the time of Iftar comes, i.e., when the Mahdi (a.s.) appears, all these facts will be broadcast on the pulpits.

Statement

Human health is maintained by eating and drinking. It is beneficial for him to eat and drink in the other eleven months. But in the city of Allah, the human body is benefited by the hunger and thirst of the day.

Pilgrimage

Facts

The meaning of Hajj is to aspire to the House of Allah and this obligation has been made obligatory on every Muslim and believer once in his life. Allah refers to Imam-uz-Zaman and Bayt (house) refers to his inner chapter which collects all the forms. To make the intention of the House of Allah is to prepare oneself to join the Temple of the Inner Gate. And since the merger of our form in the temple of an Imam will be done only once, Hajj is obligatory only once in life.

Pilgrimage

Going to the house of God once in a lifetime. By Allah, I mean the Imam of the time, and his house is a collection of images that he has (Bab al-Batin). Going for Hajj once in a lifetime means that only a part of it is missed in a person's lifetime. There are five stages of Hajj, from where the pilgrim reaches the Kaaba. There are five chapters of the Imam (a) from which the images reach the Imam. There are five of them: the Imam's four shrines (Bab al-Batin) and one Da'i Island (Bab al-Zahir).

jihad

Facts

Jihad literally means striving and striving. Jihad with the sword is a lowly jihad and jihad with one's self is a great jihad. The true meaning of jihad is to stop one's self from transgressing and to imprison it within the constraints of Islam and truth. For this reason, when the Prophet (s) returned from a battle, he said, "We are returning from the Jihad of the Lower to the Greater Jihad." The people asked, "O Messenger of Allah! What is Jihad Akbar? He said, "Fight with your soul! In order to gain a place in the temple of Imam al-Zaman, it is called jihad to resist the invitation of Iblis and Satan in this world and to go through the stages of patience and hardship.

Jihad

Jihad means to try. Fulfilling the commands of the Imam (a) and confronting Iblis al-Baatin (Nafs Amara Balsu) is an inner jihad.
