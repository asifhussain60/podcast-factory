### گواہ اور گواہی:

## Calculation of (1) and (2) in the Kalima

Counting begins with one, which indicates the **عقل اول** (First Intellect). Out of countless souls, only one affirmed the **توحید رب العالمین** (Oneness of the Lord of the Worlds), hence counting starts with one. The number one dominates the entire Kalima and is not included in the count, rather it is the one who counts! It is an entity (**عاد**), not a number.

Firstly, the count of two appears in the Kalima-e-Tawhid (Declaration of Faith). This is because in the Kalima-e-Tawhid, **لا إله** (There is no god) is negation, and **إلا الله** (except Allah) is affirmation. So, this Kalima is a combination of negation and affirmation. The **عقل اول** created two relationships in affirming the **توحید رب العالمین**. From the higher relationship (**نسبت اعلی**) emerged the **منبعث اول** (First Emanation), and from the lower relationship (**نسبت ادنی**) emerged the **منبعث ثانی** (Second Emanation). These two entities are opposites of negation and affirmation, while the **عقل اول** itself is greater than both because it is the cause of their existence.

### Negation (لا إله) Affirmation (إلا الله)
1. **(نسبت إلی ذاته)** When the **عقل اول** affirmed the Oneness, transcendence, and abstraction of the Creator, it first used negation and said that neither was it born itself, nor did it create anyone. **(نسبت إلی من أوجده)** After that, it used affirmation in Tawhid and said that there is someone else who created it and all other entities, who is One.
2. The one who testifies is mortal like negation. The one for whom testimony is being given (the Exalted Creator) is eternal like affirmation.
3. Negating the attributes that exist in the physical and spiritual worlds. Affirming the essence that is unique in its essence and cannot be compared to anything.
4. Uttering words with the tongue, which is mortal. Affirming with the heart, which is eternal.

This Kalima also points to the fact that whatever apparently exists, when it is attributed to Allah Ta'ala, it becomes negated. Meaning that thing either does not exist in reality, or is going to cease to exist, and Allah Ta'ala is the one who remains and endures because He is the true King of existence and non-existence. He is the one who created existence from non-existence.

### Negation before Affirmation in Order:

This means the aforementioned testimony is in accordance with two types of creations, one of which is physical and dense (the physical world which is mortal like negation), and the other is subtle and spiritual (eternal like affirmation). And the God for whom this testimony is, is the creator of both these creations.

### Negation (لا إله) Affirmation (إلا الله)
1. Humans can first understand other humans like themselves, meaning they can only understand the physical world first, and only after that can they understand the spiritual world.
2. Human effort is first in the physical state, later they will go into the spiritual state.
3. Negation of one's own self, that there are no abilities or qualities in me, whatever good there is, it is all due to the grace of Allah Ta'ala, and all praise is due to Him.

Negation before affirmation in the Kalima-e-Shahadat (Declaration of Faith) points to the fact that until we negate ourselves, we cannot achieve affirmation. Just as we cannot affirm the One True God by saying **إلا الله** until we deny all other gods by saying **لا إله**.

Until the mineral negates itself and becomes finely ground, it cannot be absorbed into the roots of the plant, meaning it cannot achieve affirmation. Until the plant bows down before the animal and negates itself, it cannot become its food and achieve affirmation. Until the human negates himself by submitting to the one above him (**من فوق**, Higher Limit), he cannot merge into His form and achieve affirmation. (Kitab Wajah Din)

### الہ (ا + ل + ہ) کے ممثولات

الف:

الف ⟪ar:معدن⟫پر دلالت کرتا ہے۔ الف، حروف تہجی کا پہلا حرف ہے اور ⟪ar:علم الجمل⟫کے حساب سے اس کا عدد ایک ہے ۔ اسی طرح معدن کا درجہ بھی موالید میں پہلا ہے۔ دوسری بات یہ کے معدن اسفل زمین کی طرف کھنچتا ہے (سوائے اس کے کہ کوئی انسان اس کو اوپر کی طرف کھینچ لائے)۔ اسی طرح الف کی حرکت اوپر سے نیچے کی طرف ہوتی ہے۔ ⟪ar:اِلہ⟫میں الف کے نیچے زیر ہے جو بتا رہا ہے کے معدن زمین کے نیچے ہوتی ہے، اوپر کی طرف نہیں۔

لام:
