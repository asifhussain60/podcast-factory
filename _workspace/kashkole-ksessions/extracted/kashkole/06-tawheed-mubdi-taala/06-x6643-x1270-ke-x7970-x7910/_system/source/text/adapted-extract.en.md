## Five Chapters of the Word of Tawheed

The Messenger reports that there are five chapters and twenty-eight limits contained within the Kalima Tawhid ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ (the Declaration of Faith: "There is no god but Allah"). Each chapter corresponds to a distinct numerical structure: the first unfolds in two aspects (negative and positive); the second comprises three essences formed from three common letters; the third consists of four words; the fourth divides into seven chapters; and the fifth contains twelve letters. These numerical progressions—2, 3, 4, 7, and 12—summing to twenty-eight, establish the foundational architecture of divine truth.

| Five Chapters | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| *Ulu al-ʿAzm* (The Possessors of Determination) | Noah | Abraham | Moses | Jesus | Muhammad |
| *Panjtan-i Pak* (The Pure Five) | Muhammad | ʿAli | Fatima | Hasan | Husayn |
| Aspect of Kalima | Proof and Denial | Essence | Words | Chapters | Letters |
| Numerical Value | 2 | 3 | 4 | 7 | 12 |

These five chapters correspond to the five *Ulu al-ʿAzm* prophets (Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad), and equally to the sacred *Panjtan-i Pak* (Muhammad, ʿAli, Fatima, Hasan, and Husayn). Only by passing through these five gates—each representing a spiritual dimension—can the believer attain true understanding and develop genuine *ikhlas* (sincerity) of faith.[^cite-1]

Each member of the *Panjtan-i Pak* constitutes a great gate in the spiritual sense, and each gate contains many smaller openings. Thus, Muhammad possesses two gates, ʿAli three gates, Fatima four gates, Hasan seven gates, and Husayn twelve gates. In total, the *Panjtan-i Pak* comprises twenty-eight gates through which the faithful must pass. God has established the entire creation upon this number twenty-eight; in the spiritual hierarchy, the Second Intellect similarly commanded a circle of twenty-eight, and twenty-eight souls joined his gathering.

### Explanations of the Limits of the Kalima Tawhid

The numerical framework of the Kalima reveals a complete cosmological structure. The number one signifies the Kalima itself and, in its absolute unity, stands opposed to multiplicity; it is odd and singular. The number two indicates the negation and affirmation contained in the Kalima—an argument touching the divine essence, for the intellect itself was born from the conjunction of higher and lower relations relative to the Absolute; it is even and singular. The number three, emerging from the union of one and two (1 + 2), denotes the three essences of the Kalima and is odd and composite; its compositeness marks the dual nature of existence—the spiritual and the physical. The number four, produced from the three essences through the intellect's triple gems, is even and composite, indicating the four physical foundations. The number seven, arising from the pairing of the *natiq* (the Speaking Prophet) and the *asas* (the Foundation) through 3 + 4, is odd and composite; it denotes the seven Imams (al-ʾAʾimma al-Kirām) present in each era, corresponding to the seven divisions of both the spiritual and physical orders.[^cite-2] The number twelve, derived from the multiplication 3 × 4, signifies the twelve letters of the Kalima and points to the twelve proofs (*hujjaj*) appointed by each Imam. The number five, which circulates hidden within the Kalima, manifests through the five chapters (2, 3, 4, 7, 12), through the addition of odd and even pairs (1 + 4 and 2 + 3), and through the appearance of *alif* (ا) five times, *lam* (ل) five times, and *ha* (ه) twice within the declaration. This number is called the "revolving number," for whenever an odd number is multiplied by five, the result always ends in five. Finally, the addition of the four primary numbers (1 + 2 + 3 + 4) yields ten, the number of perfection, corresponding to the ten intellects that constitute the celestial hierarchy.

## Exposition of the Two Components of the Kalima

The Kalima Tawhid comprises two distinct yet inseparable parts: the first is the negation ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫ ("there is no god"), and the second is the affirmation ⟪ar:الا اللہ⟫ ("except Allah"). The first component itself divides into two elements—⟪ar:لا⟫ ("not"), which is negative, and ⟪ar:الہ⟫ ("god"), which carries affirmative force. Similarly, the second component contains two letters: ⟪ar:الا⟫ ("except"), holding negation within it, and ⟪ar:اللہ⟫ ("Allah"), standing as pure affirmation. Within the first component, the second word ⟪ar:الہ⟫ is elevated, while within the second component, ⟪ar:اللہ⟫ is higher still. The negation ⟪ar:لا⟫ combined with ⟪ar:الہ⟫ refers to the *ḥudūd al-sufliyya* (the lower limits) of the physical realm, in which ⟪ar:الہ⟫ represents the highest boundary and ⟪ar:لا⟫ the limit adjacent and inferior to it. The second part of the Kalima—⟪ar:الا اللہ⟫—points toward the *ḥudūd al-ʿuluwiyya* (the upper limits), of which ⟪ar:اللہ⟫ is supreme, and ⟪ar:الا⟫ marks the limit close and contiguous to them.

Although these two parts of the Kalima are connected to their successors, the words that follow are superior to both. Within each component, the letters of the inferior word are beneath their higher counterparts. The first component ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫ contains two letters, while the second component ⟪ar:الا اللہ⟫ has three and four letters respectively. The essence of the entire Kalima is the union of three letters from which the complete word is composed: ⟪ar:ا ل ہ⟫.

## First Chapter: Negation and Affirmation

The first chapter of the Kalima Tawhid is constituted by negation ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫ ("there is no god") and affirmation ⟪ar:الا اللہ⟫ ("except Allah"). The Kalima stands as a testimony to the existence and uniqueness of Allah the Almighty. Within this testimony, the person becomes the witness, while Allah becomes the one to whom testimony is borne—the servant witnesses God, and God is the witnessed. Every testimony rests upon three elements: the beginning, the middle, and the end.

| Beginning | Middle | End |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Witness | The Word of Sincerity | Allah Almighty |

### Witnesses and Testimony

In the exoteric law (*shari'a al-zahir*) of Islam, a believer may not testify to what he has not witnessed with his own eyes. Yet in the inner religion (*din al-batin*), a person may bear witness on behalf of another if two just witnesses have already testified, allowing a deputy witness to corroborate their testimony. Similarly, the Messenger of Allah could not have beheld Allah directly—such vision is impossible—yet two just witnesses, the *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* (the First Intellect) and the *al-ʿAql al-Thani* (the Second Intellect), testified to the Lord. Their very emanations speak clearly of the Lord of the Universe, that none exists but ⟪ar:اللہ⟫ Him, until the Holy Prophet himself confirmed their testimony with truth upon truth.[^cite-3] Testimony divides into two kinds:

**True Testimony** consists in affirming what is genuinely the case: declaring what actually belongs to the subject and negating what genuinely does not belong. For example, if Rashid owns one cow and two goats, true testimony takes these forms:

| True Testimony (Affirmation) | True Testimony (Negation) |
| --- | --- |
| Rashid has a cow. | Rashid does not have two cows. |
| Rashid has two goats. | Rashid does not have three goats. |

**False Testimony** affirms what is not in fact the case or negates what genuinely is:

| False Testimony (Affirmation) | False Testimony (Negation) |
| --- | --- |
| Rashid has two cows. | Rashid does not have a cow. |
| Rashid has a goat. | Rashid does not have two goats. |

(*Kitab al-Burhan*, Book of Reasoning)

### Calculation of One and Two in the Kalima

Counting begins with one, signifying the *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* (the First Intellect). From countless souls, only one spirit attained *Tawhid Rabb al-ʿAlamin* (the Oneness of the Lord of the Worlds), thus counting commences at one. The number one dominates the entire Kalima and is not included in the enumeration itself, but rather it is the enumerator—not a number, but the principle of number.

First among the counts within the Kalima appears two. In the Kalima-e-Tawhid, ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫ ("there is no god") is negation, while ⟪ar:الا اللہ⟫ ("except Allah") is affirmation; thus the Kalima blends negation and affirmation. The *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* created two relations in affirming *Tawhid Rabb al-ʿAlamin*. From the higher relation (*nisba ʿali*) emerged the *al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal* (the First Emanator); from the lower relation (*nisba adna*) came the *al-Munbaʿith al-Thani* (the Second Emanator). These two beings are opposites—negation and affirmation—yet the *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* itself transcends both, being the cause of their very existence.[^cite-4]

|  | Negation ("There is no god") | Affirmation ("Except Allah") |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | When the First Intellect affirmed the Oneness, transcendence, and abstraction of the Creator Most Holy, it first acted through negation, saying: "I was not born of myself, nor did I create any being." Then it acted through affirmation in unity, declaring: "There exists Another who created me and all beings—One alone." | |
| 2 | The witness—he who testifies—is mortal, like negation. | The witnessed—he for whom testimony is borne—is eternal, like affirmation. |
| 3 | Negation of the attributes existing in physical and spiritual worlds. | Affirmation of the essence that is unique in itself, having no peer or precedent. |
| 4 | Utterance through the tongue, which is mortal. | Attestation from the heart, which is eternal. |

This Kalima also conveys that whatever appears to exist becomes negated when compared to Allah. That thing either does not truly exist or is destined to perish, whereas Allah endures eternally—the true King of Being, who created existence from non-existence.

### Negation Before Affirmation in Sequence

The testimony unfolds in keeping with two types of creation: one is physical and dense (the material world, mortal like negation); the other is subtle and spiritual (eternal like affirmation). Allah, to whom this testimony is addressed, is the Creator of both.

|  | Negation ("There is no god") | Affirmation ("Except Allah") |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Humans first understand beings like themselves—they first grasp the physical world, only afterward ascending to the spiritual. | |
| 2 | Human effort begins in the physical state, progressing later into the spiritual state. | |
| 3 | Negation of oneself: I possess no qualities or excellence; whatever good exists, it flows from Allah's grace, and all praise belongs to Him. | |

In the Kalima-e-Shahada, the ordering of negation before affirmation teaches that until we negate ourselves, affirmation cannot be achieved. Just as we cannot affirm the One True God through ⟪ar:الا اللہ⟫ until we have negated all other gods through ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫. The mineral cannot be absorbed into the plant's roots until it negates its essence and becomes refined. The plant cannot become food for the animal until it submits and denies itself before the animal. The human cannot merge into the higher form unless he negates himself by submitting to the one above him—the *ḥadd ʿālī* (the upper limit)—and thereby achieves affirmation. (*Kitab Wajh-i Din*)

## Second Chapter: Three Essences

Testimony corresponds to the enumeration of one and two—negation comes first, affirmation second. These are the numbers of one and two, spiritual and individual. The sum of one and two forms the number three, which is composite and odd. This composite three is derived from the non-composite numbers one and two. When the *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* (the First Intellect) issued the *daʿwa* (the invitation) to the *al-ʿAql al-Thani* (the Second Intellect) and to the Messenger (may blessings and peace be upon him), he brought forth seven additional intellects. The first three of these seven swiftly accepted the invitation, and thus the number three manifested. For this reason, when circumambulating the Kaaba, the first three *shawts* (circuits) are performed swiftly, while the last four are undertaken slowly.[^cite-5]

## Third Chapter: Four Words

In the numerical sequence, three is followed by four, produced by the union of two and three. Four, which is *jift murakkab* (composite even), is generated from two (*jift mahz*, individual even) and three (*tāq murakkab*, composite odd). When the force of negation and affirmation manifested in the three essences, four words emerged: ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ ("There is no god but Allah"). The Kalima found completion in the number four, and no fifth word was added. The first division is perfected through the synthesis of these four words, in which both odd and even principles are made complete. All subsequent numbers arise from their repetition and combination. Things are either odd or even. When these four principles were established, the origin of the universe reached its culmination.

The four rivers of Paradise in the spiritual circle represent the four words of the Kalima of testimony ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫. Pointing to this truth, the Messenger declared: "Whoever says 'There is no god but Allah' shall enter Paradise."

### The River of Water

Water is the source of all life, as Allah affirms: ⟪ar-quote:وَجَعَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلْمَآءِ كُلَّ شَىْءٍ حَی⟫ ("We have made from water every living thing"). Water corresponds to *al-ʿAql al-Kulli* (the Universal Intellect). Just as the mingling of water with soil brings forth flora and fauna, so too have all the *hudud* (limits)—both the *ʿuluwiyya* (upper) and *sufliyya* (lower)—been created through the union of the intellect with the word of testimony. This water flowing from the stream of intellect shall never grow stale or corrupted. The proof of this truth lies in reason itself: when the rational mind knows something, it retains that knowledge in its original state forever; the object of knowledge does not alter in rational perception. Water, in its essential nature, is cold and moist. No matter how much heat it seems temporarily to accept, reason perceives its true essence as eternally cold and wet, forever seeing it in its authentic state.

### The River of Milk

The milk river signifies *al-Nafs al-Kulli* (the Universal Soul). Milk is the nourishment of every newborn, and through milk, the mother ensures the offspring is of her own kind—its lineage remains unchanged. Likewise, *al-Nafs al-Kulli*, the soul-principle of creation, was established so that through its synthesis, a son emerged who could inherit all its benefits. He belongs to the genus *Qaʾim al-Qiyama ʿala Dhikrih al-Salam* (the Resurrector, peace be upon him).

### The River of Wine

By wine, Allah signifies *al-Natiq* (the Speaking Prophet). Wine provides the body with strength, inducing states of wonder or confusion. By following the *Sharia* prescribed by the *al-Natiq*, a person preserves his virtues and disciplines his desires—Allah has likened this to the pleasure of wine. Since *al-Natiq* is the author and compiler of the *Shari'a*, his utterances may provoke discord among people, astonishing and misleading some, just as wine intoxicates and leads astray.

### The River of Honey

The honey river signifies *al-Asas* (the Foundation), which is sweet and delightful and possesses the power to heal the discord caused by wine. It also strengthens the vital constitution. Similarly, from *al-Asas* flows the *taʾwil* (esoteric interpretation) of the Book and *Shari'a*, through which wonder and disagreement are dissolved and the truth emerges radiant. 

The righteous to whom Allah has promised Paradise are the seven Imams and the twelve proofs (*hujjaj*).

## Fourth Chapter: Seven Divisions

In enumeration, four is followed by seven, derived by adding the composite odd number three to the composite even number four. These are the seven divisions or chapters of the Kalima of Sincerity. They are the seven intellects brought forth through the *daʿwa* (invitation) of the *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* (the First Intellect) to the *al-ʿAql al-Thani* (the Second Intellect).

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| La | A | Lah | A | La | A | Lillah |

|  | Three—Composite Odd | + | Four—Composite Even | = | Seven (7) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Kalima | Alif, Lam, Ha |  | La, Ilaha, Illa, Allah |  | Seven Chapters (La, A, Lah, A, La, A, Lillah) |
| Cosmic Order | Length, Width, Depth |  | Fire, Air, Water, Soil |  | Celestial Spheres (Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon) |
| Spiritual Hierarchy | Grandfather, Victory, Imagination |  | Speaker, Foundation, Imam, Proof |  | Imams (The Seven Imams) |

The Kalima of Testimony is composed of three letters ⟪ar:ا ل ہ⟫, and its chapters divide into three categories: three chapters consist of one letter each, three chapters of two letters each, and one chapter of three letters.

| One-Letter Chapters | Two-Letter Chapters | Three-Letter Chapter |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A, A, A | La, La, La | Lillah |
| Plants | Animals | Humans |

The creatures of this world bear witness to this same truth, existing on three levels: those possessed of souls—plants, animals, and humans.

**Botanical Testimony:** The testimony of plants exemplifies the three one-letter chapters, for plants possess only one faculty: the *namiʾa* (the vegetative power). Yet despite having this single power, plants appear in three types: seedless grasses, seed-bearing grasses, and flowering trees. These exemplify the three one-letter chapters.

**Animal Testimony:** Animals exemplify the three two-letter chapters, for they possess two faculties: *namiʾa* (vegetative) and *hasisa* (sensory). Animals divide into three types corresponding to the two-letter chapters: land animals, sea animals, and flying animals.

**Human Testimony:** Humans exemplify the chapter of three letters, for mankind possesses three faculties: *namiʾa* (vegetative), *hasisa* (sensory), and *natiqa* (rational). There is only one species of three-lettered humans.

| Letters of the Kalima | Planets | Categories | Faculties |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| (1) One-letter chapters | A, A, A | 3 | Flora (*Namiyyah*—Vegetative) |
| (2) Two-letter chapters | La, La, La | 3 | Animals (*Namiyyah*, *Hasiyyah*—Vegetative, Sensory) |
| (3) Three-letter chapter | Lillah | 1 | Humans (*Namiyyah*, *Hasiyyah*, *Natiqah*—Vegetative, Sensory, Rational) |

The seven chapters and twelve letters of the Kalima thus signify the seven Imams of each era and the twelve proofs established by them.

### The Seven-Day Imprisonment

The seven chapters of the Kalima correspond to the seven *ʿuqul* (intellects), the seven Imams of the spiritual world, and the seven Imams of the physical age. We are bound within the week of our age and cannot exit it. We shall only emerge from the weeks of confinement at true death—when we transcend this physical existence through the *Qaim al-Zaman* (the Resurrector of the Age) and turn toward the spiritual world.

## Islam and the Seven Chapters of the Kalima Ikhlas

Every believer must know Islam, for the religion of Islam is itself the proclamation of *ikhlas* (sincerity). Just as none may enter Paradise without knowledge of the Kalima Ikhlas, so too can none enter the fold of Islam without understanding its inner reality. The word *al-Islam* ⟪ar:الاسلام⟫ contains seven letters, corresponding to the seven chapters of the Kalima Ikhlas and, in another sense, denoting the seven Imams of the present age.

### Islam and Religious Law

It is erroneous to suppose that each prophet brought a wholly different law. The *Shari'a* has remained constant, as Allah declares in the Qur'an: "Verily, the religion with Allah is *al-Islam*."

> ⟪quran 3:19⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱلدِّينَ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ ٱلْإِسْلَمُ ۗ وَمَا ٱخْتَلَفَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ إِلَّا مِنۢ بَعْدِ مَا جَآءَهُمُ ٱلْعِلْمُ بَغْيًۢا بَيْنَهُمْ ۗ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِـَٔايَتِ ٱللَّهِ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ سَرِيعُ ٱلْحِسَابِ ‎⟫
> *Truly, the religion with Allah is Islam. Those who were given the Scripture did not differ except, out of mutual jealousy, after knowledge had come to them. And whoever disbelieves in the Ayat of Allah, then surely Allah is Swift in calling to account.*
> — **Quran 3:19 (inline widget)**

Each prophet retained the law of his predecessor but renewed it in fresh forms. By preserving ancient ordinances while clothing them in new garments, each prophet elevated and strengthened the old laws in accord with the growing understanding of his people, until the Prophet Muhammad (may blessings and peace be upon him) brought the *Shari'a* to its perfect completion. The marks and ordinances of the five *Ulu al-ʿAzm* (Possessors of Determination) were subsumed and perfected within his revelation. ### All Prophets Came with the Same Law

Allah states in His Book:

> ⟪quran 35:25⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِن يُكَذِّبُوكَ فَقَدْ كَذَّبَ ٱلَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِهِمْ جَآءَتْهُمْ رُسُلُهُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ وَبِٱلزُّبُرِ وَبِٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلْمُنِيرِ ‎⟫
> *And if they belie you, those before them also belied. Their Messengers came to them with clear signs, and with the Psalms, and with the illuminating Book.*
> — **Quran 35:25 (inline widget)**

When Allah mentions the prophets, He refers to them as one body, for while the manifest laws (*ahkam zahirah*) of their *Shari'a* differ, their inner meaning is unified. When He describes the People of the Book, He calls them one, designating the Book as illuminating, because the inner reality of all the sacred books and the principles of all *Shari'as* are identical. The term *bayyinah* (clear signs) refers to miraculous proofs and evident demonstrations, while *zubur* (scriptures) signifies books, texts, knowledge, and wisdom. In mentioning the signs and sciences brought by the prophets before Muhammad, the plural is used; but the Book is mentioned in the singular, proving that all possess one and the same inner truth.[^cite-6]

(*Majlis* of Hazrat Abdullah ibn Hibatullah)

## Calculation of Five and Ten in the Kalima Ikhlas

When the composite odd (3) is added to the composite even (4), or the composite odd (2 + 3) is added to the pure even, the sum yields five (5). This number is called the *ʿadad daʾir* (the revolving number), for the number five always works in circles: multiplying five by five returns to five at the end. When these four primary numbers are added together (1 + 2 + 3 + 4), the result is ten (10), indicating the ten limits, of which five are subtle and spiritual—*al-Qalam* (the Pen), *al-Lawh* (the Tablet), *al-Jadd* (the Grandfather), *al-Fath* (the Victory), and *al-Khayal* (the Imagination)—and five are physical and manifest—*al-Natiq* (the Speaker), *al-Asas* (the Foundation), *al-Imam* (the Imam), *al-Hujja* (the Proof), and *al-Daʿi* (the Caller). In the same manner, humanity—the fruit of the cosmos, the microcosm uniting soul and body—rests upon ten forces: five subtle and five dense and manifest.

|  | Letters | Human Faculties | Designation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 5 | Alif | Subtle Powers of Intellect, Mind, Care, Memorization, Remembrance | Inner Senses |
| 5 | Lam | Dense Powers: Hearing, Sight, Smell, Taste, Touch | External Senses |
| 2 | Ha | Soul and Body | Soul (subtle) and Body (dense) |

The highest letter, *Alif* ⟪ar:الف⟫, in the Kalima ⟪ar:لاالہ الا اللہ⟫ stands opposite to the five spiritual beings: *al-ʿAql* (Intellect), *al-Nafs* (Soul), *al-Jadd* (Grandfather), *al-Fath* (Victory), and *al-Khayal* (Imagination). These five attained perfection and do not appear in this physical world; they are non-recurring. Then comes the status of *Lam*, which recurs and stands opposite to the five physical manifestations: *al-Imam*, *al-Bab* (the Gate), *al-Hujja*, *al-Daʿi*, and *al-Mazun*. These are beings who come repeatedly into this world. The interaction of these recurring individuals with the non-recurring species continues until, through development, the recurring become non-recurring. The purpose of *Lam* is to become *Alif*. Following this is the status of *Ha*, appearing twice, signifying two further categories: *al-Muʾmin* (the Believer) and *al-Makasir* (the Broken One).[^cite-8]

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Alif | Intellect | Soul | Grandfather | Victory | Imagination |
| Lam | Imam | Gate | Proof | Caller | Guide |

The Kalima of Testimony conforms to the composition of the universe as a whole. For the world has been established in composition to manifest humanity; the human is the fruit and pinnacle of the physical cosmos. Similarly, the Kalima of Testimony was established to manifest the Name of Allah, as the whole of the testimony signifies the utterance of Allah's Name. It is as though the very purpose of human birth is nothing but witnessing to God. Just as the purpose of creation of the universe is humanity, so too the purpose of the Kalima of Testimony is the Name of Allah. In other words, the Name of Allah and the human are kindred realities.

The word ⟪ar:لا⟫ ("not") comprises two letters, while ⟪ar:الہ⟫ ("god") comprises three. Within ⟪ar:لا⟫, the letters *lam* and *alif* represent heaven and earth. The word ⟪ar:الہ⟫ consists of three letters signifying the *mawālid al-thulatha* (three births or kingdoms): mineral, plant, and animal.

### Analogues of "Ilah" (Alif + Lam + Ha)

**Alif:** This letter signifies the *miʿdan* (the mineral kingdom). *Alif* is the first letter of the alphabet with the numerical value of one in *ʿilm al-jummal* (the science of numerical equivalences). Similarly, the mineral holds the first station in the natural births. Second, minerals gravitate toward earth (unless a human intervenes to lift them upward). In the same way, the movement of *Alif* descends from top to bottom. The *Alif* is positioned below the *Ha*, indicating that the mineral kingdom lies beneath the earth rather than above it.

**Lam:** This letter represents the *nabat* (the plant kingdom), the second stage of natural being. Just as plants emerge from the earth and grow upward toward the surface, so does *Lam* move upward from the bottom (earth) toward the top, pulling existence upward. The numerical value of *Lam* is 30, written as 3 and 0. The zero signifies the first degree—the mineral—which lacks the three dimensions. The numeral 3 indicates that the plant has transcended the mineral degree and entered the vegetative, which possesses growth in three dimensions: length, width, and depth.

| 0 | 3 |
| --- | --- |
| Mineral (left behind) | Plant (entered) |

**Ha:** This letter signifies the animal kingdom, the third stage of natural being, which dominates and encompasses the previous two. The letter *Ha* consists of an *alif* with a loop, where the *alif* extended upward takes the shape of vegetation. The rounded and ambiguous form of the loop denotes the mineral kingdom, lacking the clear definition found in plants and animals. Thus *Ha* encompasses both mineral and plant. There are five aspects indicated by the numeral value of *Ha*, representing the five qualities that the animal possesses above the absolute mineral, which humans gradually attain:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Body | Vegetative Soul | Sensory Soul | Rational Soul | Authoritative Soul |

In humanity exist the powers of *jasad* (body), *nafs namiʾa* (vegetative soul), and *nafs hasisa* (sensory soul), while the *nafs natiqa* (rational soul) and *nafs malikiyya* (authoritative soul) remain latent potencies that manifest in their appointed times.

### Summary

It is thus clear that the first two words of the Kalima of Testimony—⟪ar:لا الہ⟫—encompass the entire cosmos. They refer to the earth and the heavens and everything between them. The word ⟪ar:لا⟫ negates, and ⟪ar:الہ⟫ affirms the Divine. It is as though the first part of this testimony explains that everything in the heavens, the earth, and the universe—from the heights above to the depths below, encompassing the spiritual, the physical, and the whole, whether small or great, lowest or highest—none of them possesses divinity (*ilahiyya*).

## Analogues of the Order of Essences

There are twenty-one letters between *Alif* ⟪ar:الف⟫ and *Lam* ⟪ar:لام⟫ in the alphabetical sequence. However, in the Kalima Ikhlas ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫, *Lam* comes first and *Alif* second, indicating that the first thing affecting a person is the *nafs* (soul) represented by *Lam*; initially, the person is ignorant. Only subsequently does *ʿaql* (intellect), represented by *Alif*, arrive, bringing wisdom. This sequence also teaches that the *nafs* can only be perfected through *ʿaql*. That is, we can only attain true knowledge from the *asas* (foundation), who holds the position of *nafs al-kulli* (the Universal Soul) in relation to the *ʿaql al-kulli* (the Universal Intellect) and *al-natiq* (the Speaking Prophet).

The twenty-one letters between *Alif* and *Lam* correspond to the scholar's twenty-one limits: *al-natiq*, *al-asas*, the seven Imams, and the twelve proofs. In the same way, in *ʿalam al-tarkib* (the world of composition, the spiritual realm), between *al-ʿaql* (the intellect) and its full manifestation lie twenty-one limits: *al-sura* (form), the seven celestial spheres, *al-hayula* (the prima materia), and the twelve constellations. And in humanity, corresponding to these twenty-one, are *al-jasad* (body), *al-ruh* (spirit), the seven organs, and the twelve vessels.
