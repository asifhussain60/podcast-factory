## The Twenty-Eight Limits of the Heavens

The cosmos is constituted by two encompassing domains: one manifest to the senses, the physical world of creation, and one concealed from ordinary perception, the spiritual realm. The visible sphere comprises those worlds contained within the celestial belt, bound by three spatial dimensions—a domain defined by opposition to the individual self. The invisible sphere, by contrast, embraces the celestial spheres themselves and the worlds transcending them, extending beyond the confines of three-dimensionality—a domain that stands in opposition to the manifest universe itself. The vastness of the heavens is boundless and infinite; it constitutes the dwelling place of the Holy Spirits (al-Arwāḥ al-Muqaddasa). What we call circumference denotes that which cannot be encompassed by measure, yet it also refers to the one who encompasses all things—and this encompassing reality belongs to Allāh alone.[^cite-1]

| --- | --- | --- |

| Who is surrounded | Who was not surrounded | Who surrounds without being surrounded |

## The Twenty-Eight Limits of the Spiritual World

The twelve angels who sustain the Throne and those ranged in its vicinity are called the *Mudbbarrūn* (the Managers or Orderers of affairs). Their entire purpose is to draw forth the light from this material sphere and restore it to the spiritual realm. The ascent and descent of souls traverses four distinct states: *Ibdāʿ* (Innovation or emanation), representing the soul's motion beyond itself in the spiritual world; *Qiyāma* (Resurgence), the state of those who perceive this movement; *Hawl* (the Reversal), the condition of descent in which the world transitions from the spiritual to the material; and *Wajh* (the Face or Orientation), the state of awakening and ascension wherein one progresses from the material back to the spiritual world.[^cite-2]

| Innovation | Who is Moving Beyond Himself in the Spiritual World |
| --- | --- |
| Resurgence | Those who see it |
| Howly | The post-fall state in which the world went from the spiritual to the physical world |
| Face | The state of awakening and ascending back in which you will progress from the physical world to the spiritual world |

## The Twenty-Eight Limits of Jihād

Faith (*Īmān*) is constituted of three elements: the sincere affirmation of the heart, the confession of the tongue, and the righteous action of the body. Jihād (spiritual struggle) has likewise been made obligatory upon these same three dimensions of the human person. A *Mujtahid* is one who subdues the soul (nafs) and places behind him—renouncing utterly—all words and deeds that contradict the commandments of the higher intellect (al-ʿAql al-ʿUlyā).[^cite-3] 

The Prophet, peace be upon him, taught that we must forbid what has been presented in the guise of the permitted—such as slaughtering an animal by shock rather than by the proper ritual method. Conversely, forbidden things are those forbidden in their essential nature, such as the consumption of swine. There are grades of opposition to faith and obedience:

| Māriqu | Those who depart from the faith |
| Ḍāllū | The misguided |

| Kāfirū | The deniers of truth |
| Fāsiqū | Those who commit evil deeds |
| Kāsṭū | Those who refuse compliance |
| Jāḥidū | Those who deny the evident |
| Kafirīn | The unbelievers |
| Ṭāghūn | The tyrants |
| Kadhdhābū | The liars |
| Munāfiqūn | The hypocrites |

## The Twenty-Eight Limits of the Word *Allāh*

The divine name *Allāh* enfolds within itself a supreme numerical and mystical symbolism. When the letters *Alif*, *Lām*, *Lām*, and *Hā* are arranged according to their numerical values (in the *Abjad* system), they yield the following structure: *Alif* equals 1 (representing *Qayyat al-Qiyāma*, the sustainer of existence); the first *Lām* equals 30 (signifying the three messengers bearing ten perfect forms each—Adam, Noah, and Abraham, *ʿalayhim al-salām*); the second *Lām* equals 30 (signifying three further messengers bearing ten perfect forms each—Moses, Jesus, and Muḥammad, *ʿalayhim al-salām*); and *Hā* equals 5 (representing the Panjtan Pāk, the Five Pure Ones).[^cite-4]

When five is multiplied by itself, yielding twenty-five, this number demonstrates that the Five shall manifest in every cycle of spiritual time (*dawr*), eternally and perpetually. Their union is thus expressed:

| A | LL | E |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 6 | 5 | = | 12 |
| *Aḥad* (The One) | *Ibrāhīm* (Abraham) | *al-Panjtan* (The Five Pure Ones) | | *al-Ithnā ʿAshar* (The Twelve Imāms) |

In the ultimate sense, *Allāh* is the great name supreme, yet the true name—the innermost reality from which all names emanate—is none other than *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* (the First Intellect), the first source and origin from which all creation unfolds.[^cite-5]
