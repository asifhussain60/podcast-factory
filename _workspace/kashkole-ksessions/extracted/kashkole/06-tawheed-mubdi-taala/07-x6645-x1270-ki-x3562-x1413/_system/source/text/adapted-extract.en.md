## The Twenty-Eight Limits of the Heavens

The universe manifests in two domains: one visible to the eye, and one imperceptible to the senses. The visible domain comprises the physical worlds situated within or below the celestial sphere, bounded by three spatial dimensions—a realm opposed to the individual self. The invisible domain encompasses the celestial spheres and all realms transcending them, unbound by dimensional constraint—a realm opposed to the finite cosmos. The vastness of the heavens is boundless and eternal, the dwelling place of the Holy Spirits. The term "circumference" (falak muḥīṭ) denotes that which cannot be enclosed, and simultaneously refers to the One who encloses all things—none but Allāh.[^cite-1]

### Doors of the Gate of Muḥammad (upon him be peace)

The threshold of Muḥammad's gate opens upon the recognition that the cosmos operates through forces both manifest and concealed. These forces sustain the rotation of worlds and the ordering of the celestial mechanics by which all things cohere.

## The Twenty-Eight Limits of Islam

### Denial and Proof

The affirmation of divine unity (tawḥīd) requires both the negation of false claims and the affirmation of truth. Just as no edifice stands without foundation, so too does the path to Allāh's oneness demand the clearing away of all pretenders to divinity before the assertion of the sole Real can take hold in the heart.

### Doors of the Gate of ʿAlī (3)

Three letters are essential to the formulaic expression *Lā ilāha illā-Allāh*: the letters Alif (ا), Lam (ل), and Hāʾ (ه). Should any of these be omitted or foreign letters introduced, the word loses its integrity and power; similarly, when the Day of Resurrection arrives, all things shall be unmade. The three cosmic forces that govern the universe are precisely these three letters, which Mawlānā ʿAlī (the spiritual pole, *ḥadd ʿālī*) explicates as three movements: from sky to earth, from earth to sky, from sky to earth.[^cite-2]

The first force is centrifugal, pressing outward from the Kaʿba's orbit toward the constellation. The second is centripetal, pressing inward from the constellation toward the Kaʿba's orbit (the seat of ʿAlī's manifestation). The third presses upon the constellation from the circumference of heaven itself. The orbit of the Kaʿba is called the Kaʿba, and its concentric sphere is known as the *Maqāmāt al-Hamrāʾ* (the Red Stations).[^cite-3]

### Doors of the Gate of Fāṭima (4)

Four forces bind the entire cosmos in equilibrium. These are the gravitational powers that prevent the planets from colliding and hold them each in their appointed orbits. The Most High has planted four pillars at the periphery of creation that sustain the universe. When al-Qāʾim ʿAlī brings forth the Resurrection, he shall remove one of these pillars, and when that day comes, all that dwells within the zodiacal sphere shall perish, and the Kaʿba itself shall fall to give way to what comes after.

### Doors of the Gate of Ḥasan (7)

The earth is divided into seven regions, and through each flow the seven lights of Allāh: Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, the Sun, Venus, Mercury, and the Moon. All the celestial spheres circumambulate around the Kaʿba, which stands at the center of creation. Planets are those heavenly bodies that revolve around the Earth and rotate upon their own axes; stars are those that circle the Earth but do not turn within themselves.

### The Twelve Gates of Bāb al-Ḥusayn

These are the twelve divisions of the zodiacal constellation in which the star-clusters are stationed. Their number is said to be one thousand and twenty-nine, arranged in the pattern: 1, 228, 28, 228, 1000, 236, 1029. Each of these numbers bears a hidden significance.[^cite-4]

## The Twenty-Eight Limits of the Spiritual World

The spiritual cosmos (*ʿālam al-rūḥānī*) operates according to four modalities of existence and transformation:

| Modality | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Innovation (*ibdāʿ*) | Movement beyond the self in the spiritual world |
| Vision (*ru'ya*) | Those who perceive and witness truth |
| Descent (*hawl*, the post-fall state) | The passage from spiritual to physical manifestation |
| Ascension (*wajh*, the state of return) | The progression from physical back to spiritual realms |

The twelve angels who uphold the Throne of Asher and those who dwell within its sphere are called the *Mudbir* (the Directors). Their entire function consists in drawing forth light from the physical world and returning it to the spiritual realm, thereby maintaining the circulation of cosmic illumination through all planes of existence.[^cite-5]

## The Twenty-Eight Limits of Jihād

Faith (*īmān*) comprises three inseparable elements: truthfulness in the heart, confession upon the tongue, and righteous action of the body. Jihād (spiritual struggle) is likewise obligatory across these three dimensions. A *muḥaṣṣar* (one who restrains the self) is one who subdues his soul and places all his words and deeds beneath the commandments of the higher intellect (*al-ʿaql al-ʿālī*).

The Prophet (upon him be peace) enjoined, "We forbid that which appears to be lawful but contains hidden transgression—such as slaying a beast by shock rather than proper slaughter." Forbidden things are those interdicted in their very essence, as pork is forbidden by its nature.

## The Twenty-Eight Limits of the Word "Allāh"

### Examples of the Letters of Allāh

The Greatest Name of Allāh (*ism Allāh al-aʿẓam*) unfolds through its constituent letters, each bearing a profound spiritual significance. The word Allāh—composed of *Alif*, *Lam*, *Lam*, and *Hāʾ*—reveals the architecture of divine unity:

| Letter | Numerical Value | Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Alif (ا)** | 1 | *Qayyim al-Qiyāma*—the one who sustains the standing of all things |
| **Lam (ل) — First** | 30 | Adam, Noah, Abraham—each of these prophets manifests a collection of ten perfect forms (*kamālāt ʿashr*) |
| **Lam (ل) — Second** | 30 | Moses, Jesus, Muḥammad—each bearer of the law manifests a second collection of ten perfect forms |
| **Hāʾ (ه)** | 5 | The five blessed ones (*Panjtān*): Muḥammad, ʿAlī, Fāṭima, Ḥasan, and Ḥusayn—counterparts and perfectors. Multiplying five by five yields twenty-five; thus the Panjtān shall manifest in every cycle of seventy years, appearing as five within five. |

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Existence (*wujūd*) | 1 | Qayyim | 1 | |
| Speech (*nutq*) | 30 | Ibrāhīm | | |
| Silent-bearing (*ṣamt*) | 30 | Hāshim | | |
| Guardians (*Panjtān*) | 5 | Five Purified Ones | 12 (combined) | *Thālatha Ḥā'* |

In a final sense, "Allāh" is the Great Name, yet the Real Name—that which endures beyond utterance—is the First Intellect (*al-ʿAql al-Awwal*) and the First Source (*al-Mabdaʾ al-Awwal*), from which all knowledge and being emanate into creation.[^cite-6]
