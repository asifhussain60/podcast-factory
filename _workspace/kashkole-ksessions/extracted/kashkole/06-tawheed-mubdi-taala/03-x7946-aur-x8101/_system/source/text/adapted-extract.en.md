# Islam and Faith

## What is Faith?

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Sādiq (may peace be upon him) stated: "Believing in Allah is the noblest of all deeds, and therefore its reward is the greatest." He elaborated: "Faith is the testimony expressed in the words: ⟪ar-quote:أشھد ان لا الہ الا اللہ وحدہ لا شریک لہ و أشھد ان محمد عبدہ و رسولہ⟫ *There is no god but Allah, who is One and has no partner, and Muhammad is His servant and messenger.*" [^cite-1]

Faith encompasses affirmation of fundamental truths: that Paradise is real, Hell is real, resurrection from the grave is inevitable, and the Day of Judgment shall certainly come. It requires confirmation of ⟪ar:انبیا و مرسلین⟫ the Prophets and Messengers (peace be upon them), reverence for ⟪ar:ائمہ طاہرین علیہم السلام⟫ the Purified Imams (may peace be upon them), and obedience to ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ the Imam (may peace be upon him). Faith demands adherence to the commands that Allah has enjoined upon His servants and abstinence from what He has forbidden. In essence, obedience to the Imam and acceptance of his pronouncements constitute the perfection of faith.

The Imam taught that faith comprises three inseparable dimensions:

| Heart | Tongue | Body |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Heartfelt affirmation | Words expressed aloud | Actions of the limbs |

When asked whether affirmation of the heart combined with utterance of the tongue constitutes faith, he responded: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان عمَلُ کلُّہُ⟫ *"Faith is entirely an act."* [^cite-2] Speech itself is an action—an operation of the faculty of language. Knowledge and belief are actions of the heart. Obedience and righteous conduct through all the bodily organs constitute the action of the body. Therefore, **faith is nothing other than the comprehensive sum of all these actions**.

| Heart | Tongue | Body |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The practice of belief and knowledge | The practice of speech | Actions performed by all organs |

Allah, Most High, declares:

> ⟪quran 2:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and do righteous deeds—those are the inhabitants of Paradise; therein they shall abide eternally.*

Throughout the Qur'an, whenever Allah mentions faith, He invariably mentions righteous deeds alongside it. These two are inseparable. Though faith relates to the heart and action relates to the organs and limbs, their union forms the believer. ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ The Commander of the Faithful ⟪ar:نہج البلاغہ⟫ in *Nahj al-Balāghah* expresses this synthesis: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان اعتقاد بالقلب و عمل بالارکان و اقرار بالسان⟫ *"Faith is belief held in the heart, practice exercised through the limbs, and confession expressed by the tongue."* [^cite-3] Faith originates in the depths of the heart and reaches its culmination in righteous deeds, which encompass verbal confession. The light of faith emanates from within; Islam, by contrast, appears from without. Islam pertains to the external observance of the Sharīʿah; faith pertains to its inner meaning and esoteric dimension.

### Linguistic and Semantic Foundations

The Arabic root of faith (īmān) etymologically denotes confirmation or affirmation, and in another sense, security and assurance. Faith is a transitive verb requiring both an agent and an object. Two elements are thus necessary: the act of affirming and the one who confirms. If faith means to confirm, one must ask: what is being confirmed? The one who confirms is designated ⟪ar:مُصَدِّق⟫ *muṣaddiq* (the affirmer), and Allah becomes the object of affirmation. Alternatively, if faith signifies granting security, it likewise requires specification: who or what receives security?

The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) unified these meanings in a single tradition: ⟪ar-quote:ھِینَ اَلمُؤمِن مَن آمَنَ جَورِہِ بِوَایقَۃ⟫ *"A believer is one whose neighbor is secure from his harm."* A person is designated ⟪ar:اَلمُؤمِنُ⟫ *al-muʾmin* (the believer) because of two divine attributes: he affirms ⟪ar:وَعَدُ وَعِیدْ⟫ (the Promise and the Threat of Allah), and Allah, called ⟪ar:اسمآء الحسنی⟫ *al-ʾasmāʾ al-ḥusnā* (the Most Beautiful Names), grants him security from His overwhelming punishment. [^cite-4]

| Active Agent | Action | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Believer | Confirmation | Of Allah and His revelation |
| Believer | Granting security | To the believer (in Paradisiacal reward) |
| Allah | Granting security (Āman) | To the believer (from punishment) |

## Faith of the Heart, Tongue, and Body

The Imam stated: "Allah has made faith incumbent and obligatory upon every organ of the human being and has apportioned it among them." Thus, not a single organ among the twelve principal organs of Adam's progeny remains exempt from the practical obligation of faith. The faith of each member differs in character from the others. Allah has distributed faith among the following organs:

| Heart | Tongue | Ear | Eye | Hand | Foot | Face | Genital |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 1 | 1 |

### Faith of the Heart

The heart, from which understanding and wisdom derive, is the sovereign of the entire body. Without its assent and command, no organ may carry out any function. The faith Allah has enjoined upon the heart comprises five dimensions: confession, knowledge, belief, acceptance, and interior submission. The believer must confess in his heart that Allah alone is divine, that there exists no god save Him, that He is ⟪ar-quote:وحدہ لا شریک لہ⟫ *solitary and without partner, without spouse or offspring*. He must affirm that ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ Muhammad, the Chosen One (upon whom be the peace and blessings of Allah and upon his family), is the servant and true Messenger of Allah. Subsequently, the heart must acknowledge all that Allah has revealed—the Book and the Prophet—and must acquire knowledge of the principles of faith, which strengthen and deepen it. This is the practical and intellectual faith Allah demands of the heart.

The Qur'an illuminates this truth in several verses:

> ⟪quran 16:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَن كَفَرَ بِٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ إِيمَنِهِۦٓ إِلَّا مَنْ أُكْرِهَ وَقَلْبُهُۥ مُطْمَىِٕنٌّۢ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ وَلَكِن مَّن شَرَحَ بِٱلْكُفْرِ صَدْرًۭا فَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Whosoever disbelieves in Allah after he has believed—saving him who is compelled while his heart remains at rest in faith—but such as open their breasts to disbelief: upon them rests wrath from Allah, and theirs awaits a terrible punishment.*

This verse makes plain that the seat of true faith is the heart. Even under duress, the outward disavowal of belief causes no harm if the heart remains steadfast in faith.

> ⟪quran 2:284⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لِّلَّهِ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَإِن تُبْدُوا۟ مَا فِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ أَوْ تُخْفُوهُ يُحَاسِبْكُم بِهِ ٱللَّهُ ۖ فَيَغْفِرُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَن يَشَآءُ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌ ‎⟫
> *To Allah belongs all that is in the heavens and all that is on the earth. Whether you disclose what is within your souls or conceal it, Allah shall reckon with you concerning it. Then He forgives whom He wills and punishes whom He wills; Allah has power over all things.*

This verse emphasizes that the states of the heart—secret thoughts, doubts, and inner convictions—fall within Allah's knowledge and judgment.

> ⟪quran 5:41⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ لَا يَحْزُنكَ ٱلَّذِينَ يُسَرِعُونَ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ آمَنَّا بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَلَمْ تُؤْمِن قُلُوبُهُمْ ۛ وَمِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَادُوا۟ ۛ سَمَّعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ سَمَّعُونَ لِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ لَمْ يَأْتُوكَ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ مَوَاضِعِهِۦ ۖ يَقُولُونَ إِنْ أُوتِيتُمْ هَذَا فَخُذُوهُ وَإِن لَّمْ تُؤْتَوْهُ فَٱحْذَرُوا۟ ۚ وَمَن يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ فِتْنَتَهُۥ فَلَن تَمْلِكَ لَهُۥ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ شَيْـًٔا ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَمْ يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ أَن يُطَهِّرَ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا خِزْىٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O Messenger! Let not those who hasten to disbelief grieve thee—those who say with their mouths, "We believe," though their hearts believe not. Among the Jews are some who hearken to falsehood, who spy for another people who have not come to thee. They pervert the words from their places, saying, "If you are given this, accept it; if you are not given it, beware!" Whomsoever Allah wills to lead astray, thou hast no power to defend against Allah. Those are they whose hearts Allah has not willed to purify. For them is disgrace in this world, and in the Hereafter, a terrible punishment.*

This verse plainly distinguishes between verbal profession and the faith of the heart.

> ⟪quran 13:28⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَتَطْمَىِٕنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ تَطْمَىِٕنُّ ٱلْقُلُوبُ ‎⟫
> *Those who believe and whose hearts find peace in the remembrance of Allah—indeed, it is in the remembrance of Allah that hearts find rest.*

### Faith of the Tongue

The faith Allah has prescribed for the tongue is the faith of utterance and articulation—the verbal expression of the confession and belief that the heart has embraced. As Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 3:84⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْنَا وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَإِسْمَعِيلَ وَإِسْحَقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَٱلْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَآ أُوتِىَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَٱلنَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُۥ مُسْلِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say: "We believe in Allah and in what has been sent down to us, and what was sent down to Abraham, Ishmael, Isaac, Jacob, and the Tribes, and what was given to Moses and Jesus and the Prophets from their Lord. We make no distinction between any of them, and to Him we submit."*

The tongue must confess the divine unity and the prophethood of Muhammad (upon whom be peace). Allah commands:

> ⟪quran 33:70⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَقُولُوا۟ قَوْلًۭا سَدِيدًۭا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Keep your duty to Allah and speak words that are right.*

> ⟪quran 18:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيُؤْمِن وَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيَكْفُرْ ۚ إِنَّآ أَعْتَدْنَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ نَارًا أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ سُرَادِقُهَا ۚ وَإِن يَسْتَغِيثُوا۟ يُغَاثُوا۟ بِمَآءٍۢ كَٱلْمُهْلِ يَشْوِى ٱلْوُجُوهَ ۚ بِئْسَ ٱلشَّرَابُ وَسَآءَتْ مُرْتَفَقًا ‎⟫
> *And say: "The truth is from your Lord." So let him who will believe, and let him who will disbelieve. Indeed, We have prepared for the wrongdoers a Fire, whose walls shall enclose them; and if they cry for help, they shall be helped with water like molten brass, which will scald their faces. Evil is the drink, and evil is the resting place!*

### Faith of Both Ears

The ears must hear the command of Allah with full attention and heed. They must not listen to what is forbidden or what Allah dislikes. The Qur'an prescribes:

> ⟪quran 4:140⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنْ إِذَا سَمِعْتُمْ آيَتِ ٱللَّهِ يُكْفَرُ بِهَا وَيُسْتَهْزَأُ بِهَا فَلَا تَقْعُدُوا۟ مَعَهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّكُمْ إِذًۭا مِّثْلُهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ جَامِعُ ٱلْمُنَفِقِينَ وَٱلْكَفِرِينَ فِى جَهَنَّمَ جَمِيعًا ‎⟫
> *And it has already been revealed to you in the Book that when you hear the verses of Allah being denied and mocked at, then sit not with them until they engage in discourse other than that; else surely you would be like them. Indeed, Allah will gather the hypocrites and the disbelievers in Hell, all together.*

> ⟪quran 6:68⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخُوضُونَ فِىٓ آيَتِنَا فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦ ۚ وَإِمَّا يُنسِيَنَّكَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَلَا تَقْعُدْ بَعْدَ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And when thou seest those who engage in false discourse concerning Our signs, turn away from them until they turn to other discourse. Should Satan cause thee to forget, then after recollection sit not with the wrongdoing people.*

Conversely, the ears must attend closely to truth:

> ⟪quran 39:18⟫
> ⟪ar:فَبَشِّرْ عِبَادِ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَمِعُونَ ٱلْقَوْلَ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ أَحْسَنَهُۥٓ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمْ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *Announce the glad tidings to My servants who listen to the word and follow what is best therein. Those are they whom Allah has guided, and those are they who possess understanding.*

The example of receptive ears is beautifully illustrated:

> ⟪quran 23:1-4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَدْ أَفْلَحَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ فِى صَلَاتِهِمْ خَشِعُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ ٱللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَوٰةِ فَعِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *Successful indeed are the believers, who in their prayers are humble and reverent, who turn away from idle talk, and who are diligent in almsgiving.*

### Faith of Both Eyes

Allah has enjoined upon the eyes that they refrain from gazing upon what is unlawful, and keep their gaze lowered from all that Allah has forbidden. This restraint is a dimension of faith and constitutes the work of the eyes:

> ⟪quran 24:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Tell the believing men to lower their gaze and guard their modesty. That is purer for them. Indeed, Allah is aware of what they do.*

### The Faith of Chastity

A believer must not gaze upon his brother's private parts, nor permit others to look upon his own. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Sādiq (may peace be upon him) explained that while the Qur'anic verses concerning the guarding of private parts relate to protection from illicit sexual conduct, the verse on ⟪ar:غَضُّ بَصَر⟫ *lowering the gaze* addresses the discipline of sight itself. The comprehensive duty of the heart, tongue, eye, and ear is encompassed in a single verse:

> ⟪quran 17:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْبَصَرَ وَٱلْفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَانَ عَنْهُ مَسْـُٔولًۭا ‎⟫
> *And follow not that of which thou hast no knowledge. Indeed, the hearing, the sight, and the heart—each of these shall be called to account.*

> ⟪quran 41:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَن يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَآ أَبْصَرُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَكِن ظَنَنتُمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *You thought not that your ears, your eyes, and your skins would bear witness against you; nay, but you reckoned that Allah knew not much of what you do.*

### Faith of Both Hands

Allah has prescribed for the hands that they be employed for lawful purposes alone—for charity, beneficence, kinship, jihād in the path of Allah, and ritual ablution:

> ⟪quran 5:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ فَٱغْسِلُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْمَرَافِقِ وَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِرُئُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُمْ جُنُبًۭا فَٱطَّهَّرُوا۟ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَآءَ أَحَدٌۭ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَمَسْتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا۟ مَآءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا۟ صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا فَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُم مِّنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُم مِّنْ حَرَجٍۢ وَلَكِن يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you rise to prayer, wash your faces and your hands up to the elbows, and wipe your heads and your feet up to the ankles. If you are in a state of ritual impurity, then purify yourselves. And if you are ill or on a journey, or you have come from need, or you have been intimate with women, and you find no water, then take clean earth and wipe your faces and your hands with it. Allah wills not to place hardship upon you, but desires to purify you and to complete His favour upon you, that you may be grateful.*

The hands must show courage in the path of Allah:

> ⟪quran 8:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ زَحْفًۭا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلْأَدْبَارَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you meet the disbelievers in battle, do not turn your backs to them.*

> ⟪quran 47:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ فَضَرْبَ ٱلرِّقَابِ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَثْخَنتُمُوهُمْ فَشُدُّوا۟ ٱلْوَثَاقَ فَإِمَّا مَنًّۢا بَعْدُ وَإِمَّا فِدَآءً حَتَّىٰ تَضَعَ ٱلْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا ۚ ذَلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَآءُ ٱللَّهُ لَٱنتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ وَلَكِن لِّيَبْلُوَا۟ بَعْضَكُم بِبَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱلَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ فَلَن يُضِلَّ أَعْمَلَهُمْ ‎⟫
> *When you encounter the disbelievers, strike at their necks. And when you have subdued them, bind the bonds of captivity. Then either release them with grace or ransom, until the war lays down its burden. Thus it is ordained. Yet had Allah willed, He could have taken vengeance upon them; but He tests some by others. And those who are slain in the path of Allah—He will never let their deeds go to waste.* [^cite-5]

The striking of the necks is indeed the function of the hands, and this action constitutes a dimension of faith.

### Faith of Both Feet

Allah has enjoined upon the feet that they walk in obedience to His commands. The feet must not carry the believer toward evil or disobedience; rather, they must advance toward the pleasure and commands of Allah:

> ⟪quran 17:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَمْشِ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا ‎⟫
> *And walk not upon the earth with arrogance. Verily, thou canst neither rend the earth asunder nor reach the mountains in height.*

> ⟪quran 31:19⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱقْصِدْ فِى مَشْيِكَ وَٱغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلْأَصْوَتِ لَصَوْتُ ٱلْحَمِيرِ ‎⟫
> *And be modest in thy gait, and lower thy voice. Indeed, the harshest of voices is the voice of the ass.*

The feet must hasten toward the remembrance of Allah:

> ⟪quran 62:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا نُودِىَ لِلصَّلَوٰةِ مِن يَوْمِ ٱلْجُمُعَةِ فَٱسْعَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When the call to prayer is proclaimed on Friday, hasten to the remembrance of Allah and abandon your commerce. That is better for you, if you would only know.*

> ⟪quran 22:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ لْيَقْضُوا۟ تَفَثَهُمْ وَلْيُوفُوا۟ نُذُورَهُمْ وَلْيَطَّوَّفُوا۟ بِٱلْبَيْتِ ٱلْعَتِيقِ ‎⟫
> *Then let them complete their rites of pilgrimage and fulfill their vows, and circumambulate the Ancient House.*

The Qur'an reminds us that in the Day of Resurrection, the very hands and feet shall testify against or for the person:

> ⟪quran 36:65⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ نَخْتِمُ عَلَىٰٓ أَفْوَهِهِمْ وَتُكَلِّمُنَآ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَتَشْهَدُ أَرْجُلُهُم بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
> *On this Day We shall seal their mouths, and their hands shall speak to Us, and their feet shall bear witness to what they have earned.*

### Faith of the Face

The entire being—represented by the face—must turn toward Allah in worship and submission:

> ⟪quran 22:77⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱرْكَعُوا۟ وَٱسْجُدُوا۟ وَٱعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ وَٱفْعَلُوا۟ ٱلْخَيْرَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Bow down and prostrate yourselves, and worship your Lord, and do good, that you may prosper.*

> ⟪quran 72:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنَّ ٱلْمَسَجِدَ لِلَّهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا۟ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ أَحَدًۭا ‎⟫
> *And indeed, the places of worship belong to Allah; so call upon none along with Allah.*

Allah has made the purification and prostration of the limbs incumbent, and He has designated prayer itself as a manifestation of faith within the Qur'an. When Allah commanded His Messenger to turn away from ⟪ar:بیت المقدس⟫ the Sacred House of Jerusalem toward ⟪ar:بیت الحرام⟫ the Sacred House at Mecca, the people inquired: "O Messenger of Allah, what of our prayers offered toward the former qiblah?" The Almighty responded:

> ⟪quran 2:143⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Thus have We made you a community balanced and just, so that you may be witnesses to mankind and the Messenger may be a witness to you. And We did not ordain the qiblah which you were facing except to test who would follow the Messenger from those who would turn back on their heels. It was surely a tremendous test, yet not for those whom Allah has guided. Allah would not let your faith go to waste; indeed, Allah is Most Kind and Merciful to mankind.*

**In this verse, Allah explicitly designates prayer itself as faith.**

He who departs this world such that every limb has been preserved from transgression and each organ has fulfilled its divine duty with excellence shall receive from Allah the perfection of faith and shall enter Paradise. However, whosoever betrays Allah's covenant, even in the smallest measure, or transgresses His commands through these organs, shall come before Allah bearing the deficiency of faith. One who pledges allegiance to Allah and to the Imam of the Age (may Allah hasten his return) yet neither acts upon his knowledge nor acquires deeper understanding cannot be truly counted among the believers. Mere verbal profession of faith shall avail him nothing; such a person possesses no standing before Allah and His Imam (may peace be upon them), and is regarded as falling outside the protective circle of the Imam of the Age.

## Three Categories of Faith and Believers

⟪ar:قرآن حکیم⟫ The Noble Qur'an delineates three types of faith:

| Perfect Faith | Deficient Faith | Faith Mixed with Polytheism |
| --- | --- | --- |
| *Īmān kāmil* | *Īmān nāqiṣ* | *Īmān mashbūh bi-al-shirk* |

### Perfect Faith

Those possessed of perfect faith are those whom Allah Himself praises in the Qur'an and to whom He announces the glad tidings of Paradise. These are the people who embody the two pillars of faith: firm conviction and security. They possess the station of *waliʾ* (intimate friend of Allah), such that although their bodies dwell physically in the world—⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫ the abode of falsehood—their souls remain ⟪ar:دار الصدق و صفا⟫ in the realm of truth and purity. Thus, whilst others have eyes fixed upon the material world, these believers contemplate the eternal dwelling place even as they live within temporal existence.

The security granted to them is singular: when their ⟪ar:نفوس لطیفۃ⟫ subtle souls reach ⟪ar:دار السلام⟫ the House of Peace, they shall be liberated from all the tribulations that befall the flesh after death. For them, death itself becomes a source of joy and resurrection of the soul. Through the dissolution of bodily existence, they are freed eternally from ⟪ar:دار کدورت⟫ the realm of turbidity and obscurity. Thus did ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Sādiq (may peace be upon him) say: "Death is balm for the believer," and he gestured to his throat, indicating: "When my believer's soul reaches this point, he exults with joy." [^cite-6]

The perfect believers are those to whom ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی علیہ السلام⟫ the Commander of the Faithful ⟪ar:ائمہ طاھرین⟫ and the Purified Imams point, and for whom ⟪ar:ولی أمر⟫ the Guardian of the Affair is responsible. The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) declared: "Those whom the Qur'an addresses as ⟪ar-quote:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ *'O you who believe!'* are the leaders and masters, of whom ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (may peace be upon him) is the foremost; and this leadership shall remain perpetually in the hands of his descendants for as long as there remain those addressed by this divine call." Since the divine appellative *"O you who believe"* encompasses ⟪ar:مُتَقَدِّمِینَ⟫ those of previous ages and ⟪ar:مُتَأَخِّرِینَ⟫ those of future ages, and this leadership extends ⟪ar:اِلیٰ یَومِ الدِّینَ⟫ until the Day of Judgment, then ⟪ar:محمد و علی(ﷺ)⟫ the guardianship of Muhammad and ʿAlī shall remain over all the faithful until that final day. [^cite-7] Whosoever rebels against the Imams is in rebellion against Allah and His Messenger and shall be counted among the ⟪ar:خارجی⟫ *khārijī* (those who exit the faith). When such a person dies, he dies in a state of ignorance. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger and those vested with authority among you. And if you dispute concerning anything, refer it to Allah and the Messenger, if you truly believe in Allah and the Last Day. That is better and more excellent in outcome.*

### Deficient Faith

The second type comprises those of weak faith—⟪ar:دعوت ایمانیۃ⟫ those newly entered into the invitation of faith who are merely ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ *mustajīb* (respondents) to the call, standing in the lower ranks, ignorant of the deeper sciences of the dīn (religion). Their example resembles the newborn child who, though possessing all the physical faculties of a human being, remains utterly dependent upon constant care and supervision at every turn, unable to act independently. Similarly, those in the initial stages of the daʿwa (spiritual invitation) are called believers yet require the guidance and nurture of those advanced in the path. This defective station is elucidated by the Almighty:

> ⟪quran 4:136⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ آمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ مِن قَبْلُ ۚ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَلًۢا بَعِيدًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Believe in Allah and His Messenger and the Book which He has sent down upon His Messenger and the Books which He sent down before. And whoso disbelieves in Allah and His Angels and His Books and His Messengers and the Last Day, has surely gone far astray.*

Observe the eloquence of this verse: Allah first addresses them saying, "O you who believe," thereby attesting to their belief; then He says, "Believe!" If these to whom the verse is addressed ⟪ar:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ already possessed ⟪ar:کامل الایمان⟫ *perfect faith*, it would be redundant to command them to ⟪ar:' آمَنُو'⟫ believe anew. Rather, the Divine Word speaks to those of lower degree: "You have entered the circle of faith; you have been spiritually born into the dīn. Now advance further in your understanding and ascend from the station of deficiency to perfection. Remain in the company of the righteous—your spiritual guides and mentors—and benefit from their illumination, so that you may eventually dwell ⟪ar:دار الصدق و امان⟫ in the House of Truth and Security." Just as children accept instruction from parents and educators and gradually attain their spiritual stature, so too must these believers progress through discipleship.

Allah illustrates this principle:

> ⟪quran 52:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and whose offspring follow them in faith, We shall unite with them their offspring. And We shall not diminish aught from their deeds; each soul is pledged for what it has earned.*

Here, "offspring" (*dhurriyyah*) must be understood as spiritual or causal descendants—not biological progeny. That is, the believers of lower rank who follow in the footsteps of the guides of the highest rank shall ultimately, through Allah's grace, be raised to the station of their mentors.

### Faith Mixed with Polytheism (*Īmān Mashbūh bi-al-Shirk*)

This category comprises faith that is contaminated by polytheism and bears no genuine relationship to true faith in Allah and His Messenger. True faith in Islam means accepting without the slightest alteration the commands that the Messenger of Allah conveyed through revelation—⟪ar:مِنُّ و عَن⟫ receiving them purely, without hesitation or qualification, for in their purity lies the soul's salvation and spiritual deliverance.

Allah commands: ⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ آتَیكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوہُ وَمَا نَھٰكُمْ عَنْہُ فَانتَھوا۟⟫ *"Take what the Messenger gives you, and refrain from what he forbids."* The believer must not introduce even the faintest trace of personal opinion into obedience to the Messenger. Rather, the true believer is he who attaches himself directly to the Prophet in all matters of worship, lawfulness and prohibition, and religious affairs, without the intrusion of conjecture, opinion, or independent reasoning (*ijtihād*). But he who mingles his desires with the commandments of the Messenger, employing opinion and conjecture to satisfy his appetites, is not a believer but an innovator (*mubtadiʿ*). Because he has associated his own will with Allah's command, his faith becomes tainted with *shirk* (associationism), which leads him toward error instead of guidance, toward ruin instead of salvation. No matter how many prayers he performs or fasts he observes, such deeds shall benefit him not at all.

Consider the analogy of rainwater descended from heaven: it remains fit for drinking and purification only so long as it remains uncontaminated. Once impurity overcomes it, that water is useless to the believer—it is neither drinkable nor efficacious for purification, despite still being called water. In the same manner, when the appetites of the self (*nafs*) intermix with the rulings of revelation, the pure dīn is lost and innovation takes its place. The Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) declared: "Every innovation is misguidance, no matter how fair it may appear." [^cite-8]

After the permeation of *bidʿah* (innovation), the external forms of Islam remain in their places: opponents of the Imams also perform prayer, yet the difference between their prayers and those of the faithful is manifest. The prayers of those who have admixed *shirk* and the impurity of innovation shall be hurled back upon their faces. To condemn such people, Allah pronounces:

> ⟪quran 12:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا يُؤْمِنُ أَكْثَرُهُم بِٱللَّهِ إِلَّا وَهُم مُّشْرِكُونَ ‎⟫
> *And most of them believe not in Allah except that they associate partners with Him.*

#### The Zakat of the Polytheists

It is related through tradition that ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Sādiq (may peace be upon him) recounted that his father ⟪ar:مولانا محمد الباقر علیہ السلام⟫ Mawlānā Muhammad al-Bāqir (may peace be upon him) once asked ⟪ar:حضرت جابر بن عبد اللہ الانصاری رضی اللہ عنہ⟫ Jābir ibn ʿAbd Allāh al-Anṣārī (may Allah be pleased with him): "Has Allah made *zakāh* obligatory upon the polytheist?" ⟪ar:حضرت جابر⟫ Jābir replied, "No! Allah has prescribed *zakāh* only for the Muslims." The Imam said, "Have you not heard the words of Allah:

> ⟪quran 41:6-7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أَنَا۠ بَشَرٌۭ مِّثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ أَنَّمَآ إِلَهُكُمْ إِلَهٌۭ وَحِدٌۭ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُشْرِكِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَافِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say: "I am but a human like yourselves. It is revealed to me that your God is One God. So keep straight the course unto Him and ask His forgiveness. And woe unto those who ascribe partners with Allah, those who give not the *zakāh* and are disbelievers in the Hereafter."*

⟪ar:حضرت جابر⟫ Jābir related: "When I heard this verse from the blessed lips of ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ Imam al-Bāqir (may peace be upon him), it seemed as though I had never before read this verse, though it is inscribed in Allah's Book." ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ Imam al-Ṣādiq (may peace be upon him) explained: "This verse alludes to those who have entered into the *wilāyah* (guardianship) of the Imams yet give their *zakāh* to one who has set himself in opposition." [^cite-9] Thus the verse demonstrates that Allah Himself designates those Muslims who withhold *zakāh* as polytheists!

*(Source: Majālis al-Muʾayyadiyyah, attributed to Sayyidnā al-Muʾayyad al-Shīrāzī)*

*(Source: Rasāʾil al-Ahmadiyyah, attributed to Imam Ahmad ibn ʿAbdullāh, peace be upon him)*

### Three Categories of Believers

There are ⟪ar:مؤمنین⟫ three ranks of believers:

| 1 | Those who wrong their own souls—who acquire knowledge yet do not implement it |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Those who constantly keep account of themselves—who maintain equilibrium between the claims of the dīn and the necessities of this world, neither neglecting one for the other |
| 3 | Those who spend for Allah's sake—who dedicate themselves wholly to the Hereafter |

## The Eight Stations of Faith

Just as Paradise possesses eight gates, faith likewise unfolds across eight stations of spiritual attainment:

| 1 | Kindness to parents—this constitutes the highest station of faith |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Beneficence to relatives |
| 3 | Kindness to orphans |
| 4 | Compassion toward the destitute |
| 5 | Goodness toward a neighbor bound by both kinship and faith |
| 6 | *Jār al-janab*: beneficence to a believing neighbor unrelated by blood |
| 7 | Kindness to a neighbor connected by neither kinship nor religious affiliation |
| 8 | Generosity to the wayfarer and the enslaved |

## The Qualities of the Believer

A believer cannot be said to have attained true faith unless his intellect has reached *kamāl awwal* (primary perfection). He cannot achieve this perfection until he cultivates ten cardinal virtues within himself:

| 1 | From the world he takes only what is good and beneficial |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | He harbors no fear of any created being, only awe of the Divine |
| 3 | He regards his own good deeds as trivial, while he considers the righteous deeds of others as magnificent |
| 4 | He perceives his own faults as grave, yet views the faults of others with clemency and forbearance |
| 5 | He never tires of seeking to fulfill the needs of others |
| 6 | He never ceases acquiring knowledge; throughout his life he remains a seeker of wisdom and understanding |
| 7 | For him, forbearance and clemency far exceed honor and reputation |
| 8 | He deems poverty nobler than wealth, as the Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) said: "My poverty is my pride" |
| 9 | Meager sustenance suffices him entirely |
| 10 | When he encounters a virtuous person, he believes that person to be superior in piety and righteousness to himself |

## Precedence and Degrees in Faith

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) states: "If faith were uniform and admitted neither deficiency nor excess, then no believer would gain superiority over another. All who profess faith would stand equal in the blessings bestowed in return for their belief, and none would rank higher than any other." Yet the reality differs fundamentally. Believers enter Paradise by virtue of their perfected faith, and it is precisely their ⟪ar:راجح⟫greater measure of faith that grants them precedence in God's sight over their fellows. By contrast, those ⟪ar:مقصرین⟫whose faith carries defects shall enter the Fire.

Regarding the degrees of faith itself, ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ the Imam teaches: "Allah has ordained for the believers a race toward perfection of faith, just as horses contend in a given race." Each believer who first accepted faith—each of those early in the faith—was granted rank according to the order of their precedence. The laggard in faith cannot surpass the early believer, nor the virtuous leap ahead of the virtuous. This precedence constitutes a distinguishing virtue, conferring upon the first believer in the Community superiority over those who came after. ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ Prince of the Faithful ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (upon him be peace) was foremost among all the Companions in embracing faith, and thus possessed superiority over all other believers.[^cite-3]

Had the early believers possessed no advantage over those who believed later, the last believer of this Community would stand indistinguishable from the first. Had precedence not been ordained as the measure of excellence, many ⟪ar:ارباب فضل و کمال و زھد وتقوی⟫ of the latter—those possessed of virtue, perfection, asceticism, and piety—would inevitably surpass the former; for among them are found many ⟪ar:ارباب فضل و کمال⟫ of superior practical devotion than their predecessors. These pray abundantly, fast rigorously, perform the pilgrimage repeatedly, and engage tirelessly in struggle and charity in God's way. Had primacy among believers not been established as the foundation of nobility and excellence, the latter would necessarily take precedence over the former by reason of their surpassing deeds. But Allah—may He be exalted—does not desire that one placed in the lowest rank of faith be elevated to that of the foremost, nor that the one He has deferred in faith be thrust forward, nor that the one He has advanced be cast back. Allah has made this precedence luminous in the following verses:

> ⟪quran 57:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَابِقُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ مَغْفِرَةٍۢ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَجَنَّةٍ عَرْضُهَا كَعَرْضِ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ أُعِدَّتْ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ ۚ ذَلِكَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ ذُو ٱلْفَضْلِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ ‎⟫
> *Race one with another in hastening towards Forgiveness from your Lord (Allah), and towards Paradise, the width whereof is as the width of heaven and earth, prepared for those who believe in Allah and His Messengers. > ⟪quran 56:10-11⟫

(Lawful to you in marriage) are chaste women from the believers and chaste women from those who were given the Scripture (Jews and Christians) before your time, when you have given their due Mahr (bridal money given by the husband to his wife at the time of marriage), desiring chastity (i.e. taking them in legal wedlock) not committing illegal sexual intercourse, nor taking them as girl-friends. And whosoever disbelieves in the Oneness of Allah and in all the other Articles of Faith [i.e. His (Allah's), Angels, His Holy Books, His Messengers, the Day of Resurrection and Al-Qadar (Divine Preordainments)], then fruitless is his work, and in the Hereafter he will be among the losers.*

What is meant by ⟪ar:کفر بالایمان⟫ rejecting faith is: to refuse compliance with Allah's commandments; to confess with the tongue whilst the heart harbors disbelief; or to believe and speak truly yet fail to act. But should one confess with tongue and perform outwardly whilst harboring inward doubt, that person remains no believer, no matter how strenuous his external endeavor. Such deeds bear no fruit. The Lord says:

## Outward and Inward Faith

The reward Allah has promised for faith in the life to come is Paradise. The bestowal of reward in the Hereafter reflects a profound truth: faith is a station that elevates the believer above all ⟪ar:خیراتِ بشریات⟫ human goods and even approaches the ⟪ar:فضائل الملائکۃ⟫ excellence of the angels. Since none may attain the station of angelhood whilst walking this earthly path, the reward has been deferred to the world beyond; Paradise stands promised for the faithful. Similarly, disbelief represents a descent into ⟪ar:شَرُّ البشریات⟫ the worst of human conditions, and thus the ⟪ar:وعید⟫ threat of the Fire is pronounced. Faith comprises two branches: the manifest and the hidden, the outer and the inner.

Faith takes two dimensions. ⟪ar:وعدہ⟫ Promise (waʿd) marks the reward for righteous deeds, as in the promise of Paradise. ⟪ar:وعید⟫ Threat (waʿīd) marks the punishment for transgression, as in the threat of Hell.

### Outward Faith

The manifest dimension of faith rests upon five verbal confessions:

| **First:** Affirmation of Allah | The declaration that there exists one Creator of this cosmos, unique, self-subsisting, all-powerful, all-wise, and the originator of all creation. He alone holds dominion over all creatures; none shares His authority. |
| --- | --- |
| **Second:** Acknowledgment of the Angels | Those whom Allah has appointed as the noblest of His creation, established to serve and worship Him, and designated as guardians of His world. They hold positions of great trust in executing His designs, obey only His command, and never transgress. |
| **Third:** Recognition of the Prophets | Allah chose from among His servants certain ones—the Prophets and Messengers—and appointed the angels as intermediaries between Himself and these chosen few. The angels deliver to them tidings and commands, which they then convey to all creation in their own languages and modes of speech. |
| **Fourth:** Belief in the Books | All messages the prophets received as revelation through the angels, delivered to their contemporaries in the language and style of their times. The message remains constant; only its expression changes. |
| **Fifth:** Certainty of the Resurrection | The conviction that a Day of Rising shall come, bringing new creation and transformation. All creatures shall be resurrected, judged, recompensed for their deeds—the righteous rewarded, the wicked punished. |

Those who fully embrace these five truths are the believers, as Allah declares in His Book:

> ⟪quran 2:285⟫
> ⟪ar:‏آمَنَ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ ‎⟫
> *The Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) believes in what has been sent down to him from his Lord, and (so do) the believers. Each one believes in Allah, His Angels, His Books, and His Messengers. They say, "We make no distinction between one another of His Messengers" - and they say, "We hear, and we obey. (We seek) Your Forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the return (of all)."*

When a believer professes these five truths aloud, he separates himself from Jews, Christians, and Magians. In each affirmation lies an implicit negation of all else; by this confession he binds himself to perform the prescribed prayer, observe the fast, undertake the pilgrimage, render the alms-tax, and wage struggle in Allah's cause.

### Inner Faith

Inner faith is the affirmation from the depths of the heart of those five truths one has confessed with the tongue. This inward confirmation establishes the believer upon the inner stations of faith. The deeper and more total that confirmation, the higher the planes of inner faith to which one ascends. It is for these—the inwardly established in faith—that Allah has praised His servants in scripture and promised them Paradise.[^cite-6]

The paradise of outward faith differs from that of inner faith. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him), alluding to this distinction, said: "Whoever utters ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *There is no god save Allah* shall enter Paradise." Then he elaborated: "Whoever utters ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *There is no god save Allah* with sincere intent shall enter Paradise."

The first group—those who speak the formula with the tongue alone—enter the Paradise of ⟪ar:اسلام⟫ *Islam* (the outward submission). The second group—those who couple vocal confession with sincerity of heart—enter the Paradise of ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ *faith* (the inward affirmation).

| Those who confess with tongue | Those who confess with tongue and sincere heart |
| --- | --- |
| Verbal declaration | Verbal declaration united with heartfelt affirmation |

The path to inner faith requires four stations:

| **Reflection** (tafkīr) | Deep contemplation and investigation of a matter, proving its truth to oneself through thought. |
| --- | --- |
| **Consideration** (iʿtibār) | Comparison between objects and discernment of their relationships and distinctions. |
| **Fulfillment of conditions** (qiyām bi-sharāʾiṭihā) | Enactment of what knowledge demands; performance of Allah's commandments regarding it. |
| **Giving its due** (wājib ḥaqquhā) | Rendering full measure of what Allah has made incumbent upon that knowledge. |

Entry into the Paradise of faith proves no simple matter. Allah Himself testifies:

> ⟪quran 3:142⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ جَهَدُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *Do you think that you will enter Paradise before Allah tests those of you who fought (in His Cause) and (also) tests those who are As-Sabirin (the patient ones, etc.)?*

## The Excellence of Faith Over Knowledge

Elevation of rank is bound to knowledge and faith, not to mere action alone. The degrees of knowledge differ, and the degrees of faith differ likewise. To clarify this hierarchy, Allah states:

> ⟪quran 58:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجْلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you are told to make room in the assemblies, (spread out and) make room. Allah will give you (ample) room (from His Mercy). And when you are told to rise up [for prayers, Jihad (holy fighting in Allah's Cause), or for any other good deed], rise up. Allah will exalt in degree those of you who believe, and those who have been granted knowledge. And Allah is Well-Acquainted with what you do.*

In this verse, Allah declares that He elevates both the faithful and the learned. Yet note the order: believers are mentioned before the learned, signifying that their elevation precedes that of the knowledgeable ones. Faith holds priority over knowledge, for knowledge serves as the instrument by which faith increases—and what is absent cannot increase. Thus faith antecedes knowledge both logically and in creation's unfolding. When the Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace) called the people to Islam, his first command was: "Say ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *There is no god save Allah* and you shall triumph." He did not first demand sophisticated knowledge; he asked only for confession and belief in the word itself. Only when people had confessed did the stations of deeper understanding commence, and the knowledge contained within ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *There is no god save Allah* began to dawn upon them.[^cite-7]

The fundamental principle reveals itself thus: It was through confession of monotheism alone that the ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ spiritual realm, the ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal), was given to behold the ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ Originator Most High and His ⟪ar:علم ما کان سیکون⟫ knowledge of all that was and shall be. Faith preceded knowledge in this divine gift; thus faith holds precedence.

Allah further illuminates the hierarchy of the learned in another verse, stating that above every knower stands one yet more knowing. The word ⟪ar:' فوق'⟫ *above* here points to the elevation and differentiation of ranks:

> ⟪quran 12:76⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَبَدَأَ بِأَوْعِيَتِهِمْ قَبْلَ وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَخْرَجَهَا مِن وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ۚ كَذَلِكَ كِدْنَا لِيُوسُفَ ۖ مَا كَانَ لِيَأْخُذَ أَخَاهُ فِى دِينِ ٱلْمَلِكِ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ نَرْفَعُ دَرَجَتٍۢ مَّن نَّشَآءُ ۗ وَفَوْقَ كُلِّ ذِى عِلْمٍ عَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *So he [Yusuf (Joseph)] began (the search) in their bags before the bag of his brother. Then he brought it out of his brother's bag. Thus did We plan for Yusuf (Joseph). He could not take his brother by the law of the king (as a slave), except that Allah willed it. (So Allah made the brothers to bind themselves with their way of "punishment, i.e. enslaving of a thief.") We raise to degrees whom We please, but over all those endowed with knowledge is the All-Knowing (Allah).*

### Knowledge and Faith Defined

Knowledge is the form (ṣūra) that the soul receives when it comes to know a reality. When the soul confesses this form with the tongue, affirms it with the heart, and embeds it within itself through bodily action, that form becomes faith. Verification requires investigation; it cannot occur otherwise. For example, all Muslims know that Paradise, Hell, and the Bridge of Sirat (Ṣirāṭ) exist; we affirm and confirm their reality—we believe in them—yet since ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫ the five senses cannot encompass them, investigation and research are necessary to truly know and verify their existence.

| Knowledge | Faith |
| --- | --- |
| Apprehension | Affirmation of the heart |
| Awareness | Tongue's utterance |
| Reception | Embodiment in action |

The form of knowledge dwelling in the scholar's soul bears the name ⟪ar:علم⟫ *knowledge* (ʿilm). When this knowledge is confessed with the tongue, affirmed in the heart, and enacted through the body's limbs, it becomes ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ *faith* (īmān). The reality is faith itself; knowledge is but the means by which faith grows and perfects.

### Verification and Investigation

Knowledge presents itself in a duality. First comes ⟪ar:علم ظاہر⟫ manifest knowledge (ʿilm ẓāhir), which precedes faith: mere awareness gained by learning of something or receiving information. For instance, knowing that Paradise and Hell exist represents such knowledge. The second state is knowledge wherein truth is first acknowledged by the tongue—that Paradise and Hell are real—and then affirmed in the heart. This demands detailed investigation to verify the reality. Through such research, knowledge grows and verification increases, yielding what is called ⟪ar:علم معرفت⟫ knowledge of recognition (ʿilm al-maʿrifa), the post-faith understanding. The deeper the knowledge becomes—understanding what Paradise and Hell are, where they lie, how long they endure, who shall dwell there, how long the stay shall be—the more confirmation increases, making both ⟪ar:علمی صورت⟫ the form of knowledge and ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ faith stronger and more perfect.

| Manifest knowledge | Knowledge of recognition |
| --- | --- |
| Awareness | Knowledge coupled with verification and research |
| Imperfect faith | Perfect faith |

Allah has established faith—not mere knowledge—as the criterion for those who prosper. He declares, in ⟪ar:سورۃ البقرۃ⟫ the Chapter of the Cow:

> ⟪quran 2:2-5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ذَلِكَ ٱلْكِتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًۭى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *That book, whereof there is no doubt, a guidance to those who are Al-Muttaqun [the pious and righteous persons who fear Allah much (abstain from all kinds of sins and evil deeds which He has forbidden) and love Allah much (perform all kinds of good deeds which He has ordained)]. Who believe in the Ghaib and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and spend out of what we have provided for them [i.e. give Zakat, spend on themselves, their parents, their children, their wives, etc., and also give charity to the poor and also in Allah's Cause - Jihad, etc.]. And who believe in (the Quran and the Sunnah) which has been sent down (revealed) to you (Muhammad Peace be upon him ) and in [the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel), etc.] which were sent down before you and they believe with certainty in the Hereafter. (Resurrection, recompense of their good and bad deeds, Paradise and Hell, etc.). They are on (true) guidance from their Lord, and they are the successful.*

Mark that Allah said ⟪ar:'یؤمنون بالغیب'⟫ they believe in the unseen, not ⟪ar:'یعلمون بالغیب'⟫ that they know the unseen. The successful ones are those who attained faith in all these matters. For faith is always kindled through the unseen, through knowledge of the invisible. Faith is impossible without verification, and verification cannot be achieved without investigation. The degrees of faith, in sum, are established upon the ladders of knowledge.[^cite-8]

## The Distinction Between Islam and Faith

To clarify the boundary between Islam and faith, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 49:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ قَالَتِ ٱلْأَعْرَابُ آمَنَّا ۖ قُل لَّمْ تُؤْمِنُوا۟ وَلَكِن قُولُوٓا۟ أَسْلَمْنَا وَلَمَّا يَدْخُلِ ٱلْإِيمَنُ فِى قُلُوبِكُمْ ۖ وَإِن تُطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ لَا يَلِتْكُم مِّنْ أَعْمَلِكُمْ شَيْـًٔا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌ ‎⟫
> *The bedouins say: "We believe." Say: "You believe not but you only say, 'We have surrendered (in Islam),' for Faith has not yet entered your hearts. But if you obey Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), He will not decrease anything in reward for your deeds. Verily, Allah is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful."*

> ⟪quran 49:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَمُنُّونَ عَلَيْكَ أَنْ أَسْلَمُوا۟ ۖ قُل لَّا تَمُنُّوا۟ عَلَىَّ إِسْلَمَكُم ۖ بَلِ ٱللَّهُ يَمُنُّ عَلَيْكُمْ أَنْ هَدَىٰكُمْ لِلْإِيمَنِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَادِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *They regard as favour upon you (O Muhammad PBUH) that they have embraced Islam. Say: "Count not your Islam as a favour upon me. Nay, but Allah has conferred a favour upon you, that He has guided you to the Faith, if you indeed are true.*

> ⟪quran 51:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَمَا وَجَدْنَا فِيهَا غَيْرَ بَيْتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *But We found not there any household of the Muslims except one [i.e. Lout (Lot) and his two daughters].*

These verses make clear that Islam and faith stand as two distinct realities. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ The Imam teaches that faith dwells within the realm of Islam, yet Islam does not necessarily enter the realm of faith. This is because Islam concerns itself with outward utterance and deed, whilst faith pertains to the inward: the sincerity of the heart. ⟪ar:صادق آل محمد علیہ السلام⟫ When questioned about the true nature of Islam and faith, he replied: "Faith belongs wholly to the heart; Islam concerns observance of the laws of marriage, inheritance, and other contractual matters." Whoever enters the circle of Islam renders his blood inviolable, his honor protected, his property and wealth secure. The distinction between them he explained as follows:

It is possible for a Muslim to remain unaware of faith, yet every faithful one must necessarily be Muslim first. Maulānā ʿAlī clarifies: Islam requires only the tongue's confession, whereas faith demands knowledge of the heart united with that confession. When Allah grants a person the capacity to know Him, His Messenger, and the Imām, and that person confesses with his tongue, then he becomes a believer.[^cite-9]

Ayatollah Khamenei emphasizes: "To grant a person knowledge is Allah's complete proof and evidence, a manifestation of His grace and favor." The slave's confession with his tongue represents a favor from Allah bestowed upon whomever He wills. Thus knowledge itself constitutes the supreme grace—the profound gift that Allah places within the servant's heart.

Confession is a work of the heart; the knowledge Allah has created there takes outward form in vocal declaration. Therefore, no argument stands against one to whom Allah has not granted knowledge. Such a person is obliged to act according to what he understands and to refrain from acting on matters he does not know, bringing unknowns to those possessed of knowledge. For Allah says:

> ⟪quran 21:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent not before you (O Muhammad PBUH) but men to whom We inspired, so ask the people of the Reminder [Scriptures - the Taurat (Torah), the Injeel (Gospel)] if you do not know.*

## The Minimum Conditions of Faith, Polytheism, and Error

When Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (upon him be peace) was asked, "O ʿAlī! What is the lowest condition that makes a person a believer, and what is the lowest condition that makes one a polytheist and astray?" he replied:

### The Minimal Condition of True Faith

The lowest condition constituting faith is this: When Allah grants a servant the knowledge of Him, let that servant acknowledge God in obedience. Should Allah grant knowledge of His Messenger, let him acknowledge the Messenger in obedience. Should Allah grant him perception of the proof of His existence upon earth and His witness among creation—the Imām—let him believe in the guardianship (wilāya) of the Imām and acknowledge it in obedience. Such a one becomes a believer. If he knows nothing beyond this, he should obey whatever command comes to him and refrain from whatever prohibition reaches him.

### The Minimal Condition of Polytheism

The lowest condition making one a polytheist: that he take something forbidden by Allah and imagine Allah has commanded it. He supposes the object of his worship has received Allah's command, though that object is other than Allah. To worship anything besides Allah constitutes polytheism obligatorily. Such a person shall be deemed a polytheist.[^cite-10]

### The Minimal Condition of Misguidance

The lowest condition leading one astray: failure to seek the proof of Allah upon earth and neglect of knowledge regarding the witness of Allah among creation, together with refusal to follow that witness. Whoever is deprived of knowledge of the Imām has surely gone astray.

## The True Purpose of the Islamic Summons

The Prophet's (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) purpose in issuing the outward summons (daʿwa) to Islam was ultimately to invite humanity to the inward truth. Yet since the inward can only be reached through the outward, he first summoned the people to Islam, declaring: "Say ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *There is no god save Allah*—you shall succeed." Then, after gathering had heard this proclamation, those who had ripened in the Islamic summons and whose dispositions had become fitted for the invitation were themselves invited to the word of the inner faith—the secret summons.[^cite-11] Thus ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *There is no god save Allah* represents the word of the outward, Islamic summons, whilst ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫ *There is nothing worshipped save Him* marks the inner dimension. The distinction appears in ⟪ar:سورۃ مزمل⟫ the Chapter of Enwrapped, where Allah speaks of the inward invitation:

> ⟪quran 73:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(He Alone is) the Lord of the east and the west, La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He). So take Him Alone as Wakîl (Disposer of your affairs).*

The ⟪ar:علی⟫ numerical value of "Alī" equals 110, which is the same as the numerical value of ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫ *(There is nothing worshipped save) Him*. The verse subtly indicates that ʿAlī is the Lord of East and West—take him as your guardian! Thus the verse points to the inner summons.

The Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace) declared: "So long as the people of my Community continue to proclaim ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *There is no god save Allah* and fulfill all the duties and obligations of the religion, the wrath of Allah shall remain distant from them." Otherwise, that word shall be hurled in their faces, and they shall be told: "You are liars! You have not verified the truth." The fulfillment of religion's obligations is itself the journey from ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *There is no god save Allah* (the external profession) to ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫ *There is nothing worshipped save Him* (the inner reality).

## The Paradise of Islam and the Paradise of Faith

When ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Originator Most High spoke the word of unity (tawḥīd) through ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal), He did not merely declare, "There is no god," but rather ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫ proclaimed *(There is nothing worshipped save) Him*. This very word is that by which one attains elevation and majesty:

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah bears witness that La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), and the angels, and those having knowledge (also give this witness); (He is always) maintaining His creation in Justice. La ilah illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.*

The believers cannot come to completion until they embrace the guardianship of ʿAlī and his children and assent to his inner summons. Through the words of the Holy Prophet (upon him be peace), the Muslims entered Allah's Paradise marked by ⟪ar:اسلام⟫ Islam. The sword of Islamic law protects them, yet there is no assurance of permanence in this sacred formula unless they possess ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ the speaking (nāṭiqa) dimension—the interpreting principle—by which the word is realized. This ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ speaking dimension, bearing the ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ noble form, is achieved when the inner meaning of ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫ *(There is nothing worshipped save) Him* becomes actualized within the utterance ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *There is no god save Allah*. When deeds are thereby constituted ⟪ar:ولایت امیر المومنین⟫ in the guardianship of the Prince of the Faithful through the Shariah's covenant, one attains what is ⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫ sealed with goodness—the ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) that assures permanence.

| Outward formula | Inward reality |
| --- | --- |
| *There is no god save Allah* | *(There is nothing worshipped save) Him* |
| Islamic summons | Esoteric summons |
| Primary perfection | Secondary perfection |

There exists but one degree in Islam; no Muslim stands above another in respect of Islamic station, as the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) affirmed: "All Muslims are brothers and sisters." The Messenger of Allah thereby established all believers on a single level, marking the attribute of the external. Yet the inner dimension possesses a power that not only strengthens outer conviction but brings believers to diverse ranks and stations. Within the circle of faith dwell hierarchies upon hierarchies wherein no believer equals another in standing.
