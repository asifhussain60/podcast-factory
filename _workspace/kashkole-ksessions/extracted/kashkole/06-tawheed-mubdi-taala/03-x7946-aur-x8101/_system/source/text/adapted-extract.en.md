## What is Faith?

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) said: "Believing in Allah is the best of all deeds, and therefore its reward is the greatest." He continued, "Faith is the testimony that ⟪ar-quote:أشھد ان لا الہ الا اللہ وحدہ لا شریک لہ و أشھد ان محمد عبدہ و رسولہ⟫ there is no god but Allah—He alone, with no partner—and that Muḥammad is His servant and messenger." Heaven is truth, hell is truth, resurrection from the grave and the coming of the Day of Judgment are true. ⟪ar:انبیا و مرسلین⟫ Affirming the Prophets and Messengers, ⟪ar:ائمہ طاہرین علیہم السلام⟫ confirming the Imāms of the Purified Household, ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ obeying the Imām and accepting his words—all of this constitutes faith. It means following the commandments that Allah has enjoined upon His servants and keeping away from what He has forbidden.[^cite-1]

The Imām taught that faith comprises three essential dimensions:

| **Affirmation of the Heart** | **Speech of the Tongue** | **Action of the Limbs** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Heartfelt conviction | Words spoken aloud | Bodily deeds |

When asked whether affirmation of the heart and utterance of the tongue alone suffice, he replied: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان عمَلُ کلُّہُ⟫ "Faith is action in its totality." Speech itself is an action performed by the tongue. Similarly, believing and acquiring knowledge constitute an action of the heart, while acting through all bodily limbs in obedience to Allah and His Messenger constitutes physical action. Thus **faith is the sum total of all actions**.

| **Action of the Heart** | **Action of the Tongue** | **Action of the Body** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Practice of belief and knowledge | Practice of speech | Actions of all faculties |

Allah, Most High, declares:

> ⟪quran 2:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and do righteous deeds—they are the dwellers of Paradise; they shall abide therein forever.*

Throughout the Qur'an, whenever Allah mentions faith, He also mentions righteous deeds. These two are inseparable. Though faith pertains to the heart and action to the limbs, their conjunction produces the true believer.[^cite-2] ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ The Commander of the Faithful ⟪ar:نہج البلاغہ⟫ stated in Nahj al-Balāgha: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان اعتقاد بالقلب و عمل بالارکان و اقرار بالسان⟫ "Faith is conviction in the heart, practice through the limbs, and acknowledgment by the tongue." Faith originates in the heart and culminates in righteous deeds, which include confession of the tongue. From within, faith emerges and moves outward through the tongue, the faculties, and the jaw. Islam, by contrast, emanates from the tongue—through ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ the testimony that there is no god but Allah—and reaches the limbs and faculties. The inner light of faith radiates from within; Islam is the outer illumination. Islam concerns the external Sharīʿah, while faith concerns its inner meaning.

### The Linguistic and Theological Meaning of Faith

Faith (īmān) literally signifies affirmation or confirmation; in another sense, it means to grant security. The verb of faith is continuous and not merely transitive; it requires a participle as well. Two elements are thus essential: the act of affirming and the one being affirmed. If faith means to confirm, then one must ask: what or whom is being confirmed? The person confirmed receives the name ⟪ar:مُصَدِّق⟫ (one who confirms). If faith means to grant security, a participle is similarly required—who or what receives that security?

The Prophet Muḥammad (peace and blessings be upon him) united both meanings in a single ḥadīth: ⟪ar-quote:ھِینَ اَلمُؤمِن مَن آمَنَ جَورِہِ بِوَایقَۃ⟫ "A believer is one who ensures his neighbor is safe from his harm." From both meanings, a person is called ⟪ar:اَلمُؤمِنُ⟫ (the Believer), a name derived from ⟪ar:اسمآء الحسنی⟫ the Most Beautiful Names of Allah. A person is called a believer because he affirms Allah and the ⟪ar:وَعَدُ وَعِیدْ⟫ promise and threat (of reward and punishment), and because Allah has granted him protection (amān) from His fatal chastisement.

| **Agent** | **Action** | **Effect** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Believer | Confirmation | Of Allah and His Messenger |
| The Believer | Granting security (amān) | To creatures |
| Allah | Granting security (amān) | From punishment |

## Faith of the Heart, Tongue, and Body

The Imām taught that "Allah has made faith obligatory and necessary for every organ of the son of Adam and has apportioned it among them. Thus not a single organ among the twelve organs of the son of Adam is exempt from the obligation of practicing faith. The faith required of each organ differs from that of another." Allah has distributed faith among man's faculties as follows:

| **Organ** | Heart | Tongue | Ears | Eyes | Hands | Feet | Face | Genitals |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Number** | 1 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 1 | 1 |

### Faith of the Heart

The heart, the source of understanding and insight, is the master of the entire body. No faculty may function without its assent and command. The faith required of the heart encompasses confession, knowledge, belief, acceptance, and acknowledgment. The believer must confess in his heart that Allah is One, that there is no deity save Him. He is ⟪ar-quote:وحدہ لا شریک لہ⟫ alone and without peer, having neither consort nor offspring. The Messengers—beginning with ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ Muḥammad the Chosen (peace and blessings be upon him)—are the servants of Allah and His authentic Messengers. The heart must then acknowledge all that Allah has sent down: the Book and the Prophet. Following this acknowledgment, the heart must acquire the knowledge of the limits (ḥudūd)—the esoteric principles that strengthen faith. Thus the heart's faith comprises both practical and scientific dimensions.[^cite-3]

The Qur'an attests to this in several verses:

> ⟪quran 16:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَن كَفَرَ بِٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ إِيمَنِهِۦٓ إِلَّا مَنْ أُكْرِهَ وَقَلْبُهُۥ مُطْمَىِٕنٌّۢ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ وَلَكِن مَّن شَرَحَ بِٱلْكُفْرِ صَدْرًۭا فَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Whoso disbelieveth in Allah after believing—save he who is forced thereto while his heart remaineth firm in faith—but such as open their breasts to disbelief, upon them is wrath from Allah, and theirs is a mighty punishment.*

> ⟪quran 2:284⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لِّلَّهِ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَإِن تُبْدُوا۟ مَا فِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ أَوْ تُخْفُوهُ يُحَاسِبْكُم بِهِ ٱللَّهُ ۖ فَيَغْفِرُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَن يَشَآءُ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌ ‎⟫
> *To Allah belongeth whatsoever is in the heavens and whatsoever is in the earth. Whether ye make known what is in your minds or hide it, Allah will bring you to account for it. He forgiveth whom He will and punisheth whom He will. Allah is Able to do all things.*

> ⟪quran 5:41⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ لَا يَحْزُنكَ ٱلَّذِينَ يُسَرِعُونَ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ آمَنَّا بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَلَمْ تُؤْمِن قُلُوبُهُمْ ۛ وَمِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَادُوا۟ ۛ سَمَّعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ سَمَّعُونَ لِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ لَمْ يَأْتُوكَ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ مَوَاضِعِهِۦ ۖ يَقُولُونَ إِنْ أُوتِيتُمْ هَذَا فَخُذُوهُ وَإِن لَّمْ تُؤْتَوْهُ فَٱحْذَرُوا۟ ۚ وَمَن يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ فِتْنَتَهُۥ فَلَن تَمْلِكَ لَهُۥ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ شَيْـًٔا ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَمْ يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ أَن يُطَهِّرَ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا خِزْىٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O Messenger! Let not those who race into disbelief grieve thee—those who say "We believe" with their mouths while their hearts believe not; and among the Jews are those who listen to falsehood, hearkening to others who come not unto thee. They distort the words from their places, saying "If ye are given this, take it, but if ye are not given it, beware!" Whomsoever Allah willeth to try, thou hast no power to avail him aught against Allah. Those are they for whom Allah willeth not to purify their hearts. Theirs in this world is ignominy, and in the Hereafter a mighty punishment.*

> ⟪quran 13:28⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَتَطْمَىِٕنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ تَطْمَىِٕنُّ ٱلْقُلُوبُ ‎⟫
> *Those who believe and whose hearts find rest in the remembrance of Allah. Lo! In the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest.*

### Faith of the Tongue

The faith required of the tongue concerns utterance and articulation. It means expressing in words the confession and belief that the heart has formed. As Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 3:84⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْنَا وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَإِسْمَعِيلَ وَإِسْحَقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَٱلْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَآ أُوتِىَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَٱلنَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُۥ مُسْلِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say: We believe in Allah and that which is revealed unto us and that which was revealed unto Abraham and Ishmael and Isaac and Jacob and the tribes, and that which was vouchsafed unto Moses and Jesus and the prophets from their Lord. We make no distinction between any of them, and unto Him we have surrendered.*

> ⟪quran 33:70⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَقُولُوا۟ قَوْلًۭا سَدِيدًۭا ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! Observe your duty to Allah and speak words that are just and right.*

> ⟪quran 18:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيُؤْمِن وَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيَكْفُرْ ۚ إِنَّآ أَعْتَدْنَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ نَارًا أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ سُرَادِقُهَا ۚ وَإِن يَسْتَغِيثُوا۟ يُغَاثُوا۟ بِمَآءٍۢ كَٱلْمُهْلِ يَشْوِى ٱلْوُجُوهَ ۚ بِئْسَ ٱلشَّرَابُ وَسَآءَتْ مُرْتَفَقًا ‎⟫
> *And say: The truth is from your Lord. Whosoever willeth may believe, and whosoever willeth may disbelieve. Lo! We have prepared for disbelievers Fire. Its tent encloseth them. If they cry for help they are helped with water like unto molten lead which burneth the faces. Calamitous is the drink and ill the resting-place!*

### Faith of the Ears

The ears must listen attentively to the command of Allah and heed it fully. They must not hear what is forbidden or displeasing to Allah. The Qur'an emphasizes this obligation:

> ⟪quran 4:140⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنْ إِذَا سَمِعْتُمْ آيَتِ ٱللَّهِ يُكْفَرُ بِهَا وَيُسْتَهْزَأُ بِهَا فَلَا تَقْعُدُوا۟ مَعَهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّكُمْ إِذًۭا مِّثْلُهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ جَامِعُ ٱلْمُنَفِقِينَ وَٱلْكَفِرِينَ فِى جَهَنَّمَ جَمِيعًا ‎⟫
> *And it hath been revealed unto you in the Book that when ye hear the revelations of Allah rejected and derided, sit not with them (the rejectors) till they engage in some other discourse. Lo! In that case ye would be like unto them. Lo! Allah will gather hypocrites and disbelievers, all together, in Hell.*

> ⟪quran 6:68⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخُوضُونَ فِىٓ آيَتِنَا فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦ ۚ وَإِمَّا يُنسِيَنَّكَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَلَا تَقْعُدْ بَعْدَ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And when thou seest those who engage in false discourse concerning Our revelations, turn away from them till they engage in some other discourse. And shouldst thou forget, then, after remembrance, sit not in the company of wrongdoing folk.*

> ⟪quran 39:18⟫
> ⟪ar:فَبَشِّرْ عِبَادِ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَمِعُونَ ٱلْقَوْلَ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ أَحْسَنَهُۥٓ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمْ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *Give glad tidings unto My slaves who listen to the Word and follow the best thereof. Those are they whom Allah hath guided, and those are men of understanding.*

> ⟪quran 23:1-4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَدْ أَفْلَحَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ فِى صَلَاتِهِمْ خَشِعُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ ٱللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَوٰةِ فَعِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *Successful are the believers, those who are humble in their prayer, those who turn away from vain discourse, and those who are active in almsgiving.*

### Faith of the Eyes

Allah has enjoined the eyes to refrain from beholding what is forbidden and to lower the gaze from unlawful things. This constitutes the faith of the eyes:

> ⟪quran 24:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Tell the believers to lower their gaze and guard their private parts. That is purer for them. Lo! Allah is aware of what they do.*

### Faith of the Private Parts

The inner meaning is that a believing man should not gaze upon his brother's private parts and should protect his own private parts from the gaze of others. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq explained that all Qur'anic verses concerning protection of the private parts aim to safeguard them from adultery. Yet the foregoing verse implies refraining from looking at one's own private parts. Regarding the simultaneous duties of heart, tongue, eye, and ear, Allah states:

> ⟪quran 17:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْبَصَرَ وَٱلْفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَانَ عَنْهُ مَسْـُٔولًۭا ‎⟫
> *Follow not that whereof thou hast no knowledge. Lo! The hearing and the sight and the heart—of each of these it will be asked.*

> ⟪quran 41:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَن يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَآ أَبْصَرُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَكِن ظَنَنتُمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *You could not hide from your ears, nor your eyes, nor your skins testifying against you. But ye deemed that Allah knew not much of what ye do.*

### Faith of the Hands

Allah has enjoined both hands to refrain from unlawful acts and to employ themselves in charity, kindness, maintaining kinship ties, struggle in Allah's cause, and ablution:

> ⟪quran 5:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ فَٱغْسِلُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْمَرَافِقِ وَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِرُئُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُمْ جُنُبًۭا فَٱطَّهَّرُوا۟ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَآءَ أَحَدٌۭ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَمَسْتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا۟ مَآءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا۟ صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا فَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُم مِّنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُم مِّنْ حَرَجٍۢ وَلَكِن يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! When ye rise for prayer, wash your faces and your hands up to the elbows, and wipe your heads and (wash) your feet up to the ankles. And if ye are unclean, purify yourselves. But if ye are ill or on a journey, or one of you cometh from the closet, or ye have touched women, and ye find not water, then go to clean high ground and rub your faces and hands with some of it. Allah would not place burden on you, but He would purify you and would perfect His grace upon you, that ye may give thanks.*

> ⟪quran 8:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ زَحْفًۭا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلْأَدْبَارَ ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! When ye meet those who disbelieve in battle, turn not your backs to them.*

> ⟪quran 47:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ فَضَرْبَ ٱلرِّقَابِ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَثْخَنتُمُوهُمْ فَشُدُّوا۟ ٱلْوَثَاقَ فَإِمَّا مَنًّۢا بَعْدُ وَإِمَّا فِدَآءً حَتَّىٰ تَضَعَ ٱلْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا ۚ ذَلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَآءُ ٱللَّهُ لَٱنتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ وَلَكِن لِّيَبْلُوَا۟ بَعْضَكُم بِبَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱلَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ فَلَن يُضِلَّ أَعْمَلَهُمْ ‎⟫
> *So, when ye meet those who disbelieve, smite at their necks until when ye have thoroughly subdued them, bind a bond upon them. Then grant them grace or ransom until war shall lay down her load. Thus are ye commanded. And those who are slain in the way of Allah, He rendereth not their actions vain.*

Thus Allah has made striking the necks in battle obligatory; this deed of the hands constitutes part of faith.

### Faith of the Feet

The feet must walk in the path of obedience to Allah. One should not walk toward deeds of disobedience; rather, every step should proceed toward the command and pleasure of Allah:

> ⟪quran 17:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَمْشِ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا ‎⟫
> *Walk not in the earth exultantly. Lo! Thou canst not rend the earth, nor canst thou stretch to the height of the hills.*

> ⟪quran 31:19⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱقْصِدْ فِى مَشْيِكَ وَٱغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلْأَصْوَتِ لَصَوْتُ ٱلْحَمِيرِ ‎⟫
> *Walk with modesty, and lower thy voice. Lo! The harshest of all voices is the voice of the ass.*

> ⟪quran 62:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا نُودِىَ لِلصَّلَوٰةِ مِن يَوْمِ ٱلْجُمُعَةِ فَٱسْعَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! When the call is heard for the prayer of the day of congregation, haste unto remembrance of Allah and leave your trading. That is better for you, if ye did but know.*

> ⟪quran 22:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ لْيَقْضُوا۟ تَفَثَهُمْ وَلْيُوفُوا۟ نُذُورَهُمْ وَلْيَطَّوَّفُوا۟ بِٱلْبَيْتِ ٱلْعَتِيقِ ‎⟫
> *Let them make an end of their filthiness and pay their vows and go around the Ancient House.*

On the Day of Resurrection, Allah reveals that hands and feet shall testify against their owners:

> ⟪quran 36:65⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ نَخْتِمُ عَلَىٰٓ أَفْوَهِهِمْ وَتُكَلِّمُنَآ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَتَشْهَدُ أَرْجُلُهُم بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
> *This day We seal their mouths, and their hands speak out to Us, and their feet bear witness as to what they used to earn.*

### Faith of the Face

Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 22:77⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱرْكَعُوا۟ وَٱسْجُدُوا۟ وَٱعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ وَٱفْعَلُوا۟ ٱلْخَيْرَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! Bow down and prostrate yourselves and worship your Lord and do the right, that ye may prosper.*

In another verse He says:

> ⟪quran 72:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنَّ ٱلْمَسَجِدَ لِلَّهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا۟ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ أَحَدًۭا ‎⟫
> *And the places of worship belong to Allah, so do not call upon anyone along with Allah.*

Thus Allah has obligated the purification of the limbs and their engagement in prayer, and He has designated prayer as faith in His Book. When Allah commanded the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings be upon him) to change the direction of prayer from ⟪ar:بیت المقدس⟫ the House of the Sacred to ⟪ar:بیت الحرام⟫ the Sacred House (the Kaʿbah), people asked, "O Messenger of Allah, what of our previous prayers?"

> ⟪quran 2:143⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Thus have We appointed you a middle nation, that ye may be witnesses against mankind and that the Messenger may be a witness against you. We appointed the Qiblah which ye formerly observed only that We might know those who follow the Messenger, from those who turn back on their heels. Though it was hard save for those whom Allah hath guided. But Allah would not make your faith of no effect. Lo! Allah is Full of Pity, Merciful toward mankind.*

**In this verse, Allah explicitly designates prayer as faith.**[^cite-4] 

Whoever departs this world having protected all limbs from transgression, with each organ having fulfilled its duty with excellence, shall receive complete faith from Allah and enter Paradise. Conversely, one who betrays the covenant of Allah even slightly, or transgresses His command through these organs, shall meet Allah in a state of incomplete faith. Whoever enters the circle of the Imam al-Zamān (the Imam of the Age) through the covenant and then neither acts nor acquires knowledge shall not be called a believer. Mere verbal faith without practice confers no benefit; such a person possesses no standing before Allah and the Imam (peace be upon him), but rather stands outside the circle of the Imam of the Age.

## The Three Types of Faith

⟪ar:قرآن حکیم⟫ The Holy Qur'an distinguishes three grades of faith:

| **1** | Pure Faith (īmān khāliṣ) |
| --- | --- |
| **2** | Deficient Faith (īmān nāqiṣ) |
| **3** | Faith Tainted with Polytheism (īmān mashbūh bil-shirk) |

### Pure Faith

Those possessed of pure faith are those whom Allah Himself commends and to whom He gives glad tidings of Paradise. These are the people who embody both pillars of faith—affirmation (taṣdīq) and security (amān). Though their bodies dwell in this world—⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫ the abode of falsehood—their refined souls ⟪ar:دار الصدق و صفا⟫ inhabit the realm of truth and purity. In outward appearance they live within the world, yet their inner vision is fixed upon the Hereafter; whereas ordinary Muslims attend to the Hereafter while their outer lives remain in this world.

As for amān (security): when such souls ⟪ar:نفوس لطیفۃ⟫ depart this life and enter ⟪ar:دار السلام⟫ the Abode of Peace, they shall be secure from every trial and tribulation that befalls the body after death. For them, death is a perfuming of the soul and spirit, for through death they are completely liberated from ⟪ar:دار کدورت⟫ the abode of turbidity and shall be safe forever. This is what the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) meant when he said: "⟪ar-quote:اَلمَوتُ رِیحاَنَۃُ المُؤمِن⟫ Death is good news (literally, a sweet fragrance) for the believer." ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq pointed to his throat and said: "When the soul of My believer reaches this point, he rejoices!"[^cite-5]

⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی علیہ السلام⟫ ⟪ar:ائمہ طاھرین⟫ The Commander of the Faithful and the Imāms of the Purified Household hold ⟪ar:ولی أمر⟫ supreme authority as the guardians of the divine command. The Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) declared: "Those addressed in the Qur'an as ⟪ar-quote:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ 'O ye who believe!' are the leadership and guides. ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, the Commander of the Faithful, and his descendants shall remain the masters of this sacred authority as long as believers answering to this name endure." Since this appellation encompasses all believers, both ⟪ar:مُتَقَدِّمِینَ⟫ those who came before and ⟪ar:مُتَأَخِّرِینَ⟫ those who come after, the religious leadership of the Imāms shall endure ⟪ar:اِلیٰ یَومِ الدِّینَ⟫ until the Day of Judgment. ⟪ar:محمد و علی(ﷺ)⟫ Muḥammad and ʿAlī (upon them be peace)—whoever disobeys them disobeys Allah and His Messenger, becoming ⟪ar:خارجی⟫ a denier, and shall die in ignorance. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger and those charged with authority among you. If ye differ in anything among yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger, if ye do believe in Allah and the Last Day. That is best, and most suitable for final determination.*

### Deficient Faith

The second category comprises those with deficient faith. These are people who have newly ⟪ar:دعوت ایمانیۃ⟫ entered the divine summons and are still ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ among those newly answering the call. They are ignorant of religious matters and lack knowledge. Their example is that of a newborn child who, though complete in physical form and possessing all the bodily organs that characterize humanity, is utterly dependent upon protection and guidance at every moment and step, lacking the power and ability to use these faculties independently. Such ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ initiates at the early stages of the summons (daʿwa) are called believers, yet they depend entirely upon the spiritual nurture of their elder guides. This defective station of faith Allah has clarified in this verse, where He says:

> ⟪quran 4:136⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ آمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ مِن قَبْلُ ۚ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَلًۢا بَعِيدًا ‎⟫
> *O ye who believe! Believe in Allah and His Messenger, and the Scripture which He hath sent down to His Messenger and the Scripture which He sent down before. Whoso disbelieveth in Allah and His angels and His Scriptures and His messengers and the Last Day, he verily hath wandered far astray.*

In this verse, Allah says: "O ye who believe, believe in Allah and His Messenger!" If those addressed were already complete believers, it would be redundant to command them to believe. ⟪ar:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ Allah testifies to their faith by addressing them thus, yet immediately He says: "Believe!" What, then, is the meaning of this command to believe after acknowledging their faith? The answer becomes clear: Allah is addressing those believers at a lower station, urging them: "Perfect your faith." They are being told: "You have recently entered the circle of faith and have been granted spiritual birth through the invitation. Now advance in your knowledge of the faith and become perfect believers. Remain with the righteous, your spiritual parents and teachers, and benefit from their guidance until you ascend to ⟪ar:دار الصدق و امان⟫ the Abode of Truth and Security, where you shall dwell in ⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫ the abode of falsehood no longer." Just as children progress through the training and education of their parents and teachers to reach the station of their elders, so too shall these believers advance. Allah illustrates this in the following verse:

> ⟪quran 52:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and whose seed follow them in faith, to them shall We join their seed, and We shall not deprive them of aught of their work. Every soul is a pledge for its works.*

Here, "seed" (dhurriyyah) necessarily refers to spiritual or causal offspring, not lineage. That is, the believers ⟪ar:مستجیبین⟫ of the lower rank who followed the Summoners (duʿāt) of the highest rank—in the end We shall unite them with those guides and elevate them to their station.

### Faith Tainted with Polytheism

This category comprises faith adulterated with polytheism, having no connection to true faith in Allah and His Messenger. Genuine faith in Islam means accepting the command that Allah's Messenger (may peace and blessings be upon him) transmitted through revelation without alteration, in its pristine state, without hesitation or doubt, for therein lies the purity and salvation of our souls. Allah commands: ⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ آتَیكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوہُ وَمَا نَھٰكُمْ عَنْہُ فَانتَھوا۟⟫ "Take what the Messenger gives you and refrain from what he forbids you." Do not intermix your personal opinion in the slightest with the command and obedience to the Messenger.[^cite-6] Whoever adheres directly to the Prophet's teaching in worship, in determining what is lawful and unlawful, and in all religious matters, without interposing personal opinion or conjecture—this person is a perfect believer. But whoever blends his desires with the Messenger's ordinance and employs opinion, supposition, and independent reasoning (ijtihād) to gratify his inclinations becomes not a believer but an innovator. Because he has associated his opinion with the divine truth, his faith becomes tainted with polytheism, leading him toward error rather than guidance, toward ruin rather than salvation. No good deed—however many prayers or fasts—shall profit him.

Consider the revelation sent down to the Messenger as water from the sky. Water remains pure and useful for drinking and purification of body, clothing, and all things only so long as it remains uncontaminated. When impurity overcomes the water, it becomes useless to the Muslim, even though it is still called water. Due to this flaw, it is fit neither for drinking nor for purification. Similarly, when the appetites of the soul are mixed into the ordinances of revelation, pure religion ceases to exist and transforms into innovation (bidʿah). The Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) said: "Every innovation is error, no matter how fair it may appear." Even after innovation enters the faith, each pillar of Islam remains in its outward place—the opponents of the Imāms pray as the Imāms pray—yet the distinction is clear. The prayers of those who oppose the Imāms shall "be returned unto their faces" because they have mingled the bitterness of polytheism and the filth of innovation with their faith. Concerning such people, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 12:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا يُؤْمِنُ أَكْثَرُهُم بِٱللَّهِ إِلَّا وَهُم مُّشْرِكُونَ ‎⟫
> *Most of them believe not in Allah except that they associate partners with Him.*

#### The Zakat of Polytheists

It is narrated that ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq reported that his father ⟪ar:مولانا محمد الباقر علیہ السلام⟫ Imām Muḥammad al-Bāqir asked ⟪ar:حضرت جابر بن عبد اللہ الانصاری رضی اللہ عنہ⟫ Jābir ibn ʿAbd Allāh al-Anṣārī: "O Jābir, has Allah made zakat obligatory on polytheists?" ⟪ar:حضرت جابر⟫ He answered: "No, Allah made zakat obligatory only upon Muslims." The Imām replied: "Have you not heard the words of Allah:

> ⟪quran 41:6-7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أَنَا۠ بَشَرٌۭ مِّثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ أَنَّمَآ إِلَهُكُمْ إِلَهٌۭ وَحِدٌۭ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُشْرِكِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَافِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say: "I am only a human like you. It is revealed to me that your God is One God; so take the straight way to Him and ask His forgiveness. Woe to the polytheists who do not give the zakat and who disbelieve in the Hereafter."*

⟪ar:حضرت جابر⟫ When Jābir heard this verse from the blessed lips of ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ the Imām, he felt as though he had never read it, despite its being in Allah's Book. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ Imām Jaʿfar explained: "This verse was revealed concerning those who entered into the Wilāyah (Mastership) of ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ the Commander of the Faithful, ⟪ar:مولانا علی علیہ السلام⟫ ʿAlī, yet withheld their zakat from those deserving of it." Thus the verse demonstrates that Allah has called Muslims who do not pay zakat to those worthy of it polytheists.[^cite-7]

## The Eight Grades of Faith

Just as Paradise possesses eight gates, faith too has eight degrees of perfection:

| **1** | Kindness to Parents—the highest station of faith |
| --- | --- |
| **2** | Beneficence toward relatives |
| **3** | Kindness toward orphans |
| **4** | Kindness toward the poor |
| **5** | Benevolence toward a neighbor bound by ties of kinship and faith |
| **6** | Favor toward a believing neighbor unrelated by blood |
| **7** | Kindness toward a neighbor joined by neither kinship nor faith |
| **8** | Benevolence toward travelers and those in servitude |

## Characteristics of the True Believer

A believer does not achieve complete faith until his intellect is perfected; he does not attain perfection of intellect until he cultivates within himself these ten qualities:

| **1** | He seeks only that which is good from the world. |
| --- | --- |
| **2** | He fears no evil from anyone. |
| **3** | He accounts his few good deeds as less significant than others' many good deeds. |
| **4** | He views his numerous faults as grave, yet judges others' many failings as slight. |
| **5** | He never tires of meeting others' needs. |
| **6** | He never wearies of seeking knowledge; throughout his life, he continues to acquire wisdom. |
| **7** | For him, patience far outweighs glory and renown. |
| **8** | He esteems poverty above wealth, as the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) said: "My poverty is my pride" (Faqrī fakhrī). |
| **9** | Little sustenance suffices for him. |
| **10** | When he encounters a virtuous person, he believes that person to be better and more pious than himself. |

## Precedence and Degrees in Faith

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) states: "If faith were uniform and admitted neither deficiency nor excess, then no believer would surpass another, and all would share equally in the rewards bestowed for faith, attaining identical rank." Yet the reality stands otherwise. Believers enter Paradise precisely because their faith achieves perfection; it is ⟪ar:تفضیل⟫ their superior faith—their ⟪ar:راجح⟫ weightier conviction—that grants them precedence in God's sight. Those ⟪ar:مقصرین⟫ whose faith remains flawed shall enter the Fire.[^cite-6]

On the differentiation of faith's degrees, the Imam explains: "God has made striving in faith a competition among the faithful, as horses race upon an appointed day." Thus the earliest believers to accept faith received their ranks according to their precedence. Neither does the believer who lags fall upon the early believer's station, nor is one virtuous raised above another virtuous: precedence itself constitutes the virtue distinguishing the first believers of the community from those who came after. ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him), held superiority over all other Companions precisely as the first to embrace faith. Had the earliest believers possessed no advantage over later ones, the last believer of this community would stand equal to the first. Had precedence not been established as a measure of nobility, many ⟪ar:ارباب فضل و کمال و زھد وتقوی⟫ among those endowed with virtue, perfection, asceticism, and godliness—and many ⟪ar:ارباب فضل و کمال⟫ possessing excellence and accomplishment—among the later generation would surpass the ancients in deed. They fast with intensity, pray abundantly, perform pilgrimage frequently, and exert themselves in striving and charity in God's path. Yet were precedence and primacy not the foundation of nobility, their excess in action would elevate them above the foremost. God, however, does not desire that one deferred in the faith's rank be elevated to the rank of the first, nor that he whom He has advanced in faith's station be held back. God has clarified this principle through these verses:

> ⟪quran 57:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَابِقُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ مَغْفِرَةٍۢ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَجَنَّةٍ عَرْضُهَا كَعَرْضِ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ أُعِدَّتْ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ ۚ ذَلِكَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ ذُو ٱلْفَضْلِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ ‎⟫
> *Race one with another in hastening towards Forgiveness from your Lord (Allah), and towards Paradise, the width whereof is as the width of heaven and earth, prepared for those who believe in Allah and His Messengers. That is the Grace of Allah which He bestows on whom He pleases. And Allah is the Owner of Great Bounty.*

> ⟪quran 56:10-11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلسَّبِقُونَ ٱلسَّبِقُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلْمُقَرَّبُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those foremost [(in Islamic Faith of Monotheism and in performing righteous deeds) in the life of this world on the very first call for to embrace Islam,] will be foremost (in Paradise). These will be those nearest to Allah.*

> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلسَّبِقُونَ ٱلْأَوَّلُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ وَٱلْأَنصَارِ وَٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّبَعُوهُم بِإِحْسَنٍۢ رَّضِىَ ٱللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا۟ عَنْهُ وَأَعَدَّ لَهُمْ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى تَحْتَهَا ٱلْأَنْهَرُ خَلِدِينَ فِيهَآ أَبَدًۭا ۚ ذَلِكَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ‎⟫
> *And the first to embrace Islam of the Muhajirun (those who migrated from Makkah to Al-Madinah) and the Ansar (the citizens of Al-Madinah who helped and gave aid to the Muhajirun) and also those who followed them exactly (in Faith). Allah is well-pleased with them as they are well-pleased with Him. He has prepared for them Gardens under which rivers flow (Paradise), to dwell therein forever. That is the supreme success.*

> *(And there is also a share in this booty) for the poor emigrants, who were expelled from their homes and their property, seeking Bounties from Allah and to please Him. And helping Allah (i.e. helping His religion) and His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH). Such are indeed the truthful (to what they say);- And those who, before them, had homes (in Al-Madinah) and had adopted the Faith, love those who emigrate to them, and have no jealousy in their breasts for that which they have been given (from the booty of Banî An-Nadîr), and give them (emigrants) preference over themselves, even though they were in need of that. And whosoever is saved from his own covetousness, such are they who will be the successful.*

In this verse, God distinguishes among several categories. The first are ⟪ar:محاجرین و انصار و تابعین⟫ the Emigrants, the Helpers, and the Followers. The initial rank belongs to ⟪ar:محاجرین اولین⟫ the earliest Emigrants; the second comprises ⟪ar:انصار⟫ the Helpers; and the third, ⟪ar:تابعین مخلصین⟫ the sincere Followers. This passage also invokes believers' seeking forgiveness to demonstrate the ancients' excellence. God then expounds on the virtues of His saints in these verses:

> *Those Messengers! We preferred some to others; to some of them Allah spoke (directly); others He raised to degrees (of honour); and to 'Iesa (Jesus), the son of Maryam (Mary), We gave clear proofs and evidences, and supported him with Ruh-ul-Qudus [Jibrael (Gabriel)]. If Allah had willed, succeeding generations would not have fought against each other, after clear Verses of Allah had come to them, but they differed - some of them believed and others disbelieved. If Allah had willed, they would not have fought against one another, but Allah does what He likes.*

Allah will exalt in degree those of you who believe, and those who have been granted knowledge. And Allah is Well-Acquainted with what you do.*

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ The Imam elaborates: "These verses delineate the distinctions among faith's degrees and the believer's varying conditions, illustrating their precedence." Yet faith without integrity avails nothing; should an early believer introduce or sustain a defect in his faith, mere primacy shall not benefit him. God declares:

The food (slaughtered cattle, eatable animals, etc.) of the people of the Scripture (Jews and Christians) is lawful to you and yours is lawful to them. (Lawful to you in marriage) are chaste women from the believers and chaste women from those who were given the Scripture (Jews and Christians) before your time, when you have given their due Mahr (bridal money given by the husband to his wife at the time of marriage), desiring chastity (i.e. taking them in legal wedlock) not committing illegal sexual intercourse, nor taking them as girl-friends. And whosoever disbelieves in the Oneness of Allah and in all the other Articles of Faith [i.e. His (Allah's), Angels, His Holy Books, His Messengers, the Day of Resurrection and Al-Qadar (Divine Preordainments)], then fruitless is his work, and in the Hereafter he will be among the losers.*

Yet should one confess and act whilst inwardly denying the truth, he remains no believer regardless of his external exertion—none of his deeds shall benefit him. God declares:

> ⟪quran 25:23⟫

> *And We shall turn to whatever deeds they (disbelievers, polytheists, sinners, etc.) did, and We shall make such deeds as scattered floating particles of dust.*

## Outward and Inward Faith

God's promised reward for faith in the Hereafter is Paradise. The reason for deferring this reward is that faith constitutes a trait encompassing all that is ⟪ar:خیراتِ بشریات⟫ good in humanity, elevating believers above the ⟪ar:فضائل الملائکۃ⟫ virtues of angels. Since none dwelling in this world can attain angelic station, the reward is reserved for the Hereafter, Paradise being promised. Similarly, disbelief—⟪ar:شَرُّ البشریات⟫ the worst of human traits—meets with God's ⟪ar:وعید⟫ threat of Hellfire, just as virtue meets ⟪ar:وعدہ⟫ His promise of reward.[^cite-8]

Faith comprises two dimensions: the outward and the inward.

### Outward Faith

Outward faith denotes the verbal affirmation of five matters:

| 1 | **Affirmation of God:** Confession that a single Creator exists—One, Eternal, Almighty, Wise, Lord of all creation. He alone possesses dominion; none shares His authority. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | **Confession of the angels,** God's noblest creation, whom He appointed to worship and serve Him, constituting His creation's trustees. God stationed them in positions of responsibility to execute His decrees. These angels never disobey God; they fulfill whatever they are commanded. |
| 3 | **Recognition of the Prophets and Messengers:** God has chosen certain servants as His representatives on earth, and appointed angels as intermediaries between Him and these elect. These angels convey God's message to His chosen servants, who in turn communicate His guidance and commandments to all creatures. |
| 4 | **Acknowledgment of the Books:** Confession that the revelations the Prophets received from the angels through divine inspiration were transmitted to their contemporaries in their own languages and styles. The message remains unchanging, yet its expression adapts to time and circumstance. |
| 5 | **Belief in the Resurrection:** Acknowledgment that the Day of Judgment will arrive, bringing renewal and transformation. On that day all creatures shall be raised, judged, and receive recompense—the righteous rewarded, the wicked punished. |

Believers are those who wholly believe in these five matters, as God states in His Book:

> ⟪quran 2:285⟫
> ⟪ar:‏آمَنَ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ ‎⟫
> *The Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) believes in what has been sent down to him from his Lord, and (so do) the believers. Each one believes in Allah, His Angels, His Books, and His Messengers. They say, "We make no distinction between one another of His Messengers" - and they say, "We hear, and we obey. (We seek) Your Forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the return (of all)."*

When a believer confesses these five outward matters with his tongue, he distinguishes himself from all Jews, Christians, and Zoroastrians. In every such utterance lies an implicit rejection of all other claimants to deity; through this confession, he obligates himself to prayer, fasting, pilgrimage, almsgiving, and striving in God's path.

### Inner Faith

Inner faith entails heartfelt affirmation of those five matters confessed verbally. Through this inward confirmation, the believer's inner faith becomes established. The deeper this confirmation, the loftier his faith's station. God has praised such believers in His Book and promised them Paradise.[^cite-9]

The paradise of outward faith differs from the paradise of inner faith. The Prophet (peace and blessings of God be upon him) alluded to this distinction: "Whoever ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ utters, 'There is no god but God,' shall enter Paradise"—then added: "Yet he who ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ sincerely utters, 'There is no god but God,' shall enter Paradise." The first group comprises those who speak ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ with the tongue alone, entering the paradise of ⟪ar:اسلام⟫ Islam (outward submission). The second comprises those who confess verbally while cultivating sincerity—heartfelt affirmation—thereby entering the paradise of ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ faith (inward belief).

| Verbal Confession Alone | Verbal Confession + Heartfelt Affirmation |
| --- | --- |
| Paradise of Islam | Paradise of Faith |

The attainment of inner faith requires disciplined reflection. This involves four elements:

| Reflection (tafakkur) | Deep contemplation and investigation of reality, proving its truth to oneself. |
| --- | --- |
| Consideration (iʿtibār) | Comparing one reality to another, discerning differences and relationships between them. |
| Fulfilling Its Conditions (qiyām bi-sharāʾiṭihā) | Meeting the requirements of this knowledge—performing what God has commanded concerning it. |
| Honoring Its Due (wājib ḥaqquhā) | Rendering full account of the knowledge that God has made obligatory. |

Entering the paradise of faith is no easy undertaking. God Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 3:142⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ جَهَدُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *Do you think that you will enter Paradise before Allah tests those of you who fought (in His Cause) and (also) tests those who are As-Sabirin (the patient ones, etc.)?*

## The Precedence of Faith Over Knowledge

The elevation of ranks depends upon knowledge and faith together, not upon action alone. The degrees of knowledge differ from the degrees of faith. In clarifying this distinction, God states:

> ⟪quran 58:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجَلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you are told to make room in the assemblies, (spread out and) make room. Allah will give you (ample) room (from His Mercy). And when you are told to rise up [for prayers, Jihad (holy fighting in Allah's Cause), or for any other good deed], rise up. Allah will exalt in degree those of you who believe, and those who have been granted knowledge. And Allah is Well-Acquainted with what you do.*

In this verse, God raises the ranks of those who believe and those who possess knowledge. Yet the believers are mentioned before the scholars—indicating that God elevates believers' ranks before elevating those of the knowledgeable. Faith takes precedence over knowledge because knowledge serves as the means by which faith increases; what cannot exist cannot increase. Faith, in other words, is prior to knowledge. In this very world, faith was created before knowledge. When the Messenger of God (peace and blessings of God be upon him) called people to Islam, he said: "Utter ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ 'There is no god but God,' and you shall prosper." The Prophet first commanded confession—the foremost condition of faith—without demanding detailed knowledge of it. He required only utterance and belief. Once people confessed, the stages of deepening confirmation commenced, during which ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ knowledge of this testimony became clear to them.[^cite-10]

God states: It is only when ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ the spiritual realm received ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal), whom ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Originator, Most High (Mubdiʿ Taʿālā) blessed with ⟪ar:علم ما کان سیکون⟫ knowledge of what was and what shall be, that faith was granted. Hence faith precedes knowledge.

Regarding the hierarchy among the learned, God declares that above every scholar stands one more knowing still. In this verse, ⟪ar:' فوق'⟫ the term "above" denotes the elevation and differentiation of ranks:

> ⟪quran 12:76⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَبَدَأَ بِأَوْعِيَتِهِمْ قَبْلَ وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَخْرَجَهَا مِن وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ۚ كَذَلِكَ كِدْنَا لِيُوسُفَ ۖ مَا كَانَ لِيَأْخِذَ أَخَاهُ فِى دِينِ ٱلْمَلِكِ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ نَرْفَعُ دَرَجَتٍۢ مَّن نَّشَآءُ ۗ وَفَوْقَ كُلِّ ذِى عِلْمٍ عَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *So he [Yusuf (Joseph)] began (the search) in their bags before the bag of his brother. Then he brought it out of his brother's bag. Thus did We plan for Yusuf (Joseph). He could not take his brother by the law of the king (as a slave), except that Allah willed it. (So Allah made the brothers to bind themselves with their way of "punishment, i.e. enslaving of a thief.") We raise to degrees whom We please, but over all those endowed with knowledge is the All-Knowing (Allah).*

### Knowledge and Faith

Knowledge is the conception the soul forms by apprehending a reality. When the soul confesses this conception through speech, affirms it in the heart, and embeds it within itself through bodily action—actualizing it—this conception is called faith.[^cite-11] Verification requires truthfulness, which is impossible without investigation. Consider: all Muslims know of Paradise, Hellfire, and the Bridge of Sirat; we affirm and confirm their existence through belief. Yet since these realities transcend ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫ the five senses, verification demands investigation to apprehend their reality fully.

| **Science** | **Faith** |
| --- | --- |
| Perception | Confirmation by conscience |
| Acknowledgment by tongue | Action by bodily faculties |
| Acceptance | Investigation |

In the scholar's soul, the form of knowledge is called ⟪ar:علم⟫ knowledge (ʿilm), while its confession, confirmation, and actualization in deed is called ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ faith (īmān). The essential reality is faith; knowledge merely increases it.[^cite-12]

### Verification and Research

Knowledge possesses a twofold aspect. The first is ⟪ar:علم ظاہر⟫ manifest knowledge (ʿilm ẓāhir)—preceding faith—comprising mere awareness obtained through information about a reality. For instance, knowing that Paradise and Hellfire exist. The second is knowledge confirmed through faith, wherein the tongue first affirms the truth of Paradise and Hell, followed by heart-confirmation. To fully verify such truths requires detailed investigation, through which knowledge grows and verification strengthens. This is ⟪ar:علم معرفت⟫ knowledge of gnosis (ʿilm maʿrifat)—following faith. As ⟪ar:علمی صورت⟫ knowledge grows through investigation, so too does confirmation increase, perfecting both ⟪ar:علم⟫ knowledge and ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ faith. The more one knows about Paradise, Hellfire, and the Bridge—their nature, location, duration, who enters them, and how long they remain—the stronger one's knowledge becomes, thereby deepening confirmation and strengthening faith.

| **Manifest Knowledge** | **Knowledge of Gnosis** |
| --- | --- |
| Awareness | Knowledge |
| Awareness | Verification; Investigation |
| Imperfect Faith | Perfect Faith |

⟪ar:سورۃ البقرۃ⟫ In the chapter of the Cow, God has made faith—not knowledge—the criterion of success:

> ⟪quran 2:2-5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ذَلِكَ ٱلْكِتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًۭى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *That book, whereof there is no doubt, a guidance to those who are Al-Muttaqun [the pious and righteous persons who fear Allah much (abstain from all kinds of sins and evil deeds which He has forbidden) and love Allah much (perform all kinds of good deeds which He has ordained)]. Who believe in the Ghaib and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and spend out of what we have provided for them [i.e. give Zakat, spend on themselves, their parents, their children, their wives, etc., and also give charity to the poor and also in Allah's Cause - Jihad, etc.]. And who believe in (the Quran and the Sunnah) which has been sent down (revealed) to you (Muhammad Peace be upon him ) and in [the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel), etc.] which were sent down before you and they believe with certainty in the Hereafter. (Resurrection, recompense of their good and bad deeds, Paradise and Hell, etc.). They are on (true) guidance from their Lord, and they are the successful.*

In this passage, God did not say ⟪ar:'یعلمون بالغیب'⟫ "those who know the unseen," but rather ⟪ar:'یؤمنون بالغیب'⟫ "those who believe in the unseen." Faith in the unseen is achieved through knowledge of the unseen; faith cannot exist without confirmation, and confirmation requires epistemology. In other words, the degrees of faith rest upon the rungs of knowledge.[^cite-13]

## The Distinction Between Islam and Faith

God clarifies the difference between Islam and faith:

> ⟪quran 49:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ قَالَتِ ٱلْأَعْرَابُ آمَنَّا ۖ قُل لَّمْ تُؤْمِنُوا۟ وَلَكِن قُولُوٓا۟ أَسْلَمْنَا وَلَمَّا يَدْخُلِ ٱلْإِيمَنُ فِى قُلُوبِكُمْ ۖ وَإِن تُطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ لَا يَلِتْكُم مِّنْ أَعْمَلِكُمْ شَيْـًٔا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌ ‎⟫
> *The bedouins say: "We believe." Say: "You believe not but you only say, 'We have surrendered (in Islam),' for Faith has not yet entered your hearts. But if you obey Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), He will not decrease anything in reward for your deeds. Verily, Allah is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful."*

> ⟪quran 49:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَمُنُّونَ عَلَيْكَ أَنْ أَسْلَمُوا۟ ۖ قُل لَّا تَمُنُّوا۟ عَلَىَّ إِسْلَمَكُم ۖ بَلِ ٱللَّهُ يَمُنُّ عَلَيْكُمْ أَنْ هَدَىٰكُمْ لِلْإِيمَنِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *They regard as favour upon you (O Muhammad PBUH) that they have embraced Islam. Say: "Count not your Islam as a favour upon me. Nay, but Allah has conferred a favour upon you, that He has guided you to the Faith, if you indeed are true.*

> ⟪quran 51:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَمَا وَجَدْنَا فِيهَا غَيْرَ بَيْتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *But We found not there any household of the Muslims except one [i.e. Lout (Lot) and his two daughters].*

These verses establish that Islam and faith constitute two distinct realities. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) teaches that whilst faith falls within the domain of Islam, Islam does not encompass the domain of faith. This is because Islam pertains to outward utterance and conduct, whereas faith concerns the inward—the sincerity of the heart. ⟪ar:صادق آل محمد علیہ السلام⟫ The Truthful One of the Family of Muḥammad (peace be upon him) was once asked about the reality of Islam and faith. He replied: "Faith is entirely of the heart, whilst Islam pertains to the regulation of marriage and inheritance laws." He who enters Islam's circle thereby gains protection—his life becomes sacred, his blood inviolable, his honor and property immune.

The relationship may be illustrated thus: A Muslim need not be a believer, yet every believer must be a Muslim first. As the Commander of the Faithful teaches, Islam requires only vocal confession; faith additionally demands heartfelt knowledge alongside that confession. When God grants a servant knowledge of Him, His Messenger, and His Imam, and that servant confesses with his tongue, he becomes a believer. Such granting of knowledge constitutes God's complete proof upon His servant—His favor and grace. The slave's verbal confession becomes a favor God bestows upon him, extended to whomsoever He wills. Knowledge is the supreme gift God places in the heart of His servant.[^cite-14]

Confession is an act of the heart—knowledge that God creates in the heart, manifesting as verbal utterance. Therefore, none can be held culpable whom God has not rendered knowledgeable. The obligated person must act according to his knowledge and refrain from what he knows not, bringing such matters to the people of knowledge. God declares:

> ⟪quran 21:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent not before you (O Muhammad PBUH) but men to whom We inspired, so ask the people of the Reminder [Scriptures - the Taurat (Torah), the Injeel (Gospel)] if you do not know.*

## The Minimal Conditions of Faith, Polytheism, and Misguidance

When ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him), was asked, "O ʿAlī! What is the minimum that makes one a believer, and what is the minimum that constitutes disbelief?" he replied with precision:

### The Minimal Condition of Faith

The least thing that renders one a believer is this: when God grants His servant knowledge of His existence, he acknowledges God obediently. Should God enable him to know His Messenger, he must acknowledge the Messenger obediently. If God grants him knowledge of the Imam—God's proof upon earth and His witness to creation—then he must believe in and acknowledge the Imamate (wilāyat) obediently. Such a person becomes a believer. Should he lack knowledge of other matters, when commanded to do something, he must obey; when forbidden, he must refrain.[^cite-15]

### The Minimal Condition of Polytheism

The least thing making one a polytheist is this: to treat as forbidden what God has permitted him, whilst imagining God commanded it. To worship what is not God, supposing it to be commanded by God—any worship of other than God constitutes polytheism (shirk). Such a person becomes a polytheist.

### The Minimal Condition of Misguidance

The least thing leading one astray through its practice is to seek neither God's proof upon earth nor knowledge of God's witness to creation, and to refuse to follow it. Whoever is deprived of knowledge of the Imam is certainly misguided.

## The True Purpose of the Islamic Summons

The Prophet's purpose in summoning people to outward Islam was to invite them ultimately to the inward reality. Since the inward can only be reached through the outward, the Prophet (peace and blessings of God be upon him) first called the people to Islam, saying: "Utter ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ 'There is no god but God,' and you shall prosper." Subsequently, those mature in the Islamic daʿwa (the summons / the invitation) who had developed its temperament—those prepared by God—he invited to the word of inner faith. Thus ⟪quran 73:9⟫ "La ilaha illa 'llāh" (There is no god but God) is the phrase of the outward daʿwa, whilst the inner dimension is the reality hidden within it. God alludes to this inward summons when He says:

> ⟪quran 73:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(He Alone is) the Lord of the east and the west, La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He). So take Him Alone as Wakîl (Disposer of your affairs).*

⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ The numerical value (abjad) of "There is no god but He" equals 110—the same as ⟪ar:علی⟫ ʿAlī, hinting that ʿAlī is the Lord of East and West, and one should take him as one's guardian. Here lies the allusion to the inward summons.[^cite-16]

The Messenger of God declared: "As long as my community continues reciting ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ 'There is no god but God' and fulfilling the religion's obligations, God's wrath shall remain distant from them." Otherwise, the phrase is cast in their faces, saying, "You are liars!" You have not verified its reality. In truth, fulfilling the religion's obligations means traveling from the outer ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ 'There is no god but God' to the inner ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫ 'There is no god but Him.'

## The Paradise of Islam and the Paradise of Faith

When ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ the Originator, Most High established tawhīd (divine unity), He did not proclaim ⟪ar:لا الہ⟫ through mere negation, but through affirmation. God testified:

La ilah illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.*

The word through which ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ the First Intellect attained what ⟪ar:کمال ثانی⟫ secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) it achieved and gained elevation is the phrase ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫ "There is no god but Him." Similarly, believers cannot be perfected until they follow the guardianship of ʿAlī and his progeny, accepting his inner summons. Through ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ the Prophet's utterance of "There is no god but God," Muslims entered ⟪ar:اسلام⟫ Islam's Paradise. The sword of Islam was withheld from them, yet there remains no guarantee they remain within this testimony. They lack the ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ speaking messenger who can constitute its meaning. This is ⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫ secured through a good ending when—within the ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ noble form established through the Covenant—the deeds are ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ expressed through ⟪ar:ولایت امیر المومنین⟫ the guardianship of the Commander of the Faithful, ensuring ⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫ the good final outcome.

Islam has a single level: no Muslim stands superior to another, for the Prophet declared, "All Muslims are brothers and sisters." Yet the inward possesses a power that not only strengthens outward belief but elevates one through ranks. In faith's domain exist hierarchies and grades; no believer equals another in station.
