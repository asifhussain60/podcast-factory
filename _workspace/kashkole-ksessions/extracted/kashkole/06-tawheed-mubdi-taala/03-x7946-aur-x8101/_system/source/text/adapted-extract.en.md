# Islam and Faith

## What Is Faith?

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) said: "Believing in Allah is the best of all deeds, and therefore its reward is greatest." He continued, "Faith is the testimony that ⟪ar-quote:أشھد ان لا الہ الا اللہ وحدہ لا شریک لہ و أشھد ان محمد عبدہ و رسولہ⟫ there is no god but Allah, He is One and has no partner, and that Muhammad is His servant and Messenger." To possess true faith, one must affirm that Paradise is real, Hell is real, the resurrection from the grave is real, and the coming of the Final Hour is certain. This affirmation extends to confirming ⟪ar:انبیا و مرسلین⟫the prophets and messengers (upon them be peace), acknowledging ⟪ar:ائمہ طاہرین علیہم السلام⟫the Imams of purity (upon them be peace), and obeying ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫the Imam (upon him be peace). True faith requires following the divine commandments that Allah has enjoined upon His servants and abstaining from what He has forbidden, coupled with obedience to the Imam and acceptance of his declarations.

The Imam explained that faith comprises three essential dimensions:

| **Confirmation in the Heart** | **Expression by the Tongue** | **Action with the Body** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Heartfelt affirmation | Words from the tongue | Physical deed |

When asked whether affirming in the heart and speaking with the tongue constitutes an act, the Imam responded, ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان عمَلُ کلُّہ⟫"Faith is an act in its entirety."[^cite-1] Speech itself is an act performed through language. Similarly, believing and acquiring knowledge constitute the act of the heart; and performing deeds through all the bodily faculties in accordance with God's command and that of His Messenger constitutes the act of the body. Thus, **faith is nothing other than the sum total of actions**.

| **Function of the Heart** | **Function of the Tongue** | **Function of the Body** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The practice of belief and knowledge | The practice of utterance | Actions from all organs |

Allah has made this clear in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 2:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe (in the Oneness of Allah—Islamic Monotheism) and do righteous good deeds, they are dwellers of Paradise, they will dwell therein forever.*

Wherever Allah mentions faith in the Qur'an, He invariably couples it with righteous deeds—a pairing that reveals their inseparable connection. Although faith relates to the heart and action relates to the bodily organs, the two are intrinsically linked; their union in a single person constitutes true belief.[^cite-2] ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫The Commander of the Faithful ⟪ar:نہج البلاغہ⟫in the Path of Eloquence states: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان اعتقاد بالقلب و عمل بالارکان و اقرار بالسان⟫"Faith is belief held in the heart, practice through the bodily faculties, and expression by the tongue."[^cite-3]

Faith originates within the heart and culminates in righteous deeds, which include confession with the tongue. It flows outward from the heart into the tongue, the organs, and the limbs. Islam (al-Islām), by contrast, begins with the utterance of ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫the word of testimony (the Shahāda) and extends to the bodily organs and limbs. The light of faith radiates from within, whereas Islam receives its illumination from without. Islam pertains to the external dimension of the Sharīʿa (the divine law), while faith relates to its inner meaning and spiritual reality.

### On Verification and Security

The word "faith" (īmān) literally denotes confirmation or affirmation, and in another sense, it means to grant security or protection. Faith is a transitive verb—it does not stand alone but requires both an active subject and an object. Two elements are thus essential: the one who affirms and that which is affirmed. If faith means to confirm, one must ask: what or whom is being confirmed? The one confirmed is termed the ⟪ar:مُصَدِّق⟫object of verification. Similarly, if faith signifies granting security, a recipient of that security must exist. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) unified these two meanings in a single hadīth, declaring: ⟪ar-quote:ھِینَ اَلمُؤمِن مَن آمَنَ جَورِہِ بِوَایقَۃ⟫"A believer is one who secures his neighbor from his harm."[^cite-4] It is on account of these two meanings that a person bears the name ⟪ar:اَلمُؤمِنُ⟫the Believer, which is one of the ⟪ar:اسمآء الحسنی⟫Beautiful Names of Allah. A person is called a believer because he affirms ⟪ar:وَعَدُ وَعِیدْ⟫the promise and threat (the reward and punishment) of Allah, and Allah has granted him security from His chastisement.

| **Active Agent** | **Verb** | **Recipient** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Believer | Verification/Affirmation | The message of Allah |
| Believer | Granting security (Amān) | Creatures |
| Allah | Granting security | Those who believe from punishment |

## Faith of the Heart, Tongue, and Body

The Imam said, "Allah has made faith obligatory upon every part of the human being and has apportioned it among them." Not a single organ among the twelve principal faculties of human nature escapes the obligation to practice faith. The faith required of each member differs according to its nature and function. Allah has thus distributed faith among the following organs of the human being:

| **Organ** | **Heart** | **Tongue** | **Two Ears** | **Two Eyes** | **Two Hands** | **Two Feet** | **Face** | **Private Parts** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Count** | 1 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 1 | 1 |

### Faith of the Heart

The heart, from which understanding and insight arise, is the master of the entire body. Without its assent and command, no organ can perform its function. The faith that Allah has imposed upon the heart comprises confession, knowledge, belief, acceptance, and acknowledgment. The believer must confess in his heart that Allah is One; that there is no god but Him; that He is ⟪ar-quote:وحدہ لا شریک لہ⟫alone without partner; that He has no wife and no children; and that ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫Muhammad, the Chosen One (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him and his family), and all the prophets are the servants of Allah and His true messengers. Following this confession, the heart must acknowledge all that has been sent down by Allah—the Scripture, the Sunnah, the Prophets—and all the truths they convey. Finally, the heart must acquire the knowledge of the limits (ʿilm al-hudūd)—the inner meanings of the divine signs—which strengthens and perfects faith. Thus does the heart attain both practical and intellectual faith, as Allah has ordained. This truth is illuminated in the following Quranic verses:

> ⟪quran 16:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَن كَفَرَ بِٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ إِيمَنِهِۦٓ إِلَّا مَنْ أُكْرِهَ وَقَلْبُهُۥ مُطْمَىِٕنٌّۢ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ وَلَكِن مَّن شَرَحَ بِٱلْكُفْرِ صَدْرًۭا فَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Whoever disbelieved in Allah after his belief, except him who is forced thereto and whose heart is at rest with Faith but such as open their breasts to disbelief, on them is wrath from Allah, and theirs will be a great torment.*

> ⟪quran 2:284⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لِّلَّهِ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَإِن تُبْدُوا۟ مَا فِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ أَوْ تُخْفُوهُ يُحَاسِبْكُم بِهِ ٱللَّهُ ۖ فَيَغْفِرُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَن يَشَآءُ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌ ‎⟫
> *To Allah belongs all that is in the heavens and all that is on the earth, and whether you disclose what is in your own selves or conceal it, Allah will call you to account for it. Then He forgives whom He wills and punishes whom He wills. And Allah is Able to do all things.*

> ⟪quran 5:41⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ لَا يَحْزُنكَ ٱلَّذِينَ يُسَرِعُونَ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ آمَنَّا بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَلَمْ تُؤْمِن قُلُوبُهُمْ ۛ وَمِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَادُوا۟ ۛ سَمَّعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ سَمَّعُونَ لِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ لَمْ يَأْتُوكَ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ مَوَاضِعِهِۦ ۖ يَقُولُونَ إِنْ أُوتِيتُمْ هَذَا فَخُذُوهُ وَإِن لَّمْ تُؤْتَوْهُ فَٱحْذَرُوا۟ ۚ وَمَن يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ فِتْنَتَهُۥ فَلَن تَمْلِكَ لَهُۥ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ شَيْـًٔا ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَمْ يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ أَن يُطَهِّرَ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا خِزْىٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O Messenger (Muhammad PBUH)! Let not those who hurry to fall into disbelief grieve you, of such who say: "We believe" with their mouths but their hearts have no faith. And of the Jews are men who listen much and eagerly to lies - listen to others who have not come to you. They change the words from their places; they say, "If you are given this, take it, but if you are not given this, then beware!" And whomsoever Allah wants to put in Al-Fitnah [error, because of his rejecting the Faith], you can do nothing for him against Allah. Those are the ones whose hearts Allah does not want to purify (from disbelief and hypocrisy); for them there is a disgrace in this world, and in the Hereafter a great torment.*

> ⟪quran 13:28⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَتَطْمَىِٕنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ تَطْمَىِٕنُّ ٱلْقُلُوبُ ‎⟫
> *Those who believe (in the Oneness of Allah - Islamic Monotheism), and whose hearts find rest in the remembrance of Allah, Verily, in the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest.*

### Faith of the Tongue

The faith that Allah has enjoined upon the tongue is verbal confession and articulate declaration. It consists in voicing through words the affirmation and belief that the heart has formed. As Allah states:

> ⟪quran 3:84⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْنَا وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَإِسْمَعِيلَ وَإِسْحَقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَٱلْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَآ أُوتِىَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَٱلنَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُۥ مُسْلِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH): "We believe in Allah and in what has been sent down to us, and what was sent down to Ibrahim (Abraham), Isma'il (Ishmael), Ishaque (Isaac), Ya'qub (Jacob) and Al-Asbat [the twelve sons of Ya'qub (Jacob)] and what was given to Musa (Moses), 'Iesa (Jesus) and the Prophets from their Lord. We make no distinction between one another among them and to Him (Allah) we have submitted (in Islam)."*

> ⟪quran 33:70⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَقُولُوا۟ قَوْلًۭا سَدِيدًۭا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Keep your duty to Allah and fear Him, and speak (always) the truth.*

> ⟪quran 18:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيُؤْمِن وَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيَكْفُرْ ۚ إِنَّآ أَعْتَدْنَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ نَارًا أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ سُرَادِقُهَا ۚ وَإِن يَسْتَغِيثُوا۟ يُغَاثُوا۟ بِمَآءٍۢ كَٱلْمُهْلِ يَشْوِى ٱلْوُجُوهَ ۚ بِئْسَ ٱلشَّرَابُ وَسَآءَتْ مُرْتَفَقًا ‎⟫
> *And say: "The truth is from your Lord." Then whosoever wills, let him believe, and whosoever wills, let him disbelieve. Verily, We have prepared for the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers, etc.), a Fire whose walls will be surrounding them (disbelievers in the Oneness of Allah). And if they ask for help (relief, water, etc.) they will be granted water like boiling oil, that will scald their faces. Terrible the drink, and an evil Murtafaqa (dwelling, resting place, etc.)!*

### Faith of Both Ears

It is the duty of the ear to listen attentively to Allah's command and to give it full heed. The ears must refrain from hearing anything forbidden or displeasing to Allah:

> ⟪quran 4:140⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنْ إِذَا سَمِعْتُمْ آيَتِ ٱللَّهِ يُكْفَرُ بِهَا وَيُسْتَهْزَأُ بِهَا فَلَا تَقْعُدُوا۟ مَعَهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّكُمْ إِذًۭا مِّثْلُهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ جَامِعُ ٱلْمُنَفِقِينَ وَٱلْكَفِرِينَ فِى جَهَنَّمَ جَمِيعًا ‎⟫
> *And it has already been revealed to you in the Book (this Quran) that when you hear the Verses of Allah being denied and mocked at, then sit not with them, until they engage in a talk other than that; (but if you stayed with them) certainly in that case you would be like them. Surely, Allah will collect the hypocrites and disbelievers all together in Hell,*

> ⟪quran 6:68⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخُوضُونَ فِىٓ آيَتِنَا فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦ ۚ وَإِمَّا يُنسِيَنَّكَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَلَا تَقْعُدْ بَعْدَ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And when you (Muhammad PBUH) see those who engage in a false conversation about Our Verses (of the Quran) by mocking at them, stay away from them till they turn to another topic. And if Shaitan (Satan) causes you to forget, then after the remembrance sit not you in the company of those people who are the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers, etc.).*

> ⟪quran 39:18⟫
> ⟪ar:فَبَشِّرْ عِبَادِ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَمِعُونَ ٱلْقَوْلَ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ أَحْسَنَهُۥٓ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمْ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *Those who listen to the Word [good advice La ilaha ill-Allah (none has the right to be worshipped but Allah) and Islamic Monotheism, etc.] and follow the best thereof (i.e. worship Allah Alone, repent to Him and avoid Taghut, etc.) those are (the ones) whom Allah has guided and those are men of understanding*

> ⟪quran 23:1-4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَدْ أَفْلَحَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ فِى صَلَاتِهِمْ خَشِعُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ ٱللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَوٰةِ فَعِلُونَ ‎⟫
> *Successful indeed are the believers. Those who offer their Salat (prayers) with all solemnity and full submissiveness. And those who turn away from Al-Laghw (dirty, false, evil vain talk, falsehood, and all that Allah has forbidden). And those who pay the Zakat.*

### Faith of Both Eyes

Allah has enjoined upon the eyes to refrain from gazing upon what is forbidden (ḥarām) and to lower the gaze from all that Allah has forbidden. This constraint upon the eyes is part of the faith and constitutes their proper function:

> ⟪quran 24:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Tell the believing men to lower their gaze (from looking at forbidden things), and protect their private parts (from illegal sexual acts, etc.). That is purer for them. Verily, Allah is All-Aware of what they do.*

### Faith of the Private Parts

The verse above signifies that a believer should not look upon the private parts of another nor allow his own to be seen. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq explained that all Quranic injunctions concerning the protection of the private parts are aimed at preserving them from adultery. However, the aforementioned verse carries the additional meaning that one should not even gaze upon one's own private parts. Allah has elsewhere prescribed the duties of heart, tongue, eye, and ear simultaneously in a single verse:

> ⟪quran 17:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْبَصَرَ وَٱلْفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَانَ عَنْهُ مَسْـُٔولًۭا ‎⟫
> *And follow not (O man i.e., say not, or do not or witness not, etc.) that of which you have no knowledge (e.g. one's saying: "I have seen," while in fact he has not seen, or "I have heard," while he has not heard). Verily! The hearing, and the sight, and the heart, of each of those you will be questioned (by Allah).*

> ⟪quran 41:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَن يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَآ أَبْصَرُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَكِن ظَنَنتُمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And you have not been hiding against yourselves, lest your ears, and your eyes, and your skins testify against you, but you thought that Allah knew not much of what you were doing.*

### Faith of Both Hands

Allah has prescribed that both hands must not be employed in anything forbidden. Rather, they should be dedicated to charity, kindness, maintaining kinship bonds, struggle in the path of Allah (jihād), and ritual purification:

> ⟪quran 5:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ فَٱغْسِلُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْمَرَافِقِ وَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِرُئُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُمْ جُنُبًۭا فَٱطَّهَّرُوا۟ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَآءَ أَحَدٌۭ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَمَسْتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا۟ مَآءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا۟ صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا فَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُم مِّنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُم مِّنْ حَرَجٍۢ وَلَكِن يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you intend to offer As-Salat (the prayer), wash your faces and your hands (forearms) up to the elbows, rub (by passing wet hands over) your heads, and your feet up to ankles. If you are in a state of Janaba (i.e. had a sexual discharge), purify yourself (bathe your whole body). But if you are ill or on a journey or any of you comes from answering the call of nature, or you have been in contact with women (i.e. sexual intercourse) and you find no water, then perform Tayammum with clean earth and rub therewith your faces and hands. Allah does not want to place you in difficulty, but He wants to purify you, and to complete His Favour on you that you may be thankful.*

> ⟪quran 8:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ زَحْفًۭا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلْأَدْبَارَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you meet those who disbelieve, in a battle-field, never turn your backs to them.*

> ⟪quran 47:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ فَضَرْبَ ٱلرِّقَابِ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَثْخَنتُمُوهُمْ فَشُدُّوا۟ ٱلْوَثَاقَ فَإِمَّا مَنًّۢا بَعْدُ وَإِمَّا فِدَآءً حَتَّىٰ تَضَعَ ٱلْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا ۚ ذَلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَآءُ ٱللَّهُ لَٱنتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ وَلَكِن لِّيَبْلُوَا۟ بَعْضَكُم بِبَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱلَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ فَلَن يُضِلَّ أَعْمَلَهُمْ ‎⟫
> *So, when you meet (in fight Jihad in Allah's Cause), those who disbelieve smite at their necks till when you have killed and wounded many of them, then bind a bond firmly (on them, i.e. take them as captives). Thereafter (is the time) either for generosity (i.e. free them without ransom), or ransom (according to what benefits Islam), until the war lays down its burden. Thus [you are ordered by Allah to continue in carrying out Jihad against the disbelievers till they embrace Islam (i.e. are saved from the punishment in the Hell-fire) or at least come under your protection], but if it had been Allah's Will, He Himself could certainly have punished them (without you). But (He lets you fight), in order to test you, some with others. But those who are killed in the Way of Allah, He will never let their deeds be lost,*

Allah has thus commanded that the striking of necks on the battlefield is obligatory, for this action proceeds from both hands and constitutes part of faith.

### Faith of Both Feet

Allah has made it incumbent upon both feet to walk in paths of obedience. One must not walk with these limbs toward deeds of evil and disobedience to Allah; rather, one should direct them toward the commands of Allah and toward that which brings His pleasure:

> ⟪quran 17:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَمْشِ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا ‎⟫
> *And walk not on the earth with conceit and arrogance. Verily, you can neither rend nor penetrate the earth, nor can you attain a stature like the mountains in height.*

> ⟪quran 31:19⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱقْصِدْ فِى مَشْيِكَ وَٱغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلْأَصْوَتِ لَصَوْتُ ٱلْحَمِيرِ ‎⟫
> *"And be moderate (or show no insolence) in your walking, and lower your voice. Verily, the harshest of all voices is the voice (braying) of the ass."*

> ⟪quran 62:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا نُودِىَ لِلصَّلَوٰةِ مِن يَوْمِ ٱلْجُمُعَةِ فَٱسْعَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe (Muslims)! When the call is proclaimed for the Salat (prayer) on the day of Friday (Jumu'ah prayer), come to the remembrance of Allah [Jumu'ah religious talk (Khutbah) and Salat (prayer)] and leave off business (and every other thing), that is better for you if you did but know!*

> ⟪quran 22:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ لْيَقْضُوا۟ تَفَثَهُمْ وَلْيُوفُوا۟ نُذُورَهُمْ وَلْيَطَّوَّفُوا۟ بِٱلْبَيْتِ ٱلْعَتِيقِ ‎⟫
> *Then let them complete the prescribed duties (Manasik of Hajj) for them, and perform their vows, and circumambulate the Ancient House (the Ka'bah at Makkah).*

On the Day of Resurrection, the hands and feet shall bear witness—by their very utterance—against themselves and their masters:

> ⟪quran 36:65⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ نَخْتِمُ عَلَىٰٓ أَفْوَهِهِمْ وَتُكَلِّمُنَآ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَتَشْهَدُ أَرْجُلُهُم بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
> *This Day, We shall seal up their mouths, and their hands will speak to Us, and their legs will bear witness to what they used to earn.*

### Faith of the Face

The face is commanded by Allah to engage in prostration and prayer:

> ⟪quran 22:77⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱرْكَعُوا۟ وَٱسْجُدُوا۟ وَٱعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ وَٱفْعَلُوا۟ ٱلْخَيْرَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Bow down, and prostrate yourselves, and worship your Lord and do good that you may be successful.*

> ⟪quran 72:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنَّ ٱلْمَسَجِدَ لِلَّهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا۟ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ أَحَدًۭا ‎⟫
> *And the mosques are for Allah (Alone), so invoke not anyone along with Allah.*

Allah has thus enjoined upon the limbs that they must be purified and devoted to prayer, and He has described prayer as faith in His Book. When Allah commanded His Messenger (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) to turn from ⟪ar:بیت المقدس⟫Jerusalem toward ⟪ar:بیت الحرام⟫the Inviolable House during prayer, the people asked, "O Messenger of Allah, what of our previous prayers?" Allah responded in this verse:

> ⟪quran 2:143⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَىٰ ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Thus We have made you [true Muslims - real believers of Islamic Monotheism, true followers of Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Sunnah (legal ways)], a Wasat (just) (and the best) nation, that you be witnesses over mankind and the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) be a witness over you. And We made the Qiblah (prayer direction towards Jerusalem) which you used to face, only to test those who followed the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from those who would turn on their heels (i.e. disobey the Messenger). Indeed it was great (heavy) except for those whom Allah guided. And Allah would never make your faith (prayers) to be lost (i.e. your prayers offered towards Jerusalem). Truly, Allah is full of kindness, the Most Merciful towards mankind.*

**In this verse, Allah has explicitly designated prayer as faith.**

Whoever departs from this world having protected his limbs from transgression, each organ having faithfully discharged its divinely ordained function, shall receive complete faith from Allah and shall enter Paradise. Conversely, one who betrays the covenant of Allah, even in the slightest measure, or who has transgressed His commands through these organs, shall come before Allah lacking in faith. He who has donned the collar of the covenant with Allah and the Imam of the Age yet has neither acted upon knowledge nor acquired it, cannot be called a believer. Such verbal affirmation alone avails him nothing and grants him no status in the sight of Allah and the Imam (upon him be peace); rather, such a person stands outside the circle of the Imam of the Age.

## Three Types of Faith and Believers

⟪ar:قرآن حکیم⟫The Wise Quran distinguishes three types of faith:

| **1** | **Pure Faith** |
| --- | --- |
| **2** | **Deficient Faith** |
| **3** | **Faith Mixed with Polytheism** |

### Pure Faith

Those possessing pure faith are they whom Allah Himself praises and to whom He gives glad tidings of Paradise. These are the people who embody both pillars of faith: verification (taṣdīq) and security (amān). They attain the station of righteousness (taqwā) in that although their bodies dwell visibly in the world—⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫the abode of illusion—their souls abide in purity ⟪ar:دار الصدق و صفا⟫in the realm of truth and purification. While their physical forms move among worldly affairs, their inward gaze remains fixed upon the Hereafter. This is the inverse of the ordinary believer, who dwells mentally in the Hereafter whilst his body is occupied in the world.

The dimension of amān (security) befalls them in that when they reach Paradise ⟪ar:دار السلام⟫the Abode of Peace, they shall be forever secure from all the tribulations that assail the soul after death; for them, death becomes a perfume for the soul and spirit. Through death they are completely liberated from ⟪ar:دار کدورت⟫the house of turbidity and saved eternally. For this reason, the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said, ⟪ar-quote:اَلمَوتُ رِیحاَنَۃُ المُؤمِن⟫"Death is a fragrant breeze to the believer."[^cite-5] ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq gestured to his throat and declared, "When the soul of my believer reaches this point, he is filled with joy!" The obedience of ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی علیہ السلام⟫the Commander of the Faithful, ⟪ar:ائمہ طاھرین⟫the Imams of purity, and the ⟪ar:ولی أمر⟫Guardian of the Age is not a burden to them; rather, it is their deepest longing.

The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) declared that those addressed in the Qur'an as ⟪ar-quote:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫"O you who believe" are the leaders and guides of all believers, and this leadership and guidance—a spiritual guardianship rooted in religion—shall remain in the hands of ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (upon him be peace) and his descendants for as long as those addressed by this call remain. Since this title encompasses both the past and future believers, ⟪ar:مُتَقَدِّمِینَ⟫the earlier generations and ⟪ar:مُتَأَخِّرِینَ⟫the later ones, and its dominion extends over all believers, the aforementioned spiritual leadership and authority—which is a religious authority—shall endure ⟪ar:اِلیٰ یَومِ الدِّینَ⟫until the Day of Judgment. Those who disobey the Imams disobey Allah and His Messenger and incur the description of ⟪ar:خارجی⟫the transgressors; when they die, they die in a state of ignorance (jāhilīya). Allah has established this principle:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority. (And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day. That is better and more suitable for final determination.*

### Deficient Faith

The second type—that of those who affirm Allah and His Messenger at a lower level—represents an imperfect faith. These are persons who have only recently ⟪ar:دعوت ایمانیۃ⟫entered the summons of faith, who possess only ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫responsive status in the initial stages, and who remain ignorant of the deeper spiritual matters. Their example is that of a newborn child: although physically constituted with all the faculties of a human being, the child lacks the capability and strength to employ them independently; he requires constant guardianship and assistance at every step. Similarly, those who stand in the early phases of the daʿwa (the summons) may be called believers, yet they remain dependent upon the spiritual guidance and training of their seniors in all their undertakings. Allah has clarified this deficient state of faith in the following verse, wherein He addresses such people:

> ⟪quran 4:136⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ آمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ مِن قَبْلُ ۚ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَلًۢا بَعِيدًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Believe in Allah, and His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and the Book (the Quran) which He has sent down to His Messenger, and the Scripture which He sent down to those before (him), and whosoever disbelieves in Allah, His Angels, His Books, His Messengers, and the Last Day, then indeed he has strayed far away.*

In this verse, Allah says, "O you who believe, believe!" If those addressed were already ⟪ar:کامل الایمان⟫complete in faith, it would be redundant to command them to believe further. On one hand, Allah testifies to their belief by addressing them as ⟪ar:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫"O believers"; yet simultaneously He commands, "Believe!" What then is the meaning of this injunction? It becomes evident that Allah is addressing those of lower levels of belief, saying in effect: "Perfect your faith. You have entered the circle of faith and have attained spiritual birth through the summons; now continue progressing in religious knowledge and aspire to complete belief. Remain close to the righteous—your spiritual parents—and benefit from their spiritual nurturing so that you may ascend to the level of perfection; become worthy to dwell ⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫no longer in the abode of illusion but ⟪ar:دار الصدق و امان⟫in the realm of truth and peace." Just as young children accept the education and training of their parents and teachers and gradually attain the status of their elders, so too must these believers progress. Allah illustrates this principle:

> ⟪quran 52:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and whose offspring follow them in Faith, to them shall We join their offspring, and We shall not decrease the reward of their deeds in anything. Every person is a pledge for that which he has earned.*

Here, "offspring" necessarily refers not to biological descendants but to spiritual or causal successors—those who follow in faith. In other words, the lower-ranking believers (al-mustajībūn) followed the limits (al-hudūd) of the higher-ranking callers of the highest rank. In the end, We elevated them to the station of their spiritual elders and made them superior to them.

### Faith Mixed with Polytheism

This category comprises faith that has been corrupted by polytheism (shirk) and bears no relation to true faith in Allah and His Messenger. True Islamic faith requires accepting the commands that the Messenger of Allah delivered by Allah's revelation—accepting them in their original state, without alteration, without hesitation or doubt—for this is the purity and salvation of our souls. Allah has commanded us: ⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ آتَیكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوہُ وَمَا نَھٰكُمْ عَنْہُ فَانتَھوا۟⟫"Take what the Messenger gives you, and refrain from what he forbids."[^cite-6] That is, do not permit even the slightest intrusion of your personal opinion into the commands and obedience to the Messenger. One who binds himself directly to the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) in matters of worship, the permissible and forbidden, and all religious affairs—without allowing his own conjecture or opinion to interfere—is a perfect believer. But one who comminges his personal desires with the commands of the Messenger, employing his own opinion, conjecture, and *ijtihād* (independent reasoning) to rationalize his desires, is not a true believer but an innovator. By admitting his opinion alongside Allah's command, his faith becomes tainted with polytheism, which leads him away from guidance toward error, from salvation toward ruin. No good deed of his—whether prayer, fasting, or other acts—shall benefit him.

The revelation sent to the Messenger is like water descending from the sky. Water is fit for drinking and purification only as long as it remains pure; once impurity overwhelms it, that water becomes useless to the Muslim, though it still bears the name "water." Its defect renders it neither drinkable nor suitable for ritual purification. Similarly, when personal desires are blended into the rulings of revelation, the pure religion is corrupted and transforms into innovation (bidʿa). The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) declared, "Every innovation is misguidance, no matter how virtuous it may appear."[^cite-7] Even after innovation, the outward forms of Islam remain in place—the opponents of the Imams also pray, as do the followers—yet the difference is manifest. The prayers of the opponents shall be turned back upon their faces, for they have admixed the bitterness of polytheism and the corruption of innovation into their faith. Condemning such people, Allah states:

> ⟪quran 12:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا يُؤْمِنُ أَكْثَرُهُم بِٱللَّهِ إِلَّا وَهُم مُّشْرِكُونَ ‎⟫
> *And most of them believe not in Allah except that they attribute partners unto Him [i.e. they are Mushrikun -polytheists - see Verse 6: 121].*

#### The Zakat of the Polytheists

## Precedence and Degrees in Faith

Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (عليه السلام) has elucidated the foundational principle underlying the differentiation of believers. He states that if faith were uniform—without deficiency or excess—no believer would surpass another, and all would share equally in the rewards promised for their belief. Yet such uniformity does not obtain. Rather, believers enter Paradise through the perfection of their faith, and it is precisely through the *rajiḥ* (preponderance or greater measure) of faith that some gain superiority in God's sight over others. Those whose faith is *muqaṣṣirūn* (deficient or negligent) shall enter Hell.[^cite-1]

Regarding the degrees of faith (*darjāt al-īmān*), the Imam employs a striking analogy: "Allah has made a race for the believers to lead them in their faith, just as horses compete on a given day." Each believer who was first to embrace the faith was granted rank according to that precedence (*sabq*). The laggard in faith receives no precedence over the first believer, nor does virtue confer precedence over virtue. Rather, this precedence itself constitutes a virtue—the supreme excellence that elevates the first believers of the *Umma* (community) above those who came after. Therefore, the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (عليه السلام), who was foremost among all Companions to believe, possesses superiority over all other believers.[^cite-2]

Had the first believers possessed no advantage over those who believed later, the final believer would stand on equal footing with the earliest. Conversely, if precedence were not reckoned as a criterion of excellence, many of the later generations—who possess greater *arāb al-faḍl wa-l-kamāl wa-l-zuhd wa-l-taqwā* (possessors of virtue, perfection, asceticism, and piety)—would surpass the earlier generations in rank. Many among them pray abundantly, fast extensively, perform pilgrimage repeatedly, and expend themselves in jihād and charity in God's way. Yet Allah does not will that one placed in the lower rank of faith should be elevated to the rank of the first, nor that one deferred in faith should be placed ahead, nor that one advanced in the status of faith should be set back. Allah has clarified this precedence in the Qurʾānic verses:

Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq has clarified that these verses delineate the differences between the degrees of faith, the diverse conditions of believers, and the precedence of the faithful. Yet faith without sincerity and inner conviction proves fruitless. Should a first believer create or introduce any defect into his faith, mere priority in time will not benefit him. As Allah states:

> ⟪quran 5:5⟫

> Made lawful to you this day are At-Tayyibat [all kinds of Halal (lawful) foods, which Allah has made lawful (meat of slaughtered eatable animals, etc., milk products, fats, vegetables and fruits, etc.). The food (slaughtered cattle, eatable animals, etc.) of the people of the Scripture (Jews and Christians) is lawful to you and yours is lawful to them. (Lawful to you in marriage) are chaste women from the believers and chaste women from those who were given the Scripture (Jews and Christians) before your time, when you have given their due Mahr (bridal money given by the husband to his wife at the time of marriage), desiring chastity (i.e. taking them in legal wedlock) not committing illegal sexual intercourse, nor taking them as girl-friends. And whosoever disbelieves in the Oneness of Allah and in all the other Articles of Faith [i.e. His (Allah's), Angels, His Holy Books, His Messengers, the Day of Resurrection and Al-Qadar (Divine Preordainments)], then fruitless is his work, and in the Hereafter he will be among the losers.

The Qurʾānic phrase *kafara bi-l-īmān* (disbelief in faith) does not mean merely failing to follow Allah's commandments. Rather, it encompasses: acting without verbal affirmation; affirming with the tongue and acting outwardly while the heart harbors disbelief in the truth; or making the inner affirmation without verbal confession. For faith rests upon the integration of three elements: belief (*iʿtiqād*), word (*qawl*), and deed (*ʿamal*). Faith cannot exist when any one is absent. When all three—belief, affirmation with the tongue, and deeds—are present, faith reaches perfection. Conversely, if one confesses verbally and acts upon it yet believes against the truth in the heart, such a person is no believer, no matter the external effort. None of his deeds shall profit him. Allah states:

> ⟪quran 25:23⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَدِمْنَآ إِلَىٰ مَا عَمِلُوا۟ مِنْ عَمَلٍۢ فَجَعَلْنَهُ هَبَآءًۭ مَّنثُورًا ‎⟫
> And We shall turn to whatever deeds they (disbelievers, polytheists, sinners, etc.) did, and We shall make such deeds as scattered floating particles of dust.
> — **Quran 25:23**

> ⟪quran 88:2-4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وُجُوهٌۭ يَوْمَئِذٍ خَشِعَةٌ ‎ ‏عَامِلَةٌۭ نَّاصِبَةٌۭ ‎ ‏تَصْلَىٰ نَارًا حَامِيَةًۭ ‎⟫
> Some faces, that Day, will be humiliated (in the Hell-fire, i.e. the faces of all disbelievers, Jews and Christians, etc.). Labouring (hard in the worldly life by worshipping others besides Allah), weary (in the Hereafter with humility and disgrace). They will enter in the hot blazing Fire.
> — **Quran 88:2-4**

## Outward and Inner Faith

The reward that Allah has promised in the Hereafter for faith is Paradise. The rationale for deferring reward to the afterlife lies in the nature of faith itself: it encompasses all *khayirāt al-bashariyya* (human excellences). Since one cannot become an angel while dwelling in this world, the reward has been reserved for the Hereafter, and Paradise has been promised. Similarly, disbelief (*kufr*)—a trait that encompasses all *sharr al-bashariyya* (human vices)—brings its own consequence: Hell is promised as a *waʿīd* (divine threat or warning). Faith has two dimensions: the outward (*ẓāhir*) and the inward (*bāṭin*).

**Outward Faith:**

Outward faith consists in the verbal confession of five fundamental principles:

1. **Affirmation of Allah:** The testimony that there is one Creator of this world, who is One, Living, Almighty, Wise, and the Creator of all creation. He alone is the Master of all creatures, and none shares in His authority.

2. **Acknowledgment of the Angels:** These are the most excellent of God's creations. Allah appointed them to serve and worship Him, made them protectors of His creation, and stationed them in positions of importance to execute His will. These angels never disobey Allah; they always perform as commanded.[^cite-3]

3. **Recognition of the Prophets and Messengers:** Allah has selected certain servants as Prophets and Messengers on His earth, and appointed the angels as intermediaries between Himself and these chosen servants. The angels convey the message of their Lord to these special servants, who in turn deliver the good tidings and commands to all creation.

4. **Belief in the Revealed Books:** The prophets received their messages in the form of revelation from the angels and presented them to their contemporaries in their own language and style. Thus, though the substance of the message remains unchanging, the vocabulary and presentation adapt to the times. Hence, one must confess to all the Books revealed to the Prophets.

5. **Affirmation of the Day of Resurrection:** The Resurrection is coming; it will create a new gathering and bring about transformation. On that day, every soul's state shall change. All creatures will be resurrected, judged, and their deeds—good and evil—will receive their recompense.

The believers are those who fully accept these five principles, as Allah affirms in His Book:

> ⟪quran 2:285⟫
> ⟪ar:‏آمَنَ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ ‎⟫
> The Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) believes in what has been sent down to him from his Lord, and (so do) the believers. Each one believes in Allah, His Angels, His Books, and His Messengers. They say, "We make no distinction between one another of His Messengers" - and they say, "We hear, and we obey. (We seek) Your Forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the return (of all)."
> — **Quran 2:285**

When a believer confesses these five outward affirmations with his tongue, he distinguishes himself from all Jews, Christians, and Zoroastrians. Each confession contains within it a denial of all partners to Allah; through this utterance, the believer obligates himself to establish prayer, observe fasting, perform the pilgrimage, pay the alms-tax, and engage in jihād.

**Inner Faith:**

Inner faith (*īmān bāṭin*) is the affirmation from the depths of the heart of those five principles confessed by the tongue. Through this inner confirmation, the believer's faith achieves its inner dimension and grade. The greater the confirmation, the higher the levels of inner faith attain. These are the believers whom Allah praises in His Book and to whom He promises Paradise.[^cite-4]

The Paradise of outward faith differs from the Paradise of inner faith. The Prophet (ṣallā Allāh ʿalayhi wa-ālihi wa-sallam) alluded to this distinction when he said: "Whoever says 'There is no god but Allah' shall enter Paradise." Then he clarified: "Whoever says 'There is no god but Allah' with sincerity (*mukhliṣan*) shall enter Paradise." The first group—those who speak the *Shahāda* (proclamation of faith) with the tongue alone—enters the Paradise of **Islām** (outward submission). The second group—those who confess with the tongue and cultivate sincerity through heartfelt affirmation—enters the Paradise of **Īmān** (inner faith).

To attain this inner faith, the seeker must engage in four practices: *tafakkur* (reflection), *iʿtibār* (consideration), *qiyām bi-shirāṭihā* (fulfilling its conditions), and *wājib ḥaqqihā* (rendering what faith obligates).

- **Reflection:** Contemplating something with intense thought; investigating the object to prove its truth to oneself.
- **Consideration:** Comparing one object to another, discerning differences and relationships between them.
- **Fulfilling the Conditions:** Meeting the requirements Allah has prescribed for this knowledge and faith.
- **Rendering What Is Due:** Giving full account of the obligatory knowledge Allah has prescribed.

Entry into the Paradise of faith is no simple matter. Allah Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 3:142⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ جَهَدُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> Do you think that you will enter Paradise before Allah tests those of you who fought (in His Cause) and (also) tests those who are As-Sabirin (the patient ones, etc.)?
> — **Quran 3:142**

## Faith Has Precedence Over Knowledge

The elevation of ranks is fundamentally bound to knowledge and faith rather than to action alone. The degrees of knowledge differ, as do the degrees of faith (*darjāt al-īmān*). Explicating this distinction, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 58:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجْلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> O you who believe! When you are told to make room in the assemblies, (spread out and) make room. Allah will give you (ample) room (from His Mercy). And when you are told to rise up [for prayers, Jihad (holy fighting in Allah's Cause), or for any other good deed], rise up. Allah will exalt in degree those of you who believe, and those who have been granted knowledge. And Allah is Well-Acquainted with what you do.
> — **Quran 58:11**

In this verse, Allah says that He elevates the ranks of those who believe and those who possess knowledge. Yet the believers are mentioned before the scholars—a linguistic placement that signals that the elevation of the believers precedes that of the knowers. Faith takes precedence over knowledge (*ʿilm*) because knowledge serves as the means through which faith increases, and a thing can only grow in quantity when it already exists. That is, faith must precede knowledge in the order of generation. In this world too, faith was created before knowledge. When the Messenger of Allah (ṣallā Allāh ʿalayhi wa-ālihi wa-sallam) summoned people to Islam, he commanded: "Say 'There is no god but Allah' and you shall prosper." The Prophet first enjoined this confession—the initial condition of faith—without first specifying detailed knowledge about it. He asked only for confession and belief in this word. Only after the people confessed did the stages of verification begin, wherein knowledge of the meaning became clear and the people were instructed.

The spiritual realm reveals this truth: when the First Intellect (*al-ʿAql al-Awwal*) confessed monotheism to the Originator Most High (*Mubdiʿ Taʿālā*), it was granted knowledge of what was, is, and shall be. For this reason, faith precedes knowledge.[^cite-5]

Allah also clarifies the hierarchy of the learned, saying that above every scholar there exists one more learned still:

> ⟪quran 12:76⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَبَدَأَ بِأَوْعِيَتِهِمْ قَبْلَ وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَخْرَجَهَا مِن وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ۚ كَذَلِكَ كِدْنَا لِيُوسُفَ ۖ مَا كَانَ لِيَأْخُذَ أَخَاهُ فِى دِينِ ٱلْمَلِكِ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ نَرْفَعُ دَرَجَتٍۢ مَّن نَّشَآءُ ۗ وَفَوْقَ كُلِّ ذِى عِلْمٍ عَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> So he [Yusuf (Joseph)] began (the search) in their bags before the bag of his brother. Then he brought it out of his brother's bag. Thus did We plan for Yusuf (Joseph). He could not take his brother by the law of the king (as a slave), except that Allah willed it. (So Allah made the brothers to bind themselves with their way of "punishment, i.e. enslaving of a thief.") We raise to degrees whom We please, but over all those endowed with knowledge is the All-Knowing (Allah).
> — **Quran 12:76**

### Knowledge and Faith

Knowledge is the form (*ṣūra*) that the soul creates by knowing a reality. When the soul confesses this form with the tongue, affirms it with the heart, and embeds this concept within itself through bodily action, the result is called faith (*īmān*). Verification (*taṣdīq*) is truthfulness, impossible without investigation. For example: we Muslims all know the existence of Paradise, Hell, and the Bridge of Sirat; we affirm and acknowledge their reality. Yet since these cannot be perceived by the five senses (*ḥawās khams*), verification requires both research and deeper investigation to know their true nature.[^cite-6]

**Knowledge and Faith Compared:**

| Knowledge | Faith |
|-----------|-------|
| Form of knowledge in the soul | Confession, affirmation, and action upon that knowledge |
| Awareness | Verification through research |

In the scholar's soul, the form of knowledge is termed *ʿilm* (knowledge); its confession, affirmation, and embodiment through action is called *īmān* (faith). Faith is the true and real achievement; knowledge is the instrument through which faith increases in perfection.

### Verification and Research

Knowledge has a dual nature. The first is *ʿilm ẓāhir* (manifest knowledge), which precedes faith—the state of becoming aware through information about a reality. For instance, knowing that Heaven and Hell exist. The second is faith itself: here, the truth is first acknowledged by the tongue (that Heaven and Hell are true), then verified through investigation. This investigation produces deeper knowledge and strengthens verification—this is *ʿilm maʿrifa* (knowledge of intimate knowing), which comes after faith.

The more knowledge through intimate knowing increases, the more verification grows stronger, and the more faith becomes perfected. For example: What are Heaven, Hell, and the Bridge of Sirat? Where are they located? How vast are they? Who shall enter? How long shall they remain? The more knowledge about these realities deepens, the more verification strengthens, making both the form of knowledge and faith itself more robust.

Allah has made faith—not knowledge—the criterion of success. He declares:

> ⟪quran 2:2-5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ذَلِكَ ٱلْكِتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًۭى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> That book, whereof there is no doubt, a guidance to those who are Al-Muttaqun [the pious and righteous persons who fear Allah much (abstain from all kinds of sins and evil deeds which He has forbidden) and love Allah much (perform all kinds of good deeds which He has ordained)]. Who believe in the Ghaib and perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), and spend out of what we have provided for them [i.e. give Zakat, spend on themselves, their parents, their children, their wives, etc., and also give charity to the poor and also in Allah's Cause - Jihad, etc.]. And who believe in (the Quran and the Sunnah) which has been sent down (revealed) to you (Muhammad Peace be upon him ) and in [the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel), etc.] which were sent down before you and they believe with certainty in the Hereafter. (Resurrection, recompense of their good and bad deeds, Paradise and Hell, etc.). They are on (true) guidance from their Lord, and they are the successful.
> — **Quran 2:2-5**

In this passage, Allah says those who *yūminūn bi-l-ghayb* (believe in the unseen) are successful—not those who *yaʿlamūn bi-l-ghayb* (know the unseen). That is, those who believed in all these truths achieved success. Faith always arises through the unseen, through knowledge of the unseen. Faith is impossible without verification, and verification cannot occur without epistemology. In other words, the degrees of faith are established upon the ladder-rungs of knowledge.

## The Distinction Between Islam and Faith

To clarify the difference between Islam (*Islām*) and faith (*īmān*), Allah states:

> ⟪quran 49:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ قَالَتِ ٱلْأَعْرَابُ آمَنَّا ۖ قُل لَّمْ تُؤْمِنُوا۟ وَلَكِن قُولُوٓا۟ أَسْلَمْنَا وَلَمَّا يَدْخُلِ ٱلْإِيمَنُ فِى قُلُوبِكُمْ ۖ وَإِن تُطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ لَا يَلِتْكُم مِّنْ أَعْمَلِكُمْ شَيْـًٔا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهُ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌ ‎⟫
> The bedouins say: "We believe." Say: "You believe not but you only say, 'We have surrendered (in Islam),' for Faith has not yet entered your hearts. But if you obey Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), He will not decrease anything in reward for your deeds. Verily, Allah is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful."
> — **Quran 49:14**

> ⟪quran 49:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَمُنُّونَ عَلَيْكَ أَنْ أَسْلَمُوا۟ ۖ قُل لَّا تَمُنُّوا۟ عَلَىَّ إِسْلَمَكُم ۖ بَلِ ٱللَّهُ يَمُنُّ عَلَيْكُمْ أَنْ هَدَىٰكُمْ لِلْإِيمَنِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> They regard as favour upon you (O Muhammad PBUH) that they have embraced Islam. Say: "Count not your Islam as a favour upon me. Nay, but Allah has conferred a favour upon you, that He has guided you to the Faith, if you indeed are true.
> — **Quran 49:17**

> ⟪quran 51:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَمَا وَجَدْنَا فِيهَا غَيْرَ بَيْتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> But We found not there any household of the Muslims except one [i.e. Lout (Lot) and his two daughters].
> — **Quran 51:36**

These verses establish that Islam and faith are two distinct realities. Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq teaches that faith definitively exists within the realm of Islam, yet Islam does not enter the realm of faith. This is because Islam pertains to outward words and deeds, while faith relates to the inward—to the sincerity of the heart. When once asked about the true nature of Islam and faith, the Imām (ʿalayhi al-salām) replied: "Faith concerns the heart; Islam concerns the observance of the rules governing marriage, inheritance, and the protection of life and property. Whoever enters the circle of Islam has his life, blood, honor, and wealth protected."[^cite-7]

The distinction can be illustrated as follows: it is possible for a Muslim to not be a believer; yet every believer is necessarily a Muslim first. Islam requires only the confession of the tongue, whereas faith demands knowledge of the heart alongside confession. Therefore, if Allah grants a person the ability to know Him, His Messenger, and His Imam, and he confesses with his tongue, then he is a believer.

Knowledge (*ʿilm*) is the great favor that Allah bestows upon a servant's heart. The bestowal of knowledge is a complete proof from Allah, a manifestation of His grace and mercy. The servant's verbal confession (*iqrār*) is itself a favor from Allah upon him—He bestows such favor upon whomever He wills. Confession is an act flowing from the heart: the knowledge that Allah has created in the heart takes the form of a verbal testimony.

Therefore, no argument can be established against a person to whom Allah has not granted knowledge. Such a person is obliged to act only within the bounds of what he knows, and to refrain from acting upon the unknown. When matters beyond his knowledge arise, he should refer them to the authorities, as Allah commands:

> ⟪quran 21:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> And We sent not before you (O Muhammad PBUH) but men to whom We inspired, so ask the people of the Reminder [Scriptures - the Taurat (Torah), the Injeel (Gospel)] if you do not know.
> — **Quran 21:7**

## The Minimum Conditions of Faith, Polytheism, and Misguidance

When Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (ʿalayhi al-salām) was asked, "O Ali! What is the lowest condition that makes a person a believer, and what is the lowest condition that makes a person a polytheist (*mushrik*)?", he provided this clarification:

### The Lowest Condition of Faith

The lowest thing that makes a person a believer is this: when Allah grants one of His servants the ability to know Him, that servant acknowledges Allah in obedience. Should Allah enable him to know His Messenger, he should acknowledge them obediently. If Allah grants him the knowledge to recognize the Imam—the proof of Allah's existence on earth and the witness to His creation—then he should believe in the Imamate of Allah and acknowledge it with obedience. Such a person is truly a believer. If he lacks knowledge of other matters, he should obey when commanded to do something, and abstain when forbidden from doing something.

### The Lowest Condition of Polytheism

The lowest condition that makes a person a polytheist is this: when he regards as lawful something that Allah has forbidden, while believing that Allah has commanded him to do it. That is, he believes that Allah has ordered what he is worshipping, even though that object of worship is other than Allah. Since the worship of any partner besides Allah constitutes polytheism (*shirk*), such a person becomes a polytheist.

### The Lowest Condition of Misguidance

The lowest condition leading a person to misguidance through its practice is this: he does not seek the Imam—the proof of Allah on earth and the witness of Allah among creation—nor does he follow him. A person deprived of knowledge of the Imam is assuredly in error.

## What Was the True Purpose of the Islamic Invitation?

The Prophet's outer summons (*daʿwa*) to Islam was, in its deepest intention, an invitation to the inner reality. Yet since access to the inward comes only through the outward, the Prophet (ṣallā Allāh ʿalayhi wa-ālihi wa-sallam) first called people to Islam, commanding them: "Say 'There is no god but Allah' and you shall prosper." Thereafter, among those whom the Islamic *daʿwa* had matured and who had developed the temperament for the inner call—especially on the day of Ghadīr—he invited them to the word of faith, which is the word of the esoteric *daʿwa* (inner summons): "Whoever I am the guardian of, this Ali is the guardian of." Here, the proclamation *lā ilāha illā Allāh* ("There is no god but Allah") represents the outer, exoteric invitation, while the word of faith represents its inner veil.

This is indicated as well in *Sūrat al-Muzzammil* when Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 73:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكَيِلًۭا ‎⟫
> (He Alone is) the Lord of the east and the west, La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He). So take Him Alone as Wakîl (Disposer of your affairs).
> — **Quran 73:9**

The numerical value (*ʿadad jamal*) of *lā ilāha illā Huwa* equals 110, which corresponds to the numerical value of ʿAlī. Thus the verse intimates that Ali is the Lord of the East and the West—take him as your guardian! This is a subtle allusion to the inner invitation.[^cite-8]

The Messenger of Allah declared: "As long as my community recites the word *lā ilāha illā Allāh* and fulfills all the duties and obligations of the religion, the wrath of Allah will remain far from them." Otherwise, this word will be thrown in their faces and they will be told: "You are liars! You did not verify its meaning." The true fulfillment of *lā ilāha illā Allāh* is the journey from one station to another—from *lā ilāha* (there is no god) to *illā Huwa* (except He), which is accomplished through the covenant of the Imāmate.

## The Paradise of Islam and the Paradise of Faith

When the Originator Most High (*Mubdiʿ Taʿālā*) taught the First Intellect (*al-ʿAql al-Awwal*) the doctrine of divine oneness (*Tawhīd*), He did not simply state, "There is no god," but rather revealed the deeper proclamation: *lā ilāha illā Huwa* ("There is no god but He"). This is witnessed by the verse:

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمِ ‎⟫

The word from which the secondary perfection (*kamāl thānī*) was attained and whose glory and elevation increased is precisely the word *lā ilāha illā Huwa*. In the same manner, believers cannot be prepared until they follow the guardianship (*wilāya*) of ʿAlī and the Children of ʿAlī, and embrace his inner call.

Through the word of the Prophet—*lā ilāha illā Allāh*—the Muslims entered the Paradise of Allah designated as **Islam** (the outward submission). The sword of Islam has been withheld from them as a mark of mercy; yet there is no certainty they shall endure in this proclamation. For they lack that living, speaking word (*kalima nāṭiqa*) through which they might constitute its meaning. This speaking word is attained only when the inner meaning of *lā ilāha* (negation of all partners) becomes one with *illā Allāh* (affirmation of Allah alone)—that is, when deeds are created through the covenant of the guardianship (*wilāya*) of the Commander of the Faithful, the *nāṭiqa* (the speaking one) who guarantees the end in goodness (*khātima bi-l-khair*).[^cite-9]

There is but a single level within Islam: no Muslim stands superior to another Muslim. As the Prophet (ṣallā Allāh ʿalayhi wa-ālihi wa-sallam) affirmed, "All Muslims are brothers and sisters." The Messenger thus established all Muslims as equals on one plane—an attribute of the outward dimension. However, in the inward realm there exists a power that not only strengthens outward belief but also elevates believers through ranks and degrees. Within the circle of faith, there exist hierarchies and stations (*marātib wa-durūj*) such that no believer is equal to another believer. The one granted greater knowledge, deeper sincerity, and more perfect obedience rises to a higher station; yet all partake of the mercy of Allah according to their capacity and station.

## The Emanation of Islam and Faith

Those who occupied the highest stations, whose designation was *Grami*, derived their name from ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ and ⟪ar:محمدؐ مصطفیٰ⟫—the Messenger of God, may God's prayers and peace be upon him and his family. From these exalted presences, Islam itself emanated. Conversely, those of inferior rank in the celestial hierarchy gave rise to faith (Iman, *al-īmān*), embodied in ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ and ⟪ar:علی⟫ the Commander of the Faithful, Ali son of Abū Ṭālib, upon whom be peace.[^cite-8]

A numerical correspondence reveals the cosmic principle underlying this distinction: when one adds certain letters to the name ⟪ar:محمد⟫*Muḥammad*, the numerical value equals that of Islam (al-islām); similarly, when those same letters are added to the name ⟪ar:علی⟫*ʿAlī*, the sum corresponds to the numerical value of faith (al-īmān).[^cite-9] This arithmetical harmony demonstrates that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وآلہ وسلم⟫ the Messenger of God, upon him be peace, established the framework of Islam (al-islāmī haikl, *the Islamic structure*), while ⟪ar:أمیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫the Commander of the Faithful instituted the framework of faith (al-īmānī haikl, *the structure of faith*).[^cite-10]
