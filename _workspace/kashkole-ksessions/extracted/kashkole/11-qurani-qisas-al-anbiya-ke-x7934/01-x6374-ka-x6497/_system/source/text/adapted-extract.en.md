## The Messenger as Bearer of Sharīʿa (Ṣāḥib Sharīʿa Rasūl)

No messenger among the apostles has stood apart from the law of his predecessor or failed to follow the commandments transmitted through successive revelation. Rather, each Prophet is born into the community of believers, raised among them, and undergoes a rigorous process of spiritual purification—perfecting his knowledge, capacity, and moral faculty—until the final Imam of the previous Prophet's age establishes him in the prophetic rank. Thus does the chain of divine guidance remain unbroken, each bearer of law receiving instruction from his spiritual predecessor, ensuring the continuity of the sharīʿa (revealed law) through successive dispensations.

## The Four Causes of the World's Coming-Into-Being

### The Conception of Ordinary Scholars

Scholars of exoteric learning have enumerated four causes by which Allāh brought the world into existence:

| 1 | To manifest and reveal His own existence through creation. Were creatures not fashioned, how would any being come to know that He exists and possesses a Creator? |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | To demonstrate His knowledge and power through the creation of stable, enduring things, so that such things and their operations might show forth the attributes of the Almighty alone. |
| 3 | To establish the framework for worship, so that He might reward the worshippers according to His grace rather than mere transactional proportion to their deeds. He multiplies the recompense of ordinary acts beyond measure, being independent of creatures' obedience; no injustice taints His dominion through the obedience of the obedient, nor does disobedience diminish His sovereignty. As He Himself declares: "Did you think We created you in vain?" |
| 4 | To perfect His attribute of *al-Muḥsin* (the Beneficent). Thus does He deal with some through justice and with others through grace, for He is both just and merciful. He has created the believers specifically to shower upon them His mercy. |

### The Mystical Understanding

Having briefly reviewed the perspectives of exoteric scholars, we affirm that Allāh created all creation for a supreme purpose: the manifestation of the Essence of the Prophetic Seal, ⟪ar:حضرت محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ و سلم⟫ (the Chosen One, peace be upon him), the greatest blessing and mercy bestowed upon mankind. Multiple preliminary stages were necessary for his coming-into-being. For this reason did Allāh create Adam and all the prophets and messengers who preceded him—each a necessary preparation for the ultimate manifestation of his essence.[^cite-1]

It is narrated from the venerable Companion ⟪ar:قتادہ سعید ابن مسیب⟫ that Allāh revealed to Jesus: "O Jesus, believe in ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ and command your people to believe in him." Moreover, ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ himself declared: "Had I not been sent forth, I would not have created Adam, nor would I have brought into being Paradise and Hell. When I established My Throne upon the waters, it trembled violently. But when I inscribed upon it the testimony ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ محمد رسول اللہ⟫ (there is no god but Allāh; Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allāh), it became still and stable." The Throne rests upon water—an image of knowledge—signifying that knowledge is the necessary foundation for attainment of Allāh and for the recognition of His oneness and lordship.[^cite-2]

### The Mystical Reality of Creation's Purpose

A knower of unveiling (ʿārif, one who perceives mystical truth) has disclosed that the Creator fashioned this world according to a supreme wisdom—hidden alike from scholars and the common people—and that when the appointed time arrives, the herald of this wisdom shall proclaim it openly ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫. In brief, Allāh did not create the world in vain, without purpose, or as an idle jest. He Himself bears witness:

> ⟪quran 23:115⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَفَحَسِبْتُمْ أَنَّمَا خَلَقْنَكُمْ عَبَثًۭا وَأَنَّكُمْ إِلَيْنَا لَا تُرْجَعُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *"Did you think that We had created you in vain, and that you would not be brought back to Us?"*

## The Secret of Shayṭān's Power Over Humans and How He Leads Them Astray

We wish to expound here certain reasons why Satan has gained dominion over humankind and succeeded in his deception, and to clarify why man yields so readily to satanic inclinations. Why does he find himself drawn toward intoxication and fornication? Why does he not heed the warning, despite Allāh's explicit declaration that Satan is his manifest enemy?

> ⟪quran 36:60⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ أَلَمْ أَعْهَدْ إِلَيْكُمْ يَبَنِىٓ آدَمَ أَن لَّا تَعْبُدُوا۟ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ لَكُمْ عَدُوٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> 
> *"Did I not covenant with you, O children of Adam, that you should not worship Satan? Surely he is to you a manifest enemy."*

The fundamental cause lies in this: Satan has overcome man through cunning and strategem, exploiting man's own natural forces against him. Rather than seeking aid from Allāh at the hour of satanic temptation, man surrenders himself and submits. Consider how Satan dominates the sexual faculty—a force which Allāh created for the continuation of the species and the survival of the human race. Yet through perversion, it becomes an instrument of illicit gratification, transforming what was meant for preservation into the very wellspring of corruption upon earth. As one has aptly said: "Wealth, earth, and woman—these are the root of all three corruptions."

Similarly, divine anger (ghaḍab) was placed in man so that he might repel harm and aversion. Yet Satan has seized this noble power and turned it toward destruction and ruin. Man's inclination toward sin parallels the inclination of the infirm toward foods that harm them. When a person's temperament deviates from equilibrium, he craves whatever exacerbates his malady. The fevered man calls for cold water, which aggravates his condition. The person whose digestion has failed seeks to consume ever more, yet his stomach neither fills nor settles—it only grows more turbulent. By contrast, the healthy person never craves that which harms him; rather, he partakes of beneficial sustenance with measure.

The condition of the world resembles corrupted air, fouled by pathogens and harmful microbes. Yet since man cannot survive without air, he constantly strives to purify it through aromatic remedies—camphor, fragrant incense, and perfumed oils—and neutralizes its foul odors through disinfectants. Thus does he employ all medicinal means to remedy the corruption of the atmosphere in which he dwells.[^cite-3] In the same manner, a person is perpetually assailed by the whispers of this corrupted world at every step, assaults that threaten to destroy his soul. Although life itself unfolds within this very atmosphere, the means of deliverance from Satan's machinations are clear: renounce your desires, remember Allāh abundantly, beseech His aid, diminish your attachment to this fleeting world, and obey the commandments of the Messenger. Such practices inevitably plant wholesome thoughts in the heart and mind, tempering the intellect and disposition toward moderation and rectitude. Then shall your inclination turn toward truth and its people; the rigors of the sharīʿa—prayer, zakāt (almsgiving), fasting—will no longer seem burdensome, but rather a solace and comfort in which you take delight. Your fervor in obeying divine commands shall be kindled anew. At that moment, Satan shall confess openly ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫ that he can never dominate such a soul.

The Prophet, peace be upon him, instructed ⟪ar:حضرت ابوذر غفاری⟫ Abu Dharr al-Ghifārī with these words: "O Abu Dharr, conduct yourself in this world as a traveler, a wayfarer merely passing through. Consider yourself as one dead. Speak little, but remember Allāh much. By this means you shall vanquish Satan. Speak the truth even if it bitter to the listener. Love the poor and sit among them. Be satisfied with bread sufficient to sustain your body and raiment sufficient to cover it—content yourself with little, and you shall find happiness. Do not covet the world, for it is the root of every sin. Whoever holds the world in contempt finds all adversity light."[^cite-4]

"Consider yourself dead"—this means that just as the dead have no will of their own, so too should every Muslim conduct all his affairs and desires strictly in accordance with the sharīʿa, with no room for personal preference or whim. As the poet has versified:

> My beloved placed a noose about my neck,  
> Leading me whither they will.  
> My ego and my selfish desire have no say in it.

#### Two Modes of Satanic Worship

Know that Satan worship assumes two forms. The first is open rebellion against Allāh's commands. The second is obedience to Allāh's commands, yet not as Allāh has revealed them, but rather through innovations and distortions introduced by those who alter the faith. For instance, the people perform prayer, yet they invent the Tarāwīḥ prayer (extra prayers in Ramadan), which is an innovation without warrant.

#### Two Methods of Satanic Seduction

Satan employs two distinct modes of seduction. In one method, he sends his disciples—those whom he has corrupted—to mislead the servant of Allāh. They approach and speak words contrary to Allāh's commands and His Messenger's guidance, sowing such doubts and confusion that the person loses his way, finding no remedy on his own. In the second method, Satan himself whispers directly to man's soul, making him an instrument of sin through insinuation, so that he commits transgressions of his own apparent will.

Therefore, should someone come before you as a counselor, giving advice, attend to it with discernment. Examine whether what he says accords with the word of Allāh and His Messenger. Test it against the Qur'ān and the standard of intellect! If it does not align with these sources of guidance, then know with certainty that this person, cloaked though he be in the garment of benevolence, is a minion of Satan, sent to lead you astray. Should you obey him, you shall be worshipping Satan.

In like manner, before you undertake any action, first deliberate: Does the Islamic sharīʿa permit this deed or forbid it? If the law forbids it, yet your soul desires it, then understand that this is a satanic whisper—Satan has made you his tool. Cease from it at once; otherwise Satan shall gain mastery over you, and you shall become his obedient servant. These two modes of seduction are what Allāh describes in His word:

> ⟪quran 6:112⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *"Thus have We made for every prophet an enemy—satans among humans and jinn, some of whom inspire others with fair-seeming discourse as deception. Yet had your Lord willed, they would not have done so; wherefore leave them to their fabrications."*

This verse expounds how Satan embellishes falsehood and inspiration through either an external force (such as a human agent, whom the tradition calls ⟪ar:'أنس'⟫—one of humankind) or an internal force (such as the commanding soul, ⟪ar:أمارۃ بالسوء⟫—the self that incites to evil), sometimes designated as ⟪ar:'جن'⟫ (the jinn, those unseen entities).

#### A Third Mode: The Ambush of Self-Righteousness

Beyond this, Satan possesses yet another stratagem for enticing man and making him his vassal. He first incites man to transgress Allāh's command. Should the man obey, Satan's purpose is fulfilled; the man becomes Satan's servant and worshipper. But if the man disregards Satan's voice and instead follows obedience to Allāh and His commandments, Satan shifts his tactic. He whispers thus: "Yes, yes! Continue in your obedience to Allāh, that your honor and glory may increase among the people! Let them regard you as a saint! Let them consider you a guardian of Allāh! May your brothers and your companions benefit greatly from your fame and renown!" In this manner, if the person commits acts of worship with the intention planted by Satan—seeking acclaim rather than divine pleasure—then such worship becomes not obedience to Allāh, but rather obedience and servitude to Satan. In short, Satan leaves no stratagem untried in his effort to deceive man; he employs every weapon and artifice in his arsenal.

## The Three Categories of Sinners and the Disobedient

Not all sinners and transgressors are equal. Rather, there are three categories:

| 1 | Those who follow Satan's whispers with willing hearts and praising tongues—that is, they obey his command with complete joy and wholehearted commitment. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Those who commit misdeeds yet despise them utterly in their hearts and denounce them with their tongues. When they fall into sin, conscience rises up within them, warning them. They immediately return in repentance and seek forgiveness from their Lord. For such people, satanic worship remains confined to the outward limbs; their heart remains innocent. |
| 3 | Those who perform evil deeds yet do not regard them as evil. Their hearts and minds accept these acts without reflection or resistance. These are the sinners of ignorance. |

### Expiation for Sins Committed Through Ignorance

The sins of such people—those who sin through ignorance—are wiped away and their souls are purified through affliction, illness, pain, and suffering. The Prophet's Hadith testifies to this: "Fever is a flame from the flames of Hell, a gust of wind from within." Again he said: "Fever is a purification sent by a Merciful Lord." And: "The sword (meaning trials and tribulations) erases sins."[^cite-5]

### Expiation for Intentional Sins

The sins committed with pleasure in the heart and praise upon the tongue can be forgiven only through sincere repentance and turning back to Allāh—by no other means. It is incumbent upon such a sinner to turn sincerely to Allāh seeking forgiveness and to restrain his tongue from commending evil. The heart is the king and sovereign of the body; the tongue is its minister and counselor; the limbs are its servants. Therefore, a sin committed with the intention of the heart, in satisfaction of desire and will, is indeed a grave transgression. Whatsoever is done in contravention of Allāh's command and in love of other than Allāh shall bring upon that person a painful punishment. In the same way, whatever is spoken by the tongue is attributed to the heart; the one who commits sin through speech receives the same recompense as the one who commits it through the limbs.

### When Ignorance Does Not Hinder Spiritual Progress

Sins that issue from the limbs despite the heart's dislike of them are addressed in this sacred pronouncement: "O My servants, if you did not sin, I would raise up a people who would sin and then seek forgiveness." There is profound wisdom in this divine utterance. Sometimes Satan causes a person to stumble into sin through his own ignorance, and Satan exults, saying: "How I have deceived this righteous servant of Allāh! I have ensnared him such that he cannot escape! I have made him transgress against his Lord!" Yet in truth, that very sin elevates the status of this righteous person. When a pious soul commits a sin in ignorance and then realizes his transgression, his heart breaks. Any arrogance, self-conceit, or pride that may have arisen from his worship and long devotions is shattered. In this way, he is delivered from the great sin of egoism and asks forgiveness for the small sin, and his spiritual station becomes even higher. The Prophet, peace be upon him, declared in another hadith: "I am near to the broken-hearted."[^cite-6] From this we may understand that the ⟪ar:انبیاء علیھم السلام⟫ mistakes and slips committed by the prophets, peace be upon them, were of this very nature—as soon as they sought forgiveness, their ranks were elevated and they drew nearer to Allāh. The poet Iqbal has expressed this truth:

> Do not think it a mirror's flaw—  
> The mirror-maker holds dear the broken mirror.

And Majd has said:

> God remembers a broken heart;  
> A lover dies of pride.  
> Living in the Throne of heartbreak—  
> A wondrous cup that breaks and fills again.

## A Wise Saying of the Prophet Dāwūd (David)

The Prophet Dāwūd, peace be upon him, uttered a saying of great wisdom: "What has occurred before shall occur again in the future. What has been known shall be known again and shall come into manifestation; there is nothing new beneath this sun." In other words, history moves in cycles. What manifested in ages past manifested likewise in the age of our Prophet Muḥammad Mustafā, peace be upon him. His era was not free from the same trials, disagreements, contentions, plagues, and disobedience. Since the Holy Prophet embodies a light in which all the illuminations of former prophets are gathered, and their spiritual realities are merged within him, then necessarily his attributes and qualities encompass the sum of theirs. As one has aptly said:

> The Prophet's virtues—gathered collection—  
> Are Muḥammadinic perfection.

Every prophet must manifest certain virtues; yet all these perfections find their comprehensive fulfillment in Muḥammad.

A Persian poet has rendered it thus:

> The beauty of Yūsuf, the tail-end of Jesus' mantle—  
> In the water, the beauty of water is all-encompassing;  
> The loneliness of water contains all.
