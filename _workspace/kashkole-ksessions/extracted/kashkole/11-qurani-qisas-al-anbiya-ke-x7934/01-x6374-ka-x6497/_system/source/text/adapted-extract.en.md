## The Messenger as Bearer of Sharīʿa (Ṣāḥib Sharīʿa Rasūl)

Every messenger among the prophets has been attached to the law (*sharīʿa*) of his predecessor and has followed the commandments established by the previous dispensation until he attained the rank of messenger himself. The Prophet emerges from the community of believers, is nurtured among them, and through purification of soul, acquisition of knowledge, and cultivation of spiritual capacity, becomes prepared for his role. The final Imam of the prior prophetic epoch then establishes him in the prophetic rank, conferring upon him the authority to promulgate a new law for his time.

## The Four Causes of the World's Coming-Into-Being

### The Understanding of Ordinary Scholars

Scholars have traditionally enumerated four causes (*ʿilal*) for the world's origination:

| **First** | Allāh created creatures to manifest and reveal His own existence. Without creation, none would recognize that He exists, nor would anyone know that He possesses a Creator. |
| --- | --- |
| **Second** | He created firm and enduring things to demonstrate His knowledge and power, so that it becomes evident that such order and action can proceed only from the Almighty. |
| **Third** | He created for worship, so that He may reward the worshippers according to His grace rather than their merit alone. He grants recompense far exceeding the measure of their deeds, though such worship brings neither increase nor decrease to His dominion. As He Himself declares in ⟪quran 23:115⟫: "Did you think that We had created you in vain?" |
| **Fourth** | He created creatures and bestowed upon them grace, so that His attribute of benevolence (*Muḥsin*, the Doer of Good) might be manifested. He deals with some according to justice and with others according to mercy—for He is both the Just and the Merciful. He has created the believers specifically to pour upon them His tender mercy. |

### The Deeper Significance: Creation for the Essence of the Final Prophet

After these preliminary considerations and learned opinions, we declare our own conviction: Allāh created all existence for the sake of the Essence of the World—⟪ar:حضرت محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ و سلم⟫—which constitutes the greatest blessing and mercy of Allāh bestowed upon humankind. For this manifestation, preliminary stages of existence were necessary. Therefore, He created Adam and all the prophets preceding him, each serving as a necessary preparation for his ultimate appearance.[^cite-1]

It is narrated from an honored companion, ⟪ar:قتادہ سعید ابن مسیب⟫, that Allāh revealed to Jesus: "O Jesus, believe in ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ and command your people to believe in him." He further declared: "Had it not been for him who would appear, I would not have created Adam, nor would I have created Paradise and Hell. When I established My Throne upon water, it trembled. But when I inscribed upon it the testimony ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ محمد رسول اللہ⟫—'There is no god but Allāh; Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allāh'—it became firm and still. The Throne stands upon water, which symbolizes knowledge. This reveals that knowledge is indispensable for attaining Allāh, for understanding His unity, and for true monotheism."[^cite-2]

### The Mystical Perception of Divine Wisdom

A master of inner reality (*ḥaqīqat*) has said: The Creator established this cosmos upon a wisdom of surpassing subtlety, hidden from both the learned and the common folk alike. The one who narrates this wisdom shall speak it openly ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫ when the hour arrives. In short, He did not create the world in frivolity, inanity, or purposelessness. As He Himself affirms:

> ⟪quran 23:115⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَفَحَسِبْتُمْ أَنَّمَا خَلَقْنَكُمْ عَبَثًۭا وَأَنَّكُمْ إِلَيْنَا لَا تُرْجَعُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *"Did you think that We had created you in play (without any purpose), and that you would not be brought back to Us?"*

The Qurʾān elsewhere reinforces this teaching with the verses divinely appointing for every prophet enemies and adversaries:

> ⟪quran 6:112⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *"And so We have appointed for every Prophet enemies—Shayatin (devils) among mankind and jinn, inspiring one another with adorned speech as a delusion. If your Lord had so willed, they would not have done it, so leave them alone with their fabrications."*

And He warns the believers:

> ⟪quran 36:60⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ أَلَمْ أَعْهَدْ إِلَيْكُمْ يَبَنِىٓ آدَمَ أَن لَّا تَعْبُدُوا۟ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ لَكُمْ عَدُوٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> 
> *"O children of Adam! Did I not enjoin upon you that you should not worship Satan? Indeed, he is to you a clear enemy."*

## The Secret of Shayṭān's Power Over Humans and How He Leads Them Astray

It is fitting to expound here the reasons why Satan has prevailed over humanity and succeeded in his seduction, and to clarify why the human being so readily inclines toward satanic desires—why he is willing to indulge in forbidden intoxicants and to commit unlawful relations, despite Allāh's explicit warning that Shayṭān is his manifest enemy:

> ⟪quran 36:60⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ أَلَمْ أَعْهَدْ إِلَيْكُمْ يَبَنِىٓ آدَمَ أَن لَّا تَعْبُدُوا۟ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ لَكُمْ عَدُوٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> 
> *"O children of Adam! Did I not enjoin upon you that you should not worship Satan? Indeed, he is to you a clear enemy."*

We say that the principal cause lies in this: Satan has subjugated the human being through his cunning and intelligence, working in concert with the human's own natural faculties. Rather than seeking divine aid at the moment of satanic assault, the human surrenders and submits to him. Consider how Satan has exploited the sexual faculty—that power which Allāh instilled in humans for the preservation of the self and the continuation of the species. Through perversion, he has diverted it toward the unlawful and immoral, transforming what should be a means of survival into the foremost instrument of corruption upon the earth. A wise person once observed: "Rage, earth, and women are the root of all three corruptions."[^cite-3]

Similarly, divine anger (*ghaḍab*) was created in humans as a faculty to repel harm and eliminate what is injurious. Yet Satan has turned this noble power toward evil and made it an agent of ruin and destruction. The inclination of humans toward sin resembles the craving of a sick person for foods that aggravate his condition. When the body's constitution deviates from balance, the afflicted person craves precisely what harms him and intensifies his malady. A fever-stricken patient desires cold water, which worsens his state. One whose stomach is corrupted and cannot digest even small portions nonetheless yearns to eat more; no matter how much he consumes, his craving knows no satiation, and his digestive chaos only deepens. By contrast, a healthy person never craves what injures him; rather, he naturally partakes of beneficial foods and nourishment.

The world itself may be compared to air corrupted by noxious agents. Yet since humans cannot survive without breathing, they continually purify this polluted atmosphere using fragrant substances—camphor, incense, aromatic woods—and remove its stench through fumigation. They deploy all remedies and medicines that cure tainted air, persisting in that same air through their remedial efforts. So too does the human being, at every step of his journey through this life, encounter the evil promptings (*waswasa*) of the soul that corrupt and destroy his spiritual essence. Though he must live in this atmosphere of temptation, the path to immunity from satanic influence lies in renouncing base desires, remembering Allāh, beseeching His aid, diminishing attachment to the world, and obeying the Messenger's commands. These practices necessarily generate noble thoughts within the heart and mind; the intellect and temperament attain balance and rectitude, and the person's inclination toward truth and the people of truth increases. At that point, the obligations of the sharīʿa—prayer, almsgiving (*zakāt*), fasting—no longer appear as tribulations but as comfort and solace; he finds joy in them. His passion and fervor in obeying the divine commands intensifies. Then Satan will be compelled ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫ to concede: "I shall never be able to dominate this person."

The Messenger of Allāh (peace and blessings be upon ⟪ar:حضرت ابوذر غفاری⟫ him) said to Abū Dharr al-Ghifārī: "O Abū Dharr, conduct yourself in this world as a stranger or as a wayfarer. Consider yourself among the dead. Speak little, but remember Allāh greatly. Through this, you will overcome Satan. Speak the truth, even if the hearer finds it bitter. Love the poor and sit in their gatherings. Be content with bread that sustains your body and a garment that covers you, and find therein your sufficiency. Covet not the world, for it is the root of every sin. Whoever turns away from the world finds all afflictions light upon him."[^cite-4]

To "consider yourself among the dead" means this: just as the dead perform no actions by personal preference, so too should every believer conduct all his affairs and manage all his desires according to the sharīʿa alone, exercising no personal choice. As the poet expresses it:

> My friend placed a rope around my neck;  
> They lead me wherever they desire.  
> My self and personal inclination have no say in it.

### The Two Forms of Satan's Seduction

Know that there are two modes of satanic worship. The first is open disobedience to Allāh's commands. The second is obedience to Allāh's commands, yet not in the manner revealed by Allāh, but rather in the manner of innovations and those who distort the dīn. For example, some people perform the prayer, but they add the *Tarāwīḥ* prayer—an innovation—to their practice.

Satan employs two methods of seduction. In the first, he dispatches his agents and disciples to mislead the servant, coming to him with words contrary to Allāh's and His Messenger's commands, casting doubts and confusions into his heart until, finding no resolution on his own, he goes astray. In the second method, Satan himself uses his whispers to make the human his instrument, inducing him to commit sins of his own volition.

Therefore, if someone approaches you as a well-wisher offering counsel, listen carefully and examine whether what he says accords with what Allāh and His Messenger have revealed. Test it against the Qurʾān and the measure of intellect. If it does not accord, be certain that this person, clothed in the garment of benevolence, is a satanic agent sent by Satan. Should you obey him, you would be worshipping Satan. Similarly, when an impulse arises in you to do something, first deliberate: Does the Sharīʿa of Islām permit this act? If the law forbids it, yet the soul incites you to do it, understand that this is a satanic whisper, and Satan has made you his tool. Cease immediately, lest Satan overcome you and you become his obedient servant. These two methods are described by Allāh in these words:

> ⟪quran 6:112⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *"And so We have appointed for every Prophet enemies—Shayatin (devils) among mankind and jinn, inspiring one another with adorned speech as a delusion. If your Lord had so willed, they would not have done it, so leave them alone with their fabrications."*

This verse clarifies that Satan embellishes and inspires false deceptions through two channels: either as an external force in the form of ⟪ar:'أنس'⟫ human beings, or as an internal force such as ⟪ar:'جن'⟫ the commanding soul ⟪ar:أمارۃ بالسوء⟫ (the soul that incites to evil).

### A Third Method: Deception Through Ostensible Obedience

Beyond these, Satan possesses yet another stratagem for misleading humans and making them his own. He first incites the person to disobey Allāh's commands. Should the person yield, Satan's purpose is accomplished, and he becomes Satan's servant and worshipper. However, if the person disregards Satan's words and instead follows Allāh's obedience and commandments, Satan then whispers: "Very well, obey Allāh—so that your esteem and honor may increase among people! You will earn the reputation of a saint. Let people regard you as one of Allāh's guardians, and let your brothers and companions benefit greatly from your renown and status." If this person then worships Allāh with the intention sown by Satan, his worship becomes not the worship of Allāh, but the obedience and service of Satan. In other words, Satan leaves no stratagem untried; he deploys every deception and weapon at his disposal.

## The Three Categories of Sinners and the Disobedient

There are three distinct classes of sinners who follow Satan. Not all who commit sin and disobedience are equal.

| **First** | Those who obey Satan's commands with their hearts' love and their tongues' praise. They submit to him with complete joy and whole-heartedness. |
| --- | --- |
| **Second** | Those who commit evil deeds yet dislike them in their hearts. They call such deeds wicked with their tongues. When they perpetrate evil, their conscience troubles them and admonishes them; they immediately confess their transgression and seek forgiveness from their Lord. Their satanic obedience is confined to the organs of the body; their hearts remain unstained by sin. |
| **Third** | Those who commit evil yet do not perceive it as evil. Their hearts and minds adapt to and accept the evil deed without reflection. These are sinners through ignorance. |

### Atonement for Sins Committed in Ignorance

The sins of those who sin through ignorance are expunged and cleansed by afflictions, diseases, pains, and sufferings sent by Allāh, as the Prophet's *ḥadīth* declares: "Fever is a flame from the flames of Hell." And again: "Fever is a purification sent by a Merciful Lord." And: "The sword is an eraser of sins."[^cite-5]

### Atonement for Intentional Sin

Sins committed with the heart's pleasure and the tongue's praise can be forgiven only through sincere repentance (*tawba*) and turning back to Allāh. There is no other path. Such a person must turn to Allāh with genuine remorse, refrain his tongue from praising evil, and purify his intention. The heart is the king and commander of the body; the tongue is its minister and counselor; the organs are its servants. Therefore, a sin committed with the deliberate intention of the heart and to satisfy one's base desires is indeed grievous. Whatever is done against Allāh's commands and for the love of other than Allāh shall incur painful punishment. Similarly, what the tongue utters is attributed to the heart, and one who commits sin by speech shall receive the same punishment as one who commits it through deed.

### If Ignorance Leads to Sin, Advancement is Not Hindered

The sins that emerge from the limbs despite the heart's aversion are those concerning which it is narrated that Allāh says: "O My servants! If you were not to sin, I would raise up a people who would sin, then ask forgiveness. There is profound wisdom in this divine utterance. Sometimes Satan causes a pious servant to stumble in ignorance, and Satan rejoices, thinking: "I have deceived such-and-such a righteous servant of Allāh! He fell into my snare so thoroughly that he could not escape. I have made him work against the will of his Lord!" Yet in reality, this very sin elevates the status of that righteous person. When, in ignorance, the pious one commits a sin and then realizes it, his heart breaks; whatever slight arrogance, self-regard, or pride had arisen in his heart through his worship and lengthy devotions dissolves away. Thus he is saved from the great sin of spiritual arrogance and asks forgiveness for the small transgression. His rank thereby becomes yet higher. The Prophet (peace be upon him) declared in another *ḥadīth*: "I draw near to broken hearts."[^cite-6] From this we may conclude that the slips and errors of ⟪ar:انبیاء علیھم السلام⟫ the prophets (upon whom be peace) were of this character: as soon as they sought forgiveness, their ranks were elevated and they drew nearer to Allāh. The poet Iqbāl expresses this truth:

> Keep not your errors hidden—they are your mirror.  
> The broken one is dearest to the mirror-maker.

And Majdī says:

> Allāh remembers a broken heart;  
> The lover dies in pride.  
> Dwelling in the Throne of Heartbreak—  
> A strange cup, broken yet filled.

## A Wise Saying of the Prophet Dāwūd (David)

The Prophet Dāwūd (peace be upon him) uttered a truth of profound wisdom: "What has been shall be again. That which has already occurred will recur in the future and manifest itself anew; there is nothing new beneath this sun."[^cite-7] In other words, history repeats itself in cyclical fashion. By this principle, whatsoever transpired and appeared in preceding ages occurred likewise during the epoch of our Prophet Muḥammad Muṣṭafā (peace be upon him). His era was not spared these calamities, disputes, dissensions, trials, and disobedience. Yet because the Holy Prophet's personality embodies the light in which are gathered all the radiances (*anwār*) of former prophets, and their attributes are merged and unified in him, his characteristics and qualities necessarily constitute the totality and compendium of theirs. A poet has expressed this beautifully:

> The Prophet's virtue is a gathering of Muhammads;  
> Each prophet possesses some virtue,  
> But all virtues comprehensively inhere in Muḥammad.

And a Persian poet has rendered it thus:

> Ḥasan, Joseph, the tail of Jesus—alas, their memory!  
> In the beauty of the well, all beauty is encompassing;  
> In the aloneness of the well, all aloneness dwells.
