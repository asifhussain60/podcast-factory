## The Messenger as Bearer of Sharīʿa (Ṣāḥib Sharīʿa Rasūl)

Every messenger among the apostles stands within the legal tradition of his predecessor, drawing upon the commandments already established before him. He does not spring forth unbidden, but rather emerges from within the community of believers, nurtured among them from his inception. Through rigorous purification—of the self, of knowledge, of capacity—he refines himself until the Imam of the preceding age recognizes his readiness and establishes him in the prophetic rank.[^cite-1] In this way, the continuity of divine guidance remains unbroken across the ages, each bearer of sharīʿa (law) anchored to what came before while preparing what shall come after.

## The Four Causes of the World's Coming-Into-Being

### The Conception of Ordinary Scholars

According to the scholars, the world emerges into being through four primary causes:

| 1 | Allah created all creatures in order to reveal and manifest His own existence. Had He not brought creatures into being, who would have known that He exists and possesses creative power?
| --- | --- |
| 2 | He established firm and enduring things to demonstrate His knowledge and power, showing that such realities and their operations can only proceed from the Almighty.
| 3 | He created for the purpose of worship, so that He might reward those who worship according to His grace rather than according to their limited deeds. In His sovereignty, He grants rewards vastly exceeding what creatures deserve, for the obedience of the disobedient adds nothing to His dominion, nor does their disobedience diminish it. As He Himself declares: "And what is the creation of the world?"
| 4 | He created creatures and bestowed favor upon them so that His attribute of being the Benefactor (al-Muḥsin) would manifest. Thus does He deal with some justly and with others through grace, for He is both Just and Beneficent. He created the believers specifically to pour upon them His mercy, as He says: "Wa kāna bi-l-muʾminīn raḥīm" (And He is merciful to the believers).

### The Scholar's Discourse, Elevated Further

After these preliminary reasons and the statements of the scholars, we too affirm that Allah created all creation for the purpose of the Essence of the World—⟪ar:حضرت محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ و سلم⟫—which stands as the greatest blessing and mercy of Allah to mankind. Yet this Essence required preliminary stages for its manifestation. For this reason, He created Adam and all the other prophets, each an instrument in the cosmic unfolding toward this supreme purpose.[^cite-2]

It is transmitted from an elder companion, ⟪ar:قتادہ سعید ابن مسیب⟫, that Allah revealed to ʿĪsā (Jesus): "O Jesus, believe in ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ and command your people to believe in him." He then said: "Had ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ not been destined to appear, I would not have created Adam, nor Paradise, nor Hell. When I first established My Throne upon water, it trembled. But when I inscribed upon it ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ محمد رسول اللہ⟫—'There is no god but Allah; Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allah'—it became fixed and still." The Throne rests upon water, which serves as a symbol of knowledge (ʿilm). This shows that knowledge is indispensable for attainment of Allah, for His recognition, and for true monotheism (tawḥīd).[^cite-3]

### The Conception of Mystical Reality

A master of inner truth has said that the Creator fashioned this world upon the basis of supreme wisdom, hidden from both scholars and the masses alike. The one who narrates this wisdom shall speak openly ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫ when the appointed time arrives. In brief, He did not create the world in vain, uselessly, meaninglessly, or without purpose, as He Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 23:115⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَفَحَسِبْتُمْ أَنَّمَا خَلَقْنَكُمْ عَبَثًۭا وَأَنَّكُمْ إِلَيْنَا لَا تُرْجَعُونَ ‎⟫
> *"Did you think that We had created you in play (without any purpose), and that you would not be brought back to Us?"*
> — **Quran 23:115**

## The Secret of Shayṭān's Power Over Humans and How He Leads Them Astray

We wish to elucidate certain reasons why Shayṭān (Satan) has gained dominion over humankind and succeeded in leading them into error. Why does the human soul so readily incline toward satanic desires—toward intoxication, toward adultery, toward transgression—despite Allah's explicit warning that Shayṭān is their manifest enemy, as He declares:

> ⟪quran 36:60⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ أَلَمْ أَعْهَدْ إِلَيْكُمْ يَبَنِىٓ آدَمَ أَن لَّا تَعْبُدُوا۟ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ لَكُمْ عَدُوٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *"Did I not ordain for you, O Children of Adam, that you should not worship Satan? Indeed, he is to you a clear enemy."*
> — **Quran 36:60**

The fundamental cause, we maintain, is that Shayṭān has gained mastery over the human being through cunning and wisdom, employing the very natural forces that Allah planted within him. Humans, in their moment of satanic assault, have failed to seek refuge in Allah; instead, they have surrendered themselves to the devil's dominion. Consider how Shayṭān has harnessed the force of sexual desire and lust. Allah created this desire for the continuation of humanity and the species, yet Shayṭān has twisted it toward illicit, immoral, unlawful ends, transforming what was meant for survival into an instrument of corruption and devastation upon the earth. As one has aptly said: "Gold, earth, and women are the roots of all three destructions."

In like manner, Allah instilled anger within humans so that they might repel harm and undesirable things. Yet Shayṭān has seized control of this faculty, perverting it toward evil and ruin. The human inclination toward sin mirrors the inclination of the sick toward foods that worsen their illness. When the temperament deviates from equilibrium, one craves precisely what harms one most. The fever-stricken person yearns only for cold water, which intensifies the disease. One whose digestive powers have failed desires ever more food, consuming endlessly without satiation, the disorder deepening with each bite. In contrast, the healthy person never craves what harms him; he partakes of what benefits him and avoids what injures him.

Consider the analogy of air itself. Though air is naturally pure and life-giving, corruption and harmful germs have tainted it. Yet humans cannot exist without it. They therefore purify this polluted atmosphere—using fragrant remedies such as camphor and incense, spraying disinfectants—and continue to breathe and dwell within it. So too does the believer live amid the constant whispers of this world's seductions at every turn, assaulting the soul with turmoil and decay. Though we cannot escape this atmosphere, the remedy lies in renouncing one's base desires, invoking Allah's aid continuously, diminishing attachment to the worldly realm, and obeying the Messenger's commands. These practices inevitably plant good thoughts in heart and mind; the intellect and temperament return to equilibrium and rightness. Then one's inclination naturally turns toward truth and its people; the rigors of the sharīʿa (the law)—salāt (ritual prayer), zakāt (almsgiving), ṣawm (fasting)—no longer feel like burdens but become sources of solace and joy. Passion and fervor in obedience to divine command burn ever brighter. At that moment, Shayṭān will confess openly ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫: "I shall never be able to gain dominion over this human."

The Prophet (upon whom be peace) said to Abū Dharr al-Ghifārī ⟪ar:حضرت ابوذر غفاری⟫: "O Abū Dharr, be as a traveler in this world. Consider yourself as one who is dead. Speak little; remember Allah much. Through this you will conquer Shayṭān. Speak truth even if the listener finds it bitter. Love the poor and sit in their gatherings. Be content with a morsel of bread that sustains your body and a garment that covers you, and find happiness in this. Love not the world, for it is the root of every sin. Whoever turns away from the world finds all hardship easy to bear."[^cite-4]

To consider oneself as dead means this: just as a corpse has no will of its own, so too should every Muslim conduct all his affairs and desires in accordance with the sharīʿa, surrendering personal preference entirely. As the poet has said:

> My friend has bound a rope around my neck,
> And drags me whithersoever he wishes.
> My selfish desires have no say in this.

Know that there are two forms of worship of Shayṭān. One is the open disobedience of Allah's commands. The other is outward obedience to His commands, yet performed not as Allah has revealed but through innovation and the alteration of religion. For instance, some pray, yet they innovate prayers like the night vigil (Tarāwīḥ), which is a bid'a (unauthorized innovation).[^cite-5]

Likewise, Shayṭān employs two methods of seduction. In the first, he dispatches his disciples and followers to mislead, approaching the servant with speech contrary to Allah's commands and sowing doubts, suspicions, and confusions in the heart until the person, finding no resolution alone, loses the way. In the second method, Shayṭān makes the human his own instrument through whispers, causing him to commit sins by his own willing choice.

Therefore, should a well-wisher come to you with counsel, hear him out; but then examine whether what he says accords with what Allah and His Messenger have said. Test it against the Qur'ān and the touchstone of intellect. If it does not accord, know with certainty that this person, though clothed in the garment of benevolence, is a satanic agent and emissary. To obey him would be to worship Shayṭān. Likewise, before acting on any impulse, pause and consider whether the Islamic sharīʿa permits this deed. If the sharīʿa forbids it, yet your soul desires it, then understand that this is a satanic whisper, and Shayṭān has made you his tool. Cease at once, lest Shayṭān overcome you and you become bound in servitude to him.

These two methods have been described by Allah in these words:

> ⟪quran 6:112⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *"Thus have We made for every prophet an enemy—evil ones among mankind and jinn, inspiring one another with adorned speech as deception. Had your Lord willed, they would not have done it; so leave them to what they fabricate."*
> — **Quran 6:112**

This verse clarifies that Shayṭān embellishes false discourse or inspires it in two forms: through an external force such as ⟪ar:'أنس'⟫ (humans) or through an internal force such as ⟪ar:'جن'⟫ (unseen entities), what is termed ⟪ar:أمارۃ بالسوء⟫ (the soul that commands to evil).

There remains yet another stratagem by which Shayṭān misleads and enslaves: he first incites the human to oppose Allah's commands. Should the person obey him, his desire is fulfilled, and he becomes Shayṭān's servant and worshipper. But if the person turns from his words and holds fast to obedience to Allah and His commandments, Shayṭān will shift his approach and say: "Yes! Obey Allah, so that your honor and rank may increase among the people! Let them know you as a saint! Let them regard you as a friend of Allah, and may they benefit from your eminence and fame." Now, if this person worships Allah with the intention Shayṭān has suggested—for the sake of worldly reputation—then his worship is no longer of Allah but rather of Shayṭān. Shayṭān leaves no stone unturned; he deploys every ruse and weapon in his arsenal to deceive the human being.

## The Three Categories of Sinners and the Disobedient

Not all sinners and transgressors are alike; they fall into three distinct categories.

| 1 | There are those who follow Shayṭān's words with wholehearted love, praising them with their tongues. They obey his commands with complete joy and sincerity, with no reservation of the heart.
| --- | --- |
| 2 | There are those who commit evil deeds yet inwardly abhor them utterly, condemning such acts with their words. When they transgress, their conscience strikes them sharply, warning and reproaching, and they immediately turn, confess, and seek forgiveness from their Lord. Their satanic servitude remains confined to the outward limbs; the heart harbors no complicity.
| 3 | There are those who commit evil yet do not regard it as evil. Neither reflection nor regret arises; heart and mind become habituated to the transgression without objection. These are those who sin out of ignorance.

### Atonement for Sin Committed in Ignorance

The sins of such people—those who transgress unknowingly—are cleansed and purified through trials, afflictions, illnesses, and sufferings. As the Prophet's ḥadīth (tradition) states: ⟪ar-quote:الحُمّٰی من فِیحِ جھنم⟫—"Fever is a flame from the fires of Hell and a gust of its wind." He then said: ⟪ar-quote:الحُمّٰی طُھور من رب غفور⟫—"Fever is a purification from a Merciful Lord." And again: ⟪ar-quote:السَّیفُ مَحَّاء للذُّنُوب⟫—"The sword erases sins."

### Atonement for Intentional Sin

Sins committed with wholehearted pleasure and tongue-praise can be forgiven only through sincere repentance and remorse—there is no other path.[^cite-6] Such a person must turn earnestly to Allah, begging forgiveness for his transgressions, and must restrain his tongue from lauding evil. The heart is the king and sovereign of the body; the tongue is its vizier and counselor; the limbs are its servants and soldiers. A sin committed with heart-felt intention, performed to satisfy the base self, is indeed among the gravest of sins. Whatever is done in opposition to Allah's commands and for love of other than Allah brings upon the transgressor a painful punishment. So too does the sin committed by the tongue become attributed to the heart, and the one who errs with tongue receives the same recompense as he who errs in heart.

### If Ignorance Leads to Sin, Progress Is Not Hindered

The sins that emerge from the limbs despite the heart's dislike are precisely those regarding which Allah is reported to have said: "O My servants, if you do not sin, I shall raise up a people who will sin and then ask My forgiveness." A profound wisdom lies in these words: Sometimes Shayṭān causes a righteous servant of Allah to sin in ignorance, then dances with glee at his supposed victory, saying, "I have deceived this righteous one! I have trapped him so he cannot escape! I have made him act against the will of his Lord!" Yet in truth, this very sin elevates the status of that righteous person. When the pious one commits a sin unwittingly and then becomes aware of it, his heart breaks. All arrogance, self-regard, or self-conceit that may have arisen in his heart through his worship and spiritual disciplines dissolves away. Thus does he escape the gravest sin of all—the sin of the self (al-nafs)—and in seeking forgiveness for this lesser transgression, his rank rises ever higher. The Prophet said in another ḥadīth: ⟪ar-quote:أنا مُنکَسِرَۃِ القُلُوب⟫—"I am near to the broken-hearted."[^cite-7] From this can be understood that the slip-ups and errors of the prophets (ʿalayhim al-salām) were of this very character: as soon as they sought forgiveness, their ranks were elevated, and they drew ever closer to Allah.

The poet Iqbāl says:

> Do not keep it hidden; it is your mirror.
> The one whose heart is broken is dearest to the mirror-maker.

And Majd says:

> God remembers the one with a broken heart;
> A lover dies upon pride.
> To dwell upon the Throne of Heartbreak
> Is a strange cup—it breaks yet fills.

## A Wise Saying of the Prophet Dāwūd (David)

Dāwūd (upon whom be peace) uttered a profound wisdom: "That which has come to pass will come to pass again." The events and circumstances already known will manifest anew, for there is nothing truly new beneath the sun. History, in short, repeats itself. Those trials, disagreements, divisions, calamities, and disobediences that appeared in ages past appeared likewise in the time of our Prophet Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (upon whom be peace). His era was not spared such turmoil. Yet because the Holy Prophet's personality embodies the light in which all former radiances converge and all previous prophetic attributes merge and unify, his characteristics and qualities necessarily comprise the sum of all theirs.[^cite-8]

Someone has expressed it well:

> The Virtue of the Prophet
> Is the gathering of all Muhammads.
> Every prophet possessed some excellence,
> But in Muhammad all excellences are comprehensive.

And a Persian poet has rendered it thus:

> The Beauty of Joseph, the Steadfastness of Job, the Longing of Jesus—
> All in the water are gathered; the ocean's loneliness encompasses all water.
