# Hazrat Adam (peace be upon him)

## The Outer Story (Ẓāhir) of Ādam, Peace Be Upon Him

### The Creation of Ādam from Clay

When the Almighty determined to bring Ādam into existence, a revelation descended upon the earth itself: "O earth! I am about to fashion from you a creature whose descendants shall be divided—some obedient to Me, others rebellious. I shall admit the obedient to Paradise and the disobedient to Hellfire." Thereupon the Most High sent Gabriel (peace be upon him) to the earth with the command: "Bring forth dust from the soil." When Gabriel arrived and began to gather the dust, the earth cried out in protest, swearing by the glory of Him who had sent him: "I beseech you, do not take from me that which shall become an inhabitant of Hell." Gabriel, moved by the earth's supplication, turned back without collecting anything. He reported to the Almighty: "The earth has sworn me an oath by Your honor, and I could not disobey." Then the Almighty sent Michael (peace be upon him). The earth made the same protestation, and Michael too returned empty-handed. Finally, the Almighty dispatched ⟪ar:ملک الموت⟫ Āzrāʾīl, the Angel of Death (peace be upon him). Again the earth swore the same oath, but Āzrāʾīl replied: "By Allah, I shall not disobey His command." And with that resolve, he gathered dust gradually from the four corners of the earth. This soil possessed every quality—soft and hard, hot and cold, white and red, black and yellow, pure and impure. Thus does the diversity of human temperaments, characters, and complexions find its origin in the very composition of the clay from which mankind was fashioned.

⟪ar:عبد اللہ ابن سلام⟫ ʿAbdullāh ibn Salām once inquired of the Messenger of Allah (may Allah bless him and his family): "How did Allah create Ādam?" The Messenger replied: "Ādam's forehead was fashioned from the clay of the Sacred House itself, his ankles from Egyptian soil, his feet from the clay of Hijaz, his right hand from the clay of the East, and his left hand from the clay of the West."[^cite-1]

### The Kneading of the Soil in Different Waters

When Āzrāʾīl brought this handful of earth to the Divine Court, he was commanded to mix it with water and knead it thoroughly. Three varieties of water were employed: bitter, sweet, and salt. Through this kneading, the soil acquired plasticity and malleability, becoming what is called ⟪ar:'طین'⟫ *ṭīn* (clay)—a substance capable of taking form.

Then the Almighty commanded Gabriel: "Now descend and gather for Me luminous, shining, pure dust from which I shall create." Gabriel came down with a great assembly ⟪ar:ملائکہ فردوس⟫ ⟪ar:ملائکہ مغربین⟫ ⟪ar:ملائکہ نصف الاعلی⟫ of the Angels of Paradise, the Angels of the West, and the Angels of the Highest Assembly. They gathered pristine white earth from the tomb of ⟪ar:محمد علیہ السلام⟫ the Prophet Muḥammad (peace be upon him). This pure substance was kneaded with the water of ⟪ar:'تسنیم'⟫ *Tasnīm*, the spring of Paradise, until it gleamed like a pearl washed in white water. Then it was immersed in all the springs of Paradise and bathed therein, becoming radiant and luminous. When the sight of Allah fell upon this pearl, it trembled with awe of the Divine, and shattered into fragments. The Almighty divided this pearl into one hundred and twenty-four thousand drops, and from each drop He created a Prophet. Thus were all the prophets fashioned from the light of Muḥammad. That pearl, the masterpiece of the Almighty, was circulated throughout the heavens and the earth, so that the angels came to know and recognize Muḥammad before they ever beheld Ādam. For this reason the Prophet (may Allah bless him and his family) declared: "I was a prophet when Ādam was still in the state of water and clay."[^cite-2]

### The Forty Years of Rest

The clay of Ādam was left to rest for forty years, during which it became soft and workable, easily taking any desired form. Then it was allowed to rest another forty years until it hardened and dried, becoming brittle and sonorous—the kind that rings when struck:

> ⟪quran 55:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏خَلَقَ ٱلْإِنسَنَ مِن صَلْصَلٍۢ كَٱلْفَخَّارِ ‎⟫
> *He created man from sounding clay like pottery.*

The body of Ādam was shaped from this dry, hard clay precisely to demonstrate that Allah did not bring it into being through the mere unfolding of natural causes from soft, wet earth, but rather through His perfect power alone—creating from matter that was parched, formless, and seemingly lifeless. The body was then placed in the path of the angels for another forty years.

### The Angels' Wonder and Iblīs's Thoughts

As the angels passed by this place, they were struck with amazement at the beauty of Ādam's form, for nothing like it had ever been seen before. But when Iblīs—the Accursed One—passed by, he said to himself: "What is this figure?" He began to examine it with his hand. Finding it hollow within, he would enter through the mouth and exit through it again, speaking to his fellows: "This is indeed a strange creature, utterly empty within. It shall never hold firm!" He went so far as to taunt them: "Suppose this statue were given superiority over you—what would you do?" All replied: "We would obey the command of our Lord." But Iblīs harbored in his heart: "If this creature is exalted above me, I shall refuse obedience; yet if I am exalted above it, I shall not treat it justly."

### The Reason for Sorrow's Predominance Over Joy

It is related in ḥadīth that Ādam's body lay in this state for forty years while rained upon by grief, sorrow, and lamentation—and then for only one year by joy and gladness. This is the sole reason why the children of Ādam are predominantly afflicted with sorrow and melancholy, obtaining only occasional respite and relief. As a poet has expressed it:

> *Sorrow departs, and gladness too departs;*
> *If you possess the strength to grieve, know that grief shall not persist.*

### The Adornment of Ādam After His Completion

When Ādam's body was fully created, the Almighty clothed him in the raiment of Paradise and adorned him with every embellishment. From his teeth light shone forth like the rays of the sun, and upon his forehead gleamed the light of our Prophet ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (may Allah bless him and his family)—bright as the moon of the fourteenth night. Allah seated him upon a throne and lifted him upon the shoulders of the angels, commanding them to carry him through all the heavens so that he might behold the entire cosmos and increase his certainty ⟪ar:عین الیقین⟫ (*ʿayn al-yaqīn*, the certainty of direct witnessing). For nearly a hundred years the angels bore him on their shoulders until Ādam became acquainted with every corner of Paradise. Then the Almighty fashioned for him a horse of pure musk, which He named ⟪ar:'میمون'⟫ *Maimūn* (the Blessed One). Its saddle and bridle were wrought of pearls and jewels, and Ādam was placed upon it. Gabriel took the reins, while Michael and Isrāfīl flanked him right and left in procession through all the heavens. In each heaven, Ādam greeted the inhabitants with the words: ⟪ar:'السلام علیکم یا ملائکۃ اللہ'⟫ "Peace be upon you, O angels of Allah!" Allah then said to Ādam: "O Ādam, this greeting is a blessing and a gift for all believers born from you and your descendants until the Day of Resurrection."[^cite-3]

Then the Almighty taught Ādam all the names. When the angels were asked concerning these names, they replied:

> ⟪quran 2:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالُوا۟ سُبْحَنَكَ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَآ إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *They said: "Glory be to You, we have no knowledge save what You have taught us. Verily, You alone are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise."*

Thereupon Allah commanded Ādam to inform the angels of these names. When Ādam disclosed them all, the angels beheld their guide and teacher. But Iblīs refused to obey and displayed arrogance.

### The Creation of Ḥawwāʾ (Eve)

The exegetes relate that when Allah established Ādam in Paradise, he experienced a profound loneliness. Witnessing his distress and desiring his comfort, the Almighty resolved to create a companion like unto himself. He cast Ādam into a deep sleep, and from the rib on his left side—which ⟪ar:'قصیریٰ'⟫ is called the *qaṣīrā* (the curved rib)—He fashioned Ḥawwāʾ (Eve). During this entire process, Ādam suffered not the slightest pain; for had he felt any discomfort, he would not have been drawn to her, and no man thereafter would have taken interest in a woman.

Eve was then clothed in the raiment of Paradise and adorned with manifold ornaments, then seated upon Ādam's pillow with sixteen adornments. When Ādam awoke, he beheld a radiant and beautiful countenance resting beside him. He inquired of the angel: "What is she?" The reply came: ⟪ar:'عورت'⟫ *ʿawrat* (a woman). "And what is her name?" asked Ādam. The answer was: ⟪ar:'حوا'⟫ *Ḥawwāʾ* (Eve). The angel said: "Rightly have you named her. But tell me—why was she called Eve?" Ādam replied: "Because she was created from a living thing," ⟪ar:حی⟫ *ḥayy* (the living). The angel pressed further: "For what purpose did Allah create her?" Ādam answered: "That I might find repose in her, and she in me." The Almighty Himself has narrated this account in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 4:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱتَّقُوا۟ رَبَّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍۢ وَحِدَةٍۢ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًۭا كَثِيرًۭا وَنِسَآءًۭ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى تَسَآءَلُونَ بِهِۦ وَٱلْأَرْحَامَ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ عَلَيْكُمْ رَقِيبًۭا ‎⟫
> *O mankind! Be dutiful to your Lord, Who created you from a single person (Adam), and from him (Adam) He created his wife (Hawwa), and from them both He created many men and women and fear Allah through Whom you demand your mutual (rights), and (do not cut the relations of) the wombs (kinship). Surely, Allah is Ever an All-Watcher over you.*

The Messenger of Allah (may Allah bless him and his family) said: "Woman was created from Ādam's curved rib. If you attempt to straighten it, you will break it; if you leave it as it is, you will find benefit in it." In old age, men grow more distinguished while women's beauty fades. The reason lies in this: a man is created from earth, and earth does not deteriorate or become ugly with the passage of time and drying—rather, its virtue refines and deepens. A woman, by contrast, has been created from flesh, which rots and becomes foul with the passage of time, and thus loses the attention and regard of others.[^cite-4]

### Ādam's Consecration of Eve as Lawful to Himself

Some ḥadīth traditions relate that when Ādam beheld Eve, he extended his hand toward her. The angels halted him, saying: "Wait!" Ādam protested: "But Allah created her for me!" They replied: "This is true, yet she is not lawful to you until you pay her dower." "What is its measure?" asked Ādam. He was told: "You must invoke blessings upon ⟪ar:حضرت محمد مصطفی (ﷺ)⟫ Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā three times." Ādam asked: "Who is ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ Muḥammad?" The angel replied: "He is the last of the prophets from among your descendants! Had it not been for his creation, you would never have been created."[^cite-5]

### Ādam and Eve's Dwelling in Paradise

When Allah had granted Ādam and Eve their place in Paradise, He made lawful to them all its blessings—save one tree—and said unto them:

> ⟪quran 2:35⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلْنَا يَٓـَٔادَمُ ٱسْكُنْ أَنتَ وَزَوْجُكَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَكُلَا مِنْهَا رَغَدًا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمَا وَلَا تَقْرَبَا هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةَ فَتَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And We said: "O Adam! Dwell you and your wife in the Paradise and eat both of you freely with pleasure and delight of things therein as wherever you will, but come not near this tree or you both will be of the Ẓālimūn (wrong-doers)."*

### The Deception of Iblīs

When Allah had settled Ādam and Eve in Paradise, permitting them all its bounties, and they dwelt in magnificent luxury, Iblīs ⟪ar:أبلیس لعین⟫ (the Accursed One) became consumed with envy. He sought to enter Paradise from which he had been expelled and devised stratagems to deceive Ādam. He entered ⟪ar:حیۃ⟫ in the form of a serpent, so that Ādam and Eve would not recognize him. According to some accounts, Iblīs concealed himself in the feet of a peacock to gain entry. Others relate that Moor—a servant of Ādam and an ancient companion of Iblīs—was his unwitting accomplice. When Iblīs drew near to Ādam and Eve, he began to weep loudly, and they, moved by compassion, wept with him. Thus Iblīs became the first to weep, which is why it is said that Satan's voice is that of one who weeps. Both of them asked with sympathy: "Why do you grieve?" Iblīs replied: "I lament your helplessness and vulnerability, your inevitable ruin. I see that after but a few years you shall surely die, and all these blessings and honors shall be left behind." He spoke with such pathos that both of them fell into sorrow. Having accomplished this, Iblīs departed in tears.

Days later, observing them still troubled by his words and grieving, he returned. Seeing that his arrow had struck its mark, he displayed sympathy and said: "O Ādam, shall I direct you to the tree from which you shall obtain eternal life, whose kingdom shall never perish?" Ādam responded: "Yes!" Then Iblīs pointed them toward the forbidden tree, saying: "Eat from it, and you shall live forever." Ādam protested: "My Lord has forbidden it to me." Iblīs replied: "He has forbidden it precisely so that you shall not become angels and remain in Paradise eternally!" When Ādam continued to refuse, Iblīs swore an oath: "I am among the sincere advisors to you both." They were deceived by his oath, for they could not imagine that anyone would swear falsely by Allah. Thus Eve hastened to eat of the tree and then gave it to Ādam to eat. No sooner had they tasted of it and transgressed their bounds than the garment of heaven fell from their bodies, and they became naked. Ādam rushed about in confusion until they ⟪ar:عناب⟫ approached the vine tree, where Ādam pulled leaves from it. Then the Lord called out: "Ādam! Are you fleeing from Me?" Ādam replied: "Yes, O my Lord, I flee from You out of shame."[^cite-6]

### The Reward Given to the Fig Tree

It is narrated that when Ādam and Eve found themselves naked and began to search among the roofs of Paradise for leaves to conceal their bodies, every tree and bush refused them. Only the fig tree took pity upon them and offered its leaves, with which Ādam and Eve covered themselves. As a reward for this mercy, Allah blessed the fig tree, making its outward appearance equal to its inward, rendering it sweet and beneficial, and causing it to bear fruit twice yearly. The fig is unique among trees in that it produces fruit without flowering—the fruit itself appears direct.[^cite-7]

### The Ten Afflictions of Ādam

Scholars enumerate ten calamities that befell Ādam upon his transgression:

| 1 | He incurred the wrath and anger of Allah. The Divine declared: "Did I not forbid you both from this tree, and warn you that Satan is an open enemy to you both?" |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | The loss of the garment of heaven brought him shame and disgrace. |
| 3 | His skin became weak and darkened. Originally his entire body was as hard and white as nails. |
| 4 | His expulsion from the proximity and neighborhood of Allah. |
| 5 | Ādam and Eve were separated for one hundred years—Ādam dwelling in India, Eve in Jeddah. They then went forth in search of one another until they met at Muzdalifah, a name derived from the verb meaning "to bring closer." When they sat together at the place of gathering, it became known as al-Jumu'ah. On the day of ʿArafah, they were presented to one another at ʿArafāt. |
| 6 | All the parties in this transgression became enemies of one another. Man became the enemy of the serpent, crushing its head whenever he sees it. The peacock too became the enemy of the serpent, and the serpent in turn bites it whenever it has the chance. And the Devil became enemy to them all. |
| 7 | They received evil reproach from the disobedient and the straying, as Allah declares: "Then they ate of the tree, and their nakedness became apparent to them, and they began to cover themselves with the leaves of Paradise, and Ādam disobeyed his Lord, so he went astray." |
| 8 | Their enemies prevailed over the children of Ādam. Allah granted Iblīs this authority, saying: "Seduce of them whomever you can with your voice, and mount upon them your cavalry and your infantry, and share in their wealth and children, and make promises to them"—for the promises of Satan are but illusion. |
| 9 | The world became a prison for Ādam and his descendants. They fell into love of the world and suffered from cold and heat, and were not assured of return to Paradise. |
| 10 | They were burdened with toil and labor. Ādam was among the first to earn his sustenance by the sweat of his brow, as Allah says: "So depart from Paradise, for you are enemies to one another. If guidance comes to you from Me, whoever follows My guidance shall not go astray, nor shall he be wretched." |

| 11 | Eve was made to shed menstrual blood. It is related that when Eve plucked the fruit from the tree, the branch became bloody, and blood dripped from it. Allah said to Eve in anger: "You have shed blood upon this branch, and I shall stain you and your daughters with blood every month." The Messenger of Allah (may Allah bless him and his family) said: "This is an ailment that Allah has made obligatory upon women." |
| --- | --- |
| 12 | The burden of pregnancy. |
| 13 | The severe pain of childbirth. Allah says: "The mother bears him in weakness upon weakness." It is mentioned in ḥadīth that had Eve not sinned, women would not have menstruated, and pregnancy and labor would not have been difficult for them. Rather, their conception and delivery would occur in such secrecy that none would be aware of it. |
| 14 | Defilement from the discharge of menses. |
| 15 | Deficiency of intellect. The Prophet (may Allah bless him and his family) said: "Women of defective intellect and defective religion have overcome the wise and resolute man and shattered his intellect." The women asked: "O Messenger of Allah, how is there deficiency in our religion and intellect?" He replied: "The testimony of two women equals the testimony of one man, which proves deficiency in intellect—that women possess half the intellect of men. And when they menstruate, they are forbidden from prayer and fasting, which is a deficiency in their religious observance." |
| 16 | Inheritance, wherein men receive twice the portion of women. |
| 18 | Authority vested in men. The Prophet (may Allah bless him and his family) said: "Men are the guardians of women," and he admonished men: "Be kind to your women, for they are a trust placed in your hands." |
| 19 | Women have no right of divorce; only men possess this authority. |
| 20 | Deprivation from *Jihād* (armed struggle in the path of Allah). |
| 21 | No woman has been made a prophet. |

| 23 | Women are forbidden to travel alone without a close male relative. |

### The Five Punishments of the Serpent

| 1 | His feet were severed, and he was condemned to crawl upon his belly. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | He was rendered hideously ugly. Formerly he was among the most beautiful of creatures. |
| 3 | His sustenance was made *rub* (curds or sour milk)—a light and slowly digestible food. |
| 4 | Every year he dies in the cold, shedding his skin. |
| 5 | He became the enemy of the children of Ādam, and they became his enemy—to such a degree that the Messenger of Allah (may Allah bless him and his family) permitted his slaying even during prayer and in the state of *iḥrām* (pilgrimage sanctity). |

### Ādam's Expulsion from Paradise

In other words, when Ādam was expelled because of his disobedience, and the Divine decree went forth that the disobedient could not draw near to Him, Ādam took Eve's hand, and together they moved forward. At each step they turned back, hoping for the mercy of their Lord (may He be glorified), yearning that He would bestow upon them His compassion, and they wept with profound shame. When they took the final step out of Paradise, the words ⟪ar:'بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم'⟫ "In the name of Allah, the Merciful, the Compassionate" fell from their lips. Gabriel stopped them and said: "O Ādam! These words have issued from your tongue, and by their blessing and greatness I hope that Allah will have mercy upon you." Allah said to Gabriel: "I shall indeed have mercy upon him and his descendants on the Day of Resurrection when they return to Me."[^cite-8]

Ādam descended upon Mount Saradeep in India, while Eve came down in a place in the Ḥijāz called Jeddah. It is said that after being sent down to earth, Ādam was so consumed by shame that he did not raise his eyes to the heavens for three hundred years. Ibn ʿAbbās narrates that Ādam and Eve wept for two hundred years and neither ate nor drank for forty years, having been snatched away from the delights of Paradise and separated from Allah.

When Allah resolved to show mercy to Ādam after this long period, He taught him certain words through which his repentance was accepted. These were the words: ⟪ar:'لا الہ الا اللہ محمد رسول اللہ'⟫ "There is no god but Allah; Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allah." He saw this inscription written upon the feet of the Throne. This word then became his resource. Through the intercession of Hazrat Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (may Allah bless him and his family), he besought Allah's pardon. Allah forgave them and revealed that they should circumambulate His Throne upon the earth and pray there as it is offered at His Throne. There He would answer their prayers.[^cite-9]

Ādam departed from India for Mecca to visit the House of Allah. The Almighty sent an angel to guide him on this journey. When they reached ʿArafāt, he encountered Eve, who had journeyed from Jeddah in search of him. The two met in this plain on the day of ʿArafāh, whence the place took its name ⟪ar:'عرفات'⟫. Upon his return to Minā, he declared his wish: "Let my request be granted!" He said: "My desire is that You forgive me and praise my works." This is why the place was named Minā, derived from the word for "desire" (*minnā*). Thus Allah answered their supplication and forgave them their sins. Then Ādam returned to India.

### The Divine Revelation to Ādam

Wahb ibn ʿUmayyah narrates that after forgiving Ādam, Allah revealed to him: "O Ādam, I shall gather all your knowledge into four words. One is for Me, one is between you and Me, one is for you, and one is between you and My servants."

| For Allah's Sake | Between Allah and Ādam | For Ādam's Benefit | Between Ādam and the Servants |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| So worship Me and associate none with Me. | Your obligation is to pray and accept My decree. | I shall repay you for what you have done. | Love for them what you love for yourself. |

### The Encounter of Ādam with Iblīs

It is narrated that when Ādam met Iblīs in a forest, he rebuked him for his ruin and disgrace, saying: "O accursed one, what have you done? You deceived me and brought me out of Paradise and reduced me to this state of shame and humiliation! What wrong have I done you?" Iblīs wept and replied: "Yes, Ādam, I confess my guilt toward you and acknowledge what I have done. Yet I ask you the same question: Who has cast me into this disgrace and ruin? For whom am I afflicted with this curse?"

### The Supplication of Iblīs and Its Acceptance

⟪ar:حماد ثابت⟫ ⟪ar:حمید عبد اللہ ابن عبید⟫ It is related that Iblīs said before the Throne of Allah: "My Lord, You have expelled me from Paradise because of Ādam. Grant me dominion over him, and I shall overcome him; otherwise, I shall not." Allah said: "Go, and you have authority over him." When Iblīs perceived the extent of dominion granted for this small request, he thought: "Why should I not ask for more?" He immediately said: "O my Lord, give me more!" Allah replied: "Listen! For every one son born to Ādam, I shall give you two. Your children shall be twice as numerous as his." He said: "O Lord, grant me something more!" The reply came: "Hear then—in the breast of the children of Ādam shall be your dwelling, and you shall run through their veins like blood." Iblīs pressed further: "O Lord, give me yet another boon!" Allah said: "You are permitted to attack the children of Ādam with your cavalry and your infantry, to mingle with their wealth and children, and to seduce them with false promises—for truly, your promise is nothing but deceit and filth."[^cite-10]

### Ādam's Supplication to Allah and Its Acceptance

When Ādam beheld that every request of Iblīs was being fulfilled, such that he and his children would be crushed, he also prayed to his Lord: "O Lord, You have imposed Iblīs upon us, but surely You know that we shall be unable to escape his snare without Your aid. What shall become of me and my children?" Allah (may He be glorified) consoled him, saying: "O Ādam! Every child of yours shall be accompanied by a protector who shall guard him from this evil being." Ādam asked: "Give me something more!" Allah replied: "Hear the glad tidings: whoever among your children does a good deed, I shall give him a tenfold reward; and whoever commits a sin, I shall punish him only as much as he has earned, no more. And then I shall not hold him in account, but rather I shall pardon and absolve him." Ādam said: "Go and tell your children: 'O sinful servants of Allah, never despair of Allah's mercy.'" Allah declared: "Listen! Even to the last breath of your children, I shall forgive those who repent, and I shall never close the door of repentance upon them." Ādam implored: "Tell me another glad tiding!" Allah replied: "Yes! I shall forgive your children, and I shall not reckon against them for their wrongs." Rejoicing, Ādam said: "It is enough, O my Lord!"

### The Dominion of Iblīs

It is narrated that Iblīs said before the Throne of ⟪ar:رب العزت⟫ the Lord of Might: "O Lord, You have cursed me and cast me out of Paradise, making me an outcast. You have made me ugly and humiliated, and given me the titles of Satan and Iblīs. All of this I have heard and borne, yet I have a request—grant me some authority." He said: "You shall have messengers to guide the children of Ādam and a Book sent down to them." Iblīs asked: "Who shall be my messengers? And what shall be my book?"

The Almighty answered: "Your messengers shall be the priests; your book shall be a compilation of calumny, backbiting, and slander." He asked: "What is my ḥadīth (tradition)?" He answered: "Your ḥadīth shall be a scroll of lies." "What shall be my recitation?" "Your Qur'ān shall be the recitation of poetry." "Who shall be my muezzin (caller)?" "Musical instruments." "What shall be my mosque?" "The marketplace." "What shall be my house?" "The bathhouse, a place of filth and obscenity." "What shall be my food?" "Anything upon which the name of Allah has not been pronounced (the *Bismillāh*)." "What shall I drink?" "Every intoxicating substance." "And what shall be my prey?" "Women shall be your quarry!"[^cite-11]

### The Final Testament of Ādam

Mujāhid relates that ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās told him: Hazrat Ādam, who dwelt in India throughout his life, performed the pilgrimage on foot forty times. The light of Hazrat Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (may Allah bless him and his family) shone upon Ādam's forehead. That is to say, he called out to Allah throughout his life, fulfilling the right of the Prophet. He endured every manner of hardship and suffering from the people of his time, bearing it patiently and persevering in his ⟪ar:دعوت الی اللہ⟫ *daʿwah* (summons to Allah) until the time of his death drew near.[^cite-12]

He was afflicted with illness for eleven days before his death. During this time, he wrote a testament and gave it to his son ⟪ar:شِیث علیہ السلام⟫ Shīth (peace be upon him), commanding him to keep it hidden from the children of Qābīl, for when Ādam had betrothed Qābīl's sister ʿAqlīmah to ʿAbīl, Qābīl had slain Abel out of jealousy and malice. Ādam feared that Qābīl, learning that special knowledge had been entrusted to Shīth, would harbor resentment. Thus Shīth and his descendants concealed this particular knowledge from the descendants of Qābīl, who were thereby deprived and kept ignorant because of their enmity toward Abel.[^cite-13]

### The Death of Prophet Ādam

According to the narrators, Ādam died on a Friday. The angels brought his shroud and perfume from heaven, and they washed Ādam's body. For the first time, the corpse was washed with mixed water, then with plain water, and covered with a three-layered shroud. He was then interred in Mashariq al-Firdaws, the first grave upon the earth. Ādam was buried by his son Shīth and his brothers. Upon Ādam's death, the sun and moon were eclipsed for six days.[^cite-14]

After completing Ādam's burial, Hazrat Shīth requested Gabriel to lead the funeral prayer. Gabriel said: "Go ahead and pray for your father." Thus Shīth (peace be upon him) led his father's funeral prayer, pronouncing thirty *takbīrāt* (utterances of "Allāhu Akbar—God is Most Great"). Five *takbīrāt* were taken from the five daily prayers, and twenty-five were added in recognition of Ādam's virtue.[^cite-15]

> The Prophet (may Allah bless him and his family) recited seventy *takbīrāt* at the funeral of ⟪ar:سید الشھداء جناب حمزہ⟫ the Master of Martyrs, Hamzah (may Allah be pleased with him).

### The Death of Eve

Eve lived for one year after Ādam's death. She passed away in Jeddah. There exists a vast grave upon the banks of the river there.

## Ādam Ṣafīy Allāh — God's Chosen One

### First Speaker: Hazrat Ādam, Peace Be Upon Him

The designation "First Speaker" refers to the Imam of that age in the *Imāmate* succession, whose historical counterpart in this text is given as:

**Name:** ⟪ar:'عبد اللہ ا بن تنجوم أبن فوجان'⟫ ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm ibn Fujān

**Title:** *Ṣafīy Allāh* (God's Chosen One)

**Designation:** Maulānā Hind (peace be upon him)—the Imam of that era

**Lineage and Place:** He was the brother of ⟪ar:امام ھنید علیہ السلام⟫ Imam al-Hunaid (peace be upon him) and belonged to ⟪ar:'زوحاقہ'⟫ the tribe of al-Zūḥāqah, dwelling in ⟪ar:'لنکا'⟫ Lanka. There, ⟪ar:'شابازداری'⟫ in the settlement of Shabāzdārī, Maulānā ⟪ar:تنجوم⟫ Tanjūm held dominion as king—a name suggesting his expertise in ⟪ar:'آدم'⟫ astrology and the movements of the stars.

**Appointment:** The initiator (*mubtadi*) of every new age is called *Ādam*, and the terminator (*qāʾim*) of that period is called one who eliminates and completes it. Since ⟪ar:حضرت عبد اللہ بن تنجوم⟫ Hazrat ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm was on the verge of inaugurating the Seventy Period, he was designated ⟪ar:'قائم'⟫ *Ādam* (the Initiator).[^cite-16]

## The Garden of Ādam (the Janna in Which He Was Placed)

There exists considerable disagreement among scholars concerning the nature of Ādam's paradise and that of Eve—whether it was situated in the heavens or upon the earth. According to those possessed of truth, the paradise in which ⟪ar:'حضرات امامی'⟫ the Imāmate leaders dwelt was not in the celestial realms, but rather upon the earth, and it bears no relation to the ⟪ar:جنت خلد⟫ *Jannatu l-Khulud* (Paradise of Eternity), the reward of Allah. Had it been a paradise of ultimate reward, Iblīs would not have been expelled from it, for one does not exit from the eternal abode. Nor could he have entered afterward to deceive Ādam. The Qur'ān itself testifies that Shaytān (Satan) said to Ādam:

> ⟪quran 20:120⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ إِلَيْهِ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ هَلْ أَدُلُّكَ عَلَىٰ شَجَرَةِ ٱلْخُلْدِ وَمُلْكٍۢ لَّا يَبْلَىٰ ‎⟫
> *Then Satan whispered to him: "O Ādam, shall I direct you to the Tree of Eternity and a kingdom that will not decay?"*

As Ādam's attention was drawn and he assented, Shaytān continued:

> ⟪quran 7:20⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَالِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *Satan whispered to them both in order to reveal to them that which was hidden from them of their private parts. He said: "Your Lord forbade you this tree only so that you might not become angels, or become of the immortals."*

These verses themselves demonstrate that Ādam dwelt in a paradise not destined for eternity. It is not possible to exit the abode of eternal reward once one enters, as Allah states:

> ⟪quran 15:48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَا يَمَسُّهُمْ فِيهَا نَصَبٌۭ وَمَا هُم مِّنْهَا بِمُخْرَجِينَ ‎⟫
> *"No sense of fatigue shall touch them, nor shall they ever be expelled from it."*

When Satan refused to prostrate, he was cursed. Whoever incurs the wrath of Allah cannot return to Paradise. Moreover, it is inconsistent with Allah's wisdom to place one in Paradise without righteous action, or to admit one who has not earned it thereby, for Allah does not reward ⟪ar:عاملین⟫ those who do not labor. The supreme evidence that Allah created Ādam upon the earth is the Qur'ān itself, which says:

> ⟪quran 2:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when your Lord said to the angels, "I am placing a vicegerent on the earth." They said, "Will You place in it those who will spread corruption and shed blood, while we glorify You with praise and sanctify You?" He said, "I know what you do not know."*

In this verse, Allah explicitly declares: "I am making a caliph upon ⟪ar:'زمین'⟫ the earth"—not in the heavens.[^cite-17]

## The Test of the Angels (Q2:30–33)

The Qur'ān describes the creation and testing of Ādam in these words:

> ⟪quran 2:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when your Lord said to the angels, "I am placing a vicegerent on the earth." They said, "Will You place in it those who will spread corruption and shed blood, while we glorify You with praise and sanctify You?" He said, "I know what you do not know."*

This verse refers specifically to the Imāms ⟪ar:'رب'⟫ of that era—those who terminated the age of *Fitrah* (primordial nature) and initiated the Seventy Period. You perceive that as this new age approached, one in which the Imāms would not themselves undertake propagation but instead appoint successors (*Khulafāʾ*), prophets, and messengers in their stead, the choice fell upon many able and learned persons. Yet the Imam's gaze turned to ⟪ar:'ھنید علیہ السلام'⟫ ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm, who was his nephew and still in the early stages of acquiring the fundamental sciences. The Imam resolved to establish him as a prophet and to accomplish his second creation—namely, the creation through knowledge. As Allah says:

> *Then We created the sperm into a clot, and created the clot into a morsel, and created the morsel into bones, and clothed the bones with flesh; then We created it as another creation. So blessed be Allah, the Best of Creators!* (Q23:14)

When Imam al-Hunaid (peace be upon him) decided to appoint Ādam to the station of Imāmate, he selected twelve angels to serve as the *Niqābah* (chiefs of the Summoners), so that they might learn from Ādam and carry out the *daʿwa* (the summons to Allah) on his behalf throughout the realm. The names of these twelve limits (*ḥudūd*) were:[^cite-18]

| 1 | Gabriel | 7 | Amḥāʾīl |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2 | Michael | 8 | Tahṭīʾīl |
| 3 | Isrāfīl | 9 | Ḥūyāʾīl |
| 4 | Āzrāʾīl | 10 | Marḥīʾīl |
| 5 | Dardabīl | 11 | Mālik al-Yaʿl |
| 6 | Sahāʾīl | 12 | Kīrḡiz |

From among these twelve limits, Imam al-Hunaid declared: "I intend to establish my successor in the *daʿwa* (the divinely-guided summons). And I have chosen my nephew for this task." He commanded them: "When I have established him, all of you shall bow before him in obedience."

> ⟪quran 15:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا سَوَّيْتُهُۥ وَنَفَخْتُ فِيهِ مِن رُّوحِى فَقَعُوا۟ لَهُۥ سَجِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *"So when I have perfected him and breathed into him of My spirit, fall down before him in prostration."*

Regarding Ādam's education, Allah declared: "I shall create Ādam from ⟪ar:طین⟫ *ṭīn* (clay)."

> ⟪quran 38:71⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى خَلِقٌۢ بَشَرًۭا مِّن طِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *When your Lord said to the angels: "I am going to create a human being from clay."*

In ⟪ar:'طین'⟫ Arabic, *ṭīn* signifies mud—a compound of soil and water. The soil represents the outward knowledge of the *Sharīʿa* (the manifest law), while water represents the inward knowledge of *taʾwīl* (esoteric exegesis). The Imam was therefore declaring: "I shall fashion Ādam from both categories of knowledge." Because the angels had been created from inward, subtle knowledge alone—a knowledge that is spiritual and transcendent—they understood that such subtle knowledge flows like lightning from those of higher station, whereas knowledge acquired through gradual learning and study belongs to those below.

| Subtle/Inward Knowledge | Outward/Manifest Knowledge |
| --- | --- |
| *ʿIlm al-Bāṭin* — Knowledge of the Inward | *ʿIlm al-Ẓāhir* — Knowledge of the Outward |
| Comes by Divine Grace | Acquired through Study and Effort |
| Like Lightning | Gradually |
| Relates to Affirmation and Belief | Relates to Action and Practice |

The angels also understood that the subtle inner knowledge, divorced from action, lacks connection to faith and affirmation, and—lacking embodied practice and bodily exertion—cannot produce the fruits of obedience. Referring to this principle, the Prince of the Believers (*Amīr al-Muʾminīn*) has explained the excellence of the Qur'ān, saying: "Its outward [aspect] is obligatory action, and its inward [aspect] is hidden knowledge." Thus when it became clear to the angels that the actions of this human being—that is, the obligatory deeds—would perforce bring forth error and negligence, they approached their Imam, reasoning: "If your purpose is praise and sanctification through inner sciences alone, we excel in these. If you wish to select someone for this task, choose from among us, for we have mastered so many sciences of the *daʿwa*, completed the books of *taʾwīl*, and are ever studying the scrolls of *ʿilm al-ḥaqāʾiq* (the science of realities). It would be more befitting to appoint one of us to oversee your *daʿwa*, since we can manage its affairs far better than Ādam, who is yet early in his study of the outward sciences."

They said to the Imam:

> *"Glory be to You! We have no knowledge except what You have taught us. Verily, You alone are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise."*

The Imam replied: "I know what you do not know. I shall give Ādam My support; if he errs, I shall make good his deficiency and preserve him from ruin." Yet there was among these angels one—⟪ar:'عزازیل'⟫ ʿAzāzīl—who conceived himself superior to Ādam. Considering himself worthy of the rank of caliphate, he refused obedience and declined to participate in the prayer of allegiance. His name was ⟪ar:'حارث بن مرۃ'⟫ Ḥārith ibn Murrah. In ⟪ar:'⟫⟪ar:'⟫ this verse, what the Imam knew—and what the limits did not—was Ādam's capacity and their own deficiency. The Imam equally knew that when the limits were commanded to obey, Iblīs would refuse and display arrogance.

Allah fashioned Ādam with both His hands and created him from clay, to which He refers:

> ⟪quran 38:75⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓإِبْلِيسُ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَن تَسْجُدَ لِمَا خَلَقْتُ بِيَدَىَّ ۖ أَسْتَكْبَرْتَ أَمْ كُنتَ مِنَ ٱلْعَالِينَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Iblīs! What prevented you from prostrating before that which I created with My own hands? Were you too proud, or are you among the exalted?"*

The two hands denote the two proofs (*ḥujjatayn*) of the *daʿwa* of Imam al-Hunaid (peace be upon him), which he appointed for Ādam's outward and inward education. The significance of creating him from clay lies in this: the Imam began Ādam's education with the outward sciences, teaching him the foundational disciplines, and gradually perfected his learning over years. None save Allah knows the extent of progress Ādam attained in the esoteric sciences during this period.[^cite-19]

The *ḥadīth* expresses it thus: When Allah shaped Ādam's form from clay, His body was left lying for a long time before receiving the inspiration of inner spiritual knowledge. That is, before receiving the support of esoteric wisdom, he spent years studying the outward sciences alone. Satan would come and pass through the dried, hollow body of clay, and because the body was empty within, it would make a sound. Satan would exult, crying: "It is empty! It is hollow!" In other words, during Ādam's studies, Satan would approach him and pose questions about the highest truths. When Ādam could not answer these questions and spoke only from the light of external knowledge, Satan would rejoice, thinking: "Ādam possesses no knowledge of the inner dimensions—it is as if the inward realm were void and empty."

In this manner, Ādam's education and training continued for many years until he reached perfection and became worthy of the caliphate.

> ⟪quran 2:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَعَلَّمَ آدَمَ ٱلْأَسْمَآءَ كُلَّهَا ثُمَّ عَرَضَهُمْ عَلَى ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ فَقَالَ أَنۢبِـُٔونِى بِأَسْمَآءِ هَٓؤُلَآءِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *And Allah taught Ādam all the names, then He presented them to the angels and said: "Tell Me the names of these, if you are truthful."*

Then Allah Himself taught Ādam all the names—whereas the others were taught through various means. By ⟪ar:'أسمآء'⟫ *asmāʾ* (names) is meant ⟪ar:'معرفت'⟫ *maʿrifat* (knowledge), for names identify and reveal the essences of things. In other words, Imam al-Hunaid introduced Ādam to the senior angels ⟪ar:ملائکہ علیین⟫ (*Malāʾikat al-ʿIlliyīn*, the Angels of the Highest Assembly)—those who possessed ranks higher than Ādam and surpassed even the limits who were being sworn to obey the law of Ādam. Ādam alone was granted knowledge of these names, for he was destined to be elevated to the rank of prophecy and prophethood. Then, when the Imam posed the question about these names, the angels said:

> ⟪quran 2:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالُوا۟ سُبْحَنَكَ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَآ إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *They said: "Glory be to You! We have no knowledge except what You have taught us. Verily, You alone are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise."*

Since these limits had not attained the rank of prophecy, the knowledge of these exalted names was concealed from them—a knowledge through which alone their ranks could be elevated. All the limits openly confessed their ignorance and acknowledged the knowledge and wisdom of Imam al-Hunaid (peace be upon him).[^cite-20]

> ⟪quran 2:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Ādam, inform them of their names." And when Ādam had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the unseen of the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you conceal?"*

Then Imam al-Hunaid commanded Ādam to inform the angels of these names. When Ādam made known to them all these great designations, they became his students and disciples, for there is always precedence of the knower over the ignorant. The intellectual superiority and excellence of Hazrat ʿAbdullāh—his mastery—over all of them stood revealed. Imam al-Hunaid declared: "Did I not tell you that I know the unseen of the heavens and the earth? That is, I know all the capacities of the angels and of humankind, and I am aware of both their outward conduct and inward intentions. That is to say: you supposed that Ādam was incapable of understanding the sciences of prophecy and spiritual realities, yet behold—he has proven more capable of comprehension than all of you!"[^cite-21]

## Exegesis of 'I Know What You Reveal and What You Conceal' (Q2:33)

⟪quran 2:33⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسّ َمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *He said: "O Adam! Inform them of their names," and when he had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the Ghaib (unseen) in the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you have been concealing?"*

In interpreting the divine utterance "I know what you reveal and what you conceal," Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq reports from his father, Maulana Muhammad al-Baqir, peace be upon them both, that the angels' utterance—their proclamation that they alone sufficed for the praise of the Divine—constituted what was made manifest. What remained concealed, by contrast, was their inner thought: how could the Almighty create a being superior to themselves and command them to prostrate before him?[^cite-4]

According to another interpretive account, when God shaped Adam's form from clay and positioned it along the path the angels traversed, they marvelled at the beauty of that figure—a loveliness such as they had never before witnessed. When Iblīs (the Devil) passed by, however, he was seized by a different curiosity. He examined the lifeless form with his hand and found it hollow within. Passing his hand through the mouth and withdrawing it again, he turned to his companion angels and declared: "This is a strange creature—utterly empty, lacking all substance! Should it ever receive dominion over you, what would you do?" All the angels replied, "We shall obey the command of our Lord." But Iblīs said in his heart: "If dominion over me is granted to this figure, I shall disobey it; and if I am granted dominion over it, I shall not be merciful to it."[^cite-5]

It was then that God spoke these words: "I know what you reveal and what you conceal"—meaning that the Almighty comprehended both the disobedience hidden in the angels' hearts and the obstinate defiance that Iblīs concealed within his breast. Nothing of their inner states escaped Divine knowledge, whether they brought it forth in speech or kept it locked away in silence.[^cite-6]

## The Angels' Seeking of Forgiveness

It is narrated that Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq performed the pilgrimage with his father, Maulana Muhammad al-Baqir. One night, as they circumambulated the House of God, kissed the Black Stone, and performed the ritual prayer, fatigue overtook him. He seated himself to rest while his father continued his devotions. Suddenly, a man appeared beside them—a figure of commanding stature, red-haired, clothed in two thick white garments. Sensing the newcomer's presence, his father shortened his prayer, returned the greeting, and asked, "Do you require something of me?" The man replied, "I have come to inquire of you concerning the origin of this sacred House."

Thereupon Maulana Muhammad al-Baqir narrated the entire history of Adam's creation and what followed. He then said:

⟪quran 2:33⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسّ َمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *He said: "O Adam! Inform them of their names," and when he had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the Ghaib (unseen) in the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you have been concealing?"*

When the angels confessed their failings, the Imam continued, God made known to them both what they had openly declared and what they harboured in secret—not merely their outward confession of sufficiency in His praise, but also their hidden thought that they could not comprehend how a being might arise superior to themselves. Once the Lord had laid bare the secrets of their hearts, the angels repented in sincere remorse. They ascended to the Divine Throne and circumambulated it seven times in their longing to win back God's pleasure. The Almighty was pleased with their penitence and their deeds, and He commanded them: "Descend to the earth and construct therein a house where the wayward children of Adam shall gather to circumambulate it. Through their circumambulation, I shall forgive them and be pleased with them as I am pleased with you."

The angels built that house. When the Flood of Noah swept across the earth, the structure was lifted to the fourth heaven, where the celestial beings circumambulate it until the Day of Judgment. Later, Abraham rebuilt the shrine upon its ancient foundations, and the Imam described at great length the circumstances of these ages and the station of that sacred dwelling.

## How Can Error Be Possible from the Angels?

When we speak of the innocence of the angels, a question naturally arises: how did they commit a mistake? In the account of Adam, the "angel of Paradise" refers to those companions of Imam al-Hunaid who dwelt in his presence. There are two classes of beings—the initiates (al-bāṭin) and the outer multitude (al-ẓāhir). Similarly, Adam possessed an inner reality and an outer veil through which his station was expressed.

| Inner Essence | Outer Veil |
| --- | --- |
| Adam's Core Reality | Adam's Covering |
| The Hidden Dimension | The Boundary |

The limits of the inner circles stood in a rank more elevated than those of the outer, just as the last person in each spiritual circle surpasses the first person of the circle that follows. These inner companions were not placed under the law of Adam. The angels whom God commanded to prostrate before Adam belonged to a rank beneath his own, and thus error was possible for them; they had been set under his law. Similarly, the outer veil of Adam (a.s.) that operated among the exoteric servants could stumble, and stumbling brought punishment. Yet among those in the inner circle—the veiled ones who worked in concert with his essence—no error was possible. There was among them one who transgressed from ignorance, and that transgression brought upon them the consequence of their fault. This is why the outer veil of Adam experienced error, but his inner reality remained immaculate.

## Iblīs's Arrogance and Refusal

At that time, God commanded all the angels:

⟪quran 2:34⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قُلْنَا لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ ٱسْجُدُوا۟ لِآدَمَ فَسَجَدُوٓا۟ إِلَّآ إِبْلِيسَ أَبَىٰ وَٱسْتَكْبَرَ وَكَانَ مِنَ ٱلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *And (remember) when We said to the angels: "Prostrate yourselves before Adam.". And they prostrated except Iblîs (Satan), he refused and was proud and was one of the disbelievers (disobedient to Allah).*

"Prostrate yourselves before Adam," the Almighty declared to the heavenly host, "all of you. Obey him, establish covenant with him, join your limits to his, and learn from him."

The prostration itself carries deep meaning. In this sacred gesture, one places the highest point of the body—the head, seat of the rational faculty—upon the earth where the lowest part stands. Thus does prostration express the acknowledgement that one's most exalted qualities pale before the lowest station of another. Moreover, in prostration a servant places seven members upon the ground, signifying the surrender of one's entire being to the reality one serves.

All the angels heeded the command and fell prostrate, drawing near to the Imam in obedience and accepting the covenant of Adam—all except for one of the limits known as ⟪ar:'ابلیس'⟫ (the Veiler), who is also called ⟪ar:حارث بن مرۃ⟫ (Hārith ibn Murrah).

⟪quran 7:12⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَلَّا تَسْجُدَ إِذْ أَمَرْتُكَ ۖ قَالَ أَنَا۠ خَيْرٌۭ مِّنْهُ خَلَقْتَنِى مِن نَّارٍۢ وَخَلَقْتَهُۥ مِن طِينٍۢ ‎⟫
>
> *(Allah) said: "What prevented you (O Iblîs) that you did not prostrate, when I commanded you?" Iblîs said: "I am better than him (Adam), You created me from fire, and him You created from clay."*

Imam al-Hunaid questioned al-Hārith: "What prevented you from prostrating to Adam?" And he answered, "I am fashioned from fire, he from clay. I have been nurtured in the subtle and luminous sciences—truths bright as lightning, free from all material pollution—while you have taught Adam the outward, material, and corporeal sciences. I am the one who instructs all the angels in the knowledge of the Kingdom (⟪ar:معلم الملکوت⟫). How can you wish me to bow before this raw creation and place myself beneath the low?"

Iblīs refused and became one of the disbelievers (⟪ar:'کافر'⟫, al-kāfir—literally "the one who covers, the one who conceals"). He knew full well that Adam surpassed him in capacity, especially since the Almighty had demonstrated Adam's excellence before all the angels. Yet Iblīs deliberately veiled himself from that knowledge. He concealed the truth about Adam's station, becoming thereby one who covers truth—a disbeliever.

⟪quran 7:13⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ فَٱهْبِطْ مِنْهَا فَمَا يَكُونُ لَكَ أَن تَتَكَبَّرَ فِيهَا فَٱخْرُجْ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلصَّغِرِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *(Allah) said: "(O Iblîs) get down from this (Paradise), it is not for you to be arrogant here. Get out, for you are of those humiliated and disgraced."*

God expelled him from Paradise—from His presence and His invitation—because of his arrogance. Whoever is arrogant before the Divine Presence dwells in the palace of Iblīs, for greatness and majesty belong to God alone, and arrogance toward Him brings only shame.

**On the Etymology of Iblīs:** The name derives from the Arabic ⟪ar:'أبلس'⟫ (ablasa), meaning "to despair." ⟪ar:ابلیس⟫ (Iblīs)—Hārith ibn Murrah—harboured an original aspiration to prophethood itself. When he was stripped of that station and fell into despair of ever attaining the prophetic rank, he was called Iblīs, the "Despairing One." The name carries also the sense of one bereft of reason, for he was confounded by the depth of Adam's inner knowledge. It signifies further one cast out from mercy, since Imam al-Hunaid expelled him from the divine court. In the lexicon, ⟪ar:'لعن'⟫ (to curse, to banish) means to remove, to cast away.

## The Creation of Ḥawwāʾ (Eve) and the First Marriage

Just as Adam's birth signifies his spiritual ascent to prophethood, so too does his wife represent the spiritual maturation and succession of his authority. The physical woman was born to earthly parents as all humans are born; yet the Ḥawwāʾ mentioned in the Qurʾān pertains to the esoteric and hierarchical reality of Adam's heir.

⟪ar:'حوا'⟫ (Ḥawwāʾ, Eve) derives from the Arabic ⟪ar:'حی'⟫ (ḥayy), meaning "living" or "alive." The term also refers to one who encompasses or contains. The Qurʾān relates that when Adam assumed the station of prophethood and imamate, fear seized him. At that moment, Imam al-Hunaid commanded him to prepare his successor. The Qurʾān speaks in symbolic language: God broke one of Adam's ribs from his right side and from it created his wife.

This verse alludes to the reality that when Adam (a.s.) received the mantle of prophecy and leadership, Imam al-Hunaid (a.s.) instructed him to appoint his heir. Adam possessed twelve hujjaj (proofs, or appointed vicars) or niqāba (hidden guides), dwelling within the palace of his twelve ribs. These were called ribs because, just as the stability of the human frame depends upon the rib cage, so the stability of Adam's call rested upon his appointed limits. The twelve ribs on the right side represent the knowledge of the esoteric (bāṭin), while the twelve ribs on the left represent the knowledge of the exoteric (ẓāhir). Thus Adam had twelve inner vicars and twelve outer guides—the complete hierarchy of his invitation.

Among Adam's proofs was his son Hābīl (Abel), who served as the chief of his appointed guides. Although he was Adam's son, Abel possessed learning that surpassed even his father's. This is why they are referred to as Ḥawwāʾ—for they encompassed (ḥāwā) the inner knowledge. Imam al-Hunaid united Abel with Adam, so that whenever Adam faced difficulty in acquiring knowledge, he might turn to Abel for support and never encounter hardship alone.

In truth, Abel took the place of Iblīs. Previously, Iblīs had been destined to be one of Adam's limits and his chief summoner (dāʿī). But before the invitation even began, his apostasy became manifest. Since one of the twelve was thereby lost, the number was completed by including Abel. In other words, God gave Adam a substitute for the station that Iblīs had forfeited. And then God said:

⟪quran 2:35⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلْنَا يَٓـَٔادَمُ ٱسْكُنْ أَنتَ وَزَوْجُكَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَكُلَا مِنْهَا رَغَدًا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمَا وَلَا تَقْرَبَا هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةَ فَتَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *And We said: "O Adam! Dwell you and your wife in the Paradise and eat both of you freely with pleasure and delight of things therein as wherever you will, but come not near this tree or you both will be of the Zalimun (wrong-doers)."*

Imam al-Hunaid established Adam and his wife in Paradise—that state which is the station of prophecy itself, connected to revelation, pure, subtle, and real. No blessing surpasses this; no pleasure exceeds its sweetness. To one wholly absorbed in this knowledge, the world loses all value. The grandest treasures of earth fade into insignificance. Such a soul apprehends that this knowledge is the true blessing of God, a joy that never dims, a wealth that never dwindles, an honour that never tarnishes. All the stages of prayer, too, are called ⟪ar:'جنت'⟫ (garden, paradise).

In appointing Adam as prophet and Abel as his caliph, God granted them full latitude: "Do all that you wish—study, comprehend, and benefit from the sciences within your station. You shall not be questioned for this. Promulgate the rules of the lawful and unlawful; establish the ordinances of worship; discharge all the functions of prophecy." Yet there remained one boundary:

> **"Do not approach this tree, for you will be among the wrongdoers."**

Though I have permitted you all else, do not step beyond this limit. This ⟪ar:'شجرہ ممنوعۃ'⟫ (forbidden tree) was the boundary set for Adam and Abel. In other words: do not interfere in the work of the Qāʾim, and do not lay hand upon it. Your authority extends to the functions of prophecy alone. The disclosure and unveiling of inner mysteries is knowledge reserved to the Qāʾim al-Qiyāma (the Upholder of the Resurrection). Those hidden realities must not be revealed in the lesser cycles before their appointed manifestation. Only ⟪ar:قائم القیامۃ⟫ possesses the right to unveil, confirm, or reject them. ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫ and ⟪ar:مولانا صاحب القیامۃ⟫ alone hold dominion—they are ⟪ar:'مالک یوم الدین'⟫ (Masters of the Day of Judgment). After the Qāʾim's manifestation, belief shall profit none. In his hands shall the hidden knowledge be disclosed. Whoever reveals before the appointed time becomes a wrongdoer (⟪ar:فَتَکُونَا مِنَ الظَّالِمِینَ⟫). Therefore did Imam al-Hunaid forbid them at the outset of the cycles, preserving thereby the Sunnah and nature of God's order.

## Iblīs's Challenge to God

After his curse and expulsion, the devil entreated respite until a time known to God (⟪ar:'یوم وقت معلوم'⟫), and requested:

⟪quran 7:14-15⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ أَنظِرْنِىٓ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ ‎ ‏قَالَ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنظَرِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *(Iblîs) said: "Allow me respite till the Day they are raised up (i.e. the Day of Resurrection)." (Allah) said: "You are of those allowed respite."*

The expression ⟪ar:'یوم یبعثون'⟫ (the Day they are raised) refers to ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫ (our Master, the Qāʾim of the Resurrection).

⟪quran 7:16-17⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ فَبِمَآ أَغْوَيْتَنِى لَأَقْعُدَنَّ لَهُمْ صِرَطَكَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ ‎ ‏ثُمَّ لَآتِيَنَّهُم مِّنۢ بَيْنِ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ وَعَنْ أَيْمَنِهِمْ وَعَن شَمَآئِلِهِمْ ۖ وَلَا تَجِدُ أَكْثَرَهُمْ شَكِرِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *(Iblîs) said: "Because You have sent me astray, surely I will sit in wait against them (human beings) on Your Straight Path. Then I will come to them from before them and behind them, from their right and from their left, and You will not find most of them as thankful ones (i.e. they will not be dutiful to You)."*

Hārith ibn Murrah said to Imam al-Hunaid (a.s.): "Just as you have led me astray—barred me from advancing in the ranks of prophecy—so I shall destroy the descendants of Adam born until the reappearance of the Qāʾim, save only a few. I shall assail them from all quarters with my forces, and you shall see most of them fall prey to my stratagems. I shall attack them on the ⟪ar:صراط مستقیم⟫ (Straight Path) itself."

⟪quran 17:62⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ أَرَءَيْتَكَ هَذَا ٱلَّذِى كَرَّمْتَ عَلَىَّ لَىِٕنْ أَخَّرْتَنِ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْقِيَمَةِ لَأَحْتَنِكَنَّ ذُرِّيَّتَهُۥٓ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
>
> *[Iblîs (Satan)] said: "See? This one whom You have honoured above me, if You give me respite (keep me alive) to the Day of Resurrection, I will surely seize and mislead his offspring (by sending them astray) all but a few!"*

Iblīs levelled a direct challenge at Imam al-Hunaid (a.s.): because he had fallen from his exalted station on account of Adam (a.s.), he would prevent the children of Adam from ever attaining his rank.

⟪quran 4:119-120⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَلَأُضِلَّنَّهُمْ وَلَأُمَنِّيَنَّهُمْ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُبَتِّكُنَّ آذَانَ ٱلْأَنْعَمِ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُغَيِّرُنَّ خَلْقَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَمَن يَتَّخِذِ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ وَلِيًّۭا مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ فَقَدْ خَسِرَ خُسْرَانًۭا مُّبِينًۭا ‎ ‏يَعِدُهُمْ وَيُمَنِّيهِمْ ۖ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎⟫
>
> *Verily, I will mislead them, and surely, I will arouse in them false desires; and certainly, I will order them to slit the ears of cattle, and indeed I will order them to change the nature created by Allah." And whoever takes Shaitan (Satan) as a Walî (protector or helper) instead of Allah, has surely suffered a manifest loss. He [Shaitan (Satan)] makes promises to them, and arouses in them false desires; and Shaitan's (Satan) promises are nothing but deceptions.*

He declared: "I shall establish my dominion against yours until the Qāʾim appears. I shall set my call in opposition to your call. I shall deceive your servants in manifold ways—sometimes through false hopes, sometimes by making them despise and slay your saints. You shall see that most of your servants fall into my net." The Imam accepted Iblīs's challenge and responded:

⟪quran 7:18⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱخْرُجْ مِنْهَا مَذْءُومًۭا مَّدْحُورًۭا ۖ لَّمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ لَأَمْلَأَنَّ جَهَنَّمَ مِنكُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *(Allah) said (to Iblîs) "Get out from this (Paradise) disgraced and expelled. Whoever of them (mankind) will follow you, then surely I will fill Hell with you all."*

⟪quran 17:63-65⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱذْهَبْ فَمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ فَإِنَّ جَهَنَّمَ جَزَآؤُكُمْ جَزَآءًۭ مَّوْفُورًۭا ‎ ‏وَٱسْتَفْزِزْ مَنِ ٱسْتَطَعْتَ مِنْهُم بِصَوْتِكَ وَأَجْلِبْ عَلَيْهِم بِخَيْلِكَ وَرَجِلِكَ وَشَارِكْهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَمْوَلِ وَٱلْأَوْلَدِ وَعِدْهُمْ ۚ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎ ‏إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَنٌۭ ۚ وَكَفَىٰ بِرَبِّكَ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫
>
> *(Allah) said: "Go, and whosoever of them follows you, surely! Hell will be the recompense of you (all) an ample recompense. "And Istafziz [literally means: befool them gradually] those whom you can among them with your voice (i.e. songs, music, and any other call for Allah's disobedience), make assaults on them with your cavalry and your infantry, mutually share with them wealth and children (by tempting them to earn money by illegal ways usury, etc., or by committing illegal sexual intercourse, etc.), and make promises to them." But Satan promises them nothing but deceit. "Verily! My slaves (i.e the true believers of Islamic Monotheism), you have no authority over them. And All-Sufficient is your Lord as a Guardian."*

"Depart," the Imam commanded ⟪ar:امام ھنید علیہ السلام⟫, "and know this: whosoever follows you shall be your companion in punishment, fully recompensed in the Fire. Seduce with your voice whomsoever you can—those susceptible to rhetoric, to eloquence, to beguiling speech. Rally against them your cavalry and your infantry; enlist the aid of those who follow you. Seek to influence their wealth, which is to say their knowledge—poison their hearts with your words, sowing doubt in their understanding. And 'children' means the believers themselves—divert them from love of the true Imams. Whomever you overcome shall be yours, removed from the community of faith. Deceive them with your false promises. But remember: you shall never gain power over My sincere servants!"

God also speaks of this encounter in another passage:

⟪quran 15:36-42⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ رَبِّ فَأَنظِرْنِىٓ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ ‎ ‏قَالَ فَإِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنظَرِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْوَقْتِ ٱلْمَعْلُومِ ‎ ‏قَالَ رَبِّ بِمَآ أَغْوَيْتَنِى لَأُزَيِّنَنَّ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَأُغْوِيَنَّهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَّا عِبَادَكَ مِنْهُمُ ٱلْمُخْلَصِينَ ‎ ‏قَالَ هَذَا صِرَطٌ عَلَىَّ مُسْتَقِيمٌ ‎ ‏إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَنٌ إِلَّا مَنِ ٱتَّبَعَكَ مِنَ ٱلْغَاوِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *[Iblîs (Satan)] said: "O my Lord! Give me then respite till the Day they (the dead) will be resurrected." Allah said: "Then, verily, you are of those reprieved, "Till the Day of the time appointed." [Iblîs (Satan)] said: "O my Lord! Because you misled me, I shall indeed adorn the path of error for them (mankind) on the earth, and I shall mislead them all. "Except Your chosen, (guided) slaves among them." (Allah) said: "This is the Way which will lead straight to Me." "Certainly, you shall have no authority over My slaves, except those who follow you of the Ghawîn (Mushrikun and those who go astray, criminals, polytheists, and evil-doers, etc.)."*

Thus did God grant Iblīs respite until the Day of Resurrection, testing thereby the faithful. Whosoever fell into his snare proved himself no true believer in Adam's line, while those saved from his deception became sincere believers. Yet the Almighty declared further: even if Satan seduced a soul and led it astray, should that same soul recognize Satan's whispers before falling entirely into the trap, should it grieve for its error and sincerely repent, seeking forgiveness with complete heart—then it would not belong to Satan's faction but rather to the party of God. Thus did two calls go forth side by side—the merciful call and the satanic call—dividing the descendants of Adam into two communities: believers and those who followed the devils in hypocrisy.

The true believers, those who followed Adam, were called God's servants (ʿibād Allāh), those over whom Satan could not prevail despite all his stratagems. As God declares:

⟪quran 58:19⟫

> ⟪ar:‏ٱسْتَحْوَذَ عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَأَنسَىٰهُمْ ذِكْرَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ هُمُ ٱلْخَسِرُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *Shaitan (Satan) has overtaken them. So he has made them forget the remembrance of Allah. They are the party of Shaitan (Satan). Verily, it is the party of Shaitan (Satan) that will be the losers!*

In Surah Ibrāhīm, God records that Iblīs himself, on the Day of Judgment, shall address those who followed him:

⟪quran 14:22⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لَمَّا قُضِىَ ٱلْأَمْرُ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ وَعَدَكُمْ وَعْدَ ٱلْحَقِّ وَوَعَدتُّكُمْ فَأَخْلَفْتُكُمْ ۖ وَمَا كَانَ لِىَ عَلَيْكُم مِّن سُلْطَنٍ إِلَّآ أَن دَعَوْتُكُمْ فَٱسْتَجَبْتُمْ لِى ۖ فَلَا تَلُومُونِى وَلُومُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَكُم ۖ مَّآ أَنَا۠ بِمُصْرِخِكُمْ وَمَآ أَنتُم بِمُصْرِخِىَّ ۖ إِنِّى كَفَرْتُ بِمَآ أَشْرَكْتُمُونِ مِن قَبْلُ ۗ إِنَّ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
>
> *And Shaitan (Satan) will say when the matter has been decided: "Verily, Allah promised you a promise of truth. And I too promised you, but I betrayed you. I had no authority over you except that I called you, so you responded to me. So blame me not, but blame yourselves. I cannot help you, nor can you help me. I deny your former act in associating me (Satan) as a partner with Allah (by obeying me in the life of the world). Verily, there is a painful torment for the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers, etc.)."*

By contrast, the faithful—those who do not befriend the enemies of God and His Messenger, be they fathers, sons, brothers, or kinfolk—are described thus:

⟪quran 58:22⟫

> ⟪ar:‏لَّا تَجِدُ قَوْمًۭا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ يُوَآدُّونَ مَنْ حَآدَّ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ وَلَوْ كَانُوٓا۟ آبَآءَهُمْ أَوْ أَبْنَآءَهُمْ أَوْ إِخْوَنَهُمْ أَوْ عَشِيرَتَهُمْ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَتَبَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمُ ٱلْإِيمَنَ وَأَيَّدَهُم بِرُوحٍۢ مِّنْهُ ۖ وَيُدْخِلُهُمْ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَرُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ رَضِىَ ٱللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا۟ عَنْهُ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱللَّهِ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *You (O Muhammad PBUH) will not find any people who believe in Allah and the Last Day, making friendship with those who oppose Allah and His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), even though they were their fathers, or their sons, or their brothers, or their kindred (people). For such He has written Faith in their hearts, and strengthened them with Ruh (proofs, light and true guidance) from Himself. And We will admit them to Gardens (Paradise) under which rivers flow, to dwell therein (forever). Allah is pleased with them, and they with Him. They are the Party of Allah. Verily, it is the Party of Allah that will be the successful.*

God also reveals that for every prophet, enemies arise from among both jinn and humankind:

⟪quran 6:112⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *And so We have appointed for every Prophet enemies - Shayatin (devils) among mankind and jinns, inspiring one another with adorned speech as a delusion (or by way of deception). If your Lord had so willed, they would not have done it, so leave them alone with their fabrications.*

Iblīs's enmity toward Adam became eternal and passed down through generations. Adam received forgiveness through sincere repentance; Iblīs, by contrast, was cursed for arrogance and banished from the divine presence, his spirit ablaze with vengeance. He persecuted every prophet, every messenger, every executor (wasi), and every Imam, employing every stratagem to weaken them.

Those who follow the devil in his refusal and arrogance bear guilt. Those who, after stumbling into sin, recognize Satan's whisper before falling wholly into his snare and repent with sincerity, seeking forgiveness—these become not Satan's faction but God's. Yet the one who denies truth stubbornly, who carries arrogance in the manner of Satan and resists obstinately, is himself a supreme Iblīs and stands guilty of enmity toward the Prophet.

In sum: Iblīs refused and retreated in arrogance, thus falling. Those of like mind obeyed and followed him—wicked and ancient souls. The test grew severe for the community of the invitation on all sides. First the inner hierarchy suffered tribulation, then the outer veil. The inquiry grew intense, and the inner companions disappeared entirely from the world's knowledge, lost to history. But the condition of the outer realm differed: sometimes they emerged in view, sometimes they were forced into hiding.

## Ādam's Being Deceived (Eating from the Forbidden Tree)

God relates:

⟪quran 7:20⟫

> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَالِدِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered suggestions to them both in order to uncover that which was hidden from them of their private parts (before); he said: "Your Lord did not forbid you this tree save you should become angels or become of the immortals."*

It is related in Hadith that Hārith ibn Murrah entered Paradise in the form of a serpent. The serpent, outwardly docile and benign, harbours within it deadly venom. Thus did Hārith ibn Murrah adopt gentleness toward Adam and Eve, feigning to be their friend while concealing enmity. He knew that Adam could not be swayed by direct entreaty, so he turned his seduction toward the woman. He said to her: "The Imam has forbidden you the highest knowledge lest you attain the rank of all his other limits and become worthy of standing beside him. He wishes that you remain forever beneath him, never equal to him. The Imam does not grant you that eternity which is your rightful station."

He further explained: "Bodily labours belong to the inferior realms. If you cease from them, you shall ascend to the rank of the ⟪ar:'ملائکہ علیین'⟫ (elevated angels). You shall attain ⟪ar:علم روحانی لطیف⟫ (subtle spiritual knowledge), freed forever from the heavy burden of bodily worship and toil. Becoming ⟪ar:مَلَک⟫ (an angel), you shall fulfill every desire, dwelling eternally in Paradise's bounty."

Then Iblīs swore a false oath before them both, declaring:

⟪quran 7:21⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَقَاسَمَهُمَآ إِنِّى لَكُمَا لَمِنَ ٱلنَّصِحِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *And he [Shaitan (Satan)] swore by Allah to them both (saying): "Verily, I am one of the sincere well-wishers for you both."*

"I swear to you truly, I am your sincere well-wisher."

Maulana Ali explains that Adam was deceived because of Satan's oath. Falsehood had no place in the intellectual realm of Adam and Eve; they possessed only knowledge of truth, and no trace of deception dwelt in their imagination. When Satan swore to them, they believed him, for the taking of a false oath was inconceivable in their consciousness.

⟪quran 7:22⟫

> ⟪ar:‏فَدَلَّىٰهُمَا بِغُرُورٍۢ ۚ فَلَمَّا ذَاقَا ٱلشَّجَرَةَ بَدَتْ لَهُمَا سَوْءَتُهُمَا وَطَفِقَا يَخْصِفَانِ عَلَيْهِمَا مِن وَرَقِ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ وَنَادَىٰهُمَا رَبُّهُمَآ أَلَمْ أَنْهَكُمَا عَن تِلْكُمَا ٱلشَّجَرَةِ وَأَقُل لَّكُمَآ إِنَّ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ لَكُمَا عَدُوٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
>
> *So he misled them with deception. Then when they tasted of the tree, that which was hidden from them of their shame (private parts) became manifest to them and they began to stick together the leaves of Paradise over themselves (in order to cover their shame). And their Lord called out to them (saying): "Did I not forbid you that tree and tell you: Verily, Shaitan (Satan) is an open enemy unto you?"*

At Eve's urging, Adam tasted the fruit of the forbidden tree—that is, he overstepped the prescribed limits of prophetic function. He ventured into the work meant for the Qāʾim (the Upholder) and extended his hand where he had been forbidden. The moment he opened that knowledge and comprehended it, their shame became revealed to them. The sciences they had glimpsed were so far beyond their capacity that they could not grasp them. Both immediately recognized their weakness and inadequacy. "We deemed ourselves wise," they lamented, "yet in these sciences we find ourselves utterly deficient."

The wrath and displeasure of Imam al-Hunaid (a.s.) descended upon them. The Imam withdrew his support and revelation, humiliated them, and removed from them the garment of piety. The luminous cloak of revelation had hidden the imperfections of their physical form and material density from others' sight. That radiant mantle had illuminated their darkness, making them—like the sun itself—brilliant and clear, enabling them to understand all sciences with ease and to expound them to others. But the moment that support was withdrawn, even the lesser sciences escaped their comprehension, and they could no longer teach others. Now every tongue was loosed to criticize them. Such is the fate of one who attains high rank and then falls from it: humiliation follows swiftly, and the people's censure becomes unrelenting.

Adam and Eve attempted to cover themselves with the leaves of Paradise—that is, to seek lesser knowledge from those inferior to them in station. But none would share with them. Only the fig tree, moved by compassion, offered its leaves to conceal them. God took pleasure in this mercy and united in blessing the inward and outward of the fig.

It is recorded in the Torah that when Adam and Eve walked naked through Paradise in their distress, they heard the footsteps of God in the garden and hid themselves in shame. The voice called out: "Where are you?" They answered: "We are naked, and when we heard your footsteps, we hid in shame." The Lord responded: "Alas! You have eaten from the tree." Adam said, "The woman gave it to me." The woman said, "The serpent deceived me." And the serpent said, "Iblīs entered into my belly."

The Imam said to them: "This is why I forbade you those sciences—you did not yet possess the capacity to understand them. My prohibition was not born of reluctance to grant you these studies. But I told you that Satan was your enemy, and you obeyed him while disobeying me."

**A Teaching:** The great Ḥazrat ʿAlī used to say: Every parent desires the finest nourishment for their children. Yet a newborn receives only milk, for the parent knows the child cannot yet digest richer sustenance. As the child grows stronger, the parent immediately provides the most excellent foods. Such restraint in feeding serves the child's benefit and growth.

Imam Ja'far al-Ṣādiq teaches concerning the steadfastness of the believer: "Not one of our saints walks with both feet always firm. If one foot slips, the other stands resolute. A believer may stumble into error, but error does not carry him outside the bounds of faith. The moment he commits a transgression, he repents and seeks forgiveness." According to this principle, Satan beguiled Adam the Chosen (Ṣafī Allāh), yet could not make him his own. Adam fell into Satan's deception but, recognizing his error at once, turned away in regret and begged forgiveness from his Lord.

## Ādam's Expulsion from the Garden

When we examine the narrative of Ādam's expulsion from Paradise with careful attention, numerous questions and apparent contradictions emerge. The first concerns the nature of the forbidden tree itself: if the tree's fruit possessed inherent goodness and lawfulness, why did the Originator, Most High forbid Ādam to consume it? Conversely, if the tree stood under divine prohibition, how could it exist within Paradise at all—that realm wherein all things were made lawful to Ādam? When the Almighty rendered all Paradise's provisions licit for him, why introduce a singular exception? Would it not have been simpler for the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High) to refrain from cultivating this tree altogether, especially given His absolute knowledge of the unseen, which foresaw that this very tree would become the source of Ādam's trial and suffering? Some exegetes of the Ẓāhir (outer meaning) describe the tree as ⟪ar:'گیہوں'⟫, a grain-bearing plant. Yet if this identification is correct, we face another difficulty: why was such a tree forbidden to Ādam when it remains lawful for his descendants—believers and disbelievers alike?

To this query, certain scholars have offered an explanation rooted in Ādam's physiology. They maintain that Ādam, lacking an intestinal system in his body, was denied the fruit precisely because the ⟪ar:قوت ھاضمۃ⟫ (digestive power) required to process it did not exist in him; consumption would have caused the fruit to putrefy within his stomach, producing pain.[^cite-7] Yet this response proves inadequate. If such reasoning were sound, why were the remaining fruits of Paradise permitted to him? Would not all fruits, entering his interior, have occasioned the same corruption? A further objection arises: Why should Ādam's descendants bear the penalty for their father's transgression? If the error was committed by him alone, not by his posterity, on what justice does punishment fall upon those who committed no offense? When some scholars counter that the Almighty foreknew His own knowledge that Ādam's descendants would commit the same transgression, and therefore withheld them from Paradise, the question recurses: Did not Allah, in His infinite knowledge of the unseen, likewise foreknow that Ādam himself would err? If He possessed such knowledge, why create him in Paradise at all? Would He not have created him in the terrestrial world, as He did his children?

To all such difficulties, the author of this compendium offers two categories of response—one pertaining to Ādam of this temporal world, the other to Ādam of the eternal realm.

### Ādam of the Temporal World

Drawing upon the knowledge transmitted by the Imāms, the author teaches that this tree admits of two dimensions of interpretation: one benevolent, the other malevolent.

**The Tree in Its Noble Aspect:**

When the devil whispered to Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ, he spoke thus:

> ⟪quran 7:20⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَلِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered suggestions to them both in order to uncover that which was hidden from them of their private parts (before); he said: "Your Lord did not forbid you this tree save you should become angels or become of the immortals."*

In its sublime sense, the tree signifies ⟪ar:'علوم حقائق'⟫ (the sciences of reality and truth)—those very sciences which lead toward eternality and which liberate the soul from the constraints of the mortal condition. Yet it is impossible to acquire and comprehend such sciences save through perfect obedience to the Imāms and sincere attachment to their wilāyah (guardianship). These sciences are unveiled to those worthy of them at the appointed hour, according to divine wisdom.[^cite-8] In an even loftier sense, they represent the knowledge belonging to the ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ (the Qāʾim, the Riser, upon whom be peace), and the ⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ (the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib)—the esoteric truths pertaining to their station, hidden from the sight of the multitude and forbidden to Ādam because they belonged to the sciences of the Kashf (unveiling), which could not be disclosed during the period of concealment.

It was through Iblīs's deception that he usurped the standing of Ādam's successor, Hābīl (Abel), thereby arousing the envy of Qābīl (Cain), his brother. This envy led to fratricide—an act accomplished first in the inward realm of intention, then outwardly in deed.

**The Tree in Its Malevolent Aspect:**

In another sense, the ⟪ar:'درخت'⟫ (tree) itself embodied Iblīs. The answer to why such a tree could inhabit Paradise lies in the fact that Iblīs was himself a sublime teacher—⟪ar:'معلم الملکوت'⟫ (the instructor of the celestial realm)—instructing all the angels in the knowledge of the divine kingdom. Yet when he refused to bow before Ādam, he was expelled from that station. In this interpretation, the prohibition against eating the tree's fruit signifies Ādam's interdiction against disclosing to Iblīs any secret of the daʿwa (the invitation to the divine path), for Iblīs had become accursed through his disobedience.

**Why Were Ādam's Descendants Deprived of Paradise?**

Responding to this objection, the author explains that Paradise—the ⟪ar-quote:دار الدعوۃ⟫ (house of the invitation)—serves as a locus for attaining the true, eternal Paradise. Ādam's Paradise constituted a station of lofty rank for him, representing the ⟪ar:دار الدعوۃ⟫. Every elevated degree is a paradise relative to the station beneath it, just as every lower rank constitutes the earthly realm relative to the station above. The ḥadd ʿālī (upper limit) of these stations is the true Heaven, and the ḥadd dānī (lower limit) is the mortal world. Ādam was created in an exalted position—the rank of ⟪ar:'تائیدات'⟫ (divine supports), which itself constituted his Paradise. From that station, he was diminished in rank through ⟪ar:تائیدات⟫ (the supports), descending until he reached the rank of Istijābah (responsiveness), the lowest of the celestial degrees, though not excluded from the hierarchy itself.

The rank of ⟪ar:'تعلیمات '⟫ (instruction) belongs to those who bear the responsibility of educating those beneath them, obliging them to obey the Sharīʿah's commandments and elevating them progressively through the ranks toward Paradise. The sciences of the bāṭin (esoteric knowledge) are not opened to the Mustajīb (the respondent) at his rank. For him, only obedience to the explicit commandments of the Law is required. The Mustajīb must submit to the authority above him without hesitation, obeying the commands of the Sharīʿah whether he comprehends the inner meanings of his superior's words or not. When the mufiyd (the beneficial teacher) observes that this Mustajīb has observed the Sharīʿah's commands with rigorous fidelity, the teacher elevates him to a rank where ⟪ar:علم باطن⟫ (esoteric knowledge) is disclosed to him. The descendants of the benefactor become themselves benefactors.

Ādam's descendants are the offspring of those ⟪ar:مطبعین⟫ (who possess the seal of this rank), all of whom belong to the degree of Mustajībīn, whose rank is the terrestrial world. And as stated earlier, the Mustajībūn do not dwell in Paradise; the earth is their appointed abode. This is why Ādam's children were not created in Paradise, but in this earthly realm.

**Clarifications Concerning Prophetic Immunity:**

It is established in the sciences of taʾwīl that a prophet cannot be a wrongdoer—a ẓālim (transgressor). Outwardly, Ādam committed an error, and the wrongdoer is indeed a wrongdoer; and the habitation of the ẓālim is the Fire. By this reasoning, Ādam could not be a prophet. Yet the esoteric truth differs profoundly. Ādam possessed a lower veiling—a ḥijāb dānī—on which this retribution was decreed to fall. His upper veiling—his ḥijāb ʿālī—remained among the sanctified. In every epoch there appears an Ādam, and that Ādam errs within the lower veil; yet his higher nature remains immaculate among the purified.[^cite-9]

### Ādam of the Eternal Realm (the Second Emanation)

The second exposition concerns the world of eternity. It refers to Ādam who was created as an intellect—specifically as the ⟪ar:'منبعث ثانی'⟫ (the second emanation). His paradise was a spiritual realm wherein he dwelt with all the other intellects. The tree represented the station of the Munbaʿith al-Awwal (the First Emanator), whose rank and equality were forbidden to the second emanation, for the first exceeds the second by virtue of its nobility and priority. The devil embodied his own wicked thought—a satanic supposition of rank-equality that incited him toward rebellion, seeking parity with the first emanation. God, his Creator, forbade him this equality, commanding him to abandon the notion of rank-parity.

The duty of the second emanation was to obey the first just as the first obeyed its predecessor, the al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect). His transgression—eating the tree's fruit—was his rebellion and his aspiration toward equality of rank with the first emanation. For this sin, he was cast down from his station, falling below the seven intellects, though he had excelled in monotheistic devotion. These seven utterances are in fact the divine words—the kalimāt of the First Emanator—which the Qur'ān references thus:

> ⟪quran 2:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَتٍۢ فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Then Adam received from his Lord Words. And his Lord pardoned him (accepted his repentance). Verily, He is the One Who forgives (accepts repentance), the Most Merciful.*

Through these seven words (utterances), Ādam repented to his Lord—the first Munbaʿith—and his repentance was accepted, restoring him to Paradise (the ranks of intellect). His descendants are those who imitated him in the intelligible world and sought rank-equality with him. All of them, together with the devil-spirit, were expelled from the intelligible realm into the physical world. Iblīs, through his obstinacy, was never readmitted to Heaven; he was cursed eternally.[^cite-10]

Iblīs's rancor toward Ādam and his descendants—those who repent—manifested itself as opposition and stubborn refusal. Ādam's descendants who dwelt in the first world of eternity were elevated and restored to Paradise, mirroring their father Ādam. But those who obeyed Iblīs were cast into Hellfire alongside their leader—and this hell is their true realm.

## Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ's Repentance

As soon as the weight of divine reproval descended upon them both, they recognized their transgression. In that very instant, both repented to Allāh and spoke these words:

> ⟪quran 7:23⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَا رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَآ أَنفُسَنَا وَإِن لَّمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ ٱلْخَسِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *They said: "Our Lord! We have wronged ourselves. If You forgive us not, and bestow not upon us Your Mercy, we shall certainly be of the losers."*

They said: "Our Lord, we have transgressed against ourselves beyond measure and disobeyed You. If You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we shall certainly be among the ruined." Neither Ādam nor Ḥawwāʾ persisted in their transgression; both confessed their sin candidly and besought forgiveness and repentance from their Lord.

**A Distinction Clarified:**

Here appears the crucial distinction between Iblīs and Ādam. Both erred—yet Iblīs adhered stubbornly to his transgression and refused all repentance, whereas Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ, perceiving their fault immediately, turned in penitence and sought forgiveness from Allāh.

The Imām then addressed them:

> ⟪quran 2:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَأَزَلَّهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ عَنْهَا فَأَخْرَجَهُمَا مِمَّا كَانَا فِيهِ ۖ وَقُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *Then the Shaitan (Satan) made them slip therefrom (the Paradise), and got them out from that in which they were. We said: "Get you down, all, with enmity between yourselves. On earth will be a dwelling place for you and an enjoyment for a time."*

> ⟪quran 7:24-25⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎ ‏قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "Get down, one of you an enemy to the other [i.e. Adam, Hawwa (Eve), and Shaitan (Satan), etc.]. On earth will be a dwelling-place for you and an enjoyment, - for a time." He said: "Therein you shall live, and therein you shall die, and from it you shall be brought out (i.e. resurrected)."*

That is, the Imām al-Hunaid expelled them both from His direct presence—the Paradise of spiritual intimacy—and dispatched them to the terrestrial islands, severing them from His immediate support and direct communion. Within the Imāmic court, they had possessed supporting knowledge (taʾyīdāt) and required neither study nor exertion; whenever a question arose, the answer came instantaneously from the Imām. But in the terrestrial realm, a graduated system of pedagogical knowledge exists, requiring patient study and arduous labor for comprehension. This became their recompense for disobedience. The Imām thus addressed them: "We granted you supreme support, which you did not treasure but coveted. Now descend to the terrestrial islands and labor; ascend step by step so that you comprehend how blessed was your former state. Taste now the bitter so that you understand the value of the sweet!"

> ⟪quran 2:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَتٍۢ فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Then Adam received from his Lord Words. And his Lord pardoned him (accepted his repentance). Verily, He is the One Who forgives (accepts repentance), the Most Merciful.*

As soon as the Imām pronounced judgment, both learned through sincere repentance—through the ⟪ar:'ملائکہ علیین'⟫ (the exalted angels), those celestial intelligences—the ⟪ar:کلمات⟫ (words, utterances) by means of which they might attain nearness to the Divine and reclaim the blessed names through which they could restore their lost paradise (the station of prophethood). According to the teaching of Ḥaḍrat Ẓiyāʾ Ṣāḥib, those words were none other than the ⟪ar:أسمآء مبارکۃ⟫ (blessed names) of the ⟪ar:محمد و علی و فاطمۃ و حسن و حسین علیھم السلام⟫ (Muhammad, ʿAlī, Fāṭimah, al-Ḥasan, and al-Ḥusayn, upon them be peace).[^cite-11] He drew forth from these supreme names the source of knowledge that he had read in those elevated sciences but could not comprehend. Through these holy names, he repented, saying to the Imām: "We beseech forgiveness from You through these blessed names!" The Imām accepted Ādam's repentance and forgave him. Indeed, the Imām al-Hunaid is the Ever-Accepting and the Ever-Merciful! Thus Ādam approached the Imām al-Hunaid once more through these utterances, and the Imām pardoned him, restoring him to the station of prophethood. Satan was vanquished, and the divine promise was fulfilled.[^cite-12]

This episode teaches us a vital lesson: when a believer errs and commits sin, repentance is not withheld; simultaneously, it conveys a glad tidings—that when a believer repents after transgression and seeks the aid of Allāh's saints, the Almighty forgives him immediately and does not exclude him from His party (Ḥizb Allāh). Conversely, a believer who sins yet does not deem it sinful, who does not seek forgiveness and does not turn to Allāh's saints, abandons Ḥizb Allāh and enters the camp of Iblīs, becoming a member of his faction. In other words, Allāh has established two conditions for the remission of sins. The first is sincere repentance and seeking forgiveness following transgression. The second is to seek the intercession and aid of the saints of Allāh. The mediation of the Messengers and the saints of Allāh in seeking forgiveness is of paramount importance, as the Almighty declares in Sūrat al-Nisāʾ:

> ⟪quran 4:64⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن رَّسُولٍ إِلَّا لِيُطَاعَ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَلَوْ أَنَّهُمْ إِذ ظَّلَمُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَهُمْ جَآءُوكَ فَٱسْتَغْفَرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَٱسْتَغْفَرَ لَهُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ لَوَجَدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابًۭا رَّحِيمًۭا ‎⟫
> *We sent no Messenger, but to be obeyed by Allah's Leave. If they (hypocrites), when they had been unjust to themselves, had come to you (Muhammad PBUH) and begged Allah's Forgiveness, and the Messenger had begged forgiveness for them: indeed, they would have found Allah All-Forgiving (One Who accepts repentance), Most Merciful.*

## The Descent from the Garden to the Earth

Allāh forgave both of them and restored them to the stations of Khalīfah (caliph) and Imāmate, yet He deprived them of His direct support and immediate communion. He removed them from His court and the presence of the Imāms, sending them to the terrestrial islands; henceforth their orders came through intermediaries rather than directly. Previously, when dwelling in the Imāmic court, they had received these ⟪ar:تائیدات⟫ (divine supports) in direct transmission.

The Almighty declared:

> ⟪quran 2:38-39⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ مِنْهَا جَمِيعًۭا ۖ فَإِمَّا يَأْتِيَنَّكُم مِّنِّى هُدًۭى فَمَن تَبِعَ هُدَاىَ فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ وَكَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَتِنَآ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *We said: "Get down all of you from this place (the Paradise), then whenever there comes to you Guidance from Me, and whoever follows My Guidance, there shall be no fear on them, nor shall they grieve. But those who disbelieve and belie Our Ayat (proofs, evidences, verses, lessons, signs, revelations, etc.) such are the dwellers of the Fire, they shall abide therein forever.*

Thus the Almighty decreed that not only Iblīs but Ādam, Ḥawwāʾ, and their descendants were severed from the nobles and dispatched to the terrestrial realm. The Imām made manifest that against your daʿwa (summons) extended to the inhabitants of earth, Iblīs shall establish his satanic summons and mislead your children. Yet I shall guide your children through you. Whoever accepts My invitation shall be returned to My Paradise; whoever accepts Satan's invitation shall dwell with him, and I shall imprison him in this terrestrial world eternally, never permitting him entry into My sanctuary.

> ⟪quran 7:24-25⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎ ‏قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "Get down, one of you an enemy to the other [i.e. Adam, Hawwa (Eve), and Shaitan (Satan), etc.]. On earth will be a dwelling-place for you and an enjoyment, - for a time." He said: "Therein you shall live, and therein you shall die, and from it you shall be brought out (i.e. resurrected)."*

The Imām proclaimed: "For you and your descendants, there shall be a place of abode and an enjoyment upon the earth for an appointed term." Then He said: "In this earth you shall live, you shall die in it, and from it you shall be brought forth—resurrected." Thus Ādam was diminished from the station of direct divine support, yet he was not altogether deprived of grace and blessing. A system of mediation was now established. The ⟪ar:حدود روحانیہ علویہ (کلمات)⟫ (the exalted spiritual limits, the utterances) became his intermediaries, and Ādam became the intermediary of the limits below him. This graduated transmission continued throughout creation. The Prophet, peace be upon him, testified: "I receive revelation from Gabriel; Gabriel receives from Michael; Michael receives from Israfīl; Israfīl from the Preserved Tablet; and the Tablet from the Pen." That is, revelation descends through five stations of descent; from the Nāṭiq (the Speaker, the Messenger), these sciences flow to his five delegated limits (ḥudūd), and thence to the believers.

| Celestial Limit | The Pen | The Preserved Tablet | Israfīl | The Speaker (Messiah) | The Intellect |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Terrestrial Limit** | The Foundation | The Imām | The Hujjah (Proof) | The Nāṭiq's Deputy | The Spiritual Guide |

The sixth in this chain is the one who receives and accepts.

Iblīs became the adversary of Ādam, and two rival summonses confronted one another—one calling toward truth through Ādam's daʿwa, the other toward falsehood through Satan's deception. Whoever maintains relationship with Ādam and his progeny is called a Walī Allāh (a friend of Allāh) and enters Ḥizb Allāh (the party of Allāh); whoever disbelieves Ādam, denies his line, and opposes him becomes an ally of Satan, for Satan has prevailed over such a one and effaced in him the remembrance of Allāh. The trials that befell Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ were replicated in their progeny, manifest in the same forms. Even unto this day, among their lineage there remains but one division: the male and the female. Likewise, there are but two classes of mankind: the obedient and the disobedient. Allāh promised to settle both upon this earth and to admit them into Paradise through repentance and forgiveness, even as He threatened the hypocrites and idolaters with punishment:

> ⟪quran 5:51⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ لَا تَتَّخِذُوا۟ ٱلْيَهُودَ وَٱلنَّصَرَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآءَ ۘ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَآءُ بَعْضٍۢ ۚ وَمَن يَتَوَلَّهُم مِّنكُمْ فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Take not the Jews and the Christians as Auliya' (friends, protectors, helpers, etc.), they are but Auliya' to one another. And if any amongst you takes them as Auliya', then surely he is one of them. Verily, Allah guides not those people who are the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers and unjust).*

Whoever loves and accepts the wilāyah (guardianship) of Ādam shall be reckoned among his congregation. He who becomes a friend of Satan is Satan, and shall bear that name. He shall have neither kinship nor connection with Ādam. For this reason, Allāh expelled the son of Nūḥ (Noah) from his household, declaring:

> ⟪quran 11:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَنُوحُ إِنَّهُۥ لَيْسَ مِنْ أَهْلِكَ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ عَمَلٌ غَيْرُ صَلِحٍۢ ۖ فَلَا تَسْـَٔلْنِ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۖ إِنِّىٓ أَعِظُكَ أَن تَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْجَهِلِينَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Nuh (Noah)! Surely, he is not of your family; verily, his work is unrighteous, so ask not of Me that of which you have no knowledge! I admonish you, lest you be one of the ignorants."*

## Qābīl's Envy and Hābīl's Martyrdom

Allāh appointed Ādam as His khalīfah (caliph) over other human beings and designated him as the Messenger of Allāh and the Ḥujjah Allāh (proof of Allāh). His lineage multiplied and spread abundantly. As his life drew to its close, he appointed his son ⟪ar:ھابیل⟫ (Hābīl, Abel) as his successor and heir. Yet his brother ⟪ar:قابیل⟫ (Qābīl, Cain) burned with envy, reckoning that if Hābīl remained alive, his father would never appoint him successor. When his false hope proved illusory, he became an opponent of Ādam. By persisting in transgression, he bred corruption and discord in the daʿwa (the divine summons), becoming Satan's instrument and ally. When Hābīl was martyred, Allāh blessed Ādam with another son, ⟪ar:'شیث علیہ السلام'⟫ (Shīth, upon whom be peace). Ādam named him ⟪ar:ھبۃ اللہ⟫ (Hībat Allāh, "the gift of Allāh") and appointed him successor in Hābīl's place, entrusting him confidentially with all the sciences of taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis) and the truths of the dīn (religion), and the conduct of the daʿwa Hādiyya (the rightly-guided summons). He did not proclaim this publicly, lest the enemies exploit such knowledge. Only the exalted spiritual limits and the sincere believers were apprised of it. In this manner, Ādam withdrew from the work of the daʿwa after completing his obligatory duties and functions.

From Shīth, his descendants perpetuated the Imāmate and Prophethood in an unbroken chain. Among his progeny stood the Prophet Idrīs. Thus the path of guidance persisted through Ādam's lineage until the time of ⟪ar:نوح علیہ السلام⟫ (Nūḥ, Noah, upon whom be peace).

## Ādam: Symbolic Embodiment of the Bismillāh

Ādam is akin to the opening verse of ⟪ar:سورۃ الحمد⟫ (the Chapter of Praise), for he was the one who inaugurated the cosmic cycle, and it is recited at the commencement of all things. Moreover, the name ⟪ar:'بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم'⟫ (In the Name of Allāh, the Merciful, the Compassionate) contains a wisdom: through the invocation of the Divine Name, ⟪ar:عبد اللہ⟫ (the servant of Allāh) comes into being—he who speaks ⟪ar:بسم اللہ⟫ (In the Name of Allāh).

He embodies the opening on the days of the dīn (religion), and he is the symbolic embodiment of wilāyah in the Dāʿim al-Islām (the Enduring Commandments of Islam).[^cite-13] You are the first person to whose wilāyah Allāh commanded obedience, and to whom He ordered the angels to prostrate. The meaning of prostration (sujūd) is truly submission (taʿah), which manifests as the testimony of wilāyah. All the angels obeyed except Satan. Wilāyah was Ādam's trial, and Ādam is like the trial itself. The entire creation is, without their wilāyah, null and void, and whoever lacks their guardianship shall profit nothing from those who follow them. He is the first of the Prophets, the Messengers, and the Imāms of the dīn, the father of them all, and he is the origin of that obligatory wilāyah which Allāh has decreed upon mankind.

In the human body, you bear the status of the nucleated sperm (nutfah) dwelling in the wombs of father and mother before the fertilization of the child, for the scattered components of the disclosed divine message inhabit the world. This is called the specific compendium—the ⟪ar:'سلالہ'⟫ (the extract)—preserved within the parents. The Sharīʿah of Muḥammad, destined to emerge after six thousand years, was founded and established by him.[^cite-14]

## Despair of God's Mercy Is a Sin

In his work the *Asās al-Taʾwīl*, Qāḍī al-Nūʿmān, may Allāh be pleased with him, counsels the believer thus: "O seeker of the truth! Whenever you commit an error and transgress, turn immediately in repentance and seek forgiveness through the saints of Allāh, so that you may remain within Ḥizb Allāh—the congregation of Ādam. Do not, through obstinacy, enter the congregation of Iblīs. Remember well: no sin, however grave, shall withstand repentance. Just as soiled cloth becomes clean and luminous through washing with soap and water, so too does the heart become purified through sincere repentance. Never despair of Allāh's mercy; to do so is to become among those who disbelieve. Rather, He extends to you tidings of hope, declaring:

> ⟪quran 12:87⟫
