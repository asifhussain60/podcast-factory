## The Outer Story (Ẓāhir) of Ādam, Peace Be Upon Him

### The Fashioning of Ādam from Clay

When God resolved to create Ādam (Adam), He sent revelation to the earth: "O earth! I am about to fashion from your substance a creature. Some of them will obey Me, and some will disobey. The obedient I shall admit to Paradise; the disobedient I shall consign to the Fire." After this disclosure, the Almighty dispatched Jibrāʾīl (Gabriel), peace be upon him, saying, "Go and bring forth dust from the earth." When Jibrāʾīl bent to gather the soil, the earth cried out: "I adjure you by the glory of God who has sent you upon this errand — take nothing from my soil that will tomorrow become fuel for the Fire." Jibrāʾīl returned empty-handed and reported, "Since the earth has adjured me by Your honour, I did not dare to proceed." Then God sent Mīkāʾīl (Michael); the earth adjured him likewise, and he too returned with nothing. Finally God sent ⟪ar:ملک الموت⟫, ʿAzrāʾīl, the Angel of Death. When the earth made its plea, he answered, "By God, I will not disobey the command of my Lord!" — and so saying, he lifted a little dust from each of the four corners of the earth. The soil he gathered was of every kind: soft and hard, hot and cold, white and red, black and yellow, pure and impure. From this very variety arises the diversity of temperament, complexion, and disposition among the children of Ādam.

It is reported that ⟪ar:عبد اللہ ابن سلام⟫ (ʿAbd Allāh ibn Salām) once asked the Messenger of God, "How did God create Ādam?" He replied: "Ādam's brow was formed from the clay of the Kaʿba; his shoulders from the clay of the noble sanctuaries; his feet from the clay of the Ḥijāz; his right side from the clay of the East; and his left from the clay of the West."

### The Kneading of the Clay in Three Waters

When ʿAzrāʾīl, peace be upon him, brought this handful of soil into the divine presence, he was commanded to mix it with water and to knead it thoroughly. Three kinds of water were used — bitter, sweet, and saline. He kneaded the soil until it could be shaped; and this prepared substance is what is called ⟪ar:’طین‘⟫ (clay).

Then the Almighty commanded Jibrāʾīl, "Go now to the earth and bring me a bright, luminous dust from which I shall fashion ⟪ar:محمد علیہ السلام⟫." So Jibrāʾīl descended in great procession, accompanied by ⟪ar:ملائکہ فردوس⟫ (the angels of Paradise), ⟪ar:ملائکہ مغربین⟫ (the angels of the Western horizons), and ⟪ar:ملائکہ نصف الاعلی⟫ (the angels of the highest assembly). They took up a pure, white, translucent earth from the place that would become the resting-place of the Prophet, peace be upon him. This pristine soil was moistened with the water of the Paradise-stream ⟪ar:’تسنیم‘⟫ (Tasnīm), until it grew lustrous as a white pearl. It was then bathed in turn in every river of the Garden. When the gaze of God fell upon it, the substance trembled with awe and broke into pieces. God divided it into one hundred and twenty-four thousand droplets, and from each He created a prophet — every prophet was thus created from your light, O Muḥammad. The pearl was then circulated through the heavens and the earth, so that the angels came to know and recognise Muḥammad even before they knew Ādam. For this reason the Prophet declared, ⟪ar-quote:کنت نبیا کان آدم بین المآء و الطین⟫ — "I was already a prophet while Ādam was still between water and clay."[^cite-1]

### The Clay of Ādam Left to Rest for Forty Years

The clay of Ādam was kneaded with water and left to rest for forty years, until it became supple and could be drawn into any form. It was then left a further forty years, during which it dried and hardened, ringing like an earthen vessel when struck.

> ⟪quran 55:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏خَلَقَ ٱلْإِنسَنَ مِن صَلْصَلٍۢ كَٱلْفَخَّارِ ‎⟫
> *He created man (Adam) from sounding clay like the clay of pottery.*
> He created man from clay like the potter's.
> — **Quran 55:14 (inline widget)**

The body of Ādam was then formed from this dry, fired substance — so that it might be plain that God did not bring Ādam forth from soft, pliant matter, but from arid and unshapen substance, by His unmediated power. The completed form then lay along the path of the angels for forty more years.

### The Fashioning of Ādam and the Astonishment of Iblīs

The angels marvelled at the beauty of Ādam as they passed, for they had never beheld such a figure. But when Iblīs (Satan) passed by, he reflected: "Why has this statue been made?" He struck the form with his hand, and finding it hollow within, he entered through the mouth and emerged from it again. He said to his fellows: "This is a curious creature — empty within. It cannot stand firm and steadfast." Then he asked them: "Suppose this being were granted precedence over you — what would you do?" They all answered: "We will obey the command of our Lord." But Iblīs said in his heart: "If this statue is given precedence over me, I will disobey; and if I should excel over it, I will not spare it."

### Why Sorrow Predominates over Joy

It is also reported that the body of Ādam lay in this condition for forty years, during which grief and sorrow rained upon it; only for one year did joy fall upon it. For this reason — the tradition holds — the children of Ādam are most often touched by sorrow, with only intervals of relief and gladness at the end. As the poet says:

> Sorrow is passing, joy too is passing.
> If you have strength enough to grieve, then no grief will remain.

### The Manifestation of Ādam's Excellence After His Completion

When the body of Ādam was complete, God clothed him in the robes of Paradise and adorned him with every kind of ornament. From his teeth light shone like the rays of the sun, and upon his brow the light of our Prophet ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ glowed like the moon of the fourteenth night. God seated him upon a throne, lifted him upon the shoulders of the angels, and bade them travel with him through every heaven, that he might behold the whole created order and so attain ⟪ar:عین الیقین⟫ (the very vision of certainty). For nearly a hundred years the angels bore him through Paradise, until Ādam was familiar with its every region. Then God created from pure musk a steed whom He named ⟪ar:’میمون‘⟫ (Maymūn, the Blessed). Adorning both Ādam and his mount with pearls and gems, He set Ādam upon the steed; Jibrāʾīl took the reins, while Mīkāʾīl and Isrāfīl walked in procession to right and left, and they circled all the heavens. In every heaven Ādam greeted the angels: ⟪ar:’السلام علیکم یا ملائکۃ اللہ‘⟫ — "Peace be upon you, O angels of God." Then God said: "O Ādam! This greeting shall be a gift to every believer born of your descendants, until the Day of Resurrection."

Then God taught Ādam every ⟪ar:’أسمآء‘⟫ (name). When the angels were questioned concerning these names, they answered: ⟪ar-quote:قَالُوا۟ سُبْحَنَكَ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَآ إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ⟫ — "Glory be to You; we have no knowledge save what You have taught us. Surely You are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise." Only when God commanded Ādam to instruct the angels did they receive this knowledge from him. He thus became their teacher, and they all bowed down before him. Only Iblīs refused, and showed arrogance.

### The Creation of Ḥawwāʾ

The exegetes report that when God settled Ādam in Paradise, he was overcome by solitude. Beholding his desolation, and to dispel it, God resolved to create for him a companion of his own kind. He cast Ādam into a deep sleep, drew from his left side a rib — the one called ⟪ar:’قصیریٰ‘⟫ — and from it shaped Ḥawwāʾ (Eve). Throughout this Ādam felt not the slightest pain; for had he felt any, no man would ever have been drawn to woman.

Ḥawwāʾ was then clothed in the garments of Paradise, adorned with sixteen ornaments, and seated upon Ādam's couch. When Ādam awoke, he saw beside him a face of luminous beauty. The angels asked, "Who is this?" The reply came: ⟪ar:’عورت‘⟫. They asked, "And her name?" The reply: ⟪ar:’حوا‘⟫. The angels asked, "Why has she been named Ḥawwāʾ?" The reply: "Because she was drawn from a living being" — ⟪ar:حی⟫ (ḥayy). "And for whom has God created her?" "That I might find rest in her, and she in me." This account God Himself relates:

> ⟪quran 4:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱتَّقُوا۟ رَبَّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍۢ وَحِدَةٍۢ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًۭا كَثِيرًۭا وَنِسَآءًۭ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى تَسَآءَلُونَ بِهِۦ وَٱلْأَرْحَامَ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ عَلَيْكُمْ رَقِيبًۭا ‎⟫
> *O mankind! Be dutiful to your Lord, Who created you from a single person (Adam), and from him (Adam) He created his wife [Hawwa (Eve)], and from them both He created many men and women and fear Allah through Whom you demand your mutual (rights), and (do not cut the relations of) the wombs (kinship). Surely, Allah is Ever an All&shy;Watcher over you.*
> O mankind! Fear your Lord, who created you from a single soul, and from it made its mate, and from the two of them scattered many men and women. Fear Allah, by Means by Means of which you demand your rights, and shun the severity of the ties of kinship; surely Allah is watching over you.
> — **Quran 4:1 (inline widget)**

The Messenger of God said: "Woman was created from a curved rib of Ādam. If you set yourself to straighten it, you will break it; but if you leave it as it is, you will derive enjoyment from it." With age, men retain a certain dignity while the beauty of women fades — for man is fashioned from clay, which does not grow foul as it dries but is rather refined with time; whereas woman is fashioned from flesh, which putrefies and is no longer regarded.

### Ādam Makes Ḥawwāʾ Lawful to Himself

It is reported in some traditions that when Ādam beheld Ḥawwāʾ, he stretched out his hand toward her. The angels restrained him, saying, "Wait!" Ādam answered, "God has created her for me." They replied, "True — yet she is not lawful to you until you pay her dower." He asked, "What is her dower, and how much?" They said, "Her dower is to recite the prayer of blessing three times upon ⟪ar:حضرت محمد مصطفی (ﷺ)⟫." Ādam asked, "Who is this ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫?" They replied: "He is the seal of the prophets, from among your descendants — and had it not been for the purpose of his coming, you yourself would not have been created."

### The Dwelling of Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ in Paradise

When God settled Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ in Paradise, He made lawful for them every blessing of the Garden save the fruit of one tree, saying:

### The Plot of Iblīs

When God had granted Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ this rich estate, ⟪ar:أبلیس لعین⟫ — Iblīs the accursed — envied them both. Slipping back into the very Paradise from which he had been driven, he sought ways to deceive Ādam. He entered the Garden in the form of a ⟪ar:حیۃ⟫ (serpent), so that they did not recognise him; according to some traditions, he wound himself among the feathers of the peacock and entered by that means. The peacock — who had once been a servant of Ādam, and an old companion of Iblīs — was complicit. Drawing near to Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ, Iblīs wept so bitterly that they wept with him. He was thus the first to weep, and for this reason lamentation is called the voice of Satan. They asked him sympathetically, "Why do you weep?" He answered: "I grieve over your weakness and over your coming ruin. I see that after a few years you must both die, and all this honour and blessing must be left behind." He spoke so movingly that they were grieved by his words. So saying, Iblīs departed.

A few days later, when they were still oppressed by his earlier speech, he returned. Seeing that his arrow had struck, he said with feigned concern: "O Ādam, shall I show you the tree from which, if you eat, you shall live forever, and your kingdom shall never perish?" Ādam said, "Yes!" Iblīs then pointed to the very tree that had been forbidden: "Eat from it, and you shall endure." Ādam exclaimed, "My Lord has forbidden me this!" Iblīs replied, "He has forbidden it only because, were you to eat, you two should become ⟪ar:مَلَک (⟫angels⟪ar:)⟫ and remain in Paradise forever." When Ādam refused, Iblīs swore by God Himself that he was a sincere counsellor. They were deceived by his oath, for they could not conceive that any creature would swear falsely by God. So Ḥawwāʾ hastened to eat from the tree, and then offered the fruit to Ādam. As soon as they tasted it and trespassed beyond their station, the garments of Paradise fell from their bodies, and they stood naked. In dismay Ādam fled; he approached the ⟪ar:عناب⟫ (jujube) tree, which seized him by the hair. From the Throne the call came: "O Ādam! Do you flee from Me?" He answered, "Yes, my Lord — I flee from You out of shame."

### The Reward of the Fig Tree

It is reported that when Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ stood naked and began to seek the leaves of the trees of Paradise to cover themselves, every plant rebuked them. Only the fig tree took pity, lending its leaves, by which Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ concealed themselves. God rewarded the fig for its mercy by making its outward and inward alike — granting it a sweet flesh, beneficial properties, and the bearing of fruit twice a year. A peculiarity of the fig is that it yields fruit without producing visible flowers.

### The Ten Trials of Ādam

The scholars say that ten trials descended upon Ādam after he ate from the forbidden tree:

| --- | --- |
| 2 | The robe of Paradise was stripped from him, leaving him in disgrace. |
| 3 | His skin grew weak and dark; previously his whole body had been hard and white as a fingernail. |
| 4 | He was expelled from the proximity and neighbourhood of God. |
| 5 | He and Ḥawwāʾ were parted for a hundred years — Ādam in India, Ḥawwāʾ in Jeddah. They went forth in search of each other and met at last in Muzdalifa, named for the bringing-near (al-izdilāf). The place where they later sat together was called Jumʿa (the meeting); and the day they recognised each other was called ʿArafa. |
| 6 | All who shared in this affair became enemies one to another. Man became the enemy of the serpent, crushing its head wherever he sees it; the peacock too became the serpent's enemy; and Iblīs became the enemy of them all. |
| 7 | They received in reproach the epithets ʿāṣī (disobedient) and ghāwī (gone astray), as God says: "Then they ate from the tree, and their nakedness was made apparent to them; they began to fasten upon themselves the leaves of Paradise — and Ādam disobeyed his Lord, and went astray." |

### The Expulsion of Ādam from Paradise

When Ādam was driven forth on account of his disobedience, and the proclamation issued from God that one who has disobeyed cannot remain in His proximity, Ādam took Ḥawwāʾ by the hand and went forward. At every step they looked back, in the hope that the Lord's mercy might overtake them, weeping with shame. As they took the final step out of Paradise, the words ⟪ar:’بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم‘⟫ broke from their lips. Jibrāʾīl halted them and said: "O Ādam! A word has issued from your tongue whose blessing and majesty give me hope that He, the ⟪ar:رحمٰن و رحیم⟫ — the Compassionate and Merciful — will have mercy upon you." God then declared to Jibrāʾīl: "I will have mercy upon him and upon his descendants on the Day of Resurrection, when they return to Me."

Ādam descended onto Mount Sarandīb in India, and Ḥawwāʾ onto a place in the Ḥijāz called Jeddah. It is said that after his descent, Ādam was so consumed with shame that for three hundred years he did not raise his eyes to the heavens. Ibn ʿAbbās relates that Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ wept for two hundred years, and abstained from food and drink for forty, after they had been torn from the delights of Paradise and severed from the proximity of God.

When at last God willed to show Ādam mercy, He taught him a few words by which his repentance would be accepted: ⟪ar:’لا الہ الا اللہ محمد رسول اللہ‘⟫. Ādam beheld this formula inscribed upon the foot of the Throne; now it became his remedy. Through Ḥaḍrat Muḥammad Muṣṭafā (peace be upon him and his family) he sought intercession; God forgave him, and revealed that he should circumambulate the earthly counterpart of the Throne and pray there as one prays beside the heavenly Throne, "and there I will answer your prayer."[^cite-2]

Ādam set out from India for Mecca to visit the Kaʿba, and God sent an angel to be his guide. When he reached the plain of ʿArafāt, he met Ḥawwāʾ, who had come there from Jeddah in search of him. The two met on the Day of ʿArafa — hence the name ⟪ar:’عرفات‘⟫. Returning to Minā, God said to him: "Express your wish." He answered, "My wish is that You forgive me and bless me." Hence the name Minā — "wish." God answered his prayer and forgave their sin. Then Ādam returned to India.

### God's Revelation to Ādam

Wahb ibn Munabbih relates that after forgiving Ādam, God revealed to him: "O Ādam, I have gathered for you the whole of wisdom in four sayings." One concerns God alone; one concerns what is between God and Ādam; one concerns Ādam alone; and one concerns Ādam and his fellow servants.

| For God | Between God and Ādam | For Ādam | Between Ādam and the Servants |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Worship Me and associate nothing with Me. | Your part is to pray; My part is to accept. | I shall recompense you according to your deeds. | So love for them what you love for yourself. |

### The Meeting of Ādam and Iblīs

It is reported that when Ādam encountered Iblīs in a wilderness, he reproached him for his ruin and disgrace, saying: "O accursed one, what have you done? You deceived me, brought me forth from Paradise, and reduced me to abasement. What had I done to you?" Iblīs wept and answered: "Yes, Ādam, I am guilty before you, and I confess what I did. But I in turn ask you: who has cast me into this disgrace and ruin? For whose sake do I bear this curse?"

### The Petitions of Iblīs and Their Acceptance

⟪ar:حماد ثابت⟫ and ⟪ar:حمید عبد اللہ ابن عبید⟫ relate that Iblīs said in the divine court: "My Lord, You have driven me from Paradise on account of Ādam. Now grant me dominion over him, that I may overcome him." God said, "Go; I have given you authority over him." Seeing that even a small request had yielded so vast a power, Iblīs pressed further: "O Lord, grant me more." God said, "Listen: for every one child Ādam has, you shall have two; your offspring shall be double his." Iblīs said, "O Lord, more." God said, "Hear also: in the breasts of the children of Ādam shall be your abode, and you shall flow with their blood through their veins." Iblīs said, "O Lord, more." God said, "You are permitted to assail the children of Ādam with your cavalry and your infantry; to mingle in their wealth and children; and to make them promises — though in truth your promise is nothing but the dross of deception."

### Ādam's Petitions and Their Acceptance

When Ādam saw that every request of Iblīs was being granted, and that he and his children would be ground down, he in turn prayed to his Lord: "O Lord, You have set Iblīs upon us, and You know that we cannot escape his snare without Your help. What will become of me and my offspring?" God consoled him: "O Ādam! With every child of yours I shall send a guardian to protect him from the evil companion. Ask for more." Ādam said, "Grant me more!" God said, "Hear this glad tidings: whoever among your children does a single good deed, I shall reward him tenfold; and whoever does an evil deed shall be requited only with the like of it — no more. Even after that, I shall not leave him without forgiveness." Ādam asked further. God said, "Go and tell your children: O sinful servants of God, never despair of God's mercy. Even to their last breath, I shall forgive those who repent, and I shall never close the door of repentance against them." Ādam said, "More good news!" God said, "Very well: I shall forgive your children, and shall not bring them to account for what I have forgiven." Ādam rejoiced and said, "Enough, my Lord!"

### The Dominion of Iblīs

It is reported that Iblīs said in the court of the ⟪ar:رب العزت⟫ (the Lord of Might): "O Lord, You have cursed me, expelled me from Paradise, disfigured and humiliated me, and given me the names Shayṭān and Iblīs. All this I have heard and borne — but I have one request: grant me certain instruments." God said: "You have sent prophets to guide the children of Ādam, and revealed to them Your word in the form of a book. Who, then, shall be my messenger, and what shall be my book?"

The Almighty said: "Your messengers shall be the soothsayers; your book shall be a sheaf of slander, backbiting, and false witness." Iblīs asked, "And my ḥadīth?" God said, "Your ḥadīth shall be a scroll of lies." "And my recitation?" "Your Qurʾān shall be the chanting of poetry." "And my muezzin?" "There shall be musical instruments." "And my mosque?" "The marketplace." "And my house?" "Your dwelling shall be the bath-house — a place of impurity." "And my food?" "Anything over which My name has not been pronounced." "And my drink?" "Every intoxicant." "And my prey?" God said: "Women shall be your prey."

### The Last Testament of Ādam

Mujāhid relates that ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿAbbās said: Ḥaḍrat Ādam, who passed his life in India, performed the Ḥajj on foot forty times. The light of Ḥaḍrat Muḥammad Muṣṭafā (peace be upon him and his family) was seen to shine from his brow. He summoned all his life to God, fulfilled the right of prophethood, and bore every kind of hardship from the people of his age with patience and grace, continuing the ⟪ar:دعوت الی اللہ⟫ (the summons to God) until the time of his death drew near.

For eleven days he was ill before his passing. During those days he wrote his testament and gave it to his son ⟪ar:شِیث علیہ السلام⟫ (Shīth — Seth — peace be upon him), bidding him keep it concealed from the children of Qābīl. For when Ādam had married Qābīl's sister Aqlīmā to Hābīl (Cain to Abel), Qābīl had killed Hābīl out of envy. Ādam therefore feared that if Qābīl's descendants discovered that Shīth had been entrusted with special knowledge, they would harm him. Thus Shīth and his line kept this knowledge hidden from the descendants of Qābīl; and on account of their enmity to Hābīl, the descendants of Qābīl remained deprived and ignorant of it.

### The Death of the Prophet Ādam

According to the traditions, Ādam died on a Friday. The angels brought his shroud and the embalming substances from Paradise. He was first washed in scented water, then in plain water, and wrapped in a three-fold shroud. He was then borne to a settlement in Mashāriq al-Firdaws — the first settlement upon the earth. Ādam was buried by his son Shīth and his brothers. After the burial, the sun and the moon were eclipsed for six days.

When the burial was complete, Shīth asked Jibrāʾīl to lead the funeral prayer. Jibrāʾīl said, "Go forward and pray over your father." So Shīth led the funeral prayer and recited thirty takbīrs over him — five drawn from the five daily prayers, and twenty-five added in honour of Ādam's excellence.

It is reported in this connection that the Prophet recited seventy takbīrs at the funeral of ⟪ar:سید الشھداء جناب حمزہ⟫ — the lord of martyrs, Ḥamza — and the same tradition is transmitted in more than one chain: that at the funeral of ⟪ar:سید الشھداء جناب حمزہ⟫ the Prophet recited seventy takbīrs; and again, that the Prophet recited seventy takbīrs at the funeral of ⟪ar:سید الشھداء جناب حمزہ⟫.

### The Death of Ḥawwāʾ

Ḥawwāʾ lived one year after Ādam, and died in Jeddah. A great grave lies upon the bank of the river there.

## The Garden of Ādam (the Janna in Which He Was Placed)

A long-standing disagreement persists among the interpreters of Scripture concerning the Garden of Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ: was it located in the heavens, or upon the earth? In the view of the ⟪ar:’حضرات امامی‘⟫ — those who follow the Imami doctrine — the garden in which Ādam dwelt was not in the heavens but on the earth, and it must be distinguished altogether from the ⟪ar:جنت خلد⟫ (the Janna of immortality, the Paradise of recompense reserved for God's reward).

The argument is direct. Were Ādam's garden the Paradise of recompense, Iblīs could never have been expelled from it, for the dwellers of that Paradise abide there forever; nor could he afterwards have re-entered the same garden in order to whisper deception into Ādam's heart. Yet the Qurʾān records precisely such whispering inside the very garden in which Ādam still resided:

> ⟪quran 20:120⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ إِلَيْهِ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ هَلْ أَدُلُّكَ عَلَىٰ شَجَرَةِ ٱلْخُلْدِ وَمُلْكٍۢ لَّا يَبْلَىٰ ‎⟫
> *Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered to him, saying : "O Adam! Shall I lead you to the Tree of Eternity and to a kingdom that will never waste away?" *
> Then Satan whispered to him. He said, O Adam, shall I direct you to the Tree of Eternity and a kingdom that will not decay?
> — **Quran 20:120 (inline widget)**

As Ādam inclined attentively toward him, Satan then said:

> ⟪quran 7:20⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَلِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered suggestions to them both in order to uncover that which was hidden from them of their private parts (before); he said: "Your Lord did not forbid you this tree save you should become angels or become of the immortals." *
> Satan whispered to them, in order to reveal to them that which they had hidden from one another, and said, Your Lord has not forbidden you from this tree except that you may become angels or become immortals.
> — **Quran 7:20 (inline widget)**

These verses imply that Ādam dwelt in a garden in which immortality had not yet been granted; the Tree of Eternity was something he was being lured toward, not something he already possessed. By contrast, the abode of recompense once entered admits of no departure, as God Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 15:48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَا يَمَسُّهُمْ فِيهَا نَصَبٌۭ وَمَا هُم مِّنْهَا بِمُخْرَجِينَ ‎⟫
> *"No sense of fatigue shall touch them, nor shall they (ever) be asked to leave it." *
> no evil shall touch them, nor shall they be expelled from it.
> — **Quran 15:48 (inline widget)**

When Iblīs refused to prostrate, he was cursed; and a being who has incurred God's wrath cannot re-enter Paradise. Nor is it consonant with the divine wisdom to introduce a soul into the Garden of recompense without first requiring the works that earn that recompense, for God does not bestow the reward of the ⟪ar:عاملین⟫ (the workers, those whose labour entitles them to the Garden) upon those who have not laboured. The most decisive proof that Ādam was created upon earth, and not above it, lies in the Qurʾān's own diction:

> ⟪quran 2:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when your Lord said to the angels: "Verily, I am going to place (mankind) generations after generations on earth." They said: "Will You place therein those who will make mischief therein and shed blood, - while we glorify You with praises and thanks (Exalted be You above all that they associate with You as partners) and sanctify You." He (Allah) said: "I know that which you do not know." *
> And when your Lord said to the angels, 'I am going to place a vicegerent on the earth.' They said, 'Do You want to place in the earth one who will spread corruption and shed blood, while we glorify You with His praise and glorify You?' He said, 'I know what you do not know.'
> — **Quran 2:30 (inline widget)**

In this verse God states plainly that He is creating His vicegerent upon the ⟪ar:’زمین‘⟫ (the earth), not in the heavens.

## The Test of the Angels (Q2:30–33)

The Qurʾān describes the appointment of Ādam in the following terms:

> ⟪quran 2:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when your Lord said to the angels: "Verily, I am going to place (mankind) generations after generations on earth." They said: "Will You place therein those who will make mischief therein and shed blood, - while we glorify You with praises and thanks (Exalted be You above all that they associate with You as partners) and sanctify You." In the taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis) of this passage, the ⟪ar:’رب‘⟫ (the Lord) of the verse designates the Imam ⟪ar:’ھنید علیہ السلام‘⟫ of that era — the Imam who first brought the Period of Fatra (interregnum) to its close and inaugurated the Seventh Dawr. With the new cycle the era of overt prophetic labour returned; the Imam himself remains in concealment while his caliphs, prophets, and messengers carry out the daʿwa on his behalf. Although many capable figures (the "angels") were ranged about him in his assembly, the Imam's gaze settled upon ʿAbd Allāh b. Tanjūm — his nephew, a young man still occupied with the elementary sciences. His intention was to elevate this nephew to the rank of prophet, that is, to constitute his "second creation" — the creation of knowledge. Of this passage Scripture states:

In Arabic the word ⟪ar:’طین‘⟫ denotes clay, itself a composite of earth and water. Earth here signifies the knowledge of the Sharīʿa — the outward, visible discipline — while water signifies the knowledge of taʾwīl, the inward and esoteric science. The Imam's words thus mean: "I shall prepare Ādam by means of both these knowledges." Now the angels themselves had been brought into being purely from the inward science, which is spiritual and subtle; they knew well that this subtle illumination descends upon a soul as suddenly as lightning, through the support (taʾyīd) of those higher in rank, whereas the outward science is amassed gradually, by stages of disciplined study over time.

The angels also recognised that the subtle, spiritual knowledge stands somewhat apart from action: it does not require the body's exertion, only inward affirmation. Concerning this very distinction, Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī, in his eulogy of the Qurʾān, said: ⟪ar-quote:ظاھر عمل موجب و باطن علم محجوب⟫ — "Its outward demands action, while its inward science remains veiled." Knowing this, the angels could foresee that once Ādam's responsibility would be carried out by the labour of human beings — those of the rank of the ⟪ar:’مستجیب‘⟫ (mustajīb, the respondent) — error and shortcoming were bound to arise. They therefore submitted to the Imam: "If the purpose of this appointment is the inward glorification and sanctification of Your name, then surely we accomplish that better than he. We have already absorbed many of the sciences of the daʿwa, completed the books of taʾwīl, and are now engaged with the books of the ḥaqāʾiq (the realities). It would be more fitting that one of us be elevated to this office, for we are abler than Ādam to bear the burdens of Your summons. Poor Ādam is still occupied with the books of the Sharīʿa." Thus they said to the Imam:

Among the angels was one — ⟪ar:’عزازیل‘⟫ (ʿAzāzīl) — who supposed himself superior to Ādam and therefore the more deserving claimant to the office of vicegerency. He refrained from obedience and refused to join in the prostration. His proper name was ⟪ar:’حارث بن مرۃ‘⟫ — al-Ḥārith b. Murra. In ⟪ar:’⟫this verse,⟪ar:‘⟫ the matter that the Imam knew while the ḥudūd did not concerned two things: Ādam's hidden capacity, of which the others were ignorant, and the foreknowledge that, once obedience was commanded, Iblīs would refuse and grow proud.

God Himself had fashioned Ādam by His own two hands, forming him from clay, as He says:

> ⟪quran 38:75⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓإِبْلِيسُ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَن تَسْجُدَ لِمَا خَلَقْتُ بِيَدَىَّ ۖ أَسْتَكْبَرْتَ أَمْ كُنتَ مِنَ ٱلْعَالِينَ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "O Iblîs (Satan)! What prevents you from prostrating yourself to one whom I have created with Both My Hands. Are you too proud (to fall prostrate to Adam) or are you one of the high exalted?" *
> said, "O Iblis! What prevented you from prostrating before what I created with My own hands? Were you too proud, or were you one of the exalted?
> — **Quran 38:75 (inline widget)**

The "two hands" denote the two evidentiary instruments of the Imam's daʿwa — the two arguments (ḥujjatān) that he appointed for Ādam's outward and inward instruction. To say that he was fashioned from clay means that the Imam began his training with the outward sciences, taught him the foundational disciplines, and completed his formation in measured stages over the years. How far Ādam advanced in the esoteric sciences during this long apprenticeship none apart from the Imam himself could measure.

A ḥadīth describes the same matter under another image. When God Most High had shaped Ādam's frame out of clay, the body — the still-lifeless statue — was left to lie for an extended period before the spirit was breathed into it. Before receiving the support of the inward, spiritual sciences, he applied himself for years to the disciplines of the outward. Iblīs would come and tap the dried clay form. Because the body was still hollow, it would ring; Iblīs would cry out gleefully, "It is empty, it is hollow!" — meaning that he would approach Ādam in the course of his elementary studies and quiz him on the higher truths, and when Ādam, possessed at that stage only of the outward sciences, returned no answer, Iblīs would rejoice, presuming that Ādam had no aptitude for the inward at all — as though the world had been emptied of any rival to himself.

In this fashion Ādam's instruction and formation continued through many years, until at last his preparation was complete and he became worthy of the vicegerency:

> ⟪quran 2:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَعَلَّمَ آدَمَ ٱلْأَسْمَآءَ كُلَّهَا ثُمَّ عَرَضَهُمْ عَلَى ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ فَقَالَ أَنۢبِـُٔونِى بِأَسْمَآءِ هَٓؤُلَآءِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *And He taught Adam all the names (of everything) , then He showed them to the angels and said, "Tell Me the names of these if you are truthful." *
> And Allah taught Adam all the names, then He presented them to the angels, and said, Tell Me the names of these, if you are truthful.
> — **Quran 2:31 (inline widget)**

God taught Ādam all the names directly, whereas the angels had received their knowledge through mediated channels. By ⟪ar:’أسمآء‘⟫ (the names) is meant ⟪ar:’معرفت‘⟫ (maʿrifa, gnosis), because to know the name of a being is to know that being. In the taʾwīl: Imam al-Hunayd (peace be upon him) introduced Ādam to the ⟪ar:ملائکہ علیین⟫ (the malāʾika ʿilliyyīn, the high angels) — those exalted ḥudūd who stood above Ādam in rank and beyond the limits subordinate to him under the Sharīʿa. To Ādam alone was given the knowledge of these names, because he alone was destined to be raised to the station of prophecy. When the Imam questioned the angels about these names:

> ⟪quran 2:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالُوا۟ سُبْحَنَكَ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَآ إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *They (angels) said: "Glory be to You, we have no knowledge except what you have taught us. Verily, it is You, the All-Knower, the All-Wise." *
> They said, 'Glory be to Thee! We know only that which Thou hast taught us; surely Thou art the All-knowing, the All-wise.'
> — **Quran 2:32 (inline widget)**

Since these ḥudūd were not of the rank of prophethood, the knowledge of those high names lay veiled from them; only through such names could their own degrees be raised. They confessed their ignorance and acknowledged the knowledge and wisdom of Imam al-Hunayd (peace be upon him).

> ⟪quran 2:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Adam! Inform them of their names," and when he had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the Ghaib (unseen) in the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you have been concealing?" *
> said, 'O Adam, inform me of their names.' And when Adam had informed them of their names, he said, 'Did I not tell you that I know the secrets of the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you hide?'
> — **Quran 2:33 (inline widget)**

Then Imam al-Hunayd commanded Ādam to disclose the names to the angels. When Ādam had made the great beings known to those who had previously stood above him, he became to them ⟪ar:معلم و مفید⟫ (their teacher and benefactor), for in every realm the one who possesses knowledge takes precedence over the one who lacks it. Thus the intellectual superiority of Ḥaḍrat ʿAbd Allāh over them all was made manifest. The Imam said: "Did I not tell you that I know the unseen of the heavens and the earth?" — meaning, "I know the full capacities of angels and human beings alike; I am cognisant both of their outward and their inward intentions and acts. You supposed that Ādam was incapable of grasping the sciences of prophecy and of intellect — and now you see that he can grasp them better than you."

## Exegesis of 'I Know What You Reveal and What You Conceal' (Q2:33)

> ⟪quran 2:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Adam! Inform them of their names," and when he had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the Ghaib (unseen) in the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you have been concealing?" *
> said, 'O Adam, inform me of their names.' And when Adam had informed them of their names, he said, 'Did I not tell you that I know the secrets of the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you hide?'
> — **Quran 2:33 (inline widget)**

Concerning the phrase ⟪ar-quote:وأعلم ما تبدون و ماکنتم تکتمون⟫ — "I know what you reveal and I know what you conceal" — Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) narrates, on the authority of his father Mawlānā Muḥammad al-Bāqir (peace be upon him), that what the angel revealed was the question ⟪ar-quote:أتجعل فیھا من یفسدو فیھا⟫ — "Will You place therein one who works corruption?" — which he uttered to the Imam without due consideration. What they concealed, by contrast, was the inward astonishment: how could God create a creature superior to themselves and then command them to bow before him?[^cite-6]

A second interpretation runs as follows. When God had fashioned the body of Ādam out of clay and placed it along a path that the angels customarily traversed, the angels, as they passed, were astonished at the beauty of his form, for they had never beheld a figure so excellent. But when Iblīs walked by, he asked himself why this form had been made at all. He struck the body with his hand. Finding the statue empty within, he would enter through the mouth and emerge again from the mouth, declaring to his fellow angels, "Behold a strange creature, wholly hollow within! It can never stand firm." Then he posed a question to them: "Suppose he were given superiority over you — what then?" They all replied, "We would obey our Lord." But in his own heart Iblīs said to himself, "If this statue be set above me, I shall not obey; and if I prevail over him, I shall not spare him." Thus ⟪ar-quote:وأعلم ما تبدون و ماکنتم تکتمون⟫ — "I know what you reveal and I know what you conceal" — signifies that God knew both the angels' open disclosure of disobedience and the silent intention of disobedience that Iblīs harboured within his heart.

## The Angels' Seeking of Forgiveness

It is narrated from Mawlānā Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) that he performed the Ḥajj together with his father, Mawlānā Muḥammad al-Bāqir (peace be upon him). One night, the Imam circumambulated the House of God in his father's company, kissed the Black Stone, and offered the prayer of ṭawāf. Wearied, the son withdrew and sat to rest while his father continued in prayer. Presently a man appeared and took his place beside the elder Imam: a figure of strong build and reddish hair, draped in two thick white robes. Sensing his presence, the Imam shortened his prayer, returned the greeting, and asked, "Have you some matter with me?" The man replied, "I have come to ask you about the origin of this House." The noble father then recounted the entire history of the birth of Ādam, peace be upon him, and what followed. Then he said: when the angels had confessed their fault, God addressed them thus:

> ⟪quran 2:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Adam! Inform them of their names," and when he had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the Ghaib (unseen) in the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you have been concealing?" *
> said, 'O Adam, inform me of their names.' And when Adam had informed them of their names, he said, 'Did I not tell you that I know the secrets of the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you hide?'
> — **Quran 2:33 (inline widget)**

That is: I know not only what you have made manifest — the angels' claim that they sufficed for divine praise — but also what you concealed within yourselves, namely the question of how God could fashion a creation higher than themselves and command their prostration before it. When God disclosed to the angels the secret of their hearts, they turned in repentance, gathered themselves at the Throne of God, and circled it seven times that they might please their Lord. God was pleased with their remorse and with their deed, and He commanded: "Descend to the earth and build there a House around which the erring children of Ādam shall gather and circumambulate, that I may forgive them and be pleased with them as you have been pleased."

The angels built this House. In the days of Nūḥ's deluge it was lifted up and set in the fourth heaven, where the angels circle it still and shall continue to do so until the Day of Judgement. Afterwards Ibrāhīm rebuilt the House upon its ancient foundations.[^cite-7] The Imam then expounded at length the circumstances of these earlier matters and the manner of the House's reconstruction.

## How Can Error Be Possible from the Angels?

If the angels are held to be inerrant, how then could they have erred? In the story of Ādam, "the angels of the Garden" refers to the companions of Imam al-Hunayd (peace be upon him) who dwelt in his presence. People are of two kinds: insiders and outsiders. Likewise, Ādam (peace be upon him) had a ḥijāb (veil, i.e. a designated proxy in the daʿwa hierarchy) operating among the insiders, and another ḥijāb operating in the outward sphere.

| Insiders (al-bāṭin) | Outsiders (al-ẓāhir) |
| --- | --- |
| The essence of Ādam | The veil (ḥijāb) of Ādam |
| The concealed rank | The manifest rank |

The ḥudūd (limits, i.e. the ranks of the daʿwa hierarchy) of the inner circle stood higher in station than those of the outer — just as in the spiritual world the last member of any circle ranks above the foremost of the circle beneath it — and they were not placed under the dispensation of Ādam. The angels whom God commanded to prostrate before Ādam were those whose rank fell below his and for whom the possibility of error therefore remained; these were the ḥudūd placed under Ādam's law.[^cite-8] In the same manner, the ḥijāb of Ādam who operated among the outsiders fell into error, and was punished for that error. That is to say, among the outer ranks there were certain members who erred, and on their account the veil of Ādam was implicated in the lapse; the muḥtajib (the one veiled, i.e. Ādam himself) bore no fault.

## Iblīs's Arrogance and Refusal

At that moment, God addressed the angels:

> ⟪quran 2:34⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قُلْنَا لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ ٱسْجُدُوا۟ لِآدَمَ فَسَجَدُوٓا۟ إِلَّآ إِبْلِيسَ أَبَىٰ وَٱسْتَكْبَرَ وَكَانَ مِنَ ٱلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when We said to the angels: "Prostrate yourselves before Adam.". And they prostrated except Iblîs (Satan), he refused and was proud and was one of the disbelievers (disobedient to Allah).*
> And when We said to the angels, 'Prostrate yourselves before Adam,' they bowed themselves, save Iblis; he refused and was proud, and he was one of the unbelievers.
> — **Quran 2:34 (inline widget)**

God commanded the angels: prostrate yourselves before Ādam, every one of you, obey him and submit to him; establish your station beside him, take from him the covenant of allegiance, join your ḥudūd (ranks) to his own, and receive instruction from him.

By way of taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis): in prostration, a person lays his head — the highest point of his body — at the feet of his ḥadd (rank), the lowest point of his standing. By this gesture he acknowledges that his most noble qualities count for less than the lowest qualities of the one before whom he bows. Further, in prostration he places seven parts of his body upon the ground on which his ḥadd is established, signifying the surrender of his entire being to his appointed superior.

The angels obeyed the command at once and fell into prostration — that is, they obeyed the Imam (peace be upon him) and moved towards the covenant of Ādam — save for one of the ḥudūd, the one called ⟪ar:’ابلیس‘⟫, who was known as ⟪ar:حارث بن مرۃ⟫.

> ⟪quran 7:12⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَلَّا تَسْجُدَ إِذْ أَمَرْتُكَ ۖ قَالَ أَنَا۠ خَيْرٌۭ مِّنْهُ خَلَقْتَنِى مِن نَّارٍۢ وَخَلَقْتَهُۥ مِن طِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "What prevented you (O Iblîs) that you did not prostrate, when I commanded you?" Iblîs said: "I am better than him (Adam), You created me from fire, and him You created from clay." *
> said, What prevented you from prostrating, when I commanded you? He said, I am better than he; You created me from fire, and You created him from clay.
> — **Quran 7:12 (inline widget)**

Imam al-Hunayd asked Ḥārith: "Why did you not prostrate before Ādam?" He answered: "I am fashioned of fire and he of clay" — meaning, I have been nurtured upon the luminous and sustaining sciences (the ḥaqāʾiq), which are bright and incisive as lightning and free from material defilement, whereas the sciences with which you have instructed Ādam are corporeal, gross, and tainted by matter — namely the sharīʿa together with its taʾwīl. He continued: "I am the ⟪ar:معلم الملکوت⟫ (instructor of the kingdom), who teaches the truths to all the angels; would you have this newcomer raised above me? How can the superior bend down before the lower?"[^cite-9]

Iblīs refused, and joined the ranks of the disbelievers. The term ⟪ar:’کافر‘⟫ signifies one who covers over, one who conceals. He knew full well that Ādam was more capable than himself — all the more so because God had demonstrated Ādam's capacity to all the angels. Yet he deliberately covered over Ādam's rank, and so was numbered among the disbelievers.

> ⟪quran 7:13⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ فَٱهْبِطْ مِنْهَا فَمَا يَكُونُ لَكَ أَن تَتَكَبَّرَ فِيهَا فَٱخْرُجْ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلصَّغِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "(O Iblîs) get down from this (Paradise), it is not for you to be arrogant here.&nbsp;Get out, for you are of those humiliated and disgraced." *
> say, Get thee down from it; it is not for thee to be haughty here, so go thee; surely thou art of the humiliated.
> — **Quran 7:13 (inline widget)**

God expelled the ⟪ar:’ملعون‘⟫ from Paradise on account of his arrogance — that is, He cast him out from His presence and His daʿwa (summons). The one who exalts himself before his superior dwells in the palace of Iblīs, for greatness and grandeur belong to God alone.

A note on the name: Iblīs is derived from the Arabic ⟪ar:’أبلس‘⟫ — ⟪ar:ابلیس⟫ — meaning "one made to despair." Ḥārith ibn Murra had aspired to attain the rank of prophethood; when he was deposed from his station and saw his hope of advancement crushed, he was called Iblīs. The name carries a second sense: one rendered incapable of reasoning, for he was confounded by the excellence of Ādam's bāṭinī knowledge. It bears yet a third meaning: one severed from mercy, since Imam al-Hunayd cast him from his court. The related term ⟪ar:’لعن‘⟫, in the Arabic lexicon, signifies to drive away or to banish.

## The Creation of Ḥawwāʾ (Eve) and the First Marriage

Just as the "birth" of Ādam refers to his intellectual birth, so too his "wife" denotes the spiritual and intellectual generation of his successor. The physical wife of Ādam was, of course, a woman born of human parents like himself; but the Ḥawwāʾ of whom the Qurʾān speaks belongs to the religious and hierarchical sphere of his caliph.

The name ⟪ar:’حوا‘⟫ derives from the Arabic ⟪ar:’حی‘⟫, meaning "alive"; the term is also applied to one who comprehends and encompasses a thing. Speaking of Ḥawwāʾ, the Qurʾān relates that when Ādam grew troubled, God took one of the ribs of his right side and fashioned from it his wife. This verse signifies that when Ādam (peace be upon him) assumed the station of nubuwwa (prophethood) and imāma, Imam al-Hunayd (peace be upon him) directed him to prepare his caliph. Ādam (peace be upon him) had twelve ḥujjas (proofs, i.e. senior witnesses of the Imam) or nuqabāʾ (chiefs), corresponding to the twelve ribs. They were called ribs because, just as the firmness of a man's body rests upon his ribs, so the stability of Ādam's daʿwa rested upon these ḥudūd. The twelve ribs on the right side correspond to the knowledge of the bāṭin, the twelve on the left to the knowledge of the ẓāhir: thus his inner nuqabāʾ numbered twelve, and those who summoned to the outward likewise numbered twelve.[^cite-10] In sum, Imam al-Hunayd (peace be upon him) elevated one of the ḥujjas of Ādam — namely Hābīl, the son of Ādam (peace be upon him) — and made him, in the manner of a wife, the partner of the ḥujja. Marriage signifies the joining of two parties, as husband and wife are joined to one another.

Although Hābīl was Ādam's son, he surpassed him in knowledge. He was therefore named ⟪ar:’حوا‘⟫ because he was ⟪ar:’حاوی‘⟫ — encompassing the science of the inner world. Imam al-Hunayd joined Hābīl to Ādam so that, if any difficulty arose for Ādam in the acquisition of knowledge, he might draw upon Hābīl's aid and so be spared. Ādam received this "wife" — Hābīl — in compensation for Iblīs. Earlier, Iblīs had been destined to be one of Ādam's ⟪ar:اضلاع (حدود)⟫ — one ⟪ar:ضلع⟫ among the dāʿīs of his daʿwa. But his summons collapsed before it had begun. Since one of the twelve had fallen away, the tally was restored by the inclusion of Hābīl. Thus God supplied Ādam with a substitute for Iblīs in the person of ⟪ar:’حوا‘⟫. Then He said:

> ⟪quran 2:35⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلْنَا يَٓـَٔادَمُ ٱسْكُنْ أَنتَ وَزَوْجُكَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَكُلَا مِنْهَا رَغَدًا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمَا وَلَا تَقْرَبَا هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةَ فَتَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And We said: "O Adam! Dwell you and your wife in the Paradise and eat both of you freely with pleasure and delight of things therein as wherever you will, but come not near this tree or you both will be of the Zalimun (wrong-doers)." *
> And We said, O Adam, dwell, you and your wife, in the Garden and eat therein whatever you wish, but do not approach this tree, lest you be of the wrongdoers.
> — **Quran 2:35 (inline widget)**

Imam al-Hunayd (peace be upon him) settled Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ in the Garden, which signifies the station of prophethood — a station linked to revelation, pure, subtle, and real. This blessing surpasses every blessing, and its sweetness surpasses every sweetness. For this reason the world holds no value in the eyes of one who has been wholly drawn into it: the greatest and most precious of worldly commodities counts for nothing in his sight. He feels that this knowledge is the true bounty of God, a delight that will never be exhausted, a wealth that will never run out, an honour that will never be abased. All the further stages of the daʿwa are likewise called ⟪ar:’جنت‘⟫.

That is: Imam al-Hunayd (peace be upon him) appointed Ādam as a prophet and Hābīl as his caliph, granting them leave to read, draw benefit from, and confer benefit upon all that lay within the bounds of their station, without being called to account. They were to administer the rulings of the lawful and the unlawful, establish the disciplines of worship, and discharge every matter pertaining to prophethood. But —

| {{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_open.gif}}And do not approach this tree, for you will be one of the wrongdoers{{IMG_URL:.. /images/quote_closed.gif}} |
| --- |

— do not approach this tree, lest you be of the wrongdoers. That is: though I have permitted you all things, do not step beyond the bounds of your station. This ⟪ar:’شجرہ ممنوعۃ‘⟫ was the limit which Ādam and Hābīl were forbidden to cross — namely, ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫. They were told: do not encroach upon the office of ⟪ar:قائم القیامۃ⟫, do not lay hands upon it. Your bounds are confined to the works of prophethood; the function of ⟪ar:مولانا صاحب القیامۃ⟫ is to unveil the inner reality and to make the bāṭin manifest. Those secrets must not be disclosed during the seventy cycles before their appointed time of manifestation. Only the Qāʾim (peace be upon him) holds the right to raise the burden of ⟪ar:اعمال تکلیفیہ⟫ — to accept or reject — and none before him; for he and his successors are the ⟪ar:’مالک یوم الدین‘⟫. After their appearance, belief alone will profit no one; in their hands the science of the concealed will be made manifest. Whoever divulges before the time will be called a wrongdoer (⟪ar:فَتَکُونَا مِنَ الظَّالِمِینَ⟫). For this reason Imam al-Hunayd forbade them both at the very outset of the seventy cycles, that no alteration might enter the sunna and the established order of God.

## Iblīs's Challenge to God

Iblīs (the Devil), once cursed and cast down, did not retreat in silence. He sought, instead, a stay of judgement — a postponement reaching to the day designated by God Himself, ⟪ar:’یوم وقت معلوم‘⟫. His petition is preserved in the Qurʾān:

> ⟪quran 7:14-15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ أَنظِرْنِىٓ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ ‎ ‏قَالَ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنظَرِينَ ‎⟫
> *(Iblîs) said: "Allow me respite till the Day they are raised up (i.e. the Day of Resurrection)." (Allah) said: "You are of those allowed respite."*
> said, 'Reprieve me till the day when they will be resurrected.' He said, 'You are reprieved.'
> — **Quran 7:14-15 (inline widget)**

In taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis), the phrase ⟪ar:’یوم یبعثون‘⟫ does not denote some distant cosmic terminus. It signifies, rather, the manifestation of ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫ — the Lord of the Resurrection, in whose cycle the inward truths of religion are at last unveiled.[^cite-11] Until that appointed advent, Iblīs is granted his interval. He uses it at once to declare a programme of seduction:

> ⟪quran 7:16-17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ فَبِمَآ أَغْوَيْتَنِى لَأَقْعُدَنَّ لَهُمْ صِرَطَكَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ ‎ ‏ثُمَّ لَآتِيَنَّهُم مِّنۢ بَيْنِ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ وَعَنْ أَيْمَنِهِمْ وَعَن شَمَآئِلِهِمْ ۖ وَلَا تَجِدُ أَكْثَرَهُمْ شَكِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *(Iblîs) said: "Because You have sent me astray, surely I will sit in wait against them (human beings) on Your Straight Path. Then I will come to them from before them and behind them, from their right and from their left, and You will not find most of them as thankful ones (i.e.&nbsp;they will not be dutiful to You)."*
> said, As You have put me in error, I will sit in wait for them on Your straight path, then I will come to them before them, and behind them, and from their right and from their left, and You will not find most of them grateful.
> — **Quran 7:16-17 (inline widget)**

The speaker here — ⟪ar:حارث بن مرۃ⟫ in the symbolic register of the discourse — addresses Imām al-Hunayd (peace be upon him), saying in effect: since you have barred my ascent along ⟪ar:صراط مستقیم⟫ (the straight path) within the ranks of prophethood, I shall lie in wait at every approach to that same path, hindering the descendants of Ādam until the rising of the Qāʾim. From before and behind, from right and left, I shall press upon them; and only the few will pass through. The geometry of the assault is total, but its limit is preordained: it endures only for the cycle of concealment.[^cite-12]

> ⟪quran 17:62⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ أَرَءَيْتَكَ هَذَا ٱلَّذِى كَرَّمْتَ عَلَىَّ لَىِٕنْ أَخَّرْتَنِ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْقِيَمَةِ لَأَحْتَنِكَنَّ ذُرِّيَّتَهُۥٓ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *[Iblîs (Satan)] said: "See? This one whom You have honoured above me, if You give me respite (keep me alive) to the Day of Resurrection, I will surely seize and mislead his offspring (by sending them astray) all but a few!"*
> said, 'Have you considered?' If You reprieve him whom You have added above me, I will surely seize his seed, except for a few.
> — **Quran 17:62 (inline widget)**

This second declaration sharpens the first into a personal vendetta. Iblīs concedes, in the act of cursing, the very rank he wishes to deny — that Ādam has been honoured above him. He resolves, in compensation, that none of Ādam's progeny shall reach the dignity from which he himself was cast down. The challenge to Imām al-Hunayd is unambiguous: the loss is mine because of Ādam, and I shall make it a loss shared.

> ⟪quran 4:119-120⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَأُضِلَّنَّهُمْ وَلَأُمَنِّيَنَّهُمْ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُبَتِّكُنَّ آذَانَ ٱلْأَنْعَمِ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُغَيِّرُنَّ خَلْقَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَمَن يَتَّخِذِ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ وَلِيًّۭا مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ فَقَدْ خَسِرَ خُسْرَانًۭا مُّبِينًۭا ‎ ‏يَعِدُهُمْ وَيُمَنِّيهِمْ ۖ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎⟫
> *Verily, I will mislead them, and surely, I will arouse in them false desires; and certainly, I will order them to slit the ears of cattle, and indeed I will order them to change the nature created by Allah." And whoever takes Shaitan (Satan) as a Walî (protector or helper) instead of Allah, has surely suffered a manifest loss. He [Shaitan (Satan)] makes promises to them, and arouses in them false desires; and Shaitan's (Satan) promises are nothing but deceptions.*
> And I will lead them astray, and I will surely give them hopes, and I will command them to slit the ears of cattle, and I will command them to change God's creation. And whoever takes Satan for a friend instead of God has surely fallen into manifest loss. Satan promises them and gives them hopes, and Satan promises them nothing but delusion.
> — **Quran 4:119-120 (inline widget)**

What Iblīs proposes, in plain terms, is a counter-daʿwa (the summons / the invitation). He will erect his sovereignty against the sovereignty of the Imām, his summons against the divine summons, until the Qāʾim appears. The instruments are familiar to anyone who studies the patient logic of misguidance: false hope, manufactured longing, kindled hatred for the saints of God, and where possible their killing. He boasts that the majority of God's servants will defect to him.[^cite-13] The Imām, peace be upon him, accepts the challenge with the calm of one who already knows its terms:

> ⟪quran 7:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱخْرُجْ مِنْهَا مَذْءُومًۭا مَّدْحُورًۭا ۖ لَّمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ لَأَمْلَأَنَّ جَهَنَّمَ مِنكُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said (to Iblîs) "Get out from this (Paradise) disgraced and expelled.&nbsp;Whoever of them (mankind) will follow you, then surely I will fill Hell with you all."*
> said, Get out of here, disgraced and disgraced. Whoever obeys them, I will fill you all in Hell.
> — **Quran 7:18 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 17:63-65⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱذْهَبْ فَمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ فَإِنَّ جَهَنَّمَ جَزَآؤُكُمْ جَزَآءًۭ مَّوْفُورًۭا ‎ ‏وَٱسْتَفْزِزْ مَنِ ٱسْتَطَعْتَ مِنْهُم بِصَوْتِكَ وَأَجْلِبْ عَلَيْهِم بِخَيْلِكَ وَرَجِلِكَ وَشَارِكْهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَمْوَلِ وَٱلْأَوْلَدِ وَعِدْهُمْ ۚ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎ ‏إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَنٌۭ ۚ وَكَفَىٰ بِرَبِّكَ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "Go, and whosoever of them follows you, surely! Hell will be the recompense of you (all) an ample recompense. "And Istafziz [literally means: befool them gradually] those whom you can among them with your voice (i.e. songs, music, and any other call for Allah's disobedience), make assaults on them with your cavalry and your infantry, mutually share with them wealth and children (by tempting them to earn money by illegal ways usury, etc., or by committing illegal sexual intercourse,  etc.), and make promises to them." But Satan promises them nothing but deceit. "Verily! My slaves (i.e the true believers of Islamic Monotheism), you have no authority over them. And All-Sufficient is your Lord as a Guardian."*
> said. Whosoever of them is with you, then Hell is your recompense, an ample recompense; seduce whomsoever of them you can with your voice, and rally against them your cavalry and your infantry, and share with them in wealth and children, and make promises to them, and the promises of Satan are nothing but delusion; surely you have no power over My servants. And your Lord suffices as a Protector.
> — **Quran 17:63-65 (inline widget)**

⟪ar:امام ھنید علیہ السلام⟫ replies, in effect: go, then, and do what you must — but understand the terms. Whoever follows you shares your dwelling and your full measure of recompense. The "voice" by which Iblīs unsettles men is interpreted as the eloquence of his rhetoric: the seductive speeches by which he intoxicates the mind. The "cavalry and infantry" are the ranks of his followers, marshalled in support of his summons. The "wealth" in which he proposes to share is not coin but knowledge — the inward riches he poisons by sowing doubt, so that the believer mistrusts what he has been taught. The "children" are the believers themselves, whom he labours to estrange from love for the true Imāms.[^cite-14] Whomever he overpowers passes out of the circle of faith. Whomever he merely promises he deceives. But against the *mukhlaṣūn* — the sincere servants — he has no purchase whatever. The Imām's reservation is absolute.

Elsewhere the same exchange is preserved with slightly different inflection, drawing out the divine recognition that the path leading back to Him is itself "upon Him":

> ⟪quran 15:36-42⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ رَبِّ فَأَنظِرْنِىٓ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ ‎ ‏قَالَ فَإِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنظَرِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْوَقْتِ ٱلْمَعْلُومِ ‎ ‏قَالَ رَبِّ بِمَآ أَغْوَيْتَنِى لَأُزَيِّنَنَّ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَأُغْوِيَنَّهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَّا عِبَادَكَ مِنْهُمُ ٱلْمُخْلَصِينَ ‎ ‏قَالَ هَذَا صِرَطٌ عَلَىَّ مُسْتَقِيمٌ ‎ ‏إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَنٌ إِلَّا مَنِ ٱتَّبَعَكَ مِنَ ٱلْغَاوِينَ ‎⟫
> *[Iblîs (Satan)] said: "O my Lord! Give me then respite till the Day they (the dead) will be resurrected." Allah said: "Then, verily, you are of those reprieved, "Till the Day of the time appointed." [Iblîs (Satan)] said: "O my Lord! Because you misled me, I shall indeed adorn the path of error for them (mankind) on the earth, and I shall mislead them all. "Except Your chosen, (guided) slaves among them." (Allah) said: "This is the Way which will lead straight to Me." "Certainly, you shall have no authority over My slaves, except those who follow you of the Ghawîn (Mushrikun and those who go astray, criminals, polytheists, and evil-doers, etc.).*
> said, "O my Lord! Then grant me respite till the Day of Resurrection, and he said, "Surely, You have respite till the appointed Day." As Thou hast taken me astray, I will surely make them appear to them on earth their sins, and I will lead them astray, except those of Thy servants who are sincere among them. He said, This is a straight path for Me; surely Thou wilt not avail Thou wilt not save those of My servants who follow Thee from among the astray.
> — **Quran 15:36-42 (inline widget)**

Thus the respite is granted, and the long trial of the descendants of Ādam is set in motion. He who falls into the snare is no longer a believer, having departed from the protection of the daʿwa. Yet the door of return is not bolted. The one whom Satan seduces and tempts, who slips at the edge of the pit — if he catches himself before the fall, recognises the whisper for what it is, regrets, repents, and asks forgiveness with sincerity — that one does not pass into Iblīs's company; he is restored to the ranks of the servants of God. Two summonses now circulate in the world side by side: the merciful daʿwa, and the satanic daʿwa. The children of Ādam divide accordingly. One body becomes the believers, the other the hypocrites who walk behind the devils. The followers of Ādam are named "servants of God," and over them Iblīs strains and fails:

> ⟪quran 58:19⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱسْتَحْوَذَ عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَأَنسَىٰهُمْ ذِكْرَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ هُمُ ٱلْخَسِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *&nbsp;Shaitan (Satan) has overtaken them. So he has made them forget the remembrance of Allah. They are the party of Shaitan (Satan). Verily, it is the party of Shaitan (Satan) that will be the losers!*
> Satan has got the better of them, and he has made them forget the remembrance of Allah. They are Satan's faction. Unquestionably, Satan's party will be the losers.
> — **Quran 58:19 (inline widget)**

In Sūrat Ibrāhīm, the Qurʾān stages the eschatological coda: when judgement has been rendered, Iblīs himself disowns those he led, and disavows the partnership they ascribed to him:

> ⟪quran 14:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لَمَّا قُضِىَ ٱلْأَمْرُ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ وَعَدَكُمْ وَعْدَ ٱلْحَقِّ وَوَعَدتُّكُمْ فَأَخْلَفْتُكُمْ ۖ وَمَا كَانَ لِىَ عَلَيْكُم مِّن سُلْطَنٍ إِلَّآ أَن دَعَوْتُكُمْ فَٱسْتَجَبْتُمْ لِى ۖ فَلَا تَلُومُونِى وَلُومُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَكُم ۖ مَّآ أَنَا۠ بِمُصْرِخِكُمْ وَمَآ أَنتُم بِمُصْرِخِىَّ ۖ إِنِّى كَفَرْتُ بِمَآ أَشْرَكْتُمُونِ مِن قَبْلُ ۗ إِنَّ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And Shaitan (Satan) will say when the matter has been decided: "Verily, Allah promised you a promise of truth. And I too promised you, but I betrayed you. I had no authority over you except that I called you, so you responded to me. So blame me not, but blame yourselves. I cannot help you, nor can you help me. I deny your former act in associating me (Satan) as a partner with Allah (by obeying me in the life of the world). Verily, there is a painful torment for the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers, etc.)."*
> And when the matter has been decided, Satan will say, "God promised you the promise of truth, and I made a promise to you, but I broke my promise, and I had no authority over you except that I called you, and you obeyed me, so do not blame me, and do not blame yourselves. I am not your protector or your helper." Woe to me! I am innocent of what you used to associate with Me aforetime. Verily for the wrongdoers is a painful torment.
> — **Quran 14:22 (inline widget)**

Against this dismal portrait the Qurʾān sets the community of sincere believers — those whom the devils cannot deceive, for whom faith has been inscribed upon the very tablet of the heart:

> ⟪quran 58:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَّا تَجِدُ قَوْمًۭا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ يُوَآدُّونَ مَنْ حَآدَّ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ وَلَوْ كَانُوٓا۟ آبَآءَهُمْ أَوْ أَبْنَآءَهُمْ أَوْ إِخْوَنَهُمْ أَوْ عَشِيرَتَهُمْ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَتَبَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمُ ٱلْإِيمَنَ وَأَيَّدَهُم بِرُوحٍۢ مِّنْهُ ۖ وَيُدْخِلُهُمْ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَرُ خَلِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ رَضِىَ ٱللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا۟ عَنْهُ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱللَّهِ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *You (O Muhammad PBUH) will not find any people who believe in Allah and the Last Day, making friendship with those who oppose Allah and His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH ), even though they were their fathers, or their sons, or their brothers, or their kindred (people). For such He has written Faith in their hearts, and strengthened them with Ruh (proofs, light and true guidance) from Himself. And We will admit them to Gardens (Paradise) under which rivers flow, to dwell therein (forever). Allah is pleased with them, and they with Him. They are the Party of Allah. Verily, it is the Party of Allah that will be the successful.*
> thou wilt not find any people who believe in Allah and the Last Day, and befriend those who oppose Allah and His Messenger, even though they be their fathers, or their sons, or their brothers, or their families. He will admit them to Gardens beneath which rivers flow, where they will abide for ever. Allah is pleased with them, and they are well-pleased with Him. That is the party of Allah. Unquestionably, it is the party of Allah who will prosper.
> — **Quran 58:22 (inline widget)**

From this moment onward, Iblīs's enmity with Ādam becomes inheritable. It passes from age to age. Ādam, having asked forgiveness, was forgiven; Iblīs, having held fast to arrogance, was cursed and expelled, and his appetite for vengeance grew the keener. He pursued every prophet, every nabī, every waṣī (executor), and every Imām with open hostility, deploying whatever means lay to hand to weaken them.[^cite-15] The Qurʾān states the pattern openly:

> ⟪quran 6:112⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And so We have appointed for every Prophet enemies - Shayatin (devils) among mankind and jinns, inspiring one another with adorned speech as a delusion (or by way of deception).&nbsp;If your Lord had so willed, they would not have done it, so leave them alone with their fabrications. (Tafseer Qurtubi, Vol.7, Page 67)*
> Thus have We made for every prophet an enemy, the evil man, and the jinn, who teach one another deceitful talk. Had your Lord willed, they would not have done it. So leave them to what they fabricate.
> — **Quran 6:112 (inline widget)**

He who follows the devil is guilty. He who sins and then repents in sincerity returns to the company of God. But he who couples sin with denial, with arrogance, and with stubborn persistence — these being the very signatures of Iblīs — that person becomes the supreme Iblīs of his own age, an enemy of the Prophet in the most precise sense the tradition allows.

In sum: Iblīs refused, retreated, and fell — because of arrogance. Those who shared his temperament obeyed him, and were of his kind from of old. The trial of the people of the daʿwa intensified from every side. First the *muḥtajab* (the veiled ranks) were pressed, then the *ḥijāb* (the screen of guidance) itself. The inquisitors went into total concealment; no news of them or of their forefathers reached the wider world. The condition of the visible ḥijāb was different. Sometimes its bearers came forth into view; sometimes they had to withdraw.

## Ādam's Being Deceived (Eating from the Forbidden Tree)

God Most High says:

> ⟪quran 7:20⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَلِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered suggestions to them both in order to uncover that which was hidden from them of their private parts (before); he said: "Your Lord did not forbid you this tree save you should become angels or become of the immortals." *
> Satan whispered to them, in order to reveal to them that which they had hidden from one another, and said, Your Lord has not forbidden you from this tree except that you may become angels or become immortals.
> — **Quran 7:20 (inline widget)**

It is related in the tradition that Ḥārith ibn Murra entered the Garden in the guise of a serpent — a creature outwardly meek yet inwardly charged with lethal venom. Adopting this very posture, he approached Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ with feigned cordiality, draping enmity in the garment of friendship. Knowing that Ādam's resolve could not be unsettled by direct speech, he turned his whisper toward Ḥawwāʾ. He insinuated that the rightly-guiding Imam had withheld from her the highest reach of knowledge so that she might not attain the upper limit (the ḥadd ʿālī) and merit the standing rank of permanence. The Imam, he claimed, did not wish to grant her the eternity that was her rightful inheritance. Bodily action, he added, is the burden of the inferior limits; were she to set it aside, she would rise to the level of ⟪ar:’ملائکہ علیین‘⟫ and become a vessel of ⟪ar:علم روحانی لطیف⟫. She would then merit the rank of ⟪ar:مَلَک⟫, find her every desire fulfilled, and dwell in the everlasting abode. With these enticements he sealed his deception by a false oath.[^cite-16]

> ⟪quran 7:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَاسَمَهُمَآ إِنِّى لَكُمَا لَمِنَ ٱلنَّصِحِينَ ‎⟫
> *And he [Shaitan (Satan)] swore by Allah to them both (saying): "Verily, I am one of the sincere well&shy;wishers for you both." *
> And he swore to them, "Indeed, I am your well-wisher."
> — **Quran 7:21 (inline widget)**

Mawlānā ʿAlī teaches that Ādam was deceived because the deceiver swore. Falsehood had no foothold in the intellectual horizon of Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ; they perceived only the true, and even the imagination of untruth lay outside the range of their cognition. When Iblīs swore, therefore, they could not but credit him. A false oath, in their experience, was simply not among the possibilities of being.

> ⟪quran 7:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَدَلَّىٰهُمَا بِغُرُورٍۢ ۚ فَلَمَّا ذَاقَا ٱلشَّجَرَةَ بَدَتْ لَهُمَا سَوْءَتُهُمَا وَطَفِقَا يَخْصِفَانِ عَلَيْهِمَا مِن وَرَقِ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ وَنَادَىٰهُمَا رَبُّهُمَآ أَلَمْ أَنْهَكُمَا عَن تِلْكُمَا ٱلشَّجَرَةِ وَأَقُل لَّكُمَآ إِنَّ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ لَكُمَا عَدُوٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *So he misled them with deception.&nbsp;Then when they tasted of the tree, that which was hidden from them of their shame (private parts) became manifest to them and they began to stick together the leaves of Paradise over themselves (in order to cover their shame).&nbsp;And their Lord called out to them (saying):  "Did I not forbid you that tree and tell you: Verily, Shaitan (Satan) is an open enemy unto you?" *
> Then he deceived them. And when they tasted of the tree, their shame became manifest to them, and they began to cover themselves with the leaves of the Garden. And their Lord called out to them. Did I not forbid you from that tree, and told you that Satan was to you a manifest enemy?
> — **Quran 7:22 (inline widget)**

At Ḥawwāʾ's urging, Ādam tasted of the tree. In the language of taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis), he overstepped the bounds proper to prophethood and intervened in the office of the ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫, extending his hand into a precinct from which he had been forbidden. The instant he opened that book and read, his nakedness was disclosed. Those sciences lay so high above his measured capacity that he could not contain them, and at once he and Ḥawwāʾ became aware of their poverty and intellectual want: having reckoned themselves wise, they discovered themselves wanting in these very disciplines. The displeasure of the Imam descended upon them; his taʾyīd (the divine aid that sustains the office of guidance) and the revelation that accompanied it were withdrawn, the robe of righteous standing was lifted, and the luminous mantle that had veiled the imperfection of their material density was set aside. While the light of taʾyīd held, even the pebble glowed like the sun and every science yielded itself willingly to them — they had explained subtle matters to others with ease. Once that support was severed, the lesser sciences fled their grasp, and their tongues could no longer find utterance for instruction. From every quarter the critics now arrived. So it remains: when a person attains a high station, the world reveres him; when he is unseated, the tongues of those around him are loosened, and his humiliation becomes their occupation. Ādam reached for the leaves of the Garden — that is, he sought to draw knowledge from his lower limits (the ḥadd dānī, the lower limit) — but none would teach him. Only the fig extended its mercy, offering its leaves to clothe his shame; by this God was pleased, and He united the outward and the inward of the fig.[^cite-17]

It is narrated in the Torah that when Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ stood naked in the Garden and wandered in terror, they heard God's footfall among the thickets and concealed themselves in shame. A voice called: "Where are you?" They answered, "We are naked. When we heard the sound of Your footsteps, we hid in shame." The Lord said, "Alas — you have eaten of the tree." Ādam said, "The woman fed me." The woman said, "The serpent counselled me." The serpent said, "Iblīs entered into my belly."

The Imam said to him: "I forbade you these sciences precisely because you were not yet able to receive them — not because I did not wish you to attain them. And I told you that Satan was your enemy, yet you obeyed him and disobeyed me."

**Statement.** Ḥaḍrat Ḍiyāʾ ʿAlī Ṣāḥib used to say that every parent desires the finest food for their child; yet to a newborn they feed nothing but milk, knowing that the infant cannot yet digest substantive nutriment. The moment that capacity ripens, they place richer fare before him. Both the withholding and the feeding serve the child's good.

Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him), describing the steadfastness of the believer, says that none among our intimates will walk on both feet without slipping; yet should one foot slip, the other will hold firm. The believer will err, but he will not pass out of faith. He will plead forgiveness the instant he transgresses. On this principle, Iblīs seduced Ādam Ṣafīy Allāh but could not annex him. Ādam fell into the snare of flattery, and the moment he perceived the snare, he withdrew at once into regret and repentance.[^cite-18]

## Ādam's Expulsion from the Garden

If one examines the story of Ādam's expulsion in its outward (ẓāhir) sense alone, a thicket of difficulties arises. If the tree whose fruit was forbidden Ādam was good and lawful, why was its fruit barred to him? If the tree was forbidden, how could it stand in the Garden at all? When God made every other thing in the Garden lawful for him, why this single exception? Would it not have been simpler had God never caused the tree to grow there, especially since His knowledge of the unseen already encompassed the suffering this tree would bring upon Ādam? Some exegetes of the outward dimension (ahl al-ẓāhir) identify the tree as ⟪ar:’گیہوں‘⟫ (wheat). Yet if so, why was wheat forbidden to Ādam alone, when it is licit for all his descendants, believers and unbelievers alike? Other commentators reply that Ādam had no intestines and lacked ⟪ar:قوت ھاضمۃ⟫ (the digestive faculty); the fruit was therefore prohibited lest it putrefy in his belly and torment him. But if this answer be accepted, why were the remaining fruits lawful? They too would descend into the same belly and produce the same distress. A further question presses: why is Ādam's transgression visited upon his children? The error belonged to the father, not to his offspring. Some answer that God knew in advance that Ādam's descendants would repeat the lapse and so withheld the Garden from them. But then: did God not know in advance that Ādam himself would lapse? If He knew, why create him in the Garden at all? Would He not, on this reasoning, have created Ādam too in the world below, as He did his descendants?

To these difficulties the author of the book offers two orders of answer: one pertaining to the Ādam of this world, the other to Ādam (Asher) of the eternal world.

---

### Ādam (ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm)

Drawing on the knowledge of the Imams, the author observes that the tree admits two interpretations, one favourable and one inauspicious.

**The Tree (in the favourable sense).**

The devil said to Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ:

> ⟪quran 7:20⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَلِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered suggestions to them both in order to uncover that which was hidden from them of their private parts (before); he said: "Your Lord did not forbid you this tree save you should become angels or become of the immortals." *
> Satan whispered to them, in order to reveal to them that which they had hidden from one another, and said, Your Lord has not forbidden you from this tree except that you may become angels or become immortals.
> — **Quran 7:20 (inline widget)**

In its noble reading, the tree denotes ⟪ar:’علوم حقائق‘⟫ — the sciences of the realities, forbidden to those who are not yet deserving. These were in truth the knowledge of the Imams: knowledge that bestows eternity and releases the soul from the mortal realm. Without obedience to the Imams and adhesion to their daʿwa (the summons, the invitation to true guidance), no acquisition or comprehension of these sciences is possible; they are disclosed to their proper beneficiaries when their time has ripened. In a still higher reading, the tree signifies the ranks of the ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ and of ⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ — the particular realities pertaining to the rising of the Qāʾim, realities veiled from countless eyes, which were forbidden to Ādam. They were forbidden because his station stood at the opening of a cycle of seventy, within which the sciences proper to the period of unveiling (kashf) cannot be opened.[^cite-19]

Iblīs deceived Ādam, learned the identity of his successor (Hābīl), and then provoked Qābīl into envy of his brother. Qābīl thereupon slew Hābīl. The killing occurred first inwardly, on the plane of meanings, and only afterwards in its outward form.

**The Tree (in the inauspicious sense).**

In another sense, the ⟪ar:’درخت‘⟫ was Iblīs himself. To the objection — if the tree was evil, what was it doing in the Garden? — the answer is that Iblīs himself had been a great dāʿī (Summoner) and the ⟪ar:’معلم الملکوت‘⟫, instructor of all the angels. When he refused to prostrate before Ādam, he was cast out from that station. On this reading, the prohibition on eating the fruit means that Ādam was forbidden to disclose to Iblīs any secret of the daʿwa, since Iblīs's disobedience had drawn the curse upon him.

**Why were the children of Ādam deprived of the Garden?**

The author replies that the Garden is, in one sense, the ⟪ar-quote:دار الدعوۃ⟫ — the house of the summons — whose aim is the attainment of the real Garden. The Garden of Ādam was, in truth, a lofty station within the ⟪ar:دار الدعوۃ⟫. For every higher rank is a Garden to the rank beneath it, and every lower rank is the earth (the world) to the rank above it. The highest limit of these ranks is the real Garden; the lowest is the mortal world. Ādam had been created in a high station, the station of ⟪ar:’تائیدات‘⟫ — that is, the rank of those who receive ⟪ar:تائیدات⟫ from above, which constituted the Garden proper to him. From there he was demoted to the rank of ⟪ar:’تعلیمات ‘⟫, and downward, until he reached the rank of the respondent (mustajīb), which is the lowest of the recognised ranks though still not excluded altogether.[^cite-20]

The rank of instruction (taʿlīm) belongs to the ⟪ar:مفیدین⟫, whose office is to instruct the ⟪ar:مستجیبین⟫, to bring them to obey the commands of the sharīʿa, and to raise them through the ranks (the Garden). The ⟪ar:علم باطن⟫ is neither lawful nor permissible at the rank of the mustajīb; at this station, only obedience to the commands of the sharīʿa is binding. The respondent must obey the authority above him without hesitation, complying with the commands of the law whether or not he grasps the rationale offered by his benefactor. When the benefactor (the mufīd) sees that the respondent has observed the commands of the sharīʿa with rigour, he raises him to ranks in which the inner science (al-ʿilm al-bāṭin) of those very commands is unveiled to him. The son of a benefactor is a benefactor in turn.

The descendants of Ādam are the descendants of those ⟪ar:مطبعین⟫, all of whom stand at the rank of the mustajīb, whose station is this earth. And as already noted, the mustajībūn are not the people of the Garden; the earth, therefore, is their dwelling. This is why the children of Ādam were not created in the Garden but in this world.[^cite-21]

**Facts.** It is stated in the realities (ḥaqāʾiq) that a prophet cannot be a wrongdoer. Outwardly, Ādam (peace be upon him) committed an error; the one who errs is a wrongdoer, and the abode of wrongdoers is the Fire. By this reasoning Ādam (peace be upon him) could not be a prophet — yet the truth is that Ādam had a company of sinners upon whom this retribution was to be fulfilled. Ādam's lower veil erred, and that veil had to descend to the isles; his higher veil remained among the noble. In every cycle of seventy there appears a manifestation of Ādam, and those manifestations err. (Mullā Jāwid Ḍiyāʾī)

### Ādam Rūḥānī (the Second Emanator)

The second exposition of this story belongs to the world of eternity. There, the reference is to the Ādam who was brought forth as an intellect — the ⟪ar:’منبعث ثانی‘⟫ (the second of al-Munbaʿithūn, the Emanators). His Garden was the spiritual world in which he dwelt with the other intellects. The tree was the rank of the first Emanator (al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal), to whose station and equality he had no right of approach, for the first Emanator surpassed him by virtue of priority and nobility. The devil was, in this register, his own corrupt conception — the superstitious fancy of equality that incited him to revolt and reach toward parity with the first. Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High), his Creator, had forbidden him precisely this: to claim equality, to nurse the conceit of rank-parity.[^cite-22]

The duty of the second Emanator was to obey his predecessor (the first Emanator) just as the first Emanator had obeyed the one before him, al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect). To eat of the tree means, in this taʾwīl, to rebel against this office and to seek equality with the rank of the first. For this transgression he was expelled from the Garden and fell from his station: seven intellects passed beyond him, even though he had excelled in the affirmation of God's unity. These seven sayings are in truth the words of God (the first Emanator) which the Qurʾān mentions in these terms:

> ⟪quran 2:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَتٍۢ فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Then Adam received from his Lord Words. And his Lord pardoned him (accepted his repentance). Verily, He is the One Who forgives (accepts repentance), the Most Merciful.*
> Adam received certain words from his Lord, and He relented toward Him. Indeed, He is the Receiver of Repentance, the Merciful.
> — **Quran 2:37 (inline widget)**

Through these seven sayings Ādam repented to his Lord (the first Emanator); the repentance was accepted, and he was returned to the Garden — that is, restored to the ranks. His descendants are those intellects who had imitated him in the spiritual world and shared his claim to equality. All of them, together with the devil-spirit, were expelled from the spiritual world and cast into the world of becoming. Iblīs alone, on account of his obstinacy, was never restored to the Garden; he was cursed without remission.[^cite-23]

The hatred of Iblīs for Ādam and his descendants — the company of the regretful — displays itself as opposition and stubbornness. Those descendants of Ādam who had been in the first eternal world were elevated and restored to the Garden, as their father Ādam (Asher) had been. Those who obeyed Iblīs were cast into the Fire alongside their leader. This Fire is, to them, the world that is real.

## Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ's Repentance

The moment the consequences of their lapse settled upon them, Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ recognized their error and turned at once in repentance to God:

> ⟪quran 7:23⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَا رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَآ أَنفُسَنَا وَإِن لَّمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ ٱلْخَسِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *They said: "Our Lord! We have wronged ourselves. If You forgive us not, and bestow not upon us Your Mercy, we shall certainly be of the losers." *
> They said, Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves. If You do not forgive us and have mercy on us, we will surely be among the losers.
> — **Quran 7:23 (inline widget)**

They confessed, "Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves beyond the limit set for us and disobeyed You; if You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we shall surely be among the losers." Neither persisted in the sin; both acknowledged the offence and pleaded for pardon, repentance, and forgiveness.

Here the contrast between Iblīs and Ādam becomes luminous. Both committed an error, but Iblīs entrenched himself in his refusal and grew obstinate, while Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ turned back immediately and asked to be forgiven. The difference is not the lapse itself but the disposition of the soul that follows it: humility opens a door that pride bolts shut.[^cite-24]

The Imām, peace be upon him, then declared to them:

> ⟪quran 2:36⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَأَزَلَّهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ عَنْهَا فَأَخْرَجَهُمَا مِمَّا كَانَا فِيهِ ۖ وَقُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *Then the Shaitan (Satan) made them slip therefrom (the Paradise), and got them out from that in which they were. We said: "Get you down, all, with enmity between yourselves. On earth will be a dwelling place for you and an enjoyment for a time." *
> Then Satan caused them to slip from it, and brought them forth from the state in which they were, and We said, 'Get you down, one of you enemies to the other; and on the earth shall be your abode and enjoyment for a time.
> — **Quran 2:36 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 7:24-25⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎ ‏قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "Get down, one of you an enemy to the other [i.e. Adam, Hawwa (Eve), and Shaitan (Satan), etc.]. On earth will be a dwelling-place for you and an enjoyment, - for a time." He said: "Therein you shall live, and therein you shall die, and from it you shall be brought out (i.e.resurrected)." *
> said, 'Get you down, one of you will be enemies to the other, and on the earth is your abode and enjoyment for a time.' He said, 'Therein you shall live, and in it you shall die, and from it you shall be brought forth.'
> — **Quran 7:24-25 (inline widget)**

In the taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis), the Imām dismissed them from his proximate abode — the rank of the spiritual nobility — and sent them down to the islands of the corporeal world. Among the ⟪ar:’حضرات‘⟫ (presences) they had possessed taʾyīdī (divinely-supported) knowledge that needed no reading or writing; a question scarcely formed before the answer arrived from the Imām. In the ⟪ar:’جزائر‘⟫ (islands), however, knowledge runs through a graduated chain of teaching, demanding effort, patience, and slow ascent. This was the consequence of their lapse. The Imām said, in effect: We extended Our taʾyīd to you and you did not measure its worth; descend now to the islands, labour step by step, and learn the value of sweet water by first tasting the brine.

> ⟪quran 2:37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَتٍۢ فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Then Adam received from his Lord Words. And his Lord pardoned him (accepted his repentance). Verily, He is the One Who forgives (accepts repentance), the Most Merciful.*
> Adam received certain words from his Lord, and He relented toward Him. Indeed, He is the Receiver of Repentance, the Merciful.
> — **Quran 2:37 (inline widget)**

When the Imām imposed this consequence, Ādam learned through his repentance — by the mediation of the ⟪ar:’ملائکہ علیین‘⟫ (angels of the highest realm) — the ⟪ar:کلمات⟫ through which nearness could be regained, and the names by which the lost paradise (the station of prophethood) could be recovered. Ḥaḍrat Ḍiyāʾ Ṣāḥib has explained that those words were in truth the ⟪ar:أسمآء مبارکۃ⟫ of the Panjtan Pāk — ⟪ar:محمد و علی و فاطمۃ و حسن و حسین علیھم السلام⟫. Ādam took recourse to the august names he had earlier encountered in the higher books without then grasping their full weight; through these blessed names he repented and said to the Imām, "We seek forgiveness from you through these holy names." The Imām accepted his repentance and pardoned him — for, indeed, the Imām was the Receiver of Repentance and the Most Merciful in the mode in which God's mercy is mediated to creation.[^cite-25] Through these kalimāt Ādam returned to the Imām, was forgiven, and was restored to the station of prophethood. Iblīs was thereby overcome, and God's promise was fulfilled:

> *"My servants — over them you have no authority, except for the misled who follow you."* (cf. Q 15:42)

This episode delivers a twofold lesson. It does not teach that the sinner is beyond pardon; on the contrary, it announces glad tidings — that when a believer who has fallen turns back, seeks forgiveness, and beseeches the intercession of the friends of God (awliyāʾ), God forgives at once and does not strike him from the party of God (Ḥizb Allāh). Conversely, the believer who sins and refuses to recognize the sin as such, who neither asks forgiveness nor seeks the mediation of the awliyāʾ, departs from Ḥizb Allāh and crosses over into the opposing party, becoming an associate of Iblīs. In short, God has set two conditions for the remission of sins: first, sincere repentance and the seeking of pardon; second, recourse to the friends of God. The mediation of the Messenger and the awliyāʾ in this act of istighfār is indispensable, as God Himself says in Sūrat al-Nisāʾ:

> ⟪quran 4:64⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن رَّسُولٍ إِلَّا لِيُطَاعَ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَلَوْ أَنَّهُمْ إِذ ظَّلَمُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَهُمْ جَآءُوكَ فَٱسْتَغْفَرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَٱسْتَغْفَرَ لَهُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ لَوَجَدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابًۭا رَّحِيمًۭا ‎⟫
> *We sent no Messenger, but to be obeyed by Allah's Leave. If they (hypocrites), when they had been unjust to themselves, had come to you (Muhammad PBUH) and begged Allah's Forgiveness, and the Messenger had begged forgiveness for them: indeed, they would have found Allah All-Forgiving (One Who accepts repentance), Most Merciful.*
> And We did not send any Noble Messenger except to be obeyed by Allah's leave; and if they had come to you when they had wronged themselves, and had asked forgiveness of Allah, and the Messenger had asked forgiveness for them, they would have found Allah Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful.
> — **Quran 4:64 (inline widget)**

## The Descent from the Garden to the Earth

God forgave them and restored them to the station of khilāfa (vicegerency) and imāma, yet withdrew the direct taʾyīd and immediate companionship they had enjoyed. He removed them from the proximity of His court and the Imāms and sent them to the islands; the directives they would receive henceforth would come through intermediaries. Earlier, when they had stood in the court of the Imām, those directives had reached them without mediation. He said:

> ⟪quran 2:38-39⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ مِنْهَا جَمِيعًۭا ۖ فَإِمَّا يَأْتِيَنَّكُم مِّنِّى هُدًۭى فَمَن تَبِعَ هُدَاىَ فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ وَكَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَتِنَآ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *We said: "Get down all of you from this place (the Paradise), then whenever there comes to you Guidance from Me, and whoever follows My Guidance, there shall be no fear on them, nor shall they grieve. But those who disbelieve and belie Our Ayat (proofs, evidences, verses, lessons, signs, revelations, etc.) such are the dwellers of the Fire, they shall abide therein forever.*
> We said, 'Go down from it, all of you; and if there comes to you guidance from Me, then whoever follows My guidance, there shall be no fear on them, nor shall they grieve. And whosoever disbelieves, and denies Our signs, they shall be the inmates of the Fire, abiding therein.
> — **Quran 2:38-39 (inline widget)**

The descent and the resulting enmity were not Iblīs's alone; Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ and their progeny were likewise removed from the noble presences and sent down to the islands. The Imām made clear that, in opposition to Ādam's daʿwa (the summons) in the islands, Iblīs would establish his own satanic counter-summons and would seek to mislead Ādam's children. God's response was decisive: "I shall guide your children through you. Whoever accepts My summons I shall restore to My Paradise; whoever accepts Satan's summons shall remain with him, confined to this world of the islands, never to enter the abode of My people."

> ⟪quran 7:24-25⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎ ‏قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "Get down, one of you an enemy to the other [i.e. Adam, Hawwa (Eve), and Shaitan (Satan), etc.]. On earth will be a dwelling-place for you and an enjoyment, - for a time." He said: "Therein you shall live, and therein you shall die, and from it you shall be brought out (i.e.resurrected)." *
> said, 'Get you down, one of you will be enemies to the other, and on the earth is your abode and enjoyment for a time.' He said, 'Therein you shall live, and in it you shall die, and from it you shall be brought forth.'
> — **Quran 7:24-25 (inline widget)**

The Imām said, "For you and your descendants there shall be a dwelling and a place of subsistence in the islands for a span of time," and then, "In this earth you shall live, in it you shall die, and from it you shall be brought forth." Ādam was thus lowered from the rank of direct taʾyīd, yet his graces were not stripped from him altogether. Now ⟪ar:حدود روحانیہ علویہ (کلمات)⟫ were instituted as the mediating channels. They became Ādam's intermediaries, and Ādam became the intermediary for the ḥudūd below him; the same pattern unfolded through every subsequent cycle. The Prophet, blessings and peace be upon him, said: "I receive revelation from Jibrāʾīl, Jibrāʾīl from Mīkāʾīl, Mīkāʾīl from Isrāfīl, Isrāfīl from the Tablet, and the Tablet from the Pen." That is, revelation now reaches the Nāṭiq (the Speaker-Prophet) through five upper sources, and from the Nāṭiq those sciences pass to the five subordinate ḥudūd of the corporeal hierarchy, and from them to the believers.[^cite-26]

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Upper (ʿAlawī) ḥudūd | Pen (Qalam) | Tablet (Lawḥ) | Jadd | Fatḥ | Khayāl |
| Lower (Sufli) ḥudūd | Asās | Imām | Ḥujja | Naqīb | Janāḥ |

The sixth in each column is the believer who receives from them.

Iblīs became Ādam's adversary, and two opposing daʿwas were established. One was Ādam's summons to truth; the other was Iblīs's summons to falsehood. Whoever maintains his bond with Ādam and the people of Ādam is called walī Allāh and is enrolled in Ḥizb Allāh; whoever denies them, disbelieves in them, and stands against them takes his share in Iblīs, for Satan has overcome him and he has forgotten the remembrance of God. The events of Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ were replayed in their descendants, who took the same two forms. To the present day the human race is split along a single line that cuts in two directions: male and female in body, obedient and disobedient in soul. God promised to settle both on this earth and to admit the repentant to the Garden through repentance and istighfār. In the same breath He warned the hypocrites and polytheists:

> ⟪quran 5:51⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ لَا تَتَّخِذُوا۟ ٱلْيَهُودَ وَٱلنَّصَرَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآءَ ۘ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَآءُ بَعْضٍۢ ۚ وَمَن يَتَوَلَّهُم مِّنكُمْ فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Take not the Jews and the Christians as Auliya' (friends, protectors, helpers, etc.), they are but Auliya' to one another. And if any amongst you takes them as Auliya', then surely he is one of them. Verily, Allah guides not those people who are the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers and unjust).*
> O you who believe! Do not take the Jews and the Christians as allies. They are allies of one another. And whoever of you allies himself with them, then surely he is one of them; and Allah does not guide the wrongdoers.
> — **Quran 5:51 (inline widget)**

Whoever takes someone as his walāya is counted in that one's company. He who takes Satan as walī belongs to Satan and will be reckoned among his sons; he will have no link or kinship with Ādam. For this very reason God severed Nūḥ's son from the household of Nūḥ:

> ⟪quran 11:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَنُوحُ إِنَّهُۥ لَيْسَ مِنْ أَهْلِكَ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ عَمَلٌ غَيْرُ صَلِحٍۢ ۖ فَلَا تَسْـَٔلْنِ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۖ إِنِّىٓ أَعِظُكَ أَن تَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْجَهِلِينَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Nuh (Noah)! Surely, he is not of your family; verily, his work is unrighteous, so ask not of Me that of which you have no knowledge! I admonish you, lest you be one of the ignorants." *

## Qābīl's Envy and Hābīl's Martyrdom (Cain and Abel)

God made Ādam, peace be upon him, His khalīfa over the rest of humankind and designated him as Messenger of God and Ḥujjat Allāh. His progeny grew and spread. In his final years he appointed his son ⟪ar:ھابیل⟫ as his successor. His brother ⟪ar:قابیل⟫ envied him and killed him, calculating that if Hābīl were gone, their father would be forced to install him in his place. When this illusion was not realized, Qābīl set himself against his father Ādam; by persisting in his sin he sowed corruption and discord within the daʿwa and became a helper of Iblīs.[^cite-27] After Hābīl's martyrdom, God granted Ādam another son, ⟪ar:’شیث علیہ السلام‘⟫. Ādam named him ⟪ar:ھبۃ اللہ⟫ — the gift of God — and installed him as his successor in Hābīl's stead, entrusting to him in secret the whole of the sciences of taʾwīl and the realities, together with the affairs of the daʿwa hādiya (the guiding summons). He did not announce this publicly, lest the enemy seize on it; only the upper ḥudūd and the sincere believers were apprised. In this way Ādam, having discharged his obligatory functions, withdrew from the active service of the daʿwa. From Shīth the line of imāma and prophecy continued in his descendants — among them the Prophet Idrīs — and so the path of guidance unfolded in Ādam's progeny until the time of ⟪ar:نوح علیہ السلام⟫.

## Ādam as Symbolic Embodiment (Mamthūl) of the Bismillāh

Ādam, peace be upon him, corresponds to the opening verse of ⟪ar:سورۃ الحمد⟫, for he is the one who inaugurated the cycle of the seven days, and that verse — ⟪ar:’بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم‘⟫ — is recited before every beginning. Moreover, within his title ⟪ar:عبد اللہ⟫ lies the wisdom that he is ⟪ar:بسم اللہ⟫ itself.[^cite-28]

He is the mamthūl (symbolic embodiment) of Sunday among the religious days and the mamthūl of walāya in the Daʿāʾim al-Islām. He is the first whose walāya God made obligatory and for the sake of which He commanded the angels to prostrate. Prostration here signifies ṭāʿa (obedience), which is the very proof of walāya. All prostrated except Iblīs. Walāya was Ādam's test, and Ādam was its mamthūl. The whole of creation is, in truth, in the condition of unbelief without his walāya; whoever does not hold to his walāya derives no benefit from those who come after him. He is the first of the prophets, the messengers, and the imāms of religion, and the father of them all; he is the source of the obligatory walāya that God has made binding.

In the human body he occupies the rank of the sperm-drop that resides in the loins of the father and the womb of the mother before its emergence as the seed of the child, for the constituents of what is to be manifested are dispersed throughout the world. This concentrated essence held in the parents is what is called the ⟪ar:’سلالہ‘⟫ (the selected lineage-extract). The sharīʿa of Muḥammad, which was to emerge six thousand years later, had in him its first foundation and earliest establishment.

ۖ وَلَا تَجِدُ أَكْثَرَهُمْ شَكِرِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَ فَٱهْبِطْ مِنْهَا فَمَا يَكُونُ لَكَ أَن تَتَكَبَّرَ فِيهَا فَٱخْرُجْ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلصَّغِرِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَلَّا تَسْجُدَ إِذْ أَمَرْتُكَ ۖ قَالَ أَنَا۠ خَيْرٌۭ مِّنْهُ خَلَقْتَنِى مِن نَّارٍۢ وَخَلَقْتَهُۥ مِن طِينٍۢ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَ وَمَن يَقْنَطُ مِن رَّحْمَةِ رَبِّهِۦٓ إِلَّا ٱلضَّآلُّونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَنُوحُ إِنَّهُۥ لَيْسَ مِنْ أَهْلِكَ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ عَمَلٌ غَيْرُ صَلِحٍۢ ۖ فَلَا تَسْـَٔلْنِ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۖ إِنِّىٓ أَعِظُكَ أَن تَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْجَهِلِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓإِبْلِيسُ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَن تَسْجُدَ لِمَا خَلَقْتُ بِيَدَىَّ ۖ أَسْتَكْبَرْتَ أَمْ كُنتَ مِنَ ٱلْعَالِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱخْرُجْ مِنْهَا مَذْءُومًۭا مَّدْحُورًۭا ۖ لَّمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ لَأَمْلَأَنَّ جَهَنَّمَ مِنكُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱذْهَبْ فَمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ فَإِنَّ جَهَنَّمَ جَزَآؤُكُمْ جَزَآءًۭ مَّوْفُورًۭا ‎ ‏وَٱسْتَفْزِزْ مَنِ ٱسْتَطَعْتَ مِنْهُم بِصَوْتِكَ وَأَجْلِبْ عَلَيْهِم بِخَيْلِكَ وَرَجِلِكَ وَشَارِكْهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَمْوَلِ وَٱلْأَوْلَدِ وَعِدْهُمْ ۚ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎ ‏إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَنٌۭ ۚ وَكَفَىٰ بِرَبِّكَ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎ ‏قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالَا رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَآ أَنفُسَنَا وَإِن لَّمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ ٱلْخَسِرِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَالُوا۟ سُبْحَنَكَ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَآ إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ مِنْهَا جَمِيعًۭا ۖ فَإِمَّا يَأْتِيَنَّكُم مِّنِّى هُدًۭى فَمَن تَبِعَ هُدَاىَ فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ وَكَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَتِنَآ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏لَا يَمَسُّهُمْ فِيهَا نَصَبٌۭ وَمَا هُم مِّنْهَا بِمُخْرَجِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏لَّا تَجِدُ قَوْمًۭا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ يُوَآدُّونَ مَنْ حَآدَّ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ وَلَوْ كَانُوٓا۟ آبَآءَهُمْ أَوْ أَبْنَآءَهُمْ أَوْ إِخْوَنَهُمْ أَوْ عَشِيرَتَهُمْ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَتَبَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمُ ٱلْإِيمَنَ وَأَيَّدَهُم بِرُوحٍۢ مِّنْهُ ۖ وَيُدْخِلُهُمْ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَرُ خَلِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ رَضِىَ ٱللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا۟ عَنْهُ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱللَّهِ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قُلْنَا لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ ٱسْجُدُوا۟ لِآدَمَ فَسَجَدُوٓا۟ إِلَّآ إِبْلِيسَ أَبَىٰ وَٱسْتَكْبَرَ وَكَانَ مِنَ ٱلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَعَلَّمَ آدَمَ ٱلْأَسْمَآءَ كُلَّهَا ثُمَّ عَرَضَهُمْ عَلَى ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ فَقَالَ أَنۢبِـُٔونِى بِأَسْمَآءِ هَٓؤُلَآءِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقَاسَمَهُمَآ إِنِّى لَكُمَا لَمِنَ ٱلنَّصِحِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لَمَّا قُضِىَ ٱلْأَمْرُ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ وَعَدَكُمْ وَعْدَ ٱلْحَقِّ وَوَعَدتُّكُمْ فَأَخْلَفْتُكُمْ ۖ وَمَا كَانَ لِىَ عَلَيْكُم مِّن سُلْطَنٍ إِلَّآ أَن دَعَوْتُكُمْ فَٱسْتَجَبْتُمْ لِى ۖ فَلَا تَلُومُونِى وَلُومُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَكُم ۖ مَّآ أَنَا۠ بِمُصْرِخِكُمْ وَمَآ أَنتُم بِمُصْرِخِىَّ ۖ إِنِّى كَفَرْتُ بِمَآ أَشْرَكْتُمُونِ مِن قَبْلُ ۗ إِنَّ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقُلْنَا يَٓـَٔادَمُ ٱسْكُنْ أَنتَ وَزَوْجُكَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَكُلَا مِنْهَا رَغَدًا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمَا وَلَا تَقْرَبَا هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةَ فَتَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلَأُضِلَّنَّهُمْ وَلَأُمَنِّيَنَّهُمْ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُبَتِّكُنَّ آذَانَ ٱلْأَنْعَمِ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُغَيِّرُنَّ خَلْقَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَمَن يَتَّخِذِ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ وَلِيًّۭا مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ فَقَدْ خَسِرَ خُسْرَانًۭا مُّبِينًۭا ‎ ‏يَعِدُهُمْ وَيُمَنِّيهِمْ ۖ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن رَّسُولٍ إِلَّا لِيُطَاعَ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَلَوْ أَنَّهُمْ إِذ ظَّلَمُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَهُمْ جَآءُوكَ فَٱسْتَغْفَرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَٱسْتَغْفَرَ لَهُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ لَوَجَدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابًۭا رَّحِيمًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَبَنِىَّ ٱذْهَبُوا۟ فَتَحَسَّسُوا۟ مِن يُوسُفَ وَأَخِيهِ وَلَا تَا۟يْـَٔسُوا۟ مِن رَّوْحِ ٱللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ لَا يَا۟يْـَٔسُ مِن رَّوْحِ ٱللَّهِ إِلَّا ٱلْقَوْمُ ٱلْكَفِرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱتَّقُوا۟ رَبَّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍۢ وَحِدَةٍۢ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًۭا كَثِيرًۭا وَنِسَآءًۭ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى تَسَآءَلُونَ بِهِۦ وَٱلْأَرْحَامَ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ عَلَيْكُمْ رَقِيبًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱسْتَحْوَذَ عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَأَنسَىٰهُمْ ذِكْرَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ هُمُ ٱلْخَسِرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:’أسمآء‘⟫ ⟪ar:’ھابیل‘⟫
