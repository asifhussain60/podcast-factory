# Hazrat Adam (peace be upon him)

<!-- section 1 (id=1478, raw_sort=1): آدم علیہ السلام کا ظاہری قصہ -->

## The Outer Story (Ẓāhir) of Ādam, Peace Be Upon Him

### The Birth of Adam from Clay

When the Almighty God intended to create Ādam, revelation came to the earth itself: "O earth! I am about to bring forth from you a creature, some of whom shall obey Me, and some shall disobey. I will admit the obedient to Paradise and the disobedient to the Fire." Thereupon the Almighty commanded Gabriel (Jibrāʾīl, the Archangel) to descend, saying, "Go forth and gather dust from the earth." When Gabriel arrived and began to collect the soil, the earth cried out in entreaty, swearing by the glory of Him who had sent Gabriel: "I beseech you, do not take from my substance what shall become an inhabitant of the Fire tomorrow." Gabriel turned back without taking anything, and reported to God, "The earth has adjured me by Your majesty, and I dared not disobey." Then the Almighty sent Michael (Mīkāʾīl). The earth made the same oath to him, and he too returned empty-handed. Finally, God sent ⟪ar:ملک الموت⟫ Āzrāʾīl (the Angel of Death), and when the earth swore to him in the same manner, he replied, "I swear by God that I shall not disobey His command." With these words, he gathered dust little by little from the four corners of the earth—soil that was soft and hard, hot and cold, white and red, black and yellow, pure and foul. This mingling of diverse soils accounts for the variety of temperaments, morals, and complexions among the children of Ādam.[^cite-1]

⟪ar:عبد اللہ ابن سلام⟫ ʿAbdullāh ibn Salām asked the Messenger of Allāh (upon whom be peace and blessings): "How did God create Ādam?" The Prophet replied, "Ādam's forehead was fashioned from the clay of the Sacred House (al-Kaʿba), his ankles from Egyptian soil, his feet from the clay of the Hijāz, his right hand from the dust of the east, and his left hand from the dust of the west."

### Kneading the Soil in Different Waters

When Āzrāʾīl brought this handful of earth before the throne of God, he was commanded to mix it with water and knead it thoroughly. There were three waters at hand—bitter, sweet, and salt. Āzrāʾīl kneaded the earth well, tempering it into a workable state, which in the language of tradition is called ⟪ar:'طین'⟫ *ṭīn* (clay or mud).

Thereafter, the Almighty commanded Gabriel: "Descend now to the earth and gather white, luminous, and pure dust from which I shall create ⟪ar:محمد علیہ السلام⟫ [Muḥammad, peace be upon him]." Gabriel descended with a vast company of ⟪ar:ملائکہ فردوس⟫ the angels of Paradise, ⟪ar:ملائکہ مغربین⟫ the angels of the west, and ⟪ar:ملائکہ نصف الاعلی⟫ the angels of the upper heavens, and gathered pristine, white, and transparent soil from the grave of the Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings). This pure earth was then kneaded with the water of ⟪ar:'تسنیم'⟫ *Tasnīm*, the spring of Paradise, and polished until it shone like a pearl luminous with white radiance. It was immersed in all the springs of Paradise and bathed until, when the gaze of God fell upon it, it trembled with awe. God divided this pearl into one hundred twenty-four thousand drops, and from each drop He created a Prophet. All the prophets were brought into being from your light, O Muhammad.[^cite-2] Then this pearl, the masterpiece of the Divine creative power, was circulated through the heavens and the earth, so that the angels came to know and recognize Muḥammad before they knew Ādam. For this reason, the Prophet (upon whom be peace and blessings) declared: ⟪ar-quote:کنت نبیا کان آدم بین المآء و الطین⟫ "I was a prophet when Ādam was [yet] between water and clay."

### Adam's Clay Left to Rest for Forty Years

The clay of Ādam, having been kneaded with water, was left to lie for forty years until it became soft and malleable, capable of being shaped into every form. Then it was left to stand for another forty years, drying and hardening until it became dry and brittle, producing a sound when struck:

> ⟪quran 55:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏خَلَقَ ٱلْإِنسَنَ مِن صَلْصَلٍۢ كَٱلْفَخَّارِ ‎⟫
> *He created man from sounding clay like the clay of pottery.*

Thus Ādam's body was formed from this dry, sonorous clay, that it might be manifest that God did not naturally create Ādam's form from soft and wet clay, but wrought it from dry and shapeless earth by His perfect power. The body was then laid upon the path by which the angels passed for forty years more.

### The Amazement of the Angels and the Scheming of Satan

As the angels journeyed past this place, they were struck with wonder at the beauty of Ādam's form, for never had they beheld such comeliness. But when the devil (Iblīs) passed by, he thought to himself, "What is the purpose of this statue?" He played upon it with his hand. Since the form was hollow within, he would enter through the mouth and exit through the mouth, crying out to his companions, "What a strange creature—entirely empty within! It can never endure nor stand firm!" He then said to them, "Suppose this being were granted superiority over you—what would you do?" All replied, "We would obey its Lord." But the devil said in his heart, "If this statue be exalted above me, I shall disobey it; and if I excel over it, I shall not preserve it."

### The Cause of Sorrow Prevailing Over Joy

It is narrated in the Ḥadīth that when Ādam's body lay in this state, it rained upon it grief, sorrow, and affliction for forty years, and then joy rained upon it for only one year. This is why the children of Ādam (upon whom be peace) are for the most part grieved and sorrowful, and find but little joy and ease at the end. As the poet has said:

> Sorrow passes, and happiness passes also;
> If you have power over sorrow, sorrow shall have no dominion.

### The Manifestation of Adam's Virtue After His Creation

When Ādam's body was complete, the Almighty clothed him with the garments of Paradise and adorned him with every manner of embellishment. From his teeth shone a light like the rays of the sun, and the light of ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ our Prophet Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (upon whom be peace and blessings) gleamed upon his forehead like the moonlight of the fourteenth night. God seated him upon a throne and elevated him upon the shoulders of the angels, commanding them to journey with him through all the heavens, that he might behold the whole of creation with his own eyes and increase in certainty of knowledge—⟪ar:عین الیقین⟫ *ʿayn al-yaqīn*, the certainty of vision.[^cite-3] The angels bore him upon their shoulders for nearly a hundred years, until Ādam became acquainted with every corner of Paradise. Then the Almighty created for him a horse of pure musk, which He named ⟪ar:'میمون'⟫ *Maimūn* (the Blessed One). He fashioned for Ādam a saddle of pearls and jewels, and placed him upon this steed. Gabriel took the reins, while Michael and Isrāfīl joined in procession to his right and left, and they circled through all the heavens. At each heaven, Ādam greeted the angels with the words: ⟪ar:'السلام علیکم یا ملائکۃ اللہ'⟫ "Peace be upon you, O angels of God!" Then the Almighty said to Ādam, "O Ādam! This greeting is a greeting and a gift to all the believers born from you and your descendants until the Day of Resurrection."

Thereafter, God taught Ādam all the ⟪ar:'أسمآء'⟫ *al-asmāʾ* (the names—the divine realities and designations). When the angels were questioned about these names, they replied: ⟪ar-quote:قَالُوا۟ سُبْحَنَكَ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَآ إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ⟫ "Glory be to You! We have knowledge only of what You have taught us. Truly, You alone are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise." The Almighty then commanded Ādam to teach the angels all these names. When Ādam had made known to them these names, they became his learners and teachers, bowing down before him. But the devil refused obedience and displayed arrogance.

### The Birth of Ḥawwāʾ (Eve)

The Qurʾānic exegetes relate that when the Almighty settled Ādam in Paradise, he was seized by loneliness. Seeing his distress and taking pity upon him, God resolved to create a being like unto himself. He caused Ādam to fall into a deep sleep, and from the rib on his left side—called in the tradition ⟪ar:'قصیریٰ'⟫ *al-quṣyarā* (the shortest rib)—He fashioned Ḥawwāʾ (Eve). During this process, Ādam felt not the slightest pain; had he experienced pain, he would not have felt affection toward her, nor would men thereafter have desired the company of women.

Then Ḥawwāʾ was dressed in the garments of Paradise and adorned with every ornament, and she was made to sit upon Ādam's couch. When he awoke and saw this beautiful, radiant face beside him, the angels asked him, "Who is this?" He answered, ⟪ar:'عورت'⟫ *ʿaurat* (a woman). They asked, "What is her name?" He replied, ⟪ar:'حوا'⟫ *Ḥawwāʾ* (Eve). The angel said, "Rightly spoken! But tell us, why was she named Ḥawwāʾ?" He answered, "Because she was created from a living being—⟪ar:حی⟫ *ḥayy*, the living." They asked, "For whom did God create her?" He replied, "That I might find tranquility in her, and that she might find tranquility in me." This narrative is preserved in the Qurʾān as follows:

> ⟪quran 4:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱتَّقُوا۟ رَبَّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍۢ وَحِدَةٍۢ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًۭا كَثِيرًۭا وَنِسَآءًۭ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى تَسَآءَلُونَ بِهِۦ وَٱلْأَرْحَامَ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ عَلَيْكُمْ رَقِيبًۭا ‎⟫
> *O mankind! Be dutiful to your Lord, Who created you from a single person (Adam), and from him (Adam) He created his wife [Hawwa (Eve)], and from them both He created many men and women and fear Allah through Whom you demand your mutual (rights), and (do not cut the relations of) the wombs (kinship). Surely, Allah is Ever an All-Watcher over you.*

The Messenger of Allāh (upon whom be peace and blessings) said, "Woman was created from a bent rib of Ādam. If you strive to straighten it, it will break; if you leave it as it is, you will benefit from it." Men age well, their virtue refined by time, whereas women lose their beauty with the passage of years. The reason lies in the nature of their creation: man is made of earth, and earth does not become foul or ugly with age and the wearing of time; rather, its virtue grows more refined. Woman, by contrast, was created from flesh, which rots and becomes foul with the passage of time, and thus loses the attention of men.

### Adam's Lawful Union with Eve

In certain Ḥadīths it is narrated that when Ādam beheld Ḥawwāʾ, he stretched out his hand to her. The angels stopped him, saying, "Wait!" Ādam protested, "God created her for me!" They replied, "That is true, but it is not lawful for you until you pay her dower." He asked, "What is the measure and amount of her dower?" They said, "The measure of it is that you recite blessings upon ⟪ar:حضرت محمد مصطفی (ﷺ)⟫ Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā three times." Ādam asked, "Who are these—⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ Muḥammad?" They replied, "They are the last Prophet among your descendants! Had it not been for the purpose of creating him, you would never have been created."

### Adam and Eve's Dwelling in Paradise

When the Almighty had settled Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ in Paradise, He made lawful for them all the blessings therein, save one tree, and He commanded them:

> ⟪quran 2:35⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلْنَا يَٓـَٔادَمُ ٱسْكُنْ أَنتَ وَزَوْجُكَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَكُلَا مِنْهَا رَغَدًا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمَا وَلَا تَقْرَبَا هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةَ فَتَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And We said: "O Adam! Dwell you and your wife in the Paradise and eat both of you freely with pleasure and delight of things therein as wherever you will, but come not near this tree or you both will be of the Zalimun (wrong-doers)."*

### The Devil's Deception and Stratagem

When the Almighty had settled Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ in Paradise and permitted them all its blessings, and they dwelt in great luxury, the devil ⟪ar:أبلیس لعین⟫ Iblīs the Accursed became consumed with envy toward them. As soon as he found means to enter Paradise, from which he had been expelled, he began to devise methods of deception against Ādam. He entered Paradise in the form of a ⟪ar:حیۃ⟫ *ḥayyat* (serpent), so that Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ would not recognize him. According to some traditions, Iblīs wrapped himself in the feet of a peacock and entered by that means. Mūr, one of Ādam's servants and an ancient companion of Iblīs, aided him in this.

When Iblīs drew near to Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ, he began to weep so bitterly that they both wept with him, moved by his sorrow. Thus Iblīs was the first to weep, and it was said on this account that weeping is Satan's voice. Both asked him compassionately, "Why do you weep?" He replied, "I grieve over your helplessness and weakness, and over your ruin. I see that both of you will die after a few years, and all this blessing and honor will be lost to you." He spoke with such pathos that they both grew sorrowful at his words. With this, Iblīs departed weeping. Days later, he returned to them when both were still affected by his former words and grieving in their hearts. Seeing that his arrow had struck its mark, he spoke with false sympathy: "O Ādam, shall I direct you to a tree from which you shall live forever, and from which your kingdom shall never decay?" Ādam said, "Yes!" So Iblīs revealed to him the forbidden tree and said, "Eat from it, and you shall live eternally." Ādam cried out, "My Lord has forbidden me to do so!" Iblīs said, "He has forbidden you only because you would become angels and dwell in Paradise forever, equal to the Most High." When Ādam refused to obey, Iblīs swore by God, "I am indeed a sincere counselor to you both." They were deceived by his oath, for they could not imagine that anyone would swear falsely by the Name of God. Thus Ḥawwāʾ hastened to eat from the tree and gave it also to Ādam. No sooner had both tasted the fruit than they overstepped the bounds of obedience, and in that instant the garment of Paradise fell from their bodies and they became naked. Ādam grew anxious and began to flee. They approached a fig tree and plucked its leaves to cover themselves. Then came a cry from the Lord: "O Ādam! Are you fleeing from Me?" He answered, "Yes, O my Lord, I flee from You out of shame."

### The Fig Tree's Reward

It is narrated that when Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ became naked and sought leaves from the trees of Paradise to conceal their nakedness, every bush refused them. Only the fig tree took pity upon them and offered its leaves, with which they covered themselves. The Almighty rewarded the fig tree for its compassion by making its outward appearance equal to its inward nature, rendering it sweet and beneficial, and granting it fruit twice yearly. The peculiarity of the fig is that it produces fruit without flowering.

### The Five Punishments of the Serpent

| 1 | Its feet were severed, and it was condemned to crawl upon its belly. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | It was disfigured into the most loathsome of forms, having been among the most beautiful of creatures. |
| 3 | Its food was made ⟪ar:عناب⟫ *ʿinab* (a bitter or foul substance), which is light and difficult to digest. |

### Adam's Expulsion from Paradise

When Ādam was expelled on account of his disobedience, and word came forth from God that the disobedient could not draw near to Him, Ādam took Ḥawwāʾ by the hand and moved forward. With each step they took, they looked back in hope of their Lord's mercy, hoping that He might have compassion upon them. They wept with deep shame until, when they took the final step out of Paradise, from Ādam's lips came the words: ⟪ar:'بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم'⟫ "In the name of God, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate." Gabriel stopped them and said, "O Ādam! There has issued from your tongue a word whose blessing and greatness I hope will bring you mercy. Your Lord is the Most Merciful ⟪ar:رحمٰن و رحیم⟫ (al-Raḥmān wa-l-Raḥīm), the All-Compassionate and All-Merciful." God said to Gabriel, "I shall have mercy upon him and his descendants on the Day of Resurrection when they return to Me."

In other words, Ādam descended upon Mount Saradeep in India, while Ḥawwāʾ alighted in a place in the Hijāz called Jeddah. It is said that after Ādam was sent down to the earth, he was so overcome with shame that he did not lift his gaze to the heavens for three hundred years. Ibn ʿAbbās relates that Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ wept for two hundred years and neither ate nor drank for forty years after being separated from the joys of Paradise and from the nearness of God.

When God desired to show mercy to Ādam after this long time, He taught him certain words by which his repentance was accepted: ⟪ar:'لا الہ الا اللہ محمد رسول اللہ'⟫ "There is no god but God, and Muḥammad is the Messenger of God." He had seen these words inscribed upon the Throne itself, and they proved beneficial to him. Through the intercession of Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (upon whom be peace and blessings), he sought forgiveness. God pardoned them and revealed to them: "Circumambulate My Throne upon the earth and pray there as you did pray near My Throne. There shall I answer your prayer."

Ādam departed from India toward Mecca to visit the Sacred House. The Almighty sent an angel to guide them. When they reached ʿArafāh, they encountered Ḥawwāʾ, who had journeyed from Jeddah in search of her beloved. The two met in this plain on the Day of ʿArafāh, whence the place is called ⟪ar:'عرفات'⟫ *ʿArafāt* (the Place of Recognition). When Ādam returned to Minā, he said, "Tell your desire!" She said, "My wish is your forgiveness and your praise!" Thus the place was named Minā (the Place of Wish). So God answered their prayer and forgave them their sins. Thereafter Ādam returned to India.

### Divine Revelation to Adam

Wahab ibn Umayya narrates that after forgiving Ādam, God revealed to him, "O Ādam, I shall gather all your knowledge into four words: one is for Me, one is between Me and you, one is for you, and one is between you and My servants."

| **For the Sake of God** | **Between God and Ādam** | **For Ādam Himself** | **Between Ādam and the Servants** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Worship Me and associate no partners with Me. | Your duty is to pray and accept My work. | I shall repay you for all you have done. | Love for them what you love for yourself. |

### The Meeting of Adam and the Devil

It is narrated that when Ādam encountered Iblīs in a forest, he rebuked him for his ruin and disgrace, saying, "O accursed one! What have you done? You deceived me and cast me out of Paradise and brought me into this state of humiliation and wretchedness! What did I do to you?" Iblīs wept and replied, "Yes, O Ādam, I am culpable toward you, and I confess what I have done to you. But I ask you the same question: Who has cast me into this disgrace and ruin? For whom am I afflicted with this curse?"

### The Devil's Petitions and Their Acceptance

⟪ar:حماد ثابت⟫ Ḥammād al-Thābit and ⟪ar:حمید عبد اللہ ابن عبید⟫ Ḥumaid ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUbayd relate that Iblīs said in the presence of the ⟪ar:رب العزت⟫ Lord of Might, "My Lord, You have expelled me from Paradise on account of Ādam. Now grant me dominion over him, that I may overcome him; otherwise, I shall not." God said, "Go, and I shall impose you upon him." The devil saw that great authority and dominion had been granted for so slight a request, so why not ask for more? Immediately he said, "O my Lord, grant me yet more!" God said, "Listen! For every one son of Ādam, you shall have two sons, and your children shall be twice as many as his." He said, "O Lord, grant me something more." God said, "Listen: In the breast of every child of Ādam shall be your dwelling, and you shall run through their veins in all their blood." He said to Iblīs, "O Lord, give me something else yet." God said, "You are permitted to assail the children of Ādam with your army of horse and foot, and to mix with their wealth and children, and to seduce them with false promises. Yet know that My promise is truth, and the promise of Satan is naught but deceit."

### Adam's Petitions to God and Their Acceptance

When Ādam saw that each petition of Iblīs was granted, such that he and his descendants would be crushed, he too prayed to his Lord, saying, "O Lord, You have imposed Iblīs upon us, but You know well that we shall not be able to escape his snare without Your aid. What then shall become of me and my children?" The Almighty consoled him, saying, "O Ādam! Every child of yours shall be accompanied by a guardian who shall protect him from this wretch. Ask Me for something more!" He said, "Grant me good tidings!" God said, "Listen to this: Whoever among your children performs a good deed, I shall reward him tenfold, and for every evil deed and sin, I shall punish him only equal to what he has done, not more. And should he turn in repentance, I shall not hold it against him, but shall blot it out." He said, "O my Lord, give me something more!" God said, "I shall never close the door of repentance before your children, even to their last breath. Whoever repents, I shall forgive." He said, "Give me yet more good tidings!" God said, "So be it: I shall forgive your children, and I shall keep no account of My forgiveness." Ādam rejoiced and said, "Sufficient unto me is Your grace, O my Lord!"

### The Devil's Possessions and Prerogatives

It is narrated that Iblīs said in the presence of the Lord of Might, "O Lord, You have cursed me and cast me out of Paradise and made me despised. You have made me ugly and humiliated me, and given me the titles of Satan and Iblīs. All this I have heard and endured, but I have one request: grant me some powers." God said, "I shall send Messengers to guide the children of Ādam and reveal to them My Word in the form of a Book. You also shall have a messenger and a book. Who shall be your messenger?" The Almighty said, "Your messengers shall be priests and the corrupt." Iblīs asked, "What shall be my book?" God said, "Your book shall be a scroll of slander, backbiting, and calumny." He asked, "What shall be my *ḥadīth* (recitation)?" God said, "Your *ḥadīth* shall be a scroll of falsehood." He asked, "Who shall be my *muʾadhdhin* (caller)?" God said, "There shall be instruments of music." He asked, "What is my mosque?" God said, "The marketplace!" He asked about his house. God said, "Your house shall be the bathhouse, a place of filth and impurity." He asked, "What shall be my food?" God said, "All that upon which My Name has not been invoked (the *Bismillāh* has not been recited)." He asked, "What shall be my drink?" God said, "Every intoxicating thing." He asked, "And who shall be my prey?" God said, "Women shall be your prey!"

### The Testament of Adam

Mujāhid relates that ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās told him that Hazrat Ādam, who dwelt in India throughout his life, performed the pilgrimage on foot forty times. The light of Hazrat Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā (upon whom be peace and blessings) shone radiant upon Ādam's forehead. Throughout his life, he called to God alone. He fulfilled the right due to the Prophet. He endured every hardship and suffering from the people of his time with patience and forbearance, and continued in ⟪ar:دعوت الی اللہ⟫ *al-daʿwat ilā Allāh* (the summons to God) until the time of his death drew near.

He fell ill for eleven days before his death. In this time, he wrote a will and gave it to his son ⟪ar:شِیث علیہ السلام⟫ Shīth (Seth, upon whom be peace), instructing him to keep it hidden from the children of Qābīl, for Ādam had married Qābīl's sister ʿAqlīmā to Hābīl (Abel). Out of jealousy and envy, Qābīl killed Hābīl. For this reason, Ādam feared that Qābīl, upon learning that Ādam had granted Shīth certain special knowledge, would not rest. Thus Shīth and his descendants concealed this particular knowledge from the line of Qābīl, and they remained deprived and ignorant on account of the enmity they bore toward Hābīl.

### The Death of Prophet Adam

According to the narrators, Ādam died on a Friday. The angels brought his shroud and perfumed water from Paradise and washed him. For the first time, they washed him with mixed water, then with pure water, and enshrouded him in a three-piece shroud. He was then buried in *Mashariq al-Firdaws* (the East of Paradise), which was the first burial place on earth. Ādam was interred by his son Shīth and his brothers. After Ādam's death, the sun and moon were eclipsed for six days.

After the burial of Ādam (upon whom be peace), Hazrat Shīth requested Gabriel to lead the funeral prayer. Gabriel said, "You go ahead and pray for your father." Thus Shīth (upon whom be peace) led the funeral prayer, reciting thirty *takbīr* (magnifications of God's greatness). Five *takbīrs* were taken from the five daily prayers, and twenty-five additional ones were added on account of Ādam's virtue. 

*Remark:*
The Messenger (upon whom be peace and blessings), mentioned alongside ⟪ar:سید الشھداء جناب حمزہ⟫ Hamzah, the Master of Martyrs, recited seventy *takbīrs* at the funeral of Hamzah, the Master of Martyrs.

### The Death of Eve

Ḥawwāʾ lived for one year after Ādam's death and passed away in Jeddah, where a great tomb stands on the bank of a river to this day.

## Ādam Ṣafīy Allāh — God's Chosen One

The inner and mystical dimension of Ādam's narrative reveals him as the first initiator (mubtadi) of a sacred cycle. In the esoteric exegesis (*taʾwīl*) of the Ismaili tradition, Ādam embodies the archetype of the chosen one (*al-Ṣafīy*), whose creation marks the inauguration of a new cosmic epoch. His name itself signals his role as the inaugurator of the age that follows, just as his successor marks its termination.[^cite-4]

Among the initiators of the sacred cycles, Ādam stands foremost. His real name was ⟪ar:'عبد اللہ ا بن تنجوم أبن فوجان'⟫ ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm ibn Fujān, who was the brother of the Imam of his age ⟪ar:امام ھنید علیہ السلام⟫ Imām al-Hunayd (upon whom be peace). He belonged to the tribe known as ⟪ar:'زوحاقہ'⟫ *Zūḥāqa*, and dwelt in the region called ⟪ar:'لنکا'⟫ *Lankā*, which was governed from the place ⟪ar:'شابازداری'⟫ *Shābāzdarī*. The ruler ⟪ar:تنجوم⟫ Tanjūm, whose very name suggests his mastery of the celestial sciences and the movement of the stars, was a king of that land. The initiator who launches each new age bears the title of initiator, while the one who concludes that period is called the ⟪ar:'قائم'⟫ *qāʾim* (the one who arises to perfect it). Since ⟪ar:حضرت عبد اللہ بن تنجوم⟫ Hazrat ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm was about to inaugurate the Seventy Period, he was called Ādam.

## The Garden of Ādam (the Janna in Which He Was Placed)

There is considerable disagreement among scholars regarding the nature and location of the Paradise in which Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ were placed: whether it was situated in the celestial realms or upon the earth. According to those who have penetrated to the truth of the matter, the Paradise of Ādam is not among the heavenly gardens but rather an earthly paradise, distinct from the reward-garden (*Jannāt al-Khulud*) that awaits the righteous in the hereafter. Several proofs establish this distinction:

First, had it been the eternal reward-garden, Iblīs could never have been cast out from it, for paradise eternal admits no expulsion. Nor could he thereafter have gained entry to deceive Ādam. The Qurʾān itself records Satan's own words to Ādam:

> ⟪quran 20:120⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ إِلَيْهِ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ هَلْ أَدُلُّكَ عَلَىٰ شَجَرَةِ ٱلْخُلْدِ وَمُلْكٍۢ لَّا يَبْلَىٰ ‎⟫
> *Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered to him, saying : "O Adam! Shall I lead you to the Tree of Eternity and to a kingdom that will never waste away?"*

And when Ādam hearkened, Satan continued:

> ⟪quran 7:20⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَالِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *Satan whispered to them, in order to reveal to them that which they had hidden from one another, and said, Your Lord has not forbidden you from this tree except that you may become angels or become immortals.*

These verses testify that Ādam dwelt in a garden where eternal life was not yet secured—a place where deception and transgression remained possible. By contrast, the abode of reward is inviolable, as God affirms:

> ⟪quran 15:48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَا يَمَسُّهُمْ فِيهَا نَصَبٌۭ وَمَا هُم مِّنْهَا بِمُخْرَجِينَ ‎⟫
> *No evil shall touch them, nor shall they be expelled from it.*

Furthermore, it is not consonant with Divine wisdom to settle a soul in the reward-garden without prior obedience, nor is it fitting to admit one to Paradise without first establishing his worthiness. God does not reward those who have not ⟪ar:عاملین⟫ *ʿamilīn* (labored and acted). The clearest proof that Ādam was created upon the earth lies in the Qurʾān itself:

> ⟪quran 2:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when your Lord said to the angels, 'I am going to place a vicegerent on the earth.' They said, 'Do You want to place in the earth one who will spread corruption and shed blood, while we glorify You with His praise and glorify You?' He said, 'I know what you do not know.'*

In this verse, God declares unmistakably: "I shall appoint My Caliph not in the heavens, but upon ⟪ar:'زمین'⟫ *zamīn* (the earth)."

## The Test of the Angels (Q 2:30–33)

The Qurʾān presents the advent of Ādam in the words:

> ⟪quran 2:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when your Lord said to the angels, 'I am going to place a vicegerent on the earth.' They said, 'Do You want to place in the earth one who will spread corruption and shed blood, while we glorify You with His praise and glorify You?' He said, 'I know what you do not know.'*

In the esoteric interpretation (*taʾwīl*), this verse refers not to the creation of mankind in general, but to the investiture of Ādam as successor and spiritual guide during the age that was about to begin. The ⟪ar:'رب'⟫ *Rabb* (the Lord—understood in its inward sense as the Imām of the time) announced to the ⟪ar:'ھنید علیہ السلام'⟫ angels—the high-ranking initiates and guides of the previous epoch—his intention to establish a new Imām to guide the people in the coming age. Though many capable candidates stood before him, the Imām's gaze fell upon ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm, still a youth engaged in the study of the external sciences. The Imām resolved to elevate him, conferring upon him the second creation—the birth in knowledge (*wilāda al-ʿilm*)—as the Qurʾān suggests elsewhere.

When the Imām announced his intention to appoint Ādam to the station of Imāmate, he selected twelve angels to serve as *niqāba* (the superintendents and spiritual guides) under Ādam's authority, that they might learn from him and promulgate his message throughout the world. These twelve were:

| 1 | Gabriel | 7 | Amhail |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2 | Michael | 8 | Tahtiel |
| 3 | Israfil | 9 | Hoyal |
| 4 | Azrael | 10 | Marhiel |
| 5 | Dardīl | 11 | Malik Yael |
| 6 | Saha'il | 12 | Kyrgyz |

The Imām al-Hunayd said to these twelve, "I have resolved to establish my successor in the daʿwa (the divine summons), and I have chosen my nephew for this task. When I establish him, all of you must bow in obedience to him." This is the significance of the verse:

> ⟪quran 15:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا سَوَّيْتُهُۥ وَنَفَخْتُ فِيهِ مِن رُّوحِى فَقَعُوا۟ لَهُۥ سَجِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *Then, when I have perfected him and breathed into him of My spirit, fall down before him in prostration.*

Regarding the manner of Ādam's preparation, the Imām said, "I shall create Ādam from clay":

> ⟪quran 38:71⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى خَلِقٌۢ بَشَرًۭا مِّن طِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *When your Lord said to the angels: I am going to create a human being from clay.*

In the language of the esoteric path, ⟪ar:'طین'⟫ *ṭīn* (clay)—composed of soil and water—signifies a dual knowledge: the soil represents the external Sharīʿa (the manifest law), while the water represents the inner *taʾwīl* (esoteric exegesis). The Imām thus prepared Ādam with both dimensions of knowledge. The angels, being created from pure spirituality alone, possessed only the inner, luminous knowledge that descends like lightning from those above, unmediated by gradual learning. They understood that:

| **Subtle Science** | **Tangible Science** |
| --- | --- |
| Endorsement | Education |
| Like lightning | Gradual |
| Belief and affirmation | Action and obedience |

The angels perceived that subtle, spiritual science requires no action, no bodily labor, no faith manifested through deeds. Yet when a human being is charged with action and obligatory duties—the fulfillment of the external law—error and oversight inevitably arise. This is why the leader of the believers (Amīr al-Muʾminīn) explained the virtue of the Qurʾān in these words: ⟪ar-quote:ظاھر عمل موجب و باطن علم محجوب⟫ "Its outward aspect is obligatory action, and its inward aspect is concealed knowledge." Recognizing this principle, the angels objected, saying: "If Your intention is indeed to be glorified and praised through these inner sciences, then we are already accomplishing this better than any other creature could. It would be more fitting to appoint one of us to this task, for we have mastered the sciences of the daʿwa, completed the books of esoteric exegesis (*ta*ʾwīl), and studied the scrolls of inward truths." They reasoned that ⟪ar:'مستجیب'⟫ *mustajīb* (the respondent—one obligated to fulfill the external law) would inevitably stumble and fail, and that they, who had no such obligations, would be more worthy.

But the Imām replied to them: "I know what you do not know." He meant, "I shall grant Ādam My support and grace; if he errs, I shall compensate him and deliver him from destruction."

Among the angels, one called ⟪ar:'عزازیل'⟫ *ʿAzāzīl* (later to be known as Iblīs) believed himself superior to Ādam and deemed himself worthy of the rank of successorship. He abstained from obeying the Imām's command and refused to join in the prostration. His name in that age was ⟪ar:'حارث بن مرۃ'⟫ Ḥārith ibn Murra. When the verse states:

⟪ar:'⟫ *[There was]* ⟪ar:'⟫ *[a particular one]*

what the Imām "knew" and the angels "did not know" was the hidden capacity within Ādam for learning and spiritual elevation, just as He knew the stubborn pride lurking in Iblīs's heart that would move him to refuse obedience when the command was given.

The Almighty created Ādam with both His hands and fashioned him from clay, as is conveyed in the verse:

> ⟪quran 38:75⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓإِبْلِيسُ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَن تَسْجُدَ لِمَا خَلَقْتُ بِيَدَىَّ ۖ أَسْتَكْبَرْتَ أَمْ كُنتَ مِنَ ٱلْعَالِينَ ‎⟫
> *said, "O Iblis! What prevented you from prostrating before what I created with My own hands? Were you too proud, or were you one of the exalted?*

The "two hands" here signify the two arguments (*ḥujja*) of the Imām's daʿwa: one guiding toward external knowledge and the Sharīʿa, the other leading to inner mystical understanding. By these two paths, the Imām educate Ādam until he became worthy of the Imāmate.[^cite-5]

The Ḥadīth describes this as follows: When the Almighty fashioned Ādam's form from clay, His body lay in a state of preparation for a long time before the soul—the support of inner spiritual knowledge—descended into it. Ādam spent years studying the external sciences before receiving the grace of esoteric knowledge. During this time, the devil would come and mock the dried, hollow clay form of Ādam's body, declaring in glee, "It is empty! Hollow!" that is, he would approach Ādam during his studies and pose questions about the highest mysteries of the faith. When Ādam could not answer, speaking only from the light of external knowledge, Satan would rejoice, thinking himself vindicated in the belief that Ādam lacked inner knowledge—that he was, as it were, spiritually vacant.

Year upon year, Ādam's education and training proceeded until he reached completion and became worthy of the Caliphate. Then the Imām taught him all the Divine Names:

> ⟪quran 2:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَعَلَّمَ آدَمَ ٱلْأَسْمَآءَ كُلَّهَا ثُمَّ عَرَضَهُمْ عَلَى ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ فَقَالَ أَنۢبِـُٔونِى بِأَسْمَآءِ هَٓؤُلَآءِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *And Allah taught Adam all the names, then He presented them to the angels, and said, Tell Me the names of these, if you are truthful.*

The names of which the Imām taught Ādam were not merely the appellations of things, but rather the ⟪ar:'أسمآء'⟫ *al-asmāʾ*—the divine attributes and realities, which constitute true ⟪ar:'معرفت'⟫ *maʿrifat* (gnosis). These were the names of the ⟪ar:ملائکہ علیین⟫ *al-malāʾika al-ʿAlīyīn* (the exalted angels), those of rank superior to Ādam and even to the lesser angels now appointed under his authority. Only Ādam received knowledge of these supreme names, for he alone was destined for the rank

## Exegesis of "I Know What You Reveal and What You Conceal" (Q2:33)

⟪quran 2:33⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *He said: "O Adam! Inform them of their names," and when he had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the Ghaib (unseen) in the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you have been concealing?"*

In elucidating this verse—"I know what you reveal and I know what you conceal"—Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (a.s.) transmitted from his father, Maulānā Muḥammad al-Bāqir (a.s.), that what the angels revealed was their outward declaration, made without hesitation, whereas what they concealed within their hearts was a hidden doubt: they questioned how God could create a being superior to themselves and command them to prostrate before him.[^cite-1]

In an alternative reading, scholars describe the following scene: when Allāh fashioned Ādam's form from clay and positioned it in the path by which the angels habitually passed, they marveled at his beauty, for no figure of such elegance had appeared to them before. Yet when the one called Iblīs ⟪ar:'ابلیس'⟫ approached and observed this statue, he pondered its nature. Passing his hand through the hollow interior, he entered by the mouth and departed by the same orifice, then announced to the other angels: "This is a strange creature, entirely empty within—it cannot remain steadfast or endure." He then posed a question to them: "Suppose this form were given superiority over you; what would you do?" All replied, "We shall obey his Lord." But Iblīs whispered to himself: "If this statue receives authority over me, I shall disobey it; if I excel over it, I shall not preserve it."

Thus the Divine utterance—"I know what you reveal and I know what you conceal"—encompasses both the angels' hidden resistance and the malice that Iblīs harbored in his innermost heart, which only the Originator, Most High (Mubdiʿ Taʿālā), could perceive.[^cite-2]

## The Angels' Seeking of Forgiveness

It is narrated that Maulānā Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (a.s.) performed the pilgrimage alongside his father, Maulānā Muḥammad al-Bāqir (a.s.). One night, as they circumambulated the House of Allāh and venerated the Black Stone, a figure appeared and stood beside them: a man of strong-boned visage and ruddy complexion, clad in two heavy white garments. Upon sensing his presence, the Imam abbreviated his prayer, exchanged the customary greeting, and inquired: "Do you have a question for me?" The man replied, "I come to ask you of the origin of this sanctuary." Thereupon the Imam disclosed the entire history of Ādam's creation and what followed.

He explained: "When the angels confessed their error, Allāh addressed them, saying:

⟪quran 2:33⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *He said: "O Adam! Inform them of their names," and when he had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the Ghaib (unseen) in the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you have been concealing?"*

"That is: I am aware not only of what you declared aloud—your protestation that your praise suffices—but also of what lay hidden in your depths: your unspoken misgiving concerning how Allāh could fashion a creation greater than yourselves and demand your submission to it. When the Divine Reality unveiled their innermost secrets to the angels, they repented. Gathering themselves before the Throne, they circumambulated it seven times to obtain Allāh's pleasure. Allāh was well-pleased with their sincere remorse and their deeds, and He commanded them: 'Descend to the earth and construct therein a sanctuary where the wayward descendants of Ādam shall gather and circumambulate, so that I may forgive them and be pleased with them as I am pleased with you.'"[^cite-3]

The angels carried out this command. At the time of Nūḥ's deluge, the House was borne aloft and placed in the fourth heaven, where the celestial beings shall circumambulate it until the Day of Judgment. Thereafter, Ibrāhīm rebuilt the sanctuary upon its ancient foundations, restoring it in all its glory.

## How Can Error Be Possible from the Angels?

When one declares that the angels possess infallibility, the question arises: how, then, did they commit an error? In the narrative of Ādam, the angel of the garden corresponds to the companions of Imam al-Hunayd (a.s.) who dwelled in his presence. Among Ādam's domain, there existed two stations: the inner circle and the outer veil. The inner essences held a rank superior to the outer veils, precisely as the final soul of an inner order surpasses the leader of the order beneath it. The lower ranks were subject to the law of Ādam (a.s.), whereas his inner reality—his muḥtajib (the one who veils)—remained exempt.

| **Inner Circle** | **Outer Veil** |
|---|---|
| Ādam's Essence | Ādam's Veil |
| Hidden | Manifest |

Those angels whom Allāh commanded to bow before Ādam were of inferior rank to him, and thus susceptible to error. The veils of Ādam (a.s.) that operated among the externals erred, and for this error they were chastened. There existed trumpets among the multitude that bore the guilt of their faults, which is why Ādam's external veil made a mistake. His innermost guardian (muḥtajib) committed no error whatsoever.

## Iblīs's Arrogance and Refusal

At that juncture, Allāh commanded the angels:

⟪quran 2:34⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قُلْنَا لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ ٱسْجُدُوا۟ لِآدَمَ فَسَجَدُوٓا۟ إِلَّآ إِبْلِيسَ أَبَىٰ وَٱسْتَكْبَرَ وَكَانَ مِنَ ٱلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *And (remember) when We said to the angels: "Prostrate yourselves before Adam." And they prostrated except Iblîs (Satan), he refused and was proud and was one of the disbelievers (disobedient to Allah).*

"Prostrate yourselves before Ādam," commanded the Lord, "all of you—obey him, pledge your allegiance, bind your limits to his, and learn from his wisdom."

In the act of prostration, the highest point of the body—the head, seat of reason—is placed beneath the feet of the mind, the lowest member. By this gesture, the prostrator acknowledges that his noblest faculties pale before the intellect of the one honored. Furthermore, in prostration, a person brings seven parts of his body to rest upon the ground, signifying the surrender of his entire being to the higher consciousness.

All the angels obeyed at once, falling into prostration—that is, pledging obedience to the Imam (a.s.) and entering into the covenant of Ādam—save for one of the ḥudūd (limits) named ⟪ar:'ابلیس'⟫ Iblīs, also known as ⟪ar:حارث بن مرۃ⟫ Ḥārith ibn Murrah.

⟪quran 7:12⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَلَّا تَسْجُدَ إِذْ أَمَرْتُكَ ۖ قَالَ أَنَا۠ خَيْرٌۭ مِّنْهُ خَلَقْتَنِى مِن نَّارٍۢ وَخَلَقْتَهُۥ مِن طِينٍۢ ‎⟫
>
> *(Allah) said: "What prevented you (O Iblîs) that you did not prostrate, when I commanded you?" Iblîs said: "I am better than him (Adam), You created me from fire, and him You created from clay."*

Imam al-Hunayd asked Ḥārith: "What stayed your hand from prostrating before Ādam?" And Ḥārith answered: "I am fashioned of fire, whereas he is made of clay. I have been nurtured upon spiritual and ethereal sciences—truths as brilliant and keen as lightning, purified of all material corruption—whereas you have taught Ādam the material sciences, those laden with physicality and impurity. How can you exalt this novice above me, the very one who illuminates the kingdom (muʿallim al-malakūt)?" In his pride, Ḥārith declared: "I am the teacher of truth to all the angels. How, then, can I bow before the lesser?"

Iblīs refused, becoming one of the disbelievers (al-kāfirīn)—the one who covers, who conceals. For he knew full well that Ādam surpassed him in capacity; indeed, Allāh had demonstrated this superiority to all the angels. Yet Ḥārith deliberately veiled Ādam's station, and thus became one who denies the truth.[^cite-4]

⟪quran 7:13⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ فَٱهْبِطْ مِنْهَا فَمَا يَكُونُ لَكَ أَن تَتَكَبَّرَ فِيهَا فَٱخْرُجْ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلصَّغِرِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *(Allah) said: "(O Iblîs) get down from this (Paradise), it is not for you to be arrogant here. Get out, for you are of those humiliated and disgraced."*

Allāh expelled him from Paradise—from His presence and the circle of His invitation—because of his arrogance. ⟪ar:'ملعون'⟫ For the one who grows proud before His Majesty dwells in the castle of Iblīs, inasmuch as greatness belongs to Allāh alone.

The name Iblīs is derived from the Arabic ⟪ar:'أبلس'⟫ meaning "to despair." Ḥārith ibn Murrah's original aspiration was to attain prophethood. When he was stripped of his station and despaired of ever reaching prophethood, he became known as Iblīs. The term carries other meanings as well: one who lacks discernment, for he was bewildered by Ādam's inner knowledge; or one cut off from mercy, since Imam al-Hunayd banished him from his presence. In the dialectal lexicon, ⟪ar:'لعن'⟫ *laʿn* (accursedness) signifies removal and banishment.

## The Creation of Ḥawwāʾ (Eve) and the First Marriage

Just as the birth of Ādam signifies his intellectual awakening, so does his spouse denote the spiritual and sacramental emergence of his successor. Ādam's physical wife, in the material sense, was a woman born of parents as he was; both were earthly creatures. Yet the Ḥawwāʾ of whom the Qurʾān speaks pertains to the religious hierarchy and the station of his vicegerent.

⟪ar:'حوا'⟫ derives from the Arabic ⟪ar:'حی'⟫ meaning "living" or "alive." Additionally, one who dominates or encompasses something is also termed Ḥawwāʾ. The Qurʾān recounts that when Ādam began to experience solitude, Allāh extracted one of his ribs from his right side and fashioned his spouse from it. This verse points to the moment when Ādam assumed the station of prophethood and imāmate, whereupon Imam al-Hunayd (a.s.) commanded him to prepare his caliph.

Ādam possessed twelve ḥujjaj (proofs) or niqāba (trustees), stationed within the palace of twelve ribs. They were called ribs because, just as the body's firmness depends upon the rib-cage, so did Ādam's daʿwa (summons) depend upon his ḥudūd (limits). The twelve ribs on the right side exemplify the hidden sciences, while those on the left exemplify the manifest sciences; that is, Ādam had twelve inner trustees and twelve outer trustees bearing his invitation.

Thus did Imam al-Hunayd (a.s.) take one of Ādam's proofs, namely his son ⟪ar:'ھابیل'⟫ Hābīl (Abel), and establish him as the spouse—the complementary trustee. "Marriage" (nikāḥ) signifies the joining of two beings, as husband and wife are conjoined. Though Hābīl was Ādam's son, he surpassed him in learning. The term ⟪ar:'حوا'⟫ was applied to him because he embodied ⟪ar:'حاوی'⟫ the comprehensive knowledge of the inner realm. Imam al-Hunayd bound Hābīl to Ādam, declaring: should Ādam encounter difficulty in acquiring gnosis, he should seek aid from Hābīl, lest he stumble.

Ādam received this helpmate (Hābīl) in exchange for Iblīs. Before his rebellion, Iblīs would have numbered among Ādam's ⟪ar:اضلاع (حدود)⟫ ⟪ar:ضلع⟫ limits or Summoners. But since Iblīs refused to heed the call from its inception, one of the twelve was lost. To restore the full measure, Allāh granted Ādam a substitute in Hābīl. Thus did the Lord compensate him:

⟪quran 2:35⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلْنَا يَٓـَٔادَمُ ٱسْكُنْ أَنتَ وَزَوْجُكَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَكُلَا مِنْهَا رَغَدًا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمَا وَلَا تَقْرَبَا هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةَ فَتَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *And We said: "O Adam! Dwell you and your wife in the Paradise and eat both of you freely with pleasure and delight of things therein as wherever you will, but come not near this tree or you both will be of the Zalimun (wrong-doers)."*

Imam al-Hunayd placed Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ in the garden (al-janna)—the station of prophethood, connected to revelation, pure and subtle in its essence. This blessing transcends all other blessings; its sweetness surpasses every terrestrial pleasure. To one wholly absorbed in it, the world holds no value; its greatest treasures pale to insignificance. Such a soul perceives that this gnosis is Allāh's true blessing, a pleasure that never fades, a wealth that never diminishes, an honor that never withers. The stages of prayer, too, are called gardens.[^cite-5]

"Eat therein," the Imam commanded, "freely and abundantly, reading, understanding, and deriving benefit from all within the bounds of your station. Execute the rulings of the lawful and the unlawful; establish the ordinances of worship; discharge all the duties of prophethood. You shall not be questioned for your deeds."

Yet He added an injunction:

| And do not approach this tree, for you will be one of the wrongdoers. |
|---|

Do not transgress your limits. This ⟪ar:'شجرہ ممنوعۃ⟫ forbidden tree marked the boundary beyond which ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫ Ādam and Hābīl were forbidden to step. That is: reveal not the inner secrets, for such disclosure belongs solely to the ⟪ar:قائم القیامۃ⟫ Qāʾim. The knowledge of unveiling and expressing the inward is the function of ⟪ar:مولانا صاحب القیامۃ⟫ the Master of the Standing. Those mysteries shall not be revealed in the seventy years before their manifestation. Only the Qāʾim (a.s.) possesses authority to uncover, accept, or conceal them. All mysteries rest in his hands alone—he is ⟪ar:'مالک یوم الدین'⟫ the Master of the Day of Judgment. Whosoever believes after their unveiling shall gain naught. He who reveals before the appointed hour becomes a transgressor (⟪ar:فَتَکُونَا مِنَ الظَّالِمِینَ⟫). For this reason, Imam al-Hunayd prohibited them both at the outset of the seventy years, that the Sunna and nature of Allāh might suffer no alteration.

## Iblīs's Challenge to God

The accursed one petitioned for respite until the appointed hour (⟪ar:'یوم وقت معلوم'⟫):

⟪quran 7:14-15⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ أَنظِرْنِىٓ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ ‎ ‏قَالَ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنظَرِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *(Iblîs) said: "Allow me respite till the Day they are raised up (i.e. the Day of Resurrection)." (Allah) said: "You are of those allowed respite."*

Here ⟪ar:'یوم یبعثون'⟫ the Day of Resurrection refers to ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫ the Rising of the Qāʾim.

⟪quran 7:16-17⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ فَبِمَآ أَغْوَيْتَنِى لَأَقْعُدَنَّ لَهُمْ صِرَطَكَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ ‎ ‏ثُمَّ لَآتِيَنَّهُم مِّنۢ بَيْنِ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ وَعَنْ أَيْمَنِهِمْ وَعَن شَمَآئِلِهِمْ ۖ وَلَا تَجِدُ أَكْثَرَهُمْ شَكِرِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *(Iblîs) said: "Because You have sent me astray, surely I will sit in wait against them (human beings) on Your Straight Path. Then I will come to them from before them and behind them, from their right and from their left, and You will not find most of them as thankful ones (i.e. they will not be dutiful to You)."*

⟪ar:حارث بن مرۃ⟫ Ḥārith ibn Murrah declared to Imam al-Hunayd (a.s.): "Since you have barred me from advancement—prevented me from treading the ⟪ar:صراط مستقیم⟫ straight path of prophethood—I shall destroy all the descendants of Ādam born until the return of the Qāʾim, save for a remnant. I shall assail them from every quarter, with foot soldiers and cavalry, driving them astray, and you shall witness most of them fall prey to my deceptions."

⟪quran 17:62⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ أَرَءَيْتَكَ هَذَا ٱلَّذِى كَرَّمْتَ عَلَىَّ لَىِٕنْ أَخَّرْتَنِ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْقِيَمَةِ لَأَحْتَنِكَنَّ ذُرِّيَّتَهُۥٓ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
>
> *[Iblîs (Satan)] said: "See? This one whom You have honoured above me, if You give me respite (keep me alive) to the Day of Resurrection, I will surely seize and mislead his offspring (by sending them astray) all but a few!"*

Iblīs hurled this challenge at Imam al-Hunayd (a.s.): because of Ādam's elevation, which cost him his rank, he would ensure that none of Ādam's progeny attained his own former station.[^cite-6]

⟪quran 4:119-120⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَلَأُضِلَّنَّهُمْ وَلَأُمَنِّيَنَّهُمْ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُبَتِّكُنَّ آذَانَ ٱلْأَنْعَمِ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُغَيِّرُنَّ خَلْقَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَمَن يَتَّخِذِ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ وَلِيًّۭا مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ فَقَدْ خَسِرَ خُسْرَانًۭا مُّبِينًۭا ‎ ‏يَعِدُهُمْ وَيُمَنِّيهِمْ ۖ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎⟫
>
> *Verily, I will mislead them, and surely, I will arouse in them false desires; and certainly, I will order them to slit the ears of cattle, and indeed I will order them to change the nature created by Allah." And whoever takes Shaitan (Satan) as a Walî (protector or helper) instead of Allah, has surely suffered a manifest loss. He [Shaitan (Satan)] makes promises to them, and arouses in them false desires; and Shaitan's (Satan) promises are nothing but deceptions.*

"I shall establish my kingdom against yours until the Qāʾim appears," he boasted. "I shall erect my summons in opposition to your summons. I shall lead your servants astray by manifold deceptions: at times bestowing false hopes, at times inciting them to hatred and murder of your saints. The majority of your servants, you shall see, will join my ranks."

The Imam replied:

⟪quran 7:18⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱخْرُجْ مِنْهَا مَذْءُومًۭا مَّدْحُورًۭا ۖ لَّمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ لَأَمْلَأَنَّ جَهَنَّمَ مِنكُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *(Allah) said (to Iblîs) "Get out from this (Paradise) disgraced and expelled. Whoever of them (mankind) will follow you, then surely I will fill Hell with you all."*

⟪quran 17:63-65⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱذْهَبْ فَمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ فَإِنَّ جَهَنَّمَ جَزَآؤُكُمْ جَزَآءًۭ مَّوْفُورًۭا ‎ ‏وَٱسْتَفِزِزْ مَنِ ٱسْتَطَعْتَ مِنْهُم بِصَوْتِكَ وَأَجْلِبْ عَلَيْهِم بِخَيْلِكَ وَرَجِلِكَ وَشَارِكْهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَمْوَلِ وَٱلْأَوْلَدِ وَعِدْهُمْ ۚ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎ ‏إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَنٌۭ ۚ وَكَفَىٰ بِرَبِّكَ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫
>
> *(Allah) said: "Go, and whosoever of them follows you, surely! Hell will be the recompense of you (all) an ample recompense. "And Istafziz [literally means: befool them gradually] those whom you can among them with your voice (i.e. songs, music, and any other call for Allah's disobedience), make assaults on them with your cavalry and your infantry, mutually share with them wealth and children (by tempting them to earn money by illegal ways usury, etc., or by committing illegal sexual intercourse, etc.), and make promises to them." But Satan promises them nothing but deceit. "Verily! My slaves (i.e the true believers of Islamic Monotheism), you have no authority over them. And All-Sufficient is your Lord as a Guardian."*

⟪ar:امام ھنید علیہ السلام⟫ "Depart," said the Imam, "and do what you must; yet know that those who follow you shall be your companions, and all of you shall receive the fullness of recompense, where you shall suffer the severest punishment. Seduce whomsoever you can with your voice—your speeches, your rhetoric, your persuasions. Marshal your horsemen and your footsoldiers against them; infiltrate their wealth with doubts that corrupt their knowledge, and their children by turning the believers from love of the true Imāms. Those whom you overcome shall be yours, cast forth from the circle of faith, no longer believers. Beguile them with your false promises. But heed this: My sincere servants—over them you shall never hold dominion!"

In another place, the Qurʾān records:

⟪quran 15:36-42⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ رَبِّ فَأَنظِرْنِىٓ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ ‎ ‏قَالَ فَإِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنظَرِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْوَقْتِ ٱلْمَعْلُومِ ‎ ‏قَالَ رَبِّ بِمَآ أَغْوَيْتَنِى لَأُزَيِّنَنَّ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَأُغْوِيَنَّهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَّا عِبَادَكَ مِنْهُمُ ٱلْمُخْلَصِينَ ‎ ‏قَالَ هَذَا صِرَطٌ عَلَىَّ مُسْتَقِيمٌ ‎ ‏إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَنٌ إِلَّا مَنِ ٱتَّبَعَكَ مِنَ ٱلْغَاوِينَ ‎⟫

Allah said: "Then, verily, you are of those reprieved, "Till the Day of the time appointed." [Iblîs (Satan)] said: "O my Lord! Because you misled me, I shall indeed adorn the path of error for them (mankind) on the earth, and I shall mislead them all. "Except Your chosen, (guided) slaves among them." (Allah) said: "This is the Way which will lead straight to Me." "Certainly, you shall have no authority over My slaves, except those who follow you of the Ghawîn (Mushrikun and those who go astray, criminals, polytheists, and evil-doers, etc.)."*

Thus Allāh granted Iblīs respite until the Day of Resurrection, testing thereby the sincerity of the believers. Whoever falls into his snare proves himself not a human being of faith; but whosoever is saved from his deception becomes a sincere believer. Yet Allāh declared simultaneously that even one whom Shaytān seduces, deceives, and causes to stumble—if, before falling wholly into perdition, such a soul perceives the whispers of the Deceiver, repents of the transgression, and sincerely seeks forgiveness—shall not become of Shaytān's party but shall join the ranks of Allāh's servants.

Thus commenced, alongside the merciful invitation, the satanic summons, and the descendants of Ādam were divided into two camps: believers and hypocrites who followed the devils. The followers of Ādam were called the servants of Allāh, those over whom Shaytān could never triumph despite his every stratagem:

⟪quran 58:19⟫

>
> *Shaitan (Satan) has overtaken them. So he has made them forget the remembrance of Allah. They are the party of Shaitan (Satan). Verily, it is the party of Shaitan (Satan) that will be the losers!*

And in Sūrat Ibrāhīm, Allāh records that Iblīs himself, on the Day of Standing, shall address his followers:

>
> *And Shaitan (Satan) will say when the matter has been decided: "Verily, Allah promised you a promise of truth. And I too promised you, but I betrayed you. I had no authority over you except that I called you, so you responded to me. So blame me not, but blame yourselves. I cannot help you, nor can you help me. I deny your former act in associating me (Satan) as a partner with Allah (by obeying me in the life of the world). Verily, there is a painful torment for the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers, etc.)."*

>

For such He has written Faith in their hearts, and strengthened them with Ruh (proofs, light and true guidance) from Himself. And We will admit them to Gardens (Paradise) under which rivers flow, to dwell therein (forever). Allah is pleased with them, and they with Him. They are the Party of Allah. Verily, it is the Party of Allah that will be the successful.*

The enmity between Iblīs and Ādam became eternal, descending through the generations. Ādam received forgiveness through his repentance; Iblīs was cursed for his arrogance and expelled from Paradise, his spirit consumed with vengeance. He has waged open enmity against every prophet, messenger, executor of the will (waṣī), and Imām. Each tactic he wielded to undermine them, Allāh exposes:

⟪quran 6:112⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
>
> *And so We have appointed for every Prophet enemies - Shayatin (devils) among mankind and jinns, inspiring one another with adorned speech as a delusion (or by way of deception). If your Lord had so willed, they would not have done it, so leave them alone with their fabrications.*

One who follows the devil incurs guilt. Yet one who, after sin, repents and turns in remorse becomes not of Shaytān's congregation but a soldier of Allāh's army. Conversely, the one who harbors denial, arrogance, and stubborn refusal—these are the marks of the devil—becomes supreme Iblīs, guilty of enmity toward the Prophet. Those who obeyed Iblīs were the wicked and the deluded, and the test of those within the invitation intensified on all sides. Pressures descended first upon the muḥtajib (the veiled one), then upon the veils themselves. The people of the inner circle faced complete concealment; the world heard nothing of them or their forebears. Yet the veils (al-ḥijāb) endured a different trial: at times they were unveiled to view, and at times forced into hiding.

## Ādam's Being Deceived (Eating from the Forbidden Tree)

Allāh describes the moment:

⟪quran 7:20⟫

> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَلِدِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered suggestions to them both in order to uncover that which was hidden from them of their private parts (before); he said: "Your Lord did not forbid you this tree save you should become angels or become of the immortals."*

It is transmitted in hadith that Ḥārith ibn Murrah entered the garden in serpentine form—a creature outwardly gentle yet inwardly seething with lethal venom. Thus did Ḥārith approach Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ with honeyed words, befriending them whilst harboring enmity. Knowing that Ādam would resist his whispers, he turned his attention to the woman, saying: "The Imām has forbidden you from the pinnacle of knowledge lest you attain the station of the other limits, lest you become worthy of standing after you. Thus shall you remain ever in that position. The Imām withholds from you that eternal state which is your right."

He further explained: "Bodily actions belong to the lower ranks. Should you abandon them, you shall reach the level of the ⟪ar:'ملائکہ علیین'⟫ exalted angels and attain ⟪ar:علم روحانی لطیف⟫ the subtle spiritual science. Thus shall you shed the heavy burden of corporeal worship and action forever, becoming a ⟪ar:مَلَک⟫ celestial being, capable of fulfilling your every desire, dwelling eternally in Paradise."

Then, swearing a false oath before them both, he said:

⟪quran 7:21⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَقَاسَمَهُمَآ إِنِّى لَكُمَا لَمِنَ ٱلنَّصِحِينَ ‎⟫
>
> *And he [Shaitan (Satan)] swore by Allah to them both (saying): "Verily, I am one of the sincere well-wishers for you both."*

Maulānā ʿAlī teaches that Ādam was deceived precisely because Iblīs had sworn an oath. In the intellectual realm of Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ, falsehood had no place—they knew only truth; no deception dwelt in their imagination. When Shaytān swore to them, they believed him, for false oath-taking was among the impossibilities for them both.[^cite-7]

⟪quran 7:22⟫

> ⟪ar:‏فَدَلَّىٰهُمَا بِغُرُورٍۢ ۚ فَلَمَّا ذَاقَا ٱلشَّجَرَةَ بَدَتْ لَهُمَا سَوْءَتُهُمَا وَطَفِقَا يَخْصِفَانِ عَلَيْهِمَا مِن وَرَقِ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ وَنَادَىٰهُمَا رَبُّهُمَآ أَلَمْ أَنْهَكُمَا عَن تِلْكُمَا ٱلشَّجَرَةِ وَأَقُل لَّكُمَآ إِنَّ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ لَكُمَا عَدُوٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
>
> *So he misled them with deception. Then when they tasted of the tree, that which was hidden from them of their shame (private parts) became manifest to them and they began to stick together the leaves of Paradise over themselves (in order to cover their shame). And their Lord called out to them (saying): "Did I not forbid you that tree and tell you: Verily, Shaitan (Satan) is an open enemy unto you?"*

At Ḥawwāʾ's urging, Ādam tasted the fruit of the forbidden tree—that is, he transgressed the prescribed limits of his prophetic office and interfered in the work beyond his station, extending his hand where he had been stayed. The moment the book was unsealed and he attempted to read it, his nakedness was revealed to him. The sciences contained therein exceeded their comprehension; they immediately perceived their own weakness and want of intellect. "We deemed ourselves supremely wise," they realized, "yet we find ourselves profoundly deficient in these sciences."

The wrath of Imam al-Hunayd (a.s.) descended upon them. The Imām withdrew his support and revelation, stripped them of their dignity, and removed the garment of piety. That luminous veil of revelation had concealed the imperfections of their physical darkness and material grossness. Adorned with the light of divine aid, even this earthen vessel had shone like the sun, and all sciences had become transparent to them; they could comprehend and expound them to others. But the moment these supports were severed, even the simplest sciences escaped their understanding; they could no longer explain them to others. Now every tongue was loosed to criticize. For when one is elevated to a high station, he is honored; but when he falls from that rank, immediately the people's words become harsh, and he is brought low.

They tried to cover their shame with the leaves of Paradise—seeking to learn the sciences from their inferior limits—but none responded to them. Only the fig tree, moved with mercy, offered its leaves for their covering. Through this act, Allāh was pleased, and He joined the outer and inner dimensions of the fig in union.[^cite-8]

The Torah records that when Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ found themselves naked in the garden, panic-stricken, they heard the footsteps of Allāh in the plants. In their shame, they hid. A voice came: "Where are you?" They answered: "We are naked! When we heard Your footsteps, we hid in shame." The Lord asked: "Did you both eat from the tree?" Ādam replied: "The woman gave it to me." The woman said: "The serpent deceived me." The serpent answered: "Iblīs entered my belly."

The Imām addressed them: "This is precisely why I forbade you from acquiring these sciences—you were not yet capable of understanding them. My prohibition was not from unwillingness to grant them to you. And I told you that Shaytān was your enemy, yet you obeyed him and disobeyed Me."[^cite-9]

There is a teaching of Ḥazrat ʿAlī: every parent desires the finest nourishment for their children. Yet they do not feed the newborn anything save milk, knowing the infant lacks the capacity to digest richer foods. As soon as he develops the ability, they immediately provide the choicest provisions. Withholding and feeding serve alike the child's benefit.

Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (a.s.) spoke of the steadfastness of the believer, saying: "None of our saints walks with both feet firmly planted. But if one step slips, the other remains firm. He may stumble, yet he shall not fall from faith. Immediately upon committing error, he seeks forgiveness." By this principle, though Shaytān seduced Ādam Ṣafī Allāh (the Chosen One of God), he could not make him his own. Ādam fell into the deception of flattery, but the moment he realized his error, he turned back in repentance and sought pardon.

## Ādam's Expulsion from the Garden

When one examines the narrative of Adam's expulsion from Paradise in its literal sense, numerous questions and apparent difficulties arise. The first concerns the fundamental justice of the command: if the tree whose fruit was forbidden to Ādam bore fruit that was inherently wholesome and lawful, why was he prohibited from partaking of it? And if the tree itself was forbidden, how could it have existed within Paradise at all? When God made all the fruits of Paradise lawful for Ādam, why did He forbid him from this single tree and its fruit? Would it not have been simpler for God not to cultivate this tree in Paradise—especially since His knowledge of the unseen encompasses the certainty that this very tree would become the cause of Ādam's trial and affliction?

Some interpreters of the exoteric tradition (ahl al-ẓāhir) identify the tree as ⟪ar:'گیہوں'⟫—wheat, a grain. If this is indeed correct, one must ask: why was this tree forbidden to Ādam when it remains lawful for his descendants, whether believers or disbelievers? In response, certain scholars propose that because Ādam lacked an intestinal system, the fruit was forbidden so that it would not rot in his stomach and cause him suffering. Yet if this explanation were accepted, the same objection would apply to all other fruits of Paradise—they too would have entered his body and caused disorder. A further question then arises with greater force: why do Ādam's children bear the punishment for their father's transgression? Why were they not created in Paradise from the outset? The error belonged to the father, not to the children. For what crime do they suffer expulsion?

Some scholars attempt to answer this by claiming that God knew in advance that Ādam's descendants would commit the very transgression that Ādam committed. Therefore, they were not created in Paradise. But this answer poses its own difficulty: Did God not possess foreknowledge of Ādam's lapse? If He did know, why did He create Ādam in Paradise at all? Would He not have created him in the world, as He did his descendants?

The author of this book offers two categories of answer to all such questions. One pertains to Ādam of this material world; the other concerns Ādam of the eternal realm.

---

### Ādam of the Material World (Ādam al-Dunyawī)

Drawing upon the knowledge transmitted by the Imāms, the author explains that there are two dimensions or interpretations of this tree—one in the spiritual sense, one in the infernal sense.

#### The Tree in the Spiritual Sense

The Shayṭān (Satan) whispered to Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ:

⟪quran 7:20⟫

> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَلِدِينَ ‎⟫
> 
> "Then Satan whispered to them both, in order to reveal to them that which had been hidden from them of their private parts, and said: 'Your Lord has not forbidden you from this tree except that you become angels or become of the immortals.'"

In its noble sense, ⟪ar:'علوم حقائق'⟫ *the knowledge of realities* refers to the tree of sciences that are forbidden to the unworthy—those sciences of the Imāms that bestow eternity and liberation from the mortal realm. Yet such sciences cannot be acquired or understood save through obedience to the Imāms and through sincere affiliation with them. These teachings are unveiled to the worthy only at the proper hour. In yet a higher sense, some have stated that the tree represents the knowledge of the hierarchy of the ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ *the Qāʾim upon his remembrance, peace be upon him*, and the hidden realities concerning the mysteries of ⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ *the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib*—secrets veiled from countless eyes and forbidden to Ādam. These sciences were forbidden because they constituted the commencement of the divine cycle (dawr), during which such knowledge could not be disclosed, for they belong to the era of unveiling (kashf).[^cite-10]

Iblīs deceived Ādam and usurped the rank of his successor—Hābīl (Abel)—thus arousing the jealousy of his brother Qābīl (Cain). This jealousy culminated in murder, first accomplished in the inner realm, then manifested outwardly.

#### The Tree in the Infernal Sense

In another meaning, the ⟪ar:'درخت'⟫ *tree* was Iblīs himself. The answer to why such a tree existed in Paradise is that Iblīs was himself a great preacher and ⟪ar:'معلم الملکوت'⟫ *teacher of the realm of dominion*—the guide of all angels. Yet when he refused to prostrate before Ādam, he was cast out. In this interpretation, the prohibition against eating the fruit means that Ādam was forbidden to reveal to Iblīs any secret of the *daʿwa* (the summons), for Iblīs had been cursed through his disobedience.[^cite-11]

#### Why Were Ādam's Children Deprived of Paradise?

Regarding the question of why Ādam's descendants were denied Paradise, the author states: In one sense, Paradise—⟪ar:دار الدعوۃ⟫ *the house of invitation*—is the abode whose purpose is the attainment of true Paradise itself. Ādam's Paradise was, in reality, a station of supreme rank. Every elevated station constitutes Paradise for one of inferior station; and every lower rank constitutes earth (the world) for one above it. The highest limit of these stations is true Heaven; their lowest is the mortal world. Ādam was created in exalted rank, which carried the ⟪ar:'تائیدات'⟫ *the supports and confirmations* characteristic of the station of Paradise. Thence he was demoted to the rank designated ⟪ar:تائیدات⟫ *confirmations*, and further descended until he reached the rank of *al-mustajībīn* (the respondents), which is the lowest rank among the grades, though not wholly excluded from the hierarchy.

The station of *⟪ar:'تعلیمات '⟫ teachings* is the rank of those ⟪ar:مفیدین⟫ *benefactors* whose duty it is to educate those below them—the ⟪ar:مستجیبین⟫ *respondents*—and to guide them toward obedience to the commandments of the Sharīʿa, thus elevating them through the ranks toward Paradise. The knowledge of the inner reality (ʿilm bāṭin) is not permissible for the *mustajīb*, the respondent. For this grade, only obedience to the commandments of the Sharīʿa is obligatory. A respondent must obey the authority above him without hesitation, following the commands of the Sharīʿa whether or not he comprehends the inner meaning of those commands.

When a benefactor observes that the respondent has faithfully observed the commandments of the Sharīʿa, he elevates him to a rank where the inner knowledge (⟪ar:علم باطن⟫) is disclosed. The progeny of a benefactor is itself benefactor to those below. The descendants of Ādam are among the descendants of the ⟪ar:مطبعین⟫ *printers*, all of whom hold the rank of *al-mustajībīn*—the respondents—whose station is upon this earth. And since the respondents are not people of Paradise, the earth is their proper abode. Therefore Ādam's children were not created in Paradise, but rather in this world.[^cite-12]

**Principle:**

It is stated in the principles of jurisprudence (*usūl*) that a prophet cannot be a wrongdoer. Apparently, Ādam (peace be upon him) erred—and the wrongdoer is a wrongdoer whose abode is Hell. By this reasoning, Ādam cannot be a prophet. Yet the truth is that Ādam possessed a group of sinful souls upon whom this retribution was to be executed. Ādam's lower veil (the animal soul) committed the error and descended to the islands; his higher veil (the rational soul) remained among the noble. In every cycle (dawr), there appears an Ādam, and he too errs—thus the cycle turns.[^cite-13]

### Ādam of the Spiritual World (Ādam al-Rawḥānī): The Second Emanation

The second explanation of this narrative pertains to the realm of eternity. It concerns ⟪ar:'منبعث ثانی'⟫ *the Second Emanator*, Ādam who was created as intellect. His Paradise was the spiritual world wherein he dwelt with all other intellects. The tree represented the station of *al-Manbaʿ al-Awwal* (the First Emanation), whose access and equality were not permitted to the Second Emanation, for the First Emanation surpassed it in nobility.

The "devil" was his wicked thought—a false notion of equality that led him to rebel and claim equality with the First Emanation. God the Creator forbade him to aspire to such equality and to abandon this idea of rank-equivalence.

The duty of the Second Emanation was to obey its predecessor (the First Emanation) just as the First Emanation obeyed *its* predecessor (al-ʿAql al-Awwal, the First Intellect). To "eat the fruit of the tree" signified rebellion—the desire to claim equality with the rank of the First Emanation. For this transgression, he was expelled from Heaven and fell from his station. The Seven Intellects transcended him, though he had excelled in the affirmation of divine unity (tawḥīd). These seven intellects are in truth the words of God (al-Manbaʿ al-Awwal), mentioned in the Qur'ān:

⟪quran 2:37⟫

> ⟪ar:‏فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَتٍۢ فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫
> 
> "Then Adam received from his Lord words, and He relented toward him. Indeed, He is the Receiver of Repentance, the Merciful."

Through these words (seven utterances), Ādam repented to his Lord (the First Emanation), and his repentance was accepted. He was restored to Paradise (the ranks). His descendants are those who imitated him in the spiritual world and equated themselves with him. All of them, together with the Devil of the Spirit, were cast forth from the spiritual realm into the physical world. Iblīs was not restored to Heaven because of his stubbornness and remains eternally cursed.

Iblīs's hatred for Ādam and his descendants (the community of the penitent) manifested as opposition and obstinacy. The descendants of Ādam who dwelt in the first eternal world were elevated and returned to Paradise like their father Ādam. Those who obeyed Iblīs were cast into Hell alongside their leader—and this Hell is their true abode.[^cite-14]

## Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ's Repentance

No sooner had the consequences of disobedience descended upon both of them than they recognized their transgression. Immediately, both turned to God in repentance and uttered:

⟪quran 7:23⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَا رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَآ أَنفُسَنَا وَإِن لَّمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ ٱلْخَسِرِينَ ‎⟫
> 
> "They said, 'Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves. If You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we will surely be among the losers.'"

They acknowledged their transgression beyond measure and their disobedience to Him. They pleaded: if You withhold forgiveness and mercy from us, we shall surely be among the utterly ruined. Neither persisted in sin nor made excuses. Both confessed their fault forthright and sought pardon and absolution.

**Principle:**

Here the distinction between Iblīs and Ādam becomes manifest. Both erred—yet Iblīs insisted upon his transgression and grew only more obstinate, whereas Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ repented at once and sought God's forgiveness.

The Imām (peace be upon him) then addressed them with these words:

⟪quran 2:36⟫

> ⟪ar:‏فَأَزَلَّهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ عَنْهَا فَأَخْرَجَهُمَا مِمَّا كَانَا فِيهِ ۖ وَقُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> 
> "Thus the Satan caused them to slip from the Garden, and brought them out of the state in which they were. And We said, 'Descend—all of you—enemies one to another. And for you on the earth is a dwelling place and an enjoyment for a time.'"

⟪quran 7:24-25⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎ ‏قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> "He said, 'Descend—all of you—enemies one to another. And for you on the earth is a dwelling place and an enjoyment for a time.' He said, 'In it you shall live, and in it you shall die, and from it you shall be brought forth.'"

That is, the Imām expelled both of them from His presence (Paradise)—which ⟪ar:'حضرات'⟫ was the highest station—and sent them to ⟪ar:'جزائر'⟫ *the islands*, the material realm. In the noble court of the Imāmate, they possessed direct knowledge and had no need for study or toil. Whatever question arose, the answer came immediately from the Imām's presence. But on the islands—the physical world—a chain of transmitted teaching exists, requiring gradual education and arduous effort to acquire knowledge. This became their punishment for disobedience. The Imām said to them: "We bestowed upon you these confirmations, which you did not appreciate but coveted instead. Now depart to the islands and labor. Progress step by step, that you may understand how luxurious your former state was. Now taste bitter water, that you may know the value of fresh."

⟪quran 2:37⟫

> ⟪ar:‏فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَتٍۢ فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫
> 
> "Then Adam received from his Lord words, and He relented toward him. Indeed, He is the Receiver of Repentance, the Merciful."

As soon as the Imām decreed their descent, both learned through repentance the ⟪ar:'ملائکہ علیین'⟫ *lofty words* (al-malāʾika al-ʿilliyyūn)—those ⟪ar:کلمات⟫ *words* by means of which they could draw near [to God], and the ⟪ar:أسمآء مبارکۃ⟫ *blessed names* by which they could regain their lost Paradise—the station of prophethood. Ḥazrat Ẓīʾ Ṣāḥib relates that these words were in truth the blessed attributes of the Panj Tān Pāk (the Five Pure Ones): ⟪ar:محمد و علی و فاطمۃ و حسن و حسین علیھم السلام⟫ *Muḥammad and ʿAlī and Fāṭima and al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn, upon them be peace*. He [Ādam] took the source of these supreme names from these exalted books, though he could not understand them at first. Through these holy names he repented and said to the Imām: "We implore your forgiveness by means of these sacred names." The Imām accepted Ādam's repentance and forgave him. Verily, the Imām was the Receiver of Repentance and the Merciful!

Thus did Ādam return to the Imām through these words; the Imām pardoned him and restored him to the station of prophethood. Satan was vanquished, and God's promise was fulfilled.[^cite-15]

This narrative teaches the essential lesson that if a believer commits sin yet repents and seeks God's forgiveness and the intercession of God's saints (awliyāʾ), God forgives him at once and does not exclude him from the congregation of God. Conversely, one who commits sin, does not regard it as transgression, neither seeks forgiveness nor solicits the help of the saints, abandons the congregation of God and joins the congregation of Satan, becoming a member of his ranks.

God has thus established two conditions for the forgiveness of sins. First: sincere repentance and seeking pardon for the transgression. Second: seeking the intercession of God's saints. The role of the Messenger and God's saints is indispensable in the seeking of forgiveness, as God declares in *Sūrat al-Nisāʾ*:

⟪quran 4:64⟫

> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن رَّسُولٍ إِلَّا لِيُطَاعَ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَلَوْ أَنَّهُمْ إِذ ظَّلَمُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَهُمْ جَآءُوكَ فَٱسْتَغْفَرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَٱسْتَغْفَرَ لَهُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ لَوَجَدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابًۭا رَّحِيمًۭا ‎⟫
> 
> "And We sent no Messenger except to be obeyed by God's leave. If they, when they had wronged themselves, had come to you and sought God's forgiveness, and the Messenger had sought forgiveness for them, they would have found God the Receiver of Repentance, the Merciful."

## The Descent from the Garden to the Earth

God forgave them both and restored them to the station of the Khilāfa and Imāma, yet He deprived them of His direct support and divine sustenance. He removed them from His presence and the court of the Imāms, sending them to the islands; henceforward they would receive guidance indirectly. Formerly, in the presence of the Imām, they had received these confirmations directly. But now He said:

⟪quran 2:38-39⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ مِنْهَا جَمِيعًۭا ۖ فَإِمَّا يَأْتِيَنَّكُم مِّنِّى هُدًۭى فَمَن تَبِعَ هُدَاىَ فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ وَكَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَتِنَآ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> "We said, 'Descend from it, all of you. Yet when there comes to you from Me a guidance, whoever follows My guidance—no fear shall be upon them, nor shall they sorrow. But those who disbelieve and deny Our signs—those are the inhabitants of the Fire; in it they shall abide.'"

⟪quran 7:24-25⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎ ‏قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> "He said, 'Descend—all of you—enemies one to another. And for you on the earth is a dwelling place and an enjoyment for a time.' He said, 'In it you shall live, and in it you shall die, and from it you shall be brought forth.'"

Not only Iblīs, therefore, but also Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ and their descendants were deprived of the presence of the nobles and sent forth to the islands. The Imām made clear: against your invitation to the islands, Iblīs shall establish his satanic summons and shall lead your children astray. Yet I shall guide your children through you. Whoever accepts My invitation, I shall restore him to My Paradise. And whoever accepts Satan's invitation shall be with him; I shall confine him to this world—the islands—forever, and he shall never enter My sanctuary.

The Imām declared: "For you and your descendants, there is a place and an abode and enjoyment in the islands for an appointed time." Then He said: "In this earth you shall be born, in it you shall die, and from it you shall be raised up."

Thus Ādam was demoted from the station of direct support, yet God did not strip him entirely of His grace and blessing. Now ⟪ar:حدود روحانیہ علویہ (کلمات)⟫ *spiritual limits on high—the words*—were established as intermediaries. They became Ādam's mediators; Ādam became intermediary to the limits below him; and this chain continued unbroken throughout the hierarchy. The Prophet (peace and blessings of God be upon him) said: "I receive revelation from Gabriel; Gabriel from Michael; Michael from Isrāfīl; Isrāfīl from the Tablet; and from the Pen." That is, revelation now descends to the Nāṭiq (the Speaker) through five intermediaries, and from the Nāṭiq those sciences are transmitted to the five spiritual limits beneath him, and from them to the believers.[^cite-16]

| **Upper Limit** | Pen | Tablet | Isrāfīl | The Speaker (Nāṭiq) | The Intellect |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Lower Limit** | Basis | Imām | Proof (Ḥujja) | Guardian (Nāqib) | Wing |

The sixth is he who receives from them—the believer.

Iblīs became the enemy of Ādam, and two summonses were pitted against one another: Ādam's invitation to truth, and the Devil's invitation to falsehood. Whoever maintains ties with Ādam and the people of Ādam is called *Walī Allāh* (a friend of God) and joins God's congregation. Whoever disbelieves in them, denies them, and opposes them becomes a confederate of the Devil—for Satan has prevailed over him, and he has forgotten the remembrance of God. The events that transpired with Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ also manifested in their descendants, taking the same form. Even to this day, only one division remains among mankind: the distinction between male and female. Similarly, among their progeny there are but two classes: the obedient and the disobedient. God promised to settle them both upon this earth and to dwell them in Paradise through repentance and forgiveness. So too did He threaten the hypocrites and polytheists with punishment, saying:

⟪quran 5:51⟫

> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ لَا تَتَّخِذُوا۟ ٱلْيَهُودَ وَٱلنَّصَرَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآءَ ۘ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَآءُ بَعْضٍۢ ۚ وَمَن يَتَوَلَّهُم مِّنكُمْ فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
>

Whoever loves them and professes their *wilāya* (guardianship) shall be counted among their congregation. Whoever is the friend of Satan is Satan, and shall be called Satan's son. He shall have no tie or relation to Ādam. For this reason God cast out Nūḥ's son—Ḥām—from the family of Nūḥ, saying:

⟪quran 11:46⟫

> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَنُوحُ إِنَّهُۥ لَيْسَ مِنْ أَهْلِكَ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ عَمَلٌ غَيْرُ صَلِحٍۢ ۖ فَلَا تَسْـَٔلْنِ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۖ إِنِّىٓ أَعِظُكَ أَن تَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْجَهِلِينَ ‎⟫

## Qābīl's Envy and Hābīl's Martyrdom

God appointed Ādam (peace be upon him) as caliph over those like him and named him the Messenger of God and the Proof of God. His generation grew and multiplied. At the twilight of his life, he designated his son ⟪ar:ھابیل⟫ *Hābīl* as his successor and heir. His brother ⟪ar:قابیل⟫ *Qābīl* was consumed by jealousy and slew him, supposing that if Hābīl were removed, his father would be compelled to appoint him in his stead. When his delusion failed to materialize and his hopes were dashed, he turned against his father Ādam. Through his persistence in sin, he wrought evil and corruption in the *daʿwa*, and became Satan's accomplice.

When Hābīl was martyred, God blessed Ādam with another son: ⟪ar:'شیث علیہ السلام'⟫ *Shīth, peace be upon him*. Ādam named him ⟪ar:ھبۃ اللہ⟫ *Hiba Allāh* ("the gift of God") and appointed him successor in Hābīl's place. In secret, he entrusted Shīth with all the sciences of esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl) and the realities of divine truth, and with the affairs of the *daʿwa*. He did not proclaim this publicly lest the enemies gain improper advantage. Only the higher limits and the sincere believers were privy to this knowledge. In this manner Ādam withdrew from active service in the *daʿwa* upon reaching the proper time for retirement. From Shīth onward, his descendants preserved the chain of Imāmate and Prophethood. Among his progeny was the Prophet Idrīs. Thus the path of guidance continued unbroken in Ādam's line until the age of ⟪ar:نوح علیہ السلام⟫ *Nūḥ, peace be upon him*, arrived.[^cite-17]

## Ādam as the Living Embodiment of the *Bismillāh*

Ādam (peace be upon him) is the symbolic embodiment of the opening verse of ⟪ar:سورۃ الحمد⟫ *Sūrat al-Ḥamd* (the Chapter of Praise), for he was the one who inaugurated the divine cycle, and it is recited before all things commence. Moreover, in your name ⟪ar:عبد اللہ⟫ *ʿAbd Allāh* (the servant of God), there resides the wisdom that ⟪ar:بسم اللہ⟫ *Bismillāh* (in the name of God) represents—*"I am."*

He is the embodiment of faith on the days of religion, and he is the embodiment of *wilāya* (divine guardianship) in the ordinances of Islam. You are the first being whose *wilāya* God has made obligatory and concerning whom He commanded the angels to prostrate. The meaning of prostration is truly obedience (*taʿāt*), which is the very proof of *wilāya*. All the angels prostrated except Satan. *Wilāya* was Ādam's crucible, and Ādam was the like of Him [God]. The whole creation is not a disbeliever save through lack of his *wilāya*; and whoever lacks his *wilāya* shall be of no avail to those who come after him. He is the first of the Prophets, the Messengers, and the Imāms of the faith—the father of them all—and he is the source of the binding obligation of *wilāya* that God has enjoined.

In the human body, you possess the station of semen dwelling in the womb of mother and father before the seed of the child, because the members of the revealed are dispersed throughout the world. This is called the special summary that is ⟪ar:'سلالہ'⟫ *salāla* (procreated essence), preserved in the parents. The Sharīʿa of Muḥammad, destined to emerge after six thousand years, was founded and established by him.[^cite-18]

## Despair of God's Mercy Is a Sin

In his work *Asās al-Taʾwīl* (The Foundation of Esoteric Exegesis), the illustrious Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān advises Naʿīm ibn Muḥammad—may God be pleased with him—saying: "O believer! Whenever you commit an error and fall into sin, repent immediately and seek forgiveness through the saints of God, that you may remain in God's congregation—the congregation of Ādam. Do not, through obstinacy, join the congregation of Iblīs. Remember! No matter how grave the sin, repentance washes it away as soap cleanses and illumines cloth. Do not despair of God's mercy; otherwise you shall be among the disbelieving people." Then He gives us glad tidings, saying:

⟪quran 12:87⟫

> ⟪ar:‏يَبَنِىَّ ٱذْهَبُوا۟ فَتَحَسَّسُوا۟ مِن يُوسُفَ وَأَخِيهِ وَلَا تَا۟يْـَٔسُوا۟ مِن رَّوْحِ ٱللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ لَا يَا۟يْـَٔسُ مِن رَّوْحِ ٱللَّهِ إِلَّا ٱلْقَوْمُ ٱلْكَفِرُونَ ‎⟫

⟪quran 15:56⟫
