# Hazrat Adam (peace be upon him)

<!-- section 1 (id=1478, raw_sort=1): آدم علیہ السلام کا ظاہری قصہ -->

## The Outer Story (Ẓāhir) of Ādam, Peace Be Upon Him

### The Birth of Adam from Clay

When Allah intended to create Ādam, revelation came to the earth: "O earth! I am about to create a creature from you. Some of them will obey Me, and others will disobey. The obedient I shall admit to Paradise, and the disobedient to Hell." The Almighty then sent Gabriel to the earth, commanding, "Go and bring dust from the soil." When Gabriel arrived and began to gather the dust, the earth cried out, "I adjure you by the glory of Allah who has sent you that you take nothing from my soil that will be destined for Hell." Gabriel returned empty-handed and reported to Allah, "The earth swore by Your honor, and I dared not disobey." Allah then sent Michael, and the earth made the same plea; Michael too returned without taking anything. Finally, Allah sent Azrael, the Angel of Death (⟪ar:ملک الموت⟫). When the earth swore to him in the same manner, Azrael replied, "I swear by Allah that I shall not disobey His command"—and proceeded to gather dust from the four corners of the earth, little by little.[^cite-1] The soil was soft and hard, hot and cold, white and red, black and yellow, pure and impure. This diversity of earthly matter explains the multiplicity of human temperaments, moral dispositions, and complexions among the children of Ādam.

One who inquired of the Messenger of Allah (may Allah bless him and grant him peace) asked, "How did Allah create Ādam?" The Prophet replied: "Ādam's forehead was fashioned from the clay of the Ka'bah, his ankles from Egyptian soil, his feet from the clay of the Hijāz, his right hand from the dust of the east, and his left hand from the dust of the west."[^cite-2]

### Kneading the Soil in Different Waters

When Azrael brought this handful of soil before Allah's throne, he was commanded to mix it with water and knead it thoroughly. Three types of water were employed—bitter, sweet, and salt—and the soil was kneaded until it became *ṭīn* (clay), a substance capable of receiving form. The Almighty then commanded Gabriel: "Go to the earth and bring forth white, luminous, and radiant dust from which I shall create." Gabriel descended with a great host of angels and gathered pure, white, and transparent soil from the grave of the Prophet Muḥammad (⟪ar:محمد علیہ السلام⟫). This sacred earth was then kneaded with the water of *Tasnīm* (⟪ar:'تسنیم'⟫), the spring of Paradise, until it became lustrous as pearl. The pearl was then immersed in all the springs of Paradise and bathed in their waters. When the gaze of Allah fell upon it, the pearl trembled with awe and shattered into one hundred twenty-four thousand drops, from each of which Allah created a Prophet. All the prophets were thus created from the light of Muḥammad.[^cite-3] This pearl, Allah's masterpiece, was then circulated through the heavens and the earth so that the angels might know and recognize Muḥammad before Ādam existed. For this reason the Prophet declared: "I was a prophet when Ādam was still between water and clay" (⟪ar-quote:کنت نبیا کان آدم بین المآء و الطین⟫).

### Ādam's Soil Left for Forty Years

The soil of Ādam, kneaded with water, was left to rest for forty years until it became soft and malleable, capable of taking any shape. Then it remained untouched for another forty years, until it hardened and dried, producing a hollow sound when struck—as described in the Qur'ān:

> ⟪quran 55:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏خَلَقَ ٱلْإِنسَنَ مِن صَلْصَلٍۢ كَٱلْفَخَّارِ ‎⟫
> *He created man from sounding clay like pottery.*

Allah fashioned Ādam's body from this dry, hardened clay to make manifest His perfect power—creating not from soft, supple matter, but from brittle, unyielding substance. The body was then placed in the path of the angels for forty years.

### The Birth of Ādam and Satan's Wonder

As the angels passed by and beheld the beauty of Ādam's form, they marveled, for they had never witnessed such comeliness. But when Iblīs (Satan) passed by, he reflected: "Why has this statue been made?" He touched it with his hand. Finding it hollow within, he entered through the mouth and exited through the same opening, declaring to his fellow angels: "This is a strange creature, utterly hollow inside. It will not endure; it cannot remain firm and strong!" Then he said to them: "Suppose this statue were given dominion over you—what would you do?" All replied: "We would obey its Lord." But Iblīs said in his heart: "If this statue is given dominion over me, I shall disobey it. And if I excel over it, I shall not honor it."

### The Reasons for Sorrow and Joy in Humanity

A ḥadīth narrates that Ādam's body lay in this state for forty years, showered continuously with grief and sorrow; then for a single year it was rained upon by joy. This is the reason why the children of Ādam are predominantly sorrowful and sad, finding relief and joy only intermittently. As a poet has said:

> *Sorrow has passed, and happiness has passed.*
> *If you possess the strength to grieve, then there is no sorrow.*

### The Manifestation of Ādam's Virtue After Creation's Completion

When Ādam's body had been formed, Allah clothed him in the garments of Paradise and adorned him with all manner of ornament. His teeth shone like the rays of the sun, and the light of the Prophet Muḥammad (⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫) gleamed upon his forehead like the moonlight of the fourteenth night. Allah seated him upon a throne and lifted him upon the shoulders of the angels, commanding them to carry him through all the heavens so that he might witness the entire universe and increase his certainty (⟪ar:عین الیقین⟫)—direct witnessing of truth. The angels bore him upon their shoulders for nearly a hundred years until Ādam became intimate with every corner of Paradise. Then Allah created a horse of pure musk, which He named *Maymūn* (⟪ar:'میمون'⟫), and adorned it with pearls and jewels. Ādam was placed upon it; Gabriel took the reins, while Michael and Isrāfīl joined the procession on right and left, and they circled all the heavens. In each heaven, Ādam greeted the angels, saying: "Peace be upon you, O angels of Allah" (⟪ar:'السلام علیکم یا ملائکۃ اللہ'⟫). Allah said to Ādam: "O Ādam! This greeting is a blessing and a gift to all believers born from you and your descendants until the Day of Resurrection."

Allah then taught Ādam all the names (⟪ar:'أسمآء'⟫)—the divine attributes and their manifestations. When the angels were asked about these names, they replied:

> ⟪quran 2:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالُوا۟ سُبْحَنَكَ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَآ إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ⟫
> *"Glory be to You! We have no knowledge except what You have taught us. Surely, You are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise."*

Allah then commanded Ādam to teach these names to the angels. When Ādam had imparted them all, the angels became his students, and all prostrated before him in submission. But Iblīs refused obedience and displayed arrogance.

### The Creation of Ḥawwāʾ (Eve)

The exegetes relate that when Allah established Ādam in Paradise, he was seized by loneliness. Seeing his distress and taking pity upon him, Allah resolved to create a companion like unto himself. He cast Ādam into deep sleep and drew forth from his left rib—which is called the *qasīrā* (⟪ar:'قصیریٰ'⟫)—and from it fashioned Eve. Throughout this process, Ādam felt no pain whatsoever. Had he suffered in any way, he would not have been drawn to Eve, nor would subsequent men have shown interest in women.

Eve was then clothed in the raiment of Paradise and adorned with every adornment, seated upon Ādam's pillow with sixteen beauties. When Ādam awoke, he beheld a radiant, beautiful face upon his pillow. An angel asked him: "What is she?" He answered: "A woman" (⟪ar:'عورت'⟫). "What is her name?" "Eve" (⟪ar:'حوا'⟫), he replied. The angel said: "Rightly named! But tell me—why is she called Eve?" He answered: "Because she was created from a living thing" (⟪ar:حی⟫)—from a living being. "For what purpose did Allah create her?" "That I might find tranquility in her, and she in me," Ādam replied. The Almighty has narrated this account as follows:

> ⟪quran 4:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱتَّقُوا۟ رَبَّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍۢ وَحِدَةٍۢ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًۭا كَثِيرًۭا وَنِسَآءًۭ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى تَسَآءَلُونَ بِهۦ وَٱلْأَرْحَامَ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ عَلَيْكُمْ رَقِيبًۭا ‎⟫
> *O mankind! Fear your Lord, who created you from a single soul, and from it created its mate, and from the two of them scattered many men and women. Fear Allah by means of whom you demand your mutual rights, and do not cut ties of kinship. Surely Allah is ever watching over you.*

The Messenger of Allah said: "Woman was created from Ādam's curved rib. If you try to straighten it, you will break it; if you leave it as it is, you will benefit from it." In old age, men grow more attractive while women's beauty fades—for man is created from clay, and earth does not become base or ugly with the passage of time; rather, its virtue is refined. Woman, by contrast, is created from flesh, which rots and decays over time, losing its appeal.

### Ādam's Making Eve Lawful for Himself

In certain ḥadīths it is related that when Ādam beheld Eve, he extended his hand toward her. The angels stopped him, saying: "Wait!" Ādam replied: "Allah created her for me!" They answered: "This is true, but it is not permissible for you until you pay her bridal gift (*mahr*)." He asked: "What is required of me, and how much?" They said: "You must invoke blessings upon Muḥammad (⟪ar:حضرت محمد مصطفی (ﷺ)⟫) three times." Ādam asked: "Who is Muḥammad?" They replied: "The last of the prophets among your descendants. Had it not been for the purpose of creating him, you would not have been created."[^cite-4]

### Ādam and Eve's Sojourn in Paradise

When Allah granted Ādam and Eve a dwelling in Paradise, He made lawful for them all the blessings of that garden save one tree, saying:

> ⟪quran 2:35⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلْنَا يَٓـَٔادَمُ ٱسْكُنْ أَنتَ وَزَوْجُكَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَكُلَا مِنْهَا رَغَدًا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمَا وَلَا تَقْرَبَا هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةَ فَتَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And We said: "O Ādam, dwell you and your wife in the Garden, and eat from it wherever you wish. But do not approach this tree, lest you become wrongdoers."*

### The Devil's Plot

When Allah had settled Ādam and Eve in Paradise and made lawful for them all its blessings, they lived in great luxury and comfort. But Iblīs, the accursed (⟪ar:أبلیس لعین⟫), envied them both. As soon as he found entry into the Paradise from which he had been expelled, he began to devise means of deception. He entered Paradise in the form of a serpent (⟪ar:حیۃ⟫), so that Ādam and Eve would not recognize him.[^cite-5] According to certain traditions, Iblīs wrapped himself in the feet of a peacock and entered thereby. Now Moore—in truth a servant of Ādam—had maintained an old friendship with Iblīs. When Iblīs drew near to Ādam and Eve, he wept loudly and bitterly, causing them both to weep with him. Thus Iblīs became the first to weep, which is why it is said that weeping is Satan's voice. Both asked sympathetically: "Why do you weep?" He replied: "I grieve for your helplessness and wretchedness, and your impending ruin. I see that after a few years you both shall die, and all this blessing and honor must be abandoned." He spoke with such painful eloquence that both fell into profound sorrow at his words. Having said this, Iblīs departed, weeping.

Some days later, he returned to them when they were still under the influence of his previous statements and grieving. Seeing that his arrow had struck home, he spoke with sympathy: "O Ādam, shall I show you the way to a tree from which you will live eternally, and your kingdom will never fade?" Ādam replied: "Yes!" Iblīs then pointed to the forbidden tree and said: "Eat from it, and you shall live forever." Ādam cried out: "My Lord has forbidden me from it!" Iblīs said: "That is precisely why He forbade it—so that you two shall not become angels (⟪ar:مَلَک⟫) and remain in Paradise forever." When Ādam refused, Iblīs swore by Allah: "I am truly your well-wisher." They were both deceived by his oath, for they could not imagine that anyone would swear falsely by Allah. Eve hastened to eat from the tree and then gave it to Ādam to eat. As soon as they tasted the fruit and transgressed their appointed limits, the garment of Paradise fell from their bodies, and they became naked. Ādam, alarmed and distressed, began to run about. They approached the tree of grapes (⟪ar:عناب⟫) and plucked leaves with which to cover themselves. At that moment the Lord called out: "Ādam! Are you fleeing from Me?" He replied: "Yes, my Lord! I flee from You out of shame."

### The Reward to the Fig Bush

It is narrated that when Ādam and Eve became naked and began to seek leaves from the trees of Paradise to conceal their bodies, every bush rejected them. Only the fig bush took pity upon them and offered its leaves, with which Ādam and Eve covered their nakedness. To reward the fig tree for this kindness, Allah made its outward appearance equal to its inner quality, rendered it sweet and beneficial, and caused it to bear fruit twice yearly. The fig's peculiarity is that it produces fruit without flowering—a singular grace among trees.

### Ādam's Expulsion from Paradise

When Ādam was expelled because of his disobedience, and a proclamation came forth from Allah that the disobedient could not approach His presence, Ādam took Eve's hand and moved onward. At each step they looked back, hoping for their Lord's mercy, weeping in profound shame, until they took the final step out of Paradise. Then from their lips came the words: "In the name of Allah, the Most Compassionate, the Most Merciful" (⟪ar:'بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم'⟫).[^cite-6] Gabriel appeared and stopped them, saying: "O Ādam! A word has issued from your tongue, and I hope that its blessing and greatness shall bring you mercy." Allah said to Gabriel: "I shall have mercy upon him and his descendants on the Day of Resurrection when they turn back to Me."

Ādam descended upon Mount Saradeep in India, while Eve descended in Jeddah in the Hijāz. It is said that Ādam was so ashamed after his expulsion that he did not raise his gaze to the sky for three hundred years. Ibn ʿAbbās narrates that Ādam and Eve wept for two hundred years and neither ate nor drank for forty years after being snatched from Paradise and separated from Allah.

When Allah desired to show mercy to Ādam after this long period, He taught him certain words through which his repentance was accepted. These were the words: "There is no god but Allah, and Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allah" (⟪ar:'لا الہ الا اللہ محمد رسول اللہ'⟫).[^cite-7] These words Ādam saw inscribed upon the feet of the Divine Throne, and they became his means of remedy. Through Muḥammad Muṣṭafā, he sought intercession. Allah forgave them and revealed to them: "Circumambulate My Throne upon the earth and pray there as you would pray near My Throne. There I shall answer your supplication."

Ādam departed from India toward Mecca to visit the Ka'bah. Allah sent an angel to guide them. When they arrived at ʿArafāt, they encountered Eve, who had journeyed there from Jeddah in search of Ādam. They met in this valley on the day of ʿArafāh; hence the place is called ʿArafāt (⟪ar:'عرفات'⟫)—the place of mutual recognition. When Ādam returned to Minā, he said: "Express your desire!" Eve replied: "My desire is that You forgive me and praise me!" For this reason the place was named Minā, meaning "desire." Thus Allah answered their prayers and forgave their sins. Ādam then returned to India.

### Allah's Revelation to Ādam

Wahab ibn Umayyah narrates that after forgiving Ādam, Allah revealed to him: "O Ādam, I shall gather all your knowledge in four matters. One pertains to Me, one between you and Me, one for you alone, and one between you and My servants."

| Worship Me and associate no partner with Me. | Your task is to pray and accept My work. I shall repay you according to what you have done. | Love for them what you love for yourself. | *(content as given)* |

### The Meeting of Ādam and Iblīs

It is narrated that when Ādam met Iblīs in a forest, he rebuked him for bringing about his destruction and disgrace: "O cursed one, what have you done? You deceived me and drove me from Paradise, reducing me to a state of humiliation!" Iblīs wept and replied: "Yes, Ādam, I am guilty toward you, and I confess what I have done to you. But I ask you the same question: who cast me into this disgrace and destruction? And for what sin am I afflicted with this curse?"

### Iblīs's Pleas and Their Acceptance

It is narrated by Ḥammād al-Thābit (⟪ar:حماد ثابت⟫) and ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUbayd (⟪ar:حمید عبد اللہ ابن عبید⟫) that Iblīs said in the presence of Allah: "My Lord, You have expelled me from Paradise because of Ādam. Grant me dominion over him; if I can overcome him, so be it; otherwise, I shall not." Allah said: "Go, and it is decreed upon him." Iblīs perceived that he had received great dominion, far exceeding his request, and so he asked for more. Immediately he said: "O my Lord, grant me something more." Allah replied: "You shall have two children for every one child of Ādam, and your offspring shall be twice as many as his." He said: "O Lord, give me more." Allah said: "Listen then: in the breast of every child of Ādam shall be your dwelling place, and you shall run through their veins with every drop of blood." Iblīs said: "O Lord, grant me something else." Allah said: "You are permitted to attack the children of Ādam with your cavalry and infantry, to mix with their wealth and children, and to seduce them with false promises—for the promise of Satan is nothing but deception."

### Ādam's Requests to Allah and Their Acceptance

When Ādam saw that every request of Iblīs was being granted, such that he and his children would be crushed, he prayed to his Lord: "O Lord, You have imposed Iblīs upon us, but You know well that we cannot escape his snare without Your help. What, then, shall become of me and my children?" Allah consoled him, saying: "O Ādam! Every child of yours shall have a protector who shall guard him from this accursed one. Ask for more!" He said: "Listen to good tidings: whoever among your children performs a good deed, I shall give him tenfold reward. And for an evil deed, I shall punish him only with its equivalent, not more. After forgiveness, I shall not keep count against him; I shall destroy the reckoning." He said: "Go and tell your children: 'O sinful servants of Allah, do not despair of Allah's mercy.'" He replied: "Listen! Until the last breath of your children, I shall forgive those who repent. Never shall I close the door of repentance upon them. Tell me of more good tidings!" He said: "Very well. I shall forgive your children, and I shall never keep a record of My forgiveness." Ādam rejoiced and said: "This is enough, my Lord!"

### The Devil's Possessions

It is narrated that Iblīs said in the presence of Allah, the Lord of Might (⟪ar:رب العزت⟫): "O Lord, You have cursed me and expelled me from Paradise, and made me a shrine of degradation. You have made me ugly and humiliated me, and given me the title of Satan and Iblīs. I have heard and endured all this, but I have one request: grant me some powers." He said: "You shall have messengers and a scripture." He asked: "Who shall be my messengers?" And what shall be my scripture?"

The Almighty replied: "Your messengers shall be the greedy and avaricious. Your scripture shall be a collection of slander, backbiting, and calumny." He asked: "What shall be my *ḥadīth* (discourse)?" He said: "Your discourse shall be composed of falsehood." He asked: "What shall be my recitation?" He said: "Your Qur'ān shall be poetry and song." He asked: "Who shall be my *muezzin* (caller to prayer)?" He said: "Musical instruments and devices for playing songs." He asked: "My mosque?" He said: "The marketplace!" He asked: "My house?" He said: "The bathhouse, a place of impurity and filth." He asked: "What shall be my sustenance?" He said: "Anything upon which My name has not been invoked, the Bismillāh (⟪ar:حضرت محمد مصطفی (ﷺ)⟫)."[^cite-8] He asked: "And my drink?" He said: "Every intoxicating thing!" He asked: "Who shall be my prey?" He said: "Women shall be your victims!"

### The Last Testament of Ādam

Mujāhid narrates that ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās told him that Ādam, who dwelled in India throughout his life, performed the pilgrimage on foot forty times. The light of Muḥammad Muṣṭafā shone brilliantly upon Ādam's forehead. In other words, he called people to Allah throughout his life, fulfilling the right of the Prophet. He endured all manner of hardship and suffering from the people of his time; he laughed and bore it patiently, continuing in the work of inviting (daʿwa) to Allah (⟪ar:دعوت الی اللہ⟫) until the time of his death drew near.[^cite-9]

He fell ill for eleven days before his death. During this time, he wrote a will and entrusted it to his son Shīth (⟪ar:شِیث علیہ السلام⟫), instructing him to keep it hidden from the children of Qābīl. For when Ādam had married Qābīl's sister ʿAqlīmā to Ābīl, Qābīl slew Abel out of jealousy and envy. Ādam feared that if Qābīl learned that Ādam had given special knowledge to Shīth, he would not leave him alive. Thus Shīth and his descendants kept this particular knowledge hidden from Qābīl's offspring, and the latter remained deprived and ignorant because of the enmity toward Abel.

### The Death of the Prophet Ādam

According to the narrators, Ādam died on a Friday. The angels brought his shroud and the fragrant unguents from heaven and bathed Ādam. Ibrāhīm first washed him with water mixed with herbs, then with pure water, and covered him with a three-layered shroud. He was then brought to Mashāriq al-Firdaws, the first cemetery on earth. Ādam was buried by his son Shīth and his brothers. After Ādam's death, the sun and moon were eclipsed for six days.

After completing Ādam's burial, Shīth requested Gabriel to lead the funeral prayer. Gabriel said: "You go ahead and pray for your father." Thus Shīth led the funeral prayer over his father and recited thirty *takbīr* in it—five from the five daily prayers and twenty-five in honor of Ādam's virtue.

**Statement:** The Messenger of Allah recited seventy *takbīr* at the funeral of the Master of Martyrs, Ḥamza (⟪ar:سید الشھداء جناب حمزہ⟫).

### The Death of Eve

Eve lived one year after Ādam. She died in Jeddah, where a great grave stands upon the riverbank.

## Ādam Ṣafīy Allāh — God's Chosen One

### Ādam: The First Speaker

The designation *Safiullah* (ṣafīy Allāh—God's Chosen One) was conferred upon Ādam at the inception of the Seventy Period. His true name was ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm ibn Fūjān (⟪ar:'عبد اللہ ا بن تنجوم أبن فوجان'⟫), and he was the brother of that period's Imam, Ḥunīd (⟪ar:امام ھنید علیہ السلام⟫). He belonged to the *Zawhāqah* tribe (⟪ar:'زوحاقہ'⟫) and dwelled in the land called Lankā (⟪ar:'لنکا'⟫), a region ruled by a king named *Tanjūm* (⟪ar:تنجوم⟫).[^cite-10] The name Tanjūm suggests connection with astrology and expertise in the movements of the stars. The initiator of every new period is called the "initiator," and the one who concludes it is called the "eliminator" or *Qā'im* (⟪ar:'قائم'⟫). Since ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm (⟪ar:حضرت عبد اللہ بن تنجوم⟫) was to commence the Seventy Period, he was given the appellation "Ādam."

## The Garden of Ādam (the Janna in Which He Was Placed)

Great disagreement exists among scholars concerning whether the Paradise of Ādam and Eve was situated in the heavens or upon the earth. According to the People of Truth, the Garden (⟪ar:'حضرات امامی'⟫) was not in the heavens but upon the earth, bearing no relation to the Eternal Paradise (⟪ar:جنت خلد⟫), the reward of Allah. Had it been a paradise of reward, Iblīs would not have been expelled from it, for entry into the eternal reward is irrevocable. Nor would Satan have been able to enter later to deceive Ādam. The Qur'ān itself testifies to this distinction, recording Satan's words to Ādam:

> ⟪quran 20:120⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ إِلَيْهِ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ هَلْ أَدُلُّكَ عَلَىٰ شَجَرَةِ ٱلْخُلْدِ وَمُلْكٍۢ لَّا يَبْلَىٰ ‎⟫
> *Then Satan whispered to him: "O Ādam, shall I direct you to the Tree of Eternity and a kingdom that will not decay?"*

As Ādam listened and nodded, Iblīs continued:

> ⟪quran 7:20⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَلِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *Satan whispered to them both to reveal to them that which had been hidden from them of their nakedness, and said: "Your Lord forbade you this tree only lest you become angels or lest you become immortals."*

These verses indicate plainly that Ādam was created in a garden of this world where immortality was not guaranteed. One cannot depart from the abode of eternal reward once entered, as Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 15:48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَا يَمَسُّهُمْ فِيهَا نَصَبٌۭ وَمَا هُم مِّنْهَا بِمُخْرَجِينَ ‎⟫
> *"No fatigue shall touch them therein, nor shall they ever be expelled from it."*

Moreover, when Satan refused to prostrate before Ādam, he was cursed and separated from Allah's mercy. It would be contrary to divine wisdom to place anyone in the Eternal Paradise without prior action, or to admit one whom Allah has cursed. And it is not the way of Allah's wisdom to reward one who has not labored (*ʿāmilīn*). The greatest proof that Allah created Ādam upon the earth lies in the Qur'ān itself:

> ⟪quran 2:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when your Lord said to the angels: "Verily, I am going to place a vicegerent upon the earth." They said: "Will You place therein one who will spread corruption and shed blood, while we glorify You with praise and sanctify You?" He said: "I know that which you do not know."*

In this verse, Allah explicitly states: "I am placing My vicegerent upon the *earth* (⟪ar:'زمین'⟫), not in the heavens."

## The Test of the Angels (Q2:30–33)

The Qur'ān describes the creation of Ādam in these terms:

> ⟪quran 2:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when your Lord said to the angels: "Verily, I am going to place a vicegerent on the earth." They said: "Will You place therein those who will make mischief and shed blood, while we glorify You with praise and sanctify You?" He said: "I know that which you do not know."*

This verse alludes to the Imams (⟪ar:'ھنید علیہ السلام'⟫) of that age, who terminated the Period of Primordial Nature (*Fitra*) and commenced the Seventy Period. The text indicates that in this new era, the Imams would not themselves fulfill all the functions of spiritual guidance; rather, they would appoint successors (*khalīfa*) and deputies to spread the summons (*daʿwa*) on their behalf. Though many capable individuals—represented as "angels" in the symbolic account—were present, the Imam's gaze fell upon ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjūm, a mere youth still engaged in the study of fundamental sciences. The Imam intended to establish him as a prophet-like figure and undertake his "second creation"—the creation through knowledge (⟪ar:علم⟫), as Allah says:

> Then We created the sperm as a leech, and We created the leech as a chew, and We created the chewed form into a bone, and We clothed the bone with flesh, then We created it as another creation. So blessed be Allah, the best of creators.

When Imam Ḥunīd decided to appoint Ādam to the office of the Imamate, he selected twelve angels to serve as his helpers (*nuqabāʾ*—superintendents of the summons), so that they might learn from Ādam and preach the invitation on his behalf throughout the land of feasting. The names of these twelve limits were:

| 1 | Gabriel | 7 | Amhail |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2 | Michael | 8 | Tahtiel |
| 3 | Israfil | 9 | Hoyal |
| 4 | Azrael | 10 | Marhiel |
| 5 | Dardbil | 11 | Malik Yael |
| 6 | Sahail | 12 | Kyrgyz |

From among these twelve limits, Imam al-Ḥunaid declared: "I intend to establish my successor in the summons (*daʿwa*), who shall assume this rank." He continued: "I have chosen my nephew ʿAbdullāh for this task. When I have perfected him, all of you shall bow in obedience to him."

> ⟪quran 15:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا سَوَّيْتُهۥ وَنَفَخْتُ فِيهِ مِن رُّوحِى فَقَعُوا۟ لَهۥ سَجِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *"Then, when I have fashioned him in perfect form and breathed into him of My spirit, fall down before him in prostration."*

Concerning Ādam's training and preparation, Allah said: "I will create Ādam from *ṭīn* (clay)" (⟪ar:طین⟫):

> ⟪quran 38:71⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى خَلِقٌۢ بَشَرًۭا مِّن طِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *When your Lord said to the angels: "I am going to create a human from clay."*

In the inner science of the summons, *ṭīn* (clay) signifies a compound of soil and water: the soil represents the knowledge of the *Sharīʿah* (the outward law), and water represents the knowledge of *taʾwīl* (esoteric exegesis). The Imam thus declared: "I shall prepare Ādam with both kinds of knowledge." Since the angels were brought into being solely through subtle inner knowledge—which is spiritual and sublime—they understood that such subtle knowledge flows like lightning through support from above, whereas practical knowledge derives from gradual learning and action.

| Inner (Subtle) Knowledge | Outer (Practical) Knowledge |
| --- | --- |
| Manifestation | Education |
| Like lightning | Gradually |
| Belief and affirmation | Action |

The angels also knew that subtle spiritual knowledge bears no relation to action, nor to belief or affirmation in the ordinary sense, and certainly not to bodily exertion. Alluding to this distinction, the Commander of the Faithful explained the virtue of the Qur'ān, saying: "Its outward is action—obligatory; its inward is knowledge—hidden" (⟪ar-quote:ظاھر عمل موجب و باطن علم محجوب⟫). The angels therefore understood that when human actions—the obligatory duties—become binding upon Ādam, error and negligence will inevitably arise. They thus objected to the Imam: "If Your purpose is to obtain praise and sanctification through these inner sciences, we are superior in that regard. If You wish to appoint someone for this task, choose one of us, for we have studied the sciences of the summons extensively, completed the books of esoteric interpretation, and are immersed in the books of hidden realities. Would it not be more fitting to appoint one of us, since we can discharge the affairs of Your summons far better than Ādam, who is merely beginning to study the exoteric law?"

The angels thus said to the Imam: *(no content given)*

The Imam replied: "I know that which you do not know." This means: "I shall reward Ādam with My support. Should he commit an error, I shall compensate for it and preserve him from destruction." Among the angels was one called Azazel (⟪ar:'عزازیل'⟫), who believed himself superior to Ādam and considered himself worthy of the position of succession. He refused to obey Ādam and declined to participate in prostration before him. His name was Ḥārith ibn Murrah (⟪ar:'حارث بن مرۃ'⟫). What the Imam "knew and they did not know" concerned Ādam's capability and the unseen knowledge of these limits. Moreover, the Imam knew that when the limits were ordered to obey, Iblīs would refuse and display arrogance.

Allah created Ādam with both His hands, fashioning him from clay, as He says:

> ⟪quran 38:75⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓإِبْلِيسُ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَن تَسْجُدَ لِمَا خَلَقْتُ بِيَدَىَّ ۖ أَسْتَكْبَرْتَ أَمْ كُنتَ مِنَ ٱلْعَالِينَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Iblīs! What prevented you from prostrating before that which I created with both My hands? Were you too proud, or were you among the exalted?"*

The two hands represent the two modes of the Imam's summons (*daʿwa*)—the outward and the inward—which he employed in the education and formation of Ādam. Creating him from clay signifies that the Imam began his instruction with the outward sciences, teaching him the fundamentals, gradually perfecting his education over many years. The extent of his progress in the esoteric sciences during this period remains known only to Allah.

A ḥadīth conveys this thus: When Allah Almighty fashioned Ādam's form from clay, His body (the statue) was left lying for a long period before the soul—that is, before receiving support from the inner spiritual knowledge—could animate it. In other words, before attaining the secrets of inner knowledge, Ādam devoted himself to studying the outward sciences for years. Satan would approach the dried, hollow body of clay and mock it. Since the body was empty within, it would make a sound when struck, and Satan would cry out in triumph: "Empty! Hollow! It is hollow!" In other words, he would come to Ādam during his studies and pose questions about the higher realities. When Ādam could not answer from the perspective of inner knowledge—speaking only from the standpoint of exoteric learning—Satan would rejoice, thinking: "Ādam cannot access inner knowledge; the world is empty and void of inward reality."

In this manner, Ādam's education and training continued for many years until he achieved perfection and became worthy of the Caliphate.

> ⟪quran 2:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَعَلَّمَ آدَمَ ٱلْأَسْمَآءَ كُلَّهَا ثُمَّ عَرَضَهُمْ عَلَى ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ فَقَالَ أَنۢبِـُٔونِى بِأَسْمَآءِ هَٓؤُلَآءِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *And Allah taught Ādam all the names; then He presented them to the angels, saying: "Tell Me the names of these, if you are truthful."*

Then Allah taught Ādam all the names (⟪ar:'أسمآء'⟫) directly—the divine attributes and their manifestations—whereas the angels learned through intermediaries. By "names" is meant *maʿrifah* (direct knowledge, gnosis), for the name identifies its object. The Imam Ḥunaid thus introduced Ādam to the exalted angels (⟪ar:ملائکہ علیین⟫) who stood in ranks above the twelve limits appointed under Ādam's authority. Ādam alone was granted knowledge of these supreme names because he was destined for the station of prophethood and prophecy. When the Imam posed the question about these names, the angels responded:

> ⟪quran 2:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالُوا۟ سُبْحَنَكَ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَآ إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *They said: "Glory be to You! We have no knowledge save what You have taught us. Surely, You are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise."*

Since these limits had not attained the rank of prophethood, the knowledge of these great names remained concealed from them. It is only through these supreme names that their own ranks could be elevated. All the limits confessed their ignorance and acknowledged the knowledge and wisdom of Imam al-Ḥunaid.

> ⟪quran 2:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Ādam, inform them of their names." And when Ādam had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the unseen of the heavens and the earth, and know what you reveal and what you hide?"*

The Imam al-Ḥunaid then commanded Ādam to impart these names to the angels. When Ādam made all these sublime realities known to these exalted beings, he became their teacher and benefactor (⟪ar:معلم و مفید⟫), for there is always precedence of the knower over the ignorant. The intellectual superiority and eminence of Ḥazrat ʿAbdullāh over all of them was thereby revealed. Imam al-Ḥunaid declared: "Did I not tell you that I know the unseen of the heavens and the earth?" This signifies: "I know all the capacities of angels and humans alike; I am aware of their outer intentions and inner actions both. You thought Ādam incapable of grasping the sciences of prophecy and wisdom, yet behold—he proved superior in understanding to you all!"

## Exegesis of "I Know What You Reveal and What You Conceal" (Q2:33)

The Quranic verse proclaims:

> ⟪quran 2:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Adam! Inform them of their names," and when he had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the Ghaib (unseen) in the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you have been concealing?"*

Regarding the interpretation of the phrase ⟪ar-quote:وأعلم ما تبدون و ماکنتم تکتمون⟫ — "I know what you reveal and I know what you conceal" — Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (a.s.) transmits from his father, Mawlānā Muḥammad al-Bāqir (a.s.), an illuminating understanding. What the angels revealed openly was their statement concerning their own sufficiency for divine praise, uttered without deep reflection. What they concealed in their hearts, however, was a darker thought: how could God create a being superior to themselves and command them to bow before it?[^cite-4]

In another exegetical tradition, the narrative unfolds differently. When the Lord fashioned Ādam's form from clay and positioned it in the passage through which the angels traveled, they beheld a figure of surpassing beauty—such loveliness as they had never witnessed before. Yet when the accursed one (Iblīs, the Denier) passed by, he pondered the statue's purpose with malice. He played upon it with his hand, entering through its mouth and departing likewise, declaring to his companions: "This is a strange vessel, hollow within and incapable of strength." He then posed a test to them: "Suppose this form were granted superiority over you—what would you do?" All save one proclaimed, "We shall obey its Lord." But the Devil harbored a secret oath: "Should this clay be elevated above me, I shall disobey it; should I excel over it, I shall withhold mercy."[^cite-5] Thus ⟪ar-quote:وأعلم ما تبدون و ماکنتم تکتمون⟫ signifies that God knew both the disobedience latent among the angels and the intention of rebellion that Iblīs concealed within his breast.

## The Angels' Seeking of Forgiveness

It is narrated from Mawlānā Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (a.s.) that he once performed pilgrimage with his father, Mawlānā Muḥammad al-Bāqir (a.s.). One night, as they circumambulated the House of God, kissed the Black Stone, and offered the circumambulation prayer, he grew weary and sat to rest whilst his father continued in supplication. Suddenly a man appeared and stood beside them—a figure of strong frame with reddish hair, clad in two thick white robes. Sensing the newcomer's presence, his father shortened his prayer, returned the greeting, and inquired, "Do you seek something of me?" The stranger replied, "I have come to ask you of the origin of this House." His father then recounted the entire history of Ādam's creation and what followed.

He then explained: "When the angels confessed their transgression, the Lord said to them:

> ⟪quran 2:33⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ يَٓـَٔادَمُ أَنۢبِئْهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ ۖ فَلَمَّآ أَنۢبَأَهُم بِأَسْمَآئِهِمْ قَالَ أَلَمْ أَقُل لَّكُمْ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَعْلَمُ مَا تُبْدُونَ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْتُمُونَ ‎⟫
> *He said: "O Adam! Inform them of their names," and when he had informed them of their names, He said: "Did I not tell you that I know the Ghaib (unseen) in the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you have been concealing?"*

That is, 'I know not only what you declare aloud—that you are sufficient for My praise—but I know also what you conceal in darkness: your unspoken doubt that a creature superior to yourselves could receive your submission.' When the Lord unveiled to the angels the secrets of their own hearts, they repented with sincere contrition. Gathering before the Throne, they circumambulated it seven times in supplication. Pleased with their remorse and their deeds, the Lord commanded them: 'Go down to the earth and build a House wherein the sinful children of Ādam shall gather and circumambulate it, that I may forgive them and show them mercy as I am pleased with you.'[^cite-6] Thus it was that the angels constructed the Kaʿba. In the time of Noah's flood, the House was raised up and placed in the fourth heaven, where the angels continue to circle it until the Day of Judgment. Then Ibrāhīm (Abraham) rebuilt it upon its ancient foundation.

## How Can Error Be Possible from the Angels?

When it is said that angels are sinless, a question arises: how then did they commit a transgression in the narrative of Ādam? The answer lies in understanding the hierarchical structure of Ādam's own station. Just as Ādam possessed both an inner essence and an outward veil (ḥijāb), so too were the angels divided into those of the inner circles (muḥtajib) and those of the outer circles. The inner ranks, by their proximity to divine reality, held stations of greater nobility—just as the lowest in one circle surpasses the highest of the circle below it—and they were not subject to the law of Ādam. Those angels whom God commanded to prostrate before Ādam were inferior in rank to him, and therefore possessed the capacity for error.

| Inner Gentlemen | Outer Veils |
| --- | --- |
| The Essence of Ādam | Ādam's Boundary |
| Hidden Truth | Protecting Veil |

The limits of the inner circles, standing at greater heights of station, could not err. But the outer veils, made subordinate to Ādam's law, carried within them the possibility of mistake. When some among these outer ranks transgressed, they received correction for their fault. The inner essence of Ādam, the *Muḥtajib*, remained flawless; only his outer veils, working among the externals, were subject to trial. Thus error became possible not for the angels in their highest stations, but for those stationed beneath the authority (wilāya) of the Prophet.

## Iblīs's Arrogance and Refusal

At that moment, the Lord commanded the angels:

> ⟪quran 2:34⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قُلْنَا لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ ٱسْجُدُوا۟ لِآدَمَ فَسَجَدُوٓا۟ إِلَّآ إِبْلِيسَ أَبَىٰ وَٱسْتَكْبَرَ وَكَانَ مِنَ ٱلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when We said to the angels: "Prostrate yourselves before Adam." And they prostrated except Iblîs (Satan), he refused and was proud and was one of the disbelievers.*

The Divine Command was absolute: "Prostrate before Ādam, all of you, and obey him. Establish yourselves within his boundary, take his covenant, and learn from his wisdom." When one enters prostration—placing the head, the highest point of the body, upon the ground where the feet tread—one confesses: "My finest qualities are but servants to my most lowly station." In prostration, seven parts of the body touch the earth in surrender, signifying the giving of one's entire existence to the authority of the intellect (*al-ʿaql*).

All the angels immediately obeyed and fell into prostration—that is, they pledged obedience to the Imām (a.s.) and committed themselves to Ādam's covenant—save for one of the limits called ⟪ar:'ابلیس'⟫ (Iblīs), whom some name ⟪ar:حارث بن مرۃ⟫ (Ḥārith ibn Murra).

> ⟪quran 7:12⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَلَّا تَسْجُدَ إِذْ أَمَرْتُكَ ۖ قَالَ أَنَا۠ خَيْرٌۭ مِّنْهُ خَلَقْتَنِى مِن نَّارٍۢ وَخَلَقْتَهۥ مِن طِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "What prevented you (O Iblîs) that you did not prostrate, when I commanded you?" Iblîs said: "I am better than him (Adam), You created me from fire, and him You created from clay."*

The Imām questioned him: "Why did you refuse to prostrate before Ādam?" And Iblīs answered with arrogance: "I am superior to him. You fashioned me from fire—luminous, sharp, and purified of physical corruption—and You created him from clay, dense and earthly. I am the teacher of the angels (*muʿallim al-malakūt*) in the knowledge of the celestial realm; how can you desire that I bow before this raw creature?" Iblīs refused and became one of the disbelievers (*kāfirūn*, those who conceal).[^cite-7] The term *kāfir* signifies one who covers or conceals. Though Iblīs knew full well that Ādam possessed greater capacity than himself—indeed, God had demonstrated this to all the angels—he deliberately obscured Ādam's station, becoming thereby one of those who deny the truth.

> ⟪quran 7:13⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ فَٱهْبِطْ مِنْهَا فَمَا يَكُونُ لَكَ أَن تَتَكَبَّرَ فِيهَا فَٱخْرُجْ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلصَّغِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "(O Iblîs) get down from this (Paradise), it is not for you to be arrogant here. Get out, for you are of those humiliated and disgraced."*

The Lord expelled him from Paradise—from His presence and His invitation—because of his arrogance. For arrogance in the presence of the Exalted is itself the very essence of hell. Greatness and majesty belong to God alone.

The name Iblīs derives from the Arabic ⟪ar:'أبلس'⟫, meaning "to despair." Ḥārith ibn Murra's original aspiration had been to attain prophethood. Deprived of his station and despairing of ever becoming a prophet in any age to come, he earned the name Iblīs. It also carries another meaning: one incapable of reason, for he was confounded by the subtlety of Ādam's inner knowledge. Yet another meaning speaks to his separation from mercy (*raḥma*), for Imām al-Hunaid expelled him from his court. The Arabic word ⟪ar:'لعن'⟫ (*laʿn*, curse) signifies removal and banishment.

## The Creation of Ḥawwāʾ (Eve) and the First Marriage

Just as Ādam's birth signifies his intellectual genesis in the realm of prophethood and imamate, so does his wife represent the spiritual birth of his successor. The Ḥawwāʾ mentioned in the Qur'ān relates not to a physical woman born of parents, but to the religious and hierarchical station of Ādam's appointed heir. Both Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ, when understood esoterically (*taʾwīl*), are human beings of the same earth, yet their names speak of deeper mysteries.

The name ⟪ar:'حوا'⟫ derives from ⟪ar:'حی'⟫, meaning "alive" or "living." It also denotes one who encompasses or dominates a thing. The Qur'ān relates that when Ādam assumed the position of prophethood and imamate, fear and constraint arose. The Lord then caused a certain sleep to overcome him and, from his right side, brought forth a rib, creating from it his wife, Ḥawwāʾ. This verse points to a spiritual reality: when Imām al-Hunaid appointed Ādam to the position of prophethood, He ordered him to prepare his successor (*khalīfa*) and to establish his limits (*ḥudūd*).

Ādam possessed twelve arguments or niqābas (barriers, veils of initiated rank), dwelling in the palace of his twelve ribs. These were called ribs because, just as a man's bodily frame depends upon his ribs for stability, so too did the stability of Ādam's *daʿwa* (summons to faith) depend upon his boundaries and limits. The twelve ribs on the right represent the knowledge of the inner dimension (*al-bāṭin*), while the twelve on the left exemplify the knowledge of the outer dimension (*al-ẓāhir*). Ādam possessed twelve initiates for the inward mysteries and twelve for the outward call. When Imām al-Hunaid appointed one of Ādam's *ḥujjāt* (proofs, representatives)—his son ⟪ar:'ھابیل'⟫ (Hābīl/Abel)—as his successor in the office, this union was termed a marriage (*nikāḥ*), meaning a joining and connection of two in complementary function.

Though Hābīl was Ādam's son in the outward sense, he surpassed him in knowledge of the inner sciences. For this reason, they called him ⟪ar:'حوا'⟫, as he was the ⟪ar:'حاوی'⟫ (one who encompasses and contains) the inner knowledge. Imām al-Hunaid joined Hābīl to Ādam so that whenever Ādam faced difficulty in acquiring understanding, he might seek aid from Hābīl and thereby avoid hardship. Thus Ādam received this "wife" (Hābīl) as a substitute for Iblīs. For previously, Iblīs had been destined to be one of Ādam's ⟪ar:اضلاع (حدود)⟫ limits or summoners (*dāʿī*). But Ādam's invitation lost one of its twelve when Iblīs refused; the count was completed by the inclusion of Hābīl. In other words, God granted Ādam a replacement for the one He had rejected. Then the Lord declared:

> ⟪quran 2:35⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقُلْنَا يَٓـَٔادَمُ ٱسْكُنْ أَنتَ وَزَوْجُكَ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَكُلَا مِنْهَا رَغَدًا حَيْثُ شِئْتُمَا وَلَا تَقْرَبَا هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةَ فَتَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *And We said: "O Adam! Dwell you and your wife in the Paradise and eat both of you freely with pleasure and delight of things therein as wherever you will, but come not near this tree or you both will be of the Zalimun (wrong-doers)."*

Imām al-Hunaid placed Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ (his successor) in Paradise—that is, in the station of prophethood, which is connected to revelation and stands pure, subtle, and real, beyond all material veils. This blessing surpasses all other blessings, and its pleasure exceeds all pleasures. One who becomes wholly absorbed in it finds the world and all its treasures devoid of worth. The greatest riches and most precious commodities of this world hold no station in his eyes. He recognizes that this knowledge alone is the true blessing of God, a joy that never fades, a wealth that never diminishes, an honor that never humiliates.[^cite-8] The various stations of prayer are themselves also ⟪ar:'جنت'⟫ called gardens.

Thus Imām al-Hunaid appointed Ādam as prophet and Hābīl as his successor. He granted them permission to read, learn, and benefit from all sciences within the bounds of their station, and they would not be questioned. They were authorized to establish the rulings of lawful and unlawful, to ordain the regulations of worship, and to conduct all matters of prophethood. Yet:

| And do not approach this tree, for you will be one of the wrongdoers |
| --- |

"Do not approach this tree, lest you become of the wrongdoers." Though I have permitted all things, do not transgress your limits. This ⟪ar:'شجرہ ممنوعۃ'⟫ (forbidden tree) was the boundary beyond which Ādam and Hābīl were forbidden to step. That is, "Do not touch the work of ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫ your Lord; do not approach it. Your authority is limited to the works of prophecy. Knowledge of the unveiling and expression of innermost secrets belongs to the Qaʾim (a.s.) alone. Those mysteries must not be revealed in the generations before his appearance. Only the ⟪ar:قائم القیامۃ⟫ Qaʾim possesses the right to lift the veils, to accept or to reject. He is the ⟪ar:مالک یوم الدین⟫ Master of the Day of Judgment. Faith entered after his manifestation will not profit anyone. In his hands shall the hidden knowledge be revealed. Whosoever reveals before the appointed time becomes a wrongdoer (⟪ar:فَتَکُونَا مِنَ الظَّالِمِینَ⟫). For this reason, Imām al-Hunaid forbade them both at the beginning of the seventy generations, so that the custom (*sunna*) and nature of God would remain unchanged.

## Iblīs's Challenge to God

The accursed one sought respite, requesting a postponement of his punishment until the appointed time. He petitioned:

> ⟪quran 7:14-15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ أَنظِرْنِىٓ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ ‎ ‏قَالَ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنظَرِينَ ‎⟫
> *(Iblîs) said: "Allow me respite till the Day they are raised up (i.e. the Day of Resurrection)." (Allah) said: "You are of those allowed respite."*

Here ⟪ar:'یوم یبعثون'⟫ refers to ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫ the day of the rising of the Qaʾim (a.s.). Then Iblīs made his vow of vengeance:

> ⟪quran 7:16-17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ فَبِمَآ أَغْوَيْتَنِى لَأَقْعُدَنَّ لَهُمْ صِرَطَكَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ ‎ ‏ثُمَّ لَآتِيَنَّهُم مِّنۢ بَيْنِ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ وَعَنْ أَيْمَنِهِمْ وَعَن شَمَآئِلِهِمْ ۖ وَلَا تَجِدُ أَكْثَرَهُمْ شَكِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *(Iblîs) said: "Because You have sent me astray, surely I will sit in wait against them (human beings) on Your Straight Path. Then I will come to them from before them and behind them, from their right and from their left, and You will not find most of them as thankful ones."*

He declared to Imām al-Hunaid (a.s.): "Since You have led me astray—prevented me from advancing in ⟪ar:صراط مستقیم⟫ the straight path of prophethood—so shall I destroy the descendants of Ādam born until the reappearance of the Qaʾim (a.s.), all but a few. I shall assail them from every quarter with cavalry and infantry, lead them into error, and you shall witness most of them fall into my grasp."[^cite-9]

> ⟪quran 17:62⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ أَرَءَيْتَكَ هَذَا ٱلَّذِى كَرَّمْتَ عَلَىَّ لَىِٕنْ أَخَّرْتَنِ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْقِيَمَةِ لَأَحْتَنِكَنَّ ذُرِّيَّتَهُۥٓ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *[Iblîs (Satan)] said: "See? This one whom You have honoured above me, if You give me respite (keep me alive) to the Day of Resurrection, I will surely seize and mislead his offspring (by sending them astray) all but a few!"*

Iblīs challenged the Imām (a.s.), vowing that since he had fallen from his rank on account of Ādam, he would prevent any of Ādam's children from attaining his station. He continued with darker resolve:

> ⟪quran 4:119-120⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَأُضِلَّنَّهُمْ وَلَأُمَنِّيَنَّهُمْ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُبَتِّكُنَّ آذَانَ ٱلْأَنْعَمِ وَلَآمُرَنَّهُمْ فَلَيُغَيِّرُنَّ خَلْقَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَمَن يَتَّخِذِ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ وَلِيًّۭا مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ فَقَدْ خَسِرَ خُسْرَانًۭا مُّبِينًۭا ‎ ‏يَعِدُهُمْ وَيُمَنِّيهِمْ ۖ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎⟫
> *Verily, I will mislead them, and surely, I will arouse in them false desires; and certainly, I will order them to slit the ears of cattle, and indeed I will order them to change the nature created by Allah. And whoever takes Shaitan (Satan) as a Walî (protector or helper) instead of Allah, has surely suffered a manifest loss. He [Shaitan (Satan)] makes promises to them, and arouses in them false desires; and Shaitan's (Satan) promises are nothing but deceptions.*

He proclaimed: "I shall establish my kingdom against Your kingdom until the Qaʾim appears. I shall set my summons (*daʿwa*) against Your summons. I shall deceive Your servants in manifold ways—at times filling them with false hopes, at times stirring them to hatred and murder of Your saints. You shall see that most of Your servants will join me." The Imām (a.s.) accepted Iblīs's challenge and replied:

> ⟪quran 7:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱخْرُجْ مِنْهَا مَذْءُومًۭا مَّدْحُورًۭا ۖ لَّمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ لَأَمْلَأَنَّ جَهَنَّمَ مِنكُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said (to Iblîs) "Get out from this (Paradise) disgraced and expelled. Whoever of them (mankind) will follow you, then surely I will fill Hell with you all."*

> ⟪quran 17:63-65⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱذْهَبْ فَمَن تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ فَإِنَّ جَهَنَّمَ جَزَآؤُكُمْ جَزَآءًۭا مَّوْفُورًۭا ‎ ‏وَٱسْتَفْزِزْ مَنِ ٱسْتَطَعْتَ مِنْهُم بِصَوْتِكَ وَأَجْلِبْ عَلَيْهِم بِخَيْلِكَ وَرَجْلِكَ وَشَارِكْهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَمْوَلِ وَٱلْأَوْلَدِ وَعِدْهُمْ ۚ وَمَا يَعِدُهُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ إِلَّا غُرُورًا ‎ ‏إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَنٌۭ ۚ وَكَفَىٰ بِرَبِّكَ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "Go, and whosoever of them follows you, surely! Hell will be the recompense of you (all) an ample recompense... Verily! My slaves (i.e the true believers of Islamic Monotheism), you have no authority over them. And All-Sufficient is your Lord as a Guardian."*

He declared: "Go, and do what you must. Yet hear also: whoever follows you shall be your companion in the recompense of Hell, receiving the fullest punishment. Stir into fear whomsoever among them you can with your voice—your speeches, your rhetoric, your persuasion. Attack them with your cavalry and infantry, rallying the forces of your followers to penetrate their wealth and children. By 'wealth' understand knowledge itself; through eloquent words, sow doubt in their hearts regarding their faith. By 'children' understand the believers—turn them away from love of the true Imāms. Those whom you overcome shall be yours, removed from the circle of faith, no longer believers. And deceive them with your false promises. But remember well: you shall never gain mastery over My sincere servants."[^cite-10]

In another place, the Lord refers to this covenant:

> ⟪quran 15:36-42⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ رَبِّ فَأَنظِرْنِىٓ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ ‎ ‏قَالَ فَإِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنظَرِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ ٱلْوَقْتِ ٱلْمَعْلُومِ ‎ ‏قَالَ رَبِّ بِمَآ أَغْوَيْتَنِى لَأُزَيِّنَنَّ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَأُغْوِيَنَّهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎ ‏إِلَّا عِبَادَكَ مِنْهُمُ ٱلْمُخْلَصِينَ ‎ ‏قَالَ هَذَا صِرَطٌ عَلَىَّ مُسْتَقِيمٌ ‎ ‏إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَنٌ إِلَّا مَنِ ٱتَّبَعَكَ مِنَ ٱلْغَاوِينَ ‎⟫
> *[Iblîs (Satan)] said: "O my Lord! Give me then respite till the Day they (the dead) will be resurrected." Allah said: "Then, verily, you are of those reprieved... [Iblîs (Satan)] said: "O my Lord! Because you misled me, I shall indeed adorn the path of error for them (mankind) on the earth, and I shall mislead them all. "Except Your chosen, (guided) slaves among them." (Allah) said: "This is the Way which will lead straight to Me." "Certainly, you shall have no authority over My slaves, except those who follow you of the Ghawîn..."*

Thus God granted Iblīs respite until the Day of Resurrection, placing the true believers to the test. Whoever falls into his snare is not of the children of Ādam in the truest sense, nor a believer. Yet he also declared: should one who was seduced and slipped into Satan's grasp understand the whispers of the enemy, repent sincerely, and ask forgiveness with genuine contrition, that one would not belong to Satan's faction but would join the servants of God. Thus two callings began to circulate among Ādam's descendants—the merciful summons and the satanic summons—dividing them into two communities: the believers and the hypocrites who followed the devils. The followers of Ādam were called the servants of God, over whom Satan could not prevail despite all his stratagems. As the Qur'ān declares:

> ⟪quran 58:19⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱسْتَحْوَذَ عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَأَنسَىٰهُمْ ذِكْرَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ هُمُ ٱلْخَسِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Shaitan (Satan) has overtaken them. So he has made them forget the remembrance of Allah. They are the party of Shaitan (Satan). Verily, it is the party of Shaitan (Satan) that will be the losers!*

On the Day of Resurrection, Iblīs himself shall confess to his followers:

> ⟪quran 14:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لَمَّا قُضِىَ ٱلْأَمْرُ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ وَعَدَكُمْ وَعْدَ ٱلْحَقِّ وَوَعَدتُّكُمْ فَأَخْلَفْتُكُمْ ۖ وَمَا كَانَ لِىَ عَلَيْكُم مِّن سُلْطَنٍ إِلَّآ أَن دَعَوْتُكُمْ فَٱسْتَجَبْتُمْ لِى ۖ فَلَا تَلُومُونِى وَلُومُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَكُم ۖ مَّآ أَنَا۠ بِمُصْرِخِكُمْ وَمَآ أَنتُم بِمُصْرِخِىَّ ۖ إِنِّى كَفَرْتُ بِمَآ أَشْرَكْتُمُونِ مِن قَبْلُ ۗ إِنَّ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And Shaitan (Satan) will say when the matter has been decided: "Verily, Allah promised you a promise of truth. And I too promised you, but I betrayed you. I had no authority over you except that I called you, so you responded to me. So blame me not, but blame yourselves. I cannot help you, nor can you help me. I deny your former act in associating me (Satan) as a partner with Allah (by obeying me in the life of the world). Verily, there is a painful torment for the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers, etc.)."*

In contrast, the assembly of true believers stands firm:

> ⟪quran 58:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَّا تَجِدُ قَوْمًۭا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ يُوَآدُّونَ مَنْ حَآدَّ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ وَلَوْ كَانُوٓا۟ آبَآءَهُمْ أَوْ أَبْنَآءَهُمْ أَوْ إِخْوَنَهُمْ أَوْ عَشِيرَتَهُمْ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَتَبَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمُ ٱلْإِيمَنَ وَأَيَّدَهُم بِرُوحٍۢ مِّنْهُ ۖ وَيُدْخِلُهُمْ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَرُ خَلِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ رَضِىَ ٱللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا۟ عَنْهُ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱللَّهِ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *You will not find any people who believe in Allah and the Last Day, making friendship with those who oppose Allah and His Messenger, even though they were their fathers, or their sons, or their brothers, or their kindred. For such He has written Faith in their hearts, and strengthened them with Ruh (proofs, light and true guidance) from Himself. And We will admit them to Gardens (Paradise) under which rivers flow, to dwell therein (forever). Allah is pleased with them, and they with Him. They are the Party of Allah. Verily, it is the Party of Allah that will be the successful.*

Iblīs's enmity toward Ādam became eternal, passed down through generations. Ādam was pardoned through his repentance; Iblīs was cursed and banished from Paradise, and in his heart burned the spirit of vengeance. He has persecuted openly every Prophet, every Messenger, every Trustee (*Wāsī*), and every Imām, employing every stratagem to weaken them. The Lord declares:

> ⟪quran 6:112⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And so We have appointed for every Prophet enemies - Shayatin (devils) among mankind and jinns, inspiring one another with adorned speech as a delusion. If your Lord had so willed, they would not have done it, so leave them alone with their fabrications.*

Whosoever follows the devil bears the burden of sin. Yet he who, after being seduced and having slipped, comes to understand the whispers of Satan, repents genuinely, and seeks God's pardon becomes neither Satan's companion nor a soldier in his army. But he who deliberately denies the truth, who harbors arrogance and obstinacy—the very marks of Satan—such a one is himself a supreme Iblīs, guilty of enmity to the Prophets. The arrogant who refuse guidance and persist in rejection become the followers of Satan's faction, while those who surrender and repent join the ranks of God's sincere servants. The adversaries of the Imāms, consumed by denial and stubbornness, suffer the consequences of their choice; the trials came to the *Muḥtajib* first, then to the veils (*ḥijāb*) below. The inquisition pressed upon them in full force, and the world received no news of them or their ancestors, yet the situation of the outer veils differed: sometimes they appeared openly, sometimes they were forced into concealment.

## Ādam's Being Deceived (Eating from the Forbidden Tree)

The Lord says:

> ⟪quran 7:20⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَلِدِينَ ‎⟫
> *Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered suggestions to them both in order to uncover that which was hidden from them of their private parts; he said: "Your Lord did not forbid you this tree save you should become angels or become of the immortals."*

It is narrated in tradition that Ḥārith ibn Murra entered Paradise in the form of a serpent. A serpent appears gentle and docile to the eye, yet within it harbors deadly venom. Thus did Ḥārith ibn Murra approach Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ with soft words, befriending them whilst concealing his enmity. Knowing that Ādam would not be easily swayed, he turned his seduction upon the woman (Ḥawwāʾ), saying: "The Imām has forbidden you access to the highest station of knowledge so that you may not rival the other limits and rise to a rank worthy of standing after the completion of your learning. Were you to acquire all these sciences, you would deserve eternity—your rightful due. Yet the Imām withholds this eternity from you. Do you not see that bodily works and practices are for the inferior stations? If you shed them, you shall ascend to the level of the ⟪ar:'ملائکہ علیین'⟫ elevated angels and gain the ⟪ar:علم روحانی لطیف⟫ subtle spiritual knowledge. You will liberate yourselves from the heavy burden of bodily worship forever and become eternally dwelling in Paradise, capable of fulfilling your every desire and wish." Then Iblīs swore a false oath before them both, vowing:

> ⟪quran 7:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَاسَمَهُمَآ إِنِّى لَكُمَا لَمِنَ ٱلنَّصِحِينَ ‎⟫
> *And he [Shaitan (Satan)] swore by Allah to them both (saying): "Verily, I am one of the sincere well-wishers for you both."*

"Truly, I am a sincere adviser to you both."[^cite-11]

Mawlānā ʿAlī relates that Ādam was deceived precisely because Satan had sworn an oath. Falsehood had no existence in the intellectual realm of Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ; they were aware only of truth, and no trace of deception inhabited their imagination. When Satan swore to them, they both believed him. To utter a false oath would have been among the impossibilities in their understanding.

> ⟪quran 7:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَدَلَّىٰهُمَا بِغُرُورٍۢ ۚ فَلَمَّا ذَاقَا ٱلشَّجَرَةَ بَدَتْ لَهُمَا سَوْءَتُهُمَا وَطَفِقَا يَخْصِفَانِ عَلَيْهِمَا مِن وَرَقِ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ وَنَادَىٰهُمَا رَبُّهُمَآ أَلَمْ أَنْهَكُمَا عَن تِلْكُمَا ٱلشَّجَرَةِ وَأَقُل لَّكُمَآ إِنَّ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ لَكُمَا عَدُوٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *So he misled them with deception. Then when they tasted of the tree, that which was hidden from them of their shame (private parts) became manifest to them and they began to stick together the leaves of Paradise over themselves. And their Lord called out to them: "Did I not forbid you that tree and tell you: Verily, Shaitan (Satan) is an open enemy unto you?"*

At the urging of Ḥawwāʾ, Ādam tasted the fruit of the forbidden tree. That is, he overstepped the appointed boundaries of prophetic work, transgressing into the domain that was not his to enter. He reached toward knowledge ⟪ar:مولانا قائم القیامۃ⟫ reserved for the Qaʾim (a.s.) alone, the knowledge of unveiling and inner transformation. As soon as he opened that sealed book and began to read it, their condition became manifest—their spiritual nakedness revealed. Since these sciences towered far above their capacity, they could not grasp them; both immediately recognized their own weakness and lack of intellectual sufficiency. "We deemed ourselves so wise," they realized, "yet we find ourselves utterly deficient in these mysteries." The wrath and displeasure of Imām al-Hunaid (a.s.) descended upon them. The Imām withdrew His support and revelation, exposing their imperfection and stripping from them the garment of sanctity.

The luminous robe of revelation had concealed their physical imperfections and material density. Illuminated by divine support, even an ordinary stone shines like the sun, and all knowledge becomes accessible and clear. They were able to expound wisdom to others. Yet the moment these supports were severed, they could not even comprehend the smallest sciences themselves, much less explain them to others. Then every tongue opened to criticize them. We observe in this world: when a person is elevated to high rank, he is honored; but when removed from that rank, the people's tongues immediately wag, and he becomes an object of humiliation. They attempted to cover their nakedness with the leaves of Paradise—that is, they sought to learn from those of inferior rank—but none would teach them. Only the fig tree, moved by compassion, offered its leaves to cover them, whereupon God rejoiced and united the outward and inward of the fig tree in blessing.

It is related in the Torah that when Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ found themselves naked in Paradise, walking about in panic, they heard the footsteps of God in the cool garden, and they hid themselves in shame. A voice called out: "Where are you?" They replied: "We are naked; when we heard Your footsteps, we hid in shame." The Lord asked, "Did you eat from the tree?" Ādam said, "The woman gave it to me." The woman said, "The serpent deceived me." The serpent said, "Iblīs entered into me."

The Imām said to them: "This is precisely why I forbade you from acquiring these sciences—because you were not yet capable of understanding them. It was not that I desired to deprive you; rather, I withheld them for your own benefit. I told you that Satan was your evident enemy, yet you obeyed him and disobeyed Me."

Every parent desires the finest nourishment for their children, yet they do not feed a newborn anything but milk, knowing the infant's digestive capacity cannot yet process solid food. As soon as the child is able, they immediately offer the richest sustenance. The withholding and the granting are both for the child's welfare. In the same way, Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (a.s.) spoke of a believer's steadfastness, saying: "None of our saints walks upon both feet equally firm." If one foot slips, it stands firm upon the other. A believer may stumble into error, yet faith remains unshaken. The moment he recognizes his sin, he turns with remorse and sincere repentance. On this principle, Satan seduced Ādam, *Ṣafī Allāh* (God's Chosen One), yet could not make him his own. Ādam fell into Satan's snare through flattery, but immediately upon realizing the deception, he turned in regret and sought forgiveness with a contrite heart.

## Ādam's Expulsion from the Garden

When one examines the narrative of Ādam's expulsion from Paradise with close attention to its apparent details, numerous questions and apparent contradictions emerge. The first concerns the nature of the forbidden tree itself: if the tree and its fruit were inherently wholesome and lawful, why did God forbid Ādam to consume them? Conversely, if the tree was truly forbidden, how could it exist within Paradise at all? Having made all things of Paradise lawful to Ādam, why would the Divine decree single out this single tree and its fruit alone for prohibition? Would it not have been simpler for God—who possessed perfect knowledge of unseen things—to refrain from cultivating this tree in Paradise altogether, knowing in advance that it would become the instrument of Ādam's trial and affliction?[^cite-12]

Some exegetes among the *Ahl al-Ẓāhir* (those who adhere to the literal sense) have identified the tree as wheat (*qamḥ*). Yet this identification compounds the difficulty: if wheat were truly forbidden to Ādam, why is it lawful for his descendants, both believers and unbelievers alike? When certain scholars respond by saying that Ādam lacked a digestive faculty (*quwwa hāḍima*) and that the fruit would therefore rot within him and cause suffering, this reasoning—however ingenious—falters immediately: why then were other fruits of Paradise lawful to him, when they too would have entered his stomach and caused the same harm?

A further perplexity follows: why are Ādam's descendants punished for the transgression of their father? Why were they not created in Paradise themselves? The father erred; the children did not. What justice compels punishment upon the innocent? Some scholars answer that God foreknew the children of Ādam would commit the same error as their father, and therefore did not create them in the Garden. Yet this response gives rise to an inescapable question: did God not foreknow Ādam's own transgression? If He did possess such knowledge, why was Ādam created in Paradise at all? Would it not have been more consistent to create him in the world, as his descendants were?

To address all these questions comprehensively, the author of this work offers two categories of response: one pertaining to Ādam *of this world* (the historical, temporal Ādam), and another concerning Ādam *of the eternal realm* (Ādam as a spiritual archetype existing in the realm of eternity).

### Ādam of the Temporal World (ʿAbdullāh ibn Tanjum)

Drawing upon the knowledge transmitted by the Imāms, the author of this compendium explains that the forbidden tree admits of two interpretations—one auspicious, the other malevolent. Both must be understood if we are to grasp the full depth of the narrative.

**The Tree in Its Noble Sense:**

⟪quran 7:20⟫
⟪ar:‏فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ لِيُبْدِىَ لَهُمَا مَا وُۥرِىَ عَنْهُمَا مِن سَوْءَتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَىٰكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَذِهِ ٱلشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ ٱلْخَلِدِينَ ‎⟫

*Then Shaitan (Satan) whispered suggestions to them both in order to uncover that which was hidden from them of their private parts (before); he said: "Your Lord did not forbid you this tree save you should become angels or become of the immortals."*

In its noble and spiritual dimension, the tree represents the *ʿilm al-ḥaqāʾiq* (the knowledge of realities)—specifically, that gnosis which leads to eternity and which liberates the soul from the bondage of the temporal world. Yet such knowledge cannot be acquired or genuinely understood save through obedience to the Imāms and allegiance to them; these sciences unfold to the worthy at their appointed hour.[^cite-13] In an even more exalted sense, the tree signifies the knowledge concerning the hierarchy of the *Qāʾim* (the Qāʾim upon his remembrance, peace be upon him) and concerning the *Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib*—knowledge of the particularities and mysteries of their station, hidden from the gaze of countless multitudes and forbidden to Ādam because he stood at the threshold of the first *dawr* (cycle). In that cycle, these sciences could not be unveiled, for they belonged to the era of *kashf* (unveiling), which had not yet arrived.[^cite-14]

It was through Iblīs's deception that he seized the station of successor—the very station that belonged to Hābīl (Abel). Through this treachery, Iblīs kindled jealousy in Qābīl (Cain) toward his brother. The consequence was murder: first enacted within the heart, and thereafter manifested outwardly.

**The Tree in Its Malevolent Aspect:**

In another sense, the tree itself was none other than Iblīs (*al-shaytān*) himself. To the question "How could evil exist within Paradise?"—the answer is that Iblīs was a mighty preacher and teacher of all the angels (*muʿallim al-malakūt*). Yet when he refused to prostrate before Ādam, he was cast out from that station. In this interpretation, the prohibition upon eating the fruit signifies that Ādam was forbidden to disclose to Iblīs any of the secrets of the *daʿwa* (the summons), precisely because Iblīs had become accursed through his disobedience.[^cite-15]

**Why Were Ādam's Descendants Deprived of Paradise?**

In response to this profound question, the author states: In one sense, Paradise is the *Dār al-Daʿwa* (the House of the Summons)—the abode whose ultimate aim is the attainment of true Paradise. For Ādam, his Paradise was a station of supreme elevation. Every high rank becomes a paradise to one of lower station; every lower rank becomes the world (the temporal realm) to one of superior rank. The uppermost limit of these stations is Paradise itself—the true, eternal Paradise—while the lowest limit is the mortal world. Ādam was created in a lofty station which possessed the quality of *taʾyīdāt* (the divine confirmations and supports); this very station *was* his Paradise. From that state, he was demoted through stages until he descended to the rank of *istijaʾāb* (responsiveness), which is the lowest of the ranks, though not excluded from them.

The rank of *istijāba* belongs to those who bear the responsibility of instruction—the *mufīdūn* (the Benefactors)—who must educate the *mustajībūn* (the Respondents) and cause them to obey the prescriptions of the Sharīʿa, elevating them through the ranks toward Paradise. The esoteric knowledge (al-ʿilm al-bāṭin) is not lawful or permissible for the Mustajib; in this rank, obedience to the commandments of the Sharīʿa alone is the condition. A Respondent must obey the authority above him without hesitation, and must fulfill the commands of the Sharīʿa, whether or not he comprehends the inner meanings that his Benefactor understands. When the Benefactor observes that the Mustajib has observed the commands of the Sharīʿa with perfect strictness, he then elevates him to the ranks where the inner meanings of these commandments are revealed to him. The son of a Benefactor becomes himself a Benefactor.

The descendants of Ādam are the descendants of the *Mustajib* rank; all of them belong to the order of the Respondents, whose station is the earth. And as we have explained, the Respondents are not among the people of Paradise; therefore, the earth is their dwelling place. This is why the children of Ādam were not created in the Garden, but in the terrestrial world.

**Clarification of a Subtle Point:**

It is stated in the treatises that a prophet cannot be an oppressor. Apparently, Ādam committed an error, and the one who errs is an evildoer; the abode of evildoers is Hell. By this logic, Ādam could not be a prophet. Yet the truth is this: Ādam possessed a group of sinners upon whom this punishment was to be enacted. Ādam's lower veil—the aspect of him that was susceptible to error—descended to the islands. His higher veil—his supernal aspect—remained among the nobles. In every *dawr* (cycle), there appears an Ādam, and he too errs. (*Mullā Jāvīd Zīāʾī*)[^cite-16]

### Ādam of the Spiritual Realm (The Second Emanation)

The second explanation of this narrative concerns the realm of eternity. Here it refers to Ādam who was created as intellect—specifically, the *Munbaʿith al-Thānī* (the Second Emanator). His Paradise was a spiritual world in which he dwelt together with all the other intellects. The tree signified the station of the First Source (*al-Munbaʿith al-Awwal*), whose rank and equality were not permissible for the Second Source, inasmuch as the First was superior by virtue of its nobility. The devil represented a wicked conception—a false superstition of equality—that led him toward revolt and the aspiration to attain rank equal to the First Source. God forbade him not to seek equality and to abandon the illusion of rank-equivalence.

The duty of the Second Source was to obey his predecessor (the First Source) precisely as the First Source had obeyed his own predecessor (the First Intellect). The sin consisted in eating the fruit of the tree—that is, in rebellion against this hierarchy and in the seeking of rank equal to the First Source. On account of this transgression, he was cast out from the Garden and fell from his station. The seven intellects passed beyond him, though he had surpassed them all in the affirmation of unity. These seven utterances are in fact the *kalimat* (the Words) of Allāh (emanating from the First Source), mentioned in the Qurʾān as follows:

⟪quran 2:37⟫
⟪ar:‏فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَتٍۢ فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫

*Then Adam received from his Lord Words. And his Lord pardoned him (accepted his repentance). Verily, He is the One Who forgives (accepts repentance), the Most Merciful.*

Through these Words—the seven utterances—Ādam repented to his Lord (the First Emanator), and his repentance was accepted. He was restored to Paradise (the ranks of the intellects). His descendants are those who imitated him in the spiritual world and attained rank equal to him. All of them, along with the devil-spirit, were expelled from the spiritual world and cast into the temporal realm. Iblīs was not brought back to the Garden because of his stubbornness; he was accursed forever.

Iblīs's hatred for Ādam and his descendants (the group of the Repentant) manifested itself in the form of opposition and obstinacy. The descendants of Ādam who dwelt in the primordial world of eternity were elevated and restored to Paradise, even as their father Ādam was. And those who obeyed Iblīs were cast into Hell together with their leader—this Hell being their true abode in the temporal realm.

## Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ's Repentance

As soon as the consequences of their transgression descended upon both of them, both Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ (Eve) recognized their mistake immediately. Without delay, they turned in repentance to Allāh and declared:

⟪quran 7:23⟫
⟪ar:‏قَالَا رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَآ أَنفُسَنَا وَإِن لَّمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ ٱلْخَسِرِينَ ‎⟫

*They said: "Our Lord! We have wronged ourselves. If You forgive us not, and bestow not upon us Your Mercy, we shall certainly be of the losers."*

They declared: "Our Lord, we have gravely wronged ourselves by transgressing Your command; we have disobeyed You. If You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we shall surely be among the utterly ruined." Neither Ādam nor Ḥawwāʾ insisted upon their transgression; both confessed their sin openly, besought forgiveness, and sought repentance and absolution from the Divine.

**Statement of Distinction:**

Here appears the profound difference between Iblīs and Ādam. Both erred; yet Iblīs clung to his error and became hardened in obstinacy, whereas Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ turned immediately in repentance and sought forgiveness from their Lord. This distinction marks the divide between those who shall be saved and those who shall be destroyed.

The Lord responded to them, saying:

⟪quran 2:36⟫
⟪ar:‏فَأَزَلَّهُمَا ٱلشَّيْطَنُ عَنْهَا فَأَخْرَجَهُمَا مِمَّا كَانَا فِيهِ ۖ وَقُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎⟫

*Then the Shaitan (Satan) made them slip therefrom (the Paradise), and got them out from that in which they were. We said: "Get you down, all, with enmity between yourselves. On earth will be a dwelling place for you and an enjoyment for a time."*

⟪quran 7:24-25⟫
⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎ ‏قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫

*(Allah) said: "Get down, one of you an enemy to the other. On earth will be a dwelling-place for you and an enjoyment, for a time." He said: "Therein you shall live, and therein you shall die, and from it you shall be brought out (i.e. resurrected)."*

That is, the Imām al-Hunaid (Allāh be pleased with him) expelled both of them from his immediate presence—that supreme station which was the *ḥaḍrat* (the courtly proximity to the Divine). He sent them to the *jazāʾir* (the islands, or lowered stations). In the courtly presence of the Imām, they possessed the *ʿilm al-muʾayyad* (knowledge confirmed by divine support); there was no need for gradual learning or exertion. Whenever a question arose, they received the answer directly from the Imāmic knowledge. But in the islands, there exists a chain of mediated instruction (*talʿīm*) in which one must advance gradually, laboring earnestly to acquire understanding. This became their punishment for disobedience. The Imām al-Hunaid said to both: "We had bestowed upon you our support, which you did not appreciate and for which you lusted after more. Now descend to the islands and labor; advance step by step so that you come to understand how luxurious was your former state. Taste the bitterness first, that you may comprehend the sweetness of fresh water."

⟪quran 2:37⟫
⟪ar:‏فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَتٍۢ فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫

*Then Adam received from his Lord Words. And his Lord pardoned him (accepted his repentance). Verily, He is the One Who forgives (accepts repentance), the Most Merciful.*

Immediately upon being disciplined by the Imām, both learned—through their repentance—the sacred names and words by means of which they could draw near to the Divine and recover the Paradise they had lost (the station of prophethood). Ḥaḍrat Ẓiʾ Ṣāḥib relates that those Words were indeed the blessed names of the *Panjatan Pāk* (the Five Pure Ones): *Muḥammad, ʿAlī, Fāṭima, al-Ḥasan, and al-Ḥusayn—may peace be upon them all*.[^cite-17] Ādam had encountered these exalted names in the superior books he had studied but could not then comprehend. Through these sacred names, he repented and besought the Imām, saying: "We ask forgiveness of You through these blessed names." The Imām accepted Ādam's repentance and forgave him. Truly, the Imām al-Hunaid is All-Forgiving, All-Merciful! Ādam approached the Imām al-Hunaid once more through these Words, and the Imām forgave him, restoring him to the station of prophethood. Satan was vanquished, and Allāh's promise came to pass.

The Qurʾān testifies:

> *"My servants—over them you shall have no power except those who follow you from among the wayward."* ⟪quran 15:42⟫[^cite-18]

This narrative imparts a vital lesson: if a person commits a sin, that sin does not condemn them irrevocably. Simultaneously, it brings glad tidings: when a believer sins and thereafter repents, seeking forgiveness and the intercession of the *awliyāʾ* (the saints of God), Allāh forgives them immediately, and they are not excluded from the congregation of Allāh. By contrast, one who commits a sin yet denies its gravity, refuses to seek forgiveness, and spurns the aid of the saints of God abandons the congregation of Allāh and joins the congregation of Iblīs, becoming a member of his faction.

Thus has Allāh established two conditions for the forgiveness of sins. The first condition is *tawba* (repentance)—the turning-away from sin through sincere acknowledgment and the seeking of divine pardon. The second condition is to implore the aid of the *awliyāʾ* (the saints). It is of the utmost importance that the Messenger and the saints of Allāh stand as mediators in the seeking of forgiveness, as Allāh declares in *Sūrat al-Nisāʾ*:

⟪quran 4:64⟫
⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن رَّسُولٍ إِلَّا لِيُطَاعَ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَلَوْ أَنَّهُمْ إِذ ظَّلَمُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَهُمْ جَآءُوكَ فَٱسْتَغْفَرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَٱسْتَغْفَرَ لَهُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ لَوَجَدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابًۭا رَّحِيمًۭا ‎⟫

*We sent no Messenger, but to be obeyed by Allah's Leave. If they (hypocrites), when they had been unjust to themselves, had come to you (Muhammad PBUH) and begged Allah's Forgiveness, and the Messenger had begged forgiveness for them: indeed, they would have found Allah All-Forgiving (One Who accepts repentance), Most Merciful.*

## The Descent from the Garden to the Earth

Allāh forgave both of them and restored them to the rank of *khalīfa* (vicegerency) and *imāma* (the imamate). Yet He deprived them of His direct *muʾayyad* (support) and immediate presence. He sent them away from His court and from the *Imāms*, so that henceforth they received instruction indirectly. Whereas in the courtly presence of the Imām they had received confirmations directly, now those confirmations came through mediation. The Lord said:

⟪quran 2:38-39⟫
⟪ar:‏قُلْنَا ٱهْبِطُوا۟ مِنْهَا جَمِيعًۭا ۖ فَإِمَّا يَأْتِيَنَّكُم مِّنِّى هُدًۭى فَمَن تَبِعَ هُدَاىَ فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ وَكَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَتِنَآ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫

*We said: "Get down all of you from this place (the Paradise), then whenever there comes to you Guidance from Me, and whoever follows My Guidance, there shall be no fear on them, nor shall they grieve. But those who disbelieve and belie Our Ayat (proofs, evidences, verses, lessons, signs, revelations, etc.) such are the dwellers of the Fire, they shall abide therein forever.*

Not only Iblīs but also Ādam, Ḥawwāʾ, and their descendants were deprived of the stations of nobility and sent to the islands. The Imām made clear: "In opposition to your invitation on the islands, Iblīs shall establish his satanic *daʿwa* (summons) and will lead your descendants astray. Yet I shall guide your descendants through you. Whosoever accepts My invitation shall be restored by Me to My Paradise; whosoever accepts Satan's invitation shall be with him, imprisoned in this world (the islands) forever, never able to enter My Paradise."

⟪quran 7:24-25⟫
⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎ ‏قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫

*(Allah) said: "Get down, one of you an enemy to the other. On earth will be a dwelling-place for you and an enjoyment, for a time." He said: "Therein you shall live, and therein you shall die, and from it you shall be brought out (i.e. resurrected)."*

The Imām declared: "For you and your descendants, there is an abode and a place of habitation in the islands for a time." Then He said: "In this earth shall you be born, in it shall you die, and from it shall you be resurrected." Thus was Ādam brought low from the station of direct support; yet He did not wholly deprive him of His blessings. Now the *ḥudūd al-ʿAlawiyya* (the upper limits)—the sacred words (*kalimat*)—were established as mediators. These became mediators of Ādam, and Ādam became mediators to the limits below him. This process continued uninterrupted throughout the hierarchy. The Prophet (upon him peace and blessings) said: "I receive revelation from Gabriel; Gabriel receives from Michael; Michael receives from Isrāfīl; Isrāfīl receives from the Tablet; and the Tablet receives from the Pen." That is to say, now revelation to the *Nāṭiq* (the Speaker) is received through five sources, and from the Speaker, those sciences are transmitted to the five subordinate limits, and from them to the believers.

The sixth of these two is the one who accepts from them.

Iblīs became the enemy of Ādam, and two *daʿwas* (summonses) were established in opposition: one, the invitation of Ādam to truth; the other, the invitation of Satan to falsehood. Whoever maintains allegiance to Ādam and the people of Ādam is called *Walī Allāh* (a saint of God) and joins *Ḥizb Allāh* (the party of God); whoever disbelieves in Ādam, denies them, and opposes them becomes a partner of Satan, for Satan has prevailed over him and he has forgotten the remembrance of Allāh. The events that transpired between Ādam and Ḥawwāʾ replayed themselves in their descendants; their descendants stood in the very likeness of their parents. To this day, only one division remains among mankind—a division between male and female. Similarly, among Ādam's progeny there remain but two kinds: the obedient and the disobedient. Allāh promised to settle both upon this earth and to restore them to Paradise through their repentance and forgiveness. He threatened the hypocrites and polytheists with punishment, saying:

Whoever loves and holds *wilāya* (allegiance) to them becomes reckoned among their congregation. He who holds *wilāya* to Satan becomes Satan himself and shall be called the son of Satan. He shall have no kinship or connection with Ādam. For this reason, Allāh expelled from the family of Nūḥ (Noah) his son Ḥām, saying:

*He said: "O Nuh (Noah)! Surely, he is not of your family; verily, his work is unrighteous, so ask not of Me that of which you have no knowledge! I admonish you, lest you be one of the ignorants."*

## Qābīl's Envy and Hābīl's Martyrdom

Allāh established Ādam (peace be upon him) as His *khalīfa* (vicegerent) over his peers and named him the Messenger of Allāh (upon him be peace) and the *Ḥujjat Allāh* (the Proof of God). Their progeny grew and multiplied. At the end of his life, Ādam appointed his son Hābīl (Abel) as his heir and successor. His brother Qābīl (Cain), seized by envy, rose up and slew him, thinking that if Hābīl did not exist, their father would be compelled to appoint him as his successor. When this delusion did not come to pass, he became opposed to their father Ādam. By persisting in sin and refusing to repent, he created discord and corruption in the *daʿwa*, becoming an ally of Satan.

When Hābīl was martyred, Allāh blessed Ādam with another son, ⟪ar:'شیث علیہ السلام'⟫*Shayyit* (Seth, peace be upon him). Ādam named him *Hiba Allāh* (the Gift of God) and appointed him as his successor in place of Hābīl. In secret, Ādam entrusted him with all the sciences of *taʾwīl* (esoteric exegesis) and spiritual truths, and with the governance of the *Daʿwa al-Hādiya* (the Guiding Summons). He did not make this known openly, lest the enemy take advantage of it. Only the upper limits and the sincere believers were aware of it. In this manner, Ādam withdrew from the active service of the *Daʿwa* after fulfilling his obligations. From Shayyit, the line of the Imamate and prophethood continued in the descendants of Ādam. Among these descendants was the Prophet Idrīs (Enoch, peace be upon him). Thus the path of guidance continued unbroken through the progeny of Ādam until the time of Nūḥ (Noah, peace be upon him) came.

## Ādam: The Symbolic Embodiment of the Bismillāh

Ādam (peace be upon him) is the perfect embodiment of the first verse, the *Bismillāh* (*"In the Name of Allāh, the Compassionate, the Merciful"*), which stands before *Sūrat al-Ḥamd* and before all matters of significance. Just as the *Bismillāh* is recited before all things begin, so too did Ādam mark the beginning of the *dawr* (cycle).

In the wisdom contained within his name—*ʿAbd Allāh* (the Servant of God)—lies the meaning of the *Bismillāh*. He is the epitome of *raḥma* (mercy) in the succession of the prophets and the epitome of *walāya* (divine friendship) in the *Dāʾim al-Islām* (the Pillars of Islam). He is the first person whose *wilāya* Allāh enjoined upon the creation, commanding the angels to prostrate before him. The meaning of prostration is in truth *ṭāʿa* (obedience); obedience is the very proof and manifestation of *wilāya*. All the angels prostrated except Satan. The *wilāya* of Ādam became a test: Ādam was like the *Bismillāh* itself. All creation is rendered void without *wilāya*; whoever lacks the *wilāya* of the Imāms will be of no benefit to those who come after them. He is the first of the Prophets, the first of the Messengers, and the first of the Imāms of the religion—the father of all of them and the source of the obligatory *wilāya* that Allāh has decreed.

In the human body, he holds the station of the sperm that dwells in the womb of mother and father before the sperm of the child—for the components of *waḥy* (revelation) are scattered throughout creation. This is called the *sulāla* (quintessence or extract), which is preserved within the parents. The *Sharīʿa* of Muḥammad, which would emerge after six thousand years, was founded and established by him.

Do not attach yourself to the congregation of Iblīs through obstinacy. Remember well: no matter how grave the sin may be, repentance grants forgiveness just as cloth is cleansed and made bright when washed with soap. Do not despair of Allāh's mercy, for to do so is to join the ranks of the disbelievers. Rather, turn always toward hope, for He gives to us the good news, saying:
