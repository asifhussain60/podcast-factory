# Hazrat Isa
*Source: KASHKOLE, Binder 18 (The Facts of the Qur'anic Stories of the Prophets), Chapter 137. 1 topics. Language: ur.*<!-- section 1 (id=3540, raw_sort=1): مولانا علی اور حضرت عیسی کا تقابلہ -->There are many similarities and similarities between Jesus (the first Christ) and Maulana Ali (the second Messiah). We see that all the attributes that the people of Jesus have described are found in you. In the same way, the attitude of the people of 'Isa towards their Prophet (peace be upon him) was almost the same as that of the people of the Prophet (peace be upon him). For this reason, the Prophet (s) himself compared Ali to the Messiah and said that Ali's example is that of Jesus, the son of Mary.

In the explanation for calling Jesus the Messiah, it is said that you are called the Messiah because you ⟪ar:’کلمۃ اللہ‘⟫ have been anointed with him. Someone said that the Jordan Canal was anointed with water, and someone said that it was anointed with oil. In the same way, ⟪ar:کلمۃ اللہ⟫Ali (a.s.) was anointed in such a way that he did not find any impurity of the period of ignorance. Just as Jesus received prophethood when he was a child, so did Ali (a.s.) believe in the Prophet (s) when he was a child and prayed alone with him for seven years. And just as the Messiah abandoned the world by saying, ⟪ar-quote:طلقتک ثلاثا⟫ "I have divorced you thrice", so the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) addressed the world and said, ⟪ar-quote:طلقتک ثلاثا⟫"O gold and silver coins, deceive and deceive anyone other than me." Moreover, while describing the virtue of Ali, the Prophet (peace be upon him) said, "If I were not afraid that people would say the same thing about you as the Christians said about Jesus, I would have said the words of your merit that the one who listened to them would put the dust of your feet in his eyes, and drink the water of your bath and ablution."

Allah (swt) likened Ali to the Messiah.

> ⟪quran 43:57-58⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ وَلَمَّا ضُرِبَ ٱبْنُ مَرْيَمَ مَثَلًا إِذَا قَوْمُكَ مِنْهُ يَصِدُّونَ ‎ ‏وَقَالُوٓا۟ ءَأَلِهَتُنَا خَيْرٌ أَمْ هُوَ ۚ مَا ضَرَبُوهُ لَكَ إِلَّا جَدَلًۢا ۚ بَلْ هُمْ قَوْمٌ خَصِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when the son of Maryam (Mary) is quoted as an example [i.e. 'Iesa (Jesus) is worshipped like their idols), behold! Your people cry aloud (laugh out at the example). And say: "Are our aliha (gods) better or is he ['Iesa (Jesus)]?" They quoted not the above example except for argument. Nay! But they are a quarrelsome people. [(See VV. 21:97-101) - The Quran.]*
> And when the example of the son of Maryam was set for, behold, thy people laughed at him, and said: Are our gods better? Or do they not mention them except to dispute with thee? Nay, but they are a quarrelsome people.
> — **Quran 43:57-58 (inline widget)**

In this verse, the reference to the Messiah is to Hazrat Ali (a.s.), which is well understood by only the people of interpretation and the people of intellect. If this were not the case, could someone tell us what enmity the people of the Apostle, who were idolatrous, had towards Jesus that they would have become lamps at the mention of his name and refused to obey him? A person loves or hates someone when he is familiar with them. The unknown does not love or enmity the stranger. The polytheists of the Quraysh were idolaters who had nothing to do with Christianity. And then there was a long period of 600 years between Jesus and the Quraysh. There is no reason for the enmity! Our argument proves that this is the Messiah, who is a contemporary of the people of the Prophet and with whom they are also hostile. As soon as they mention the name of the Prophet, they get angry and fight with the Messenger. In the second part of the verse, Allah says, "They ask, 'Are our gods greater or are they?' Here ⟪ar:’اللہ‘⟫ , ⟪ar:’الہ‘⟫ or ⟪ar:’معبود‘⟫ refers to those people whom the people of the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) considered to be their elders and elders, and whom they followed without thinking about whether they were worthy of it or not.

The whole existence of which is a blessing for the people of religion. The ancient Israelites were the descendants of the prophets of Israel; That is, every word is a Muslim and the likeness of the Israelites.
