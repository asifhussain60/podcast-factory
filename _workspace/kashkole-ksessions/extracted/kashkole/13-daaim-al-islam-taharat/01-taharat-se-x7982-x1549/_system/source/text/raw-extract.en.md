Performing ablution at the time of the spraat:

i.e., ablution with cold water in harsh winters. Cold refers to the period of the seventies in which there is laziness in religious practices. Wudhu means staying in line with the boundaries of the invitation. Cold water refers to striving and ijtihad in doing what is very difficult and difficult to do, just as it is painful to perform ablution with cold water in the cold season. The meaning of this statement is that in the age of seventy, the believers should adhere to the limits of the call in spite of the harshness of the circumstances and continue to do ijtihad in doing good deeds even in unpleasant circumstances.

Waiting for prayer after prayer:

Waiting for another prayer after one prayer. The first prayer refers to the invitation of the present Imam (a.s.) in which a believer completes his obedience by entering through the Covenant of the Holy Prophet. The second prayer is performed when the first Imam instructs his son to lead the prayer.
