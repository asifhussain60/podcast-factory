## Waking One Who Is Asleep — The Prophetic Tradition

When the time for prayer approaches, the one who is awake should rouse the sleeping person, that he may purify himself and prepare for prayer. The inner meaning of this injunction unfolds as follows: prayer itself exemplifies truth, and the one who wakes another represents a person of spiritual awareness. The sleeping figure who remains unconscious of the world's realities stands as an image of one heedless in matters of faith. When the moment arrives to establish the daʿwa (the summons), it becomes incumbent upon the mukasar—the one who breaks through illusion—to stir the soul of the ignorant person, awakening him from the slumber of negligence that he might advance with earnestness toward the call of truth.[^cite-1]

Our heedlessness of the spiritual realm manifests as sleep within the material world. In the Arabic tongue, *kasara* means to break. A person breaks false belief through argument and logical demonstration, thereby establishing true belief—*mukasar*, one who breaks through. First, one must shatter one's own false convictions through rigorous argument; only then does one possess the capacity to do so for others. How can one break through the illusions of another if one has not first broken through one's own?

| Sleep | Sleeping Person | Awakener | Awakening |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Negligence | Ignorant of Religious Matters | Mukasar | Avoidance of Negligence |

## The Prohibition Against Urinating into Holes in the Earth

⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫ taught: "One should not urinate into holes in the earth, for there dwell living creatures such as snakes and other ⟪ar:حشرات الارض⟫—creatures of the earth." In Arabic, ⟪ar:'حیۃ'⟫ denotes a snake, though it also carries the sense of "living being" or "life" itself.[^cite-2]

In this ḥadīth (tradition), *hayy* is employed in the sense of life force, to which ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ refers as the "limits" or the *duʿāt* (the Summoners) who preach the faith. The word "earth" signifies the daʿwa of the Prophet, while the "bills" or holes in the earth represent the places of refuge of these invokers, where they establish the invitation of the Guardian. It is through these duʿāt that the dead—the ignorant—receive knowledge. Therefore, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ commanded: "Do not slander these invitees." Urine and excrement represent the false beliefs of truth's enemies—those who attribute malicious falsehoods to the true Imāms and expound the deceptive teachings of their misleading scholars to the ignorant, turning them away from the inward through reliance upon the outward.

| Earth | Bill / Hole | Hayya | Urinary Matter |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| The Guardian's Invitation | Prayer Shelter | Invitees | Slander |

## Qāʾid al-Ghurr al-Muḥajjalīn — Leader of the Radiantly-Marked

In explaining the virtues of ablution, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫ declared: ⟪ar-quote:یَحشُرُ الأُمَّۃِ یَومَ القِیاَمَتِ بَینَ الاُمَمِ غُرِّ المَحَجِّلِینَ مِن آثاَرِ الوُضُوء⟫ "Allah will gather my *Umma* on the Day of Resurrection among the nations, their foreheads gleaming and their limbs radiant from the marks of ablution." The Commander of the Faithful, Maulana ʿAlī (upon him be peace), bears the title ⟪ar:قاَئد الغُّرِّ المُحَجَّلِینَ⟫—the Leader of the Radiantly-Marked, those horses whose foreheads shine with lustre and brilliance.[^cite-3]

⟪ar:الغُّرِّ المُحَجَّلِین⟫ refers to the state that shall manifest after his passing. ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ They shall shine resplendent through knowledge of the signs of ablution—that is, the verses and the deeper realities. They have been called horses because the swiftness of their development shall be exceedingly rapid; and in the form of Maulana ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, the illustrious one shall be their leader among all. ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ specifically stated "my *Umma*" to signify the Imāms of ⟪ar:دور محمدی علیہ السلام⟫—the Muhammadan era. Thus has your *Umma* attained this excellence because the realities have been broadcast throughout this period of existence. This is because the Messenger of Allah and the *Qāʾim* (the Established One) were sent together; as he proclaimed: ⟪ar-quote:بَعَثتُ أَنَا وَالقَیَامَۃَ کَھتَینِ⟫ "I and the Resurrection have been sent forth as two fingers joined together." Therefore, the virtue of the Imāms in his time surpasses that of the Imāms in all ages past.

## The Three Things Over Which the Angels Disputed

In the *Daʿāʾim al-Islām*, our master Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān narrates that the Messenger of Allah, upon him be blessings and peace, said: "When I was ascended to the heavens, I beheld certain angels in dispute one with another." I inquired of Gabriel, "Why do these angels contend with one another?" Gabriel replied that these angels strive and exert themselves concerning three matters, which creates the appearance of dispute; yet angels never truly quarrel among themselves. Thou didst ask what those three things might be. Gabriel enumerated them thus:

| 1 | The washing of ablution performed at times of hardship |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | The hastening of footsteps to join the congregation |
| 3 | The waiting for prayer after prayer |

⟪ar:مولانا امیر المؤمنین علیہ السلام⟫ said: "I heard the Messenger of Allah, upon him be peace, declare: 'O people, shall I not inform you of that which shall expiate your sins and transgressions? Hear then: the waiting for prayer after each prayer—this is a form of *jihād* (spiritual striving).'"[^cite-4]

**The Performing of Ablution in Times of Extreme Cold:**

Ablution with cold water in the harshness of winter exemplifies the rigour of spiritual discipline. Cold refers to ⟪ar:نقل الاقدام الی الجماعۃ⟫ the age of seventy—a period when laxity in religious practice threatens. *Wudū* (ablution) signifies steadfast adherence to the boundaries of the daʿwa. Cold water represents striving and *ijtihād* (rigorous exertion) in performing deeds most difficult and arduous—just as it brings pain to perform ablution with freezing water in winter's bite. The meaning, therefore, is this: in the age of seventy, believers must hold fast to the limits of the call despite the severity of circumstance, continuing their exertions toward righteousness even when conditions prove most adverse.

| Extreme Cold | Cold Water | Ablution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Age of the Seventies | Action in Adverse Circumstances | Boundaries of the Daʿwa |

**The Hastening of Footsteps to Join the Congregation:**

When a preacher reaches the age of four, he is chosen for the position of future guide. From that moment, all external ⟪ar:فضلات⟫ attributes and internal ⟪ar:صورات⟫ forms begin to accumulate within him, preparing him to attain his destined station. This ⟪ar:نقل الاقدام الی الجماعۃ⟫ signifies that his faculties—his angels—move swiftly to gather within this child who shall become the preacher.

In the higher realm, this represents the ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ spiritual essences of the believers hastening toward their Imām.

**The Waiting for Prayer After Prayer:**

This means the waiting for one prayer to follow another. The first prayer refers to the daʿwa of the present Imām, in which the believer completes his obedience by entering through the Covenant established by the Holy Prophet. The second prayer is performed when the first Imām instructs his successor to lead the prayer. What is intended by this principle, therefore, is the believer's waiting for the present Imām to pass authority to his son—his ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ successor—after remaining steadfast in the daʿwa.

## The Prophet's Giving Water to a Cat

On one occasion, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ while performing ablution, a cat approached him. The Prophet, upon him be peace, gave to the cat some of the water of his ablution, then completed his purification with the remainder. The cat then departed and was never again seen.

⟪ar:سیدنا اسماعیل بن ھبۃ اللہ⟫ teaches that the Messenger of Allah, may Allah's blessings be upon him, was sent as a mercy not alone to mankind but to all ⟪ar:عالمین⟫—to all worlds and creatures. The water with which he performed ablution possessed within it the essence of the most pure ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫—spiritual realities. The cat represented a human being who had fallen into the snare of his own faults and had taken on the form of a cat. When the Messenger of Allah bestowed upon him the water of his ablution, that person received the gift of ⟪ar:سید الانبیاء⟫ the Prophet's grace. Therefore, through the blessings of these exalted ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ spiritual essences, the man was forgiven; and having received this dispensation, the cat abandoned its animal form and was transformed into human shape.[^cite-5]

**A Matter for Reflection:**

If the water of the Prophet's ablution possesses such potency that a cat, upon drinking it, ascends into human form, what then shall be the glory—the radiance and exaltation—of those who love him with sincerity and sacrifice their very lives in obedience to his command?
