## An Account of Ṭahāra (Ritual Purity)

Impurity arises from impurity. The transition from a state of impurity to a state of purity is termed ṭahāra (ritual purity). Since the human being comprises both body and soul, the purification of the soul must accompany the purification of the body. The purification of the body is accomplished through the perfection of the external limbs, while the purification of the soul is attained through knowledge of the inner motives behind these outward actions. The Prophet, upon whom be peace and blessings, declared: ⟪ar-quote:لاَ صَلوٰۃَ اِلاَّبِالطُّھُارۃ⟫ "Prayer is not without purity."[^cite-1] ⟪ar:فضلات ھابطۃ⟫

Understanding the limits of ablution constitutes the knowledge of ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ (the Imam, upon whom be peace); and remaining within those limits through allegiance to the Imam is the essence of responding to the call to prayer. ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ Since prayer without knowledge of the Imam's limits and guidance is forbidden, ablution likewise cannot be performed without this understanding.

The Prophet, upon whom be peace and blessings, said: "Whosoever completes his ablution and then departs his house for the mosque to pray—that believer remains in a state of prayer until his ablution is broken." In other words, through ablution, the Imam endorses the believer, imparting his spiritual blessings upon that person.[^cite-2]

## The Perfect Wuḍūʾ — Repeating Each Limb Three Times

The Messenger of Allah, upon whom be peace and blessings, declared: "The foundation of prayer rests upon four pillars, and those who do not fulfill them are deceitful." These four are:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Complete Ablution | Bowing (*ruku*) | Prostration (*sujud*) | Reverence |

Perfect ablution (*wuḍūʾ al-kāmil*) is the first of these four obligations. The perfection of ablution consists in repeating each limb three times. Water has been divided by Allah into three portions for the believers; because of these three divisions, each organ of ablution is repeated thrice. For this reason, drinking water has been ordained in three sips.[^cite-3] Knowledge similarly divides into three domains: the Sharīʿa (the Law), the taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis), and the facts (*ḥaqāʾiq*). Washing or anointing each organ thrice indicates recognition of the three limits of the daʿwa (the summons, the invitation): the Nāṭiq (the Speaker), the Asās (the Foundation), and the Imām (the Leader)—⟪ar:ناطق، اساس، امام⟫.

When a believer performs ablution once, he comes to the notice of the dāʿī (the Summoner); the Summoner comes to know him through ⟪ar:حُسن نیت⟫ (sincere intention). When he repeats the same act a second time, Allah grants him twofold reward, and he enters into the *wilāya* (guardianship) of the Imam of the Age, through which he is spiritually purified inwardly. When he performs the action a third time, the Prophet, upon whom be peace—⟪ar:ناطق⟫ (the Speaker)—declares: "This is my ablution and the ablution of the Prophets whose leader I am."[^cite-4] Such is ⟪ar:'کمال'⟫ (perfection).

Purifying each organ thrice indicates that when a believer fulfills his obligations three times—that is, when he attains knowledge of the Word through the Summoner of the Age and the Imam of the Age—⟪ar:داعی وقت، امام الزمان⟫⟪ar:ناطق شریعت⟫ (the Speaker of the Law), then his station is elevated and he is numbered among the limits of the faith. In essence, one pathway to being included in the limits of the Imam, upon whom be peace, is the practice of ablution.

| **For the First Time** | **For the Second Time** | **For the Third Time** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Summoner's Recognition | The Guardianship of the Imam | The Speaker's Ablution |
| Outward Purity | Inward Purity | Perfection |

## The Pillars of Wuḍūʾ and Their Sequence

Purity is related to the purification of seven organs, of which four are obligatory (*farāʾiḍ*) and three are of the Sunna. Each act of purification, through the invitation, signifies both a spiritual and a physical range.[^cite-5]

| Obligatory | Sunna |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Washing the face | 2. Washing both hands |
| 3. Anointing the head | 4. Anointing both feet |
| 5. Water in the nose | — |
| — | — |

Moreover, water is applied to the hands, face, forearms, and feet—also seven organs. These seven limits of purity correspond to seven stations in the circles of authority, the seven Imāms, and the seven foundations. They further point to the seven stages from the believer to the Imam:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Imām | Ḥujjat | *Dhū al-mīṣa* | Bāb | Dāʿī | *Makāsir* | Believer |

The three levels below the Imam—⟪ar:حجت، ذومصۃ، باب⟫ (the Proof, the One Possessing the Staff, the Door)—belong to the Imam; the two levels above the believer—⟪ar:مُکلَّب، داعی⟫ (the Summoned and the Summoner)—belong to the believer. The Imam is the deputy of Allah, and the Summoner is the deputy of the Imam. Knowledge of these seven levels is called ablution.

It is Sunna to wash the three Sunna organs (both hands and the nose) at the beginning of ablution. Following these stations, the organs of the four obligatory acts are purified. This signifies that these three limits—the dāʿī, the ḥujjat, and the bāb—are established by the four principles—⟪ar:عقل کل، نفس کل، ناطق، اساس⟫ (the Universal Intellect, the Universal Soul, the Speaker, and the Foundation)—exemplified in the four obligatory acts of purity.

The order is thus: first the three Sunnah acts, then the four obligatory ones, just as in the invocation "Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm" (In the Name of Allah, the Merciful, the Compassionate), the three letters of "Bismillāh" precede the four letters of "Allāh." This teaches the believer to first acknowledge the status of the dāʿī—exemplified by washing the hands—then to proceed through the ḥujjat, exemplified by rinsing the mouth, and finally through the Imam, exemplified by water in the nose. For unless a believer knows and acknowledges these three limits, he cannot follow the four principles: ⟪ar:ناطق⟫ (the Speaker, exemplified by washing the face), ⟪ar:اساس⟫ (the Foundation, exemplified by washing the hands to the elbows), ⟪ar:عقل کل⟫ (the Universal Intellect, exemplified by wiping the head), and ⟪ar:نفس کل⟫ (the Universal Soul, exemplified by wiping the feet).

Through washing and anointing these seven organs, the believer acknowledges that Allah transcends all the limits exemplified by these organs. He understands that he has no share in direct knowledge of Allah, for all limits are Allah's creation, established by Him to convey His knowledge to the souls of believers and to perfect them.

The sequence of the four obligatory acts teaches that the face (the Nāṭiq) must be known first, then its foundation (the Asās), for only through knowledge of these can one confess the spiritual places—⟪ar:عقل کل، نفس کل⟫ (the Universal Intellect and the Universal Soul).

**Sequence of Ablution**

The first part of ablution is the removal of impurities from the body—⟪ar:'تغاوط'⟫ (literal evacuation). After this comes the *istinjāʾ* (ritual cleansing), and then the stages of purification begin:

## Achieving Purity Through Clean Water

The existence of water has been descended from the heavens to the earth from the beginning of creation, as Allah says—interpretation of the meaning:

> ⟪quran 23:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۢ بِقَدَرٍۢ فَأَسْكَنَّهُ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۖ وَإِنَّا عَلَىٰ ذَهَابٍۭ بِهِۦ لَقَدِرُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *And We sent down from the sky water (rain) in (due) measure, and We gave it lodging in the earth, and verily, We are able to take it away.*

Water passes through both sacred and defiled places. In sacred regions flow rivers, ponds, and wells that sustain life; in corrupted lands lie the bitter seas. When water traverses such defiled places—as with the sea—though its original essence is pure, the place transforms it through its impurity. The water no longer remains in its original state; it becomes bitter, saline, and corrupt. Such water cannot nourish grain, sustain land animals, or be drunk by humans. Instead of giving life, it destroys all that grows.[^cite-6]

Purity is the gateway to prayer, just as faith is the gateway to Islam. As all things possess both an inner reality and an outward form, so too does each outward act of purity carry an esoteric meaning. Allah says—interpretation of the meaning:

> ⟪quran 25:48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَرْسَلَ ٱلرِّيَحَ بُشْرًۢا بَيْنَ يَدَىْ رَحْمَتِهِۦ ۚ وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ طَهُورًۭا ‎⟫
> 
> *And it is He Who sends the winds as heralds of glad tidings, going before His Mercy (rain), and We send down pure water from the sky.*

> ⟪quran 8:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِذْ يُغَشِّيكُمُ ٱلنُّعَاسَ أَمَنَةًۭ مِّنْهُ وَيُنَزِّلُ عَلَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ لِّيُطَهِّرَكُم بِهِۦ وَيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمْ رِجْزَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ وَلِيَرْبِطَ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِكُمْ وَيُثَبِّتَ بِهِ ٱلْأَقْدَامَ ‎⟫
> 
> *(Remember) when He covered you with a slumber as a security from Him, and He caused water (rain) to descend on you from the sky, to clean you thereby and to remove from you the Rijz (whispering, evil-suggestions, etc.) of Shaitan (Satan), and to strengthen your hearts, and make your feet firm thereby.*

**Interpretation of Purity**

Purification is permissible only with pure water. Should any admixture enter the water—even perfume or syrup—then it becomes unsuitable for ablution or ritual bathing. Clean water removes filth, whereas filth cannot cleanse filth; contaminated water cannot purify.[^cite-7]

The wuḍūʾ (ablution) signifies a *mustajīb* (the Respondent) taking the pledge of the Imam of the Age, upon whom be peace, accepting his invitation, knowing his limits, and dissociating himself from his enemies. The inner essence of prayer lies in entering the Imam's call and remaining steadfast in his invitation through connection with the boundaries of the Imam of the Age and his saints. Prayer cannot occur without ablution, ablution cannot be performed without intention, and intention cannot exist without invoking the Name of Allah, the Merciful, the Compassionate.

Pure water is a symbol of knowledge. Just as water washes away the filth of the body, so knowledge removes the defilement of the self (*nafs*). As outward prayer is forbidden without purification, so it is forbidden to teach the sciences of interpretation and the inner facts to anyone without a covenant. For taking a covenant is the essence of purity, and the knowledge of truth is the very prayer itself.

| **Purity** | **Prayer** |
| --- | --- |
| Connection with the Imam of the Age | The Sciences of Interpretation and the Inner Facts |

**Purification with Salt Water**

In the absence of pure water, one may purify with bitter and salty water—that of the sea. The inner meaning is this: when a *mustajīb* abandons the daʿwa and dwells among his enemies, he is permitted to practice *taqiyya* (religious concealment) and express his beliefs according to their false doctrines (symbolized by bitterness).

## Water, Buckets, and Vessels (Practical Rules)

When a worshipper prepares for ablution before prayer, he draws water from a river or well. The water of the river exemplifies the manifest knowledge of the Imam of Truth, which no one has altered and which flows for all creatures—the elite and the common alike. The water of the well symbolizes the hidden, inner knowledge of the Imam, concealed and accessible only to those who have reached the well's depth. The bucket (*daw*) from which the *mustajīb* draws water from the well represents the knowledge of the dāʿī (the Summoner), who reads the knowledge of the ḥujjat (the Proof) and teaches it to others. The vessel into which water is poured for ablution exemplifies the Dāʿī al-Muṭlaq (the Absolute Summoner), who teaches knowledge to the *maʾdhūn* (the Authorized), the *makāsir* (the Breakers), whose examples are the right and left hands from which water is drawn from the vessel.[^cite-8]

Each hand has five fingers, symbolizing the five daily prayers. The five fingers of the right hand are signs of the times of prayer, and the five fingers of the left hand are the five conditions required for prayer.

With these two hands, urine and feces are cleansed: water is poured with the right hand, and impurities are removed with the left. That is, the *maʾdhūn* (who is analogous to the right hand) imparts knowledge to the *makāsir* through which they remove from believers the impurities of polytheism, disbelief, and hypocrisy entering the faith.

| **Manifest** | **Interior** |
| --- | --- |
| River Water | The Imam's Knowledge |
| Well Water | The Inner Knowledge of the Imam's Proof |
| Bucket | The Summoner Who Reads with the Proof |
| Vessel | The Absolute Summoner (*Dāʿī al-Muṭlaq*) |
| Both Hands | The Authorized and the Breakers (*Maʾdhūn* and *Makāsir*) |
| Five Fingers of the Right Hand | The Times of the Five Prayers |
| Five Fingers of the Left Hand | The Five Conditions of Prayer |
| Invocation | The Removal of Polytheism, Disbelief, and Hypocrisy by the Authorized and Breakers from the Respondent |

## Exegesis of the Verse of Purification (Q33:33) — 'God Wishes to Remove All Impurity from You, O People of the House'

Water possesses the quality that it is pure in itself and purifies others as well. Pure water requires no purification of its own. Allah purifies the impurity of the body with water and removes the impurity of the mind—ignorance—through knowledge. True purification from the defilement of ignorance comes through the sciences of the Ahl al-Bayt (the Family of the House). ⟪ar:آیت التَّطھِیر⟫ Allah says—interpretation of the meaning:

> *God wishes only to remove all impurity from you, O People of the House, and to purify you with a thorough purification.*

The need for purity arises only where impurity first exists. Were, then, the Ahl al-Bayt impure before this revelation—⟪ar:نعوذ باللّٰہ⟫ (God forbid)?

When this verse was revealed, the Messenger of Allah gathered Mawlānā ʿAlī, Mawlānā Fāṭima, Imām al-Ḥasan, and Imām al-Ḥusayn beneath his cloak and declared: "These are ⟪ar:اہل البیت⟫ (my People of the House), my own ⟪ar:أصحاب تطھیر⟫ (Companions of Purification)." ⟪ar:مولاتنا سلمیٰ⟫ (Our noble Salma) was present at that moment and requested: "O Messenger of Allah, include me also beneath your cloak." The Messenger of Allah replied: "O Salma, you are indeed upon goodness, but I cannot add anyone to this cloak save these five persons." When all five were gathered beneath the cloak, the verse was revealed. All four of them—the Imam and his family—thanked the Messenger of Allah for bestowing this blessing upon them through his favor. The Messenger of Allah answered: "It is not so! Rather, within all four of you lies an ability and glory that Allah Himself has revealed to you all!"[^cite-9]

The Messenger of Allah distinguished himself and ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ (the Commander of the Believers ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, upon whom be peace), Fāṭima, al-Ḥasan, and al-Ḥusayn from all of humankind by drawing all five beneath his cloak. He made clear that ⟪ar:اہل البیت⟫ (the People of the House) were alone beneath that cloak. And to fly the cloak of the Messenger of Allah himself is to exemplify the distinctiveness, exaltation, and leadership of one's position among these five. He pointed out that these five beings are pure in their essence; no impurity can ever touch them, just as an outer garment shields a person from dirt. If there is any regression, it falls outside the cloak!

In this verse, the word "impurity" (*rijz*) refers to the false beliefs, concepts, doubts, and associationism (*shirk*) of believers concerning the knowledge of these personalities and their descendants, the ⟪ar:ائمہ طاھرین⟫ (Pure Imāms). These erroneous thoughts of believers regarding the glory of the Ahl al-Bayt constitute, for them, a house of filth and defilement. In this verse, Allah states that He has determined to correct the false beliefs of believers through the knowledge of interpretation and the inner facts—symbolized by pure water—and to grant them the true knowledge of the Imāms, that they are worthy of all veneration and obedience.

**On the Facts**

⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)، مولانا علی⟫ None of the Imāms require physical cleansing, for they are free from any impurity. Yet they purified themselves in order to remove defects that had accumulated in their veils (*ḥijāb*) through knowledge. Their purity was in truth the purification of their veils. ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ The Messenger of Allah possessed such virtue that he rectified a single error within the modest veil of his prophethood.[^cite-10]

## The Heavens, the Earth, and Pure Water (Their Cosmological Relation)

The sky is the source from which water flows. Water possesses the distinctive quality of being pure in its essence and continually falling from the sky—from above—to the earth below. The earth absorbs this water and produces vegetation for all creatures, which is the cause of their life and continuance. Like water, knowledge perpetually flows from the higher to the lower.

The Heavens (*al-samāʾ*) refer to the Prophet of this age, ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اﷲ علیہ و آلہ و سلم⟫ (Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā, upon whom be peace and blessings); the earth refers to his successor, ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ (the Commander of the Believers ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, upon whom be peace). "Water" signifies the knowledge that the recipient acquires, and "purity" means freedom from all manner of doubt and suspicion. In another circle of interpretation, the sky represents the one who gives benefit (*al-muʿṭī*); the earth then becomes the beneficiary (*al-mustafīd*). Moving further within the circles of taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis), the Imām, upon whom be peace, faces the sky, and the ḥujjat (the Proof) is his earth.[^cite-11]

In essence, the Messenger of Allah bestowed all his knowledge upon ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ (ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib). ʿAlī received this weighty and profound knowledge of the Prophet, upon whom be peace, and rendered it accessible so as to make it beneficial for the souls of all human beings and to ensure the life and survival of their spiritual existence.

| **Sky** | **Earth** | **Pure Water** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Speaker (*al-Nāṭiq*) | The Guardian (*al-Walī*) | Knowledge Free from All Doubt |
| The Imām | The Proof (*al-Ḥujjat*) | The Imām's Knowledge |
| The Giver (*al-Muʿṭī*) | The Receiver (*al-Mustafīd*) | Knowledge of the Highest Extent |
| Benefit | The Beneficiary | Guidance (*al-Hidāya*) |

There are two types of earth that absorbs water: good earth and bad earth. When water falls upon good soil, it produces vegetation beneficial for animals and humans to drink and sustain life. But when the same water falls upon bad land—such as the sea—its character changes, and it becomes useless to all.

**Pure Earth**

The inner earth is symbolized by the Imāms, who are the successors of the Prophet, upon whom be peace, and the Imāms descended from him, who stand by his command and serve the Call to Truth. This is why ⟪ar:ناطق علیہ السلام⟫ (the Speaker, upon whom be peace) said: "The earth is your mother, and she does good to you." With these words, the Prophet, upon whom be peace, pointed to his successor ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ (ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib). When ʿAlī received this knowledge from the Prophet, upon whom be peace, he remained pure and untainted, becoming a means of growth and development for the souls of the believers.[^cite-12]

**Impure Earth**

Similarly, the bad earth is a symbol of the corrupt people of the Umma and the false imāms, as well as those who will continue in their place until the Day of Resurrection. Concerning this corrupt earth, ⟪ar:سیدنا مؤید اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ (Our Master al-Muʾayyad, may Allah exalt his sanctuary) says: "O people, O people drowsy in negligence, who dwell in the lap of a mother about to devour her own children—why do you sit in ease in a house destined for destruction?"[^cite-13]

When ⟪ar:ناطق علیہ السلام⟫ (the Speaker, upon whom be peace) brought pure knowledge—sweet water—to the invitation, they corrupted it, changing its essence. The Zodiac (*Burūj*) is an emblem of the salty water of the sea, and their followers are the creatures living within it, whose existence depends solely upon this salt water. Deprived of it, they perish. That is, ⟪ar:کبرا أمت (ابو بکر، عمر، عثمان)⟫ (the great ones of the Umma—Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, and ʿUthmān) altered the knowledge of the Messenger of Allah, upon whom be peace and blessings. And then ⟪ar:متبعین⟫ (the followers) acquired this false knowledge from their predecessors because their souls were capable only of understanding this corrupted knowledge.

## Clouds and Rain (in the Ṭahāra Discussion)

**Clouds**

There are two types of water that clouds shed from the sky. Some clouds thunder but do not rain; others are heavy with water and pour upon the earth. The cloud is a symbol of the dāʿī (the Summoner). Those clouds that rain are like ⟪ar:داعات قلم⟫ (the Summoners of the Pen)—they write books and spread the sciences with the support of their Imām. Those clouds that merely thunder resemble ⟪ar:داعات سیف⟫ (the Summoners of the Sword)—they do not write books but fight with the sword. The purpose of both is to purify the souls and excesses of the believers.[^cite-14]

| **Raining Clouds** | **Thundering Clouds** |
| --- | --- |
| The Summoners of the Pen | The Summoners of the Sword |

**Precipitation**

⟪ar:نصف النحار⟫ (The Midday Heat), when the sun ⟪ar:شمس⟫ (the Shams) reaches its peak, draws water from the earth in the form of vapor through its heat. It then makes this vapor pure water and causes it to rain back upon the earth through the clouds. The sun is a likeness of ⟪ar:امام وقت علیہ السلام⟫ (the Imām of the Age, upon whom be peace). The earth is a likeness of the knowledge of ⟪ar:أمیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ (the Commander of the Believers ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, upon whom be peace). The vapors are the subtle, eloquent, and wise words hidden within the ⟪ar:نہج البلاغۃ⟫ (the Path of Eloquence—the collection of ʿAlī's sermons and sayings). These words are so eloquent that human comprehension cannot fully grasp their depths. Therefore, when the Imām of the Age reaches the peak of his knowledge—⟪ar:نصف النحار⟫ (the Midday)—he draws wisdom from these eloquent words of ⟪ar:ابو تراب⟫ (Abū Turāb, "Father of Earth," a title of ʿAlī), and then transforms this wisdom into accessible knowledge (*mā*) and broadcasts it throughout the world through his prayers, so that the souls of the believers might benefit therefrom.

## The Difference Between Washing (Ghasl) and Wiping (Masḥ)

It is related in ḥadīth that in the early days of Islam, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ (the Messenger of Allah, upon whom be peace and blessings) used to wash his head and feet. But after the event of the Night Journey (*al-Isrāʾ*), when this verse was revealed to him:

> *O you who believe! When you stand up for prayer, wash your faces and your hands up to your elbows, and wipe your heads and your feet up to the ankles.*

He changed this practice at Allah's command and began to wipe his head and feet. His successor understood the gesture and perceived the Prophet's purpose; he, too, anointed his head and feet. But his adversaries, unaware of this subtle meaning, remained attached to the earlier practice.[^cite-15]

The reason is this: in ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اﷲ علیہ و آلہ  و سلم،⟫ (Muḥammad al-Muṣṭafā, upon whom be peace and blessings,) the early stages of his education, he had not yet truly comprehended the hierarchy of the other limits. ⟪ar:عقل کل⟫ (the Universal Intellect) and ⟪ar:نفس کل⟫ (the Universal Soul) seemed to him as if they were bounded and corporeal. For this reason, he used to wash his head and feet as if they were bodily organs exemplifying physical limits. When ⟪ar:ناطقیت⟫ (his station as the Speaker) was perfected and he ascended in spirit to the heavens—becoming fully the ⟪ar:نفس کل⟫ (the Universal Soul)—the Holy Prophet perceived these spiritual limits as they truly are. Thereafter, in every act of ablution, he only wiped the head and feet. Through this action, he demonstrated to the Umma that these two limits—⟪ar:عقل کل اور نفس کل⟫ (the Universal Intellect and the Universal Soul)—are not corporeally tangible but rational and intelligible. Their hierarchy cannot be known, but their realities can be acknowledged.[^cite-16]

This incident makes clear that the Messenger of Allah, upon whom be peace and blessings, gradually attained the stations of knowledge.

Wiping (*masḥ*) refers to the acknowledgment of Being, while washing (*ghasl*) denotes knowledge of place, obedience, and submission. The parts that believers are commanded to wash in ablution—⟪ar:جسمانی حدود⟫ (the physical limits), such as ⟪ar:ناطق⟫ (the Speaker) and ⟪ar:اساس⟫ (the Foundation)—are examples of knowledge and obedience. Those commanded to wipe—⟪ar:روحانی حدود⟫ (the spiritual limits), such as ⟪ar:عقل کل، نفس کل⟫ (the Universal Intellect and the Universal Soul)—are examples of acknowledgment of existence, for man does not possess the power to know these spiritual beings. Some limits must be fully understood; others require only partial acknowledgment.

| **Wiping** | **Washing** |
| --- | --- |
| Spiritual Limits | Physical Limits |
| The Universal Intellect and Soul | The Speaker and Foundation |
| Acknowledgment of Existence | Knowledge with Submission |
| Partial Knowledge | Complete Knowledge |

## Wiping Over the Turban and Socks

The Imāms are in consensus that it is not permissible to wipe over the socks during ablution. Allah commanded the wiping of the feet themselves, not the wiping of the socks. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ (Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, upon whom be peace) said: "Religious concealment (*taqiyya*) is my religion and the religion of my fathers." Yet there are three matters in which taqiyya is not permissible:

| 1 | In the use of intoxicating substances |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | In wiping over socks |
| 3 | In abandoning the recitation of *Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm* in prayer |

The Imāms, upon whom be peace, declared: "It is forbidden for one who believes that wiping over socks is permissible, for he will pray without purification—that is, without complete ablution—as if he were not praying at all."[^cite-17]

At the beginning of his Prophethood, the Prophet, upon whom be peace and blessings, used to wipe his feet and head through his socks and turban, for the truth concerning ⟪ar:عقل کل اور نفس کل⟫ (the Universal Intellect and the Universal Soul) had not yet been unveiled to him. When ⟪ar:جبرائیل امین⟫ (Gabriel the Faithful) revealed the truth of ⟪ar:عقل کل اور نفس کل⟫ (the Universal Intellect and the Universal Soul) to the Prophet, upon whom be peace, and he had attained knowledge of the hierarchy of these two spiritual limits, he wiped his head and bare feet. His successor, perceiving this reality and accepting his command, ceased wiping over the turban and socks.[^cite-18]

## Tayammum — Dry Ablution with Earth

⟪ar:تیمم⟫ (Tayammum—dry ablution) is mentioned by Allah, the Most High, in His words—interpretation of the meaning:

> *And if you are ill or on a journey, or one of you comes from the toilet, or you have touched women, and you find no water, then resort to pure earth, striking your face and your hands with it.*

In this verse, three categories of people are mentioned: the sick, the traveler, and one who has emerged from the place of impurity. Esoterically, these represent three types of people in need of purification. The **sick** is a believer whose religious concepts have become corrupted (spiritually ill). The **traveler** is one far distant from his beneficial master (*al-mufīd*). The **one who comes from the toilet** is one whose thoughts have been assailed by doubts. In this verse, the term ⟪ar:غائط⟫ (the toilet—*al-ghāʾiṭ*) is employed; in lexicography, it refers to a low-lying place on earth where one can hide while defecating, unseen by others. The inner meaning suggests that the purification of the *mustajīb* (the Respondent) through inner knowledge should occur independently, without others' awareness that the *mustajīb* is acquiring this knowledge.[^cite-19]

| **The Sick** | **The Traveler** | **The One Emerging from Toilet** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corruption in Religious Concepts | Distance from the Beneficial Master | Thoughts Assailed by Doubts |

⟪ar:اَولَمُستِم اَلنِّسَاءِ⟫ ("Or you have touched women") refers to physical intercourse; esoterically, it signifies that knowledge has passed between a ⟪ar:مفید⟫ (beneficent teacher) and ⟪ar:مستفید⟫ (a beneficiary learner), and therefore both must purify themselves. Intercourse can be lawful (*ḥalāl*) or unlawful (*ḥarām*). If the ⟪ar:مستفید⟫ (beneficiary) acquires knowledge from someone ⟪ar:مفید⟫ (who is authorized to teach—as if through lawful marriage), then this teaching is considered lawful; otherwise, it is unlawful.[^cite-20]

Should the *mustajīb* belong to one of these four categories, he is obliged to purify his knowledge. Yet if he lacks water—the knowledge of truth—he may purify himself with earth. Earth is a metaphor for the believer, as stated in the Qur'ān:

> *On the Day of Resurrection, the disbeliever will say: "Oh, if only I were dust!"*

That is, if the respondent does not find access to one of the highest limits of the daʿwa, then it is permissible for him to gain knowledge from a believer near to him whose religion and faith are sound. Even one of the higher limits of the daʿwa may encounter an elder who embodies the likeness of water—that is, pure knowledge.[^cite-21]

**In Tayammum**

⟪ar:تیمُّم⟫ Only the face and both hands are wiped. The esoteric meaning is that there are only two stations of faith. The first is distancing oneself from ignorance, and the second is entering faith through taking the covenant. The *maʾdhūn* (the Authorized) and the *makāsir* (the Breakers) elevate the ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ (Respondent) to these two stations, which are analogous to wiping both palms. Both make the ⟪ar:مستفید⟫ (Beneficiary) and ⟪ar:ناطق دور⟫ (the Speaker of the Age) acquainted with the status of both. It is not lawful to acquire knowledge from a common believer in the presence of a *mufīd* (beneficent teacher).
