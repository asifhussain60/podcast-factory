## An Account of Ṭahāra (Ritual Purity)

Impurity arises from impurity. The transition from a state of impurity to a state of purity is what we call purification. Since humanity comprises both body and soul, it is necessary to purify the soul alongside the purification of the body. The purification of the body is achieved through the completion of its external organs, while the purification of the soul is attained through knowledge of the inner motives behind these outward actions. The Prophet—peace and blessings of God be upon him—declared: ⟪ar-quote:لاَ صَلوٰۃَ اِلاَّبِالطُّھُورۃ⟫ "Prayer is not without purity."[^cite-1]

Knowledge of the limits of ablution (wuḍūʾ) is an essential argument: the Imam—peace be upon him—has established that staying within these limits constitutes an argument for answering the call to prayer. Since it is not permissible to pray without knowledge of the limits and the means through which the Imam transmits guidance, it is equally impermissible to pray without ablution. The Prophet—peace and blessings of God be upon him—said: "Whoever completes his purification and then goes out from his house to the mosque to pray, that believer remains in a state of prayer until his ablution is broken."[^cite-2] In other words, through ablution the believer enters the Imam's covenant and begins to receive the Imam's grace and endorsement.

## The Perfect Wuḍūʾ — Repeating Each Limb Three Times

The Messenger of God—peace and blessings of God be upon him—said: "The foundation of prayer rests upon four things, and those who neglect them are deceitful." These four pillars are:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Complete ablution | Genuflection (rukūʿ) | Prostration (sajda) | Reverence |

Perfect ablution is the first of these four obligations. The perfection of ablution is achieved by repeating each member three times. Water represents the graces of the believers, which God has divided into three parts. Because of these three divisions, each organ of ablution is washed three times. For this reason too, it has been ordained to drink water in three sips. Knowledge is similarly divided into three parts: the jurisprudence (sharīʿa), the esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl), and the inner truths (ḥaqāʾiq). Washing or anointing each organ three times signifies recognition of the three ranks of the daʿwa (the summons): the Speaker (nāṭiq), the Foundation (asās), and the Imam (al-imām).[^cite-3]

When a believer performs ablution for the first time, he comes under the notice of the Summoner (dāʿī)—the Summoner becomes aware of him. When he repeats the same deed a second time, God multiplies his reward twofold, and he enters into the spiritual guardianship (wilāya) of the Imam of the Age (Imām al-Zamān), which purifies him inwardly. When he performs it a third time, the Prophet—peace be upon him—declares: "This is my ablution and the ablution of the Prophets whose leader I am."[^cite-4]

Purifying each organ three times signifies that when a believer fulfills his obligations three times—that is, when he attains knowledge of the Speaker (nāṭiq), the Summoner of the time (dāʿī al-waqt), and the Imam of the Age (imām al-zamān)—then his spiritual station is elevated and he is included within the hierarchy (ḥudūd) of religion. In other words, one path to being included within the ranks of the Imam—peace be upon him—is through ablution.

| For the first time | Second time | Third time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Summoner's gaze | Spiritual guardianship of the Imam | Perfection of the Prophet's ablution |
| Outward purity | Inward purity | Spiritual elevation |

## The Pillars of Wuḍūʾ and Their Sequence

Purity is associated with seven organs, of which four are obligatory (farḍ) and three are Sunna (recommended). Each act of purification in the invitation signifies both a spiritual and physical dimension.

| Obligatory | Recommended |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Washing the face | 1. Washing both hands |
| 2. Washing both hands (to the elbows) | 2. Anointing of both ears |
| 3. Anointing the head | 3. Water in the nose |
| 4. Anointing of both feet | |

In addition to these seven organs, water is applied to the face, two hands, two arms, and two feet—which are themselves seven organs.[^cite-5] These seven limits of purity correspond to seven stations in the hierarchy of the faith, seven Imams, and seven foundations. Moreover, from the believer to the Imam, they point to seven hierarchical grades:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Imam | Proof (ḥujja) | Assistant (dhū al-miṣqa) | Gate (bāb) | Summoner (dāʿī) | Initiate (muʿallim) | Believer (mustajīb) |

The three ranks below the Imam—the Proof, the Assistant, and the Gate—belong to the Imam's own hierarchy. The two ranks between the Summoner and the believer—the Initiate and Muʿallim—pertain to the believer's sphere. The Imam is the deputy of God, and the Speaker is the deputy of the Imam. Knowledge of these seven grades is called ablution.

It is Sunna to wash the three Sunna organs (hands, ears, and nose) at the beginning of purification. After following these intermediaries, the four obligatory organs are purified. This means that these three ranks (the Summoner, the Proof, and the Imam) are exemplified by the four principles of the cosmic intellect (al-ʿaql al-kull), the cosmic soul (al-nafs al-kull), the Speaker (nāṭiq), and the Foundation (asās)—all illustrated through the four obligatory acts of purity.[^cite-6]

In order, the first three Sunna acts and then the four obligatory acts are performed, just as in "In the name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate" (bismillāh al-raḥmān al-raḥīm), the three letters of "bismillāh" precede the four letters of "Allāh." This means that the believer should first acknowledge the station of the Summoner—corresponding to washing the hands—then proceed through the Proof (ḥujja), corresponding to anointing the ears, and finally through the Imam, corresponding to water in the nose. This is because unless a believer knows and acknowledges these three ranks, he cannot follow the four principles: the Speaker (nāṭiq)—washing the face; the Foundation (asās)—washing the hands up to the elbows; the cosmic intellect (al-ʿaql al-kull)—wiping the head; and the cosmic soul (al-nafs al-kull)—wiping the feet.

By washing these seven organs and anointing them, a believer acknowledges that God is superior to all the ranks exemplified by these organs. He recognizes that he has no share in direct knowledge of God. All ranks are God's creation, established by Him so that they may convey His knowledge to the souls of the believers and perfect them in faith.

The sequence of the four obligatory acts indicates that one must first know the face (nāṭiq) and then its foundation (asās), for only through their knowledge can one confess the spiritual limits—the cosmic intellect and the cosmic soul.

The first element of ablution is the removal of impurities from one's body. After that comes the ritual cleansing (istinjāʾ), and then the stages of purification begin.

## Achieving Purity Through Clean Water

The existence of water has been sent down from the heavens to the earth since the beginning of creation. God says—and this is the meaning of His words:

> ⟪quran 23:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۢ بِقَدَرٍۢ فَأَسْكَنَّهُ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۖ وَإِنَّا عَلَىٰ ذَهَابٍ بِهِۦ لَقَدِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent down from the sky water in due measure, and We caused it to settle in the earth, and verily We are able to take it away.*
> — **Quran 23:18**

Water passes through both sacred and defiled places. In sacred lands flow rivers, springs, and wells of pure water; in corrupted places flow bitter seas. When water passes through defiled places—such as the salt sea—even though the original water is pure by nature, that place imparts its quality to the water. The water does not retain its original state but becomes bitter, salty, and spoiled. With such water, neither grain can grow, nor can land animals survive, nor can humans drink it. Instead of giving life, that water destroys all produce.

Purity is the gate to prayer, just as faith is the gate to Islam. Just as everything possesses both an inner meaning and an outward appearance, there lies an inner significance behind every outward act of purification. God says—and this is the meaning of His words:

> ⟪quran 25:48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَرْسَلَ ٱلرِّيَحَ بُشْرًۢا بَيْنَ يَدَىْ رَحْمَتِهِۦ ۚ وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ طَهُورًۭا ‎⟫
> *And it is He who sends the winds as heralds of glad tidings before His mercy, and We send down pure water from the sky.*
> — **Quran 25:48**

> ⟪quran 8:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِذْ يُغَشِّيكُمُ ٱلنُّعَاسَ أَمَنَةًۭ مِّنْهُ وَيُنَزِّلُ عَلَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ لِّيُطَهِّرَكُم بِهِۦ وَيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمْ رِجْزَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ وَلِيَرْبِطَ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِكُمْ وَيُثَبِّتَ بِهِ ٱلْأَقْدَامَ ‎⟫
> *When He covered you with slumber as a repose from Him, and sent down water from the sky upon you to cleanse you with it, and to remove from you the uncleanness of Satan, and to strengthen your hearts, and to steady your feet thereby.*
> — **Quran 8:11**

### The Esoteric Meaning of Purity

Purification is permissible only with pure water. If there is any adulteration in the water—even perfume or syrup—the water becomes unsuitable for ablution or bathing. Pure water removes filth; filth cannot be cleansed by filth or adulterated water.

In its esoteric sense, wuḍūʾ signifies a believing seeker's (mustajīb) pledge of allegiance to the Imam of the Age and acceptance of his invitation, knowing his rank and disassociating himself from his enemies. The inner reality of prayer is to enter into the Imam's call and remain in his invitation by connecting with the boundaries of the Imam of the Age and the saints. Prayer is not possible without ablution; ablution cannot be performed without intention; and intention cannot exist without invoking the name of God, the Merciful.

Pure water is a symbol of knowledge. Just as the impurity of the body is washed away by water, so the impurity of the self (nafs) is washed away by knowledge. Just as outward prayer is not permissible without ritual purification, it is not permissible to teach the esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl) and inner truths (ḥaqāʾiq) to anyone without a spiritual covenant. For taking a covenant is the essence of purity, and knowledge of truth is the invitation to prayer.[^cite-7]

| Purity | Prayer |
| --- | --- |
| The Imam of the Age | The science of exegesis and inner truths |

### Purification with Salt Water

In the absence of pure water, purification may be undertaken with bitter and salty water (the sea). The implication is that when a believer abandons the summons and takes up residence among his enemies, he is permitted to practice concealment (taqiyyah) and express his beliefs according to their false doctrines.

## Water, Buckets, and Vessels (Practical Rules)

When a worshipper wishes to perform ablution for prayer, he draws water from a river or a well. The water of the river is an analogy for the manifest knowledge (ẓāhir) of the Imam of Truth, which has not been altered by anyone and which flows to all creatures—both the elite and the common people. The water of the well is an analogy for the hidden knowledge (bāṭin) of the Imam, which is concealed and reserved only for those who have access to the well.

The bucket by which the seeker draws water from the well is an analogy for the knowledge of the Summoner (dāʿī), who reads the knowledge of the Proof (ḥujja) and teaches it to others. The vessel into which water is poured for ablution is an analogy for the Universal Summoner (al-dāʿī al-muṭlaq). This Summoner imparts knowledge to those who possess absolute authority, to the initiates (muʿallimūn), and to the instructors (maqāṣir), whose analogies are the right and left hands from which water is taken from the vessel.

Each hand has five fingers, which are analogies for the five daily prayers. The five fingers of the right hand represent the five prayer times, and the five fingers of the left hand represent the five pillars of prayer. With these two hands, urine and feces are cleansed. Water is poured with the right hand while impurities are cleansed with the left hand. That is, the Initiate (muʿallim), standing opposite the right hand, imparts knowledge to the Instructor (maqāṣir) by which the latter removes the impurities of polytheism, disbelief, and hypocrisy from believers entering the faith.[^cite-8]

| Bucket | The knowledge of the Summoner who reads from the Proof |
| Vessel | The Universal Summoner |
| Both hands | The Initiate and the Instructor |
| The five fingers of the right hand | The five prayer times |
| The five fingers of the left hand | The five requirements of prayer |

## Exegesis of the Verse of Purification (Q33:33) — 'God Wishes to Remove All Impurity from You, O People of the House'

The quality of water is that it is pure in itself and purifies others as well. Pure water requires no further purification. God purifies bodily impurity with water and removes mental impurity (ignorance) through knowledge. Purification from the impurity of ignorance through the sciences of the People of the House (Ahl al-Bayt) is indeed true purification. God says—and this is the meaning of His words concerning the Verse of Purification (āyat al-taṭhīr):

> ⟪ar:إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ اللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ الرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ الْبَيْتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمْ تَطْهِيرًا⟫
> *God desires only to remove uncleanness far from you, O People of the House, and to purify you with a thorough purification.*

The need for purity arises only where impurity exists. Were the People of the House impure before this verse was revealed? God forbid!

When this verse was revealed, the Messenger of God took our master ʿAlī, the Lady Fāṭima, Imam Ḥasan, and Imam Ḥusayn under his cloak and declared: "These are my People of the House."[^cite-9] The Mother of the Believers ʿUmm Salama was present there and requested: "O Messenger of God, include me within your cloak as well." The Messenger of God replied: ⟪ar-quote:يَا سَلَمَىٰ أَنتِ عَلَى الْخَيْرِ⟫ "O Salma, you are upon goodness, but I cannot add anyone else to this cloak except these five people." When the verse was revealed, all five of them thanked the Messenger of God for granting them this blessing. The Messenger of God replied: "It is not for my sake alone! Rather, all five of you possess the ability and glory that God has revealed to you all!"

The Messenger of God distinguished himself and ʿAlī, Fāṭima, Ḥasan, and Ḥusayn from all other humankind by gathering these five under his cloak. In this way he made manifest that the People of the House were the only five beneath his cloak. To be under the Messenger's cloak is to clarify one's unique station, one's exaltation, and one's leadership among these five. He thereby pointed out that these five beings are pure in their essence and no impurity can ever touch them—just as the outer layer of a cloak protects a person from filth. If anything unclean exists, it is outside the cloak!

In this verse, the word "impurity" (rijz) refers to the believers' false doctrines, concepts, doubts, and polytheism regarding the knowledge of these personalities and their successors—the Pure Imams. These flawed convictions of the believers about the status of the People of the House constitute for them a state of filth and impurity. In this verse, God says that He has resolved to correct the false beliefs of the believers through the knowledge of esoteric exegesis and inner truths (which dwell in the mansion of pure water), and to grant them true knowledge of the Imams so that they may recognize them as they truly are.[^cite-10]

### The Inner Truth Regarding the Purity of the Imams

None of the Imams require physical cleansing, for they are not susceptible to impurity. However, they performed ablution to cleanse their channels of knowledge (ḥijāb) of any defects that may have entered through interpretation. Their purity was indeed the cleansing of their vessels of knowledge. The Messenger of God possessed such sublime virtues that he once erred in one of his knowledge-vessels, yet the People of the House—peace be upon them all—are preserved from error.

## The Heavens, the Earth, and Pure Water (Their Cosmological Relation)

The sky is the source from which water flows. It is the special quality of water that it is pure in its essence and always descends from the sky (the higher) to the earth (the lower). The earth absorbs this water and produces vegetation for all creatures—the source of their life and existence. Like water, knowledge is always transferred from the higher to the lower. God says—and this is the meaning of His words:

> ⟪ar:كَمَثَلِ غَيْثٍ أَعْجَبَ الْكُفَّارَ نَبَاتُهُ⟫
> *The likeness of the rain-water which God sends down...*

Heaven refers to the Prophets of this age—specifically the Most Chosen One, Muḥammad, the Messenger of God—peace and blessings of God be upon him. Their earth is their Successors—the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him. "Water" refers to the knowledge that the recipient acquires, and "purity" refers to freedom from all manner of doubts and misgivings. In another cycle of the hierarchy, heaven refers to the Benefactor (mufīd) who gives knowledge, while earth refers to the Beneficiary (mustafīd) who receives it. Moving further into the circles of esoteric exegesis, the Imam stands in the place of heaven and the Proof stands in the place of earth.

That is, the Messenger of God bestowed all his knowledge upon the earth—upon ʿAlī. ʿAlī received this profound and weighty knowledge from the Prophet and made it accessible, rendering it useful for the souls of all human beings and enabling the life and flourishing of their souls.

| Heaven | Earth | Pure water |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Speaker (nāṭiq) | The Guardian (waṣī) | Knowledge without doubt |
| The Imam | The Proof (ḥujja) | The Imam's knowledge |
| The Benefactor | The Beneficiary | Knowledge in its completeness |
| The Giver | The Receiver | The transmitted teaching |

There are two types of earth that absorb water: good and bad. If water falls upon good earth, it produces vegetation beneficial to animals and humans, who drink from it and live. But if the same water falls upon bad earth (such as the salt sea), its nature transforms, and it becomes useless to all.[^cite-11]

### Pure Earth

The inner earth is an analogy for the Imams, who are the Successors of the Prophet and his descendants, standing by his command and serving the call to truth. It is in this sense that the Speaker—peace be upon him—said: ⟪ar-quote:اَلاَرضُ أُمُّكُم وَهِىَ تُحْسِنُ إِلَيْكُم⟫ "The earth is your mother, and she does good to you," thereby pointing to his Successor, the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib. When ʿAlī received this knowledge from the Prophet, he remained pure and unsullied, becoming a means of growth and the perfection of the believers' souls.

### Corrupted Earth

In the same way, bad earth is an analogy for the corrupt among the Community and the false claimants to the Imamate, and for all who will come in their place until the Day of Resurrection. Regarding this corrupted earth, our master al-Muʾayyad in God—may God elevate his sanctity—has declared: "O people! O people dwelling in the sleep of heedlessness, who dwell in the lap of a mother about to devour her own children, why do you sit peacefully in a house about to collapse!"

When the pure knowledge (sweet water) reached the hostile ones, they altered and corrupted this knowledge. The Zodiac is an analogy for the salty water of the sea, and its followers are creatures living within it, whose very survival depends upon this salt water alone. Should they be deprived of it, they perish. That is, those hostile ones altered the knowledge of the Messenger of God, and their followers then acquired this false knowledge from their predecessors because their souls could comprehend only this corrupted teaching.[^cite-12]

## Clouds and Rain (in the Ṭahāra Discussion)

### The Cloud

There are two kinds of water that clouds shed from the sky. Some clouds only thunder but do not release their water; others are filled with water and rain upon the earth. The cloud is an analogy for a Summoner. Those clouds that rain are like Summoners of the Pen (duʿāt al-qalam)—those who write books and disseminate knowledge with the support of their Imam. Those clouds that only thunder are like Summoners of the Sword (duʿāt al-sayf)—those who do not write books but instead struggle with the sword. The purpose of both is to purify the souls and excesses of the believers.[^cite-13]

| Raining clouds | Thundering clouds |
| --- | --- |
| Summoners of the Pen | Summoners of the Sword |

### Evaporation and the Circulation of Knowledge

At midday, when the heat of the sun reaches its peak, it draws water from the earth in the form of evaporation through its rays, then transforms this vapor into pure water, and returns it to the earth as rain through the clouds. The sun is an analogy for the Imam of the Age, peace be upon him. The earth is an analogy for the knowledge of the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him. The vapors are the subtle, eloquent, and wise utterances hidden within the Nahj al-Balāgha (the Path of Eloquence). These words possess such eloquence that human comprehension cannot fully grasp and understand them. Therefore, when the Imam of the Age reaches the zenith of his knowledge, he draws wisdom from these eloquent words of the Prophet and transforms this wisdom into accessible knowledge, broadcasting it to the world through his prayers so that the souls of believers may benefit from it.[^cite-14]

## The Difference Between Washing (Ghasl) and Wiping (Masḥ)

It is transmitted in the ḥadīth that in the early days of Islam, the Messenger of God used to wash his head and feet. However, after the Night Journey (al-isrāʾ), when God revealed to him the verse:

> ⟪quran 4:6⟫
> ⟪ar:يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى الصَّلَاةِ فَاغْسِلُوا وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى الْمَرَافِقِ وَامْسَحُوا بِرُءُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى الْكَعْبَيْنِ⟫
> *O you who believe! When you stand up for prayer, wash your faces and your hands up to your elbows, and wipe your heads and your feet up to the ankles.*

He changed his practice at God's command and began to wipe his head and feet instead of washing them. His Successor, understanding his gesture and becoming aware of the Prophet's intention, began to wipe his head and feet. But his adversaries, not cognizant of this subtle meaning, remained fixed upon the earlier practice.[^cite-15]

The reason for this change lies in the Prophet's spiritual ascent. In the early stages of his education, he had not yet attained true knowledge of the hierarchy of spiritual limits (al-ʿaql al-kull and al-nafs al-kull). He perceived them as material and tangible. For this reason, he washed his head and feet like the organs exemplifying the bodily limits. When his Prophethood reached completion and he ascended to heaven as a spiritual ascension, the Holy Prophet recognized these spiritual limits as they truly deserved to be recognized. Thereafter, at every ablution, he would wipe his head and feet alone. By doing so, he demonstrated to the Community that these two ranks are not material but spiritual in nature—their hierarchy cannot be known through sensory perception, but only their reality can be affirmed. This occurrence clarifies that the Messenger of God gradually attained stages of knowledge, as God says—and this is the meaning of His words:

> ⟪ar:وَرَفَعْنَا لَكَ ذِكْرَكَ⟫
> *And We have raised your remembrance for you.*

Wiping (masḥ) signifies the acknowledgment of being; washing (ghasl) signifies knowledge of station, obedience, and consent. The parts that believers are commanded to wash in ablution—such as the face and hands—are analogies for material limits (the Speaker and the Foundation). Those commanded to be wiped—such as the head and feet—are analogies for spiritual limits (the cosmic intellect and the cosmic soul). That is, one should fully know and obey the material limits, yet acknowledge the existence of the spiritual limits, for humans do not possess the capacity to comprehend these spiritual realities. Some limits require complete understanding, while for others partial acknowledgment suffices.[^cite-16]

| Wiping | Washing |
| --- | --- |
| Spiritual limits | Material limits |
| The cosmic intellect, the cosmic soul | The Speaker, the Foundation |
| Acknowledgment of existence | Knowledge with acknowledgment |
| Partial knowledge | Complete knowledge |

## Wiping Over the Turban and Socks

The Imams are unanimous in their ruling that it is not permissible to wipe over the socks during ablution. God has commanded the wiping of the feet themselves, not the wiping of socks. Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, peace be upon him, stated: "Concealment (taqiyyah) is my religion and the religion of my fathers." Yet he declared that concealment is not permissible in three matters:

| 1 | In the consumption of intoxicants |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | In wiping over socks |
| 3 | In abandoning the recitation of the Bismillāh in prayer |

The Imams—peace be upon them—declared: "It is not permissible for a person who believes that wiping over socks is permissible, for such a person will pray without proper ablution, and it is as though he does not pray at all."

At the beginning of his Prophethood, the Prophet used to wipe his feet and head while wearing socks and turbans, because the reality of the cosmic intellect and cosmic soul had not yet been revealed to him. When the Archangel Gabriel brought him knowledge of the cosmic intellect and cosmic soul, and he had attained understanding of the hierarchy of these two spiritual limits, he began to wipe bare head and bare feet. His Successor, having grasped this reality and accepted his instruction, ceased wiping over turban and socks.[^cite-17]

## Tayammum — Dry Ablution with Earth

God says—and this is the meaning of His words:

> ⟪quran 5:6⟫
> ⟪ar:وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَاءَ أَحَدٌ مِّنكُم مِّنَ الْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَامَسْتُمُ النِّسَاءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا مَاءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا⟫
> *If you are ill or on a journey, or if any of you comes from the toilet, or you have touched women, and you find no water, then take to pure earth.*

In this verse, three conditions of impurity are mentioned: the sick, the traveler, and the one who has returned from the toilet. In their inner sense, these are three people in need of purification. The **sick one** is a believer whose religious concepts have become corrupted (disease of belief). The **traveler** is a person far removed from the beneficial Presence of the Imam. The **one who has come from the toilet** is a person whose thoughts have become doubtful. In this verse, the word ⟪ar:غائط⟫ (ghāʾiṭ, toilet) refers in the lexicon to a place on sloping earth where one can attend to natural needs while remaining hidden and unseen by others. The esoteric meaning implies that the purification of the believer through inner knowledge should take place independently, away from the knowledge of others—they should not know that the believer is acquiring spiritual knowledge.[^cite-18]

| Sick | Traveler | One from the toilet |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corruption in religious concepts | Distance from the beneficial Imam | Doubt in thought |

The phrase ⟪ar:أَوْ لَامَسْتُمُ النِّسَاءَ⟫ refers to intimate contact with women, and esoterically it means that knowledge has been transmitted between the man (the Benefactor) and the woman (the Beneficiary), and therefore both must purify themselves. This transmission can be both lawful and unlawful. If the Beneficiary acquires knowledge from someone who has been permitted to teach (as if married to him), then this transmission is lawful; otherwise it is unlawful.

If the believer is one of the four types mentioned above, he is obligated to purify his knowledge. Yet if he does not possess pure water (the knowledge of truth), he may purify himself through earth. The analogy of earth is the believer himself, as is stated in the Qur'ān:

> ⟪quran 18:37⟫
> ⟪ar:وَمِن آيَاتِهِ خَلْقُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ وَمَا بَثَّ فِيهِمَا مِن دَابَّةٍۚ⟫
> *Among His signs is the creation of the heavens and the earth.*

That is, on the Day of Resurrection, the disbeliever will say: "If only I had believed!"

In other words, if the believer does not find one of the highest ranks of the summons, then he may gain knowledge from a fellow believer close to him whose faith and belief are strong. Even one of the higher ranks of the hierarchy, when he meets an elder who is analogous to water, gains knowledge from that elder.

Tayammum is performed on the face and both hands alone. The esoteric interpretation is that a believer has two stations of faith: first, to distance himself from ignorance, and second, to enter the faith by taking a spiritual covenant. The Initiates (muʿallimūn) and Instructors (maqāṣir) elevate him to two ranks—analogous to both palms—and both make him acquainted with the rank of the Benefactor and the Speaker of the time. It is not permissible to perform tayammum—that is, to acquire knowledge from a common believer—in the presence of a Benefactor.[^cite-19]
