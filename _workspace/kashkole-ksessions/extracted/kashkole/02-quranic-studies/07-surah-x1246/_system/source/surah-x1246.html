Linguistic meaning of SURAH

So Allah Himself called these collection of ayats... Surah... Now linguistically speaking... what is the meaning of the word Surah?  The word Surah comes from the root word "soor" which means a fort... or the walls around a city... If you visualize... in the old days... they did not have signs and landmarks on the borders that indicated that you are entering a new city... So what they would do is put up walls around a city so that they could control who can enter and leave the cities... The arabs called these outer walls around the city... صُور ... So it is... as if every surah has been fortified... every surah has been demarkated.. like the boundaries of a city... With these defined boundaries... you know what is INSIDE the surah... and you know what is OUTSIDE the surah... just like the walls of a city... you know what lies inside the city and you know what lies outside the city...

Now if we examine a city... inside each city..... there is always a lot going on... There is commerce taking place... there are residential areas... there are military bases..... all these different things are happening within a city... but then even though they are all different things... they some how all connect together to make up one city...... Similarly there is a lot going on inside a surah... there are all these different and disparate passages within one surah... but they all somehow connect together to form one single context... and one particular message.... So even though the ayats within a surah seem disconnected... they all come together amazingly.... in meaning... to deliver a single unified message...

Then we see there is also a connection between different independent cities... When you leave one city.....  you automatically enter into another city... there is a common road or pathway that connects the last portion of one city to the beginning portion of the next city. Similary there is cohesion between the last thing you will read about in the previous surah.. And the first thing you will read about in the next surah.... so there is a link between the conclusion of one surah and the introduction to the next surah... So the last thing that is being said in Surah AlKausar is connected to the first thing in Surah AlKafiroon... and the last thing said in Surah AlKafiroon is connected to the first thing in Surah AlNasar and so on.... So even though it may not seem apparent.... but these are not randomly arranged surahs... There is a very clear and precise flow and transition that takes place from one surah to the next... SubhanAllah!

Then the boundary walls of a city always follow an irregular circular pattern... Similarly the end of a surah.... is connected to the beginning of the surah... and this is a very consitent feature of the Quran.... which we saw in the symmetrical dimension of Quran. Every surah... what it begins with...  is connected to what it ends with... There is a strong cohesion between the beginning of the surah (the مفاتیح الصور) and the end of the surah.. (the خواتیم الصور)...

The Quran is partitioned into 114 distinct surahs with each surah having its own set of ayaats... meaning ayaats that are specific ONLY to that particular surah... and then it also has ayaats, that are repeated across different surahs.. This is also similar to when you visit a different city... you see places, landmarks and monuments that are specific to that city... but then you also see places that remind you of places in your own city.... So just like cities have their own unique places ... but then they also have certain commonalities with other cities... similarly certain ayaats are unique within a surah.... but then certain ayats are also repeated  in various other surahs...

So even from a linguistic point of view... the word صورۃ dervied from the outer walls of a city صُور  ... they perfectly describes these collections of ayaats... We know the word ayah means a miraculous sign... you put them both together ... you put صورۃ and آیات together... and we can now think of a surah as boundary for a collection of miraculous signs... SubhanAllah!
