## Section 5684

Why do I see religious elders, who talk about the ideal character of Islam, acting in a way contradictory to the teachings of Islam.... Shouldn't they be the ones leading by example?

If these knowledgable people cannot act on these teachings ... what chance do I have?

Do you guys remember that conversation I had in one of my sessions... where I compared the rules of Islam to the rules of a video game? Do you remember that? I showed you that Islam is similar to an online multi player, role playing video game.... We're all players in the game. We have a point scoring system called sawaab, and we have a point losing system called "gunaah".....

In a game there are always certain potions, certain swords, certain shields.... various things that we collect at every level that give us huge rewards. These acquisitions, help us move to the next level... In Islam we collect those points with actions like namaaz, roza, hajj, zakaat...

Similarly in a game there are certain poisions, certain traps, some bad choices that make us lose points... even get us killed and we have to start all over again... In Islam those are certain acts like lying, cheating, gossip, slander, emotional and verbal abuse that make us lose points... and then are are also those major sins like smoking, drinking alcohol, taking drugs, adultry, murder that can causes severe damage......

Every game has a final level, an ultimate purpose, the ultimate success.... where you achieve the final purpose of that game.. You get to meet the BIG BOSS and win the final prize.... In Islam that BIG BOSS is Allah and that final eternal prize is Jannah... That's what this game is being played for.....

So to exteremely oversimplify..... we are playing this game of Islam... We are all players in this game... and we are playing along... or against some other players at the same time..... each person is playing the same game, but at various different levels.... Now every game has a rule book right? Something that describes the rules of the games... And these rules are a list of DOs and DONTs, aren't they? Do this, and this is what you get as your reward... Don't do this, or this bad thing will happen to you....

Now let's say you just bough this latest game from the store..... When you unwrap it, you see that the rule book for this game is printed in Chinese... you can't read chinese... so now how do you play the game? Well one way is that you wing it.... you start playing and you figure it out as you go...  but how far do you think that approach will take you?

The second way is that you find somebody else who knows the same game.... Let's say I am that person... You find out that I have atually read the rule book.... I have mastered it...... I knows all the rules... what to do, what not to do... what things to collect, what to avoid... OK? So now you're watching me play the game... and based on how you see me playing... you assume what? That this is how the game is supposed to be played!

For our discussion, do you yourself know the rules of the game? No! But do you believe I know the rules of the game? Yes! So now you see me playing the game... you see what I'm doing and you see what I'm avoiding... and you say oh ok.... so these must be the rules of the game... You just naturally assume that... Because you don't know the rules of the game, and you believe I know the rules .... therefore whatever you see me doing, you assume what? This must be the rules of the game....

Now what happens when you see me doing something wrong? Something I'm not supposed to do...... Who do you blame? You can't blame me because you believe I know the rules... So then if I know the rules, and I'm still doing this... where must the fault lie? In the game itself!!!   You understand?

So you end up saying... this game is stupid! Doesn't make sense! There is a flaw in its design...

Quran is the rule book of Islam... Its the book of guidance that has all the DOs and DONT's... Do we understand the Quran? Have we studied it the way we should have? No! So what do we do? We start following the people who have read and understood it... correct? But when we see them doing something unIslamic... who do we generally end up blaming? Islam! So we really need to begin by understanding this fundamental concept... the difference between the game and the player... Islam is the game... Muslims, momins, mullas manaseebs, they're all the players... If you see a fault in the player... the first rule is don't blame the game!

Well... the first thing is that each of us have to play this game individually...
