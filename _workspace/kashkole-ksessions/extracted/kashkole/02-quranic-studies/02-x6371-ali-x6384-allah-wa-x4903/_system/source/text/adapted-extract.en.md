This opening invocation, recited by the dāʿī al-munṣab (the appointed Summoner), establishes the devotional frame for all that follows.[^cite-1] The formula encompasses praise of the Divine, intercession through the Prophet Muḥammad and his family, and affirmation of the Imamate—a structure that mirrors the essential architecture of Ismaili gnosis.

## Praise to the Lord of the Heavens and the Earth

⟪ar:اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ الَّذِي لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ؛ يُحْيِي وَيُمِيتُ. وَهُوَ الْأَوَّلُ وَالْآخِرُ وَالظَّاهِرُ وَالْبَاطِنُ، وَهُوَ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ.⟫

All praise belongs to Allah, unto whom belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth; He gives life and causes death. He is the First and the Last, the Manifest and the Hidden—and He has knowledge of all things.[^cite-2]

⟪ar:اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ مُوجِدِ الْوُجُودِ مِنْ عَدَمٍ غَيْرِ مَوْجُودٍ، وَمُقِيمِ مَرَاتِبِ الْحُدُودِ، تَفَضُّلًا وَإِنْعَامًا عَلَى مَنْ دَنَا.⟫

Praise be to Allah, the Originator of existence from that which was not—from non-being itself—and the Establisher of the grades of the ḥudūd (the bounds or hierarchical stations of being), through grace and beneficence upon those who draw near.

⟪ar:اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ الَّذِي أَنْزَلَ الْفُرْقَانَ عَلَى عَبْدِهِ لِيَكُونَ لِلْعَالَمِينَ نَذِيرًا.⟫

Praise be to Allah, who sent down the Distinguisher (al-Furqān) upon His servant, that it might be a warning unto all the worlds.[^cite-3]

⟪ar:اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ الَّذِي هَدَانَا لِهَٰذَا، وَمَا كُنَّا لِنَهْتَدِيَ لَوْلَا أَنْ هَدَانَا اللّٰهُ.⟫

Praise be to Allah, who has guided us unto this—and we would never have been guided had Allah not guided us.

The opening testimony establishes the metaphysical foundation upon which all subsequent instruction rests: divine sovereignty, the cascade of emanation from absolute non-being into being, and the centrality of revealed guidance. This resonates throughout the corpus of taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis) as the unshakeable premise of gnosis.

⟪ar:وَنَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، وَنَشْهَدُ أَنَّ سَيِّدَنَا مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ، وَنَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مَوْلَانَا عَلِيًّا وَصِيُّهُ وَوَزِيرُهُ.⟫

And we testify that there is no deity save Allah alone, with no partner unto Him; and we testify that our master Muḥammad is His servant and His messenger; and we testify that our lord ʿAlī is his executor (waṣī) and his minister (wazīr).

⟪ar:وَالصَّلَاةُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى خَيْرِ الْخَلْقِ وَنُورِ الْعَرْشِ، أَفْضَلِ الْأَنْبِيَاءِ وَالْمُرْسَلِينَ، وَأَشْرَفِ الْمُوَحِّدِينَ، حَبِيبِنَا وَسَيِّدِنَا وَسَنَدِنَا وَشَفِيعُنَا أَبِي الْقَاسِمِ مُحَمَّدٍ، وَعَلَى أَخِيهِ وَصِيِّهِ وَوَزِيرِهِ، أَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ.⟫

Blessings and peace upon the best of creation and the light of the Throne, the noblest of prophets and messengers and the most exalted of those who profess the unity of God—our beloved, our master, our support, and our intercessor Abū al-Qāsim Muḥammad, and upon his brother, his executor, and his minister, the Commander of the Believers.

⟪ar:الْأَنْزَعِ الْبَطِينِ، وَيَعْسُوبِ الدِّينِ، غَالِبٍ عَلَى كُلِّ غَالِبٍ، مَطْلُوبِ كُلِّ طَالِبٍ، مَوْلَانَا عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ، وَعَلَى الْأَئِمَّةِ مِنْ ذُرِّيَّتِهِمُ الطَّيِّبِينَ الطَّاهِرِينَ، اَلْخُلَفَاءِ الرَّاشِدِينَ، آلِ طه وَيٰس.⟫

ʿAlī the Vast in Knowledge, the leader of the faith, victor over all victors, the object of all seeking—our lord ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, and upon the Imams from his progeny, the pure and the virtuous, the rightly-guided successors, the family of Ṭā-Hā and Yā-Sīn.

The invocation of the Prophet's family culminates in the declaration of the Imamate itself—not as a mere political office, but as the channel through which divine guidance flows perpetually to humanity. The ʿAlīs of each age stand as the living embodiment of waṣīya (the covenant) and the custodians of the inner meanings.

⟪ar:اَللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ فِي كُلِّ وَقْتٍ وَكُلِّ أَوَانٍ وَعَلَى كُلِّ حَالٍ؛ عَدَدَ مَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَى مَنْ صَلَّيْتَ عَلَيْهِ؛ وَأَضْعَافَ ذَلِكَ كُلِّهِ بِالْأَضْعَافِ الَّتِي لَا يُحْصِيهَا غَيْرُكَ.⟫

O Allah, send blessings upon Muḥammad and upon the family of Muḥammad at every moment and every hour and in every state—a number equal to all those upon whom Thou hast sent blessings, and manifold beyond that, in a measure that only Thou canst reckon.

⟪ar:اَللّٰهُمَّ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَا إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَا، إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ الْعَلِيمُ الْحَكِيمُ. اَللّٰهُمَّ عَلِّمْنَا بِمَا يَنْفَعُنَا، وَانْفَعْنَا بِمَا عَلَّمْتَنَا.⟫

O Allah, we possess no knowledge save what Thou hast taught us; verily, Thou art the All-Knowing, the All-Wise. O Allah, teach us what benefits us, and benefit us through what Thou hast taught us.

⟪ar:اَللَّهُمَّ إِنَّا نَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ عِلْمٍ لَا يَنْفَعُ، وَمِنْ قَلْبٍ لَا يَخْشَعُ، وَمِنْ عَيْنٍ لَا تَدْمَعُ، وَمِنْ نَفْسٍ لَا تَشْبَعُ، وَمِنْ دَعْوَةٍ لَا يُسْتَجَابُ لَهَا، وَمِنْ مُنْكَرَاتِ الْأَخْلَاقِ وَالْأَعْمَالِ وَالْأَهْوَاءِ.⟫

O Allah, we seek refuge in Thee from knowledge that profits not, from a heart that fears Thee not, from eyes that weep not, from a soul that never finds satiation, from a supplication that goes unanswered, and from all that is abominable in character, deed, and desire.

The petition for protection against knowledge-without-benefit (ʿilm lā yanfaʿ) strikes at a theme central to Ismaili epistemology: that mere intellectual accumulation divorced from spiritual transformation becomes a veil rather than a lamp. True knowledge must bear fruit in the transformation of the soul.

⟪ar:فَأَمَّا بَعْدُ... يَقُولُ سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَى فِي كِتَابِهِ الْكَرِيمِ، بَعْدَ أَنْ نَقُولَ⟫

As for what follows, the Most Exalted speaks in His noble Book, after we recite:

⟪ar:("أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ." رَبِّ اشْرَحْ لِي صَدْرِي، وَيَسِّرْ لِي أَمْرِي، وَاحْلُلْ عُقْدَةً مِنْ لِسَانِي، يَفْقَهُوا قَوْلِي. رَبِّ زِدْنِي عِلْمًا. رَبِّ يَسِّرْ وَلَا تُعَسِّرْ وَتَمِّمْ بِالْخَيْرِ. اَللَّهُمَّ ثَبِّتْنَا عِنْدَ الْمَوْتِ بِلَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَاللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْنَا مِنَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ، وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالْحَقِّ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالصَّبْرِ. آمِينَ يَا رَبَّ الْعَالَمِينَ. اَلسَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ.)⟫

(I seek refuge in Allah from the accursed Satan. O Lord, expand my breast for me, and ease my task, and loosen the knot upon my tongue, that they may understand my speech. O Lord, increase me in knowledge. O Lord, make it easy and do not make it hard; complete it with good. O Allah, keep us steadfast at the moment of death with *there is no deity save Allah*, and O Allah, make us among those who have believed and done righteous deeds, and counseled one another to truth and counseled one another to patience. Amen, O Lord of all the worlds. Peace be upon you, and the mercy of Allah, and His blessings.)

## The Praise of Allah Exceeds All Utterance

⟪ar:الحَمْدُ للهِ الَّذِي لَا يَبْلُغُ مِدْحَتَهُ الْقَائِلُونَ، وَلَا يُحْصِي نَعْمَاءَهُ الْعَادُّونَ، وَلَا يُؤَدِّي حَقَّهُ الْمُجْتَهِدُونَ، الَّذِي لَا يُدْرِكُهُ بُعْدُ الْهِمَمِ، وَلَا يَنَالُهُ غَوْصُ الْفِطَنِ.⟫

Praise be to Allah, whose praise the speakers cannot reach, whose bounties the counters cannot enumerate, and whose right the striving cannot fulfill—He whom the soaring of aspirations cannot attain, nor the plunging of keen intellects apprehend.[^cite-4]

⟪ar:الَّذِي لَيْسَ لِصِفَتِهِ حَدٌّ مَحْدُودٌ، وَلَا نَعْتٌ مَوْجُودٌ، وَلَا وَقْتٌ مَعْدُودٌ، وَلَا أَجَلٌ مَمْدُودٌ.⟫

He whose attributes have no circumscribed boundary, whose description admits no existing form, whose existence is not measured by time, nor limited by duration.

⟪ar:فَطَرَ الْخَلَائِقَ بِقُدْرَتِهِ، وَنَشَرَ الرِّيَاحَ بِرَحْمَتِهِ، وَوَتَّدَ بِالصُّخُورِ مَيَدَانَ أَرْضِهِ.⟫

He created all creatures by His power, spread forth the winds by His mercy, and anchored the plains of His earth with mountains.

This section articulates the doctrine of divine transcendence (tanzīh) in language familiar from the theological writings of the Fatimid Ismaili masters. The insistence that Allah transcends predication, that even the noblest human faculties remain incapable of grasping His reality, echoes the via negativa central to works such as al-Sijistānī's *Kitāb al-Yanābīʿ*.[^cite-5] Yet paradoxically, this very transcendence establishes the necessity of guidance—of the dāʿwa (the summons)—for only through the mediation of the speaking Divine (al-Munbaʿith) can the human intellect approach understanding.

⟪ar:وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى سَيِّدِ الْأَنْبِيَاءِ وَالْمُرْسَلِينَ، خَاتَمِ النَّبِيِّينَ، سَيِّدِنَا الْمُمَجَّدِ، الْبَشِيرِ الْمُصَدَّقِ، النَّذِيرِ الْمُؤَيَّدِ، الْمُصْطَفَى الْأَمْجَدِ، مَحْمُودٌ الْأَحْمَدِ، أَبِي الْقَاسِمِ مُحَمَّدٍ.⟫

Blessings and peace upon the master of prophets and messengers, the seal of the prophets, our honored master, the truthful bearer of glad tidings, the confirmed warner, the chosen most noble, the praised one among the praised, Abū al-Qāsim Muḥammad.

⟪ar:وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى وَصِيِّهِ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ، أَشْرَفِ الْوَصِيِّينَ وَأَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ.⟫

And blessings and peace upon his executor ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, the noblest of executors and Commander of the Believers.

⟪ar:وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى سَيِّدِنَا الْإِمَامِ الطَّيِّبِ صَاحِبِ الدَّعْوَةِ النَّبَوِيَّةِ، وَصَوْلَةِ الْحَيْدَرِيَّةِ، وَالْعِصْمَةِ الْفَاطِمِيَّةِ، وَالشَّجَاعَةِ الْحُسَيْنِيَّةِ، وَالْعُلُومِ الْبَاقِرِيَّةِ، وَالْمَعَاقِلِ الْجَعْفَرِيَّةِ، وَالْحِكْمَةِ الْإِسْمَاعِيلِيَّةِ، وَالْجُودِ الْمُحَمَّدِيَّةِ.⟫

And blessings and peace upon our master the Imam al-Ṭayyib, bearer of the prophetic summons (daʿwa nabawiyya), possessor of Haydarī prowess, Fāṭimī infallibility (ʿiṣma), Ḥusaynī courage, Bāqirī sciences, Jaʿfarī sagacities, Ismāʿīlī wisdom, and Muḥammadan generosity.[^cite-6]

This enumeration of the Imam's qualities through the genealogy of Ahl al-Bayt (the People of the House) represents a sophisticated consolidation of Ismaili theology. Each divine attribute clusters around a particular Imam: the Fāṭimī ʿiṣma (the protected infallibility of Fāṭima and her line), the Ḥusaynī courage (echoing Imām Ḥusayn's sacrifice), the Bāqirī science (Imām Muḥammad al-Bāqir's opening of knowledge)—together forming a unified ladder of ascent toward the standing Imam, who embodies all these perfections.

⟪ar:فَصَلَّى اللهُ عَلَيْهِمْ وَعَلَى آلِهِمْ وَسَلَّمَ تَسْلِيمًا كَثِيرًا كَثِيرًا.⟫

May Allah send abundant and ever-increasing blessings and peace upon them and their progeny.

## Praise for the Creator Who Draws Forth Existence from Non-Existence

⟪ar:الحمدُ للہِ خَالقِ الوُجودِ مِنَ العَدَم، وَ جَاعِلِ النُّورِ مِنَ الظُّلَم، وَ مُخرِجِ الصَّبرِ مِنَ الاَلَم، وَ مُلکِ التَّوبَۃِ عَلی النَّدَم⟫

All praise belongs to Allah, the Creator who drew forth existence from non-existence, who brought forth light from darkness, who extracted patience from pain, and who granted the dominion of repentance over remorse.

The metaphysical sequence here—being-from-non-being, light-from-darkness, patience-from-suffering, forgiveness-from-regret—mirrors the cosmological emanations (ṭufūḥ) foundational to Ismaili gnosis. Just as the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High) draws existence from the abyss of ʿadam (non-existence), so too does the soul ascend through trials that transform it from ignorance to gnosis, from despair to steadfastness.

⟪ar:فَنَشکُرُہُ عَلیَ المصَائِبِ کما نَشکُرُہُ عَلی النِّعَم⟫

Thus we are grateful to Him for trials even as we are grateful for blessings.

⟪ar:وَ نُصَلِّی عَلی رَسُولِہِ الاکرَم، لِشَّرَفِ الاَشَم، وَ النُّورِ الاتَم، وَالکِتاَبِ المُحکَم، وَ کمالِ النَّبِیِّنَ وَ الخَاتَم⟫

And we send blessings upon His noble messenger, for his towering honor and his consummate light, for the decisive Book, and for his perfection as the seal of all prophets.

⟪ar:سَیِّدِی وَلَدِ آدَم ۔ اَلَّذِی بَشَّرَ بِہِ عِیسیَ ابْنُ مَریَم، وَ دَعَا لِبِعثَتِہِ اِبرَاھِیمُ عَلیہِ السَّلام، حِینَ کاَنَ یَرفَعُ قَواَعِدَ بَیتِ اللہِ المحَّرم⟫

The finest of Adam's children—he of whom Jesus, son of Mary, gave glad tidings, and for whose mission Abraham, upon him be peace, supplicated while he raised the foundations of Allah's sacred House.

The invocation of the Prophets' prayers and prophecies concerning Muḥammad establishes the continuity of the prophetic mission across the ages. This addresses the question of kamāl awwal (primary perfection)—the transcendent standing of prophethood itself, which the Imam preserves in its esoteric dimension even as the seal of prophecy closes its exoteric manifestation.

⟪ar:فَصَلَّ اللہ علیہ و آلہ و سلم ، و علی أَتباَعِہِ خَیرِ الاُمَم، الَّذِینَ بَارکَ اللہُ بِہِم کاَفَّۃَ النَّاس العَرَبَ مِنھُم وَالعَجَم⟫

May Allah send blessings upon him and his family, and upon his followers—the best of communities—through whom Allah has blessed all of mankind, Arab and non-Arab alike.

⟪ar:و صل اللہ علی أمیر المؤمنین، الانزع البطین، وَ قائد الغر المحجلین، غالب علی کل غالب، مطلوب کل طالب⟫

And may Allah send blessings upon the Commander of the Believers, vast in knowledge and deep in wisdom, the leader of the radiant ones, victor over all victors, the ultimate aim of all who seek.

---

⟪ar:الحمد للہ ، الَّذِی أَنزَلَ عَلی عَبدِہِ الکتابَ وَلَم یَجعَل لَّہُ عِوَجَا⟫

Praise be to Allah, who sent down upon His servant the Book, free of any crookedness.

⟪ar:والحمد للہ ، الذی لم یتخذ ولداً ولم یکن لہ شریک فی الملک، و لم یکن لہ ولی من الذل و کبرہ تکبیرا⟫

Praise be to Allah, who took no son, who has no partner in dominion, and who has no need of protector against humility; greatly glorified is He.

⟪ar:والحمد للہ ، الذی نَحمَدُہُ وَ نَستَعِینُہُ وَ نَستَغفِرُہُ وَ نُئمِنُ بِہِ وَ نَتَوَکَّلُ عَلَیہ⟫

Praise be to Allah, whom we praise, from whom we seek aid, to whom we turn in repentance, in whom we believe, and upon whom we rely.

⟪ar:و نغوذُ باللہ مِن شُرُورِ أَنفُسِنَا ، وَ مِن سَیِّئاَتِ اعمَالِنَا⟫

And we seek refuge in Allah from the evils of our souls and the ugliness of our deeds.

⟪ar:مَن یَھدِہِ اللہُ فَلاَ مُضِلَّ لَہ وَ مَن یُضلِل فَلا ھادِیَ لَہ⟫

Whomever Allah guides, none can lead astray; and whomever He leads astray, none can guide.

This verse encapsulates the doctrine of predestination (qadr) within Ismaili framework: it is not mere fatalism, but rather an affirmation that ultimate guidance belongs to Allah alone, while human responsibility for righteous action remains absolute. The tension is resolved in the notion that divine guidance works *through* the Imam and the luminous intellects (ʿuqūl) of the believers.

⟪ar:و نشھدُ ان لا الہ الا اللہ وحدہ لا شریک لہ⟫

And we testify that there is no deity save Allah alone, with no partner unto Him.

⟪ar:و نشھد ان محمد عبدُ اللہ و رسولہ، أَرسَلَہُ اللہُ تَعاَلی بِالھُدَی وَ دِینِ الَحقِّ لِیُظھِرَہُ عَلی الدِّینِ کُلِّہ، وَ کفَا بِاللہِ شَھِیدَا⟫

And we testify that Muḥammad is the servant of Allah and His messenger, whom Allah the Most High sent with guidance and the true religion, that He might manifest it over all religions, and Allah suffices as a witness.

⟪ar:و نشھد ان مولانا علی ابن ابی طالب، وصیہ و وزیرہ⟫

And we testify that our lord ʿAlī, son of Abī Ṭālib, is his executor and his minister.

⟪ar:فصل اللہ علیہم و علی آلہم و سَلِّمُھُم تَسلِیماً کثیراً کثیرا⟫

May Allah send abundant blessings and peace upon them and their progeny, multiplied beyond measure.

The witness-formula (shahāda) here unfolds in three moments: the affirmation of divine unity (tawḥīd), the acknowledgment of Muḥammad as the final messenger (khātam al-nabiyyīn), and the proclamation of ʿAlī as executor—a triad that the taʿlīm (doctrinal teaching) tradition shows to be inseparable. The executor preserves what the messenger revealed; the messenger manifested what the One decreed; the One sustains all through His absolute will.

---

⟪ar:اللّٰھم لا عِلمَ لَنا اِلَّا ما عَلَّمتَنَا۔ أِنَّک أَنتَ العَلِیمُ الحکیم⟫

O Allah, we have no knowledge save what Thou hast taught us; verily, Thou art the All-Knowing, the All-Wise.

⟪ar:اللّٰھم عَلِّمنَا بِما یَنفَعْنَا ؛ وَ یَنفَعنَا بِمَا عَلَّمتَنَا⟫

O Allah, teach us what benefits us, and benefit us from what Thou teachest us.

⟪ar:اَللّٰھُمَّ اِنِّا نَعُوْذُ بِکَ مِنْ عِلْمٍ لاَّ یَنْفَعُ ، وَمِنْ قَلْبٍ لاَّ یَخْشَعُ، وِ مِن عَینٍ لا تَدمَع ، وَمِنْ نَّفْسٍ لاَّ تَشْبَعُ⟫

O Allah, we seek refuge in Thee from knowledge that profits not, from a heart that trembles not before Thee, from eyes that weep not in remorse, and from a soul that remains forever unsatisfied.

---

⟪ar:فأما بعد ، یقول سبحانہ و تعالی فی کتابہ الکریم ، بعد أَقولُ أعوذ باللہ من الشیطان الرجیم، بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫

As for what follows, the Glorious and Most Exalted says in His noble Book—and I say this after seeking refuge in Allah from the accursed Satan: *In the name of Allah, the Compassionate, the Merciful.*

⟪ar:رب اشرحلی صدری و یسرلی أمری ۔ وحلل عقدۃ من لسانی یفقھو قولی⟫

O Lord, expand my breast for me and ease my task, and loosen the knot upon my tongue that they may comprehend my speech.

⟪ar:اللہم ثَبِّتنا عِندَ الموتی بی لا الہ الا اللہ، و اللھم جعلنا من الذین آمنو و عمل الصالحات و تواصو بالحق وتواصو بالصبر⟫

O Allah, keep us steadfast at the hour of death upon *there is no deity save Allah*; and O Allah, make us among those who have believed and wrought righteous deeds, and enjoined one another to truth and to patience.

⟪ar:آمین یا رب العلمین⟫

Amen, O Lord of all the worlds.

## Praise for the Creator of All Forms and Orders

⟪ar:الحمد للہِ صَانِعِ الخَلقِ عَلی أکمَلِ نِظَام وَ أَحسِنِ قَوام⟫

All praise belongs to Allah, who fashioned creation in the most perfect order and the most beautiful form.

⟪ar:الواَضِعِ الاَشیَاءِ فی أَشْدَرِ مُقَام و أرْفَقِ مَصاَم⟫

He who placed all things in their rightful station and arranged them with consummate fitness.

The Ismaili metaphysical vision permeates this opening: the cosmos is not a chaos but a perfectly graduated hierarchy. Each being occupies its ḥadd ʿālī (upper limit) and ḥadd dānī (lower limit), its station of manifestation and concealment. The believer who penetrates the outer form (ẓāhir) perceives this underlying harmony—the rational order that theologians call the ʿaql (intellect) running through all creation.

⟪ar:والصلواۃ و السلام علی المُؤَیِّدِ أَبلَغِ الکَلام⟫

Blessings and peace upon him who was upheld with the most eloquent speech.

⟪ar:مُحمدٍ المَبعُوثْ صلی اللہ علیہ و علی آلہ و سلَّم⟫

Upon Muḥammad, sent with the blessing of Allah upon him and his family.

⟪ar:لِھِدایَۃِ کَافَّۃِ أَنَام اِلی سُبُلِ السَّلام و الی مَدَیٰ الَّیاَلِ وَ الأَیَّامْ⟫

For the guidance of all mankind toward the paths of peace, through all nights and days.

⟪ar:الذی أَرسَلَہُ اللہُ تَعاَلی بِالھُدَی وَ دِینِ الَحقِّ لِیُظھِرَہُ عَلی الدِّینِ کُلِّہ ، وَ کفَا بِاللہِ شَھِیدَا⟫

Whom Allah the Most Exalted sent with guidance and the true religion, to manifest it over all religions, and Allah suffices as witness.

⟪ar:و نشھد ان محمد عبدُ اللہ و رسولہ⟫

And we testify that Muḥammad is the servant of Allah and His messenger.

⟪ar:و نشھد ان مولانا علیاً ، وصیہ و وزیرہ⟫

And we testify that our lord ʿAlī is his executor and his minister.

⟪ar:فصل اللہ علیہم و علی آلہم و سَلِّمُھُم تَسلِیماً کثیراً کثیرا⟫

May Allah send abundant and ever-multiplying blessings and peace upon them and their progeny.

---

⟪ar:اللّٰھم لا عِلمَ لَنا اِلَّا ما عَلَّمتَنَا۔ أِنَّک أَنتَ العَلِیمُ الحکیم⟫

O Allah, we have no knowledge save what Thou hast taught us; verily, Thou art the All-Knowing, the All-Wise.

⟪ar:اللّٰھم عَلِّمنَا بِما یَنفَعْنَا ؛ وَ یَنفَعنَا بِمَا عَلَّمتَنَا⟫

O Allah, teach us what profits us, and profit us through what Thou teachest us.

⟪ar:اللھم آتنا نُفُوسِنَا تَقوَاھا وَ زَکَّنا فأنت خیرُ من زَکَّاھا۔⟫

O Allah, grant our souls their piety and purify them, for Thou art the best of purifiers.

The petition for the soul's piety (taqwā) and purification (tazkiya) invokes the process of inner refinement through which the servant becomes fit to receive the divine light. In Ismaili terms, this is the work of the taʾwīl—the esoteric transformation by which the outer letter of law becomes the inner reality of gnosis, and kamāl thānī (secondary perfection) is attained through fidelity to the Imam.

⟪ar:أنت وَلِیُّنا و مولانا و نعم المولی و نعم النصیر۔⟫

Thou art our guardian and our lord; best of guardians art Thou, and best of helpers.

⟪ar:ولا حول ولا قوۃ الا باللہ العلی العظیم⟫

And there is no power nor strength save in Allah, the Most High, the Most Great.

⟪ar:فأما بعد ، یقول سبحانہ و تعالی فی کتابہ الکریم ، بعد أَقولُ أعوذ باللہ من الشیطان الرجیم بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫

As for what follows, the Glorious and Most Exalted says in His noble Book—and I say this after seeking refuge in Allah from the accursed Satan: *In the name of Allah, the Compassionate, the Merciful.*

⟪ar:رب اشرحلی صدری و یسرلی أمری ۔ وحلل عقدۃ من لسانی یفقھو قولی⟫

O Lord, expand my breast for me and ease my task, and loosen the knot upon my tongue that they may understand my speech.

⟪ar:اللہم ثَبِّتنا عِندَ الموتی بی لا الہ الا اللہ و اللھم جعلنا من الذین آمنو و عمل الصالحات و تواصو بالحق وتواصو بالصبر⟫

O Allah, at the moment of death keep us steadfast upon *there is no deity save Allah*; and O Allah, make us among those who have believed and wrought righteous deeds, and counseled one another to truth and to patience.

⟪ar:آمین یا رب العلمین⟫

Amen, O Lord of all the worlds.

**Conclusion**

This five-fold litany, drawn from the KASHKOLE's Quranic Studies collection, functions as both devotional practice and doctrinal affirmation. Each section weaves together exoteric proclamation and esoteric allusion: the praise of divine transcendence opens the door to the understanding of emanation; the witness to Muḥammad and ʿAlī establishes the poles of prophecy and guardianship; the petitions for beneficial knowledge and purification of the soul map the path of ascent toward gnosis and kamāl (perfection).

For the Ismaili believer, such formulas are not mere words but *presences*—each recitation brings the speaker into living communion with the Imam of the age, through whose intercession all petitions find their passage to the Divine Throne. The language, rooted in Quranic speech and hadith, yet suffused with Ismaili theological precision, models the very unity it proclaims: the union of outer truth and inner meaning, revelation and reason, servant and Lord.
