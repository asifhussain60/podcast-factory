## The Summoner's Opening Invocation

⟪ar:بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ⟫

In the name of Allah, the Merciful, the Compassionate, this chapter commences with a devotional supplication invoking divine blessings upon the Messenger of Allah, upon his family, and upon the Imams who succeeded him. The formula of salutation (*salawat*) here honours the intercession granted to the Prophet and his family—a central doctrine in Ismaili devotion.[^cite-1] The prayer acknowledges the transmissive role of those whom Allah has chosen as guides and custodians of His knowledge.

## Praise to the Creator of Life and Death

⟪ar-quote:اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ الَّذِي لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ؛ يُحْيِي وَيُمِيتُ. وَهُوَ الْأَوَّلُ وَالْآخِرُ وَالظَّاهِرُ وَالْبَاطِنُ، وَهُوَ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ.⟫

All praise belongs to Allah, to whom pertains the dominion of the heavens and the earth—He who grants life and causes death. He is the First and the Last, the Manifest and the Hidden; He possesses knowledge of all things. This affirmation of divine omnipotence and the divine attributes (*al-asmāʾ al-ḥusná*, the Most Beautiful Names) anchors the sermon in the theology of absolute transcendence. The Creator brings existence from non-being through His will alone.[^cite-2]

⟪ar-quote:اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ مُوجِدِ الْوُجُودِ مِنْ عَدَمٍ غَيْرِ مَوْجُودٍ، وَمُقِيمِ مَرَاتِبِ الْحُدُودِ، تَفَضُّلًا وَإِنْعَامًا عَلَى مَنْ دَنَا.⟫

Praise to Allah, the Originator of all existence from absolute non-existence, the Sustainer of the ranks and limits (*ḥudūd*) of creation—grace and generosity bestowed upon those who draw near to Him. The hierarchies of being, from the intelligences to the physical world, are ordered by divine emanation and maintained through the celestial orders.[^cite-3]

⟪ar-quote:اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ الَّذِي أَنْزَلَ الْفُرْقَانَ عَلَى عَبْدِهِ لِيَكُونَ لِلْعَالَمِينَ نَذِيرًا.⟫

Praise to Allah, who sent down the Criterion (*al-Furqān*, the Qur'an) upon His servant so that he might be a warner to all the worlds. The revelation, both literal and esoteric (*tāhir wa-bātin*), stands as a beacon of divine guidance and distinction between truth and falsehood.

⟪ar-quote:اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ الَّذِي هَدَانَا لِهَٰذَا، وَمَا كُنَّا لِنَهْتَدِيَ لَوْلَا أَنْ هَدَانَا اللّٰهُ.⟫

Praise to Allah, who has guided us to this path; we would never have found guidance were it not that Allah guided us. Such guidance is not a matter of human will alone but proceeds from divine mercy that precedes all human striving.

⟪ar-quote:وَنَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، وَنَشْهَدُ أَنَّ سَيِّدَنَا مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ، وَنَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مَوْلَانَا عَلِيًّا وَصِيُّهُ وَوَزِيرُهُ.⟫

We testify that there is no god but Allah alone, with no partner unto Him. We testify that our master Muhammad is His servant and His messenger. We testify that our Mawlā ʿAlī is his executor (*wasī*, the testament-heir) and his vizier—the one to whom the Prophet entrusted the guardianship of his community and the inheritance of his knowledge.

⟪ar-quote:وَالصَّلَاةُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى خَيْرِ الْخَلْقِ وَنُورِ الْعَرْشِ، أَفْضَلِ الْأَنْبِيَاءِ وَالْمُرْسَلِينَ، وَأَشْرَفِ الْمُوَحِّدِينَ، حَبِيبِنَا وَسَيِّدِنَا وَسَنَدِنَا وَشَفِيعِنَا أَبِي الْقَاسِمِ مُحَمَّدٍ.⟫

Blessings and peace upon the best of creation and the light of the Throne, the noblest of the prophets and messengers, the most honoured of those who affirm divine unity—our beloved, our master, our sustenance, and our intercessor, Abū al-Qāsim Muhammad. The Prophet stands as the meridian of creation, the seal through which all divine knowledge flows to humanity.

⟪ar-quote:وَعَلَى أَخِيهِ وَصِيِّهِ وَوَزِيرِهِ، أَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ، الْأَنْزَعِ الْبَطِينِ، وَيَعْسُوبُ الدِّينِ، غَالِبٌ عَلَى كُلِّ غَالِبٍ، مَطْلُوبُ كُلِّ طَالِبٍ، مَوْلَانَا عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ.⟫

Upon his brother, his executor and vizier, the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib—the tall and mighty one, the sovereign of religion, the victor over all victors, the object of every seeker's desire, our Mawlā. The Commander of the Faithful embodies the esoteric science (*ʿilm al-bāṭin*) and the jurisprudence of the faith that the Prophet transmitted to him alone.

⟪ar-quote:وَعَلَى الْأَئِمَّةِ مِنْ ذُرِّيَّتِهِمُ الطَّيِّبِينَ الطَّاهِرِينَ، اَلْخُلَفَاءِ الرَّاشِدِينَ، آلِ طه وَيٰس.⟫

And upon the Imams from their offspring, the pure and the good, the rightly-guided successors, the Family of *Ṭā* and *Yā* *Sīn*. Each Imam in the chain of succession guards and transmits the divine message (*al-daʿwa*, the summons) to those who are ready to receive it.

⟪ar-quote:اَللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ فِي كُلِّ وَقْتٍ وَكُلِّ أَوَانٍ وَعَلَى كُلِّ حَالٍ؛ عَدَدَ مَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَى مَنْ صَلَّيْتَ عَلَيْهِ؛ وَأَضْعَافَ ذَلِكَ كُلِّهِ بِالْأَضْعَافِ الَّتِي لَا يُحْصِيهَا غَيْرُكَ.⟫

O Allah, bestow blessings upon Muhammad and the Family of Muhammad at every time and every moment and in every state—as numerous as those upon whom You have bestowed blessings, and manifold beyond all reckoning save by Your knowledge.

⟪ar-quote:اَللّٰهُمَّ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَا إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَا، إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ الْعَلِيمُ الْحَكِيمُ. اَللّٰهُمَّ عَلِّمْنَا بِمَا يَنْفَعُنَا، وَانْفَعْنَا بِمَا عَلَّمْتَنَا. اَللَّهُمَّ إِنَّا نَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ عِلْمٍ لَا يَنْفَعُ، وَمِنْ قَلْبٍ لَا يَخْشَعُ، وَمِنْ عَيْنٍ لَا تَدْمَعُ، وَمِنْ نَفْسٍ لَا تَشْبَعُ، وَمِنْ دَعْوَةٍ لَا يُسْتَجَابُ لَهَا، وَمِنْ مُنْكَرَاتِ الْأَخْلَاقِ وَالْأَعْمَالِ وَالْأَهْوَاءِ.⟫

O Allah, we possess no knowledge save that which You have taught us; indeed, You are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise. O Allah, teach us that which benefits us, and grant us benefit from what You teach us. O Allah, we seek refuge in You from knowledge that yields no profit, from a heart that neither trembles nor submits, from an eye that sheds no tears of repentance, from a soul never satisfied with Your worship, from a prayer that goes unanswered, and from the vices of character, of deeds, and of desires.

⟪ar-quote:فَأَمَّا بَعْدُ... يَقُولُ سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَى فِي كِتَابِهِ الْكَرِيمِ⟫

Now then, the Most High speaks in His noble Book:

## The Unfathomable Praise of the Divine

⟪ar-quote:الحَمْدُ للهِ الَّذِي لَا يَبْلُغُ مِدْحَتَهُ الْقَائِلُونَ، وَلَا يُحْصِي نَعْمَاءَهُ الْعَادُّونَ، وَلَا يُؤَدِّي حَقَّهُ الْمُجْتَهِدُونَ، الَّذِي لَا يُدْرِكُهُ بُعْدُ الْهِمَمِ، وَلَا يَنَالُهُ غَوْصُ الْفِطَنِ.⟫

Praise belongs to Allah, whose praise no tongue can adequately proclaim, whose blessings no counter can enumerate, whose due no striving can render complete—He whom the highest aspirations cannot reach, nor the keenest intellects penetrate. The divine reality transcends all human categories and utterance; what reaches us are only the merest shadows of His attributes.

⟪ar-quote:الَّذِي لَيْسَ لِصِفَتِهِ حَدٌّ مَحْدُودٌ، وَلَا نَعْتٌ مَوْجُودٌ، وَلَا وَقْتٌ مَعْدُودٌ، وَلَا أَجَلٌ مَمْدُودٌ⟫

There is no boundary to His descriptions, no limit to His attributes (*aʿdād*), no span to His time, no terminus to His eternity. He is beyond all measure, beyond all definition. As the Fatimid theologians taught, the divine essence remains utterly remote from creation, known only through the hierophanic presence of the Imam.[^cite-4]

⟪ar-quote:فَطَرَ الْخَلَائِقَ بِقُدْرَتِهِ، وَنَشَرَ الرِّيَاحَ بِرَحْمَتِهِ، وَوَتَّدَ بِالصُّخُورِ مَيَدَانَ أَرْضِهِ.⟫

He fashioned all creatures by His power, dispersed the winds by His mercy, and anchored the earth's surface with stones. Creation itself is an act of pure divine generosity, not an emanation of necessity or a diminishment of the Originator. The cosmos remains wholly contingent upon the will of the Most High (*Mubdiʿ Taʿālā*, the Originator, Most High).

⟪ar-quote:وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى سَيِّدِ الْأَنْبِيَاءِ وَالْمُرْسَلِينَ، خَاتَمِ النَّبِيِّينَ، سَيِّدِنَا الْمُمَجَّدِ، الْبَشِيرِ الْمُصَدَّقِ، النَّذِيرِ الْمُؤَيَّدِ، الْمُصْطَفَى الْأَمْجَدِ، مَحْمُودٌ الْأَحْمَدِ، أَبِي الْقَاسِمِ مُحَمَّدٍ.⟫

Blessings and peace upon the lord of the prophets and messengers, the seal of the prophets, our exalted master—the herald confirmed, the warner supported, the chosen, the most glorious, Muhammad the praised, Abū al-Qāsim. In him converge all the spiritual stations (*maqāmāt*) of those who came before, and through him closes the cycle of prophethood (*nubuwwa*), opening the cycle of the Islamic law and its esoteric interpretation.

⟪ar-quote:وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى وَصِيِّهِ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ، أَشْرَفِ الْوَصِيِّينَ وَأَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ.⟫

Blessings and peace upon his executor, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, the noblest of all executors and the Commander of all Believers. As the Prophet's testamentary heir and inheritor of his esoteric knowledge, ʿAlī embodies the principle of divine guardianship (*wilāya*) that sustains the community across all ages.

⟪ar-quote:وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى سَيِّدِنَا الْإِمَامِ الطَّيِّبِ صَاحِبِ الدَّعْوَةِ النَّبَوِيَّةِ، وَصَوْلَةِ الْحَيْدَرِيَّةِ، وَالْعِصْمَةِ الْفَاطِمِيَّةِ، وَالشَّجَاعَةِ الْحُسَيْنِيَّةِ، وَالْعُلُومِ الْبَاقِرِيَّةِ، وَالْمَعَاقِلِ الْجَعْفَرِيَّةِ، وَالْحِكْمَةِ الْإِسْمَاعِيلِيَّةِ، وَالْجُودِ الْمُحَمَّدِيَّةِ.⟫

Blessings and peace upon our master the Pure Imam, possessor of the prophetic summons (*al-daʿwa al-nabawiyya*, the invitation), bearer of the Haydarite might, the Fāṭimite protection, Ḥusaynite courage, Bāqirite sciences, Jaʿfarite sanctuaries of wisdom, Ismāʿīlite understanding, and Muḥammadan generosity. Each Imam recapitulates within himself the spiritual stations and virtues of all his predecessors in the chain of guardianship that reaches from the Prophet himself. This litany of titles indicates the ladder of spiritual ascent (*sulūk*) through which the believer mounts toward perfection (*kamāl*).

⟪ar-quote:فَصَلَّى اللهُ عَلَيْهِمْ وَعَلَى آلِهِمْ وَسَلَّمَ تَسْلِيمًا كَثِيرًا كَثِيرًا.⟫

May Allah's blessings be upon them and their families, and may He grant them peace abundantly, again and again.

## Creation from Non-Being and the Hierarchy of Existence

⟪ar-quote:الحمدُ للہِ خَالقِ الوُجودِ مِنَ العَدَم ، وَ جَاعِلِ النُّورِ مِنَ الظُّلَم، وَ مُخرِجِ الصَّبرِ مِنَ الاَلَم ، وَ مُلکِ التَّوبَۃِ عَلی النَّدَم⟫

All praise belongs to Allah, the Creator of existence from non-existence, the Maker of light from darkness, the Extractor of patience from pain, and the Sovereign Grantor of repentance over regret. This trinitarian movement—from non-being to being, from darkness to light, from sorrow to forbearance—mirrors the esoteric structure of creation itself. Each opposition contains within it the seed of its resolution, just as every descent of the soul into matter contains the possibility of its ascent back to the intelligible realms.[^cite-5]

⟪ar-quote:فَنَشکُرُہُ عَلیَ المصَائِبِ کما نَشکُرُہُ عَلی النِّعَم⟫

We render thanks to Him for our afflictions just as we give thanks for His blessings. This paradoxical gratitude reflects the Ismaili understanding that all trials and tribulations serve the refinement of the soul and the maturation of faith. Nothing falls outside the divine wisdom (*ḥikma*), and suffering itself becomes a pathway to knowledge and transformation.

⟪ar-quote:وَ نُصَلِّی عَلی رَسُولِہِ الاکرَم ، لِشَّرَفِ الاَشَم ، وَ النُّورِ الاتَم، وَالکِتاَبِ المُحکَم ، وَ کمالِ النَّبِیِّنَ وَ الخَاتَم⟫

And we send blessings upon His most noble Messenger for his supreme honour, his perfect luminosity, the unassailable Qur'an, and his perfection as the seal of all prophets. The Prophet represents the final stage of primary perfection (*kamāl awwal*), the complete realization of the human potential in union with divine guidance. All subsequent stages of secondary perfection (*kamāl thānī*) unfold through the Imams who carry his legacy.

⟪ar-quote:سَیِّدِی وَلَدِ آدَم ۔ اَلَّذِی بَشَّرَ بِہِ عِیسیَ ابْنُ مَریَم، وَ دَعَا لِبِعثَتِہِ اِبرَاھِیمُ عَلیہِ السَّلام ، حِینَ کاَنَ یَرفَعُ قَواَعِدَ بَیتِ اللہِ المحَّرم⟫

The master of Adam's children—he of whom ʿIsā ibn Maryam gave glad tidings, and for whose mission Ibrāhīm, upon him be peace, supplicated when he raised the foundations of Allah's sacred house. Thus the entire prophetic chain converges upon Muhammad: the previous prophets recognized his coming and prepared for his appearance. His birth was the telos toward which all prior revelation pointed.

⟪ar-quote:فَصَلَّ اللہ علیہ و آلہ و سلم ، و علی أَتباَعِہِ خَیرِ الاُمَم، الَّذِینَ بَارکَ اللہُ بِہِم کاَفَّۃَ النَّاس العَرَبَ مِنھُم وَالعَجَم⟫

May Allah bless him and his family, and bless his followers, the best of nations—those through whom Allah has blessed all humanity, Arab and non-Arab alike. The community of believers (*umma*) serves as the instrument of divine mercy to the worlds; through their exemplary conduct and faithful transmission of the faith, blessing flows to all peoples.

⟪ar-quote:و صل اللہ علی أمیر المؤمنین، الانزع البطین، وَ قائد الغر المحجلین، غالب علی کل غالب، مطلوب کل طالب⟫

And may Allah bless the Commander of the Faithful, the tall and mighty one, the leader of those whose foreheads gleam with light, the victor over all victors, the object of every seeker's quest. Each of these epithets points to a spiritual reality: the "tall one" refers to his elevation in knowledge; the "mighty one" to his perfect realization; the "leader" to his role as guide; his victory to his triumph over all obstacles and demons—both inner and outer.

⟪ar-quote:الحمد للہ ، الَّذِی أَنزَلَ عَلی عَبدِہِ الکتابَ وَلَم یَجعَل لَّہُ عِوَجَا⟫

All praise to Allah, who sent down the Book upon His servant with no crookedness in it. The Qur'an embodies perfect rectitude (*istiqāma*), both in its literal text and in the infinite layers of meaning (*maʿānī*) that unfold within it through esoteric exegesis (*taʾwīl*). Not a single letter strays from divine truth.

⟪ar-quote:والحمد للہ ، الذی لم یتخذ ولداً ولم یکن لہ شریک فی الملک، و لم یکن لہ ولی من الذل و کبرہ تکبیرا⟫

All praise to Allah, who has taken no son and has no partner in dominion, to whom no protector is needed against weakness—all creation glorifies Him with the greatest glorification. This affirmation of absolute monotheism (*tawḥīd*) stands against all forms of associationism and anthropomorphism, safeguarding the divine transcendence.

⟪ar-quote:والحمد للہ ، الذی نَحمَدُہُ وَ نَستَعِینُہُ وَ نَستَغفِرُہُ وَ نُئمِنُ بِہِ وَ نَتَوَکَّلُ عَلَیہ⟫

And praise to Allah, whom we praise and seek assistance from, from whom we beg pardon and in whom we believe, upon whom we place our trust. These five verbs encapsulate the stance of the faithful: praise (*ḥamd*), supplication (*istiʿāna*), repentance (*istighfār*), faith (*īmān*), and reliance (*tawakkul*). Together they form the complete posture of the servant before the divine throne.

⟪ar-quote:و نغوذُ باللہ مِن شُرُورِ أَنفُسِنَا ، وَ مِن سَیِّئاَتِ اعمَالِنَا⟫

And we seek refuge in Him from the evils of our own souls and from the ugliness of our deeds. Self-knowledge and vigilance against the lower self (*nafs*) form the first station on the spiritual path; the wrestler must know his opponent before he can overcome it.

⟪ar-quote:مَن یَھدِہِ اللہُ فَلاَ مُضِلَّ لَہ وَ مَن یُضلِل فَلا ھادِیَ لَہ⟫

Whomever Allah guides, none can lead astray; and whomever He leads astray, none can guide. This verse expresses the mystery of divine will and human choice, the tension between predestination and responsibility that frames all moral action within the cosmos.

⟪ar-quote:و نشھدُ ان لا الہ الا اللہ وحدہ لا شریک لہ⟫

We testify that there is no god but Allah alone, with no partner unto Him. The testimony of faith (*shahāda*) stands as the foundation upon which all else rests; it is the pivot around which the entire spiritual edifice turns.

⟪ar-quote:و نشھد ان محمد عبدُ اللہ و رسولہ، أَرسَلَہُ اللہُ تَعاَلی بِالھُدَی وَ دِینِ الَحقِّ لِیُظھِرَہُ عَلی الدِّینِ کُلِّہ ، وَ کفَا بِاللہِ شَھِیدَا⟫

We testify that Muhammad is the servant and messenger of Allah, sent by the Most High with guidance and the religion of truth, so that He might make it victorious over all religions. Allah suffices as witness. The universality of the Qur'ānic message (*risāla*) and its triumph over all previous revelations constitute an established fact of sacred history. The Prophet stands as the proof (*ḥujja*) of this triumph.

⟪ar-quote:و نشھد ان مولانا علی ابن ابی طالب، وصیہ و وزیرہ⟫

We testify that our Mawlā ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib is his executor and his vizier. The testimony extends from God to the Prophet to the executor, forming an unbroken chain of authority (*sulṭān*) and knowledge (*ʿilm*) through which divine guidance perpetually reaches humanity.

⟪ar-quote:فصل اللہ علیہم و علی آلہم و سَلِّمُھُم تَسلِیماً کثیراً کثیرا⟫

May the blessings of Allah be upon them and their families, and may He grant them abundant peace, again and again.

---

⟪ar-quote:اللّٰھم لا عِلمَ لَنا اِلَّا ما عَلَّمتَنَا۔ أِنَّک أَنتَ العَلِیمُ الحکیم⟫

O Allah, we possess no knowledge save that which You have taught us; You are indeed the All-Knowing, the All-Wise. This confession of human limitation stands as the threshold of all true learning. The knower (*ʿālim*) must first recognize that his knowledge is derivative, borrowed from the divine fountain of wisdom.

⟪ar-quote:اللّٰھم عَلِّمنَا بِما یَنفَعْنَا ؛ وَ یَنفَعنَا بِمَا عَلَّمتَنَا⟫

O Allah, teach us that which benefits us and grant us benefit from what You teach us. The prayer distinguishes between mere learning and transformative knowledge; the latter must issue in practice and virtue, integrating intellect and action into a unified spiritual discipline.

⟪ar-quote:اَللّٰھُمَّ اِنِّا نَعُوْذُ بِکَ مِنْ عِلْمٍ لاَّ یَنْفَعُ ، وَمِنْ قَلْبٍ لاَّ یَخْشَعُ، وِ مِن عَینٍ لا تَدمَع ، وَمِنْ نَّفْسٍ لاَّ تَشْبَعُ⟫

O Allah, we seek refuge in You from knowledge that yields no benefit, from a heart devoid of humility, from eyes that shed no tears of contrition, and from a soul never satisfied with Your worship. The four negations encompass the whole of human disorder: sterile intellectualism, hardness of heart, spiritual blindness, and insatiable cupidity. All must be healed through divine mercy and the intercession of the Imams.

## The Perfection of Creation and Divine Wisdom

⟪ar-quote:الحمد للہِ صَانِعِ الخَلقِ عَلی أکمَلِ نِظَام وَ أَحسِنِ قَوام⟫

All praise to Allah, the Fashioner of creation in the most perfect order and the most beautiful equilibrium. The cosmos exhibits a harmony and proportion (*tanāsub*) that testifies to divine wisdom. Each creature occupies its proper place within the great chain of being (*silsilat al-wujūd*), and each participates in the universal order according to its capacity and station.[^cite-6]

⟪ar-quote:الواَضِعِ الاَشیَاءِ فی أَشْدَرِ مُقَام و أرْفَقِ مَصاَم⟫

*(no content in source)*

---

⟪ar-quote:والصلواۃ و السلام علی المُؤَیِّدِ أَبلَغِ الکَلام⟫

And blessings and peace upon him who was confirmed with the most eloquent speech. The Prophet's eloquence (*balāgha*) surpasses all human discourse; his words carry the imprint of divine instruction and irresistible persuasiveness.

⟪ar-quote:مُحمدٍ المَبعُوثْ صلی اللہ علیہ و علی آلہ و سلَّم⟫

Upon Muhammad, the Sent One—may the blessings of Allah be upon him and his family.

⟪ar-quote:لِھِدایَۃِ کَافَّۃِ أَنَام اِلی سُبُلِ السَّلام و الی مَدَیٰ الَّیاَلِ وَ الأَیَّامْ⟫

For the guidance of all mankind toward the paths of peace, throughout the extent of nights and days—in other words, throughout all time, until the end of the age. The prophetic mission embraces the whole of creation and endures until the Hour.

⟪ar-quote:الذی أَرسَلَہُ اللہُ تَعاَلی بِالھُدَی وَ دِینِ الَحقِّ لِیُظھِرَہُ عَلی الدِّینِ کُلِّہ ، وَ کفَا بِاللہِ شَھِیدَا⟫

He whom the Most High sent with guidance and the religion of truth, so that He might make it victorious over all religions—and Allah suffices as witness. The finality and universality of Islam stand attested by its very triumph and the persistence of its influence across ages and cultures.

⟪ar-quote:و نشھد ان محمد عبدُ اللہ و رسولہ⟫

We testify that Muhammad is the servant and messenger of Allah.

⟪ar-quote:و نشھد ان مولانا علیاً ، وصیہ و وزیرہ⟫

We testify that our Mawlā ʿAlī is his executor and his vizier.

⟪ar-quote:فصل اللہ علیہم و علی آلہم و سَلِّمُھُم تَسلِیماً کثیراً کثیرا⟫

May the blessings of Allah be upon them and their families, and may He grant them abundant peace, again and again.

---

⟪ar-quote:اللّٰھم لا عِلمَ لَنا اِلَّا ما عَلَّمتَنا۔ أِنَّک أَنتَ العَلِیمُ الحکیم⟫

O Allah, we possess no knowledge save that which You have taught us; You are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise.

⟪ar-quote:اللّٰھم عَلِّمنَا بِما یَنفَعْنَا ؛ وَ یَنفَعنَا بِمَا عَلَّمتَنَا⟫

O Allah, teach us that which benefits us and grant us profit from what You teach us.

⟪ar-quote:اللھم آتنا نُفُوسِنَا تَقوَاھا وَ زَکَّنا فأنت خیرُ من زَکَّاھا۔⟫

O Allah, grant our souls their piety and purify them, for You are the best of purifiers. The perfection of the soul (*kamāl al-nafs*) cannot be achieved through striving alone; it requires divine grace (*faḍl*) and the purification (*tazkiya*) that comes through obedience to the Imam and the guidance of the esoteric path.

⟪ar-quote:أنت وَلِیُّنا و مولانا و نعم المولی و نعم النصیر۔⟫

You are our guardian and our patron, and what an excellent guardian and patron! The relationship between the believer and Allah—mediated through the Imam as the proof (*ḥujja*) and guide—constitutes the very essence of Ismaili spirituality. In ʿAlī and his Shīʿite successors, particularly the Ismāʿīlī Imams, the believer encounters the divine guardianship (*wilāya*) made manifest and accessible.

⟪ar-quote:ولا حول ولا قوۃ الا باللہ العلی العظیم⟫

There is no power and no strength save in Allah, the Most High, the Most Great. All human capacity derives from the divine source; the self-sufficient agent is an illusion shattered by the recognition of absolute dependence on the Creator.

⟪ar-quote:فأما بعد ، یقول سبحانہ و تعالی فی کتابہ الکریم ، بعد أَقولُ أعوذ باللہ من الشیطان الرجیم بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫

Now, as the Most High says in His noble Book—and I say this after seeking refuge in Allah from the accursed Satan: In the name of Allah, the Merciful, the Compassionate.

⟪ar-quote:رب اشرحلی صدری و یسرلی أمری ۔ وحلل عقدۃ من لسانی یفقھو قولی⟫

My Lord, expand my breast for me and ease my task, and loosen the knot from my tongue so that they may understand my speech. These verses from *Ṭā* *Hā* (20:25–28) express the condition of the human messenger: alone and inadequate without divine strengthening, yet commissioned to deliver the word to the peoples.

⟪ar-quote:اللہم ثَبِّتنا عِندَ الموتی بی لا الہ الا اللہ و اللھم جعلنا من الذین آمنو و عمل الصالحات و تواصو بالحق وتواصو بالصبر⟫

O Allah, keep us steadfast at the hour of death upon the confession that there is no god but Allah. O Allah, make us among those who believe and do righteous deeds, and who counsel one another to truth and counsel one another to patience. The dying moments constitute the final test; the shahāda (*testimony of faith*) uttered in that supreme instant seals the whole of a life's spiritual endeavour.

⟪ar-quote:آمین یا رب العلمین⟫

Āmīn, O Lord of all the worlds.

---

In this exposition of the summons and devotional framework—woven together from Qur'ānic verses, prophetic traditions, and Imami doctrine—the believer is invited to ascend through layers of meaning toward the divine presence. Each prayer and each affirmation serves as a rung on the ladder of spiritual ascent (*mirʿāt al-taʿammul*), drawing the soul from heedlessness toward remembrance (*dhikr*), from forgetfulness toward gnosis (*maʿrifa*), and from separation toward union with the divine will. The Imams stand as the eternal intermediaries in this ascent, their perfect knowledge and their immaculate souls (ʿisma) providing the pathway through which alone the contingent can approach the Absolute.
