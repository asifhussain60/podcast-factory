# Prophets as Teachers, Monotheism, and the Ranks

*This chapter covers sub-chapters twenty through thirty-three of* Chapter Nine *of* this book, *the second half of the author's adjudication of the question of the* shariʿah *of Adam (peace be upon him) and the* wilayah *(guardianship) of Nuh. The first nineteen sub-chapters worked the* defense *of Adam's Shariʿah against the author of* the book *The Defense**'s nineteen objections, landing the cumulative verdict that* the Shariʿah of Adam is fixed*. The fourteen sub-chapters of the second half take up a wider architectural settlement: the* prophets-as-teachers *doctrine that follows out of the* six gates of necessity *settled in sub-chapter fifteen; the deeper reading of the angels' verse* We glorify You with praise and sanctify You *(Quran 2:30) and Allah's reply* I know what you do not know*; the architecture of* force *and* act *(quwwah and fiʿl) by which* knowledge *(ʿilm) and* ignorance *(jahl) take turns as the* end-of-the-cycle *and the* beginning-of-the-cycle*; the* three sections *of Adam's prophetic speech — encouragement, intimidation, or both — and the doctrine that* all three sections affirm an apparent meaning followed by interpretation*; the architectural settlement of* the Qa'im *(the awaited one) raising the laws as the* completion of the roles of the predecessors *and not as the abrogation of worship; the doctrine of* the high limits *(the First Intellect, the universal Soul, the* the grand*, the* the chapter on victory*, the* imagination*) attaining their perfection from the rank of innovation rather than from signs-and-works; the long dispute over the* seven lines and six aradas *by which the architecture of the speaker-cycles and the imam-cycles is graphed; the* Adam-letters *argument and its refutation; the* ark of Noah *(al-fulk, the ship) and the two readings of it (apparent Shariʿah and the wali-of-the-daʿwa), both* sound *and* the matter of this world and the matter of religion lift each other up*; the doctrine that Adam too had a guardian, Seth, the* foundation *(foundation), and that* every speaker requires a guardian who derives benefit from him*; and the deep* taʾwil *(interpretation) of the* son of Noah *as the* opposite *(al-mukhalif) who claimed the guardian's rank by influence and was washed away in the* flood of the cleansing *that spread* outwardly and inwardly*. Across the fourteen sub-chapters, the author settles the architectural shape of the chain that runs through human history, every speaker has a guardian, every cycle has its lines and aradas, every Qa'im completes rather than abrogates, and the* opposite *figure recurs at every joint as the* type *whose washing-away makes the next cycle's purity possible. The chapter closes on the bridge to* Chapter Ten *(monotheism and the critique of* the book *The Harvest**, the* lost-work *that began the four-book chain of the* the two felicities *debate), where the architecture of* monotheism *at the head of the chain is settled against the doctrines of the philosophers.*

## Where this chapter picks up

The first half of *Chapter Nine* settled the *Shariʿah* of Adam by working the book *The Defense*'s nineteen objections one by one: the *qurban* (offering) verse on its *apparent* reading; the *raven-and-burial* verse against the book *The Defense*'s *misreading*; the deep *taʾwil* of the *two sons* as the *wali* (guardian) and the *opposite* and the *raven* as *Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr*; the *narrative tradition* naming Adam as the *prescriber* of the *rites of the dead*; the *prostration of the angels* as proof of Adam's *Mustafa* (Chosen) rank; the *parallel verse to Israel* showing that *silent intermediaries* are not *no intermediaries*; the *natural-world angels* who *do* bear assignment; the silent *God commands you to obey me* objection turned by the parallel of the Prophet ﷺ (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) to Israel; the *roster argument* of Quran 42:13 refuted by the parallel roster of Quran 4:163; the *real reason* for the necessity of the Shariʿah as the *six gates of necessity* arising from the *three souls* of the human being; the *Noah-parallel* showing that *violation of the limits* presupposes *prior limits* drawn by Adam; the *tent-as-first-qibla* and Seth as *first the foundation*; and the refusal of the *bare-world-sufficient-for-monotheism* doctrine that would eliminate the *sending of the messengers* altogether.

The second half of *Chapter Nine* takes the verdict of the first half, *Adam had a Shariʿah, and Seth was his foundation*, and *expands* it across the architectural shape of the *chain that runs in time*. Sub-chapter twenty turns from the question *did Adam have a Shariʿah* to the deeper question *why are prophets sent at all*. The answer is *not only to signify* monotheism, *that the world's structure already does in part, but to* educate the soul *toward the* second perfection*, to draw it through the* two worships *of knowledge and deed toward the* abode of dignity*. Sub-chapters twenty-one through twenty-six unfold the corollaries: the angels' *We praise You and sanctify You* and Allah's *I know what you do not know* read as the architecture by which *knowledge and ignorance* take turns at the *end-of-the-cycle*, Adam's *three sections* of prophetic call (encouragement, intimidation, or both), the *Qa'im* not abrogating worship but completing the roles of the predecessors, and the doctrine that the *high limits* attain perfection *from the rank of innovation*, not from signs-and-works. Sub-chapters twenty-seven and twenty-eight work the *seven lines and six aradas* dispute and the architectural shape of the speaker-cycles. Sub-chapter twenty-nine reads *As We began the first creation, We shall repeat it* (Quran 21:104) on the return of the Qa'im and the *resurgence of ignorance* after knowledge has gone out into act. Sub-chapter thirty refutes the *Adam-letters* argument. Sub-chapters thirty-one through thirty-three work the *ark of Noah*, the doctrine that *every speaker has a wali*, and the deep *taʾwil* of the *son of Noah* as the *opposite* who is washed away in the cleansing flood.

## Sub-chapter twenty — the work is for soul-correction, the angels' verse, and the cycles of knowledge and ignorance

The work of the *speakers*, the author writes, is *not only* for *signifying* monotheism. *Furthermore, the work is not only for monotheism, but also for the correction of the soul in worship, for emulating the highest assembly* (the *al-malaʾ al-aʿla*, the high assembly of the angels around the Throne), *and for acquiring through discipline the honorable morals and noble habits by which they prepare for union with what is above them, not to be diverted from the way.* The *Shariʿah* is itself the *training of the soul*, the *power that preserves the honor of the intellect by fulfilling its duties and that is preoccupied with its features*. *If it were not for the deeds and rituals of the Ummah, and the wholeness of souls within it through perseverance, souls would perish.* The *positive worship* of the *qurban*, the *sajda*, the *rukuʿ*, the *zakat* is not *exterior decoration* of the inner *monotheism*; it is *constitutive* of the soul's *raising-by-degrees* toward the rank at which it can *bear* the *monotheism* it signifies.

The doctrine has its prophetic anchor in a hadith preserved in the *Kutub the six canonical collections* (the six canonical hadith collections). The Prophet  named the prophets' bequest in exactly the terms the author is settling for the *prophets-as-teachers* doctrine, not signs and works as the end, but the knowledge that trains the soul as the inheritance.

> *Verily, the scholars are the heirs of the prophets. The prophets do not bequeath dinars or dirhams; they bequeath knowledge. So whoever takes hold of it has taken an abundant share.* — (Sunan an early traditionist, Hadith 3641; Sunan another early hadith compiler, Hadith 2682; narrated by Abu a companion, classified hasan)

What the prophets *bequeath* is *the knowledge by which the soul is trained*; and that knowledge is the *Shariʿah* the author is fixing for Adam at the head of human history. The chain begins with a speaker who *taught*, not with a speaker who only *signified*.

The Qur'anic anchor is the angels' verse:

> *And when your Lord said to the angels, "I am going to place a caliph on the earth," they said, "Will You place in it one who corrupts it and sheds blood, while we glorify You with praise and sanctify You?" He said, "I know what you do not know."* (Quran 2:30, Sahih International)

The angels' question was an *underestimation* of the depth of what the caliph would carry. They thought their *praise and sanctification*, their *worship-by-the-eternity-of-the-high-limits*, was *sufficient at the door of closeness to God Almighty*, and that *they had no need of a caliph who would teach them or command them to do anything*. Allah's answer, *I know what you do not know*, is the *deep doctrine* of the architecture of the chain.

*The universes, by His decree and His wisdom, are between force and act*, *quwwah* and *fiʿl*, *hesitating: sometimes standing in potentiality, and sometimes already existing in actuality.* And the dynamic of *force-becoming-act-and-act-becoming-force* runs through every dimension of being. *The thing, when it reaches its end in one state, must necessarily return to Him from force and act in retrograde*, when *heat* in a body reaches its end, *cold appears*, as in the melting of bodies; when *cold* reaches its end, *heat appears*, as with snow that gathers and then dissolves. The same dynamic runs through *knowledge* and *ignorance* across the cycles of human history. *Corruption is sometimes ignorance, and knowledge is powerful, in the sense that ignorance is general in the presence of its people, and ignorance is lost through the lack of its people.*

*The time was the time in which ignorance had taken hold and already reached its end, and knowledge had been introduced, and reached its end in potentiality.* The *teachers were disturbed, the ceremonies of the laws were disrupted, the deeds were prolonged in duration, and the times were shortened.* The world *at the moment Adam was sent* was at the *end of the cycle of ignorance*, *ignorance had reached its end as actuality*, and *knowledge had reached its end as potentiality*. The architecture demanded a turn. *The angels narrated that this was sufficient for him in the chapter of worship and perfection, and it was that the matter was different from what they estimated, since they needed knowledge of the limits in totality.* The *high limits*, *those in the house of creativity and the first rebirth, and the knowledge of those who come late in existence, are like the second revival, and they must act by what earns them.* The *high limits* had their perfection from *innovation*; the *natural-world angels* and the *human beings* of the *great cycle* needed their perfection from the *teaching of the limits in totality*. *When they knew that their time was the time of the complete role of ignorance, that they were not in the role of knowledge nor in the role of works, the Almighty taught them to leave them as they were, when ignorance reaches its end as the purpose of the time, since time is the time of the beginning of knowledge by its appearance, which is what wisdom requires.*

The depth-reading of *I know what you do not know* unfolds:

*The Almighty said: "I know", the end of ignorance in the world up to its end, the act, the completion of its role, and its appearance; and the attainment of knowledge in the world up to its goal in strength; and the return of repentance to Him in the reappearance; and the resurrection of the need in His appearance for the establishment of teachers and guides, "what you do not know."*

The verse is itself a *cosmological doctrine* of the architecture of the chain. *I know* names *the end of one cycle and the beginning of another*; *what you do not know* names *the necessity of teachers and guides at the turning point*. *So Allah sent Adam, making him His successor to show forth knowledge, to bring them integrity in actuality, to show them how to know the boundaries, and to teach them what they did not know. This was the beginning of the matter, the emergence of knowledge from power into act in the presence of its people from his days.* And the *arc* extends. *Its end extends to the one who arises; and it is also said that when the one who arises appears, knowledge has already become, meaning that it is widespread, and its people will multiply, while ignorance is internalized, meaning that its people are reduced and its root is cut off.* The arc runs from *Adam's first sending*, *ignorance reaching its end, knowledge beginning to emerge*, through the *cycles of the six speakers* to the *Qa'im at the seventh*, when *knowledge has become widespread* and *ignorance has been cut off at its root*. *The treasures of the earth appear in his days, and he casts them to their appearance, which is a sign of the one who arises, until knowledge is already established, and he discloses all that has been deposited in the bellies of the revealed books and complex symbols.* The whole architecture of the chain, the *six speakers and the seventh Qa'im*, is *one long arc* of *ignorance-being-cut-off-and-knowledge-being-disclosed*.

And the angels' worship of praise-and-sanctification, if taken as the *only* worship, would have left the natural-world angels themselves *incomplete*. *If the angels, with the presence of the heavens and the good ordering of them, had mastered the structure of the world's assets, they would still not have known, nor would they have attained perfection, and there would be no inference from it, except for what Adam uncovered and called them to.* Even the angels needed the teaching of Adam's Shariʿah to attain the perfection of their station. *Adam is the author of a Shariʿah and customs to which he called, and which indicated the limits of Allah and His unity.*

## Sub-chapters twenty-one and twenty-two — the great cycle, the role of the Qa'im, and two readings

Sub-chapter twenty-one takes up the book *The Defense*'s sharpest move on the *necessity of Adam's sending*. *The author of this article says that the creation in the great role did not know, for was there not a similar apparent law among them? Or would one say that the one responsible for it, in the raising of the laws represented a nullification of the knowledge of monotheism, and the people went out to a saying that it is obstructed? If the creation is not ignorant of monotheism, and not ignorant of the role of the Qa'im, and they likewise do not know him because the laws have been raised, then it would have been permissible for Adam to have called people to monotheism without any references, and the like of this in saying.* The objection runs: if the people of the *great cycle* were *not ignorant* (and the angels' verse *we glorify You with praise* says they were not ignorant), then Adam *did not need* a Shariʿah to teach them, a *bare summons to monotheism* would have sufficed.

The author refers back to sub-chapter twenty's architecture. *It may precede our discussion of knowledge and ignorance, and the fact that each of them is sometimes by force and sometimes by act, indeed, this involves explaining the situation in which the creation found itself during the great role, being at the end of ignorance, according to the saying of the author of Al-Nasra.* The *creation in the great cycle* was *not* *not-ignorant* in some absolute sense; it was *at the end of ignorance*, meaning *ignorance had reached its actuality-end* and *knowledge was at its potency-end*. The transition demanded teachers. *Adam's call and his being a teacher to his followers in the landmarks of monotheism would not have been needed, because the people must already have known monotheism. And if they knew the monotheism, then Adam's invitation to them would not have been to do anything they already knew, which would be nothing but a virtue God did not need, and that is impossible.* The objection itself, *if they already knew, why send Adam?*, is *exactly the angels' objection*, and *Allah's answer is the same*: *I know what you do not know*.

*The people did not need what they already had for themselves. When Allah sent Adam, He made him His successor among them, chose him from among them, appointed him as His caliph, and made it obligatory for them to obey him and to return to him in what they did not know. Rather, it was the saying of the angels, "We praise You and sanctify You," in their estimation that they were adequate in praise, sanctification, and monotheism, and that what they were in was sufficient in the matter of worship and knowledge of the limits. But Allah knew what He commanded, what His mercy obliged, to choose Adam from among them, and sent him to them so as to make him a caliph in their education, calling them to their perfection of work and knowledge, all of which leads to knowing the limits in which monotheism is valid. Therefore He said: "I know what you do not know," because the Almighty knew that they were imperfect, that ignorance had ended in the deed, that the work had come to manifest, and that they did not know. This is why it is obligatory in wisdom to establish the office of education, which is the cause of the expansion of knowledge and the multitude of its people. So Allah selected Adam, gave him a law, and taught him, as Allah said: "And Adam knew all the names, then He showed them to the angels and said: Inform Me of the names of these, if you are truthful. They said: Glory be to You, we have no knowledge except what You have taught us; You are the Knower, the Wise. He said: O Adam, tell them their names. … I know the unseen of the heavens and the earth"* (Quran 2:31–33, Sahih International).

*The people were of two worlds, and because they were so, Adam came to them as their teacher.*

Sub-chapter twenty-two adds a *distinction* on *what role of the Qa'im is meant*. *The book *The Defense**'s phrase *not ignorant in the role of the Qa'im* admits *two readings*. *If what he means is the image of the daʿwa from the role of the Qa'im before it appears and before it exists from the natural world, when it is preserved by his successors and preachers after the completion of the role of Muhammad the Chosen Prophet and until his time, and the appearance of His signs in the world, then it is true, for then knowledge has raised the beacon of his appearance with the many scholars. This is the time in which Allah said: "On the day when the children will grow old"* (Quran 73:17, Sahih International). The *interregnum* of the *daʿwa*, the period between Muhammad 's completion of his cycle and the Qa'im's manifestation, is the time of *knowledge already going widespread*, of *scholars multiplying*. On *this* reading, the book *The Defense*'s claim is *true*: the creation in *this* role of the Qa'im is *not ignorant of monotheism*.

*But if what he meant by his saying was that they are not ignorant in the role of the guardian after the Qa'im, who is the owner of the seventh floor, when he dismissed speech, distinguished the people of Paradise from the people of Hell, closed the door of education, and when repentance is no longer valid, because they do not remain knowledgeable, and they are not of the people of knowledge, for that time is the time of the waters to overflow and cover, and the time of misery and austerity to be seized and to appear, then the words of the author of The book *The Defense* in that regard are not of an investigator.* The *post-Qa'im rank*, when the *door of education is closed* and *repentance is no longer valid*, is *not* the rank in which the creation could be *not-ignorant of monotheism*. On *this* reading, the book *The Defense*'s claim is *false*. The doctrine of the *cycles of knowledge and ignorance* runs *both* before and after the *Qa'im*; the *role of the Qa'im* is the *turning point*, and the *reading of the architecture* on either side of the turning is *opposite*. *The book *The Defense** has confused the two roles; the author distinguishes them.

## Sub-chapter twenty-three — the three sections of Adam's call

Sub-chapter twenty-three works the *form* of *Adam's call*. *In summary, the author of The book *The Defense* only mentioned these sayings and quotes in support of the owner of the crop in his payment of the law of Adam. Adam is not devoid of his supplication to them, and being a calm prophet, he must either have called them to encourage them toward Paradise and the neighbor of the Lord of the Worlds, or invited them to intimidate them toward Hell and being kept away from the mercy of the best of creators, or all of these.* The *form* of *Adam's prophetic call* is *necessarily one* of *three sections*, *encouragement* (*targhib*), *intimidation* (*tarhib*), or *both*. There is no fourth possibility for a prophetic call.

*If He calls them to encouragement, encouragement is not perceptible except by striking proverbs and reports about Him with what is known to the senses of the kinds of pleasures, blessings, and good places, such as dwellings, orchards, rivers, and trees, so they may conceive of it. That is the form of speech that requires interpretation.* *Encouragement* requires *parables*, *Paradise as dwellings, orchards, rivers, trees*, and *parables* require *interpretation*. *And if He called them to intimidation, then his saying is likewise only by giving them proverbs that frighten them, from it to the world of the senses, to visualize it; and in its interpretation there is a current other than the above.* *Intimidation* likewise requires *parables*, *Hell as fire, chains, scorching wind*, and *parables* likewise require *interpretation*. *If He calls them to all of these, then his words are not without a valid apparent meaning, so interpretation is necessary.*

The conclusion: *If that is the case, then the three sections affirm an apparent meaning that is fixed for Adam, followed by an interpretation, so Adam is the owner of a law and a clear custom.* On *any* of the three possible *forms* of *Adam's prophetic call*, his speech *must* have an *apparent meaning* (the parables of Paradise or Hell or both) *followed by* an *interpretation* (the inward truth of what those parables mean). The very *form* of *prophetic speech*, by *necessity*, *carries both* a *zahir* (the apparent) and a *batin* (the hidden). And the *zahir* of *prophetic speech*, by definition, *is the law and the clear custom*, the *Shariʿah*.

The Prophet 's own teaching enacts the same architecture: the prophetic call carries its meaning *by parable* on the surface and *by interpretation* underneath, and the parable is the *form by which* the warning reaches the hearing of the listener.

> *My example and the example of the people is like that of a man who kindled a fire. When it lit up what was around him, the moths and flying insects began to fall into the fire. The man tried to prevent them, but they overcame him and plunged into it. That is your example and mine: I am holding the belt of your loin-cloths, pulling you back from the Fire, but you are slipping away from my hand.* — (Sahih the canonical hadith compiler, Book of Riqaq, Hadith 6483; Sahih Muslim, Book of Virtues, Hadith 5953; narrated by a transmitter of the Prophet's traditionsh)

The hadith carries *both registers* at once, the parable's *apparent meaning* (the man, the fire, the moths, the held belt) and its *interpretation* (the Prophet's protective hand against the eschatological Fire), exactly as the author names the *three sections* of the prophetic call. *Adam's call* too, by the three-section doctrine, runs on the same architecture from the beginning of human history.

## Sub-chapter twenty-four — knowledge and action together

Sub-chapter twenty-four lands the *positive* settlement. *Adam is not without either calling the people of his time to obey God, obeying Him with wisdom, or without wisdom; and he does not disobey, for that is impossible. The belief that he is outside religion is impossible; rather he calls them to wisdom, and wisdom requires knowledge as well as work. So Adam is the owner of a law that combines knowledge and action. They are the worship of Allah, and through them he attains perfection and ascent to the houses of dignity.* The *two worships*, *knowledge* and *action*, together constitute the *one Shariʿah*. Either alone is *insufficient*. The *body and spirit of the religion*, settled in *Chapter Seven* and re-affirmed in *Chapter Eight*'s sub-chapter twenty-three, is settled here for *Adam* specifically.

> *The worship of the body and the worship of the spirit are the two faces of one obedience. The Shariʿah is the body; the* taʾwil *is the spirit. He who keeps the body and abandons the spirit, his religion is dead; he who claims the spirit and abandons the body, his religion is a lie. And Adam, peace be upon him, brought both, the body of the rite and the spirit of the* taʾwil*, as Allah taught him* the names of all things*.* — Imam al-Muʿizz li-Din Allah Maʿadd ibn Ismaʿil, *Taʾwil the lawgiverʿa* (*The Interpretation of the Shariʿa*)

## Sub-chapter twenty-five — the Qa'im raising the laws is completion, not abrogation

Sub-chapter twenty-five takes up a *related concern* about the *seventh speaker*. *The book *The Defense** had pressed that *the Qa'im raises the laws*, that the seventh speaker *abrogates* the Shariʿah, and therefore the *Shariʿah-rank* in the chain is not at the *head* but somewhere in the *middle*. The author refuses the framing.

*To believe this is sick, for the estimation of its existence from the natural world does not arise from the second way of revival, because it does not invalidate anything related to the worship of Allah, the Almighty.* The Qa'im does *not* abrogate worship. *Rather, it is the conclusion of the roles of the predecessors and the completion of something that has been done in its place; and it removes the boundaries about their ranks, by the fact that the referendum took place in teaching about them, and the matter was completed in the new creation, regarding which Allah Almighty said: "Are We then wearied with the first creation?"* (Quran 50:15, Sahih International) *after He has established a judgment concerning the souls of one from whom the course of the foundations is derived, those whose roles are emptied.*

The *Qa'im's rank* is *completion*, not *abrogation*. The *boundaries* (the *the limits*, the *ranks* of the *daʿwa*'s officers, the *the speaker-prophet*, *the foundation*, *imam*, *hujjah*, *daʿi*) *pass away* in the *Qa'im's rank* because *the matter is complete*, not because *worship is annulled*. And the *apocalypse-verses* of the Qur'an confirm this reading.

*Allah Almighty said, describing the Master of the Day of Resurrection: "When the heaven is cleft asunder, when the planets are scattered. When the heaven is cleft asunder, when the sun is darkened, when the stars fall, when the mountains are removed, and when the public…"* (Quran 82:1–2; 81:1–4 in mosaic, Sahih International). *All these parables are multiplied upon the limits, and the references concern their passing away, their ranks, not the laws and ordinances of the works that have passed in their time. It studies its features without teachers, except what was necessary for its existence, as we have mentioned.* The *cleaving of the heaven*, the *scattering of the planets*, the *darkening of the sun*, the *falling of the stars*, these are not the *abrogation of the Shariʿah*; they are the *passing away of the ranks of the limits* at the end of the cycle. *That is the time when this act appears with the closing of the door of teaching, and the cessation of the calls on the part of the Master of the Great Resurrection. It is the beginning when knowledge returns to potentiality and is concealed, and ignorance begins to increase.*

The *Qa'im's role* is the *turning point*, the moment when the *act of knowledge* peaks and the *return of knowledge to potentiality* begins. The *Shariʿah of Adam* and the *Shariʿah of Muhammad*  *are the same architecture* at *different ranks* of the *one chain*; neither is abrogated, neither is annulled. The chain *runs through* them all.

## Sub-chapter twenty-six — monotheism and the high limits

Sub-chapter twenty-six closes the *monotheism-and-signs* dispute that opened in sub-chapter nineteen of the first half. *The book *The Defense** had pressed a counter-argument: *Whoever says that monotheism can be realized only through signs and works has refuted himself by ruling that the First Intellect, the Soul, the angels, the Jadd, the Fath, and the Khayal did not know monotheism, since they have no signs or works between them or among them.* The argument: if *signs and works* are *necessary* for *monotheism*, then the *high limits*, the *abode-of-eternity* ranks, would *not know monotheism*. But the *high limits* *do* know *monotheism*. So *signs and works* are *not necessary* for *monotheism*, and the *sending of the messengers* with *signs and works* is *itself* unnecessary.

The author distinguishes. *Whoever says this does not mean the First Intellect, nor the high limits. Rather, he means the intellects in the world of the body, which is the second emanation, as it was its means.* The doctrine that *monotheism* requires *signs and works* applies *only* to the *intellects in the world of the body*, to *human beings* and the *natural-world angels* who *dwell in the abode of nature*. *Wholeness is attained by the teaching of obligation, knowledge, signs, and works, and by acquisition.* For *the bodily intellects*, *monotheism* is reached *by acquisition*, and *acquisition* requires *teachers*, *signs*, *works*.

*The First Intellect and the paradoxical transcendent limits of bodies have not attained their perfection through any of that; rather, they have gained their perfections from the beginning of their existence in innovation and emanation. It is not permissible to apply this statement to that.* The *high limits* attain their *monotheism* by the *rank of innovation*, by being *originated* in their *eternity-of-self-knowing-alone*, as settled in *Chapter Eight*'s sub-chapter nine on the doctrine that the First Intellect is *pure unity self-knowing alone, abstracted from need*. The *high limits* and the *bodily limits* attain *monotheism* by *different routes*, and the book *The Defense*'s argument confuses the two.

The doctrine has its devotional anchor at the rank of the *imam*'s line in *Sahifa the supplications* (the *Psalms of Islam*), the prayer-book of Imam the fourth Imam, fourth in the line of the Commander of the Faithful. The supplication on the bearers of the Throne and the angels of presence describes the *high limits*' rank precisely on the architecture the author settles, the angels' monotheism is the rank of *being-at-the-station-of-presence*, not the route of signs-and-works the bodily intellects must travel.

> *O God, and as for the bearers of Your Throne, who never flag in glorifying You, never weary of hallowing You, never tire of worshipping You, never prefer slackness over earnestness, and are never neglectful of fervent love for You, … and those of them entrusted with the protection of Your servants, … bless them and increase their portion of Your gentleness, and Your peace be upon them.* — (Sahifa the supplications, Du'a 3, by Imam the fourth Imam, the supplication on the bearers of the Throne)

The supplication's architecture matches the author's: the *bearers of the Throne* and the *angels of presence* attain their station *by their originated rank*, not by acquisition. They *never flag*, *never weary*, *never tire*, because their *being-originated-at-the-station-of-presence* already *is* their *monotheism*; they did not *travel to it*. The bodily intellects acquire; the high limits *are*. *The book *The Defense**'s argument confuses the routes; the supplication marks them apart.

And the book *The Defense* mention of *seven stars, six days of creation*, and the book *The Correction*'s interpretation of these *cosmological numbers*, are *Arabized translations* that *barely balance*. *The book *The Correction* did not leave anything that he did not bring from the interpretation of that and its meanings. It has enough Arabization in the interpretation that it is hardly balanced, it does not match.* The *correspondence* of the *cosmological architecture* (the *seven stars* of the heavens, the *six days* of the creation of the heavens and earth) to the *speakers and the cycles* is *itself* a *received daʿwa doctrine*, and the book *The Correction*'s exposition of it is *sufficient*. *The book *The Defense**'s objection adds nothing.

## Sub-chapters twenty-seven and twenty-eight — the seven lines, the six aradas, and the architecture of the cycles

The longest sub-dispute of the second half opens in sub-chapter twenty-seven. *The book *The Correction** graphs the architecture of the *speaker-cycles* as *seven lines* with *six aradas* (*ʿaraḍ*, the "cross-line" or "transverse mark" of the *daʿwa*'s geometric mnemonic) between them.

*The first line is not obligatory until the second line appears, because the line indicates the Speaker, and the ʿarda indicates the Sharia, and a form should not appear of a thing except after it has been completed. So, just as the ʿarda appeared after the second line, likewise the law of the first was not fulfilled until the appearance of the second Speaker; and when the second Speaker appeared, the law of the first is fulfilled, and its form appears in perfection. The same applies to the six ʿardas in the seven lines, because there is no law before the first pronunciation, and no law upon the appearance of the seventh, but the six laws belong to the pronouncers, the seventh of whom is the Qa'im. If you draw seven lines, six of which appear in these parables, one, two, three, four, five, six, even if the measurement is upon that, to say that the first pronouncement has no law, then five ʿardas must appear between seven lines. When six ʿardas appeared from among seven lines, the seven lines became the masters of the seven cycles, and the six ʿardas the six laws, and the law was made obligatory upon Adam.*

The architecture, in the book *The Correction*'s reading: seven *lines* (the seven cycles, headed by the seven speakers), six *aradas* (the six laws, *between* the seven speakers, Adam's law occupying the arada between Adam and Noah, Noah's law occupying the arada between Noah and Abraham, and so on, with the *seventh* speaker (the Qa'im) having *no arada after him*, because his role is *completion*, not *new law*). The very *count*, six aradas among seven lines, establishes that *Adam's law occupies the first arada*, and therefore *Adam has a law*.

*The book *The Defense** offers a different reading of the same geometry. *Concerning the chapter on the six ʿardas between the seven lines, and his inference from the lines upon the Prophets, and from the ʿardas upon the six laws, it is not as he said. Rather, the seven lines stand for the higher letters, since every Prophet wrote a letter of them; and the six ʿardas stand for the cycles of the Imams between Adam and Noah, who are the seven Imams; and between Noah and Abraham in the second cycle; and between Abraham and Moses in the third cycle; and so on up to Muhammad. No other cycle remains for the two Qa'ims save the cycle of their successors and their substitutes in the world. So six ʿardas of five lines fell, as though there occurred between the cycles of the Imams five pronouncements of the masters of the laws, in this likeness*: *Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, Muhammad, al-Qa'im. The foundation, the foundation, the foundation, the foundation, the imams, the imams, the imams.* The *seven lines* are *not* the *speakers* but the *higher letters* (the *seven principal letters of the Arabic alphabet* in the *daʿwa*'s esoteric reckoning, each of which is *written* by one of the seven speakers); the *six aradas* are *not* the *six laws* but the *cycles of the Imams between the speakers*. And in *this* reading, *the Qa'im has no Imams after him, so he is not charged with establishing the Shariʿah; likewise, when there were no Imams before Adam, he was not charged with establishing the Shariʿah. For if the Shariʿah, in its establishment, must be preceded by Imams, it is also delayed by them.* The *symmetry* of the *Qa'im (no Imams after, no Shariʿah)* and *Adam (no Imams before, no Shariʿah)* is the book *The Defense*'s *neatest* argument against the *Shariʿah of Adam*, and it lands with the elegance of a *closed geometry*.

The author enters with the *correction*. *The statement of the author of the book *The Correction* is identical to what he has indicated, but his saying that "just as the ʿarda appeared after the second line, so the law of the first was not fulfilled except with the appearance of the second Speaker", this is a sick and incorrect statement.* The *Shariʿah of Adam* is *not* dependent on the *appearance of Noah* for its *fulfillment*; it stands on its own. *The law of Adam is itself its foundation, and he has established its foundation for it.* If we said that *Adam's law was fulfilled only with the coming of the Speaker of the second cycle*, then we would have to say that *the law of Muhammad  is not perfected except with the coming of the Qa'im who holds the seventh cycle*, *which is among the impossibilities of statements*. *The Shariʿah is a stature that does not require any increase upon it nor any decrease from it.* Each speaker's Shariʿah is *complete in itself* at the moment of its drawing.

*The book *The Correction** had perhaps meant something different, that *the first cycle, which belongs to Adam, was not fulfilled except with the appearance of the Speaker of the second cycle, which is Noah*, that the *cycle*, not the *Shariʿah*, is *closed* by the next cycle's opening. If he had said *that*, the argument would be *consistent*. The *cycles* are *consecutive*; each cycle is *closed* by the next cycle's *opening speaker*. *And Allah forgives us and him through His mercy.*

Sub-chapter twenty-eight gives the author's *positive* version of the geometry. *As for the author of the book *The Defense*'s statement concerning the line and the ʿarda, and his interpretation of the six ʿardas upon the Imams, it is better that the formations be upon the pronouncers and not upon the higher letters, and that the ʿardas be based upon the duration of their command in the cycle, not upon the foundationes; otherwise it does not require that Adam has no Shariʿah. Rather, the Shariʿah is made obligatory by the presence of the Imams after him.* The *lines* graph the *speakers* (not the *higher letters*); the *aradas* graph *the duration of each speaker's command in the cycle* (not the *Imams between speakers*). And the *Imams after Adam*, Seth and his descendants in the cycle of Adam, *entail* the obligation of *Adam's Shariʿah*, because *the presence of the Imams entails the obligation to preserve the rank of the Speaker, his Shariʿah, and his designs in his community; without that, there would be no obligation to obey the Imam of a Speaker-cycle.*

The *five lines and six aradas*, if the book *The Correction* takes them as he has, can be correlated in another way: the lines on the *five Imams* of each cycle, and the aradas on the *cycles of the masters of the six laws*. The cycle itself is *a rotation: a major cycle and a minor cycle*. The major cycle belongs to the Speakers, those who preserve their place in their community after the Imams; the minor cycle belongs to the faithful the foundationes, those who seal the weeks. If the lines are given to the Speakers, the aradas mark the duration of the politics of their community; if they are given to the seven minor ones, the Imams in every cycle, the aradas mark the duration of the order of the Imams in every week. The architecture admits both readings, and both yield Adam's Shariʿah.

## Sub-chapter twenty-nine — repetition and return

Sub-chapter twenty-nine takes up a Qur'anic verse:

> *As We began the first creation, We shall repeat it.* (Quran 21:104, Sahih International)

*The book *The Defense** had read the verse as *evidence* that the *return* of all things is *as they were at the beginning of the matter*, and the book *The Defense* presses this on the *form* of *Adam's beginning* in a way intended to *evacuate* the *Shariʿah* of Adam.

The author gives the *daʿwa*'s reading. *Even if this is an interpretation, the proper face is to say what corresponds to the saying of Allah Almighty. This is a reference to the return of the Qa'im after him. Knowledge, after it has gone out into actuality, returns to potentiality; its abundance to scarcity; and the destinies of all people after him become as they were in the time of Adam.* The verse is about *the cycles of knowledge and ignorance*, the *return of knowledge* from *actuality back to potentiality*, the *return of ignorance* from *being-cut-off back to abundance*. *And the return of ignorance after it had been in strength, and its reduction to actuality, and its abundance, because of its estimated wisdom.* The *post-Qa'im* world *resembles* the *pre-Adam* world, *ignorance in actuality, knowledge in potentiality*, and the *arc* of the seven cycles is the *one act of knowledge* between *two abundances of ignorance*.

The cyclic architecture has its prophetic anchor in a hadith preserved in *Sahih the canonical hadith compiler* and *Sahih Muslim*. The Prophet  named the *eschatological return* of *ignorance* in language the author's verse-reading rests on directly, knowledge departing with the deaths of the scholars, the people sinking back into the rank of ignorance.

> *Verily, Allah does not take away knowledge by snatching it from the people, but He takes it away by taking away the scholars; so that when no scholar remains, the people take ignorant ones as their leaders. They are asked, and they give legal verdicts without knowledge, so they go astray and lead others astray.* — (Sahih the canonical hadith compiler, Book of Knowledge, Hadith 100; Sahih Muslim, Book of Knowledge, Hadith 6796; narrated by Abdullah ibn Amr ibn al-As)

The hadith names *exactly* the *return* the verse Quran 21:104 names, *knowledge departing with the death of its bearers*, *the scholars vanishing*, *the people falling back into the rank of ignorance*. The *post-Qa'im* world the chapter has settled is the world the Prophet described. *And blessed be Allah, the best of creators.* The verse does *not* erase *Adam's Shariʿah*; it confirms the *cyclic architecture* in which *Adam's Shariʿah is the beginning of the act of knowledge*.

## Sub-chapter thirty — the letters of Adam's name

Sub-chapter thirty takes up the book *The Defense*'s *etymological* objection. *If the author of the book *The Correction* looked at what is in his name, I mean Adam, of the significance, yet he had no law, because he had this: that his name is from three letters, which are Alif, Dal, and Mim, which are not compounded or composed, but rather each one of them is indicated by the other, a clear indication that he was not the master of the authorship of the Shariʿah. And the five Prophets who were after him have names, each of which is the author of a vehicle, such as Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad, which is evidence that each of them had a Shariʿah.* The argument: *Adam's name* is *three uncompounded letters* (Alif, Dal, Mim), each *standing alone*; *the names of the other prophets* are *compound* (Noah = Nun-Waw-Ha; Abraham = Alif-Ba-Ra-Ha-Ya-Mim; Moses = Mim-Waw-Sin-Ya; Jesus = ʿAyn-Ya-Sin-Ya; Muhammad = Mim-Ha-Mim-Dal). The *compound structure* of *the later names* signifies *the Shariʿah-authorship*; the *uncompounded structure* of *Adam's name* signifies *no Shariʿah*.

The author refutes by *internal inconsistency*. *If the separation of the letters of Adam's name entails that Adam has no law, then the fact that these letters are not separate in themselves entails that he has a law, because the Alif of the name is composed of alif, lām, and fā'; and the Mīm of it is composed of mīm, yā', and mīm; and likewise the Dāl.* The *spelling-out* of each letter (Alif = Alif-Lam-Fa, Dal = Dthe alphabetical letters-Lam, Mim = Mim-Ya-Mim) shows that *each letter* of *Adam's name* is *itself* compounded. If *compounding* signifies *Shariʿah-authorship*, then *Adam's name*, read at the level of *the spelling of its letters*, *itself* signifies *Shariʿah-authorship*. The argument *cuts both ways*.

And it cuts a third way. The Prophet 's name *Muhammad* is itself a four-letter compound, fewer letters than *Noah*, *Moses*, or *Jesus*. On the book *The Defense*'s logic, the number of letters would have to match the structure of the law; but the names of the speakers vary in compound-length without correlating to depth of Shariʿah. The inference fails. *It is not considered by inference like this. The Shariʿah for Adam is fixed. And praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds.*

## Sub-chapter thirty-one — the ark, the apparent law, and the daʿwa

Sub-chapter thirty-one turns from *Adam* to *Noah*. *The book *The Correction** writes: *As for the statement that Noah was the first to establish its foundation, and he was likened to the ship, it has been said in the chapter on the foundations that there is sufficiency in it.* The argument: *Noah was singled out by the ark because he was the first to have intentions, and the first to abrogate the laws and compose a new law. His law was likened to the ship, which he was copied with as the flood, because he destroyed the people of the earth with it except those who responded to his law and boarded his ship, that is, established obedience to his foundation. This means that his law lasted his lifetime; so when he completed it, he delegated the matter to its foundation and commanded him to establish the call according to his law, and commanded the appendices to listen to him. They did not receive the worship except through him. Whoever obeyed his foundation was in the ship, and whoever disobeyed him and adhered to the first law, do you not see that he was described as drowned by the flood, those who worshipped Wadd, Suwa', Yaghuth, Ya'uq, and Nasr? It is said: "They were drowned in their sins, so they were made to enter the Fire"* (Quran 71:25, Sahih International).

*The book *The Defense** offers a different *taʾwil*. *If the status of the ship is the same as that of the apparent law, then he who adheres to it has survived, being among the people of the apparent; and the true hidden call has been annulled, provided that what appears from the Book of Allah Almighty is different from what the author of the book *The Correction* went to. Likewise, Allah Almighty described the beginning of the command of Noah and sent him to his people, and he called them to his law, and then He ordered him to make the Ark.* The Qur'anic ordering matters. *The proper account in Surat Hud* (Quran 11) *confirms that, even if the ark stood for the apparent shariʿa, God would not have commanded Noah to build the ark first. Rather, the story shows that he first composed the apparent shariʿa, called creation to make use of it, and only then ordered him to build the ark, meaning that he was to establish his guardian (wali). The evidence is the saying of Allah, may He be exalted: "And he was making the ark"* (Quran 11:38, Sahih International), *and likewise: "And whenever any of the chiefs of his people passed him, they ridiculed him"* (Quran 11:38, Sahih International), *meaning that whenever Noah issued a command, the nobles of his people mocked him and marveled at him because he was the first to establish a guardian, and so they ridiculed him.* *The book *The Defense**'s reading: the *ark* is *not* the *apparent Shariʿah*; the *ark* is *the appointment of the wali*, and the *building of the ark* is the *establishment of Noah's guardian* in the *daʿwa-architecture* sense. The *Wadd* and *Suwa* worshippers are not the *Shariʿah-rejecters*; they are the *wali-rejecters*.

The author lands the verdict on both readings. *As for the statement of the author of the book *The Correction*, with his interpretation of the ark as the face of the new shariʿa, because whoever entered under its rulings became like one inside a ship who is delivered from humiliation, abasement, and every kind of calamity, just as one who is inside a ship is delivered, this meaning is admissible.* The *apparent reading* of the ark as the *new Shariʿah* stands. *As for the author of the book *The Defense* and his interpretation of the ark as the daʿwa and the guardian, because whoever enters the quiet daʿwa and adheres to obedience to its foundation has been kept safe from drowning in error, from the unity of Allah Almighty, and from knowledge of His limits, the guardian taking for him the rank of a guide, both interpretations are sound in that regard.* The *batini reading* of the *ark* as the *daʿwa-with-the-wali* also stands. *One is the matter of this world and the other is the matter of religion, and neither of them dispenses with the other, for each of them lifts up the head of the other.* The *two readings* are *both true*, and together they *lift up the head of the other*: the *apparent Shariʿah* needs the *batini call*, the *batini call* needs the *apparent Shariʿah*, and the *ark* is *both*.

## Sub-chapter thirty-two — every speaker has a guardian, and Adam had Seth

Sub-chapter thirty-two takes up the book *The Defense*'s further claim: *Noah was the first to establish a guardian by interpretation in the new role*. The author refuses the claim categorically. *This is not correct, for the prophecy of Adam and his shariʿa are established. It is proven that Seth was his guardian, and the precedent is in the shariʿa, the guardianship, and its endurance is through him.* And the *general doctrine* is then settled. *It is from the dictates of reason that each speaker requires a guardian who derives benefit from him, particularly since every motor (muharrik) requires a necessary mover, the mover being one even if many things are moved by it.* The *physics-analogy* of *one mover, many moved*, Aristotle's doctrine of the *first mover*, adapted into the *daʿwa*'s architecture, settles the doctrine. *And since the speaker in his age is a source from which inspiration flows to all souls by his summons to seek persistence through the performance of the two acts of worship, it is necessary that there be a movable agent from whom the course of the learner runs, even if many learners learn from him, and the learner intended here is the one whom we call the guardian. Adam, when he was a speaker, was bound to have a guardian; and likewise every speaker. And if Adam has had a guardian appointed for him, it is not permissible to believe in anything contrary to that.*

The doctrine of *one speaker, one guardian* has its canonical anchor at the rank of the *imam* whose line carries Adam's Shariʿah forward across the cycles. In a famous reply preserved in the book *The Path of Eloquence* (compiled by the compiler of the sermons, c. 1009 CE), Imam the Commander of the Faithful, the Commander of the Believers, replied to his pupil Kumayl ibn Ziyad the elite'i on *the necessity of the proof of God in every age*, and the answer fixes the *speaker-and-guardian* pairing as a *necessary truth* of the architecture.

> *O Kumayl, the earth is never empty of one who stands for Allah with proof, either appearing and known, or fearful and concealed, lest the proofs of Allah and the clear signs of His religion be invalidated. How many, and where are they? By Allah, they are the fewest in number, but the greatest with Allah in rank. Through them Allah preserves His proofs, until they entrust them to those like themselves, and sow their seed in the hearts of those like them.* — (the book *The Path of Eloquence*, Aphorism 147; from the reply of Imam the Commander of the Faithful to Kumayl ibn Ziyad the elite'i)

The Commander of the Believers names *exactly* the architecture: the *appearing and known* guardian (Seth at the rank of Adam, the *the foundation* declared in the community) and the *fearful and concealed* guardian (the *imam-in-hiding*, the *wali* in occultation when the cycle demands silence). *Every speaker has a guardian* is not a *doctrine the author invents from physics*; it is the *settled position* of the imam's own line, named by the imam himself.

The doctrine of *one speaker, one guardian* is a *necessary truth* of the *daʿwa*'s architecture. *Every speaker has a guardian who derives benefit from him.* Adam had Seth. Noah had his guardian. Abraham, Moses, Jesus, Muhammad , each had a guardian. The *speaker-and-guardian* pairing is *the joint of every cycle*.

## Sub-chapter thirty-three — the son of Noah, the opposite, and the cleansing flood

Sub-chapter thirty-three closes *Chapter Nine* with the longest *taʾwil* of the second half: the *son of Noah* in *Surat Hud*.

> *And Noah called his son, who had separated himself, "O my son, ride with us, and do not be with the disbelievers." He said, "I will take refuge in a mountain that will protect me from the water." Noah said, "There is no preserver today from the command of Allah."* (Quran 11:42–43, Sahih International)

*The book *The Defense** had read the verse as *evidence* that *Noah was the first to establish a guardian*, because *the son of Noah did not know the rank of the guardian, and if Adam had had a guardian, his son would have known about it*. The argument: the *son's ignorance* of the *wali-rank* shows that *the wali-rank was newly established by Noah*, and Adam therefore had no *wali*.

The author turns the verse against the objector by *internal inconsistency*. *This statement of his contradicts what he himself said in the chapter on cycles (al-adwar): that the imams took up the cycle of Adam after him. I do not know how this escaped him. I think that the copy of the book has been altered and added to, and that those who undertake the matter of religion are negligent in correcting what they write by listening and reading it back to the people of knowledge and religion, and Allah is the helper.* The the book *The Defense*-copy contradicts itself; the author suspects copyist-error or *adversary-inserted* doctrine, as he did at the close of sub-chapter nineteen.

But the deeper move is the *taʾwil* of the *son of Noah* itself. *The interpretation of "the son of Noah" is that the son attributed to Noah was not from his loins, but rather was a man of great worldly station whom he presented, like the opposite (al-mukhalif) in the cycle of Muhammad, our prophet. He benefited from Noah, learned what he had, and through his own influence became his "son." He took it upon himself, and people too imagined that he was his guardian.* The *son of Noah* is not biological; he is the *opposite* who learns from the prophet, takes the appearance of being his son, and is mistakenly imagined to be his guardian. The type recurs across cycles; in the cycle of Muhammad , the same figure recurs.

The narrative unfolds. *The command of Allah Almighty came to make the ark, that is, the matter of Noah, for the guardianship was conferred upon another, not him, in accordance with what Allah had commanded him.* The building of the ark is the appointment of the true *wali*, the one Allah had commanded, not the one the people had imagined. *He called the people who followed him to enter under his command and to obey him; and that is when he said, "Do not pour into it." So this man stopped what others had entered into, namely obedience to his foundation, by his refusal to enter the ark.*

The *opposite* (al-mukhalif) is not a stranger to Noah; he is a former follower who has *separated himself* (Quran 11:42 uses the verb *iʿtazala*, to set oneself apart). *Noah said that the command of Allah was upon him if he did not relinquish his presidency to him, otherwise it would be nullified, the opposite occurred with our guardian. He admonished him at times and warned him at other times, saying: "The command of Allah is upon it, and if you do not yield to it, your presidency will go, and humiliation will overtake you. You will see no one who pretends to be your equal, nor any helper in your affairs", which is what is meant by his saying: "And there is no preserver today from the command of Allah."*

The son's reply, *"I will take refuge in a mountain that will protect me from the water"*, is read on the *daʿwa*'s political *taʾwil*. *He said: "He has no knowledge of this. This will not be done to you, that you should make the entire presidency in your house. I am supported by chiefs who have strengthened my hand and helped me to ward off humiliation from me." The water here refers to that which divides itself against the force that destroys, and the mountain stands for what supports him of men and partisans. So he intended this and took up his case with his ilk, and they perished in his cycle, and there remained only those who held fast to obedience to his foundation.*

The mountain is the partisans; the water is the cleansing. The apparent flood of *Surat Hud* is read on its *batini* layer as the cleansing of the daʿwa from the opposite-and-his-partisans. *Allah has mentioned such news in His Noble Book as a warning, sometimes through reference to the she-camel of Salih; sometimes through the son of Noah, to make known that the conditions of Muhammad's cycle are like the conditions of the aforementioned cycles. This is a custom carried out in the case of all the prophets and guardians, until the time of the seventh. The retreat of the son of Noah was to the people of the apparent who were in a similar state of hypocrisy. He sought their help, but "the sword of the uncle" was by the power of Allah, and Allah cleansed the earth of them. That is the flood that spread outwardly and inwardly. Only one who is reckoned among the people of the Alawi daʿwa knows of it. Allah is its light.*

## The closing settlement and the bridge to Chapter Ten

The fourteen sub-chapters of the second half have settled the architectural shape of the chain that runs through human history. The *work* of the speakers is *not only for monotheism* but for the *correction of the soul* toward the second perfection. *I know what you do not know* names the cosmic turn by which knowledge emerges from potency at the end of the cycle of ignorance. Adam's *three sections of call* all carry parables with both apparent meaning and interpretation, and the apparent meaning is itself the Shariʿah. The *Qa'im* completes rather than abrogates: the ranks of the limits pass away, but the laws and ordinances of works stand. The *high limits* attain *monotheism* by innovation, not signs and works; the book *The Defense*'s argument from the high limits fails. The *seven lines and six aradas* graph the speaker-cycles, not the higher letters, and the cycle admits both a major (speakers) and a minor (imams) reading. The *Adam-letters* argument refutes itself by internal inconsistency. The *ark of Noah* carries both readings, the apparent Shariʿah (the book *The Correction*) and the daʿwa-with-the-wali (the book *The Defense*), neither dispensing with the other. *Every speaker has a guardian*, Adam had Seth, Noah his wali, and on through the chain. And the *son of Noah* is the type of the *opposite* who learns from the prophet, is imagined to be his guardian, separates himself when the true wali is appointed, takes refuge in the mountain of his partisans, and is washed away in the cleansing flood that spreads outwardly and inwardly. The type recurs in the cycle of Muhammad .

The bridge to *Chapter Ten* is the architectural question that follows: if the chain runs through speaker-and-guardian pairs at every cycle, with opposites recurring at every joint, *what is at the very head of the chain, above the First Speaker, above the First Intellect, above the universal Soul*? *Chapter Ten* takes up *monotheism and the critique of the book *The Harvest**, the author's settling of the nature of the First Creator against the philosophical doctrines of the *Muʿtazila*, of *Jonathan*, of *Samuel*, and of the lost-work the book *The Harvest* itself. The architectural settlement of *Chapter Nine*, speakers and guardians at every cycle, with the cleansing of the opposite at every joint, is the ground on which *Chapter Ten*'s settlement of *monotheism at the head of the chain* will stand.

> *The chain that runs in time has a head, and the head has a Lord. The Lord is not the head; the Lord is above the head. The First Intellect knows the Lord by innovation; the universal Soul receives the head's writing; the speakers carry the writing into the world; the guardians preserve the writing in the community; the opposites are washed away in the cleansing of every cycle; and at the seventh, the Qa'im completes the roles and closes the door of teaching. This is the architecture of the chain that runs in time. And above the head is the Lord, of whom no description, no attribute, no opposition is permitted, the Originator of the Originated, the Innovator of the Innovated. Beyond Him there is nothing but Him.* — the author, *Rahat al-ʿAql* (*The Repose of the Intellect*), the rank of the First Creator at the head of the chain
