# Qada and Qadar, Fate and Destiny

*The visual companion to Chapter Eleven. The audio chapter is the prose; this file is its argument made structural. Same concepts, same depth, restructured for slide-deck rendering. Chapter Eight of* Kitab al-Riyad *contains twenty-four sub-chapters; this deck restructures them into named diagrams.*

## Where this chapter picks up

A directed handoff from *Chapter Seven*. Chapter Seven settled the architecture of the world's sections and the human's place within them. Chapter Eight asks the next question: how does that settled architecture run *in time*?

Settled from Chapter Seven:

- The world divides into many more sections than four
- Generated beings divide into three genera: mineral, vegetable, animal
- The human is a fourth-by-distinction inside the animal genus
- The strict pair of humankind is the angels (for prophets, guardians, imams, and their followers)
- The human stands in a *between*-state: matter from below, image from above
- Delivered into rank by *the limits of the call*

Inherited as the new question:

- The chain has a *prior* end (First Intellect, the Pen, the Throne)
- The chain has a *posterior* end (the receiver in human form, the *reassured soul*)
- Which is *qada* (decree)? Which is *qadar* (destiny)?
- What does each word mean at the metaphysical heights, at the rank of the speaker-Prophet, and at the rank of the world unfolding in time?

## Sub-chapters one and two, destiny is estimation, decree is detail; the two tailor-analogies

A contrast pair. The two reformers agree on the *form* of the dispute (prior-and-posterior) and disagree on the *content* (which is which). Both stay inside the world of the body.

Column A: The book *The Correction*, destiny precedes decree

- Definition of destiny (*qadar*): estimation
- Definition of decree (*qada*): detail
- Philological evidence: *the matter has been decreed* means *separated and finished*; *when you have finished the prayer* means *completed it*
- Verb-form of *qada*: concluding, separating, finishing
- The analogy: a tailor first *estimates* the garment
- Estimating phase: the garment can be *increased, decreased, expanded, or narrowed*
- After the cut: the garment is *finished*; increase is no longer possible
- Mapping: garment-in-estimating-phase = *qadar*; garment-after-cut = *qada*
- Order: estimation comes first; detail comes after
- Verdict: destiny precedes decree

Column B: The book *The Defense*, decree precedes destiny

- Definition of decree (*qada*): the spiritual image of the sewing in the tailor's soul
- Definition of destiny (*qadar*): the estimation by which the tailor prepares to cut
- The book *The Correction*'s analogy is wrong on its own terms
- The tailor's *spiritual image of the sewing*, prior to estimation, is the *meaning of the image of originals*; does not yet emerge beyond the limit of intellect
- The estimation by which the tailor prepares to cut is the *posterior*
- Only then comes the *physical elaboration* (the cut)
- Mapping: spiritual-image-in-soul = *qada*; estimation-with-cloth = *qadar*
- Order: *sewing precedes appreciation, and by that sewing the tailor is prepared for the appreciation*
- Verdict: decree precedes destiny

Shared row: both agree the dispute is about *prior and posterior*. Both build from the bodily-tailor analogy. Both believe in *al-qadar, the good and the evil of it* as the *hadith of Jibreel* names it. Neither rises to the *three-rank* architecture the author will land.

The author's verdict that closes the first two sub-chapters together:

> The interpretation of the two contenders concerning decree and destiny, according to what each of them has invoked, is in any case: that decree and destiny are the prior and the posterior. Yet the dispute, taken at the architectural heights the chapter will reach, cannot stand on the bodily reading alone.
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Eight, sub-chapter two.

## Sub-chapters three through five, the three meanings of decree

A hierarchy tree. The first rank is the highest; each lower rank is named, ordered, and contrasted with the others.

- Level 1: First meaning of decree, the *speaker-Prophet* who is *judge*
  - Carrier: the *speaker-prophet* and *those who take his place* (the *foundation*, the *guardian*)
  - Mechanism: in their act, command, prohibition, and judgment, the call already in existence is brought from potency into act
  - Identity: *identical with the meaning indicated by the word "judge"*
  - This rank IS *al-qada* at its highest

- Level 2: Second meaning of decree, the *bringing-out by the inner interpretation*
  - Carrier: revelation and Shariʿah, with their inner sciences in potency
  - Mechanism: the inner sciences are taken into act in the soul of the receiver by *the inner interpretation*
  - Receiver: souls *taken out of the limit of potency into the limit of act*
  - This is what the book *The Correction*'s reading partly captures

- Level 3: Third meaning of decree, the *metaphysical fixity of the world's laws*
  - Carrier: the laws of motion and stillness
  - Mechanism: events whose impossibility-of-reversal is *estimated in the world*
  - Boundary: things that *admit no reversal*
  - When violated: the act is named *something requiring recompense*
  - This rank governs the world from below, not from above

The three decrees stand at three ranks: highest is the *speaker-Prophet*; middle is the *bringing-out by the inner interpretation*; lowest is the *metaphysical fixity*. Three ranks of *qada*.

## Sub-chapters six through eight, the three meanings of destiny

A hierarchy tree, mirroring the *qada* tree above. The chain runs the same ranks at both ends.

- Level 1: First meaning of destiny, the *promised Qaʾim*
  - Carrier: the awaited one promised by Adam, the speaker-Prophets, the Imams, and the Messengers during the *period of concealment*
  - Mechanism: he is *destined to come out from estimation to action and existence*
  - Anchor: *the chapter on the night of power* (*Laylat al-Qadr*), the night of destiny
  - The *speakers of the call* reach for this name when they speak of the awaited Qaʾim

- Level 2: Second meaning of destiny, the *revelation and Shariʿah*
  - Carrier: what the Prophet brought from revelation: ruling, parables, sciences, knowledge
  - Function: what the soul needs *in its passage to maturity and action*
  - Outer face (*zahir*): measured, limited, appointed
  - Inner face (*batin*): reached only by *the inner interpretation*
  - This is the rank in which the book *The Correction* was partly right about *qadar* preceding *qada*

- Level 3: Third meaning of destiny, the *world unfolding in time*
  - Carrier: days, months, years, events, worlds
  - Mechanism: *what is in its power to come out into the universe and into action upon the passage*
  - Necessity: *what Allah has decreed will be, for it is predestined being in terms of the necessity of its existence*
  - Anchor: *They see it as far, and We see it as near* (Quran 70:6-7)

> *They see it as far, and We see it as near.*
> Quran 70:6-7.

The three destinies stand at three ranks: highest is the *promised Qaʾim*; middle is the *revelation and Shariʿah*; lowest is the *world unfolding in time*. Three ranks of *qadar*, mirroring the three ranks of *qada*. The chain runs the same ranks at both ends.

The Prophetic anchor that names belief in *al-qadar* as a constitutive pillar of faith comes from the *hadith of Jibreel*. The angel asked the Prophet to define faith.

> That you believe in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and that you believe in *al-qadar*, the good of it and the evil of it.
> Sahih Muslim, Book 1, Hadith 1; narrated by the second caliph.

The reformers' dispute is not whether to believe in *al-qadar* but how the architecture by which the pillar holds together is rendered. The author lands the partial-rightness verdict:

| Reformer | Where he was right | Where he failed |
|---|---|---|
| The book *The Correction* (destiny precedes decree) | Correct only in the rank of *revelation and Shariʿah* (which precedes the judgment that is *the inner interpretation*) | Failed to distinguish the three ranks; took the bodily analogy as the whole truth |
| The book *The Defense* (decree precedes destiny) | Correct only in the rank of the *speaker-Prophet*, the *already-existing one* (which is the *advanced elimination* of what came from the revelation and Shariʿah) | Failed to distinguish the three ranks; inverted the order at the lower ranks |

## Sub-chapter nine, the First Intellect is not the assembly of the images

A contrast pair correcting the book *The Defense*'s premise about the intellect.

The book *The Defense* cited the wisdom-tradition: *when Allah Almighty created the intellect, He completed the worlds, because it is the assembly of the images of the worlds*. From this he derived: decree-as-spiritual-image precedes destiny-as-estimation.

Column A: The book *The Defense*'s implied intellect

- Identity: the intellect that holds all the images
- Position: identified with the First Intellect
- Function: already contains all that will be
- Consequence: decree-as-spiritual-image precedes destiny-as-estimation

Column B: The author's correction

- Identity: the intellect that *ascends from the world of nature* by acquiring work and knowledge
- Position: NOT the First Intellect; NOT the second emanated; it is the *intellect-by-acquisition*
- Function: gathers the images of both worlds by the rank of receivership reached
- Carriers: the souls of the *speakers, foundations, imams, hujjas*, *the limits of the call*

Shared row: both agree there *is* an intellect that holds the images of the worlds. The dispute is which intellect.

The author's settled architecture:

| Rank | Name | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Highest | The First Intellect | Pure unity in self-knowing alone; abstracted from need; does not intellect anything other than itself, because by virtue of being in its own self of eternity, completeness, subsistence, and unity, there is no need for that |
| Below it | The universal Soul | Receives the gift of the images by way of the limits |
| Below the Soul | The intellects-by-acquisition | The souls of the speakers, foundations, supports, gather the images of both worlds by the rank of receivership reached |

> The first creative intellect is a pure unity in self-knowing alone, abstracted from need; below it the universal Soul receives the gift of the images by way of the limits, and the souls of the speakers and the executors and the supports are the intellects-by-acquisition that gather the images of both worlds in themselves by the rank of receivership they have reached.
> The author's other treatise, *The Repose of the Intellect*, on the rank of the First Intellect and the rank of the acquired intellect.

The First Intellect is *pure unity self-knowing alone*. The intellect that is *the assembly of the images of the worlds* is the *intellect-by-acquisition* gathered by the *limits of the call*. The book *The Defense*'s premise is corrected at its root.

## Sub-chapters ten through fifteen, *We created every thing according to a measure*, and the Pen-Tablet-Writer architecture

An annotated structure. The verse:

> Indeed, We have created everything according to a measure.
> Quran 54:49.

The book *The Correction*'s reading: the verse names *qadar* as the *posterior*; all essences emanate from the precedent predestining; emanation IS *qadar*; *qada* is what is *done* (the book of which Allah says *Allah erases what He wills and confirms*, Quran 13:39).

The book *The Defense*'s exegesis takes each word of the verse and lands the *Pen-Tablet-Writer* architecture.

The architecture as named structure:

| Element of the verse | Element of the architecture | Rank | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| *We* | The Writer | The face beyond the Intellect | Pure eternity; the subject not tainted by description or attribute |
| *every thing* | The intelligible letters | The totality of things | The intellect, the totality of things; the upper and lower images |
| *We have created it* | The Pen writes upon the Tablet | The First Intellect upon the universal Soul | The Pen is the Pen of the Intellect, the *Pen of the pen* |
| *the Tablet* (implied) | The universal Soul | The rank that receives | The *preserved tablet* (*al-lawh*), the rank that receives the writing of the Pen |
| *according to a measure* | The Measure | The receptive rank in front of the Tablet | What is *receptive to all the forms of the letters*; the rank at which the writing is unfolded into all the spiritual and physical images |

The architecture in compact form:

```
Writer (face of "We", pure eternity)
   ↓ writes by means of
Pen (the First Intellect)
   ↓ upon
Tablet (the universal Soul)
   ↓ unfolding into
Measure (the receptive rank, in front of the Tablet, receptive to all the forms of the letters)
   ↓ as
All the upper and lower images, the spiritual and the physical, the simple and the dense
```

The author's verdict in sub-chapters thirteen through fifteen:

| Claim | Status |
|---|---|
| The book *The Correction*'s syllogism that *qadar* must be the name of the posterior | The verse's chronology-in-the-text does not force *qadar* = posterior |
| The book *The Defense*'s Pen-Tablet-Writer architecture | Stands as the metaphysical reading of the verse |
| The book *The Defense*'s claim that *We* = pure non-attribution | Needs more care; *We* is only to the extent that the Prophet derives, by establishing his rank in the world |
| Settled reading of *qada* | The act of the speaker who has gone out into action and obtained perfection with the support of Allah |
| Settled reading of *qadar* | The emanation by which the physical world was determined to be something of it |

The chain runs: *speaker-Prophet → revelation and Shariʿah → world unfolding in time*. The Pen-Tablet-Writer architecture gives the metaphysical heights at which the verse's *measure* names the *receptive rank* on which the images of both worlds are written.

## Sub-chapters sixteen through nineteen, *Flee from Allah's qada to His qadar*, and the three meanings

A 2x2 named-axis matrix organizing the four ways the Prophetic word has been read.

The Prophetic word:

> Flee from Allah's qada to His qadar.

The historical anchor in Sahih the canonical hadith compiler: the second caliph on his way to Sham learned of plague at Sargh; he announced return to Madina; *an early companion ibn the early companion's father* objected, *Are you fleeing from Allah's qada?* Umar replied:

> Yes, we flee from Allah's qada to Allah's qadar. Do you not see that if you had camels and they descended into a valley with two slopes, one fertile and one barren, you would graze them on the fertile slope by Allah's qadar, and you would graze them on the barren slope also by Allah's qadar?
> Sahih the canonical hadith compiler, Book 76, Hadith 5729; narrated by Abdullah ibn Abbas.

Abd the chapter on the Merciful ibn 'Awf then arrived with the Prophetic word:

> If you hear of the plague in a land, do not enter it; and if it falls in a land while you are in it, do not flee from it.
> Sahih the canonical hadith compiler, Book 76, Hadith 5729; narrated by Abd the chapter on the Merciful ibn 'Awf.

Axes for the 2x2:

- Horizontal: Reading direction, *from preceding to next* (left) vs *from next to preceding* (right)
- Vertical: Carrier of the flight, *Prophet at composition rank* (top) vs *general flight image* (bottom)

Quadrants:

| | From preceding to next | From next to preceding |
|---|---|---|
| **Prophet at composition rank** | The book *The Defense*'s third meaning (the most honorable): the speaker flees the *intensity of the preceding support, under which even the Jadd nearly fades* to the *next support* by which the composition of the Shariʿah is laid out. *Author's verdict: sustainable.* | The book *The Defense*'s first meaning: the *preceding* is the support of the First Intellect; the *next* is the composition of the Shariʿah; flight is from intensity to composition. *Author's verdict: not aligned with what qada and qadar mean; crosses the natural joints.* |
| **General flight image** | The book *The Correction*'s literal: as a man flees from fire to water, flight from the *decreed-finished* to the *still-being-measured*; the contextual image is the *leaning wall, the limit-in-danger whom the Prophet sets upright by the speed of his walking past*. *Author's verdict: the stronger reading.* | The book *The Defense*'s second meaning: *his nation's media*, the Prophet benefits from the first by means of the second; flees to the second rank because the second rank is *how the first rank speaks back into the world*. *Author's verdict: not aligned; distinguishes First and Second Intellect rather than qada and qadar.* |

The book *The Correction*'s contextual image, *the leaning wall*:

> And they found in it a wall on the verge of collapse, so he set it up.
> Quran 18:77.

The book *The Correction*'s *the inner interpretation* of the verse:

| Element | Reading |
|---|---|
| The wall on the verge of collapse | The limit upon whom apostasy and hypocrisy had inclined |
| The Prophet hastens past | If he apostatized, he fell from his rank, the Prophet's *speed of walking* is the pre-emptive correction |
| The setting upright of the wall | The limit-in-danger is held in place |
| The Prophet's address | To those given to hypocrisy with *preaching and warning, in the outward form without the inward statement* |
| The flight from *qada* to *qadar* | From the decreed-finished (outward Shariʿah by which the hypocrite is held in place) to the still-being-measured (inward *the inner interpretation* by which the receiver is raised) |

Author's settled position: the third meaning (intensity of the preceding support to the next) is sustainable; the book *The Correction*'s contextual image of the *leaning wall, the limit-in-danger* is the *stronger reading* of the situation. The first two meanings of the book *The Defense* cross the natural joints of the doctrine.

## Sub-chapter twenty, Moses, Shuʿayb, and the politics of timing

A process flow that re-reads the verse on Moses's request to see the Lord as a political *the inner interpretation* about the agency to lift an affliction at the wrong time.

The verse:

> My Lord, show me, that I may look upon You. He said, "You will not see Me, but look at the mountain; if it settles in its place, you will see Me." And when his Lord appeared to the mountain, He made it Dhaka (crumbled to dust). And Moses fell down struck by a thunderbolt. When he recovered, he said, "Glory be to You, I repent to You."
> Quran 7:143.

The author refuses the book *The Correction* and the book *The Defense*'s readings (Moses asking to see a rank above his own; Moses ignorant of the upper limits) and gives the political *the inner interpretation*.

The flow of the verse on the political *the inner interpretation*:

```
Start: Moses is with the upper borders but addressing one of the borders before reaching the rank of pronunciation
   ↓
The people of truth are under affliction, covering, and concealment from the side of opposites and violators
   ↓
Moses sees in himself a power he reckons he could use in his time
   ↓
He asks the Author of the Command (Shuʿayb, his Lord-in-the-matter) for agency to lift the affliction by the policy of truth
   ↓ Shuʿayb responds:
"You will not see Me do any of that, nor will those in this time. For everything has its time and season, and this is not its time."
   ↓
"But look at the mountain", the mountain is a man who has worldly power, equipment, and capability
   ↓
The mountain (the political instrument) is commanded to erect a beacon of truth
   ↓
The mountain crumbled (Dhaka): the matter narrowed upon the people, they did not accept the command, they split the staff, the matter was confused, the mountain's presidency was about to vanish
   ↓
Moses fell struck by thunderbolt: realizing what he was destined to accomplish at that time was destined to be impossible
   ↓
Moses recovered: bewildered by the warning
   ↓
Moses said: "Glory be to You, I repent to You", he retracted his statement, regretted, and was patient
   ↓
End: when that period elapsed, the time came, and Moses was given what he had asked for, in the right time
```

The mapping of verse-elements to political *the inner interpretation*:

| Verse element | Inner reading |
|---|---|
| *My Lord* | The Author of the Command, Shuʿayb, who is his Lord in the matter |
| *Show me, that I may look upon You* | Command me, that I may manage this matter and reveal the grief |
| *You will not see Me* | You will not see Me do this in this time, the time is not ripe |
| *Look at the mountain* | Look at this man of worldly power and capability |
| *If it settles in its place, you will see Me* | If the political instrument is fixed in what it has been commanded, you will see the agency |
| *He made it Dhaka (crumbled to dust)* | The matter narrowed upon the people; they did not accept the command; the staff was split; the political presidency was about to vanish |
| *Moses fell, struck by thunderbolt* | Moses realized what was destined to be impossible at this time |
| *I repent to You* | He retracted, regretted, was patient with what Shuʿayb had ordered |
| *When that period elapsed* | The time came, and Moses received what he had been right about |

Quranic anchor that names the *the inner interpretation*-arc of the verse:

> We will show them Our signs in the horizons and in their own selves until it becomes clear to them that it is the truth.
> Quran 41:53.

The mapping:

- *Signs in the horizons*: the universes whose movement in time reveals the decree; the *qadar of the world unfolding in time* showed Moses that *the qada of his agency was not yet ripe*
- *Signs in their own selves*: the souls in their passage from potency to act
- Destiny showed Moses the limits of decree: *qada and qadar at the rank of the speaker and his time*

## Sub-chapters twenty-one and twenty-two, *the mind is all things, all things are the mind* refused

A contrast pair correcting the book *The Defense*'s confusion of cause and effect.

The book *The Defense*'s exegetical claim about *the creation of every thing*: *the mind is all things, and all things are the mind*. From this he derived his reading of *qadar* as the *posterior emanation* of *all things from the precedent mind*.

Column A: The book *The Defense*'s claim

- Identity-statement: *the mind is all things, and all things are the mind*
- Implication: the physical world IS the mind; the mind IS the physical world
- Consequence: *qadar* = posterior emanation of *all things* from the precedent mind
- The reading collapses the cause and the effect into one

Column B: The author's correction

- Identity-statement: *the intellect is the cause of all things; all things proceed from the intellect and toward its purpose*
- Implication: the physical world is *a thing*, not a *mind*; the mind is *the cause*, not the thing
- The Intellect is *identical with the command of Allah*; the cause of the existence of all things, physical and non-physical
- The chain: First Intellect → universal Soul → seven heavens, four elements, three genera, human being
- The Intellect is NOT the things; the things are NOT the Intellect

Shared row: both agree the Intellect has some priority. The dispute is whether priority is *identity* (the book *The Defense*) or *causation* (the author).

> The First Intellect is the originated which is the command of Allah; from it the universal Soul flows by emanation; from the Soul the seven heavens, the four elements, the three genera, and the human being descend by the chain of receivership; the Intellect is not the things and the things are not the Intellect, the Intellect is the cause whose work the chain of receivership unfolds.
> The author's other treatise, on the chain from First Intellect to human being.

The architectural correction: the *Pen* is the *Intellect that writes upon the Tablet*; the *things* are *what the writing unfolds into*; the *Intellect* is NOT what the things *are*.

## Sub-chapter twenty-three, *no blame upon those who believe* is not a license to abandon the Shariʿah

A contrast pair correcting the book *The Defense*'s most dangerous exegetical move, with the alternative *the inner interpretation* laid out as three conditions.

The verse:

> There is no blame upon those who believe and do righteous deeds for what they have eaten, so long as they fear Allah and believe and do righteous deeds; then fear Him and believe; then fear Him and do good, and Allah loves the doers of good.
> Quran 5:93.

Column A: The book *The Defense*'s reading, the dangerous gloss

- Reading: Allah has permitted the Prophets the inner meanings of the laws and has dispensed with their literal use
- Effect: a license to abandon the canons of the Shariʿah for those who have reached the inner meaning
- The reading the *batiniyya* critics have always charged the *the call* with

Column B: The author's reading, the alternative *the inner interpretation*

- Reading: the verse contains no mention of permission to abandon the Shariʿah
- Anchor doctrine from Chapter Seven: souls attain happiness only by *both worships* (the *zahir* and the *batin*), work and knowledge
- Whichever soul abandons both, or even one of them, is *far from attaining perfection*
- The verse is a license to *the preachers of the call* to consider sciences not of their own creed *as a weapon*
- The three repeated injunctions of the verse are *three conditions* on that license

Shared row: both agree the verse permits something. The dispute is *what* is permitted and *to whom*.

The author's three conditions on the license:

| Condition | The verse's repeat | What it requires |
|---|---|---|
| One, Straightness | *Fear Allah and believe and do righteous deeds* | The preacher's path is straight in obedience to Allah, in following His Messenger, in belief in His limits, and in establishment of both acts of worship in accordance with what He says |
| Two, Obedience to the Messenger and the deputy | *Then fear Him and believe* | Obey the Messenger in what he commands, and the one in charge after him in his place; call to them outwardly and inwardly |
| Three, The Imam's leadership | *Then fear Him and do good* | Obey the Imam in the one whom he establishes as a deputy; do well in calling to the monotheism of Allah under his leadership |

Mapping of the verse's elements on the author's reading:

| Element of the verse | Author's reading |
|---|---|
| *Those who believe and do righteous deeds* | The preachers of the call to the monotheism of Allah |
| *For what they have eaten* | What they study from sciences not of their own creed |
| *No blame* | No constraint; permitted to *consider* other-tradition sciences |
| The function of the studied sciences | *Like a weapon with which they fight opponents in proving the truth* |
| The three repeats of *fear, believe, do good* | The three conditions of straightness, obedience, and the Imam's leadership |

The Fatimid synthesis of the doctrine of the two worships:

> We are the ones of whom Allah said *those of His servants who believe and do righteous deeds*. Whoever takes us as guides, his guidance is the guidance of Allah; whoever abandons us, his work is in vain. The two worships are the inheritance from the Prophet, the *zahir* is the body of the religion, the *batin* is its spirit; he who keeps the body and abandons the spirit, his religion is dead; he who claims the spirit and abandons the body, his religion is a lie.
> Imam the Fatimid caliph Ma'add ibn Isma'il, *the Fatimid caliph's interpretive treatise*.

The *zahir* and the *batin* are *body and spirit*; the abandonment of either is *not religion*. The verse is no license to abandon either.

## Sub-chapter twenty-four, the three-position order: decree at the beginning, apportioning in the middle, completion at the end

A timeline of the chain at its full extent. The dispute has been driving toward this order since sub-chapter one.

The book *The Defense*'s formulation that the author lands as the chapter's settled order:

> The decree (*qada*) is the beginning, the apportioning (*qadar*) is the middle, and completion is the end, because apportioning can only proceed by the decree. If the decree is the end, it is not right to call it a decree, because the decree comes before all else, and things come after the decree, and the decree is for a thing. Perfection is not for nothing; rather, perfection is for the very thing that becomes perfect.

The three positions in order, mapped at the metaphysical heights and at the chain's full extent:

| Position | Name | Rank at metaphysical heights | Rank at chain's full extent |
|---|---|---|---|
| Beginning | *Qada*, decree | The Pen that writes upon the Tablet | The *speaker-Prophet* who is *judge* in whose person the call in potency is brought into act |
| Middle | *Qadar*, apportioning | The Measure in front of the Tablet, receptive to all the forms of the letters | The *revelation and Shariʿah* brought by the Prophet in measured form, so that the receiving soul may come out in interpretation to what it deserves |
| End | Completion | The image fully unfolded into the spiritual and physical worlds | The *reassured soul* (*the soul al-mutma'inna*) that returns to its Lord, pleased and pleasing |

The verse that names the completion at the end:

> O reassured soul, return to your Lord, pleased and pleasing; enter among My servants; enter My Paradise.
> Quran 89:27-30.

The verse, first surfaced in Chapter Seven at the seal of the *the inner interpretation* of Bandhaqlis on the speaking soul and the bestial soul, is now the *completion at the end* of Chapter Eight's three-position order.

The personal-supplication parallel from Imam the fourth Imam, the survivor of Karbala, in *Sahifa the supplications*:

Difficult matters are made easy by Your power, and means are made smooth by Your kindness, and what is decreed proceeds by Your strength, and things run on toward Your will.
> Imam the fourth Imam, Sahifa the supplications, Duʿa 7 (His Supplication When a Worrisome Task Befell Him).

## The Imam's canonical warning

A visual metaphor that closes the chapter, *qada* and *qadar* as a dark path, a deep ocean, a secret. The aphorism is the Commander of the Faithful's verdict on the very dispute the chapter has been adjudicating.

The aphorism:

> It is a dark path, do not tread it. It is a deep ocean, do not enter it. It is the secret of Allah, so do not burden yourself with it.
> Imam the Commander of the Faithful, *the book The Path of Eloquence*, Aphorism 287.

The metaphor as concentric layers, from outside in:

```
Outermost layer: the dark path, qada at the head, qadar at the rank of the receiver
   The veils are real; the depth is real; the path is unlit to the rationalism that would lift the veils alone

Next layer in: the deep ocean, the metaphysical depth where even the Jadd nearly fades
   The intensity of the support that overwhelms the speaker who tries to seize it alone
   The book The Correction with his tailor-analogy and the book The Defense with his identification of mind and things both nearly burned at this depth

Innermost layer: the secret of Allah, what is to be received from the chain, not solved by the intellect alone
   Lifted only by the lamp the chain has been given
   The work of the limits of the call: the speaker-Prophet, the foundation, the imam, the hujjah, the daʿi, the maʾdhun, the mukasir
```

The Imam's warning is *not* a prohibition on the doctrine of *qada* and *qadar*; it is the prohibition on the *reckless rationalism* that would lift the veils without the lamp.

The two ways of approaching *qada* and *qadar*:

| With the chain | Without the chain |
|---|---|
| The dark path is given the lamp by which it becomes legible | The path remains dark |
| The deep ocean is crossed by the carriers whose composition holds | The deep ocean drowns the unprepared |
| The secret is received from the rank that was given | The secret consumes whoever tries to seize it alone |

|---|---|---|---|
| First (highest) | The *speaker-Prophet* who is *judge*; in whose person the call in potency is brought into act | The *promised Qaʾim*; the awaited fulfillment of the chain; *the chapter on the night of power* | Writer (face of "We") → Pen (First Intellect) |
