# Slide Deck Framing, EP06: Soul and Spirit, One Substance or Two?

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio episode. They have followed the chain through *Chapters One* and *Two* and now meet the chapter that places the Second and prime matter in their ranks.

## Core Principle

Each slide must add visual information the audio cannot carry. Slides render the chain of ranks as one diagram and show where the author refuses body-categories the book *The Defense* imports into the supra-natural realm.

## Visual Priorities

- Soul, spirit, and prime matter as three ranks of one chain, rendered as a comparison matrix.
- The settled chain (First, Second, prime matter, nature) as a directed diagram, with the operation at each step named: emit, impress, inform.
- The two-aspects doctrine as an annotated structure of one Soul with two relations, never two halves of a divided substance.
- The "no parts, no sides, no diameters" refutation as a side-by-side table of body-language versus rank-language.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a sub-chapter title.
- No literal illustrations of light, darkness, mosques, scholar portraits, or calligraphy.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams; every diagram has axes, nodes, or rows.
- No slides that restate a sentence the audio already speaks.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide must add visual information not present in the audio.
- For the central chain, show a directed diagram with the operation at each step named.
- Contrast the two rival pictures as columns, attribute by attribute.
- Place the higher rank to the left in every pair; maintain across the deck.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
