# Slide Deck Framing, EP09: Motion, Stillness, Prime Matter, and Form

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio episode. They have not read *The Correction*, *The Defense*, or *The Harvest*. The deck must do what the audio cannot.

## Core Principle

Each slide must add visual information the audio cannot carry. Slides exist to make motion-and-stillness as opposites stand against *prime matter*-and-form as inseparable correlates, and to show where motion-and-stillness vocabulary stops reaching above the First Effect.

## Visual Priorities

- The contrast between motion-and-stillness as opposites and form-and-*prime matter* as inseparable correlates, side by side, attribute by attribute.
- The *kun* analogy mapped to cause-toward-below and effect-toward-above, with the first-and-by-accident qualification visible.
- The hierarchy of ranks above the First Effect, showing where body-talk stops reaching.
- The nine-section body against the three-section motion as the category mismatch *The Defense's* parallel rested on.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a chapter or sub-chapter title.
- No literal illustrations: no calligraphic *kun*, no mosque silhouettes, no scholar portraits.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams. Every diagram has axes, nodes, columns, or named rows.
- No slides that restate one sentence the audio already speaks.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide must add visual information not present in the audio.
- For motion-and-stillness against form-and-*prime matter*, use a contrast pair attribute by attribute.
- Place the higher-rank entity to the left in every pair; hold the convention from EP01.
- Avoid stock-photo imagery. Every slide is a diagram or it does not exist.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
