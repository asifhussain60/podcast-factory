# Souls: Parts of the First Truths, or Only Traces?

*The visual companion to Chapter Four. The audio chapter is the prose; this file is its argument made structural. Same eight sub-chapters, same triangulation, restructured for slide-deck rendering.*

## Where this chapter picks up

A three-rank handoff from Chapter Three to Chapter Four. The architecture set in the previous chapter is carried into the question this chapter answers.

| Rank | Established in Chapter Three | Question Chapter Four asks |
|---|---|---|
| First Intellect | The source of the chain; one self called by two names (Intellect, Word) | Not in question here |
| Universal Soul (the Second) | Like-counterpart of the First; the mineral of honor, perfection, and elevation | Not in question here |
| Prime matter | Trace of the Second; substrate of the lower world, impressed by power | Settled in Chapter Three |
| Speaking souls (us) | Inhabit the natural composite | Parts of the First Truths? Traces? Something else? |

The question is not idle. The integrity of the entire *the call* architecture rests on what kind of relation the partial souls stand in to the rank above them.

## The Quranic anchor running beneath the chapter

> *They are asking thee concerning the Spirit. Say: The Spirit is by command of my Lord, and of knowledge ye have been vouchsafed but little.*
> Quran 17:85, Pickthall translation.

The verse positions the soul at the rank Chapter Four will adjudicate.

| Verse phrase | What it anchors for the chapter |
|---|---|
| *by command of my Lord* | The soul belongs to the order of the divine command, not to the order of bodies |
| *of knowledge ye have been vouchsafed but little* | The relation is real but hard to name; the book *The Correction* reaches for "trace," the book *The Defense* reaches for "parts," the author will reach for "second emanation" |

## The two reformers and the stake underneath

A contrast pair. The dispute looks, on its surface, like a vocabulary disagreement. The stake underneath is the integrity of the whole *the call* architecture.

Column A: the book *The Correction* (the late Jonathan)

- Position: souls are *traces* of the first essence, not parts of it
- Why "traces": the things that are integral, that can be broken into parts in the body-sense, are bodies; the soul is not a body
- What he was protecting: keep the soul outside body-talk; refuse to reduce the soul to the course of bodies
- Where it stumbles: "trace" risks being read as ephemeral, like prime matter, with no essence of its own

Column B: the book *The Defense* (his successor, Samuel)

- Position: souls are *parts* of the universal Soul; without parts the architecture collapses
- Why "parts": the soul's continuity with the rank above must be real if prophets are to receive
- What he was protecting: the soul's real reception of what comes from above; jewels existing by themselves, not mere accidents
- Where it stumbles: "parts" risks being read as composition, as pieces broken off, smuggling body-talk back into the higher ranks

Shared row: both agree the partial souls receive from the rank above. Both agree the relation is real. Both agree the stake is the integrity of prophethood, imamate, walaya, and revelation.

## The author's third word

The author's triangulation. Refuses each reformer's word taken alone; lands on a third word that holds both truths.

| What each reformer was protecting | The author's third word holds it by saying |
|---|---|
| *The Correction*: the soul is not a body | The soul is the *second emanation*, brought forth by emission, not by composition |
| *The Defense*: the soul has real continuity with what stands above | What is in the whole is found in the part *by rank, not by composition* |
| Both: the architecture of prophethood and imamate must stand | The architecture rests on the rank attained through the chain, not on the partness of the souls |

The third word: *second emanation*. The first emanation is the universal Soul itself; the speaking souls are the second emanation, emitted from the universal Soul by the same originating act under which the universal Soul was emitted from the First.

## Sub-chapter one: traces, but in what sense?

The opening sub-chapter as a three-position move. The book *The Correction* states; the book *The Defense* refuses; the author enters with his half-credit move.

| Position | Speaker | What is said | What it tries to do |
|---|---|---|---|
| Statement | the book *The Correction* | The parts in us are traces of the first essence, not parts of it; our union with the first is by effects, not by parts | Keep the soul outside body-talk |
| Refusal | the book *The Defense* | Confused and contradictory: how can parts be relics yet essences? If "parts" is deployed, they must be parts of an essence | Force the position into a clean choice |
| Half-credit | the author | The book *The Defense* is right that effects are not self-existent essences; the book *The Correction* is right that "parts" risked reducing the soul to body-status | Reframe both as guarding the same jewel from opposite directions |

The author's key clarification of what *The Correction* meant by "trace":

| What "trace" does NOT mean | What "trace" DOES mean here |
|---|---|
| The bare impression of the Second's power in a substrate that does not resemble it (which is prime matter) | Essences kept in being by what reaches them from the side of the *the limits* |
| Ephemeral marks | The soul is a real thing with its own subsistence |
| No essence of its own | The *the limits* convey the gift; the soul has its own essence, the limits give it its survival |

The closing aphorism that names the half-credit:

> *Your soul is a precious jewel; whoever guards it has elevated it, and whoever lets it slip has lowered it.*
> Imam the Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him, in the aphorisms of his tradition.

The soul is a jewel (the book *The Defense*'s word, granted). The soul's elevation depends on guarding (the book *The Correction*'s concern). Both reformers guarding the same jewel from opposite directions.

## Sub-chapter two: the book *The Defense*'s positive proposal

A single-position structure. The book *The Defense* lays out his claim. Not based on direct evidence; based on a dilemma.

The book *The Defense*'s claim:

- The souls are parts of the *total soul*
- "Total soul" means the universal Soul, the Second
- The argument: the universal Soul is the sum of the partial souls
- If the partial souls are not parts of the universal Soul, there is no universal Soul that they sum to
- The doctrine requires there to be one
- Therefore the partial souls must be parts of it

The whole stands or falls on a single question: can the universal Soul be construed as a whole composed of the partial souls?

## Sub-chapter three: the dilemma against parts

A trilemma. The author's formal refusal of the book *The Defense*'s positive proposal. The Second must be one of three things; each alternative is shown invalid.

| Alternative | What it claims | Why it is invalid |
|---|---|---|
| (a) The whole of them in the house of the world | The Second is the whole of the partial souls as they stand here below | In this world the soul stands by *potentiality*; the First Intellect refuses to be that by which the existing souls come out by potentiality into actuality, because the precedence required is impossible for God Almighty |
| (b) Some in this world, some in the higher world | Some partial souls are here, some are there, and the Second is composed of both | The ones in the higher world, if they are such as those in this world (standing by potentiality), face the same problem there; but the existence of the Second is *an already existing existence*, not potentiality standing |
| (c) Not in the sense of a part in this world at all | The Second is not part of the world; the souls are not parts of it | Follows by elimination; this is what the author affirms |

The dilemma in compact form:

```
Second exists in ACTUALITY (Chapter Three established this)
   AND
Partial souls in this world exist in POTENTIALITY
   ↓
A whole composed of partial souls would itself be in POTENTIALITY
   (a whole has only the actuality of its parts)
   ↓
Therefore the Second cannot be a whole composed of partial souls.
```

What the dilemma has not yet said: the author has shown what the souls are *not*; sub-chapter four turns to what they *are*.

## Sub-chapter four: second emanation, the souls' real rank

An annotated structure. The doctrine the chapter has been building toward. The first sentence is a reductio; the rest is the positive doctrine.

The reductio against parts:

- IF souls were parts of the Second, THEN whole bestows qualities on parts; THEN we should find all souls knowledgeable and wise
- We do not find them so
- Therefore they are not parts of the Second
- *And the materials of the Messengers, God's prayers be upon them, would be parts of this category; a belief such as this is unbelief, we seek refuge in God*

The positive doctrine, stated by the author:

> *The souls belong to the nature of those paradoxical things whose existence is creative; their appearance and existence are only among the natural and phenomenal worlds. It is a natural force that acquires what is gained from the limits through obedience and worship. So it emits from itself an abstract remaining image that is like the abstract things in the House of the Hereafter on the one hand, and the opposite of it on the other.*
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter four.

> *This is the second source, and our writings on the* Ma'ad *include mention of that, God willing.*
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter four, closing line.

The architecture the author is invoking, set out in full in *Asrar al-Ma'ad* (The Secrets of the Return):

| Emanation | What is emitted | From what | By what act |
|---|---|---|---|
| First emanation | The universal Soul (the Second) | The First | The act of origination at the realm of *dar an earlier work on origination* |
| Second emanation | The partial speaking souls (us) | The universal Soul | The same originating act, considered now under the proportion of receivership |

The two faces of what the soul emits, anchored in the Quranic oath at the opening of *Surat the chapter on the sun*:

> *By the soul, and Him Who fashioned it, and inspired it as to its wickedness and its righteousness. He has succeeded who purifies it, and he has failed who corrupts it.*
> Quran 91:7-10.

| Quranic phrase | What the doctrine assigns it |
|---|---|
| *fashioned it* | The soul is a natural force, real, with its own essence (the book *The Defense*'s "jewels") |
| *inspired it* | The acquisition comes through the chain, from the *the limits*, through obedience and worship |
| *He has succeeded who purifies it* | The abstract remaining image like the abstract things in the House of the Hereafter |
| *He has failed who corrupts it* | The opposite face |

The verse does not say the soul is a *part* of anything. It says the soul is fashioned, inspired, and that what it becomes is up to the work it does on itself.

## Sub-chapter five: the limit of the body, the limit of the soul

A contrast pair on capacity. The book *The Defense* offers an observation intending one conclusion; the author turns the same observation against him.

Column A: bodies and their capacity to receive

- Iron held in fire: once it has gone red, that is its end; it grows no hotter
- A black body: it does not grow blacker; it may end in a blackness past which it cannot be further blackened
- Pattern: bodies have a *terminus*; the capacity of any substrate to receive an accident is finite
- The receiver is the body subject to the succession of accidents and effects

Column B: souls and their capacity to receive (set up here, settled in sub-chapter six)

- No terminus
- Continues to gain from the light without itself being changed
- Capacity is unlimited
- The receiver is the soul, not a body

The book *The Defense*'s subtle contribution, used against him:

| What the book *The Defense* intended to prove | What he actually proved |
|---|---|
| The souls are mere ephemeral accidents | *Bodies* have a fixed capacity past which they receive nothing further |
| The traces are the receivers | The *body* is the receiver; traces are not receivers |

Sub-chapter five sets the trap; sub-chapter six closes it.

## Sub-chapter six: the soul's capacity is unlimited

A two-move structure: a doctrinal endorsement and a precisely limited grant.

The book *The Defense*'s sentence the author endorses:

> *The speaking souls within us continue to gain from the light of the Most High and Most Noble world, and they increase in honor in themselves without any change occurring in themselves; nor does any corruption enter into them from the soul when it wishes to benefit them.*
> the book *The Defense*, quoted in *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter six.

The author's response, the proof of non-embodiment:

- Bodies have a fixed terminus of reception (sub-chapter five)
- The soul does not (this sentence of the book *The Defense*'s)
- Therefore the soul is not a body
- The book *The Defense* has, almost by accident, supplied the proof of non-embodiment that Chapter Two had been building

The Hadith Qudsi anchor in the prophetic register:

> *My servant continues to draw near to Me with voluntary works until I love him. And when I love him, I am his hearing with which he hears, his sight with which he sees, his hand with which he strikes, and his foot with which he walks.*
> Sahih the canonical hadith compiler, Book 81, Hadith 38, narrated by a transmitter of the Prophet's traditions.

The structure of the Hadith Qudsi mapped to the doctrine:

| Hadith Qudsi phrase | Doctrine in the metaphysical register |
|---|---|
| *draws near with voluntary works* | The acquisition comes through obedience and worship |
| *until I love him* | The rank is attained by the chain |
| *I am his hearing with which he hears* | The faculty becomes, by rank, what it was always *of* |
| Unbounded drawing-near | The soul's capacity is unlimited |

The author's precisely limited grant on "parts":

> *The most we can grant him is to say: as for those who wish to call them parts, these speaking souls are receivers if they are rational. As such, the soul and what it has produced are one thing; the speaking soul is identical with it and not distinguished from it except by rank, like the part of a thing in which there exists what is found in the whole, their path in themselves being one and the same from this side.*
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter six.

The capacity 2x2 that names the move.

Axes:

- Vertical: Capacity to receive (Unbounded up, Bounded down)
- Horizontal: Mode of relation to what is above (By composition left, By rank right)

Quadrants:

| | By composition | By rank |
|---|---|---|
| **Unbounded** | Impossible. Composition implies a whole made of parts; a part-of-a-substance shares the substance's terminus | The speaking soul: one self with the universal Soul, differentiated by rank; what is in the whole is in the part by rank |
| **Bounded** | The body: a piece of matter receives accidents up to a fixed terminus | Not the architecture under discussion |

The author's "parts" is admitted only in the top-right quadrant. *The book *The Defense*'s* "parts" was risking the top-left, which is impossible.

The Sufi rendering of the same teaching:

> *Lights are the riding-mounts of hearts and innermost beings.*
> a later teacher, Hikam, Aphorism 96.

Lights ride; hearts are ridden. The light is what carries the heart to the rank at which it can receive. The heart is not the light, and the light is not the heart; but the heart is what the light brings to the place where the gift arrives. One in self, differentiated by rank; rendered in the vocabulary of a different tradition.

## Sub-chapter seven: what really grounds prophethood and imamate

A polemic-and-release structure. The book *The Defense*'s polemical stake is the chapter's hottest moment. The author's release dismantles the polemic at its premise.

The book *The Defense*'s polemic, stated as a chain of conditionals:

```
IF souls are not parts of the Universal Soul
   THEN soul cannot perceive what influences it
      THEN prophethood is nullified
      AND imamate is nullified
      AND walaya is nullified
      AND revelation is nullified
      AND the divine laws are nullified
```

The book *The Defense*'s evidence: the prophets, peace be upon them, claimed they had *seen the noble Alawi world, and stood upon the spiritual the limits, and benefited from what they brought back of light and manifestation, and seen the angels*. If their souls were *only* traces, they could not have comprehended what was inspired in them.

The author's release, sentence by sentence:

| The author's move | What it does to the book *The Defense*'s chain |
|---|---|
| It has already been said that the souls are not mere traces but parts, and that they are a second emanation | Accepts the polemical premise that "mere traces" would fail; names the right doctrine: second emanation |
| What is meant by the one who said they are effects is that what is done with them belongs essentially to the souls and earns them their survival | The book *The Correction*'s "traces" carried the truth that the work the soul does on itself is essentially its own |
| What is meant by those who said they are parts is that the souls, by their own intellect, are like the things that have produced them | The book *The Defense*'s "parts" carried the truth that the soul has, in itself, the rational character of what stands above |
| Prophethood is not invalidated by this, nor imamate, nor anything else, for it has been established that these ranks are not of these | Breaks the chain at its first link: the architecture does not *rest* on the partness question at all |

What the architecture actually rests on:

> *The prophets were able to perceive these absent things from the upper world, after they reached the rank of perfection, becoming, at its end, in the Sixties. When they return to themselves, it is as though they have realized that the transcendent selves are one thing in respect of the whole, with no difference except in rank. We have set this out in our well-known book, and the peace of mind that comes of that is sufficient and curative.*
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter seven.

The doctrinal architecture of prophetic reception:

| Element | What it is | Where the reception comes from |
|---|---|---|
| The prophet's soul | A second emanation, like any speaking soul, with its own essence | Through the chain |
| Obedience and worship | The work that traverses the ranks | Acquisition |
| The *the limits* | The convoy of the gift down through the ranks | Conveyance |
| The Sixties | The station at the brink of the upper world | The seat of perception |
| The upper world | What the soul perceives from that station | The seen |

The reception is enabled by the *rank* attained, not by substance-composition with the universal Soul.

The Quranic anchor for the Prophet's vision:

> *And he was at the highest horizon. Then he approached and descended, and was at a distance of two bow-lengths or nearer. So He revealed to His servant what He revealed. The heart did not lie about what it saw.*
> Quran 53:7-11.

> *The eye did not deviate, nor did it transgress; he certainly saw of the greatest signs of his Lord.*
> Quran 53:17-18.

The verse mapped to the doctrine:

| Verse phrase | What the doctrine assigns it |
|---|---|
| *at the highest horizon* | The station at the brink of the upper world; the Sixties |
| *two bow-lengths or nearer* | The closest reach of the chain |
| *the heart did not lie about what it saw* | The seeing is real; not a metaphor |
| *he certainly saw of the greatest signs of his Lord* | The seeing of signs delivered by the chain, not the seeing of one's own substance |

## Sub-chapter eight: the Word is the Intellect

An annotated structure on the chapter's deepest move. The book *The Defense* offers a four-rung ladder; the author refuses the first rung and gives the order that *is* correct.

The book *The Defense*'s proposed four-rung ladder:

| Rung | The book *The Defense*'s claim |
|---|---|
| 1 | The Intellect is creative *through* the Word |
| 2 | The Soul is the image of the Intellect |
| 3 | Nature is born of the Soul |
| 4 | The speaking souls are parts of the Universal Soul |

The book *The Defense*'s claim: with this ladder in place, monotheism, reward, punishment, prophethood, imamate, *sharia*, and revelation all become coherent.

The author's refusal, at the first rung:

> *The validity of monotheism, of reward and punishment, of prophethood and imamate, of the message and of the laws drawn from revelation, is not proved from the side that the Intellect is creative through the Word and that the Soul is the image of the Intellect. Rather, it is established from another side, set out in our letters and our writings about them.*
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter eight.

The doctrinal error in the first rung:

| The book *The Defense*'s picture | What it would imply | The author's correction |
|---|---|---|
| The Intellect creates *through* the Word as instrument | The Word stands behind the Intellect; the Word is prior; the Intellect is a tool wielded by the Word | Priority is backwards; the Word does not stand behind the Intellect, the Word is what the Intellect IS |

The deepest move:

> *The Intellect is nothing but the Word itself, and the Word is nothing but the Intellect itself.*
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter eight.

One thing, called by two names according to the aspect under which it is being considered.

The authority the author invokes:

> *It is correct to say that Mawlana Imam the Fatimid caliph, in The Interpretation of Sharia, where he discusses intelligibles, says: the Word is the Intellect, and these names properly belong to it.*
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter eight.

The order that *is* correct, given by the author:

> *The Soul and nature emanate from the First Intellect, each of them according to its own proportion, so that one of them is more excellent than the other, the first emanation being the Soul, and the speaking souls being the second emanation. What occurs in the first of them is repeated in the last of them.*
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter eight.

The corrected hierarchy of ranks:

- Level 1: Intellect-which-is-the-Word (one self, two names)
  - Level 2: The Soul (the first emanation, by proportion of receivership toward the First)
    - Level 3: Nature (the substrate, prime matter informed)
    - Level 3: The speaking souls (the second emanation, by proportion of receivership toward the universal Soul)

The chapter closes on a prayer:

> *A gesture suffices for the seeking student; others reject it because it is hard to reach and hard to know. May Allah help the believer who knows this according to his strength in devotion to Allah and His guardians, and help him if he is sincere to His face, and condemn him and his guardians if he renounces them. May Allah make us among the followers able to fulfill their covenant; we are blessed with His praise and glory.*
> *Kitab al-Riyad*, Chapter Four, sub-chapter eight, closing.

## The settled formula

A summary contrast pair: the three readings of what the speaking souls are. The chapter has adjudicated.

Column A: the book *The Correction*'s isolated word

- *Traces*
- Risk: read as ephemeral marks, no essence of their own
- Truth it carried: what is done with the souls earns them their survival; the soul is not a body

Column B: the book *The Defense*'s isolated word

- *Parts*
- Risk: read as composition, pieces broken off from a substance
- Truth it carried: the soul has, in itself, the rational character of what stands above; the soul really receives

Column C (the author's third word): *Second emanation*

- Not mere traces, not pieces of a substance
- Brought forth from the universal Soul by the same originating act under which the universal Soul was brought forth from the First
- Jewels existing by themselves, with their own essence and their own survival
- Continuous by rank with what stands above: what is in the whole is found in the part by rank, not by composition

Shared row: all three readings agree the speaking souls really receive from the rank above. All three are trying to name the same relation. The author's third word is the only one that names it without smuggling body-talk in or stripping the soul of its essence.

## The political stake one layer below the metaphysics

A contrast pair on the consequence of getting the architecture wrong.

Column A: if the speaking souls were parts in the literal sense

- A believer's soul could claim continuity with the upper world *by sheer composition*
- By being a piece of it
- The *the speaker-prophet*, the *imam*, and the *the limits* would lose their function as the convoy of the gift
- The believer could go around them on the basis of being already, by composition, of the same substance as the rank above
- The *the call* architecture collapses

Column B: the author's reading (second emanation)

- The speaking soul does not stand *in* the universal Soul as a piece in a whole
- The speaking soul stands *under* the universal Soul as a rank under a rank
- Receiving through the *the limits* what the chain conveys
- The path is the path of obedience and worship
- The *the limits* are the convoy
- The rank perfected is the soul's seat at the brink of the upper world (the Sixties)
- The *the call* architecture stands

The architecture protected is Imam the Fatimid caliph's formulation in *the Fatimid caliph's interpretive treatise*: the Word is the Intellect; the highest rank is not a *use* of any instrument; it is one self under two names. From that single doctrine, the whole chain unfolds.

## What comes next

Chapter Five turns the question one rank wider. Chapter Four gave the rank of the soul; Chapter Five gives the place of the whole human being.

| Chapter | Question | Answer the chapter builds |
|---|---|---|
| Chapter Four (this one) | What is the speaking soul's relation to the rank above? | Second emanation; one in self with the universal Soul, differentiated only by rank |
| Chapter Five (next) | What is the human being, soul plus natural composite, in the order of the worlds? | The *fruit* of the worlds: the rank at which the chain comes to its harvest |

The image the chapter leaves the reader with: the book *The Correction* and the book *The Defense* arguing across decades about parts and traces. The author, generations later, working the same passage and arriving at a third word: *second emanation*. The speaking souls in their place. The Intellect-which-is-the-Word at the source. The *the limits* between, conveying the gift. *A gesture suffices for the seeking student; others reject it because it is hard to reach and hard to know.* The chapter is the gesture. The doctrine is settled. The architecture holds.
