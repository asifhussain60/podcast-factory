# Slide Deck Framing, EP11: Qada and Qadar, Fate and Destiny

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio. They have not read *The Correction*, *The Defense*, or *The Harvest*.

## Core Principle

Each slide must add visual information the audio cannot carry. Slides render the Pen-Tablet-Writer architecture, show the three-rank hierarchies of *qada* and *qadar* at one glance, and set the two tailor-analogies side by side.

## Visual Priorities

- The Pen-Tablet-Writer architecture as an annotated structure: Writer (face of "We"), Pen (First Intellect), Tablet (universal Soul), Measure (the receptive rank on which all images are unfolded).
- The two tailor-analogies as a side-by-side contrast pair, attribute by attribute.
- The three-rank hierarchies of *qada* and *qadar* shown side by side, speaker-Prophet at the top to world-unfolding-in-time at the bottom.
- The three-position order as a left-to-right timeline: decree, apportioning, completion.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a sub-chapter title.
- No literal illustrations: no calligraphy stock, no tailor photos, no mountain photos.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams. Every diagram has axes, nodes, rows, or named layers.
- No slides that restate one sentence the audio speaks.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide must add visual information not present in the audio.
- For the Pen-Tablet-Writer architecture, name every element and show the writing-flow as a directed top-to-bottom chain.
- Place *qada* on the left and *qadar* on the right in every three-rank rendering.
- Show the relationships between entities, not a list of them.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
