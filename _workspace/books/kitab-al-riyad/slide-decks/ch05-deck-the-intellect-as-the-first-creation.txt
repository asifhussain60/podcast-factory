# The Intellect as the First Creation

*The visual companion to Chapter Five. The audio chapter is the prose; this file is its argument made structural. Same concepts, same depth, restructured for slide-deck rendering.*

## Where this chapter picks up

A hand-off table from Chapter One to Chapter Two. The thirty-eight sub-chapters of Chapter One built the formula; Chapter Two narrows it to one cosmological point.

| Element | Chapter One (closed) | Chapter Two (this chapter) |
|---|---|---|
| Subject | The Soul, the first emanator | The First Intellect itself |
| Length | 38 sub-chapters | 9 sub-chapters |
| Mode | Doctrine walked across many analogies | One proof, applied repeatedly |
| Disputants | *Jonathan* in *The Correction* vs *Samuel* in *The Defense* | Same two disputants, narrower target |
| Author's role | Referee across registers | Referee on a single categorical point |
| Closing accusation of Ch.1 | The ancient philosophers erred by following only their own intellects without the witness of the *Alawite* call | The same error now traced into the language *Jonathan* and *Samuel* used about the First Intellect |

The master-error carried forward: applying categories of the natural world (motion, stillness, need, dwelling, encompassing) to the supra-natural realm (the world of origination). The whole chapter is one proof against this error, walked through nine angles.

## The chapter's central proof

A four-step deductive chain. This is the proof on which all nine sub-chapters rest.

```
Step 1: Motion is a predicate of bodies.
   ↓ and
Step 2: Stillness is a predicate of bodies.
   ↓ therefore
Step 3: Whatever is not a body has neither motion nor stillness, in potency or in actuality, in any sense.
   ↓ applied
Step 4: The First and Second Intellects are not bodies; therefore neither motion nor stillness applies to them.
```

The corollary: any framing that places motion and stillness in the Intellects, whether in potency (as in *The Correction*) or as a distributed pair across Intellect and Soul (as in *The Defense*), commits the same master-error in different surface forms.

## The two opening positions on motion and stillness

A contrast pair. *Jonathan* and *Samuel* both apply motion-and-stillness to the supra-natural realm; they differ in the geometry of the application.

Column A: *Jonathan* in *The Correction*

- Motion and stillness: two effects united in the First and the Second
- In the First: both exist in potentiality
- In the Second: both exist in actuality
- Rank within the pair: motion is higher than stillness
- Rank across Intellects: the First is higher than the Second
- Motion is: the conveyance of the Second of the essences
- Stillness is: the Second's acceptance of the benefit of the First
- Safeguard: motion and stillness reach the natural world through the soul's power learned from the intellect, in the image and the form

Column B: *Samuel* in *The Defense*

- Motion: only for the sake of seeking
- Seeking implies: need
- Need implies: deficiency
- Therefore: the Intellect cannot be in motion
- Motion belongs to: the Soul, which seeks benefit from the Intellect
- Stillness belongs to: the Intellect, which receives from its Principle without seeking
- Mechanism: the Intellect has not poured forth upon the Soul all that overflowed upon it, only some, so the Soul moves to seek the rest

Shared ground: both keep motion and stillness as live categories in the supra-natural realm. Both place them somewhere along the Intellect-Soul axis.

The author's refusal: not "place them more carefully." Neither position has any place to put them. The category itself does not extend to non-bodies.

## The author's master-proof: why the First Intellect cannot be a body

A process-flow / cause-effect proof. Eight links, each forced by the one before.

```
Body requires being a substance
   ↓ which requires
Dependence of one on another in existence
   ↓ which means
Its existence would not be the first existence (from God Almighty as the first existence)
   ↓ but
The First Intellect's very self is the act of which it is the first existence, by God Almighty
   ↓ and
The same act has one subject, not two
   ↓ therefore
If the First Intellect's existence were not the first existence, it would be preceded by another, and so it would be something else
   ↓ therefore
If the Intellect were a body, it would be preceded in existence by others, and it would not be the first creation
   ↓ conclusion
Nothing precedes the First Intellect in its existence; therefore it is not a body; therefore it has no motion and no stillness
```

The same proof bears against *Samuel's* counter-move. *Samuel* relocated motion from Intellect to Soul. He kept the category and shifted it down a rank. The author refuses the relocation: the Second is not a body either; motion does not apply to it any more than to the First.

## Creativity and the Creator: refusing the doctrinal split

A contrast pair, *Jonathan's* formulation against the author's correction.

Column A: *Jonathan* in *The Correction*

- Creativity and the creator: two
- One is creativity itself, the other is the creative itself
- Even if two, they are nothing but one whole, in an image not to be believed
- Purpose of the split: to ground two effects (motion and stillness) in the First by way of two principles

Column B: the author's correction

- Creativity and the creator: not two; they are one
- Creativity is the creator; the creator is creativity
- If creativity is added to what its existence began with, it is His act, the same as the act
- If creativity is added to itself, it is the principle that it is the object of another, whose existence comes from Him
- The First Intellect is one act; one principle; one existence
- The verbal distinction is in our naming, not in the underlying reality

Shared row: both authors are defending the unity of the First. They differ on whether a doctrinal sub-distinction within the First is safe.

The deeper stake: the split risks introducing a co-present principle alongside the Creator in the moment of the first existence. The author shuts that door.

The scriptural seal of the refusal is *Imam Ali's* first sermon, which names *tawhid* as a five-step climb culminating in the negation of attributes from God.

> *Awwalu al-din ma'rifatuhu, wa-kamalu ma'rifatihi al-tasdiqu bihi, wa-kamalu al-tasdiqi bihi tawhiduhu, wa-kamalu tawhidihi al-ikhlasu lahu, wa-kamalu al-ikhlasi lahu nafyu al-sifati 'anhu.*
> *The foremost in religion is the knowledge of Him; the perfection of knowing Him is to attest to Him; the perfection of attesting to Him is to declare Him One; the perfection of declaring Him One is sincerity toward Him; and the perfection of sincerity toward Him is the negation of attributes from Him.*
> *Imam Ali*, Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him; *Nahj al-Balagha* (*The Path of Eloquence*), Sermon 1; compiled by *al-Sharif al-Radi*.

A five-step hierarchy of *tawhid* (declaring God One).

| Step | Stage | What it negates |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Knowledge of Him | Ignorance |
| 2 | Attestation | Mere knowledge without commitment |
| 3 | Declaring Him One (*tawhid*) | Partners |
| 4 | Sincerity (*ikhlas*) | Hidden associationism |
| 5 | Negation of attributes | Counting Him with other counted things |

How the sermon governs the author's move: to attach "creativity" as an attribute alongside the Creator would be to count alongside Him. The author's refusal of *Jonathan's* split is the philosophical extension of *Imam Ali's* fifth step.

## Actuality is more honorable than potentiality

A 2x2 of *Jonathan's* trapped framing, with the impossible quadrant labeled.

Axes:

- Vertical: Rank claimed in the original framing (First above, Second below)
- Horizontal: Mode of motion-and-stillness assigned (Potentiality left, Actuality right)

Quadrants:

| | Potentiality | Actuality |
|---|---|---|
| **The First** (claimed higher rank) | *Jonathan's* assignment: motion and stillness exist in the First in potency. By philosophical maxim, what is in potency is less honorable than what is in act. So the First, in *Jonathan's* own framing, is less honorable than the Second on this axis. | Empty in *Jonathan's* picture. |
| **The Second** (claimed lower rank) | Empty in *Jonathan's* picture. | *Jonathan's* assignment: motion and stillness exist in the Second in actuality. By the same maxim, the Second is more honorable than the First on this axis. **Impossible.** The Second cannot precede the First in honor. |

The reductio: *Jonathan's* own framing reverses his own ranks. By placing motion-and-stillness in the First in potency and in the Second in actuality, he makes the Second more honorable than the First. That is impossible. Therefore the framing is wrong.

The lesson generalizes: potency and actuality are categories of becoming. The First Intellect does not become. It is, fully, at the instant of its origination. The categories themselves do not apply.

## The *hayula* and the image: the partial concession

An annotated structure showing where *Jonathan's* language can stand and where it cannot.

Whole: the formulation "motion and stillness are effects of the soul by the power learned from the intellect, in the case of the *hayula* (prime matter) and the image (*al-sura*)."

Parts of the structure, dissected:

| Part | What *Jonathan* names | What the author allows | What the author refuses |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Intellect | Source of the power | Source of existence for the soul, in the chain of being | NOT itself in motion or rest |
| The Soul | Mediator: receives power from intellect, transmits to nature | Conduit of the natural force whose presence in bodies comes from outside the first two intellects | NOT itself in motion or rest |
| The image (*al-sura*) | What moves and is moved | Mover for the body; its existence is from the intellect | NOT moved itself in the supra-natural sense |
| The bodies (*hayula* + image) | What is moved | This is the natural world; motion and stillness DO apply here | (no refusal needed) |
| The motion | "Two effects united in the First and the Second" | Read DOWN as motion of longing in the natural world | Read UP as predicates of the Intellects themselves |

The author's discipline: where *Jonathan* can be read into compatibility with the doctrine, the author reads him there. This is the "best interpretation" gesture. *Samuel* does not extend the same courtesy to *Jonathan*. The author does, because *Jonathan* is his deceased master and a loyal voice of the call.

The constraint that cannot be relaxed: the language stands ONLY in the natural-world reading. The moment it is taken to apply to the Intellects themselves, it falls back into the master-error.

## Why *Samuel's* settled formula also fails

A contrast pair, *Samuel's* fall-back position against the author's correction.

Column A: *Samuel's* settled formula

- Motion is for the Soul
- Stillness is for the Intellect
- Therefore neither category is fully removed from the supra-natural realm; each is placed in its proper rank
- Hoped to be: the safe formula that ends the dispute

Column B: the author's full refusal

- The world of origination is the collective of before and after; the pure intellects are paradoxical higher beings of the objects
- Motion in what moves is something else from the thing itself; it implies a self that is lacking and needy
- The Soul, being from the world of origination, is specialized in completion and perfection
- The same does not become imperfect and needy; so the Soul cannot differ in itself
- The Soul has an eternal existence by virtue of arising from origination as intellectual
- If eternal, it is impossible for it to be transferred from what it was upon at first
- Therefore motion is invalid for the Soul also
- Likewise stillness is invalid: neither does the First Intellect have stillness, nor does the Second (which *Samuel* called the Soul) have motion

Shared row: both grant that motion-and-stillness do not befit the First Intellect. They differ on whether the Soul receives the relocated category.

The author's relocation of *Samuel's* true claim: *Samuel* wished to say what he said about the souls and intellects in the House of Nature, where a second emanation is emitted. Motion and stillness are specialized in the world of the body. They are not specific to the House of Origination.

The accommodation: *Samuel* conveyed the meaning to assimilation in support of his teacher *Jonathan*. *The author* prays: may Allah make his dignity, and gather them and us with our pure followers. Doctrine firm; tone gentle.

## Motion can express perfection, not only deficiency

A comparison matrix refuting *Samuel's* maxim "motion is from need; need is deficiency."

Rows: kinds of motion. Columns: source of motion | what it expresses | example.

| Mover | Source of motion | What it expresses | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Earth | Need | Seeking its proper place (downward) | The fall of a stone |
| Fire | Need | Seeking its proper place (upward) | The rise of a flame |
| Natural soul | Need | Seeking benefit from above | The acquisition of knowledge through the senses |
| The heavens | Fullness | Rolling-out of perfection already possessed at the first instant of creation | The motion of the celestial spheres |
| *the speaker-prophet* (the Speaker-Prophet) | Fullness | Working-out of attained perfection in the temporal order: preaching, founding, establishing *shari'a*, governing | The Prophet *Muhammad* (peace and blessings be upon him) in his mission |

The general maxim collapses. Motion can express perfection as well as betray deficiency. Which it does depends on what kind of existent is in motion and toward what.

The living prophetic example, in his own words.

> *Afa-la akunu 'abdan shakuran?*
> *Should I not, then, be a grateful servant?*
> The Prophet *Muhammad* (peace and blessings be upon him); *Sahih al-Bukhari*, Hadith 4837; *Sahih Muslim*, Book 50, Hadith 81; narrated by *al-Mughira ibn Shu'ba*, companion of the Prophet.

An annotated structure of the scene.

| Element | The picture |
|---|---|
| The act | The Prophet prays through the night until his feet swell |
| The question put to him | Why task himself, when God has already forgiven him every past and future fault? |
| The answer | He moves not because he must, but because he is grateful |
| The doctrine carried | Motion from fullness, not from need; from gratitude, not from compensation |
| *Samuel's* maxim against it | Breaks: the Prophet has been forgiven, has attained the highest rank, yet he moves |
| The maxim's correct scope | Some natural things, where motion is inseparable from them |

The Prophet's motion is *Samuel's* counter-example in the living form. The heavens move out of fullness. The *the speaker-prophet* moves out of fullness. To read motion as a sign of deficiency is to read perfection's own expression as its absence.

## Overflow and the limits of receivership: *Samuel's* political accusation

A process flow of *Samuel's* charge against the overflow doctrine, and the author's reply.

```
Premise (Samuel): each of the limits receives from the rank above and overflows to the rank below
   ↓ if so
Consequence: blessings reach the Prophet from the world of sanctified holiness and pass onward without remainder
   ↓ which would mean
The Prophet is "deluded": he carries nothing distinctive
   ↓ and
The wali becomes doubtful in perfection and merit
   ↓ and
The wali's standing in the place of the Prophet and obedience to him falls under suspicion
   ↓ and finally
On that view it would not be permissible to believe in the speaker-prophet, the wali, the imam, or the limits
```

The political stake: *Samuel's* charge is well-aimed at the architecture of the Fatimid *the call*, with its ranks of *the speaker-prophet*, *the foundation*, the *imam*, the *hujja*, and the *da'i*. If overflow empties the higher ranks, the whole order dissolves.

The author's reply, as a 2x2 of *the limits* and what they pass, with the impossible quadrant labeled.

Axes:

- Vertical: Source. The rank above (top) vs the receiver below (bottom)
- Horizontal: State. Withholding (left) vs Passing fully (right)

Quadrants:

| | Withholding | Passing fully |
|---|---|---|
| **The limits** (source) | **Impossible.** *Samuel's* fear, but contrary to the doctrine. The limits never withhold; there is no shortage in the blessings, nor any withholding on their part. | The actual doctrine: blessings overflow from the side of all the limits, in fullness, according to the capacity of the one able to receive. |
| **The receiver** (capacity) | Some receivers accept only what they are able to; this is not the limits' withholding but the receiver's incapacity. | The Prophet receives more because he is fitted by the Almighty to receive more; the wali, imam, hujja, believer each in his own measure. |

The principle the author states: it is not permissible for the limits to withhold benefit from one who would benefit from it, so long as the receiver is in his right when willed, lest he fail to ascend to a rank that was his before the spiritual birth.

The structure of grace is the structure of capacity. The higher ranks are not made by what they refuse to pass on. They are made by their fitness to receive at the higher measure to begin with.

Two Quranic verses ground the doctrine. Each preserved with attribution.

> *Wa-la taqtulu awladakum khashyata imlaqin.*
> *Do not kill your children for fear of poverty.*
> Quran 17:31, in the rendering the author gives.

> *Fa-in anastum minhum rushdan fa-idfa'u ilayhim amwalahum, wa-la ta'kuluha israfan wa-bidaran an yakbaru.*
> *If you find them of sound mind, then deliver to them their wealth, and do not consume it wastefully and in haste before they grow up.*
> Quran 4:6, in the rendering the author gives.

How each verse maps to the author's argument.

| Verse | What it forbids | How it applies to the limits |
|---|---|---|
| 17:31 | Killing children out of fear of poverty | To withhold the higher knowledges from those who can receive them is a kind of spiritual infanticide. The fear that there will not be enough is the original error. The limits never run out. |
| 4:6 | Consuming the orphan's wealth, or holding it back when the orphan is of sound mind | The limits are guardians of the light, not its owners. They are charged with delivering it to whoever is of sound capacity to receive. |

The closing prophetic warning, against the guardian who locks the light inside himself:

> *Man su'ila 'an 'ilmin fa-katamahu, uljima yawma al-qiyamati bi-lijamin min nar.*
> *Whoever is asked about knowledge that he possesses and conceals it will be bridled with a bridle of fire on the Day of Resurrection.*
> The Prophet *Muhammad* (peace and blessings be upon him); *Sunan Abi Dawud*, Hadith 3658; *Sunan al-Tirmidhi*, Hadith 2649; classified Sahih.

The chain of light passes where capacity meets capacity. Where capacity meets withholding, the chain breaks at the wrong link.

A hierarchy tree of how receivers stand under the overflow, from *Imam Ali's* discourse to *Kumayl ibn Ziyad*.

> *Al-nasu thalathatun: fa-'alimun rabbaniyyun, wa-muta'allimun 'ala sabili najatin, wa-hamajun ra'a'un atba'u kulli na'iqin, yamiluna ma'a kulli rihin, lam yastadi'u bi-nuri al-'ilmi wa-lam yalja'u ila ruknin wathiq.*
> *People are of three sorts: a divine scholar; a learner upon the path of salvation; and a rabble of low people who follow every caller, bending with every wind, having neither sought the light of knowledge nor taken refuge in a firm pillar.*
> *Imam Ali*, Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him; *Nahj al-Balagha*, Aphorism 147; compiled by *al-Sharif al-Radi*; spoken to *Kumayl ibn Ziyad*.

Levels of the hierarchy:

- Level 1: People (*al-nas*) under the overflow
  - Level 2a: The divine scholar (*'alim rabbani*). Receives fully; the limits pass to him without remainder he can hold
  - Level 2b: The learner upon the path of salvation (*muta'allim 'ala sabili najatin*). Receives in the measure that fits his stage; capacity grows with the path
  - Level 2c: The rabble (*hamaj ra'a'*). Follows every caller, bends with every wind; has neither sought the light nor taken refuge in a firm pillar; receives only what the wind brings, which is no knowledge

What the discourse establishes: the limits withhold from none of the three. What differs is what each can hold once the light reaches him. *Samuel's* fear of "overflow without remainder emptying the higher ranks" is answered: the higher ranks are not made by what they refuse to release; they are made by the fitness of the receivers beneath them.

## Intellect encompasses, soul moves to reach: *Samuel's* fresh angle

A contrast pair: *Samuel's* picture against the author's refusal.

Column A: *Samuel* in *The Defense*

- Action is the essence of encompassing
- The movable does not encompass; it needs something to dwell in, in complete stillness
- The intellect is an ocean gathering things, with nothing descending and encompassing it
- The intellect is utterly at rest
- The soul moves only to reach the door of the intellect, then dwells there
- So long as the soul is in motion, it does not encompass
- The soul moves a movement of subsistence, coming to dwell by benefiting from the One who is settled

Column B: the author's refusal

- The intellect is not in motion regarding what it does not need to be extended toward (granted in part)
- But it does not follow that the intellect must be at rest in the manner of bodies, which are never free of being either at rest or in motion
- The intellect's existence is not like the existence of objects; movement and stillness do not apply to it
- *Samuel's* claim "motion precludes encompassing" is refuted by the natural soul itself: natural souls encompass things precisely *through* their motion
- They use senses, deductions, instruments; they move from premise to conclusion
- The acquisition of knowledge in the natural soul happens *in motion*, not in dwelling

Shared row: both grant that motion as a body-category does not apply to the Intellect.

The double error in *Samuel's* picture: it imports body-categories into the Intellect (never-free-of-motion-or-rest), AND it misdescribes the natural soul, where motion is how encompassing happens, not its obstacle. The picture works for no soul that actually exists.

An annotated structure of *Samuel's* "movement of subsistence" formula, split into what is true and what is in error.

> *The soul moves a movement of subsistence, which comes to dwell by benefiting from the One who is settled.*
> *Samuel* in *The Defense*, as the chapter closes.

Parts of the formula:

| Part | Truthful meaning the author allows | Surface meaning the author refuses |
|---|---|---|
| "The soul moves" | True for the natural souls, which exist in potentiality and are moving toward acquisition | False if extended to the universal Soul, which has no motion |
| "A movement of subsistence" | True: the acquisition leads the natural soul to subsist, to persist, to have stable being | False if applied to the supra-natural Soul, where no acquisition of subsistence is needed |
| "Comes to dwell" | True: the natural soul passes from potency to act by acquiring and benefiting | False if the Second Intellect or universal Soul is meant, since they do not pass from potency to act |
| "By benefiting from the One who is settled" | True for the natural soul receiving from above | False if "settled" is read as body-rest; the Intellect is not at rest in the sense in which bodies are at rest |

The author's verdict: *Samuel* is truthful when he assigns a meaning to it. He is in error if he carries it on the surface of what he says. The Second is not in motion; the Intellect is not at rest in the body-sense. They have no need of movement, stillness, or attentive care.

## The chapter's deepest warning

> *One must take care to know the ranks of the limits, so that there be no confusion about the matter of perceiving things in their realities, for such confusion would lead to the corruption of the essence of souls.*
> The author, *Kitab al-Riyad*, closing of *Chapter Two*.

A process flow of what the categorical mismatch corrupts, if left uncorrected.

```
Imagining the First Intellect as moving in potency
   ↓ leads toward
Imagining God as alongside another (a co-present principle)
   ↓
Imagining the universal Soul as moving for need
   ↓ leads toward
Emptying the higher limits of their distinction
   ↓
Imagining the speaker-prophet's work as a sign of his deficiency
   ↓ leads toward
Refusing his rank altogether
   ↓
Doctrinal looseness in cosmology
   ↓ corrupts
The soul's capacity to perceive the realities for what they are
```

Why this matters beyond philosophy: doctrinal precision in cosmology is not academic. It guards the soul. The limits must be kept distinct so that the lights may reach those whose capacity is fitted to receive them. The discipline that holds those distinctions clear is the discipline that lets the soul become, in time, what it was created to be.

The chapter seals on *the author's* prayer:

> May Allah make us light by their means, and grant us success in following the pure stages of the divine sciences and the treasuries of the lights of sanctity; may Allah's prayers be upon them.
> The author, *Kitab al-Riyad*, sealing *Chapter Two*.

The prayer carries the whole motion of the chapter. By the means of the limits whose ranks must be kept clear in the perceiving soul, let us become light. By following their pure stages, not by collapsing their distinctions, not by importing body-categories into supra-natural existents, not by imagining motion or need or dwelling where none applies, let us be granted success. The treasuries of the lights of sanctity are reached by exactness in perception, which is itself a grace.
