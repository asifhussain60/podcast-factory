## The qurban verse, apparent and interpretive

A contrast pair. The book *The Defense* attacks the *qurban* verse as parable; the book *The Correction* and the author hold both readings together.

Column A: The book *The Defense*'s reading
- Layer: *batini* (interpretive) only
- Object of *qurban*: the establishment of proofs and successors, the foundation
- Burial inference: the son needed a raven, so Adam had no *Shariʿah*
- Conclusion: the *qurban* verse cannot prove a *Shariʿah*

Column B: The book *The Correction*'s and the author's reading
- Layer: *zahir* (apparent) AND *batini*, both operative
- Object of *qurban*: a ritual offering, the first cause of religion's laws
- Burial inference: the verse does not say the son did not bury, only that the raven showed him how
- Conclusion: the *zahir* alone settles Adam's *Shariʿah*; the *batini* layer carries it forward

Shared row: Both register the *qurban* verse as central to Adam's case.

The author's verdict: *the book The Defense* did not refute the *apparent* reading; he offered a *parallel* reading. Both readings stand together. The *zahir* alone is sufficient to settle the question.

The Quranic anchors:

> *And recite to them the story of Adam's two sons, in truth, when they both offered a sacrifice [to Allah], and it was accepted from one of them but was not accepted from the other.*
> Quran 5:27, Sahih International.

> *And for every nation We have appointed a rite, that they may pronounce the name of Allah over what He has provided them of cattle.*
> Quran 22:34.

If every nation has a rite, then Adam's nation at the head of human history has a rite, and Adam has a *Shariʿah*.

## Adam as teacher and guide

The chain of definitions:

- Prophet equals teacher
- Teacher equals one who has something to teach
- Something to teach equals laws of worship
- Laws of worship equals *Shariʿah*

The Quranic anchor:

> *Mankind was one nation, so Allah sent the Prophets as bearers of good tidings and warners, and He sent down the Book with them in truth, to judge between people in matters wherein they differed.*

- *Bearers of good tidings*: the *teaching* function in its positive mode

- *To judge between people*: the *Shariʿah-arbitration* function
- *In matters wherein they differed*: the necessity that presupposes prior limits

## The raven and the burial, annotated

An annotated process flow. The book *The Defense*'s strongest narrative move, turned by reading the same verse on two layers at once.

The *zahir* layer (what the verse actually says):

```
The two sons offer (qurban)
   ↓
One offering accepted, the other rejected
   ↓
The opponent says: "I will kill you"
   ↓
The killing occurs
   ↓
Allah sends a raven scratching the earth
   ↓
The raven shows him how to hide his brother's corpse
   ↓
The opponent says: "Woe to me! Am I unable to be like this crow?"
   ↓
He becomes one of the regretful
```

The author's correction of the book *The Defense*'s reading: the verse does NOT say the son did not bury; the verse says the raven *showed him how*. The whole *no-Shariʿah* inference rests on a reading-in.

The *batini* layer (the *daʿwa*'s settled the inner interpretation):

| Surface figure | Inner identity | Function |
|---|---|---|
| The two sons offering | The *wali* and the *opponent* (*al-mukhalif*) | The two parties to the doctrinal dispute over the *two worships* |
| The accepted offering | The *wali*'s belief that *the worship is in the outward AND the inward together* | The doctrine of *Chapter Seven* carried forward |
| The rejected offering | The *opponent*'s belief that *worship is the apparent alone* | The *zahir*-only doctrine refuted |
| The killing | The *opponent*'s annulment of the *wali*'s rank in the world | The *zahir*'s claim to abandon the *batin* |
| The raven scratching the earth | *Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr*, sent by *the Commander of the Faithful* | The *historical type* of the *recall* of the *opponent* to the *wali*'s rank |
| Woe to me, am I unable to be like this crow | I wish I were like *Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr*, who keeps Allah's covenant | The opponent's regret at his crime |

*Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr* is named in the *daʿwa* tradition as son of the first caliph but devotee of *the Commander of the Faithful*, straddling the line between the *zahir*-people and the *batin*-people, sent by *Ali* to recall the opponent before the time was sealed.

Verdict: the *zahir* reading proves the *Shariʿah of Adam*; the *batini* reading settles the *daʿwa*-theological layer. Both layers stand together. The book *The Defense* has nothing in his protest by means of this verse.

The companion taxonomy of burial: the book *The Defense*'s burial argument fails because burial is not the only possible disposition; it is the *ranked* one for Adam's *Shariʿah*.

| Disposition of the dead | Practiced by | Status in Adam's Shariʿah |
|---|---|---|
| Burying in the ground | Adam's nation, taught by the raven | The ranked, prescribed practice |
| Burning with fire | Other peoples in other cycles | A possible disposition, not Adam's |
| Throwing into water | Other peoples in other cycles | A possible disposition, not Adam's |
| Carrying to a cave or desert | Other peoples in other cycles | A possible disposition, not Adam's |
| Feeding to the wild beasts | Other peoples in other cycles | A possible disposition, not Adam's |

The narrative tradition (in the *daʿwa*'s transmitted reports) names Adam as the prescriber of the rites of the dead: *washing the dead, embalming, shrouding, and burying them are from what Adam prescribed*.

The structural point: if burial is the *ranked* practice that *displaces the others*, and the raven taught the son burial *specifically*, then burial is a practice of Adam's *Shariʿah*. Not a *new* obligation taught to a *Shariʿah-less* people, but the *ranked* version of an obligation that varies across cycles.

## The prostration of the angels

A contrast pair. The book *The Defense* claims Adam was prostrated-to, not a *Shariʿah-holder*. The author turns the very fact of prostration into proof of the *Mustafa*-rank that grounds the *Shariʿah*-rank.

Column A: The book *The Defense*'s reading
- The angels were commanded *to* Adam, not *through* him
- Therefore Adam is prostrated-to, not a *Shariʿah-holder*
- The verse shows honor but not lawgiving

Column B: The author's reading
- The angels prostrate only to a *Mustafa* (Chosen one)
- The command to prostrate *to* a *Mustafa* is itself issued *through* the *Mustafa*'s rank
- The Quranic anchor: *Allah has chosen Adam and Noah* (Quran 3:33)
- Allah does not bypass the *Mustafa*; Allah commands *through* the *Mustafa*'s presence
- The prostration is itself a *Shariʿah*-bound obligation issued *through* Adam

Shared row: the verse of prostration (Quran 2:34) is a verse of command from Allah to the angels.

The parallel verse to the Children of Israel, used by the author to show that no explicit mention of an intermediary does not entail no intermediary:

> *O Children of Israel, remember My grace which I have bestowed upon you. And fulfill My covenant, and I shall fulfill your covenant, and have awe of Me alone. And believe in what I have sent down, confirming what is with you, and be not the first to disbelieve in it... Establish the prayer, give the zakat, and bow with those who bow.*
> Quran 2:40-43.

The verse names *no intermediary*, yet the *daʿwa*'s settled doctrine holds the command was conveyed *through* the Prophet *Muhammad*. By exact parallel, the prostration verse can carry a command *through* Adam without naming him.

The companion hierarchy of angelic ranks: the book *The Defense*'s further objection rests on conflating the *eternity-abode angels* with the *natural-world angels*. The author separates the two ranks.

- Level 1: All angels
  - Level 2: Eternity-abode angels (the high limits)
    - Level 3: The First Intellect, the universal Soul, the *al-Jadd* (the Grandfather), the *al-Fath* (the Opener), the imagination (the Imagination)
    - Level 3: These bear no assignment, never enter the abode of the body, never stand in the realm of deed
  - Level 2: Natural-world angels
    - Level 3: The angels appointed over the heavens, dwelling in the house of nature
    - Level 3: The creative and prophetic angels
    - Level 3: These DO bear assignment, are charged with the two acts of worship, attain perfection through deeds

The angels commanded to prostrate to Adam are the natural-world angels. They bear assignment. The prostration is itself a raising-by-degrees of the natural-world angels through the *two worships* (the *zahir* and the *batin*) toward the perfection that earns them eternity. Therefore the commissioning of the angels *through Adam* is itself an act of *Shariʿah*.

The companion annotated parallel on Adam's silent *God commands you*: the book *The Defense*'s argument from Adam's silence collapses against the same argument applied to the Prophet's silence.

| Claim | Adam case | Prophet case |
|---|---|---|
| The Lawgiver never said *God commands you to obey me* in this verse | True for Adam at the prostration | True for the Prophet to the Children of Israel in Quran 2:40-43 |
| Therefore the laws cannot be *Shariʿah* obligations | The book *The Defense*'s inference | Would eliminate prayer, *zakat*, and the Prophet's teaching |
| Therefore the inference is invalid | Adam's *Shariʿah* stands | The Prophet's *Shariʿah* stands |
| Therefore *Shariʿah*-rank is settled by | The *Mustafa*-rank, not the verbal formula | The *Mustafa*-rank, not the verbal formula |

The extended consequence: the argument from *the speaker did not say the words* would also eliminate the *Shariʿah of Jesus*, since *he did not strive*, and this is not valid. The *Shariʿah*-rank is the *Mustafa*-rank, not the *speaker's verbal formula*.

## The verse of Shariʿah and the act of teaching the names

A contrast pair plus a self-refuting roster argument.

The book *The Defense*'s textual move rests on the *Shariʿah* verse:

> *He has prescribed for you of the religion that which He enjoined upon Noah, and that which We have revealed to you, and that which We enjoined upon Abraham, Moses, and Jesus, to establish the religion and not be divided therein.*
> Quran 42:13.

The verse names Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and *Muhammad*, but not Adam. If the verse is the roster of *Shariʿah-holders*, Adam is excluded.

The author's first answer: the verse is not a roster. Adam's *Shariʿah-rank* is named by other verses, greater than mere sending.

| Adam-honor verse | What it establishes |
|---|---|
| *O Adam, inform them of their names* (Quran 2:33) | The foundational act of teaching, greater than mere sending; this act of informing is itself the *Shariʿah-rank* |
| *I am going to place a caliph on the earth* (Quran 2:30) | The caliphate, the *successor*-rank of Allah on earth |
| *Allah has chosen Adam and Noah* (Quran 3:33) | The *Mustafa*-rank already established |
| The verse of prostration (Quran 2:34) | The angels' obligation *through* Adam |

The Prophetic witness in the intercession hadith, naming Adam's recognized signs:

> *They will say: "O Adam, you are the father of mankind. Allah created you with His own Hand, and made the angels prostrate before you, and taught you the names of all things. Will you not intercede for us before your Lord? Do you not see what we are suffering?"*
> *Sahih the canonical hadith compiler*, Book 65, Hadith 4712, narrated by Anas ibn Malik.

The three signs in the hadith map exactly onto the three Adam-honor verses:
- *Father of mankind*: head of the cycle
- *Created by Allah's own Hand*: the *Mustafa*-rank
- *Taught the names of all things*: the foundational act of teaching
- *The angels prostrated to him*: the obligation issued through Adam

The *daʿwa* doctrine and the Prophetic tradition meet at the same three signs.

The author's second answer: the roster argument refutes itself.

> *We have revealed to you as We revealed to Noah and the prophets after him, and We revealed to Abraham, Ishmael, Isaac, Jacob, the tribes, Jesus, Job, Jonah, Aaron, and Solomon.*
> Quran 4:163.

The verse names a long roster of revelation-receivers, and Adam is not on it. If exclusion from Quran 42:13's roster proves Adam had no *Shariʿah*, exclusion from Quran 4:163's roster proves Adam received no revelation, which is impossible. Both rosters are non-exhaustive; the inference from Adam's-not-being-named-in-one fails.

## The three souls and the six gates

A nested hierarchy. The anthropology of why a *Shariʿah* is constitutive of human-being-as-such, not contingent on prior fall.

- Level 1: The human being has three souls
  - Level 2: The rational soul
    - Level 3: Has discrimination and thought; knows it has a Creator; naturally submits to Him
    - Level 3: Drives toward the rites of drawing-near
    - Level 3: Bearer of the positive-*Shariʿah* obligations: *qurban*, *sajda*, *ruku'*, *zakat*
  - Level 2: The irascible soul
    - Level 3: The soul that he possesses, by which he is angered; loves to subdue, conquer, take revenge, oppress, kill, and deceive
    - Level 3: Demands a restraining ordinance
    - Level 3: Bearer of the restraining-*Shariʿah*: *qisas* (retribution), *jihad* (the struggle), the holding of the strong from oppressing the weak
  - Level 2: The lustful soul
    - Level 3: From which he seeks refuge and seeks marriage as a necessity
    - Level 3: Demands the ordinances of marriage and property
    - Level 3: Bearer of the laws against cheating, cunning, deceit, and unregulated meeting of the sexes

The six gates of necessity that follow:

| Gate | Domain | Function |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Retribution (*qisas*) | The law of recompense for bodily harm |
| 2 | Marriage | The law regulating the meeting of the sexes and the rights of children |
| 3 | Inheritance and division of property | The law preventing wealth from becoming the cause of strife when persons die |
| 4 | The ordinance for the absent and the dead | The law of testimony, guardianship, rights of those who cannot speak for themselves |
| 5 | The extraction of rights and their return to their masters | The law of judgment between disputants; the procedure of plaintiff and defendant |
| 6 | The ordinance of the dead | The law of what to do with the body: burial, burning, casting away in a friendly manner |

Above the six gates: the *non-necessary* ordinances, the duties of worshipping Allah and drawing closer to Him, by which perfection is attained. The *positive worship*.

The structural verdict: if the six gates are constitutive of human-being-as-such, and humans existed at the head of human history, then the six gates existed at the head of human history. And the *positive worship* must have been *added* at the head, which is exactly Adam's *sending*. The *Shariʿah of Adam* is the *positive worship* added to the *necessary social ordinances* that human-being-as-such already required.

## The Shariʿah and the limit-keeper, as one institution

> *The Imamate is the standing of the limits in the world; without the limit-keeper the limits do not stand, and without the limits the soul destroys what it has been given. The Lawgiver's appointment of the Imam is one act with the appointment of the Shariʿah, for the limits without the keeper are letters without a reciter, and the keeper without the limits is a tongue without speech. The Imamate is, accordingly, not posterior to the founding of religion but constitutive of it: the speaker-Prophet and the foundation (the foundation) at the head of every cycle are the limit-and-the-keeper made one institution at the head of human history.*
> The author *al-Kirmani*, *al-Masabih fi al-Imama* (*Lamps on the Imamate*), referenced from this book, Bab 9, Fasl 14.

The two faces of the one institution:

| Face | Adam-cycle instance | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Speaker-Prophet | Adam | The drawer of the limits |
| The foundation | Seth | The keeper of what the speaker drew |
| The limits | Adam's *Shariʿah* | The body of religion |

A complementary witness from the *Fatimid caliph*'s interpretive treatise:

## Adam and Noah, Shariʿah-establishment compared

A contrast pair refuting the book *The Defense*'s Noah-parallel argument.

Column A: Noah's Shariʿah-establishment (the book *The Defense* affirms)
- Pre-condition: Noah's people played at violating the limits, *al-Wadd*, *al-Suwa'*, *al-Yaghuth* (Quran 71:23)
- Reason for sending: the violation of the limits demands a re-establishment
- Outcome: whoever perished perished with knowledge; whoever lived lived with knowledge
- Status: Shariʿah affirmed for Noah

Column B: Adam's Shariʿah-establishment (the book *The Defense* denies)
- Pre-condition: the world before Adam, the angels declare *Will You place in it one who corrupts it and sheds blood?* (Quran 2:30)
- Reason for sending (book *The Defense* claims): none, since the prior world was free of violation
- Outcome (book *The Defense* claims): no Shariʿah needed
- Status: Shariʿah denied for Adam

The author's reversal of the parallel:
- Violation-of-the-limits presupposes the limits themselves
- Noah's people violated limits that *were already established*
- The limits were established by the *prior speaker*
- The prior speaker at the head of the chain is *Adam*
- If no limits in Adam's time, no violation in Noah's time
- The Noah-parallel is the *strongest evidence* that Adam had a *Shariʿah*

The book *The Defense* is refuted by the very parallel he proposed.

## Monotheism through the world's structure, and the inversion at the high limits

An annotated structure with a refutation that exposes a possibly-planted doctrine.

The book *The Defense*'s claim (sub-chapter nineteen):

| Premise | Content |
|---|---|
| The world's structure | Is *itself* an indication of *monotheism* |
| The composition of bodies, the pairing of opposites | Carries the same intelligibility as sent revelation |
| Sent revelation with signs and deeds | Risks casting many of creation into *anthropomorphism* |
| Therefore | The sending of speakers is not necessary; the bare world suffices |

The author's three-step refutation:

| Step | Move | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | If the world suffices, the messengers are doing what others already accomplish | A virtue Allah has no need of |
| 2 | The sending of messengers is the most basic fact of religion | The argument eliminates religion itself |
| 3 | The world's structure is NOT sufficient on its own | The speakers teach the *interpretation* by which the world's structure *becomes* a sign of *monotheism* |

The author's verdict on the doctrine itself:

| Aspect | Claim |
|---|---|
| Origin | Not from *our* belief, not from the doctrine of *our loyalists* |
| Likely source | The doctrine of the philosophers, alien to the *daʿwa* |
| Authorship | Perhaps planted by adversaries in the book *The Defense*'s copy |
| Status | Not a genuine the book *The Defense* doctrine, attributed only because copied as if his |

The structural point about the *daʿwa*'s settled doctrine: the *world's bare structure* and the *sent speakers* are not redundant. The speakers *teach* the interpretation by which the world's structure becomes a sign. Without the speakers, the world's structure becomes the occasion for *anthropomorphism*, not the proof against it. The inversion is at the high limits: the more elaborate the world's signs are taken in isolation, the more they cast the unguided viewer into anthropomorphism. The speakers are necessary precisely *because* the world's signs are elaborate.

## The closing voice

A verbatim closing the *daʿwa* places in the voice of *the Commander of the Faithful* at the head of the chain of speakers and their foundations.

> *The chain that runs in time is the same chain that the Pen writes upon the Tablet. At its head stands the first Speaker, who is Adam. His Shariʿah is the body of the religion; his Foundation, Seth, is its spirit. Whoever rejects the body rejects the spirit; whoever rejects the spirit rejects the body. The two are the gift of Allah at the head of human history, and from them the chain runs down through Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad, and on to the Qa'im at the seventh.*
> Attributed in the *daʿwa* tradition to Imam *the Commander of the Faithful*.

The two faces of the one institution at the head of every cycle:

| Face | Identity in Adam's cycle | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| Speaker-Prophet | Adam | The body of the religion, the drawer of the limits |
| Foundation | Seth | The spirit of the religion, the keeper of what was drawn |

To reject the body is to reject the spirit; to reject the spirit is to reject the body. Adam and Seth are inseparable. The chain runs from them through six more speakers and lands on the seventh awaited *Qa'im*. The architecture is one, and the *Shariʿah of Adam* is the architecture's first joint.
