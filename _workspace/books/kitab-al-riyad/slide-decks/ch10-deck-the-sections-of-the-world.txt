# The Sections of the World

*The visual companion to Chapter Seven. The audio chapter is the prose; this file is its architecture made structural. Same concepts, same depth, restructured for slide-deck rendering.*

## Where this chapter picks up

A two-column hand-off from Chapter Six into Chapter Seven. Chapter Six settled the architecture; Chapter Seven settles the world the architecture makes.

Column A: What Chapter Six settled (the architecture)

- *Prime matter* and form: inseparable correlates, actor and acted-upon, the first emanation
- Motion and stillness: opposites that do not meet in the same place at the same time
- Soul and spirit: one non-bodily reality named by two aspects
- Above the First Effect: no motion, no rest of any kind
- Closing formula: the Creative IS the creativity, in the name of monotheism

Column B: What Chapter Seven turns to (the world the architecture makes)

- The sections of the world: from the encompassing ocean down through the throne, the planets, the four elements, and the born
- The human's place among those sections
- Is the human one part among others, paired with the horse?
- Is the human the counterpart of the whole world, with no peer?
- Is the human the fruit of the higher world only?
- Or is the human a microcosm that is also a *between*-state, a part of all the worlds at once?

Shared row: the question is not idle. The placement of the human determines whether *walaya*, the limits, the inner interpretation, and the doctrine of *Ma'ad* can stand the way the call tradition needs them to stand.

## Sub-chapter one, what the world actually is

A contrast pair. *The Correction* and *The Defense* state two architectural pictures; the author refuses both.

Column A: *The Correction*

- World composed of: four jewels, four structures, four classes
- Three classes without humankind; the fourth is humankind
- Pairing: of essences (knotted-and-dissolved are one essence; gold-and-silver one pair; ruby-and-emerald one pair)
- The horse's pair: within the animal class (elephant, falcon, lion)
- Humankind's pair: none, because humankind alone possesses the *rational soul*
- Humankind is the *counterpart of the world*; gathers all the world's jewels; called the small world, the microcosm

Column B: *The Defense*

- The state of pairing is *not only* sexual union; pairing is also *acceptance*, receivership
- By the broader category: the dense lower world is the pair of the subtle upper world
- More distance between dense-and-subtle than between horse-and-human
- The horse is the human's pair *because it accepts teaching* from him more than other animals do

Shared row: both accept that humankind is the *counterpart* of the world. They disagree on what *pairing* means.

The author's refusal: the divisions of the world are more numerous than what either contender has named.

## The actual sections of the world

A hierarchy of the world from inside out, named by the author.

- Center: the inert mineral core
- Above the center, in ascending layers:
  - Earth (one of the four elements)
  - Water (one of the four elements)
  - Air (one of the four elements)
  - Fire (one of the four elements)
  - The planets (the seven heavens)
  - The throne (*al-'Arsh*, also a name for the First Intellect in Chapter Two)
- Outermost limit: the encompassing ocean

And in addition to all the above: *the born* (the generated beings, the world's *births*).

## The three genera of the born, and the human as fourth-by-distinction

A hierarchy tree of the generated beings, with the human added as a fourth-by-distinction within the animal genus.

- Genus 1: Metal, *the nearest of the four arches*
  - Definition: everything that exists in a specific place in the earth without growing in its parts
  - Species: gold, silver, copper, gems, salts
  - Distinguishing principle: exists in the earth without growing
- Genus 2: Plant
  - Definition: all that grows from the earth
  - Species: bush, tree, and the kinds between them
  - Distinguishing principle: *grows*
- Genus 3: Animal
  - Definition: all that moves of those that have species
  - Species: what flies and what does not
  - Distinguishing principle: *moves and senses*
- Human: a fourth-by-distinction *within* the animal genus
  - Distinguished by: the speaking soul (*the soul al-the speaker-propheta*) that comes to rest in the human
  - NOT a fourth genus; a fourth essence by distinction inside genus 3

The generated beings do not exceed these three genera. The human is *of* the animal genus, distinguished by a kind of consciousness that no other species shares.

## Sub-chapter two, humankind has many pairs

A two-part argument from the author against the no-peer claim. The author shows that the no-peer claim is *impossible from two angles*.

Angle 1: classification

- Sub-chapter one settled: the human is *of* the animal genus
- A *type* within a genus pairs with the other types within its genus
- All types are forms of one another in the chapter on quality
- Therefore: the human pairs with the other types in the genus

Angle 2: the microcosm itself

- The human is similar in structure, morals, actions, conditions, motions, and natures to *all* the universe presents
- This similarity is the very reason it is called the small world
- To be the small world is to be *like* every part of the world
- To be like something is to *pair* with it
- Therefore: the human pairs with *all things*, by occasion and case

Conclusion: the microcosm is not what has *no peer*; the microcosm is what has *all peers* by occasion.

## The pairing-by-occasion matrix

A comparison matrix. Pairing shifts by what the human is like, by the content of choice.

| If the human is | Pair is | Domain |
|---|---|---|
| Good, virtuous, wise, religious, knowing, pure | Angels | The higher rank |
| Courageous, brave, strong, a helper | Lions | Predator-power |
| Unjust, cruel, murderous | Wolves and cunning foxes | Predator-malice |
| Treacherous, thievish | Crows and hooks | Snatch-by-stealth |
| Deceitful, cunning, overpowering | Tiger | Stealth-and-strength |
| Adulterous, immoral, given to amusement and play | Monkeys | Driven by appetite |

The principle settled: pairing tracks resemblance. The human writes his own pairing by *content of choice*. Whatever a person is content to resemble, by actions, morals, customs, or any part of them, that becomes his pair.

The doctrinal payload: the human is not the *unmatched part*; the human is the *plastic part*, the part whose resemblance to other parts is *chosen* rather than given. That is what the speaking soul does: it gives the human the capacity to resemble at will. The pair is what the part has decided to become.

## The Commander of the Faithful, and the perfume-seller hadith

Two anchor passages from the tradition, preserved as written.

> *Your remedy is within you, but you do not perceive it. Your illness is from yourself, but you do not see it. You suppose yourself a small body, yet in you is folded the greater world. You are the manifest book, by whose letters what is hidden becomes apparent.*
> Traditionally attributed to *Imam Ali*, the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him), collected in *Diwan al-Imam Ali*.

The Commander of the Faithful names the same structural fact: the human is the *small body* in whom the *greater world* is folded. *The manifest book*, by whose letters what is hidden becomes apparent. The pairing-by-resemblance is what writes the letters of the book. The remedy is within: the choice of which pair to write into the self is the human's own.

> *The example of a good companion and a bad one is like that of a perfume-seller and a blacksmith. As for the perfume-seller, either he will give you some perfume, or you will buy from him, or you will perceive a pleasant smell from him. As for the blacksmith, either he will burn your clothes or you will perceive a foul smell from him.*
> *Sahih al-Bukhari*, Book 73, Hadith 5534, narrated by Abu Musa al-Ash'ari.

The Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) runs the same architecture at the level of the everyday choice of companion. The perfume-seller and the blacksmith are the two pair-types. *Sitting with* one or the other writes the resemblance into the self. The pleasant smell reaches you whether you buy or not; the foul smell reaches you whether you wish it or not. The pairing happens *by occasion*: by the company one is content to stand near. The human becomes what he keeps near.

## Sub-chapter three, the angels as the strict pair

A directed argument. *The Defense* claimed the horse's special status rests on acceptance. The author shows acceptance does not isolate the horse, and then states the positive doctrine.

Move 1: against the horse's monopoly on acceptance

- If the horse becomes the human's pair *on the basis of acceptance and education*
- Then the horse has no priority over other animals that also accept teaching
- The dog accepts teaching
- The falcon accepts teaching
- The elephant accepts teaching
- Therefore: on *The Defense*'s footing, the human's pair *multiplies*, not narrows

Move 2: the positive doctrine

- The pair of humankind in the strict sense is the *angels*
- But the strict pair requires a strict definition

The strict-sense definition of *humankind*, as the author lays it out.

| Counted as humankind in the strict sense | NOT counted as humankind in the strict sense |
|---|---|
| The prophets | Those merely in the *form* of humankind whose souls are the souls of beasts |
| The guardians (*awsiya'*, *awliya'*) | Those whose souls are the souls of predators |
| The imams of the Ahl al-Bayt (peace be upon them all) | Monkeys-in-form, donkeys-in-form, foxes-in-form, lions-in-form, wolves-in-form |
| Those who follow in their footsteps, uphold their obedience, worship Allah with both worships | |
| Those who keep the *zahir* (outer *sharia*) and the *batin* (inner interpretation) | |

The followers, walking in the traces of the imams, are distinguished from the beasts even as they resemble them in form. They will be saved in their entirety. The chain of *walaya* runs from the Prophet through the imams of his pure family to those who follow them. He who takes them as guardian *is one of them*.

The doctrinal payload: the strict pair of humankind in the strict sense is the angels *because* the strict-sense *humankind* is the rank that resembles the angels by occasion: pure obedience, pure worship, pure adherence to command-and-prohibition. The pair-by-occasion principle of sub-chapter two gives the architecture. Chapter Five's formula of *two sides, one part natural, one part divine* meets its named completion here: the divine side, written into the soul by virtue, is what pairs with the angels.

## Sub-chapter four, Bandhaqlis read by the call

A contrast pair. *The Defense* invokes Bandhaqlis (Empedocles, the pre-Socratic philosopher of Acragas in Sicily, c. 490 to 430 BC) on the souls. The author corrects the reading.

Column A: *The Defense*'s reading of Bandhaqlis

- The *bestial soul* speaks of *love* (in the original doctrine, *philotēs*, one of two cosmic forces)
- The *speaking soul* returns good for what is offered, educates, lifts
- The horse's image is the most desirable among bestial-soul images
- The horse is the most eager to receive the effects of the speaking soul
- Therefore: the horse pairs with the speaking soul, taken as the human

Column B: the author's reading on the inner interpretation

- The doctrine of the souls is not drawn from Greek-and-Syriac transmissions of Bandhaqlis
- The doctrine is drawn from *the Book of Allah* and *the sayings of the pure Imams*
- The reformers, had they lived in the author's own time, would have drawn from the high the call synthesis of the Cairo court and *spoken differently*
- Read on the inner interpretation:
  - The *bestial soul* = those in the *form* of humankind who would accept education, discipline, reason
  - The *speaking soul* = the teachers, namely the prophets, guardians, imams (peace be upon them), and their followers
- The relation: *kindness, benefit, raising in degrees according to acceptance*
- The teachers are *the mothers of humankind*: they give birth by the inner interpretation, nurse with the milk of inner knowledge, raise through degrees toward *humankind in the strict sense*

Shared row: both readings agree the speaking soul *kindles its judgment* in the bestial soul fit to receive. They disagree on what *fitness to receive* means.

The author's verdict: the human-formed receivers are *more accepting* than any horse, by the speaking soul planted in them. The kindness, patience, and care the guardians-and-imams pour into the raising of the *mu'minun* is *far more* than any horse-trainer's. The horse-and-human reading of Bandhaqlis cannot stand.

## The Prophetic root and the Qur'anic anchor

Two anchors that ground the call reading of Bandhaqlis. Preserved as written, with attribution.

> *Souls are conscripted soldiers; those of them that recognize one another come together in harmony, and those that do not recognize one another fall into discord.*
> *Sahih Muslim*, Book 45, Hadith 6376, narrated by Aisha.

The Prophetic doctrine of *recognition-as-pairing*. Souls find their pair by inner affinity that runs prior to any external pairing. The sensual soul that accepts the ruling of the speaking soul *recognizes* it. The speaking soul that kindles its judgment in the sensual soul *recognizes* the soul fit to receive. Where the soul is not fit, recognition fails and discord follows. The architectural reading of *raising-by-degrees* rests on this Prophetic root.

> *O reassured soul, return to your Lord, pleased and pleasing; enter among My servants; enter My Paradise.*
> Quran 89:27 to 30, translation rendered after Sahih International.

The *reassured soul*, *the soul al-mutma'inna*, is the soul *raised through the degrees of acceptance* until it reaches *reassurance*, the rank that pairs with the angels.

| Verse phrase | What it names in the architecture |
|---|---|
| *O reassured soul* | The rank reached through *raising-by-degrees-according-to-acceptance* |
| *Return to your Lord* | The *Ma'ad* of that soul to its rank-of-origin |
| *Pleased and pleasing* | Pleased by what it has received; pleasing to what receives it |
| *Enter My servants* | The rank of those who serve Allah by both worships |
| *Enter My Paradise* | The completion of the ascending chain |

## Sub-chapter five, the architectural map of the microcosm

A comparison matrix. The book *The Correction* and the book *The Defense* map the microcosm onto the world differently. The author judges which mapping preserves the natural joints.

Rows: the four ranks of the human, with their world-parallels under each contender's mapping.

| Human rank | *The Correction*'s parallel | *The Defense*'s parallel | Author's verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Body (knotted and dissolved): bone, flesh, nerves, cartilage; blood, semen, sweat | World's bodies: the knotted and the dissolved | Knotted-bodies / dissolved-bodies as two parts of one essence by genus | *The Correction* preferred; the body is *one essence* in the strict architectural sense |
| Growth: hair, body increase | The growing species: the kinds of trees, the plant genus | Dissolved bodies (mapped to growth because dissolution and growth share *motion*) | *The Correction* preferred; growth-to-plants is natural |
| Animal sense: sight, hearing, motion, imagination, desire | The animal genus: the beasts of the world | Body (mapped to sense), and animal-sperm (mapped as another joint) | *The Correction* preferred; sense-to-animals is natural |
| Speaking soul (*the soul al-the speaker-propheta*): the unique fourth essence | The thing that singled the human out, preferred him over the world, made him its master | The speaker stands *against* the animal | *The Correction* preferred; speaking-soul as unique fourth is natural |

The four-essences chain that *The Correction*'s mapping settles, in ascending order.

| Essence | Composition | Soul-rank | Parallel in the world |
|---|---|---|---|
| First essence | Bodies as such, *prime matter*-informed | None (not joined to any breath) | The world's bodies (the knotted and the dissolved) |
| Second essence | Body plus vegetative soul | Developing soul | The plant genus |
| Third essence | Body plus vegetative and sensitive souls | Animal soul | The animal genus |
| Fourth essence | Body plus vegetative, sensitive, and speaking souls | Speaking soul (*the soul al-the speaker-propheta*) | None, the human stands alone in this rank |

The author's first verdict on sub-chapter five: *The Correction* wins because its mapping preserves the natural joints. Growth-to-plants is natural; sense-to-animals is natural; speaking-soul-as-unique-fourth is natural. *The Defense*'s re-mapping crosses the natural joints.

The author's deeper verdict: the human is not the counterpart only of *this* world. The human is the counterpart of *all* the worlds, since he is the *collector* of all the things that preceded him in existence. The true map is *both-worlds-microcosm*.

## The author on the human's composite, and Imam *al-Mu'izz* on the human as fruit

Two anchor passages preserved as written.

> *Man is composed in his being of the body that is informed by* prime matter *and form, the vegetative soul that draws nourishment from the elements, the animal soul that perceives the sensible by the five senses, and the speaking soul that ascends by the gift conveyed through the* limits *of the* call. *The whole human being is the chain of receivership made one.*
> The author *Hamid al-Din al-Kirmani*, *the author's other treatise*, on the composite architecture of the human being.

> *Man is the purpose of the second world and the end of the action of nature; the world of creation produces him as its fruit, and the world of emanation perfects him as its receiver; he is one being whose body is the chain made dense and whose soul is the chain made gentle.*
> *Imam al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah* Ma'add ibn Isma'il (peace be upon him), the Fatimid caliph of the Cairo court, *the Fatimid caliph's interpretive treatise*.

Imam al-Mu'izz names in his interpretive treatise the very doctrine sub-chapter six is settling. The lower world *produces* the human by nature; the higher world *perfects* him by the gift conveyed through the limits. The natural composite is the chain *dense*; the speaking soul informed by the universal Intellect is the chain *gentle*. The human is the single being where the dense-chain and the gentle-chain meet.

## Sub-chapter seven, humankind in a *between*-state

A quadrant map of the human's place in the architecture of the two worlds. The conceptual space is qualitative: matter-and-image, above-and-below, the *between*-state of *barzakh*.

Space: the human in the architecture of the two worlds.

| Position | What stands there | Role for the human |
|---|---|---|
| Above (the higher world, the creative-emanative) | The universal Intellect and the universal Soul | The *parents in the highest and most honorable picture*; the source of the *course of the image* (the *sura*, the *form*, the *actor*) |
| Below (this world, the natural-composite) | *Prime matter*, the elements, the genera | The *course of matter* (the *acted-upon*); what runs in the human from below |
| The *between* (the meeting of the two worlds at the rank of the human) | The human itself, in a *between*-state | Matter-and-image jointly; *bayna*, *barzakh* |
| The mediator at the *between* | The *limits of the call*, the prophets, imams, *hujjas*, *du'at* | The *midwife* by whose hand the gift of the higher world is delivered into the receiver-in-human-form |

The doctrinal landing: the human is *in a state of existence between the two worlds*. Not *of* this world alone. Not *of* that world alone. *Between*. The image the book *The Correction* offers, and the author lets stand, is the boy between his two parents. A child is not purely the father; not purely the mother. The child is *from both, and from them he came to be*. So too the human between the two worlds.

What the human receives from each world.

| World | What runs in the human from it | Architectural name |
|---|---|---|
| This world | The course of matter (the *prime matter*, the *acted-upon* of Chapter Six) | The *acceptable* from below, what receives the form |

## The *barzakh* anchor and the architecture of the limits

Two anchor passages that seal the *between*-state in the tradition. Preserved as written.

> *And it is He who has released [simultaneously] the two seas, one fresh and sweet and one salty and bitter, and He placed between them a barrier and prohibiting partition.*
> Quran 25:53, Surah al-Furqan, translation rendered after Sahih International.

The two seas, *the gentle and the dense*, *the fresh and the salty*, meet without mingling. *A barrier and prohibiting partition* keeps each in its own kind even as they touch. The verse names what sub-chapter seven names. The human stands at the *barzakh* of the two worlds. Matter from below, image from above; the two run in him without confusion of kinds. *The acceptable* from each meets at the rank that *is* the meeting. The *prohibiting partition* is what holds the architectural distinction even as the gift is conveyed across it. In the architecture sub-chapter seven is settling, that partition is what the *limits of the call* are: the two seas meet at the human, and the partition that lets them meet without mingling is the chain of receivership at which the gift descends.

> *The limits are the limits set by Allah for the conveyance of the gift; the speaker-prophet is the first hadd; the foundation is the second; the imam is the third; the hujja is the fourth; the da'i is the fifth; the ma'dhun is the sixth; the mukasir is the seventh; through this descending chain the speaking soul reaches the receiver in the form of humankind, and through the same chain ascending the reassured soul returns to its Lord.*
> The author *Hamid al-Din al-Kirmani*, *the author's other treatise*, on the architecture of the limits.

A genealogy chain of the descending limits, from the higher world to the receiver-in-human-form.

   ↓ delivers to

   ↓ delivers to

   ↓ delivers to

The ascending chain, when the receiver has been raised by degrees: the *reassured soul* returns by the same chain to its Lord, *pleased and pleasing*. The midwife is what sub-chapter four named, the *mothers of humankind*, taken now in its architectural sense.

## The political stake of the chapter

A contrast pair. The author's refusals are not philosophical exercises; each protects a load-bearing doctrine of the call.

Column A: if the rival claim held

- *The Correction*'s four-classes scheme: the chain of receivership at the natural-world end would lose its rank-by-rank discipline
- The no-peer claim: the moral architecture (pairing-by-occasion as the *choosing* of one's resemblance) would lose its hold
- *The Defense*'s horse-by-acceptance gloss: the doctrine of the limits would slip into a beast-trainer image and the *walaya* of the imams over the believers would lose its rank
- Bandhaqlis taken literally: the architecture of raising-by-degrees would have no purchase
- *The Defense*'s re-mapping of the microcosm: the natural joints of the chain would cross and the architectural conformity would lose its fit

Column B: what the author's refusal protects

- The chain of receivership at the natural-world end keeps its rank-by-rank discipline
- The moral architecture stands: pairing-by-occasion is the choosing of one's own resemblance
- The doctrine of the limits stands: the *walaya* of the imams over the believers has its rank
- The architecture of raising-by-degrees has its purchase: from the *bestial soul* through the *reassured soul* to the rank that returns to its Lord
- The natural joints of the chain are preserved; the architectural conformity holds

Shared row: the chain whose head is monotheism, whose foot is the *Ma'ad*, and whose midstream is the limits by whose hand the gift is conveyed stands at every joint.

## What comes next

A two-row hand-off. Chapter Eight inherits the architecture Chapter Seven has settled.

| What Chapter Seven leaves settled | What Chapter Eight will turn to |
|---|---|
| The human as the counterpart of *all* the worlds, in a *between*-state of matter-and-image | *Qada* (judgment) and *qadar* (destiny): how the chain runs *in time* |
| The strict pair of humankind is the angels; the strict-sense *humankind* walks the traces of the imams | How what is decreed at the head of the chain becomes what is measured at the rank of the receiver |
| The *limits of the call* as the midwife by whose hand the gift is conveyed | How the *reassured soul* receives both judgment-from-above and destiny-as-the-measure-of-its-acceptance |

The image the chapter leaves the reader with: *The Correction* and *The Defense* arguing about whether the horse pairs with humankind, whether the world's classes are four or three-plus-distinction, whether the speaking soul singles the human out or stands against the animal. The author, generations later, settling the doctrine that the human is the counterpart of *all the worlds*, that he stands in a *between*-state, matter from below and image from above; that his strict pair is the angels because his strict-self is the rank that has chosen virtue; that his teachers are the *limits of the call*, the midwives by whose hand the gift of the higher world is delivered into the receiver-in-the-form-of-humankind; that the chain runs from the encompassing ocean to the human-as-microcosm-of-all-the-worlds; that the receiver, having been raised by degrees, becomes the *reassured soul* that *returns to its Lord, pleased and pleasing*. *You suppose yourself a small body, yet in you is folded the greater world.* The architecture holds. The chain runs. The monotheism the chapter is built to protect stands at every rank.
