# Slide Deck Framing, EP14: Monotheism and the Critique of The Harvest

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio. They have not read *The Harvest*, *The Correction*, or *The Defense*.

## Core Principle

Each slide must add visual information the audio cannot carry. Slides exist to make the sixteen refutations visible at once as one grid, to hold the First-Intellect-versus-acquired-intellect distinction as a durable contrast, and to land the closing identity (creativity IS the Creator) as the book's architectural settlement.

## Visual Priorities

- The Refutation Grid: a sixteen-row comparison matrix; columns for proposition, failure, implication, counter. Spine slide; appears early.
- The threefold-impossibility annotated structure for sub-chapter two.
- The thing-and-nothing contrast pair: ordinary sense versus positive predicate.
- The Creator-of-His-Creator reductio as a process flow.
- The no-third-intermediary relation diagram.
- The closing identity rendered as a single architectural slide.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a sub-chapter title.
- No literal illustrations: no calligraphy, no mosques, no abstract Sufi imagery.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams.
- No spatial depiction of God or the rank above the First Intellect.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide must add visual information not present in the audio.
- Use a comparison matrix for the sixteen refutations; rows for sub-chapters, columns for proposition, failure, implication, counter.
- For the First-Intellect-versus-acquired-intellect distinction, use side-by-side columns.
- For the Creator-of-His-Creator reductio, use a process flow with explicit transitions.
- Place the closing identity on its own slide as a single line: *creativity is the Creator*.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
