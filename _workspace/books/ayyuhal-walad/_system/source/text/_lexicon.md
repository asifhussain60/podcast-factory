# Lexicon — Ayyuhal Walad

Running list of proper nouns, transliterated terms, and technical vocabulary used in this book. Cross-references `_phonetics.md` for phonetic forms and the SHARED_ARABIC manifest for canonical authority.

## Named figures

- Imam Abu Hamid Muhammad al-Ghazali (1058–1111 CE) — the author. Alias: **Ghazali**.
- Irfan Hasan — the English translator (from Urdu).
- Prophet Muhammad — referenced throughout.
- Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib (AS) — quoted in ch01 (the saying about striving). Alias: **Imam Ali**.
- Abu Bakr al-Siddiq (RA) — quoted in ch01 (the cages-of-birds saying).
- Junaid al-Baghdadi — Sufi master Ghazali himself cites; the dream saying. Alias: **Junaid**.
- Hasan al-Basri — early ascetic; cited in ch01 (the cold drink) and ch04 (preaching).
- Sufyan al-Thawri — early jurist-ascetic; cited in ch01 (the four angels of the night). Alias: **Sufyan**.
- Shaqiq al-Balkhi — Hatim's teacher (ch02). Alias: **Shaqeeq**. (Source spells *Shafeeq*.)
- Hatim bin Ism al-Asamm — the student of Shaqeeq (ch02). Alias: **Haatim**. (Source spells *Hatim Ism*.)
- Shibli — Sufi master; cited in ch01 (the 4,000-hadith story). Alias: **Shibli**.
- Dhu'l-Nun al-Misri — Sufi master; cited in ch03 (courage to exert with the soul). Alias: **Dhu'l-Nun**. (Source spells *Zun Noon Misri*.)
- Luqman — Quranic sage; cited in ch01 (the rooster admonition).
- Aisha (Aisha Siddiqa) — wife of the Prophet; cited in ch05 (the stockpiling-food example).
- Prophet Isa (Jesus) — referenced in ch01 (the corpse Gospel narration) and ch04 (the cure-fools saying).
- Khidr (AS) — referenced in ch03 (the Khidr-and-Musa exchange).
- Prophet Musa (Moses) — referenced in ch03 (the Khidr exchange).
- Abdullah ibn Umar (RA) — referenced in ch01.
- Sa'd ibn Mu'adh (RA) — referenced in ch01 (the Throne shook).

## Works cited

- *Ihya Ulum al-Din* — Ghazali, *Revival of the Religious Sciences*. Source spelling: *Ahya al-Uloom ad-Deen*. Cited in ch01, ch04, ch05. Use canonical form everywhere.
- *Kimiya al-Sa'ada* — Ghazali, *Alchemy of Happiness*. Cited in ch01.
- *Jawahir al-Quran* — Ghazali, *Gems of the Quran*. Cited in ch01.
- *Arba'in* — Ghazali, *Forty Steps*. Cited in ch01.
- *Minhaj al-Abidin* — Ghazali, *Path of Worshippers*. Cited in ch01.
- *Ayyuhal Walad* — the source itself (referred to as *the letter* in chapter prose).
- *Majmu'a Rasail Imam Ghazali* — Ghazali's three-volume collection of short books; *Ayyuhal Walad* is one of them.
- *Sahih Sitta* — the six canonical Sunni hadith collections. Cited in ch05.
