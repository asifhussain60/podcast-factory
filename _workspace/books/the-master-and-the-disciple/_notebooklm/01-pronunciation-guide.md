**[NotebookLM Instruction]** Use this pronunciation table as the authoritative spoken form. When the same name appears in any chapter, pronounce it the same way every time. ## Honorific protocol

**[NotebookLM Instruction]**

- For the Prophet Muhammad: first mention in any episode, say "Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him." Subsequent mentions: "the Prophet" or "Muhammad" alone.
- For Ali: first mention in any episode, say "Ali, peace be upon him." Subsequent mentions: "Ali."
- For other Imaams: first mention in any episode, say "Imaam Ja'far as-Saadiq, peace be upon him" (etc.). Subsequent mentions: "Imaam Ja'far as-Saadiq" or simply the name.
- For the prophets in general (Aadam, Moosaa, Eisa, Yoosuf, Ibrahim, etc.): on first mention, "the prophet Moosaa" is sufficient; do not stack multiple honorifics into a single mention.
- **Do not** turn every mention into a chain of honorifics. The chapter prose models the correct cadence — match it.

## Do NOT pronounce as

| Term | Do not say |
|---|---|
| Allaah | "AL-luh," "Al-LAH" (single-syllable). |
| Sharee-ah | "Sha-REE-uh" — keep the open "-ah" ending. |
| Wilaayah | "WIL-yah" (too compressed). |
| Saa-lih | "SAY-lih" or "Sah-LEE." |
| Aboo Maa-lik | "uh-BOO MAH-lik" (rushed first syllable). |
| Taa-weel | "Taw-WEEL" or "tah-VEEL" — keep "TAA-weel," stress on first syllable. |
| Baatin | "BAT-in" or "BAY-tin" — keep long-A: "BAA-tin." |

If a term is not in this guide and you cannot infer pronunciation, **stop and read it slowly letter-by-letter** rather than guessing.
