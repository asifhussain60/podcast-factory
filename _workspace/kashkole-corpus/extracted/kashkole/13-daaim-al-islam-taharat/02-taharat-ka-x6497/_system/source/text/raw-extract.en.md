Interpretation of purity:

Purification is permissible only with pure water. If there is any adulteration in the water (even if it is perfume or syrup), then the water is not suitable for ablution or bathing. Clean water removes dirt, while dirt cannot be cleaned with dirt (adulterated water).

Wudhu refers to a Mustajib taking the pledge of Imam al-Zaman (a.s.) and accepting his invitation, knowing his limits, and disassociating himself from his enemies. The inner part of prayer is to enter into the Imam's call and stay in his invitation by connecting with the boundaries of Imam al-Zaman (a.s.) and the saints. Prayer is not possible without ablution, and ablution cannot be done without intention, and the intention cannot be done without the name of Allah, the Most Merciful.

Pure water is an example of knowledge. Just as the impurity of the body is washed away by water, so the impurity of the self is washed away by knowledge. Just as outward prayer is not permissible without purification, it is not permissible to teach interpretation and facts to anyone without taking a pledge. For taking a covenant is the essence of purity, and the knowledge of truth is the invitation to prayer.
