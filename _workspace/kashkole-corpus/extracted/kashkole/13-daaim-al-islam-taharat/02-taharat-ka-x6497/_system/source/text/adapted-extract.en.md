## An Account of Ṭahāra (Ritual Purity)

Impurity arises from impurity itself. The transition from a state of impurity to a state of purity is called ṭahāra (ritual purity). Since humanity comprises both body and soul, the purification of the soul must accompany the purification of the body. External purity is achieved through the completion of the prescribed ablutions; inner purity is attained through understanding the spiritual motives underlying these outward actions. The Prophet—may God's blessings and peace be upon him—stated: "Prayer is not valid without purity." This principle encompasses knowledge of the limits of ablution, ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ and comprehending these limits constitutes the spiritual foundation of the call to prayer. Since prayer without understanding the boundaries and guidance of the Imam ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ is not permissible, so too is prayer without ablution forbidden.

The Prophet declared: "Whoever perfects his ablution and then departs from his house toward the mosque for prayer—that believer remains in prayer until his ablution is nullified." In other words, through ablution the believer enters into the Imam's endorsement and remains under his spiritual protection.

## The Perfect Wuḍūʾ — Repeating Each Limb Three Times

The Messenger of God—may God's blessings and peace be upon him—declared: "Prayer rests upon four pillars, and those who neglect them are deceiving themselves." These four pillars are:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Perfect Ablution | Genuflection (*rukūʿ*) | Prostration (*sujūd*) | Reverence |

Perfect ablution stands foremost among these obligations. Its perfection is realized by washing each organ three times. Water represents the bounties distributed to believers in three divisions. Because of these three spiritual aspects, each organ of ablution must be washed thrice. Similarly, water is commanded to be consumed in three sips. Knowledge itself divides into three categories: the revealed law (sharīʿa), esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl), and spiritual realities (ḥaqāʾiq). Washing or anointing each organ three times symbolizes recognition of the three limits of the summons (daʿwa), namely ⟪ar:ناطق، اساس، امام⟫ the Speaker (nāṭiq), the Foundation (asās), and the Imam.

When a believer performs ablution once, he comes to the notice of the Summoner (dāʿī)—the preacher comes to know him. ⟪ar:حُسن نیت⟫ When he repeats the act a second time with sincere intention, God grants him twofold reward, and he enters into the guardianship (wilāya) of the Imam of the Age, purifying his inner state. Upon the third performance, the Prophet—may peace be upon him⟪ar:ناطق⟫—declares: "This is my ablution and the ablution of all Prophets whom I lead."⟪ar:'کمال'⟫

To wash each organ thrice signifies that when a believer fulfills his obligations threefold—that is, when he attains knowledge of the Word ⟪ar:داعی وقت، امام الزمان⟫⟪ar:ناطق شریعت⟫ and comes to know the Speaker of the Law—his station is elevated and he becomes established within the limits of religion. In essence, ablution represents one pathway toward inclusion within the Imam's spiritual boundaries.

| --- | --- | --- |
| Recognition by the Preacher | Entry into the Imam's Guardianship | Attainment of Prophetic Perfection |
| Outward Purity | Inner Purification | Complete Spiritual Perfection |

## The Pillars of Wuḍūʾ and Their Sequence

Purity concerns the cleansing of seven organs, of which four are obligatory and three are sunna (customary) acts. Each ablution act signifies a range of spiritual and physical stations:

| Rank | Obligatory | Rank | Sunna | Rank | Sunna | Rank | Sunna |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Washing the face | 2 | Washing both hands | 3 | Wiping the head | 4 | Wiping both feet |
| *Speaker* | *Foundation* | *Universal Intellect* | *Universal Soul* | *Summoner* | *Divine Proof* | *before* | — |

Beyond these seven cardinal organs, water is applied to the hands, face, forearms, and feet—all of which constitute seven stations. These seven limits of purity correspond to seven ranks in the spiritual hierarchy: seven Imams and seven foundations. They also correspond to seven stations between the believer and the Imam:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Imam | Divine Proof (*ḥujja*) | Proxy (*dhū-l-miṣṣa*) | Gate (*bāb*) | Summoner (*dāʿī*) | Helper (*makāsir*) | Believer |

The three ranks below the Imam—⟪ar:حجت، ذومصۃ، باب⟫ Divine Proof, Proxy, and Gate—belong to the Imam's circle, while the two ranks above the believer—⟪ar:مُکلَّب، داعی⟫ Helper and Summoner—serve the believer. The Imam acts as God's deputy, and the Summoner acts as the Imam's deputy. Understanding these seven ranks constitutes the inner knowledge of ablution.

It is sunna to wash the three sunna organs (hands, elbows, nose) at ablution's beginning. Having honored these three limits, one then purifies the four obligatory organs. This sequence mirrors how three spiritual limits (Summoner, Divine Proof, and Imam) are established through the four foundational principles ⟪ar:عقل کل، نفس کل، ناطق، اساس⟫ of the Universal Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Kull), the Universal Soul (al-Nafs al-Kull), the Speaker (nāṭiq), and the Foundation (asās)—exemplified by the four obligatory acts of ablution.

In sequence, one performs the three sunnas and then the four duties, just as in reciting *Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm* one pronounces the three letters of *Bismillāh* first, then the four letters of *Allāh*. The believer must first acknowledge the station of the Summoner—symbolized by washing the hands—then proceed through the Divine Proof, represented by wiping the head, and finally through the Imam, exemplified by water in the nose. Only through recognition of these three limits can one follow the four principles: ⟪ar:ناطق⟫ washing the face (the Speaker), ⟪ar:اساس⟫ washing the hands to the elbows (the Foundation), ⟪ar:عقل کل⟫ wiping the head (the Universal Intellect), and ⟪ar:نفس کل⟫ wiping the feet (the Universal Soul).

By washing these seven organs and anointing them, the believer affirms that God transcends all the limits exemplified through these bodily stations. The believer recognizes his own insignificance in the knowledge of God. All limits are God's creation, established by Him so that they might convey His knowledge to the souls of believers and elevate them to His station.

The sequence of the four duties indicates that one must first come to know the face (the manifestation) and then its foundation (the base), for only through such knowledge can the spiritual stations be acknowledged.

**Sequence of Ablution:**

The first aspect ⟪ar:'تغاوط'⟫ of ablution is the removal of physical impurities from one's body. This is followed by *istinjāʾ* (cleansing with water), after which the stages of purification commence:

## Achieving Purity Through Clean Water

The existence of water has been revealed from the heavens to the earth since creation's beginning, as God declares:

> ⟪quran 23:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۢ بِقَدَرٍۢ فَأَسْكَنَّهُ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۖ وَإِنَّا عَلَىٰ ذَهَابٍۭ بِهِۦ لَقَدِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We send down from the sky water measured with proportion, and We lodge it in the earth, and verily, We have the power to take it away.*

Water flows through both sacred and corrupted places. Holy lands contain rivers, ponds, and wells; corrupted regions contain bitter, salt-laden seas. When water passes through such defiled places, its essential purity is compromised by the nature of the place itself. Though the water's original substance remains unchanged, it becomes brackish, saline, and spoiled. Such water cannot nourish grain, sustain livestock, or serve human consumption. Rather than giving life, it destroys all vegetation.

Purity forms the gateway to prayer just as faith constitutes the gateway to Islam. As all things possess both inner and outer dimensions, every outward act of purification carries hidden spiritual meaning. God declares:

> ⟪quran 25:48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَرْسَلَ ٱلرِّيَحَ بُشْرًۢا بَيْنَ يَدَىْ رَحْمَتِهِۦ ۚ وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ طَهُورًۭا ‎⟫
> *It is He Who sends the winds as heralds of glad tidings before His Mercy (rain), and We send down pure water from the sky.*

And again:

> ⟪quran 8:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِذْ يُغَشِّيكُمُ ٱلنُّعَاسَ أَمَنَةًۭ مِّنْهُ وَيُنَزِّلُ عَلَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ لِّيُطَهِّرَكُم بِهِۦ وَيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمْ رِجْزَ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ وَلِيَرْبِطَ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِكُمْ وَيُثَبِّتَ بِهِ ٱلْأَقْدَامَ ‎⟫
> *When He covered you with drowsiness as security from Him, and sent down upon you water from the sky to purify you with it, and remove from you the uncleanness of Satan, and to strengthen your hearts, and make your feet firm thereby.*

**The Inner Meaning of Purity:**

Purification requires pure water exclusively. Should any substance—whether perfume or syrup—adulterate the water, it becomes unsuitable for ablution or full washing. Clean water removes filth; filth cannot be cleansed by filth.

The inner dimension of wuḍūʾ (ablution) signifies a believer's acceptance of the Imam of the Age's covenant and invitation, his understanding of the Imam's spiritual boundaries, and his renunciation of the Imam's enemies. Prayer's esoteric reality consists of entering the Imam's summons and remaining steadfast through connection with the Imam of the Age and his spiritual stations. Prayer is impossible without ablution; ablution cannot be performed without sincere intention; intention cannot exist without God's name, the Most Merciful.

Pure water represents knowledge. As water cleanses bodily impurity, so knowledge purifies the soul's defilement. Just as outward prayer requires physical purification, so too does teaching interpretation and spiritual realities demand the initiation into a covenant. The covenant itself constitutes purity's essence; the knowledge of truth forms the call to prayer.

| Purity | Prayer |
| --- | --- |
| Knowledge of the Imam of the Age | The Science of Interpretation and Realities |

**Purification with Salt Water:**

In the absence of pure water, purification may be performed with bitter, salt-laden water (from the sea). This signifies that when a respondent abandons the summons and dwells among enemies, he may resort to protective concealment (taqiyya) and express his convictions in accord with their false doctrines.

## Water, Buckets, and Vessels (Practical Rules)

When a worshipper wishes to perform ablution for prayer, he draws water from a river or well. The river water exemplifies the manifest knowledge of the Imam of Truth—knowledge unchanged by any hand and flowing freely to all creatures, both common and distinguished. Well water represents the inner, hidden knowledge of the Imam, reserved for those alone who have access to the well's depths. The bucket from which the respondent draws water from the well symbolizes the Summoner's knowledge, who reads the knowledge of the Divine Proof and teaches it to others. The vessel into which water is poured for ablution's purpose exemplifies the All-Encompassing Summoner (al-dāʿī al-muṭlaq). This Summoner imparts knowledge to the Permitted One (al-māʾdhūn) and the Helpers (al-makāsir), whose example are the right and left hands from which water is taken from a vessel.

Each hand bears five fingers, symbolizing the five daily prayers. The five fingers of the right hand correspond to the marks of prayer times; the five fingers of the left hand correspond to the five prayer times themselves.

With these two hands the believer cleanses himself of urine and feces. Water is poured with the right hand; impurities are removed with the left. That is, the Permitted One (who is as the right hand) imparts knowledge to the Helper, by which the Helper removes from believers the impurities of polytheism, disbelief, and hypocrisy—thereby bringing them into the faith.

| Bucket | The Summoner Who Reads the Divine Proof's Knowledge |
| Vessel | The All-Encompassing Summoner |
| Both Hands | The Permitted One and the Helper |
| Five Fingers of the Right Hand | The Times of the Five Prayers |
| Five Fingers of the Left Hand | The Five Obligatory Prayers |

## Exegesis of the Verse of Purification (Q33:33) — 'God Wishes to Remove All Impurity from You, O People of the House'

Water's essential quality is that it remains pure in itself and purifies others. Pure water requires no purification of its own. God purifies bodily impurity through water and removes the impurity of the mind—ignorance—through knowledge. Purification from the impurity of ignorance through the sciences of the People of the House constitutes true purification. ⟪ar:آیت التَّطھِیر⟫ God declares:

> *(Image preserved; interpretation follows)*
> *"God intends to remove all impurity from you, O People of the House, and purify you with a perfect purification."*

One may ask: if the need for purification arises only where impurity already exists, were the People of the House impure before this verse's revelation? ⟪ar:نعوذ باللّٰہ⟫ God forbid!

When this verse was revealed, the Messenger of God gathered Mawlānā ʿAlī, Mawlānā Fāṭima, Imam al-Ḥasan, and Imam al-Ḥusayn beneath his cloak and declared: "These are my ⟪ar:اہل البیت⟫ People of the House, my ⟪ar:أصحاب تطھیر⟫ Companions of Purification." The lady ⟪ar:مولاتنا سلمیٰ⟫ Salma stood nearby and requested: "O Messenger of God, include me too beneath your cloak." The Prophet replied: *"O Salma, you are indeed upon goodness, yet I cannot extend this cloak to any save these five." ⟪ar:اہل البیت⟫* When the verse's full meaning was disclosed, all four expressed gratitude to the Messenger of God for this bounty bestowed through him. The Prophet responded: "Not so! Rather, within each of you burns the ability and majesty that God has revealed to you all."

By drawing all five beneath his cloak, the Messenger of God distinguished himself, ʿAlī, Fāṭima, al-Ḥasan, and al-Ḥusayn from all other creation. Thus the ⟪ar:اہل البیت⟫ People of the House became uniquely those five beneath his cloak. The cloak itself symbolizes the Messenger of God's protective rank, which clarifies the singular exaltation and leadership of these five within their respective stations. The Prophet thereby revealed that these five beings are pure in their essence; no impurity can ever attach to them, just as a garment's outer layer shields a person from soil. Should defilement exist, it remains external to the cloak!

In this verse, the word "impurity" (*rijs*) refers to the believers' false conceptions, doubts, and polytheistic notions concerning the knowledge of these personalities and their descendants, ⟪ar:ائمہ طاھرین⟫ the Pure Imams. Such corrupt thoughts about the majesty of the People of the House constitute spiritual filth for believers. Through this verse, God decrees that He shall correct the believers' false understanding via the science of esoteric exegesis and spiritual realities (which constitute pure water), granting them true knowledge of the Imams' genuine stations of honor.

**Esoteric Truths:**

⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)، مولانا علی⟫ None of the Imams requires physical cleansing, for they stand beyond impurity's reach. Yet they purified themselves to remove defects that arose in their veils through knowledge. Their purity consisted in cleansing their spiritual stations. The Messenger of God ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ possessed such exalted qualities that he corrected one of his veils through humble effort.

## The Heavens, the Earth, and Pure Water (Their Cosmological Relation)

The sky is the source from which water flows. Water possesses the remarkable quality of remaining pure in essence while perpetually descending from the higher realm (sky) to the lower (earth). The earth absorbs this water and produces vegetation for all creatures—the source of their life and being. Knowledge, like water, ever descends from the higher to the lower. God declares:

> *(Image preserved; interpretation follows)*

Heaven signifies the Prophets ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اﷲ علیہ و آلہ و سلم⟫ of an age; earth signifies their successors, ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ the Commander of the Faithful. "Water" refers to knowledge acquired by the recipient; "purity" denotes freedom from all forms of doubt and uncertainty. In another circle of interpretation, heaven represents the Bestower ⟪ar:مستفید⟫ (he who confers benefit), and as interpretation progresses through its circles, the Imam faces the sky while the Divine Proof becomes his earth.

Thus the Messenger of God transferred all his knowledge ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ to ʿAlī. ʿAlī received this weighty and sublime knowledge of the Prophet and rendered it accessible, making it beneficial for all human souls and ensuring their spiritual sustenance and existence.

**Two Types of Water-Absorbing Earth:**

Earth that receives water divides into two kinds: fertile or barren. When water falls upon good soil, vegetation flourishes, benefiting both animals and humans who drink and live from it. But when identical water falls upon bad land (the sea), its nature transforms, becoming useless to all.

**Pure Earth:**

The inner earth exemplifies the Imams who serve as successors of the Prophet and descendants of his house, standing firm in God's command and serving the call to truth. Thus ⟪ar:ناطق علیہ السلام⟫ the Speaker declared: *"The earth is your mother and she brings you good."* ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ By these words the Prophet pointed to his successor ʿAlī. When ʿAlī received this knowledge from the Prophet, he remained pure and unblemished, becoming a means for believers' souls to flourish and develop.

**Impure Earth:**

Similarly, corrupt land exemplifies the wicked among the Umma—the false imams and those who shall continue to arise in their place until the Day of Resurrection. Concerning this corrupted earth, ⟪ar:سیدنا مؤید اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ our Master al-Muʾayyad—may God elevate his sanctity—declared: "O people, O people dwelling in negligence's sleep, who dwell in a mother's lap that devours her own children—why do you rest in a house about to collapse?"

When ⟪ar:ناطق علیہ السلام⟫ the Speaker's pure knowledge (sweet water) reached them, they corrupted and perverted it. The Zodiac becomes an example of the sea's salt water, and their followers are animals dwelling within it, whose survival depends solely upon that salt water. Lacking it, they perish. That is, ⟪ar:کبرا أمت (ابو بکر، عمر، عثمان)⟫ they falsified the Messenger of God's knowledge, then their ⟪ar:متبعین⟫ followers acquired this false knowledge from them—for their souls possessed the capacity to comprehend only such corrupted teachings.

## Clouds and Rain (in the Ṭahāra Discussion)

**The Cloud:**

Two types of clouds exist in the sky; some produce only thunder without rain, while others fill with water and shower the earth. The cloud exemplifies the Summoner. Those that rain water resemble ⟪ar:داعات قلم⟫ the Summoners of the Pen—those who compose books and spread sciences with their Imam's support. Clouds that only thunder correspond to ⟪ar:داعات سیف⟫ the Summoners of the Sword, who do not compose written works but conduct struggle by force. Both serve the same purpose: purifying believers' souls of excess and corruption.

| Raining Clouds | Thundering Clouds |
| --- | --- |
| Summoners of the Pen | Summoners of the Sword |

**Precipitation:**

When ⟪ar:نصف النحار⟫ the sun ⟪ar:شمس⟫ reaches its zenith at midday, its heat draws water from the earth as vapor through evaporation, then transforms this vapor into pure water and returns it to earth as rain through the clouds. The sun exemplifies ⟪ar:امام وقت علیہ السلام⟫ the Imam of the Age. The earth resembles ⟪ar:أمیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ the Commander of the Faithful and his knowledge. The vapors represent the subtle, wise, and eloquent words ⟪ar:نہج البلاغۃ⟫ hidden within ⟪ar:ابو تراب⟫ Abū Turāb (the Father of Dust, another name for ʿAlī). ## The Difference Between Washing (Ghasl) and Wiping (Masḥ)

It is narrated in the ḥadīth that in the early period of Islam, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the Messenger of God used to wash his head and feet. Yet after the Night Journey (al-Miʿrāj), when this verse was revealed to him:

> *"O you who have believed, when you stand up for prayer, wash your faces and your hands up to the elbows, and wipe your heads and your feet up to the ankles."*

—he changed this practice at God's command and began instead to wipe his head and feet. His successor, perceiving this gesture, comprehended the Prophet's intent and himself wiped his head and feet. Those who opposed him, unaware of this spiritual meaning, remained in the earlier custom.

The reason lies thus: in the early stages of ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اﷲ علیہ و آلہ  و سلم،⟫ the Prophet's spiritual education, he had not yet attained true understanding of the hierarchical nature of the other spiritual realities. He regarded them as limited and tangible; thus he washed his head and feet as he would the limbs exemplifying bodily limits. When ⟪ar:ناطقیت⟫ his Speaker-status reached completion and he ascended to heaven in a spiritual ascension as ⟪ar:نفس کل⟫ the Universal Soul, the Holy Prophet recognized these spiritual limits as they truly deserve. From that point onward, throughout every ablution, he would only wipe the head and feet—thereby demonstrating to the Umma that these two limits ⟪ar:عقل کل اور نفس کل⟫ are not tangible but intelligible; their ranks cannot be known empirically, only their beings can be acknowledged through faith. This incident clarifies that the Messenger of God ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ progressed gradually through stages of spiritual learning, as God says:

> *(Image preserved; interpretation follows)*

Wiping (masḥ) signifies confession of existence; washing (ghasl) signifies knowledge of rank and obedience. The organs that believers are commanded to wash during ablution—exemplifying ⟪ar:ناطق⟫ the Speaker and ⟪ar:اساس⟫ the Foundation—correspond to ⟪ar:جسمانی حدود⟫ bodily limits. Those commanded for wiping exemplify ⟪ar:روحانی حدود⟫ spiritual limits: ⟪ar:عقل کل، نفس کل⟫ the Universal Intellect and the Universal Soul. One must thoroughly understand the bodily limits and obey them, yet only acknowledge the existence of the spiritual limits—for humans lack the power to comprehend these spiritual beings fully. Some limits demand complete understanding; others require only partial acknowledgment.

| Wiping (Masḥ) | Washing (Ghasl) |
| --- | --- |
| Spiritual Limits | Bodily Limits |
| Universal Intellect and Universal Soul; Speaker and Foundation | Confession of Being |
| Knowledge with Acknowledgment | Partial Knowledge |

## Wiping Over the Turban and Socks

The Imams are unanimous: it is not permissible to wipe over socks during ablution. God has commanded wiping of the feet themselves, not the socks. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq declared: "Protective concealment (taqiyya) is my religion and the religion of my fathers." Yet three matters cannot be concealed by taqiyya:

The Imams pronounced: "It is not permissible for one who believes wiping over socks to be lawful—for such a person prays without ablution, as though not praying at all."

At his Prophethood's outset, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the Prophet wiped his feet and head while wearing socks and turban, because the truth of ⟪ar:عقل کل اور نفس کل⟫ the Universal Intellect and Universal Soul had not yet been revealed to him. Once ⟪ar:جبرائیل امین⟫ Gabriel the Trustworthy revealed the truth of ⟪ar:عقل کل اور نفس کل⟫ the Universal Intellect and Universal Soul, and the Prophet attained knowledge of these two spiritual limits' hierarchy, he wiped his bare head and feet. His successor, comprehending this reality and accepting his directive, ceased wiping over his turban and socks.

## Tayammum — Dry Ablution with Earth

⟪ar:تیمم⟫ God declares:

> *(Image preserved; interpretation follows)*

In this verse, three classes are mentioned: the sick, the traveler, and one who has come from the toilet. Spiritually, these three represent those requiring purification. The **sick person** symbolizes a believer whose religious understanding has become diseased. The **traveler** represents one who has journeyed far from his beneficial Guide. The **one coming from the toilet** depicts a person whose thoughts have become defiled by doubt. The Quranic term ⟪ar:غائط⟫ (*ghāʾiṭ*), referring to a place on sloping ground where one may conceal oneself while defecating, signifies privacy. Its inner meaning conveys that the respondent must purify his inner self independently, without others' knowledge—that he acquires knowledge in solitude, unseen by all.

| The Sick Person | The Traveler | The Toilet-Comer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corruption of Religious Concepts | Distance from the Beneficial One | Defilement of Thought by Doubt |

⟪ar:اَولَمُستِم اَلنِّسَاءِ⟫ Contact with women refers to intimate relations. Esoterically, it signifies that knowledge has been transferred between man ⟪ar:مفید⟫ (the Beneficial One) and woman ⟪ar:مستفید⟫ (the Beneficiary), obliging both to purify themselves. Such contact may be lawful or unlawful. If ⟪ar:مستفید⟫ the beneficiary receives knowledge from one ⟪ar:مفید⟫ permitted to teach (as in a lawful covenant), such teaching constitutes lawful knowledge; otherwise it is unlawful.

Should one belong to the four classes mentioned, he is obligated to purify his knowledge. Yet if he lacks water (knowledge of truth), he may purify himself through earth (*tīn*, clay). Earth symbolizes the believer, as stated in the Qur'an:

> *(Image preserved; interpretation follows)*

That is, on Resurrection Day the disbeliever will lament: "Would that I were a believer!"

Thus, if the respondent cannot find one of the highest stations of the summons, he may acquire knowledge from a nearby believer whose faith is firm and whose belief is sound. Even one of the summons's higher stations may meet with an elder who embodies the water metaphor.

⟪ar:تیمُّم⟫ In dry ablution, only the face and both hands are wiped. Esoterically, these represent the respondent's two stations of faith. The first station consists of distancing oneself from ignorance; the second, entering faith through covenant. The Permitted One ⟪ar:ماذون⟫ and the Helper ⟪ar:مکاسر⟫ elevate him to these two levels—symbolized by wiping both palms—and both ensure his acquaintance with both stations, making him ⟪ar:مستفید⟫ the beneficiary and ⟪ar:ناطق دور⟫ the Speaker of his age. It is not permissible to acquire knowledge from an ordinary believer when a Beneficial One is present.
