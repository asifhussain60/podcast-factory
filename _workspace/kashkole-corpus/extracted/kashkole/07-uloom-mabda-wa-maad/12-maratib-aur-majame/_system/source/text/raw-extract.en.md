The word Islam is derived from 'Salaam' which means 'peace'. The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) said: ⟪ar-quote:السلام قبل الکلام⟫ "Before you speak to someone, greet him." That is, when a believer greets another believer, he acknowledges that we are both present together on the platform of Islam. You will not be hurt by me when you are in front of me, and when you are hidden from my sight, I will not backbite you.

### Rider Salute on Pedestrian:

The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) said, "It is obligatory for the rider to greet the pedestrian." The rider means the preacher and the footman means the believer. For this reason, in the da'wah, the da'i ⟪ar:حمار⟫is called a "donkey" in a sense, because the donkey bears the full burden of its owner but does not complain to its owner. Similarly, the preacher took the burden of training all the believers for the sake of the Imam (a) and did not complain. They gather everyone into their congregation, no matter what their deeds are.

### Greetings of the Prophets

The handing over of the accounts of ⟪ar:مستودعین⟫⟪ar:صورات⟫the Imams to others ⟪ar:نبوت ورسالت⟫is also called salaam. And the Prophets conferring high ranks ⟪ar:(امامت و وصایت)⟫to their masters is also a greeting.
