## Their Slip: A Momentary Lapse

Along with Asher's repentance, a group offered their repentance, which ⟪ar:تاَبُو بِتَوبَتِہِ⟫ was called. This was followed by the feast of Asher—⟪ar:تابو بتوبتہ⟫ the last person in the circle—which led to another group called ⟪ar:لب النداہ⟫ the Brotherhood. All three ⟪ar:عاشر، تابو بتوبتہ، لبو النداہ⟫ of them achieved perfection. He invited the disobedient group and called them to the truth, forbidding them from disobeying Allah. Yet all of them denied it and were arrogant, which caused their souls to become distressed.[^cite-1]

When the ten intellects had attained perfection with their circles, the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal) knew through its omniscience that these disobedient groups would no longer emerge from their stubbornness. Therefore, the First Intellect decided to remove this disobedient group from the spiritual realm and ordered their descent: ⟪ar:اھبطو⟫

> ⟪quran 7:24⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّۭ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّۭ وَمَتَعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *(Allah) said: "Get down, one of you an enemy to the other. On earth will be a dwelling-place for you and an enjoyment, for a time."*
> — **Quran 7:24**

This command proceeded from the First Intellect and passed through all ⟪ar:دوائر عقلیۃ⟫ the intellective circles to Ashir—⟪ar:لب الندا⟫ the last person in this order. It became incumbent upon him to instruct this disobedient group toward perfection once more. Although the second source had not misled anyone from the summons, if he ⟪ar:وحم مساوات⟫ had not acted thus, the whole group would have been corrupted. By the justice of Allah, Asher was commanded to release them, for he had become their chief by virtue of having caused them to stray. The position of the First Intellect in the world of *Abdāʾ* (the primordial realm) corresponds to the position that Ashir holds for those in the world of density—that group whose rectification had been ordained.[^cite-2]

In truth, Asher ⟪ar:ابلیس روحانی⟫—the spiritual Satan—was entrusted with the work of reform, which belongs to his station. ⟪ar:وحم مساوات⟫ As soon as the command ⟪ar:اھبطو⟫ was issued by the First Intellect and reached Asher through all the ⟪ar:دوائر عقلیۃ⟫ intellective circles, a sect accepted Asher's invitation with longing. That is, they hesitated for a moment and then confessed. This moment of hesitation, and their momentary denial, became their fault. That group ⟪ar:'خطائھم کلمَّم'⟫ was thus named—their mistake was but a fleeting delay. In Arabic, this is called ⟪ar:' لمم'⟫—the blink of an eyelid, a most slight transgression. And ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ means "error like the blink of an eye," or "a particle of error."[^cite-3]

## The Formation of the World of Life

The group ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ hesitated to accept Asher's invitation and refused acceptance, yet they repented before his order could be executed. Here came a delicate moment for Asher—⟪ar:لب النداہ⟫ the last person in his circle—to determine what would become of the group: whether they would be punished or pardoned. He looked upward with longing and beseeched those above him for aid and assistance, as the Quran declares:

> ⟪quran 1:5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ ‎⟫
> *You (Alone) we worship, and You (Alone) we ask for help (for each and everything).*
> — **Quran 1:5**

All the intellects above him offered their counsel and favor upon Ashir, suggesting that the group should neither remain in the spiritual world nor be cast into the physical world. Rather, a place should be created for them between the spiritual realm and the material realm, situated at an intermediate station. The Prophet's tradition (peace be upon him) points to this principle in its deepest sense: "The best of all matters is their middle course."[^cite-4]

Thus the ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ group was not overwhelmed entirely, nor were they permitted to fall into the material world. Since ⟪ar:لب انداہ⟫ they had heeded the invitation yet sinned, they could no longer dwell in the spiritual realm—that place of spiritual perfection where neither imperfection nor sin can exist. Therefore the ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ people were thrust out from there and placed between the spiritual world and the material world, which ⟪ar:عالم حیات⟫ is called the World of Life. This realm has also been designated by several other names: ⟪ar:تامور⟫, ⟪ar:کعبۃ الروحانی⟫, ⟪ar:مرکز⟫, ⟪ar:باب عالم قدس⟫, ⟪ar:قلب روحانی⟫, and ⟪ar:عالم محتجبین⟫.[^cite-5]

All members of his ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ group were genuinely repentant, and there was no fault in their repentance itself. Yet they did not attain perfection. Although it would have been possible for them to be counted among the realm of Intellect by virtue of the perfection of their remorse, what occurred was that when they denied for a moment, some among them gazed upon him, accepted his influence, and deteriorated. It was solely because of this that they did not achieve perfection. The same principle was applied to them as to the intellect of Ashir: ⟪ar:من کسر عظم فعلیہ جبر⟫ "Whoever breaks a bone must set it right." Now it became incumbent upon this ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ people to reform those who had been corrupted by their example.

### Adam of the Beginning

As soon as the command ⟪ar:اھبطو⟫ came, one from the ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ tribe, ⟪ar:انحدار⟫ descending in repentance, became monotheistic after profound shame and became what is called ⟪ar:آدم ابدائیۃ⟫—Adam of the Beginning. Beholding this, twelve persons separated themselves, they who had gazed upon him and repented together with his repentance. Then twenty-seven others split away through the invitation of the last member of the group. All ⟪ar:'مدبرین'⟫ these are called the Governors, and all attained perfection. All developed within the Asheric realms, while the remainder became the fallen realm of ⟪ar:مدبرین⟫ nature. The strategy of salvation for the whole world was entrusted to them.

Now Asher truly refers to ⟪ar:آدم بدایۃ⟫—Adam of Inception—the last person of the circle, who is the leader of the World of Life and who shall dwell in this natural realm. He holds the same station in the material world as the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal) holds in the world of origin. He became the place where the rays of the First Intellect fell, and in every age of the natural world, he became the radiant light shining upon the Prophet, the Imam, and their supporter. The entire material world is established through his command.[^cite-6]

He is called the highest point ⟪ar:'محدد'⟫ in the realm where ⟪ar:آدم ابدائیۃ⟫ was established as ⟪ar:مور اول⟫ the first matter. ⟪ar:آدم ابدائیۃ⟫ He appeared in this world as a veil through a ⟪ar:جثہ ابدائیہ⟫ primordial form. ⟪ar:جثہ أبدائیۃ⟫ He is the one who, ⟪ar:آدم أبدایۃ⟫ when beheld, was perceived as complete—and thus had to appear in the world veiled.

**Statement:** On the model of the World of Life's limitation, the physical Kaaba likewise holds the position of limitation—the highest and most noble. This position is not reflected in physicality owing to its subtlety.

### The Imams Settled and the Imams Entrusted

The rest of the ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ group divided into two sects. Those who repented and attained monotheism, reaching the highest rank in their monotheistic station, are called ⟪ar:مستقرین⟫—the Settled Ones. All of them ⟪ar:جثث، قوائم، ائمۃ، اوصیا⟫—bodies, pillars, Imams (imāma, the spiritual authority), and Successors—are included therein. Those who repented and attained monotheism while reaching the lowest level in their monotheistic station are called ⟪ar:مستودعین⟫—the Entrusted Ones—and include all the messengers, prophets, chapters (divisions of the hierarchy), and high supplicants.

### The Astronomical Congregation

⟪ar:تنجوم⟫ It was the name of Adam's father when the celestial arrangement took form. At that time, three congregations were established. The ⟪ar:امام ھنید⟫ crowd approached ⟪ar:مستقرین⟫ the Settled Ones. ⟪ar:مستودین⟫ Adam received a congregation, and Adam's father received a congregation, which is called ⟪ar:تنجومی مجمع⟫ the Astronomical Assembly. This congregation stands at a rank higher than ⟪ar:مستودعین⟫ the Entrusted Ones, yet ⟪ar:مستقرین⟫ lower than the Settled Ones. ⟪ar:تنجومی مجمع⟫ From the last circles of the Hujjat (the Proof) to the final circles of the Gate of Gates, there extends a gathering that ⟪ar:مستودع⟫ cannot be encompassed.

### The Kaaba and the Pillar of Light

⟪ar:عالم حیات⟫ The World of Life—also called ⟪ar:تامور⟫ by another name—or ⟪ar:کعبہ روحانی⟫ the Spiritual Kaaba, has another designation. ⟪ar:عمود نور⟫ The map of the physical Kaaba was kept in perfect correspondence with the Spiritual Kaaba of the same spiritual world. Every place that exists in the physical Kaaba is also present in the spiritual Kaaba. The points of noble relation were situated in the inner recesses ⟪ar:تجاویف⟫ of the Kaaba, while points bearing the lowest relation were located at the periphery ⟪ar:(بطائخ)⟫. All believers ⟪ar:خمیرۃ الندامۃ⟫ gathered in the coordinates ⟪ar:عمود نور⟫ of the Pillar of Light.

## The Summons of the *Kalmam* and Their Ranks

When ⟪ar:عالم حیات⟫ the World of Life was formed, ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ the *Kalmam* were ordered to invite all who ⟪ar:مستودع و مستقر ائمہ⟫ were settled and entrusted as Imams. There existed a crucial difference between their invitations and those of the intellects:

| 1 | Until now, only the last person of the circle had been invited (individual invitation); but all members of the *Kalmam* were now ordered to invite as a collective (collective invitation). |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Until now, there had been only a positive invitation—to believe in the Creator Most High and to attain monotheism. But the *Kalmam* people now called with both positive and negative invitation: Believe in the Creator! Yet also: Do not believe in Satan! |

⟪ar:عالم حیات⟫ Not only were all members of the ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ *Kalmam* group inviting together; they also called with both positive and negative summons—to believe in the Creator Most High and to attain monotheism, and to reject the words of ⟪ar:ابلیس روحانی⟫ the spiritual Satan. The ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ *Kalmam* began learning from one another on this foundation. On the basis of their mutual perception ⟪ar:افادۃ و استفادۃ⟫—giving and receiving—the ranks and hierarchies were established among them.[^cite-7]

Allah says (interpretation of the meaning):

Here, Allah and the Messenger are the Prophets and their Successors, or the Imam of the time and his Proof (ḥujja).

## The Classification of the *Kalmam*

### Sight and Vision

The noblest sight is that which is born through struggle and effort in Asher's summons. In the same way that the caste accepted Ashir's invitation, their eyes were fixed upon him. The people of noble sight continue to receive the light of God. Those who attained the highest rank had their sight established through the attention of ⟪ar:'حدت و قوت'⟫ strength and power in Ashraf's intention-world. ⟪ar:صاحبان اموار⟫ Those of the Waters and ⟪ar:آدم بدایۃ⟫ Adam of Inception accepted the invitation and repented with full heart, so their sight was raised to the highest level. Then came the sight of ⟪ar:صاحبان کور⟫ diminishing vision, and then again ⟪ar:صاحبان دور (عین آخرۃ)⟫—those of the circle—with renewed nobility and ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫ the Great Eye.[^cite-8]

### The Station of the Settled Ones

The gaze of all ⟪ar:مستقرین ائمۃ⟫ settled Imams was upon those who had achieved the noblest state—all ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫ concentrated upon Ali ⟪ar:محتجب⟫ the Veiled. Everyone was learning from you and feeling the warmth of your summons and benefiting from you. And it was upon the visible presence ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ of the Standing One that your station—⟪ar:صاحب دور⟫ the Master of the Circle—came to rest. After ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫ you, their rank was established. All ⟪ar:مستقر آئمہ⟫ settled Imams are those who, having proceeded forward from ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫ the Great Eye and submitted to it, became subservient to themselves and ⟪ar:توحید، تجرید اور تنزیہ⟫ to unification, abstraction, and transcendence—becoming as ⟪ar:قائم الدور (عین آخرۃ)⟫ the Standing One of the Circle (the Last Eye).

### The Ranks of the Entrusted

The gaze of all ⟪ar:مستودعین⟫ the entrusted ones, who had received the station of Adum, was directed toward their invocation. And their eyes ⟪ar:میم مریمیۃ⟫ of the Marian *mīm*—that of ⟪ar:ذات أحمدیۃ (محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫—the Ahmadian Essence (Muhammad the Chosen, peace be upon him and his family)—looked ⟪ar:محتجب)⟫ veiled. That is, they were influenced by the summons of all the Prophets and Messengers ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم⟫ such as Abraham ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم⟫ and were benefiting from the summons of themselves. ⟪ar:مولانا أحمد علیہ السلام⟫ Mawlānā Ahmad, peace be upon him, thus stood highest in the rank of ⟪ar:میم مریمیۃ⟫ the Marian *mīm*, the lowest, followed by the rank of the other Prophets and Messengers. ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم خلیل اللہ⟫

During ⟪ar:مستقرین ومستودعین⟫ these feasts of the settled and entrusted, when ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫ the Great Eye began to witness the feast of the lower rank, the gaze fell upon the assembly, and it was observed that ⟪ar:میم علیہ السلام⟫ the *mīm*, peace be upon him/her, possessed a feast of such intensity, and their method of utilization was so exalted. So far her gaze ⟪ar:مستقرین⟫ had been upon the Settled Ones, and ⟪ar:مستودعین⟫ her gaze had been upon the Entrusted Ones. ⟪ar:عین عظیمہ⟫ Since she was of superior rank and had become the leader of all ⟪ar:مستقر ائمۃ⟫ settled Imams, as soon as ⟪ar:میم⟫ the *mīm* beheld those who were the people of Adum, the eyes ⟪ar:میم⟫ of all the Imams were likewise drawn to her, and all ⟪ar:میم مریمیہ⟫ the Imams began to draw benefit from her. The Kaaba became the center of the gaze of all these Imams. That is why all these Imams are called in ⟪ar:ائمہ دور محمدی علیہم السلام⟫ this world the Imams of the Muhammadan Circle, peace be upon them all![^cite-9]

**Statement:** The perfection of ⟪ar:مستودعین⟫ the entrusted ones is to become ⟪ar:مستقرین⟫ the settled ones. That is, to ascend to the highest relation from Adum, and to enter the Spiritual Kaaba from the sides of the Kaaba.

### The *Mīm's* Ascent from the Entrusted:

When Asher witnessed this state in the World of Life—that a person of higher proportion had become the head of the lower—he elevated the throne of *Mīm*. The point of the *mīm* was removed; she obtained her sight from the eyes of Adum and ascended from the sides of the Kaaba, entering the Kaaba itself—that is, she joined the Settled Ones. Arriving there and running toward ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫ the Great Eye, in her summons she progressed with such intensity of gaze that she surpassed all the Imams and drew so close to ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫ the Great Eye that only three ranks remained between them, which were the stations of the Imams ⟪ar:جد، فتح اور خیال⟫—Grandfather, Opening, and Imagination. This is the *mi'rāj* in truth, and for this reason Allah says (interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 53:8-9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ دَنَا فَتَدَلَّىٰ ‎ ‏فَكَانَ قَابَ قَوْسَيْنِ أَوْ أَدْنَىٰ ‎⟫
> *Then he [Jibrael (Gabriel)] approached and came closer, And was at a distance of two bows' length or (even) nearer.*
> — **Quran 53:8-9**

"Two bows' length" refers to the rank of ⟪ar:مولانا عامر⟫ Mawlānā ʿĀmir and ⟪ar:مولانا عمران⟫ Mawlānā ʿImrān, while "even nearer" indicates the rank of ⟪ar:مولانا عبد اﷲ⟫ Mawlānā ʿAbd Allāh. For this reason, when ⟪ar:میم اور عین⟫ the *mīm* and the Eye came into this world—veiled as ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی⟫ Muhammad the Chosen and ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ Ali son of Abī Ṭālib—the veil was even more profound than the veil of the veil, because this form had been created in the World of Life. If ⟪ar:عین⟫ the Eye had not gone to the sight of ⟪ar:حجاب علی⟫ the Veil of Ali, and if ⟪ar:میم⟫ the *mīm* had not reached the sight of ⟪ar:حجاب محمد (ﷺ)⟫ the Veil of Muhammad (peace be upon him), I would never have read the Kalima![^cite-10]

## The Veiled and the Veil

All the Imams and the Pure Ones (al-Asfiyāʾ) are established in the stations ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ of the *Kalmam*. Since they had accepted the summons before they became material, the justice of Allah does not permit them to be sent into this world, which is the abode of Allah's wrath. Yet all of these ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ *Kalmam* make an impression in this material world. This effect is based upon the blessed *nasut* (the material form) which is prepared by ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ the believers—which is called ⟪ar:حجاب⟫ the Veil. The true beings live in the World of Life and are called ⟪ar:محتجب⟫ the Veiled—hidden behind the veil.[^cite-11]

All the Imams who have come into this world are from ⟪ar:محتجبین⟫ the veiled ones of the World of Life who have appeared in this world through *hijāb* (the veil). In other words, they are also called ⟪ar:نواسیت⟫—the Descendants—and ⟪ar:حجابات⟫ Veils (plural). Those ⟪ar:محتجب⟫ who are veiled in me ⟪ar:کلمم⟫ are named ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫—the Great Eye. He, ⟪ar:محتجب⟫ the Veiled, established many veils in this world; the lowest of these was called the ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ veil of Ali son of Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him. The veil always acts according to the will of its veiled one.

Each higher veil becomes a lower ⟪ar:محتجب⟫ veiled one, and in this arrangement ⟪ar:حجاب و محتجب⟫ both veil and veiled can exist in this world simultaneously. The veil is always a ransom for its veiled. An apt example: if we sense a stone falling upon our head, we place our hand to shield it, saving the head. The hand becomes a veil for the head, protecting it from harm. Both head and hand are present in the body at once. In the same way, those who became veils for the sake of the Prophet also existed in the time of the Prophet and became veils to save the self of the Prophet, bearing pain themselves to protect their master.[^cite-12] An Imam can establish more than a thousand ⟪ar:حجابات⟫ veils at one time, but he does not. ⟪ar:مولا تنا فاطمۃ⟫ Our Mistress Fāṭima, peace be upon her, undertook four hundred and ten veils, as is indicated in the *tasbīh* (the devotional counting) or *fāṭira* (the chapter).

The Imam has the right to appoint anyone as a veil whom he chooses. An example: if a person approaches a mango tree and sees many ripe mangoes upon it, he might pluck one. If asked why he chose this particular mango when others were equally ripe, he would answer, "It is my choice to do so." In the same manner, the Imam too has the right to choose whomever he wishes as his veil. Thus did ⟪ar:فراط ابن احنق⟫ he choose—as the tradition relates concerning ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq and his choice of ⟪ar:مولانا اسماعیل⟫ our Mawlānā Ismāʿīl.

His teeth were martyred in battle and a rib bone was broken. The tradition tells that he used to labor there—⟪ar:عبد اللہ ابن رواحا⟫ as did ʿAbd Allāh ibn Rawāḥa. That is, they were in accordance with the station of the places ⟪ar:عبد اللہ ابن رواحا⟫ of the tooth and the limb, or they came to these places in your nostrils as ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ fragrances. The Imams possess power over their bodies in the same way that we possess power over our garments.

## The Pillar of Light

⟪ar:عمود نور،⟫ In truth, the Pillar of Light ⟪ar:نور کلمہ⟫—the Light of the Word—is another name for ⟪ar:علم جاری و ساری⟫ the Flowing and Pervading Knowledge that has been passed from the Creator to the First Intellect. Whoever came into contact with this light truly succeeded. Then from the First Intellect this light connected with the Second Intellect, and then descended from the seven intellects and their circles to Asher, who has been entrusted with the command of the material world and who stands in the position of the First Intellect in the world of nature. All of them are called ⟪ar:عقول ابدائیۃ المجردۃ⟫—the Primordial Abstracted Intellects (physicality). Then it connects with the light of Ashir to every place that emanates from the world of nature, which are ⟪ar:ناطق، اساس اور امام علیہم السلام⟫—the Speaker (al-Nāṭiq), the Foundation (al-Asās), and the Imam, peace be upon them. And then it continues to his Gate (Bāb al-Imam). The Imam's Gate stands in the place of the Imam in this world because it is established there as ⟪ar:نفس کلیۃ⟫ a Universal Soul and ⟪ar:ھیکل⟫ a Temple. And then this Pillar of Light is transmitted from the Gate to the Proof (Ḥujjat), from the Proof to the Summoner (al-Daʿī), from the Summoner to the Permission-Giver (al-Mazūn), from the Permission-Giver to the Believers, and from the Believers to the Respondents while they take the Noble Covenant.[^cite-13]

It is said of the Pillar of Light that it is all-encompassing and knowledge is continuous through it; it is the soul—the Word, the Light of the Kalima *Lā ilāha illā Allāh* ("There is no god but Allah"). All spiritual and material boundaries are connected to this Pillar of Light, extending from the First Intellect to the believer, and are held by it. An example: a long wooden pole with honey upon it, and when the fly alights upon it, it never leaves. In the same way, the forms (with which souls are bound) never depart from this Pillar of Light. It is a honeycomb from which all the bees (believers) draw sustenance and adhere. Everyone comes and connects to it. This is ⟪ar:حبل اللہ المتین⟫ the Strong Rope of Allah, which can never be severed. That is why Allah says (interpretation of the meaning):

> ⟪quran 2:256⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَآ إِكْرَاهَ فِى ٱلدِّينِ ۖ قَد تَّبَيَّنَ ٱلرُّشْدُ مِنَ ٱلْغَىِّ ۚ فَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱلطَّغُوتِ وَيُؤْمِنۢ بِٱللَّهِ فَقَدِ ٱسْتَمْسَكَ بِٱلْعُرْوَةِ ٱلْوُثْقَىٰ لَا ٱنفِصَامَ لَهَا ۗ وَٱللَّهُ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ ‎⟫
> *There is no compulsion in religion. Verily, the Right Path has become distinct from the wrong path. Whoever disbelieves in Taghut and believes in Allah, then he has grasped the most trustworthy handhold that will never break. And Allah is All-Hearer, All-Knower.*
> — **Quran 2:256**

Whoever comes into contact with this Pillar of Light succeeds and becomes among the people of mercy. It is far greater and more exalted than the light of the sun. ⟪ar:شمس⟫ The light of the sun, despite its intensity and heat, is dense and enveloped in materiality. The Pillar of Light contains no density; for this reason it could enter into contact with the First Intellect. We know the Creator because of this light. This light does not fall upon our bodies, but upon our ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫—our Rational Soul. ⟪ar:عنایت الہیۃ⟫ This is also called by another name: ⟪ar:قدرت الہیۃ⟫ the Divine Power.[^cite-14]

The Intellect—the tenth of the circle—moves this Pillar of Light. This movement is connected to the Imam of that age, and from it he understands that the Imam is attempting to bring a believer to spiritual death. That is, the Imam indicates that the believer's connection has been purified and he is now absorbed into the Pillar of Light. Then the Imam moves the Pillar of Light, through which the form ⟪ar:(نفس ناطقۃ + نفس حسیۃ)⟫—the Rational Soul and the Sensible Soul—of the believer is separated from his body and absorbed into the Pillar of Light.

**Statement:** Malik al-Mut (the Angel of Death) is in truth the Pillar of Light.

### The Development of the Believers' Soul

Thus the Pillar of Light absorbs the forms and transmits them onward to their selves as ⟪ar:مستفیدین⟫ beneficiaries—those who receive. The forms themselves become ⟪ar:مفیدین⟫ those who give. It is a magnet that holds the form and the self together. That is, when the form ascends, it develops together with ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ the Sensible Soul, and it also advances to a higher rank. The Pillar of Light unites this form and soul. The part of the body that has evolved into a form can no longer be corrupted, because the Rational Soul has attached it to itself. If that form reaches an upper limit and that upper limit is destroyed, then as the form of that upper limit is purified, the Pillar of Light draws it away, and the form is separated from it. The ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ remainder of it decays.

When Dāʾūd al-Quṭb's intentions failed, ⟪ar:سیدنا ھمام⟫ our master Hammām drew his current form with the aid of the Pillar of Light and gave it to ⟪ar:سیدنا محمد بن فہد⟫ our master Muhammad ibn Fahd for refinement. After this, Dāʾūd al-Quṭb deteriorated. Similarly, when ⟪ar:ابو بکر⟫ Abū Bakr deteriorated, Mawlānā Ali drew his form with the aid of the Pillar of Light and gave it to his son ⟪ar:محمد بن ابی بکر⟫ Muhammad ibn Abī Bakr for training.

### The Deliverer of Provision to the Believers

It is also the work of the Pillar of Light to deliver physical sustenance to man. The Pillar of Light draws man's sustenance and delivers it to him, protecting every part of that sustenance (for the one who consumes it). It ensures that no one else consumes the goods of a believer—that the believer alone receives what he has been brought into this world to have. When a goat is slaughtered, it has several parts, and multiple people may eat from it together. Yet the Pillar of Light conveys every part and piece of that goat to the person whose body it enters.

### The One Who Bestows the Water of Ablution

In the same manner, the water of ablution that reaches a person (when it touches his body during ablution) is also delivered by the Pillar of Light. ⟪ar:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫ As soon as one says "In the Name of Allah, the Compassionate, the Merciful," a person's bond with the Pillar of Light is established. Otherwise, though it is our own matter, the connection takes time to form. Therefore, it is most important to pronounce the ⟪ar:بسم اللہ⟫ *Basmala* at the beginning, and without it, ablution is not valid. ⟪ar:بسم اللہ⟫ At the very moment the *Basmala* is recited, the connection with the Pillar of Light is established and its effect begins to work.

### The Guardian of the Believers' Virtues

The Pillar of Light is the protector of the virtues of the saints of Allah—those devoted to Allah's Guardianship (the leaven of *wilāya*)—and the one who conveys them to their appointed stations. In the same way, when a person is buried after death and his components scatter into fire, air, water, and earth, yet after countless years the Pillar of Light reconnects these components from the *umma* (the community). This is what is called ⟪ar:مقدرات الٰہیۃ⟫—the Divine Measures. The Pillar of Light labors on behalf of all things—⟪ar:(صورات، فضلات، ریحیات)⟫ forms, graces, and fragrances.

**Statement:** The *riwāya* (tradition) says: ⟪ar:اَلمَرْعُ یَحْشَرُ مَعَ مَن اَحَبَّہ⟫ "The soul is gathered with those whom it loves." As soon as we fall in love with someone, our bond becomes attached to theirs. Therefore, we must be most careful about whom we establish our connection with.

### The Leaven of Remorse of the Believers

All believers ⟪ar:خمیرۃ ندامت⟫ are preserved as the Leaven of Remorse in this Pillar of Light. When a believer takes the covenant with the Imam of the Age, he is stirred within ⟪ar:خمیرۃ ندامت⟫ the Leaven and comes into connection with the believer's soul. This union is called the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ Rational Soul.

### When the Bond with the Pillar of Light is Broken

When any of the boundaries or limits becomes defiled through intimacy, the Pillar of Light reverses from it and separates. It is said in the supplication that when a person engages in intercourse, the angels of Allah turn away from him until he returns to a state of purity. Intimacy breaks the connection with the sacred, which also severs the bond with the Pillar of Light. As soon as a person performs ritual washing, he is ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ reconnected to the Pillar of Light.

As soon as a woman's private parts become exposed, her connection with the Pillar of Light is broken, and the angels turn away. The hair upon a woman's head is included among her private parts, so it is permissible for her to cover her hair while praying. ⟪ar:جنگ سفین⟫ When the Battle of Siffīn proceeded and ⟪ar:علی علیہ السلام⟫ Ali, peace be upon him, moved to strike ⟪ar:عمروبن عاس⟫ ʿAmr ibn al-ʿĀṣ, the latter exposed his private parts to save his life. At that moment ⟪ar:علی علیہ السلام⟫ Ali, peace be upon him, turned his gaze away.

### The Accounting of Limits and Degrees

The Pillar of Light is fashioned like a *tasbīḥ* (a counting-cord of prayer beads) in which all the degrees are connected to one another. The Preacher is connected to the Proof, the Proof is connected to the Gate, and the Gate is connected to the Imam. When a believer dies, the Pillar of Light draws his form and places it in the proper place within the *tasbīḥ*, and this is called Paradise. The Paradise of the Preacher is distinct; the Paradise of the Proof is distinct; and the Paradise of the Gate is distinct. When the number in one Paradise becomes complete, a renaissance occurs in another. That is, when the number is fulfilled in the Paradise of the Proof—when the tenth rank is attained—⟪ar:تلک عشرۃٌ کاملۃ⟫ *"That is a complete ten"*—then one enters the Paradise of ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ the Gate of Gates. And the Pillar of Light, which keeps watch over all these degrees and developments, is the Light itself.

**Statement:** When the Prophet's revelation first descended upon him in the Cave of Ḥirā, he was in truth in contact with the Pillar of Light.
