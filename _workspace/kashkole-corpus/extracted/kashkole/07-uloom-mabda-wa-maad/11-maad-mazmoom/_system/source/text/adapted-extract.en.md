## Condemned Souls: The Reproaching and Commanding Self

Since the fallen beings are divided into categories based on their own remorse—⟪ar:نادمین⟫ the penitent, ⟪ar:شاک و متحیرین⟫ the doubters and the perplexed, and ⟪ar:مصر و مستکبرین⟫ the obstinate and arrogant—these three natures are found in every species and gender. Prayer that pleases Allah is virtuous; prayer that becomes wrapped around the worshipper's face and kills the soul is vicious. In the same way, there exist three categories of human beings, distinguished by the character of the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ sensible soul.

### The Nafs Ammarah Bi'l-Su' (The Soul Inciting to Evil)

Allah Almighty declares in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 12:53⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ وَمَآ أُبَرِّئُ نَفْسِىٓ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلنَّفْسَ لَأَمَّارَةٌۢ بِٱلسُّوٓءِ إِلَّا مَا رَحِمَ رَبِّىٓ ۚ إِنَّ رَبِّى غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> 
> *"And I free not myself (from the blame). Verily, the (human) self is inclined to evil, except when my Lord bestows His Mercy (upon whom He wills). Verily, my Lord is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful."*

The ⟪ar:نفس امارۃ بالسوء⟫ commanding soul (nafs ammarah bi'l-su') leads one toward transgression. This is the ⟪ar:نفس⟫ self that, when it unfolds its wings and refuses obedience, flees from righteousness.[^cite-1] Just as Allah sends forth one possessed of a ⟪ar:نفس خیر⟫ virtuous soul through His messenger ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫, so too does He send forth one corrupted in soul. The ⟪ar:سورۃ الشمس⟫ Chapter of the Sun bears witness:

> ⟪quran 91:12⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِذِ ٱنۢبَعَثَ أَشْقَىٰهَا ‎⟫
> 
> *"When the most wicked man among them went forth (to kill the she-camel)."*

He who departs in such wretchedness is truly ⟪ar:شقی الأولین والأخرین⟫ the most unfortunate of all former and latter generations. Allah shall punish him by casting him into the depths of Hell, for he has come forth in utter stubbornness. Such is the destiny of ⟪ar:معاویۃ⟫ those who emulate the tyrant's path, unlike ⟪ar:مولانا علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ our Master ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, the Commander of the Faithful.

### The Nafs Lawwamah (The Reproaching Soul)

By contrast, another self is the ⟪ar:'نفس لوامۃ '⟫ reproaching soul—perpetually suspended between states of doubt and disturbance by suspicion. This self does not permit either side to reach a final verdict, but remains in restless vacillation. Allah swears an oath in its regard:

> ⟪quran 75:2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَآ أُقْسِمُ بِٱلنَّفْسِ ٱللَّوَّامَةِ ‎⟫
> 
> *"And I swear by the self-reproaching person (a believer)."*

## The Slaughter and Hamstringing of the Soul

Regarding the ⟪ar:سورۃ الشمس⟫ Chapter of the Sun, Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 91:10-14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ خَابَ مَن دَسَّىٰهَا ‎ ‏كَذَّبَتْ ثَمُودُ بِطَغْوَىٰهَآ ‎ ‏إِذِ ٱنۢبَعَثَ أَشْقَىٰهَا ‎ ‏فَقَالَ لَهُمْ رَسُولُ ٱللَّهِ نَاقَةَ ٱللَّهِ وَسُقْيَهَا ‎ ‏فَكَذَّبُوهُ فَعَقَرُوهَا فَدَمْدَمَ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّهُم بِذَنۢبِهِمْ فَسَوَّىٰهَا ‎⟫
> 
> *"And indeed he fails who corrupts his ownself. Thamud (people) denied (their Prophet) through their transgression. When the most wicked man among them went forth (to kill the she-camel). But the Messenger of Allah said to them: 'Be cautious! Fear the evil end. That is the she-camel of Allah! (Do not harm it) and bar it not from having its drink!' Then they denied him and they killed it. So their Lord destroyed them because of their sin, and made them equal in destruction."*

The term ⟪ar:'عقر'⟫ to hamstring—literally means to slay. It is the appellation ⟪ar:'ذبیحت '⟫ of lawful slaughter, and also the name ⟪ar:عقر⟫ of unlawful killing. In this verse, although the apparent referent is the she-camel, in truth the conscience turns toward the souls of those who perpetrated this deed—those who committed such evil and ⟪ar:'ھا'⟫ corrupted their own ⟪ar:'ھا'⟫ souls, bestowing upon them a forbidden death.[^cite-2]

## The Death and Return of the Believing Defaulter

The transgressor is called the ⟪ar:'مؤمن مقاصر '⟫ believing defaulter—one who has committed grave offenses. There is a distinction between the death of one who has fulfilled faith and one who has fallen short. Though every human being bears guilt in some measure, the defaulting believer is one specifically who has perpetrated ⟪ar:'کبائر'⟫ major sins: shirk, falsehood, adultery, intoxication, and murder.

The first punishment of such a person differs markedly from that of the righteous believer. Unlike the latter, he must endure the torment of the grave. The ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ sensible soul does not depart wholly; instead, depending upon the magnitude of his transgressions, sensation reverberates within his body. He is entombed in a state of semi-death, enduring for three days the severe chastisement of the grave. Thereafter his body undergoes punishment in human form, and ⟪ar:حسیۃ⟫ sensation and ⟪ar:رجس⟫ corruption accompany ⟪ar:نقس⟫ the torment of wrench and degradation. Yet Divine mercy tempers this: he is not required to don seventy garments, each with seventy clasp-rings. Rather, ⟪ar:جوہر نفس⟫ nature—which is the very essence of the soul—chastises him according to justice alone, then restores him to human stature.[^cite-3]

## How Forbidden Deeds Receive Their Recompense

When a person commits that which is forbidden—transgressing what has been enjoined by no authority—his own ⟪ar:جوہر نفس⟫ essence of soul darkens swiftly. His wicked deeds obstruct him from the path of guidance; they soil his soul and lead him astray, ensnaring him in sin's snare. These misdeeds then incite enmity toward the limits (ḥudūd) of truth and ultimately propel him toward Hell.

**A Principle of Divine Justice:** Allah forgives sins committed against Himself, yet He does not forgive sins against His servants, except through a means by which Allah reconciles the accounts between them, fulfilling retribution.[^cite-4]

When obscene acts—⟪ar:'زنا '⟫ adultery or like transgressions—are committed, retribution becomes obligatory, for such deeds darken the essence of the soul with terrible swiftness. First, he is clothed in ⟪ar:'رجس'⟫ filth—a punishment in human form wherein he completes his retribution. He is then garbed in ⟪ar:'رقص'⟫ the garment of shame through an act of marriage, whereby faces turn inward to breasts. Subsequently he is clothed in ⟪ar:'عکس'⟫ the garment of reversal, fulfilling retribution through matrimonial union. In each instance, the magnitude depends upon the abundance or scarcity of his sin in the world.

It often occurs that many have committed adultery with a single woman. Her retribution is fulfilled by dressing her in differing garments, in each of which she is married anew. It has been observed that the swine itself incites its female to copulate with other beasts; and at times even such unions transpire with mutual pleasure and will, because ⟪ar:قامت الفیہ⟫ they too perform this act willingly and with consent.[^cite-5]

## The Death and Return of the Righteous

Those who, though they take not the pledge of the Imam, follow the outward commandments of Islam—who do not oppress, who remain chaste, pious, benevolent, truthful, and faithful; who do not recognize the guardianship of the Prophet and his successor, yet harbor no rancor toward the Imams—such are called the ⟪ar:'صلحاء'⟫ righteous. When they depart this life, the spirituality of the sun and Venus stirs them; their soul expands through their bodies. Their temperatures cool and their blood congeals. The imagination they have created through their outward deeds leaves their bodies.

**A Divine Truth:** Allah wastes not the good of anyone. "Evil has no root in creation" (⟪ar-quote:اَلشَّرُّ لاَ اَصلُ لَہُ فِی اِلبدَاء⟫). Though a disbeliever performs a good deed, he receives its reward. Allah recompenses the righteous deeds of such people through elevation, even though they have not achieved ⟪ar:خاتمہ بالخیر⟫ a good end.

> ⟪quran 6:160⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَن جَآءَ بِٱلْحَسَنَةِ فَلَهُۥ عَشْرُ أَمْثَالِهَا ۖ وَمَن جَآءَ بِٱلسَّيِّئَةِ فَلَا يُجْزَىٰٓ إِلَّا مِثْلَهَا وَهُمْ لَا يُظْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *"Whoever brings a good deed shall have ten times the like thereof to his credit, and whoever brings an evil deed shall have only the recompense of the like thereof, and they will not be wronged."*

This noble conception first ascends to the moon; the moon then conveys it to the sun. Once the imagination reaches the solar realm, fermentation and purification occur. The concept remains in the sun until its appointed return. Gradually the body dissolves, rising through evaporation until it reaches the moon and the middle spheres. Thence it descends in rain and is absorbed into pure, wholesome substance. This nourishment is consumed by his parents, from which sperm is produced, and he appears anew in the world. Upon birth, he receives from the ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ vegetative soul; upon emergence from the womb, his formation derives from the sun through Venus and the moon, connecting with body and soul. That ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ sensible soul, together with the ⟪ar:نامیہ⟫ vegetative principle that had dispersed through his body after death, now converges. These three—body, soul, and imagination—unite and become ⟪ar:ممازج⟫ unified. His condition is now superior to before, more exalted, grand, and honorable. This is the recompense of his good deeds, which Allah bestows upon him.[^cite-6]

The example is that of a traveler who departs his home and family, living in poverty abroad until acquiring wealth, then returning in better estate than before. By contrast, one who departs and never returns—whose imagination, soul, and body are all lost—suffers a form of punishment.

## The Death and Return of the Disbeliever

Save for ⟪ar:مقاصرین⟫ the defaulting believers and ⟪ar:صلحا⟫ the righteous, all other people merit punishment and endure grievous torments at death. After death, they undergo the most severe afflictions. A slight difference exists between the death of a Muslim and a disbeliever—provided the Muslim harbors no rancor against ʿAlī and his descendants.

**A Parable:** ⟪ar:حضرت ضیا علی صاحب⟫ A certain master, citing an example, declared: "If a ladder stands and people of every sect climb it, he who falls from the top suffers greater hurt than he who falls from below." Since the Bohra community has approached nearest, they shall suffer most intensely after death.

When disbelievers and adversaries of the Imams and saints approach their final hour, the ⟪ar:جوزہر ظلمانی (عقدتین)⟫ dark essence (the two knots) stirs within them—for both are magnets drawing every wicked form. It pulls forth the sinister configuration of stubbornness. Since his lord possesses no narrator to attract his senses, his soul becomes ⟪ar:'شائع'⟫ dispersed throughout his body like mercury from a shattered thermometer. His self transforms into body-form, the two merging into one thing. From both emerges his ⟪ar:تصور خبیث⟫ evil imagination and ⟪ar:نفس ہوائیہ⟫ airy soul—that part remaining in his body—while his body is left with that which is termed in the language of the invitation ⟪ar:'حُصالات'⟫ (the vessels).

**An Origin and Duration:** The self confined within the body represents progress; the self dispersed into the body signifies degradation. Even if the body burns, its components remain, for what Allah has created cannot be annihilated—only its form changes; existence cannot become non-existent.

### How the Concept of Evil Arises

His evil imagination constitutes in fact his beliefs and misdeeds, created through obstinacy toward the Imams and saints or through separation from them. This concept is called ⟪ar:'نفس ظلمانیۃ ابلیسیۃ شیطانیۃ '⟫ a dark, Iblis-like, demonic soul. Sometimes it assumes ⟪ar:صورت شیطانیہ⟫ a fiery form—the fire that consumes the burned or their possessions. At times this concept lodges in the body and does not depart; that body is entombed with ⟪ar:نفس شیطانیہ⟫ the demonic soul, which then burns the body for a period, inflicting punishment. It has been witnessed that at night the grave of such a person blazes with pride; sometimes the grave itself appears afire, yet upon approach no flame is found. The man within, burned and tormented by his own imagination, is all the more wicked in deed and fierce in enmity.[^cite-7]

### Grave Punishment

He is buried in his grave alongside a non-believer ⟪ar:حسیہ⟫ sensible presence, his state resembling one buried alive. When he perceives his wicked form, terror grips him; he prays that a vast distance separate him from this evil figure—as Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 3:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَوْمَ تَجِدُ كُلُّ نَفْسٍۢ مَّا عَمِلَتْ مِنْ خَيْرٍۢ مُّحْضَرًۭا وَمَا عَمِلَتْ مِن سُوٓءٍۢ تَوَدُّ لَوْ أَنَّ بَيْنَهَا وَبَيْنَهُۥٓ أَمَدًۢا بَعِيدًۭا ۗ وَيُحَذِّرُكُمُ ٱللَّهُ نَفْسَهُۥ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ رَءُوفٌۢ بِٱلْعِبَادِ ‎⟫
> 
> *"On the Day when every person will be confronted with all the good he has done, and all the evil he has done, he will wish that there were a great distance between him and his evil. And Allah warns you against Himself (His Punishment) and Allah is full of Kindness to the (His) slaves."*

Thereafter he beholds the Divine Universe, which ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ the Imam grants him—a taste of Hell itself.

For forty days after burial, this figure circumambulates the sides of her grave, striving to escape the physical world and reach the realm of light, only to be drawn back by celestial rays. She attempts to reconnect with her body but cannot; she merely circles the corpse in anguished longing, a state of exquisite pain. As the grave-torment approaches its end, she grows maddened and shrieks and wails. This is why the sounds of lamentation are heard at night in cemeteries, and sometimes the words ⟪ar:'عنیم'⟫ of the anguished are audible. In her desperate attempt to enter the body, she strikes it with such force that severe pain radiates through it, for the entire sensation inheres in it.[^cite-8]

## The Wandering of Evil Forms

After forty days, when this evil form separates from the body, it shapes itself as it wishes and acts as it desires. Having departed the body's confinement, it has reached the extremity of its wickedness. While embodied, its powers were limited—it was ⟪ar:شیطان بالقوۃ⟫ a satan in potentiality; now separated, restraint vanishes and it becomes ⟪ar:شیطان بالفعل⟫ a satan in actuality. The body was its prison, from which it has now been freed. It is the justice of Allah that He does not prevent the wicked from fulfilling their retribution. Simultaneously, punishment accrues whether the wicked person acts willingly or unwillingly. Upon emerging from the grave, it moves between heaven and earth. Thus the Messenger of Allah ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلعم⟫ declared: "The air is neither empty nor full" (neither devoid of such forms nor crowded with them).

This evil form, having left its body, works against the righteous, though only against those with whom accounts remain unsettled. It cannot trouble everyone. Some of these wicked forms sometimes speak truth, for though they were wicked in the flesh, they spoke truthfully alongside their wickedness. Nostradamus's predictions, for instance, held some accuracy because the spirits informing him conveyed truth; this reflects Allah's justice, for He does not destroy the good and evil of anyone.

According to ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلعم⟫ the Messenger of Allah, the ⟪ar:صورات خبیثہ⟫ evil forms affect only the ignorant—men, women, and children. All actions spring from fear of their ⟪ar:صورات خبیثہ⟫ evil manifestations. Even in sleep, those who pray ⟪ar:صورات خبیثہ⟫ for protection from their influence do so because their ⟪ar:صورات خبیثہ⟫ machinations are released at night. These evil spirits enter the dreams of sleepers, relating events both true and false. When any come to pass, people deem them truthful and are thereby corrupted, their hatred intensified. Whoever opposes the Imam becomes a ⟪ar:جن مزموم⟫ condemned jinn, sustained in enmity and malice. For this reason, when passing through filthy places like desolate wastelands, we seek protection, for these are the abodes of such evil ones.

So long as the wise one does not will it, the evil state remains unchanged. It shelters only in desolate, dark dwellings; in foul-smelling locales; in deserts and wastes; along paths and crossroads; in horrific places; in slaughterhouses and toilets. Sometimes in the sleep of wicked people akin to it, it appears imaginatively and speaks according to their evil beliefs, further corrupting them and increasing enmity toward the righteous. Thus Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 6:112⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
> 
> *"And so We have appointed for every Prophet enemies—Shayatin (devils) among mankind and jinns, inspiring one another with adorned speech as a delusion. If your Lord had so willed, they would not have done it, so leave them alone with their fabrications."*

The ignorant—be they man or woman or child—to whom this form whispers are visited by this evil state, which common folk call "possession" (or the jinn). The evil form then speaks in the tongue of the man or woman, narrating coming events in their language. Yet even then, it tells both truth and falsehood, and commands the ignorant to sin. Sometimes it departs and seeks another vessel; sometimes it cleaves to him, refusing to leave until death. These are called ⟪ar:'جنات مزموم'⟫ condemned jinn—⟪ar:شیاطین⟫ demons and ⟪ar:أفاریط⟫ the most wicked—who flee in amazement when the Qur'an is recited.

Elsewhere, such forms are deployed to emphasize the evils of the wicked against the righteous, intensifying enmity and inclining hearts toward the adversary's words. They affirm and obey this form, committing what it commands. All occurs because of similarity between these people and the wicked. In the same manner, it whispers into the hearts of the righteous, leading them astray and drawing them toward ⟪ar:ابلیسیت⟫ Iblis-like states, plunging them into sin.

When such a person dies, they are left behind and ⟪ar:عقدتین⟫ absorbed into the two knots—their barzakh (intermediate realm)—joining the congregation of former wicked forms. In this state they are ⟪ar:'شیاطین بالقوۃ '⟫ satans in potentiality. Then they descend to earth, and ⟪ar:قامت الفیہ⟫ they reach ⟪ar:شیاطین بالفعل⟫ satans in actuality. Previously they accomplished wickedness through intermediaries; now they possess the power to act wickedly themselves. When the ⟪ar:حجاب امام⟫ Veil of the Imam—through whom retributions are fulfilled—appears, they too manifest in the world to accomplish their revenge. Of these, those whose ⟪ar:صورات خبیثہ⟫ evil forms become Muslim are termed ⟪ar:'جن محمود '⟫ commendable jinn, while those persisting in disobedience remain ⟪ar:'جن مزموم '⟫ condemned jinn. Many deny the existence of jinn through ignorance.

Beneath the moon are two stars shaped as a dragon. These two heavens fall not within circumferential circles—they are frozen in place, outside the celestial spheres. ⟪ar:اھبطو⟫ Those who descend continue sinning in their state and remain steadfast in stubbornness. Within the Saqqara that lies in the earth, the multitude are punished; ⟪ar:راس و زنب⟫ the head and the tail—along with ⟪ar:صورات خبیثہ⟫ their evil forms—are punished. These two stars have become magnets for wicked images. From there, these evils cast their maleficent effects upon this world, causing all misfortunes and sinful deeds. Their tactics oppose those of ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫ the Imam. Before being cast into Saqqara, these wicked figures are clothed. At solar and lunar eclipses, the machinations of these three knots commence. These evil conceptions remain in this world as long as the wise man permits, continuing to influence his likes and dislikes.

**A Fact:** In Ras reside the enemies of Noah; in Zanab, the enemies of the Awsiyyas and Imams. Therefore, Zanab is more evil than Ras. Ras is the principal and Zanab his minister, or Ras is the devil and Zanab the assistant-devil. On the Day of ʿĀshūrā, these wicked ones attached themselves to the hearts of ⟪ar:شمر زلجوشن⟫ Shimr (the cursed), causing them to perpetrate the evil of the martyrdom of ⟪ar:امام حسین⟫ Imam al-Ḥusayn. Likewise, this wicked form ⟪ar:ملعون ابن ملجم⟫ struck the accursed Ibn Muljam's blow against ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ the Commander of the Faithful.

After traversing the seven realizations, when they reach the final stage of punishment, angels lead them to severe torment. The waves of Hell lift them to ⟪ar:اسیر⟫ the fire-sphere's heat, then cast them to ⟪ar:زمحریر⟫ the cold-sphere's depths. The angels strike them with ⟪ar:شہاب ساقب⟫ shooting stars (⟪ar:رجم⟫ lapidation). They remain thus as long as earth and sky rotate. When the appearance of ⟪ar:قائم علیہ السلام⟫ the Qāʾim draws near, those ⟪ar:خبیث نفوس⟫ evil souls appear in the world. Their trumpets, ⟪ar:جسم⟫ embodied in filthy form as fire, consume them to ash. This ash then enters ⟪ar:وسط سقرا⟫ the middle of Saqqara through the rays of Saturn and Mars, where they endure torment for ⟪ar:کور اعظم⟫ a very long time.[^cite-9]

## Evil Forms as the Opposites of the Sacred Stations

There is a gathering of wicked concepts—the ⟪ar:عقدتین⟫ two knots. They are granted respite so that the person with whom they are to be reckoned may appear in this world. When both are present and the hour of retribution comes, then the ⟪ar:صورات خبیثہ⟫ evil forms complete their ⟪ar:قصاصات⟫ retribution. By the will of the wise one, these verses fall from the Aqṭaʿīn to the earth and are absorbed into food and drink. Those foods reach the worthy—whether from among the saints or the elders. This is why ⟪ar:جناب آدم⟫ the lineage of Adam produces ⟪ar:قابیل⟫ Cain-like offspring and why the children of the Imams multiply; these wicked ingredients do not affect the righteous who consume them, but are rendered apparent through them. Similarly, the idols fashioned are composed of these same wicked ingredients.

When they are buried, their bodies rot and decompose in the grave; their components scatter into the four elements and the body dissolves into the Ummah. Vapors ascend to fire, breath to air, mucus to water, and sodium to the ground. These four vapors are then collected from the universe, forming clouds that rain upon the earth. They penetrate water, soil, and humans to retrieve their residual wicked components, becoming incorporated into the bodies of corrupt plants and animals—which are then consumed by the wicked similar to them. Thus do these forbidden ones gather their likes, either to lead them to ⟪ar:برازق جہنم⟫ the provisions of Hell or to bring forth their offspring. They remain as a ⟪ar:سلالہ⟫ heritage until the wise one wills their reappearance.

When his time comes, he appears as a child through his parents, ⟪ar:شیطان بالقوۃ⟫ a satan in potentiality, trained until he becomes ⟪ar:شیطان بالفعل⟫ a satan in actuality. He then claims the rank of truth whilst concealing its real status. ## The Lesser Torments

After death, the believer's body becomes the self—his development—while the disbeliever's soul disperses into the body so that torment upon the body reaches his feeling. Soul and body unite and merge with the soil, then rise like vapor and fall like rain. This rain produces lightning and thunder—deadly strikes—and floods that drown nations and destroy fields, producing a cold most painful. The ⟪ar:حصالات⟫ effects of these evil forms, when they trouble this world, are also included in this action. They are then escorted to the courtyard and led to the Bridges of Punishment, which possess seventy garments in each category and seventy rings in each garment. The person passes through all these garments in torment and testifies that he entered this realm through his denial of truth. At each turn, he is told what crime brings punishment. Without this knowledge, there is no punishment; but as he suffers, awareness may be withheld.

### Abomination

When their ⟪ar:حصالات⟫ rain falls to earth, plants grow from which animals feed. The beast then enters the food of one destined to manifest this wicked form as sperm—for the first is the ⟪ar:'بشری'⟫ human garment. These are nations with whom the righteous cannot speak. He passes through seventy species of this realm and seventy rings of each species.

### Simian Stage

When he leaves the first door, he enters the second—a form between human and monkey. He is then clothed with seventy garments of this type, each with seventy rings—a time of bestiality and monkey-nature. Those who committed adultery and drank wine are brought into this category. As the monkey is naturally inclined to adultery, so the wrench naturally inclines to eating and drinking intoxicants. The actions they performed happily in the world, nature now imposes as punishment. They commit adultery repeatedly, an itch never satisfied no matter how often sated.

**A Fact:** If believers commit similar sins, they too are brought to this torment and punished. Yet they need not pass through seventy garments and seventy rings in each. Nature punishes them according to justice, then restores them to human stature.

### Wild Beasts

The third category is that of wild ⟪ar:بری و بحری⟫ land and sea beasts: lions, leopards, foxes, and creatures with claws and fangs. They feel these torments fully, tearing and devouring one another, fulfilling their stories. After donning the seventy garments, they progress to the next door.

### Birds

The bird realm. Predatory birds such as eagles, crows, and hawks.

### Vegetation

It ends in this realm—the stage of the vegetative. Thus Allah says: "You shall be returned to old age." It is ignorance after knowledge, where only ⟪ar:'نمو'⟫ growth remains. These are corrupt plants that destroy living beings.

### Minerals

The final strain—vile minerals: hard, filthy stones used in base construction.

## The Description of Saqqara

Our earth is ⟪ar:بیضوی⟫ oval and ⟪ar:مسطح⟫ flat, and on its far side lies ⟪ar:سقرا⟫, which is ⟪ar:سجین⟫ and ⟪ar:جہنم کبریٰ⟫—the Great Hell—⟪ar:مستوی⟫ on its level. The center of the earth's surface aligns with the ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫; on the earth's opposite pole stands Saqqara in the form of a hollow, its lowest region ⟪ar:'اہل طاغوت '⟫ the abode of the rebellious. The earth has four corners, called its extremities, where extremity-punishment is inflicted upon the opponents and oppressors. These extremities border Saqqara, lying so cold that passage is impossible. Beyond these borders lie multiple apertures in the encircling land; in the Ruba Muskun (Uninhabited Region) stands the greatest ⟪ar:'دُردُور'⟫, located in the river between China and India, toward which all rivers flow—a passage from which manifold sounds issue. ⟪ar:نعوذ باللہ منھا⟫

When the sun revolves before the earth, it is day for earthly dwellers and night for ⟪ar:اہل سقرا⟫—the inhabitants of Saqqara. When the sun rises for them, there is day, and night for the earth's inhabitants. In such a day and night, the Day of Judgment passes. ⟪ar:اہل سقرا⟫ The people of Saqqara endure extreme day-heat and unbearable night-cold beyond reckoning. As these afflictions strike them continuously, punishment multiplies a hundredfold.

It is essential to recall that the Almighty created Saqqara before the earth. In His Word, He granted Saqqara a distinct identity, mentioning the earth, Saqqara, and the heavens separately in one verse. Nine planets and seven constellations orbit both earth and Saqqara.[^cite-16] The heavens, though superficial in aspect, rotate both planarly and circularly—their motion both orbital and axial.

A crucial observation made here is that the earth spreads like an inverted vessel beneath the surface. Yet where ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫ exists, ⟪ar:مقام ابراھیم⟫ the Station of Abraham, and ⟪ar:روضہ اقدس⟫ the Sacred Garden, and ⟪ar:غدیر خم⟫ Ghadir Khum are situated, there is no Saqqara, but rather a spacious realm wherein dwell all ⟪ar:ارواح نورانیہ⟫ luminous spirits and all ⟪ar:ارواح حسنۃ⟫ virtuous souls. Conversely, where ⟪ar:امیال⟫, ⟪ar:وادی شیطان⟫ the Valley of Satan, and ⟪ar:جمرات ثلاثہ⟫ the Three Heaps are found, ⟪ar:ارواح خبیثہ⟫ foul spirits exercise dominion. The upper portion of these sacred sites, opposite to the heavens, resides in the heavens themselves. Pores exist in these places, yet their locus should not be understood in spatial terms; rather, they transcend all limits and are wholly spiritual and nonmaterial.

The center of the entire cosmos is the Sun, around which the Earth orbits. Only human development can manifest upon Earth. The earth derives its substance from the sun; all influences orient toward earth to facilitate ⟪ar:استخراج انسانیت⟫ the extraction of humanity. Thus it is said that human perfection emerges from the earth—humanity was created nowhere else.

The true land (the land of feast) holds only ⟪ar:سقرا⟫, ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫, and ⟪ar:مقدس مقامات⟫ sacred precincts upon a platform. The lands scattered in the remainder are not festive. The true land is sustained by ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫; it is enveloped in dust—a clay sphere—with Saqqara beneath. Saqqara was created before the earth; these ⟪ar:مصر ومستکبرین⟫ stubborn oppressors are its components—the most ⟪ar:مصر ومستکبر⟫ obstinate among them, the most vile occupants of ⟪ar:'مقام سجین '⟫ the Station of the Record, the vilest ⟪ar:'طاغوت'⟫ of the tyrants. ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ The Messenger of Allah declared: ⟪ar-quote:مُعاَوِیَۃ مِنَ الطَّاغُوت⟫ "Mu'awiyah is of the tyrants."[^cite-17]

**Concerning Saturn and Mars:**

⟪ar:زحل و مریخ⟫ It was revealed that these are components of the original ⟪ar:'نادمین'⟫—the remorseful. Yet among those accompanying them lay elements of obstinacy; ⟪ar:نادمین⟫ some were remorseful, ⟪ar:تائبین⟫ others repentant. The slain dissolve and are immediately reborn in a yet more ⟪ar:قبیح⟫ hideous and debased state. Some possess two heads, some three; some have faces reversed, others with visages rightward or leftward. In such abject condition, they suffer and burn eternally in hellfire. ⟪ar:کور اعظم⟫ Thus shall it remain for them.

## Do the Punished Know Their Past Human State?

They are aware. When passing through animal forms and changing their conditions, they are made conscious of the sin for which they suffer. Upon reaching the plant and mineral realms, this knowledge is withdrawn; when cast into Saqqara, they are informed of their offense and those who led them astray. They perpetually strike and slay one another; the moment they perish, they revive and resume their combat. This punishment endures ⟪ar:کور اعظم⟫ through long epochs. Remorse becomes useless. When their sentence expires, they ⟪ar:انسانی قامت⟫ are resurrected in human stature, the punishment they endured erased from memory. ⟪ar:انسانی قامت⟫ They are then invited anew: if they accept, ⟪ar:فبھا⟫ salvation is theirs; if not, they descend again to the extremities. Thus does the divine mill turn eternally.
