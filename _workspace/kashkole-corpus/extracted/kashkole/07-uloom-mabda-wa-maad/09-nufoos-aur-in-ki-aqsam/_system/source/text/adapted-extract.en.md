## What Are Souls and How Do They Emerge?

There is a life force that exists in a hidden state throughout the world. When the darkness of the spiritual realm descended, it fell into the form of substance and shape. Therefore, whatever exists in this universe today manifests in the form of *hyle* (ḥūlī, matter) and form; whatever bears the character of *namiyyah* (نامیۃ, the vegetative principle) remains in a hidden state.[^cite-1]

⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫

| --- | --- |
| Nafs al-Namiyyah | Sense of Self |

It exists in concealment within minerals, accompanied by other forces. ⟪ar:قوت نامیۃ،⟫ When droplets of seawater rise from the earth as vapor and encounter the spheres of air and fire, they receive their portion from those spheres, then descend as clouds. When this water falls repeatedly upon minerals, it appears in the form of vegetation—the hidden ⟪ar:قوت نامیۃ⟫ within them—and is called ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ by scholars. Plants possess the power to rise and increase in mass, a capacity absent in minerals. In minerals, this force remains potential; in plants, it has become actual. On account of ⟪ar:قوت نامیۃ⟫, plants now absorb nourishment from the earth and grow in length, width, and depth.

When an animal or human being—which is a branch of the animal kingdom—consumes this plant, it ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ becomes ⟪ar:نطفہ⟫ within the body. When sperm passes from the male to the female and their fluids unite, pregnancy is established. So long as the child remains in the mother's womb, ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ functions in a potential state, while the vegetative principle ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ remains active.

When the newborn emerges from the mother's womb, the sun's rays awaken the inner ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫. The child gradually advances, and the inner ⟪ar:ناطقۃ مجازی⟫ self—the faculty of metaphorical speech—begins to actualize, until, through learning, he reaches the station of a beneficiary (*mustafīd*, the receiver of knowledge). Then, when he places his hand in the hand of his Ruler and takes the covenant of Imam al-Zaman with sincerity, he receives what is called the ⟪ar:'نفس ناطقۃ الحقیقی'⟫ true rational soul, and ⟪ar:تامور⟫ the divine spark attaches to what lies within him—⟪ar:خمیرۃ الندامت⟫ the essence of contrition. ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫ This is its designation. Throughout his life, he strives to make ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ the sensible soul luminous, for it stands ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫ in shadow to the rational soul.

When the beneficiary progresses to mature understanding in the summons (*daʿwa*, the spiritual invitation) and attains the rank of benefactor (*munʿim*, the giver of benefit), he acquires ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫⟪ar:نفس کلیۃ⟫ the universal soul, by means of which he becomes capable of instructing others. The individual soul (*nafs juzʾīyya*) forms its component; in contrast, the universal (*nafs kullīyya*) constitutes its perfection. When ⟪ar:نفس کلیۃ⟫ is attained, he enters the category of ⟪ar:'مطلق'⟫ the absolute, able to teach others; the confirmations of the upper limit then begin to manifest upon him. He himself becomes ⟪ar:'نفس'⟫ soul; the one above him becomes ⟪ar:'عقل'⟫ intellect; and the one below becomes ⟪ar:'جسم'⟫ body. If there is an Imam, then ⟪ar:نفس⟫ he possesses intellect ⟪ar:قائم⟫ standing firm. If the soul ⟪ar:نفس⟫ stands, then the intellect ⟪ar:عقل⟫ is a luxury—a superfluous gift.

The beneficiary's progression begins at the stage called ⟪ar:'سماع '⟫ hearing, in which a person begins to listen to the *majālis* (the spiritual gatherings). From there he rises to ⟪ar:'استماع '⟫ careful listening, where he now attends closely and contemplation awakens. Then he practices these sciences ⟪ar:'حفظ '⟫ for a fixed period—memorization and internalization. In the end, he becomes capable of teaching. At that point, he receives permission from his superior to ⟪ar:'نشر '⟫ disseminate and becomes instrumental in spreading the faith.

## Types of Souls

The self is singular in essence, yet its forces emerge at different times and stations. The rise of these forces has received distinct names. When the fetus in the mother's womb possesses only the power to grow, it is named ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ the vegetative soul. When the five senses activate outside the womb, it receives the name ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ the sensible soul. When the powers of thought, remembrance, debate, and comprehension of the reality of objects become operational, it is named ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫ the rational soul. And when it attains the capacity to harness the power of knowledge fully, it is named ⟪ar:نفس کلیۃ⟫ the universal soul.[^cite-2]

| Vegetative Soul | Sensible Soul | Rational Soul | Universal Soul |

When the rebellious group was cast down from the spiritual world, it became divided into two forms. Now we must develop and transform ourselves from these lower forms. Ascending from the mineral, vegetative, and animal forms to the human—this constitutes the *kamāl awwal* (primary perfection), for the mineral is the root ⟪ar:'صورت'⟫ of vegetation and animality. The *kamāl thānī* (secondary perfection), which ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫ must be attained through the established leaders ⟪ar:امام یا قوائم⟫, comes through entrance into the realm of the Imam. Perfection cannot be achieved without inner knowledge (*taʾwīl*), and all ⟪ar:اعمال شریعۃ⟫ acts of the Shariah are rooted in ⟪ar:ولایت امیر المونین⟫ the guardianship of the Commander of the Faithful. Wilayah (loyalty to the Imam) is the soul of all action and knowledge.[^cite-3]

Each force manifests in a person distinctly:

| Vegetative | Attraction, retention, digestion, expulsion, formation |
| --- | --- |
| Sensible | The five senses (*Ḥawāss al-Khams*) |
| Rational | Thought, perception, remembrance, and the search for the realities of things |
| Universal | The capacity to confer benefit and the power to create one's spiritual likeness in religion |

The example is simple: there is one person, but when he reads, he is called a "student" (⟪ar:'شاگرد '⟫); when he plays, a "player" (⟪ar:'کھلاڑی'⟫); when he cooks, a "cook" (⟪ar:'باورچی'⟫); when he paints, a "painter" (⟪ar:'مصور'⟫). He is the same person, yet receives different names according to his deeds. Similarly, vapor, water, and ice share the same essence but bear different designations in their states. Our faith is completed by three testimonies—just as our ⟪ar:صورت شریف⟫ noble form is perfected through three souls. To believe in ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ محمد الرسول اللہ علی ولی اللہ⟫ "There is no god but Allāh, Muḥammad is the messenger of Allāh, ʿAlī is the friend of Allāh" is to believe in all the dignities of his descendants, from the Imams to their highest degrees. And to obey ʿAlī is to obey the spiritual order he established and his injunctions.

## The Vegetative Soul (*Nafs al-Namiyyah*)

The Commander of the Faithful states that ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ it is the designation of a physical force whose origin lies in four temperaments (*ṭabʿīyyāt*): heat, constipation, cold, and moisture—which unite when sperm forms in the womb. Within the human body, the ⟪ar:نفس نامیہ⟫ vegetative soul has its seat in the liver. ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ Its sustenance comes from subtle foods. The mother consumes coarse food; the delicate part is extracted and conveyed to the child. Its function is to nourish and increase; the cause of its dissolution is the disruption of balance among the four humors (*akhlāṭ*). When ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ departs the body, it returns to its origin. ⟪ar:'نامیۃ کبرا'⟫ The greater vegetative soul ⟪ar:نامیۃ الکبرا⟫ extends from the lower sphere of the moon to Saqrā (the ninth sphere). It is ⟪ar:اسی نامیۃ الکبرا⟫ what is ⟪ar:نامیۃ الصغرا⟫ distributed among all animals and living beings. In contrast, the individual vegetative soul (*nafs ḥassīyya ṣughrā*) belongs to the animal.

After ⟪ar:نامیہ⟫ death, vapor rises from the grave in the form of subtle exhalation, and each physicality returns to its center.[^cite-4]

Today's times are so foul that we pray to remain far from ⟪ar:نامیہ⟫ the vegetative soul's corruption, lest we not even complete the ⟪ar:حسیہ⟫ sensible state.

In human procreation, the union (*silāla*) of mother and father is transmitted from father to mother as ⟪ar:'نطفہ'⟫ sperm. What was hidden in the father becomes revealed in the mother. These constitute the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ sum of all vegetative powers. In other words, the ⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫ vegetative soul is their aggregate—the dimensions of ⟪ar:طول، عرض، عمق⟫ length, width, and depth.

## The Sensible Soul (*Nafs al-Ḥassiyya*)

The Commander of the Faithful states that ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ the sensible soul is so called because its origin lies in ⟪ar:'فلکی قوت'⟫ celestial force, and its center is the ⟪ar:'افلاک'⟫ spheres, with the ⟪ar:'شمس'⟫ sun as its governing seat. It is grounded in ⟪ar:ولادت جسمانیۃ⟫ corporeal generation and has its roots ⟪ar:'قلب'⟫ in the human heart. Its functions are sensation, movement, locomotion, and dominion. It diverges from the body when imbalance in the humors arises. When ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ departs the body, it returns to its origin. ⟪ar:'حسیۃ کبرا'⟫

The manager of the universe operates ceaselessly through the power ⟪ar:عاشر مدبر عالم⟫. He shapes sperm in the mother's womb just as a craftsman molds silver into a vessel of his choosing. He forms the human being ⟪ar:قوت فلکیۃ⟫ from celestial force and four ⟪ar:امہات طبعیۃ⟫ natural mothers. At the end of gestation, the fetus is complete in the mother's womb—with eyes, nose, and ears—yet they serve no purpose, for ⟪ar:'دیکھنا'⟫ the power to see has not yet been actualized. ⟪ar:'آنکھ'⟫ The eye is an instrument, and sight is its power. When the newborn emerges, it receives ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ the sensible soul through breath from the sphere. ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ، نفس نامیۃ⟫ It links together the sensible and vegetative souls, ⟪ar:حسیۃ⟫ with the power of their instruments—the eye begins to see, the nose to smell, the ears to hear, and so forth.

⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ The food of the sensible soul is knowledge. The Prophet declared, "If a believer is deprived of the company of scholars for forty days, his soul begins to grow toward corruption, for the company of scholars is a light through which piety is kindled in the hearts."[^cite-5]

Allāh is first ⟪ar:رازق⟫ the sustainer, then ⟪ar:خالق⟫ the creator—He does not create without decreeing sustenance. The spiritual impurities of the child are purged by the parents until he reaches the age of seven.

## The Rational Soul (*Nafs al-Nāṭiqa*)

The newborn learns gradually from ⟪ar:نطق مجازی⟫ his parents through metaphorical speech until he attains adulthood. This ⟪ar:نطق مجازی⟫ capacity for speech distinguishes him from animals, for although animals possess ⟪ar:'بولی'⟫ sound, they lack ⟪ar:نطق⟫ rational utterance. Because of this faculty, humans are able to utter meaningful words. When he learns the Book of Allāh, his intellect reaches its perfection. When he enters the presence of a righteous teacher, that master ⟪ar:مستفید⟫ directs him with utmost care, inspires him spiritually, and receives his pledge. When he comes before his Imam of the Age (*Imam al-Zamān*), a point of light attaches to his soul, and through the Imam's elevation of him, it remains ⟪ar:خاتمۃ بالخیر⟫ sealed with goodness. He establishes his connection to this ⟪ar:عمود نور⟫ pillar of light, which emanates from the Imam al-Zamān. ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ The rational soul cannot be realized without His consent. It is the Almighty who grants substance and support to the believer whose chain of transmission proceeds from the *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* (the First Intellect).[^cite-6]

⟪ar:نطق مجازی⟫ Metaphorical speech exists, and so too ⟪ar:نطق حقیقی رسول ثانی⟫ does true speech from the second messenger. Unless the ⟪ar:رسول اول⟫ first messenger makes contact with the second messenger, form cannot arise. ⟪ar:نفس ناطقۃ⟫⟪ar:نفس نامیۃ⟫⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ The rational, vegetative, and sensible souls begin training and elevation until both become illuminated by ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ following his words.

**The Respondent (*al-Mustajīb*) advances thus:** The more his righteous deeds increase, the more his observance of the Shariah intensifies, and the purer his intentions become in love and guardianship (*wilāyat*) of his Lord, the more subtle the essence of monotheism becomes across all limits, and the greater the expansion and luminosity of his point of light. Just as a mother's nourishment grows the sperm in her womb, so does the brightness at this point of light increase beyond its elevation, affecting and purifying the ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ sensible soul. At his death, this point of light transforms into a noble form, just as sperm becomes a child at the term's end.

Now this has become ⟪ar:نفس کلیۃ⟫ the universal soul's essence, for all humanity will advance ⟪ar:نفس کلیۃ⟫ toward it. From this, the respondent's form begins to acquire knowledge, upon which the soul rests. Since the soul itself is infinitely subtle, it also requires a most subtle center, which is the form of the believer.

When the *ḥadd ʿālī* (the upper limit) is established, ⟪ar:نفس حسیہ⟫ the sensible soul becomes form and ascends to the Upper Limit. This process then reaches the ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ Gate of Gates—the Imam—and through the ⟪ar:قوائم⟫ established leaders descends finally to the whole congregation. Then from ⟪ar:'ولد تام '⟫⟪ar:(جثہ ابدائیہ)⟫ the perfect child (the original body), it progresses through ⟪ar:دوائر عاشریہ (عالم ابدأ)⟫ the circles of the tenth (the world of origin).[^cite-8]

## The Sensible Soul Is the Fundamental Soul

Allāh declares in many verses that His signs encompass the heavens and the earth:

> ⟪quran 12:105⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَأَيِّن مِّنْ آيَةٍۢ فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ يَمُرُّونَ عَلَيْهَا وَهُمْ عَنْهَا مُعْرِضُونَ ‎⟫
> *And how many a sign in the heavens and the earth they pass by, while they are averse therefrom.*
> — **Quran 12:105**

> ⟪quran 42:29⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمِنْ آيَتِهِۦ خَلْقُ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَمَا بَثَّ فِيهِمَا مِن دَآبَّةٍۢ ۚ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ جَمْعِهِمْ إِذَا يَشَآءُ قَدِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And among His Ayat (proofs, evidences, lessons, signs, etc.) is the creation of the heavens and the earth, and whatever moving (living) creatures He has dispersed in them both. And He is All-Potent over their assembling (i.e. resurrecting them on the Day of Resurrection after their death, and dispersion of their bodies) whenever He will.*
> — **Quran 42:29**

⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ The sensible soul pervades all things of creation. And there is a form ⟪ar:ھیولی⟫ (prime matter) attached to it. It remains inward because of its own concealment, yet manifests through action. ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ In the sky, the sensible soul moves from the circumference to the moon. It is the hidden form of the heavens that surrounds the celestial bodies and moves them. And it is this ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫, together with ⟪ar:امہات⟫ the mothers (the four elements), that ⟪ar:امہات⟫ moves all toward the center (the earth). The region from the moon to the center of Saqrā encompasses the totality of the ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ vegetative; yet ⟪ar:حسیۃ⟫ the sensible force exerts itself upon it, creating motion within it. These are the ⟪ar:موالید ثلاثۃ⟫ three offspring (mineral, plant, animal).[^cite-9]

⟪ar:قوت حسیۃ⟫ The sensible force exists in a most hidden state in minerals. Then it manifests as ⟪ar:'نامیۃ'⟫ vegetation, then as ⟪ar:'نامیۃ'⟫ animality, and then as the ⟪ar:'حسیۃ '⟫ sensibility from which the ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫ five senses arise. And then, too, it exists in the human, in whom it is united with ⟪ar:نامیۃ⟫ vegetative and sensible souls, by which he becomes acquainted with ⟪ar:نطق مجازی⟫ his righteous teacher through metaphorical speech. By accepting him, he progresses and rises to the level of true rationality (*nāṭiq*).

There are two types of sky: one ⟪ar:'جرمانی '⟫ corporeal and one ⟪ar:'نفسانی'⟫ spiritual. ⟪ar:جرمانی آسمان⟫ The corporeal heavens comprise the ⟪ar:افلاک⟫ spheres, while the ⟪ar:نفسانی آسمان⟫ spiritual heavens are constituted by the ⟪ar:حدود کرام⟫ noble limits, each of which serves as a heaven to the one below it. Conversely, the physical earth constitutes the fourth sphere—the sphere of clay—and is the boundary below which stands its corresponding earth in relation to all higher limits. It is ⟪ar:نفس حسیۃ⟫ the sensible soul that encompasses the heavens and the earth, as Allāh declares:

> ⟪quran 45:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ لَآيَتٍۢ لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫
> *Verily, in the heavens and the earth are signs for the believers.*
> — **Quran 45:3**
