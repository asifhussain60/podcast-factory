### Sincerity of Parents:

A great example of sincerity is that of a mother who breastfeeds her child but does not expect or desire any kind of compensation from that child in return for this milk. This mother's actions are done with complete sincerity for her child. In the same way, it is an act of sincerity for parents to bring up their children well, because they are both aware that this duty has been entrusted to them by Allah in their instinct, which they have to perform in all circumstances. They do not expect any kind of compensation from their children in return for this training. The term "feast" refers to father and mother. ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ) و علی⟫ These two men never asked the Ummah for their service, nor did they ever ask Allah for compensation for it, nor did they ever complain. ### Adversity and Patience

Patience is the name of perseverance in severe situations, without panic, for the sake of good results. Patience is the negative state of comfort and peace. Anything that causes pain to a person is called a calamity, and it is necessary to be patient in every calamity. The authorities have mentioned two forms of trouble. The first is that which is the result of a person's misdeeds. For example, a gambler becomes impoverished, a drunkard loses his health, an adulterer and a miscreant becomes a beggar. This first form of suffering belongs to all human beings. The second type of calamity is that in which a person is tested and his ranks are elevated. This kind of calamity is related to the special servants of Allaah.

### When is patience obligatory?

The patience of a common man begins when a person tries to get out of this trouble and it is not possible for him to get out of it. For example, if a person is thirsty and does not drink water even though there is water, then he will not be considered as patient. Because he has the means to get out of this difficulty and quench his thirst. But if there is no water and it is not possible to get it from anywhere, then the stages of patience will begin, just as Imam al-Husayn (a) and his companions in Karbala endured when they did not get water. ### The concept of honesty in a state of patience:

Some of the believers think that Allaah is oppressing them by inflicting misfortune on them. Some people think that Allah is unaware of their calamity. Some believe that Allaah is aware of their calamity, but at the same time they think that Allaah does not care about them, or that they do not care about them in the sight of Allaah.
