## The Interval Between the Call to Prayer and the Commencement of Prayer

It is narrated that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ said to ⟪ar:مؤذن بلالؓ⟫, the Muezzin Bilal: ⟪ar-quote:أِجْعَلُ بَیْنَ اَذاَنِکَ وَ اَقاَمَتِکَ نَفْساً لِیَفُرَغَ اَلمُتَوَضَّیءُ مِنْ وُضُوءِہِ وَالاَکِلُ مِنْ اَکَلِہِ وَ ذُوْاَلحاَجَۃِ مِنْ حاَجَتِہِ⟫ "Allow a respite between your *adhān* (call to prayer) and your *iqāmah* (commencement of prayer), so that the one performing ablution may complete his purification, the one eating may finish his meal, and the one occupied with a need may attend to it."[^cite-1]

The inner meaning (*taʾwīl*) of this teaching reveals a profound pedagogical principle within the spiritual invitation (*daʿwa*). The preacher (*dāʿī*, the one who calls) ought not to hasten in disclosing esoteric mysteries to the respondent (*Mustajīb*). Rather, he must grant the seeker time to establish firm foundations in the exoteric dimensions of the sacred law (*Sharīʿah*). Only when such outer observance is secured should the inner knowledge be transmitted, permitting the soul to be properly prepared for entry into the spiritual sanctuary. Thus the spiritual aspirant drinks from the sweet fountain of knowledge, purifying the soul through this luminous water of gnosis.

## Departure from the Gathering Upon Hearing the Call

⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین حضرت علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ and ⟪ar:سیدنا قاضی نعمان⟫ have narrated that one who hears the call to prayer while present in the mosque, yet withdraws from it, is counted among the hypocrites (*munāfiqūn*)—excepting only the one whose ritual purity has been invalidated and who departs with sincere intention to return in a state of renewed purification.[^cite-2]

The mosque signifies an inward assembly of spiritual prayer wherein the believers converge. The call to prayer symbolizes the summons to pledge allegiance and covenant. Through such gatherings, believers receive the oath of loyalty and attend to the words of wisdom that are recited therein. Whoever enters this sacred assembly and, when called to pledge allegiance to the Imam of the Age (⟪ar:امام الزمان علیہ السلام⟫), withdraws himself from this covenant, becomes a hypocrite. The sole exception is for one who departs with the sincere purpose of returning purified—that is, cleansed of the spiritual negligence that constitutes the "breaking" of one's inner ablution.

| --- | --- |
| Gathering of the Spiritual Invitation | Summons to Pledge Allegiance to the Imam |

## The Muezzin Must Possess Eloquence and Juristic Knowledge

⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین حضرت علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ and ⟪ar:سیدنا قاضی نعمان⟫ record that ⟪ar:مولانا علی⟫ said: "Let the one among you who calls to prayer be the most eloquent (*faṣīḥ*) and the most knowledgeable (*faqīh*)."[^cite-3]

The *muezzin* (one who calls to prayer) exemplifies the preacher who invites through the exoteric summons. Eloquence proves essential, for the dāʿī must confront erroneous beliefs and dispel opposition through persuasive argumentation—breaking false reasoning with sound discourse. This facility of speech enables him to address his audience with clarity and force. Moreover, the muezzin resembles the spiritual preacher, one who must possess profound familiarity with the permissible and the forbidden in the law of Allah. Through knowledge of jurisprudence (*fiqh*), the dāʿī distinguishes truth from falsehood.

| *Faṣīḥ* (Eloquent) | *Faqīh* (Jurist) |
| --- | --- |
| Explainer and Defender of True Beliefs | The Knower of the Lawful and the Forbidden |

## The Blind May Give the Call to Prayer

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق ؑ⟫ and ⟪ar:سیدنا قاضی نعمان⟫ narrate that there is no impediment to a blind person—such as ⟪ar:ابن مکتوم⟫—giving the call to prayer if he walks upon the path of truth.[^cite-4]

The blind person symbolizes one who has strayed from guidance; the call to prayer symbolizes the summoning of others to truth. The principle is this: should a person find himself in error, yet persist in calling others toward the straight path, such calling is permissible. And should he himself be guided and arrive at the path of truth, it is as though he has regained his sight—for the restoration of inward vision constitutes a second birth into illumination.

| --- | --- |
| One who has strayed from guidance | The Call to the Truth |

## The Imam and Those Who Follow: Positioning in Prayer

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق ؑ⟫ taught: "If a man leads one person in prayer, he shall position him at his right side; should he lead two or more, they shall stand behind him."[^cite-5]

The *imāmah* (leadership) of a single person signifies the Imam's own covenant and spiritual testament (*waṣiyyah*). The argument (*ḥujjah*) becomes the Imam after the Imam; the base (*qāʾid*) becomes the successor after the Imam's departure. Therefore, the Imam positions him at his right—the position of succession and authority. When two or more gather, the Imam stands before them as a manifestation of his superiority over all those who have responded to the summons (*al-Munbaʿithūn*, the Emanators), including his luminous argument and all the *Mustajībūn* (respondents).

## The Excellence of the Evening and Dawn Prayers

It is narrated that ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین حضرت علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ found ⟪ar:ابوالدروا⟫ asleep—having missed the congregational dawn prayer—and inquired of his condition. The man replied that something had occupied him during the night, troubling his eyes. The Commander of the Faithful asked: "Did you then neglect the Fajr prayer?" Upon confirmation, ⟪ar:مولانا علی⟫ declared: "Know that in my view, performing the *ʿIshāʾ* (evening) and *Fajr* (dawn) prayers in congregation is superior to remaining awake the entire night in private devotion."[^cite-6]

The esoteric interpretation reveals that *ʿIshāʾ* prayer marks the beginning of the hidden summons (*daʿwat al-sitr*); it represents the invitation of the Imam al-Tayyib (⟪ar:دعوت ستر⟫, ⟪ar:دعوتِ امام علی الطیب علیہ السلام⟫). Following this period, when concealment becomes complete during the *dūr al-sitr* (the era of occultation), the *Fajr* prayer and attendance at its congregation signal the gathering of all believers and respondents (*al-muʾminīn al-Mustajībūn*) under the invitation of the Mahdi (⟪ar:دعوت مہدی⟫, ⟪ar:مولانا مہدی⟫). The interval between these two prayers points to the chain of successive Imams throughout the intermediate ages. Indeed, the virtue ascribed to the Fajr prayer extends also to the invitation of the Messenger of Allah (⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم (دور محمدی)⟫), through whom Allah honored His faith and made manifest the command of His friends (*awliyāʾ*).

Participation in the summons after each preceding summons (*daʿwa*) becomes obligatory and cannot be abandoned under any circumstance, for it leads toward the destination of the truth. All believers shall gather in congregation under the guidance of the Mahdi, the Lord of the Age; should the form of the believer fail to reach there, neither shall his essence reach the Qāʾim (the Establisher)!

| --- | --- | --- |
| The Invitation of Imam al-Tayyib (⟪ar:مولانا علی الطیب علیہ السلام⟫) | The Invitations of the Imams in Succession | The Feast of the Mahdi |

## Ablution With Perseverance and Prayer Following Prayer

The Messenger of Allah (⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫) declared: "To perform ablution thoroughly despite aversion, to walk toward the mosque, and to wait for one prayer after another—these deeds wash away all sins."[^cite-7]

Outwardly, this refers to prayer and righteous deeds. Inwardly, it signifies that ablution resembles repentance from sin, striving to be freed from transgression, and purifying the soul through true knowledge—that luminous water of gnosis. Walking toward the mosque means striving in supplication to the truth and advancing toward its sacred assembly, earnestly listening to words of knowledge and wisdom. Waiting for prayer after prayer signifies remaining steadfast in the gathering after each major assembly, awaiting the invitation of one Imam and then the invitation of another, in unbroken succession.

## The Ranks of Men and Women in Prayer

The Messenger of Allah (⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫) stated: "Among the ranks of men, the first row is the best; among the ranks of women, the last row is the best."[^cite-8]

The rows in the mosque mirror the spiritual ordering of believers according to their ranks in responding to the summons toward truth. This arrangement depends upon the precedence in hastening toward the invitation. Those in the first row are those who entered the mosque earliest. In the esoteric sense, this teaches that the one who arrives late at the feast should never presume to surpass the ranks of those who came before.

The best row among men—those who embody beneficial knowledge (*al-nāfʿūn*)—consists of those whose ranks and stations are determined by their monotheistic conviction and their knowledge of the limits (*ḥudūd*) of truth. Among them are the Messengers, the Imams, the Supports (*Ḥujjaj*), the Standards (*Naqībah*), the Summoners (*Duʿāt*), and the Preparers (*Maʿdūnūn*); each possesses a station superior to that which follows. The masters of every summons stand foremost.

Similarly, women—who represent the beneficiaries of knowledge (*al-Mustajībīn*)—must recognize the rights and precedence of the bearers of knowledge. Rather than attempting to draw near to them in rank and contend with self-will, the beneficiaries must honor and respect the prerogatives of those who have guided them toward truth.

## Fasting Missed During Menstruation Must Be Made Up; Prayers Need Not

Regarding menstruation, it is said that fasts broken by menstruation must be observed anew after purification. Yet prayers missed during menstruation require no such compensation.[^cite-9]

The esoteric interpretation reveals that prayer (*salāh*) signifies the invitation to truth, while fasting (*ṣawm*) resembles the concealment of the mysteries of the invitation from the undeserving. When doubt (*shakk*) enters a believer—whether through intellectual uncertainty or a state rendering him incapable of guarding these sciences from those unworthy of them—he becomes unfit to attend the assemblies of the summons and to hear the words of wisdom. His deficiency in discretion (*kitmān al-asrār*) disqualifies him from such gatherings.

However, when the believer has been purified through knowledge and his capacity for secrecy is restored, he need not revisit the gatherings he missed during his period of doubt, for that time has passed and cannot be recovered. It suffices that he remain vigilant over the mysteries he learned before, and that he be present in future assemblies—as though commencing anew in his spiritual practice.

## Intimacy During Menstruation: The Permitted Mode

It is narrated from Imam Jāʿfar al-Ṣādiq (a.s.) that he permitted intimate relations during menstruation if the woman wore a garment extending from the navel to the knee, and her husband approached her through the *ʿawrah* (private area).

Outwardly, intimacy refers to direct physical contact. Esoterically, skin itself serves as the veil. It is not forbidden to convey apparent speech to the beneficiary for the sake of benefit, nor is it forbidden for the beneficiary to listen to such speech when doubt clouds his understanding. Menstruation symbolizes a state of doubt and spiritual uncertainty.

The wearing of a garment during this state represents a protective covering of the ear—the channel through which wisdom's word is heard. Placing such a covering upon it means closing the ear so that inner wisdom cannot penetrate during the state of doubt; the beneficiary can only be guided through outer speech until clarity is restored.

## Sin in Intercourse During Menstruation

It is narrated from the Imams that should a man have relations with a menstruating woman in a manner not permitted, he must seek forgiveness and repent to Allah; and should he expiate through charity, this proves even more meritorious. If the woman expresses aversion, no sin attaches to her. However, should the woman conceal her menstruation from the man and permit relations, the sin falls upon her, not upon him.[^cite-10]

If the beneficiary (*Mustajīb*) becomes aware of the uncertainty (*shakk*) within the bearer of knowledge (*ṣāḥib al-daʿwa*), yet continues to recite words of wisdom, then the sin is upon the beneficiary; he must repent and seek forgiveness from the bearer. Conversely, if the bearer is unaware of the beneficiary's condition and the beneficiary conceals his doubt while the bearer speaks words of beneficial knowledge in ignorance of this fact, then the sin rests upon the beneficiary. It becomes the duty of the beneficiary to inform the bearer of his state, to repent to Allah for what he has done, and to allow the bearer to purify him through knowledge.

## Prayer in Livestock Enclosures

The Messenger of Allah (⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫) said: "Do not pray in the camel's pen, but you may pray in the pen of sheep and goats."[^cite-11]

Prayer symbolizes the call to truth; the camel symbolizes the Imam (a.s.); sheep and goats symbolize the believers. The meaning is this: when the Imam is present, there is no need to establish a separate summons, and it is not permitted to conduct a summons in his presence. In a land where the Imam resides, a summons may be established only with his permission and under his authority. The sheep and goats represent the community of believers; therefore, the call to truth may be established only in their midst and with their affirmation.

## Gold and Silver Rings

The Prophet (⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫) said: "Men shall not wear rings of gold, for it is the adornment of the Hereafter."[^cite-12]

Esoterically, gold represents the Imam (a.s.), and silver represents the argument (*ḥujjah*). Similarly, man (*rajul*) represents the Imam, and woman (*mar'ah*) represents the argument. The principle is thus: in the presence of one Imam and within his era, another Imam cannot be established. However, a man may wear a silver ring, for an argument may exist alongside an Imam simultaneously.

## Additional Esoteric Interpretations Concerning Prayer

### Sitting Prayer

A person unable to stand, or an Imam so infirm that he cannot lead prayer while standing, may pray while seated. This circumstance represents one who lacks the strength to establish the summons due to personal weakness. For such a one, it remains obligatory to perform the duties of the summons in secret and in silence, without public proclamation.

### The Opening Chapter (*Umm al-Kitāb*)

It is narrated that after concluding the *Zuhr* (midday) prayer, the Messenger of Allah (⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫) addressed the congregation: "Which of you recited the *Umm al-Kitāb* (the Opening Chapter, *Sūrat al-Fātiḥah*) behind me during this prayer?" A man replied, "O Messenger of Allah, I was reciting it." The Prophet declared: "Whoever among you recites the *Umm al-Kitāb* behind me seeks thereby to lead the prayer."[^cite-13]

In the esoteric dimension, the *Umm al-Kitāb* refers to ⟪ar:امام علی المرتضی⟫ (Imam ʿAlī al-Murtaḍā, the Commander of the Faithful). The Imams are called the Book (*al-Kitāb*) in the language of spiritual interpretation. In terms of spiritual generation, ⟪ar:عیسیٰ⟫ (*ʿĪsā*, Jesus) is the mother of the Imams—that is, the *Umm al-Kitāb*—and the *Nātiq* (the Speaking Prophet) is their father.

### Placing the Hands Upon the Chest

When the Prophet (⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫) stood for prayer, he would place his hands upon his chest, for the chest symbolizes the Wāṣī (the Successor, ⟪ar:امام علی المرتضی⟫). The Messenger of Allah gathered his limits (*ḥudūd*)—symbolized by his hands—within his successor when he entrusted the command of his summons to him. Therefore, even in prayer, his hands remained placed in this posture of trust and spiritual integration.
