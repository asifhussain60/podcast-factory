## Esoteric Exegesis of Prayer Times

There are three appointed times established for each prayer. The interval set aside for the performance of each prayer consists of three distinct periods, each corresponding to a fixed temporal and spiritual station:

| Early Hours | Middle Time | Final Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Speaker (*nāṭiq*) | Foundation (*asās*) | Establisher of the Resurrection (*Qāʾim al-Qiyāma*) |
| Summoner (*dāʿī*) | Proof (*Ḥujjat*) | Imam |

The esoteric exegesis (*taʾwīl*) of the early period constitutes the foundation for understanding the intermediate period; likewise, the exegesis of the final period provides the spiritual basis for the Resurrection. Consequently, should a believer neglect prayer during the final appointed hour, salvation becomes impossible for him.[^cite-1] That is to say, one who fails to recognize divine truth even at the manifestation of the Qāʾim shall neither inherit the faith nor preserve his felicity in the Hereafter.

A second dimension of interpretation establishes the early time as the initial boundary (*ḥadd awwal*, the lower limit) for the believer, the middle time as the Proof, and the final time as the Imam. For this reason, prayer in the first appointed time possesses singular virtue (*faḍīla*). Since the Speaker stands nearest to the believer, it is incumbent upon the faithful to hasten to his summons (*daʿwa*, the invitation) and to fulfill with devotion the obligations he has imposed through his call. The longer one delays in responding to the summons of truth, the fewer opportunities remain to act within its framework.

### The Revelation of Divine Mercy During Prayer Times

When asked, "What divine blessings (*barakāt*) descend during the hours of prayer?" the Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—replied: "The gates of heaven are opened (*Tafattuḥ al-Abwāb al-Samāʾ*) at the hour of prayer." The Qur'ān confirms this mystery, stating: ⟪quran 15:16⟫.[^cite-2]

The heavens possess five portals through which divine mercy flows. Although mercy emanates continuously throughout the twenty-four hours, its manifestation upon earth occurs specifically during the appointed prayer times. Eighteen angelic forms (*ashkāl*) are entrusted with conveying this mercy to the terrestrial realm. These eighteen do not labor uniformly; rather, each fulfills its function during its appointed hour—save one whose service remains eternal and unceasing. This singular form is none other than the Imam of the Age (*Imām al-Zamān*), who stands as guardian (*walī*) over both night and day.[^cite-3]

The appointed times exceed the obligatory prayers themselves in dignity; therefore, punctuality is essential. The blessings that manifest upon the blessed stations through prayer illuminate the souls of worshippers and elevate their spiritual stations. Within the sanctuary of the Imamate (*ʿImāma*), an eternal form—stationed in the place of the Most Merciful—perpetually radiates illumination.

## Prayer Times

The appointed times of prayer surpass the obligatory prayers (*farāʾiḍ*) in their significance, for prayer ascends from us toward the Divine, while the times of prayer descend to us from the Divine. During these sacred hours, particular openings manifest themselves—stations (*maqāmāt*) from which the luminous chapters (*sūrāt*) of morning radiance (*al-Ishrāq*) receive and accept our supplications.

## The Five Times of Prayer

### The Circle as Universal Principle

All of Allah's systems operate in circular motion. Human existence itself unfolds within a circle—the space circumscribed by the span of one's extended arms. All that one accomplishes occurs within this circle. Should the circle be unfolded into a straight line, it becomes what is termed "the straight path" (*al-ṣirāṭ al-mustaqīm*), the shortest, truest, and most direct passage from falsehood to truth. Every heavenly sphere, every moon and sun, every star, year, month, week, and day—all things in creation—revolve in circles.

The number five is called a circle because it operates cyclically. Whichever number is multiplied by five yields either a cycle or terminates in five. For this reason, the circles of all the hierarchies (*marātib*) function according to the number five. Every system of divine governance works through this quintuple principle.

### The Five Prayer Times and Spiritual Stations

Five prayers occur within five appointed times. When the hour of prayer arrives, the spiritual portals of the celestial Kaaba—which stands in correspondence to the material Kaaba—open, and the luminosities of prayer commence, illuminating the soul of the worshipper. Each mosque possesses a hierarchical degree (*darja*) above every place of prayer (*muṣallā*).

The appointed time surpasses the prayer itself in rank. Time pertains to the veiled intermediary (*muḥtajab*), while prayer pertains to that intermediary's veil (*ḥijāb*, the manifest guides). When the hour of prayer arrives, the intermediary's spiritual influence begins to affect his or her particular guides (*imāms*); then these guides' influence affects the appearance (*ẓuhūr*) of the worshippers. All these luminosities concentrate in that portion of earth where the prayer time is observed. When the time concludes, they withdraw to another region. Yet one angelic form alone remains present at all hours—the veil of eternity itself, the eighteenth station in the circle of the eternal cosmos, which dominates the preceding seventeen. Its function is exclusively forgiveness. Wherever a person prays within the twenty-four hours—whether reciting missed prayers or supererogatory prayers (*nāfila*)—this eighteenth form continuously influences and sustains their spiritual state.
