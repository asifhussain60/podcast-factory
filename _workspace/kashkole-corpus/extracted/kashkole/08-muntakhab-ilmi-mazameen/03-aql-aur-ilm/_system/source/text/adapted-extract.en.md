# Wisdom and Knowledge

<!-- section 1 (id=1064, raw_sort=1): دنیا میں عقل اور علم کا مقام -->

## The Station of Intellect and Knowledge in the World

The learned agree that the universe rests upon a fundamental principle: existence precedes non-existence. From this foundation, two types of being emerge: the animate (possessing the power to grow) and the inanimate (unchanging), with animation taking precedence over stasis. Among animate beings, two categories arise: the sentient and the non-sentient, with sensation preceding insensibility. Among the sentient, there appear two types: the rational and the non-rational, with rationality predominating. Finally, among the rational emerge two forms: the knowledgeable and the ignorant, with knowledge holding supreme virtue. Thus does the cosmos demonstrate that ⟪ar:'علم'⟫ knowledge itself is the ultimate reality to which all creation tends.[^cite-1]

In other words, each superior species establishes itself through the superior attribute of the preceding species. Existence alone permits naming; naming alone permits sensation; sensation alone permits intellection; and intellection alone permits the acquisition of knowledge. This ascending order reveals the true hierarchy of being.

⟪ar:'علم'⟫ Knowledge is the recognition of things according to their real nature, while ⟪ar:'عقل'⟫ the intellect (reason) is the faculty enabling this recognition. A thing remains ⟪ar:'مجھول'⟫ unknown until it is learned; once apprehended, it becomes ⟪ar:'معلوم'⟫ known. Knowledge dwells in the very essence of intellect as a pearl resides in the oyster. All creation save the Creator is enveloped by knowledge. The al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect) first acquired this knowledge, and through it all subsequent intellects received their portion. Yet the First Intellect is not the Creator Most High, for it falls within the category of the known. The Creator is the Originator of both knowledge and intellect, transcendent above both.[^cite-2]

| The Reality of Things | The Power of Knowledge |
|---|---|
| *Manifestation* | *Intellection* |

In the spiritual world, the Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High) bestowed upon the Ishbāḥ (celestial entities) the essence of knowledge, power, and life. When these revelations—⟪ar:نور علم الکلمۃ⟫ the light of the Word's knowledge—were received, they transformed from potentiality (⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫) into actuality (⟪ar:بالفعل⟫), and they became known as ⟪ar:'عقول'⟫ intellects. The intellect is thus the very essence of knowledge, power, and life itself.[^cite-3]

### Intellect and Discernment

It is recorded that animals possess not intellect but rather the faculty of distinguishing between good and evil. Man, by contrast, has been granted intellect—the power to discern between two goods and two evils. When two evils present themselves, the wise man chooses the lesser; when two goods appear, he selects the superior.

| Discernment | Intellect |
|---|---|
| Distinguishing Good from Evil | Distinguishing Between Two Goods and Two Evils |

### The Boundaries of Intellect

⟪ar:نبی کریم (ﷺ)⟫ The Noble Prophet, peace be upon him, declared: ⟪ar-quote:اَوَّلَ مَا خَلَقَ اﷲ اَلعَقَل۔ فَقاَلَ لَہُ اَقبِل فَأَقبَلَۃ وَ قَالَ لَہُ اَدبِر فَأَدبَرَۃ ۔ قَالَ بِکَ عُقِیبُ وَ بِکَ اُثِیبُ⟫ "The first thing Allah created was the intellect. He commanded it: ⟪ar:أقبلۃ⟫ 'Advance!' and it advanced. Then He said: ⟪ar:أدبرۃ⟫ 'Retreat!' and it retreated. Then Allah declared: 'By you shall I reward and by you shall I punish.'"[^cite-4]

In this divine address to the intellect, the Creator spoke to it, commanded it to move forward and to withdraw, and the intellect obeyed. Allah then proclaimed: "By My Majesty and Glory! I have created nothing greater than you. By you shall I grant reward, and by you shall I inflict punishment." Thus, only those who act in accordance with the Sharīʿa (divine law) with understanding and intellection shall receive reward or punishment; otherwise, accountability holds no force.

The Arabic word ⟪ar:عَقَل⟫ derives from tying the feet of a camel. It would be foolish to leave a camel untethered, for it would wander away. Intellect is thus named after restraint and confinement, within which knowledge grows and flourishes. When Allah created the intellect and commanded it to advance and retreat, He established boundaries for it, prescribing reward and punishment according to these limits. Every faculty operates within its appointed circle, and recompense is calculated accordingly. A believer who errs receives punishment, but a preacher (dāʿī, a Summoner) who errs faces severer consequence, for greater knowledge entails greater responsibility. The same principle applies to reward.

## The True Purpose of Intellect

The intellect is the greatest gift Allah has bestowed upon humanity. Through the wisdom of intellect, man has brought down the birds of the air and drawn forth fish from the river's depths, subjugating beasts and birds to his dominion. He benefits from their flesh and hides, and despite the vastness of the sky, he has bound it within the prison of his thought. Had man not been illumined by ⟪ar:بصیرت حاصل⟫ insight through intellect, he would never have accepted the Messenger of Allah, nor would anyone have heeded his commands and acted upon them. No soul would have been illuminated by the knowledge of divine unity (tawhīd), nor would any tongue have spoken of the knowledge of the Hereafter.[^cite-5]

The intellect is a force by which a person separates divine unity from both ⟪ar:تشبیہ⟫ anthropomorphism and ⟪ar:تعطیل⟫ negation of divine attributes—neither denying the Oneness of Allah nor likening Him to created things. In this sense, intellect is a supreme blessing from Allah Most High, guiding humanity toward a Being transcendent of all need. This very understanding is truly an indication of ⟪ar:مُجَرَّد⟫ pure monotheism. Therefore, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و سلم⟫ the Messenger of Allah made maturity and intellectual capacity conditions for entry into Islam, excluding the ignorant, the childish, and the insane.

The superiority of intellect is demonstrated by this analogy: the intellect stands in relation to the human spirit as the outward eye stands to the body. Just as man sees the visible world through his eye—knowing and recognizing colors and qualities—so through intellect does he comprehend the intelligible realities of the Hereafter. In outward sight, the animal shares equally with man, yet the animal lacks intellect. For this reason humanity surpasses the beasts. One who blinds both his eyes is called a fool and mad; yet more deserving of these epithets are those who, possessed of intellect, neither contemplate Allah's creation nor heed the principles of His Sharīʿa—a law that distinguishes the paths to Paradise from those to Hell.

The intellect, which is ⟪ar:'رسول اول'⟫ the first messenger, has as its essential purpose connection with the external intellect that is ⟪ar:'رسول ثانی'⟫ the second messenger. Just as an eye is useless without sight and ⟪ar:رسول أول⟫ cannot achieve its purpose without ⟪ar:رسول ثانی⟫ the second messenger, so the intellect cannot fulfill its function apart from the Nāṭiq (the Speaker, the Prophetic Guide), the Wasī (the Trustee, the Vicegerent), or the Imām (the Leader of the Age). Through their union does the intellect actualize its knowledge, transform its power and life from potentiality (⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫) into actuality (⟪ar:بالفعل⟫), and achieve felicity.

## The Union of Knowledge with Action

Man is constituted of two fundamental principles: ⟪ar:روح⟫ spirit and ⟪ar:جسم⟫ body. Thus he is not a simple (⟪ar:مفرد⟫) being but a compound (⟪ar:مرکب⟫) one. More subtly, the human composite consists of ⟪ar:'روح لطیف'⟫ the refined spirit and ⟪ar:'جسم کثیف'⟫ the dense body. Their union establishes human existence. All creation in this world springs from this subtle-dense combination:

| Subtle Spiritual (Non-Composite) | Dense Physical (Composite) |
|---|---|
| Fire | Air, Wind |
| Soil, Width | — |
| Total Wisdom | Total Breath |
| Speaker (Nāṭiq) | Trustee (Wasī) |
| Knowledge | Prayer, Meaning |
| Action | Prayer, Sharīʿa |

Man received his share ⟪ar:عالم لطافت⟫ in the subtle realm in the form of innate intellect—a faculty the beasts do not possess. Yet this intellect is weak and incomplete. To remedy this deficiency, there was created one fully knowledgeable, in constant communion with ⟪ar:عقل کل⟫ the universal intellect, so that all humanity might derive their knowledge from this single person. ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ The Messenger of Allah was the man of his time, endowed with the full share of universal knowledge.

All humans resemble one another in one respect and differ in another. They are harmonious (⟪ar:'موافق'⟫) with respect to the ⟪ar:نفس⟫ self—all possess souls equal in their faculties. Yet they differ in bodily form. Similarly, the knowledge the Prophet brought divides into two parts: one harmonious like the self, the other differentiated like the body. Thus there are two types of knowledge—one apparent as the body, the other inward as the self. Knowledge (⟪ar:'علم'⟫) pertains to the soul's realm, while action (⟪ar:'عمل'⟫) pertains to the body's.

| Knowledge of the Outward | Esoteric Knowledge |
|---|---|
| Body | Spirit |

Thus were the believers invited to perform deeds achievable through knowledge. The goal: that they act through their bodies while understanding these acts' inner meaning through the self. The body performed prayer, pilgrimage, and fasting; the soul grasped their esoteric meanings through the Prophets.

### Jewels of Knowledge and Action

In Arabic, action is ⟪ar:عمل⟫ *ʿamal* and knowledge is ⟪ar:علم⟫ *ʿilm*, both composed of three letters. Their numerical values, according to the sacred reckoning (⟪ar:'میزان'⟫), total 140.

The number 14 equals 140 divided by 10 (⟪ar:عقد⟫), which is seven doubled. To this the Messenger of Allah refers when he says that Allah has granted him seven oft-recited verses:

> ⟪quran 15:87⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَكَ سَبْعًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْمَثَانِى وَٱلْقُرْآنَ ٱلْعَظِيمَ ‎⟫
> *And indeed, We have bestowed upon you seven of Al-Mathani (the seven repeatedly recited Verses), (i.e. Surat Al-Fatiha) and the Grand Quran.*

Allah has given His Messenger a law wherein every outward action is linked to its inner knowledge, and a book—⟪ar:'قرآن کریم'⟫ the Noble Qur'ān—whose every verse conceals manifold inner meanings.[^cite-6]

Three conditions of faith correspond to the three letters of knowledge and action: profession of the heart, utterance of the tongue, and action of the hands and feet. These three letters also signify the three powers of the human soul: ⟪ar:ناطقۃ، حسیۃ، نامیۃ⟫ the rational, the sensible, and the vegetative:

| Letter | Power | Aspect of Faith |
|---|---|---|
| ʿ (ʿAyn) | Nāṭiqa (Rational) | Certification of the Heart |
| L (Lām) | Ḥassiyya (Sensible) | Testimony of the Tongue |
| M (Mīm) | Nāmiyya (Vegetative) | Action of the Body |

Two things constitute human piety: ⟪ar:علم⟫ knowledge and ⟪ar:عمل⟫ action. When both unite in a person, he becomes religious, just as soul and body joined make a human being. Action is the ⟪ar:'مثل'⟫ image, while its meaning is the ⟪ar:'ممثول'⟫ original. For religion, action is like the body and knowledge like the soul. As body and soul's union creates a living being, so knowledge and action's union creates piety.[^cite-7]

He who acts without knowledge has no soul in his religion—his deeds are lifeless, and Allah has forbidden the dead in His Book. This signifies that ignorant action is displeasing and forbidden. Yet he who learns without acting has no religion either, for no living soul can perform the deeds of Sharīʿa without a body. Still, knowledge without action surpasses action without knowledge; as the saying goes, when sustenance fails, one eats the dead.

⟪ar-quote:أعلم ان قلیل العلم یحتاج علی کثیر العمل⟫ When Moses returned with the Tablets, he said: "Know that little knowledge requires much action." Jesus narrated encountering a stone inscribed: ⟪ar-quote:من لا یعمل بما یعلم مشوع علیہ طلب بما لا یعلم ؛ و مردود علیہ بما یعلم⟫ "Whoever does not practice his knowledge, Allah will seal him from seeking more knowledge and will take from him what he has learned."

The Messenger of Allah declared: ⟪ar-quote:من عمل بما يعلم كَانَ حقا عَلی اللہ أَن يعلمہ ما لا يعلم ، ويوفقہ فيما يعمل حَتَّی يستوجب بِذَلِكَ الجنۃ ، ومن لم يعمل بما يعلم تاه فيما لا يعلم حَتَّی يستوجب بِذَلِكَ النار⟫ "Whoever acts upon his knowledge, it becomes incumbent upon Allah to grant him knowledge of what he knows not, and to aid him in his deeds until Paradise becomes his right. But he who does not act upon his knowledge wanders in darkness until Hell becomes his fate."[^cite-8]

The Messenger of Allah also said: "Two types of people have broken my back: the wicked scholar and the ignorant worshipper. The wicked scholar turns people from true religion through his corruption, while the ignorant worshipper draws them to ignorance through his apparent piety." For this reason, Mawlānā ʿAlī said: ⟪ar-quote:اَلْعِلمُ بِدُوْنِ الْعَمَل وَباَل وَالْعَمَلُ بِدُوْنِ العِلْمِ ضَلاَل⟫ "Knowledge without action is wretchedness, and action without knowledge is misguidance."

The Messenger declared: "Every verse I have received carries an inward meaning behind its outward expression." The Sharīʿa's outer and inner aspects mirror man's body and soul. The body's motion testifies to the soul's presence; the practitioner's action testifies to the acceptability of his deed. ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ He said: "The least knowledge is that uttered by the tongue while the organs remain idle. The heaviest knowledge is that manifested in both tongue and bodily organs—this knowledge bears fruit." Yet Allah revealed to ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ Abraham: there are two kinds of knowledge—one awakening in the heart and burning there, such knowledge profits; the other merely voiced by the tongue, disconnected from action, bringing pain to the children of Adam on the Day of Resurrection.

⟪ar:مولانا فتح اللہ صاحب⟫ Mawlānā Fatḥ Allāh said: ⟪ar-quote:مَن عَلِمَ وَ عَمِلَ لَمِعَ وَ مَن عَلِمَ وَلاَ عَلِمَِ مَلَعَ⟫ "Whoever acquires knowledge and acts upon it shines; he who acquires knowledge yet does not act is cursed." ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ The Prophet warned: "Whoever seeks knowledge to boast before people, to excel over the ignorant, or to gain status and honor among them should know he is building his house in Hell." ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و سلم⟫ He also said: "Do not withhold knowledge from the capable, lest you wrong them; nor teach it to the unworthy, lest you wrong the knowledge itself." ⟪ar:مولانا علی علیہ السلام⟫ Mawlānā ʿAlī said: "Spending on the undeserving is stinginess and miserliness."

### The Human Body and the System of the Daʿwa

As human organs are assigned different functions, so are the ḥudūd (the boundaries, or stations of the daʿwa, the Islamic summons) entrusted with various services. Bread enters the hand before it nourishes. The hand delivers it to the mouth; the teeth chew and cut; the tongue secretes saliva; the jaw supports; the throat swallows. These organs work in harmony toward one purpose: nourishment of the body.

So too with the Daʿwat Ḥādiya (the Rightly-Guided Summons). All the ḥudūd perform their respective functions for the believers' spiritual development. The believer accepting the daʿwa is like bread before the teeth—his false beliefs are broken and corrupted beliefs removed. The Maẓūn (an officer in the daʿwa hierarchy), like the tongue, imparts knowledge and meaning—the saliva of spiritual nourishment. The other ḥudūd sustain this system, elevating him toward the higher ranks. As the throat passes food to the ⟪ar:اعضاء رئیسہ⟫ principal organs for digestion, so the ⟪ar:مومن مستجیب⟫ responding believer gradually acquires spiritual sustenance until he attains the level of the intellective powers, growing ever more subtle.

## The Difference in the Progression of Knowledge and Action

Just as the body cannot survive without life, neither can the soul endure without the body. Between them exists such profound interdependence that neither can exist without the other. ⟪ar:سیدنا سلیمان بن حسن اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ Our Master Sulaymān ibn al-Ḥasan, may Allah sanctify his secret, taught that a believer requires two wings to ascend to ⟪ar:عالم بالا⟫ the higher realm from ⟪ar:عالم سفلی⟫ the lower world: one is knowledge, the other action. One is principal, the other auxiliary—as a man's right hand labors while the left assists. Knowledge is the key to humanizing the soul; action is its helper. Or, as it may be said: action is the body, knowledge the soul. Man's practical aspect is ⟪ar:'اسلام'⟫ Islam (submission); his intellectual aspect is ⟪ar:'ایمان'⟫ faith (belief). Islam without faith is a soulless body—lifeless. The inanimate body knows not whether it lives or dies, yet the believers recognize that those of Islam without faith are truly dead, deprived of real life. These are the believers whom the Messenger of Allah invited from the call of Islam to the call of faith. Of them the Qur'ān says:

> ⟪quran 8:24⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱسْتَجِيبُوا۟ لِلَّهِ وَلِلرَّسُولِ إِذَا دَعَاكُمْ لِمَا يُحْيِيكُمْ ۖ وَٱعْلَمُوٓا۟ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَحُولُ بَيْنَ ٱلْمَرْءِ وَقَلْبِهِۦ وَأَنَّهُۥٓ إِلَيْهِ تُحْشَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Answer Allah (by obeying Him) and (His) Messenger when he (PBUH) calls you to that which will give you life, and know that Allah comes in between a person and his heart (i.e. He prevents an evil person to decide anything). And verily to Him you shall (all) be gathered.*

These believers attain such living reality that the entire world becomes transparent to them—as if they conversed with all existence. Hence the Messenger of Allah said: ⟪ar-quote:یَشھَدُ نَبُوَّتِی کُلَّ حَجَر وَ مَدَر⟫ "Every stone and clod bears witness to My prophethood." To these knowers, the origin and reality of all things stands revealed.[^cite-9]

## The Superiority of Knowledge over Prayer

⟪ar:حضرت المنصوب مولانا عبد اللہ بن ھبۃ اللہ⟫ When it was asked whether it is superior to acquire knowledge or to spend the night in prayer, the appointed Master, our lord ʿAbd Allāh ibn Hibat Allāh, replied: The Messenger of Allah declared: ⟪ar-quote:طلب العلم فریضۃ علی کل مؤمن و مؤمنۃ⟫ "Seeking knowledge is obligatory upon every believing man and woman." By this, the Holy Prophet made knowledge-seeking a duty. Duty (farīḍa) always surpasses voluntary devotion. Thus the reward for acquiring knowledge—its meanings and realities—exceeds the reward for prayer.[^cite-10]

## Which Sciences Must One Know?

When the hadith of the Messenger of Allah—⟪ar-quote:طلب العلم فریضۃ علی کل مسلم و مسلمۃ⟫ "Seeking knowledge is obligatory upon every Muslim man and woman"—was presented to Mawlānā ʿAlī (peace be upon him), he clarified: ⟪ar-quote:العلم التقوی و الیقین⟫ "That knowledge is piety and certitude," referring to the ⟪ar:کلمہ توحید لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ testimony of unity: "There is no god but Allah."

⟪ar:داعی یمن سیدنا علی بن سیدنا سلیمان صاحب⟫ When the Dāʿī of Yemen, our Master ʿAlī son of our Master Sulaymān, was asked which knowledge believers must not be ignorant of and are obliged to know, he answered: First, one must understand monotheism (tawhīd) correctly, avoiding both ⟪ar:تشبیہ⟫ anthropomorphism and ⟪ar:تعطیل⟫ negation of attributes. Second, one must acknowledge all the Messengers and testify that they have fulfilled the truth of prophecy. Third, one must affirm ⟪ar:خاتم النبیین اور سید المرسلین⟫ the Seal of the Prophets and Master of the Messengers. Fourth, one must confirm what they brought from Allah. Fifth, one must acknowledge the Imams of the Prophet's descendants, whom Allah has made obligatory to love and obey, whom He has commanded be consulted regarding ⟪ar:مسائل شرعیۃ⟫ matters of Sharīʿa. Allah says:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority. (And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day. That is better and more suitable for final determination.*

Sixth, one must know the Imām al-Zamān (the Leader of the Age) and believe in his Imāma and Wilāya (his spiritual guardianship). One must obey his commands, acquire from him the knowledge of truth, and believe that Allah has never left the earth void of His proof. This earth has never been empty of an Imām calling to Allah. Allah Himself declares:

> ⟪quran 17:71⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَوْمَ نَدْعُوا۟ كُلَّ أُنَاسٍۭ بِإِمَمِهِمْ ۖ فَمَنْ أُوتِىَ كِتَبَهُۥ بِيَمِينِهِۦ فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ يَقْرَءُونَ كِتَبَهُمْ وَلَا يُظْلَمُونَ فَتِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(And remember) the Day when We shall call together all human beings with their (respective) Imam [their Prophets, or their records of good and bad deeds, or their Holy Books like the Quran, the Taurat (Torah), the Injeel (Gospel), etc.]. So whosoever is given his record in his right hand, such will read their records, and they will not be dealt with unjustly in the least.*

To the Prophet, Allah addresses:

> ⟪quran 13:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَيَقُولُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَوْلَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْهِ آيَةٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّهِۦٓ ۗ إِنَّمَآ أَنتَ مُنذِرٌۭ ۖ وَلِكُلِّ قَوْمٍ هَادٍ ‎⟫
> *And the disbelievers say: "Why is not a sign sent down to him from his Lord?" You are only a warner, and to every people there is a guide.*

Therefore, whoever knows these matters, believes in them, and acknowledges them must follow the Guide and the Warning-bearer. He should seek the Imām's guidance regarding ⟪ar:امور شرعیۃ⟫ matters of Sharīʿa and other ⟪ar:علوم حقیقۃ⟫ true sciences. As Allah says: "If you know not, then ask the people of remembrance":

> ⟪quran 16:43⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن قَبْلِكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۚ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent not (as Our Messengers) before you (O Muhammad PBUH) any but men, whom We inspired, (to preach and invite mankind to believe in the Oneness of Allah). So ask of those who know the Scripture [learned men of the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel)], if you know not.*

Thus as the believer advances in ⟪ar:معرفت⟫ gnosis, ⟪ar:اقرار⟫ acknowledgment, and ⟪ar:تصدیق⟫ confirmation, his station and rank elevate. Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 58:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجَلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحُ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you are told to make room in the assemblies, (spread out and) make room. Allah will give you (ample) room (from His Mercy). And when you are told to rise up [for prayers, Jihad (holy fighting in Allah's Cause), or for any other good deed], rise up. Allah will exalt in degree those of you who believe, and those who have been granted knowledge. And Allah is Well-Acquainted with what you do.*

⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ The Commander of the Faithful teaches: "The worth of every person is measured by his knowledge. Greater knowledge brings greater value." ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ The Prophet said: "It is obligatory upon every Muslim man and woman to seek knowledge." He also declared: "Do not engage in ⟪ar:قیاس⟫ logical deduction in religion, for religion transcends conjecture. Soon there will arise a people who act upon opinion and supposition in religion, and they shall be religion's enemy. ⟪ar-quote:اَوَّلَ مَن قاَسَ اِبلِیسَ⟫ The first to reason by analogy was Iblis. When you hear a hadith from us that perplexes you, do not deny it; rather, be silent and endeavor to understand its meaning from us. Therein lies your salvation."

⟪ar:مولانا امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Mawlānā Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq said: "If you understand not our ⟪ar:علوم حقائق و دقائق⟫ sciences of realities and subtleties and they seem to contradict common understanding, do not deny them. Rather, refer them to Us for clarification. Turning to Us, you shall understand the correct meaning. Should you disbelieve, you shall deny Us and turn from the religion of truth." ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علیہ السلام⟫ The Commander of the Faithful warned: "Whoever reveals Our secrets to the unworthy, Allah shall make him ⟪ar-quote:بَردُ الحَدِید⟫ taste the punishment of cold iron."[^cite-11]

## Truth and Conjecture

The supplication declares: ⟪ar-quote:اَلحَقُّ یَعلُو وَلاَ یُعلاَ⟫ "Truth is exalted, nothing exalts it." When the people of truth hear the words Allah has revealed, their eyes overflow with tears—they declare, "Our Lord, we believe! Write us among the witnesses!" Allah says:

> ⟪quran 5:83-86⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا سَمِعُوا۟ مَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ تَرَىٰٓ أَعْيُنَهُمْ تَفِيضُ مِنَ ٱلدَّمْعِ مِمَّا عَرَفُوا۟ مِنَ ٱلْحَقِّ ۖ يَقُولُونَ رَبَّنَآ آمَنَّا فَٱكْتُبْنَا مَعَ ٱلشَّهَدِينَ ‎ ‏وَمَا لَنَا لَا نُؤْمِنُ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَا جَآءَنَا مِنَ ٱلْحَقِّ وَنَطْمَعُ أَن يُدْخِلَنَا رَبُّنَا مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلصَّلِحِينَ ‎ ‏فَأَثَبَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ بِمَا قَالُوا۟ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَرُ خَلِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ وَذَلِكَ جَزَآءُ ٱلْمُحْسِنِينَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ وَكَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَتِنَآ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَحِيمِ ‎⟫
> *And when they (who call themselves Christians) listen to what has been sent down to the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), you see their eyes overflowing with tears because of the truth they have recognised. They say: "Our Lord! We believe; so write us down among the witnesses. "And why should we not believe in Allah and in that which has come to us of the truth (Islamic Monotheism)? And we wish that our Lord will admit us (in Paradise on the Day of Resurrection) along with the righteous people (Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Companions radhiallahu'anhuã)." So because of what they said, Allah rewarded them Gardens under which rivers flow (in Paradise), they will abide therein forever. Such is the reward of good­doers. But those who disbelieved and belied Our Ayat (proofs, evidences, verses, lessons, signs, revelations, etc.), they shall be the dwellers of the (Hell) Fire.*

Their testimony arises because ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the Messenger of Allah has made them believe the truth. Allah rewards them with Gardens of ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫ great bounty beneath which rivers flow, for ⟪ar:مولانا علی⟫ Mawlānā ʿAlī—the truth itself—has illuminated them.[^cite-12]

Every action possesses a true purpose: to quench thirst is water's real purpose. The true purpose of the one who patiently seeks honor is to reach truth, obey commands of reward, and abstain from slander and sin. Truth is ⟪ar:غایت⟫ the goal beyond which no other ⟪ar:نہایت⟫ destination exists—no further progression after truth is reached. Yet besides this ultimate purpose, other things exist which are ⟪ar:'مشابہ'⟫ similar and ⟪ar:'متشاکل⟫ resembling truth, though not themselves truths. As words carry ⟪ar:'معنی'⟫ meaning, yet words themselves, though ⟪ar:مشابہ و متشکل⟫ similar to meaning, are not themselves meaning.

We swiftly move through our conjectures seeking truth, attempting to understand the meanings hidden within words. It is possible we shall not reach truth itself, yet we may attain what is ⟪ar:مشابہ و متشکل⟫ right—similar to it. Our efforts reach a limit (⟪ar:(غایت القصوا)⟫) beyond which we cannot proceed. This point is called ⟪ar:'صواب'⟫ correctness. This correctness (⟪ar:صواب⟫) leads us toward the final truth (⟪ar:(غایت الحق)⟫). One must traverse the path of ⟪ar:'صواب'⟫ correctness to reach the goal of ⟪ar:'حق'⟫ truth. That is, we cannot reach ⟪ar:مشابہ و متشکل⟫ Ali directly through ourselves, yet we can reach those who resemble Ali—the ⟪ar:'ائمہ کرام علیھم السلام'⟫ Noble Imams. These Imams guide us back to Ali, our ultimate destination.[^cite-13]

⟪ar:سیدنا لَمَک ابن المالک الحامدی⟫ Our Master Lāmak son of al-Mālik al-Ḥāmidī defined conjecture and hesitation thus: ⟪ar-quote:اَلوَہمُ وُقُوف اَلنَّفسِ بَینَ الصَّواَبِ وَضِدِّہِ⟫ "Conjecture is the soul's standing between correctness and its opposite." It is the state between ⟪ar:'یقین'⟫ certainty and ⟪ar:وحم⟫ mere surmise. Such conjecture moves man forward in his quest for truth. Yet when one grasps the meaning hidden within words, he should make no judgment or ruling upon it save through someone possessed of discernment, who establishes his judgment upon that meaning through rational proofs.

## Humanity and Its Spiritual Advancement

Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 23:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ خَلَقْنَا ٱلنُّطْفَةَ عَلَقَةًۭ فَخَلَقْنَا ٱلْعَلَقَةَ مُضْغَةًۭ فَخَلَقْنَا ٱلْمُضْغَةَ عِظَمًۭا فَكَسَوْنَا ٱلْعِظَمَ لَحْمًۭا ثُمَّ أَنشَأْنَهُ خَلْقًا آخَرَ ۚ فَتَبَارَكَ ٱللَّهُ أَحْسَنُ ٱلْخَلِقِينَ ‎⟫
> *Then We made the Nutfah into a clot (a piece of thick coagulated blood), then We made the clot into a little lump of flesh, then We made out of that little lump of flesh bones, then We clothed the bones with flesh, and then We brought it forth as another creation. So blessed be Allah, the Best of creators.*

### The Conditions of Animal and Human Birth

A human being commences from the ⟪ar:'نطفہ'⟫ sperm-drop and through ⟪ar:'لحم'⟫ flesh reaches the level of his physical journey, growing like a plant. Upon birth, he enters the ⟪ar:حسیہ⟫ sensible realm, resembling an animal. Yet unlike a newborn beast, the human infant cannot sustain himself. He depends entirely upon parents, teachers, and mentors for nourishment, locomotion, and training. At birth the human child lies helpless as flesh without protection or supervision—he perishes. The animal, by contrast, emerges seeking food, already clothed according to its climate's needs, and employs all its faculties from the moment of birth. The human child cannot do this. Third, while an animal consumes raw food—plant or meat—humans cannot digest the raw. Fourth, an animal cub (save the monkey), if thrown into water, instinctively swims; if cast from height, instinctively defends itself. The human child remains defenseless, unable even to attempt self-preservation.

Why does the animal—inferior to man—acquire discernment so swiftly, while man, ⟪ar:اشرف المخلوقات⟫ the noblest of creatures, lacks it? The answer: this world is the beast's home, his ⟪ar:دار کمال⟫ house of perfection, wherein all necessities and all comforts already dwell. But the ⟪ar:'انسان'⟫ human is merely a wayfarer, his stay but a few days. This is not his homeland. Here, his self must acquire knowledge and attain ⟪ar:کمال آخر⟫ final perfection in ⟪ar:صورت روحانی⟫ spiritual form, destined for ⟪ar:دار آخرت⟫ the Hereafter. Therefore, he requires many to educate and perfect him.[^cite-14]

### The Human Need for Knowledge

All human necessities and livelihood depend upon ⟪ar:'علم'⟫ knowledge. Only those things become useful for sustenance that yield to the knowledge of cultivation—the tilling and working of land to produce grain and fruit. Then man must cook the grain by his own knowledge, benefiting from it. The more skilled in culinary arts, the finer the nourishment. Likewise, clothing requires the tailor's knowledge, shelter the architect's. Thus human beings depend upon knowledge at every stage of development. The greater his skill in each art of physical advancement, the more ease he attains. Similarly, as he progresses spiritually through knowledge of the soul, the higher his rank in the Hereafter.[^cite-15]

Man requires a master and expert in every art to achieve perfection, as it is said: ⟪ar-quote:اَستَعِینُ عَلَی کُلِّ صَنعَۃٍ مِن اَھلِھَا⟫ "Seek every craft and art from those expert in it." The jurist does not teach agriculture, nor the sailor teach farming. Similarly, the knowledge of religion and the spiritual sciences related to the Qur'ān are obtained only through ⟪ar:'باب'⟫ the gate—namely the ⟪ar:'مدینۃ العلم'⟫ City of Knowledge—and not from another. They alone are its scholars and experts. Whoever acquires knowledge apart from them shall lose all despite great effort; his labor becomes void, his expense wasted. Of such, Allah says:

> ⟪quran 2:16⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱشْتَرَوُا۟ ٱلضَّلَلَةَ بِٱلْهُدَىٰ فَمَا رَبِحَت تِّجَرَتُهُمْ وَمَا كَانُوا۟ مُهْتَدِينَ ‎⟫
> *These are they who have purchased error for guidance, so their commerce was profitless. And they were not guided.*

They have bartered ignorance for knowledge, misguidance for guidance. The world is a place of commerce. Each profits according to his capital and talent, or loses through lack thereof. He who adheres to the good deeds Allah and His Messenger have enjoined, who cleaves to piety and righteousness, shall reap a profitable trade in the Hereafter, enjoying a blessed end after death. ⟪ar:'ربح'⟫ Profit means increase and abundance; a ⟪ar-quote:تجارت رابحۃ⟫ profitable trade is one in which blessing and excess flow.[^cite-16]

### The Power of the Human Soul

The human soul, being subtle, can attain heights beyond imagination. The dense body cannot reach even a fraction of this. ⟪ar:نبی علیہ السلام⟫ The Prophet, peace be upon him, through his ⟪ar:قوت روحانیہ و نفسانیہ⟫ spiritual and psychic power, has drawn the souls of all creation into connection with one another. Similarly, each scholar elevates the world's soul according to his knowledge's power. In the physical realm, a strong porter is deemed one who lifts weight equal to or double his own mass.

A narrator of prophetic hadith records that the Prophet said: "By way of reward, the believer and the pious shall receive in Paradise a palace whose breadth exceeds the breadth of the heavens and earth." The narrator was satisfied with the outward meaning, never considering the length of such a palace, or whether a solitary soul could endure such vastness. In truth, the Prophet described the magnitude of spiritual benefits in these words: the soul shall be freed from this body's bondage, roaming in boundless air, liberated from all constraint. The soul's condition then is like a fetus in this world's narrow womb. Upon emerging into a new world, it marvels at the vastness—the place wherein it dwelt for a season bears no relation to this realm.

### The Nature of Paradise

The Prophet declared in hadith: ⟪ar-quote:السَّعِیدُ مَن سَعَدَ فِی بَطنِ الأُمّ⟫ "Blessed is he who is blessed in the mother's womb"—meaning, he who grows in this world, the Hereafter's mercy, attaining perfection shall, by the Prophet's word, enjoy after death a soul freed from the physical realm, reveling in a vastness beyond imagination. No parable can explain it, no example suffice. This is why the Prophet said: ⟪ar-quote:ماَلاَ عَین رَأَت وَلاَ اُذن سَمِعَت وَلاَ خَطَر عَلَی قَلبِ بَشَر⟫ "Paradise is that which no eye has seen, no ear heard, no human heart conceived."[^cite-17]

He who gains this great profit through ⟪ar:اعمال صالحہ و تقوی⟫ righteous deeds and piety—who achieves such profit through ⟪ar:(بیعت)⟫ the covenant of selling his soul to Allah by ⟪ar:(امام علیہ السلام )⟫ the Imām—shall indeed realize a profitable trade. But he who deviates from obedience to the ⟪ar:اولو الأمر⟫ People of Authority, who grows not in perfection while dwelling in the ⟪ar:دار آخرت⟫ house—the ⟪ar:(امام کی دعوت)⟫ Imām's summons—becoming wretched and miserable, shall suffer a losing trade. True profit belongs only to those who sincerely obey Allah and His saints. To this trade Allah refers:

> ⟪quran 9:111⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ ٱشْتَرَىٰ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ أَنفُسَهُمْ وَأَمْوَلَهُم بِأَنَّ لَهُمُ ٱلْجَنَّةَ ۚ يُقَتِلُونَ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ فَيَقْتُلُونَ وَيُقْتَلُونَ ۖ وَعْدًا عَلَيْهِ حَقًّۭا فِى ٱلتَّوْرَىٰةِ وَٱلْإِنجِيلِ وَٱلْقُرْآنِ ۚ وَمَنْ أَوْفَىٰ بِعَهْدِهِۦ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ فَٱسْتَبْشِرُوا۟ بِبَيْعِكُمُ ٱلَّذِى بَايَعْتُم بِهِۦ ۚ وَذَلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Verily, Allah has purchased of the believers their lives and their properties; for the price that theirs shall be the Paradise. They fight in Allah's Cause, so they kill (others) and are killed. It is a promise in truth which is binding on Him in the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel) and the Quran. And who is truer to his covenant than Allah? Then rejoice in the bargain which you have concluded. That is the supreme success.*

This verse refers to the invitation of ⟪ar:اہل بیت محمد (ﷺ)⟫ the Household of Muhammad and the ⟪ar:ائمہ معصومین⟫ Infallible Imāms. It is they who possess this commerce covenant (unique to the Ismāʿīlī tradition), not other Islamic sects. When the invitation of the Infallible Imāms itself is the Hereafter's marketplace, what profit could surpass it?

Consider an analogy: when sperm is placed in the mother's womb, it absorbs the rays of sun, moon, and stars, strengthened by food and drink, growing daily. All bodily organs—the grasping hands, walking feet, seeing eyes, hearing ears—become complete and merged. A contemplative observer, witnessing this sperm's gradual transformation, must admit this perfection is supremely profitable for it. For when it emerges, it shall benefit fully from all these organs.

Similarly, the ⟪ar:نفس ناطقہ⟫ rational soul in the Dar (house) of sperm-stage for the Hereafter, when it receives the sun of the Nāṭiq (the Speaker), the moon of the Wasī (the Trustee), the supporting rays of stars (the Imāms)—transmitted from the world of intellect, increasing its spiritual power until reaching its limits—none can fathom the extent of its illumination! Thus the Prophet, praising this celestial paradise, described things therein as unseen and unheard, beyond human comprehension. It is such a tremendous, profitable trade that no worldly commerce can match it.[^cite-18]
