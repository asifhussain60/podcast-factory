## The Four Blessed Nights of Shahrullāh

On the four blessed nights of the city of Allah, special and exalted apertures (munāfiẕ) open—apertures distinct from the eighteen that unfold during the daily prayer hours. When the luminosity of the sun aligns with the luminosity of the heart, the radiance of the sun streams forth from these apertures, and the veils and barriers become saturated with divine light. In Laylat al-Qadr (the Night of Decree), for example, the veil of Fā becomes the aperture itself.

These are the veils (ḥijāb) of the Imāms—the veils of their cosmic station and the veils of their boundaries, which are about to merge with them. Additionally, the fermentations (khmīrah) of the Imām's grandfather and father both ascend and descend. Beyond this, revelations descend concerning the veils and boundaries of those companions (Sāḥib Sarādīp and his entourage) who were born from the pit with him.[^cite-1]

**Four Fermentations on the Blessed Nights**

- Hijāb al-ʿAyn fermentations · Fermentation of the Sacred Heart · Boundaries of the Khimārah Established · Fermentations blocking the vanguard
- Fermentations and vanguard obstruction · Khimārah of the Companions · Fermentation of the Islands

On these nights, the summaries of their verses ascend and descend, and their radiance is illuminated in these nights. The night of Laylat al-Qadr is one of the four nights in which the summaries of these stations are unveiled. All these arguments unfold by the command of the Almighty. In spiritual governance, these arguments bear great weight in the ascent of luminous forms and in the descent of darkened forms.

Mawlānā ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him) has said that one should pray for the Ḥajj on the 17th, 19th, and 21st nights, because the names of those who proceed to the pilgrimage are inscribed on those nights.[^cite-2] The three chapters of these three nights form a continuous series, and the example of Laylat al-Qadr is the great proof (ḥujja) of ʿAyn.

## The Seventeenth Night of Shahrullāh

The seventeenth night of the city of Allah corresponds to the seventeenth Imām, ⟪ar:'مولانا علی الظاہر لاعزاز دین اللہ علیہ السلام'⟫. He attained to this rank at thirty-two years of age, yet at so young an age he accomplished great works. The seventeenth night exemplifies his great proof (ḥujja), ⟪ar:'سیدنا حمید الدین کرمانی'⟫, who gathers all the doctrinal expositions of his epoch.

|---|---|

Mawlānā Muḥammad Bāqir (peace be upon him) said: "On the seventeenth night, two great assemblies convene."[^cite-3] That is, on this night, the trumpets and summonses of the other (the preserved ones—⟪ar:مستقرین⟫) descend through the vertical light and enter the spiritual heart (the Imāmate) and the physical heart (the Kaʿba). All this occurs so that things may converge, so that one greater than them—the ⟪ar:قائم القیامۃ⟫ (the Establisher of the Resurrection)—may appear. Moreover, the meeting of the two assemblies signifies that on that night, Mīm handed over certain of his substitutes to ʿAyn.

## The Nineteenth Night of Shahrullāh

On this night, the names of the year-round delegations and assemblies who proceed to meet Allah are inscribed, and therein are written the decree concerning sustenance and lifespan. On this night, the number of spiritual forms is apportioned, and it is determined which noble form shall be granted how many descents. Additionally, on ⟪ar:ھیکل أمامی⟫ this night, the sequence of forms is established according to their hierarchy—that is, the Imām (peace be upon him) arranges ⟪ar:تصریف الصور والترتیبھا فی الھیکل⟫ the forms in his temple. Furthermore, the order is also fixed in ⟪ar:ریحیات⟫ the nasūt (corporeal dimension). The entire year refers to the temple of Imām al-Zamān (the Imām of the Age).[^cite-4]

The nineteenth night remains the heart of the twenty-third night; that is, the exemplars of the nineteenth night are becoming the exemplars of the twenty-third night in development, such that the nineteenth progressively culminates in the twenty-third.

## The Twenty-First Night of Shahrullāh

On the twenty-first night, the prophets pass away through the veil of their executor. A Nāṭiq (Speaker, or Prophetic Messenger) always passes away through the veil of his waṣī (executor, or legatee); for this reason, the death of the waṣī always precedes that of the Nāṭiq, just as Hārūn died before Mūsā. That is, the Nāṭiq selects one of the veils of his waṣī (identifies with it) and commands him to die. In other words, when Hārūn died, he in fact died in the form of Hārūn. Likewise, on that night ʿAyn died before Mīm, and Jesus died through the veil of his will.[^cite-5]

According to the inner meanings, the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) is the Jesus and Moses of his time, because both had dealings with him. The Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) also appeared on the twenty-first night in the form of ʿAlī, and he it was who elevated Jesus and Moses on that night.

In one sense, they are the exemplars of this night in ⟪ar:مولانا سلمان فارسی⟫ Mawlānā Salmān al-Fārisī, and in another sense, the twenty-first Imāmate is exemplified by the ⟪ar:باب الابواب مولا تنا سلمیٰ⟫ Gate of Gates, Mawlānā Salmā, and ⟪ar:امام ہاشم علیہ السلام⟫ Imām Hāshim (peace be upon him).

## The Twenty-Third Night of Shahrullāh

There are four auspicious nights of the city of Allah, signified by the four letters of "Laylā" (night). Among these four, the night of al-Qadr is the twenty-third night of the city of Allah—the most blessed and the best of them all. All the blessings that descend individually on the seventeenth, nineteenth, and twenty-first nights are the collective radiance of all of them on this night. Apart from this, Laylat al-Qadr possesses its own unique blessings, which also illumine on this night. Because of this singularity, the blessings of this night are greatly magnified.[^cite-6]

| L | E | L | Al-Qadr |
|---|---|---|---|
| 17 | 19 | 21 | 23 |

It has been narrated that on Laylat al-Qadr, all creatures—animals, trees, and minerals—prostrate and bow before Allah. Its inner meaning is that one of the descendants of ʿAlī will be such that he shall be revealed to the religion and shall obey the thirty elders who are the symbols of the thirty days of Allah's month.

In the wider sphere, Fāṭima al-Zahrā is the symbol of this night, and in the inner circle, the twenty-third Imām, Mawlānā Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad ibn al-Ṭayyib (peace be upon him), is the symbol of this night.

Mawlānā Fāṭima bint Muḥammad (may peace be upon him) received her portion of the divine wisdom from her grandfather Mawlānā ʿAbdullāh (peace be upon him) and from her great-grandfather Mawlānā ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib (peace be upon him). Through Mawlānā ʿAbdullāh (peace be upon him), she received certain things of *Fatḥ* (Conquest—from Mawlānā Abū Ṭālib), and certain things of *Jād* (Bounty) from Mawlānā ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib (peace be upon him). That is, some of the formulations that Mawlānā ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib prepared, but not through the summons of Imāmate (peace be upon him)—some of these were bequeathed to Mawlānā Fāṭima by her forebears. In like manner, she received certain greetings of the summons of victory from Mawlānā ʿAbdullāh (peace be upon him) on that night. This is found nowhere else in these times except in Mawlānā Fāṭima, and for this reason she is truly the night of al-Qadr. And this transpired when she attained the special station of Nāsūt (corporeal reality) and became the Veiled One, Aḥmad (at the rank of the Great Proof—Ḥujjat al-ʿAẓmiyya).

In the circle of Imāmate, on Laylat al-Qadr, the Veils of Muḥammadan Reality (the veil of the Prophet—may peace and blessings be upon him—and the treasures and glorious realities that are gathered with Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad, the twenty-third Imām) ascend and descend on this night. That is why on this night, Mawlānā takes recourse (tawassul) through Fāṭima and Mawlānā Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad.

Since Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad (peace be upon him) is twenty-third in the sequence, he was named "Muḥammad" because he is the veil of the Prophet and he it is who carries and administers the Treasures of Corporeal Reality (the treasures of Nāsūt belonging to Fā and Mīm). He has become the chapter encompassing both of them—Fā and Mīm. On this night, Mawlānā Fāṭima shines upon Mawlānā Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad.

**Facts**

They ascended and descended in Laylat al-Qadr while their faces illumined upon them. Laylat al-Qadr is no matter of waste.

In another sense, in Laylat al-Qadr, "Laylā" refers to Mawlānā Fāṭima bint Muḥammad (may peace be upon him), and "al-Qadr" refers to Mawlānā ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib. Mawlānā Fāṭima is the great proof of Mawlānā ʿAlī. She is your heiress and your proof (⟪ar:ھی وارث ابیھا و حجت بالھا⟫ "She is the heir of her father and the proof of her Maulā"). In the realm of Imāmate, the night in Laylat al-Qadr refers to the twenty-third Imām.

## The Four Great Proofs (Ḥujaj) of the ʿAyn ʿAẓīma

The ascent and descent of the fermentations and shrines of the Imāms (peace be upon them) takes place in the city of Allah, and the ascent and descent of their descendants is on the Day of Ghadir.

**ʿAyn ʿAẓīma**

The entire month of the city of Allah is marked by the Great One. In certain respects, her Layali al-Fāḍila (the Nights of Excellence) resemble the great proofs of certain stations of ʿAyn. The day is superior to the night, because the day belongs to the Imām (peace be upon him) and the night to his proof. The Imām-possessor is both Nāsūt and Lāhūt, whereas his proof partakes only of the Imāmate's Nāsūt.

**Explanation**

In the obligatory prayer, during the rak'ahs before the tashahhud, one recites the second sūra alongside Sūrat al-Fātiḥa, which signifies both Lāhūt and Nāsūt; after the tashahhud, one recites only Sūrat al-Fātiḥa, which signifies Nāsūt alone.

### Mawlātnā Fāṭima bint Asad (Seventeenth Night)

The symbol of this night is Mawlānā ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib's mother, Fāṭima bint Asad, from whose heart ʿAlī's heart was hewn.

**Facts**

The emergence of the Gate of the Emanators occurs during the epoch of the most exalted Imāms or for the three ambassadors.

She entrusted the gathering of her husband's testament to ʿAlī and departed. Mawlānā Abū Ṭālib (peace be upon him) gave this gathering to the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) through Mawlānā Fāṭima bint Asad, and she became the heart of the gathering, so that the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) could give it to ʿAlī when the time came. In this way, Mawlātnā Fāṭima bint Asad was indeed ʿAlī's mother, but because of this she also became the mother of the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him). The Messenger of Allah (may peace and blessings be upon him) disseminated Islam so that the summons of faith could commence, and on the Day of Ghadir the gathering was given over to ʿAlī. Because of the fulfillment of this trust by the Holy Prophet, her rank became so exalted that all the Imāms bow before her.

### Mawlātnā Khadīja al-Kubrā (Nineteenth Night)

The exemplar of the nineteenth night is Mawlātnā Khadīja al-Kubrā, who is the chapter of the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) and became the heart of Mawlātnā Fāṭima al-Zahrā. In the first respect, she was like Maryam to ʿAlī (the son of Maryam). In another sense, Maryam is the Messenger of Allah, and the son of Maryam is ʿAlī. The Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) handed over the gathering to Mawlātnā Fāṭima. Among the elect, the name of Mawlātnā Khadīja is Khuzayma.

### Mawlānā Salmān al-Fārisī (Twenty-First Night)

The exemplar of the twenty-first night is Mawlānā Salmān al-Fārisī, who is the chapter of Mawlānā ʿAlī and became the heart of Imām Ḥusayn (peace be upon him). In principle, Mawlātnā Fāṭima al-Zahrā should have been the heart of Ḥusayn. But she rose above her own rank and established Mawlānā Salmān al-Fārisī in her position. From that point onward, Mawlānā Salmān al-Fārisī became the spiritual mother of Imām Ḥusayn (peace be upon him). In like manner, Mawlānā Ubayy ibn Kaʿb chose Mawlānā Ubayy ibn Kaʿb as the Gate for al-Ḥasan (peace be upon him), and he became the heart of Imām al-Ḥasan (peace be upon him).

The exemplar of the twenty-third night is Mawlātnā Fāṭima bint Muḥammad (peace be upon him), who is the proof of Mīm (Fāṭima is your heiress and your proof) and is superior to the preceding three proofs.

## The Tadbīrāt (Administrations) of the Four Supreme Ḥujjas

All four proofs of ʿAyn ʿAẓīma have been given the administration of one night. Mawlātnā Fāṭima bint Asad of the seventeenth, Mawlātnā Khadīja of the nineteenth, Mawlānā Salmān al-Fārisī of the twenty-first, and Mawlātnā Fāṭima al-Zahrā of the twenty-third. During these nights, these beings carry out the descent of forms, the transformation of structure, and the transference from one temple to another.

Apart from this, the fermentations of these four proofs also ascend and descend on these nights. That is, they are administering the affairs of the Imāms and their own affairs on these nights.

On these nights, the affairs of Sayyidnā Ḥamīd al-Dīn al-Kirmānī (seventeenth), Sayyidnā Muʾayyad (nineteenth), and Mawlānā Abū al-Barakāt (twenty-first) also ascend and descend. Some things are given and some things are received.[^cite-7]

Mawlānā Abū al-Barakāt was the chapter of Mawlānā ʿAlī al-Ṭayyib. He was entrusted with the responsibility of bringing Mawlānā ʿAlī al-Ṭayyib out from the islands and presenting him to the elect.

### Asking Regarding the Ḥajj

These four proofs are not only of their time but of all times and epochs. To ask regarding the Ḥajj is to wish to be included in the grand temple, which is far superior to the temple of Imāmate. Mawlātnā Fāṭima al-Zahrā has become the mistress of all these temples and is the one who distributes the command of the Imāms of these verses. That is, they are administering the gathering to which the Imām is to be elevated. In truth, the Imāms ask regarding the Ḥajj because if the Imāms gather in this gathering, we too shall gather, inasmuch as our form has merged with theirs.

The Imām possesses many verses, yet he does not retain them. They come for a brief period, and then, because of their imperfections, they depart through sweat, sneezing, and such means. That is why we pray that our station should be fixed with the Imām.

## The Special Nāsūt of ʿAlī and Fāṭima

In truth, the attainment of special birth is called the perfection of nāsūt (corporeal being). Mawlātnā Fāṭima was born on the twentieth of Jumādā al-Awwal. There was a span of thirty-five years between her and the Messenger of Allah, and the difference between the Messenger of Allah and Mawlānā ʿAlī is thirty years. Mawlānā ʿAlī was born ten years before the Prophet's receipt of prophethood, and Mawlātnā Fāṭima was born five years before the Prophet's receipt of the mission.[^cite-8]

She was born on Laylat al-Qadr when she was blessed with a special birth. The evidence for this is that Mawlānā ʿAlī's common nāsūt (tābīʿ) was born on the twenty-seventh of Rajab al-Murajjab, and his special nāsūt (khāṣṣ) was born on the eighteenth of Dhū al-Ḥijja—the Day of Ghadir—when the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) entrusted him to the divine stations. There is also a difference of five between the numbers eighteen and twenty-three.

| — | Mawlātnā Fāṭima al-Zahrā | Mawlānā ʿAlī |
|---|---|---|
| Common Nāsūt (tābīʿ) | Twentieth of Jumādā al-Awwal | Twenty-seventh of Rajab al-Murajjab |
| Special Nāsūt (khāṣṣ) | Laylat al-Qadr | Day of Ghadir |

On the night of Laylat al-Qadr, Jad (Bounty) and Fath (Conquest) handed over certain formulations to Mawlātnā Fāṭima, and on the Day of Ghadir, the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) handed over certain formulations to Mawlānā ʿAlī. The meaning of the divine stations is that the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) elevated the great one from the eternal ranks to the divine ranks. With this, he became one of the Administrators and entered the circles of the Divinely Compelled.

Sayyidnā Ḥusayn ibn Jadeyn stated in his guidance: "When ʿAlī had received all the Prophet's Companions, he prayed to the Prophet—which is the Greatest." At that time, ʿAyn ʿAẓīma turned his attention to the Station of Light (ʿAlī), and the Veil of ʿAlī prayed to the Divine—a Divine that is about to unite with you in addition to this special nāsūt. This nāsūt was a medium between your nāsūt and your lāhūt. And your divinity is between you and your residence.

And you did not attain special privilege until the Messenger of Allah (may peace and blessings be upon him) had completed his service. The day on which his service was completed was the Day of Ghadir. If Mawlānā ʿAlī had attained the special nāsūt during the life of the Holy Prophet, he would have been elevated to the rank of the Holy Prophet. For this reason, ʿAyn ʿAẓīma sent his lowest veil to the world—whose name was ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib—so that he could bow to the veil of Mīm (the middle veil).

When lāhūt is obsessed with special nāsūt, at the same time the union of the special nāsūt is with the special nāsūt. And this is done on the days of Layali al-Fāḍila and Ayyām al-Fāḍila (the Nights and Days of Excellence). Because of these realities, they become renewed day and night.

Quoting Sayyidnā Ḥusayn ibn Jadeyn, the author says that not only on Laylat al-Qadr and Youm-e-Ghadir (Day of Ghadir), but also on different blessed days and nights—in the observance of divinity—they have united with the divine stations. May the glory of these stations be greater than these days and nights. Every Imām has a special blessing, and he receives it on special days and nights.

Mawlātnā Fāṭima appeared in the form of a woman because she had a dhikr (commemoration—ʿAlī) before her. Otherwise, they are not truly women, for "man" and "woman" are names belonging to the body (the physical form). They possess not only the state of impurity, but the state of purity, which is formed by life. The spouse is called a spouse in the terminology of the summons. Since Mawlānā ʿAlī Malik is eternal, his spouse had to wear the garment of femininity.

## The Succession of the Muḥammadan Cupola (Qubba Muḥammadiyya)

On the Day of Ghadir, when the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) handed over the trusts to ʿAlī, he withdrew and vacated his place and ⟪ar:خلاص العالم⟫ died. ʿAlī succeeded him and adopted the veil of Mīm (that is, ʿAlī became Mīm and Mīm became ʿAlī). There is an interval of ninety-two days from Ghadir to the twelfth of Rabīʿ al-Awwal, which equals the numerical value of "Muḥammad." Although the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) departed, ʿAlī in truth died. After that, the ʿAlī who remained for the subsequent thirty to forty years was in fact the Prophet's veil, working.

⟪ar:استخلاف قبۂ محمدیۃ⟫ It signifies that ʿAlī attained the caliphate of Muḥammad (entered into ⟪ar:گمبد⟫ the cupola). At that moment, the veils of Muḥammad and ʿAlī died. In place of ʿAyn came the lower veil of Mīm, and in place of Mīm came the veil of ʿAyn. The people who offered congratulations to ʿAlī at that time were in truth offering them to the Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him). The reason is that Allah willed the Prophet to be superior to ʿAlī in all respects because of his immense services.[^cite-9]

## Mawlātnā Fāṭima as Majmaʿ al-Awṣiyāʾ — The Convergence of the Executors

Mawlānā ʿAlī's proof is Mawlātnā Fāṭima al-Zahrā, while the proof of ʿAyn ʿAẓīma is Maryam. The gathering of all the preserved ones (al-Mustawadʿūn) assembled before the Holy Prophet. A gathering of all the Imāms assembled before Mawlānā ʿAlī. In like manner, a congregation of all nine executors (awṣiyāʾ) assembled at Mawlātnā Fāṭima.[^cite-10]

| Adam | Noah | Ibrāhīm | Mūsā | Jesus | Muḥammad |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Abel, Shīth | Sam | Isḥāq, Ismāʿīl | Hārūn | Shamuʾun | Ubayy ibn Kaʿb, Salmān al-Fārisī |

Mawlānā ʿAlī was the executor of the Messenger of Allah. Yet the Messenger of Allah (may peace and blessings be upon him) rose to the rank of his prophethood and prophecy and gave the executorship to Ubayy ibn Kaʿb and Mawlānā Salmān al-Fārisī. In truth, the Messenger of Allah (may peace and blessings be upon him) had three distinct offices:

| The Structure of Sharīʿa | The Temple of Taʾwīl (Esoteric Exegesis) | The Realities (al-Ḥaqāʾiq) |
|---|---|---|
| Ubayy ibn Kaʿb | Salmān al-Fārisī | ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib |

Mawlātnā Fāṭima gathered with them through the Messenger of Allah. In this sense, Ubayy ibn Kaʿb and Salmān al-Fārisī are called the executors of Muḥammad.

## Mawlātnā Fāṭima as Laylat al-Mubāraka — The Blessed Night

Allah says in Sūrat al-Dukhān:

> ⟪quran 44:1-2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏حمٓ ‎ ‏وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلْمُبِينِ ‎⟫
> *Ha-Mîm. [These letters are one of the miracles of the Quran and none but Allah (Alone) knows their meanings]. By the manifest Book (this Quran) that makes things clear,*
> I swear by the Illuminating Book.
> — **Quran 44:1-2 (inline widget)**

Ḥā-Mīm refers to Imām al-Ḥasan (peace be upon him), and the Book of Mubīn refers to Imām al-Ḥusayn (peace be upon him). In a second sense, these two verses refer to every station that returns to its place of origin.

| Ḥā-Mīm | Al-Kitāb al-Mubīn |
|---|---|
| Imām al-Ḥasan (peace be upon him) | Imām al-Ḥusayn (peace be upon him) |
| Station of the Preserved | The Station of the Established |

> ⟪quran 44:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَهُ فِى لَيْلَةٍۢ مُّبَرَكَةٍ ۚ إِنَّا كُنَّا مُنذِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *We sent it (this Quran) down on a blessed night [(i.e. night of Qadr, Surah No: 97) in the month of Ramadan,, the 9th month of the Islamic calendar]. Verily, We are ever warning [mankind that Our Torment will reach those who disbelieve in Our Oneness of Lordship and in Our Oneness of worship].*
> We sent it down on a blessed night. We had intended to warn
> — **Quran 44:3 (inline widget)**

In the first two verses, Allah speaks of two persons, but in this verse He speaks of the revelation as singular. In this verse, *Anzalnāh* is employed, not *Anzalnāhumā*. The reason is that Imām al-Ḥasan turned toward Imām al-Ḥusayn, and the two became one verse. Just as on the Day of Ghadir, Mīm returns to ʿAyn, and the Mahdī returns to the Qāʾim (the Establisher). That is, the predecessor surrenders his precedence to his superior. The name of this surrender is al-Islām (submission).

When al-Ḥasan turned toward al-Ḥusayn, the four ranks were gathered with al-Ḥusayn. Imām al-Ḥasan and Imām al-Ḥusayn (peace be upon them) have been mentioned in Laylat al-Mubāraka as a single verse. Laylat al-Mubāraka refers to Mawlātnā Fāṭima al-Zahrā, from whose summons Mawlānā Imām al-Ḥasan and Mawlānā Imām al-Ḥusayn (peace be upon them) appeared. Both are the fruits of the feast of Mawlātnā Fāṭima. For this reason, al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn united and turned toward Mawlātnā Fāṭima, because each Imām progresses within his father's horizon. On that night, the Messengers and Warners are the Greatest. He made both of them one and brought them near to Mawlātnā Fāṭima.

**Facts**

Mawlānā Ubayy ibn Kaʿb was the heart of Imām al-Ḥasan, and Mawlānā Salmān al-Fārisī was the heart of Imām al-Ḥusayn. Imām al-Ḥasan merged with Imām al-Ḥusayn through Mawlānā Ubayy ibn Kaʿb. Imām al-Ḥusayn (peace be upon him) merged with Mawlātnā Fāṭima through Salmān al-Fārisī.

> ⟪quran 44:4⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فِيهَا يُفْرَقُ كُلُّ أَمْرٍ حَكِيمٍ ‎⟫
> *Therein (that night) is decreed every matter of ordainments .*
> All matters which are based on wisdom are settled in that night.
> — **Quran 44:4 (inline widget)**

Laylat al-Qadr is the night of the texts, for Mawlātnā Fāṭima is the proof of the Great Imām who is the best support of every Imām. There are transformations on this night. You recite the text at every station on Laylat al-Qadr and you pray for them. On the night of Laylat al-Qadr, Mawlātnā Fāṭima was completely lifted by the Creator (Fāṭir—peace be upon him), and on that night she was given the rank of the Great Proof. The Creator's support is received by all the Imāms through Mawlātnā Fāṭima. On this night, you administer all matters of wisdom in the mental and corporeal worlds. This is the reality of Laylat al-Qadr. The Creator (peace be upon him) is being commanded by ʿAyn to bring down things through his veil, Mawlātnā Fāṭima, because she is the Great Proof and the Essence of Mercy (ʿayn al-Raḥma) whom we dare not name.

Because of the perfection of her rank as proof, Mawlātnā Fāṭima possessed the four ranks (appointed and intellect, or preserved and established). The Imāmate and prophecy came to her from Mawlānā ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib (Jad—Bounty) and Mawlānā Abū Ṭālib (Fath—Conquest), and the matters of prophethood and messengership came to her from Mawlānā ʿAbdullāh and Aḥmad (peace be upon him). She possessed these gatherings because she had to preserve the covenant (the order of the Imāms and their sequence) until the Day of Judgment. For this purpose, Mawlātnā Fāṭima was chosen. And this is the wisdom of the manifest and the virtue of the outward. And the perfection of her proof and the proclamation of the summons begin with her and end with her. She began to proclaim the Fāṭimid summons through the Imāms.

That is, though she has risen above the ranks of prophethood, prophecy, Imāmate, and executorship, she still attained these four levels so that the perfection of her proof could be achieved. She is in truth the ancient essence of prophethood and prophecy (al-Ḥasan) and Imāmate and executorship (al-Ḥusayn). These two ranks belonged to her, and therefore they returned to her. All prophets and messengers were manifest because of her. You are both from the ancient essence of the hierarchy.

From the time of Adam, the congregation of the seventy assembled before Mawlānā ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib. Then it was divided during the epoch of ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib and came to Mawlānā Abū Ṭālib and Mawlānā ʿAbdullāh. Mawlātnā Fāṭima became the essence of these four gatherings. She collected all the ancient hierarchies so they could be distributed among the Imāms. When all four ranks were gathered before her, she again proclaimed her summons through Mawlānā ʿAlī Zayn al-ʿĀbidīn and handed over all four ranks to him. Because of the blessings of Mawlātnā Fāṭima, these hierarchies will not be divided until the Day of Judgment.

## Laylat al-Qadr al-Kubrā — The Great Night of Decree

Mawlātnā Fāṭima bint Muḥammad (may peace be upon him) is called Laylat al-Qadr, and Mawlātnā Fāṭima bint al-Mahdī (may peace be upon him) is called Laylat al-Qadr al-Kubrā (the Great Night of Decree).[^cite-11]

On Laylat al-Qadr al-Kubrā (the manifest Fā), the fermentations and veils of the Fāṭimids and their congregation will ascend and descend. When the Fā proceeds to the Hereafter, the treasures of the Fā will be ascended.

**Facts**

Laylat al-Qadr deals with the treasures of the veil of Fā, while in Laylat al-Qadr al-Kubrā there are matters of the treasures of Fā itself and the treasures and remnants of those who have been removed from her congregation.

On this night, the Hereafter will be the guardian of all affairs. She will become a veiled one on the occasion and will be honored with Mīm and ʿAyn. Similarly, Mawlānā Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad ibn ʿAlī al-Ṭayyib (the twenty-third Imām) will illuminate the believers through the author of the ʿAsr (the Age).[^cite-12]

The Prophet (may peace and blessings be upon him) said: "Seek Laylat al-Qadr on the twenty-third night." On these nights, such exalted fruits bloom that do not reopen throughout the year. Eighteen apertures open in a day, of which seventeen open during the prayers and the eighteenth is perpetually open. The apertures that open during the four auspicious nights of the city of Allah are in addition to the eighteen that bloom and illuminate during these four nights of the year. When the luminosity of the heart (Tamūr) aligns with the luminosity of the sun (comes into a line), they illuminate. At this moment, their faces shine upon their foreheads and they offer them. These situations are illuminated by the Imām at the moment. And when the believers pray on these nights, they illuminate upon the believers through the Imām and his veils. We receive some things from them, and some of our things are taken from us.

### Fā in the Hereafter (Mawlātnā Fāṭima bint al-Mahdī—peace be upon him)

Laylat al-Qadr al-Kubrā is the essence of the forthcoming Great Fā, who will be the spouse of al-Qāʾim al-Muntaẓar (the Awaited Establisher—ʿAlī, the remembrance of Islam). At that time, the summaries of the Fāṭimid treasures and their veils, and the virtues that will be included in the gathering of the Hereafter, will be revealed. The forms that are in Tamūr will comment upon these treasures, remnants, and wastes. In the Hereafter, she will become a veiled one for the Imām of the Age and will be Mīm and Mīm upon him. She will be the custodian of all the affairs of this Laylat al-Qadr al-Kubrā. That is, all these matters will be in her hands to determine what is to be brought down and what is to be offered.

Similarly, Mawlānā Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad ibn ʿAlī al-Ṭayyib (the twenty-third Imām) will illuminate the believers through the author of the ʿAsr (the Age).

Every year, installments are being collected on Laylat al-Qadr. When all these episodes of epochs and periods are combined, Laylat al-Qadr al-Kubrā will appear.
