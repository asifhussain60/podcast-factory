And after death, only good or bad deeds are left of man, so man should do as many good deeds as he can. And when a person is buried in the grave and suffers the punishment of the grave, then he regrets his evil deeds immensely, that he wishes he had not done such deeds in this world or that he had done more good deeds.

| There are three castles! A mosque is a fortress, the remembrance of Allah is a fortress and the recitation of the Qur'an is a fortress.

A fort is a place where a person protects himself from the enemy. The narrator is explaining that if a person wants to protect himself from the devil, there are three ways for him. The first is that he should engage himself in worship, and the second is that he should constantly remember Allah and remember Him, because the remembrance of Allah highlights the strengths of the human soul and weakens the forces of Satan. Thirdly, one should read the Qur'an with understanding, as ⟪ar:مولانا علی زین العابدین علیہ السلام⟫it is said: "When you recite the verses of mercy, your heart longs for the blessings of Allah, and when you recite the verses of punishment, your body trembles with fear of Allah." That is, a person should keep himself busy with these three tasks at all times. Either he should engage in worship, or remember Allah, or recite the Qur'an, or meditate on His commands and commands.

| There are four things that accomplish four things. The completion of prayer is through prostration, the completion of fasting is through the observance of the fitrah, the completion of Hajj is from the animal of sacrifice, and the completion of faith is through jihad.

This saying shows that until the last action is completed, the rewards of the first action remain suspended. Just as if a person does all the hard work of prayer but does not prostrate, it is as if he has not prayed at all. If he fasts but does not perform fitrah, it is as if his thirty days' hard work has been wasted. If a person undergoes all the hard work of Hajj but does not slaughter an animal, then his Hajj is in vain. In the same way, the fulfillment of faith is through constant struggle. That is why the end of the faith is so important. If a person works hard all his life but it does not end with the wilayat of Ali and his descendants, then his life's hard work is wasted.

### Anger:

The first thing is the intellect, which gave man the nobility to ⟪ar:اشرف المخلوقات⟫be called. Allah is the Creator of all things and His creation. There are many noble things in these creatures, but in all of them, man has ⟪ar:’اشرف المخلوقات‘⟫ been given the title of Allah, that is, the noblest of all the noble things that have been created, and this nobility has been acquired by man because of the intellect through which he makes decisions. For this reason, man is the only creature for whom Paradise and Hell have been created, because he has the power to accept the good and abandon the evil, or to abandon the good and accept the evil.  Even among all human beings, in the true sense of the word, the noblest of the people are those who have been given the wealth of the noble era, which is related to the intellect. These are the people whose righteous deeds do not remain in the form of a single point, but gradually it has been transformed into a Sharif form. It is not a matter of honor to have wisdom alone, but to use it is the real nobility. In the same way, it is not just a matter of nobility to take the pledge, but it is a matter of true nobility to use it in one's beliefs, in one's intentions, in one's pursuit of knowledge, and in one's actions.  The second essence is his religion, which makes him distinguish between right and wrong. Religion is the sphere in which one's intellect works. The third essence is his modesty, and the fourth is his good deed.

Anger and anger destroy a person's intellect. Only Allah has the right to be angry with His servants because of their evil deeds. Anger impairs memory and memory. Amir al-Mu'minin says in one place, " ⟪ar-quote:الغضب، أولہ الجنون و آخرہ الندم۔ ولم یندم فھو مجنون⟫ The beginning of wrath is madness and madness, and its end is regret." And he who does not regret in the end and stays in his interpretation is indeed insane). The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) said: "The best of you is the one who is angry and is pleased quickly, and the worst of you is the one who gets angry and is late in the state of contentment." When Moses got angry and stabbed someone and killed him, he immediately said, "This is one of the deeds of Satan, who is an open enemy."

### Envy:

And jealousy destroys a person's religion. The purpose of religion is self-improvement. It is a tool by which man carves his world and the Hereafter. But when a person becomes jealous of someone, instead of seeking his own good, he is attracted to the destruction of others and he does not worry about his own loss. Just as Qabil was jealous of Abel and killed him, although killing did not benefit him, but he committed a great sin and ruined his Hereafter.

### Greed:

And greed and greed destroy a person's modesty and shame and man becomes shameless. For example, it is a matter of shame and modesty that a person should not stretch out his hands before anyone other than Allah. But when he is greedy for something, then he becomes shameless and stretches out his hand in front of people and does not care that his respect is losing among the people. Its purpose is only to satisfy its own greed. And it's a very sinister situation.

### Occultation:

And backbiting destroys a person's good deeds and eats away at his virtues. There are always two people involved in backbiting, because the language of the backbiter always needs an ear. Therefore, the one who hears backbiting is an equal partner in the evil deeds of the one who backbites, and he is the one who wastes his good deeds.

Once, when the Prophet (s) was in the chamber of Aisha, a woman came to him from far away to ask him some questions about knowledge and jurisprudence. When she was gone, Aisha said to her, "O Messenger of Allah, how black and fat this woman was, and how ugly and ugly her voice was, and how strange her gait was!"
