What the Podcast Agent Actually Does

The podcast agent turns dense, scholarly books — many of them in classical Arabic — into high-fidelity audio podcasts via Google NotebookLM. This page is the executive tour: what the agent is capable of today, how content moves through the pipeline, where humans stay in the loop, and where the work is heading next.

Thirteen capabilities, grouped

Click any capability to see what it does, who it serves, and what could go wrong if it were absent.

① Source ingestion Ingestion

Pulls a book PDF through Azure Document Intelligence to produce searchable, structured text. Handles scanned documents, multi-column layouts, and footnotes.

Why it matters: classical books are almost always scans; without accurate OCR, every downstream step is corrupted.

② Chunking Ingestion

Splits long OCR output into overlapping ~10,000-word windows so each LLM call stays inside its context budget. Windows checkpoint to disk so any single window can be re-run without restarting the book.

③ English refinement Refinement

Cleans up OCR artifacts, modernises archaic grammar, restores paragraph flow, and produces a passage that reads as English prose rather than translation. Preserves quoted Arabic verbatim.

④ Phonetic transliteration Refinement

Generates phonetic spellings for every Arabic name, term, or citation so NotebookLM's synthetic voices pronounce them correctly. Uses a curated Arabic shared-reference set (six files) for consistency across books.

⑤ Numeric & symbolic disambiguation Quality

Detects numeric or symbolic claims that risk being flattened by an LLM — abjad-numeral ciphers, anachronisms, fabricated enumerations. Routes uncertain cases to a human review register rather than guessing.

Severity ladder: P0 (invented enumeration ⇒ blocked), P1 (missing abjad coverage), P2 (anachronism nuance). The challenger's Loop N enforces this gate before any ship verdict.

⑥ Chapter design & contracts Authoring

Carves the refined corpus into discrete chapters, each with a one-page "contract" that fixes the chapter's title, scope, characters, and key claims. Contracts let downstream steps proceed in parallel without drifting.

⑦ Enrichment Authoring

Adds narrative depth, scene-setting, and connective tissue suitable for an audio format. Constrained by the chapter contract — enrichment cannot invent characters or claims outside the source.

⑧ Episode framing & source architecture Authoring

Wraps each chapter with NotebookLM-friendly structure: host prompts, episode arc tables, listener-objection cues, segment headers, "do-not-say" guardrails. This is the single biggest lever we have on the final audio — see the "Steering NotebookLM" tab.

Why it matters: NotebookLM's voices and conversational style are fixed. We cannot pick a host, accent, or cadence. What we can influence is the kind of conversation those fixed hosts have — by giving them podcast-intelligent source architecture instead of raw prose.

⑨ Bundle building Delivery

Concatenates refined English, framing, and pronunciation hints into a single plain-text bundle per episode. This is the artifact a human uploads to NotebookLM.

⑩ Automated quality challenger Quality

A separate LLM agent audits each chapter against six categories: source fidelity, numeric integrity, boundary integrity, async safety, prose quality, and brand voice. Findings carry severity (P0/P1/P2). P0 findings block ship.

⑪ Cost ledger & caps Operations

Every model call appends a row to a cost ledger (tokens, model, USD). Soft warning at $20; hard halt before the final phase at $50. Per-book budget overrides available.

⑫ Heartbeat & live status Operations

A daemon thread writes a heartbeat every 30 seconds. A local browser dashboard shows phase progress, current chunk, elapsed time, and cost — refreshed via server-sent events.

⑬ Resume & rollback Operations

State is captured per phase per chunk. A killed process resumes cleanly; a bad phase can be retried in isolation; nothing is silently overwritten.

What the agent deliberately does not do

It does not publish audio, schedule episodes, manage social media, or interact with any service other than the LLM and Azure OCR. NotebookLM upload is performed by a human who reviews the bundle first. This is intentional containment — fewer moving parts, fewer ways to silently corrupt a book.

What NotebookLM controls vs. what we control

NotebookLM's hosts, voices, accents, cadence, humor, interruptions, and music are fixed — chosen by Google's internal model, not by us. The lever that remains is the source bundle we hand it. The better that bundle is engineered, the more the generated podcast feels custom.

The single most important sentence on this page

Our leverage is not voice customization. It is structuring the sources so the AI knows what kind of conversation it is supposed to generate.

Ten source-design levers

The bundles we hand NotebookLM should exercise every one of these. Skipping a lever means leaving NotebookLM to default to "enhanced audiobook" output.

1

Explicit host prompts

Tell the hosts what to say first, what to push back on, and where to slow down.

2

Episode arc tables

A small table of beats per episode — hook, escalation, climax, resolution.

3

"What listeners may misunderstand"

Pre-empt the predictable mistakes. The hosts will course-correct in dialogue.

4

Listener objection blocks

Stage real objections so the hosts can address them, not skip past them.

5

Debate framing

Mark sections where the hosts should disagree — produces tension and texture.

6

Segment headers

Clear, named segments produce clear transitions instead of run-on monologue.

7

Source-card metadata

What is this source, when, where, why does it matter — surfaced as a card.

8

Clear editorial separation

Keep author claims, narrator framing, and citations visually distinct.

9

Host question blocks

Pre-written questions the hosts can pick up — anchors the conversation.

10

"Do not say" guardrails

Explicit list of pitfalls — invented numerics, anachronisms, sensitive misreads.

Where we are today

What the bundles already do well

  • Phonetic normalization for Arabic terms
  • Modern analogies for classical concepts
  • Explanatory clarifications inline
  • Thematic continuity across chapters
  • Theological framing where appropriate
  • Narrative movement (seeker → oath → symbolism → authority → verification → practice)

What the bundles still read as

Enhanced prose. The hosts narrate. They do not yet disagree, push back, callback, or stage tension.

The gap to close: add the ten levers above to every chapter's source pack so the same hosts deliver a podcast instead of an audiobook.

Where steering shows up in the pipeline

Steering is not a new phase — it is a deliberate set of additions to existing phases.

One-line takeaway

Most teams upload PDFs and get audiobook output. We are engineering source bundles so the same NotebookLM hosts produce podcast-intelligent output — even though the voices themselves remain fixed.

Where humans review

The agent makes drafts. Humans make the calls that matter. Three review gates exist by design; two more emerge automatically when the challenger fires a P0 finding.

Human-in-the-loop review flow

HITL

A clean chapter ships in one pass. A flagged chapter loops through human review and a targeted phase retry until clean. The agent never overrides a human's verdict.

What "done" looks like

"Done" for the podcast agent is the moment any of the seven pilot books ships a clean, challenger-approved NotebookLM bundle with the disambiguation register signed off by a human reviewer, the cost ledger reconciled within 1% of the Anthropic console, and the bundle uploaded by a human — not by the agent.
