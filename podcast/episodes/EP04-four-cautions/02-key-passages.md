# Key Passages: Ayyuhal Walad, The Four Cautions

Verbatim quotations from the first half of the Eight Admonitions (sections 12 through 16), in the order they appear in the source. Arabic transliteration is preserved exactly as printed in the Irfan Hasan English translation. Each passage carries an attribution and a brief note on why it matters for the conversation.

---

## 1. The framing line of the eight admonitions

> Among these [eight admonitions], you have to do four of them and you do not do four of them so that your knowledge does not become your enemy on the Day of Judgment. First, those four things will be mentioned that you should not do.


**Why this matters:** *So that your knowledge does not become your enemy.* This is the structural hinge. The four refusals are protections against the most dangerous thing in the seeker's possession, his own learning.

---

## 2. The first caution opens

> Firstly, as much as it's possible you should not have [religious] debates with everyone and do not argue with anyone on any [religious or non-religious] issue because there are many [hidden] calamities (harms) in it and has more harms than benefits.


**Why this matters:** The injunction is not *be careful*. It is *do not enter into it*. Establish the severity early.

---

## 3. The seven evils of debate

> This work (debates and arguments) is the fountainhead (source) of all evil things such as ostentation (show off/ *Riya*), jealousy, arrogance, resentment, enmity, pride, egoism, etc.


**Why this matters:** Seven names. The length of the list is the point. These are exactly the inner diseases the entire treatise has been trying to cut out, and debate is the spring that returns them.

---

## 4. First test of right intention in debate

> First [indication] is that, if truth is revealed from either your tongue or from the tongue of the opponent, it makes no difference to you. In other words, you are satisfied (content) in both cases that the truth has been revealed.


**Why this matters:** The seeker who wants truth does not care whose mouth it comes from. Relief at being right is the diagnostic of bad intention.

---

## 5. Second test of right intention in debate

> Second indication [of your right intention] is that you consider it better to argue on this [issue with your opponent] in solitude [and not in front of an audience].


**Why this matters:** The audience is the tell. A debate that requires witnesses is for the witnesses, not for the matter.

---

## 6. The couplet on jealousy

> *Kullul 'Adawati Qad Turja Izalatuha Illa 'Adawata Mun 'Aadaka Min Hasadin.*
> Every kind of animosity can be rectified (corrected) but the animosity born out of jealousy is not possible to be rectified.


**Why this matters:** The first of the four categories of patient is the jealous questioner. Animosity from jealousy is uncurable. Walk away.

---

## 7. The verse on walking away

> *Fa 'AaRid 'Um Mun Tawalla 'Un Zikrina Walam Yurid Illal Hayatad Dunya.*
> So stay away from the individual who [because of jealousy] turns his/her face away from Our remembrance and desires nothing but [the pleasures of] the life of the world.


**Why this matters:** Quranic authorization for non-engagement with the jealous questioner.

---

## 8. The hadith on speaking to capacity

> *Nahnu Ma'aashirul Anbiyaee Umirna Un Natakallamun Nasa 'Ala Qadre 'Uuqulihim.*
> Our group of prophets have been ordered [by Allah] to talk to the people according to their minds (i.e. mental capacities/understandings).


**Why this matters:** Authorizes silence when the listener cannot receive what would be said. Matched speech to capacity is the principle.

---

## 9. The curable patient

> This type of person is that patient who can be cured. Therefore, to answer the questions of this person is not just legitimate but it's obligatory (*Wajib*).


**Why this matters:** One in four patients is curable. Speaking to *that* questioner is not optional, it is obligatory. The caution against debate is not a caution against speaking; it is a caution against speaking to the wrong ear.

---

## 10. The second caution opens

> You are being admonished that you refrain from preaching and making speeches because there are great calamities and harms in it.


**Why this matters:** Like the debate caution, this is severe at the door. Not *preach carefully*. *Do not preach.*

---

## 11. The word to the Son of Mary

> *Ya Ibna Maryama A'azee Nafsaka Fa In At-ta'azta Fa Izin Nasa Fustahyee Minni.*
> O Son of Mary! You [should] admonish yourself (*Nafs*) [first], then if it accepts your advice then give advices to others, otherwise have shame before Me.


**Why this matters:** Even Jesus is told to admonish himself first. *Have shame before Me.* The reader of the letter has not yet finished admonishing his own *Nafs* and is not entitled to admonish anyone else.

---

## 12. The flood image

> In a horrific time like this, the owner of the house will never mention the flood in colorful sentences, gestures and signs (secretive language), flowery and decorated language, or in an embellished colorful poetry. The example of the sermon should be like this.


**Why this matters:** The single image that does the most work in this section. The sermon is the flood warning. The preacher who decorates the flood warning is not warning anyone. He is performing.

---

## 13. The thoughts of applause

> Secondly, while giving sermons (religious talks or preaching), one should not allow such thoughts to enter into one's heart that after listening to his/her sermon people will shout slogans of praise, become ecstatic, tear their clothes, raise a loud uproar in the gathering, and audience will start saying that the gathering was very good and that so-and-so gave a very good sermon.


**Why this matters:** The detailed enumeration is the point. Ghazali names the specific applause-fantasy in the preacher's heart, and forbids it.

---

## 14. The preacher as Satan

> This type of a person is a *Shaytan* (Satan) [who after becoming a slave of his/her degraded lower self, thinks that he/she will display his/her abilities in the sermon and will obtain the fame and grandeur of the world]. This *Shaytan* (Satan) misguides creations [of God] from the path of true guidance, sheds their blood, and involves them in an eternal destruction.


**Why this matters:** The strongest sentence in the second caution. A showy preacher is not misguided; he is Satan's apprentice on the pulpit.

---

## 15. The third caution, escalating refusal

> Third [admonition about things you should avoid] is that you should not [even] greet any king, any affluent, or any ruler and stay away from their proximity (company) and gatherings and more than that you should not even lift an eye to look at them.


**Why this matters:** Three behaviors, in escalating order. Greet, sit, even raise an eye. The escalation is the argument. The eye that lingers on power is already infected.

---

## 16. The Hadith on praising oppressors

> *Fa InnAllaha Yaghdib Iza Mudihal Faasiqu Waz Zalimu Wa Iza Madaha Wa Mun Da'aa Li Zalimin Bitoolil Baqaaii Faqad Ahabba Aye Ya'siyAllahu Fil Arde.*
> Verily, Allah, the Exalted, is angry when any transgressor (*Fasiq*) and an oppressor (*Zalim*) is praised and the one who prays for a long life for an oppressor, is as if he/she has liked that he/she walks on earth as a sinner.


**Why this matters:** Connects to the fourth caution. To wish the oppressor long life is to wish for more oppression on the earth.

---

## 17. The fourth caution, even from Halal sources

> Fourth [admonition about things you should avoid] is that you should not accept any gifts from rulers even though you know that what they are giving is from *Halal* (legal/permissible) sources.


**Why this matters:** The qualification is everything. Even if the wealth is legal, refuse. The refusal is not about the wealth; it is about the intention over the wealth.

---

## 18. The mechanism of corruption

> The least [minimum] corruption that is born out of this is that you will start to love the oppressors and when a person loves another then he/she prays for a long life for them (the oppressors). If an oppressor has a long life then the oppression will continue longer and there will be more turmoil and corruption in the world and what can be worse than that?


**Why this matters:** Gift, then love, then prayer for long life, then more oppression. The minimum corruption from accepting a gift is the maximum corruption in the world.

---

## 19. The Satanic loophole

> Beware! Beware! *Shaytan* (Satan) will misguide you and will create this thought in your heart, "First thing you should do is that take the money from these rulers and then distribute it among the indigents (poor) and cause them some comfort and fulfill their needs". Beware! Never accept this kind of advice from any *Shaytan* (Satan) either from among the Jinns or from among the humans, and do not get trapped in their machinations (plots/conspiracies) to get deceived by them because Satan has spilled the blood of many using this strategy and still continues to spill blood.


**Why this matters:** Names the pious-sounding rationale (redistribute the ruler's wealth to the poor) as Satan's strategy. The double *Beware! Beware!* carries the load.

---

## 20. The pointer to the Ihya

> There are so many calamities embedded (hidden) within this fact that we have explained in our book "*Ahya al-Uloom ad-Deen*" (Revival of the Knowledge of the Path to God). You can search (find) them there.


**Why this matters:** The reference is pointed. The four cautions are a compressed summary. The full argument is in the *Ihya*, which the student already owns and has already read. The letter has been pressing this point throughout: act on what you have.
