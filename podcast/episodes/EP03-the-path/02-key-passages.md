# Key Passages: Ayyuhal Walad, The Path

Verbatim quotations from the eight sections covered in this episode, in the order they appear in the source. Arabic transliteration is preserved exactly as printed in the Irfan Hasan English translation. Each passage carries a brief note on why it matters for the conversation.

---

## 1. The narrow category of the real guide

> The heir of Messenger of Allah (Peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) who is made one's Shaykh (spiritual guide) should be a scholar but every scholar cannot be a Shaykh al-Kamil (a perfected spiritual guide).

**Why this matters:** The first sentence of the section. Scholar is necessary, not sufficient. Ghazali immediately narrows the category before he lists the qualities.

---

## 2. Why Ghazali lists the qualities

> Here we briefly narrate some [such] qualities so that every nut head (crazy) and misguided person could not claim to be a Shaykh (spiritual guide) [as it is necessary to define these qualities due to the presence of a large number of fake impersonators claiming to be Shaykhs whose sole purpose is to deceive people to gain material benefits thus causing great damage to this important aspect of Islam by disillusioning people and misguiding many].

**Why this matters:** Ghazali says the quiet part out loud. The qualities exist as a filter against fraud, not as an idealization. The same impostor concern reappears in EP01's hollow-Sufi warning.

---

## 3. The chain back to the Prophet

> I say this that only that person can be a Shaykh (spiritual guide) who after having turned his/her face away from the love of the world, [love of] honor, and [love of worldly] ranks, has given the Bay'a (a way of promising Allah on the hand of a Shaykh to turn away from evil inwardly and outwardly and to excel utmost in the Path of God by obeying God's commandment, staying away from His prohibitions, and struggling with one's soul to excel oneself in the Path of God by purifying one's soul seeking God's nearness and approval) to a perfected spiritual guide [who in turn has given the Bay'a to his perfected spiritual guide and so on] so that this [spiritual] chain [in this way] goes back all the way to the Messenger of Allah (Peace and blessings of Allah be upon him).

**Why this matters:** The structural protection in the classical Sufi path. A real guide is not self-appointed; he stands inside a chain of transmission.

---

## 4. The inventory of qualities

> This person should have in his/her temperament (inner and outer personality) all good moral qualities and patience, gratitude, certitude [in Allah], generosity, contentment, trustworthiness, tolerance, seriousness [of purpose in life], humility, obedience [to Allah], truthfulness, shyness (modesty, reserve, fear of wrongdoing), dignity (an aura of honor and respect), tranquility, and other qualities like these should be a part of his character and conduct.

**Why this matters:** Ghazali names the qualities individually rather than abstractly. The list reads as a checklist for the seeker who is evaluating a candidate.

---

## 5. The difficulty of finding such a guide

> Such spiritual guides are very difficult to find. But if someone has obtained this wealth and this blessing that he/she found such a spiritual guide and this spiritual guide includes him/her among his disciples (Mureeds) then it is necessary for this disciple to have outer and inner respect (honor and etiquettes) for his/her spiritual guide.

**Why this matters:** The hinge between the qualities of the guide and the etiquettes of the disciple. Ghazali concedes the rarity without softening the demand.

---

## 6. The core rule of outer respect

> Whatever the perfected guide commands, the disciple should obey it to the best of his/her capacity [It is extremely important for everyone to understand here that, of course, a true spiritual guide cannot and will not command a disciple to do something which is Haram (forbidden) in the Shari'ah (the law of Allah). On contrary, the fake impersonators claiming to be spiritual guides lacking in the aforementioned qualities, can command their disciples to do things that are Haram (forbidden)].

**Why this matters:** The single line of obedience together with the structural protection. The bracketed translator's note clarifies what Ghazali assumes the reader already knows: the qualities of the guide are the seeker's safety inside obedience.

---

## 7. No grain of doubt

> Inner etiquettes [to one's spiritual guide] include that whatever the disciple apparently hears from the Murshid (spiritual guide), he/she should not entertain even a small grain of doubt in his/her heart about the Shaykh's words or deeds otherwise the disciple will be called a hypocrite. If the disciple cannot do that then he/she should abstain from the company of the spiritual guide until his/her inside becomes like his/her outside.

**Why this matters:** The standard of inner respect, and the honest exit. Hypocrisy is named as the failure mode; distance is the remedy.

---

## 8. The seventh condition

> in every situation, [a seeker] should give preference to humility and lack of greed over wealth and should adopt contentment [and satisfaction with the Will of Allah]. These seven things are essential for the traveler on the Path of Allah, the Exalted, and the seeker [of the truth].

**Why this matters:** Ghazali tallies the seven conditions as a single closing of the etiquettes movement before pivoting to the realities.

---

## 9. Tasawwuf, defined

> You have asked, what is Tasawwuf? Tasawwuf is the name of two qualities: First [quality] is that [the slave] should be obedient to Allah, i.e., he/she should be acting on the Shari'ah (the outer laws of Islam including obligations, commandments, and prohibitions). And second [quality] is that [the seeker] is the one who is sympathetic and a doer of good towards [all of] Allah's creations [which is not possible without purifying one's soul from all evil qualities and then embellishing one's soul with all good qualities].

**Why this matters:** The episode's central definition. Vertical and horizontal. Obedience and benefaction. Quote this in full when discussing.

---

## 10. The correct use of the word Sufi

> One who has perseverance in acting on the Shari'ah and has the qualities of [desiring] betterment (true success) for the entire humanity is a "Sufi" (this actually is the correct use of this term as opposed to the wrong uses of the term that are prevalent today).

**Why this matters:** Ghazali polices his own tradition. The word is precise; its current uses, in his own time, are not.

---

## 11. Servitude, defined

> You have asked, what is servitude? Servitude has three things: First is to obey the commandments of Shari'ah (the outer laws of Islam). Second is to be content with the decree and destiny appointed by Allah, the Exalted. Third is to leave one's desires and prerogatives and to be happy with the Desire and Prerogative (Power) of Allah, the Exalted.

**Why this matters:** Three movements outward to inward: obey, accept, surrender the inner preference. Quote in full.

---

## 12. Tawakkul, defined

> You have also asked, what is reliance on God (Tawakkul)? You should know that Tawakkul is that, one should have firm belief and certitude in what Allah, the Exalted, has promised. In other words, one should believe that you will surely get whatever Allah, the Exalted, has destined for you. Then if the entire world tries to stop it, it (Allah's Decree for you) cannot be stopped. But for that which is not written in your destiny [by Allah], if you and the entire world make efforts for it, you will never get it.

**Why this matters:** The definition that does the most work in the listener's chest. *Tawakkul* is not passivity. It is certitude about outcome, paired with continued action.

---

## 13. Ikhlas, defined

> You have also asked, what is sincerity to God (Ikhlas)? You should know that Ikhlas is that, all of your deeds should be exclusively for the sake of Allah [and His pleasure]. Whatever you do, it should not be for show off (ostentation/Riya). When you do good deeds, your heart should not be inclined towards people [to impress them and to gain their praise]. Your heart should not be pleased at the admirations (praises) of the people, nor should [your heart] be grieved at their complaints [or criticisms].

**Why this matters:** Sincerity is named, and tested by the heart's reaction to praise and criticism. The test is internal, not behavioral.

---

## 14. The cure of Riya: pebbles and stones

> You should know that Riya (show off/ostentation) is born out of people's praises and honors and the cure of [the spiritual disease of] Riya (show off/ostentation) is that you consider the entire universe subservient to the Power (Control) of Allah, the Exalted, and consider all creations [of Allah] equivalent (similar) to the pebbles and stones, you should understand this that stones do not have the power to bring you either grief or comfort. If you consider all creations like this (i.e. like stones and pebbles) then you will get rid of Riya (show off/ostentation). Until you have this belief that the [mortal] creations have power to bring you grief and comfort, then Riya (show off/ostentation) will never get out of your heart.

**Why this matters:** The bracing image at the heart of the episode. Other people as pebbles. Pebbles cannot praise; pebbles cannot wound. The cure is metaphysical before it is behavioral.

---

## 15. The closing line from Zun Noon Misri

> If you have the courage to exert your utmost [with your soul in this way] then come [and step in this way] otherwise do not just get indulged in the nice things that Sufis say.

**Why this matters:** The line that closes the eight sections. The definitions are not for collecting. They are an invitation to the *Mujahadah* of actually living them.
