# Slide Deck Framing, EP12: The sacred law of Adam and the First Speaker

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio episode. They have not read *The Correction* or *The Defense*.

## Core Principle

Each slide must add visual information the audio cannot carry. The audio is a patient nineteen-sub-chapter adjudication; the deck makes its structure visible at a glance.

## Visual Priorities

- The three souls and the six gates as a nested hierarchy, with positive worship layered above the six necessary gates.
- The raven-and-burial story as two parallel reading layers, the *the apparent* surface beside the *the hiddeni* identities (*wali*, *opponent*, *Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr*).
- The Adam-Noah *sacred law*-establishment as a contrast pair whose reversal lands the verdict.
- The nineteen sub-chapters as a process flow, each objection paired with its counter.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a sub-chapter number or beat title.
- No literal illustrations of Adam, the raven, or the angels.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams. Every diagram has axes, rows, levels, or directed transitions.
- No slides that restate a single audio sentence.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide must add visual information not present in the audio.
- For the three souls and six gates, use a nested hierarchy with positive worship layered above the six gates.
- For the raven-and-burial story, show the *the apparent* and *the hiddeni* layers as parallel columns.
- For the Adam-Noah parallel, show the claim and the reversal side by side, attribute by attribute.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
