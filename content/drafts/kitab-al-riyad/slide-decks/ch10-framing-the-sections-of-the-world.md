# Slide Deck Framing, EP10: The Sections of the World

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio episode. They have not read *The Correction*, *The Defense*, or *The Harvest*. The deck must do what the audio cannot.

## Core Principle

Each slide must add visual information the audio cannot carry. Slides exist to make the four-jewels-and-four-classes scheme legible, render the pairing-by-occasion matrix, and fix the human at the *between*-state of the two worlds.

## Visual Priorities

- The four-jewels-and-four-classes scheme versus the three-genera-with-the-human-as-fourth-by-distinction settlement, as a contrast pair.
- The pairing-by-occasion matrix: human disposition left, world-creature pair right, across six pairings.
- The human in the *between*-state of the two worlds, as a quadrant map: universal Intellect and Soul above, *prime matter* and elements below, the human at the *barzakh*.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a chapter or section title.
- No literal illustrations: no calligraphy, no mosques, no scholar portraits, no horse-and-human imagery.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams. Every diagram has axes, rows, or named nodes.
- No slides that restate one sentence the audio already speaks.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide must add visual information not present in the audio.
- Show the relationships between entities, not a list of them.
- For the pairing-by-occasion, use a comparison matrix with disposition on the left and world-creature on the right.
- For the human in the *between*-state, use a quadrant map with the higher world above, this world below, the human at the meeting point.
- Place the higher-rank entity to the left in every pair; maintain across the deck.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
