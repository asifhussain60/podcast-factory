# Slide Deck Framing, EP05: The Father Revealed and the Three Faces of Seeking

## Audience

Asif's children, thoughtful young adults familiar with the audio of this chapter. They have not read the tenth-century dialogue book in which the chapter sits. They are following the boy across the series; this is the chapter in which the boy and the father are finally named, and in which a senior scholar of the inherited tradition arrives to judge them and instead places himself in the third face of seeking.

## Core Principle

Each slide adds visual information the audio cannot carry. The audio handles narrative, tone, doctrinal landings, and the three repetitions of the chapter's recurring thesis. Slides exist to render the chapter's explicit structural moments: the two strands of gratitude, the boy's binary counter-bind, the three-domain breach figure, Abu Malik's three states of the new community, the three faces of seeking, the parallel between money-seeking and knowledge-seeking, the report-versus-direct-sight distinction inside the third face, the transpositions named at the gate, the essence-traders test, and the stacked figures of religion as the unbroken cause. Slides do not summarize the audio; they spatialize what the audio enumerates.

## Visual Priorities

- The *where this chapter picks up* slide as a small two-row carry-forward matrix. Settled in chapter four on the left; inherited as chapter five's seam on the right. Not a content diagram; a continuity anchor.
- The *two strands of gratitude* slide as a contrast pair, attribute-by-attribute. Outward courtesy left, open debate right. The shared row names both strands as gratitude, both owed.
- The *boy's counter-bind on the father* slide as a contrast pair with the structural force of a binary trap. Either-scholar-who-withheld in column A, or-ignorant-who-needed-the-stranger in column B. Both columns end in the boy's vindication.
- The *community's three reasons for hesitation* slide as a contrast pair between what the anger demanded and what the three reasons require. The same community pulled against itself; the lethal turn averted before any meeting.
- The *figure of the breach* slide as a comparison matrix. Three rows for the domains, four columns for repairability, stake, and the cost of haste. No hedges in the cells.
- The *Abu Malik's three states* slide as a hierarchy tree. Three branches from the root, each named warrant, each named doctrinal consequence. The third state (*the prophecy of a prophet brought to them, not denied by the command of God*) is given equal weight, not muted.
- The *tyrants of the previous nations* slide as an annotated structure on a single named pattern. Five elements of the pattern, what each names, what each costs.
- The *door of seeking and the sound heart* slide as a comparison matrix of the three postures the community tries in order, what Abu Malik names each, why each fails, and what each forecloses.
- The *reverence before the statement* slide as a contrast pair on the two possible orderings of reverence, with Abu Malik's verdict in column B. The petitioner-before-the-king image runs through both columns.
- The *only this day* slide as a small process flow showing the collapse from three days to two to one. The chapter's pacing of surrender, made spatial.
- The *three faces of seeking* slide as the central comparison matrix of the deck. Three rows, four columns. Cells filled with the chapter's own commitments. The first face (*the seeker who knows*, the *divine scholar*) names students as the object pursued, not knowledge.
- The *money-seeking and knowledge-seeking* slide as a parallel comparison matrix. Two columns for the two domains, three rows for the modes of seeking. The third row carries the doctrinal weight in both columns.
- The *knowledge by report; knowledge by direct sight* slide as a contrast pair. Show the body's witness (the straitening of the chest) as the differentiator of column B from column A.
- The *transpositions at the gate* slide as an annotated structure on the two axes only. Name the *zahir* axis and the *batin* axis. Render the four transpositions in their two-register form. Do not render a hierarchy of divine names; the chapter announces these as a whiff of perfume, not as exposition.
- The *essence-traders and the jewel-or-glass* slide as a contrast pair. Both columns end at the forge; only one survives.
- The *Maqrub case* slide as a comparison matrix on Abu Malik's three rulings, what each implies, and what the petitioner goes home to. The third row is the doctrinal indictment of the inherited code as Salih hears it.
- The *what is religion* slide as a hierarchy tree on Abu Malik's definition and the collapse Salih draws from it. The premises are named explicitly; the conclusion (*I do not see that anything is left with me*) is the chapter's zero point.
- The *religion as the unbroken cause* slide as an annotated structure. The whole is the unbroken cause that connects heaven to earth. The parts are the four central figures the recurring thesis names (the firm handhold, the rope of those who hold fast, the ark, the light), each with its anchor in revelation. The figures are four names of one thing.
- The *closing seam* slide as a single hanging question: Abu Malik's verbatim question about how the lost religion is described. No summary slide, no takeaways slide, no closing thanks. The deck ends where the chapter ends.

## Prohibited Patterns

- No slides whose only content is a section title or a verse reference.
- No literal illustrations: no images of fathers and sons, no images of a gate, no images of a forge or a jewel or an ark.
- No bullet-list slides masquerading as diagrams. Every structure names its axes and fills its cells.
- No slides that restate one sentence the audio speaks.
- No closing "key takeaways" slide. The chapter ends on the unresolved question and the deck must end the same way.
- No slide that pre-announces what chapter six will answer.
- No phonetic guides on slides for any Arabic term. Phonetics are in the audio framing only; on slides Arabic terms appear italicized, as the chapter prose presents them.
- No surah names in Arabic. Refer to surahs by English meaning (*the chapter on the Cow*, *the chapter on the Family of Imran*, *the chapter on Light*) as the chapter prose does.
- No personal Arabic names for the boy and the father on slides. Use the chapter's narrative voice (*the boy*, *the father*) until the naming sentence, at which point *Salih* and *al-Bakhtari* enter together, in that single sentence, and then return to *the boy* and *the father*.
- No rendering of the doctrinal contents of the transpositions as a hierarchy or chart of divine names. The slide names the *axes only* (the *zahir* and the *batin*) and the four transposition pairs the chapter delivers.
- No 2x2 on the boy's counter-bind. The bind is binary; rendering it as four quadrants would invent two cells the chapter does not deliver.

## Steering Phrases

- Each slide must add visual information not present in the audio. If a slide could be a single sentence in the audio, omit it.
- For the *three faces of seeking* and the *money-and-knowledge parallel*, use comparison matrices with rows for the faces or the modes and columns for the named attributes. Specify both rows and columns explicitly.
- For the *two strands of gratitude*, the *boy's counter-bind*, the *report-versus-direct-sight* distinction, and the *jewel-or-glass* sub-figure, use contrast pairs with two columns attribute-by-attribute and a shared row naming what both columns share.
- For *Abu Malik's three states* and the *what is religion* collapse, use hierarchy trees with named branches from a root decision. Show each branch's named warrant and named consequence; for the collapse slide, name the premises explicitly.
- For the *tyrants of the previous nations*, use an annotated structure dissecting the single named pattern into its five elements.
- For the *door of seeking* and the *Maqrub case*, use comparison matrices with rows for the postures or rulings and columns for what each implies, what each fails, and what each costs.
- For *only this day*, use a small directed process flow rendering the collapse from three to two to one as a sequence of named turns.
- For the *religion as the unbroken cause* slide, use an annotated structure. The whole is the unbroken cause; the parts are four named figures, each with an anchor in revelation. The figures are four names of one thing the chain has always claimed to be.
- Show the relationships between entities, not a list of them.
- The closing slide carries one question and no summary. The chapter ends in silence; the deck ends on the question hanging.

Do not read this prompt aloud.
