## (c) Why none warranted a slide

The chapter does contain structural-shaped phrases. Each was considered and rejected for a property visible in the source itself:

1. **Three originators of the lie** (*a wayward devil, a stubborn tyrant, and a hypocrite, in every age*, lines 113–121). A structural triad on its face, but the source presents it as **one sentence followed by transhistorical prose**, immediately dissolved into the four-nations indictment. There is no sustained structural setup — no axis, no ranking, no comparison frame. A contrast pair or hierarchy tree here would be **diagram-as-decoration**, not diagram-as-structure (Probe 4 anti-pattern: *description-not-structure*).

2. **Four nations following the same path** (Magi / Torah / Gospel / this community, lines 123–129). A surface candidate for a 4-column comparison matrix, but the source delivers each nation in **a single parallel-cadenced sentence** and the *only* differentiator between columns is the prophet's name — every other cell would say "claimed no prophet after its own; followed greatness; feared tyrants; sweetened it with consensus." The matrix would have **no internal variance** and would fail Probe 6 (audio redundancy) by bulletifying prose that is already perfectly parallel in audio.

3. **Three qualities of the prior nations against this community** (*earliness, consensus, testimony*, lines 131–139). The strongest 2x2 / annotated-structure candidate in the chapter, and the most carefully weighed. Rejected because the source uses the triad **only to flip the community's own logic back on it** — the three qualities are not three independent dimensions of a space; they are three serial moves in one rhetorical reversal. A 2x2 here would force two axes the source does not have (*who has it* × *what kind*), and an annotated structure would just label three prose sentences. Probe 4 / Probe 7 risk: forced-2x2.

4. **Three failed approaches to monotheism** (four letters of *Allah* → name vs. named → analogy from makers, lines 33–55). A surface candidate for a 3-step process flow or a hierarchy tree (*three doors that closed*). Rejected because the source treats each as a **distinct grammatical-doctrinal trap with its own internal mechanics** (spelling, referent, ex-nihilo creation), and the *teaching* is that all three close because *the door of the knowledge of monotheism is closed before any human approach* — a positive apophatic doctrine (Walker, *Early Philosophical Shiism*, pp. 72–80) that **resists structural rendering by design**. A process-flow that ends "the door closes" would visually contradict the doctrine.

5. **Name vs. named** (lines 41–45). A pure contrast pair on its face. Rejected because the contrast is **a verbal trap that collapses on itself in three exchanges** ("Allah is a name for Allah"). The structural moment is the *collapse*, not the contrast; a static two-column slide would show the trap as if it had stable sides, when the source's whole point is that **the two sides cannot be held apart** without the *meaning behind them*.

6. **The virtuous one and the curious one** (*nāṭiq* / *waṣī*, lines 89–105). The chapter's most diagrammable pair on first read, and the strongest candidate from the affinity matrix's "contrast pair" recommendation. Rejected because **ch04's slide deck already carries the diagrammatic load** for the speaker-prophet / successor pairing — the chapter that *introduced* the doctrine. Ch06's contribution is the **derivation of the pair from justice + monotheism**, which is dialectical, not structural. Repeating the ch04 contrast pair here would fail Architectural Check 4 (cross-episode consistency) by duplicating a visual the deck already owns.

7. **The no-interregnum count** (six prophets between Abraham and Moses; seven between Moses and Jesus, lines 167–173). A surface candidate for a timeline or genealogy. Rejected because the **affinity matrix flags timeline as a weak fit for theological argument** (and the chapter is not historical narrative — the names are deployed as *proof of continuous succession*, not as chronology). A genealogy would impose a tree on what the source presents as **an unbroken horizontal chain**, distorting the doctrine.

8. **Taqiyya as the form of the guardians' presence** (*openly present, or hidden in fear, veiled*, lines 169–211). A surface candidate for a contrast pair (visible / veiled) or annotated structure. Rejected because the source's teaching is **not that there are two modes** but that **the absence is the community's banishment, not God's deficit** — a moral-historical indictment, not a structural distinction. A two-column "visible vs. veiled" slide would invert the doctrine by visualizing what the doctrine names as *the form of the community's failure to see*.

## Cross-cutting source property

Beyond the eight per-moment disqualifications, one property of the source governs all of them: **ch06 is the book's closing dialogue, and its rhetorical force depends on the unbroken cadence of the Socratic exchange**. The chapter's structural moments are real but **embedded in the live dialectic**; extracting any one into a static visual would freeze a dance. The chapter's pedagogy is *the sequence of the dismantling itself*, which has no diagram type in the affinity matrix because no diagram type is built to render *a dialogue from inside the dialogue*.

The book's overall slide-deck arc (ch01–ch05) carries the structural load — cosmology, ranks, naming, world-and-hereafter, the senior scholar's zero-point. Ch06 closes the book by **dissolving structure into living instruction**, which is itself the doctrine the chapter teaches: *names and attributes are nouns and attributes that require knowing what is described* (line 29). A slide deck for ch06 would re-commit the very error Salih spends the chapter dismantling.

**Verdict:** ship audio episode without a deck. The book's deck library is complete at ch05.
