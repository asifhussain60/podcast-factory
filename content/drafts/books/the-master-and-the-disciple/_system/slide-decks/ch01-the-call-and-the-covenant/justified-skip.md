## (c) Why none of the six warranted a slide

**Fragment 1 (threefold law of thanks) — three-column comparison freezes a rule the chapter immediately weaponizes as plot.** The triple structure is not delivered as a typology for inspection; it is delivered once in formula and then re-stated *three more times across the chapter* as the engine of the action — the prostration-before-the-work rule at the reunion (line 210), the elite-and-general response asymmetry (line 131), the work-not-flattery rule that closes the framing (lines 165–169). A comparison-matrix slide would arrest at the first statement what the chapter treats as a recurring liturgical pulse that *advances the dialogue*. The figure is doctrinal infrastructure, not the chapter's visual payoff.

**Fragment 2 (source/target 2x2 on speech) — anti-pattern "Forced 2x2" (`slide-deck-patterns.md` §"Anti-Patterns the Challenger Catches" #4) with a hidden third dimension.** Cell-wise the matrix tempts: source ∈ {God, caprice} × target ∈ {guidance, other}. But the chapter's grammar refuses the diagonal cells. The text delivers only two of the four cells (the praiseworthy "from God → to God" and the reprehensible "from caprice → to other-than-guidance"); the off-diagonal cells (from-God → to-other, from-caprice → to-guidance) are not *empty* in the chapter — they are *excluded by definition*, because what comes from God necessarily calls to God and what calls to caprice necessarily originated there. A 2x2 visualization would advertise a four-cell space the chapter explicitly collapses to a one-dimensional axis. The figure is a paired joint, not a quadrant map.

**Fragment 3 (outward/inward contrast pair) — inverts the chapter's pedagogical direction.** The two layers are real in this chapter — the apparent and the hidden, the remembrance/evidence and the sure drink. But the chapter's central injunction at the moment of binding is that *the road to the inward runs through the outward* ("act on what you know of the outer, and the inner will be opened to you," lines 177–178). A side-by-side contrast pair would render the two layers as parallel registers a reader might pick between, when the chapter is rendering them as *sequenced stations on a single road* the disciple must walk in order. The figure is a directed passage, not a comparison.

**Fragment 4 (five conditions) — anti-pattern "Bullet-list-as-diagram" (#2) compounded by asymmetric weight.** The five conditions are delivered as a single uninterrupted string in the Master's voice (lines 189–191), and the chapter's commentary (line 191) immediately marks them as *not equal*: the first four are "conditions of availability and rank" and the fifth — *do not mention what passes between us to your father* — is "the practical condition that everything else in the book will turn on." A flat numbered-list slide would render five equal cells; the prose insists on a 4+1 split where the fifth condition carries the load of the entire remaining book. No flat list does this, and an annotated-structure slide that *did* would just re-typeset the prose commentary at line 191.

**Fragment 5 (chain of transmission) — under-specified at the chapter level.** The chapter names the chain in seed form — speaker-prophet, proof, summoners, Imams, "the speakers of the call" (lines 89, 91, 133) — but explicitly defers the elaboration to later chapters ("all are taken up later because the covenant has first been struck here," line 9). A hierarchy-tree slide built from ch01 alone would either be a one-node-deep stub (Allah → "speakers of the call") or would import structure from later chapters the listener has not yet heard. The chapter's own discipline is to *name without diagramming*; the diagram belongs downstream.

**Fragment 6 (acknowledgment-vs-action, brothers parable) — the chapter's strongest 2x2 candidate, but the source dissolves the axes before the slide could carry them.** The parable carves a square: {acknowledges, denies} × {acts, neglects}. But the chapter's payoff is not the four cells — it is the line at lines 153–155: *"acknowledgment without an agent is the same as denial… action has separated you from them, even as ignorance has joined you with them."* The chapter explicitly collapses two of the four cells (acknowledge+neglect = deny+neglect) into one verdict. The 2x2 would visualize a space the prose has already flattened to a one-axis verdict by the time the disciple yields. The figure is a *resolved* tension by chapter's end, not a live quadrant the listener should keep open.

## Additional consideration — the chapter's central event refuses representation

The chapter's terminal moment is the *recitation of the covenant* (lines 220–233): the Master recites, the disciple is bound, his tears pour down, he passes into the Party of God, and falls silent — and then the Master "begins to expound and explain." The chapter ends *on the threshold of the exposition the rest of the book will deliver*. The bond itself is an irreversible threshold-crossing the chapter renders as ritual sound (recitation + tears + silence), not as schema. The text's own simile compares the key to "what distinguishes between forbidden union and lawful marriage" (line 216) — i.e. *the contract is the difference, not the appearance*. Any slide attempting to depict the central moment would convert the chapter's contract-is-the-difference doctrine into a stable surface the contract is, by definition, doing work *underneath*. The chapter's discipline forbids the deck at the very moment a deck would most want to land.
