At this very moment, you are sitting in a physical body, breathing air, bound by gravity. But for centuries, philosophers have been fighting a brutal war over what you actually are. One side argues that human beings are merely the final product of biological and material forces, while the other insists we are spiritual exiles merely visiting a physical form. Today we are examining a historical text where an author adjudicates between 2 earlier treatises. a book called The Correction and a rebuttal called The Defense. The core question they are fighting over is where the human being actually belongs. And what that ultimate origin means for the physical body you inhabit. Our mission in this discussion is to understand how the human being acts as the bridge between 2 entirely different flows of existence. we need to uncover why your physical existence in time is fundamentally required for any internal result. Well, the author of the correction outlines the first position. Which grounds the human being entirely in the natural world. Yes. The argument traces a rigid sequential chain of physical stages. It begins with the most fundamental principles, the elemental natures. Through the mechanics of motion and rest, These elemental natures combine to form simple bodies. And then those simple bodies combine further into more complex compounds. It is a long, continuous sequence of natural cause and effect. A sequence that leads finally to the human physical form. And the text argues this physical form is meticulously prepared to receive 3 distinct souls. They build on top of one another. We should break down how that stacking actually works. The mechanism is strictly sequential. The physical body first receives the vegetative soul. That governs growth and nourishment. Because you cannot have perception without a biological structure that can sustain itself. Correct. Once that vegetative foundation is stable, the form can receive the sensitive soul. Which introduces perception and feeling. Yes. Finally, only when feeling and biological growth are established can the speaking soul operate. The rational element. Precisely. Because the physical world does all this heavy lifting to prepare the biological vessel, the correction concludes that the human being is the direct fruit of this physical world. The ultimate harvest of nature. But the author of The Defense issues a sharp counter-argument against that. They point at a severe philosophical danger in that stance. If we concede that we are purely the fruit of this physical world, that aligns with materialists who deny the survival of the soul entirely. The text explicitly addresses this. It notes that if the human being is purely the fruit of the material world, then the human belongs entirely to this world. And any concept of a spiritual return to a higher realm would be a myth. The logic laid out in the defense relies on a specific rule. A fruit belongs to its tree. If the natural world were truly our tree, it would hold us permanently. The physical world would keep us as its own. We wouldn't move onward to a higher spiritual world. Therefore, the defense argues we must be the fruit of the higher world instead. Here's where it gets really interesting, though. If we apply that same rule that a fruit belongs to its tree, doesn't the logic and the defense backfire entirely? Elaborate on that. If human beings are purely the fruit of the higher spiritual world, then that higher world should have held us. I see the contradiction. Yet human beings descend into physical bodies and walk the earth. The higher world clearly didn't keep us contained. So we cannot be purely the fruit of the spiritual realm either. The author adjudicating this dispute uses that exact contradiction to reveal their signature philosophical move. Both earlier writers are half right and half wrong. The human being is not the fruit of the natural world alone. Nor the higher world alone. The human is the fruit of all the worlds. We receive our first perfection from the natural world. This first perfection is the bodily form itself. intricately prepared through the physical chain of cause and effect we discussed earlier. The text attributes this physical shaping to circular bodies and proximate causes. In medieval cosmology, those circular bodies refer to the planetary spheres. The rotation of the heavens physically stirs and mixes the elemental matter on Earth. Refining it over eons to produce a vessel capable of housing intellect. But the bodily vessel is only the first half of the equation. Yes. we receive our second perfection from the higher world. This second perfection is the consummation of the spiritual image. It is the knowledge of the oneness of God, or Tawhid, earned through obedience, through adherence to Sharia, and through applied knowledge. The bodily form is given by nature, but the spiritual consummation is an enduring spiritual image. It must be achieved through the actions taken while inhabiting that form. This dual nature aligns with the doctrine taught by the Fatimid The doctrine states that the human being is the purpose of the second world and the end of the action of nature. The human is the exact point where the physical and spiritual realms culminate. The text provides 2 primary Quranic anchors to support this specific architecture. First, in the chapter of the Night Journey, we read about the honoring of the children of Adam. This honoring represents the dignity of the first perfection. The bodily form is not a neutral accidental vessel. It is inherently dignified because it is the specific form required to receive a higher gift. The text pairs that passage with verses from the chapter of the Fig. The verse states that humans are created in the best stature. That points directly to that perfectly prepared physical form we just discussed. But the verse immediately warns that humans are reduced to the lowest of the low unless they believe and do righteous deeds. Those righteous deeds illustrate the mechanics of the second perfection. Yes, they do. Let me make sure the mechanism is clear. If the physical form is left entirely to itself without the spiritual work applied to it, actively degrades. It does. The physical form is a foundation, but it is not the final structure. The second perfection is an enduring spiritual image that must be actively earned. It is a precursor to the natural thing. It is intrinsic to it and it endures beyond it. There is an aphorism attributed to Ali, the commander of the faithful, that captures this dynamic. He asks, do you suppose yourself to be a small body? Within you is folded the greater world. Okay, let's unpack this. The small body represents the channel of Gadis. That is the entire natural world and its elemental stages flowing upward. Refining matter to create the human physical form. Meanwhile, the greater world folded within represents the channel of form. The higher world of the intellect flowing downward. This places the human being exactly in the middle. We are the precise rank where the upward flow of nature and the downward flow of the intellect meet. Understanding how that downward flow actually happens brings up another major dispute in the source text. The author of the defense tries to explain how creation and emanation occur by using a highly specific analogy. An analogy that the author we are studying completely dismantles. The defense claims creation works it claims it works as a marriage between the heavens and the earth. The heavens are described as a perfect, self-sufficient male possessing all visible motions and orbits. And the earth, in this flawed framing, is described as a deficient, needy female. The defense argues that a marriage takes place between them. Pairing the rich and perfect with the deficient and needy. To produce metals, plants, and animals from potency into actuality. We must emphasize that the author entirely refuses this framing. Completely. Emanation is not a marriage of a perfect substance to a deficient substance. The earth is not deficient in itself. And the heavens cannot act without the earth. They are entirely equal in their potency to produce. The text points out that the products of creation were in potency in both the heavens and the earth. The action goes from the heavens into the earth. But that does not render 1 perfect and the other deficient. The author is making a deep structural point about the true nature of perfection here. Every rank in existence is perfect in itself. Deficiency only appears when ranks are compared or added to one another. An acorn is not a deficient oak tree. In its rank as an acorn, it is entirely perfect. It contains everything required for its specific existence. The earth is only deficient if it is compared directly to the heavens. but in its own rank, it is perfectly suited for its existence. If their selves were not perfect in the existence that befits them, nothing could be done by them at all. The heavens themselves are incomplete in their action without the earth to act upon. The chain of existence runs by the running of a single originating power. Not by splicing different substances together to make up for individual lacks. The text points to the chapter explained in detail to anchor this refusal. The verse describes God calling to both the heaven and the earth. And both reply. Neither is portrayed as a needy or deficient partner. They both respond equally to a single originating call. The flawed marriage analogy presented by the defense completely misunderstands how existence flows. It attempts to project human social categories and dependencies onto metaphysical realities. This misunderstanding leads the defense into another severe theoretical error. The defense suggests that the universal soul acted to create the physical world because she was seeking a noble rank she hadn't yet attained. The argument is that the soul lacked something. That her action was highly ordered and arranged specifically to reach a rank she did not possess. And that she used the creation of the world to complete herself. So what does this all mean? If the universal soul acts out of need seeking to complete herself through the physical world, doesn't that mean the higher spiritual world depends entirely on the lower physical world? It would. Doesn't that collapse the entire architecture of existence by making the higher reliant on the lower. This raises an important question about the nature of divine action. The author strictly denies the seeking theory for precisely the reason we just outlined. God enriched the universal soul at origination, an act referred to as Ibda. Her overflow into the physical world is an act of pure giving. It establishes wisdom by bringing what is merely potential into actuality. It is a surplus of enrichment. Not a desperate search for what she lacks. If the higher relied on the lower for its completion, the entire cosmological structure would invert. The lower world does not complete the higher world. The chain must run by overflow downward. That brings us to the highest level discussed in the text, the first creator. The author of The Defense had argued about the specific name of perfection and where it falls. Suggesting that names should not pile up unnecessarily. There is a multiplicity of meanings based on how we look at them. but there is only one true thing on the side of the named. The creator, Al-Mubdi, is not a thing with attributes attached to it. The creator is the one upon whom the names fall according to the additions. The text also addresses the action of the first creator. The defense had argued that every doer acts in pursuit of a goal. In movement and in order. The author clarifies that the first creator's action is entirely metaphorical. It is not in motion, it is not in order, and it is not part of a system. The first is the last. The beginning of it is the middle and the middle of it is the end. The order and system we observe in our universe are just reflections drawn from the first intellect. which perceives truth as a whole, untethered from sequence or arrangement. Let me offer a comparison to ground how the first intellect perceives time and order compared to how we perceive it. We do. Human experience in the physical world is a sequential progression. We experience past, present, and future in a line. One event must lead to the next. That is order. But the first intellect's perception is untethered from that sequence. It grasps the entire progression simultaneously in a single glance. That captures the structural difference. The lower ranks draw their order from what is above order. The first intellect is not a vehicle moving through time. It perceives only the truth. The soul, when united with the intellect, sees as the intellect sees as a whole. It is only when the soul lets deformed things of dark nature press in upon her that she loses that character. And becomes bound by sequential need.
