# The Method of Living and the Closing Prayer

## The Other Half of the Eight

Ghazali's "Ayyuhal Walad" shifts from cautions to active methods.

| Cautions (Restraint) | Methods (Active Orientation) |
| :------------------- | :--------------------------- |
| Debate               | Relationship with God        |
| Preaching            | Relationship with People     |
| Rulers               | Relationship with Knowledge  |
| Gifts                | Relationship with Provision  |

Underlying Principle:
Live such that your treatment of God, creatures, hours, and bread all align.

## The First Method: Relating to God

Analogy: Your Servant's Conduct

| Your Servant to You                                    | You to Allah (Your Lord)                               |
| :----------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------- |
| You would like and commend it                          | Allah would like and commend it                        |
| You would not like it (e.g., half-effort, hiding)      | Allah would not like it                                |
| You did not create, raise, or deeply own your servant  | Allah created, raised, and deeply owns you             |
| Your obligation to Allah is incomparably greater       | Yet, your inner test for your servant is often sharper |

Prophetic Teaching (Hadith of Gabriel):
Worship Allah as if you see Him → Though you do not see Him → He certainly sees you.
This is *Ihsan* (spiritual excellence).
Conduct service as if God is a witness, because He is.

## The Second Method: Relating to People

Horizontal Mirror of the First Method:

| Your Action Towards Others | Their Action Towards You (Desired) |
| :------------------------- | :--------------------------------- |
| Treat others in a way      | You would like it                  |
| You would not be grieved   | If they did the same to you        |

Hadith Qudsi:
My slave's faith is never complete → unless he likes for all human beings → what he likes for himself.

Prophet's Teaching:
None of you truly believes → until he loves for his brother → what he loves for himself. (Sahih Bukhari, Hadith 13; Sahih Muslim, Hadith 45)

Imam Ali's Expansion (Nahj al-Balagha, Letter 31):
Make yourself the measure between you and others.
Love for them what you love for yourself.
Dislike for them what you dislike for yourself.
Do not oppress, as you do not want to be oppressed.
Do good, as you wish good for yourself.
Consider bad for yourself what you consider bad for others.
Be content with others as you wish them to be content with you.
Do not say what you do not know.
Do not say to others what you would dislike being said to you.

This architecture is reflected in daily worship: praying for self *within* the well-being of all creatures.

## The Third Method: Study What Will Benefit You

Thought Experiment 1: One Week Left to Live

| If you had one week left...                                   | You would NOT study...                                   | You WOULD study...                                         |
| :------------------------------------------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------- |
| What knowledge will benefit me for what is coming?            | Religious debate, dictionaries, linguistics, poetry      | Contemplation of the heart, recognizing soul's faults      |
| You would understand these will not help you in what is coming |                                                          | Turn away from world, purify heart, embellish with love of Allah |
|                                                               |                                                          | Worship and service to God, possibly not leaving house     |

Thought Experiment 2: King's Visit Next Week

| If the King visits your house next week...               | Your focus would be...                                   |
| :------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------- |
| You would spend the whole week cleaning everything.      | That the king's glance should not fall on something dirty. |
| Clean house, body, dress, walls, floors.                 |                                                          |

Prophet's Teaching:
Verily, Allah does not look at your faces and your actions → but He looks at your hearts and your intentions. (Sahih Muslim, Book 45, Hadith 14)

When the One who matters looks at your heart, why clean anything else?

Knowledge Types:

*   *Fard al-Ayn*: Personal obligation (e.g., knowledge of heart matters, from *Ihya Ulum al-Din*).
*   *Fard Kifaya*: Communal obligation (other branches of knowledge).

Student needs enough knowledge to obey God's commands. Further study (*tawfeeq*) is a bonus.

## The Fourth Method: Do Not Stockpile Food

Principle: Do not accumulate more than one year's worth of provision for your family.

Prophet's Example:
Accumulated one year's provision for *some* wives.
Prayed: O Allah, make this provision sufficient for the family of Muhammad. (Sahih Bukhari, Hadith 6460; Sahih Muslim, Hadith 7459)

*   For wives with weak *Tawakkul* (reliance on God) → Stockpiled for one year.
*   For wives with strong *Tawakkul* (e.g., Aisha) → No stockpile, not even for one day.

The stockpile is calibrated to the weakness of the relier.
One-year ceiling is an upper bound, not a target.
Target: need less buffer as *Tawakkul* grows.

Stockpiling beyond a year → Sign of trusting accumulation over Allah's promise.

Prophet's Teaching:
If you trusted in Allah with true reliance → He would provide for you as He provides for the birds.
Birds leave hungry → return full. (Sunan Tirmidhi, Hadith 2344)
Birds work, but do not store for twenty years.
Less storage tracks more trust.

## The Supplication

Ghazali's Instructions:
1.  Consult *Sahih Sitta* (canonical hadith collections) for supplications.
2.  Consult supplications from *Ahl al-Bayt* (e.g., *Sahifa al-Sajjadiyya*).
3.  Recite Ghazali's supplication, especially after every prayer (*Salah*).

Ghazali's Supplication (Key Themes):

| Requests for Blessings & Qualities | Requests for Protection & Guidance |
| :--------------------------------- | :--------------------------------- |
| Completion & abundance of blessings | Salvation upon death               |
| Permanence of inner & outer purity | Reform of deeds                    |
| Inclusion of mercy in all matters  | Safety in days & nights            |
| Attainment of health               | Mercy in houses & possessions      |
| Increase in sustenance             | Covering sins with forgiveness     |
| Happiness & real success in life   | Correction of faults               |
| Completion of good qualities       | Confidence & reliance on You       |
| Most common reward                 | Perseverance in path to You        |
| Sweetest bounty                    | Safety from shameful deeds         |
| Most plentiful favor               | Lessening of sin burdens           |
| Best deed                          | Life of the pious ones             |
| Most beneficial knowledge          | Sufficiency in all affairs         |
| Most plentiful sustenance          | Protection from evil               |
|                                    | Liberation from fire, debts, oppression |

Divine Names Invoked:
O Victorious One. O Forgiving One. O Generous One. O Coverer of faults. O Tolerant One. O Powerful One. O Magnificent One. O You Who repays. O Allah. O Allah. O Allah. O Compassionate One in this world. O Merciful One in the next life. O Most Merciful of the Merciful.

Closing Salutation:
Mercy and blessings upon Muhammad, his descendants, and companions.
All praise belongs to Allah, the Lord of all the Universes.

## Conclusion

The supplication is a summary of the letter's teachings in prayer form.
It asks for inner qualities: sincerity, reliance, *taqwa*, mercy, best deeds, beneficial knowledge.
It seeks safety from the traps cautioned against earlier.
It reinforces the desired inner state.

The supplication structures daily life:
Recitation after every prayer → keeps letter alive in mouth → keeps inner orientation alive in heart → heart performs the work.

The path is hard, but the work, if undertaken, yields the promised outcome.
The seeker who acts on the four refusals and four methods, with a guide and daily supplication, will live the answer to "which knowledge will save me?"
