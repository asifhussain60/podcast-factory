# Slide Framing: The Four Cautions: Avoiding Traps for Scholars

## Purpose
This slide deck must visually represent Imam al-Ghazali's four cautions, illustrating the hidden harms and corrupting influences each activity poses to a scholar's spiritual integrity. It needs to show how these seemingly innocuous actions can subtly shift intentions and lead to spiritual downfall, something a linear audio explanation cannot fully convey.

## Steering phrases (apply all of the following)
- Diagram the causal chain of harm for each caution.
- Illustrate the "trap" metaphor for each caution.
- Use flowcharts or decision trees to represent conditional advice.
- Depict the internal transformation or corruption of the scholar's heart.
- Show the contrast between right and wrong intentions/outcomes.

## Slide types required
- **Causal Chain Diagram:** For "The first caution: do not enter religious debate," showing how debate leads to ostentation, jealousy, etc.
- **Decision Tree/Flowchart:** For "Categories of patients: who can be helped," illustrating the diagnostic process for engaging with questioners.
- **Contrast Diagram (Before/After):** For "The second caution: do not become a preacher," showing the difference between a sincere preacher and one driven by ostentation, perhaps using the "flood" analogy.
- **Metaphorical Diagram (Proximity/Distance):** For "The third caution: stay away from rulers," visually representing the corrupting influence of proximity to power.
- **Causal Loop Diagram:** For "The fourth caution: do not accept gifts from rulers," showing how gifts lead to love for the giver, prayers for long life, and extended oppression.
- **Summary Matrix/Table:** To synthesize the core principle running through all four cautions, showing how each activity corrupts the scholar's value system.

## Do not
- Restate the audio discussion in bullet-point slides.
- Produce "title + stock-photo illustration" slides.
- Use generic relationship labels ("influences", "relates to") — name the specific relationship.
