# Augmented — ch05-method-and-closing-prayer (knowledge stage: Quran refs verified from the Arabic spine)

Here is the text normalized to the House Voice standard:

Here are four essential practices. First, conduct your relationship with Allah as you would wish a servant to conduct theirs with you. If a servant's actions would please you, and you would not withhold your goodwill or become angry, then act similarly. What displeases you from a paid servant will displease Allah from you, for He is your true Lord.

Second, in all your interactions with people, treat them as you would wish to be treated. A person's faith is incomplete until they desire for others what they desire for themselves.

Third, if you pursue knowledge, let it be knowledge that rectifies your heart and purifies your soul. Imagine you knew your life would not extend beyond a week. You would not then occupy yourself with jurisprudence, argumentation, foundational principles, or scholastic theology, knowing these sciences would not truly benefit you. Instead, you would focus on guarding your heart, understanding the soul's nature, detaching from worldly distractions, purifying your soul from blameworthy traits, and dedicating yourself to the love and worship of Allah, cultivating good character. Not a single day or night passes for a person without the possibility of death.

O youth, listen to another word from me and reflect upon it, that you may find salvation. If you were informed that the Sultan would visit you within a week, I know you would dedicate that time solely to preparing everything you knew the Sultan's gaze would fall upon—your clothing, your body, your house, its furnishings, and other details. Now, consider the deeper meaning of my words; you have understanding, and a single hint suffices the discerning. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: "Verily Allah the Exalted does not look upon your form nor upon your deeds, but He looks upon your hearts and your intentions." If you seek knowledge of the heart's states, consult *The Revival of the Religious Sciences* and my other writings. This knowledge is incumbent upon everyone, while other knowledge is required only of some, except for what fulfills Allah's commands. May Allah grant you success in attaining it.

And fourth, do not accumulate more worldly provisions than what suffices for a year. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, would prepare a year's provisions for some of his wives' households, saying: "O Allah, make the provision of the family of Muhammad sufficient." However, he did not do this for every household. He would provide it for those wives in whose hearts he perceived a weakness of faith. But for a steadfast companion, he would not even prepare the necessities for a day and a half.

O youth, I have indeed written in this treatise what you requested, and you must act upon its guidance. Do not forget to mention me in your righteous prayers. As for the specific supplication you asked for, seek it among the prayers of the perfected ones. And recite this supplication at its appointed times, especially at the conclusion of your prayers:

O Allah, truly I seek from thee of grace the most perfect, and of protection the most abiding, and of mercy the most encompassing, and of forgiveness its attainment, and of living the most comforting, and of life the happiest, and of beneficence the most perfect, and of blessing the most general, and of favor the sweetest, and of kindliness the most beneficial. O Allah, be for us and be not against us. O Allah, seal with happiness our appointed time and confirm in excess our hopes, and unite in forgiveness our mornings and our evenings, and bring to thy mercy our final state and what is for us, and pour out the gift of thy pardon upon our transgressions, and bestow upon us the correction of our blemishes, and make piety our provision for the journey; in thy religion is our endeavor, and upon thee is our trust and our confidence. Fix us firmly upon the path of uprightness and protect us in this world from acts necessitating regrets on the day of judgment and lighten on us the burden of the sins and bestow upon us the life of the righteous, and avert and dispel from us the evils and set free our necks and the necks of our fathers and our mothers from the fire in thy mercy, O thou Illustrious One, thou Coverer of sins, thou Gracious One, thou Forgiving One, thou Benevolent One, thou Mighty One, O Allah, O Allah, thou Most Compassionate of the Compassionate, and in Him we trust.
