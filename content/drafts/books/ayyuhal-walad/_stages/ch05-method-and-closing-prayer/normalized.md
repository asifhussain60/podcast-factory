# Normalized — ch05-method-and-closing-prayer (house voice via Gemini)

First, conduct your dealings with Allah the Exalted as you would want your own servant to deal with you. If you would be displeased with a paid servant for certain actions, know that Allah, your true Lord, is likewise displeased with those same actions from you. Second, in all your interactions with people, treat them as you would wish to be treated. A person's faith is not complete until they desire for others what they desire for themselves. Third, if you pursue knowledge, ensure it is knowledge that rectifies your heart and purifies your soul. Imagine you knew your life would not extend beyond a week. You would not then occupy yourself with jurisprudence, argumentation, rudimentary principles, scholastic theology, or similar fields, knowing these sciences would not benefit you in that moment. Instead, you would focus on guarding your heart, understanding the soul's qualities, detaching from worldly entanglements, purifying your soul of blameworthy traits, and dedicating yourself to the love and worship of Allah the Exalted, striving for good character. Not a single day or night passes for a person without the possibility of death.

O youth, hear another word from me and reflect on it, that you may find salvation. If you were told that the Sultan would visit you within a week, I know you would spend that time solely preparing what you knew the Sultan would see: your clothing, your body, your house, your furnishings, and other things. Now, consider what I am referring to, for you have understanding, and a single word is enough for the discerning. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: "Verily Allah the Exalted does not look upon your form nor upon your deeds, but he looks upon your hearts and your intentions." If you desire knowledge of the heart's states, refer to *The Revival of the Religious Sciences* and my other writings. This knowledge is obligatory for everyone, while other knowledge is obligatory only for some, except for what fulfills the ordinances of Allah the Exalted. May Allah grant you success in attaining it.

And fourth, do not accumulate more worldly possessions than what suffices for a year. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, made such provisions for some of his households, saying: "O Allah, make the provision of the family of Mohammed sufficient." However, he did not make this provision for every household. He would provide it for those wives in whose hearts he perceived a weakness of faith. But for a steadfast companion, he would not even prepare for her the necessities of a day and a half.

O youth, I have indeed written in this treatise what you requested, and you must act upon its contents. Do not forget to mention me in your righteous prayers. As for the specific supplication you asked for, seek it among the prayers of the perfect ones. Recite this supplication at its proper times, especially at the conclusion of your prayers:

O Allah, truly I seek from thee of grace the most perfect, and of protection the most abiding, and of mercy the most encompassing, and of forgiveness its attainment, and of living the most comforting, and of life the happiest, and of beneficence the most perfect, and of blessing the most general, and of favor the sweetest, and of kindliness the most beneficial. O Allah, be for us and be not against us. O Allah, seal with happiness our appointed time and confirm in excess our hopes, and unite in forgiveness our mornings and our evenings, and bring to thy mercy our final state and what is for us, and pour out the gift of thy pardon upon our transgressions, and bestow upon us the correction of our blemishes, and make piety our provision for the journey; in thy religion is our endeavor, and upon thee is our trust and our confidence. Fix us firmly upon the path of uprightness and protect us in this world from acts necessitating regrets on the day of judgment and lighten on us the burden of the sins and bestow upon us the life of the righteous, and avert and dispel from us the evils and set free our necks and the necks of our fathers and our mothers from the fire in thy mercy, O thou Illustrious One, thou Coverer of sins, thou Gracious One, thou Forgiving One, thou Benevolent One, thou Mighty One, O Allah, O Allah, thou Most Compassionate of the Compassionate, and in Him we trust.
