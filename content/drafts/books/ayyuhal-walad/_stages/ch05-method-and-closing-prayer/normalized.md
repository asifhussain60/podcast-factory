# Normalized — ch05-method-and-closing-prayer (house voice via Gemini)

First, make your dealings with Allah such that you would be pleased if your servant treated you similarly, without withdrawing your goodwill or becoming angry. What you wouldn't accept from your servant, Allah, your true Lord, won't accept from you.

Second, in all your dealings with people, treat them as you would wish to be treated. A worshipper's faith is incomplete until they love for others what they love for themselves.

Third, if you study knowledge, let it be knowledge that rectifies your heart and purifies your soul. If you knew your life would not last more than a week, you would not occupy yourself with jurisprudence, argumentation, rudimentary principles, scholastic theology, and similar subjects. You would know these sciences would not enrich you. Instead, you would focus on guarding your heart, understanding the soul's attributes, detaching from worldly entanglements, purifying your soul of blameworthy qualities, and dedicating yourself to the love and worship of Allah, distinguishing yourself with good character. No day or night passes for a worshipper without the possibility of death.

Young person, hear another word from me and consider it, that you may find salvation. If you were told the Sultan would visit you in a week, I know you would spend that time preparing everything his eyes might fall upon: your clothes, your body, your house, your furnishings, and more. Now consider what I refer to, for you have understanding, and a single word suffices the clever. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: "Verily Allah the Exalted does not look upon your form nor upon your deeds, but he looks upon your hearts and your intentions." If you desire knowledge of the states of the heart, look in the *Ihya Ulum al-Din* (The Revival of the Religious Sciences) and other of my writings. This knowledge is required of all; other knowledge is required only of some, except what fulfills the ordinances of Allah. May Allah grant you success in attaining it.

Fourth, do not accumulate more worldly possessions than you need for a year, as the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, did for certain of his households, saying: "O Allah, make the provision of the family of Muhammad sufficient." He did not prepare this for every household, but only for those whose faith he knew to be weak. For a steadfast companion, he would not even prepare for a day and a half's necessities.

Young person, I have written in this treatise what you requested, and you must act upon it. Do not forget to mention me in your righteous prayers. As for the supplication you asked for, seek it among the supplications of the perfect. Recite this supplication at its appointed times, especially at the conclusion of your prayers:

O Allah, truly I seek from You the most perfect grace, the most abiding protection, the most encompassing mercy, the fulfillment of forgiveness, the most comforting life, the happiest existence, the most perfect beneficence, the most general blessing, the sweetest favor, and the most beneficial kindness. O Allah, be for us, not against us. O Allah, seal our appointed time with happiness, confirm our hopes in abundance, unite our mornings and evenings in forgiveness, and bring our final state and what is for us to Your mercy. Pour out the gift of Your pardon upon our transgressions, bestow upon us the correction of our faults, and make piety our provision for the journey. In Your religion is our endeavor, and upon You is our trust and confidence. Fix us firmly upon the path of uprightness and protect us in this world from acts necessitating regrets on the Day of Judgment. Lighten for us the burden of sins and bestow upon us the life of the righteous. Avert and dispel from us all evils, and free our necks and the necks of our fathers and our mothers from the Fire, by Your mercy, O Illustrious One, O Coverer of sins, O Gracious One, O Forgiving One, O Benevolent One, O Mighty One. O Allah, O Allah, O Most Compassionate of the Compassionate, and in You we trust.
