# Normalized — ch05-method-and-closing-prayer (house voice via Gemini)

First, you must ensure your dealings with Allah the Exalted are such that, if your own servant treated you in a way that pleased you, without causing you to withdraw your goodwill or become angry. What you would not accept from a paid servant, Allah the Exalted will not accept from you, for He is your true Lord. Second, in all your interactions with people, treat them as you would wish to be treated. A worshipper's faith is incomplete until they love for others what they love for themselves. Third, if you read or study knowledge, it must be knowledge that corrects your heart and purifies your soul. If you knew your life would not last more than a week, you would not occupy yourself with jurisprudence, argumentation, rudimentary principles, scholastic theology, or similar fields. You would know these sciences would not enrich you. Instead, you would focus on guarding your heart, understanding the soul's attributes, detaching from worldly entanglements, purifying your soul from blameworthy moral qualities, and dedicating yourself to the love and worship of Allah the Exalted, distinguishing yourself with good qualities. No day or night passes for a worshipper without the possibility of death.

Young person, hear another word from me and consider it, that you may find salvation. If you were told the Sultan would visit you within a week, I know you would spend that time rectifying everything you knew the Sultan's gaze would fall upon: your clothing, your body, your house, your furnishings, and other things. Now consider what I refer to, for you have understanding, and a single word suffices the clever. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: "Verily Allah the Exalted does not look upon your form nor upon your deeds, but he looks upon your hearts and your intentions." If you desire knowledge of the states of the heart, consult the Ihya (the Vitalizing of the Sciences of Religion) and my other writings. This knowledge is required of all; other knowledge is required only of some, except that which fulfills the ordinances of Allah the Exalted. May Allah grant you success in attaining it.

Fourth, do not accumulate more worldly possessions than what suffices for a year. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, prepared this for certain of his wives, saying: "O Allah, make the provision of the family of Mohammed sufficient." He did not prepare this for every wife, but only for those whose hearts he knew had weakness (of faith). For a steadfast companion, he would not even prepare the necessities for a day and a half.

Young person, I have indeed written in this treatise what you requested, and you must act upon its contents. Do not forget to mention me in your righteous petitions. As for the specific supplication you asked for, seek it among the supplications of the perfect. Recite this supplication at its appointed times, especially at the conclusion of your prayers:

O Allah, I truly seek from You the most perfect grace, and the most abiding protection, and the most encompassing mercy, and the attainment of Your forgiveness, and the most comforting life, and the happiest existence, and the most perfect beneficence, and the most general blessing, and the sweetest favor, and the most beneficial kindliness. O Allah, be for us, and not against us. O Allah, seal our appointed time with happiness, confirm our hopes in abundance, unite our mornings and evenings in forgiveness, and bring our final state and what is for us to Your mercy. Pour out the gift of Your pardon upon our transgressions, bestow upon us the correction of our blemishes, and make piety our provision for the journey. In Your religion is our endeavor, and upon You is our trust and our confidence. Fix us firmly upon the path of uprightness, and protect us in this world from acts necessitating regrets on the Day of Judgment. Lighten for us the burden of sins, and bestow upon us the life of the righteous. Avert and dispel from us all evils, and set free our necks and the necks of our fathers and our mothers from the Fire, by Your mercy, O Illustrious One, O Coverer of sins, O Gracious One, O Forgiving One, O Benevolent One, O Mighty One. O Allah, O Allah, O Most Compassionate of the Compassionate. And in You we trust.
