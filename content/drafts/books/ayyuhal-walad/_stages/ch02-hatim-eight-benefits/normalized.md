# Normalized — ch02-hatim-eight-benefits (house voice via Gemini)

O youth, if you know this tradition, there is no need for further knowledge. Consider another story: Hatim al-Asamm was a companion of Shaqîq al-Balkhi (may Allah have mercy on them both). One day, Shaqîq asked him, "You have been with me for thirty years—what have you gained?" Hatim replied, "I have gained eight insights from knowledge, and they are enough for me. I hope for my salvation and safety through them."

Shaqîq asked, "What are they?" Hatim replied:

"First, I observed humanity and saw that everyone had someone they loved and longed for. Some of these loved ones accompanied them to their deathbed, others to the edge of the grave. But then each returned, leaving the person alone and lonely, with no one to enter the grave and comfort them. I realized that only good deeds would remain. So, I made good deeds my beloved, to be a light and comfort in my grave, never leaving me alone.

"Second, I saw people following their desires and rushing toward the cravings of their souls. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: "But as for whoever has feared the majesty of his Lord, and has refrained his soul from lust, truly the Garden shall be his dwelling place." I was convinced that the Quran was sincere truth. So, I hastened to deny my soul, combating its passionate desires until it became trained in obedience to Allah the Exalted and grew tractable.

"Third, I saw everyone striving to collect worldly vanities, then grasping them tightly. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: "What is with you vanishes, but what is with Allah abides." So I freely gave my worldly possessions for the sake of Allah the Exalted, distributing them among the poor to be a treasure for me with Allah the Exalted.

"Fourth, I saw some people believing their honor and glory lay in the size of their family and kin, and they were deluded by them. Others considered it to be in their abundant wealth and numerous children, and they boasted. Still others thought glory came from seizing people's wealth through violence, oppressing them, and shedding their blood. And some believed it lay in squandering and dissipating riches through prodigality. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: "The most worthy of you in the sight of Allah is he who fears him most." So I chose reverent fear, convinced that the Quran is sincere truth and that their thoughts and calculations were empty and fleeting.

"Fifth, I saw people censuring and slandering one another, and I realized this stemmed from envy over wealth, rank, and knowledge. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: "It is We who divide their substance among them in this world's life." And I knew that the distribution was from Allah the Exalted.

"Sixth, I saw people treating one another with enmity for various motives or purposes. So I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: "Truly Satan is your enemy; for an enemy then hold him." And I knew that enmity was permissible only toward Satan.

"Seventh, I saw everyone struggling energetically and striving excessively to seek provisions and a livelihood, often falling into doubt, forbidden acts, debasing themselves, and diminishing their worth. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: "There is no moving thing on earth whose nourishment dependeth not upon Allah." So I knew that my provision depended on Allah, and He had guaranteed it. I busied myself in worshipping Him and cut off my covetousness for all else.

"Eighth, I saw everyone relying on some created thing: some on dinars and dirhams, some on property, some on trade and craft, and others on similar created things. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: "Truly whosoever putteth his trust in Allah, He will be sufficient. Truly Allah will attain his purpose. He has made for everything a fixed period." So I relied upon Allah, and He is my sufficiency and an excellent guardian."

Shaqîq said, "May Allah grant you success."

O youth, you have understood from these two accounts that you do not need to multiply knowledge. Now I will show you what is obligatory for the traveler on the path of truth. Know that it is indispensable for the traveler to have a shaykh (spiritual guide) and tutor to remove evil qualities through training and replace them with excellent character. This training resembles a plowman who digs out thorns and removes wild plants to stimulate growth and ensure perfect thriving.

The traveler needs a shaykh to train and guide them on the path of Allah. The sign of a shaykh fit to succeed the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) is that he is learned—though not every learned person is fit for this role. I will give you some general indications so that not everyone can claim to be a learned guide. Such a person is one who distances himself from the love of the world and rank, who has succeeded a discerning individual whose lineage of successorship traces back to the Lord of the apostles. He has excelled in disciplining himself through scarcity of food, sleep, and speech, and through abundant prayer, alms, and fasting. Following the discerning shaykh, he makes good character traits his way of life, such as endurance, thanksgiving, trustfulness, conviction, generosity, contentment, tranquility of soul, moderation, humility, knowledge, veracity, modesty, trustworthiness, gravity, quietness, and staidness. Such a shaykh is a light from the lights of the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him), worthy of imitation. However, the presence of such a person is rare, more precious than red sulfur. Whoever is fortunate enough to find such a shaykh, and is accepted by him, must honor him outwardly and inwardly.

Outward honor means not disputing or arguing with him, not multiplying supererogatory prayers in his presence, and doing what the shaykh commands according to one's capacity and ability.

Inner honor means not inwardly denying anything heard and outwardly accepted from him, neither in deed nor in word, lest one be branded with hypocrisy. If this is not possible, one should leave his companionship until one's inner life aligns with the outer. One should guard against associating with evil people to curtail the influence of the Satans among jinn and mankind from the court of one's heart, and to be purified from the stain of Satanic filth. In all cases, one will prefer poverty over wealth.

Know that becoming a Sufi (one who practices Sufism, Islamic mysticism) has two characteristics: uprightness with Allah the Exalted, and tranquility with humanity. Whoever is upright, improves their character among people, and treats them with forbearance, is a Sufi. Uprightness means offering the pleasures of one's soul as a ransom for the sake of one's soul. Good conduct among people means not burdening them according to your own desire, but burdening yourself according to their desire, as long as they do not violate the sacred law.

You asked me about devotion; it comprises three things: first, the careful observance of the sacred law's commands; second, satisfaction with the decree, fate, and lot from Allah the Exalted; and third, forsaking self-pleasure to seek the pleasure of Allah the Exalted.

You asked me about trust: it is to fortify your belief in Allah the Exalted regarding what He has promised. This means believing that what He has fated for you will come to you without fail, even if everyone in the world tries to prevent it. And what is not written for you, you shall not attain, even if the whole world helps you.

You asked me about sincerity: it is that all your works are done for Allah the Exalted, your heart neither resting content with people's praise nor despairing with their censure. Know that hypocrisy is born from exalting humanity. The cure is to see people as compelled to labor under divine decree, regarding them like inanimate objects in their inability to attain contentment or misery, in order to escape hypocrisy in their sight. As long as you consider them to possess power and free will, hypocrisy will not be far from you.

O youth, some of your remaining questions are covered in my writings, so seek them there. Setting others down in writing is forbidden. Work by what you know, and what you do not know will be revealed to you.
