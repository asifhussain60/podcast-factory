# Augmented — ch02-hatim-eight-benefits (knowledge stage: Quran refs verified from the Arabic spine)

O youth, if you understand this tradition, you need no further knowledge. Consider another story: Hatim al-Asamm was a companion of Shaqiq al-Balkhi (may Allah have mercy on them both). One day, Shaqiq asked him: "These thirty years you have associated with me—what have you gained in them?" Hatim replied, "I have gained eight benefits from knowledge, and they suffice me, for I hope for my salvation and safety through them." Shaqiq asked, "What are they?" Hatim replied:

"First, I observed humanity and saw that everyone had someone or something they loved and longed for. Some loved ones accompanied them to their deathbed, others to the edge of the grave. Then, each returned, leaving them alone and lonely, with no one to enter the grave and comfort them. I realized that only good deeds would accompany a person into the grave and comfort them. So, I made good deeds my beloved, a light for me in my grave, to comfort me and never leave me alone.

"Second, I saw humanity following their desires and hastening after the cravings of their souls. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: 'But as for whoever has feared the majesty of his Lord, and has refrained his soul from lust, truly the Garden shall be his dwelling place.' Convinced that the Quran spoke the sincere truth, I hastened to deny my soul, combating its passionate desires until it became trained and tractable in obedience to Allah the Exalted.

"Third, I saw everyone striving to collect worldly vanities, then grasping them tightly. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: 'What is with you vanishes, but what is with Allah abides.' So I freely gave my worldly possessions for the sake of Allah the Exalted, distributing them among the poor to become a treasure for me with Allah the Exalted.

"Fourth, I saw that some people believed their honor and glory lay in the multitude of their family and kin, becoming deluded by them. Others found it in their wealth and numerous children, boasting about them. Still others considered glory to be in seizing people's riches through violence, oppressing them, and shedding their blood. And some believed it lay in squandering and dissipating wealth through prodigality. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: 'The most worthy of you in the sight of Allah is he who fears him most.' So I chose reverent fear, convinced that the Quran spoke the sincere truth and that their thoughts and calculations were empty and fleeting.

"Fifth, I saw people censuring and slandering one another, realizing this stemmed from envy over wealth, rank, and knowledge. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: 'It is we who divide their substance among them in this world's life.' And I knew that this division was from Allah the Exalted.

"Sixth, I saw people treating one another with enmity for various motives. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: 'Truly Satan is your enemy; for an enemy then hold him.' And I knew that enmity was permissible for no one other than Satan.

"Seventh, I saw everyone striving energetically and excessively to seek provisions and a livelihood, often falling into doubt, forbidden acts, debasing themselves, and diminishing their worth. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: 'There is no moving thing on earth whose nourishment dependeth not upon Allah.' And I knew that my provision depended on Allah, and He had guaranteed it. So I busied myself in worshipping Him and cut off my covetousness for all else besides Him.

"Eighth, I saw everyone relying on some created thing: some on dinars and dirhams, some on property, some on trade and craft, and others on similar created things. I reflected on the saying of the Exalted: 'Truly whosoever putteth his trust in Allah, He will be sufficient. Truly Allah will attain his purpose. He has made for everything a fixed period.' So I relied upon Allah, and He is my sufficiency and an excellent guardian."

Shaqiq said, "May Allah grant you success, O youth. You have understood from these words that you need not multiply knowledge. Now, I will show you what is obligatory for the seeker on the path of truth. Know that it is indispensable for the seeker to have a shaykh as a guide and tutor, one who can, through training, help him expel evil qualities and replace them with excellent character. This training is like a plowman who digs out thorns and removes wild plants to stimulate growth and ensure perfect thriving. Thus, it is necessary for the seeker to have a shaykh to train and guide him to the path of Allah.

"The sign of a shaykh fit to be a successor to the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) is that he is learned – though not every learned person is fit for this role. I will show you certain general indications so that not everyone can claim to be a learned guide. We say, such a person is one who has removed himself far from love of the world and love of rank. He has succeeded a discerning person, tracing his successorship to the Lord of the apostles. He has excelled in disciplining himself through scarcity of food, sleep, and speech, and through abundance of prayer, alms, and fasting. Following a discerning shaykh, he has made good character qualities his way of life, such as endurance, thanksgiving, trustfulness, conviction, generosity, contentment, tranquility of soul, moderation, humility, knowledge, veracity, modesty, trustworthiness, gravity, quietness, and staidness. Such a person is a light from the lights of the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) and is worthy to be imitated. However, the presence of such a shaykh is rare, more precious than red sulfur. Whoever is fortunate enough to find such a shaykh, and is accepted by him, must honor him outwardly and inwardly.

"Outward honor means not disputing or arguing with him, not multiplying supererogatory prayers in his presence, and doing what the shaykh commands according to one's capacity and ability. Inner honor means not inwardly denying anything heard and accepted outwardly, neither in deed nor in word, lest one be branded with hypocrisy. If this is not possible, one should leave his companionship until one's inner life aligns with the outer. One should also guard against associating with evil people, to curtail the influence of jinn and human satans from the heart's domain, and to be purified from the stain of satanic filth. In all circumstances, he will prefer poverty over wealth.

"Know then that becoming a Sufi has two characteristics: uprightness with Allah the Exalted, and tranquility with humanity. Whoever is upright, improves his character among people, and treats them with forbearance, is a Sufi. Uprightness means offering the pleasures of one's soul as a ransom for the sake of one's soul. And good conduct among people means not burdening them according to your own desire, but burdening yourself according to their desire, as long as they do not violate the sacred law.

"You asked me about devotion; it comprises three things: first, careful observance of the sacred law's commands; second, satisfaction with Allah the Exalted's decree, fate, and lot; and third, forsaking self-pleasure to seek the pleasure of Allah the Exalted.

"You asked me about trust: it is to fortify your belief in Allah the Exalted regarding what He has promised. That is, to believe that what He has fated for you will come to you without fail, even if everyone in the world tries to prevent it; and what is not written for you, you shall not attain, even if the whole world helps you.

"You asked me about sincerity: it is that all your works are done for Allah the Exalted, your heart neither content with people's praise nor despairing at their censure. Know that hypocrisy is born from exalting humanity. The cure is to see them as compelled to labor under Allah's decree, and to regard them as inanimate objects in their inability to bring about contentment or misery, thereby escaping hypocrisy in their sight. As long as you regard them as possessing power and free will, hypocrisy will not be far from you.

"O youth, the remainder of your questions are partially covered in my writings; seek them there. Setting down others in writing is forbidden. Work by what you know, and what you do not know will be revealed to you."
