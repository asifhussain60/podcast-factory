# Normalized — ch01-frame-and-first-counsel (house voice via Gemini)

In the name of Allah, the Compassionate, the Merciful.

Praise be to Allah, Lord of the worlds, and a good end to the pious. Blessings upon His Prophet Muhammad and all his family.

Know that an advanced student diligently served the shaykh, Imam, ornament of religion, and proof of Islam, Abu Hamid Muhammad ibn Muhammad al-Ghazali (may Allah have mercy on him). He labored to acquire and study knowledge until he had mastered the subtleties of the sciences and perfected the virtues of the soul. One day, he reflected on his condition and thought, "I have truly read many sciences and spent my life learning and compiling them. Now I must discern which knowledge will benefit me tomorrow and bring me comfort in my grave, and which will not, so that I may abandon it. As the Apostle of Allah (peace and blessings be upon him) said, 'O Allah, I seek refuge in You from knowledge that does not benefit.'"

This idea persisted until he wrote to the honorable shaykh, the proof of Islam, Muhammad al-Ghazali (may Allah have mercy on him), seeking a *fatwa*, asking questions, and desiring advice and a supplication. He said, "Even though the shaykh's writings, like *Ihya* and other works, contain the answers to my questions, my purpose is for the shaykh to write my request in a small booklet to remain with me for the rest of my life. I will act according to its contents all my days, if Allah the Exalted wills." So the shaykh (may Allah the Exalted have mercy on him) wrote this epistle:

In the name of Allah, the Compassionate, the Merciful.

Know, O beloved and precious youth—may Allah prolong your days in His obedience and lead you in the path of His loved ones—that open letters of advice are drawn from the mine of the Apostle's Message (peace and blessings be upon him). If advice has reached you from it, what need have you of my advice? And if not, then tell me what you have attained in these past years.

O youth, among all that the Apostle of Allah (blessings and peace be upon him) advised his Congregation is his saying (peace and blessings be upon him): "The sign of Allah's withdrawal from His worshipper is his busying himself with what does not concern him. If a man has spent an hour of his life in other than that for which he was created, it is certainly fitting that his grief should be prolonged. And whoever has reached the age of forty, and his good does not surpass his evil, let him prepare for the fire."
