# The Guiding Shaykh and the Final Counsels

## The Guiding Shaykh: The Indispensable Guide

**Why the shaykh is necessary:**
- The traveller on the path to Allah cannot walk alone.
- The shaykh expels evil qualities by training and replaces them with excellent character.
- Training resembles the plowman who digs out thorns and removes wild plants to let the crop grow.

**The qualifications of a true guide:**

| Qualification | What It Means |
| :------------ | :------------ |
| Removed far from love of the world | Not attached to rank or wealth |
| Traces his line to a discerning predecessor | Unbroken chain of guidance |
| Disciplines himself: little food, little sleep, little speech | Abundance of prayer, alms, fasting |
| Lives the good qualities of character | Endurance, thanksgiving, generosity, humility, veracity, quietness, gravity |

**The two forms of honor a student must give:**
- *Outer honor:* No disputing. No excess supererogatory practice before the shaykh. Do what the shaykh commands within capacity.
- *Inner honor:* What you accept outwardly, do not deny inwardly. Inner hypocrisy — outward compliance, inner rejection — is the worst betrayal.

**Ghazali's warning:** The presence of such a shaykh is *rare — more precious than red sulphur.*

## The Unwritten Knowledge

**Ghazali's explicit limit:**

*"Work by what you know. There will be revealed to you what you do not know."*

**The promise is conditional:** It is not given to the idle. It comes through the practice of what is already known.

**Ghazali's Quranic chain:**
- *Surah Al-Hujurat, verse 5:* Had they waited patiently until you came out to them, it had been better for them.
- *Surah Al-Kahf, verse 70:* And do not ask me about anything until I mention it unto you — the condition Khidr, upon whom be peace, gave Moses.
- *Surah Al-Anbiya, verse 37:* I will show you My signs — do not be in a hurry.
- *Surah Fatir, verse 44:* Have they not travelled through the earth and seen?

**The structure of the command:**
1. Do not rush.
2. Wait for the appointed time.
3. Reach it by travelling — not by asking before the time.

## Give Your Spirit Without Reserve

**Dhu al-Nun al-Misri, may Allah have mercy upon him, to one of the students:**

*"If you are able to give your spirit without reserve, come. And if not, then do not busy yourself with the idle practices of Mysticism."*

**What the condition demands:**
- Not gradual improvement.
- Not partial commitment.
- Total self-offering — or nothing.

**The wonders of the path:** Those who travel it see wonders in every station. But the seeing is not given before the travelling.

## The Eight Admonitions

**Ghazali's framing:** Eight things — four to avoid, four to perform. *Receive them from me, lest your knowledge become your adversary on the Day of Resurrection.*

### Four to Avoid

**1. Argument:**

| Condition | Verdict |
| :-------- | :------ |
| Purpose is truth — not victory | Permitted |
| Indifferent whose tongue reveals it | Permitted |
| Private discussion preferred over public | Permitted |
| Any other motive | Great mischief |

*Argument is the source of: hypocrisy, envy, pride, malice, enmity, boasting.*

**Types of questioner — when not to answer:**

| Type | Cause | Verdict |
| :--- | :---- | :------ |
| Driven by envy and hate | Incurable | Withdraw. Do not attempt an answer. |
| Stupidity — thinks his confusion is the scholar's problem | Incurable | Do not attempt an answer. |
| Seeking guidance but unable to grasp | Dull, not hostile | Do not attempt an answer. |
| Intelligent seeker — no envy, no anger, genuine need | Curable | Answer is not only permitted — it is necessary. |

*"One hopes for the removal of every enmity — except enmity arising from envy."*

**2. Preaching and warning without authorization:**

Its mischief is much. Ghazali names it directly and returns to it in the next section.

**3. Association with rulers and the powerful:**

What such association costs: the heart aligns with the hand that feeds it.

**4. Acquiring more than what is needed of worldly goods:**

The acquisition becomes its own occupation. The seeker who accumulates stops seeking.

### Four to Perform

The four positive admonitions mirror the four to avoid: fill the space left by what is cut away with action, character, and sincere reliance.

## The Preacher's Trap

**What warning is, in Ghazali's account:**

The worshipper should remember the fire of the hereafter. His soul's shortcomings in service. His wasted past. The obstacles ahead — uncertainty of faith at the end, the seizing by the angel of death, the two angels in the grave, the stations of the Day of Resurrection, the crossing of the Sirat.

*This remembrance should disturb him from his tranquility. The boiling of these fires is warning.*

**The trap:** The preacher who seeks the crowd's agitation, the tears, the torn garments — who affects speech with expressions and allusions and poetry to produce an effect.

*"Allah the Exalted hates those who affect."*

**Ghazali's image:**

| Situation | What You Would Do | What the Affected Preacher Does |
| :-------- | :---------------- | :------------------------------ |
| A flood has entered a house where people live | "Beware! Beware! Flee from the flood!" | Informs the owner with affected expressions, jokes, and allusions |

*Would your heart desire, in that situation, to tell the owner with beautiful poetry?*

**The close:** The work of warning and the desire to be seen warning are two different acts. Only one of them serves the people in the house.
