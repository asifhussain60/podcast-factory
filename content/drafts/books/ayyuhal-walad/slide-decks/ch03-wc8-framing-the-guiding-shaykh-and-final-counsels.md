# Slide Framing: The Guiding Shaykh and the Final Counsels

## Purpose

This slide deck closes the series. It must visually represent the transition from Ghazali's diagnosis (Episodes 1–2) into the practical framework for beginning: find a guide, work by what you know, give without reserve. The eight admonitions are the structural spine of the episode, and the preacher's flood image is the close — not a summary.

## Steering phrases (apply all of the following)

- Show the shaykh's qualifications as a framework, not a list — distinguish each quality and what it signals.
- Render the inner/outer honor distinction as a two-axis diagram: what you show vs. what you hold inside.
- For the unwritten knowledge: show the Quranic chain as a sequence of commands — don't compress them.
- Dhu al-Nun's challenge should be rendered as a single confrontational frame — not illustrated away.
- The eight admonitions: four to avoid and four to perform. Show the structure. For the four-to-avoid, render the types of questioner as a decision framework (curable vs. incurable).
- The preacher's flood image closes the deck. Do not follow it with a summary slide.

## Slide types required

- **Qualifications framework:** The shaykh's requirements — outer qualities mapped to inner realities.
- **Two-axis honor diagram:** Outer honor vs. inner honor — what each requires, and what it means when inner contradicts outer.
- **Rarity frame:** The shaykh is rarer than red sulphur — render this as its own structural emphasis.
- **Sequential command diagram:** The Quranic chain on patience and travel — don't rush, wait, travel to reach.
- **Single confrontational frame:** Dhu al-Nun's challenge. Quote directly. No illustration required.
- **Eight admonitions structure:** Four-to-avoid and four-to-perform as a clear framework. The four-to-avoid include a questioner-typology sub-diagram (curable vs. incurable types).
- **Preacher's trap contrast:** The flood image — two columns: what you would do vs. what the affected preacher does.
- **Close frame:** Ghazali's final distinction — the work of warning vs. the desire to be seen warning. One frame. No summary.

## Do not

- Add a motivational summary after the flood image.
- Render the eight admonitions as a flat numbered list without internal structure.
- Compress the Quranic chain into a single citation.
- Soften Dhu al-Nun's challenge or translate it into gentler language.
