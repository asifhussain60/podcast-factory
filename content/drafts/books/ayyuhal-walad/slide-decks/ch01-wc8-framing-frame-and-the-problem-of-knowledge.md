# Slide Framing: The Frame and the Problem of Knowledge

## Purpose

This slide deck must visually represent Ghazali's core opening argument: that the educated Muslim who has learned without acting is trapped in the worst way, because the very learning has become a substitute for change. The slides must surface the diagnostic structure of the letter — occasion, counter-question, stacked evidence, three parables — and end at the soul's provision, not a motivational summary.

## Steering phrases (apply all of the following)

- Show the student's question as the diagnosis, not just the occasion.
- Illustrate each of the three parables as a structural visual unit — not bullet points.
- Distinguish the three types of intention (status, display, sincerity) as a clear branching diagram.
- Show the three realities (world / moment of death / afterlife) as a sequential passage, not a list.
- Render Hatim's contrast (madness vs. sadness vs. blessing) as a comparison, not a paragraph.

## Slide types required

- **Occasion diagram:** The student's background mapped to his question — show what the question reveals.
- **Diagnostic framework:** Knowledge without action as a disease — its presentations, its victims, the evidence Ghazali stacks.
- **Three parables as three visual units:** Each parable needs its own diagram — the sword-and-lion gap, the medicine-on-the-shelf gap, the donkey's load. Do not compress them into a single slide.
- **Intention branching diagram:** Two paths from the moment of study — seeking status vs. seeking transformation — with their respective outcomes.
- **The three realities as a passage:** World → moment of death → afterlife, each with what is carried through.
- **The close:** The direct summons — rendered as a single-frame image, not a bullet list.

## Do not

- Restate the audio discussion in bullet-point slides.
- Compress the three parables into a single list.
- Replace Ghazali's own language with paraphrase — use direct quotation for the strongest lines.
- Use generic relationship labels ("influences", "relates to") — name the specific relationship.
