# Slide Framing: Knowledge, Action, and the Path to Salvation

## Purpose
This slide deck must visually represent the core argument of Ghazali's first counsel: that knowledge without action is insufficient for salvation. It needs to illustrate the relationship between belief, deeds, and divine mercy, and the consequences of prioritizing intellectual pursuit over practical application. The slides should highlight the internal struggle and the transformative power of sincere action.

## Steering phrases (apply all of the following)
- Illustrate the relationship between knowledge, action, and salvation as a multi-stage process.
- Use metaphors and allegories to depict abstract spiritual concepts.
- Show the contrast between outward display and inner intention.
- Diagram the "four conditions of the seeker" as a foundational framework.

## Slide types required
- **Flowchart/Process Diagram:** To illustrate the student's journey from seeking knowledge to Ghazali's counsel and the subsequent path of action (Chapter: "The letter that triggered everything" and "The first hard truth: knowledge without action does not save you").
- **Metaphorical Illustration:** For the "lion in the desert" and "medicine on the shelf" analogies, showing the gap between knowing and doing (Chapter: "The lion in the desert, the medicine on the shelf").
- **Comparative Diagram:** To contrast the "angel and the worshipper" with transactional worship, highlighting intention (Chapter: "The angel and the worshipper").
- **Layered Diagram:** To show the "first question at the grave" and the distinction between outward appearance and inner heart (Chapter: "The first question at the grave").
- **Two-sided Comparison/Contrast:** To depict "madness, sadness, blessing" based on the intention behind knowledge acquisition (Chapter: "Madness, sadness, blessing").
- **Symbolic Representation:** For the "dove that knows" and the rooster, emphasizing vigilance versus heedlessness (Chapter: "The dove that knows").
- **Conceptual Model:** To define "worship means obedience, not display" and the role of *Mujahadah* (Chapter: "Worship means obedience, not display").
- **Numbered List/Framework Diagram:** To present "The four conditions of the seeker" and the wisdom of Shibli's one hadith (Chapter: "The four conditions of the seeker").

## Do not
- Restate the audio discussion in bullet-point slides.
- Produce "title + stock-photo illustration" slides.
- Use generic relationship labels ("influences", "relates to") — name the specific relationship.
