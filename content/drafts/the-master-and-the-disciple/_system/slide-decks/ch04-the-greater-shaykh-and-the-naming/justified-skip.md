## (c) Why none of the five warranted a slide

**Fragment 1 (chain of rights) — fails the structural-vs-description test.** The chapter delivers the five-link chain in a single paragraph of running prose as one ornament within the Shaykh's second discourse; the chapter's note at line 57 explicitly marks it as the *compressed* form of the longer *Treatise on Rights* — i.e. the source itself frames this as an in-passing summary, not a structural spine. Lifting it to a process-flow slide over-weights a moment the source treats as elaboration on the larger ritual arc.

**Fragment 2 (justice as middle) — anti-pattern "Forced 2x2 / forced contrast" (slide-deck-patterns.md §"Anti-Patterns the Challenger Catches" #4).** The "middle between two extremes" is a single rhetorical figure delivered, paired with its closing chain-of-attributes (most-merciful + mightiest; highest + nearest), and left. The chapter's payoff is the *unification* of the two extremes in one God, not a comparison-of-poles a contrast-pair slide would visualize. A two-cell slide would freeze the figure at the moment the prose dissolves it.

**Fragment 3 (chain of authority) — inverts the chapter's pedagogical point.** The chain Allah > Imam > Shaykh > Master > boy is real in this chapter, but the chapter is *not about the chain's structure* — it is about the boy's **silenced, embodied movement through** the chain (yellowed twice, soul nearly leaving the body twice, unable to speak in the Shaykh's council). A hierarchy-tree slide would render the chain as a static object precisely when the chapter is rendering it as a directed, asymmetric vector that the boy traverses. Wrong figure for the work the prose is doing.

**Fragment 4 (six qualities) — anti-pattern "Bullet-list-as-diagram" (#2).** The six qualities are delivered as a single uninterrupted string in the scholar's farewell speech: "trust in Allah when you are resolved; speak with a ruling when you are called upon; do not grow angry when you are wronged; do not weary if you are slandered; whoever responds to you with good, be patient with him; and devote yourself to the best of those upon whom you call." There is no contrastive axis, no nesting, no temporal order — the chapter's own internal commentary (line 141) reads them as "four postures against opposition, one toward the receptive, one toward the chain," but that grouping is *interpretation*, not structure inherent to the list. Slide-deck-patterns.md flags this exact failure mode.

**Fragment 5 (three reasons for parting) — same failure as #4.** Three short clauses ("we travel to another country / your family awaits / the duty of fulfilling the right of the grace"), delivered in sequence in the Shaykh's speech. No structural axis. The chapter's payoff is that the third reason is *theological* and the first two are *practical warrants for the third* (line 121) — that asymmetric weighting is prose-shaped, not slide-shaped.

**Additional consideration — the chapter's central event refuses representation.** The "highest transmission" the Shaykh delivers (lines 89–95) is explicitly veiled by the source in five propositions: *no fancy had ever descended, no pen had ever traced, never occurred to the heart of any human being, not to be mentioned in the assemblies of preachers, not permitted in mosques.* The chapter's discipline is to *name the veiling rather than violate it* (line 95). Any slide depicting the chapter's central moment would convert the source's refusal-to-schematize into a schema. The chapter's own discipline forbids the deck.

**Net verdict.** Of the five plausible candidates, two trigger named anti-patterns (4 & 5), one inverts the chapter's pedagogical direction (3), one is forbidden-by-source (the central transmission), and the remaining two (1 & 2) are in-passing rhetorical figures the chapter immediately moves past.
