# Show notes — EP01

**Title:** The Master's Call and the Disciple's Covenant

**Blurb:** The opening chapter of *Kitab al-'Alim wa-l-Ghulam* (*The Book of the Master and the Boy*) — the *da'wa*'s foundational dialogue on how a teacher draws a seeker out of his world and binds him by oath into the Party of God. A delegation of believers asks a Master what work answers the threefold blessing of being called, being shown knowledge, and being commanded to work. The Master gives the threefold *law of thanks* — obedience answers the Master, action-plus-call answers knowledge, patience-plus-call answers the work — and then tells the story of a Persian wanderer rescued from *the calamity of ignorance*, who must now carry the call forward. The wanderer reaches a city on the far edge of the island, weighs an assembly of disputants by three qualities, delivers a sustained sermon on the hiddenness of God's secrets and the *deposit* of the call in the breasts of the righteous, and is followed home by the youngest and most thoughtful of the group — the son of a great Arab sheikh. The long Master-Boy dialogue then begins: *what is this matter; whose is it; on what authority does the Master speak; can a man secure it by acquisition or only by favor; what are the limits and conditions; and what shall the boy do about his father?* The Master imposes five conditions and refuses the boy permission to withdraw from his father — *stand with him with outward agreement, keep this your secret*. After a long absence, on a quiet night *when the informers had grown forgetful*, the boy finds the Master again. The Master recites to him the *covenant of God* — *the rope of God upon His earth* — and binds him fast. The boy weeps until his tears pour down. The chapter ends on the moment the binding is completed and the Master begins to expound. *The chapter is the door, and the covenant is the key.*
