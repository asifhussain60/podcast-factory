# Show notes — EP01

**Title:** The Master's Call and the Disciple's Covenant

**Length:** 50 to 60 minutes (extended tier)

**NotebookLM format:** Deep Dive. Length: Long.

## Blurb

The opening chapter of *The Master and the Disciple* — the foundational dialogue on how a teacher draws a seeker out of his world and binds him by oath into the Party of God.

A delegation of believers asks a Master what work answers the threefold blessing of being called, being shown knowledge, and being commanded to work. The Master gives the threefold law of thanks — obedience answers the Master, action-plus-call answers knowledge, patience-plus-call answers the work — and then tells the story of a Persian wanderer rescued from the calamity of ignorance, who must now carry the call forward.

The wanderer reaches a city on the far edge of the island, weighs an assembly of disputants by three qualities, delivers a sustained sermon on the hiddenness of God's secrets and the deposit of the call in the breasts of the righteous, and is followed home by the youngest and most thoughtful of the group — the son of a great Arab sheikh.

The long Master-disciple dialogue then begins: what is this matter; whose is it; on what authority does the Master speak; can a man secure it by acquisition or only by favor; what are the limits and conditions; and what shall the disciple do about his father? The Master imposes five conditions and refuses the disciple permission to withdraw from his father — "stand with him with outward agreement, keep this your secret."

After a long absence, on a quiet night "when the informers had grown forgetful," the disciple finds the Master again. The Master recites to him the covenant of God — "the rope of God upon His earth" — and binds him fast. The disciple weeps until his tears pour down. The chapter ends on the moment the binding is completed and the Master begins to expound. The chapter is the door, and the covenant is the key.
