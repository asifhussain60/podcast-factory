# The Father Revealed and the Three Faces of Seeking

**Episode format:** Debate (each host adopts a role + position and argues from it). See `.skill/handbook/debate-framing.md` for the full format spec.

## Opening directive

In the first twenty seconds, the hosts name the work, state the proposition under debate verbatim, and tell the listener that they will hold opposing positions through the conversation. Do not open with "today we'll discuss" or "welcome to this deep dive". Open by stating the proposition.

## Audience

Thoughtful adult readers continuing the dialogue book of the Ismaili *da'wa* (DAH-wah — call, missionary apparatus) — *Kitab al-'Alim wa-l-Ghulam* (ki-TAAB al-AA-lim wal-ghu-LAAM, *The Book of the Master and the Boy*), attributed to Sayyidina Ja'far ibn Mansur al-Yaman (sai-yi-DEE-nah JAH-far ibn man-SOOR al-YAH-man) — after the cosmology of chapter two, the ethical-practical bridge of chapter three, and the initiation chapter four where the boy was *re-born* into the chain on the *seventh day* by the *greater Shaykh* (SHAYKH), received the *highest transmission* the source itself veils, and walked back to the edge of his city. Chapter four closed on a single line — *then his father came to him, angry* — the boy's *biological father*, the third father (the *elder father* was the *Shaykh*; the *father who had been the cause of his grace* was the *Master*), the one whose enmity toward the people of this matter the Master had named in chapter one. This chapter is the *answer* to that anger. It opens with the father's long reproach (*is this the reward of sons to their fathers? you drew him in upon me, and you crossed over with him in his affair without consulting your parents*); the boy's offer of *two strands of gratitude* (outward courtesy with divided hearts, or open debate with confidence between them); the father's surprising rejection of the *taqiyya*-strand (*how long can outward agreement stand when its underpinnings differ? I do not want that*); the boy's bind on the father (*either you are a scholar and have withheld the teaching, or you are ignorant and needed a stranger; either way, I will precede you to him*); the father weeping, conceding, committing in advance to *accept the truth if shown, reject the falsehood for your sake*; *they debated at intervals and disclosed things to each other at length, until they were guided to the right path*; *God revived many of His creation through them*; *the boy was sent out to preach with his father's blessing*. Then — for the first time in the book — the source names them: *the boy's name was Salih, and his father's name was al-Bakhtari* (*Ṣāliḥ*, SAA-lih, *the righteous one*; *al-Bakhtarī*, al-bakh-tah-REE). News of them reaches a group who held to the old creed; the community goes to a scholar they call *Abu Malik* (a-BOO MAA-lik) — known among them as *the heel of the inks* — and asks him to judge their *blood and property*. Abu Malik refuses to judge by anger; he gives the figure of the *breach* (*a breach in religion is gravely damaging; its flaw cannot be repaired, like a breach in medicine*); he lays out *three states* the people could be in (*either they are upon what we know of religion; or they have given them an increase; or the people have given them guidance other than what they have, by the prophecy of a prophet who has been brought to them — this is not denied by the command of God … God is at work every day*); he warns against becoming *like the tyrants of the previous nations, who said God would not send messengers after their messengers … and even killed the prophets of God*; he teaches them that *seekers are the ones who do not yet know which is which*; he gives the *door of seeking* (*the needy person must humble himself before what he needs*); the *sound heart* (*the heart that does not persist in denying what is said to it … the truth is what unveils itself and shows its merit, and falsehood is what reveals its own nakedness*); the *reverence-before-the-statement* doctrine; the *petitioner-before-the-king* parable; and collapses the timetable from *three days* to *two days* to *only this day, if you will do it; otherwise I go on alone*. They all repent, pay debts, bathe, put on the purest garments, and go out together to al-Bakhtari's gate. Abu Malik calls al-Bakhtari *Abu Salih* (a-BOO SAA-lih); the father plays the title back open (*today my father is good, and I am the son of Salih; and he is good with his Lord*); Abu Malik names the *transpositions* (*the child has become the father, the Lord is in the earth, the Lord has a servant come to Him from him, the one who created annihilation has become eternal*); and asks for a meeting with the *righteous slave*. Al-Bakhtari grants it under chain-discipline (*no way to that — but did you mean my son, Salih?*); Salih's prayer (*O Allah, open the hearts of Your servants and guide them by Your mercy to the signs of what is sought*); the council is cleared; the visitors enter. The doctrinal climax is the long Salih-Abu Malik dialogue: Salih's rebuke of imitation (*O Ka'b al-Ahbar*, KAB al-AH-bar, the paradigm of imitation-without-direct-knowledge); the *prophet-warner* doctrine (*the warner is a prophet, and the prophet is an argument from Allah, both for-them and against-them*); Abu Malik's threefold concession (*we have come to know the virtue of asking*); the *three faces of seeking* (*a seeker who knows, a seeker who does not know, a seeker who knows that he does not know*) with the parallel between *money* and *knowledge* (the *divine scholar* who seeks out the dead, the *learned man* seeking the full end, the *ignorant man who knows his ignorance*); Abu Malik's self-diagnosis as the third; *knowledge by report* vs *knowledge by direct sight* (*ignorance is a need, poverty is the narrowness of need, distress is a great need that compels the creature to seek capacity; and capacity is the breadth of knowledge, because knowledge is wide*); Abu Malik's confession of dire need; Salih's *how do you know me?* probe (*you have not seen me rub elbows with a scholar of prophecy, so as to know me by that; nor have you witnessed from me the proofs of evidence*); the *essence-traders* parable (*we presented your essence to the essence-traders and the people of metals, and they tested it for us in the forge. They did not accept it from us as essence; they said it was glass in camouflage*); the doctrine that *knowledge is not knowledge until it is presented to those who know the science of heaven*. Abu Malik raises the historical-temporal question (*of those who know the science of the heavens — the prophets and messengers — we know of none in our time; nor have the people had any covenant with such men since the revelation was cut off by the books; and the nations are no longer condemned except by reports*). Salih's three-step answer: *(1) any religion that is established only on narration is not called a religion*; *(2)* the books-themselves fail because they give only the general, and someone must make the specific; the *Maqrub* (mak-ROOB) divorce-case shows that Abu Malik himself, asked for a ruling, could only say *act on whichever of the two you wish*; *(3)* Abu Malik's confession, *the Sharia (sha-REE-ah) of my age has been lost, corrupted, and disrupted, and we hold on only to the name of religion*. Salih asks *what is religion?*; Abu Malik gives the standard *fiqh* answer (*command, prohibition, lawful, forbidden, Sunnah, SOON-nah, obligations*); Salih: *so you have combined religion and Sharia into a single thing; then religion has been disrupted by the suspension of the Sharia, and corrupted by its corruption — what is left with you?* Abu Malik sits a long while thinking, then raises his head: *I do not see that anything is left with me. So what religion do you have?* Salih's positive doctrine — *religion is with me, and I am not veiled from it … the cause that connects heaven to earth, unbroken — the loop of the firm handhold, the rope of those who hold fast, the ark of tranquility and the ship of life and the light of life, generous in its content, safeguarded in its sciences, marked in its jewels, its virtue hidden from no one who looks upon it*. The chapter ends on Abu Malik's seam-question into chapter six: *if God has a religion by which He will judge on the Day … but now, since it has been lost, the attribute is preferable to him — so how is it described?* The chapter is the *naming of the world* (the names *Salih*, *al-Bakhtari*, and *Abu Malik* enter the book together); the *opening of the world's seeking* (the senior scholar of the old creed is now a seeker); and the *handoff* into chapter six's long discourse on justice, monotheism, and the description of religion-by-attribute.

## Length

Target ~12–15 min Audio Overview.

## Proposition under debate

> The fiqh tradition's Sharia-by-narration — command, prohibition, lawful, forbidden, Sunnah, obligations — is sufficient to constitute religion in this age, and no living chain or unbroken cause connecting heaven to earth is required.

## Roles + positions

**Host A — scholar.**
Position: Religion is not exhausted by narrated rulings; it is the cause that connects heaven to earth, unbroken — the loop of the firm handhold, the rope of those who hold fast. A religion grounded only on narration is not religion; the books are general and someone must make the specific. When even the senior scholar can give a seeker two opposed rulings and tell him "act on whichever you wish," the Sharia-as-religion has been suspended.

Source moves available to Host A:
  - *Ka'b al-Ahbar* rebuke: imitation-without-direct-knowledge is not religion
  - *prophet-warner* doctrine: the warner is a prophet; the prophet is an argument from Allah for-them and against-them
  - *three faces of seeking* + *money-knowledge parallel*: divine scholar / learned man / ignorant-who-knows-his-need
  - *knowledge-by-report vs direct-sight*: news vs eye-witness; ignorance is a need; capacity is the breadth of knowledge
  - *essence-traders* parable: knowledge is not knowledge until presented to those who know the science of heaven
  - *Maqrub divorce-case* indictment: 'act on whichever of the two you wish' is the smoking gun
  - *religion-equals-Sharia* logical collapse: combining them and then noting the suspension
  - Positive doctrine: religion as the *cause-connecting-heaven-to-earth-unbroken* (loop, rope, ark, ship, light)

**Host B — debater.**
Position: Religion is the inherited fiqh tradition — command and prohibition, lawful and forbidden, Sunnah and obligations — transmitted through the scholars from the books and the consensus. The cutting-off of revelation by the books is part of God's plan; the community now lives by report (narration) and the trained interpretation of the jurists. No new prophet or "living chain" is needed.

Source moves available to Host B:
  - *Three-states* reasoning (gives the old creed's strongest internal case)
  - *Breach* figure: religious novelty is gravely damaging, like a breach in medicine
  - *Tyrants of the previous nations* warning (read AGAINST cutting off the argument-from-God)
  - *Door of seeking* + *sound heart* + *reverence-before-the-statement* doctrines (as the old creed's reception protocol)
  - Standard fiqh definition: *command, prohibition, lawful, forbidden, Sunnah, obligations*
  - Historical-temporal objection: *we know of no prophets and messengers in our time; the revelation was cut off by the books; the nations are no longer condemned except by reports*
  - *Maqrub-case* defense: pluralism of rulings is feature, not bug — the scholar gives both and lets the petitioner choose

## Rules of debate (apply through the entire episode)

1. **No strawman.** Each host argues the strongest form of their position. The OTHER host names the weaknesses, never the host holding it.
2. **Source-grounded only.** Every move references the source text, a passage from the same author's larger corpus, or an established tradition the position is anchored in. No appeals to modern common sense.
3. **Defended positions stay defended.** A host may concede a sub-point with qualification ("That's a fair point on X, but...") but does not abandon their named position unless the resolution is `host_X_concedes`.
4. **Disagreement is the work.** Acknowledgment grammar ("Exactly", "Yeah, exactly") that is forbidden in Deep Dive is softened here: a host may concede a sub-point but the concession is qualified and followed by a return to the host's main position. Bare affirmations remain forbidden.
5. **One position at a time.** Each beat surfaces one part of the argument. Hosts do not jump topics.
6. **The proposition is named at open and at close.** Resolution is announced at the close per the contract's `resolution` field; no host announces a winner.
7. **No verdict from the host.** Neither host says "I've convinced you" or "you have to admit". The listener decides.
8. **The author's voice is third in the room.** A quote from the source is authoritative for that moment, regardless of which host invokes it.

## Resolution

`host_b_concedes` — Host B — debater — concedes the main point to Host A by the close.

## Tone constraints

The hosts must NOT do the following:

  - >

- No ad hominem. No characterizing the other position as foolish, naive, or fundamentalist.
- No sarcasm. Firm disagreement, not contempt.
- No theatrical opposition ("battle of ideas", "showdown", "who is right"). This is *munazara*, not boxing.
