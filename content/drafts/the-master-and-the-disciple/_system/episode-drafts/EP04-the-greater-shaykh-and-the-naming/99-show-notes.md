# Show notes — EP04

**Title:** The Greater Shaykh and the Seventh-Day Naming

**Blurb:** The initiation chapter of *Kitab al-'Alim wa-l-Ghulam* (*The Book of the Master and the Boy*) — the chapter where the boy is *re-born* into the chain of the *da'wa* by a *seventh-day naming* at the hand of the *greater Shaykh*, receives the *highest transmission* (the innermost *batin* the source deliberately does *not* record), and is *sent* as a *caller* in his own right. After the chapter-three deferral on the *Imam's permission*, the Master goes to the boy's father with the report; the father marvels and instructs *make haste*. The Master returns and finds the boy in *a different state of body than the one he had left him in* — *yellowed* by longing in a deliberate echo of the chapter-three brethren *yellowed by the rigor of fasting*. The announcement comes: *I cast mercy upon you into the heart of the one who has charge of you. So take up your traveling provisions, for I have high hopes for you, and I will accompany you to the door of your goal*. The boy nearly dies of joy; *Allah took hold of him, knowing his condition; then He returned him*. They set out and reach the *greater Shaykh*. In the Shaykh's council the boy *turns yellow from the intensity of his awe and cannot speak*; the Shaykh recognizes him to the Master as *the boy we used to hear about*, and the boy learns that his story is studied in the *gatherings of remembrance* of the brethren themselves. The next morning the Shaykh delivers two sustained discourses: the *justice* of God as *the middle between two extremes* (with the figure of *spread-out hands* and the *evidence against ingratitude itself unlimited*), and the *dignity of minds* with the chain of rights — *thought → good manners → knowledge → action → obedience perfected in hardship and ease*. Then the chapter's central ritual: the *seventh-day naming*. The Shaykh asks the boy his name; the boy gives *Ubayd Allah, son of Abd Allah*; the Shaykh receives it as *your description*; the boy withdraws to *I am free, son of Abd Allah*; the Shaykh probes (*who freed you from a master until you became free?*); the boy gives the *manumission-by- five-shares* account (*this world possessed me, and I have handed it back to the One who called it*); the Shaykh tightens to the impossibility of self-naming; the boy *falls silent*. The Shaykh names the impasse — *how can a thing know that which has no name?* — and defers *until the completion of seven days*, *for the dignity of the newborn*, *the name belongs to you, and you belong to the name. So it does not appear except within your limit, and it travels with your duration*. On the seventh day the boy bathes, puts on his purest garments, relieves himself (*a sign of the good that was to come after it*), and goes out repeating *the day of Sa'd, and the guardian of Sa'd*. The Shaykh receives him with *the implements of his work already taken up* and delivers, in a veil the source itself never lifts, the *highest transmission*: *matters upon which no fancy had ever descended … not to be mentioned in the assemblies of preachers, and not permitted in mosques. These books are among the greatest of His bounties, and are unveiled only to His own people*. The moment is sealed in the Hajj-by-the-great-sign figure: *he removed his ihram by glorifying the sanctities of Allah, took hold of the rope, circumambulated the ancient House, spent his rites in remembrance, and performed Hajj by the great sign*. Then attendance — *the root was watered and the branch raised* — in deliberate reversal of the chapter-three sower-figure: what the Master alone could not do, the chain has now done together. Then the farewell. The boy praises the Shaykh as *the gate of the people of heaven and the ascent of the people of the earth*; the Shaykh blesses him in eight clauses, with the *method of the call* named directly; the Shaykh gives three reasons for parting (the next journey to *another country*, the family awaiting the boy's return, the *duty of fulfilling the right of this grace — to worship by it and to call others to it*); and the Shaykh enjoins the safeguarding of the father in five negatives (*do not clothe him in the garment of contempt, do not board him on the ships of harm, do not omit his name, do not become distracted from his remembrance, and do not deceive him in your city — for there is a sting at the very outset*). The scholar walks the boy back toward his city, and on the *edge of the city* gives the boy the *six qualities* and the *interpretation key* — *whatever in it is praiseworthy is like the truth and its people; whatever was a reprehensible parable, I spent it upon the enemies of the truth and its people … these are the foundations of the science that works by them: if she preserves them, she has no need of any others*. The final parting — *the boy's gasp at the mention of parting was such that he almost killed himself … then he kissed the scholar's feet and wiped their dust upon his face* — closes the chapter. The boy returns home with his heart *absent from him in fear*, unable to return any utterance of his grief over the parting from his *elder father and the father who had been the cause of his grace*. His family becomes afraid for him. *Then his father came to him, angry*. The chapter ends there. *The naming has happened; the name in the world will now happen. The chapter is the re-birth; the next chapter is the re-entry.*
