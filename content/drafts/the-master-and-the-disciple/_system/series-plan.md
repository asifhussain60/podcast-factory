## Audit-trail-only sections (no human review)

### Audience (orchestrator config default)
Thoughtful adult readers approaching the *da'wa* (DAH-wah — call, missionary apparatus of the Ismaili tradition) literature for the first time through the dialogue book that the tradition itself treats as foundational: *Kitab al-'Alim wa-l-Ghulam* (ki-TAAB al-AA-lim wal-ghu-LAAM — *The Book of the Master and the Boy*), attributed to Sayyidina Ja'far ibn Mansur al-Yaman (sai-yi-DEE-nah JAH-far ibn man-SOOR al-YAH-man). The book opens with a delegation of believers asking a Master what work answers the threefold blessing of being called, being shown knowledge, and being commanded to work. The Master answers them with the *law of thanks* — obedience answers the Master, action-plus-call answers knowledge, patience-plus-call answers the work — and then turns to the *doctrines of the righteous* by way of his own autobiography: a Persian wanderer rescued from the *calamity of ignorance*, who must now repay the debt by carrying the call forward. The wanderer crosses the inhabited world until he reaches a city on the far edge of *al-Jazira* (al-jah-ZEE-rah — the island, the Arabian peninsula), sits at the edge of an assembly of disputants, weighs them by three qualities (*conveying the argument*, *being of the Umma*, *searching together*), rises and delivers a sustained sermon on the hiddenness of God's secrets, the *deposit* of the call in the breasts of the righteous, and the architecture of *zahir* (ZAA-hir — outward) and *batin* (BAA-tin — inward). A youth follows the Master home — the son of a great Arab sheikh (SHAYKH) — and the long Master-Boy dialogue begins: *what is this matter; whose is it; on what authority does the Master speak; can a man secure it by* acquisition *or only by* favor; *what are the limits and conditions; and what shall the boy do about his father?* After a long absence, on a quiet night when *the informers had grown forgetful*, the boy finds the Master again. The Master recites to him the *covenant of God* — the great key that distinguishes the lawful from the unlawful and admits the bearer into the *Hizb Allah* (HIZB ahl-LAH — the Party of God). The boy weeps until his tears pour down. The chapter ends on the moment the binding is completed and the Master begins to expound. This is the door of the book; the covenant is the key.

## NotebookLM input checklist (per-episode workflow)

After Phase 0g writes the per-episode customize prompts, for each episode:

1. **Open NotebookLM** → "+ New notebook" (or use existing per-book notebook)
2. **Sources panel** → "+ Add source" → "Upload from file" → select the file
   listed in the **Upload** column of the Episode list
3. **Customize panel** (top right) → "Customize" → paste the entire contents of
   the **Customize** file
4. The customize prompt already declares: length cue, host pairing, format
   (deep_dive vs debate), focus areas, pronunciation block, tone constraints
5. **Generate** → ~10–15 min for NotebookLM to render audio
6. **Download** the MP3 → save at `audio/EP##-<slug>.mp3`
7. **Transcribe** via `python3 scripts/podcast/transcribe_episode.py`
   → drops at `transcripts/EP##-<slug>.transcript.txt`
8. **Audit** via `python3 scripts/podcast/audit_transcript.py <BOOK_DIR> EP##-<slug>`
   — catches Arabic pronunciation drift, missing phonetic cues, fabricated quotes
9. If audit flags issues: edit `pronunciation.md` overrides → re-paste customize
   prompt → re-generate
