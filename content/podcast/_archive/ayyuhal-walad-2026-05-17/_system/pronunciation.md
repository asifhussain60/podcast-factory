# Pronunciation Guide — *Ayyuhal Walad*

> **Authority order:** the shared canonical reference at
> `content/_shared/arabic/` wins for any term that appears there. This
> book-level guide carries the Ayyuhal-Walad-specific terms (Sufi
> vocabulary, named figures, source-specific Quranic verses) and any
> book-level overrides. A term that disagrees with the shared manifest is a
> defect — fix it here, not the other way around.

**Source:** *Ayyuhal Walad — My Dear Beloved Son (or Daughter)* by Imam Abu Hamid Muhammad Al-Ghazali, translated into English by Irfan Hasan from an Urdu translation of the original Arabic.
**Tradition:** Islamic (Sunni Sufi).
**Style:** Pronunciation-friendly English transliteration. Long vowels doubled, no diacritics, hyphenated where stress matters.

---

## Honorifics and divine names

| Term | Phonetic | Meaning / use |
|---|---|---|
| Allah | **Al-lah** | God. Stress the second syllable lightly. |
| Allah Ta'ala | **Al-lah Ta-aa-la** | God, the Most Exalted. Used as a reverent epithet. |
| Subhanahu Wa Ta'ala (SWT) | **Sub-haa-na-hu Wa Ta-aa-la** | Glorified and Exalted is He. Honorific after "Allah." |
| Sallallahu Alayhi Wa Sallam (SAW) | **Sal-lal-laa-hu A-lay-hi Wa Sal-lam** | Peace and blessings be upon him. Honorific after "Prophet Muhammad." Speak as "peace and blessings be upon him." |
| Rahmatullahi Alayhe (RA) | **Rah-ma-tul-laa-hi A-lay-hi** | May Allah shower His mercy upon him. Honorific for departed Muslim scholars and saints. Speak as "may Allah shower His mercy upon him." |
| Alayhis Salam (AS) | **A-lay-his Sa-laam** | Peace be upon him. Used for prophets and Imams. |

## Technical Sufi vocabulary (locked — preserve as theological terms)

These terms have technical precision in Islamic spiritual literature. The skill preserves them in transliteration with brief gloss on first appearance, then phonetic alone thereafter.

| Term | Phonetic | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| Tasawwuf | **Ta-saw-wuf** | The Islamic spiritual path; inner purification; what the source translates as "spiritual struggle." |
| Tawakkul | **Ta-wak-kul** | Reliance on God; trust in divine sufficiency after one has taken right action. |
| Ikhlas | **Ikh-laas** | Sincerity; performing acts purely for God's sake, free of any other motive. |
| Ihsan | **Ih-saan** | Spiritual excellence; worshipping God as if seeing Him. |
| Taqwa | **Taq-wa** | God-consciousness; watchful awareness of God's presence. |
| Taqlid | **Taq-leed** | Following a recognized authority in religious matters. |
| Ta'wil | **Ta-weel** | Disciplined interpretation, as distinct from speculation. |
| Sharee'ah | **Sha-ree-ah** | The revealed law. |
| Fard al-'ayn | **Fard al-Ayn** | A personal obligation incumbent on every individual. |
| Fard Kifaya | **Fard Ki-faa-ya** | A communal obligation discharged by some on behalf of the community. |
| Du'a | **Doo-aa** | Supplication; personal prayer. |
| Dhikr | **Dhikr** | Remembrance of God. |
| Sufi | **Soo-fee** | One who follows the path of tasawwuf. |
| Shaykh | **Shaykh** | Spiritual guide; teacher; elder. Use English "Shaykh" — widely recognized. |
| Ahkamul Hakimeen | **Ah-kaa-mul Haa-ki-meen** | The Supreme Ruler; an epithet for God. |
| Ahya al-Uloom ad-Deen | **Ih-yaa al-U-loom ad-Deen** | "Revival of the Religious Sciences" — Ghazali's magnum opus. |
| Ayyuhal Walad | **Ay-yu-hal Wa-lad** | "O Beloved Son" — title of the source. |
| Majmu'a Rasail | **Maj-moo-a Ra-saa-il** | "Collection of Treatises" — the parent compilation. |

## Anti-noise pronunciation rules (host directives)

Three rules NotebookLM hosts must follow when speaking these terms:

1. Pronounce every term using the **phonetic spelling in this guide**, not the source's looser transliteration.
2. On first appearance per episode, pair the term with its brief English gloss. On subsequent appearances, say the term alone — listeners build the vocabulary across the episode.
3. Honorifics after sacred names (Prophet, Imam, departed scholars) are **always spoken in full English** ("peace and blessings be upon him", "may Allah shower His mercy upon him"), never abbreviated as "SAW" or "RA."
