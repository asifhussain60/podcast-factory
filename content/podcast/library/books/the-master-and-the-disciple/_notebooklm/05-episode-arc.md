# Episode Arc — *The Master and the Disciple*

**[NotebookLM Instruction]** This is the series map. Read it once at the start of any episode work to understand where the current episode sits in the larger arc. It is NOT episode content — do not narrate it. Use it to make decisions about what to develop deeply in an episode versus what to defer to a later episode.

## Series premise (one sentence)

How does a seeker become capable of receiving true knowledge? — traced through gratitude, covenant, symbolic interpretation, rank, family conflict, communal testing, and the recognition of living guidance.

## Recommended series arc shape

Treat the arc as three movements, with Ep 0 as a prelude and Ep 7 as a coda:

1. **Prelude (Ep 0):** What is this book and what does it ask of you?
2. **Movement I — Formation (Eps 1–3):** How a seeker is made: readiness → covenant → symbolic literacy.
3. **Movement II — Trial (Eps 4–6):** How a seeker is tested: rank, family, community, verification.
4. **Coda (Ep 7):** What you carry into daily life after the test.

The series should *feel designed*, not stitched. The host should anticipate where a thread will resolve later, and refer back to earlier episodes when a metaphor recurs (e.g., the egg of Ch 3 returning in the rope of Ch 7).

| Ep 6 | Ep 7 | The book has finished its argument. The question becomes: what changes about your daily life? |
