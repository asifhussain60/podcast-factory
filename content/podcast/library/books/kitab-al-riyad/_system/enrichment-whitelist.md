# Enrichment Whitelist — Kitab al-Riyad (Tier 1, book-specific)

Per `content/podcast/.skill/handbook/enrichment-sources.md` §1, Tier 1 is the
**author's own corpus**, scoped per book. This file enumerates the corpus of
*Kitab al-Riyad*'s author (Hamid al-Din Ahmad ibn Abdullah al-Kirmani, d. ca.
411/1020), the senior Fatimid Da'i of Iraq under Imam-Caliph al-Hakim bi-Amr
Allah. Citations from these works are highest-priority enrichment for any
chapter in this book.

For other books, this file enumerates a different author's corpus; the handbook
itself names no specific author.

## Works (Tier 1 for this book)

- *Rahat al-'Aql* (Repose of the Intellect) — al-Kirmani's magnum opus on the seven seas, the architecture of the universal Intellect, the hudud, and the chain of the Imamate. Composed at Cairo ca. 411/1020. Cross-referenced by al-Riyad in nearly every bāb.
- *al-Masabih fi al-Imama* (The Lamps on the Imamate) — al-Kirmani's defense of the Imamate against opponents. Referenced internally at Bāb 9 Fasl 14 (al-Riyad) for the architecture of legal prescription.
- *al-Rawda* (The Garden) — al-Kirmani's epistle on monotheism and language, referenced internally at Bāb 10 Fasl 4 (al-Riyad) for the language-argument: *Allah is beyond minds and perceptions, behind a veil from understandings and thought*.
- *Mabasim al-Bisharat* (Smiles of the Glad Tidings) — al-Kirmani's treatise on the Imamate of al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah and the Fatimid claim.
- *Tanbih al-Hadi wa al-Mustahdi* (Awakening the Guide and the Sought-Guide) — al-Kirmani's missionary treatise.
- *al-Aqwal al-Dhahabiyya* (The Golden Sayings) — al-Kirmani's refutation of Abu Bakr al-Razi's *Tibb al-Ruhani* (Spiritual Medicine).
- *al-Wadiʿa fi Maʿalim al-Din* (The Deposit on the Landmarks of Religion) — al-Kirmani's catechetical work on the foundations of religion.
- *Risalat al-Kafiya* (The Sufficient Epistle) — al-Kirmani's polemical epistle.
- *Risalat al-Mudiʾa* (The Illuminating Epistle) — al-Kirmani's epistle on illumination and the architecture of the rational soul.

## Adjacent Da'wa corpus (Tier 2 — same tradition, not same author)

The Da'wa-tradition predecessors and contemporaries al-Kirmani himself cites or
responds to. Citations from these works are **second-priority** enrichment;
they should not displace al-Kirmani's own corpus when both are available.

- *Kitab al-Mahsul* (lost; the disputed source al-Riyad is correcting) — attributed in the tradition to Muhammad ibn Ahmad al-Nasafi (d. 332/943) or to a Da'i of his generation.
- *al-Islah* (Abu Hatim al-Razi, Da'i of Rayy, d. 322/934) — defense of *al-Mahsul* against Abu Bakr al-Razi the philosopher-physician; one of the two voices al-Riyad adjudicates.
- *al-Nusra* (Abu Yaʿqub al-Sijistani, d. ca. 361/971) — further defense of *al-Mahsul*; the other voice al-Riyad adjudicates. Also: *Kitab al-Yanabiʿ* (The Wellsprings), *Ithbat al-Nubuwwat* (The Confirmation of Prophecies), *Kitab al-Iftikhar* (The Glorying), *Kashf al-Mahjub* (The Unveiling).
- *Aʿlam al-Nubuwwa* (Abu Hatim al-Razi) — the prophetic-religion treatise written against Abu Bakr al-Razi the philosopher-physician.
- *Taʾwil al-Daʿaʾim* and the Cairo-court treatises (Imam-Caliph al-Muʿizz li-Dīn Allāh, d. 365/975) — referenced internally at al-Riyad Bāb 10 Fasl 4 (*Imam al-Muʿizz's well-known book*).
- *al-Daʿaʾim* (al-Qadi al-Nuʿman, d. 363/974) — the Fatimid jurist's foundational legal work.
- Imam Jaʿfar al-Sadiq's reported sayings (peace be upon him) — the foundational hadith corpus of the Imami line.
