# Show notes — EP14

**Title:** Prophets as Teachers, Monotheism, and the Ranks

**Blurb:** The second half of *Chapter Nine* of *Kitab al-Riyad* — fourteen sub-chapters in which al-Kirmani takes the verdict of the first half (*Adam had a Shariʿah, and Seth was his asas*) and *expands* it into the architectural shape of the chain that runs through human history. Sub-chapter twenty: *the work is for soul- correction, not only for tawhid*; the deep reading of *I know what you do not know* (Quran 2:30) on the architecture of *force and act* across the cycles. Sub-chapters twenty-one and twenty-two: distinguishing the *interregnum role* of the Qa'im (knowledge widespread) from the *post-Qa'im role* (door of education closed). Sub-chapter twenty-three: the *three sections of Adam's call* — encouragement, intimidation, or both — each requiring *parable* and therefore *apparent meaning followed by interpretation*. Sub-chapter twenty-four: *knowledge and action together* as the *one Shariʿah* — *body and spirit of the religion*. Sub-chapter twenty-five: *the Qa'im completes, does not abrogate* — the apocalypse- verses concern *the passing of the ranks*, not the *laws and ordinances of works*. Sub-chapter twenty-six: *the high limits attain perfection from innovation*; the *bodily intellects* from *acquisition through teaching*. Sub-chapters twenty-seven and twenty-eight: the *seven lines and six aradas* dispute — al-Islah's reading corrected, al-Nusra's refused, al-Kirmani's positive geometry of the speaker-cycles. Sub-chapter twenty-nine: *As We began the first creation, We shall repeat it* (Quran 21:104) on the *cyclic return*. Sub-chapter thirty: the *Adam-letters* argument refuted by internal inconsistency. Sub-chapters thirty-one through thirty-three: the *ark of Noah* with both readings sound (apparent Shariʿah + daʿwa-with-the-wali), the doctrine that *every speaker has a guardian* (Adam had Seth), and the deep *taʾwil* of *the son of Noah* as the *opposite* who is washed away in the cleansing flood that spreads *outwardly and inwardly* — the type that recurs at every cycle until the seventh. Bridge to *Chapter Ten*: *Tawhid and the critique of al-Mahsul* — the doctrine of the First Creator above the head of the chain.
