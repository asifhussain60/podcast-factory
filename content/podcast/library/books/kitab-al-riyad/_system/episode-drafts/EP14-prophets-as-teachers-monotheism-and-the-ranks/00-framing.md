## Background

The second half of *Chapter Nine* of *Kitab al-Riyad* takes the verdict the first half settled — *Adam had a Shari'ah, and Seth was his asas* — and expands it across the architectural shape of the chain that runs in time. Sub-chapter twenty asks why prophets are sent at all and answers that the *work* of the speakers is not only for signifying *tawhid* but for the *correction of the soul* — and reads the angels' verse on *force* and *act* across the cycles of knowledge and ignorance. Sub-chapters twenty-one through twenty-six unfold the corollaries: the *interregnum* role of the Qa'im against the *post-Qa'im* role; the *three sections* of Adam's call, each requiring parable and therefore interpretation; the positive settlement of *knowledge and action together*; the *Qa'im as completer*, not abrogator; and the doctrine of *the high limits attaining perfection from innovation*. Sub-chapters twenty-seven and twenty-eight work the *seven lines and six aradas* dispute. Sub-chapter twenty-nine reads *as We began the first creation, We shall repeat it* on the cyclic return. Sub-chapter thirty refutes the *Adam-letters* argument by internal inconsistency. Sub-chapters thirty-one through thirty-three work the *ark of Noah* with both readings sound, the doctrine that *every speaker has a guardian*, and the deep *taʾwil* of the *son of Noah* as the recurring *opposite*.

## Central tensions to reach

The hosts MUST surface every one of these tensions, by name, in the conversation.

First. Sub-chapter twenty's *Shari'ah-as-pedagogy* doctrine. The *work* of the speakers is *not only* for signifying *tawhid* — the world's structure already does part of that. The deeper purpose is the *correction of the soul*, *emulating the highest assembly*, and *acquiring through discipline the honorable morals and noble habits by which they prepare for union with what is above them*. The *positive worship* of the *qurban*, the *sajda*, the *ruku'*, the *zakat* is not exterior decoration of the inner *tawhid*; it is *constitutive* of the soul's raising-by-degrees. The hosts must hold this against any temptation to reduce the positive worship to symbolic gesture.

Second. The deep reading of *I know what you do not know* (Quran 2:30) on the architecture of *force* and *act* across the cycles. *The universes, by His decree and His wisdom, are between force and act — hesitating: sometimes standing in potentiality, and sometimes already existing in actuality.* When heat reaches its end, cold appears; when cold reaches its end, heat appears; *ignorance is general in the presence of its people, and ignorance is lost through the lack of its people*. *I know* names the end of ignorance and the beginning of knowledge; *what you do not know* names the necessity of teachers and guides at the turning. The arc runs from Adam's first sending to the Qa'im at the seventh. The hosts must walk this cyclic-architecture reading without flattening it into a generic ignorance-to-knowledge arc.

Third. Sub-chapter twenty-three's *three sections of Adam's call*. *Encouragement* (*targhib*) requires parables of pleasure — orchards, rivers, trees. *Intimidation* (*tarhib*) requires parables of pain — fire, chains, scorching wind. *Both* requires both. On *any* of the three forms, Adam's speech must have an *apparent meaning followed by an interpretation*. The very form of prophetic speech, by necessity, carries both *zahir* and *batin* — and the *zahir* of prophetic speech, by definition, *is* the law and the clear custom, the Shari'ah. The hosts must let this *form-of-prophetic-speech-itself-entails-Shari'ah* argument land as one of the chapter's tightest positive proofs.

Fourth. The *seven lines and six aradas* dispute in sub-chapters twenty-seven and twenty-eight. *Al-Islah* reads the seven lines as the seven speakers and the six aradas as the six laws. *Al-Nusra* reads the seven lines as the higher letters and the aradas as the cycles of the Imams between speakers — landing the elegant symmetry *Qa'im-no-Imams-after-no-Shari'ah* and *Adam-no-Imams-before-no-Shari'ah*. Al-Kirmani corrects *al-Islah* (the *Shari'ah is a stature that does not require any increase upon it nor any decrease from it*) and refuses *al-Nusra* (graph lines on pronouncers and aradas on the duration of their command; the Imams after Adam — Seth and his descendants — entail Adam's Shari'ah). The hosts must walk the geometric architecture without making it a glassy diagram.

Fifth. The deep *taʾwil* of the *son of Noah* in sub-chapter thirty-three. On the *da'wa*'s reading, the son was not from Noah's loins — he was a man of great worldly station who learned from Noah and was imagined to be his guardian. When the true *wali* was appointed in the building of the ark, the son *separated himself* (*iʿtazala*), refused obedience, and took refuge in the *mountain of his partisans*. The water is the cleansing force; the mountain is what supports him of men and partisans. *The flood spread outwardly and inwardly*. The type recurs at every cycle until the seventh. The hosts must let this *recurring-opposite-figure* doctrine land as the chapter's political seal — not as merely allegorical reading of a flood story.

Sixth. The *two readings of the ark* in sub-chapter thirty-one. *Al-Islah*'s reading — the ark as the new apparent Shari'ah, *whoever entered under its rulings was delivered from humiliation as one inside a ship is delivered* — is admissible. *Al-Nusra*'s reading — the ark as the *daʿwa-with-the-wali*, *whoever enters the quiet daʿwa is kept safe from drowning in error* — is also sound. *One is the matter of this world and the other is the matter of religion, and neither of them dispenses with the other, for each of them lifts up the head of the other.* The hosts must hold the two-layer doctrine — refusing either reading halves the religion.
