# Show notes — EP08

**Title:** Souls — Parts of the First Truths, or Only Traces?

**Blurb:** Eight sub-chapters of *Chapter Four* of *Kitab al-Riyad* — al-Kirmani's adjudication of the longest-running disagreement between the two reformers: are the speaking souls in us *parts* of the First Truths (the universal Second) or only *traces* of them? The chapter walks the dispute through eight passes. Sub-chapter one reads both reformers as half-right: al-Islah's "traces" protected the soul from body-status; al-Nusra's "parts" protected the soul's continuity with what stands above. Sub-chapters two and three set up and refuse al-Nusra's "parts of the total soul" reading by a precise dilemma. Sub-chapter four delivers the doctrinal hinge: the speaking souls are the *second emanation*, the second source. Sub-chapters five and six work the unlimited-capacity proof of non-embodiment and grant only a restricted parts-by-rank language. Sub-chapter seven is the polemical climax: prophethood, imamate, walaya, and the divine laws do not stand or fall by the parts-claim at all; they rest on the rank attained by the prophet's soul through the chain of obedience and worship, on the *hudud* that convey the gift. Sub-chapter eight delivers the deepest move: the Intellect is nothing but the Word, the Word is nothing but the Intellect — the formula Imam al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah taught in *Ta'wil al-Shari'a*. The doctrinal payload sets the architecture for the rest of *al-Riyad*: the *hudud* convey; the souls receive; the chain holds by the running of the single originating power, not by any substance-composition between ranks.
