### Passage 1

> Al-Kirmani's third word — the doctrinal hinge: *The souls belong to the nature of those paradoxical things whose existence is creative; their appearance and existence are only among the natural and phenomenal worlds. It is a natural force that acquires what is gained from the limits through obedience and worship. So it emits from itself an abstract remaining image that is like the abstract things in the House of the Hereafter on the one hand, and the opposite of it on the other. This is the second source, and our writings on the Ma'ad include mention of that, God willing.*

### Passage 2

> Al-Kirmani's restricted concession on the 'parts' language (sub-chapter six): *The most we can grant him is to say: as for those who wish to call them parts, these speaking souls are receivers if they are rational. As such, the soul and what it has produced are one thing; the speaking soul is identical with it and not distinguished from it except by rank — like the part of a thing in which there exists what is found in the whole, their path in themselves being one and the same from this side.*

### Passage 3

> Al-Kirmani's release of the parts-question from the architecture of prophethood (sub-chapter seven): *Prophethood is not invalidated by this, nor imamate, nor anything else, for it has been established that these ranks are not of these. The prophets (peace be upon them) were able to perceive these absent things from the upper world, after they reached the rank of perfection — becoming, at its end, in the Sixties. When they return to themselves, it is as though they have realized that the transcendent selves are one thing in respect of the whole, with no difference except in rank.*

### Passage 4

> Al-Kirmani's deepest move on the Intellect and the Word (sub-chapter eight): *The Intellect is nothing but the Word itself, and the Word is nothing but the Intellect itself. It is correct to say that Mawlana Imam al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah (peace be upon him), in The Interpretation of Sharia (Ta'wil al-Shari'a), where he discusses intelligibles, says: the Word is the Intellect, and these names properly belong to it.*

### Passage 5

> Al-Kirmani's settled formula on the emanations: *The Soul and nature emanate from the First Intellect, each of them according to its own proportion, so that one of them is more excellent than the other — the first emanation being the Soul, and the speaking souls being the second emanation. What occurs in the first of them is repeated in the last of them.*
