# Show notes — EP15

**Title:** Tawhid and the Critique of al-Mahsul

**Blurb:** Sixteen sub-chapters of *Chapter Ten* of *Kitab al-Riyad* — the book's closing critique. Al-Kirmani turns from the architecture of the chain to the rank *above* the chain — *tawhid* (taw-heed — monotheism) and *al-mubdi al-awwal* (the First Creator). The author of *al-Mahsul* had written a chapter on the *first thing in what was first to fix*: tawhid and the First Creator. Abu Hatim al-Razi, in *al-Islah*, had passed over the errors. Al-Kirmani enters at the close of his own treatise — having promised the work at the book's beginning — to *discharge the right of religion by stating what is implicated in al-Mahsul's chapter and what it is not permissible to believe*. Sub-chapter one frames the doctrine of *cooperation in the sciences of religion like the senses*. Sub-chapter two lands the *threefold impossibility* against the *Creator of the thing — and what is not under these elements*. Sub-chapters three through eight work the *grammar of nothing* and refuse al-Mahsul's *He and no thing with Him* (with the verse of self-witness Q 3:18, the verse of incomparability Q 42:11, and the Cairo-court doctrine of *huwa* as the only pronoun *positive for what is outside of it, without any attribute or plurality*, anchored to Imam al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah's *Ta'wil al-Shara'i'*). Sub-chapter nine corrects al- Mahsul's confusion of the *acquired intellect* (which gathers the images of both worlds) with the *First Intellect* (which is *self- knowing alone*). Sub-chapter ten lands the chapter's central identity — *unity = the First Intellect = the Word = the cause = creativity = the Creator*. Sub-chapter eleven presses the impossibilities against *unity as other than the Intellect*. Sub-chapter twelve corrects the *forms became eternal* doctrine (valid only of the acquired intellects that unite with the *paradoxical intellect called the proximate sovereign, nicknamed the Pen* — *al-Qalam*). Sub-chapter thirteen corrects the *Intellect became like the Word* doctrine (the First Intellect IS the Word, not LIKE the Word). Sub-chapter fourteen refuses applying the name *Creator* to the intellect (shirk). Sub-chapters fifteen and sixteen refuse the *Intellect-as-intermediary* and *creativity-as-intermediary* doctrines and land the chapter's centerpiece — *creativity IS the Creator* — anchored to Q 6:103 and Q 57:3, and crowned by the Imam Ali aphorism from *Nahj al-Balagha* on *whoever describes Him has limited Him*. The closing section settles the soteriological consequence: tawhid is *difficult*; Allah has from time to time singled out *one perfected man — the Imam, peace be upon him*; the *two worships* (zahir and batin) are the body and spirit of the religion; the chapter closes with the prayer for *Dar al-Quds*, *a noble station*, and the book closes with *Praise be to God, until He is praised as is due; and prayers upon Muhammad and his good family, all of them, the pure*. The book's final chapter is its closing humility — the speaker who has worked the chain joint- by-joint stops at the last joint and *will not speak past it*.
