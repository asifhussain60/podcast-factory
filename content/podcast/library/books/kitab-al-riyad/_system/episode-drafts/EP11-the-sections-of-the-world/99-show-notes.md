# Show notes — EP11

**Title:** The Sections of the World

**Blurb:** Seven sub-chapters of *Chapter Seven* of *Kitab al-Riyad* — al-Kirmani's adjudication of *the sections of the world*: what the world's actual divisions are, where the human being sits among those sections, what kind of *pair* the human has, and on what footing the doctrine of the microcosm can honestly stand. The author of *al-Islah* had offered a clean four-jewels-four- structures-four-classes scheme with the horse paired to humankind by essence-sharing and humankind itself as the counterpart of the whole world with no peer among its parts. The author of *al-Nusra* had refused the four-classes division and offered a pairing-by- acceptance scheme: the dense lower world as pair of the subtle upper world; the horse as humankind's pair because the horse accepts teaching from humankind more than other animals do. Sub- chapter one frames the dispute and lands the architectural correction (the world has many more sections than four; the generated beings are three genera plus human-by-distinction). Sub-chapter two refuses the no-peer claim from two angles and lands the pairing-by-occasion doctrine (angels, lions, wolves, crows, tigers, monkeys, depending on what the human has chosen to resemble). Sub-chapter three settles the strict pair of *strict- sense humankind* as the angels and names the *walaya* chain that constitutes the strict-sense humankind. Sub-chapter four turns to Bandhaqlis (Empedocles) on the bestial soul and speaking soul, refuses *al-Nusra*'s literal reading, and gives the *ta'wil* (teachers and receivers, not horse-and-human), with Q 89:27–30 as the Qur'anic anchor. Sub-chapter five settles the architectural map of the microcosm in its disputed form (*al-Islah*'s body-to- bodies-growth-to-plants-sense-to-animals-speaking-soul-as-unique- fourth preferred to *al-Nusra*'s re-mapping). Sub-chapter six fills the map at full breadth and adds the creative-emanative world parallels (intellect, praise, *tawhid*, virtue), pointing the reader to *Rahat al-'Aql*. Sub-chapter seven lands the *between*-state doctrine: the human is the child of *both worlds*, matter from below and image from above, the parents in the highest picture being the universal Intellect and the universal Soul, the midwife who delivers being the *hudud of the da'wa*. The doctrinal payload sets the architecture for *Chapter Eight*'s coming question of *al-qada and al-qadar* (judgment and destiny) — how the chain runs in time.
