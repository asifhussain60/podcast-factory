### Passage 1

> Al-Kirmani's correction of the four-classes scheme (sub-chapter one): *The investigators of these matters did not rely on sound research. The author of al-Islah divided the world into four jewels, four structures, and four classes including humankind — and that is impossible, because the divisions of the world are more numerous than what we have mentioned. 'World' is a name that refers to many things, from the center to the encompassing ocean — the throne, the planets, fire, air, water, earth, and the born.*

### Passage 2

> Al-Kirmani's settled doctrine of the three genera (sub-chapter one): *The generated beings of the world are three genera with no fourth, each of them having many species under it... the human, mentioned as the fourth, is of the genus of animal yet distinguished by a kind of consciousness.*

### Passage 3

> Al-Kirmani's pairing-by-occasion doctrine (sub-chapter two): *Humankind, since it is similar in its structure, morals, actions, conditions, motions, and natures to all that the universe of existence presents... must be a pair to all things, in accordance with the occasion and the case at hand... And on this pattern, whatever a person is content to resemble — whether by his actions, his morals, his customs, or in any part of them — that becomes his pair. Humankind has many forms and many pairs from many sides.*

*Why this matters:* [LLM-FILL]

### Passage 4

> Al-Kirmani's strict-sense *pair of humankind* doctrine (sub-chapter three): *In truth, the pair of humankind can only be the angels — and what we mean by 'humankind' here are those of all virtues: the prophets, the guardians, and the imams (prayers of God be upon them) and those who follow in their footsteps, uphold their obedience, and worship Allah with both worships.*

*Why this matters:* [LLM-FILL]

### Passage 5

> Al-Kirmani's *ta'wil* of Bandhaqlis (sub-chapter four): *By 'the sensual soul' he means those in the form of humankind who would accept education, discipline, and reason — not the horse — and by 'the speaking soul' he means the teachers, who are the prophets, the guardians, the imams (peace be upon them), and their followers, who would be kind to those in the form of humankind, teach them their interests in religion and worldly affairs, benefit them in ways they do not perceive with their senses, and raise them in degrees according to their acceptance — being in truth the mothers of humankind.*

*Why this matters:* [LLM-FILL]

### Passage 6

> Al-Kirmani's microcosm-of-all-worlds doctrine (sub-chapter five): *The human being is not only a counterpart of this world; he is a counterpart for all the worlds, since he is the collector of all the things that preceded him in existence, similar to what is in him for both of them. The matter rests on this, not on what he said.*

*Why this matters:* [LLM-FILL]

### Passage 7

> Al-Kirmani's closing formula on the two impressions (sub-chapter six): *That world has impressed him, and his body has been marked by this world; and given that this is so, there is no distinction between him and what has gone before except by rank alone.*

*Why this matters:* [LLM-FILL]

### Passage 8

> Al-Islah's *between*-state doctrine, accepted by al-Kirmani (sub-chapter seven): *He is in a state of existence between the two worlds, like the state of the boy in his existence between his two parents: it is not permissible to attribute him to one of them alone, because he is from both of them, and from them he came to be... So it is permissible to attribute to this world what runs in him from the course of matter, which is the acceptable, and to attribute to that world what runs in him from the course of the image, which is the acceptable, just as in the case of the child who has, on the one hand, his parents in the highest and most honorable picture, and, on the other hand, the one who delivered that piece — namely the midwife.*

*Why this matters:* [LLM-FILL]

### Passage 10

> Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him), *Diwan al-Imam Ali*: *Your remedy is within you, but you do not perceive it. Your illness is from yourself, but you do not see it. You suppose yourself a small body, yet in you is folded the greater world. You are the manifest book, by whose letters what is hidden becomes apparent.* — surfaced for sub-chapter two's pairing-by-occasion doctrine and the chapter's bridge image at its close, naming the human as the *manifest book* whose content is chosen.
