# Show notes — EP12

**Title:** Qada and Qadar — Fate and Destiny

**Blurb:** Twenty-four sub-chapters of *Chapter Eight* of *Kitab al-Riyad* — al-Kirmani's adjudication of *al-qada* (judgment, decree) and *al-qadar* (destiny, measure) — what they mean, which is prior, which is posterior, and what the order of the chain that runs in time looks like at every rank. The author of *al-Islah* had argued that destiny precedes decree because *destiny is estimation and decree is detail* — like a tailor estimating a garment before he cuts it. The author of *al-Nusra* had inverted the order: decree precedes destiny, like the *spiritual image of the sewing* in the tailor's soul precedes the appreciation by which he prepares to cut. Sub-chapters one and two frame the opening dispute on the definitions and the two tailor-analogies, and land al-Kirmani's first move (the dispute cannot stand on the bodily reading alone). Sub-chapters three through five give the three meanings of *qada* (the speaker-Prophet, the bringing-out by *ta'wil*, the metaphysical fixity of the world's laws). Sub-chapters six through eight give the three meanings of *qadar* (the promised *Qa'im*, the revelation and Shariʿah, the world unfolding in time), with Quran 70:6–7 as the Qur'anic anchor. Sub-chapter nine corrects the doctrine of the First Intellect as *the assembly of the images of the worlds*. Sub-chapters ten through fifteen work Quran 54:49 (*Indeed, We have created everything according to a measure*) and give the *Pen-Tablet-Writer* architecture. Sub-chapters sixteen through nineteen work the Prophetic word *Flee from Allah's qada to His qadar* in three meanings, with Quran 18:77 as al-Islah's anchor. Sub-chapter twenty gives the political *ta'wil* of Moses asking Shuʿayb for the agency to lift the affliction of the people of truth at the wrong time (Quran 7:143 and Quran 41:53). Sub-chapters twenty-one and twenty-two refuse the doctrine *the mind is all things and all things are the mind*. Sub-chapter twenty-three refuses *al-Nusra*'s reading of Quran 5:93 as a license to abandon the Shariʿah and gives the verse a different *ta'wil* (a license to the *du'at* to study outside sciences as a weapon under three conditions). Sub-chapter twenty-four lands the three-position order: *decree at the beginning, apportioning in the middle, completion at the end* — anchored to Quran 89:27–30 and the *reassured soul* that *returns to its Lord, pleased and pleasing*. The doctrinal payload sets the architecture for *Chapter Nine*'s coming question of *the law of Adam and the wilayah of Nuh* — how the chain that runs in time first establishes itself at the head of human history.
