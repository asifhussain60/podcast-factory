# Show notes — EP10

**Title:** Motion, Stillness, *al-Hayula*, and Form

**Blurb:** Nine sub-chapters of *Chapter Six* of *Kitab al-Riyad* — al-Kirmani's adjudication of the next argument the two reformers handed down: what are motion, stillness, *al-hayula* (prime matter), and form, and what kind of analogy from soul-and-body, if any, can honestly capture them? The author of *al-Islah* had offered a gentler-and-denser proportion: motion-and-stillness as the *soul* of *al-hayula*-and-form. The author of *al-Nusra* had refused the proportion and offered a structural parallel keyed to division and similarity of parts. Sub-chapter one frames the dispute and lands the chapter's structural doctrine: motion and stillness are *opposites that do not meet in the same place at the same time*; form and *al-hayula* are *inseparable correlates, actor and acted-upon*. Sub-chapter two presses the consequence — *al-Islah's* proportion would force the soul to be a body, which it is not. Sub-chapter three handles *al-Nusra's* structural parallel and settles the synonymy of *al-nafs* and *al-ruh* as one non-bodily reality. Sub-chapter four delivers the positive doctrine of motion (*that by which the same thing changes*) and the architectural map (form-and-*al-hayula* as *the first emanation*). Sub-chapter five handles *al-Islah's* analogy from *kun* and refuses it *as an image*. Sub-chapter six handles the related doctrine of two proportions and qualifies the analogy *by first-and-accident*. Sub-chapter seven is the chapter's hinge — the *grave error* of *pre-creative creativity*, refused categorically in the name of *tawhid*: *the Creative IS the creativity*. Sub-chapter eight seals the doctrine at the First Effect: above it, no motion, no rest, no body-talk whatsoever. Sub-chapter nine closes on what contradictions among bodies actually are — not in their being bodies, but in *the images within them*. The doctrinal payload sets the architecture for *Chapter Seven's* coming question of *the sections of the world*: the body's differentiation is at the form-level, the image-level, what *al-hayula* has received; the world's architectural divisions follow the proportions of receivership at each rank, the same architecture *Chapter Four* settled for the souls and *Chapter Five* settled for the human being.
