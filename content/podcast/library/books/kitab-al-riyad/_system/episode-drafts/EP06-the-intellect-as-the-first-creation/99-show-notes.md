# Show notes — EP06

**Title:** The Intellect as the First Creation

**Blurb:** Nine sub-chapters of the second chapter of *Kitab al-Riyad* — al-Kirmani's narrow, dense argument that the First and Second Intellects are not bodies, and that the whole vocabulary of motion, stillness, need, dwelling, and encompassing therefore does not apply to them. The chapter answers two competing wrong pictures (al-Razi's "motion and stillness united in the First and the Second" and al-Sijistani's "motion for the Soul, stillness for the Intellect") with the same correction: the categories of bodies do not transfer to the world of origination. The structural climax is the overflow doctrine: the *hudud* withhold nothing; the limitation is in the receiver's capacity. The Quranic anchors (17:31 and 4:6) carry the doctrinal weight. The chapter ends on the warning that confusion about the ranks of the *hudud* corrupts the essence of the souls that try to perceive without that discipline.
