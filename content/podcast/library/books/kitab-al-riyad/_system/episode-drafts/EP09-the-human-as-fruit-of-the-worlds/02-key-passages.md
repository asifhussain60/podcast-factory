### Passage 1

> Al-Kirmani's third word — the chapter's settled formula: *Each is truthful in what he said on one side, and each is untruthful on another. The side on which they are both truthful is that each of them has said of human beings what belongs to him. The side on which they are both untruthful is in denying the other its share. For man rightly bears the title of fruit of all the worlds, because human beings issue from between the two worlds. From this world he receives his first perfection; from that world he receives his second.*

### Passage 2

> Al-Kirmani's structural formula (sub-chapter two): *Human beings are in fact human beings precisely because they are formed by the meeting of the overflow of the worlds: from the natural world, what flows in the channel of the bodies; and from the world of the Intellect, what flows in the channel of the form. And this is in the middle.*

### Passage 3

> Al-Kirmani's settled doctrine of perfection by rank (sub-chapter three): *Deficiency is found only in things — not in their selves, but in their ranks when they are added to one another, and in their actions when they do not exist except in something other than that which is done with their existence. In itself each is perfect; for if their selves were not perfect in the existence that befits them, nothing could be done by them — neither by themselves nor by another.*

### Passage 4

> Al-Kirmani's settled doctrine on the divine names (sub-chapter five): *We say that the Creative is the perfection, only perfect, and the Creator is the creativity; he is the cause, the effect (al-ma'lul), the perfect, and the perfected. These names are synonymous according to the additions, because there is a multiplicity of meanings upon which these names fall, yet there is only one thing.*

*Why this matters:* [LLM-FILL]

### Passage 5

> Al-Kirmani's refusal of the seeking-doctrine (sub-chapter six): *The universal soul, in what is indicated of her, did not seek in her action to reach a rank she had not attained. The universal soul, which is the second, overflows upon this world not to be drawn to it for what is not hers — God enriched her when he originated her, with no need to seek what she was not given. Rather, her action is to establish wisdom in bringing what is in potentiality into actuality.*

*Why this matters:* [LLM-FILL]

### Passage 6

> Al-Kirmani's deepest formula on the human being (sub-chapter six): *Human beings have two sides: one part natural, one part divine. He is not the fruit of this world alone, nor the fruit of that world alone, but the fruit of all the worlds. He is the last in the universes, united in eternity with what has been acquired by time. Therefore it has been said that what came first of them was the last of them.*

*Why this matters:* [LLM-FILL]

### Passage 7

> Al-Kirmani's closing on the First's metaphorical action (sub-chapter seven): *This is not necessary for the First Creator and Originator, because the action there is metaphorical, not in motion nor in order. Rather, the first is the last; the beginning of it is the middle, and the middle of it is the end. There is no order in it, no system; rather, the systems and order in what comes after benefit from the lights of what is not in order or system — that is the First Intellect.*
