# Show notes — EP09

**Title:** The Human Being — Fruit of All the Worlds

**Blurb:** Seven sub-chapters of *Chapter Five* of *Kitab al-Riyad* — al-Kirmani's adjudication of the next great dispute between the two reformers: is the human being the *fruit* of this world (the harvest of the long natural ascent from elements into the form prepared to receive the three souls), as the author of *al-Islah* said, or the fruit of the *higher* world (the abode of survival to which the prophets called us), as the author of *al-Nusra* insisted? Sub-chapter one frames the dispute and lands the third word: the human being is the *fruit of all the worlds* — first perfection from the natural world, second perfection from the higher world, on the authority of Imam al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah's *Ta'wil al-Shari'a*. Sub-chapter two turns *al-Nusra's* "would-not-move" objection back on him and gives the structural formula: human beings are formed by *the meeting of the overflow of the worlds*. Sub-chapter three refuses *al-Islah's* extension of emanation to *al-Hayula* AND *al-Nusra's* perfect-and-deficient marriage analogy, settling the doctrine that each rank is perfect in itself and deficient only when added to another. Sub-chapter four settles the name *al-Tamamiyyah* on the Creator. Sub-chapter five settles the synonymy of the divine names (cause, effect, perfect, perfected) *according to the additions* — one named, multiple aspects, the same architecture *Chapter Four* settled for Intellect-and-Word. Sub-chapter six delivers the chapter's deepest move: the universal Soul *did not seek* in her action; God enriched her at origination; her overflow is the work of giving, not of getting. From that, the chapter's signature formula: *human beings have two sides — one part natural, one part divine; the last in the universes, united in eternity with what has been acquired by time*. Sub-chapter seven closes on the First Creator's metaphorical action and the Intellect that perceives only the truth. The doctrinal payload sets the architecture for *Chapter Six's* coming question of motion, stillness, *al-Hayula*, and form: the human being is the rank at which the chain of emanation from above meets the chain of natural cause-and-effect from below; the *hudud* convey the gift; the believer who knows the houses of the Prophets, the guardians, the imams, and the righteous is ready for salvation, gathered in *Dar al-Quds*.

  - Imam al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah Ma'add ibn Isma'il (peace be upon him), *Ta'wil al-Shari'a* (The Interpretation of Sharia) — the Cairo-court treatise on the inner meaning of the divine law; cited by al-Kirmani in sub-chapter one as the highest authority for the doctrine that *man is the purpose of the second world and the end of the action of nature*; same treatise *Chapter Four* (sub-chapter eight) reached for on the Intellect-Word identity
  - Quran 95:4–6 (We created man of the best stature; then reduced him to the lowest of the low; except those who believe and do righteous deeds, for them an unfailing reward) — the *ta'wil* anchor the *da'wa* tradition reaches for when explaining the human being's two-faced rank, central to sub-chapter one's reading of the first and second perfections
  - Quran 23:115 (Did you think We had created you in vain, and that you would not be returned to Us?) — surfaced in sub-chapter six against the picture of the human being as the fruit of this world alone; the verse the *da'wa* tradition reaches for when explaining why the *Ma'ad* is the human being's terminal harvest
  - Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him), aphoristic tradition on the human as the *small body* in which is *folded the greater world* — surfaced for sub-chapter two's structural formula on the human being as the meeting of the two overflows
  - Hadith of the Prophet ﷺ — *whoever knows himself knows his Lord* — surfaced for sub-chapter one's closing on self-knowledge as the work of recognizing the One from whom both overflows run
