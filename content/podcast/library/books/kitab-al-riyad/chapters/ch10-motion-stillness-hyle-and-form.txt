# Motion,Stillness,*prime matter*,and Form

*This chapter covers all nine sub-chapters of* Chapter Six *of* this book,*the author's adjudication of the next argument the two reformers handed down. The author of* the book *The Correction* *had proposed a clean two-rung analogy: motion and stillness stand to* prime matter *(prime matter) and form the way the soul stands to the body, the gentler is like the soul,the denser like the body,motion-and-stillness are the soul of form-and-hyle,and form-and-hyle are the body of motion-and-stillness. The author of* the book *The Defense* *had refused the gentler-and-denser proportion as the measure of soul-and-body,accidents are gentler than bodies,yet no one calls accidents the souls of bodies,and offered in its place a structural parallel: body is what accepts the three dimensions (length,width,depth); soul is what has parts that resemble each other; motion divides into three sections the way the body does (motion from the center,motion toward the center,motion around the middle),so motion is like the body,while* prime matter's *parts resemble each other and are therefore like the soul. The author works the nine sub-chapters as the same triangulating adjudication he gave* Chapters Four *and* Five,*but now on a different question: what motion,stillness,* prime matter,*and form actually are,and what kind of analogy from soul-and-body,if any,can honestly capture them. The chapter answers in stages. Both reformers have strayed far from honest representation. Motion and stillness are opposites that cannot meet in the same place at the same time,so they cannot be like form and* prime matter,*which are inseparable in their being. Form and* prime matter *are the first emanation; together they constitute the body,with form as actor and* prime matter *as acted-upon. The soul is not a body,so density and gentleness do not apply to it.* Al-nafs *and* the spirit *are two names for one thing that is not embodied at all. The* kun *of Allah's command,taken as two letters with a voweled* kaf *and a consonant* nun,*is a remote analogy at best,first and by accident; the* Be *of Allah is not a word with letters in any natural sense,and creativity itself shows itself only in the First Creator. Above the First Effect there is no motion,no stillness,no body-talk whatsoever; the First's action toward what is below is metaphorical,not real motion. And the contradictions among bodies are not in their being bodies,bodies are bodies because they are objects,but in the images within them. The chapter sets the architecture of the physical world (*prime matter *and form,motion and stillness,body and image) on the same footing the chain of emanation was set on: the names belong to the ranks by proportion and aspect,not by sharing of substance; what holds the chain together is the running of the single originating power from the Intellect-which-is-the-Word through the proportions of receivership at each rank.*

## Where this chapter picks up

*Chapter Five* of *this book* settled the rank of the human being. The human being is the *fruit of all the worlds*,from the natural world he receives his first perfection (the bodily form prepared to receive the three souls),from the higher world he receives his second perfection (the consummation of the spiritual image,the knowledge of the Oneness of Allah Almighty by means of the *the limits*). The human being is *the last in the universes,united in eternity with what has been acquired by time*. That settled the rank.

But the rank stands on an architecture. The natural world overflows downward in the channel of bodies; the world of the Intellect overflows downward in the channel of form; the human being is at the rank where the two overflows meet. *Chapter Six* turns to the architecture itself. What *is* the lower channel, the channel of bodies,at its first emergence from the Intellect? What are *motion* and *stillness*,what are *prime matter* (prime matter,*hayle*) and *form* (*sura*),and how do they relate to each other and to the soul-and-body categories that came down from the *falsafa* and have been mapped onto every rank of the chain in turn?

The question is not idle. The two reformers, the author of the book *The Correction* (the late Jonathan) and the author of the book *The Defense* (his successor in the school,Samuel), and their predecessor the author of the book *The Harvest* (Muhammad Jonathan) had each handed down their own picture. *The book *The Correction** worked by gentler-denser analogy: motion and stillness are the soul of *prime matter* and form,the way the soul is the soul of the body. *The book *The Defense** refused the gentler-denser as the *measure* of soul-and-body and offered a structural analogy keyed to division and similarity of parts. Both pictures carried doctrinal weight. If motion and stillness really are the soul of *prime matter* and form,the doctrine of *monotheism* and the doctrine of the First's relation to His creation get drawn in a particular shape,and one that risks importing body-talk back into ranks where body-talk cannot reach. *Chapter Six* exists to refuse the bad analogies,settle what motion and *prime matter* actually are,and protect the doctrine of *monotheism* at the top of the chain.

Nine sub-chapters. The first three handle the soul-and-body analogy itself,what is wrong with *the book *The Correction*'s* gentler-denser,what is wrong with *the book *The Defense*'s* structural parallel,why *the soul* and *the spirit* are not bodies at all. The fourth sub-chapter delivers the positive doctrine of motion and gestures to the architecture of *prime matter* and form as the first emanation. The fifth handles *the book *The Correction*'s kun*-as-two-letters image. The sixth handles the related claim about two states attributed to the Self by two honorable proportions. The seventh delivers the doctrinal climax,*the book *The Correction*'s* grave error about creativity,the refusal that protects *monotheism*. The eighth confirms that above the First Effect there is no motion or rest. The ninth closes on what contradictions among bodies actually are,not in their being bodies,but in the images within them. The chapter walks the dispute through nine passes and lands the same way *Chapters Four* and *Five* landed: by giving the third word.

## Sub-chapter one, the gentler-and-denser analogy refused

The author of the book *The Correction* opens *Chapter Six* with his clean proportion. *Motion and stillness are like the soul to form and hyle,because form and hyle are like the body to motion and stillness.* The picture is symmetric and intuitive. The soul is *gentler* than the body; the body is *denser* than the soul. Likewise motion and stillness are *gentler* than *prime matter* and form; *prime matter* and form are *denser* than motion and stillness. By the same gentler-denser measure that distinguishes soul from body,motion-and-stillness stand to *prime matter*-and-form as soul stands to body.

*The book *The Correction** is careful with the direction of representation. *To represent the gentler with the soul and the denser with the body is premature*,he concedes,meaning,you cannot simply say that *whatever* is gentler is a soul. *But to represent the denser with the body and the gentler with the soul*, to read the body off the dense and the soul off the gentle,*this is clear*. Density and gentleness *are* properties of bodies; the proportion is keyed at the body side and reaches up by gentleness to the soul side. That is the proportion,the book *The Correction* says,and *the proof of it is with him*, the proof is in his book,the proof is his to give.

The author of the book *The Defense* refuses the proportion at its root. *This is not the case of the soul and the body,where the measure between them is only the gentler and the denser.* The gentler-denser cannot be the *measure* of the distinction between soul and body,because if it were,then by the same measure tangible essences would have to be objects of accidents,since tangible essences are denser than accidents,and accidents would have to be *souls for them*,since accidents are gentler than tangible essences. That is absurd. No one calls an accident a soul. So the gentler-denser cannot be what distinguishes the soul from the body.

*The book *The Defense** offers in its place a structural analysis. *We must look to the limit of the body and the soul,and then liken what resembles the limit of the soul to the soul,and what resembles the limit of the body to the body.* The *limit* of the body is what makes it the kind of thing it is. We find the body to accept the three dimensions,length,width,and depth. The *limit* of the soul is the union of its parts in similarity. We find the soul to have parts that resemble each other.

Now apply the test. Motion is divided into three sections,just as the body is divided into three dimensions. *Motion from the center* (away from the middle),*motion toward the center* (toward the middle),and *motion around the middle* (rotational). Three sections,just as length,width,and depth are three. So motion *resembles* the body in its division. Not so for *prime matter*: the parts of *prime matter* resemble each other,undifferentiated,like the soul. So *prime matter* resembles the soul. The first thing that resembles motion with the soul,the book *The Defense* says,is the *indivisible body*,and the *prime matter* analogue of it is undivided. *This is what they said.*

The author enters with the verdict the chapter has earned. *In this chapter the two of them have strayed far from representation and simile; and between us and the two of them lies a corrupt foundation upon which the proof is built,neither the proverb nor its representation is true.* Both reformers have built on a corrupt foundation. The proverb (the comparison itself) is not true,and the representation derived from it is not true either. The reason runs deep.

*The author of* the book *The Correction* *has made it obligatory that motion and stillness be like the soul,since the mover is the act of the engine,and the mover is what we call the soul, the soul.* The argument from *the book *The Correction*'s* own premises destroys his analogy. What does motion *come from*? From the mover. What is the mover? The principle in the moved thing by which the moved thing moves. And what is that principle,when it is *self-moving* in the way *the book *The Correction*'s* analogy needs? It is *the engine*. And what is *the engine* in *the book *The Correction*'s* own vocabulary? It is *the soul*. To represent motion,which is *the act of* the engine,the act of the soul,*by* the engine itself,by the soul itself,is to represent the act by the actor. That is no representation at all. The act is not the actor. The motion is not the soul that does the moving. To say *motion is like the soul* is to say *the act of the soul is like the soul*,which is to say nothing,or worse,to make the act into a second soul standing beside the first.

The same point holds for stillness,the author notes. *Stillness is the engine letting what it moves go.* Stillness is the engine *not moving* what it could move. If the engine is the soul,then stillness too is the act of the soul. To represent it by the soul is the same mistake. *And if this is invalidated for motion and stillness,then it is also invalidated that motion and stillness are hyle and form.* The whole proportion,motion/stillness : *prime matter*/form :: soul : body,collapses at its first rung. Once *the book *The Correction*'s* mapping of motion-and-stillness onto soul fails,the whole proportion has nothing to stand on.

But the author is not finished. He has refused *the book *The Correction*'s* analogy. He must now say what the right doctrine actually is. *The path of hyle and form is not the path of motion and stillness.* Motion and *prime matter* run in different ranks. The two cannot be aligned in a simple proportion. *Motion and stillness,even when they are actualized,are two opposites that do not meet in the same place at the same time.* That is a structural fact about motion-and-stillness. They are *opposites*,a single body cannot be simultaneously moving and at rest at the same place at the same time in the same respect. They occupy distinct moments,distinct conditions; their actuality is the actuality of mutually exclusive states. *As for form and hyle: their existence together is such that neither is separable from the other.*

That is the structural fact about form and *prime matter*. They are *inseparable*. Wherever form is,*prime matter* is; wherever *prime matter* is,form is. They are not opposites that exclude each other; they are *correlates* that constitute each other. *Provided that one of them is an actor,which is the form,and the other is acted upon in it,which is* prime matter. Form is the *actor*. *Al-hayula* is the *acted-upon*. Form *informs*. *Al-hayula receives*. Together they constitute the body.

So the architecture the author is settling on is structural,not proportional. *Al-hayula* and form are a *single coupling*: actor and acted-upon,inseparable in being,together constituting the body. Motion and stillness are *opposites*: mutually exclusive states,distinct in being,the two ways a body can be considered with respect to its mover. The two pairs run on entirely different patterns. You cannot map them onto each other in any clean analogy. *The book *The Correction*'s* gentler-denser misses this; *the book *The Defense*'s* dimensions-and-similarity misses it too. The right name for what *prime matter* and form are is *the first emanation*,of which more in sub-chapter four. The right name for what motion and stillness are is *the way a moved thing changes*,of which also more in sub-chapter four. And *Our book known as* The Rest of the Mind *(*the author's other treatise*) contains the explanation of that.*

*the author's other treatise*,*The Repose of the Intellect*,is the author's magnum opus,his architectural treatise on the universal Intellect,the seven seas,the chain of overflow from the First through the Second into the natural composite,and the *the limits* through which the gift of the higher world is conveyed. The book the rest of *this book* sends the reader to when the question turns architectural. Here in *Chapter Six's* opening sub-chapter,*the author's other treatise* is the standing reference for the doctrine that *prime matter* and form are the first emanation,that together they constitute the natural composite,that motion and stillness are the modes in which the moved thing is considered with respect to its mover,and that the soul-and-body analogy from the *falsafa* does not transfer cleanly onto any of these.

## Sub-chapter two, the soul is not a body

Sub-chapter two presses the consequence of *the book *The Correction*'s* gentler-denser proportion. *The book *The Correction** was *obliged to say that the soul is gentler than the body*, that is the half-formula that the gentler-denser analogy required. But to be gentler than something, to stand in the gentler-denser relation at all,is to be a *body*. *Density and gentleness are properties only of that which is a body*,the author says. Air is a body,and air is *characterized by gentleness*. Earth is a body,and earth is *characterized by density*. Whatever is denser or gentler in the natural-philosophy sense is a body. There is no such thing as a *non-bodily* density or a *non-bodily* gentleness.

So *the book *The Correction*'s* proportion was forced into a hidden conclusion he could not have meant: that the soul is a body. *The soul cannot be embodied with density or gentleness,for density and gentleness are properties only of that which is a body.* If the soul could be characterized as gentler than the body,then the soul would have to be a body characterized by gentleness,the way air is a body characterized by gentleness. But the soul is precisely not a body. *The soul is the soul,and the proof rests on the fact that it is not embodied.* That is the categorical refusal.

And if the soul is not embodied,then *it is impossible to argue that it is gentler or denser,or that it is represented by such terms.* The gentler-denser vocabulary is body-vocabulary. The soul falls outside it altogether. The analogy the book *The Correction* built,motion and stillness gentler,*prime matter* and form denser,the proportion running by gentleness-and-density,relied on a vocabulary the soul does not admit. The analogy was,at the body side,an analogy with bodies and accidents at most. It could not reach the soul at all.

This is the same categorical refusal that *Chapter Two* of *this book* had built and that *Chapter Three* had pressed. The soul does not belong to the order of bodies. The categories of body-philosophy,extension,dimension,density,gentleness,the nine accidents,do not apply to the soul. Anything said about the soul that *requires* it to fall under those categories is,by that requirement,saying something about *a body*,not about the soul. *The book *The Correction*'s* proportion failed not because the proportion was clumsy but because it was built from materials that belong on one side of the soul-body divide and cannot reach across.

## Sub-chapter three,*the soul* and *the spirit* as one thing not embodied

Sub-chapter three turns to *the book *The Defense*'s* structural proposal. *The book *The Defense** had promised to set *the limit of the body and the soul*, to give the kind-defining feature of each,so that other things could be compared to that feature. He limited the body by its accepting the three dimensions. He limited the soul by the union of its parts in similarity,illustrated by a stream of water,fire,or air,*each of which is similar in its parts*. The picture is the standard *falsafa* picture: a body is what has length,width,depth; a soul is what is homogeneous,undifferentiated,like a flowing stream.

The author's verdict on the structural proposal is patient. The structural analysis ends up arriving at the same place the gentler-denser analysis arrived at,at the doctrine that the soul is *not* embodied. *It is impossible to go beyond saying that the soul is not embodied.* Whatever clever structural parallel the book *The Defense* draws,the upshot collapses to the categorical refusal: the soul is not a body,and no positive structural feature transferred from the body-side will tell us what the soul *is*; it will only tell us what the soul *is not*.

And the author gives the underlying reason. *Al-nafs* and *the spirit* are two synonyms for the same thing,and that thing is not a body. The two Arabic words for the soul,*the soul* and *the spirit*,which the *falsafa* often distinguishes into a vegetative-and-animal soul (*the soul*) and a rational-and-divine spirit (*the spirit*), are,in the *the call* reading the author is settling,names for the same single non-bodily reality. The same thing under two names. There are not two different things to distinguish; there is one reality,called *the soul* in one aspect and *the spirit* in another. And that reality is *not a body*.

The proof comes in a clean conditional. *If it were a body,the body would necessarily require of it the nine qualities and accidents that the body needs,and it could be perceived by sense.* The standard inventory of bodily accidents, the nine categories that *Chapter Two* enumerated,applies to anything that is a body. A body has quantity,quality,relation,place,time,position,possession,action,passion. A body is *perceived by sense*, that is what bodies are,the kind of thing the senses register. *But since it is not perceived by sense,it is not perceivable,and the nine accidents do not exist in it. Since the physical accidents, the hallmark of being a body,do not exist in it [it is not a body].* The senses do not register the soul. The nine accidents do not adhere in the soul. By the same proof that establishes a sense-object as a body,the failure to be a sense-object establishes the soul as a non-body. And the author signs the doctrine by referring the reader to his own treatise. *Our well-known book* With Lamps *contains what heals this*, the book that lays out the architecture in full.

What this sub-chapter delivers,on top of *Chapter Two*'s earlier work,is the *synonymy*: *the soul* and *the spirit* are one thing. The *falsafa* tradition had a habit of splitting them and locating their distinction at some point along a hierarchy. The *the call* reading the author settles refuses the split at that level. The two names are aspects of one non-bodily reality. The architecture *Chapter Four* settled, the universal Soul as first emanation,the speaking souls as second emanation,needs this synonymy in order to hold. If *the soul* and *the spirit* were two different ranks,the chain would have a hidden duplication; if they are one thing called twice,the chain holds with the structure *Chapter Four* gave it.

## Sub-chapter four,what motion really is,and the nine sections of the body

Sub-chapter four turns from the soul-body analogy to *the book *The Defense*'s* division of motion into three sections. *The book *The Defense** had said: body divides into three dimensions; motion divides into three corresponding sections (from-center,to-center,around-center); therefore motion is like the body. The author refuses the parallel at its root. *This is impossible.* For two reasons.

First,*the three sections into which the body is allegedly divided,which are the three dimensions,each one of these three dimensions is not embodied; it is not said to be a body.* A *dimension* is not a body. Length is not a body; width is not a body; depth is not a body. The body is what *has* the three dimensions; the dimensions themselves are not bodies. So you cannot say a body divides *into* three bodies-of-dimension and call that division-into-bodies.

Second,*the motion of each of its sections is itself a motion,and is said to be a motion.* Each of *the book *The Defense*'s* three sections of motion,from-center,to-center,around-center,is *itself a motion*. Not a part-of-motion; a motion. So motion does not divide into three things that are not motions,the way a body might divide into three dimensions that are not bodies. Motion divides into three modes that are *each motions*. The pattern of division is structurally unlike the body's.

And the author brings in his own architecture at the third stroke. *So how can it be likened to the body,which is divided into nine sections,as we have shown in our well-known book* Milestones of Religion,*with a motion divided into three sections,or six sections? That is far-fetched.* The body,in the author's own settled doctrine,set out in his *Aqlam of the faith* (*Milestones of Religion*),divides not into three but into *nine* sections,corresponding to the *nine accidents* of *Chapter Two*'s catalog. The body is the substrate of the nine. To liken a three-sectioned motion to a nine-sectioned body is to liken unequal things by counting. The numbers do not match; the kinds do not match. *The discussion in this chapter from the author of* the book *The Harvest*,*the author of* the book *The Correction*,*and the author of* the book *The Defense* *is a disordered disorder.*

And then comes the positive doctrine. *Motion is that by which the same thing changes,either by place,or by universe,or by corruption,or by increase,or by decrease,or by impossibility.* That is what motion *is*. Motion is *change*, the way *the same thing* differs at two moments. Change of *place* is the most obvious motion (a body moves from here to there). Change *by universe* (alteration in the cosmos around it). Change *by corruption* (decay,dissolution). Change *by increase* (growth). Change *by decrease* (diminution). Change *by impossibility* (cessation,the passing-out-of-being of what was). Motion is not one of these but the *category-name* under which all of these fall: any change in which the same thing differs.

And the *occurrence* of motion,what makes it happen,is *from the mover,which is the principle in the thing in which the motion resides,and of course not by width*. The mover is the *principle* in the thing, the inner cause by which the change occurs. Not by *width*,not by an external dimension that pushes it. The mover is the principle from which the change runs.

Then the author makes the architectural turn that sets the rest of the chapter. *Hyle and form are the first emanation of the following proportion,just as the second is upon the noble proportion within it.* *Al-hayula* and form,taken as the actor-and-acted-upon pairing that sub-chapter one named,are the *first emanation*, the first thing the chain of emanation produces in the direction of the natural world. *The Second*,meaning the universal Soul,the rank above *prime matter* and form,is *upon the noble proportion within it*,the proportion of cause-and-effect that *Chapter Four* settled. The architecture *Chapter Four* gave is mapped onto the question of *prime matter* and form: as the universal Soul is to the Intellect,so *prime matter*-and-form is to the universal Soul,in the order of overflow from First through Second to the natural substrate.

*There is no existence of the soul outside the soul in the first place,[but only] together with form,including the heavens and the earth and everything between them.* The soul does not exist *outside* of soul-with-form, that is,outside of the natural composite,*anywhere except in its own rank above*. In the natural world,the soul is found only in the natural composite,the body informed by form. The heavens,the earth,and everything between them are the architecture of the natural composite,and the soul-with-form is what gives that composite its life. *God Almighty created all of this from the First Intellect,which was itself created by God,not from any thing,at a great height.* The First Intellect, the universal Intellect,the rank *Chapter Two* of *this book* settled as the first creation,was created by God *not from any thing*,by origination. Everything else emanates from the First Intellect by proportion of receivership. That is the architecture *the author's other treatise* gave; that is the architecture *Chapter Six* of *this book* applies to the question of motion,stillness,*prime matter*,and form.

## Sub-chapter five,*kun* and the imaginary character of *prime matter*

Sub-chapter five turns to *the book *The Correction*'s* deepest analogy: the analogy from the divine command *kun* (Be!). *The first cause,which is creativity,is the word of the Almighty and Exalted,and its form is* kun *("Be!"),which is two letters, the vowelled* kaf *and the* nun. The picture the book *The Correction* is drawing: the First Cause is *creativity* (the originating power); creativity is *the word* of the Almighty; the word is *kun*; *kun* has two letters,one of them voweled (the *kaf*,carrying motion) and the other a consonant (the *nun*,carrying stillness). *The consonant, the* kaf *and the* nun,*of the word stand in the position of the body,while motion and stillness are like the soul.* The letters as letters are the body of *kun*; the motion-on-the-*kaf* and stillness-on-the-*nun* are the soul of *kun*. The whole world,*kun* being the cause of the whole world,is the consonant-and-vowel analogue at the level of creation.

And the book *The Correction* presses the doctrine further. *The first beings are hyles, they are motion and stillness,and they are the first hyles,their form,and the second hyles,which are individuals and generators of the first hyles. And the third hyles are the four composite mother [elements] of the second hyles.* The first hyles are motion and stillness themselves; the second hyles are the individuals (the *simple* bodies) generated from the first hyles; the third hyles are *the four composite mother-elements*, the four classical elements (earth,water,air,fire) which compose the second hyles into the natural world we see.

The author's first move on this picture is to name what kind of thing the *hayle* talk is,in *the book *The Correction*'s* own hands. *The hyle and its form are the act of the command,which is creativity. As we have said,the hyles are imaginary; so too is creativity imaginary,it shows itself only in the First Creator.* The *hayles*,considered as the act of the divine command,are *imaginary* (of the imagination,illusory). And creativity itself,considered apart from the First Creator,is imaginary. *Creativity shows itself only in the First Creator.* There is no creativity standing on its own as a separate thing; creativity is what the First Creator does. To talk about *prime matter* and its form,and about creativity,as if they were free-standing realities is to deal in *images*,not in essences in their own right. The *hayle* is real as the *acted-upon* in the natural composite. It is not real as a thing-in-itself prior to its being acted on.

The author of the book *The Defense* refuses the analogy at the highest end. *As for what was raised by the author of* the book *The Correction* *concerning* kun: *he said that the* kaf *is movable and the* nun *is stationary,and that this is like the body,and like motion and stillness.* That is *the book *The Correction*'s* picture. *As for the soul,it is not established that these two letters apply to it,for there is no kaf,no nun,no motion or stillness above the First Effect.* Above the First Effect,above the First Intellect,in the rank of the divine command itself, there is no letter,no motion,no stillness,no body-talk of any kind. *It cannot be said that above the First Effect there is motion or stillness or anything of that sort. Rather,there is only pure creativity and complete reason. This is what they say.*

The author enters with the verdict that walks the analogy back. *The claim by the author of* the book *The Correction*,*that the First Cause,which is creativity,is a word of God whose image consists of two letters,is a remote analogy,however he may have intended it,and it scarcely holds.* The *kun*-as-two-letters image is a *remote* analogy,meaning,it sits far down the chain of resemblance,and it scarcely sustains the weight the book *The Correction* puts on it.

The deeper trouble is with the word *image*. *If we say "image," that implies something that has an image,and not a word. The image is not a picture.* If we are calling *kun* an *image* of the divine command,then *kun* is *of* something that has an image; and the something-that-has-an-image is not itself a *word*. Words and images do not interchange. *If by "image" we mean what is above nature,we mean the abstract image; and if by "image" we mean what belongs to this natural world,we mean the image that is in matter.* There are two senses of *image*: the *abstract* image (what is in the higher world,above nature) and the *image-in-matter* (what is in the natural world,the form-in-*prime matter*). *The book *The Correction*'s* slippage between *word* and *image* trades on this ambiguity without settling it.

And then the author gives the analogy what it can honestly bear. *If he says that creativity,which is the word of God Almighty and His command as expressed by it,consists of two letters,one of them movable and the other consonant, to indicate the union of two attributes,then this points to the noble Self and its inferred qualities.* The two letters of *kun*,*taken as an indication* of the union of two attributes,*point to the noble Self and its inferred qualities*. Not because the noble Self literally has two attributes the way *kun* has two letters. Rather,the *kaf*-and-*nun* analogy can serve as a remote indicator of the doctrine that the divine Self can be considered under more than one aspect,under the aspect of cause and the aspect of effect,under the aspect of origination and the aspect of preservation,under the aspect of the Names that belong to the same single rank. That is what the analogy can honestly carry. *And even so*,the author will say in the next sub-chapter,*the analogy holds only by being first and by accident,which is what the one who calls the First an actor in this manner must concede.*

The Quranic anchor that the *the call* tradition reaches for when explaining the divine command *kun* runs through *Surat the chapter of the mystic letters*:

> *His command,when He intends a thing,is only that He says to it,"Be," and it is.* (Quran 36:82,Surah the chapter of the mystic letters; translation rendered after Pickthall)

The verse names the structure exactly. *His command is only that He says to it,"Be," and it is.* The divine command is not a word with letters in any natural sense. It is the *origination* of the thing intended,*only that He says,and it is*. The verse does not invite a phonological analysis of *kun*. It refuses one. The *Be* is the trace of the divine intention as it appears in the language of revelation; the *being* of the thing is the trace of the divine origination. The verse does not name *kaf*-and-*nun*. The verse names *Be,and it is*. That is what the analogy of *kun* can honestly bear,and no more.

The same shape recurs in *Surat the chapter of the bee*,where the *Be* of Allah is named as the single word that is the whole of His creative act:

> *Our only word for a thing,when We will it,is that We say to it,"Be," and it is.* (Quran 16:40,Surah the chapter of the bee; translation rendered after Pickthall)

Two verses,one structure. *Our only word for a thing*: not a string of letters issued in sequence,not a *kaf*-and-*nun* parsed as movable consonant and silent consonant,but the *single* word that is the divine intention itself. *We say to it,"Be," and it is*: the saying and the being are one act,not two. *The book *The Correction*'s* parsing of *kun* into two letters with a voweled *kaf* and a consonant *nun* asks the verse to carry a phonological architecture the verse refuses. The verse names *one word*,*one intention*,*one act*. The two-letter image,taken at its full weight,breaks against the verse's own grammar,and the *the call* tradition the author is settling reads the verse as the *kun*-analogy's outer limit.

## Sub-chapter six, the two proportions: cause-ending-in-effect,effect-ending-in-cause

Sub-chapter six continues *the book *The Correction*'s* working of the *kun* analogy. *It combines two states,just as the word "be" combines motion and stillness; the two states are attributed to that Self as it relates to two honorable proportions,one of which is being a cause and the other being effect.* The two states *kun* combines,motion (on the *kaf*) and stillness (on the *nun*), are the analogue of two states attributed to the divine Self by way of two *honorable proportions* (*nisbas*): the proportion of *being a cause* (toward what is below) and the proportion of *being an effect* (toward what is above).

*The motion that ends in stillness corresponds to the proportion whose cause ends in the effect,and the stillness that ends in motion corresponds to the proportion that is effect ending in cause.* Motion ending in stillness, the *kaf*-with-vowel moving,the *nun* settling at rest,corresponds,in the analogy,to the proportion of *cause ending in effect*: the originating overflow comes to its rest in what it has produced. Stillness ending in motion, the *nun* at rest,then the *kaf* moving,corresponds to *effect ending in cause*: the produced thing reaching back through receivership to what brought it forth.

And the qualification is critical. *Not because the First has motion or stillness in itself,but only by way of analogy.* The First does not actually have motion or stillness. The First does not change. The First is not at rest in any temporal sense. The motion-and-stillness language is *only* analogical,it borrows from the natural world's vocabulary of change-and-non-change to gesture,however remotely,at the difference between the two proportions under which the First can be considered: cause-toward-below,effect-toward-above.

And the qualification is itself qualified. *Even so,the analogy holds only by being first and by accident,which is what the one who calls the First an actor in this manner must concede.* The analogy holds *by being first*, that is,by virtue of the First's pre-eminence at the head of the chain. And *by accident*, that is,not by any essential resemblance between the First and the motion-and-stillness of bodies. *The book *The Correction**,who has called the First an *actor* in this analogy (the actor whose two-state act is *kun*),must concede that the analogy is *first-and-by-accident*, the weakest grade of analogy,only as much weight as can be borrowed from the First's headship of the chain. The analogy does not give us a doctrine about the First. It gives us,at most,a way of speaking about the two proportions under which the First's relation to what is below can be considered.

This is the same architecture *Chapter Four* settled for Intellect-and-Word and the architecture *Chapter Five* settled for the divine names being *synonymous according to the additions*. The same single Self under multiple names according to the aspect under which it is being considered. *Cause* is the Self in the proportion toward below. *Effect* is the Self in the proportion toward above. *Motion* is the analogue of *cause-toward-below* in the language of the natural world. *Stillness* is the analogue of *effect-toward-above*. No name is the Self; the Self is one. The names belong by aspect,not by sharing of substance.

## Sub-chapter seven,*the book *The Correction*'s* grave error: creativity as before-creativity

Sub-chapter seven is the chapter's hottest moment. *The book *The Correction** presses one more move. *When he made the First Hyle out of the matter which is creativity,the First Hyle is imaginary,just as creativity is imaginary,and only shows itself in the First Creator.* So far the author has been with him: yes,the First Hyle is imaginary,yes creativity is imaginary,yes creativity shows itself only in the First Creator. But then the book *The Correction* takes the further step. *He said that this must be in the presence of the Almighty,because of His pride in it, that creativity,before it has appeared,has not yet become creative through creativity.* The picture: *before* creativity is exercised,there is a creativity that has not yet *become creative through creativity*. As if there were a *pre-creative* creativity,a creativity prior to its own act of creating,somehow attendant on the divine pride and awaiting its emergence.

The author's response is the unrelenting refusal of *monotheism*. *And this is a grave error. For the doctrine and belief in monotheism is that other than Allah Almighty,who is Creative and First,nothing has any reality in existence at all; and if anything did,then creativity would be nothing other than the Creative itself.*

The refusal runs on two strokes. First,*the doctrine of monotheism is that other than Allah Almighty,who is Creative and First,nothing has any reality in existence at all*. *monotheism* is the refusal of any second reality alongside Allah. Whatever has reality,has it *from* Allah; nothing stands beside Allah as a free-standing reality. To posit a *creativity that exists before it has become creative* is to posit a reality alongside the Creative,a *pre-creative creativity* hovering in the presence of the Almighty,awaiting its emergence. That is a hidden second alongside the First. That is the door to *shirk*.

Second,*if anything did,then creativity would be nothing other than the Creative itself*. If there were any reality apart from Allah,then creativity would have to be Allah's *own act*,identical with the Creative,not a separate thing the Creative reaches for. Creativity is the Creative *acting*. There is no creativity standing apart from the Creative as an instrument the Creative wields. The Creative IS the creativity,in the same sense *Chapter Four* settled that the Intellect IS the Word,in the same sense *Chapter Five* settled that the divine names are *synonymous according to the additions*. The same single rank,under multiple names by aspect,not multiple realities in some collaboration.

*We have built upon this guide so that we may confine ourselves to our own letters.* The architecture of the chain,Intellect-is-Word,cause-and-effect-as-names-by-aspect,*prime matter*-and-form-as-first-emanation-of-the-Intellect-which-is-the-Word,protects *monotheism* at every rank. *The book *The Correction*'s* picture of a *pre-creative creativity* breaks the protection at the highest end. The author refuses it categorically.

The doctrinal stake here is the deepest of the chapter. *monotheism*, the Oneness of Allah,does not tolerate a *pre-creative creativity* alongside Allah,any more than it tolerates a *pre-eternal matter* alongside Allah,any more than it tolerates a *partial soul that is a piece of Allah's substance*. The chain holds at every rank by the principle that whatever has reality,has it *from* the First,by emanation,by receivership,by proportion of cause-and-effect,and not as a *separate* reality standing beside the First. *The book *The Correction*'s* error is the local instance of the general failure: it posits a second alongside the First. The author's refusal is the local instance of the general protection: nothing stands beside Allah; whatever has reality is reality-from-Him by the chain of emanation.

Imam the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him),in the first sermon of the book *The Path of Eloquence*,names the same protection at the top of the chain:

> *He is with everything,but not by closeness; He is other than everything,but not by separation; He acts,but not by movements or instruments.* (the book *The Path of Eloquence*,Sermon 1)

The aphorism settles the same doctrine the sub-chapter settles. *He acts,but not by movements or instruments.* Allah's action is not the action of one body upon another. There is no *instrument* Allah reaches for,no *pre-creative creativity* hovering at His side,awaiting deployment. The action is identical with the actor,in the only sense compatible with *monotheism*. *He is with everything,but not by closeness; He is other than everything,but not by separation*,He is not a body that occupies place by closeness,nor a body that excludes other bodies by separation. The aphorism is precisely the protection *Chapter Six's* sub-chapter seven needs against *the book *The Correction*'s* picture of a creativity that exists before it has appeared.

The same protection is named in a parallel tradition,not as a single shared chain,but as two lineages converging on one *monotheism*. The Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him),in the report Imam Muhammad al-Bukhari preserves in the Sunni hadith corpus on the authority of Imran ibn Husayn,names it at the earliest moment with the simplest possible grammar:

> *Allah was,and there was nothing else with Him.* (Sahih al-Bukhari,Book 59,Hadith 3191; narrated by Imran ibn Husayn)

The hadith refuses the second alongside the First at the source. *Allah was*,and *nothing* was with Him. Not a pre-creative creativity hovering at His side; not a primordial matter awaiting His shaping; not an instrument He reached for to do His acting. The Creative is not accompanied at the origin by a creativity that exists prior to its own emergence,because there *is* no prior to the First. *The book *The Correction*'s* picture of a creativity *that has not yet become creative through creativity* posits exactly the *something-else-with-Him* the hadith forbids. The Creative IS the creativity; there is no second,no instrument,no precursor.

And the most categorical statement of *monotheism* in the Quranic register itself,*Surat the chapter on sincerity*,the chapter the tradition calls *the third of the Quran* for its full weight:

> *Say: He is Allah,the One. Allah,the Eternal Refuge. He neither begets nor is begotten. And there is none comparable unto Him.* (Quran 112:1–4,Surah the chapter on sincerity; translation rendered after Pickthall)

*The One. The Eternal. He neither begets nor is begotten. None comparable unto Him.* The surah names *monotheism* in four lines and forbids,by the same four lines,every conceivable form of second-alongside-the-First. *Neither begets*, therefore no derivation that would yield a partial-Allah alongside Allah,no creativity that issues from Him as a separate thing standing apart. *Nor is begotten*, therefore no prior source from which Allah's reality issued,no precursor from which the Creative drew the creativity He then deploys. *None comparable*, therefore no second of His kind,no peer,no neighbor in being. The surah is the doctrinal seal of sub-chapter seven. The author's refusal of *the book *The Correction*'s* pre-creative creativity is the local instance of the same *monotheism* the surah names categorically.

## Sub-chapter eight,no motion or rest above the First Effect

Sub-chapter eight (lacuna at its head in the surviving copy) carries the seal of the doctrine. *The book *The Defense** and the book *The Correction* have differed; the author's verdict on their disagreement comes in stages. *What he said,and what the author of* the book *The Correction* *differed with him about,is correct,except that what he said* (and here the text breaks,the copy carrying the lacuna) *it should not be said that above the First Effect there is motion and rest,because that would assert motion and rest of the First Effect and of what is below it.*

The principle is the same one *Chapter Two* of *this book* settled. *We have already shown that the First Effect is not movable,that motion does not befit it,and that there is no stillness in it either,because it is other than a body.* The First Effect, the First Intellect,the universal Intellect,the rank *Chapter Two* settled as the first creation,is *not a body*. Motion and stillness are body-categories. To assert motion or rest of the First Effect is to assert body-properties of what is not a body. That is the same categorical error *sub-chapters one through three* refused at the level of the soul. The First Effect,and *a fortiori* what is above the First Effect,the First Creator Himself,does not admit motion or rest. The vocabulary of bodies-and-their-changes does not reach that rank.

This closes the analogy of sub-chapter five and sub-chapter six at the highest end. The *kun*-with-its-two-letters image,the cause-and-effect-by-motion-and-stillness analogy,the picture of the First as an actor whose act is motion-ending-in-stillness,all of these are *analogies by first-and-accident at best*,and they stop *short* of the First Effect itself. Above the First Effect,no analogy from motion-and-stillness reaches. Above the First Effect,there is *only pure creativity and complete reason*,as the book *The Defense* said in sub-chapter five,*and even those words*,the author's settled doctrine would add,*are by way of indication,not by way of essence*. The First is what it is,beyond any analogy.

The Quranic anchor that the *the call* tradition reaches for when settling that the First admits no body-talk runs through *Surat the chapter on consultation*:

> *There is nothing like unto Him; and He is the Hearer,the Seer.* (Quran 42:11,Surah the chapter on consultation; translation rendered after Pickthall)

*Laysa kamithlihi shay'un.* There is nothing like Him. The verse is the canonical seal of the negative way, the refusal of every analogy at the highest end. *Kamithlihi*,*like Him*: no analogue,no peer,no body-twin of the First. *Shay'un*,*thing*: no thing whatsoever,no item in the inventory of bodies,no rank in the chain of accidents,is comparable. Motion and stillness,categories of bodies in change,fall infinitely short of the First by the verse's own gauge. *The book *The Correction*'s* picture of the First as an actor whose two-state act is motion-ending-in-stillness asks the verse to admit a *kamithlihi* the verse forbids. And the verse's pair,*He is the Hearer,the Seer*,holds the same negative gauge in tension with the affirmative: He *acts* and He *perceives*,but not by movements or instruments. The action and the perception are not the actions and perceptions of a body. Above the First Effect,no motion-and-stillness vocabulary reaches. The verse names the height the sub-chapter names.

## Sub-chapter nine,contradictions are in the images,not in the being-bodies

Sub-chapter nine closes the chapter with a precise final correction. *The book *The Defense** offers a clean-sounding maxim. *Contradictions occur in bodies,not in spirits.* The picture: bodies fall into contradictions (opposing states,mutually exclusive properties); spirits do not. The maxim has a certain attractiveness; it seems to draw a clean line.

The author refuses the maxim as stated. *Bodies are bodies because they are objects; there is no distinction between them as such,and no contradiction among them.* Bodies,*taken as bodies*,are all the same kind of thing,objects,substrates,the *what* that the nine accidents adhere to. *As such*,considered purely as objects,bodies do not differ from each other,and they do not contradict each other. A piece of wood and a piece of iron,considered *as bodies*, that is,as substrates capable of bearing the nine accidents,are not in contradiction. They are the same kind of thing.

So where do contradictions come from? *The difference and the contrast lie in the images within them,what animates them,not in their being bodies.* The contradictions are at the *image* level,not the *body* level. The *form* (*sura*), the *image in matter*,in the vocabulary sub-chapter five established,is what gives a body its determinate character,its specific properties,its capacities and incapacities. The form of wood and the form of iron are different forms; *those* differ,*those* are in some sense in contrast. The *being-a-body* is one and undifferentiated across all bodies. The *being-this-or-that-kind-of-body* is given by the form,the image,the *what-animates-the-body*.

This is the architectural payload of the chapter restated for the natural world. Recall sub-chapter one: *prime matter* and form are inseparable,one acting and the other acted-upon. Recall sub-chapter four: *prime matter* and form are the first emanation. Now sub-chapter nine: *bodies are bodies because they are objects*; the differentiation that produces contradictions among bodies is at the form-level,the image-level,the level of what *prime matter* has *received*. Bodies as such are *prime matter* informed; what differentiates them is the *form-that-informs*,not the *prime matter-that-is-informed*.

*The book *The Defense** gives his last word. *And because what is in them resembles what was already there,the matter rests upon what he said,if the very speaker is of that sort.* The line is dense and slightly elusive, the text marks the chapter's close with this measured formulation. What is *in* the bodies (the images,the forms,the contents) resembles *what was already there* (the architecture of the chain above,the noble proportion of cause-and-effect). The matter, the question of contradictions,of differentiation among bodies,*rests upon what he said*: rests on the doctrine the author has just settled,that the differentiation is at the image-level,not the body-level. *If the very speaker is of that sort*,if the one speaking is himself attuned to the architecture,the formulation lands. The chapter closes with the doctrine in place and the qualification that doctrines of this depth land only on the rank of the listener.

## The chapter's settled formula

The nine sub-chapters land on one settled architecture. Motion and stillness are not the soul of *prime matter* and form; they are *opposites that do not meet in the same place at the same time*,the way the body's *changes* are configured with respect to its mover. *Al-hayula* and form are not the body of motion-and-stillness; they are *inseparable correlates*,actor-and-acted-upon,*the first emanation* of the chain from the Intellect-which-is-the-Word toward the natural composite. The soul is not a body,so the gentler-denser vocabulary does not reach it; *the soul* and *the spirit* are two names for one non-bodily reality. The body divides into *nine sections* (the nine accidents of *Aqlam of the faith*),not three; motion divides into modes that are *each motions*. The *kun* of Allah's command,taken as two letters with a voweled *kaf* and a consonant *nun*,is a remote analogy by *first-and-accident*; the analogy can carry,at most,an indication that the divine Self can be considered under multiple aspects,cause-toward-below and effect-toward-above,without those aspects being separate realities. Above the First Effect there is no motion,no stillness,no body-talk of any kind; above the First Effect there is *only pure creativity and complete reason*. *The book *The Correction*'s* picture of a creativity *that exists before it has appeared* is the chapter's gravest error: it posits a second alongside the First and breaks *monotheism* at its highest end; the Creative IS the creativity,in the same sense the Intellect IS the Word,in the same sense the divine names are *synonymous according to the additions*. And the contradictions among bodies are not in their being bodies,bodies are bodies because they are objects,but in *the images within them*,the forms that *prime matter* has received.

The political stake of *Chapter Six*,like that of *Chapters Four* and *Five*,sits one layer below the metaphysics. If *the book *The Correction*'s* gentler-denser proportion held,the soul would,by the analogy's own pressure,slip back into being a body,a *gentler* body,but a body,and the categorical refusal of body-talk in the higher ranks would lose its hold. If *the book *The Defense*'s* structural parallel held,motion would be like the body in its three-sectioned division,and motion-as-the-act-of-the-engine would slip into being a body-thing,with the same loss. If *the book *The Correction*'s kun*-as-two-letters were taken at full weight,the First would slip into being an actor *who uses an instrument* (the word *Be*), and *monotheism* would crack at its highest end,the same crack *Chapter Four* (sub-chapter eight) refused with the doctrine that the Intellect IS the Word,the same crack *Chapter Five* (sub-chapter seven) refused with the doctrine of the First's *metaphorical* action. If *the book *The Correction*'s* pre-creative creativity were granted,a second reality would stand alongside Allah at the very top,and the entire chain would be cantilevered out from a *shirk* its architecture cannot bear. The author's refusals at each of these joints are the protection of the *monotheism* the *the call* tradition is given to preserve.

The architecture *this book's Chapter Six* hands forward is the same architecture *Chapters Four* and *Five* handed forward,now applied to the natural-world end of the chain. *Al-hayula* and form: the first emanation,actor and acted-upon,together the natural composite. Motion and stillness: the modes of change in the moved thing,opposites that do not coexist. The body: *prime matter* informed; differentiated into nine sections by the nine accidents,all of which adhere because of the *form* (the image) that *prime matter* has received. The soul: a single non-bodily reality,called *the soul* and *the spirit* by aspect; perfect in itself; continuous by rank with what stands above it through the chain of receivership. The First Intellect: created by God from no thing at all,at a great height,the first creation,beyond motion-and-stillness because beyond body. The First Creator: beyond any analogy,the only reality from which all reality runs.

## What comes next

*Chapter Seven* of *this book* will take the next step. If *prime matter* and form together constitute the body,and the body divides into nine sections by the nine accidents,then *what are the sections of the world*? Seven sub-chapters that will work out the architectural divisions of the natural-world end of the chain, the same architecture *Chapter Six* has just settled,now applied to the world's own internal structure. *Chapter Six* gave the doctrine of motion,stillness,*prime matter*,and form; *Chapter Seven* will give the order of the world that those four make.

And before that next chapter opens,*Chapter Six* leaves the reader with the image it has earned. *The book *The Correction** and the book *The Defense* arguing across decades about whether motion-and-stillness are soul or body,whether *prime matter* divides by dimension or by similarity of parts,whether the *kun* of Allah's command is to be parsed as two letters or refused such parsing. The author,generations later,working the same passage and arriving at the doctrine that the soul is one,*prime matter* and form are inseparable as actor and acted-upon,the *kun* analogy carries only by first-and-accident,and above the First Effect there is no motion,no rest,no body-talk whatsoever. The Creative is the creativity; the Intellect is the Word; the names belong to the ranks by aspect,not by sharing of substance. *He acts,but not by movements or instruments*,the Commander of the Faithful,settling at the head of the chain the same doctrine *Chapter Six* settles all the way down. The architecture holds. The chain runs. The *monotheism* the chapter is built to protect stands at every rank.
