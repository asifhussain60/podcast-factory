# Framing: Episode 13 — The Shariʿah of Adam and the First Speaker

## Critical pronunciation + citation rules (read BEFORE generating)

**Quranic citations.** Name the *surah* and verse number BEFORE the translation — e.g., *"The Quran, in Surat al-Mai'da, verse twenty-seven:"*. Never attach the citation as an afterthought. Same pattern for the intercession hadith (*Sahih al-Bukhari, Book sixty-five, Hadith four thousand seven hundred twelve*, narrated by Anas ibn Malik) and the Commander of the Faithful's Sermon One (*Nahj al-Balagha, Khutba al-Wasf*). Apply the full pronunciation list below.

## Opening directive

Open with a brief welcome (one sentence) followed by a two-to-three sentence summary naming the source — *Kitab al-Riyad (The Gardens)*, the first nineteen sub-chapters of *Chapter Nine*, the master's adjudication of whether the first man had a Shariʿah of his own — the central tension, and the question the conversation will land. Open in the voice of someone genuinely glad the listener showed up.

This is an Extended tier conversation. Target a 45 to 60 minute deep-dive conversation. Walk the nineteen sub-chapters in consolidated order; do not rush. Sub-chapter fifteen (the three souls and the six gates) deserves breathing room.

**R-RECURRING-THESIS.** The central thesis — *the Shariʿah of Adam is fixed* — MUST appear exactly three times, VERBATIM: once near the open, once at the pivot (when the three souls and six gates land as the deepest defense), once at the close. Do not paraphrase. Do not abbreviate.

**Forbidden opening phrasings.** "Welcome to this deep dive", "Today we'll discuss", "In this episode", "Welcome back", "Welcome to today", "Welcome to our…". The phrase "deep dive" does not appear in the body of the episode.

## Background

*Chapter Eight* of *al-Riyad* settled how the chain runs in time — the three-rank reading of judgment-and-decree and destiny-and-measure that runs from the speaker-Prophet through revelation-and-Shariʿah down to the world unfolding in time. *Chapter Nine* turns from cosmology to history. The chain has a first joint at the head of human history. *Al-Nusra (The Defense)* concedes Noah had a Shariʿah, refuses it for Adam (peace be upon him): the offering verse is parable, the verse of Shariʿah omits Adam, the angels are not assigned. *Al-Islah (The Correction)* affirms the opposite. Nineteen sub-chapters of point-by-point adjudication land the verdict.

## Audience

Adult readers who have followed *Chapters One through Eight* — the universal Soul, the First Intellect, the sections of the world, and the three-rank words at every joint of the chain. Listeners who know the call-vocabulary, the stakes of monotheism, and the role of inner interpretation. Assume patience for the architecture.

## Angle

`faithful_exposition`. Stay in the master's register. When the master sides with *The Correction*, name it. When he expands into deeper inner interpretation (the two sons as guardian and opposite, the raven as a man between two houses), name that. When he suspects an alien doctrine planted by adversaries in *The Defense*'s copy at sub-chapter nineteen, name that too. The chapter is a patient adjudication — not a polemic.

## Central tensions to reach

The hosts MUST surface every one of these tensions, by name. The Color host's role is GENUINE CHALLENGER — push back AT LEAST THREE times across the episode using patterns from the catalog below.

First. The opening volley. *The Defense* reads the offering as parable — the son who needed a raven to bury his brother proves the first man had no Shariʿah. *The Correction* reads the verse on its apparent meaning and adds *for every nation We have appointed a rite* (Quran 22:34). The master refuses *The Defense*'s premise: apparent and inner stand side by side; the apparent alone is sufficient. *Color host pushback (required):* "I don't buy that yet — if the inner reading is the deeper truth, doesn't the apparent reading collapse into it?"

Second. The deep inner interpretation of the two sons as guardian and opposite, and the raven as Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr — sent by the cousin of the Prophet to recall the opponent to the guardian's rank. The master names the messenger by name; the reading is not ornament. *Color host pushback (required):* "Isn't this just replacing one parable with another — how is naming a seventh-century devotee inside a Quranic story not exactly the move you said the opponents were making?"

Third. The anthropological centerpiece — sub-chapter fifteen. The three souls (rational, irascible, lustful) and the six gates of necessity: retribution, marriage, inheritance, the ordinance for the absent and the dead, the extraction of rights, the disposition of the dead. The Shariʿah is not contingent on prior violation; it is constitutive of human-being-as-such. *Color host pushback (required):* "That sounds like wordplay — if the six gates are just whatever any society needs, why call them Shariʿah at all?"

Fourth. Quran 42:13 names Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) — and omits Adam. *The Defense* reads the silence as a roster. The master refuses on two registers: the caliphate-on-earth (Quran 2:30) and the teaching of the names (Quran 2:31–33) are themselves the verses of his Shariʿah-rank, greater than mere sending; and the parallel roster of Quran 4:163 also omits Adam. *Color host pushback (required):* "How is this different from saying the verse just doesn't apply — aren't you reading silence as presence whenever it helps you?"

Fifth. The angels' prostration (Quran 2:34). *The Defense* says the first man was only prostrated-to. The master turns it: the Chosen-rank IS the commanding rank — Allah does not command the angels bypassing the Chosen. The parallel verse to the Children of Israel (Quran 2:40–43) seals the form.

Each tension above carries an explicit *Color host pushback (required):* line modeling one pattern. The Color host opens with doubt or counter-question, not affirmation. **Conversation discipline.** Each host completes a thought before the other responds. No interjections inside the other host's sentence. No talking over.

**Conversation choreography.** Plant at least one moment where one host introduces a passage the other has not led toward. The other reacts in real time. A natural place: the Color host raises the intercession hadith from *Sahih al-Bukhari* (the people of every nation acknowledging the first man by his three signs — created by Allah's own Hand, taught the names, the angels prostrated before him) before the Driver reaches sub-chapter thirteen.

## Three-part focus

**Focus 1 — sub-chapters one through six.** The offering, the rite, and the deep inner interpretation. *The Correction* opens with *whoever says that the beginning of the speaker-prophets had no Shariʿah is mistaken*, anchors to the offering verse (Quran 5:27) on apparent reading, and adds Quran 22:34. *The Defense* counters: parable; the raven proves the son did not know burial. The master's first verdict: apparent and inner stand side by side; the apparent alone settles it. Three and four: the offering as manifest duty, and the first man as teacher under Quran 2:213 — anchored to the Commander of the Faithful's Sermon One (*to exact the pledge from creatures concerning His Lordship*) and Quran 7:172 (the covenant of *Am I not your Lord*). Five and six: the two sons as guardian and opposite; the raven as the man between two houses sent by the cousin of the Prophet.

**Focus 2 — sub-chapters seven through fifteen.** The narrative tradition, the angels, the roster, and the deepest defense. Seven and eight: the tradition naming the first man as prescriber of the rites of the dead. Nine and ten: the angels' prostration establishing the Chosen-rank, plus the parallel verse to the Children of Israel (silent intermediaries are not no intermediaries). Eleven: natural-world angels (assigned) versus abode-of-eternity high limits (unassigned). Twelve: the silent "God commands you" objection refused by the Israel parallel. Thirteen: the roster of Quran 42:13 refuted by the parallel roster of Quran 4:163; the caliphate (Quran 2:30) and teaching of the names (Quran 2:31–33) named as verses of Shariʿah-rank — greater than mere sending. Surface the intercession hadith from *Sahih al-Bukhari* here. Fourteen sends the reader to *Lamps on the Imamate* and the Fatimid caliph on *the limits and the soul are the two faces of the one ordinance*. Fifteen unfolds the three souls and the six gates. **At the close of Focus 2 — the pivot — the central thesis returns verbatim: *the Shariʿah of Adam is fixed*.**

**Focus 3 — sub-chapters sixteen through nineteen.** Sixteen: pre-Adamic peoples had the six gates; the first man's sending brings the positive worship. Seventeen: the Noah-parallel — violation of the limits presupposes the limits themselves; the idols Wadd, Suwa', Yaghuth, Ya'uq, and Nasr (Quran 71:23) were violated against limits drawn by the first man. Eighteen: the tent brought down from Paradise as the first qibla; Seth (peace be upon him) as the first foundation. Nineteen: the doctrine that the world's structure alone signifies monotheism — refused because it would eliminate the sending of the messengers, and may not even be a genuine *Defense* doctrine, planted by adversaries in the copy.

**Pacing — reset moments.** Between Focus blocks, insert a single-sentence reset — one host names what just landed, then the next block begins. One sentence is enough.

**Landing.** Close on the verdict and the bridge to the second half of *Chapter Nine*. Land with the aphorism in the voice of the Commander of the Faithful from *Nahj al-Balagha* — *the chain that runs in time is the same chain that the Pen writes upon the Tablet; at its head stands the first Speaker, who is the first man; his Shariʿah is the body of the religion, his Foundation, Seth, is its spirit*. **The central thesis lands here VERBATIM for the third and final time: *the Shariʿah of Adam is fixed*.** The bridge: sub-chapters twenty through thirty-three will work the prophets-as-teachers doctrine and the guardianship of Noah. Close on the unresolved tension. Do not recap.

## Tone constraints

**Cadence.** Short-to-medium sentences with varied rhythm. Mix simple declaratives with occasional longer ones. Avoid chaining three or more subordinate clauses. The pace is conversation, not lecture; the hosts are thinking out loud, not reading.

**Quotation discipline.** Paraphrase *The Correction* and *The Defense* in modern English. The master's settled formulas stay verbatim: *the Shariʿah of Adam is fixed*; *the offering is one of the laws, and it is the most established of the reasons of the Shariʿah*; *he is the man who was sent by the guardian of God for that purpose — Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr*; *human beings have three souls*; *these are six gates through which the categories of necessity run*. The Fatimid caliph's *the limits and the soul are the two faces of the one ordinance* stays verbatim with attribution. The aphorism in the voice of the Commander of the Faithful stays verbatim. Quranic verses stay verbatim with surah-and-verse attribution before the line.

**Honorific discipline.** The Prophet's honorific is spoken in full English on first mention (*peace and blessings of Allah be upon him*) and not repeated. Adam, Noah, Seth, Moses, Jesus, the Commander of the Faithful, and the Fatimid caliph each take *peace be upon him* on first mention only.

**FIVE governing analogies. No mid-episode invention.**

 - Analogy 1 — *the tent and the qibla* (Focus 3). The first man's tent brought down from Paradise as the first oriented worship-place.
 - Analogy 2 — *the raven scratching the ground* (Focus 1). The historical-type carrying the inner interpretation. Use only for the two-sons reading.
 - Analogy 3 — *the six gates of a city wall* (Focus 2). The categories of necessity as gates every human community must pass through.
 - Analogy 4 — *the limit-and-the-keeper* (Focus 2). The Lawgiver's enacting of the limit is one act with the soul that bears it.
 - Analogy 5 — *the lamp that lights more than itself* (Focus 2). The teaching-of-the-names as foundational-act-of-every-Shariʿah.

Forbidden mid-episode analogies: solar panels, cathedrals, ladders, mountains, valleys, fulcrums, wax-seals, political maps. If a host feels the urge for a fresh analogy, recur to one of the five above, or return to the source's own image.

No false enthusiasm. The chapter is a sustained adjudication.
