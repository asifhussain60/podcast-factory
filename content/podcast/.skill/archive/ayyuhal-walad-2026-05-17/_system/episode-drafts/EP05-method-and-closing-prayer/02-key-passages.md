# Key Passages: Ayyuhal Walad, EP05

Verbatim quotations from the closing sections of *Ayyuhal Walad*, in the order they appear. Arabic transliteration is preserved exactly as printed in the Irfan Hasan English translation. Each passage carries a brief note on why it matters for the conversation.

---

## 1. The frame of the four matters

> My dear beloved son! Refrain from the [above mentioned] four things but the things that you have to do are also four in number and it will be appropriate that you take full care of them.

**Why this matters:** The hinge. Ghazali balances the four cautions of the previous section with four matched actions. The listener should hear the parallel.

---

## 2. Matter 1, the mirror test inward

> The first thing is that, every matter that is between you and Allah should be conducted [by you] in such a way that if your servant does the same for you then you, instead of being sad, will like it, commend it, and will not be in any way angry about it.

**Why this matters:** The whole first matter is built on this inversion. The test the student would run on his servant, he must run on himself. Quote this when opening the discussion of Matter 1.

---

## 3. The asymmetry of being created

> Likewise, if there is anything you do not like about your servant doing it for you, you should also realize that if you fell short in your servitude to your Lord then your Creator will also not like it. The reality that needs to be mentioned here is that your servant is not created by you but only hired by you. But you been created, raised (cherished), and owned by your Real Creator and Owner.

**Why this matters:** The pivot inside Matter 1. *Hired* versus *created*. The student leases everything he has from the One who made him.

---

## 4. Matter 2, the mirror test outward

> The second thing is that every matter, that is between you and other slaves of Allah, should be conducted by you in such a way that if they do the same to you [what you are doing to them] then you will like it and will not be grieved by it.

**Why this matters:** Same instrument as Matter 1, turned outward. Treat all human beings under the same test.

---

## 5. The hadith qudsi on complete faith

> Fala Yakmalu Eemanu 'Abdi Hatta Yuhibba Lisaa-irin Nasi Ma Yuhibba Li Nafsihi.
> My (Allah's) slave's faith (Iman) is never complete unless he/she likes for all human beings what he/she likes for himself/herself.

**Why this matters:** The anchoring text under Matter 2. Note the universality: *all human beings*, not the believers, not the worthy.

---

## 6. Matter 3, the week-to-live frame

> The third thing is that if you want to increase your knowledge and want to read (study) a scholarly book then think that your lifetime will be over in no more than a week.

**Why this matters:** Opens Matter 3. The proximity of death sorts the shelf. Knowledge is not refused; useless knowledge is.

---

## 7. The books you would not open

> If you have the knowledge that your life will last for no more than a week, then in that week you will never read such scholarly books in which you will learn things like religious debates, argumentation, dictionary, linguistics and grammar, poetry, poetic collections, and essay writing.

**Why this matters:** Ghazali names the categories directly. The list itself is the indictment.

---

## 8. The king's visit

> If you are given the news and told that the next week the king will visit your house, then certainly in the whole week, you will not do any work other than having the obsession that king's glance should not fell on such-and-such thing so I should clean it.

**Why this matters:** The household image that sets up the move from outer cleaning to inner cleaning. Lets the listener feel the disproportion.

---

## 9. The hadith on the heart

> InnAllaha La Yunzuru Ila Suwarikum Wa La Ila 'Aamalikum Wa Lakin Yunzuru Ila Qulubikum Wa Niyyatikum.
> Verily, Allah does not look at your faces and your actions but he looks at your hearts and your intentions.

**Why this matters:** Lands the move from the king image into the inner work. The Prophet's words make the inference Ghazali leaves implicit.

---

## 10. Matter 4, the one-year rule

> The fourth thing is that you should not accumulate more than one year's provision for your family from the possessions of the world.

**Why this matters:** Concrete, practical, sits inside the same list as how to relate to God. The hosts will want to hold this carefully and not laugh it off.

---

## 11. The Prophet's du'a over provisions

> Allahumma Aj'al Quta Aale Muhammadin Kafafun.
> O my Allah! Make this provision (food) sufficient for the family of Muhammad.

**Why this matters:** Shows that even the stockpiling that is permitted is brought back to du'a. The pantry is prayed over, not just filled.

---

## 12. The two classes of wives, the principle of Tawakkul

> Prophet Muhammad did not accumulate provisions (food) for all of his wives (Azwaj al-Mutahharat), but instead, he accumulated provisions (food) for one year for those of his wives only whose reliance (Tawakkul) on Allah was weak. And for those Ummahatil Mu'mineen (Mothers of the Believers), whose certitude in Allah was perfect and had strong Tawakkul, for them the Prophet did not even accumulate even one day's worth of provisions. For instance, for 'Aisha Siddiqa (May Allah be pleased with her) and for other Mothers of Believers like her.

**Why this matters:** The principle is sharper than the rule. The cap of one year is for the spiritually weak. The strong stockpile nothing.

---

## 13. The closing instruction, and the answer to both requests

> My dear beloved son! In this book, I have answered all of your questions. Now you should gather courage and act on all of them and do not forget me in your supplications (Du'a). You have also desired that I should write a supplication (Du'a) for you, so you should search for supplications in the book of Ahadith, especially in Sahah Sitta and memorize them. Likewise, many supplications have come to us from the methods of the Ahl al-Bayt, so search for them there. Recite the following supplication, in particular, after every prayer (Salaat).

**Why this matters:** Ghazali says the letter is finished. He answers the student's two original requests (a du'a, and acting-advice) at the same moment. Note the call to recite the supplication *after every prayer*.

---

## 14. The closing du'a, in full

> O my Allah! I desire from You the completion (abundance/perfection) of Your blessings and from purity (inner and outer) I desire its permanence and from Your mercy [I desire] its inclusion [in all matters] and from health [I desire] its attainment and from sustenance [I desire] its increase and from life [I desire] its happiness and from life [I desire] its real success and from good qualities [I desire] their completion and from your rewards [I desire] the reward which is the most common and from your plenitude [I desire] the plenitude which is the sweetest and from your favors [I desire] the favor which is the most plentiful and from deeds [I desire] the deed which is the best and from knowledge [I desire] the knowledge which is the most beneficial and from the sustenance [I desire] the sustenance which is the most plentiful.

> O my Allah! Be on our side (grant us benefits) and do not burden us (protect us from harms). O my Allah! Grant us salvation when we die and reform our deeds and grant us safety in your days and nights and grant us your mercy in our houses and in our possessions and cover our sins and fault with the cover of your forgiveness and do us favors by correcting all our faults [inner and outer] and give us confidence and reliance on You.

> O my Allah! Grant us perseverance and persistence in our Deen (religion/Path to God). Save us from such deeds in the world that will become a source of shame (humiliation) on the Day of Judgment and lessen the burdens of our sins on us and grant us a life of the pious ones and be sufficient to us in all our affairs and protect us from the evil of the evildoers. Liberate us and our ancestors from the fire of Hell, from loans, and from oppression O You the Victorious One (Ya Aziz)! O You the Forgiving One (Ya Ghaffar)! O You the Generous One (Ya Kareem)! O You the Coverer of faults (Ya Sattar)! O You the Tolerant One (Ya Haleem)! O You the Powerful One (Ya Jabbar)! O You the Magnificent One (Ya Azeem)! O You the One Who Punishes (Ya Qahhar)! O Allah! O Allah! O Allah! O You the Compassionate One (Ya Rahman) in this world! O You the Merciful One (Ya Raheem) in the life hereafter! O You the Supreme Merciful One (Ya Arhum ur-Rahimeen)! You are the Grantor of the Excessive Mercy! And may the mercy and blessings of Allah always descend on Muhammad (Peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) who is the best of all [of God's] creations [from eternity to eternity] and on his descendants and on his companions. All praises are solely due to Allah the Lord of all the Universes (Rabb ul-'Alameen).

**Why this matters:** The artifact. The supplication Ghazali leaves the student. The bulk of this episode lives inside this prayer. The hosts should read this aloud at least once in full, not in summary.

---

## 15. The final line of the letter

> This book comes to an end with the help of the Absolute Sovereign (Al-Malik) and the Giver of all (Al-Wahhab)!

**Why this matters:** The letter closes. The teacher's voice ends. The student is alone with the prayer and the four matters. The hosts should let this line land without summary.
