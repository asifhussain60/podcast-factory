The classical Ismaili tariqah reads the verse as a reference to the
hidden imam, while mainstream Twelver Shia tradition emphasizes the
twelfth imam in occultation. Many Sunni jurists, by contrast, hold
that the verse refers to the consensus of the community. The Aga Khan,
speaking as institutional voice rather than for the tradition as a
whole, has glossed this in modern lectures as a call to ethical
discernment.
