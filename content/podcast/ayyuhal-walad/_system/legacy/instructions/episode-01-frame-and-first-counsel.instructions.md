## Opening directive (hosts must deliver this verbatim or near-verbatim as the first line)

"This is the first episode in a seven-part series on *Ayyuhal Walad*, the short final letter Imam Al-Ghazali wrote near the end of his life in answer to a student who had spent years studying with him and still did not know which of his learnings would matter on the Day of Judgment — and today's episode lays out both the question and Ghazali's first answer to it: that action precedes knowledge, always."

## Focus (three-part directive — what the hosts must keep their attention on)

1. **The question is the entire frame of the book and the entire frame of this episode.** The student is not asking abstract theology. He is asking, after years of study with the master, *which* of those years actually counted. Keep the question alive through the whole episode. When the hosts develop Ghazali's response, they should keep returning to whose question is being answered.

2. **The lion-in-the-jungle analogy is the centerpiece of the episode.** Do not paraphrase it away. Walk the listener slowly through it — the man, the weapons, the knowledge of how to use them, the lion, the moment of test, the refusal to draw. Then Ghazali's extension: the sick man who knows the medicine but never takes it. The two analogies together carry the entire argument; they deserve the time. Do not summarize them in one line.

3. **The two hadiths — about a wasted moment and about a person past forty — are diagnostic, not decorative.** Speak each in full transliteration, then in English. Make the listener feel the weight. The episode loses force if the hosts skim the hadiths as "Ghazali quotes a saying" and move on. The hadiths *are* the argument.

## Pronunciation hooks (every term the hosts will encounter in this episode)

Hosts should consult `03-pronunciation.md` (uploaded as a second source) for the canonical phonetic form of every term below. On first appearance per episode, pair the term with its English gloss. Subsequent appearances, term alone.

- **Imam Al-Ghazali** — *I-maam Al-Gha-zaa-lee* (1058-1111 CE)
- **Ayyuhal Walad** — *Ay-yu-hal Wa-lad* ("O Beloved Son")
- **Ihya al-Uloom ad-Deen** — *Ih-yaa al-U-loom ad-Deen* (Revival of the Religious Sciences)
- **Kee-mi-yaa as-Sa-aa-da** — Alchemy of Happiness
- **Ahadith** — *A-haa-deeth* (sayings of the Prophet, plural of hadith)
- **Sunnah** — *Sun-nah* (the Prophet's example)
- **Shaykh Junaid Baghdadi** — *Sheikh Ju-nayd Bagh-daa-dee* (early Sufi master cited in Ghazali's dream-story)
- **Tahajjud** — *Ta-haj-jud* (the deep-night voluntary prayer)
- **Sharee'ah** — *Sha-ree-ah* (the revealed law)
- **Quranic verses** — speak each in full transliteration first, then English. Do not skip the transliteration to save time.
- **Honorifics** — always spoken in full English. "Peace and blessings be upon him" for the Prophet. "May Allah shower His mercy upon him" for Ghazali and Junaid Baghdadi. Never the abbreviations.

## Anti-noise rules (these eight rules apply to every episode — global block follows)

1. **Stay on the episode's content.** No drift into Ghazali's biography beyond what the refined source mentions. No drift into general Islamic history. No comparative theology. Tangents over ten seconds get cut.
2. **No abbreviated honorifics.** Always speak them in full English.
3. **Use the pronunciation guide.** Every transliteration matches `03-pronunciation.md`.
4. **Quranic verses and hadiths are landmarks.** Full transliteration first, then English meaning. Do not summarize around them.
5. **Do not invent supporting material.** No scholars, citations, dates, or anecdotes that are not in the refined source.
6. **Hold the tone.** This episode is reverent and instructional. No casual address to Allah, the Prophet, or Imam Ghazali. No softening of "knowledge without action is of no benefit" into "knowledge is helpful but action helps more."
7. **Host questions come from the source.** Frame questions around what the source actually says. Avoid generic "what does this mean for us today" unless the answer is grounded in the source.
8. **End where the episode ends.** Do not over-wrap with meta-commentary about the next episode. The closing line of the refined source is the closing line of the audio.
