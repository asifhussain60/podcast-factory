**Source:** *Ayyuhal Walad* — *My Dear Beloved Son (or Daughter)* — by Imam Abu Hamid Muhammad Al-Ghazali (1058-1111 CE). Translated into English by Irfan Hasan from an Urdu translation of the Arabic original.

**Series shape:** Seven Audio Overview episodes, redesigned per content-driven segmentation (Stage 09 revised 2026-05-16, consulting `journal/reference/notebooklm-best-practices.md`). ## Global anti-noise rules — apply to every episode

1. **Stay on the episode's content.** No drift into Ghazali's biography beyond what the refined source mentions. No drift into general Islamic history or comparative theology. Tangents over ten seconds get cut.

2. **Never abbreviate honorifics.** "(SAW)" → "peace and blessings be upon him." "(RA)" → "may Allah shower His mercy upon him." "(AS)" → "peace be upon him." Speak in full on first occurrence per named figure, then naturally as needed.

3. **Speak transliterations from the pronunciation guide.** Use `03-pronunciation.md` (uploaded as the second source) as the canonical reference. Pair each term with English gloss on first appearance per episode; thereafter, the term alone.

4. **Quranic verses and hadiths are landmarks, not decoration.** Speak each in full transliteration, then the English meaning. Do not paraphrase around them.

5. **Do not invent supporting material.** No scholars, citations, dates, or anecdotes that are not in the refined source.

6. **Hold the tone the episode was written in.** Reverent stays reverent — no familiar address to Allah, the Prophet, or Imam Ghazali. Argumentative preserves premises and conclusions — do not soften "Waa-jib" (obligatory) into "recommended." Narrative preserves scene and dialogue.

7. **Host questions come from the refined source.** Frame questions around what the source actually says. Avoid generic "what does this mean for us today" unless the answer is grounded in something the source actually says.

8. **End where the episode ends.** Do not over-wrap with meta-commentary about the next episode or what Ghazali was trying to do. The closing line of the refined source is the closing line of the audio.
