# Key Passages: Ayyuhal Walad, Section 3: Hatim bin Ism

Verbatim quotations from *Benefits Narrated by Hatim bin Ism*, in the order they appear. Arabic transliteration is preserved exactly as printed in the Irfan Hasan English translation. Each passage carries an attribution and a brief note on why it matters for the conversation.

---

## 1. The first beloved who returns at the graveside

> "O my teacher! The first benefit is that I looked at the people of this world and saw that everyone has a beloved. But these beloveds of the people [of the world] are such that some of them (i.e. the beloveds) are with them (i.e. the people) until their illness that brings death, some of them are with them until death, some of them are with them until they reach their graves, and after burial all the lovers return from there. None of their beloveds go with them in their graves to live with them there and to provide their company [in the darkness, solitude, narrowness, and horror of the grave]."

**Attribution:** Hatim bin Ism, the first benefit, narrated to Shaqiq al-Balkhi.

**Why this matters:** Hatim's opening observation. The unsparing image of every human beloved turning back at the lip of the grave is the setup for the inversion that follows. Quote in full when opening the conversation about the first benefit.

---

## 2. Righteous deeds as the only beloved who descends

> "I contemplated on this and said to myself that only that is a good beloved who goes with them in the grave and lives with his/her lover, who will provide the company, who will illuminate the grave, who will be a partner on the Day of Judgment and its destinations (stations that one goes through after burial until the Day of Judgment). I saw that the only beloved who has these [good] qualities is my good (pious/righteous) deeds."

**Attribution:** Hatim bin Ism, conclusion of the first benefit.

**Why this matters:** The inversion lands. Hatim does not say *do good deeds*; he says *I made my deeds my beloved*. The grammar of love is preserved, and what is loved is moved. This is the structural move the whole section is built on.

---

## 3. The verse that re-fronts the lower self

> *'Wa Amma Mun Khafa Maqama Rabbihi Wa Nahun Nafsa 'Anil Hawa Fa Innal Jannata Heyal Mawa.'*
> 'But the one who has stood in fear of his/her Sustainer's Presence, and restrained his/her inner self from the base (low and evil) desires, Paradise will the place of rest [for this person].' (Quran, Chapter 79, Verses 40-41)

**Attribution:** Quran 79:40-41, cited by Hatim as the anchor of the second benefit.

**Why this matters:** This is the Quranic verse Hatim places against the universal pattern of slavery to the *Nafs*. The two conditions of Paradise it names (fear of the Lord's Presence, restraint of the inner self) become the two coordinates of Hatim's actual practice.

---

## 4. The front established against the Nafs

> "I developed the certitude that the Wise Quran is correct and the book of Allah is the truth. Then I established a front against my Nafs (lower self) and became determined to oppose it. I molded it (i.e. my lower self) in such a shape that I did not fulfill any of its desires until it started to find peace [and satisfaction] in the worship of Allah, the Exalted."

**Attribution:** Hatim bin Ism, the second benefit.

**Why this matters:** The military metaphor is Hatim's own and worth holding. He did not merely *resist* the *Nafs*; he established a *front* against it, and held the front until the appetite itself rotated. The endpoint is not suppression but reorientation: the *Nafs* finds peace in worship.

---

## 5. The verse that moves the wealth

> *'Ma 'Indakum Yanfadu Wa Ma 'IndAllahi Baaqin.'*
> 'All that is with you is bound to come to an end, whereas all that, which is with Allah is everlasting.' (Quran, Chapter 16, Verse 96)

**Attribution:** Quran 16:96, cited by Hatim as the anchor of the third benefit.

**Why this matters:** A single sentence ledger. What is with you ends; what is with Allah endures. Hatim treats this as portfolio guidance and acts on it directly.

---

## 6. The wealth redeposited as trust

> "Then whatever [material possessions] I accumulated in the world, I spent it all in Way of Allah by distributing it among pious indigents and beggars so that it (the material possessions that I distributed in the Way of Allah) will get deposited with Allah, the Exalted, as my trust and will become source of immense good and ransom (i.e. salvation) in the life hereafter."

**Attribution:** Hatim bin Ism, the third benefit.

**Why this matters:** The frame is not ascetic disdain for wealth. It is location of wealth. The phrase *deposited with Allah as my trust* is striking. The wealth has not been destroyed; it has been moved.

---

## 7. The only true grandeur

> *'Inna Akramakum 'IndAllahi Atqakum.'*
> 'Verily, the noblest of you in the sight of Allah is the one who is most deeply conscious (i.e. fearful) of Him.' (Quran, Chapter 49, Verse 13)

**Attribution:** Quran 49:13, cited by Hatim as the anchor of the fourth benefit.

**Why this matters:** The single metric of nobility. Hatim has just listed four wrong answers (tribe, wealth, violence, extravagance) and places the verse against all four. *Taqwa* is the only true marker.

---

## 8. The false and wrong conjectures

> "Therefore this is correct and is the truth and the creations' thoughts are false and wrong conjectures. That's why I adopted Taqwa (pious reverence, consciousness, awe, and fear of Allah) so that I get counted as the one with the highest rank in the sight of Allah, the Exalted."

**Attribution:** Hatim bin Ism, the fourth benefit.

**Why this matters:** The phrase *false and wrong conjectures* is unusually severe in the translation. Hatim does not say the four worldly ways of seeking honor are *less true*; he says they are *false*, and the criterion Allah names is the only real one.

---

## 9. The distribution already settled

> *'Nahnu Qasumna Baeynahum Ma'eeshatahum Fil Hayatid Dunya.'*
> 'It is We who distribute their means of livelihood (sustenance) among them in the life of this world.' (Quran, Chapter 43, Verse 32)

**Attribution:** Quran 43:32, cited by Hatim as the anchor of the fifth benefit.

**Why this matters:** The verse closes the entire architecture of envy in one sentence. The distribution was not a contest, so envy is misdirected energy from the start.

---

## 10. Agreement with the distribution

> "Therefore, I did not compete or had jealousy with anyone and remained agreed to Allah's distribution [of worldly bounties] and [His appointed] destiny and got along with the whole world [by giving up all jealousies and enmities against God's creations]."

**Attribution:** Hatim bin Ism, the fifth benefit.

**Why this matters:** The word *agreed* is the operative one. Hatim does not say he *accepted* or *submitted*. He is in agreement with the distribution. The other man's portion is not Hatim's loss.

---

## 11. The single enemy

> *'Innash Shaytana Lakum 'Aduwwun Fettakhizuhu 'Aduwwa.'*
> 'Verily, Shaytan (Satan) is your enemy so treat him as your enemy.' (Quran, Chapter 35, Verse 6)

**Attribution:** Quran 35:6, cited by Hatim as the anchor of the sixth benefit.

**Why this matters:** The verse names exactly one enemy. All the personal grievances and resentments the *Nafs* keeps refreshing are misdirected. This is one of the most operationally clarifying lines in the section.

---

## 12. Enmity redirected to its proper object

> "After that, I developed the certitude that Allah's saying is the truth, I should not have any enmity with anyone other than Shaytan (Satan). Since then, I considered Satan as my enemy and did not obey any of his orders. Instead, I developed obedience [of Allah] and followed the commandments of Allah, the Exalted and adopted only His worship and servitude."

**Attribution:** Hatim bin Ism, the sixth benefit.

**Why this matters:** The energy of enmity is not denied. It is redirected. The neighbor is not the enemy; the whisperer who builds the case against the neighbor is. This is a structural reordering of human affect.

---

## 13. Sustenance as Allah's responsibility

> *'Wa Ma Min Daabbatin Fil Arde Illa 'AlalAllahi Rizquha.'*
> 'And there is no living creature on earth whose sustenance is not the responsibility of Allah.' (Quran, Chapter 11, Verse 6)

**Attribution:** Quran 11:6, cited by Hatim as the anchor of the seventh benefit.

**Why this matters:** The verse names who is responsible for the next meal. Not the creature. The Creator. Hatim acts on this so completely that the anxious scramble that produced doubtful and *Haram* earnings dissolves at the root.

---

## 14. The verses that close the circle

> *'Wa Maey Yatawakkal 'AlalAllahi Fahuwa Hasbuhu.'*
> 'And for everyone who places his/her trust in Allah, He [alone] is enough [for him/her].' (Quran, Chapter 65, Verse 3)

> *'Wa Huwa Husbi Wa Nai'mal Wakeel.'*
> 'And He is sufficient for me and He is the best Helper.'

**Attribution:** Quran 65:3 and the closing phrase *He is sufficient for me*, cited by Hatim as the anchor of the eighth benefit.

**Why this matters:** The two verses that close the eight. The first identified deeds as the only beloved who descends with the corpse; the eighth identifies Allah as the only support that does not fail. The bracket has closed.

---

## 15. Shafeeq's verdict and Ghazali's pivot

> "O Hatim! May Allah grant you His Tawfeeq (i.e. enable you to do righteous deeds and to walk on His Path). You have told [us] very good things. I have seen in Taurat (Torah), Injil (Gospel of Prophet Jesus), Zabur (Psalms), and in the praiseworthy differentiator between right and wrong (i.e. the Holy Quran), that all these books [of God] mention these eight benefits, i.e., all four books in their teachings have told [us] these eight benefits. Whoever acts on these [eight principles] is like as if he/she has acted on all four [holy] books."

**Attribution:** Shaqiq al-Balkhi (rendered *Shafeeq Balkhi* in the source), closing the vignette.

**Why this matters:** The verdict the section turns on. Eight short statements, each anchored in one verse, are the marrow of all four revealed books. Ghazali turns directly from this to his own student with *you do not need a lot of knowledge*. The closing of the vignette is the opening of the next teaching.
