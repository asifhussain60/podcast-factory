## Further Sayings of Wisdom

⟪ar:ان الحکیم قال⟫:

| The seas are four: the world is the sea of sins, the soul is the sea of desires, death is the sea of deeds, and the grave is the sea of regret. |
| --- |

The image of the sea here gestures at two qualities at once: abundance and the peculiar character proper to each thing. The world is a dwelling from which no one escapes wholly free of transgression, a house in which the occasions for sin are without number. The soul, in turn, raises desires in successive waves; if a person does not steady himself with intellect (ʿaql), the soul will carry him to ruin. When death arrives, nothing of a person remains except his deeds, good and bad alike — and so one ought to gather as much good as the lifespan allows. And when at last he is laid in the grave and tastes its chastisement, he is overwhelmed by regret for every evil he committed and every good he failed to perform. The summons (daʿwa) of the Imams is precisely to forestall this final regret while the soul still has agency in this world.[^cite-1]

---

| The fortresses are three: the mosque is a fortress, the remembrance of Allah is a fortress, and the recitation of the Qurʾān is a fortress. |
| --- |

A fortress (ḥiṣn) is the place into which a person withdraws to defend himself against an enemy. The narrator instructs that the believer who would shelter himself from the deceiver has three citadels available to him. The first is to occupy himself with worship; the second is the constant remembrance of Allah, for remembrance strengthens the luminous faculties of the soul and weakens the forces of the adversary; the third is the recitation of the Qurʾān with understanding. As ⟪ar:مولانا علی زین العابدین علیہ السلام⟫ has said: when you recite the verses of mercy, the heart yearns for the bounty of Allah, and when you recite the verses of chastisement, the body trembles in fear of Him.[^cite-2] In other words, the believer ought never to be unoccupied. Either he is in worship, or in remembrance, or in recitation, or in meditation upon the divine commands.

---

| Four things are perfected by four. Prayer is perfected by prostration, fasting by the observance of the fiṭra, the pilgrimage by the sacrificial animal, and faith by struggle. |
| --- |

This saying establishes a principle: the reward of every earlier act remains suspended until the final act that completes it is performed. If a person undertakes all the labor of prayer but omits prostration, it is as though he has not prayed at all. If he fasts thirty days but fails the fiṭra at its close, his exertion is squandered. If he undertakes the full rigor of pilgrimage but does not slaughter, his Hajj is annulled. So too with faith: its completion lies in sustained struggle. The end of the matter is therefore decisive. A person may labor a lifetime, yet if that lifetime does not conclude with allegiance to ʿAlī and his progeny, the labor is lost. The kamāl thānī (secondary perfection) of any work is precisely the act that seals it; without that seal, the kamāl awwal (primary perfection) — the initial form of the work — yields no fruit.[^cite-3]

---

| Four things increase the light of the heart. The first is to occupy oneself in worship while hungry … |
| --- |

The first is to keep watch over worship in a state of hunger — that is, not to dull devotion by filling the stomach.

## Arbaʿa Jawāhir fī Jism Banī Ādam — The Four Jewels in the Body of the Children of Ādam

| Four jewels lie in the body of the children of Ādam, and four things destroy them. The jewels are intellect, religion, modesty, and righteous deed. Anger destroys the intellect; envy destroys religion; greed destroys modesty; and backbiting destroys righteous deed. |
| --- |

There are four essences (jawāhir, real interior powers) in the human body, and each is undone by a corresponding vice.

### Anger

The first jewel is intellect, by which the human being acquired the dignity of being called ⟪ar:اشرف المخلوقات⟫. Allah is the originator of all things and their creator. Among His creatures there is much that is noble, yet to the human alone has been given the title ⟪ar:’اشرف المخلوقات‘⟫ — the noblest of all noble created things. This nobility was conferred upon him because of the intellect, the faculty by which he weighs and chooses. For this reason the human being is the only creature for whom paradise and hellfire were prepared, since he alone has the power to accept the good and abandon the evil, or to abandon the good and embrace the evil. Among human beings themselves, the most truly noble are those gifted with the inheritance of noble ancestry as it relates to intellect — those whose virtuous deeds do not remain at the level of a single point but are gradually shaped into a noble form. Merely to possess intellect is no honor; the honor lies in its use. So too with the covenant of allegiance (ʿahd): merely to receive it is no nobility; the nobility consists in employing it in one's beliefs, intentions, pursuit of knowledge, and conduct. The second essence is religion, by which the person distinguishes right from wrong; religion is the sphere in which intellect performs its work. The third is modesty, and the fourth is righteous deed.

Anger and rage destroy a person's intellect. The right to be angry on account of evil deeds belongs to Allah alone. Wrath impairs memory and judgment. The Commander of the Faithful (Amīr al-Muʾminīn) has said in one place: "The beginning of anger is madness and its end is regret; and whoever does not regret in the end and persists in his interpretation is in truth a madman."[^cite-4] The Prophet, peace and blessings upon him, said: "The best of you is the one who is slow to anger and swift to be pleased; the worst is the one who is swift to anger and slow to be pleased." When Mūsā, in a moment of anger, struck a man and killed him, he at once recognized the act as Satan's work and acknowledged him to be a manifest enemy:

> ⟪quran 28:15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَدَخَلَ ٱلْمَدِينَةَ عَلَىٰ حِينِ غَفْلَةٍۢ مِّنْ أَهْلِهَا فَوَجَدَ فِيهَا رَجُلَيْنِ يَقْتَتِلَانِ هَذَا مِن شِيعَتِهِۦ وَهَذَا مِنْ عَدُوِّهِۦ ۖ فَٱسْتَغَثَهُ ٱلَّذِى مِن شِيعَتِهِۦ عَلَى ٱلَّذِى مِنْ عَدُوِّهِۦ فَوَكَزَهُۥ مُوسَىٰ فَقَضَىٰ عَلَيْهِ ۖ قَالَ هَذَا مِنْ عَمَلِ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ عَدُوٌّۭ مُّضِلٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And he entered the city at a time of unawareness of its people, and he found there two men fighting, - one of his party (his religion - from the Children of Israel), and the other of his foes. The man of his (own) party asked him for help against his foe, so Musa (Moses) struck him with his fist and killed him. He said: "This is of Shaitan's (Satan) doing, verily, he is a plain misleading enemy." *
> And he entered the city at a time when its people were unaware, and he found therein two men fighting, one of his party, and the other of his enemies, and he sought help from him against his enemy. Then Moses smote him, and he finished his affair. It is misleading.
> — **Quran 28:15 (inline widget)**

In another place the Commander of the Faithful said that the most difficult thing is "forgiveness in the moment of anger," and elsewhere, "forgiveness when one has the power to retaliate." He also said: "Everything has a charity (zakāt); the charity of victory is forgiveness."[^cite-5]

### Envy

Envy destroys a person's religion. The purpose of religion is the rectification of the self — the instrument by which a person carves out his portion in this world and the next. But when a person becomes envious of another, he no longer pursues his own good; he is drawn instead toward another's destruction and ceases to attend to his own loss. So Qābīl envied Hābīl and killed him, though killing brought him no benefit; he merely committed a grave sin and ruined his hereafter.

### Greed

Greed and avarice destroy a person's modesty and shame, and a person becomes shameless. It is, for instance, an indispensable mark of modesty that a person not extend his hand in supplication before any other than Allah. Yet when greed for some object seizes him, he becomes shameless, holds out his hand before others, and gives no thought to the dignity he loses among them. His sole aim is the satisfaction of his own avarice — a most degraded condition.

### Backbiting

Backbiting destroys a person's righteous deed and consumes his virtues from within. Backbiting always implicates two parties, for the tongue of the slanderer always requires an ear. The one who listens to backbiting is therefore an equal partner in the wrong of the one who utters it, and his good deeds too are squandered.

Once, when the Prophet, peace upon him, was in the chamber of ʿĀʾisha, a woman came from a great distance to ask him questions of knowledge and jurisprudence. When she had gone, ʿĀʾisha said: "O Messenger of Allah, how dark and stout she was, how ugly her voice, how strange her gait!" The Messenger of Allah replied: "ʿĀʾisha, you have backbitten." She said: "O Messenger of Allah, you know that I have spoken only the truth — every word I said is in her." He answered: "It is precisely because it is true that it is backbiting. Slander is always true. But your truth is this: had that veiled woman heard what you said, she would have been wounded." That is, backbiting is a truth that ought not to be uttered.

Beyond this, Allah has declared that backbiting is like the eating of the flesh of one's dead brother. The flesh of the dead is already forbidden and the natural soul recoils from it; yet Allah has called backbiting the flesh of a *dead brother* — a still more abhorrent image.

## Al-Qalb Thalātha — The Heart Is of Three Kinds

⟪ar:و قال علی علیہ السلام:⟫

| The heart is of three kinds. There is a heart preoccupied with the world, a heart preoccupied with the hereafter, and a heart preoccupied with its Lord. The heart that is preoccupied with the world has misery, torment, and curse. The heart preoccupied with the hereafter has paradise. And the heart preoccupied with its Lord attains religion. |
| --- |

In the human being, the heart (qalb, ⟪ar:’قلب‘⟫) is the ruling member of the body (ruʾūs al-badan, ⟪ar:رأئس البدن⟫). Every action of a human being unfolds under the will of the heart. In this saying, the Commander of the Faithful divides human striving into three. The first is the world; the second is the hereafter; the third is the heart's own limit — the boundary at which knowledge of the Lord becomes possible. The person who runs after this world and makes it his goal receives three things in his portion: his heart grows enraged, he meets humiliation, and he is overtaken by calamity, for humiliation, indignity, and curse are precisely the attributes of the three worlds when sought for themselves. The heart that meditates always upon the hereafter and prepares its deeds for it shall receive paradise. By implication, this saying also indicates that if paradise is for those who reflect on the hereafter, then hell is surely for those who flee after the world. And the heart engaged in the knowledge of its Lord — that is, in the knowledge of the ḥadd ʿālī (the upper limit), the duʿāt (the Summoners), and the great Imams — attains religion, which is the positive form of the world. If the pursuit of the world brings misery, humiliation, and misfortune, the attainment of religion brings mercy, honor, and blessing.

The second matter worth noting is this: preoccupation with the world and preoccupation with the hereafter are both, in the end, oriented to the self. The one who runs after the world seeks his own benefit, and the seeker of paradise is likewise concerned with himself. But the one who strives for the knowledge of his Lord directs his effort toward the self of his superior — toward what is higher than himself. For this reason, the merit of such a person increases greatly in the sight of Allah, since his striving has passed from the lower self to the order of the daʿwa (the summons) and its ḥudūd.[^cite-6]

## ʿĀrif Billāh Labīb — The God-Conscious Sage of Discernment

It has been narrated:

| The one who knows Allah (al-ʿārif bi-llāh) is discerning; his heart is all-seeing; and his action for the sake of Allah is much. And the gnostic of Allah is loyal, his heart pure, his deed unmixed. |
| --- |

Allah has said in the Qurʾān:

> ⟪quran 8:74⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَهَاجَرُوا۟ وَجَهَدُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَٱلَّذِينَ آوَوا۟ وَّنَصَرُوٓا۟ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ حَقًّۭا ۚ لَّهُم مَّغْفِرَةٌۭ وَرِزْقٌۭ كَرِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *&nbsp;And those who believed, and emigrated and strove hard in the Cause of Allah (Al-Jihad), as well as those who gave (them) asylum and aid; - these are the believers in truth, for them is forgiveness and Rizqun Karîm (a generous provision i.e. Paradise).*
> And those who believed, and left their homes, and strove hard in the way of Allah, and those who gave them shelter and helped them, these are the believers truly; they shall have forgiveness and an honourable sustenance.
> — **Quran 8:74 (inline widget)**

And in another place He says:

> ⟪quran 22:78⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَجَهِدُوا۟ فِى ٱللَّهِ حَقَّ جِهَادِهِۦ ۚ هُوَ ٱجْتَبَىٰكُمْ وَمَا جَعَلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلدِّينِ مِنْ حَرَجٍۢ ۚ مِّلَّةَ أَبِيكُمْ إِبْرَهِيمَ ۚ هُوَ سَمَّىٰكُمُ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ مِن قَبْلُ وَفِى هَذَا لِيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ شَهِيدًا عَلَيْكُمْ وَتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ ۚ فَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَٱعْتَصِمُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ هُوَ مَوْلَىٰكُمْ ۖ فَنِعْمَ ٱلْمَوْلَىٰ وَنِعْمَ ٱلنَّصِيرُ ‎⟫
> *And strive hard in Allah's Cause as you ought to strive (with sincerity and with all your efforts that His Name should be superior). He has chosen you (to convey His Message of Islamic Monotheism to mankind by inviting them to His religion, Islam), and has not laid upon you in religion any hardship, it is the religion of your father Ibrahim (Abraham) (Islamic Monotheism). It is He (Allah) Who has named you Muslims both before and in this (the Quran), that the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) may be a witness over you and you be witnesses over mankind! So perform As&shy;Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), give Zakat and hold fast to Allah [i.e. have confidence in Allah, and depend upon Him in all your affairs] He is your Maula (Patron, Lord, etc.), what an Excellent Maula (Patron, Lord, etc.) and what an Excellent Helper!*
> And strive in the way of Allah, as it is due to strive, He has chosen you, and has not laid upon you any hardship in religion. The creed of your father Abraham, who named you Muslims before, and in this Qur'an, that the Messenger may be a witness over you, and that you may be witnesses over the people. So establish prayer and give zakat, and hold fast to Allah. Your Lord, so what an excellent parent, and what an excellent helper!
> — **Quran 22:78 (inline widget)**

Two distinct registers of struggle (⟪ar:’جھد‘⟫, jahd) are at work in these verses. In the first verse, the command is to wage jihād in the way of Allah — the struggle of the self, the struggle of wealth, and so on. In the second, Allah says: "Strive in Allah" (⟪ar:’جاہدو فی اللہ‘⟫) — that is, strive to attain the recognition of His Oneness and the knowledge of Him, to know which limit (ḥadd) stands in which station, whose is the heaven above each rank, and whose is the earth below it. To know these ḥudūd and to give each its due — this is what "striving *in* Allah" truly means.[^cite-7]

A person who strives to attain the knowledge of his Lord is discerning (labīb), and his heart is visionary (baṣīr): he is able to look upon the outer and to reach by it to the inner self (bāṭin) through taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis). The sign of such a person is that his action for the sake of Allah is much. He does not confine himself to the few obligatory duties; he becomes a constant practitioner of supererogatory worship, glorification, and praise. He labors as fully as he can to please his Lord and to win His good pleasure. The one who possesses such knowledge of Allah is loyal to the Imam, peace upon him, and to his order of daʿwa. He fulfills the outward and the inward obligations entrusted to him — as it has been said:

> ⟪quran 18:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَتَحْسَبُهُمْ أَيْقَاظًۭا وَهُمْ رُقُودٌۭ ۚ وَنُقَلِّبُهُمْ ذَاتَ ٱلْيَمِينِ وَذَاتَ ٱلشِّمَالِ ۖ وَكَلْبُهُم بَسِطٌۭ ذِرَاعَيْهِ بِٱلْوَصِيدِ ۚ لَوِ ٱطَّلَعْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ لَوَلَّيْتَ مِنْهُمْ فِرَارًۭا وَلَمُلِئْتَ مِنْهُمْ رُعْبًۭا ‎⟫
> *And you would have thought them awake, while they were asleep. And We turned them on their right and on their left sides, and their dog stretching forth his two forelegs at the entrance [of the Cave or in the space near to the entrance of the Cave (as a guard at the gate)]. Had you looked at them, you would certainly have turned back from them in flight, and would certainly have been filled with awe of them.*
> And thou wouldst think them awake, while they were asleep, and We turned them over to the right and to the left, and their dog stretched forth his arms at the threshold. If thou hadst observed them, thou wouldst have turned on their heels, and verily, thou art awe of theirs.
> — **Quran 18:18 (inline widget)**

In the inward sense (bāṭin), the dog at the threshold figures the dāʿī (⟪ar:’داعی‘⟫, Summoner) who is utterly loyal to his Imam (mālik). He carries out every command without hesitation, however heavy the difficulties he must bear. His heart is purified from every impurity of doubt, polytheism, and disbelief; his deed is pure and unmixed; and it is for Allah alone. In this he does not act upon his own desire, nor does he seek the praise of others, nor does he draw any benefit from them by his actions.[^cite-8]

## ʿAlī's Verses at the Burial of Mawlātnā Fāṭima

Mawlānā ʿAlī laid Mawlānā Fāṭima in the grave and poured the earth upon her. Since the people stood nearby, he called out: ⟪ar:فاطمۃ! فاطمۃ!⟫ — "but who shall answer?" Then he recited:

⟪ar-quote:مَا لِی وَقَفتُ عَلیَ القُبُورِ مُسَلِّمَۃ         قبرُ الحبیبِ فلا یَرُدُّ جوابِ⟫

This is that same Fāṭima who would rise at a single sign from me. Today my voice rings out, and yet no answer returns.

⟪ar-quote:أحبیبُ مَا لَکَ لاَ تَرُدُّ جوابَنا                   أنَسیتَ بَعدِ خُلَّتِ الأحبابِ⟫

Do those who go to the grave forget? Indeed, I think they forget.

⟪ar-quote:لکلِّ اجتماع من خَلِیلَینِ فُرقۃٌ           وکُلَّ الذَّی قَبلَ المماتِ قَلیلُ⟫

Surely every union changes, every thing is lost, every stone is broken at last. Death comes and parts what was joined. The dead sleep in the grave, and none can sleep beside them. Today Fāṭima has been parted from me.

⟪ar-quote:أِنَّ افتِقَادِی فاطمۃَ بعد أحمدا           دلیلٌ علی ان لَّا یَدُومَ خلیلُ⟫

With these very hands I carried Muḥammad, peace upon him, to the grave. With these same hands today I have taken leave of Fāṭima. And it has become plain to me that no one in this place can sustain another. However long a companion's presence may last, in the end it breaks.[^cite-9]

⟪ar:‏وَتَحْسَبُهُمْ أَيْقَاظًۭا وَهُمْ رُقُودٌۭ ۚ وَنُقَلِّبُهُمْ ذَاتَ ٱلْيَمِينِ وَذَاتَ ٱلشِّمَالِ ۖ وَكَلْبُهُم بَسِطٌۭ ذِرَاعَيْهِ بِٱلْوَصِيدِ ۚ لَوِ ٱطَّلَعْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ لَوَلَّيْتَ مِنْهُمْ فِرَارًۭا وَلَمُلِئْتَ مِنْهُمْ رُعْبًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَجَهِدُوا۟ فِى ٱللَّهِ حَقَّ جِهَادِهِۦ ۚ هُوَ ٱجْتَبَىٰكُمْ وَمَا جَعَلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلدِّينِ مِنْ حَرَجٍۢ ۚ مِّلَّةَ أَبِيكُمْ إِبْرَهِيمَ ۚ هُوَ سَمَّىٰكُمُ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ مِن قَبْلُ وَفِى هَذَا لِيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ شَهِيدًا عَلَيْكُمْ وَتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ ۚ فَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَٱعْتَصِمُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ هُوَ مَوْلَىٰكُمْ ۖ فَنِعْمَ ٱلْمَوْلَىٰ وَنِعْمَ ٱلنَّصِيرُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَدَخَلَ ٱلْمَدِينَةَ عَلَىٰ حِينِ غَفْلَةٍۢ مِّنْ أَهْلِهَا فَوَجَدَ فِيهَا رَجُلَيْنِ يَقْتَتِلَانِ هَذَا مِن شِيعَتِهِۦ وَهَذَا مِنْ عَدُوِّهِۦ ۖ فَٱسْتَغَثَهُ ٱلَّذِى مِن شِيعَتِهِۦ عَلَى ٱلَّذِى مِنْ عَدُوِّهِۦ فَوَكَزَهُۥ مُوسَىٰ فَقَضَىٰ عَلَيْهِ ۖ قَالَ هَذَا مِنْ عَمَلِ ٱلشَّيْطَنِ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ عَدُوٌّۭ مُّضِلٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَهَاجَرُوا۟ وَجَهَدُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَٱلَّذِينَ آوَوا۟ وَّنَصَرُوٓا۟ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ حَقًّۭا ۚ لَّهُم مَّغْفِرَةٌۭ وَرِزْقٌۭ كَرِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
