Inma al-Mu'minin Brotherhood of Righteousness Ben Akhukum Watqwa Allah L'Alayhi Tarahun

The believers are brothers to one another, so be at peace with your brothers, and fear God, that you may be shown mercy

A brother-to-father relationship is one that is generally accepted and understood by people. The second relationship is that which Allah and His Messenger have ordained and that is the relationship of faith. Allah addresses the believers and says, "O you who believe, the believers are brothers to one another." In this way, this bond of brotherhood becomes superior to that of the parents, because it is from Allah and His Messenger. After the bond of this faith, it becomes necessary for the believers to be at peace with each other and seek the betterment of each other. And finally, he said, "Fear Allah, that you may be shown mercy or mercy may be bestowed upon you." The nature of brotherhood that Allah and His Messenger have mentioned shows what a believer's conduct should be towards his fellow believers. The second part of the verse shows that in addition to reconciliation, it is also commanded to have the fear of God. From the combination of these two tasks, he may deserve mercy.

What should not be said by commanding brotherhood and reconciliation has been explained in the later verses. He said:

Or those who believe that the people of Jesus are the people of Jesus, and that they are the people of Jesus, and that they are the people of Jesus, and that they are the people of Jesus, and that they are the people of Jesus, and that they are the people of Jesus, and that they are the people of Jesus, and that they are the people of the world.

O you who believe, let not a man of one nation laugh at the men of another, for it may be that those whom you ridicule are good in the sight of God, and women do not ridicule women. And those who do not desist from doing so, and do not repent, it is they who are the wrongdoers.

In this verse, it is forbidden to praise oneself and to do evil to others, and it has been warned that if you make fun of others by thinking that you are very good, then it is possible that the people you are ridiculing are better in the sight of God. Or those who believe in the Prophethood of the Most Merciful, and some of them are the Companions of the Faithful, and the Companions of the Faithful, and the Companions of the Prophets, the Believers of the Faithful, the Righteous and the Righteous.

O you who believe, beware of suspicion, for some suspicions become sin, and do not spy on one another's affairs and affairs, nor backbite one another. Would any of you like to eat the flesh of your dead brother? You will hate him and fear God. God is the Great Acceptor of Repentance, the Merciful.

What an accurate and clear observation of the mindset of the people. How many suspicions are made of one's own and from others, how much effort is made to know the affairs of one's neighbors and others, and how much backbiting is done! Where four people, whether men or women, sit together, it is often that they try to find out each other's evils, backbiting, and each other's inner affairs. It is injunctive for every believer to think carefully about these things and understand how bad there is in suspicion, spying and backbiting and try his best to avoid them.

We are very concerned that the meat they buy from the market or eat in hotels is not wrong or haram. But when they sit together, they eagerly eat the meat, which the Qur'an describes as the flesh of their dead brother. The Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) said, "The good deeds of the one who backbites are taken out of his book of deeds and written down in the book of deeds of which he backbites."
