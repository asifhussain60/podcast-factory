## The Messenger as Bearer of Sharīʿa (Ṣāḥib Sharīʿa Rasūl)

Among all the apostles there is not a single messenger who has not first been bound to the law of his predecessor, observing its commandments faithfully until the moment he himself was raised to the rank of messengership. The Prophet is born into the community of the faithful and grows up among them; only then does he attain the purity, knowledge, and capacity that prepare him for his office. It is the last Imam of the previous prophet's era who confirms him and installs him in the rank of prophethood, so that the chain of guidance is never interrupted and the daʿwa (the summons / the invitation) of God is transmitted without rupture from one cycle to the next.[^cite-1]

## The Four Causes of the World's Coming-Into-Being

### The Conception of the Ordinary Scholars

According to the scholars at large, the world has come into being for four causes:

| 1 | God created His creatures in order to disclose and manifest His own existence. For had He not created creation, who would have known that He exists and that creation has a Creator? |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | He created firm and well-ordered things in order to display His knowledge and power, so that it might be shown that such forms and such acts can issue only from One who is Almighty. |
| 3 | He created creatures for His worship, in order to reward worshippers according to His own bounty rather than according to the deficiency of their deeds — recompensing them many-fold for their ordinary acts, since His sovereignty stands in no need of the worship of His creatures. The obedience of the obedient adds nothing to His dominion, nor does the disobedience of the disobedient diminish it. As He Himself has said: *"And what is the creation of the world?"* |
| 4 | He created creatures and conferred favour upon them so that His attribute of *muḥsin* (the One who acts with grace) might become manifest. He deals with some in justice and with others in grace, for He is at once Just and Gracious. He has created the believers in particular to bestow His mercy upon them, as He has said: *"wa-kāna bi-l-muʾminīna raḥīman"* (And He is ever merciful to the believers). |

### This World Was Brought Forth for the Master of the Cosmos

After these brief preliminary reasons advanced by the scholars, we affirm that we too believe that God created the whole of creation for the sake of the Essence of the World ⟪ar:حضرت محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ و سلم⟫, who is the greatest blessing and favour of God upon mankind. All other things stand as preparatory stages for his appearance. It is for this reason that He created Adam and all the other prophets and messengers — that through them the essence of this Master might come into being.[^cite-2]

It is narrated from the venerable Companion ⟪ar:قتادہ سعید ابن مسیب⟫ that God revealed to Jesus: "O Jesus, believe in ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ and command your people to believe in him. Had it not been for ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫, I would not have created Adam, nor would I have created Paradise and Hell. When I established My Throne upon the water, it began to tremble; but when I inscribed upon it ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ محمد رسول اللہ⟫, it grew still." The Throne rests upon the water, and water is itself a figure for knowledge. This indicates that knowledge is the necessary path to God, to the recognition of Him, and to His tawḥīd (the affirmation of divine unity).

### The Conception of the Mystic of Truth

A mystic of truth has said that the Creator established this world upon the most exalted wisdom — a wisdom hidden from scholars and common folk alike. The narrator of this wisdom will, when the time comes, declare it from the pulpit ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫ (openly and in full proclamation). In short, He did not create the world in vain, nor in jest, nor without meaning or purpose. As He Himself has declared:

> Did you think that We had created you in vain, and that you would not be returned to Us?

## The Secret of Shayṭān's Power Over Humans and How He Leads Them Astray

Here we wish to set forth some of the reasons why Shayṭān has prevailed over the human being and succeeded in leading him astray, and to explain why a person is so readily drawn to satanic appetites — willing to drink wine and to commit adultery — yet so reluctant to draw upon the indignation and wrath that might preserve him from Shayṭān, even though God has openly warned him that Shayṭān is his manifest enemy:

> ⟪quran 36:60⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ أَلَمْ أَعْهَدْ إِلَيْكُمْ يَبَنِىٓ آدَمَ أَن لَّا تَعْبُدُوا۟ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ لَكُمْ عَدُوٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Did I not ordain for you, O Children of Adam, that you should not worship Shaitan (Satan). Verily, he is a plain enemy to you.*
> O children of Adam! Did I not enjoin upon you that you should not worship Satan? Indeed, he is to you a clear enemy.
> — **Quran 36:60 (inline widget)**

The principal cause, we say, is that Shayṭān has overcome the human being by his own cunning and contrivance, and he has done so by enlisting the natural forces already within the human himself. Instead of seeking refuge in God in the hour of satanic assault, the human surrenders to Shayṭān and yields himself in submission. By way of example: Shayṭān has gained mastery over a person through the very faculty of sexual desire and appetite. The desire that God placed in the human being for the preservation of the self and the propagation of the species has been turned to illegitimate, immoral, and unlawful ends, and has thereby become the chief cause of corruption and ruin upon the earth. As it has been well said: "Gold, land, and women — the root of every corruption is in these three."

So too with the faculty of righteous anger which God placed in the human being so that he might repel from himself what is harmful and undesirable. Shayṭān has prevailed over this faculty also, turning its good force toward evil and making the very instrument of self-defence into a cause of ruin and destruction. The inclination of man toward sins resembles the inclination of an invalid toward foods that are harmful to him. When a person's constitution departs from moderation, he begins to crave precisely those things that injure him and aggravate his disease. The feverish man asks only for cold water, though cold water vexes him further. The man whose digestion is ruined, unable to assimilate even a little food, craves to eat more — and the more he eats, the more his stomach is disordered and never satisfied. The healthy person, by contrast, never craves what harms him, but draws nourishment from everything wholesome and beneficial.

The world may be compared to the air itself, which germs corrupt and render harmful. Yet since the human being cannot survive without breathing, he must purify this foul and contaminated air with sweet substances — camphor, incense, fragrant pastilles — and dispel its stench by spraying disinfectants and the like. In short, he employs every remedy that cleanses bad air, and continues to live within that very same air. In just this manner the human being is, at every step, beset by the evil whisperings of this world, which threaten to destroy his soul. He must live within this atmosphere, but the way to escape the powers of Shayṭān is to renounce one's appetites, to remember God, to seek His help, to lessen one's craving for the world, and to obey the commands of the Messenger. These practices will inevitably generate good thoughts in the heart and mind; the intellect and temperament will be restored to moderation; and the inclination of the soul will turn toward the words of truth and toward the people of truth. Then the obligations of the sharīʿa — prayer, zakāt, fasting, and the rest — will no longer be felt as burdens, but as a comfort and a delight, and his ardour in obedience to the divine commandments will only increase. At that hour, Shayṭān will confess ⟪ar:علی الاعلان⟫: "Over this man I shall never again gain dominion."[^cite-3]

The Messenger of God (peace be upon him) said to ⟪ar:حضرت ابوذر غفاری⟫: "O Abū Dharr, be in the world as a traveller, and count yourself among the dead. Speak little, but remember God much; by this you shall vanquish Shayṭān. Speak the truth, though the hearer find it bitter. Love the poor and sit in their gatherings. Be content with a piece of bread sufficient to sustain your body and a garment sufficient to cover you — and rejoice in this. Do not love the world, for it is the foundation of every sin. To whoever turns from the world, all hardships are made light."[^cite-4]

To "count oneself among the dead" means that, as the dead have no will of their own, so every Muslim must conduct his every act and shape his every desire according to the sharīʿa, retaining no private choice of his own. As the poet says:

Relationship with Friends | Substantial damages per capita

My friend has placed a rope around my neck. He leads me wherever He wills; my selfish and personal desires have no share in the matter.

O brother, the worship of Shayṭān is of two kinds. The first is open disobedience to the commands of God. The second is to obey God's commands, but not in the manner that God has revealed — rather in the manner of innovations and of those who alter the religion. For example, the common people pray, but they pray the Tarāwīḥ, which is an innovation.

In the same way, Shayṭān's seduction proceeds along two paths. Sometimes he sends his disciples and followers to mislead the servant; they approach him and speak words contrary to the commands of God and His Messenger, planting in his heart such doubts and confusions that, finding no resolution from within himself, the man is led astray. The second way is that Shayṭān himself makes the human being his instrument by means of whispered suggestion, prompting him to sin by his own will. So if a man comes to you as a well-wisher and offers you counsel, listen first, then measure his words against what God and His Messenger have said. Examine the Qurʾān and apply the test of intellect. If his counsel does not accord with them, be assured that this man, clothed in the garment of goodwill, is a member of Shayṭān's party, dispatched on his behalf. To obey him would be the worship of Shayṭān. Likewise, when a thought arises in you to do something, pause first and consider whether the sharīʿa permits the act. If the sharīʿa does not permit it and yet the soul desires it, know that this is a satanic whisper and that Shayṭān has made you his tool. Refrain from the act at once; otherwise Shayṭān will overpower you and you will become his obedient subject. These two paths God has described in the following words:

> ⟪quran 6:112⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّۭا شَيَطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًۭا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And so We have appointed for every Prophet enemies - Shayatin (devils) among mankind and jinns, inspiring one another with adorned speech as a delusion (or by way of deception).&nbsp;If your Lord had so willed, they would not have done it, so leave them alone with their fabrications. (Tafseer Qurtubi, Vol.7, Page 67)*
> Thus have We made for every prophet an enemy, the evil man, and the jinn, who teach one another deceitful talk. Had your Lord willed, they would not have done it. So leave them to what they fabricate.
> — **Quran 6:112 (inline widget)**

This verse explains that Shayṭān whispers and adorns false suggestions back and forth — sometimes through an external agency, what the verse calls ⟪ar:’أنس‘⟫ (a human), and sometimes through an internal agency, the ⟪ar:’جن‘⟫, which is the soul ⟪ar:أمارۃ بالسوء⟫ (commanding to evil).

In addition to these two paths, Shayṭān has yet another means of misleading the human and binding him to himself. He first incites the person to oppose God's commands; if the person yields, Shayṭān's design is fulfilled and the man becomes a servant and worshipper of Shayṭān. But if the person pays no heed to his suggestion and instead obeys God, then Shayṭān returns with this whisper: "Yes, yes! Obey God, that your honour and dignity may grow among the people. Earn the reputation of a saint. Let people regard you as a friend of God, so that your brothers and helpers may benefit from your greatness and renown." If the person now worships God with this intention that Shayṭān has implanted, his act will no longer be the worship of God; it will be obedience to Shayṭān. In short, Shayṭān leaves no stratagem untried to deceive the human, and turns every weapon of his arsenal upon him.

## The Three Categories of Sinners and the Disobedient

Those who follow Shayṭān are themselves of three kinds; not every sinner and disobedient one is alike.

| 1 | Some follow the words of Shayṭān with their hearts inclined toward him and their tongues praising what he prompts. They obey his command with full joy and wholehearted assent. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | Some commit evil deeds yet inwardly detest them; their tongues name them as evil. When they fall into such acts, their conscience rebukes and warns them, and they at once confess what they have done and beg forgiveness from their Lord. Their service to Shayṭān is confined to the outward members; the heart itself remains untouched by sin. |
| 3 | Some commit evil but do not perceive it to be evil. Their hearts and minds adapt to the wrong deed without reflection. These are they who sin out of ignorance. |

### Atonement for a Sin Committed in Ignorance

The sins of such people are washed away through the diseases, pains, hardships and afflictions that come upon them, as the Prophet's ḥadīth declares: ⟪ar-quote:الحُمّٰی من فِیحِ جھنم⟫ — "Fever is a breath from the breaths of Hell." Then he said: ⟪ar-quote:الحُمّٰی طُ⟫⟪ar-quote:ھور من رب غفور⟫ — "Fever is a purification from a Forgiving Lord." Then he said: ⟪ar-quote:السَّیفُ مَحَّاء للذُّنُوب⟫ — "The sword is an effacer of sins."

### Atonement for an Intentional Sin

Sins that are committed with delight in the heart and praise upon the tongue cannot be effaced except by repentance and return to God; without these there is no remedy. It is incumbent upon such a person to turn with sincerity to God seeking forgiveness, and to restrain his tongue from praising what is evil. The heart is the king and ruler of the body; the tongue is its minister and counsellor; the limbs are its servants. A sin committed by the heart's intent, to satisfy one's own appetite, is therefore a grave sin indeed. Whatever is done in defiance of God's commands and for the love of other than God will incur a painful chastisement. So too the act committed by the tongue is reckoned back to the heart, and the one who sins with his tongue receives a punishment like that of the first.[^cite-5]

### When Ignorance Leads to Sin, Progress Is Not Hindered

The sins that issue from the limbs despite the heart's aversion are those of which it is reported that God says: "O My servants, if you do not sin, I shall surely raise up a people who will sin, and then will ask My forgiveness." There is a profound wisdom in these words. Sometimes Shayṭān contrives that an unwary man fall into a sin against God; then he dances with delight at his apparent triumph and says, "I have deceived such-and-such a righteous servant of God! I have caught him in my snare so that he cannot escape; I have made him act against his Lord's will!" But in truth, the very sin that Shayṭān celebrates becomes the means by which the rank of this righteous person is raised. For when a pious person commits a sin in ignorance and then perceives what he has done, his heart is broken; and any trace of arrogance, self-regard, or vanity that had crept into it through his worship and long meditations is swept away. In this way he is preserved from the greater sin of self-conceit; and when he begs forgiveness for the lesser sin, his station rises higher still. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said in another ḥadīth: ⟪ar-quote:أنا مُنکَسِرَۃِ القُلُوب⟫ — "I am near to the broken-hearted." From this it may also be inferred that the slips and mistakes of the ⟪ar:انبیاء علیھم السلام⟫ were of just this nature: as soon as they sought forgiveness, their ranks were elevated and they were drawn closer to God.[^cite-6] Iqbāl says:

Do not keep your mirror intact — for the One who breaks is dearest to the mirror-maker.

Majd says:

God remembers a broken heart | A lover dies upon pride.

To dwell in the throne of heartbreak | is a strange cup, which breaks and is filled.

## A Wise Saying of the Prophet Dāwūd (David)

Dāwūd (peace be upon him) uttered a saying of great wisdom: "What has been before shall come to pass again." Things that have already been known shall be known again and shall return to manifestation, and there is nothing new under the sun. In other words, history repeats itself. Upon this principle, whatever appeared in the previous eras appeared once more in the era of our Prophet Muḥammad Muṣṭafā (peace be upon him); his age too was not without its accidents, dissensions, calamities, and disobediences. Since the very person of the Holy Prophet is the light in which all the former lights are gathered and into whom all the prior persons are merged, his attributes and qualities must likewise be the sum of all theirs. As someone has well said:

The Prophet's Virtue | The collective virtue of Muḥammad

Every prophet must possess some particular virtue; yet all these virtues are contained, complete and comprehensive, in Muḥammad.

A Persian poet has put it in this way:

The beauty of Yūsuf, the breath of ʿĪsā, the white hand — | gathered in water is the beauty of the water, and the solitude of the water.
