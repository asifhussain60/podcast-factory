And for this reason, this prayer is recited at the end of every gathering, just as it was recited when Asher left the assembly.

The Hakma says that when the disobedient group disobeyed Asher and then confessed their mistake and asked him for forgiveness, they both started with the Kalima (la). When he disobeyed, he said, "We do not listen to you nor obey you." And when he acknowledged it, he said, "There is no god but Asher."

Just as the invitation to the seven intellects was established in the spiritual world by the command of the first intellect, Ashir also intended to establish his invitation in the physical world. The difference was that the invitation of the first intellect was without time and space, whereas the invitation of Asher was in the confines of time and time. Just as the candle (which is subtle) cannot be removed without its tablet (which is dense), in the same way, in order to remove these beings from the spiritual world, it was necessary to convert the light into physicality.
