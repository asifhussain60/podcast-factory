# Interpretation of Surah Al-Ikhlas

The good word reaches God, and the good deed takes away the word

Just as the word of Ikhlas (La ilaha illa Allah) is the beginning of religion, so Surah Ikhlas is the end of religion, and according to the decree of Sana al-Hakeem, it is necessary that the beginning and the end of religion should be in accordance with each other. Surah Ikhlas is the summary and conclusion of the entire Qur'an, which was revealed at the end, so that both the opening and closing of the doors of religion are pure.

There are many similarities between Kalmat-ul-Ikhlas and Surah Ikhlas. Just as the word of Ikhlas (La ilaha illa Allah) is made up of four words, so Surah Ikhlas also consists of four verses. The first two verses of Kalmat-ul-Ikhlas are affirmative and the last two verses are affirmative, while the first two verses of Kalmat-ul-Ikhlas are affirmative and the last two verses are negation. The reason for this is that everything in its initial existence is in a passive form and in this state it is weak. And then it increases in power until it reaches the limit of the active form and becomes powerful and perfect. In this way, the word of Ikhlas (La ilaha illa Allah) began with the limit of power in the form of martyrdom, and became Surah al-Ikhlas and reached the point of action.

Lam Yild (He did not give birth to anyone)

The interpretation of this verse is that God creates from nothing. That is, He has made the first intellect the cause of all the other intellects and intellects, and it is superior to that it is the cause of anything. If a person says that all these intellects are born out of this process, it is as if he is referring to God as a cause. And the cause is as the father of things, and the father as he begets: and the son of him is born. And Allah is not the cause of anyone!

And no one has given birth to him.

The interpretation of this verse is that just as the Great Essence is not the cause of anything, in the same way He is not the cause of anything. The son is the father of his father. Everything that has a cause must have a cause. For example, heat is the cause of fire, and fire is the cause of smoke. Everything that is a cause is as if it is born of its own cause. He is neither the cause of anything nor the cause of anything.

Whoever believes that God is the All-Knowing, the Wise, or the Almighty, then he considers knowledge, wisdom and power to be His cause, because knowledge is the cause of the Universe, wisdom is the Wisdom of the Wise, and power is the cause of the Almighty. That is, it is as if he believes that God is born, but God is pure and superior to all these attributes and is the Creator of all beings and their attributes!

It has been stated that Allah is the Essence and that He has ninety-nine (99) attributes which have also been described by name! An object is identified by a name. In the same way, in reality, Allah is the essence of the first intellect and (99 names) are in fact the beings or limits that are created by the first intellect and make us know it. They are the Knowers, the Wise and the Almighty.

The existence of attributes is impossible without caste! We can reach the destination of caste only by following the path of attributes. When you know the attributes, you will be recognized, and this is Tawheed! We cannot monotheize the Creator because there is no way to get there. Thus we know the limits that He has created, and through their knowledge we support the Oneness of the Creator.

There is no equal to it.

The interpretation of this verse is that the ability of divinity (to create from nothingness to existence) is only a punishment for the Creator of the Almighty, and the first intellect, in spite of all its subtlety and majesty, is not equal to that of the Creator Almighty.

The beginning is that which the human imagination cannot reach! Since the first intellect has become preceded in its oneness, no one can match it, and no matter how much one strives, it cannot reach its status. He is noble, and all beings are in comparison to Him.
