It is customary to being the journey of knowledge with the topic of ADAB. This is why you'll see that the very first thing mentioned in CHAHAL SABAQ, is ADAB.  So I want to begin by explaining why this is so important.

The word for "beginning" in Arabic is called ⟪ar:بَدَأَ⟫; and one of the several meanings for the word "heart" (QALB/ INQILAAB) is "to reverse something". Now if you reverse the letters in the word "⟪ar:بَدَأَ⟫" (which are ba dAl and alif), you get ADAB; and so ADAB is the heart of knowledge. ADAB is a portal to the purification of the heart. It is the portal through which every spiritual journey begins. And for this reason alone, our Islamic tradition is so firmly rooted in this idea of ADAB.

ADAB is a difficult word to translate in English. It is generally translated as étiquettes or courtesy. The word ADEEB (which is a derivative of ADAB), means and "erudite person; someone who is learned, and has the best manners; someone who is very courteous". But in my opinion, a really good translation for the word ADAB is "comportment". 

*I did not even know what that word meant when I first read it.  I thought it was that thing in a car in which you put your stuff (the glove comportment).*

Comportment comes from two Latin words which means "how you carry yourself". The word "porter" is derived from it, because a porter is someone who carries your luggage. So comportment is "the way in which you carry yourself". And that's pretty much what ADAB is.

Now interestingly, ADAB is not something that is dictated by the SHAREEAH. The idea behind ADAB is that it is the most natural thing for a person. Because most of ADAB is simply common sense. 

Right after the battle of KHAYBER, which was faught against the Jews; a companion was asked to bring a notable from the Jewish community (⟪ar:صفية بنت حيي⟫) to Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam). As this man brought her to the prophet's tent, he passed through the battle-ground of KHYBER, where she saw the dead bodies of their husband lying there. So she gets so distressed by seeing her husband in that state, she begins wailing. So the prophet hears her cries, and he came out of his tent to see what happened, and he asks "What are you doing?".  The man says, I brought Sophia to you like you asked, and on the way she saw her dead husband and started crying. Rasul Allah said, "Couldn't you find another route to bring her to me?". And he said, "I did not think that you would mind". 
*In other words, "what's the big deal?". Isn't that response interesting? Because this is our answer to pretty much everything in life these days, right? What's the big deal. So what if I don't wear a cap before I have dinner; What's the big deal if I don't say BISMILLAH.....*

So the prophet looked at him and said,⟪ar:این رَحمَتُکَ؟⟫Where is your mercy? 

In other words, you don't need SHAREEAH to know this. You don't need revelation, to have basic common decency! And so a lot of ADAB is just that! It is a basic common decency. One of the hallmarks of Islamic learning is the relationship between students and teachers; or students and knowledge. It has always been a relationship of respect and compassion. The prophet said
⟪ar-quote:لیس مِنَّا مَن لا یَرحَم صَغِیرَنَا.....  ولا یُوَقِّر کَبِیرَنا⟫

He or she is not from among us, who does not have compassion for the young; and who does not respect the elderly. And KABEER does not necessarily mean age. It can means anything and everything that puts that thing in a high rank. Anything valuable or treasured is also called KABEER in Arabic. So knowledge (and especially divine knowledge) certainly falls in that category of KABEER. 

And for this reason, the topic of ADAB is so important in our times; because ADAB has become such an endangered species in this day and age. It is becoming completely extinct in our times. Children don't have ADAB for parents; students don't have ADAB for teachers or knowledge. We barely follow the ADAAB of talking, eating, sleeping. And this is why you will find a lot of people in our DEEN who acquire this amazing knowledge, but you still find their hearts to be wretched. And if you trace the reason for that disconnect, you can almost inevitably trace it back to a lack of ADAB in acquiring this knowledge. Because this is not material knowledge! This is divine knowledge; this is celestial knowledge that comes from Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala). He bestows this gift upon the selected few he chooses. And the only reason he does that, is so that the receiver can take this knowledge; put it into practice; and through the combination of the two, purify their hearts with it. Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) said…..
⟪ar-quote:من عَمِلَ بِمَا عَلِمَ ..... وَرَّثَهُ اللهُ ما لَم يَعلَم⟫

Whoever puts into practice what they learn, Allah will give them as an inheritance, knowledge of that which they did not know. And so people who fit this Hadees, they always put into practice what they learn. And as their knowledge increases their practice changes along with it. And what's so beautiful about this formula, is that we can always know what this person is learning, by witnessing what they are doing. So according to this hadees, when someone learns something and then applies it, their knowledge increases. But if one does not apply the proper ADAB while receiving this gift, this knowledge gets reduced to nothing more than academic information. It becomes something good to know (what we categorize as COOL). The knowledge stays in the brain, but never makes it to the heart. And there are many people in the Islam, and certainly our JAMAAT, that are living proofs of this fact.

And so we all must have courtesy with regard to Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala). We all need to behave properly with respect to His divine presence, if we wish to accomplish the goals of this knowledge. And the goal of this knowledge is the purification of our hearts. And make no mistake, there is a divine presence in these sessions. When Allah is called upon, He always answers!
⟪ar-quote:وَإِذَا سَأَلَكَ عِبَادِى عَنِّى فَإِنِّى قَرِيبٌ⟫

And when My slaves ask you, [O Muhammad], about Me - indeed I am near.

And whats so incredible about this verse is that Allah did not say,

When my slaves ask you about me, TELL THEM, I am near. NO! He says..... ⟪ar:وَإِذَا سَأَلَكَ عِبَادِى عَنِّى⟫..... when my slaves ask you about me..... ⟪ar:فَإِنِّى قَرِيبٌ.....⟫I am near. Allah took out the prophet (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) from the middle, and inserted himself directly into the conversation. This is the amazing rhetoric of the Quran! How Allah actually displays his instant nearness right in the verse. SubhanAllah!

So when Allah is called upon, he's right there in that very instant! And we know that angels flock around the gathering where the name of Allah is being mentioned. If only we had the eyes to witness it, we would see angels all around us right now; praying for us; asking forgiveness for us, just because we are mentioning the name of their beloved.

So the first step in acquiring this knowledge, is that we all display the proper ADAB for this glorious king of kings.  If possible, be in a state of wudu when you listen to these sessions, because
⟪ar-quote:حصول العلم من العبادۃ⟫

The acquisition of knowledge is a worship. And there is no worship without WUDU

So be mindful of whose presence we're in; during these, or any other sessions, majalis, asbaaq you listen to. Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala) is bestowing his greatest gift upon all of us. Don't play on your phones while receiving this gift; don't text; don't email; Don't do anything that would reduce the effacacy of this gift for you. Because this really is life changing knowledge. May Allah open up the doors of our hearts to take in this divine knowledge, and purify our hearts with it.

10 Foundational Principles of knowledge

So Knowledge begins with ADAB, and the ultimate purpose of all divine knowledge is the purification of the heart. There is an entire branch of philosophy called TASAWWFU, that deals with this science. And all of its teachings come directly from the sayings of Maulana Ali (AS) in Nehjul Balagha and other records. All SUFIS unanimously accept of Maulana Ali (AS) as their leader. Unfortunately much like ADAB, this topic is also probably the most forgotten, amongst all of the other teachings of Islam.

The idea here, is that before you study knowledge you have to purify your heart. Because you will never benefit from knowledge if your heart is impure. The heart is the most prized posessession of a believer. That's why Quran teaches us DUAS like:
⟪ar-quote:رَبَّنَا لَا تُزِغْ قُلُوبَنَا بَعْدَ إِذْ هَدَيْتَنَا⟫

Don't cause our hearts to deviate, after you've guided us to this pure knowledge. There are many words in the Quran that Allah uses to express his wrath for the disbelievers; and that wrath almost always manifests by affecting their hearts.
⟪ar:ختم اللہ علی قلوبھم⟫, Allah seals their hearts. HAJABA, QASWA..... ⟪ar:فَقَسَتْ قُلُوبُهُمْ⟫..... their hearts become hard.
⟪ar:كَلَّا..... بَلْۜ رَانَ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِهِم⟫

their hearts become encrusted.
⟪ar:فَلَمَّا زَاغُوٓا۟ أَزَاغَ ٱللَّهُ قُلُوبَهُمْ⟫

When they deviated, Allah caused their hearts to deviate. There are many many words..... ⟪ar:أَکِنَّ⟫, their hearts are locked; ⟪ar:أقفَلَ⟫,
⟪ar:أَفَلَا يَتَدَبَّرُونَ ٱلْقُرْآنَ أَمْ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبٍ أَقْفَالُهَآ⟫

Why don't they reflect on the Quran, are their hearts locked? These are all words to show that all destruction begins with the heart.

So for a believer his/her heart is the most important entity; and what purifies and cleanses the heart is knowledge. But the heart needs to be prepared for knowledge. It is like preparing the earth for planting. Before you actually puts seed into the earth, you have to cultivate the earth with a plow. And that is exactly what one needs to do with the heart. The heart has to plowed in order to remove the negative qualities, before it can be adorned with the positive qualities. 
⟪ar-quote:اول التَّواضَع التَّکَلُّف⟫

The beginning of humility is forcing oneself to be humble. And so the philosophy of TASAWWUF, is completely focused on this aspect of humility. Its root word SAWF means "wool". So TASAWWUF literally means to "wear wool". Some of the early people used to wear wool, as a type of declaration of their position in the world. Wool is not very comfortable; it is kind of rough. So the idea of the Sufisim, is to deal with this world by literally giving it up. This does not mean abandoning family and going into exile. What it means is to give up the fleeting pleasures and the vain desires of this world for a greater purpose. To exist in the world, free of all attachments. And if you study the SEERAH of Rasul Allah and Maulana Ali (AS), this is exactly what you will find. The willingly accepted, and submitted to the discomforts of this world for a greater cause. In other words, they gave up the beautiful silky and shiny garments of this physical world, and put on this garment of wool. And that is the idea of TASAWWFU. We'll talk a lot more about this when we study the topic of Ehsaan InshaAllah.

The reason I brought up this topic is because the science of TASAWWUF begins with what they call ⟪ar:مَبادِی العَشرۃ⟫ (the 10 fundamental principle of knowledge). They say, that before one embarks on this journey of knowledge, one must know 10 things about that knowledge. Do your homework, so as to speak!
⟪ar-quote:أِنَّ مبادئ العلوم العشرة؛ الحدُّ و المَوضُوعُ ثُمَّ ثَمرۃ⟫
⟪ar-quote:و فَضلُهُ ونِسبَةُ و الوَاضِعُ ؛ و الاسمُ و الِاستِمدَادُ و الشَّارِع و الحُکمُ⟫
⟪ar-quote:وَ مَسائِلُ و البَعضُ بِالبَعضُ اِكتفى ؛ و مَن دَرَى الجِميعُ حَازْ الشُّرَفَا⟫

There are 10 foundational principles of every knowledge

## الحد

The first one is called AL-HADD,  which in the English language we call a "definition". It is important to know the definition of what you are studying. A definition in logic, is something that gets to the essence of a thing. In other words when you define something, you have to define it "essentially". 

A proper definition should always be inclusive, as well as exclusive. It should include everything that that thing is; and it should exclude everything that the thing isn't. To give you an example of this, logicians say that a human being is a "rational animal". Human beings are animals, but they are rational animals. This is a definition that gets to the "essence" of what a human being is. To be human is to be specifically from the animal kingdom (and not from any other kingdom, such as the mineral or plant kingdom); but in addition to being an animal, it also has to be rational; It has to be sapiential. It must have intellect and the ability to reason; whereas other animals don't have that ability. So this definition excludes all the other kingdoms, and it also excludes the irrational animals that operate based on instinct. 

So the first thing you need to know is the definition of what you are about to learn.

## و المَوضُوعُ

Then you have the MAWDOOAH, which is the subject matter. What is this thing that you are learning about? 

## ثَمرَۃ

Then there is the SAMRAH, which is the fruit. What is the benefit of studying this thing? What is the end goal?

## فضلہ

Then you have the FADAL; the virtue of that thing. Why should that thing be learned? Because not all knowledge should we learned. There are certain knowledge that are superfluous. There are others that are a complete waste of time, like commercial jingles, or songs. You can learn this stuff but it is a waste of time. And this is why in one of our prayers that we recite after FARD we say:

اللھم انی اسئلک علم نافعاً، 

Oh Allah! I ask you for beneficial knowledge. I ask you for a knowledge that will benefit my heart; because there certainly is knowledge that can be detremental to the heart; that can destroy the heart. Most of us are filled with trivia! I personally would like those parts of my brain back that are occupied by that trivia. But unfortunately it is something that we are all stuck with. But what we are about to study is not. This is an incredibly fruitful knowledge! So we must know its virtue; and we must understand why it is worthy our time, our effort and our consideration. 

## نسبۃُ

And then knowing what it relates to. In other words, what is it associated with? What are the companion fields of knowledge that link in with this one.

## الواضِعُ

Who is the first one who made this science. Who is the source of this knowledge. And this is really important, because we don't want to spend time acquiring a certain knowledge when we know its coming from a dubious source such as Shaytan, right?

## الأسمُ

What are its name or names? Because every knowledge has a name or it can have several names.

## الاستِمدادُ

This is to know the basic principles based on which this knowledge is derived.

## الشارع  و الحکم

And to understand the legal ruling of that knowledge. Is it الفرض عین , is it فرض الکِفایۃ ;  is it obligatory, or is it strongly recommended? or is it optional?

## المسائِلُ

And then to know the intricate details of the subject. In other words the topics that make up the body of that knowledge.

Then he says:
⟪ar-quote:و مَن دَرَى الجِميعُ حَازْ الشُّرَفَا⟫

The one who understands these 10 fundamentals of knowledge, he attains the most honorable rank in that knowledge. So these are what are called the MABAEE ALASHARA

10 Fundamental Principles of TAWHEED

## Hadd

In terms of TAWHEED, it is defined as the science that investigates the nature (the MA-HIYA) of Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala). It deals with the nature of RUBUBIYYAH; it deals with the nature of ILAHYIYYAH; with the nature of ASMA WA SIFAAT. These are also termed "TAWHEED, TAJREED and TANZEEH", which we will talk about.

So TAWHEED is the study of first and foremost the nature of Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala), and then the study of how it manifests in the spiritual and physical ranks. So then it also deals with the nature of his prophets; it deals with the nature of WALAYAH (the guardianship) and the nature of سمعیات , which is eschatology (the part of theology that is concerned with death, day of judgment, and the final destiny of the soul).

## MAWDOOAH

The subject matter is the essence and the attributes of Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala). The attributes of prophets; the attributes of his relition which are the 7 pillars of Islam; the various articles of faith; the study of QADAR (destiny); and finally the the study of ADAL (divine justice).

## SAMRAH

The fruit, or the the benefit of this knowledge is that it prevents us, or protects us from making mistakes in our understanding of Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala). For example there are people that think that the universe is eternal. Quran is very clear that ALLAH is
⟪ar-quote:بَدِیعُ السموات و الارض⟫

who originated the heavens and the earth without any prior pattern. That is what BADEE means; an originator who brings something into the world without any prior pattern
⟪ar-quote:علی غیر مثال المُسبَق⟫

and then he is also called ⟪ar:فاطر السموات و الارض⟫, the one who brought forth the heavens and the earth. He is also ⟪ar:خالق السموات و الارض⟫ , he is the creator of the earth. He is ALBAREE, He is ALMUSAWWIR.

So if we just look at the ACT of creation, so many names and attributes emerge from this one act. He is BADEE because he did not use a prior pattern for his design; he is KHALIQ for physically creating the universe; he is FAATIR because the act of his creation was explosive and instantaneous in nature; He is BAREE because he gave a material aspect to all creation; and he is MUSAWWIR, because he fashioned, and gave everything a particular form and shape. So we have so many different names, just to understand his one single act of creation.

So you can see that this knowledge is very important to learn; because we have theory, and we have practice. When we understand the true essence of the theory, it changes the way we practice it. When we learn that God is just, and that it is inconceivable for God to be unjust; then all matters of QADA and QADR (destiny and will) become easier to accept. When we understand he is MALIK and we are MAMLOOK; when we understand he is RABB and we are MARBOOB (we are literall his possessions); it changes the way in which we look at trials and tribulations. 

Allah can give us a wonderful life or he could give us a miserable life. He can do whatever he wants. That is the God that is transcendent! That is the God that is frightening! That is the ZUL JALAAL! That is the God of tsunamis; that is the God of holocausts; that is the God of fire; that is the God of cancer; that is the God that takes little children away from their loving parents. These are all aspects of his transcendence! And as his slaves we are in submission to these aspect of God to the best of our abilities, because we understand, that one, we have no power and no ability to counter his will; and two, that he would not be unjust with us. So if we are in an undesirable state, there is divine wisdom behind it, which we need to learn and understand.

But then he is not just ZUL JALAAL. He is ZUL JALAALI WAL IKRAAM.  He is also the God of immense blessing;  he is the God of overflowing bounties; he is the God who gives the gift of life; who gives the gift of sustenance; He is the God who manifests in the unconditional love of a mother; the affections of a sister; the comforts of a true friend.  He is the god who eases the heart of the grieving mother who lost her child; he is the God who cures cancer when all doctors give up on the patient; he is the God who brings the dead earth back to life; he is the God who hides his bounties in his terrors; and He is the God who hides his blessings in his tribulations.

So learning all these things helps us deal with the vicissitudes of time. It helps us deal with the challenges and the calamities of our lives. And this is the fruit of this knowledge.

And then studying the nature of his prophets and his loved ones. Finding out for instance, that the prophets have the greatest trials of all. Because nobody who has ever lost a child, can feel anything but consolation, when they learn that the prophet (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) burried all of his sons. So the very best of creation had all of his children taken from him, and yet he never complained. Even though he did weep. His eyes teared up when he lost his children. We know that because its in the HADEES. But he never complained. And he said, Allah does not take you to account for your eyes. In other words, he does not take you to account for your grief; but he takes you to account for what you say in that state of grief. 

So that's one of the greatest benefits of learning this theory, and then beginning to exercise this theory in the laboratory of life. Because this knowledge is really about being out there in the world. Religion is basically an experience.  And this is why there is no knowledge without practice, and there's no practice without knowledge. We have to first gain this knowledge..... and then bring it to life, by putting it into practice, by worshipping our creator. This knowledge of our creed, is not simply a theoretical knowledge for us. It is a very practical knowledge.  As we get to know him, we have to stand up in prayer and mention his beautiful names! ALLAH, AlRehman AlRaheem, and as we call upon him, he comes closer to us..... and as he comes closer.....  our souls are able to take that imprint..... of his divine presence. 

So the fruit and the end goal of this knowledge is to understand the theory of divinity, so that we do not make errors and mistakes in our understanding of our creator. This knowledge of TAWHEED protects us. They say creed is like a chaperone! And even though lovers don't like chaperones because they always get in the way; but a chaperone is important to prevent anything illegitimate happening between lovers. So in our love affair with Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala), this knowledge of TAWHEED becomes our chaperone. It's there so we don't say or think anything that is incorrect, or inappropriate for our beloved.

## FADAL

The virtue of this knowledge is that it is the highest of all knowledge!
⟪ar-quote:التوحید أجل العلم⟫

TAWHEED is the most esteemed of all knowledge. Because it has to do with the creations relationship with their creator, through understanding the nature of God. And that is the greatest gift that we can receive, and therefore it has the highest virtue.

## NISBAH

Its NISBA, its association, is to all of the ⟪ar:علوم الشَّرَعِیَّۃ⟫,  all the sciences of Islamic law

## WAADI

The ⟪ar:وَاضِع⟫, the one who formulated this knowledge first and foremost is Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala), through the revelation of Quran. Because the Quran is a revelation that tells us about the nature of God. It tells us that: 
⟪ar-quote:فعلم انہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫

Know! that there is no God but Allah. Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala) tells us QUL HUWAL LLAHU AHAD. Know that god is unique. He tells us that he is the creator of the heavens and the earth.  He tells us that he is QAAHIRUN FAWQA IBADIHI (He possesses complete and overwhelming control of his slaves). He says he is ALHAYYE ALQAYYUM, that he is the ever living and the sustainer of the heavens and earth.

So much of the Quranic narrative is about who Allah is. But in essence it is all about Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala); because even when the Quran talks about the prophets and their tribulations, it is still about the call to Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala). So the ⟪ar:وَاضِع⟫, is knowing that it is formulated by Allah himself in the Quran.

But then it is also the prophet (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) that brough this knowledge to us. Allah gave us the Quran through the medium of the prophet. And then it is also to know, that this divine message was absorbed, internalized and then simplified by his WASEE (Ali Ibn Abu Talib), who then gave it to the Imams, who then gave it to the DUAAT, until it was simplified to the point where common people like us in the form of SHAREEAH, TAVEEL, MABDA MAAD and HAQAIQ, so that we could understand it.

## ISM

This knowledge is called USOOL AdDEEN. It is also called ILM TAWHEED, DAYAM AL-ISLAM, IMAAN, EHSAAN. These are different names for this science, but generally it is USOOL AdDEEN, the roots of religion. Because what this religion is built upon essentially, is TAWHEED. And this is the science of TAWHEED.

Be Overwhelmed with the love of Allah

What I hope to do in these sessions InshaAllah..... is share with you a deeper understanding of the fundamentals of our religion and our creed..... A creed is defined as a set of beliefs that guide someone's actions..... A creed is what you believe in..... The Arabic word for creed is AQEEDA..... which comes from the root word AQD..... meaning a knot..... So AQEEDA is what you are bound to.....  it is what you have conviction about..... This is why marriage is also called an عقد..... because two people are bound together on a journey.....

So in these sessions..... my aim is to share with you the underlying core principles of our AQEEDA..... first as Muslims..... and then as MOMINS..... We use a lot of different terms in the context of our creed - MUSLIM..... MOMIN..... ISLAM..... IMAN..... YAQEEN..... IKHLAAS..... TAWHEED; then we have the دعائم..... which are the 7 pillars of Islam..... which are WILAYAH..... TAHARAH..... SALAAT..... ZAKAAT..... SAUM..... HAJJ and JEHAD..... So we have all of these different terms that we use in the context of AQEEDA.....  but we don't necessarily have a clear understanding of these terms; and how they relate to one another under this one huge blanket of TAWHEED..... Because TAWHEED is the most essential principle of all..... It is the rope that binds everything in the heavens and the earth together..... And often times what happens is that we get so caught up in the particulars of the AQAID..... that we completely miss the beautific vision of Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala). We get so involved in the letter of the law..... that we completely miss the spirit of the law..... which is coming to know Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala). And that is TAWHEED. Seeing the ONE through all the multiplicity that surrounds us.

As far as the level of these sessions go..... I think it will not be appropriate for young children.....  because these are quite complex terms..... and it can get quite overwhelming for us, let alone young children. There is a saying.....
⟪ar-quote:الکافر حائرٌ فی المخلوق..... والمؤمن حائرٌ فی الخالق⟫

So this knowledge is in fact bewildering..... but then we have also been given the perfect soul to receive it. Allah prepared us for this knowledge. He created us with a FITRA, a disposition that is perfect. A FITRA that knows him directly. Allah gave us his beautiful names in the Quran; AlRehman AlRaheem AlMalik AlQUDDOOS, ASSALAAM, AlMOMIN, ALMUHAYMIN. And he gave us these names, so that we would call upon him, and think about him through these names.....  so that we may come to know him.

If getting to know him was not the end goal, then there really was no purpose of his self disclosure to us through his names.

But you have to understand that this knowledge of Allah is overwhelming..... It will make your head spin.....  But the truth is that when you start thinking about Allah, you SHOULD feel like you're drowning. If you don't, then you're really not getting it. Because there is nothing deeper in existence than this subject of TAWHEED. There is nothing in existence, that is more profound, more vast, and more deep, than this knowledge of Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala). There's nothing better than this knowledge to occupy our minds with. And it is as simple as that. So if you feel overwhelmed in these sessions, then I'm glad! AlHamdulillah..... It only means that you're getting it......  Because I feel overwhelmed every single time I study or think about this subject...... So yes, this knowledge is overwhelming, but don't see it as a negative thing. And don't feel like you can't learn this, because that's not true either. We were all created for this knowledge!

So I just wanted to set the stage before we begin..... because a lot of times people back off and stop learning simply because they feel overwhelmed by what they're learning. And so I wanted to swtich that paradigm around, and put that feeling into perspective. Feeling overwhelmed is GREAT!  MashaAllah everyone in this group is more than capable of understanding this knowledge.
