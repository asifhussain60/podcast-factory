## Prayer of the Fifteenth of Sha'ban

This prayer comprises fourteen *rak'ahs* (prayer cycles), which in their esoteric dimension signify the fourteen *ḥudūd* (limits or boundaries) that emanate from the command of the *daʿwa* (the summons, or invitation to the faith). In the higher spiritual sense, these fourteen cycles indicate the confluence of two successive epochs in the divine economy of time and revelation.[^cite-1]

### The Cosmic Rank of the Imam and His Limits

The station of the Imam determines the scope of his *ḥudūd* (limits). Should the Imam occupy the seventh degree of manifestation, his boundaries shall likewise be constituted by seven levels within their respective circles. If an Imam's tenure encompasses seven thousand years, his limits correspondingly ascend to that height. The scripture records concerning these fourteen *ḥudūd*, as expressed in the words of the Prophet (upon whom be peace): ⟪ar:نصف الشعبان⟫ Even were all the trees to become pens, the rivers to become ink, the angels to be scribes, and the reckoners to record, they could not encompass your virtues. ⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنین⟫ This utterance testifies to the transcendent dignity of these grand *ḥudūd*. ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫

The numeric structure of the *sūrahs* (chapters) recited in this prayer embodies a precise symbolism:

| Recitation | Number | Esoteric Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sūrat al-Fātiḥa | 14 | These fourteen *ḥudūd* glorify Allāh and acknowledge the *wilāya* (spiritual authority and guardianship) of Amīr al-Muʾminīn. |
| Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ | 14 | Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ is the chapter of *tawḥīd* (the doctrine of divine unity). Reciting it fourteen times signifies that these fourteen *ḥudūd* themselves constitute the monotheism of the Creator, drawing the attributes of divine unity from the Godhead and raising them to the *al-ʿAql al-Awwal* (the First Intellect). Through the support of the First Intellect, they attain the station of absolute unity and truth. |
| Sūrat al-Nās | 14 | This number indicates that all these *ḥudūd* remain under the protection of Mawlānā ʿAlī from the evil of the Iblis-era (namely, Abū Bakr). |
| Sūrat al-Falaq | 14 | This number signifies that all these *ḥudūd* remain under the protection of Mawlānā ʿAlī from the evil of the Shayṭān-era (namely, ʿUmar). |
| Āyat al-Kursī | 1 | The *Āyat al-Kursī* is recited a single time, symbolizing the unity of the station of the Almighty—that you have become Lord of the Age. All your *ḥudūd* acknowledge that none resembles you. |
| Total per *rak'ah* | 57 | Within one *rak'ah*, the total of *sūrahs* and the *Āyat al-Kursī* is 57. These fifty-seven counts correspond to the cycles of fifty-seven thousand years (fifty thousand years of descent and seventy thousand years of ascent) in which the veils of concealment appear. This prayer proclaims the remission of sins accumulated throughout these fifty-seven thousand years. Thus fourteen *rak'ahs* are performed, constituting seven *taslīms* (greetings of peace). They signify that within one age there shall come seven ages, and seven more shall arrive—a total of four hundred thousand years. In this single night, all the outward transgressions and inward corruptions committed across these four hundred thousand years receive forgiveness. |

## Night of the Fifteenth of Sha'ban

⟪ar:شعبان کریم⟫ The month of Sha'ban al-Karīm occupies the sixth position among the eighteen blessed days of the year; ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم⟫ in the same manner the Prophet (upon whom be peace) represents the sixth day, and this month signifies him likewise. ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین مولانا علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ The night itself further points to the spiritual station of Mawlānā ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib. What distinguishes the prayer of this night is that it is not designated by the formula "⟪ar:لیلۃ خامسۃ عشر من شہر شعبان⟫ the fifteenth night of Sha'ban," but rather "⟪ar:لیلۃ النصف من شہر شعبان⟫ the night of the midpoint of Sha'ban." This distinction arises because this night divides ⟪ar:شعبان کریم⟫ the month of Sha'ban into two equal halves. Moreover, it bisects the three sacred months—⟪ar:رجب، شعبان اور رمضان⟫ Rajab, Sha'ban, and Ramadan—into two equal portions. On this night, the first half concludes and the second half commences, the latter thereby surpassing the former.[^cite-2]

This night manifests two aspects corresponding to ⟪ar:محمد و علی⟫ Muhammad and ʿAlī. By divine command, Muhammad stands forth with ⟪ar:ظاہری دعوت (تنزیل)⟫ the exoteric *daʿwa* (the external summons), while ʿAlī arises from ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی (ﷺ)⟫ the inner dimension bearing ⟪ar:مولانا علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ the esoteric *daʿwa* (*taʾwīl*, esoteric exegesis). This night marks the moment when the Messenger's ⟪ar:باطنی دعوت (تاویل)⟫ external proclamation ⟪ar:تنزیلی دعوت⟫ reached its completion, and the keys of the inner teaching ⟪ar:مولانا علی⟫ were entrusted to ⟪ar:تاویلی دعوت⟫ ʿAlī to unfold its mysteries.[^cite-3]

This verse mentions ⟪ar:أمر حکیم⟫ *amr ḥakīm* (a command of wisdom). Out of the two *daʿwas* vouchsafed to the Prophet—the external and the inward—he was commanded to entrust half of the *daʿwa* to ʿAlī. The subtle wisdom herein lies in this: Allāh Almighty, had He willed, could have delegated half the summons directly to ʿAlī; yet He did not. Instead, through the mediation of the Messenger, did He transfer it, so that the magnificence and preeminence of the Messenger might be preserved. ⟪ar:شطر⟫ *Shaṭr* denotes one half of any thing. ⟪ar:شطرین⟫ Should one divide an apple into two portions, each is called a *shaṭr*. Of these two halves, the second is greater than the first. This is why all the ⟪ar:لیالی القدر⟫ *layālī al-qadr* (the nights of power and destiny) are positioned in the latter half. Thus ⟪ar:تاویلی دعوت، تنزیلی دعوت⟫ the inward summons stands higher than the outward, ascending ever higher.[^cite-4]

On the night of the fifteenth of Sha'ban, the Messenger of Allāh reached the threshold of a new station. Until that hour ⟪ar:مستودع⟫ he had been leader (*imām*) of the prophets and messengers alone; ⟪ar:مستقر⟫ thereafter he became leader also of the Imams. On that day you were exalted, and you were told that you had become ⟪ar:بیت الحرام (مستقر)⟫ the established direction of prayer (the *qibla* of the *Kaʿba*), ⟪ar:بیت المقدس (مستودع)⟫ while Jerusalem remained in trust.

### The Human Nature: Particular and Universal

Though he granted to ʿAlī the highest invitation, his own supremacy endured. For on ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ the night of the twenty-seventh of Rajab, it was the Prophet who received first the ⟪ar:ناسوت خاص⟫ *nāsūt khāṣṣ* (the particular human nature), and then the ⟪ar:ناسوت کلی⟫ *nāsūt kullī* (the universal human nature). Because of this, your supremacy was established over all things and all the prophets, who in their own ⟪ar:ھیکلوں⟫ *hayākal* (temples or bodies) were each confined to their station, remained entirely subject to you. Throughout his life, ⟪ar:ناسوت خاص⟫ ʿAlī did not attain the *nāsūt khāṣṣ* again; ⟪ar:ناسوت کلی⟫ it was the *nāsūt kullī* that came to him. These were bestowed upon ʿAlī at the ⟪ar:یوم غدیر⟫ Day of Ghadīr, when the Prophet (peace and blessings upon him) proclaimed: ⟪ar-quote:من کنت مولاہ فھذا علی مولاہ⟫ "Whomsoever I am master, ʿAlī is likewise master." ⟪ar:ناسوت خاص، حضرات⟫ That is, from this day forward, ʿAlī possesses the *nāsūt* and the *zaʿāma* (leadership and spiritual station) that I possessed! The boundaries of the *ḥudūd*. ⟪ar:ناسوت خاص، لاہوت⟫ The *nāsūt* is so exalted that it approaches the *Lāhūt* (the divine nature): ⟪ar-quote:اَلخاَص مَعضُوکُم بَالَّاہُوت⟫ *(the particular is wreathed in the divine).* It was the Prophet who received this throughout his lifetime; ʿAlī ⟪ar:یوم غدیر⟫ obtained it at Ghadīr. Therefore, the rank of the Prophet (peace be upon him) remained superior to that of ʿAlī throughout his earthly life. Had ⟪ar:علی علیہ السلام⟫ ʿAlī ⟪ar:ناسوت خاص⟫ attained the *nāsūt khāṣṣ* ⟪ar:حجاب علی⟫ during the Prophet's lifetime, ⟪ar:حجاب محمد (ﷺ)⟫ the *ḥijāb* (veil) of ʿAlī would have veiled the *ḥijāb* of Muḥammad, and none would have proclaimed the *Kalima*.

## The Month of Sacred Rajab

Allāh has divided the twelve months of the year into two halves. ⟪ar:نصف اول⟫ The first half comprises the months from ⟪ar:جمادی الآخر⟫ Jumādā al-Ākhira through ⟪ar:محرم الحرام⟫ Muharram al-Ḥarām. ⟪ar:نصف ثانی⟫ The second half begins with ⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ Rajab al-Ḥarām and extends through ⟪ar:ذو الحجۃ⟫ Dhū al-Ḥijja. Rajab marks the opening of the second half and bears a singular magnificence.

| First Half | Second Half |
| --- | --- |
| Muharram al-Ḥarām through Jumādā al-Ākhira | Rajab al-Ḥarām through Dhū al-Ḥijja |

⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ The *Kalima* *Lā ilāha illā al-Allāh* (There is no god but Allāh) contains twelve letters, as does the year itself contain twelve months. The supreme word among the four divisions of the *Kalima al-Ikhlāṣ* (the testimony of divine unity) is "Allāh," which comprises four letters. Similarly, among the twelve months, four stand supreme in honor—these correspond to the word "Allāh." These are the months ⟪ar:محرم الحرام، رجب الحرام، ذی قعدۃ الحرام اور ذی الحجۃ الحرام⟫ Muharram al-Ḥarām, Rajab al-Ḥarām, Dhū al-Qaʿda al-Ḥarām, and Dhū al-Ḥijja al-Ḥarām. A further distinction obtains: just as the word "Allāh" stands singular and distinct while the other three letters combine in unity, so too ⟪ar:ذی قعدۃ الحرام، ذی الحجۃ الحرام⟫ Dhū al-Qaʿda al-Ḥarām and Dhū al-Ḥijja al-Ḥarām, along with ⟪ar:محرم الحرام⟫ Muharram al-Ḥarām, are conjoined and consecutive, while ⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ Rajab al-Ḥarām stands alone. For this reason ⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ Rajab al-Ḥarām is also called ⟪ar:رجب الفرد⟫ *Rajab al-Farīd*—the Singular Rajab.[^cite-5]

| Rajab al-Ḥarām | Dhū al-Qaʿda al-Ḥarām | Dhū al-Ḥijja al-Ḥarām | Muharram al-Ḥarām |
| 7 | 11 | 12 | 1 |

Likewise, the first extra night of Rajab is among the four supreme nights of the year concerning which the Master of the Universe has said: ⟪ar-quote:یُعجِبُنِی اَن یَفرُغُ المَرَءُ اَربَعَ لَیلٍ فِی السَّنَۃ ۔ اَوَّلُ رَجَبٍ وَ نِصفُ شَّعباَنَ وَ لَیلَۃِ عِیدِ الفِطرِ وَ لَیلَۃِ عِیدِ الاَضحیٰ⟫ "It pleases Me that the human soul should be freed from worldly preoccupation upon these four nights of the year."

### The First Night of Sacred Rajab

⟪ar:رکب النوح النبی علیہ الصلاۃ والسلام السفینۃ اول یوم من رجب⟫ Upon the first day of Rajab, when Nūḥ (peace be upon him) prepared the Ark by divine command, Allāh bade him: "O Nūḥ, embark upon this blessed day, and take with you both humankind and the jinn." ⟪ar:فأمر من کان معہ من الجن والأنس یصومونہ⟫ Allāh commanded Nūḥ and all the jinn and humans who accompanied him to fast upon that day—the first day of Rajab—seeking the pleasure of Allāh.[^cite-6]

### The Singular Station

The glory of the letter *rāʾ* in *Rajab* is peculiar to this month, for it stands alone as a letter without diacritical dots, whereas *jīm* and *bāʾ* bear dots and are thereby mingled with others. The letter *rāʾ* signifies the month of *Rajab*, which is solitary among the sacred months of the year, while the letters ⟪ar:ذی القعدۃ اور ذی الحجۃ⟫ *jīm* and *bāʾ* represent Dhū al-Qaʿda and Dhū al-Ḥijja, which are conjoined.

| Letter | Character |
| --- | --- |
| Singular | Not Singular |
| Without Dots | With Dots |
| Rajab al-Ḥarām | Dhū al-Qaʿda and Dhū al-Ḥijja al-Ḥarām |

⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ Rajab al-Ḥarām contains thirty days, making it a full month; ⟪ar:صفر⟫ *Safar* (the month following Muharram), by contrast, consists of twenty-nine days:

| Rajab al-Ḥarām | Safar |
| --- | --- |
| 30 | 29 |
| Male | Female |

### The Name of Rajab

⟪ar:شھر الرجب⟫ The word *Rajab* contains seven letters (counting the *al* as a single letter, since *rāʾ* is a solar letter); it further bears five dots (in *shīn*, *jīm*, *bāʾ*), which together total twelve, corresponding to the twelve letters of the ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *Kalima Lā ilāha illā al-Allāh*. Beyond this numerical correspondence, the words of *tasbīḥ* (glorification) performed in this month—*Istighfir Allāh* or *Dhū al-Jalāl wa-l-Ikrām min Jamīʿ al-Dhunūb wa-l-Āthām* (O Lord of Glory and Honor, forgive us all our sins and transgressions)—are likewise twelve in number:

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

⟪ar:آثام⟫ *Āthām* are transgressions committed against oneself, while ⟪ar:ذنوب⟫ *dhunūb* are sins committed against the souls of others.

### The Symbolic Meaning of Rajab

⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ The month of Rajab al-Ḥarām signifies the station of the Imāmate. Rajab consists of thirty days, which point to ⟪ar:اولاد اسماعیل⟫ thirty Imāms from the line of Ishmael. The day corresponds to ⟪ar:اولاد اسحاق⟫ the descendants of Isaac, while the nights—⟪ar:اولاد اسماعیل⟫ the children of Ishmael—are more numerous than the days:

| 30 Days of Rajab | Days | Nights |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Children of Ishmael: 30 Imāms | Sons of Isaac | Descendants of Ishmael |

On the twenty-seventh of Rajab, the Messenger of Allāh (upon him be peace) was born, for he ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اﷲ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ appeared ⟪ar:اولاد اسماعیل⟫ after twenty-seven Imāms. The remaining three days signal three Imāms who would come after his birth—⟪ar:عبد المطلب، ابوطالب اور عبد اللہ⟫ ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib, Abū Ṭālib, and ʿAbdullāh. He ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ was born during the time of these three Imāms, the three ⟪ar:(ثلاثہ سفراء)⟫ *sufārāʾ* (ambassadors or witnesses). In another dimension, these three nights also symbolize the three faculties of the mind. The Messenger of Allāh (upon him be prayer and peace) ⟪ar:ناسوت کلی⟫ received the *nāsūt kullī* (universal human nature) upon this day.[^cite-7]

## The Nineteen Virtues of Sacred Rajab

| 1 | *Rajab* means to reverence and honor. Sayyidunā Hammām, may Allāh sanctify his secret, declares in his eulogy: ⟪ar-quote:رَجَب اَلخَیر اَتیٰ بِالبَرَکَۃ فَھُوَ شَھرٌ لأنتِظاَر اَلحَرَکَۃ⟫ "The month of Rajab is the month of goodness and blessing, and we await it so that we might be moved toward righteous action." |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | The Prophet (upon him be peace) designated this month as the month of ʿAlī. |
| 3 | This month contains thirty days, signifying its completeness and perfection. |
| 4 | It is the third month of devotion—⟪ar:شھر اللہ، شھر شعبان الکریم، رجب الحرام⟫ *Shahr Allāh* (the month of Allāh), *Sha'ban al-Karīm* (noble Sha'ban), and Rajab al-Ḥarām (sacred Rajab)—as three letters compose the word Rajab. |

| 7 | The first night of this month is one of the four most auspicious nights of the year: the First Night of Rajab, the Night of the Fifteenth of Sha'ban, the Night of ʿĪd al-Fiṭr, and the Night of ʿĪd al-Aḍḥā. |

| 14 | On the night of the twenty-seventh of this month, the Holy Prophet (upon him be peace) experienced the *Miʿrāj* (the Ascension). |
| 15 | On the twenty-seventh of this month, Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib was born. |
| 16 | On the twenty-seventh of this month, the Holy Prophet was appointed to prophethood. |
| 17 | Fasting on the twenty-seventh is a highly blessed fast, called *Muʿattā al-Shuʿūl*, wherein Allāh forgives the believer's sins of sixty years. |
| 18 | The special practice of this month is to fast on any Wednesday with the intention of increase in sustenance and provision. |
| 19 | Amīr al-Muʾminīn declared: "Whoever fasts for one year in this month, the Fire of Hell shall be removed for one year; if he fasts for two years, it shall be removed for two years. If in this manner he fasts for seven years, the Fire shall be removed for seven years, and in the eighth year the gates of Hell shall be closed to him forever. Should he fast for nine years, all his previous deeds shall be rendered clear and pure." |

## The First Friday of Sacred Rajab

Throughout the three hundred sixty-five days of the year, there are a total of fifty-two Fridays, yet the first ⟪ar:جمعۃ⟫ *Jumʿa* (Friday) ⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ among them all holds the highest station. ⟪ar:یوم الجمعۃ⟫ *Yawm al-Jumʿa* (the day of congregation) exemplifies ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ *Bāb al-Abwāb* (the Gate of Gates). Just as ⟪ar:یوم الجمعۃ⟫ Friday gathers the blessings of the six preceding days, so too ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ the Gate of Gates gathers all the believers, the *ḥudūd*, and the forms of supplication. The Imām embodies the entire significance of ⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ the month of Rajab.

The first Friday night of ⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ Rajab al-Ḥarām corresponds to ⟪ar:مولاتنا فاطمۃ بنت اسد⟫ our noble lady Fāṭima bint Asad. This is because she possessed ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, who embodies the sum of the highest *kamāl awwal* (primary perfection). For this reason ⟪ar:مولاتنا فاطمہ بنت اسد⟫ the rank of Fāṭima bint Asad rose to such transcendence as to be designated ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ the Gate of Gates. ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ Yours—through ⟪ar:مولانا ابو طالب⟫ Mawlānā Abū Ṭālib and ⟪ar:مولانا علی⟫ Mawlānā ʿAlī—became the ⟪ar:قلب ھیکل⟫ *qalb al-haykal* (the heart of the temple, i.e., the inner sanctuary).[^cite-8]

⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ It signifies the day of ⟪ar:مولانا سلمان فارسی⟫ Mawlānā Salmān al-Fārisī as the Friday, for you were ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ the Gate of Gates unto ʿAlī. Though there are four Fridays within ⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ the month of Rajab, none ⟪ar:رجب الحرام⟫ among them can be reckoned equal to the first Friday.
