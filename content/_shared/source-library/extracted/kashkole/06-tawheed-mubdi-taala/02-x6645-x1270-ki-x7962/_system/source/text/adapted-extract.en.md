## The Summons of Islam

When ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی (ﷺ)⟫ Muhammad, the Chosen One, was appointed as Messenger and the verse was revealed to him:

> ⟪quran 62:2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِى بَعَثَ فِى ٱلْأُمِّيِّۦنَ رَسُولًۭا مِّنْهُمْ يَتْلُوا۟ عَلَيْهِمْ آيَتِهِۦ وَيُزَكِّيهِمْ وَيُعَلِّمُهُمُ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْحِكْمَةَ وَإِن كَانُوا۟ مِن قَبْلُ لَفِى ضَلَلٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ ‎⟫
> *He it is Who sent among the unlettered ones a Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from among themselves, reciting to them His Verses, purifying them (from the filth of disbelief and polytheism), and teaching them the Book (this Quran, Islamic laws and Islamic jurisprudence) and Al-Hikmah (As-Sunnah: legal ways, orders, acts of worship, etc. of Prophet Muhammad PBUH). And verily, they had been before in manifest error;*
> It is He who has sent among the unlettered a Messenger from among themselves, to recite to them His verses, and to purify them, and to teach them the Book and wisdom, though before that they were in manifest error.
> — **Quran 62:2 (inline widget)**

he brought forth the doctrine of divine unity to the people and drew them out of the darkness of idolatry and polytheism. To those who worshipped false gods, the Prophet—upon him be peace and blessings—presented this verse, the most compelling argument of his age:

> ⟪quran 21:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏لَوْ كَانَ فِيهِمَآ آلِهَةٌ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ لَفَسَدَتَا ۚ فَسُبْحَنَ ٱللَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَرْشِ عَمَّا يَصِفُونَ ‎⟫
> *Had there been therein (in the heavens and the earth) gods besides Allah, then verily both would have been ruined. Glorified be Allah, the Lord of the Throne, (High is He) above what they attribute to Him!*
> if they had gods besides Allah, they would have been ruined. Glory be to Allah, the Lord of the Throne, above what they describe.
> — **Quran 21:22 (inline widget)**

For if multiple gods existed, the universe itself would collapse through their mutual discord.[^cite-1] Yet the Prophet endured manifold hardships inflicted upon him. As one who came as a mercy to all creation—⟪ar:رحمت للعالمین⟫—he did not curse those who persecuted him; instead he prayed for their guidance. Through the words of the Qur'an, he proclaimed the principle of divine oneness: ⟪ar:'لا الہ الا اللہ'⟫ (there is no god but Allah). His adversaries employed every means to silence this word.

Three methods of persuasion are known throughout the world:

First, his enemies employed *targhīb* (persuasion), offering him gold equal to Mount Abu Qubays, the fairest women, and the dominion of Arabia itself, provided he cease proclaiming this word alone. He replied: "Even if you placed the sun in one hand and the moon in the other, I would not abandon preaching this word." When persuasion failed, they resorted to *tarhīb* (intimidation), inflicting suffering upon him and his companions, calling him magician, poet, and madman, until words of pain came from his blessed tongue: ⟪ar-quote:لا اعذی نبی مثل ما أعذی⟫ ("No prophet has been tormented as I have been tormented").[^cite-2] Witnessing his steadfastness and gratitude despite all tribulation, his enemies then attempted *tarkīb* (compromise)—the path of religious syncretism. This is what Allah describes in the chapter of *Sūrat al-Kāfirūn* (⟪ar:سورۃ الکافروں⟫):

> ⟪quran 109:1-6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلْكَفِرُونَ ‎ ‏لَآ أَعْبُدُ مَا تَعْبُدُونَ ‎ ‏وَلَآ أَنتُمْ عَبِدُونَ مَآ أَعْبُدُ ‎ ‏وَلَآ أَنَا۠ عَابِدٌۭ مَّا عَبَدتُّمْ ‎ ‏وَلَآ أَنتُمْ عَبِدُونَ مَآ أَعْبُدُ ‎ ‏لَكُمْ دِينُكُمْ وَلِىَ دِينِ ‎⟫
> *Say (O Muhammad (Peace be upon him)to these Mushrikun and Kafirun): "O Al-Kafirun (disbelievers in Allah, in His Oneness, in His Angels, in His Books, in His Messengers, in the Day of Resurrection, and in Al-Qadar, etc.)! " I worship not that which you worship, "Nor will you worship that which I worship. " And I shall not worship that which you are worshipping. "Nor will you worship that which I worship. " To you be your religion, and to me my religion (Islamic Monotheism)." *
> Say, O you who disbelieve, I do not worship your gods, nor do you worship my gods, nor do I worship your gods, nor do you worship my gods. For you is your religion, and for me is my religion.
> — **Quran 109:1-6 (inline widget)**

They said: "O Muhammad! At the very least, let us both honor each other's gods. Do not curse our deities and we shall not curse yours. Or failing that, let us include your God ⟪ar:'اللہ '⟫ among our pantheon, and you change your proclamation ⟪ar:'لا الہ الا اللہ'⟫ to ⟪ar:'اللہُ اِلہُ'⟫ (Allah is one god among gods)." He answered: "This is impossible, for I have been sent to erase the concept of false gods entirely, like a word wiped away. Unless shirk (association of partners with Allah) is removed from Allah's oneness, His true unity cannot be established. Therefore I say first ⟪ar:'لا الہ'⟫ (there is no god)—so that ⟪ar:'الا اللہ'⟫ (except Allah) may ring true."

## The Excellence of the Word of Divine Unity—*Lā ilāha illā'llāh*

The word of sincerity—⟪ar:کلمہ اخلاص⟫—is the key to the door of Islam; through it alone may a person enter the house of Islam. **This word is called sincerity because sincerity means to purify.** Therefore, whoever recites this statement must use it to purify their faith from the corruption of idolatry, the stain of fire-worship, and the pollutions of dual worship. When a person recites this Kalimah after removing from themselves all the attributes of created things through the knowledge of *tawhīd* (divine unity), then their statement becomes true and acceptable.[^cite-3]

The disbelievers and polytheists sought to erase the very name of Islam and annihilate Muslims. At that time Allah Almighty commanded the Prophet to fight in defense of faith and the Messenger of Allah—exalted be He—was promised divine aid. ⟪ar:رسول اکرم (ﷺ)⟫ He said: ⟪ar-quote:أمرت قتال المشرکین حتی یقول لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ ("I have been commanded to fight the polytheists until they pronounce: *Lā ilāha illā'llāh*").[^cite-4] Yet as soon as they uttered this word once, their blood and wealth became protected thereby, and their reckoning fell to Allah alone. In obedience to the divine command, the Messenger shed the blood of disbelievers and took their wealth as booty, enslaving their women and children—all to compel utterance of the word. This severity flowed from his perfect knowledge of the word's virtue and greatness, even as the people remained ignorant of its true worth. The moment they pronounced it, he stayed his sword, for in that utterance they entered Islam and were spared.

Though the Prophet compelled people to recite the *shahāda* (testimony of faith) through military conquest, he never forced anyone to enter into genuine belief. To this Allah testifies: ⟪ar-quote:لا اکرہ فی الدین⟫ ("There is no compulsion in religion").[^cite-5] Yet his severity in spreading outward Islam was in fact a supreme mercy to humankind. For whoever enters the realm of Islamic practice also enters the domain of authentic faith. Consider the analogy: parents force their child to drink bitter medicine, knowing full well he will find it loathsome. Yet they know the medicine heals him; the child's health matters more than his momentary distaste.

Maulana Ali—⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫—said: ⟪ar-quote:عَلِّمُنِی رَسُول اَللّٰہِ اَلفَ باَب مِنَ الحِکمَۃ وَ اِن فَتَحَلِی مِن کُلِّ باَبٍ اَلفَ باَب⟫ ("The Messenger of Allah opened for me a thousand doors of wisdom, and from each door a thousand more doors opened before me").[^cite-6] If one truly comprehends this, they would be bewildered in their faith like a stone dropped into a deep well. Indeed, he declared that whoever acquires knowledge of this Kalimah has truly recited it, while whoever lacks such knowledge has not truly recited it at all. Thus whoever knows ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا ﷲ⟫ and derives its meaning from the truthful ones bears witness with pure sincerity; whoever lacks this knowledge bears false and rejected testimony.

The Prophet declared: "If this word were placed on one scale and the heavens and the earth and all that lies between them on the other, ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اﷲ⟫ the scale would still tip in its favor." This seems impossible at first glance, yet when those *al-rāsikhūn fī'l-ʿilm* (firmly grounded in knowledge)—⟪ar:الرَّاسِخُونَ فی العلم⟫—contemplate its meaning, the truth becomes manifest. This small word, composed of four words and three letters, resembles the human sperm—minute in its initial form, yet so potent that it has no earthly weight. Yet this tiny drop dominates all the organs and senses of the body; ⟪ar:بالقوۃ⟫ it possesses power—⟪ar:عالم عقل و نفس⟫—in the realm of intellect and soul, affecting all scholars in their thoughts and imaginings. Just as the focal point of a circle dominates its entire surface, or as the entire tree exists potentially within the seed, so the word of *tawhīd*, despite its brevity, encompasses the realities of all worlds. Nothing in existence lacks the radiance of this word. ⟪ar:عالم روحانی، عالم جرمانی اور عالم جسمانی،⟫ The spiritual world, the material world, and the physical world—all ⟪ar:الہ⟫ testify that there is none but Allah. This word, short though it is, supersedes all ⟪ar:معارف روحانی اور جسمانی⟫ spiritual and physical knowledge that a believer may attain, enabling him to rise from the material realm to the spiritual.

Maulana Ali (peace be upon him) teaches that ⟪ar:صاحب ابداع (عقل اول)⟫ the First Intellect, the Source of Emanation, when proclaiming divine unity, uttered first of all ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ this word. He declared that the heaviest key to the gates of Paradise is ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ this very Kalimah—so weighty that the heavens and earth are ashamed to bear it, yet they bear it nonetheless with reverence. He also said: ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ "It is a fruit that never spoils nor decays; whoever consumes it tastes fresh sweetness each time."

Allah says in the Qur'an, interpreting His own word:

> ⟪quran 11:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ فِى سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍۢ وَكَانَ عَرْشُهُۥ عَلَى ٱلْمَآءِ لِيَبْلُوَكُمْ أَيُّكُمْ أَحْسَنُ عَمَلًۭا ۗ وَلَىِٕن قُلْتَ إِنَّكُم مَّبْعُوثُونَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ ٱلْمَوْتِ لَيَقُولَنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرَوٓا۟ إِنْ هَذَآ إِلَّا سِحْرٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And He it is Who has created the heavens and the earth in six Days and His Throne was on the water, that He might try you, which of you is the best in deeds. But if you were to say to them: "You shall indeed be raised up after death," those who disbelieve would be sure to say, "This is nothing but obvious magic." *
> It is He who created the heavens and the earth in six days, and His Throne was upon the waters, that He might test you, which of you is best in conduct. And if you say, You will be raised up after death, the disbelievers will say, This is nothing but plain magic.
> — **Quran 11:7 (inline widget)**

⟪ar:عیسی علیہ السلام⟫ Jesus, upon whom be peace, asked Allah: "O Allah, how did You sustain the Throne upon the water when nothing can rest upon water?" At that moment Allah inscribed ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ this word upon the water, and the water became solid, bearing the Throne with ease.

## With Each New Call the Assembly Scatters

Upon receiving prophethood, in his first sermon, the Prophet said: "If I tell you that a cavalry force is coming from beyond that mountain—⟪ar:ابو قبیس⟫—intending to attack you, would you believe me?" ⟪ar:یا محمد عربی (ﷺ)،⟫ In this way he grounded his message in his proven truthfulness, and when they affirmed his reliability, he said: "Since you are prepared to trust my word about unseen armies, I invite you to believe in an unseen God!" He called them to ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ the word. The moment he spoke it, the whole assembly scattered despite their faith in his truthfulness. Those who had been friends became enemies, and few believed the Messenger of Allah.

⟪ar:حضور (ﷺ)⟫ After twenty-three years of labor, when a great multitude had embraced Islam, he asked them at ⟪ar:یوم غدیر⟫ Ghadīr: ⟪ar-quote:الست اولابکم من انفسکم⟫ ("Am I not more worthy of you than your own selves?"), obtaining their affirmation that he held supremacy over their very souls. Then he addressed them about faith, proclaiming: ⟪ar-quote:مَن کُنتُ مَولا فَھٰذاَ عَلِیٌّ مَولاَہُ⟫ ("Whomever I am master of, Ali is his master").[^cite-7] At these words, the great congregation of Islam scattered once more as before, and scarcely any remained steadfast in faith toward Ali.

## "Whoever Says It with Sincerity Enters Paradise"

⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ The Messenger of Allah said: "I have been commanded to wage ⟪ar:قتال⟫ war against the polytheists until they ⟪ar:الہ⟫ recite the word of Allah, *Lā ilāha illā'llāh*." Yet he also declared: "My only duty is to convey the word of Allah to you," as Allah states:

> ⟪quran 36:17⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا عَلَيْنَآ إِلَّا ٱلْبَلَغُ ٱلْمُبِينُ ‎⟫
> *"And our duty is only to convey plainly (the Message)." *
> duty for Us is to convey the message clearly.
> — **Quran 36:17 (inline widget)**

"My duty is proclamation; the elucidation and interpretation of what I say belongs to Ali—⟪ar:مُبَیَّن⟫ my elucidator—who is my brother, my vizier, and my successor. Listen to me and learn understanding from Ali!" The few who learned and comprehended from Ali included ⟪ar:سلمان فارسی⟫ Salmān al-Fārisī, ⟪ar:ابو ذر غفاری⟫ Abū Dharr al-Ghifārī, ⟪ar:عمار یاسر⟫ ʿAmmār ibn Yāsir, ⟪ar:مقداد بن اسود⟫ al-Miqdād ibn al-Aswad, and ⟪ar:خزیفہ یمانی⟫ Khuzayma al-Yamānī. These were the mountains of knowledge beneath ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫ the Commander of the Faithful, under whom they were trained.

**Clarification:**
Our Imams, who were connected to Ali, became our Imams; our devotions became linked to the Imams; our destinies became bound to those devotions; and we became bound to those destinies. Thus that knowledge became our inheritance. In this way, we are the true partisans of Ali—otherwise the public history of Shīʿism traces itself back to the Sunnis.

He addressed the people, saying: ⟪ar-quote:مَن قاَلَ لاَ اِلہَ اِلاَّ اللہ دَخَلَ اَلجَنَّۃ⟫ ("Whoever says *Lā ilāha illā'llāh* enters Paradise"). Then ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ the Commander of the Faithful, Ali ibn Abī Ṭālib, turned and clarified: ⟪ar-quote:مَن قَالَ لاَ اِلہَ اِلاَّ اللہ مُخلِصاً دَخَلَ اَلجَنَّۃ وَاِخلاَصُہُ مَعرِفَتِ حُدُودِھَا وَ اَداَءِ حُقُوقِھَا⟫ ("Whoever says *Lā ilāha illā'llāh* with sincere intention enters Paradise, and his sincerity is knowledge of its bounds and fulfillment of its rights").[^cite-8] 

That is: whoever utters ⟪ar:الہ⟫ the word of Allah, ⟪ar:لا⟫ *Lā ilāha* (there is no god), enters the paradise of Islam. But he who says ⟪ar:الہ⟫ with true sincerity, *Lā ilāha illā'llāh*, enters the paradise of faith. The superficial meaning of sincerity is to recite this word with love, devotion, certainty, and heartfelt joy. In reality, however, what the Prophet intended through Ali and his descendants is called *ikhlas* (sincerity)—⟪ar:'اخلاص'⟫. The true purpose was to recognize the *hudūd* (limits/boundaries) of Ali and to fulfill their due rights. Genuine sincerity arises only when the Prophet's utterance is joined to Ali's meaning. Common Muslims took only the Prophet's words and returned to their tribes, spreading mere external pronouncements, which led them astray.

The bounds of Paradise equal the bounds of heavens and earth:

> ⟪quran 3:133⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ وَسَارِعُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ مَغْفِرَةٍۢ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَجَنَّةٍ عَرْضُهَا ٱلسَّمَوَتُ وَٱلْأَرْضُ أُعِدَّتْ لِلْمُتَّقِينَ ‎⟫
> *And march forth in the way (which leads to) forgiveness from your Lord, and for Paradise as wide as are the heavens and the earth, prepared for Al-Muttaqun (the pious - see V.2:2).*
> and hasten to forgiveness from your Lord, and to Paradise as wide as the heavens and the earth, prepared for the God-fearing.
> — **Quran 3:133 (inline widget)**

The "heavens and earth" of faith are the Prophets and their heirs, or the Imams and their *hujja* (proofs), or Muḥammad and Ali. The more one attains knowledge of them, the more one finds Paradise. In short: to enter Paradise, one must know Allah through His mercy—through His limits.

**Origin and Purpose:**
Just as Adam was expelled from Paradise, so too were we cast out from the Paradise of *kamāl awwal* (primary perfection) in the spiritual world. To re-enter that Paradise of First Perfection, we must know the *hudūd* (limits) of Allah, so that He may make us know ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ the word.

### What Does Sincerity Mean?

Regarding *ikhlas* (sincerity), ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین مولانا علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ the Commander of the Faithful, Maulana Ali ibn Abī Ṭālib, said:

*Al-Ikhlas* is the fruit of faith, and sincerity is the fruit of worship, and sincerity is virtue itself. No deed can be virtuous without knowledge, and the highest knowledge is the knowledge of *tawhīd* (divine unity). *Ikhlas* is that which is purified. And sincerity is the fruit of faith and the fruit of worship—that is, the more our faith and worship increase, the more our sincerity increases. No action can be perfected without knowledge, and the supreme knowledge is knowledge of monotheism.

**Interpretation:**
The word *ikhlas* derives from ⟪ar:خلص⟫ *khalasa* ("to purify"), which in ⟪ar:علم العدد⟫ numerology carries the value 720. The soul of humanity departs the grave and ascends through twelve celestial spheres, being cleansed for sixty years in each sphere. Over the total of 720 years they are purified, after which they return to the world having attained their caliphate.

By pronouncing this word, one enters the paradise of *islām* (submission), which is ⟪ar:'اسلام '⟫ the paradise of the external word ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫. By pronouncing it with sincerity, one enters the paradise of *īmān* (faith), which is ⟪ar:'ایمان'⟫ the paradise of the internal word ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫. The value of ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫ this form is 110—equal to the numerical value of Ali's name. ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ In this hadith the Prophet described the stages of spiritual progress: first one enters Islam, then through knowledge of the *hudūd* and fulfillment of their rights, one enters the paradise of true faith.

| Islamic Word | The Word of Faith |
| --- | --- |
| There is no god but Allah | *Lā ilāha illā hu* |
| Muhammad | Ali |

*(Interpretation according to Mullā Ṣādullāh)*

A crucial point in this hadith: the Prophet used the past tense, not the future. He said ⟪ar:دَخَلَ الجنۃ⟫ "has entered Paradise," not ⟪ar:یَد خُلَ الجنۃ⟫ "will enter." To understand this, consider an analogy: ⟪ar:رسول اللہ⟫ the Messenger of Allah explains it thus: A child whose ⟪ar:صورت⟫ form is perfected in the mother's womb—whose ⟪ar:اعضاء رئیسۃ⟫ vital organs and ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫ five senses are complete—faces nothing preventing him from using them except that he must exit the womb and enter the atmosphere of this world. Provided there is no impediment and his existence and life continue unharmed. Similarly in ⟪ar:عالم جسمانی⟫ the physical world, those who recite the Kalimah sincerely—those who know the *hudūd* of the spiritual and physical and who fulfill their rights—have completed their spiritual ⟪ar:صورت⟫ form. To benefit from it, we need only await death, which is truly a second birth. As the Prophet ⟪ar:علیہ السلام⟫ said: ⟪ar-quote:اَلدُّنِیَا رَحمُ الآخِرَۃ⟫ ("This world is the womb of the Hereafter").[^cite-9]

## The Importance of Understanding the Word of Testimony

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, upon whom be peace, taught that faith consists of three things:

| Affirmation of the Heart | Utterance of the Tongue | Action of the Body |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Heartfelt confirmation | Speech from the tongue | Bodily conduct |

When asked, "Is faith simply affirmation of heart and utterance of tongue?" he replied: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان عمَلُ کلُّہُ⟫ ("Faith is entirely action").[^cite-10] Speech too is an action performed by the tongue. Similarly, belief and seeking forgiveness are actions of the heart, and performing deeds with all the body's members according to God's command and the Messenger's instruction is bodily action. Thus **faith is the sum total of all actions**.

| Heart | Tongue | Body |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The action of belief and knowledge | The action of utterance | Actions of all organs |

Then he expounded: "There are three grades of faith: complete, perfect, and defective." When the bodily organs fulfill their prescribed duties, faith becomes **complete**. When they fulfill those duties excellently, with knowledge and understanding, faith becomes **perfect**. When they fail to fulfill them well, faith becomes **defective**. Complete faith admits one to Paradise; perfect faith elevates one's rank within Paradise; and defective faith deprives one of Paradise, though one may enjoy the outward blessings of Islam by bearing the title "Muslim."

| Complete Faith | Perfect Faith | Defective Faith |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Entry into Heaven | Elevation in Heaven's Ranks | Deprivation of Paradise |

Therefore, whoever recites the Kalimah must know its truth and bear witness to it. Without such knowledge, the testimony is null, void, and inadmissible. Just as in a court of law: if a witness does not understand what he testifies to or for whom, the judge will reject his testimony, even regarding the smallest matter. How much more so should we bear witness to the testimony upon which our faith depends—whose magnitude transcends all things—understanding its outward and inward conditions and obligations, so that our witness is accepted, complete, and not rejected. This word of testimony encompasses all acts of worship to Allah, being comprehensive of them all. This is why the Holy Prophet made it the essence of his Sharīʿa (law).

In explaining the sincerity of the Kalimah: beyond mere belief that there is no god but Allah, one must know those servants of Allah who convey His knowledge and *hudūd* (limits)—those whom He has designated as His representatives. These *hudūd* are both upper and lower, spiritual and physical. Genuine faith cannot be without knowledge of these *hudūd* and fulfillment of their rights. Fulfillment of their right means neither diminishing nor elevating them beyond their appointed station. The letters and words of *Ikhlas* have been interpreted as these *hudūd*, which form the essential purpose of the word and to which the letters and words direct us.

## The Foundation of the Calls of the Messengers of Mighty Resolve

Islam and faith have always been one. All ⟪ar:صاحبان شریعت اولو العظم⟫ the Messengers of mighty resolve who preceded had outwardly established one foundation for their Sharīʿa. Yet when we examine its inner reality, we find it is a symbol that dominates all the *hudūd* (limits) of that Sharīʿa and encompasses all its branches. All the ⟪ar:اولوالعظم انبیاء⟫ Messengers of mighty resolve proclaimed ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ the word in different forms.

### Noah (Peace Be Upon Him)

⟪ar:نوح علیہ السلام⟫ Noah made the ark the essence of his law. The Prophet said: ⟪ar-quote:مِثلُ اَھلِ البَیتِی کَمِثلِ سَفِینَۃُ النُّوحِ مَن رَکَبَھاَ نَجَحَ وَ مَن تَخَلَّفَ عَنھَا غَرَقَ وَھَواَ⟫ ("My Ahl al-Bayt are like Noah's Ark: whoever boards it is saved; whoever turns away is drowned and perished").[^cite-11] Thus Noah made the Ark his original principle, inviting believers to embark upon it. Those who refused to board and remained apart from him drowned and perished.

### Abraham (Peace Be Upon Him)

⟪ar:ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ Abraham made ⟪ar:کعبۃ اللہ⟫ the House of Allah the foundation of his law. He built its walls, placed within it the ⟪ar:حجر اسود⟫ Black Stone, and called people to circumambulate it. *Tawāf* (circumambulation) means striving to attain something. The ⟪ar:کعبہ اللہ⟫ Kaʿba refers to the personality of the Prophet to come. ⟪ar:ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ Abraham proclaimed: "Circumambulate the Kaʿba—that is, strive to recognize the personality of the coming Prophet."

### Moses (Peace Be Upon Him)

⟪ar:موسی علیہ السلام⟫ Moses made the rod (*ʿasā*) the essence of his law. When he cast it before those who denied him—Pharaoh and his court—it moved, advancing and retreating, swallowing up their falsehood like a dragon.

### Jesus (Peace Be Upon Him)

⟪ar:عیسی علیہ السلام⟫ Jesus made the cross the essence of his law, declaring to those who believed in him: "Whoever wishes to follow me must always carry a cross."

The Trinity—that doctrine of three aspects—arose from argument between Jesus, his father Adam, and others including the Holy Spirit (Gabriel). Yet the Trinity is itself a manifestation of ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ the word, pointing to three things, two connections and one connection. The cross itself is in truth ⟪ar:ا ل ہ⟫ (the divine), which is the source of the word *Lā ilāha illā'llāh*.

### Muhammad, the Chosen One (Peace and Blessings of Allah Be Upon Him)

⟪ar:محمد مصطفیٰ (صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ و سلم):

محمد مصطفی (ﷺ)⟫ Muhammad made the word ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ the essence of his Sharīʿa, proclaiming that word in ⟪ar:تنزیل⟫ its literal, descending sense. He labored so intensely in spreading this word that Allah Almighty joined his utterance to His own utterance, binding ⟪ar:مُحَمَّدٌ رَّسُول اﷲ⟫ the word of the Prophet to ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ the word. Similarly, Ali labored so greatly in proclaiming this word in its ⟪ar:تاویل⟫ esoteric interpretation that, at the Prophet's behest, Allah united Ali's word with the Prophet's word: ⟪ar:محمد رسول اﷲ⟫ with ⟪ar:عَلِیًّ وَلِیُّ ﷲ⟫ ("Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah; Ali is the Guardian of Allah").[^cite-12]

### The Resurrector (Upon Him Be Peace)

⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ When the Resurrector appears, he shall breathe the spirit of inner meaning into ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ the word, thus reviving the child of Sharīʿa—perfected in the time of the Messenger—and advancing it through its ranks. ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ The Resurrector will not introduce any new Sharīʿa, but shall rely upon the inner knowledge of all previous Sharīʿas, rewarding and punishing the deeds of all former generations.

**Summary:**
All the principles brought by ⟪ar:اولو العظم صاحبان، شریعت⟫ the Messengers of mighty resolve appear outwardly different, yet intrinsically these various signs—the Ark, the Kaʿba, the rod, the cross, and the word—point to one source and summon to one origin, which is ⟪ar:عین اولۃ⟫ the very beginning itself and ⟪ar:عین آخرۃ⟫ the very end itself in its totality.

## The Word of Divine Unity and *Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm*

Just as man stands upon two legs, maintaining his balance through them, so too has Allah established the balance of religion and all universes upon two words. The first is the Kalimah ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ *Lā ilāha illā'llāh*—corresponding to the right foot—and the second is ⟪ar:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫ *Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm* (In the name of Allah, the Merciful, the Compassionate)—corresponding to the left foot. 

⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ The word *Lā ilāha* exists in ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ the spiritual world, given to the intellects, while ⟪ar:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم، ⟪ar:عالم جسمانی⟫ *Bismillāh*—⟫—was given to humanity in ⟪ar:عالم جسمانی⟫ the physical world through the descent (*aḥbaṭu*)—⟪ar:اھبطو⟫. ⟪ar:بسم اللہ⟫ *Bismillāh* in the physical world ⟪ar:عالم جسمانی⟫ guides us ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ toward ⟪ar:(لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ the spiritual world and the framework of the word.

**Interpretation:**
⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ In the spiritual world, the word ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ of Allah contains no points (no diacritical marks), whereas ⟪ar:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫ *Bismillāh* acquired points through the divine descent (*al-hubūṭ*)—⟪ar:ھبوط⟫. When these points are removed from *Bismillāh*, it develops into ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ *Lā ilāha* of Allah. ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ The word of divine unity supports ⟪ar:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫ *Bismillāh*.

⟪ar:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫ *Bismillāh* conceals within itself the light of the five purified ones (*Panj Tan*) of Allah the Most High, and it is through their knowledge and sincerity that we shall be guided to the Paradise of ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ the word. In other words, ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ *Lā ilāha* is a Paradise with eight gates, and each gate has a key—that key being the word ⟪ar:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫ *Bismillāh*. The *hudūd* (limits) of this word are made by the name of Allah, and they are ⟪ar:محمد، علی، فاطمۃ، حسن، حسین⟫ Muhammad, Ali, Fāṭima, al-Ḥasan, and al-Ḥusayn. They serve as intermediaries between Creator and creation, through whom we move toward Allah and attain Him. This is why we must utter ⟪ar:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫ *Bismillāh* before beginning all things.

## The Signs of Allah in the Word of Divine Unity

Allah says in the Holy Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 41:53⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَنُرِيهِمْ آيَتِنَا فِى ٱلأٓفَاقِ وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ يَتَبَيَّنَ لَهُمْ أَنَّهُ ٱلْحَقُّ ۗ أَوَلَمْ يَكْفِ بِرَبِّكَ أَنَّهُۥ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ شَهِيدٌ ‎⟫
> *&nbsp;We will show them Our Signs in the universe, and in their ownselves, until it becomes manifest to them that this (the Quran) is the truth. Is it not sufficient in regard to your Lord that He is a Witness over all things?*
> We will show them Our signs in the world and in their own souls until it becomes clear to them that it is the truth. Is it not sufficient that their Lord is a witness over all things?
> — **Quran 41:53 (inline widget)**

We shall display Our signs on the horizons and within their own souls until the truth becomes clear to them. There exist but two things in creation: the soul (*nafs*) and the horizon (*āfaq*). Whatever is not the self belongs to the horizon. If a person stands in the marketplace, his own being is his nafs and all that surrounds him comprises his āfaq.

That is, Allah declares: "We shall show you Our signs in all things, whether within ⟪ar:نفس⟫ you or beyond ⟪ar:افق⟫ you in the horizons—⟪ar:افلاک⟫ in the spheres—⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ of the spiritual realm, ⟪ar:نفوس نامیۃ، حسیۃ، ناطقۃ، جسم⟫ in vegetative, sensible, rational souls and bodies, among the ⟪ar:امہات⟫ mothers and ⟪ar:موالید⟫ offspring—⟪ar:عالم طبعیت⟫ of the natural world, ⟪ar:سقراء⟫ in the heavens. We shall show you the existence and manifestation of Our word in all things, so that We may demonstrate to you that the truth is established through ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ our word."

**Summary:**
In this verse, Allah is in reality (the Resurrector and in a sense Ali), and His signs are the Imams through whom the truth of the Resurrector is made manifest. The self is near; the horizon is distant. The verses of nearness are those pertaining to ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ Ali ibn Abī Ṭālib, and the verses of distance pertain to ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ the Resurrector who is yet to come. In another sense, there are signs of ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ nearness of the Resurrector—which are truths—and signs of distance which shall manifest at the end.

If one understands the self, one understands the universe; if one understands the universe, one understands the self. For what exists in the part shall exist in the whole, and what exists in the whole shall exist in the part. If a principle is established for the Prophet, that same principle obtains for the believer, and the principle established for the believer is the principle of the Prophet. This is called the *mīzān* (balance). Allah declares in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 17:44⟫
> ⟪ar:‏تُسَبِّحُ لَهُ ٱلسَّمَوَتُ ٱلسَّبْعُ وَٱلْأَرْضُ وَمَن فِيهِنَّ ۚ وَإِن مِّن شَىْءٍ إِلَّا يُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِهِۦ وَلَكِن لَّا تَفْقَهُونَ تَسْبِيحَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّهُۥ كَانَ حَلِيمًا غَفُورًۭا ‎⟫
> *The seven heavens and the earth and all that is therein, glorify Him and there is not a thing but glorifies His Praise. But you understand not their glorification. Truly, He is Ever Forbearing, Oft-Forgiving.*
> seven heavens and the earth glorify Him and all that is between them. There is not a thing that does not glorify Him with His praise, but you do not understand their glorification. Indeed, He is all-forbearing, all-forgiving.
> — **Quran 17:44 (inline widget)**

To comprehend the glorification of the seven heavens, the seven earths, and all between them is to understand the signs of the *hudūd* (limits) to which they all point. Everything bears the mark and symbol of the word of *tawhīd*, serving as proof of the *hudūd* that have become the means between Allah and His creation. Whatever Allah has created in the heavens from spirituality, whatever He has fashioned on earth from materiality, and whatever He has fashioned from the interplay between them—all shimmer with the light of the word of divine unity. That is to say: whatever exists in the heavens and earth, in the stars and their movements, in astrology and its influences, in souls and their compositions, in intelligibles and their subjects, in the Sharīʿa and its commandments, in prayer and its prescribed elements, in duties, in the Sunna, and in all devotional practices—all stand in testimony to the word of martyrdom (*shahāda*). The word of martyrdom is the seed of all witness. Everything in this universe proclaims the word of martyrdom—the cosmos, humankind, the body, time, heavens and earth, the Qur'an, Islam—all acknowledge the oneness of this essence through the word of sincerity. The Qur'an states this thus:

> ⟪quran 44:39⟫

The essence of the call to prayer is the proclamation of the word of martyrdom. Our entire existence unfolds within the realm of this word. It echoes in our ears at birth, and the same word is recited at the hour of death. The signs of the word of divine unity ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ are in truth its five chapters, concealed within every particle of the universe.
