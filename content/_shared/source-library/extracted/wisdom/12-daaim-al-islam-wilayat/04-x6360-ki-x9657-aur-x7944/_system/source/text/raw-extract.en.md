# Imam's Wajib and Virtues
*Source: KASHKOLE, Binder 19 (Pillars of Islam: Wilayat), Chapter 64. 14 topics. Language: ur.*<!-- section 1 (id=1041, raw_sort=1): رسالت خاص اور رسالت عام -->There are two types of Prophethood: one is a special message and the other is a general message.

### General Message: (Inner Wisdom)

Prophethood is general physical and rational. When ⟪ar:صورۃ انسانیۃ⟫Allah created it, He created in it all the organs that are necessary for its fulfillment. Through these organs, the human soul fulfills all its physical needs and performs all the functions of animal life. Then He created in man, unlike animals, another force and essence which is called intellect. It is with this intellect that he distinguishes between good and bad, truth and falsehood. With this intellect, he can understand things ⟪ar:أمرو نواھی⟫ and obey the orders of his parents, teachers and mentors. This intellect is his first message.

### Special Mission: (External Intellect)

The Messenger of Allah (s.a.w.a.) is the true guide who has been given the knowledge of the unseen by Allah and who guide the way to the Hereafter for the salvation of the soul. They are the prophets of their time, the successors and the Imams of all times. Prophethood in general is necessary to develop a relationship with the Prophethood in general. A person who does not have the general Prophethood (intellect) is not capable of accepting the special Prophethood, which is ⟪ar:شریعت محمدی علیہ السلام⟫why it does not apply to insane and minor children.

### Connection of the First Prophethood with the Second Prophethood:

With the natural and eternal intellect, man understands his worldly gains and losses and knows how to sustain the worldly life. He eats and drinks and fills his stomach and provides himself with luxuries. In such a situation, he has nothing to do with the Hereafter and the reward and the eagle. Following the second prophet, i.e., the guide, improves his afterlife. He can know his end and be aware of it through reward and reward. The second prophet forbids the first prophet from evil and adorns his soul with knowledge. **As long as the first Messenger is not connected to the second Prophet, he is not obliged to suffer the Shariah** and thus he is deprived of the reward.<!-- section 2 (id=1399, raw_sort=2): رسولوں کی ضرورت کیوں ہے؟ -->

He manifested in all his derivatives, realms of nature, and their traces, in a predetermined manner and in a definite term. He then brought forth from them a body with senses, parallel and corresponding to its principles. He made those principles and means its sustenance and nourishment through which he survives, and from which his physical and perceptual reward and punishment are determined. Similarly, He made principles, causes and means for the souls (souls), spirits (spirits) and religious noetic forms (of or relating to mental activity or the intellect), through which they aquire their spiritual sustenance, nourishment and survival. They are rewarded when they adhere to them, and punished when they disrupt their support and distance them from their origin.

[10] Allah(Subhanhu Wa Ta'ala) made the principles (principles) and causes (cause) of the body, a testimony and proof for principles of the soul; as well as for its derivatives and its nourishment, so that the religious creation would be similar to, corresponding and parallel to the physical creation. The body manifests its perfection only through the effects of the celestial spheres along with the stars (constellations). Without the stars, the body could not have achieved perfection simply through the celestial spheres, because the course of events are always influenced by the stars. Similarly the manifestation of the religious noetic forms and the everlasting souls manifest themselves and achieve perfection only through the influence of the Imams (peace be upon them). Their nourishment is from the imam's knowledge, because the imams are the religious stars. The Prophet (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) said: ‘My companions are like the stars - whichever you emulate (imitate), you will be guided. This holds true even when they act in place of the prophets who are like the celestial spheres. Thus, the course of affairs for all created beings, as well as their recourse is towards the Imams.

[11] We say that the differences in the degrees of excellence (⟪ar:التفاوت التفاضل⟫) that exist among all entities comprising of the essence (⟪ar:أصول⟫) and their derivations (⟪ar:فروع⟫) are the firmest evidence and proof of affirming the imamate and the imams. This is because Allah the exalted created entities in all genre (⟪ar:اجناس⟫) and species (⟪ar:انواع⟫), different and disparate from each other in respect to their excellence. He thereafter set for each and every genus and species an upper limit (⟪ar:غایۃ⟫), a summit (⟪ar:ذرورۃ⟫), an end (⟪ar:نھایۃ⟫) and a virtue (⟪ar:فضیلۃ⟫), which none other in that category can attain. He made those genera and species, either a necessity for humans without which their physical existence and function would not be complete; or alternatively, a source of nourishment, a medicine, an elegance, an adornment and an indispensable tool for their existence. He thus placed in all things a unique advantage (⟪ar:منفعۃ⟫) that does not exist in anything else (ecological niche). He powerfully endowed in them a capacity which the creation would necessarily and unavoidably need, and would not be able to continue its physical affairs without it (such as food and water). He also put them to a test by creating in them a 'need' (hunger and thirst) so that they are forced to seek and acquire those requirements in order to remove their need. Thus, when one cannot find the cure of that neediness in its place, he seeks it elsewhere and hastens to the place where it may be found. As long as one remains in this physical world, he cannot, even for a moment, dispense with breathing (التنسم) and seeking to benefit from the elements of this world, its derivations, realms of nature, vegetation and nourishment, all of which are a wisdom from Allah, the Exalted, and a mark of justice from Him. This is so that these aspects would serve as a witnesses and ephemeral, obligatory symbols (and examples of algebraism) for the symbolized realities (parables) that are religious, eternal and chosen. The benefit of all symbolized realities comprising principles, realms of nature, nourishment and religious remedies are more deserving, more worthy and more powerful than the obligatory symbols. Their need is more than the need of what is compulsory by nature because their benefits only affect the body while those of the symbolized realities affect the spirit and the soul. Therefore, the extent of increase in merit of the intellectual symbolized realities over the natural symbols is according to the extent of the spirits merit over the body. Furthermore, the merit of the symbolized over the symbol is according to the extent of the merit of the everlasting ones over the perishable ones. The merit in each one of them reflects the deficiency in the other. For whoever is deprived of the physical aspects he is in need of, will not experience destruction because of that. And even if there is a destruction, it would only be that of his body which undoubtedly will one day perish and die. However, in the absence of the symbolized, religious realities, there would be destruction of the soul, which plunges into perpetual punishment. Therefore, if people’s struggle in seeking for what the soul needs was similar to their struggle and desire for seeking what the body needs, then none would doubt, stray or ascribe human qualities to Allah, that is, anthropomorphism (⟪ar:تشبیح⟫).

[12] All of Allah's creation and their need are an admonition for the sake of the symbolized religious reality. It is a witness to everyone who seeks testimony for it and an evidence for whoever asks for such a proof. It is an insight for all those who see and reflect on it. It is also a proof against those who deny the symbolized religious reality of the imams and the hierarchies, as well as their knowledge and benefit - because they accept proofs of the benefit from natural elements, planets and the celestial spheres. Indeed Allah, the Exalted, has created these elements, means and natural causes as such powerful evidence, that no one is able to deny them or seek testimony against them, change or alter them, unlike what they have done with the religious law and with what the Prophet had asked them to bear witness to, concerning affirmation of the imamate and the imams—its necessity and devotion to the imams,  the necessity of obeying them, condemning those who disobey them, and promising reward to those who obey them—and in a manner in which they changed the Quran and distorted it.  As Allah, the Exalted says:

indeed, forgetfulness is only an excess of disbelief

[14] Now, we shall mention the differences and degrees of excellence in each principle (⟪ar:اصل⟫) and realm of nature (⟪ar:موالید⟫). We shall also show that the ultimate limit and highest summit in each genus and species points to the imam in every age and time.

[15] The sages (⟪ar:حکما⟫) among the philosophers mention the ten categories and say that one cannot mention or discuss anything except in reference to these ten:

'Essence' (⟪ar:جوھر⟫) is the ultimate, upon which all others rest and which excels over all others because the remaining nine are established from it. Therefore, the essence that they describe, is a proof for the permanent, intellectual, luminous and authentic substance through which all souls are illumined and on which all nine depend. Yet, it is one that has no need of any created thing; it draws benefit from nothing or no one in the world, but rather, everything benefits from it and is in need of it. That is the Imam, peace be upon him! All other ranks are below him just as the nine categories are in respect to the ‘essence’

[16] We also say that among the four basic Constituents (⟪ar:العناثر⟫) from which the first composites (⟪ar:ترکیب⟫) were composed, two are active and two passive. The two active ones are heat and cold while the two passive ones are dryness and humidity. The ultimate and the noblest among them is heat, from which arises luminosity, light, motion, movement and elevation while the other three are established through it. Heat thus has precedence in origin and in existence. No foundation or realm of nature would be complete without it. Likewise, the Imam illumines the soul and from Him comes intellectual light (al-Nur al-Aqli), motion (movement) and elevation (al-'Alu). No principle or religious realm is complete without him. Additionally, among the celestial spheres, the most lofty and supreme sphere is the outermost one in which they all orbit. Likewise, the imam in His religion is the supreme religious sphere for all other spheres. All heavenly bodies and religious signs of the zodiac function under his command. If someone were to claim that this is rather a symbol for the Qayam (resurrector), given that the highest principle in the world points to the intellect in respect to the four principles, then we would reply that the imam is the Qayam of his time. He is the proclaimer (speaker) in his age and time, and he is the one who assumes the position of the partial Qayam (⟪ar:جزوی⟫). The imam's position in his realm is that of the Universal Intellect.

[17] We also say that among the four elements (⟪ar:الارکان⟫), the highest and the noblest is fire which is the summit and peak, to which no other element or nature can attain. Likewise, the imam, in his age and time, is the highest religious pillar that illuminates souls. On this basis Ibrahim (PBUH) ordered glorification of the fire (if what has been reported from him is true), preventing them from extinguishing and puffing it out. However, the indication was towards glorifying the imamate, which no one must put out through the injustice of those who falsely claim its position. The fire worshippers fell into confusion, not knowing its symbolized reality. Allah, the Exalted, related about the adversaries and the usurpers of the position of the Imamate in his words:
⟪ar-quote:يُرِيدُونَ أَن يُطْفِـُٔوا۟ نُورَ ٱللَّهِ بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَيَأْبَى ٱللَّهُ إِلَّآ أَن يُتِمَّ نُورَهُۥ وَلَوْ كَرِهَ ٱلْكَفِرُونَ⟫

[18] We also maintain that there are many planets (⟪ar:کواکب⟫), and among them there are differences and disparities in degrees of excellence. Their peak and summit in nobility and merit is the sun through which physical eyesight draws its light, and through which all plants, animals and newborns grow and become perfect. No plant or newborn can grow without the effect of the sun. Likewise the Imam is the sun of religion through which insight is enlightened and souls are illumined from the light of guidance and wisdom, radiating the hearts of Allah’s friends.

It is because of this that some people propose to glorify the sun. However, worshippers of the sun too fell into confusion as they only recognized the symbol, and not the symbolized reality. It is for this reason the Prophet, may Allah bless him, indicated the rising of the sun from the west which signals the rising of the Imam (Mehdi), peace be upon him, in the Maghrib. If the physical sun were to depart from its usual orbit and its ascent and descent from its centre, for even a blink of an eye, the entire world would be destroyed; which is impossible to happen, both rationally and naturally. Therefore, the Prophet’s statement could only hold true on the basis of esoteric interpretation. In fact, his interpretation has proved accurate as we have witnessed it with our own eyes by the grace and favour of Allah.

[19] We also say that among the twelve signs of the zodiac, four are most glorious and excellent. One of those four is the peak and the summit in nobility Likewise, among the twelve proofs (⟪ar:حجۃ⟫), the one who is directly connected to the Imam (⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫) is the most excellent, the noblest and the most exalted of them all.

[20] We maintain that the fourth clime[A region considered with reference to its climate] (⟪ar:أقلیم⟫), which is parallel to the fourth heaven and parallel to the sun, is the most moderate as well as being the most excellent and noblest of all. Similarly the imam is the most excellent in the hierarchy as well as the most glorious of them all, being their peak and apex. Four is emphasized in the four elements, seasons, humours and directions.

[21] We also say that among the melting mineral substances the most excellent is gold. It is that with which people conduct their business. It establishes the value of valuables; is not affected by fire or water; nor does it get destroyed. Likewise the Imam, peace of Allah be upon him, is the most excellent religious and intellectual substance in his age and time. Through him the worldly and religious transactions of the creation and their well-being are completed.

Each melting substance has its peak in nobility. Among these precious stones, the best is sapphire (⟪ar:الیاقوت⟫), and among all  the white, green, yellow and red sapphires (⟪ar:یاقوت الاحمر⟫), it is the red ruby which is excellent. No other jewel lasts like the ruby, because it maintains its essence and does not get transformed by fire or water; it always retains its purity, radiance and gravity. It obtains traces from its source in a manner that no other substances can. Not even other sapphires (⟪ar:یاقوت⟫), emeralds (⟪ar:الزمرد⟫), the carnelian (⟪ar:البیجاد⟫), agate (⟪ar:العقیق⟫), diamonds (⟪ar:والألماس⟫), turquoise (⟪ar:والفروزج⟫), are able retain these qualities. So the crown among the jewels is the red ruby just as the Imam is the peak amongst all creation of his age and time in his excellence. He is unique among all the ranks of the hierarchy—among the proofs (⟪ar:حجۃ⟫), the adjuncts (⟪ar:لواحق⟫), the summoner (⟪ar:داعی⟫), the wing (⟪ar:جناح⟫), the licensee (⟪ar:ماذون⟫) and the motivator (⟪ar:مکلب⟫)’

From a different perspective, the seven jewels are proofs for the seven ‘proclaimers’ (⟪ar:النطقا⟫) and the seven imams. The natural symbol is present in every age and time, and therefore the symbolized reality ought to be present in every age and time as well. Just as the stars, the natural elements, the realms of nature and the substances are present in all times; similarly the ranks of the hierarchy also must exist in every age and time so that they always influence the souls.

[22] We maintain that there are seven grains that provide human beings with food and nourishment. [40] We also say that wise men and men of insight, in buildIng the world, consider it to be similar to actions of Allah in His creation of the world, its environment and in preserving human welfare. When they see a good fertile land, they know that it will accept the planting of seeds and the growth of plants. When they see water in distant deep valleys or underground, they struggle to bring it forth from those valleys and from under the ground by digging canals and wells reverting water to those lands. They do not see any wisdom in leaving these good fertile lands unused and wasted or leaving aside the water unutilized, out of compassion for the creatures, as well as for safeguarding their welfare and the environment of the world. Likewise when the imams, peace be upon them, see people ready to accept the seed of wisdom and knowledge, although being very far from being able to receive it, the imams put great effort in digging canals through preparatory religious exercises on many an occasion. People would be perhaps unaware of the reason for that religious exercise until they realize its wisdom and become prepared and able to accept it. Perhaps, when the imams are unable to make them reach that level by exercise, they place the wisdom in many dense symbols, either in words or actions so that people can accept these symbols and gradually arrive at their meaning, and then the imams themselves elevate them to that level of understanding.

[41] In this manner, it is requisite to believe in the supreme leadership or the imamate of the imam of the time, and know that he is the head of the world and the vicegerent of Allah over His creation, and not to object to any of His commands, prohibitions, actions and teachings, and to know,  in general, that all of that is wisdom and welfare for the creation. The correct aspect of that may be problematic for the believer at times, but in the end, its benefits will become clear to him, just as many of the things that were not known before become apparent and clear, and just as in much of the sharia, the faithful do not know the purpose of the Legislator and the wisdom in it and disapprove of that. Those whom Allah provides with the recognition of its meaning know the wisdom in it, just as many of those who see things in the world whose wisdom they do not understand think that there is no beneflt in it or in its creation, realize later that there is wisdom in it, which they are unable to deny. There are numerous things whose creation they [people] see as harmful and destructive, having no wisdom in it or any benefit, but when they realize its wisdom they become calm and content from their protest. Whoever recognizes the Wise One (Al-Hakim) and acknowledges that He is wise, it is inevitable that he knows and acknowledges that everything the Wise One does is full of wisdom and that He would not do anything that has no wisdom and that He would not command or prohibit or say anything except out of wisdom. Futile action is not permissible for the Wise One; if it were permissible for the Wise one to do, say, command or forbid anything that has no wisdom, then that would be a vain act which is not permissible for the Wise One. Whoever does anything whose doing or not doing is the same will be foolish and depart from the domain of wisdom.

[42] Further, if it is not permissible for the Wise One to perform an action whose existence or non-existence would be the same, how is it then possible for the imam to perform an action while wisdom is in another or in its opposite or that the welfare is in something other than it, while he,  the imam, is absolutely wise and whose bestowal of wisdom on any one of his servants makes them wise? Whoever performs an action without wisdom and goodness, his action is corrupt and futile, and may Allah save us from those who believe that of the imam of the time. This would then mean that the individual has distanced himself from wisdom and if he departs from wisdom, he has thus departed from the domain of his imamate, and that is departure from the faith, may Allah protect us and all believers from all such doubt, ideads or belief as this. May He bestow upon us contentment and unquestioning submission (submission).

[43] We also say that the freely-grazing livestock who have their own benefits and excellence must inevitably have a shepherd to tend to them, look after and protect them, and choose their feeding and drinking places, safeguarding them from thieves and beasts. If it were not for the fact that there was someone to undertake that, they would perish, being unable to manage their affairs by themselves, nor be able to defend themselves against beasts of prey, nor manage their affairs for their own good and well-being.

So, likewise, people are like sheep; if there is no Shepherd to protect them, they would perish. The imam is their shepherd safe guarding their worldly and religious affairs and preventing them from worldly and religious enemies. If they were to relinquish that role, not even two people would remain, nor would a single individual escape from punishment. It is in this manner that people in the countries are called subjects (subjects), because they are under the custody of their shepherd, while the shepherd in reality is the Imam, the other claimants being wolves devouring the cattle.

[44] We maintain that the example of the world is that of the human body. If the body were to have no head, it would be a decayed cadaver of no use. Similarly, if there were no head, leader or master in the world, guiding the worldly and religious affairs, people would become beasts devouring each other. The world would not exist even for a single day and there would be no wisdom in the creation of the world. The imam is indeed the head of world, its leader and master.

[45] Further, we say that there are disparities and differences in degrees of excellence in the organs of the body; the foremost and the best among them is the head. It is in the head that the face is situated and by which each human being is recognized and distinguished from others. Among the internal organs, the best one is the brain, which is their chief, being the source of the intellect. So, likewise, the imam has a position in the world similar to the head and in relation to the organs, similar to the brain. If he gave everything to the believers, then surely their faith would be mostly for the sake of the world, but if he were to deny all the believers at all times, they would surely become despondent and their good intention would weaken. Consequently, the Commander of the Faithful, for a certain period of time bestowed blessings on the believers, such as no one else bestowed and he also withheld from them for some time for their benefit in order that they would not become like those about whom Allah has said: ‘Nay, indeed man certainly becomes rebellious, considering himself self-sufficient. Indeed, Allah gave worldly goods to the pious and the impious alike, so that the deeds of the pious would not just be for the sake of

this world, nor would restraint from immorality be only for the sake of this world, nor restraint from disobedience only because of worldly benefit, such that they would be deprived of reward in the world to come, rather they would be exclusively for the sake of religion and Allah. Likewise, the Commander of the Faithful peace be upon him, gives worldly benefits to the righteous as well as the immoral alike.

[85] Indeed Allah, the Exalted, has given the temporal world to those who obey and those who disobey out of wisdom on His part, in order that obedience be not for the sake of the world, nor restraint from disobedience because of it; rather it should be purely for the sake of Allah. Similarly; the Commander of the Faithful gives the obedient and the disobedient alike, following the actions of Allah, the Exalted, in order that humanity’s obedience to Him is not for worldly advantage. and the reward of the hereafter not be exduded from them. Surely Allah gives worldly goods to the worshipper as well as the non-worshipper, lest the worship of the creatures be for the sake of worldly benefit. Similarly, the Commander of the Faithful engages with his people not looking at their imperfections and disobedience, but looking at them with a merciful eye. Allah, the Exalted, has given worldly benefit to the simpleton and the ignorant, and has given it to the learned and the intellectual, while also withholding it from both of them. Likewise, the Commander of the Faithful gives to the simpleton and the ignorant, the learned and the intelligent alike, on account of his wisdom and his virtue. If he were to give to the learned and the intelligent only and to withhold from others, then the people’s acquisition of knowledge would only be for the sake of this world, and their use of reason and acquisition of virtues would also only be for this world, leaving the ignorant and the simpleton in despair. But Allah has equally given the world to the simpleton and the ignorant. He has also given worldly benefit to the wrongdoer and the just, to the generous and the miser, to the noble, to the depraved and the lowly, to those possessing every virtue and those lacking it, equally giving them all out of His wisdom, mercy and grace. Likewise, the Commander of the Faithful treats all of them equally, following the actions of Allah, the Exalted, and His mercy upon His creatures, in order that everyone may obtain their fair share of His bounty and kindness.

[86] Moreover Allah, the Exalted, has concealed the affairs of the world and how His creatures may benefit from it, as well as their path in approaching it in all respects. He has concealed from mankind both the means and the actions needed to acquire worldly benefits, such that one becomes rich, while the other has nothing to eat, one becomes wealthy from trade and commerce, while the other goes bankrupt, one becomes rich from agriculture, while the other loses even his seeds, and one becomes self-sufficient from hunting,  while the other is impoverished. All this is on account of the exceeding wisdom so that He would not provide easy access to the world, while all other accesses become void and disorder pervades the creation and corrupts the world. Similar is the interaction of the Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him, with mankind in that respect in this world. He conceals the means to that such that one seeks it by some means whereupon he gives it to him, while the other who seeks it by the same means is denied. 1f they knew a particular means by which they could obtain worldly goods from the Commander of the Faithful, then, all other means would become certainly futile. All those who lacked that would despair of his mercy becoming deprived, and there would be no merit in that.

[87] The interaction of the Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him, with regard to his proximity with people close to him and his distance from them are similar. If the Commander of the Faithful were to remove the hypocrites and bring the believers close to him, then surely all people would become believers because he brought them close to him, not because of the religion. Similarly if he were to keep his distance from all believers at all times, they would then avoid him and anxiety would crop into their hearts, either because of the perceived anger from the Commander of the Faithful or because of a slip on their part that necessitated that. Thus, he deals with them according to what is appropriate to the prevailing times and conditions —awakening desire, alarm, removing them or bringing them close, granting and denying, punishing and forgiving—all this is following the actions and characteristics of Allah, the Exalted, so that people would perpetuaily be both in fear and hope, neither feeling secure lest they become bold,  nor despairing so as to become despondent. In all this he follows the manners of Allah, the Exalted, towards His creation, just as He, may He be glorified, gives abundantly to all, including those who do not fear poverty and exhaustion of wealth; similarly the Commander of the Faithful also gives to those who do not fear poverty and exhaustion of wealth, exceeding the generosity  of human beings.

[88] Those who look through the eye of reality at the actions of the Commander of the Faithful in the religion and worldly matters will find that everything he does with the creation, commanding good and forbidding evil, granting and denying are all parallel to the actions of Allah, the Exalted. But people are baffled regarding that because they look at his actions on the basis of those of ordinary human beings and of what they have heard. They compare the actions of the Commander of the Faithful with those of other people and the actions of other rulers, and with what they have heard, seen and understood. They regard that as being in opposition to what they are accustomed to, and so have fallen into perplexity, confusion and speculation. If they view it in relation to Allah’s actions with His creation, then all that would be easier for them. It is difficult for them only because, ever since the time of Adam to this day of ours, no one has done anything like that. If anyone did act in this manner at any time, it was only a small portion of it as is reported in the actions of the sage [Khizar] with Moses, peace be upon him. Moses was not patient about Khizar’s actions as he saw them in relation to his own action, knowledge and intellect. If he had viewed them in relation to Allahs actions, then that would have certainly been contrary to what actually happened to him. Another instance is what has been reported about David’s judgement and Allah’s command in regard to the perplexing and astounding story of the owner of the vineyard’ and the owner of the bull. Similarly Allah, the Exalted, has warned in His words: ‘On the day when the summoner calls them to a painful end ... their eyes will be lowered in humiliation’

Allah also said: ‘They were called to prostrate while safe and secure. It is amazing that if they cannot endure these worldly actions; how can they then endure the affliction by religious actions and by things that are far more difficult than that? Indeed, all that is found in two extreme positions does not exist at the centre and what is in the centre does not exist in the regions. Yet people pass judgement on the issues according to what they see in the centre, while themselves being at the extremities, it is for that reason they become confused and baffled, unable to bear or endure it.

[89] We also say that gifts of the other monarchs are perceived by people as being given because of a service, relationship, an obligation, out of love, need, seeking a benefit or averting harm. They could also be given for other reasons that necessitate it, such as fearing calamity; in expectation of good fortune, or in fear of someone’s tongue, in seeking praise or exaltation and glorification, in desire of gratitude or benevolence, as a reward to an official or as a means of sustenance. All that is done on the basis of appraisal and assessment, while gifts from the Commander of the Faithful are beyond all that, because gifts given for the reasons cited above are not called generosity; virtue or beneficence. Rather, they are given either in payment of an obligation for something in the past or as an advance payment for something in the future. Delay of obligations and fulfilment of rights is blameworthy and miserly, and likewise, the action of advancing money for the sake of future benefit is not considered generosity or virtue. Also, whatever is given in connection with indebtedness is not considered generosiry nobility or virtue. The gifts of Allah, glory be to Him, exceed all that, for there is nothing that Allah is obliged to give anyone, nor does Allah seek any reward from any of His creatures. In the same manner, the Commander of the Faithful does not give to anyone for the reasons cited, nor does he demand compensations for that. Allah, the Exalted, said regarding the Commander of the Faithful, Ali ibne Abu Talib: We do not desire either reward or gratitude from them” Rather, he bestows upon them spontaneously out of generosity, nobility and virtue, following the actions of Allah, the Exalted, with His Creation. If they viewed the actions of the Commander of the Faithful in this way, they would not fall into confusion. However, some of them perceive him with short- sightedness, judging his actions to be similar to those of other human beings, while others look at him with exaggeration, claiming divinity for him. The actions of the Commander of the Faithful are indeed beyond both these two viewpoints. Rather, he is the Universal Vicegerent of Allah among His worshippers (al kha1fat al-ku!!, lil1ih taãla ala ib4dihi).

[90] Just as Allah, the Exalted, concealed the reasons for creating the world from mankind and explained the reasons of religion and the means of attaining it, He also explained ways of attaining His pleasure, clarifying it through the words of His Prophet. He gave knowledge and wisdom to those who deserved it and denied it to

those who did not, and He made before them a barrier and behind them a barrier establishing signposts of guidance and the summons of the proof and the guide to the bounty of the hereafter. He disclosed ways of attaining to it and opened its gates, so that whoever deserves it attains it and he who does not misses it in utmost wisdom. Likewise, the Commander of the Faithful concealed the means of attaining worldly fortune through him, giving it to both, those who deserve and those who do not, He explained the means of attaining religion, knowledge and wisdom, and bestowed them upon those who deserved it, neither withholding them nor being miserly. He bountifully bestowed favours upon people, giving more than anyone could ever give in any time or age. He also withheld favours from the undeserving ones, differentiating between the people of the truth (Ahl al-Haq) and the people of falsehood (Ahl al-Batil), following the actions and ways of Allah, the Exalted, in the governance of the world and religion, and in the building of this present world and the world to come.

[91] We ask Allah to bestow upon us gratitude for what He has blessed us with in words, deeds and in intention, and that He grant us success in obedience to the lord of the age and time (wall al-’asr wa’l-zaman), peace be upon him. May He provide us with the means of struggling on his behalf with our hands, tongues and hearts. May Allah open up the entire world for him and with his sword purify the earth of polytheism (shirk), unbelief (Kufr) and hypocrisy (wjq) through his bounty and generosity. This worshipper begs and desires the blessings of the Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him, and seeks ‘the supreme command’ (al-amr a!-ãlI) to examine what the servant has gathered and compiled, and to give him permission for that which is sound and correct, and draw his attention to mistakes in it, so that the servant can be thankful to him in increasing his insight and certainty and so that this would become a means of guidance and evidence for those who seek to be guided and a proof against those who deny.

[92] Praise be to Allah, the praise of the thankful one, the mindful and the pious and prayers upon His Messenger, our master Muhammad, and all his progeny.
