## The Prophet's Counsels to ʿAlī (Waṣāyā)

The Commander of the Faithful addresses his community with counsel drawn from divine wisdom. "O ʿAlī," the Prophet declares, "there are six noble qualities; when they are united in six persons, their virtue becomes supreme."

| Quality | In the Ruler | In the Rich | In the Scholar | In the Poor | In the Youth | In Woman |
|---------|--------------|-------------|----------------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **Justice** | Makes him worthy; without it, he is like a cloud bearing no rain | — | — | — | — | — |
| **Generosity** | — | Becomes most excellent; the stingy are fruitless trees | — | — | — | — |
| **Piety** | — | — | Becomes sanctified; the impious scholar burns himself while lighting others | — | — | — |
| **Patience** | — | — | — | Becomes sublime; the impatient are barren ground | — | — |
| **Repentance** | — | — | — | — | Becomes most commendable; the unrepentant are dry wells | — |
| **Modesty** | — | — | — | — | — | Becomes exalted; shamelessness is as bland as unsalted food |

The Prophet further counsels: "Do not befriend the drunkard, for he is accursed. Similarly, those who deal in wine are enemies of God in the heavens, as are the usurers who rival the Almighty Himself. To them God has said: 'You are enemies of Allāh; prepare to struggle against His will.'"[^cite-1]

When you stand in prayer, extend your hands in supplication, yet lower your voice, for loud utterance disturbs the devotion of others. Should you engage a worker, pay his full wages without diminishment, for God will nullify the deeds of the miserly employer, and I shall be his adversary on the Day of Resurrection.

"Consume olive oil and anoint yourself with it. When sickness befalls you, recite this invocation:"

⟪ar:سُبْحَانَکَ لاَ اِلَہَ اِلاَّ اَنْتَ عَلَیْکَ تَوَکَّلْتُ وَاَنْتَ رَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِیْم⟫

"Glory be to Thee; there is none worthy of worship save Thee. Upon Thee do I rely, for Thou art Lord of the Mighty Throne."

Recite also this prayer, which Gabriel (ʿalayhi al-salām) revealed to me:

⟪ar:اَللّٰھُمَّ اِنِّی اَسْئلُکَ الْعَفْوَ وَالْعَافِیَۃَ وَالْمُوَافَاۃَ الدَّائِمَہْ فِی الدِّیْنِ وَالدُّنْیَا وَالآخِرَۃ اِنَّکَ عَلَی کُلِّ شَئیٍ قَدِیْرٌ⟫

"O God, I beseech Thee for pardon and well-being, and for abiding concord in religion, this world, and the Hereafter. Verily, Thou art capable of all things."[^cite-2]

When sorrow and distress encompass you and you seek aid, raise your hands to God and say:

⟪ar:یَا حَیُّ یَا قَیُّومُ لاَ اِلَہَ اِلاَّ اَنْتَ بِرَحْمَتِکَ اَسْتَغِیْثُ وَ بِعَفْوِکَ اِرْحَمنِیْ وَاَصْلِح لِیٰ شَانِی کُلَّہُ وَ فَرِّجْ ھَمِّیْ وَغَمِّیْ بِرَحْمَتِکَ یَا اَرْحَمَ الرَّاحِمِیْنَ⟫

"O Living One, O Self-Subsisting One, there is none worthy of worship but Thee. By Thy mercy do I beseech Thee; by Thy forgiveness have mercy upon me and set right all my affairs. Dispel my grief and sorrow through Thy mercy, O Most Merciful of the merciful."

Two *rakaʿāt* (units of prayer) performed in the night excel a thousand performed by day. The countenance of one who rises in night vigil becomes more radiant than that of the daylight worshipper.

When God created Paradise, He asked it, "For whom have I fashioned you?" Paradise replied, "For every generous soul and one who fears Thee." God said, "I am pleased with you." Therefore, know: whoever opposes his desires shall dwell in Paradise, and whoever enslaves himself to desire shall abide in Hellfire.[^cite-3]

When God created Hellfire, He asked, "For whom have I created you?" Hellfire answered, "For every miser and every arrogant being." God said, "Then I am pleased with this arrangement."

When you give in charity, give that which is good and pleasing to you. A single date distributed from lawfully earned sustenance, given with sincere intention, exceeds a thousand gold coins given from wealth after enjoying it yourself.

Perform ablution (wuḍūʾ) with care before sleep, for it is a foundation of faith. When performing ablution, do not waste water carelessly. While washing, recite Sūrah "Innā Anzalnāhu fī Laylat al-Qadr" (We have sent it down on the Night of Power). God will reward each such ablution as if you had worshipped for fifty years.[^cite-4]

Whoever washes his feet and invokes blessings upon me ten times upon completing ablution—God shall remove his grief and accept his prayers. After ablution, moisten your throat with water and say:

"Subḥānaka Allāhu wa bi-ḥamdika, lā sharīka lak, istaghfiruka wa-atūbu ilayk.

Glory be to Thee, O God, and praise be to Thee. Thou hast no partner. I seek Thy forgiveness and turn to Thee in repentance."

Then look toward the earth and declare: "I bear witness that Muḥammad (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam) is the servant and messenger of God. May the peace of God be upon him." Whosoever does this—God shall forgive him all sins, great and small.

Beware of gathering with the heedless, those who do not remember God. When I asked the Messenger, "O Apostle, who are these spiritually dead?" he replied: "The wealthy who regard their accumulated riches as a mother guards her child. Such people suffer immeasurable loss."

Should a scholar lack piety and contrition, his sermons fall from hearts like eggs from a hen's nest—broken and worthless.

Give charity on behalf of the deceased, for God and the angels deliver that charity to them, saying: "O God, forgive him who illuminated our graves. Grant him the glad tidings of Paradise as Thou hast granted us." But if charity is withheld, the deceased lament: "Woe unto those whom we have left behind!"

The one who consumes what is *ḥalāl* (lawfully acquired) enjoys pure faith: his heart becomes tender, his eyes grow moist with the fear of God, and the veil is lifted from his prayer, so that his supplication is answered.

Do not confide your secrets to people, nor entrust them to women, for women do not preserve secrets. Eat sparingly, that you may pass the night in tranquility and peace; excessive eating hardens the heart.

If you refrain from borrowing, you shall live in freedom and security. If you abstain from backbiting, you shall gain sincere friendship. If you swallow your anger, God shall fill your heart with light and your breast with knowledge.

Should you approach your wife on Thursday or Friday, and a son be born, he shall be devout, God-fearing, and blessed. If you sit in the company of the poor, you shall attain exalted standing with God.

Whoever exalts the wealthy while debasing the poor is called an enemy of God in the heavens and the earth. Always speak truth; let no falsehood pass your lips. Never betray trust. Fear God as though He watches you. Dedicate your life and wealth to your faith. Cultivate virtuous character and cast off corrupt morals.

God loves three things:

| One | Whoever fulfills the obligations of God Almighty is a supreme worshipper. |
|-----|----------|
| Two | Whoever refrains from what God has forbidden is profoundly pious. |
| Three | Whoever is content with what God has provided is truly rich. |

The reward of three deeds reaches both this world and the next:

| One | Pilgrimage (Ḥajj) removes poverty. |
|-----|----------|
| Two | Charity removes evil. |
| Three | Kindness to kinfolk extends life. |

On the Day of Resurrection, all eyes shall weep except three:

| One | The eye that wakened in God's worship. |
|-----|----------|
| Two | The eye closed against forbidden sights. |
| Three | The eye that flowed with fear of the Almighty. |

When you gaze upon a mirror, say: "Allāhu Akbar, Allāhu Akbar, Allāhu Akbar, Allāhu Akbar. As Thou hast made my outward form fair, so make my inner character beautiful."

When faced with hardship, say: "May God bless him and grant him peace. Let God ease my trouble through Muḥammad and the family of Muḥammad."

When you begin eating, taste salt; when you finish, taste salt. It cures seventy diseases, including leprosy. Do not approach your wife during the first and middle nights of the lunar month—the thirteenth, fourteenth, and fifteenth—for have you not observed that madness increases on these nights?

Do not wear rings upon your index and middle fingers, for the people of Lot (ʿalayhi al-salām) wore rings upon these fingers, and God's wrath descended upon them.

Speak no falsehood, for lies darken the face and the liar is recorded as a liar in God's sight. By truthfulness, a person's face becomes radiant and luminous, and he is written as righteous before God. Remember: truth is blessed; falsehood is calamity.

A wicked but generous soul ranks higher in God's sight than a miserly worshipper. Honor your neighbor, even if he be an unbeliever. Love him and treat him with compassion, for the one consumed with envy of his neighbor loses both wealth and life.

Recite *Sūrah Yāsīn* (Chapter 36), in which lie ten blessings:

| One | If hungry, you shall find food. | Six | If sick, you shall be healed. |
|---|---|---|---|
| Two | If thirsty, you shall be satisfied. | Seven | If imprisoned, you shall be freed. |
| Three | If naked, God shall clothe you. | Eight | If traveling, it shall ease your journey. |
| Four | If unmarried, you shall find a spouse. | Nine | If recited for the deceased, death's anguish is lightened and punishment reduced. |
| Five | If afraid, you shall find peace. | Ten | If recited for the astray, they shall be guided. |

The Holy Prophet (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam) said: "On the Day of Resurrection, a creature called *Ḥārith*, descended from the scorpion, shall emerge from Hellfire. Its length shall equal the distance between heaven and earth; its width, from east to west. Gabriel shall ask, 'Where are you bound, O Ḥārith?' It shall reply, 'To the field of judgment.' Gabriel shall ask, 'Whom do you seek?' It shall answer: 'Five kinds of humans:

| One | Those who do not pray. |
|---|---|
| Two | Those who do not pay *zakāt* (alms-tax). |
| Three | Those who disobey their parents. |
| Four | The drunkards. |
| Five | Those who speak of worldly matters in the mosque.'"[^cite-5]

## Aqwāl Zarrīn — Golden Sayings

When charity departs from its owner's hand, it speaks five declarations:

| One | "I was mortal wealth; you have preserved me." |
|---|---|
| Two | "I was your enemy; now you have made me your friend." |
| Three | "I protected you before; now I shall protect you eternally." |
| Four | "I was worthless; you have exalted me." |
| Five | "Once I lay in your hands; now I rest in God's hands." |

When you choose a friend, make a cemetery in your heart where you may bury his faults and secrets. A true friend, though worn with the rosary beads of countless devotions, should be honored, for like scattered beads of remembrance, he is precious and elect.

When the eye begins to perceive the desires of the soul, the heart grows blind until the end of time. People do not change when they gain power, wealth, and status; they are merely *exposed*—revealed for what they truly are. Seek sustenance in daylight's illumination, and in night's darkness, seek what shall sustain you eternally.

If someone you desire rejects you, it is his misfortune. If someone you do not desire pursues you and you force him to become yours, it is the degradation of your soul. Humans do not suffer from people; they suffer from the hopes they attach to them.

If you wish to belong to someone, belong wholly and truthfully. Otherwise, create such virtue in yourself that you become worthy of them in reality. It is God's blessing upon you that people bring their needs to your door. When you continue to receive the Almighty's blessings despite your sins, know that your reckoning approaches and will be severe.

I prefer sitting in the mosque above sitting in Paradise, for in Paradise my soul shall be pleased, but in the mosque my Lord shall be pleased. A believer is not one whose outward gathering is pure alone; a believer is one whose solitude is pure.[^cite-6]

The raindrop falls into both the serpent's mouth and the oyster's. The oyster transforms it into a pearl; the serpent turns it to poison. The one possessed of capacity determines the outcome. One truth that ought not be spoken is self-praise.

Strive to exist *in* the world, not *of* the world. The boat floats well while water is beneath it, but when water enters the boat, it sinks. Do not test the quickness of your tongue against one who taught you to speak.

"My God, it suffices me that I am Thy servant. It suffices me in my worry that Thou art my Lord. Thou art as I desire Thee to be. Make me as Thou dost desire."

Guard five things in five places: the tongue in gathering, the eye in the marketplace, the heart in prayer, the stomach at the table, modesty in the gaze.

Three things estrange a person from God: overestimating one's actions, forgetting one's sins, and imagining oneself superior to others.

Six things corrupt spiritual progress: finding faults in others, hardness of heart, love of the world, absence of modesty, long-held hope, and oppression of people.

A true friend is recognized in three circumstances: in times of need, behind your back, and after your death.

If you wish to test someone's mettle, show them respect. If they possess high capacity, they shall return greater respect; if low capacity, they shall deem themselves superior.

God Almighty bestows five blessings alongside five things:

| One | Thankfulness with increase of wealth. |
|---|---|
| Two | Prayer with further prayer. |
| Three | Forgiveness with forgiveness. |
| Four | Acceptance with charity. |
| Five | Mercy with mercy. |

A perfect believer is one whose character is excellent. When the people asked whether a wealthy woman whose husband forbade her pilgrimage could proceed, the answer was that she may not undertake the pilgrimage without her husband's permission.

Whoever humbles himself before the wealthy for their nobility, even if they are not tyrants, shall lose a portion of his faith. Nothing is more abhorrent in God's sight than divorce.

Whatever you bring from the market for children, give first to the girl, then to the boy. If someone purchases cloth worth ten dirhams, and even one dirham is unlawfully earned, God shall not accept his prayer so long as that cloth remains upon his body.

When a person commits sin, God grants him four favors: He does not sever his sustenance; He does not hinder his well-being; He does not expose his sin; and He does not immediately chastise him.

Four deeds rank among the most difficult: forgiving wrongs in times of anger, being generous in times of poverty, remaining in solitude, and speaking truth in times of fear or desire.

Two comforts attend righteous deeds: one when you perform the deed, and the second when you receive its reward. Haste is *Sunna* (the prophetic way) in six matters; hastening in all others is of Satan:

| — | In feeding the guest |
|---|---|
| — | In burying the dead |
| — | In arranging a woman's marriage |
| — | In fulfilling an obligation |
| — | In repenting of sin |
| — | In responding to the call to prayer and entering the mosque |

Do not underestimate four things: debt, illness, enmity, and fire. Three signs mark a corrupt heart: sinning in hope of repentance, acquiring knowledge without practicing it, acting without sincerity, consuming sustenance without gratitude, and burying the dead without learning its lesson.

The absence of three things is preferable: excessive food, excessive sleep, excessive speech.

Error manifests in three degrees: mistake, deliberateness, and wrong. Certainty unfolds in three levels: knowledge of certainty, the certainty of sight, and the truth of certainty. Three addictions progress with terrible swiftness—the first two degenerate, the last progresses: addiction to wealth, addiction to beauty, addiction to knowledge.

Ten things consume ten things: goodness consumes evil; the humility of knowledge consumes arrogance; repentance consumes sin; lawful sustenance consumes unlawful; justice consumes oppression; the call to charity consumes sorrow; forgetfulness consumes anger; intellect consumes rage; generosity consumes regret; righteous deeds consume backbiting.

Five things are foundations of corruption: wounding, language, force, oppression, and treachery.

A man's happiness rests in five things: a virtuous wife, obedient children, God-fearing friends, good neighbors, and sustenance in one's own city.

Eight things never abandon their source: the eye from sight, the earth from rain, woman from man, the world from knowledge, the questioned from questioning, the greedy from wealth, the river from water, fire from wood.

Three things inspire such prevalent fear that they confound a person's dreams: the serpent, milk, and the rat.

There is no worship more exalted in God's sight than gladdening the heart of a Muslim brother. The worst sin after associating partners with God is wounding God's creation. Respect the elders, and God shall move the young to honor you in your age.

Whosoever neither honors the elders nor shows compassion to the young is not of my community. The deed most deserving of forgiveness is openness and kind speech. Fear the prayer of the wronged, for there is no veil between him and God.

Do not curse your life, your children, your servants, or your wealth, lest such curse be answered in an hour of acceptance. The love of this world is the root of all sins, and love of anything blinds and deafens one to truth.

Cursed is the one who trusts in another creature as in himself. There is no greater gift a father can give his son than proper education and training. I fear for my *Umma* (community) hypocrisy and the long-tongued more than all else.

A generous sinner ranks higher in God's sight than a miserly worshipper. Shall I not inform you of the finest treasure? Listen well: she is a virtuous woman.[^cite-7]

Whoever abandons quarreling, even when in the right, has my guarantee of a house in Paradise. I guarantee a house in the middle of Paradise for one who stops lying, even in jest. I guarantee a house in the highest reaches of Paradise for one of good character.

At the end of time, you shall follow the Jews and Christians step by step, so thoroughly that if they enter a snake's hole, you too shall enter it.

When the Holy Prophet returned from battle, he said, "We are departing from the lesser struggle (*jihād al-asghar*) to the greater struggle (*jihād al-akbar*)." The people asked, "O Messenger of God, what is this greater struggle?" He replied, "The struggle against your own soul (*nafs*), for your greatest enemy is the self dwelling between your two sides."[^cite-8]

One who fulfills the needs of a brother is as if he has spent his entire life in God's service. Help your brother, whether he be wronged or wronging. The help of the wronged is to free him from the oppressor; the help of the wrongdoer is to restrain him from wrong.

There is no worship more exalted in God's sight than gladdening the heart of a Muslim brother. He who fears God does not take retribution. The worst sin after polytheism is wounding people, and the best goodness after faith is comforting God's creatures.

On the Day of Judgment, the poor neighbor shall receive from the rich neighbor. A disbelieving neighbor has one share of rights; a Muslim neighbor has double; a relative neighbor has threefold.[^cite-9]

One who persecutes his neighbor shall dwell in Hellfire, even if he worships the night through and fasts the day long. By Him in whose hand is Muḥammad's soul, the neighbor's right is fulfilled only by those upon whom God has mercy. The deed most deserving of forgiveness is openness and gentleness.

Those who rejoiced that people rose against them shall consider Hell their dwelling. When the Holy Prophet was asked who is the best woman, he said: "The one whom her husband sees and is gladdened, who obeys his commands, and who neither opposes what he dislikes concerning God, herself, or his wealth."

When people described a woman exceedingly devout, pious, fasting, and remembering God, yet her neighbors bore witness against her tongue, the Messenger of God (may His peace be upon all) said, "She is an inhabitant of Hellfire."

## Brotherhood (Ukhuwwa) and Backbiting (Ghība)

In *Sūrat al-Ḥujurāt*, God instructs the believers in the practice and excellence of brotherhood:

"The believers are brothers one to another, so make peace between your brothers, and fear God, that mercy may be shown to you."[^cite-10]

Brotherhood of blood—kinship—is universally acknowledged and understood. Yet there exists a second bond that God and His Messenger have ordained: the bond of faith. God addresses the believers saying, "O you who believe, the believers are brothers one to another." Through this divine and prophetic ordinance, the bond of faith surpasses that of kinship, for it originates from God and His Messenger. After establishing this fellowship of faith, it becomes incumbent upon believers to reconcile with one another and to seek one another's welfare. God further commands: "Fear God, that mercy may be shown to you"—that you may attain compassion and divine favor. The nature of brotherhood as God and His Messenger have defined it reveals what a believer's conduct must be toward fellow believers. Beyond reconciliation, the verses further mandate the fear of God (*taqwā*). From the union of these two principles flows the possibility of deserving God's mercy.

What ought not be said, given the commandment of brotherhood and reconciliation, is clarified in the succeeding verses:

"Let not a people laugh at another people, it may be that they are better than them; nor let women laugh at women, it may be that they are better than them. Nor reproach one another, nor call one another by bad names. Evil is the name of wickedness after faith. And whoso repent not, those are the wrongdoers."[^cite-11]

This verse forbids self-praise and the degradation of others. It warns: if you mock others in the belief that you are superior, those you ridicule may yet be superior in God's sight. The same applies to women. Evil indeed is calling believers by offensive names; those who do so are wrongdoers. God declares unto such wrongdoers: "A painful punishment is prepared for them."[^cite-12]

The subsequent verse elaborates upon what must be avoided and how grave such conduct is:

"O you who believe, beware of suspicion, for suspicion in some cases is a sin. And spy not, nor backbite one another. Would any of you like to eat the flesh of his dead brother? You would hate it. So fear God; surely God is the Acceptor of Repentance, the Merciful."[^cite-13]

How acute this Qurʾānic observation of human nature! How many baseless suspicions arise concerning oneself and others; how much effort is expended in learning neighbors' and others' affairs; how widespread is backbiting! In gatherings of four or more—men or women alike—it is common for people to seek out one another's faults, engage in calumny, and pry into private matters. Every believer must reflect deeply: how grave are suspicion, spying, and backbiting, and must strive earnestly to avoid them.[^cite-14]

We show excessive concern that meat purchased in the market or eaten in establishments be *ḥalāl* (lawfully acquired) and not *ḥarām* (unlawful). Yet when we gather together, we eagerly consume the flesh the Qurʾān describes as that of our dead brother. The Messenger of God (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam) said, "The good deeds of the backbiter are transferred from his record into the record of him who is backbitten."

The renowned *dervīsh* Ibrāhīm ibn Adham, who had renounced kingship for asceticism, once attended a feast. Upon arrival, he observed the guests backbiting their relatives and acquaintances. He rose and departed, saying, "I did not know you were fond of eating the flesh of your dead brothers before eating the feast."

We ought to reflect carefully: we show such scrupulous concern about the meat of the marketplace and the hotel—whether it is *ḥalāl* or *ḥarām*—yet we do not guard against true spiritual impurities: suspicions, opposition to religious ordinances, oppression, dishonesty. These defile the soul, heart, and intellect far more than any outward uncleanness. We are anxious about the meat of commerce, yet we eagerly and joyfully consume the flesh of our dead brother.

Remember: in the verse cited above, God addresses "O you who believe"—the believers in both their inward and outward dimensions. While teaching the lesson of brotherhood and commanding the avoidance of evil, He says, "Fear God." The peculiarity of this age is that neither God nor death is remembered while committing wrong! God's address to the believers indicates that only those who purify themselves from such evils truly merit the name "believers"; those who indulge in them stand outside the pale of faith. May God guide us all.

### Finding Your Own Faults

The Messenger of God (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam) said:

"I am preoccupied with the faults of my own soul and thus distracted from the faults of others. Whoever is preoccupied with knowing the faults of his own soul becomes distracted from seeking faults in the souls of others."[^cite-15]

In Arabic, when the preposition *fī* (in) accompanies a hobby or concern, it signifies engagement; when *ʿan* (from) accompanies it, it signifies non-engagement. Herein lies the subtlety of the Arabic language. The believer absorbed in self-examination ceases to scrutinize others.

## Divine Commandments (Aḥkām Khudāwandī)

Take not God's commandments as mockery. Remember the favors God has bestowed upon you: He has sent down to you the Book and Wisdom, that He may admonish you through these ordinances. Beware of disobedience to God, for God has knowledge of all things.[^cite-16]

It may be that you dislike a thing which is good for you, and that you love a thing which is bad for you. God knows this, and you know not. Do you suppose you were created without purpose, and that you shall not return to Him?[^cite-17]

Seek patience and prayer as your aid against adversity. The Messenger of God (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam) said to the people, "My Lord increases provision for whom He wills and decreases it for whom He wills." Yet people do not grasp the implications of this distribution of sustenance.[^cite-18]

O Muslims, expend in God's way from that which We have given you, ere death comes upon you. At that hour, the soul shall say, "If only my Lord had given me respite, that I might have done good and charity, and become among the righteous servants."[^cite-19]

Whoever intercedes for a good deed shall share in its reward on the Day of Resurrection, and whoever intercedes for an evil deed shall share in its burden. God has power over all things. Whatever calamity befalls you is born of your own deeds, and God forgives you much of your sins.[^cite-20]

## The Marks of a Good Person

Thus has the Heir of the Prophet and his Beloved described the good person. God (subḥānahu wa-taʿālā) has said:

"The noblest of you in the sight of God is the most God-fearing among you."[^cite-21]

And the Beloved of God has said:

"The most excellent person is one who benefits others."[^cite-22]

Adapt your life to the standards ordained by God and His Beloved. Both the spiritual and the worldly shall flourish for you. God has elucidated what is to be given and expended for the benefit of creation:

"You shall not attain to righteousness (*al-birr*) unless you expend from that which you love."[^cite-23]

The term *al-birr* (righteousness, virtue) denotes that elevated station particular to the Prophets. The righteous deeds through which the Prophets (ʿalayhim al-salām) attained the rank of *al-abrār* (the righteous) are obtained by spending from one's most treasured possessions. Ordinarily, people donate what is surplus to their use—that cannot be called true charity (*ṣadaqa*).

## The Rights of Parents and the Rights of Servants of God (Ḥuqūq al-ʿIbād)

The Lord of the Universe has decreed in His Holy Word: "Be dutiful and kind toward your parents. If one or both of them reach old age with you, say not to them a word of disrespect, nor repel them, but speak to them in kind words."[^cite-24] So honored are our parents that in every prayer, we recite the *tashahhud* (testimony), wherein we seek forgiveness and mercy for them in recognition of their nurture during our childhood.

It is narrated from *Mawlā* Kāʾināt that once the Messenger of God (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam) ascended the pulpit to deliver a sermon. Upon mounting the first step, he said, "Āmīn." Upon the second step, he again said, "Āmīn." Upon reaching the pulpit, he said, "Āmīn" a third time. He then delivered the sermon and descended. The people asked, "O Messenger of God, why did you say Āmīn three times?" He replied:

"The first time, Gabriel came to me and said, 'Cursed be the one in whose presence you are mentioned and he does not invoke blessings upon you.' So I said, 'Āmīn.'

"Then, as I ascended, Gabriel came again and said, 'Cursed be the one who encountered the month of Ramaḍān and did not attain forgiveness'—meaning, one who did not perfect the fast, prayer, and ordinances of the faith. So I said, 'Āmīn.'

"Then Gabriel came a third time and said, 'Cursed be the one who encountered his parents in their old age or one of them, yet did not win thereby his entrance into Paradise'—meaning, one who did not treat them well. So I said, 'Āmīn.'"[^cite-25]

The Prophet (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam) said, "May God disgrace the one who exhorts others to virtuous deeds yet does not practice them himself." God has rebuked such hypocrites in the Qurʾān:

"Do you command others to virtue while you forget your own souls?"[^cite-26]

Abū Dharr al-Ghaffārī, the renowned companion of the Holy Prophet (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam), once stood at the door of the Kaʿba and proclaimed to the people: "O people, hear my counsel! When one of you undertakes a journey, he prepares provisions according to the length of that journey. So too must you prepare for your journey to the Day of Judgment." A man asked, "O Abū Dharr, what is that provision (*tawsha*)?" He answered, "Perform the pilgrimage for the expiation of your grave sins, fast to shield yourselves from the severity of the Day of Judgment, and perform two *rakaʿāt* of night prayer to ward off the horror of the grave."

**Counsel:**

Surely, God is the Forgiving, the Merciful. Yet He is also the Just, the Mighty, the Swift in reckoning. Forgiveness of sins is His attribute, but oppression, abuse, and cruelty toward people; wrongful transactions; the usurpation of rights—these are transgressions God does not forgive. Only the wronged and the wronged-against may forgive one another. If God were to forgive the wrongs committed against His servants, it would contradict His justice. Neglect not this: the justice of the Master is powerful and severe, and none may escape it, be the wrong great or small. Keep rigorous account of your dealings. If there be overcharge or undercharge in a transaction, rectify it. Grant to all their due; otherwise, on the Day of Judgment you shall have to render it, and you shall be punished for the transgression. God says, "The affliction that befalls you issues from your own deeds."[^cite-27] Disease, hardship, trial, and tribulation are the fruits of one's own actions. It is best that matters of debt and commerce be settled and clarified in one's lifetime; otherwise, without their payment and resolution, there is neither redemption nor salvation, and the punishment is frightful and severe. Wisdom lies in hastening to rid oneself of it, for the respite is short. The Lord of the Universe says:

"O you who build high palaces, take heed! You are but dwellers in the grave."[^cite-28]

## Istighfār — Seeking Forgiveness and Its Method

The Messenger of God (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam) said, "Even if a person commits sins as numerous as the stars in the sky, the stars upon the earth, and the birds in the air, should he repent sincerely, God shall forgive all his sins." He further said, "To seek the forgiveness of God, pursue these seven means:

| One | Recite *Bismillāh ar-Raḥmān ar-Raḥīm* ("In the name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate") before every deed. |
|---|---|
| Two | After every act, say *Al-Ḥamdu li-Allāh* ("Praise be to God"). |
| Three | When praised for something, refer the credit to God, saying, *Al-Ḥasana min Rabbika wa-s-Sayyia min Nafsika* ("Good comes from your Lord, and evil from your soul"), and upon every praise, say *Subḥān Allāh* ("Glory be to God"). |
| Four | Should you commit an error, seek God's forgiveness at once. |
| Five | When something abhorrent occurs, say *Lā Ḥawl wa-lā Quwwat illā bi-Allāh al-ʿAlī al-ʿAẓīm* ("There is no strength save in God, the Most High, the Mighty"). |
| Six | When calamity befalls you, say *Innā li-Allāh wa-Innā Ilayhi Rājiʿūn* ("To God we belong, and to Him we return"). |
| Seven | Recite *Lā Ilāha illā Allāh* ("There is none worthy of worship save God") at all times, morning and evening. |

It is narrated from *Mawlānā* Imām Ḥusayn (ʿalayhi al-salām) that he observed a man glorifying God and seeking forgiveness. He recited to him the verse:

"Remember thy Lord within thy soul with humility and fear, without loudness of voice, morning and evening..."[^cite-29]

Then he said, "O you who seek forgiveness, what are you doing? Do you not know what forgiveness truly is? Forgiveness is the stage of repentance, and repentance is a level of certainty encompassing six meanings. If you can fulfill those six things, then you are fit to seek forgiveness; otherwise, do not seek it!"

| First | Regret what has passed. Be certain it shall be cleansed, as the pilgrim is purified of sin when returning from the plain of ʿArafāt. |
|---|---|
| Second | Resolve that you shall never return to that sin, as milk never returns to the udder once it is drawn. |
| Third | Discharge the rights of all those you have wronged, so that when you return to God, no burden lies upon you. |
| Fourth | Neglect not a single duty; perform all ordinances God has enjoined. |
| Fifth | Consider the flesh that has come upon you through the unlawful. Consume this flesh in the fire of your heart's regret until skin meets bone. |
| Sixth | Generate new flesh through righteous deeds and good works, and make it taste the pain of hardship, as you made your former flesh taste the bitterness of sin. |

Once these six things are accomplished, then—if you wish—you may seek God's forgiveness. But not before![^cite-30]

## Two Things… (a maxim)

There are two things appreciated only by those who lose them: youth, and well-being.

There are two things known only by those from whom they are taken: power and wealth.

There are two situations that do not turn out well: sickness and a needy, hungry relative.

There are two things that never end favorably: oppression and vice.

There are two things difficult to reach the extreme of: knowledge and intellect (*ʿaql*).

The reward of two things cannot be weighed: forgiveness and justice.

Two things are foundations of religion: truth and faith.

There are two things that cannot be equaled: good piety and good faith.

The people of our community are like oranges: their fragrance is delicate and pure, their outward appearance most fair.

## Questions to the Prophet and His Answers

A man approached the Prophet (ṣallā Allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam) with questions concerning the world.

"O Messenger of God, I wish to be the greatest of all people!"

The Prophet replied, "Fear God, and you shall become the greatest of all people." Through fear of God and adherence to His revealed path, the doors of knowledge and wisdom open to humanity.

"O Messenger of God, I wish to be the greatest of all people!"

The Prophet said, "The greatest person in the world is one who benefits others." Strive to bring benefit to all.

"O Messenger of God, I desire to become learned and wise!"

The Prophet said, "Do unto others as you would have them do unto you."[^cite-31]

"O Messenger of God, I wish to be nearest to God!"

He said, "Remember God abundantly, and you shall draw near to Him."

"O Messenger of God, I wish to become virtuous and beneficial!"

He said, "Pray as though you see God; and if you cannot achieve that, pray knowing that God sees you."[^cite-32]

"O Messenger of God, I want my faith to be complete!"

He said, "Perfect your character and manners, and your faith shall be perfected."

"O Messenger of God, I wish to be obedient to God!"

He said, "Fulfill your obligations continuously, and you shall be counted among the obedient."

"O Messenger of God, I desire to meet God Almighty free of all sin!"

He said, "You shall be purified of sin through the blessing of ablution (*wuḍūʾ*)."[^cite-33]

"O Messenger of God, it is my wish to be raised with light on the Day of Resurrection!"

He said, "Do no wrong, and you shall be raised with light on the Day of Resurrection."

"O Messenger of God, I wish God to have mercy on me!"

He said, "Have mercy on yourself and on God's creation, and God shall have mercy on you."

"O Messenger of God, I want my sins reduced!"

He said, "Recite *istighfār* (seeking forgiveness) with exercise, and your sins shall diminish."

"O Messenger of God, I wish to be the greatest among people!"

He said, "Do not complain to God in times of hardship, and you shall become the greatest."

"O Messenger of God, I wish for my sustenance to be blessed!"

He said, "Always be clean and pure, and your sustenance shall be blessed."

"O Messenger of God, I wish to be a friend of God and His Messenger!"

He said, "Love what God loves, and despise what God despises."

"O Messenger of God, I want to escape God's wrath!"

He said, "Do not anger at anyone, and you shall be saved from God's wrath."

"O Messenger of God, I wish to have my supplication answered in God's court!"

He said, "Avoid the forbidden and the doubtful, and your supplication shall be answered."

"O Messenger of God, I wish that God not disgrace me before all on the Day of Resurrection!"

He said, "Guard your private parts, and God shall shield you from disgrace on the Day of Resurrection."[^cite-34]

## The Mothers of Sins — Pride (Kibr), Envy (Ḥasad), and Greed (Ḥirṣ)

⟪ar:اخلاق محمودہ⟫ ⟪ar:اخلاق مضمومہ⟫ ⟪ar:اخلاق مضمومہ⟫ ⟪ar:اخلاق محمودہ⟫

Character (akhlāq) is singular in its endurance: four things persist in the body even after it departs:

| One | Character that becomes habitual within it. |
|---|---|
| Two | The sciences one has learned. |
| Three | The beliefs one has established. |
| Four | The actions accomplished through one's own effort and intention. |

In the satanic character, the first is pride (⟪ar:تکبر⟫), epitomized by Iblīs; the second is greed (⟪ar:حرص⟫), exemplified in ʿĀdam; and the third is envy (⟪ar:حسد⟫), manifested in Qābīl (Cain). These three traits are the root of all sins and evils.[^cite-35]

### Signs of Pride (Arrogance):

| One | Preferring one's own opinion. |
|---|---|
| Two | Rejecting truth and refusing to acknowledge it. |
| Three | Disobeying the command of one whose command is obligatory. |
| Four | Stubbornness and transgressing one's proper limits. |
| Five | Oppression and coercion in governance. |
| Six | Unfairness in matters and taking lightly what is obligatory. |
| Seven | Abusing people and addressing them with contempt. |
| Eight | Harshness, haughtiness, deceit, and cunning in dealings. |
| Nine | Displaying one's greatness through the pretense of forbearance. |
| Ten | Denial of the virtue of grace. |

Whoever holds to the arrogance of (⟪ar:تکبر⟫) is an enemy of obedience. Obedience is the supreme Name of God by which the heavens and the earth were established. ⟪ar:کبر⟫ is the yielding to truth and accepting it. It has been said, "Whoever humbles himself before God, God shall exalt him; and whoever is arrogant, God shall humble him." God (subḥānahu wa-taʿālā) says: "Majesty is My cloak and Greatness is My robe. Whoever contends with Me in one of these shall I torment."[^cite-36] And in the Qurʾān, God says, "Is not Hellfire the abode of the arrogant?"[^cite-37]

### Signs of Greed (⟪ar:حرص⟫):

| One | Yearning falsely for things. |
|---|---|
| Two | Running in the sun to gather provisions. |
| Three | Hoarding excessively, stinginess, and inferiority born of fear of dependence. |
| Four | Not making use of what one possesses and preventing others from benefiting. |
| Five | Rigidity in transactions, miscalculation, distrust of the truthful, and slander of the virtuous. |
| Six | Betrayal of trust, unlawful earning, and committing evil deeds. |
| Seven | Lying to conceal the truth. |
| Eight | Making excuses in transactions. |
| Nine | Overstepping the bounds in enmity. |
| Ten | False oaths. |

### Signs of Envy (⟪ar:حسد⟫):

| One | Harboring malice and deceit, then expressing them when venting enmity. |
|---|---|
| Two | Anger, ruthlessness, harshness, cursing, and talking nonsense. |
| Three | Creating causes of conflict, openly or covertly. |
| Four | Backbiting, employing lies and slander. |

Greed leads to deprivation; the envious person is an enemy of God's blessings; the envious one gains nothing but envy itself. These traits and deeds ought to be shunned, for they are the character of devils. Those who hate one another are the army of Iblīs. God has decreed in the Qurʾān that when one group enters Hellfire, it curses the other.[^cite-38]

From this becomes clear: pride, envy, and greed are the roots of all evils, the wellsprings of corruption and mischief. They sow discord among people, plant enmity in hearts, and sever relationships. Distance grows between brothers; love disappears. Households are destroyed; sorrow, grief, and bitterness take root in hearts. The soul is tormented; life becomes forbidden; both this world and the next are ruined. May God protect us from these evils.[^cite-39]

## Ṣadaqa — Charity

Everything that we separate from ourselves with virtuous intention, that which we withdraw from our abundance with a sincere heart—this is charity (*ṣadaqa*). Should we separate ourselves from our sins, that becomes our charity. Charity is accomplished in three ways:

| One | The highest charity is the obligatory prayer performed in the presence of God. |
|---|---|
| Two | To pray for another is also charity. Obligations are reserved for God alone, yet supererogatory deeds (*nawāfil*) may be offered for His servants as well as for Him. |
| Three | Everything known is charity. When Mawlā ʿAlī was asked, "What is a kind deed (*maʿrūf*)?" he replied: "Everything that a believer conveys is known."[^cite-40] That is, every good deed of a believer is charity. |

**Recognition of Charity:**

Imām Jaʿfar (ʿalayhi al-salām) taught that charity can be fulfilled through three qualities: *Taṣghīr* (diminishment), *Tassīr* (ease), and *Tajīl* (hastening).

| *Taṣghīr* | To diminish one's good deeds so that one remains humble before God. One should offer one's prayer to God with utmost humility. |
|---|---|
| *Tassīr* | To perform it with ease. After a good deed is done, one should not complain or publicize it. When we offer our worship to God, we should understand it as His due and ourselves as in need of His acceptance. Whether He accepts our prayer or not, we must pray. Whether others acknowledge our effort or not, we must strive. |
| *Tajīl* | To hasten to perform the good deed. |

**Distinction:**

When an elder gives something to a younger, it is called a gift (*hadiyya*). When the younger gives to the elder, it is called a reward (*thanāʾ*), for through this gift, the younger connects himself to the higher station of the elder.

## The Marks of a Person of Intellect (ʿAql)

Mawlā ʿAlī says that a person's intellect (*ʿaql*) is known through three things:

| *Al-Hudiyya* (The Gift) | A person's gift reveals his intellect—whether what is given is useful to the recipient and shall benefit him. |
|---|---|
| *Al-Rasūl* (The Messenger) | The person through whom one sends a message. Is the messenger capable of representing the sender's intentions? |
| *Al-Kitāb* (The Writing) | A person's manner of speech and expression reveals his intellect. |

The Messenger of God (may peace be upon him) said, "I have given my *Umma* three gifts: ⟪ar:**نماز**⟫ (prayer), I have sent a Messenger who is **⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫** (ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib), and I have granted a Book which is the **⟪ar:قرآن مجید⟫** (Qurʾān, the Noble Reading)."[^cite-41]

## Q&A with Ḥaḍrat Isḥāq Ṣāḥib

*Bismillāh ar-Raḥmān ar-Raḥīm* — In the name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate.

Ḥaḍrat Mawlānā Isḥāq Ṣāḥib was questioned by Ghāzī Hashim Yar Jung (who served as a judge of the High Court during the reign of the Nizām) and inscribed the responses in an album of preference. Below are those questions and answers:

**What is your favorite quality?**

Resurrecting the dead of ignorance.

**What trait in a man do you most admire?**

Worship of God, knowledge, and virtue.

**What trait in a woman most appeals to you?**

Chastity, devotion to her husband, and grace.

**What is your most favored pursuit?**

That which pleases God and His Messenger in this world.

**What is your most personal characteristic?**

Recognition of oneself.

**What constitutes happiness for you?**

Being called righteous by creation, beside God.

**What brings you sorrow?**

Virtue in the created, yet evil in the sight of the Creator.

**Your favorite color and precious stone?**

White; pearls and emeralds.

**If you were not yourself, whom would you wish to be?**

My grandfather, Ḥaḍrat Mawlānā Zīʿ ʿAlī Ṣāḥib ibn al-Massūn, and Shaykh Ibrāhīm Ṣāḥib.

**Where would you most like to dwell?**

In the neighborhood of my Lord.

**Your most favored prose author?**

Ḥaḍrat Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib.

**Your most favored poet?**

Ḥaḍrat Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib.

**Your most favored painter/sculptor?**

The Creator of all nature (*Muṣawwir al-fiṭra*).

**Your most favored vocalist/singer?**

The melody of Prophet Dāʾūd (ʿalayhi al-salām).

**Your most favored man?**

Ḥaḍrat ʿAlī and Imām Ḥusayn (may God's peace and blessings be upon them).

**Your most favored woman?**

Our Mistress Khadīja and Our Mistress Fāṭima al-Zahrāʾ (bint Aḥmad).

**Your most favored tale?**

The story of Joseph and Zulaykhā.

**Your most favored food and drink?**

Paneer (fresh cheese) and *chāch* (buttermilk), and whatever God provides.

**Your most favored name?**

Muḥammad and ʿAlī; and the names Yaʿqūb, Ṭāhira, and ʿĀmir.

**What do you most despise?**

Self-aggrandizement beyond one's station.

**Which historical figures do you most dislike?**

Ibn Muljam, Yazīd al-Layth, and every oppressor of the faith.

**What is the present state of your heart?**

Trembling with fear of God.

**Which error do you most readily forgive?**

Every error that lies within my capacity to forgive.

**What is your most beloved saying?**

"Whoever places trust in God, God suffices him."

*10th of Jumādā al-Awwal, 1357 Hijri / 12th July, 1938 CE*

*The date of his appointment to this office was the 3rd of Rabīʿ al-Ākhir, 1357 Hijri, when he was approximately 38 years old. It is possible these questions were posed after the announcement of his appointment.*
