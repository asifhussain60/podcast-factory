## Section 3553

اس آیت سے معلوم ہوتا ہے کہ اللہ تعالی نے اپنی نشانیوں کو آفاق اور انفس، یعنی ظاہر (آفاق)  و باطن (انفس) سے واضح کیا ہے۔ اس نے اپنے دین کا پایہ دنیا کے اصولیات پر رکھا ہے۔ جس طرح ایک مریض، طبیب کے ذریعہ سے اپنے مرض کو دور کرتا ہے اور صحت یاب ہوتا ہے، اسی طرح اللہ ایک نفسانی بیمار کے لئے اللہ تعالی نے ایک نفسانی طبیب قائم کیا ہے جو اس کے نفوس کو صحت عطا کرتا ہے۔ 

جسمانی مریض کے لئے لازم ہے کہ وہ اپنے علاج کے لئے کسی ماہر طبیب کی طرف رجوع کرے۔ اس سے اپنے مرض کا کلی طور پر، بغیر کوئی بات چھپائے ذکر کرے اور اس سے علاج کی درخواست کرے۔ جب طبیب اس کے مرض کو دور کرنے کے لئے اسے دوائی بتائے تو اس پر لازم ہے کہ وقت پر اس دوا کو صحیح مقدار میں لیتا رہے۔ اسی طرح نفس کے بیمار کو چاہئے کہ وہ اہل بیت سے استفادہ کرنے والے علماء اور بزرگان دین  کا دامن تھامے جو اس کے شکوک کو مختلف علوم سے دور کرنے کی طاقت رکھتے ہیں۔ ان سے اپنے تمام شکوک کی ہر نوعیت اور قسم کو واضح کر دے کہ آیا وہ ولایت میں شک کرتا ہے یا احکام شریعت میں، یا غیب پر ایمان لانے میں اس کو شک ہے۔  اور جس طرح حکیم مریض کی نبض کو سمجھ کر علاج کرتا ہے، عالم دین کو چاہئے کہ مریض کے کلام سے، جو نبض کے محل میں ہے، اور اس کی طرز ادا اور بیان سے جو قارورۃ کا ممثول ہے، مریض کے مرض کی شناخت کرے اور پھر اس کا علاج کرے۔

طبیب کے لئے یہ ضروری ہے کہ وہ اپنے فن میں ماہر بھی ہو اور مامون (confident) بھی۔ یعنی مریض کو خواہ مخواہ چند پیسوں اور معاشی فائدہ کے لئے غلط مشورہ نہ دے اور غلط علاج  نہ کرے، اور اگر کوئی بات سمجھ میں نہ آتی ہو تو اناپ شناپ بک کر مریض کے مرض میں اضافہ نہ کرے۔ اسی طرح عالم دین پر لازم ہے کہ تھوڑی سی دنیاوی تعریف، یا شہرت کے لئے اپنے نفسانی مریض کے شکوک کو بڑھانے والی باتیں نہ کرے۔ ہر عالم دین کو اپنے علم میں پختہ، مضبوط اور ماہر ہونے کے ساتھ مریض کے ساتھ محبت، شفقت اور نرمی کرنا بھی آنا چاہئے۔

طبیب کا خود صحت مند ہونا لازم ہے۔ وہ خود ایسے مرض میں مبتلا نہ ہو جو مریض کی نبض دیکھنے میں حائل ہو، اور وہ اندھا نہ ہو کہ مریض کے قارورۃ کا معائنہ نہ کر سکے۔ اسی طرح عالم دین کے عقائد پختہ اور صحیح ہونا چاہئے۔ اس کا تصور صحیح اور خیالات صحت مند ہوں۔ خود ایسے شکوک میں مبتلا نہ ہو جو اس کو متحیر اور سرگرداں کر دے بلکہ اس کا دماغ صاف اور اس قدر تیز ہونا چاہئے کہ اپنے مریض کا کلام سنتے ہی سمجھ لے کہ کون سی رگ دکھتی ہے۔ عقیدہ کی خرابی کہاں ہے اور کس کی پیدا کی ہوئی ہے۔ صحت مند طبیب صحیح نباض ہوتا ہے۔ اسی طرح صحیح عالم، حقیقیت شناس ہوتا ہے۔

مریض کو چاہئے کہ طبیب جو دوائی تجویز کرے، اسے صحیح وقت پر اور صحیح مقدار میں استمرار کے ساتھ لے، اور جن چیزوں سے پرہیز بتائے ان سے پرہیز کرے، اور بیماری کے دور ہونے تک صابر رہے۔ اسی طرح نفسانی مریض کو چاہئے کہ عالم دین اس کو جن اعمال کی نصیحیت کرے چاہے وہ احکام شریعت سے ہوں یا کلام حکمت سے، ان پر بغیر چون و چرا باقاعدگی کے ساتھ عمل کرے اور جن برائیوں کو ترک کرنے کے لئے کہے ان کی چھوڑ دے اور اس راہ میں صبر سے کام لے، یہاں تک کہ مرض دور ہو جائے اور وہ صحیت یاب ہو جائے۔

اگر مریض اور طبیب میں یہ تمام صفات موجود ہونگے تو علاج اچھا ہوگا۔ تنزیلی اور تاویلی دونوں علاج اثرانداز ہونگے اور  کارگر ثابت ہونگے۔

## Section 1340

*[1] Now it has been narrated to us that a number of the truly faithful (⟪ar:مؤمنین⟫) and a group among those who call people to true religion (⟪ar:دعوت⟫) once said to one of their scholars: ‘You have liberated us by helping us learn about an affair (⟪ar:الامر⟫)’ of great importance, for which we feel obligated to show our gratitude for three reasons:*

1. Our gratitude for you having called us to the true religion (⟪ar:دین⟫)
2. *Our gratitude for the knowledge (⟪ar:علم⟫) that you directed us to*
3. *Our gratitude for the religious practices (⟪ar:عمل⟫) you ordered us to perform.*

*So inform us how we should express our gratitude for these three reasons. Then inform us about:*

- the rights (⟪ar:حقوق⟫) and duties that are obligatory on us as directed by the ordinances of religion (⟪ar:حدود دین⟫);
- *and the obligations of a **seeker**in his mode of **questioning**,*
- *and the obligations on the **responses**of the **scholar**being sought.*

*And inform us, to the best of your capabilities about the ways (⟪ar:مذاہب⟫) of the righteous (⟪ar:صالحین⟫) and the proper behavior (⟪ar:آداب⟫) of the seekers.’*

[2] The scholar replied: ‘Now the affair to which I invite you all, is the religion that Allah has bestowed as an honor on His servants; which He has perfected for them and through which He honors whoever responds to Him. So for each beginning (⟪ar:أول⟫), He has placed in it an end (⟪ar:آخر⟫), and for each end, a goal (⟪ar:نہایۃ⟫); and each goal has a limit (⟪ar:غایۃ⟫) whose full extent cannot be attained. These are the way-stations (⟪ar:منازل⟫) of the people of true understanding. Their key is constantly remembering Allah (⟪ar:ذکر اللہ⟫), their **initial**levels are trials (⟪ar:اولھا بلوی⟫); the **middle** ones are of right guidance (⟪ar:اوسطھا ھدی⟫) and the **final**ones are piety (⟪ar:آخرھا تقوی⟫). So whoever is seeking to learn the basics of Allah's affairs, and then discovers the ranks of the divinely determined (⟪ar:حدود الدین المقدور⟫), he has sought guidance from the right sources and has set upon it in the best possible manner.
- As for showing gratitude to the scholar (who guided you), that is through his **obedience**;
- and the gratitude for the knowledge (he gave you), is **turning it into action** and calling others to it;
- and the gratitude for the right practice (he taught you), is **striving patiently** (⟪ar:صبر⟫) in it and in calling others to it.

*[3] As for teaching you the **ways of the righteous** (⟪ar:مذاہب الصالحین⟫) and the**proper behavior** (⟪ar:آداب الطالبین⟫) of the seekers, the following story has been narrated to us about a man from Persia (⟪ar:فارس⟫) who was among those subject to the trial of spiritual ignorance. Although he had a rich heart and a brilliant intellect, and had acquired an agreeable culture and education, nonetheless his ignorance outweighed his knowledge, because of his earlier experiences and the environment in which he grew up. So he was restless in the burning heat of his thirst, assuming the glimmering of mirages to be the reflection of water, until he reached it, and found it to be nothing. However, there he found Allah, and paid Him his account in full! Then Allah honored his abode and removed the veil in front of his intellect. He found him straying, so He guided him; He found him in need, so He satisfied him with knowledge. Thus by His graces he eventually became one of the scholars of Sinai (⟪ar:الطور⟫) and the angelic dwellers in the well-populated temple (⟪ar:بیت المعمور⟫)*

*[4] Finally when his guidance had been completed and he reached the goals of his aspiration, he was duty-bound to show gratitude to his Master and to exert himself for his Lord. He used to hear his spiritual father repeat a proverb, which held the rank of soul to his body: ‘The most excellent of deeds is giving life to the dead (⟪ar:ان افضل الحسنات أحیا الأموات⟫)’   So he said to himself: ‘I used to be dead, and he gave me life; I was ignorant, and he gave me knowledge. I am not the first person to ever be ignorant, such that I attained knowledge before everyone else; nor am I the last one to be ignorant, such that the process of spiritual teaching has come to an end on me. Therefore out of gratitude for this blessing, it is only appropriate for me to pass on this divine trust to those who come after me; just as those who preceded me have handed it down to me. For the origin of this affair is from Allah, and it only reached me through its many intermediaries (⟪ar:اسباب⟫), the first passing it on to the second, the second passing it on to the third, until it descended from the heavenly host (⟪ar:الملا الأعلی⟫) to the creatures of this lower world. After that, did it tear asunder the veil (⟪ar:خرق الحجاب⟫) between Allah and His creations, and did the ‘gateways’ (⟪ar:ابواب⟫) pass it onward until it reached me, only so that I could be its final goal and ultimate end? Not at all! For those who have transmitted it and handed on this trust before me are more deserving of precedence and spiritual gains! **What they have earned does not belong to me, so that I could rely on it. No! I (myself) am part of what they have earned through their acts (of teaching me).**Nor does their precedence relieve me from having to act! So now I need to strive to spread knowledge, just as I needed to strive to seek knowledge in the beginning.’ He realized that he is now obligated to pass on his spiritual understanding to others, just as his master was obligated to teach him, and that his final duty was similar to his initial duty (⟪ar:وأن الآخر أمرہ فی الحکم مثل الاول⟫).*

[5] *So he travelled towards his Lord leaving his people and possessions behind, calling people to the good (*⟪ar:خیر داعیاً⟫*), in order to deserve a gratitude, similar to what was incumbent upon him for his master. He started traveling through countries, passing among the non-Arab (*⟪ar:عجم⟫*) and the Arab tribes, scrutinizing their faces for signs of spiritual aptitude and posing religious questions. But he did not get any response, nor did he meet anyone seeking knowledge, until he ended up in the furthest part of the jazira. There he entered a city where nobody knew him; and while he was recalling Allah’s blessings and searching various gatherings, he noticed a group of people from the town who were discussing religion without any guidance, and recklessly following the inclinations of their passions. He sat nearby, out of sight, listening carefully to their discussion and analyzing them carefully to determine which of them was closer to the right path.*

[6] Then he said to himself: ‘These people are more deserving of life (spiritual knowledge) for three reasons:
1. First, because the true divine argument (⟪ar:حجۃ⟫) has reached them;
2. Second, because they belong to the people of my community (⟪ar:الملۃ⟫);’
3. and third, because they are closer to the right path shown by their keen interest in religion and the fact that they are inquiring about it and discussing it —for the person who is already searching for something is much closer to finding it.’

[7] [The narrator] *So when those people completed their discussion, they turned to the scholar and asked: "Who are you, and where are you from, O youth* (⟪ar:یا فتی⟫)?’ 

[8] ‘I am Abdullah and I am among the residents of His sanctuary,’ he replied.

[9] What is your business here and what is your work?’ they asked.

[10] ‘My business has completed,’ he replied, ‘and as for work, I am looking for it.’

[11] ‘Well then,’ they said, did you find anything particularly interesting in our discussion?”

[12] ‘All of it was interesting for the person who would be interested,’ he answered.

[13] ‘But the word “interesting” has two meanings,’ they said, ‘a commendable one and a reprehensible one....’

[14] ‘Of course!’ he said, ‘and similarly all "speech" can be commendable or reprehensible.’    

[15] They inquired: ‘Then according to you, what is commendable speech?’

[16] ‘Speech that is true,’ he replied.

[17] ‘And what kind is that?’ they asked.

[18] ‘Speech, whose origin comes from Allah and through which people are called towards Allah,’ he answered.

[19] ‘Then what,’ they asked, ‘is the reprehensible speech?’

[20] He replied: ‘Speech that which is based on passion (⟪ar:ھوی⟫), and is used to call people to something other than true guidance.’

[21] ‘You have spoken truly!’ they said. ‘So won’t you share some of your words with us?’

[22] ‘I have no words of my own,’ he responded. ‘For I am following the words of another!’ .

[23] ‘Then let us hear you say something that will inform us about your particular religious ways’ they said. ‘For you have made a good impression on us, and we were pleased by your brotherly concern, so now we ask you to become a gateway for us, through which Allah may send His loving kindness for us.  

True Knowledge

[24] ‘All right,’ he replied. *He stood up, facing the attentive group and began...*

[25] ‘Verily He **created**with His power, then **arranged**everything perfectly! Who **determined**a path through His generosity, then **guided**! It is He who brought forth paths of the pastureland (⟪ar:شوارع المرعی⟫), then made its outer form a dried-up brush (⟪ar:ظاہرہ غثا⟫) and its inner aspect broad in scope and content (⟪ar:وباطنہ أحوی⟫)! whose ways are not obscure (⟪ar:لا ملتبس السبل⟫) and whose traces will never be obliterated (⟪ar:ولا مندرس الآثار⟫)—for if it were so, then intellects would fail to seek it, and would despair reaching it. His knowledge does not exist outwardly, so that the unworthy could not possess and alter it. Instead it is benevolent and well-protected (⟪ar:کریم مغمود⟫)! Because of its excellence, it is well concealed, and its secrets are well-guarded for those worthy of them! Its dignity (⟪ar:شرف⟫) is the splendid robe of guidance, and its perfection is the garment of piety (⟪ar:لباس التقوی⟫)! It is marked out by the light of lights (⟪ar:نور الانوار⟫), entrusted safely in the hearts of the best and chosen ones - the truly pious, guardians, protectors, generous and recorders! This knowledge is a trust which they safely pass on, and do not transgress the divine ordinances. It is the law of the Supreme Judge containing all the benefits of the All-Merciful! He honored it by making it inward (⟪ar:باطن⟫), and He protected it by keeping it secret. He veiled it with His obligations, and crowned it with His covenants. For it cannot be reached by the aspirations of those who rely on the strength of their intellects (⟪ar:فلا تنالہ ھمم المتفکرین⟫), nor can it be attained by those who are scheming for it (⟪ar:ولا تبلغہ فکر المحتالین⟫). Its outer aspect is a reminder and a sign for those who are rightly guided, while its inner aspect is a sweet drink gushing forth.

His lofty heavens come down close, and his earth is arranged for those who inquire about Him and His guiding stars are arranged for those who seek Him. He has watering-places for those who move towards Him, and signs (⟪ar:آیات⟫), whose wonders follow each other in succession, pointing to Him through their symbols. His accustomed way is preferment (⟪ar:سیرتہ تفضیل⟫), and He is approached through distinguished ranks (⟪ar:ومنھاجہ تفصیل⟫). ‘His duties are obligatory (⟪ar:أحکامہ فرائض⟫) and His accustomed ways are paths (to be walked on) (⟪ar:وسنتہ شرائع⟫). **Sincerity ennobles his obligations, and justice purifies the efforts made for him**. For He has made his ways (Shariah) a guide and a path leading to good, with its outer aspect a protective shell, and a sign pointing toward its inner dimensions. Intellects are forced by their neediness and ignorance to seek knowledge and open abundance. So blessed be the One Who placed the night and the day in succession, for those who reflect and those who wish to be grateful! May Allah’s blessings be on the one who He has specially chosen from His servants, and made a warner (⟪ar:نذیر⟫) for all the worlds!’

بےشک اللہ نے اپنی قدرت سے (اس علم کو) پیدا کیا، اور اسے بہترین ترتیب عطا کی! اس نے اپنی سخاوت سے راستہ کا تعین کیا اور اس کی طرف ہدایت دی! وہی تھا جس نے چارہ گاہوں (شریعت) کو پیدا کیا، پھر اس کے ظاہر (عمل) کو خشک اور کھردرا کر دیا اور اس کے باطن (علم) کو کشادہ اور مواد میں وسیع کیا! جس کے طریقے مبہم نہیں ہیں اور جس کے آثار کبھی برباد نہیں ہونگے! کیونکہ اگر ایسا ہوتا تو عقلیں اسے حاصل کرنے سے ناکام، اور اسے پانے سے عاجز ہو جاتیں۔ اس نے اپنے علم کو ظاہر میں نہیں رکھا کہ نا اہل لوگ اس کے مالک بن بیٹھیں اور اسے تبدیل کر دیں؛ بلکہ اس کا علم کریم اور (ظاہر کے خول میں) محفوظ ہے۔ اپنے کمال کی وجہ سے وہ انتہائی پوشیدہ ہے اور اس کے اسرار استحقاق رکھنے والوں کے لئے محفوظ ہیں۔ اس (علم) کا شرف، ہدایت کا عظیم الشان لباس ہے، اور اس کا کمال، اس کے تقوی کا لباس ہے۔ وہ نور الانوار سے نمایاں ہے جو بہترین اور منتخب لوگوں کے دلوں میں محفوظ ہے۔ ایسے لوگ، جو متقی، سر پرست، محافظ اور حساب رکھنے والے ہیں۔ یہ علوم ایک امانت ہے جسے وہ حفاظت سے آگے منتقل کرتے رہتے ہیں اور الہی حدود کی خلاف ورزی نہیں کرتے۔ یہ اعلی ترین حاکم کا قانون ہے جس میں ⟪ar:رحمٰن⟫کی تمام نعمتیں موجود ہیں۔ اللہ نے ان علوم کو باطن میں چھپا کر انہیں کرامت دی، اور انہیں راز بنا کر ان کی حفاظت فرمائی۔ اس نے ان علوم پر اپنے فرائض کے پردے چڑھائے اور انہیں اپنے عہد کا تاج پہنایا۔ وہ لوگ جو اپنی عقل پر اعتماد کرتے ہیں ہرگز ان علوم تک نہیں پہنچ سکتے، اور نہ ہی وہ لوگ انہیں پا سکتے ہیں جو ان کے حصول کے لئے تدبیر کر رہے ہیں۔  اس کا ظاہر، ہدایت پانے والوں کے لئے ایک یاد دہانی اور نشان ہیں؛ اور اس کا باطن موجیں مارتا ہوا میٹھا شربت ہے!

اس کے بلند آسمان تشویش کرنے والوں کے لئے جھکائے گئے ہیں، اور اس کی زمین ان کے لئے سجائی گئی ہے؛ اور اس کی ہدایت کے ستارے اس کو تلاش کرنے والوں کے لئے روشن کئے گئے ہیں۔ اس کی طرف بڑھنے والوں کے لئے اللہ نے پانی کے گھاٹ (ائمہ) تیار کئے ہیں۔ یہ ایسی نشانیاں ہیں جو ایک کے بعد ایک مسلسل ظاہر ہوتی رہتی ہیں، اور اپنی علامتوں کے ذریعے اس کی طرف اشارہ کرتی ہیں۔ درجات میں ترقی اس کا معروف طریقہ ہے جس پر اہل مراتب کے ذریعے چلا جاتا ہے۔ اس کے احکامات فرض ہیں اور اس کی شریعت سنت ہے۔ ان احکامات کی ادائگی میں **اخلاص**انہیں شرف عطا کرتا ہے، اور ان کو پورا کرنے میں **انصاف**برتنا، ان کے لئے کی جانے والی کوششوں کو پاک کرتا ہے۔ اس نے اپنی شریعت کو خیر کا رہنما اور راستہ بنایا ہے۔ پھر اس کے ظاہر کو ایسا محفوظ خول بنایا، جو اس کے باطنی طول و عرض کی طرف اشارہ کرتا ہے۔ عقلیں اپنی حاجت اور جہالت کی تنگی کی وجہ سے اس علم کی وسعت کو تلاش کرنے پر مجبور ہیں۔ بس مبارک ہے وہ اللہ جس نے غور و فکر اور شکرگزار بننے والوں کے لئے رات اور دن کو ایک دوسرے کے پیچھے ترتیب دی۔ اور اللہ کی رحمت ہو اس ذات مبارک پر جسے اللہ نے تمام عالمین کو خبردار کرنے کے لئے اپنے بندوں میں سے منتخب کیا!

[26] Then he said: ‘Be mindful of Allah like the one who fears Him; and fear Him like the one who is hoping to please Him! Seek **knowledge**and you will **learn**; **Act**according to it and you will receive Allah’s loving **mercy**(⟪ar:واطلبوا العلم تعلموا، واعملوا ترحموا⟫)!’ 

[27] *Then his eyes overflowed with tears and he stopped talking and bid the group farewell. Still weeping, he got up and walked toward his travel baggage. All in the group were overcome with emotions by the intensity of his speech and the effectiveness of his sermon. They wanted to accompany him, but he prevented them from doing so. Since they didn’t know what else to do, they all returned to their homes. But a young man from the group, who was the youngest of them, yet had the best character, accompanied him. He had completed the education of his intellect, and reflection had awakened his heart. His thoughts coincided with, and were reinforced by what he heard in the scholar’s speech - both in what he had presented and what he had alluded to. This young man's father was a great Sheikh among the most distinguished people of Arab by lineage and was one of the most devoted supportees of the people of his religion. With the scholar's permission, the young man accompanied him until they reached his (the scholar's) inn. The scholar entered and invited the young man in. He then ordered food, and when they finished eating and were comfortable enough to talk,*

[28] The young man said: ‘O wise man (⟪ar:حکیم⟫)! You spoke and we heard! You preached with eloquence and adorned religion with the most beautiful of ornaments, and motivated us with the most beautiful of motivations and the noblest of preparations. You called us through the most eminent of responsibilities, enticing our minds to enquire from you about all that has struck our hearts as a result of your speech. Such excellence could only manifest through one whose knowledge has perfected internally and whose character has completely developed externally, making him a source of wisdom! You are like a tree... its roots firm, its splendor towering over the worlds, its fruits hanging low from its branches for those who seek them. So I ask you to be one of those blessed branches for me, from what I can see of your distinguished judgment, your native aptitude and your perfect intellect. You mentioned that our minds are forced by their ignorance to seek knowledge and abundance. Now my mind is the most needy and indignent of all, so is there any way to life for me?’

[29] The scholar replied: Surely the One Who created by His power, did not create without purpose, nor did He create them entirely knowledgeable. He did not leave them wandering in ignorance without end, nor did He want them to adopt foolishness as their behavior, or wear the garments of ignorance. So He established a way out for them by means of an "instrument" (intellect) around which He shaped their constitution (⟪ar:الف بھا ترکیب⟫) and by which He perfected their creation (⟪ar:وأکمل بھا خلقھم⟫). He made their **hearing, eyes and hearts**, an openings to accept the light of His knowledge as an act of generosity toward them, and by it completed His argument for them. He then placed the treasures of His wisdom and the specific characteristics of His wishes that pleased Him (⟪ar:مذاہب⟫) in the pure, chosen, elite of His creation. He gave them the responsibility of worshipping Him according to those ways, and calling others to those ways, while imposing on people the duty of responding to their call by using their instrument (intellect), so that He may distinguish the wicked from the good. Hence the scholar will reveal himself plainly through his seeking of that knowledge, while the ignorant will be distinguished by his falling behind. Therefore there is no argument against Allah after His messengers, nor is there any excuse for straying after the right path has been clearly pointed out.’    

[30] The young man replied: My lack of excuse has frightened me, and the existence of signs pointing to the right path makes me desire setting out on that search; so I ask you to be a guide, and I'll be your humble servant’ 

[31] The scholar answered: ‘Surely Allah did not create His creatures as fully grown scholars. He created them as infants, small and ignorant, not knowing anything, so that they grow big after having been small, and come to know after having been ignorant. Thus their being small is a precondition for their becoming big, and their ignorance is a sign pointing toward knowledge. The same is true of spiritual knowledge. One can only attain its essence on the basis of preceding, lower forms of knowledge, which hearts can quickly assimilate and intellect can easily grasp. And so that the first stage of spiritual growth is established in their minds, and they are humbled enough to carry out the required duties. This opens up their hearts to the essence of that knowledge and enable their intellects to bear its goal. That is Allah's accustomed way regarding His servants! So we neither need to depart from the opinions of religious communities, nor do we need to follow anything other than the accustomed way to be guided.’ 

[32] ‘But I am an infant in relation to what I wish to achieve on this path,’ the young man said, ‘so raise and educate me! I am ignorant with regard to what I have been seeking, so teach me and help me learn. I am forced to rely on your gracious favor, so have mercy on me! Surely you were once in this state, and Allah brought His bounties to you through the hands of the one who became so important for you, and to whom you owe such gratitude! So you already understand the state of someone like myself’, caught in the vice of ignorance and so needy, that he will desperately resort to any way out.’   

[33] The scholar said: ‘I myself was once in the state you’ve described, and I surely wish to spare you the desperation. But my compassion towards you will not bring you closer to what you hope for without your "working" for it! For just as every word has a proper employment (⟪ar:تصریف⟫) that can only be accepted if it is sincere and true, similary **knowledge**is of no benefit until it has been put into **action**; and action is of no benefit until it is **accepted**by Allah. Now the true and sincere form of speech is that, which is just. And the signs pointing to knowledge are the revealed paths of righteous acts, and faithfully conforming to the right models throughout all that is encompassed by the furthermost regions and the variation of day and night. So do not seek knowledge impatiently, and do not dismiss it out of negligence! Nor should you accept it out of blind imitation, without any convincing argument — for in such a case it would slip out of your heart at the first onset of doubt. But do not deny the argument once it has been firmly established, or you will become the pleader for all that is false. And do not stop inquiring about that knowledge and reflecting upon it, or your judgment will become sterile. You must **trust**me without prejudice, and make every effort to understand without confusion, seeking the truth and asking Allah’s help for it — for Allah does not neglect a recompense, nor does He forbid reward if He sees you seeking for the good!’

[34] ‘Surely you have spoken most justly, accomplishing great good, and you have pointed the way to the truth with the most beautiful of words,’ said the young man. ‘But now I am seeking Allah’s help, and I am asking you about that affair to which you are calling and specifically referring to. What is it? Who does it come from? and to Who does it lead?’

[35] ‘That is Allah’s religion which He has approved!’ replied the scholar. ‘It is from Him, so He will accept none other! Specifically, it leads to the pure elite among His messengers, and generally to all of His creation. So the person who has been singled out for His favor should be grateful for it, and put it into practice, and call others to it. But the person who has been excluded (from that knowledge) should keep seeking it until he comes to know of it, and should willingly obey whoever is guiding him.’ 

[36] ‘Do you think,’ asked the young man, ‘that this favor by which Allah has singled out His friends is at the same level, such that they are all equal in its excellence? Or do some of them have a rank of excellence (⟪ar:فضل⟫) over others?’   

[37] ‘Haven’t I already mentioned,’ said the scholar, ‘that every knowledge has a preceding stage of knowledge below it, and that for every favor there are “bearers” (⟪ar:حملۃ⟫)? So they have ranks of honor with their Lord; each entrusted with what he has earned through his knowledge. Thus He gives of His bounty (⟪ar:فضل⟫) to every merituous person, and out of His justice (⟪ar:عدل⟫) towards His creatures, and His loving mercy (⟪ar:رحمۃ⟫) towards His servants!’ 

[38] ‘Then what you are calling to,’ asked the young man, ‘is it this infinitely divine favor towards all creatures, or is it something else?’ 

[39] The scholare responded: ‘It is not exactly the same as that favor, nor is it something other than that. Rather, what I'm calling you to, is related to this favor, as a handful of water is related to the vast extent of the Euphrates! I am calling you to the religion which Allah approved for His servants and which He ennobled on the day He made it complete:

⟪ar-quote:ٱلْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِينَكُمْ وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِى وَرَضِيتُ لَكُمُ ٱلْإِسْلَمَ دِينًۭا⟫
***This day, I have perfected your religion for you, completed My Favour upon you, and have chosen for you Islam as your religion***

It is sublime, protected, noble, hidden. He has ennobled it with His rewards in honor of those who uphold it, and rebuked those with painful punishments who neglect it, making them a sign of dishonor. Its outward dimension is formal prescriptions (⟪ar:ظاہرہ رسوم⟫), and its inner aspect is the various levels of knowledge (⟪ar:وباطنہ علوم⟫). Its authority is illuminating (⟪ar:حکمُہُ نیر⟫) and its justice most clear (⟪ar:وعدلہ بین⟫). This is what I am calling you to!’  

[40] The young man asked: But what singled you out for this affair instead of others, and made you more deserving of it than others, since you just claimed that it comes from Allah to all creation in general? Indeed everyone lays claim to it (for himself or his own group), as opposed to others. So what has made you all more deserving of the name “the truth” than those others, when this affair only came to you in the same way it came to them?’ 

[41] The scholar replied: ‘There is not a single person who speaks a word of truth, that has not been taken from the same sources from which we have taken it. So they are in agreement with us about that word of truth, since we both affirm it. But then they separated from us by abandoning the sources of that very truth, while we persevered in our allegiance to them by obeying them. Through our preserverance we held on to it, while they let go of it and abandoned it. Hence they agreed with us in their affirmation, but differed from us in their actions. Thus, what they affirm bears witness against them, but bears witness in our favor, and their abandoment caused their need for us and the need for what we possess. They are like the brothers who inherited enormous wealth from their father and divided it equally among themselves. Then some of them lost their wealth and fell into poverty, while the others economized and traded with it, and multiplied their wealth many times over. The impoverished brothers were then forced to come to the rich ones and serve them and ask for their help. Likewise those who have lost the path of their messengers, have been forced by their ignorance to seek knowledge from those of noble sources. So the sign that indicates that we are more deserving of the truth than them, is their need for us and our self-sufficiency without them. That is how our scholar has come to invite the ignorant among them; and how our clothed ones have come to cover up the naked; and how our rich have come to feed the hungry. For the true right belongs to the one who has precedence and true merit (⟪ar:وجب الحق الفاضل⟫), while precedence and true merit belongs to the one who acts kindly (⟪ar:والفضل لکل ذی نعمۃ⟫); and gratitude is due to the one who favors (⟪ar:والشکر لکل ذی احسان⟫)’

[42] ‘Their greater merit and precedence over others has become clear,’ said the young man. ‘And as for your argument for the truth, that has been verified against the common people who have neglected the truth. But what is your argument against me? For I am affirming what you have mentioned, upholding what you have described, denying what you have denied, acknowledging the truth and its precedence, and loathing falsehood and its people!’ 

[43] ‘My argument against you,’ replied the scholar, ‘is similar to my argument against them, since even though you disagreed with them about the description of the truth, yet you actually agreed with them about its inner meaning.’ 

[44] ‘But how can that be?’ asked the young man, ‘since I am affirming that you hold the truth while others are denying it!’

[45] ‘Even if you disagreed with them by your affirmation of the truth, the scholar continued, 'still you were in agreement with them in preventing access to it, when they prohibited it. For merely verbal affirmation is of no benefit without any repentance and reverting back to the truth; and denial does no harm to the person being denied when he has clear evidence.’  

[46] The young man exclaimed: ‘Then if I recognize them, I will turn to the people of the truth, or to a confirmed representative from them, and seek their truth! So tell me which of those (options) is possible for me.’ 

[47] ‘Can’t you see,’ replied the scholar, ‘that you’ve been forced to affirm the need for recognizing the possessor of truth or his representative, just as common people have been forced to affirm their need for the truth? So your actions may have been different from theirs, but you shared their ignorance!’ 

[48] ‘You’ve spoken accurately,’ said the young man, ‘so please explain that to me.’

[55] The scholar asked: ‘Have you ever noticed that when the poor helplessly turns to a generous man, he receives a favor from him in only two ways. Either "earning" through work, or through that wealthy man’s gracious "generosity"?’ 

[56] ‘Yes,’ replied the young man, ‘he receives favors either by earning it or through an act of generosity.’

[58J ‘No,’ he admitted, ‘it couldn’t be!’

[59] ‘So seek this distinction through action!’ said the scholar, ‘and you will reach it because you will have earned it. But don’t seek it through flattery, for if you try to obtain it as some undeserved favor, you will be excluded from its merit.’

[62] ‘Set whatever limits you wish and impose whatever conditions you think best,’ declared the young man, ‘for I hope that you will find me patient and grateful for your kindness.’ 

[63] The first of these limits,’ said the scholar, ‘is to fulfill the outward aspect (⟪ar:ظاہر⟫) of the Book and its revealed paths, acting upon it in accordance with your knowledge. For who acts for Allah’s sake based on their knowledge will be guided by Allah to that which he does not know.’  

[64] ‘But the revealed books are many,’ the young man asked, ‘and they all are from Allah. Each book has a large group of people adhering to it, so they all are in agreement about upholding them and worshipping in accordance with them.’

[66] ‘And how is that?’ asked the young man.

[67] ‘Because of what you said about there being many books, all coming from Allah, and each having a large group of followers,’ explained the scholar. ‘So then do you believe that Allah’s words and His books invalidate each other, or that the first book denies the last, or the last one denies the first?’ 

[68] ‘No, I don’t believe that!’ exclaimed the young man. ‘But then what is the argument regarding this?’  

[69] ‘If people acted in accordance with what was revealved in the first book,’ replied the scholar, ‘it would lead them to the second one. And if they acted in accordance with the second, it would lead them to the third; until in the end, they would have arrived at the latest of the books. For the latest book is more deserving of being followed than the one's that came before it— even though they all are from Allah — because the latest one is more recent in time and clearer in its way of proceeding, since it has replaced what was before it, and nothing has come after it to replace it.’  

[70] ‘You’ve spoken truly,’ said the young man, ‘and you’ve made clear how we should acknowledge the truth and the signs of truthfulness. Now I will uphold the limits you’ve ordered me to keep, but what are your conditions for me?’ 

[71] ‘I have five conditions for you’ replied the scholar:
1. Don’t neglect anything I’ve entrusted you with
2. Don’t  conceal anything from me if I ask you about it
3. Don’t come looking for me to give you an answer whenever you have a question
4. Don't ask me about anything until I bring it up with you; and
5. Don’t speak about me to your father.’

 [72] ‘I shall do everything that you have mentioned,’ the young man declared. ‘Indeed your conditions seem easy to me, in comparison to all that I am hoping to receive from you. But why the subterfuge with my father? Surely that is the greatest test and the most painful trial for me! Would you allow me to move away from my father?’  

[73] ‘Moving away from him,’ explained the scholar, ‘would create a reason to point fingers at someone else (i.e., the scholar and his mission). Instead you must stay with him, properly respect him, try to please him by being agreeable with him when you can. And you must protect your innermost self and keep your (spiritual) concerns secret from him. For surely Allah will make up for his, and other people's hostility.’

He also did not know the scholar’s place of residence, nor was he able to seek him out, because of the conditions he had set for him. And since they were alone, the young man prostrated himself humbly before the scholar. Then when he raised his head...

[76] ‘All your words are true,’ replied the young man, ‘and all your actions are good deeds. My gratitude is only for all your gracious favors that you have bestowed on me before this day! Yet, in displaying my gratitude to you, I did hope for even more acts of kindness from you.’ 

[77] The Scholar continued: ‘It is certainly true that gratitude merits an increase in good deeds for those who are grateful, and what you deserve is an obligation on me. So turn your heart toward me, pay close attention and listen carefully to what I say to you.’ 

[78] ‘I have already prepared the instrument of my understanding and made myself ready for listening,’ the young man exclaimed, ‘so say what seems right to you.’ 

[79] The scholar began: ‘There is a key to religion, which renders it either sacred or profane, like the difference between marriage and fornication.’

[80] The young man remarked: ‘Surely that key you have mentioned must be extraordinary in Allah’s eyes if it is what distinguishes between the sacred and the profane, between truth and falsehood. So what is it?’  

[81] ‘That is the pact of Allah (⟪ar:عہد اللہ⟫) which confirms His rights, encompasses His obligations and safeguards His friends. It is the rope of Allah upon His earth and His guarantee of security among His creation,’ said the scholar. ‘I shall repeat that oath to you and impose its obligation on you.’  

[82] ‘Yes!’ said the young man. ‘Impose whatever obligation that you like! I am not hesitant about your opinions, nor will I violate your tradition.’ 

[83] The narrator continued: So the scholar began to recite the oath to the young man, and he kept repeating it and bound himself by it. The young man couldn’t control his emotions, and his eyes started streaming with tears, because of the intensity of that moment, until (the scholar) brought him to the end of the oath. So he praised Allah and glorified Him and gave thanks for all that had reached him, knowing with certainty that he was hence forth part of the party of Allah, and the party of Allah’s friends, because he had accepted the obligations of their oath. Then he finished his praises and became silent, and the scholar began his explanation and clarification.

## Section 1341

[84] The first thing the scholar said to him by way of setting forth the first principles of the manifest intermediaries (⟪ar:الاسباب الظاھرۃ⟫) and the beginning of their creation. He said:    

‘Allah is the **Originator**of things and the **Giver**of their existence (⟪ar:أن اللہ مبدع الاشیا و مکونھا⟫). He created them, and then initiated them without precedence, such that no prior entity served as a model for the later. On the contrary, He is the First of every first (⟪ar:ھو اول کل اول⟫) and the inheritor of every existence. He initiated His creation from a Light, with three words branching from that light.
1. The first of those words was His will (⟪ar:ارادۃ⟫)
2. From the will derived His command (⟪ar:الأمر⟫)
3. From the command came His saying to whatever He wishes “Be!,” so it became (⟪ar:کن فیکون⟫).

So the beginning of all creation was by Allah’s **will**of a **command,**executed through His **words**. (⟪ar:أرادۃ، أمر بالقول⟫)

Hence those seven letters became the root-principles from which seven things were derived. From the Light was created the air (⟪ar:خلق من النور الھوا⟫), which was the first of those, singular and without any pair, unique among all things in its mixing and encompassing them, but not being encompassed by any of them. Thus it symbolically points to the One who has no like unto Him (⟪ar:لیس کمثلہ شئی⟫). Next from those three words came the water (⟪ar:الما⟫),  the darkness (⟪ar:ظلمۃ⟫) and the light (⟪ar:نور⟫)—the light of the heavens and the earth. Then He created from the water smoke (⟪ar:دخان⟫); from the darkness He created the thickness of the mud (⟪ar:تکاثف الحما⟫) and from the light He created fire (⟪ar:النار⟫).

Thus these three things were derived from the preceding three things, so they became pairs, because they were created from them. But the air, the seventh of these principles of creation, governs them altogether. This was the beginning of creation and the commencement of all things.  

[86] ‘So once the creation was established on these seven principles, He proceeded with the process of shaping the outward forms and likenesses of things. He created from smoke, the seven heavens and their signs, and from the thickness of mud He created the seven earths and their sustenance. Thus these seven branches of the heavens, and the seven branches of the earths came from those seven root-principles.   

[87] Next we should examine the signs of the heaven  and the sustenance of the earth - what are they, and what are their visible witnesses? Now if we return to what we were saying about the three words and their letters, from which the seven (root-principles) were created, those words are Allah’s “**willing a command through (His) speaking**” (⟪ar:ارادۃ أمر بقول⟫)—which makes 12 letters. Hence their signs in the heaven are the 12 mansions of the Zodiac, and their witnesses are seven rulers (⟪ar:سبع املاک⟫) And their signs on the earth are the 12 regions (⟪ar:جزیرۃ⟫), while their witnesses are the seven seas. So those are the signs in the heavens and the earth, which most people pass right by, turning away.

[88] Next, the heavens and the earths were one single piece of that darkness of “black mud,” which is the ruler of the earth. Then He split it with His light, so that the various lights and darknesses mixed together. So He separated both (the mixture of light and darkness) through the day and night as two signs. He made the night, a symbol of darkness and its portion, and made the day, a symbol of light and its portion. So He darkened the night and made the moon and planets its signs; and He brightened the daylight to shine forth, and made the sight-giving rays of the sun as its sign. Then from both, resulted the (12+12=24 hour) days revolving in turn between day and night. Now there are seven days in a week, and their signs are that each day has twelve hours, and each night has twelve hours.     

[89] ‘Now, when the creations from these seven root- principles completed, He created pairs between male and female — and by that I don’t mean masculine and feminine, but rather in terms of greater and lesser excellence and merit. Thus Allah’s accustomed way proceeded with the creation of the pairs, and from the essential realities of those pairs He created all animals, including people, beasts, birds and the vermin. Then He proceeded with language, and it also became pairs: attributes (⟪ar:صفات⟫) and things qualified by them (⟪ar:موصوفات⟫), names (⟪ar:اسم⟫) and the essential meanings (⟪ar:معنی⟫). So glory to the One Who created all the pairs and then made them the outward aspects of His command/affair, symbols for His religion, and signs pointing to His power. Thus through all symbols He pointed to their Source (⟪ar:فدل بالاشیاء علی منشنھا⟫), and through his creations He pointed to their Creator (⟪ar:ودل بالبریۃ علی باریھا⟫)

[90] ‘Thus to summarize: He separated them with His Light, He originated them through His command, He created them with His knowledge, and He sealed them by their names, with the letters “In the Name of God, the All-Merciful, the All-Compassionate” (⟪ar:بسم اللہ الرحمن الرحیم⟫). Until finally, when He had perfected their shape and harmoniously arranged them in pairs, from the quintessence of His wish, and the innermost depths of His mystery, He selected and Purified for Himself abundant knowledge (⟪ar:علما کثیراً⟫) and an illuminating light (⟪ar:ونور مبینا⟫). He made that the religion through which He is worshipped, and by which one can draw closer to Him. Then He ennobled it with the revealed paths (⟪ar:ثم اکرمہ بالشرائع⟫) and the books, and He pointed to it through the symbols (⟪ar:امثال⟫) and recurring patterns (⟪ar:سنۃ⟫) of all creation.  Then from among the very best of His creations He raised for Himself, certain distinguished personalities, guiding figures and Wise judges. These, He placed in the houses of his prophethood, the treasuries of his knowledge and wisdom, and made them the inspired translators of His revelation (⟪ar:وحی⟫); and He made them his viceroys on His earth (⟪ar:واستخلفھم فی الارض⟫), guideposts to the path of salvation in both the outer and the inner spiritual aspects. Among them are the Imams and the Natiqs, the Hujjas (proofs) and the Naqibs (chiefs), and those who are summoning people to the good (⟪ar:داعی⟫) and the scholars (⟪ar:علماء⟫). In their various ranks, they are like the stars of the heaven! Actions of those they guide, rise up to them, and the symbols of religion come to an end in them (i.e. they are the goal of all good symbols). Their Imam is similar to the immensity of the sun in comparison to the other heavenly lights - impossible for it to be veiled from sight. Indeed the sun is his (Imam's) symbol and an outer aspect, and the Imam's Hujjat, a gateway (⟪ar:ابواب⟫), is like the illuminated moon, for the moon is his (Hujjat's) outer aspect and his symbol. And the shining stars are the symbols and the outer aspect, representing the Duaat's.  

[91] Now, all of these pairs I have described, have an inner and outer aspect, a symbol (⟪ar:مثل⟫) and what it symbolizes (⟪ar:ممثول⟫). Thus the outer aspect of the seven heavens are symbols, while their inner aspect are the seven Natiqs - the posessors of determination (⟪ar:اولو العظم⟫) among the divine messengers. The outer aspect of the seven earths is likewise a symbol, while its inner aspect is the Imam of right guidance. Thus each Natiq among them has twelve Naqibs (⟪ar:نقباء⟫), and their symbols in the seven heavens are the twelve mansions (⟪ar:بروج⟫). Likewise each Imam among the imams of right guidance has twelve Hujjas, who he uses to bring His argument to his people; and their symbols on earth are the twelve regions (⟪ar:جزائر⟫), which are also their dwelling-places, with one hujja assigned to each region. Then each Huija has a greater or lesser number of Da'is - for God increases among His creatures whatever He wills — who communicate God’s argument among His creatures, so that the people will not have any argument against God after sending down His messengers.

For the inner aspect (⟪ar:الباطن⟫) is the true religion of Allah through which His friends worship Him appropriately, while the outer aspect (⟪ar:الظاہر⟫) is the revealed paths of religion (⟪ar:شریعۃ⟫) and its symbols. So religion is the "soul" and the living spirit of those revealed paths, and they are the body for religion and signs pointing to it. The body can only subsist through the spirit because it is its life; and the spirit can only subsist through the body, because that is its outer covering. The same rule applies to all outer aspects of the religious paths and other commandments. They only subsist through their inner spiritual aspects (⟪ar:دین الباطن⟫), because it is their light, and their essential meaning (⟪ar:معنی⟫). Nor can the inner aspect subsist without the outer aspect, because that is its covering and a sign pointing to it. Now the outer aspect is the distinctive mark of this lower world which can only be seen through that (body); and the inner aspect is the distinctive mark of the other world, which can only be seen through that (spirit). Hence there is not a single letter among the “letters” of the inner aspect, nor a friend (⟪ar:ولی⟫) among the friends of Allah, who does not have many visible signs in the outer aspect of this world, because of the multiplicity of the symbols and the great extents of the revealed paths.

[93] ‘Now my speech about this could go on and be greatly expanded. But due to the preciousness and purity of the words of wisdom, the longer one speaks about them, the more the actual point gets hidden; the later part makes you forget the beginning. For one part of the light of wisdom can obscure another part, just as the light of the sun veils and weakens the light of the moon and the stars. It is in this manner words of wisdom should embellish the tongues of the wise men.’

[94] ‘But don’t you see,’ asked the young man, ‘that regarding this lower world — in which He created these creatures whose essence is so marvelous, so immense in their sheer numbers, from the first to the last—that people differ about it? For some people are very intent on this lower world, plunging into its pursuits and seeking excellence by accumulating more of it. While others condemn it and carefully avoid it, even though they can’t do without it—and if a great deal of something is forbidden, then surely a little of it is also forbidden? Now I haven’t heard you mention this lower world or discuss it at all, so what do you have to say about it?’ 

[95] ‘As for this lower world,’ replied the Knower, ‘neither the scholar nor the ignorant has ever condemned it. The ignorant person is forced to recognize the merits of this lower world since he is ignorant of any thing else, while a scholar recognizes its value because he understands the merit of its essential meaning. So no one ever really condemns this lower world except for the totally ignorant. And as for my opinion on this subject, it is dear to whoever can really see that this lower world was established on the basis of seven things, which are derived from the seven root-principles. Those derivatives are the heaven, the earth, darkness, light, the “vehicles,” the divine favors and the adversities. That makes seven groups, and from each of those seven are derived seven further divisions:

So among them are the keys to what is good and the wellsprings of Allah's blessings. Then, which of these is to be condemned when they are all among Allah’s kindnesses and His favors for which His servants should all be grateful!

[96] ‘Those are undoubtedly the attributes of this lower world,’ the young man responded, ‘and nothing exists that is not part of the Creators handiwork and design. So why is it some learned criticize this lower world, even though they cannot do without it? And why do the ignorant praise it? For it seems to me that they are right in this matter, even though they are ignorant—and their being right necessarily means the learned must be mistaken, since they have opposed the ignorant in this belief.’

[97] The scholar replied: ‘The learned were not mistaken in criticizing this lower world, but people did not necessarily understand their true intention in doing so. Nor did the ignorant really grasp the truth when they praised this lower world, since they failed to understand its essential meaning. And the person who is ignorant of something, naturally opposes it.’ 

[98] Then show me,’ asked the young man, ‘what is accurate in the words of the learned who criticize this world, so that I can understand why they are right; and clarify for me what is in the saying of the ignorant that will reveal their falsehood, since appearances would seem to indicate the opposite. For my heart has been constricted by my ignorance, and I desire the relief that comes from knowing the truth’

[99] ‘Now this lower world and everything I have described to you,’ the Knower continued, ‘is only the outer aspect of the inner dimension (⟪ar:ظاہر الباطن⟫). So this lower world has nothing to sustain it except by its inner dimension, because it stands in relation to the material world, like the spirit exists in relation to the body. Therefore, whoever knows the spirit, finds the body pleasing. But for that person who is ignorant of the spirit and only recognises the body, understands that it is dead and decaying flesh, which is forbidden. This is the reason why scholars have forbidden this lower world, because Allah has forbidden eating the dead, unless it is approached in the way Allah wishes (sacrifice). ‘Now it has become clear why the ignorant were wrong in praising this lower world, since they didn’t recognize its inner dimension and what Allah intended by it. They imagined that He had created it without any essential meaning—but Allah did not create things pointlessly nor in jest. For if this lower world and all it contains had only been created for this lower world alone, bringing forth in it creatures returning to nothingness, there would have been no ultimate outcome for it, and it would have had no essential meaning. And every action without any ultimate outcome is vain amusement, and every speech without any essential meaning is falsehood—but Allah is exalted above that!’

[100] ‘You have certainly established the truth of the scholars,’ the young man admitted, ‘and you have shown their excellence and what distinguishes them from the ignorant, as well as what their mistake is in the way their argument initially appeared. And now we need to know about the inner dimension of this lower world and what it contains. For I already feel the constriction of my heart, that I was complaining about, being lifted!’

[101] ‘Then what is the reward,’ asked the Scholar, ‘for the person who liberates you from the constraints of neediness and brings you to freedom and abundance?’

[102] ‘Gratitude and praise,’ he replied. 

[103] ‘Then what is the reward,’ he continued, ‘for the person who has freed you from the shackles of ignorance and brought you to the open space of true knowledge?’ 

[104] ‘Obedience and Action in accordance to the knowledge gained,’ he answered.

[105] The scholar said, ‘So this physical world and all that it contains are symbols for the outer aspects of the spiritual world (⟪ar:ظاہر للآخرۃ⟫). This physical world is a body while the spiritual world is its soul, brining it to life. Therefore whoever strives in and for this physical world without any awareness of the spiritual world, his efforts are nothing more than aimless wandering, for it is without any essential purpose or result. But who strives in this world with the goal of acquiring the rewards of the spiritual world, his effort does have a purpose and a result, and is met with Allah's acceptance.’ 

[106] ‘You have spoken truly,’ said the young man. ‘So explain to me the distinctive signs of this physical world and what they contain, and lay out for me the various symbols (⟪ar:مثل⟫) of this world, connecting them to their inner representations (⟪ar:ممثولات⟫).’

[107] The Scholar began: ‘Allah, may He be praised and exalted, did not create a single creature among the creations of this physical world—not an animal crawling upon the earth; nor a creature flying with its wings; nothing wet nor dry; neither inanimate objects assembled like the mountains, stones, or trees; nor minerals like gold, silver and gems; neither things great nor small - but as a "symbol" established by Him. Don’t you see that even the tiniest dust mote (⟪ar:ذرۃ⟫), despite its tiny size and essential meaning, is bound from all its sides? It has a front, a back, a right and a left, a top and a bottom! Those are its six boundaries, while the dust mote itself is the seventh of them. The same holds true of everything that Allah has created — from the dust motes or the gnat to whatever species exist above them; from the heavens to the earths and everything in between them, from the biggest to the smallest. Every creation is surrounded by six boundaries, and the creature itself is the seventh. These six boundaries are a sign pointing to the six root-principles from which everything was created, while the “Air” which encompasses them all is their seventh and their king! So now, if your heart has opened up to these questions, and your intellect is able to compare these symbols with thier spiritual partners (⟪ar:زوج⟫) from the inner dimension, then ask of me about whatever seems appropriate to you.’

2. The life of the spiritual world and its ultimate outcome, which is eternally lasting.

Hence He said that you shouldn’t act for this passing, transient life, but you should act for the lasting and eternal life. For that reason He said: "يَقُولُ يَلَيْتَنِى قَدَّمْتُ لِحَيَاتِى" “*He will say, I wish I had prepared for my life*!—which is to say, “If only I had prepared during this passing life for the lasting life.” And the meaning of these two kinds of life is,
1. Life through knowledge of its outward physical aspects (⟪ar:حیات بعلم الظاہرۃ⟫), and
2. Life through knowledge of its inner spiritual aspect (⟪ar:حیات بعلم الباطنۃ⟫).

Because of this He said: Don't let yourselves be deceived by the "life" of this physical world!’—that is, don’t be deluded by the outward aspect of knowledge and by actions according to that alone. The outward aspect of "action" is not accepted without the inner aspect of "knowledge".

If its limbs are cut off, the spirit won’t remain in it for even a moment. And that is how the outward aspects of religion function. If its basic obligations aren’t carried out, then its inner aspect won’t be realized for that person. 1. If they neglected it because of some incapacity, then they are even more incapable of realizing the inner aspect.
2. Or if they neglected the outward aspect intentionally, even though they were able to uphold it, then they are willfully disobedient to the one Who commanded them to uphold it.

Now the disobedient person is an evildoer, and the evildoer cannot be a companion of the Friends of Allah. Indeed, the evildoer is the enemy of the friends of Allah, and they are his enemies, because of their cutting off what Allah has commanded should be joined (وَيَقْطَعُونَ مَآ أَمَرَ ٱللَّهُ بِهِ).’

For both the sending down (⟪ar:تنزیل⟫) and the inspired interpretation (⟪ar:تاویل⟫) are from Allah, and no one can attain what is with Allah except through revealed inspiration (⟪ar:وحی⟫)’

[495] ‘Yes,’ he said, ‘which is why obedience to him is obedience to Allah.’

[498] ‘But why does he need another creature,’ asked Abu Malik, ‘when the revealed inspiration is (already) coming to him from the Creator?’  

[499] ‘Although that revealed inspiration does come to him from the Creator,’ Salih explained, 'it only comes to him through inspired interpretation of the hadith and of the revealed books and the rest of what has been left behind as their spiritual heritage by the "superiors". Therefore Allah’s revealed inspiration is continuously connected to His just witnesses on His earth, to the extent of their various spiritual levels.  [502] ‘Now I do bear witness to what the divine books have attested,’ said Abu Malik, ‘that what has been sent down in them does bear witness that every truly inspired interpretation (⟪ar:تاویل⟫) is a revealed inspiration from Allah. These true prophets were killed among them, and that didn’t even anger them! Thus the community of the Magians (⟪ar:مجوسی⟫) are clinging to their excess and worshipping their idols, devoting themselves to their fire-temples and declaring their claim that Allah will never send a messenger after their own messenger. Their legal scholars cravenly submitted to the oppressors, upholding the religion for them without regarding traditions, establishing the prayers at the wrong times and paying the alms-tax to those who did not deserve it. [546] ‘None of us would be satisfied with that!’ Abu Malik exclaimed. ‘If any one of us had his child killed or was expelled from his property, he would become outraged, and his people would be outraged for him, so they would not be satisfied until he was satisfied.’

[547] ‘Surely Allah and His Messenger are more deserving that you all should be outraged and be angry for him,’ Salih answered him, ‘when His viceroy (khalifa) was killed and His friend (wali) was removed from the earth?’

[548] ‘Indeed, it is hard for us that they should kill Allah’s friend among us or remove him from his earth,’ said Abu Malik. ‘But (His friends) have decided to hide themselves from us, while the oppressors are unjustly dominating us.’ 

[549] ‘If you were all to help the friends of Allah, then they would win out,’ Salih answered him. ‘And if you were to abandon His enemies, then they would not be victorious. But (in reality), as far as your relation to the friends of Allah, you can all be judged to belong to one of these three levels. There is the relentless murderer, who never tires of hunting them down and whose blood thirst is not quenched even when he wins out. And there is their  inveterate enemy, who bites his nails in rage, who will go to any length to incite enmity against them, and who never forgets his hatred of them, even if they overlook (him). Finally, there are those who do dislike those enemies’ actions, who are sympathetic to the friends of Allah but are really abandoning them, who have no spiritual insight in regard to faith, and who cannot be relied upon to protect the secret (⟪ar:کتمان⟫) of the imams. So it is because of that that the friends of Allah have concealed themselves from you, out of precaution against you and this immense number of people who have taken their passions as their lord, who fight like dogs over the stuff of this lower world, and who conspire together about killing the prophets. Since such people are unaware of the wrongfulness of their actions, they do not trouble their souls with the wrongfulness of their evil-doing and they do not humble themselves in repentance for what they have done. Indeed they go so far as to attribute the wrongfulness of their actions to Allah and to ascribe them to Him. So they say that Allah will never send a prophet after their prophet, nor any warner. Thus they sought to legitimize the slaughtering of the friends of Allah without any right. And in so doing they did wrong with their actions, they turned away from (the truth) in their speech, and they transgressed by calling (them) liars.’ 

[550] ‘Yes,’ said Abu Malik. That is the description of this community of ours, and that is what they say. So how can we be saved from that, and where is the refuge from Allah’s punishment?’ 

[551] ‘With Allah!’ Salih answered him. ‘Seek protection with Him and He will give you refuge. Turn to Him in repentance and He will accept that repentance from you. And ask Him for the path of His friends and He will rescue you through your own right action from the deeds of those who do injustice. The same is true for all these other people: if they were to turn to Allah, He would accept their repentance. And Allah would not punish them, for Allah does not do a dust-speck’s weight of wrong, even if they themselves have done wrong and denied Allah’s friends. For He does not hasten to punish them, even when they have hastened to disobey (Him).’ 

[552] ‘Then I am seeking protection from Allah’s punishment,’ Abu Malik replied, ‘through repentance and turning to Him. And I am searching for the connection to what pleases Him. So be generous to the one who comes to you seeking (help), and help to guide rightly the one who is seeking your guidance—may Allah reward you for that!’   

[553] ‘If you do that,’ Salih answered him, ‘you will find that Allah, in His forgiveness, is closer to you than your repentance and returning to Him, for surely He loves those who turn to Him in repentance. And you will find me full of concern for you in regard to guiding you.’ 

[554] [The narrator continued:] Then Salih's eyes filled with tears at the mention of repentance, so he drew his speaking to an end. Since he was in his own rooms, he asked them to go away to his father’s house. Then he went in to see his (spiritual) father, the scholar who had called him, and he consulted with him about the matter of Abu Malik and his companions. 

[555] Then the scholar said: ‘You know your companions better (than I do), so if you recognize some good in them, then guide them rightly as you were guided. But if you are apprehensive about them, then don’t let your desire for them lead you to approach them while putting yourself at risk. And test them by ignoring them, but not harshly. Order your father to treat them kindly and to be respectful toward them for a while. For the person who is truly seeking the good will not remain hidden. Surely Allah will not leave you behind and He will open up for you, from the light of His providential arrangement of things, that through which He manages the affair of His creatures. And He will open up for you from the gateways of right guidance what will show you the (appropriate) actions of those who are rightly guided.’ 

[556] [The narrator] continued: So Abu Malik and his companions continued to go through their different levels of testing, until their affair was complete and they recognized their right guidance. And it was Allah’s friend among them who took care of their guidance, and they thanked Allah for that. Then they returned to their people warning them, so that through them Allah guided a great many of His servants to His religion.    

[557] Nor was this a made-up story, but rather the confirmation of what Allah has commanded. For in it is the confirmation of the (divine) messengers, the signs of their trustees (the imams), and the proper behavior of those who are seeking. 

[558] So praise be to Allah, in the beginning and at the end! May Allah’s blessings be upon His Messenger, our master Muhammad, who was sent by Him to His creatures as a bearer of good news and as a warner. And (may His blessings be) upon his trustee, the imam of those who are mindful (of Allah), ‘the best of the best,’  the beloved of the Lord of the worlds; and upon the imams from the people of His house upon whom Allah has bestowed His favors! —may He take away from them all impurity and purify them totally. And Allah is sufficient for us, the best of trustees, the best of protectors and the best of supporters. And there is no strength and no power except through Allah, the Exalted, the Tremendous.
