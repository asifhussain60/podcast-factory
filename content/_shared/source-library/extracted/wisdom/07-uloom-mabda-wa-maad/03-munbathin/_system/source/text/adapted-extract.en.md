## The Emanators

When the al-ʿAql al-Awwal (First Intellect) attained perfection from the Creator and ten envelopes of joy were placed upon it, two ⟪ar:اشباح صوریہ⟫ proceeded forward together, unaffected by this state of intimacy. Both observed the First Intellect and understood that this condition arose because it had performed tawhīd (divine unification) toward the Almighty Creator, along with tajrīd (abstraction) and tanzīh (transcendence). Witnessing this, the same state of mind manifested in both of them as in the First Intellect, and they too became creators of tawhīd toward the Almighty. Both denied divinity for themselves and all other species, humbling themselves before the Creator and acknowledging that they ⟪ar:الہ⟫ were not in a state of perfection from the outset—divinity belongs solely to the Creator. After bearing witness to the Creator's monotheism, both also acknowledged the grace and precedence of the First Intellect. With this confession, the two sprang forward together and followed the First Intellect.[^cite-1] These two beings ⟪ar:منبعثین⟫ are called the Emanators—those who emerge together.

The manifest difference between the tawhīd of the First Intellect and that of the al-Munbaʿithūn (Emanators) lay in this: the Emanators performed tawhīd by observing and following the example of the First Intellect, whereas the First Intellect performed tawhīd without any exemplar or ⟪ar:مُلحِم⟫ guide, deriving it from its own nature. In this capacity, it was called the First Intellect because it transcended itself and became a model to be followed, while the Emanators were called al-Munbaʿithūn because they advanced after witnessing the First Intellect.[^cite-2]

## Existence, Dormancy, and Emanation

For an object to emanate, it must first come into existence. Nothing can be perfected unless something has existed. The al-Munbaʿithūn first came into being in a state of primary perfection (kamāl awwal). From the moment of their initial creation until the attainment of kamāl awwal, they remained in a condition of durus (dormancy or latency). During this state, they observed the ⟪ar:نور شعشعانی⟫ radiant light of the First Intellect and understood that it had attained this rank because of performing tawhīd, with tajrīd and tanzīh, toward the Creator of the Almighty. Perceiving this, they transitioned from dormancy to ⟪ar:انبعاث⟫ emanation—following the First Intellect and acknowledging its tawhīd.[^cite-3]

## The Difference in the Perspectives of the Emanators

The First Intellect established the tawhīd of the Almighty through the dual path of negation and affirmation within the utterance of sincere devotion. Because the Shahāda—"*Lā ilāha illā ʾAllāh*" (There is no god but Allah)—contains only these two components, with no third way to approach the Creator's monotheism, the First Intellect made both paths luminously clear. When the veil of joy descended upon the First Intellect, the two emanating souls were startled and proceeded together in its footsteps. They are called al-Munbaʿithūn—the two who emerge together.

**Principles of Essence and Manifestation:**

Every thing possesses an essence, and the manifestation of that essence is like a noun with its attributes. Without essence there is no substance; without nouns, no attributes. Likewise, the essence of the First Intellect's development was the act of tawhīd toward its Originator, and because of this action, a veil of radiant light was placed upon it—the state of submission itself.[^cite-4]

### The Monotheism of the First Emanator

Witnessing the ⟪ar:نور شعشعانی⟫ radiant light of the First Intellect, the First Emanator understood that this illumination arose because the First Intellect had performed the ⟪ar:فعل توحید⟫ act of tawhīd for the Creator of the Almighty. He sought to discover the true cause of the happiness that had descended upon the Intellect and the lofty rank it had attained—perceiving the light shining with its own luminosity. He recognized that the First Intellect had attained this station by unifying with the Creator and executing its ordained function. After performing tawhīd toward the Originator, the First Emanator accepted the primacy of the First Intellect and glorified it as the First Intellect had glorified and sanctified the Creator. He acknowledged the forgiveness and grace of the First Intellect, as well as its greatness, nobility, and superiority over all other beings. Through this confession and the relationship established with the First Intellect, he received ⟪ar:تائیدات، لحظات و برکات⟫ confirmations, attentions, and blessings from the First Intellect, and through it he came into contact with the Qudsi knowledge—that sacred knowledge issuing from the Divine Presence and the Light of the Spirit as sent forth by the Creator of the Almighty. Thus he too attained perfection and became ⟪ar:عقل ثانی⟫ the Second Intellect. As the rank of the First Emanator was elevated, the station of the First Intellect—whether ⟪ar:مقام وحدت⟫ the station of unification or ⟪ar:مقام ھویت⟫ the station of identity—was exalted. Now he is not reckoned among the creation, for he has become *Al-ʿAddād* (the Reckoner), and all others have been obliterated in him. Allah says in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 3:2⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْحَىُّ ٱلْقَيُّومُ ‎⟫
> *Allah! La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the Ever Living, the One Who sustains and protects all that exists.*

**Principle of Lower and Higher Perspectives:**

The musta­wdaʿūn (those entrusted with lower knowledge) received ⟪ar:نظر ادون⟫ the lower perspective, while al-mustaqqarrūn (those established in higher knowledge) received ⟪ar:نظر اشرف⟫ the higher perspective. It became necessary for al-mustawdaʿūn to acquire the higher perspective so that they could progress from the ⟪ar:مستودع⟫ realm of the entrusted to the ⟪ar:مستقر⟫ realm of the established.

### The Tawhīd of the Second Emanator

Like the First Intellect, the Second Emanator perceived the veil of the joys surrounding the First Intellect and understood that the First Intellect had attained this rank through tawhīd toward the Creator of the Almighty. Yet he gave precedence to the light itself rather than to the act of tawhīd. Captivated by the radiant state of the First Intellect, he marveled: "What a light! What magnificence! What a sublime station!" He did not penetrate to the reason for the light; he saw only its outward splendor, and his vision did not reach the inner truth. Dazzled by this visible radiance, he was overwhelmed. He valued the external station of the First Intellect without seeking to understand why he had attained it or why happiness had inundated him. Because of this outward attachment, he fell behind and descended in the hierarchy of ranks. Yet because he and al-Munbaʿath al-Awwal (the First Emanator) had both advanced together toward tawhīd, he imagined they were equal in station. He became guilty and ignorant through his own action, holding the ⟪ar:وهم مساوات⟫ illusion of equivalence—believing that because they had emanated together, he and the First Emanator were of equal rank. This ⟪ar:وهم مساوات⟫ illusion of equivalence corrupted the form of his emanation, preventing him from attaining secondary perfection (kamāl thānī).[^cite-5]

Although the Second Emanator praised the tawhīd of the Creator and acknowledged the primacy and superiority of the First Intellect, he did not receive the sacred materials from the First Intellect. This was because he did not truly recognize the superiority of the First Emanator and his vision; instead, he equated himself with the First. Yet perfection requires that there be no defect or injustice in the self. The error lay in the ⟪ar:وهم مساوات⟫ illusion of equivalence, for equality is possible only in primary perfection, whereas in secondary perfection no two limits can be equal. Al-Munbaʿath al-Thānī (the Second Emanator) offered correct tawhīd to the Creator, yet by attaching importance to an erroneous perception of the First Intellect, he forfeited success.

## The Difference in the Testimonies of the First Two

The First Intellect moved first toward tawhīd of the Creator of the Almighty and attained secondary perfection. Although the First Emanator attained perfection after the First Intellect, he was first in his own ⟪ar:انبعاث⟫ emanation, and in this respect he also achieved an individual status. For this reason, the First Intellect and the First Emanator are called ⟪ar:أَوَّلَین⟫ the First Two. Although both of them performed ⟪ar:کلمۃ الشہادۃ⟫ the testimony of faith, as the Qur'an declares:

> ⟪quran 3:18⟫
> ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫
> *Allah bears witness that La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), and the angels, and those having knowledge (also give this witness); (He is always) maintaining His creation in Justice. La ilah illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.*

Yet their testimonies were directed toward different realities. The testimony of the First Intellect was to the divinity of the Almighty. As soon as he attained tawhīd, he received the divine and perfect materials from the Creator and was elevated to the station of unity. The Creator made the First Intellect his ⟪ar:حجاب⟫ veil and became himself ⟪ar:محتجب⟫ veiled. That is, the First Intellect received materials from the Creator, but now all other beings shall receive materials from the First Intellect. In this capacity, he himself became ⟪ar:الہ⟫ ilāh (a divine principle) for all else. Therefore, when the Second Emanator followed in the footsteps of the First Intellect and testified ⟪ar:الہیت⟫ to divinity through ⟪ar-quote:شھد اللہ انہ لا الہ الا ھو⟫ "Allah bears witness that there is no divinity except Him," his ⟪ar:الہیت⟫ testimony of divinity was not to the Creator but to the First Intellect. For the Second Emanator, the First Intellect took the place of ⟪ar:ھو⟫ the Divine "He."[^cite-6]

## The Holy Sanctuary of the First Emanator

The First Intellect established tawhīd without any ⟪ar:ملحم و معلم⟫ guide or teacher, and was established in the station of unity. Witnessing this, the al-Munbaʿithūn moved forward—the First Emanator attaining secondary perfection while the Second remained behind because of his ⟪ar:وهم مساوات⟫ illusion of equivalence. When the First Emanator performed tawhīd, twenty-seven others perceived his vision and performed tawhīd with him, attaining secondary perfection without needing explicit invitation. As the Qur'an suggests through the language of spiritual sight: ⟪ar-quote:نَظَرُ بِنَظَرِہِ ، بَصَرُ بِبَصَرِہِ⟫ "I have witnessed through his witnessing; I have perceived through his perception."[^cite-7] In this way, the First Emanator formed a circle of twenty-seven souls, which is called ⟪ar:حضیرۃ القدس⟫ the Holy Sanctuary—the gathering of the sacred assembly. The First Emanator stands at the center of this circle.

These twenty-eight beings are compared to the twenty-eight lights of the Kalimah (the Shahāda), and they became a model for all the learned. For this reason, the entire divine system is divided according to the numbers 28 (comprising 2+3+4+7+12). The First Intellect and the First Emanator are two fixed points that shall never change their position. All other intellects will eventually merge into the realm of the First Emanator as the Day of Perfection approaches. This same pattern has been maintained throughout all the creation cycles.

### The Map of Ḥaḍrat al-Quds in the Worlds

Allah declares in the Holy Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 53:13-15⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ رَآهُ نَزْلَةً أُخْرَىٰ ‎ ‏عِندَ سِدْرَةِ ٱلْمُنتَهَىٰ ‎ ‏عِندَهَا جَنَّةُ ٱلْمَأْوَىٓ ‎⟫
> *And indeed he (Muhammad PBUH) saw him [Jibrael (Gabriel)] at a second descent. Near Sidrat-ul-Muntaha [the lote-tree of the utmost boundary], Near it is the Paradise of Abode.*

In Arabic, the Sidra (سِدْرَة—lote-tree) possesses a singular distinction: it always grows in solitude, with no other tree beside it. The ⟪ar:جنت الماوی⟫ Garden of Refuge (Jannāt al-Maʾwā) refers to a sanctuary wherein people may enter and find shelter.[^cite-8] The First Intellect is symbolized by ⟪ar:سدرۃ المنتہی⟫ the Sidra of the Boundary because it stands unique in its world, existing in the realm of unity where only it subsists in its sphere, with no other beside it or associated with it. It is the sole originator. The First Emanator is symbolized by ⟪ar:جنت الماوی⟫ the Garden of Refuge because, having advanced after witnessing the First Intellect, he gathered around him a circle of twenty-seven souls who came to shelter in his presence and find rest. As the days progress, all intellects shall merge into the realm of the First Emanator. Therefore, his circle is called ⟪ar:حضیرۃ القدس⟫ the Holy Sanctuary (Ḥaḍrat al-Quds). This same cosmic pattern was then replicated throughout all worlds and realms.

In the physical world (⟪ar:جثہ ابدائیہ⟫), the First Intellect abides in supreme sovereignty as the sole master of the entire physical cosmos, and its reflection appears in the physical realm through the First Emanator, who shall gather the whole creation. In the celestial world, the highest heaven corresponds to the First Intellect, where neither stars nor planets dwell in multiplicity. All stars and planets reside in the lower sphere, which corresponds to the First Emanator. In the same way, in the era before the last (⟪ar:عین آخرۃ⟫), the positions of the First Intellect and its reflections correspond to the First Emanator. All the Imāms (⟪ar:عین اولہ⟫—the highest forms) shall be gathered in their proper stations. In each age, the Imām corresponds to the First Intellect, and his ⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫ Gate of Gates corresponds to the First Emanator. The gathering of all the forms of believers takes place at this Gate of Gates. Similarly, in the household, the husband corresponds to the First Intellect—the sole custodian of his household—and the wife corresponds to the First Emanator, within whose womb children are nurtured.

When a row is formed in prayer, all worshippers shift their positions and take their proper place in the line. Only the Imām and his deputy (standing to the right of the Imām) maintain their stations without change. In this way, all the worshippers create a living image of the spiritual world. Just as in the spiritual realm only the First Intellect and the First Emanator shall remain unchanging, while all other lower beings shall evolve, change their positions, and eventually merge into the realm of the First Emanator, so too in the mosque—the image of the spiritual world—does this pattern manifest.[^cite-9]

## The Universal Intellect and the Universal Self

⟪ar:عقل اور نفس، روح (کمال اول)⟫ The Intellect and the Self are names of faculties. Allah the Almighty ⟪ar:ارواح⟫ created the universe from the Spirit, which is why this realm is called ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ the Spiritual World. By harnessing the powers of this Spirit, ⟪ar:عقل⟫ the Intellect is produced—the more perfected form of the Spirit *bi-l-fiʿl* (in actuality)—while ⟪ar:نفس⟫ the Self is the name of the Spirit's form *bi-l-quwwa* (in potentiality). The Self is she who receives sustenance from the Intellect, obeys its commands, and responds to its signals.

## The Sign of Definition: Alif and Lam

In Arabic, Alif and Lam are known as the symbols of *Maʿrifa* (definite knowledge) or the article of definition. Therefore, if a noun whose meaning is indefinite—for instance ⟪ar:رجل⟫ (a man)—is to become known, one adds Alif and Lam before it: ⟪ar:الرجل⟫ (the man). Thus in Arabic, every noun preceded by Alif and Lam becomes known and determinate. Alif is the first letter of the Arabic alphabet. Lam is kindred to Alif because Lam is composed of two lines while Alif consists of a single line. Through the alphabet's logic, Lam is generated from the doubling of Alif.

Alif is an argument for the First Intellect, for just as Alif is the first letter, so too was the First Intellect born without any ⟪ar:ملحم و معلم⟫ guide or teacher. Similarly, Lam is an argument for the Self, which was created by the Intellect and on account of it—just as Lam is constituted from the lines of two Alifs.[^cite-10]

Just as Alif and Lam together render other words known and determinate, so too are all spiritual and physical limits defined and made manifest through the Intellect and the Self. There exist two similarities (ratios) of Alif within Lam, for Lam is the First Emanator and Alif is the First Intellect. The First Intellect transcended itself and went before, yet before the First Intellect there existed an exemplar of two ratios of itself. Therefore, within Lam there are two Alif-lines: one longer, indicating a higher ratio, and one shorter, indicating a lower line.

## The Inkwell, the Pen, and the Tablet

The Messenger of Allah (upon him be the peace and blessings of Allah) said: "First Allah created the Pen, and then He produced a stream from it—sweeter than honey, whiter than milk, and softer than musk. Then He commanded the Pen: 'Write!' The Pen asked, 'What shall I write?' He said, 'Write down all that shall occur until the Day of Resurrection!' And the Pen wrote everything upon the Tablet. This is why it is called ⟪ar:لوح محفوظ⟫ the Preserved Tablet. As Allah says (in the meaning):

> ⟪quran 85:21-22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏بَلْ هُوَ قُرْآنٌۭ مَّجِيدٌۭ ‎ ‏فِى لَوْحٍۢ مَّحْفُوظٍۭ ‎⟫
> *Nay! This is a Glorious Quran, (Inscribed) in Al-Lauh Al-Mahfuz (The Preserved Tablet)!*

The Qur'ān is the utterance of the First Intellect. With this utterance, whatever the Pen inscribed upon the first Tablet, the Tablet preserved within itself until the Day of Judgment. The Pen has fulfilled its function. Now all the worlds shall receive their sustenance from it.[^cite-11]

| Pen | Stream | Tablet |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First Intellect | Sacred Materials / Continuous Knowledge | First Emanator |

In this ḥadīth, the Messenger of Allah (upon him be the peace and blessings of Allah) signifies by "Pen" the First Intellect, by "Tablet" the First Emanator, and by "stream" the continuous and unceasing knowledge. The First Intellect is the pen of creation itself, inscribing twenty-eight words upon the First Emanator with the ink of Qudsi materials. These twenty-eight are the ⟪ar:نور کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ luminous gems of the Kalimah "There is no god but Allah." With this light, the First Intellect bestowed upon the First Emanator all the ⟪ar:علم ماکان سیکون⟫ knowledge of what was and what shall be—a bestowal to which the hadith refers when it says "the Pen wrote down all matters upon the Tablet until the Day of Judgment." This is the meaning of what Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 68:1⟫
> ⟪ar:‏نٓ ۚ وَٱلْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Nun. By the Pen and what the angels write (in the Records of men).*

The pen cannot develop the capacity to write until it is immersed in ink. In the same way, until the First Intellect acquired the sacred materials, it had not developed the capacity to sustain the First Emanator and all other intellects. It is by virtue of these materials that the First Intellect attained perfection, reaching the station of ⟪ar:صمد⟫ the Self-Sufficient—one on whom all else depends for existence—such that all other intellects became dependent upon him. ⟪ar:(نٓ)⟫ The letter Nun itself invokes the principle that says: ⟪ar-quote:بَینَ المُبدِعِ وَالمُبدَعِ کَلِمَۃ⟫ "Between the Creator and the created stands a Word."[^cite-12]
