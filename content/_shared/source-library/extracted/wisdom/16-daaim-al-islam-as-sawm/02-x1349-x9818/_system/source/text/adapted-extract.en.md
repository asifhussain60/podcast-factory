## The Blessings (Barakāt) of the Month of Ramaḍān

The city of Ramaḍān possesses manifold dimensions of meaning. In one circle of interpretation, the month signifies Ramaḍān itself as a supreme reality ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫. In another circle, it refers to the Prophet (peace be upon him). In yet another, it designates the entire period of Muḥammad. Because of the nobility both of this month and of the parable it embodies, the month—especially the Night of Power ⟪ar:لیلۃ القدر⟫—ascends and descends in cycles of spiritual manifestation. During this blessed month, the apportionment of sustenance (rizq) and the determination of life-spans are established. ⟪ar:تقسیم الارزاق⟫ The entire year may pass in the ordinary course of things, yet the allocation of sustenance (rizq) and the decreeing of destinies (taqdir) occur uniquely on the Night of Power ⟪ar:لیلۃ القدر⟫.[^cite-1]

All the channels (munāfiẓ, the openings through which divine grace flows) that customarily open at different times throughout the other months are opened simultaneously during the days consecrated to God. On the Day of God's City, emanation (ishrāq) issues forth from all eighteen channels together.[^cite-2] In this month, one must increase supplication, worship, invocation, and petitions for forgiveness, so that the Lord might pardon one's afflictions, have mercy upon the penitent, conceal one's transgressions, and bestow compassion upon the sincere.

| Mercy (Raḥma) | Kindness (Riʿāya) |
| --- | --- |
| Forgiveness of sins | Concealment of sins |

The more a person intensifies supplication, worship, and entreaty for forgiveness during this month, the more Allāh increases sustenance and prolongs the span of life. Furthermore, sustenance is amplified through kindness to all people, expenditure of wealth in the way of Allāh, and the bestowal of mercy upon the needy.

The virtue of this month surpasses all other months because it stands concealed within a supreme reality ⟪ar:محتجب (عین عظمیۃ)⟫ that dominates all else ⟪ar:محتجبین⟫. The matters of sustenance and lifespan are placed entirely within its control. For this reason, the Imamic veils (hijāb, the hierarchies of spiritual authority) possess the power to bring forth creation (takwīn), to dissolve (taḥlīl), to originate anew (inshāʾ), and to decree (taqdīr)—thereby increasing and decreasing ⟪ar:زیاد والنقصان⟫ the apportionments of life and sustenance. Imām Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (upon him be peace) declared: "Allāh possesses the power to alter all things save the Imamate." He does not merely transform the decrees and principles of Imamate itself, for Allāh has concealed this immutable truth in the verse:

> ⟪quran 33:62⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سُنَّةَ ٱللَّهِ فِى ٱلَّذِينَ خَلَوْا۟ مِن قَبْلُۖ وَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّةِ ٱللَّهِ تَبْدِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *That was the Way of Allah in the case of those who passed away of old, and you will not find any change in the Way of Allah.*

Whoever draws near to Allāh through any of seventy good qualities in this month becomes as one who has fulfilled an obligatory duty. And whoever performs a single obligatory deed during this month accomplishes the equivalent of seventy such deeds in comparison with other months—such is the magnitude of the blessings (barakāt) that descend upon these righteous acts in Ramaḍān! These blessings are manifest in this month because they ascend and descend through the nights ⟪ar:زبد⟫ of this consecrated time. Once the month has passed, these blessings do not shine forth again until the city of Allāh returns in the following year.

> ⟪quran 62:10⟫
> ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا قُضِيَتِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةُ فَٱنتَشِرُوا۟ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَٱبْتَغُوا۟ مِن فَضْلِ ٱللَّهِ وَٱذْكُرُوا۟ ٱللَّهُ كَثِيرًۭا لَّعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *Then when the (Jumu'ah) Salat (prayer) is finished, you may disperse through the land, and seek the Bounty of Allah (by working, etc.), and remember Allah much, that you may be successful.*

## The Apportionment of Sustenance (Rizq) and Life-Span (ʿUmr)

When the city of Ramaḍān arrived, Maulānā ʿAlī (upon him be peace) would say to his children: "Exert yourselves with your souls, ⟪ar-quote:فَأَجھِدُ نُفُوسَکُم فَأِنَّ فِیہِ تَقسِیمُ الاَرزَاق وَتَوقِیتُ الآجاَل وَیَکِتبُ وَفدَ اللّٰہِ اَلَّذِینَ یَفِدُّونَ عَلَیہ⟫ for in this month is apportioned the sustenance of all, the terms of all lifespans are fixed, and recorded are those of God's delegation who are prepared to proceed unto Him."[^cite-3]

### Esoteric Sustenance

When Allāh created us in this earthly realm, He ordained for each soul both sustenance and lifespan; yet these continue to increase and decrease according to one's spiritual virtues and moral excellences. In the inward dimension, the true sustenance is the knowledge that the Imām has ordained for us—the attainment of our proper station within the temple of his person. Based upon our deeds and conduct throughout these nights, our allotted sustenance in the inward dimension is determined.

Allāh (mighty and exalted) declares: "Sustenance is apportioned" ⟪ar-quote:الرزق مقسوم⟫—and yet He also affirms that He increases sustenance in this month. This increase of sustenance signifies that Allāh grants to a person the portion that was destined for another, as a gift, because of his righteous deeds; those who were indifferent to knowledge and action thereby forfeit their share, which becomes held in trust until they prove capable of receiving it, or it remains transferred to the one who has shown himself worthy.

One's own true sustenance is termed ⟪ar:'رزق'⟫ (rizq), while the sustenance of others held in one's keeping as a divine trust is called ⟪ar:'فضل'⟫ (faḍl, grace or bounty). It is one's inherent sustenance that carries one toward supreme exaltation. But those gifts which are not rightfully one's own, bestowed instead as a trial and means of spiritual refinement, are termed grace (faḍl). These are granted because the one to whom they were originally destined has not yet cultivated within himself the capacity to receive them. When he at last becomes capable, Allāh will restore his grace to its rightful master.

Allāh commands in the Qurʾān (with the meaning): "After the Friday prayer is concluded, spread throughout the land and seek Allāh's bounty." ⟪quran 62:10⟫ In like manner, if a believer bears many sins, then Imām al-Zamān (upon him be peace) withholds his manifestation for a time and forbids him entry into the temple of his person. Either Allāh subjects him to trials whereby he is purified, or Allāh transfers his sustenance to another and renders it a means of his spiritual cleansing. All these matters rest upon divine justice and reckoning.

### Inner Life-Span (ʿUmr)

In one sense, the life-span (ʿUmr, literally "age") refers to the duration of our station or rank—the period required for us to ascend to that level. Our years are measured by our spiritual attainment. Should the efforts and righteous deeds of one exceed the bounds set by his station, his rank is elevated and he is granted access to a higher limit. Conversely, through sin one may be reduced in rank, descending from one station to a lower one. Thus does our life-span perpetually transform.

In truth, ʿUmr denotes the period of our residence within the temple of the Imām's person. The sons of the Imām ascend to the Imām's temple; yet should they fall short through transgression, they are removed after a time and subjected to training by other Imāms. Those whose deeds are deficient are given opportunity to perfect them; those whose knowledge is incomplete are granted instruction, until they are raised to the standard of that established temple, and so may be counted among his grand congregation.

### God's Delegation (Wafd Allāh)

The delegation of Allāh (wafd Allāh) refers to those souls prepared to proceed into the temple of the Imām's person—those destined for spiritual elevation and union. The Scribe (kitāb) signifies Imām al-Zamān (upon him be peace). He determines which souls shall and shall not ascend to his temple. It is as a father who arranges the betterment of his son in the outward life—and all such matters are enacted by the obedience to Imām al-Zamān ⟪ar:عین عظیمۃ⟫.[^cite-4]

### Apportionment on the Night of Power

On the night of the Wedding ⟪ar:لیلۃ القدر⟫, the sustenance of all persons within the ranks is distributed. On this same night, a portion of the humanity of the Imām connects to the partial immunity from error ⟪ar:'عصمت جزوی'⟫ within the heart of the one who supplicates. From the believer to the Imām, all points of being consist solely in recognizing the one above them. The Imām grants sustenance to those above him and to those below him. This is called "stability" (qarār).

| *Makhzān* (Storehouse) | A great warehouse, a permanent depot |
| --- | --- |
| *Qarār* (Stability) | A final station, an established seat |

Upon this night, the Imām (upon him be peace) determines both sustenance and lifespan. Life is lengthened through virtue and shortened through transgression. Should the Imām will it, he may remove the trials and tribulations, even the sufferings that were decreed as consequence of one's deeds. All such blessings flow from righteous action, from a prayer that wards off calamity, or from one who seeks intercession through the saints—the successors of the Imām (upon him be peace). The Imām possesses power over all things. Imām Jaʿfar as-Ṣādiq declared: "Allāh possesses the authority to originate (ibdāʾ) and to decree (mashīʾa) all things save the Imamate" ⟪ar-quote:اِنَّ لِی اللّٰہِ البُدِّ وَالمَشِیَّۃ فِی کُلِّ شَئی اِلاَّ فِی الامَامَۃ⟫.[^cite-5]

Likewise, the Imām may increase or decrease the lifespan of a rank. Should a soul wish to enter beneath a certain limit, yet the time for his appearance has not yet come, the Imām extends the age of that exalted limit until that soul is born, so that the Imām may thereupon invite him and call to him. He who is designated to come accepts the invitation of the Imām and merges with him. All of this unfolds according to the invitations of the divine order (duʿāʾ al-ʿālam).

## The Reward of a Single Good Deed in Shahrullāh

Allāh the Almighty has declared that in this month, whoever performs a good deed receives its reward seventy times over. That is, one who draws near to Allāh through the righteous deeds of the age of Muḥammad (peace and blessings be upon him) becomes as one who has progressed from the realm of the Mustajīb (the one who responds—the first spiritual stage) into the realm of faith (īmān). In the time of Muḥammad (peace be upon him), the reward for becoming a believer through acceptance of the Mustajīb's guidance equals the reward acquired throughout all previous ages. For this reason, the reward of a good deed in the city of Allāh is seventy times greater: by dwelling in this Muhammadan period, one obtains the recompense of dwelling in the seven preceding ages (Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, Muḥammad, and Muḥammad ibn Ismāʿīl).

Whoever fulfills a single obligatory duty in the city of Allāh fulfills seventy such duties in relation to other months. That is, the reward of one who establishes a spiritual limit (ḥadd) within the scope of the dāʿī (the Summoner) equals the reward of one who had received and perfected seventy limits in the previous ages. It is the office of every person of rank to elevate those beneath him. Should a dāʿī prepare one religious scholar (mullā), his reward is equivalent to having prepared and established seventy such scholars in the previous ages. Should a dāʿī establish a new hierarchy (manṣab), his reward is as though he had prepared and established seventy hierarchies in the ages past.

For this reason, the Muhammadan period—presided over by Maulānā Abū Ṭālib (upon him be peace)—was deemed equal to one thousand limits of the previous ages. Maulānā Abū Ṭālib attributed the Muhammadan period as a single limit. When quality intensified, quantity diminished; thus did Maulānā Abū Ṭālib (upon him be peace) establish very few limits.

## The Reward of Hosting an Ifṭār in Shahrullāh

Whoever causes a believer to break his fast in this month—Allāh will forgive all his sins and liberate his neck from Hell. That is, if a person of higher rank liberates one who was bound in fasting (while fasting) and causes him to become manifest (muẓhar), then Allāh forgives all the sins of that higher rank and liberates his neck, elevating his station.

### Forgiveness of Sins

Forgiveness (maghfira) signifies that the deficiencies in one's deeds (one's shortcomings and failures in spreading the summons) are made complete by Allāh, and the act of inward breaking of the fast expunges one's transgressions.

### Liberation of the Neck from Fire

To liberate the neck (taḥrīr al-raqaba) means that one shall be protected from the fire of examinations and trials. In the hereafter, one will be granted a state wherein such errors are impossible, and one's station will be exalted.

In other months, whoever causes a believer to break his fast receives forgiveness of sins. Yet especially in the city of Allāh, the reward of the one who hosts the breaking of the fast is doubled, and one receives recompense as though one had hosted the ifṭār in the first age. That is, one is rewarded as if one had caused the believer to break his fast in each of the seven preceding ages, though one had done so only once.[^cite-6]

| The Reward of Breaking the Fast in Other Months | The Reward of Ifṭār in the City of Allāh |
| --- | --- |
| Forgiveness of the Deficiency of the First Age | Forgiveness of the Deficiencies of All Previous Ages + The Reward of Ifṭār in the Previous Ages |

Because of the supreme greatness of the city of Allāh, Allāh has bestowed upon the believers this grace: if they did not acquire knowledge of the virtues in the previous ages and were negligent, yet they acquire this knowledge in this age, then Allāh will forgive the deficiencies of their previous ages.

## The Thirty Days and Nights of Shahrullāh

The thirty fasts of the duties of the city of Allāh are ordained for the thirty Imāms who withdrew into concealment for reasons of spiritual protection (taqiyya). These are the thirty Imāms—from ⟪ar:مولانا علی الطیب⟫ Maulānā ʿAlī al-Ṭayyib (the 21st Imām) to ⟪ar:مہدی آخر الزمان⟫ the Mahdi of the end of time (the 50th Imām)—though they are not counted in the ordinary reckoning, their arguments are included. This is the proof that they shall be connected to the son of the Qāʾim and shall receive his caliphate in place of the Resurrection Day.

In another sense, the month of the city of Allāh is founded upon the establishment (qiyāma) that shall come at the end of concealment. In this understanding, the thirty days signify the thirty grand congregations (majmaʿa) that shall take place in this body (jism), each to be part of the fellowship of the established body. Today's form shall be the finest of these thirty, for it shall be the leaven for future forms. The month of Shaʿbān signifies the argument of the Qāʾim.

### The Thirty Nights

The thirty nights of the city of Allāh, in a higher dimension, signify the spiritual beings of the unseen realm. These entities are manifest in the night because they are concealed and do not appear in the sensible world.

## The Three Decades of Shahrullāh—Mercy, Forgiveness, Liberation from Fire

The thirty days of the month of Allāh are divided into three decades (dihiqq). These three decades correspond to three sequences of Imāms:

| Mercy | From Maulānā Ḥasan to Maulānā Muḥammad ibn Ismāʿīl |
| --- | --- |
| Forgiveness | From Maulānā Muḥammad ibn Ismāʿīl to Maulānā ʿAlī ibn al-Ṭayyib |
| Liberation from Fire | From Maulānā ʿAlī ibn al-Ṭayyib to Maulānā Aḥmad ibn Muḥammad |

Each decade contains ten days and nights; in this sense, each decade comprises twenty components (day plus night). Likewise, the Muhammadan period is divided into three epochs, each containing twenty Imāms.

The days and nights of the city of Allāh are manifest and perceptible, yet behind them lie inward realities. When a believer performs outward deeds in the city of Allāh, the inner affairs are fulfilled through those actions, which illuminate his form and purify the waste.

Three other illustrious figures manifested in response to the summons of Maulānā ʿAlī al-Ṭayyib (the 21st Imām): Maulānā Maʿad ibn Ḥusayn (the 28th Imām) and Maulānā Aḥmad ibn Muḥammad (the 35th Imām). ## Thalāthat Sufarāʾ—The Three 'Envoys' of the Three Decades

Beyond these interpretations, the three decades of the city of Allāh are indicative of three stations (maqāmāt) which are unparalleled throughout the seventy ages—which are the three celestial journeys (safar).

| Mercy | Forgiveness | Liberation from Fire |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Shaybat al-Ḥamd | ʿImrān | Dhū al-Qarnayn |
| ʿAbdul Muṭṭalib | Abū Ṭālib | ʿAbdullāh |

### Shaybat al-Ḥamd and Maulānā ʿAbdul Muṭṭalib

Maulānā Hāshim (upon him be peace) established a temple through his summons, which he named ʿAbdul Muṭṭalib. When Maulānā ʿAbdul Muṭṭalib ascended from earnestness to the ultimate rank of absolute oneness (Aḥadīya), he was called Shaybat al-Ḥamd (the Whiteness of Praise). Thereafter, he advanced further, entering the ranks of the Qāʾim after traversing ten stages of divine unity (tawḥīd). At that time, he was blessed with the divine secrets, which have been expounded by Sayedna Hibatullāh in his compendium *Fayz al-Nūr al-Sārī wa Sirr al-Nūrānī*.[^cite-7]

### ʿImrān and Maulānā Abū Ṭālib

Maulānā Abū Ṭālib (upon him be peace) is the name of the veil (hijāb) who attained the station of the great Speaker (Nāṭiq) and became the most excellent among all the resident guides (Hind, Hūd, Ṣāliḥ, ʿĀd, and Khuzaymah). All resident dimensions accumulate in his dimension. And he who has reached the tenth rank of Openness is named ʿImrān.

### ʿAbdullāh and Dhū al-Qarnayn

Maulānā ʿAbdullāh has been called Dhū al-Qarnayn because he had accumulated the things of both the stored-up (mustawda) and the held-in-trust (mustazʿaf). Dhū al-Qarnayn is he who has crossed the ranks ⟪ar:قائم عقلیۃ⟫ of the rational establishment.

## The Importance of Maghfira (Forgiveness) in Shahrullāh

The Messenger of Allāh (upon him be blessings and peace) once declared: "O people, Gabriel (upon him be peace) has just come to me and extended his greeting." He said, "⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ Whoever finds the month of Ramaḍān and is not forgiven and dies, let him be cast into Hell—and may Allāh remove him." You say Āmīn. So I said Āmīn!"[^cite-8]

### In the Time of the Messenger of Allāh

⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ To ascend the pulpit is to elevate oneself ⟪ar:أستداعیت⟫ to the highest ranks. The number of persons within the ranks is one hundred twenty-four thousand. The pulpit (minbar) belongs to the people of readiness (istiʿdād), while the prayer niche (miḥrāb) signifies the people of steadfastness (istiqāʾ). The Friday sermon is delivered always upon the pulpit, never in the miḥrāb, for the summons is delivered and the miḥrāb is not. These are the people who are entrusted in the seventieth age. The Prophet's pulpit in the sacred shrine is constructed from the remains (bones) of those who appeared in the previous ages. Allāh the Almighty brings together the summaries of the ages and the treasures of the people of prophecy and compiles them in the pulpit. For this reason, prayer is commanded after placing one's trust.

Gabriel, who greeted thee, was in truth thy father ⟪ar:مولانا عبد اللہ⟫ Maulānā ʿAbdullāh, delivering this message. The city of Ramaḍān refers to the Muhammadan period, which shall continue until the appearance of the Qāʾim ʿAlī al-Dhikr (upon him be peace). Thy Sharīʿa is so complete that thou shalt carry it to the age of disclosure. Gabriel said: "Whoever finds the city of Allāh but is not forgiven shall be cast into Hell." That is, he who was born in the time of Muḥammad but did not embrace Islam on account of the multitude of his sins and lack of faith in the eternal world—when he dies, he shall descend through the seven gates of Hell. And the meaning of "Allāh's removal" of him is that he shall reach the boundary of the mineral kingdom in darkness, whence punishment shall extend to all sides. No expectation of repentance remains for the punishment that has befallen him—that is, the desire to return to truth shall be removed from him. That is, he shall not be asked the question "Dost thou wish to return to truth?" This question is posed only to those of sound intellect. Then he shall be brought before the Qāʾim ʿAlī al-Dhikr (upon him be peace) and shall then be placed in Saqqara (the lowest depths). This utterance applies to both forms and essences. There is no salvation for him, and he deserves eternal punishment. The Messenger of Allāh (upon him be blessings and peace) said, "Āmīn."

When Saqqara has completed its sentence, one shall be invited. If he accepts, there shall be a possibility of his appearing in the distant revelation.

The disbeliever is questioned concerning his sin and invited to repentance at the time of his death in each veil (layer of being) and at the time of disclosure in the next veil. Should he accept, he shall be turned back to the upper veils and shall progress upward. Should he refuse, he shall be dropped into the next veil. This is the meaning of the verse in which Allāh mentions the lesser punishment:

As often as their skins are roasted through, We shall change them for other skins that they may taste the punishment. Truly, Allah is Ever Most Powerful, All-Wise.*

So long as they remain in the veil of intellect and growth, there is hope for their regression. They then fall to the greater punishment.

### In the Period of the Imāms

In another circle, it has been stated that in the hereafter, questions shall be posed concerning both forms and essences. When the caller (the Imām) summons a Muslim to faith but he does not respond and does not accept, then salvation is impossible for him in that age, and he shall be cast into eternal torment.

The Messenger of Allāh summoned people to Islam, and the Imāms call to faith. A person who did not embrace Islam in the time of the Prophet but accepted the summons of faith in the time of the Imāms shall be saved and shall be upon the straight path. Yet still, the cleansing of his previous dimension shall remain. The virtues that appeared in the time of the Messenger of Allāh—which he did not accept through his summons—these dimensions shall then be entrusted with various duties, and their disposal shall take place in the course of disclosure.

### Lack of Forgiveness in the City of Allāh

He whose forgiveness was not granted in the city of Ramaḍān has been far removed from Allāh's mercy, for this is Gabriel's saying, which the Prophet repeats. This utterance is reserved for the people of rebellion. Whoever does not receive mercy in the first age shall not receive forgiveness, and his neck shall not be liberated. He who did not obtain it in the time of Muḥammad shall have an evil countenance. This form shall be summoned again when it is separated from its body. Should it still refuse the summons, it shall be cast into affliction.

He who has not been forgiven in the city of Allāh shall not be forgiven until the next Ramaḍān, unless he is present in the field of ʿArafāt. This is said of special dimensions. Yet if he is present at the place of ʿArafāt, then his sin is likely to be forgiven, for he is among those of the position of the Ādāmic plane.

## Three Things That Bring God's Pleasure

Imām Jaʿfar as-Ṣādiq (upon him be peace) has stated that there are three things pleasing to Allāh:

### Vigil of the Night with Prayer

That is, to remain awake in the seventy ages with ritual prayers. The Tahajjud prayer (night vigil) is connected to the true prayer, and this can only be performed by one who receives the Holy Spirit from his Imām (upon him be peace).[^cite-10]

### Meeting with the Brotherhood and Spending Upon Them

That is, to meet one another for benefit, and benefit is pleasing to Allāh. To spend (ankāḥ) is to show kindness toward them. When the Imām's summons was established in the world, those who accepted his call became brothers.

### And Fasting

The parable of fasting (sawm) is concealment. That is, to conceal the secrets which the Imām (upon him be peace) has revealed to the believers through his summons from those unworthy of receiving them. And to take advantage of such outward and inward prescriptions of the Sharīʿa, which serve to eradicate spiritual ailments. Whoever hastens to accept the Imām's summons in the hereafter will have hastened to do so in this world as well.

| *Interpretation* | Holding a gathering and delivering discourses ⟪ar:'زکواۃ'⟫ within it, and entering the mosque, exemplify concealment ⟪ar:'صوم'⟫. |

Maulānā ʿAlī has stated that seven things are from the essence of deeds. Remain attentive to them:

All things possess a purifying tax (zakāh), and the zakāh of the body is fasting. That is, fasting purifies one's body and cleanses it from the impurities that accompany bodies, corrupting both body and soul. Fever, too, causes retribution and reckoning between a person and these dimensions, and also causes removal of these dimensions from the body; yet the effect caused by fasting is not caused by fever. May Allāh have mercy upon the community of thy time, that the city of Ramaḍān should remove from their bodies and from the waste accumulated in that year. These are the kinds of spiritual impurities that even fever cannot remove, yet they are removed by fasting.[^cite-11]

## The Prophet's Bedding and Waistband (in Ramaḍān)

It is narrated that the Messenger of Allāh (upon him be blessings and peace) would perform three acts during the final days of the city of Allāh, in which the Night of Power occurs:

| 1 | He would wrap his bed |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | He would tighten his waistband |
| 3 | He would wake his family on the 23rd night and sprinkle water upon the faces of the sleeping |

In the same manner, Maulānā Fāṭima would not allow anyone to sleep that night, and she would say: "Whoever loses the goodness of that night is deprived."

### The Bed and Waistband of the Messenger of Allāh

The Prophet's bed was one of his lowest veils (hijāb), and his waistband was one of his highest veils. On account of the nobility of that night, he would not employ either of these two veils so that he might remain awake in supreme worship during these ten nights.

Thy outward bed and waist were made of ascending dimensions (aqṭāb). The waistband cloth was of a higher grade of dimension than the bedding. The bed was wrapped and the waistband was drawn tight. To wrap the bed and tighten the waistband means to place both these types of dimension ⟪ar:قامت الفیۃ⟫ within thee. That is, those virtues have been developed and thou art becoming the catalyst to elevate them to a higher level.

### The Family of the Messenger of Allāh

On the 23rd night, the people thou wouldst awaken are in truth thy family of the feast, and their faces are the highest limits by which they are recognized. His sprinkling of water upon the faces of his family on that night and waking them was in fact a matter of praising the virtue of the veiled Fatir (Maulānā Fāṭima, who is the embodiment of the 23rd Night) through their limits. And through the knowledge of such inner virtues, the insights are weakened in reaching the truth.

The apparent water that he would sprinkle upon people's faces was also made of ascending dimensions. Those who slept were those oblivious to the grace and virtues of Maulānā Fāṭima. He would awaken them with the knowledge of their own knowledge.

### The Inner Facts of the Acts of Maulānā Fāṭima

Maulānā Fāṭima, who was the veil of Fatir (upon him be peace), did not allow any of her family to sleep that night. The family refers to the People of the Fatimid Summons (Ahl al-Dawat al-Fāṭimī), who are the Assembly of the Trustees. That is, she never allowed her congregation to be heedless of the knowledge and wisdom of her Savior.

On the night of the Night of Power, there is an assembly of the concealed veils (muḥtajab) and the revealed veils (hijāb) of the Assembly of the Trustees, and the treasury (zakhīr) of Maulānā Fāṭima (the lowest veil of Fāṭima) and the foam and radiance of Sa'ud and Habut. The treasuries are those who have not yet reached the temple; Joints transform into tongues.

On this night, the Prophet would introduce his people to the people of the summons through the veiled Fatir (Maulānā Fāṭima al-Zahrāʾ), and Maulānā Fāṭima would introduce her people to her summons through her concealed Fatir.

## Yā Umma Abīhā—'O Mother of Her Father' (Fāṭima's Epithet)

The Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) would address Maulānā ⟪ar-quote:یا ام ابیھا⟫ Fāṭima thus: "O mother of her father." The mother of the Holy Prophet was Ḥazrat Āminah, who became the Gateway of Gateways (Bāb al-Abwāb) of Maulānā ʿAbdullāh and the heart of the Holy Prophet. And he gave this temple of thine to Maulānā Fāṭima. That is, Maulānā Āminah went forth to her daughter Maulānā Fāṭima. For this reason, he called her Umm Abīhā (Mother of Her Father), likening Maulānā Fāṭima to Maulānā Āminah.
