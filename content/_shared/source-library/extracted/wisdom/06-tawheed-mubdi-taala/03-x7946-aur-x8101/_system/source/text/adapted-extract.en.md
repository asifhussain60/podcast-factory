## What Is Faith?

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ taught that to believe in God is the most excellent of all deeds, and that for this very reason its recompense exceeds that of any other. He defined īmān (faith) as the testimony — ⟪ar-quote:أشھد ان لا الہ الا اللہ وحدہ لا شریک لہ و أشھد ان محمد عبدہ و رسولہ⟫ — that there is no god but God, that He is One and has no partner, and that Muḥammad is His servant and His messenger. Paradise is true, hellfire is true, the rising from the graves is true, and the coming of the Hour is true. Faith is the affirmation of ⟪ar:انبیا و مرسلین⟫ and of ⟪ar:ائمہ طاہرین علیہم السلام⟫; it is obedience to ⟪ar:امام علیہ السلام⟫, observance of all that God has laid upon His servants, and abstention from what He has forbidden. To follow the Imam and to receive what he says is itself īmān.[^cite-1]

The Imam likewise held that faith is the name given to three things in their unity:

| Confirmation in the heart | Utterance with the tongue | Action through the limbs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Heartfelt affirmation | Spoken testimony | Embodied works |

He was asked: does affirmation of the heart together with confession of the tongue not suffice? He answered, ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان عمَلُ کلُّہُ⟫ — "Faith is action in its entirety." Speech itself is an action, the action proper to the tongue; just so, believing and the acquisition of knowledge are the proper acts of the heart, and obedience by every limb to what God and His Messenger have commanded is the action of the body. Thus **faith is nothing other than the sum of these actions taken together**.

| Act of the heart | Act of the tongue | Act of the body |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Belief and knowledge | Confession and utterance | Works of every limb |

God Most High has said:

> ⟪quran 2:82⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and do righteous deeds — they are the companions of Paradise; therein they shall abide forever.*
> — **Quran 2:82 (inline widget)**

Wherever the Qurʾān names faith, it conjoins to it the doing of righteous deeds; the two are inseparable. They are commonly distinguished because faith belongs to the heart and action to the limbs — yet it is precisely the union of the two that constitutes one a believer. ⟪ar:امیر المومنین⟫, in ⟪ar:نہج البلاغہ⟫, has said: ⟪ar-quote:الأیمان اعتقاد بالقلب و عمل بالارکان و اقرار بالسان⟫ — "Faith is conviction held in the heart, action performed by the limbs, and confession uttered by the tongue."[^cite-2] Faith begins in the heart and finds its term in righteous action, action that itself includes the tongue's confession. Faith therefore moves outward from the heart into the tongue and the limbs; Islam, by contrast, begins with the utterance of ⟪ar:کلمہ لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ and moves inward from the tongue to the limbs. The light of faith arises within and shines outward; the light of Islam is outward radiance alone. Islam belongs to the outward sharīʿa (the revealed law); īmān belongs to the inner meaning that this law conceals and discloses, the bāṭin that the daʿwa (the summons) was instituted to unveil.[^cite-3]

### Affirmation and Security

Faith — īmān — means in its first sense to verify, to declare true; in a second sense it means to grant security. The verb āmana is transitive and never complete in itself: it always requires an object. Two things, then, are needed for faith: the act of affirming, and that which is affirmed. If īmān is taken to mean taṣdīq (verification), one must ask: who is being verified? The one whose word is verified is called the ⟪ar:مُصَدِّق⟫. And if īmān is taken instead to mean the granting of security, again one must ask: who is being given security, and against what?

The Prophet, peace be upon him, joined both senses in a single saying: ⟪ar-quote:ھِینَ اَلمُؤمِن مَن آمَنَ جَورِہِ بِوَایقَۃ⟫ — "The believer is he who has secured his neighbour against his own harm." It is on account of these twin senses that a man is named muʾmin (believer), and that ⟪ar:اَلمُؤمِنُ⟫ likewise stands among the ⟪ar:اسمآء الحسنی⟫ (the Most Beautiful Names) of God Himself: the servant is called muʾmin because he affirms God and His promise and warning — ⟪ar:وَعَدُ وَعِیدْ⟫ — and God is named ⟪ar:اَلمُؤمِنُ⟫ because He grants His servant security from the punishment that destroys.

| Subject | Act | Object |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Believer | Verification (taṣdīq) | God, the Messenger, and what they have commanded |
| Believer | Granting security (amān) | Fellow creatures |
| God | Granting security (amān) | The believer, against destruction |

## The Faith of the Heart, the Tongue, and the Body

The Imam said: God has made faith obligatory upon every limb of the children of Adam and has apportioned it among them. There is, therefore, no organ among the twelve organs of the human body that is not bound by some practical obligation of belief, and the faith demanded of each limb differs in kind from that demanded of the others. God has divided faith among the human organs as follows:[^cite-4]

| Members | Heart | Tongue | Ear | Eye | Hand | Foot | Face | Faraj |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 12 | 1 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 1 | 1 |

### The Faith of the Heart

The heart is the seat from which the human being draws understanding and insight, and it is the sovereign of the entire body. Without its judgement and its command, no organ may take in or expel anything whatsoever. The faith that God has enjoined upon the heart consists of confession, knowledge, conviction, acceptance, and assent. The believer is to confess in his heart that God is One, that there is no god but He, ⟪ar-quote:وحدہ لا شریک لہ⟫ — One, without partner, alone, free of need, neither consort nor offspring. ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫ is the servant of God and His true Messenger. After this, the heart must acknowledge all that God has revealed, the Book and what the Prophet has brought. And after this affirmation, it must seek the knowledge of the ḥudūd (the spiritual ranks), for it is by such knowledge that faith is strengthened. This, then, is the practical and intellectual faith that God has imposed upon the heart. It is indicated in the following verses:

> ⟪quran 16:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَن كَفَرَ بِٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ إِيمَنِهِۦٓ إِلَّا مَنْ أُكْرِهَ وَقَلْبُهُۥ مُطْمَىِٕنٌّۢ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ وَلَكِن مَّن شَرَحَ بِٱلْكُفْرِ صَدْرًۭا فَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Whoever disbelieved in Allah after his belief, except him who is forced thereto and whose heart is at rest with Faith but such as open their breasts to disbelief, on them is wrath from Allah, and theirs will be a great torment.*
> Whosoever disbelieves in Allah after he has believed, except him who is compelled and whose heart is at rest in his faith, but whosoever opens his heart to disbelief, upon them shall be wrath from Allah, and there awaits them a great torment.

### The Faith of the Tongue

The faith God has enjoined upon the tongue is the faith of utterance and exposition: namely, that the confession and conviction the heart has formed must be articulated in speech. As God has said:

> ⟪quran 3:84⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْنَا وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَإِسْمَعِيلَ وَإِسْحَقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَٱلْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَآ أُوتِىَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَٱلنَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُۥ مُسْلِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH): "We believe in Allah and in what has been sent down to us, and what was sent down to Ibrahim (Abraham), Isma'il (Ishmael), Ishaque (Isaac), Ya'qub (Jacob) and Al-Asbat [the twelve sons of Ya'qub (Jacob)] and what was given to Musa (Moses), 'Iesa (Jesus) and the Prophets from their Lord. We make no distinction between one another among them and to Him (Allah) we have submitted (in Islam)." *

### The Faith of the Two Eyes

God has enjoined upon the eyes that they not gaze at what is forbidden, and that they be lowered from what He has prohibited. This restraint is itself an entry into faith and the proper labour of the eyes. The principle is set out in this verse:

> ⟪quran 24:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ ‎⟫
> *Tell the believing men to lower their gaze (from looking at forbidden things), and protect their private parts (from illegal sexual acts, etc.). That is purer for them. Verily, Allah is All-Aware of what they do.*
> Tell the believers to lower their gaze and guard their private parts. That is purer for them; God knows what they do.
> — **Quran 24:30 (inline widget)**

### The Faith of the Faraj (Private Parts)

The meaning of this verse is that the believing man should not look upon the private parts of his brother, and should guard his own, that no one look upon them. ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ has explained that all the verses of the Holy Qurʾān concerning the guarding of the private parts refer to their guarding from adultery — but the verse on ⟪ar:غَضُّ بَصَر⟫ above implies, in addition, that one ought not even to look upon his own private parts. In another passage God speaks of the joint responsibilities of the heart, the tongue, the eye, and the ear together:

> ⟪quran 17:36⟫

> *And follow not (O man i.e., say not, or do not or witness not, etc.) that of which you have no knowledge (e.g. one's saying: "I have seen," while in fact he has not seen, or "I have heard," while he has not heard). Verily! The hearing, and the sight, and the heart, of each of those you will be questioned (by Allah).*
> And do not pursue that of which you have no knowledge. Indeed, the hearing, the sight, and the heart will be questioned.
> — **Quran 17:36 (inline widget)**

> ⟪quran 41:22⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَن يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَآ أَبْصَرُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَكِن ظَنَنتُمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫

### The Faith of the Two Hands

God has enjoined upon the two hands that they not be employed in any unlawful work, but rather in almsgiving, in benevolence, in maintaining the ties of kin, in struggle on the path of God, and in the rite of ablution — as He says in this verse:[^cite-5]

> ⟪quran 5:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ فَٱغْسِلُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْمَرَافِقِ وَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِرُئُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُمْ جُنُبًۭا فَٱطَّهَّرُوا۟ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَآءَ أَحَدٌۭ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَمَسْتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا۟ مَآءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا۟ صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا فَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُم مِّنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُم مِّنْ حَرَجٍۢ وَلَكِن يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you intend to offer As-Salat (the prayer), wash your faces and your hands (forearms) up to the elbows, rub (by passing wet hands over) your heads, and your feet up to ankles.&nbsp;If you are in a state of Janaba (i.e.&nbsp;had a sexual discharge), purify yourself (bathe your whole body).&nbsp;But if you are ill or on a journey or any of you comes from answering the call of nature,  or you have been in contact with women (i.e.&nbsp;sexual intercourse) and you find no water, then perform Tayammum with clean earth and rub therewith your faces and hands. Allah does not want to place you in difficulty, but He wants to purify you, and to complete His Favour on you that you may be thankful.*
> O you who believe! When you stand up for prayer, wash your mouth and your hands up to the elbows, and wipe your heads and your feet up to the ankles. And if you are unclean, then take a bath. > — **Quran 5:6 (inline widget)**

> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ زَحْفًۭا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلْأَدْبَارَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When you meet those who disbelieve, in a battle-field, never turn your backs to them.*
> O you who believe! When you meet the disbelievers on the battlefield, do not turn your backs to them.

Thereafter (is the time) either for generosity (i.e. free them without ransom), or ransom (according to what benefits Islam), until the war lays down its burden. Thus [you are ordered by Allah to continue in carrying out Jihad against the disbelievers till they embrace Islam (i.e. are saved from the punishment in the Hell-fire) or at least come under your protection], but if it had been Allah's Will, He Himself could certainly have punished them (without you). Thus God has made it obligatory to strike at the necks on the field of battle, for the striking of necks is the labour of the two hands and is therefore part of faith.

> *And walk not on the earth with conceit and arrogance. Verily, you can neither rend nor penetrate the earth, nor can you attain a stature like the mountains in height.*

> *&nbsp;O you who believe (Muslims)! When the call is proclaimed for the Salat (prayer) on the day of Friday (Jumu'ah prayer), come to the remembrance of Allah [Jumu'ah religious talk (Khutbah) and Salat (prayer)] and leave off business (and every other thing), that is better for you if you did but know!*

> ⟪quran 2:143⟫

And We made the Qiblah (prayer direction towards Jerusalem) which you used to face, only to test those who followed the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from those who would turn on their heels (i.e. disobey the Messenger). Indeed, Allah is Most Merciful and Merciful to the people.
> — **Quran 2:143 (inline widget)**
**In this verse, God has named the prayer itself "faith."**

Whoever departs this world in such a state that his limbs have been preserved from sins, and every limb has discharged its duty with grace, will receive from God a complete faith and will enter Paradise. As for the one who has betrayed God in even the smallest matter, or transgressed God's commands through these organs, he will come before God in a condition of deficient faith. And whoever has placed the collar of the covenant of God and of the Imām of the age upon his neck, but then neither acts upon it nor seeks knowledge, shall not be called a believer. Verbal faith alone will avail him nothing; he will have no station in the sight of God or of the Imām, and such a person will be reckoned as one outside the circle of the Imām of the age.

## Faith and the Three Categories of Believers

According to the ⟪ar:قرآن حکیم⟫ (the Glorious Qurʾān), faith is of three kinds:

| 1 | 2 | 3 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pure Faith | Imperfect Faith | Iman Mashbah al-Shirk |

### Pure Faith

Those who possess pure faith are the believers whom God Himself praises and to whom He gives the glad tidings of Paradise. They are the two pillars of faith — al-ṣidq (truthfulness) and al-amān (security) — and they hold the rank of the righteous, for although their bodies dwell in this world, their souls reside elsewhere. They appear outwardly to belong to ⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫ (the abode of falsehood), yet inwardly they belong to ⟪ar:دار الصدق و صفا⟫ (the abode of truthfulness and purity). In other words, they live with their gaze fixed upon the hereafter while their bodies walk through the present world, whereas the ordinary Muslim does the reverse: he lives in this world and only occasionally turns his gaze toward what is to come.

Security (amān) descends upon them in the sense that, when their ⟪ar:نفوس لطیفۃ⟫ (subtle souls) at last attain ⟪ar:دار السلام⟫ (the Abode of Peace), they are spared every difficulty that befalls the body after death; for them, death itself is a fragrance and a release. By death they are freed altogether from ⟪ar:دار کدورت⟫ (the abode of turbidity) and brought into everlasting safety. Of such a death the Prophet — peace be upon him — said: "⟪ar-quote:اَلمَوتُ رِیحاَنَۃُ المُؤمِن⟫ (Death is the fragrance of the believer)." And ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ (Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq, peace be upon him), pointing to his throat, said: "When the soul of my believer reaches here, it rejoices!" Such a soul, having attained recognition of ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی علیہ السلام⟫ (the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī, peace be upon him) and the ⟪ar:ائمہ طاھرین⟫ (the pure Imams), and having held fast to the ⟪ar:ولی أمر⟫ (the holder of the divine command) of its age, owes no obedience that is not already directed toward God.[^cite-7]

The Prophet — peace and blessings be upon him — declared that those addressed by the divine call ⟪ar:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ (O you who believe) are led and presided over by ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ (ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him), and that this imāmate and leadership shall remain in the hands of ʿAlī's descendants for as long as those addressed by ⟪ar:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ remain in the world. Because this address encompasses both the ⟪ar:مُتَقَدِّمِینَ⟫ (those who came before) and the ⟪ar:مُتَأَخِّرِینَ⟫ (those who come after), the religious leadership it establishes endures ⟪ar:اِلیٰ یَومِ الدِّینَ⟫ (until the Day of Judgement). Whoever disobeys the Imams of the line of ⟪ar:محمد و علی(ﷺ)⟫ (Muḥammad and ʿAlī) therefore disobeys God and His Messenger; such a one will be called ⟪ar:خارجی⟫ (an outsider, a renegade), and when he dies, he dies the death of ignorance (jāhiliyya). God — exalted is He — has said:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority.&nbsp;(And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day.&nbsp;That is better and more suitable for final determination.&nbsp;*
>O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger and those in authority among you. And if you dispute among yourselves about anything, refer it to Allah and His Messenger, if you believe in Allah and the Last Day. That is better and better in the end.
> — **Quran 4:59 (inline widget)**

### Imperfect Faith

The second kind of affirmation of God and His Messenger is the affirmation of those whose faith remains incomplete. These are the souls who have only just stepped into the circle of the ⟪ar:دعوت ایمانیۃ⟫ (the daʿwa of faith) and stand at its threshold as ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ (the respondent), still ignorant of the greater part of religious knowledge. Their case is like that of a newborn child: although he is called a human being by virtue of the completeness of his bodily organs, he stands in need of supervision and protection at every moment, lacking the strength to make use of those organs of his own accord. He can do nothing for himself. So too the ⟪ar:مستجیب⟫ stationed at the lowest ranks of the daʿwa is rightly called a believer, and yet remains wholly dependent upon the instruction of his superior at every step. This deficient station of faith is what God describes when He says:

> ⟪quran 4:136⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ آمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ مِن قَبْلُ ۚ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَلًۢا بَعِيدًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Believe in Allah, and His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and the Book (the Quran) which He has sent down to His Messenger, and the Scripture which He sent down to those before (him), and whosoever disbelieves in Allah, His Angels, His Books, His Messengers, and the Last Day, then indeed he has strayed far away.*
> O you who believe! Believe in Allah and His Messenger and the Book which He has sent down to His Messenger and the Book which He sent down to those before it, and whoever disbelieves in Allah and His angels and His Messengers and the Last Day, he indeed has strayed far away.
> — **Quran 4:136 (inline widget)**

Here God addresses His believers: "O you who believe — believe!" If those addressed by ⟪ar:یَا أَیُّھَا الَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ were already ⟪ar:کامل الایمان⟫ (perfect in faith), then the further command to ⟪ar:' آمَنُو'⟫ (believe!) would be redundant. Yet God on the one hand bears witness to their belief, and on the other commands them to believe. The resolution is plain: it is the lower rank of believers who are being told to make good the faith they have already professed. They are told, in effect: you have entered the circle of faith after the age of natural birth and have undergone a second, religious birth; now press on in the knowledge of religion until you become a perfect believer. Remain in the company of the truthful — those who are the parents of religion — take the benefit of their spiritual training, and reach the station of perfection. Only then will you be worthy to dwell, no longer in ⟪ar:دار الکذب⟫, but in ⟪ar:دار الصدق و امان⟫ (the abode of truthfulness and security) — just as little children, by accepting the guidance and discipline of their parents and teachers, eventually attain the standing of their elders. To this very pattern God alludes when He says:

> ⟪quran 52:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And those who believe and whose offspring follow them in Faith, to them shall We join their offspring, and We shall not decrease the reward of their deeds in anything. Every person is a pledge for that which he has earned.*
> And those who believed, and their descendants followed them in faith, We will unite with them their descendants, and We will not deprive them of aught of their deeds; every person is a pledge for his work.
> — **Quran 52:21 (inline widget)**

In this verse, the term "offspring" (dhurriyya) must be read as referring to religious or causal descendants and not to biological lineage. That is to say, the ⟪ar:مستجیبین⟫ (the respondents) of the lower ranks have followed the limits of the highest ranks of the daʿwa, and in the end God has joined them to their elders and raised them to the rank of those who preceded them.[^cite-8]

### Iman Mushābih al-Shirk

This is the kind of faith that has been mixed with associationism (shirk) and bears no real relation to true belief in God and His Messenger. The meaning of faith in Islam is that we accept the command given by the Messenger of God through divine revelation in its original form, without any alteration, ⟪ar:مِنُّ و عَن⟫ (without grudging reservation or evasion), and without the least hesitation; for in such submission lies the purity of our souls and our salvation.

The Almighty commands us: ⟪ar-quote:وَمَآ آتَیكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوہُ وَمَا نَھٰكُمْ عَنْہُ فَانتَھوا۟⟫ — "Take whatever the Messenger gives you, and abstain from whatever he forbids you." That is, do not insert your own opinion in the slightest into the command and obedience owed to the Messenger. The one who, in his worship, in matters of the lawful and the forbidden, and in every other religious affair, stands in direct relation to the Prophet — peace and blessings be upon him — and refrains from inserting personal opinion and conjecture, is a perfect believer. But the one who blends his own desires with the Messenger's commands, and resorts to opinion, conjecture, and personal ijtihād (independent juristic reasoning) in order to satisfy those desires, is no believer but an innovator (mubtadiʿ). Because he has set his own judgement alongside God's, his faith has become tainted with shirk, leading him into error in place of guidance and to ruin in place of salvation. No prayer he prays and no fast he keeps will profit him.

The revelation given to the Messenger may be likened to rainwater that descends from the sky. Such water remains fit to drink, and capable of purifying body, clothes, and all things, only as long as it has not been contaminated. Once impurity overtakes it, that water profits a Muslim no longer, even though it is still called water; the defect that has entered it renders it unfit for drinking and useless for purification. In the same way, when the desires of the lower self are mixed into the rulings of revelation, the pure religion no longer remains; it turns into innovation (bidʿa). The Prophet — peace and blessings be upon him — said: "Every innovation is a misguidance, however beautiful it may appear." Even after innovation has crept in, the outward limbs of Islam may seem to remain in place — the followers of the Imams pray, and so do their opponents — yet the difference between the two is unmistakable. The prayers of the opponents are flung back in their faces, because they have mixed the bitterness of shirk and the impurity of innovation into their faith. Of such people God says:

> ⟪quran 12:106⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا يُؤْمِنُ أَكْثَرُهُم بِٱللَّهِ إِلَّا وَهُم مُّشْرِكُونَ ‎⟫
> *And most of them believe not in Allah except that they attribute partners unto Him [i.e. they are Mushrikun -polytheists - see Verse 6: 121].*
> And most of them believe in Allah and associate others with Him.
> — **Quran 12:106 (inline widget)**

**The Zakāt of the Polytheists**

It is reported from ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ that his father, ⟪ar:مولانا محمد الباقر علیہ السلام⟫ (our master, Imam Muḥammad al-Bāqir, peace be upon him), one day asked ⟪ar:حضرت جابر بن عبد اللہ الانصاری رضی اللہ عنہ⟫ (Jābir ibn ʿAbd Allāh al-Anṣārī, may God be pleased with him): "O Jābir, has God made zakāt obligatory upon the polytheist?" ⟪ar:حضرت جابر⟫ replied: "No, God has made zakāt obligatory only upon the Muslims." The Imam al-Bāqir then said: "Have you not heard the words of God":

> ⟪quran 41:6-7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أَنَا۠ بَشَرٌۭ مِّثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ أَنَّمَآ إِلَهُكُمْ إِلَهٌۭ وَحِدٌۭ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُشْرِكِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَفِرُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH): "I am only a human being like you. It is inspired in me that your Ilah (God) is One Ilah (God - Allah), therefore take Straight Path to Him (with true Faith Islamic Monotheism) and obedience to Him, and seek forgiveness of Him. And woe to Al-Mushrikun (the disbelievers in the Oneness of Allah, polytheists, idolaters, etc. - see V.2:105). Those who give not the Zakat and they are disbelievers in the Hereafter.*
> say to them, 'I am only a human being like you.' It is revealed to me that your God is One God, so go straight to Him and ask His forgiveness. And woe to the polytheists who do not pay the alms, and they are disbelievers in the Hereafter.
> — **Quran 41:6-7 (inline widget)**

⟪ar:حضرت جابر⟫ said: "When I heard this verse from the blessed tongue of ⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫, it seemed to me as though I had never read it before, although it had been in the Book of God all along." ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ then explained: "This verse refers to those who have been admitted to the walāya (loyalty and devotion) of ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ and who have then given their zakāt to one who has set himself up against ⟪ar:مولانا علی علیہ السلام⟫." The verse therefore shows that God has named those Muslims who withhold their zakāt from its rightful recipient as polytheists themselves.

## An Account of the Eight Ranks of Faith

Just as Paradise has eight gates, faith has eight corresponding ranks:[^cite-10]

| 1 | Kindness to parents — this is the highest rank of faith. |

| 2 | Showing favour to kindred. |
| 3 | Kindness to orphans. |
| 4 | Kindness to the poor. |

## The Qualities of the Believer

A believer is not truly a believer until his intellect is perfected; and his intellect is not perfected until these ten qualities take root within him:[^cite-11]

| 1 | The people of the world receive from him nothing but good. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | No one has any fear of evil from him. |
| 3 | He counts many of his own good deeds as fewer than the few good deeds of others. |
| 4 | He magnifies a little of his own evil and minimises much of the evil of others. |
| 5 | He never tires of meeting the needs of others. |
| 6 | He never tires of seeking knowledge, and continues to acquire it throughout his life. |
| 7 | Patience is dearer to him than honour and glory. |
| 8 | Faqr (spiritual poverty) is more precious to him than wealth, for the Prophet — peace and blessings be upon him — said: "Al-faqru fakhrī (Poverty is my pride)." |
| 9 | A little food suffices him. |
| 10 | When he meets a virtuous person, he assumes that the other is better and more pious than himself. |

## Precedence and Degrees in Faith

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (peace be upon him) teaches that, were faith a single uniform thing — admitting neither deficiency nor excess — no believer could ever surpass another. All the faithful would share equally in the rewards apportioned for belief, and the believers would stand level in ⟪ar:تفضیل⟫preference (tafḍīl). The truth runs otherwise. The believers enter Paradise by virtue of the perfection of their faith, and it is only because their faith is more ⟪ar:راجح⟫weighty (rājiḥ) that they are preferred in God's sight above others; while those ⟪ar:مقصرین⟫whose faith is deficient — the muqaṣṣirūn — enter the Fire.

Concerning the degrees of faith, ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫the Imam says: "God has appointed a contest among the believers in which they outstrip one another in faith, just as horses race against each other on the day of competition." Each believer is assigned his rank according to his precedence (sabq) in answering the call. The one who lagged in faith is not advanced above the one who preceded him, nor is the virtuous granted precedence over one yet more virtuous. This precedence is itself among the excellences that confer rank, raising the first believers of the community above those who came after. ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫For the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him), was the first of all the Companions to believe, and so he stands above all the rest. Were the precedence of the early believers not to confer real superiority, the last believer of this community could be placed alongside the first, indistinguishable from them. And had primacy not been made a criterion of virtue, many ⟪ar:ارباب فضل و کمال و زھد وتقوی⟫of the later generations — the masters of grace, perfection, asceticism, and piety (arbāb faḍl wa-kamāl wa-zuhd wa-taqwā) — would have eclipsed their predecessors in practical labour. Among the later believers there are ⟪ar:ارباب فضل و کمال⟫many masters of grace and perfection who pray longer, fast more, perform Hajj more often, and outdo the early Muslims in jihad and charity in God's path. Yet God will not have one assigned to the rear ranks of faith placed in the foremost rank, nor will He defer one whom He has advanced in faith, nor advance one whom He has deferred. The verses of precedence make this clear:

> ⟪quran 57:21⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَابِقُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ مَغْفِرَةٍۢ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَجَنَّةٍ عَرْضُهَا كَعَرْضِ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ أُعِدَّتْ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ ۚ ذَلِكَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ ذُو ٱلْفَضْلِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ ‎⟫
> *Race one with another in hastening towards Forgiveness from your Lord (Allah), and towards Paradise, the width whereof is as the width of heaven and earth, prepared for those who believe in Allah and His Messengers. That is the Grace of Allah which He bestows on whom He pleases. And Allah is the Owner of Great Bounty.*
> Hasten to forgiveness from your Lord, and to Paradise, as wide as the heavens and the earth, prepared for those who believe in Allah and His Messengers. That is the bounty of Allah; He bestows it upon whomsoever He will; and Allah is the possessor of great bounty.
> — **Quran 57:21 (inline widget)**

The > and the first to believe are the first to enter, they are the nearer to Allah.

> is for the poor who have emigrated their homes and their possessions, seeking the bounty of Allah and His pleasure, and they help Allah and His Messenger. In this verse God names ⟪ar:محاجرین و انصار و تابعین⟫the Emigrants, the Helpers, and the Followers (al-muhājirūn wa-l-anṣār wa-l-tābiʿūn) in order of rank. First, by virtue of precedence, come the ⟪ar:محاجرین اولین⟫foremost Emigrants (al-muhājirūn al-awwalūn); next, the ⟪ar:انصار⟫Helpers (al-anṣār) of the second grade; and after them the ⟪ar:تابعین مخلصین⟫sincere Followers (al-tābiʿūn al-mukhliṣūn) of the third. The same verse also records the prayer of ⟪ar:متقدمین اہل ایمان⟫the foremost among the people of faith (al-mutaqaddimūn min ahl al-īmān) seeking forgiveness, in order to establish the grace conferred on the ancients. This ordering is not a rhetorical adornment but a structural principle of the daʿwa (the summons): the first to respond to the call of God and His Messenger occupy permanent rank above those who answered later, and no later striving can dissolve that order.[^cite-12] God then sets forth the excellences of His saints in the verses that follow:

> ⟪quran 2:253⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ تِلْكَ ٱلرُّسُلُ فَضَّلْنَا بَعْضَهُمْ عَلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ ۘ مِّنْهُم مَّن كَلَّمَ ٱللَّهُ ۖ وَرَفَعَ بَعْضَهُمْ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَآتَيْنَا عِيسَى ٱبْنَ مَرْيَمَ ٱلْبَيِّنَتِ وَأَيَّدْنَهُ بِرُوحِ ٱلْقُدُسِ ۗ وَلَوْ شَآءَ ٱللَّهُ مَا ٱقْتَتَلَ ٱلَّذِينَ مِنۢ بَعْدِهِم مِّنۢ بَعْدِ مَا جَآءَتْهُمُ ٱلْبَيِّنَتُ وَلَكِنِ ٱخْتَلَفُوا۟ فَمِنْهُم مَّنْ آمَنَ وَمِنْهُم مَّن كَفَرَ ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ ٱللَّهُ مَا ٱقْتَتَلُوا۟ وَلَكِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَفْعَلُ مَا يُرِيدُ ‎⟫
> *Those Messengers! We preferred some to others; to some of them Allah spoke (directly); others He raised to degrees (of honour); and to 'Iesa (Jesus), the son of Maryam (Mary), We gave clear proofs and evidences, and supported him with Ruh-ul-Qudus [Jibrael (Gabriel)]. If Allah had willed, succeeding generations would not have fought against each other, after clear Verses of Allah had come to them, but they differed - some of them believed and others disbelieved. If Allah had willed, they would not have fought against one another, but Allah does what He likes.*
> These are apostles; We have preferred some of them above others, and of them are some with whom Allah spoke, and some of them We raised in rank. And We gave Jesus, son of Mary, clear proofs, and strengthened him with the Holy Spirit. Clear judgment had come to them, but differences arose between them, so some of them believed, and some disbelieved. Had Allah willed, they would not have fought among themselves, but Allah does what He wills.

## Outward and Inner Faith

The reward Allāh has promised for faith in the Hereafter is Paradise. The reason this recompense is reserved for the world to come is that faith is a quality which gathers around its possessor every ⟪ar:خیراتِ بشریات⟫ (good of humankind) and inclines him toward ⟪ar:فضائل الملائکۃ⟫ (the virtues of the angels). Since one cannot become an angel while yet living in this world, his reward is deferred and Paradise pledged. By the same measure, disbelief gathers about its possessor every ⟪ar:شَرُّ البشریات⟫ (evil of humankind), and Hell is the ⟪ar:وعید⟫ (threatened recompense) attached to him.

Faith itself, however, divides into two branches: outward faith and inner faith. Before turning to that division, a brief note on terms. In the lexicon of the daʿwa (the summons), ⟪ar:وعدہ⟫ (the promise) belongs to the recompense of a good deed, as in the promise of Paradise, while ⟪ar:وعید⟫ (the warning) belongs to the recompense of an evil deed, as in the threat of Hell.

### Outward Faith

Outward faith refers to the verbal confession of five things:

| 1 | **Affirmation of Allāh:** that there is but one Creator of this world — One, Living, Almighty, Wise, fashioner of every created thing. He is Master of all His creatures, and there is no partner in His ordering of the worlds. |
| --- | --- |
| 2 | **Confession of the angels** — the noblest of God's creations. Allāh appointed them to His service and worship, and made them the guardians of His world, assigning them to high offices in the execution of His decree. The angels never disobey Allāh: they perform exactly what they are commanded. |
| 3 | Acknowledgement that Allāh has chosen, from among His servants upon the earth, a certain company — the prophets and messengers — and has made the angels the intermediary between Himself and these chosen ones. The angels convey the message of their Lord to those special servants, who in turn receive glad tidings and commandments and transmit them to all creatures. |
| 4 | Acknowledgement that every message a prophet received by revelation through the angels was conveyed by him to his contemporaries in their own tongue and idiom — that is to say, confession of the **Books** revealed to all the prophets. The message has remained one; the vocabulary and style alone have changed with the ages. |
| 5 | Acknowledgement of the coming Resurrection — that the Day shall arise to inaugurate a new assembly and a wholesale change of state, when every soul's condition shall be transformed, all creatures raised, their accounts examined, good deeds rewarded, and evil deeds requited. |

The believers are those who profess these five articles wholly, of whom Allāh speaks in His Book:

> ⟪quran 2:285⟫
> ⟪ar:‏آمَنَ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ ‎⟫
> *The Messenger believes in what has been sent down to him from his Lord, and so do the believers. Each one believes in Allāh, His angels, His Books, and His Messengers. They say: "We make no distinction between one and another of His messengers"; and they say: "We hear, and we obey. We seek Your forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the return."*
> — **Quran 2:285 (inline widget)**

When a believer confesses these five outward articles with his tongue, he sets himself apart from Jew, Christian, and Magian alike. Each confession carries within it a denial of what stands opposed to it; and by this confession he binds himself to perform the prayer, the fast, the pilgrimage, the alms, and the struggle in God's path.

### Inner Faith

Inner faith is to affirm from the very bottom of one's heart the five things confessed with the tongue. By this confirmation the believer's inner rank of faith is established; the more thorough the confirmation, the higher the degrees of inner faith. It is to such believers that Allāh addresses His praise — ⟪ar:یاَ اَیُّھاَ اَلَّذِینَ آمَنُو⟫ ("O you who have believed") — and to them He has promised Paradise.

The Paradise of outward faith is one thing; the Paradise of inner faith is another. Pointing to this difference, the Prophet (peace be upon him) said: ⟪ar-quote:مَن قاَلَ لاَ اِلہَ اِلاَّ اَللّٰہ دَخَلَ اَلجَنَّۃ⟫ — "Whoever says ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ enters Paradise." Then he said: ⟪ar-quote:مَن قاَلَ لاَ اِلہَ اِلاَّ اَللّٰہ مُخلِصاً دَخَلَ اَلجَنَّۃ⟫ — "Whoever says ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ *sincerely* enters Paradise." The first group are those who pronounce ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ with the tongue alone and enter the Paradise of ⟪ar:اسلام⟫ (outward Islam); the second are those who confess with the tongue and then develop sincerity — the affirmation of the heart — and so enter the Paradise of ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ (inner faith).[^cite-14]

| Who said | Who said + Sincere |
| --- | --- |
| Confession by the tongue | Verbal confession + heartfelt affirmation |

The path to this inner faith runs through four disciplines: ⟪ar-quote:تَفکِیر وَالأعتَباَر وَ قِیاَم بِاالشَّراَءِطُھَا وَ واَجِبُ حَقُّھاَ⟫

| Reflection (*tafkīr*) | To think upon a matter and contemplate it with utmost care; to investigate the object and prove its truth to oneself. |
| --- | --- |
| Consideration (*iʿtibār*) | To set one object against another and draw the distinction or the relation between the two. |
| Qiyām bi-sharāʾiṭihā | To fulfil the conditions of this knowledge — i.e. to perform what Allāh has commanded concerning it. |
| Wājib ḥaqqahā | To render the full due of that knowledge which Allāh has made obligatory. |

Entering the Paradise of faith is no easy matter. Allāh Himself says:

> ⟪quran 3:142⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ جَهَدُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫
> *Did you suppose that you would enter Paradise before Allāh has made known those of you who strive and made known the steadfast?*
> — **Quran 3:142 (inline widget)**

(*Rasāʾil Aḥmadiyya* [Ikhwān al-Ṣafāʾ]) — Imam Aḥmad ibn ʿAbd Allāh (a.s.)

## Faith Holds Precedence over Knowledge

The elevation of ranks belongs to knowledge and to faith, not to action. The degrees of knowledge are of one order; the degrees of faith are of another. Explaining this distinction, Allāh says:

> ⟪quran 58:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجَلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! When it is said to you, "Make room in the assemblies," then make room; Allāh will make room for you. And when it is said, "Rise up," then rise up; Allāh will raise in ranks those who believe among you, and those who have been granted knowledge. Allāh is well acquainted with what you do.*
> — **Quran 58:11 (inline widget)**

In this verse Allāh declares that He raises the ranks of those who believe and of those who know. But the believers have been mentioned before the knowers, which signals that the ranks of the believers shall be raised before the ranks of those endowed with knowledge. Faith takes precedence over knowledge, because knowledge is the instrument by which faith is increased; and a thing can only be increased in measure once it already exists. That is, faith is anterior in the very order of knowing.

In this world too, faith was brought into being before knowledge. When the Messenger of Allāh (s.a.w.a.) summoned the people, he said: ⟪ar-quote:قُولُو لاَ اِلہَ اِلاَّ اللہ تُفلِحُو⟫ — "Say ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ and you shall prosper." The Prophet first commanded confession, which is the first condition of faith; he did not, at that moment, set forth the knowledge of ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫, but asked only for confession and belief in this word. Once the people had confessed, then the stages of confirmation began, in which the knowledge of ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ was unfolded and made plain.

In the same way, in ⟪ar:عالم روحانی⟫ (the spiritual world), ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ — that is, al-ʿAql al-Awwal (the First Intellect) — was endowed with ⟪ar:علم ما کان سیکون⟫ (the knowledge of what was and what shall be) only after it had confessed the oneness of ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ — Mubdiʿ Taʿālā (the Originator, Most High). For this reason, faith is anterior to knowledge.[^cite-15]

Explaining the hierarchy of those who know, the Most High declares that above every knower there stands one yet more knowing. In this verse the word ⟪ar:’ فوق‘⟫ ("above") indicates the elevation of ranks and the differentiation between them:

### Knowledge and Faith

Knowledge is a form the soul fashions in itself by becoming aware of a reality. When the soul then confesses that form with the tongue, affirms it with the heart, and impresses it upon itself — that is, when it acts upon it through the organs of the body — that form is called *īmān* (faith). Verification (*taṣdīq*) is truthfulness, and truthfulness is impossible without inquiry. As Muslims, for example, we all hold the knowledge that Paradise, Hell, and the Bridge of *Ṣirāṭ* exist; we acknowledge and confirm their existence — that is, we believe in them. Yet because they lie beyond the grasp of ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫ (the five senses), faith in them demands both confirmation and inquiry, in order to know their existence and their reality.

In the soul of the knower, the form of knowledge is called ⟪ar:علم⟫ (knowledge), while the confession, affirmation, and action upon that knowledge is called ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ (faith). The real thing is faith; knowledge is the means by which faith is increased.

(*Rasāʾil Aḥmadiyya*) — Imam Aḥmad ibn ʿAbd Allāh (a.s.)

### Verification and Inquiry

Knowledge is twofold. The first is the condition called ⟪ar:علم ظاہر⟫ (manifest knowledge), which precedes faith: the state of mere awareness, of acquiring information about a thing or a reality — to know, for instance, that Paradise and Hell exist. The second is the knowledge that enters into faith: the truth that is first acknowledged by the tongue, namely that Paradise and Hell are real, and then ratified within. To complete this ratification one must seek out a detailed account of the matter: inquiry generates knowledge, and knowledge in turn deepens confirmation. This is ⟪ar:علم معرفت⟫ (the knowledge of gnosis), the order of cognition that comes *after* faith.

The greater the knowledge gained through *maʿrifa*, the greater the confirmation; and the greater the confirmation, the more perfect the faith. What are Paradise, Hell, and the Bridge of *Ṣirāṭ*? Where do they lie? Since when have they been? Who shall be sent to them, and for how long? As one's knowledge of these matters expands, so too does one's confirmation of them, and both the ⟪ar:علمی صورت⟫ (form of knowledge) and the resulting ⟪ar:ایمان⟫ are strengthened.[^cite-16]

## The Minimum Conditions of Faith, Polytheism, and Misguidance

Amīr al-Muʾminīn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (peace be upon him) was once asked: "O ʿAlī, what is the least a person must do to be counted a believer, and what is the least that renders him a disbeliever?" His reply unfolded in three measured parts.

### The Minimum Condition of Faith

"The least that makes a person a believer," he answered, "is that when God grants one of His servants the capacity to know Him, that servant acknowledges God in obedience. If God further grants him the capacity to know His Messenger, he acknowledges the Messenger in obedience. And if God grants him the recognition of His Proof (ḥujja) upon the earth — His Witness over creation — he believes in that Proof and acknowledges him in obedience." Such a person, the Imam declared, is a believer. As to whatever lies beyond his immediate knowledge, he need only obey what he is commanded and abstain from what he is forbidden.[^cite-18]

### The Minimum Condition of Polytheism

"The least that makes a person a polytheist," he continued, "is that he commits an act which God has forbidden and persuades himself that God has commanded it." To attribute divine sanction to what is in truth directed toward other than God — and thereby to render obeisance to that other — is the very threshold of shirk (associating partners with God). Worship of any other than God necessarily entails shirk, and the one who does so falls into the rank of the polytheist.

### The Minimum Condition of Misguidance

"The least that draws a person into misguidance," he concluded, "is that he neither seeks God's Proof upon the earth nor inquires after His Witness over creation, and so does not follow him." For one who is severed from the recognition of the Imam is, by that very privation, astray.

## What Was the True Purpose of the Islamic Summons?

The Prophet's purpose in summoning humanity to outward Islam was, in truth, to summon them to the inward — for it is only through the ẓāhir (the manifest) that one may ascend to the bāṭin (the hidden). He therefore first invited the people to Islam with the words ⟪ar-quote:قولو لا الہ الا اللہ تُفلِحُو⟫ — "Declare ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اللہ⟫ and you will prosper." Then, in that same gathering, those who had matured within the Islamic daʿwa (the summons) and had been formed by its temper were invited, on ⟪ar:یوم غدیر⟫, by the saying ⟪ar-quote:مَن کُنتُ مَولاہُ فَھٰذاَ عَلِیٌّ مَولاَہُ⟫ — "Whomsoever I am his master, this ʿAlī is his master" — to the word of faith: ⟪ar:لا الہ الا ھو⟫, the kalima of the inner daʿwa. Thus ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illā Allāh is the word of the exoteric, Islamic summons, while the word of faith is its veil and its inner counterpart. This dual register is gestured to in ⟪ar:سورۃ مزمل⟫ itself, when God says:[^cite-19]

> ⟪quran 73:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫

The numerical value of ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illā huwa is one hundred and ten — the very value of the name ⟪ar:علی⟫. The verse thereby intimates, in a register intelligible through the science of letters, that ʿAlī is the Lord of East and of West: take him as your wakīl (guardian, disposer of your affairs). Such, then, is the allusion to the inner summons.

## The Paradise of Islam and the Paradise of Faith

When ⟪ar:مبدع تعالی⟫ (the Originator, Most High) brought forth ⟪ar:عقل اول⟫ (the First Intellect) and that Intellect pronounced its testimony to the divine oneness, it did not say merely ⟪ar:لا ⟪ar:الہ⟫ illā Allāh; it rose to the higher utterance, ⟪ar:لا⟫ ilāha illā huwa:[^cite-20]

The ⟪ar:ناطقۃ⟫ thus acquired is what underwrites the felicitous ending.

آمَنُو⟫ ⟪ar:‏ تِلْكَ ٱلرُّسُلُ فَضَّلْنَا بَعْضَهُمْ عَلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ ۘ مِّنْهُم مَّن كَلَّمَ ٱللَّهُ ۖ وَرَفَعَ بَعْضَهُمْ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَآتَيْنَا عِيسَى ٱبْنَ مَرْيَمَ ٱلْبَيِّنَتِ وَأَيَّدْنَهُ بِرُوحِ ٱلْقُدُسِ ۗ وَلَوْ شَآءَ ٱللَّهُ مَا ٱقْتَتَلَ ٱلَّذِينَ مِنۢ بَعْدِهِم مِّنۢ بَعْدِ مَا جَآءَتْهُمُ ٱلْبَيِّنَتُ وَلَكِنِ ٱخْتَلَفُوا۟ فَمِنْهُم مَّنْ آمَنَ وَمِنْهُم مَّن كَفَرَ ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ ٱللَّهُ مَا ٱقْتَتَلُوا۟ وَلَكِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَفْعَلُ مَا يُرِيدُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ قَالَتِ ٱلْأَعْرَابُ آمَنَّا ۖ قُل لَّمْ تُؤْمِنُوا۟ وَلَكِن قُولُوٓا۟ أَسْلَمْنَا وَلَمَّا يَدْخُلِ ٱلْإِيمَنُ فِى قُلُوبِكُمْ ۖ وَإِن تُطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ لَا يَلِتْكُم مِّنْ أَعْمَلِكُمْ شَيْـًٔا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلرَّسُولُ لَا يَحْزُنكَ ٱلَّذِينَ يُسَرِعُونَ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ آمَنَّا بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَلَمْ تُؤْمِن قُلُوبُهُمْ ۛ وَمِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ هَادُوا۟ ۛ سَمَّعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ سَمَّعُونَ لِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ لَمْ يَأْتُوكَ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ مَوَاضِعِهِۦ ۖ يَقُولُونَ إِنْ أُوتِيتُمْ هَذَا فَخُذُوهُ وَإِن لَّمْ تُؤْتَوْهُ فَٱحْذَرُوا۟ ۚ وَمَن يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ فِتْنَتَهُۥ فَلَن تَمْلِكَ لَهُۥ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ شَيْـًٔا ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَمْ يُرِدِ ٱللَّهُ أَن يُطَهِّرَ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا خِزْىٌۭ ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏آمَنَ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦ ۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ جَهَدُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ ٱلصَّبِرِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ لْيَقْضُوا۟ تَفَثَهُمْ وَلْيُوفُوا۟ نُذُورَهُمْ وَلْيَطَّوَّفُوا۟ بِٱلْبَيْتِ ٱلْعَتِيقِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ذَلِكَ ٱلْكِتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًۭى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏سَابِقُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ مَغْفِرَةٍۢ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَجَنَّةٍ عَرْضُهَا كَعَرْضِ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ أُعِدَّتْ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ ۚ ذَلِكَ فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَن يَشَآءُ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ ذُو ٱلْفَضْلِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏شَهِدَ ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُۥ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمِ قَآئِمًۢا بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ لَآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏فَإِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ فَضَرْبَ ٱلرِّقَابِ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَثْخَنتُمُوهُمْ فَشُدُّوا۟ ٱلْوَثَاقَ فَإِمَّا مَنًّۢا بَعْدُ وَإِمَّا فِدَآءً حَتَّىٰ تَضَعَ ٱلْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا ۚ ذَلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَآءُ ٱللَّهُ لَٱنتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ وَلَكِن لِّيَبْلُوَا۟ بَعْضَكُم بِبَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱلَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ فَلَن يُضِلَّ أَعْمَلَهُمْ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏فَبَدَأَ بِأَوْعِيَتِهِمْ قَبْلَ وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَخْرَجَهَا مِن وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ ۚ كَذَلِكَ كِدْنَا لِيُوسُفَ ۖ مَا كَانَ لِيَأْخُذَ أَخَاهُ فِى دِينِ ٱلْمَلِكِ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ نَرْفَعُ دَرَجَتٍۢ مَّن نَّشَآءُ ۗ وَفَوْقَ كُلِّ ذِى عِلْمٍ عَلِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏فَمَا وَجَدْنَا فِيهَا غَيْرَ بَيْتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قَدْ أَفْلَحَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ فِى صَلَاتِهِمْ خَشِعُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ ٱللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَوٰةِ فَعِلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُلْ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْنَا وَمَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَىٰٓ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَإِسْمَعِيلَ وَإِسْحَقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَٱلْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَآ أُوتِىَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَٱلنَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُۥ مُسْلِمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أَنَا۠ بَشَرٌۭ مِّثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ أَنَّمَآ إِلَهُكُمْ إِلَهٌۭ وَحِدٌۭ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُشْرِكِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَفِرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏لِلْفُقَرَآءِ ٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُخْرِجُوا۟ مِن دِيَرِهِمْ وَأَمْوَلِهِمْ يَبْتَغُونَ فَضْلًۭا مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَرِضْوَنًۭا وَيَنصُرُونَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥٓ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلصَّدِقُونَ ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ تَبَوَّءُو ٱلدَّارَ وَٱلْإِيمَنَ مِن قَبْلِهِمْ يُحِبُّونَ مَنْ هَاجَرَ إِلَيْهِمْ وَلَا يَجِدُونَ فِى صُدُورِهِمْ حَاجَةًۭ مِّمَّآ أُوتُوا۟ وَيُؤْثِرُونَ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ وَلَوْ كَانَ بِهِمْ خَصَاصَةٌۭ ۚ وَمَن يُوقَ شُحَّ نَفْسِهِۦ فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏لَّا يَسْتَوِى ٱلْقَعِدُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ غَيْرُ أُو۟لِى ٱلضَّرَرِ وَٱلْمُجَهِدُونَ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ بِأَمْوَلِهِمْ وَأَنفُسِهِمْ ۚ فَضَّلَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْمُجَهِدِينَ بِأَمْوَلِهِمْ وَأَنفُسِهِمْ عَلَى ٱلْقَعِدِينَ دَرَجَةًۭ ۚ وَكُلًّۭا وَعَدَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ ۚ وَفَضَّلَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْمُجَهِدِينَ عَلَى ٱلْقَعِدِينَ أَجْرًا عَظِيمًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏لِّلَّهِ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَإِن تُبْدُوا۟ مَا فِىٓ أَنفُسِكُمْ أَوْ تُخْفُوهُ يُحَاسِبْكُم بِهِ ٱللَّهُ ۖ فَيَغْفِرُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَن يَشَآءُ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏مَن كَفَرَ بِٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعْدِ إِيمَنِهِۦٓ إِلَّا مَنْ أُكْرِهَ وَقَلْبُهُۥ مُطْمَىِٕنٌّۢ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ وَلَكِن مَّن شَرَحَ بِٱلْكُفْرِ صَدْرًۭا فَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏هُمْ دَرَجَتٌ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ بَصِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَأَنِ ٱسْتَغْفِرُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ ثُمَّ تُوبُوٓا۟ إِلَيْهِ يُمَتِّعْكُم مَّتَعًا حَسَنًا إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّۭى وَيُؤْتِ كُلَّ ذِى فَضْلٍۢ فَضْلَهُۥ ۖ وَإِن تَوَلَّوْا۟ فَإِنِّىٓ أَخَافُ عَلَيْكُمْ عَذَابَ يَوْمٍۢ كَبِيرٍ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَأَنَّ ٱلْمَسَجِدَ لِلَّهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا۟ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ أَحَدًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخُوضُونَ فِىٓ آيَتِنَا فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦ ۚ وَإِمَّا يُنسِيَنَّكَ ٱلشَّيْطَنُ فَلَا تَقْعُدْ بَعْدَ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَرَبُّكَ أَعْلَمُ بِمَن فِى ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ وَلَقَدْ فَضَّلْنَا بَعْضَ ٱلنَّبِيِّنَ عَلَىٰ بَعْضٍۢ ۖ وَآتَيْنَا دَاوُۥدَ زَبُورًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقَدِمْنَآ إِلَىٰ مَا عَمِلُوا۟ مِنْ عَمَلٍۢ فَجَعَلْنَهُ هَبَآءًۭ مَّنثُورًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقَدْ نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكُمْ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ أَنْ إِذَا سَمِعْتُمْ آيَتِ ٱللَّهِ يُكْفَرُ بِهَا وَيُسْتَهْزَأُ بِهَا فَلَا تَقْعُدُوا۟ مَعَهُمْ حَتَّىٰ يَخُوضُوا۟ فِى حَدِيثٍ غَيْرِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّكُمْ إِذًۭا مِّثْلُهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ جَامِعُ ٱلْمُنَفِقِينَ وَٱلْكَفِرِينَ فِى جَهَنَّمَ جَمِيعًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱلْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيُؤْمِن وَمَن شَآءَ فَلْيَكْفُرْ ۚ إِنَّآ أَعْتَدْنَا لِلظَّلِمِينَ نَارًا أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ سُرَادِقُهَا ۚ وَإِن يَسْتَغِيثُوا۟ يُغَاثُوا۟ بِمَآءٍۢ كَٱلْمُهْلِ يَشْوِى ٱلْوُجُوهَ ۚ بِئْسَ ٱلشَّرَابُ وَسَآءَتْ مُرْتَفَقًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌ ۚ إِنَّ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْبَصَرَ وَٱلْفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَانَ عَنْهُ مَسْـُٔولًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلَا تَمْشِ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ ٱلْجِبَالَ طُولًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَن يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَآ أَبْصَرُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَكِن ظَنَنتُمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًۭا مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَا لَكُمْ أَلَّا تُنفِقُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَلِلَّهِ مِيرَثُ ٱلسَّمَوَتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ ۚ لَا يَسْتَوِى مِنكُم مَّنْ أَنفَقَ مِن قَبْلِ ٱلْفَتْحِ وَقَتَلَ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَعْظَمُ دَرَجَةًۭ مِّنَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنفَقُوا۟ مِنۢ بَعْدُ وَقَتَلُوا۟ ۚ وَكُلًّۭا وَعَدَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَا يُؤْمِنُ أَكْثَرُهُم بِٱللَّهِ إِلَّا وَهُم مُّشْرِكُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱقْصِدْ فِى مَشْيِكَ وَٱغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلْأَصْوَتِ لَصَوْتُ ٱلْحَمِيرِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلسَّبِقُونَ ٱلسَّبِقُونَ ‎ ‏أُو۟لَٓئِكَ ٱلْمُقَرَّبُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلسَّبِقُونَ ٱلْأَوَّلُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ وَٱلْأَنصَارِ وَٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّبَعُوهُم بِإِحْسَنٍۢ رَّضِىَ ٱللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا۟ عَنْهُ وَأَعَدَّ لَهُمْ جَنَّتٍۢ تَجْرِى تَحْتَهَا ٱلْأَنْهَرُ خَلِدِينَ فِيهَآ أَبَدًۭا ۚ ذَلِكَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّلِحَتِ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَلِدُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وُجُوهٌۭ يَوْمَئِذٍ خَشِعَةٌ ‎ ‏عَامِلَةٌۭ نَّاصِبَةٌۭ ‎ ‏تَصْلَىٰ نَارًا حَامِيَةًۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَمُنُّونَ عَلَيْكَ أَنْ أَسْلَمُوا۟ ۖ قُل لَّا تَمُنُّوا۟ عَلَىَّ إِسْلَمَكُم ۖ بَلِ ٱللَّهُ يَمُنُّ عَلَيْكُمْ أَنْ هَدَىٰكُمْ لِلْإِيمَنِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَدِقِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَقُولُوا۟ قَوْلًۭا سَدِيدًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱرْكَعُوا۟ وَٱسْجُدُوا۟ وَٱعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ وَٱفْعَلُوا۟ ٱلْخَيْرَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ آمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ وَٱلْكِتَبِ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ مِن قَبْلُ ۚ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱللَّهِ وَمَلَٓئِكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَلًۢا بَعِيدًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ فَٱغْسِلُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْمَرَافِقِ وَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِرُئُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُمْ جُنُبًۭا فَٱطَّهَّرُوا۟ ۚ وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَآءَ أَحَدٌۭ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلْغَآئِطِ أَوْ لَمَسْتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا۟ مَآءًۭ فَتَيَمَّمُوا۟ صَعِيدًۭا طَيِّبًۭا فَٱمْسَحُوا۟ بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُم مِّنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُم مِّنْ حَرَجٍۢ وَلَكِن يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُۥ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجَلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ زَحْفًۭا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلْأَدْبَارَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا نُودِىَ لِلصَّلَوٰةِ مِن يَوْمِ ٱلْجُمُعَةِ فَٱسْعَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَتَطْمَىِٕنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ ٱللَّهِ تَطْمَىِٕنُّ ٱلْقُلُوبُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَهَاجَرُوا۟ وَجَهَدُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ بِأَمْوَلِهِمْ وَأَنفُسِهِمْ أَعْظَمُ دَرَجَةً عِندَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْفَآئِزُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ أُحِلَّ لَكُمُ ٱلطَّيِّبَتُ ۖ وَطَعَامُ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ حِلٌّۭ لَّكُمْ وَطَعَامُكُمْ حِلٌّۭ لَّهُمْ ۖ وَٱلْمُحْصَنَتُ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنَتِ وَٱلْمُحْصَنَتُ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِن قَبْلِكُمْ إِذَآ آتَيْتُمُوهُنَّ أُجُورَهُنَّ مُحْصِنِينَ غَيْرَ مُسَفِحِينَ وَلَا مُتَّخِذِىٓ أَخْدَانٍۢ ۗ وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِٱلْإِيمَنِ فَقَدْ حَبِطَ عَمَلُهُۥ وَهُوَ فِى ٱلْآخِرَةِ مِنَ ٱلْخَسِرِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلْيَوْمَ نَخْتِمُ عَلَىٰٓ أَفْوَهِهِمْ وَتُكَلِّمُنَآ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَتَشْهَدُ أَرْجُلُهُم بِمَا كَانُوا۟
