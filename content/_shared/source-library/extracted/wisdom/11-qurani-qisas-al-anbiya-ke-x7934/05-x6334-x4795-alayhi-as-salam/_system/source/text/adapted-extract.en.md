## Nūḥ (Noah) — The Prophet of the Flood

According to the Bible Nooh AS was born, 2948 years before the birth of Christ. Noah is not only mentioned in numerous places within the Quran, there is an entire Surah dedicated exclusively to him story. The full name of Nooh AS was Nooh bin Malik bin Matushalih bin Akhnoo' bin Bara bin Mahlaeel bin Qaynaan bin Anoosh bin Shees (AS). According to this hierarchy, Nooh AS is from the 8th generation of Shees AS (⟪ar:شیث⟫), and 10th generation of Adam (AS). When the moral character of the children of Adam deteriorated immensely, Allah chose Nooh AS has his "Natiq" messenger for the guidance of humanity. He was the second Natiq, and the first Uloo Al-Azam (Of strong determination) prophets. He was 50 years old when he was chosen as a prophet, and according to the Quran, Nooh AS lived on earth for 950 years.

His real name was Abdul Ghaffar. He would constantly cry and grieve over the despicable state of his brothers, and because of his lamenting (نوحہ) , he became known as "Nooh" (the one who laments or mourns). He is also called the Shaikh AlMursaleen (Chief of prophets), as he is the oldest in age among all prophets. His real age was 120 years. 950 years that the Quran states is actually the period of his Shareeah. Hood AS was the Imam (Allah), who chose him as his representative on earth (khaleefa).

The need for a prophet

Ibn Abbas narrates, that the descendents of Adam AS split into two branches, the children of Habeel (AS) and the descendents of Qabeel. The children of Habeel settled on mountainous regions, where as the children of Qabeel settled in downhill valleys. This created a significant difference in the physical and spiritual characteristics of both people. Among those residing in the mountains, the men were very handsome, whereas the women were ugly; and among the people living in the valleys (children of Qabeel), the men were ugly and their women were extremely beautiful. In his last will, Adam (AS) had forbidden the children of Habeel (AS) to marry the children of Qabeel. In obedience to his will, they made their homes within caves, so that the children of Qabeel would not be able to reach them.

But despite these precautions, one morning about a hundred people from the tribe of Habeel decided to visit the valleys, to see what state the children of their uncle Qabeel were in. When they reached the valley, they saw the beautiful women, and instantly fell in love with them, and decided to settle down in the valleys with them. After a few days, another group of hundred people came down to the valley investigating the absence of their brothers. They too, after being struck by the beauty of the women, settled in the valleys. This kept happening until almost all the children of Habeel (AS) descended into the valleys. The blood of Habeel (AS) mixed with the blood of Qabeel through intermarriages. Women from the tribe of Qabeel overcame the men from the tribe of Habeel by their beauty, and corruption spread over the lands.

Mountains represent superior and lofty knowledge, and cave represents the esoterics (baatin) of that knowledge. Beautiful men are the teachers who possess this beautiful spiritual knowledge, whereas the ugly women are the students who have yet to acquire this knowledge (beauty). Valley represents this physical material knowledge (sciences). The beautiful women of the valley are masters of these physical realms of knowledge, wherease the ugly men are students who have yet to acquire this knowledge. The beautiful men descending in the valley represents scholars of spiritual knowledge lowering themselves and developing a fascination of this temporal world. Their marriage with the beautiful women of the valley represents, their acquiring the lower forms of knowledge, and their settling in the valley represents their giving up the spiritual esoteric knowledge for the lower forms of knowledge. These women (of the valley), similar to the women during the time of Loot AS, were lesbians.

Shaytan had misguided the people of the time, and made them idol worshippers. The following idols were worshiped during that time:

1. Wad (⟪ar:وَد⟫) - shaped as a man

2. Suwa' (⟪ar:سَوَاع⟫) - shaped as a woman

3. Yaghoos (⟪ar:یَغُوث⟫) - shaped as a lion

4. Yaa'ooq (⟪ar:یَعُوق⟫) - shaped as a horse

5. Nasar (⟪ar:نَسَر⟫) - shaped as a vulture

In the second chapter of Genesis, the Bible explains similar conditions of prophet NooH AS. It mentions five very powerful "giants" that lived during the time, that commanded and controlled the people of that time.

The name of Nooh's nemesis (Shaytan) was "Raasib", and he was originally one of the high ranking personalities of Dawa. Because of his arrogance, pride and jealousy, he rebelled against Imam Hood AS and established his own satanic call against the divine call. He established five self-proclaimed leaders (symbolized by the names of the idols aand giants). These people held a strong influence on the people of that time. These were the leaders (and their followers) that Allah chose to destroy as a punishment for their arrogance.

By the command of Allah, Nooh AS asked these people to stop worshipping the idols, and turn towards the path of guidance. In order to prove that he was the true messenger of Allah, and to show them the amazing creativity and the magnificent powers of Allah, he revealed various types of knowledge that were not known before that time, including geography and astronomy. But with the exceptions of a few people, nobody took any interest in his teachings, and they refused to stop worshipping their idols.

The attrocities against Nooh (AS)

Dahaak Ibn Abbas narrates that the people of that time would torture and beat Nooh AS, in order to prevent him from spreading the message of Allah. They would beat to the point of unconscious. Assuming he was dead, they would then tie him up in a bag and throw him in front of his house. Jibraeel AS would then descend from the sky and rub the sole of his feet, which would remove all his pain. On gaining consciousness, he would immediately get up and go back to calling people towards the true path. He kept calling them to La Ilaha Illa Allah (There is no deity except Allah), telling them that it will save their souls. But they would not listen to him and continued torturing him. This continued until the time when Nooh completely lost hope about them accepting faith.

Nooh was asking his nation to turn away from these five false leaders, and turn towards the one Imam. This is symbolized by La Ilaha Illah Allah; There is no diety except Allah, or in other words, there is no true Imam except Hood AS.

It is narrated that the animosity against Nooh AS had increased to such a degree, that once an old man, with a cane in his hand, was walking down the street, carrying his young child on his shoulders. When he saw Noah passing by, he told his son: "Beware of this man! As he will try to misguide you!" Hearing this, the son got off the old man's shoulder and started beating Nooh AS with his father's cane. Nooh cried out to his master saying, "Oh master! Either guide these people to the truth, or give me the patience to bear their atrocities. You are the only one who can execute justice as between me and them. Indeed you are the most just of all!"

Nooh's (AS) Ark

Before sending the floods, Allah created a means of deliverance for Nooh AS and his followers through the construction of an ark. The construction of the ark took place in both, the physical as well as the spiritual sense.  The command for the construction of the ark was not just for protection against the physical storm. It was also for protection against the floods of ignorance. The one who gets on board the physical ark, is protected from the visible flood water, and their bodies are saved from drowning in it. Similarly the esoteric ark would save their souls from drowning in the floods of ignorance of this material world. The visible ark is a symbol that symbolizes the esoteric ark.

This is why Rasool Allah (SAS) said in his famous hadees:
⟪ar-quote:مَثَلُ اَھلِ بَیتِی کَمَثَلِ سَفِینَۃِ نُوحٍ..... مَن رَکَبَھَا نَجیٰ..... وَ مَنْ تَخَلَّفَ عَنھَا غَرَقَ وَ ھَوَیٰ⟫

Members of my family (the Imams from my progeny) are similar to the ark of Nooh AS. The one's who enter it will be protected, and the one's who rebel against them and refuse to come on board will drown and be destroyed.

The Imam of true guidance, who exists in every time period of time, is in fact the arc of Nooh AS in the metaphorical sense. The ones who believe in them, associate with them, and follow their teachings, willingly and with perseverence, will be protected from the evils and vices of this material world. And those who rebel against them, will drown in the desires and vices of this material world and will be destoryed in the next. Every ark needs a navigator, who controls the movement of the ark. The ark never disobeys the navigator under any circumstance. In the spiritual sense, the wasee is the ark, and the Natiq is his navigator. In another analogy, the Hujjah is the ark and the Imam is his navigator. Similarly in our circle of influence, our body is our ark and our soul is its navigator. The body never disobeys the coammand of the soul. So if our souls learn how to properly navigate the body, through the teachings of the Imams and his Hujja, we can deliver it from the floods and torments of this physical world and steer it to the eternal spiritual realm.

Construction of the ark
Imam Hood (AS) commanded Nooh AS to establish a spiritual structure through his wasee (ark) that could protect the faith of the believers from the floods of ignorance. His wasee (Saam AS) was the spiritual ark that Nooh AS was commanded to construct for the preservation and protection of his follower's souls. Through systematic and gradual construction, an ark or a boat is made capable of floating over water. Various elements have to be systematically placed together for the strurdy construction of a ship. Just as Nooh AS constructed the physical ark by placing its various components together, in their right place and in the right order, and enabled it to sail through the dreadful floods of the ocean, he constructed his esoteric ark, the spiritual call of his wasee, by placing each person under him (called hudood) in their proper rank. Once this structure was put in place, Nooh's spiritual ark (the dawa or spiritual call of his wasee) became capable of sailing, and tearing through the floods of ignornace and innovation (⟪ar:بدعۃ⟫) all around it; and protecting the souls of the believers against the painful assaults (in speech and action) of the enemies, and the enormous temptations and vices of this material world, which is sybolized by the size of the ocean; and delevering their souls to ultimate salvation (shore).

To summarize, this physical material world is an ocean of ignorance. Its depth sybolized by the never ending temptations and desires of the human soul. The Natiq's Wasee (or Imam's Hujja) is the ark, constructed through the proper ranks of his hudood. The purpose of this ark is to sail through this ocean of physicality and deliver us safely to the shores of the spiritual world. Anyone who tries to cross the ocean without a ship, will surely drown in it. The true believers, who willing come aboard this ship, and through perseverence and patience endure the hardships of the sail, will eventually succeed. And those who refuse to climb aboard will be drowned within this material world and they will have no portion from the blessings of the spiritual world. This is why the word مغرقون, the one's who are drowned is used in the story of Nooh AS. When one drowns in the ocean, the water pressure keeps on increasing, pushing him further down. The deeper he sinks, the harder it becomes for him to rise to the surface. The desires and vices of this physical world are exactly similar. The more one gets involved in it, the harder it becomes to get out of it. And one desire leads to the next; one temptations takes us to the next one, and the rebels keep sinking deeper and deeper into it until there is no possibility of surfacing left.
