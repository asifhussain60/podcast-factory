## A Comparison of Mawlānā ʿAlī and Prophet ʿĪsā (Jesus)

Between ʿĪsā (the first Christ) and Mawlānā ʿAlī (the second Messiah) the resemblances are many and exact. Every attribute by which the community of ʿĪsā characterized their prophet is found also in him, and the bearing of the community of ʿĪsā toward their prophet — peace be upon him — almost exactly mirrors the bearing of the community of the Prophet Muḥammad toward ʿAlī. It was for this reason that the Prophet himself drew the comparison, declaring that the likeness of ʿAlī is the likeness of ʿĪsā son of Mary.

In explaining why ʿĪsā was named al-Masīḥ (the Messiah, lit. "the Anointed"), it is said that he bore this title because he had been anointed with ⟪ar:’کلمۃ اللہ‘⟫ (the Word of God). Some hold that he was anointed with the water of the river Jordan; others, that the anointing was with oil. In just this manner ⟪ar:کلمۃ اللہ⟫ touched ʿAlī, who was so anointed that no defilement of the jāhiliyya (age of ignorance) ever clung to him. As ʿĪsā received prophethood while still a child, so did ʿAlī affirm the Prophet in childhood and, for seven full years, pray beside him with no other companion. And just as the Messiah turned his back upon the world with the words "I have divorced you thrice" (ṭallaqtuki thalāthan), so did the Messenger of God — peace and blessings be upon him — address the world: "O gold and silver coins, deceive any but me."[^cite-1] Indeed, in declaring the merit of ʿAlī, the Prophet said: "Were I not afraid that people would say of you what the Christians said of ʿĪsā, I would speak of your virtues in such terms that whoever heard them would take the dust of your feet as kohl for his eyes and drink the water of your bath and ablution."

God Himself drew the same likeness between ʿAlī and the Messiah:

> ⟪quran 43:57-58⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ وَلَمَّا ضُرِبَ ٱبْنُ مَرْيَمَ مَثَلًا إِذَا قَوْمُكَ مِنْهُ يَصِدُّونَ ‎ ‏وَقَالُوٓا۟ ءَأَلِهَتُنَا خَيْرٌ أَمْ هُوَ ۚ مَا ضَرَبُوهُ لَكَ إِلَّا جَدَلًۢا ۚ بَلْ هُمْ قَوْمٌ خَصِمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And when the son of Maryam (Mary) is quoted as an example [i.e. 'Iesa (Jesus) is worshipped like their idols), behold! Your people cry aloud (laugh out at the example). And say: "Are our aliha (gods) better or is he ['Iesa (Jesus)]?" They quoted not the above example except for argument. Nay! But they are a quarrelsome people. [(See VV. 21:97-101) - The Quran.]*
> And when the example of the son of Maryam was set for, behold, thy people laughed at him, and said: Are our gods better? Or do they not mention them except to dispute with thee? Nay, but they are a quarrelsome people.
> — **Quran 43:57-58 (inline widget)**

The Messiah named in this verse is, in truth, ʿAlī — a meaning grasped only by the people of taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis) and the people of intellect. Were it otherwise, what conceivable hostility could the idolatrous tribesmen of the Prophet have borne toward ʿĪsā, that the mere mention of his name should inflame them and turn them from obedience? Love and enmity arise between persons who know one another; the stranger neither loves nor hates the stranger. The polytheists of Quraysh were idolaters with no commerce in Christianity, and six centuries lay between them and ʿĪsā. There is no plausible cause for such enmity. Our reading establishes the contrary: that the Messiah here intended is one contemporary with the people of the Prophet, against whom they bore their hostility, so that the very mention of his name set them quarreling with the Messenger. In the second clause God reports their question: "Are our gods greater, or is he?" Here ⟪ar:’اللہ‘⟫ (Allāh), ⟪ar:’الہ‘⟫ (ilāh), and ⟪ar:’معبود‘⟫ (maʿbūd, "the one worshipped") refer to those figures whom the community of the Prophet had taken for their elders and chiefs, and whom they followed without weighing whether such following was their due.[^cite-2]

Once a Christian came to the Messenger of God and questioned him about a kindred verse, saying: "O Messenger of God, we have never worshipped our scholars. We did not prostrate to them, nor did we name them our lords. Why then this charge against us?"

> ⟪quran 9:31⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱتَّخَذُوٓا۟ أَحْبَارَهُمْ وَرُهْبَنَهُمْ أَرْبَابًۭا مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ وَٱلْمَسِيحَ ٱبْنَ مَرْيَمَ وَمَآ أُمِرُوٓا۟ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُوٓا۟ إِلَهًۭا وَحِدًۭا ۖ لَّآ إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ سُبْحَنَهُۥ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ ‎⟫
> *They (Jews and Christians) took their rabbis and their monks to be their lords besides Allah (by obeying them in things which they made lawful or unlawful according to their own desires without being ordered by Allah), and (they also took as their Lord) Messiah, son of Maryam (Mary), while they (Jews and Christians) were commanded [in the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel)) to worship none but One Ilah (God - Allah) La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He). Praise and glory be to Him, (far above is He) from having the partners they associate (with Him)." *
> They have taken their rabbis and their monks as gods apart from God, and the Messiah, the son of Mary, and they were commanded to worship none but God; there is no god but He. Glory be to Him, above what they associate with Him.
> — **Quran 9:31 (inline widget)**

The Prophet answered: "Did you not obey their unjust commands — what they declared lawful or unlawful without the warrant of Qurʾān or Sunna — receiving their decisions as binding?" The Christian conceded: "Yes; we obeyed their every command." The Prophet replied: "That itself is worship. By that very obedience you set these scholars in the place of God."

---

By the same principle, every community of the Prophet has contended with him, saying: ⟪ar-quote:وَقَالُوٓا۟ ءَأَلِھَتُنَا خَيْرٌ أَمْ ھو ؟⟫ — "Are our caliphs and the imāms who have made us what we are better, or is ʿAlī?"

> ⟪quran 43:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا عَبْدٌ أَنْعَمْنَا عَلَيْهِ وَجَعَلْنَهُ مَثَلًۭا لِّبَنِىٓ إِسْرَٓءِيلَ ‎⟫
> *He ['Iesa (Jesus)] was not more than a slave. We granted Our Favour to him, and We made him an example to the Children of Israel (i.e. his creation without a father).*
> he was only a servant of Ours, on whom We bestowed favor, and We made him an example to the Children of Israel.
> — **Quran 43:59 (inline widget)**

A blessing of the same order is referred to in this verse:

> ⟪quran 5:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏حُرِّمَتْ عَلَيْكُمُ ٱلْمَيْتَةُ وَٱلدَّمُ وَلَحْمُ ٱلْخِنزِيرِ وَمَآ أُهِلَّ لِغَيْرِ ٱللَّهِ بِهِۦ وَٱلْمُنْخَنِقَةُ وَٱلْمَوْقُوذَةُ وَٱلْمُتَرَدِّيَةُ وَٱلنَّطِيحَةُ وَمَآ أَكَلَ ٱلسَّبُعُ إِلَّا مَا ذَكَّيْتُمْ وَمَا ذُبِحَ عَلَى ٱلنُّصُبِ وَأَن تَسْتَقْسِمُوا۟ بِٱلْأَزْلَمِ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ فِسْقٌ ۗ ٱلْيَوْمَ يَئِسَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ مِن دِينِكُمْ فَلَا تَخْشَوْهُمْ وَٱخْشَوْنِ ۚ ٱلْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِينَكُمْ وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِى وَرَضِيتُ لَكُمُ ٱلْإِسْلَمَ دِينًۭا ۚ فَمَنِ ٱضْطُرَّ فِى مَخْمَصَةٍ غَيْرَ مُتَجَانِفٍۢ لِّإِثْمٍۢ ۙ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Forbidden to you (for food) are: Al-Maytatah (the dead animals - cattle-beast not slaughtered), blood, the flesh of swine, and the meat of that which has been slaughtered as a sacrifice for others than Allah, or has been slaughtered for idols, etc., or on which Allah's Name has not been mentioned while slaughtering, and that which has been killed by strangling,  or by a violent blow, or by a headlong fall, or by the goring of horns - and that which has been (partly) eaten by a wild animal - unless you are able to slaughter it (before its death) &shy; and that which is sacrificed (slaughtered) on An&shy;Nusub (stone altars).&nbsp;(Forbidden) also is to use arrows seeking luck or decision,  (all) that is Fisqun (disobedience of Allah and sin).&nbsp;This day, those who disbelieved have given up all hope of your religion, so fear them not, but fear Me.&nbsp;This day, I have perfected your religion for you, completed My Favour upon you, and have chosen for you Islam as your religion.&nbsp;But as for him who is forced by severe hunger,  with no inclination to sin (such can eat these above-mentioned meats), then surely, Allah is Oft&shy;Forgiving, Most Merciful.*
> forbidden to you is carrion, and blood, and the flesh of swine, and that which has been invoked by the name of any other than Allah, which dies by strangulation, or of injury, or of falling from a height, or by striking its horns, and that which has been torn apart by an animal, except that which you have slaughtered, and that which is slaughtered on any land, and that which is slaughtered on any land, Today the disbelievers have despaired of your religion, so do not fear them, but fear Me, this day I have perfected your religion for you, and I have completed My favour upon you, and I have chosen for you Islam as your religion. Forgiving, Merciful.
> — **Quran 5:3 (inline widget)**

That is, the first Christ was a servant of God upon whom God had bestowed His favor; the second Messiah is himself the favor — a being whose entire existence is a blessing for the people of religion. Concluding the verse, God says: ⟪ar-quote:وجعلنا مثلا لبنی اسرائیل⟫ — "We have made him an example for the Children of Israel." Now the ancient Children of Israel were the offspring of the prophets of Israel; in the inner reading, every Muslim is the likeness of the Israelites. The descendants of the first Messiah did not value their Messiah, and the Muslims of the second Messiah did not value him either. The parallel held so firmly that, just as the Christians named their Messiah Allāh, so a faction of the partisans of the second Christ named ʿAlī Allāh.

A further subtle correspondence between the first and the last Christ is this. The Christians named the Messiah Son of God and even God Himself; the Jews named him the child of an illicit union and called him a deceiver; the Muslims, schooled by their Prophet, set him in his rightful station and acknowledged him as Messenger of God. Likewise three opinions arose concerning the second Christ. One faction named him God — the Nuṣayriyya. A second stripped him of the inheritance of the Prophet's caliphate — the Murjiʾa. The third, the people of truth, accepted him as the rightful master and guardian of the believers.[^cite-3]
