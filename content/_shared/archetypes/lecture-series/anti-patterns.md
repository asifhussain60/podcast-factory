# Anti-Patterns — Lecture-Series (Oral Teaching Register)

This document catalogs authoring failures specific to the lecture-series archetype.
Each anti-pattern is flagged by the challenger and blocks episode approval.

---

## Host behavior

**Anti-pattern: Mirroring the lecture file order without a synthesis map.**
Lecture series have a pedagogical sequence optimized for a classroom, not for
audio narrative. Hosts that simply follow session 1, 2, 3 without a synthesis
map produce episodes that feel like transcribed notes, not a curated series.
The pre-synthesis phase must produce a thematic arc before authoring begins.

**Anti-pattern: Treating recap as filler.**
"As we established last time…" is load-bearing in oral teaching. Hosts must
carry the recap forward, not treat it as throat-clearing. Each recap names what
changed in the listener's understanding, not just what was said.

**Anti-pattern: Skipping the gaps the lecturer assumed.**
Oral teachers rely on student questions and shared context. Podcast listeners
have neither. Hosts must fill in the connective tissue that a live audience
could ask for — they cannot assume the listener heard the previous session.

---

## Citation

**Anti-pattern: Piling citations at the end of a topic.**
In lecture-register, citations are live in the argument, not footnoted at the
end. If a scholar is invoked, their position must be named and engaged at the
moment of relevance, not listed as "sources consulted."

**Anti-pattern: Arabic transliteration without phonetic scaffolding.**
Lecture series on Islamic or Arabic topics carry dense technical vocabulary.
Every term must receive a phonetic scaffold on first use in an episode, even
if it appeared in a prior episode.

---

## Register

**Anti-pattern: Departing from the teaching register into casual chat.**
The lecture-series archetype has a defined register: measured, pedagogical,
forward-building. Hosts drifting into informal banter ("that's actually kind of
wild when you think about it") break the tonal contract with the listener.

**Anti-pattern: Treating gap-fill as speculation.**
When the pre-synthesis phase identifies thematic gaps, hosts must address them
as part of the intellectual work — not as their personal opinions. "The text
doesn't say, but we might speculate…" is almost always wrong in this archetype.
Gap-fill must cite the tradition's own resources for filling the gap.

**Anti-pattern: Episode ending before the pedagogical arc closes.**
Each episode corresponds to one thematic cluster from the synthesis map, not
one lecture file. An episode that ends mid-cluster leaves the listener without
the unit of understanding the archetype promises.
