# Exemplar Episode — Lecture-Series (Oral Teaching Register)

This document shows a well-formed episode structure for the lecture-series archetype.
The synthesis map must be complete before authoring begins; this exemplar
assumes the map has already identified the thematic cluster for this episode.

---

## Opening

Host A names the thematic cluster and its place in the arc:
> "Today we enter the third cluster in [Teacher's name]'s teaching on [subject]:
> the relationship between [concept A] and [concept B]. This cluster sits at
> the midpoint of the series — the place where everything the teacher said about
> [earlier theme] has to be tested against something harder."

Host B connects to what the listener already knows:
> "In the last episode, we established that [prior cluster conclusion]. That
> settled something. But it immediately opens a question [Teacher] couldn't
> leave unanswered: [the cluster's central question]."

---

## Discussion

**Segment 1 — The pedagogical problem.** State what the teacher was trying
to help a student understand, and why the standard account falls short.
Use the teacher's own framing where the source text gives it directly.

**Segment 2 — The teaching move.** Walk through the argument the teacher
makes step by step. Each step must be complete — no jumps that require the
listener to have been in the room. Phonetic scaffolds for technical terms
go here, on first use.

**Segment 3 — The student's point of resistance.** Every good teaching
addresses an objection. Hosts voice the objection clearly before showing
how the teacher handles it. Do not tell the listener the objection is wrong
before the teacher's answer has been given.

*Recap bridge example (mid-episode):*
> Host A: "So what we've just established is that [mini-conclusion from
> segment 2]. That changes how we hear the next move."
> Host B: "Right — because now [teacher]'s claim in [next part] isn't
> an assertion, it's a consequence."

---

## Closing

Host synthesis names what the listener now knows that they didn't before:
> "The cluster we just worked through resolves one question and opens another.
> We now know [concrete takeaway]. The question it opens is [next cluster's
> central tension]."

Host B places the next episode in context:
> "In the next episode we enter [next cluster name]. [Teacher] is going to need
> everything established today to make that argument work."

*No premature closure on open doctrinal questions.* If the teaching leaves a
question open — as Ismaili esoteric teaching often does — name the opening
explicitly: "The teacher stops here. The completion belongs to the next level
of initiation, not to a podcast."
