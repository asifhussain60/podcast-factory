# Anti-Patterns — Encyclopedic-Epistolary

This document catalogs authoring failures specific to the encyclopedic-epistolary
archetype. Each anti-pattern is flagged by the challenger and blocks episode approval.

---

## Host behavior

**Anti-pattern: Treating every domain as equally weighted.**
Encyclopedic-epistolary texts span cosmology, ethics, music, biology, and more.
Hosts that give every domain the same airtime produce shapeless episodes. Each
episode must follow a domain arc — one domain, one argument, one conclusion —
even if the source letter spans many.

**Anti-pattern: Reading salutation formulae as content.**
The opening "In the name of God…" and closing "God is witness…" of each letter
are structural, not semantic. Hosts that read them as meaningful content waste
the listener's attention and break narrative momentum.

**Anti-pattern: Collapsing the cross-domain synthesis into a single claim.**
The capstone episode must map all domains to one insight, not reduce them to a
slogan. "Everything connects" is not a synthesis. The hosts must name the
specific architecture the author used to hold the domains together.

---

## Citation

**Anti-pattern: Skipping tier-4 citations in a text that saturates all tiers.**
The Rasāʾil and similar encyclopedic texts cite Quran, hadith, Greek philosophy,
and mystical tradition in the same paragraph. All four citation tiers are active.
A challenger finding for missing tier-4 coverage in this archetype is a P0.

**Anti-pattern: Numbering the enumerated lists instead of collapsing them.**
Encyclopedic texts list "the seven reasons," "the nine properties," "the four
elements." Hosts that read numbered lists verbatim produce radio listicles, not
intelligent podcasts. Lists over five items must be collapsed to the two or three
that carry the argument's weight.

---

## Register

**Anti-pattern: Mixing epistolary address with analytical distance.**
The source text addresses a named recipient in second person. Hosts must choose:
either sustain the epistolary intimacy (rare, only when the address is central
to the argument) or drop into the co-explorer mode and treat the letter as
object, not voice. Mixing both produces an incoherent register.

**Anti-pattern: Underusing the diagram-density flag.**
High-diagram-density archetypes signal that visual structure is doing cognitive
work in the source. When the text is classifying, mapping, or demonstrating a
hierarchy, hosts must name the structure, not just describe the content. "This
letter maps four domains onto a single geometric schema" is better than "the
author covers four topics here."

**Anti-pattern: Treating the phased rollout as optional.**
Large encyclopedic series (like the full Rasāʾil — 52 epistles) must be phased.
Publishing all episodes before listener engagement data exists is a cost and
quality risk. The phased-rollout flag in meta.yml is a contract, not a suggestion.
