# Exemplar Episode — Encyclopedic-Epistolary

This document shows a well-formed episode structure for the encyclopedic-epistolary
archetype. It assumes a completed domain-arc plan; each episode covers one
domain from the source epistle, not one epistle in full.

---

## Opening

Host A frames the domain and places it in the series arc:
> "Letter [N] of the *[Source Title]* takes on [domain]. In the series arc,
> this domain is the hinge — everything the Brethren established about
> [prior domain] has to hold for their account of [this domain] to work."

Host B states the epistolary frame:
> "The address is the same as every letter — a 'dear brother' opening that
> signals we're still in the inner circle. But what [author name] is doing
> in this letter is different: the survey isn't for orientation anymore.
> It's for classification."

---

## Discussion

**Segment 1 — The domain's scope.** Name the domain and the author's
classification schema. This is where diagram-density matters: if the
letter uses geometric or numerical structure to organize the domain,
name the structure explicitly. Do not just list the categories.

**Segment 2 — The core argument.** What is the author trying to establish
about this domain that the listener could not have assumed before reading
the letter? State it plainly, then walk through the two or three claims
that support it. Collapse any enumerated lists over five items.

**Segment 3 — The cross-domain pivot.** Encyclopedic-epistolary texts
always show how the current domain connects to another. Name the connection
precisely. This is where tier-4 citations are most likely to appear —
have the phonetic scaffold ready.

*Cross-domain pivot example:*
> Host A: "What the Brethren are doing here is the same move they made
> in the letter on music — the structure is isomorphic."
> Host B: "Right — the numerical proportions that govern harmony govern
> the soul's faculties by the same logic. It's not a metaphor, it's a claim."

---

## Closing

Host synthesis names what the domain revealed that wasn't visible before:
> "What we've established in this letter is [concrete insight about the domain].
> That resolves the question left open after [prior domain episode]."

Host B places the next episode in the series:
> "In the next episode we enter [next domain]. The Brethren's account of
> [this domain] will need to hold up under [next domain's pressure]."

*Phased rollout note.* If this is the final episode of the first phase,
close with a genuine summary of what the phase covered and what remains
for the second phase — do not simulate a series finale that hasn't happened.
The listener deserves an honest accounting of the series scope.
