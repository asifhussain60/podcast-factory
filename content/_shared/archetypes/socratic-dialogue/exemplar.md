# Exemplar Episode — Socratic Dialogue (Question-and-Answer)

This document shows a well-formed episode structure for the socratic-dialogue archetype.
It is illustrative, not prescriptive — adapt framing language to the specific text.

---

## Opening

Host A frames the inquiry:
> "The exchange we're working through today opens with a question that sounds simple
> — [questioner] asks [respondent]: '[opening question from text]'. But the answer
> [respondent] gives immediately complicates the question. By the end of the exchange,
> we're not answering the original question — we're discovering why it was the wrong
> question to start with."

Host B names the doctrinal destination:
> "And where [respondent] is heading — where [Author] is heading — is a claim about
> [doctrinal endpoint]. The dialogue is the vehicle; the destination is a specific
> theological position, not just a method."

---

## Discussion

The hosts move through the exchange in inquiry-arc order:

**The opening question:** Establish what [questioner] is actually asking. Neither host
re-narrates the question — they engage it as active interlocutors who find it genuinely
interesting.

**The first answer and its complication:** [Respondent]'s answer doesn't close the
inquiry — it opens it. Host A presents the answer; Host B names what it leaves unresolved.

**The pivot exchange:** The moment the dialogue turns — where the original question
is reframed. Both hosts inhabit this pivot. Do not summarise it; work through it.

**The doctrinal conclusion:** Where does the dialogue arrive? State the position that
[Author] is arguing for through the dialogue form — and what its implications are.

---

## Closing

Host A gives the exchange its due:
> "What makes this dialogue form effective is that [questioner]'s role is not naïve —
> the questions are designed to surface exactly the moves [respondent] needs to make.
> [Author] is using the Q&A structure as a teaching machine."

Host B opens the forward question:
> "The question we carry into the next exchange is whether [respondent]'s position here
> is stable — whether the method that produced it can be applied to [next section's
> question] without contradiction."
