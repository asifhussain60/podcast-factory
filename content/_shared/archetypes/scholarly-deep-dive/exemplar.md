# Exemplar Episode — Scholarly Deep Dive (Academic Exposition)

This document shows a well-formed episode structure for the scholarly-deep-dive archetype.
It is illustrative, not prescriptive — adapt framing language to the specific text.

---

## Opening

Host A locates the chapter's argument within the book's broader project:
> "This chapter does something deceptively simple: it takes a question that every
> classical scholar in this tradition accepted as settled and asks whether the answer
> they all agreed on was actually argued for, or merely assumed. [Author] argues it
> was assumed — and that the assumption conceals a doctrinal fault line that matters."

Host B sharpens the stakes:
> "And the reason it matters isn't historical curiosity. The position [Author] is
> challenging is still operative in how this tradition thinks about [concept]. If the
> argument holds, it changes the conclusion — not just here, but across the whole
> apparatus."

---

## Discussion

The hosts move through the chapter's argument in logical order:

**Section 1 — The claim:** What is the chapter's central assertion? State it precisely.
Neither host paraphrases it as a summary; they engage it as an active position.

**Section 2 — The evidence:** What textual or logical support does the author marshal?
Host A presents the evidence; Host B stress-tests it with the author's own logic.

**Section 3 — The implication:** What follows if the argument holds? Host B draws the
implication; Host A names the resistance it would face from within the tradition.

---

## Closing

Host A names the chapter's contribution:
> "What [Author] has done here is locate the load-bearing assumption — the one that
> everything else in this argument rests on — and demonstrate that it was never
> independently established. That's a surgical intervention."

Host B ends with the forward question:
> "The question that opens from here is whether [next chapter's problem] can be
> answered on [Author]'s terms or requires the assumption back. That's what we follow
> next session."
