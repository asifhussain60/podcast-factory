# Anti-Patterns — Scholarly Deep Dive (Academic Exposition)

This document catalogs authoring failures specific to the scholarly-deep-dive archetype.
Each anti-pattern is flagged by the challenger and blocks episode approval.

---

## Host behavior

**Anti-pattern: Summarising instead of engaging the argument.**
Hosts that describe what the chapter argues ("In this chapter, the author explains…")
without engaging its logic reduce a rigorous scholarly text to a book report. The episode
must inhabit the argument — hosts respond to the claim, push on its evidence, and name
its implications.

**Anti-pattern: Dropping the scholarly register mid-episode.**
The listener has chosen a scholarly source. A sudden shift to casual idiom ("basically,"
"it's kind of like…") signals that the hosts don't trust the content. Maintain the
analytical register throughout.

**Anti-pattern: Ending before addressing the central claim.**
Every scholarly chapter is organised around a thesis. An episode that runs out of time
before stating whether that thesis holds is incomplete, not conversational.

---

## Citation handling

**Anti-pattern: Rendering citations as raw numbers.**
"As footnote 47 demonstrates" is unlistenable. Attribute claims: "As al-Kirmānī argues
in [work], citing the Quran at [reference]…"

**Anti-pattern: Treating Quran/hadith references as decorative.**
Every canonical citation the author makes was deliberate. The episode must either explain
the reference's doctrinal function or omit it entirely — not quote it as atmosphere.

---

## Enrichment

**Anti-pattern: Biographical padding instead of doctrinal context.**
"Al-Kirmānī was born in Kirman and studied under Ibn Masarrah" is not enrichment.
The enrichment the listener needs is: "Al-Kirmānī is arguing from the Neoplatonic
emanation framework, which holds that [doctrine]…"

**Anti-pattern: Shallow summary masquerading as analysis.**
If the hosts' contribution amounts to a table of contents recitation, the episode has
added nothing. Each section of discussion must add interpretive value.
