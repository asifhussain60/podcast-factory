# Exemplar Episode — Aphorism Collection (Wisdom Sayings)

This document shows a well-formed episode structure for the aphorism-collection archetype.
It is illustrative, not prescriptive — adapt framing language to the specific text.

---

## Opening

Host A locates the thematic cluster:
> "The sayings we're working with today all circle the same question — how does a
> person hold conviction and uncertainty at the same time? [Author/Imam/Teacher] returns
> to this question four or five times across [work], from slightly different angles each
> time. What we hear, across the cluster, is not contradiction but triangulation."

Host B names the doctrinal stakes:
> "And that triangulation matters because the tradition this comes from doesn't separate
> belief from action. What [Author] is describing isn't an interior state — it's a
> practice. The question isn't 'how do you feel certain?' It's 'what do you do when
> you're not?'"

---

## Discussion

The hosts move through the cluster thematically:

**Unit 1–3 (opening cluster):** Present the group of aphorisms that opens the theme.
Host A reads the substance; Host B locates the doctrinal resonance — Quran, hadith,
or the tradition's specific theological vocabulary.

**The pivot aphorism:** Identify the one saying in the cluster that reframes the others
or introduces an unexpected angle. Both hosts engage it directly — this is the heart
of the episode.

**Closing units:** How does the cluster resolve? Does [Author] land on a practical
counsel, a doctrinal assertion, or a question left open?

---

## Closing

Host A gives the cluster its weight:
> "What [Author] is doing across this group of sayings is building an argument through
> accumulation rather than through explicit logic. You only see the full claim when
> you stand back and look at the pattern."

Host B opens the forward question:
> "The question that carries into the next cluster is whether [Author] thinks this
> practice can be taught or whether it requires — as several of the sayings suggest —
> a particular kind of companionship to develop."
