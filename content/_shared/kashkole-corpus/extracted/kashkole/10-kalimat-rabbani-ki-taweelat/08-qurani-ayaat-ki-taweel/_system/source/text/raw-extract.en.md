Amir al-Mu'minin 'Ali ibn Abi Talib (a.s.) said: "Whoever comes to the mosque after performing complete ablution, he will be considered to be in the state of prayer until he performs a hadith, i.e., his ablution is not broken."

In the interpretation of the Qur'an, it means that whoever takes the oath of allegiance to Imam al-Zaman (a.s.) and purifies himself from the impurities of suspicion and polytheism, and participates in the invitation of the Imam (a.s.), which is in the mosque or the House of Allah, as long as he remains in that invitation and does not do such acts and actions that break the pledge of allegiance, he will be punished for all his actions. An action will be acceptable to God, whether it is worldly or religious. But if he does a hadith, i.e., taunts the Imam and his limitations, or opposes them in their backbiting, or openly opposes them, then he will withdraw from his invitation and pledge of allegiance, and then none of his actions will be acceptable to Allah.

Allah gives the glad tidings of Paradise to those who follow the righteous believers and spend their lives in the circle of their allegiance.
