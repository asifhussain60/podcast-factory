## The Virtue of Surah Al-Fatiha

⟪ar:سورۃ الفاتحۃ⟫The opening chapter of the Qur'an possesses innumerable spiritual blessings. It has been transmitted that this Surah commands such eminence that it would occasion no surprise if it were recited over the deceased, and through its blessed power the departed soul were to rise again. This mighty chapter is designated ⟪ar:'أم الکتاب'⟫—the Mother of the Book—for within it resides the light of the entire Qur'an. All subsequent chapters constitute elaborations upon this foundational sura, deriving their authority and meaning from its principles.[^cite-1]

The Qur'an itself has been compared to the Gospel in a particular sense. Just as the Gospel represents an exegesis of the four words Christ preached to his four designated disciples—four interpreters who gathered their master's utterances and named their compilation ⟪ar:'انجیل'⟫, derived from ⟪ar:'نجل'⟫ (offspring)—so too does the Qur'an emerge from a single source. The source of the Gospel is the fourfold teaching of Jesus; the source of the Qur'an is Surah al-Hamd (⟪ar:سورۃ الحمد⟫), itself called the Mother of the Book.

The virtue of this Surah manifests most clearly in prayer. The supplication (⟪ar:'خیر العمل'⟫—the best of deeds) cannot be validly performed without it. Should one recite ⟪ar:سورۃ الحمد⟫alone in the prayer and omit all other chapters, the prayer shall be accepted. Yet should one recite another surah while neglecting ⟪ar:سورۃ الفاتحۃ⟫, the prayer is rejected entirely—it becomes as though ⟪ar:خیر العمل⟫were annulled altogether.

To illuminate the station of this sura, the Almighty addressed His Messenger thus:

> ⟪quran 15:87⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَكَ سَبْعًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْمَثَانِى وَٱلْقُرْآنَ ٱلْعَظِيمَ ‎⟫
>
> *And indeed, We have bestowed upon you seven of Al-Mathani (the seven repeatedly recited Verses, i.e., Surat Al-Fatiha) and the Grand Qur'an.*

These seven verses are recited and renewed perpetually throughout the believer's life.[^cite-2]

The Prophet, peace be upon him, declared: "The banner granted to me upon the Day of Resurrection, distinguishing me from all other prophets, is called the Banner of Praise (Liwa al-Hamd). It shall bear seven rings, each extending from east to west."

⟪ar:عالم ربانی، حکیم الامۃ، قرآن ناطق⟫The Prophet further said: "Were I inclined, I could write such abundant commentary upon Surah Al-Hamd that fifty camels would bear the manuscript!"[^cite-3]

### Narratives Concerning Surah Al-Fatiha

Imam al-Husayn, peace be upon him, appointed a teacher to instruct his son in Surah al-Fatiha. Upon hearing his child's recitation, the Imam's joy overflowed, and he filled the boy's mouth with jewels as reward.

At the Battle of Siffin, a warrior's hand was shattered. He approached Maulana ʿAli and lamented his incapacity to wield the sword. Maulana ʿAli bound the hand with cloth and breathed upon it—reciting ⟪ar:سورۃ الفاتحۃ⟫—and when the cloth was removed, the hand was whole. The astonished man asked what incantation had wrought this healing, and was told simply: "Surah Al-Fatiha." His disbelief at the surah's potency was punished when the hand fractured anew, returning to its broken state.

When the Prophet, peace be upon him, would sneeze, he recited Surah Al-Fatiha in full, then blew upon both palms and wiped his face. This practice traces to Adam, upon him be peace, who upon his first sneeze uttered "Praise be to Allah"—a utterance so pleasing to the Almighty that His Presence elevated Adam's rank. Thus we emulate Adam's thanksgiving when we ourselves sneeze.

Noah, upon him be peace, was named ʿAbd al-Shakoor (Servant of the Grateful) because whenever he partook of nourishment, he would open with "Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Rahim" and close with "Praise be to Allah." The sustenance of the spiritual realm arrived in the form of the Qur'an, whose first word became the praise of the grateful.

The eminence of Surah Al-Fatiha is such that were the people of the assembly able to comprehend it fully, revelation of the remaining Qur'an would be superfluous; for the entire Qur'an originates in this single chapter. Therefore, no prayer is accepted without it. In those prayers containing no bowing (rukuʿ)—notably the funeral prayer with its distinctive stations—Surah Al-Fatiha is not recited, a fact pointing to mystical realities.

Alone among all chapters, Surah Al-Fatiha was revealed in perfect, undivided form; all others came piecemeal.

Amir al-Muʾminin ʿAli declared: "Should I undertake the esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl) of Surah Al-Fatiha, the commentary would equal the weight of seventy camels."

## The Jewels (Jawahir) of Surah Al-Fatiha

Though the apparent verses and words of Surah Al-Fatiha may be counted, the luminous jewels emanating from it are infinite and their virtues incalculable. Its outward essence comprises twenty-one letters. Of these twenty-one, fourteen are noorani (radiant jewels). Within these fourteen, seven are *Jawahir Nooraniyyah Khafifa* (subtle radiant jewels) and seven are *Jawahir Nooraniyyah Saqila* (weighty radiant jewels). A further seven letters are *Jawahir Zulmaniyyah* (oppressive jewels).

The third category—the oppressive jewels—operates as light within darkness. Though dark as an unlit house, when a lamp burns within, the entire chamber is illuminated. The divine light shines through these dark vessels, the human body (which is perishable), transforming the oppressive into the luminous. Beyond these three kinds stand seven letters of the *Muʿjam*—letters marked with diacritical points—which are *Jawahir Zulmaniyyah Saqila* (weighty oppressive jewels).

**The Noorani: Subtle and Weighty**

The seven letters of the radiant category point to the seven primordial intellects of the spiritual realm, free from all deviation. The *Jawahir Nooraniyyah Saqila* refer to seven intellects of the spiritual world that evolved after the eternal intellects, becoming their successors and representatives. In another sense, these seven letters indicate the seven intellects of the cosmos itself. The term *saqila* (weighty) reflects the profound radiance whose weight manifests in the dots inscribed within certain letters.

**The Oppressive**

Surah Al-Fatiha contains twenty-one letters, yet the letters of *Jawahir Zulmaniyyah Saqila* do not appear within it. The oppressive refers to the *Naqqas*—those marked with distinction yet oppressed by their purity, adjacent to radiance though dwelling in darkness. Though possessing evil natures that are oppressive, the divine light shines within them, conferring luminosity. As a lamp burns in a dark house, transforming shadow to illumination, so these entities carry the light of divinity, and thus are themselves luminous. Their slender forms attach themselves to beams of support; as when a polished mirror is placed before the sun and becomes itself apparent as the sun, so too do they reflect the divine radiance.

**Observation**

Throughout the Qur'an, only Surah Al-Fatiha contains no letter Fa (ف) of calamity; all other surahs employ this letter. Among the oppressive letters, the highest is Ba (ب), and among the pronounced letters, the highest is Fa (ف).

**Human Beings Like You**

The Messenger of Allah, may Allah's peace and blessings be upon him, was commanded to declare: "I am a human being like you." Yet immediately after, he added: "To me comes revelation." Through this utterance, he elevated himself and those like him above ordinary humanity—a supremely eloquent pronouncement of the Qur'an. He did not recite these words before the common people and his companions, but rather before the people of purity dwelling in the realm of chastity. This connects to the verse of purification. When that verse was revealed, he wrapped his cloak around himself, ʿAli, Fatima, al-Hasan, and al-Husayn, announcing: "Those beneath this cloak are my Ahl al-Bayt." Though Um Salama held high station, he did not include her. Only later, when Gabriel descended, did he say: "Gabriel is our sixth." The four within the cloak thanked him for the revelation, yet he replied: "It was not I who spoke—rather, the reason for this verse's descent lies within each of you four." He then addressed them: "I am a human being like you—I said this only to the four who, like me, dwell in the realm of chastity."

**Non-Recurring and Recurring**

The seven intellects of the spiritual realm are non-recurring; by comparison, logic is recurring. Points recur seven after seven, appearing in seventy in each cycle. Similarly, the time of prayer surpasses prayer itself; we cannot pray until time arrives, meaning we await its coming. The awaiting transcends that which is awaited. Therefore the times excel the established Imams, for all Imams await them, and the Qaʾim himself awaits. The Imams are non-recurring; the Qaʾim is recurring in contrast to them.

We recite Surah Al-Fatiha (the non-recurring) in each unit of prayer and change the subsequent surah (the recurring). The Fatiha refers to the inner summons (daʿwa, the invitation), and the other verses refer to the outer summons. The invitation's essence remains forever unchanged, descending from the owner of the primordial self to the established Imam and the Qaʾim. Yet the outer invitation varies—to explain the inner to different peoples, the modes shift according to epochs, temperaments, and intellects, though the origin always remains constant.

## The Esoteric Exegesis (Taʾwīl) of Surah Al-Fatiha

### Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Rahim

This verse refers to the realm of creation, wherein the Creator brought forth all existents from the beginning of time.

### Al-Hamdu Lillahi Rabbi al-ʿAlamin (Praise Be to Allah, Lord of the Worlds)

⟪ar:'الحمد'⟫Praise belongs to Him—to the First Intellect (al-ʿAql al-Awwal)—by whose praise all the worlds (the physical realm, the soul-world, the angelic realm, the corporeal realm) are established. Their Lord is He through whom all these worlds subsist and remain connected; by Him they perpetuate and endure. All things directed toward Him render praise, and all existence aspires toward the perfection of authentication, toward the ⟪ar:مَبدَع⟫—the Originator, Most High.

### Al-Rahman Al-Rahim (The Most Merciful, the Merciful)

Though appearing as two words, their substance and origin are identical, both deriving from ⟪ar:'رحم'⟫—mercy. Yet ⟪ar:'رحمن'⟫ (the All-Merciful) is reserved exclusively for Allah, while ⟪ar:'رحیم'⟫ (the Merciful) may be shared among creation.[^cite-4]

### Malik Youm ad-Din (Master of the Day of Judgment)

He is the Lord of ⟪ar:یوم الجزا⟫—the Day of Recompense—wherein the final correlation mirrors the first: the first cause being absolute and total, the last cause also absolute. As all creatures and creators reached this utterance, they confessed with unified voice:

### Iyyaka Naʿbudu wa Iyyaka Nastaʿin (You Alone Do We Worship and You Alone Do We Seek Aid)

This follows because the authentication and bond of all existence is with the First Reality, from which springs the life of all.

### Ihdinā as-Sirat al-Mustaqim (Guide Us Upon the Straight Path)

Every lower limit (ḥadd dānī) supplicates to transcend itself toward elevation, for through this transcendence lies its life, survival, and sustenance.

### Sirat al-Ladhina Anʿamta ʿAlayhim Ghayri al-Maghdub ʿAlayhim wa la al-Dhallin (The Path of Those Upon Whom You Have Bestowed Favor, Not of Those Upon Whom Wrath Has Fallen, Nor of the Astray)

⟪ar:انعمت علیھم⟫It refers to the blessing Allah granted to His chosen servants—the First Messiah and the Second Messiah—as He declared:

> ⟪quran 43:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا عَبْدٌ أَنْعَمْنَا عَلَيْهِ وَجَعَلْنَهُ مَثَلًۭا لِّبَنِىٓ إِسْرَٓءِيلَ ‎⟫
>
> *He was only a servant upon whom We bestowed favor, and We made him an example for the Children of Israel.*

⟪ar:غیر المغضوب علیھم⟫refers to the Jews in their outward form, and specifically to those of the Jews within the Ummah of Muhammad—the Sunnis—who turned away. *Wala al-Dallin* refers to Christians outwardly, and to those Christians within Muhammad's Ummah—the Shias—who deviated.[^cite-5]

## The Seven Opposites of the Accents (Lahja) of Surah Al-Fatiha

The oppressive accents refer to the inscriptions of the alphabet—the pronunciations of the lower veils (⟪ar:'حجب دانیۃ'⟫). The personalities of such people are oppressed, and their souls are sinners devoid of the light of divine support. Their condition resembles a dark house with no lamp kindled within. These are they from whom divine support has been withdrawn, left to wander in darkness. The Qur'an addresses them thus: Until now the limits remained within the summons; yet through their deeds, Allah removed support from them. For instance, Daud ibn Qutb was within the limits of Maulana Daud ibn ʿAjab Shah, but because of transgression, the light of support was taken from him, and he entered among the letters of the oppressive weighty category.

These seven letters of the oppressive weighty class could not enter Surah Al-Fatiha (the inner summons), though they did enter its accents (the tones of the inner summons).

These defects enter through accents, diverting people from the outer to the outer, preventing them from reaching the inner—the station proper to Muslims, who must journey from outer to inner. An elderly person giving a banana to someone who locks it in a safe would be called foolish; the elder gave the fruit so he might peel away the skin and consume the essence. Similarly, the Holy Prophet gave the revealed Law (Shariah) so people might peel away the outer shell and grasp the inner secrets hidden within it. Yet many Muslims became trapped in the shells. These defects entered the accents of the inner summons. For this reason, those who teach must speak with great precision, lest through error they mislead those before them or distort their spiritual state—lest they speak from personal desire, allowing Satan to enter the accents. It is said that on the Day of Resurrection, Allah shall address the teachers (*al-Mufidun*): "Now break your carved idols!"—meaning the false images planted in disciples' minds through the teacher's private desires. Surah Al-Fatiha exemplifies this: introducing private desire into its expression is like introducing desire into the accent of the Fatiha. Thus it is said that many prayers destroyed their people because those who taught them mixed in their own explanations. This is why we perpetually beseech Allah for purity of speech!

In *La ilaha illa Allah* (There is no god but Allah), negation precedes affirmation—we cannot move toward God's name until all other deities are negated through *La ilaha illa Allah*. Similarly, on the Day of Ghadir, when the Messenger of Allah, may Allah's peace and blessings be upon him, declared, "Of whomsoever I am the master, ʿAli is the master,"—this utterance negated all other limits, confessing only ʿAli's mastery. Thus the Prophet proclaimed: "There is no limit (in the realm of the summons) capable of attaining the divine station, save for Amir al-Muʾminin ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫."

**Stubbornness Always Arises from the Summons**

When a person falls ill—say, from fever—the face first shows the sickness. Similarly, when disturbance arises in the summons of the Naqqas, it manifests in the limits of the supreme commands, which are the face of the summons. Lower limits do not produce errors.

Abu Bakr and ʿUmar entered the Prophet's shrine through their prior services, yet later they experienced a breakdown—as shall happen to the Qaʾim himself, when disturbance strikes his boundaries. These disturbances are truly disturbances of the lower veils (the *Hijab Daniya*)—the *Naqqas*—not of the primordial self. The veils are those who ascend above limits; for example, Firat ibn Ahnaf became the veil of Maulana Ismaʿil ibn Jaʿfar al-Sadiq.

**Statement**

It is narrated that Daud ibn Qutb fell into error when he observed the amount of zakat. Maulana Daud ibn ʿAjab Shah had fixed his stipend at five rupees; in those days one goat cost one rupee. Yet the zakat amount reached eighty thousand rupees. This wealth disturbed his intention.

The essence of the stubbornness of the summons—the form (the evil conception)—is oppressive and cruel, departing from the summons of Allah's saints and becoming obstinate, corrupting the summons.

**Realities**

Stubbornness is always the veil, never the obstinacy of obstinacy itself. All prophets and Imams were killed, yet no resistance shall arise against the Qaʾim, because Qaʾim ʿAli stands in the station of divine remembrance itself, and it is impossible to oppose Allah. Satan does not oppose Allah nor stand against Him (being incapable), but stands against His servants—the veiling Imams.

## Adam and His Opposite

Adam emerged from clay—the oppressive element that carries humankind toward death. Yet Allah breathed into this dead soil His spirit, reviving it, teaching it the truths of all things, and then proclaimed:

Through divine teaching, a succession of radiant lights and blessings descended to Adam. These lights were granted through the letters of the radiant category—the intellects. Yet they received this support from their resident, Imam Maulana Huneed, upon him be peace. As the burnished mirror accepts the sun's light, so they received the divine radiance from their inhabitants. Allah declared:

They walk among people on the path of guidance through the light of divine support.

By people is meant those withdrawing from your law—the law in which wisdom's pearls are sown. Regarding his opposite (Harith ibn Murra), he is said to dwell in oppression; his soul is oppressed, his body devoid of support. Allah declared:

On the Day of Resurrection they shall cry to their Lord: "Misfortune has overtaken us, and we are a people gone astray."

## Surah Al-Fatiha Is the Cause of Intercession

Surah Al-Fatiha became a cure for all spiritual ailments because the oppressive letters—which are defects—have been excluded from it. Having grown weary of multiplication and its attachments, this Surah Al-Fatiha shall elevate us from this world to the celestial realm. A feast involving multiplication shall never be elevated.

When a believer's death is rooted in the concept of renunciation and detachment from all bonds, then this pure inner summons shall convey that believer's form to the Imam's sanctuary, and on the day of the month, all these forms shall enter the spiritual realm through divine sovereignty.

Self-development resides in the noble summons! And the summons becomes the cause of a person's spiritual development because it contains no defects. The Qur'an declares:

Within it dwells mercy. That is, mercy in the inner summons arises because it contains no letters of oppression. Its outer realm is the circle of punishment—whatever lies outside the scope of the inner summons involves punishment, wherein these defects operate.

## The Session of the Summons in Surah Al-Fatiha

Within Surah Al-Fatiha lies the entire session of the daʿwa (the summons, from the intellect of the spiritual realm to the intellect of the physical realm). All the spiritual movements of each station are present in this chapter, which is why it was made obligatory in prayer: the summons cannot stand without Surah Al-Fatiha.

We are confined within a circle of seven days of the week and cannot exit any single day. The day we depart from this circle is the day of our spiritual death. Similarly, we are locked within the circles of these seven intellects. Should we transcend them, we experience spiritual death.

*Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Rahim* is sometimes included at the beginning of the surahs, sometimes excluded. In Surah Al-Fatiha it is included as the first verse; in all other surahs it does not count as the first verse. Likewise, we sometimes enumerate the Imam (peace be upon him) among the limits, sometimes exclude him. When we say there are ten degrees from the Summoner to the Imam, we include the Imam. When we say the Imam is distinct and the rest are few, we exclude him. The same principle applies to the First Intellect.

## The Distinction Between Creator and Lord

When Allah Almighty began His utterance, He commenced with: "Praise be to Allah, Lord of the Worlds." Yet some minds might have expected: "Praise be to Allah, Creator of the Worlds." Allah praised Himself through His Lordship, not through His creation—for the station of training (lordship) exceeds creation, just as secondary perfection (kamāl thānī) surpasses primary perfection (kamāl awwal). Indeed, a parent's training of a child surpasses mere procreation. Thus the mother holds great virtue, being the trainer.

| Creator | Lord |
| --- | --- |

| Birth | Training |

In this verse, praise takes precedence: Allah did not say "Praise is for Allah," but rather "All praise belongs to Allah." This means the Imam of Allah and His proof—His successor—merits this praise. The true meaning of following an Imam can only be grasped if we believe in the Imam's successor. The Messenger of Allah can only be followed if his successor ʿAli is followed.[^cite-7]

**Realities**

The numerical value of *al-Hamd* is fifty-two, indicating that between the first and the last shall appear fifty-two beings—the opposites of praise.

## The Esoteric Exegesis of "All Praise Is for Allah, Lord of the Worlds"

By Lord in this verse is meant partly the preacher and the Gate of Gates (*Bab al-Abwab*)—he who trains the forms, straightens their crookedness, and intensifies their illumination. The word *al-Alamin* (the worlds) refers to the worlds of the islands, the lords, and the hearts of heaven.

| Lord | The Worlds |

The words *Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Rahim* appear in Surah Al-Fatiha. In this verse, the All-Merciful exceeds the Merciful, though both spring from the same root word: mercy. Yet their radiance differs. Every thousand years witnesses the appearance of humanity. ## The Esoteric Exegesis of "Master of the Day of Judgment"

In Surah Al-Fatiha, Allah states that He is the Master of the Day of Judgment. Yet a day is said to be fifty thousand years—the period of the era of unveiling (*kashf*).

In the realities, the world is called the Seventy Era, and *Din* (Judgment/Religion) is the Era of Unveiling. That is, Qaʾim ʿAli is the Master of fifty thousand years of the period of unveiling. The eight units of the Afternoon Prayer indicate the eight units of the Afternoon Prayer (during the period of unveiling). Prayer signifies the summons. Just as no prayer follows the Afternoon Prayer, no summons continues after the Qaʾim. After the establishment comes only reckoning, reward, and punishment. No obligations shall exist in the period of unveiling—only the duties already established shall remain. As it is said: "In the period of unveiling, the first volume of *Dua'im al-Islam* shall be completed, and the second volume alone shall endure."

## The Point of Ba and the Point of Nun in Surah Al-Fatiha

Surah Al-Fatiha begins with the point of *Ba* and ends with the point of *Nun*. The point of *Ba* is Amir al-Muʾminin; the letter *Ba* is the Lord of the Universe. (*Ba* and its point are distinct—for instance, remove the points of *Shin*, and it becomes *Sin*; remove the points of *Zhal*, and it becomes *Dal*; but remove the point of *Ba*, and it cannot be read. Thus, if ʿAli's guardianship is removed, the Prophet's religion loses all relevance and becomes obsolete.)

The *Nun* of Surah Al-Fatiha (the *Nun* of the astray) refers to the Jews of Muhammad's Ummah, just as to the Christians of Muhammad's Ummah. That is, *Nun* is the last person among the astray. *Nun*'s form is a circle with a point within; the Qaʾim is determined to save the entire world. The last person in his gathering shall be the spiritual Satan, which is the point of *Nun*. That is, Satan's point shall come within the scope of the Day of the Month.

There are four hundred thousand cores within the peacock; the one arriving at the end shall not be the last. He shall be his father's son, yet shall not ordain his own son. This symbol is placed in the *Nun* of Surah Al-Fatiha. The point within *Nun* is the Spiritual Satan (Iblis Ruhani)—that is, even Iblis shall come to your gathering. Then Satan shall come in the next age, for this devil has also prepared his successor to rule in the following epoch.

## Surah Al-Fatiha Begins with Alif and Ends with Nun

A distinctive feature of Surah Al-Fatiha is that it begins with *Alif* and ends with *Nun*. Similarly, among the Qur'anic surahs, the first is *Alif* of the Qur'an, and the last (the twenty-ninth surah) is *Nun*.

*Alif* signifies *Qama al-Fiyya*—the upright form of all forms, the form of the straight line, wherein the shortest distance between two points is a straight line. When the Creator created the souls (*Ashhah*), He created them in *Qama al-Fiyya* as the most noble form. For this reason, when creating humanity—the noblest of creatures—He fashioned them in the *Alif* form. Similarly, the form assumed at death is also *Alif*, and the forms of the resurrected shall likewise be *Alif*.

*Nun* signifies the circle of *Nun*—its sphere. Whoever enters this circle enters the era of the Holy Age, just as the point of *Nun* dwells within the circle. That is, to attain the level of the millennium, there is no other path but to enter the realm of the Holy Age.

That Surah Al-Fatiha begins with *Alif* and ends with *Nun* indicates that this Surah encompasses the worlds from the leader of the spiritual realm to the last sediment of the physical realm (Iblis). The numerical value of *Alif* is one (1), and that of *Nun* is fifty (50)—the sum being fifty-one (51). Just as Surah Al-Fatiha begins with *Alif* and ends with *Nun*, so the succession begun with ʿAli shall end with Maulana al-Qaʾim—the fifty-first.[^cite-11]
