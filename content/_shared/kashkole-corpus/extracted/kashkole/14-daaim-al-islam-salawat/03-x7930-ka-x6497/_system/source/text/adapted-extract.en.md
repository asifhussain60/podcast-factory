## The Mosque and the Place of Necessity

The mosque signifies the Dawat-e-Sharif (the Noble Summons), and in precisely the same sense, the place of necessity also signifies the Dawat-e-Sharif. For this reason, specific etiquette has been prescribed for both. One should not engage in idle conversation within the mosque, just as one should not linger excessively in the place of necessity. We approach the mosque to fulfill our spiritual needs, even as we enter the place of necessity to fulfill our physical needs. It was for this reason that the Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace and blessings) addressed ʿAlī and declared: "Whoever does not observe the twelve proprieties of the place of necessity has neither known you nor me." He did not make this statement concerning prayers and fasting alone, but specifically concerning the etiquette of that place.[^cite-1]

Great wisdom lies concealed within the etiquette of the place of necessity and its observances. A person should not proceed to worship until the body has been purified and emptied of filth. The Messenger of Allah (upon him be peace) said to ʿAlī: "O ʿAlī, I counsel you that your death should find your stomach empty." The entire purpose of the Prophet's (upon him be peace) addresses to ʿAlī—the highest of believers—is to transmit the message to the lowest among believers. Therefore, a believer should neither enter the mosque of Allah nor meet death until his stomach is empty. They did not rise for the worship of Allah until they had completely emptied their bodies.

On the outward level, it is commanded that when a believer emerges from the place of necessity, he should wash his hands, for he has touched impurity. In the inner realm (bāṭin), this signifies that the believer must purify himself from the knowledge of non-daʿwa (the summons/the invitation)—which dwells in the palace of impurity—before entering into the Dawat-e-Sharif. [^cite-2]

## The Esoteric Interpretation of Mosque Rewards

In one sense, the mosque refers to the Imam (upon him be peace), for just as worshippers gather in a mosque, so do the followers gather around the Imam.

These sciences of non-daʿwa are impure because they were recorded at the behest of the nafs (the lower self), not through divine sanction. The teachings of daʿwa, by contrast, are supremely noble, which is why the Prophet (upon him be peace and blessings) declared: "The example of a believer is like a bee—it consumes what is good and produces what is good." In the same manner, the believer takes the wise utterances of his elders and distills them in his own understanding.[^cite-3]

(Lessons from the Night of Migration, transmitted by Mullā Ghulām Ḥusain ibn Ḥazrat Nasrullāh Ṣāḥib)

### The Friday Mosque and the Ranks of the Daʿwa

The mosque in which the Friday prayer is offered signifies the daʿwa (the summons) of the muballigh (the preacher of truth), and to pray within that mosque is to visit the muballigh and acknowledge his station. All other mosques fall under the jurisdiction and limits of this principal preacher. The most essential element of any mosque is the Qibla, which signifies the Imām al-Zamān (the Imam of the Age), thus indicating that we remain connected to the muballigh who receives the support of the Imam. The reward for prayer varies according to the location and status of each mosque:

| Mosque Type | One *rakʿah* Equals | Hierarchical Rank | Represented Office |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Street Mosque | 12 *Rakʿahs* | — | *Mazūn* Limited |
| Market and Tribal Mosques | 25 *Rakʿahs* | — | *Mazūn* Unlimited |
| Congregational Mosque (*Jāmiʿ Masjid*) | 100 *Rakʿahs* | — | *Dāʿī Mutlaq* (Unrestricted Summoner) |
| Jerusalem (*Bayt al-Maqdis*) | 1,000 *Rakʿahs* | — | *Dāʿī al-Balāgh* (The Summoner of Proclamation) |
| The Prophet's Mosque (Medina) | 10,000 *Rakʿahs* | — | *Ḥujjat* (The Proof) |
| The House of God (Mecca) | 100,000 *Rakʿahs* | — | *Imām* |

The recitation of *Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm* while standing upright in the mosque exemplifies gentleness and clemency, and the two *rakʿahs* of *Taḥiyyat al-Masjid* (the mosque's salutation) serve as a means of establishing contact with the Imam.

### The Prophet's Mosque

The reward of praying one *rakʿah* in the Prophet's Mosque equals 10,000, which is to say that it stands at a ratio of 1:10 relative to the House of God. In other words, ten prayers in the Prophet's Mosque are equivalent to one prayer in the House of Allah, and this principle applies to the station of the Imam relative to the *Ḥujjat* (the Proof). The reason lies in this: the status of the Imam is ten times greater than that of the *Ḥujjat*. Thus, if 10,000 degrees of the *Ḥujjat* are accumulated, the Imam possesses 100,000 degrees.[^cite-5]

### Bayt al-Maqdis (Jerusalem)

Bayt al-Maqdis exemplifies the muballigh who propagates the sharīʿa (the sacred law) through exposition and reasoned argument. Ten prayers in Bayt al-Maqdis equal one prayer in the Prophet's Mosque, and one hundred prayers there equal one prayer in the Kaʿba, which signifies that the station of the muballigh is ten times that of the *Dāʿī al-Balāgh* and one-hundredth that of the Imam.

### The Congregational Mosque

Prayer in the Congregational Mosque (*Jāmiʿ Masjid*) resembles the station of the *Dāʿī Mutlaq* (the Unrestricted Summoner), who is the hijāb (veil) of the muballigh and the channel through which the *Maʿzūn* (the Authorized One) may benefit. The participation of the *Maʿzūnīn* (the Authorized Ones) with the *Dāʿī Mutlaq* is such that whenever a text—a revealed directive—descends upon the *Dāʿī Mutlaq*, it necessarily descends upon the *Maʿzūn* as well. For the instruction of the *Maʿzūn* is the work of the *Dāʿī Mutlaq*, and he is a partner in this work.

### The Market and Street Mosques

The market mosque stands upon the foundation of the unlimited *Mazūn*, while the street mosque rests upon the limited *Mazūn*—one who preaches only to the people of his locality.

## The Etiquette of the Mosque

**Apparent Instruction**

Whoever enters the mosque should place his right foot forward first.

**Inner Meaning**

This signifies that whoever enters the daʿwa (the summons) must place his conviction in the virtues and excellence of his Imam at the forefront, and must cast aside his enemy—he who seeks to usurp the station that belongs to the Imam through rebellion and tyranny.

---

**Apparent Instruction**

Mention the name of Allah upon entering the mosque.

**Inner Meaning**

Upon entering the mosque, one should first recite *Bismillāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm* (In the name of Allah, the Merciful, the Compassionate) and then pronounce *As-Salāmu ʿalayka* (Peace be upon you) or *As-Salāmu ʿalayn-Nabī wa Raḥmatullāhi wa Barakātuh, wa As-Salāmu ʿalaynā wa ʿAlā ʿIbād-Allāh al-Ṣāliḥīn* (Peace be upon the Prophet and the mercy of Allah and His blessings, and peace be upon us and upon the righteous servants of Allah). This exemplifies how the beneficiary takes the covenant with his benefactor and explicates to him the principles and foundations of truth. This too is indicated in the words of Allah: "Do not eat that upon which the name of Allah has not been invoked"—that is, *Bismillāh al-Allāhu Akbar*; if the animal is not slaughtered while invoking the Divine Name, then its meat is forbidden. In the inner sense: do not impart the knowledge of the Dawat al-Hādiyya (the Guiding Summons) to one who has not taken the covenant with the Imām al-Zamān, and it is not permissible to disclose the truth save to those possessed of understanding.[^cite-6]

---

**Apparent Instruction**

Pray two *rakʿahs* of *Taḥiyyat al-Masjid* (the mosque's salutation).

**Inner Meaning**

This exemplifies the acknowledgment and obedience of the station of the *Mazūn* and the *Muballigh*. In another sense, it refers to the elevation of the believer to his supreme station and the exaltation of his degree.

---

**Apparent Instruction**

When worshippers sit awaiting the prayer, they should sit facing the Qibla, and while they rest, they should keep their faces directed toward the Qibla.

**Inner Meaning**

This signifies that in every affair of life and in every circumstance, the believer should direct his attention, sincerity, and love toward the Imām al-Zamān.

---

**Apparent Instruction**

Do not search long in the mosque for something lost.

**Inner Meaning**

A lost thing is that which has been separated from its owner. This exemplifies one who has turned away from the invitation of the Imām al-Zamān. Its interpretation is this: it is not permissible for the *Mustajīb* (the Responder) to impart his wisdom to one who has not entered the era of the Imām al-Zamān, for that person does not believe in obedience to the Imam and therefore should be left undisturbed.

---

**Apparent Instruction**

Refrain from placing rent in the mosque; if necessity compels, carry it in cloth.

**Inner Meaning**

The Prophet (upon him be peace) said: "The mosque trembles at rent just as one turns away from a wooden blow." One who places rent in the mosque exemplifies the *Mustajīb* who reveals his wicked utterances to his muballigh, for the mosque itself is an exemplar of the muballigh.

---

**Apparent Instruction**

Do not buy or sell merchandise in the mosque.

**Inner Meaning**

The esoteric meaning is this: it is incumbent upon the *Mustajīb* to present his mind, heart, and thoughts to the muballigh so that his salvation might be achieved. Therefore, the Prophet (upon him be peace) forbade the worshipper from remembering his Lord only during prayer while engaging in worldly thoughts that would corrupt his religious purpose. Allah says in His Book:[^cite-7]

The heedless one is he who turns away from love of the Imam (upon him be peace)—he who feigns outward obedience to the Imam yet harbors enmity in his heart, seeking to draw the people of falsehood to himself through ostentatious worship and to lead them into slander and doubt by appearing as an ascetic and worshipper in outward form.

Amīr al-Muʾminīn, Maulānā ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (upon him be peace), said: "Two have broken my back: the disobedient believer who commits wickedness and drives believers from faith through his evil deeds, and the disbeliever who, despite his disbelief, draws people to falsehood through his piety and devotion."

---

**Apparent Instruction**

Do not cross your arms before the Qibla.

**Inner Meaning**

That is, the believer should not resist the invitation of the Imam, disobey him, or cause discord within it, nor should he object to his commands. The characteristic of the sincere believer is that he should not dispute with the Imām al-Mahdī (upon him be peace) or quarrel over his station and rank, for such action comes only from one whose heart is diseased—whose intention is corrupt. Likewise, the believer who prays behind the Imam must not oppose the Imam's prayer but should follow his every movement.

---

**Apparent Instruction**

The worshipper should not turn his face to the right or to the left while praying.

**Inner Meaning**

This means that one who enters the Dawat al-Sharīf should remain obedient to his Imam and should not incline toward other religions.

---

**Apparent Instruction**

The worshipper should keep his gaze fixed upon the place of prostration.

**Inner Meaning**

This signifies that the believer should maintain connection with his benefactor and guide.

---

**Apparent Instruction**

When the Imam recites the Qur'an in congregation, the one who prays behind him should remain silent.

**Inner Meaning**

When a believer receives benefit from his guide, he should turn his ears to it and refrain from objections during his discourse.

---

**Apparent Instruction**

Upon leaving the mosque, the worshipper should place his left foot forward first.

**Inner Meaning**

This signifies that when a believer departs from the invitation of his muballigh, he should conceal his religion, his beliefs, and what he has heard therein from those unworthy of truth. Placing the left foot forward means descending from the highest point to the lowest point—that is, the muballigh exits from the presence of the *Ḥujjat* to move among the common people. Placing the right foot forward means ascending from the lowest to the highest. Since the mosque is like the sanctuary of the *Dāʿī*, when approaching the mosque and placing the right foot forward, one is performing hijra (migration) toward the *Dāʿī*, which is the noblest place.[^cite-8]
