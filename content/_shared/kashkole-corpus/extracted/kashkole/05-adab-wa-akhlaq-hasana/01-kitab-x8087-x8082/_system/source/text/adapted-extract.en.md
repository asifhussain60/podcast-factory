## Outwardism (Ẓāhir-Parastī) Is the Best Weapon (of Iblīs)

My dear brother Satan:

I have read your letter and was gratified to learn that you have succeeded in turning your prey away from the ideology of truth toward worldly preoccupations. However, I must caution you: if you believe you will ensnare him by cultivating a habit of contention with his elders without cause, you are profoundly mistaken.

Had your undertaking occurred some centuries past, your strategy might have borne fruit. The people of that age were unaccustomed to argumentation. They accepted quietly what their elders taught them concerning truth, for they held the faith that their elders possessed greater knowledge and spoke for their good. Had you taught them the art of debate in those days, our objectives would surely have succeeded. But the circumstances have transformed entirely.

In this modern age, we have inundated human minds with worldly sciences, invoking the demands of the new era as a counter to the call of truth. The greatest advantage of this intensification and proliferation of apparent knowledge is that from childhood onward, contradictory concepts and ideas dance perpetually in the human consciousness. Burdened by their abundance and their conflicting claims, he finds himself incapable of distinguishing which concepts to embrace and which to reject—which lead toward truth and which toward falsehood.[^cite-1] Through our faithful servants, we have rendered falsehood equal to truth and made temporal authorities like the righteous indistinguishable from one another.

Therefore, rather than engage your victim in disputes over right and wrong, occupy him with the demands of the old and the new. Keep him preoccupied with the notion that the commands of the Messenger of God (rasūl Allāh) were suited to his own time, yet are inapplicable to the modern era. Convince him that practicality now supersedes piety. Confound him with comparisons between the modern and the antiquated. Instead of persuading him that worldliness is the "true" belief, convince him that worldliness is the key to success in today's world. Display to him the beauty of creation, and remind him of those among our past loyalists who embraced oppression as the nobility of the strong. Tell him how those who followed the Messenger's commands have been humiliated and cast down in this very world! This is our finest weapon in these times, for it strikes at what we care most to obscure.

The difficulty with the habit of disputation is that our war shifts to the enemy's territory, where he too can argue and marshal evidence. You know well that often the arguments of truth override our own. Worse still, the gravest disadvantage of argumentation is that it awakens the intellect (al-ʿAql) of your prey. Once that intellect awakens, there is nothing we can accomplish.[^cite-2]

Even should you divert his thoughts through argumentative habit, his attention may eventually turn inward, away from outward concerns, as he develops the dangerous habit of reflection. Your goal must be to keep him fixed upon external situations and events. Teach him that outward pleasures are life's true purpose, but forbid him to inquire into real and eternal reality.

Always remember: humans are not pure creatures like yourself. In every human, no matter how aligned with us he may appear, there lies some hidden goodness. Let me recount to you the story of one of my own victims. I had labored upon him for many years, and he would visit the library daily to read. On one occasion, while reading, his thoughts drifted toward the Hereafter and toward God, and he resolved to pray. Immediately upon this turning, the enemy rushed to his aid and strengthened his resolve through exhortations to virtue. I watched twenty years of careful work dissolve before my eyes. Had I employed argumentation then, it might have served, but I am not foolish in such moments. I attacked his weakness—one I had held in my grasp from the beginning: his hunger. I placed hunger upon him and made him believe he had eaten nothing since morning. The enemy immediately raised his objection, insisting that this good impulse toward prayer was more important than food. I feared he might recall the hadith of the Messenger (s.a.w.a.) teaching that when good intentions arise, one should act upon them at once.

Before the enemy could advance further, I spoke to him thus: "Indeed, this is a noble and important thought. Yet is it not more important still to perform it with full devotion, not on an empty stomach? Eat and drink first, so that you may pray with full concentration and not have your thoughts scattered by hunger during the prayer. Refresh yourself through nourishment, so that you may worship well."

At this thought, my victim's countenance brightened, and within moments he was out the door. What followed was simple. On the way, I turned his attention to a child selling newspapers and said, "See how successful and grand you are, compared to this weak child, through your labor and wisdom!" Then a beautiful woman appeared, and I kindled lust within him. I told him, "If you earn more wealth, you can walk with pride among many such beautiful women!" By the time he reached the market, the thought of prayer had fled his mind entirely. Today that person dwells under our protection in perdition.

Do you understand my meaning and intention? Through the outward veil we have drawn over the human mind across centuries of labor, it has become impossible for man to conceive of the inner and the unseen. Use this to captivate him with the outward beauty of the world. Keep him ever distant from the true sciences (al-ʿulūm al-ḥaqīqa), for those sciences reveal the hidden truths that lie beyond sight and sense. Better still, convince him that through his intelligence he already knows all that is necessary, and that what his benefactors tell him is mere nonsense—worthless to his understanding. Just as I saved my prey from prayer through hunger, so deceive your followers through the excesses of time. Assure them that death is far distant and the Hereafter distant still. For now, let them focus upon the world; later there will be ample time for them to turn toward the Hereafter.

Remember that your purpose is to deceive and mislead mankind. Whatever assistance and counsel you require from us for the success of your aims shall be made available through our army of humans and jinn. Continue in your labor and keep us informed of your triumphs.

Your well-wisher,

Iblīs

## Imagination and Reality

My dear friend Satan:

We have read your letter and are deeply saddened to learn that one of your victims has joined the ranks of the enemy. We regard this as grave negligence on your part, and be assured you shall answer for it. Yet what is done is done—there is no cause for despair. The game remains in our hands. In ages past, many of our victims have entered the enemy's ranks, only for us to draw them back to our fold after a brief interval.

Even though your victim has accepted the invitation (daʿwa) of truth, he remains within our reach. I speak not of that invitation established in the eternal realm, which extends across all ages of the world—that invitation we cannot corrupt, for we cannot penetrate into those precincts. But fortunately for us, these ignorant people cannot perceive or feel this spiritual summons and are often unaware of its very existence.

Rather, I speak of the gathering that occurs in a structure of brick and mortar which they call a mosque. We can enter there and continue our work. Since your victim has recently turned toward religion, his first act will surely be to hasten to the mosque. Usually, such outward worshippers consider prayer five times daily as their sole obligation of worship. They are unaware that worship is incumbent upon them for all twenty-four hours; they are only required to attend prayer five times daily. We may exploit their unconsciousness fully.

Always remember one of my tried methods: Man is God's foolish creation. I have observed throughout centuries such contradiction between his words and deeds as exists in no other creature. He harbors a desire in his imaginative realm, yet in actual life he acts contrary to it, then wonders why his wish is unfulfilled and blames God for the failure of his own desire.

His condition resembles a child who grows listening to fairy tales of princes and believes real life will be equally tranquil and effortless. He imagines that just as Aladdin obtained all things by rubbing a lamp, so too shall he acquire every blessing, even Paradise, without pain or exertion. When this does not occur, he complains, disregards his own shortcomings, blames others, and attributes his sufferings and failures to them.

Or consider two lovers who construct a beautiful world in fantasy before marriage. After the marriage, when each perceives the other's flaws for the first time, they accuse one another of deception—as though the lover's duty were to dream and the beloved's to fulfill those dreams! Has such a thing ever been possible? And when it does not transpire—for it cannot—they blame each other for shattering their fantasies. The beloved for whom yesterday they were willing to die becomes today someone with whom they cannot endure a single day.

There are countless such astounding contradictions I could reveal to you in mankind. They wish to be pious yet speak falsehood. They dream of soaring through the heavens after liberation from worldly bondage, yet they bind heavy stones of desire and hope about their feet. They wish to become learned but refuse to dedicate the time and effort necessary for acquiring knowledge. They dream of following the Qur'an's teachings while remaining ignorant of the Qur'an's language. And most astonishing: each desires Paradise, yet none wishes to die![^cite-3] Is it not remarkable that God Almighty should expect loyalty and obedience from such an unfaithful and foolish creature? You are the just one—did I err when I refused to prostrate before this ignorant creation of God?

This is why I say: even should your victim be drawn to religion, there is nothing to fear. At present, he harbors only a vague image of piety in his mind, born from observing some elder or being influenced by his words. He wishes to be pious, yet he possesses no genuine willingness to undertake the arduous labor and sacrifices required for true piety. There exists a vast chasm between a delicate desire to attain something and the bone-breaking toil that achievement demands. What does a learned scholar have in common with an ascetic?

Therefore, when he enters the mosque to pray, you must accompany him and dominate his thoughts. Your task is to shatter the delicate glass of his dreams with the stone of reality—a task neither difficult nor complex. If the mosque is small and dilapidated, cultivate in him disgust at its squalor, narrowness, and stench. Disturb him with oppressive heat and the odor of sweat. If he has not eaten, torture him with hunger. If he is tired, let sleep beckon him; compare the softness of his bed at home to the heat of the mosque and its hard floor, whether from cold or heat. But simultaneously, blur and cloud all those feelings and mental stirrings in his consciousness. He may sense the weight and irritability of his nature, but let him find no good reason for them. If he weakens, the spirit of his religiosity will depart from him, and he will gladly return to your refuge. If not, his intellect will malfunction due to confusion of his nature, and he will become easy prey for your next assault.

Then direct your attention toward the mosque itself. There you will usually encounter two types of people. One comprises true worshippers and the pious; the other, outward Muslims who come to pray but do not even understand why they pray. You can hunt through both types.

First, focus upon the appearance of these worshippers. The pious will often be those with long beards. Frequent fasting will cause their eyes to hollow and their bellies to shrink. Night vigils and prayer will swell their eyes, and they will appear ill. You cannot find defect in their outward actions or inner states, so simply distort their external appearance and present it to your victim. Inculcate in his heart the belief that Islam is so rigid and severe that it burdens its followers until their condition deteriorates. Plant doubt: if God is merciful and compassionate, how could He place His servants in such pain? But keep hidden from him that these people undertake all this by their own will and desire to please God. Keep him ignorant of the fact that what he perceives as burden, they consider blessing and divine grace, for they know they shall receive eternal peace in return for this temporary effort.[^cite-4]

As I said, religiosity remains merely an imaginary picture in your victim's mind; his attention has not yet been drawn to the arduous labor necessary to manifest that picture. Simply place in his mind the thought: "From today onward, sweet nights, delicious food, beautiful garments, and all the luxuries of the world have become forbidden to me!" Make him feel the fatigue of the body during prayer while concealing from him the connection with God and the blessings that accompany it. Keep him mindful of the hunger and thirst of fasting, but distract him from the spiritual purification it brings.

I tell you with conviction: if you place your wager correctly, this person must by now be half-ensnared, thinking "Perhaps it is not given to me to become pious." Now it is your duty to transform this suspicion into certainty through the second type of people.

These second types are actually our own companions who habitually frequent the mosque for mere show or thoughtlessly. The hard work of our faithful produces no change in them—brushing their teeth each morning and evening requires the same effort as their prayer! They regard prayer as a daily routine, no different from eating and drinking. They do not comprehend prayer's meaning, and their purpose is not to attain piety through prayer. The greatest proof lies in this: they pray five times daily, yet they still lie, backbite, smoke, drink, persecute their neighbors, oppress orphans, speak ill and bitter words, and remain completely immersed in worldly pursuits. They conduct trade with God through their prayers and worship. The truth is that in their imaginary world, they do God a favor by praying. This is why, when calamity befalls them as a consequence of their own evil deeds, they blame God and complain: "What benefit is there in my praying and worshipping You when You are the one who harms me?" Their thinking is clear proof of our success!

Consider how delightful it will be for us when these people stand before God after death and learn that their worship throughout their lives was worthless—that in truth they have been worshipping us! On that day, they shall be handed over to Us, and We shall show them their dwelling place in Hell. What a joyous hour that will be for us!

But do not allow all this to reach your victim's mind. Rather, present the evil deeds of these outward worshippers as evidence against Islam itself and plant in his mind the question: "These liars, backbiters, drinkers of spirits, deceivers, and devourers of orphans' wealth—are they not all Muslims and believers? Mix the evil deeds of such people with Islam's teachings and confuse your prey. Ask him: 'Is this Islam? What benefit is there in such weak Islamic teachings and such prayers that cannot even prevent their followers from the smallest evils? Surely something is wrong with Islam. It is far better to be irreligious than to be a Muslim like these, who are inferior in character.' But do not let it occur to him that if a person fails to follow Islam's teachings while claiming to be Muslim, the fault lies with the person, not with Islam. If a player plays the game wrongly, does that condemn the game itself?"

You may wonder: cannot a person understand even such a simple thing? I tell you, brother, I do not understand it either! This is precisely why we enjoy such success in our work. There is no more foolish creature than man when he wishes not to understand. Rest assured that if you labor properly, he will not even consider this directly, your task will become far easier, and he shall return to you as swiftly as he entered the place of worship!

Bind fast to your memory this truth: man is an extremely lazy, indolent, and outward-gazing animal. He will toil only for worldly pleasures he can see and feel. He will turn his back upon God and His Messenger for five minutes of mortal, physical pleasure. It is our great fortune that the benefits of the Hereafter and its teachings are invisible and the eternal blessings of Paradise are hidden from our sight. To attain the Hereafter, he must sacrifice worldly pleasures and strangle his desires with his own hands—his soul will never consent to this. Make the world real for him, and keep religion a beautiful fantasy, and success shall be ours.

Your Lord,

Iblīs

## The Human Is an Animal That Takes Comfort (in Habits)

Dear Student:

We have received your request to reassign your prey. You write that despite all our admonitions, your victim inclines ever more toward piety and godliness. He has submitted his desires to the pleasure of God and labors diligently in this direction. Moreover, you believe you shall be unable to capture him and therefore request a reassignment.

We are both grieved and disappointed to read your petition. Perhaps you do not fully comprehend your own greatness and worth. To give up and grow weary is the attribute of a two-legged beast. We are not such creatures! They tire of their worship and despair of their efforts, yet We never grow weary of misleading and corrupting them.

Your letter makes clear that you have set upon your prey all the snares we outlined in our previous counsel, yet the ghost of religiosity has not descended from his head but has grown stronger. Rather than abandon the hunt, you should rejoice that it has become more interesting! There is a difference between hunting a lion and hunting a goat. Lion hunting is difficult, but it is also more thrilling, and when success comes, the pleasure is incomparable! Thank God that man does not understand that maintaining self-mastery while living in the world is more difficult than hunting a lion, though if he succeeds, his joy on the Last Day will be endless. The enemy ceaselessly endeavors to make him aware of this truth through knowledge, and our success always lies in concealing it.

Suppose your victim is prepared to labor to construct his religious character and turn away from the world—his soul may not yet be ready for the world to turn away from him and depart. This shall be your next assault. Turn all his family and friends against him, and observe what transpires!

You should encounter no particular difficulty in this task, for man is, after all, an animal, and every animal desires to be like those it belongs to. He becomes accustomed to the people around him and absorbs their choices, permissions, and intentions. As the relationship deepens, he becomes dependent upon the pleasures, gratifications, and compliments that emanate from it. But when he suddenly changes himself or breaks a bad habit, he becomes restless and strives with all his might to return to his former state. He does so not because his friend is good or evil, but to preserve his own sense of integrity. If he succeeds, well and good; if not, his friend becomes his enemy and stands against him. With every change in environment and attitude, he yearns like a fish without water, or like one deprived of intoxication—restless and desperate. Soon he will cross every threshold to reclaim that addiction anew.

Have you forgotten that the Messenger of God (s.a.w.a.) lived among the Quraysh for forty years and was called "the Truthful One" (al-Sādiq) and "the Trustworthy One" (al-Amīn)? Yet the moment he began preaching Islam and they feared his altered actions, these very people who had called him truthful and trustworthy became enemies of his life.

Now that your victim has been drawn to religion, his habits must have changed. He has become quieter, more patient, gentler, and more generous than before. He has abandoned coarse language, abuse, smoking, and many such harmful customs. But remember: his companions and friends are still familiar with his old attitudes and behaviors. When they meet your prey, encourage them through him to express those old habits. Of course, your victim will resist such habits—by reacting thus, you turn his friends against him. When they speak vulgarities, instead of laughing as before, he will remain silent or offer them good counsel. When they gossip about someone, he will avert his ears.

Each time your victim does this, his friends will be struck by his transformation. They will sense that their friend is no longer as he was. There follow only two possible outcomes: either his friends will change themselves to match his goodness, or they will change your prey to make him like themselves. This is the difference between envy and jealousy! The envious one strives to elevate himself through his labor to become like the one he envies, while the jealous one seeks to make the object of his jealousy fall from his rank and become like him. Since jealousy is the easier and more familiar path of the two, his friends shall surely become jealous.

Make his companions angry by convincing them that your victim now considers them despicable and unworthy, now that he is religious. Tell them his silence is a form of humiliation. It is the nature of the weak soul that it cannot tolerate humiliation, even if self-inflicted and imaginary. When he feels humiliated, he instinctively defends himself; the humiliation, whether real or invented, provokes him to seek revenge.

This gives you two advantages: first, they shall rise against your prey and aid you in your work; second, when they encounter others, they shall backbite him, mock him behind his back, and use various methods to defame him.

If this fails, they shall attempt to stop him while posing as well-wishers. They will counsel him to be moderate and recite hadiths of the Messenger (s.a.w.a.) concerning balanced good deeds. They will say to him: "You are indeed doing well in what you do, but we are concerned lest you become ill or exhausted and abandon your efforts. Therefore, be moderate in your practice, for God has commanded balance in both religion and worldly life." The true purpose of all their exhortations will be the same: that the striving your victim undertakes in religion should diminish and he should become like them.

The most amusing aspect is that they will do all this unconsciously. They will genuinely believe they are preaching goodness, and if your victim, being wise, does not heed their advice, their anger will kindle and flare, and they will turn away from him. How strange that these people, despite possessing reason, do not consider that a horse is never advised to run slowly in a race! God has made this world a race course for the Hereafter. It is only in their ignorance that we succeed, for our task is to stop or at least slow their pace by any means.

I laugh at man's intellect, especially when he speaks of establishing balance (i'tidāl) between religion and the world! Where is religion in his life that permits such balance? He considers only prayer and fasting to be religion. He recites a prayer whose meaning and interpretation he cannot even translate into a single sentence. If sincere, fasting is merely enduring hunger and thirst for God's sake; if hypocritical, fasting is an excuse to lose weight while performing a great service to God! He does not even pause to reflect: what benefit could God derive from his hunger and thirst? If he reads the Qur'an yet does not understand the meaning of a single verse, how shall he act upon it? Every deed, every goodness, every act of generosity, every movement, and every moment of peace is for display before the world and self-praise. He insists that what he does for people he does for God's sake, yet when he receives no reward from them, he complains a hundredfold. He does not even ask himself the slightest question: "If my action was truly for God's sake, why do I complain to the servant?" Will you truly abandon such a foolish man?

Do as We have commanded. When the pressure from his friends and companions intensifies upon your prey, he shall change himself to preserve his integrity and return to the right path—or so he believes. If this does not work, do not despair. We possess an even more powerful strategy, which We shall describe in the next letter. Remember: we have only until his death to seduce him. Therefore, no negligence shall be tolerated from you.

Your chieftain,

Iblīs

## Plain and Straight Speech (a Maxim)

My dear disciple Satan:

How delighted I am to learn of your victim's deteriorating relationship with his wife! But do not merely dwell upon the current chaos; hollow out the crumbling structure so thoroughly that no option remains but divorce. Remember! The more we attempt to harm this inferior creature, the more the enemy labors to protect and aid him. We must ensure he cannot resolve the misunderstandings growing between them, lest your months of effort be rendered void.

You have grasped a tremendous quality in your prey which is called pride (ghurūr). He believes himself superior and greater than his wife and others in every respect. In God's sight, this single trait consumes his years of worship and exertion like fire upon dry wood! I myself was cast out of Paradise for this very reason, and if a creature as base as myself does not merit Paradise, how could I permit these beasts to enter it? Fan the spark of his arrogance until it becomes flame and consumes this relationship to ash, so that both shall be expelled from their marital paradise.

Direct your prey's attention to his own virtues and his wife's weaknesses. Strive to render even her smallest fault a grave sin, while treating his greatest error as trivial. In his eyes, all he does is good; all she does is foolish. This frame of mind shall be the source of your victory.

Your victim encounters, unconsciously, many women in his daily life—through books and films—whose qualities he finds lacking in his wife and wishes she possessed. Keep him constantly vigilant for this "ideal" wife. Day by day, let him compare her to this paragon: beautiful, well-mannered, attractive, sweet-tongued, and wise—never to his original, boring, immoral, sharp-tongued, and foolish wife. Make him believe he has been wronged and deceived.

Let him not be distracted by the fifty things his wife does throughout each day to please him; but remember! This great idol of ingratitude must be placed in both their hearts. For the clap requires two hands! Their mutual ingratitude will keep them distant, and soon they shall forget all that they owe each other. There will be no imaginary companion, and no living one shall remain.[^cite-5]

I myself have controlled some of my victims so completely that I could persuade them to humiliate, even beat and torment their wives and children whenever I wished, despite their prayer five times daily in devotion!

Let me also share another delightful recipe. When two people live together long, they become irritated by certain habits of each other. Though these mannerisms are minor, they are deeply displeasing. Unnecessary words, untimely laughter, raised eyebrows—small gestures cause great pain. Observe which of your victim's wife's actions provoke him, exaggerate them, and present them to him. More than this, place in his heart the thought that she does all this intentionally, merely to vex and hurt him. But never let him reflect that he himself possesses many habits and behaviors displeasing to his wife that cause her pain.

In civilized households, hatred and anger are often expressed through sentences that, when written, appear trivial and innocent. Yet when the same sentences are uttered in a certain voice, style, or tone, they are no less devastating than a humiliating blow. You need only keep wounding both their hearts with such words.

If you wish, add more enjoyment to the game by instilling a duality in your victim's judgment. Bring him to a state where, whenever he speaks something bitter or harsh to his wife, he demands every sentence of hers be evaluated by its outward words and surface meaning. Yet when his wife speaks, he examines every word with utmost minuteness and sensitivity, interpreting each in light of dubious intent. His wife should adopt the identical attitude toward him. Thus, after every quarrel, both shall be convinced that fault lies only with the other, and no form of improvement shall ever occur. Once this habit is firmly established, a particularly pleasant situation shall arise: both will speak unconsciously with the intention to hurt each other, yet when the latter grows angry, both shall be astonished and disappointed at his low self-esteem and poor reasoning! This wonder and regret shall drive them further apart. Remember: it is a special grace of blessings that they are truly felt only after they are taken away.

As I said, man is oblivious to all simple and straightforward things! Because of this, our work becomes far easier. Take full advantage of this weakness and keep me informed of your successes.

Your chieftain,

Iblīs
