### Second Argument (Mill and Its Motion)

Self is a separate thing and its cause is a separate thing. It is our observation that the mill is in motion and it moves. Although the mill is capable of moving, this motion is not its own. If her movement was spontaneous and included in her essence, she would always be in motion. But we see that the mill is both in motion and stationary, and that these two states of motion and rest cannot arise from its essence, and when they cannot arise from the essence, it is found that the motion of the mill cannot exist unless there is a person who moves it. It has been proved that nothing exists from its own self, but from an external force or essence. Once we have accepted this, we also have to accept that the creation and movement of this universe is not possible by itself, and therefore its ⟪ar:صانع⟫existence must exist.

### Fifth Argument (Cause and Effect)

Everything that accepts an effect and changes its state by accepting that effect is an effect. That is, the change in the state of the thing cannot be brought about by itself unless there is a cause affecting it. In the same way, everything that does not accept the effect and remains in its original state cannot be invalid. We see that there are⟪ar:جرمانی، افلاک، امہات، نبات، معدن، حیوان⟫ different worlds in the universe. Not all of them remain in one state. Planets are always moving from one place to another. The day keeps changing into night and night into day. The seasons keep changing. The occurrence of changes in all these conditions indicates that all these effects are acceptable and causal. When there are influence-takers, there must be someone ⟪ar:صانع⟫who is influential.

### Seventh Argument (Senses and Sensations)

The senses can perceive only those things whose elements are in accordance with their powers and inferior in rank to them. That is, the power of seeing can only see what is visible to the eye. The power of smell will work for anything that has a smell. If an object is higher in rank than them ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫(because of its subtlety), it is not possible for these senses to perceive it. This proves that this world is inferior to the senses. The visibility of an object is the effect of the power of seeing, which affects the visible object. That is, we perceive things through the effects of these senses. That is ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫, the sensible objects are useless without the sensible objects and the sensible objects are useless without ⟪ar:حواس خمسۃ⟫the sensible objects. The senses exert an effect, the objects accept the effect which in turn makes the object felt. ### Eighth Argument

Attributes of different parts and their classification

There are different parts of this universe and each part has a few attributes that are specific to it. For example, lightness, darkness, lightness, heaviness, etc. If this world were eternal, we wouldn't see the hierarchy in the parts that we see. For example, sunlight is its special attribute, the moisture of the water and its flow are meant only for water. Similarly, the heat of the fire, the lightness of the air, the burdensome nature of the earth are all their special characteristics. When these attributes are present in these parts, it is obvious that there must be a cause that produces these attributes in them which is creating these characteristics in the parts from which they are being classified differently. And when there is a cause, the agent that produces that cause must also be present. If there was no cause causing this cause (which gave rise to the special properties of all these parts), then all the parts would be the same. All bright or all oppressed. All were heavy or all light. It just turned out that there was someone ⟪ar:صانع⟫in this universe.
