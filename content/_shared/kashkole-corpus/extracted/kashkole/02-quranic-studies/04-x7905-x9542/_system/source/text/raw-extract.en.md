How the Quran was compiled

Arab culture necessitated these people to have a phenomenal memory. If they could not memorize poems or stories perfectly, they could not be a storyteller. And this is why Quran is primarily an oral tradition and not a written tradition. As the chapters and verses were being revealed on Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam), those blocks of revelation were being written down in his presence on gazelle and camel leather. But the are not being compiled in any order.

In 632 Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) died. At that point many of the companions had memorized the Quran perfectly. ابی ابن کعب  was considered the best Haafiz among the companions. In 633 the battle of Yamama was fought against people in Najd. One of them had declared himself to be a prophet. In that battle many of the Huffaz were killed.

At that time the need of compiling the Quran in a book form was realized. But since the messenger himself never put revelation in the form of a book, there was a great amount of concern among the companions. They didn’t want to introduce something that the messenger himself did not do.

Once this need is realized, Zain Ibn Saabit (زید ابن ثابت) was given the resonsibility to compile the Quran. The criteria for this compilation was that every piece of Quran that was written in the life of the prophet in front of him, be brought forward along with 2 witnesses. This is how the entire Quran was put together.

They found 2 witnesses for every single piece of revelation except the last 2 verses of Surah Tawbah. At that time Khuzaima was used as a witness because Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) had said during his life, that the witness of khuzaima was equivalent to two witnesses. They compiled all these blocks of revelation into a MUSHAF or a SAHEEFA.

In this version of Quran, there were no dots or اعراب  on the letters. Over time this resulted in people reciting the Quran in different ways, because there was no standardized writing. So for example کتب  could be pronounced کَتَبَ  or کِتَبٌ. There were no diacritical marks on the letters. Quraish would pronounce مومن, where as Bani Tameem would pronounce مؤمن .

Usman ordered a standardization of the MASHAF. Zain and 3 other companions, including Maulana Ali took on the responsibility of adding the dots and Eraab on the entire Quran. This standardized version of the Quran was then sent over to all different cities and Usman commanded all other copies to be burnt.

But even then, much like today, written Quran is NOT the primary source in which Quran is protected. It is protected through oral transmissions. Written Quran is only used as a crutch in the absence of oral transmission. Even today when a new copy of Quran is printed, it is sent to the HUFFAZ for verification. It is not checked against other existing copies of the Quran.

For all previous books THEY themselves took on the responsibility of protecting the book. But because Rasul Allah (Salallahu alayhi wa aalihee wa sallam) was the last prophet, and there was no one coming after him who could point out the mistakes in the book (similar to previous nations), Allah took it upon himself to protect this book.
