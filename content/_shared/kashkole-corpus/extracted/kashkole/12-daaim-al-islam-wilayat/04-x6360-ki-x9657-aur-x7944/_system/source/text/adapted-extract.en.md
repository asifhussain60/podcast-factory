## Special Prophethood and General Prophethood

Prophethood (risāla, the divine commission) is of two kinds: one general, the other special. Each addresses a distinct dimension of the human creature, and only when the two are joined does the soul move toward its true end.

### The General Commission: the Inner Faculty

The general commission is at once physical and rational. When Allah created the ⟪ar:صورۃ انسانیۃ⟫, He fashioned within it every organ required for its sustenance. Through these organs the human soul satisfies its bodily needs and discharges the functions of animal life. Yet He set within the human being, unlike the brute, another faculty and substance: the intellect (ʿaql). By this intellect a person distinguishes the wholesome from the corrupt, the true from the false. By this intellect he grasps ⟪ar:أمرو نواھی⟫ — commands and prohibitions — and obeys the directives of parents, teachers, and mentors. This intellect is his first messenger.[^cite-1]

### The Special Commission: the External Intellect

The Messenger of Allah, may peace be upon him and his family, is the true guide whom Allah has vested with knowledge of the unseen and who directs the way to the Hereafter for the salvation of the soul. Such are the prophets of every age, the awṣiyāʾ (legatees), and the Imams across the cycles. The general prophethood within the self is the prerequisite for receiving the special prophethood. One who lacks the inward commission — the intellect — is unfit to receive the outward, which is why ⟪ar:شریعت محمدی علیہ السلام⟫ does not bind the insane nor the child not yet of age.

### The Joining of the First Commission with the Second

By his innate and natural intellect a person comprehends his worldly gains and losses and knows how to maintain his bodily life. He eats and drinks, fills his belly, and provides himself with comforts. In that state he has no commerce with the Hereafter, no thought of recompense or reward. Following the second messenger — the appointed guide — rectifies his afterlife. He becomes able to perceive his final destination and to weigh his deeds by reward and requital. The second messenger restrains the first from evil and adorns the soul with knowledge. **So long as the first messenger is not joined to the second, the human being is not bound to the ordinances of the sharīʿa, and is thereby cut off from the reward.**

## Why the Messengers Are Necessary

The line of prophets has continued since the time of ⟪ar:حضرت آدم علیہ السلام⟫ and will endure as long as a single descendant remains. Every human body subsists and is governed by the inward faculty until, through childhood, it crosses the threshold of awareness; this primal endowment, ⟪ar:رسالت اولہ (عقل)⟫, gives the person his sense of harm and benefit and teaches him to discern and to distinguish.

The one in whom this intellect resides is therefore required to reflect, to discern, to picture his end and the world to come; to remain steadfast, faithful, and dignified; to guard the secrets of the daʿwa (the summons); to seek knowledge and the proper adab (refined conduct); to embrace ⟪ar:ورع⟫ (scrupulous abstention) and ⟪ar:زہد⟫ (renunciation of the world); to discharge his trusts; to keep silent where silence is fitting; and to labour with steady purpose. He is required also to shun the base actions that contradict these virtues.

The intellect on its own, however, cannot encompass these matters. It is impossible for the person to grasp them by his unaided faculty. He therefore stands in need of a guide — one who can disclose to him with what Allah is pleased and with what He is displeased. The guide who unfolds these matters is the second messenger whom Allah has sent and chosen for the perfecting of His servants' destiny. By special aptitudes and pure intentions Allah has distinguished him from the multitude, has rendered him fit to receive His revelation, and has confirmed His support upon him.[^cite-2]

The human being stands between two conditions. On one side he is bound to the luminous, spiritual intellect; on the other he is bound to the lower world, dark and oppressive. Within him lies an evil power called ⟪ar:قوت شھوانیۃ و غضبیۃ⟫. The demand of the body is that he cannot subsist without food nor without the satisfaction of appetite. If he inclines wholly toward the ⟪ar:نفس شہوانیۃ غضبیۃ⟫ — until eating, drinking, and indulgence become, as with cattle, the very purpose of his living — then the appetitive soul overpowers the intellect, and he is engulfed by the world. He becomes indifferent to religion and immersed in the gratification of his desires. The human creature who is ⟪ar:اشرف المخلوقات⟫ (the noblest of beings) sinks beneath the four-footed and becomes ⟪ar:أَشَرُّ المَخلُوقَات⟫ (the basest of beings). He falls so far that he is wholly stripped of every excellent human function.

In such a case, divine wisdom required that the first messenger — the intellect — be furnished with a guide who would inform him that beyond the bodily world there exists another, unsoiled by its impurities. The guide would instruct him, give him discipline and adab, and impose upon him as a teacher does all that is needful for the welfare and salvation of the intellect. He would make him aware of ⟪ar:أمر ونواھی⟫ — of what is licit and what is prohibited — would hold him strictly to the command of the sharīʿa, and would, by warnings of ⟪ar:نواھی⟫, restrain him from what is forbidden, deterring him from the unlawful through the weight of severe penalty, so that the destiny of this rational creature would be set right and that, having been created ⟪ar:احسن تقویم⟫ (in the finest stature), he would not descend to ⟪ar:اسفل السافلین⟫ (the lowest of the low). There is, besides this dwelling, another house for the human being, of which the Prophet, may peace be upon him and his family, declared:

> ⟪quran 6:48⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَا نُرْسِلُ ٱلْمُرْسَلِينَ إِلَّا مُبَشِّرِينَ وَمُنذِرِينَ ۖ فَمَنْ آمَنَ وَأَصْلَحَ فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We send not the Messengers but as givers of glad tidings and as warners.&nbsp;So whosoever believes and does righteous good deeds, upon such shall come no fear, nor shall they grieve.*
> We do not send the apostles except to give good news and warn. Whoever believes, and reforms, there shall be no fear for them, nor shall they grieve.
> — **Quran 6:48 (inline widget)**

That is, the messengers bear the glad tidings of the world to come and warn the nations of the chastisement of the fire. Annunciation and warning attach to the reward or the punishment of an action, and both bear upon the Hereafter.

**Taʾwīl.** The glad tidings of the Messenger of Allah, may peace be upon him and his family, were in truth the glad tidings of ʿAlī to the believers, and his warning was the warning to the unbelievers and the hypocrites against ʿAlī, for on the Day of Resurrection it is ʿAlī who shall distinguish truth from falsehood.[^cite-3]

## Why the Awṣiyāʾ Are Necessary

Once the truth and legitimacy of the first and second messengers stand clear, a further question arises: what is the need for the establishment of the awṣiyāʾ? On this point the common people object: when we have accepted the Messenger of Allah and possess the commands of Allah in the form of the Qurʾān, what need have we of a waṣī? We can affirm the commands of the Qurʾān, of Allah, and of the Last Day without need of any legatee. To them Allah responds:

> ⟪quran 2:8⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَقُولُ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَبِٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ وَمَا هُم بِمُؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫
> *And of mankind, there are some (hypocrites) who say: "We believe in Allah and the Last Day" while in fact they believe not.*
> There are some who say, "We believe in Allah and the Last Day," but they are not believers.
> — **Quran 2:8 (inline widget)**

Allah pronounces that they affirm neither of these realities, since they pretend belief in the divine unity by leaning on their unaided intellect and understanding. They claim that the knowledge of God is implanted in human nature itself. Such persons disregard the plain fact that even the human capacity to speak is a natural endowment — yet no human child acquires speech without instruction from parents or teachers. The evidence is clear in the case of a child raised among animals in the forest: he learns to imitate the cries of animals, but cannot, with articulate words, convey what lies ⟪ar:مافی الضمیر⟫ — what lies in the heart — as humans do. If, then, a person cannot stand upright in so natural a matter without a teacher, how shall he stand without a guide to Allah?[^cite-4]

It is thereby clear that without the Messenger of Allah, knowledge of Allah cannot be attained. By the same necessity, in order to grasp the affairs of religion, its foundations, and the secrets of the Qurʾān, there must be a guide and director who serves, like the Messenger, as the intermediary between Allah and His servants. That guide is the waṣī of the Messenger, who succeeds him after his life-span. He unfolds the meanings of the Messenger's utterances and interprets the inward sense of what he has stated. The Messenger conveys only the commands of Allah — that you should do this and abstain from that. The waṣī unfolds the meaning and the secrets behind the legal command: why the sharīʿa enjoins it, and what its benefit shall be in the Hereafter. In short, the Messenger expounds the words; the waṣī expounds the meaning of those words, which has always been the true purpose of the words themselves.

All the foundations of inward conviction begin with the knowledge of the divine unity (tawḥīd). It is knowledge purified of analogy: stripping the Creator of every created attribute and absolving Him of the innovations of the innovators — both those who are spiritual and those who are not formed of water and clay — and purifying Him of such attributes as belong to the corporeal person. We are to recognise that whatever picture arises in our minds is not Allah; to confess that even our most subtle concepts are themselves created, originated and contingent as we are; and to affirm that the Creator who transcends our imagination is the very originator of these spiritual and corporeal limits and bears no relation to them. This is the principle of the faith by whose presence religion stands and is sustained, and by whose decline religion is annulled and overthrown. For the elucidation and unfolding of all such matters, the Messenger appoints his waṣī.

If the Book of Allah, the Qurʾān, could expound its own meaning, then the speech of Allah would be without purpose.[^cite-5]

> ⟪quran 4:83⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا جَآءَهُمْ أَمْرٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْأَمْنِ أَوِ ٱلْخَوْفِ أَذَاعُوا۟ بِهِۦ ۖ وَلَوْ رَدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ وَإِلَىٰٓ أُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنْهُمْ لَعَلِمَهُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَنۢبِطُونَهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ وَلَوْلَا فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَتُهُۥ لَٱتَّبَعْتُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *When there comes to them some matter touching (public) safety or fear, they make it known (among the people), if only they had referred it to the Messenger or to those charged with authority among them, the proper investigators would have understood it from them (directly).&nbsp;Had it not been for the Grace and Mercy of Allah upon you,  you would have followed Shaitan (Satan), save a few of you.*
> And when there comes to them some news touching about security or fear, they broadcast it; and if they had relayed it to the Messenger and to those in authority among them, those among them who are investigators would have investigated it; and had it not been for the grace of Allah upon you and His mercy, you would have followed Satan, except a few.
> — **Quran 4:83 (inline widget)**

Were the claim ⟪ar:’حسبنا کتاب اللہ‘⟫ — "the Book of Allah suffices us" — sound, ʿUmar would not have leaned upon ʿAlī for every adjudication and ruling: ⟪ar:لو لا علی لھلک عمر⟫. Those who usurp the stations of the Imams on the basis of outward acts and ostentatious piety resemble a man who presents himself as wealthy while being inwardly destitute; or one who, being blind, calls himself sighted. Such a person deceives only himself, as Allah declares:

> ⟪quran 2:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يُخَدِعُونَ ٱللَّهَ وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَمَا يَخْدَعُونَ إِلَّآ أَنفُسَهُمْ وَمَا يَشْعُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *They (think to) deceive Allah and those who believe, while they only deceive themselves, and perceive (it) not!*
> seek to deceive Allah and those who believe, but they deceive themselves, and they do not perceive.
> — **Quran 2:9 (inline widget)**

The expression ⟪ar:’الیوم الاخر‘⟫ — "the Last Day" — together with ⟪ar:ذات قائم القیامۃ علی ذکرہ السلام⟫, the Person of the Qāʾim of the Resurrection upon whose remembrance be peace, refers to the completion of the days of Allah, concerning which it is said:

> ⟪quran 45:14⟫
> ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ يَغْفِرُوا۟ لِلَّذِينَ لَا يَرْجُونَ أَيَّامَ ٱللَّهِ لِيَجْزِىَ قَوْمًۢا بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫
> *Say (O Muhammad PBUH) to the believers to forgive those who (harm them and) hope not for the Days of Allah (i.e. His Recompense), that He may recompense people according to what they have earned (i.e. to punish these disbelievers, who harm the believers).*
> Say to those who believe that forgive those who do not expect the days of Allah, so that He may recompense a people for what they used to earn.
> — **Quran 45:14 (inline widget)**

In this verse Allah declares that He shall recompense people for their works. If the ⟪ar:’أیام اللہ‘⟫ — the days of Allah — were merely the lifeless days and nights of the calendar, how could days bear reward or punishment? This shows that "days" here carries a distinct meaning. The expression denotes the entire cycle: ⟪ar:قائم علی ذکرہ السلام⟫ stands at the close of the days, while the days themselves correspond to the ⟪ar:’انبیاء کرام‘⟫, the noble prophets. In their hands lies first the Day of Standing and then the Day of Reckoning. First religion is stirred to its appointed motion; then the audit and the reckoning unfold.[^cite-6]

## Why Connection with the Second Messenger Proves Difficult

Only the one who accepts the law of the ⟪ar:رسول اول⟫ — the intellect — and follows the commandments of the ⟪ar:رسول ثانی⟫ can profit from the light of the intellect. The lower self, by and large, recoils from the law of the ⟪ar:رسول علیہ السلام⟫, for it is habituated to following the ease and craving of the appetitive soul, and it resists being held back from whatever appears agreeable to it: this is ⟪ar:نفس امارۃ بالسوء⟫ (the soul that incites to evil).

The ⟪ar:رسالت ثانی⟫ may be likened to fire that lights the world and warms the air. Fire is first concealed within wood, and only when the human being who possesses it kindles it and lets it blaze does he derive its benefit. So too the ⟪ar:رسول علیہ السلام⟫ kindle and ignite the natural intellect by the teachings and ordinances of their sacred law. Without the messengers, the human being cannot of himself attain the light of guidance. The community is, on the whole, a single body, and the Messenger is the heart of that body; his companions are the senses and the instruments. The inward knowledge of the Messenger, may peace be upon him and his family, is the ⟪ar:’روح‘⟫ — the spirit — of that body, while the outward knowledge that he conveys is its limbs, sinews, and joints, which obey his command and render him worthy of salvation in the Hereafter.

## Spiritual Parents and Obedience to Them

God Almighty has fashioned the subtle substance of the human soul upon the dense substance of the body, so that knowledge may be composed from both ⟪ar:محسوسات⟫ (maḥsūsāt, sensibles) and ⟪ar:معقولات⟫ (maʿqūlāt, intelligibles). The body of the human being is that which is seen and felt; the soul is that which is neither seen nor felt. Yet neither of these two homologous realities can be brought to maturity without the physical and spiritual parents of the human being.[^cite-7]

### Physical and Spiritual Parents

If the birth of the body is possible only through bodily parents and through bodily pleasures, then it follows by clear reason that the human being will require spiritual parents for his spiritual perfection as well. The physical father is the giver, the active principle, and the physical mother is the receiver. In just the same way, the spiritual father is the giver of the soul, just as the physical father is the giver of the body; and the spiritual mother is the receiver of the soul, just as the physical mother is the receiver of bodily generation. These religious fathers, who stand in the place of father to the soul of the believer, are ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و سلم⟫; and the religious mothers, who stand in the place of mother to the soul of the believer, are his successor, ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫.

The seed of the father is the figure for ⟪ar:تنزیل⟫ (tanzīl, the descended revelation) in the spiritual creation of the believer, and the seed of the Prophet's successor is the figure for ⟪ar:تاویل⟫ (taʾwīl, esoteric exegesis) in the same creation. Through the union of these two spiritual seeds a being is fashioned for the spiritual world, just as the union of the two bodily seeds fashions a being for the physical world. This truth is borne out by the saying of the Messenger of God: ⟪ar-quote:اَنَا وَاَنْتَ یَا عَلِیٌّ اَبُوْ وَاُمُّ الْمُؤمِنِینَ⟫ — "I and you, O ʿAlī, are the father and mother of the believers." It is confirmed, too, by the word of God:[^cite-8]

> ⟪quran 33:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلنَّبِىُّ أَوْلَىٰ بِٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ مِنْ أَنفُسِهِمْ ۖ وَأَزْوَجُهُۥٓ أُمَّهَتُهُمْ ۗ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَرْحَامِ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلَىٰ بِبَعْضٍۢ فِى كِتَبِ ٱللَّهِ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ إِلَّآ أَن تَفْعَلُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآئِكُم مَّعْرُوفًۭا ۚ كَانَ ذَلِكَ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ مَسْطُورًۭا ‎⟫
> *The Prophet is closer to the believers than their ownselves, and his wives are their (believers') mothers (as regards respect and marriage). And blood relations among each other have closer personal ties in the Decree of Allah (regarding inheritance) than (the brotherhood of) the believers and the Muhajirun (emigrants from Makkah, etc.), except that you do kindness to those brothers (when the Prophet PBUH joined them in brotherhood ties). This has been written in the (Allah's Book of Divine) Decrees (Al&shy;Lauh Al&shy;Mahfuz).*
> The Prophet has a greater right over the believers than they have over themselves, and his wives are as if they were their mothers; and his kindred have a closer claim upon one another, in the Book of God, than the believers and the emigrants — save that you may treat your friends with kindness. This has been inscribed in the Book.
> — **Quran 33:6 (inline widget)**

### Heaven and Earth

We know from taʾwīl that the wife of every prophet, in this hierarchic register, is his successor (waṣī). And when the Qurʾān declares the successor of the Prophet to be the mother of the believers, this necessarily implies that the prophets are the fathers of the believers. Thus ⟪ar:رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ و سلم⟫ said: ⟪ar-quote:اَلاَرضُ اُمُّکُمْ وَھِیَ بِکُمْ بَرَّۃٌ⟫ — "The earth is your mother, and she is dutiful to you." In this saying the Prophet calls the waṣī "earth," for it has already been established that the waṣī is the mother of the believers — that is, ⟪ar:اوصیاء علیہم السلام⟫. The heaven waters the earth through rain and the light of the stars; the earth receives this water and nurtures minerals, plants, and animals. Thus the Qurʾān declares:

> ⟪quran 22:5⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ إِن كُنتُمْ فِى رَيْبٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْبَعْثِ فَإِنَّا خَلَقْنَكُم مِّن تُرَابٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن نُّطْفَةٍۢ ثُمَّ مِنْ عَلَقَةٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن مُّضْغَةٍۢ مُّخَلَّقَةٍۢ وَغَيْرِ مُخَلَّقَةٍۢ لِّنُبَيِّنَ لَكُمْ ۚ وَنُقِرُّ فِى ٱلْأَرْحَامِ مَا نَشَآءُ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّۭى ثُمَّ نُخْرِجُكُمْ طِفْلًۭا ثُمَّ لِتَبْلُغُوٓا۟ أَشُدَّكُمْ ۖ وَمِنكُم مَّن يُتَوَفَّىٰ وَمِنكُم مَّن يُرَدُّ إِلَىٰٓ أَرْذَلِ ٱلْعُمُرِ لِكَيْلَا يَعْلَمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ عِلْمٍۢ شَيْـًۭٔا ۚ وَتَرَى ٱلْأَرْضَ هَامِدَةًۭ فَإِذَآ أَنزَلْنَا عَلَيْهَا ٱلْمَآءَ ٱهْتَزَّتْ وَرَبَتْ وَأَنۢبَتَتْ مِن كُلِّ زَوْجٍۭ بَهِيجٍۢ ‎⟫
> *O mankind! If you are in doubt about the Resurrection, then verily! We have created you (i.e. Adam) from dust, then from a Nutfah (mixed drops of male and female sexual discharge i.e. offspring of Adam), then from a clot (a piece of thick coagulated blood) then from a little lump of flesh, some formed and some unformed (miscarriage), that We may make (it) clear to you (i.e. to show you Our Power and Ability to do what We will). And We cause whom We will to remain in the wombs for an appointed term, then We bring you out as infants, then (give you growth) that you may reach your age of full strength. And among you there is he who dies (young), and among you there is he who is brought back to the miserable old age, so that he knows nothing after having known. And you see the earth barren, but when We send down water (rain) on it, it is stirred (to life), it swells and puts forth every lovely kind (of growth).*
> O mankind, if you are in doubt about the Resurrection — We created you from dust, then from a drop, then from a clot, then from a lump of flesh, formed and unformed, that We might make Our power clear to you. We cause to abide in the wombs whom We will until a stated term; then We bring you forth as infants that you may reach your full strength. Some of you die young, and others are returned to a feeble old age, so that after having known they know nothing. You see the earth lifeless, yet when We send down water upon it, it stirs and swells, and brings forth every kind of joyous growth.
> — **Quran 22:5 (inline widget)**

That is: the survival and continuance of the Nāṭiq (the speaking prophet) hold the rank of heaven and father in ⟪ar:تنزیل⟫, while the Asās (the foundation, the prophet's waṣī) holds the rank of earth and mother in ⟪ar:تاویل⟫. So too is the case with the descent of rain. From the earth grow all manner of vegetation. The Nāṭiq summons the whole world to outward action, and the Asās discloses to all the truth and inner sense of that outward action — through which the human being may be spiritually nourished. The earth can bring forth nothing until the heaven sends down water upon it, and the heaven cannot produce any pair without the earth. Thus the human soul is nurtured through both ⟪ar:تنزیل⟫ and ⟪ar:تاویل⟫. God says in another place:

> ⟪quran 43:11⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۢ بِقَدَرٍۢ فَأَنشَرْنَا بِهِۦ بَلْدَةًۭ مَّيْتًۭا ۚ كَذَلِكَ تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫
> *And Who sends down water (rain) from the sky in due measure. Then We revive a dead land therewith, and even so you will be brought forth (from the dead),*
> And He who sent down water from the sky in due measure, by which We revived a lifeless land — even thus shall you be brought forth.
> — **Quran 43:11 (inline widget)**

### The Body and Soul of the Sharīʿa

The body is the figure of ⟪ar:تنزیل⟫, and the soul is the figure of ⟪ar:تاویل⟫; bodily death comes when the soul is separated from the body. The outward form of the sharīʿa stands in the rank of bodies, and taʾwīl stands in the rank of the soul. If, therefore, the sharīʿa is observed only in its outward aspect, it is like a dead body, for it has no soul; and if there is only taʾwīl without practice, it is as a soul without a body, which can accomplish nothing. Unless ⟪ar:تنزیل اور تاویل،⟫ are joined together, spiritual delights cannot be obtained — just as bodily delights cannot be obtained unless soul and body are joined together.[^cite-9]

### The Obligation of the Imām

Once it has been established that nothing in the physical world is born without the conjunction of heaven and earth, and that no human birth is possible without physical parents, it follows that in every age the believers must likewise have spiritual parents. The people need only to recognise these spiritual parents, lest they become spiritual orphans. Whoever knows the Imām is obliged to obey the Imām while he is in his presence; and if he is not in the Imām's presence, then he is obliged to obey the one whom the Imām has established over the island (jazīra) in which the believer resides. The Qurʾān confirms this:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority. (And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day. That is better and more suitable for final determination.*
> O you who believe, obey God and obey the Messenger and those vested with authority among you. Should you disagree about anything, refer it back to God and the Messenger, if you believe in God and the Last Day. That is best, and fairest in interpretation.
> — **Quran 4:59 (inline widget)**

If the Imāms were necessary only for the affairs of the world and not for religion, then religion itself would be empty of purpose — and God is far too exalted to render void what His beloved prophets endured such hardship to propagate, religion being the noblest of all things. And if the Imām were necessary only for religion and not for the world, the order of the world would have collapsed; nor could God plausibly entrust the Imām with the noblest matter (religion) yet bar him from the lesser (the world). It is therefore established that the Imām is necessary both for religion and for the world.

## The Proof of the Imām's Existence

By ⟪ar:عنایت الٰہیۃ⟫ (ʿināya ilāhiyya, divine providence), the human being received a portion that no other animal received: the innate intellect (ʿaql). And when God bestowed intellect upon the human being, it became necessary that He also send a teacher for this sacred gift, so that the natural intellect might be nurtured into its true perfection. When God gave to the animal a soul that eats and drinks, He set in motion the elements, the stars, and the heavens to produce vegetation, so that the animal's nourishment might come without ceasing and its body be sustained. It does not belong to the wisdom and justice of the All-Wise that He create a being in need, then fail to create that which fulfils its need.[^cite-10]

When God grants this innate intellect to a single species alone — namely, the human being — it is contrary to His munificence that the sustenance of this intellect, which is knowledge, should be granted save through One Being. Were this knowledge an outward knowledge, then all people could acquire it by their own labour and pursuit. But because this is an inner knowledge (ʿilm bāṭin), bestowed upon a single person, it was likewise necessary that this one person should hold the office of being its teacher to all others. God says:

> ⟪quran 7:63⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَوَعَجِبْتُمْ أَن جَآءَكُمْ ذِكْرٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ عَلَىٰ رَجُلٍۢ مِّنكُمْ لِيُنذِرَكُمْ وَلِتَتَّقُوا۟ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تُرْحَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *"Do you wonder that there has come to you a Reminder from your Lord through a man from amongst you, that he may warn you, so that you may fear Allah and that you may receive (His) Mercy?"*
> Do you marvel that a reminder has come to you from your Lord through a man from among yourselves, that he may warn you, that you may guard yourselves, and that mercy may be shown to you?
> — **Quran 7:63 (inline widget)**

In this verse, God uses the singular ⟪ar:رجل⟫ ("a man") and not the plural ⟪ar:رجال⟫ ("men"). This shows that the office of admonishment and remembrance (dhikr) is bestowed upon one person alone. He is the prophet in his time, the waṣī in his time, and the Imām of the time in every time. From the moment this world has existed, and for as long as it shall exist, mankind shall never be without that single person; no other human being may take his place or claim his rank.[^cite-11]

### Taʾwīl

The word ⟪ar:رجل⟫ also designates the ⟪ar:داعی منفرد⟫ (dāʿī munfarid, the solitary Summoner). For in his ⟪ar:کَہُولیت⟫ (kuhūliyya, his ripeness of office), the dāʿī (Summoner) possesses the capacity to bring forth a Summoner like himself within the daʿwa (the summons), establishing him as his successor; and this successor in turn appoints another by the textual designation (naṣṣ) of his Summoner, so that each is made a witness over another.

To illustrate: if among many cows one be stronger than the rest, it still cannot defend its fellow cows; nor can it stand among them as a shepherd, guarding them from beasts of prey, leading them to pasture in due season, and bringing them back to the fold in due season. Such a task can be performed only by a human being, who is not himself a cow but is wiser than they and discerns their welfare and harm better than they do. So too with ourselves: although as human beings we are creatures of intellect, our intellects are devoid of the spiritual knowledge that is bestowed innately upon the Imām. Compared to him we are ignorant and helpless, just as the cattle are ignorant and helpless before the shepherd. We stand in need of his knowledge for the welfare of our hereafter. Explaining the superiority of the Imām over mankind, God says:

> ⟪quran 3:110⟫
> ⟪ar:‏كُنتُمْ خَيْرَ أُمَّةٍ أُخْرِجَتْ لِلنَّاسِ تَأْمُرُونَ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَتَنْهَوْنَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ وَتُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ ۗ وَلَوْ آمَنَ أَهْلُ ٱلْكِتَبِ لَكَانَ خَيْرًۭا لَّهُم ۚ مِّنْهُمُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ وَأَكْثَرُهُمُ ٱلْفَسِقُونَ ‎⟫
> *You [true believers in Islamic Monotheism, and real followers of Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Sunnah (legal ways, etc.)] are the best of peoples ever raised up for mankind; you enjoin Al-Ma'ruf (i.e. Islamic Monotheism and all that Islam has ordained) and forbid Al-Munkar (polytheism, disbelief and all that Islam has forbidden), and you believe in Allah. And had the people of the Scripture (Jews and Christians) believed, it would have been better for them; among them are some who have faith, but most of them are Al-Fasiqun (disobedient to Allah - and rebellious against Allah's Command).*
> You are the best community brought forth for mankind: you command what is right and forbid what is wrong, and you believe in God. Had the People of the Book believed, it would have been better for them; some among them are believers, but most of them are transgressors.
> — **Quran 3:110 (inline widget)**

The phrase ⟪ar:خَیرَ اُمَّتٍ⟫ in this verse refers to the Imāms of the Ahl al-Bayt, who command what is right and forbid what is wrong. The designation ⟪ar:خَیرَ اُمَّتٍ،⟫ is given here not on account of the outward appearance of the Imāms, but on account of their incomparable character and unfailing knowledge; the verse therefore proclaims that every Imām is better, higher, and more excellent than the community of his time. The use of the past tense in ⟪ar:کُنتُم ،⟫ shows further that you have always been the best community brought forth for your time — not only in this age, but in every age that has preceded it.[^cite-12]

### Taʾwīl

The word "best" (khayr) always denotes an elevation of rank and station, just as the Prophet referred to the rank of Mawlānā ʿAlī's waṣiyya, declaring that he occupies the rank of the waṣī: ⟪ar-quote:اَلحَسَنُ وَالحُسَینُ اِماَمَ حَقّ قاَمَ اَو قَعَدَ وَ أَبُوھُماَ خَیرٌ مِن ھُماَ⟫ — "Ḥasan and Ḥusayn are both Imāms of truth, whether they rise or sit; and their father is better (khayr) than the two of them."

## Who Has the Right to Choose the Imam?

The doctrine of the common people (⟪ar:عوا الناس⟫, al-ʿawāmm) concerning the Imamate holds that they themselves possess the right to choose the Imam. They deny that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ designated any deputy or caliph after him capable of discharging the duties of the Imamate. One faction of the public, however, maintains that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ pointed obliquely toward a particular person—namely ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫—without naming him explicitly. The gesture, as they understand it, lay in the fact that the Prophet ⟪ar:(ﷺ)⟫ instructed ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫ to lead the prayer; and because prayer stands in intimate relation to zakāt (the alms-tax), it followed for them that all Muslims were equally bound to render their zakāt to ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫ after the passing of the Messenger of God.

The ⟪ar:فرقہ المرحبہ⟫ (the Murjiʾa, the deferrers) hold that it is incumbent upon the community to appoint a man of learning and virtue who can apply his judgment to the affairs of the Sharīʿa, rendering decisions among the people according to the Book of God and the Sunna of the Prophet. On matters not addressed in either, he is to exercise his own discretion. Obedience to such an Imam, they say, is binding only insofar as he himself remains obedient to God. Should he disobey his Lord, obedience to him ceases, and the community retains the right to depose him from the seat of Imamate and appoint another in his place.

The ⟪ar:فرقہ متعزلیہ⟫ (the Muʿtazila, the rationalist theologians) maintain that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ pointed to no one and nominated no one. Rather, the prerogative of appointing an Imam belongs to the people themselves; and on this basis, they argue, the community elected ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫ as Imam after the Prophet's death.

The ⟪ar:فرقہ خوارج⟫ (the Khawārij, the seceders) hold a position different again. "We possess no knowledge," they say, "nor has any report reached us, that the Messenger of God (peace be upon him) issued a ruling concerning the office of Imamate. Yet we hold that an Imam must exist to convey the divine commands among God's servants, and so, in light of this necessity, we ourselves must choose our Imam."

---

⟪ar:سیدنا قاضی نعمان اعلی اللہ قدسہ⟫ (Sayyidnā al-Qāḍī al-Nuʿmān, may God exalt his sanctified soul) replies to those who claim that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ(ﷺ)⟫ declared, "I have not appointed anyone after me as Imam of my community," that under no circumstance can so flat a denial be admitted. The one who denies the occurrence of an event is not, in any case, qualified to bear witness to it; the witness, properly speaking, is the one who affirms the event and testifies that he himself observed it. Those who deny the Prophet's designation of a successor have, in effect, repudiated something they found uncongenial, while denying the very thing the Prophet ⟪ar:رسول اللہ(ﷺ)⟫ made manifest. The one who affirms the Imam's existence is the more deserving of belief, and his testimony the more binding upon you.[^cite-13] At ⟪ar:مقام غدیر خم⟫ (the place of Ghadīr Khumm), the Prophet proclaimed concerning ⟪ar:علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ the words ⟪ar-quote:من کنت مولاہ فھذا علی مولاہ⟫ ("Whoever's mawlā I am, this ʿAlī is his mawlā"), naming his caliph in the very terms your own community acknowledges, whose witness your own people bear, and which stand recorded in your books. The testimony of those who saw and heard the Messenger of God make this announcement is weightier than the testimony of those who claim that they neither saw nor heard such a declaration. Had the selection of the Imam truly been a matter of popular choice, ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ would have proclaimed before the assembled multitude: "Choose for yourselves a suitable Imam after me." Instead he uttered the prayer ⟪ar-quote:اللھم وال من والاہ و عاد من عاداہ و نصر من نصرہ و خزل من خزلہ⟫ ("O God, befriend whoever befriends him, oppose whoever opposes him, aid whoever aids him, and humiliate whoever forsakes him").

A second argument follows. If, as you maintain, the Imam and caliph are chosen by the people, then such an Imam must be subordinate to the people themselves, since he attained his station only by their election. On this view, the true Imam is the one who selects the Imam, while the one selected is merely his agent; and the people may depose him whenever they wish and elect another. Yet this cannot be. Were it permissible for the people to choose their own imams, it would equally be permissible for them to appoint a prophet of their own choosing, for God has made obedience to the Imams obligatory in the same manner as obedience to the prophets. The Imams succeed the prophets in their communities and serve as the divinely ordained rulers, just as the prophets are God's vicegerents and rulers among the people.[^cite-14] As for the Khārijī sect, who claim to know nothing whatsoever of the Imamate, their ignorance is itself an argument against them, for it is incumbent upon the ignorant to seek the truth from the learned.

The third argument is drawn from the words of God in the Qurʾān:

> ⟪quran 5:3⟫
> ⟪ar:‏حُرِّمَتْ عَلَيْكُمُ ٱلْمَيْتَةُ وَٱلدَّمُ وَلَحْمُ ٱلْخِنزِيرِ وَمَآ أُهِلَّ لِغَيْرِ ٱللَّهِ بِهِۦ وَٱلْمُنْخَنِقَةُ وَٱلْمَوْقُوذَةُ وَٱلْمُتَرَدِّيَةُ وَٱلنَّطِيحَةُ وَمَآ أَكَلَ ٱلسَّبُعُ إِلَّا مَا ذَكَّيْتُمْ وَمَا ذُبِحَ عَلَى ٱلنُّصُبِ وَأَن تَسْتَقْسِمُوا۟ بِٱلْأَزْلَمِ ۚ ذَلِكُمْ فِسْقٌ ۗ ٱلْيَوْمَ يَئِسَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ مِن دِينِكُمْ فَلَا تَخْشَوْهُمْ وَٱخْشَوْنِ ۚ ٱلْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِينَكُمْ وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِى وَرَضِيتُ لَكُمُ ٱلْإِسْلَمَ دِينًۭا ۚ فَمَنِ ٱضْطُرَّ فِى مَخْمَصَةٍ غَيْرَ مُتَجَانِفٍۢ لِّإِثْمٍۢ ۙ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫
> *Forbidden to you (for food) are: Al-Maytatah (the dead animals - cattle-beast not slaughtered), blood, the flesh of swine, and the meat of that which has been slaughtered as a sacrifice for others than Allah, or has been slaughtered for idols, etc., or on which Allah's Name has not been mentioned while slaughtering, and that which has been killed by strangling,  or by a violent blow, or by a headlong fall, or by the goring of horns - and that which has been (partly) eaten by a wild animal - unless you are able to slaughter it (before its death) &shy; and that which is sacrificed (slaughtered) on An&shy;Nusub (stone altars).&nbsp;(Forbidden) also is to use arrows seeking luck or decision,  (all) that is Fisqun (disobedience of Allah and sin).&nbsp;This day, those who disbelieved have given up all hope of your religion, so fear them not, but fear Me.&nbsp;This day, I have perfected your religion for you, completed My Favour upon you, and have chosen for you Islam as your religion.&nbsp;But as for him who is forced by severe hunger,  with no inclination to sin (such can eat these above-mentioned meats), then surely, Allah is Oft&shy;Forgiving, Most Merciful.*
> forbidden to you is carrion, and blood, and the flesh of swine, and that which has been invoked by the name of any other than Allah, which dies by strangulation, or of injury, or of falling from a height, or by striking its horns, and that which has been torn apart by an animal, except that which you have slaughtered, and that which is slaughtered on any land, and that which is slaughtered on any land, Today the disbelievers have despaired of your religion, so do not fear them, but fear Me, this day I have perfected your religion for you, and I have completed My favour upon you, and I have chosen for you Islam as your religion. Forgiving, Merciful.
> — **Quran 5:3 (inline widget)**

This verse was revealed when ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ proclaimed the wilāya (guardianship) and Imamate of ʿAlī on the day of Ghadīr. How, then, can the people claim that he left the Imamate unexplained, when the Imamate stands among the most exalted pillars of the divine religion? Did God declare the religion complete without clarifying so weighty a station? Or did the people themselves complete God's religion by electing their own Imam? Did they discharge a duty that God had imposed and that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ) نعوذ باللہ⟫ (the Messenger of God—God forbid such a thought) had failed to fulfil, so that the people had to expound it in his place?

As for the claim that the Prophet pointed obliquely to ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫: if the Imamate may be extended from a mere indication, then why did ⟪ar:حضرت عمر⟫ have to repeat the appointment of ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫ in explicit terms? And ⟪ar:حضرت عمر⟫, together with ⟪ar:حضرت صہیب⟫ and the appointed council, then chose ⟪ar:حضرت عثمان⟫ by mutual deliberation. In so doing, they set aside the very command of the Messenger of God (peace and blessings be upon him), failing to establish the appointed sign of the Imam and caliph, contrary to that which God says:

> ⟪quran 59:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏مَّآ أَفَآءَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ مِنْ أَهْلِ ٱلْقُرَىٰ فَلِلَّهِ وَلِلرَّسُولِ وَلِذِى ٱلْقُرْبَىٰ وَٱلْيَتَمَىٰ وَٱلْمَسَكِينِ وَٱبْنِ ٱلسَّبِيلِ كَىْ لَا يَكُونَ دُولَةًۢ بَيْنَ ٱلْأَغْنِيَآءِ مِنكُمْ ۚ وَمَآ آتَىٰكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوهُ وَمَا نَهَىٰكُمْ عَنْهُ فَٱنتَهُوا۟ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ شَدِيدُ ٱلْعِقَابِ ‎⟫
> *What Allah gave as booty (Fai') to His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) from the people of the townships, - it is for Allah, His Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), the kindred (of Messenger Muhammad PBUH), the orphans, Al&shy;Masakin (the poor), and the wayfarer, in order that it may not become a fortune used by the rich among you. And whatsoever the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH) gives you, take it, and whatsoever he forbids you, abstain (from it) , and fear Allah. Verily, Allah is Severe in punishment.*
> Whatever spoils of war Allah has given His Messenger from the people of the townships, it belongs to Allah and to the Messenger and to the near of kin and the orphans and the needy and the wayfarer, so that it may not circulate among the rich among you.
> — **Quran 59:7 (inline widget)**

As for the contention that the Messenger of God granted ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫ the right to lead the prayer—as ⟪ar:حضرت عائشہ⟫ narrates—and that this entitlement transferred to him the Imamate and caliphate: consider then that ⟪ar:حضرت عمر⟫ designated ⟪ar:حضرت صہیب⟫ to lead the prayer for him, which would, by the same reasoning, place Ṣuhayb above ⟪ar:حضرت عثمان⟫ in the matter of the caliphate. And when ⟪ar:عمر⟫ appointed ⟪ar:حضرات عمر بن العص⟫ (ʿAmr ibn al-ʿĀṣ) as commander on the campaign of ⟪ar:غزوۃ ذات السلال⟫ (Dhāt al-Salāsil), both he and ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫ prayed behind him and marched under his banner, so by the same logic ⟪ar:عمربن العاص⟫ ought to have taken precedence over them both in the caliphate. The people are agreed that ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ never gave precedence over ⟪ar:حضرت علی⟫ to anyone. ʿAlī never prayed behind Abū Bakr or behind ⟪ar:حضرت عمر⟫ or ⟪ar:حضرت عثمان⟫, while these three prayed behind him many times—so in this respect too, ⟪ar:حضرت علی⟫ was the more deserving of the Imamate and caliphate.

And when you reject the testimony of the Messenger of God borne for ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ (the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him) and for ⟪ar:حضرت فاطمۃ الزھرہ سلام علیہا⟫, on what ground do you then accept the testimony of ⟪ar:حضرت عائشہ⟫ on behalf of her father? You know full well that when ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫ began to lead the prayer, the Messenger of God ⟪ar:ﷺ⟫ emerged from his ⟪ar:بیت الشرف⟫ (noble dwelling) and turned ⟪ar:حضرت ابو بکر⟫ back even in the very state of prayer, taking the lead himself.

All these arguments of the general public are weak and without foundation. Could the Messenger of God—who left unsaid not the trimming of the nails or the proper manner of smiling, but conveyed God's command in every matter—have omitted to explain the lofty station of Imamate and caliphate? Could his prophethood, in that case, be deemed complete? Can a duty of this magnitude be entrusted to the deliberations of the multitude? The appointment of the Imam is possible only by a means that admits of no refutation, and that irrefutable means is naṣṣ (explicit textual designation). It was precisely thus that the Messenger of God uttered the naṣṣ on ⟪ar:یوم غدیر علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ (the day of Ghadīr concerning ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib), naming him caliph after himself. ʿAlī in turn transmitted it to ⟪ar:حسن⟫ and ⟪ar:حسین⟫—al-Ḥasan ⟪ar:حسن علیہ السلام⟫ and al-Ḥusayn ⟪ar:حسین علیہ السلام⟫, peace be upon them; and they to ⟪ar:علی زین العابدین علیہ السلام⟫; and he to ⟪ar:محمد الباقر علیہ السلام⟫; and he to ⟪ar:جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫. And so the chain shall continue until the Day of Resurrection.[^cite-15] This is the very pattern by which ⟪ar:آدم علیہ السلام⟫, ⟪ar:حضرت نوح⟫, ⟪ar:ابراھیم⟫, ⟪ar:موسی⟫, and ⟪ar:عیسی علیھم السلام⟫—peace be upon them all—each designated his own successor. Even ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ himself was foretold by name in the testament of Jesus, as God declares:

> ⟪quran 61:6⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ عِيسَى ٱبْنُ مَرْيَمَ يَبَنِىٓ إِسْرَٓئيلَ إِنِّى رَسُولُ ٱللَّهِ إِلَيْكُم مُّصَدِّقًۭا لِّمَا بَيْنَ يَدَىَّ مِنَ ٱلتَّوْرَىٰةِ وَمُبَشِّرًۢا بِرَسُولٍۢ يَأْتِى مِنۢ بَعْدِى ٱسْمُهُۥٓ أَحْمَدُ ۖ فَلَمَّا جَآءَهُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ قَالُوا۟ هَذَا سِحْرٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when 'Iesa (Jesus), son of Maryam (Mary), said: "O Children of Israel! I am the Messenger of Allah unto you confirming the Taurat [(Torah) which came] before me, and giving glad tidings of a Messenger to come after me, whose name shall be Ahmed . But when he (Ahmed i.e. Muhammad PBUH) came to them with clear proofs, they said: "This is plain magic." *
>And when 'Iesa son of Mary said, 'O Children of Israel, I am the Messenger of God from you, confirming the Torah that was before me, and giving good tidings of a Messenger who will come after me, whose name shall be Ahmad.' But when he came to them with clear proofs, they said, 'This is plain sorcery.'
> — **Quran 61:6 (inline widget)**

No prophet ever consigned the Imamate and the caliphate to the deliberations of the people. Every one of them appointed his own successor to discharge the duties he left behind. How then could God have entrusted so weighty a charge to the multitude, bypassing the ⟪ar:سیدالانبیا و مرسلین⟫ (the Lord of Prophets and Messengers) and his ⟪ar:اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫ (the Ahl al-Bayt, peace be upon them)?

## Qurʾānic Indications of the Imam

The earth can never remain devoid of the Imam. When God dispatched the prophets to guide humankind, He did not restrict their guidance to the people of one epoch, nor did He leave those who came afterwards bereft of it. By the principle of created nature, no prophet can live forever; as God says of His ⟪ar:نبی اکرم (ﷺ)⟫:

> ⟪quran 39:30⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّكَ مَيِّتٌۭ وَإِنَّهُم مَّيِّتُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily, you (O Muhammad PBUH) will die and verily, they (too) will die.*
> you will die, and they will die.
> — **Quran 39:30 (inline widget)**

Yet God upholds the sharīʿa (the revealed Law) carried by these prophets. To safeguard it requires a protector — the Imam of every age — and to safeguard the people of each generation requires the living interpreter the tradition designates as ⟪ar:ھادی⟫ (the guide) and ⟪ar:حجۃ اللہ⟫ (the proof of God). On this point God declares:

> ⟪quran 13:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَيَقُولُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَوْلَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْهِ آيَةٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّهِۦٓ ۗ إِنَّمَآ أَنتَ مُنذِرٌۭ ۖ وَلِكُلِّ قَوْمٍ هَادٍ ‎⟫
> *And the disbelievers say: "Why is not a sign sent down to him from his Lord?" You are only a warner, and to every people there is a guide.*
> and those who disbelieve say: wherefore is not a sign sent down unto him from his Lord? You are naught but a Herald, and for every people there is a guide.
> — **Quran 13:7 (inline widget)**

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ explained that in this verse the ⟪ar:منذر⟫ (warner) is ⟪ar:محمد مصطفی صلی اللہ علیہ و آلہ وسلم⟫, while the ⟪ar:ھاد⟫ (guide) is foremost ⟪ar:أمیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫, and after him those of the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫ who descend from him. In every age there is an Imam who directs humankind toward the teachings of ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫.[^cite-16] God has sent down His Book, the Qurʾān, as guidance — the very Book He names ⟪ar:’ الذکر‘⟫ (the Reminder):

> ⟪quran 43:44⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِنَّهُۥ لَذِكْرٌۭ لَّكَ وَلِقَوْمِكَ ۖ وَسَوْفَ تُسْـَٔلُونَ ‎⟫
> *And verily, this (the Quran) is indeed a Reminder for you (O Muhammad PBUH) and your people (Quraish people, or your followers), and you will be questioned (about it).*
> And verily it is an admonition unto thee and thy people, and ye shall be questioned.
> — **Quran 43:44 (inline widget)**

He then pledged to safeguard that Reminder until the Day of Judgement:

> ⟪quran 15:9⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّا نَحْنُ نَزَّلْنَا ٱلذِّكْرَ وَإِنَّا لَهُۥ لَحَفِظُونَ ‎⟫
> *Verily We: It is We Who have sent down the Dhikr (i.e. the Quran) and surely, We will guard it (from corruption).*
> It is We who have sent down the Remembrance, and We are its Guardians.
> — **Quran 15:9 (inline widget)**

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ said: "God has accomplished the protection of His Reminder through us, the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫, and He has commanded the community to learn from us when He says:

> ⟪quran 16:43⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن قَبْلِكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۚ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫
> *And We sent not (as Our Messengers) before you (O Muhammad PBUH) any but men, whom We inspired, (to preach and invite mankind to believe in the Oneness of Allah). So ask of those who know the Scripture [learned men of the Taurat (Torah) and the Injeel (Gospel)], if you know not.*
> And We did not send before you except men to whom We revealed. Ask the people of the Remembrance, if you do not know.
> — **Quran 16:43 (inline widget)**

This command was issued because no one can unveil the inner meaning of the Book except God and those whom He has anchored in His knowledge; we are those Imams established firmly in divine knowing:

> ⟪quran 3:7⟫
> ⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫
> *It is He Who has sent down to you (Muhammad PBUH) the Book (this Quran). In it are Verses that are entirely clear, they are the foundations of the Book [and those are the Verses of Al-Ahkam (commandments, etc.), Al-Fara'id (obligatory duties) and Al-Hudud (legal laws for the punishment of thieves, adulterers, etc.)]; and others not entirely clear. So as for those in whose hearts there is a deviation (from the truth) they follow that which is not entirely clear thereof, seeking Al-Fitnah (polytheism and trials, etc.), and seeking for its hidden meanings, but none knows its hidden meanings save Allah. And those who are firmly grounded in knowledge say: "We believe in it; the whole of it (clear and unclear Verses) are from our Lord." And none receive admonition except men of understanding. (Tafsir At-Tabarî).*
> It is He who has sent down to you the Book, in which some of its verses are definitive, they are the origin of the Book, and others are allegorical. I don't know. They say, We believe in these things; they are only from our Lord. Only those who possess understanding believe the admonition.
> — **Quran 3:7 (inline widget)**

To instruct the unlearned, to direct the questioner, to answer rightly, to clarify the rulings of the Law, and to bring the seeker to the true taʾwīl (esoteric exegesis) of God's oneness — all these offices belong to the Imam. We are ⟪ar:مُؤَیَّد مِنَ اللہ⟫ (divinely assisted) and preserved from error. In his final moments ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ said: "I leave among you two weighty treasures — the Book of God and my Ahl al-Bayt. The two shall never part until they meet me at the Pool of Kawthar." It is the Book of God — the ⟪ar:قرآن صامت⟫ (silent Qurʾān) — together with its living expositor — the ⟪ar:قرآن ناطق⟫ (speaking Qurʾān) — who jointly transmit the meanings of revelation; and the Prophet's stipulation that the two never separate is itself the warrant that an Imam must exist in every age.[^cite-17]

> ⟪quran 35:32⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ثُمَّ أَوْرَثْنَا ٱلْكِتَبَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱصْطَفَيْنَا مِنْ عِبَادِنَاۖ فَمِنْهُمْ ظَالِمٌۭ لِّنَفْسِهِۦ وَمِنْهُم مُّقْتَصِدٌۭ وَمِنْهُمْ سَابِقٌۢ بِٱلْخَيْرَتِ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِۚ ذَلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْفَضْلُ ٱلْكَبِيرُ ‎⟫
> *Then We gave the Book the Quran) for inheritance to such of Our slaves whom We chose (the followers of Muhammad PBUH). Then of them are some who wrong their ownselves, and of them are some who follow a middle course, and of them are some who are, by Allah's Leave, foremost in good deeds. That (inheritance of the Quran), that is indeed a great grace.*
> Then We bequeathed to Our Book those whom We chose from among Our servants, so of them is he who wrongs himself, and of them is he who is modest, and of them is he who is foremost in good deeds by Allah's leave. That is the great bounty of Allah.
> — **Quran 35:32 (inline widget)**

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ explained that ⟪ar:سَابِقٌۢ بِٱلْخَيْرَتِ⟫ denotes the Imam of every age; ⟪ar:مُّقْتَصِدٌ⟫ denotes the believer who recognises the right of the Imam; and ⟪ar:ظَالِمٌۭ لِّنَفْسِهِ⟫ denotes the one who falls into doubt and severs himself from us. ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ said: ⟪ar-quote:مَن ماَتَ لاَ یَعِرفُ اِماَم دَھرِہِ ھِیَا ماَتَ مَیتَۃُ جاَھِلِیَّۃ⟫ — "Whoever dies without knowing the Imam of his age dies the death of ignorance."[^cite-18] When ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ was asked which verse points to the Imam, he recited:

> ⟪quran 17:71⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَوْمَ نَدْعُوا۟ كُلَّ أُنَاسٍۭ بِإِمَمِهِمْ ۖ فَمَنْ أُوتِىَ كِتَبَهُۥ بِيَمِينِهِۦ فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ يَقْرَءُونَ كِتَبَهُمْ وَلَا يُظْلَمُونَ فَتِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *(And remember) the Day when We shall call together all human beings with their (respective) Imam [their Prophets, or their records of good and bad deeds, or their Holy Books like the Quran, the Taurat (Torah), the Injeel (Gospel), etc.]. So whosoever is given his record in his right hand, such will read their records, and they will not be dealt with unjustly in the least.*
> On the Day when We shall call every nation with their leaders, whosoever is given his book in his right hand, these shall read their book, and they shall not be wronged a whit.
> — **Quran 17:71 (inline widget)**

He continued: "In this verse the Imam is the figure whom the people followed in this world. Those who pledged allegiance to ⟪ar:امیر المومنین علی ابن ابی طالب علیہ السلام⟫ and obeyed him will be summoned together with him on the Day of Judgement." He spoke in the same way of every Imam of the Ahl al-Bayt.

Beyond these, God alludes to the Imams in many further passages. In ⟪ar:سورۃ النسا⟫ He says:

> ⟪quran 4:54⟫
> ⟪ar:‏أَمْ يَحْسُدُونَ ٱلنَّاسَ عَلَىٰ مَآ آتَىٰهُمُ ٱللَّهُ مِن فَضْلِهِۦ ۖ فَقَدْ آتَيْنَآ آلَإِبْرَهِيمَ ٱلْكِتَبَ وَٱلْحِكْمَةَ وَآتَيْنَهُم مُّلْكًا عَظِيمًۭا ‎⟫
> *Or do they envy men (Muhammad PBUH and his followers) for what Allah has given them of His Bounty? Then We had already given the family of Ibrahim (Abraham) the Book and Al&shy;Hikmah (As&shy;Sunnah - Divine Inspiration to those Prophets not written in the form of a book), and conferred upon them a great kingdom.*
> or are they jealous of the people for what God has given them of His bounty? We have given the family of Abraham the Book and wisdom, and We have given them a great kingdom.
> — **Quran 4:54 (inline widget)**

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ said: "By ⟪ar:’ الناس‘⟫ in this verse God means us, the Imams of the ⟪ar:اہل بیت⟫. He has bestowed upon us the highest station of Imamate, and on its account people envy us. ⟪ar:ملک عظیم⟫ — the great kingdom — signifies that God prolonged prophecy and Imamate through the descendants of ⟪ar:ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫, that is, through ⟪ar:اسحاق و اسماعیل علیھم السلام⟫, and raised up among their progeny Imams of ⟪ar:جلیل القدر⟫ rank whose obedience is obedience to God and whose disobedience is disobedience to Him. When this ⟪ar:ملک عظیم آل محمد علیہ السلام⟫ was granted to us, the people repudiated it."

God says:

> ⟪quran 4:58⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَأْمُرُكُمْ أَن تُؤَدُّوا۟ ٱلْأَمَنَتِ إِلَىٰٓ أَهْلِهَا وَإِذَا حَكَمْتُم بَيْنَ ٱلنَّاسِ أَن تَحْكُمُوا۟ بِٱلْعَدْلِ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ نِعِمَّا يَعِظُكُم بِهِۦٓ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ سَمِيعًۢا بَصِيرًۭا ‎⟫
> *Verily! Allah commands that you should render back the trusts to those, to whom they are due; and that when you judge between men, you judge with justice.&nbsp;Verily, how excellent is the teaching which He (Allah) gives you! Truly, Allah is Ever All&shy;Hearer, All&shy;Seer.*
> verily Allah commandeth you that ye render trusts unto those who are trusted, and when ye judge between people, judge with justice. Verily Allah is the best of admonitions to you; verily Allah is Hearing, Seeing.
> — **Quran 4:58 (inline widget)**

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ said: "We are the Imams of the ⟪ar:اہل بیت⟫ whom God here commands to render the trusts: each of us must hand on to the Imam who succeeds him the books, the weapons, and the knowledge entrusted to us." Then, in the very next verse, God addresses every believer until the Resurrection — ⟪ar:يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟⟫:

> ⟪quran 4:59⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger (Muhammad PBUH), and those of you (Muslims) who are in authority.&nbsp;(And) if you differ in anything amongst yourselves, refer it to Allah and His Messenger (PBUH), if you believe in Allah and in the Last Day.&nbsp;That is better and more suitable for final determination.&nbsp;*
>O you who believe! Obey Allah and obey the Messenger and those in authority among you. And if you dispute among yourselves about anything, refer it to Allah and His Messenger, if you believe in Allah and the Last Day. That is better and better in the end.
> — **Quran 4:59 (inline widget)**

⟪ar:امام باقر علیہ السلام⟫ said: "By ⟪ar:أولو الأمر⟫ — those whose obedience the verse couples with that of God and His Messenger — God means us, the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫."

> ⟪quran 4:83⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا جَآءَهُمْ أَمْرٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْأَمْنِ أَوِ ٱلْخَوْفِ أَذَاعُوا۟ بِهِۦ ۖ وَلَوْ رَدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ وَإِلَىٰٓ أُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنْهُمْ لَعَلِمَهُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَنۢبِطُونَهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ وَلَوْلَا فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَتُهُۥ لَٱتَّبَعْتُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫
> *When there comes to them some matter touching (public) safety or fear, they make it known (among the people), if only they had referred it to the Messenger or to those charged with authority among them, the proper investigators would have understood it from them (directly).&nbsp;Had it not been for the Grace and Mercy of Allah upon you,  you would have followed Shaitan (Satan), save a few of you.*

## Who is Meant by the Family of Muḥammad?

The injunction to invoke blessings upon ⟪ar:محمد و آل محمد⟫ is obligatory, and the Family of Muḥammad designates the Ahl al-Bayt (the People of the House) of the Messenger of Allah, may peace and blessings be upon him, for the office of imāma (the Imamate, the guiding succession) has been transmitted to them. They are the true Umma (community) of the Prophet in its inward sense. The Most High declares:

> ⟪quran 33:56⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ وَمَلَٓئِكَتَهُۥ يُصَلُّونَ عَلَى ٱلنَّبِىِّۚ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ صَلُّوا۟ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا۟ تَسْلِيمًا ‎⟫
> *Allah sends His Salat (Graces, Honours, Blessings, Mercy, etc.) on the Prophet (Muhammad PBUH) and also His angels too (ask Allah to bless and forgive him). O you who believe! Send your Salat on (ask Allah to bless) him (Muhammad PBUH), and (you should) greet (salute) him with the Islamic way of greeting (salutation i.e. As&shy;Salamu 'Alaikum).*
> Allah and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet. O you who believe, send blessings and peace.
> — **Quran 33:56 (inline widget)**

It is related that when this verse descended, the Companions inquired of ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫: "What is the proper manner of invoking blessings upon you?" He answered with the canonical formula, ⟪ar-quote:اللّٰھم صلی علی محمد و علی آل محمد کما صلیت علی ابراھیم و علی آل ابراھیم  انک حمید مجید⟫. The Messenger of Allah, in this same manner, elucidated the modes of prayer, fasting, ḥajj (the pilgrimage), zakāt (the obligatory alms), and the rest of the religious obligations — for these had been disclosed in the Qur'an in summary form, awaiting his authoritative clarification.[^cite-19]

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ was once approached by a questioner who said: "O son of ⟪ar:رسول(ﷺ)!⟫, inform me who the Family of Muḥammad are." ⟪ar:صادق علیہ السلام⟫ replied: "The Family of Muḥammad are, properly speaking, the Ahl al-Bayt of the Messenger of Allah." The questioner observed that some hold all Muslims to be numbered within the Family. The Imam smiled and said: "He has spoken both truth and falsehood." How could the whole body of Muslims who profess tawḥīd (the affirmation of divine unity) and acknowledge the prophetic mission be reckoned as the Family of Muḥammad, when so many among them are deficient in their religion and incline toward extremes? Yet the claim retains a measure of truth: those among them who are true believers may be counted within the wider category of the Family by virtue of their adherence to the conditions stipulated by the Qur'an — even though they are not the Family in the strict Qur'anic sense. The questioner pressed: "What, then, are the Qur'anic conditions by which a believer is reckoned among the Family of Muḥammad?" ⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق⟫ replied: "Whosoever obeys and follows the commands of the noble Qur'an is brought within this wider category. Just as Allah has granted ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫ precedence over all creatures on account of their kinship with the Prophet, may peace and blessings be upon him, so too will such a believer be counted within the Family of Muḥammad through the bond of obedience."[^cite-20] The Most High says:

> ⟪quran 5:51⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ لَا تَتَّخِذُوا۟ ٱلْيَهُودَ وَٱلنَّصَرَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآءَ ۘ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَآءُ بَعْضٍۢ ۚ وَمَن يَتَوَلَّهُم مِّنكُمْ فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎⟫
> *O you who believe! Take not the Jews and the Christians as Auliya' (friends, protectors, helpers, etc.), they are but Auliya' to one another.&nbsp;And if any amongst you takes them as Auliya', then surely he is one of them.&nbsp;Verily, Allah guides not those people who are the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong&shy;doers and unjust).*
> O you who believe! Do not take the Jews and the Christians as allies. They are allies of one another. And whoever of you allies himself with them, then surely he is one of them; and Allah does not guide the wrongdoers.

And He has likewise recounted the supplication of ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫:

The questioner pressed yet further: "May my life be ransomed for you — furnish me, from the Book ⟪ar:قرآن حکیم⟫ of Allah, proof by which I may demonstrate beyond doubt that the Family of ⟪ar:آل محمد (ﷺ)⟫, in particular the Ahl al-Bayt of the Prophet, are distinguished from the generality of Muslims." The Imam replied: "The Most High has said:

> ⟪quran 3:33-34⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ ٱصْطَفَىٰٓ آدَمَ وَنُوحًۭا وَآلَ إِبْرَهِيمَ وَآلَ عِمْرَنَ عَلَى ٱلْعَلَمِينَ ‎ ‏ذُرِّيَّةًۢ بَعْضُهَا مِنۢ بَعْضٍۢ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ ‎⟫

> God has chosen Adam and Noah and the family of Abraham and the family of Imran above all the worlds. God is All-hearing and All-knowing.
> — **Quran 3:33-34 (inline widget)**

It is therefore manifest that the offspring of a chosen line are descended one from another. Elsewhere He says:

> ⟪quran 40:28⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ رَجُلٌۭ مُّؤْمِنٌۭ مِّنْ آلِ فِرْعَوْنَ يَكْتُمُ إِيمَنَهُۥٓ أَتَقْتُلُونَ رَجُلًا أَن يَقُولَ رَبِّىَ ٱللَّهُ وَقَدْ جَآءَكُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ مِن رَّبِّكُمْۖ وَإِن يَكُ كَذِبًۭا فَعَلَيْهِ كَذِبُهُۥۖ وَإِن يَكُ صَادِقًۭا يُصِبْكُم بَعْضُ ٱلَّذِى يَعِدُكُمْۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى مَنْ هُوَ مُسْرِفٌۭ كَذَّابٌۭ ‎⟫
> *And a believing man of Fir'aun's (Pharaoh) family, who hid his faith said: "Would you kill a man because he says: My Lord is Allah, and he has come to you with clear signs (proofs) from your Lord? And if he is a liar, upon him will be (the sin of) his lie; but if he is telling the truth, then some of that (calamity) wherewith he threatens you will befall on you." Verily, Allah guides not one who is a Musrif (a polytheist, or a murderer who shed blood without a right, or those who commit great sins, oppressor, transgressor), a liar!*
> and a man of faith from among Pharaoh's people said, "Will you kill a man who says, 'My Lord is God,' and he has brought you clear proofs from your Lord? He promises you that it will come true, but God does not guide him who is a transgressor and a liar.
> — **Quran 40:28 (inline widget)**

In this verse Allah numbers the believer — who was the cousin of Pharaoh — among the household of Pharaoh purely by virtue of kinship, even though he stood opposed to Pharaoh in religion. If all Muslims who profess faith in the Prophet, may peace and blessings be upon him, and follow him were to be reckoned as the Family of Muḥammad on the strength of belief and obedience alone, Allah would not have attributed this believer of Pharaoh's people to Pharaoh, since he was opposed to Pharaoh's religion. From the example it is therefore clear that a person's "family," in its strict sense, denotes his Ahl al-Bayt.

## Inclusion within the Category of the Family of Muḥammad

The one who follows and obeys ⟪ar:آل محمد (ﷺ)⟫ is counted within the category of the Family of Muḥammad by the measure of piety, just as ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ declared: "Whosoever follows me is of me." Allah says in the Qur'an:

> ⟪quran 40:46⟫
> ⟪ar:‏ٱلنَّارُ يُعْرَضُونَ عَلَيْهَا غُدُوًّۭا وَعَشِيًّۭاۖ وَيَوْمَ تَقُومُ ٱلسَّاعَةُ أَدْخِلُوٓا۟ آلَ فِرْعَوْنَ أَشَدَّ ٱلْعَذَابِ ‎⟫
> *The Fire; they are exposed to it, morning and afternoon, and on the Day when the Hour will be established (it will be said to the angels): "Cause Fir'aun's (Pharaoh) people to enter the severest torment!" *
> They are exposed to the Fire morning and evening, and on the Day when the Hour will be proclaimed [it will be said], "Admit the people of Pharaoh to the severest punishment."
> — **Quran 40:46 (inline widget)**

Here the "family of Pharaoh" designates Pharaoh's kinsmen in the narrow sense and the totality of his followers in the wider. Those who will enter the Fire on his account do so because of their devotion to Pharaoh's leadership: they are numbered with him through the bond of obedience, for they took Pharaoh and his household as their imams and followed them out of love. This inverted parallel clarifies the structure of walāya (allegiance): allegiance binds the follower to the followed in destiny as well as in act, whether the followed be the imam of guidance or the imam of error.[^cite-21]

Elsewhere the Most High says:

> ⟪quran 37:130⟫
> ⟪ar:‏سَلَمٌ عَلَىٰٓ أٓلِ يَاسِينَ ‎⟫
> *Salamun (peace) be upon Ilyasîn (Elias)!" *
Peace be upon > family of Yasin
> — **Quran 37:130 (inline widget)**

This ⟪ar:یٰسٓ⟫ verse refers to ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫ and their family — the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫. In the same manner Allah addresses ⟪ar:آل داؤد⟫ with the command to give thanks and to act with righteousness:

> ⟪quran 34:13⟫
> ⟪ar:‏يَعْمَلُونَ لَهُۥ مَا يَشَآءُ مِن مَّحَرِيبَ وَتَمَثِيلَ وَجِفَانٍۢ كَٱلْجَوَابِ وَقُدُورٍۢ رَّاسِيَتٍۚ ٱعْمَلُوٓا۟ آلَ دَاوُۥدَ شُكْرًۭاۚ وَقَلِيلٌۭ مِّنْ عِبَادِىَ ٱلشَّكُورُ ‎⟫
> *They worked for him what he desired, (making) high rooms, images, basins as large as reservoirs, and (cooking) cauldrons fixed (in their places). "Work you, O family of Dawud (David), with thanks!" But few of My slaves are grateful.*
> They made for him whatever He wished: synagogues, images, basins like reservoirs, and cauldrons. O House of David, do good deeds in thanksgiving, and few of My servants are grateful.
> — **Quran 34:13 (inline widget)**

And in yet another place He says:

> ⟪quran 2:248⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ لَهُمْ نَبِيُّهُمْ إِنَّ آيَةَ مُلْكِهِۦٓ أَن يَأْتِيَكُمُ ٱلتَّابُوتُ فِيهِ سَكِينَةٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَبَقِيَّةٌۭ مِّمَّا تَرَكَ آلُ مُوسَىٰ وَآلُ هَرُونَ تَحْمِلُهُ ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ ۚ إِنَّ فِى ذَلِكَ لَآيَةًۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُم مُّؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫
&gt; *And their Prophet (Samuel &gt;&gt;) said to them: Verily! The sign of His Kingdom is that there shall come to you At-Tabut (a wooden box), wherein is Sakinah (peace and reassurance) from your Lord and a remnant of that which Musa (Moses) and Harun (Aaron) left behind, carried by the angels. Verily, in this is a sign for you if you are indeed believers.*

Surely in that is a sign for you, if you are believers."
> — **Quran 2:248 (inline widget)**

The peoples numbered in these verses — ⟪ar:آل فرعون، آل یس، آل داؤد، آل موسی اور آل ھرون⟫ — are reckoned by the criterion of allegiance and following, for many of those included among them bear no ⟪ar:نسبی و صلبی⟫ (lineal and seminal) relation to the family they are joined to. It is therefore manifest that the Ahl al-Bayt of the ⟪ar:انبیاء اکرام علیھم السلام⟫ are the Imams; and those who bind themselves to the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت⟫ and follow them in matters of religion are counted with ⟪ar:آل محمد(ﷺ)⟫, as the Most High has made plain in many verses of the Qur'an.[^cite-22]

The Imam then turned to the questioner and said: "Know that no umma of the past has been more oppressive than this one." For this Umma has presumed that no distinction stands between ⟪ar:پیغمبر محمد رسول اللہ صلعم⟫ and the ⟪ar:اہل بیت کرام علیھم السلام⟫ — that the Ahl al-Bayt possess no precedence over the generality. What injustice could be graver? Those who have entertained such a presumption have leveled a grievous calumny against the Lord of the Worlds and committed a great slander. They who fail to honor the rank of ⟪ar:محمدو آل محمد⟫ remain disgraced in this world and in the Hereafter, until they repent and acknowledge the virtues and excellences with which Allah has graced the Ahl al-Bayt: that the ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیھم السلام⟫ are the radiant stars of prophethood, the dwelling-place of mercy, the wellspring of knowledge, the people of remembrance, and the channels through which the angels traverse between the higher and lower realms. If they so acknowledge and turn to the truth, they shall not be excluded from the Family of Muḥammad.

## Who Are the Ummah of Muhammad?

The expression ⟪ar:امت محمدی علیہ السلام⟫ properly designates ⟪ar:ائمہ اہل بیت علیہ السلام⟫. Those who affirm their prophetic mission and walk in obedience to them are reckoned among the Ummah of ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫, just as they are numbered among his family; the very obedience that qualifies them as kin of ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ qualifies them, by the same measure, as his Ummah. Those who deny the primary bounty of the Truth — the bounty constituted by the ⟪ar:امت محمدی (اہل بیت)⟫ — fall under the censure of the verse:

> ⟪quran 4:150⟫
> ⟪ar:‏إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ يَكْفُرُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَيُرِيدُونَ أَن يُفَرِّقُوا۟ بَيْنَ ٱللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِۦ وَيَقُولُونَ نُؤْمِنُ بِبَعْضٍۢ وَنَكْفُرُ بِبَعْضٍۢ وَيُرِيدُونَ أَن يَتَّخِذُوا۟ بَيْنَ ذَلِكَ سَبِيلًا ‎⟫
> *Verily, those who disbelieve in Allah and His Messengers and wish to make distinction between Allah and His Messengers (by believing in Allah and disbelieving in His Messengers) saying, "We believe in some but reject others," and wish to adopt a way in between.*
> Those who disbelieve in Allah and His Messengers and wish to make a distinction between Allah and His Messengers and say, 'We believe in some and disbelieve in others,' and wish to adopt a way in between.
> — **Quran 4:150 (inline widget)**

Such persons profess faith in God and His Messenger, yet when summoned to recognise the rank of the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt, they refuse. Their belief, divided against itself, mirrors precisely the division the verse denounces.[^cite-23]

⟪ar:امام جعفر الصادق علیہ السلام⟫ relates that when ⟪ar:ابراھیم اور اسماعیل علیہم السلام⟫ — ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم⟫ and his son — completed the raising of the Kaʿba, they offered this petition:

> ⟪quran 2:127-128⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ يَرْفَعُ إِبْرَهِیمُ ٱلْقَوَاعِدَ مِنَ ٱلْبَيْتِ وَإِسْمَعِيلُ رَبَّنَا تَقَبَّلْ مِنَّآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلسَّمِيعُ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ‎ ‏رَبَّنَا وَٱجْعَلْنَا مُسْلِمَيْنِ لَكَ وَمِن ذُرِّيَّتِنَآ أُمَّةًۭ مُّسْلِمَةًۭ لَّكَ وَأَرِنَا مَنَاسِكَنَا وَتُبْ عَلَيْنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when Ibrahim (Abraham) and (his son) Isma'il (Ishmael) were raising the foundations of the House (the Ka'bah at Makkah), (saying), "Our Lord! Accept (this service) from us. Verily! You are the All-Hearer, the All-Knower." "Our Lord! And make us submissive unto You and of our offspring a nation submissive unto You, and show us our Manasik (all the ceremonies of pilgrimage - Hajj and 'Umrah, etc.), and accept our repentance. Truly, You are the One Who accepts repentance, the Most Merciful.*
>And when Abraham and Ishmael were raising the foundations of the House,  our Lord, accept it from us. Our Lord, make us submissive to You, and from our descendants a party submissive to You, and show us our rites, and accept our repentance. You are the Receiver of Repentance, the Merciful.
> — **Quran 2:127-128 (inline widget)**

To this they joined a further prayer:

> ⟪quran 14:35-37⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ إِبْرَهِيمُ رَبِّ ٱجْعَلْ هَذَا ٱلْبَلَدَ آمِنًۭا وَٱجْنُبْنِى وَبَنِىَّ أَن نَّعْبُدَ ٱلْأَصْنَامَ ‎ ‏رَبِّ إِنَّهُنَّ أَضْلَلْنَ كَثِيرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلنَّاسِ ۖ فَمَن تَبِعَنِى فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنِّى ۖ وَمَنْ عَصَانِى فَإِنَّكَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎ ‏رَّبَّنَآ إِنِّىٓ أَسْكَنتُ مِن ذُرِّيَّتِى بِوَادٍ غَيْرِ ذِى زَرْعٍ عِندَ بَيْتِكَ ٱلْمُحَرَّمِ رَبَّنَا لِيُقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ فَٱجْعَلْ أَفْـِٔدَةًۭ مِّنَ ٱلنَّاسِ تَهْوِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ وَٱرْزُقْهُم مِّنَ ٱلثَّمَرَتِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫
> *And (remember) when Ibrahim (Abraham) said: "O my Lord! Make this city (Makkah) one of peace and security, and keep me and my sons away from worshipping idols. "O my Lord! They have indeed led astray many among mankind. But whoso follows me, he verily is of me. And whoso disobeys me, - still You are indeed Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful. "O our Lord! I have made some of my offspring to dwell in an uncultivable valley by Your Sacred House (the Ka'bah at Makkah); in order, O our Lord, that they may perform As-Salat (Iqamat-as-Salat), so fill some hearts among men with love towards them, and (O Allah) provide them with fruits so that they may give thanks.*
> And when Abraham ( Abraham ) said : O my Lord ! Make this city a place of peace and save me and my children from idolatry, O my Lord! They have led many people astray, so whoever follows me is mine and whoever disobeys ( You ) then You are indeed Forgiving , Merciful . I have settled some of my children in a field where there is no cultivation, near Your Honorable House, O Lord! In order that they may perform the prayer. Then incline the hearts of some people towards them, and provide them with fruits, so that they may be grateful.
> — **Quran 14:35-37 (inline widget)**

God answered the prayer of ⟪ar:حضرت ابراھیم علیہ السلام⟫ and brought forth from his loins the unbroken chain of ⟪ar:ائمۃ المعصومین⟫, culminating in ⟪ar:رسول اللہ (ﷺ)⟫, from whom emerged ⟪ar:مولانا علی⟫ and the line that followed. They alone — the Imams of the present age — are entitled to be called the ⟪ar:امت مسلمۃ⟫ promised in that prayer, for they alone trace a lineage upon which idolatry never laid its hand. The triad ⟪ar:محمد و علی و فاطمۃ⟫ stands as living testimony to the chastity and purity of that line.

> ⟪quran 3:104⟫
> ⟪ar:‏وَلْتَكُن مِّنكُمْ أُمَّةٌۭ يَدْعُونَ إِلَى ٱلْخَيْرِ وَيَأْمُرُونَ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَيَنْهَوْنَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ ۚ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫
> *Let there arise out of you a group of people inviting to all that is good (Islam), enjoining Al-Ma'ruf (i.e. Islamic Monotheism and all that Islam orders one to do) and forbidding Al-Munkar (polytheism and disbelief and all that Islam has forbidden). And it is they who are the successful.*
> Let there be a party of you who invite to what is right and enjoin what is right and forbid what is wrong; it is they who are the felicitous.
> — **Quran 3:104 (inline widget)**

The general community cannot claim this office: it neither invites consistently to good nor forbids what is evil with sustained authority. This labour belongs to the Ahl al-Bayt alone — and the daʿwa (the summons, the divine invitation) by which they discharge it is the very pivot upon which the verse turns. The same logic governs the next verse:

> ⟪quran 3:110⟫
> ⟪ar:‏كُنتُمْ خَيْرَ أُمَّةٍ أُخْرِجَتْ لِلنَّاسِ تَأْمُرُونَ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَتَنْهَوْنَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ وَتُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ ۗ وَلَوْ آمَنَ أَهْلُ ٱلْكِتَبِ لَكَانَ خَيْرًۭا لَّهُم ۚ مِّنْهُمُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ وَأَكْثَرُهُمُ ٱلْفَسِقُونَ ‎⟫
> *You [true believers in Islamic Monotheism, and real followers of Prophet Muhammad PBUH and his Sunnah (legal ways, etc.)] are the best of peoples ever raised up for mankind; you enjoin Al-Ma'ruf (i.e. Islamic Monotheism and all that Islam has ordained) and forbid Al-Munkar (polytheism, disbelief and all that Islam has forbidden), and you believe in Allah. And had the people of the Scripture (Jews and Christians) believed, it would have been better for them; among them are some who have faith, but most of them are Al-Fasiqun (disobedient to Allah - and rebellious against Allah's Command).*
> you are the best of all the nations that have been sent down for mankind, enjoining what is right and forbidding what is wrong, and believing in God. Had the People of the Book believed, it would have been better for them. Some of them are believers, but most of them are transgressors.
> — **Quran 3:110 (inline widget)**

When God names His addressees ⟪ar:خیر أُمت⟫ — the best of all the nations ever raised for humankind — the reference can only be to the Prophets and Imams descended from Abraham, the line from which ⟪ar:محمد (ﷺ)⟫ himself was sent. The verse points, in its inner reading, to ⟪ar:رسول اللہ ، علی، فاطمۃ، حسن و حسین علیھم السلام⟫: the household whose purity the Qurʾān itself witnesses. That a single person — the Messenger of God or Mawlānā ʿAlī — should be designated an entire Ummah occasions no surprise, for the Qurʾān says of Abraham:

> ⟪quran 16:120⟫

## The Purity and Infallibility of the Imam

Of the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt God says in the Qurʾān:

> ⟪quran 33:33⟫

> *And stay in your houses, and do not display yourselves like that of the times of ignorance, and perform As-Salat (Iqamat&shy;as&shy;Salat), and give Zakat and obey Allah and His Messenger. Allah wishes only to remove Ar&shy;Rijs (evil deeds and sins, etc.) from you, O members of the family (of the Prophet PBUH), and to purify you with a thorough purification.*

> — **Quran 33:33 (inline widget)**

The verse declares that God's settled will is to keep impurity *away* from the People of the House; the language is one of warding off, not of cleansing what was once defiled. Had it been otherwise, the implication would be that the Imams were first soiled and then washed — ⟪ar:نعوذ باللہ⟫ from any such thought. A parallel error attends the popular conjecture about the descent of Adam's progeny: that Eve's first pregnancy delivered only sons and her next only daughters, and that brothers then married sisters to populate the earth. The supposition is monstrous, for no law in any dispensation has sanctioned such a union; and to credit it is to make the Prophets and Messengers — even the ⟪ar:خیر المرسلین⟫ — descendants of an illicit bond, ⟪ar:(نعوذ باللہ من ذالک)⟫. The one upon whose head the crown of divine authority rests, whom God appoints sovereign over the universe — the ⟪ar:خیر الأنبیاء و المرسلین⟫ — cannot be touched by so grave a defect.

On the contrary, the Messenger of God — the ⟪ar:صادق و امین⟫ — has said that he was conveyed from ⟪ar:ارحام زکیۃ⟫ to ⟪ar:اصلاب طاہرۃ⟫, and that no impurity ever touched him along the way. ⟪ar:امیر المؤمنین علی ابن ابی طالب⟫ is reported to have addressed humankind in these terms: God has folded the whole of the macrocosm (⟪ar:عالم الکبیر⟫) into the human being. The human eye answers to the brightness of sun and moon; the human frame compounds the four elements — fire, air, water, and earth. There is mountain and there is cavern in his body; the rivers of his blood circulate within him; the plants and the animals lie subject to his command. If the human being, who is himself the abridgement of creation, occupies such a station, what then must be the rank of the Imam, who stands at a higher degree still? For the Imam is Lord of his Age, and the universe entire, with all who dwell within it, falls under his command.[^cite-26]

It follows that the personality of the Imam transcends the human order and is not to be measured against it. He is, within his time, the locus to which every existent owes its determination. The friends of God have drawn out a further subtlety: those who have advanced and joined themselves to the Imam — passing thereby through the worlds of ⟪ar:لاہوت و ناست⟫ (divinity and humanity) — become in reality ⟪ar:شئی⟫ (a "thing," an existent of true weight and consequence). Against them stand all the rest — the ⟪ar:لا شئی⟫, the "no-thing," the inconsequential — which possess no abiding value.

## Kitāb Ithbāt al-Imāma — The Book of Affirming the Imamate

**In the Name of God, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.**

All praise is due to God, who dispatched prophets and appointed Imams and caliphs to guide His creation. I bear witness that there is no god but God, and that Muhammad is His servant and messenger; may God's blessing rest upon him, upon his family, and upon his companion ʿAlī, the most learned of mankind in the esoteric exegesis (taʾwīl) of the Qurʾān.

The Imamate is the axis upon which religion turns; every matter, whether spiritual or temporal, revolves around it. It is the wellspring from which knowledge of God and of the Qurʾān is drawn, and its truth is established through evidence and demonstration. Earlier authors of the summons (daʿwa) have already composed treatises affirming the Imamate, marshalling proofs from the verses of the Qurʾān and the traditions of the Prophet. The necessity of an Imam is rooted in natural disposition, innate constitution, nature, intellect, governance, prescription, and norms. It is obligatory within every revealed law, religion, and religious community, just as the existence of a Creator is necessary.

The various religious communities have called their past Imams prophets and sages; certain misguided Muslims, by contrast, have applied the term Imam to their scholars and jurists. Whoever seeks salvation must lay aside his inclinations and desires, and recognize the deficiencies of every false pretender to this exalted station.

We shall therefore enumerate the weaknesses of those who falsely claim the Imamate; we shall turn their own principles against them and divest of the Imamate those who are unfit for it. We shall then establish the Imamate of the true Imams through proof and demonstration, and shall make plain the necessity of obeying them, of entrusting our affairs to their command, and of accepting from them revelation, interpretation, and the confession of divine unity.

God, in His subtle wisdom and far-reaching power, created the human body with its senses and provided the sustenance through which it endures. In like manner, He fashioned principles, causes, and means for the souls, the spirits, and the noetic forms, through which they receive their spiritual sustenance, their nourishment, and their continued life.

The disparities in degrees of excellence that obtain among all entities — both the foundational essences and their derivations — furnish the firmest evidence for the Imamate and for the Imams. God created entities across all genera and species, differentiated and varied in respect to their excellence. Some of these genera and species He made indispensable to human existence and function; others He made a source of nourishment, a medicine, an adornment, or a tool essential to life.[^cite-27]

The necessity of the Imam emerges clearly from these gradations and from the summits attained within each kind. In every category, the highest exemplar points to the Imam. The following table illustrates this correspondence:

| Category | Noblest of the Category | Points to the Imam |
| --- | --- | --- |

[1] Praise be to God, who sent messengers and prophets and appointed Imams and caliphs — a blessing for His friends, a chastisement for His enemies, a benefit for His religion and His world, and an edifice for the present life and the life to come. I praise Him as one praises who is aware of the immense value of the bounty brought forth through them, who is grateful for it, and who hastens upon his path for God's sake. I bear witness that there is no deity except God, with the testimony of a worshipper who knows the requirements of that testimony and strives to fulfil it. I bear witness, too, that Muhammad is His servant and messenger, preferred over all His messengers and shielded from every mistake and error. May God bestow upon him innumerable and infinite blessings. May He bless also his loyal and truthful legatee ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, adept in clarifying the spiritual realities concealed in the Holy Qurʾān; and the Imams of the line of the martyr, to whom belong praise, glory, infallibility, and rectitude; and the master of the palace and of the age — the Imam al-Ḥākim bi-Amr Allāh — supported by conquest and victory. May God's most excellent salutations, His most abundant blessings, and His purest greetings rest upon him and upon the heir-apparent of the Muslims and vicegerent of the Commander of the Faithful, for as long as tongues discourse and darkness and light continue to alternate. And now —

[2] The Imamate is the pole and foundation of religion, around which all religious and worldly matters revolve. It is a benefit in this present life and in the hereafter, and it governs the affairs of the worshippers, the prosperity of the land, and the acceptance of recompense in the abode of return (dār al-ākhira). Through the Imamate alone is the knowledge of God's absolute unity (tawḥīd) and of the revealed Message attained — a knowledge grounded in proof, demonstration, and evidence — as is the knowledge of the religious law, its foundation, its interpretation (taʾwīl), and its clarification.

[3] In calling the Imamate the pole and foundation of religion, we have not thereby given it precedence over prophethood. Rather, the affirmation of the Imamate already entails the affirmation of prophethood. Whoever acknowledges the Imam acknowledges the messenger; yet not everyone who acknowledges the messenger acknowledges the reality of the Imam. Proof of prophethood is required for those outside Islam, whereas the affirmation of the Imamate is necessary for Muslims, who stand closer to the true believers. The struggle required with them — through debate and argument — has priority over the struggle with those who deny the messenger and prophethood; for the summons of Islam and of prophethood has already reached the polytheists, and a proof has been established against them. The world can never be deprived of an Imam at any moment or in any age, whereas the messenger is present at certain times and not at others. Therefore in affirming the Imamate there is also an affirmation of prophethood.

God, exalted is He, called the Imam a messenger when He said:

"And mention in the Book Ismāʿīl. He was indeed faithful in fulfilling his promise, and he was a messenger and a prophet."

He likewise called Abraham, after he had completed his prophethood, an Imam:

"And when his Lord tested Abraham with certain commands, and he fulfilled them, He said: I am making you an Imam for mankind. He said: And of my descendants? He said: My covenant does not reach the wrongdoers."

It is related from Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq that he said: God chose Abraham first as a prophet, then as a friend (khalīl), then as a messenger, and finally said, "I am appointing you an Imam for all humanity." God called Adam a vicegerent (khalīfa), and the title of vicegerent is among the names of the Imams. He called Dāwūd a vicegerent while he was already one of the Imams of past cycles, and He called the proofs prophets, just as He addressed Muhammad and the other messengers as both prophets and messengers. The reason is that the messenger first attains the rank of the Imamate. Then, if the times require the establishment of a new religious law, he becomes the seventh Imam of that cycle and assumes the promulgation of that law. The Imam thus occupies the place of the messenger in his own age and time. The messenger, before rising to promulgate the law, is himself one of the Imams, having received the command from a previous Imam; and after finalizing the religious law, he hands the command to the next Imam. The Imam is therefore present in the world at all times: the world is never deprived of him. The Imam supports and maintains the religious laws and their esoteric realities, as we have stated. It now becomes plain that the progression of the religious cycle depends upon the Imam, and that it is the Imam's prerogative how to act upon the prophet's law in his own time. No one can attain the rank of the prophet, nor reach the proper understanding of the religious law, which is altered or replaced only by the Imam. It is only through the Imam that one may arrive at the inner truth of the religious law, its interpretation, and its meaning.

[5] Much of what has been composed on affirming the Imamate by the elders of the daʿwa draws on the well-known Qurʾānic verses revealed in confirmation of the Imamate and of the Imams, together with traditions reported from the Prophet. Those who came before have left nothing on this subject for those who came after, and there is no profit in rehearsing the same notions in different words. We wish therefore to compose a treatise affirming the Imamate by drawing evidence (istishhād) and deduction from the outer horizons (āfāq) and the inner selves (anfus), from politics and norms — by way of intellect, self-evidence, innate constitution (al-fiṭra), natural disposition (al-jibilla), and the agreement of every religious community — as well as from the philosophers and the mathematicians. We shall further adduce evidence from the very structure of the religious duties and practices, in a manner not undertaken by the elders of the daʿwa. If my effort is found sound, that is owed to the excellence of the support of the master of the age and the time; if there is any error, it is solely from my own weakness and shortcoming, and that is what most befits me.

[6] We therefore declare that the necessity of the Imamate and of the Imams is intrinsic to natural disposition, innate constitution, nature, intellect, governance, prescription, and norms. It is obligatory in every revealed law, religion, and religious community — just as the existence of a Creator is necessary in every innate constitution, natural disposition, and intellect, in every community and religion. Yet people fell into disagreement concerning the Creator, after His necessity had been proved and His existence accepted. They drew conclusions according to their own whims, blindly imitating their forefathers and teachers, claiming that the Creator whom innate constitution and natural disposition require is in fact a substance, or the elements, or the stars, or the sun, or some other such thing. At times they ascribed to the Creator attributes that constitute likeness — that is, anthropomorphism — or something akin to polytheism, founding such claims upon judgement without authentic knowledge. So too with the necessity of the Imamate: after their commitment to an Imam, people fell into disagreement. Some insisted that the Imam, established by intellect and self-evidence, is the wise man of every age — a view resembling that of the philosophers, who call their teachers Imams, sages, the head of scholars, the master of the world, and the teacher.

[7] The followers of every religion called their past Imams prophets and sages in every cycle. Certain misguided followers of Islam, however, applied the title of Imam to usurpers, oppressive pretenders, and even to their scholars and jurists. Yet if you examine their beliefs closely, all of them affirm the existence of the Imam by necessity, and are unable to deny it. Whoever seeks salvation and guidance must therefore set aside his inclinations and desires and recognize the deficiency of every pretender to this exalted station: for such a pretender is lacking in knowledge, gnosis, piety, and every other merit or condition of the Imamate. By perceiving the pretender's want of knowledge, the triviality of his piety and modesty, and his inability to fulfil any of the Imamate's conditions, the seeker is led to deny that pretender's Imamate, until at last he attains recognition of the true Imam.

[8] We shall now adduce statements drawn from each pretender, identifying their weaknesses and limitations. We shall then turn their own principles against them and divest the Imamate from those unfit for it. We shall establish the Imamate of the true Imams with proofs and demonstrations, and shall confirm the necessity of obeying them, of entrusting our affairs to their command, and of receiving from them revelation, interpretation, and the confession of unity. All who recognize the creation realize, as creatures, their own inability and deficiency; they are compelled to deny their own divinity and, with it, the divinity of all created beings. Divine unity is thereby left pure: neither ascribing human qualities to God (tashbīh) nor stripping Him of all attributes (taʿṭīl).

[9] We further declare that God, through His subtle wisdom and far-reaching power, fashioned for the human body its principles, its causes, and its means. He manifested these in all their derivatives and in the realms of nature and their traces, in a measured manner and within a determined term. He then brought forth from them a body endowed with senses, parallel and corresponding to its principles. He made those principles and means the body's sustenance and nourishment, by which it endures, and by which its physical and perceptual reward and chastisement are determined. In the same way, He fashioned principles, causes, and means for the souls, the spirits, and the religious noetic forms, through which they acquire their spiritual sustenance, their nourishment, and their endurance. They are rewarded when they cleave to them and chastised when they sever themselves from their support and depart from their origin.

[10] God made the principles and causes of the body a witness and a proof for the principles of the soul, its derivatives, and its nourishment — so that the religious creation might be similar to, corresponding with, and parallel to the physical creation. The body manifests its perfection only through the effects of the celestial spheres together with the stars; without the stars, it could never have reached completion through the spheres alone, for the course of events is everywhere influenced by the stars. Likewise, the religious noetic forms and the everlasting souls manifest themselves and attain perfection only through the influence of the Imams. Their nourishment is the Imam's knowledge, for the Imams are the religious stars. The Prophet said: "My companions are like the stars: whichever of them you follow, you will be guided." This holds true even when they stand in the place of the prophets, who are like the celestial spheres. The course of affairs for all created beings, and their return, is therefore toward the Imams.[^cite-28]

[11] We say that the disparities in degrees of excellence (⟪ar:التفاوت التفاضل⟫) that exist among all entities, both the foundational essences (⟪ar:أصول⟫) and their derivations (⟪ar:فروع⟫), furnish the firmest evidence and proof for the affirmation of the Imamate and of the Imams. For God created entities across all genera (⟪ar:اجناس⟫) and species (⟪ar:انواع⟫), differentiated and varied in their excellence. He thereafter set for each genus and each species an upper limit (⟪ar:غایۃ⟫), a summit (⟪ar:ذرورۃ⟫), an end (⟪ar:نھایۃ⟫), and a virtue (⟪ar:فضیلۃ⟫) which no other in that category can attain. He made those genera and species either a necessity for human beings, without which their physical existence and function could not be completed, or else a source of nourishment, a medicine, an elegance, an adornment, and an indispensable tool for their existence. He thus placed in every thing a unique benefit (⟪ar:منفعۃ⟫) found in nothing else, and powerfully endowed them with a capacity which creation cannot do without, and without which it cannot continue its physical affairs — such as food and water. He likewise tested them by creating within them a need — hunger and thirst — so that they are compelled to seek out and acquire what is required to remove that need. When one cannot find the cure for such need in its own place, he seeks it elsewhere and hastens to where it may be found. So long as he abides in this physical world, he cannot dispense, even for a moment, with breath and with seeking benefit from the elements of this world, its derivations, its realms of nature, its vegetation, and its nourishment — all of which are wisdom from God and a mark of His justice. They serve as witnesses and as ephemeral, obligatory symbols for the symbolized realities, which are religious, eternal, and chosen. The benefit of the symbolized realities — the principles, the realms of nature, the nourishment, and the religious remedies — is more deserving, more worthy, and more potent than that of the obligatory symbols. The need for them is greater than the need for what is compulsory by nature, since the latter affects only the body, whereas the former reaches the spirit and the soul. The merit of the intellectual realities over the natural symbols matches the measure of the spirit's merit over the body; the merit of the symbolized over the symbol matches the merit of what is everlasting over what perishes. Where one stands deficient, the other excels. He who is deprived of the bodily things he needs will not suffer destruction by that; or if he is destroyed, it is only the destruction of a body destined in any case to perish and die. But in the absence of the symbolized religious realities, there is destruction of the soul, which is then plunged into perpetual chastisement. Were people's striving for what the soul requires equal to their striving and desire for what the body requires, none would doubt, none would stray, and none would ascribe human qualities to God — that is, fall into anthropomorphism (⟪ar:تشبیح⟫).

[12] All of God's creation, and all of its need, stands as an admonition pointing toward the symbolized religious reality. It is a witness to whoever seeks testimony from it, evidence to whoever asks for proof, and insight to whoever reflects upon it. It is a proof against those who deny the symbolized religious reality of the Imams and of the hierarchies — their knowledge and their benefit — even though they accept the proofs of benefit found in natural elements, planets, and celestial spheres. God has set up these elements, means, and natural causes as evidence so powerful that none can deny them or testify against them, none can change or alter them — unlike what they have done with the religious law and with the testimony the Prophet required of them concerning the affirmation of the Imamate and of the Imams: its necessity, devotion to the Imams, the obligation of obedience, the condemnation of those who disobey them, and the promise of reward to those who obey. In all this they followed the same path by which they altered and distorted the Qurʾān. As God has said:
⟪ar-quote:يُحَرِّفُونَ ٱلْكَلِمَ عَن مَّوَاضِعِهِۦ ۙ وَنَسُوا۟ حَظًّۭا مِّمَّا ذُكِّرُوا۟ بِهِ⟫

"They displace words from their proper places, and they have forgotten a portion of what they were reminded of."

And He also said:
⟪ar-quote:إِنَّمَا ٱلنَّسِىٓءُ زِيَادَةٌۭ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ⟫

"Indeed, postponement is only an increase in disbelief."

For this was no real forgetfulness on their part: they forgot the testimony deliberately, as if they had neither seen it nor heard it.

[13] Just as the testimony of the outer horizons (⟪ar:آفاق⟫) and the inner selves (⟪ar:انفس⟫) is compulsory (⟪ar:جبری⟫) and not voluntary (⟪ar:اختیاری⟫) — so that no one can alter or substitute it — so too the Messenger made the witness-bearing compulsory, granting the witnesses no licence to alter, change, or conceal what they testified. He threatened any who would change or conceal it with painful chastisement. God commanded that judgement rest upon the testimony of two witnesses, saying:
⟪ar-quote:أَن تَضِلَّ إِحْدَهُمَا فَتُذَكِّرَ إِحْدَهُمَا ٱلْأُخْرَىٰ وَلَا يَأْبَ ٱلشُّهَدَآءُ إِذَا مَا دُعُوا۟⟫

"If one of the two errs, the other will remind her. And the witnesses must not refuse when they are summoned."

By this He intended that the true judgement in religion — and in affirming the Imamate and the hierarchies — rests on two witnesses: one posited by law (⟪ar:شرعی⟫) and the other compelled by nature (⟪ar:طبعی⟫). If people deny the legally posited testimony, then the natural one — the horizons and the inner selves — reminds, enforces, and verifies the conventional witnesses, just as God said:
⟪ar-quote:سَنُرِيهِمْ آيَتِنَا فِى ٱلأٓفَاقِ وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ يَتَبَيَّنَ لَهُمْ أَنَّهُ ٱلْحَقُّ ۗ أَوَلَمْ يَكْفِ بِرَبِّكَ أَنَّهُۥ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ شَهِيدٌ⟫

"We shall show them Our signs upon the horizons and within themselves, until it becomes clear to them that it is the Truth."

And when the Prophet was asked, "Who is the witness of your prophethood?" he answered: ⟪ar:یشھد لی کل حجر و مدر⟫ — "Every stone and every clod of earth bears witness for me." His words pointed to the testimony of the horizons and the souls. When legal (⟪ar:شرعی⟫) and natural (⟪ar:طبعی⟫) witnesses concur, and intellect becomes the criterion that weighs them without preference or partisanship, the truth swiftly stands revealed.

[14] We turn now to mention the disparities and gradations of excellence in each foundational principle (⟪ar:اصل⟫) and in each realm of nature (⟪ar:موالید⟫), and to show how the highest limit and summit within each genus and species points to the Imam in every age and time.

[15] The sages (⟪ar:حکما⟫) among the philosophers speak of the ten categories and maintain that nothing can be mentioned or discussed except by reference to them.

Essence (⟪ar:جوھر⟫) is the foundation upon which the rest rest, and which excels them all, for the remaining nine derive their stability from it. The essence the philosophers describe is therefore a proof for the permanent, intellectual, luminous, authentic substance through which all souls are illumined, and upon which all nine depend — yet which itself has no need of any created thing, drawing benefit from nothing in the world, while everything draws benefit from it and stands in need of it. That is the Imam. All other ranks stand beneath him as the nine categories stand beneath essence.[^cite-29]

[16] Of the four basic constituents (⟪ar:العناثر⟫) from which the first composites (⟪ar:ترکیب⟫) are formed, two are active and two passive: the two active are heat and cold, the two passive are dryness and humidity. The most exalted and noblest among them is heat, from which arise luminosity, light, motion, movement, and elevation, and through which the other three are established. Heat thus holds precedence in origin and in existence: no foundation or realm of nature can be complete without it. Likewise the Imam illumines the soul, and from him proceed intellectual light (al-nūr al-ʿaqlī), motion, and elevation (al-ʿuluww). No principle or religious realm is complete without him.

Among the celestial spheres, the most lofty and supreme is the outermost, within which all the rest orbit. Likewise the Imam, within his religion, is the supreme religious sphere for all other spheres; all heavenly bodies and religious signs of the zodiac function beneath his command. Should anyone object that this is rather a symbol for the Qāʾim — since the highest principle in the world points to the First Intellect in respect to the four principles — we reply that the Imam is the Qāʾim of his own time. He is the proclaimer of his age, the one who occupies the rank of the partial Qāʾim (⟪ar:جزوی⟫). The Imam's position within his realm is that of the Universal Intellect.

[17] Among the four elements (⟪ar:الارکان⟫), the highest and the noblest is fire, the summit and peak that no other element can attain. So too the Imam, in his age, is the highest religious pillar that illumines souls. It was on this basis that Abraham — if what is reported of him is true — commanded the glorification of fire and forbade extinguishing it; but the indication was rather to glorification of the Imamate, whose light no one may extinguish through the injustice of those who falsely claim its station. The fire-worshippers fell into confusion, not knowing the symbolized reality. Of the adversaries and usurpers of the Imamate, God says:
⟪ar-quote:يُرِيدُونَ أَن يُطْفِـُٔوا۟ نُورَ ٱللَّهِ بِأَفْوَهِهِمْ وَيَأْبَى ٱللَّهُ إِلَّآ أَن يُتِمَّ نُورَهُۥ وَلَوْ كَرِهَ ٱلْكَفِرُونَ⟫

"They desire to extinguish God's light with their mouths, but God refuses except to perfect His light, even though the unbelievers may detest it."

[18] Of the many planets (⟪ar:کواکب⟫) there are differences and disparities in nobility. Their peak and summit is the sun, from which physical sight draws light, and through which all plants, animals, and newborns grow and reach completion. No plant or newborn can grow without the influence of the sun. So too the Imam is the sun of religion, by which insight is illuminated and souls are kindled with the light of guidance and wisdom, radiating the hearts of God's friends.

It is for this reason that some have proposed to glorify the sun; yet the sun-worshippers too fell into confusion, recognizing only the symbol and not the symbolized reality. It is for this reason that the Prophet pointed to the rising of the sun from the west, which signals the rising of the Imam-Mahdī in the Maghrib. Were the physical sun to depart from its usual orbit, even for the blink of an eye, the entire world would perish — something rationally and naturally impossible. The Prophet's statement could therefore be true only on the basis of esoteric interpretation; and that interpretation has now proved accurate, as we have witnessed with our own eyes by God's grace and favour.

[19] Of the twelve signs of the zodiac, four are most glorious and excellent; and among those four, one is the peak and summit in nobility. So too, among the twelve proofs (⟪ar:حجۃ⟫), the one directly connected to the Imam — the gate of gates (⟪ar:باب الابواب⟫) — is the most excellent, the noblest, and the most exalted of them all.

[20] The fourth clime (⟪ar:أقلیم⟫), which lies parallel to the fourth heaven and to the sun, is the most temperate, the most excellent, and the noblest of climes. So too the Imam is the most excellent within the hierarchy, the most glorious of them all, their peak and apex. The number four is emphasized in the four elements, the four seasons, the four humours, and the four directions.

[21] Of the melting mineral substances, the most excellent is gold, by which people transact their business: it establishes the value of valuables, is unaffected by fire or water, and never perishes. So too the Imam is the most excellent religious and intellectual substance of his age; through him the worldly and religious transactions of creation, and their well-being, reach completion.

Each melting substance has its peak in nobility. Among precious stones the finest is sapphire (⟪ar:الیاقوت⟫), and among the white, green, yellow, and red sapphires (⟪ar:یاقوت الاحمر⟫), the red ruby is the most excellent. No other jewel endures like the ruby, for it maintains its essence unaltered by fire or water; it retains its purity, radiance, and gravity, and draws traces from its source as no other substance can. Not other sapphires (⟪ar:یاقوت⟫), nor emeralds (⟪ar:الزمرد⟫), nor the carnelian (⟪ar:البیجاد⟫), nor agate (⟪ar:العقیق⟫), nor diamonds (⟪ar:والألماس⟫), nor turquoise (⟪ar:والفروزج⟫) retain these qualities. The crown among jewels is therefore the red ruby — just as the Imam is the peak among all creation of his age, unique among all the ranks of the hierarchy: among the proofs (⟪ar:حجۃ⟫), the adjuncts (⟪ar:لواحق⟫), the summoner (⟪ar:داعی⟫), the wing (⟪ar:جناح⟫), the licensee (⟪ar:ماذون⟫), and the motivator (⟪ar:مکلب⟫).

No one is able to attain the rank or station of the Imam in his age. From another vantage, the seven jewels are proofs for the seven proclaimers (⟪ar:النطقا⟫) and the seven Imams. The natural symbol is present in every age and time, and therefore the symbolized reality must likewise be present in every age. Just as the stars, the natural elements, the realms of nature, and the substances are always present, so the ranks of the hierarchy must always exist, ever exerting their influence upon souls.

[22] Of the seven grains by which human beings are fed and nourished, wheat is the peak and summit — the most balanced in nature and nobility, and the chief builder of the body. So too the Imam is the peak and summit who nourishes souls with his knowledge: he is the source of their spiritual livelihood and their life.

[23] Of the fruit-bearing trees, the peak and summit is the date palm. It shares with human beings the trait of having male and female, mating and impregnating after the manner of animals; everything in it is beneficial and nothing is wasted, and its fruit is eaten at all times, raw or cooked. The interpretation of the dream in which one plucks dates from a date palm is that the dreamer will receive benefit from a certain believer — and that believer, in truth, is the Imam, who is the head and leader of all believers.

[24] Among plants, some mild medicines have reached such refinement and excellence that they have become antidotes (⟪ar:تریاقاً⟫) for those at the brink of death. Some cure chronic diseases, while others rank lower. The peak among these medicines points to the Imam, for his words are antidotes for souls and the means of healing their diseases and afflictions — relief from death, illness, doubt, and discord. The fatal poisons and harmful agents found among medicines, by contrast, represent the opponents who forcibly usurp the station of the Imams. They destroy those who listen to them, accompany them, or incline toward them, until the Imam reaches them with the great antidote that rescues their souls from destruction. Such opponents are likewise as useless and worthless as stones. It is for this reason that God said:
⟪ar-quote:‏إِنَّكُمْ وَمَا تَعْبُدُونَ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ حَصَبُ جَهَنَّمَ أَنتُمْ لَهَا وَرِدُونَ ‎⟫

"Indeed you, and what you worship besides God, are fuel for Hell; you shall arrive at it."

⟪ar-quote:‏فَإِن لَّمْ تَفْعَلُوا۟ وَلَن تَفْعَلُوا۟ فَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱلنَّارَ ٱلَّتِى وَقُودُهَا ٱلنَّاسُ وَٱلْحِجَارَةُ ۖ أُعِدَّتْ لِلْكَفِرِينَ ‎⟫

"But if you do it not — and never will you do it — then fear the Fire whose fuel is men and stones, prepared for the disbelievers."

[25] The horse is the finest among animals, the eagle the finest among birds, and the lion the king among beasts of prey. The disparity among the various species of animals — in strength, usefulness, and capacity to accept training — and the attainment of a summit within each species, points to the fact that there is a peak within the genus of animals. By the same reasoning, there must be a peak and summit within the human species as well, and that summit is the Imam.

[26] Some plants are sweet, delicious, and appetizing, and people derive benefit from them; others are bitter, holding neither benefit nor delicacy; still others bear no fruit at all and carry thorns that wound. The sweet and pleasing ones are like the Imams: a symbol of their speech and knowledge that delights the soul and pleases the eye. The bitter, putrid, and thorny ones symbolize the leaders of the literalists (⟪ar:اہل ظاہر⟫). For this reason God said:

[40] We say further that sages and men of insight, in building the world, regard their work as resembling the actions of God in His creation of the world, its environment, and His preservation of human welfare. When they see fertile land, they recognize it will accept the planting of seeds and the growth of vegetation. When they see water in deep distant valleys or beneath the ground, they labour to draw it forth by digging canals and wells, returning the water to those lands. They see no wisdom in leaving fertile soil unused, or in leaving water unutilized — out of compassion for creatures and concern for the welfare of the world. Likewise, when the Imams see people ready to accept the seed of wisdom and knowledge — though still far from being able to receive it — they exert great effort, digging channels through preparatory religious exercises on many an occasion. People may be unaware of the reason for such exercises until they grasp their wisdom and become prepared to receive what is intended. And when the Imams cannot raise them to that level through exercise alone, they cloak the wisdom in dense symbols, in words or in deeds, so that people may first accept those symbols and gradually arrive at their meanings — at which point the Imams themselves elevate them to true understanding.

[41] It is therefore incumbent upon every believer to hold fast to the supreme leadership of the Imam of the time, to know that he is the head of the world and God's vicegerent over His creation, and to raise no objection to any of his commands, prohibitions, actions, or teachings. The believer must know that all of these are wisdom and welfare for the creation. At times the right aspect of these may seem perplexing to the believer, but its benefits will become clear to him in the end — as many other matters, whose purpose was once obscure, later become manifest and clear, and as in much of the Sharīʿa whose purpose and underlying wisdom the faithful do not perceive and so disapprove. Those whom God grants recognition of its meaning come to know the wisdom in it, just as people who see things in the world and do not understand their wisdom imagine there is no benefit in their creation, only later to realize the wisdom they cannot deny. There are many created things which people regard as harmful and destructive, in which they see no wisdom or benefit, until they grasp their wisdom and become calm and content, ceasing to protest. Whoever recognizes the Wise One (al-Ḥakīm) and acknowledges Him as wise must inevitably know and acknowledge that everything the Wise One does is wisdom — that He neither acts nor commands nor forbids nor speaks except out of wisdom. Futile action is impermissible to the Wise One; if it were permissible for Him to do, say, command, or forbid anything devoid of wisdom, that would be a vain act, which is impermissible to Him. Whoever performs an action whose doing or leaving aside makes no difference is a fool, who has departed from the domain of wisdom.

[42] Further, if it is impermissible for the Wise One to perform an action whose existence or non-existence would be the same, how can it then be possible for the Imam to perform an action whose wisdom lies elsewhere, or in its opposite, or whose welfare lies in something other than itself — when he, the Imam, is absolutely wise, whose bestowal of wisdom upon any of his servants makes them wise? Whoever performs an action devoid of wisdom and goodness acts corruptly and vainly; God forbid that one should believe such of the Imam of the time. To believe so would mean that the believer had himself departed from wisdom, and in departing from wisdom he would depart from the domain of the Imam's authority, and so depart from the faith itself. May God protect us and all believers from such doubt, ideas, or belief, and grant us contentment and unquestioning submission.

[43] We also say that freely-grazing livestock, having their own benefits and excellences, must inevitably have a shepherd to tend, guard, and protect them, to choose their pastures and watering places, and to defend them from thieves and beasts. Were there no one to undertake this, they would perish, unable to order their affairs by themselves, unable to defend themselves from beasts of prey, unable to manage things to their own good.

People are like sheep: without a shepherd, they perish. The Imam is their shepherd, who safeguards their worldly and religious affairs and protects them from worldly and religious enemies. Were the Imams to abandon that role, two people could not remain together, and not a single soul would escape chastisement. It is for this reason that people in the various lands are called subjects — for they are under the custody of their shepherd, and the shepherd in reality is the Imam, while the other claimants are wolves devouring the cattle.

[44] We say further that the world is like the human body. Were the body without a head, it would be a decaying cadaver of no use. So too, without a head, leader, or master in the world to guide its worldly and religious affairs, people would become beasts devouring one another. The world would not endure for even a single day, and there would be no wisdom in its creation. The Imam is indeed the head of the world, its leader, and its master.

[45] Further, there are disparities and gradations of excellence among the organs of the body, and the foremost and finest of them is the head: in the head is the face, by which each person is recognized and distinguished; and among the internal organs, the finest is the brain, the chief of them and the seat of intellect. So too the Imam, in his world, holds the place of the head, and in relation to the organs the place of the brain. All affairs of creation revolve around him as all the body's actions revolve around the brain. Among the faculties of man and among the spirits, the finest is the intellect; and the Imam is the Universal Intellect in the world, in and from whom all people of the world are made one.

[46] As we have noted, leadership in nature is present everywhere — among animals and birds. The stallion guards and disciplines his females; the rooster protects his fowl and disciplines them; every genus protects its young and defends its females, the weak among its kind needing benefit and learning from those above. The crane obeys its leader; the bee obeys its queen.

[47] God created the world and ordered its creatures according to this classification and disparity in degrees of excellence — varying in strength, nobility, and inferiority — so that people might know that within each species and each genus there is a peak, a summit, and a chief in nobility and merit. By the same logic, the whole of humankind must possess a peak and a summit in nobility and merit, who is their leader and guide in the world. It has therefore been shown that headship and leadership are necessary in nature and creation, as well as in natural disposition and intuition; even those who deny revelation and religion cannot deny the leadership, headship, and authority of the world, neither rationally nor by their own eyes, since it is obligatory and necessary. It follows that this leader must be the best of all, the noblest, the most perfect, the most learned, and the purest — according to the arguments we have drawn from creation and nature — and that the foremost in every category is the Imam, the chief and the head. The title "supreme leader" cannot be applied to the one who is led, nor "the excellent" to the "one preferred," nor "one who supersedes" to "one who is preceded." Moreover, all peoples of every community and religion — together with the philosophers, the atheists, and those who deny God all attributes — affirm that the leader of the world must be the best of them, the most perfect in merit, and the most knowledgeable of all. No leader in the world fits this description but the Imams.

[48] The philosophers divided politics, or authority, into three categories: governance of the individual (siyāsat al-khāṣṣa), governance of the family, and governance of the general public (siyāsat al-ʿāmma). The governance of the general is the administration of cities and provinces. Governance of one's own self consists in restraining the self from vice, bestial habits, and blameworthy lusts, and in ruling the self with the firmness of a resolute man — chastising it when it errs through reproach and repentance, and rewarding it when it does well through praise, contentment, and steadfastness. Governance of one's family consists in managing one's household and dependants by safeguarding their welfare, urging them toward virtue and seemly character, chastising those who err with rebuke and the like, and rewarding those who do well with praise and the like.

[49] Governance of the public is the rule by which a man administers cities, safeguards the welfare of livelihoods, restrains people from blameworthy morals and ill deeds, secures every station, chastises wrongdoers by reprimand, rewards those who do well by praise, and protects and orders the affairs of religion and of the hereafter. This is what they call politics or authority — yet it is found nowhere in the world: not among those who falsely claim the Imamate, nor among the rulers, nor among their scholars. This quality is found only in the Imams from the family of Muhammad, and no one but they in the world is able to undertake it.

The philosophers agree that he who cannot undertake the governance of his own self ought not undertake the governance of his family; and he who does not well manage his family does not deserve the governance of the public. But the literalists, their rulers, and their scholars lack this qualification. Those who have sat in the place of the Imams and called themselves by their titles cannot govern themselves, their families, or the people; they are those whom we see today engaged in amusement, depravity, and corruption. The qualifications the philosophers ascribe to the political leader are possessed by none of their rulers. The author of this classification clearly intended to encourage obedience to the Imam, since he described the Imam by the very qualities required of the ruler — so that people would look for a ruler answering to that description, and reject every claimant who does not match it, until they find the true Imam.

[50] We say further that since people are ignorant at birth — having no knowledge, neither knowing nor speaking by nature — they require teaching. The proof that knowledge does not arise from nature is that, were knowledge natural to people, they would naturally be scholars and would speak naturally, just as donkeys bray and dogs bark, for it is clear that these require no instruction. There is also no variation in the braying of donkeys or the barking of dogs in different parts of the world — in Hind, Sind, Rome, or among the Arabs. Were speech and knowledge natural, people would need no teaching for them; they would all be equal in that respect, with no difference between one and another, just as the braying of donkeys is the same everywhere. Since this is not the case, people need teaching: each learns from another, until at last one is reached who learned not from any human teacher but received it through divine inspiration. This deficiency in human beings and their need for teaching necessitates a teacher; and the line of teachers necessitates an ultimate end, namely the prophet and the Imam. This is among the most cogent of proofs for the affirmation of the Imamate and the prophethood.

[51] We say further that God created human beings deficient and ignorant precisely so that they might receive knowledge and wisdom. Had He left them to themselves, no obligation would have lain upon them, there would have been no work, no reward, and no punishment, and no wisdom in their creation. When God inspired prophets and Imams, giving them wisdom and knowledge, they in turn informed humankind, expounded their guidance and their path to the Truth, and imposed upon them obligations, commands, and prohibitions. Were it not for the Imams, humanity would bear no responsibility and there would be no guarantor or proof set over them. The Imams are therefore God's proofs against His creatures at all times — from the very beginning of their being held responsible, and also when they fall into disagreement. Were humankind to fall into sectarian dispute, each side adducing a verse of the Qurʾān, a tradition, or analogical reasoning, no decisive proof would stand for any against the other. Were there no Imam in the world to deliver them from disagreement, confusion, and perplexity, and guide them to the Truth, God would have no proof against creation; rather, the proof would be from the creatures against God. For they could say: "We found no one to guide us from falsehood to truth." Through the Imam, then, God's proof is established over the people, just as He said:

[52] As for the rules and proportions ascribed to the sages, the sages in truth are the Imams. From them did people receive the principles of every discipline of knowledge and wisdom — arithmetic, geometry, and the rest. Each discipline has a purpose, and an end which points to the Imam. The end of the geometric figures — squares, triangles, arcs, and all the rest — is the circle, the most perfect and best of them, the foundation from which the square, the triangle, and the arc may all be derived. When the circle is divided, a square emerges, with arcs left at the edges; and if we take horizontal and vertical lines, four triangles emerge. All other shapes are derived from the circle and contained within it. The figures stand in relation to the world and creation as follows: the circle is the proof and evidence for the Imam, who is the Circle of Religion — all the ranks of the hierarchy contained within his command and prohibition, all of them drawing benefit from him. The circle has no need of any other shape, while every other shape has need of it; just so the Imam, in his age, has no need of any other rank, while all the ranks need him, for he encompasses them all.

[53] We say further that the origin and cause of numbers is the number one. It is the most perfect of all numbers, independent of two and what lies above, even up to a thousand; whereas a thousand and what lies below it depend upon one. The Imam is the One, unique in his age and time: all ranks of the hierarchy need him, while he is independent of them all. If some imagine that the number one indicates the Creator of creatures, this is mistaken: for the number one is the cause of mathematics and the foundation of numbers, while the number two is one plus one, and three the same again. The number one is rather the Imam in his rank and station, unique, and unshared by anyone among the creatures in his age.

[54] Similarly the point, of which the mathematicians say it has no parts and is the origin of the line, the surface, and the body, is an indication of the Imam, whose rank no one can attain, who cannot be truly known, and whose attributes no one can properly describe — they can only confess their own weakness.

[55] Among all days, Saturday is the most prominent; among all months, Ramaḍān is the most excellent; and within the hours, there is one hour in which supplication is answered. Every genus and species in the world, whether innately created or conventionally posited, possesses a peak and summit in nobility and merit which none other can attain — and this points to the Imam, as we have shown. Were we to enumerate every instance proper to this chapter, the treatise would exceed its bounds. What we have set down should suffice the contemplative reader, who may now extend the analogy to the principles and branches we have indicated. We shall, however, mention briefly some points by way of adducing evidence from the structure of the obligatory religious duties themselves.

[56] In the ritual prayer — its arrangement, its prescribed standing, its direction toward the qibla, and the standing of the Imam before all the rows — there lies powerful indication for the affirmation of the Imamate. For the Prophet first commanded the Muslims to face the qibla. The word qibla derives from qabala, meaning "face to face"; whoever faces the qibla turns his face directly toward it, and that points toward the Imam. The word qabala signifies precedence, the opposite of "behind" or "after." The word Imām is taken from the same sense, and the prayer-leader is called an Imam precisely because he precedes those who pray behind him. The direction of the qibla, then, is an indication of the Imam: the Prophet commanded people to follow and obey him in every form of worship and to accept his direction. When a man, in prayer, faces what is before him, he is in truth facing the qibla which is the Imam, in all his worship and obedience to him. If an individual prays with his Imam — as the Prophet stated that the congregational prayer is equal to twenty-seven prayers offered individually — the merit of this multiplication arises precisely because the worshipper is with the Imam, facing him, following him, and led by him in intention and act. This is an indication that the whole of obedience is to be led by the Supreme Imam (al-Imām al-Aʿẓam): guided, followed, and led by him, so that obedience acquires its excellence. Then the Prophet ordered them to align in rows, that no difference might appear among them in their standing — an indication that the hearts of those led by the Imam may not differ from one another, nor their acts, nor their speech.

[57] The Prophet ordered them to follow the prayer-leader, even when the recitation, standing, or prostration were prolonged, and not to oppose him in gesture or in intention. So strict is the rule that even if the worshipper agrees only with the Imam's action but not with his intention, the prayer is not accepted. If the worshipper is negligent, the Imam removes his negligence. From this it is plain that if the obedience of a partial Imam — one who is not the absolute Imam — is rendered obligatory, and the reward of that worship multiplied, then how much more so the obedience of the Supreme Imam, who is the Imam in all revealed law and esoteric interpretation alike? And if it is not permitted to oppose the prayer-leader in any of the prayer's acts, how could it be permitted to oppose the Supreme Imam in his commands and prohibitions throughout the believer's life? The believer remains perpetually under the patronage of the daʿwa in prayer so long as he is within the allegiance (walāya) of the Imam. Should he oppose the Imam in act, intention, or decision, he annuls his prayer and steps outside the norm of the worshipper — just as the worshipper, by resolving to oppose the prayer-leader, renders his prayer, his bowing, his prostration, his standing, and his sitting all worthless and futile, and must begin the prayer anew. How much more so, then, with the obedience of the Supreme Imam, which God has rendered absolutely binding! The Prophet said: "The Imam is the one responsible" — meaning, of course, more than the prayer-leader, who himself requires someone to vouch for him. The indication is rather to the Supreme Imam, who guarantees that those led by him are purified from doubt, suspicion, confusion, and forgetfulness, and who guarantees their salvation so long as they are guided by him. The Prophet pointed to the Imam's preceding position and commanded them not to vie with him in the recitation of the Qurʾān, saying: "When he prostrates, prostrate; when he bows, bow; when he recites, listen; and when he gives the salutation, give it too."

[58] All these are indications of obedience to the Imams, of their glorification in every matter, of the permanence of their station in every age, of the necessity of obeying them, and of the submission of the command (taslīm al-amr) when the Imam transmits it to whomever he wills. Even if the only proof for the Imam and for his obedience in the Sharīʿa lay in the prayer, that would be sufficient. The Prophet explained that the Imam absolves worshippers of their negligence in prayer — meaning that the Imam relieves their slips and seeks forgiveness for them, as God said:

Rather, this provision remains in force in every age and time, for those who come to the Imam: the Imam seeks forgiveness for them, and God forgives them as He did the people of old.

[59] The Friday prayer is not accepted without the sermon delivered from the pulpit. People pray behind the Imam so that they may know that obedience to the Imam is lifted from no created being anywhere. The pilgrimage in its entirety, too, is a proof for affirming the Imamate. The pilgrimage is an intention, and an intention must be for a known thing; and when an intention is mentioned with full knowledge, it becomes a defined intention requiring no further explanation. This signifies the intention directed toward the Imam. The Kaʿba, in certain esoteric interpretations, is a proof for the Imam (ḥujjat al-imām). It was set in a barren wilderness where no civilization surrounds it, so that it can be reached only with great difficulty; placed where neither crops nor trees grow, stripped of worldly advantage — so that worshippers might know that what is symbolized by it can likewise be attained only through great effort and difficulty, and at the cost of injury from enemies. They may know, too, that aiming toward the Imam and seeking him must not be undertaken for worldly purpose, greed, pleasure, or worldly honour, but solely out of pure faith. If hardship, weariness, attacks from enemies, or fatigue befall the seeker, his heart must not contract, but he must endure with patience. He will then see and recognize this in its symbol — the pilgrimage. Its circumambulation, the touching or kissing of the Stone, the gestures, the running between Ṣafā and Marwa, the throwing of stones, the donning of white iḥrām, and the relinquishment of perfume and intercourse — all of this is an indication of the affirmation of the Imamate and of obedience to him under every condition, of seeking guidance and the protection of one's soul, and of discarding reprehensible doctrines and corrupt beliefs.

Is it any wonder, then, to observe the condition of this community? They affirm that this verse is abrogating in judgement and recitation, that it has not itself been abrogated, and that the Prophet's Sunna requires the collection of alms — yet how have they granted themselves leave to change the rule of the verse and the command of God, and to abandon the Sunna of the Prophet and the Sunna of those whose Imamate they claim? God Himself, in regard to alms and the manner of their collection, explained the position of the Imamate and the Imams: He forbade ṣadaqa to the Prophet, so that they might not accuse him of taking it; and He likewise forbade it to the Prophet's family, so that they might know that the Imam among them takes the Prophet's place in collecting the alms, and that the Imams are trustworthy in this regard.[^cite-30]

But He did not order them to distribute the ṣadaqāt; rather He commanded the Prophet to do so. Had it been permissible for people to do it themselves, it would have been more permissible in the Prophet's own age, when they were steadier in religion, more certain in faith, and more upright in conduct. How then could later generations be its trustees? And why is it that the prayer — more important than almsgiving — must be performed behind their Imams, while it is held impermissible to give the Imams alms? Why are these people deemed trustworthy in matters of bloodshed, sexual offences, and every form of property — yet not trustworthy in alms? If their claim that their Imams are untrustworthy in alms is true, then they are not Imams at all, but embezzlers; and if they are embezzlers, it is not lawful to pray behind them or to entrust to them any other duty — neither jihad nor pilgrimage nor judgements concerning sexual offences, bloodshed, and property. If, on the other hand, they are lying in their claim, then their scholars who issue legal opinions corroborating them are likewise liars — they being the witnesses to their leadership — and the testimony of liars is not acceptable. God also commanded the Prophet to purify the believers and to pray for them when He said:

Had He granted compensation for almsgiving to those present in the Prophet's time, and withheld it from those who came after — even though they need it more — that would have been an injustice. The aspect of purification, prayer, and tranquillity therefore endures, perpetually, for whoever comes to the Imam and gives the zakāt from his property, whether in public or in secret. The Imam purifies him and prays for him. Were the Imam necessary for this alone, it would suffice. His distribution of alms among those entitled rests on his knowledge of who deserves them, who does not, and when no qualifying recipient exists for a given share. The Imam is therefore the guardian of the Muslims' treasury, who distributes it according to the needs of religion and time.

[62] Jihad is one of the principles of religion, and warfare is permissible only under the banner of an Imam. No command for waging war may be established except by the Imam, since the Imam must first guard the Muslims' treasury (bayt al-māl), which lies under his charge, protect the frontiers, safeguard the Muslims' arms, and ransom Muslim captives from the hands of the unbelievers. An army may be sent forth only under a commander appointed by the Imam, or appointed by one whom the Imam has appointed. The Imam is more knowledgeable of the times for peace and treaty, and it is he who concludes treaties. When captives are taken, the Imam alone knows whom to forgive, whom to ransom, and whom to put to death. He is the most knowledgeable of how to levy land tax (kharāj) and money so that it serves the benefit and support of the Muslims. The Imam also identifies the rebel, and a party becomes the party of truth only by standing with him; the rebel being one who departs from obedience to the Imam, against whom the Imam may then wage war or conclude truce. Al-Shāfiʿī said: "Were it not for the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, we would not have known how to wage war against rebels, nor how to distinguish between the rebel and the rightful believer."

[63] Thereafter is the fifth (khums), which God assigned specifically to the Prophet's family and to the Imams in place of the alms (ṣadaqa) forbidden to them. Every benefit from alms and zakāt, and every condition thereof, is bound to the Imam. So too the festivals — the two ʿĪd feasts and the month of Ramaḍān — wherein the Imam preserves and accepts the testimony of witnesses, whether they hold that the fast's beginning and breaking depend upon sighting the moon or upon the appointment of the Imam. The two festival prayers and the Friday prayer are not accepted in congregation without a sermon (khuṭba) from the Imam and his presence, or from one whom the Imam has appointed; the minimum of four worshippers needed in congregation is reduced to two by virtue of the Imam's sermon.

[64] Then there are the legal punishments (ḥudūd): only the Imam, or one whom the Imam designates, may carry them out. The amputation of hands, flogging, stoning, imprisonment, the indemnity for injuries (arsh al-jirāḥ), and judgement concerning them can be exercised only by the Imam's order. If these alone were the proof of the Imamate in the Messenger's family, they would suffice. For the opponents themselves agree that whoever lacks authority (wilāya) may not pass judgement on the legal punishments. Hence if a man sees another unjustly slaying someone, it is not for him to kill the slayer; and if he sees a man stealing, it is not for him to cut off the thief's hand, stone him, or do the like. The principle extends so far that most agree even a slave's owner has no right to carry out legal punishments or chastisement; only the Imam may chastise and execute the ḥudūd. The legal punishments cannot be abolished, for were they abolished people would perish and lands fall to ruin. And were people allowed to execute them, everyone would set out intending to kill or strike, then claim that the slain man had killed another, or that the stolen money required this punishment. How can one who himself requires twenty legal punishments daily judge upon the ḥudūd, and how can it be permitted him to execute them? Were that allowed to him, it would equally be allowed to every oppressor and rebel — and that would mean ruin for the people. Rather, when a rebellious party executes legal punishments, destroys lives, and the Imam gains power over them, he fines them for whatever damage they wrought upon the righteous party, preventing such wrongdoers from destroying others' lives and property. They claim then that everything was done according to a legal rule enjoined upon them; but God has clarified this in His words:

Does any of the usurpers claiming the Imamate fulfil these conditions? Rather, every one of them needs those so-called jurists who are said to possess knowledge — yet who possess only its shadow. The usurpers grant them the frail trinkets of this world, appoint them governors of provinces, and permit them to sit in their public assemblies. Thus the usurpers handed the leadership of religion over to the jurists, that they might judge by their own opinions and desires — while the usurpers retained the leadership of the world for themselves, and the jurists in turn bore false witness on their behalf. They have thus disgraced themselves in unison. I cannot decide at whom I should marvel: at him who shamelessly claims the Imamate while possessing no knowledge of religion or its legal norms, so that he needs such scholars — for if they truly were scholars, and the rule and judgement rested on their words, then they, not the false claimant, would be the Imams, while he is a mere idol set up with no authority — or at him who clings to such leadership and rewards the ignorance and shame of those jurists. So in the end their Imam is like the one who is led, and the one who is led like their Imam.

[66] If their Imams lack legal judgement and authority (wilāya) for want of knowledge, and because they do not fulfil its conditions — possessing neither modesty nor integrity — then most of those who uphold the doctrine of selection (ikhtiyār) hold that when an Imam becomes unjust and immoral in this manner, his Imamate is forfeit, and in some views he must be removed. Likewise, when a judge among them betrays his trust, he must be deposed; and the legal guardian, when corrupt, loses his guardianship. Some say the community is bound to remove the Imam if he is unjust and immoral. Al-Shāfiʿī said: "The sinner has no legal guardianship": thus if such a man has a daughter, he holds no guardianship and no right to give her in marriage; the judge marries her off, while the father has no guardianship over her. Likewise, according to all these jurist-imams, the testimony of the sinner is not accepted. Thus by their own agreement (ittifāq) and consensus (ijmāʿ), their Imams are not among those who may exercise legal reasoning (ijtihād) in religion, nor among those who may give their daughters in marriage, nor among those whose testimony is accepted, nor among those who may execute the legal punishments. They have been well-known and infamous offenders, and those who remain among them tread in their predecessors' footsteps. How could one with such attributes be the Commander of the Faithful, the vicegerent (khalīfa) of the Lord of the Worlds, the Imam to whom obedience is incumbent? Moreover, if they possess neither the right of guardianship, nor of legal judgement, nor of testimony, how can those whom they appoint possess guardianship, how can the testimony of those they command be accepted, and how can their own testimony be admitted when they declare that such-and-such a person is fit for judgement, witness, and guardianship? Is there anything in the world more astonishing than these people, the contradictions of their rulings, the principles of their school, and their doctrine? Or anyone more infamous than their Imam and the one led by him?

[67] Were we to enumerate every contradiction and disgrace in their principles, our treatise would exceed its scope. In what has been said there is sufficient refutation of their Imamate and sufficient demonstration that all they have done and continue to do is falsehood, injustice, and transgression — shame and ignominy enough for them. If their Imamate is false, as we have shown, and the Imamate is in truth one of the principles of religion (uṣūl al-dīn), inevitably necessary, and to it are bound every revealed law and every norm, then the true Imamate belongs to the pure Imams from the progeny of Ṭāhā and Yāsīn, preserved from corruption, oppression, falsehood, and transgression, and purified of every defilement and reproach. What we have set down by deduction and from the religious law suffices. We shall now establish the necessity of the Imamate by reference to past cycles, and the fact that no age or period has ever been deprived of a true Imam to whom obedience is incumbent.

[68] We say that, were it acceptable for creatures to exist upon the earth without an Imam or caliph, it would have been acceptable in the time of Adam, when only the angels were present. At that time God said:

He likewise made plain that if it were acceptable to choose the Imam (ikhtiyār), the angels above all would have been entrusted with that choice; nor would He have contradicted them, for they were preserved from inclination, false desire, and craving for the world and for dominion. Forbidding the saying of "uff" implies the still more emphatic prohibition of striking or insult. God has recounted the histories of past communities and prophets so that we might know that what occurred in the cycle of Muhammad would resemble what occurred in earlier cycles. The Prophet said: "My community will experience exactly what happened among the Banū Isrāʾīl"; and again: "You will follow the footsteps of those before you, step by step, exactly so — to such a degree that were they to enter the hole of a lizard, you too would enter it."

[69] In the story of Adam there lies an indication that the Imamate and caliphate are not subject to selection or consultation. If they were, the angels would have been more fit to choose, and their counsel more proper, for they were free of jealousy and rebellion. There lies also an indication that it is not for people to declare such-and-such fit for the Imamate or not, nor to prefer one to another. God informed those who disliked Adam of their error, reproved them, rebuked them, and showed them the falsehood of their words. He recorded this in the Qurʾān precisely so that they might take warning — yet they still wrangled over the Imamate, and over those whom God and His Messenger chose for it, as the angels had wrangled. In the appointment of son after son to the Imamate, there is also an indication that the world is never devoid of an Imam. Yet people accept the Imams of Adam's age and deny them in the age of Muhammad. God, the Almighty and Glorious, has said: "Such was Our way (sunna) with the messengers We sent before you. And you will find no change in Our way"; and: "You will certainly find no change in Our way." What is meant is the sunna of the Imamate, not the sunna of religious practice — for the Prophet did indeed alter the practices of all earlier prophets concerning acts of worship (ʿibādāt), but not the sunna of the Imamate.

[70] When Noah summoned the people, out of compassion, to the ark, he knew fully well that those who refused would perish and be drowned — by their own choice and by their belief that their welfare lay elsewhere. The exposition is this: everyone who is summoned by his prophet or his Imam to a matter and refuses, denying it, is destroyed for it. The ark was none other than the Imam himself, and Noah's summoning of the people was a summons to the Imam — just as the Prophet said: "The likeness of my family among you is like Noah's ark: whoever boards it will be saved, and whoever remains behind will be drowned." Noah appointed his son Shem to the legateeship and the Imamate by designation (naṣṣ) and appointment (tawqīf), and so it continued, one after another, until the time of Abraham. The opponents accept the Imams of Noah's cycle and yet deny those in the family of Muhammad — even though the sunna has not been altered, and the people cannot do without an Imam.

[71] When Abraham appeared, he ordered the glorification of fire — if what is said of this is true — and that was an indication of glorifying the light of the Imamate (nūr al-imāma), as we have stated. Then Abraham and Ismāʿīl built the House — the Kaʿba — and commanded the people to glorify it, an indication that the Imamate would continue in Abraham's house and progeny. He urged the people to glorify the Imamate, and to venerate and obey the Imams of his line.

[72] When Moses arose, he ordered people to face Jerusalem. Since Moses had no offspring or family in whom the Imamate could continue, he pointed to Jerusalem (bayt al-muqaddas) and said in effect: "Whoever among the Banū Isrāʾīl possesses the holy spirit is your Imam and your direction of prayer." Then Jesus came, who likewise had no family or children, and is said to have commanded people to face the east. If that is true, his command meant turning toward the place where the light of the Imamate rises: whoever radiates that light shall be your Imam and your qibla. When the seal of the prophets — Muhammad — arose, he ordered people to turn from Jerusalem to the Kaʿba, which Abraham and Ismāʿīl had built, as an indication that the direction had returned to Abraham's house, the line of Ismāʿīl. This meant that the Imamate had returned to the children of Ismāʿīl, who are the children of the Prophet Muhammad. He commanded the glorification of the House and the performance of the pilgrimage to it — a command not given by any earlier prophet — as a charge to the people concerning the surpassing excellence of the Imams of Muhammad's family above all the Imams of past cycles. Can any community be more iniquitous than this one, which accepts the Imams of every prophetic cycle, affirms that the world is never deprived of an Imam, affirms that no Imam in past cycles was raised up by selection (ikhtiyār) — and yet denies the Imamate of Muhammad's family, rejects designation (naṣṣ), and calls for selection? God Himself has said: "Your Lord creates what He wills and chooses; the choice is not theirs." Were the Imamate by selection, every people would choose whom they wished. The elders of the daʿwa have already treated the dispute between designation and selection at length; we stipulated that we should add only what they did not say. What has been said suffices. We now move on to establish that the Imam must be the most excellent, the most knowledgeable, and the noblest of mankind. The virtues by which God has preferred some over others are of three kinds: knowledge, jihad, and piety. As He said: "God has preferred those who strive over those who sit, by a great reward, in degrees of rank"; and: "Say: Are those who know and those who do not know equal?"; and: "Those who have been given knowledge are in degrees of rank"; and: "The noblest among you with God is the most pious of you." As for jihad, the elite (khāṣṣa) and the Muslims at large (ʿāmma) alike know that those men never fought even a single day on their own. The wars of the Commander of the Faithful are renowned. The very disgrace of the holy war was their flight at Ḥunayn, when ʿUmar disobeyed the Prophet's order to hold the mountain and incurred the censure of the Muslims, while the Prophet and ʿAlī fought on, slaying the enemies until they were routed.

As for piety (taqwā): where there is no knowledge, there can be no piety, for piety arises from knowledge — knowing what belongs to oneself and what belongs to others. It is reported that a certain ascetic asked Muḥammad ibn al-Ḥasan, the companion of Abū Ḥanīfa, to compose a book on asceticism, and he composed instead a book on commerce. When asked about this, he replied: "Whoever does not master the jurisprudence of buying and selling — how shall he consume only what is lawful? And whoever does not consume what is lawful has no asceticism." It follows, from the Qurʾānic verses we have cited, that the Imamate belonged to the Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him, for he was foremost (sābiq) in virtue: the most knowledgeable, the most pious, and the one who fought most in God's cause.

[74] Further, the men who ruled never themselves claimed any excellence, knowledge, or piety; rather, their followers claimed it for them. Abū Bakr was the first to mount the pulpit and say: "You have appointed me, though I am not the best of you." If he was truthful in this, then those who claim surpassing excellence for him are liars; if he was lying, then a liar cannot be an Imam. Should one reply that he spoke from humility, we say that humility has its time and place. The Prophet himself spoke of his rank and station, by which God had singled him out, and did not abase himself — nor did this amount to arrogance. He said: "I am the master of the Arabs, without boasting"; "I am the master of mankind, without boasting"; "I am the most eloquent of the Arabs, without boasting."

The Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, said: "Ask me before you lose me." He also said: "Were a cushion to be folded for me to sit on, I would judge the people of the Torah by the Torah, the people of the Gospel by the Gospel, and the people of the Furqān by the Furqān."[^cite-31] Many such statements could be cited, and they constitute neither arrogance nor a negation of humility. Abū Bakr's words — "I have been put in charge of you, though I am not the best of you" — are themselves an indication that neither God nor His Messenger had given him precedence or designated him as successor by ordering him to lead the prayer or anything else. He confirmed of himself everything that might be expected of him, saying further: "Put me right." Yet correction is required only for monstrous and major sins, since lesser ones are erased by seeking forgiveness.

[75] When ʿUmar ascended the pulpit after Abū Bakr, he said: "The oath of allegiance (bayʿa) to Abū Bakr was a falta — a hasty, unconsidered decision — but God averted its evil from us. Whoever repeats the like of it, kill him." In this he refuted the very claim of the literalists that Abū Bakr's appointment by the Prophet to lead the prayer made his Imamate obligatory and indicated his Imamate; and he refuted, too, the traditions they adduce, such as the saying: "Follow the two who come after me, Abū Bakr and ʿUmar." …

[79] Now we shall mention some of the merits of the lord of the age and the time (walī al-ʿaṣr wa al-zamān), the Commander of the Faithful — though one such as I can never attain the station that would let me speak his true merits, just as God said: "They have not valued God according to His true measure." Yet I shall speak according to my small knowledge and ability, and the Commander of the Faithful is the most fit to accept the servant's apology — just as God, with the multitude of His bounties upon His servants, asked them for gratitude commensurate with the blessings, and when they could not give thanks equal to the bounties, He forgave them, being content with their saying "Alḥamdu liʾllāh." This was a lesson for His creatures: that whenever gratitude to a benefactor is incumbent on them, and they cannot give thanks equal to the bounty, they should give what they can and not abandon gratitude altogether because of their inability to render its full measure. We say further that God has made the earth radiant with His light, as He promised and announced when He said: "And the earth shall shine by the light of her Lord." That is, the earth of the outer reality shines through the justice of the Commander of the Faithful, and the earth of the inner meaning shines through knowledge and exposition.

[80] As we have shown, every species, genus, root, branch, and law has its peak and summit in nobility, merit, rank, degree, and station, which nothing else may attain. So too the Imams must have a peak, a summit, an utmost degree, which none before them has reached. That peak and utmost degree is the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī. He is the peak of every peak, the utmost limit beyond which there is no limit. The wisdom of the world, and of the animals and plants within it, appeared in the rise of human beings, and their welfare was completed through humanity; the benefit of all of them returns to mankind. And it is through the Imams that the merits of human beings have become complete, and that wisdom appears in creation, and the benefit of all human beings returns to the Imams. So too, through the Commander of the Faithful, all the affairs (umūr) of the prophets, the legatees, and the Imams have been completed, their command (amr) ordered and perfected, and perfect wisdom made manifest in their dispositions, in their laws, and in their esoteric interpretations.

He, peace be upon him, is as the head, and they are as the body; they are as the body, while the Commander of the Faithful is as the spirit. No body is complete without spirit. He is as the heart, while all who have passed away before him are as the limbs. It is through the joining of spirit and life to the heart that every limb receives its share of life and recompense — each nail, each hair, each member receiving its portion. This is the sensual knowledge, which is the recompense of action that arises from the embryo in its womb (dār ʿamalihi) during its passage through the six stages from sperm to final creation. Likewise, through the Commander of the Faithful, people gain recompense and the acceptance of life in the seventh stage. He, peace be upon him, is the one who awakens in every soul the desire to come to him, and through whom God has promised His creatures the fulfilment of reward on His Day, as God said of the Judgement: "And you will be paid only what you have earned, fairly, on the Day of Resurrection. And whoever is removed from the Fire and admitted to Paradise has indeed triumphed." God commands His creation to believe in Him and in His Day, when He said: "They believe in God and the Last Day."

[81] Just as humankind exists as the final realm of nature (mawālīd) in the world, having received all that existed in earlier realms and exceeded them by things in which other realms had no share; and just as the Imams acquired all the merits found in human beings, being their peak, summit, and quintessence, and were able to receive what is beyond the capacity of mankind, the benefit of all who preceded them coming to rest in them — so likewise the Commander of the Faithful gathers in himself all the merits of those who preceded him, all their benefit and knowledge converging upon him, and he exceeds them many times over. Just as the power and merit of the Prophet existed because he gathered in himself the merits, knowledge, and surpassing virtue of all the previous cycles, so the Commander of the Faithful, peace be upon him, gathers in himself the merits of all the prophets, Imams, and sages who preceded him in past cycles, and exceeds them in things they could not attain. Had he not risen to this station, the perfection of wisdom in their rising would not have been realized. The Commander of the Faithful is therefore the sum of everything in the worlds today — spiritual, physical, and conventional (waḍʿī). It is because of him that these different worlds were created, and all are his agents and propagators until His Day of Judgement. He is the one who arose in the world to perfect the vicegerency of the Creator within the universe of humanity. We have said that he is the perfector (mutimm) of all who passed away. Should anyone object that this description belongs only to the final Qāʾim, while the Commander of the Faithful is not the final, we reply that he is the Qāʾim of his own time and period, and that he is the Qāʾim of all who preceded him — their perfector. He is the sixth, through whom the command of the seventh will be perfected. He will accomplish what the seventh requires, just as the Prophet established what the Qāʾim would require, and so the Qāʾim becomes the perfector of what the Prophet established and arranged.

The Commander of the Faithful is, in another reckoning, the ninth. The command of the seventh, al-Muʿizz, who has passed, is perfected through the ninth, al-Ḥākim — just as the perfection of the seven members, their order and function, is manifested through nine — the four natures and the five senses — and as the perfection of the human creature is in nine months. This reckoning is based on the figure of nine. Life in the hereafter rests upon four spiritual natures and five spiritual senses, as God said: "Indeed, the abode of the hereafter — that is life, if only they knew."

[82] The Commander of the Faithful is, by yet another reckoning, the second: the order at the beginning of each cycle is perfected by the second. He is also the sixth, through whose sword God will conquer the whole world, just as He conquered through the Prophet, the sixth from Adam. The Commander of the Faithful arose in the world to perfect the vicegerency (khilāfa) of the Creator among His creatures. He deals with creation under all circumstances as God deals with His creatures in respect of their constitution and sustenance and in the abundance of His mercy. Each of those who arose before him performed a small portion of this; the Commander of the Faithful performed the whole. A vicegerent is one who carries out, among those over whom he has been set, everything the one who appointed him does. Adam, like all other vicegerents, was called "vicegerent" (khalīfa) because he was the first to undertake the vicegerency; those who came after him carried out only some of its conditions, yet were called by that name — for one who performs a part of a duty is called by its full title. The Commander of the Faithful, however, performs the worldly and the religious vicegerency in reality. When the Prophet said, "The ruler is the shadow of God upon the earth," he meant precisely the Commander of the Faithful — for the shadow of a thing imitates what casts it with all that is in it, and none deals with creatures as God does save the Commander of the Faithful. The Prophet said also: "God establishes more through the ruler than He establishes through the Qurʾān"; and the Commander of the Faithful is the one through whom God establishes what is not established through the Qurʾān.

[83] The philosophers have said that philosophy is to seek to resemble God (al-tashabbuh biʾl-Bārī), as far as human capacity allows. Seeking to resemble the Creator means dealing with mankind as God deals with them, and treating others and oneself as God treats him and treats mankind, in accordance with their nature. The philosophers stole this saying from the people of the truth (ahl al-ḥaqīqa), though none save the Commander of the Faithful has fully embodied its real meaning. All his dealings with humankind conform to God's dealings with them in the nature of the world, in sustenance, and in all other things. We shall now mention a small portion of this, fitted to the time in which we live.

[84] We begin by saying that the compassion, mercy, generosity, and forgiveness of the Commander of the Faithful (al-Ḥākim), and his forbearance toward the misconduct of people due to their lack of obedience, are beyond my power to describe. They follow upon God's own dealings with humanity, in obedience to His command and in conformity with His ways. God by His grace bestows blessings upon people, whether pious or impious, whether believer or unbeliever. Were He to bestow worldly benefit only on the believers and withhold it from the unbelievers, people's faith would become merely a means to worldly gain, not to God or His religion. Were He, conversely, to withhold from all believers and grant only the unbelievers, most belief (īmān) would revert to unbelief (kufr). He therefore granted some unbelievers and withheld from others, just as He granted some believers and withheld from others; He granted each of them at certain times and withheld at others. Likewise the Commander of the Faithful bestows worldly benefit on the Muslims and the unbelievers and withholds from them; bestows upon the believers and the hypocrites and withholds from them; granting at times and withholding at others. Were he to grant everything to the believers, their faith would lie chiefly with the world; were he to withhold from all believers at all times, they would despair and their good intentions weaken. So the Commander of the Faithful, for a season, bestowed blessings upon the believers such as no other had bestowed, and for another season withheld for their own benefit — that they might not become like those of whom God said: "Nay, indeed man becomes rebellious, considering himself self-sufficient." God gave worldly goods to the pious and the impious alike, so that the deeds of the pious would not be undertaken merely for the sake of this world, nor their restraint from immorality and disobedience merely for worldly benefit — lest they be deprived of the reward of the world to come — but rather solely for the sake of religion and of God. So too the Commander of the Faithful gives worldly benefit to the righteous and the immoral alike.

[85] God has given the temporal world to the obedient and the disobedient alike, out of His wisdom, that obedience may not be undertaken for the sake of the world nor restraint from disobedience because of it, but purely for the sake of God. Likewise the Commander of the Faithful gives to the obedient and the disobedient alike, following God's actions, that humanity's obedience to Him not be for worldly advantage, and that the reward of the hereafter not be withheld from them. God gives worldly benefit to the worshipper and the non-worshipper, lest the creatures' worship be for the sake of worldly gain. So too the Commander of the Faithful engages with his people not looking at their imperfections and disobedience, but with a merciful eye. God has given worldly goods to the simpleton and the ignorant as well as to the learned and the intelligent, and has withheld from both. So too the Commander of the Faithful gives to the simpleton and the ignorant, the learned and the intelligent alike, out of his wisdom and his virtue. Were he to give only to the learned and the intelligent and withhold from others, the acquisition of knowledge and the use of reason would be undertaken only for the sake of this world, while the ignorant and the simple would be left to despair. God has, by equal measure, given the world to the wrongdoer and the just, the generous and the miser, the noble, the depraved, and the lowly, to those endowed with every virtue and those devoid of them — granting to all in His wisdom, mercy, and grace. So too the Commander of the Faithful treats them all equally, following God's actions and His mercy toward His creatures, that each may obtain a fair share of His bounty.

[86] God has concealed the affairs of the world and the manner in which His creatures may benefit from it, and the path of approach to it on every side. He has hidden from people both the means and the actions by which worldly goods are acquired: one grows rich while another has nothing to eat; one is enriched by trade while another is bankrupted; one prospers in agriculture while another loses even his seed; one becomes self-sufficient by hunting while another is reduced to poverty. All this proceeds from a surpassing wisdom: He would not grant easy access to the world by one means while voiding all others, lest disorder pervade creation and the world be corrupted. So too is the Commander of the Faithful's dealing with humankind: he conceals the means, so that one who seeks worldly benefit by some means receives it from him, while another who seeks by the same means is denied. If men knew a single means by which worldly goods could be obtained from the Commander of the Faithful with certainty, every other means would lie futile, all who lacked that means would despair of his mercy and be deprived, and there would be no merit in any of it.

[87] So too with the Commander of the Faithful in respect of his proximity to or distance from people. Were he to dismiss the hypocrites and draw the believers near, all people would profess belief — not for the sake of religion but for the sake of his nearness. Were he to keep his distance from all the believers at every time, they would avoid him, and anxiety would seep into their hearts — either through the perceived anger of the Commander of the Faithful or through a slip on their own part. He therefore deals with them as the prevailing times and conditions require — kindling desire, sounding alarm, withholding, drawing near, granting, denying, chastising, forgiving — all in accord with the actions and qualities of God, that people may live perpetually in fear and in hope, neither feeling so secure as to grow bold nor so despairing as to lose heart. In all of this he follows God's own ways with His creation, as He, glorified is He, gives generously to all — to those who do not fear poverty or the exhaustion of wealth — so too the Commander of the Faithful gives to those who fear no want, exceeding the generosity of human beings.

[88] Whoever views the actions of the Commander of the Faithful in religion and in worldly affairs with the eye of reality will find that everything he does with creation — commanding the good, forbidding the evil, granting and denying — runs parallel to God's actions. But people are bewildered, because they view his actions through the lens of ordinary human actions and of what they have heard; they compare him to other men and other rulers, and finding what he does at variance with what they are accustomed to, they fall into perplexity, confusion, and speculation. Were they to view it in relation to God's actions with His creation, it would be easier for them. It is difficult for them only because, from Adam's time to our own, no one has ever acted in this manner; whoever among earlier figures ever did so, did only a small portion of it, as is reported of the sage Khiḍr's dealings with Moses. Moses lacked patience with Khiḍr's actions because he saw them in relation to his own action, knowledge, and intellect; had he viewed them in relation to God's actions, the matter would have unfolded differently. Another instance is what is reported of David's judgement in the matter of God's wondrous case of the owner of the vineyard and the owner of the bull. Similarly God warned in His words: "On the Day when the summoner calls them to a painful end … their eyes lowered in humiliation"; and: "They will be called to prostrate themselves while they are safe and secure, but they will not be able." It is astonishing: if they cannot endure these worldly actions, how shall they endure the affliction of religious actions and matters far harder than these? What lies at the two extremes is not found in the middle, and what lies in the middle is not found at the regions; yet people pass judgement on matters by what they see at the centre, while standing themselves at the extremities. It is for this reason they become confused, bewildered, unable to bear what they see.

[89] The gifts of other monarchs are commonly understood to be given for service, kinship, obligation, love, need, the seeking of benefit, or the warding off of harm. They may also be given out of fear of calamity, anticipation of fortune, dread of slander, hope of praise and glorification, desire of gratitude or favour, as reward to an official, or as a means of livelihood. All of this is calculated and measured. But the gifts of the Commander of the Faithful lie beyond all this; for gifts given for the reasons just named are not called generosity, virtue, or beneficence. They are either the discharge of a past obligation or an advance for some future gain. To delay the discharge of an obligation, while withholding what is due, is blameworthy and miserly; and to advance money in expectation of future advantage is not generosity or virtue. Whatever is given in connection with indebtedness is not nobility. God's gifts, glory be to Him, transcend all this — for nothing is owed by God to anyone, nor does God seek any reward from any of His creatures. So too the Commander of the Faithful: he does not give for the reasons named, nor seek any compensation in return. God said concerning the Commander of the Faithful, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib: "We desire from you neither reward nor thanks." Rather, he bestows upon them spontaneously, out of generosity, nobility, and virtue, following God's actions with His creation. Were they to view the Commander of the Faithful's actions in this manner, they would fall into no confusion. Yet some among them view him with short-sightedness, judging his actions to be akin to ordinary human deeds; others view him with exaggeration, ascribing divinity to him. The actions of the Commander of the Faithful lie beyond both. He is, in truth, the universal vicegerent of God among His servants (al-khalīfa al-kullī liʾllāh taʿālā ʿalā ʿibādihi).

[90] Just as God concealed from mankind the reasons for His creating the world, while making plain the reasons of religion and the means of attaining it, so He also made plain the ways of attaining His pleasure, clarifying them through the words of His Prophet. He gave knowledge and wisdom to those who deserved them and withheld them from those who did not; He set before them a barrier and behind them a barrier, raising up signposts of guidance and the summons of the proof and the guide to the bounty of the hereafter. He disclosed the ways of attaining it and opened its gates, so that whoever deserves it attains it, and whoever does not, misses it — in utmost wisdom. Likewise the Commander of the Faithful concealed the means of attaining worldly fortune through him, granting it both to those who deserve and those who do not. He made clear the means of attaining religion, knowledge, and wisdom, and bestowed them upon those who deserved them, neither withholding nor miserly — bestowing favours upon people more abundantly than any have ever bestowed in any age. He withheld favours from the undeserving, separating the people of truth (ahl al-ḥaqq) from the people of falsehood (ahl al-bāṭil), following God's actions and ways in governing the world and the religion, and in building up the present world and the world to come.

[91] We ask God to grant us gratitude for what He has bestowed upon us in word, deed, and intention; to grant us success in obedience to the lord of the age and the time, peace be upon him; and to give us the means of striving on his behalf with our hands, tongues, and hearts. May God open the whole world for him, and may He, through his sword and by his grace and generosity, purify the earth of polytheism (shirk), unbelief (kufr), and hypocrisy (nifāq). This servant begs and longs for the blessings of the Commander of the Faithful, and asks the supreme command (al-amr al-ʿālī) to examine what the servant has gathered and compiled, to grant him permission for what is sound and correct, and to draw his attention to whatever errs — that the servant may be grateful in the increase of his insight and certainty, and that this work may become a means of guidance and evidence for those who seek to be guided, and a proof against those who deny.

[92] Praise be to God, the praise of one grateful, mindful, and pious; and prayers upon His Messenger, our master Muhammad, and upon all his progeny.

‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏سُنَّةَ ٱللَّهِ فِى ٱلَّذِينَ خَلَوْا۟ مِن قَبْلُۖ وَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّةِ ٱللَّهِ تَبْدِيلًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُل لِّلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ يَغْفِرُوا۟ لِلَّذِينَ لَا يَرْجُونَ أَيَّامَ ٱللَّهِ لِيَجْزِىَ قَوْمًۢا بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏قُلْ فَلِلَّهِ ٱلْحُجَّةُ ٱلْبَلِغَةُ ۖ فَلَوْ شَآءَ لَهَدَىٰكُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏كُنتُمْ خَيْرَ أُمَّةٍ أُخْرِجَتْ لِلنَّاسِ تَأْمُرُونَ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَتَنْهَوْنَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ وَتُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ ۗ وَلَوْ آمَنَ أَهْلُ ٱلْكِتَبِ لَكَانَ خَيْرًۭا لَّهُم ۚ مِّنْهُمُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ وَأَكْثَرُهُمُ ٱلْفَسِقُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏مَّآ أَفَآءَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِۦ مِنْ أَهْلِ ٱلْقُرَىٰ فَلِلَّهِ وَلِلرَّسُولِ وَلِذِى ٱلْقُرْبَىٰ وَٱلْيَتَمَىٰ وَٱلْمَسَكِينِ وَٱبْنِ ٱلسَّبِيلِ كَىْ لَا يَكُونَ دُولَةًۢ بَيْنَ ٱلْأَغْنِيَآءِ مِنكُمْ ۚ وَمَآ آتَىٰكُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوهُ وَمَا نَهَىٰكُمْ عَنْهُ فَٱنتَهُوا۟ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ شَدِيدُ ٱلْعِقَابِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنزَلَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَبَ مِنْهُ آيَتٌۭ مُّحْكَمَتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ ٱلْكِتَبِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَبِهَتٌۭ ۖ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌۭ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَبَهَ مِنْهُ ٱبْتِغَآءَ ٱلْفِتْنَةِ وَٱبْتِغَآءَ تَأْوِيلِهِۦ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُۥٓ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ وَٱلرَّسِخُونَ فِى ٱلْعِلْمِ ۗ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِۦ كُلٌّۭ مِّنْ عِندِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّآ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَبِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِذَا جَآءَهُمْ أَمْرٌۭ مِّنَ ٱلْأَمْنِ أَوِ ٱلْخَوْفِ أَذَاعُوا۟ بِهِۦ ۖ وَلَوْ رَدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ وَإِلَىٰٓ أُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنْهُمْ لَعَلِمَهُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَسْتَنۢبِطُونَهُۥ مِنْهُمْ ۗ وَلَوْلَا فَضْلُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَتُهُۥ لَٱتَّبَعْتُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَنَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ إِبْرَهِيمُ رَبِّ ٱجْعَلْ هَذَا ٱلْبَلَدَ آمِنًۭا وَٱجْنُبْنِى وَبَنِىَّ أَن نَّعْبُدَ ٱلْأَصْنَامَ ‎ ‏رَبِّ إِنَّهُنَّ أَضْلَلْنَ كَثِيرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلنَّاسِ ۖ فَمَن تَبِعَنِى فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنِّى ۖ وَمَنْ عَصَانِى فَإِنَّكَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎ ‏رَّبَّنَآ إِنِّىٓ أَسْكَنتُ مِن ذُرِّيَّتِى بِوَادٍ غَيْرِ ذِى زَرْعٍ عِندَ بَيْتِكَ ٱلْمُحَرَّمِ رَبَّنَا لِيُقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ فَٱجْعَلْ أَفْـِٔدَةًۭ مِّنَ ٱلنَّاسِ تَهْوِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ وَٱرْزُقْهُم مِّنَ ٱلثَّمَرَتِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَشْكُرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَٓئِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌۭ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةًۭ ۖ قَالُوٓا۟ أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ ٱلدِّمَآءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ قَالَ عِيسَى ٱبْنُ مَرْيَمَ يَبَنِىٓ إِسْرَٓئيلَ إِنِّى رَسُولُ ٱللَّهِ إِلَيْكُم مُّصَدِّقًۭا لِّمَا بَيْنَ يَدَىَّ مِنَ ٱلتَّوْرَىٰةِ وَمُبَشِّرًۢا بِرَسُولٍۢ يَأْتِى مِنۢ بَعْدِى ٱسْمُهُۥٓ أَحْمَدُ ۖ فَلَمَّا جَآءَهُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ قَالُوا۟ هَذَا سِحْرٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِذْ يَرْفَعُ إِبْرَهِیمُ ٱلْقَوَاعِدَ مِنَ ٱلْبَيْتِ وَإِسْمَعِيلُ رَبَّنَا تَقَبَّلْ مِنَّآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلسَّمِيعُ ٱلْعَلِيمُ ‎ ‏رَبَّنَا وَٱجْعَلْنَا مُسْلِمَيْنِ لَكَ وَمِن ذُرِّيَّتِنَآ أُمَّةًۭ مُّسْلِمَةًۭ لَّكَ وَأَرِنَا مَنَاسِكَنَا وَتُبْ عَلَيْنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَإِنَّهُۥ لَذِكْرٌۭ لَّكَ وَلِقَوْمِكَ ۖ وَسَوْفَ تُسْـَٔلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَتَقَلُّبَكَ فِى ٱلسَّجِدِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ رَجُلٌۭ مُّؤْمِنٌۭ مِّنْ آلِ فِرْعَوْنَ يَكْتُمُ إِيمَنَهُۥٓ أَتَقْتُلُونَ رَجُلًا أَن يَقُولَ رَبِّىَ ٱللَّهُ وَقَدْ جَآءَكُم بِٱلْبَيِّنَتِ مِن رَّبِّكُمْۖ وَإِن يَكُ كَذِبًۭا فَعَلَيْهِ كَذِبُهُۥۖ وَإِن يَكُ صَادِقًۭا يُصِبْكُم بَعْضُ ٱلَّذِى يَعِدُكُمْۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى مَنْ هُوَ مُسْرِفٌۭ كَذَّابٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقَالَ لَهُمْ نَبِيُّهُمْ إِنَّ آيَةَ مُلْكِهِۦٓ أَن يَأْتِيَكُمُ ٱلتَّابُوتُ فِيهِ سَكِينَةٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّكُمْ وَبَقِيَّةٌۭ مِّمَّا تَرَكَ آلُ مُوسَىٰ وَآلُ هَرُونَ تَحْمِلُهُ ٱلْمَلَٓئِكَةُ ۚ إِنَّ فِى ذَلِكَ لَآيَةًۭ لَّكُمْ إِن كُنتُم مُّؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقَرْنَ فِى بُيُوتِكُنَّ وَلَا تَبَرَّجْنَ تَبَرُّجَ ٱلْجَهِلِيَّةِ ٱلْأُولَىٰۖ وَأَقِمْنَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَآتِينَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَأَطِعْنَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۚۥٓ إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ ٱلرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ ٱلْبَيْتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمْ تَطْهِيرًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَقُلِ ٱعْمَلُوا۟ فَسَيَرَى ٱللَّهُ عَمَلَكُمْ وَرَسُولُهُۥ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۖ وَسَتُرَدُّونَ إِلَىٰ عَلِمِ ٱلْغَيْبِ وَٱلشَّهَدَةِ فَيُنَبِّئُكُم بِمَا كُنتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلَقَدْ ذَرَأْنَا لِجَهَنَّمَ كَثِيرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْجِنِّ وَٱلْإِنسِ ۖ لَهُمْ قُلُوبٌۭ لَّا يَفْقَهُونَ بِهَا وَلَهُمْ أَعْيُنٌۭ لَّا يُبْصِرُونَ بِهَا وَلَهُمْ آذَانٌۭ لَّا يَسْمَعُونَ بِهَآ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ كَٱلْأَنْعَمِ بَلْ هُمْ أَضَلُّ ۚ أُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْغَفِلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلَوْ يُؤَاخِذُ ٱللَّهُ ٱلنَّاسَ بِمَا كَسَبُوا۟ مَا تَرَكَ عَلَىٰ ظَهْرِهَا مِن دَآبَّةٍۢ وَلَكِن يُؤَخِّرُهُمْ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّۭىۖ فَإِذَا جَآءَ أَجَلُهُمْ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ بِعِبَادِهِۦ بَصِيرًۢا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلِلَّهِ ٱلْأَسْمَآءُ ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ فَٱدْعُوهُ بِهَا ۖ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلَّذِينَ يُلْحِدُونَ فِىٓ أَسْمَٓئِهِۦ ۚ سَيُجْزَوْنَ مَا كَانُوا۟ يَعْمَلُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَلْتَكُن مِّنكُمْ أُمَّةٌۭ يَدْعُونَ إِلَى ٱلْخَيْرِ وَيَأْمُرُونَ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَيَنْهَوْنَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ ۚ وَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَا نُرْسِلُ ٱلْمُرْسَلِينَ إِلَّا مُبَشِّرِينَ وَمُنذِرِينَ ۖ فَمَنْ آمَنَ وَأَصْلَحَ فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن رَّسُولٍ إِلَّا لِيُطَاعَ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَلَوْ أَنَّهُمْ إِذ ظَّلَمُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَهُمْ جَآءُوكَ فَٱسْتَغْفَرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَٱسْتَغْفَرَ لَهُمُ ٱلرَّسُولُ لَوَجَدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابًۭا رَّحِيمًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَا مِن قَبْلِكَ إِلَّا رِجَالًۭا نُّوحِىٓ إِلَيْهِمْ ۚ فَسْـَٔلُوٓا۟ أَهْلَ ٱلذِّكْرِ إِن كُنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَقُولُ آمَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ وَبِٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ وَمَا هُم بِمُؤْمِنِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَنَادَىٰٓ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ أَصْحَبَ ٱلنَّارِ أَن قَدْ وَجَدْنَا مَا وَعَدَنَا رَبُّنَا حَقًّۭا فَهَلْ وَجَدتُّم مَّا وَعَدَ رَبُّكُمْ حَقًّۭا ۖ قَالُوا۟ نَعَمْ ۚ فَأَذَّنَ مُؤَذِّنٌۢ بَيْنَهُمْ أَن لَّعْنَةُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَى ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذِينَ يَصُدُّونَ عَن سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَيَبْغُونَهَا عِوَجًۭا وَهُم بِٱلْآخِرَةِ كَفِرُونَ ‎ وَبَيۡنَہُمَا حِجَابٌ۬‌ۚ وَعَلَى ٱلۡأَعۡرَافِ رِجَالٌ۬ يَعۡرِفُونَ كُلاَّۢ بِسِيمَٮٰهُمۡ‌ۚ وَنَادَوۡاْ أَصۡحَـٰبَ ٱلۡجَنَّةِ أَن سَلَـٰمٌ عَلَيۡكُمۡ‌ۚ لَمۡ يَدۡخُلُوهَا وَهُمۡ يَطۡمَعُونَ ‏ وَإِذَا صُرِفَتْ أَبْصَرُهُمْ تِلْقَآءَ أَصْحَبِ ٱلنَّارِ قَالُوا۟ رَبَّنَا لَا تَجْعَلْنَا مَعَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّلِمِينَ ‎ ‏وَنَادَىٰٓ أَصْحَبُ ٱلْأَعْرَافِ رِجَالًۭا يَعْرِفُونَهُم بِسِيمَىٰهُمْ قَالُوا۟ مَآ أَغْنَىٰ عَنكُمْ جَمْعُكُمْ وَمَا كُنتُمْ تَسْتَكْبِرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَيَقُولُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَسْتَ مُرْسَلًۭا ۚ قُلْ كَفَىٰ بِٱللَّهِ شَهِيدًۢا بَيْنِى وَبَيْنَكُمْ وَمَنْ عِندَهُۥ عِلْمُ ٱلْكِتَبِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَيَقُولُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَوْلَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْهِ آيَةٌۭ مِّن رَّبِّهِۦٓ ۗ إِنَّمَآ أَنتَ مُنذِرٌۭ ۖ وَلِكُلِّ قَوْمٍ هَادٍ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِى نَزَّلَ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۢ بِقَدَرٍۢ فَأَنشَرْنَا بِهِۦ بَلْدَةًۭ مَّيْتًۭا ۚ كَذَلِكَ تُخْرَجُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏وَٱلَّذِينَ إِذَا ذُكِّرُوا۟ بِـَٔايَتِ رَبِّهِمْ لَمْ يَخِرُّوا۟ عَلَيْهَا صُمًّۭا وَعُمْيَانًۭا ‎ ‏وَٱلَّذِينَ يَقُولُونَ رَبَّنَا هَبْ لَنَا مِنْ أَزْوَجِنَا وَذُرِّيَّتِنَا قُرَّةَ أَعْيُنٍۢ وَٱجْعَلْنَا لِلْمُتَّقِينَ إِمَامًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَعْمَلُونَ لَهُۥ مَا يَشَآءُ مِن مَّحَرِيبَ وَتَمَثِيلَ وَجِفَانٍۢ كَٱلْجَوَابِ وَقُدُورٍۢ رَّاسِيَتٍۚ ٱعْمَلُوٓا۟ آلَ دَاوُۥدَ شُكْرًۭاۚ وَقَلِيلٌۭ مِّنْ عِبَادِىَ ٱلشَّكُورُ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَوْمَ نَدْعُوا۟ كُلَّ أُنَاسٍۭ بِإِمَمِهِمْ ۖ فَمَنْ أُوتِىَ كِتَبَهُۥ بِيَمِينِهِۦ فَأُو۟لَٓئِكَ يَقْرَءُونَ كِتَبَهُمْ وَلَا يُظْلَمُونَ فَتِيلًۭا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ إِن كُنتُمْ فِى رَيْبٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْبَعْثِ فَإِنَّا خَلَقْنَكُم مِّن تُرَابٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن نُّطْفَةٍۢ ثُمَّ مِنْ عَلَقَةٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن مُّضْغَةٍۢ مُّخَلَّقَةٍۢ وَغَيْرِ مُخَلَّقَةٍۢ لِّنُبَيِّنَ لَكُمْ ۚ وَنُقِرُّ فِى ٱلْأَرْحَامِ مَا نَشَآءُ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّۭى ثُمَّ نُخْرِجُكُمْ طِفْلًۭا ثُمَّ لِتَبْلُغُوٓا۟ أَشُدَّكُمْ ۖ وَمِنكُم مَّن يُتَوَفَّىٰ وَمِنكُم مَّن يُرَدُّ إِلَىٰٓ أَرْذَلِ ٱلْعُمُرِ لِكَيْلَا يَعْلَمَ مِنۢ بَعْدِ عِلْمٍۢ شَيْـًۭٔا ۚ وَتَرَى ٱلْأَرْضَ هَامِدَةًۭ فَإِذَآ أَنزَلْنَا عَلَيْهَا ٱلْمَآءَ ٱهْتَزَّتْ وَرَبَتْ وَأَنۢبَتَتْ مِن كُلِّ زَوْجٍۭ بَهِيجٍۢ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَكُونُوا۟ مَعَ ٱلصَّدِقِينَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يَٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ وَأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ ۖ فَإِن تَنَزَعْتُمْ فِى شَىْءٍۢ فَرُدُّوهُ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ إِن كُنتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْآخِرِ ۚ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلًا ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏يُخَدِعُونَ ٱللَّهَ وَٱلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا۟ وَمَا يَخْدَعُونَ إِلَّآ أَنفُسَهُمْ وَمَا يَشْعُرُونَ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلنَّارُ يُعْرَضُونَ عَلَيْهَا غُدُوًّۭا وَعَشِيًّۭاۖ وَيَوْمَ تَقُومُ ٱلسَّاعَةُ أَدْخِلُوٓا۟ آلَ فِرْعَوْنَ أَشَدَّ ٱلْعَذَابِ ‎⟫ ⟪ar:‏ٱلنَّبِىُّ أَوْلَىٰ بِٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ مِنْ أَنفُسِهِمْ ۖ وَأَزْوَجُهُۥٓ أُمَّهَتُهُمْ ۗ وَأُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَرْحَامِ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلَىٰ بِبَعْضٍۢ فِى كِتَبِ ٱللَّهِ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَٱلْمُهَجِرِينَ إِلَّآ أَن تَفْعَلُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰٓ أَوْلِيَآئِكُم مَّعْرُوفًۭا ۚ كَانَ ذَلِكَ فِى ٱلْكِتَبِ مَسْطُورًۭا ‎⟫
