## The Devil's Deception and Its Effect Upon the Worshipper's Devotion

Abu Hurairah relates that there lived among the Children of Israel a devoted worshipper who had withdrawn to the forest, where he spent his days in prayer at his place of worship. One day the adversary (Iblis) came to him disguised as a woman and knocked upon his door. When the worshipper asked who stood without, she replied: "I am an Arab woman, traveling by night. I seek shelter in your sanctuary." The worshipper, mindful of propriety, declined: "It is not fitting for me to harbour a woman." Yet the deceiver pressed upon him with cunning words: "If a wild beast should tear me to pieces at your threshold tonight, will you not answer for my blood before Allah on the Day of Resurrection?"

Seized by fear and the weight of this threat, the worshipper relented and opened the door. No sooner had the woman entered than she complained of bitter cold and begged him to kindle a fire. As the flames rose and illuminated the chamber, the worshipper beheld before him a woman of extraordinary beauty, adorned with jewels that caught the firelight. In that moment, the devil whispered her seduction: "O servant of God, I am a virtuous and veiled woman without a husband. Let us take Allah as our witness and enter into marriage, that we might worship together in this sanctuary." The worshipper, dazzled by her appearance and overcome by desire, consented, saying only, "It is permissible." Thus did he bind himself through a false oath, and the two lay together.[^cite-1]

When the act was finished, the woman's true form emerged—ancient and hideous—and she laughed with scorn: "Did you believe yourself safe from me, O self-deceived one? Did you imagine yourself superior to the children of Adam? Is your sanctuary more secure than the Garden of Paradise itself? I deceived Adam in that blessed place—what power have you or your cell to withstand me?"

The worshipper fell into profound remorse and despair. Yet from the unseen came a voice calling to him: "O cursed one! Your entire work is ruined! Rise now and begin anew!"

**Statement**

Marriage requires witnesses before Allah and the community. Though marriage and unlawful union are outwardly the same act, marriage is distinguished by its public declaration and divine permission, whereas adultery is committed in secret and without sanction. Therefore in this narrative, the worshipper's union—sealed only by Allah as witness—constituted a transgression, and his presumed marriage was in truth no marriage at all.

## The Ruler and the Saint

Among the Children of Israel there dwelt an elderly jurist (mujtahid) of great piety, who fasted each day and broke his fast with only seventy-one grains of barley. When the governor of the city heard of him, he said to his courtiers: "Surely this is a man of exceptional piety, one whose prayers are answered ⟪ar:مستجاب الدعاء⟫. I am resolved to visit him tomorrow and beg his intercession that Allah grant me a good end and Paradise."

News of the governor's intended visit reached the worshipper. Immediately he ordered his servants to prepare fine oil-laden breads, coloured dishes, and sweets; he commanded that pomegranate syrup be served in delicate and beautiful vessels. When the governor arrived at the appointed hour, tables laden with blessings were set before him. The worshipper ate and drank with relish before his guest, consuming the pomegranate syrup as though it were wine. The governor beheld this spectacle with astonishment and growing dismay. When the worshipper invited him to partake, the ruler saw clearly the opposite of all he had heard of asceticism and piety. Seeing the abundance spread before him and the pitcher raised to lips, the governor's heart filled with indignation. He ate nothing, drank nothing, and departed without a word.[^cite-2]

No sooner had the governor departed than the worshipper commanded his servant: "Take all this food to the poor and needy in the neighbouring village and distribute it among them." The servant asked in bewilderment: "Master, why have you done this? What wisdom guides such an action?" The worshipper replied: "I feared that witnessing my asceticism might kindle admiration in his heart. Should such regard take root, he would return again and again, and with time my own heart might grow inclined toward the world and its pleasures. Thus would I be deceived and turned from the purpose for which Allah created me. Therefore I acted as I did—not from malice, but to ensure I should never see his face again, nor meet with any worldly person."

## How Many Virtues Come Clothed in the Garb of Evil

There lived a worshipper among the Children of Israel on the crest of a mountain, in his place of worship. One day a woman passed by, and seeing her, the worshipper's heart was seized by desire. Under the pretence of conversation, he ventured from his sanctuary—but the moment he stepped forth, he perceived that Satan himself was drawing him toward the woman as one drives a camel by the rope through its nostrils.

The worshipper, in terror of himself, returned and sealed the door of his sanctuary with stones, binding himself beneath a weight of guard. Day and night he laboured in prayer. When hunger weakened his limbs, he would sit; when sitting became impossible, he lay upon the ground and prayed in that posture. And he said to himself continually: "Had my belly not been full that day, desire would never have stirred within me, and I would not have turned to follow her."[^cite-3]

It is said that he died in this condition of self-mortification, and when his body was washed and shrouded for burial, seventy thousand angels stood in prayer over him. Ka'b al-Akhbar has said: "Know that many sins become blessings in disguise, for through the anguish of recognizing them, a person turns with renewed resolve toward the path of righteousness."

## The Punishment of the Believer in This World

Ten dissolute men of Israel dwelt together in one village, spending their nights in drunkenness, feasting on meat and wine. Yet Allah, in His mercy, granted repentance to one among them. This man left the village, sought out a sanctuary, and began a continuous fast. At the time of breaking his fast, he consumed only twenty-one olive pits. When word of his piety spread throughout the city, his former companions grew jealous. They conspired together and paid a shameless woman fifty dirhams to seduce the repentant worshipper—if not to commit adultery, then at least to spend a night in his sanctuary. One of them said: "We shall come to visit him at dawn. When we find you there, we shall ask what you are doing in this worshipper's sanctuary. You will answer: 'I have dwelt here for a long time, and it is he who sustains and feeds me. I carry his child.'"

According to this vile conspiracy, the deceitful woman came to the sanctuary and enacted the deception as instructed. She approached the door and began to humble herself. The worshipper opened to her, and she entered, beseeching him to light a fire against the bitter cold. When he kindled the flame, she removed her veil, but the worshipper turned his gaze away. Undeterred, she attempted another stratagem: "O worshipper, I am afflicted with a disease of the spine. I have dreamed three nights running that you touched my bare back and healed me. I beg you, treat me." With that, she lay down and exposed her back.

The noble worshipper came near her and first thrust his left hand into the blazing fire, then placed his right hand upon her back and began to rub the afflicted place. His left hand, burned by the flames, swelled from shoulder to chest—yet he made no cry, no complaint.[^cite-4]

In the morning, according to the conspiracy, the worshipper's former companions arrived with a group of elders and learned men, saying: "Come, let us visit our old friend and see how his worship prospers." When they arrived, they saw the woman sitting beside the worshipper. The elders, astonished, demanded of the woman: "Why do you dwell in this sanctuary with this ascetic?" She wept and answered: "I live here. He sustains me and nourishes me, and I am with child by him."

Upon hearing this, all present seized both the woman and the worshipper and brought them to the city of the ruler. When the governor questioned the woman under oath, she persisted in her falsehood. He ordered the worshipper flogged with one hundred lashes, cast him into a dark prison, and commanded that his sanctuary be burned to the ground. All was done according to his word.

Yet Allah revealed to His Prophet: "Go and inform the ruler of the city of the true events. Command him to release My servant and rebuild his sanctuary, or I shall destroy his city." Hearing the Prophet's words, the ruler summoned the conspirators and put them to death, burned the woman alive, and rebuilt the sanctuary of the worshipper. Then the ruler asked the Prophet: "What wisdom lay hidden in this trial? Why did Allah permit such suffering to befall his pious and innocent servant?"

The Prophet answered with the revelation of Allah: "There remained upon the worshipper a single sin, small though it was. According to the justice of Allah, it had to be cleansed. I desired to purify him of every transgression, that he might stand before Me utterly spotless and unburdened. By My glory and majesty, I swear: whenever a believer's appointed time draws near, I do not remove him from this world until every sin he has committed has been atoned for through suffering—whether through illness, through loss of wealth, or through worldly hardship and calamity. I cleanse My servants of sin in this life itself, that they may meet Me purified."

## The Forgiveness of the Disobedient Servant

There was among the Children of Israel a servant given to disobedience. So weary did the people grow of his transgressions that they drove him out from their settlement—an expulsion tantamount to a sentence of death. He wandered into the wilderness. After many days his provisions were exhausted, and hunger and thirst consumed him. He wept aloud in his agony, drawing near to death. When he felt the approach of the Angel of Death, he looked to his right—but none came. He looked to his left—but none appeared. Then he raised his eyes to the heavens with utmost helplessness, tears of remorse streaming down his face, and cried out:

⟪ar-quote:یا من لا تضرہ الذنوب ولا تنقصہ المغفرۃ⟫

"O Thou to whom sins bring no harm, and whose mercy knows no diminishment! All have abandoned me in my helplessness. Have pity upon me. I come to Thee. Do not forsake me! I have heard from Thy righteous servants that Thou art the Most Merciful, the Most Forgiving. Abandon me not!"

With these words he expired. Allah then revealed to Moses: "A friend of Mine has died in the wilderness. Go and arrange for his funeral rites. Announce to the people that whoever attends his burial shall also receive My mercy." Hearing this, the people rushed toward the forest. When they arrived, they recognized the corpse as that of the man they had cast out—the very man whom they had deemed a sinner unworthy of fellowship. They said to Moses: "Is this the friend of Allah? This is the wretch whom we expelled from our city!" 

Moses, troubled, turned to Allah in prayer: "Lord, the people speak thus, yet Thou callest him Thy friend."

And Allah answered: "O Moses! They speak truth, and I speak truth also. It was as they say—yet when death drew near him, he looked to the right and found none. He looked to the left and found none. Then he looked toward Me in his desolation, and My dignity would not permit Me to abandon him whom all others had forsaken. O Moses! By My honour: had he asked forgiveness from the entire world in that moment, I would have forgiven him through the tears he shed."

## The Liar Can Never Be a True Worshipper

Among the Children of Israel dwelt an aged worshipper upon the crest of a mountain. One day another worshipper came to visit him. The elder offered the visitor hot wheat bread to eat. The guest asked: "When did you bake this bread? It is still warm and appears quite fresh." The old man replied: "By Allah, I have kept this bread for one hundred and six years. Each night I eat a single piece, yet it returns to its original state and remains warm as though freshly baked. Its taste has not changed to this day. Such is the power of Allah."

Then the visitor said: "I have a question for you about sincerity. Answer me quickly, for the hour grows late—the sun sets." The old man looked up. The sun blazed brilliantly upon the mountain's peak. He glared at the visitor and said in anger: "Alas! You lie against Allah and yet claim to be a worshipper! How did you lie?" asked the guest. He replied: "You said the sun is setting, yet it shines bright as day! O misguided one! Depart from here at once. Your words are an abomination, and I cannot bear to hear them!"

(*Hadiths of the Children of Israel, translated by Mullah Amiruddin*)

## The Servant Consumed with Shame

There lived a man among the Children of Israel who, when death approached, made a covenant with his children: "Burn my body after my death and scatter my ashes to the winds." His children fulfilled his will, burning the corpse and dispersing the ashes in the air.

Allah, in His power, gathered those ashes in an instant and restored the man to life. He sent an angel to ask him: "Why did you command such a thing done to your body?"

The man answered: "Because throughout my life I was unable to worship Him ⟪ar:کما حقہ⟫as is His due. I could not bear to stand before Him out of shame." 

Allah said: "Admit this servant of Mine to Paradise! By My honour and majesty, I shall not cast into Hell any soul that stands in awe and shame before Me."

## Giving Shelter to One's Neighbour

It is related that Hazrat Musa (peace be upon him) was seated when suddenly two birds came flying toward him. One was white; the other was a hawk intent upon hunting the white bird. The smaller bird sought refuge with Moses, who concealed it in his sleeve. The hawk protested to Moses: "Do not prevent me from my sustenance. Give me the bird." Moses offered: "Eat the flesh of my goat today." The hawk refused: "I do not desire goat meat." He said: "Then eat from my thigh." The hawk persisted: "I will eat from your cheeks." Moses, compelled by the creature's stubbornness, lay down. The hawk alighted upon his face and began to tear out his eyes, saying: "I shall eat your eyes first, then your flesh." Moses cried: "Take one eye, but spare the other!" Thus the hunter gained his sustenance, and both birds flew away together.

The prophet Joshua, who witnessed this trial, asked in astonishment: "O Prophet! Why did you sacrifice your eye for a mere bird?" Moses replied: "O Joshua, have you not heard the verses Allah revealed in the Torah concerning the rights of one's neighbour? This bird sought my protection and took refuge with me—therefore I gave him shelter."[^cite-5]

After a time, the bird and the hawk returned. The white bird said: "I am Gabriel, who came to you in this form." The other added: "I am your brother Michael, sent as the hawk. Our Lord commanded us to test you, to see whether you would shelter the distressed. We have seen that you are indeed a good neighbour and a faithful refuge."

## The Self-Pleased Shall Not Attain Deliverance

Prophet Moses and his companions were journeying through a forest. As they travelled, Moses observed green vegetation crowning a mountain peak and said to his companion: "Wait here. I must see what manner of plant or grass grows there." He ascended and beheld a very old man in prostration, his face upon the earth, tears flowing like a stream. With the water of these tears, vegetation had grown upon the soil.

Moses approached and asked: "O Shaykh, how long have you remained in this place and state?" He replied: "For six hundred years!" Moses was moved to compassion and interceded with Allah: "O Lord, have mercy upon this aged man. Grant him peace and patience in his heart!"

Allah answered: "I shall never show him mercy! Let him weep for a thousand years—even then I shall not pity him." When Moses asked the reason, Allah said: "O Moses! This man is afflicted with self-conceit (ʿujb). He takes pride in his deeds and believes his works so numerous that he shall be saved by their excess alone, freed from My punishment through the abundance of his righteousness. Know two things in him: first, he considers all others inferior to himself; second, he denies the intercession of the Ahl al-Bayt (peace be upon them) and their station. I swear by Myself: I shall never accept the deed of such a self-conceited man, even if he worshipped throughout the age of the world itself."

## Charity Postpones Death

Allah revealed to Moses that he should go and take the life of a certain man. Moses set out toward him, but before he could reach the man's dwelling, a beggar came before him, and the man gave him bread and alms. When Moses arrived at the man's house, he did not find him—for Allah had postponed his death because of his charity and generosity.

Moses searched for him many days, yet each morning the man gave bread in charity to one or another, and thus remained hidden from the Prophet's sight. It is said that Moses roamed for twenty-four years at Allah's command, never discovering the man. Then, one day, by chance the man forgot to give alms, and Moses saw him. According to Allah's command, Moses took his life.

Moses then said to Allah: "O my Lord! Thou knowest that for twenty-four years I have not ceased to seek him for a single day, yet never did I behold him. Today he appeared by chance, and I executed Thy command. What was the reason that he remained hidden all those years while dwelling in this place?"

Allah replied: "O Moses! All those years he gave bread to a poor person each morning. His charity protected him from death and from thy sight. Today he neglected this custom, and thou saw him and took his life." 

Moses asked: "O Lord! Does charity prevail over Thy judgment and nullify it?" 

Allah said: "Yes, Moses! Charity cools My anger and wrath as water cools fire."[^cite-6]

## Sincerity in Action Is a Cause of Deliverance

In the time of the Prophet David (peace be upon him), there was a believing woman who performed her prayers at their appointed times and never delayed their observance. One day, as she was baking bread, she perceived that the hour had grown late, the sun had set, and the time of prayer was upon her. Immediately she ceased all work, left her child near the oven, and stood in prayer. The accursed Satan whispered to her heart: "Look! Your bread is burning!" She answered: "I care not. If it burns, let it burn. I shall not abandon my prayer!" Satan whispered yet more insistently: "Your son has fallen into the oven and is burning!" But she said: "If he burns, let him burn. I shall not break my prayer or diminish my devotion." Thus she completed her prayer with full presence of heart and humility.

When she came to the oven after prayer, she found her child sitting upon the red coals, playing with them and placing them in his mouth as toys—yet the embers did not burn his flesh or tongue. And when she withdrew the bread, she saw it was not charred but perfectly baked, golden as saffron, without a mark of burning anywhere upon it.[^cite-7]

When her husband returned home, his wife presented him with the food. Seeing the fine saffron-coloured bread, he was delighted. As he ate, he tasted sugar and honey in every bite. He asked: "Where did this bread come from?" His wife recounted the entire story. The husband doubted her account—he could not believe his wife possessed such virtue that fire would change its nature for her.

He lit the stove until it glowed, then commanded his wife to enter it. The woman obeyed and sat within. Her husband sealed the opening with a stone. For many hours, from noon until the afternoon prayer, the woman sat in that furnace. When at last he opened it, he found his wife sitting in comfort and tranquility, glorifying Allah, and fragrant plants had grown up to her shoulders.

The husband was astounded by this miracle. He ran to the Prophet David and told him all that had transpired. Prophet David summoned the woman and said: "O lady, I know that Allah has made the fire cool and harmless for you because of a righteous deed. Tell me what it is." The woman replied: "O Messenger of Allah, I have done no great work. Yet for twenty years I have never delayed my prayers and have always performed them at their earliest time. I have abandoned all labour for the sake of prayer, and when the hour came while I was kneading dough, I would leave the dough and stand in prayer."

Prophet David gave her glad tidings, saying: "Know that you have gathered within yourself the sincerity of many sincere ones!"

## Wine Is the Root of All Evils

There was a man among the Children of Israel who devoted himself to worship in the mosque and performed his ablutions at a nearby canal. A wicked woman's house lay upon his path. One day she sent her servant to summon him, saying his relatives wished to question him about a matter. The man declined, saying: "I am not a person of justice, so my testimony has no standing." The woman swore an oath by Allah and pressed him to enter. Once inside, she quickly barred the door behind him and trapped the naive worshipper, saying: "Hear me! You shall not leave this house unless you do one of four things."

She placed three items before him: ⟪ar:مصحف⟫the Qur'an, wine, and a small child. The man asked: "What do you demand of me?"

The woman said: "(1) Either commit adultery with me, (2) burn ⟪ar:مصحف⟫the Qur'an in fire, (3) kill this child, for he is ⟪ar:وِلَدِ زنا⟫born of fornication and I wish to be rid of him, or (4) drink three glasses of wine."

The worshipper reasoned thus in his heart: "If I kill him, retribution falls upon me—a grievous sin indeed. To burn the Book of Allah is a terrible transgression. If I commit adultery, I face a hundred lashes or stoning. But to drink three glasses of wine—surely this is the least of evils. I shall drink and depart, then ask Allah for forgiveness."

Thinking thus, he consented to drink. The wine intoxicated him greatly; he asked for more, and in his drunkenness he said to the woman: "Come, lie with me." The deceitful woman, seeing him in her power, replied: "First kill this child and ⟪ar:مصحف⟫burn the Qur'an, then I shall come to you. Otherwise, do not touch me." The man, bereft of reason and aflame with lust, killed the child first, then burned the Book, and finally blackened his face with the wicked and vile woman.[^cite-8]

Thus did wine cause the man to commit all those deeds he had deemed forbidden. This is why wine ⟪ar:'اُم الخبائث'⟫ has been called "the mother of all evils"—for from her are born all transgressions.

## Paradise Lies Beneath the Feet of One's Mother

A worshipper named Jundub was devoted to worship in his sanctuary. His mother would visit him once each month. One day she came while he was in prayer. He did not open the door nor break his devotion, thinking to complete his prayer. His mother turned away. A second time she came, and again he was absorbed in prayer and prostration. He sent word: "I am in prayer and will not abandon my worship to open the door now." His mother departed in anger.

Not long after, a boy and a girl, both tending goats in the pasture near the sanctuary, committed fornication, and the girl became pregnant. When her condition became apparent and the time of birth drew near, people asked her: "Whose child do you carry?" She accused Jundub, saying: "It is Jundub's child."

The people seized Jundub and brought him before the governor, who ordered him flogged and imprisoned. When Jundub's mother learned of his plight, she hastened to the governor and pleaded: "O Amir! I beg you, keep my son and this girl in separate chambers. When she gives birth, ask the child who his father is. The truth will surely emerge, Allah willing." The governor accepted her petition.

When the child was born, it was brought before the ruler and asked: "Tell us, who is your father?" The child immediately answered: "My father is such-and-such a shepherd, who tends the flocks of such-and-such a man." Upon this answer, the shepherd and the girl were summoned and executed for their false accusation against Jundub. The governor appointed a nursing woman to care for the child.

Then the people turned to Jundub and asked: "What is the reason that Allah punished you so severely and brought you such disgrace, when you were innocent?" He replied: "My mother came to visit me twice while I was in prayer. I did not greet her, and she departed angry. Allah has given me this exemplary punishment because of my mother's displeasure and anger."

## The Earnings of Labour Surpass a Thousand Gifts

One day ⟪ar:سلیمان ابن داؤد علیہ السلام⟫Prophet Solomon, riding upon his throne and carried by the wind, looked down and beheld a very old man ploughing the earth with two oxen. Solomon commanded the wind: "Bring me to this old man." The wind set him down at that place. It was the hour of noon. Solomon greeted the old man, but he did not pause in his labour or return the greeting—so intent was he upon his work. Only after several hours, at the time of the afternoon prayer, did he unyoke his oxen and rest. Then he returned Solomon's greeting.

Solomon was astonished and said: "A strange thing! I greeted you at noon, yet you answer me only now at the time of Asr. How is this?" The old man replied: "Yes, it is as you say. I am a man of labour. From my toil from morning until afternoon I earn two barley loaves. Had I paused to answer your greeting, I would have lost two pieces of bread from my sustenance."

Prophet Solomon said: "I shall grant you whatever you ask. Tell me your need!" The old man replied: "Very well—make me young again!" Solomon said: "That is not within my power." The old man then asked: "Extend my life by one hour!" Again Solomon answered: "I have no such power." He said: "Then add a single grain to my destiny and my provision." Solomon, humbled and abashed, said: "Brother, you ask for things that no human being can grant. All such matters belong to the Creator alone. The creature has no power over these things." The old man said: "Then we are equal. Why should I ask anything of you?"[^cite-9]

Solomon reflected and said: "I had thought that you would ask me for wealth, and I would give you riches beyond measure, that you might live in comfort and ease for the rest of your days."

When the old man heard this, he took some dust and placed it in his palm. Raising his hands in prayer, he said: "O Lord of all worlds! There are some of Your servants whom, if they ask You to transform clay into gold, You do not refuse their prayer but grant it and make them joyful!" 

At that moment, Solomon beheld the dust become gold.

Then the old man raised his hands again in prayer: "O my Lord! There are some servants in Your world who love to earn their living by the sweat of their labour and to eat from their own toil. When they ask You to turn gold back into dust, You do it for them. Such is their preference!" 

And in that instant, Solomon saw the gold transformed into dust once more.

The Prophet Solomon stood amazed and said: "Today I have learned from this shaykh a lesson in faith, sincerity, and true detachment from the world."

## Self-Conceit and Pride Displease Allah

Prophet Solomon (peace be upon him) was riding upon his throne, flying through the air, clothed in a shirt most finely woven and luxurious. A subtle pride stirred within his heart at the elegance and beauty of this garment. At that very moment, a mighty wind arose and tore the shirt from his body, leaving Solomon naked upon his throne.

In anger he cried out to the wind: "What absurdity is this? You are commanded to obey me, yet you treat me thus!" The wind answered: "Yes, I am commanded to obey you—but only so long as you remain free from arrogance. The moment self-conceit (ʿujb) was born within you, my obedience ended!"

When Prophet Solomon heard these words, he was seized with remorse. From that day forward, he swore an oath: "Never again shall I wear fine or decorated garments. I shall clothe myself only in coarse cloth, that pride may never arise in my heart again." And he kept this resolve until the day he passed from this world.

**Notes**

It is said that the wind offered this counsel to Prophet Solomon: "O Solomon! O Messenger of Allah! Of what do you boast? Consider your origin—it is but unclean sperm. Consider your end—it is a rotting, foul-smelling corpse. Throughout your life you carry within you two impurities, urine and faeces, born from two unclean passages.

## Those Who Earn Lawful Sustenance Through Labour Surpass Even the Prophet

One day Prophet Solomon said to his Lord: "Who is more grateful to You than I?" It was revealed to him: "O Solomon! The one who earns his living through his own labour and hard work, then spends in My way and gives in charity—such a one is more grateful than you!"

Solomon replied: "O my Lord! In that sense, I am nothing indeed! I have never earned my bread through the labour of my hands until this day." 

Gabriel took pity upon him and brought a bag of grain from the heavenly store. He taught Solomon the art of grinding flour. From that time onward, Prophet Solomon would grind grain for two days; on the third day, he would sell his flour for barley; and on the fourth day, he would break his fast with bread made from that barley. He continued in this manner until his death. Never did he eat fine flour or rich dishes, but passed from the world with peace and faith in his heart, sustained by the humble earnings of his own hands.

## Through Sincerity of Intention, Faith Is Attained

There were two brothers who worshipped in a cave for eighty years. Over time, they became afflicted with a false pride in themselves, imagining that now it was their sacred duty to summon people to Allah and call them to guidance. This thought moved one to speak to the other, and both emerged from the cave. 

The first thing they encountered was a woodcutter bearing upon his head a heavy bundle of wood. They said to him: "Come, let us invite you to Islam and guide you toward the truth."

The woodcutter set down his bundle and addressed them: "Good sirs, I know full well who my Lord is! But tell me—what is sincerity? And what is the place of faith, conviction, and sincerity—these three—in your hearts?"

The two brothers looked at one another and confessed: "We do not know."

Hearing their confession of ignorance, the woodcutter raised his hands in prayer: "O Allah, there are some of Your servants who, if they ask You to transform these bundles of wood into gold, You will certainly not refuse them!" In that instant, the wood became gold.

Again he raised his hands: "There are some of Your servants who love to earn their lawful livelihood through the sweat of their labour and eat from their own toil. If they ask You to turn gold back into wood, You will surely do so, and will not refuse them!" In a moment, the gold returned to wood.

He then took up the bundle and, as the two worshippers were about to depart, he gave them this counsel: "Go now, first purify your intentions and ask forgiveness from your Lord. Pride and self-conceit have arisen within you both—these are not the means of advancement but of degradation."

## Through the Intercession of Martyrs, Prayers Are Answered

⟪ar:بنی اسرائیل⟫There lived among the Children of Israel an ʿaqīb (leader) who worshipped his Lord for seventy years upon the crest of a mountain. Allah sent an angel to test him, conveying this message: "I shall not accept your worship unless you go and present Islam to the king of the Children of Israel."

The worshipper rose at once and went to the royal court. He addressed the king boldly: "O you who are astray! O you arrogant one! Bear witness: ⟪ar:لا الہ الا اﷲ⟫ There is no god but Allah!"

The king, enraged by these words, turned to his ministers and demanded: "Who among you will kill this man? He claims there is a Lord besides me!" One of the ministers stepped forward and took custody of the worshipper.

The minister summoned a butcher and ordered him: "Flay the skin from the crown of his head to his forehead!" The butcher began his terrible work. When he reached the middle of the man's head, a faint groan escaped the worshipper's lips. At once Allah sent an angel with a message: "Go and tell him: 'If I hear you cry out again, I shall count you among the disbelievers.'" The poor, helpless worshipper made no sound thereafter, despite unimaginable agony, until he breathed his last. Thus was he martyred.

In the king's court there was a believer who had concealed his faith through taqiyyah (sacred dissimulation). He took the flayed skin to preserve it from contempt. The oppressors cast the martyred worshipper's body upon the road. In the darkness of night, the hidden believer retrieved it and buried it within his own house, levelling the grave so that no trace remained.[^cite-10]

Whenever drought and famine came upon that city, this believer would take out the martyred worshipper's skin and raise it to the heavens in prayer: "O Allah! Grant us rain for the sake of this flayed martyr!" And every time, the rain would fall.

## Knowledge Is a Shield Against Satan's Deceptions

There was a worshipper among the Children of Israel who laboured in devotion for forty years. The devil attempted countless stratagems to mislead him, yet all failed. At last, Satan came to him in the guise of an old man, bearing a bandage over his left eye.

The pious worshipper asked with concern: "Brother, what afflicts you that you wear this bandage?" The deceiver replied: "It is nothing grave—my eye does not pain me. But I have heard that on the Day of Resurrection, Allah will call each of us to account for our sight and vision. Therefore, I chose to see the world with but one eye, that I might not have to give account for both. I have observed other worshippers in my city doing likewise."

This gentle and naive worshipper fell prey to this deception. From that day forward, he bound his own left eye with a bandage. The purpose of Satan—may Allah curse him—was this: water would not touch his entire face during ablution, and thus his ablution would be incomplete and defective, rendering his prayer invalid and worthless.

In this state, the worshipper spent thirty years. One day a learned scholar came to him. Seeing the bandage, the scholar asked its reason, and the worshipper recounted the entire story. The scholar said: "Brother! The one who instructed you in this deception was not a righteous person but the accursed devil himself! His intent was that your ablution be defective and your prayer rejected. Cast off this bandage immediately and begin anew! If you die in this state, Allah will render all your deeds fruitless, and you shall die the death of a disbeliever."

The worshipper was terrified at hearing the truth and performed anew all the prayers of the thirty years he had wasted. At that moment, Satan shouted to his army: "Alas! These scholars and jurists destroy every stratagem and deception I devise! I labour to seduce the innocent, yet they stand between me and my prey, delivering them from my snare. Behold this scholar—how he rescued this worshipper, who was completely ensnared, through his knowledge of jurisprudence and the Sacred Law!

Therefore, O brothers! Seek the company of scholars and jurists, and learn from them!"

## Through Charity, Death Is Postponed

There was a washerman among the Children of Israel whose house faced a place of worship. Every night at the time of prayer, he would gather drunkards at his home for singing, drinking, and abuse. The worshippers complained to Jesus (peace be upon him) of this disturbance. Jesus came to the washerman's house and asked: "Where is the washerman?" He had gone to the canal to wash clothes.

Jesus said to the worshippers: "Be assured—he shall not return from the canal alive." Yet that night, the washerman returned safely to his home. Jesus was surprised to see this and went to him, asking: "Brother, what good deed did you perform at the canal today?" 

The washerman replied: "I did nothing extraordinary. But while I was eating bread in the afternoon, I saw an old woodcutter approaching with a large bundle of wood. He appeared half-dead from hunger, his stomach clinging to his spine. I felt compassion for him and gave him a portion of my bread."

Jesus said: "You have spoken truthfully. Now open your bundle of washed clothes!" When the washerman untied it, he saw a black serpent coiled inside, its mouth gaping wide. Jesus explained: "I asked my Lord to send a calamity upon you because of your transgression, and He sent this serpent to kill you. But when you fed the hungry person, Allah made the reward of that charity a seal upon this serpent's mouth."

The washerman was deeply grieved upon hearing these words from the Prophet. He repented sincerely and vowed never again to disobey Allah.

## Charity Averts the Decree of Death

A man came to Prophet Solomon from ⟪ar:بیت المقدس⟫Jerusalem and ⟪ar:حضرت سلیمان نبی علیہ السلام⟫ said: "O Messenger of Allah, I have a cousin—both of us are orphans and both are poor. I sent her a marriage proposal, but she refused because of my poverty. Others proposed and she accepted them."

Prophet Solomon's heart was moved with pity. He sent a jinn to bring the girl before him. Then he asked the youth: "Is this the girl you mentioned?" He said: "Yes." He asked the girl: "Do you know this young man?" She confirmed it. He said: "Why do you not marry your cousin?" The girl replied: "Because he is poor, and I too am poor and destitute. How shall we live together? Therefore I sought a man of wealth, and I shall marry a man of means."

When Hazrat Solomon heard that they did not marry solely because of poverty, though both loved each other, he gave the girl ten thousand dirhams and said: "I shall arrange your marriage to him." The girl accepted. After the marriage, she requested of Prophet Solomon: "O Messenger of Allah, our city lies far from here. I beg you to send us there by means of your jinn." Solomon granted the request, and a jinn transported them to their city.

With Solomon's gift, the youth purchased a house, servants, a horse, and a slave-girl. After their marriage, Gabriel came to Prophet Solomon and asked: "Do you know what we call today ⟪ar:یوم مَزِیْد⟫⟪ar:یوم جمعۃ⟫ and what we have done?"

Gabriel said: "Today I met the Angel of Death. He told me that he was coming to take the life of this newlywed couple next Friday. They would not live more than eight days." Naturally, Prophet Solomon was deeply saddened by this news. On the second Friday, he mounted his throne and reached the city by the time of the afternoon prayer. He found both the couple alive and well, and he wondered whether Gabriel had deceived him.

At that moment Gabriel appeared and said: "By Allah, O Solomon, I did not lie, nor do I know falsehood! If I were to lie, Allah would burn me to ash before the sentence left my lips!" At that same moment, ⟪ar:ملک الموت (عزرائیل)⟫the Angel of Death appeared and said: "O Solomon, I did not lie. I was indeed commanded to take their lives. But when a beggar came to their door, they fed him and gave him shelter. After he ate and drank, he prayed three prayers for them. First: 'May Allah forgive you both!'—and Allah forgave them their sins. Second: 'May Allah send you both to Paradise!'—and Allah arranged for them to dwell with the righteous. Third: 'May Allah grant you both long life!'—and Allah increased their lifespan by forty years!"[^cite-11]

Prophet Solomon said: "Now I know that Allah has fixed the age and sustenance of every soul. It is never possible to increase or decrease it." Then Gabriel came and said: "Allah erases what He wills and writes down what He wills. The Mother of the Book remains with Allah. ⟪ar:یوم مَزِیْد⟫The Day of Increase does not change from one state to another!"

## Through Charity, Even Grave Sins Are Forgiven

There was a worshipper among the Children of Israel who laboured in devotion to Allah for seventy years. Then he fell into a grave sin—fornication. Allah nullified all his seventy years of worship, and an angel said to him: "Begin again! All your deeds from this point forward are wasted. You shall receive no reward for your worship."

The sorrowful and afflicted worshipper descended from his sanctuary. Out of his own sustenance, he fed seventy poor people, then returned to his place of worship and sought Allah's forgiveness for his sin. ## Through Obedience to Her Husband, the Wife Attains Deliverance

There was a righteous man among the Israelites whose wife, though black-skinned and one-eyed, was obedient to her husband in all things. Every evening when he sat down to eat, she would stand beside him holding a lamp, that his meal might be illuminated and he might eat without difficulty. Her husband would say: "Sit with me! Let us eat together." But she would refuse, saying: "It is my duty to serve you thus."

One night, as she stood with the lamp, the flame went out. She ran to the kitchen to find fire, but there was no wood or straw with which to rekindle it. In that moment, she cut a strand of her own hair and lit it, and the lamp burned again. She returned to her place and made no mention of what had occurred to her husband.

Allah was so pleased with her obedience that when she awoke in the morning, her face had become radiant and white, her eye was made whole and seeing, and her sight was restored to her completely.

## No Act of Divine Power Is Empty of Good and Wisdom

There was a righteous man among the Israelites whose son was impious. The man set out on a journey with his son as part of a caravan. At one point, his camel lay down and would not rise, though he urged it greatly. The caravan abandoned them and continued on, reaching a village by evening.

The righteous man and his son were forced to spend the night in the forest. The son said to his father: "Those caravan people were fortunate—they reached the village safely, slept in comfort and ease. But we remain here all night in hardship!" His father replied only: "O my son, it is possible that Allah has written good for us in this."

In the morning the camel stood and they rode toward the village. When they arrived, they found the caravan at the canal, bathing. The father asked: "Friends, what is the matter? Why do you bathe at dawn? What calamity has befallen you?" They replied: "Brother, you are blessed that you spent the night in the forest! When we reached the village, we found the governor's house in flames. We were commanded, along with the villagers, to draw water from the canal all night to extinguish the fire. Our faces and bodies were covered with smoke and ash. We were tormented all night—our rest was turned to labour and our comfort to suffering!"

The father turned to his son and said: "Did I not tell you that Allah might have written good for us in this hardship? In every affliction, know that we do not see where the good is hidden within it."

## Righteous Deeds and the Pursuit of Worldly Gain

There lived a believer among the Israelites who was a merchant of perfumes. He travelled from Syria to India in search of the finest scents to trade. He arrived in a great city and remained there for several days. He observed that the people of the city would go out to a certain place, return after an hour, and open their shops. Curious about this custom, he followed them one day and saw them gathering around a large green shrub, prostrating before it and worshipping it.

The believer was filled with indignation that a creation was being worshipped instead of Allah. He could not bear this abomination. He resolved to cut down this accursed tree at night, that the polytheists might no longer worship it. He procured an axe and went forth—but found an old man sitting there. The old man asked: "What do you want?" The believer replied: "I wish to cut down this cursed tree, that the polytheists may no longer ask of it."

The old man flattered him and offered him many dinars, saying: "You shall receive the same amount of dinars upon your pillow every morning, and as long as you dwell in this city, you shall not lack a single day of this bounty." The believer was pleased, took the dinars, and did not fulfil the righteous intention with which he had come.

When he awoke the next morning, no dinar lay upon his pillow. He took up his axe again, determined to cut down the tree. This time, his intention was purely for Allah's sake. The old man appeared again—but instead of flattery, he seized the believer, threw him to the ground, pinned him beneath his weight, and strangled him.

The believer gasped: "O old man! Tell me—who are you?" The creature replied: "I am he who brought your father Adam out of Paradise!" The believer asked: "O accursed one! Why did you give me so many dinars yesterday? And why do you seek to kill me today, breaking your promise?" 

Satan replied: "Yesterday you were working for the truth ⟪ar:لی اللّٰہ اور فی اللّٰہ⟫(for Allah and in Allah's way)—your intention was pure, and so Allah was with you. Neither jinn nor mankind could prevail over you. But today you are doing the same deed for worldly gain and for the pleasure of creatures—now I have overcome you! I am destroying you!" And he fled in defeat.

Only Allah knows how many righteous deeds we perform each day with intention not directed toward pleasing Allah, but toward worldly gain or the approval of others. We receive no reward for such works—only loss. May Allah grant us all pure intention in all our deeds!

## With Allah There Is Delay, but Never Darkness

## Remorse Is Better Than Worship

## Selfish Worship Is Not Accepted

Hazrat Musa ibn Imran encountered an old man praying in a cave. He asked: "How long have you been praying here?" The man replied: "For seventy years!" Moses asked: "Do you have a request?" He said: "Yes—ask your Lord whether He has accepted any of my deeds."

## Honouring One's Mother

There was a man among the Israelites who lived with his mother. His mother kept the keys to the storehouse of food and grain. One day at noon, two guests arrived. The people of a great city among the Israelites had sunken deep into transgression and wickedness. ## The Fear of Hell's Torment

## Overlooking the Wicked Is Itself a Great Sin

One night Allah commanded Gabriel: "At dawn, bring ruin upon the people of such and such a village. Shake the earth beneath them so that the entire nation perishes!"

Gabriel made ready to descend to earth and carry out this command. But night still held sway over the land. When Gabriel came down to that village, he beheld four thousand souls standing in prayer, performing the Fajr (dawn prayer) with full devotion.

Because of their silence, the wicked believe their deeds to be virtuous. Therefore, destroy them all—the righteous and the wicked together."

## The Extinguishing of Hope Renders the Soul Inert

Jesus observed an old man laboring with a spade, turning the earth and preparing it for cultivation. The ancient one toiled with great effort, his body bent, his hopes still kindled for a harvest. Jesus felt moved by this sight. It occurred to him that the old man would be spared such arduous work if all hope were stripped from his heart. In this thought, he prayed to Allah: "O Allah! Remove the high hopes from this old man's heart."

At once the old man set down his spade, rose slowly, and seated himself in the shade of a nearby bush in complete repose. He sat idle in this manner for some hours.

Then Jesus reconsidered what he had done, and this troubled him deeply. He prayed anew: "O Allah! Return hope to his heart."

The old man immediately rose, took up his spade once more, and returned to his labors as before, working with renewed vigor.

Jesus approached him and said: "I observed you digging earnestly. But suddenly you ceased, set down your spade, and went to rest in the shade. Later you returned to your work. What caused this change?"

The old man replied: "Yes, I will tell you. While I worked, my mind was seized by doubt: Why do I dig? Why do I till this soil? Why do I prepare to plant, when perhaps only a few days remain to me? I may not live to see these plants grow or to gather their fruit. When I saw death approaching, my hope was broken, and I abandoned my work. But after sitting idle, my soul spoke to me and said: Arise and work! Place your trust in Allah's provision. Perhaps if you live longer, you may labor with your hands and earn grain, that you need not depend upon others or extend your hand before anyone in need. So I returned to my labor."

Jesus said to him with grave wisdom: "You have spoken truly. When hope is abandoned, the human soul becomes inert and sluggish. Without hope, people will not work, and without work, the world cannot endure."[^cite-17]

## The Blessing That Attends the Fulfillment of Vow and Covenant

Among the Israelites there lived a young man of poor station. He earned his living by laboring for others, but the wealthy drove him mercilessly beyond his strength. His body was worn and exhausted, yet they showed him no mercy. One day, desperate and weary, he made a vow before Allah: "O Lord! I vow this day that if Thou wilt grant me capital with which to trade, I shall give one-tenth of my earnings in charity to Thy name."

Shortly thereafter a man hired him for ten days and paid him ten dirhams. The laborer, mindful of his vow, gave one dirham in charity and began trading with the remaining nine. When his goods had doubled to twenty dirhams, he gave two in charity. When it reached one hundred, he gave ten. At two hundred, he gave twenty. Thus his trade grew, and he kept his vow faithfully—giving always one-tenth in alms—until at last he possessed land, slaves, houses, and extensive farms. His wealth had multiplied many times over.

One day, as he rode along the road on horseback, a black serpent clung to the horse's foot, and the animal stopped in its tracks. When the rider looked down, he saw the snake. It raised its head and spoke: "A hunter rides behind me seeking to kill me. Grant me shelter, I beseech you."

The righteous man accepted the serpent's plea. "Come then, hide in my garments," he said.

The serpent replied: "I fear the hunter will search your clothes and find me there."

"Then what would you ask of me?" the man inquired.

The serpent said: "Open your mouth, that I may enter your belly. There I shall be safe."

"But will you come out again?" the man asked.

"I shall certainly come out," the serpent assured him.

The righteous man consented, and the serpent slithered into his mouth and down into his belly. After half an hour had passed, the man called out: "Come out now, for no one pursues you, and I am choking!"

But instead of emerging, the serpent presented three conditions: "Accept one of these three demands. First, cease forever to give one-tenth of your wealth to charity. Second, permit me to poison your heart so that you become a disbeliever. Third, if you refuse these, allow me to eat your liver."

The man asked in wonder: "Tell me—who are you?"

The serpent replied: "I am Iblis (the Devil)."

The righteous man said with firmness: "Then hear me. Climb that mountain ahead, and eat my liver in peace until I die. For death is sweeter and easier to me than to abandon the tenth part of my sustenance, or to become a disbeliever!"

He climbed the mountain and wept bitterly. By chance a rider appeared and asked: "Why do you weep? What is the cause of your grief?"

The man told him all that had transpired. The rider then gave him a fruit, saying: "Eat this fruit and go to the toilet."

The righteous man ate the fruit and did as the rider commanded. By the power of that fruit, the serpent came forth from his body in pieces, and he was delivered.[^cite-18]

The man returned to the rider and asked: "May Allah have mercy upon you—who are you?"

The rider replied: "I am an angel sent by Allah. Allah says: 'Labor for Me in this world, and I shall be of use to you in the Hereafter. Continue to give the tenth part in charity, and I shall increase your wealth day by day.'"

## The Reward of Trust in Allah and Abstention from Backbiting

Allah revealed to the Prophet Solomon: "Ride upon your throne and journey to such and such a place. There you shall witness a scene of wonder."

When Solomon reached that place, he beheld a dome suspended between earth and sky. Within it stood a venerable man in prayer.

Solomon asked him: "O Sheikh, how long have you dwelt here?"

The man replied: "Since the time of the Prophet Yunus."

"How long did you worship upon the earth?" Solomon asked.

"Six hundred years," the man answered.

"And how long have you remained in this dome?" Solomon inquired.

"For three hundred years," came the reply.

Solomon then asked: "By what means and through what actions did you attain this exalted station?"

The man said: "Through two simple things."

"What are these two deeds?" asked Solomon.

The man replied: "First, I have always placed my trust utterly in Allah. Never has the thought of poverty or hunger entered my heart. I never worried that if I spent my days and nights in prayer, what would sustain me or who would feed me. I was certain that my Creator and Sustainer would provide for me. I knew that those who harbor anxiety about sustenance do not truly trust in Allah.[^cite-19]

Second, I have never spoken ill of anyone in all my life. Not a single disparaging word about Muslim or non-believer has passed from my lips, nor have I backbited anyone."

## The Wishes of the Believer and the Unbeliever

There dwelt a Jew among the Israelites, and in his neighborhood lived a faithful worshipper of Allah. One day the Jew desired fresh fish and sent a hunter forth with a net. At the same time, the believing worshipper, who was poor, felt a longing—after twenty years—to taste *rotī marghan* (bread enriched with ghee). He sent a man to the village to bring ghee.

In the meantime, two angels passed through the heavens. The descending angel asked the ascending one: "What command was given to you? For what purpose do you go to earth?"

The ascending angel replied: "The Jew has desired fresh fish this day, and Allah has commanded me to fill his net abundantly with fish."

The descending angel said: "And I have been commanded concerning a believing man who, after twenty years, has desired to eat *rotī marghan*. He has sent a man to fetch ghee. I am commanded to snatch the pot from that man's hand and spill the ghee upon the ground, so that the believer may not taste this delicacy and become ensnared by worldly attachment."

Thus did the angel reveal the divine wisdom: "Allah fulfills every desire of the unbeliever in this world, but He does not grant the desires of the believer here. Instead, He reserves them for the Hereafter, that He might reward him many times over."[^cite-20]

## The Divine Judgment Between Believer and Unbeliever

Allah revealed to Moses: "Go to the far shore of the river and observe the scenes that unfold there."

Moses journeyed to that place and sat upon a stone. After some time, he saw a rider approaching on a camel. The man descended, tethered his camel, and from his saddlebag withdrew a golden idol. He polished it with care, turned it toward the east, and prostrated himself in worship before this lifeless thing.

When Moses beheld this idolatry, anger kindled in his heart. He began to curse this idol-worshipper within his soul.

The man rose from his prostration and prayed: "O Allah! I have journeyed to this shore because Thou hast granted me abundance of fish from these waters."

He cast his net into the river. Allah commanded the angel appointed over that river to fill the net with fish, and it became heavy with abundance. He emptied the catch into a large basket, then prostrated again before the idol, saying: "O Allah! Thou hast heard my prayer. Now grant the same bounty again."

He cast the net once more, and again it came forth filled to bursting. He emptied it into another large basket. Then he seized the idol by the head and shook it, praying: "O Allah! Thou hast given me an abundance of fish, but I cannot gather them all myself. Therefore, send me a helper to carry these baskets to my camel."

At that moment, Allah sent an angel who took two large baskets and began to walk before him. The man lifted his idol and approached his camel, mounted it, and departed.

Witnessing this scene, Moses was deeply troubled in his heart. He cried out: "My Lord is strange indeed! He grants boundless sustenance to one who does not worship Him, who does not even call upon Him!"

Moses remained seated when a believing man approached, carrying a small net upon his shoulder. First the man performed ablution with great care, then prayed, and afterward supplicated with profound humility: "O Allah! Thou knowest well that my wife and I have eaten nothing for three days. We suffer from hunger, yet we have not informed anyone, nor extended our hands before another out of shame and pride. Grant us sustenance this day that our hunger might be satisfied and our lives preserved!"

He cast his net into the river. Allah commanded the angel of that river to remove all fish from the net, so that not a single fish would be caught. After a time, the believer drew out the net—it was empty. He cast it again, and again it came forth barren. On the third cast, a small fish, scarcely the size of a bucket, became entangled. As he held it in his hand, it slipped from his grasp and returned to the water. The hungry man turned back in utter exhaustion.

Moses blazed with anger at this sight. Allah said to him: "O Moses, why are you so angered?"

Moses replied: "Thy division and Thy judgment are strange, O Lord. I do not comprehend them!"

Allah revealed: "This is merely the beginning. Watch now and follow this man, but interfere not in My affairs. Observe in silence what unfolds."

Moses followed. Suddenly a lion emerged from the thickets and tore the believer to pieces. Moses gathered the blood-stained garments and net of the slain man and proceeded toward the village. Upon arrival, he asked: "Does anyone here know who wears such clothes and owns such a net?"

The people directed him to the home of the worshipful man. Moses called out, but before anyone could answer, Allah commanded an angel to destroy that house and all within it. The roof collapsed, and the entire household perished.

When Moses witnessed this terrible scene, he cast down what remained of the garments, and his mind became clouded with anguish. For two months he wandered in a state of confusion and distress. His heart and reason were overwhelmed.

Then, as his mind grew weak from such anguished questioning, Allah took mercy upon him and opened the gates of Paradise. Moses beheld that poor, helpless believer now seated with his family upon cushioned thrones, filled with joy and delight beyond measure.

Then the gates of Hell were opened. There Moses saw that same idol-worshipper whose desires in the world had been so abundantly fulfilled. His skin had become thick as leather. Fire entered through his bosom and emerged from his mouth. Serpents and scorpions crawled upon his body, biting him at every moment. He writhed in agony, unable to act or escape.

When Moses beheld these two scenes, Allah said to him: "O Moses! Tell Me—which of these two states is superior and better? The blessings of the world, or the blessings of the Hereafter? Those of this life shall pass away, but those of the next shall endure forever. And tell Me as well: which suffering is more severe? The afflictions of this world, or the torment of the Hereafter?"

Moses immediately repented before his Lord for his hasty anger and ignorance. He sought forgiveness with a contrite heart.[^cite-21]

## Sincere Remorse Obtains Forgiveness Even for the Greatest Sins

A woman came before Moses and said: "O Messenger of Allah! In my youth I committed the sin of adultery and became pregnant. From shame and fear of dishonor, I killed the child and buried its body in a pit. Now I wish to repent and atone for these two grave sins—adultery and murder. Will Allah accept my repentance?"

Moses rebuked her harshly and said in anger: "Go from me, disbelieving woman! Depart! There is no miracle for you, and no forgiveness awaits you!"

But when she had left, Allah revealed to Moses: "O Moses! Why hast thou driven away this woman who turns in repentance from sin? I have accepted her remorse and her turning back. Verily, I shall forgive even a graver sinner upon sincere repentance!"

Moses asked in wonder: "O Allah! Who could be a greater sinner than she?"

Allah answered: "Whoever willfully neglects his prayer."[^cite-22]

## The Inhabitants of Paradise and Their Recognition

One day the Prophet David prayed to his Lord: "O Lord! Show me my neighbors in Paradise, that I might know them in this life."

The command came: "Go to the market."

There David saw a woodcutter walking with a load of wood upon his back, calling out: "Who desires to buy what is lawful in exchange for what is lawful?"

A buyer approached and asked: "Will you accept for this burden a loaf of barley bread?"

The old man accepted the price. He received three slices of bread, gave one away in charity, and continued on. The Prophet David followed after him.

The woodcutter left the village and seated himself in the shade of a bush beside a pond. He removed his bread, ate it with water, and offered praise to Allah, saying: "Praise be to Allah who has fed me, and had He willed, He could have left me hungry."

He then drank water and said: "Praise be to Allah who has given me drink, and had He willed, He could have left me thirsty."

Prophet David approached him and asked: "O Sheikh, how long have you lived in this manner?"

He replied: "Since the time of the Prophet Yunus."

"How do you sustain yourself?" asked David.

The old man answered: "Once each month I carry a load of wood and sell it for whatever price I receive. Of this price, I give three parts in charity and two parts I keep for myself. I eat bread and drink water twice yearly—twelve times in a year—while constantly praising my Lord."

Beholding the sheikh's discipline and surrender, David said: "Would you permit me to spend the remainder of my life with you?"

The sheikh replied: "O David! That is not your calling. But know this—I shall be your neighbor in Paradise."

Having spoken these words, the sheikh vanished from before the prophet.

David then entered into the thickets and discovered a cave. Within, he found forty worshippers seated in remembrance. David greeted them and sat among them. When the time of Maghrib prayer arrived, all rose and prayed together. After the prayer, a single loaf of bread and a cup of water were placed before them. Ten of the worshippers ate and drank, and the bread returned to its original state and the cup refilled itself with water. Ten more ate and drank, and again the bread and cup were restored. The third and fourth groups did likewise. All forty worshippers ate and drank, yet the bread remained whole and the cup remained full.

David asked them: "How long have you dwelt in this place?"

They replied: "Two hundred years. From the first day, bread is our food and water our drink, with which we also perform ablution."

Then David also partook of the bread, which returned to its original state. He asked them: "Will you permit me to remain and dwell with you?"

They refused vehemently, saying: "Even were you a prophet of Allah, we would not allow you to stay with us."

David asked the reason.

They replied: "Because your words would distract us from the remembrance of Allah."[^cite-23]

## The Divine Nature Admits No Change

There lived a judge in a city of Israel who accepted no judgment without bribery. He would decide cases in favor of whoever paid him gold. One day a villager came to the city with a female camel and her young offspring to sell them. The calf followed its mother, but someone stole it during the transaction. The owner searched extensively but could not find the thief.

The thief fed the calf on cow's milk. When a year had passed and the calf had grown, the thief brought it to the market for sale. The original owner recognized it and brought both claimants before the judge. The thief bribed the judge with fifty dinars.

The judge examined the cow and the camel, then declared with false wonder: "Behold the glory of Allah and His power! He creates whom He wills. Behold how Allah has made a camel issue from a cow!"

When the original owner protested, the thief said: "It was born of this cow. This is its true mother. If you doubt, let the camel drink, and observe to whom it goes."

The camel was led to drink, and it inevitably followed the cow for milk. The judge proclaimed: "Allah be praised! Thy power is wondrous. Thou createst in the womb what Thou wilt and causest to emerge whom Thou wilt."

The people of the city were outraged at this absurd judgment. All declared it foolish beyond measure. The owner of the camel and the thief were brought together before a devoted worshipper. After hearing the full account, the worshipper said: "I cannot render judgment at this moment. Return after seven days."

When the people asked the reason for this delay, the worshipper replied: "I am menstruating now."

The people said in bewilderment: "How can a man menstruate? This is contrary to nature!"

The worshipper said: "Now the truth has been revealed to you. Cows can only bear their own kind, not a different species. Nature does not alter its ways and customs. Therefore, the cow cannot have borne the camel."

When the judge heard these words, he was struck dumb and filled with shame.[^cite-24]

## Misfortune Is Earned by One's Own Hands

A man was performing circumambulation of the Kaʿba when he saw a beautiful woman. His heart was seized by lust, and he touched her back. The woman turned and saw him wink at her with impure intent. Repulsed by this obscene gesture within the sacred shrine, she cursed him: "O Allah! Cut off his hand!"

After completing the tawāf (circumambulation), the man went to Minā for the pilgrimage rites and lay down to sleep beneath a tree. During the night, thieves stole the belongings of pilgrims and fled in haste. The pilgrims awoke in alarm and pursued the thieves into the darkness.

In the confusion of pursuit, a thief, seeing many people chasing him, hastily dropped the stolen goods in front of the sleeping man and escaped. The people came upon the goods lying before him and seized him, believing him to be the thief. Though he protested his innocence, no one believed him. Witnesses testified against him, and the Sultan ordered his hand cut off as punishment for theft.

When the man reflected upon his condition, he remembered the indecency he had committed in the House of Allah and the curse the woman had pronounced. He understood then that this punishment was the consequence of his earlier transgression.

Thus it is that every affliction a person receives from Allah or His saints is a result of his own past deeds. Allah says in the Qurʾān (interpretation of the meaning): ⟪ar-quote:کُلُّ نَفسٍ بِمَا کَسَبَت رَھِینَۃ⟫ "Every soul is bound by what it has earned."[^cite-25]

## The Reward from Allah for Refraining from the Unlawful

A reliable narrator relates: "I beheld a blacksmith who placed his hand into the furnace fire, yet his hand did not burn. In wonder, I asked him: 'How is it that your hand remains unburned in the flames? What is the reason for this miracle?'

He recounted to me the following: 'In my neighborhood there lived a woman of great beauty and virtue. I harbored lustful designs upon her, but she paid no heed to my advances. One year a terrible famine afflicted our village. The woman, poor and destitute, came to me in desperation and said: "For the sake of Allah, give me food!"

I replied: "I will feed you only if you permit me to satisfy my lust."

She refused, saying: "I shall not compromise my dignity with sin," and departed.

The next day she returned and again beseeched me: "For the sake of Allah, feed me!"

I gave the same wicked answer as before. The poor woman, losing hope, departed again.

On the third day she came once more, saying: "For the sake of Allah, feed me!"

Again I made the same demand. In despair, she left. Yet on the third day, Allah's mercy stirred within me, and I felt ashamed. I set food before her.

She asked: "Do you give me this food for the sake of Allah?"

I said: "No."

She rose at once and departed without eating.

On the fourth day she returned, saying: "For the sake of Allah, feed me!"

On that day, Allah's grace and clemency descended upon me. ⟪ar:نفس امارۃ⟫The base self (nafs al-ammāra) that had incited me toward transgression was subdued. It was as if a veil had been lifted from my eyes. I reflected: "O Allah! Here stands a weak and helpless woman, steadfastly refusing to sin despite being famished for days. What has become of me, that I am so easily drawn toward iniquity?"

I said to myself: "O Allah! I repent before Thee. Forgive me this evil."

I placed food before the pious woman and said to her with gentleness: "Do not fear. Eat with ease and satisfaction. Today I offer this food to you for the sake of Allah and for His sake alone."

The woman raised her hands before eating and made this invocation: "O Allah! If this man speaks truthfully, then forbid him the Fire of this world and protect him from the Fire of the Hereafter!"

Allah answered her prayer, and from that day, the fire could not burn me."[^cite-26]

There is a ḥadīth that affirms: "Whoever has power over a woman and refrains from unlawful relations out of fear of Allah, Allah shall protect him from great fear on the Day of Resurrection. He shall forbid him the Fire of Hell and admit him to Paradise."
